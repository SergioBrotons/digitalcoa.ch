## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: DigitalCoa.ch Geneva Cross-Border M&A Decision Support — CHF 50-500M Mid-Market Integrated Advisory
The Geneva cross-border M&A decision support ICP (CHF 50-500M mid-market) requires an integrated advisory approach combining legal structure (Swiss/Geneva M&A mechanics), finance (valuation, deal financing, earn-out structuring), ESG (environmental liability, sustainability DD), and cybersecurity (target IT infrastructure, data breach liability). No single firm in Geneva offers this as an integrated service for mid-market transactions. DigitalCoa.ch's independent positioning is a structural advantage here — conflicted advisors (Big 4, bank-affiliated, law firm with opposing side relationships) cannot credibly offer the integrated candor this ICP segment requires. The engagement model is project-based with a 2-3 month diagnostic-to-close cycle, with optional 90-day post-close integration advisory retainer.

### Finding 02: Post-CSRD Board Sustainability Decision Architecture — Federated Governance Responsibility
The CSRD Phase 2 large-entity deadline (June 2026) has made board-level sustainability decision architecture a distinct advisory sub-segment. Boards need to understand their federated governance responsibility for sustainability disclosures — which committee (audit, risk, nomination) owns which aspect, what the board's liability exposure is for false or misleading sustainability statements, and how to structure the board's oversight of the double materiality assessment process. This is a governance and fiduciary advisory sub-segment distinct from the operational CSRD compliance advisory covered in Phase 03. Geneva boards of listed companies and large unlisted companies with EU market exposure are the primary ICPs.

### Finding 03: Geneva UHNW Cantonal Migration Advisory — Post-Geneva Tax Increase + Zug/SZ/NW Competition
Geneva's post-2026 tax increase (municipal and cantonal tax rate adjustments following cantonal referendum outcomes) has restarted UHNW cantonal migration advisory demand. Competing cantons (Zug, Schwyz, Nidwalden, Vaud for certain profiles) are actively courting Geneva UHNW families considering relocation. The advisory is not just tax optimization — it covers lifestyle infrastructure, family office ecosystem, international school access, diplomatic immunity considerations, and succession jurisdiction. This is a multi-disciplinary advisory (tax + legal + lifestyle + family governance) that independent advisors can credibly provide without the conflict exposure of bank-affiliated advisors managing the family's existing mandates.

### Finding 04: Geneva Humanitarian Sector Executive Advisory — IO/NGO Non-Executive Board Positioning
Geneva's concentration of international organizations (UNOG, ITU, WTO, WIPO, WHO, ILO, UNHCR, ICRC, WTO, WHO, OHCHR) creates a distinct non-executive board advisory ICP: departing IO officials transitioning to private sector boards and Geneva foundations seeking qualified non-executive directors with IO credibility. The advisory covers board composition strategy, foundation governance (Art. 80ff ZGB), IO transition career structuring, and Geneva foundation board positioning. This ICP is underserved because it requires both Geneva institutional credibility and board-level governance expertise — a combination that independent advisors with Geneva IO networks can credibly offer.

### Finding 05: DigitalCoa.ch Conflict-Free Independent Positioning — Documented Candor Framework as ICP Trigger
The independent vs. Big 4/bank/law firm conflict-free candor positioning is most effectively triggered when a Geneva executive has experienced a conflict situation — a bank-affiliated advisor recommending a product that served the bank's interest, a law firm conflicted out of representing them in a dispute with a bank counterparty, a Big 4 advisor with a conflicts check that prevented honest advice. Documenting real conflict scenarios (without identifying clients) and using them as illustrative case studies in content (with explicit anonymization) creates a powerful trust-building mechanism for the ICPs who have experienced this. The "documented candor" framework — showing, not just claiming, independence through illustrative conflict scenarios — is the most effective conversion mechanism for this positioning.

### Finding 06: DigitalCoa.ch Geneva CFO Treasury Decision Support — SNB Rate Environment + CHF Volatility Advisory
The current SNB rate environment (SNB easing amid EUR/CHF volatility) and post-tariff CHF/USD vol spike create a time-bounded treasury decision support advisory opportunity for Geneva CFOs. The advisory covers FX exposure assessment and hedging strategy, SNB policy interpretation and rate environment communication to the board, working capital optimization in volatile currency environment, and treasury risk governance framework design. This is a 2-5 day diagnostic engagement with optional 90-day retainer for implementation monitoring. The advisory is differentiated from bank treasury advisory (conflict-free) and from generalist financial advisory (specific SNB/FX expertise).
