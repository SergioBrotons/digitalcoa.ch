# Market Signals: Geneva Consulting — Small Boutique (2–50 employees)

**Phase:** C301 Phase 03
**Market mode:** Consequence management — retroactive documentation impossible, forced self-assessment in progress.

---

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

### 1. Documentation Debt — The Unsigned ledger
The most common failure pattern: consulting firms signed AI governance attestations in the last 12–18 months as client procurement formality, without any supporting methodology documentation. Now clients and carriers are following up with detailed questionnaires and the firms have no defensible answer.

**What it looks like:**
- "We signed the attestation. We didn't build the process."
- "The client questionnaire asks about our AI methodology. We don't have one documented."
- "I have to respond to a carrier AI questionnaire in two weeks. I have nothing to show them."

**Consequence:** Firms are in reactive mode, scrambling to build retroactive documentation that is obviously post-hoc and potentially worthless as evidence.

### 2. Client AI Governance Questionnaires — The Request That Triggers Exposure Awareness
Clients — especially large corporations and financial institutions — are sending detailed AI governance questionnaires to their consulting vendors as part of procurement and contract renewal cycles.

**Why this is escalating in 2026:**
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 2024) requires supervised institutions to ensure their AI service providers meet governance standards.
- EU AI Act high-risk provisions activating August 2026 are triggering upstream compliance demands.
- Large corporate procurement teams are now requiring AI governance attestation from all vendors, including small consulting boutiques.

**Failure mode:** Consultants fill out questionnaires defensively (whatever sounds good), creating a documented record of claims they can't substantiate. If a client ever audits those claims, the consultant is exposed.

### 3. Carrier Third-Generation Attestation Requests — The Renewal Surprise
Professional liability carriers have moved from optional AI questionnaires to mandatory attestation as part of coverage renewal. This is the third generation of carrier AI assessment:

- **Gen 1:** "Do you use AI?" (yes/no)
- **Gen 2:** "Describe your AI use" (free text)
- **Gen 3:** "Document your AI methodology, tooling, audit trail, and attestation process" (structured evidence request)

**The shock:** Small boutiques are being asked to produce methodology documentation, tooling inventories, and client AI governance compliance records. Firms without this infrastructure face coverage gaps, increased premiums, or outright non-renewal.

### 4. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation — The Accountability Gap
When a small boutique engages a sub-consultant or specialist for part of an engagement, the sub-consultant may use AI tools the lead consultant knows nothing about.

**The problem:** The client contract is with the lead boutique. The client holds the lead consultant accountable for all deliverables, including parts subcontracted. But the lead consultant has no visibility into the AI tools, methodologies, or documentation practices of the sub-consultant.

**Consequence:** Lead consultants are potentially exposed for AI-related failures in work they subcontracted and didn't supervise.

### 5. Proposal Methodology Defensibility — The Audit Trail Nobody Has
When consultants use AI tools in proposal development, analysis, or deliverable creation, they have no way to:
- Document which AI tools were used
- Explain the AI's role in the methodology
- Show the human validation process
- Demonstrate reproducibility of AI-assisted findings

**The fear:** "If a client challenges my recommendation, and I used AI — can I prove what the AI did vs. what I did? Can I defend it?"

### 6. nFADP Article 9 — The Profiling Line Uncertainty
Small consulting firms doing work in HR consulting, financial advisory, or operational optimization often encounter AI-adjacent profiling without recognizing it.

**The uncertainty tax:** Even when they're not directly running AI systems, recommending AI tools that make automated decisions about individuals potentially triggers nFADP Article 9 requirements. The personal liability for executives (up to CHF 250,000) makes this a high-stakes unknown.

**What they can't answer:** "Am I advising on something that counts as profiling under Swiss law? And if it is — what do I need to have documented?"

## What's Not Working in the Market

**Generic AI governance frameworks:** Off-the-shelf frameworks built for large enterprises don't translate to 5-person boutiques. The documentation overhead is impractical for firms that size.

**Consultants trying to retrofit compliance:** By the time a small boutique realizes they have a documentation problem, they're already in crisis mode. The retrospective approach never works well.

**Isolated attestation vs. methodology:** Firms that tried to answer carrier questionnaires without an underlying methodology practice are now exposed — the attestation references a process that doesn't exist.

**Treating AI governance as IT security:** The AI governance questionnaire from clients and carriers is fundamentally different from IT security questionnaires. Firms that answered the wrong questions are now dealing with follow-up requests.

---

## Net-New Findings for C301 Phase 03

1. **Carrier attestation has become the primary forcing function** — not regulation. Small boutiques are more afraid of insurance non-renewal than regulatory action.

2. **Client AI governance questionnaires are triggering retroactive documentation crisis** — firms signed attestations in 2024–2025 as procurement formality without building any supporting infrastructure.

3. **Sub-consultant AI delegation accountability gap** — lead boutique exposed for subcontractor AI methodology they have no visibility into.

4. **Proposal methodology defensibility has become existential** — AI-assisted consulting work without documentation is a liability in any future client dispute.

5. **nFADP Art 9 uncertainty is creating a paralysis tax** — consultants avoiding AI-adjacent work they could legitimately do because they can't assess the exposure.

6. **Geneva label as dual signal** — signals expertise to clients but also triggers higher scrutiny from carriers and compliance teams reviewing vendor attestations.
