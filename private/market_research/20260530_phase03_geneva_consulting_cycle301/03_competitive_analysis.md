# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Consulting — Small Boutique (2–50 employees)

**Phase:** C301 Phase 03
**Focus:** What exists in the market vs. what's missing for this ICP.

---

## Competitive Landscape

### What Exists for Small Consulting Boutiques

**1. Generic AI Governance Platforms and Frameworks**
- ISO 42001 certification programs (Expensive, enterprise-focused, ~12-month implementation)
- NIST AI RMF frameworks (Heavy for 2–50 person firms)
- EU AI Act compliance checklists (Designed for high-risk system operators, not advisory firms)
- AI governance software platforms (OneTrust, TrustArc, etc. — enterprise pricing, 6-figure contracts)

**Problem:** All designed for organizations that have dedicated compliance teams. Not adapted for boutiques that need practical, lean solutions.

**2. Traditional Consulting Methodology Documentation**
- Project management frameworks (PMBOK, etc.)
- ISO 9001 quality management for consulting firms
- Generic proposal templates and deliverable standards

**Problem:** These frameworks predate AI. They don't have a concept of "AI-assisted methodology documentation" or "AI audit trail requirements."

**3. Legal Firms Offering nFADP Compliance**
- Major law firms (Lenz & Staehelin, Pestalozzi, etc.) — too expensive for small boutiques
- Boutique data protection lawyers — available but reactive (post-signing crisis mode)
- Legal tech platforms — focus on GDPR/nFADP documentation, not consulting methodology

**Problem:** Legal help is reactive and expensive. They solve the problem after it's already a liability event.

**4. Insurance Brokers**
- Professional liability specialists in Geneva
- Brokers who work with consulting firms on coverage renewal

**Problem:** Brokers inform clients about carrier attestation requirements but don't provide the methodology infrastructure to actually satisfy those requirements.

---

## What's Missing for This ICP

### 1. Lean AI Governance for Boutiques
No market solution addresses the specific needs of a 5–30 person Geneva consulting boutique that needs:
- A lightweight methodology documentation system (not a full enterprise GRC platform)
- Something they can actually use in their day-to-day work without a compliance team
- Something that produces defensible evidence, not just a policy document

**Gap:** The space between "do nothing" and "buy an enterprise GRC platform" is empty.

### 2. Consulting-Specific AI Methodology Documentation
All AI governance tools are built for:
- AI system operators (tech companies, manufacturers)
- Financial institutions (FINMA-supervised entities)
- Healthcare organizations (complying with specific sector regulations)

None address:
- Consultants using AI in analysis and proposal development
- Deliverable documentation with AI audit trail requirements
- Multi-party consulting arrangements with sub-consultant delegation

**Gap:** A methodology documentation practice purpose-built for advisory work doesn't exist.

### 3. Pre-Client-Questionnaire Preparation
No service that helps small boutiques prepare for client AI governance questionnaires before they're sent:
- Understanding what clients are actually asking for
- Building the documentation infrastructure in advance
- Creating defensible responses vs. post-hoc scrambling

**Gap:** Consultants find out they're exposed when they receive the questionnaire — not before.

### 4. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework
No structured approach for lead consultants to:
- Assess sub-consultant AI governance practices
- Contractually manage AI methodology accountability
- Document delegation chain for client/regulatory scrutiny

**Gap:** This is a genuine unaddressed risk for multi-party consulting arrangements.

### 5. Carrier Attestation Preparation Service
No service that:
- Preps small boutiques for carrier AI attestation requirements
- Builds the documentation package carriers expect
- Provides representation quality attestation vs. bare-minimum compliance

**Gap:** The carrier is becoming the primary forcing function, but there's no advisory service helping boutiques prepare for it.

### 6. Geneva-Local AI Governance Advisory
The existing market is either:
- Global enterprise consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte, etc.) — wrong scale and price point
- Generic Swiss compliance consultancies — not consulting-practice-specific
- International SaaS platforms — no Geneva presence or local context

**Gap:** A Geneva-based advisory that understands the small boutique context, the local business culture, the carrier landscape, and the regulatory environment — in French and English.

## Key Insight

The market has not produced a solution for small Geneva consulting boutiques facing AI governance attestation from clients and carriers. The existing competitive landscape serves either enterprise firms with compliance teams or provides reactive legal counsel after the liability event.

The gap is: a lean, consulting-practice-specific, Geneva-local solution that helps small boutiques build proactive documentation infrastructure, prepare for client questionnaires, satisfy carrier attestation requirements, and manage sub-consultant AI delegation — before the crisis hits.

That's the position DigitalCoa.ch can credibly occupy.
