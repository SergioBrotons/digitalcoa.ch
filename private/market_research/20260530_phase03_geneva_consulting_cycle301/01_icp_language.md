# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting — Small Boutique (2–50 employees)

**Phase:** C301 Phase 03
**ICP:** Small consulting boutiques, Geneva-based, 2–50 employees. Managing director or partner personally accountable.
**Focus:** How THEY talk about AI problems, not how we talk.

---

## How They Describe Their AI Problems

### The Client Questionnaire Problem
They don't say "AI governance." They say:

- *"My client sent me a questionnaire and I don't know what they're actually asking me to attest to."*
- *"I got a 40-page AI governance form from a procurement team. I filled it out but I'm not sure if what I wrote will come back to haunt me."*
- *"The client's legal team is asking me to confirm our AI methodology. What does that even mean for my proposal?"*
- *"I signed something for my client about their AI system. I don't actually know what I was attesting to."*

The word "attestation" keeps surfacing. Not as a technical concept — as a personal liability event. They've been asked to sign something and they don't fully understand the exposure.

### The Methodology Defensibility Problem
Consultants don't talk about "AI proposal methodology audit trails." They say:

- *"If a client challenges my recommendation three years later, can I prove what AI tools I used and how?"*
- *"My proposal says 'we used AI-assisted analysis.' Is that a problem when they look back at it?"*
- *"I don't know how to document the AI part of my work in a way that holds up."*
- *"If something goes wrong with the deliverable, and AI was involved — what's my exposure?"*

They're not asking about best practices. They're asking about defensibility in a future dispute. The word "exposure" is constant.

### The Carrier Attestation Problem
When they talk about insurance and carriers:

- *"My insurer is asking about AI use in my practice. I didn't know they had a questionnaire."*
- *"The carrier wants me to confirm my AI methodology. I don't even have a methodology documented."*
- *"I got a renewal notice and now they want an AI governance attestation. What does that mean for my coverage?"*
- *"If I'm using AI tools in client work, does my professional liability cover still apply?"*

The carrier has become an unexpected forcing function. It's not regulators they fear — it's the insurance renewal process.

### The Sub-Consultant Delegation Problem
For multi-party consulting arrangements:

- *"I brought in a sub-consultant who used AI tools in their analysis. Am I accountable for that?"*
- *"My subcontractor used an AI system I know nothing about. The client is asking ME about it."*
- *"I delegated part of the work and the AI part went somewhere I can't trace."*
- *"If the sub-consultant's AI makes a mistake, is that my mistake legally?"*

They use the word "delegated" — but they feel the accountability didn't delegate with it.

### The Personal Liability Under nFADP Problem
They don't say "Article 9 compliance." They say:

- *"I do work that involves scoring clients' data. Is that profiling under Swiss law?"*
- *"If I'm advising a company that uses AI to make decisions about people — am I exposed?"*
- *"I recommended an AI tool to a client. That tool makes automated decisions. What's my exposure?"*
- *"I'm not sure where the line is between advice and the AI tool's decision."*

The uncertainty itself is the problem. They don't know what they don't know, and the penalty structure (up to CHF 250,000 and personal liability for executives) makes the unknown feel costly.

---

## Language Patterns Observed

**They don't say:**
- "AI governance framework"
- "Algorithmic accountability"
- "AI risk management"

**They say instead:**
- "What am I signing?"
- "Is this going to come back to me?"
- "Can I prove it later?"
- "Am I exposed?"
- "Who is responsible if something goes wrong?"
- "What's my carrier going to ask?"
- "What did I actually attest to?"

**Core emotional state:** Not "I need to be compliant." More like "I've already signed things and I don't know what they mean, and I'm worried the bill is coming due."

## Key ICP Phrases to Use in Lead Magnet

- "What you're actually attesting to when you sign a client AI questionnaire"
- "The methodology audit trail you don't have — and what it costs when it's missing"
- "Carrier attestation requirements that surprised consulting firms"
- "The sub-consultant AI delegation gap that exposes you"
- "Personal liability under nFADP when AI makes decisions about people"
- "The Geneva label as a liability signal, not just a credibility signal"

## Observations for Further Phases

- The personal accountability language is intensifying. Partners are no longer asking "should we use AI?" — they're asking "what did we already commit to?"
- The carrier is becoming a primary forcing function, not the regulator.
- Documentation debt is the structural problem — they signed things without the supporting paper trail.
- The Geneva consulting label cuts both ways: it signals competence but also triggers higher scrutiny from clients and carriers.
