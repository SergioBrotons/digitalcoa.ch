# Findings Summary: C301 Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting AI Accountability

**Phase:** C301 Phase 03
**Output date:** 2026-05-30
**Cycle:** C301 continuous research rotation
**ICP:** Small Geneva consulting boutiques, 2–50 employees, managing director/partner personally accountable

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Carrier Attestation Is the Primary Forcing Function
Not regulators. Not clients. The professional liability insurance renewal is the immediate crisis point for most small Geneva consulting boutiques. Carriers have moved to third-generation attestation requests requiring methodology documentation, tooling inventory, and audit trail evidence — and small boutiques have none of it. This is creating coverage gaps, premium increases, and non-renewal risk.

### 2. Client AI Governance Questionnaires Are Triggering Documentation Debt Crisis
Small consulting firms signed AI governance attestations as procurement formalities in 2024–2025 without building any supporting infrastructure. Now clients — particularly FINMA-supervised financial institutions — are following up with detailed methodology questionnaires and audit requests. The retroactive documentation approach doesn't work well and is obviously post-hoc when inspected.

### 3. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Is a Structural Accountability Gap
Lead boutiques engage sub-consultants who use AI tools the lead consultant knows nothing about. The client holds the lead boutique accountable for the entire deliverable, including subcontracted AI-assisted work. There's no market solution for managing this accountability chain. The lead consultant has no visibility, no documentation, and full exposure.

### 4. Proposal Methodology Defensibility Has Become Existential
AI-assisted consulting work without documentation is a liability in any future client dispute. The question "can I prove what AI tools I used and how?" has no good answer for most small boutiques. The audit trail that would make AI-assisted work defensible doesn't exist.

### 5. nFADP Art. 9 Creates a Paralysis Tax
Consultants are avoiding AI-adjacent work they could legitimately do because they can't assess the exposure. The personal liability (up to CHF 250,000 for executives) makes uncertainty costly. The profiling definitions catch consulting work in HR, financial advisory, and operational optimization — domains where small boutiques routinely operate.

### 6. The Geneva Label Is a Dual Signal
Being Geneva-based triggers higher scrutiny from clients and carriers. The label signals competence and regulatory sophistication — but it also means clients hold you to a higher standard in AI governance attestation. There's no advantage to playing down the Geneva positioning; the scrutiny is coming regardless.

## Ready-to-Use Language

### For the Documentation Problem
*"You signed an attestation to something you can't prove. The audit trail that would make your AI-assisted work defensible doesn't exist. That's the problem."*

### For the Carrier Problem
*"Your carrier moved from 'do you use AI?' to 'document your AI methodology' in one renewal cycle. The next one will ask for evidence. If you can't produce it, your coverage gets priced as an unknown risk."*

### For the Sub-Consultant Problem
*"You engaged a sub-consultant who used AI tools you know nothing about. Your client holds you accountable for the entire deliverable. The delegation happened; the accountability didn't delegate with it."*

### For the nFADP Problem
*"The fine is personal — CHF 250,000, not to the firm, to you. The question isn't whether you're doing something wrong. The question is whether you can show you thought about it carefully and handled it properly."*

### For the Client Questionnaire Problem
*"The client questionnaire was the first request. The audit follow-up is the second. When they ask for your methodology documentation, what are you going to show them?"*

## Net-New Findings (Distinct from C214–C300)

1. **Carrier attestation is the primary forcing function** — insurance renewal is creating more urgency than regulatory deadlines for small boutiques
2. **Client AI governance questionnaires driven by FINMA Guidance 08/2024** — supervised financial institutions are required to ensure vendor compliance, cascading AI governance demands to consulting vendors
3. **Sub-consultant AI delegation accountability gap** — structural problem in multi-party consulting arrangements with no market solution
4. **Documentation debt is the structural problem** — firms signed attestations without any underlying methodology infrastructure
5. **nFADP Art 9 creates a paralysis tax** — uncertainty about profiling definitions is causing consultants to avoid legitimate AI-adjacent work
6. **Geneva label increases scrutiny** — being Geneva-based triggers higher client and carrier expectations in AI governance attestation

## What DigitalCoa.ch Should Do With This

### Messaging Emphasis
Lead with the carrier problem — it's the most immediate, most viscerally felt. Frame AI governance documentation as insurance maintenance, not compliance overhead. The message: "You can handle this before the renewal questionnaire arrives, or you can handle it under pressure when it does."

### Service Design
A lean documentation practice for small consulting boutiques — not a full GRC platform, not a legal engagement, but a practical methodology documentation system they can actually use. Components: AI use policy, per-engagement methodology format, client disclosure approach, sub-consultant delegation protocol, carrier response package.

### Positioning
Geneva-local, consulting-practice-specific, bilingual (EN/FR). The competitive gap is clear: enterprise firms are too expensive and too heavy, legal counsel is reactive, generic Swiss compliance consultancies aren't consulting-practice-specific. A lean, boutique, Geneva-based AI governance advisory for small consulting firms doesn't exist in the market.
