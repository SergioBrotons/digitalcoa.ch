# 01 — ICP: Brotons.net Audience

## Target Segment: Brotons.net Readers

### Audience Profile
- **Primary:** Geneva executives, decision-makers, professionals in law/finance/consulting/commodity trading
- **Secondary:** International executives with Switzerland/Geneva interest
- **Language:** EN primary (70%), FR secondary (30%)
- **Reading behavior:** Thought leadership consumers; prefer long-form, analytical, Geneva-specific content
- **Status:** Brotons.net serves as the intellectual authority hub; Substack/LinkedIn serve as distribution channels

### Reader Decision-Making Unit
| Reader Type | Profile | Content Preferences |
|-------------|---------|---------------------|
| Geneva Executive | CEO/Partner/C-level at Geneva company | Strategic analysis, Geneva market intelligence, leadership thinking |
| Geneva Professional | Senior manager, lawyer, banker, consultant | Industry analysis, regulatory change, professional development |
| International Executive | CEO/strategy with CH interest | Switzerland positioning, Geneva as hub, cross-border intelligence |
| Geneva Academic/Policy | IHEID, UNOG, policy professional | Geneva institutional analysis, IO/UN developments, policy intelligence |

### Reader Pain Points (C125 Update)
1. **Content discovery and routing** — finding relevant Brotons.net content across platforms; Substack vs. website navigation confusion
2. **Language preferences** — EN vs. FR content availability; consistent voice across languages
3. **Content freshness** — knowing when new content is published; not missing relevant pieces
4. **Depth vs. brevity** — some readers want quick takeaways; others want deep analysis; need both formats
5. **Geneva-specific intelligence** — seeking Geneva-specific content not available from generic business publications
6. **Trust and credibility** — confirming Brotons.net content is reliable, non-AI-cliché, intellectually honest

## Target Segment: LinkedIn Newsletter Audience

### Audience Profile
- **Primary:** Professional network connections; LinkedIn newsletter subscribers
- **Secondary:** Brotons.net readers who discovered content via LinkedIn
- **Language:** Predominantly EN
- **Reading behavior:** Mobile-first, shorter attention span, newsletter format

### Subscriber Pain Points (C125 Update)
1. **Email overload** — too many newsletters; need compelling reason to open Brotons.net newsletter
2. **Algorithm dependency** — LinkedIn algorithm changes affecting content visibility; distribution uncertainty
3. **Consistent engagement** — staying top-of-mind without over-communicating
4. **Cross-platform awareness** — knowing Brotons.net has more content beyond LinkedIn newsletter
5. **Bilingual content** — FR content not reaching EN-dominant LinkedIn audience
6. **Content repurposing quality** — social posts derived from long-form pieces often feel shallow

### Partnership Value Proposition
- Content partnership: Brotons.net content aligned with institutional calendar

### Partnership Barriers
