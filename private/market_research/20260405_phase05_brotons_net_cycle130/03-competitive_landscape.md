# 03 — Competitive Landscape
## Brotons.net

**Content Competitors:**
- Geneva-based media: Le Temps, Bilan, Le Matin (surface-level)
- Big 4 thought leadership: PWC Insights, Deloitte TPR, KPMG Thought Leadership (product-driven)
- LinkedIn thought leaders: Geneva executives, consultants (variable quality, inconsistent)
- Academic: Graduate Institute (IHEID) publications (academic tone)
- Industry associations: FINMA, SFAMA, CCIG publications (institutional voice)
- International media: FT, WSJ, Bloomberg (not Geneva-specific)

**Differentiation:**
- Interpretive depth: Not raw information, not academic — strategic synthesis
- Geneva institutional specificity: Local context, contacts, dynamics
- Independent voice: No audit conflict, no product agenda
- EN+FR bilingual value: Rare in Geneva English-language content
- Return reader quality: Trust-based relationship, not volume

**Positioning:**
- "The Geneva lens for strategic signal" — intellectual hub for Geneva executives
- Owned platform: Independent of Substack/LinkedIn algorithm changes
- Quality compounding: Trust accrues over time
- Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch pathway: Unduplicated advantage

**Channel Strategy:**
- Newsletter: Primary delivery, direct relationship
- LinkedIn: Discovery + engagement, secondary
- Search: Article-level, passive discovery
- Substack: Syndication, audience expansion (NOT primary)
- EN+FR bilingual: Swiss market differentiation

**Editorial Cadence:**
- 2-4 pieces/month: Sustainable quality
- Institutional calendar alignment: Timing relevance
- Pillar clusters: 4-6 core topics, deep authority
- Quality over quantity: Trust preservation
