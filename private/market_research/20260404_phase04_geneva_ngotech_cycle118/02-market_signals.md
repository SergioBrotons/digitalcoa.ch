# 02 — Market Signals: Phase 04 Geneva NGO/Tech (C118)

## Market Signals (Geneva NGO/Tech, April 2026)

### Signal 1: Geneva Venture Debt and Late-Stage Funding Gap
- Seed funding strong in Geneva; Series A/B remains constrained
- Swiss venture debt providers (LOMAR, Schroder) active but limited
- Growth stage companies (CHF 5M-30M ARR) struggling to find late-stage capital
- **Signal:** Advisory demand for fundraising preparation, investor positioning, growth financing structure
- **Source:** Swiss Venture Club data, SECA statistics, canton de Genève funding reports

### Signal 2: WIPO IP Services — Geneva-Based Intellectual Property Advisory
- WIPO headquarters in Geneva; full range of IP registration and dispute services
- Swiss companies underutilizing WIPO trademark registration systems
- PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) filings from Geneva companies growing
- **Signal:** WIPO-adjacent IP advisory — trademark strategy, PCT filing, IP portfolio management
- **Source:** WIPO annual reports, Swiss IP statistics, IPO analytics

### Signal 3: Humanitarian Logistics — Geneva as UN Logistics Hub
- UNOG, WFP, ICRC, UNICEF supply chain operations based/routed through Geneva
- Switzerland contributing to humanitarian logistics capacity (SDC, logistics bureau)
- Geneva作为 humanitarian logistics training center; professional education offerings growing
- **Signal:** Humanitarian logistics advisory — supply chain optimization, customs facilitation, last-mile delivery

### Signal 4: Voluntary Carbon Market Developments
- VCM industry evolving post-2023 integrity concerns; IC-VCM Core Carbon Principles established
- Geneva-based actors: South Pole, Climate Focus, Carbon Trust have Geneva presence
- Article 6 implementation under Paris Agreement creating new carbon market rules
- **Signal:** Carbon market advisory — VCM strategy, Article 6 alignment, carbon credit procurement
- **Source:** IC-VCM publications, State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO), Swiss carbon market actors

### Signal 5: Cybersecurity Capacity Building — UN/IO Demand
- UN agencies and IO partners increasingly targeted by cyber attacks
- Switzerland contributing to global cybersecurity capacity building via SDC, NCSC
- Geneva cybersecurity conference (cyd) and ecosystem growing
- **Signal:** Cybersecurity advisory for Geneva-based IO and humanitarian organizations
- **Source:** UNOG IT reports, NCSC international cooperation reports, media coverage

### Signal 6: Quantum Technologies — Emerging Geneva Niche
- Geneva: CERN quantum computing research; ID Quantique (quantum-safe cryptography) Geneva-based
- Swiss National Fund supporting quantum research at CERN and EPFL
- Emerging commercial applications: quantum-safe encryption, quantum sensing
- **Signal:** Quantum readiness advisory — post-quantum cryptography migration for Geneva financial sector
- **Source:** CERN quantum initiatives, ID Quantique public filings, Swiss quantum strategy

## Synthesis
Geneva NGO/Tech demand driven by:
1. Late-stage funding gap → fundraising advisory
2. WIPO presence → IP strategy
3. Humanitarian logistics → supply chain advisory
4. Carbon markets → climate advisory
5. Cybersecurity → IO demand
6. Quantum → emerging niche
