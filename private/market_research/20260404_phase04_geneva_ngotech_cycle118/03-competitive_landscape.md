# 03 — Competitive Landscape: Phase 04 Geneva NGO/Tech (C118)

## Competitive Set: Geneva NGO/Tech Advisory

### Tier 1 — International Consultancies (Geneva offices)
**KPMG, Deloitte, PwC, Accenture (public sector practices)**

- UN/IO engagement; humanitarian sector experience
- Weakness: generic frameworks, high cost, slow
- **Threat:** Large IO procurement preferences for brand-name firms

### Tier 2 — Geneva and Swiss Specialists
**Local consultancies, EPFL spin-offs, Geneva-based NGOs with advisory arms**

- Geneva-specific; language capability; relationships with cantonal government
- Weakness: limited international reach, narrow scale
- **Opportunity:** Quantum readiness and cybersecurity advisory for financial sector

### Tier 3 — Impact Investors and VC Networks
**Swiss Impact Hub, CV VC, serial entrepreneurs**

- Network effects; deal flow
- Weakness: focused on investment, not advisory services

## Underserved Niches (Differentiation Opportunities)
1. **Quantum readiness for Geneva financial sector** — nascent, few advisors
2. **WIPO IP strategy for Swiss startups** — underdeveloped market
3. **Voluntary carbon market advisory (Article 6)** — growing, few specialists
4. **Humanitarian logistics optimization** — niche, relationship-driven
5. **Cybersecurity for Geneva IO/NGOs** — high need, underserved
6. **Venture fundraising advisory (late-stage)** — growing demand, limited supply

## Positioning Recommendation
**"The Geneva advisor for technology ventures and international organizations"**
- Sectors: tech startups, climate tech, quantum, IO/humanitarian
- Services: regulatory navigation, fundraising preparation, IP strategy, cybersecurity
- Differentiator: Geneva depth + international network
