# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 01 Geneva Legal

## Research Question
What is the addressable market for enterprise legal management (ELM) advisory targeting large Geneva law firms and in-house legal departments at financial institutions?

## Key Findings (C97 — NET-NEW)

### Finding 1: 2026 is an exceptional regulatory year for Geneva financial legal market
Six significant regulatory changes took effect or were announced in early 2026 alone:
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026: Crypto custody supervisory expectations (January 2026)
- FINMA Circular 2026/1: Nature-related financial risks (January 2026)
- Operational resilience requirements fully in force (January 2026)
- BFSA UK-Switzerland agreement in force (January 2026)
- AMLA revision with new "advisor" category (H2 2026)
- LETA beneficial ownership federal register (H2 2026)

**Implication:** Geneva GCs and legal departments face their highest regulatory workload since FinSA/FinIA (2020). This creates a rare advisory window.

### Finding 2: AI in legal practice has moved from experimental to production
Geneva law firms are deploying AI for contract review, risk assessment, document summarization, and knowledge management. The demand for "legal engineers" (hybrid legal/tech professionals) is rising.

**Implication:** Legal operations advisory now includes AI governance, vendor risk assessment, and AI-related compliance documentation — a new service line.

### Finding 3: AMLA revision creates regulated "advisor" category affecting Geneva legal firms
Legal advisors who participate in financial transactions related to real estate, entity creation/management/domiciliation become regulated entities requiring SRO affiliation, due diligence procedures, and documentation.

**Implication:** Geneva law firms with corporate, M&A, real estate, and private client practices face new regulatory burden. Advisory opportunity for AMLA compliance gap assessment and SRO affiliation guidance.

### Finding 4: LETA beneficial ownership register creates significant new workload
Swiss companies must identify, verify, document, and report beneficial owners to a federal register by H2 2026. Corporate law practices, M&A teams, and private client advisors all affected.

**Implication:** New billable work for law firms; opportunity for DigitalCoa to position beneficial ownership advisory as part of broader corporate transparency service offering.

### Finding 5: Outside counsel management continues to evolve toward strategic partnerships
Geneva legal departments shifting from reactive billing negotiations to proactive performance scorecards, rate governance, and staffing controls. ALSPs increasingly used.

**Implication:** Advisory opportunity for outside counsel guideline frameworks, rate governance tools, and legal operations maturity assessments.

### Finding 6: Competitive positioning gap persists — first-mover opportunity on 2026 regulatory content
Most Geneva legal advisors have not yet published interpretative content on the 2026 FINMA regulatory package. Borel & Barbey is leading thought leadership on regulatory updates. KPMG Law active on legal transformation.

**Implication:** DigitalCoa can establish itself as the authoritative English-language voice on FINMA 2026 regulatory implications for legal operations before the market gets crowded.
