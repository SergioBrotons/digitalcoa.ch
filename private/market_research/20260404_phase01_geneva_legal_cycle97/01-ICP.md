# 01 — ICP: Geneva Large/ELM Legal Firms

## Target Segment: Large Law & Enterprise Legal Management (ELM)

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** 50+ attorneys, typically 100-500+
- **Revenue:** CHF 50M–500M+ annual
- **Locations:** Geneva primary, Zurich secondary, possible Swiss romande presence
- **Practice Areas:** Corporate/M&A, banking & finance, litigation, IP, tax (full-service or specialized)
- **Status:** Often Swiss member of international networks (Allen & Overy, Latham, Baker McKenzie affiliates)

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| General Counsel / Managing Partner | C-suite or senior equity partner | Portfolio visibility, cost containment, outside counsel management |
| COO / Finance Director | Non-lawyer executive | Invoice validation, matter budgeting, e-billing compliance |
| IT / Knowledge Management | Senior IT manager or CIO | Document management, IP protection, AI tool integration |
| Practice Group Leaders | Senior partners | Matter profitability, staffing efficiency, client reporting |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Lenz & Staehelin (Geneva HQ, 300+ lawyers)
- Pestalozzi Attorneys at Law (Geneva, international network)
- Vischer AG (Geneva/Zurich, 80+ lawyers)
- Baker McKenzie (Geneva affiliate)
- Walder Wyss (Geneva office, 100+ lawyers)
- Lalive, Schellenberg Wittmer, Borel & Barbey, Froriep, MLL Legal SA, CMS Switzerland

### Pain Points (Validated + Updated C97)
1. **Outside counsel cost opacity** — invoice auditing is manual, error-prone
2. **Matter overflow** — spikes in M&A or litigation create staffing gaps
3. **Regulatory change velocity** — FINMA/SECO compliance demands faster turnaround
4. **Document security** — client confidentiality in AI tool adoption
5. **E-billing standardization** — heterogeneous invoice formats from multiple firms
6. **Knowledge retention** — senior partner departure risk
7. **NEW — Crypto custody advisory** — FINMA Guidance 01/2026 creates new compliance workload for institutions offering crypto services
8. **NEW — AMLA revision** — new regulated "advisor" category requires SRO affiliation for legal advisors in financial transactions

### Buying Triggers (Updated C97)
- New GC appointment (new broom)
- Cost audit revealing >15% outside counsel overspend
- Merger or office expansion
- Regulatory examination findings
- Competitor adoption of legal tech (FOMO)
- **NEW — LETA beneficial ownership register** — companies need legal support to identify/report beneficial owners (H2 2026)
- **NEW — Operational resilience full enforcement** — all banks must demonstrate compliance (as of January 2026)

### Buying Barriers (Updated C97)
- Partner autonomy culture resists transparency tools
- Client confidentiality concerns with cloud/AI vendors
- Existing e-billing vendor lock-in (Symfact, AP Data Services)
- IT procurement bureaucracy (long sales cycles: 6-18 months)
- **NEW — AMLA compliance cost** — new SRO affiliation and due diligence burden for smaller advisors

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva; English widely spoken in international firms
- German useful for Zurich-connected roles
- Direct, reserved relationship style — no hard sell
- Formal titles and credentials matter
