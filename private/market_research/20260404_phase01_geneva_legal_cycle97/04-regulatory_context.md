# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal Sector

## Swiss Legal & Compliance Framework

### Key Regulators
| Regulator | Jurisdiction | Relevance to Legal Firms |
|---|---|---|
| FINMA | Swiss financial markets | Banks, insurance, asset managers — most Geneva legal clients |
| EDÖB (FDPIC) | Data protection (Swiss GDPR equivalent) | Document handling, client confidentiality, AI tools |
| SECO | Economic affairs | M&A review, sanctions, foreign investment |
| Swiss Bar Association (SAV/FSA) | Legal profession | Attorney conduct rules, billing, advertising |
| Geneva Bar Association (Ordre des Avocats Genevois) | Geneva-specific | Local practice rules, mandatory insurance |

### 2026 Regulatory Developments (NEW — C97 Priority)

#### FINMA Guidance 01/2026 — Crypto Custody (12 January 2026)
- **What:** Supervisory expectations for custody of crypto-based assets
- **Who:** Banks, securities firms, managers of collective assets, portfolio managers offering crypto custody/trading/investment
- **Key risks identified:** Operational DLT risks, cyber-attacks, private key protection, third-party custodian dependency, cross-border bankruptcy exposure
- **Legal implication:** Swiss institutions must ensure crypto assets are segregable custody assets with bankruptcy protection — requires legal documentation, contractual frameworks, due diligence on custodians
- **Advisory opportunity:** Crypto custody legal risk assessment, third-party custodian contractual review, bankruptcy remoteness structuring

#### FINMA Circular 2026/1 — Nature-Related Financial Risks (1 January 2026)
- **What:** Systematic integration of climate and environmental risks into governance and risk management
- **Who:** Banks and insurance companies (phased by prudential category)
- **Legal implication:** New ESG due diligence requirements, climate risk disclosure documentation, potential increase in environmental litigation
- **Advisory opportunity:** ESG legal risk framework, climate risk legal assessment methodology, DORA-adjacent resilience frameworks

#### Operational Resilience — Fully In Force (1 January 2026)
- **What:** All transitional periods for FINMA Circular 23/01 concluded
- **Who:** All FINMA-supervised institutions
- **Legal implication:** Full compliance required; legal functions must support operational resilience documentation, third-party risk management (including legal vendors)
- **Advisory opportunity:** Legal function operational resilience maturity assessment, legal vendor risk framework

#### AMLA Revision — New "Advisor" Category (H2 2026)
- **What:** Legal advisors who participate in financial transactions related to real estate, creation/management/domiciliation of non-operational legal entities become regulated entities
- **Who:** Attorneys, legal consultants, wealth managers acting in scope transactions
- **Requirements:** Due diligence, documentation, SRO (self-regulatory organization) affiliation
- **Legal implication:** Significant compliance burden for Geneva law firms advising on M&A, real estate, trusts, corporate domiciliation
- **Advisory opportunity:** AMLA compliance gap assessment, SRO affiliation guidance, due diligence procedure design

#### LETA — Beneficial Ownership Register (H2 2026)
- **What:** Federal register of beneficial owners of Swiss legal entities
- **Who:** Unlisted public limited companies, LLCs, SICAV, SICAF, SCmPC, foreign entities with Swiss presence
- **Requirements:** Identify, verify, document, report beneficial owners within strict deadlines
- **Legal implication:** Substantial new workload for corporate law practices, M&A due diligence, private client teams
- **Advisory opportunity:** LETA compliance framework, beneficial ownership identification process, corporate transparency advisory

#### BFSA — UK-Switzerland Financial Services Agreement (1 January 2026)
- **What:** Mutual recognition regime for cross-border financial services between Switzerland and UK
- **Legal implication:** Cross-border service delivery requires understanding of both Swiss and UK regulatory frameworks; new contractual arrangements needed
- **Advisory opportunity:** BFSA compliance advisory, cross-border legal framework design

### AI-Specific Regulation
- **Switzerland:** No hard AI law. Guidance-based via algorithms and AI report (2023)
- **ISO 42001:** AI management system standard — emerging as de facto certification
- **EU AI Act implications:** Swiss firms with EU operations may need to comply voluntarily
- **FINMA AI guidance:** Banks must disclose AI model risk — affects legal documentation needs
- **Geneva AI adoption:** Moving from experimental to production use in contract review, risk assessment, document summarization

### DORA / Operational Resilience
- EU directive being adapted in Switzerland
- Third-party risk (including legal vendors) = legal operations framework need
- Legal function as both subject and contributor to operational resilience
