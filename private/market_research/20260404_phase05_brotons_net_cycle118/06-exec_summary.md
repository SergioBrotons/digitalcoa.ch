# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 05 Brotons.net (C118)

## Research Question
How should Brotons.net grow reach, engagement, and conversion to advisory client relationships?

## C118 Key Findings

### Finding 1: Content Repurposing — Multi-Format Distribution (NET-NEW C118)
- Long-form essays are core asset; repurposing multiplies reach
- LinkedIn: short commentary + article links
- Podcast: audio version of essays (executive commute habit)
- **Implication:** Systematic repurposing workflow; each essay → 3-5 content pieces across platforms

### Finding 2: Newsletter Growth — Quality Over Quantity (NET-NEW C118)
- Geneva professional audience values depth and relevance over frequency
- Quality subscriber (GC, executive) > volume of low-engagement subscribers
- Geneva network effect: each quality reader refers peers
- **Implication:** Focus on subscriber quality; optimize for retention, not just acquisition

### Finding 3: Search / SEO Strategy — Long-Tail Geneva Terms (NET-NEW C118)
- Low competition for specific Geneva regulatory/policy terms
- SEO potential: "Geneva financial centre," "Swiss sanctions compliance," "Geneva arbitration"
- Long-form analytical content ranks well; Google AI Overviews favor quality depth
- **Implication:** Systematic SEO; target specific Geneva long-tail terms; optimize existing content

### Finding 4: Geneva Institutional Calendar Alignment (NET-NEW C118)
- Content published before/during relevant Geneva events gets higher engagement
- Key calendar moments: WTO Public Forum (June), UNGA (Sept), Geneva Finance Dialog (Oct)
- Annual Geneva regulatory calendar drives content relevance
- **Implication:** Align editorial calendar with Geneva institutional calendar

### Finding 5: Community Building — Audience Development (NET-NEW C118)
- Private community (small group, high-trust) outperforms public social media for loyalty
- Geneva peer circles: IHEID alumni, SFAMA events, CCIG network
- Small intimate events create stronger loyalty than large conferences
- **Implication:** Build audience community, not just subscriber list; explore private circles

### Finding 6: Monetization Beyond Substack (NET-NEW C118)
- Substack subscription revenue limited; advisory engagements more valuable
- Brotons.net as credibility engine → advisory mandate conversion
- Executive education and workshops: untapped opportunity for Geneva market
- Speaking fees from Geneva institutions: established revenue stream
- **Implication:** Multi-channel monetization; Brotons.net → trust → advisory relationship

## Consolidated Strategic Implications
1. **Platform strategy:** Brotons.net + Substack + LinkedIn + Podcast (systematic repurposing)
2. **Quality focus:** Geneva executive audience; network effect; retention > acquisition
3. **SEO investment:** Long-tail Geneva terms; optimize existing content library
4. **Community:** Private circles; small events; high-trust relationships
5. **Monetization:** Advisory > subscriptions; Brotons.net as credibility engine
