# 03 — Competitive Landscape: Phase 05 Brotons.net (C118)

## Competitive Set: Geneva Thought Leadership Platforms

### Tier 1 — Established Geneva Platforms
** Geneva Finance Dialog, IHEID Policy Briefs, Graduate Institute Insights**

- Strengths: institutional backing, established audience, credibility
- Weakness: academic tone, less personal voice, slower publication
- **Threat:** None — different positioning

### Tier 2 — Individual Practitioners
** LinkedIn thought leaders, Substack writers (Swiss/finance niche)**

- Strengths: personal voice, authentic, engaged audience
- Weakness: inconsistent quality, limited depth, no systematic strategy
- **Opportunity:** Systematic quality + depth = differentiation

### Tier 3 — Media Outlets
** Le Temps, Le Matin, RTS (radio)**

- Strengths: broad reach, journalistic resources
- Weakness: shallow analysis, no follow-up, generic
- **Opportunity:** Depth + Geneva specificity + continuity

## Underserved Positioning (Differentiation Opportunity)
1. **Systematic Geneva regulatory intelligence** — no one doing it consistently with quality
2. **Bilingual EN+FR** — most competitors choose one language
3. **Geneva institutional depth** — specific, not general
4. **Long-form analytical voice** — not news, not opinion — interpretation
5. **Trusted peer publication** — executives trust peer-quality thinking

## Positioning Recommendation
**"Brotons.net: The Geneva intelligence platform for executives who need to understand what's actually happening"**

- Not news (Le Temps does news better)
- Not academic (Graduate Institute does academia better)
- Interpretation + intelligence + context = the gap Brotons.net fills
- Voice: calm, sharp, authoritative, independent
