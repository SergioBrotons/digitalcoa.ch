## Portfolio Architecture

### The Universal Entry Point: nFADP + AI Governance

All four lead magnets share a common architectural logic:

```
Pain Acknowledgment → Regulatory Hook → Decision Framework → CTA
```

**Pain Acknowledgment:** Every SMB professional practice faces the same tension — AI tools are proliferating, staff pressure is real, but the regulatory and liability implications are unclear.

**Regulatory Hook:** nFADP is the universal entry point. It is mandatory, understood, and creates urgency. The question is not whether nFADP applies — it is whether the firm's AI governance meets nFADP standards.

**Decision Framework:** Each lead magnet provides a practical 3-axis framework:
- Axis 1: Data classification and sensitivity (nFADP Art. 5)
- Axis 2: AI system accountability and oversight
- Axis 3: Compliance documentation and evidence

**CTA:** "Ready to assess where you stand? Book a 30-minute AI Readiness Check."

## Content Ladder Strategy

### Stage 1: Awareness — Lead Magnet (Free PDF)
Each lead magnet is a free downloadable decision framework PDF in both EN and FR.

**Purpose:** Establish DigitalCoa.ch as the thought leader in SMB AI governance for each vertical. Capture leads who are researching AI governance questions.

**Distribution Channels:**
- Geneva professional association newsletters (BAR, VFF, FMH, SBO)
- LinkedIn organic (positioned articles citing framework)
- Direct outreach to vertical referral networks
- SEO: "AI governance [vertical] Geneva" keywords

**Lead Capture:** Name, firm, email, vertical, and one optional qualifying question ("What's your biggest AI governance question right now?")

### Stage 2: Consideration — AI Readiness Check (Free/Low-Cost)
30-minute structured conversation using a condensed version of the lead magnet framework.

**Purpose:** Qualify leads, demonstrate expertise, identify urgency, create relationship. No cost; high value.

**CTA:** "Downloaded the framework? Let's walk through it for your specific situation."

### Stage 3: Decision — Advisory Engagement
Phased engagement structure:
- Phase 1: AI Governance Assessment (CHF 8K–15K)
- Phase 2: Implementation Roadmap (CHF 15K–30K)
- Phase 3: Ongoing Advisory Retainer (CHF 2K–5K/month)

## Cross-Sell and Laddering Strategy

### Cross-Sell Triggers
When a client downloads one vertical's lead magnet, they are automatically invited to view the full portfolio:

- **Legal → Finance/WM:** "Your clients in finance face similar AI governance questions — here's our framework for wealth management firms."
- **Finance/WM → Consulting:** "The same nFADP + AI logic applies to consulting firms — here's the consulting-specific version."
- **Consulting → Healthcare:** "Healthcare practices have the most complex AI governance requirements — but the same framework applies."
- **Healthcare → Legal:** "Legal firms were actually among the first to ask these questions — here's our framework for law practices."

### ICP Expansion Path
One vertical engagement creates natural cross-sell opportunities:
- Law firms → their WM clients
- WM firms → their business owner clients
- Consultants → their client companies
- Healthcare practices → their professional network

## Portfolio Coherence: Brand Consistency

### Shared Visual Identity
All four lead magnets should share:
- Consistent color palette and typography
- Same framework architecture (3 axes)
- Same CTA structure
- Same document format (8–12 pages)
- Both EN and FR versions

### Messaging Discipline
Each lead magnet adapts the core positioning to vertical-specific language:
- **Legal:** Professional liability, client confidentiality, BAR compliance
- **Finance/WM:** FINMA obligations, client asset data, AMLA considerations
- **Consulting:** ISO 42001, project confidentiality, client trust
- **Healthcare:** Patient data (Art. 5(c)), Swissmedic, clinical liability

The underlying message is identical: "AI governance is a competitive advantage, not just a compliance burden."
