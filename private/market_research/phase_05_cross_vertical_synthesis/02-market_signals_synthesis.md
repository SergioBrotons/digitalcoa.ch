## Cross-Vertical Pattern Analysis

### Pattern 1: AI Adoption Gap Is Structural, Not Temporary
Across all four verticals, the pattern is identical: principals are aware of AI tools, have experimented with individual tools (ChatGPT, Copilot, Doctolib), but have not made systematic AI governance decisions. The gap is not awareness — it is strategic framework.

### Pattern 2: nFADP Is the Universal Wake-Up Call
In every vertical, nFADP is the regulatory event that converts general AI curiosity into active concern. nFADP creates a concrete compliance obligation that forces principals to ask: "Are we handling data correctly in our AI tools?"

### Pattern 3: Peer Adoption Is the Strongest Signal
Across all verticals, seeing a peer practice/firm successfully navigate AI governance is more powerful than any marketing message. This creates a network-effect opportunity: early clients become referral sources.

### Pattern 4: Staffing Shortage Accelerates AI Interest
The Swiss labor market tightness — particularly acute in Geneva for medical assistants, legal secretaries, and administrative staff — creates operational urgency that outweighs AI caution.

### Pattern 5: No Boutique Advisor Has Claimed This Space
The market signal across all four verticals is identical: no Geneva-based boutique advisory firm has positioned itself as the trusted AI governance advisor for SMB professional practices. This is the clearest market signal of all.

## Market Entry Window Assessment

**Window Status:** OPEN — and closing within 12–18 months

**Rationale:**
- Regulatory frameworks are defined enough to advise on, not so mature that commoditization has occurred
- No boutique competitor has established position in Geneva
- AI tool adoption is accelerating, creating growing demand for governance guidance
- Early-mover advantage in trust-based advisory is durable (not easily reversed by later entrants)

**Risk of Waiting:**
- First-mover advantage erodes as other advisors notice the gap
- nFADP enforcement cases create panic-buying of cheapest compliance solutions
- AI tool vendors may add advisory layers, capturing governance narrative
