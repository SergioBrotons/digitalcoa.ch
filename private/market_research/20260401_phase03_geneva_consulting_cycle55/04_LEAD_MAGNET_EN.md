## What GPAI Actually Means for Small Consulting Firms

The Global Partnership on AI (GPAI) Code of Practice was published July 10, 2025. High-risk AI obligations become operative August 2, 2026 — approximately 4 months from now.

For small consulting firms, "high-risk" AI includes:
- AI systems used to analyze clients or generate client deliverables
- AI systems that make or materially influence business decisions
- AI agents operating with moderate to high autonomy in client-facing workflows

**August 2, 2026 is not a preparation deadline. It is an operative date. High-risk obligations are already running.**

## The Three-Dimensional Exposure

Small consulting firms face three overlapping exposures simultaneously:

### Layer 1: GPAI Obligations (Arriving August 2, 2026)

- Human oversight requirements for high-risk AI
- Transparency obligations toward clients on AI use
- Documentation and record-keeping for AI systems
- Data quality and governance for AI training and deployment
- Bias monitoring and non-discrimination obligations

### Layer 2: Professional Standard of Care (SBA Guidelines — June 2024)

Swiss Bar Association Guidelines apply to law firms; the professional standard of care for consulting firms follows analogous logic. Using AI in client work without adequate governance establishes a breach of professional duty.

### Layer 3: Agentic AI Liability (Immediate)

Agentic AI systems — which can autonomously plan, execute, and adapt — are entering consulting workflows. The liability rule is absolute: the firm is always responsible for AI decisions, regardless of autonomy level.

> "It wasn't me, it was my AI" is not a defense.

## The "Envelope vs. Letter" Framework for Consulting

The distinction that determines both risk and opportunity:

**Envelope (Safe to Automate):**
- Client intake and onboarding
- Scheduling and calendar management
- Report formatting and document preparation
- Research synthesis and literature reviews
- Billing and invoice preparation
- Internal administrative workflows

**Letter (Higher Risk — Requires Governance):**
- Client strategy analysis and recommendations
- Market intelligence interpretation
- Deliverable drafting that constitutes advice
- AI systems with access to client confidential data
- Any AI operating at the agentic (autonomous execution) level

**The efficiency opportunity:** 8–14 hours per week are recoverable through safe automation (envelope) in a typical small consulting firm. This is both efficiency and risk mitigation — automating the envelope removes the highest-risk administrative AI usage while recovering partner capacity.

## What the First Decision Actually Is

Most consulting firms start with: "Should we use AI?"

The correct first decision is: **"What decisions is the AI we're already using actually making?"**

This is the Decision 0 audit — it comes before any GPAI preparation, before any policy drafting, before any vendor selection.

The AI Decision Audit answers:
- Which AI tools are your consultants actually using in client work?
- Are any of those AI systems operating at the agentic level?
- Does usage involve client confidential data processed outside Switzerland?
- Are GPAI high-risk criteria triggered by any current AI usage?
- Is there meaningful human oversight on AI-assisted client deliverables?

**Most firms discover AI usage they didn't know about. This is the real risk. And GPAI August 2, 2026 makes it a compliance issue.**
