## For DigitalCoa.ch (Executive / Advisory Positioning)

### Angle 1: "The SECO Enforcement Wave Geneva Companies Are Not Ready For"
- **Hook:** 68 penalty proceedings, 24 final fines, 9 convictions since September 2024 — Swiss sanctions enforcement is accelerating and most Geneva companies are operating on outdated assumptions
- **Core argument:** enforcement is real, penalties are up to CHF 540,000/5 years imprisonment for intentional violations; voluntary self-disclosure has no formal statutory basis but works in practice; the gap between Swiss-only and EU-subject companies creates hidden risk
- **CTA:** sanctions compliance gap assessment / enforcement readiness review
- **Tone:** calm, authoritative, executive-grade

### Angle 2: "FINMA Now Has Sanctions Teeth: The AML Integration Shift"
- **Hook:** Switzerland has embedded sanctions compliance into AML law — FINMA is now a co-enforcer alongside SECO, with power to revoke licenses and confiscate profits
- **Core argument:** financial institutions need comprehensive risk assessments, client beneficial ownership updates, internal controls, and staff training; non-compliance means enforcement proceedings, not just fines
- **CTA:** AML-sanctions compliance framework audit
- **Tone:** direct, compliance-focused, time-sensitive

### Angle 3: "The A7A5 Stablecoin Ban: Switzerland's First Crypto Sanctions Case"
- **Hook:** Switzerland banned a ruble-backed stablecoin — Geneva's crypto exchanges, fintech custody providers, and commodity traders using crypto settlement now face new compliance obligations
- **Core argument:** this is a preview of how crypto-asset sanctions will expand; crypto-wallets, accounts, and custody services are in scope; advisory opportunity is immediate
- **CTA:** crypto compliance review
- **Tone:** technical, precise, forward-looking

## For Brotons.net (Thought Leadership / Interpretive)

### Angle 4: "Switzerland's Sanctions Identity Crisis: Alignment Without Membership"
- **Hook:** Switzerland is not legally bound to EU sanctions, yet has consistently mirrored them — but deviations are real and growing. This reveals something structural about Swiss regulatory identity.
- **Core argument:** interpretive essay on the Swiss autonomous sanctions regime, the political cost of alignment vs. divergence, and what the REPO Task Force refusal really means for Switzerland's international position
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — analytical, independent, substantive

### Angle 5: "The Supply Chain Traceability Requirement: How SECO's Healthcare Exemption Became a Trap"
- **Hook:** Geneva pharmaceutical traders and medical device exporters believe they are exempt from Russia sanctions — but SECO's demand for full supply chain traceability is blocking most exemptions
- **Core argument:** long-form piece on the gap between legal exemption text and SECO's enforcement interpretation; the compliance infrastructure most companies don't have
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — investigative, granular, valuable

### Angle 6: "The Circumvention Century: How Global Trade Law Is Being Rewritten"
- **Hook:** EU 15th-17th packages and Switzerland's parallel measures all focus on circumvention — this is not a temporary measure but a structural shift in how sanctions law operates globally
- **Core argument:** interpretive piece on the shift from "did you trade with Russia?" to "could your supply chain be used to circumvent sanctions?" — the burden of proof has changed
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — broad, strategic, intellectually rigorous
