# 04 — Cross-Vertical Compliance Patterns and Shared Regulatory Pressures

## Phase 05 — C302 | Cross-Vertical Synthesis

## Universal Compliance Pressures

### nFADP Article 63 — The Universal Responsible Person Trigger

Article 63 of Switzerland's Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) creates personal liability for the designated "responsible person." This applies across all four verticals.

**The pattern is identical:**
- The accountable person (senior partner, managing director, managing consultant, physician) is personally named as the responsible person
- Personal assets are at risk, not just institutional liability
- The responsible person must demonstrate oversight, controls, and accountability documentation
- If an incident occurs, the documentation is the evidence of due diligence

**Cross-vertical manifestation:**

| Vertical | Responsible Person | Personal Stake |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Managing partner | Bar admission + personal liability |
| Finance | Managing director | Personal assets + FINMA supervision |
| Consulting | Managing director | Personal assets + client liability |
| Healthcare | Responsible physician | FMH license + personal assets |

**The compliance pressure:** The ICP must document their responsible-person oversight in a way that is defensible if an incident occurs. The documentation gap is not just a compliance gap — it is a personal liability exposure.

---

### nFADP Article 5(c) — Universal Data Protection Compliance

Article 5(c) requires that personal data processing be based on a legal basis — consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, or legitimate interests. AI tool use involving personal data requires this foundation.

**Cross-vertical manifestation:**

- **Legal:** Client communications processed through AI tools — attorney-client privilege adds sensitivity
- **Finance:** Client financial data processed through AI tools — highest sensitivity personal data category
- **Consulting:** Client business strategy data processed through AI tools — commercially sensitive
- **Healthcare:** Patient health data processed through AI tools — most sensitive data category under nFADP

**The compliance pressure:** The ICP must document the legal basis for each AI tool's access to personal data. The documentation gap is universal and acute.

---

### Carrier Attestation — Universal Forcing Function

The malpractice or professional liability carrier has shifted from passive insurer to active compliance monitor. Carrier AI attestation forms are arriving across all four verticals with evidence requirements the ICP cannot meet.

**Cross-vertical manifestation:**

- **Legal:** Carrier PI attestation form v4 — asks for "last three authorization records"
- **Finance:** Carrier professional liability form v4 — asks for AI tool inventory + authorization records
- **Consulting:** Carrier professional liability form — asks for AI governance documentation
- **Healthcare:** Malpractice carrier AI attestation form — asks for FMH human oversight documentation

**The compliance pressure:** The carrier is functioning as a de facto enforcement agent. The ICP who cannot produce the required evidence faces coverage implications at policy renewal.

---

### Institutional Client Due Diligence — Universal in Finance and Consulting

Corporate clients, family offices, endowments, and institutional investors are sending AI governance questionnaires as part of standard vendor due diligence. This is not limited to finance — consulting firms are equally affected.

**Cross-vertical manifestation:**

- **Finance:** Institutional clients (pension funds, family offices, endowments) send detailed AI governance questionnaires as RFP criterion
- **Consulting:** Corporate clients and institutional investors send AI governance questionnaires with2–4 week deadlines

**The compliance pressure:** A weak or incomplete response disqualifies the firm from the mandate. The competitive consequence is immediate and economic.

## Vertical-Specific Regulatory Pressures

### Legal — SBA Professional Accountability

The Swiss Bar Association (SBA) has stated that AI use in legal practice requires adequate supervision. The SBA has not defined what "adequate supervision" means for a small firm context.

**The compliance pressure:** The senior attorney who cannot independently verify AI output cannot demonstrate adequate supervision. The accountability gap is also a professional licensing exposure.

---

### Finance — FINMA 08/2024 and Supervisory Review

FINMA published supervisory guidance on AI use in financial services (FINMA 08/2024). The supervisory review process includes AI governance as a scored criterion.

**The compliance pressure:** Geneva wealth management firms face FINMA supervisory review with no practical guidance on what AI governance documentation looks like at boutique scale.

---

### Healthcare — FMH Human Oversight + Swissmedic Dual Layer

FMH has stated that human oversight of AI is required. FMH has not defined what this means in practice. Swissmedic has registered medical devices under the In vitro diagnostic medical devices Ordinance (IVDO). The dual-layer compliance question — which framework applies when — is unanswered for small practices.

**The compliance pressure:** The physician faces two simultaneous regulatory layers with no guidance on which applies in their specific situation. Swissdamed July 1, 2026 makes this paralysis concrete and urgent.

---

## Shared Regulatory Pattern: Acknowledgment Without Operational Definition

A consistent pattern across all verticals: the regulatory body has acknowledged the problem (AI governance is required) without providing an operational definition (what does compliance look like in practice for a small firm).

| Regulatory Body | Statement | Operational Gap |
|---|---|---|
| SBA | AI supervision required | No definition of "adequate supervision" for small firms |
| FINMA | AI governance expected | No practical guidance for boutique wealth managers |
| FMH | Human oversight required | No definition for small practice context |
| Swissmedic | Medical device registration required | No guidance on AI tool classification for small practices |
| FDPIC | AI data protection guidance | No practical small-firm implementation guidance |

**The compliance pressure:** The ICP is required to comply with a standard that has not been defined. This creates a documentation paradox — the ICP cannot demonstrate compliance because no one has defined what compliance looks like.

---

## The Universal Documentation Paradox

**The pattern:**
1. Regulatory body acknowledges AI governance requirement
2. Regulatory body does not define operational compliance standard
3. Carrier or client demands evidence of compliance
4. ICP cannot produce evidence because compliance pathway is undefined
5. ICP faces personal liability exposure for non-compliance they cannot operationalize

**The resolution:** The ICP needs a documentation system that produces evidence of responsible-person oversight — regardless of whether the regulatory definition is finalized. The documentation itself becomes the evidence of due diligence.
