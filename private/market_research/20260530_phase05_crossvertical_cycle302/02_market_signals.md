# 02 — Cross-Vertical Market Signals and Common Pain Points

## Phase 05 — C302 | Cross-Vertical Synthesis

## Universal Market Signals

### Signal 1: The Evidence Gap Is Universal and Structural

Across all four verticals, the ICP is being asked to produce evidence that their systems were never built to capture. This is not a compliance gap — it is a structural documentation debt.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** Carrier form v4 requires "last three authorization records" — systems never captured this data
- **Finance:** Institutional client questionnaires ask for AI tool inventory + authorization records + decision attribution — no system exists to produce this
- **Consulting:** Client RFPs score AI governance methodology — the reasoning chain was never documented
- **Healthcare:** Carrier attestation forms ask for FMH human oversight documentation — FMH has not defined what this means

**The structural nature:** The evidence gap cannot be closed by working harder. The systems that would produce the evidence were never built. Retroactive reconstruction is not difficult — it is structurally impossible. The only resolution is a forward-looking evidence system.

---

### Signal 2: The Junior-Senior Fluency Inversion Is Universal

The accountable person (senior partner, managing director, physician) lacks technical fluency. The technically fluent person (junior associate, junior consultant, clinical staff) lacks accountability. This inversion is structural across all firm sizes (2–50 employees) and all verticals.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** Senior partner signs carrier attestation; junior associate uses AI tools daily; no capture system between them
- **Finance:** Managing director approves AI tools; investment team uses AI daily; decision attribution gap at the point of approval
- **Consulting:** Managing director signs client questionnaire; junior consultants adopt AI tools independently; no documentation of delegation
- **Healthcare:** Physician authorizes AI tools; clinical staff uses AI tools; patient data access undocumented

**The structural nature:** This is not a training problem. The solution is not "educate the senior person about AI." The solution is a system that makes the accountable senior person operationally capable of supervising AI output they cannot independently verify.

---

### Signal 3: The Vendor Contract Trap Is Permanent in Its Historical Dimension

AI vendor contracts signed in 2023–2024 included data retention for model training provisions. The ICP discovered this after signing. Switching vendors does not resolve historical data exposure. New contracts don't retroactively fix past terms.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** Law firm AI contract — client data already in training set; switching doesn't undo past exposure
- **Finance:** Wealth manager AI vendor — historical client financial data retained; new contract is forward-looking only
- **Consulting:** Sub-consultant AI tools — delegation chain includes undocumented vendor data practices
- **Healthcare:** Medical AI device vendor — patient data already retained; Swissmedic registration doesn't retroactively resolve past data handling

**The structural nature:** The trap is permanent for the historical dimension. The ICP must accept past exposure as a given. The only productive response is a forward-looking evidence system that prevents future accumulation while acknowledging past exposure cannot be erased.

---

### Signal 4: nFADP Article 63 Personal Liability Is the Universal Wake-Up Call

The "responsible person" designation under nFADP Article 63 means personal assets at risk — not just institutional liability. This is true for attorneys, wealth managers, managing consultants, and physicians. The personal dimension converts abstract concern to action.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** Managing partner — personal liability for AI-related data breach involving client communications
- **Finance:** Managing director — personal liability for AI-related breach involving client financial data
- **Consulting:** Managing director — personal liability for AI-related breach involving client business strategy data
- **Healthcare:** Responsible physician — personal liability for AI-related breach involving patient health data + FMH license at stake

**The structural nature:** The personal liability dimension is identical across verticals. The ICP who understands this is not asking whether to act — they are asking how to document their responsible-person oversight in a defensible way.

---

### Signal 5: Peer Enforcement Events Are the Primary Conversion Trigger

Across all verticals, documented peer enforcement events convert abstract concern to action faster than regulatory frameworks. The ICP recognizes themselves in the peer story.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** U.S. federal court AI citation sanctions — Geneva attorneys hearing about it through professional networks
- **Finance:** Institutional client losing a mandate due to AI governance questionnaire failure — competitive urgency
- **Consulting:** Peer firm losing a client engagement over AI governance documentation gap
- **Healthcare:** Peer physician breach scare — anticipatory fear of being "the next headline"

**The structural nature:** Peer enforcement events create urgency independent of regulatory timelines. The ICP acts to avoid being the person without documentation in a peer context — not to comply with a regulatory deadline.

---

### Signal 6: The Geneva Standard Is Defined by Demand, Not Supply

"Geneva standard" functions as a quality threshold marker across all verticals. Clients, carriers, and peers expect Geneva-based professionals to meet a high documentation standard. No one has defined what "Geneva standard" means for AI governance documentation at small-firm scale.

**Manifestation by vertical:**

- **Legal:** ODAGE March 21 event confirms professional body acknowledges burning risk without providing solution
- **Finance:** Institutional clients set high AI governance standards; Geneva WM firms must meet them to win mandates
- **Consulting:** Geneva clients (banks, pharma, commodity trading) are among the most sophisticated in AI governance globally
- **Healthcare:** FMH has stated human oversight is required without defining what it means in practice

**The structural nature:** The first entity to define "Geneva-standard AI governance documentation" for small firms becomes the authority. The ICP will seek out whoever defines the standard first.

## Universal Documentation Debt Structure

The documentation debt follows a consistent structure across all four verticals:

1. **Accumulation phase (2023–2025):** AI tools adopted rapidly without documentation infrastructure; debt accumulates silently
2. **Awareness phase:** ICP discovers the gap — through carrier form, client questionnaire, peer conversation, or professional event
3. **Paralysis phase:** ICP cannot identify the compliance pathway; no trusted advisor exists for small-firm context
4. **Forcing function phase:** Carrier renewal, client deadline, or peer enforcement event creates compressed urgency
5. **Resolution phase:** ICP seeks evidence system, not framework — forward-looking documentation that produces carrier-compatible evidence

**The universal debt structure:**

```
Tool adopted → No authorization record created → No decision attribution recorded
→ No vendor DPA documentation filed → No oversight log maintained
→ Evidence gap accumulates silently → Forcing function arrives
→ ICP cannot produce required evidence → Personal liability exposed
```
