# 01-ICP.md — Geneva Consulting

## Geneva Consulting — ICP Definition

### Primary ICP: SME/Mid-Market Owner-Managers and CFOs
- **Sectors:** Geneva SMEs (manufacturing, professional services, trading, hospitality), mid-market companies CHF 20-500M revenue
- **Role:** Owner, CEO, CFO, Managing Director
- **Pain:** Regulatory change management (CSRD, DORA, LkSG), talent shortage, cost restructuring, digital transformation, succession
- **Trigger events:** Regulatory deadline, founder succession, M&A, cost crisis, growth plateau
- **Buying behaviour:** Peer-recommended; outcome-based; project rather than retainer initially
- **Language:** FR-primary in Geneva for SME; EN for international/multinational

### Secondary ICP: Family Business Boards
- Family-owned Geneva enterprises (CHF 10-200M) in 2nd-3rd generation transition
- Board chairs and family council members managing succession
- Distinct need: governance + emotional dynamics + tax + legal (multi-disciplinary)

### Tertiary ICP: Humanitarian/IO Sectors
- UN agencies, ICRC, international NGOs with Geneva presence
- Finance/administration directors managing efficiency mandates, USD/CHF volatility, regulatory compliance in humanitarian context

### What This ICP Does NOT Want
- Generic consulting frameworks applied without Swiss legal/regulatory context
- Advice that requires cantonal or federal law knowledge but ignores Geneva-specific practice
- Advisory that creates more complexity than it resolves
- Firm with conflicts (e.g., consulting to a competitor in same sector)

### Strategic Implication
Geneva consulting ICP responds to trusted-peer referral and concrete regulatory intelligence. Independent positioning is critical — conflicts are a dealbreaker. The pathway is Brotons.net thought leadership → trusted peer network → advisory mandate.
