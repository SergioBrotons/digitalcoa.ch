## 6 NET-NEW Findings — C114 Phase 02

### Finding 1 — FINMA Guidance 01/2026: Crypto Custody (NEW — Jan 12, 2026)
FINMA published Guidance 01/2026 on January 12, 2026, setting supervisory expectations for regulated institutions (banks, securities firms, asset managers) offering custody, trading, or investment services in crypto-based assets. Strong focus on client and investor protection, operational resilience, and custody risk management. Also clarifies bankruptcy protection for crypto-based assets held in third-party custody. All Geneva financial institutions with crypto exposure need to assess and update custody frameworks by mid-2026.

**Advisory angle:** Crypto custody compliance advisory is a new, time-sensitive service line for Geneva banks, asset managers, and family offices with digital asset exposure.

### Finding 2 — Swiss Financial Institutions Act Amendment: New Stablecoin/Crypto Licence Categories (Consultation closes Feb 6, 2026)
Federal Council opened consultation on October 22, 2025 for amendment to the Financial Institutions Act. Two new licence categories proposed: (1) "Payment instrument institutions" (replacing fintech licence) and (2) new "Crypto-institutions" for custody, trading, and services related to crypto-assets with trading characteristics. Stable crypto-based payment instruments explicitly regulated — issuance restricted to Payment Instrument Institutions, with white paper requirements and segregation obligations. Consultation closes February 6, 2026; Federal Council dispatch to Parliament expected H2 2026.

**Advisory angle:** Regulatory mapping for Geneva financial institutions transitioning to or establishing crypto/stablecoin operations; new licence application advisory; white paper preparation.

### Finding 3 — Project Helvetia Phase Extension to June 2026: Wholesale CBDC Settlement of Real Bond Transactions
Project Helvetia (SNB wholesale CBDC pilot) extended to June 2026 minimum. SIX Digital Exchange (SDX) operating as central securities depository and stock exchange based on DLT under FINMA approval. World Bank priced digital bond on SDX settled using Swiss Franc wholesale CBDC in May 2024 — first live monetary policy operations on regulated DLT infrastructure globally. International collaboration ongoing: Project Jura and Project Mariana (BIS + multiple central banks) exploring cross-border settlement and FX using wholesale CBDCs. No retail e-franc planned.

**Advisory angle:** Wholesale CBDC advisory for Geneva financial infrastructure (custodians, payment service providers, institutional asset managers); DLT-based settlement implications for Geneva commodity trading and trade finance.

### Finding 4 — DORA "Soft Adoption" in Switzerland: Indirect Compliance Driving Advisory Demand (Effective Jan 2025)
DORA (EU Digital Operational Resilience Act) became mandatory for directly impacted EU entities January 17, 2025. Swiss financial institutions with EU operations or EU service providers must comply for market access. FINMA monitoring EU DORA implementation and expected to incorporate best practices into Swiss circulars — "soft adoption" pathway. Swiss firms advised to proactively conduct DORA readiness assessments against internal controls for audit readiness with EU partners. 2026 focus: embedding DORA principles into operational resilience strategies. ICT third-party risk, incident reporting, and digital resilience testing all affected.

**Advisory angle:** DORA readiness assessments, ICT third-party risk framework reviews, and operational resilience gap analyses for Geneva institutions with EU connectivity.

### Finding 5 — "Sovereign AI" in Swiss Private Banking: Geneva's Competitive Differentiator in AI Adoption
Swiss private banking AI adoption accelerating: 50% of Swiss FIs already using AI (FINMA Nov 2024-Jan 2025 survey); 91% of AI-enabled institutions using GenAI. Key trend: decisive shift toward "sovereign AI" solutions — self-contained, privately-hosted AI systems keeping sensitive client data within Swiss secure perimeter. Banks like Lombard Odier, Julius Baer, Pictet, Vontobel co-developing sovereign AI solutions. This addresses the core Swiss private banking competitive advantage: absolute client confidentiality under Swiss banking law. Geneva institutions see AI as a way to enhance (not replace) relationship managers — "phygital" model preferred.

**Advisory angle:** Sovereign AI strategy, vendor assessment for confidential AI, and AI governance frameworks specifically designed for Geneva private banking confidentiality requirements — new advisory niche.

### Finding 6 — Swiss AI-Powered Asset Management Market at USD 1.2 Trillion: Fintech AI Priorities Shifting
Swiss AI-powered asset management market valued at approximately USD 1.2 trillion and growing. Swiss fintech ecosystem analytics, big data, and AI became the most prevalent technology category in 2025. Geneva private banks (Lombard Odier, Julius Baer, Pictet, Vontobel) all actively investing in digital platforms. "Phygital" model emerging: digital self-service for administrative/transactional steps, physical advice for strategic investment decisions. Mass affluent and younger HNWI segments increasingly served via digital channels. AI for portfolio and suitability analysis, automated document processing, and conversational AI chatbots all mainstream.

**Advisory angle:** Digital wealth management platform advisory, AI integration strategy for Geneva private banks and IAMs, client digital experience assessment — growing demand from institutions needing to close digital capability gaps.
