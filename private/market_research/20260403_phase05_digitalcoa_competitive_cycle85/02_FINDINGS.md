### 1. "Swiss Business Under the Swissness Rule Change" — IP Risk Advisory

**Finding:** IPI March 23, 2026 reinterpreted Swissness rules. Swiss cross + "Swiss Engineering/Research" now possible for foreign production. IP specialists alarmed. Geneva companies with Swiss branding built on domestic manufacturing face competitive differentiation risk if the rule is diluted.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:**  
Few Swiss executive advisory firms are positioning IP/branding risk as a board-level strategic issue. Most treat Swissness compliance as a legal trademark matter — not a brand equity and competitive differentiation problem. DigitalCoa can own the framing: "Swissness at the crossroads — what the IPI rule change means for your brand strategy."

**Competitor gap:** Legal firms (IP specialists) see it as trademark issue. Marketing firms see it as brand issue. Neither frames it as board-level strategic risk requiring integrated legal, brand, and competitive analysis.

**Action:** Create briefing document: "The Lex On Problem: What the Swissness Rule Change Signals About Swiss Brand Risk." Target Geneva executive audience.

---

### 2. "Iran War + US Tariffs: The Geneva CFO's Dual Exposure" — Geopolitical Risk Advisory

**Findings:** Trump/Iran war (Feb 28, 2026) → WTI $113/barrel, German 10Y 3.03% (2011 highs), EUR/CHF volatility. US pharmaceutical tariffs 15% on Swiss exports. WTO moratorium on digital tariffs expired March 31.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:**  
Swiss CFOs face simultaneous: (1) commodity price inflation from oil spike, (2) US tariff costs on pharma, (3) digital trade tariff risk. Few advisory firms are framing these as an interconnected Geneva CFO exposure requiring integrated geopolitical + trade + finance analysis.

**Competitor gap:** Most Swiss consulting firms treat these as separate issues. BNP Paribas WM commentary sees "financing costs" as a concern but doesn't frame it as a strategic positioning problem. Geneva CFOs need: "In a world of simultaneous geopolitical shocks, what does your capital allocation and hedging strategy look like?"

**Action:** Executive briefing on dual exposure framework for Geneva CFO audience.

---

### 3. "The Konzernverantwortungsinitiative Compliance Clock" — Board Governance Advisory

**Finding:** Federal Council counter-proposal (LGDE) open for consultation until July 9. Geneva multinationals will face either: (a) the popular initiative's strict global liability, or (b) the lighter LGDE regime. Either way, risk analysis + sustainability reporting + supervisory authority compliance obligations are coming.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:**  
Board-level governance advisory on Konzernverantwortungsinitiative compliance posture. Most Geneva boards are treating this as a legal/compliance issue — but it has strategic dimensions: supply chain restructuring implications, competitive cost of compliance vs. non-compliance, strategic positioning relative to EU standards.

**Competitor gap:** Law firms are the primary advisors on this topic. Management consulting firms are less present. DigitalCoa can occupy the "strategic governance" space: "It's a legal question. It's also a board strategy question."

**Action:** LGDE consultation analysis — which Geneva companies are submitting? What's the strategic risk differential between initiative and counter-proposal? Publish as executive briefing before July 9 consultation close.

---

### 4. "Swiss Hospitals, Digital Sovereignty, and the AI Infrastructure Question" — Institutional Advisory

**Finding:** HUG + Valais Hospital co-developing DPI+ (Swiss, independent). CHUV choosing Epic Systems (US). Vaud parliament examining data sovereignty. Geneva institutional strategy: build your own. Vaud institutional strategy: buy American.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:**  
This is a case study in institutional AI governance: when do you build vs. buy? What does "digital sovereignty" actually cost and benefit in practice? The HUG vs. CHUV contrast is a perfect teaching case for Geneva boards thinking about their own AI infrastructure strategy.

**Competitor gap:** No Swiss executive advisory firm is using this case study for board education. DigitalCoa can use it to position itself as the advisor that makes complex institutional AI governance legible for non-technical boards.

**Action:** Case study brief: "Geneva's Hospital AI Choice: What HUG vs. CHUV Tells Us About Institutional AI Governance."

---

### 5. "The Fragile Innovation Ecosystem: What EPF Budget Cuts Mean for Swiss Corporate Talent Pipelines"

**Finding:** Le Temps editorial: EPF budget cuts = quasi-suicidal threat to Swiss innovation foundation. Scale-ups seeking US capital. Amazon acquiring Swiss tech champions. Talent pipeline for Swiss corporations is at structural risk.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:**  
Boards of large Swiss corporations have a stake in EPF system health — their future talent, their technology partners, their competitive positioning. This is not just an academic issue. Boards should be asking: "What is our dependency on the EPF talent pipeline, and what would EPF decline mean for our 10-year strategy?"

**Competitor gap:** EPF budget cuts are discussed in science/education circles. They're not being framed as a strategic risk for Swiss corporate boards. DigitalCoa can lead this framing.

**Action:** Executive brief on innovation ecosystem dependency risk for Swiss corporate boards.
