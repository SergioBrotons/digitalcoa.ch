## C40 New Findings (1/2 searches succeeded — Gemini 429 quota hit on search 2)

### ⭐ CRITICAL: 3-TIER ADOPTION PYRAMID (C40 NET-NEW)

**Tier 1 — Individual/Practice Level: 71% solo + 67% small firm**
- 72% of solo legal professionals using AI (solo practitioners)
- 67% of small firm legal professionals using AI in some capacity
- "Fast but thin" adoption: 56% use general GenAI tools (ChatGPT etc), only 14% specialized legal AI

**Tier 2 — Firm-Level Formal Adoption: 28% formal policies (Thomson Reuters 2025)**
- 28% of firms ≤50 lawyers have formal GenAI adoption (Thomson Reuters 2025)
- 43% of firms have NO formal AI policy and NO plans to create one (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026)

**Tier 3 — Firm-Level Deep/Widespread Adoption: 4% small firms**
- Only 8% of solos and 4% of small firms have adopted AI "widely or universally"
- 20% small firms (≤50 lawyers) adopted GenAI at firm level (late 2025 study)

**Implication: The governance gap is even wider than previously quantified**
- 67-72% using AI individually → 28% formal firm adoption → 4% deep/widespread use
- 43% have NO policy AND NO plans (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026)
- Opportunity: "govern the AI employees already use at individual level"

### ⭐ NEW: 90% of law firms expected to incorporate AI by 2027
- Expert forecast: 90% of all law firms routinely incorporating AI by 2027
- Implies ~12-18mo first-mover window narrowing rapidly

### ⭐ NEW: 59% of law firms believe GenAI should be integrated into legal work
- December 2025 survey — majority belief in necessity of GenAI integration
- Gap between belief and formal policy implementation: 59% believe vs 28% formal adoption

### ⭐ NEW: Growing small firms 2x more likely to use AI vs stable/shrinking
- Competitive advantage signal: AI-using firms growing faster
- Efficiency/competitive pressure as adoption driver

### ⭐ NEW: GenAI improves lawyer productivity in memo writing by 35%
- Quantified productivity gain specific to legal work
- Corroborates 260h/year efficiency (Thomson Reuters 2025) — consistent magnitude

### ⭐ NEW: Data residency barrier confirmed for Swiss law firms
- US CLOUD Act creates data sovereignty risk for Swiss firms
- Swiss/EU-hosted AI tools preferred (FABIAN, CASUS Swiss-sovereign confirmed)
- Many AI tools trained on US/UK law — limited Swiss law applicability
