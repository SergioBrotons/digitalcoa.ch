## Cross-Vertical ICP Profiles

### 1. Geneva Legal ICP — Small Law Firm 2–5 Attorneys

**Who they are:** Small Geneva law practices operating under SAV governance with Art.321 StGB professional secrecy obligations. Managing partner signs all attestations personally. No IT staff, no compliance officer.

**Accountability language they use:**
- "We don't use AI" (while associates use consumer ChatGPT on client matters)
- "Our vendor is Swiss-hosted" (DPA never executed)
- "We reviewed the SAV guidelines" (no decision tree available)
- "We have human review" (process not designed for attestation evidence standard)
- Matter-level AI restriction from corporate clients — no operationalization system

**What keeps them awake:**
- PI renewal with carrier AI attestation they cannot answer
- SAV audit with no SAV-validated AI register
- Client instruction not to use AI on their matter — cannot operationalize
- nFADP Art.9 DPA absent, Art.63 personal liability CHF 250,000
- Attorney-client privilege collision with vendor data retention

**Entry trigger:** PI carrier renewal arrives with AI attestation. 3-week deadline. Managing partner realizes they cannot answer the form.

### 2. Geneva Finance ICP — Boutique Wealth Management 50–200 Employees

**Who they are:** Boutique wealth managers regulated by FINMA with SRO oversight. Multi-carrier relationships creating compound non-standardized attestation obligations. Multi-generational client relationships where 2024-2025 questionnaire responses now function as persistent comparative intelligence.

**Accountability language they use:**
- "We have institutional approval" (first-generation questionnaire response, now obsolete)
- "Our carriers review our AI governance" (carrier third-gen questions activating undisclosed Art.9 obligations)
- "Human authorization is documented" (authorization chain never built)
- "We use third-party AI for portfolio construction" (processor chain sub-delegation unmanaged)

**What keeps them awake:**
- Institutional client second-generation questionnaire with comparative benchmarking against peer responses
- Four independent documentation systems (FINMA, institutional questionnaires, nFADP, carrier attestation) with no cross-reference methodology
- Agentic AI incompatibility with authorization architecture designed for discrete decisions
- Multi-carrier attestation instruments with materially different requirements across simultaneous carrier relationships

**Entry trigger:** Second-generation institutional DDQ arrives. Boutique discovers 2024 response now functions as live comparative intelligence.

### 3. Geneva Consulting ICP — Small Consulting Boutique 2–50 Employees

**Who they are:** Independent consulting boutiques operating without Big Four infrastructure. Client AI questionnaires functioning as vendor selection instruments measuring governance seriousness, not compliance adequacy. Sub-consultant delegation creating Art.9 prime liability without contractual privity.

**Accountability language they use:**
- "We respond to client questionnaires" (without understanding questionnaire architecture drives vendor selection, not compliance validation)
- "Our sub-consultants are independent" (Art.9 obligations arise from data processing activity not presence of DPA)
- "We have an AI governance framework" (multi-tool stack aggregate surface un-enumerable)
- "Deliverables are AI-assisted" (Art.398 CO mandate-period model version uncontemporable)

**What keeps them awake:**
- Client AI questionnaire arriving from prime with governance measurement architecture they cannot decode
- nFADP Art.9 processor chain prime boutique liability without contractual privity — operative before delegation decision
- Cross-client commitment proliferation: AI governance representations scattered across engagement letters, questionnaire responses, carrier forms, vendor agreements with no cross-reference tracker
- Geneva-contextualized expertise as non-replicable moat against global consultancies — cannot be built with content investment alone

**Entry trigger:** Client AI DDQ arrives blocking new mandate. Firm scrambles without SME-calibrated response framework.

### 4. Geneva Healthcare ICP — Independent Practice 2–10 Physicians

**Who they are:** Independent medical practices under FMH governance with Swissdamed Tier 1/Tier 2 classification deadline of 1 July 2026. Multi-physician practice with independent AI adoption creating practice-level accountability diffusion. Carrier attestation arriving under 2-4 week deadline producing stampede documentation.

**Accountability language they use:**
- "We don't use AI in clinical decisions" (while AI tools active in scheduling, documentation, administrative)
- "Our staff are trained on AI tools" (vendor certificates inadequate to FMH standard, creates false compliance confidence)
- "We have consent for AI processing" (patient-override-of-AI documentation void — deliberation moment undocumented)
- "Swissdamed registration complete" (Tier classification without proportionate methodology, over- or under-compliance)

**What keeps them awake:**
- Carrier attestation under 2-4 week deadline creating stampede effect — self-incriminating evidence produced under urgency
- Swissdamed classification with no proportionate methodology for independent practice scale
- AI-associated adverse outcome documentation architecture absence — incident review infrastructure has no category for AI system state, tool version, AI recommendation
- Multi-physician accountability diffusion: signing physician certifies to aggregate AI surface across independent physician decisions they cannot enumerate

**Entry trigger:** Carrier AI attestation arrives. Swissdamed registration deadline 1 July 2026. Two simultaneous pressures, different deadlines.

## Universal Cross-Vertical Language

### The Accountability Gap Phrase
EN: "The person who signed has no control over the AI decisions that created the exposure."
FR: "Celui qui a signé n'a aucun contrôle sur les décisions d'IA qui ont créé l'exposition."

### The Authorization Architecture Phrase
EN: "Your process was designed for human decisions. AI recommendations arrived later. The process doesn't produce the evidence the attestation requires."
FR: "Votre processus a été conçu pour les décisions humaines. Les recommandations d'IA sont arrivées ensuite. Le processus ne produit pas les preuves que l'attestation exige."

### The Regulatory Accumulation Phrase
EN: "You're subject to five frameworks simultaneously. None of them were designed to work together. None of them were designed for a 2–50 person practice."
FR: "Vous êtes soumis à cinq cadres simultanément. Aucun n'a été conçu pour fonctionner avec les autres. Aucun n'a été conçu pour une pratique de 2 à 50 personnes."

### The Self-Incrimination Phrase
EN: "The attempt to comply is producing evidence that will be used against you."
FR: "La tentative de conformité produit des preuves qui seront utilisées contre vous."

### The Formation Exclusion Phrase
EN: "The standards being set in Bern will apply to you. You weren't in the room."
FR: "Les normes en cours de fixation à Berne s'appliqueront à vous. Vous n'étiez pas dans la pièce."

## Language Precision Rules

1. **Never say "AI strategy"** — ICP experiences this as productivity framing, not accountability framing
2. **Say "accountability infrastructure"** — names the actual gap without implying they have been negligent
3. **Say "attestation evidence"** not "compliance documentation" — attestation evidence is the specific standard that matters
4. **Say "human authorization chain"** not "human oversight" — chain names the structural requirement, oversight is vague
5. **Say "whoever signed"** — avoids implying negligence while naming the accountability locus precisely
