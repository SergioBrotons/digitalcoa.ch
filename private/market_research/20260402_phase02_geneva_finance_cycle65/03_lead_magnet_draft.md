## EN Version

# The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist
### Q2 2026 — Geneva Finance

---

**Swiss institutional trust is excellent. Your AI governance should be.**

Switzerland's financial centre reputation is documented as "excellent" by the Federal Council. But as AI becomes embedded in wealth management — from portfolio allocation to client communications — the question is whether your firm's AI governance matches the trust standard your clients expect.

**This checklist helps Geneva's boutique wealth managers assess where they stand across six regulatory dimensions:**

- EU AI Act GPAI obligations (already in force since August 2025)
- Section 301 forced labour ESG exposure
- FADP DPIA requirements for AI-assisted decision-making
- Swiss-EU bilateral package data sovereignty positioning
- Swiss Bankers Association digital transformation engagement
- BVG pension sector AI governance changes

---

### Section 1: EU AI Act GPAI — Are You Already in Scope?

The EU AI Act's GPAI obligations have applied since **August 2, 2025**. The August 2, 2026 deadline is for **legacy systems** to achieve conformity.

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Do any AI tools you use serve clients or operations within the EU?
- [ ] Is your AI tool stack being used for EU-facing portfolio management, reporting, or advisory?
- [ ] Do you use AI chatbots for client communication (FINMA: 91% of Swiss FIs already do)?
- [ ] Have you assessed whether your AI tools qualify as "general-purpose AI" under the EU Act?
- [ ] Do you have documentation ready for your current AI tool conformity status?

**Why it matters now:** Geneva WMs with EU-based clients or EU investment mandates likely fall within EU AI Act extraterritorial scope. The 122 days to August 2, 2026 is not much if you haven't started.

---

### Section 2: Section 301 Forced Labour — ESG Portfolio Exposure

**April 15, 2026: ~12.5 days** — written comments deadline still open.

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Do any ESG-mandated portfolios include companies with US supply chain exposure?
- [ ] Are your ESG screening tools capturing forced labour risk factors?
- [ ] Do you have documentation ready for ESG product forced labour compliance?
- [ ] Have you communicated forced labour risk to ESG mandate clients?

**Why it matters:** Section 301 Investigation B targets insufficient measures against forced labour imports. Swiss WMs managing ESG mandates — even indirectly through portfolio holdings — face compounded compliance obligations. Geneva WMs who advise clients on ESG products: forced labour documentation is now a service requirement.

---

### Section 3: FADP DPIA — AI in Client Portfolio Management

The FDPIC has confirmed: **FADP applies directly to AI systems**. For wealth managers:

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Have you completed a DPIA before deploying AI in portfolio management?
- [ ] Do your AI-assisted client reporting tools trigger FADP Art. 21 (automated decision-making)?
- [ ] Are clients informed when AI influences their portfolio decisions?
- [ ] Do you have a human review mechanism for AI-influenced outcomes?
- [ ] Do your AI-generated client communications include required disclosures?

---

### Section 4: Swiss-EU Bilateral — Data Sovereignty as Differentiation

The bilateral stabilization package adopted by the Federal Council on April 1, 2026 confirms: **Horizon Europe participation and innovation-location objective reinforced**.

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Is your AI infrastructure hosted on Swiss or EU servers?
- [ ] Have you documented the data jurisdiction of your AI tools for client disclosure?
- [ ] Is your Swiss-hosted AI positioning communicated to clients as a trust advantage?
- [ ] Do your AI vendor contracts address cross-border data flow compliance?

**Why data sovereignty matters:** Swiss institutional trust is documented as excellent. Your AI infrastructure should reflect the same standard.

---

### Section 5: SBA Digital Transformation — Engaging the Sector Framework

The Swiss Bankers Association — through Richard Hess (Head of Digital Finance) — is actively working on digital transformation for Swiss banking. Geneva WMs should engage:

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Are you monitoring SBA Digital Finance guidance developments?
- [ ] Have you participated in any SBA consultation on AI in banking?
- [ ] Is your firm's AI governance aligned with SBA prudential expectations?
- [ ] Do you have AML/compliance AI tools that reflect SBA self-regulation standards?

---

### Section 6: BVG Pension Sector — Institutional Client AI Governance

The OAK BV published the BVG structural reform evaluation on April 1, 2026. Pension funds are major WM clients:

**Questions to answer:**
- [ ] Do you manage assets for BVG-covered pension funds?
- [ ] Are your AI governance practices aligned with pension fund supervisory expectations?
- [ ] Do you monitor OAK BV guidance for pension fund AI governance developments?
- [ ] Have you assessed how BVG structural reform might affect your institutional client requirements?

## FR Version

# La Liste de Contrôle de Readiness Gouvernance IA pour Gérants de Fortune
### Q2 2026 — Finance Genève

---

**La confiance institutionnelle suisse est excellente. Votre gouvernance IA devrait l'être aussi.**

La réputation du centre financier suisse est documentée comme « excellente » par le Conseil fédéral. Mais à mesure que l'IA s'intègre dans la gestion de fortune — de l'allocation de portefeuille aux communications clients — la question est de savoir si la gouvernance IA de votre cabinet correspond au standard de confiance que vos clients attendent.

---

### Section 1: IAO de l'Acte européen sur l'IA — Êtes-vous déjà concerné?

Les obligations IAO de l'Acte européen sur l'IA s'appliquent depuis le **2 août 2025**. La date du **2 août 2026** concerne la conformité des systèmes hérités.

**Questions clés:**
- Vos outils d'IA servent-ils des clients ou des opérations dans l'UE?
- Utilisez-vous des chatbots IA pour la communication client (FINMA: 91% des banques suisses)?
- Avez-vous évalué si vos outils d'IA relèvent de l'IAO?
- Disposez-vous de la documentation sur l'état de conformité de vos outils?

---

### Section 2: Section 301 Travail Forcé — Exposition ESG des Portefeuilles

**~12,5 jours** — délai de commentaires écrite

**Questions clés:**
- Vos portefeuilles ESG incluent-ils des entreprises exposées aux chaînes d'approvisionnement américaines?
- Vos outils de screening ESG capturent-ils les facteurs de risque travail forcé?
- Avez-vous documenté la conformité Forced Labour de vos produits ESG?

---

### Section 3: LPDo-2 AIPD — IA dans la Gestion de Portefeuille Client

Le PFPDT a confirmé: **la LPDo-2 s'applique directement aux systèmes d'IA**.

**Questions clés:**
- AIPD complétée avant le déploiement d'IA dans la gestion de portefeuille?
- Outils de reporting IA client: déclenchent-ils l'Art. 21 LPDo-2?
- Mécanisme de révision humaine en place pour les résultats influencés par l'IA?
- Communications clients générées par l'IA: mentions obligatoires?

---

### Section 4: Paquet Bilatéral Suisse-UE — Souveraineté des Données

Le paquet de stabilisation bilatérale du 1er avril 2026 confirme: **participation à Horizon Europe et objectif de lieu d'innovation renforcés**.

**Questions clés:**
- Infrastructure IA hébergée en Suisse ou dans l'UE?
- Positionnement IA hébergée en Suisse communiqué comme avantage de confiance?
- Contrats fournisseurs d'IA: conformité aux flux transfrontaliers?

---

### Section 5: Transformation Numérique SBA

La SBA — via Richard Hess (Head of Digital Finance) — travaille activement sur la transformation numérique. **Questions clés:**
- Surveillance des évolutions SBA sur l'IA?
- Gouvernance IA conforme aux attentes prudentielles SBA?
- Outils de compliance AML conformes à l'autorégulation SBA?

---

### Section 6: Secteur LPP — IA dans les Caisses de Pension

L'OAK BV a publié l'évaluation de la réforme structurelle LPP le 1er avril 2026.

**Questions clés:**
- Gestion d'actifs pour des caisses de pension LPP?
- Pratiques de gouvernance IA alignées sur les attentes de surveillance?
- Impact de la réforme LPP sur vos exigences clients institutionnels?
