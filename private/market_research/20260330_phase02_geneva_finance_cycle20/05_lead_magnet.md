# The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist
### A 9-Section Framework for Geneva Independent Wealth Managers and Family Offices

---

## Section 1: The Situation

CARF has been live for three months.

The Automated Exchange of Financial Account Information framework — covering CRS/CFATR — became operative on January 1, 2026. If your firm has been processing financial account data with AI-assisted tools (portfolio management systems, risk analytics, client reporting platforms) without addressing CARF obligations in that context, you have a three-month compliance gap.

That's the posture of the Geneva boutique wealth management sector right now: CARF operative, AMLA/LETA approaching (~3-6 months), FATF mutual evaluation creating external pressure, and EU AI Act obligations ~4.5 months away for firms with EU client nexus.

The boutique wealth manager using AI for portfolio analytics, client reporting, or risk modeling faces a specific problem: existing compliance frameworks weren't built for AI-augmented financial services delivery. The obligations are new, the frameworks are emerging, and the FATF evaluation timeline creates urgency that doesn't exist in a normal regulatory environment.

- **Client reporting platforms** (AI-assisted report generation, performance attribution)

For each tool: Is client financial data being processed? Has CARF compliance been addressed? Is there AI transparency disclosure to clients?

## Section 3: The 3-Layer Compliance Stack

**Layer 1 — Swiss Domestic (Active Now)**
- FinSA/FinSO conduct rules (client suitability, disclosure, documentation)
- FINMA Circ. 2026/1 on nature risks (operative Jan 1 2026)
- SRO membership obligations

**Layer 2 — Swiss International (CARF Live; AMLA/LETA Approaching)**
- CARF/CRS 2.0 operative since Jan 1 2026
- AMLA/LETA H2 2026 (~3-6 months) — 30-day discrepancy reporting; SRO expansion
- FATF mutual evaluation 2026-2027 — external pressure elevating AMLA compliance

**Layer 3 — EU Extraterritorial (August 2, 2026)**
- EU AI Act high-risk obligations if EU client nexus or EU-directed output
- GDPR + EU AI Act overlap for client financial data processed by AI

The FATF evaluation timeline is the forcing function. Switzerland's mutual evaluation creates a window where FINMA and SROs will be under international scrutiny to demonstrate enforcement. Boutique wealth managers who have documented AI governance frameworks will be in a materially different position than those who don't.

## Section 4: CARF — The Already-Operative Obligation

**CARF/CRS 2.0 has been live since January 1, 2026.**

For AI-augmented wealth management:
- AI systems processing financial account data must be documented within the CARF compliance framework
- AI-assisted data collection and processing must meet CARF reporting standards
- The 3 months already elapsed represent a measurable compliance gap if AI tools are in use

**CARF compliance questions for your AI tools:**
1. Have you documented which AI systems process financial account data subject to CARF?
2. Have you assessed whether AI-augmented processing changes your CARF reporting obligations?
3. Do you have a process for AI-assisted data quality review under CARF?
4. Has your AI vendor provided CARF compliance documentation?

---

## Section 5: AMLA/LETA — The Approaching Deadline

**AMLA/LETA implementing ordinance expected H2 2026 (~3-6 months).**

**The FATF pressure:** Switzerland's FATF mutual evaluation (2026-2027) means AMLA compliance will be reviewed against international standards. Wealth managers are a primary evaluation target. The FATF evaluation creates a specific urgency for documenting AMLA readiness — including AI applications in transaction monitoring and AML screening.

---

## Section 6: The EU AI Act — Does It Apply to Your Firm?

**EU AI Act high-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months).**

The EU AI Act applies extraterritorially when:
- Your firm has EU-based clients
- Your AI systems produce output intended for EU data subjects
- You operate AI systems that process data about EU individuals

For boutique wealth managers: if you manage portfolios for EU-based families, advise EU-domiciled entities, or your investment recommendations affect EU securities markets — you're likely within scope.

**High-risk obligations include:** conformity assessment, technical documentation, human oversight, transparency to users, registration with relevant EU authority.

---

## Section 7: FATF Evaluation — The External Pressure You Can't Ignore

**Switzerland's FATF mutual evaluation is 2026-2027.**

The FATF evaluation covers:
- AMLA enforcement effectiveness
- AMLA compliance with FATF recommendations
- SRO/FINMA supervision quality
- Case outcomes and enforcement actions

Wealth managers will be specifically reviewed for:
- AMLA compliance program quality
- Transaction monitoring effectiveness
- **AI applications in AML/compliance (new area of FATF scrutiny)**
- Client due diligence completeness

**Step 1 — AI/CARF Inventory (Week 1-2):**

**Step 2 — CARF Gap Assessment (Week 2-3):**
Assess your CARF compliance posture specifically for AI-augmented processing. Do you have documentation for AI-assisted data collection?

**Step 3 — AMLA/LETA Readiness (Week 3-4):**
Review AMLA compliance program for AI applications in transaction monitoring and AML screening. Assess SRO membership implications. Prepare documentation for FATF evaluation readiness.

**Step 4 — EU AI Act Exposure Assessment (Week 4):**
Determine whether your EU client nexus triggers high-risk obligations under EU AI Act. If yes: initiate conformity assessment process with 4.5-month runway.

**Step 5 — AI Governance Policy (Week 5):**
Draft a firm AI governance policy covering: approved AI tools, data handling rules, client disclosure standards, CARF/AMLA compliance integration, incident response.
