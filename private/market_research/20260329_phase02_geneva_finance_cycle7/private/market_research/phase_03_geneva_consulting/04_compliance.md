## Regulatory Landscape (Preliminary)

### General Business Regulation
- Swiss Code of Obligations (OR): consulting contracts, liability
- Geneva cantonal business registration (OCPM)
- No specific consulting license required for general management/strategy consulting

### Data Protection (nFADP)
- Applies to consulting firms processing personal data of clients or contacts
- Client data (business information, contact details, project data) = personal data under nFADP
- ROPA (Record of Processing Activities) may be required if processing is systematic
- Data breach notification obligations apply
- Privacy by Design applies to client data systems

### Financial Consulting (If Applicable)
- Insurance mediation (BVV): required for pension consulting
- Financial advisory: FINMA licensing may apply for investment-related consulting
- Distinction: general strategy consulting vs. regulated financial advice

### HR Consulting (If Applicable)
- No specific FINMA license; general employment law compliance
- GDPR/nFADP for employee data processing

### Contractual Liability
- Swiss Code of Obligations: liability for professional services
- Professional liability insurance: standard for consulting firms
- AI-generated recommendations: liability questions emerging

---

## AI-Specific Considerations

### AI Tool Use by Consultants
- AI assistants (Claude, ChatGPT, Perplexity): for research, drafting, analysis
- No specific Swiss regulation of AI tool use by non-regulated businesses
- However: nFADP applies to any personal data processed via AI tools
- Confidentiality: client data uploaded to AI tools may create data protection obligations
- Disclosure: no current requirement to disclose AI use in consulting deliverables

### Emerging Concerns
- AI accuracy: consultants remain liable for advice quality regardless of AI assistance
- AI vendor due diligence: if using AI SaaS tools with client data
- Cross-border AI tools: nFADP applies to data processing even outside Switzerland
