## Universal Regulation: nFADP — Explicitly Confirmed

### FDPIC May 8, 2025: The Universal Wake-Up Call
**Source:** Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner, May 8, 2025
**Relevance:** ALL 5 VERTICALS

The FDPIC confirmed explicitly: **The Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP / LRPD) applies directly to AI-based data processing.**

This is the single most important cross-vertical regulatory signal. It means:
- Every Geneva SMB using AI tools is processing personal data under nFADP
- nFADP obligations are operative immediately — not optional
- The FDPIC has explicitly addressed AI-specific obligations

### nFADP Articles by Relevance to DigitalCoa.ch Advisory

| Article | Content | Vertical Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) | Sensitive personal data categories | Healthcare (highest), Legal (client privilege), Finance (financial), RE (tenant data), Consulting (client data) |

| Art. 14 | Right to access | Universal |
| Art. 26 | Data security obligations | Universal |
| Art. 29 | Data protection impact assessments (DPIA) | Universal for AI systems; particularly relevant for RE (tenant screening, facial recognition), Healthcare (patient data), Legal (client data) |

## EU AI Act — The New Cross-Vertical Pressure Layer

### August 2, 2026: High-Risk AI Systems Deadline
**Source:** EU AI Act official timeline; Lenz & Staehelin analysis
**Relevance:** ALL 5 VERTICALS with EU market exposure

The EU AI Act has extraterritorial scope (similar to GDPR). **Swiss SMBs are subject if they:**
- Place AI systems or GPAI models on the EU market (SaaS, cloud, API accessible from EU)
- Operate AI systems where output is intended for use within the EU
- Act as importers or distributors making non-EU AI systems available in the EU

### High-Risk Obligations Triggering August 2, 2026
For Geneva SMBs whose AI systems fall into high-risk categories:

| Obligation | Description |
|---|---|
| Risk management system | Systematic process for identifying and mitigating AI risks |
| Data governance | Quality, relevance, and appropriateness of training data |
| Human oversight | Ability for humans to supervise, correct, and override AI decisions |
| Technical documentation | Detailed records of system design, capabilities, and limitations |
| Conformity assessments | Third-party verification of compliance before market placement |
| Cybersecurity | Protection against manipulation, attacks, and exploitation |
| Transparency | Users must be informed they are interacting with AI |
| Fairness | Non-discrimination and accuracy requirements |

### EU Third-Country Representative Requirement
**Non-EU providers** of high-risk AI systems must appoint an **EU-established Third-Country Representative** by August 2, 2026.

**Implication for Geneva SMBs:** If using or providing AI systems to EU clients, this obligation may apply or be contractually required.

### Article 55 SMB Support Measures
The EU AI Act includes Article 55 measures to support SMBs, but compliance cost challenges remain real for smaller entities.

### The Brussels Effect
EU regulations often become de facto global standards. Geneva SMBs with any EU market presence cannot ignore EU AI Act obligations without risking market access.

**Cross-vertical implication:** Dual compliance (nFADP + EU AI Act) is now the operational reality for Geneva's internationally-oriented professional services community.

## Universal Standard: ISO 42001 (AI Management Systems)

**Status:** Published 2023; adoption growing; becoming procurement-relevant

ISO 42001 is the first international standard for AI management systems — certifiable framework for how organizations govern AI.

**Why ISO 42001 Matters Cross-Vertically:**
- Transcends industry-specific regulation
- Creates a defensible standard clients can point to
- Positions DigitalCoa.ch as ISO 42001 implementation advisor
- Increasingly required in procurement (especially consulting firms with enterprise clients)
- Swiss landscape: SQS, EY, KPMG, ACTAGIS offer ISO 42001 certification

**Cross-vertical advisory stack:** nFADP (table stakes) + ISO 42001 (competitive differentiation)

## The Three-Layer Regulatory Logic (Unchanged — Confirmed Universal)

All five verticals face the same three-layer regulatory logic:

1. **Data Layer:** nFADP obligations for any personal/sensitive data processed
2. **AI System Layer:** Questions about what AI tools are being used, how, and whether they comply
3. **Governance Layer:** How the organization structures decision-making, accountability, and oversight for AI systems

**This three-layer logic is identical across all five verticals.** DigitalCoa.ch's advisory framework can be built once and adapted per vertical — operational efficiency confirmed.
