## Purpose
Synthesize the ideal customer profile across all five verticals — identifying the common ICP characteristics, segment-specific nuances, and the unified positioning that serves all five Geneva professional services markets.

## Universal ICP Characteristics

### Demographic Profile
- **Age:** 45–65 years old
- **Role:** Principal, partner, or practice owner (individual decision-maker)
- **Authority:** Single-person budget decisions for consulting services (up to CHF 5,000–15,000 initial engagement)
- **Firm size:** 1–20 employees across all verticals
- **Location:** Geneva canton (primary); Vaud extension for healthcare
- **Language:** French primary; English for international-facing firms

### Psychographic Profile
- **Primary concern:** Regulatory compliance and professional liability
- **Secondary concern:** Staffing pressures and operational efficiency
- **AI stance:** Using or considering AI tools; uncertain about obligations
- **Decision trigger:** Peer adoption, regulatory signal, or security incident
- **Deal velocity:** 6–16 weeks from first contact to signed engagement

### Common Pain Points (All 5 Verticals)
1. **nFADP uncertainty** — "Am I using AI in a way that creates obligations?"
2. **Regulatory complexity** — "What exactly do I need to do and by when?"
3. **Staffing pressure** — "I can't hire enough staff; AI seems like the answer but I'm not sure"
4. **Competitive parity** — "Are my peers already ahead of me on this?"
5. **EU market exposure** — "Do my EU clients require me to comply with the EU AI Act?"

## Vertical-Specific ICP Refinements

### Legal (Phase 01 — Cycle 4)
**Refined ICP:** Small law firms, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva canton, French-speaking, serving private clients and SMEs. Managing partner makes decisions. AI use: research tools, document review, billing automation. Key concern: professional secrecy obligations + nFADP client data obligations simultaneously.

**Buying trigger (Cycle 4 reinforced):** FDJP CoE AI Convention consultation draft expected end-2026. ANBLES AI Working Group February 2026 signals active industry tracking. ABA AI guidance March 2026 signals global norm acceleration. First peer adoption creates competitive pressure.

**Firm archetype:** "The cautious adopter" — already using or trialing AI tools; aware something needs to be done; waiting for regulatory clarity before committing.

**Addressable market:** ~150–250 small Geneva law firms (1–5 attorneys)

---

### Finance / WM (Phase 02 — Cycle 4)
**Refined ICP:** Boutique wealth managers, family offices, independent asset managers, CHF 50M–500M AUM, Geneva-based, French-speaking principals. AI use: portfolio analytics, client reporting, risk modeling. Key concern: FINMA scrutiny + nFADP client data obligations simultaneously.

**Buying trigger (Cycle 4 updated):** FINMA Circ. 2025/02 operative January 1, 2025 (FinSA/FinSO conduct rules). CARF + CRS 2.0 operative January 1, 2026. AMLA revision September 2025 / mid-2026. LETA H2 2026. Named Swiss AI platforms (InvestGlass, Additiv, Altoo, Neurons Lab, Nevis) now serving this segment — creates both competitive context and nFADP exposure.

**Firm archetype:** "The compliance-conscious" — already subject to FINMA oversight; understands regulatory risk; seeking structured AI governance framework that satisfies both FINMA and nFADP.

**Addressable market:** ~50–100 Geneva boutique WM firms; ~200–400 Geneva family offices / independent managers

### Consulting (Phase 03 — Cycle 4)
**Refined ICP:** Small management consulting firms, 2–10 consultants, Geneva-based, serving mid-market and large corporate clients. Principal makes decisions. AI use: project delivery, research, slide preparation. Key concern: ISO 42001 becoming procurement criterion; EU AI Act GPAI obligations for firms with EU clients.

**Buying trigger (Cycle 4 sharpened):** EU AI Act GPAI rules operative August 2, 2025. Advisory window NOW through end-2026 (before Federal Council sector-specific regulations). IT/tech sub-segment identified as highest nFADP exposure + fastest conversion. Staff departure as acute trigger.

**Firm archetype:** "The ambitious operator" — serving demanding clients who ask about AI governance; already using or piloting AI tools; competitive pressure from clients requiring AI governance evidence.

**Addressable market:** ~300–500 small Geneva consulting firms (1–10 consultants)

---

### Healthcare (Phase 04 — Cycle 4)
**Refined ICP:** Small medical practices, 1–3 physicians, Geneva canton + Vaud, French-speaking. Practice owner/physician makes decisions. AI use: administrative automation, scheduling, possibly diagnostic support. Key concern: swissdamed mandatory July 1, 2026 (MOST CONCRETE CROSS-VERTICAL DEADLINE); nFADP Art. 5(c) patient data obligations; professional liability for AI-assisted decisions.

**Buying trigger (Cycle 4 reinforced):** Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — ~15 months away. FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report Jul 2025 enforcement trajectory confirmed. MGMA January 2026: only 42% of medical groups have formal AI governance policies. CHUV Meditron testing Swiss medical LLM May 2026 institutional context.

**Firm archetype:** "The time-pressed physician" — high patient volume; administrative overload; wants AI to help but fears liability; waiting for clear guidance on swissdamed obligations.

**Addressable market:** ~1,500–2,000 Geneva medical practices; Vaud extension: ~2,000–2,500 addressable practices

### Real Estate (Phase 06 — Cycle 1 — Integrated into Synthesis)
**Refined ICP:** Small real estate agencies, 1–10 employees, Geneva-based, residential focus, property management portfolios. Principal makes decisions. AI use: property valuations, tenant screening, marketing, smart building management. Key concern: 67% already using AI but low regulatory awareness. nFADP DPIA obligations for AI tenant screening, smart building systems, facial recognition access control.

**Buying trigger (NEW insight):** Paradox — highest AI adoption (67%) + lowest regulatory awareness = strongest advisory entry point. Regulatory pressure + EU AI Act extraterritorial for agencies with EU clients/properties. GAIGI launching Fall 2025 signals Geneva AI governance awareness will increase.

**Firm archetype:** "The enthusiastic adopter" — already heavily using AI tools; unaware of nFADP obligations; retroactive compliance approach resonates strongly ("you're already using AI, here's what that means legally").

**Addressable market:** ~250–500 small Geneva real estate agencies
