## Headline Findings

### 1. Healthcare Is the Strongest nFADP Vertical — Now With Enforcement Evidence
- nFADP Art. 5(c): health data = "sensitive" — highest protection category
- FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (July 1, 2025): enforcement trajectory confirmed; healthcare data breaches are a priority enforcement area
- FDPIC joint statement on AI-generated images (February 23, 2026): cross-DPA enforcement coordination signal
- X/Twitter preliminary investigation (March 2025): FDPIC AI enforcement precedent
- Swissmedic swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 (~15 months)
- EU AI Act medical device extension August 2027 (indirect relevance for Swiss practices using EU-market tools)
- CoE AI Convention implementation expected end-2026; healthcare amendments will add transparency + human oversight obligations

### 2. AI Governance Gap Is Quantified — 42% Lack Formal Policies
- MGMA (January 2026): only 42% of Swiss medical groups have formal AI governance policies or AI use guidelines
- Structural governance gap confirmed — this is not a niche observation
- Market urgency is real: majority of Swiss medical groups are underserved on AI governance
- FDPIC enforcement beginning → gap between AI use and governance is now also a legal risk

### 3. Swiss Institutional AI Adoption Signals Peer Pressure on Small Practices
- CHUV (Lausanne University Hospital) testing Meditron, a Swiss medical LLM, in emergency room from May 2026
- Meditron: open-source Swiss medical AI model; signals Swiss AI sovereignty direction
- Institutional AI adoption in Vaud accelerating → small practices will face same governance questions
- Gap between CHUV-level AI governance and small practice governance = advisory market opportunity
- HUG (Geneva) similarly positioned; small practices follow institutional leads

### 4. Competitive Gap Confirmed 4th Consecutive Cycle
- Named competitors identified: Lucendra, Congenius, st-consulting, AlpineAI, Swiss Cyber Institute, HealthAI — all different segments
- None serve: small Geneva or Vaud medical practice AI governance advisory (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff)
- Swiss Cyber Institute is the closest in AI governance positioning, but enterprise pricing + hospital/enterprise focus
- First-mover window: open and unclaimed; confirmed 4 consecutive cycles

### 5. Geneva Positioning Events Create Concrete Windows
- AI for Good Global Summit 2026: July 7-10, 2026 (exact dates confirmed) — Geneva global AI hub
- Geneva Health Forum 2026: May 18–22 and November 10–12 — healthcare professional community reach
- No Geneva boutique advisory has used these events for content positioning

### 6. Market Is Substantial and Structurally Stressed
- ~1,000–1,400 addressable Geneva medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff)
- ~2,000–2,500 addressable Vaud practices (Lausanne anchor)
- Nearly 40% of Geneva doctors aged 60-64 plan retirement at 65 (AMGe 2023)
- Medical assistant shortage: Geneva highest wages in Switzerland — structural crisis
- Clause du besoin July 2025: efficiency investment = succession planning
- Federal Council confirmed Feb 2025: no comprehensive AI law — sector-specific is operative standard through 2026+

## ICP Summary

> **EN:** Geneva and Vaud medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff) led by experienced physicians aged 45–65, facing a structural staffing crisis as nearly 40% of Geneva doctors plan retirement within 5 years (AMGe 2023), struggling with administrative overload, medical assistant shortages (Geneva highest wages in CH), patient data security, and AI adoption decisions that affect patient privacy, clinical liability, and practice efficiency. FDPIC's 2024/2025 Annual Report (Jul 2025) confirms nFADP enforcement has begun — healthcare data breaches are a priority. Only 42% of Swiss medical groups have formal AI governance policies (MGMA Jan 2026). swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 adds device-level compliance urgency. No clear trusted advisor exists for small medical practice AI governance in Geneva or Vaud.

> **FR:** Cabinets médicaux de Genève et de Vaud (1 à 8 praticiens, 2 à 15 collaborateurs), dirigés par des médecins expérimentés âgés de 45 à 65 ans, confrontés à une crise structurelle du personnel alors que près de 40% des médecins genevois prévoient de prendre leur retraite dans les 5 ans (AMGe 2023), et qui luttent contre la surcharge administrative, la pénurie d'assistantes médicales (salaires genevois les plus élevés de Suisse), la sécurité des données patients et les décisions d'adoption de l'IA. Le rapport annuel 2024/2025 du PFPDT (juillet 2025) confirme que l'application de la nFADP a commencé — les violations de données de santé sont une priorité. Seulement 42% des groupes médicaux suisses disposent de politiques formelles de gouvernance de l'IA (MGMA, jan. 2026). Aucun conseiller de confiance n'existe pour la gouvernance de l'IA des petits cabinets médicaux à Genève ou Vaud.
