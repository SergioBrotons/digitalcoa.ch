## Executive Summary: Geneva Legal AI Governance — Phase 01 Cycle 3

### Headline Findings

**1. The regulatory picture is now clear — and urgent.**
The FDPIC issued explicit AI+nFADP guidance on May 8, 2025. The Swiss Bar Association issued AI Guidelines in June 2024. The Federal Council announced its AI strategy in February 2025 and signed the Council of Europe AI Convention in March 2025. Geneva attorneys now face a defined standard of care: nFADP + SBA Guidelines = the operative framework for AI governance through 2026.

**2. No boutique competitor serves small Geneva law firms.**
Research across all four verticals confirms: no Geneva advisory targets small law firms (1–8 attorneys) specifically for AI governance. Large Geneva firms (Gilliéron, CROCE, Quinze) serve mid-to-large clients. Associations provide guidance without hands-on delivery. Tool vendors sell tools, not governance frameworks. DigitalCoa.ch's first-mover position is intact.

**3. The three-decision framework is validated by two regulators.**
The lead magnet's Decision 1 (data classification), Decision 2 (data location), Decision 3 (client notification) is now directly supported by FDPIC May 2025 (nFADP obligations) and SBA June 2024 (professional conduct). The framework is not a marketing construct — it is a synthesis of regulatory requirements.

**4. The window is open; the timeline is now.**
Draft CoE AI Convention implementation bill expected end of 2026. After that: new obligations will emerge. The firms that establish AI governance practices in 2025–2026 will be ahead. The firms that wait for the 2026 law will be playing catch-up.
