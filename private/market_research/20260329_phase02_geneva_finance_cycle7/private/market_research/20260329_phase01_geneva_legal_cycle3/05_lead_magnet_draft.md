## Document Architecture

### Opening Section: The Moment of Clarity (Cycle 3 Updated)

**Context paragraph:**
Two authorities have now clarified what Geneva attorneys must do before using AI tools with client data:

1. **The FDPIC (May 8, 2025)** — Switzerland's data protection authority confirmed: nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing. If you're processing client personal data through an AI tool, you're subject to nFADP obligations.

2. **The Swiss Bar Association (June 2024)** — The SBA issued official AI Guidelines: professional secrecy applies to AI tool use, and attorneys must maintain control over and accountability for AI-assisted work product.

These two documents create a clear standard of care. This framework walks you through the three decisions you need to make before using any AI tool.

*(FR: Deux autorités ont désormais clarifié ce que les avocats genevois doivent faire avant d'utiliser des outils d'IA avec les données de leurs clients : le PFPDT (8 mai 2025) et la SBA (juin 2024). Ce cadre vous guide à travers les trois décisions à prendre.)*

### Decision 1: What Category Does Your Client's Data Fall Into?

**The question:** Are you processing sensitive personal data (Art. 5 nFADP)?

**The framework:**
- **Category A: Ordinary personal data** — contact details, billing information, general case information
  - Lawful basis required (typically consent or legitimate interest)
  - Transparency obligations apply (Art. 12)
  
- **Category B: Sensitive personal data (Art. 5(c))** — client's legal situation, financial information, health data (in legal contexts), personal circumstances relevant to litigation
  - Explicit consent required for processing
  - AI processing of case-related information almost always falls here
  - Heightened documentation requirements

**Why it matters for AI:** If you're putting client case data into an AI tool, you're processing sensitive data. Most AI tools process this data on external servers. This is Decision 2.

---

### Decision 2: Where Can That Data Go?

**The question:** What are your nFADP + professional secrecy obligations when using an AI tool?

**The framework:**
- **Internal tools only** — local installation, Swiss-hosted, no external data transmission
  - Lowest nFADP exposure
  - Best for sensitive case data
  - Higher cost, lower convenience
  
- **Cloud/SaaS tools with Swiss or EU hosting** — data processing agreement required
  - Art. 27 nFADP: data processing agreement mandatory
  - Must verify: server location, sub-processors, data retention, breach notification
  - Professional secrecy: attorney must verify tool vendor meets confidentiality standards

- **Cloud/SaaS tools with non-Swiss/non-EU hosting** — highest risk
  - Data transferred outside Switzerland: adequacy decision or standard contractual clauses required
  - FDPIC May 2025: extraterritorial AI processing still subject to nFADP if data subject is in Switzerland
  - Not recommended for sensitive client data without explicit consent + DPA

**Checklist for any AI tool:**
- [ ] Does the vendor have a data processing agreement?
- [ ] Where are the servers located?
- [ ] Who can access the data?
- [ ] How long is data retained?
- [ ] Can we request data deletion?
- [ ] Has the vendor had a data breach? How was it handled?

---

### Decision 3: Do You Need to Notify Your Clients? (NEW — FDPIC May 2025 Reinforced)

**The question:** What does your transparency obligation require when using AI?

**The FDPIC position (May 2025):**
Data subjects have the right to know when their personal data is processed by automated systems, including AI. For attorneys, this means: clients should generally be informed when AI tools are used in their matter.

**The SBA position (June 2024):**
Professional secrecy obligations require that attorneys maintain client confidentiality and control over client information — including information generated or processed through AI tools.

**The practical answer:**
1. **Update engagement letters** — disclose AI tool use for the client's matter
2. **Obtain explicit consent** where required (sensitive data = Art. 5(c))
3. **Document AI use** — maintain records of what tools are used for which matters
4. **Review outputs** — attorney must review and take responsibility for AI-assisted work product

**Template language for engagement letter (Cycle 3):**
> *"Le Cabinet utilise des outils d'IA sélectionnés et évalués pour leur conformité aux obligations professionnelles et à la loi suisse sur la protection des données (nFADP). Les données relatives à votre dossier ne sont traitées que dans la mesure strictement nécessaire à la prestation de services法律. Vous pouvez vous opposer à l'utilisation de l'IA pour votre dossier en informant le Cabinet."*
