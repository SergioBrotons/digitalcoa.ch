## NEW: EU AI Act GPAI — Now Operative (Cycle 4 Addition)

### Status: August 2, 2025 — GPAI Rules Active
The EU AI Act's General Purpose AI (GPAI) rules **became operative on August 2, 2025**. This is not a future date — it is now active law for companies serving EU markets.

**What this means for Geneva consulting firms:**
- If a Geneva consulting firm has EU-based clients, they are already subject to GPAI obligations
- GPAI obligations include: technical documentation, transparency (including training data copyright), designing models to prevent illegal content generation
- Systemic risk GPAI models face enhanced obligations
- **Non-compliance penalties: €7.5M–35M or 1%–7% of global annual turnover**

### The Dual Compliance Challenge (Cycle 4 Addition)
Geneva consulting firms with EU clients now face **two regulatory frameworks simultaneously**:

| Framework | Status | Geographic Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **EU AI Act (GPAI)** | Operative since August 2, 2025 | Extraterritorial — applies to Swiss companies serving EU markets |
| **Swiss nFADP** | In force since September 2023; FDPIC enforcement active | Switzerland |

**This dual compliance challenge is the new market reality for Geneva consulting firms — and it is not being addressed by any boutique advisor.**

## Swiss Federal Council 2026 AI Action Plan (Cycle 4 Addition)

### Key Elements
- **Sector-specific approach**: No horizontal AI act (unlike EU AI Act). Switzerland opts for targeted amendments to existing laws.
- **FDJP consultation draft**: End of 2026 — will define scope of transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight requirements
- **Non-binding measures**: Industry solutions and voluntary commitment declarations — federal administration developing implementation plan by end of 2026
- **Federal Administration AI sub-strategy 2026–2030**: Skills, trust, efficiency — signals what "good AI governance" looks like in Swiss context
- **Political framing**: "Innovation-friendly framework without excessive regulatory burden" — Geneva business community will respond positively to this framing

### Implication for Advisory Window
The FDJP consultation draft doesn't arrive until end of 2026. The period **2026 through early 2027** is the advisory opportunity window — before Swiss law hardens. After the consultation and parliamentary ratification, the landscape will be more defined and potentially more restrictive.

**The window is open now. It closes when Swiss law catches up.**
