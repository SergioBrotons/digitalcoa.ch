## NEW: EU AI Act GPAI — Competitive Dynamics (Cycle 4 Addition)

### GPAI Operative Since August 2, 2025: What Changed
The EU AI Act's GPAI rules are now **operative**. This creates a new competitive dynamic:

**GPAI creates demand for compliance advisory** — but who is serving small Geneva consulting firms?
- Big Four (KPMG, PwC, Protiviti, BearingPoint) offer EU AI Act compliance — but at enterprise pricing
- They target mid-market and large clients — not small boutiques
- **The GPAI advisory gap at small firm level is unaddressed**

**GPAI systemic risk models** — the highest obligation tier:
- Only applies to the most powerful GPAI models (e.g., frontier models from OpenAI, Google, Anthropic)
- Not directly relevant to small consulting firm advisory
- But: documentation and transparency requirements cascade down to users

**Competitive implication:** The GPAI operative date creates a new trigger conversation — "are your EU clients asking about your AI compliance?" This is a sales entry point that wasn't available 6 months ago.

## IT & Technology Consultants — Highest GPAI + nFADP Exposure (Cycle 4 Update)

**Cycle 3 identified this sub-segment. Cycle 4 sharpens the GPAI angle:**

- IT/technology consultants (~25% of ICP addressable market) are already AI-adjacent
- **GPAI operative since August 2025**: If IT consultants are building or deploying AI-powered solutions for EU clients, they face GPAI obligations directly
- High likelihood of using AI tools extensively in client delivery
- Often send client data (code, architectures, requirements) to AI tools
- **Dual exposure**: EU AI Act GPAI (if serving EU clients) + Swiss nFADP
- May be the most responsive sub-segment to AI governance advisory — they understand technology risk, they need to understand regulatory risk

**Implication for DigitalCoa.ch:** The IT/technology consulting sub-segment is the fastest-converting lead within Phase 03's ICP. Frame the conversation as: "you're already doing this — here's the compliance layer, and here's why it matters for your EU clients right now."

## Competitive Summary (Cycle 4)

### Who is winning?
- AI **tool vendors** are winning at individual consultant level (personal productivity)
- **Big Four + large boutiques** (KPMG, PwC, Protiviti, BearingPoint) are winning at enterprise level
- **No clear winner** has emerged for firm-level AI strategy and governance for small boutiques in Geneva
- **GPAI advisory gap** at small firm level is unaddressed — new competitive opportunity

### The Gap (Confirmed in Phase 05 + 4 Consecutive Cycles)
There is no established Swiss/Geneva boutique advisory firm explicitly positioning around AI governance for small consulting firms. The named competitors (KPMG, PwC, Protiviti, BearingPoint) target mid-market and large clients. Specialized nFADP firms (LEXR, Swiss Cyber Institute, Swiss Infosec AG) focus on compliance tools, DPO services, or training — not AI governance advisory for consulting firms. The EU AI Act GPAI operative status creates a new gap: no one is addressing GPAI compliance for small Geneva consulting firms with EU clients.

**First-mover window is open — and confirmed by named competitor analysis across 4 cycles.**
