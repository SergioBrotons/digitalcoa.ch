# Phase 03 Geneva Consulting — ICP (Cycle 5)
## Target Vertical: Small Consulting Firms (2–15 Consultants), Geneva

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** 2–15 consultants; micro-firm to small boutique
- **Location:** Geneva (emerging clusters: Praille-Acacias-Choin, Jonction, Perle du Lac); home office increasingly common post-pandemic
- **Structure:** Independent strategy, management, HR, finance, IT, or marketing consultants; increasingly: freelancers with Sàrl orähn harness structure
- **Revenue model:** Daily rate (CHF 800–2,500/day) or project fee; retainer for ongoing clients

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary decision-maker:** Founder / Managing Partner
- **Secondary:** Senior consultants (if 5+ staff)
- **Decision dynamic:** Founder-driven; informal; fast
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for client delivery risk; moderate for technology adoption

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Laptop + cloud suite (Google Workspace or Microsoft 365); video (Zoom, Teams); CRM (HubSpot, Pipedrive, or Excel); AI use increasingly personal and ad hoc
- **AI exposure:** High — consulting = information work = prime AI use case; research, analysis, presentation drafting, client communication, proposal writing
- **Technology trust:** High for productivity; low for client deliverable integrity without human review
- **IT support:** Often none; founder manages; occasional external IT

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **EU AI Act GPAI** — OPERATIVE since August 2, 2025; GPAI obligations NOW active for Geneva consulting firms with EU clients or EU-market AI tools
2. **nFADP** — operative since September 2023; AI processing of client data = Art. 5(c) + DPIA Art. 22
3. **Federal Council 2026 AI action plan** — sector-specific AI governance framework in development
4. **FDJP CoE AI Convention consultation** — end-2026; will define Geneva consulting AI governance standards
5. **Dual compliance** — EU AI Act + nFADP = operational reality for consulting firms with EU exposure

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Client requirement or EU AI Act compliance deadline (consulting firms with EU MNC clients will face contractual AI governance requirements)
- **Language:** French or bilingual EN/FR; English-only for international consulting
- **Trust marker:** Swiss-specific regulatory reference (FDPIC, FDJP) essential for credibility; EU AI Act reference for internationally-oriented firms
- **Objection:** "We're just advising — AI is our tool, not our product" — rebuttal: AI tool use = data processing = nFADP obligations regardless of what you're delivering
- **Sales cycle:** 4–10 weeks; Geneva consulting community is close-knit; referral-based trust is key
- **Budget:** CHF 2,000–10,000 for initial AI governance framework; retainer for ongoing monitoring
