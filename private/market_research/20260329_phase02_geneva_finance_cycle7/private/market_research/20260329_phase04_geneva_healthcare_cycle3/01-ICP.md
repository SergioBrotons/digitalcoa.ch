## ICP Summary

> **EN:** Geneva medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff) led by experienced physicians aged 45–65, facing a structural staffing crisis as nearly 40% of doctors plan retirement within 5 years (AMGe 2023), struggling with administrative overload, medical assistant shortages (Geneva highest wages in CH), patient data security, and AI adoption decisions that affect patient privacy, clinical liability, and practice efficiency. The "clause du besoin" (need clause) effective July 2025 makes every remaining staff member more critical. Federal Council February 2025 confirmed no comprehensive AI law — sector-specific regulation means nFADP + Swissmedic remain the operative standard. Swissmedic swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 adds device-level compliance urgency. No clear trusted advisor exists for small medical practice AI governance in Geneva.

> **FR:** Cabinets médicaux genevois (1 à 8 praticiens, 2 à 15 collaborateurs), dirigés par des médecins expérimentés âgés de 45 à 65 ans, confrontés à une crise structurelle du personnel alors que près de 40% des médecins genevois prévoient de prendre leur retraite dans les 5 ans (AMGe 2023), et qui luttent contre la surcharge administrative, la pénurie d'assistantes médicales (salaires genevois les plus élevés de Suisse), la sécurité des données patients et les décisions d'adoption de l'IA qui affectent la confidentialité, la responsabilité clinique et l'efficacité du cabinet. La « clause du besoin » en vigueur depuis juillet 2025 rend chaque membre du personnel plus précieux. Le Conseil fédéral a confirmé en février 2025 qu'il n'y aura pas de loi exhaustive sur l'IA — l'approche sectorielle signifie que la nFADP + Swissmedic restent la norme applicable. L'enregistrement obligatoire swissdamed de Swissmedic dès le 1er juillet 2026 ajoute une urgence de conformité au niveau des dispositifs médicaux. Aucun conseiller de confiance n'existe pour la gouvernance de l'IA des petits cabinets médicaux à Genève.

## Psychographics

### Core Physician Beliefs
1. "My patients' data must be protected — I take this seriously"
2. "I want to adopt AI to reduce admin burden, but I don't know what's safe"
3. "My staff is my most precious resource — I can't afford to lose anyone"
4. "The regulations are changing and I need to understand what's required"
5. "I trust peer recommendations more than marketing"
6. "I don't have time for lengthy consulting engagements — give me practical clarity"

### Technology Adoption Stage
- Early majority (approaching majority) — not leading edge, not laggard
- Personal AI use: ChatGPT, Dictation (Dragon Medical), Doctolib scheduling
- Firm-level AI governance: Mostly absent
- AI tool evaluation: Vendor-driven; physicians lack internal framework to assess
- Awareness of nFADP: Moderate (general data protection awareness); specific AI implications: Low

### Decision Triggers
1. **Staff departure** — losing a medical assistant forces immediate efficiency evaluation
2. **Regulatory signal** — FDPIC enforcement, Swissmedic notice, cantonal audit
3. **Peer behavior** — another physician's experience or incident
4. **GDPR-level incident** — high-profile Swiss or EU case raises awareness
5. **swissdamed registration** — July 2026 deadline creates urgency for AI tool users
6. **Retirement planning** — efficiency investment as succession preparation

## Pain Points

### Top 5 Pain Points (Ranked)

1. **Administrative overload** — physicians spend 40–50% of time on admin; AI promises relief but implementation is risky
2. **Medical assistant shortage** — Geneva has highest medical assistant wages in Switzerland; retention crisis is structural (AMGe confirmed)
3. **Patient data security** — nFADP Art. 5(c) sensitive data; FDPIC enforcement risk; reputational risk from breach
4. **AI tool evaluation** — no trusted framework to assess which AI tools are compliant, safe, effective
5. **Regulatory uncertainty** — Federal Council Feb 2025 confirmed no comprehensive AI law; sector-specific rules evolve; physicians unsure what applies

### Secondary Pain Points
- **Professional liability** — who is liable when AI-assisted decision is wrong?
- **Swissmedic device classification** — is the AI tool I use a "medical device" requiring registration?
- **Staff training** — how to bring team along on AI adoption without risk
- **Cost vs. benefit clarity** — what is a reasonable investment in AI governance?
- **Data quality** — AI systems need good data; small practices have fragmented records
- **Legacy IT infrastructure** — outdated practice management systems don't integrate with modern AI
