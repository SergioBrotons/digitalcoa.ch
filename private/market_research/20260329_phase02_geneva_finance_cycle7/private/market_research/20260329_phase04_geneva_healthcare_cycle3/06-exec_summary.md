## Headline Findings

### 1. Healthcare Is the Strongest nFADP Vertical (Confirmed + Deepened)
- nFADP Art. 5(c): health data = "sensitive" — highest protection category; FDPIC enforcement risk is the highest of all 4 verticals
- FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit: nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing — healthcare data processing by AI tools is squarely covered
- FDPIC prioritizes health data breach enforcement
- Swissmedic swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 adds concrete device-level compliance deadline
- EU AI Act medical device extension August 2027 (indirect relevance for Swiss practices using EU-market tools)
- CoE AI Convention implementation expected end-2026; healthcare amendments will add transparency + human oversight obligations

### 2. Competitive Gap Confirmed with Named Evidence
- Named competitors identified: Lucendra, Congenius, st-consulting (MedTech/hospital segment), AlpineAI (AI tool vendor), Swiss Cyber Institute (enterprise), HealthAI (international/government)
- None serve: small Geneva medical practice AI governance advisory (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff)
- Swiss Cyber Institute is the closest in AI governance positioning, but enterprise pricing + hospital/enterprise focus
- First-mover window: open and unclaimed

### 3. Geneva Healthcare Events Create Positioning Opportunity
- ITU AI for Good Global Summit: Geneva July 2025/2026 — global AI governance hub positioning
- Geneva Health Forum 2026: May 18–22 and November 10–12 — healthcare professional community reach
- No Geneva boutique advisory has used these events for content positioning

### 4. Swiss French Region (Vaud) Extension Confirmed
- Canton Vaud: ~830,000 residents, ~3,600 physicians, ~2,000–2,500 addressable practices
- Lausanne = 2nd-largest healthcare hub in French-speaking Switzerland
- Same regulatory stack (nFADP + cantonal health law + Swissmedic); CMPR analogous to AMGe
- Phase 2 natural expansion target; content transferable with minor localization

### 5. Market Is Substantial and Structurally Stressed
- ~1,000–1,400 addressable Geneva medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff)
- Nearly 40% of Geneva doctors aged 60-64 plan retirement at 65 (AMGe 2023)
- Medical assistant shortage: Geneva highest wages in Switzerland — structural crisis
- Clause du besoin July 2025: efficiency investment = succession planning
- Federal Council confirmed Feb 2025: no comprehensive AI law — sector-specific is operative standard through 2026+

## ICP Summary

> **EN:** Geneva medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff) led by experienced physicians aged 45–65, facing a structural staffing crisis as nearly 40% of doctors plan retirement within 5 years (AMGe 2023), struggling with administrative overload, medical assistant shortages, patient data security, and AI adoption decisions that affect patient privacy, clinical liability, and practice efficiency. The "clause du besoin" (need clause) effective July 2025 makes every remaining staff member more critical. FDPIC May 8, 2025 confirmed nFADP applies directly to AI-based processing. swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 adds device-level compliance urgency. No clear trusted advisor exists for small medical practice AI governance in Geneva.

> **FR:** Cabinets médicaux genevois (1 à 8 praticiens, 2 à 15 collaborateurs), dirigés par des médecins expérimentés âgés de 45 à 65 ans, confrontés à une crise structurelle du personnel alors que près de 40% des médecins genevois prévoient de prendre leur retraite dans les 5 ans (AMGe 2023), et qui luttent contre la surcharge administrative, la pénurie d'assistantes médicales, la sécurité des données patients et les décisions d'adoption de l'IA qui affectent la confidentialité, la responsabilité clinique et l'efficacité du cabinet. La « clause du besoin » en vigueur depuis juillet 2025 rend chaque membre du personnel plus précieux. Le PFPDT a confirmé en mai 2025 que la nFADP s'applique directement au traitement de données par l'IA. L'enregistrement obligatoire swissdamed dès le 1er juillet 2026 ajoute une urgence de conformité. Aucun conseiller de confiance n'existe pour la gouvernance de l'IA des petits cabinets médicaux à Genève.
