### SECTION 1: Geneva Boutique WM AI Reality Check
**Hook:** Your wealth management firm is likely using AI already — for portfolio suggestions, client communication drafting, risk reporting, or CRM intelligence. The question is not whether you use AI, but whether your AI use is compliant with Swiss and international regulations.

- CARF/CRS 2.0 operative January 1, 2026 — reporting obligations already active
- Revised AMLA operative mid-2026 — KYC/AML enhanced obligations hit in ~3 months
- LETA H2 2026 — cross-border legal assistance obligations for international client bases
- FINMA Circ. 2026/01 (nature risks) operative January 1, 2026 — ESG risk disclosure required
- nFADP operative since September 2023 — AI processing of personal financial data requires DPIA
- EU AI Act high-risk obligations August 2, 2026 — ~15 months for WMs with EU nexus

**Standfirst:** *"You have at least three regulatory AI deadlines converging in 2026. AMLA hits mid-year. LETA follows in H2. The EU AI Act high-risk deadline is August 2, 2026. Is your firm's AI governance framework ready for any of them?"*

### SECTION 3: AI Tool Inventory — The Starting Point
**Before any compliance work: know what you're running.**

Create a written inventory of every AI tool used by your firm:
- Portfolio construction or risk analytics AI
- Client communication or reporting AI (email drafting, performance reports)
- CRM intelligence or client data AI
- Third-party platform AI (your core banking or WM platform's embedded AI)
- Personal device AI use by RMs or staff (often untracked)

For each tool: name, purpose, data access scope, provider jurisdiction, EU market nexus.

### SECTION 4: AMLA + nFADP — The KYC/AI Intersection
**Revised AMLA operative mid-2026: Enhanced KYC obligations**

Key questions for AI governance:
- Is AI used in client onboarding or KYC verification? → AMLA + nFADP dual obligations
- Is AI used in ongoing transaction monitoring? → AMLA + nFADP dual obligations
- Is AI used in beneficial ownership verification? → AMLA + nFADP dual obligations

If AI processes personal identity data or financial transaction data for AML purposes:
1. Establish lawful basis under nFADP Art. 6
2. Conduct DPIA under nFADP Art. 22
3. Document AMLAI-specific controls (FINMA guidance emerging)

---

### SECTION 5: CARF/CRS 2.0 + LETA — The Data Governance Layer
**CARF/CRS 2.0 already operative (January 1, 2026)**

AI systems processing CARF-reportable financial account data:
- Must have documented data governance controls
- Must ensure data accuracy and completeness (AI hallucination risk = CARF accuracy risk)
- Must have audit trail for regulatory inspection

**LETA H2 2026 operative**

Geneva WMs with international client bases: LETA governs cross-border information requests.
AI systems processing client data potentially subject to LETA procedures need:
- Legal hold and data retention protocols
- Cross-border data transfer documentation
- Client consent management under nFADP Art. 8–13

---

### SECTION 6: EU AI Act — The August 2, 2026 Deadline
**For Geneva WMs with EU market nexus**

If your WM uses AI systems with:
- EU-based data centers or API endpoints
- AI outputs intended for EU clients or EU-listed products
- AI acting as EU importer/distributor of financial services

You face EU AI Act high-risk obligations by August 2, 2026.

**AI governance requirements for high-risk systems:**
- Transparency obligations (AI-generated output must be disclosed)
- Human oversight requirements
- Accuracy and robustness standards
- Non-discrimination (AI-driven credit/portfolio allocation must not discriminate)

**The Geneva WM unique exposure:** Most Geneva boutique WMs serve international UHNWI clients with EU connections. The probability of EU AI Act nexus is higher than a domestic WM context would suggest.
