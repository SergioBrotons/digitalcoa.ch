## Competitive Positioning Summary (Cycle 2 baseline + Cycle 3 update)

**No boutique competitor exists specifically serving 2–20 person Geneva WM firms on AI governance.** This was confirmed in Cycle 2 and has not changed in Cycle 3.

The competitive field consists of:
1. **Large global advisory firms** (Big 4, strategy consultancies) — wrong scale, wrong price point
2. **WM-specific SaaS tool vendors** (Domalys, QBIS) — operational tooling, not governance advisory
3. **Salesforce FSC / HubSpot CRM** — operational CRM, not AI governance
4. **Generic Swiss compliance consultants** — regulatory box-ticking, not AI-native

**DigitalCoa.ch opportunity:** Position as the Geneva boutique WM firm's trusted AI governance advisor — proportionate, Swiss-native, understand the personal relationship brand, and aware of FINMA expectations.

## Cycle 2 Competitive Findings (carried forward)

### Domalys
- Swiss wealth management SaaS platform
- Client lifecycle management, reporting, document management
- Does NOT address AI governance
- Competitive only in adjacent operational tooling space

### QBIS
- Portfolio management system for WM
- Swiss-focused; institutional and boutique
- Does NOT address AI governance or AI tool due diligence
- Competitive only as operational alternative

### Salesforce FSC (Financial Services Cloud)
- Large-scale CRM + advisory workflow
- Enterprise price point; implementation complexity
- Does NOT address proportionate AI governance for boutiques
- Competitive for large WM; not for micro-boutiques

### HubSpot
- General CRM with WM use cases
- No Swiss-specific regulatory integration
- Not positioned as AI governance solution

## Differentiation Options

### Option A: ISO 42001 + nFADP Certification Path
- Propose ISO 42001 (AI management system) + nFADP compliance as dual certification
- Differentiator: No Swiss boutique advisor is offering this stack
- Audience: WM firms wanting a "certifiable" framework rather than advisory opinion

### Option B: FINMA-Proportionate AI Governance Framework
- Develop a FINMA Guidance 08/2024-aligned framework specifically for boutique WM
- Proportionality is key: "appropriate to your size and complexity"
- Differentiator: Understands WM discretion culture; not generic compliance

### Option C: Vendor Due Diligence Service
- Help WM firms evaluate AI tool vendors (Domalys, QBIS, others) against FINMA + nFADP requirements
- Practical, operational entry point that surfaces governance gaps
- Differentiator: Most consultants either do governance OR tooling — not both
