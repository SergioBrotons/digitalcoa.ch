## Concept Rationale

### Why This Format Works for WM
- **Checklist format** is low-commitment; time-poor founders can scan quickly
- **Self-assessment** surfaces gaps without requiring a sales conversation
- **Regulatory framing** (FINMA + nFADP) creates urgency and external validation
- **Proportionality message** ("appropriate to your scale") addresses the boutique anxiety about enterprise frameworks
- **Dual obligation framing** (FINMA + nFADP) is novel — most WM firms know FINMA but may not connect AI to nFADP

### What the Checklist Should Cover
1. AI tool inventory — what AI does your firm use, on what data, for what purposes?
2. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 self-assessment — governance, vendor due diligence, documentation
3. nFADP obligations for AI-based data processing — ROPA, DPIA, Art. 26 disclosure, data agreements
4. Vendor due diligence — are your AI SaaS providers' contracts nFADP-compliant?
5. Human oversight — can you explain and reproduce every AI-assisted decision?
6. Breach response — do you have a 72-hour FDPIC breach notification protocol?

### CTA Alignment
- CTA: "Get the full AI Governance Readiness Assessment + a 30-minute debrief" → consultation booking
- Bilingual framing: EN primary (WM internationally oriented), FR for Geneva credibility
- From Phase 05 synthesis: consistent structure across 4 verticals (Legal, WM, Consulting, Healthcare)

## Lead Magnet Structure (Draft Outline)

### Title
**The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist**
*Are You Compliant with FINMA Guidance 08/2024 and Switzerland's nFADP?*

### Subtitle / Hook
*For Geneva-based wealth management firms with 2–20 employees and CHF 50M–2B AUM*

### Section 1: Why This Checklist Matters Now
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 2024) — what it means for boutique WM
- FDPIC clarification (May 2025) — nFADP applies to AI-based data processing
- The 91% problem: 91% of Swiss FIs using external AI SaaS; vendor due diligence is the blind spot

### Section 2: AI Governance Readiness Checklist
**Part A — AI Inventory**
- [ ] Do you have a written inventory of all AI tools used in your firm?
- [ ] Do you know what client data each AI tool processes?
- [ ] Do you have contracts (DPAs) with each AI vendor?

**Part B — FINMA Guidance 08/2024 Self-Assessment**
- [ ] Does your firm have a governance policy for AI use?
- [ ] Is there a named person responsible for AI oversight?
- [ ] Can you demonstrate human review of AI-assisted decisions?
- [ ] Do you have documentation for your AI risk assessment?

**Part C — nFADP Obligations**
- [ ] Have you completed a ROPA (Record of Processing Activities) that includes AI processing?
- [ ] Have you conducted a DPIA for high-risk AI processing?
- [ ] Can you demonstrate lawful basis (consent or Art. 5(c) legitimate interest) for AI data processing?
- [ ] Do you have 72-hour breach notification protocol in place?
- [ ] Do EU-client records comply with GDPR + nFADP dual requirements?

**Part D — Vendor Due Diligence**
- [ ] Have you reviewed your AI vendor contracts for nFADP Art. 9 compliance?
- [ ] Do your AI vendors provide incident notification within your breach response window?
- [ ] Have you assessed AI vendor sub-processors?

### Section 3: What a Proportionate Framework Looks Like for a Boutique WM Firm
- Minimum viable AI governance: 5 essential elements
- ISO 42001 + nFADP dual certification path
- Timeline: What to do in the next 30 / 60 / 90 days

### Section 4: Next Steps
- Download the full assessment framework
- Book a 30-minute AI governance readiness debrief (no-cost, no-obligation)
- FR: *Planifier un bilan de maturité IA* (30 minutes, offert)

### Compliance Note
*This checklist is for informational purposes and does not constitute legal or regulatory advice. Geneva WM firms should consult a qualified Swiss regulatory advisor for their specific obligations under FINMA Guidance 08/2024 and the nFADP.*
