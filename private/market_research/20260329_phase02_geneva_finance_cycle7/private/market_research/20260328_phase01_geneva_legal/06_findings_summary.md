# Phase 01 Findings Summary

## Geneva Legal — Small Law Firms
## Research Date: 2026-03-28
## Status: COMPLETE

## Key Insights

### ICP Language Patterns
1. Fear-driven: "Nous avons peur" (confidentiality, professional secrecy)
2. Uncertainty: "Nous ne savons pas", "On n'est pas sûrs"
3. Barrier-focused: "Nous hésitons à cause de...", "Le secret professionnel nous empêche"
4. Outcome-seeking: "Comment utiliser l'IA sans risquer..."
5. Trigger language: "concrètement", "par où commencer", "en pratique"

### Market Entry Signals (Triggers)
1. OAG training modules — attendees are already aware and seeking answers
2. AI incident in the news (fake precedents, data breaches) triggers anxiety
3. When a client asks "why aren't you using AI?"
4. Small firms feel they can't afford to get it wrong
5. Pressure to show efficiency gains as client expectations evolve

### Gap in Current Market Offering
1. No one speaks to the "I want AI benefits but I'm terrified of the risks" attorney
2. Formal governance frameworks are designed for large firms, not 2-5 attorney shops
3. The question "which AI can I safely use for which client data?" has no practical answer for small firms
4. Internal AI policies needed but no template adapted to small practice reality

### Lead Magnet: COMPLETE
**Title:** "The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question: A Decision Framework for Geneva Attorneys Who Want AI Benefits Without the Risk"

**Core argument:** The problem isn't "how to use AI." It's "how to make decisions about AI when you're responsible for client confidentiality and have no compliance department."

**Structure:** 3 variables that matter + 5-step decision process + positioning as governance architect

### Ready-to-Use Language
1. "Nous hésitons à cause du secret professionnel"
2. "On ne sait pas par où commencer"
3. "Comment utiliser l'IA sans risquer une violation du secret professionnel?"
4. "Nos clients nous font confiance, on ne peut pas se permettre une erreur"
5. "En pratique, comment on fait?"
