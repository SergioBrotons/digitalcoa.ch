# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal AI Space

## Phase: 01 — Geneva Legal
## Last Updated: 2026-03-28 (recreated)

## What Others in This Space Are Saying
- Most AI marketing to lawyers focuses on efficiency gains ("save time", "automate research")
- Generic productivity tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) being marketed without compliance framing
- Swiss-specific tools emerging: Silex, Ailegis, Omnilex, DeepLaw, Swiss-Noxtua, Genie AI
- Training courses from OAG and private providers (but focused on "how to use AI", not "how to use AI safely")
- Decision framework exists in theory (90-day pilot, DPIAs, governance committees) but NOT accessible to small firms

## What They're Getting Wrong (The Gap)
- Positioning AI as a "time saver" without addressing the core fear: confidentiality
- Tools marketed as "compliant" without clear definition of what that means for small firms
- Training that teaches tool usage, not governance thinking
- No one is speaking to the attorney who wants AI benefits but is paralyzed by risk
- The formal governance frameworks (DPIA, AI committee, 90-day roadmap) are designed for large firms, not 2-5 attorney shops

## How Attorneys Are Currently Solving This
- Some have banned ChatGPT entirely
- Others using personal accounts (shadow IT) without firm knowledge
- Waiting for clearer regulations before acting
- Attempting to use AI tools without understanding data flows
- Downloading AI tools without internal policies
- Assuming "Swiss-hosted" = "compliant" without verification

## What No One Is Talking About
- The decision framework for a 2-5 attorney firm (not a 50-attorney firm)
- "Which AI can I safely use with which type of client data?" — the risk matrix
- How to build internal AI policies when you have no compliance team
- The 90-day pilot roadmap adapted for a 3-attorney firm with no IT support
- The human factor: "How do we maintain judgment while using AI tools?"

## Notes
The competitive landscape is focused on either: (1) selling AI tools, or (2) teaching AI skills. No one addresses the decision-making gap for small firms. This is Sergio's exact entry point.
