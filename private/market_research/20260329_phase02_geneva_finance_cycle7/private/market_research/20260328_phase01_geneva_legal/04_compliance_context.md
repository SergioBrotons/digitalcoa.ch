# Compliance Context: Geneva / Switzerland Legal AI

## Phase: 01 — Geneva Legal
## Last Updated: 2026-03-28 (recreated)

## Swiss Legal Confidentiality Framework
- Article 13 LLCA (Loi sur la profession d'avocat): secret professionnel obligatoire
- Article 4 CPC (Code de procédure civile): confidentialité des affaires
- Swiss Data Protection Act (LPD) révisée, en vigueur depuis 1er septembre 2023
- Switzerland ratified Council of Europe AI Convention (février 2025)
- Loi sur l'IA expected: avant-projet d'ici fin 2026

## Attorney-Client Privilege + Cloud AI
- Primary risk: outils d'IA génériques (ChatGPT etc.) envoient données à servers externes (souvent USA)
- Données pourraient être utilisées pour entraîner les modèles = violation du secret professionnel
- Attorneys remain responsible even when using third-party providers ("auxiliaire")
- Must ensure provider complies with FSA outsourcing recommendations
- 68% of Swiss law firms have banned ChatGPT for professional secrecy reasons

## GDPR / Swiss DPA Considerations
- LPD révisée impose: sécurité des données, transparence
- Données clients = données personnelles sensibles
- Externalisation informatique must follow FSA guidelines
- Solutions hébergées en Suisse ou UE preferred (garanties équivalentes)
- GDPR applies to Swiss firms targeting EU residents

## Bar Association Rules on Technology
- SAV/FSA Lignes directrices IA (juin 2024)
- CCBE guide on ethical use of generative AI (October 2025)
- OAG offre des modules de formation continue sur l'IA
- Key obligation: vérification systématique des résultats IA

## What Attorneys Legally Cannot Do With AI (Current)
- Upload client data to non-compliant cloud services
- Use AI output without human verification
- Rely on AI-generated precedents without confirmation
- Blindly trust AI for legal research (risque de "fictives jurisprudences")

## Geneva-Specific Regulatory Temperature
- Active development: Switzerland still building AI-specific law
- Existing framework: LPD + LLCA + bar guidelines
- Economic ties to EU = EU AI Act applies to Swiss firms doing business in EU
- August 2026: stricter EU AI Act rules come into effect

## Notes
The gap is clear: attorneys know they need to be careful, but they don't know what "safe" looks like in practice. They're asking for guidance.
