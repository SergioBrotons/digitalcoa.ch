## Active Regulatory Framework

### 1. Swissdamed (Switzerland Medical Device Database)
- **Operative:** July 1, 2026 (~3 months away)
- **Content:** Swissmedic registration for all medical devices including Software as a Medical Device (SaMD)
- **Small practice implication:** AI tools used in clinical workflow may qualify as SaMD:
  - AI diagnostic tools (imaging AI, symptom checkers)
  - AI scheduling and patient communication tools
  - AI EMR scribes and documentation tools
  - Remote patient monitoring AI
- **Registration requirement:** Practice must determine if AI tools are SaMD and register with Swissdamed before July 1, 2026
- **Enforcement:** Swissmedic; non-compliance = market withdrawal of unregistered SaMD

### 2. Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP)
- **Effective:** September 1, 2023
- **Healthcare relevance:** Patient data is sensitive personal data under nFADP Art. 3(c); highest protection standard
- **AI processing:** AI processing of patient records, scheduling, communication = Art. 5(c) + DPIA Art. 22
- **FDPIC enforcement:** Healthcare + legal = FDPIC priority sectors (FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report, July 2025)
- **DPIA requirement:** Mandatory before AI system use for patient data processing
- **FDPIC May 8, 2025:** Explicit — nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing

### 3. EU AI Act — SaMD Extraterritorial
- **GPAI operative:** August 2, 2025
- **High-risk obligations:** August 2, 2026
- **Healthcare SaMD relevance:** AI SaMD placed on EU market or with EU-intended output faces EU AI Act conformity assessment
- **Geneva implication:** Small practices using AI SaMD with EU market nexus (increasingly common) face dual Swiss + EU compliance
- **Dual compliance:** Swissdamed + nFADP + EU AI Act = three-layer healthcare AI compliance

- **Market implication:** CHUV adoption signals Swiss medical AI best practices; small practices will follow institutional lead
