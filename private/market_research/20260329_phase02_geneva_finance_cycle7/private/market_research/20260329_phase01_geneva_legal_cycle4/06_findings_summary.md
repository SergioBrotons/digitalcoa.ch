## Executive Summary: Geneva Legal AI Governance — Phase 01 Cycle 4

### Headline Findings (Cycle 4)

**1. Regulatory picture is stable — and sharpening.**
The FDJP is actively preparing the consultation draft for CoE AI Convention implementation (expected end-2026). Focus areas confirmed: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, and oversight in AI contexts. The operative framework (nFADP + SBA Guidelines + FDPIC May 2025) is unchanged and remains the standard through 2026.

**2. Swiss legal community is actively tracking.**
ANBLES AI Working Group Report (February 2026) confirms: Swiss legal professionals are not waiting passively. Attorneys following SBA publications and ANBLES work are already aware AI governance is a real obligation. The gap between aware attorneys and non-aware attorneys is widening — opportunity for DigitalCoa.ch increases as awareness grows.

**3. No boutique competitor for small Geneva law firms — confirmed.**
Four cycles of research confirm: no Geneva advisory targets small law firms (1–8 attorneys) specifically for AI governance. Large Geneva firms and international firms serve mid-to-large clients. ANBLES is a policy body, not an advisory service. Window is open and stable.

**4. ABA AI guidance (March 2026) — global signal.**
The American Bar Association published AI guidance in March 2026. While US-specific, this confirms: global regulatory norm for attorney AI use is accelerating. Geneva attorneys who attend international conferences or serve multinational clients are already feeling this pressure.

**5. First-mover window: 18–24 months before potential competitive entry.**
No immediate competitive threat. Window is structural (large firms won't pivot to small firm pricing; legaltech won't add governance advisory). Monitor for new boutique entrant through 2026.
