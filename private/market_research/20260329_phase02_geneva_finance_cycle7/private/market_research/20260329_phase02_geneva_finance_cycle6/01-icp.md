# Phase 02 Geneva Finance/SMB — ICP (Cycle 6)
## Target Vertical: Boutique Wealth Management, Geneva

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** CHF 50M–500M AUM; 2–15 relationship managers; 1–3 compliance/operations staff
- **Location:** Geneva (Rue du Rhône, La Ville, or emerging Perle du Lac / Champ-Baron area)
- **Structure:** Independent boutique WM (not tied to a large banking group); family offices (CHF 30M–200M AUM); external asset managers (EAM) affiliated with cantonal banks
- **Revenue model:** Management fee (AUM basis points) + performance fees; advisory mandates

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary:** CEO / Managing Partner / CIO
- **Secondary:** Chief Compliance Officer (if exists) or senior RM
- **Decision dynamic:** Owner-partner driven; small team; fast informal decisions possible
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for regulatory exposure; moderate for technology adoption

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Bloomberg Terminal, MSCI, RiskMetrics; core banking platform (Finantix, InvestGlass, Additiv, or legacy); CRM (Salesforce, custom)
- **AI exposure:** Emerging — portfolio construction suggestions, risk reporting, client communication drafting, CRM intelligence; often personal device AI use not firm-sanctioned
- **Technology trust:** High for data aggregation; moderate for AI advisory output
- **IT support:** Small internal team or external IT vendor

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **CARF/CRS 2.0** — operative January 1, 2026; already 3 months live; AI processing of CARF data = obligations already triggered
2. **FINMA Circ. 2025/02** — FinSA/FinSO conduct rules; client suitability, documentation
3. **Revised AMLA** — ~2-3 months away (mid-2026); KYC/AML enhanced obligations hit imminently
4. **LETA** — ~3-6 months away (H2 2026); cross-border legal assistance obligations
5. **FINMA Circ. 2026/01** — Nature-related financial risks; ESG integration; AI in ESG reporting = nFADP obligations
6. **AI governance** — nFADP applies; no boutique WM-specific framework exists in the market
7. **EU AI Act extraterritorial** — August 2, 2026 high-risk operative; WM AI systems with EU nexus exposed

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Regulatory deadline or peer activity (Geneva WM community is close-knit; peer adoption is strong signal)
- **Language:** French or bilingual EN/FR; English-only acceptable for international family offices
- **Trust marker:** FINMA reference, Swiss banking regulation credibility; Geneva WM community references
- **Objection:** "We have compliance covered" — typically means traditional AML/KYC, not AI governance
- **Sales cycle:** 6–16 weeks; Geneva WM relationships are trust-based; referral introduction is key
- **Budget:** CHF 3,000–15,000 initial AI governance compliance framework; retainer for ongoing monitoring
