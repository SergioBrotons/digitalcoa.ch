### 2. Market Opportunity — 67% AI Adoption (Highest of All 5 Verticals)

**Finding:** Geneva RE SMEs have the highest AI adoption rate of any vertical in the portfolio — 67% in Q1 2024 (likely 70%+ by Q1 2026).

This creates a specific advisory dynamic:
- The efficiency case for AI is proven — adoption advocates don't need convincing
- The regulatory awareness gap is structural — most RE agencies believe their AI vendor covers compliance
- The advisory entry point is retroactive: "You've already adopted AI. Are you compliant?"

**Addressable market:** ~250–500 small Geneva RE agencies (OFL-licensed, 1–10 employees); ~170–335 active AI users; estimated 5–15% near-term conversion = 5–37 potential clients in Geneva alone.

### 3. Lead Magnet Positioning — Retroactive Compliance (Unique Across Portfolio)

**Finding:** The Geneva RE lead magnet occupies a distinct position vs. other verticals.

| Vertical | Lead Magnet Framing |

The RE vertical is unique: the adoption decision is made; the question is whether the agency is meeting its nFADP obligations for what it already uses.

### 4. Regulatory Framework — nFADP + EU AI Act Dual Compliance

**Finding:** Geneva RE agencies face dual compliance burden (confirmed from Phase 05 Cycle 4 synthesis).

- **nFADP (Swiss domestic):** In force Sep 1, 2023; Art. 5(c) + Art. 22 DPIA applies to AI tenant screening, smart building, facial recognition
- **EU AI Act (extraterritorial):** August 2, 2026 high-risk operative; Swiss RE agencies with EU market nexus subject to both frameworks
- **CoE AI Convention:** Implementation end-2026; transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight obligations
- **Managing director personal liability (Art. 24):** Strong fear trigger for owner-managed agencies

### 5. GAIGI — Operative Institutional Context

**Finding:** Geneva AI Governance Institute (GAIGI) has been operative since Fall 2025 (~3–5 months as of March 2026).

- Category legitimacy increased — Geneva RE agencies increasingly aware of AI governance
- GAIGI serves enterprise/institutional clients — SMB gap confirmed
- Potential referral pathway: GAIGI clients redirected to SMB-focused advisors
- Geneva positions as global AI governance hub — non-overlapping with DigitalCoa.ch SMB positioning
