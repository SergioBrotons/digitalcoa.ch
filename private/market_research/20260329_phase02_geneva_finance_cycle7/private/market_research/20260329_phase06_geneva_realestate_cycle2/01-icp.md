### Pain Points

1. **"AI vendor told me I was covered"** — AI tool vendors typically operate as data processors; RE agency remains data controller with full nFADP obligations. Source of false confidence.

2. **"I didn't know I needed a DPIA for tenant screening"** — AI tenant screening tools process sensitive financial data; nFADP Art. 5(c) may apply; DPIA likely required before use.

3. **"Smart building systems came with the building — I didn't choose them"** — IoT/smart building systems process occupant behavior data; data controller liability rests with the property manager or agency.

4. **"AI valuations don't replace my expert accountability"** — AI valuations are aids; certified experts remain legally responsible for valuations. AI does not shift liability.

5. **"I don't have an IT department to handle this"** — Small agencies lack internal compliance resources; need a practical, proportionate framework.

6. **"Swissdamed is for healthcare — does it affect me?"** — Swissdamed (July 1, 2026) is healthcare-specific. RE agencies handling health-related tenant data (e.g., disability accommodations, medical notes) may have indirect exposure.

### nFADP Obligations Summary (RE-Specific)

| Obligation | Trigger | RE Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) — Sensitive data processing | Financial data, health data, biometric data | HIGH for RE — tenant screening, access control |
| DPIA — Data protection impact assessment | High-risk processing (Art. 22 nFADP) | Required for AI tenant screening, smart building, facial recognition |
| Art. 6 — Accountability | All processing | Managing director personally accountable |
| Art. 8 — Transparency | All data subjects | Tenants must be informed of AI use |
| Breach notification — "as soon as possible" | Any data breach | Cloud AI tool breaches = agency's notification obligation |
| Data processing agreements | AI tool vendors | RE agency must have DPAs with all AI vendors |
| Register of processing activities | All processing | Must document AI systems and data flows |

## ICP Summary Statement

**The Geneva small real estate agency or property manager using AI tools — particularly tenant screening, smart building systems, or facial recognition access control — who believes they are nFADP-compliant because their AI vendor told them so, but has never conducted a DPIA or executed formal data processing agreements.**

**Entry point:** "You've already adopted AI. Do you know your nFADP obligations?"

**Key differentiator from other verticals:**
- Legal: "Should you adopt AI at all?" (adoption hesitancy)
- Finance/WM: "AI is coming — are you ready?" (anticipatory)
- Consulting: "EU AI Act creates dual compliance challenge" (regulatory threat)
- Healthcare: "Swissdamed is your #1 compliance deadline" (concrete trigger)
- **Real estate: "You've already adopted AI — are you compliant?"** (retroactive compliance — unique framing)

## Timing Context — Cycle 2 Update

- **GAIGI operative ~3–5 months:** Geneva AI Governance Institute launched Fall 2025. By March 2026, category awareness among Geneva RE professionals has likely increased. GAIGI legitimizes the category but serves enterprise/institutional clients — non-competitive with SMB advisory.
- **nFADP enforcement trajectory:** FDPIC confirmed enforcement mode (2024/2025 Annual Report, July 2025). Healthcare is FDPIC priority #1; financial and RE sectors are secondary but not exempt.
- **EU AI Act August 2, 2026:** Swiss RE agencies with EU market nexus (EU clients, EU-intended AI output, EU distribution) face high-risk obligations. Dual compliance (nFADP + EU AI Act) now operational.
- **CoE AI Convention:** Implementation expected end-2026. Will add oversight and transparency obligations. Window for advisory entry before CoE Convention creates compliance rush.
- **Advisory window:** NOW through end-2026 — before regulatory convergence creates compliance rush and before potential boutique competitor entry.
