## Geneva Real Estate Market Overview

### Market Size and Structure

**Geneva Real Estate Market 2025:**
- Residential property prices: CHF 15,480/sqm average (Q2 2025)
- Prime district prices: CHF 18,000–20,000/sqm
- Residential price forecast 2026: +3.0% to 4.5%
- Office availability rate: 6.3% (Q3 2025); prime rents stable
- Market character: Strong demand, limited supply, premium-priced, highly regulated

**Market structure:**
- Large/luxury agencies: FGP Swiss & Alps (Forbes Global Properties), BARNES Geneva, Moser Vernet & Cie (130+ years), Comptoir Immobilier (est. 1825), John Taylor Geneva, Naef Commercial Knight Frank
- Small SMB agencies: Residential focus, 1–10 employees, local market
- Property managers: Nessell and similar boutique operators
- OFL licensing required for all Geneva RE agencies

**Key Geneva RE market characteristics:**
- Highly regulated cantonal framework (OFL — Office cantonal du logement)
- International tenant demand (diplomats, multinationals, expats)
- Strict tenant protection laws
- High entry barriers (cantonal licensing required)

## The Regulatory Awareness Gap — Primary Market Signal (Unchanged)

**Critical finding:** Many Geneva RE companies have limited knowledge of AI regulations despite high AI adoption rate (67%).

This creates a specific advisory opportunity:
- High adoption = efficiency case already proven
- Low awareness = compliance gap is structural
- Entry point: "You're already using AI. Do you know your nFADP obligations?"

### What RE Agencies Are Getting Wrong

1. **Assuming AI vendor = compliance covered:** AI tool vendors typically operate as data processors; RE agency remains data controller with full nFADP obligations
2. **Not recognizing DPIA requirements:** AI tenant screening and smart building systems likely require DPIAs under nFADP
3. **Not understanding accountability:** AI valuation tools do NOT replace human expert accountability — the certified expert remains legally responsible
4. **Missing breach notification obligations:** nFADP requires "as soon as possible" breach notification
5. **Ignoring international data transfers:** Cloud AI tools may involve international data transfers requiring specific safeguards

## nFADP Compliance Requirements Specific to RE

### Data Types in RE That Trigger nFADP Obligations

| Data Type | nFADP Sensitivity | Example Processing |
|---|---|---|
| Tenant personal data | High (Art. 5(c) possible) | Name, address, financial info, lease terms |
| Tenant financial data | High (Art. 5(c)) | Credit checks, income verification |
| Biometric data | Highest (Art. 5(c) explicit) | Facial recognition access systems |
| Buyer/seller personal data | Standard | Contact details, financial capacity |
| Property data | Low — not personal | Property characteristics, valuation data |
| Smart building usage data | High (potentially personal) | Occupant behavior, access patterns |
| Employee data | Standard | HR records, access logs |

### nFADP Obligations for RE Agencies Using AI

**Minimum obligations:**
1. **Transparency:** Inform data subjects (tenants, buyers) about AI processing
2. **Purpose limitation:** Use AI data only for stated purposes
3. **Consent:** Where required, obtain valid consent
4. **Data minimization:** Collect only what is necessary
5. **Breach notification:** Report breaches "as soon as possible"
6. **Data subject rights:** Honor access, rectification, deletion requests

**Enhanced obligations (for sensitive processing):**
7. **DPIA:** Data protection impact assessment required for AI tenant screening, smart building systems, facial recognition
8. **Art. 5(c) compliance:** For financial data, health data (if applicable), biometric data

**Accountability obligations:**
9. **Register of processing activities:** Maintain records of AI systems and data processing
10. **Data processing agreements:** Ensure vendor DPAs cover AI tool use
11. **Managing director liability:** Personal liability for non-compliance
