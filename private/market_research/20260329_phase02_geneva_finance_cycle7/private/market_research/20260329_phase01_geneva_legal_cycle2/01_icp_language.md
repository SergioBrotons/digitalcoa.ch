# ICP Language: Geneva Small Law Firms
## Phase: 01 — Geneva Legal — CYCLE 2
## Target ICP: Managing partner / individual attorney at small law firm (2–5 attorneys)
## Last Updated: 2026-03-29

---

## Cross-Vertical Synthesis Integration (Phase 05 Learnings)

Phase 05 confirmed: **nFADP is the universal entry point** across all 4 verticals. Every small professional practice — regardless of sector — first asks: "What data am I allowed to put into AI, and what do I have to tell clients?" Geneva attorneys are no different. The ICP language below is refined to anchor on this universal pain.

Phase 05 also confirmed: **no boutique competitor** exists in the small practice AI governance space across any vertical. The window is open.

---

## How They Talk About AI (Cycle 2 Refined)

### Primary Frustrations (In Their Words)
- "Nous ne savons pas comment intégrer l'IA"
- "Nous hésitons à cause du secret professionnel"
- "Les outils d'IA générique comme ChatGPT envoyent les données à des serveurs externes"
- "Qui est responsable si l'IA fait une erreur?"
- "Nous manquons d'expertise interne"
- "Le retour sur investissement n'est pas clair"
- **NEW from Phase 05 synthesis:** "Le RGPD Suisse — qu'est-ce que ça change pour nous?" (nFADP confusion is cross-vertical)
- **NEW from Phase 05 synthesis:** "Est-ce qu'on doit informer les clients? Comment?" (consent/data notification)

### What They Say They Want
- Des outils d'IA conformes au droit suisse
- Des solutions hébergées en Suisse ou en Europe
- Une utilisation qui préserve le secret professionnel
- Une efficacité réelle sans risque pour le client
- "On veut être sûrs avant de se lancer"
- **REINFORCED from Phase 05:** A decision framework — not more tools. They want to know which decision to make, not which product to buy.

### Phrases That Signal Pain
- "On n'a pas le temps de former toute l'équipe"
- "Nos clients nous font confiance, on ne peut pas risquer une fuite de données"
- "L'IA nous paraît être un investissement incertain"
- "On ne sait pas par où commencer"
- "Les outils grand public nous semblent trop risqués"
- "On entend parler d'IA partout mais concrètement..."
- **Phase 05 cross-vertical signal:** "Est-ce qu'on est obligés de dire aux clients qu'on utilise de l'IA?" (data rights notification obligation)

### Language to Avoid
- "Boostez votre productivité avec l'IA"
- "L'IA transformera votre cabinet"
- "Adoptez la révolution numérique"
- "Optimisez vos processus juridiques"
- "L'IA est l'avenir du droit"
- **Avoid also:** "Conformité IA" (too abstract) — say instead "Ce que vous avez le droit de faire avec les données de vos clients"

## Refined Positioning (Cycle 2)

### The Core Frame (from Phase 05 Universal Positioning)
**"Before you choose an AI tool, you need to make three decisions about data."**

This works for attorneys because:
1. It answers the question they actually have (not "which tool" but "what am I allowed to do")
2. It positions Sergio as a decision guide, not a tool vendor
3. It creates a low-friction entry: "let me ask you three questions" vs. "let me sell you something"

### Phase-05-Refined ICP Descriptor
> "The managing partner at a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm who is NOT asking 'which AI tool should we use' — they're asking 'what are we actually allowed to do with client data, and how do we make that decision responsibly?' They feel the gap between AI theory and safe practice. They want a framework, not a demo."

---

## Notes
Key pattern (unchanged): They're not asking "how do we adopt AI?" They're asking "how do we adopt AI without putting our clients at risk?" Fear of getting it wrong outweighs desire for efficiency.

Phase 05 synthesis addition: The nFADP consent/notification dimension is now a confirmed pain point across all verticals. Legal firms add a second layer: professional secrecy (Art. 13 LLCA) + nFADP. The combination is unique to legal.
