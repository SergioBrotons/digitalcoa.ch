## Lead Magnet: Cycle 2 Refined Version

**Title (EN):** "The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question: A Decision Framework for Geneva Attorneys Who Want AI Benefits Without the Risk"

**Title (FR):** "L'IA au service de votre cabinet : Le cadre décisionnel pour les avocates et avocats genevois"

**Core argument (Phase 05 refined):**
The problem isn't "which AI tool should we use?" The problem is "what are the three decisions that determine whether any AI use is safe for our clients?" Answer the three decisions first. The tool choice becomes obvious.

**Why this works for attorneys specifically:**
- Attorney identity: They are trained to identify risk before taking action. A decision framework fits their professional mindset.
- Professional secrecy: The framework is built around the Art. 13 LLCA question — what data can safely enter an AI system?
- nFADP overlay: The consent/data rights dimension is now an additional decision layer (Phase 05 cross-vertical finding)
- Scale: The 2–5 attorney reality means they need something they can actually implement, not a 50-page policy template

**Structure:**

```
1. The Three Decisions (the core of the document)
   - Decision 1: Which client data, if any, enters AI tools?
   - Decision 2: Which AI tools are approved for which data types?
   - Decision 3: How do you tell clients about AI use (or not)?

2. The Risk Matrix (visual or table)
   - Tool type × Data sensitivity = Approved / Proceed with caution / Don't
   - Adapted for 2–5 attorney firm (no IT department)

3. The 90-Day Implementation Sketch
   - Week 1–4: Make the three decisions
   - Week 5–12: Document and communicate
   - Month 4+: Review and refine

4. What to Say to Clients (scripted language)
   - When asked "do you use AI?"
   - When disclosing (or not) in engagement letters
   - When a client raises a concern

5. The SAV/FSA Alignment
   - How this framework maps to the June 2024 guidelines
   - What "reasonable AI governance" looks like for a small firm
```

**Length:** 8–12 pages (substantial but not overwhelming)

**Format:** PDF download, professional but not corporate — should feel like something written by a peer, not a vendor.

**CTA:** "Download the Framework" → "Schedule a 30-Minute Briefing Call" (warm, no-pressure)

**Language:** Bilingual EN/FR throughout. FR slightly weighted (Geneva primary audience).

## Delivery Format Options

| Format | Pros | Cons |
|---|---||
| PDF + gated landing page | Measurable, warm lead capture | One more step between content and audience |
| PDF + email capture | Builds email list | Lower immediate download volume |
| Direct PDF link (no gate) | Highest reach, trust-building | No lead capture |
| PDF + Telegram/LinkedIn distribution | No gate needed; peer network carries it | No direct lead capture |

**Recommendation (Cycle 2):** No gate on Phase 01 legal. It's the highest-trust-threshold vertical and needs maximum reach. Gate on Phase 02 (wealth management) where commercial intent is higher. This reflects Phase 05's insight that legal is the anchor, not the cash cow.
