## Deepest Obligations by Article — WM-Specific

### nFADP Art. 5(c) — Automated Decision-Making (HIGH RELEVANCE)
**Applies when:** AI systems make decisions or significantly influence decisions with **legal effect** on clients.

**Examples in WM context:**
- AI-driven portfolio rebalancing without human review
- Automated risk profiling that determines advisory recommendations
- AI-driven client suitability scoring for specific products
- Algorithmic credit/asset allocation decisions

**Obligations:**
- Client must be informed when automated decisions are made
- Right to request human review / manual intervention
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) required before deployment

**FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation:** AI-based data processing falls directly under nFADP Art. 5(c). No ambiguity.

### nFADP Art. 26 — ADMD Disclosure (HIGH RELEVANCE)
**Art. 26 ADMD Disclosure:** Before deploying AI tools that process client data, wealth managers must:
- Inform clients about the AI tool's nature and purpose
- Document the legal basis for data processing
- Ensure vendor DPA (Data Processing Agreement) is in place

**Vendor DPA Requirements:**
- AI SaaS vendors (InvestGlass, Altoo, Neurons Lab, Nevis, etc.) must sign DPAs with wealth manager clients
- DPA must specify: processing scope, security measures, sub-processor restrictions, breach notification, data retention

### FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — AI Governance Obligations
**Operative since December 2024.** Key requirements:
- Maintain AI tool inventory (all AI systems in use)
- Risk-classify each AI tool
- Define accountability and responsibility per AI application
- Implement risk mitigation measures
- Monitor AI system performance and drift
- Report material AI incidents to FINMA

**Reality check:** Most small boutiques are in early stages of establishing formal AI governance — per FINMA's own assessment.

### FINMA Circ. 2025/02 — FinSA Conduct Rules (NEW — Cycle 4)
**Effective Jan 1, 2025** (transition to June 30, 2025):
- Duty to provide information on financial instruments and risks
- Suitability and appropriateness tests
- Conflict of interest management
- Third-party compensation disclosure

**AI relevance:** When AI tools generate recommendations or support suitability assessments, the firm remains responsible for FinSA compliance. AI does not transfer liability.

### Revised AMLA + LETA — New Compliance Burdens (NEW — Cycle 4)
**Effective mid-2026 / H2 2026:**
- Wealth managers working with legal advisors (notaries, lawyers) face new AML obligations on those advisors
- Central beneficial owner register requires verification and updating
- CHF 500,000 maximum fine for intentional breaches
- Discrepancy reporting required from financial intermediaries

### CARF + CRS 2.0 — Crypto Reporting (NEW — Cycle 4)
**Effective Jan 1, 2026:**
- New tax due diligence for crypto-asset exposure
- Integration into automatic exchange of information
- Wealth managers with crypto-structured products or direct crypto holdings face reporting obligations

### EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Pressure
Swiss wealth managers serving EU-based clients must observe:
- **GPAI rules:** August 2025
- **Full compliance:** August 2026

**Relevance:** HNW clients with EU residence or EU-domiciled entities require EU AI Act compliance awareness.
