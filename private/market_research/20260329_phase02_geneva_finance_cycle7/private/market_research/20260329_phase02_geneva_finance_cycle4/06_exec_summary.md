## Key Cycle 4 Findings

### 1. New Regulatory Obligations Confirmed — 2026 Compliance Convergence
Multiple new regulations effective or coming into force in 2026, creating a layered compliance burden for boutique Geneva wealth managers:

- **FINMA Circ. 2025/02** (FinSA/FinSO conduct rules) — operative since Jan 1, 2025
- **Revised AMLA** — expected mid-2026, broadens scope to financial advisors
- **LETA** (Transparency of Legal Entities) — expected H2 2026, central beneficial owner register
- **CARF + CRS 2.0** — effective Jan 1, 2026, crypto tax reporting
- **FINMA Circ. 2026/01** (nature-related risks) — staged from Jan 1, 2026
- **SIF AI Report** — expected Q4 2025 or Q1 2026, financial sector AI regulatory mapping

**Implication:** Wealth managers already managing FINMA 08/2024 AI governance obligations now face a second wave of new obligations in 2026. Urgency is compounding.

### 2. AI Tool Ecosystem Active — Named Swiss Platforms Confirmed
Six named Swiss-hosted AI platforms confirmed active in the Geneva WM space:
- InvestGlass AI (Swiss-hosted AI co-pilot)
- Additiv (addWealth) — modular wealth platform
- Altoo — wealth data aggregation
- Neurons Lab (ARKEN) — RM automation
- Nevis — AI meeting summaries and email
- Linvo — Swiss multi-family office with high AI compliance focus

These are technology vendors — not advisors. Wealth managers using these tools still bear full regulatory responsibility. This creates a service opportunity, not a competitive threat.

### 3. Competitive Landscape — No Boutique Competitor (4th Consecutive Cycle)
No boutique advisory firm confirmed targeting small Geneva wealth management firms (2–20 employees, CHF 100M–500M AUM) for AI governance services. Large firms (Deloitte, PwC, Protiviti) and large law firms (Lenz Staehelin, Pestalozzi) remain large-institution-only.

**First-mover window: Open — 18–24 months.**

### 4. FINMA Supervisory Reality Check
FINMA's own guidance (08/2024) notes most Swiss financial institutions are still in early stages of establishing formal AI governance structures. This is especially acute for small boutiques with limited compliance resources.

### 5. SIF AI Analysis — Regulatory Clarity Pending
State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF) AI analysis expected Q4 2025 or Q1 2026. Will map regulatory gaps in financial market regulations related to AI — may trigger additional FINMA guidance.
