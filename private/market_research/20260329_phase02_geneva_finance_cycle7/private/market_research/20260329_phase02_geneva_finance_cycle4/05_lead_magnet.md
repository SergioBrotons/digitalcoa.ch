## Lead Magnet: "The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist"
## (Self-Assessment Edition — Updated for 2026 Regulatory Landscape)

### Lead Magnet Concept

**Format:** Self-assessment checklist + explanatory framework
**Entry point:** Regulatory urgency (multiple new obligations effective 2025–2026)
**Primary hook:** FINMA Circ. 2025/02 + AMLA + LETA + CARF — the 2026 compliance convergence
**Secondary hook:** FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation + FINMA 08/2024 operative
**Tertiary hook:** 91% external AI SaaS adoption — most WM firms already exposed

**Core value proposition:** A boutique Geneva wealth manager can assess their current AI governance exposure and identify gaps across 5 dimensions in under 10 minutes.

**Bilingual strategy:** Checklist structured for EN delivery; FR adaptation natural for Geneva WM audience (dual-language canton).

---

### Lead Magnet Structure

#### Section 1: Your AI Tool Inventory (FINMA 08/2024 Requirement)
*Are you tracking every AI tool that touches client data?*

Checklist items:
- [ ] InvestGlass AI / Altoo / Additiv / Nevis / Neurons Lab (if applicable)
- [ ] Email AI assistants (if processing client data)
- [ ] Portfolio analytics with AI components
- [ ] CRM with AI-driven client scoring
- [ ] Document management with AI search
- [ ] Third-party AI APIs integrated into workflows

#### Section 2: Automated Decision-Making — Art. 5(c) Exposure
*Is any AI system making or significantly influencing decisions with legal effect on your clients?*

Checklist items:
- [ ] AI-driven portfolio rebalancing (fully automated or threshold-based)
- [ ] Algorithmic suitability scoring for financial products
- [ ] AI-generated investment recommendations without human review
- [ ] Automated risk profiling determining advisory approach
- [ ] AI-driven client segmentation affecting service levels

If YES to any: nFADP Art. 5(c) obligations apply — client notification + manual review right required.

#### Section 3: Vendor Due Diligence — Art. 26 ADMD + FINMA 08/2024
*Do you have signed DPAs with all AI vendors?*

Checklist items:
- [ ] Data Processing Agreement signed with each AI SaaS vendor
- [ ] Sub-processor restrictions documented
- [ ] Breach notification procedures defined in vendor contracts
- [ ] Data retention and deletion terms agreed
- [ ] AI vendor risk classified in your AI inventory

#### Section 4: FINMA Circ. 2025/02 — Conduct Rules (Effective Jan 1, 2025)
*Is your AI-generated advice FinSA-compliant?*

Checklist items:
- [ ] Client suitability documented before AI-assisted recommendations
- [ ] Risk disclosure provided for AI-supported product recommendations
- [ ] Conflicts of interest identified in AI-driven workflows
- [ ] Third-party compensation disclosure updated for AI-era advice
- [ ] AI does not replace human accountability — responsibility remains with the firm

#### Section 5: 2026 Regulatory Convergence — AMLA, LETA, CARF
*Are you preparing for the mid-2026 / H2 2026 compliance wave?*

Checklist items:
- [ ] AMLA scope review: Are any of your legal advisors now subject to AML obligations?
- [ ] Beneficial owner data current for all client entities?
- [ ] LETA readiness: Central register requirements mapped to your onboarding?
- [ ] Crypto exposure identified: CARF reporting obligations assessed?
- [ ] Timeline: Implementation work started or scheduled for 2026?

#### Section 6: The Compliance Gap — What This Means for You

*Framework explanation:*

**If you scored 0–2:** Early stages. AI tools likely in use informally. Regulatory exposure exists but not yet formalized. First step: AI inventory.

**If you scored 3–5:** Developing. Core AI governance structures forming. Key gaps likely in vendor DPAs and Art. 5(c) client notification.

**If you scored 6+:** Advanced. Formal AI governance in place. 2026 regulatory wave (AMLA/LETA/CARF) is the next horizon.
