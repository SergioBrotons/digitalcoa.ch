## Active Regulatory Framework

### 1. Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) — OPERATIVE
- **Effective:** September 1, 2023
- **Geneva legal relevance:** Law firms process substantial personal client data; AI-assisted processing = nFADP Art. 5(c) triggered
- **FDPIC enforcement:** Active (FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report, July 2025 — enforcement trajectory confirmed; healthcare + legal are FDPIC priority sectors)
- **DPIA requirement:** Any AI system processing personal data requires prior DPIA — most small firms have zero process
- **FDPIC May 8, 2025:** nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing; no separate AI-specific law required
- **Key obligations for small firms:**
  - Lawful basis establishment (Art. 6 nFADP)
  - DPIA for AI-based processing (Art. 22 nFADP)
  - Data subject rights (Art. 8–13 nFADP)
  - Data breach notification (Art. 24 nFADP)

### 2. Council of Europe AI Convention (CoE) — Implementation Pending
- **Status:** Open for signature; Switzerland participating; FDJP preparing consultation draft
- **FDJP consultation:** Expected end-2026 — concrete 9-month window
- **Focus areas:** Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- **Timeline to implementation:** Likely 2027–2028 (post-consultation + parliamentary process)
- **Geneva legal implication:** Small firms should begin AI governance readiness now — consultation draft will trigger market-wide awareness spike
- **Cycle 6 sharpening:** FDJP consultation timing moved from "expected" to "expected within 9 months" — urgency now concrete

### 3. EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Application
- **GPAI operative:** August 2, 2025
- **High-risk obligations:** August 2, 2026 (~15 months)
- **Geneva legal implication (direct):** Attorneys using EU-market AI systems for client matters face EU AI Act obligations if:
  - Placing AI systems on the EU market
  - Operating AI systems with EU-intended output
  - Acting as EU importer/distributor of AI systems
- **Dual compliance:** nFADP + EU AI Act = operational reality for Geneva firms with EU nexus
- **Cross-border practice:** Geneva attorneys with EU client matters directly exposed
- **Cycle 6 sharpening:** EU AI Act Aug 2, 2026 now positioned as #2 forcing function in Geneva regulatory calendar (behind Swissdamed Jul 1, 2026)
