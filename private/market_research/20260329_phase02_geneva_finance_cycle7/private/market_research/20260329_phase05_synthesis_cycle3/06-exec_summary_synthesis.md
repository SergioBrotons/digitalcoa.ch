## Cross-Vertical Findings — Key Takeaways

### Finding 1: Healthcare Has the Strongest Conversion Trigger
Swissdamed mandatory July 1, 2026 is the most concrete, unmissable deadline across all five verticals. Combined with Vaud extension (~2,000–2,500 addressable practices) and AMGe retirement crisis, healthcare is the highest-urgency vertical.

### Finding 2: Real Estate Is the Surprise High-Adoption Vertical
67% of Geneva real estate companies already using AI (Q1 2024) — the highest sector adoption rate. But with limited regulatory awareness. This creates a distinct advisory entry point: "you're already using AI, do you know your nFADP obligations?"

### Finding 3: IT/Tech Consulting Is the Fastest-Conversion Sub-Segment
Within consulting, IT/technology firms have the highest nFADP exposure (client project data, IP) and the fastest decision velocity (technology-forward culture). Priority sub-segment for Phase 03 go-to-market.

### Finding 4: The nFADP–ISO 42001 Stack Is the Core Differentiation
- nFADP = universal entry point (table stakes, all verticals)
- ISO 42001 = competitive differentiation (advanced, certifiable, procurement-relevant)
- The stack works across all five verticals with vertical-specific overlays

### Finding 5: EU AI Act Adds Cross-Vertical Pressure
EU AI Act extraterritorial scope (August 2026 for high-risk; GPAI rules August 2025) creates a new urgency dimension: Swiss SMBs with EU clients face direct compliance requirements. Particularly acute for Geneva's international professional services community.
