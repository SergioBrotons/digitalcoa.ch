## Content Outline

### Section 1: Why Geneva Executives Need a LinkedIn Presence Now
- LinkedIn algorithm 2026: what changed and why it matters
- Geneva business community: LinkedIn as primary professional network
- Thought leadership compounding: how consistent presence builds influence over time
- The Geneva executive paradox: busy but need visibility; the 30-minutes-a-week solution

### Section 2: The Geneva Framework — 4 Content Types That Work
- Type 1: Geneva-specific observation (local angle, intellectual signal)
- Type 2: Professional insight (industry-specific, expertise demonstration)
- Type 3: Framework or model (teaching; shows analytical thinking)
- Type 4: Commentary on current events (timeliness; algorithm favor)
- How often: 4-6 posts/month; what to batch create

### Section 3: The 30-Minute Weekly System
- Batch creation workflow: 1 hour/month for content creation
- Content repurposing: Brotons.net newsletter → LinkedIn post framework
- Writing templates: hooks, body structure, call-to-action
- Tools: LinkedIn scheduler (MeetEdgar, Buffer), headline analyzer
- Consistency vs. perfection: why regular beats brilliant

### Section 4: What Geneva Executives Get Wrong
- Mistake 1: Posting about firm achievements (feels promotional)
- Mistake 2: Sharing news without Geneva angle (not distinctive)
- Mistake 3: Writing too long (executives don't have time to read)
- Mistake 4: Posting randomly (no audience-building strategy)
- Mistake 5: Ignoring engagement (LinkedIn rewards interaction)

### Section 5: The Geneva Executive LinkedIn Content Calendar
- Monthly template: content type mix by week
- Seasonal angles: Geneva business calendar (CCIG events, economic reports, sector news)
- Bilingual approach: EN primary; FR for key posts
- Engagement strategy: how to respond to comments; when to engage others' content
- Measurement: what to track; vanity metrics vs. signal metrics
