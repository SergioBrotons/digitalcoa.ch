## Key Platform Developments (C124)

### 1. LinkedIn Newsletter — Algorithm and Format Evolution
- LinkedIn 2026: newsletter feature mature; text posts still effective for reach
- LinkedIn newsletter: subscriber notification; algorithm favors newsletter content
- Ghostwriting: disclosure norms evolving; LinkedIn doesn't require disclosure of ghostwriting
- Algorithm: LinkedIn favoring "substantive" content (500+ words); short posts deprioritized

### 2. Substack — Platform Competition and Deliverability
- Substack competition: Beehiiv, Ghost, Buttondown, Ghostlines competing
- Substack deliverability: improving but Gmail still flags some newsletters
- Substack new features: video, podcast hosting, community features, Substack Payments
- Monetization: Substack takes 10% of paid subscriptions; direct revenue model possible

### 3. Email Marketing Regulations — GDPR/FADP Compliance
- Swiss FADP (nDSG): effective 2023; newsletter subscriber data covered
- Requirements: consent for email marketing; unsubscribe mechanism; data access rights
- GDPR: applies to EU subscribers; similar requirements
- Switzerland-EU adequacy: ongoing; data transfer implications

### 4. AI-Generated Content — Platform and Regulatory Approach
- LinkedIn: allows AI-assisted content; doesn't require disclosure
- Substack: no specific AI disclosure requirements
- Swiss Media Law: not applicable to independent newsletters (below threshold)
- Editorial standard: human curation vs. AI generation; reader trust implications

### 5. Content Platform — Swiss Business Media Context
- Le Temps: Geneva-based Swiss business newspaper; EN/FR
- Bilan: Swiss business magazine; FR
- CCIG publications: Geneva business intelligence; partnership opportunity
- PME: Swiss SME business media; FR/EN
- Competitive landscape: Brotons.net differentiation vs. established media
