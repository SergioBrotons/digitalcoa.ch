# 01 — ICP: Brotons.net — Geneva Executive Thought Leadership Audience

## Target Segment: Brotons.net Reader — Geneva Executives, Decision-Makers, UHNW Principals

### Reader Profile
- **Role:** C-suite executives, senior partners, UHNW principals, family office leaders
- **Sector:** Financial services, commodity trading, professional services, family businesses
- **Location:** Geneva primary; some Zurich, international
- **Language:** English primary (70%), French 30%
- **Behavior:** Active LinkedIn users, receive multiple newsletters, time-constrained

### Decision-Making Unit (Reader Journey)
| Stage | Reader Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Geneva executive encountering Brotons.net via LinkedIn or search | Limited time; needs immediate signal that content is worth reading |
| Consideration | Reader opens newsletter, reads 1-2 pieces | Content quality, relevance, and brevity; lack of Geneva-specific depth |
| Engagement | Reader subscribes, returns for 2-3 pieces | Consistency of quality; editorial voice; topics aligned with professional interests |
| Retention | Regular reader, 6+ months | Newsletter fatigue; multiple competing publications; needs differentiation |
| Advocacy | Highly engaged reader | No clear pathway to engage further or refer peers |

### Known Geneva Reader Examples
- UHNW principals with Geneva family offices
- Geneva private bank relationship managers and CIOs
- Commodity trading executives (Vitol, Trafigura, Mercuria Geneva offices)
- Geneva-based executive coaches and management consultants
- International organization senior staff (UNOG, WHO, ITU)
- Geneva law firm partners (Lenz & Staehelin, Walder Wyss)

### Pain Points (C124 Update)
1. **LinkedIn ghostwriting need** — Geneva executives want LinkedIn presence but lack time; opportunity to offer ghostwriting as service leveraging Brotons.net content quality
2. **Bilingual content gap** — Geneva executives split EN/FR; current Brotons.net not systematically bilingual; FR executives underserved
3. **Retention loop missing** — current newsletter model lacks engagement mechanisms beyond publication; readers go passive
4. **CCIG institutional calendar untapped** — Geneva institutional calendar provides content timing signal; partnership opportunity unexplored
5. **ICP decision journey not mapped** — content organized by topic, not by reader journey stage; internal linking not optimized for reader progression
6. **Newsletter deliverability suboptimal** — spam filter issues reducing reach; potential readers not receiving content

### Buying/Engagement Triggers
- LinkedIn algorithm changes affecting organic reach
- Newsletter deliverability issues discovered
- Geneva-specific content competitor emerging
- Owner decides to monetize or systematize Brotons.net audience
- DigitalCoa.ch lead generation pathway needs strengthening

### Buying Barriers
- No direct monetization model (yet) — audience development vs. revenue
- Owner approval required for significant Brotons.net operational changes
- Technical changes (deliverability) require email platform access
- Partnership with CCIG requires owner relationship

### Language / Cultural Fit
- English primary for Geneva international business community
- French for Swiss Romande executives; growing segment
- Bilingual: 70% EN / 30% FR ratio appropriate for Geneva audience
- Editorial voice: intellectually serious, Geneva-appropriate, restrained confidence
