## Finding 3: Brotons.net Reader Retention Loop Mechanism Design

### What
Current newsletter model: publish and distribute. Missing: retention loops that keep readers engaged between publications and prevent passive unsubscribes. Mechanism design needed: (1) onboarding sequence, (2) quarterly reader check-in, (3) exclusive content tier, (4) community signal, (5) re-engagement flow for lapsed subscribers.

### Evidence
- Newsletter industry data: average unsubscribe rate 0.5-1%/month; retention loops reduce to 0.2-0.3%
- Onboarding: welcome sequence (3 emails) typically increases retention 20-30%
- Quarterly check-in: survey or personal note increases reader investment
- Exclusive content: readers-only deep dives or data; increases perceived value
- Lapsed re-engagement: win-back sequence (3-5 emails) typically recovers 5-15% of lapsed

### Implication for Brotons.net
- Implementation: email platform automation required (Substack or equivalent)
- Cost: minimal (automation setup 1-2 days); ongoing maintenance low
- Impact: could increase newsletter retention 20-40% over 12 months
- Sequencing: implement onboarding first, then quarterly check-in, then lapsed re-engagement

## Finding 6: Brotons.net Newsletter Deliverability Optimization

### What
Gmail/Outlook spam filter optimization for newsletter deliverability. Technical fixes needed: (1) authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC), (2) list hygiene, (3) engagement metrics optimization. Content optimization: subject line testing, preview text, send time optimization. Impact: deliverability rates improve from 70-85% to 85-95%.

### Evidence
- Email deliverability industry standard: 85-95% inbox placement for well-managed lists
- Gmail 2024-2026: increasingly aggressive spam filtering for newsletters
- Substack: manages some deliverability; not all technical requirements on publisher side
- Key factors: SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication, list hygiene (bounce <2%), engagement signals
- Subject line: open rate impact 15-25%; preview text impact 10-15%
- Send time: optimal send time varies by audience; testing needed

### Implication for Brotons.net
- Implementation: email platform technical settings + content optimization
- Owner action needed: Substack deliverability settings review; possible platform migration consideration
- Cost: minimal; can be done without external support
- Impact: 10-20% increase in effective readership
