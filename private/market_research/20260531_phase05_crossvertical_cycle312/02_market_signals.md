# 02 — Cross-Vertical Market Signals
## C312 Phase 05 — Cross-Vertical Synthesis
**Cycle:** C312 | **Date:** 2026-05-31

---

## Cross-Vertical Problems That Appear in All Four Verticals

### Market Signal 1: The Carrier Form Has Become the De Facto Standard

**What is happening:**
Carrier forms are no longer compliance checklists. They are creating documented standards of professional conduct that did not previously exist as formal regulatory requirements.

**Legal:** Carrier form names the managing partner personally. The form requires attestation to AI use practices the partner may not have reviewed. Signature creates documented standard without the partner having reviewed the specific questions.

**Finance:** Carrier form requires attestation to human oversight adequacy. The form asks for evidence of oversight the managing director has not systematically reviewed. The attestation creates a documented standard for what "adequate" means in that specific firm.

**Consulting:** Carrier form requires attestation to sub-consultant AI delegation practices. The managing director signed the engagement authorization, not the AI tool authorization. The carrier is now asking for delegation chain documentation the MD never designed.

**Healthcare:** Carrier form requires attestation to AI diagnostic authorization within FMH specialty scope. The form asks which specific tools, which specific functions, within which specific specialty scope. The physician certified at the practice level; the carrier asks at the function level.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
The carrier form has shifted from organizational compliance document to individual professional accountability instrument. The individual professional's name is on the form. The professional did not design the specification. The professional is accountable for meeting it.

---

### Market Signal 2: The Authorization Chain Is the Liability

**What is happening:**
Across all four verticals, the professional is personally accountable for AI use they did not directly authorize. The authorization chain runs through people and tools the professional did not systematically review.

**Legal:** Associate using AI on client matters. Managing partner has no documentation of which tools, which matters, which clients. Associate's unauthenticated AI use creates managing partner liability.

**Finance:** Associate activating AI tools within the portfolio construction workflow. Managing director authorized the engagement, not the specific AI implementation. Vendor implementation created infrastructure the managing director never reviewed.

**Consulting:** Sub-consultant using AI on deliverables. Prime consultant authorized the engagement, not the AI tools. Sub-consultant is an independent professional who cannot be directed, only contracted. The delegation is invisible in the documentation.

**Healthcare:** Medical assistant using AI on patient data. Physician authorized the assistant's employment, not AI tool use. Patient data routes through AI servers outside the HIN corridor. Physician is personally liable for data processing they did not authorize.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
The liability is not "AI was used." The liability is "AI was used by someone I didn't authorize, for something I'm accountable for, with no documentation of the chain." Authorization chain documentation is absent in every vertical.

---

### Market Signal 3: The OaF Survey Creates Two-Tier Visibility

**What is happening:**
The Own Assessment Form (OaF) survey creates a documented baseline that reveals the gap between what management believes is happening and what is actually happening.

**Legal:** Managing partner completes OaF. Survey asks about firm-level AI policies. Managing partner believes adequate policies exist. Associate AI use is invisible to the survey because it's unauthenticated.

**Finance:** Managing director completes OaF. Survey asks about human oversight adequacy. Managing director believes oversight is maintained. Associate-implemented AI tools are invisible to the oversight framework because they were never reviewed.

**Consulting:** Managing director completes OaF. Survey asks about AI methodology disclosure. Managing director believes client methodology is appropriately documented. Sub-consultant AI methodology is invisible to the framework because it's unauthenticated delegation.

**Healthcare:** Physician completes OaF. Survey asks about AI diagnostic tool use within specialty scope. Physician believes patient care is appropriately documented. Medical assistant AI use is invisible to the oversight framework because it was never formally authorized.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
Two tiers of visibility: what the professional believes is documented (firm-level policy, adequate supervision, appropriate methodology, proper scope) and what is actually happening (associate/vendor/sub-consultant/assistant AI use that was never formally authorized or documented). The OaF survey reveals the gap without closing it.

### Market Signal 4: Vendor Compliance Assumption Creates Hidden Exposure

**What is happening:**
In every vertical, the professional assumes vendor compliance = their compliance. The vendor contract is universally silent on record production obligations. The carrier is now asking for records the vendor does not hold.

**Legal:** Law firm uses AI legal research tool. Vendor certification presented as evidence of compliance. Carrier asks for documentation of how the tool was used on specific matters. Vendor has no record production obligation in the contract.

**Finance:** Wealth manager uses portfolio construction AI tool. Vendor processor chain has three or more hops. Carrier asks for documentation of data processing. Each vendor in the chain has its own data handling practices, none documented in the firm's records.

**Consulting:** Consulting boutique uses AI analysis tool for client deliverables. Vendor contract has no provision for producing methodology documentation. Client asks for evidence of how AI was used in the analysis. The vendor has no obligation to produce this.

**Healthcare:** Physician practice uses AI diagnostic tool. Vendor is "certified." Patient data routes through vendor's AI processing servers. HIN covers transport security, not processing security. Carrier asks for evidence of data processing compliance. The vendor has no obligation to produce this.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
Vendor certification creates a false compliance assumption. The vendor contract is the governing document for what records the vendor must produce. No vendor contract in any vertical addresses record production for carrier audit purposes. The professional is accountable for records they do not hold and cannot produce.

### Market Signal 5: "Adequate" Is the Universal Floating Signifier

**What is happening:**
"Adequate" appears in every carrier form, every regulatory guidance, and every professional standard — and is never defined operationally.

**Legal:** "Adequate supervision of AI-assisted work" — carrier form. No operational definition. No evidence specification. No calibration standard.

**Finance:** "Adequate human oversight of AI-influenced investment decisions" — FINMA guidance. No operational definition. No evidence specification. No calibration standard.

**Consulting:** "Adequate disclosure of AI methodology in client deliverables" — client questionnaire. No operational definition. No evidence specification. No calibration standard.

**Healthcare:** "Adequate human oversight of AI-assisted diagnostic decisions" — FMH guidance and carrier form. No operational definition. No evidence specification. No calibration standard.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
"Adequate" is the universal paralyzing term. The professional cannot define it operationally, cannot document to it consistently, and cannot demonstrate compliance without guessing. The carrier knows what they mean by it; the professional does not. This is the primary action blocker across all four verticals.

---

### Market Signal 6: Retroactive Reconstruction Is the Primary Market Problem

**What is happening:**
In every vertical, the professional has 12–24 months of undocumented AI use. The linear forward-only solution — "implement better documentation going forward" — is structurally insufficient.

**Legal:** 12–24 months of associate AI use on client matters. No tool authorization documentation. No matter-level evidence. Carrier asking specific questions about specific AI uses on specific matters.

**Finance:** 12–24 months of AI-assisted portfolio construction. No model authorization documentation. No model drift monitoring. Institutional DDQ asking for monitoring evidence going back to implementation.

**Consulting:** 12–24 months of AI-assisted proposal methodology. Client asking how AI was used on their specific project. The deliverable was produced; the methodology was never documented. Client as third-party witness.

**Healthcare:** 12–24 months of AI-assisted diagnostic support. Patient asking about AI involvement in their care. The AI suggestion was given; the authorization chain was never documented. Patient record contains AI output without documented physician review.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
Retroactive reconstruction is the primary market problem in every vertical. The professional is not asking "how do I document AI going forward?" They are asking "how do I document what I already did?" The linear forward-only solution is the wrong tool for the primary problem. A distinct retroactive reconstruction methodology is required in every vertical.

---

### Market Signal 7: Patient/Client Conversations Are Creating Documentation Obligations

**What is happening:**
Conversations with patients or clients about AI involvement are creating documented records — and no professional has a response protocol.

**Healthcare:** Patient asks physician about AI involvement in diagnostic decision. The response creates a record in the patient file. nFADP Article 63 activation point. No documented consent framework. No response protocol.

**Consulting:** Client asks consulting boutique how AI was used on their project. The response creates a record. nFADP Article 52 activation point. No documented methodology disclosure framework. No response protocol.

**Finance:** Client asks wealth manager how AI was used in their portfolio construction. The response creates a record. No documented AI involvement disclosure. No response protocol.

**Legal:** Client asks law firm how AI was used on their matter. The response creates a record. No documented AI involvement disclosure. No response protocol.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
Patient and client conversations about AI involvement are creating documentation obligations the professional did not anticipate. The conversation is documented; the professional's response is improvised; the resulting record reveals the documentation gap. No professional in any vertical has a documented response protocol for when a patient or client asks directly about AI involvement.

---

### Market Signal 8: Commercial Enforcement Precedes Regulatory Enforcement

**What is happening:**
The commercial consequence of undocumented AI use arrives before the regulatory consequence.

**Legal:** Client loses confidence in firm that cannot explain its AI methodology. Engagement letter has no AI clause. Carrier renewal asks specific questions about associate AI use. Regulatory investigation follows mandate loss.

**Finance:** Institutional client declines next mandate due to inadequate AI governance evidence. DDQ response was insufficient. Competitor had coherent documentation. Regulatory inquiry follows competitive failure.

**Consulting:** Boutique loses RFP because proposal methodology cannot be defended. Client AI questionnaire revealed documentation gaps. Carrier renewal arrives after client loss.

**Healthcare:** Patient files complaint after learning AI was used without documented consent. Carrier asks for AI diagnostic authorization evidence. Swissmedic inquiry follows patient complaint.

**Cross-vertical pattern:**
Commercial consequence (mandate loss, coverage gap, client loss, complaint) activates before regulatory consequence. The professional loses the client before the regulator arrives. The competitive disadvantage precedes the compliance violation. This creates a structural window: commercial pressure is the forcing function, not regulatory deadline.
