## What the Carrier Form Actually Creates

When you sign your carrier attestation, you are creating a documented standard of professional conduct that applies to you personally.

In legal: your carrier form now asks which AI tools your associates use, on which matters, with what supervision. Your associate's unauthenticated AI use is your personal liability.

In finance: your carrier form now asks for evidence of human oversight adequacy for AI-influenced investment decisions. "Adequate" is not defined. The carrier has a definition. You don't.

In consulting: your carrier form now asks for evidence of your sub-consultant delegation practices. Your sub-consultant is an independent professional. You authorized their engagement. You did not authorize their AI tools. The carrier is asking for the authorization you never created.

In healthcare: your carrier form now asks which specific AI diagnostic tools you use, for which specific functions, within which FMH specialty scope. Your medical assistant's AI use is your personal liability. Your patient's question about AI involvement creates a record in their file. The carrier is asking for evidence you don't have.

The form has your name on it. You didn't design the specification. You are accountable for meeting it.
