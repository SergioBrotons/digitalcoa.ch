### Finding 02: AI Safety Research Ecosystem — UNIDIR, EPFL ELLIS, ICRC Geneva Triangle
UNIDIR (UN Institute for Disarmament Research, Geneva) is the Geneva anchor for AI safety in international security — working on dual-use AI governance, autonomous weapons policy, and responsible AI development in military contexts. EPFL's ELLIS unit on trustworthy AI and large language models represents the academic pillar. ICRC's digital legal frameworks and autonomous weapons positions (Montreux Document context) provide the humanitarian law anchor. Advisory opportunity: connecting Swiss defense technology firms and dual-use AI companies to UNIDIR policy research; facilitating academic-industry translation for EPFL AI safety research; ICRC-aligned AI governance frameworks for Geneva tech firms with humanitarian sector clients. Distinct from C129 F1 (ISO/IEC standards) and C129 F5 (humanitarian logistics digitalization) — this is the security/arms control/academic AI safety nexus, not technical standards compliance or field implementation.

### Finding 03: Swiss Carbon Verification AI — Environmental Markets Digital MRV Advisory
Swiss-based carbon project developers (South Pole, South Pole Group, MinCount) and voluntary carbon market platforms (Xpansiv, Gold Standard registry) are deploying AI for automated monitoring, reporting, and verification — satellite imagery analysis, supply chain monitoring, remote sensing. Voluntary carbon markets face sustained integrity pressure (IC-VCM, VCMI) requiring credible MRV. Advisory opportunity: AI-enabled MRV system selection and implementation strategy; voluntary carbon market integrity compliance; Swiss carbon registry digitalization alignment; EU CBAM AI data requirements integration. Distinct from climate fintech investment coverage of prior cycles — this is carbon market operations and MRV technology implementation advisory, not capital allocation or financial product advisory.
