# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva NGO/Tech

## Key Regulatory Framework

### IO/NGO Regulatory Considerations
| Area | Framework | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| IO immunity | VCDR Art. 9-15; Swiss-Host State Agreement | Commercial contracts with IOs face immunity complications |
| NGO status | Swiss Civil Code Art. 60ff; cantonal recognition | NGO legal status, tax treatment, public benefit designation |
| IO procurement | UN Procurement Rules; WTO GPA; bilateral agreements | Vendor registration requirements; framework agreements |
| Data protection (IOs) | nFADP; IO-specific data agreements | Humanitarian data processing, cross-border data transfers |
| AML (NGOs) | GwG revised 2024-2025 | NGO donor compliance, suspicious activity reporting for large funds |

### Tech Startup Regulatory Considerations
| Area | Framework | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| FinTech licensing | FinIA revision; FINMA guidance | Payment institutions, crypto-institutions requiring FINMA license |
| HealthTech approval | Swissmedic; EU MDR; IVDR | Market access for medical devices, digital health tools |
| AI regulation | Council of Europe AI Convention; domestic CH AI law consultation | High-risk AI deployments; compliance timeline uncertainty |
| Data protection | nFADP (Art. 9 AI training data consent) | AI training data compliance; consumer health data |
| Corporate/startup law | OR; VC structures; stock option plans | Incorporation, investor structures, employee equity |

### Geneva-Specific Considerations
- **Host State Agreement (Accord de Siège)** — Governs IO legal status, privileges, procurement exemptions
- **Geneva cantonal startup support** — FONGIT, cantonal innovation grants, tax incentives for R&D
- **Swiss federal startup support** — Innosuisse, Swiss Venture Fund, ESA Business Incubator
- **CERN IP framework** — IP commercialization rules for CERN spin-offs (distinct from EPFL/industry)
