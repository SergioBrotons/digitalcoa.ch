# 03 — Competitive Landscape: Geneva NGO/Tech

## Primary Competitors

### IO/NGO Advisory Boutiques
- **ICF** (international development consulting)
- **Mathematica** (policy research, development)
- **寻路** (no known Geneva boutique)
- **UNOPS** (IO procurement itself, not advisory)
- **Mercy Corps / Save the Children** (in-house consulting arms)

### Geneva Tech Startup Advisory
- **FONGIT** — Startup accelerator, not advisory
- **Geneva Startup Accelerator ecosystem** — Genilem, X, others
- **CERN Entrepreneurship Centre** — Tech transfer support
- **Venturelab / Platform** — Startup support, not advisory
- **Startuptrails** — Demo days, networking

### IO Procurement Consulting Niche
- **寻路** — No Geneva-focused IO procurement advisory boutique identified
- Big Four (KPMG, PwC) handle large IO consulting contracts
- Small Geneva boutiques blocked from IO procurement by complexity

### Tech Startup Advisory
- **Venture capital firms** (not advisory — investing)
- **Law firms** (legal, not strategy)
- **Big Four** (too expensive for early-stage startups)
- **Freelance consultants** (fragmented, inconsistent quality)

## Competitive Positioning Gaps

### Underserved / Differentiated Opportunities
1. **IO procurement advisory for Geneva boutiques** — No Geneva boutique helps other boutiques navigate UN/IO procurement rules
2. **Humanitarian AI strategy** — ICRC, UNHCR, major NGOs need AI strategy but lack internal capability; market not served by Geneva boutiques
3. **Geneva healthtech regulatory navigation** — Swissmedic/EU MDR regulatory strategy for Geneva healthtech startups — genuinely underserved
4. **CERN/EPFL tech transfer advisory** — IP commercialization, spin-off structuring, international market entry for Geneva deep tech
5. **Geneva startup AI governance** — Geneva tech startups deploying AI need governance frameworks but lack budget for Big Four

## Competitive Threats
- Big Four bundling IO/NGO consulting with large institutional contracts
- AI-native advisory platforms (automated consulting) disrupting low-end NGO consulting
- In-house IO/NGO strategy teams absorbing work previously outsourced
- Geneva tech startups defaulting to Zurich VCs/advisors for growth-stage needs
