## Findings

1. **UN/IO AI Policy Coordination — Advisory Demand from Multi-Stakeholder AI Governance Complexity** — The UN Secretary-General's AI Advisory Body (established 2023) and ITU AI standards work are converging in Geneva, creating a distinct advisory demand among Geneva IOs, NGOs, and diplomatic missions for internal AI governance capacity. Geneva institutions face genuine pressure to develop coherent institutional AI positions for multi-stakeholder processes (UNESCO AI Ethics Recommendation, Council of Europe AI Convention, ITU standards). Advisory opportunity: internal AI governance frameworks for Geneva IOs/NGOs; position paper drafting for Geneva-based institutions engaging in AI policy processes; AI policy literacy training for Geneva diplomatic missions. This is distinct from procurement reform (C145 F1) and ISO/IEC technical standards compliance (C129 F1) — this is institutional policy coordination.

2. **AI Safety Research Ecosystem — UNIDIR, EPFL ELLIS, and ICRC as Geneva's Distinct AI Safety Triangle** — UNIDIR (UN Institute for Disarmament Research, Geneva) is the Geneva anchor for AI safety in international security, working on dual-use AI governance, autonomous weapons policy, and responsible AI development in military contexts. EPFL's ELLIS unit on trustworthy AI and large language models represents the academic pillar of this ecosystem. ICRC's digital legal frameworks and autonomous weapons positions (Montreux Document context) represent the humanitarian law anchor. Advisory opportunity: connecting Swiss defense technology firms and dual-use AI companies to UNIDIR's policy research; facilitating academic-industry translation for EPFL AI safety research; ICRC-aligned AI governance frameworks for Geneva tech firms with humanitarian sector clients. Distinct from C129 F1 (ISO/IEC standards) and C129 F5 (humanitarian logistics) — this is the security/arms control/academic nexus of AI safety, not technical standards or field logistics.

3. **Swiss Carbon Verification AI — Advisory for Environmental Markets Digital MRV Infrastructure** — Swiss-based carbon project developers (South Pole, South Pole Group, MinCount) and voluntary carbon market platforms (Xpansiv, Gold Standard) are deploying AI for automated monitoring, reporting, and verification (MRV) of carbon credits — using satellite imagery analysis, supply chain monitoring, and remote sensing. This creates advisory demand from Swiss carbon market actors needing: AI-enabled MRV system selection and implementation; voluntary carbon market integrity compliance (IC-VCM, VCMI); Swiss carbon registry digitalization alignment; EU CBAM AI data requirements integration. Advisory angle: digital MRV infrastructure strategy for Swiss carbon developers; AI compliance for carbon market integrity standards. Distinct from climate fintech investment coverage of prior cycles — this is carbon market operations and MRV technology advisory.

4. **EPFL AI Spin-off Commercialization — Lausanne-Deep Tech Advisory Distinct from CERN Route** — EPFL technology transfer (via EPFL Technology Transfer Office, Enable, and kick-start programs) generates AI spin-offs with distinct needs from CERN route companies: EPFL AI companies focus on AI-native applications (health AI, sustainability AI, robotics), face EPFL-specific IP licensing terms (different from CERN framework), and operate within the Lausanne innovation ecosystem (different VC network, different industry connections from CERN cluster). Advisory opportunity: EPFL IP license negotiation support (distinct from CERN terms); AI regulatory strategy for EPFL health AI spin-offs (Swissmedic/EU MDR); EPFL AI company fundraising strategy within the Lake Geneva deep tech VC ecosystem; international market entry for EPFL AI spin-offs. Distinct from C145 F4 (CERN Tech Transfer) — same advisory category (tech transfer), different institution (EPFL vs CERN), different product type (AI-native applications vs hardware/quantum), different ecosystem (Lausanne vs Geneva-CERN).

5. **Digital Health Platform Regulatory Strategy — Swissmedic/EU MDR/EU HDHS Multi-Layer Compliance** — Digital health platforms (telemedicine platforms, AI diagnostics platforms, health data exchange infrastructure, hospital AI systems) face a layered regulatory challenge in Geneva: Swissmedic SaMD classification, EU MDR for clinical software, EU Health Data Space (EHDS) cross-border data governance requirements, and nFADP data protection compliance simultaneously. This platform-level regulatory complexity is distinct from single-product Swissmedic navigation (C145 F3) — platforms must architect for multi-jurisdictional regulatory compliance from inception. Advisory opportunity: platform-level regulatory architecture design for Geneva healthtech platforms; parallel Swissmedic/EU MDR compliance strategy; EHDS cross-border health data governance for Swiss platforms serving EU markets; QMS integration for platform-scale operations. Greenfield advisory — platform regulatory architecture for healthtech is not currently served by Geneva boutiques.

6. **Humanitarian Logistics AI Governance — Donor-Driven AI Accountability Frameworks for ICRC/UNHCR Field Deployments** — ICRC, UNHCR, and WFP are moving AI systems from pilot to production in humanitarian logistics (demand forecasting, beneficiary matching, supply chain optimization). Donors (SDC, ECHO, FCDO, USAID) are beginning to require AI-specific accountability frameworks as a condition of funding — including AI risk assessments, algorithmic impact evaluations, and data protection documentation for AI deployments in conflict zones. This donor-driven accountability demand creates a specific advisory opportunity: AI governance frameworks for humanitarian logistics AI aligned with donor requirements; ICRC/UNHCR AI deployment assessment methodology; humanitarian AI impact evaluation frameworks; FADP/GDPR compliance documentation for AI processing of beneficiary data. Distinct from C129 F5 (humanitarian logistics digitalization — implementation partners) and C145 F2 (humanitarian AI adoption lag — general strategy) — this is specifically donor-mandated AI accountability compliance for field-deployed humanitarian AI systems.
