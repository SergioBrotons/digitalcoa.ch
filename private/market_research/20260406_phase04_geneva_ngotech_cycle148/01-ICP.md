# 01 — ICP: Geneva NGO/Tech (International Organizations / NGOs / Tech Startups)

## Target Segment: Geneva International Organizations, NGOs, and Tech Startups

### Sub-Segment A: International Organizations (IOs)
- **Profile:** UN agencies (UNOG, UNHCR, WHO, WTO, ITU, UNCTAD, ILO, WMO, ICAO), diplomatic missions, intergovernmental organizations
- **Size:** Major IOs have 1,000-10,000+ staff in Geneva; smaller bodies 50-500
- **Status:** Procurement-driven; heavy compliance burden; Geneva as IO hub stable but evolving

### Sub-Segment B: NGOs
- **Profile:** International NGOs (ICRC, MSF, Save the Children, WWF, Greenpeace, human rights orgs), humanitarian organizations, development NGOs
- **Size:** 20-5,000+ staff; budget-driven; project-based funding
- **Status:** Post-pandemic humanitarian funding pressure; digital transformation accelerating; AI adoption lagging

### Sub-Segment C: Geneva Tech Startups
- **Profile:** Fintech, biotech, healthtech, AI startups, digital platforms, deep tech (CERN/EPFL transfer)
- **Size:** 5-200 employees; seed to Series B
- **Locations:** Geneva (Plan-les-Ouates, CERN, EPFL transfer, FONGIT, Geneva biotech cluster)
- **Status:** VC funding landscape tightening 2025-2026; regulatory导航 (FinTech, HealthTech) creating advisory demand

### Pain Points (Initial Hypotheses)
1. **IO/NGO procurement complexity** — framework agreements, vendor registration, compliance requirements block access
2. **NGO funding pressure** — post-pandemic donor fatigue, humanitarian crises competing for funding
3. **Tech startup regulatory navigation** — FinTech licensing, HealthTech Swissmedic approval, AI regulatory uncertainty
4. **IO/NGO AI adoption lag** — AI adoption in humanitarian/development sector significantly behind private sector
5. **CERN/EPFL tech transfer** — IP commercialization complexity, spin-off financing, international market entry
6. **Digital transformation capacity gaps** — IOs/NGOs lack internal digital transformation expertise

### Buying Triggers (Initial Hypotheses)
- New strategic plan or organizational redesign
- Regulatory change requiring specialist knowledge (FinTech license, Swissmedic approval)
- AI mandate from IO/NGO leadership or board
- Fundraising campaign or major donor requirement
- Tech startup funding round (regulatory milestone)
- CERN/EPFL spin-off creation

### Buying Barriers (Initial Hypotheses)
- IO procurement bureaucracy (long vendor registration, framework restrictions)
- NGO budget constraints and donor restrictions on consulting spend
- Tech startup limited budget for advisory services
- IO/NGO data security concerns with external consultants
- AI tool adoption concerns (humanitarian principles, data protection)
