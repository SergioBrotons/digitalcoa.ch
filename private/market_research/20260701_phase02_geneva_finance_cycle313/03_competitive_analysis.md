## 6 NET-NEW Competitive Findings (Distinct from C214–C313 Phase 01)

### Finding 01: DDQ Form-Completion Vendor Market Gap — Auditing the New Competitive Entry

**Mechanism:** The most significant C313 competitive shift in the boutique WM market is the entry of DDQ form-completion vendors — third-party tools offered to boutique WM firms to "simplify" institutional DDQ response. These vendors process portfolio construction methodology, mandate-specific authorization chain logic, and vendor processor chain details through their own AI. The vendor market is operating on click-through terms the boutique has not evaluated. The exposure created by the form-completion vendor is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 carrier form-completion finding, but applied to the institutional DDQ context. No Geneva competitor is auditing which form-completion vendor the boutique is using, what data that vendor processes, whether its terms create nFADP Art.9 exposure, and whether the response it produces is generic (form-completion vendor output) or anchored to the boutique's specific authorization chain evidence.

The first Geneva boutique WM service to publish a DDQ form-completion vendor audit methodology owns the category. The service is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 carrier form-completion vendor review, applied to the institutional DDQ context. The service includes: vendor terms review, data flow analysis, response generic-vs-anchored assessment, alternative direct-DDQ-response methodology.

**C313 Phase 01 bridge:** Phase 01 named the carrier form-completion vector; Phase 02 names the DDQ form-completion vector as the parallel WM manifestation through the institutional client channel. Same exposure mechanism, different document, different vendor category, different institutional client relationship.

**Market signal:** Boutique WM firms using DDQ form-completion vendor tools are submitting responses that are structurally indistinguishable from each other — the institutional client's investment committee reads past the generic language, and the boutique's specific authorization chain evidence is invisible. The boutique's mandate is at risk because the response is generic.

**IMO differentiation angle:** DDQ form-completion vendor audit + alternative direct-response methodology — only Geneva service auditing which DDQ form-completion vendor the boutique uses and providing the direct-response alternative anchored to the boutique's own authorization chain documentation.

**Market signal:** Geneva boutique WM firms winning new institutional mandates in 2026 are creating documented authorization chain gaps within weeks of mandate start. The mid-year questionnaire 30-60 days later will surface the gap.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Mandate-handover authorization documentation — only Geneva service producing authorization chain documentation refreshed at the rate of mandate book change, with documented handover for both mandate wins and mandate losses.

**Mechanism:** The Geneva boutique WM market has a structural service gap in portfolio construction vendor succession / data portability planning. No Geneva competitor is building portfolio construction vendor succession methodology that addresses: pre-vendor-selection succession terms in vendor contracts (data export obligations, transition period, controller-change disclosure); mid-vendor-relationship succession monitoring (vendor financial signals, acquisition signals, service-termination signals); post-vendor-exit response (data portability execution, controller-change documentation, successor-controller audit, institutional client notification architecture).

The service is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 vendor succession / data portability finding, applied to the portfolio construction vendor context with its specific institutional client mandate data complexity and Art.9 obligations. The first Geneva boutique WM service to publish a portfolio construction vendor succession methodology owns the category.

**C313 Phase 01 bridge:** Phase 01 named the vendor succession gap for legal (Article 321 obligations); Phase 02 names the portfolio construction vendor succession gap for WM (Article 9 obligations + institutional client contractual obligations). Same mechanism (vendor exit creates data exposure the boutique is accountable for), different obligation framework (legal professional secrecy vs. financial data protection + institutional client contractual).

**Market signal:** Portfolio construction AI vendors are entering acquisition / consolidation phase in 2026. The boutique WM MD has no succession planning methodology and no contractual terms addressing vendor exit. When vendor exit happens, the institutional client mandate data transfers to a successor controller without boutique or institutional client consent.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Portfolio construction vendor succession / data portability planning — only Geneva service building the pre-vendor-selection succession terms, mid-vendor-relationship monitoring, and post-vendor-exit response architecture for portfolio construction AI vendor relationships with institutional client mandate data exposure.
