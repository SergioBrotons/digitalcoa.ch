## Section 2: The Most Urgent Deadline in Your Portfolio

### Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (approximately 12 weeks away)

Swissmedic has confirmed: **there is no grace period.**
If your practice uses AI diagnostic imaging or AI symptom triage classified as high-risk SaMD (Software as a Medical Device), your compliance obligations are due on July 1, 2026 — not December 31, 2026.

**What this means:**
- AI diagnostic imaging: immediate Swissdamed obligation on July 1
- AI symptom triage: immediate Swissdamed obligation on July 1
- No extension, no transition period, no grace

**What Swissmedic requires:**
- Technical documentation for high-risk SaMD
- Quality management system alignment
- Post-market surveillance documentation
- Incident reporting mechanism

**The urgency:**
Most professional services deadlines have a grace period or transition.
Swissdamed does not.
12 weeks from today, July 1, 2026 is the deadline.

## Section 3: What's Already Active (Before July 1)

### nFADP Art. 5(c) — Active Since 2023

If your AI makes decisions about patients based solely on automated processing — patient selection, triage prioritization, care pathway assignment — you're already required to have:
1. A documented DPIA for each AI system processing personal data
2. A mechanism for patients to request human review
3. Records of your AI governance decisions

**FDPIC is building healthcare AI enforcement cases.**
The 2024/2025 Annual Report confirms healthcare as an enforcement priority.
July 1, 2026 is your Swissdamed deadline.
2023 was your nFADP Art. 5(c) deadline.
