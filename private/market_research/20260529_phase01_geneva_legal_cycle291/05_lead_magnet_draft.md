## What Your Carrier Is Actually Asking For: The Geneva Small Law Firm AI Governance Briefing

*A 90-minute briefing for Geneva attorneys at 2–5 person firms*

---

You renewed your professional liability insurance. The form had new questions.

Questions about AI. About which tools you use. About what your policy says about AI-assisted work. About what happens if something goes wrong.

You answered something. You weren't sure what you were certifying.

That uncertainty is the Geneva small law firm AI governance problem in miniature. The question arrived before the answer existed. The carrier is asking a question the industry hasn't answered yet. Your signature is on the form. The commitment is yours.

This is not a training session on AI tools. This is a 90-minute structured examination of what your carrier's questions actually commit you to, what adequate AI governance means for a 2–5 attorney firm, and what you can build before the documentation window closes.

---

## What This Briefing Addresses

### The Carrier Attestation Question

Your carrier is asking you to confirm your AI review process is "adequate." Adequate against which standard — the form doesn't say. What you signed commits you to something the form doesn't define.

This is not a documentation problem. This is a personal liability problem. The signature is yours. The commitment is yours. The standard is undefined.

**What this briefing does:** Walks through what your carrier's questions actually commit you to, what "adequate" has come to mean through carrier iteration patterns, and what a proportionate response looks like at your firm's scale.

---

### The Client AI Question

More clients are asking: "Are you using AI for my matter?" Some want AI efficiency. Some explicitly don't. Some want to know how you use it.

You don't have a script for how to answer professionally. Your engagement letters predate your AI use. They don't address it.

**What this briefing does:** Maps the specific gap between what your engagement letters cover and what your delivery practice involves, and outlines a proportionate engagement letter architecture for new matters.

---

### The Shadow AI Problem

You banned ChatGPT. Your associates are still using it. On client matters. Through personal accounts you can't see.

The ban didn't stop the behavior. It drove it underground. Your exposure is the same. Your visibility is lower.

**What this briefing does:** Names the structural problem honestly — banning AI without providing a proportionate alternative produces shadow AI, not zero AI — and outlines what a small-firm AI governance policy actually looks like.

---

### The Verification Question

Your carrier wants to know you have human oversight of AI-assisted work. What does "oversight" mean when AI generates a contract clause in seconds and you're responsible for everything that goes out under your name?

The supervision obligation is real. The discharge mechanism is structurally undefined.

**What this briefing does:** Provides the specific documentation architecture for AI-assisted legal work that creates evidence of human oversight without requiring a compliance department.

---

### The Monday Morning Question

You don't want a framework. You want to know what you can do Monday morning.

This briefing is structured differently. We go through the six decisions you need to make before the documentation window closes. Each decision is specific. Each has a concrete next step. You leave with a written summary and a 90-day roadmap.

**What this briefing does:** Gives you the six decisions, the reasoning behind each, and the specific action for each. Nothing theoretical. Everything applicable.
