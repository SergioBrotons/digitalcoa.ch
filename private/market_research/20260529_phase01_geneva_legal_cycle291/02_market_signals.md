## Current Problems and Pain Points

### 1. The Carrier Attestation Gap (NEW in C291)

Law firms are now being **asked by their professional liability insurers** to describe how they use AI. This is a new dynamic. 

- Insurers starting to require AI usage disclosure in renewal questionnaires
- No clear standard for what "adequate AI governance" looks like at small firm scale
- Attorneys don't know what to answer when their carrier asks about AI use
- Fear that disclosing AI use could lead to higher premiums or coverage exclusions
- Fear that NOT disclosing AI use could void coverage if something goes wrong
- The attestation question: "Are we covered if AI assists in a matter and something goes wrong?"

This is the new trigger point. Insurance renewal season is becoming an AI governance wake-up call.

### 2. The Internal Shadow AI Problem

Despite formal bans on ChatGPT and similar tools:
- Individual attorneys continuing to use personal ChatGPT accounts for client work
- No visibility for the managing partner on what's actually being used
- Risk compounds: once data leaves the firm through an employee's personal account, the firm's control is gone
- Problem is structural: they banned tools without providing an alternative that fits their workflow

### 3. Client Expectations Shifting

- More clients asking "are you using AI for my matter?"
- Some clients explicitly requesting AI NOT be used for their data
- Others expecting AI-driven efficiency to reduce fees
- Attorneys caught between: deliver AI efficiency vs. maintain absolute confidentiality
- No script for how to answer client AI questions professionally

### 4. The FINMA-Adjacent Client Complexity

Growing subset of clients in Geneva with financial services exposure (fintech, asset management, wealth structures). These clients carry:
- Higher regulatory sensitivity
- More complex data classification
- Greater consequence if a confidentiality breach occurs
- Attorneys treating these matters differently but without a systematic approach

### 5. The Hallucination Problem — Real Incidents Now Visible

Unlike abstract "AI might hallucinate" warnings, small firm attorneys are now seeing:
- AI-generated contract clauses that look correct but conflict with Swiss law
- AI-generated case citations that do not exist (Forty-Four Avocats published an alert on this, November 2024)
- AI-drafted correspondence that attorneys almost sent before verification caught the errors
- The concrete fear: "I almost submitted something wrong to a court or a client"

### 6. The Training Gap — They Know They Have It

Post-OAG and SAV training, attorneys realize:
- Understanding AI tools ≠ knowing how to make governance decisions
- They have awareness of risks but no framework for systematic management
- The problem is no longer "should we use AI?" — it's "which AI, for what, with what controls?"

## Trigger Moments (Updated)

1. **Insurance renewal** — AI questions on the questionnaire, no good answer to give
2. **Client asks about AI policy** — they feel exposed, no professional response ready
3. **Near-miss incident** — almost submitted AI-generated error to court or client
4. **Competitor/colleague adopting AI** — the "falling behind" anxiety
5. **News incident** — another law firm AI failure in the press
6. **Staff starting to use AI tools** — without any firm guidance or policy

## Swiss-Specific Market Context

- **68% of Swiss law firms have banned ChatGPT** for professional secrecy reasons (stable finding)
- **SAV/FSA AI guidelines (June 2024)** — widely known but practically insufficient
- **CCBE AI guide (October 2025)** — raising awareness but not solving the small-firm problem
- **Switzerland AI Convention** — ratified February 2025, practical implications still unclear to practitioners
- **EU AI Act** — Geneva firms with EU client exposure already affected; August 2026 stricter rules coming
- **revFADP (September 2023)** — already applies to AI data processing, attorneys still learning what it means for their practice

## Notes

The C291 signals show the market has moved from "should we use AI?" anxiety to "how do we govern AI decisions responsibly at our scale?" The insurance angle is the new pressure point that did not exist 12 months ago with this intensity.
