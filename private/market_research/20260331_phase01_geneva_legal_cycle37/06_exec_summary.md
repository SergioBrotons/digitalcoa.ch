## Situation

Geneva small law firms (2–5 attorneys) face a widening gap between their actual AI use and their legal obligations. The majority of associates and paralegals are already using AI tools individually (90%+), but 43% of firms have NO formal AI policy and NO plans to create one (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026). Meanwhile, FADP Art. 5(c) has been operative since September 1, 2023 — making this a present obligation, not a future risk.

## Key Findings — Cycle 37

### Primary Urgency: 43% NO Formal AI Policy + NO Plans

Wolters Kluwer / Thomson Reuters March 2026 confirms that 43% of law firms have no formal AI policy and no intention of creating one. This is the most urgent market signal in the portfolio — it defines the urgency gap between actual AI use and formal governance.

### The Efficiency Paradox

65% of law firms using gen AI save 1–5 hours per week (Legartis 2026). This efficiency is real but informal — happening at the individual level without formal governance. Firms with formal AI strategy are 4x more likely to experience significant benefits. The gap between individual efficiency and firm-level governance is where exposure lives.

### FADP Is Already Operative — Not Future Risk

FADP Art. 5(c) (transparency), Art. 26 (Privacy by Design), and Art. 27 (DPIA) are all active since September 1, 2023. The FDPIC expects purpose, functionality, and data source disclosure for every AI tool in use. FDPIC enforcement is absent from the legal sector — but absent is not immune. The enforcement pattern (transport, retail, social media) suggests the professional services wave is coming.

### The May 2026 Pressure Event

Federal Council First Draft: ~8 WEEKS away (May 2026). digitalswitzerland milestone confirmed. This is the next external pressure event that will create urgency across the Geneva legal market.

### Competitive White Space Confirmed — 10th+ Cycle

No Geneva boutique is serving the small law firm AI governance ICP. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL is the only structural competitor — confirmed enterprise-only (Chambers 2026). VISCHER, Walder Wyss, Homburger, and Lenz & Staehelin are all enterprise-only. First-mover window: ~12–18 months intact.
