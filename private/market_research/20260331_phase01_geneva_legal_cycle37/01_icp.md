## Cycle 37 | 2026-03-31

## ICP Definition

**Vertical:** Geneva small law firms — 2 to 5 attorneys (solo to boutique-adjacent)

**Core ICP Characteristics:**
- Solo practitioner to 5-lawyer firm, Geneva canton
- Primary practice areas: SMB corporate, real estate, family law, succession, employment
- Already using AI individually (90%+ individual use confirmed Cycle 35–36 carry-forward)
- NO formal AI policy (43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans — Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026)
- Under pressure from clients who use AI; managing director personally liable under FADP
- Data-sensitive: client confidentiality non-negotiable; Swiss data residency requirement
- Primary regulatory exposure: FADP Art. 5(c) (active since Sep 1, 2023), potential EU AI Act Annex III for contract analysis/due diligence tools
- Decision-maker: Managing director / partner personally exposed under FADP criminal liability (up to CHF 250,000 for intentional failures)

**Addressable Need:**
- Governance framework for AI tools already in use (Shadow AI problem)
- DPIA capability for high-risk AI-supported processing
- FADP compliance documentation without disrupting workflow
- Competitive differentiation through formal AI governance

**Primary Urgency Levers (carry-forward + Cycle 37 additions):**
1. 43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026) — PRIMARY
2. 65% save 1–5h/week with generative AI (Legartis 2026)
3. 90%+ individual AI use confirmed (Shadow AI = primary risk)
4. 46-point governance gap (69% individual vs 23% formal policies)
5. FADP Art. 5(c) active since Sep 1, 2023 (2.5+ years operative)
6. **Art. 21 FADP — right to human review (NEW from Cycle 37)** — automated decisions producing legal effects must allow human review; directly relevant to AI-assisted legal analysis
7. CASUS Swiss-hosted zero data retention confirmed (Swiss solution)
8. Geneva AI Action Summit 2027 confirmed
9. FADP consultation end-2026 (next regulatory pressure)
10. Managing director personal liability (FADP criminal route)
11. Federal Council First Draft May 2026 (~8 WEEKS)
12. **Dual compliance burden (NEW from Cycle 37)** — Swiss firms with EU nexus must comply with both FADP and EU AI Act

**Secondary Urgency Levers (carry-forward):**
- 40% potential workflow reduction from AI
- 14% specialized vs 56% general tool adoption depth gap
- Spellbook competitive signal (SMB law firm AI targeting)
- FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL only structural competitor confirmed (enterprise-only)
