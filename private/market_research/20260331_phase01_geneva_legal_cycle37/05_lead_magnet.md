---

### Section 3: The FADP Compliance Stack for Small Law

**Purpose:** Operational clarity — what exactly does FADP require, in what order.

**Content:**
- **Layer 1: Disclosure** (Art. 5(c)) — purpose + functionality + data sources for every AI tool in use
- **Layer 2: Privacy by Design** (Art. 26) — AI procurement assessment before adopting new tools
- **Layer 3: DPIA** (Art. 27) — required for high-risk AI processing (contract analysis, due diligence = likely candidates)
- **Layer 4: Human Review** (Art. 21) — clients have right to human review of automated decisions producing legal effects; law firms must ensure AI-assisted outputs can be reviewed by a human lawyer
- **Layer 5: Shadow AI Audit** — Decision 0 — inventory of AI tools already in use without formal approval
- Timeline: All five layers are present obligations — no grace period

**Decision 0 — Shadow AI Audit:**
1. What AI tools are my associates using individually?
2. What client data are those tools processing?
3. Do I have documentation for those tools?
4. Can I answer FDPIC's three questions: purpose, functionality, data sources?

### Section 4: The Contract Analysis AI Problem

**Purpose:** High-risk AI use case specific to law firms — creates urgency for DPIA.

**Content:**
- AI contract analysis = likely EU AI Act Annex III high-risk for EU-nexus matters
- AI due diligence = likely high-risk under EU AI Act
- FADP DPIA required regardless of EU AI Act applicability
- DPIA for AI contract review: purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation
- CASUS as Swiss-hosted DPIA-compliant alternative

**Key Question:** "If you're using AI to analyze contracts, have you documented the AI's purpose, data sources, and accuracy limitations in a DPIA?"

### Section 8: The 30/60/90-Day Governance Sequence

**Purpose:** Actionable pathway — make it concrete, not abstract.

**Content:**

**Day 0–30: Shadow AI Audit**
- Inventory: What AI tools are in use? (associates, paralegals, admin)
- Documentation: For each tool — purpose, functionality, data sources
- Risk triage: Which tools process client data? Which are high-risk?
- Decision: Approve, restrict, or retire each tool

**Day 30–60: FADP Documentation**
- Draft AI use policy (purpose, functionality, data sources for each approved tool)
- Draft DPIA for high-risk AI processing (contract analysis, due diligence)
- Implement Privacy by Design checklist for new AI tool evaluation
- Assign AI governance responsibility (managing director or delegate)

**Day 60–90: Formalization**
- Board/founder adoption of AI use policy
- Staff acknowledgment of AI policy
- CASUS or equivalent Swiss-hosted AI tool evaluation for high-risk use cases
- Annual review trigger for FADP compliance
