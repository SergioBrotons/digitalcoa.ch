# Market Signals: Cross-Vertical — C296 Phase 05

## Cross-Vertical Market Patterns

---

## The Universal Retrospective Confirmation Effect

Every vertical arrives at the accountability conversation through the same mechanism: they have already experienced the triggering event and are now processing what it means. The ICP is not asking "what might go wrong" — they are asking "what did I already commit to when I signed."

**The triggering events by vertical:**
- Legal: PI carrier attestation signed; associate using AI without disclosure
- Finance: Institutional client questionnaire responded to; carrier renewal approaching
- Consulting: Client DDQ received; engagement declined or modified
- Healthcare: Carrier attestation signed; patient brought in AI-generated analysis

The underlying mechanism is identical: retrospective exposure recognition.

## Seven Cross-Vertical Market Patterns

### Pattern 1 — The Three-Party Delegation Chain
Across all four verticals, the accountable professional has accountability for decisions made two steps down the delegation chain:
- Legal: Partner → Associate → Consumer AI tool
- Finance: MD → Portfolio Manager → AI recommendation engine
- Consulting: Partner → Sub-Consultant → AI tool
- Healthcare: Physician → Staff → AI administrative tool

The person with accountability has the least agency over the AI decisions creating that accountability. This is not a firm-specific failure — it is a structural feature of how AI was adopted across all four verticals.

### Pattern 2 — The Attestation Stampede Effect
Carrier attestation deadlines (2–4 weeks) create urgency that produces retroactive documentation using vendor-provided templates. The documentation is signed and becomes the evidence standard the firm is measured against. The attempt to comply produces self-incriminating evidence. This pattern is named in healthcare, legal, and finance simultaneously.

### Pattern 3 — The Documentation Architecture Impossibility
The evidence standard implied by attestation forms (contemporaneous documented human authorization for each AI-assisted decision) is architecturally unobtainable with boutique internal processes. The processes were not designed for this evidence purpose. The gap is not procedural — it is architectural. The fix is not a better form — it is a different documentation architecture.

### Pattern 4 — The Cross-Mandate Coherence Paradox
Four independent documentation systems operate simultaneously:
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (finance)
- Institutional client questionnaire responses
- nFADP ROPA/DPIA
- Carrier attestation evidence

All four describe the same AI governance reality with different evidence standards, update cycles, and disclosure scopes. No Geneva methodology exists for cross-referencing them. They are likely inconsistent without any systematic check.

### Pattern 5 — The Institutional Comparison Distortion
Institutional clients send questionnaires to full manager rosters simultaneously without disclosure. The aggregate of independent responses creates a de facto market standard based on documentation quality, not governance architecture. Boutique with weak governance and strong documentation outperforms boutique with strong governance and weak documentation.

### Pattern 6 — The Formation Exclusion Mechanism
Standards form in governed rooms (Federal Council AI deliberation, FINMA/SRO frameworks, SAV/FMH guidance development). The target population — independent boutiques, 2–50 person firms — structurally cannot access these rooms. The standards that apply to them form without their participation.

### Pattern 7 — The Insurance Market Pricing Distortion
Insurance brokers across all four verticals are already pricing AI governance risk into renewals. This is happening before any standardized SMB framework exists. The non-standardized underwriting creates current commercial distortion: firms with documentation are treated differently from firms without. This is live and immediate, not future risk.

## Market Sizing Signal

Geneva has an estimated population of:
- Small law firms (2–5 attorneys): ~200–400 firms
- Boutique wealth management (50–200 employees): ~150–300 firms
- Small consulting boutiques (2–50 employees): ~400–800 firms
- Independent medical practices (2–10 professionals): ~300–500 practices

Total addressable: ~1,050–2,000 firms across four verticals.

Average engagement value: CHF 2,200 (90-min briefing) + CHF 1,500/month retainer.

The market is being created by regulatory urgency, not searched for by buyers. The firms that build documentation architecture first will define the market standard.
