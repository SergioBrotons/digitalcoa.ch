# Compliance Context: Cross-Vertical — C296 Phase 05

## The Regulatory Framework Across All Four Verticals

---

## The Universal Regulatory Anchor: nFADP Art.63

The revised Federal Act on Data Protection (in force September 1, 2023) applies to all four verticals. The critical provision is Art.63: criminal liability for unauthorized personal data processing. The CHF 250,000 ceiling is personal, not just entity-level. The managing director, partner, or practice leader who signed the engagement letter, the vendor agreement, or the carrier attestation has personal exposure.

**The timing shift (May 2025):**
FDPIC enforcement posture confirmed that nFADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data. Enforcement is live now. The "we're waiting to see how enforcement develops" delay strategy is no longer available.

## Vertical-Specific Regulatory Frameworks

### Legal
- **nFADP Art.9**: DPA requirements for AI tool vendors — violated by most small firms using consumer AI
- **Art.167a CPC / Art.321 CP**: January 2025 amendment creates external vs. in-house counsel accountability split
- **SAV Guidelines (June 2024)**: Principles-based, no boutique-scale implementation path
- **Art.63 nFADP**: Criminal liability — personal exposure unnamed by most firms

### Finance
- **FINMA Guidance 08/2024**: Seven-dimension framework operative, no proportionality guidance for boutiques
- **nFADP Art.8**: Profiling notifications for AI-assisted portfolio construction
- **nFADP Art.20/21**: Right to explanation conflicting with proprietary methodology
- **Carrier third-gen attestation**: Evidence standard requires documented authorization moments
- **SRO vacuum**: VQF/FINIA have said nothing; primary regulatory relationship unavailable as reference

### Consulting
- **nFADP Art.9**: Processor cascade through sub-consultant chain — undisclosed exposure
- **Client DDQ**: Contract risk through undisclosed acceptance pathway
- **FINMA extension**: Carrier attestation extending FINMA obligations to supply chain
- **Proposal methodology**: Art.28 CO professional work product attribution problem

### Healthcare
- **FMH human oversight**: Stated without operational definition — governance impossibility at small scale
- **nFADP Art.5(c)**: Treatment consent vs. data processing consent vs. AI disclosure — mutually incompatible
- **Swissmedic**: Medical device AI governance separate framework
- **Patient-generated AI content**: Three-party liability chain without protocol

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Patterns

### Pattern 1 — The Attestation Architecture Incompatibility
Carrier attestation documentation is designed for underwriting risk evaluation. It is architecturally incompatible with criminal defense documentation (nFADP Art.63). The evidence standards are different. The evidentiary thresholds are different. The defense requirements are different. Firms building documentation to satisfy carrier attestation may be building the wrong architecture for their personal exposure.

### Pattern 2 — The Documentation Window Irreversibility
The documentation window is a present-tense positioning event. Organizations that build documentation architecture now will have demonstrable adequacy when the ecosystem solidifies. Organizations that wait will be building retroactive evidence — which reveals itself as retroactive when examined. The window has not closed. But it will not stay open indefinitely.

### Pattern 3 — The Formation Participation Asymmetry
The standards that apply to Geneva boutiques are forming in rooms they cannot access. Federal Council AI deliberation, FINMA/SRO frameworks, SAV/FMH guidance development — participation in these rooms is structurally unavailable to independent boutiques. The formation exclusion means the standards that will govern them are being set without their input.

### Pattern 4 — The Multi-Track Obligation Accumulation
Four regulatory tracks are building simultaneously:
- nFADP (live, enforcement maturing)
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (live, proportionality undefined)
- Client/institutional questionnaires (market pressure, not regulation)
- Carrier attestation (underwriting pressure, not regulation)

The tracks do not coordinate. A firm may be simultaneously violating nFADP, failing FINMA expectations, and unable to satisfy carrier requirements — while institutional clients are comparing them against peers who have equally incomplete documentation.
