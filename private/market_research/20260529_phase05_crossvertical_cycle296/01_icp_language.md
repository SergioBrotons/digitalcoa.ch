# ICP Language: Cross-Vertical Synthesis — C296
## Phase 05 — Cross-Vertical ICP Language Across All Four Verticals

**Cycle:** C296 | **Date:** 2026-05-29
**Source verticals:** Phase 01 Legal · Phase 02 Finance · Phase 03 Consulting · Phase 04 Healthcare

---

## The Universal Entry Point: Retrospective Commitment Without Comprehension

Across all four verticals, the ICP arrives at the accountability question through the same door:

> *"I committed to something. I don't know what. And now I'm finding out."*

This is not a hypothetical risk. It is a present-tense retrospective recognition that the ICP signed, attested to, or otherwise bound themselves to an AI governance standard — and is only now discovering what that commitment actually required. The market is driven by retrospective exposure recognition, not anticipatory planning. This pattern has been consistent across cycles. What changes cycle to cycle is the specific mechanism through which the commitment was made, and the specific moment of discovery.

## Cross-Vertical Language Map — C296

### 1. The Continuity Authorization Vocabulary

*New for C296: The framing has shifted from "I used AI" to "AI has been running continuously and I authorized it once."*

| Vertical | The Continuity Authorization Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "I authorized the AI tool at the firm level in 2024. I never authorized specific queries. Those queries are on the record." |
| **Finance** | "We authorized the portfolio AI system. It runs continuously. Every trade is an AI-assisted decision. There's no authorization moment for each trade." |
| **Consulting** | "I authorized the sub-consultant's engagement. I didn't authorize their AI tool. I don't know when that AI ran or what it produced." |
| **Healthcare** | "I activated AI documentation assist. It's been running for 18 months. I documented the day I signed up. I didn't document each patient interaction." |

The continuity authorization vocabulary names a specific structural problem: AI operates continuously, but the accountability framework assumes discrete authorization moments. The ICP authorized the system — not the specific AI actions the system has taken continuously since.

### 2. The Dual-Track Documentation Anxiety

*New for C296: ICPs are beginning to understand that administrative compliance documentation and criminal defense documentation are different instruments with different evidentiary standards — and they don't know which one they're building.*

| Vertical | Dual-Track Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "My carrier attestation documentation — would that hold up if there's a criminal investigation? Those are different standards, right?" |
| **Finance** | "We built our AI governance documentation for the carrier. If FINMA opens a file, is that documentation built for the right proceeding?" |
| **Consulting** | "The client asked us to confirm human review. We documented it. But if something goes wrong, is that the same as demonstrating we actually reviewed?" |
| **Healthcare** | "The carrier form asks me to confirm adequate AI governance. If there's an adverse outcome and the FMH asks the same question — is my carrier documentation evidence of the same thing?" |

The dual-track anxiety is emerging across all four verticals simultaneously: the ICP has built documentation for one evidentiary standard and doesn't know whether it satisfies another.

### 3. The Embedded Obligation Discovery Vocabulary

*New for C296: The discovery that existing client contracts already contain AI governance obligations — through standard boilerplate language that was never evaluated as an AI governance commitment.*

| Vertical | Embedded Obligation Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "The client sent us their standard terms. There's a clause about 'service delivery standards' and 'technology integration.' We didn't think of that as an AI governance clause." |
| **Finance** | "Our mandate contracts have a clause about 'investment methodology transparency.' When the institutional client sent their AI questionnaire, I realized that clause was already an AI governance commitment." |
| **Consulting** | "The engagement letter says we need to be able to explain our analytical methodology. We signed it two years ago. We couldn't explain it today." |
| **Healthcare** | "Our patient services agreement has a clause about treatment approach documentation. The AI documentation assist we activated affects that clause. We never read it that way." |

The embedded obligation discovery is a new entry-point vocabulary for C296: the ICP discovers they are in present-tense breach of contractual obligations embedded in their existing client contracts — not because they signed a new AI-specific clause, but because standard contract language that has always existed now applies to AI-assisted delivery.

### 4. The Processor Chain Liability Vocabulary

*New for C296: The three-party chain is not a theoretical concern — it is a present-tense structural exposure that the ICP has never mapped.*

| Vertical | Processor Chain Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "I have co-counsel on two matters. They use AI. I'm the attorney of record for the client. I don't know what AI my co-counsel used or when." |
| **Finance** | "We have a sub-advisor relationship. The sub-advisor's team uses AI tools. Our name is on the portfolio. We have no processor agreement with them covering AI." |
| **Consulting** | "My sub-consultant used AI on a client deliverable. My firm's name is on the deliverable. I don't know what AI they used, and I can't document it." |
| **Healthcare** | "I refer patients to a specialist. The specialist uses AI in their diagnostics. I referred the patient. The patient came back with an AI-assisted diagnosis. I'm the referring physician." |

The processor chain vocabulary names a specific structural exposure: the accountable professional is the last discloseable entity in a chain that includes AI decisions they did not make, cannot document, and cannot stop without losing the relationship.

### 5. The Documentation Window Vocabulary

*New for C296: The window language has shifted from "approaching" to "closed." The ICP is beginning to understand the irreversibility.*

| Vertical | Documentation Window Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "The decisions I made in 2024 using AI — I can't go back and document those decisions now. The documentation window for those decisions is closed." |
| **Finance** | "The portfolio AI has been running for 18 months. The human authorization moments that should have existed — they didn't happen. We can't recreate them retroactively." |
| **Consulting** | "We did AI-assisted work in 2024 and 2025. We have no record of which clients received AI-assisted deliverables. We can't produce that record now." |
| **Healthcare** | "The AI documentation assist has been running for 18 months. Each note was authored under my name. The AI's contribution is invisible. The window to document that authorization is closed." |

The documentation window vocabulary for C296 has moved past "approaching deadline" to "closed irreversibly on decisions already made." The ICP is beginning to understand that the window was a positioning event, not a compliance deadline — and that it closed before they understood it was open.

---

### 6. The Geneva Informal Consensus Vocabulary

*New for C296: The ICP has begun to understand that "what adequate looks like" is being formed through informal channels — and that they are outside those channels.*

| Vertical | Informal Consensus Language |
|---|---|
| **Legal** | "I hear from colleagues that the SAV is telling firms X. I don't know if that's accurate because the SAV hasn't published it." |
| **Finance** | "FINMA hasn't said anything specific about boutique WM AI governance. But the carriers are all asking the same things. Where did that come from?" |
| **Consulting** | "There's no SRO for consulting. The carriers have effectively set the standard. I don't know where they coordinated on it." |
| **Healthcare** | "FMH says 'adequate human oversight.' A colleague told me HUG has internal AI guidance. I don't know if that's what 'adequate' actually means." |

The informal consensus vocabulary names a specific structural condition: the operative standard is being formed through informal Geneva channels (carrier coordination, institutional circuit, peer network) before any formal publication. The ICP is being measured against a standard they cannot read.

## ICP Psychographic Notes — C296

**Retrospective exposure recognition** remains the primary market driver, but the mechanism has shifted:

- **C294–C295 ICP**: "I signed something and now I know there's a problem."
- **C296 ICP**: "I signed something, and I'm discovering there are multiple overlapping accountability tracks — each with different evidence standards — and I don't know which documentation I built satisfies which one."

The C296 ICP has developed a second-order awareness: they know that accountability is multi-track, and they know they haven't mapped the tracks. This creates a different urgency signature than the C294–C295 ICP — more specific, more anxious, more precisely targeted at documentation architecture rather than general awareness.

**Scale disadvantage** is no longer abstractly acknowledged — it is operationally felt. The C296 ICP explicitly names the structural impossibility of building enterprise-grade documentation with a three-person operation. This creates a specific receptivity to a service that is designed for their scale, not a scaled-down version of enterprise guidance.

**Language preference**: C296 ICP responds to structural precision, not aspirational framing. "Continuous AI" vs. "AI tool" is a meaningful distinction they feel immediately. The ICP who has been operating a continuous AI system without documented authorization moments recognizes the problem the moment it is named precisely.
