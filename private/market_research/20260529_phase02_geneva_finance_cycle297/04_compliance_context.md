## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Environment for Boutique WM AI Accountability

### 1. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — AI in Financial Services

**Current status:** In force. Published by FINMA, addresses AI use in Swiss financial services.

**Boutique WM application:**
- Sets expectation for documented AI governance
- Defines "adequate AI governance controls" without proportionate methodology for boutique scale
- Human oversight principle stated without operational endpoint definition for boutique operations
- Evidence standard: documentation of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions

**Key obligations:**
- AI inventory: documented inventory of AI systems in use
- Authorization chain: documented human authorization preceding AI-assisted decisions
- Oversight: documented human oversight of AI system operation
- Risk management: AI risk integration into existing risk management framework

**Boutique WM exposure:**
- Evidence standard for authorization chain is operational record, not policy document
- Portfolio construction AI as specific accountability category with own evidence requirements
- No FINMA-published proportionate methodology for boutique WM (50–200 employees)
- SRO has provided no Geneva-specific interpretation of FINMA guidance

**FINMA enforcement posture:**
- No specific FINMA enforcement action against boutique WM for AI governance failures
- Carrier attestation instruments operating as de facto enforcement mechanism
- Carrier third-generation questions targeting portfolio construction AI specifically
- Third-generation evidence standard requires specific human authorization moments, not general policy compliance

### 3. nFADP — Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (as revised)

**Current status:** In force. Applies directly to AI systems processing personal data. FDPIC confirmed May 2025 enforcement posture shift from guidance-first to enforcement-live.

**Key articles for boutique WM AI accountability:**

**Art. 5 — Lawful basis:**
- AI processing of client financial data requires lawful basis
- Boutique WM using AI vendor platforms may be operating without documented lawful basis assessment
- Processor agreements with AI vendors required under Art.9

**Art. 6 — Purpose limitation:**
- AI system purpose must be defined and limited
- Vendor terms may expand purpose beyond what boutique documented
- Purpose limitation documentation gap is present-tense exposure

**Art. 8 — Profiling notification:**
- AI-assisted portfolio construction is profiling under Art.8
- Clients have right to notification of profiling and right to human review
- Profiling notification is present-tense obligation boutiques are not addressing as category
- Named obligation not addressed in any Geneva boutique WM documentation program

**Art. 9 — Third-party processor:**
- AI vendor as processor requires Art.9 data processing agreement
- Click-through terms may create undisclosed sub-processor chains
- Carrier third-generation questions activating Art.9 disclosure obligations boutique has never evaluated
- Processor chain documentation is vendor-dependent, not firm-owned

**Art. 15 — AI inference accuracy:**
- Correction/erasure obligation for inaccurate AI-inferred personal data
- AI inferences about client financial behavior may be inaccurate
- Inaccurate inference embedded in client report activates Art.15 obligation
- Different from model performance accuracy and raw data accuracy

**Art. 20 — Right of access:**
- Client right to know what data AI systems hold and how it's processed
- AI vendor architecture may not support client access request at boutique scale
- Access infrastructure different from general data subject access

**Art. 21 — Automated decision obligations:**
- Right to explanation for AI-assisted decisions affecting client
- Right to human review of automated decisions
- Client-facing AI recommendations may trigger Art.21 obligations
- nFADP Art.21 + FINMA Circular 2016/01 simultaneously triggered by same AI-assisted decision

**Art. 22 — Reversal right:**
- Client right to request reversal of AI-substantially-informed decisions
- Portfolio decisions may have reversal right implications
- No Geneva protocol for implementing Art.22 in portfolio management context

**Art. 63 — Criminal liability:**
- Criminal liability for unlawful data processing affecting clients
- Personal criminal exposure for managing director for AI tool deployment decisions
- Criminal defense evidentiary standard architecturally different from administrative compliance documentation
- Carrier attestation documentation built for administrative compliance may be inadequate for criminal defense
