### 1. Carrier Attestation Instrument Variation — Compound Non-Standardized Exposure

**Signal observed:** Boutiques with multiple carrier relationships receiving materially different attestation instruments. Each carrier sending questionnaire with different documentation requirements, different question framing, different evidence standards.

**Complaint pattern:** "I signed the Zurich assay form and now Swiss Re is asking something different — am I now exposed on both fronts?"

**Failure mode:** Documentation commitments generated across carriers are non-standardized. Cross-carrier coherence is undisclosed structural liability. Boutiques assume signing the carrier form is a single categorical event; it generates compound non-standardized documentation obligations across simultaneous carrier relationships.

**Market implication:** Multiple carrier relationships creating documentation burden that no Geneva boutique has mapped. Each signature potentially creates different evidentiary footprint with no cross-reference methodology.
