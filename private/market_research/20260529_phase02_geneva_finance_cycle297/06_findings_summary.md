#### Finding 1: Multi-Carrier Instrument Variation Creating Compound Non-Standardized Exposure

**The finding:** Boutiques with multiple carrier relationships receiving materially different attestation instruments. Each carrier sends questionnaires with different documentation requirements, different question framing, different evidence standards. Documentation commitments generated across carriers are non-standardized and cross-carrier coherence is undisclosed structural liability.

**Distinct from prior findings:** Prior carrier attestation findings named individual carrier attestation problem and pre-signing review absence. This finding isolates specific multi-carrier non-standardized instrument variation creating compound non-standardized exposure simultaneously across multiple carrier relationships — different from single carrier attestation problem or service absence naming.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "If you have multiple carriers, you're potentially signing non-standardized attestation instruments with different documentation requirements — under assumption that signing the carrier form is a single categorical event. It's not. Each signature generates different evidentiary footprint with no cross-reference methodology in the market."

#### Finding 2: Second-Generation Institutional Client Questionnaire Response Recycling Effect

**The finding:** 2024-2025 responses functioning as live comparative intelligence for 2026-2027 mandate decisions. Institutional clients using responses for portfolio construction AI methodology comparisons across manager roster. First-generation response documentation built as point-in-time obligation disclosure now functioning as persistent comparative intelligence instrument.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292/C293 named institutional cross-firm simultaneous comparison mechanism without isolating specific second-generation data use mechanism where 2024-2025 responses feed 2026-2027 selection decisions boutique never anticipated when responding. Different from naming market-level distortion effect — this names the specific temporal data use mechanism creating undisclosed strategic exposure.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Your 2024-2025 questionnaire responses are functioning as live intelligence for 2026-2027 mandate decisions. You answered questions two years ago not knowing those answers would determine whether you keep that client. You cannot retract what was represented. You cannot update what was disclosed. You likely don't know how those responses were interpreted and applied."

#### Finding 3: Portfolio Construction AI Authorization Chain as Discrete Sequential Integrity Requirement

**The finding:** Sequential chaining documentation from AI output to human authorization moment with own evidence standard. None of current AI governance frameworks address chaining documentation as discrete requirement. Boutiques asked to document sequential authorization must reconstruct never-built record.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292/C293 third-generation carrier evidence targeting portfolio construction named evidence of specific human review without isolating that structural gap is specifically the chaining documentation between AI output and authorization moment with its own sequential integrity requirement. Different from naming evidence requirement — this names the specific chaining architecture gap as distinct documentation category.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Third-generation carrier attestation doesn't ask for your tool inventory or policy documents. It asks specifically which AI-generated recommendation was reviewed by which PM at which moment before which decision. You don't have that. Nobody built it because the question didn't exist before. The AI was operating as co-decision-maker without documented authorization chain — and now the question is arriving."

#### Finding 4: Carrier Third-Generation Processor Chain Questions Activating Undisclosed Art.9 Obligations

**The finding:** Carriers beginning to ask about AI vendor sub-processors, data termination protocols, acquisition scenarios. Questions probe nFADP Art.9 third-party processor obligations boutique may not know they have. Click-through vendor terms accepted without evaluation created undisclosed processor chains. Carrier functioning as de facto nFADP enforcement instrument through questionnaire interrogation activating Art.9 disclosure obligations accumulated for years through unevaluated click-through acceptance.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292/C293/C294 vendor due diligence naming addressed vendor terms generally without isolating specific processor chain disclosure mechanism through carrier third-generation questionnaire. Different from naming vendor terms acceptance problem or general processor chain exposure — this isolates specific carrier interrogation activating undisclosed Art.9 obligations that predate the questionnaire by years.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Your carrier is asking about your AI vendor's sub-processors. If you're like most boutiques, you don't know who your sub-processors are — because you accepted vendor terms through click-through without evaluating what you were accepting. The carrier is functioning as a de facto nFADP enforcement mechanism. The questions they're asking about processor chains are activating disclosure obligations you've been accumulating since the day you signed the vendor contract."

#### Finding 5: Cross-Mandate Four-System Documentation Coherence Paradox as Discrete Service Category

**The finding:** Four independent documentation systems (FINMA Guidance, institutional client questionnaires, nFADP ROPA/DPIA/Art.26, carrier attestation evidence) describing same AI governance reality with different formatting, evidence standards, update cycles, named responsible persons, and disclosure scopes. No Geneva methodology for cross-referencing these four documentation streams. Documentation may be internally inconsistent despite individually satisfying each accountability track.

**Distinct from prior findings:** All prior parallel accountability documentation findings addressed individual track adequacy not specific four-system coherence paradox and cross-reference gap across multi-obligation integrated portfolio. Different from naming documentation quality or adequacy — this isolates specific cross-reference architecture absence as discrete service category with own solution requirements.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "You have four documentation programs describing the same reality. Each was built independently with different logic. When someone asks you to explain why your FINMA response, your client questionnaire responses, your nFADP register, and your carrier evidence don't perfectly align — you can't. The inconsistencies are there, they're undisclosed, and they're creating structural liability that no single-track documentation improvement addresses."

---

#### Finding 6: Agentic AI Authorization Architecture Incompatibility as Structurally Unresolvable Gap

**The finding:** Authorization architecture designed for discrete decisions incompatible with continuous AI operation. No Geneva methodology exists for proportionate continuous authorization documentation. Documenting authorization for discrete events cannot be applied to continuous operation. Gap cannot be cured by improving documentation quality — the documentation model itself is incompatible with the operational model.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292 agentic AI naming addressed continuous autonomous execution exposure without isolating why the gap is structurally unresolvable at boutique scale and cannot be cured by documentation improvement. Different from naming the exposure — this isolates specific architectural incompatibility mechanism creating the structural unresolvability.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Your authorization documentation framework was built for human decisions made at discrete moments. Agentic AI makes decisions continuously — not at moments that can be authorized and documented in the conventional sense. You can't solve this by improving documentation quality. You need a different architectural approach to continuous AI authorization. This isn't an operational gap. It's a structural problem that documentation alone cannot address."
