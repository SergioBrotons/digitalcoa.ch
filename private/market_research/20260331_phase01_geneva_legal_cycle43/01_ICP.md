# ICP — Geneva Legal AI Governance
## Phase 01 Cycle 43

## Ideal Client Profile
- **Title**: Law firm managing partner / sole practitioner
- **Firm size**: 1–20 lawyers (solo and small firm primary)
- **Location**: Geneva, Vaud, Zurich (French-speaking CH focus)
- **Technology posture**: Active AI user (individual) without formal AI governance framework
- **Current state**: Using AI tools (contract analysis, legal research, due diligence, client communications) without documented policies, DPIA, or FADP compliance stack
- **Pain**: Increasing client scrutiny, FADP obligations, potential FDPIC inquiry, reputational risk from AI errors/hallucinations
- **Urgency triggers**: FADP Art. 21 (automated decisions), Swissdamed (~13 weeks), digitalswitzerland May 2026 external pressure, 31-point belief-action gap

## Key ICP Insights
- Solo/small firm agility paradox: faster AI adoption + minimal governance = highest risk
- Individual-level AI use without firm-level formalization = Shadow AI exposure
- Managing director personal liability (CHF 250,000 for intentional FADP failures) — urgency lever
- FADP directly applicable to AI (FDPIC May 2025 — no new law needed)
- Enforcement gap: FDPIC absent from legal sector (enterprise firms defend clients, not themselves being enforced)
