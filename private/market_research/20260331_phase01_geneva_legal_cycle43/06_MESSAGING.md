## Primary Pain Points (Ranked)
1. **Governance gap** — using AI without formal policies (43% no policy)
2. **FADP exposure** — obligations already operative, enforcement absent ≠ compliance absent
3. **Client scrutiny** — Swissdamed spillover, increasing AI awareness
4. **Personal liability** — managing director CHF 250,000 for intentional FADP failures
5. **Hallucination risk** — AI errors producing legal effects without human review

## Urgency Framing (Two-Timeline)
- **Immediate pressure** (May 2026): digitalswitzerland AI Action Plan First Draft → clients will ask questions
- **Formal deadline** (end-2026): Federal Council legislation → compliance obligation arrives

## Objection Handling

### "We're too small for this"
→ 71% individual AI use / 43% no policy — size doesn't create exposure, AI use does.
→ FADP applies to any firm processing personal data with AI, regardless of size.

### "We haven't been contacted by FDPIC"
→ Enforcement gap ≠ obligation gap. FDPIC absent from legal sector is a timing signal, not a free pass.
→ 2.5+ years of operative FADP obligations already accumulated.

### "Our IT vendor handles this"
→ Vendor provides tools. Your firm owns the compliance obligations for how those tools process client data.
→ Privacy by Design assessment is your firm's responsibility.

### "Clients don't ask about AI governance"
→ Swissdamed spillover (healthcare) → client awareness increasing across all professional services.
→ 90%+ already using AI — when something goes wrong, the question becomes urgent.

Executive-grade credibility. Geneva professional services voice.
