## Key Regulatory Developments (C124)

### 1. Swiss Quantum Initiative (SQI) — Active Since 2024
- Swiss government commitment: CHF 50M+ over 5 years
- Geneva node: University of Geneva quantum research (Prof. Gisin group)
- Financial sector application: UBS, Swiss banking exploring quantum computing
- Post-quantum cryptography: NIST standards finalized 2024; FINMA beginning to consider quantum risk

### 2. Swissmedic Digital Health Regulatory Pathway — Updated 2025
- Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) guidance finalized 2025
- Clinical evidence requirements for digital health tools clarified
- Fast-track pathway for low-risk digital health tools under discussion
- CE/FDA alignment: partial harmonization reduces dual filing burden

### 3. ICRC/HCR Humanitarian Tech Procurement — Ongoing
- ICRC Innovation Accelerator: annual funding rounds
- UNHCR Biometric Identity Management System (BIMS): expansion ongoing
- Humanitarian data standards: ICRC guidelines on data protection in humanitarian contexts
- UN procurement portals: UNGM (UN Global Marketplace) for vendor registration

### 4. ITU AI for Good Summit — Geneva AI Governance Platform
- Annual Geneva event; AI governance standards emerging
- ITU AI governance guidelines: reference for UN/IO procurement
- Global AI governance frameworks: being developed at Geneva-based IOs
- Vendor implications: AI procurement standards increasingly binding

### 5. Swiss Remote Work / International Employment — Tax and Labor Law
- Swiss-UK/CH-EU remote work: tax treaty implications complex
- Swiss payroll requirements: international remote workers
- Employer of Record (EOR) regulation: Swiss law requirements
- Cross-border employment: French frontier worker agreements updated

## Geneva Tech Regulatory Risk Summary
- **High regulatory risk:** Swissmedic pathway — required for digital health market access
- **Medium regulatory risk:** Quantum cryptography — emerging standards, FINMA attention increasing
- **Medium regulatory risk:** UN/IO AI procurement standards — evolving, early mover advantage
- **Low regulatory risk:** Humanitarian tech contracting — established procurement rules
- **External risk:** Geopolitical — ICRC/HCR funding tied to political situations
