# 01 — ICP: Geneva Tech Startups, Cleantech Ventures, Digital Health

## Target Segment: Geneva Tech Startups, Cleantech Ventures, Digital Health Companies

### Firm/Client Profile
- **Stage:** Seed to Series B (primarily), growth-stage
- **Sector:** Cleantech, digital health, quantum/hardware, AI/ML, humanitarian tech
- **Revenue:** Pre-revenue to CHF 10M ARR
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; EPFL/Lausanne corridor; some Basel presence
- **Investors:** Geneva-based VCs, Swiss institutionals, family offices, UN/IO procurement

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CEO / Founder | Technical founder, often not commercial | Fundraising, commercial strategy, team scaling |
| CTO / Technical Lead | Technical expert | Hiring, technology selection, IP protection |
| CFO / Finance Lead | Finance professional | Financial modeling, investor readiness, burn rate |
| Board Member / Chairman | Experienced operator or investor | Strategic direction, fundraising, exit options |
| Business Development | Commercial lead | Enterprise sales, UN/IO procurement, partnership |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Cleantech: Climeworks (carbon capture), South Pole (carbon markets), Global Bioenergy (Geneva HQ)
- Digital Health: Sophia Genetics (digital health, EPFL spinout), Covariants (clinical AI)
- Humanitarian Tech: ICRC/HCR tech vendors (various small vendors)
- Quantum: Zurich Instruments (quantum instrumentation, Zurich HQ with Geneva ties)
- AI/ML: Eightfold.ai (San Francisco but Geneva roots), AdNovum (Geneva-based)
- University spinouts: EPFL, UniGe, HEC Geneva startups

### Pain Points (C124 Update)
1. **Geneva cleantech Series A gap** — strong seed, weak Series A; post-Series B growth capital scarce; advisory opportunity for fundraising readiness
2. **Quantum computing readiness** — Geneva financial institutions exploring quantum; quantum-secure cryptography becoming compliance issue; advisory market emerging
3. **UN/IO AI procurement standards** — Geneva institutions issuing AI procurement guidelines affecting global vendor market; advisory opportunity
4. **Digital health regulatory pathway** — Swissmedic/FOPH approval process complex; EPFL/CHUV/HUG digital health tools need regulatory strategy
5. **Humanitarian tech ICRC/HCR procurement** — ICRC innovation accelerator funding; HCR biometric expansion; need advisory to navigate procurement
6. **Geneva tech talent pipeline** — talent shortage critical; remote work enabling global hiring; cross-border employment compliance advisory

### Buying Triggers
- Series A fundraising process starting
- Investor requiring commercial strategy development
- UN/IO procurement opportunity identified
- Regulatory pathway unclear for digital health product
- ICRC/HCR call for proposals issued
- Quantum computing vendor selection needed

### Buying Barriers
- Early-stage: limited budget for external advisory
- Founders prefer equity deals over cash payments
- Long fundraising cycles reduce urgency until acute
- UN/IO procurement timelines unpredictable
- Regulatory approval timelines uncertain for health tech

### Language / Cultural Fit
- English dominant in tech startup ecosystem
- French useful for Geneva institutional relationships
- German useful for Swiss institutional investors
- Direct, pragmatic communication style
- Startup culture: fast decisions, lean teams
