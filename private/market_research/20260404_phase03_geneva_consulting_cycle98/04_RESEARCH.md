# Finding 04 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 03: Geneva Consulting
**Source:** Executive search industry (AESC), Geneva executive search firms, Swiss HR association  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Geneva Executive Search Market Tightens — "Quiet Hiring" and Internal Mobility Reduce External Mandates**

Geneva's executive search industry — concentrated in financial services, luxury goods, pharma, and international organisations — is facing structural demand pressure from internal mobility platforms and "quiet hiring" practices adopted by major Geneva employers.

Key dynamics:

**1. Internal talent marketplaces reduce external search:** Large Geneva employers (banks, commodity trading houses, pharma) have invested in internal talent marketplace platforms. High-value roles that previously went to external search firms are now being filled internally.

**2. Retained search still dominant at board/C-suite level:** Despite internal mobility growth, board-level and C-suite placements in Geneva remain predominantly external. The complexity, confidentiality, and relational demands at this level sustain premium retained search.

**3. Functional specialisation increasing:** Geneva search firms are specialising further — separating CIO search from CFO search, ESG executive search as distinct from generalist search. Specialisation commands higher fees and higher conversion rates.

**4. EU-level executive search in Geneva:** Geneva hosts the European headquarters of many multinationals, creating a specific market for executives with EU regulatory expertise. Candidates with EU regulatory backgrounds command premiums in Geneva's pharma, financial services, and sustainability sectors.

**5. Interim executives for transformation mandates:** Geneva companies in transformation (post-merger integration, AI adoption, regulatory crisis) are using interim executive arrangements more frequently. Search firms offering interim placement generate higher margin than retained search.
