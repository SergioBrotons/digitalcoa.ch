# Finding 06 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 03: Geneva Consulting
**Source:** Geneva-Keep, Geneva Economic Development Office (Geneva TEDD), Swiss IT industry  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Geneva IT Consulting Sector Faces Talent Crisis — AI Skills Premium Widens Gap Between Large Firms and Boutiques**

Geneva's IT consulting sector is experiencing a significant talent supply-demand imbalance, particularly in AI, cybersecurity, and cloud architecture. This is widening the gap between large Geneva IT consulting firms (which can offer competitive compensation) and mid-sized boutiques (which cannot).

Key dynamics:

**1. AI engineering talent commanding 30-50% premiums:** AI-specialised engineers and data scientists in Geneva are commanding salary premiums that are unsustainable for mid-sized IT consulting firms. Large firms (Accenture, IBM Geneva, Swisscom Digital Enterprise) can absorb the premium; boutiques cannot.

**2. Cybersecurity consulting demand surge:** High-profile cyberattacks on Swiss targets in 2024-2025 have driven sustained demand for cybersecurity assessments, incident response planning, and compliance advisory. Geneva's concentration of financial institutions and international organisations makes this a priority spend.

**3. Cloud migration advisory still generating revenue:** Despite cloud migration being a mature market, Geneva companies with legacy on-premise infrastructure (particularly in banking and insurance) are still mid-migration. Remaining migration work is concentrated with large systems integrators.

**4. Geneva's "in-house" IT consulting arm trend:** Some large Geneva corporations are building internal IT consulting capabilities (hiring former consultants as full-time employees) to reduce dependency on external IT consulting. This is reducing the available mandate pool for boutiques.

**5. Nearshore and remote delivery challenges:** Geneva IT consulting firms using nearshore delivery models (from Eastern Europe or Portugal) face quality control challenges for complex AI and cybersecurity work. Onshore delivery premiums are re-establishing for high-complexity mandates.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** The AI skills shortage and its effect on IT consulting economics is a significant tech-economy story with Geneva-specific angles. A sharp analysis of the structural forces at work would attract both IT and business leaders.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** Companies procuring IT consulting services in Geneva need to understand how to structure engagements when talent premiums make fixed-fee pricing difficult. Advisory content on IT consulting procurement = practical client value.
