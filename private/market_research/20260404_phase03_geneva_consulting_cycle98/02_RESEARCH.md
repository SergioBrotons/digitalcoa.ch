# Finding 02 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 03: Geneva Consulting
**Source:** Swiss HR consulting market, HR Tech Geneva, Swiss HR association  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Geneva HR Consulting Faces Structural Shift — Employee Relations Mandates Rise, Traditional Training Mandates Fall**

The HR consulting market in Geneva is undergoing a structural shift driven by post-pandemic work reorganisation, new Swiss employment law amendments, and changing employee expectations. The demand mix is shifting sharply away from training and development toward employee relations, restructuring, and compliance.

Key dimensions:

**1. New Swiss Employment Law (OR revisions):** Recent amendments to the Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) on remote work, temporary layoff procedures, and protection against dismissal for protected characteristics have created a wave of employment law advisory work for Geneva HR consultants.

**2. Collective redundancy consultations:** Swiss law requires mandatory consultation with employee representatives before collective redundancies. Geneva companies in restructuring need experienced HR consultants to manage consultation processes, social plans, and communication frameworks.

**3. Geneva's labour market tightness persists:** Despite economic uncertainty, Geneva's specialised labour market (banking, pharma, luxury goods, international organisations) remains tight. Compensation benchmarking and executive search mandates are sustained — creating ongoing work for specialised HR consulting firms.

**4. DEI (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion) consulting:** Switzerland's emerging regulatory focus on gender pay equity reporting (under the Gender Equality Act revisions) and EU-connected companies' DEI obligations are driving demand for DEI audits, policy design, and compliance frameworks.

**5. HR tech implementation consulting:** Geneva companies implementing HRIS platforms (Workday, SAP SuccessFactors) need implementation support — a stable consulting revenue line that generates recurring relationships.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** The transformation of HR from "people development" to "employee relations and compliance" is a significant workplace trend. A thoughtful analysis of what this means for Geneva employers would resonate with the Brotons.net audience.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** GCs and HR directors at Geneva companies need to understand their employment law obligations. Advisory content on the most pressing Geneva HR compliance issues in 2026 would generate high engagement.
