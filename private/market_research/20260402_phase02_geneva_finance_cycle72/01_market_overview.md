# 02 — Geneva Finance: Structural Overview

## Swiss Financial Centre — Market Position

Switzerland is the world's largest cross-border wealth management centre, managing approximately CHF 7–8 trillion in assets under management (2024 record). Geneva and Zurich serve as the two primary hubs, with Geneva holding particular strength in private banking, asset management, and family office services.

## Geneva's Financial Sector

### Core Segments
- **Private Banking:** Ultra-high-net-worth individuals (UHNWI), HNWI, family offices
- **Asset Management:** Institutional asset managers, hedge funds, real estate funds
- **Banking Services:** Corporate banking, trade finance, letters of credit
- **Insurance:** Life insurance, reinsurance (Geneva-based insurers)
- **Financial Infrastructure:** Banks, securities dealers, fund administrators

### Geneva Concentration
- 2024 assets under management: all-time high (Banking Barometer 2025)
- Estimated 25–30% of Swiss financial sector employment
- Major Geneva-based institutions: UBS (global headquarters), Crédit Suisse legacy operations, Pictet, Lombard Odier, Bordier, Baumann & Cie, Mirabaud, etc.
- Foreign bank branches: Concentrated in Geneva for international private banking

### Competitive Positioning
- **vs. Luxembourg:** Cross-border fund distribution; Luxembourg leads in UCITS, Switzerland leads in WM
- **vs. Singapore:** APAC wealth management; Singapore leads in Asian WM, Switzerland in European/Middle Eastern WM
- **vs. Dubai (IHC):** UAE wealth; Geneva serves European HNWIs; growing Middle East coverage
