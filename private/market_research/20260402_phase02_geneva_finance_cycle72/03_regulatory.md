# 03 — Geneva Finance: Regulatory & Compliance Landscape

## Financial Services Regulation

### FINMA AI Governance — Guidance 08/2024
- **Published:** December 18, 2024
- **Framework:** Concrete AI governance requirements for supervised institutions
- **AI Desk:** Established by FINMA
- **Federal Council rules:** Expected end 2026 (mandatory rules)
- **Geneva relevance:** Private banks, wealth managers, asset managers operating in Geneva must assess AI exposure
- **Key obligations:** AI risk assessments, model governance, human oversight, explainability for high-stakes decisions

### Stablecoin Regulation — FinIA Amendment (Draft)
- **Context:** US GENIUS Act (federal stablecoin law) creates competitive urgency
- **Federal Council proposal:** New rules for stablecoins
- **SBA opposition:** Mandating subsidiary structure for banks = "Luxembourg scenario" = competitive disadvantage
- **SBA position:** Swiss banks should issue stablecoins under existing banking licence (aligned with EU)
- **Key risk:** Disintermediation if non-bank payment instrument institutions collect large volumes
- **Stress test demand:** SBA calling for comprehensive risk analysis before legislation
- **Concrete example:** AllUnity (German e-money institution, Switzerland-franc stablecoin announced)
- **Timeline:** FinIA amendment in parliamentary process; Geneva banks monitoring closely
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch/stablecoin position

### DLT Act 2021
- **Significance:** Switzerland's early milestone in digital asset regulation
- **Scope:** Distributed ledger technology securities; blockchain-based financial instruments
- **Market development:** Basis for Swiss digital asset ecosystem
- **Competitive note:** Early advantage now under pressure from US GENIUS Act

### Project Helvetia III — Wholesale CBDC
- **Extended:** June 2026 / mid-2027 (from C71 confirmed)
- **Participants:** SNB + SIX Digital Exchange
- **Scope:** Wholesale central bank digital currency for settlement
- **Geneva relevance:** Settlement infrastructure for financial institutions

### Berne Financial Services Agreement (CH-UK)
- **In force:** January 1, 2026 (confirmed C71)
- **Scope:** Cross-border financial services; wealth management
- **MoU:** FINMA-FCA-BoE cooperation
- **Legal services:** Not covered (financial services only)

## ESG / Sustainable Finance

### SBA ESG Self-Regulation
- **Effective:** January 1, 2026 (confirmed C71)
- **Compliance:** 86% bank compliance
- **Trend:** Sustainable finance increasingly becoming table stakes rather than differentiator

### Nature Transition Financing — BCG / Quantis Study (SBA)
- **Annual need:** CHF 5.3 billion by 2050
- **Current spending:** CHF 3.2 billion/year
- **Gap:** CHF 2.1 billion/year
- **Public/private:** 85% publicly financed currently; private capital mobilization needed
- **Key areas:** Water infrastructure (largest), regenerative agriculture, hydropower rehabilitation
- **Kunming-Montreal GBF:** Switzerland committed 2022; national implementation framework developing
- **Strategic implication:** Banks as financiers of nature projects; advisory opportunity

## Capital Markets Regulation

### Basel III / Basel IV Implementation
- **Swiss implementation:**FINMA applying Basel III standards
- **UBS specific:** G-SIB surcharge; higher capital requirements post-CS integration
- **Market risk:** SA-CCR for derivatives; ongoing implementation

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- **Swiss framework:** FINMA supervised; MROS as FIU
- **Cross-border:** CRS/AEOI automatic exchange; FATCA
- **2025/2026:** Enhanced scrutiny of HNWI clients; beneficial ownership transparency

## Tax Regulation

### OECD Pillar Two
- **Swiss implementation:** January 1, 2025 (in force)
- **Rate:** 15% minimum corporate tax
- **Geneva cantonal:** Adjustments maintaining cantonal competitiveness

### Withholding Tax
- **EU agreement:** Amended bilateral package (March 13, 2026 submission to Parliament)
- **UK agreement:** Berne Agreement covers withholding tax on certain instruments
