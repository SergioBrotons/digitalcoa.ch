# ICP — Small Geneva Consulting Firm AI Governance
## Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting — Cycle 42

---

## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Target:** Small/mid-market management and IT consulting firms in Geneva, 1–20 consultants
- **Alternative ICP:** Independent consultants / solo advisors in Geneva
- **NOT:** Large consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Accenture — enterprise); IT firms with dedicated legal/compliance departments
- **Geography:** Canton Geneva primary; broader Swiss French-speaking region secondary

### Psychographics
- **Pain:** Using AI tools for proposals, reports, research, deliverables without formal governance; 73% abandoned AI projects due to skills gap; GPAI obligations for EU-nexus firms already operative
- **Trigger:** GPAI ~8 months already operative (Aug 2, 2025); Federal Council May 2026 First Draft approaching; Phase 01 C42 70% projected AI adoption increase signals market normalization
- **Urgency:** GPAI transparency obligations already active; 40% skills gap = failed AI projects; 3-tier pyramid applies (67% individual → 28% formal → 4% deep); 90% by 2027 forecast
- **Gatekeeper:** Managing partner / founding consultant with P&L responsibility

### Consulting-Specific Pain Points
1. **GPAI ~8 months already operative** — EU AI Act Annex I high-level general-purpose AI models; if consulting firm uses GPAI tools (most do), transparency obligations apply — already in violation for EU-nexus firms
2. **40% AI skills gap** — 73% of AI projects abandoned due to skills shortage (PwC 2024); governance frameworks help consultants select and deploy AI tools that stick
3. **Deliverable accountability** — AI-assisted proposals, reports, analysis = client-facing output = FADP Art. 21 right to human review if client data is processed
4. **Swiss data residency** — consulting firms with Swiss or EU clients need Swiss/EU data residency for AI tools (client confidentiality)
5. **FADP directly applicable to consulting AI** — FDPIC May 2025 confirmation; obligations already operative for AI-assisted client work

### Decision-maker Motivations
- Avoid GPAI transparency violations (already operative for EU-nexus firms)
- Reduce AI project failure rate (73% abandoned — governance framework increases success probability)
- Protect client confidentiality (Swiss/EU data residency)
- Capture efficiency gains (260h/year applicable to proposals, reports, research, deliverables)
- Stay ahead of Federal Council legislation (end-2026 formal deadline)
