# Lead Magnet: "The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework"
## Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting — Cycle 42

---

## Positioning
For Geneva small/mid-market consulting firms and independent consultants who are using AI tools for proposals, reports, and client deliverables — but have no formal governance framework, have experienced AI project failures (73% abandoned due to skills gap), and may be subject to GPAI transparency obligations without knowing it.

**Primary urgency lever (Cycle 42):** GPAI ~8 months already operative since Aug 2, 2025. If the consulting firm uses high-level general-purpose AI models (most do — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.), GPAI transparency obligations already apply. EU-nexus firms are already potentially in violation. The Federal Council May 2026 First Draft is ~4 weeks away — the external pressure event is imminent.

**Secondary urgency lever:** 73% of AI projects abandoned due to skills gap (PwC 2024). The governance framework is not just a compliance tool — it is a failure-prevention tool. Consultants who govern their AI use properly have higher project success rates.

---

## ICP Entry Points

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit (GPAI Entry)
Identify every AI tool currently used by consultants in the firm: ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, specialized consulting AI tools, etc. Determine which tools are GPAI (most general-purpose AI models qualify). Document the firm's GPAI compliance status.

**Why this works:** GPAI obligations are already operative. Most small consulting firms do not know they likely qualify as GPAI users with existing transparency obligations. The Shadow AI audit surfaces this gap without requiring immediate action.

### Decision 1: GPAI Transparency Assessment
For each GPAI tool identified: assess whether the firm has provided required transparency disclosures to users (other consultants, clients) about AI generation. GPAI obligations include: (1) making available adequate technical documentation, (2) summarizing training data disclosure upon request, (3) compliance with EU Copyright Directive (attribution for AI-generated content).

**Why this works:** GPAI compliance is already mandatory for EU-nexus firms. The May 2026 digitalswitzerland First Draft will sharpen market awareness. Firms that document their GPAI compliance position now will be ahead of the curve.

### Decision 2: AI Project Governance Framework
Based on the 40% AI skills gap: implement a lightweight governance framework for selecting and deploying AI tools. This includes: (1) use case assessment, (2) tool evaluation criteria, (3) data residency check, (4) FADP compliance basis, (5) human review protocol for client deliverables.

**Why this works:** 73% of AI projects fail due to skills gap. A lightweight governance framework reduces failure probability by creating selection discipline. This is a value-add framing, not a compliance framing.

### Decision 3: Client Deliverable AI Policy
Document the firm's policy for using AI in client-facing deliverables (proposals, reports, presentations, analysis). Include: (1) disclosure to clients when AI is used, (2) human review before delivery, (3) data residency for client data processed by AI tools.

**Why this works:** FADP Art. 21 right to human review applies when AI-assisted decisions affect clients. FADP Art. 5(c) transparency requires disclosure. A client deliverable AI policy addresses both obligations and builds client trust.

### Decision 4: Skills Development Integration
AI governance should include a skills development component: (1) critical AI evaluation skills, (2) prompt engineering for consulting use cases, (3) AI hallucination detection for research and analysis.

**Why this works:** 40% skills gap + 73% failure rate = the problem is partly skills, not just governance. Integrating skills development into the governance framework creates immediate value beyond compliance.

### Decision 5: Federal Council May 2026 Readiness Assessment
Assess the firm's readiness for the upcoming Swiss AI regulatory framework. Document which obligations are already met, which are in progress, and which require external support. This positions the firm as proactive rather than reactive.

**Why this works:** The Federal Council end-2026 formal deadline is ~6 months away. The digitalswitzerland May 2026 First Draft is ~4 weeks away. Consulting firms that have done a self-assessment will be better positioned to adapt.

## Compliance Stack (GPAI + FADP, Obligations Already Active)
1. **GPAI Layer:** Transparency obligations for general-purpose AI model users — operative since Aug 2, 2025
2. **FADP Layer:** Art. 21 right to human review + Art. 5(c) transparency + Art. 16 data source disclosure + DPIA for high-risk processing
3. **Copyright Layer:** EU Copyright Directive attribution for AI-generated content in deliverables
