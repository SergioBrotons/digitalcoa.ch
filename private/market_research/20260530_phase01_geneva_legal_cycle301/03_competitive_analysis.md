# Phase 01 — Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal (2–5 Attorneys)
## Cycle 301 | 2026-05-30

---

## What Currently Exists in the Market

### Geneva Legal AI Service Providers

**Platform vendors:** AI legal research and document drafting platforms (Thomson Reuters, Lexis+AI, Harvey, etc.) — positioned as productivity tools, not accountability infrastructure. Their terms create data processing relationships the ICP did not consciously evaluate. Their SLA documentation does not address professional liability exposure.

**Bar CE providers:** SBA and cantonal bar continuing education programs now include AI content. Attendance-based — credential tracks presence, not competence. An attorney who has not integrated AI governance receives the same credential signal as one who has. The credential does not reflect the accountability exposure it was designed to signal.

**Compliance template providers:** Document templates for engagement letter AI clauses, processing register frameworks, AI tool disclosure language. These address the documentation surface but not the structural accountability chain from research query to filed brief.

**Insurance brokers:** Carrier AI attestation forms reviewed in isolation. The review addresses whether the form was answered correctly, not what the signature commits the signatory to personally or whether existing coverage responds to that commitment.

### What's Missing for the 2–5 Attorney Firm

**1. Pre-signing attestation review from personal liability perspective**

No Geneva service addresses the specific problem of: "I am about to sign this carrier form. What does my signature commit me to personally? Does my existing PI coverage respond to this commitment? What would a claim look like under this attestation?"

The carrier form is written by carrier lawyers to protect carrier interests. The market offers compliance adequacy reviews (did you answer the questions correctly?) but not liability exposure reviews (what are you personally committing to, and does your coverage address it?).

**2. Research-to-filing accountability chain reconstruction**

No Geneva service addresses the specific failure mode where: research was conducted with AI tools, client data was processed in AI systems, briefs were drafted with AI assistance, and the documentation that would allow the managing partner to explain what happened — to a client, a carrier, a court, or the bar — does not exist.

The market treats research confirmation bias, reasoning chain preservation, brief authentication, and post-error disclosure protocol as separate compliance topics. They are not separate. They are one continuous accountability chain, and the failure mode is that the chain cannot be reconstructed retroactively.

**3. nFADP Art. 21 protocol for legal services**

No Geneva service has mapped the Article 21 client data subject request protocol for the specific context of legal services — where the information requested is attorney work product, where disclosure may constitute privilege waiver, and where non-disclosure creates nFADP liability.

The existing guidance is general. The legal services context is specific and has no documented protocol.

**4. June 19 SBA event preparation specifically**

No Geneva service is offering specific preparation for the June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers in terms of: what the managing partner needs to understand about their current AI exposure, what documentation they need to be able to speak to, and what questions they should be asking before they walk into that room.

The event is in approximately two weeks. This is a time-bound advisory opportunity that has no current market response.

**5. Label de Bonne Gouvernance IA preparation pathway**

The Geneva label (Q3 2026 launch, TÜV Switzerland audit) requires documentation infrastructure that the ICP does not have. No clear preparation pathway exists for the small firm to build that infrastructure and be ready for Q3 2026 certification.

The market has the label concept but not the preparation pathway for the 2–5 attorney firm that needs to get there from a starting point of no documentation.

**6. Vendor exit governance**

No Geneva service addresses the specific problem of: client data is in AI platforms chosen through practice management bundles, exit would disrupt client service, but staying creates ongoing attestation exposure from terms updates the ICP did not evaluate.

The structural problem is that the ICP cannot easily exit and cannot comfortably stay. No service addresses the governance structure for managing that specific dilemma.

---

## What No One Is Addressing: The Structural Gap

The market is organized around the AI adoption decision. The ICP's problem is not the adoption decision — the adoption decision was made 6–18 months ago. The ICP's problem is the accountability infrastructure that should have been built around that decision and was not.

The structural gap: **there is no service that addresses the ICP's current position, which is that they have already adopted AI, have already signed attestations, have already processed client data in AI systems, and are now living in the consequences of that — with no documentation, no reconstruction capability, and a June 19 event that will force public self-assessment.**

The market is selling to the pre-adoption decision. The ICP needs help with the post-adoption consequence.

---

## The Timing Dimension

June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers creates a specific market condition: forced self-assessment for managing partners who have been deferring it. The event is not advisory — it is a professional accountability occasion. The ICP will be in a room where AI governance will be discussed by peers, and the ICP has signed something they cannot explain.

This is not a market that needs convincing. It is a market that needs help preparing for a specific imminent occasion.
