# Phase 03 — ICP: Geneva Small Consulting Firms

## Vertical Definition
Consulting firms in Geneva operating with 2–15 employees, generating CHF 500K–5M annual revenue. Predominantly sole proprietors and boutique practices offering specialized advisory services to mid-market and large clients. Sectors include: strategy consulting, IT/technology consulting, organizational/HR consulting, sustainability consulting, and financial consulting (non-regulated).

---

## Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

### Firmographics

| Attribute | Target Range |
|---|---|
| **Headcount** | 2–15 employees |
| **Annual Revenue** | CHF 500K–5M |
| **Legal Form** | Sàrl (SARL), SA (AG), Groupement, some sole proprietorship |
| **Location** | Geneva (Canton): Geneva city center, La Praille, Grand-Saconnex, Lancy |
| **Founded** | 2005–2020 (established, not startup) |
| **Client Type** | B2B — mid-market companies, multinationals, public institutions |
| **Industry Focus** | Diversified; common: pharma, commodities, trade, finance-adjacent |

### Decision-Making Profile

- **Decision Maker:** Managing Partner / Founder / CEO
- **Age Cohort:** 40–60 (experience-heavy, digitally-aware but not native)
- **AI/Data Decision Driver:** Efficiency, competitive differentiation, client delivery quality
- **Average Deal Velocity:** 4–12 weeks for consulting service contracts
- **Budget Authority:** Individual project budget CHF 10K–100K

### Psychographic / Pain Indicators

- **Time Poverty:** Billable hours consumed by admin, reporting, proposal writing
- **Client Expectation Gap:** Clients increasingly expect data-driven deliverables, real-time reporting
- **Talent Pressure:** Difficulty hiring junior staff; must do more with fewer people
- **Technology Confusion:** Overwhelmed by AI tool options; fear of choosing wrong stack
- **Regulatory Exposure:** Mild–moderate; concerns about client data confidentiality, AI-generated content liability
- **Competitive Anxiety:** Larger consultancies downscaling; boutique firms need to differentiate on specialization

### Technology Posture

- **Current State:** Mostly individual productivity tools (Office 365, Google Workspace, basic CRM)
- **AI Adoption:** Early/experimental — individual tools (ChatGPT, Copilot), not firm-wide strategy
- **Data Maturity:** Low-to-moderate; many manage client data in spreadsheets or entry-level CRM
- **Infrastructure:** Cloud-first (most use OneDrive, Google Drive, basic cloud ERPs)

### Buying Triggers

1. Competitor adopting AI-driven delivery model
2. Client explicitly requesting data-driven outputs
3. Proposal win rate decline
4. Key senior consultant departure (knowledge loss)
5. Regulatory change affecting client industry (knock-on advisory need)

### Buying Blocks

- Trust: "Will AI compromise my client confidentiality?"
- ROI Uncertainty: "What's the actual payback?"
- Integration Complexity: "My team won't adopt new tools"
- Cost Visibility: Unclear total cost of AI-augmented service delivery

## Addressable Segments Within ICP

### Segment A: Strategy & Management Consultants (Largest)
- Focus: Corporate strategy, organizational design, process optimization
- Pain: Proposal and deliverable production is heavily manual
- AI Relevance: High — document drafting, market research synthesis, presentation generation
- Estimated share of ICP: ~35%

### Segment B: IT & Technology Consultants
- Focus: ERP implementations, software selection, IT security, cloud migration
- Pain: Keeping pace with rapid tech changes; delivering to non-technical clients
- AI Relevance: Very High — already adjacent to AI, risk of commoditization
- Estimated share of ICP: ~25%

### Segment C: HR & Organizational Consultants
- Focus: Talent acquisition, leadership development, change management
- Pain: High-touch delivery model limits scale; remote/hybrid transition challenges
- AI Relevance: Medium-High — candidate sourcing, assessment, report writing
- Estimated share of ICP: ~20%

### Segment D: Sustainability & ESG Consultants
- Focus: ESG reporting, carbon footprint analysis, regulatory compliance (pre-GDPR/ESRS)
- Pain: Rapidly evolving regulatory landscape; data collection from clients
- AI Relevance: High — data aggregation, reporting, scenario modeling
- Estimated share of ICP: ~15%

### Segment E: Miscellaneous Specialists
- Focus: Legal consulting (non-law firm), tax advisory, interim management
- AI Relevance: Moderate — document review, research, client communication
- Estimated share of ICP: ~5%

---

## ICP Summary Statement

> **EN:** Small Geneva consulting firms (2–15 employees, CHF 500K–5M revenue) led by experienced managing partners aged 40–60, struggling with billable hour efficiency, rising client expectations for data-driven deliverables, and technology adoption decisions — particularly around AI-augmented service delivery and client data governance.

> **FR:** Petits cabinets de conseil genevois (2 à 15 employés, CHF 500K–5M de chiffre d'affaires), dirigés par des associés-gérants expérimentés âgés de 40 à 60 ans, confrontées à des défis d'efficacité facturable, à des attentes client croissantes en matière de livrables axés sur les données, et à des décisions d'adoption technologique — notamment autour de la prestation de services augmentée par l'IA et de la gouvernance des données clients.
