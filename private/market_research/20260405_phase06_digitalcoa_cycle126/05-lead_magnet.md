# 05 — Lead Magnet: DigitalCoa.ch Client Acquisition
**Phase:** 06 DigitalCoa.ch | **Cycle:** C126 | **Source:** Domain knowledge + Geneva advisory market analysis

---

## Lead Magnet Concept: "Geneva Executive Briefing Call"

**Format:** Free 30-minute advisory briefing call
**Target audience:** Geneva CFO, CEO, Board Chairman with regulatory or strategic challenge
**Purpose:** Demonstrate DigitalCoa.ch advisory quality; convert qualified prospects to engaged clients

---

## Lead Magnet Strategy

### The Briefing Call as Lead Magnet

**Rationale:**
- Geneva executives value direct access to expertise over content downloads
- A free 30-minute briefing call demonstrates advisory quality without content production
- Briefing call attendees are highly qualified (they took time to speak)
- Conversion rate from briefing call to paid engagement: 40-60% (high)

**Target conversion funnel:**
1. Brotons.net reader / LinkedIn connection / referral
2. Personalized invitation to briefing call (not generic)
3. 30-minute discovery call (free; high value)
4. Proposal for paid engagement (where appropriate)
5. Advisory engagement

---

### Briefing Call Invitation Framework

**Invitation message (LinkedIn or email):**
"Hi [Name], I've been following [Company]'s work in [relevant area] and noticed [specific observation about their situation]. Would you be open to a 30-minute conversation about [specific regulatory/strategic challenge]? No pitch — just a genuine exchange of perspectives. If it's useful, we can talk about whether there's a way I might be able to help. [ICR/FH]"

**Key elements:**
- Specific observation about the prospect (not generic)
- Clear value proposition (30-minute conversation)
- No pressure ("no pitch")
- Low-risk framing ("whether there's a way I might be able to help")
- Personal (not template)

---

### Briefing Call Discovery Framework

**30-minute call structure:**

**Minutes 1-5: Establish rapport**
- Thank them for taking the time
- Brief personal introduction (2 sentences)
- "Help me understand your situation before we dive in"

**Minutes 6-15: Discovery**
- Open questions: "What's driving this for you?" "What have you tried?" "What are your constraints?"
- Listen more than talk
- Take notes
- Ask clarifying questions

**Minutes 16-25: Initial assessment**
- "Based on what you've shared, here's what I'm seeing..."
- Share 1-2 relevant observations or frameworks
- Be genuinely helpful, even if no engagement follows
- Assess whether DigitalCoa.ch engagement makes sense

**Minutes 26-30: Next steps**
- "Would it make sense to explore how we might be able to help with this?"
- If yes: Propose specific scope and investment
- If no: Explain why (genuinely); offer alternative resources

---

### Post-Call Follow-Up Framework

**Same day or next day:**

**For engaged prospects (proposal likely):**
"Hi [Name], it was great speaking with you today. As discussed, here's my summary of what we're seeing and a proposed approach: [attached proposal]. Happy to discuss further. [ICR/FH]"

**For engaged prospects (more information needed):**
"Hi [Name], thank you for our conversation today. Based on what you shared, I'd suggest we [specific next step]. Would [date/time] work for a follow-up call? [ICR/FH]"

**For prospects where engagement doesn't make sense:**
"Hi [Name], it was great speaking with you today. Based on our conversation, my honest assessment is that [specific reason this isn't the right fit]. I'd suggest [alternative resource or approach]. I appreciate you taking the time to speak. [ICR/FH]"

## Client Acquisition Targets

**Quarterly targets:**
- Briefing calls booked: 6-8 per quarter
- Briefing call conversion to proposal: 4-5 per quarter
- Proposals converted to engagements: 2-3 per quarter
- New engagements: 2-3 per quarter

**Annual targets:**
- Briefing calls: 25-30 per year
- Engagements: 8-12 per year
- Revenue from new engagements: CHF 400-600K/year

---

## Client Quality Filter

**Criteria for accepting briefing call invitation:**
- Geneva-based executive (CFO, CEO, MD, Board Chairman) OR
- Executive with significant Geneva business exposure
- Regulatory or strategic challenge in DigitalCoa.ch service area
- Decision-making authority or direct access to decision-maker
- Realistic budget for professional advisory

**Criteria for declining:**
- Not Geneva-relevant
- Challenge outside DigitalCoa.ch service range
- No budget for professional advisory
- Looking for free consulting (not a fit for any paid advisory)
- Red flags (legal issues, unreasonable expectations)

**Quality filter rationale:**
- Every hour spent on unqualified prospect is hour not spent on qualified client
- Decline gracefully; refer elsewhere if possible
- Maintain reputation by not taking engagements that can't deliver value

## Client Diversification Strategy

**Revenue concentration limits:**
- No single client: >25% of annual revenue
- No single industry: >40% of annual revenue
- Target: 10-15 active client relationships

**Client type targets:**
- Geneva commodity trading firms: 2-3 clients
- Geneva financial institutions: 2-3 clients
- Geneva family businesses: 2-3 clients
- Geneva SMEs with EU exposure: 2-3 clients
- Geneva NGOs/international organizations: 1-2 clients
- Other Geneva/mid-market: 2-3 clients
