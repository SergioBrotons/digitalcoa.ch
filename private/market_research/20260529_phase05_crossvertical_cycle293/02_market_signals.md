# Market Signals: Cross-Vertical — C293

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures Across All Four ICPs

### The Universal Retrospective Exposure Moment

Every vertical is being triggered by the same structural event: they have already signed, attested, or responded to something AI-related, and the meaning of that commitment is now becoming clear. The market has moved from anticipatory anxiety to retrospective exposure recognition.

**Timing pattern:** The specific triggering events vary by vertical:
- Legal: PI carrier attestation at renewal
- Finance: Institutional client AI governance questionnaire
- Consulting: Client DDQ blocking engagements or requiring response
- Healthcare: Carrier attestation personal liability instrument

But the underlying mechanism is identical: retrospective confirmation of prior commitment.

### Shared Structural Failures

**1. The Evidence Architecture Failure**
Every vertical faces structurally identical evidence problems:
- Attestation forms imply a standard of contemporaneous documentation that no internal process currently produces
- The gap between required evidence and available evidence is not firm-specific — it is systemic
- The solution is architectural, not procedural

**2. The Personal Liability Discovery**
Every vertical has discovered personal liability exposure through a triggering event:
- Legal: Art.63 nFADP — criminal liability, not administrative
- Finance: FINMA personal accountability + nFADP Art.63
- Consulting: nFADP CHF 250,000 personal liability ceiling
- Healthcare: License irreplaceable; personal signature on carrier attestation

The discovery pattern is identical: "I didn't know this was personal."

**3. The Vendor Trust Collapse**
Every vertical selected tools based on "Swiss hosting" confidence and later discovered:
- Data residency ≠ processing location
- Swiss storage + US-based API processing = Art.321 CP exposure
- DPAs required under revised FADP are absent at most firms
- The trust model was purchased, not earned

**4. The Distributed Accountability Problem**
Every vertical faces the same internal governance failure:
- Legal: Associates using AI without disclosure to managing partner
- Finance: Portfolio managers deploying AI tools without central governance
- Consulting: Sub-consultants using AI outside engagement documentation
- Healthcare: Staff using AI for administrative tasks without physician awareness

The accountable person does not control the AI decisions being made in their name.

### Cross-Vertical Market Timing Signals

**The Window is Now**
All four verticals are in the same phase of market development:
- Geneva accountability ecosystem still forming
- No dominant provider has captured the "boutique-scale AI governance" position
- Attestation/questionnaire pressure creates acute urgency without proportionate solution
- First to establish credible position in each vertical captures the relationship before others arrive

**The Convergence Effect**
Every vertical is experiencing the same acceleration:
- Regulatory developments (FINMA 08/2024, CPC January 2025, FDPIC enforcement May 2025, AI Convention March 2025) provide confirmation, not projection
- Client questionnaires are arriving from multiple directions simultaneously
- The market has moved from "should I think about this" to "I need to handle this now"

**The Size Paradox**
All four verticals face the same irony:
- The smaller the firm, the less infrastructure available to address AI governance
- The smaller the firm, the more personally exposed the managing professional is
- No current provider has designed a solution for this specific size and exposure profile
