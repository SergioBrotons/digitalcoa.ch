# Compliance Context: Cross-Vertical — C293

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape Across All Four ICPs

### The Regulatory Architecture

Geneva AI accountability operates across five distinct regulatory layers:

**1. nFADP / Revised Federal Act on Data Protection**
- Applies to all four verticals (any organization processing Swiss personal data)
- Art.63: Criminal liability for unauthorized personal data processing — personal, not just entity-level
- Art.20/21: Right to explanation for automated decision-making — client-facing obligation
- Art.5: Lawfulness of data processing — inferred characteristics are personal data
- DPA requirements: Data processing agreements required, not optional

**2. FINMA Circular 2016/01 (as updated)**
- Applies to: Finance vertical (regulated financial institutions)
- AI governance expectations: Board accountability, risk management framework
- Documentation requirements: Algorithmic trading, AI-assisted decisions require audit trail
- Personal accountability: FINMA holds individuals personally accountable

**3. Swiss Banking Ombudsman / SRO Framework**
- Applies to: Finance vertical (member institutions)
- AI governance vacuum: No published guidance on AI-specific disputes
- Jurisdictional ambiguity: Traditional dispute resolution not designed for AI-adjacent complaints

**4. FMH / Medical Professional Standards**
- Applies to: Healthcare vertical (physicians, medical practices)
- Human oversight requirement: Stated without operational definition
- Tuesday translation problem: "Human oversight" has no standard Tuesday (operational) translation
- Medical standard of care: Both AI-use AND AI-non-use potentially create liability

**5. EU AI Act / Council of Europe AI Convention**
- Applies to: Any Geneva firm with EU nexus or EU-based clients
- Art.4 (EU AI Act): Deployer due diligence obligation — live since February 2025
- AI Convention: Council of Europe framework — Switzerland signatory, implementation in progress
- Extraterritorial effect: Geneva firms serving EU clients face live obligations

**6. Art.167a CPC / Legal Professional Standards**
- Applies to: Legal vertical (attorneys, law firms)
- January 2025 CPC amendment: External counsel (Art.321 CP) vs. in-house counsel (Art.167a CPC) accountability split
- Attorney-client privilege: AI system selection affects privilege scope
- Bar guidance vacuum: SBA guidelines too general, Geneva Bar has not issued canton-specific guidance

### The Cross-Vertical Regulatory Pattern

**The Documentation Paradox**
Every regulatory framework creates documentation requirements that:
- Are stated without operational definition (FMH "human oversight", nFADP "adequate governance")
- Imply evidence standards that no current internal process produces
- Operate retrospectively — the documentation must exist at the moment of inquiry, not after

**The Personal Liability Layer**
The regulatory frameworks across all four verticals share one feature:
- Personal liability exists independently of entity-level liability
- The managing professional's signature activates personal exposure
- The exposure is unnamed in most engagements — the professional does not know what they have committed to

**The Timing Acceleration**
Every framework is converging on the same moment:
- FINMA 08/2024: AI governance expectations now, not future
- FDPIC May 2025: Enforcement posture live, not guidance-first
- CPC January 2025: Accountability architecture now required
- AI Convention March 2025: Deployer obligations in force

The regulatory window is not approaching — it has arrived.

### The Geneva-Specific Regulatory Void

**Geneva Canton has not issued sector-specific AI guidance for:**
- Small law firms (Geneva Bar has not filled the SBA guidance gap)
- Boutique wealth management (SRO framework silent on AI-specific disputes)
- Small consulting boutiques (no Geneva-specific consulting AI governance guidance)
- Independent medical practices (FMH guidance stated without operational definition)

This void is the market opportunity: the provider who names the obligations and builds the proportionate documentation architecture for Geneva boutiques occupies a position that regulatory development will only reinforce.

---

### The FDPIC Enforcement Posture Shift

**May 2025 FDPIC enforcement posture:**
- nFADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data
- Enforcement is live now, not future risk
- Material shift from prior guidance-first framing

This changes the market calculus: "we're waiting for enforcement" is no longer available as a delay strategy. The exposure is present tense.
