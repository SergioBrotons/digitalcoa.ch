# ICP Language: Cross-Vertical Synthesis — C293

## How All Four ICPs Talk About AI Problems

### Universal Patterns Across Legal, Finance, Consulting, Healthcare

**The Retrospective Confirmation Frame**
Across all four verticals, the primary market driver is retrospective — ICPs are not asking "what might go wrong" but "what have I already committed to." Language pattern: "I already signed something" / "I already told the carrier..." / "I already responded to a client questionnaire..."

**The Adequacy Anxiety Pattern**
Every vertical uses "adequate" without definition:
- "How do I know what adequate governance looks like at our size?"
- "What does adequate human oversight mean in practice?"
- "The carrier asks if we have adequate AI governance — I don't know what that means"
- "We have adequate controls but I can't demonstrate it"

**The Accountability Gap Vocabulary**
Legal: "If I delegate to AI, am I still responsible?"
Finance: "The algorithm makes a recommendation — am I accountable for it?"
Consulting: "I recommended something based on AI analysis — am I liable?"
Healthcare: "The AI said X, I documented it, and now..."

**The Evidence Problem**
Every vertical describes the same structural failure: they cannot produce contemporaneous evidence of human authorization, review, or decision-making for AI-assisted work. The evidence standard implied by attestation forms is unobtainable with current internal processes.

**The Vendor Trust Gap**
Every vertical expresses the same underlying anxiety: "We selected a Swiss-hosted tool, but I don't actually know what happens to the data when I use it."

**The Budget Paradox**
Every vertical describes the same blocking pattern: governance investment is quantifiable, ROI is invisible, authorization is deferred while exposure grows.

## Phase 01–04 ICP Language Sources

### Phase 01 (Legal) — Boutique Law Firm
- "PI renewal is coming and I don't know what they're going to ask"
- "I have adequate controls but I can't demonstrate it"
- "My associates are using AI and I don't know what they're doing with it"
- "Swiss hosting doesn't mean what I thought it meant"

### Phase 02 (Finance) — Boutique Wealth Management
- "The institutional client sent a questionnaire and I didn't know how to answer most of it"
- "If the algorithm recommended it, am I responsible?"
- "We have a DPA — I think"
- "Our carrier asked about AI and we had nothing to show them"

### Phase 03 (Consulting) — Small Consulting Boutique
- "A client asked us to confirm our AI governance in their DDQ — we had to decline the engagement"
- "I'm recommending this AI tool to clients but I can't defend the methodology"
- "My sub-consultants are using AI and I have no idea what they're doing"
- "nFADP personal liability — I had no idea that was a thing"

### Phase 04 (Healthcare) — Small Medical Practice
- "A patient brought in an AI analysis and I had to pretend I understood it"
- "The FMH says human oversight — fine, but what does that actually mean?"
- "My colleague sent me an AI-generated second opinion and it's in my record now"
- "I signed the carrier attestation and I didn't really understand what I was signing"

## Cross-Vertical Ready-to-Use Language

| ICP Problem | Phrase They Use | What They Actually Mean |
|---|---|---|
| Don't know what AI is deployed | "shadow AI" | Unmanaged, unmonitored AI use across the organization |
| Can't demonstrate adequacy | "I can't show them what they want to see" | No documentation, no evidence, no audit trail |
| Personal liability unclear | "Am I actually personally exposed?" | Entity-level vs. personal exposure unclear |
| Vendor trust gap | "Swiss hosting — that should be fine, right?" | Data residency confused with processing location |
| Budget paralysis | "I know we need to do something but..." | Investment quantifiable, ROI invisible |
| Client questionnaire pressure | "The client asked us to confirm our AI governance" | Exposure moment that converts latent anxiety to acute urgency |

## Bilingual Marker Phrases

### English
- "adequate AI governance" — undefined adequacy standard
- "I signed something and I'm not sure what it means"
- "human oversight" — principle without operational definition
- "Swiss hosting" — data residency proxy for compliance
- "the carrier asked" — external accountability trigger
- "the client asked" — commercial accountability trigger

### French
- "gouvernance IA adéquate" — adequacy without definition
- "j'ai signé quelque chose sans vraiment comprendre" — retrospective exposure recognition
- "surveillance humaine" — FMH principle without Tuesday translation
- "hébergeur suisse" — proxy for compliance confidence
- "le carrier a demandé" — external accountability trigger
- "le client a demandé" — commercial accountability trigger
