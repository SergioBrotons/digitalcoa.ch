# Market Signals: Geneva Legal — Small Law Firms (2-5 Attorneys)

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

---

### 1. The nFADP Clock Started Without Their Knowledge

The most acute current problem: attorneys using cloud AI tools since 2023-2024 have been accumulating nFADP data processing obligations without a documented lawful basis. The clock did not start when they became aware of nFADP. It started when they first processed personal data through a cloud AI tool.

**The failure mode:** Attorneys believe they are in a preparation phase. They are in an active violation phase. The FDPIC has not yet audited a Geneva law firm. The absence of enforcement is being read as absence of obligation. This is a misreading with compounding consequences.

**The specific complaint:** "I didn't know I needed a legal basis. The tool provider said the data wasn't used for training. I assumed that was the end of the question."

**Structural dimension:** nFADP applies to personal data processing. AI query content in legal matters frequently contains personal data. The processing by the AI vendor's servers constitutes third-party processing under Art. 9 nFADP, requiring either consent or a specific legal basis. Most mandate letters do not contain AI processing authorization. Most attorneys have not identified this gap.

---

### 2. The Supervision Obligation Is Real and the Discharge Mechanism Is Absent

The SVA and Geneva bar have not published a standard for what "adequate supervision of AI-assisted work" means. Attorneys are operating under an obligation they cannot discharge, signing documents that represent review they cannot perform.

**The failure mode:** Attorneys are signing AI-assisted work product without a theory of supervision. When a matter goes wrong, when a client challenges a document, when a court questions a filing, the attorney will need to demonstrate what the supervision covered.

**The specific complaint:** "I reviewed the output. I can't tell you what the AI actually did to produce it. If you asked me to explain the reasoning, I couldn't."

**Structural dimension:** This is not a training problem. Training would give the attorney AI fluency. The problem is structural: even a fully AI-literate attorney faces the same discharge failure. The supervision obligation requires something the attorney cannot provide: access to the AI's reasoning process.

---

### 3. Client Questions Are Arriving Before the Bar Has Issued Guidance

The most tangible current signal: sophisticated clients (banks, multinationals, financial institutions) are beginning to ask Geneva law firms about their AI tool usage. The questions are specific: Which model? Where is data processed? Who reviews the AI output? What is the firm's AI governance policy?

**The failure mode:** The attorney has no prepared response. The client's questions reveal expectations the attorney cannot meet with current documentation. The client is effectively conducting a mini audit of the firm's AI governance. The firm has no idea what to show.

**The specific complaint:** "My client sent a questionnaire about AI. Three pages. I had to admit I didn't have answers to most of it."

**Structural dimension:** Client-driven AI governance disclosure pressure is arriving through commercial relationships before regulatory pressure arrives through the bar or FDPIC. This is a market mechanism creating obligations, independent of formal regulatory development.

---

### 4. The PI Insurance Questionnaire Revealed a Coverage Gap

Geneva law firm PI insurers have begun sending AI-specific questionnaire supplements to renewal applicants. The questionnaires probe: Which AI tools are used? How is AI-assisted work reviewed? What is the firm's AI governance policy?

**The failure mode:** Attorneys completing the questionnaire discovered they were attesting to the existence of an AI governance framework that does not exist. The questionnaire was the first moment the coverage gap became concrete.

**The specific complaint:** "I filled out the form. The questions were reasonable. My answers were not. I confirmed things I couldn't back up."

**Structural dimension:** The carrier is functioning as a de facto standard-setter. The questionnaire defines what adequate AI governance looks like for insurance purposes. The attorney is being rated against criteria that were never published as operational standards.

---

### 5. The Geneva Court AI Disclosure Expectation Is Locally Variable and Unmapped

Geneva courts have not published consistent AI disclosure requirements for filed documents. Practitioners report inconsistent judicial expectations: some judges ask about AI assistance, others do not. The variation is by judicial assignment, not by rule.

**The failure mode:** An attorney who used AI to draft a filing faces different disclosure obligations depending on which judge is assigned. There is no map. There is no advance notice. The discovery happens at the hearing.

**The specific complaint:** "I used AI to draft a memorandum. The judge asked whether AI was used. I said yes. I had no idea if that was the right answer, the wrong answer, or what the consequences were."

**Structural dimension:** Geneva civil procedure has no AI disclosure protocol. The inconsistency is not informal: it is the actual operative standard. Attorneys are navigating a procedurally undefined variable with professional conduct consequences.

---

### 6. The Bar CE Credit Market Is Measuring the Wrong Variable

The SVA and Geneva bar have introduced AI-related continuing education requirements. Attorneys are attending seminars, earning credits, and receiving certificates. The content addresses AI awareness. It does not address operational AI governance adequacy.

**The failure mode:** Attorneys complete CE requirements, receive certificates, and believe they have addressed the AI governance question. The CE system documents attendance. It does not assess whether the attorney's actual practice meets any standard of adequate AI governance.

**The specific complaint:** "I have my credits. I still don't know what I'm supposed to do on Monday morning."

**Structural dimension:** CE credits create a false adequacy signal. The bar is measuring attendance. The question of whether the attorney's practice is defensible remains unaddressed. The credits provide legal protection for the bar without providing operational protection for the attorney.

---

### 7. The Associate Is Using AI More Competently Than the Partner Reviews

At small firms, junior attorneys are typically more AI-fluent than supervising partners. This creates a structurally inverted supervision dynamic: the person who needs supervision is more capable with the tool than the person providing supervision.

**The failure mode:** Senior attorneys are approving AI-assisted work they cannot substantively evaluate. The signature represents a review it cannot guarantee. Junior attorneys know this. The dynamic is known but not named within the firm.

**The specific complaint:** "My associate produces better AI-assisted work than I can review. I sign off knowing I'm not actually supervising the AI portion."

**Structural dimension:** This is not a generational training problem. Even if the senior attorney received AI training, the supervision failure is structural. The AI's reasoning process is not accessible to the supervisor regardless of AI fluency.

---

### 8. The Vendor Term Changes Are Unmonitored and Accumulating

AI vendors update terms, privacy policies, and data processing agreements continuously. Each update may alter the legal basis for data processing, the scope of permitted use, or the data residency provisions.

**The failure mode:** Small law firms have no vendor term monitoring protocol. Terms change. The firm's legal basis for processing may have changed with them. The firm has no awareness of the change.

**The specific complaint:** "We signed up two years ago. I'm sure the terms have changed since then. I have no idea what they say now."

**Structural dimension:** Vendor term monitoring is a compliance function small firms do not perform. The accumulation of unmonitored changes creates an undocumented compliance surface that grows with time.

---

## Cross-Cutting Pattern

The common thread across all eight failure modes: attorneys are operating under obligations they cannot discharge, against expectations they cannot anticipate, using tools whose terms they do not monitor, supervised by standards that do not exist, and measured by metrics that do not assess what matters.

The market signal is not panic. It is quiet,结构性 uncertainty. The attorney knows something is wrong. They do not have a language to describe the specific problem, and they do not have a pathway to resolve it even if they could name it.
