# ICP Language: Geneva Legal — Small Law Firms (2-5 Attorneys)

## ICP Profile
Managing partner or individual attorney at a small Geneva law practice (2-5 attorneys), operating under the SVA (Schweizerische Verteidigerinnen- und Verteidigervereinigung / Swiss Lawyers Association) and cantonal bar supervision by the Geneva Bar Association (Ordre des avocats de Genève). Primary concern: deploying AI tools without a framework that satisfies confidentiality obligations, professional conduct rules, and emerging regulatory expectations.

---

## How the ICP Actually Speaks

The following phrases reflect how managing partners and individual attorneys describe their AI situation in informal Geneva professional settings. These are not marketing terms. They are working language.

---

### On the Confidentiality Problem

**"I know what I'm not supposed to do. I don't know what I am supposed to do."**
This is the foundational articulation. The attorney knows that attorney-client confidentiality under Art. 321 CP is strict. They know that cloud AI tools involve data processing by third parties. The gap is not awareness of the rule. The gap is the absence of a compliant pathway.

**"My client didn't consent to their matter being processed by a server in Ireland."**
Increasingly, this statement surfaces not from the attorney but from clients who have read about data residency. Clients are beginning to ask. The attorney has no prepared answer.

**"The tool provider's terms say the data isn't used for training. But what does that actually mean for my matters?"**
Vendor compliance language is read as inadequate. Attorneys sense a gap between vendor's contractual assurances and professional obligation, but cannot articulate the specific gap.

**"I have a duty of confidentiality. I'm also supposed to understand what I'm signing. These two things are not the reconciled."**
The attorney recognizes the contradiction without a resolution framework.

---

### On the Supervision Problem

**"I signed off on the AI tool. I have no idea what it's actually doing with the queries."**
The managing partner selected the tool, approved the subscription, but has no operational understanding of data flows. The supervision obligation is real; the discharge mechanism is absent.

**"My associate uses it more than I do. I'm supervising something I don't use."**
Generational inversion in AI competence creates a structural supervision problem. The senior attorney cannot substantively review AI-assisted work produced by junior colleagues who are more fluent with the tool.

**"I can review the output. I cannot review the process."**
This is the precise articulation of the supervision failure. Output review is possible. Process review is not available to the attorney who lacks AI fluency.

**"What am I actually certifying when I sign a pleading the AI helped draft?"**
Signature discharge mechanism absent. The attorney is certifying review without a theory of what that review covered.

---

### On the Regulatory Uncertainty Problem

**"The SVA hasn't said anything specific. The Geneva bar hasn't either. So I'm waiting."**
Bar guidance absence experienced as wait-state. The attorney is not ignoring the problem; they are waiting for authoritative direction that has not arrived.

**"My PI insurer sent a questionnaire about AI. I answered it. I'm not sure what I was confirming."**
Carrier questionnaire revealing documentation absence after the fact. The attorney completed the form without a prior framework.

**"What does adequate AI governance look like for a two-person firm?"**
Scale-specific question the attorney believes has no available answer. Enterprise frameworks do not translate.

**"If the FDPIC audits my firm, I don't know what I'd show them."**
nFADP enforcement anxiety without remediation pathway. Present-tense exposure acknowledged without resolution.

---

### On the Client Expectation Problem

**"Clients are starting to ask what AI tools I use. I don't know what to tell them."**
Client sophistication outpacing attorney disclosure framework. Not a crisis yet, but a structural tension the attorney anticipates will sharpen.

**"If a client asked me to explain how their matter was processed by AI, I couldn't do it."**
Reconstructability failure acknowledged. The attorney cannot provide an account of AI processing for a specific client matter.

**"My client is a bank. They have more AI governance policy than I do."**
Client-side AI governance sophistication creating asymmetric expectations. The attorney serving sophisticated clients faces disclosure pressure before the bar has issued guidance.

---

### On the Business Risk Problem

**"If I don't use AI, I'm less efficient. If I do use it, I don't know what I've done to my liability position."**
Efficiency pressure vs. liability uncertainty as simultaneous competing forces. No resolution available at boutique scale.

**"The associates want to use it. The partners don't understand it. Nobody owns the policy."**
Ownership vacuum for AI governance policy at small firm. The question of who is responsible for the firm's AI governance is not resolved.

**"I have no idea what happens to my AI-assisted work if the tool provider changes their terms."**
Vendor term dependency as unexamined business continuity risk. No small Geneva law firm has a vendor term monitoring protocol.

---

### On the Training Problem

**"The bar offers AI seminars. They don't tell me what I need to know."**
CE credit market providing attendance documentation without practice adequacy assurance. The attorney is completing requirements without addressing the actual governance question.

**"I've been practicing for twenty years. The AI is new. Why does the bar think a seminar fixes this?"**
Competence framework mismatch. The bar's CE response assumes the gap is knowledge acquisition. The attorney knows the gap is operational discharge mechanism.

---

## Language Pattern Summary

The Geneva small law firm attorney in 2026 is speaking in a specific register characterized by:

1. **Acknowledged uncertainty without remediation pathway** — knows the problem, cannot name the solution
2. **Scale-specific frustration** — enterprise frameworks recognized as inapplicable, no boutique-scale alternative exists
3. **Supervision failure articulation** — precise description of why output review ≠ adequate supervision
4. **Client anticipation anxiety** — not yet confronted by direct client questions, but expecting them
5. **Institutional guidance absence** — bar/SVA silence experienced as wait-state rather than permission
6. **Efficiency-liability paralysis** — simultaneous pressure in opposite directions, no resolution available

## French-Language Register (Registre de Langue)

**"Je sais ce que je ne dois pas faire. Je ne sais pas ce que je dois faire."**
L'expression fondatrice. L'avocat connaît le devoir de confidentialité (Art. 321 CP). Il sait que les outils IA impliquent un traitement par des tiers. Le problème n'est pas l'ignorance de la règle. C'est l'absence de parcours conforme.

**"Mon client n'a pas consenti à ce que son affaire soit traitée par un serveur en Irlande."**
Déclaration qui émerge de plus en plus, non pas de l'avocat, mais des clients ayant pris connaissance des questions de résidence des données.

**"Je supervise quelque chose que je n'utilise pas."**
L'inversion générationnelle en compétence IA crée un problème structurel de supervision.

**"Je peux réviser le résultat. Je ne peux pas réviser le processus."**
L'articulation précise de l'échec de supervision. Le output est révisable. Le processus ne l'est pas.

**"Si le PFPDT audite mon étude, je ne sais pas ce que je leur montrerais."**
Anxiété nFADP sans parcours de remediation. Exposition actuelle reconnue sans résolution.

**"Si un client me demandait d'expliquer comment son affaire a été traitée par IA, je ne pourrais pas le faire."**
L'aveu de l'impossibilité de reconstruction. L'avocat ne peut pas fournir un compte rendu du traitement IA pour une affaire spécifique.

**"Le bareme de la Fédération me demande de justifier l'utilisation de l'IA. Ce qu'il veut dire par 'adéquat', je ne le sais pas."**
Le carrier questionnaire révèle l'absence de documentation après coup. Le questionnaire activate une obligation dont les contours restent imprécis.

**"Mon client est une banque. Il a plus de politique de gouvernance IA que moi."**
Attentes asymétriques créées par la sophistication croissante des clients en matière d'IA.
