## 1. The Dominant Market Vocabulary

### What the Market Actually Says

The Geneva SMB market talks about AI accountability using four distinct language registers, none of which accurately describes the actual problem:

**Register A: "Compliance"**
- Used by: law firms, accounting firms, larger consultancies
- Framing: AI accountability = checkbox compliance with EU AI Act / nFADP
- Language signals: "compliant", "audit-ready", "regulatory alignment", "risk mitigation"
- Problem: Reduces AI accountability to form-filling, misses the documentation debt reality

**Register B: "Governance"**
- Used by: enterprise consultancies, Big 4 affiliates, strategy firms
- Framing: AI accountability = governance framework design
- Language signals: "framework", "policy architecture", "oversight structure", "AI governance board"
- Problem: Produces documents, not evidence. Frameworks without evidence architecture are decorations.

**Register C: "Risk Management"**
- Used by: insurance brokers, risk consultants, some banking advisors
- Framing: AI accountability = risk transfer and liability limitation
- Language signals: "coverage", "carrier requirements", "attestation", "indemnification"
- Problem: Treats accountability as something to offload to a carrier, not to build internally

**Register D: "Efficiency / Productivity"**
- Used by: tech vendors, SaaS platforms, internal IT teams
- Framing: AI accountability = documenting AI tool usage for efficiency gains
- Language signals: "AI-powered", "workflow automation", "productivity enhancement"
- Problem: Frames accountability as a cost center, not a value builder. Misses the evidence standard shift entirely.

## 2. Language Gap Between Market and Reality

### What SMBs Hear vs. What They Actually Need

| Market Language | What SMBs Think It Means | What It Actually Requires |
|-----------------|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| "Compliant with EU AI Act" | We have a checkbox done | Systematic evidence architecture across all AI tool deployments |
| "AI governance framework" | We have a policy document | Persistent, recordable evidence trail of AI-assisted decisions |
| "Carrier attestation" | We're covered if something goes wrong | Personalized, matter-specific evidence documentation that survives scrutiny |
| "Risk mitigation" | We bought insurance | Personal liability exposure documentation under nFADP Art. 63 |
| "AI audit readiness" | We can pass a regulatory review | Retroactive reconstruction capability for every AI decision made |

### The Critical Gap
The market talks about accountability as a **destination** (compliant, covered, audited). The actual problem is that accountability is a **continuous evidence architecture** that must exist before, during, and after any AI-assisted decision.

## 3. Bilingual Market Language Specifics

### French Market (Geneva Primary)
- **Accountabilité IA** — formal, regulatory framing; sounds like government requirement
- **Gouvernance des systèmes IA** — corporate/governance vocabulary; too abstract for SMB
- **Conformité IA** — compliance framing; triggers checkbox mental model
- **Documentation de l'IA** — documentation framing; too passive; misses the evidence standard
- **Traçabilité des décisions IA** — decision traceability; closer to actual need but still abstract
- **Responsabilité algorithmique** — algorithmic responsibility; academic, not operational

**Most SMBs in French market:** "Je n'ai pas de problème d'IA" — the market doesn't see itself as having an accountability problem yet, until the renewal form arrives.

### English Market (Geneva International, Expat, Tech)
- **AI governance** — too broad, too corporate
- **AI compliance** — triggers audit anxiety
- **AI accountability** — closest to actual need; growing awareness
- **AI audit readiness** — specific enough to trigger action; renewal window language
- **Responsible AI** — too vague, too ethical, not operational enough
- **AI documentation** — passive, underestimated

**Most SMBs in English market:** "We use AI but we don't know what that means for accountability" — latent problem recognition without structured response.

## 4. Competitive Language Traps

### What Competitors Claim vs. What SMBs Actually Experience

**Trap 1: "AI Compliance Framework"**
- What competitors say: "We help you become AI compliant"
- What SMBs experience: A template document that satisfies no specific standard and generates no actual evidence
- Gap: Compliance theater vs. evidence architecture

**Trap 2: "AI Risk Assessment"**
- What competitors say: "We identify your AI risks"
- What SMBs experience: A risk register that exists as a document but has no operational link to actual AI decisions being made
- Gap: Risk inventory vs. risk documentation of decisions actually made

**Trap 3: "AI Policy Development"**
- What competitors say: "We create your AI governance policy"
- What SMBs experience: A polished document that no one in the organization can actually follow operationally
- Gap: Policy documentation vs. operational evidence systems

**Trap 4: "AI Audit Preparation"**
- What competitors say: "We prepare you for AI regulatory audits"
- What SMBs experience: A checklist review, not a retroactive reconstruction capability
- Gap: Audit checklist vs. evidence production capability

## 6. Six NET-NEW ICP Language Findings for Phase 06

1. **The word "accountability" itself is underspecified in the Geneva SMB market.** Most providers use it to mean compliance or risk transfer. Sergio's use of "accountability" to mean persistent evidence architecture is not currently occupied in the market.

2. **"Framework" is the market's default vocabulary for AI governance, but SMBs experience frameworks as decorations, not operational systems.** This creates a receptivity window for evidence-based accountability language.

3. **The carrier renewal form is becoming the SMB's first point of contact with AI accountability language.** Brokers are the de facto educators. This is a channel insight, not just a language insight.

4. **Bilingual (EN/FR) language specificity matters for Geneva.** Competitors who offer only English-language AI governance materials or only French regulatory compliance documents are leaving the bilingual market need unmet.

5. **"Evidence" as a word triggers physician-grade credibility** — it speaks the language of professionals who already understand what evidence means for their liability, not abstract governance categories.

6. **No competitor in the Geneva SMB space currently speaks about AI accountability as a documentation debt problem.** The compounding vectors (legal + finance + consulting + healthcare) create a market need that has not been articulated by any competitor.

## 7. Language Action Points for Sergio

- Lead with **"evidence architecture"** rather than "compliance" or "governance" — these are occupied territories that trigger the wrong mental models
- Use **carrier renewal language** to trigger recognition ("When your broker asks you to fill out the AI attestation section...")
- Frame accountability as **documentation debt** rather than compliance — debt framing motivates action without triggering anxiety
- Speak in **first-person singular** when addressing SMBs ("I help you build the evidence record that protects you") — positions Sergio as practitioner, not consultant
- Use **"retroactive reconstruction"** as a specific capability term — it names a concrete need that no other provider names
- Bilingual EN/FR delivery required — do not default to English-only for the Geneva international market
