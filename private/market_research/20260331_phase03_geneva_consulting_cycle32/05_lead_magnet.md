# The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework
### A Practical AI Governance Guide for Geneva's Boutique Consultancies

---

## Executive Summary

The EU AI Act's GPAI transparency obligations have been operative since August 2, 2025 — you've been operating under them for 8.5 months. Most small consulting firms don't know this. If your firm uses AI tools in client engagements, delivers AI-enhanced recommendations, or processes client data through AI systems, you have immediate obligations under the GPAI framework.

This framework gives you a clear path to:
1. Understand which GPAI obligations apply to your firm
2. Assess your current AI governance posture
3. Build a practical compliance framework

**GPAI obligations are already active. Your window to get compliant is now.**

---

## Section 1: What GPAI Means for Your Firm

**The EU AI Act GPAI (General Purpose AI) framework — operative since Aug 2, 2025:**

**Transparency obligations (already active):**
- You must inform clients when AI is used in your deliverables
- AI-generated content must be marked as AI-generated
- You must document AI tools used in client engagements
- Human oversight must be maintained over AI-assisted work

**Who it affects:**
- Any consulting firm that uses AI tools in client work
- Firms providing AI-enhanced analysis, reports, recommendations
- Firms processing client data through AI systems
- Firms with EU nexus (EU clients, EU projects, EU-regulated industries)

**Phase 01 finding (cross-phase):** 69% of professionals use AI individually. 34% of firms have formal AI policies. The 35-point governance gap means most small consulting firms are likely non-compliant with GPAI transparency obligations without knowing it.

---

## Section 2: The Dual Compliance Challenge

**As a Geneva consulting firm, you face two frameworks simultaneously:**

**1. EU AI Act (GPAI) — ACTIVE since Aug 2, 2025**
- Transparency obligations: inform clients, mark AI content, document AI tools
- For firms with EU clients or EU-regulated industry work
- High-risk AI provisions activate Aug 2, 2026 (~4mo)

**2. Swiss nFADP — ACTIVE since Sep 1, 2023**
- FADP applies to AI-supported data processing without new law
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing
- Purpose + functionality + data sources (FDPIC three-part test)
- Privacy by Design in AI procurement

**The overlap:** Both frameworks require documenting AI tool use, maintaining human oversight, and informing clients. A single governance framework can satisfy both.

## Section 3: GPAI Obligations — What to Do Now

**30-day actions:**
- [ ] Inventory AI tools used in client engagements
- [ ] Identify which clients are affected by EU AI Act GPAI
- [ ] Draft AI tool disclosure language for client communications
- [ ] Assess which AI tools process client data (FADP scope)
- [ ] Flag high-risk AI uses (client decision-making, compliance recommendations)

**60-day actions:**
- [ ] Implement AI tool documentation process
- [ ] Develop client-facing AI transparency statement
- [ ] Conduct DPIA for high-risk AI tools (contract analysis, compliance AI)
- [ ] Establish human oversight requirements for AI-assisted work
- [ ] Train consultants on GPAI obligations

**90-day actions:**
- [ ] Deploy AI governance policy firm-wide
- [ ] Integrate AI disclosure into client engagement terms
- [ ] Document GPAI compliance posture
- [ ] Review AI tool contracts for GDPR/nFADP compliance
- [ ] Prepare for Aug 2, 2026 high-risk AI activation (~4mo)

## Section 4: The Shadow AI Problem in Consulting

**What it looks like:**
- Consultants using ChatGPT to draft project deliverables → client data shared with OpenAI
- Research teams using AI tools to analyze market data → client confidential information processed externally
- Project managers using AI to generate status reports → internal project data shared with AI providers

**The exposure:**
1. **GPAI:** Non-compliance with transparency obligations (AI use not disclosed)
2. **nFADP:** Client data processed by AI tools without documented consent or DPIA
3. **Client contracts:** Confidentiality breaches if client data is shared with AI providers
4. **Professional liability:** AI-generated recommendations not properly reviewed or documented
