## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Geneva Litigation Funding Market — Active, Institutional, Growing
- Swiss Federal Supreme Court endorsement since 2004; no specific regulatory regime
- Minimum claim: CHF 100,000–500,000; expected success rate >60%
- Geneva-based players: Omni Bridgeway, Swiss Legal Finance (Lawdragon 100 2025)
- Funders cover attorney fees, court fees, expert costs, adverse costs in exchange for % of recovery
- Key implication: access to justice for larger commercial disputes; creates advisory demand for litigation funding structuring and independent lawyer independence compliance

### Finding 02: EU DSA + EU Data Act Creating Cross-Border Compliance Advisory Work
- EU DSA (applicable Feb 2024): Swiss digital service providers serving EU users face content moderation, transparency reports, EU legal representative obligations
- EU Data Act (applicable Sep 2025): IoT data rights, fair data sharing contract terms, SaaS portability/interoperability requirements — major for Swiss manufacturers and cloud providers
- Swiss FADP revision (Sep 2023, no transition): new MCCs required distinct from EU SCCs; Swiss-US DPF effective Sep 2024 simplifies transfers to certified US companies
- Geneva digital/tech companies need compliance frameworks covering both DSA and Data Act

### Finding 03: Swiss Arbitration Record Caseload + New Procedural Framework
- Swiss Arbitration Centre: +25% new cases 2025 (record); Geneva remains premier seat for non-US international disputes
- Supplemental Swiss Rules for Trust, Estate and Foundation Disputes — in force July 1, 2025: specialized Geneva offering for private wealth/estate disputes
- Swiss CPC revision (Jan 2025): cost advance capped at 50% of estimated costs; winning party risk eliminated; commercial courts may conduct proceedings in English for international disputes ≥CHF 100,000
- Emergency arbitrator procedures growing; Russian-displaced cases still creating complex caseload

### Finding 04: mHRDD Enforcement Live + FINMA Greenwashing Liability Active
- mHRDD civil liability operative; CSDDD dual-compliance premium advisory for EU-CH groups
- Supply chain mapping (tier 2/tier 3 visibility) = primary compliance gap
- FINMA greenwashing enforcement active 2024-2025; several investment product cases opened
- Board personal liability for approving misleading ESG statements
- Advisory opportunities: mHRDD gap assessments, greenwashing risk reviews, board training, dual CSDDD/mHRDD frameworks

### Finding 05: FADP + AI Enforcement — No Grace Period; Gap Assessments in Demand
- FDPIC confirmed: FADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing with no transition period
- AI DPIAs mandatory for high-risk AI processing; vendor due diligence on AI tools required
- AI training data: licensing/rights unresolved; FDPIC guidance evolving
- Geneva tech/pharma concentration = high exposure for AI R&D companies
- Cross-border AI data flows require both Swiss MCCs and EU SCCs depending on direction
- Advisory entry points: AI DPIAs, vendor due diligence, AI breach response, cross-border flow mapping

### Finding 06: AML Enforcement Intensifying — UBO Thresholds Cut to 10%, CRS/FATCA Penalties Rising
- AMLD6 alignment: beneficial ownership threshold cut to 10%; continuous monitoring required
- Legacy Geneva private wealth structures frequently have UBO documentation gaps — remediation demand
- FINMA: US DOJ NPAs/DPAs revoked where AML programs inadequate; Swiss banks with US cross-border exposure at highest risk
- CRS/FATCA reporting errors (TIN format, controlling person classification) = common penalty triggers
- Whistleblower protections strengthened → internal investigation demand rising
- Advisory: UBO remediation, AML program gap assessments, CRS/FATCA penalty response, RM training
