# Phase 01 — Objection Handling
## Cycle 52 — 2026-04-01

## Common Objections — Geneva Small Law Firms

### Objection 1: "AI isn't relevant to my practice"
**Response:** 70% of legal professionals already using AI tools. 70% projected to increase AI adoption in 2026. The question is not IF AI is used — it is whether the firm has governance over AI already in use (Shadow AI audit Decision 0).

### Objection 2: "FADP doesn't apply to us"
**Response:** FADP is technology-neutral. FDPIC confirmed May 2025: applies to ALL AI-supported data processing. Law firms explicitly in scope. No new law required — existing obligations are operative. Enforcement absent = timing risk not absence.

### Objection 3: "We're too small for formal AI governance"
**Response:** 190h/yr efficiency gain from AI. 40% reduction in manual workloads. Formal governance is not complex — a Shadow AI audit (Decision 0) takes 30 minutes and identifies the gap. First-mover advantage: clients increasingly selecting firms based on responsible AI use.

### Objection 4: "CASUS/FABIAN handles our data compliance"
**Response:** FABIAN and CASUS address Swiss data residency. FADP compliance requires broader stack: Art. 21 human review + DPIA + Privacy by Design + transparency obligations + client communication. Data residency is necessary but not sufficient.

### Objection 5: "The Federal Council is delaying AI regulation"
**Response:** Two-timeline: digitalswitzerland May 2026 (~4 weeks) is external pressure without formal obligation — but May 2026 First Draft signals direction. End-2026 formal legislation (CoE AI Convention). FADP obligations are operative NOW. Enforcement absent is timing risk.

### Objection 6: "Our enterprise clients don't require AI governance"
**Response:** AI governance is becoming a client trust differentiator. Clients selecting counsel based on responsible AI use (CMS/Wolters Kluwer 2026). Forward-thinking clients will require AI governance documentation. First movers capture premium positioning.

### Objection 7: "We use AI responsibly already"
**Response:** Formal AI governance means documented policies, staff training, oversight protocols, and client transparency. Individual responsible use ≠ firm-level formal governance. The 36-point gap (70% individual vs 34% firm-level formal) quantifies this.
