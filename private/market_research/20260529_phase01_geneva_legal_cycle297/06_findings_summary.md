# Findings Summary — Geneva Legal C297 Phase 01

## Six NET-NEW Findings

### Finding 01: AI Vendor Wind-Down Liability — The Unnamed Accountability Window

**Core finding:** Small Geneva firms using AI tools that have been discontinued, acquired, or significantly changed terms. AI outputs produced under discontinued tool configurations remain in client files. The attorney is accountable for work product from tool configurations that no longer exist, with no reconstruction capability.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** Prior findings addressed tool selection, vendor compliance, and configuration state generally. This finding isolates the temporal dimension — the accountability for AI outputs produced under tool configurations that no longer exist, creating a specific and nameable problem with urgency drivers operating now.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Vendor consolidation is accelerating. The outputs from tools that no longer exist are still in your client files. You're accountable for them."

**Urgency driver:** Vendor consolidation wave 2026–2027. Matter files with AI-assisted work from discontinued tools accumulating. Retroactive reconstruction impossible.

---

### Finding 02: Matter-Level AI Authorization Architecture — The No-AI Instruction Problem

**Core finding:** Corporate clients and some private clients instructing Geneva firms not to use AI on their matters. Boutique firms have no system for flagging matter-level AI restrictions, tracking which matters carry no-AI instructions, and ensuring no AI-assisted work enters restricted files. The instruction exists but cannot be honored operationally.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** Prior findings named client AI concern generally. This finding isolates the operational architecture gap — the translation failure between client instruction and firm practice. Unique to small firms where one person holds both client relationship management and AI tool usage.

**Ready-to-use language:** "When a client says no AI on their matter, do you have a system for making that instruction actually work? Most small Geneva firms don't. The instruction exists. It can't be honored."

**Urgency driver:** Corporate client AI governance questionnaires increasingly including matter-level restrictions. Instructions arriving faster than firms can operationalize.

---

### Finding 03: AI-Assisted Billing Derivation — Fee Attribution Opacity

**Core finding:** When AI assists in drafting briefs, letters, research memos, or contract analysis, and the firm bills time for the work, the billing entry contains time for AI assistance and time for attorney review. Clients have no visibility into this split. In the event of fee disputes or client complaints about AI-assisted work, firms cannot demonstrate what portion of fees were for attorney judgment versus AI generation.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** Prior billing findings addressed efficiency gains not converting to firm economics. This isolates the client-facing fee transparency question — what does the client receive when they pay for AI-assisted work, and can the firm demonstrate the attorney review contribution?

**Ready-to-use language:** "When AI assists your billing, what does the client actually receive? What portion of that invoice is attorney judgment? Can you demonstrate that split if the client asks?"

**Urgency driver:** Client sophistication increasing. Sophisticated clients beginning to question AI involvement in billed work.

---

### Finding 04: Privilege Architecture and Vendor Data Retention — Specific Collision Point

**Core finding:** AI vendor data retention policies creating privilege interaction that has not been specifically mapped for Geneva practice. When vendor terms include data retention for query and response content, and attorney-client communications about matters flow through vendor systems, the question of privilege waiver through vendor access is unresolved and unnamed in Geneva guidance.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** Prior findings named Swiss hosting as a consideration and nFADP Art.9 compliance generally. This isolates the specific mechanism — vendor data retention creating privilege waiver risk — and identifies the absence of any Geneva-specific guidance on this intersection.

**Ready-to-use language:** "When your client's matter communications flow through an AI vendor's systems, and those systems retain the content, what just happened to your privilege? Nobody in Geneva has mapped this specific collision."

**Urgency driver:** Vendor terms updating regularly. Each update potentially modifies the retention posture of matter communications.

---

### Finding 05: Geneva AI Incident Response Protocol Gap — The 72-Hour Problem

**Core finding:** When something goes wrong with AI use in a client matter — wrong advice, hallucinated case law, incorrect calculation, data processing error — small Geneva firms have no defined incident response protocol. No Geneva-specific guidance exists for what a boutique firm should do in the first 24, 48, or 72 hours after discovering an AI-assisted error.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** Prior findings addressed AI research hallucination as liability and client disclosure sequencing. This isolates the response protocol gap — what to do in the acute window after discovery, when reconstruction decisions must be made before the trail disappears. Named as discrete gap with specific time sensitivity.

**Ready-to-use language:** "When an AI error hits a client matter, you have 48-72 hours before the trail disappears. Do you know what to do in that window? Most Geneva firms are making it up as they go."

**Urgency driver:** AI errors in legal practice becoming more frequent as AI use intensifies. No Geneva-specific incident response protocol means errors compound before any structured response occurs.

---

### Finding 06: SAV Guidelines Operational Translation Gap — The Missing Decision Tool

**Core finding:** The SAV published AI guidelines. Managing partners at small Geneva firms describe them as conceptually correct but operationally unusable — principles without decision trees, responsibility without implementation paths, accountability standards without measurement methodology. The gap between "you're accountable" and "here's how to implement that at boutique scale" is unnamed in any Geneva resource.

**Why distinct from C214–C296:** C294 Finding 01 named the accountability gridlock pattern from SAV framework without decision tree. This finding isolates the operational translation gap as a discrete service product opportunity — the missing tool is not just a gap, it is a named product concept: a decision-tree implementation guide for SAV AI guidelines at boutique scale.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The SAV guidelines are correct. They tell you you're responsible. They don't show you how to discharge that responsibility on a Tuesday with two attorneys and no IT department. That's the gap."

**Urgency driver:** Carrier attestation questions explicitly referencing SAV guidelines. Attorneys need operational translation, not more principles.

## ICP Language — Key Patterns

**How Geneva legal ICPs talk about AI problems:**

1. **"I signed the carrier form. I don't know what I committed to."** — The personal signature crystallizes the exposure. Carrier attestation is the acute accountability moment.

2. **"What does 'adequate' mean for a two-person firm?"** — Adequacy is undefined at boutique scale. The obligation is real; the discharge mechanism is absent.

3. **"The tools arrived. I didn't choose them."** — AI arrived through software updates, embedded features, staff activation. Accountability for AI they didn't consciously authorize is structurally unavoidable.

4. **"I can't supervise what I can't understand."** — The supervision problem is architectural, not technological. AI reasoning inaccessibility makes human oversight impossible regardless of attorney AI fluency.

5. **"The SAV says I'm responsible. They don't tell me what responsible looks like."** — Principles without decision trees. Accountability without implementation paths.

6. **"Vendor terms changed. What did I just accept?"** — Each software update potentially modifies data processing terms accepted by default.

7. **"Something went wrong. I have no idea what to do first."** — No Geneva-specific incident response protocol for boutique firms. Scrambling in real time.
