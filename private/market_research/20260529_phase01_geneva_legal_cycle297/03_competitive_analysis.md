# Competitive Analysis — Geneva Legal (C297 Phase 01)

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

### Current Market Landscape

**Big Four / Enterprise Providers:**
- Present in Geneva for large financial institution clients
- AI governance frameworks designed for 500+ employee organizations
- Engagement structures: large project budgets, 3–6 month implementations
- Do not serve 2–5 attorney boutiques
- Language: enterprise risk, compliance architecture, regulatory change management
- Not accessible to Geneva small law firms on cost or complexity grounds

**Legal Technology Vendors:**
- Clio, LawPAY, and practice management platforms adding AI features
- Selling productivity tools, not accountability infrastructure
- Terms of service disclaimers making them legally harmless
- No Geneva-specific adaptation for SAV guidelines or Swiss professional obligations
- No accountability framework — tool vendors benefit from AI adoption regardless of outcome

**Swiss Bar Association (SAV):**
- Published AI guidelines acknowledging attorney AI accountability
- No operational implementation guidance for small firms
- No decision-tree framework, no attestation preparation support
- Guidelines positioned as principles document, not operational manual
- SAV not providing small-firm implementation services — structural gap

**Geneva Cantonal Bar (ORDRE des Avocats Genevois):**
- Cantonal guidance less specific than SAV in many areas
- No Geneva-specific AI governance operational guide for small practices
- Not positioned as service provider, professional self-regulation body
- No active small-firm AI governance support program

**Insurance Brokers (PI specialists):**
- Asking AI-specific questions at renewal
- Not providing answers — framing the risk, not solving it
- Using AI governance as underwriting variable
- No Geneva-calibrated framework for small firm AI governance compliance
- No service helping firms prepare for what the carrier is actually asking

**International AI Governance Frameworks:**
- ISO 42001 (AI management systems)
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- EU AI Act compliance approaches
- Designed for enterprise-scale organizations
- Not proportional to 2–5 attorney firms
- Not adapted to Swiss attorney professional obligations or SAV guidelines

### What's Missing — The Specific Gaps

**Gap 01: SAV Guidelines Operational Decision Tree**

What exists: SAV AI guidelines as principles document.
What's missing: Decision-tree implementation guide for SAV guidelines at boutique scale.
Why it matters: Attorneys need to discharge SAV accountability obligation but have no operational path.
Market gap: No Geneva resource converts SAV principles into boutique-scale decision trees.
Product implication: SAV Implementation Guide for Small Geneva Firms — discrete named product, CHF 1,800–2,200.

**Gap 02: Carrier Attestation Preparation Service**

What exists: Carriers asking AI questions at renewal.
What's missing: Service helping small firms prepare responses that satisfy carrier requirements without creating new exposure.
Why it matters: Attorneys signing attestations to undefined adequacy standards without preparation.
Market gap: No Geneva service offering carrier attestation review before submission.
Product implication: Carrier Attestation Preparation — pre-signing review ensuring attestation is supportable.

**Gap 03: Matter-Level AI Authorization Management**

What exists: Some corporate clients sending AI-use instructions at matter level.
What's missing: System for small firms to track matter-level AI restrictions, honor no-AI instructions operationally, and document compliance.
Why it matters: Firms cannot honor client AI instructions without operational infrastructure.
Market gap: No Geneva service offering matter-level AI restriction management for boutiques.
Product implication: Matter Authorization Management System — operational infrastructure for no-AI instructions.

**Gap 04: AI Incident Response Protocol for Boutiques**

What exists: General incident response frameworks, not adapted to professional services or Geneva context.
What's missing: Geneva-specific AI incident response protocol for small law firms — first 72 hours.
Why it matters: AI errors in legal matters create immediate client disclosure, documentation, and carrier notification obligations.
Market gap: No Geneva-calibrated professional services AI incident response protocol for small firms.
Product implication: Geneva Legal AI Incident Response Protocol — discrete service with 24/48/72-hour checklist.

**Gap 05: Vendor Term Review Service for Small Firms**

What exists: Vendor terms accepted by default, reviewed rarely.
What's missing: Systematic vendor term review against Swiss professional obligations — Art.321 CP, nFADP Art.9, SAV guidelines.
Why it matters: Attorney liability for vendor data processing decisions they never consciously evaluated.
Market gap: No Geneva service offering boutique-scale vendor term review against professional obligations.
Product implication: Vendor Term Review — systematic evaluation of AI vendor terms against professional liability exposure.

**Gap 06: Geneva AI Governance Register for Small Firms**

What exists: No Geneva-specific AI governance documentation standard for small firms.
What's missing: SAV-aligned AI register adapted to 2–5 attorney firm constraints — practical, attestation-ready, operational.
Why it matters: Carriers and clients asking for AI governance evidence; no Geneva-specific standard for what small firms should document.
Market gap: No Geneva resource translating AI governance requirements into small-firm-appropriate documentation standards.
Product implication: AI Governance Register for Geneva Small Firms — operational documentation standard, not enterprise framework.

### Competitive Positioning

**Where no player operates:**
Geneva small law firm AI accountability infrastructure — boutique-scale, SAV-aligned, operationally feasible, carrier-ready.

**What this requires that others don't have:**
- SAV guidelines operational translation methodology
- Geneva cantonal practice context (ORDRE, CPTG, OAV)
- Small firm implementation feasibility (2–5 attorneys, no IT, no compliance officer)
- Carrier attestation response calibration
- Privilege and professional secrecy adaptation for AI tool usage

**What cannot be replicated without sustained Geneva participation:**
- Geneva informal accountability circuit intelligence (carrier underwriting evolution, bar sessions dynamics, peer problem patterns)
- SAV guideline evolution tracking
- Geneva court AI procedural pattern recognition
- FDPIC enforcement trajectory intelligence
- Cross-vertical synthesis (legal + finance + consulting patterns inform legal advisory)

**Strategic position:** Accountability infrastructure for Geneva small law firms — the operational implementation layer the SAV guidelines describe but don't provide, calibrated for boutique constraints, serving the market Big Four and generic frameworks don't serve.
