# ICP Language — Geneva Legal (C297 Phase 01)

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### English — Managing Partner / Senior Associate, 2–5 Attorneys

**Core accountability framing:**
- "I signed the carrier form. I don't know what I committed to."
- "What does 'adequate' even mean for a two-person firm?"
- "The SAV guidelines tell me I'm responsible. They don't tell me what responsible looks like."
- "My associate is using AI. I can't review what I can't understand."
- "If something goes wrong, I'm the one who signed the attestation. That's the part that keeps me up at night."
- "There's no Geneva standard for what adequate AI governance means at boutique scale."
- "The frameworks don't tell me what to do on a Tuesday."
- "I have tools I didn't consciously choose — they came with the software, or the associate turned them on."
- "Swiss hosting sounds good but I don't know what it means for attorney-client privilege."
- "The bar says I'm accountable. The carrier asks questions I can't answer. The vendor says none of this is their problem."
- "I'm being asked to attest to something nobody has defined."
- "Client confidentiality and AI tool processing — those are in tension and nobody has mapped the specific collision."
- "I don't know what 'adequate' looks like until someone examines me."

**Urgency triggers:**
- PI renewal with AI-specific questions
- Client asking about AI governance at matter level
- Associate joining with AI habits formed elsewhere
- Vendor updating terms without notification
- Counterparty questionnaire requiring methodology disclosure
- Colleague mentioning a problem they encountered

**Self-description patterns:**
- "Small Geneva practice"
- "We don't have an IT department or compliance officer"
- "I'm the one who makes these decisions"
- "I wear multiple hats — I'm the attorney, the office manager, and apparently now the AI governance officer"
- "We use what works, we don't have a formal process"
- "I trust my people but I can't supervise what I don't understand"

- « J'ai signé le document de l'assureur. Je ne sais pas ce à quoi je me suis engagé. »
- « Qu'est-ce que 'adéquat' signifie concrètement pour un cabinet de deux personnes ? »
- « Les directives du SAV me disent que je suis responsable. Elles ne me disent pas ce que responsable veut dire. »
- « Mon collaborateur utilise l'IA. Je ne peux pas réviser ce que je ne peux pas comprendre. »
- « Si quelque chose tourne mal, je suis celui qui a signé l'attestation. C'est ça qui me garde éveillé la nuit. »
- « Il n'existe pas de standard genevois pour ce que signifie une gouvernance IA adéquate à l'échelle d'un petit cabinet. »
- « Les cadres ne me disent pas ce que je dois faire un mardi. »
- « J'ai des outils que je n'ai pas consciemment choisis — ils sont venus avec le logiciel, ou le collaborateur les a activés. »
- « L'hébergement suisse sounds good but I don't know what it means for attorney-client privilege. »
- « Le barreau dit que je suis responsable. L'assureur pose des questions auxquelles je ne peux pas répondre. Le fournisseur dit que ce n'est pas son problème. »

**Déclencheurs d'urgence :**

- Questionnaire de la contrepartie nécessitant la divulgation de méthodologie
- Collègue qui mentionne un problème qu'il a rencontré

## Key Linguistic Markers

**The Geneva legal ICP:**
- Uses "I" not "we" when discussing accountability decisions — personal ownership is immediate and visceral
- Frames AI governance as a personal liability problem, not a compliance efficiency problem
- Describes tools as something that happened to them, not something they chose
- Has no operational translation for "adequate" — the word is present but undefined
- Experiences regulatory obligations as accumulating pressure with no resolution map
- Trusts peer experience over published guidance because published guidance doesn't resolve their specific situation
- Describes their firm as too small for formal governance structures — simultaneously true and creating ungoverned exposure
- Distinguishes between AI they chose and AI that arrived through software updates, embedded features, and staff activation
- Uses carrier attestation language as shorthand for the acute accountability moment — "signed the carrier form" = the personal signature event that crystallized their exposure
