# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Boutique Law Firms Talk About AI Problems

**Phase:** C311 Phase 01 | **ICP:** Geneva Boutique, 2–5 Attorneys | **Date:** 2026-05-31

---

## Primary Frame: The Attestation Trap

The dominant entry point is the carrier attestation form. The managing partner signed it — either personally or by default — without understanding what it committed the firm to. The language they use reflects this:

> *"On a signé un truc pour l'assureur. Je ne sais pas exactement ce qu'on a certifié."*

("We signed something for the insurer. I don't know exactly what we certified.")

This is not a hypothetical. This is the modal state of Geneva boutique firms at Q2 2026.

---

## Language Register: What They Say vs. What They Mean

### When They Say "On a un système IA"
**They mean:** Someone on the team uses ChatGPT. Maybe. Sometimes. On their own device.

**Translation:** No documented AI use. No inventory. No oversight chain. The firm's AI surface is completely invisible to the managing partner.

**Why this matters:** The carrier form asked about AI systems. They checked a box. But if asked to enumerate actual AI use across their matters — they cannot. The attestation was signed against an unknown inventory.

---

### When They Say "C'est nos avocats qui utilisent ça, pas nous"
**They mean:** The junior associates use AI tools. The managing partner does not.

**Translation:** Knowledge asymmetry. The person with the least AI literacy (the managing partner, 45–65) is the one who signed the attestation. The person with the most AI fluency (junior associate, 25–35) is the one generating the firm's actual AI exposure.

**Why this matters:** The liability sits with the signer. The risk sits with the user. There is no bridge between these two realities.

---

### When They Say "On n'a pas de budget pour ça"
**They mean:** They believe AI governance is a large-firm problem. They are the 3-attorney firm on Rue de Rive that cannot afford a CISO, a compliance department, or a formal framework.

**Translation:** They have self-selected out of the AI governance conversation. They believe it is not for them.

**Why this matters:** This is a window. The framing they respond to is not "enterprise compliance" — it is "what does the attestation actually require, and what is the minimum defensible answer for a 3-attorney firm in Geneva."

---

### When They Say "Le barreau n'a rien dit"
**They mean:** No official guidance from OaF (Ordre des Avocats de Genève). No circular. No directive. No FAQ. Nothing.

**Translation:** They are waiting for the bar to tell them what to do. The bar has not spoken. They interpret this as permission to wait.

**Why this matters:** The bar guidance vacuum is not a neutral condition — it is an active liability. When the bar finally speaks (and it will), retroactive reconstruction will be impossible. The firms that prepared proactively will have documentation. The ones who waited will have nothing.

---

### When They Say "Notre étude est correcte"
**They mean:** They believe their current practice is adequate. The word "adequate" comes directly from the carrier form — but no one defined it.

**Translation:** Adequate relative to what? There is no benchmark. No standard. No reference point. "Adequate" is a floating signifier that makes every firm feel safe while providing no actual protection.

**Why this matters:** The word "adequate" on the carrier form is the highest-value intervention point. It is where the managing partner's anxiety lives. It is where the conversation should start.

---

## Functional Vocabulary: Terms Geneva Boutiques Actually Use

| In French | In English | What They Signal |
|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| *attestation de l'assureur* | Carrier attestation | The triggering document; the thing they signed |
| *liste des outils IA* | AI tool inventory | What they don't have; what they need |
| *conformité* | Compliance | Vague, scary, large-firm word — but they know it matters |
| *responsabilité personnelle* | Personal liability | The thing that makes the managing partner pay attention |
| *usage документирован* | Documented use | They know they need it; they don't know how |
| *Junior qui utilise ChatGPT* | Junior using ChatGPT | The actual risk source they can identify |
| *le barreau n'a rien dit* | Bar said nothing | Permission structure for inaction |
| *priorité au client* | Client priority | Their legitimate reason for not having dealt with this yet |

---

## The Framing They Respond To

**They do not respond to:** "AI governance framework." "Enterprise compliance posture." "NIST alignment." "ISO 42001."

**They respond to:** "What does that form you signed actually require from you, specifically, as the signing managing partner of a 3-attorney firm in Geneva?"

**They respond to:** "Can you show your carrier what AI tools were used, by whom, on which matters, in the last 12 months? If the answer is no — you're not adequately prepared."

**They respond to:** "When the OaF finally issues guidance — and they will — which firms will be able to demonstrate they were already working in good faith? Those are the ones the carrier will treat differently."

---

## Key Insight for Phase 01

The ICP is not language-resistant. They are language-mismatched. The market speaks about AI governance in a register that excludes small Geneva firms — it sounds like large enterprise, large firm, big budget. The intervention required is a translation: reframe AI governance as a direct ответ на the attestation they already signed, in the register of a 3-attorney Geneva boutique where the managing partner is personally accountable and has no compliance staff.

## EN Version

### When They Say "We Use AI"
**They mean:** Someone on the team uses ChatGPT. Perhaps. On their own device. No documentation. No oversight. The managing partner has no visibility into the firm's actual AI surface.

**They respond to:** "Can you list every AI tool used across your matters in the past 12 months? If the answer is no — your attestation was signed against an unknown."

### When They Say "The Bar Has Said Nothing"
**They mean:** No guidance from OaF. No directive. They interpret silence as permission to wait.

**They respond to:** "The bar will speak. When they do, which firms will have documentation proving they acted in good faith — and which will have nothing?"

### When They Say "We Can't Afford This"
**They respond to:** Not a budget conversation — a carrier conversation. "What does your attestation actually require? What is the minimum defensible answer for your size and structure?"
