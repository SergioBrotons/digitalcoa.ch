# 01 — ICP Language: Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability
## Phase 04 C305 | Geneva Healthcare | Independent Physician / Small Group Practice (2–10 physicians)

---

## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

### Primary Language Register

The Geneva independent physician does not describe AI governance as a compliance exercise. They describe it through the vocabulary of **professional autonomy erosion and personal exposure**:

- *"Mon système m'a ajouté des fonctions IA sans me demander"* (My system added AI features without asking me)
- *"Je ne sais pas ce que mes outils font exactement — et je suis responsable comme si je le savais"* (I don't know exactly what my tools do — and I'm responsible as if I did)
- *"Mon assistant(e) utilise l'IA pour les comptes rendus — c'est sous ma responsabilité, mais je ne sais pas ce qu'elle fait avec"* (My assistant uses AI for reports — it's under my responsibility, but I don't know what they're doing with it)
- *"On a signé l'attestation pour garder la couverture — je n'avais pas le temps de vérifier tout ce qu'on certifiait"* (We signed the attestation to keep coverage — I didn't have time to verify everything we were certifying)
- *"Si je dois explicar ce que l'IA a fait dans ce dossier, je ne peux pas — ce n'est pas documenté"* (If I have to explain what AI did in this record, I can't — it's not documented)
- *"L'outil IA a recommandé quelque chose, le patient a suivi cette recommandation, et ensuite ça s'est mal passé — qu'est-ce que je prouve ?"* (The AI tool recommended something, the patient followed that recommendation, and then it went wrong — what do I prove?)

### Emotional Topology

The Geneva independent physician experiences AI accountability in this intensity sequence:

1. **Loss-of-control anxiety** — AI features activated without their decision, documented without their architecture, updated without their knowledge
2. **Delegation accountability confusion** — staff use AI tools under their supervision, they are accountable for decisions they did not make or review
3. **Attestation certification fear** — signed documents certifying governance adequacy for a surface they cannot enumerate
4. **Patient rights erosion unawareness** — FADP Art.21 obligations they are unknowingly violating daily
5. **Criminal exposure incomprehension** — personal liability under Art.63 FADP for data processing decisions made by vendors
6. **Post-incident evidence absence** — after any adverse outcome, inability to demonstrate what the AI contributed to the decision

### Specific Vocabulary: Where What They Say Diverges from What They Mean

| What They Say | What They Actually Mean |
|---|---|
| *"On a un bon système EHR"* | "The EHR vendor handles compliance" |
| *"Mon assistant(e) est formé(e)"* | "They know how to use the tools — which means they're responsible for using them correctly" |
| *"On a signé pour la continuité"* | "I certified to AI governance adequacy I cannot define, under time pressure, to keep coverage" |
| *"Je reste maître de la décision"* | "I believe my clinical judgment overrides AI input — but the record doesn't show the override moment" |
| *"Notre vendor est sérieux"* | "The vendor told us we're compliant — the vendor has a commercial interest in that answer" |
| *"On n'a pas de problème de données"* | "We haven't been caught — enforcement is something that happens to other people" |
| *"C'est proporportionné pour notre taille"* | "We don't have a documented approach — but we're a small practice so it should be simpler" |
| *"J'ai arrêté cet outil quand j'ai vu les rapports"* | "I stopped using the AI tool — but I have no documentation of why, what happened to the data, or what the decision was based on" |

### French-Language Specific Expressions (Geneva register)

- *"Ça m'est arrivé par-dessus la tête"* — AI features deployed to the practice without any decision event
- *"Je ne sais même pas tout ce qui est activé dans mon système"* — Complete enumeration of active AI features is impossible
- *"C'est ma signature, c'est ma responsabilité"* — The physician's name on the attestation form is the only thing connecting them to accountability
- *"On fait confiance au système"* — The practice has decided not to examine AI governance because examination would require confronting exposure
- *"Je ne suis pas assez grand pour avoir ce problème"* — The belief that small practice scale reduces accountability exposure (structurally incorrect)
- *"C'est la même chose que avant, juste avec plus de teknologi"* — The physician believes their core practice is unchanged despite AI integration
- *"Mon vendor m'a dit que c'était en ordre"* — The vendor provides the compliance answer the vendor needs the physician to believe

### Key Distinction: Delegation Vocabulary vs. Accountability Vocabulary

**Delegation vocabulary** (what they use): "My staff use AI," "The assistant handles documentation," "I delegate to the team"

**Accountability vocabulary** (what it actually means): "I am professionally responsible for decisions made by staff using AI tools I did not evaluate, under supervision structures I did not architect, for records that bear my professional authority"

The gap between delegation vocabulary and accountability vocabulary is where Geneva independent practice AI accountability exposure lives. The physician uses delegation language because that is the framework they have for understanding staff-assisted practice. The problem is that AI-assisted delegation is not equivalent to human-assisted delegation — the AI tool has its own decision logic that is not supervised in the way a human assistant's decisions are supervised.

### What Triggers Action vs. What Produces Paralysis

**Produces action:**
- Staff member mentions using a new AI tool — immediate, concrete, delegation-visible problem
- Adverse patient outcome where AI input was involved — post-incident clarity is too late but produces urgency
- Vendor announces AI feature update — software update as accountability expansion event
- Colleague admits they don't know what their AI tools do — peer circuit vulnerability signal
- Attestation form arrives with short deadline — creates signature pressure without decision clarity

**Produces paralysis:**
- "Proportionate" — term used without defining what proportionality means for 2-10 physician practice
- "Best practices" — enterprise-designed best practices produce intimidation rather than action
- "FMH guidance" — principle without operational endpoint generates no actionable path
- "Ask your vendor" — commercial interest guidance produces false confidence
- Multi-year regulatory timeline — "eventually something will be required" sounds deferrable

### The Three Unspeakable Problems

The Geneva independent physician holds three problems they cannot name in peer settings:

**Problem 1 (Delegation):** *"I don't know what my staff are doing with AI tools, and I am professionally responsible for it"*

**Problem 2 (Attestation):** *"I signed something certifying my AI governance is adequate, and I cannot define what that means or what my AI surface consists of"*

**Problem 3 (Evidence):** *"If a patient is harmed and the record is reviewed, I cannot demonstrate what the AI contributed to the decision — the review will look at my name on the record and assume the decision was mine alone"*

These three problems are structurally unspeakable because speaking them requires admitting to conditions that would be indefensible under current carrier attestation standards.

### Language Markers for Accountability Depth

**Surface-level concern** (early engagement): "I'm worried about AI"
**Mid-level concern** (active concern): "I don't know what my EHR is doing with AI"
**Deep concern** (acute exposure): "I signed an attestation for AI governance I cannot define, my staff use AI tools I didn't evaluate, and if something goes wrong I cannot show what the AI did"

The progression from surface to deep concern tracks the ICP's developing understanding of their exposure. Most engagement happens at surface level. The goal of any initial contact is to move the ICP one level deeper in accountability recognition.
