# Phase 02 Geneva Finance — Cycle 41: ICP Definition

**Cycle 41 — March 31, 2026**

---

## Target ICP: Geneva Boutique Wealth Managers

### Primary ICP
**Geneva wealth/asset management Boutiques (AUM CHF 50M-2B)**
- Independent WM firms (not bank-owned)
- 2-20 relationship managers
- Family office structures
- UHNW/HNW client base
- Geneva-based operations

### Decision-Makers
- Managing partners / founders
- Chief Investment Officers
- Compliance officers (often external/outsourced at boutique level)

### Characteristics
- High AI adoption intent (74% plan to incorporate in 2026)
- Low formalization (only 17% in deployment phase; 40% ideation/pilot)
- Shadow AI acute: RMs using personal AI tools for client research, reporting
- FINMA Circ. 08/2024 obligations already applicable
- FATF evaluation pressure 2026-2027/2028

### Psychographic Profile
- Aware AI is necessary for competitiveness
- Concerned about FINMA/FATF compliance exposure
- Uncertain how to formalize AI governance
- Values Swiss/EU data sovereignty (client confidentiality)

### Pain Points
- No clear framework for WM-specific AI governance
- CARF/CRS 2.0 already live (Jan 1 2026)
- FINMA Circ. 08/2024 obligations not fully addressed
- Agentic AI (78% exploring) introduces new risk
- FATF evaluation creates external pressure

### Buying Triggers
- FATF mutual evaluation preparation
- FINMA examination findings
- Peer WM firms receiving FINMA inquiries
- May 2026 AI Action Plan First Draft (external pressure)

---

## Secondary ICP
**Geneva family offices and independent asset managers**

### Characteristics
- Similar compliance exposure as boutique WMs
- Potentially less sophisticated AI governance
- High client confidentiality requirements
- Swiss/EU nexus (FATF + FINMA + FADP)

---

## ICP Size Estimate
- Geneva boutique WMs: ~50-100 firms
- Geneva family offices: ~30-50
- Switzerland UHNW/HNW WM market: ~200-400 addressable firms
- Market segmentation: ~1,000-2,000 addressable Swiss WM firms total
