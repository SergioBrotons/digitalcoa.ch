# Phase 02 Geneva Finance — Lead Magnet: "The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist" — Cycle 41

**Geneva Boutique Wealth Managers — AI Governance Readiness Framework**

---

## C41 Lead Magnet Framing

### The Core Insight
**The WM sector has the widest governance gap in the portfolio.**

95% of wealth/asset managers have scaled AI.
Yet only 28% have formal firm-level AI policies.
And 74% plan to incorporate AI in 2026 — but only 17% have reached deployment phase.

74% plan AI adoption in 2026.
40% are still in ideation/pilot phases.
Only 17% have actually deployed.

**The service opportunity is not adoption — it is formalization.**

---

## The Shadow AI Problem (Decision 0)

**The most urgent WM AI governance issue is Shadow AI:**
- RMs using personal AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for client research
- AI-assisted portfolio analysis without firm-level approval
- Client reporting generated with AI without disclosure protocols
- Agentic AI (78% exploring) = autonomous actions without oversight

**FINMA Circ. 08/2024 requires**: AI tool inventory, risk classification, responsibility assignment, staff training.

**Most boutique WMs have not completed this inventory.**

---

## 3-Layer Compliance Stack

**Layer 1 — FINMA Circ. 08/2024**
AI tool inventory + risk classification + responsibility assignment + staff training.

**Layer 2 — CARF / CRS 2.0**
CRS 2.0 LIVE Jan 1 2026 (e-money, CBDC, crypto derivatives).
CARF design constraints active; reporting postponed to 2027.
AI tools used for CRS/CARF compliance = FINMA Circ. 08/2024 scope.

**Layer 3 — FADP Art. 21**
Human review for AI-assisted financial decisions.
Portfolio recommendations, risk assessments, trading = Art. 21 applicability.
**FADP directly applicable to AI** (FDPIC May 2025 — no new law required).

## The 8-Section Framework

**Section 1: Shadow AI Audit (Decision 0)**
Identify all AI tools used by RMs and staff (approved and unapproved).
Risk: FINMA Circ. 08/2024 inventory gap + FADP Art. 21 + Art. 5(c) exposure.

**Section 2: FINMA Circ. 08/2024 Inventory**
Document all AI tools in operations.
Classify by risk level.
Assign responsibility.

**Section 3: CARF/CRS 2.0 Compliance**
Assess AI tools used for CRS/CARF reporting.
Ensure CARF-ready systems architecture.
TIN validation and due-diligence compliance.

**Section 4: FADP Art. 21 Human Review**
AI-assisted portfolio recommendations = must allow human review.
Document human oversight protocols.

**Section 5: FATF AI Governance Readiness**
Assess AI governance against FATF recommendations.
Document AI risk management framework.
Prepare FATF evaluation narrative.

**Section 6: Staff Training & Responsibility**
FINMA Circ. 08/2024 mandatory training.
AI usage policies for RMs and staff.
Agentic AI protocols (78% exploring = new risk category).

**Section 7: Agentic AI Risk**
78% of Swiss financial institutions exploring agentic AI.
Agentic AI = autonomous AI agents taking actions without human-in-the-loop.
New risk category requiring governance framework.

**Section 8: Client Disclosure & Transparency**
FADP Art. 5(c) = must disclose AI in client communications.
AI-generated reports, recommendations = disclosure required.
Client consent protocols for AI-assisted services.
