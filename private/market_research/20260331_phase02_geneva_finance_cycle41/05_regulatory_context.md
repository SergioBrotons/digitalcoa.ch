# Phase 02 Geneva Finance — Cycle 41: Regulatory Context

**Cycle 41 — March 31, 2026**

---

## Regulatory Architecture (3-Layer WM Compliance Stack)

### Layer 1: FINMA Circular 08/2024 (Primary Source)
**Status: Operative since December 2024**

- AI tool inventory mandatory
- Risk classification of AI tools required
- Responsibility assignment for AI systems
- Staff training mandatory
- Primary source: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (Dec 2024)

**Key obligation**: WM firms must document all AI tools used in operations, classify by risk level, assign clear responsibility, and train staff.

---

### Layer 2: CARF / CRS 2.0

**CRS 2.0 — LIVE since January 1, 2026**
- Expanded scope: e-money products, CBDCs, indirect crypto via derivatives
- Tighter due-diligence requirements
- TIN (Tax Identification Number) validation mandatory
- Real-time/reporting requirements tightened

**CARF — Design Constraints Active; Reporting Postponed to 2027**
- OECDCrypto Asset Reporting Framework (CARF)
- Swiss legislation (AEOIA + AEOI Ordinance) enters force Jan 1, 2026
- Systems must be CARF-ready by Jan 1, 2026
- Actual reporting postponed to 2027
- Design constraints = systems architecture must accommodate CARF

**WM Implication**: AI tools used for CRS/CARF compliance reporting may fall under FINMA Circ. 08/2024 obligations.

---

### Layer 3: FADP (Federal Act on Data Protection)
**Status: Operative since September 1, 2023 — 2.5+ years live**

**FDPIC confirmed (May 2025)**: FADP directly applicable to AI-supported data processing
- No new legislation required — existing obligations already operative
- FDPIC enforcement posture: guidance → enforcement when sector identified as priority

**Art. 21 FADP: Human Review**
- Automated decisions producing financial effects must allow human review
- AI-assisted portfolio recommendations, risk assessments, trading = Art. 21 applicability

**Art. 5(c) FADP: Transparency**
- Must disclose AI involvement in client communications
- AI-generated reports, recommendations, risk assessments = disclosure required

**Art. 16 FADP: Data Integrity**
- AI systems processing personal data must maintain integrity and security
- Client financial data confidentiality extends to AI tools

**DPIA**: Required for high-risk AI (portfolio analysis, risk assessment AI)

---

## FATF Evaluation — Primary Forcing Function

**Timeline**: Mutual evaluation 2026-2027/2028
- FATF (Financial Action Task Force) conducts peer reviews of member jurisdictions
- Switzerland expected evaluation window: 2026-2028
- Geneva FS sector = priority focus area
- AI governance gaps = FATF evaluation risk for Swiss financial center

**WM Implication**: FATF evaluation creates external pressure for WM firms to demonstrate AI governance readiness.

---

## Secondary: EU AI Act (Extraterritorial)

**GPAI provisions operative since August 2, 2025**
- WM firms with EU nexus (EU clients, EU operations) = EU AI Act applicable
- Annex III high-risk: AI for creditworthiness, insurance risk assessment
- Transparency + human oversight for high-risk AI
- Dual compliance: FINMA + EU AI Act for EU-nexus WM firms
