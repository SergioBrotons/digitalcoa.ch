## ⭐ NET-NEW FINDINGS (Cycle 62 vs. Cycle 61)

### 1. SBA Explicitly Rejects EU AI Act Approach — Supports Federal Council Technology-Neutral Framework
**Source:** Swiss Bankers Association (SBA), AI and Big Data section
- SBA explicitly: "The European Union has created a comprehensive regulatory framework with its AI Act and set about implementing it in stages"
- SBA explicitly: "The Federal Council has opted not to follow this example. Instead, it favours a targeted, technology-neutral and principles-based approach"
- SBA supports "carefully thought-out amendments to existing legislation" rather than new AI law
- **Implication for Geneva WM:** SBA pushing for lighter regulatory burden — but firms with EU clients still face EU AI Act GPAI obligations; the SBA approach doesn't protect against extraterritorial EU AI Act scope

### 2. Swiss Banks Currently Using AI Primarily for Compliance (AML/Fraud) — Not Client Advisory
**Source:** SBA AI and Big Data page
- Current AI applications: transaction monitoring (anti-ML, fraud prevention), customer identification, process automation, knowledge base querying
- **Gap:** Client-facing AI advisory tools and AI-driven portfolio management are not listed as current primary uses
- **Implication:** Geneva WMs still in early stages of AI client advisory; August 2, 2026 GPAI deadline may catch firms unprepared if they adopt AI portfolio tools quickly

### 3. SBA Named Richard Hess (Head of Digital Finance) Among Switzerland's Top 100 Digital Shapers 2026
**Source:** Swiss Bankers Association news, March 26, 2026
- SBA actively recognized for digital innovation leadership
- SBA's digital finance team actively shaping Swiss banking AI trajectory
- **Implication:** SBA as key stakeholder in Federal Council AI policy — their influence pushes toward lighter regulation

### 4. Swiss-EU Bilateral Package — Finance Sector Specific Provisions
**Source:** admin.ch Federal Council April 1, 2026 press release
- Package includes: electricity, health, food safety agreements
- No AI-specific provisions in bilateral package
- No specific financial services AI harmonization
- **Implication:** Swiss WMs continue to face compound compliance burden — FADP + FINMA principles + EU AI Act (for EU-facing activities) + potential future Swiss rules

### 5. Section 301 Forced Labor — ESG Documentation Demand for WM Supply Chain Clients
**Source:** USTR / HK Law / SBA Sustainable Finance
- Forced labor investigation: hearings April 28, 2026 (~26 days)
- Swiss WMs with ESG mandates face forced labor supply chain exposure
- SBA self-regulation on sustainable finance covers ESG factors but focused on climate/Paris Agreement
- **Implication:** New SMB consulting product angle confirmed (from Phase 01 C62) — supply chain forced labor documentation for WM ESG clients
