## Competitive Landscape Overview

The Geneva consulting boutique AI governance advisory market is nascent and fragmented. The primary competitive dynamics are:

1. **Large advisory firms** (Big4, major strategy consultancies) — targeting larger clients, priced for enterprise, not proportionate to boutique
2. **Legal tech platforms** — offering AI governance tools for law firms, not general consulting, different risk profile
3. **Insurance brokers** — flagging AI governance as a coverage issue, not providing methodology advisory services
4. **Generic AI policy template providers** — one-size-fits-all templates that don't address boutique risk profiles
5. **Regulatory consultants** — focused on large-institution compliance, not small boutique operations

None of these competitors are specifically serving the Geneva consulting boutique ICP with proportionate, sequenced, commercially urgent, bilingual advisory services.

## What Exists (C308 Phase 03 Assessment)

### Large Advisory Firms (Big 4, Major Strategy Consultancies)

**Positioning:** Comprehensive AI governance frameworks, multi-jurisdiction compliance, enterprise-grade documentation systems.

**Strengths:** Brand credibility, regulatory relationships, comprehensive coverage, established methodologies.

**Weaknesses for Geneva Consulting Boutique ICP:**
- Price point (CHF 15,000–50,000+ for initial engagement) — not proportionate to boutique scale
- Institutional orientation — not designed for 2–50 person firms
- Not Geneva-specific — no understanding of Geneva commercial dynamics, client base, peer networks
- Not bilingual in practice — EN/FR delivery without operational fluency
- Not proportionate — solutions designed for compliance departments, not managing partners who are also the compliance function

**Gap:** No established offering specifically for 2–50 person Geneva consulting boutiques at proportionate price with Geneva-specific market knowledge.

---

### Legal Tech Platforms (Clio, Iron Mountain, legal-specific AI governance tools)

**Positioning:** AI governance tooling for law firms, practice management, document management with attorney-client privilege considerations.

**Strengths:** Practice management integration, privilege considerations built in, law firm-specific workflow understanding.

**Weaknesses for Geneva Consulting Boutique ICP:**
- Oriented toward law firms — different client base, different risk profile, different professional obligations
- Not applicable to general consulting boutiques (non-legal)
- Different regulatory overlay (legal professional obligations vs. consulting commercial obligations)
- Pricing and complexity oriented toward larger legal practices

**Gap:** Legal tech platforms are not designed for, and are not marketing to, general consulting boutiques. This is not a competitive threat — it is a distinct market segment.

---

### Insurance Brokers (Large Brokers)

**Positioning:** AI governance as insurance risk factor, carrier attestation guidance, coverage gap identification.

**Strengths:** Access to carrier attestation forms, understanding of coverage gaps, broker-client relationship with managing partner trust.

**Weaknesses for Geneva Consulting Boutique ICP:**
- Not advisory in the methodology sense — flag problems but don't resolve them
- Broker liability concerns limit how much specific guidance they provide
- Do not produce the documentation that closes the gap between attestation and actual practice
- Do not address client RFP AI gates, proposal methodology defensibility, or sub-consultant delegation
- Do not provide the operational sequencing that the Phase 03 ICP needs

**Gap:** No structured advisory service to help boutiques actually complete carrier attestations accurately and maintain evidence architecture for renewal. Brokers are referral sources, not competitors in the methodology advisory space.

---

### Generic AI Policy Template Providers

**Positioning:** Off-the-shelf AI governance policies, ISO 42001 alignment, AI usage policies, downloadable templates.

**Strengths:** Low cost, fast delivery, broad coverage, accessible to non-experts.

**Weaknesses for Geneva Consulting Boutique ICP:**
- Templates designed for larger organizations — not proportionate
- Do not address sub-consultant delegation, carrier attestation accuracy, client RFP gates, or personal liability
- Do not provide the operational sequencing the Phase 03 ICP needs
- Do not produce evidence in the format carriers, clients, and courts actually require
- No Geneva-specific market knowledge, no bilingual delivery

**Gap:** The template market is not a direct competitor to methodology advisory — it serves a different buyer need (cheap, fast, self-service) that the Phase 03 ICP has already evaluated and found inadequate.

---

### Regulatory Consultants (Compliance Boutiques)

**Positioning:** Regulatory compliance advisory, nFADP/GDPR compliance, EU AI Act preparation, sector-specific regulatory guidance.

**Strengths:** Regulatory expertise, documentation frameworks, compliance methodology.

**Weaknesses for Geneva Consulting Boutique ICP:**
- Not consulting-sector-specific — generalist regulatory consultants do not understand consulting boutique commercial dynamics
- Not Geneva-focused — no understanding of Geneva client base, peer networks, commercial pressures
- Not proportionate to small boutique operations — scope and pricing oriented toward larger clients
- Sales cycle too long for boutique urgency — managing partners need action in days, not weeks
- Do not address the commercial gate pressure (client RFPs, carrier attestation) — focused on regulatory compliance only

**Gap:** No regulatory consultant offering specifically designed for small Geneva consulting boutique immediate needs (client questionnaire response, carrier attestation accuracy, proposal methodology defensibility).

## What's Missing (C308 Phase 03 White Spaces)

### White Space 1: Client RFP AI Response Service (Pre-Pitch Intervention)

**What it is:** A structured service to help Geneva consulting boutiques respond to client AI governance questionnaires embedded in RFPs. Not a generic policy — a client-specific response document that documents the boutique's actual practice against the client's specific criteria.

**Why it exists:** Client RFP AI gates are a commercial consequence, not a regulatory requirement. The value is winning the mandate, not just achieving compliance. No competitor offers a pre-pitch intervention that produces client-specific AI governance evidence.

**Service design:** 3–5 day sprint. Review client's RFP criteria. Document boutique's actual AI practice. Produce response document. Review for accuracy and defensibility. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction:** Pre-pitch intervention, not post-problem remediation. The value is commercial — winning the mandate — not just compliant.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 2,200–3,500 for bounded sprint engagement.

---

### White Space 2: Carrier Attestation Accuracy Review (Renewal-Specific)

**What it is:** A structured review of what a managing partner actually certified when signing the carrier attestation, compared against actual practice. Produces a gap analysis, a corrected attestation narrative, and a documentation improvement plan. Specifically designed for renewal context.

**Why it exists:** Carrier attestation renewal forms are more specific than initial attestation forms. The ICP who is more informed at renewal is paradoxically more exposed if the attestation is inaccurate. No competitor offers a renewal-specific attestation accuracy review.

**Service design:** 2–3 day review engagement. Document current AI practice. Compare against carrier attestation language. Identify misrepresentation risk. Produce corrected documentation and attestation narrative. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction:** The attestation accuracy review is distinct from general AI governance advisory — it is specifically about the document the managing partner signed and the personal exposure it creates at renewal.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 2,200–3,500 for bounded review engagement.

---

### White Space 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework (Boutique-Proportionate)

**What it is:** A lightweight documentation framework specifically designed for Geneva consulting boutiques with 1–3 sub-consultants. Addresses the accountability chain from managing partner to sub-consultant AI use. Produces a delegation register, a use log, and a client disclosure protocol.

**Why it exists:** Enterprise AI governance platforms are not designed for sub-consultant delegation scenarios. Legal tech platforms are for law firms. The boutique needs a proportionate framework that fits their operational reality. No competitor offers a sub-consultant AI delegation framework specifically for small Geneva consulting boutiques.

**Service design:** 1-day workshop + documentation production. Map sub-consultant relationships. Document AI tool use per engagement. Create delegation register template. Produce client disclosure protocol. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction:** Structurally distinct risk that larger advisory firms don't address at boutique scale. The sub-consultant accountability gap is invisible until it becomes a client-facing problem — the service prevents the problem, not just documents it.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 1,650–2,200 for workshop + documentation package.

---

### White Space 4: Proposal Methodology Defensibility Package (Professional Liability Focus)

**What it is:** A structured documentation package that makes proposal methodology defensible under professional liability standards. Addresses both disclosure requirements and traceability requirements. Produces a methodology documentation template, a disclosure checklist, and a defensibility review process.

**Why it exists:** Generic AI governance frameworks don't address proposal methodology specifically. The professional liability exposure from AI-assisted recommendations is not addressed by templates designed for AI tool deployment — it requires a methodology documentation practice.

**Service design:** 2-day engagement. Review current proposal methodology. Document AI-assisted components. Produce methodology documentation template. Create defensibility review checklist. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction:** No competitor offers a proposal methodology defensibility service specifically for consulting boutiques. The service is distinct from general AI governance advisory because it addresses the proposal document and the professional liability exposure it creates.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 2,200–3,300 for bounded engagement.

---

### White Space 5: Personal nFADP Article 52 Exposure Assessment (Managing Partner Focus)

**What it is:** A structured assessment of managing partner personal exposure under nFADP Article 52. Produces a personal liability exposure report, a documentation adequacy assessment, and a personal asset protection strategy. Distinct from corporate compliance assessment.

**Why it exists:** Most nFADP advisory services are oriented toward corporate compliance officers, not managing partners with personal liability exposure. The personal asset dimension is distinct and requires separate advisory attention. Brokers are beginning to raise this question explicitly — but don't provide the assessment.

**Service design:** 1-day assessment engagement. Review current AI practice. Assess personal liability exposure under nFADP Article 52. Review insurance coverage for personal fines. Produce personal exposure report and documentation strategy. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction:** No competitor offers a personal nFADP Article 52 exposure assessment specifically for managing partners of small consulting boutiques. This is distinct from corporate compliance advisory because the liability is personal, not institutional.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 2,200–3,500 for assessment engagement.

---

### White Space 6: Proportionate AI Governance Retainer for Geneva Consulting Boutiques

**What it is:** An ongoing advisory retainer designed specifically for small Geneva consulting boutiques. Provides proportionate, practical AI governance support on an as-needed basis. Bilingual EN/FR. Geneva-based. Operationally concrete, not abstractly strategic.

**Why it exists:** The retainer model for AI governance advisory doesn't exist at the small boutique tier. Large advisory firms offer retainers for large clients. Legal tech platforms offer subscriptions for law firms. But a proportionate, Geneva-based, bilingual retainer for small consulting boutiques — covering client questionnaire response, carrier attestation accuracy, sub-consultant delegation, and methodology defensibility — is not available.

**Service design:** Monthly retainer, 4–6 hours/month. Priority access. Bilingual EN/FR. Geneva-based. Covers all Phase 03 service components on an ongoing basis.

**Competitive distinction:** No competitor is offering a proportionate monthly retainer for small Geneva consulting boutiques. The retainer model creates durable revenue and positions the advisor as the ongoing AI governance partner for this ICP.

**Pricing signal:** CHF 1,500–2,500/month.

## Structural Moats (C308 Phase 03 Update)

### Moat 1: Geneva-Specific Market Knowledge
Understanding of Geneva consulting boutique operations, client base, peer networks, and commercial dynamics — including the specific sequencing challenges that Geneva managing partners face and the specific commercial consequences of failing to respond to client AI governance criteria.

### Moat 2: Bilingual EN/FR Capability
Client communications in both languages without translation overhead. Not translation — native delivery in both languages. Critical for Geneva market where English and French are both operational languages for institutional clients.

### Moat 3: Proportionate Service Design
Services designed for 2–50 person firms — not scaled-down enterprise frameworks. The operational reality of a 5-person boutique where the managing partner is also the compliance function is fundamentally different from a 50-person firm with a compliance department.

### Moat 4: Personal Liability Focus
Managing partner personal exposure addressed directly — not buried in corporate compliance frameworks. The "me personally" framing is the activation trigger for this ICP. Services that treat personal liability as a secondary concern do not resonate.

### Moat 5: Sequenced Entry Logic
The service sequence (client RFP → carrier attestation → methodology defensibility → sub-consultant delegation → personal liability) is itself a competitive asset. It demonstrates understanding of the ICP's operational reality and provides a credible answer to the "what do I do first" question.

### Moat 6: Pre-Pitch Intervention Positioning
The client RFP AI response service is a pre-pitch intervention — the value is commercial (winning the mandate), not just compliant. No competitor is positioned as the pre-pitch AI governance partner for Geneva consulting boutiques.

## Competitive Vulnerability

### Primary Vulnerability
Large advisory firms entering the boutique tier with lower-priced standardized offerings — particularly if the consulting boutique market becomes more visible through regulatory pressure or broker referrals.

### Mitigation
- Geneva-specific market knowledge: requires sustained presence in the Geneva market, not just a product localization
- Bilingual capability: requires operational fluency, not just translated content
- Proportionate service design: requires service architecture designed from scratch for boutique scale, not scaled-down enterprise product
- Personal liability focus: requires understanding of managing partner psychology and personal asset exposure
- Sequenced entry logic: requires understanding of ICP operational reality and commercial consequence hierarchy

## C308 Phase 03 Competitive Positioning Statement

**The market has frameworks. It needs evidence in the format that clients, carriers, and courts actually require.**

The Geneva consulting boutique faces four simultaneous pressures:
1. Client RFP AI scoring criteria (commercial gate)
2. Carrier attestation accuracy (insurance consequence)
3. Proposal methodology defensibility (professional liability)
4. Personal nFADP Article 52 exposure (personal asset protection)

These four pressures require one coherent evidence architecture — not four separate documentation exercises.

The service model:
- Entry: Vendor Contract Review (CHF 1,100, bounded, specific)
- Activation: Carrier Attestation Accuracy Review (CHF 2,200–3,500)
- Core: Client RFP AI Response Sprint (CHF 2,200–3,500)
- Ongoing: Proportionate AI Governance Retainer (CHF 1,500–2,500/month)

*One evidence architecture. Four commercial and regulatory overlays. Not four products.*
