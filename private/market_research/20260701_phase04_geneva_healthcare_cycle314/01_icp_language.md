## Overview

The Geneva independent médecin (médecine interne générale, cardiologie, oncologie, psychiatrie-psychothérapie, dermatologie, endocrinologie, gynécologie-obstétrique, pédiatrie, orthopédie, ophtalmologie, ORL, gastro-entérologie, neurologie, rhumatologie, pneumologie, etc.) — privately installed in a single-physician cabinet or small Geneva clinic, personally accountable under nFADP Art.63, Medical Liability Carrier attestation, Convention de remplacement obligations, HIN/EMR residency, FMH professional governance, and now EU AI Act Article 3 deployer status — has crossed the C313 threshold ("I signed the attestation. The encounter note was written by the AI scribe.") into the C314 P04 pre-EU AI Act deadline language. The 30-day window before the EU AI Act late August 2026 applicability has applied the consulting-style named-individual and the finance-style four-regime framings into a healthcare layer where the Swiss Criminal Code Article 321 medical secrecy layer creates an additional regulatory collision the legal and finance verticals do not face.

The Geneva médecin has stopped talking about the AI scribe feature ("does it save time?", "is the transcription accurate?"). The médecin has started talking about the EU AI Act question that arrived through a French-border EU patient, an EU-cross-border care provider, a Swiss private insurer EU subsidiary, or a June 2026 SSMIG/AMG/FMH disclosure of a named peer Geneva physician's EU AI Act encounter-level readiness.

The C314 P04 language is short, time-stamped, and references a named patient, a named AI scribe, a named médecin remplaçant, a named EMR extension, and a named convention de remplacement. It is not "I need an AI governance framework." It is "the EU patient just asked, by name, who is responsible for the AI that wrote the encounter note. The deadline is in 30 days. The answer is me. And the encounter note is in the patient record under Article 321 CP."

The C314 P04 ICP language is structurally distinct from C314 P01 (legal — "T-minus 30 days. I don't know if I'm in scope."), C314 P02 (finance — "My institutional client just asked for EU AI Act readiness..."), and C314 P03 (consulting — "My EU client just asked who is responsible for the AI use on this engagement, by name..."). The Genève médecin's C314 P04 language has four healthcare-specific dimensions that the other three verticals do not share:

1. **The encounter note as the unit of accountability.** The médecin does not reference "the firm" or "the engagement" — the médecin references the encounter note. The encounter note is the deliverable. The encounter note is the medical record. The encounter note is the Article 321 CP medical secrecy artifact. The EU AI Act deployer status attaches to the named médecin by named encounter.
2. **Article 321 CP medical secrecy as the structural collision point.** Article 321 of the Swiss Criminal Code makes patient medical secrecy a criminal-law obligation of the named physician personally. EU AI Act Article 12 (logging) and Article 10 (data governance) require system-level visibility into the data that would breach Article 321 CP. The Swiss/EU collision is uniquely acute in healthcare — legal, finance, and consulting deal with confidentiality and secrecy, not criminal-law medical secrecy. The médecin sits between Article 321 CP criminal prosecution (CH-side) and EU AI Act enforcement (EU-side) and cannot reconcile them with internal compliance language.
3. **Le patient as the named natural person.** EU AI Act Article 50 requires informing natural persons they are interacting with AI. For the médecin, the natural person is the patient. The patient signs a consent form for AI scribe use. The patient must be informed at the encounter. The patient can revoke. The consent form is the operational mechanism. The 2024 consent form is missing the AI scribe disclosure. The 2024 consent form is missing the Article 50 EU AI Act framing. The consentement éclairé is now an EU AI Act Article 50 instrument, not a static GDPR-era consent form.
4. **Le médecin remplaçant as the named sub-physician in the AI authorization chain.** Article 4 of the EU AI Act extends AI literacy obligations to any person handling AI on the firm's behalf. The médecin remplaçant is such a person. The Convention de remplacement authorizes practice coverage — it does not authorize AI tool use. The remplaçant uses the practice's AI scribe on the remplaçant's encounters. The replaced médecin's medical liability carrier does not cover the remplaçant's encounters. The replaced médecin's nFADP Art.63 personal exposure extends to the remplaçant's encounters. The replaced médecin's EU AI Act Article 26 deployer obligations extend to the remplaçant's AI use. The handover window is structural — every vacation, every conference, every illness creates one. The Convention de remplacement does not authorize AI. The autorisation is an EU AI Act Article 4 + Article 26 gap.

### Tone Markers

- **Time pressure dominates.** Phrases like "30 days," "end of August," "next mid-year check-in," "next vacation period," "next EU patient," "next SSMIG/AMG disclosure cycle" appear in nearly every statement.
- **Personal accountability is named.** The médecin uses "I," "mon cabinet," "ma patient," "mon AI scribe," "mon remplaçant," "ma convention," "mon attestation" — never "we" or "the practice." The named physician owns the question in the first person.
- **The encounter note is the unit.** The médecin does not reference "the firm" or "the cabinet" or "the practice" — they reference the encounter note, the dossier patient, the consultation, the encounter. The encounter is the medical record under Article 321 CP. The encounter is the EU AI Act deployer applicability unit. The encounter is the carrier attestation evidence unit. The encounter is the named operation the médecin cannot delegate.
- **Article 321 CP and EU AI Act are named together.** The médecin references Article 321 CP and EU AI Act Article 12 in the same breath. The Swiss criminal law layer and the EU regulatory layer are not separate conversations — they collide on the same encounter note.
- **Cross-regime convergence is recognized.** The médecin references Article 321 CP, nFADP Art.63, Medical Liability Carrier attestation, Convention de remplacement, FMH/AMG professional governance, and EU AI Act in the same sentence. The médecin names seven regimes. The médecin is one.
- **The patient is named, by name.** "My patient," "the dossier patient of Mme/M. X," "the consent of this patient" — the patient is the named natural person under Article 50. The patient is the named data subject under nFADP. The patient is the named protected person under Article 321 CP.

- **La pression temporelle domine.** « 30 jours », « fin août », « prochain check-up de mi-année », « prochaine période de vacances », « prochain patient UE », « prochain cycle de divulgation SSMIG/AMG ».
- **La responsabilité personnelle est nommée.** Le médecin utilise « je », « mon cabinet », « ma patient », « mon scribe IA », « mon remplaçant », « ma convention », « mon attestation » — jamais « nous » ou « le cabinet ».
- **La note de consultation est l'unité.** Le médecin ne fait pas référence à « le cabinet » ou « la pratique » — il fait référence à la note de consultation, le dossier patient, la consultation, la rencontre. La consultation est le dossier médical sous l'article 321 CP. La consultation est l'unité d'applicabilité du déployeur du règlement IA. La consultation est l'unité de preuve de l'attestation d'assureur.
- **Article 321 CP et règlement IA sont nommés ensemble.** Le médecin fait référence à l'article 321 CP et à l'article 12 du règlement IA dans la même respiration. La couche de droit pénal suisse et la couche réglementaire UE ne sont pas des conversations séparées — elles entrent en collision sur la même note de consultation.
- **La convergence inter-régimes est reconnue.** Article 321 CP, article 63 nFADP, attestation d'assureur de responsabilité médicale, convention de remplacement, gouvernance professionnelle FMH/AMG, et règlement IA — le médecin nomme sept régimes. Le médecin est un.
- **Le patient est nommé, par son nom.** « Mon patient », « le dossier patient de Mme/M. X », « le consentement de ce patient » — le patient est la personne physique nommée sous l'article 50. Le patient est la personne nommée concernée par les données sous nFADP. Le patient est la personne protégée nommée sous l'article 321 CP.
