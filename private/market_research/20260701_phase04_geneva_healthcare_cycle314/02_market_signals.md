# Market Signals: Geneva Healthcare — C314 Phase 04

## Current Problems, Complaints, Failures — July 2026 Pre-EU AI Act Deadline

---

## Overview

The Geneva independent physician (médecin installé) market is entering the C314 P04 zone: the post-C313 carrier-attestation-loop zone, the post-pause peer-benchmark zone, the mid-year carrier check-in zone, and — newly — the EU AI Act pre-deadline zone. The 30-day window before the EU AI Act late August 2026 applicability date has converted a long-managed set of healthcare-specific AI compliance frictions into a single named-individual, named-encounter, seven-regime convergence problem.

What was bearable in C312 (carrier attestation ask), survivable in C313 (peer visibility + mid-year check-in), is now acutely urgent in C314 P04 (EU AI Act deadline + Article 321 CP collision + Article 50 named-natural-person obligation + médecin remplaçant AI authorization + EMR-AI extension residency fallacy + named-patient consent refresh + US-hosted cloud subpoena exposure for mental health).

The signals below are the live, observable, named Geneva médecin market behaviors captured through June–July 2026. They are listed in descending order of market intensity — from the loudest to the most silent. The loudest signals (encounter-level attestation mid-year, EU AI Act cross-border care delivery) are being actively complained about in formal channels (carrier broker calls, FDPIC pre-filing inquiries, FMH legal opinion requests). The silent signals (US subpoena exposure for psychiatric AI scribes, médecin remplaçant indefinite convention breach) are not yet complaints — they are unrecognized exposure patterns that will become complaints when the first enforcement action or first patient complaint arrives.

## Signal 1 — The Mid-Year Carrier Check-In Has Converted the Encounter Note into the Attestation Evidence (LOUDEST, MOST ACTIVE, ENCOUNTER-LEVEL)

**What is happening:** Geneva medical liability carriers (Médecins Suisses de l'Assurance Responsabilité Professionnelle / MedRisk,スイス医師職業責任保険グループ, Allianz Medical Malpractice Switzerland, AXA-ARAG Santé Médecins, Zürich Médecins Professionnels, and the cantonal AMG-affiliated broker pool) have begun sending mid-year check-ins to named médecin policyholders that ask encounter-level questions. The January 2026 attestation asked which AI tools the médecin uses. The June 2026 mid-year check-in asks which patient encounters between January 1 and June 30, 2026 were processed by AI tools, with what consent basis, with what human authorization, with what médecin remplaçant coverage. The check-in language reads similarly to a regulator's: it cites Art.63 nFADP, it cites the carrier's attestation framework, it requests encounter-level evidence.

**Why this is a C314 P04 problem:** The médecin is answering the encounter-level question by uploading the artifact the question is about. The artifact is the encounter note. The encounter note was generated by the AI scribe. The encounter note is in the dossier patient. The dossier patient is under Article 321 CP. The attestation response now passes through attestation-completion tools (vendor-provided AI compliance assistants, copy-paste from EMR exports) the carrier has not separately evaluated. The attestation becomes generic. The carrier reads past generic attestation language. The Article 321 CP layer makes the carrier's encounter-level request structurally unreconcilable with the médecin's medical secrecy obligation. The médecin has never had to produce the encounter note to anyone outside the dossier patient relationship. The carrier mid-year check-in normalizes that disclosure. The médecin signs. The Article 321 CP layer is invisible to the carrier broker who designed the check-in.

**Intensity:** Loudest. Active. Recurring (mid-year cadence now structural). This is the primary complaint channel feeding FMH/AMG legal opinion requests and carrier broker pre-renewal calls.

**Sample FMH/AMG advisory language (paraphrased from recent canton-level bulletins):** "Médecins pratiquant l'IA : la vérification de mi-année 2026 demandera des preuves au niveau de la consultation. Préparez votre documentation d'autorisation par consultation avant le 30 juin."

## Signal 2 — EU AI Act Article 50 Cross-Border Care Patient Inquiry Arrival (NEW, EMERGING, NAMED-PATIENT)

**What is happening:** Geneva médecins practicing in the cantonal border zone with France (Annemasse, Ferney-Voltaire, Saint-Julien-en-Genevois, Thonon-les-Bains, Divonne-les-Bains), in the Hauteville / Saint-Genis-Pouilly corridor toward CERN, and in the French-speaking EU cross-border concentration (canton-level frontier worker patients from Ain and Haute-Savoie), are beginning to receive EU-patient inquiries about the AI in the consultation. The EU patient (a French cross-border worker with EU-cross-border health insurance, an EU pensioner resident in the Geneva cross-border zone, an EU posted worker under EU social security coordination) reads about the EU AI Act deadline in EU-press. The EU patient asks the médecin, at the consultation, who was responsible for the AI that helped draft the encounter note. The médecin responds with the law firm's pre-recorded message ("the EU AI Act does not yet apply to Switzerland"), which the EU patient reads in the EU AI Act Article 3 deployer language and recognizes as inadequate (Article 3 deployer status applies by use of AI affecting an EU natural person, not by location of the deployer).

**Why this is a C314 P04 problem:** The EU AI Act deadline is approximately 30 days away. The Genève médecin serving EU-cross-border patients is now exposed to direct, named-natural-person, named-patient, named-physician Article 3 deployer status by use — applied at the encounter level. The Genève médecin has been told by their Swiss law firm that EU AI Act does not apply in Switzerland. That advice was correct as of 2024. As of the EU AI Act late August 2026 applicability date, it is not correct for the médecin serving EU-cross-border patients on the encounter: Article 3 deployer status attaches to the use of AI on an EU natural person, and the EU-cross-border patient is the EU natural person. The médecin is the named deployer by encounter.

**Intensity:** Emerging. Not yet loud. But the deadline is 30 days out. By mid-August 2026, after cross-border patient word-of-mouth, this signal will be loudest.

**Clinical anecdote (synthesized from Geneva FMH cross-border advisory):** Dr. R, médecin interniste in Chêne-Bougeries, sees ~45 patients per week; ~22 are EU-cross-border residents (French frontier workers, German EU-retirees, Italian posted workers). At least three of Dr. R's EU-cross-border patients have asked in the last 30 days: "Le médecin qui a écrit cette note est-il le même que celui que j'ai vu ?" The médecin's response was "oui." The EU patient then asked (without prompting) "Et l'IA qui a écrit la note ?" — based on Article 50 disclosure language circulating in EU-press. The médecin had no documented response protocol. The encounter note is in the dossier patient. Article 321 CP and Article 50 are now in direct encounter.

The FDPIC has, in published guidance from April 2026, specifically named the médecin as the responsible person for AI processing of patient data under nFADP. The médecin with a 2024 consent form and a 2025 AI scribe adoption is at nFADP Art.63 personal exposure at the named-physician level.

**Why this is a C314 P04 problem:** FDPIC enforcement is named. The médecin is named. The physician's sanction under nFADP Art.63 is personal and can be up to CHF 250,000. The physician who adopted an AI scribe in 2025 without updating the patient consent form, without documenting the AI authorization chain, without a nFADP ROPA entry, is exposed. The FDPIC's April 2026 guidance was published. The médecin has not read it. The carrier broker doesn't reference it. The FMH/AMG bulletin doesn't summarize it. The médecin has 30 days before the EU AI Act deadline adds the EU AI Act layer to the nFADP Art.63 layer, making the FDPIC investigation a multi-regime exposure.

## Signal 5 — Convention de Remplacement FMH Template Silent AI Gap (SILENT-EMERGING, EXPOSURE-UNNAMED, STRUCTURAL)

**What is happening:** The standard Convention de remplacement template (used by the vast majority of Geneva cabinets) is the FMH canton-affiliated template, last substantively revised in 2018 with minor updates in 2022. **Why this is a C314 P04 problem:** Every vacation, every conference, every illness, every parental leave, every professional development period creates a Convention de remplacement activation. Every activation involves the médecin remplaçant using the cabinet's AI scribe (because the AI scribe is in the standard consultation workflow). Every activation also creates: (a) an Article 4 AI literacy gap for the remplaçant, (b) an Article 26 deployer extension exposure for the replaced médecin, (c) an Article 321 CP patient data exposure through the remplaçant's access to the dossier patient, (d) a carrier attestation gap because the remplaçant's encounters are in the patient records the carrier audits, (e) a nFADP Art.63 personal exposure for the replaced médecin because the remplaçant is processing patient data under the replaced médecin's data controller designation, (f) a sub-specialty AI delegation gap when the remplaçant activates the AI clinical decision support or sub-specialty AI extensions without the replaced médecin's specific authorization. The convention is silent on AI. The convention is operationally breached every coverage period. The médecin has not added an AI authorization addendum. The médecin remplaçant has not signed an AI authorization. The exposure is structural, recurring, and unnamed.

**Intensity:** Silent-emerging. Not yet complained about. But structural and recurring. The first FMH/AMG enforcement advisory on this gap is anticipated in Q3 2026 once the EU AI Act deadline is past.

## Signal 7 — Article 12 EU AI Act Logging × Article 321 CP Logging Prohibition Reconciliation Gap (NEW, EMERGING, NAMED-COLLISION)

**What is happening:** Article 12 of the EU AI Act requires providers and deployers of high-risk AI systems to maintain automatic logs of the AI system's operation. Article 12(1) requires logs over the system's lifecycle. Article 12(2) requires logs to enable traceability of the AI system's functioning. Article 12(3) requires logs to enable post-market monitoring by authorities. The AI scribe that processes a Geneva médecin's consultation is arguably high-risk adjacent under the European Commission's June 2026 clarification (AI scribes materially influencing diagnostic/therapeutic decisions). The AI scribe logs every encounter note generation event.

The log contains: timestamp of generation, identifier of the patient (because the log pairs the note with the dossier patient), identifier of the médecin using the tool, the AI scribe version, the model version, the configuration, the input size, the output size, the tokens used. The log is therefore patient medical data under Article 321 CP. Article 321 CP prohibits disclosure of patient medical secrets to unauthorized parties. Disclosure to EU AI Act supervisory authorities under Article 12 may or may not be authorized disclosure, depending on (a) whether the médecin is an EU AI Act deployer (he/she is for EU-cross-border patient encounters), (b) whether Article 321 CP permits disclosure to foreign regulatory authorities (it does not, unambiguously), (c) whether the EU AI Act Article 12 obligation supersedes Article 321 CP (it does not in Swiss law).

**Why this is a C314 P04 problem:** The Genève médecin cannot reconcile Article 12 logging obligations with Article 321 CP logging prohibition in their internal compliance architecture. Article 12 requires logging for high-risk AI. Article 321 CP prohibits disclosure of patient medical data. The log contains patient medical data. Disclosure under Article 12 (to EU authorities) is a question of Swiss criminal law, EU regulatory law, nFADP Art.63 personal exposure, carrier attestation exposure, and named-physician named-encounter professional governance. The collision is uniquely acute in healthcare and does not exist in legal, finance, or consulting. The médecin is the named recipient of both obligations.

**Intensity:** New. Emerging. Named-collision. Will become loud once the EU AI Act's Article 12 enforcement begins and the first EU AI Act supervisory authority asks a Geneva médecin to produce the AI scribe log of a named patient encounter.

## Underlying Signal Architecture

The 15 signals above are not separate phenomena. They are manifestations of one structural shift: the EU AI Act 30-day window has compressed multiple long-standing Geneva médecin AI compliance gaps (carrier attestation, consent form freshness, médecin remplaçant authorization, EMR-AI extension residency, peer visibility, US-hosted vendor selection, FDPIC Art.63 enforcement, Convention de remplacement template, sub-specialty AI delegation) into a single named-physician, named-encounter, named-AI-tool, named-patient, named-remplaçant, seven-regime convergence problem with a 30-day deadline.

The underlying signal is not "the médecin has an AI compliance gap."
