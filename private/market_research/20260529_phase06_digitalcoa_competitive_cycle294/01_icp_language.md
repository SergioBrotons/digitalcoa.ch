# Phase 06 — 01: ICP Language — How Vertical ICPs Talk About DigitalCoa.ch

**Research Date:** 2026-05-29
**Phase:** 06 — DigitalCoa.ch Competitive Intelligence
**Status:** COMPLETE
**Cycle:** C294

---

## Purpose

Map how each of the four C294 vertical ICPs — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — describes the problem that DigitalCoa.ch is positioned to solve. The language patterns are distinct by vertical. Effective positioning and lead magnet messaging must match each vertical's naming pattern, not impose a unified cross-vertical vocabulary.

---

## The Core Language Problem

Across all four verticals, the structural accountability gap is the same problem. But the vocabulary the ICP uses to name their situation — before they find DigitalCoa.ch — is vertical-specific.

This matters operationally: a lead magnet that uses the legal vocabulary to reach a finance ICP will not convert. The finance ICP does not recognize themselves in the legal frame. They have their own frame.

The four naming patterns below come directly from C294 Phases 01–04 findings.

---

## Legal ICP — Naming Pattern

**Before they can name the problem:** "I have a framework problem — I have SAV guidelines but I don't have a decision tree. I know what the framework says. I don't know what it requires me to do on a Tuesday when an associate wants to use Copilot on a client matter."

**What surfaces the urgency:** "The carrier renewal questionnaire asked me questions about AI authorization architecture. I couldn't answer them from my existing documentation. I had to write something. I wrote something that wasn't true."

**What triggers the search:** "I realized my shadow AI situation — whatever that is — is my problem. Not my IT vendor's. Not Microsoft 365's. Mine."

**What they need before they trust:** "I need to know this practice has seen exactly my situation before. Not a similar situation. My situation — a Geneva attorney with a specific documentation gap and a carrier renewal in 90 days."

**How they describe the ideal outcome:** "I want something I can put in front of the SAV if they ever ask. I want to know what I actually signed when I signed the Microsoft DPA. I want to be able to tell my board what our AI methodology is and have it mean something."

## Finance / WM ICP — Naming Pattern

**Before they can name the problem:** "The carrier third-generation questionnaire asked me to attest to something I don't fully understand. I signed it anyway because the renewal was time-sensitive. I have been thinking about that signature ever since."

**What surfaces the urgency:** "An institutional client sent an AI methodology DDQ. I realized I couldn't respond to it without exposing things I don't want exposed. I also realized I don't have a methodology."

**What triggers the search:** "The cross-firm comparison happened at a peer dinner. Someone mentioned their AI documentation. I realized mine was a fraction of theirs. I started asking questions."

**What they need before they trust:** "I need to know this practice has tracked carrier iteration — not just what the current questionnaire asks, but how the logic has moved across renewal cycles and what that means for what I'm personally attesting to."

**How they describe the ideal outcome:** "I want to walk into my next carrier renewal knowing exactly what I'm signing and why. I want to be able to show institutional clients a methodology document that holds up to scrutiny. I want the documentation to survive the examination."

---

## Consulting ICP — Naming Pattern

**Before they can name the problem:** "We lost a client engagement because we couldn't respond to their AI DDQ. We had to admit we don't have an AI governance framework. That admission cost us the mandate."

**What surfaces the urgency:** "My sub-consultant used AI on a client deliverable. I presented it as my work. I'm now accountable for AI decisions I didn't make. I don't know what my liability is."

**What triggers the search:** "My insurance broker asked three AI governance questions at renewal. I couldn't answer two of them. The third answer I gave was probably wrong."

**What they need before they trust:** "I need to know what the CHF 250,000 personal liability ceiling actually means for my specific situation. Nobody has ever explained it in terms that relate to what I do on a Wednesday afternoon."

**How they describe the ideal outcome:** "I want a methodology I can put in proposals. I want to know what I can and can't delegate to sub-consultants who use AI. I want to be able to respond to client AI DDQs without exposing myself."

---

## Healthcare ICP — Naming Pattern

**Before they can name the problem:** "I don't know what happens to my patient data when it goes through the AI tool I use. I signed the vendor terms. I didn't understand what I signed."

**What surfaces the urgency:** "A patient asked me to explain how their data is processed by my AI tool. I couldn't give them a coherent answer. I realized I don't have a coherent answer."

**What triggers the search:** "At an FMH event, a colleague mentioned they'd had their vendor terms reviewed. It hadn't occurred to me that was something I should do."

**What they need before they trust:** "I need to know whether the Art.22 reversal right and medical record integrity obligations can actually be reconciled — or whether they're in genuine conflict. And if they're in conflict, what do I do on Monday morning?"

**How they describe the ideal outcome:** "I want to be able to show a patient how their data was processed. I want to be able to pass an FMH audit without having rebuilt my entire documentation system. I want to know whether my AI triage tool is actually covered by my malpractice insurance if something goes wrong."

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language Summary

| Context | Legal | Finance/WM | Consulting | Healthcare |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Urgency trigger** | Carrier renewal + shadow AI | Carrier 3rd-gen questionnaire + institutional DDQ | Lost client engagement + insurance broker | Patient question + FMH peer disclosure |
| **Naming the problem** | "I have a framework but no decision tree" | "I signed something I don't fully understand" | "I'm accountable for decisions I didn't make" | "I don't know what happens to patient data" |
| **Fear** | SAV audit without documentation | Personal liability under carrier attestation | CHF 250K personal fine + lost mandate | Art.321 CP exposure + malpractice gap |
| **Ideal outcome** | Defensible documentation for SAV/carrier | Survive carrier renewal + institutional scrutiny | Client DDQ response + methodology in proposals | Audit-readiness + data extraction clarity |
| **Trust trigger** | "You've seen my exact situation" | "You've tracked the carrier iteration logic" | "You've explained the personal liability ceiling for my practice type" | "You've named the Art.22 paradox for outpatient practice" |

## Implications for DigitalCoa.ch Messaging

**Lead magnets must match vertical naming patterns.** A single cross-vertical lead magnet using unified vocabulary will underperform vertical-specific versions using each ICP's naming language. The Phase 06 lead magnet drafts (file 05) address this with EN + FR variants per vertical.

**Pre-briefing qualification call is the first trust occasion.** The call must demonstrate vertical-specific familiarity before proposing any engagement structure. The ICP's trust forms through recognition — the practice describing their situation in their vocabulary before any analysis is offered.

**The documentation window urgency is cross-vertical but framed differently by each ICP:**
- Legal: "decisions you've already made without documentation"
- Finance: "the signature you already signed that you couldn't fully explain"
- Consulting: "the sub-consultant decisions already made without protocol"
- Healthcare: "the patient data already processed through vendor infrastructure"
