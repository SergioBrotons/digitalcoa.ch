# The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework
## A 9-Section Framework for Geneva Consulting Firms (2–15 Consultants)

---

## Section 1: The Geneva Consulting AI Reality Check

**What small Geneva consulting firms need to understand in March 2026.**

The Geneva consulting sector is experiencing two simultaneous shifts:
1. **M&A rebound (+16% in 2025, IT sector +28%)** — more consulting demand in IT/tech due diligence and post-merger AI integration
2. **Labor shortage of 400,000 workers in Swiss SMEs** — SMBs turning to AI to address labor gaps

Both shifts increase AI adoption among your clients — and increase the AI governance exposure of consulting firms themselves.

The EU AI Act's GPAI obligations have been **operative since August 2, 2025** (~8.5 months). If your consulting firm has any EU market nexus — EU clients, EU-delivered services, AI outputs intended for EU use — you face active compliance obligations now.

---

## Section 2: The IT/Tech Sub-Segment Is Under the Most Pressure

**IT consulting firms have the most immediate GPAI exposure.**

IT services/software accounted for **28% of Swiss domestic and inbound M&A transactions** in 2025 (up from 14% in 2024). This means:
- More IT consulting engagements involving AI systems
- Post-merger AI integration consulting
- AI due diligence in M&A transactions
- More AI tools used in delivering client work

**IT consulting firms are the leading edge of GPAI pressure in Geneva's consulting sector.**

## Section 3: What AI Is Your Consulting Firm Actually Using?

**Mapping AI applications to compliance obligations.**

Common AI tools in small consulting firms:
- Client analysis AI (market research, financial analysis, data modelling)
- Proposal and deliverable generation AI
- Project management and workflow AI
- Knowledge management AI
- Client communication AI

**For each AI tool, identify:**
1. Does it process client data?
2. Is the output delivered to EU clients or used in EU contexts?
3. Does it generate content that could be classified as AI-generated under EU AI Act?
4. Are you using it as a GPAI system provider, distributor, or importer?

## Section 4: The GPAI Compliance Window — Active Since August 2, 2025

**GPAI obligations have been live for 8.5 months — the compliance gap is growing.**

**If your consulting firm has EU nexus, you face these GPAI obligations NOW:**
- Transparency: Disclose when AI generates content in client deliverables
- Technical documentation: Maintain documentation of your AI systems
- Copyright compliance: Ensure training data used by your AI tools respects copyright
- Systemic risk monitoring: Monitor for systemic risks from AI systems you provide

**The May 2026 moment:** When the Federal Council publishes its AI Action Plan First Draft in May 2026, EU-focused consulting firms will face heightened awareness. Your clients may start asking questions about GPAI compliance. You want to be the advisor.

## Section 5: The Labor Shortage AI Adoption Driver

**Swiss SMEs are turning to AI to solve the 400,000-worker labor shortage.**

As a consulting firm advising SMBs, you are already seeing this:
- Clients asking about AI tools to replace/augment staff
- AI adoption projects driven by labor constraints
- AI integration in post-merger phases

**Your advisory opportunity:** Help SMB clients adopt AI responsibly. This requires AI governance advisory — understanding what AI tools are appropriate, what data is being processed, what compliance obligations exist.

**Your own exposure:** The more AI tools you recommend or implement for clients, the more governance responsibility you carry.

---

## Section 6: The Dual Compliance Challenge

**Swiss consulting firms with EU nexus face two regulatory frameworks simultaneously.**

**nFADP (Swiss domestic):**
- Applies to AI processing of personal data in Switzerland
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing
- FDPIC enforcement trajectory building

**EU AI Act (if EU nexus):**
- GPAI transparency obligations NOW
- High-risk AI obligations August 2, 2026
- Extraterritorial reach for Swiss firms with EU market access

**The Federal Council's "lean approach" means Switzerland will not rush a cross-sector AI law. Your Swiss domestic obligations under nFADP are active now. Your EU obligations under EU AI Act are active now if you have EU nexus.**

## Section 7: Your 3-Layer AI Governance Framework

**A practical structure for small Geneva consulting firms.**

**Layer 1 — AI Inventory and Classification**
- Document every AI tool used in client work
- Classify by: EU nexus (yes/no), data sensitivity, output significance
- Map which AI tools trigger GPAI obligations

**Layer 2 — Client Data AI Governance**
- DPIA for high-risk AI processing (nFADP Art. 22)
- Data processing agreements with AI vendors
- Client consent framework for AI-generated work product
- Output disclosure protocol (EU AI Act transparency)

**Layer 3 — Advisory Service Extension**
- Position AI governance as an advisory service for your SMB clients
- Address the labor shortage AI adoption wave
- Develop AI governance offerings for M&A due diligence engagements
- Integrate AI governance into existing consulting engagements
