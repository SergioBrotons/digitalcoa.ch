## The Five-Layer Problem

### Layer 1: Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — 3 Months with New Leadership

Swissdamed is the Swiss medical device database, entering into force July 1, 2026. It covers AI systems used in patient care that qualify as medical devices.

Vincenza Trivigno became Swissmedic's Executive Director in January 2026 — during the Swissdamed implementation window. New leadership at a regulatory agency during the pre-enforcement period is a signal. Not a guarantee of aggressive enforcement on day one — but a signal that the practices assuming a soft launch may be misreading the environment.

**What this means practically:**
- AI diagnostic tools, imaging analysis software, AI-assisted treatment planning, and AI patient monitoring systems likely require registration
- Registration requires: device classification, intended use documentation, clinical evaluation evidence, post-market surveillance plan
- No grace period means no extension after July 1

**If you're using an unregistered AI medical device after July 1:** You're operating outside Swissmedic authorization. The liability exposure is not theoretical.

### Layer 2: The FOM Human Review Standard

The Federal Office of Public Health has clarified the clinical standard for AI in treatment decisions:

- AI-assisted diagnoses, treatment recommendations, and therapy planning must be reviewed by a qualified physician before clinical application
- AI must be disclosed to the patient: patients have a right to know when AI is involved in their care
- The physician's professional liability insurance must cover AI-assisted care — verify with your insurer

**The governing principle: AI as a second opinion, not an autonomous decision-maker.**

### Layer 3: Three-Layer Data Governance for Patient AI

Patient data processed through AI tools triggers three concurrent frameworks:

**FADP (Federal Act on Data Protection):** Human review required for automated decisions affecting patients. AI processing patient data must have a documented legal basis, data minimization, and purpose limitation.

**Human Research Act (HRA):** If AI processing of patient data constitutes research, HRA consent and ethics approval requirements apply. The boundary between clinical care and research AI is an active compliance question.

**Federal AI Ordinance:** AI tools used in patient care are classified as high-risk. This requires documented human oversight, transparency to data subjects, and — for Swissdamed-registered devices — post-market surveillance.

### Layer 4: Healthcare Labor Shortage — AI Is Also a Workforce Strategy

Switzerland faces a structural healthcare labor shortage. The KOF study (March 2026) confirms the scale: hundreds of thousands of workers short across the economy over the next decade, with healthcare among the most severely affected sectors.

This matters for your AI decisions in two ways simultaneously:

**Compliance dimension:** Swissdamed requires registration, FOM requires human review, FADP requires data governance. These are non-negotiable.

**Workforce dimension:** AI tools in scheduling, documentation, patient communication, and telemedicine are not just regulatory exposures — they are operational necessities. The practices that build compliant AI systems are building infrastructure that reduces reliance on scarce clinical and administrative staff.

The Swissdamed governance framework is also a workforce strategy framework. Compliant AI adoption addresses both dimensions.

### Layer 5: G7 Évian — International Patient Data Governance

The G7 summit in Évian (June 15–17, 2026) creates a security perimeter covering Geneva, Vaud, and Valais. Geneva's private medical practices serve an internationally mobile patient population.

G7 security measures may affect patient travel to Geneva practices, cross-border care arrangements, and international patient data flows. AI systems managing international patient data during the G7 period may attract additional regulatory scrutiny — particularly if patients are traveling from G7 countries and their data flows through AI systems with US-based infrastructure.

If your practice serves international patients, the G7 period is a practical trigger to audit your patient data AI governance before June 15.

## The Swissdamed 90-Day Countdown

**Days 1–30: Inventory and Assessment**
- List every AI tool used in patient care in your practice
- For each: vendor, intended use, data processed, device classification under Swissdamed
- Identify which tools require Swissdamed registration and which don't
- Check: any AI vendor with US-based infrastructure processing patient data?

**Days 31–60: Registration and Documentation**
- Prepare Swissdamed registration dossiers for qualifying devices
- Establish FOM-compliant human review protocols for AI-assisted decisions
- Execute DPA agreements with AI vendors processing patient data
- Verify insurance coverage for AI-assisted care with your liability insurer

**Days 61–90: Patient Disclosure and Staff Training**
- Draft patient-facing AI disclosure materials (FADP Art. 26 compliance)
- Train clinical staff on human review requirements and documentation
- For practices with international patients: assess G7 data flow implications
- Final Swissdamed registration submissions

## The AI as Second Opinion Protocol

For any AI-assisted clinical decision:

**1. AI generates output** — the AI system produces its analysis, recommendation, or diagnostic impression.

**2. Physician reviews independently** — before clinical application, the physician reviews the AI output in the context of their clinical knowledge of the patient. The physician does not simply accept the AI output.

**3. Physician documents review** — the patient record documents: (a) that AI was used, (b) the AI output, (c) the physician's independent assessment, (d) the final clinical decision and its basis.

**4. Patient is informed** — the patient is told AI was used in their care. They have a right to know. This is also good practice for building trust in AI-assisted care.

## Three-Layer Data Governance Checklist

**Layer 1 — FADP:**
- Legal basis documented for each AI processing of patient data
- Data minimization assessed: is only the minimum necessary data processed?
- Human review checkpoint for automated decisions

**Layer 2 — Human Research Act:**
- Determined whether AI processing constitutes research (vs. clinical care)
- If research: HRA consent and ethics approval in place
- If clinical: FADP obligations fully addressed

**Layer 3 — Federal AI Ordinance:**
- AI tools classified by risk level
- High-risk AI: human oversight documentation in place
- Swissdamed registration complete or in progress
