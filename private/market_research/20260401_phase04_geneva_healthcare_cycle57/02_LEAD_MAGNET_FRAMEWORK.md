## Problem Statement (5 layers)

### Layer 1: Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — 3 Months, New Leadership, Enforcement Confidence Rising
Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Database) registration is required for AI tools used in patient care that qualify as medical devices. Deadline: July 1, 2026. No grace period. Vincenza Trivigno assumed leadership of Swissmedic (effective January 2026) during the Swissdamed implementation window. New regulatory leadership during the pre-enforcement period is historically associated with heightened initial enforcement activity. Geneva medical practices should not assume a soft launch. **C57 update: the enforcement confidence interval has shifted upward since C56.**

### Layer 2: FOM Human Review Standard for AI-Assisted Treatment
AI in treatment decisions must be: (a) reviewed by a physician before clinical application, (b) disclosed to the patient, (c) covered by professional liability insurance. AI as second opinion, not autonomous decision-maker. This standard applies regardless of Swissdamed registration status.

### Layer 3: Three-Layer Data Governance (FADP + HRA + AI Ordinance)
Patient data processed through AI tools: FADP human review + Human Research Act obligations + Federal AI Ordinance risk classification. Three concurrent frameworks. One breach triggers all three. With Swissdamed now 3 months away, the risk of an audit finding a data governance gap AND an unregistered AI tool simultaneously is material.

### Layer 4: Healthcare Labor Shortage = AI Adoption Has a Workforce Dimension
KOF study (March 2026): Switzerland faces a structural healthcare labor shortage. AI tools in scheduling, documentation, telemedicine, and patient communication serve a dual purpose: regulatory compliance AND operational labor substitution. Medical practices that treat AI purely as a compliance burden miss the operational imperative. **C57 addition: the Swissdamed framework is not just about avoiding penalties — it's about building an AI infrastructure that addresses workforce constraints while staying compliant.**

### Layer 5: G7 Évian — International Patient Data Governance
G7 summit in Évian (June 15–17, 2026) security arrangements affect Geneva, Vaud, and Valais. Geneva's private medical practices serve an internationally mobile patient population. G7 security may affect: patient travel to Geneva practices, cross-border care arrangements, and international patient data flows. AI systems managing international patient data during the G7 period need additional governance attention — data flows involving patients traveling from G7 countries may attract regulatory scrutiny.
