## Finding 1: Swiss Class Action Legislation (Verbandsklage) — Post-2025 Implementation

### Signal
The Swiss Parliament passed amendments to the Civil Code enabling qualified entities to file collective actions for consumer protection. Implementation at cantonal level began Q4 2025, with Geneva courts expecting first filings in 2026.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Corporations with Swiss consumer-facing operations need compliance with new class action exposure rules
- **Distinct angle:** Not mass arbitration (C99) — this is domestic consumer protection enforcement with qualifying entity gatekeepers
- **Revenue model:** Compliance counseling + litigation support for affected sectors (retail, financial services, telco, insurance)
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva courts likely to become preferred venue for French-speaking consumer class actions

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "What the Verbandsklage Means for Your Swiss Operations"
- Target ICP: Compliance officers, GCs at consumer-facing companies

## Finding 2: Revised Lugano Convention — UK Re-accession Implications for Geneva Arbitration

### Signal
The UK applied to re-join the Lugano Convention (pending EC and contracting party approval). Geneva is one of Europe's leading arbitration seats; Lugano governs enforcement of civil judgments across Switzerland-EU-UK.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** UK-Swiss commercial relationships require clarity on judgment enforcement post-Brexit Lugano status change
- **Distinct angle:** Not international arbitration seat (C99/C118) — this is enforcement of court judgments specifically
- **Revenue model:** Cross-border contract drafting, enforcement opinions, litigation strategy
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's courts + arbitration institutions benefit from clear enforcement pathways

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Lugano Convention Update: What UK-Swiss Business Means for Your Contracts"
- Target ICP: International GCs, trade law clients, family offices with UK/Swiss assets

## Finding 3: Revised Swiss Data Protection Ordinance (RDSV) — Beyond GDPR Alignment

### Signal
The Federal Council's revised Data Protection Ordinance (RDSV) contains provisions that go beyond GDPR minimum standards, particularly on: automated decision-making disclosure, cross-border transfer mechanisms for non-adequate countries, and specific rules for HR data.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Companies operating in Switzerland need specific RDSV compliance, not just GDPR conformance
- **Distinct angle:** Not FADP general enforcement (C118/C99) or AI training data (C118) — this is ordinance-level specificity
- **Revenue model:** Data protection audits, policy drafting, cross-border transfer agreements, HR compliance programs
- **Geneva specificity:** Multinationals with Geneva HQs need RDSV-specific compliance distinct from EU entities

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "RDSV vs. GDPR: The Compliance Gap Swiss Companies Must Address"
- Target ICP: DPOs, compliance officers, HR directors, multinationals

## Finding 4: SECO Russian Sanctions Enforcement — Frozen Asset Restructuring Advisory

### Signal
SECO (State Secretariat for Economic Affairs) has been intensifying enforcement of Russian sanctions, including frozen asset reporting requirements and restrictions on passive payments. Advisory demand emerging for asset restructuring strategies.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Asset managers, family offices, and corporations holding Russian-adjacent assets need compliant restructuring options
- **Distinct angle:** Not FATF AML (C118) or CARB sanctions (C118) — this is SECO-specific asset freeze enforcement and restructuring
- **Revenue model:** Compliance opinions, asset restructuring planning, SECO reporting support
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's private banking and commodity trading sectors directly affected

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "SECO Russian Sanctions: Compliance Obligations for Swiss Asset Holders"
- Target ICP: Private bankers, family offices, commodity traders, compliance officers

## Finding 5: NOTfonds Liability Extension — Climate-Related Natural Catastrophe Coverage

### Signal
The Swiss Natural Catastrophe Insurance (NOTfonds) framework is under revision to expand coverage scope to include climate change intensifying events. This creates new liability questions for property owners, insurers, and municipalities.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Liability questions around climate risk disclosure, property developer obligations, municipal responsibilities
- **Distinct angle:** Not previously covered in any Geneva legal cycle
- **Revenue model:** Liability counseling, contract risk allocation, insurance coverage disputes
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva lakefront and riverside properties at specific risk

## Finding 6: WIPO Beijing Treaty Implementation — Audiovisual Performers Rights

### Signal
The Beijing Treaty on Audiovisual Performances entered into force for Switzerland in 2026. It grants performers exclusive rights over their audiovisual fixations, creating new licensing and royalty frameworks.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Production companies, streaming platforms, and performers need to renegotiate contracts to account for performers' new rights
- **Distinct angle:** Not IP general (no prior Geneva legal cycle covered audiovisual performer rights specifically)
- **Revenue model:** Contract drafting, licensing framework design, dispute resolution
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's production industry and international organizations affected

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Beijing Treaty in Force: What Swiss AV Production Companies Must Do Now"
- Target ICP: Production companies, streaming services, performer agents, media lawyers
