# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal — C310 Phase 01

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

The Geneva AI accountability market for small legal firms has a specific structural gap: the services that exist address the problem the market used to have, not the problem it now has. C309 revealed the market moved from discovery failure to implementation failure. C310 reveals the competitive landscape has not updated to match.

---

## Competitive Landscape

### What Exists

**1. AI Governance Frameworks (International/Swiss)**
Products: ISO 42001 (AI Management Systems), NIST AI RMF, various EU AI Act advisory frameworks.
Position: Framework language. Designed for large enterprises. Translated into Swiss context by consultancies.
Gap: Frameworks are policy documents — they answer "do you have a system?" not "can you show me the record?" No framework product produces contemporaneous evidence of AI use in specific client matters. No framework product is built to carrier form specification.

**2. Legal Tech Vendors (AI Tool Providers)**
Products: Claude for legal research, Copilot for document review, various drafting tools.
Position: Tool提供者. They sell the AI. They are not accountable for governance.
Gap: Vendors have no governance product. Their terms of service do not address attorney-client privilege. Their records are discoverable through the vendor — a privilege exposure the ICP has independently identified. No vendor offers a documentation system for AI use in client matters.

**3. Swiss Bar / OFL Guidance**
Products: Various OFL publications on AI use, bar association discussions.
Position: Regulatory guidance. Words like "adequate" without operational definition.
Gap: OFL has not defined "adequate." No bar guidance addresses carrier form mapping. No bar guidance produces evidence architecture. The guidance is descriptive; the market needs prescriptive. OFL guidance has no carrier form intelligence function.

**4. Insurance Broker Risk Advisory**
Products: Risk management advice from brokers, some AI governance content.
Position: Channel. Brokers have law firm clients and are looking for AI governance services to refer.
Gap: Brokers do not have an AI governance product to refer. The referral gap is the channel problem — the broker wants to send a client to a specific, bounded, credible service and cannot find one. The broker is the highest-value acquisition channel and is currently dormant.

**5. IT Service Providers**
Products: General IT security, data governance, compliance.
Position: Technical security. They cover cybersecurity, not AI governance specifically.
Gap: AI governance is distinct from IT security. IT providers do not address carrier attestation, attorney-client privilege through vendor records, or matter-level AI use documentation. The IT provider has no carrier form intelligence and no evidence architecture product.

## What's Missing — The White Spaces

**1. Pre-Signing Carrier Form Review Service**
What it is: A bounded, deliverable service — review the carrier's attestation form before signing, map questions to existing documentation, identify gaps, produce a gap memo. Delivered in one 90-minute session.
What it solves: The pre-signing intervention point is the highest-value moment in the accountability chain and is completely unoccupied. The ICP who reviews the form before signing avoids the post-renewal discovery problem.
Why nobody has built it: Requires carrier form reading expertise — must track multiple carriers' form evolution across renewal cycles. Requires Geneva legal market specialization. Requires bilingual EN/FR documentation capability.
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

**2. Matter-Level AI Use Record System**
What it is: A documentation system designed for the specific question "what AI was used in this specific matter, by whom, with what authorization, and what did the client authorize?" Produces contemporaneous records at matter level, not firm level.
What it solves: The matter-level form shift (C310 Finding 03). The documentation architecture required to answer matter-level carrier questions does not exist as a product.
Why nobody has built it: Requires understanding of both legal practice operations and carrier form architecture. Requires design for small firm workflow (not enterprise). Requires bilingual EN/FR.
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

**3. Retroactive AI Documentation Reconstruction Service**
What it is: A structured process to reconstruct 12–24 months of undocumented AI use in a defensible form — specific to the carrier's questions, specific to the firm's matter types, with a methodology that produces records the carrier will accept.
What it solves: The non-linear documentation debt problem (C310 Finding 06). The ICP who has tried linear forward-looking solutions and failed needs retroactive reconstruction — not another policy.
Why nobody has built it: Retroactive reconstruction is technically complex — requires understanding of what "contemporaneous" means for records that weren't created contemporaneously. Requires carrier form specification knowledge. Requires a methodology that produces defensible retroactive records.
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

**4. Authorization Chain Documentation for AI-Incompetent Managing Partners**
What it is: A supervision architecture specifically designed for the managing partner who cannot personally evaluate AI output — produces documentation of reasonable supervision without requiring substantive AI evaluation.
What it solves: The invisible authorization chain problem (C310 Finding 04) and the competence gap problem (C309 Finding 03). The managing partner needs to document supervision of associates they cannot evaluate.
Why nobody has built it: Requires understanding of what "adequate supervision" means in the context of AI output the supervisor cannot evaluate. Requires a specific documentation design that preserves the supervision record without requiring the supervisor to understand the AI.
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

**5. Broker Referral Program for AI Accountability Services**
What it is: A structured referral program designed for Geneva insurance brokers who serve law firm clients — with a specific product, a specific delivery format, and a specific referral process.
What it solves: The broker channel (highest-value acquisition path) is dormant because no AI governance provider has built a broker-referral product. The broker has clients who need the service and no one to send them to.
Why nobody has built it: Requires a specific product design for broker referral (bounded, credible, deliverable), a referral process, and a track record. Requires Geneva market presence and credibility.
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

**6. Fee Agreement AI Clause Drafting Service**
What it is: A specific, bounded drafting service — produce an AI clause for the attorney's fee agreement that addresses disclosure, authorization, billing basis, and data processing consent.
What it solves: The client question problem (C310 Finding 05) and the fee agreement AI billing question (C309 Finding 05). The client-facing document that resolves both the commercial question and the client trust problem.
Why nobody has built it: Requires legal drafting expertise (Swiss fee agreement conventions), AI governance knowledge (what needs to be disclosed), and client communication design (how to frame AI use in a client-appropriate way).
Current state: Zero competitors. First-mover opportunity.

## The Critical Finding: Carrier Form is the Design Specification — Nobody Is Reading It

The most important competitive insight for C310 is not what exists in the market — it is what the market's design standard actually is. The carrier's attestation form is the de facto standard for AI governance evidence in the Geneva legal market. The form is raising its sophistication faster than any other reference point. The form is the only auditable standard. The OFL has not defined "adequate." The carrier form is the standard.

No competitor is reading carrier forms as a market intelligence function. No competitor is tracking the evolution of carrier forms across renewal cycles. No competitor is mapping evidence requirements to carrier form questions. This is the most durable competitive advantage available: "I read carrier forms for a living."

## C310 Competitive Synthesis

**What the market has that is adequate:** 
- Written policies (firm-level, policy documents, answer wrong question type)
- Peer-comparison adequacy signals (false security, carrier standard is higher)
- Vendor relationships (unreviewed for privilege exposure)

**What the market needs that doesn't exist:**
- Pre-signing carrier form review
- Matter-level AI use record system
- Retroactive documentation reconstruction
- Authorization chain documentation for AI-incompetent managing partners
- Broker referral program
- Fee agreement AI clause

**The positioning that occupies the white space:**
One evidence architecture — not four separate products. The six white spaces above are all variations of one problem: documenting AI use in client matters in a form the carrier will accept. The service that solves this problem in one integrated architecture, with matter-level records, retroactive reconstruction capability, and carrier form mapping — owns the white space.
