### Vector 03
- **Topic:** Swiss Federal Administrative Court — AI cannot be inventor (DABUS ruling, June 2025)
- **Finding:** June 26, 2025 ruling confirmed AI systems cannot be recognized inventors under Swiss patent law. Inventions created with AI assistance are patentable if a natural person is named inventor and meaningfully contributed (training AI, supplying data, interpreting output, identifying patentable result). Human-AI collaboration must be documented at each stage. Dr. Thaler's DABUS involvement (training + data + identification) was sufficient for human inventorship.
- **Source:** Federal Administrative Court June 26, 2025; lenzstaehelin.com; haugpartners.com; haerting.ch
- **Signal Strength:** HIGH — binding court precedent, not guidance
- **Advisory Opportunity:** Geneva IP practice AI invention documentation protocols; human-AI collaboration invention disclosure checklists; R&D advisory for Geneva precision manufacturing and biotech with AI development pipelines; patent portfolio strategy for AI-assisted innovations
- **Status:** NET-NEW C145 ✅
