## Common Briefing Architecture

### The Problem Frame
Every Geneva professional facing AI accountability has the same underlying problem: the accountability ecosystem formed around them without their participation, the standards that apply to them were set in rooms they didn't access, and the documentation they need to demonstrate compliance doesn't exist in a form designed for their scale.

The briefing names this pattern precisely before addressing specific obligations.

### The Evidence Architecture Problem
The central insight: the documentation standards implied by carrier attestation forms, institutional questionnaires, and professional standards are architecturally unobtainable with current boutique internal processes — because those processes were not designed to produce this kind of evidence.

The briefing doesn't offer a better form. It offers a different architecture.

### The Personal Liability Layer
Entity-level compliance and personal liability are different exposures, governed by different standards, with different consequences. The briefing names the personal liability provisions that apply to each vertical and the documentation architecture required to defend against them personally.

### The Window Positioning
The documentation window is a present-tense positioning event, not a future deadline. Organizations that build documentation architecture now will have demonstrable adequacy when the ecosystem solidifies. Organizations that wait will be building retroactive evidence — which reveals itself as retroactive when examined.
