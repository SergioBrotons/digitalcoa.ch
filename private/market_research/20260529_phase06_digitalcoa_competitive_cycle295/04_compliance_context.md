## The Geneva Accountability Ecosystem: A Practice in Formation

### The Regulatory Stack by Vertical

**nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)**
Status: In force September 1, 2023.
Application: Universal — all organizations processing Swiss personal data.
Key articles for the practice:
- Art.9: Lawfulness of data processing — AI tool selection requires compliant DPA
- Art.20/21: Right to explanation for automated decisions — client-facing obligation
- Art.22: Right to not be subject to automated decision — profiling limitations
- Art.63: Criminal liability — personal liability ceiling for unauthorized processing

The practice must maintain nFADP compliance for its own operations while advising clients on their nFADP obligations. The advisor who cannot demonstrate their own compliance posture faces credibility collapse.

**FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (AI Governance and Risk Management)**
Status: Published December 18, 2024. Operative now.
Application: Swiss financial institutions supervised by FINMA.
The practice advising finance/wm clients on FINMA 08/2024 obligations must demonstrate their own governance framework meets comparable standards.

**SAV Guidelines (Swiss Bar Association)**
Status: Published June 2024.
Application: Swiss attorneys, Geneva Bar members.
The practice advising legal clients on SAV AI guidance must operate under equivalent accountability standards.

**FMH Medical Professional Standards**
Status: Ongoing.
Application: Physicians, medical practices.
The practice advising healthcare clients on FMH human oversight obligations must demonstrate comparable internal governance.

**Council of Europe AI Convention**
Status: Signed March 2025. Switzerland signatory. Implementation in progress.
Application: All AI deployers in signatory states.
Deployer obligations are live during implementation period — the practice must maintain documentation demonstrating deployer compliance.

## The Practice's Own Regulatory Exposure

### Personal Liability as Practitioner
The practice is operated by Sergio Brotons as an individual practitioner. Every client engagement creates personal exposure under:
- nFADP Art.63 (personal criminal liability for unauthorized data processing)
- Professional standards (attorney-client privilege, professional secrecy obligations)
- Client contract terms accepted personally

The practice cannot advise on personal liability exposure without managing its own.

### Documentation Obligations
As an AI accountability advisor, the practice's own documentation obligations are elevated:
- Every client engagement produces documentation (emails, notes, frameworks, recommendations)
- That documentation may be subject to client disclosure requests
- The practice must maintain evidence architecture for its own recommendations

### Carrier Attestation Exposure
The practice itself uses AI tools. Carrier attestation forms submitted for the practice's own PI coverage create the same exposure the practice advises clients on.
