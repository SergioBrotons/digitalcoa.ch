## Lead Magnet Framework

### Title
**The Small Real Estate Agency AI Governance Question:**
*Your Agency Is Already Using AI. Here Is How to Govern It.*

---

### Section 1 — 67% AI Adoption: Your Agency Is Already Behind

**Hook:**
67% of Swiss real estate agencies are already using AI. If your agency is among the 33% not yet using it, you face a competitive gap. If you ARE using AI without governance, you face a liability gap.

**Key points:**
- Property valuation AI
- Listing description generation
- Tenant screening AI
- Market analysis AI
- Customer relationship AI

---

### Section 2 — The AI You Already Have (Retroactive Inventory)

**Hook:**
This is not about adopting new AI. This is about governing the AI you are already using.

**Key questions:**
- What AI tools does your agency use?
- What data is being processed?
- Tenant names, financial information, property details?
- Who is accountable for AI decisions?

---

### Section 3 — GAIGI: The Industry Association That Is Not Helping You

**Hook:**
The Geneva Real Estate Association (GAIGI) has AI guidance — for large institutions. Small agencies: gaigi.ch returned a 404. Your governance gap is not being filled by your industry association.

---

### Section 4 — nFADP Art. 5(c): Your AI Processing Already Has Obligations

**Hook:**
The Federal Act on Data Protection has been in force since September 1, 2023. AI processing of personal data — tenant information, financial details, property data — requires DPIA under nFADP Art. 5(c).

**Key points:**
- Tenant screening AI = personal data processing
- Property valuation AI = financial data processing
- AI-generated listing descriptions = potentially personal data

---

### Section 5 — Swissdamed Spillover (~13 Weeks)

**Hook:**
The healthcare AI deadline is not about real estate. But it is creating a ripple effect: your clients across all sectors are now asking their service providers — including real estate agencies — about AI governance.

---

### Section 6 — Retroactive Compliance: The Unique RE Entry Point

**Hook:**
Unlike other sectors facing upcoming deadlines, real estate's advantage is this: you do not need to prepare for something coming. You need to govern what you already have.

**Key points:**
- Inventory → Assess → Document → Review
- Lower friction than anticipatory compliance
- Lower resistance from agency principals

---

### Section 7 — Seven-Step Retroactive AI Governance Checklist

**Framework:**
1. [ ] AI inventory: What AI is your agency using?
2. [ ] Data mapping: What personal/tenant/property data is in each tool?
3. [ ] nFADP Art. 5(c) DPIA: Completed for each AI tool?
4. [ ] GAIGI gap: Document that industry guidance is not SMB-accessible
5. [ ] Tenant screening AI: Specific DPIA completed?
6. [ ] Vendor agreements: Data Processing Agreements in place?
7. [ ] Annual review: Scheduled?
