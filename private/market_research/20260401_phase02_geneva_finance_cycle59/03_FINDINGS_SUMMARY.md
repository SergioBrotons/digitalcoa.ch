## NET-NEW C59 Findings

### 1. FINMA AI Survey — 50% of Swiss Financial Institutions Using AI, High BigTech Dependency
- **What:** FINMA formal survey (Nov 2024 - Jan 2025), ~400 institutions — 50% using AI, 91% using generative AI, only 50% with explicit AI strategy. BigTech dependency increasing. FINMA explicitly flags outsourcing risk.
- **Source:** FINMA (finma.ch), April 2025
- **Why it matters for WM:** Boutique WMs using US-hosted AI face compounded exposure: FADP + FINMA outsourcing oversight. Only half of Swiss financial institutions have an explicit AI strategy — governance gap is systemic.

### 2. EU AI Act August 2026 Full Enforcement — Cross-Border Regulatory Convergence
- **What:** EU AI Act high-risk AI obligations fully enforced August 2026 for asset managers. Swiss WMs with EU clients face cross-border compliance obligations.
- **Source:** Web search synthesis (EY, Oliver Wyman)
- **Why it matters for WM:** Three concurrent regulatory frameworks now: Federal AI Ordinance + FINMA guidance + EU AI Act. Boutique WMs serving EU clients must build governance that satisfies all three.

### 3. Agentic AI — Liability Shift in Wealth Management
- **What:** AI systems autonomously executing financial tasks (not just advising) emerging as distinct category. Autonomous rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, AI-driven trade execution within parameters.
- **Source:** Web search synthesis (InvestSuite, Infront)
- **Why it matters for WM:** Human oversight requirement of Federal AI Ordinance becomes a direct operational obligation when AI acts autonomously. WM firm's liability for AI actions intensifies.

### 4. XAI (Explainable AI) — Competitive Differentiator Confirmed
- **What:** HNW clients demand explainability; FINMA requires it. Swiss-hosted, sovereign AI infrastructure with explainability built in answers both regulatory and client expectations.
- **Source:** ZHAW blog, FINMA survey synthesis
- **Why it matters for WM:** Swiss data sovereignty + XAI = competitive positioning. Boutique WMs can differentiate on governance quality.

### 5. Geneva SME AI Talent Gap — Governance Implementation Barrier
- **What:** Significant portion of Swiss SMEs lack in-house AI skills for implementation and governance.
- **Source:** Web search synthesis (Deloitte, Oliver Wyman)
- **Why it matters for WM:** Boutique WMs face double bind: need AI governance but lack resources to build it. External advisory market opportunity confirmed.
