## Core Promise

## Problem Statement (3 layers — C59 Update)

### Layer 1: Three-Framework Regulatory Convergence
Boutique Geneva WMs now face simultaneous obligations under:
- **Federal AI Ordinance** (Jan 1, 2025 — 15+ months operative)
- **FINMA AI Guidance** (Guidance 08/2024, April 2025 survey confirming systemic AI adoption)
- **EU AI Act** (high-risk obligations enforced August 2026 — ~4 months)

Most boutique WMs are exposed across all three without a documented AI governance framework.

### Layer 2: BigTech Dependency + FINMA Outsourcing Risk Flag
FINMA's April 2025 survey confirms: 91% of Swiss financial institutions using generative AI via external (BigTech) providers. FINMA explicitly flags outsourcing to third-party providers as a key operational risk. Using US-hosted AI for portfolio analytics, client reporting, or risk assessment creates compounded FADP + FINMA outsourcing oversight exposure.

### Layer 3: Agentic AI + Human Oversight Obligation
Agentic AI (autonomous execution of financial tasks) is emerging as a distinct category. When AI acts autonomously, the Federal AI Ordinance's human oversight requirement becomes a direct operational obligation — not a theoretical one. Boutique WMs using AI-driven rebalancing, tax-loss harvesting, or risk models are already exposed.

## CTA Framework

**Self-Assessment First:** A structured checklist that WM firms can complete in 20 minutes to assess their current state across the three regulatory frameworks. Score below 7/10 = clear gap requiring action.

**The Checklist Covers:**
1. Current AI tool inventory (what's using client data, where is it hosted)
2. FINMA outsourcing risk assessment (BigTech dependency check)
3. EU AI Act high-risk exposure (do you serve EU clients with AI-assisted advice?)
4. Federal AI Ordinance human oversight documentation
5. FADP Art. 21/26 compliance for AI-assisted decisions
6. Agentic AI audit (is any AI autonomously acting vs. advising?)
7. XAI explainability documentation (can you explain AI decisions to clients and regulators?)
