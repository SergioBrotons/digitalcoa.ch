# Findings Summary: Cross-Vertical Synthesis — C298 Phase 05

## 6 NET-NEW Findings: Distinct from C214–C297

---

### Finding 01 — The Guidance Vacuum as Structural, Not Temporary

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, the authority that should provide operational guidance has published only principles. The vacuum is not a delay — it is a structural feature of how regulatory guidance is produced. Principles require consensus. Operational specifics require agreement that cannot be reached among diverse membership. The boutique is left to determine proportionality alone, with no reference point for what "adequate" means at their scale.

**Mechanism:** SAV (legal), FINMA (finance), FMH (healthcare), and no authority (consulting) publish principles because operational specifics require consensus that doesn't exist. The guidance vacuum will not be filled in a timeframe relevant to current accountability exposure.

**Implication:** The provider who names this vacuum and produces proportionate implementation methodology for Geneva boutiques occupies a structural position that authorities cannot easily replicate — because the authority's comparative disadvantage is precisely the consensus requirement that produces the vacuum in the first place.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The SAV published principles. The Geneva Bar published nothing. FINMA published a framework without telling you what proportionate means for a 60-person Geneva wealth manager. That's not an accident — it's how regulatory guidance works."

### Finding 02 — The Personal Signature as Universal Activation Moment

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, personal liability activates through a universal mechanism: the personal signature on an instrument that commits the signatory to an undefined adequacy standard. The signature creates personal exposure before the signatory knows what they committed to. No Geneva service intercepts this moment pre-signature.

**Mechanism:** The managing partner signs the carrier attestation. The MD signs the institutional questionnaire response. The partner signs the engagement letter with AI clauses. The physician signs the carrier attestation. In each case, the personal signature activates personal exposure under Art.63, professional standards, or contract law — before the signatory has reviewed what they're signing.

**Implication:** Pre-signing review is the discrete service that intercepts the activation moment. It is the highest-value intervention because it prevents exposure creation rather than managing exposure after it exists.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The signature is the moment. Before you sign is when I can help you. After you sign, we're managing what you already committed to."

### Finding 03 — The Attestation Architecture Mismatch

**The finding:** The documentation built for carrier attestation purposes is architecturally incompatible with the documentation required for criminal defense under nFADP Art.63. The carrier's form measures documentation completeness. The criminal standard requires evidence of substantive compliance. These are different evidentiary standards. The documentation that satisfies the carrier may produce evidence that is used against the signatory in a criminal proceeding.

**Mechanism:** Carrier attestation forms are designed for underwriting risk evaluation. They reward documentation that appears complete. nFADP Art.63 criminal liability requires proof that unauthorized processing occurred — a different evidentiary standard entirely. The attestation submission may contain representations that cannot be supported when examined for criminal liability purposes.

**Implication:** The pre-signing review must assess attestation responses from both evidentiary perspectives simultaneously: does this response satisfy the carrier AND is it consistent with what I could defend under criminal proceedings?

**Ready-to-use language:** "The carrier wants to see your documentation. The prosecutor wants to see evidence of intent and compliance. Those are different things. The form that satisfies the carrier may not protect you in a criminal proceeding."

### Finding 04 — The Regulatory Timing Convergence Creating Compound Urgency

**The finding:** Multiple regulatory developments are converging on the same period (2025–2026): nFADP enforcement maturation, FINMA 08/2024 operative, EU AI Act Art.4 live, Swissdamed implementation, AI Convention implementation. Each requires documentation. Each has different evidentiary standards. Each creates different exposure. The boutique is facing all of them simultaneously — with no methodology for managing them as a portfolio.

**Mechanism:** The boutique that has built documentation for FINMA guidance discovers that the same AI tool usage requires different documentation for nFADP compliance. The carrier attestation asks for evidence that satisfies neither framework specifically. The institutional questionnaire probes yet another dimension. Each framework operates independently; none reference each other.

**Implication:** Cross-mandate coherence methodology — one framework that satisfies multiple documentation requirements simultaneously — is the missing tool. This is distinct from any single-mandate compliance solution.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're not facing one regulatory development. You're facing five simultaneously — and they're all asking for different evidence of the same thing."

### Finding 05 — The Distributed Accountability Structural Problem

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, the accountable professional has accountability for AI decisions made by people they cannot fully supervise: associates (legal), portfolio managers (finance), sub-consultants (consulting), staff (healthcare). The person with accountability does not control the AI decisions creating that accountability. This is not a firm-specific governance failure — it is a structural feature of how AI was adopted across all four verticals simultaneously.

**Mechanism:** AI was adopted for productivity reasons before any governance evaluation. The adoption created distributed accountability that the accountable professional did not recognize. The documentation standard implied by attestation forms (contemporaneous evidence of human authorization) is unobtainable with the informal authorization processes that characterize boutique operations.

**Implication:** The governance framework must be designed for the organizational structure, not the idealized structure that larger organizations operate within. A governance framework that requires capabilities the boutique doesn't have is not a solution — it is a description of the problem.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The person who signed the attestation doesn't control the AI decisions. That's the exposure no one's naming."

### Finding 06 — The First-Mover Window Structure

**The finding:** The first-mover window is structured by regulatory timing, not by competitive entry. The window remains open because no competitor has claimed the Geneva boutique accountability infrastructure position — and because regulatory developments continue to create new accountability moments (Swissdamed, EU AI Act enforcement, carrier attestation evolution). The window does not close through competitive saturation; it closes when regulatory development stabilizes and the market becomes predictable.

**Mechanism:** The market is being created by regulatory urgency, not by search demand. The ICP is not looking for an AI accountability advisor — they are encountering the accountability problem through carrier renewals, institutional questionnaires, and professional obligation updates. The provider who is present when the ICP encounters the problem owns the relationship.

**Implication:** The first-mover advantage is not about being first to publish content. It is about being present in the rooms where standards form and being present in the ICP's awareness when the triggering event occurs. Both require sustained presence, not campaign-based marketing.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The window isn't closing because a competitor is arriving. It's closing because regulatory development is accelerating. Every month that passes, more of the market has been formed without you in it."
