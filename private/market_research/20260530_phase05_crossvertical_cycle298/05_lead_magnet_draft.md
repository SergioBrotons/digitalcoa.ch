## The Core Problem

Every Geneva professional — managing partner, managing director, practice leader — is facing the same moment.

You signed something. You responded to a client questionnaire. You deployed an AI tool. You told your carrier you had adequate AI governance.

Now you're realizing that "adequate" isn't defined anywhere. That the documentation you produced doesn't meet the standard the form implied. That the signature on the attestation committed you personally, not just your firm.

This briefing is not about AI technology. It is about the accountability architecture you agreed to when you signed — and what you need to build before the next asking.

---

## What the Briefing Covers

**1. The retrospective exposure problem**
Every professional in Geneva who has signed a carrier form, responded to a client AI governance questionnaire, or deployed an AI tool has already created accountability exposure. The content of that commitment is only now becoming clear. We name what you signed and what it requires.

**2. The evidence gap**
The documentation standards implied by attestation forms and client questionnaires are unobtainable with current boutique internal processes. We explain why the gap is architectural — not procedural — and what change in documentation approach would close it.

**3. The personal liability layer**
Entity-level compliance and personal liability are different exposures governed by different standards. We name the personal liability provisions that apply to Geneva professionals and the documentation required to defend against them personally.

**4. The Swiss hosting assumption**
Data residency and processing location are different things. Most Geneva firms that selected Swiss-hosted AI tools are operating without required data processing agreements and with US-based API processing that creates exposure regardless of storage label.

**5. The distributed accountability problem**
Your associates, portfolio managers, sub-consultants, or staff are using AI tools you did not evaluate and cannot fully control. You are accountable for decisions made using those tools. We map where accountability sits in your organization and what governance structure closes the gap.

**6. The documentation window**
Organizations that have already built AI governance documentation have a present-tense competitive advantage. We explain why the window is still open and why it will not stay open indefinitely.
