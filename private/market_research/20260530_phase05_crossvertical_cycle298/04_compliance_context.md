## The Universal Regulatory Architecture

### nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)
**Status:** In force September 1, 2023. FDPIC enforcement posture live since May 2025.
**Applies to:** All four verticals — universal application.

Key provisions across all verticals:
- **Art.9:** DPA requirements with AI-specific processing obligations — violated by most firms using consumer AI tools without compliant agreements
- **Art.63:** Criminal liability ceiling (CHF 250,000) — personal, not just entity-level
- **Art.8:** Profiling notification obligations — applies to AI-assisted profiling in finance and consulting
- **Art.20/21:** Right to explanation for automated decisions — conflicts with proprietary methodology in finance

### FINMA Guidance 08/2024
**Status:** Operative since December 2024.
**Applies to:** Finance vertical primarily.

Seven-dimension framework: governance, inventory, data quality, testing, documentation, explainability, independent review. Proportionality guidance for smaller institutions absent.

### SAV Guidelines (June 2024)
**Status:** Published June 2024.
**Applies to:** Legal vertical.

Principles-based. No boutique-scale implementation path. Geneva Bar (Ordre des Avocats de Genève) has published nothing Geneva-specific.

### FMH Medical Professional Standards
**Status:** Ongoing.
**Applies to:** Healthcare vertical.

Human oversight requirement stated without operational definition. No Geneva-specific small practice methodology exists.

### EU AI Act / Council of Europe AI Convention
**Status:** Art.4 live since February 2025. Convention signed March 2025.
**Applies to:** Any Geneva firm with EU clients or EU nexus.

Extraterritorial obligations for Swiss firms serving EU clients or operating in EU market.

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Patterns

### Pattern 1 — The Guidance Vacuum Structure
Every vertical faces the same structural problem: the authority that should provide operational guidance has published only principles.
- Legal: SAV guidelines too general; Geneva Bar silent
- Finance: FINMA proportionality guidance absent; SROs silent
- Consulting: No Geneva-specific consulting AI governance guidance
- Healthcare: FMH human oversight stated without operational definition

The vacuum is not temporary — it is structural. The authorities publish principles because they cannot agree on operational specifics. The boutique is left to determine proportionality alone.

### Pattern 2 — The Attestation Architecture Mismatch
Carrier attestation forms are designed for underwriting risk evaluation. They create documentation that:
- Satisfies the carrier's current questions
- Does not satisfy criminal defense evidentiary requirements (nFADP Art.63)
- Does not satisfy professional standards documentation requirements
- May create self-incriminating evidence when reviewed retrospectively

The documentation built for attestation purposes may be architecturally inadequate for the exposure it was meant to address.

### Pattern 3 — The Personal Signature Activation
Across all four verticals, personal liability activates through signature:
- Legal: Managing partner signs carrier attestation, engagement letters, vendor agreements
- Finance: MD signs carrier attestation, institutional questionnaire responses
- Consulting: Partner signs engagement letters, sub-consultant agreements, vendor terms
- Healthcare: Physician signs carrier attestation, patient consent forms

The personal signature is the moment where entity-level exposure becomes personal exposure. The pre-signing review is the service that intercepts this activation.

### Pattern 4 — The Regulatory Timing Convergence
Multiple regulatory developments are converging on the same period:
- **nFADP:** Live since September 2023, enforcement maturing 2025–2026
- **FINMA 08/2024:** Operative since December 2024
- **EU AI Act Art.4:** Live since February 2025
- **Swissdamed:** Implementation approaching
- **AI Convention:** Switzerland signatory, implementation in progress

The boutique is facing multiple regulatory developments simultaneously — each requiring documentation, each with different evidentiary standards, each with different enforcement mechanisms.

## The Cross-Vertical Compliance Gap

The gap is not knowledge of regulations — the regulations are published. The gap is:

1. **Proportionate implementation:** What does "adequate" mean for a 3-attorney Geneva law firm? A 60-person wealth manager? A 10-person consulting boutique? A 2-physician practice?
2. **Personal liability architecture:** How does the managing professional protect personal assets from Art.63 criminal exposure?
3. **Evidence documentation:** How does the boutique produce contemporaneous evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions?
4. **Multi-mandate coherence:** How does the firm maintain coherent documentation across FINMA guidance, institutional questionnaires, nFADP ROPA, and carrier attestation simultaneously?
