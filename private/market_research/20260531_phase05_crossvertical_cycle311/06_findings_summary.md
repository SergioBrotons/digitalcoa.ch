### Finding 1: Carrier Form Is the Universal Design Specification — No One Reads It as Market Intelligence

**The insight:** In every vertical, the carrier form or client questionnaire arrives as a triggering event — and no professional in any vertical had the form reviewed before signing. The form is the design specification for the documentation the professional needs. But no competitor reads the carrier form as a market intelligence document that reveals exactly what evidence is required, in what format, before the signature creates the exposure.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal: Carrier renewal form asks matter-level AI questions. Managing partner signed without knowing the specific evidence the questions require.
- Finance: Carrier attestation and institutional DDQ are two different documents requiring different evidence. Managing director signed both without reading them as distinct intelligence documents.
- Consulting: Client AI questionnaire arrives as procurement trigger. Managing director answers under deadline pressure without reviewing what the questions commit him to.
- Healthcare: Physician-personal carrier attestation requires name and license number. Physician signs without reviewing what documentation supports the certification.

**The structural pattern:** The form is the design specification. The form names the specific evidence required. The professional signed the form without reviewing the evidence gap before signing. The exposure was created at signing — and only becomes visible when a peer mentions a carrier question, a client sends a questionnaire, or a colleague describes their experience.

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified carrier forms as triggering events. C311 Phase 05 identifies the specific structural pattern: the carrier form is a market intelligence document that reveals exactly what evidence is required — and no one reads it as such before signing. Pre-signing review is the highest-value intervention point, and it is zero-occupied.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Your carrier form is a market intelligence document. It reveals exactly what evidence your carrier requires — in what format, for what period, with what specificity. Read it before you sign it. I'll tell you exactly what you're committing to, and exactly what documentation you need to support it."

### Finding 2: Authorization Chain Is the Universal Liability Structure — Named and Unnamed

**The insight:** In every vertical, the professional is personally liable for AI use they did not authorize — through people (junior associate, vendor implementation, sub-consultant, medical assistant) and tools they did not directly control. The authorization chain is the universal liability structure across all four verticals. No competitor has named it as such. No competitor offers a proportionate authorization chain documentation framework for small Geneva firms.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal: Managing partner signed carrier attestation. Junior associate uses ChatGPT on personal device on firm matters. The authorization chain was never documented. The managing partner is personally liable.
- Finance: Managing director authorized the portfolio system. Vendor implemented an AI module within it. Associate activated it. The MD authorized the system — not the AI module. Both undocumented steps create personal liability.
- Consulting: Managing director authorized sub-consultant's engagement. Sub-consultant used AI tools on client's work. The delegation chain was never documented. The MD is personally responsible for the deliverable, including the AI component.
- Healthcare: Physician signed carrier attestation. Medical assistant uses AI tools on patient data. No delegation framework exists. The physician is personally liable for what the medical assistant does with patient data.

**The structural pattern:** The authorization chain runs from the signing professional through people and tools they didn't directly control. The professional is at the top of the chain. The AI use occurs at the bottom. The documentation is absent in the middle. The gap between top and bottom is the exposure — and it is personal.

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified authorization chain problems in specific verticals. C311 Phase 05 identifies it as the universal liability structure across all four verticals — structurally identical, only the terminology changes. The authorization chain documentation framework is one architecture with four vertical overlays. No competitor has built it.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "You're personally responsible for AI use you didn't authorize. Through people and tools you didn't directly control — junior associate, vendor, sub-consultant, medical assistant. That's the authorization chain. It's the same structure in every sector. And it's undocumented. That's the exposure. And it's personal."

### Finding 3: Documentation Debt Is Non-Linear — Linear Solutions Are Structurally Insufficient

**The insight:** In every vertical, the documentation debt is retrospective: 12–24 months of undocumented AI use. Linear solutions (policy for going forward, forward-only documentation practice) address only the future. The professional needs retroactive reconstruction for the period that already passed — and the solution must be credible and defensible under scrutiny, not fabricated.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal: Firm has used AI tools for two years without documentation. The 12–24 months prior to today are undocumented. Linear forward-only documentation practice leaves the past exposure intact.
- Finance: AI model has been running for 18 months without monitoring documentation. Model drift has occurred. The institutional client asks for evidence of monitoring. The evidence doesn't exist.
- Consulting: Firm has produced AI-assisted deliverables for 12–18 months without methodology documentation. The managing director cannot describe the AI's role in the work produced.
- Healthcare: Medical assistant has been using AI tools on patient data for over a year without delegation framework documentation. OaF survey asks for deployment dates and documentation. Only today's documentation exists.

**The structural pattern:** The documentation debt is retrospective and non-linear. Linear forward-only solutions address only the future — the past exposure remains. The professional needs retroactive reconstruction for the period that already passed. Retroactive reconstruction is technically possible but legally fragile if not done correctly — reconstructed records carry lower evidentiary weight than contemporaneous documentation. The solution must include both retroactive reconstruction (credible, not fabricated) and forward documentation architecture (designed for the next renewal cycle).

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified documentation debt as retrospective. C311 Phase 05 identifies the non-linear structure: linear solutions are structurally insufficient, not just incomplete. The retroactive path must be distinct from the forward path. Both are required. They are not the same solution.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "You've been using AI for 12–24 months without documentation. Linear solutions address only the future. The past exposure is still there. You need retroactive reconstruction — not fabricated evidence, but a credible, defensible record of what was reasonable at the time. And you need forward documentation architecture designed for your next renewal cycle. Both are required. They're not the same solution."

### Finding 4: "Adequate" Is a Universal Floating Signifier — Never Defined, Universally Paralyzing

**The insight:** The word "adequate" appears in every carrier form and every regulatory instrument across all four verticals. It has never been defined — not by any carrier, not by any regulator, not by any professional bar, not for a Geneva firm of any size in any sector. Every professional reads the word and has no reference point for what it means for their firm. The word "adequate" is the highest-value intervention point in every vertical — and no one has defined it.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal: Carrier form asks for "adequate" AI governance documentation. OaF guidance uses "adequate." No definition exists for a 3-attorney Geneva boutique.
- Finance: FINMA guidance uses "adequate." Institutional DDQ asks about "adequate" AI governance practices. No definition exists for a boutique WM firm.
- Consulting: Carrier form asks about "adequate" AI governance documentation. No definition exists for a 25-person Geneva consulting boutique.
- Healthcare: Carrier asks if the practice has "adequate" AI governance documentation for physician oversight. FMH uses "adequate" for human oversight. No definition exists for a small practice with 4 physicians.

**The structural pattern:** "Adequate" is a floating signifier across all regulatory instruments and carrier forms. The requirement exists; the standard does not. Every professional who signed the "adequate" attestation was exposed without knowing it — because the standard they were supposed to meet was never defined.

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified the "adequate" word as an anxiety trigger. C311 Phase 05 identifies it as the universal floating signifier — and provides the specific resolution: define "adequate" for each vertical as a concrete, proportionate, Geneva-specific documentation standard. The definition is the product. The definition is the service.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "The word 'adequate' appears on every carrier form in every sector. It has never been defined. That's not an accident — it's a gap. The requirement exists; the standard doesn't. Every professional who signed the 'adequate' attestation was exposed without knowing it. Adequate means: you can describe, specifically and in writing, for each AI system you use, who authorized it, how you review it, how your staff use it, and how you monitor it over time. That's the definition. And that's what your carrier is asking for when they ask if your governance is adequate."

### Finding 5: Commercial Enforcement Activates Before Regulatory — and the Gap Creates a Structural Window

**The insight:** In every vertical, the commercial consequence (mandate loss, coverage gap, client loss, procurement gate) activates before the regulatory consequence (FINMA inquiry, OaF guidance, Swissmedic classification, carrier audit). The professional loses the mandate before they hear from the regulator. The timeline is shorter than the professional thinks. And the regulatory instruments are not yet sufficiently developed to provide the documentation standard the professional needs — so the professional is caught between an immediate commercial problem and an incomplete regulatory framework.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal: Client questions about AI methodology activate before OaF issues formal guidance. The attorney faces client scrutiny before bar guidance arrives. The mandate is at risk before the regulator becomes a direct problem.
- Finance: Institutional DDQ activates before FINMA examination. The mandate is at risk before the regulator becomes a direct problem. The institutional client's questions are more specific than FINMA's principles.
- Consulting: Client AI questionnaire activates before nFADP enforcement. The procurement gate closes before the regulatory deadline arrives. The client deadline is immediate; the regulatory deadline is further away.
- Healthcare: Carrier form with physician-personal certification activates before Swissmedic formalizes operator classification guidance. The physician signs the attestation before the regulatory classification is clear. The coverage consequence is immediate; the regulatory consequence is pending.

**The structural pattern:** Commercial enforcement activates first in every vertical. The professional faces mandate loss, client loss, or coverage gap before regulatory enforcement becomes a direct problem. The regulatory framework is incomplete — "adequate" is undefined, human oversight is required but not defined — but the commercial consequence is already active. The structural window is: act before the commercial consequence activates, while the regulatory framework is still incomplete and the "adequate" standard is still being formed.

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified the enforcement sequence as a pattern. C311 Phase 05 identifies the structural window this creates: the professional is caught between an immediate commercial problem and an incomplete regulatory framework. The window is the period between commercial activation and regulatory formalization — and it is the period when the "adequate" definition can still be shaped by proactive documentation rather than retroactive compliance.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "The commercial consequence activates before the regulatory one. You'll lose the mandate, or the client, or the coverage — before the regulator becomes a direct problem. That's the sequence. And while you're waiting for the regulatory framework to become clear — the commercial pressure is already here. The window is now. That's when documentation has the most value: before the consequence activates, not after."

The structural identity means one evidence architecture serves all four verticals. The architecture is the same; only the specific overlay (carrier form language, client questionnaire format, regulatory standard, professional liability framing) changes.

**What holds across all four verticals:**
- Legal overlay: Carrier form language (matter-level, associate AI use, retrospective documentation), OaF guidance context, Swiss civil liability standard, fee agreement gap.
- Finance overlay: Institutional DDQ language, carrier attestation language, FINMA principles, nFADP Article 63 personal liability, multi-mandate coherence requirement.
- Consulting overlay: Client questionnaire language, carrier attestation language, engagement letter gap, nFADP Article 52 personal liability, sub-consultant delegation framework.
- Healthcare overlay: Physician-personal carrier form language, OaF survey language, Swissmedic operator classification, FMH human oversight requirement, medical assistant delegation framework, Swissdamed incident format gap.

**The structural pattern:** One evidence architecture is the product. The architecture addresses: (1) pre-signing carrier form review, (2) authorization chain documentation, (3) retroactive reconstruction, (4) "adequate" definition, (5) commercial document AI clause, (6) ongoing monitoring and review documentation. The four overlays are vertical-specific calibrations of the same universal architecture.

**Why this is NET-NEW (not in prior cycles):** Prior cycles identified the cross-vertical pattern in specific findings. C311 Phase 05 identifies the structural product advantage: one evidence architecture, four vertical overlays, as the core product design. The architecture is the moat. The vertical overlays are the calibration. The one-system approach is the competitive advantage that no competitor has built.

**Ready-to-use language:**

Only the specific form language changes. Your carrier form in your sector is the overlay. The evidence architecture underneath is the same. That's what makes this defensible — and that's what no competitor has built."
