# 01 — ICP Language: Cross-Vertical Patterns — How All Four Verticals Talk About AI Problems

**Phase:** C311 Phase 05 | **Cycle:** 311 | **Date:** 2026-05-31
**Verticals:** Legal · Finance · Consulting · Healthcare

---

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language: What Holds Across All Four

The four vertical ICPs (Geneva legal, Geneva finance, Geneva consulting, Geneva healthcare) share a common linguistic structure — not identical vocabulary, but identical underlying anxiety patterns. They use different words for the same problem. They respond to the same triggers. They make the same errors. The cross-vertical language is therefore not a translation exercise — it is a structural mapping.

---

## The Universal Entry Point: Carrier Form / Client Form / Procurement Form

**Every vertical arrives at the same moment:** a form arrives with the professional's name on it personally, requiring a signature, with a deadline.

**In legal:** "The carrier sent a renewal form. It has questions I can't answer. I signed it anyway."
**In finance:** "The institutional client sent a DDQ. It asks for things I don't have. I answered what I could."
**In consulting:** "The client sent an AI questionnaire with the contract renewal. Two weeks to respond."
**In healthcare:** "The carrier form now requires my name and license number personally. I signed it."

**What they all mean:** The form is the forcing function. The form is the moment when abstract AI governance becomes a concrete, personal, deadline-driven problem. No professional in any vertical initiated an AI governance project proactively. The form initiated it for them.

---

## The Universal Anxiety: "I signed something I don't understand"

**Legal ICP:** "On a signé un truc pour l'assureur. Je ne sais pas exactement ce qu'on a certifié."
**Finance ICP:** "I signed the carrier attestation. I also signed the DDQ response. Are those the same thing?"
**Consulting ICP:** "I signed something about AI at renewal. I don't know what it requires."
**Healthcare ICP:** "The carrier form now requires my personal certification. My name. My license number. I signed it. I don't have the documentation to support what I certified."

**The pattern:** Across all four verticals, the managing partner / managing director / physician signed a document that creates personal accountability — and does not have the documentation to support what they certified. This is not a legal-specific, finance-specific, consulting-specific, or healthcare-specific problem. It is a universal professional liability problem with a universal linguistic form.

---

## The Universal Structural Gap: Authorization Chain

**Legal:** "My junior associate uses ChatGPT on their personal device. I didn't authorize it. I signed the carrier form."
**Finance:** "The vendor implemented the AI module. My associate activated it. I authorized the portfolio system — not the AI module. Both steps are undocumented. I'm personally liable for all three."
**Consulting:** "I authorized the sub-consultant's engagement. I didn't authorize the AI tools they used. But I'm responsible for the deliverable — including any AI inside it."
**Healthcare:** "My medical assistant uses AI on patient data. I didn't authorize specific tools. I have no delegation framework. I'm personally liable for what she does with patient data."

**The pattern:** The authorization chain runs through people and tools the professional did not directly control — and creates personal liability for the professional who signed the attestation. The chain is: principal (signed) → agent (did the work) → AI tool (produced the output). The professional is at the top of the chain. The documentation is at the bottom. The gap between top and bottom is the exposure.

---

## The Universal Trigger: Pre-Signing Review Gap

**Every vertical:** "I signed before I knew what I was committing to."
**Legal:** "I signed the renewal form. Three weeks later I read the questions properly."
**Finance:** "I signed the carrier attestation. I also responded to the institutional DDQ. I didn't realize they were different documents requiring different evidence."
**Consulting:** "I signed the carrier form at renewal. The AI questions required documentation infrastructure I didn't have."
**Healthcare:** "I certified personally — my name, my license number — and I didn't review what documentation I had before signing."

**The pattern:** Pre-signing review is the highest-value intervention point in every vertical. No professional in any vertical had their carrier form or client questionnaire reviewed before signing. The service that reads the form before the professional signs it — identifies exactly what the questions require, exactly what documentation exists, exactly what gaps need to be filled — occupies the highest-value, lowest-competition point in the market.

---

## The Universal "Adequate" Problem

**Legal ICP:** "The word 'adequate' on the carrier form — no one has ever told me what it means for a 3-attorney Geneva firm."
**Finance ICP:** "FINMA gave principles. The institutional client gave questions. The questions are more specific than the principles. I don't know what 'adequate' means."
**Consulting ICP:** "The carrier form asks about AI governance adequacy. I don't know what 'adequate' looks like for a 25-person Geneva consulting boutique."
**Healthcare ICP:** "The carrier asks if we have 'adequate' AI governance documentation. I don't know what 'adequate' means for a small practice with 4 physicians."

**The pattern:** "Adequate" is a floating signifier across all four verticals. It has never been defined — not by any carrier, not by any regulator, not by any professional bar. Every ICP reads the word and has no reference point. The word "adequate" on every carrier form is the most important word in the market — and no one has defined it.

**Ready-to-use language for all four verticals:**
> "The word 'adequate' on your carrier form has never been defined — not by your carrier, not by FINMA, not by the OaF, not by Swissmedic, not by anyone. 'Adequate' for your firm in Geneva means: you can describe, in writing, for each AI system you use, who authorized it, how you review it, how you monitor it, and what your staff is authorized to do with it. That's the definition. Now the question is whether your documentation exists."

## The Universal Documentation Debt: 12–24 Months, Non-Linear

**Legal:** "We've been using AI for two years. I have no documentation from the beginning."
**Finance:** "The AI model has been running for 18 months. I don't know if it's still doing what it was designed to do."
**Consulting:** "We've produced AI-assisted deliverables for the past 18 months. I can't describe exactly what the AI did in the work."
**Healthcare:** "My medical assistant has been using AI tools for over a year. I didn't start documenting from day one."

**The pattern:** The documentation debt is retrospective across all four verticals. Linear forward-only solutions are structurally insufficient. The professional needs retroactive reconstruction for the period that has already passed — not just a policy for going forward. Retroactive reconstruction is technically possible but legally fragile if not done correctly.

---

## The Universal Commercial Document Gap: Fee Agreement / Engagement Letter / Client Questionnaire

**Legal:** "Clients are starting to ask directly about AI use in their matters. My fee agreements have no AI clause."
**Finance:** "The institutional client's DDQ is the commercial gate. If I can't answer it, I lose the mandate."
**Consulting:** "The client sent an AI questionnaire as part of the procurement process. My engagement letter has no AI clause."
**Healthcare:** "Patient communication about AI is becoming a trust question. No standard protocol exists."

**The pattern:** Every vertical has a governing commercial document that predates AI — fee agreement, engagement letter, client questionnaire, patient communication standard. The document now creates accountability the professional didn't know existed when they signed it. Adding an AI clause is a structural fix that creates defensibility going forward.

---

## The Universal Enforcement Sequence: Commercial Before Regulatory

**Legal:** "The OaF will issue guidance. When they do, the firms with documentation will be in a different position than the ones who waited."
**Finance:** "The institutional client asks before FINMA asks. The mandate is at risk before the regulator becomes one."
**Consulting:** "The client questionnaire is a procurement deadline. The regulatory deadline is further away. Which one do you respond to first?"
**Healthcare:** "Swissmedic operator classification is a real question now. The formal guidance isn't there yet. But the classification question is."

**The pattern:** Commercial enforcement (mandate loss, coverage gap, client loss, procurement gate) activates before regulatory enforcement (FINMA inquiry, OaF guidance, Swissmedic classification, carrier audit) in every vertical. The professional loses the mandate before they hear from the regulator.

---

## The Universal Persona: "The Form Has My Name On It"

**Legal ICP frame:** "I signed in my own name. Not the firm's. Mine."
**Finance ICP frame:** "Article 63 applies to me personally. Not the firm. Me."
**Consulting ICP frame:** "Article 52 is personal. I'm the responsible person. I signed in my own name."
**Healthcare ICP frame:** "The carrier form now requires my name, my license number, my personal attestation. I signed it personally."

**The pattern:** Every vertical has the same first response to AI governance anxiety: personal. The professional is not abstractly worried about "firm exposure." They are specifically worried about their own name on a document that creates personal liability. This instinct is accurate. The solution must address the personal liability dimension directly — not redirect to firm-level risk.

## Cross-Vertical Ready-to-Use Language

**For every first contact:**
> "The form arrived. It has your name on it. You signed it. The question now isn't whether you have a problem — it's whether you have the documentation to support what you certified."

**The universal "adequate" resolution (any vertical):**
> "The word 'adequate' has never been defined for a Geneva firm of your size in your sector. That doesn't mean you're not accountable for it. Adequate means: you can describe, specifically and in writing, what AI systems you use, who authorized them, how you review them, how your staff use them, and how you monitor them over time. That's the definition. Now — do you have that documentation?"

**The universal authorization chain explanation (any vertical):**
> "You authorized the firm's engagement. You didn't authorize every tool your staff used in executing it. But you're responsible for the output — including the AI component inside it. The chain runs through people and tools you didn't directly control. That's the exposure. And it's personal."

**The universal retroactive explanation (any vertical):**
> "Retroactive documentation is not the same as having documented at the time. If you've already signed an attestation without documentation — or already answered a client questionnaire with incomplete information — we need to build the record differently than if you're preparing for a future form. Both are solvable. But they're not the same solution."

**The universal enforcement sequence (any vertical):**
> "The commercial consequence comes before the regulatory one. You'll lose the mandate, or the client, or the coverage — before the regulator or the carrier becomes a direct problem. That's the sequence. And it means the timeline is shorter than you think."

## FR: Langage ICP Inter-Vertical (Français)

**Le point d'entrée universel :** Le formulaire est arrivé. Votre nom est dessus. Vous l'avez signé. La conversation commence là.

**L'anxiété universelle :** « J'ai signé quelque chose que je ne comprends pas pleinement, et je n'ai pas la documentation pour soutenir ce que j'ai certifié. »

**Le problème structurel universel :** La chaîne d'autorisation traverse des personnes et des outils que vous n'avez pas directement contrôlés — et crée une responsabilité personnelle pour vous, le professionnel qui a signé.

**Le problème universel du mot « adéquat » :** Le mot apparaît sur chaque formulaire du porteur et n'a jamais été défini pour une étude genevoise de votre taille dans votre secteur.

**La dette de documentation universelle :** 12 à 24 mois d'utilisation de l'IA non documentée. Les solutions linéaires sont structurellement insuffisantes. Une reconstruction rétroactive est nécessaire.

**L'écart universel du document commercial :** Votre document commercial directeur — accord d'honoraires, lettre de mission, questionnaire client, formulaire du porteur — précède l'IA et crée désormais une responsabilité que vous ne connaissiez pas.

**La séquence d'application universelle :** L'application commerciale (perte de mandat, trou de couverture, perte de client) s'active avant l'application réglementaire dans chaque secteur vertical.

**Le cadre de responsabilité personnelle universel :** « Vous avez signé en votre nom. Pas celui du cabinet. Le vôtre. »
