## Regulatory Context

### CARF/CRS 2.0 — Live Since January 1, 2026 (~3 months)
**Status**: ACTIVE NOW — ~3 months already operative as of March 30, 2026
**Obligation**: AI systems processing CRS-covered financial account data require documented governance
**WM relevance**: Portfolio analytics, automated reporting, AI-powered client reporting tools
**Gap**: Most boutique WM firms have not addressed CARF obligations for AI systems

### AMLA (Anti-Money Laundering Act) — H2 2026 (~3–6 months)
**Status**: Consultation machinery active (concluded January 30, 2026)
**Obligations**: 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register; enhanced due diligence for AI-assisted client onboarding
**SRO triggering activities**: Real estate transactions, non-operational entity establishment, domicile services
**FATF connection**: FATF mutual evaluation 2026–2027 elevates AMLA to international compliance event

### LETA (Lawyers and Trustees) — H2 2026 (~3–6 months)
**Status**: Same machinery as AMLA; H2 2026 timeline
**Obligation**: 30-day discrepancy reporting
**Scope**: Lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs under LETA

### FATF Mutual Evaluation — 2026–2027
**Status**: International pressure driver now active
**Implication**: Geneva WM firms face international AML scrutiny; AI governance becomes FATF examination topic

### nFADP Art. 5(c) — Active Since 2023
**Status**: ACTIVE NOW
**Obligation**: AI making automated decisions about individuals requires DPIA + human review mechanism

### EU AI Act GPAI — Active Since August 2, 2025 (~8 months)
**Status**: ACTIVE NOW
**Obligation**: Transparency documentation for EU-nexus firms
**High-risk**: August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months away)

### FINMA Guidance Vacuum
No FINMA 2026 specific AI circular. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 remains baseline. Confirmed via Phase 01 Cycle 16 research.

### Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)
**Status**: Hard deadline, no grace period
