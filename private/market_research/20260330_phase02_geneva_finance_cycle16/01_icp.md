# Phase 02: Geneva Finance/SMB — ICP
# Cycle 16 — 2026-03-30

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary Segment
Boutique wealth management firms in Geneva: independent asset managers, family offices, small-to-mid WM practices (typically 2–15 employees), often FINMA-regulated or SRO-affiliated.

### Core Problem
They use AI for portfolio analytics, client reporting, risk modeling, and AI-powered advice tools — without a governance framework. Three regulatory layers are converging simultaneously: CARF (live), AMLA/LETA (~3–6 months), and FATF evaluation (2026–2027). No FINMA-specific AI guidance exists for boutique WM.

### Regulatory Triggers (3-Layer Compliance Stack)

**Layer 1 — Swiss Domestic (Active)**
- nFADP Art. 5(c): Active since 2023 — AI making automated decisions about individuals requires DPIA + human review
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024: current baseline (no 2026 specific AI circular confirmed Cycle 16)

**Layer 2 — Swiss International (Active + Approaching)**
- CARF/CRS 2.0: Live since January 1, 2026 (~3 months already operative as of Mar 30, 2026)
- AMLA/LETA: H2 2026 (~3–6 months from late March 2026)
- FATF mutual evaluation: 2026–2027 (international pressure driver)

**Layer 3 — EU Extraterritorial (Active)**
- EU AI Act GPAI: Active since August 2, 2025 (~8 months live)
- EU AI Act high-risk: August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months away)

### ICP Statement
Geneva boutique wealth manager using AI for portfolio management, client reporting, or risk analysis, with no formal AI governance framework, that handles client financial data and may face CARF obligations now, AMLA/LETA obligations in ~3–6 months, and FATF evaluation scrutiny in 2026–2027.

### Addressable Market
~50–80 Geneva boutique WM firms (FINMA-licensed or SRO-affiliated; estimate 40–50% using AI tools)

### Competitive Absence (10th+ cycle)
No boutique Geneva advisor specializes in small WM firm AI governance.
FINMA has issued no 2026 specific AI circular.
Named competitors (AI Risk, MTF Solutions, TA Advisory Geneva) are in adjacent spaces.
Structural fact confirmed across 16 cycles.

### Timing Summary (from late March 2026)
- CARF: LIVE ~3 months (Jan 1 2026 baseline)
- AMLA/LETA: ~3–6 months (H2 2026)
- FATF: 2026–2027 (exact date TBC)
- Swissdamed: ~12 weeks (spillover into WM awareness)
- EU AI Act high-risk: ~4.5 months (Aug 2 2026)
