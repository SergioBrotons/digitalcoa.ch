- **FINMA AI monitoring:** Actively monitoring AI risks; survey Nov 2024-Jan 2025
- **EU AI Act implications:** Swiss managers with EU distribution face voluntary compliance pressure
- **ISO 42001:** AI management standard emerging as de facto framework
- **SFOMA** (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Act): Governs licensing for asset managers

### C115 Regulatory Updates (2026-04-04)
- **SECO sanctions enforcement escalation — criminal referrals + EU 16th-19th packages + Open Mineral gold case** — SECO referred two commodity trading investigations to the Federal Attorney General for criminal prosecution (Feb 2024, ongoing in 2025); Open Mineral (Baar/Zug) searched and investigated for Russian gold sanctions violations (Sep 2025); Switzerland adopted EU 16th sanctions package (May 2025 — strengthened crude oil transaction reporting), EU 18th package (Oct 2025 — petroleum product restrictions in third countries), EU 19th package (Dec 2025 — expanded sanctions lists); Geneva commodity traders face highest-ever compliance burden; sanctions evasion now carries material criminal risk; creates compliance program overhaul and ongoing screening advisory demand for Geneva FIs (Sources: SECO.admin.ch, DUANEMORRIS sanctions blog, Pestalozzi Law, Lenz Staehelin, swissinfo.ch)

### C113 Regulatory Updates (2026-04-04)
- **FINMA AI model risk governance maturing** — FINMA increasingly requiring documented model validation, backtesting, and human-oversight for AI-assisted investment decisions; Geneva AMs using AI for portfolio construction, risk scoring, or client profiling need formal model risk frameworks
- **Swiss sustainable finance taxonomy alignment** — EU Taxonomy binding for Swiss firms with EU nexus; Swiss Sustainable Finance Association developing Swiss green taxonomy; Geneva ESG funds need dual-compliance documentation
- **FINMA DLT/tokenization guidance operative** — tokenized assets and digital asset custody now under established FINMA guidance; SDX (SIX Digital Exchange) operational; institutional adoption accelerating; advisory demand in DLT asset classification, custody governance, and regulatory mapping
- **SFAO family office regulatory expectations emerging** — no formal license requirement but de facto supervisory expectations for multi-family offices managing institutional-adjacent capital; Geneva family offices need governance/compliance framework upgrades

## Regulatory Trends Affecting Geneva AMs

### 1. ESG Disclosure Requirements
- SFAMA mandatory ESG disclosure (2023-2024 implementation)
- SFDR-equivalent Swiss requirements for funds distributed in EU
- **Impact:** New compliance workload, client reporting changes, data infrastructure needs
- **Advisory opportunity:** ESG data management, reporting optimization, regulatory interpretation

### 2. DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)
- EU directive on operational resilience; Switzerland evaluating adoption
- **Impact:** Technology risk management, incident reporting, testing requirements
- **Advisory opportunity:** DORA readiness assessment, technology risk framework

### 3. AML / KYC Intensification
- Post-CS/UBS: Enhanced scrutiny on private banking client onboarding
- New FINMA AML guidance on beneficial ownership
- **Impact:** Slower client onboarding, higher compliance costs
- **Advisory opportunity:** AML process optimization, KYC technology assessment

### 4. Cross-Border Rules (CRD VI / AIFMD)
- CRD VI (June 2024): Swiss banks restructuring EU operations → ripple effects on AM distribution
- Swiss fund passporting into EU: Ongoing uncertainty
- **Impact:** Legal structure reviews, potential restructuring
- **Advisory opportunity:** Regulatory structure advisory, cross-border distribution

### 5. FINMA Technology Risk Guidance
- Updated operational risk circular including cyber and technology risks
- Cloud outsourcing governance requirements
- **Impact:** Technology vendor due diligence, data residency requirements
- **Advisory opportunity:** Vendor assessment, cloud governance framework
