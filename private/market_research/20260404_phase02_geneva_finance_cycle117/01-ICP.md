# 01 — ICP: Geneva Asset Managers & Family Offices

## Target Segment: Geneva-Based Asset Managers / Wealth Managers

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** CHF 500M–10B+ AUM (mid-to-large); single family offices typically CHF 100M-1B
- **Revenue:** Management fees + performance fees; revenue correlates directly with AUM
- **Locations:** Geneva primary (Lac Léman corridor: Geneva-Lausanne); some Zug for crypto/alternative
- **Clientele:** UHNWI, family offices, institutional investors, pension funds (for larger AMs)
- **Structure:** Independent asset managers (IAMs), multi-family offices (MFOs), single-family offices (SFOs), bank-affiliated

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CIO / Investment Director | Senior executive, often founder or long-tenured | Portfolio analytics, benchmark attribution, risk reporting |
| CEO / Managing Partner | Business leader | Client reporting, operational scalability, regulatory compliance burden |
| COO / Operations | Non-investment executive | Middle/back office efficiency, data management, vendor oversight |
| Risk Manager | Dedicated role (larger firms) | Regulatory risk reporting, stress testing, ESG risk |
| Client Reporting Lead | Relationship manager + operations | Custom client reporting, PDF generation, client portal management |

### Known Geneva Examples
- **Large independent AMs:** Reyl & Cie (Geneva, CHF 20B+ AUM), Bordier & Cie (Geneva, multi-family office), Girardet (Geneva)
- **Multi-family offices:** VZ VermögensZentrum (Zurich/Geneva), Swiss Rock (Zurich-based, Geneva presence)
- **Bank-affiliated AM arms:** UBS Wealth Management (Geneva), Credit Suisse (post-merger), Pictet (Geneva HQ, CHF 600B+ AUM)
- **Family offices:** Lombard Odier (Geneva, CHF 270B+ AUM), Naturally these also serve UHNWI clients

### Pain Points (Initial Hypotheses)
1. **Regulatory reporting burden** — FINMA, SFAMA, AIFMD-equivalent Swiss rules creating compliance overhead
2. **Client reporting fragmentation** — multiple custodians, multiple asset classes, multiple formats
3. **Data management silos** — portfolio data in Excel, CRM, custodian systems not integrated
4. **Technology adoption lag** — smaller IAMs still using legacy systems; AI adoption nascent
5. **Talent shortage** — difficulty recruiting quant/tech talent in Geneva for AM operations
6. **Fee compression** — competitive pressure on management fees driving cost efficiency focus

### Buying Triggers (Initial Hypotheses)
- AUM growth requiring operational infrastructure upgrade
- FINMA examination finding compliance gaps
- Key personnel departure exposing knowledge silos
- New regulatory requirement (AIFMD, SFAMA ESG rules)
- Board/investor demand for better reporting

### Buying Barriers (Initial Hypotheses)
- Risk aversion toward cloud/proprietary systems
- Data privacy concerns (client data in external systems)
- Vendor lock-in with existing custodian/platform providers
- Long sales cycles (3-12 months for strategic advisory)
- Decision-makers are often owners (founders) with strong opinions

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva-based IAMs and family offices
- English essential for international investor-facing roles
- German useful but not required for Geneva
- Relationship-driven, trust-first sales approach
- Formal but collegiate tone
