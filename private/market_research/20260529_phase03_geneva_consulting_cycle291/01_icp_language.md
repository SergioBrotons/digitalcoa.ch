## How They Talk About AI Problems (English)

### Primary Concern Framing

These are not technology conversations. They are **accountability conversations**.

When a Geneva managing director or partner says "AI," what they actually mean is: **"something that might come back to me personally."**

The language they use is legal, personal, and risk-focused — not tool-focused.

---

### Core Vocabulary (How They Actually Say It)

| What We Might Say | How They Say It |
|---|---|
| AI governance | "keeping control of what we're responsible for" |
| AI tool deployment | "putting something in front of a client" |
| Carrier attestation | "the insurance form asking if we're using robots" |
| Proposal defensibility | "can I stand behind this in front of a board" |
| Sub-consultant AI delegation | "sending work to someone who uses AI — am I liable" |
| Client AI questionnaires | "that form the client sent — I don't know what they're really asking" |
| Methodology documentation | "proving we didn't just run a prompt" |
| Personal exposure | "my name is on the door — it's my liability" |
| AI output verification | "how do I know what came out is right" |
| Compliance documentation | "having a paper trail that protects me, not exposes me" |

---

### Exact Phrases They Use

> *"The client sent us their AI questionnaire. I filled it in, but I don't know if what I wrote is going to come back to haunt me."*

> *"We're a 12-person firm. If I send something out with AI-generated content and it's wrong, it's my name on it."*

> *"I need to be able to show a client — if they ask — exactly how we produced this deliverable. I can't do that if I don't know what the tool actually did."*

> *"Our sub-consultant says they're using AI. Fine. But if their AI produces something wrong and we delivered it, who is responsible?"*

> *"The carrier asked us about AI use in our proposal. I said we use it carefully. That was the safest answer I could think of."*

> *"I'm not against AI. I just need to understand the liability before I put it in front of a client."*

> *"The methodology question — that's the one that keeps me up at night. Can I defend this if a client challenges it?"*

> *"Everyone talks about AI efficiency. Nobody talks about what happens when it goes wrong and it's your signature on the deliverable."*

---

### The Geneva-Specific Layer

Geneva-based consultants speak with a particular kind of **precise liability awareness**. They are accustomed to:

- **Banking-adjacent discretion** — the expectation that what you do with client data is documented and defensible
- **Regulatory scrutiny** — not panic, but the assumption that at some point, someone will ask
- **Personal name risk** — many are SARL or Sàrl structures where the managing director is personally exposed

This is not abstract risk aversion. It is **operational lived experience** translated into language.

---

### Tone Markers

- They say "I" — not "we" — when discussing personal liability
- They ask "what does this mean for me" before "how do I use it"
- They respond to concrete scenarios more than abstract frameworks
- They are skeptical of AI evangelism and deeply receptive to someone who acknowledges the real risks first

---

### Questions They Ask Before Engaging

1. "Will this expose me to more liability than it saves?"
2. "Can I document what we did if a client asks?"
3. "Does this create an audit trail or destroy one?"
4. "What's my personal exposure if this goes wrong?"
5. "Does this comply with Swiss law and nFADP — not just EU AI Act?"
6. "If I delegate to a sub-consultant using AI, am I still responsible?"
