# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Small Consulting Market
## Phase 03 — C291 | DigitalCoa.ch Market Research

---

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

### Current Market Offerings (What Exists)

#### 1. Generic AI Governance Frameworks

**What it is:** Large consulting firms (Big Four, major strategy houses) have published AI governance methodologies. These are:
- Designed for large enterprises with dedicated compliance teams
- Language is corporate and regulatory
- Not adapted to small boutique operations
- Not calibrated to Swiss regulatory context
- Priced for enterprise consumption

**The gap:** Small Geneva firms cannot implement these frameworks. They don't have the people, the time, or the legal budget to operationalize materials designed for multinationals.

---

#### 2. EU AI Act Compliance Resources

**What it is:** A proliferation of EU AI Act guides, compliance checklists, and legal interpretations. Available from:
- Law firms (expensive retainers)
- Industry associations (generic, not boutique-specific)
- Online publications (surface-level, not actionable)
- AI tool vendors (biased toward their own products)

**The gap:** None of these are written from the perspective of a 12-person Geneva consulting firm. None address the specific liability questions managing directors face. None are bilingual EN/FR. None are calibrated to Swiss regulatory frameworks (nFADP, not just GDPR).

---

#### 3. Professional Liability Carrier AI Provisions

**What it is:** Carriers have added AI-related questions to proposal and renewal forms. Some have published general guidance on AI use.

**The gap:** The carrier guidance is defensive and minimal. It tells firms what they can't do without explaining what they should do. It creates documentation requirements without providing a framework to meet them. It asks binary questions about complex, nuanced AI deployment contexts.

---

#### 4. Sub-Consultant AI Tools (Uncontrolled)

**What it is:** Sub-consultants and freelancers are actively using AI tools. Some disclose this; many don't. The market has no standard for AI disclosure in consulting subcontracting relationships.

**The gap:** Primary firms have no framework, no contract language, no verification mechanism for managing sub-consultant AI use. The risk is entirely unmanaged.

---

#### 5. Generic "AI Strategy" Consulting

**What it is:** Consultants offering AI strategy services to other consultants and professional services firms.

**The gap:** These offerings are typically:
- Technology-forward, not liability-forward
- Focused on efficiency gains, not risk management
- Priced at a level that doesn't make sense for a 12-person firm
- Not adapted to the Geneva regulatory environment

---

### What's Missing (The Market Gaps)

#### Gap 1: Boutique-Specific AI Governance

**Missing:** A governance framework specifically designed for small professional services firms (2–50 people). Not enterprise governance scaled down — a fundamentally different approach built for:
- Managing directors who are personally accountable
- Small teams without dedicated compliance staff
- Client-facing work where AI use needs to be disclosed and defensible
- Swiss regulatory context (nFADP, not just GDPR)

**Opportunity:** An offer that speaks directly to the managing director's liability concerns, in their language, calibrated to their firm's size and risk profile.

---

#### Gap 2: Client AI Questionnaire Support

**Missing:** A service that helps consulting firms respond to client AI governance questionnaires. Not legal advice, but:
- Framework for understanding what questions are actually asking
- Language for answers that are honest and protective
- Documentation that supports the answers given
- Process for ongoing questionnaire management

**Opportunity:** A white-glove support service that transforms a confusing, anxiety-inducing experience into a manageable, documented process.

---

#### Gap 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework

**Missing:** Contract language, disclosure requirements, and verification processes for managing AI use in the sub-consultant relationship. Something a small firm can actually implement.

**Opportunity:** Standard contract clauses, AI disclosure forms, and a lightweight audit process that fits a boutique operation.

---

#### Gap 4: Methodology Documentation System

**Missing:** A practical system for documenting how deliverables were produced — including AI-generated components. Not an academic framework, but a working process that fits into actual project delivery.

**Opportunity:** A methodology documentation template and process that a consultant can use in a real project, not just reference in a compliance manual.

---

#### Gap 5: Bilingual EN/FR Content in Managing Director Voice

**Missing:** Content written specifically for Geneva managing directors — in English and French — that addresses their actual concerns in their language. Not marketing content. Not thought leadership. Direct, practical, liability-focused guidance.

**Opportunity:** A content library that speaks to this ICP with precision and credibility. This is a trust-building asset, not just a marketing channel.

---

#### Gap 6: Personal Liability Awareness (nFADP)

**Missing:** Clear, practical guidance on nFADP obligations for small consulting firms — specifically addressing:
- When a managing director is personally liable
- What documentation is required
- What AI tool usage creates data protection obligations
- How to document compliance without a dedicated DPO

**Opportunity:** A practical guide that cuts through the regulatory complexity and focuses on what a 12-person firm actually needs to do.

---

### Competitive Positioning Insight

The market is not empty of AI governance content. It is empty of **boutique-specific, liability-focused, Swiss-calibrated, personally accountable AI governance for small consulting firms.**

Every existing solution is either:
- Too enterprise for a 12-person firm
- Too technology-focused for a liability-focused buyer
- Too generic for a Swiss-specific context
- Not in the language the managing director thinks in

**The positioning opportunity:** Speak to the managing director's specific fear, in their specific context, with specific, actionable, implementable guidance.
