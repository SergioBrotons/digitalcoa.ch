## How Physicians Talk About AI Problems (C309)

### Primary ICP Language Pattern

**The Core Frame:** "I signed my carrier's AI attestation and didn't realize my physician license was on the line personally."

This is the entry point. Physicians do not think of themselves as "AI governance customers." They think of themselves as physicians whose license is at risk.

### The Five Simultaneous Pressures (C309 Frame)

Physicians in Geneva are managing five pressures that all converge on their personal medical license:

1. **FMH human oversight stated, undefined in practice**
   - Physician language: "FMH says I need to maintain human oversight. They don't explain what that means in concrete terms. I read the guidance three times — it's principles, not protocol."
   - This is distinct from finance (FINMA defines adequate) or legal (SBA has procedures). For physicians, there is no FMH-published protocol for AI oversight in small practice.

2. **Carrier attestation shifting from practice-level to physician-personal**
   - Physician language: "My carrier sent me a form with my name on it. It's not the practice — it's me, personally, signing that I understand how AI is being used."
   - This is the carrier form sophistication escalation (C309 Phase 01 finding) applied to healthcare — now physician-personal certification.

3. **Efficiency-liability paradox**
   - Physician language: "Every AI tool I use makes me more efficient and more exposed. If I document every AI-assisted decision, I spend all my time documenting. If I don't document, I'm exposed."
   - Core tension: AI makes practice sustainable but creates documented accountability gaps.

4. **Staff AI tool use on patient data — invisible accountability gap**
   - Physician language: "My medical assistants use AI tools. I authorized the tools. But I don't know what they're putting through it, and I'm the one who's responsible."
   - The physician is personally liable for staff AI tool use on patient data. No operational system exists to manage this.

5. **Swissdamed registration — ambiguous operator obligations**
   - Physician language: "My AI tool vendor says they're 'certified.' Does that mean I'm compliant? Does it mean I'm a medical device operator under Swissmedic? Nobody has explained this to me in terms I can use."

### The Physician-to-Physician Channel

Physicians do not learn about AI incidents through regulatory notices. They learn through peer networks:

- **How physicians hear about problems:** "A colleague called me. She had a patient incident and Swissmedic sent inquiries. She didn't know her AI tool was a medical device requiring registration."
- **How physicians share solutions:** Medical society conversations, AMGe network, peer referrals — not broker channels.
- **Trust formation:** "I trust what my colleague tells me about their experience. I don't trust a consulting firm's brochure."
- **Decision trigger:** "I heard from three people at the last AMGe event that carriers are now asking about AI use during credentialing. That's when I started taking this seriously."

### The Peer Question Sequence (How Physicians Probe Each Other)

1. "Have you signed your carrier's new AI attestation form? What did you put?"
2. "Did you understand what you were certifying when you signed?"
3. "Do you have a protocol for documenting your AI oversight?"
4. "Did you check if your AI tools are medical devices under Swissmedic?"
5. "What did your medical assistant actually use AI for last week — do you know?"

These questions circulate through medical society networks, AMGe events, and informal peer conversations.

### The Evidence Language (Not Framework Language)

Physicians do not respond to "AI governance framework." They respond to:

- "Evidence of your oversight"
- "Documentation that your judgment was involved"
- "A record that shows you knew what the AI was doing and why you agreed"
- "A Swissmedic-compliant device registration record"
- "Your carrier form with your signature and documentation to match it"

### The Personal License Frame

**Core positioning anchor (C309 confirmed):** "For physicians, evidence is personal."

This is not a marketing line. This is the structural difference between healthcare and all other verticals. A wealth manager's license can be suspended. A physician's license can be suspended — and the patient safety consequences are immediate and irreversible.

### What Physicians Are NOT Saying

Physicians are not saying:
- "I need an AI governance framework" — this is consultant language, not physician language
- "I want to understand the regulatory landscape" — abstract, not personal
- "Can you help me with my AI strategy?" — strategy is not the immediate concern
- "I need a comprehensive compliance program" — compliance is not the frame

Physicians ARE saying:
- "How do I document that I was actually overseeing the AI without spending all my time on documentation?"
- "My carrier is asking questions I don't know how to answer — what do I put on the form?"
- "If Swissmedic sends me an inquiry about my AI tool, what do I show them?"
- "My medical assistant uses AI on patient data — how do I know what's going through?"
- "If something goes wrong and a patient is harmed, what documentation do I need to protect myself?"

### Bilingual Reality

Geneva physicians operate in both French and English. The AMGe network is French-dominant. International physicians (WHO, UN, HUG) operate in English. Content must be genuinely bilingual — not translated, but designed for both.

**French physician language sample:**
- "Le PFPDT a confirmé en mai 2025 que la nFADP s'applique directement au traitement par l'IA. Mais qu'est-ce que ça signifie concrètement pour mon cabinet?"
- "Swissmedic me demande d'enregistrer mes dispositifs médicaux. Mon outil d'IA est-il un dispositif médical? Comment je le sais?"
- "Ma responsabilité médicale personnelle est engagée. Qui me dit ce que 'supervision humaine' signifie dans la pratique?"

**English physician language sample:**
- "I signed the carrier form. Now I'm realizing I don't have the documentation to support what I signed."
- "My AI tool vendor says they're Swissmedic compliant — but what does that mean for my personal liability?"
- "If Swissmedic sends me a letter about my AI tool, what do I show them? What's the standard?"

### Decision Trigger Sequence (C309)

1. **Peer incident:** A colleague had a Swissmedic inquiry on their AI tool. Word spreads through AMGe.
2. **Carrier attestation received:** New form with physician's name, not just practice name.
3. **swissdamed deadline approaching:** July 2026 registration deadline creates urgency.
4. **Staff AI use observation:** Medical assistant using AI in ways physician doesn't track.
5. **Medical society discussion:** AMGe event where peers discuss AI incidents.
6. **Credentialing question:** Carrier asks about AI use during credentialing renewal — physician realizes they need documentation.

### The Urgency That Works (C309)

"The form your carrier sent you is the specification. Your signature is personal. Your documentation must match your signature."

"Swissmedic doesn't ask if you have a framework. They ask: is your device registered? Do you have operator compliance documentation?"

"For physicians, evidence is personal. And your license is on the line."
