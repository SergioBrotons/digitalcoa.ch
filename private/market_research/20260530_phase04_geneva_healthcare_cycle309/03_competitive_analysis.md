### C309 Assessment: Why Existing Competitors Don't Address the New Signal

**The physician-personal certification sub-space is unaddressed.**

**This is the gap that matters in C309.** The market has moved from practice-level AI governance to physician-personal accountability. No competitor has repositioned for this.

## Competitive Gap Analysis (C309)

### The New Competitive Sub-Space

**"Physician-personal AI accountability documentation for small Geneva practice"**

| Need | Current Provider | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Carrier attestation documentation matching physician signature | None | No one explains what "matching documentation" means for physician-personal forms |
| Swissmedic operator classification for small practice | Swissmedic self-service | No advisory explains what "operator" means for a 3-physician practice using Dragon Medical |
| Staff AI delegation accountability system | None | No practical system for tracking medical assistant AI tool use on patient data |
| FMH human oversight operational protocol | None | No published small practice protocol for what FMH-compliant human oversight looks like |
| Post-incident documentation format | None | No standardized Swiss primary care AI incident documentation format |
| Evidence architecture for physician license protection | None | No service positions "evidence" as the primary product vs. "framework" |

### Why No Competitor Has Moved

1. **Healthcare regulatory complexity** — requires deep nFADP + Swissmedic + FMH knowledge. Most AI governance consultants are tech-focused, not healthcare regulatory-focused.

2. **Small practice economics** — CHF 2,200 engagement is too small for enterprise consultancy. Medical society networks require relationship building, not cold outreach.

3. **Physician trust barrier** — physicians trust peer recommendations. New entrant must build reputation through content + AMGe network, not marketing.

4. **Bilingual requirement** — Geneva requires EN + FR. Most competitors are EN-only or FR-only.

5. **Liability stakes** — physician license is on the line. The advisory must demonstrate deep healthcare regulatory knowledge, not generic AI governance.

## First-Mover Window Status (C309)

**Window:** Open and narrowing.

What has changed in C309:
- Carrier forms have shifted to physician-personal certification — creating new documentation requirements competitors don't address
- swissdamed July 2026 deadline is 13 months away — urgency is real but understanding is low
- Physician peer incident network is activating — Swissmedic inquiries are circulating through AMGe

What has not changed:
- No boutique competitor has repositioned for physician-personal accountability
- Large consultancy has not launched SMB healthcare AI practice
- IT vendors remain tool-centric
- Legal firms remain opinions-only

The window remains open because the competitive repositioning required is non-trivial: deep healthcare regulatory knowledge + evidence architecture product + physician trust network + bilingual capability. This combination does not exist in the current competitive set.

## Broker Channel in Healthcare Context

**Note:** Physician peer network operates differently from the broker referral channel dominant in finance/legal.

- **Primary channel:** AMGe peer network, medical society events, colleague referrals
- **Secondary channel:** Medical IT vendors (not broker referrals)
- **DigitalCoa approach:** Content that circulates through AMGe network; peer recommendation from established physician contacts

This is why lead magnet distribution through medical society network is the primary acquisition path in healthcare — not broker referral as in finance/legal verticals.
