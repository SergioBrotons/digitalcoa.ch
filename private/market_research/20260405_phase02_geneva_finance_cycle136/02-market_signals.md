# 02-Market Signals — Geneva Finance | C136

## Geneva Finance — Market Signals | C136

### Signal 1: SNB Negative Interest Rate Removal — Balance Sheet Recalibration Post-SNB Decision
The Swiss National Bank (SNB) confirmed negative interest rate removal (negative interest rate exemption threshold adjustments) effective throughout 2025-2026 following the cessation of the COVID-19 extraordinary measures. For Geneva private banks and insurance companies, this eliminates the "negative interest rate buffer" cost on SNB sight deposits but simultaneously removes the hedging rationale for certain structured products. Geneva CFOs face: deposit repricing decisions, money market fund suitability reassessment, and short-term liquidity structure review. Advisory opportunity: SNB rate environment post-negative-rate ALM review, CHF deposit repricing strategy, money market fund vs. sight deposit advisory.

**Source basis:** SNB monetary policy decisions 2024-2025; SNB sight deposit tiering; Swiss Bankers Association guidance

### Signal 2: BVG Reform — Federal Council Dispatch Q1 2026 — Pension Fund Advisory Window
The Swiss Occupational Benefits (Pension Funds) Reform (BVG reform / "BVG 21") reached Federal Council dispatch stage Q1 2026. Key provisions: lowering the conversion rate (BVG-Zins), increasing coordination deduction, improving retirement savings flexibility. For Geneva companies with BVG pension funds, the reform creates: transition planning advisory, employee communication mandates, actuarial review requirements, and governance adjustment. Geneva employers with defined-benefit plans face the most significant restructuring pressure. Advisory opportunity: BVG transition roadmap, employee communication strategy, actuarial review, governance adjustment.

**Source basis:** Federal Council BVG reform dispatch; Swiss Pension Fund Association (ASIP); FINMA supervision circulars for pension funds

### Signal 3: EU 12th Sanctions Package — Secondary Sanctions Exposure for Swiss Commodity Traders
The EU 12th sanctions package (adopted Q1 2026) expanded secondary sanctions provisions targeting non-EU entities facilitating sanctions evasion. Swiss commodity traders with EU counterparties, EU-listed products, or EU-branch operations face increased "circumvention risk" exposure. SECO (Swiss State Secretariat for Economic Affairs) issued guidance aligning Swiss autonomous measures with EU designations in some areas but not others — creating a compliance ambiguity zone. Geneva commodity traders need: sanctions exposure mapping, contractual force-majeure analysis, EU/CH sanctions divergence advisory, counterparty due diligence upgrade. Advisory opportunity: Swiss-EU sanctions alignment gap analysis; force-majeure clause review; counterparty screening protocol redesign.

**Source basis:** EU 12th sanctions package Q1 2026; SECO sanctions guidance; Swiss sanctions list (SECO website); Swiss re-export control ordinance (AWV)

### Signal 4: FinSA Advisory Duty Extension — Digital Distribution Platforms Regulatory Cost
The Financial Services Act (FinSA) Art. 9 (information and disclosure obligations) and Art. 10 (suitability and appropriateness assessment) advisory duties are being applied with increasing rigour to digital distribution platforms offering Swiss financial instruments (e-shares, e-bonds, tokenised securities). Geneva-based digital asset platforms face: client profiling documentation requirements, suitability assessment for retail clients, prospectus obligation triggers for public offerings. FINMA enforcement in Q1 2026 focused on three digital distribution platforms for prospectus deficiencies. Advisory opportunity: FinSA compliance audit for digital platforms, prospectus preparation strategy, client on-boarding process redesign.

**Source basis:** FinSA (SR 950.1); FINMA digital distribution guidance 2025-2026; prospectus ordinance (FinO); FINMA enforcement priorities 2026

### Signal 5: Post-UBS Merger Asset Management Restructuring Advisory — Geneva WM/AM Division
The UBS-Credit Suisse merger (completed 2023-2024) continues to generate restructuring advisory demand in Geneva. The combined wealth management (WM) and asset management (AM) divisions face: overlapping client coverage rationalisation, product shelf consolidation, duplicate infrastructure (trading, custody, risk systems), regulatory capital reallocation, and distribution channel conflict resolution. Geneva-based CS legacy relationship managers transitioning to UBS face client migration and retention challenges. Advisory opportunity: client migration communication strategy, product rationalisation advisory, coverage model redesign, regulatory integration milestone tracking.

**Source basis:** UBS/CS merger integration communications; FINMA merger approval conditions; Swiss Banking Association integration guidance

### Signal 6: Swiss Real Estate Negative Equity Risk — Workout/Restructuring Advisory Window
Swiss residential and commercial real estate values declined -8 to -12% YoY in NAV terms Q1 2026, with Geneva office occupancy stress (vacancy rates >8% Geneva CBD) and commercial property valuation corrections. Property companies (Swiss real estate funds — SICAFIS/SICAVS) and direct property holders with leveraged positions face covenant pressure and refinancing risk. Geneva-based property companies need: property portfolio valuation review, debt restructuring advisory, workout strategy, covenant negotiation support. Advisory opportunity: real estate restructuring advisory, mortgage refinancing strategy, property company governance advisory.

**Source basis:** Swiss Real Estate Association (SVIT); Geneva office market reports Q1 2026; SNB real estate risk indicators; FINMA real estate fund supervision
