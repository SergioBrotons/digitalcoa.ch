# 01-ICP.md — Geneva Finance

## Geneva Finance — ICP Definition

### Primary ICP: CFO / Finance Director / Treasurer
- **Sectors:** Geneva private banks, commodity trading houses, insurance companies (Vie/Lloyd's branches), family offices, IO treasury functions (UNOG, ITU, WHO)
- **Role:** CFO, Group Treasurer, Head of Finance, Head of ALM, Head of Risk
- **Pain:** Regulatory capital management, FX volatility, SNB rate environment, post-merger integration complexity, sanctions compliance, payment system migration
- **Trigger events:** FINMA examination cycle, SNB rate decisions, balance sheet stress, cross-border transaction complexity, ESG reporting mandates
- **Buying behaviour:** Trusted-advisor relationship over vendor; independent analysis over bank/product pitch
- **Language:** EN-primary for international; FR for regulatory/domestic

### Secondary ICP: Finance Function Consulting Buyers
- Boards of Geneva foundations (Art. 80ff ZGB) requiring financial governance
- Geneva NGOs with >CHF 10M annual budget needing finance function advisory
- Geneva SME owners with complex multi-entity treasury needs

### What This ICP Does NOT Want
- Generic Swiss finance content
- Product-selling disguised as advisory
- UK/US-centric analysis without Swiss regulatory nuance
- Surface-level summary without actionable implication

### Strategic Implication
Finance decision-makers respond to: concrete SNB/FINMA data points, quantitative implications, specific action steps. They are evidence-driven, skeptical of generalisations, and time-constrained. Content must be precise, data-grounded, and Geneva-specific.
