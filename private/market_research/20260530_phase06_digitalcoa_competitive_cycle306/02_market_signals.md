## Purpose

Document what Geneva AI accountability service providers claim to offer versus what they actually deliver to SMB clients. The gap between claim and delivery is the white space. It is also the primary source of ICP disillusionment — the principal who hired a competitor and still doesn't have evidence when the carrier asks.

## Market Signal 01: International Consultancies — Beautiful Frameworks, No Evidence

**What they claim:**
- "Comprehensive AI governance framework designed for your organization"
- "End-to-end AI compliance advisory with implementation support"
- "Swiss market expertise with Geneva circuit knowledge"
- "Boutique-appropriate methodology scaled to your practice"
- "Practical tools your team can implement immediately"

**What they actually deliver:**
- 40–80 page framework document describing what adequate AI governance looks like in theory
- Gap analysis identifying what the firm doesn't have
- Implementation roadmap requiring internal compliance resources the firm doesn't have
- General Swiss regulatory context without Geneva-specific circuit calibration
- One-time assessment with no ongoing evidence production methodology
- Pricing calibrated to CHF 80,000–200,000 for enterprise delivery

**The evidence gap:**
The framework document answers the question "what should our AI governance look like?" The carrier's fourth-generation form asks "can you show us evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions?" These are different questions. The framework doesn't answer the carrier's question. The principal paid CHF 100,000 for a document that describes the destination — and produces no evidence of the journey.

**Market signal:** Geneva boutique engagement with enterprise frameworks consistently fails at implementation. The principal receives the framework, reads it, understands it intellectually, and has no capacity to execute it on Tuesday morning. The framework becomes a reference document in the compliance cabinet — consulted never, referenced in anxiety.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** The evidence gap between what the framework describes and what the carrier asks for is a discrete engagement. The bounded vendor contract review (2 weeks, specific deliverable) + carrier attestation preparation (CHF 2,200) converts the framework into evidence without replacing the framework.

## Market Signal 02: Geneva Law Firms — Legal Analysis, Wrong Layer

**What they claim:**
- "AI governance advice integrated with your professional obligations"
- "Article 321 CC expert guidance for law firms"
- "Professional secrecy compliance for AI-assisted client services"
- "Regulatory compliance advisory with legal privilege protection"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Legal analysis of what Article 321 CC requires (the theoretical standard)
- Legal opinion on vendor DPA adequacy (does the contract meet legal requirements?)
- Reference to SAV guidance (principles without Tuesday translation)
- No evidence production methodology for carrier attestation
- No human decision authorization documentation system
- No operational implementation for running practice operations

**The evidence gap:**
The law firm can tell you what Article 321 CC requires in theory. They cannot produce the contemporaneous documentation of human authorization that the carrier's attestation actually asks for. The legal opinion is a document about what the law says. The carrier asks for evidence of what the practice actually did.

**The structural problem:** The law firm's AI governance work is legal analysis — it operates in the legal register. The carrier's attestation operates in the documentation register. The law firm produces documents that satisfy the legal analysis requirement. They don't produce documents that satisfy the carrier's documentation requirement.

**Market signal:** Geneva law firms are receiving AI governance questions from their own clients and don't have the methodology to answer them operationally. The trusted advisor doesn't have the trusted operational answer. The law firm principal who has legal analysis and no evidence is in the same position as the wealth manager principal.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Evidence architecture briefing (operational, not legal) + carrier attestation preparation (documentation layer) = what the law firm can't produce for themselves and what the principal needs before the carrier asks.

## Market Signal 03: IT Security Firms — Tool Security, Wrong Layer

**What they claim:**
- "AI tool security assessment and vulnerability analysis"
- "Vendor due diligence for AI system deployment"
- "Technical AI governance and control assessment"
- "Cybersecurity compliance for AI environments"
- "AI data protection and privacy controls"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Security assessment of AI tools (are they vulnerable? can they be exploited?)
- Technical review of vendor infrastructure (is their environment secure?)
- Penetration testing for AI systems
- Data protection technical controls assessment
- No calibration for professional liability context
- No carrier attestation preparation
- No human decision authorization documentation

**The evidence gap:**
The IT security firm answers the question "is this AI tool safe to use?" The professional liability framework asks "is your use of this AI tool documented as authorized by a human?" These are different questions. The IT security firm confirms the tool is safe. The carrier asks whether the principal authorized the specific decision to use the tool in the specific context.

**Market signal:** The AI tool that passes the security assessment can still create professional liability exposure if its use wasn't documented as human-authorized. The tool being secure is not the same as the use being accountable. The IT security firm produces evidence that the tool is safe. The carrier wants evidence that the principal is accountable.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Carrier attestation preparation (human authorization documentation, not tool security assessment) + evidence architecture (the accountability layer, not the security layer).

## Market Signal 04: Data Protection Consultancies — FADP Layer, Wrong Standard

**What they claim:**
- "Complete nFADP compliance for AI systems"
- "AI data protection impact assessments"
- "Privacy-by-design for AI tool deployment"
- "FADP Art.9 compliant DPA drafting and review"
- "GDPR/nFADP compliance for AI-assisted processing"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Privacy policy updates (what the firm tells data subjects about AI processing)
- Processing register entries (what the firm has in its inventory)
- DPIA documents (risk assessment for AI processing)
- Standard FADP-compliant vendor DPAs
- No Article 321 CC calibration for law firm professional secrecy
- No carrier attestation preparation for AI governance adequacy
- No human decision authorization documentation

**The evidence gap:**
The data protection layer and the professional liability layer operate on different documentation standards and different evidentiary requirements. The FADP-compliant documentation satisfies the FDPIC's requirements. The carrier's attestation asks for evidence of human authorization — a different standard, a different artifact.

**Market signal:** The ICP who has FADP-compliant documentation may still fail the carrier's attestation review. The FADP attestation and the carrier's AI attestation are two different documentation products. No current provider addresses both simultaneously for the Geneva boutique context.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Vendor contract review that maps FADP obligations AND professional liability exposure simultaneously + carrier attestation that satisfies both evidentiary standards.

## Market Signal 05: Online Platforms — Templates Without Operators

**What they claim:**
- "AI governance made simple"
- "Automated compliance for AI tools"
- "Complete AI compliance toolkit for professional firms"
- "Step-by-step governance with document templates"
- "Self-service AI compliance for SMBs"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Template libraries (policies, procedures, documentation frameworks)
- Self-service assessment tools (checklist-style gap identification)
- Training modules (awareness, not implementation)
- Generic frameworks requiring boutique-scale adaptation
- No Geneva circuit calibration
- No carrier form language expertise
- No operator to execute the documentation — only a platform to guide execution

**The evidence gap:**
The template is not the evidence. The self-service tool produces a document describing what the firm should have. It does not produce the contemporaneous record of what the firm actually did. The principal who uses the platform has a better policy than before — and no evidence for the carrier.

**Market signal:** Platform adoption by Geneva boutiques consistently fails at the documentation execution stage. The solo practitioner has no one to operate the platform. The principal who buys the platform subscription has the tool. They still don't have the evidence. The platform produces documentation about what should exist — not records of what actually happened.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Turnkey evidence production — the operator produces the documentation, not the platform user. The solo practitioner who needs an operator has no viable alternative in the current market.

## Market Signal 06: Professional Associations — Guidance Without Referral

**What they claim:**
- "Member guidance on AI governance best practices"
- "Professional standards for AI use in your practice"
- "Resources and tools for AI accountability"
- "Training and education on AI compliance"
- "Member support for AI governance questions"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Principles documents (use AI responsibly, document adequately, ensure human oversight)
- Workshop sessions (awareness, 1–2 hours, CE credits)
- Reference to external advisors for implementation
- No Tuesday translation for boutique practice operations
- No carrier attestation preparation
- No evidence production methodology

**The evidence gap:**
The association guidance creates the obligation (adequate AI governance) without providing the implementation pathway. The guidance is free. The implementation requires paid external help. The association publishes guidance and refers members to external advisors — making the guidance a demand generator for the advisory market rather than a substitute for it.

**Market signal:** The institution acknowledges that members need external support. The guidance is positioned as free information that creates demand for paid implementation. The association doesn't compete with boutique advisors — it markets for them.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Circuit participation (being known by the institutional gatekeepers who write the guidance and receive the member questions) produces the referral relationship. The advisor who is present at institutional events becomes the natural referral destination for the member who reads the guidance and needs implementation help.

## Market Signal 07: Insurance Brokers — Problem Identification Without Solution

**What they claim:**
- "Complete insurance solutions for AI risk"
- "Professional liability coverage for AI-assisted services"
- "Guidance on AI coverage requirements"
- "Risk assessment and coverage placement"
- "Claims support and renewal administration"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Insurance product placement (coverage identification, policy placement)
- Annual renewal administration
- Claims support after events
- No AI governance methodology or advisory
- No carrier attestation preparation
- No pre-signing review of attestation language

**The quadruple bind:**
The broker must identify inadequate AI governance to place coverage appropriately (broker's first function). The broker cannot advise on AI governance without creating new liability exposure for themselves (advisory creates liability). The broker knows clients need documentation they cannot specify (problem identification without solution language). The broker cannot refuse to place coverage based on undocumented AI governance (obligation vs. commercial interest).

**Market signal:** The broker sees the problem before anyone else. The broker cannot solve the problem. The broker must refer. The named broker relationship is the primary referral pathway for the Geneva SMB AI accountability market.

**Serviceable by DigitalCoa:** Named broker relationships (not sales relationships — circuit relationships) + specific engagement format for broker referrals ("Send them to me for the vendor contract review, I'll give them what they need in two weeks"). The broker who can name you specifically (not "find someone" but "call Sergio Brotons") has solved their referral problem. That solution is a permanent channel.

## The Net Market Signal: Seven Competitor Categories, Zero Evidence Production

**Across all seven competitor categories, the pattern is consistent:**
- All claim AI governance coverage
- None produce evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions
- None calibrate documentation to fourth-generation carrier form language
- None operate at the solo practitioner scale with a turnkey evidence production model
- None address the vendor contract review as a bounded entry engagement
- None provide bilingual EN + FR delivery with Geneva circuit calibration

**The evidence gap is universal across the competitive landscape.** The white space is not a partial gap — it is a complete absence of evidence production services in a market where evidence is the only thing that protects the principal's name when the carrier asks.
