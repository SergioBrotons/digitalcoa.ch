# ICP Language: Geneva Legal — C306 Phase 01

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems — C306

---

### The Geneva Legal ICP

Boutique firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based. Managing partner personally accountable. Signed carrier attestation without understanding what it committed them to. No documentation of AI use in any matter. Junior associates more fluent with AI than managing partner. Bar guidance vacuum. Client questions about AI now arriving at intake. "Adequate" undefined.

---

## ICP Language Patterns — C306

### The Carrier Signature Problem
- "I signed it at renewal. Like every year."
- "I didn't read every line — it's insurance paperwork."
- "Then I found out the signature was personal. Not the firm's. Mine."
- "I didn't know I was signing something that could affect my license."
- "The broker handed it to me, I signed, that was it."

### The Broker Role Problem
- "The broker told me it was standard. Just sign here."
- "They know insurance. I trusted them to tell me if there was a problem."
- "They didn't explain that the form creates a personal standard."
- "Now I find out they also didn't know — or they did and didn't say."
- "The broker is also my client's broker. How do I separate their advice from mine?"

### The Evidence Architecture Problem
- "I have an AI policy. It's on the shelf."
- "The carrier asked for evidence. I don't have any."
- "The policy isn't evidence — it's just words."
- "Evidence is what you actually did. What you documented. When."
- "I have no documentation of anything I actually did with AI."

### The Tool Sprawl Problem
- "We started with one tool. Now there are four."
- "Different associates use different tools. I don't track which."
- "Every new tool is a new vendor. A new server. A new contract I didn't read."
- "The tool sprawl happened organically. No one decided to have five AI tools."
- "I don't know how many we're using now. It's not in any system."

### The Audit Anticipation Problem
- "I keep waiting for the letter."
- "Not the carrier audit — I can prepare for that. The real audit."
- "What if a client complaints? What if the Ordre asks? What if FINMA?"
- "I don't know which authority would actually come after me first."
- "They're all asking questions. None of them define what I should have done."

### The French/English Operational Bifurcation
- "My French clients ask different questions than my English ones."
- "The French clients assume I know the local rules. They don't ask about AI."
- "The English-speaking clients have read more about AI risks. They ask more."
- "I have to explain the same uncertainty in two languages."
- "The bilingual complexity adds another layer to every conversation."

## French ICP Language — C306

### Le problème de la signature personnelle
- "J'ai signé à la renovación. Comme chaque année."
- "Je n'ai pas lu chaque ligne — c'est un document d'assurance."
- "Puis j'ai découvert que la signature était personnelle. Pas celle du cabinet. La mienne."
- "Je ne savais pas que je signais quelque chose qui pouvait affecter ma licence."

### Le problème du courtier
- "Le courtier m'a dit que c'était standard. Signez ici."
- "Il connaît l'assurance. Je lui ai fait confiance pour me dire s'il y avait un problème."
- "Il n'a pas expliqué que le formulaire crée une norme personnelle."

### Le problème de l'architecture de preuve
- "J'ai une politique IA. Elle est sur l'étagère."
- "L'assureur a demandé des preuves. Je n'en ai pas."
- "La politique n'est pas une preuve — c'est juste des mots."

### Le problème de la prolifération des outils
- "Nous avons commencé avec un outil. Maintenant il y en a quatre."
- "Chaque nouvel outil est un nouveau fournisseur. Un nouveau serveur. Un nouveau contrat que je n'ai pas lu."

### L'anticipation de l'audit
- "J'attends la lettre."
- "Pas l'audit d'assureur — je peux m'y préparer. Le vrai audit."
- "Qu'est-ce qui se passe si un client se plaint ? Si l'Ordre demande ? Si FINMA ?"

## The Emotional State — C306

The C306 ICP is not just worried about having signed something they don't understand. They are now worried about:
- Whether the broker had an obligation to explain the form's implications
- What the evidence of their actual AI use would show if an audit arrived today
- How many AI tools are now in use across their practice without oversight
- Which regulatory authority would be the first to make a formal demand
- How to explain their AI governance situation to French-speaking clients who assume they already know the local standard

The service that speaks to this layered anxiety — broker accountability, evidence architecture, tool sprawl, multi-regulator sequencing — in the ICP's own language gets the call.

The framing shift from C305: the ICP is past "what did I sign?" and into "what do I do now, and who do I call first?"
