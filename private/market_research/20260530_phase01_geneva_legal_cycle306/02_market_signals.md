# Market Signals: Geneva Legal — C306 Phase 01

## Current Problems, Complaints, Failures — C306

---

## Signal 01: Broker Accountability Gap Named as Discrete Problem

**The signal:** Geneva managing partners are beginning to ask whether their broker had an obligation to explain the carrier attestation form's personal liability implications before facilitating the signature. The broker typically presents the form at renewal as routine administrative work. The form requires the managing partner's personal signature — not a firm signature. The broker collects the signed form and submits it. The managing partner now faces a documented personal commitment to an undefined standard, signed without broker explanation of what the commitment meant.

**The question being asked:** "Did my broker have an obligation to explain this to me? Or were they just processing paperwork?"

**Market gap:** No Geneva broker has published or communicated a methodology for explaining carrier form personal liability implications to attorney clients. The broker's E&O coverage may not extend to explanation obligations on forms they didn't design. The attorney-broker relationship creates a trust vector that is structurally different from an attorney's relationship with their own professional advisor — but no one has mapped where that difference becomes legally material.

**Implication:** Broker liability review as a pre-audit due diligence step is a discrete service. Understanding whether the broker's facilitation created additional exposure — or whether the broker's failure to explain constitutes a separate liability — changes the defense architecture.

## Signal 02: Evidence Architecture Deficit — Not Policy Gap

**The signal:** The market is beginning to understand that AI governance policies are not evidence. A policy states what should happen. Evidence documents what did happen. Carrier attestation forms are architected to collect evidence — representations about what the firm actually documented — not policy statements. Managing partners with AI governance policies on file are discovering that the policy does not satisfy the carrier's evidence request. The documentation they built is the wrong documentation type.

**The question being asked:** "I have a policy. Why isn't that enough?"

**Market gap:** No Geneva service differentiates between policy documentation and evidence documentation for AI governance purposes. The service that produces evidence — contemporaneous AI use records, supervision documentation, vendor review logs, decision authorization chains — is architecturally different from the service that produces policy documents. The market confusion about which type of documentation satisfies which type of demand is creating a specific service gap.

**Implication:** Evidence architecture design is a discrete service distinct from policy template delivery. The output is records, not documents.

## Signal 03: Tool Sprawl as Uncontrolled Organic Proliferation

**The signal:** Small Geneva firms did not decide to adopt multiple AI tools. The proliferation happened organically — associates brought tools, different practice areas adopted different solutions, free trials became standard subscriptions. The managing partner did not approve the proliferation and cannot track what is in use across the practice. The firm now has a tool inventory problem they didn't create intentionally. Every tool added a new vendor contract, a new data processing relationship, a new server location, and a new Article 321 exposure they never evaluated.

**The question being asked:** "How many AI tools are we actually using? I don't know."

**Market gap:** No Geneva service offers AI tool inventory documentation specifically for small firms where the inventory developed organically without central oversight. The service requires: identifying all tools in use, documenting vendor contracts and data processing relationships, assessing Article 321 compliance for each, and producing a consolidated inventory that satisfies carrier evidence requests.

**Implication:** Tool inventory documentation is a discrete bounded service — and it surfaces the deeper problem: the vendor contracts were never reviewed against professional secrecy obligations.

## Signal 04: Multi-Regulator Audit Anticipation Sequence Problem

**The signal:** Geneva managing partners are now aware that multiple authorities could make demands related to their AI use: the carrier at renewal, the Ordre des Avocats de Genève if a complaint arises, FINMA if financial services are involved, the FDPIC if data processing is challenged, or a cantonal court in litigation. The question is not whether something could happen — it is which authority would arrive first and what each would ask for. The anticipation is not a single event but a sequence risk.

**The question being asked:** "If the Ordre asks, what do I show them? If FINMA asks, what do I show them? Are they asking for the same thing?"

**Market gap:** No Geneva service maps which documentation satisfies which regulatory authority's demand. The documentation architecture that protects against carrier exposure may not protect against Ordre disciplinary proceedings or civil discovery. The cross-regulator documentation compatibility problem is unnamed in the market.

**Implication:** Cross-regulator evidence architecture — documentation that satisfies multiple authority types simultaneously — is a discrete service. The managing partner needs to know that a single evidence architecture protects across carrier, Ordre, and litigation discovery simultaneously.

---

## Signal 05: French/English Client Communication Bifurcation Creating Separate Exposure Moments

**The signal:** Geneva small firms serve both French-speaking and English-speaking clients. The two groups are asking different questions about AI in different languages, creating a communication burden the firm is not equipped to manage. French clients assume the attorney knows the local Geneva standard and do not probe AI questions. English-speaking clients — often more informed about AI risks from international media — ask more specific questions about how their data will be used, whether AI will be involved in their case, and what the firm's AI governance policy is. The firm cannot give a consistent answer in either language because they do not have a defined AI governance standard to communicate.

**The question being asked:** "How do I explain my AI governance to clients who ask in two different contexts?"

**Market gap:** No Geneva service addresses the bilingual client communication problem for AI governance. The engagement letter template that satisfies French client expectations is not the same template that satisfies English-speaking client expectations, and neither has been specifically designed to address AI governance disclosure in a manner that protects the firm in both linguistic contexts.

**Implication:** Bilingual client-facing AI governance communication — engagement letter clauses, intake disclosures, client communication templates — is a discrete service. The output must work in both linguistic contexts with different implicit client expectations.

---

## Signal 06: Post-C306 Cycle Audit Visibility as New Anxiety Vector

**The signal:** Managing partners who have now been through one carrier renewal cycle with a signed AI attestation are experiencing a new anxiety: the knowledge that they signed a form creating a documented standard, and that the carrier now has that form on file. The carrier could ask for evidence at any renewal. The next renewal is not in their control. The documentation gap they accepted at signing is now a documented gap on record with their insurer. They cannot undo the signature. They can only build backward-looking evidence in a context where backward-looking evidence is structurally difficult to produce.

**The question being asked:** "The form is already signed. The gap is already there. What do I do now?"

**Market gap:** No Geneva service specifically addresses the post-signature evidence reconstruction problem — building defensible documentation architecture for AI governance when the original signing moment has already created a documented standard the firm cannot demonstrate it met. The service requires: reconstructing what AI use occurred before documentation existed, building forward-looking evidence architecture, and creating a documented response that demonstrates current state without claiming the past was different from what it was.

**Implication:** Post-attestation evidence reconstruction is a discrete service with a specific framing: not "undo the past" but "document what actually happened and build what comes next."
