# Phase 03 Findings Summary — Geneva Consulting (Small Boutique)
## Cycle 288 (2026-05-28)

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Client Questionnaire Portfolio Accumulation Effect
Geneva consulting boutiques receiving multiple AI governance questionnaires from multiple clients simultaneously, each processed as an isolated event. The portfolio-level picture — cross-client AI governance commitment inconsistency — is invisible to the firm because no tracking system maps it. Each response commits the firm to something slightly different; the accumulation creates an inconsistency exposure the firm does not know it has already created.

**Market language:** *"Three clients sent questionnaires in the same quarter. I answered them separately. I don't know if my answers are consistent."*

**Ready-to-use language:** Portfolio-level AI governance commitment tracking is a discrete service category — distinct from questionnaire response — that the Geneva consulting market has not built.

---

### Finding 2: Carrier Attestation Post-Signature Realization
MDs sign third-generation carrier AI attestation forms under time pressure, then realize they have made personal commitments to undefined adequacy standards. The pre-signing review service does not exist in the Geneva consulting market. The carrier form is provided; the review is not. Post-signature anxiety emerges without a clear reassessment path.

**Market language:** *"I signed the carrier attestation form. Then I realized it was a personal commitment to a standard that nobody has defined."*

**Ready-to-use language:** Pre-signing carrier AI attestation review is a discrete engagement type — entering the market exactly when third-generation attestation forms are arriving in MDs' inboxes. The service exists at the intersection of personal liability exposure and form receipt.

---

### Finding 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Three-Party Chain Without Authorization Architecture
The three-party AI delegation chain — client → boutique MD → sub-consultant → AI tool — is operating without documented authorization architecture. Engagement letters predate AI adoption; they have no AI delegation clause. nFADP Article 9 third-party processor obligations flow from boutique to sub-consultant without mapping. No proportionate framework defines what adequate sub-consultant AI disclosure to the prime requires. No engagement letter mechanism resolves the structural incompatibility between vendor AI terms and boutique controller obligations.

**Market language:** *"When my sub-consultant uses AI in our joint deliverable, whose deliverable is it?"*

**Ready-to-use language:** Three-party AI delegation authorization is a discrete engagement type: engagement letter architecture review and update for AI delegation exposure, including nFADP Article 9 processor chain mapping.

---

### Finding 4: AI Model Silent Update Propagation Into Active Client Deliverables
AI model silent updates — standard vendor practice — alter analytical frameworks embedded in client deliverables without announcement. The MD cannot stand behind methodology or conclusions that may have changed since analytical work was conducted. Engagement letters have no version-locked deliverable standard. Version provenance documentation for AI-influenced analytical conclusions is structurally absent.

**Market language:** *"I described my analytical approach in my proposal. AI may have changed it since I wrote the proposal."*

**Ready-to-use language:** Active mandate version provenance protocol is a discrete service for boutique consulting firms with active client engagements. It addresses the version dependency problem before any model update produces a question the firm cannot answer.

---

### Finding 5: Documentation Window Close as Burden-of-Proof Inversion
The documentation window is a present-tense opportunity becoming a past-tense constraint. Post-window, the burden of proof inverts: MD must demonstrate adequacy of AI governance practices, not merely engagement with the question. Retroactive reconstruction of reasoning not documented during the window is practically impossible. The MD who uses the window to wait for guidance faces a compounding problem: decisions made without documentation, and a window that has closed on the evidence.

**Market language:** *"I think I'm waiting for the same guidance everyone else is waiting for. Nobody seems to have it."*

**Ready-to-use language:** The documentation window is a positioning tool — not a compliance deadline. Using it strategically means generating evidence during the window that establishes what the MD evaluated, when, and why. The service is the documentation architecture that makes past reasoning reconstructions valid — before the window closes on the evidence.

---

### Finding 6: Geneva Accountability Vacuum as Structural Amplification Mechanism
Geneva management consultants have no sector association with AI governance guidance authority. No SVA equivalent. No ORDRE equivalent. No institutional path to trigger formal responses regardless of question validity. Carrier questionnaires function as de facto operative standard by default — designed without Geneva consulting input, adapted from institutional contexts. The vacuum amplifies five other problems by distributing aggregate market pressure across individual MDs who have no collective response pathway.

**Market language:** *"My carrier's questionnaire is the closest thing to a standard I've seen. I think that's a problem."*

**Ready-to-use language:** The Geneva consulting accountability vacuum is the structural opportunity: a Geneva peer forum mechanism — or a first-mover advisory position that bridges the vacuum before institutions arrive — captures the standard-setting function for a sector with no current claimants.
