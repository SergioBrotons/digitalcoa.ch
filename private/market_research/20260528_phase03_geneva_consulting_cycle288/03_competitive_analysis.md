## What's Missing in the Market

### Market Gap Inventory

**Gap 1: Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review Service**
No Geneva service offering systematic pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation forms for small consulting boutiques. The MDs sign under time pressure and discover post-signature that they have made personal commitments to undefined adequacy standards. The market provides the form but not the review. The pre-signing review service is a discrete named service category that does not exist in the Geneva consulting market.

**Gap 2: Cross-Client AI Governance Commitment Mapping**
No service manages cross-client AI governance commitments for small consulting boutiques. Each questionnaire is processed as an isolated event. The portfolio-level accumulation effect — multiple clients, multiple questionnaires, inconsistent commitments — is invisible to the firm because no tracking system maps it. Cross-client commitment management is a service category gap.

**Gap 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Authorization Framework**
No framework exists in the Geneva market defining what adequate sub-consultant AI disclosure to the prime consultant requires. No engagement letter architecture addresses three-party AI delegation (client → boutique → sub-consultant → AI). No procurement standard exists for evaluating sub-consultant AI practices. The prime boutique is exposed in the delegation chain with no operational tool for managing it.

**Gap 4: Analytical Framework Version Management for Active Mandates**
No service offers version-locked analytical methodology for active client mandates in which AI models may update between proposal date and deliverable conclusion. Engagement letters have no version-locked deliverable standard. No version provenance documentation is available to preserve what analytical framework produced which conclusion for which client at which point in time. This is an unnamed discrete service for boutique consulting.

**Gap 5: Geneva Management Consulting AI Accountability Peer Forum**
No peer forum exists for Geneva management consultants to observe standards forming and share AI governance practices. The accountability vacuum for management consultants is institutionally permanent — no SVA equivalent, no ORDRE equivalent, no Geneva professional association with AI governance mandate. A Geneva consulting AI accountability forum or working group serves the entire sector and does not exist.

**Gap 6: Client Questionnaire Response Architecture**
Geneva consulting boutiques receive AI governance questionnaires from clients and have no response architecture for them. They field each questionnaire as an event requiring an ad-hoc answer. The portfolio-level commitment implications of each response are invisible. A structured questionnaire response methodology — mapping each answer to existing commitments, flagging consistency issues, and managing the portfolio-level picture — is a discrete service that does not exist in the Geneva consulting market.

## Competitive Assessment

**Technology vendors** (AI contract analysis, proposal platforms, GRC tools) are enablers. They address internal tooling, not the MD's personal accountability exposure. They do not compete with a boutique advisory service that provides pre-signing review, delegation framework, client questionnaire management, and version provenance documentation.

**Regulatory compliance advisors** (Abilene, large compliance firms) serve institutional clients with structured regulatory mandates. They are not adapted to small Geneva consulting boutiques where:
- The managing director is personally in scope
- The firm's AI practices are informal and evolving
- The document infrastructure is limited
- The exposure is defined by carrier forms and client questionnaires, not by regulatory calendars

**Geneva peer networks** provide informal peer exchange but no institutional guidance, no structured methodology development, no accountability framework.

**What the market has not built and is ready to receive:**
A boutique-appropriate AI governance advisory service for 2–50 employee Geneva consulting firms — addressing the MD in the room, the carrier form in the inbox, the sub-consultant chain in the operating model, the questionnaire portfolio, the version dependency in the active mandate, and the documentation window.
