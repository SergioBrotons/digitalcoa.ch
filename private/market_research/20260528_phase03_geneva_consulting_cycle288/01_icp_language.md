# Phase 03 ICP Language — Geneva Consulting (Small Boutique)
## Cycle 288 (2026-05-28)

## ICP
Small consulting boutique, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable. Concerned about client AI governance questionnaires, proposal methodology defensibility, carrier attestation, sub-consultant AI delegation, and personal exposure under nFADP.

---

## How the ICP Actually Talks

### On Client AI Governance Questionnaires

- *"My client sent me a 30-page AI governance questionnaire. I don't know what they want or how to answer it."*
- *"I think they sent this to all their consultants. I don't even know if mine is the only one or if they're building a benchmark."*
- *"My proposal went out three months ago. Now they want to know what AI I used. I signed it. I can't remember what I said I'd use."*
- *"They're asking about methodology. I've never had to document methodology like this before."*
- *"I answered their questions but I don't know if what I wrote is defensible. I don't know against what."*
- *"The client wants AI governance documentation. I don't know what category of document they mean."*
- *"I sent an AI methodology paragraph in my proposal. Now they're asking follow-ups about it. I don't know what I committed to."*
- *"Three clients sent questionnaires in the same quarter. I answered them separately. I don't know if my answers are consistent."*
- *"The questionnaire came in as part of a vendor audit. Now I'm part of their AI governance supply chain somehow."*

### On Carrier Attestation

- *"My carrier sent a form. It requires my personal signature. I don't understand what I'm attesting to."*
- *"I signed the carrier attestation form. Then I realized it was a personal commitment to a standard that nobody has defined."*
- *"My PI carrier wants me to confirm my AI use doesn't create exposure. I can't confirm what I don't know about."*
- *"The form says I'm responsible for AI decisions I make in client work. I'm not sure I've made any explicit decisions."*
- *"Signing the carrier form felt like accepting liability for things that aren't clear to me."*
- *"My broker said the attestation is now standard. I don't know if that's true or if they're telling me that so I'll sign it."*
- *"After signing, I started wondering whether I was covered or whether I just confirmed I wasn't."*
- *"The carrier form asks about AI methodology. I don't think they're equipped to evaluate what I write."*
- *"I want to review the form before signing it. Nobody offered me that. I just got a signature request."*
- *"I think I signed something related to AI. I'm not certain what I confirmed."*

### On Sub-Consultant Delegation

- *"I work with independent contractors. I don't know what AI they're using. I'm not sure I need to know."*
- *"I delegated part of the analysis to a sub-consultant. They used AI. I don't know which AI or how."*
- *"When my sub-consultant uses AI in our joint deliverable, whose deliverable is it?"*
- *"I have no framework for what I can ask my sub-consultants about their AI use."*
- *"I brief sub-consultants on my methodology. AI is changing my methodology and I haven't updated the brief."*
- *"If my sub-consultant uses an AI tool that produces a flawed recommendation, am I responsible for it?"*
- *"I have verbal agreements with my sub-consultants. There's no AI use clause in any of them."*
- *"I hired a specialist for part of an engagement. They told me they used AI. I didn't ask which one or how."*
- *"I don't think sub-consultant AI use is my responsibility. But I signed something to my carrier that suggests it might be."*
- *"The chain of delegation confuses me. Client to me to sub-consultant to their AI. Who owns what?"*

### On Proposal Defensibility

- *"I wrote AI-assisted methodology into my proposals starting this year. I don't know if what I wrote is what I actually do."*
- *"I can't stand behind every AI tool mentioned in my proposals. I use them opportunistically."*
- *"My client asked about AI methodology in the proposal stage. I gave a generic answer. Now they want specifics."*
- *"I pitch my methodology as distinctive. But AI is making methodology harder to distinguish between firms."*
- *"I described my analytical approach in my proposal. AI may have changed it since I wrote the proposal."*
- *"My proposal says "evidence-based analysis." AI produces evidence. I don't know what attribution that chain requires."*
- *"I don't know what the standard for proposal defensibility is. I don't know who's setting it."*
- *"My client referenced a section of my proposal in a follow-up question. I barely recognized what I'd written."*
- *"The proposal I sent two years ago has AI references. I'm not sure they meant anything at the time."*
- *"I don't think what I write in proposals is what clients are evaluating me on. But it creates a record."*

### On Personal Liability Under nFADP

- *"I know nFADP is live. I don't know what it means for my consulting practice specifically."*
- *"The law firm advising me on nFADP doesn't understand what consulting firms do with AI."*
- *"I read about nFADP Article 9 third-party processing obligations. My sub-consultants might be third parties. I'm not sure."*
- *"I'm a sole practitioner. Every liability is personal. AI adds a category I can't quantify."*
- *"I read that personal liability under nFADP is real. I don't know what it looks like in practice for consulting."*
- *"The FDPIC hasn't issued guidance specific to consulting firms. That means there is none."*
- *"I think nFADP applies to my firm. I don't have a methodology for determining how."*
- *"I know GDPR-adjacent thinking from EU clients. Swiss nFADP is different but I don't know where."*
- *"I keep reading that accountability is personal for managing directors. Nobody tells me what that specifically requires."*
- *"Switzerland signed the AI Convention. I don't know what that means for my current engagements."*

## Distinct Phrase Patterns

### Urgency Without Language
The ICP does not say "I have an AI governance problem." They describe situations where problems are already underway:

- **Question-first framing:** *"I don't know if what I wrote is defensible."* — problem already in document
- **Retrospective exposure:** *"I think I signed something. I'm not certain what I confirmed."* — signature already applied
- **Accumulation blindness:** *"Three clients sent questionnaires in the same quarter."* — pattern invisible to the ICP
- **Passive adoption:** *"My carrier wants me to confirm X. I can't confirm what I don't know."* — problem arrived without decision

### Accountability Concentration
The ICP speaks as if liability is already personal before they've evaluated whether it should be:

- *"I'm a sole practitioner. Every liability is personal."*
- *"I signed the carrier form. Then I realized it was a personal commitment."*
- *"I think I'm the only one who doesn't know. I suspect I'm not."*

### The Geneva Accountability Vacuum
The ICP names the absence of an institutional forum as both frustration and signal:

- *"I checked. There's no association for management consultants in Geneva that handles these questions."*
- *"I'm waiting for SRO guidance. Someone told me management consulting has no SRO."*
- *"My carrier's questionnaire is the closest thing to a standard I've seen. I think that's a problem."*

### Methodology Uncertainty
The ICP has a specific language deficit around what constitutes AI methodology and how to document it:

- *"I use AI opportunistically. I can't stand behind every tool mentioned in proposals."*
- *"I described my analytical approach. AI may have changed it since I wrote the proposal."*
- *"I don't know what attribution that chain requires."*

## French Language Register (Manner of Speaking)

*Observations on French register for Geneva consulting ICP:*

- High French formality in written carrier communications and client questionnaires
- Pragmatic, direct register in internal MD conversations among Geneva peers
- "Attestation," "déontologie," "responsabilité personnelle," "gouvernance IA" — formal French equivalents for the formal register used in client-facing and regulatory documents
- "Je ne sais pas trop" — mild epistemic hedge popular among Geneva MDs — signals uncertainty without liability admission
- "Ça se complique" — pragmatic acknowledgment when discussing regulatory complexity
- "Je signe et puis on verra" — resignation register for attestation signing — action without acknowledged understanding
- "Je n'ai pas de cadre" — direct statement of framework absence — no softening
- French-language carrier documentation creates an additional comprehension barrier layered over technical complexity

## What Is NOT Said

The ICP does NOT use these framings (they do not yet exist in their vocabulary):
- "Artificial intelligence governance framework" — too abstract, framework language belongs to the compliance world the ICP is not yet inside
- "AI risk management system" — risk management vocabulary feels like it belongs to banks
- "Algorithmic accountability" — academic register, not practitioner register
- "Third-party processor obligations under Article 9 nFADP" — legal vocabulary, not how the ICP speaks
- "ISAE 3000 attestation chain" — carrier/auditor vocabulary the ICP encounters but does not use
- "AI model version provenance" — technical vocabulary outside the ICP's current frame

The ICP uses situation descriptions, not category names. They describe experiences of the problem, not the regulatory classification of the problem.
