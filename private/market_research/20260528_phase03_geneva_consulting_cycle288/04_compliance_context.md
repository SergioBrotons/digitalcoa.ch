## Primary Regulatory Framework

### 1. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection)
**Status:** Operative since June 1, 2024. Switzerland's independent data protection framework. Not GDPR copy — structurally distinct.

**Article 9 — Third-Party Processor Obligations**
nFADP Article 9 requires that where a data controller uses third-party processors (sub-consultants who process personal data as part of client engagements), the controller must ensure the processor provides sufficient guarantees for data protection, and processor obligations flow through the contract. For Geneva consulting boutiques:
- Client → boutique → sub-consultant → AI tool creates a three-party processor chain
- Boutique is primary data controller; sub-consultant is processor; AI tool processing may or may not constitute regulated processing depending on personal data involvement
- Engagement letters pre-dating AI adoption have no third-party AI processor clause
- The sub-consultant's AI vendor terms may be structurally incompatible with boutique's controller obligations — a named structural conflict with no current engagement letter mechanism to resolve it
- FDPIC has not issued guidance specific to consulting firms on Article 9 processor chain obligations

**Article 21 — Automated Decision Obligations**
If a consulting firm's AI-assisted analysis constitutes automated decision-making affecting clients (e.g., AI-influenced strategic recommendation treated as basis for client decision), Article 21 gives data subjects the right to request human review. For Geneva consulting:
- Deliverable recommendation may constitute automated decision
- No current engagement letter addresses AI-influenced decision disclosure obligations
- Right to human review is available to clients but infrastructure to respond does not exist in most boutiques
- Boutique most exposed precisely when most confident in AI-assisted quality

**Article 63 — Personal Criminal Liability**
nFADP Article 63 establishes personal criminal嗔 liability for intentional violations. For managing directors at small boutiques:
- Solo MD or small partnership: every liability is personal, no institutional buffer
- nFADP violation in client work flows directly to MD without corporate shield
- FDPIC enforcement trajectory established through Phase 01 research — small firms not exempt because they are small

**nFADP DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)**
nFADP requires DPIA before processing that is likely to result in high risk to data subjects. For Geneva consulting processing client data via AI:
- AI-assisted analysis processing personal client data: likely triggers DPIA obligation
- Most Geneva consulting boutiques have not conducted nFADP DPIA for AI systems
- DPIA for a boutique engagement requires proportionate methodology not yet defined by FDPIC

### 2. Swiss AI Convention (Council of Europe AI Convention)
**Status:** Switzerland signed March 2024; ratification in process; domestic implementation obligations flowing through existing mandate contracts and carrier underwriting terms.

**Deployer Obligations Creating Present-Tense Exposure**
The AI Convention creates deployer obligations — applying to firms that deploy AI systems in professional services — before implementation legislation arrives. For Geneva consulting:
- AI tool deployment in client mandates = current deployer obligations
- Client mandate contracts may already function as AI Convention deployer documentation
- Carrier underwriting terms increasingly reference AI Convention compliance
- Implementation legislation has not arrived but deployment obligations are already present
- Informational circuit mechanisms in Geneva consulting community form standards before formal process arrives

### 3. EU AI Act (Extraterritorial Application)
**Status:** GPAI provisions operative August 2, 2025; full AI Act August 2, 2026.

**If Geneva Consulting Firm Has EU Market Nexus**
- EU market nexus: firm has EU-based clients, places AI systems on EU market, or acts as AI system importer/distributor
- GPAI obligations already in effect for GPAI system users
- Full obligations for high-risk AI systems from August 2, 2026
- General-purpose AI obligations for firms using OpenAI, Claude, Gemini API — already operative
- nFADP and EU AI Act create dual compliance obligation for firms with EU nexus — structurally incompatible in some provisions

**Market Nexus Assessment as Discrete Service**
Determining whether a Geneva consulting boutique has EU market nexus is itself a discrete advisory service. Most small boutiques assume they are not in scope without having evaluated it. EU nexus determination is a named engagement type.

### 4. FINMA AI Guidance (December 2024)
**Status:** Non-legal, sector-specific measures for supervised entities. Baseline for firms with FINMA oversight. Not directly applicable to consulting firms without FINMA authorization — but carrier influence means it functions as de facto reference standard even for non-FINMA firms.

**Four Pillars:**
1. Governance: Board-level accountability for AI systems; documented AI inventory
2. Risk Management: DPIA for AI processing personal data; ongoing monitoring
3. Transparency: Client disclosure of AI-assisted decisions; explainability where feasible
4. Non-Discrimination: Testing for algorithmic bias; fairness audit procedures

**Boutique Consulting Relevance:**
Even without FINMA authorization, Geneva consulting boutiques use FINMA AI Guidance as a reference standard because:
- Carrier attestation forms reference AI governance expectations aligned with FINMA pillars
- Institutional clients benchmark consulting firms against FINMA-standard language
- Geneva consulting MDs have no other structured reference for what adequate AI governance requires

### 5. Carrier Attestation Documentation Standard
**Status:** Third-generation carrier AI attestation forms require MD personal signature. Forms ask about AI methodology without defining methodology. No proportionate scale for small consulting boutique.

**Documentation Category Impossibility:**
Third-gen carrier attestation requires performance evidence assuming routinized processes. Bespoke consulting analysis deliverables cannot produce this evidence category. The documentation category and the work category are structurally incompatible:
- Highest-quality bespoke consulting firms most unable to produce required evidence
- Carrier attestation standard imported from institutional context directly conflicts with consulting practice properties
- Boutique has no engagement letter architecture specifying what evidence AI-assisted methodology should produce
- PI carrier cannot confirm coverage scope for AI-assisted errors if attribution is architecturally impossible

## Geneva-Specific Institutional Context

### No SVA Equivalent for Management Consultants
SVA (Swiss Insurance Association) covers insurance; not consulting. Management consultants in Switzerland have no equivalent professional body. ORDRE (GenevaBar) covers attorneys; not management consultants. The Geneva consulting ICP has no sector-specific professional association with AI governance mandate or guidance authority.

### Carrier Questionnaire as De Facto Operative Standard
Without institutional standard-setting from a sector body, carrier AI governance questionnaires function as the de facto operative standard for Geneva management consultants:
- Carrier forms designed without Geneva consulting input
- Carrier forms reference standards adapted from institutional financial services context
- MDs sign to maintain PI coverage without understanding the standard they are committing to
- No Geneva consulting voice participates in standard formation — no institution exists to convene it

### Documentation Window — Burden-of-Proof Inversion
The documentation window represents the period in which the MD can demonstrate engagement and good-faith effort. Post-window close, the standard inverts:
- Before: MD demonstrates engagement with AI governance question
-During/after: MD must demonstrate actual adequacy of AI governance practices
- Retroactive reconstruction of reasoning not documented during window is practically impossible
- The documentation window is a present-tense opportunity becoming a past-tense evidentiary constraint

### Geneva Consulting Accountability Ecosystem
Geneva's consulting ICP forms accountability expectations through informal mechanisms:
- Geneva MD peer networks exchange information on carrier forms, client questionnaires, sub-consultant issues — but with no institutional standing
- Geneva institutional circuit (not formal regulatory process) forms standards before Bern formal process arrives
- SRO guidance vacuum means MDs manage AI governance through risk tolerance rather than information — waiting strategy that compounds exposure
- Circuit participation non-fungible: participation requires sustained access, cannot be systematized — the informal circuit is the standard-formation mechanism
