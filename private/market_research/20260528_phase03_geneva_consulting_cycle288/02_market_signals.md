# Phase 03 Market Signals — Geneva Consulting (Small Boutique)
## Cycle 288 (2026-05-28)

## Phase
Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting, small boutique 2–50 employees

## ICP Problem Statement
MD or partner at small Geneva consulting boutique personally accountable for AI tool deployment in client engagements. Struggling with carrier attestation obligations, client AI governance questionnaires, sub-consultant AI delegation, proposal methodology defensibility, and personal exposure under nFADP. No SVA equivalent, no ORDRE equivalent. Carrier questionnaire operates as de facto sector standard without Geneva consulting input.

---

## Market Signals: Current Problems, Complaints, Failures

### Signal 1: Client Questionnaire Portfolio Accumulation Effect

**Pattern:** Geneva consulting boutiques are receiving AI governance questionnaires from multiple clients simultaneously, without realizing there is a portfolio-level accumulation problem beneath firm awareness.

**What's actually happening:**
- Multiple clients sent questionnaires in same quarter — firm answers each independently without cross-reference
- Each response commits the firm to something slightly different; consistency between responses unmanaged
- Client A's questionnaire triggers a commitment; Client B's questionnaire the following month asks a question the commitment was supposed to answer
- Firm does not see the portfolio-level picture because each questionnaire arrives as a discrete event
- No documentation system maps what the firm has committed to across all active client relationships
- Carrier attestation form asks about AI methodology — firm has no methodology document to reference

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"Three clients sent questionnaires in the same quarter. I answered them separately. I don't know if my answers are consistent."*
> *"My proposal went out three months ago. Now they want to know what AI I used. I signed it. I can't remember what I said I'd use."*
> *"The client wants AI governance documentation. I don't know what category of document they mean."*

**Market failure frame:**
This is a documentation architecture problem, not a questionnaire content problem. The firm has no system for managing cross-client AI governance commitments. Each questionnaire is processed as an isolated event. The accumulation effect creates inconsistency exposure the firm does not know it has already created.

### Signal 2: Carrier Attestation Post-Signature Realization

**Pattern:** MDs sign carrier attestation forms under time pressure, then realize they have made a personal commitment to an undefined adequacy standard.

**What's actually happening:**
- Carrier attestation arrives with short deadline — MD reviews quickly, signs, submits
- Post-signature realization: form commits to standards and practices the MD has not evaluated
- Carrier attestation language is generic — applicable to large institutions and small boutiques simultaneously
- No pre-signing review service exists in the Geneva consulting market
- Signed attestation creates personal liability exposure for AI practices the MD is still forming
- Broker or carrier representative frames the form as "standard" — removes urgency for independent review
- Post-signing anxiety emerges without clear path to reassessment

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"I signed the carrier attestation form. Then I realized it was a personal commitment to a standard that nobody has defined."*
> *"After signing, I started wondering whether I was covered or whether I just confirmed I wasn't."*
> *"I want to review the form before signing it. Nobody offered me that. I just got a signature request."*
> *"My broker said the attestation is now standard. I don't know if that's true or if they're telling me that so I'll sign it."*

**Market failure frame:**
The carrier attestation is a personal liability instrument — it requires the MD's signature committing to undefined adequacy — but the market offers no pre-signing review service. The MD signs without understanding what they have committed to. This is not a problem of the MD's diligence; it is a structural absence in the market for pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation forms.

### Signal 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Three-Party Chain Without Authorization Architecture

**Pattern:** Small Geneva consulting boutiques work with independent sub-consultants, many of whom use AI tools. The delegation chain creates a three-party AI accountability structure with no documented authorization framework.

**What's actually happening:**
- Client → MD/boutique → sub-consultant → AI tool
- Engagement letter governs client-to-boutique relationship — no AI authorization language, no sub-consultant AI clause
- Sub-consultant uses AI tools independently — boutique MD does not know which tools, which models, which versions
- No framework exists for what constitutes adequate sub-consultant AI disclosure to the prime consultant
- nFADP Article 9 third-party processing obligations flow from boutique to sub-consultant — but the boutique has no mapping of what the sub-consultant actually processes
- When sub-consultant AI produces a flawed deliverable component, accountability chain is legally ambiguous
- Prime boutique cannot simultaneously satisfy engagement letter proper performance obligations and vendor AI terms

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"I work with independent contractors. I don't know what AI they're using. I'm not sure I need to know."*
> *"I delegated part of the analysis to a sub-consultant. They used AI. I don't know which AI or how."*
> *"When my sub-consultant uses AI in our joint deliverable, whose deliverable is it?"*
> *"I have no framework for what I can ask my sub-consultants about their AI use."*
> *"I think I signed something. My broker said it covers sub-consultant delegation. I'm not sure what that means in practice."*

**Market failure frame:**
The three-party AI delegation chain — client → boutique → sub-consultant → AI — is an unnamed accountability structure in the Geneva consulting market. No engagement letter architecture addresses it. No sub-consultant AI disclosure standard exists. No framework telling the prime boutique what adequate oversight of sub-consultant AI use requires. The exposure exists before any failure occurs.

### Signal 4: AI Model Silent Update Propagation Into Active Client Deliverables

**Pattern:** AI model updates happen silently — between proposal date and deliverable date, or between deliverable date and client follow-up date. The MD cannot stand behind a methodology or conclusion that may have changed since the analytical work was done.

**What's actually happening:**
- MD uses AI-assisted analysis in client proposal — methodology documented as of proposal date
- AI model updates between proposal submission and active engagement conclusion
- Analytical framework embedded in deliverable may differ from framework at proposal date
- Client references a conclusion from a deliverable produced months earlier — AI may have changed methodology since then
- MD cannot explain recommendation in current model conditions because the model conditions under which it was produced are no longer accessible
- No version-locking mechanism exists in the boutique's engagement letter or AI tool selection
- Engagement letters have no version-locked deliverable standard — no defined AI model version commitment

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"I described my analytical approach in my proposal. AI may have changed it since I wrote the proposal."*
> *"My client asked about AI methodology in the proposal stage. I gave a generic answer. Now they want specifics."*
> *"My proposal says 'evidence-based analysis.' AI produces evidence. I don't know what attribution that chain requires."*
> *"The proposal I sent two years ago has AI references. I'm not sure they meant anything at the time."*
> *"My client referenced a section of my proposal in a follow-up question. I barely recognized what I'd written."*

**Market failure frame:**
Enterprise AI vendors conduct silent model updates as a standard practice. For Geneva consulting boutiques — where analytical methodology is the core product — this creates active mandate undisclosed analytical dependency. The MD cannot confirm which model version produced the conclusions a client still references. The deliverable version dependency is structurally unmanaged.

### Signal 5: Documentation Window Close as Burden-of-Proof Inversion

**Pattern:** The 90-day window for documenting AI governance practices before obligations sharpen is being treated as a compliance deadline rather than a burden-of-proof positioning exercise. The MD does not understand what is at stake in failing to use the window strategically.

**What's actually happening:**
- Post-deadline, the burden of proof inverts: MD must demonstrate adequacy rather than demonstrating engagement
- Documentation window is a present-tense opportunity that becomes a past-tense impossibility on expiry
- Retroactive reconstruction of reasoning is practically impossible — documents were not created during the window
- MD waits for guidance, waits for clarity, waits for certainty — using the window as waiting time
- When obligations sharpen, the MD faces retroactive reconstruction of decisions that were made without documentation architecture
- Carrier, client, and regulator audits all face the same evidence gap: reasoning that was not documented does not exist forensically

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"I know I should do something about AI governance. I don't know what to do so I'm waiting to see if there's guidance."*
> *"My carrier sent a form. I don't know what to put in it so I haven't sent it back."*
> *"I think I'm waiting for the same guidance everyone else is waiting for. Nobody seems to have it."*
> *"The 90 days was supposed to be for preparation. I used it to figure out whether I was in scope."*

**Market failure frame:**
The documentation window close is not a compliance deadline in the conventional sense — it is a burden-of-proof inversion moment. Before: MD demonstrates engagement. After: MD must demonstrate adequacy. For the Geneva consulting MD paying attention to this moment at scale in May 2026, the window is still open but tightening. The window period defines what evidence is reconstructable versus what evidence requires retroactive fabrication.

### Signal 6: Geneva Consultant Accountability Vacuum as Structural Amplification Mechanism

**Pattern:** Geneva management consultants face a structural accountability vacuum — no SVA equivalent covers them, no ORDRE equivalent exists, no Geneva-specific professional association for management consultants with AI governance mandate. Carrier AI questionnaires function as de facto operative standard, designed without Geneva consulting input. The vacuum amplifies individual MD personal liability by removing the collective response mechanism that exists for other professions.

**What's actually happening:**
- Medical doctors: FMH provides institutional pathway for physician AI accountability
- Lawyers: Geneva Bar provides guidance mechanism through formal interpretation process
- Financial advisors: SRO structure creates collective accountability architecture
- Management consultants: nothing. No sector association covers AI governance questions
- No peer forum exists for Geneva management consultants to observe standards forming before they arrive
- Carrier questionnaire displacement: carrier forms become the operative standard by default — not because they are designed for consulting firms, but because no other standard-setting mechanism exists
- Individual MDs absorb aggregate market pressure that other professions distribute across institutional structures
- Informal peer networks (Geneva MD circle) provide emotional support but not institutional guidance

**Complaint language (ICP):**
> *"There's no association for management consultants in Geneva that handles these questions. I checked."*
> *"I'm waiting for SRO guidance. Someone told me management consulting has no SRO. I'm not sure what I'm waiting for."*
> *"My carrier's questionnaire is the closest thing to a standard I've seen. I think that's a problem but I don't know what to substitute."*
> *"I talk to other MDs informally. Nobody has a clear picture. We all seem to be managing the same uncertainty independently."*
> *"The frameworks coming from Bern feel like they were written for banks and insurers. I don't know how to translate them."*

**Market failure frame:**
The Geneva management consulting accountability vacuum is structurally permanent — no institutional path to trigger formal responses regardless of question validity. This vacuum is not a delay; it is an architectural characteristic. The market has no mechanism for collective response to AI governance pressures. Each MD absorbs the pressure individually. This is why carrier questionnaires became the de facto standard: they were the only available frame of reference.
