# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Finance

## Key Regulatory Developments — Geneva Finance (C125)

### Regulatory Item 01: SNB Monetary Policy — Rate Normalization with Inflation Complication
**Authority:** Swiss National Bank (SNB)
**Status:** SNB continuing rate normalization (cuts) into 2026; inflation above 0-2% target creating cross-currents
**Key Dynamics:**
- CHF/EUR: weaker CHF helping exports but increasing import costs
- Swiss inflation above SNB target, complicating further cuts
- Real estate: Geneva mortgage holders facing effective cost increases despite rate normalization
- Bond markets: duration risk, negative carry concerns for CHF bond funds
- FX hedging: EUR/CHF hedging costs elevated; corporate treasury advisory demand
**Geneva Impact:** All Geneva financial institutions — private banks, asset managers, and corporate treasury functions
**Advisory Implications:** Portfolio rebalancing advice, FX hedging frameworks, real estate financing strategy, bond duration management
**Source:** SNB

### Regulatory Item 03: Swiss ESG Fund Taxonomy Revision — FINMA Consultation
**Authority:** FINMA, State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF)
**Status:** FINMA consultation open; revised taxonomy expected 2026
**Key Issues:**
- Swiss taxonomy criteria diverging from EU taxonomy
- Classification: "sustainable", "ESG-aligned", "transition" categories
- Disclosure requirements: pre-contractual, periodic reporting
- Cross-border funds: parallel EU + CH classification needed
- Transition period: timeline for existing fund reclassification
**Geneva Impact:** All Geneva fund managers with ESG-labeled products; distributors in EU and Switzerland
**Advisory Implications:** Fund reclassification analysis, disclosure document updates, investor communication frameworks, cross-border distribution strategy
**Source:** FINMA, SFAMA

## Geneva-Specific Financial Regulatory Dynamics

**FINMA's Dual Role:**
FINMA is simultaneously managing multiple Geneva-relevant processes: crypto license pipeline, ESG taxonomy consultation, private bank consolidation approvals, and Basel IV preparation guidance. Firms with multiple regulatory projects need to coordinate FINMA engagement strategically.

**SNB and Monetary Policy Complexity:**
The SNB's rate normalization with above-target inflation is an unusual policy environment. Geneva financial institutions need sophisticated advisory on portfolio implications, FX risk, and client communication — different from standard low-inflation rate cycle advisory.

**Swiss-EU Financial Services Framework:**
The EU-CH institutional agreement negotiations affect financial services market access. Geneva fund managers and private banks are watching closely — any deal or breakdown has significant implications for EU distribution and market access.

**Direct Lending as Regulatory Innovation:**
Direct lending funds represent an evolving regulatory category. FINMA is processing authorizations case-by-case. Geneva managers launching direct lending strategies need regulatory counsel familiar with FINMA's current posture and precedent-setting approach.
