## NET-NEW Findings: 6 Key Discoveries (C125 Phase 02)

### Finding 01: SNB Rate Normalization with Inflation Complication
SNB continues rate cuts into 2026 despite Swiss inflation remaining above the 0-2% target. CHF/EUR cross-currents creating advisory complexity: weaker CHF helps exports but increases import costs and erodes real returns. Geneva financial institutions need sophisticated portfolio advisory, FX hedging frameworks, and client communication strategies for a non-standard monetary environment.

### Finding 02: FINMA Crypto License Pipeline — 7 Entities Active
Seven entities are in active FINMA crypto license application process as of Q1 2026. Geneva-based traditional financial institutions are watching closely. Tokenization of fund units, digital asset custody for UHNW clients, and tokenized securities trading are within scope. Advisory demand emerging for governance frameworks, compliance programs, and institutional custody solutions.

### Finding 03: Swiss ESG Fund Taxonomy Diverging from EU — FINMA Consultation
FINMA's consultation on revised Swiss ESG fund taxonomy reveals significant divergence from EU taxonomy criteria. Geneva fund managers distributing cross-border (EU + Switzerland) need parallel classification systems and disclosure frameworks. Reclassification work for existing ESG-labeled products is a near-term compliance obligation.

### Finding 04: Wholesale CBDC — Project Agora Pilot (SNB + MAS)
BIS Innovation Hub Project Agora: SNB and MAS piloting wholesale CBDC for CHF/SGD cross-border payments and settlement. Geneva banks with Asian wholesale operations (FX, trade finance, cross-border settlement) need to monitor implications for correspondent banking relationships and settlement infrastructure.

### Finding 05: Geneva Private Bank Consolidation — 3 Banks Active
Three Geneva private banks in active merger or integration discussions Q1 2026. Margin compression, regulatory cost increases, and technology investment requirements are driving deals. Advisory opportunities: client migration planning, RM retention programs, regulatory filings, and post-merger integration.

### Finding 06: Direct Lending Fund Formation Surge — 4 New Q1 2026
Four new direct lending funds formed in Switzerland Q1 2026, driven by credit gap in Swiss SME/mid-market and bank lending tightening post-Basel IV. Geneva-based managers capturing demand. Regulatory authorization, documentation, and investor relations support creating advisory work.
