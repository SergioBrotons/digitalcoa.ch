# 01 — ICP: Geneva Asset Management & Swiss Fund Managers

## Target Segment: Swiss Fund Managers & Asset Management (Geneva)

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** CHF 500M–10B+ AUM; typically 10-100 employees
- **Revenue:** Management fees + performance fees; revenue driven by AUM and fund performance
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; Zurich for larger managers; some with Luxembourg fund vehicles
- **Product Types:** Equity funds, bond funds, mixed allocation, ESG/sustainable funds, alternative funds (direct lending, private credit, real estate)
- **Status:** Switzerland is Europe's #2 fund jurisdiction after Luxembourg; Geneva is the French-speaking fund hub

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CEO / Managing Partner | Senior executive, often founder | AUM growth, regulatory compliance, ESG product demand |
| CIO | Investment professional | Portfolio construction, ESG integration, risk management |
| CFO / Finance Director | Non-investment executive | Regulatory capital, audit, fund administration |
| Compliance Officer | Regulatory specialist | FINMA compliance, ESG disclosure, cross-border distribution |
| Distribution Head | Client-facing executive | EU/CH distribution, investor relations, UCITS/CH-Fund passport |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Pictet Asset Management (Geneva, CHF 250B+ AUM)
- Lombard Odier Investment Managers (Geneva, ~CHF 150B AUM)
- Swisscanto (Zurich/Geneva, cooperative bank group)
- Union Bancaire Privée (UBP, Geneva, ~CHF 150B AUM)
- Lombard Odier (Geneva, ~CHF 80B AUM private banking + asset management)
- Akros (Zurich/Geneva, smaller boutique)
- SYZ Asset Management (Geneva, boutique)
- Plus many smaller Geneva-based boutique managers (10-50 employees)

### Pain Points (C125 Update)
1. **ESG fund taxonomy revision** — FINMA consultation on revised ESG taxonomy; Swiss sustainable finance taxonomy diverging from EU; fund managers need to reclassify products, update disclosures, and communicate changes to investors
2. **FINMA crypto license pipeline** — 7 entities in licensing process; traditional asset managers exploring tokenization of fund units or digital asset exposure; advisory demand for governance, custody, and compliance frameworks
3. **Direct lending fund formation** — 4 new direct lending funds formed Q1 2026; credit gap financing for Swiss SME and mid-market; fund managers capturing demand but need regulatory, documentation, and investor relations support
4. **Wholesale CBDC implications** — Project Agora SNB/MAS pilot; Geneva-based wholesale FX and cross-border payment managers exploring CBDC impact on settlement and correspondent banking
5. **SNB rate normalization** — continued SNB rate cuts into 2026 despite inflation above target; bond fund duration management, hedging costs, and client communication demand
6. **EU-CH sustainable finance divergence** — Swiss taxonomy not aligned with EU; fund managers distributing in EU vs. CH need parallel frameworks; documentation and disclosure complexity

### Buying Triggers
- FINMA ESG taxonomy finalization (expected 2026)
- FINMA crypto license approvals
- Direct lending fund investor demand exceeding internal capacity
- Wholesale CBDC pilot results published
- SNB rate decisions affecting bond portfolio valuations
- EU-CH institutional agreement sustainable finance provisions

### Buying Barriers
- Established relationships with existing service providers (custodians, fund administrators, auditors)
- Regulatory change fatigue — multiple simultaneous reforms
- ESG taxonomy confusion — firms waiting for final rules before acting
- Tokenization advisory: nascent market, limited vendor options

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva; English for institutional/asset management
- German useful for Zurich-connected managers
- Institutional business culture: formal, credentialed, data-driven
- Swiss fund industry culture: conservative, compliance-first

## 01 — ICP: Geneva Private Banking & Wealth Management

## Target Segment: Geneva Private Banks & UHNW Wealth Management

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** CHF 10B–200B+ AUM; typically 50-500 relationship managers + investment, compliance, operations
- **Revenue:** Management fees + transaction fees + performance fees; margin pressure from low interest rates
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; Zurich; international offices (London, Singapore, Dubai)
- **Client Segment:** UHNW and HNW individuals, families, family offices, foundations
- **Status:** Geneva is global #1 private banking center; consolidation continuing (3 banks in merger discussions Q1 2026)

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|------|---------|-------------|
| CEO / Managing Director | Senior executive | Consolidation integration, regulatory capital, margin pressure |
| Chief Investment Officer (CIO) | Investment professional | Market volatility, ESG integration, private assets allocation |
| Head of Wealth Planning / Advisory | Client-facing senior | UHNW succession planning, family governance, multi-jurisdiction estate planning |
| Head of Compliance | Regulatory specialist | FINMA compliance, AEOI-CRS, FATCA, AML |
| COO / CFO | Operations executive | Technology modernization, cost efficiency, regulatory reporting |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Lombard Odier (Geneva, ~CHF 80B AUM private banking)
- Union Bancaire Privée (UBP, Geneva, ~CHF 150B AUM)
- Pictet Private Banking (Geneva, part of Pictet Group)
- Edmond de Rothschild (Geneva, ~CHF 80B AUM)
- Rothschild & Co (Geneva branch, Paris HQ)
- Crédit Agricole Private Banking (Geneva subsidiary)
- Many smaller Geneva private banks (Banquiers Privés)

### Pain Points (C125 Update)
1. **Geneva private bank consolidation** — 3 Geneva private banks in active merger/integration discussions; client migration, relationship manager retention, systems integration creating advisory demand
2. **UHNW succession planning wave** — aging UHNW client base accelerating succession and estate planning demand; multi-jurisdiction wealth structuring; family governance advisory
3. **Wholesale CBDC and tokenization** — UHNW clients exploring tokenized assets; private banks developing digital asset custody and advisory capabilities; regulatory uncertainty creating advisory need
4. **SNB rate normalization and wealth planning** — CHF/EUR dynamics affecting real estate and portfolio allocation decisions for UHNW clients; hedging and rebalancing advisory
5. **ESG integration in private wealth** — UHNW clients increasingly requesting ESG-aligned portfolios; private banks need advisory frameworks and documentation
6. **Direct lending as wealth diversification** — UHNW clients seeking direct lending fund exposure for yield; private banks as distribution channel and co-investor

### Buying Triggers
- Consolidation transaction closing (client migration advisory)
- UHNW client succession event (death, incapacity, business sale)
- FINMA approval of digital asset custody frameworks
- SNB rate decision affecting CHF/EUR portfolio allocation
- EU-CH sustainable finance agreement provisions affecting cross-border wealth management

### Buying Barriers
- Sticky client relationships — RMs control client access
- Regulatory complexity — FATCA/AEOI compliance handled in-house
- Tokenization: nascent, no clear regulatory framework yet
- Consolidation: existing service providers retained post-merger

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva UHNW clients
- English for international UHNW clients
- German useful for Swiss-German UHNW clients
- Relationship-based culture: trust, continuity, discretion valued
