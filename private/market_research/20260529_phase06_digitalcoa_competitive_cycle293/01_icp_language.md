## How ICPs Find and Describe DigitalCoa.ch

### The Arrival Pattern

ICPs who reach DigitalCoa.ch follow a consistent trajectory:

1. **Peer resonance first** — They have discussed AI accountability with colleagues in their own vertical and adjacent verticals and noticed the same structural problem. The pattern recognition came from peer conversation, not from DigitalCoa.ch content.

2. **Existing solutions inadequate** — They have already contacted or considered: law firms (too legal, not operational), Big4 (too enterprise, wrong scale), IT vendors (conflict of interest, no independence), professional associations (guidance without advice). Each option was evaluated and found architecturally wrong for their situation.

3. **The search for the right person** — The search language is specific: they are not searching for "AI governance" generically. They are searching for someone who understands Geneva, their vertical, and the specific accountability moment they are in. The search is for a person, not a firm.

4. **Confirmation, not education** — The ICP who arrives has already formed the hypothesis. They are looking for someone to confirm what they have observed and provide the path forward. The entry conversation is a confirmation conversation.

### The Language That Converts

**What the ICP needs to hear (from their own vertical's framing):**

- "You already signed something. Now you need to understand what that signature committed you to."
- "The evidence standard is unobtainable with your current process — not because your process is broken, but because it wasn't designed for this standard. You need a different architecture."
- "Entity-level compliance and personal liability are different exposures. Most frameworks address the first and not the second."
- "The person who signed the attestation doesn't control the AI decisions. That's the exposure no one's naming."
- "The standards that will apply to you are being written in rooms you don't access. I participate in those rooms."

**What the ICP hears when the language is wrong:**

- "AI governance framework" — enterprise, IT, wrong scale
- "AI policy" — bureaucratic, HR, doesn't match personal liability moment
- "AI compliance" — regulatory, legal, doesn't match the accountability briefing format
- "AI strategy" — technology planning, doesn't match the urgency of an existing attestation

**What DigitalCoa.ch does differently:**
- Names the accountability moment, not the technology category
- Speaks to the managing director's personal exposure, not the firm's IT infrastructure
- Addresses adequacy at boutique scale, not enterprise governance
- Operates in Geneva accountability ecosystem participation, not methodology documents

### What ICPs Say They Were Missing Before Finding DigitalCoa.ch

1. **Geneva-specificity** — Not Swiss-wide, not global framework, not EU-standard. Geneva accountability ecosystem.
2. **Personal liability naming** — Someone who names the personal exposure specifically, not entity-level compliance review.
3. **Boutique-scale proportionality** — Someone who understands that "adequate governance" means something different for a 3-person law firm than for a 200-person institution.
4. **Formation ecosystem participation** — Someone who is tracking Bern, FDPIC, FINMA, FMH, and Geneva Bar developments in real time.
5. **Cross-vertical synthesis** — Someone who sees the pattern across all four verticals and can name it precisely.
