# Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting: Ideal Client Profile
## Cycle 20 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Small Consulting Firms

---

## ICP: The Geneva Small Consulting Firm

### Demographics
- **Firm size:** 1–10 consultants (often solo or micro-firm)
- **Location:** Geneva (primary); Vaud, Zurich secondary
- **Practice areas:** SMB strategy, IT consulting, HR consulting, financial advisory, ESG/sustainability, marketing/digital, management consulting
- **Technology posture:** Active AI adoption — research, analysis, deliverable creation, client communication
- **Client base:** Geneva/Swiss SMBs, occasionally EU-based clients

### Pain Points
1. **GPAI obligations active:** EU AI Act GPAI transparency obligations ACTIVE since Aug 2 2025 (~8 months elapsed)
2. **Dual compliance:** nFADP active + EU AI Act EU-nexus obligations — no Geneva consulting-specific guidance
3. **May 2026 awareness event:** digitalswitzerland First Draft ~2 months away will trigger client questions
4. **40% AI skills gap:** Geneva SME AI skills gap creates client demand for AI guidance
5. **No professional body guidance:** No Geneva consulting-specific AI governance guidance published

### Timing Assessment
- **GPAI transparency:** ~8 months already active (Aug 2 2025)
- **Federal Council First Draft (digitalswitzerland):** ~2 months (May 2026)
- **EU AI Act high-risk:** ~4.5 months (Aug 2 2026)
- **Federal Council formal legislation:** End-2026+
- **Advisory window:** ~9 months to Aug 2 2026

### Competitive Landscape
**No boutique competitor for small consulting firm AI governance (9th+ confirmation)**
- Swiss Cyber Institute, Govern AI, Swiss Infosec AG, AI Legal & Strategy AG, Modulos AG, GAIGI: all enterprise-focused

### Addressable Market
- **Geneva: ~600–900 small consulting firms**
- **Geneva + Vaud: ~900–1,400**
- **Switzerland-wide: ~2,500–4,000 small consulting firms**
