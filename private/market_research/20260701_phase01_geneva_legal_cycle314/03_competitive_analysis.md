## White Space Analysis

### White Space 01: Cross-Border Applicability Analysis
**Existing market:** None. Bar, carrier, vendor, big-four, and boutique consultants do not offer boutique-specific cross-border applicability analysis under Article 6(3) of the EU AI Act.

**C314 service module:** Cross-border applicability memo. Maps the firm's EU-jurisdiction matters against Article 6(3) ancillary exception test. Specifies whether the firm is in scope. Documents the analysis for carrier attestation and bar supervision file.

### White Space 02: Article 321 vs EU AI Act Logging Conflict Resolution
**Existing market:** None. The conflict is documented in academic literature but not in any service offering.

**C314 service module:** Logging conflict resolution methodology. Specifies which regime takes precedence (Swiss criminal law / Article 321 typically takes precedence for attorney-client communications). Produces a documented configuration: AI vendor with zero-retention / privacy-preserving logging configuration that satisfies Article 321 while addressing EU AI Act logging requirements through alternative documentation. Documents the conflict and the resolution for the carrier attestation.

### White Space 03: AI Hallucination Verification Protocol
**Existing market:** Partial. Vendor training programs teach prompt engineering to reduce hallucination. Carrier guidance says "verify AI outputs." Neither produces a named, documentable verification protocol.

**C314 service module:** Hallucination verification protocol. Specifies: which AI outputs require verification (every citation, every case reference, every statute reference, every quoted text); what verification steps are required (database lookup, source verification, citation format check); who signs off (the responsible attorney); how the verification is documented (verification log with date, attorney signature, verification source); how the protocol is referenced in the carrier attestation.

### White Space 04: Privilege-Preservation Documentation
**Existing market:** None specific to Geneva / Swiss regime. Vendor documentation offers zero-retention configurations but does not produce firm-specific privilege-preservation documentation.

**C314 service module:** Privilege-preservation documentation protocol. Maps the firm's AI vendor configurations against Swiss professional secrecy requirements. Specifies: which vendors preserve privilege (and under what configuration); what client disclosure language is required (informed consent for AI ingestion); how the firm's authorization documentation reflects privilege-preservation; how the carrier attestation references privilege-preservation.

### White Space 05: Cross-Matter Contamination Audit
**Existing market:** None. Vendor training data documentation is not audited at the firm level.

**C314 service module:** Cross-matter contamination audit. Maps the firm's AI vendor training data sources, identifies contamination risk (other firms' privileged content), specifies mitigation (zero-retention vendors, prompt engineering to avoid contamination patterns, vendor contractual restrictions on training use), produces documented contamination exposure language for the carrier attestation.

### White Space 06: Three-Regime Reconciliation Memo
**Existing market:** None. Bar + carrier + EU AI Act are three separate regimes with no integrated reconciliation service.

**C314 service module:** Three-regime reconciliation memo. Maps bar (OaF / FSA supervision obligations) + carrier (attestation obligations) + EU AI Act (Articles 9-15 high-risk obligations) onto the firm's specific practice. Identifies conflicts. Specifies precedence where regimes disagree. Produces the documentation the firm can attach to its carrier attestation and bar supervision file.
