# Market Signals: Cross-Vertical Synthesis — C290 Phase 05

## Cross-Vertical Market Signal Overview

Across Geneva's four professional services verticals — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — market signals are converging on identical structural patterns, each vertical experiencing them in isolation from the others. The cross-vertical view reveals that the same mechanisms are operating across all four verticals simultaneously, creating a market-wide accountability formation event without precedent in Geneva professional services.

---

## Signal 01: Carrier Attestation as Primary Acute Pressure — Universal Entry Point

**Observation:** All four verticals identify carrier attestation (or equivalent professional instrument) as the acute entry point driving AI governance urgency — not strategic planning, not regulatory compliance, but a predetermined calendar event forcing reckoning with undefined obligations.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** SBA carrier attestation personal signature committing to undefined adequacy standard
- **Finance:** Third-generation carrier attestation with MD personal signature, Art.63 nFADP personal criminal liability convergence
- **Consulting:** Carrier questionnaire attestation operating as de facto operative standard without consulting input
- **Healthcare:** Carrier questionnaire attestation as de facto operative standard, FMH principle without operational endpoint

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Carrier attestation is not just the entry point — it is the most operationally specific standard-setting mechanism in the Geneva market, operating below official doctrine. The carrier form defines what "adequate" means for the sector before Bern publishes any guidance.

**Market signal interpretation:** Carrier attestation is simultaneously a compliance document and a liability instrument. Firms signing it have no pre-signing review service available in the Geneva market. The market has created the obligation without creating the discharge mechanism.

## Signal 02: Documentation Window as Burden-of-Proof Reversal — Universal Timing Property

**Observation:** Across all four verticals, the documentation window is described as a positioning occasion before formal standards arrive — evidence built during the window period cannot be retroactively reconstructed after the window closes.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Pre-2027 regulatory vacuum creates specific urgency window; documentation cannot reconstruct window-period reasoning
- **Finance:** 90-day documentation window before carrier renewal; retroactive reconstruction structurally impossible
- **Consulting:** Documentation window close as burden-of-proof inversion requiring pre-architecture not retroactive reconstruction
- **Healthcare:** Documentation window as positioning exercise not compliance deadline; new documentation cannot reproduce window-period evidence

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Before formal standards crystallize, firms building documentation are evaluated against forming standards. After crystallization, firms without documentation are evaluated against arrived standards. The window has a specific structural property: it closes permanently.

**Market signal interpretation:** The documentation window is a one-time positioning occasion. Firms that build proportionate evidence architecture during the window achieve a defensible evidentiary position that cannot be replicated after the window closes through subsequent documentation efforts.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** SBA guidelines principle without practice; bar formal interpretation mechanism absence
- **Finance:** SRO guidance vacuum; FINMA anticipation without specific WM guidance; institutional client AI governance questionnaire as de facto standard through guidance vacuum
- **Consulting:** No SVA equivalent; carrier questionnaire functions as de facto sector standard without consulting input; peer accountability vacuum structural
- **Healthcare:** Service de la santé publique has issued no canton-specific AI guidance; FMH guidance de facto operative standard without proportionality filter; CHUV/HUG institutional decisions forming effective standards without methodology transmission

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Geneva's institutional density generates operative standards through sustained informal interaction before any published doctrine arrives. The informal circuit is not just faster — it is the primary accountability formation mechanism, and formal process only catches up after the fact.

**Market signal interpretation:** Waiting for formal doctrine means operating in a vacuum while obligations form elsewhere. The market signal is that the informal circuit is the actual standard-setter, and participation in it is the only way to observe what the standards actually are before they crystallize.

## Signal 04: Personal Liability Convergence — Universal MD/Principal Exposure

**Observation:** All four verticals identify personal liability for the Managing Director or principal as the specific accountability category that motivates action, distinct from corporate liability or compliance failure.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Article 321 CP criminal exposure with no AI-specific carve-out; partner signature concentration disproportionate to governance control
- **Finance:** nFADP Art.63 personal criminal liability; MD as de facto Chief AI Officer without infrastructure; carrier attestation personal signature committing to undefined standard
- **Consulting:** MD personal liability through engagement letter architecture; Art.398 CO proper performance incompatibility with vendor terms
- **Healthcare:** FMH physician accountability for AI output not deployed or supervised; medical record AI authorship ambiguity; physician accountable for AI from systems not consciously authorized

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The MD or principal signature appears on instruments that commit them to undefined adequacy standards with personal liability consequences — and no Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of what that signature actually commits them to.

**Market signal interpretation:** Personal liability convergence is the primary acute pain point across all four verticals. The market has created personal liability exposure through carrier instruments without creating the pre-signing review service that would allow principals to understand and manage that exposure before signing.

## Signal 05: Institutional Intelligence Inaccessibility — Universal Structural Disadvantage

**Observation:** All four verticals describe the same structural positioning disadvantage: institutional actors (CHUV/HUG in healthcare, institutional clients in finance, carrier legal teams in insurance, bar committees in legal) are forming effective standards through sustained participation in informal Geneva circuits, while independent practices and small firms are making autonomous decisions without access to the intelligence those institutions hold.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Bar formal interpretation mechanism absence; court AI-specific procedural directions forming without small-firm input
- **Finance:** Institutional client AI governance questionnaire as competitive intelligence instrument; FINMA supervisory expectation intelligence derived from carrier iteration pattern analysis
- **Consulting:** Geneva peer accountability vacuum; no SVA equivalent; carrier questionnaire operating as de facto standard without consulting input
- **Healthcare:** CHUV/HUG institutional decisions forming effective standards without methodology transmission; Geneva cantonal institutional caution signal inaccessible to independent practices; FMH guidance de facto operative without proportionality filter

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Institutional intelligence is structurally inaccessible to independent practices and small firms — not because the information is secret, but because it forms through sustained informal participation that most exposed principals have least time for.

**Market signal interpretation:** The most exposed principals have the least access to the intelligence that would help them. The market signal is that institutional intelligence inaccessibility is a structural condition, not a temporary information gap — and that the briefing format as thinking space for unspeakable problems is itself a service addressing this structural disadvantage.

**Observation:** Across all four verticals, the structural recognition is emerging that Geneva operates as a sui generis accountability jurisdiction — not a scaled-down version of Bern, not a cantonal jurisdiction, but an accountability formation environment with its own mechanisms that formal Swiss process cannot observe or control.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** SBA guidelines principle without practice; Geneva French-language underserved segment; carrier attestation operating as de facto operative standard through informal circuit
- **Finance:** SRO guidance vacuum; institutional client questionnaire as primary standard-setting mechanism; carrier iteration pattern tracking as discrete intelligence function unique to Geneva
- **Consulting:** No SVA equivalent; carrier questionnaire displacement as structural consequence mechanism; Geneva accountability vacuum as structural amplification mechanism

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The vertical accountability geometry is the same across all four verticals: Federal AI Coordination Office + cantonal regulators + Geneva institutional ecosystem + carrier underwriting + institutional counterparty. Decisions at one level are evaluated against standards at another. Horizontal coherence is structurally impossible by design.

**Market signal interpretation:** The market is recognizing that Geneva's accountability formation mechanism is geographically specific and structurally non-replicable. More accurate Geneva-calibrated methodology equals less portable outside Geneva — creating a ceiling on output reach that simultaneously performs market positioning signal of accurate self-assessment. The Geneva specificity ceiling is simultaneously a competitive moat.

## Signal 07: AI Tool Accumulation Without Governance Architecture — Universal Structural Problem

**Observation:** All four verticals describe the same structural AI governance failure: AI arrived through incremental updates, staff activation, and embedded features before any governance choice was made, creating accumulated AI surface that principals are now responsible for without having authorized.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Staff shadow AI as primary governance blind spot; associate AI competency supervision structural inversion
- **Finance:** Portfolio construction AI as unnamed primary exposure category; AI configuration defaults as path-dependent governance artifact
- **Consulting:** Internal AI governance surface operating as unmanaged accountability domain; AI tool proliferation debt accumulating infrastructure audit gap
- **Healthcare:** Incremental AI accumulation without governance decision; patient-initiated AI content entering medical records; vendor AI terms accepted through click-through activating features that cannot be technically disabled

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The structural problem is not AI adoption — it is AI accumulation without governance decision. The AI arrived before the governance question was asked, and now principals are accountable for AI usage they did not consciously authorize.

**Market signal interpretation:** The market is discovering that the AI governance question was never asked when it needed to be, and now the accumulated AI surface is a liability the principal cannot fully characterize. This is the primary structural problem across all four verticals, distinct from tool selection, disclosure, or quality control problems.

## Signal 08: Evidence Architecture vs Compliance Documentation — Universal Artifact Confusion

**Observation:** All four verticals demonstrate confusion between compliance documentation (documents built to satisfy regulatory requirements) and evidence architecture (documents that prove methodology performed as intended) — and carriers and institutional clients are asking for the latter while firms are building the former.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Pre-AI engagement letter AI audit as unnamed discrete service; billing record structurally misaligned with carrier AI evidence standards
- **Finance:** Methodology performance evidence as specific documentation artifact carriers require distinct from methodology design documentation; point-in-time approval document staleness as active AI system behavior change mechanism
- **Consulting:** Bespoke analytical work performance evidence architecture as consulting-specific documentation category with no proportionate methodology; carrier questionnaire operating as de facto audit not policy compliance exercise
- **Healthcare:** AI training documentation as discrete exposure category; human oversight documentation as discrete service category

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Carriers and institutional clients are probing whether methodology performed — not what the policy says. Firms have policy documents, not performance evidence. The documentation artifact gap is structural: the category required (performance evidence) is different from the category built (policy documentation).

**Market signal interpretation:** The market signal is that evidence architecture is a discrete service category, and the Geneva market has no proportionate methodology for building it at boutique scale. This is a structural service opportunity across all four verticals.
