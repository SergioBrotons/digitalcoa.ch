# ICP Language: Cross-Vertical Synthesis — C290 Phase 05

## Cross-Vertical ICP Definition
Senior principals and decision-makers across Geneva's four professional services verticals — legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare — share a common structural position: AI accountability obligations have arrived through multiple independent channels simultaneously, each vertical is navigating these obligations in isolation, and the informal circuit that forms effective operative standards moves faster than any published framework can track.

## Vertical-Specific ICP Language

### Legal ICP (Phase 01)
**Firm type:** Small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based
**Decision-maker:** Managing partner, senior associate with supervisory responsibility
**Acute pressure point:** Carrier attestation renewal — personal signature on undefined adequacy standard
**Internal language:** "We've been using AI for two years and I don't know what we committed to"; "The carrier form asks questions I don't know how to answer"; "I signed something and I'm not sure what it means for my personal exposure"
**Anxiety register:** Personal liability, bar disciplinary exposure, client confidentiality breach, Article 321 CP criminal exposure
**Urgency trigger:** Carrier renewal date, associate departure, client audit request, bar guidance absence
**Geneva-specific reference frame:** French-language legal tech content gap, SBA guidelines as principle without practice, Ordre des Avocats guidance vacuum

### Finance ICP (Phase 02)
**Firm type:** Boutique wealth management, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based
**Decision-maker:** Managing Director / CEO, Senior Portfolio Manager
**Acute pressure point:** Institutional client AI governance questionnaire arriving quarterly, carrier third-generation attestation, FINMA anticipation
**Internal language:** "My client sent a 40-page AI governance questionnaire — I don't know what they're actually asking for"; "The carrier form requires my personal signature for something undefined"; "I think I'm running AI governance but I can't show the documentation"
**Anxiety register:** Personal criminal liability under nFADP Art.63, institutional client DDQ response failure, carrier coverage denial, MD as de facto Chief AI Officer without infrastructure
**Urgency trigger:** Institutional client audit, carrier renewal, portfolio construction AI review, documentation window close
**Geneva-specific reference frame:** SFAMA/AMAS/SRO guidance vacuum, institutional client questionnaire as competitive intelligence instrument, carrier iteration pattern tracking as discrete intelligence function

### Consulting ICP (Phase 03)
**Firm type:** Small consulting boutique, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based
**Decision-maker:** Managing Director, Partner
**Acute pressure point:** Client questionnaire portfolio accumulation, carrier attestation personal signature, sub-consultant AI delegation chain
**Internal language:** "We've done AI-assisted work for clients and I don't know what's in their engagement letters about AI"; "When a sub-consultant uses AI, am I responsible for their output?"; "I signed the carrier form and I still don't know what 'adequate' means"
**Anxiety register:** Personal liability through engagement letter architecture collapse, client counter-questionnaire accountability chain, peer circuit silence as accountability embarrassment signal, Art.398 CO proper performance with vendor terms incompatibility
**Urgency trigger:** Client AI governance audit, carrier renewal, engagement letter review, new client onboarding with AI questions
**Geneva-specific reference frame:** No SVA equivalent, carrier questionnaire as de facto operative standard without consulting input, Geneva accountability vacuum as structural amplification mechanism

### Healthcare ICP (Phase 04)
**Firm type:** Independent physician practice, 2–10 physicians, Geneva-based
**Decision-maker:** Lead physician, practice manager with compliance function
**Acute pressure point:** Patient-initiated AI content entering medical records, FMH guidance without operational endpoint, vendor terms technical inability to opt out
**Internal language:** "Patients are arriving with AI printouts and I don't know what to do with them"; "The software updated and now there's an AI feature I didn't choose to activate"; "I have zero documentation of AI training and I'm not sure what I'm supposed to maintain"
**Anxiety register:** FMH accountability for AI output not deployed or supervised, medical record authorship ambiguity, cantonal guidance vacuum, second-physician AI consultation accountability
**Urgency trigger:** Patient complaint, carrier attestation, medical record audit, staff departure with AI knowledge
**Geneva-specific reference frame:** Service de la santé publique canton-specific guidance absence, CHUV/HUG institutional intelligence gap, Geneva cantonal institutional caution signal

## Cross-Vertical Shared Language Patterns

### Universal Entry Signal
All four verticals report carrier attestation or equivalent professional instrument as the acute entry point — not strategic AI governance planning, not regulatory compliance, but a predetermined calendar event forcing reckoning with undefined obligations.

### Documentation Window Framing
Across all verticals, the documentation window is described as a positioning occasion before formal standards arrive — "before the frameworks named it" is the consistent temporal framing for why now matters.

### Geneva Accountability Formation Language
- "Geneva-specific" — used across all verticals to distinguish operative standards formed in Geneva from formal Swiss doctrine
- "Informal circuit" — the non-publishable intelligence network where accountability expectations actually form
- "Carrier iteration tracking" — the observation that carrier questionnaires themselves are the most operationally specific standard-setting mechanism
- "Accountability formation velocity" — the structural observation that informal mechanisms move faster than formal process

### Personal Liability Language
Across all verticals: "personal signature," "personal exposure," "I signed something I'm not sure I understood," "my name is on the document."

### Evidence Architecture Language
"Documentation that survives the question" — the framing that carrier and institutional clients are not asking for compliance documentation but evidence architecture that proves methodology performed.

### Geneva Sui Generis Language
"Geneva is not just a Swiss jurisdiction for AI accountability purposes" — the structural observation that Geneva's institutional density creates operative accountability mechanisms Bern cannot observe or control.
