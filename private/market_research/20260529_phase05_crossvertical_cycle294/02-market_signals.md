## Cross-Vertical Market Pattern Analysis — C294

### Pattern 1: Structural Accountability Gap Is the Market

Across all four verticals, the market is not defined by a lack of AI awareness. It is defined by a structural inability to build accountability documentation proportionate to practice scale. This gap is not fillable by improving existing documents — it requires a different documentation architecture. No Geneva service currently offers this.

**Market characterization:** ~1,800–2,630 firms across four verticals simultaneously facing:
- Acute personal liability exposure
- Carrier / association attestation pressure with no methodology
- Documentation that cannot answer the questions being asked
- No boutique-calibrated methodology anywhere in the market

### Pattern 2: Carrier Attestation Wave — Differential Timing, Same Destination

The carrier / association attestation wave is arriving on different timelines across verticals — but all four will converge within 18–24 months.

| Vertical | Attestation Wave Status | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| **Legal** | Active — SAV guidelines published June 2024; firms beginning to be asked | NOW |
| **Finance** | Active — third-generation carrier questions arriving; institutional client DDQs | NOW |
| **Consulting** | Moderate — insurance brokers pricing AI governance at renewal | 6–12 months |
| **Healthcare** | Early — carrier pre-attestation beginning; FMH guidance principle-level | 6–12 months |

**Implication:** All four verticals will face carrier attestation pressure within 12–24 months. The documentation window is current for legal and finance; opening for consulting and healthcare. This is the market entry window.

### Pattern 3: Documentation Window Is Present-Tense and Irreversible

The most powerful cross-vertical market signal from C294: firms building documentation during the open window are building defensible position. Firms deferring documentation are accumulating exposure that cannot be discharged retroactively by subsequent documentation effort.

**Why irreversibility:** Retroactive documentation produces records that reveal themselves as retroactive upon examination. Regulators and carriers will know the difference between contemporaneous documentation (built while decisions were being made) and reconstructed documentation (built after the fact, when questions arrive).

This means:
- The documentation gap is widening for firms that haven't started — not static
- Waiting does not reduce exposure; it increases it by accumulating window-period decisions without documentation
- The window is not a deadline metaphor — it is a present-tense positioning event

### Pattern 4: Peer Disclosure Unlocks Action Across All Verticals

Across all four verticals, peer disclosure is the single strongest activation mechanism:

- Legal: "A colleague showed me their SAV documentation checklist" → action
- Finance: "My carrier renewal is in 90 days — what did your MD do?" → urgency
- Consulting: "Our client's AI DDQ revealed we couldn't respond" → lost engagement
- Healthcare: "A physician at the FMH event mentioned they'd reviewed their vendor terms" → event attendance

The Geneva informal circuit — professional dinners, cantonal association events, peer referrals — operates as the trust transmission mechanism. DigitalCoa.ch's sustained participation in this circuit is itself the primary distribution channel.

### Pattern 5: Sub-Associate-Level AI Use Is Ubiquitous but Ungoverned

Shadow AI has arrived across all four verticals — but in different forms:

- **Legal:** Associates using consumer ChatGPT on client matter data without authorization
- **Finance:** Principals deploying agentic AI (continuous portfolio management) without authorization documentation architecture
- **Consulting:** Sub-consultants using AI on client projects; primary consultant presenting sub-consultant AI output as own
- **Healthcare:** Staff using AI tools for administrative tasks (scheduling, billing, patient communication) without policy

**The common result:** Zero visibility, zero control, no compliant DPA, and full accountability falls on the principal regardless of who made the decision.

### Pattern 6: Insurance Market Pricing AI Governance Now

Insurance brokers at firms across all four verticals are asking AI governance questions at proposal and renewal — and pricing accordingly. Firms without documented AI governance are being quoted higher premiums or declined outright. This is happening before any standardized SMB framework exists.

This makes insurance underwriting a present-tense sales barrier and a present-tense competitive differentiator:

- Firm with documentation → different premium treatment
- Firm without documentation → declined or priced into a risk category

**Strategic opportunity:** Offer insurance underwriting documentation as a discrete service component — not generic AI governance, specifically insurer-ready documentation that a broker can evaluate.

### Pattern 7: No Boutique Advisor Has Claimed This Space — First Mover Window Open

The market signal across all four verticals is identical to C294 (and consistent with prior cycles): no Geneva-based boutique advisory firm has positioned itself as the trusted accountability infrastructure provider for SMB professional practices using AI. The competitive landscape remains:
- Big 4: Structurally out of range (price, firm size, enterprise focus)
- Vertical-specific vendors: Tool-centric (not advisory or accountability infrastructure)
- Professional associations: Guidance only, no hands-on methodology
- nFADP compliance consultants: General data protection, not AI-specific, not practice-scale calibrated

**Window assessment:** Open; estimated 12–18 months before clearer competitive signals emerge.

## Timing Signals

### Immediate (NOW — Q2 2026)

Legal and finance verticals: carrier attestation questions already arriving at renewals. Third-generation carrier questions now asking for specific human authorization evidence architecture. Documentation window irreversibility actively accumulating for firms not yet building.

### 6–12 Months (Q3–Q4 2026)

Consulting and healthcare verticals: carrier / insurance broker pressure will intensify as AI governance questions become standard at renewal. Institutional clients' AI DDQs will become more sophisticated and more common across fin/consulting.

Healthcare attestation wave arrives in force. First enforcement reference cases emerge (FDPIC, FINMA, FMH). Early adopters begin sharing results — positive (peer validation) and negative (documentation failures). nFADP enforcement cases begin building the reference case library.

Competitive landscape begins to form more clearly. Price points for SMB AI governance advisory stabilize. ISO 42001 becoming standard procurement qualification for consulting firms and WM boutiques.
