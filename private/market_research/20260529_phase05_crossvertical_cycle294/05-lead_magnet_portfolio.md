## Portfolio Structural Logic

All four lead magnets share the same architecture:

```
PAIN ACKNOWLEDGMENT
    ↓
REGULATORY HOOK (nFADP — universally applicable)
    ↓
NAMED PROBLEMS (6 findings, names in plain language)
    ↓
DOCUMENTATION FRAMEWORK AXES (3 per vertical)
    ↓
CTA: "Book a 30-minute AI Readiness Check — No cost, no obligation"
```

This architecture is:
- Consistent across all four verticals
- Adaptable per vertical (replace findings, keep framework structure)
- Producible once the Phase 01 draft is finalized (structure ready for Phase 02–04)

## Laddering and Cross-Sell Strategy

### Content Stage

All four lead magnets: free PDF download in EN + FR.

**Cross-sell trigger at download stage:**
"Your firm's specific vertical is [legal/finance/consulting/healthcare]. If you're a [adjacent vertical] and want the framework for your sector specifically, download that one too — they're free and designed to be read together."

**Network effect built in:**
- Legal firm principal → has WM clients, consulting clients, healthcare contacts
- Consulting firm MD → has legal, finance, healthcare contacts in client network
- Healthcare practice owner → has physician peer network, professional society contacts

### Readiness Check Stage

30-minute structured conversation using condensed framework.

**Cross-sell trigger at readiness check:**
"As we're going through your [legal/finance/consulting/healthcare] AI documentation — I happen to have done similar work with firms in [adjacent vertical]. The same nFADP logic applies but with different attestation requirements. Here's what came up for them."

### Advisory Engagement Stage

Primary engagement by vertical; cross-sell to adjacent verticals through established relationship.

**Referral network integration:**
Accountants, banks, and insurance brokers in Geneva see all four verticals. A referral relationship with a Geneva insurance broker reaches consulting, legal, and healthcare simultaneously.

## Criteria for Lead Magnet Quality

**Every lead magnet must:**
- Start with a named, specific problem (not "AI governance is important")
- Use the ICP's language, not DigitalCoa.ch's language
- Connect regulatory obligations directly to personal principal liability
- Provide a usable framework, not just advice
- Have a clear, single CTA at the end
- Be downloadable in EN and FR

**No lead magnet should:**
- Sound like vendor marketing
- Use generic AI governance language ("harness the power of AI")
- Promise outcomes that cannot be verified
- Be longer than 12 pages in final form
- Be written before the 6 NET-NEW findings are confirmed
