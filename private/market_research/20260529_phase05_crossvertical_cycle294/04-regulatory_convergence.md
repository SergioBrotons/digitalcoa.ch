## Regulatory Landscape — Universal and Vertical-Specific

### The Universal Layer: nFADP

nFADP (Federal Act on Data Protection / LRPD) applies without exception to all four verticals. It is the universal regulatory hook — mandatory, enforceable, and creating urgency across every vertical.

**nFADP Articles Core to AI Accountability — All Four Verticals:**

| Article | Content | Vertical Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) | Sensitive personal data categories: health, financial, client | All — healthcare highest sensitivity |
| Art. 8 | Profiling — right to notification | WM (portfolio profiling), Legal (client risk profiling) |
| Art. 9 | Conditions for processing sensitive data | All |
| Art. 12 | Information obligations (right to know data processed) | All |
| Art. 14 | Right to access (data subjects) | All |
| Art. 20/21 | Right to object / explanation rights | All |
| Art. 22 | Automated decision reversal right | Healthcare (most acute), Legal, Finance |
| Art. 26 | Data security obligations | All |
| Art. 29 | Data protection impact assessments | All (for AI systems processing sensitive data) |
| Art. 63 | Criminal sanctions (up to CHF 250,000) | All — personal liability, named in C294 every vertical |

**Universal regulatory signal:** nFADP is not just a compliance checkbox. It is a personal liability framework. Art. 63 criminal liability applies to individuals who sign engagement letters, vendor agreements, and carrier attestations. This is under-communicated across all four verticals and is the specific hook that activates principal-level urgency.

## The Documentation Architecture Problem — Cross-Vertical Finding (C294)

The most strategically significant regulatory convergence insight from C294 is not about which articles apply — it is about documentation architecture.

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, the specific documentation gap is architectural, not operational. Firms are building documents (policies, DPAs, governance statements) and finding that those documents cannot answer the questions carriers, associations, and clients are actually asking.

**Two types of documentation architecture — which most firms are building vs. what they need:**

| Documentation Type | Purpose | What It Cannot Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Administrative compliance documentation** | Satisfy checkbox regulators; produce evidence of general controls | Withstand regulatory scrutiny from attestation instruments; provide criminal defense architecture |
| **Carrier / attestation evidence documentation** | Satisfy underwriting / attestation standards (designed by carriers) | Satisfy nFADP Art.63 criminal liability standard; demonstrate contemporaneous decision trail under cross-examination |

**The architectural incompatibility:** Most firms are building administrative compliance documentation and assuming it will serve both purposes. The two documentation architectures are designed for different standards, different evidentiary thresholds, and different defense mechanisms.

**Why this is cross-vertical:** This architectural gap is confirmed across all four verticals:
- Legal: "A framework without a decision tree" — SAV attestation without documentation
- Finance: Carrier attestation architecture (underwriting) ≠ criminal defense architecture (Art.63)
- Consulting: Insurance broker documentation ≠ nFADP Art.63 response
- Healthcare: Consent architecture (treatment + nFADP + AI) → three incompatible obligations

## The Documentation Window Concept — Cross-Vertical Finding (C294)

**The finding:** The documentation window is not a deadline metaphor. It is a present-tense, irreversible positioning event.

Firms building documentation during the current window are building evidence of what human authorization preceded AI-assisted decisions. Firms deferring AI governance documentation investment are accumulating decisions made without documentation — and retroactive documentation cannot reconstruct window-period reasoning.

Retroactive documentation produces records that demonstrate themselves as retroactive upon examination. Regulators and carriers operating sophisticated scrutiny will know the difference between contemporaneous documentation (built while decisions were being made) and reconstructed documentation (built after the fact).

**The implication:** Waiting to build documentation until a carrier asks specific questions means building against the current standard (not the window-period standard), producing documents that reveal themselves as reconstructed, and not being able to discharge the window-period exposure through subsequent effort.

This is a present-tense obligation against a future-tense deadline. The window is closing on decisions already made.

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Convergence Insights

### Insight 1: nFADP Art.63 Personal Liability Is the Universal Urgency Trigger

Across all four verticals, C294 confirmed that nFADP Art.63 criminal liability (up to CHF 250,000 personal fine, applies to individuals) is under-communicated to the specific ICP. General liability insurance does not cover nFADP criminal liability. Personal assets are exposed. The individual who signs engagement letters, vendor agreements, and carrier attestations has personal exposure they have not named, quantified, or protected against.

**This is the universal hook.** Every principal across all four verticals has signed vendor agreements and AI tool terms of service personally. Most don't know what those signatures committed them to personally.

### Insight 2: The Regulatory Framework Stack Is the Core Differentiation

The combination of:
- **nFADP compliance** (table stakes, universally understood)
- **ISO 42001 AI governance** (differentiating, becoming procurement-relevant for consulting and WM)
- **Professional-specific liability framing** (legal: Art.13 BGFA; finance: carrier attestation; consulting: Art.63; healthcare: Art.321 CP)

This stack positions DigitalCoa.ch as the advisor that moves SMBs from basic nFADP compliance to attestation-ready accountability infrastructure. The nFADP-ISO 42001 stack is the cross-vertical value proposition.

### Insight 3: Attestation Instruments Are Becoming Cross-Vertical Market Signals

Carrier / association attestation documents are now arriving across all four verticals:
- Legal: SAV AI guidelines create de facto attestation expectations
- Finance/WM: Third-generation carrier attestation questionnaires active
- Consulting: Insurance brokers pricing AI governance risk at renewal
- Healthcare: Carrier pre-attestation documents beginning to arrive

The attestation instrument is the market's way of converting regulatory uncertainty into specific documentation requirements. DigitalCoa.ch's pre-signing review (before the principal signs the attestation) is a discrete service component that no competitor is providing.

**The service concept:** Map what the attestation commits you to before you commit to it. Build documentation that's designed for that specific attestation instrument, not generic compliance documentation.
