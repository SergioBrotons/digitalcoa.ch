### Universal Pain — Named in C294

**"Accountability without a map"** — every vertical's ICP has personal liability for AI decisions they cannot confidently defend.

- **Legal:** Personal liability for auxiliary persons under Art.13 BGFA / Art.321 StGB; SAV accountability without a compliance path
- **Finance:** Carrier attestation commits personal liability without defining what "adequate" means
- **Consulting:** nFADP Art.63 criminal liability for individuals; personal assets exposed
- **Healthcare:** Same physician holds user + compliance + billing roles simultaneously — structurally impossible to fulfill "oversight" as a principle

**"Documentation that doesn't answer the questions actually being asked"** — documents exist but can't withstand scrutiny.

- **Legal:** DPA template doesn't embed BGFA obligations; policy exists but can't be shown to SAV
- **Finance:** Carrier attestation evidence architecture designed for underwriting, not criminal defense
- **Consulting:** Insurance broker asking documentation the firm doesn't have
- **Healthcare:** No consent document calibrated to Art.22 / Art.5 / AI deployment simultaneously

**"No methodology exists at my scale"** — all four verticals experience the same absence.

- No Geneva-calibrated tool-assessment framework for 2–5 attorney firms (Legal)
- No carrier pre-signing review methodology for boutique WM (Finance)
- No project-based sub-consultant AI delegation protocol (Consulting)
- No medical-practice-calibrated AI governance audit system (Healthcare)

## Cross-Vertical NET-NEW Finding: The Accountability Architecture Gap

**Finding:** Across all four verticals, the specific market gap is not more AI tooling, more guidelines, or more templates. It is a way to make defensible AI decisions and document them for clients, regulators, carriers, and peers. The accountability infrastructure for small professional practices using AI does not exist in the Geneva market.

**What exists everywhere:** AI tools, SAV guidelines (Legal), FINMA guidance (Finance), generic DPA templates, enterprise GRC frameworks, ISO 42001 certification bodies, professional association guidance.

**What does not exist for any vertical at boutique scale:**
- A practical tool-assessment framework against specific professional obligations
- A written attestation product defensible before clients, SAV, carriers, and courts
- A scaled, proportionate governance framework defining "adequate" for 1–15 person practices
- A short, actionable resource distilling vertical-specific obligations into immediate steps
- A pre-signing review methodology before committing to carrier attestation

**The strategic implication for DigitalCoa.ch:** Positioning should not be "AI legal platform" or "AI governance consultant." It should be **"the accountability infrastructure for Geneva professional practices using AI."** The entry point is personal responsibility, not productivity gains.
