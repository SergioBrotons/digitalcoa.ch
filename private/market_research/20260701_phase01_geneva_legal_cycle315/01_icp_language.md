### The Language They Use (C315 — July 2026 register)

**"The Swiss FMEC has been designated as the AI federal coordinator. What does that mean for my firm?"**
Member State authority activation. The Swiss Federal Ministry of Economic Affairs, Education and Research (WBF/FMEC) — alongside FOEN — has been designated as the federal coordinator for AI Act coordination with the EU AI Office. EU Member States (Germany BNetzA, France CNIL/DGCCRF, Italy AgID, Spain AESIA, Netherlands AP) have designated or are finalizing market surveillance authorities. The Geneva firm is asking which authority will send it a notice. The answer is conditional on the firm's classification (provider, deployer, importer, distributor), the firm's location (CH vs EU), and the firm's clients (EU persons or matters in EU).

**"My peer firm in Geneva was named in a formal notice from a market surveillance authority. Is that what I should expect?"**
First named-individual deployer action. The first market surveillance notices have been issued to legal services deployers. The notices are addressed to the legal person (the firm) but reference named individuals (the managing partner, the associate who used the tool, the AI tool vendor contact). The Geneva firm is asking whether its peer has just become a template for what is coming. The carrier will ask at next renewal. The bar will ask on next OaF survey. The answer to the peer is becoming the answer to the market.

**"My carrier added three new EU AI Act attestation clauses at the mid-year refresh. I do not know what they mean."**
Carrier attestation update cycle. Carriers (Swiss Re, Zurich Insurance, Allianz, AXA, Generali, Bâloise) have updated annual attestation language to reflect the post-deadline liability environment. The new language includes EU AI Act Article 9 risk management attestation, Article 12 logging audit response attestation, and Article 26 named-sub-principal attribution attestation. The Geneva firm that signs without analysis is signing an attestation it has not understood.

**"My carrier sent me a letter asking me to respond to an Article 12 logging audit request from a market surveillance authority. The vendor logs show my firm's activity. What do I do?"**
Article 12 logging audit trigger. The first market surveillance authorities have begun Article 12 logging audit requests to legal AI vendors (Harvey, Thomson Reuters CoCounsel, Lexis+ AI, Westlaw AI, Spellbook, vLex Vincent). The vendor's logging infrastructure is the regulator's window into the firm's compliance state. The Geneva firm that has not configured zero-content logging is exposed through the vendor's logs. The auditor sees: which tool, which matter, which user, which timestamp. The Geneva firm sees: a letter from the carrier asking how to respond.

**"The OaF sent a post-deadline guidance. Article 26 references an associate as named-sub-principal. Is the OaF following EU AI Act now?"**
Article 26 named-sub-principal enforcement trigger. Article 26 of the EU AI Act extends deployer obligations to natural persons (named individuals) who authorize or use AI on behalf of the deployer. The OaF (and FSA cantonal directives) have begun referencing Article 26 in post-deadline guidance. The named-sub-principal is the associate who used the tool. The bar is now read to enforce EU AI Act through its own disciplinary instrument. The Geneva firm is asking whether its associates are now personally exposed under both regimes simultaneously.

**"My client is now asking whether my firm has been cited, audited, or sanctioned under EU AI Act. How do I answer?"**
Client-driven enforcement inquiry reversal. The C314 reversal was client → firm (asking readiness). The C315 reversal is client → firm (asking enforcement history). The institutional EU client that asked about readiness in C314 is now asking about enforcement in C315. The Geneva firm that has never been the subject of a market surveillance action cannot cite one. The Geneva firm that has been is now publishing it. The Geneva firm that has not been is now answering "no enforcement history" and watching the question get sharper.
