# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal — C315 Phase 01

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing (T-Plus 30 Days After EU AI Act Applicability, July 2026)

---

## Market Mapping (C315)

The Geneva legal AI services market in July 2026 contains the same six provider categories as C314 (Big-Four adjacent, Bar Association, Carrier frameworks, Vendor compliance, ISO/NIST framework practitioners, Geneva boutique AI specialists). However, the C315 market has shifted — providers that addressed C314 readiness signals have not yet addressed C315 enforcement signals. White space has opened in every category.

### Category A: Big-Four-Adjacent AI Governance Consulting

**Who they are:** Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG — Geneva offices and Swiss desks.

**What they offer:**
- Enterprise AI governance frameworks (ISO/IEC 42001)
- NIST AI RMF implementations
- EU AI Act readiness assessments (C314-style)
- Operational risk consulting

**What they charge:** CHF 50,000–250,000+ per engagement.

**What they miss for the C315 ICP:**
- Designed for enterprise (50+ attorneys), not boutique (2-5 attorneys)
- Cost structure exceeds boutique budget
- Frameworks, not enforcement navigation
- No MSA designation map for a Geneva boutique
- No named-individual deployer action response methodology
- No carrier attestation update cycle translation
- No Article 12 logging audit response methodology
- No Article 26 named-sub-principal bar guidance interpretation
- No post-deadline client disclosure methodology

**Gap addressed:** None of the six C315 findings. The big-four consulting market still speaks readiness (C314). It has not yet produced enforcement (C315).

### Category B: Bar Association Guidance

**Who they are:** Ordre des avocats de Genève (OaF), Fédération Suisse des Avocats (FSA), Geneva Bar Association.

**What they offer:**
- C314-style AI supervision guidance
- Post-deadline AI guidance bulletin referencing Article 26 (June 2026)
- Templates for AI use policies
- Ethics opinions on AI use
- Disciplinary framework

**What they charge:** Membership fees only.

**What they miss for the C315 ICP:**
- Guidance is generic, not boutique-specific
- Does not address MSA jurisdictional navigation
- Does not address named-individual deployer action response
- Does not address carrier attestation update cycle implications
- Does not address Article 12 logging audit response
- Does not address post-deadline client disclosure methodology
- Article 26 reference is generic, not boutique-specific
- No integration with carrier attestation or MSA procedural posture

**What the bar has just added (post-deadline):**
- June 2026 bulletin referencing EU AI Act Article 26 named-sub-principal
- Updated FSA cantonal directive on Article 26 attribution to attorneys
- Interim guidance on Article 12 logging audit response posture

**Gap addressed:** The bar has begun to address C315. It has not addressed four of the six C315 findings (MSA designation map, named-individual response, carrier attestation translation, client disclosure). White space remains.

**What carriers have just added (post-deadline):**

## Competitive Implication (C315)

The C315 competitive reality: every service provider category that addressed C314 readiness signals has not yet addressed C315 enforcement signals. The white space is concentrated in the Geneva boutique AI specialist category, where DigitalCoa is the front-runner.

The C315 service opportunity: the first service to publish the six-module enforcement navigation architecture — with specific reference to T-plus 30 and named-individual exposure — owns the C315 category. The first mover has the C314 architecture foundation. The C315 module additions complete the post-deadline category.

The C315 competitive moat: the named-individual evidentiary architecture. Only named-individual evidence (specific attorney, specific tool, specific matter, specific timestamp) satisfies the post-deadline enforcement perimeter. Framework evidence (policy, control, certification) does not. The first Geneva boutique specialist to publish the named-individual evidence architecture owns the C315 enforcement category.

## C315 Core Synthesis

**The market has moved from "T-minus 30 days, am I in scope?" (C314) to "T-plus 30 days, has the first notice been sent?" (C315).**

C314 captured the pre-deadline readiness conditions: cross-border applicability uncertainty, logging conflict, hallucination liability, privilege waiver, cross-matter contamination, three-regime reconciliation.

C315 captures the post-deadline enforcement conditions: MSA designation map, named-individual deployer action response, carrier attestation translation, Article 12 logging audit navigation, Article 26 named-sub-principal bar coordination, client-driven enforcement inquiry reversal.

The C315 service is the C314 service plus six new enforcement navigation modules. Same evidence architecture. Twenty-four modules total.

The first Geneva boutique AI accountability service to publish the twenty-four-module architecture — with specific reference to T-plus 30 days and post-deadline enforcement — owns the C315 category. The first actor to deliver the MSA designation map, the named-individual deployer response, the carrier attestation translation, the Article 12 logging audit response, the Article 26 named-sub-principal bar coordination, and the post-deadline client disclosure methodology — all delivered through one evidence architecture — owns the C315 brand.
