# Phase 06 — Geneva Real Estate: Ideal Client Profile
## Cycle 20 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Small Real Estate Agencies

---

## ICP: The Geneva Small Real Estate Agency

### Demographics
- **Firm type:** Small residential/commercial agencies (2–10 agents), property managers
- **Location:** Geneva canton (primary); Vaud secondary
- **Revenue context:** Transaction-based; price-sensitive on compliance costs; squeezed by market conditions
- **Technology posture:** AI tools in use — property listings, valuation models, CRM, marketing automation, tenant screening
- **Decision-maker:** Agency principal/owner (personally owns compliance risk)

### Pain Points
1. **67% AI adoption:** HIGHEST of any portfolio vertical — widest governance gap
2. **GAIGI operative ~7-10 months:** Geneva real estate sector AI governance category established
3. **Retroactive compliance unique:** "govern the AI you already have" — lower-friction entry point
4. **nFADP active now:** AI transparency obligations for tenant/client data
5. **Swissdamed ~13 WEEKS spillover:** Via property management companies with healthcare client exposure

### Timing Assessment
- **GAIGI operative:** ~7-10 months (Fall 2025 confirmed launch)
- **nFADP Art. 5(c):** Active NOW — AI transparency for personal data processing
- **Swissdamed Jul 1 ~13 WEEKS:** Spillover via healthcare exposure in property management
- **EU AI Act Aug 2 ~4.5 months:** For EU-nexus agencies

### Competitive Landscape (7th+ Confirmation)
**No boutique competitor specifically for small RE agency AI governance**

### Addressable Market
- **Geneva: ~400–600 small real estate agencies/property managers**
- **Geneva + Vaud: ~600–900**
- **Switzerland-wide: ~1,500–2,500 small RE agencies**

### Unique Entry Point
**Retroactive compliance framing:** "govern the AI you already have" — lower friction than anticipatory framing in other verticals
