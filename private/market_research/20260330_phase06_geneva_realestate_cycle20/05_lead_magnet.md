## 7-Section Framework

### Section 1: Geneva RE AI Reality Check

**67% AI adoption — highest of any portfolio vertical.**

AI tools in use in Geneva RE agencies:
- Property valuation AI (automated valuation models, comparable analysis)
- Tenant screening AI (credit checks, background screening)
- CRM and marketing AI (lead scoring, automated follow-up)
- Property listing optimization AI
- Maintenance request triage AI

Most agencies: using these tools daily. None: have documented AI governance.

---

### Section 2: Q1 — What AI Are You Using?

For each AI tool in your agency:
- Who has access?
- What decisions does it influence?
- Is it working correctly?

**If you can't answer these three questions, you have a governance gap.**

---

### Section 3: Q2 — What Data Is Being Processed?

AI tools processing:
- Tenant personal data (names, addresses, financial information)
- Owner financial information
- Property transaction data
- Employee data

**AI transparency obligations under nFADP Art. 5(c) are active.** You must disclose AI use when processing personal data.

---

### Section 4: Q3 — Do You Have nFADP Obligations?

If you process personal data through AI tools (and you almost certainly do): YES.

nFADP Art. 5(c) requires transparency when AI processes personal data.
nFADP Art. 22 may require a DPIA for high-risk AI processing.

These obligations have been active since September 1, 2023.

**The gap: most RE agencies haven't addressed them.**

---

### Section 5: The Human Accountability Principle

Real estate transactions involve large financial commitments, personal lives, and significant liability.

AI tools make recommendations. Humans bear the accountability.

**The governance question isn't: should we use AI?**
**The governance question is: how do we use AI responsibly while maintaining human accountability for every decision?**

### Section 7: Retroactive Compliance Path

**"Govern the AI you already have."**

Unlike other verticals (anticipatory framing: "prepare for a future deadline"), RE has a unique entry: **retroactive compliance.**

Most RE agencies are already using AI. The question isn't whether to adopt AI — it's whether to govern the AI you're already running.

**Step 1 — AI Inventory (Week 1):** Document every AI tool in use. What data enters, what processing occurs, who has access, what decisions does it influence?

**Step 2 — Disclosure (Week 2):** Add AI disclosure to tenant/buyer representation agreements. One paragraph.

**Step 3 — Accountability Policy (Week 3):** Document who is accountable for AI-assisted decisions. One page.

**Step 4 — Review (Quarterly):** Review AI tool usage when adopting new tools.
