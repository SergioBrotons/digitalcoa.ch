# 01 — ICP Language: Cross-Vertical ICP Language — The Universal Register

*Phase 05 · Cycle 289 · 2026-05-28*
*Synthesis across Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs*

---

## Purpose

This file identifies the language patterns that function identically across all four ICP verticals — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — and explains why the same linguistic structures emerge from structurally identical accountability problems.

---

## The Core Finding

The four Geneva professional ICPs — small-law attorney, boutique WM managing director, consulting MD, independent physician — use the same four linguistic structures when describing their AI accountability problems.

They have never been told this. They do not know they are all saying the same thing.

This is the cross-vertical ICP language insight: **the accountability experience is sector-identical even though the sector context is different.**

---

## The Four Universal Linguistic Structures

### Structure 1: "I Signed Something I Didn't Understand"

**Legal:**
> "I signed the carrier attestation because I had to. I didn't know what I was committing to personally."

**Finance:**
> "I checked the box on the carrier attestation. That's not a strategy."

**Consulting:**
> "I signed it and then realized what it meant. The attestation was written by the carrier's lawyers."

**Healthcare:**
> "I signed the carrier form because that's what you do at renewal. I didn't review what I was committing to personally."

**The linguistic mechanism:** First-person past-tense of signing, followed by realization, followed by the discovery that the document was created by someone else's legal team and commits to an undefined standard. The ICP signs under time pressure. The meaning arrives after the signature.

**Why it works across verticals:** The carrier form is the same instrument in every sector — written by underwriting teams for carrier risk evaluation, presented as a signature requirement for policy renewal, experienced by the signatory as a personal liability instrument they never evaluated. No Geneva professional body in any sector offers pre-signing review of the carrier form from the signatory's perspective.

---

### Structure 2: "AI Arrived Before I Governed It"

**Legal:**
> "My associates use AI constantly. I didn't review what they were using."

**Finance:**
> "Every vendor contract disclaimers liability back to me. I deployed the tool. The liability followed me."

**Consulting:**
> "Sub-consultants are using AI tools I haven't evaluated. I'm accountable for everything that goes out under my name."

**Healthcare:**
> "I didn't decide to use AI. It arrived through a practice management update. I didn't sign anything."

**The linguistic mechanism:** Passive adoption framing — AI is received, not chosen. The ICP did not make a governance decision; governance arrived too late. The AI surface in their professional practice predates any policy, review, or authorization architecture.

**Why it works across verticals:** At small-firm and independent-practice scale, AI adoption occurs through staff self-service, vendor platform updates, and embedded features in existing software — not through documented governance decisions made by the accountable principal. The accountable principal is then personally liable for AI they did not authorize.

---

### Structure 3: "The Guidance Doesn't Tell Me What to Do on a Tuesday"

**Legal:**
> "SBA says adequate supervision. I don't know what adequate supervision means for a 3-attorney firm on a Tuesday."

**Finance:**
> "FINMA hasn't told us what they expect. But you feel it coming. I want to be ready but I don't know what ready looks like."

**Consulting:**
> "We don't have a framework for that. FMH guidance for physicians is the same problem — principles without operational translation."

**Healthcare:**
> "FMH says appropriate oversight. It doesn't define what oversight looks like when you're the only person making decisions and you're also responsible for everything."

**The linguistic mechanism:** The ICP has read available guidance. The guidance is correct in principle. The guidance does not provide operational endpoints — it does not answer the question "what do I do this week." The ICP experiences the guidance as confirmation that the guidance vacuum exists, not as resolution of it.

**Why it works across verticals:** Professional body guidance in every sector is calibrated to the scale of large institutions, not to the structural configuration of the small firm or independent practice where one person holds user, compliance, and accountability roles simultaneously. The guidance is structurally inapplicable at the scale where it is needed most.

---

### Structure 4: "I Don't Know What Standard I'm Being Evaluated Against"

**Legal:**
> "I had no idea we were supposed to have answers for this. The client sent a 30-page questionnaire."

**Finance:**
> "Institutional clients are making AI governance part of their manager selection criteria. Firms that can't respond lose mandates."

**Consulting:**
> "Each client questionnaire creates commitments I can't see as a system. The third response may contradict the first."

**Healthcare:**
> "CHUV is being careful. I see that. I don't know how. The standards forming in those rooms will be applied to me."

**The linguistic mechanism:** Accountability is forming through external mechanisms — client questionnaires, carrier forms, institutional signals — that the ICP did not observe forming and cannot evaluate against any published standard. The ICP is being governed by standards they had no voice in creating.

**Why it works across verticals:** The accountability formation process in Geneva operates faster than information circulates to the individual professional. Standards arrive as requirements, not as guidance. The ICP experiences accountability formation as a retrospective discovery event, not a participatory process.

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language: The Common Register

The four verticals converge on the same register when describing their accountability situation:

| Theme | Legal marker | Finance marker | Consulting marker | Healthcare marker |
|-------|-------------|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Personal signature as accountability moment | "my bar number is on the document" | "I'm the one who signs" | "I'm the one who signs everything" | "my name is on the record" |
| Vendor liability disclaimer gap | "I don't know what the vendor does with data" | "vendor disclaims all liability to me" | "sub-consultant vendor terms I never reviewed" | "the system updated, I didn't authorize it" |
| Guidance inapplicable at scale | "SBA principles don't translate to a 3-attorney firm" | "SRO guidance is generic, not operational" | "no Geneva consulting equivalent to SRO or Ordre" | "FMH oversight principle doesn't scale to one-person practice" |
| Undefined operative standard | "I don't know what adequate AI governance means" | "carrier asks me to attest to something I can't define" | "adequate means what the carrier decided" | "Swissdamed tier descriptions don't describe what I do" |
| Accumulation effect | "I can't evaluate what associates are doing" | "vendor AI accumulating without documentation" | "client questionnaire commitments accumulate" | "AI arrived through updates I approved without reading" |

## The Cross-Vertical Language Pattern

**What all four ICPs are actually saying:**

> "I am personally accountable for something I did not consciously authorize, cannot fully evaluate, and do not have a Geneva-calibrated standard to measure myself against."

This sentence is sector-neutral. It describes the accountability position of:
- A 3-attorney Geneva law firm partner
- A boutique Geneva wealth management MD
- A Geneva consulting boutique managing director
- A Geneva independent physician practice principal

The specificity is identical. The sector context changes the details, not the structure.

## French Cross-Vertical Register

**Structure 1 — "J'ai signé quelque chose que je ne comprenais pas":**

> *Avocat:* « J'ai signé l'attestation parce que je n'avais pas le choix. Je ne savais pas ce que je m'engageais à faire personnellement. »

> *Gérant de fortune:* « J'ai coché la case sur l'attestation. Ce n'est pas une stratégie. »

> *Consultant:* « Je l'ai signé, puis j'ai compris ce que ça signifiait. Le document a été rédigé par les avocats du courtier. »

> *Médecin:* « J'ai signé le formulaire carrier parce que c'est ce qu'on fait au renouvellement. Je n'ai pas examiné ce que je m'engageais à faire. »

**Structure 2 — "L'IA est arrivée avant que je ne la gouvernance":**

> *Avocat:* « Mes collaborateurs utilisent l'IA en permanence. Je n'ai pas examiné ce qu'ils utilisaient. »

> *Gérant de fortune:* « Chaque contrat fournisseur dénie la responsabilité vers moi. J'ai déployé l'outil. La responsabilité m'a suivi. »

> *Consultant:* « Les sous-conseillers utilisent des outils IA que je n'ai jamais évalués. Je suis responsable de tout ce qui porte le nom du cabinet. »

> *Médecin:* « Je n'ai pas décidé d'utiliser l'IA. Elle est arrivée par une mise à jour du système de gestion. Je n'ai rien signé. »

**Structure 3 — "Les directives ne me disent pas quoi faire un mardi":**

> *Avocat:* « La SBA parle de supervision adéquate. Je ne sais pas ce que cela signifie pour un cabinet de 3 avocats un mardi. »

> *Gérant de fortune:* « La FINMA ne nous a pas dit ce qu'elle attend. Mais on sent que ça arrive. Je veux être prêt mais je ne sais pas à quoi ressemble 'prêt'. »

> *Consultant:* « On n'a pas de cadre pour ça. Les directives FMH pour les médecins, c'est le même problème — des principes sans traduction opérationnelle. »

> *Médecin:* « Le FMH parle de surveillance appropriée. Ça ne définit pas à quoi ressemble la surveillance quand je suis la seule personne à décider et que je suis responsable de tout. »

**Structure 4 — "Je ne sais pas contre quelle norme je suis évalué":**

> *Avocat:* « Je n'avais aucune idée qu'on était censés avoir des réponses à cela. Le client a envoyé un questionnaire de 30 pages. »

> *Gérant de fortune:* « Les clients institutionnels font de la gouvernance IA partie de leurs critères de sélection des gérants. Les cabinets qui ne peuvent pas répondre perdent les mandats. »

> *Consultant:* « Chaque questionnaire client crée des engagements que je ne peux pas voir comme un système. La troisième réponse peut contredire la première. »

> *Médecin:* « Le CHUV est prudent. Je le vois. Je ne sais pas comment. Les normes qui se forment dans ces pièces seront appliquées à moi. »

## What This Means for Positioning

The four verticals will respond to the same framing language because they share the same underlying accountability structure. Content that works for Geneva legal will contain linguistic structures that resonate with Geneva finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs — not because the topics are the same, but because the accountability experience is identical.

The cross-vertical lead magnet and all cross-vertical content should use this shared register: **first-person personal accountability, undefined standard, institutional abandonment, retrospective governance, passive AI adoption.**
