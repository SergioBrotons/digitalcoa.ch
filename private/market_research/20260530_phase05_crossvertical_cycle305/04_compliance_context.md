## 6 NET-NEW Cross-Vertical Compliance Context Findings — C305

### Finding 1: The Regulatory Standard Migration — Policy-First to Evidence-First

**Pattern across:** Legal (SAV) + Finance (FINMA) + Consulting (client DDQ) + Healthcare (FMH)

**The pattern:** Regulatory frameworks across all four verticals have shifted from a policy-first to an evidence-first standard. The market is moving from "do you have a policy" to "demonstrate your oversight." This migration is visible in:
- FINMA's shift from documentation review to evidence of ongoing oversight (Phase 02)
- FMH guidance implying human authorization documentation, not just training records (Phase 04)
- Institutional client questionnaires asking for methodology evidence, not policy descriptions (Phase 03)
- Carrier attestation forms measuring documentation completeness, not governance effectiveness (Phase 01 + 04)
- SAV guidelines requiring documented human review, not adequate policy existence (Phase 01)

**Why it is cross-vertical:** The migration is not vertical-specific. Every framework is moving to the same standard: evidence of actual human authorization, not evidence of policy existence. The ICP who built compliance infrastructure based on the policy-first standard is now operating under an evidence-first standard they did not build for.

**Implication:** The compliance infrastructure the ICP built in 2024 does not satisfy the 2026 accountability standard. The gap is structural — not fixable with better policies, only fixable with evidence production.

### Finding 2: The Professional Secrecy Architecture Parallel — Article 321 CC as Universal Exposure

**Pattern across:** Legal (Article 321 CC) + Healthcare (Article 321 CC) + Finance (client confidentiality) + Consulting (client confidentiality)

**The pattern:** Article 321 CC and equivalent professional secrecy obligations create a universal exposure across all four verticals. The specific mechanism:
- AI vendor contracts contain data processing terms that do not account for Swiss professional secrecy requirements
- The vendor's standard DPA does not include the specific protections required for professional client data
- The vendor's change clause allows terms modification without renewed consent — potentially modifying professional secrecy protections without the professional's knowledge
- The professional is liable for the vendor's data processing decisions — the vendor's compliance is not the professional's compliance

**Why it is cross-vertical:** Every Geneva professional practice — legal, healthcare, finance, consulting — has client data that carries professional secrecy obligations. Every practice that has signed an AI vendor DPA has potentially created a professional secrecy disclosure trigger. The exposure is not vertical-specific — it is a function of the vendor contract structure, not the professional type.

**Implication:** The AI Vendor Contract Review + Professional Secrecy Risk Memo is the universal entry service across all four verticals. The deliverable is specific, bounded, and creates evidence of professional review.

### Finding 3: The Documentation Compounding Structure — Three Debt Vectors Simultaneously

**Pattern across:** Consulting (Phase 03) + Legal (Phase 01) + Finance (Phase 02) + Healthcare (Phase 04)

**The pattern:** Documentation debt compounds along three independent vectors simultaneously:
1. **Time vector:** The longer AI tools are used without evidence architecture, the larger the documentation gap grows
2. **Tool count vector:** Each new AI tool added to the practice creates additional documentation obligations without corresponding infrastructure
3. **Staff use vector:** Each additional staff member using AI tools multiplies the documentation requirement across the practice

Each vector compounds independently. The ICP who has adopted 5 tools, has 3 staff using them, and has been operating for 18 months has three compounding debt vectors active simultaneously — not a single documentation gap, but three concurrent accumulating gaps.

**Why it is cross-vertical:** The compounding structure is universal. Every small practice in every vertical faces the same three-vector documentation debt accumulation. The structure is not vertical-specific — it is a function of AI adoption patterns and solo practitioner capacity constraints.

**Implication:** The debt freeze service addresses all three vectors simultaneously. "Stop the compounding. Build forward evidence capture for each vector independently." The entry service must freeze all three vectors, not address them sequentially.

### Finding 4: The Attestation Instrument Design Flaw — Forms Measure the Wrong Thing

**Pattern across:** Carrier attestation forms (Legal + Healthcare) + Institutional questionnaires (Finance + Consulting)

**The pattern:** Every attestation instrument in the Geneva market measures documentation completeness — whether the ICP has policy documents, training records, and framework descriptions. None of them measure governance effectiveness — whether the ICP has documented evidence of human authorization for each AI-assisted decision.

The form creates an adequacy proxy. The ICP completes the form using their policy documents. The carrier receives a completed form. Both parties believe the attestation is adequate. Neither party can distinguish between documentation completeness and governance effectiveness — because the form does not ask for the evidence that would differentiate them.

**Why it is cross-vertical:** The attestation instrument design flaw is not unique to any one carrier or form. It is a structural feature of current attestation instrument design across all four verticals. The market has not yet evolved to require the specific evidence that would demonstrate governance effectiveness.

**Implication:** The attestation adequacy paradox is the mechanism behind the market's current false sense of security. The ICP who believes they have satisfied the attestation requirement has only demonstrated documentation completeness — not evidence of authorization. The gap is invisible to both parties.

**Why it is cross-vertical:** The timeline asymmetry means the ICP is operating under multiple regulatory obligations simultaneously, with different levels of awareness of each. The regulatory pressure environment is not a sequence of discrete events — it is a sustained environment that will intensify through 2026 and 2027.

**Implication:** The ongoing AI Governance Advisory retainer is the only service model that addresses the sustained regulatory pressure environment. ### Finding 6: The Personal vs. Organizational Liability Disconnect — Structural Across All Verticals

**Pattern across:** Legal (Art.63) + Healthcare (Art.63) + Finance (FINMA personal accountability) + Consulting (professional liability)

**The pattern:** Every regulatory framework creates a structural disconnect between organizational liability and personal liability. The carrier's exposure runs to the organization — they pay claims, they face sanctions. The professional's personal exposure runs to their license, their name, and their career — and is not resolved by the carrier's payment of the organizational claim.

The disconnect is:
- FINMA: carrier pays, seeks subrogation against responsible individuals under Art.63
- Healthcare: carrier pays malpractice claim, FMH personal accountability for physician remains
- Legal: carrier pays professional liability claim, personal disciplinary exposure for attorney remains
- Consulting: carrier pays professional liability claim, personal exposure for consulting principal remains

**Why it is cross-vertical:** The personal vs. organizational liability disconnect is universal across all Swiss professional liability frameworks. It is not specific to any vertical — it is a structural feature of how Swiss professional liability operates.

**Implication:** The personal liability framing must lead every ICP conversation. The ICP who understands that the carrier's payment of the organizational claim does not protect their personal license will invest differently than the ICP who thinks the carrier's coverage resolves their exposure.

**Ready-to-use language:**
> "Your carrier paid the organization's claim. That doesn't protect your name. Article 63 creates personal liability for the responsible individual — not just organizational liability. Your carrier's payment was for the firm. Your exposure is personal."
