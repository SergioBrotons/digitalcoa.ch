## Purpose

Identify the language patterns that appear consistently across all four verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) — the shared vocabulary and phrases that signal an ICP in a regulatory pressure moment. Document how THEY talk, not how we talk.

---

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language Patterns

### Pattern Category 1: The Pre-Crisis Language

**"I didn't know I had to document that"**

- Found across: Legal (Phase 01), Healthcare (Phase 04), Consulting (Phase 03)
- Specific variant: "I thought the form was the documentation"
- Specific variant: "The vendor said we were covered under their compliance"
- Specific variant: "We have a policy — isn't that what they wanted?"

The ICP has made reasonable decisions under asymmetric information. The gap is not negligence — it is a documentation category they did not know existed.

**EN translation:** "The documentation gap is not your fault. It is a category no one told you existed."

**FR translation:** « Le déficit de documentation n'est pas de votre faute. C'est une catégorie que personne ne vous a signalée. »

---

### Pattern Category 2: The Attestation Language

**"They sent us a form and I don't know what they're actually asking for"**

- Found across: Healthcare (Phase 04), Legal (Phase 01), Finance (Phase 02)
- Specific variant: "The form has a section on AI governance and I'm not sure what to put there"
- Specific variant: "I filled it in but I'm not confident it's the right answer"
- Specific variant: "The carrier is asking us to confirm things we don't have records of"

The carrier attestation is the highest-intent trigger across all verticals. The ICP knows the form is consequential. They do not know whether what they submitted is adequate.

**EN translation:** "You know the form matters. You don't know if what you put in it is enough."

**FR translation:** « Vous savez que le formulaire compte. Vous ne savez pas si ce que vous avez mis est suffisant. »

---

### Pattern Category 3: The Vendor Language

**"The vendor said we were compliant — but I'm not sure what that means for us"**

- Found across: Legal (Phase 01), Finance (Phase 02), Healthcare (Phase 04)
- Specific variant: "We signed their DPA — I assume that means it's sorted"
- Specific variant: "The vendor is a Fortune 500 company, they must be compliant"
- Specific variant: "They said our data stays in Switzerland but I saw something in the terms"

The vendor trust signal is deeply embedded. The ICP trusts institutional vendors but has never read the contract. They suspect something is wrong but do not know how to find out.

**EN translation:** "The vendor's compliance is not your compliance. Two different things."

**FR translation:** « La conformité du fournisseur n'est pas votre conformité. Deux choses différentes. »

---

### Pattern Category 4: The Authority Language

**"Is this actually required, or is it just best practice?"**

- Found across: All four verticals
- Specific variant: "Is there actually a law that requires this, or just guidance?"
- Specific variant: "If there's no enforcement, why do I need to do this?"
- Specific variant: "My colleague said they don't do this and they're fine"

The ICP calibrates to enforcement probability. The framing challenge: make the consequence tangible rather than regulatory. The license, the attestation, the personal name — not the fine.

**EN translation:** "The question isn't whether it's required. The question is what happens when someone asks you to prove it."

**FR translation:** « La question n'est pas de savoir si c'est requis. La question est ce qui se passe quand quelqu'un vous demande de le prouver. »

---

### Pattern Category 5: The Time Language

**"I don't have time to figure this out properly"**

- Found across: All four verticals — solo practitioners and small practices
- Specific variant: "I know I should deal with this but I'm running the practice"
- Specific variant: "Between clients and admin, there's no time left"
- Specific variant: "I need someone who will just do it, not teach me"

The solo practitioner has no internal capacity. They need an operator, not an educator. The service design implication: produce the evidence, do not explain the theory.

**EN translation:** "You don't need a course. You need someone who produces the evidence for you."

**FR translation:** « Vous n'avez pas besoin d'une formation. Vous avez besoin de quelqu'un qui produit les preuves pour vous. »

---

### Pattern Category 6: The Consequence Language

**"What does this actually mean for my license?"**

- Found across: Legal (Phase 01), Healthcare (Phase 04), Finance (Phase 02)
- Specific variant: "If something goes wrong, what happens to me personally?"
- Specific variant: "Is this just a fine or is my license at risk?"
- Specific variant: "My carrier paid the claim — does that mean I'm protected?"

Personal liability is the actual trigger. The ICP distinguishes between organizational exposure (fine, sanction) and personal exposure (license, name, career). They care about the second.

**EN translation:** "Your license is not the same as your organization's fine. Personal exposure is different."

**FR translation:** « Votre licence n'est pas la même chose que l'amende de votre organisation. L'exposition personnelle est différente. »

---

### Pattern Category 7: The Future Language

**"This is going to get more complicated, isn't it?"**

- Found across: All four verticals — sophisticated principals only
- Specific variant: "I feel like this is just starting"
- Specific variant: "There's more coming — I should get ahead of it"
- Specific variant: "The people I know who are ignoring this — I don't think they're going to be fine"

The sophisticated ICP understands the regulatory trajectory. They want to get ahead of it, not react to it. They will pay for ongoing advisory, not just crisis response.

**EN translation:** "The window to get ahead of this is still open. It's closing."

**FR translation:** « La fenêtre pour devancer cela est encore ouverte. Elle se ferme. »
