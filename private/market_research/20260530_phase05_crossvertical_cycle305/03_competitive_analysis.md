## Purpose

Identify the cross-vertical competitive white space — where the market is not serving the ICP adequately across all four verticals, and where the first-mover position is still open.

## 6 NET-NEW Competitive White Space Findings — C305

### Finding 1: The Evidence Production Category Does Not Exist in the Market

**White space across:** Legal + Finance + Consulting + Healthcare — Geneva SMB

**The gap:** No Geneva-based boutique advisor offers evidence production as a discrete service. The market offers:
- AI governance frameworks (technology vendors, international consultancies)
- Compliance templates (law firms, compliance vendors)
- Training programs (professional associations, online platforms)
- Data protection advisory (data protection officers, law firms)

None of these produce evidence. They produce documents the ICP reads, policies the ICP implements, or training the ICP completes. None of them produce the Human Decision Record — the contemporaneous documentation showing that a named human authorized a specific AI recommendation before implementation.

**Why it is white space:** The service the ICP actually needs — evidence they can produce when required — is not available in the Geneva market. The closest offerings are framework vendors who sell documentation templates and consultancies who sell training programs. Neither produces evidence.

**Implication:** "Evidence production" is a category-defining positioning. The first actor to name it and deliver it occupies the category.

### Finding 2: The Vertical Specificity Trap — Competitors Solve Vertical Problems, Not Cross-Vertical Problem

**White space across:** Legal AI governance tools + Finance AI compliance platforms + Healthcare AI governance solutions

**The gap:** Each vertical has specialized vendors offering vertical-specific solutions. None of them address the structural similarity across verticals — the universal documentation debt structure, the same evidence architecture gap, the same carrier attestation paradox. A legal AI governance platform does not speak to the finance ICP. A healthcare compliance tool does not serve the consulting firm. The market is fragmented by vertical, but the problem is universal.

**Why it is white space:** The ICP who operates across verticals — or who is a principal in a multi-disciplinary practice — needs a cross-vertical view that no current vendor offers. The service model that can serve the same principal across legal, finance, and consulting work is not available.

**Implication:** One evidence architecture, four vertical overlays. The service is not vertical-specific AI governance — it is evidence production with vertical application expertise. This is a different positioning from any current market actor.

---

### Finding 3: The Solo Practitioner Operational Inaccessibility Gap

**White space across:** All four verticals — small practices, solo practitioners, boutique firms

**The gap:** Every competitive offering in the Geneva AI governance market assumes the ICP has internal capacity — a compliance team, an IT department, a data protection officer, a practice manager. The solo practitioner — the lawyer with no support staff, the physician running their own practice, the wealth manager working alone — is excluded from every current offering by design. The offerings require internal execution capability the solo practitioner does not have.

**Why it is white space:** The solo practitioner is the majority of the Geneva professional market. They have the same documentation debt, the same evidence gap, and the same personal liability exposure as the larger firm — but no internal capacity to address it. Every current offering is designed for a client who has already solved the internal capacity problem.

**Implication:** Turnkey evidence production — not advisory, not training, not templates — is the only service model that serves the solo practitioner. Someone produces the evidence for them. That is the product.

### Finding 4: The Bilingual EN-FR Structural Absence

**White space across:** Geneva market — all verticals, all service levels

**The gap:** No Geneva-based professional services firm offers bilingual EN-FR AI governance evidence production. The professional services market is split: French-speaking advisors who serve French-speaking clients, English-speaking advisors who serve English-speaking clients. The bilingual ICP — who operates in both languages, serves clients in both languages, receives carrier attestations in both languages — has no bilingual option for the specific service they need.

**Why it is white space:** Geneva is a bilingual canton. The regulatory documents arrive in both languages. The client communications happen in both languages. The professional obligation documentation must function in both languages. A bilingual evidence production service — with deliverables in both languages, regulatory monitoring in both languages, client communications in both languages — serves a market that no current competitor is serving.

**Implication:** Bilingual EN-FR is not a nice-to-have — it is a market requirement for the Geneva SMB professional. The service must be deliverable in both languages as standard, not as an option.

### Finding 5: The Post-Adoption Evidence Capture Gap

**White space across:** All four verticals — existing AI tool users

**The gap:** Every current AI governance offering assumes the ICP is either:
- About to adopt AI (pre-adoption guidance)
- Auditing their existing AI governance (retrospective assessment)

No current offering addresses the specific situation of the ICP who has already adopted AI, has accumulated 12–36 months of documentation debt, and needs to freeze the debt and build forward evidence capture without reconstructing the past. The market treats the evidence gap as a retrospective problem. The ICP's actual problem is forward-looking: they need evidence architecture from today forward, not archaeology of the past 24 months.

**Why it is white space:** The post-adoption debt freeze service — stop the compounding, build forward capture — is not available. The ICP who has already adopted AI is the majority of the market. Every current offering is designed for a different client profile.

**Implication:** The debt freeze framing is the correct entry message. "Stop the accumulation. Build what you need going forward. We don't reconstruction the past — we build what protects you from today."

### Finding 6: The Regulatory Pressure Convergence Service Gap

**White space across:** All four verticals — ICPs facing multiple simultaneous regulatory triggers

**The gap:** When the ICP receives their carrier attestation, they need:
- Carrier attestation preparation (specific, bounded, high-intent)
- nFADP Art.63 exposure assessment (distinct, related)
- Vendor contract review (separate, urgent)
- Staff AI use authorization protocol (if staff exist)

These are four separate services from four separate vendors. The ICP in the convergence moment needs all four simultaneously — and has no single advisor who can deliver all four in an integrated way. The market fragmentation means the ICP must coordinate multiple service providers, none of whom have the full picture.

**Why it is white space:** The ICP who is facing simultaneous regulatory pressure — carrier attestation + nFADP + vendor contract review + staff protocol — needs an integrated advisory relationship, not four separate vendor engagements. The integrated AI Governance Advisory retainer — covering all four workstreams under one relationship — is not available in the Geneva market.

**Implication:** The ongoing retainer is the durable revenue model. The entry point is the discrete bounded service (carrier attestation preparation, vendor contract review). The conversion is to the retainer when the ICP realizes the regulatory pressure is not a single event — it is a sustained environment.

## First-Mover Position Assessment

**Category:** Evidence production as a discrete professional service
**Status:** Unoccupied
**Window:** Open — closing as regulatory pressure increases and market awareness grows
**Defensibility:** Named standard (Human Decision Record) + methodology + bilingual delivery + SMB accessibility

The first actor to define the evidence production category in ICP language, deliver it as a turnkey service, and maintain it as ongoing advisory occupies the position that international consultancies cannot serve (too expensive, not SMB-accessible), technology vendors cannot serve (no professional liability lens), and law firms cannot serve (not AI-native, not evidence production oriented).
