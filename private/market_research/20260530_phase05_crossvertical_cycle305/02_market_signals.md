## 6 NET-NEW Cross-Vertical Market Signals — C305

### Signal 1: The AI Tool Count Inflation Without Evidence Architecture

**Observed across:** Legal (Phase 01) + Finance (Phase 02) + Consulting (Phase 03) + Healthcare (Phase 04)

**The signal:** Geneva SMB professional practices have adopted 3–8 AI tools in the past 18 months without any corresponding documentation infrastructure. The adoption was driven by efficiency need, competitive pressure, and staff retention. The evidence production was never addressed because no one framed it as required. The gap is now structural — the tools are in use, the documentation does not exist, and the principal cannot produce evidence of human authorization for any AI-assisted decision made in the past 12–24 months.

**Why it is a market signal:** The principal has already committed to AI adoption. They cannot reverse it without significant efficiency loss. They are now in a situation where more AI use creates more liability. The only resolution is evidence architecture that captures going forward without requiring reconstruction of the past.

**Implication:** "Stop adopting AI tools" is not a viable message. "Adopt with evidence" is the only viable message.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "You've added three AI tools since January. Each one creates documentation debt. None of them come with evidence production built in. That's the gap."

### Signal 2: The Sub-Principal Staff AI Use Without Authorization Protocol

**Observed across:** Healthcare (Phase 04) + Consulting (Phase 03) + Legal (Phase 01) + Finance (Phase 02)

**The signal:** The principal is not the only AI user in the practice. Staff — associates, assistants, junior professionals — are using AI tools daily without the principal's knowledge of what tools, how, or with what client data. The principal's attestation confirms "we have AI governance" — which includes staff use. The principal has no evidence of what staff are doing, what authorization was given, or what client data touched AI systems.

**Why it is a market signal:** The principal signed an attestation that covers staff AI use. The attestation is accurate regarding policy existence. The attestation is false regarding evidence of actual supervision. The gap is not policy — it is supervision documentation. This is the accountability inversion: the principal is liable for what they cannot see.

**Implication:** The authorization protocol for staff AI use is a discrete deliverable. The principal needs a named system for documenting which staff use which tools with which client data categories — not a general policy.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "Your attestation covers your staff's AI use. Do you know what tools your team is using and what client data they touch? You attested to it. That's your exposure."

### Signal 3: The Regulatory Timeline Convergence as Acute Trigger

**Observed across:** All four verticals — converging in Q3–Q4 2026

**The signal:** Multiple regulatory pressure points are arriving simultaneously — carrier attestations (legal active, healthcare active, finance and consulting near-term), Swissdamed July 1, 2026 (medical devices scope), EU AI Act awareness spike (August 2026), Swiss Federal Council AI consultation results (end 2026), nFADP ongoing obligations. The ICP who has been watching is now facing multiple simultaneous triggers for the first time.

**Why it is a market signal:** The ICP who was planning to "deal with AI governance eventually" is now in a situation where multiple parties are asking simultaneously. The planning window has closed. The documentation debt is now undeniable because multiple external parties are requesting evidence in the same quarter.

**Implication:** Q3 2026 is the highest-intent market activation window in the cycle. Every ICP is being asked questions they cannot answer. The timing is now — not later.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "You've been waiting for the right moment to address this. Three things just arrived at once. The moment is now."

### Signal 4: The Vendor Contract Post-Signature Discovery Pattern

**Observed across:** Legal (Phase 01) + Finance (Phase 02) + Healthcare (Phase 04) + Consulting (Phase 03)

**The signal:** The ICP signed an AI vendor DPA 6–24 months ago. They recently learned — from a conversation, an article, a colleague — that the contract may contain terms that affect their professional secrecy obligations. They do not know what the contract says. They do not know what their obligations are. They cannot determine whether the vendor's change clauses have modified the terms since signature without their knowledge.

**Why it is a market signal:** This is the highest-conversion entry conversation. The ICP has a specific document (the contract), a specific fear (something might be wrong), and a specific consequence (Article 321 CC or equivalent professional obligation). They want someone to read the contract and tell them what it means for them specifically. This is bounded, high-intent, and creates trust for subsequent services.

**Implication:** AI Vendor Contract Review is the universal entry service. Every vertical has the same pattern. The deliverable is a Professional Secrecy Risk Memo — specific to their situation, in their language, actionable.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "You signed an AI vendor contract. You haven't read it. Something in it might create exposure under Article 321 CC or your professional code. That's worth knowing."

### Signal 5: The Competitor/Peer Benchmark Discovery Moment

**Observed across:** Finance (Phase 02 institutional DDQ) + Legal (Phase 01 SBA) + Consulting (Phase 03 client DDQ) + Healthcare (Phase 04 FMH guidance)

**The signal:** The ICP received a questionnaire, attestation, or RFP from a client, carrier, or professional body. The questionnaire asked for specific evidence of AI governance — not just a policy description. The ICP discovered they could not produce the evidence. Separately, they learned that a peer — a similar-size practice — was awarded a mandate or retained a client specifically because they had the documentation the ICP lacked.

**Why it is a market signal:** The ICP now has concrete evidence that the documentation gap has a revenue consequence, not just a regulatory consequence. The motivation shifts from "avoid penalty" to "win business." The peer benchmark is a powerful conversion trigger — it is specific, local, and financially meaningful.

**Implication:** The institutional DDQ as competitive gate (Finance, Phase 02) is the template for all verticals. The client or carrier questionnaire that measures evidence, not policy, is the forcing function. When the ICP sees a peer win because of documentation, the investment in evidence architecture becomes competitive necessity, not just compliance cost.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "A firm like yours lost a mandate last month because they couldn't produce evidence of AI authorization. The client awarded it to a firm that could. Documentation is a revenue variable now."

### Signal 6: The Personal Liability Awareness Spillover

**Observed across:** Legal (Phase 01) + Healthcare (Phase 04) + Finance (Phase 02) + Consulting (Phase 03)

**The signal:** A specific incident — a case, a claim, a regulatory action, a peer situation — made the ICP realize that their personal liability is distinct from their organization's liability. The carrier paid a claim. The fine was issued to the organization. But the individual's license, reputation, and career are at risk in a different way. The personal liability dimension is now salient in a way it was not 12 months ago.

**Why it is a market signal:** The personal liability framing converts abstract regulatory anxiety into concrete personal stakes. The ICP who understands that their name, not just their firm's name, is what is at risk will invest in evidence production differently than the ICP who thinks of this as an organizational compliance issue.

**Implication:** Lead with personal exposure before regulatory obligation. The license is more motivating than the fine. The name is more motivating than the organization.

**Cross-vertical activation language:**
> "Your carrier paid the claim. That doesn't protect your license. Your carrier paid for the organization's exposure — not yours. Article 63 is personal."
