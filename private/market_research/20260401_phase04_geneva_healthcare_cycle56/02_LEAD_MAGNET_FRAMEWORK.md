## Problem Statement (4 layers)

### Layer 1: Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — The Non-Negotiable Deadline
Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Database) registration is required for AI tools used in patient care that qualify as medical devices. Deadline: July 1, 2026. No grace period. If you're using an unregistered AI tool in patient care after July 1, you're operating outside the law.

### Layer 2: FOM Human Review Standard for AI-Assisted Treatment
AI in treatment decisions must be: (a) reviewed by a physician before clinical application, (b) disclosed to the patient, (c) covered by professional liability insurance. AI as second opinion, not autonomous decision-maker.

### Layer 3: Three-Layer Data Governance (FADP + HRA + AI Ordinance)
Patient data processed through AI tools: FADP human review + Human Research Act obligations + Federal AI Ordinance risk classification. Three concurrent frameworks. One breach triggers all three.

### Layer 4: Demand-Awareness Gap (Highest in Portfolio)
69% of patients want AI in their care; 52.9% of physicians actively considering it. Highest gap in the portfolio. This creates both pressure and opportunity: patients are ready, physicians need governance frameworks to move forward confidently.
