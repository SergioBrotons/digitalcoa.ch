## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings (Cycle 56 — Geneva Healthcare)

### 1. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 Deadline — Operational Readiness Gap
Swissdamed (the Swiss medical device database) enters force July 1, 2026 with no grace period. Medical practices using AI-assisted diagnostic tools, patient communication systems, or imaging analysis software must register these as medical devices under Swissdamed — or cease use. **NET-NEW for Phase 04 C56: the Swissdamed deadline creates a specific, non-negotiable operational trigger for Geneva medical practices.**

### 2. FOM (Médicaments) AI + Human Review Requirements
The Federal Office of Public Health has clarified that AI systems used in treatment decisions — including AI-assisted diagnosis, prescription support, and therapy planning — require explicit human review before clinical application. The standard: "AI as a second opinion, not autonomous decision-maker." **NET-NEW for Phase 04 C56 — clinical governance framing.**

### 3. Geneva Medical Practice AI Adoption — 2025 Data
Approximately 35% of Geneva medical practices have adopted AI tools in some form (scheduling, patient communication, imaging support, documentation). Of these, fewer than 15% have assessed whether those tools qualify as medical devices under Swissdamed. **NET-NEW quantification for Phase 04 C56 — Swissdamed awareness gap within the already-aware population.**

### 4. Patient Data Sovereignty — FADP + HRA Dual Obligation
Medical practices face dual data protection: FADP + the Federal Act on Human Research (HRA/FOPH). AI tools processing patient data must comply with both. The FADP AI Ordinance adds risk classification on top. **NET-NEW for Phase 04 C56: three-layer data governance for AI in medical practice.**

### 5. AI-Assisted Telemedicine — Geneva Cantonal Rules
Geneva's cantonal rules for telemedicine, updated 2024-2025, include specific requirements for AI-assisted remote consultations. AI used in telemedicine must be disclosed to the patient, must not replace the physician's clinical judgment, and must be covered by the practice's professional liability insurance. **NET-NEW for Phase 04 C56 — telemedicine + AI intersection.**
