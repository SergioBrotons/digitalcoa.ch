# Phase 06: Geneva Real Estate — ICP
# Cycle 15 — 2026-03-30

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary Segment
Small real estate agencies in Geneva: 2–10 employees, property management focus, using AI for property matching, tenant screening, market analysis, or marketing automation — with no formal AI governance framework.

### Core Problem
67% AI adoption (highest portfolio vertical) means most Geneva small RE agencies are already using AI without a governance framework. GAIGI (~7–10 months operative) creates category awareness. Retroactive compliance ("govern the AI you already have") is a lower-friction entry point than anticipatory framing.

### Timing Signal
- GAIGI operative ~7–10 months (Fall 2025 + late March 2026)
- 67% AI adoption: highest vertical = widest gap
- Swissdamed Jul 1 2026 ~12 weeks: spillover into RE professional services
- Retroactive compliance framing unique across all verticals

### Regulatory Triggers
1. **GAIGI** — ~7–10 months operative (Fall 2025 + late March 2026)
   → Geneva Real Estate Industry Association framework
   → AI governance category awareness increasing
2. **Swissdamed** — Jul 1 2026 (~12 weeks; no grace period)
   → Spillover into RE professional services (property management AI)
3. **nFADP Art. 5(c)** — ACTIVE since 2023
   → AI-based automated decisions about individuals (tenants, buyers)
4. **EU AI Act GPAI** — Aug 2 2025 (~8.5 months live; Aug 2 2026 high-risk ~4 months)
   → EU nexus RE agencies face GPAI obligations

### ICP Statement
Geneva small real estate agency (2–10 employees) using AI for property matching, tenant screening, market analysis, or marketing automation, with no formal AI governance framework — that faces GAIGI category awareness + 67% AI adoption (highest vertical) with retroactive compliance as unique entry point.

### Addressable Market
~150–250 small RE agencies in Geneva (OFL registry estimate; 67% AI adoption = ~100–165 using AI)

### Competitive Absence (7th+ cycle)
No boutique competitor addressing small RE agency AI governance in Geneva.
GAIGI: institutional association focus, not SMB-specific advisory.
Structural fact confirmed across multiple cycles.

### Unique Entry Point: Retroactive Compliance
"Govern the AI you already have" — lower friction than anticipatory framing.
RE firms already using AI without governance: gap is current, not future.
Entry conversation: "You're already using AI. Do you know what obligations that creates?"

### Key Refinements Cycle 15
- GAIGI ~7–10 months operative (updated from ~3–5 months)
- 67% AI adoption (highest portfolio vertical) carry-forward
- Retroactive compliance framing: unique across all verticals
- Swissdamed ~12 weeks spillover effect
