### 3. FDPIC Explicitly Confirms Existing Data Protection Law Already Applies to AI Systems (NET-NEW C61)
**Source:** FDPIC.admin.ch (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner, via cms.law/edoeb.admin.ch citation)

The FDPIC has explicitly confirmed that the revised **Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP)** — in force since September 1, 2023 — is **directly applicable to AI-supported data processing**. This is not a new law; it is an existing law being applied to AI. For Geneva law firms, this means:
- FADP Art. 21 transparency obligations apply to AI systems processing personal data
- DPIAs required for high-risk AI processing
- Automated individual decision transparency obligations apply

**Legal sector implications (NET-NEW C61):** This FDPIC confirmation closes the "which law applies to our AI?" question. Geneva law firms advising clients on AI tools must lead with FADP applicability — not wait for a Swiss AI Act. The enforcement signal from FDPIC (who has authority to investigate and sanction) is now explicit.
