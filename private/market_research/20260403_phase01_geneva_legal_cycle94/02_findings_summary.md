## F1: Switzerland Russia Sanctions — Full EU 19th Package Implementation (Feb 25/26, 2026)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin, Baker McKenzie, Swiss admin.ch  
**Signal:** HIGH — policy shift, sustained compliance demand

On February 25, 2026, the Swiss Federal Council adopted extensive amendments to the Ordinance on Measures in Connection with the Situation in Ukraine (UKRO), completing implementation of the EU's 19th sanctions package. Measures entered force February 26, 2026, with staggered effective dates through April 25, 2026.

Key provisions:
- Full ban on Russian liquefied natural gas (LNG) — financial services, brokering, technical assistance prohibited from Feb 26; core import ban from April 25, 2026
- Transitional period for existing long-term contracts until January 1, 2027 (if no "significant amendments")
- Crypto-asset transaction prohibitions
- Special Economic Zone (SEZ) restrictions
- Financial sector restrictions intensified
- Professional services expansion — provision of ALL non-prohibited services to Russian government now requires authorization
- Oil price cap lowered from USD 47.60 to USD 44.10 (February 1, 2026)

Legal advisory demand: compliance reviews, contract restructuring, licensing applications, enforcement defense.

## F3: Russian LNG Ban — Full Implementation April 25, 2026
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin  
**Signal:** MEDIUM-HIGH — commodity traders, Geneva energy sector

Full prohibition on purchase, import, transit and transport of LNG of Russian origin takes effect April 25, 2026. Geneva commodity traders (Trafigura, Vitol Group presence) face supply chain restructuring. Existing long-term contracts can continue until January 1, 2027 if not significantly amended. SECO FAQ clarifies that price reductions, quantity reductions, and intra-group transfers do not constitute "significant amendments" — allowing contract adjustments without triggering the ban.
