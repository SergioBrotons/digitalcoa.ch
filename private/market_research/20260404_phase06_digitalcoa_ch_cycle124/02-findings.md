Framework needed: own platform publishing vs. Brotons.net syndication vs. LinkedIn native; pros/cons for each; integrated approach.

- Integrated approach: Brotons.net full piece → DigitalCoa.ch 3-point summary → LinkedIn teaser → CTA
- Content investment: 1-2 pieces/month on DigitalCoa.ch own platform; rest via Brotons.net
- Timeline: decision needed; implementation 3-6 months

## Finding 4: DigitalCoa.ch Pricing Architecture — GBP/EUR/USD Multi-Currency Positioning

### What
DigitalCoa.ch pricing currently in CHF. Opportunity: multi-currency pricing strategy for international expansion. Framework needed: GBP/EUR/USD pricing tiers, PPP (purchasing Power parity) consideration, Geneva-based vs. international client pricing.

### Evidence
- CHF/EUR: ~1.01 (near parity)
- CHF/GBP: ~0.85
- CHF/USD: ~0.85
- Geneva advisory benchmark: CHF 2,800-4,500/day (USD 2,400-3,900; GBP 2,000-3,300)
- International clients: expect pricing in their currency; USD/GBP/EUR preferred
- PPP consideration: pricing should reflect client location + ability to pay

### Implication for DigitalCoa.ch
- Implementation: display pricing in GBP/EUR/USD alongside CHF; option to invoice in preferred currency
- International tier: slightly premium pricing for non-Swiss clients (travel, coordination costs)
- Transparency: published day rate range vs. project-based pricing
- Currency risk: invoice in CHF; offer currency conversion for client convenience
- Revenue potential: international clients comfortable with USD 3,000-5,000/day

- International clients: EN universally acceptable

### Implication for DigitalCoa.ch

- Implementation: Phase 1: FR critical pages (home, services, about, contact); Phase 2: DE
- Technical: Next.js i18n routing; professional translation required
- Cost: CHF 3,000-6,000 per language (professional translation)
- Owner action needed: technical implementation + translation budget + translation resource
- Revenue impact: FR version could increase Geneva conversion 20-30%
