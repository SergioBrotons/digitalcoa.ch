## Content Outline

### Section 1: Why Geneva Executives Need a Decision Framework
- Decision quality determines outcome quality
- Geneva specific context: regulatory complexity, international exposure, family dynamics
- The cost of bad decisions: CHF millions, reputation, relationships
- What separates executives who consistently make good decisions

### Section 2: The 7 Geneva Executive Decision Questions
- Question 1: What is the actual decision we need to make? (clarity of scope)
- Question 2: What would the best outcome look like? (vision alignment)
- Question 3: What are we most afraid of losing? (risk identification)
- Question 4: Who will be most affected and how? (stakeholder mapping)
- Question 5: What does the next 24 months look like with vs. without this decision? (scenario planning)
- Question 6: What would we need to reverse this if it goes wrong? (exit strategy)
- Question 7: What would we advise our best friend in this situation? (detachment test)

### Section 3: Applying the Framework — Geneva Case Studies (Anonymized)
- Case 1: Family business succession decision
- Case 2: Market entry vs. partnership decision
- Case 3: Investment vs. liquidity decision
- Framework application: how each question surfaced key considerations

### Section 4: The Geneva Executive Decision Worksheet
- 7 questions, each with guided prompts
- Space for notes and structured thinking
- Decision matrix: probability × impact × reversibility
- Timeline: when to decide vs. when to gather more information

### Section 5: When to Engage External Advisory
- Internal capability vs. external perspective
- When the decision is irreversible
- When stakeholder complexity is high
- When time pressure prevents full internal analysis
- How to select the right advisory partner
