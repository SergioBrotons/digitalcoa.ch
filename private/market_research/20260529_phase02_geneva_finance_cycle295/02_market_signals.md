# 02_market_signals.md — Phase 02 C295
# Geneva Finance ICP: Boutique Wealth Management (50–200 employees)

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

---

## Carrier Channel Signals

### Third-Generation Carrier Attestation Wave Accelerating

**Signal:** PI and D&O carriers circulating third-generation AI attestation instruments requiring evidence of specific human authorization moments for AI-assisted decisions — not policy acknowledgment, not tool inventory. Boutique MDs completing renewals discovering they signed prior attestations committing to undefined adequacy standard. Carrier attestation architecture serving underwriting risk evaluation, not professional accountability documentation.

**What boutiques are reporting:**
- Carrier renewal questionnaires now explicitly probe AI decision documentation — "describe three AI-assisted decisions made in the past 12 months and the human review that preceded each"
- No Geneva market service offers pre-signing carrier attestation review at proportionate boutique scale
- MD signature on carrier instruments creates personal liability exposure without proportionate review pathway

### Carrier AI Questionnaire Iteration Pattern Tracking

**Signal:** Carriers iteratively refining AI questionnaires across renewal cycles — each iteration introducing more specific evidence requirements. Pattern suggests carriers developing proprietary AI governance evidence standards through iteration, not adopting external frameworks. Boutique documentation built to current carrier standard may be inadequate against next iteration.

**Implication:** Documentation investment has a half-life measured in renewal cycles.

### PI Coverage Gap Discovery

**Signal:** Boutique MDs discovering their PI policies may not explicitly cover AI-assisted professional decisions. Coverage question treated as yes/no, answered as one with the policy, but gap only visible post-incident. No carrier proactively disclosed AI coverage position in writing.

## Institutional Client Channel Signals

### Institutional DDQ as Primary Wake-Up Call

**Signal:** Institutional clients (family offices, institutional investors, pension funds) sending increasingly sophisticated AI governance questionnaires — some exceeding 40 pages. Boutique receiving questionnaire as first concrete signal that AI governance is an accountability issue, not just operational efficiency.

**Behavioral pattern:** Institutional questionnaire arrives → boutique recognizes exposure for first time → boutique attempts response without response architecture → response produces unintended commercial representations → boutique's response becomes de facto operative commitment.

### Simultaneous Multi-Boutique Comparison Effect

**Signal:** Institutional clients running simultaneous AI governance comparisons across manager roster without notifying boutiques. Each boutique responds independently, unaware comparison is occurring. Aggregate of independent responses creates de facto market standard based on documentation sophistication, not governance architecture quality.

**Market distortion:** Boutique with weak actual governance but strong documentation may outperform boutique with strong governance and weak documentation in institutional comparison. Market standard rewarding documentation polish over actual accountability infrastructure.

### Institutional Client Own Governance Immaturity

**Signal:** Sophisticated institutional clients sending detailed AI governance questionnaires often have incomplete own AI governance — questionnaire reflects where institutional governance is heading (2026–2027), not where it currently is. Boutique measured against tightening future standard while institutional client operates under current standard.

**Timing implication:** Window for boutique to position proactively won't remain open indefinitely. Institutional clients building more rigorous governance will expect corresponding governance from managers.

## Regulatory Channel Signals

### FINMA 08/2024 Operationalization Gap

**Signal:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 published with seven-dimension framework (governance, inventory, data quality, testing, documentation, explainability, independent review). Boutique MDs aware of document, unsure what proportionate implementation looks like at 50–200 employee scale. No FINMA-calibrated small-institution methodology. Guidance operates as aspirational checklist, not operational playbook.

### SRO Guidance Vacuum as Structural Condition

**Signal:** Asset management SROs (OARG, AVK, etc.) have not published AI governance guidance proportionate to boutique WM scale. No SRO has published Geneva-specific interpretation of what adequate AI governance means for boutique wealth manager. SRO vacuum creates accountability gap filled by carrier questionnaires and institutional DDQs.

### SIF Q2 2026 Regulatory Gap Analysis Pending

**Signal:** State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF) expected to publish financial market AI regulatory gap analysis in Q2 2026. Results will inform Bern's regulatory approach. Boutique MDs watching for specific guidance on what Swiss law requires vs. what is market practice.

### CoE AI Convention Implementation Trail

**Signal:** Switzerland signed CoE AI Convention March 27, 2025. Consultation draft for implementation expected end of 2026. Convention creates deployer obligations before implementation legislation — existing client mandates may contain AI governance commitments accepted through contract acceptance. Boutique unaware of this present-tense exposure.

## Boutique Operational Failure Signals

### Documentation Accumulation Without Coherence

**Signal:** Boutiques accumulating AI governance documentation (tool inventories, policies, attestations, client disclosures) without applying coherence requirement to system as whole. Individual documents defensible, system picture incoherent under examination. Carrier examination and institutional client review assess coherence across documents — boutique documentation quality program builds individual documents, not system coherence.

### AI Model Version State Untracking

**Signal:** AI model updates silently propagating through active client mandate book — no version tracking architecture, no reassessment trigger protocol. Active client recommendations may be operating under model conditions that no longer exist. Client may reference conclusions produced under model states boutique cannot reproduce or verify.

### Budget Authorization Paradox

**Signal:** AI governance investment quantifiable, ROI unquantifiable. MD cannot demonstrate revenue from risk reduction. Partners defer budget approval. Regulatory exposure grows while authorization deferred. Structural market barrier not firm-specific management failure — no Geneva service priced for boutique WM proportionate scale solves this with clear ROI narrative.

### Personal MD Accountability Without Institutional Infrastructure

**Signal:** Boutique MD operating as de facto Chief AI Officer with no proportionate governance infrastructure — no AI committee, no dedicated compliance resource, no defined review protocol, no evidence standard definition. MD personally accountable for decisions that require institutional capacity to discharge properly.

### Cross-Generational Accountability Gap

**Signal:** Senior MDs with decades of domain expertise signing carrier attestations about AI systems configured and operated by younger PMs they cannot independently evaluate. Accountability and competence inverted across generations — most AI-competent personnel least professionally accountable, most accountable personnel (MD) least able to evaluate AI-assisted decisions.

## nFADP Enforcement Channel Signals

### FDPIC Enforcement Posture Live

**Signal:** FDPIC has confirmed existing nFADP applies directly to AI processing — no regulatory gap requiring new legislation. Enforcement posture shifting from guidance-first to enforcement-live. nFADP Art.63 personal criminal liability for responsible persons operating AI systems with inadequate governance.

### Art.8 Profiling Notification Unawareness

**Signal:** AI-assisted portfolio construction constitutes profiling under nFADP Art.8. Boutique wealth managers not issuing profiling notifications as category. Obligation present-tense regardless of firm awareness. AI inference creating risk tolerance, spending pattern, financial behavior conclusions about specific clients activates profiling notification right.

### Data Provenance and Lawful Basis Gap

**Signal:** AI systems inferring client characteristics (risk tolerance, spending patterns, life stage) from transaction data without explicit consent — inferred characteristics personal data under nFADP, lawful basis for inference undocumented upstream. Upstream data lawfulness problem before disclosure obligation even arises.

### Art.20/21 Explanation Right vs. Proprietary Methodology Conflict

**Signal:** nFADP Art.20/21 grants clients right to explanation of how AI affected specific decisions. Genuine algorithmic explanation may require disclosing proprietary methodology. Vague explanation doesn't satisfy legal standard. Boutique built competitive advantage on methodology confidentiality. Structural conflict unnamed in Geneva boutique WM discourse.

## Competitive/Market Failure Signals

### No Boutique-Proportionate Market Solution

**Signal:** Geneva market has no service offering proportionate AI governance support for boutique WM 50–200 employees. Big-four and enterprise consulting firms operate above boutique price/engagement threshold. Regulatory boutiques operate above operational complexity threshold for AI governance. Market structurally underserved at exactly the scale where accountability pressure is most acute.

### Geneva Accountability Formation Velocity Gap

**Signal:** Accountability standards forming through informal Geneva channels (carrier iteration, institutional client pressure, peer network) before Bern formal doctrine arrives. Boutique outside informal circuit building documentation against arrived standards while operative standards set through channels they don't participate in. Intelligence gap structurally unavailable through published sources.

### Documentation Window Close Acceleration

**Signal:** Carrier third-generation attestation requirements crystallizing faster than boutique documentation investment. Window closing on burden-of-proof positioning — firms deferring AI governance documentation accumulating positioning exposure that cannot be discharged retroactively.
