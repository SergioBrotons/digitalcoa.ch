# Findings Summary: Geneva Finance — C295 Phase 02

## 6 NET-NEW Findings: Distinct from C214–C294

---

### Finding 01 — Third-Generation Carrier Attestation Wave as Structural Forcing Function

**The finding:** Carrier AI attestation has evolved through three generations. First-generation: policy acknowledgment. Second-generation: tool inventory. Third-generation: specific documented evidence of human authorization moments for AI-assisted decisions. Boutique MDs completing renewals are discovering that prior attestation commitments — signed without understanding the evidence standard — created personal exposure without proportionate documentation architecture. No Geneva market service offers pre-signing carrier attestation review at proportionate boutique scale.

**Mechanism:** Third-generation instruments require documented evidence of specific human authorization preceding each AI-assisted decision. The documentation must exist at the time of the decision, not reconstructed retrospectively. Boutique wealth management firms operating without this architecture face a structural impossibility: the evidence standard is unobtainable with current informal authorization processes.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named carrier attestation as accountability trigger without isolating the third-generation evidence architecture as discrete forcing function, and without identifying the pre-signing review absence as specific market gap.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The carrier isn't asking if you have a policy anymore. They're asking you to show them three decisions where AI was involved and the evidence that a human authorized each one before it happened."

### Finding 02 — Carrier AI Questionnaire Iteration Creating Proprietary Evidence Standards

**The finding:** Carriers are iteratively refining AI questionnaires across renewal cycles, each iteration introducing more specific evidence requirements. This is not adoption of external AI governance frameworks — it is carriers developing proprietary evidence standards through iteration. Documentation built to current carrier standard may be inadequate against next iteration before the renewal cycle completes.

**Mechanism:** Each renewal cycle introduces refined questions based on prior response patterns, claims experience, and market developments. The carrier is building an internal AI governance evidence database across all insured firms. The documentation investment has a half-life measured in renewal cycles, not years.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named carrier attestation requirements without isolating the iterative proprietary standard formation as distinct from external framework adoption, and without identifying the documentation investment half-life implication.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The carrier's AI questionnaire this year will be different from last year. Whatever you build to answer this year's questions probably won't fully satisfy next year's."

---

### Finding 03 — PI Coverage Gap Discovery as Pre-Incident Blind Spot

**The finding:** Boutique MDs are discovering that their PI policies may not explicitly cover AI-assisted professional decisions. The coverage question has been treated as yes/no ("we have PI coverage"), answered affirmatively, and the gap only visible post-incident. No carrier has proactively disclosed their AI coverage position in writing to boutique WM clients.

**Mechanism:** PI policies were underwritten before AI-assisted portfolio management was widespread. The policy terms do not explicitly address AI-assisted decisions. When a claim involves AI-assisted decision-making, the coverage determination will be made at the moment of claim — after the MD has signed attestations committing to adequate AI governance without coverage clarity.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named personal liability exposure and carrier attestation without isolating the PI coverage gap as a pre-incident blind spot distinct from post-incident coverage disputes.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your PI policy was written before AI-assisted portfolio management existed. Has anyone told you in writing whether it covers AI-assisted decisions? If not, you don't have that answer."

### Finding 04 — Simultaneous Multi-Boutique Comparison Creating Documentation Market Distortion

**The finding:** Institutional clients are running simultaneous AI governance comparisons across their manager roster without notifying the boutiques. Each boutique responds independently, unaware that their response is being compared against peers. The aggregate of independent responses is creating a de facto market standard based on documentation sophistication, not actual governance quality. Boutique with weak actual governance and strong documentation outperforms boutique with strong governance and weak documentation.

**Mechanism:** Institutional client sends questionnaire to 10–15 managers simultaneously. Responses evaluated as relative ranking, not absolute pass/fail. The comparison produces a market standard that rewards the appearance of governance over governance architecture itself. Firms optimizing for institutional comparison are building documentation to impress peers, not accountability infrastructure for personal protection.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named institutional questionnaire as accountability trigger without isolating the simultaneous multi-boutique comparison mechanism creating market-level documentation distortion distinct from individual firm response dynamics.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your institutional client's questionnaire is a competitive intelligence instrument. They're comparing your AI governance documentation against every other manager on your roster simultaneously — and you don't know who else is on it."

### Finding 05 — FINMA 08/2024 Operationalization Gap as Proportionate Implementation Void

**The finding:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 establishes a seven-dimension framework (governance, inventory, data quality, testing, documentation, explainability, independent review) that applies to all Swiss financial institutions supervised by FINMA, including boutiques. No FINMA-calibrated small-institution proportionality methodology has been published. The guidance operates as aspirational checklist, not operational playbook. Boutique MDs are responsible for determining what proportionate implementation means for their specific setup — without a reference point for what that looks like.

**Mechanism:** The seven dimensions require documented evidence across all AI applications, testing protocols, independent review processes, and explainability documentation. For a 50–200 employee boutique WM, the same framework that applies to large banks must be implemented without the compliance infrastructure, legal department, or IT resources. Proportionality is the MD's responsibility to define — with no external standard for what proportionate means.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named FINMA 08/2024 as regulatory pressure without isolating the specific operationalization gap for boutiques and the absence of FINMA-calibrated small-institution methodology as discrete void.

**Ready-to-use language:** "FINMA 08/2024 tells you what adequate AI governance looks like in seven dimensions. It doesn't tell you what adequate means for a 60-person Geneva wealth manager. That's your problem."

---

### Finding 06 — SRO Guidance Vacuum as Structural Permanent Condition

**The finding:** SRO guidance on AI governance for boutique wealth management is absent. This is not a delay — it is a structural condition. SROs (OARG, AVK, asset management industry bodies) have published general AI guidance statements, but Geneva-specific boutique WM guidance does not exist and no timeline for it exists. The SRO vacuum is not temporary — it reflects the structural reality that SRO frameworks are designed for larger institutional members, not 50–200 person boutiques.

**Mechanism:** SROs represent their membership's collective interests, publish guidance that reflects member consensus, and move at the speed of member agreement. Boutique wealth managers are not the gravitational center of SRO influence. The guidance vacuum will not be filled by SROs in a timeframe relevant to current MD accountability exposure. Firms waiting for SRO guidance are waiting for a condition that will not materialize.

**Distinctness:** Prior findings named SRO silence as accountability vacuum without isolating the vacuum as structurally permanent (not temporarily absent) and without distinguishing the SRO guidance vacuum from FINMA guidance absence as separate voids requiring separate positioning response.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The SRO is not going to publish boutique-scale AI governance guidance before your next carrier renewal. That's not a prediction — it's a structural fact about how SROs work."
