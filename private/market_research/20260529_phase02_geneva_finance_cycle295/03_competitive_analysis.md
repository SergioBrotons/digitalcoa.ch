# 03_competitive_analysis.md — Phase 02 C295
# Geneva Finance ICP: Boutique Wealth Management (50–200 employees)

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

---

## Existing Market Offerings

### Regulatory Consultancy (Big-Four and Enterprise)

**What exists:** Big-four firms (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) offer AI governance frameworks, FINMA compliance assessments, and nFADP gap analyses. Swiss private banks and larger asset managers are primary clients.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Engagement sizes start at levels incompatible with CHF 5M–50M AUM boutique economics. Methodologies designed for institutional banking, not wealth management boutiques. Outputs not proportionate to 50–200 employee organization. No Geneva-specific calibration. Language and cultural positioning misses boutique WM dynamics.

### nFADP/Swiss Data Protection Consultancy

**What exists:** Several Geneva and Swiss firms offer nFADP compliance programs, data protection officer services, and privacy impact assessments. Some offer AI-specific data protection reviews.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Programs designed for general commercial data processing — not AI-assisted portfolio decision-making in wealth management context. No integration with carrier attestation requirements or FINMA guidance. No portfolio construction AI documentation methodology. Art.63 criminal liability dimension not addressed as distinct from administrative compliance.

### Legal Firms (Geneva and Swiss)

**What exists:** Several Geneva law firms offer regulatory compliance counsel, some with FINMA or investment management specialization. Some offer AI governance legal opinions.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Hourly billing structure incompatible with proportionate engagement. AI governance as legal opinion limited to legal question (what does the law require?) without operational implementation methodology (how does a boutique WM actually document?). Carrier pre-signing review market absent. No ongoing accountability maintenance architecture.

### Technology/Vendor Solutions

**What exists:** AI governance platforms (OneTrust, Vanta, Drata, etc.) offer policy management, compliance automation, and evidence collection for enterprise. Some US/UK vendors expanding to European market.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Platforms designed for enterprise compliance programs, not boutique professional services. Heavy implementation requirements, subscription costs disproportionate to boutique scale. No Geneva/Swiss regulatory calibration. No integration with Swiss carrier attestation instruments. No nFADP-specific content for wealth management. Output is documentation infrastructure, not accountability positioning.

### SRO Guidance

**What exists:** SROs (OARG, AVK, asset management industry bodies) have published general AI guidance statements, some specific to wealth management.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Guidance is principles-based without operational implementation path. No Geneva-calibrated small-institution methodology. No carrier attestation pre-signing review. SRO guidance vacuum confirmed as structural condition — no timeline for Geneva-specific boutique guidance.

### Academic/Professional Education

**What exists:** Universities (UNIGE, ETH Zurich), professional associations (CFA Society Geneva, Swiss Banking Association), and training firms offer AI in finance programs.

**Gap for boutique WM:** Programs address AI technical literacy and investment applications, not AI governance accountability for managing directors. No Geneva-specific accountability briefing format for boutique WM MDs. No carrier attestation preparation. No nFADP personal liability framing.

## What's Missing (Market White Space)

### 1. Carrier Pre-Signing Attestation Review

**Missing:** No Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation instruments at boutique WM scale and price point. MDs signing carrier documents committing to undefined adequacy standard without independent analysis.

**Why it matters:** Carrier attestation is primary accountability instrument creating personal MD exposure. Carrier documents designed for underwriting risk evaluation, not MD professional liability protection. No review pathway = MD personal signature on documents without understanding what they commit to.

**What would serve the market:** A structured pre-signing review methodology calibrated to Swiss professional liability doctrine, priced for boutique WM (CHF 2,000–5,000 for initial review), delivered by Geneva-calibrated practitioners who understand the carrier instrument design logic.

### 2. Boutique-Proportionate AI Governance Documentation System

**Missing:** No market service offers proportionate AI governance documentation architecture for boutique WM 50–200 employees. Existing solutions either enterprise-grade (too heavy) or generic (not calibrated to Swiss WM context).

**Why it matters:** FINMA 08/2024 seven-dimension framework requires documentation across governance, inventory, data quality, testing, documentation, explainability, and independent review. No Geneva-calibrated small-institution methodology exists showing how boutique WM satisfies each dimension without enterprise compliance infrastructure.

**What would serve the market:** A boutique-scale AI governance documentation system with pre-built templates calibrated to FINMA 08/2024 dimensions, carrier third-generation evidence requirements, and nFADP obligations. Integrated system, not individual document templates. Architecture produces coherent documentation system, not collection of defensible individual documents.

### 3. Institutional Client Response Architecture

**Missing:** No market service helps boutique WM prepare institutional client AI governance questionnaire responses with competitive intelligence awareness. Responses currently prepared as compliance exercises, unaware questionnaire may be part of simultaneous multi-boutique comparison.

**Why it matters:** Institutional client questionnaire is primary wake-up call for Geneva boutiques. Response creates commercial representations. Sophisticated institutional clients using responses as competitive intelligence. No Geneva-calibrated methodology for calibrating response against competitive context.

**What would serve the market:** A structured institutional questionnaire response methodology that accounts for disclosure vs. competitive intelligence tension, calibrates response detail level against comparative context, and produces responses that serve both compliance function and competitive positioning.

### 4. Geneva Accountability Architecture Briefing Format

**Missing:** No Geneva-calibrated briefing format addresses boutique WM MD personal accountability across carrier attestation, FINMA guidance, nFADP obligations, and institutional client pressure simultaneously. No service frames AI governance as personal MD accountability problem — not operational efficiency, not compliance checklist.

**Why it matters:** Existing AI governance services frame as technical compliance or efficiency optimization. Boutique WM MDs experiencing AI governance as personal liability exposure. Market has not named the specific accountability architecture problem or produced proportionate service format.

**What would serve the market:** A 90-minute executive briefing format (CHF 2,200) specifically designed for boutique WM MDs addressing personal accountability across all four channels simultaneously, in Geneva context, with actionable immediate positioning steps.

### 5. MD Personal Liability Defense Documentation Architecture

**Missing:** No market service distinguishes between carrier attestation documentation architecture and nFADP Art.63 criminal defense documentation architecture. Existing programs build documentation for administrative exposure, inadequate for criminal defense.

**Why it matters:** nFADP Art.63 personal criminal liability has different evidentiary standard, different defense logic than administrative fine. Documentation built to carrier attestation standard may be architecturally inadequate for criminal defense if Art.63 liability triggers. Boutique MD has no framework for evaluating which architecture serves which exposure.

**What would serve the market:** A documentation architecture assessment that maps existing AI governance documentation against both carrier evidence requirements and criminal defense evidentiary requirements, identifying architectural gaps and recommending proportionate documentation architecture serving both.

### 6. Geneva-Specific nFADP AI Application Guidance for WM

**Missing:** No Geneva-calibrated guidance maps nFADP articles specifically to boutique WM AI use cases. General nFADP compliance programs do not address AI-assisted portfolio construction, client profiling notifications, inference accuracy obligations, or Art.21 automated decision explanations in wealth management context.

**Why it matters:** AI-assisted portfolio construction activates multiple nFADP articles simultaneously (Art.6 lawful basis, Art.8 profiling notification, Art.13 accuracy, Art.20/21 explanation rights, Art.63 criminal liability). No Geneva-specific guidance maps these simultaneous obligations to specific boutique WM documentation actions.

**What would serve the market:** An nFADP AI obligations mapping specific to boutique WM AI use cases, with actionable documentation requirements for each article, written for managing directors not privacy counsel.

---

## Competitive Positioning Implication

Geneva boutique WM AI governance market has structural white space at exactly the scale where accountability pressure is most acute. No existing market participant is positioned as the Geneva-calibrated, boutique-proportionate, MD-accountability-focused service provider.

The boutique WM ICP is structurally underserved — too small for enterprise solutions, too Geneva-specific for generic compliance programs, too personally exposed for abstract regulatory guidance.

**Competitive moat:** Sustained participation in Geneva accountability formation ecosystem producing pattern knowledge non-replicable through methodology documents or expertise claims. Most operationally relevant intelligence for this ICP is not publication-eligible — requires sustained institutional participation to observe standards forming, not arrived.
