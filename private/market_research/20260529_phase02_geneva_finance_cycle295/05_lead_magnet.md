# Geneva Finance ICP: Boutique Wealth Management (50–200 employees)

## Lead Magnet: The Geneva Wealth Manager's AI Accountability Briefing

---

## Positioning Logic

**Core positioning:** The Geneva Wealth Manager's AI Accountability Briefing addresses personal MD accountability across carrier attestation, FINMA guidance, nFADP obligations, and institutional client pressure — simultaneously, in Geneva context, for managing directors of boutique wealth management firms.

**Why this framing:** Existing AI governance services frame as technical compliance, operational efficiency, or regulatory checklist. The boutique WM MD experiences AI governance as personal liability exposure. The managing director's name is on the carrier attestation, the personal signature creates personal exposure, and the AI decisions made under their authority are their professional accountability. This briefing leads with the personal accountability frame that activates the MD's decision reflex before intellectual interest.

**Who this is for:** Managing directors of Geneva boutique wealth management firms, 50–200 employees, who have signed or are about to sign carrier AI attestation instruments, received institutional client AI governance questionnaires, or are operating AI-assisted portfolio management without clear documentation of human authorization.

**Who this is not for:** Enterprise financial institutions with dedicated compliance infrastructure; wealth managers who have already completed a comprehensive AI governance program with Geneva-calibrated methodology; firms where AI is limited to back-office reporting without client-facing portfolio construction role.

---

## Lead Magnet Specifications

**Format:** 90-minute executive briefing — synchronous, individual (not group), delivered by Geneva-calibrated practitioner

**Language:** English primary; French available for bilingual delivery on request

**Price:** CHF 2,200 per firm (90-minute session); CHF 3,800 half-day (extended session with documentation review)

**Positioning:** "What You're Actually Responsible For — Before Your Carrier Asks"

**Maximum frequency:** 2 engagements per calendar month (intentional scarcity performing accurate self-qualification)

**Pre-briefing requirement:** Client completes brief AI governance situation questionnaire before session — defines specific accountability questions relevant to their situation

**Post-briefing output:** Written accountability positioning summary identifying three priority actions with Geneva-specific framing — does not produce comprehensive AI governance program (different engagement)

## Briefing Agenda

### Opening Frame (15 minutes)

The accountability architecture problem: why Geneva boutique wealth managers are facing their most significant personal AI governance exposure precisely when they have the least infrastructure to address it. FINMA 08/2024, carrier third-generation attestation, nFADP obligations, and institutional client pressure converging on the managing director personally.

### The Carrier Attestation Problem (20 minutes)

What the carrier is actually asking for and why current documentation probably doesn't satisfy it. Third-generation evidence requirements probing specific human authorization moments — not policy documents, not tool inventories. Why carrier evidence architecture is designed for underwriting risk evaluation, not professional accountability documentation or criminal defense. What "adequate AI governance controls" commits you to personally and what evidence standard that standard actually requires.

### The nFADP Exposure Map (20 minutes)

The four nFADP articles most relevant to AI-assisted portfolio management: which ones your current AI use activates, which ones you are probably violating without knowing, and why awareness alone doesn't discharge the obligation. Art.8 profiling notifications for AI-assisted portfolio construction — what they require, why Geneva boutiques are not issuing them, and what the exposure is. Art.20/21 explanation rights and why they conflict with proprietary methodology in ways not yet named in Geneva boutique WM discourse.

### The Institutional Client Question (15 minutes)

What happens when an institutional client sends a 40-page AI governance questionnaire — not as a compliance exercise but as part of a simultaneous comparison across their manager roster. Why your response becomes a competitive intelligence data point. How to think about disclosure calibration when the same information serves compliance function and creates competitive exposure.

### Documentation Window Positioning (15 minutes)

Why the documentation window is not a deadline metaphor — it is a present-tense positioning event with irreversible properties. Why building documentation after the window closes cannot produce the evidence that would have been built during the window. What the window is actually deciding and why the positioning choice must be made now.

### Priority Actions and Close (15 minutes)

Three specific actions the MD can take in the next 30 days that address the highest-probability accountability exposure points. What the Geneva-calibrated approach looks like that is different from enterprise compliance programs. When and how to engage for ongoing support.

## Lead Magnet Differentiation

**From regulatory seminars:** Regulatory seminars teach what the frameworks require. This briefing addresses what the managing director is personally responsible for and what evidence that responsibility requires in practice.

**From compliance checklists:** Compliance checklists produce document collections. This briefing produces accountability positioning clarity with three specific priority actions.

**From big-four frameworks:** Big-four frameworks designed for institutions with compliance departments. This briefing is calibrated for managing directors of 50–200 person firms who carry personal accountability without institutional infrastructure.

**From nFADP gap analyses:** nFADP gap analyses map obligations against current state. This briefing addresses the personal liability dimension, the carrier attestation instrument specifically, and the institutional client pressure simultaneously — none of which general nFADP reviews cover.

## ICP Decision Trigger Language

**Entry signals:**
- "My carrier renewal includes an AI questionnaire this year"
- "I signed the attestation last year and I'm not sure what it commits me to"
- "We received an institutional client AI governance questionnaire"
- "I've been running AI-assisted portfolios for 18+ months and I realized I can't explain to myself how the AI made specific decisions"
- "Our PI insurer changed their questionnaire — AI wasn't mentioned two years ago"

**Emotional register:** Personal accountability anxiety, not abstract regulatory fear. "I'm the one on the line" framing. Decision paralysis from undefined standards. Retrospective recognition ("the questionnaire arrived") with forward-looking urgency ("what happens when they ask the next question").

**Engagement decision criteria:** ICP decides to engage when the personal liability framing resonates with their specific situation — not when they decide they have a general AI governance problem. The briefing must feel like it is about their name on the attestation, their signature, their personal exposure — not about the firm's compliance program.
