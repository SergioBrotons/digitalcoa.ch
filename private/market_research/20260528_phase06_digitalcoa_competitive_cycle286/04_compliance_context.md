## Current Regulatory Timeline

### Federal Council AI Consultation Draft — Expected Before Summer 2026

**What it is:** The Federal Council's consultation draft on AI regulation in Switzerland. This is the formal moment when regulatory standards begin to crystallize from formation to arrival.

**Why it matters:** The documentation window (the period before the draft arrives) is not a compliance deadline — it is a burden-of-proof exercise. What professionals document during the window is evidence of what they determined adequate, when, and why. After the draft arrives and the window closes, new documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct window-period reasoning.

**Timing implication:** The active window is now. Professionals who engage with DigitalCoa.ch before the draft arrives are building evidence of appropriate governance during formation. Professionals who wait engage with DigitalCoa.ch after the window closes, during explanation.

**Market positioning language:**
*"The consultation draft is the moment when the standard stops forming and starts arriving. The window before that draft — that's when you're building evidence of what you thought adequate and why. After it closes, you're explaining your absence."*

### nFADP Enforcement Transition — Ongoing

**What it is:** The revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) has been in force since September 2023, with enforcement in transition. Personal data processing obligations, DPIA requirements, and data subject rights are live obligations.

**Key obligations affecting DigitalCoa.ch ICPs:**

| Article | Obligation | ICP Segment |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Art. 6 | Purpose limitation for AI processing | All verticals |
| Art. 9 | Third-party processing chain accountability | Consulting, Finance |
| Art. 13 | Explanation obligation for AI-influenced decisions | Healthcare, Finance |
| Art. 15 | Right to correction of inaccurate AI inferences | Finance (portfolio) |
| Art. 21 | AI-influenced decision explanation | Finance |
| Art. 22 | DPIA mandatory for high-risk automated processing | Healthcare, Legal |
| Art. 63 | Personal criminal liability for serious violations | Finance, Legal |

**Why it matters:** nFADP obligations are present-tense and active for all four ICP verticals. The Art. 63 personal criminal liability provision is specifically under-recognized — most managing directors have not fully processed that "the worst case" is not a corporate fine but personal criminal exposure.

**Market positioning language:**
*"Art. 63 nFADP is personal criminal liability. Not company liability — yours personally. If you're running a firm and using AI, you need to know exactly where that exposure sits and what your documentation says about it."*

### FINMA 08/2024 AI Guidance — Active

**What it is:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 on artificial intelligence and machine learning, published by the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority.

**Why it matters:** This formalizes supervisory expectations for AI use in FINMA-supervised institutions. For Geneva wealth managers who are SRO members, this creates a formal expectation hierarchy: FINMA publishes AI guidance, the SRO acknowledges it in silence, the professional is left without SRO-mediated interpretation.

**The specific gap:** FINMA 08/2024 requires AI inventory and governance documentation for supervised institutions. Boutique wealth managers (50–200 employees) are not directly FINMA-supervised but are affected through SRO membership. The FINMA expectation creates pressure without proportionate methodology.

**Market positioning language:**
*"FINMA 08/2024 requires an AI inventory and governance documentation. You're not directly supervised by FINMA — you're an SRO member. Your SRO hasn't translated what FINMA expects for your size firm. That's the gap."*

### SRO AI Obligation Silence — Confirmed

**What it is:** SROs (Self-Regulatory Organizations) in Swiss financial services have issued zero AI governance guidance to their members. This is confirmed across all C286 Phase 02 cycles.

**Why it matters:** The Geneva wealth manager who is an SRO member has a formal membership relationship with an organization that has provided no AI governance guidance. This creates a specific regulatory vacuum: the professional is exposed to FINMA expectations without SRO-mediated interpretive support.

**Market positioning language:**
*"Your SRO is a membership organization that hasn't published anything on AI governance. You are paying membership fees to a body that's given you nothing to work with on this. The vacuum doesn't mean you're fine. It means you're navigating it alone."*

### Council of Europe AI Convention — Switzerland Ratified February 2025

**What it is:** The Council of Europe AI Convention (AI Convention / CoE AI Convention), ratified by Switzerland in February 2025, creates private law obligations for AI systems operating in member states. Implementation requires domestic legislation.

**Why it matters:** The convention's ratification path creates present-tense obligation through existing mandate contracts and carrier underwriting terms. Professionals who signed carrier attestations before ratification may have committed to adequacy standards that now incorporate convention obligations they were not made aware of.

**Specific implication:** The convention creates a private law overlay on existing client mandates. The obligations are not yet fully implemented in Swiss domestic law, but the ratification signals where the standard is moving — and professionals are already committing to it through carrier attestation language.

### FMH Digital Competency Requirements — Active

**What it is:** The FMH (Swiss Medical Association) has published working papers on digital competencies for physicians using AI. FMH guidance is clear on principle (physician remains fully responsible for clinical decisions even with AI) and silent on procedure (no operationalization protocol published).

**Why it matters:** The Geneva independent physician ICP reads the FMH guidance and understands the principle. They are looking for someone who can translate principle into procedure. This is the specific service gap: the guidance exists, operationalization does not.

**Market positioning language:**
*"The FMH says you remain responsible. What they haven't published is what 'responsible' looks like when you're staring at an AI-generated recommendation at 10pm before signing a prescription. That's the gap."*

---

### MedDO Classification and swissdamed Registration — Active

**What it is:** The Medical Devices Ordinance (MedDO) applies to AI tools that qualify as medical devices. Swissmedic's swissdamed registry (voluntary since August 2025, mandatory from July 2026) requires registration of AI tools meeting device classification thresholds.

**Why it matters:** Independent physician practices are the ICP segment with the clearest near-deadline trigger. Between now and July 2026, every independent practice using an AI tool that may qualify as a medical device needs to determine its classification and, if required, register before the mandatory deadline.

**Market positioning language:**
*"You're using an AI tool in your practice. Does it meet Swissmedic's classification threshold for medical devices? If it does and you're not registered before July 2026, you're running an unregistered medical device. The deadline is real and the classification question isn't being answered anywhere else."*
