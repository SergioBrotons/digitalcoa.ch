## How ICPs Describe What They Need

### The Universal Entry Language (All Four Verticals)

**The question that opens every conversation:**
- *"Par où on commence?"* / *"Where do we even start?"*
- *"I don't even have an AI inventory."*
- *"I know I'm supposed to do something. I don't know what."*

**The accountability recognition language:**
- *"I signed the AI attestation on my renewal. I didn't know I was signing something personal."* (legal, consulting, finance, healthcare)
- *"My client sent me a 40-page AI governance questionnaire. I had nothing to answer it with."* (finance)
- *"The FMH says I remain responsible. Nobody's told me what that means in practice."* (healthcare)
- *"My engagement letters were written before we started using AI tools."* (consulting)

**The synthesis recognition language:**
- *"You're the first person who's explained it across all of them at the same time."*
- *"I've been talking to people in finance and in law and we're all experiencing the same thing — and nobody was connecting the dots."*
- *"I didn't realize the carrier question and the client question and the regulatory question were all the same question."*

**What ICPs search for (inferred from entry signals):**
- "Geneva AI governance consulting" / "conseil gouvernance IA Genève"
- "AI accountability briefing Geneva" / "briefing reddition comptes IA Genève"
- "carrier AI questionnaire preparation" / "préparation questionnaire carrier IA"
- "Geneva wealth management AI compliance" / "conformité IA gestion patrimoine Genève"
- "Geneva law firm AI governance" / "gouvernance IA cabinet juridique Genève"

## What Resonates vs. What Doesn't

### What Resonates

**Naming the specific mechanism (not the category):**
✅ *"Your carrier's ISAE 3000 report proves your vendor's controls. It doesn't prove yours."*
❌ *"AI governance is important for your business."*

✅ *"The documentation window is a burden-of-proof exercise. After it closes, you can't recreate the evidence."*
❌ *"You need to document your AI use."*

**Matching the entry signal language:**
✅ *"The entry signal is identical across all four verticals: 'Par où on commence? Je n'ai même pas d'inventaire.'"*
❌ *"We offer comprehensive AI governance advisory services."*

**Demonstrating Geneva-specific participation:**
✅ *"I see the same problem across attorneys, wealth managers, and consultants — because I'm in all three communities."*
❌ *"We have expertise in AI governance best practices."*

**Naming the tension the ICP feels:**
✅ *"You want to be compliant. You also want to keep operating. The question is how to do both when the standard is forming."*
❌ *"You need a governance framework."*

### What Doesn't Resonate

- Generics: "AI governance framework," "compliance roadmap," "AI strategy"
- Enterprise framing: "enterprise AI governance," "board-level AI oversight"
- Training framing: "AI training course," "workshop on AI tools"
- Vendor framing: "AI tool evaluation," "vendor selection"
- Certification framing: "get certified for AI," "ISO 42001 preparation"

## Vertical-Specific ICP Language Patterns

### Geneva Legal (Small Law Firms, 2–5 Attorneys)

**Entry signals:**
- Carrier renewal with AI attestation section requiring MD signature
- Opposing counsel beginning to ask about AI methodology
- Bar guidance vacuum confirmed by bar's own silence

**Language ICP uses:**
- *"Je ne sais pas ce que je signe."*
- *"The bar says I remain responsible. No procedure exists."*
- *"I didn't know the engagement letters governing my AI-assisted work were the old letters."*

**What would feel like "for me":**
- Naming the specific mechanism: carrier attestation + personal signature + undefined adequacy standard combined
- Naming the bar's guidance vacuum structurally (not as ignorance but as institutional limitation)
- Naming the engagement letter obsolescence as active exposure, not documentation gap

### Geneva Finance (Boutique Wealth Management, 50–200 Employees)

**Entry signals:**
- Institutional client sends 40-page AI governance DDQ
- Carrier renewal arrives with third-generation AI attestation
- Staff AI shadow use discovered internally
- FINMA 08/2024 published but SRO silent

**Language ICP uses:**
- *"Mon client m'a envoyé un questionnaire de 40 pages sur l'IA. Je n'avais rien à répondre."*
- *"Je ne sais pas quoi faire avec le rapport ISAE 3000 de mon carrier."*
- *"On m'a dit que l'art. 63 nFADP prévoir des sanctions pénales personnelles."*

**What would feel like "for me":**
- The ISAE 3000 chain confusion clearly named
- The institutional DDQ as the specific trigger moment (not abstract compliance anxiety)
- The 90-day roadmap concept: starting point that is proportionate to boutique scale

### Geneva Consulting (MD/Partner, 2–15 / Boutique 50–200)

**Entry signals:**
- Third-gen carrier questionnaire arrives requiring MD personal signature
- Client asks AI methodology question, MD cannot produce documentation
- Proposals from different boutiques beginning to look stylometrically similar

**Language ICP uses:**
- *"Nos lettres de mission ont été rédigées avant que nous commencions à utiliser l'IA."*
- *"Quand je mandate un sous-consultant qui utilise aussi l'IA, pour quoi suis-je exactement responsable ?"*
- *"J'ai signé les conditions du vendor. Je ne les ai jamais lues."*

**What would feel like "for me":**
- Naming the proposal stylometric convergence as a structural property before any individual firm's quality is questioned
- The vendor terms incompatibility with MD personal liability clearly mapped
- The sub-consultant delegation chain as a discrete unnamed problem

### Geneva Healthcare (Independent Practice, 2–10 Physicians)

**Entry signals:**
- Hospital or institutional client requires AI governance documentation
- Vendor pitched something; ICP couldn't evaluate the claims
- FMH guidance read but "responsible" not operationalized
- Swissdamed registration deadline approaching (July 2026)

**Language ICP uses:**
- *"La FMH dit que je reste responsable. Personne n'a publié ce que ça veut dire en pratique."*
- *"L'outil que j'utilise est-il corrects selon Swissmedic ?"*
- *"Mes formulaires de consentement patient ont été rédigés avant les outils IA."*

**What would feel like "for me":**
- Naming the consent form obsolescence specifically
- The "Swiss hosted" vs. full data chain distinction clearly explained
- The MedDO classification question answered proportionally for independent practice scale

## Cross-Vertical Patterns That Apply to DigitalCoa.ch

### What the Universal Entry Signal Tells Us About Positioning

The "par où on commence?" signal is consistent across all four verticals — not because the problem is identical in content, but because the structural experience is identical: acknowledged urgency, undefined standard, no clear first action.

**Positioning implication:** ICPs are not looking for someone who can name the framework. They are looking for someone who can name the problem in the language they experience it — and who can tell them what to do first.

DigitalCoa.ch's positioning language should:
- Start with the entry language, not the solution vocabulary
- Acknowledge the "where do I start" paralysis explicitly
- Name the specific mechanism producing the urgency (not just that urgency exists)
- Demonstrate Geneva participation through specific named examples

### What the Synthesis Moat Tells Us About Differentiation

The C286 Phase 05 finding: "the pattern is only visible from sustained participation in all four verticals." This is the specific structural moat that no single-vertical player can replicate.

**Positioning implication:** DigitalCoa.ch's differentiation is not methodology quality, not publication record, not certifications. It is that Sergio sees the same accountability ecosystem problem appearing in four different professional contexts simultaneously — because he is present in all four.

**Language that performs differentiation:**
- *"I see the same problem across all four verticals because I'm in all four verticals."*
- *"The pattern becomes visible only when you're looking at all four at the same time — not from analyzing each separately."*
- *"That's not something you can reverse-engineer from a publication."*
