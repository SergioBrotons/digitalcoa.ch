## The AVM in Your Property Listing Is Your Biggest Compliance Risk. Most Geneva Agencies Don't Know It.

The Federal AI Ordinance has been in force since January 1, 2025. That's 16 months. Most Geneva real estate agencies have been operating under it without a documented AI governance framework — and without knowing it.

The most common culprit: Automated Valuation Models (AVMs). If your agency uses an AVM to generate property valuations — for listings, client reports, market analyses, or pricing recommendations — you are likely exposed under two frameworks simultaneously.

**This is the PILOT TRAP:**

Your AVM generates an automated valuation. That valuation is an automated decision affecting a data subject (buyer, seller, or tenant). Under FADP Article 21, any automated decision affecting a person requires human review. Under the Federal AI Ordinance, high-risk AI decisions require documented human oversight.

If you're using an AVM without documented human review of its outputs, you're exposed under both frameworks — simultaneously.

This checklist helps you close that gap.

## The Five AI Tools in Your Agency

Most Geneva real estate agencies use AI in at least these five areas:

**1. Automated Valuation Models (AVMs)**
The highest-risk use case. Affects buyers, sellers, tenants. Generates automated decisions requiring human review.

**2. Tenant Screening AI**
Automated screening of rental applications. Affects data subjects. FADP Art. 21 applies. Geneva rental law adds additional requirements.

**3. CRM Automation + Lead Scoring AI**
Automated client communication, lead prioritization, follow-up scheduling. Lower-risk, but touches personal data.

**4. Property Matching AI**
AI matching properties to buyer criteria. Affects data subjects through recommendations.

**5. Client Communication AI**
AI-assisted responses to property inquiries, appointment scheduling. Lower-risk if no substantive decisions are made.

## Your AVM Human Review Checklist

For every AVM-assisted valuation, document:

**1. What was the AVM output?**
[Property address, valuation amount, date]

**2. Who reviewed it?**
[Agent name, qualifications]

**3. What human judgment was applied?**
[Comparison with local market knowledge, adjustments made, rationale]

**4. Was the client informed an AVM was used?**
[Yes/No — disclosure may be required under FADP]

**5. Can you demonstrate this review process if asked by a regulator?**
[Documentation system: Yes/No]

If you answered "No" to question 5, you have a gap to close.
