## Problem Statement (3 layers)

### Layer 1: AVM + FADP Art. 21 — The PILOT TRAP
AVMs generate automated valuations that affect buyers, sellers, and tenants. Under FADP Art. 21, any automated decision affecting a data subject requires human review. Under the Federal AI Ordinance (operative January 1, 2025 — now 16+ months), high-risk AI decisions require human oversight. Most Geneva agencies using AVMs are exposed under both — without knowing it.

### Layer 2: No Sector Guidance + Smallest Agencies Most Exposed
Geneva real estate is the only sector without AI-specific regulatory guidance. Most Geneva agencies are small (1-10 agents) with no internal compliance resources. The belief-action gap (25% AI adoption, ~5% governance policy) is the widest of all six sectors.

### Layer 3: G7 Évian + International Buyer Market
Geneva luxury real estate serves international HNW buyers. G7 Évian (June 15-17, ~75 days) may create near-term transaction uncertainty. International buyers' data on Swiss platforms raises FADP + data sovereignty questions.
