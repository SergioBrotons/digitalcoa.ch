### 1. Federal AI Ordinance 16+ Months — Geneva Real Estate Most Exposed
**Source:** Cross-phase C58 synthesis + Federal AI Ordinance (Jan 2025)

The Federal AI Ordinance has been operative for 16+ months. Geneva real estate agencies are among the most exposed of all six sectors because:
- No sector-specific AI guidance exists for real estate
- Most Geneva agencies are small (1-10 agents), without internal compliance resources
- AVMs (Automated Valuation Models) used in property listings are the highest-risk AI use case in real estate
- Human review of AVM outputs is required under the ordinance — but almost no agency has documented this process

**Real estate implications (NET-NEW C59):** The belief-action gap in real estate (25% using AI, ~5% with governance policy) is the widest of all six sectors. The Federal AI Ordinance's human oversight requirement is most acute for AVM use, which is ubiquitous in Geneva luxury real estate.

### 2. The AVM + FADP Art. 21 Convergence — PILOT TRAP Confirmed and Expanded
**Source:** Cross-phase C58 synthesis + FADP principles (The Compliance Digest)

AVMs used in property listings = high-risk AI under Federal AI Ordinance + FADP Art. 21 simultaneously. This is the PILOT TRAP:
- AVM generates a valuation (automated decision)
- That decision affects a data subject (buyer, seller, tenant)
- FADP Art. 21 requires human review of automated decisions affecting individuals
- Federal AI Ordinance requires human oversight of high-risk AI decisions

Every Geneva agency using AVMs without documented human review is doubly exposed — under two frameworks simultaneously.

**Real estate implications (NET-NEW C59):** The PILOT TRAP is not theoretical. It's the standard practice of virtually every Geneva real estate agency. The exposure is systemic.
