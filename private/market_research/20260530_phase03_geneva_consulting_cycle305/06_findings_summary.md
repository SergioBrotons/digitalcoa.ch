# Findings Summary: Geneva Consulting — C305 Phase 03

## 6 NET-NEW Findings: Distinct from C214–C304

### Finding 01 — Client AI Governance Questionnaires Function as Undocumented RFP Scoring Gates

**The finding:** Client AI governance questionnaires are functioning as procurement scoring gates in Geneva consulting RFPs. Firms that cannot produce adequate responses are being scored lower regardless of methodology quality. The competitive consequence is active, measurable, and happening now — before any regulatory mandate requires it. The firm losing mandates does not know the documentation gap is the reason.

**Mechanism:** Client RFP → AI governance questionnaire scored as weighted criterion → firm cannot respond adequately → procurement score reflects documentation gap → mandate awarded to competitor with better documentation → firm attributes loss to price or relationship.

**C305 context advance:** C304 Phase 03 identified client questionnaires as creating documented evidence of inconsistent response. C305 Phase 03 finds the competitive gate function is active and measurable — the documentation gap now has direct revenue consequence, not just compliance exposure.

**Implication:** The client questionnaire response file is a discrete service with a direct revenue consequence. The service is not "help me write an AI policy" — it is "produce the response file that wins the mandate."

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your client sent a 40-question AI governance checklist. You couldn't answer most of them. The competitor who won the mandate could. That's not a compliance gap — it's a revenue gap."

### Finding 02 — Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Chain Is Accountable Without Being Visible

**The finding:** Geneva consulting firms retain specialist sub-consultants whose AI tool use is invisible to the managing director. The managing director is personally accountable for sub-consultant AI use in client deliverables under professional liability and carrier attestation terms. The accountability is documented; the visibility is not. When a client challenges a deliverable containing AI-generated content, the accountability chain runs to the managing director, who cannot produce the authorization, scope definition, or monitoring record.

**Mechanism:** Engagement → sub-consultant retained → sub-consultant uses AI tools without disclosure → deliverable contains AI-generated content → client challenges methodology → managing director personally accountable under carrier attestation and professional liability → no delegation record exists.

**C305 context advance:** C304 Phase 03 identified sub-consultant delegation as creating accountable exposure without accountability visibility. C305 Phase 03 finds the exposure is now active — client methodology challenges are arriving, sub-consultant AI use is increasing, and the documentation gap is compounding across every engagement.

**Implication:** A sub-consultant AI delegation authorization framework is a discrete service. The framework enables managing directors to authorize AI use by specialists while maintaining the accountability documentation that the carrier attestation requires.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're personally accountable for what your sub-consultants do with AI. You have no visibility into it. That's not a relationship problem — it's a documented accountability gap with your signature on it."

### Finding 03 — "Methodology Defensibility" Is the Core Asset at Risk, Not a Secondary Concern

**The finding:** The consulting firm's methodology is the product. When clients begin asking how recommendations were derived and whether AI was used in the analysis, the methodology's integrity becomes the issue. A consulting firm that cannot explain the provenance of its recommendations — what AI tools were used, what data was input, what human judgment evaluated the output — has a methodology defensibility problem that undermines its core value proposition. This is categorically different from a compliance gap; it is a competitive positioning problem.

**Mechanism:** Client receives recommendation → client asks about AI role in methodology → consulting firm cannot explain with precision → client confidence in methodology undermined → relationship damaged regardless of recommendation quality.

**C305 context advance:** C304 Phase 03 identified "AI-assisted analysis" as not being a documentation category. C305 Phase 03 names the specific consequence — the methodology itself becomes indefensible, not just the documentation. The problem is not administrative; it is strategic.

**Implication:** A methodology provenance documentation standard is a competitive positioning tool, not just a compliance exercise. Firms that can credibly explain their AI-assisted methodology earn more client trust than firms that cannot — regardless of the actual AI tools used.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your methodology is what you sell. If you can't explain how AI was part of producing your recommendations, your methodology becomes indefensible — to clients, to carriers, and in court. That's not a documentation problem. That's a business problem."

---

### Finding 04 — Carrier Attestation Evidence Production Is Structurally Separate from Form Completion

**The finding:** Carrier attestation form completion and evidence production are different activities served by different documentation architectures. The form asks for representations about governance standards. The evidence demonstrates what actually existed at the time of signing. Consulting firms are completing forms without producing the evidence record the form architecture assumes. The carrier will request evidence at renewal or at claim. The documentation gap is created at signing, not at the incident.

**Mechanism:** Carrier attestation signed → managing director represents adequate oversight → evidence does not exist → carrier requests evidence at renewal → firm scrambles to reconstruct → retroactive documentation is inadequate → personal signature creates personal exposure.

**C305 context advance:** C305 Phase 01 identified the attestation architecture problem (form creates representation, not just acknowledgment). C305 Phase 03 extends this: the evidence gap is not just about what was attested — it is that the evidence production methodology for consulting firms has never been defined. The form and the evidence are structurally separate, and no one is producing the evidence architecture.

**Implication:** Separating attestation form completion from evidence production is the service design insight. The managing director needs both — but they need them in the right order. Evidence first, then form. Not the reverse.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You signed the attestation. What you didn't produce was the evidence record the form assumes exists. The carrier will ask for it. When they do, the form you signed becomes evidence of the gap — not evidence of the governance."

### Finding 05 — nFADP Article 63 Personal Structuring Is Distinct from Corporate nFADP Compliance

**The finding:** The Geneva consulting market has nFADP corporate compliance offerings (privacy policies, data processing agreements, consent mechanisms). It does not have Article 63 personal structuring for managing directors. These are different services serving different protection objectives. Corporate nFADP compliance protects the firm. Article 63 personal structuring protects the managing director's individual assets and professional license from personal criminal exposure — a risk that corporate structure does not address. The managing director who believes their corporate structure protects them from Article 63 is operating under a structural misunderstanding with active personal exposure.

**Mechanism:** Data processing during consulting engagement → unauthorized or undocumented legal basis → Article 63 personal criminal liability triggered → managing director personally exposed → corporate structure does not discharge Article 63 liability → personal assets and license at risk.

**C305 context advance:** C304 Phase 03 identified nFADP Art. 63 personal liability as requiring specific structuring beyond corporate structure. C305 Phase 03 names the specific service gap: the market has corporate nFADP compliance — it does not have managing-director-specific Art. 63 personal structuring for consulting firms. The two are not the same service.

**Implication:** A discrete Article 63 personal structuring review for managing directors of consulting firms — assessing what the corporate structure does and doesn't protect, what personal measures are required, what documentation constitutes personal due diligence evidence.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your corporate nFADP compliance protects the firm. It doesn't protect you personally from Article 63. Those are different risks requiring different structuring. Most firms only have one."

---

### Finding 06 — Geneva Consulting Firms Face Three Compounding Documentation Debt Vectors Simultaneously

**The finding:** Geneva consulting firms are accumulating documentation debt across three independent vectors with no framework for stopping the accumulation: (1) client engagement records accumulating without AI documentation, (2) carrier attestation records accumulating without evidence production, and (3) sub-consultant delegation records accumulating without authorization documentation. Each vector compounds independently. The firm has no framework for measuring the total exposure, no methodology for stopping the accumulation, and no Geneva-calibrated protocol for addressing any of the three. The debt is not hypothetical — it is present-tense, active, and accelerating.

**Mechanism:** Engagement completed → AI used without documentation → Vector 1 debt added. Carrier renewal signed → evidence not produced → Vector 2 debt added. Sub-consultant retained → delegation not documented → Vector 3 debt added. Next engagement: same pattern. Each cycle adds debt without resolution.

**C305 context advance:** C304 Phase 03 identified retention debt as a general concept. C305 Phase 03 maps the specific three-vector structure of the documentation debt for consulting firms, names the independence of each vector, and identifies that no Geneva-calibrated framework exists for addressing any of the three. This is the consulting-specific documentation debt topology.

**Implication:** A three-vector debt freeze and forward capture protocol is the entry service. The protocol: stop the accumulation (freeze), establish the documentation architecture for new engagements (forward capture), and produce the evidence record for active carrier exposures (retroactive review where appropriate and feasible). This is not retrospective reconstruction — it is debt stabilization plus forward architecture.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're accumulating documentation debt in three directions simultaneously — with clients, with carriers, and with sub-consultants. You don't have a protocol for any of them. I can give you a three-vector debt freeze that stops the accumulation and builds the architecture for new engagements."
