# Market Signals: Geneva Consulting — C305 Phase 03

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

---

### Signal 01 — Client RFP AI Governance Checklists Are Now Standard in Geneva Consulting Procurement

**Signal:** Large corporate clients and international organizations procuring Geneva consulting services are including AI governance checklists as standard RFP attachments. The checklist is not optional — responses are scored and weighted. Firms that cannot respond to AI governance questions are being flagged in procurement systems regardless of the quality of their core consulting proposal.

**Specific complaint:** "We lost a mandate to a firm that was more expensive. The procurement team said our AI governance response was inadequate. We didn't know that was a scoring criterion."

**Mechanism:** RFP → AI governance checklist scored → documentation absent or weak → procurement flags firm → mandate awarded to competitor with better documentation → firm doesn't know why they lost beyond price.

**Market evidence pattern:** AI governance documentation has moved from a trust signal to a procurement gate. The consulting firm's competitive positioning now includes evidence of AI oversight capability — not just quality of consulting methodology.

---

### Signal 02 — Sub-Consultant AI Use Creating Client Deliverable Quality Variance With No Accountability Chain

**Signal:** Geneva consulting firms are receiving sub-consultant deliverables that contain AI-generated content with no标识, no provenance, and no methodology documentation. When clients receive these deliverables and challenge quality or appropriateness, the managing director has no documentation of what was AI-generated, what was human-reviewed, and what was original sub-consultant work.

**Specific complaint:** "We hired a specialist for a technical section. The deliverable looked polished. The client came back with questions. We realized half of it was AI-generated and the specialist couldn't explain the methodology behind it."

**Mechanism:** Engagement → sub-consultant hired → sub-consultant uses AI tools → deliverable received → client challenges quality or appropriateness → managing director cannot explain provenance → firm accountability for sub-consultant output → no documentation of delegation scope or AI use.

**Market evidence pattern:** Sub-consultant AI use is invisible to the managing director and creates firm-level accountability for AI-generated content the firm did not produce and cannot defend.

---

### Signal 03 — Consulting Firms Cannot Define "AI Oversight" for Their Own Practice

**Signal:** When carrier attestation forms ask consulting firms to describe their "AI oversight mechanisms," firms produce language copied from AI governance frameworks without understanding what it means for their specific practice. The oversight language is aspirational — it describes what should exist, not what actually exists.

**Specific complaint:** "The carrier asked us to describe our AI oversight. We wrote something about regular reviews and documented procedures. I don't actually know what that means for how we work."

**Mechanism:** Carrier form asks for oversight description → firm produces framework language → carrier accepts form → client incident occurs → carrier requests evidence of oversight → oversight described on form cannot be demonstrated → attestation becomes evidence of inadequate governance.

**Market evidence pattern:** The attestation architecture assumes a documented oversight standard exists. Consulting firms are completing the form without producing the underlying documentation architecture the form assumes.

---

### Signal 04 — "Methodology Integrity" Questions Appearing in Client Challenges

**Signal:** Clients are beginning to challenge consulting recommendations not on the conclusion but on the methodology — specifically asking whether and how AI was used in producing the analysis. The challenge is not adversarial in tone; clients are asking genuine questions about how recommendations were derived. The consulting firm cannot answer with precision.

**Specific complaint:** "The client's CFO asked: 'How much of this analysis was AI-generated versus your team's judgment?' I didn't have a good answer."

**Mechanism:** Client receives recommendation → client asks about AI role in methodology → consulting firm cannot explain with precision → client confidence in recommendation undermined → relationship damaged → methodology integrity becomes the issue.

**Market evidence pattern:** The consulting firm's value proposition is based on the credibility of its methodology. If the methodology's AI component cannot be explained, the defensibility of the entire recommendation is undermined — regardless of whether the recommendation was correct.

---

### Signal 05 — Carrier Attestation Creates Personal Signature Exposure for Managing Directors

**Signal:** Geneva consulting firms' professional liability carriers are requiring managing directors to personally sign AI governance attestations. The personal signature creates personal accountability for representations about AI oversight that the firm cannot demonstrate it meets. The managing director is unaware of the personal exposure at signing.

**Specific complaint:** "The broker sent over a form. I signed it as the managing partner. I didn't realize I was personally attesting to something."

**Mechanism:** Carrier renewal → attestation form requires managing director signature → managing director signs without reviewing what representation is made → carrier requests evidence of oversight at renewal → firm cannot produce evidence → personal signature on attestation creates documented personal exposure.

**Market evidence pattern:** Carrier attestation architecture is designed to create documented personal accountability at the point of signature. The documentation gap is created at signing, not at the point of a client incident.

---

### Signal 06 — Geneva Consulting Firms Are Accumulating AI Documentation Debt Across Three Vectors Simultaneously

**Signal:** Geneva consulting firms are compounding documentation debt across three distinct vectors with no protocol for stopping the accumulation: (1) client engagement records without AI documentation, (2) sub-consultant delegation records without AI scope, and (3) carrier attestation records without oversight evidence. Each vector adds debt independently. The firm has no framework for addressing any of them.

**Specific complaint:** "Every engagement adds to a pile of undocumented AI use. I don't even know where to start."

**Mechanism:** Engagement completed → AI used with no documentation → retention period undefined → next engagement adds more → sub-consultant work adds more → carrier attestation adds more → compounding debt with no stop and no Geneva-defined resolution.

**Market evidence pattern:** Documentation debt is not a future problem — it is a present-tense active accumulation. The managing director cannot see the total exposure because no framework exists for measuring it.
