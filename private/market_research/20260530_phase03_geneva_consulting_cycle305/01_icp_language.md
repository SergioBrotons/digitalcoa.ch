# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting — C305 Phase 03

## ICP Definition
Small consulting boutique, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable. Core product is advice, analysis, and methodology defensibility. Concerned about client AI governance questionnaires, proposal methodology defensibility, carrier attestation, sub-consultant AI delegation, and personal exposure under nFADP.

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### On Client AI Governance Questionnaires

**What they say:**
- "Our client sent us a 40-question AI governance form. We had no idea how to answer most of it."
- "The questionnaire asks about AI oversight mechanisms. We don't even know what that means for a consulting practice."
- "We submitted a response and I have no confidence it was the right one."
- "The client is asking questions we don't have answers to. I signed something — I think."

**What they mean:** The client is requiring AI governance documentation as a condition of the engagement or RFP. The consulting firm has no framework for producing that documentation. They are guessing and documenting their guess in a formal client record.

---

### On Proposal Methodology Defensibility

**What they say:**
- "We use AI to support our analysis. But if a client challenges our recommendation, can we explain exactly what AI did?"
- "Our proposal methodology is our product. If AI use undermines the defensibility of that methodology, what are we actually selling?"
- "I don't know how to describe our methodology in a way that accounts for AI assistance."
- "What happens when a client asks: what exactly did the AI do in your analysis?"

**What they mean:** The consulting methodology is the core asset. AI integration creates a defensibility gap — the firm cannot explain the provenance of its recommendations with precision. The value proposition is based on human judgment, and AI assistance is not transparently integrated.

---

### On Carrier Attestation

**What they say:**
- "The carrier sent us an AI governance attestation form. I signed it because the broker said to."
- "I don't know what I attested to. I assume it's fine because everyone signs them."
- "The attestation asks about supervision of AI tools. We don't have a process for that."
- "I think we covered this in our AI policy, but I'm not sure what the carrier actually wants."

**What they mean:** The carrier attestation is treated as a formality. The managing director signs without understanding what representation they are making. The representation creates personal accountability for a standard the firm cannot demonstrate it meets.

---

### On Sub-Consultant AI Delegation

**What they say:**
- "We use specialists for specific work. What they do with AI is their business."
- "If a sub-consultant uses AI and something goes wrong, is that our liability?"
- "I have no visibility into what sub-consultants are using. I just review their final deliverables."
- "We retain sub-consultants for expertise. Asking about their AI tools feels like micromanagement."

**What they mean:** The delegation of work to sub-consultants creates an accountability gap. The managing director is personally accountable for sub-consultant work including AI use, but has no authorization framework, no scope definition, and no monitoring mechanism.

---

### On nFADP and Personal Liability

**What they say:**
- "I'm the managing partner. Am I personally exposed if there's an AI data issue?"
- "We processed some client data through an AI tool. I don't know where that data went."
- "Our corporate structure protects us, right?"
- "I've heard about nFADP. I don't know what it means for a consulting practice."

**What they mean:** Personal exposure under nFADP is not hypothetical — it is specific and active. The managing director does not know whether their current AI tool use creates Article 63 personal criminal exposure. They are uncertain whether corporate structure provides protection.

---

### On AI Tool Use in Client Work

**What they say:**
- "We use AI for research, drafting support, data analysis. It's faster."
- "I don't document which tool I used for which part of the project."
- "If I had to document every AI use, I would need to change my entire workflow."
- "The tools are integrated into how we work. Going back isn't an option."

**What they mean:** AI tools are embedded in the consulting workflow without documentation. The speed and efficiency benefit is real but unquantified against the documentation and liability cost. The firm is accumulating operational and legal debt simultaneously.

## Emotional State Summary

- **Primary:** Exposed without knowing exactly how. Accountable without understanding the standard.
- **Secondary:** Efficient users of AI who have not accounted for the documentation cost.
- **Tertiary:** Concerned about client trust, competitive positioning, and personal liability — in that order.
- **Trigger state:** Client AI governance questionnaire arrival. Carrier attestation renewal. Sub-consultant incident. Client challenge to methodology.

---

## What They Think They Need

- An AI policy document
- Training for associates
- A form for client AI governance questionnaires
- Guidance on which AI tools are "safe"

## What They Actually Need

- Evidence architecture for methodology defensibility
- Client questionnaire response framework producing defensible documentation
- Sub-consultant delegation framework with authorization and monitoring
- Personal liability structuring under nFADP Art. 63
- Carrier attestation evidence production, not form completion
