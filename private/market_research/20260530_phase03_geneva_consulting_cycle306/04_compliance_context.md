# Phase 03 Compliance Context — Geneva Consulting Boutique
## C306 Phase 03 (2026-05-30)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

### 1. nFADP — Individual Liability Under Article 52
- **Status:** Operative since September 1, 2023; FDPIC enforcement active
- **Key provision:** Article 52 — administrative liability for natural persons
- **Penalty:** Fine up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations
- **In scope:** Managing directors, partners, any natural person responsible for data processing
- **Boutique consulting relevance:** Managing partners are explicitly in personal scope. AI-assisted client work is data processing under nFADP Article 5(c) — FDPIC guidance May 8, 2025 confirmed AI-based data processing falls under Article 5(c).

### 2. Client AI Governance Questionnaires — Commercial Regulatory Proxy
- **Nature:** Not regulatory instruments — commercial due diligence
- **Origin:** Large corporate clients and institutional buyers responding to their own EU AI Act and nFADP obligations
- **Content:** Typically cover AI tool inventory, data processing practices, human oversight procedures, incident response, sub-consultant AI governance
- **Boutique consulting relevance:** The questionnaire functions as a supply-chain compliance gate. A consulting boutique that cannot respond may lose the client engagement. The questions mirror regulatory requirements (EU AI Act Article 14 human oversight, Article 16 GPAI documentation, nFADP Article 16 DPIA equivalents).

### 3. Carrier AI Attestation — Insurance Sector Requirements
- **Nature:** Commercial condition for professional liability policy renewal
- **Origin:** Swiss insurance carriers (property/casualty, professional liability, E&O)
- **Content:** AI tool inventory, data processing of client information, sub-consultant AI use, AI incident reporting
- **Boutique consulting relevance:** Attestation accuracy creates liability. Signing an inaccurate attestation may constitute misrepresentation to an insurer — with implications for coverage in the event of a claim.

### 4. EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Application to Consulting Firms
- **Date:** August 2, 2026 (~14 months; GPAI obligations already operative August 2, 2025)
- **Boutique consulting relevance (extraterritorial):**
  - If consulting firm uses EU-based AI providers (OpenAI EU, Microsoft EU, Google EU AI)
  - If consulting firm advises EU-based clients
  - If consulting firm places AI-enabled services on the EU market
- **GPAI obligations (already live):** Transparency about AI model use, copyright compliance, technical documentation
- **Specific risk:** Consulting firms don't typically know which AI providers are GPAI-compliant or what documentation their AI providers are required to maintain

### 5. Swiss Federal Council AI Consultation — End of 2026
- **Timeline:** Consultation draft expected end of 2026 (~7 months)
- **Nature:** Swiss domestic AI regulatory framework
- **Boutique consulting relevance:** Will likely create additional obligations for firms using AI in client service delivery. Timeline is roughly 7 months out — advisory planning window.

### 6. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation — Structural Accountability Gap
- **Nature:** No specific Swiss regulation governs AI delegation chains in consulting engagements
- **Problem:** Managing partner is accountable for deliverables; sub-consultant uses AI; no documentation of AI use in the chain
- **Current exposure:** Client questionnaires and carrier attestations ask about sub-consultant AI use — managing partner must answer without knowing what the sub-consultant used
- **Risk:** Deliverables incorporating AI-assisted sub-consultant work may not meet client methodology expectations or disclosure requirements

## Geneva/Switzerland Specific Considerations

### Bilingual Market (FR/EN)
- Geneva consulting boutiques operate in French and English
- Client questionnaires may arrive in either language
- Carrier attestations are typically in English or German
- Advisory service must be bilingual (FR/EN) to serve this market effectively

### Geneva Business Community Dynamics
- Geneva consulting community is close-knit; word-of-mouth amplifies first-mover advantage
- Managing partners know each other through industry associations, chambers of commerce, and professional networks
- Personal recommendation is the primary trust mechanism for boutique advisory services

### Professional Liability Insurance
- Swiss professional liability market is tightening on AI-related exposure
- Carriers are introducing AI attestation as a condition of renewal
- Non-response or inaccurate attestation may affect coverage validity

### Personal Asset Protection
- Swiss managing partners in small boutiques often have personal assets tied to the business
- nFADP Article 52 individual liability creates direct personal financial exposure
- Professional liability insurance may not cover nFADP administrative fines

## Key Compliance Insight
The Phase 03 consulting boutique faces a **layered accountability structure** with no single regulatory framework governing it:
- **Commercial layer:** Client AI governance questionnaires (supply-chain compliance)
- **Insurance layer:** Carrier AI attestation (policy condition)
- **Regulatory layer:** nFADP Article 52 (individual liability)
- **Extraterritorial layer:** EU AI Act (if EU nexus)
- **Incoming layer:** Swiss Federal Council AI framework (end of 2026)

The managing partner is accountable across all layers simultaneously — and no existing advisory service addresses this specific multi-layer accountability structure for a2–50 person boutique.
