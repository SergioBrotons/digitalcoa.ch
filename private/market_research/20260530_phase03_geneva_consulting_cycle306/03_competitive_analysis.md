## What's Missing: The Boutique Consulting AI Governance Advisory Gap

### What Exists
1. **Enterprise AI governance frameworks** — ISO42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act compliance for large organizations
2. **General compliance platforms** — OneTrust, TrustArc, VComply; enterprise SaaS tools
3. **Big Four advisory** — KPMG, EY, Deloitte, PwC; AI governance for large clients
4. **Legal firms** — AI law practices; regulatory advice; not consulting methodology advisory
5. **Technology vendors** — AI tools; compliance enablers; not advisory services

### What's Missing for Geneva Consulting Boutique (2–50 Employees)
1. **Client AI governance questionnaire response support** — helping consulting firms answer client questionnaires specifically
2. **Carrier AI attestation preparation** — preparing attestation responses, reviewing accuracy, managing corrections
3. **Proposal methodology defensibility review** — reviewing AI-assisted methodology for disclosure, traceability, quality
4. **Sub-consultant AI delegation governance** — structuring the accountability chain for AI use by sub-consultants
5. **Personal nFADP liability advisory for managing partners** — individual exposure assessment and coverage review
6. **Boutique-appropriate AI governance framework** — scaled to 2–50 person firm, actionable, not enterprise overhead

## Competitive Assessment

### Why No One Is Serving This Market
- **Scale mismatch:** Enterprise frameworks (ISO 42001, NIST) require resources beyond2–50 person boutique capacity
- **Pricing mismatch:** Big Four advisory fees are not viable for boutique consulting firms
- **Language mismatch:** Most AI governance content is English-language enterprise material; Geneva French/English bilingual market underserved
- **Structure mismatch:** Compliance platforms (OneTrust, TrustArc) require implementation teams; boutique firms need operator-assisted service, not software
- **Advisory gap:** Legal firms advise on law; they don't advise on methodology defensibility or sub-consultant AI delegation

### First-Mover Opportunity
No Geneva consulting boutique has entered the AI governance advisory space for their peers. The gap is confirmed across multiple prior consulting phases. The market is characterized by:
- Commercial gate pressure (client questionnaires, carrier attestation) arriving without warning
- Managing partners personally accountable with no dedicated advisory service
- No boutique-appropriate AI governance framework available at appropriate scale

### What Would Be Required to Serve This Market
- Bilingual EN/FR advisory capability
- Understanding of both regulatory (nFADP) and commercial (client questionnaire, carrier attestation) pressure points
- Ability to structure sub-consultant accountability chains
- Personal liability advisory for managing partners
- Proposal methodology defensibility review service
- Carrier attestation response preparation

## Key Competitive Insight
The competitive gap is not just in size — it's in **service type**. Enterprise AI governance is about compliance infrastructure. Boutique consulting AI governance is about **commercial defensibility**: responding to client questionnaires, preparing carrier attestations, defending proposal methodology, managing sub-consultant chains, and protecting personal liability. These are advisory services, not compliance platforms.
