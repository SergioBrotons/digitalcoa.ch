# The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework
### A 9-Section Framework for Geneva Small Consulting Firms

---

## Section 1: The Situation

The EU AI Act's GPAI transparency obligations have been active since August 2, 2025.

That means approximately 8 months have already elapsed since Geneva consulting firms with EU-nexus became subject to AI transparency requirements. If your firm uses AI tools for client engagements — research, analysis, presentations, deliverable creation — and you have EU-based clients or EU-directed output, you've had 8 months to comply.

Most small consulting firms haven't addressed this.

The Federal Council's "lean approach" means Switzerland won't impose domestic AI regulation before the end of 2026 at the earliest. That creates a window where Geneva consulting firms face EU AI Act obligations but no Swiss domestic AI law.

This framework is for the Geneva consulting firm that wants to understand: what are my AI governance obligations, what does the timeline look like, and what should I do now?

---

## Section 2: GPAI — The Obligation That's Already Active

**GPAI (General Purpose AI) transparency obligations under EU AI Act: ACTIVE since August 2, 2025.**

If your consulting firm:
- Has EU-based clients
- Produces deliverables intended for EU use
- Uses AI systems deployed on EU infrastructure
- Advises EU-based companies

Then GPAI transparency obligations apply to you NOW.

**GPAI transparency requirements:**
- Disclose to recipients when AI is used in your deliverables
- Publish summary of training data used
- Maintain documentation of AI system prompts and outputs

**8 months already elapsed = compliance gap if EU nexus**

## Section 3: The Dual Compliance Challenge

Geneva consulting firms face dual compliance requirements:

**Layer 1: EU AI Act (Active since Aug 2 2025 for GPAI; high-risk Aug 2 2026)**
- Applies extraterritorially to EU-nexus consulting
- Transparency obligations now; high-risk obligations in ~4.5 months

**Layer 2: Swiss nFADP (Active since Sep 1 2023)**
- AI transparency under Art. 5(c) applies to all Swiss firms
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing

**The gap:** No Geneva professional body has published AI guidance for consulting firms. You're navigating dual compliance without a professional framework.

## Section 4: The May 2026 External Pressure Event

The digitalswitzerland AI Action Plan "First Draft" is expected in May 2026 (~2 months from March 30).

This is a separate process from the Federal Council formal approach (Feb 12 2025). The digitalswitzerland process is a multi-stakeholder initiative involving BAKOM and Accenture — it's a private-sector consultation document, not a government regulation.

But it will generate press coverage, industry discussion, and client questions.

**What your clients will ask after May 2026:** "Does this affect our engagement?"

**What you should be able to answer:** Yes, here's how — and here is what we're doing about it.

## Section 6: Advisory Window — Why Now

**Window:** ~9 months to August 2, 2026 (EU AI Act high-risk obligations)

The Federal Council's "lean approach" means Switzerland isn't introducing domestic AI law before end-2026 at the earliest. During this window:
- No Swiss domestic AI law applies (nFADP applies; horizontal AI law doesn't)
- EU AI Act GPAI obligations ARE active for EU-nexus firms
- The Federal Council First Draft (May 2026) will create market awareness
- The high-risk obligations (Aug 2 2026) will create client urgency

**What happens at Aug 2 2026:** Consulting firms using AI in high-risk contexts (hiring decisions, financial advice, essential services access) face full EU AI Act compliance requirements. For consulting, this means: if you're advising clients on hiring decisions, financial matters, or access to services, high-risk obligations may apply.

## Section 8: A Practical 5-Step Path

**Step 1 — EU AI Act Nexus Assessment (Week 1)**
Determine whether your firm has EU-nexus clients and EU-directed deliverables. If yes: GPAI obligations are active now.

**Step 2 — AI Tool Inventory (Week 1-2)**
List every AI tool used in client engagements. Document: what data enters, what processing occurs, what output is produced.

**Step 3 — GPAI Transparency Implementation (Week 2-3)**
Implement AI disclosure standards in engagement letters and deliverables. Disclose AI use to clients.

**Step 4 — nFADP DPIA Lite (Week 3-4)**
Conduct simplified DPIA for your primary AI tools. Address Art. 5(c) compliance.

**Step 5 — Monitoring (Ongoing)**
Set calendar reminders for May 2026 (First Draft), Aug 2 2026 (high-risk activation), end-2026 (Federal Council formal legislation).
