## Cycle 19 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Small Consulting Firms

## ICP: The Geneva Small Consulting Firm

### Demographics
- **Firm size:** 2–15 consultants (solo practitioner to boutique)
- **Location:** Geneva canton; expanding to Vaud, Zurich as secondary
- **Sub-segments:** Management consulting, IT/Tech consulting, HR consulting, Strategy advisory, M&A advisory
- **Revenue context:** CHF 300K–3M annual; price-sensitive; project-based
- **Technology posture:** High AI adoption — AI for research, presentation creation, client analysis, project delivery
- **Decision-maker:** Managing Partner or founder (owns all decisions including compliance)

### Pain Points
1. **GPAI already operative:** EU AI Act GPAI transparency obligations ACTIVE since August 2, 2025 (~8 months)
2. **Dual compliance:** EU AI Act + nFADP simultaneously — no guidance from Geneva professional bodies
3. **May 2026 external pressure:** Federal Council First Draft (digitalswitzerland process) ~2 months away
4. **40% AI skills gap:** PwC 2024 data — 40% of Geneva SMEs lack AI skills; creates both opportunity and risk
5. **ANBLES/professional body silence:** No Geneva consulting-specific AI guidance published

### Timing Assessment (as of March 30, 2026)
- **GPAI transparency:** ACTIVE since Aug 2, 2025 (~8 months already)
- **Swissdamed spillover:** ~13 WEEKS
- **Federal Council First Draft (digitalswitzerland):** ~2 MONTHS
- **EU AI Act high-risk:** Aug 2 2026 ~4.5 MONTHS

### Competitive Landscape
No boutique competitor for small consulting firm AI governance (9th+ confirmation)

### Addressable Market
- Geneva: ~400-600 small consulting firms
- Switzerland-wide: ~1,500-2,500 small consulting firms
