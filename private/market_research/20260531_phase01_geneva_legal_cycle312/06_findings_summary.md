# Findings Summary: Geneva Legal — C312 Phase 01

## 6 NET-NEW Findings: Distinct from C214–C311

---

### Finding 01 — Carrier Form Architecture Creates Documented Standard Without Notification

**The finding:** The carrier form is architecture — it creates a documented standard at the moment of signing. The managing partner signs believing they are acknowledging something, while actually committing to a standard they cannot demonstrate they meet. The form changed question types (from firm-level binary attestation to matter-level open-form descriptive) without market-wide notification. The documentation debt accumulated silently through tool adoption without documentation events.

**Mechanism:** Carrier attestation → personal signature → "adequate supervision" representation → no documentation created → carrier asks for evidence at renewal → firm cannot produce documentation → attestation becomes evidence of inadequate governance.

**Implication:** The service is not "help me complete the form." The service is "help me produce the evidence the form architecture assumes I have." This distinction is critical — the form was never asking for acknowledgment, it was asking for evidence.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The form wasn't asking you to acknowledge something. It was asking you to commit to a standard. And now the carrier wants to see if you can prove you meet it."

### Finding 02 — Post-Attestation Evidence Reconstruction Is the Primary Market Need

**The finding:** The majority of the market is in post-attestation mode — they already signed, they already have personal exposure, they need evidence now. Retroactive reconstruction is the primary need. Linear forward-only solutions (policies, frameworks) are structurally insufficient — they address new AI use only, leaving 12–24 months of undocumented AI use unaddressed. Reconstructed records may carry no legal weight — firms are discovering this at the worst moment.

**Mechanism:** AI adopted 12–24 months ago → no documentation created at adoption → documentation debt accumulated silently → linear forward-only solution addresses only new AI use → retroactive gap remains → no dedicated reconstruction service exists.

**Implication:** Retroactive reconstruction is a discrete, high-value service. It requires a specific methodology developed through cycles of research and client work. The methodology improves with each reconstruction.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You've been using AI for months or years without documenting it. I can reconstruct what happened and build forward from where you are — but reconstruction has limits. Let me show you what evidence you can actually produce."

### Finding 03 — Unauthorized Delegation Is Active and Undocumented

**The finding:** Associates are using personal AI tools (ChatGPT on personal devices) on firm matters without documented authorization. The managing partner didn't authorize it. They didn't know about it. They are personally liable for it. The authorization chain was never documented. This is not a theoretical risk — it is an active exposure appearing on carrier forms.

**Mechanism:** Associate uses personal AI tool on firm matter → managing partner unaware → no authorization documented → carrier asks about supervision of AI usage in practice → managing partner personally accountable for AI use they didn't know existed → attestation creates exposure rather than protection.

**Implication:** An authorization chain documentation system is a discrete service. It documents who used what AI tools, for which matters, with what review — not to prevent use retroactively, but to produce the evidence of what happened.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your associates are using AI tools you didn't authorize. You're personally liable for supervising AI use in your practice — including AI use you don't know about. I can help you document what happened and build a system going forward."

### Finding 04 — OaF Survey Creates Two-Tier Visibility

**The finding:** The OaF (Omnibus Abstimmung für die Anwälte / survey for lawyers) participation creates two-tier visibility. When guidance arrives, the bar will know who was prepared and who wasn't. The participation gap is creating a two-tier market — firms that engaged early vs. firms that waited. Non-participation is itself a signal.

**Mechanism:** OaF survey distributed → participation optional → early adopters visible to bar → guidance arrives → non-participating firms face implicit scrutiny → two-tier market forms.

**Implication:** OaF participation guidance is a discrete service. It positions the firm as prepared, creates contemporaneous evidence of engagement, and avoids the two-tier visibility problem.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The OaF survey creates a record. When guidance arrives, the bar will know who engaged and who waited. Participation is itself a signal."

---

### Finding 05 — Vendor Compliance Assumption Creates Hidden Exposure

**The finding:** The vendor said they were compliant. The managing partner assumed the vendor's compliance covered their obligations. It doesn't. The vendor's terms of service may grant data access that creates Article 321 CC privilege waiver. The vendor's DPA protects the vendor, not the client. The managing partner is personally accountable for professional secrecy obligations they didn't know existed.

**Mechanism:** Vendor claims compliance → managing partner assumes vendor compliance covers their obligations → data processed on vendor servers → vendor terms of service accepted without review → Article 321 CC exposure created → no documentation of vendor contract analysis → if challenged, cannot demonstrate compliance.

**Implication:** A systematic Article 321 vendor contract review is a discrete service. The review produces documentation of: where data goes, who can access it, what obligations the vendor has, whether professional secrecy is protected.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your AI vendor processes data on their servers. Their terms of service — which you accepted — may conflict with Article 321. Most firms haven't looked. That's your exposure."

### Finding 06 — "Adequate" Is Undefined and Universally Paralyzing

**The finding:** No Geneva-specific, size-specific, operational definition of "adequate AI governance" exists for 2–5 attorney law firms. The carrier asks for "adequate" without defining it. The FSA has acknowledged the obligation without defining it. The managing partner cannot determine whether they meet a standard that has never been defined. This paralysis prevents action even when the need is clear.

**Mechanism:** Carrier asks for adequate → FSA acknowledges obligation → no definition exists → managing partner operates in uncertainty → if challenged, no standard to defend against → first to define controls the standard.

**Implication:** Defining "adequate" for small Geneva firms is the long-term brand moat. The first to publish a specific, operational definition that the carrier form architecture assumes will become the reference standard.

**Ready-to-use language:** "No one has defined what 'adequate' means for a two-to-five attorney Geneva firm. The carrier assumes it exists. It doesn't. I can define it for you — and that's how you become the standard."
