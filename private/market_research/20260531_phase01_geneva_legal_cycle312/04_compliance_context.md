# 04 — Compliance Context: Switzerland / Geneva Regulatory Environment

**Phase:** C312 Phase 01 | **ICP:** Geneva Boutique, 2–5 Attorneys | **Date:** 2026-05-31
**Context:** Build on C311 — OaF guidance still pending; nDSG transition deadline passed September 2025; carrier attestation now primary regulatory instrument.

---

## Switzerland's Regulatory Architecture for AI in Professional Services

### Layer 1: Federal AI Strategy — Still No AI-Specific Legislation

Switzerland continues to operate without a standalone AI law equivalent to the EU AI Act. The federal approach remains:
- Principled rather than prescriptive
- Ecosystem-oriented rather than compliance-oriented  
- Focused on positioning Switzerland as an AI-friendly jurisdiction

**The implication for Geneva law firms:** The obligation does not come from federal AI legislation — it comes from carriers and bar associations. This has not changed. The regulatory gap remains, but the practical obligations are being set by private actors (carriers) rather than public ones (government).

---

### Layer 2: The OaF (Ordre des Avocats de Genève) — Still No Guidance, But the Survey Data Is Being Used

**Current state (Q2 2026):**
- No formal AI governance guidance has been issued
- The mid-2025 voluntary AI usage survey results are being used internally to shape future guidance
- No mandatory disclosure requirements
- No standards for AI use in client matters
- No ethics opinions addressing AI-assisted legal work

**The market interpretation (C312 NET-NEW):** The OaF's continued silence is being interpreted by some firms as "nothing is required." This interpretation is becoming harder to sustain as carrier renewal forms become more specific. A firm waiting for OaF guidance while their carrier's form evolves independently is accepting a growing documentation gap.

**The data leverage opportunity:** The firms that participated in the OaF survey have already demonstrated awareness. If guidance eventually comes, the OaF will know who engaged and who did not. This is not a formal compliance advantage — but it is a visibility advantage that could matter in an audit or complaint scenario.

---

### Layer 3: Carrier Attestation — The de Facto Regulatory Instrument (Now Acute)

**Current state (Q2 2026):**
- AI-related questions are now standard on renewal forms for professional liability coverage
- Question type has shifted from binary to open-form descriptive across multiple carriers
- Carriers are using attestation responses to underwrite risk and inform premium decisions
- Carrier AI questions are now sufficiently specific to expose the documentation gap in real time

**The renewal confrontation (C312 NET-NEW):** The carrier attestation has gone from being a background requirement to being the primary confrontation point. A firm that signed a binary attestation in 2023–2024 and is now encountering the open-form renewal question is experiencing the gap in real time. The carrier is asking the question that exposes the documentation failure.

**Carrier fragmentation continues:** Each carrier uses different AI attestation language. No harmonized standard. A firm that completed one carrier's attestation may not satisfy another's requirements. This creates confusion but also creates an opportunity: a fixed-fee carrier form review, specific to the firm's carrier, produces immediate value regardless of which carrier they use.

---

### Layer 4: Attorney-Client Privilege in the AI Context — Still No Geneva Guidance

**The problem persists (C312 NET-NEW):** The privilege questions arising from AI tool usage in legal matters remain completely unaddressed by the Geneva bar. The specific questions that have no guidance:

1. **Data transmission to third-party servers:** When an attorney uses ChatGPT or Claude to assist with a matter, client data is transmitted to servers in the US. Is this a breach of privilege? No Geneva guidance exists.

2. **Model training and data retention:** Does using an AI tool that retains inputs for model training constitute a waiver of privilege? No guidance.

3. **Work product protection for AI-generated drafts:** If an AI tool generates a draft memorandum or brief, is that draft covered by work product protection? No Geneva guidance.

4. **Documentation requirement for privilege assertion:** Can the firm assert privilege over work they cannot describe — specifically, work produced with AI tools they have not documented? No guidance.

**The liability accumulation:** The firm that uses AI without a documented protocol is accumulating privilege exposure alongside carrier attestation exposure. These are separate liability vectors with the same root cause — no documentation of AI use. A malpractice claim involving AI-assisted work would trigger both simultaneously.

---

### Layer 5: nDSG Transition Deadline — September 1, 2025 Has Passed

The revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nDSG) transition period ended on September 1, 2025.

**The current compliance state of Geneva boutiques:**
- Most boutiques do not have data processing agreements with AI vendors
- Most boutiques have not documented the legal basis for AI processing of client data
- Most boutiques have not conducted a DPIA for AI processing of client data
- The transition period that allowed this to go unaddressed has ended

**The convergence with carrier attestation (C312 NET-NEW):** The documentation gap for nDSG compliance is identical to the documentation gap for carrier attestation compliance. A firm that documents AI use — what tools, on which matters, with what legal basis, with what review — addresses both requirements simultaneously. The two frameworks have the same core requirement: know what AI you are using, document it, and show your review process.

**The business case for addressing both together:** Solving for both nDSG and carrier attestation with one documentation system creates a more compelling investment case for the managing partner who has been reluctant to act. "You're not just satisfying the carrier — you're also closing the nDSG exposure that's been open since September 2025."

### Layer 6: The Personal Liability Stack — Now More Acute

The managing partner who signed the carrier attestation in their own name has the following liability vectors (C312 update):

| Vector | Source | Exposure Type | Status at C312 |
|--------|--------|---------------|----------------|
| Carrier attestation | Signed form | Personal liability on the form | Being experienced at renewal |
| Professional liability insurance | Policy terms | Coverage for AI-related claims | Renewal confrontation |
| OaF professional rules | Future bar guidance | Disciplinary exposure if guidance violated | Still pending, but accumulating |
| nDSG compliance | Data processing | Fines, breach notification, civil liability | Deadline passed September 2025 |
| Attorney-client privilege | 321 StGB / cantonal rules | Waiver, breach, malpractice | Still no guidance |

**The convergence (C312 NET-NEW):** At Q2 2026, the managing partner who signed the attestation in 2024 and has done nothing since is facing:
1. The carrier renewal with the new form question they cannot answer
2. nDSG non-compliance that has been running since September 2025
3. No OaF guidance, but guidance that is now more likely than it was in 2024
4. A privilege exposure they cannot describe

The documentation system is the common denominator across all of these. Closing the documentation gap is not just a carrier attestation requirement — it is the single action that reduces exposure across all four liability vectors simultaneously.

## The Compliance Gap: What Exists vs. What the ICP Needs

**What exists:**
- Carrier attestation as primary practical obligation (now acute)
- nDSG as data protection law (deadline passed)
- OaF survey data being used (still no guidance)
- EU AI Act (not applicable in Switzerland, but influential on carrier behavior)

**What the ICP needs:**
- A definition of "adequate" for a 2–5 attorney Geneva boutique
- A documentation system that satisfies both carrier attestation and nDSG requirements
- A retroactive reconstruction pathway for firms already exposed
- Clarity on privilege implications of AI tool usage (no guidance exists, but the question must be addressed)
- Geneva-specific pricing and language for a solution designed for small firms

**The gap:** The regulatory environment has not provided clarity — but it has created urgency through the convergence of the carrier renewal cycle and the nDSG deadline. The Geneva boutique that has been waiting for guidance is now further behind than they were at C311, because two deadlines have passed while they waited.

---

## NET-NEW Compliance Findings for C312

1. **OaF guidance vacuum is now a two-year-old problem:** Firms have been waiting for OaF guidance since mid-2025. Carriers have not waited. The documentation gap is growing, not shrinking, while the market waits.
2. **nDSG deadline has passed — convergence now acute:** The September 2025 nDSG deadline means the documentation gap is no longer theoretical. Firms are now out of compliance and exposed. The carrier attestation and nDSG share the same core documentation requirement — one system satisfies both.
3. **Privilege questions still have zero Geneva guidance:** The three core privilege questions (data transmission, model training, work product) remain completely unaddressed. The managing partner using AI without understanding the privilege implications is accumulating exposure on a vector that has no defined standard.
4. **Carrier renewal is now the confrontation moment:** C311 anticipated the new form. C312 is experiencing it. The renewal conversation is where the documentation gap becomes concrete, present, and unavoidable.
5. **Broker reassurance is surfacing as a liability:** The broker who told the managing partner they were "good" at signing is now creating a false confidence problem at renewal. The broker's credibility was used as authority, but the broker was not evaluating documentation depth.
6. **The documentation system is the common denominator across all liability vectors:** Carrier attestation, nDSG, OaF future guidance, privilege — all of these have the same root cause (no documentation of AI use) and the same solution (a documentation system proportionate to a small Geneva boutique).
