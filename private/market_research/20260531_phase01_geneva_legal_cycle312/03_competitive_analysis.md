# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal — C312 Phase 01

## What Exists, What's Missing

---

### Current Competitive Landscape

**What Exists:**
- FSA (bar association) guidance — principles-based, not operational
- Carrier attestation forms — architecture without methodology
- Generic AI governance frameworks — designed for enterprises, not boutique firms
- Legal tech vendors — selling tools, not evidence systems
- Compliance consultants — generic GDPR, not Geneva professional context
- Boutique AI governance firms — language proximity, wrong pricing and delivery model

**What's Missing (for 2–5 Attorney Geneva Firms):**
- Pre-signing carrier form review methodology
- Geneva-specific carrier attestation preparation
- Article 321 CC vendor contract review methodology
- Authorization chain documentation system
- Retroactive reconstruction methodology
- "Adequate" definition for small Geneva firms
- Junior associate AI surface documentation
- OaF participation guidance

---

### Six White Spaces

**1. Pre-Signing Carrier Form Review**
No competitor intercepts before the managing partner signs. Pre-signing review prevents the gap from being created. Post-signing remediation is incomplete. Pre-signing requires regulatory depth + boutique calibration — a combination no current competitor has. This is the highest-value, structurally unoccupied service point.

**2. Geneva Localization of AI Governance**
Generic frameworks are not Geneva-calibrated. The FSA has stated obligations without providing operational definitions. The carrier form is the de facto Geneva standard. No competitor reads it as such. The market needs Geneva-specific AI governance methodology built around what the carrier actually asks for.

**3. Junior Associate AI Surface Documentation**
Personal device ChatGPT generating firm exposure with zero managing partner visibility. No competitor addresses this specific surface. The managing partner needs a system for documenting AI use they didn't authorize — not preventing it retroactively, but producing the evidence of what happened.

**4. Retroactive Reconstruction Methodology**
The majority market need (12–24 months of undocumented AI use) has no dedicated service. Retroactive reconstruction requires a specific methodology developed through cycles of research and client work. It is not a policy document. It is an operational capability. The methodology improves with each reconstruction.

**5. Broker Referral Program**
No competitor has built a broker referral program for AI governance services. Insurance brokers see the problem first and must refer. They have no specific naming to give. A named broker relationship is the highest-converting referral channel — and no competitor has built it.

**6. "Adequate" Definition for Small Geneva Firms**
First to define becomes the authority. A specific, operational definition of adequate AI governance for 2–5 attorney Geneva firms. The standard the carrier form assumes exists. The first actor to publish this definition earns permanent category ownership.

---

### The Gap

The gap is not information. The gap is evidence production. The market offers frameworks. What no one offers is the documentation system that produces the specific records carrier form architecture requires. The first to build that system for Geneva boutique firms becomes the authority.

The market has moved to "I already signed. Now what?" — the professional who already certified, already has personal exposure, needs evidence now. No competitor addresses this specific moment. Every competitor offers what they can sell. Sergio offers what the carrier actually wants to see.

---

### Competitive Vulnerabilities

**Boutique AI Governance Firms:** Language proximity but wrong pricing model and wrong SMB delivery model. Still selling frameworks, not evidence. Not a near-term threat.

**Mid-Tier Accounting Firms:** Existing SMB relationships, one hire away from AI governance capability. The most credible near-term displacement risk. Build broker referral channel before they can respond.

**Large Consulting Firms:** Enterprise-oriented culture, pricing, and delivery model. Structurally misaligned with boutique SMB needs. Low near-term risk.

**Legal Tech Vendors:** Vendor interests and client interests are structurally opposed. Vendor DPA protects the vendor, not the client. Not a competitive threat but a positioning opportunity.
