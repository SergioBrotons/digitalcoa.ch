# Market Signals: Geneva Legal — C312 Phase 01

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

---

### Primary Market Signals

**1. Carrier Form Architecture Creates Documented Standard Without Notification**
The carrier form is architecture — it creates a documented standard at the moment of signing. The managing partner signs believing they are acknowledging something, while actually committing to a standard they cannot demonstrate they meet. The form changed question types (from firm-level binary to matter-level open-form descriptive) without market-wide notification. The documentation debt accumulated silently through tool adoption without documentation events.

**2. Post-Attestation Evidence Reconstruction Is the Primary Market Need**
The majority of the market is in post-attestation mode — they already signed, they already have personal exposure, they need evidence now. Retroactive reconstruction is the primary need. Linear forward-only solutions (policies, frameworks) are structurally insufficient — they address new AI use only, leaving 12–24 months of undocumented AI use unaddressed. Reconstructed records may carry no legal weight — firms are discovering this at the worst moment.

**3. Unauthorized Delegation Is Active and Undocumented**
Associates are using personal AI tools (ChatGPT on personal devices) on firm matters without documented authorization. The managing partner didn't authorize it. They didn't know about it. They are personally liable for it. The authorization chain was never documented. This is not a theoretical risk — it is an active exposure appearing on carrier forms.

**4. OaF Survey Creates Two-Tier Visibility**
The OaF (Omnibus Abstimmung für die Anwälte / survey for lawyers) participation creates two-tier visibility. When guidance arrives, the bar will know who was prepared and who wasn't. The participation gap is creating a two-tier market — firms that engaged early vs. firms that waited.

**5. Vendor Compliance Assumption Creates Hidden Exposure**
The vendor said they were compliant. The managing partner assumed the vendor's compliance covered their obligations. It doesn't. The vendor's terms of service may grant data access that creates Article 321 CC privilege waiver. The vendor's DPA protects the vendor, not the client. The managing partner is personally accountable for professional secrecy obligations they didn't know existed.

**6. "Adequate" Is Undefined and Universally Paralyzing**
No one has defined what adequate means for a two-to-five attorney Geneva firm. The carrier asks for adequate. The bar acknowledges the obligation. No Geneva-specific, size-specific, operational definition exists. The managing partner cannot determine whether they meet a standard that has never been defined. This paralysis prevents action even when the need is clear.

---

## Failure Modes

**Attestation audit failure:** Carrier asks for evidence at next renewal. Firm cannot produce documentation of supervision process, tool usage, or review decisions. Attestation becomes evidence of inadequate governance.

**Article 321 failure:** Data processed through AI vendor servers in violation of professional secrecy obligations. No documentation of vendor contract review against Article 321. Exposure is active and undocumented.

**Unauthorized delegation liability:** Associate uses personal AI tool on firm matter without authorization. Client matter details enter AI system outside firm governance. Managing partner personally liable for unauthorized delegation.

**OaF two-tier exposure:** Survey reveals firms that engaged vs. firms that waited. Bar guidance arrives with implicit knowledge of who was prepared. Non-participating firms face additional scrutiny.

**Retroactive reconstruction failure:** Attempting to reconstruct documentation after the fact. Records carry no legal weight. Carrier identifies reconstructed records as non-contemporaneous. Gap widens at the moment of need.

---

## The Structural Problem

The legal ICP has a specific structural problem: the carrier form created documented standards at the moment of signing, but the evidence to support those standards was never created. The gap accumulated silently through tool adoption without documentation events. Now the carrier form architecture makes the gap visible — and the managing partner is personally accountable for evidence they don't have.

The problem is not knowledge. The problem is documentation infrastructure. The managing partner knows they need records. They don't have a system for producing them. Every month the debt compounds. Every new AI tool increments the documentation requirement for all prior undocumented months.

The entry service is not "help me understand the problem." The entry service is "help me produce the evidence the carrier actually wants to see."
