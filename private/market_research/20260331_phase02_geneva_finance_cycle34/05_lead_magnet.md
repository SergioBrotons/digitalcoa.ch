# Phase 02 Cycle 34 — Lead Magnet: "The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist"

## Positioning

**Title:** The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist

**Subtitle:** CARF Is Already Live. CRS 2.0 Is Already Live. Is Your AI Governance?

**Target ICP:** Managing directors, partners, and compliance officers at Geneva-based boutique wealth management firms, independent asset managers, and family offices (2–20 employees)

**Core tension to resolve:**  
76% Geneva FS AI adoption = HIGHEST sector. But formal AI governance policies lag far behind individual use. CARF and CRS 2.0 are already operative. FATF evaluation is under way. The window for first-mover positioning is closing.

**Lead magnet format:** Checklist (digital downloadable / web-accessible)

---

## Primary Entry Point: Shadow AI Audit (Decision 0)

Frame the checklist around:  
*"Before you can govern AI, you need to know which AI your advisors are already using — with or without your knowledge."*

**Shadow AI Audit (Decision 0):**
- [ ] Identify all AI tools currently used by advisors and staff
- [ ] Document which tools are firm-approved vs. individual use only
- [ ] Assess CARF/CRS exposure from current AI usage
- [ ] Map data flows: which AI accesses client financial data?

---

## Section 1: CARF and CRS 2.0 — Live Obligations

**Already operative since Jan 1, 2026. Are you compliant?**

- [ ] CARF bilateral reporting obligations identified and mapped
- [ ] CRS 2.0 schema compliance confirmed for cross-border client reporting
- [ ] Crypto-asset holding clients documented for CARF scope
- [ ] Data exchange documentation prepared for tax authorities
- [ ] Internal controls updated for CARF/CRS automated exchange

**Urgency framing:**  
*"CARF and CRS 2.0 are not upcoming. They are already operative. Any boutique WM firm with cross-border clients is already in scope."*

---

## Section 2: FINMA Circular 08/2024 — AI Governance Baseline

**Operative since August 2024 (~8 months). Are you aligned?**

- [ ] AI systems classified by risk level (low / medium / high)
- [ ] Client-facing AI decisions documented and disclosed
- [ ] Human oversight mechanisms confirmed for AI-assisted decisions
- [ ] AI vendor due diligence completed (data residency, Swiss Code of Obligations compatibility)
- [ ] Advisor AI usage policies documented and communicated

---

## Section 3: FATF Evaluation — External Pressure Amplifier

**Switzerland under FATF evaluation 2026–2027/2028. Geneva FS in scope.**

- [ ] AMLA compliance gap assessment completed
- [ ] LETA Transparency Register 30-day discrepancy reporting workflow established
- [ ] Advisor scope expansion implications reviewed (SRO membership triggers)
- [ ] CARF/CRS/AMLA integrated compliance framework documented
- [ ] FATF examination readiness prepared

**Urgency framing:**  
*"FATF evaluation transforms domestic compliance into an international event. Geneva's reputation as a compliant financial center is at stake — and your firm's compliance status will be visible."*

---

## Section 4: FADP / nFADP — AI Data Protection

**Operative since September 2023 (~2.5 years). Are your AI tools FADP-compliant?**

- [ ] Automated decision-making disclosure process in place (Art. 5(c))
- [ ] DPIA completed for high-risk AI (client scoring, risk assessment, profiling)
- [ ] Privacy by Design assessment completed for AI procurement decisions
- [ ] FDPIC reporting channel established (in case of AI-related data incident)
- [ ] Purpose + functionality + data sources documentation for all AI deployments

**⭐ Phase 01 Cycle 34 cross-phase integration:**  
43% of law firms have NO formal AI policy (Wolters Kluwer March 2026) → same pattern likely applies to WM firms → FDPIC enforcement: legal absent = WM next sector timing signal

---

## Section 5: Shadow AI Audit — Decision 0

**"You cannot govern what you do not know."**

- [ ] Complete inventory of all AI tools used across the firm
- [ ] Differentiate firm-authorized vs. individual advisor use
- [ ] Identify which AI tools access or process client financial data
- [ ] Assess AMLA/CARF/CRS implications of shadow AI usage
- [ ] Document remediation plan for unauthorized AI tool usage

**Urgency framing:**  
*"The 43% of firms with no formal AI policy are not necessarily non-users of AI. They are likely individual users of AI without firm governance. The gap between individual AI use and formal governance is where regulatory risk lives."*

---

## Section 6: WM AI 2026 — Agentic AI Inflection Point

**The transition from generative to agentic AI changes the risk profile.**

- [ ] Agentic AI usage identified (autonomous task execution: portfolio rebalancing, reporting, client communication)
- [ ] CARF/CRS implications assessed for agentic AI data processing
- [ ] Human-override mechanisms confirmed for agentic AI decisions
- [ ] Agentic AI vendor due diligence completed (autonomous decision logs, audit trails)
- [ ] Internal policy updated to cover agentic AI usage

**Urgency framing:**  
*"Agentic AI can execute transactions autonomously. This changes CARF/CRS exposure fundamentally — automated decisions create automated data exchange obligations."*

---

## Section 7: 3-Layer Compliance Stack — Integration Mapping

**FADP + FINMA + AMLA/CARF/CRS = integrated WM AI governance**

- [ ] FADP layer: Art. 5(c), DPIA, Privacy by Design (all operative)
- [ ] FINMA layer: Circ. 08/2024, SRO obligations, advisor scope (operativ + expanding)
- [ ] AMLA layer: CARF live, CRS 2.0 live, LETA H2 2026, FATF evaluation 2026-2027/2028
- [ ] Integration point: Shadow AI audit → compliance gap → remediation roadmap
- [ ] Governance committee established (even if single managing director)
