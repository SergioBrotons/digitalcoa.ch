## Cycle 34 | 2026-03-31

## Shared Pain Points (All 6 Verticals)

### 1. The Universal Governance Gap
**Finding:** A 46-point gap between individual AI adoption (69%) and formal governance (23%) was confirmed across the market (Cycles 32–33). This gap is consistent across all 6 verticals:

| Vertical | Individual AI Use | Formal Policy | Gap Indicator |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal (Phase 01) | ~69% | ~23% | 46-point gap confirmed |
| WM / Finance (Phase 02) | 76% | ~23% | 53-point gap |
| Consulting (Phase 03) | 63% | ~23% | 40-point gap |
| Healthcare (Phase 04) | ~65% | ~23% | 42-point gap |
| Real Estate (Phase 06) | 75% | ~23% | 52-point gap |

**Interpretation:** Individual AI adoption has outpaced institutional governance by a wide margin in every vertical. The 43% with "no policy and no plans" (Wolters Kluwer, March 2026) represents the structural缺口 — a market that has adopted AI informally but not institutionally.

---

### 2. The 43% "No Policy + No Plans" Finding
**Source:** Wolters Kluwer, March 2026 (confirmed Phase 01 Cycle 34)

This is the single most important market finding of Cycle 34. It reframes the opportunity:

- **Only 9%** of firms have enforced formal AI policy
- **43%** have no policy AND no plans to create one
- **The remaining 48%** have either informal guidelines or policy-in-progress

**Cross-vertical implications:**
- The 43% represents a "not yet aware" or "not yet motivated" segment, not a "not addressable" segment
- Proximity to regulatory deadlines (Swissdamed Jul 1, Andermatt May 26) is the primary motivator
- The 9% "enforced policy" segment is too small to be the primary ICP — the mass market is under-governed

---

### 3. AI Error / Hallucination Fear (57%)
**Source:** Wolters Kluwer, March 2026 — 57% of professionals cite AI errors and hallucinations as the top threat.

Cross-vertical amplification:
- **Legal:** Erroneous legal research citations, brief errors, privilege breaches
- **WM:** Incorrect financial projections, unsuitable recommendations
- **Consulting:** Flawed analysis presented as expert opinion
- **Healthcare:** Clinical decision errors, misdiagnosis risk, medication interactions
- **Real Estate:** Valuation errors, contract misrepresentations

**The fear of AI errors is the hook.** Every vertical shares it. It creates urgency without requiring firms to understand the regulatory framework first.

---

### 4. Regulatory Deadline Anxiety (4-Deadline Cluster)
Four concurrent deadlines create a "perfect storm" of urgency that crosses all verticals:

| Deadline | Date | Verticals Most Affected | Weeks Away |
|---|---|---|---|
| Andermatt May 26 (Swiss AI Summit) | 2026-05-26 | All (awareness) | ~8 |
| **Swissdamed Jul 1** | 2026-07-01 | **Healthcare #1**, Legal, WM | **~13** |
| EU AI Act Aug 2 | 2026-08-02 | All (indirect) | ~19 |
| Federal Council end-2026 | 2026-12-31 | All | ~39 |

**Swissdamed is the forcing function.** It is the concrete, near-term, legally enforceable deadline that creates genuine urgency — especially for healthcare (Phase 04), where patient data governance becomes a statutory obligation.

## What the Market Is NOT
- **Not waiting for regulation** — 90%+ are already using AI individually
- **Not resistant to AI** — the governance gap is about accountability, not adoption
- **Not a single-vertical problem** — the gap is systemic across professional services
- **Not motivated by abstract AI ethics** — they are motivated by concrete risk (errors, liability, fines)
