# Phase 05 — Cross-Vertical Synthesis: Competitive Landscape
## Cycle 34 | 2026-03-31

---

## Who Is Currently Serving This Market (Poorly or Not At All)

### 1. Large Technology Vendors (OpenAI, Microsoft, Google)
- **Positioning:** AI adoption, not AI governance
- **Gap:** They sell AI tools; they do not address institutional accountability, regulatory compliance, or policy frameworks
- **Relevance to this market:** Low — the governance gap is a professional services problem, not a technology problem

### 2. Traditional Management Consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Oliver Wyman)
- **Positioning:** Enterprise-level AI strategy, board-level AI governance
- **Gap:** Minimum engagement sizes far above the SMB professional services firm; generic frameworks not adapted to Swiss legal/regulatory context
- **Relevance to this market:** Near-zero for firms under 50 employees

### 3. Swiss Legal Industry Associations
- **Positioning:** Professional standards, CPD, ethics guidance
- **Gap:** No concrete AI governance frameworks adapted to small law firm operations; Swissdamed readiness guidance is general and not yet enforced
- **Relevance to this market:** Low-to-medium; awareness exists but actionable frameworks absent

### 4. Insurance Brokers (D&O, Professional Liability)
- **Positioning:** AI-related professional liability coverage
- **Gap:** Coverage frameworks are emerging but AI exclusion clauses are common; no proactive governance tools offered
- **Relevance:** Indirect — insurance pressure may actually motivate governance adoption, but no vendor is connecting the dots

### 5. Generic "AI Compliance" SaaS Platforms
- **Positioning:** EU AI Act compliance checklists, data governance tools
- **Gap:** Designed for enterprise; expensive, complex, not adapted to Swiss SMB professional services
- **Relevance:** Low in current form — Swissdamed-specific tools almost entirely absent from market

### 6. In-House / DIY
- **What:** Firms attempting to draft their own AI policies using generic templates
- **Gap:** Templates exist (e.g., from bar associations, Wolters Kluwer) but implementation guidance is absent; "checkbox" policies that look good but are not enforceable
- **Relevance:** This is the default — and the problem. The 43% "no policy + no plans" includes many who tried DIY and abandoned it

---

## What No One Is Doing: The Unoccupied Position

**No vendor or advisor is offering:**
1. A Swiss-specific, SMB-professional-services AI governance framework
2. A cross-vertical lead magnet portfolio that speaks to the shared governance gap
3. A Swissdamed-first sequencing model for professional services outreach
4. A 4-deadline cluster communication strategy that creates urgency without hype
5. A framework that bridges individual AI adoption (90%+) with institutional accountability (9%)

---

## Positioning for DigitalCoa.ch

**The unoccupied position:**  
*"Swissdamed-ready AI governance for professional services firms — practical, vertical-specific, compliance-connected."*

**Why it is defensible:**
- Swiss-specific regulatory context (Swissdamed, FDPIC, Federal Council AI framework) requires local expertise — generic AI governance vendors cannot replicate this
- SMB professional services (2–50 employees) is structurally different from enterprise — large consultancies will not compete here
- Cross-vertical synthesis (the 46-point gap, the 43% no-policy finding) demonstrates market-wide insight that single-vertical advisors lack

**The synthesis is the moat.** Competitors can copy one vertical framework; they cannot easily replicate the cross-vertical synthesis that shows the market-wide nature of the governance gap.
