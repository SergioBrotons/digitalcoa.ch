# 01-ICP.md — Geneva NGO/Tech

## Geneva NGO/Tech — ICP Definition

### Primary ICP: Geneva International Organisations and NGOs
- **Organisations:** UNOG, ITU, WIPO, WHO, WTO, ICRC, UNHCR, WFP, IOM, OHCHR, UNEP, UNDP Geneva presence
- **Role:** CIO / Head of IT, Director of Operations, Head of Finance/Administration, Technology Advisor
- **Pain:** IT procurement reform, cybersecurity compliance (donor requirements, NIST CSF), AI governance, digital transformation, multi-stakeholder standard-setting, operational resilience
- **Trigger events:** Donor cybersecurity mandates, AI Convention obligations, technology partnership agreements, budget efficiency mandates
- **Buying behaviour:** Through established procurement frameworks; peer-recommended; multi-year relationship for trusted advisors
- **Language:** EN-primary; FR for Swiss domestic engagement

### Secondary ICP: Geneva Tech/Digital Health Startups
- Digital health companies (SaMD, telemedicine, health data platforms) with Swissmedic/FOPH regulatory pathway needs
- Cleantech companies seeking Series A/B funding and corporate partnership
- Space tech companies with Geneva-satellite operations and licensing needs

### Tertiary ICP: Humanitarian Sector Suppliers/Vendors
- Technology vendors seeking ICRC/WFP/UNHCR procurement qualification
- Cyber/IT consulting firms targeting IO/NGO sector
- Health technology companies seeking UN/health agency partnership

### What This ICP Does NOT Want
- Generic cybersecurity or AI governance frameworks without humanitarian/sector nuance
- Sales-driven vendor pitches to under-resourced IO procurement teams
- Solutionism — technology recommendations without understanding operational constraints
- Compliance theatre without genuine operational improvement

### Strategic Implication
Geneva NGO/Tech ICPs are reachable through established procurement pathways, sector conferences, and peer networks. They are relationship-driven, compliance-aware, and increasingly focused on AI governance and cybersecurity. The pathway is through sector publications (Brotons.net) + sector network introduction.
