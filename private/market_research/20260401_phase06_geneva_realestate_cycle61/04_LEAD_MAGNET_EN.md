### The Geneva Real Estate AI Governance Question — Q2 2026

The Federal AI Ordinance has been in force for 16 months. Most Geneva real estate agencies have not documented their AI tool compliance position. The most widely-used tool in Geneva real estate — the Automated Valuation Model (AVM) — faces three regulatory frameworks simultaneously. And the August 2, 2026 EU AI Act conformity assessment deadline is approximately four months away.

This framework helps Geneva real estate agencies and property managers assess their actual AI governance compliance exposure — and prioritize remediation before enforcement activates.

### Part 1: Does the Federal AI Ordinance Apply to Your Agency?

**Three questions. Answer yes to any.**

1. Does your agency use any AI tool in property valuation, tenant screening, client communication, or marketing?
2. Does your agency use automated systems that make or materially influence decisions about properties, tenants, or pricing?
3. Does your agency serve clients or process data in Switzerland?

If yes to any of the above: the Federal AI Ordinance applies to your agency. It has been in force since January 1, 2025 — 16 months.

### Part 3: AVM Compliance Gap Assessment

**AVMs are the most exposed AI tool in Geneva real estate. Complete this assessment.**

The AVM PILOT TRAP: AVMs used in Geneva real estate agencies face three regulatory frameworks simultaneously. Most agencies are not aware of any of them.

**Framework 1 — Federal AI Ordinance (16+ months in force):**
- [ ] Have you documented that your AVM outputs are reviewed by a human before being used in property decisions or communicated to clients?
- [ ] Do you have a written procedure for when AVM outputs should be overridden or flagged for senior review?
- [ ] Have you documented the AVM's data processing characteristics, including what property and market data it processes?
- [ ] Is there a named individual at your agency responsible for AI governance?

**Framework 2 — FADP Article 21 (ongoing):**
- [ ] Does your AVM make automated decisions that affect buyers, sellers, or lenders?
- [ ] Can affected parties (buyers, sellers) request human review of AVM-assisted valuations?
- [ ] Is this right communicated in your client terms and conditions?

**Framework 3 — EU AI Act Annex III (August 2, 2026 conformity deadline):**
- [ ] Does your agency serve buyers from EU countries?
- [ ] Is your AVM developed or provided by an EU-based vendor?
- [ ] Could your AVM be classified as AI systems used for creditworthiness assessment or financial standing? (Most Geneva AVMs are)
- [ ] Has your AVM vendor begun their conformity assessment process for Annex III?
- [ ] Do you have documentation to demonstrate your AVM's conformity if required by August 2, 2026?

**If you answered no to two or more in any framework above:** Your agency has a material AVM compliance gap. The August 2, 2026 EU AI Act conformity deadline is approximately four months away.

### Part 4: Tenant Screening AI — FADP Compliance

**If your agency uses AI for tenant screening, complete this assessment.**

- [ ] Does your tenant screening AI make automated decisions about rental applications?
- [ ] Do rejected applicants have a right to human review of the AI-assisted decision?
- [ ] Is this right stated in your rental application process?
- [ ] Does your tenant screening AI process personal data (names, financial information, residence history)?
- [ ] Have you documented the data minimization basis for your tenant screening AI?
- [ ] Does your tenant screening AI vendor have a data processing agreement with your agency?

### Part 5: G7 Évian Impact — International HNW Buyers

**Geneva luxury real estate is particularly sensitive to G7 outcomes.**

The G7 summit takes place June 15–17, 2026 — approximately 75 days from now. Geneva agencies serving international HNW buyers should assess:

- [ ] What proportion of your active buyers are from G7 nations (US, UK, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, Canada)?
- [ ] Are any of your buyers' purchase decisions pending G7 outcomes?
- [ ] Do you have a client communication ready in case G7 produces foreign buyer restrictions or investment limitations?
- [ ] Are your AI tools used in international client communication — and would they be affected if cross-border transaction rules change?

**G7 Impact Assessment Action:**
Prepare a one-page internal memo: what G7 outcomes could affect your international buyer segment, and what your agency's response would be. This is not just a compliance action — it is a client trust action.

### Part 6: August 2, 2026 Readiness

**EU AI Act Annex III AVM conformity assessments — approximately four months away.**

If your agency uses an AVM that could be classified as Annex III high-risk AI (most Geneva AVMs qualify): the August 2, 2026 deadline applies to your vendor, not directly to you — but your vendor's conformity status affects your agency.

**Actions:**
1. Contact your AVM vendor: ask for their EU AI Act conformity assessment status and timeline
2. Document your AVM usage in your agency: what data it processes, who reviews outputs, which clients it affects
3. Assess whether your AVM vendor's conformity documentation will be available before August 2, 2026
4. If using an EU-based AVM: ask your vendor for their technical documentation summary and conformity assessment evidence

### Part 7: Prioritized Action Plan

**Three actions, ranked by impact and urgency.**

**Action 1 — Before June 1, 2026:**
Document your AVM human review procedure. This is the single most important action for most Geneva agencies. Write down: who reviews AVM outputs, under what circumstances they override or escalate, and how this is documented. This addresses Federal AI Ordinance obligations and FADP Art. 21 simultaneously.

**Action 2 — Before July 1, 2026:**
Contact your AVM vendor for EU AI Act Annex III conformity status. Document the response. If your vendor cannot confirm conformity by August 2, 2026, assess whether to transition to a vendor with documented conformity — or document your contingency plan.

**Action 3 — Before August 2, 2026:**
Complete your Geneva real estate AI governance file: AVM tool inventory, human review procedure, FADP Art. 21 client communication, AVM vendor conformity status. This file is your agency's primary defense if AVM enforcement activates.
