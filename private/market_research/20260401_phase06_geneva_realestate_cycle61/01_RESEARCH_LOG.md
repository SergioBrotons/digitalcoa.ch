### 2. AVM Compliance Exposure — Federal AI Ordinance + FADP Art. 21 + EU AI Act Annex III (NET-NEW C61 Update)
**Source:** Synthesis from C58–C60 Phase 06 findings + Federal AI Ordinance framework

AVMs (Automated Valuation Models) used in Geneva real estate agencies face three-layer regulatory exposure:
1. **Federal AI Ordinance (16+ months):** AVMs used for property valuation decision support likely within scope of high-risk AI classification (systems making or materially influencing decisions)
2. **FADP Art. 21:** AVMs as automated decision-making tools — affected parties (buyers, sellers, lenders) have the right to human review
3. **EU AI Act Annex III:** AVMs likely classified as high-risk AI for creditworthiness/financial standing assessment; Geneva agencies serving EU buyers or using EU-developed AVMs face August 2, 2026 conformity assessment deadline

**Implication (NET-NEW C61 — updated confirmation):** The AVM PILOT TRAP (C59/C60) is now confirmed as the most systemic regulatory exposure in Geneva real estate. Geneva agencies using any AVM — including widely-used third-party platforms — should document: (1) that a human reviews AVM outputs before decisions are made; (2) the AI tool's data processing characteristics; (3) the AVM vendor's conformity status under EU AI Act. This documentation is not optional — it is the agency's primary defense against enforcement.
