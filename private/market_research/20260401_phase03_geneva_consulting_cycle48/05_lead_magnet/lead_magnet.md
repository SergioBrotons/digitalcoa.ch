## The Envelope Test for Consulting Firms

Before any formal policy review, apply the Envelope Test:

Safe (automating the envelope):
- Client meeting scheduling and calendar management
- Document formatting and template population (proposals, status reports, slide decks)
- Project tracking and milestone reminders
- Billing preparation from time entries
- Competitive intelligence aggregation (routing and formatting only)
- Research article summaries (from published sources, not client data)

Not safe without DPA and DPIA (automating the letter):
- Drafting or editing client deliverables using AI
- Summarizing or analyzing client strategic documents
- Generating recommendations from client financial or operational data
- AI-assisted due diligence on client matters
- Generating client proposals with substantive AI content
- AI analysis of proprietary client business data

## The GPAI Obligations for Consulting Firms with EU Clients

If your consulting firm serves EU-based clients, the GPAI Regulation (in force since August 2, 2025) applies to AI systems that:
- Are placed on the EU market
- Are used in the EU
- Produce outputs used in the EU

**Minimum GPAI obligations:**
1. **Transparency**: Clients must be informed when they are interacting with AI
2. **Technical Documentation**: For high-risk AI systems, documentation of training data, model logic, and risk assessments
3. **Fundamental Rights Impact**: AI systems must not undermine fundamental rights — including privacy, non-discrimination, and economic freedom

The GPAI obligations are not future obligations. They have been operative since August 2, 2025. Consulting firms with EU client exposure should already have documentation.

## The Swiss Data Residency Reality

Swiss consulting firms handle highly sensitive client data: strategic plans, financial information, organizational dynamics, competitive positioning. This is precisely the data that should not be processed through US-based AI tools subject to the CLOUD Act.

The Envelope Test applies here: if your AI tool processes client strategic data through US servers, attorney-client confidentiality and Swiss data protection principles may both be at risk.

Swiss-hosted alternatives (FABIAN for enterprise; CASUS and Meikai for SMB-accessible) keep data within Swiss jurisdiction.

## The Three-Layer Liability Stack for Consulting Advisors

Consulting advisors using AI carry personal exposure at three layers:

**Layer 1 — FADP Compliance Obligation**
Already operative. Art. 5(c), Art. 16, Art. 21, DPIA, Privacy by Design — all already in force. Criminal penalties up to CHF 250,000 for individuals.

**Layer 2 — Professional Liability**
As a consulting advisor, you bear responsibility for the quality and accuracy of your deliverables — AI-assisted or not. If AI produces flawed strategic recommendations, the client holds you accountable, not the AI vendor.

**Layer 3 — GPAI Product Liability**
If the AI system you deployed causes harm to a client (through a flawed recommendation, a data breach, or a fundamental rights violation), GPAI and general product liability principles may attach.

## Why 73% of Consulting AI Projects Fail

The 73% failure rate is not a technology problem. It is a governance problem. The pattern:

1. Pilot launched with enthusiasm
2. Success demonstrated at small scale
3. Governance questions arise: who is responsible for the AI's outputs? What data went in? How do we document this for the client? Does this comply with FADP? Do we need a DPA?
4. Questions not answered → project stalled
5. Stalled long enough → project abandoned

The consulting firms that successfully move AI from pilot to production are the ones that built the governance framework before launching the pilot.

The governance framework does not need to be elaborate. It needs to be documented, current, and applied consistently.

## A Minimal Viable AI Governance Framework for Consulting Firms

1. **AI Tool Inventory with Envelope Test**: Document every AI tool in use, what data it accesses, and whether it is envelope or letter
2. **DPA Register**: Signed DPAs for every vendor processing client data, with Swiss or EU residency confirmed
3. **Client Communication Policy**: How you disclose AI use in deliverables, proposals, and communications
4. **Quality Control Procedure**: How AI-assisted work is reviewed before client delivery
5. **GPAI Self-Assessment** (for EU-nexus firms): Is any AI system in use subject to GPAI obligations? If yes, is the documentation in place?
