# 01-ICP.md — Geneva NGO/Tech Decision-Makers

## Geneva NGO/Tech — International Organisations / Geneva Tech Sector

### Refined ICP Definition
Technology and procurement decision-makers at:
- International organisations with Geneva presence (ICRC, UNHCR, WFP, WHO, ITU, UPU, UNOG, WMO, WTO)
- Geneva-based NGOs and humanitarian organisations
- Geneva cleantech and deep tech ventures (Series A-B)
- Geneva health tech and MedTech ventures
- Foundations with Geneva operations ( philanthropic, family, institutional)

### Technology Decision-Maker Profile
- **Role:** CIO, CTO, Head of IT, Procurement Director, Programme Director
- **Concern:** Digital transformation cost-effectiveness, cybersecurity compliance, vendor governance, AI governance, standards compliance
- **Trigger events:** Vendor contract renewal, cybersecurity incident, AI deployment decision, regulatory change (NIS2, EU AI Act, DORA), donor compliance mandate
- **Buying behaviour:** Committee-driven; donor/board approval; evidence-based; long procurement cycles
- **Budget authority:** CHF 100K-10M technology/programme spend; multi-year framework agreements
- **Language:** EN-primary; FR for Geneva domestic; DE for federal-level

### What This ICP Does NOT Want
- Generic enterprise tech vendor pitches
- AI solutions without explainability and accountability framework
- Unproven vendor claims without institutional reference
- Complex procurement without clear compliance pathway

### Strategic Implication
IO/NGO technology decisions are committee-driven and evidence-based. Brotons.net content creates awareness; institutional peer reference drives consideration. Vendor credibility with other IOs/NGOs is critical.
