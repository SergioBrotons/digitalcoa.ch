## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 2)

### EN
**Family Business Governance — Geneva's Under-Served Consulting Segment**

Geneva has high density of family-controlled enterprises (watchmaking, trading, commodity, real estate, professional services). Key demand drivers:
- Succession crisis: baby boomer founders reaching exit stage en masse 2025-2030; most Geneva family businesses lack formal succession plans
- Family constitution/advisory board: structured governance frameworks separating family and business decisions = growing demand
- Next-generation capability: professionalizing next-gen family members with structured development programs
- Family office governance: wealth preservation frameworks distinct from operating business governance
- Conflict resolution: family business disputes (sibling buyouts, in-law tensions, passive active shareholder conflicts) generating mediation and advisory work

Geneva-specific dynamics:
- Cross-border families: Swiss, French, Italian, and other nationals in same family requiring multi-jurisdictional succession planning
- Roman Law succession regimes: Geneva/Cantonal succession law creating specific constraints not well understood by international advisors
- Concentration of family office wealth managers and private banks as intermediaries creating referral ecosystem

Market gap: most Geneva consulting firms treat family business as sub-segment of SME consulting; specialized boutique firms are rare and in demand. Professionalizing family business governance = premium niche

### FR
**Gouvernance d'entreprise familiale — segment sous-servi du conseil genevois**

Genève a une haute densité d'entreprises familiales (horlogerie, trading, matières premières, immobilier, services professionnels). Drivers clés :
- Crise de succession : fondateurs baby-boomers atteignant le seuil de sortie 2025-2030 ; plupart des entreprises familiales genevoises sans plans de succession formels
- Constitution familiale : cadres de gouvernance séparant décisions familiales et commerciales
- Capacité de la prochaine génération : programmes de développement structurés
- Gouvernance family office : cadres de préservation de fortune distincts de l'entreprise opérationnelle
- Résolution de conflits : rachats entre frères/sœurs, tensions belles-filles, conflits actionnaire passif/actif

Dynamiques genevoises spécifiques :
- Familles transfrontalières :Suisses, Français, Italiens nécessitant planification successorale multi-juridictionnelle
- Régimes successoraux de droit romain : loi successorale genevoise/cantonale créant des contraintes spécifiques
- Concentration de family offices et banques privées comme intermédiaires créant un écosystème de référencement
