## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Regulatory Change Management — New Consulting Category
- CSRD, DORA, EU Data Act, Swiss mHRDD cumulative wave creating new "regulatory change management" consulting category
- "Regulatory PMO" niche emerging — project management office for multiple simultaneous regulatory changes
- Premium pricing: 20-30% above standard management consulting due to specialist knowledge and liability
- Cross-border dimension: Swiss companies managing EU regulatory changes need dual CH/EU regulatory expertise
- Geneva consulting firms with regulatory expertise positioning as one-stop change management partners

### Finding 02: Family Business Governance — Under-Served Geneva Segment
- Geneva high density of family enterprises (watchmaking, trading, commodity, real estate, professional services)
- Succession crisis: baby boomer founders exiting 2025-2030; most lack formal succession plans
- Family constitution/advisory board frameworks: growing demand for structured governance separating family and business
- Cross-border families (Swiss/French/Italian) requiring multi-jurisdictional succession planning
- Roman Law succession regimes creating Geneva-specific constraints not well understood internationally
- Market gap: specialized boutique firms rare; professionalizing family business governance = premium niche

### Finding 03: Data Strategy Consulting — Beyond Compliance to Value Creation
- Geneva companies moving from FADP compliance to data as strategic asset
- AI data readiness, data architecture strategy, data monetization for commodity trading firms
- EU Data Act + Swiss MCC simultaneously: complex cross-border data flow governance demand
- International organizations in Geneva creating unique data governance challenges (diplomatic immunity considerations)
- Market gap: most Geneva IT consulting firms treat data governance as IT project; business-focused data strategy consulting underserved

### Finding 04: Operational Excellence — FX-Driven Efficiency Pressure
- CHF strength vs. EUR creating structural efficiency pressure for Geneva internationally-exposed companies
- Shared services consolidation (Geneva → Lausanne/Ticino/French side)
- Procurement consulting, lean/six sigma, automation (RPA/IPA) for margin defense
- Geneva-specific: international organizations creating unique operational challenges (UN salary scale, diplomatic immunity HR, multi-currency treasury)
- Commodity trading firms and banks automating middle/back office functions

### Finding 05: Geopolitical Risk Consulting — Geneva's Unique Position
- Geneva commodity traders requiring real-time geopolitical intelligence for physical commodity flows
- Swiss sanctions compliance intelligence: beyond legal compliance to scenario planning
- Switzerland's neutral status: protection + complexity for operating in sanctioned jurisdictions
- Geneva diplomatic hub: informal intelligence networks; consultants with diplomatic access commanding premium
- Market gap: Geneva geopolitical risk consulting dominated by large firms (Control Risks, Marsh, Kroll); boutiques rare

### Finding 06: Interim Management — Higher-Margin Transformation Model
- Transformation mandates (digital, regulatory, M&A, operational) driving interim executive demand
- C-suite gaps: CHF 2,500-5,000+ day rates for senior interim executives in Geneva
- Swiss employment law making permanent hires expensive to terminate: interim model attractive
- Business model advantages: higher margin than consulting, predictable revenue, natural upsell to permanent/consulting
- Geneva commodity trading/finance: interim CFO, CRO, compliance head mandates
- Specialized niche opportunity: interim executives for regulatory transformation (mHRDD, DORA, AI governance)
