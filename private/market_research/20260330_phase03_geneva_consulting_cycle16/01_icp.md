# Phase 03: Geneva Consulting — ICP
# Cycle 16 — 2026-03-30

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary Segment
Small consulting firms in Geneva: boutique strategy, IT, management consulting, professional services (typically 2–15 consultants), no in-house compliance team.

### Core Problem
They use AI for client deliverables, research, proposal generation, and internal operations — without a governance framework. The EU AI Act GPAI obligations are already live (~8 months). Federal Council First Draft is ~2 months away (May 2026). No sector-specific guidance exists for small consulting firms.

### Regulatory Triggers

**EU AI Act GPAI — Active Since August 2, 2025 (~8 months)**
Transparency obligations already live for firms with EU nexus. High-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months away).

**Federal Council First Draft AI Action Plan — May 2026 (~2 months)**
Multi-stakeholder "AI Action Plan for Switzerland" First Draft presentation. External pressure event. Demand for guidance will spike.

**nFADP Art. 5(c) — Active Since 2023**
AI-based automated decisions about individuals require DPIA + human review.

**CoE AI Convention — End-2026**
Federal Council formal consultation draft expected end-2026 (sector-specific adjustments, not horizontal law).

### ICP Statement
Geneva small consulting firm (2–15 consultants) using AI for client work, research, or internal operations, with no formal AI governance framework, that may have EU clients or EU-intended outputs — and faces GPAI transparency obligations already live.

### Addressable Market
~200–400 Geneva small consulting firms (estimate 30–40% with AI exposure; EU-nexus subset smaller)

### Competitive Absence (10th+ cycle)
Swiss Cyber Institute, Govern AI, Swiss Infosec AG, AI Legal & Strategy AG, Modulos AG, GAIGI — all enterprise-focused. No boutique competitor for small consulting firm AI governance. Structural fact confirmed across 16 cycles.

### Key Timing (from March 30, 2026)
- GPAI: ~8 months live; transparency obligations ACTIVE NOW
- Federal Council First Draft: ~2 months (May 2026)
- EU AI Act high-risk: ~4.5 months (Aug 2, 2026)
- FDJP consultation draft: ~9 months (end-2026)
- Swissdamed: ~12 weeks (spillover)
