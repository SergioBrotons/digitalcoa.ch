# 01 — ICP: Geneva NGOs & Tech Organizations

## Target Segments: Two Distinct Verticals

### Segment A: International Organizations & NGOs

#### Profile
- **Size:** Varies widely — small NGOs (5-20 staff) to large IOs (UN agencies with 500+ staff in Geneva)
- **Revenue:** NGO budgets vary from CHF 500K to 100M+; IOs funded by member states
- **Locations:** Geneva (Rue de Lausanne corridor, La Perle du Lac area)
- **Examples:** ICRC (International Committee of the Red Cross), UN agencies (WHO, WTO, ITU, UNCTAD, UNHCR), ICC,救护 (humanitarian), human rights NGOs

#### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| Executive Director / Country Director | Senior leader | Strategy alignment, donor reporting, governance |
| Operations Director / CFO | Non-program executive | Grants management, compliance, reporting |
| Program Manager | Mid-level | Data management, M&E (monitoring & evaluation) |
| IT / Data Manager | Technical role | Data security, AI adoption, technology selection |
| HR / Admin | Support function | Remote work policies, compliance, training |

#### Pain Points (NGOs)
1. **Donor reporting burden** — multiple funders, multiple formats, high admin overhead
2. **Data sovereignty** — sensitive beneficiary data requiring specific jurisdictions
3. **Grant compliance** — strict rules on expenditure, documentation, audits
4. **Security concerns** — geopolitical risk, data security for sensitive operations
5. **Remote work infrastructure** — post-2022 still adapting hybrid setups
6. **AI/data governance** — nascent awareness, no clear frameworks

#### Known Geneva IO/NGO Examples
- ICRC (Geneva HQ, 20,000+ staff globally)
- WHO (Geneva HQ, 7,000+ staff)
- WTO (Geneva, 700+ staff)
- Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International (Geneva offices)
- Geneva Call, ICTJ (smaller human rights NGOs)
- SwissAid, HEKS (Swiss-based international NGOs)

### Segment B: Geneva Tech Startups & Scale-ups

#### Profile
- **Stage:** Seed to Series B (Geneva/Lausanne corridor)
- **Sectors:** Fintech, healthtech, SaaS, AI/ML, cybersecurity
- **Funding:** VC-backed or bootstrapped; CHF 500K to 30M raised
- **Locations:** Fongit (Geneva tech incubator), EPFL Innovation Park (Lausanne), Plan-les-Ouates
- **Examples:** Abionic (healthtech), Flybotix, Climeworkds, Scandit (though Zurich-based), Tesla AI Europe (Geneva presence)

#### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CEO / Founder | Often technical background | Fundraising, strategy, board management |
| CTO / Head of Engineering | Technical leader | Tech stack decisions, AI integration, security |
| VP Operations / COO | Business operations | Process scaling, hiring, vendor management |
| Head of Product | Product leader | User research, roadmapping, AI feature decisions |
| Data / ML Lead | Specialist | Model governance, data quality, AI ethics |

#### Pain Points (Tech)
1. **Scaling operations** — from startup chaos to structured processes
2. **AI/ML governance** — model risk, bias, explainability concerns
3. **Data quality** — fragmented data sources, no clear data governance
4. **Cybersecurity** — limited internal expertise, increasing threats
5. **Regulatory compliance** — evolving Swiss and EU tech regulations
6. **Fundraising pressure** — investor reporting, metric optimization

#### Known Geneva Tech Examples
- Abionic (nano-bio sensors)
- Flybotix (drone tech)
- Climeworks (carbon capture — Zurich but Geneva-linked)
- Anágl (healthtech)
- Bestmile (autonomous vehicles — Lausanne)
- Actight (energy tech)

## Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for NGOs; English essential for IOs
- Tech startups: English often preferred (especially post-Series A)
- Geneva NGO sector: Relationship-driven, formal, trust-based
- Tech sector: More direct, speed-oriented, proof-of-concept focused
