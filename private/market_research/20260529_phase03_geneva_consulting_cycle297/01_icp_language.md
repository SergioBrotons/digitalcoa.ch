# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Small Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems
## Phase 03 C297 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees | EN

---

## How They Describe the Problem

*"I signed something. I'm not sure what I signed."*

*"My client sent me their AI questionnaire. I read it three times. I had nothing to put in half the fields."*

*"I've got three carriers now. Each one sends something different. I signed all of them. Nobody ever reviewed what I was signing."*

*"The sub-consultant produced the analysis. I put my name on it. I can't tell you which part is whose."*

*"When they ask me to justify my proposal methodology, I hand them a PDF. I've got nothing that shows you the reasoning chain."*

*"My carrier is asking questions that sound like they're about my clients' data. I'm the middle of something and I don't know where it goes."*

---

## Key Language Patterns

### On Client AI Governance Questionnaires
- *"My client sent their AI questionnaire and I realized I had no idea what we were actually doing"*
- *"It's 40 pages. Half of it I don't understand what they're asking for"*
- *"I responded to it but I'm not sure the response is accurate"*
- *"I sent it to my lawyer friend. Nobody knew what to tell me"*
- *"The response I gave three months ago — I don't know if it's consistent with what I say today"*

### On Carrier Attestation
- *"I got the carrier form. Has my signature line on it. I was on a plane that night"*
- *"Three carriers, three different forms, same signature, same me"*
- *"I asked my broker what 'adequate AI governance' means. He said 'just sign it'"*
- *"I didn't know I was signing something personal until they sent the renewal"*
- *"The form was written by the carrier's legal team. I was not their client"*

### On Sub-Consultant Delegation
- *"The analysis came from somewhere else. I presented it. I stand behind it. I can't audit it"*
- *"My sub-consultant uses AI. I don't know which tools. I don't ask"*
- *"When my client challenges the deliverable, I'm the one who answers"*
- *"The sub-consultant is covered by their own PI. My client is covered by mine. I have no idea where it meets"*
- *"I delegated the work. I didn't delegate the accountability"*

### On Proposal Methodology Defensibility
- *"The client asked how we arrived at this recommendation. I showed them the output. The machine did it"*
- *"I've been doing this 15 years. I can explain my old methodology. I can't explain this one"*
- *"When the client's technical team starts asking questions, I realize I don't have the chain"*
- *"I can show you the deliverable. I cannot show you the analytical reasoning"*
- *"My methodology lives in my head. It never made it into a document"*

### On Personal Liability
- *"My name is on the engagement letter. That's the part that wakes me up at 3am"*
- *"D&O covers money. It doesn't cover governance documents when they come for those"*
- *"I'm 48. I'm not building a practice to hand it to someone when this lands"*
- *"The carrier form. Signed my name. I still don't know what I certified"*
- *"Every time a client questionnaire arrives, I think about what's in that file"*

### On nFADP and Data Processing
- *"My vendor's terms of service. I clicked yes. I don't know what I agreed to"*
- *"Client data goes to the AI. I don't know where it goes after that"*
- *"GDPR — that's for Google and Facebook. I'm a small consulting firm in Geneva"*
- *"The nFADP stuff. I keep hearing about 2027. I don't know what's active now"*
- *"My client data is in three AI tools I couldn't name in a court right now"*

---

## What They Don't Say (Yet)

They do not say "accountability architecture."
They do not say "processor chain."
They do not say "nFADP Art.9 third-party compliance."
They do not say "attestation evidence standard."
They do not say "sequential authorization chain."

They say:
- *"I signed something"*
- *"I don't know what I agreed to"*
- *"I can't explain it"*
- *"I'm the one who answers"*
- *"Nobody reviewed it"*

The language is personal, present-tense, and anxious — not regulatory.
