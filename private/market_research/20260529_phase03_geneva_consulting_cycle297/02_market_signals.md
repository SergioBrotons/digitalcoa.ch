# 02 — Market Signals: Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures
## Phase 03 C297 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees

---

## Primary Market Signals

### Signal 1: Client AI Governance Questionnaire Arrival — No Response Architecture Exists
**What's happening:** Managing directors at Geneva consulting boutiques are receiving AI governance questionnaires from clients — large corporates, institutional clients, EU-domiciled entities with their own AI governance obligations — with no methodology for responding. Questionnaires arrive with tight deadlines (2–4 weeks). MD scrambles, produces inconsistent responses, has no cross-client tracking system to detect contradictions.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"I responded to three of them last quarter. Three different clients. I have no idea if what I said to one is consistent with what I said to another."*

**Failure mode:** Retrospective scramble producing self-inconsistent documentation that becomes a liability when clients compare notes. Each response also functions as a commercial representation — the firm is documenting its own AI governance practice in writing, committed to what's written.

---

### Signal 2: Carrier Attestation — Post-Signature Realization
**What's happening:** Carrier AI attestation forms arrive, MD signs because waiting means delays. Signature is personal. Carrier form is written by carrier legal team. The form uses undefined adequacy standard. No Geneva-calibrated pre-signing review service exists. Post-signature, the MD realizes they certified something they cannot explain.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"I signed it on a Thursday evening. I read the first page and the last page. The middle was underwriting language. I didn't get a review before I signed."*

**Failure mode:** Personal liability on undefined standard. Documentation created to satisfy carrier underwriting does not document actual governance practice. Attempt to comply creates documentation that works against the MD.

---

### Signal 3: Proposal Methodology Defensibility — When Clients Push Back
**What's happening:** After AI-assisted proposals are delivered, clients' internal technical teams or AI governance officers begin asking detailed questions about methodology, source data, model versions, human review steps. MD cannot answer. Deliverable was produced with AI. Reasoning chain was never documented. Analytical attribution is architecturally impossible to reconstruct.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"The client's technical team started asking about our AI methodology. I realized I had nothing. Not because we're doing anything wrong — because I never thought to document the reasoning chain."*

**Failure mode:** Client relationship damage and exposure when MD cannot explain the analytical methodology behind recommendations. Inability to answer creates suspicion where none existed. Specific high-stakes moment: client technical challenge arrives after delivery.

---

### Signal 4: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation — Invisible Accountability Chain
**What's happening:** Geneva boutiques routinely use sub-consultants for specialized work. Sub-consultants are using AI tools. The prime boutique cannot audit sub-consultant AI use. Engagement letters contain no AI governance clause. The prime MD's name is on the deliverable. Downstream AI vendor terms are accepted without evaluation. Three-party chain (prime → sub-consultant → AI vendor) creates processor flows the prime boutique bears responsibility for without any documentation.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"We use specialists on maybe a third of our mandates. I don't ask what they run. I probably shouldn't know. But when there's a problem, they call me."*

**Failure mode:** nFADP Art.9 third-party processor obligations triggered by delegation. Client attribution chain cannot be explained. Sub-consultant AI failure becomes prime boutique liability. No engagement letter AI clause covering this scenario exists in the Geneva boutique market.

---

### Signal 5: nFADP Transition Period Closed — Active Compliance Gap
**What's happening:** nFADP transition period ended. Most small Geneva consulting boutiques are not compliant. They are using AI tools with client data without Data Processing Agreements that satisfy nFADP Art.9, without documented lawful basis, without client notification, without cross-border transfer assessment. The obligations are active and statutory. The awareness is minimal. The market has no proportionate solution.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"I keep hearing '2027' like it's a deadline. My lawyer friend told me last week that's when the transition ends. So it's active right now? Nobody told me."*

**Failure mode:** Active statutory violations. Personal liability for managing director. No awareness of what needs to be fixed first, second, or third. No Geneva-calibrated methodology for a 2–50 person consulting firm to achieve nFADP compliance.

---

### Signal 6: Personal Liability as the Core Fear — Nothing Addresses It
**What's happening:** The managing director at a Geneva consulting boutique carries personal liability. Engagement letter has their name. Carrier attestation has their signature. D&O insurance covers financial exposure — it does not cover governance paper trail adequacy. No product exists that creates a documented governance record for personal protection.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"D&O tells me they'll cover the judgment. They don't tell me how to govern AI so I never need to use the coverage. That's not what they do."*

**Failure mode:** MD is personally exposed with no proportionate governance documentation system. The coverage gap between financial protection and governance documentation is unnamed in the Geneva market. No service creates the documented continuity that proves the MD's governance diligence over time.

---

## Observable Market Failure Pattern

The failure pattern is not one specific problem. It is a convergence of six simultaneous problems at the same boutique scale:

1. Client questionnaires trigger documentation obligations with no response methodology
2. Carrier attestation creates personal liability without pre-signing review
3. Sub-consultant delegation generates invisible three-party processor chain
4. Proposal methodology cannot be defended when challenged
5. nFADP obligations are active with no compliance path
6. Personal liability is uncovered by available insurance products

None of these are addressed by a single existing market service. The consolidation point is the managing director personally — which is where the boutique accountability structure terminates.
