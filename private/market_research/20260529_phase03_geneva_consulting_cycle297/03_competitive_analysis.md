# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists versus What's Missing
## Phase 03 C297 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees

---

## What Exists in the Market

### What Exists: Generic AI Governance Frameworks
**Products:** ISO 42001 certification pathway, IT governance consultants offering AI policy templates, vendor-provided compliance guides (Microsoft, Google, AWS AI governance documentation)

**The gap these leave open:** Enterprise-grade frameworks designed for organizations with dedicated IT departments, compliance officers, and procurement functions. Zero proportionate methodology for 2–50 person Geneva consulting boutique where the managing director holds all governance roles simultaneously. Templates require adaptation the firm cannot perform. Certification costs more than the annual consulting revenue of the firms being served.

---

### What Exists: Legal Industry AI Guidance
**Products:** SAV/FSA national guidelines (principle-level, de facto operative through guidance vacuum), Ordre des Avocats de Genève signaling (guidance vacuum named by the market itself), international bar association AI principles

**The gap these leave open:** National guidelines too general for firm-level decisions. Canton-level guidance absent at Geneva scale. No Geneva-consulting-specific guidance. The legal market has named the problem without solving it operationally. No service offers Geneva-calibrated pre-signing review for attorneys. Same absence applies to consulting market — no equivalent body exists to fill the vacuum.

---

### What Exists: Carrier-Provided AI Attestation Forms
**Products:** Carrier AI governance attestation questionnaires (designed by carrier underwriting teams, not calibrated to Geneva professional liability doctrine)

**The gap these leave open:** Forms are documents that create liability — they do not discharge it. No pre-signing review service in the market. No Geneva-specific interpretation of undefined adequacy standards. No service that shows the MD what they are signing before they sign it. The attestation instrument exists as a liability-creation mechanism without any counterbalancing review infrastructure.

---

### What Exists: nFADP Compliance Documentation Templates
**Products:** Online templates for Data Processing Registers (Art.12 nFADP), DPIA documentation boilerplates, consent form generators, data retention schedules

**The gap these leave open:** Templates assume a compliance department. Template completion does not assess proportionality at 2–50 person boutique scale. No template maps nFADP obligations against Geneva consulting delivery practice specifically. No service addresses the specific AI-delivery-layer documentation that nFADP requires for a consulting firm using AI tools in client engagements. Templates create compliance-looking evidence that does not survive substantive challenge.

---

### What Exists: Sub-Consultant Engagement Letters and Contract Boilerplates
**Products:** Standard engagement letter templates from Swiss professional associations, NDA templates, IT service agreements

**The gap these leave open:** No engagement letter template in the Geneva boutique market contains an AI governance clause. No contract language exists for structuring sub-consultant AI delegation with proportionate processor documentation requirements. No standard language maps the three-party chain (prime boutique → sub-consultant → AI vendor) against nFADP Art.9 processor obligations. The gap between existing engagement letter practice and AI governance requirements is unnamed in the market.

---

### What Exists: D&O Insurance Products
**Products:** Swiss corporate D&O policies, professional liability policies with director coverage extension

**The gap these leave open:** D&O covers financial exposure after a judgment or settlement. It does not create a governance paper trail that would prevent the judgment from occurring. The MD's actual need is not "what happens after a claim" but "what documentation proves governance diligence over time." No insurance product addresses the documentation layer. No insurance broker has positioned pre-signing review as the governance act that prevents claims, not just covers them.

---

## What Is Specifically Missing for Geneva Consulting ICP

### Missing 1: Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review Service
A service that reviews carrier AI attestation forms against: (a) the actual governance practice of the firm, (b) Geneva professional liability doctrine, (c) the personal liability exposure created by each specific commitment, (d) what documentation the firm would need to have in order to survive a challenge to what was certified. At proportionate price point for a 2–50 person boutique. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

### Missing 2: Client AI Questionnaire Response Architecture
A service that designs response architecture before questionnaires arrive — triage taxonomy distinguishing: (a) routine questions answerable immediately, (b) substantive questions requiring pre-documentation build, (c) overbroad questions where disclosure risk exceeds response value and tactical pushback is appropriate. Cross-client consistency tracking. Methodology for building the documentation layer before the response requires it. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

### Missing 3: Sub-Consultant AI Processor Chain Documentation Service
A service that maps the prime boutique's three-party processor chain: (a) identifying which sub-consultants are using AI tools, (b) structuring engagement letter AI clauses that establish proportionate documentation obligations on sub-consultants, (c) building the nFADP Art.9-compliant processor chain documentation that the prime boutique can produce on request without auditing sub-consultant AI systems directly. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

### Missing 4: Proposal Methodology Documentation as Discrete Consulting Deliverable
A service that builds the documented analytical authorization chain for AI-assisted proposals and deliverables: (a) the reasoning chain from client question to analytical framework to AI-assisted analysis to recommendation, (b) version-locked analytical validity under mandate conditions, (c) defensibility documentation that survives client technical challenge without requiring retrospective reconstruction. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

### Missing 5: nFADP Compliance Layer Specific to Consulting Delivery Practice
A service that applies nFADP compliance at the consulting delivery layer — not general data protection but specifically: (a) AI tool lawful basis, (b) client data processing agreements proportionate to boutique consulting use, (c) cross-border transfer restrictions on AI-assisted analysis, (d) documentation architecture that satisfies nFADP Art.9 processor obligations for a firm using AI tools in client engagements, (e) integrated with existing engagement letter architecture. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

### Missing 6: Personal Liability Governance Architecture
A service that creates the documented governance record proving the managing director's personal governance diligence over time — not a policy document, not a one-time attestation response, but a continuous documentation architecture covering: (a) what governance decisions were made, (b) when, (c) on what basis, (d) what evidence standard was applied, (e) how the MD authorized AI tool use in each client engagement. Proof of governance continuity. Evidence that the MD did not merely sign documents but evaluated what they were signing. Does not exist in Geneva market.

---

## White Space Summary

**The white space is not "no one offers AI governance consulting."** The white space is: no one offers a Geneva-calibrated, boutique-proportionate, managing-director-facing personal liability protection service built around documented governance continuity for a small consulting firm using AI in client engagements. The gap is structural, not volume-based. The service category is unnamed. The ICP cannot buy the solution because the solution has not been named as a discrete category.

**Naming the white space:** The first firm to name and position "pre-signing carrier review for Geneva consulting MDs" and "AI governance documentation for boutique consulting" as specific service categories will define the market. The existing market offers frameworks, templates, and policy documents — not personal liability protection architecture.

**The moat:** Bilingual EN/FR content in the managing director's voice with liability-specific language. Geneva-specific positioning. Pattern knowledge from sustained ICP engagement. Cross-client intelligence accumulation. Not replicable through document production.
