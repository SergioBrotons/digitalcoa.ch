# 04 — Compliance Context: Geneva/Switzerland Specific Regulatory
## Phase 03 C297 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees

---

## Regulatory Environment Overview

### Layer 1: nFADP — Active Statutory Obligations
The Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) is in force. Transition periods have closed or are closing. For a Geneva consulting boutique using AI tools with client data:

**Art.9 Third-Party Processor Obligations:** When a Geneva consulting firm uses an AI tool to process client data, the AI vendor is a third-party data processor. The firm must have a compliant Data Processing Agreement with each AI vendor before processing client data. Most boutique firms have not evaluated this. The obligations are active.

**Art.6 Lawful Basis:** Client data processing by AI tools requires documented lawful basis — typically contract performance (Art.6(1)(c)) or legitimate interest. The basis must be evaluated and documented. Most firms accepted vendor terms without this evaluation.

**Art.26 Controller Obligations:** The Geneva consulting firm acting as prime to clients is the data controller. The firm is responsible for the lawfulness of all processing, including processing done by AI vendors acting under the firm's instructions. No vendor terms can transfer this responsibility away from the controller.

**Cross-Border Transfer Restrictions:** If the AI tool processes data outside Switzerland (most US-based AI vendors), additional restrictions apply. "Swiss-hosted" vendor marketing does not resolve processing location compliance. Data residency and processing location are different compliance questions. Most Geneva boutiques cannot answer whether their AI tool processing is cross-border.

---

### Layer 2: Client AI Governance Questionnaires — Contractual Obligations
EU-domiciled clients and institutional clients sending AI governance questionnaires are creating contractual AI governance obligations through the questionnaire response and acceptance. When the firm responds to a client AI questionnaire, it is accepting documented commitments about its AI governance practice. These responses may operate as commercial representations in dispute scenarios.

**Key point:** Cross-client questionnaire responses accumulate across the mandate portfolio with no tracking mechanism. The third response may contradict the first without the firm's detection. Each response also functions as a historical documentation point — the firm made representations about its AI governance practice at a specific date, and those representations are now fixed in the client's file.

---

### Layer 3: Carrier Attestation — Personal Liability Creation
Carrier AI attestation forms are written by carrier underwriting teams to manage carrier risk. The MD's personal signature carries personal liability. The undefined "adequate AI governance controls" standard is designed by the carrier's legal team — not calibrated to Geneva professional liability doctrine. No Geneva-calibrated reference standard exists for what "adequate" means at boutique consulting scale.

**The CoE AI Convention** creates deployer obligations that are present-tense through existing mandate contracts and carrier underwriting terms, before implementing legislation arrives. Geneva consulting firms with EU nexus are potentially in scope for Convention deployer obligations operating now.

---

### Layer 4: Professional Liability Doctrine — Geneva boutique consulting is not legally equivalent to a law firm
Geneva consulting firms are not attorneys. They are not subject to Art.321 CP professional secrecy. But they are subject to:
- Art.398 CO proper performance obligations on engagement letters
- General contractual liability to clients
- Personal liability of the managing director for engagement letter commitments
- nFADP controller liability (Art.82)

No Geneva guidance document specific to consulting firm AI governance exists. Unlike the legal market (where the SAV has published national guidelines and the Ordre has signaled guidance vacuum), the consulting market has no equivalent body that has even named the problem.

---

### Layer 5: Documentation Window — Present-Tense Positioning Paradox
The documentation window is an active, present-tense positioning event, not a future compliance deadline. Geneva consulting firms currently in active client relationships are building documentation history that will be evaluated retrospectively from future dates. Documentation created now will be presented as evidence of governance adequacy or inadequacy when clients, carriers, or regulators review.

**Structural paradox:** The documentation that would best represent the firm's actual AI governance practice is different from the documentation that satisfies carrier attestation requirements. The firm may be creating documentation to satisfy carrier underwriting standards while under-documenting the governance decisions that would actually protect the MD personally.

New documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct window-period reasoning. The 90-day window is not primarily a compliance exercise — it is a burden-of-proof positioning event where the evidence created now will determine the firm's position when challenged.

## Immediate Compliance Action Points for This ICP

1. **Identify which AI tools the firm uses with client data** — make the actual inventory, not a policy document
2. **Map which client data flows to which AI vendor** — initiate Data Processing Agreement assessment
3. **Review engagement letters for AI governance clause compatibility** — existing engagement letters predate firm AI adoption
4. **Design pre-signing carrier attestation review as discrete governance act** — not form completion, personal liability analysis
5. **Build sub-consultant AI delegation architecture** — engagement letter AI clause, processor chain documentation
6. **Establish methodology documentation as discrete deliverable** — client question → analytical framework → AI-assisted analysis → recommendation

The regulatory environment is not the problem. The problem is: no Geneva-calibrated, boutique-proportionate methodology exists for a 2–50 person consulting firm to address all five regulatory layers simultaneously. **That is the specific white space.**
