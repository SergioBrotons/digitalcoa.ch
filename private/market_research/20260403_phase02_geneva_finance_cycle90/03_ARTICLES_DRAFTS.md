## Draft Article 01
**Target:** Brotons.net | **Cluster:** Swiss Financial Centre / AML | **TIER:** TIER 1

### EN Version
**Headline:** The MBaer Case Is Not Julius Baer's Problem. It Is Still Switzerland's.

The enforcement action filed by the US Financial Crimes Enforcement Network against MBaer Merchant Bank reads like a case study in reputational contagion. Mike Baer — descendant of the Julius Baer dynasty — founded MBaer as a separate entity in 2018. The bank is now accused by FinCen of laundering money for three of the most sensitive categories of actors in global finance: Venezuelan oil traffickers, Russian oligarchs, and Iran's Revolutionary Guard.

The bank was excluded from the US financial system approximately one month ago. The executive behind it faces potential arrest on US soil.

For Julius Baer itself, the entities are legally distinct. But the Swiss financial centre does not always make that distinction cleanly — especially in contexts where the Julius Baer name carries structural brand equity built over more than a century. The MBaer case is a reminder that AML enforcement has lengthened its reach: it now follows family names, corporate structures, and transaction patterns across jurisdictions.

What this means for Geneva as a financial centre: FinCen's reach into Swiss-linked structures is now explicitly documented. Geneva private banks and wealth managers should treat this as a signal about the standard of documentation and due diligence that US regulators consider minimum.

Le cas MBaer rappelle que l'application des règles AML a étendu sa portée: elle suit désormais les noms de famille, les structures d'entreprise et les schémas transactionnels à travers les juridictions.

## Draft Article 02
**Target:** Brotons.net | **Cluster:** Geneva Commodity Trading / Geopolitics | **TIER:** TIER 1

### EN Version
**Headline:** The Hormuz Toll Is Real. Geneva Already Paid the Premium.

Iran is implementing a strait toll at Hormuz — the world's most critical oil chokepoint — modelled on the French and Italian highway vignette system. Tehran is asserting sovereignty over the strait and generating revenue from it. Shipowners and charterers have already agreed to pay, according to Le Temps.

Geneva is not just listening to this news. Geneva's commodity trading houses — among the largest in the world by volume — are absorbing the cost. The Hormuz strait carries approximately 20-25% of global oil consumption. Any toll, any insurance premium increase, any deterrence to transit, flows directly into the P&L of Geneva-based trading houses that move physical oil.

This is not a one-time event. It is a structural cost addition to a business model built on thin margins and high volume. The Geneva trading community has lived through sanctions regimes, shipping insurance volatility, and geopolitical risk premiums before. The Hormuz toll adds another layer — one that is durable, because it is rooted in sovereign assertion, not temporary disruption.

For Geneva as a commodity hub, the question is not whether the toll will stick. It is whether the trading houses have priced it correctly into their forward contracts.

---

### FR Version
**Titre:** Le péage d'Ormuz est réel. Genève a déjà payé la prime.

L'Iran met en œuvre un péage au détroit d'Ormuz — le point de passage pétrolier le plus stratégique au monde — sur le modèle des vignettes routières françaises et italiennes. Téhéran affirme sa souveraineté sur le détroit et en tire des revenus. Les armateurs et affréteurs ont déjà accepté de payer, selon Le Temps.

Genève ne se contente pas d'écouter cette nouvelle. Les maisons de trading de matières premières genevoises — parmi les plus importantes au monde en volume — absorbent le coût. Le détroit d'Ormuz transporte environ 20 à 25 % de la consommation pétrolière mondiale. Tout péage, toute hausse de prime d'assurance, tout frein au transit, entre directement dans le résultat des maisons de trading basées à Genève qui commercialisent le pétrole physique.

Ce n'est pas un événement ponctuel. C'est un coût structurel ajouté à un modèle économique fondé sur des marges minces et des volumes élevés.

## Draft Article 03
**Target:** DigitalCoa.ch | **Cluster:** AML / Compliance | **TIER:** TIER 1

### EN Version
**Headline:** FinCen's MBaer Action: What US AML Enforcement Means for Swiss Board Governance

**Subhead:** The US Financial Crimes Enforcement Network's action against MBaer Merchant Bank is not just a enforcement event. It is a governance signal for every Swiss board with international banking relationships.

FinCen filed to exclude MBaer Merchant Bank from the US financial system approximately one month ago, citing systematic money laundering facilitation for Venezuelan oil traffickers, Russian oligarchs, and Iran's Revolutionary Guard. Mike Baer — descendant of the Julius Baer dynasty — founded the bank in 2018.

For Swiss corporate boards — particularly those with banking, wealth management, or asset management operations — the case carries several governance implications:

**US enforcement reach is structural, not selective.** FinCen acted against a Swiss-connected institution, not a US domestic one. The jurisdictional reach of US AML enforcement is now explicitly documented against a European family-name institution.

**Family name provides no insulation.** The MBaer case shows that corporate distance from a legacy brand does not provide regulatory insulation. The enforcement targeted a separate entity with a shared family name.

**Documentation standards are now the floor, not the ceiling.** Swiss institutions with any US correspondent banking relationships should treat FinCen's action as establishing the documentation standard that US regulators consider minimum viable.

- La portée de l'application américaine est structurelle, non sélective
- Le nom de famille n'offre aucune isolation réglementaire
- Les normes de documentation sont désormais le plancher, pas le plafond

## Draft Article 04
**Target:** DigitalCoa.ch | **Cluster:** Commodity Trading / Risk | **TIER:** TIER 2

### EN Version
**Headline:** Hormuz Toll and Geneva Commodity Trading — Executive Risk Assessment

**Subhead:** Iran's strait toll is not a shipping story. It is a Geneva commodity trading story with direct P&L implications.

Iran is implementing a Hormuz strait toll — the world's most critical oil transit chokepoint — modelled on French and Italian highway vignettes. According to Le Temps, clients have already agreed to pay. Tehran is asserting sovereignty and generating revenue simultaneously.

For Geneva-international companies with commodity trading exposure, the risk vectors are:

1. **Direct toll cost** — per-barrel cost addition to any oil moving through Hormuz
2. **Insurance premium increase** — transit through a tolled, geopolitically contested strait increases war risk premiums
3. **Contract renegotiation risk** — FOB/CIF contract terms may shift depending on who bears Hormuz transit cost
4. **Supply chain routing** — some oil may route around the Cape of Good Hope, increasing delivery times and costs

Geneva commodity houses have navigated sanctions, sanctions evasion, war risk premiums, and geopolitical disruption repeatedly. The Hormuz toll is a structural addition to the cost base, not a temporary shock.

**Executive action:** Review forward contracts for Hormuz-exposed positions; confirm war risk insurance coverage adequacy; assess pricing adjustments in FOB contracts.

---

### FR Version
**Titre:** Péage d'Ormuz et trading de matières premières à Genève — Évaluation des risques pour dirigeants

Le péage d'Ormuz n'est pas une histoire maritime. C'est une histoire de trading de matières premières genevoise avec des implications directes sur le résultat.

Pour les entreprises internationales genevoises exposées au trading de matières premières, les vecteurs de risque sont:
1. Coût direct du péage
2. Augmentation des primes d'assurance
3. Risque de renégociation des contrats
4. Reroutage de la chaîne d'approvisionnement

**Action exécutive:** Examiner les contrats à terme pour les positions exposées à Ormuz; confirmer l'adéquation de la couverture d'assurance risque guerre.
