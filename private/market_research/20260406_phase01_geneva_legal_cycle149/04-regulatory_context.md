# 04 — Regulatory Context | Geneva Legal | C149 Phase 01

## Active Regulatory Framework

### nFADP Art. 6 — Cross-Border Data Transfers
- **Instrument:** Revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP), effective 2023-2024
- **Requirement:** Adequacy decision OR Safeguards+ (SCCs + Transfer Impact Assessment) OR specific derogation for personal data transfers to countries without equivalent protection
- **Swiss adequacy status:** EU (adequate); UK (adequate); US (no adequacy — Data Privacy Framework partial remedy only); China, India, Singapore (no adequacy)
- **Geneva exposure:** US cloud vendor transfers (AWS, Microsoft, Google) — latent unaddressed exposure; FDPIC enforcement posture increasing H2 2026
- **Key authority:** FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)
- **Citation:** Art. 6 nFADP; FDPIC guidance 2023-2024

### Basel IV AI Model Risk — FINMA Supervisory Expectations
- **Instrument:** Basel III Finalized reforms (Basel IV), effective January 2025 for Swiss banks under FINMA scope
- **IRB approach:** Internal Ratings-Based approach for credit risk requires documented model governance for AI/ML credit scoring models
- **FRTB:** Fundamental Review of the Trading Book — AI market risk models subject to model documentation requirements
- **FINMA expectation:** BCBS239-aligned model risk management; conceptual soundness documentation; independent validation; backtesting
- **Geneva exposure:** AI credit/lending models at Geneva banks not yet documented to FINMA standards
- **Key authority:** FINMA; Basel Committee on Banking Supervision
- **Citation:** Basel III Finalized Framework (December 2017); FINMA Circular on model risk management

### Swiss Climate Due Diligence Directive (SDD) — Supply Chain Obligations
- **Instrument:** Swiss Climate and Innovation Act (OCS, in force January 2024) + Federal Council implementing ordinance (expected H2 2026)
- **Supply chain scope:** Companies subject to OCS climate disclosure + EUDR (deforestation) + Art. 964j OR (human rights due diligence) simultaneously
- **Geneva exposure:** Commodity trading houses and manufacturing companies with supply chain exposure; OCAN/SGE cantonal enforcement
- **Key authority:** Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN); Canton Geneva OCAN/SGE
- **Timing:** Compliance planning window open now; implementing ordinance deadline est. late 2026

### SICC — Swiss International Commercial Court (Geneva)
- **Instrument:** Federal Act on the Swiss International Commercial Court (SICCA); Code of Civil Procedure Art. 179-191
- **Seat:** Geneva
- **Governing law:** Swiss law (mandatory)
- **Procedural language:** English
- **Jurisdiction:** International commercial disputes CHF 500K–50M; parties can opt-in above/below range
- **Geneva significance:** SICC seated in Geneva creates distinct three-track dispute resolution landscape with Geneva Commercial Court and ICC/SCAI arbitration
- **Key authority:** SICC Geneva; Federal Supreme Court supervisory appeal
- **Citation:** SR 274.2; CPC Art. 179-191

### BGer Digital Asset / Smart Contract Rulings 2025-2026
- **Topic:** Proprietary trading desk digital asset custody; smart contract enforceability (OR Art. 1); SchKG Art. 37 crypto as collateral; tokenized asset treatment under Swiss law
- **Key authority:** Swiss Federal Supreme Court (Bundesgericht)
- **Geneva significance:** Rulings directly relevant to Geneva commodity trading blockchain trade finance applications
- **Status:** Body of jurisprudence maturing — advisory positioning now viable

### GIG/LEG + nFADP — AI Employment Tools
- **Gender Equality Act (GIG/LEG):** Art. 3 (indirect discrimination prohibition); Art. 13 (burden of proof reversal in discrimination claims)
- **nFADP:** Art. 9 (sensitive data/profiling); Art. 6 (cross-border transfer for employee data); general data processing principles
- **Federal guidance gap:** No comprehensive Federal Council guidance on AI in employment contexts (2026 reform signals pending)
- **Geneva cantonal activity:** Commission for Equality of Women and Men Geneva (CMSHG) — active scrutiny of algorithmic discrimination in hiring
- **Key authority:** EBG (Federal Office for Equality); Geneva CMSHG; FDPIC
- **Citation:** GIG SR 151.1; nFADP SR 235.1
