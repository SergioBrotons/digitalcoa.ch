# Phase 01: Geneva Legal — Regulatory Context
# Cycle 16 — 2026-03-30

## Regulatory Context

### 1. nFADP (Swiss Data Protection Act) — Active Since 2023
**Status**: ACTIVE NOW
**Key provision**: Art. 5(c) — automated decision-making/profiling requiring DPIA and human review mechanism
**FDPIC confirmation**: FADP is technology-neutral and directly applicable to AI-based data processing (confirmed multiple times)
**Penalty mechanism**: No direct FDPIC fines; criminal prosecution route for serious violations
**Obligation for law firms**: If AI makes decisions about individuals (clients, matters, billing) based solely on automated processing → DPIA + human review mechanism required now

### 2. EU AI Act GPAI — Active Since August 2, 2025
**Status**: ACTIVE NOW (~8.5 months live)
**Obligations already live**: Technical documentation, downstream user transparency, training data summaries
**High-risk obligations**: Activate August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months away)
**Swiss nexus**: Firms with EU clients, EU-facing operations, or EU market-intended outputs face immediate obligations
**Geneva relevance**: Many Geneva law firms have EU client relationships → GPAI likely applies

### 3. Federal Council AI Regulatory Strategy — Announced February 12, 2025
**Lean approach confirmed**: Targeted sector-specific adjustments, NOT a comprehensive horizontal AI law
**Timeline**:
  - Formal consultation draft (CoE AI Convention implementation): end-2026
  - Multi-stakeholder "AI Action Plan for Switzerland" First Draft: May 2026 (Digital Switzerland initiative with Federal Councillor Albert Rösti)
**Key implication**: No cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026 at earliest; sector-specific approach through existing regulatory bodies

### 4. FDJP Consultation Draft — End-2026
**Scope**: CoE AI Convention implementation
**Focus areas**: Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
**Timeline**: Consultation draft expected end-2026
**Geneva relevance**: Will affect all Swiss businesses using AI, including law firms

### 5. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)
**Status**: Hard deadline — NO grace period for high-risk SaMD
**Obligation**: AI diagnostic imaging and AI symptom triage systems face immediate obligations
**Spillover effect**: Raises overall AI governance awareness in Geneva professional services; law firms using AI diagnostic tools directly affected

### 6. ANBLES/OFL Bar Guidance — Silent
**Status**: No AI guidance issued by Swiss (ANBLES) or Geneva (OFL) bar associations
**Impact**: Law firms have no bar association support on AI governance obligations
**White space**: Confirmed as uncontested advisory territory

### 7. FDPIC Enforcement Portfolio
**Status**: Active — 4 AI investigations 2025-2026
**Cases**: Transport company (Feb 2026), joint statement (Feb 2026), X/Twitter (Mar 2025), Coop (Oct 2025)
**Law firm targeting**: NOT YET — pattern established, enforcement building across sectors
**Enforcement gap**: Window ~12–18 months before law firm targeting likely
**Penalty architecture**: No direct fines; criminal prosecution route only (lowers near-term enforcement probability but doesn't eliminate risk)

### 8. ABA AI Guidance — March 2026
**Signal**: US bar guidance signals global norm acceleration for attorney AI obligations
**Geneva relevance**: Cross-jurisdictional expectation-setting; Swiss attorneys increasingly measured against international standards

### 9. Geneva AI and Law Ecosystem
**University of Geneva Law Faculty**: Hosting conferences on AI and law
**GCSP Geneva Process on AI Principles**: Defense/military AI principles — raises Geneva's AI governance profile
**Implication**: Geneva attorneys are in a high-awareness environment for AI issues — receptivity to governance frameworks increased
