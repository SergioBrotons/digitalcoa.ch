# Phase 01: Geneva Legal — ICP
# Cycle 16 — 2026-03-30

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary Segment
Small law firms in Geneva: 2–5 attorneys, independent or small partnership, no in-house compliance team.

### Core Problem
They are using AI tools (document review, research, client communication, case management) without a governance framework — and regulators are building enforcement portfolios with law firms as future targets.

### Timing Signal (FDPIC Enforcement Gap)
- FDPIC opened 4 AI investigations 2025–2026: transport company (Feb 2026), joint statement (Feb 2026), X/Twitter (Mar 2025), Coop (Oct 2025)
- None targeting law firms — pattern is clear, enforcement is building
- Window: ~12–18 months before FDPIC enforcement reaches Geneva law firm segment
- ANBLES (Swiss bar): silent on AI governance — no guidance, no toolkit, confirmed Cycle 16
- OFL (Geneva bar): no AI guidance, no toolkits — white space confirmed

### Regulatory Triggers
1. **EU AI Act** (Aug 2 2026) — GPAI transparency obligations ACTIVE NOW (~8.5 months live)
   → Firms with EU nexus face immediate obligations
2. **nFADP Art. 5(c)** — ACTIVE NOW (since 2023 for AI-based profiling)
   → Art. 5(c) triggered by AI used to make decisions about individuals based solely on automated processing
3. **Federal Council** — announced Feb 12, 2025: "lean approach" confirmed (sector-specific, NOT horizontal AI law)
   → Formal consultation bill: end-2026; Multi-stakeholder First Draft: May 2026 (Digital Switzerland initiative)
4. **FDJP consultation draft** — CoE AI Convention implementation, end-2026
   → Focus areas: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
5. **Swissdamed** (Jul 1, 2026 — ~12 weeks) — high-risk SaMD, no grace period
   → Spillover effect into professional services awareness
6. **ABA AI guidance** (Mar 2026) — global norm acceleration signal

### ICP Statement
Geneva small law firm (2–5 attorneys) using AI for document review, legal research, or client communication, with no formal AI governance policy, that may have EU nexus through clients or operations — and whose bar association (ANBLES/OFL) has issued no AI guidance.

### Addressable Market
~150–250 small law firms in Geneva (OFL registry; estimate 20–30% have AI exposure)

### Competitive Absence (10th+ cycle)
No boutique competitor addressing small law firm AI governance in Geneva.
Large firms (CMS, Pestalozzi, White & Case, Schellenberg Wittmer, Lenz & Staehelin) enterprise-only — now confirmed via FDPIC enforcement coverage.
Structural fact confirmed across 16 cycles.

### First-Mover Window
~12–18 months — stable across cycles.
FDPIC enforcement gap = positive signal (portfolio building, not ignoring).
ANBLES/OFL silence = white space (confirmed Cycle 16 — no new guidance found).
No boutique competitor = uncontested space.

### Key Refinements Cycle 16
- Federal Council "lean approach" explicitly confirmed (Feb 12, 2025 strategy announcement)
- TWO distinct timelines: (1) Federal Council formal consultation draft end-2026; (2) Multi-stakeholder "First Draft" May 2026 (Digital Switzerland)
- ANBLES silence confirmed Cycle 16 — no AI guidance found for 2026
- FDPIC penalty mechanism: no direct fines under nFADP; criminal prosecution route only — lowers near-term enforcement probability but not eliminates risk
- GPAI ~8.5 months live (Aug 2 2025 baseline; Aug 2 2026 high-risk activation ~4.5 months away)
- Swissdamed ~12 weeks = spillover effect into professional services awareness
