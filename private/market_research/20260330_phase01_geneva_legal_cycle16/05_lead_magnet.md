## Section 3: What You're Already Legally Obligated to Do

### nFADP Art. 5(c) — Active Since 2023
If your AI makes decisions about individuals based solely on automated processing (hiring clients, billing, matter assignment, case prioritization), you are already in violation without a documented DPIA and human oversight mechanism.

**Three things you must have now:**
1. A documented DPIA for each AI system processing personal data
2. A mechanism for affected individuals to request human review
3. Records of your AI governance decisions

*Note: Under nFADP, the FDPIC cannot impose direct fines — but certain violations may lead to criminal prosecution. Enforcement risk is real, even without fine mechanisms.*

### EU AI Act GPAI — Active Since August 2, 2025
If your AI has EU users, outputs intended for EU markets, or you're operating as an EU importer/distributor: your transparency obligations are already live.

**GPAI transparency obligations you may already owe:**
- Technical documentation for your AI systems
- Transparency to downstream users
- Summary of training data and model characteristics
