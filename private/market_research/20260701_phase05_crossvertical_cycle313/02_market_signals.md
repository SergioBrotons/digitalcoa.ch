## Cross-Vertical Market Signals — The Universal C313 Conditions

The market signals that appear across all four C313 verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) reveal a single structural event: the 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) was not a market pause. It was a market acceleration masked as a pause. While Geneva professionals were not actively attending to AI accountability, every accountability channel in every vertical restructured its question from tool-level (do you use AI?) to encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence (which decisions did the AI make, with what consent, with what supervision, with what authorization chain?). The Geneva professional who returned from the pause discovered the new question type is now universal across all four verticals.

## Signal 01: The Attestation Loop Is Closed in Every Vertical — and the AI Used to Answer the Question Is the AI Being Asked About

**What it is:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel (carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier) is now asking encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level questions about AI-assisted decisions. The Geneva professional is answering these questions by using AI attestation-completion tools (or AI assistants, or copy-paste from EMR exports) — and the AI tools used to answer the questions process the very data the questions ask about. The attestation loop is closed. The accountability framework reads through the loop.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** The carrier attestation asks which client matters between January 1 and June 30, 2026 involved AI tools, with what client consent, with what matter-level supervision. The managing partner enters the response into an AI attestation-completion workflow. The workflow processes client confidences through its AI. Article 321 CC reads through. The attestation response is now generically similar to every other firm's response (because every firm uses the same attestation-completion AI). The carrier reads past the generic to find matter-specific evidence. The matter-specific evidence is invisible in the generic response.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** The institutional DDQ asks which mandate decisions between [date range] involved AI portfolio construction tools, with what mandate agreement authority, with what oversight log. The managing director enters the response into an AI DDQ-completion workflow. The workflow processes mandate data through its AI. Article 9 nFADP reads through. The DDQ response is now generically similar to every other boutique's response (because every boutique uses the same DDQ-completion AI). The institutional client reads past the generic to find mandate-specific evidence. The mandate-specific evidence is invisible.
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** The client RFP follow-up asks which engagement deliverables between [date range] involved AI research or analysis, with what methodology disclosure, with what sub-consultant AI authorization. The managing partner enters the response into an AI RFP-completion workflow. The workflow processes client methodology data through its AI. Article 52 nFADP reads through. The RFP follow-up response is now generically similar to every other boutique's response. The client reads past the generic. The engagement-specific evidence is invisible.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** The medical liability carrier attestation asks which patient encounters between January 1 and June 30, 2026 involved AI tools (scribe, clinical decision support, drug-interaction, image analysis), with what patient consent, with what physician oversight. The physician enters the response into an AI attestation-completion workflow. The workflow processes patient confidences through its AI. Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP read through. The attestation response is now generically similar to every other physician's response. The carrier reads past the generic. The encounter-specific evidence is invisible.

**The market failure:** No existing service addresses the attestation loop specifically. The market offers policy templates, framework documents, legal opinions, and compliance checklists — all of which the ICP could produce before the question changed. The new question (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence) requires evidence records that did not exist before. The ICP is now answering the new question with the old answer pattern (policy + framework + attestation). The pattern no longer matches the question.

**Why this is universal across C313:** The accountability channel restructuring happened simultaneously in all four verticals. The Geneva carrier (legal), the Geneva institutional client (finance), the Geneva commercial client (consulting), and the Geneva medical liability carrier (healthcare) all shifted the question in the same direction (from tool-level to evidence-level) for the same structural reason (AI governance policies do not reliably predict AI governance capability). The shift is market-wide, not vertical-specific.

**The service implication:** The attestation loop is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It audits the attestation-completion workflow the Geneva professional uses (in any vertical), assesses whether that workflow processes patient / client / mandate / engagement confidences through vendors not separately evaluated by the requesting authority, evaluates whether the attestation response becomes generic (and therefore loses vertical-specific evidence), and produces a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to vertical-specific encounter evidence. The service is universal across verticals because the loop is universal across verticals.

## Signal 02: The Documentation Cadence Has Compressed From Annual to Multi-Per-Period — and the ICP's Documentation Cycle Has Not Compressed

**What it is:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel has compressed the documentation cadence from annual to multi-per-period (mid-year, quarterly, monthly). The ICP's documentation cycle has not compressed. The gap widens with every new cycle. Documentation dated within the carrier's audit period (6 months for mid-year check-in, 3 months for institutional follow-up, 3 months for engagement follow-up) is now the freshness standard. ICP documentation review cycles remain annual or longer.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** Carrier annual attestation + new mid-year check-in at month six. Some carriers now send monthly "AI use log" requests. The firm's documentation cycle is annual. Documentation from January 2026 is dated within the mid-year check-in window (June 2026). Documentation from 2024 is not. The gap widens every mid-year cycle. - **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** Institutional client annual DDQ + new mid-year questionnaire at month six. Some institutional clients now send quarterly AI governance updates. The boutique's documentation cycle is annual. Documentation from the prior DDQ response is dated within the mid-year window. Documentation from earlier mandates is not. The gap widens every mid-year cycle. Multi-mandate firms face coherence gaps (DDQ response from mandate A is fresh; DDQ response from mandate B is dated).
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** Commercial client RFP at proposal stage + new mid-engagement follow-up at months 3, 6, 9 of the engagement. The boutique's documentation cycle is engagement-anchored (proposal → mid-engagement → deliverable). Documentation from the proposal stage is dated within the mid-engagement window. Documentation from prior engagements is not. The gap widens every follow-up. Sub-consultant authorization documentation freshness gap compounds the engagement-level gap.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** Medical liability carrier annual attestation + new mid-year check-in at month six. The physician's documentation cycle is annual. Patient consent form from 2024 is outside the freshness standard. Patient consent form from January 2026 is within. The gap widens every mid-year cycle. Médecin remplaçant coverage period documentation (Convention de remplacement AI clause) is dated within or outside depending on coverage date.

**The market failure:** The ICP's documentation systems were designed for annual cycles. The accountability channels have moved to multi-per-period. The market offers compliance templates and policy documents designed for annual review. The market has not produced a documentation system designed for multi-per-period freshness. The ICP is now being asked to refresh documentation on a cadence the ICP's documentation infrastructure cannot support.

**Why this is universal across C313:** The cadence compression is identical across verticals. The accountability channels all decided that annual attestation is insufficient. The ICP's documentation systems have not been redesigned to match. The structural mismatch compounds because every new cycle adds a documentation demand the ICP cannot meet with annual-cycle infrastructure.

**The service implication:** Multi-per-period documentation refresh is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It audits the ICP's documentation cycle against the accountability channel's audit cadence (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and channel), produces a documentation cycle redesign that matches the audit cadence, and delivers the multi-per-period documentation refresh at the audit cadence required. The service is universal across verticals because the cadence compression is universal across verticals.

## Signal 04: The Swiss-Hosted Vendor Residency Fallacy Is Now Live in Every Vertical — and the Sub-Processor Chain Is the Actual Data Flow
