## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Patterns — The Post-Pause Restructuring

The regulatory environment that affects Geneva professional practices underwent a structural shift during the 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1). The shift was not a regulatory change (no new law was passed during the pause). The shift was an accountability channel change — every carrier, institutional client, commercial client, and medical liability carrier operating in Geneva restructured its question type during the pause. The regulatory framework the C312 ICP signed under is the same regulatory framework the C313 ICP must answer to. But the question the framework is now being asked to answer has changed structurally.

---

## Pattern 01: The Post-Pause Question Is Encounter-Level — and the C312 Answer Was Tool-Level

**The cross-vertical structure:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel (carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier) shifted the question from tool-level (which AI tools do you use?) to encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level (which decisions did the AI make, with what consent, with what supervision, with what authorization chain, on which specific patient / client / mandate / engagement?). The regulatory framework that governs the question is the same in C313 as in C312 (Article 321 CC / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63). The question the framework is now asking has changed.

**Legal:** The carrier's C312 attestation form asked: "Do you use AI tools in your practice? If yes, list the tools." The carrier's C313 attestation form now asks: "For each client matter closed between January 1 and June 30, 2026, identify any matter where AI tools were used. For each such matter, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the date and duration of use, (c) the client consent form covering the AI use, (d) the supervising attorney who authorized the AI use, (e) the matter-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." The Article 321 CC framework is unchanged. The question the framework is now asking is encounter-level, not tool-level.

**Finance:** The institutional client's C312 DDQ asked: "Do you use AI tools for portfolio construction, risk modeling, or automated rebalancing? If yes, list the tools." The institutional client's C313 DDQ now asks: "For each institutional mandate active between [date range], identify any portfolio decision where AI tools were used. For each such decision, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the mandate agreement authority for AI use, (c) the supervising portfolio manager who authorized the AI use, (d) the mandate-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." The Article 9 nFADP framework is unchanged. The question the framework is now asking is mandate-level, not tool-level.

**Consulting:** The commercial client's C312 RFP questionnaire asked: "Do you use AI tools for research, analysis, or deliverable generation? If yes, list the tools." The commercial client's C313 RFP follow-up now asks: "For each engagement deliverable produced between [date range], identify any deliverable where AI tools were used. For each such deliverable, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the engagement letter authority for AI use, (c) the supervising consultant who authorized the AI use, (d) the engagement-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." The Article 52 nFADP framework is unchanged. The question the framework is now asking is engagement-level, not tool-level.

**Healthcare:** The medical liability carrier's C312 attestation form asked: "Do you use AI tools (scribe, clinical decision support, drug-interaction, image analysis) in your practice? If yes, list the tools." The medical liability carrier's C313 attestation form now asks: "For each patient encounter between January 1 and June 30, 2026, identify any encounter where AI tools were used. For each such encounter, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the patient consent form covering the AI use, (c) the supervising physician who authorized the AI use, (d) the encounter-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." The Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP framework is unchanged. The question the framework is now asking is encounter-level, not tool-level.

**The cross-vertical implication:** The C313 ICP cannot answer the post-pause question with the C312 answer pattern. The C312 answer (tool-level disclosure, policy documentation, framework compliance) does not address the post-pause question (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence with authorization chain documentation). The regulatory framework is the same. The answer pattern must change.

## Pattern 02: The Post-Pause Cadence Is Multi-Per-Period — and the C312 Cadence Was Annual

**The cross-vertical structure:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel has compressed the documentation cadence from annual to multi-per-period (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and channel). The regulatory framework does not specify a documentation cadence — the cadence is set by the accountability channel. The cadence has shifted in every vertical simultaneously.

**Legal:** The carrier's C312 attestation cadence was annual (at policy renewal). The carrier's C313 attestation cadence is now annual + mid-year check-in at month six. Some carriers now send monthly "AI use log" requests during peak renewal windows. The mid-year check-in asks the same encounter-level questions as the annual attestation, with the documentation dated within the prior six months. The Article 321 CC framework does not specify a cadence — the cadence is set by the carrier.

**Finance:** The institutional client's C312 DDQ cadence was annual (at mandate renewal or annual review). The institutional client's C313 DDQ cadence is now annual + mid-year questionnaire at month six. Some institutional clients now send quarterly AI governance updates. The mid-year questionnaire asks the same mandate-level questions as the annual DDQ, with the documentation dated within the prior six months. The Article 9 nFADP framework does not specify a cadence — the cadence is set by the institutional client.

**Consulting:** The commercial client's C312 RFP questionnaire cadence was at proposal stage (per engagement). The commercial client's C313 RFP questionnaire cadence is now at proposal stage + mid-engagement follow-up at months 3, 6, 9 of the engagement. Some commercial clients now send monthly AI use updates during long engagements. The mid-engagement follow-up asks the same engagement-level questions as the proposal stage, with the documentation dated within the prior three months. The Article 52 nFADP framework does not specify a cadence — the cadence is set by the commercial client.

**Healthcare:** The medical liability carrier's C312 attestation cadence was annual (at policy renewal). The medical liability carrier's C313 attestation cadence is now annual + mid-year check-in at month six. The mid-year check-in asks the same encounter-level questions as the annual attestation, with the patient consent form dated within the prior six months and the AI tool inventory dated within the prior three months. The Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP framework does not specify a cadence — the cadence is set by the medical liability carrier.

**The cross-vertical implication:** The C313 ICP's documentation cycle must match the accountability channel's audit cadence. The C312 documentation cycle (annual) does not match the C313 audit cadence (multi-per-period). The mismatch is structural and widening with every cycle. The regulatory framework does not impose a documentation cadence — the accountability channel does.

## Pattern 03: The Post-Pause Authorization Chain Is Split — and the C312 Authorization Chain Was a Single Event

**The cross-vertical structure:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel has split the authorization chain into two separate events: (a) principal relationship authorization (already documented in C312 — employment contract / mandate agreement / sub-consultant engagement / Convention de remplacement); (b) AI use authorization (not documented in C312 — never separated from principal relationship authorization). The regulatory framework requires human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The C313 question is: can you demonstrate that authorization as a separate event from the principal relationship authorization?

**Legal:** The carrier's C312 attestation did not distinguish between the employment authorization (summer associate hired in June) and the AI use authorization (summer associate used AI on firm matters). The carrier's C313 attestation now distinguishes: "For each matter where AI tools were used, identify the supervising attorney who authorized the AI use." The summer associate's employment authorization does not satisfy the AI use authorization requirement. The Article 321 CC framework requires human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The C313 question is whether the authorization is documented as a separate event from the employment authorization.

**Finance:** The institutional client's C312 DDQ did not distinguish between the mandate authorization (new institutional mandate won in March) and the AI use authorization (portfolio construction AI used on mandate decisions). The institutional client's C313 DDQ now distinguishes: "For each mandate where AI tools were used, identify the supervising portfolio manager who authorized the AI use." The mandate authorization does not satisfy the AI use authorization requirement. The Article 9 nFADP framework requires human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The C313 question is whether the authorization is documented as a separate event from the mandate authorization.

**Consulting:** The commercial client's C312 RFP questionnaire did not distinguish between the sub-consultant engagement authorization (sub-consultant engaged in May) and the AI use authorization (sub-consultant used AI on client deliverables). The commercial client's C313 RFP follow-up now distinguishes: "For each deliverable where AI tools were used, identify the supervising consultant who authorized the AI use." The sub-consultant engagement authorization does not satisfy the AI use authorization requirement. The Article 52 nFADP framework requires human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The C313 question is whether the authorization is documented as a separate event from the sub-consultant engagement authorization.

**Healthcare:** The medical liability carrier's C312 attestation did not distinguish between the médecin remplaçant coverage authorization (Convention de remplacement for May coverage) and the AI use authorization (remplaçant used AI scribe on practice encounters). The medical liability carrier's C313 attestation now distinguishes: "For each encounter where AI tools were used, identify the supervising physician who authorized the AI use." The Convention de remplacement does not satisfy the AI use authorization requirement. The Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP framework requires human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The C313 question is whether the authorization is documented as a separate event from the coverage authorization.

**The cross-vertical implication:** The C313 ICP must document AI authorization as a separate event from principal relationship authorization. The C312 documentation did not separate these events. The regulatory framework requires human authorization — the C313 question is whether the human authorization is documented separately from the principal relationship event that triggered the AI access.

## Pattern 04: The Post-Pause Sub-Processor Chain Is the Data Flow — and the C312 Marketing Claim Was the Surface

**The cross-vertical structure:** In every C313 vertical, the accountability channel now asks for per-tool data residency attestation — not the vendor's own server location, but the per-tool sub-processor chain (authentication, telemetry, model inference, backup, parent-company cloud). The regulatory framework does not specify which layer of the data processing chain must be Swiss-hosted. The C313 question is: where does the data actually flow, through which sub-processors, with what professional accountability implications?

**Legal:** The carrier's C312 attestation asked: "Where is your AI vendor hosted?" (Swiss-hosted was the answer). The carrier's C313 attestation now asks: "For each AI tool used in client matters, provide: (a) the vendor's own server location, (b) the authentication sub-processor location, (c) the telemetry sub-processor location, (d) the model inference sub-processor location, (e) the backup sub-processor location, (f) the parent-company cloud location." The Swiss-hosted marketing claim does not satisfy the per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure. The Article 321 CC framework requires professional secrecy protection for client information — the C313 question is whether the professional secrecy protection extends through the entire sub-processor chain.

**Finance:** The institutional client's C312 DDQ asked: "Where is your portfolio construction AI vendor hosted?" (Swiss-hosted was the answer). The institutional client's C313 DDQ now asks: "For each AI tool used in mandate decisions, provide: (a) the vendor's own server location, (b) the authentication sub-processor location, (c) the telemetry sub-processor location, (d) the model inference sub-processor location, (e) the backup sub-processor location, (f) the parent-company cloud location." The Swiss-hosted marketing claim does not satisfy the per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure. The Article 9 nFADP framework requires data protection for mandate information — the C313 question is whether the data protection extends through the entire sub-processor chain.

**Consulting:** The commercial client's C312 RFP questionnaire asked: "Where is your proposal management AI vendor hosted?" (Swiss-hosted was the answer). The commercial client's C313 RFP follow-up now asks: "For each AI tool used in engagement deliverables, provide: (a) the vendor's own server location, (b) the authentication sub-processor location, (c) the telemetry sub-processor location, (d) the model inference sub-processor location, (e) the backup sub-processor location, (f) the parent-company cloud location." The Swiss-hosted marketing claim does not satisfy the per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure. The Article 52 nFADP framework requires data protection for client methodology — the C313 question is whether the data protection extends through the entire sub-processor chain.

**Healthcare:** The medical liability carrier's C312 attestation asked: "Is your EMR Swiss-certified?" (HIN-protected was the answer). The medical liability carrier's C313 attestation now asks: "For each AI tool used in patient encounters (scribe, clinical decision support, drug-interaction, image analysis), provide: (a) the vendor's own server location, (b) the authentication sub-processor location, (c) the telemetry sub-processor location, (d) the model inference sub-processor location, (e) the backup sub-processor location, (f) the parent-company cloud location." The Swiss-certified EMR marketing claim does not satisfy the per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure (and does not extend to the AI extensions at all). The Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP framework requires professional secrecy protection for patient confidences — the C313 question is whether the professional secrecy protection extends through the entire AI extension sub-processor chain.

**The cross-vertical implication:** The C313 ICP must audit the vendor's per-tool sub-processor chain against the professional accountability framework. The C312 documentation did not audit the sub-processor chain. The regulatory framework requires professional secrecy / data protection — the C313 question is whether the professional secrecy / data protection extends through the actual data flow, not just the vendor's marketing-visible layer.

## Pattern 06: The Post-Pause Vendor Succession Risk Is Live — and the C312 Vendor Terms Were Silent

**The cross-vertical structure:** In every C313 vertical, AI vendor succession events (bankruptcy, acquisition, service termination, regulatory pivot) are now live in 2026 — not hypothetical. The Q2 2026 precedents (US AI vendor Chapter 11, European AI vendor strategic review, US tech giant acquisition of Swiss AI vendor, US tech giant acquisition of US AI clinical decision support vendor) are public, named, and learnable. When succession happens, the data processing assets transfer to the successor controller. The professional accountability obligations (Article 321 CC / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy / Article 63 / physician personal license) do not transfer with the assets. The obligations stay with the Geneva professional. The C312 vendor terms were silent on this continuity gap.

**Legal:** A Geneva law firm's AI vendor (legal research AI, contract review AI, EHR AI feature) was acquired by a US tech giant in Q2 2026. The acquisition transferred the AI vendor's client data processing assets to the acquirer. Article 321 CC obligations remain with the managing partner. The acquirer did not inherit Article 321 CC obligations. The client consent form did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's C312 terms of service did not address Article 321 CC continuity on succession. The C313 question is: what succession terms should have been in the C312 vendor contract to preserve the managing partner's professional accountability protection?

**Finance:** A Geneva wealth management boutique's portfolio construction AI vendor was acquired by a larger vendor in Q2 2026. The institutional client's mandate agreement did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's C312 terms of service did not address Article 9 continuity on succession.
