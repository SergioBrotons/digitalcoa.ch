## Cross-Vertical Competitive Landscape — C313 White Space After the Pause

The competitive landscape across all four C313 verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) reveals a consistent pattern: the 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) shifted the accountability channels in every vertical simultaneously — and no competitor has built a service response to the post-pause question type (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence with vertical-specific accountability framework calibration). The white space is not about vertical expertise. It is about evidence architecture designed for the post-pause C313 question.

---

## What Exists Across Verticals

### Category 1: Vertical-Specific Compliance Consultancies (Pre-Pause Architecture)

**What they offer:** Regulatory compliance firms that serve one vertical — legal compliance firms, financial regulatory consultancies, healthcare compliance consultants. They produce documentation calibrated to the pre-pause question type (tool-level attestation, policy documentation, framework templates).

**Structural limitation:** Their documentation architecture was designed for the pre-pause question. The C313 question is encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence with the AI used to answer the question being the AI being asked about. Their deliverables (policy documents, framework templates, compliance checklists) do not address the C313 attestation loop. Their pricing model (project-based document delivery) does not match the C313 multi-per-period cadence requirement.

**Why this fails the C313 ICP:** The C313 ICP needs evidence that survives the new question type — documentation that demonstrates what the AI actually did, with what authorization chain, with what consent form freshness, with what sub-processor chain, with what vendor succession planning. Vertical-specific compliance consultants produce documents designed for the pre-pause question.

---

### Category 2: Enterprise AI Governance Frameworks (Pre-Pause Calibration)

**What they offer:** ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act compliance frameworks designed for large institutions with legal departments, compliance teams, and documented governance processes. Comprehensive and well-structured frameworks.

**Structural limitation:** They were designed for the pre-pause question type and for institution-scale ICPs. They require implementation resources the C313 ICP (2-50 person firm, 2-10 physician practice) does not have. They produce policy documents, not evidence records. They are English-language frameworks without Geneva French/English bilingual calibration. They do not address the C313 attestation loop, the C313 multi-per-period cadence, the C313 authorization chain handover gap, or the C313 named-peer benchmark comparison.

**Why this fails the C313 ICP:** The C313 ICP needs evidence production anchored to the post-pause question — not a framework that describes what should exist. The enterprise framework produces a document the C313 ICP puts on a shelf. The C313 ICP needs a service that produces evidence records continuously, calibrated to the post-pause question type.

---

### Category 3: Legal Firms (Pre-Pause Service Architecture)

**What they offer:** Legal opinions on AI governance obligations, contract review services, regulatory interpretation authority. Legal privilege protection and regulatory interpretation expertise.

**Structural limitation:** Legal firms produce legal interpretations — they describe what should exist. They do not produce evidence records. Their hourly billing model creates cost uncertainty. They are not positioned for continuous evidence production. They do not address the C313 attestation loop (the AI used to answer the question is the AI being asked about). They do not address the C313 named-peer benchmark comparison (which is not a legal opinion but a documentation specificity comparison).

**Why this fails the C313 ICP:** The C313 ICP needs someone who produces evidence records as an ongoing service — not someone who tells them what their evidence should look like once. The legal opinion is a document. The C313 ICP needs a documentation system.

---

### Category 4: Technology Vendors (Pre-Pause Risk Architecture)

**What they offer:** AI governance documentation embedded in vendor terms of service, compliance packages for enterprise clients, attestation templates aligned with carrier requirements. Direct access to model documentation and technical specifications.

**Structural limitation:** Vendor documentation serves vendor risk management — it describes the tool, not the ICP's use of the tool. Vendor terms of service do not satisfy carrier or institutional client or commercial client or medical liability carrier evidence requirements. Vendor attestation templates create false compliance confidence. Vendor documentation does not address the C313 sub-processor chain audit (the vendor does not disclose its own sub-processors in a form that maps to professional accountability frameworks). Vendor documentation does not address the C313 vendor succession planning (the vendor's terms of service do not include professional accountability continuity clauses).

**Why this fails the C313 ICP:** The C313 ICP needs independent assessment — evidence that is not produced by the party whose interests are served by the ICP believing they are compliant. The vendor cannot be the ICP's evidence producer.

---

### Category 5: Insurance Brokers (Pre-Pause Channel Architecture)

**What they offer:** Carrier attestation form preparation, coverage continuity guidance, AI risk language for insurance renewal. Carrier relationships and insurance renewal process expertise.

**Structural limitation:** Broker services are structurally constrained by carrier relationships — their advice must not contradict carrier positions. They cannot provide independent evidence architecture. They cannot map multi-standard compliance simultaneously. They do not address the C313 named-peer benchmark comparison (the broker does not track Geneva conference circuit publications). They do not address the C313 vendor succession planning (the broker does not audit vendor contracts). They do not address the C313 authorization chain handover gap (the broker does not document employment / mandate / engagement / coverage authorization for AI use).

**Why this fails the C313 ICP:** The broker's interest is coverage continuity. The ICP's interest is personal accountability protection and evidence production. These are not the same thing. The broker's coverage advice may increase the ICP's personal exposure while maintaining coverage.

### Category 6: Geneva Conference Circuit (C313 New Signal Source — Not a Competitor)

**What it offers:** Named-peer AI governance artifacts published at Geneva professional conferences. Public attestation language, consent form templates, Convention de remplacement AI clauses, DDQ response patterns, proposal methodology documents.

**Structural limitation (why this is not a competitor):** The Geneva conference circuit publishes named-peer artifacts — but does not provide a service to map the C313 ICP's documentation against the named-peer benchmark. The conference circuit is a signal source, not a service provider. The C313 ICP needs someone to monitor the conference circuit, identify the named-peer artifacts relevant to their vertical and practice structure, and produce a benchmark gap analysis. The conference circuit does not provide this service.

**Why this creates C313 white space:** The named-peer benchmark is now public. The C313 ICP can read named-peer attestation language, named-peer consent form templates, named-peer Convention de remplacement AI clauses, named-peer DDQ response patterns, named-peer proposal methodology documents. No competitor is providing a systematic service to map the C313 ICP's documentation against the named-peer benchmark.

## Cross-Vertical White Space: What's Missing Across All Verticals

### Missing Capability 01: Post-Pause Attestation Loop Closure Audit

**What the market offers:** Pre-pause attestation preparation (firm-level policy documentation, tool-level disclosure, framework templates).

**What's missing:** A service that audits the attestation-completion workflow the C313 ICP uses in any vertical — assessing whether the AI tool used to answer the post-pause question is the AI being asked about, evaluating whether the attestation response becomes generic (and therefore loses vertical-specific evidence), and producing a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to vertical-specific evidence.

**Why it's missing:** The attestation loop is a C313 phenomenon. The pre-pause question type did not create the loop (the pre-pause question asked tool-level disclosure, which the ICP could answer manually). The post-pause question type (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence) creates the loop because the ICP cannot manually produce the volume of evidence required — and uses AI to do so, closing the loop. No competitor built a service for the post-pause loop because the post-pause question type did not exist before the pause.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that audits the attestation-completion workflow the C313 ICP uses, evaluates whether the AI tool used to answer the question is the AI being asked about, and produces a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to vertical-specific evidence.

---

### Missing Capability 02: Multi-Per-Period Documentation Refresh Service

**What the market offers:** Annual documentation review and update. Policy document refresh at annual cycle.

**What's missing:** A documentation refresh service calibrated to the post-pause multi-per-period cadence (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and accountability channel). The refresh service is triggered at each audit cadence, produces fresh documentation at the freshness standard required (6 months for mid-year check-in, 3 months for follow-up), and maintains a documentation cycle that matches the accountability channel's audit cycle.

**Why it's missing:** The pre-pause cadence was annual. The post-pause cadence is multi-per-period. The market offers annual services. No competitor has built a multi-per-period documentation refresh service because the post-pause cadence did not exist before the pause.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that delivers documentation refresh at the multi-per-period cadence the post-pause accountability channels require — not annual, not periodic, but at the audit cadence the channel demands.

---

### Missing Capability 03: Authorization Chain Handover Documentation System

**What the market offers:** Employment documentation / mandate documentation / engagement documentation / coverage documentation. Principal relationship authorization.

**What's missing:** AI authorization as a separate event from principal relationship authorization. The C313 ICP needs: (a) AI authorization documentation triggered at each principal relationship event (hire date / mandate start / sub-consultant engagement / coverage period start); (b) AI tool inventory update at each handover (the new principal may use different AI tools than the prior principal); (c) authorization chain handover documentation refresh at each audit cadence; (d) AI tool usage log calibrated to the new principal's relationship.

**Why it's missing:** The pre-pause question type did not require authorization chain documentation. The post-pause question type requires evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The authorization chain handover gap is structurally permanent in every vertical. No competitor has built a documentation system for AI authorization as a separate event because the post-pause question type did not require it before the pause.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that documents AI authorization as a separate event from principal relationship authorization — with template documentation for each vertical's principal relationship type, handover documentation triggers at each principal relationship event, and AI tool inventory updates at each handover.

---

### Missing Capability 04: Vendor Sub-Processor Chain Audit Against Professional Accountability Frameworks

**What the market offers:** General data protection reviews (nFADP / GDPR compliance documentation). Vendor security assessments (SOC 2, ISO 27001). Vendor security questionnaires.

**What's missing:** A systematic audit of vendor sub-processor chains against professional accountability frameworks (Article 321 CC for legal / Article 9 nFADP for finance / Article 52 nFADP for consulting / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 for healthcare). The audit identifies which sub-processors create extraterritorial exposure, assesses whether each sub-processor is compatible with the professional accountability framework, and produces a per-tool sub-processor audit memo.

**Why it's missing:** The sub-processor chain audit requires expertise in both professional obligations (what the ICP is accountable for under their professional framework) and vendor contract terms (what the vendor actually obligates the ICP to). This combination does not exist in any existing service. General data protection consultants don't know Article 321 CC or Article 321 CP medical secrecy. Legal firms don't know the operational context of vendor sub-processor chains in independent practice AI use. Technology vendors don't disclose their own sub-processors in a form that maps to professional accountability frameworks.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that maps vendor sub-processor chains against professional accountability frameworks — identifying extraterritorial exposure, assessing professional accountability compatibility, and producing per-tool audit memos calibrated to the C313 ICP's vertical-specific accountability framework.

### Missing Capability 05: Named-Peer Benchmark Monitoring Against Geneva Conference Circuit

**What the market offers:** General benchmarking surveys, peer comparison frameworks, industry guidance documents. None specific to Geneva conference circuit publications.

**What's missing:** A systematic monitoring service for named-peer AI governance artifacts published at Geneva professional conferences (legal — Ordre / SBA / Geneva Bar / OAF; finance — Geneva Private Banking Week / Geneva Family Office Forum / Swiss WM Association; consulting — Geneva Strategy Network / Geneva Management Consulting Council / Swiss Consulting Association / IMC Switzerland; healthcare — AMG Geneva specialty sections / FMH CME / SSMIG Journée / HUG Grand Rounds). The service maps the C313 ICP's current documentation against the named-peer benchmark, identifies specific gaps, and produces a benchmark gap analysis with remediation priorities.

**Why it's missing:** The Geneva conference circuit named-peer publications are a C313 phenomenon. The conferences have always published; the named-peer AI governance artifacts with public attribution are new in C313 (June 2026 cycle). No competitor has built a monitoring service for these publications because they did not exist before the pause.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that systematically monitors named-peer AI governance artifacts at Geneva professional conferences and maps the C313 ICP's documentation against the named-peer benchmark — across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare verticals.

### Missing Capability 06: AI Vendor Succession Planning Calibrated to Professional Accountability Continuity

**What the market offers:** Vendor contract reviews (general legal review of terms of service). Vendor risk assessments (security and compliance reviews). Vendor transition playbooks (operational handover documentation).

**What's missing:** A succession planning service that addresses the specific intersection of AI vendor succession and professional accountability continuity — the gap between data asset transfer and professional obligation transfer. **Why it's missing:** AI vendor succession with professional accountability continuity is a C313 phenomenon. The Q2 2026 precedents (US AI vendor Chapter 11, European AI vendor strategic review, US tech giant acquisition of Swiss AI vendor, US tech giant acquisition of US AI clinical decision support vendor) are recent. No competitor has built a succession planning service calibrated to professional accountability continuity because the precedents did not exist before the pause.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that plans AI vendor succession with professional accountability continuity — addressing the gap between data asset transfer and professional obligation transfer across Article 321 CC (legal), Article 9 nFADP (finance), Article 52 nFADP (consulting), Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP (healthcare).
