## Universal ICP Language Patterns — C313 Cross-Vertical Synthesis

The language patterns that hold across all four C313 verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) are not abstract similarities. They are the same six conditions, surfacing through different accountability channels (carrier attestation, institutional DDQ, client RFP, medical liability attestation), and converging on the same ICP moment: the Geneva professional who returned from the 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) and discovered that the market had not paused with them.

---

### Pattern 01: "The form is asking about the AI. The AI is filling out the form."

**Appears in:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** Carrier attestation asks which AI tools the firm uses. The firm enters the answer into an AI attestation-completion workflow. The attestation-completion tool processes client confidences through its AI. The carrier's form now asks about the encounter the AI wrote, the consent form the AI drafted, the supervision record the AI assisted — and the response was generated by the AI the form is asking about.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** Institutional DDQ asks which AI tools the boutique uses for portfolio construction, risk modeling, automated rebalancing. The boutique enters the response through an AI DDQ-completion workflow. The DDQ completion tool processes mandate data through its AI. The institutional client's questionnaire is now asking about the portfolio decisions the AI made, the vendor integration the AI assessed, the oversight log the AI wrote — and the response was generated by the AI the questionnaire is asking about.
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** Client RFP asks which AI tools the boutique uses for research, analysis, deliverable generation. The boutique enters the response through an AI RFP-completion workflow. The RFP completion tool processes client methodology data through its AI. The client's commercial gate document is now asking about the proposal the AI wrote, the methodology the AI documented, the sub-consultant the AI screened — and the response was generated by the AI the gate is asking about.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** Medical liability carrier attestation asks which patient encounters between January 1 and June 30, 2026 were processed by AI tools. The physician enters the response through an AI attestation-completion workflow. The attestation completion tool processes patient confidences through its AI. The carrier's attestation is now asking about the encounter note the AI scribe wrote, the consent form the AI assessed, the médecin remplaçant encounter the AI documented — and the response was generated by the AI the attestation is asking about.

**The structure of the problem:** The accountability channel (carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier) shifted in C313 from tool-level to encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level / encounter-level documentation. The shift was structural — the form is no longer asking "do you use AI?" The form is asking "which decisions did the AI make, with what consent, with what supervision, with what evidence, and can you demonstrate it?" The ICP is answering by using the same kind of AI the form is asking about. The attestation loop is closed. Article 321 CC (legal), Article 9 nFADP (finance), Article 52 nFADP (consulting), Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP + physician personal license (healthcare) all read through that loop.

**Cross-vertical universality:** This is not a tool problem. It is an accountability-channel problem. The carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier has restructured the question. The question is now evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions, not attestation that AI is used responsibly. The ICP is answering the new question using the old answer pattern (the AI-assisted attestation response). The response is now legally exposed by the same framework the question references.

**The implication for positioning:** The service is not "help me document my AI use better." The service is "break the attestation loop before the next attestation cycle makes it undeniable." The first Geneva AI accountability service to publish the attestation-completion vendor audit owns the C313 cross-vertical category.

### Pattern 02: "I thought it was once a year. It isn't anymore."

**Appears in:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** The carrier used to ask the AI attestation once a year at renewal. In C313, the carrier's mid-year check-in form asks the same questions — encounter-level, with consent form date and AI tool inventory — six months after renewal. The firm's documentation cycle is annual. The gap widens every mid-year cycle.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** The institutional client used to send the AI governance DDQ once a year. In C313, the institutional client sends the DDQ annually AND a mid-year questionnaire at month six. The boutique's documentation cycle is annual. The gap widens every mid-year cycle. Some institutional clients now ask at months 3, 6, 9 (pro-rated quarterly).
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** The commercial client used to send the AI governance questionnaire at proposal stage. In C313, the same client sends follow-up questionnaires at months 3, 6, and 9 of the engagement. The boutique's documentation cycle is engagement-anchored (proposal → mid-engagement → deliverable). The gap widens at every follow-up.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** The medical liability carrier used to ask the AI attestation once a year at renewal. In C313, the carrier sends a mid-year check-in asking for encounter-level AI use between January 1 and June 30, 2026, with patient consent form date and per-tool data residency attestation. The physician's documentation cycle is annual. The gap widens every mid-year cycle.

**The structure of the problem:** The accountability channel has compressed the documentation cadence from annual to multi-per-period (mid-year, quarterly, monthly). The ICP's documentation cycle has not compressed. The gap widens with every new cycle. Documentation dated within the carrier's audit period (6 months for mid-year check-in, 3 months for follow-up) is now the freshness standard. ICP documentation review cycles remain annual or longer. The freshness standard is unannounced in published templates.

**Cross-vertical universality:** The cadence compression is identical across verticals. The accountability channel has decided that annual attestation is insufficient. The ICP has not built the multi-per-period documentation system to match. The structural mismatch compounds because every new cycle adds a documentation demand the ICP cannot meet.

**The implication for positioning:** "Your attestation is annual. Their questions are not. The gap widens every six months." The service is not "produce more documentation." The service is "build the multi-cadence documentation system the new cadence demands."

### Pattern 03: "I authorized the coverage. I didn't authorize the AI."

**Appears in:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** The managing partner authorized the summer associate's employment. The employment authorization is documented (offer letter, employment contract). The summer associate's AI use is not documented. The summer associate uses AI on firm matters. The AI contributions to those matters are not documented. The carrier form asks for evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions. The authorization chain runs through the managing partner. The managing partner cannot produce evidence of authorization for the summer associate's AI use because the AI authorization was never separated from the employment authorization.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** The boutique managing director authorized the new institutional mandate. The mandate authorization is documented (mandate agreement, investment management agreement, IPS documentation). The portfolio construction AI use on the mandate is not documented. The AI makes allocation decisions on the mandate. The institutional client's DDQ asks for evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted portfolio decisions. The authorization chain runs through the managing director. The managing director cannot produce evidence of authorization for the AI use because the AI authorization was never separated from the mandate authorization.
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** The boutique managing partner authorized the sub-consultant engagement. The engagement authorization is documented (sub-consultant agreement, SOW). The sub-consultant's AI use is not documented. The sub-consultant uses AI on client deliverables. The AI contributions to those deliverables are not documented. The client's RFP follow-up asks for evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted deliverables. The authorization chain runs through the managing partner. The managing partner cannot produce evidence of authorization for the sub-consultant's AI use because the AI authorization was never separated from the engagement authorization.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** The Geneva physician authorized the médecin remplaçant's coverage. The coverage authorization is documented (Convention de remplacement). The remplaçant's AI use is not documented. The remplaçant uses the practice AI scribe on their encounters with the practice's patients. The medical liability carrier's attestation asks for evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted encounters. The authorization chain runs through the replaced physician. The replaced physician cannot produce evidence of authorization for the remplaçant's AI use because the AI authorization was never separated from the coverage authorization.

**The structure of the problem:** Authorization for the principal relationship (employment / mandate / engagement / coverage) is documented. Authorization for the AI use within that relationship is not documented. The accountability channel (carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier) has split the question into principal authorization (already answered) and AI authorization (not answered). The ICP is answering the AI question with the principal authorization documentation. The documentation does not satisfy the AI question.

**Cross-vertical universality:** The authorization chain handover gap is structurally identical across verticals. The new principal enters the practice / firm / engagement / coverage relationship. The new principal uses AI within that relationship. The AI authorization was never a separate event. The new principal's AI use is the old principal's personal liability. The cycle repeats every employment cycle, every mandate win, every sub-consultant engagement, every médecin remplaçant coverage period.

**The implication for positioning:** "You authorized the relationship. You didn't authorize the AI. The AI use is your personal liability. The handover gap is structural and recurring — not a one-time error to fix." The service is not "document the AI use of every new principal." The service is "build the authorization chain handover system that documents AI authorization as a separate event from principal authorization."

### Pattern 04: "The vendor is Swiss-hosted. The data isn't."

**Appears in:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** The Geneva small law firm uses a Swiss-hosted EHR or practice management vendor. The vendor's marketing claims "Swiss-hosted," "HIN-protected," "nFADP-compliant." The vendor's sub-processors include US-based authentication (often Okta, Auth0, Microsoft Azure AD), US-based telemetry (often Datadog, New Relic, Splunk), US-based model inference for AI features (often OpenAI, Anthropic, Microsoft Azure OpenAI), US-based backup (often AWS S3, Azure Blob). Patient / client confidences under Article 321 CC flow through these sub-processors. Article 321 CC reads through the Swiss-hosted marketing claim.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** The Geneva boutique wealth manager uses a Swiss-hosted portfolio construction vendor. The vendor's marketing claims "Swiss-hosted," "FINMA-aligned," "nFADP-compliant." The vendor's sub-processors include US-based authentication, US-based telemetry, US-based model inference for portfolio analytics AI, US-based risk modeling compute. Mandate data under Article 9 nFADP flows through these sub-processors. Article 9 nFADP reads through the Swiss-hosted marketing claim.
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** The Geneva consulting boutique uses a Swiss-hosted proposal management vendor. The vendor's marketing claims "Swiss-hosted," "GDPR-aligned," "nFADP-compliant." The vendor's sub-processors include US-based authentication, US-based telemetry, US-based model inference for proposal drafting AI, US-based document storage. Client methodology data under Article 52 nFADP flows through these sub-processors. Article 52 nFADP reads through the Swiss-hosted marketing claim.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** The Geneva physician uses a Swiss-certified EMR (HIN-protected). The EMR's certification covers the EMR software. The EMR's AI extensions — AI scribe (US-based), clinical decision support (US-based), drug-interaction checker (US-based), AI image analysis for sub-specialty referrals (US-based) — operate in US-based cloud AT REST. Patient confidences under Article 321 CP medical secrecy flow through these AI extensions. Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP read through the EMR certification.

**The structure of the problem:** The vendor's marketing addresses the most visible layer of the data processing chain (the vendor's own server, often Swiss-hosted, often certified). The vendor's sub-processor chain addresses the less visible layers (authentication, telemetry, model inference, backup, parent-company cloud). The ICP trusted the marketing claim without auditing the sub-processor chain. The sub-processor chain is the actual data flow. The accountability framework (Article 321 CC / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63) reads through the entire chain, not just the marketing-visible layer.

**Cross-vertical universality:** The Swiss-hosted marketing fallacy is structurally identical across verticals. The vendor has a commercial interest in the ICP believing the data is Swiss-hosted. The ICP has an accountability interest in knowing where the data actually flows. These interests diverge. The vendor's terms of service protect the vendor's interest, not the ICP's. The ICP has not audited the divergence.

**The implication for positioning:** "Your vendor is Swiss-hosted. Your authentication isn't. Your telemetry isn't. Your model inference isn't. Your backup isn't. Article 321 / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy reads through your vendor's marketing." The service is not "find a better vendor." The service is "audit the sub-processor chain your vendor didn't disclose."

### Pattern 06: "The vendor was acquired. The patient data transferred. My obligation didn't."

**Appears in:**
- **Legal (C313 Phase 01):** A Geneva law firm's AI vendor (legal research AI, contract review AI, EHR AI feature) was acquired by a US tech giant in Q2 2026. The acquisition transferred the AI vendor's client data processing assets — including the law firm's client confidences stored in the AI tool — to the acquirer. Article 321 CC obligations remain with the managing partner. The acquirer did not inherit Article 321 CC obligations. The patient consent form the patient signed did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's terms of service did not address Article 321 CC continuity. The succession gap is unnamed in the contract.
- **Finance (C313 Phase 02):** A Geneva wealth management boutique's portfolio construction AI vendor (portfolio analytics AI, risk modeling AI, automated rebalancing AI) was acquired by a larger vendor in Q2 2026. The acquisition transferred the AI vendor's mandate data processing assets — including the boutique's institutional client mandate data — to the acquirer. Article 9 nFADP obligations remain with the managing director. The acquirer did not inherit Article 9 obligations. The institutional client's mandate agreement did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's terms of service did not address Article 9 continuity. The succession gap is unnamed in the contract.
- **Consulting (C313 Phase 03):** A Geneva consulting boutique's proposal management AI vendor (RFP response AI, proposal drafting AI, proposal methodology documentation AI) filed for strategic review or was acquired in Q2 2026. The transfer moved the AI vendor's client methodology data processing assets — including the boutique's client methodology, sub-consultant relationships, engagement documentation — to the acquirer. Article 52 nFADP obligations remain with the managing partner. The acquirer did not inherit Article 52 obligations. The client's engagement letter did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's terms of service did not address Article 52 continuity. The succession gap is unnamed in the contract.
- **Healthcare (C313 Phase 04):** A Geneva physician's AI scribe / clinical decision support / drug-interaction AI vendor filed for Chapter 11 / strategic review / was acquired by a US tech giant in May 2026. The transfer moved the AI vendor's patient encounter transcript processing assets — including the physician's patient confidences — to the acquirer. Article 321 CP medical secrecy criminal obligations remain with the physician. The acquirer did not inherit Article 321 CP medical secrecy obligations. The patient's consent form did not authorize the acquirer. The vendor's terms of service did not address Article 321 CP continuity. The succession gap is unnamed in the contract.

**The structure of the problem:** AI vendor succession events (bankruptcy, acquisition, service termination, regulatory pivot) are now live in 2026 — not hypothetical. Multiple Q2 2026 precedents are documented across verticals (US AI vendor Chapter 11, European AI vendor strategic review, US tech giant acquisition of Swiss AI vendor, US tech giant acquisition of US AI clinical decision support vendor). When succession happens, the data processing assets transfer to the successor controller. The professional obligations (Article 321 CC, Article 9, Article 52, Article 321 CP medical secrecy, Article 63, physician personal license) do not transfer with the assets. The obligations stay with the Geneva professional. The Geneva professional is now criminally / administratively / professionally exposed for records in the successor's hands.

**Cross-vertical universality:** AI vendor succession is structurally identical across verticals. The data transfer mechanism is the same. The professional obligation continuity gap is the same. The Q2 2026 precedents (now public, now named, now learnable) make this a live exposure for every Geneva professional in every vertical.

**The implication for positioning:** "Your AI vendor was acquired. Your client / patient / mandate / engagement data transferred to the acquirer. Article 321 / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy stayed with you. The acquirer didn't inherit your obligation. Your consent form doesn't authorize the acquirer. I'm building the succession terms into your vendor contracts now — before the next exit event, before the next attestation cycle makes the exposure undeniable."
