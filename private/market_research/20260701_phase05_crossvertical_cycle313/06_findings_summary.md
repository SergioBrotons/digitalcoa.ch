**The finding:** The 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) was the moment every accountability channel in every Geneva vertical restructured its question from tool-level (do you use AI tools, which?) to encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level (which decisions did the AI make, with what consent, with what supervision, with what authorization chain, on which specific patient / client / mandate / engagement?). The AI tools used to answer the post-pause question process patient / client / mandate / engagement confidences through vendors not separately evaluated by the requesting authority. The attestation loop is closed. Article 321 CC (legal), Article 9 nFADP (finance), Article 52 nFADP (consulting), Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP + physician personal license (healthcare) all read through the loop.

**Mechanism:** Accountability channel (carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier) shifts question from tool-level to encounter-level → professional enters response into AI attestation-completion workflow → AI workflow processes confidences through its AI → AI workflow produces response → response submitted → response now incorporates AI-generated artifacts processed through a separately-disclosed attestation-completion tool → professional accountability framework reads through the loop → response becomes generic (every professional using the same workflow produces similar language) → requesting authority reads past generic language → professional-specific evidence invisible → attestation cycle fails on encounter-specific evidence.

**Implication:** Post-pause attestation loop closure audit is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It audits the attestation-completion workflow the Geneva professional uses in any vertical, assesses whether the AI tool used to answer the question is the AI being asked about, evaluates whether the response becomes generic (and therefore loses vertical-specific evidence), and produces a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to vertical-specific evidence. Universal across verticals.

**Ready-to-use language (cross-vertical):** "The form your carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier sent you is asking about AI use at the encounter / matter / mandate / engagement level. So you are answering the post-pause question by uploading the artifact the post-pause question is asking about. Article 321 CC (legal) / Article 9 nFADP (finance) / Article 52 nFADP (consulting) / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP (healthcare) reads through that workflow. I'm auditing the attestation-completion workflow you're using and showing you how to anchor the response to your specific evidence — before the next post-pause attestation cycle makes the loop undeniable."

### Finding 02: Post-Pause Multi-Per-Period Cadence — The Freshness Gap Widens Every Mid-Year Cycle

**The finding:** The 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) was the moment every accountability channel in every Geneva vertical compressed the documentation cadence from annual to multi-per-period (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and channel). The Geneva professional's documentation cycle has not compressed. The freshness gap widens with every new cycle. Documentation dated within the carrier's audit period (6 months for mid-year check-in, 3 months for institutional follow-up, 3 months for engagement follow-up) is now the freshness standard. Professional documentation review cycles remain annual or longer. The freshness standard is unannounced in published templates.

**Mechanism:** Professional's documentation cycle is annual → accountability channel compresses to annual + mid-year + quarterly → mid-year check-in / institutional questionnaire / engagement follow-up / medical liability check-in arrives 6 months after annual cycle → form asks for documentation dated within prior 6 months → professional's annual documentation is now dated within the window for the first cycle but outside for the next → gap widens every mid-year cycle → documentation dated 12+ months ago is structurally outside the freshness standard → professional is now structurally behind the freshness cadence.

**Implication:** Post-pause multi-per-period documentation refresh is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It audits the Geneva professional's documentation cycle against the post-pause audit cadence (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and channel), identifies the freshness gap between the documentation cycle and the audit cadence, produces a documentation cycle redesign matching the audit cadence, and delivers the documentation refresh at the audit cadence required. Universal across verticals.

**Ready-to-use language (cross-vertical):** "Your attestation / DDQ response / RFP response / consent form is annual. Their questions are not. The mid-year check-in / institutional questionnaire / engagement follow-up / medical liability check-in is asking for documentation dated within the prior 6 months. Your last cycle's documentation is dated within. Your cycle before that is dated outside. The gap widens every mid-year cycle. Going forward doesn't fix what's already outside the freshness window. I'm building the multi-per-period documentation system the new cadence demands."

### Finding 03: Post-Pause Authorization Chain Split — Relationship Authorized, AI Not

**The finding:** The 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) was the moment every accountability channel in every Geneva vertical split the authorization chain into two separate events: (a) principal relationship authorization (already documented — employment contract for legal / mandate agreement for finance / sub-consultant SOW for consulting / Convention de remplacement for healthcare); (b) AI use authorization (not documented — never separated from principal relationship authorization). The Geneva professional who authorized the new hire / new mandate / new sub-consultant engagement / new remplaçant coverage is now accountable for the AI use within that relationship — without a separate AI authorization event. The authorization chain handover gap is structurally permanent in every vertical.

**Mechanism:** New principal (summer associate / new institutional mandate / new sub-consultant / new remplaçant) enters relationship → principal relationship authorization documented (employment contract / mandate agreement / sub-consultant SOW / Convention de remplacement) → principal uses AI within the relationship → AI authorization never separated from principal relationship authorization → carrier / institutional client / commercial client / medical liability carrier asks for evidence of AI authorization as a separate event → evidence does not exist → principal's AI use is the old principal's personal liability → gap compounds annually / per mandate / per sub-consultant engagement / per coverage period.

**Implication:** Post-pause authorization chain handover documentation is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It produces: (a) AI authorization template documentation for each vertical's principal relationship type; (b) authorization chain handover documentation triggered at each principal relationship event; (c) AI tool inventory update at each handover; (d) authorization chain handover documentation refresh at each audit cadence. Universal across verticals with vertical-specific calibration.

**Ready-to-use language (cross-vertical):** "You authorized the relationship — the summer associate / new institutional mandate / sub-consultant engagement / médecin remplaçant coverage. You didn't authorize the AI. The summer associate / mandate portfolio manager / sub-consultant / remplaçant uses AI on firm matters / mandate decisions / client deliverables / patient encounters. The encounter notes / mandate decisions / client deliverables / patient encounters are in your records. The carrier's mid-year check-in / institutional client's DDQ / commercial client's RFP follow-up / medical liability carrier's attestation is asking about coverage period AI use. ### Finding 04: Post-Pause Sub-Processor Chain Is the Data Flow — The Swiss-Hosted Marketing Fallacy

The Geneva professional trusted the vendor's Swiss-hosted marketing claim without auditing the sub-processor chain. The sub-processor chain is the actual data flow. Article 321 CC (legal), Article 9 nFADP (finance), Article 52 nFADP (consulting), Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63 nFADP (healthcare) all read through the sub-processor chain, not the marketing-visible layer.

**Mechanism:** Vendor markets as Swiss-hosted / HIN-protected / FINMA-aligned / nFADP-compliant / Swissmedic-certified → professional trusts marketing claim → professional does not audit sub-processor chain → authentication, telemetry, model inference, backup, parent-company cloud operate in US-based / non-Swiss infrastructure → confidences flow through sub-processors at every AI-assisted decision → post-pause attestation asks for per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure → professional cannot produce the disclosure → professional's accountability framework reads through the entire sub-processor chain → exposure documented.

**Implication:** Post-pause vendor sub-processor chain audit is a discrete, named C313 cross-vertical service. It maps the vendor's per-tool sub-processor chain against the professional accountability framework (Article 321 CC / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63), identifies which sub-processors create extraterritorial exposure, assesses whether each sub-processor is compatible with the professional accountability framework, and produces a per-tool sub-processor audit memo with vertical-specific professional accountability compatibility assessment. Universal across verticals with vertical-specific calibration.

**Ready-to-use language (cross-vertical):** "Your vendor is Swiss-hosted / HIN-protected / FINMA-aligned / nFADP-compliant / Swissmedic-certified. Your vendor's authentication isn't.
