## ⭐ NET-NEW C63 Findings (vs. C62)

### 1. FDPIC AI & Data Protection Page — UPDATED September 24, 2025 (NET-NEW vs. C60-C62)
- **Source:** edoeb.admin.ch/en/ai-and-data-protection (published 24.09.2025)
- **NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles referenced FDPIC May 8, 2025 communication. The September 24, 2025 update is FRESHER and adds:
  - Face manipulation programs (AI-generated faces/images/voice messages of identifiable persons) must **always be clearly indicated** — new specific obligation
  - Such manipulation "may even be completely unlawful in a specific case due to prohibitions under criminal law" — criminal law dimension newly articulated
  - FADP requires greatest possible degree of control over personal data for AI-affected individuals
  - DPIA required for high-risk AI-supported data processing
  - Authoritarian-state AI applications (real-time facial recognition, social scoring) explicitly prohibited under FADP
- **Legal sector implication:** Geneva law firms must advise clients on the criminal law dimension of AI-generated content — a newly sharpened risk

### 2. Swiss Bar Association AI Guidelines — June 2024 (CONFIRMED in C63 research)
- **Source:** chambers.com, lextechinstitute.ch
- **Content:** Guidelines on Use of AI for Swiss Lawyers — adopted June 2024
- **Key provisions:** Professional secrecy obligations; data protection compliance; human oversight required for strategic decisions; AI tools acceptable for legal research and contract drafting with oversight
- **Implication:** Geneva law firms have an official bar framework to point to — creates two-track standard (bar guidelines + FADP + EU AI Act for cross-border work)

### 3. MME Legal AI Services — Active Geneva/Swiss AI Governance Advisory (CONFIRMED)
- **Source:** mme.ch
- **Services confirmed:** Strategic AI consulting/workshops; AI guidelines/governance frameworks; AI development/implementation/deployment advice; liability risk assessment; data protection/copyright/trade secret compliance; AI training sessions
- **Implication:** Swiss boutique firms already occupy the AI governance advisory space — competitive landscape confirmed

### 4. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — AI Expectations for Swiss Financial Institutions (NET-NEW detail)
- **Source:** chambers.com, whitecase.com
- **Content:** FINMA sets clear AI expectations for Swiss FIs — governance, risk management, outsourcing
- **Legal sector implication:** Law firms advising financial clients must factor FINMA AI expectations into AI governance mandates

### 5. CoE AI Convention — Consultation Draft End-2026 (CONFIRMED timeline)
- **Source:** chambers.com, getcasus.com
- **Switzerland signed March 2025; consultation draft amendments end-2026; expected to address transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight**
- **Legal sector implication:** August 2026 deadline = before CoE implementation — law firms need to advise on existing obligations, not future framework

### 6. FDPIC Joint Statement — February 23, 2026 (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** getcasus.com
- **Content:** FDPIC joined 60 national DPAs in joint statement on AI-generated images and privacy
- **Four principles:** (1) Robust protective measures against misuse; (2) Transparency requirements; (3) Effective and accessible erasure mechanisms; (4) Specific child protection
- **Implication:** Geneva law firms advising AI companies must address these four principles — compliance surface area broadens
