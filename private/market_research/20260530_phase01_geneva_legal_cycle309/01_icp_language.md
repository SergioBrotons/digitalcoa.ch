# ICP Language: Geneva Legal — C309 Phase 01

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems — C309

---

## Primary Language Register

The Geneva legal ICP at C309 speaks in a register shaped by a new accumulated experience: they have already tried to respond to the carrier attestation, they have already talked to peers, and they are now confronting a specific new problem — **the gap between what they produced and what the carrier actually needs**. The C309 ICP has moved beyond "am I exposed?" and beyond "what do I do first?" The C309 ICP is asking: "I built something — why is it not enough?"

This is the decisive language shift from C308 to C309.

---

## The "I Built Something" Language (What They Did)

The C309 ICP describes their AI governance response in terms of partial accomplishment:

- **"I wrote a policy"** — not "I built an evidence architecture"
- **"I did what they asked"** — the carrier attestation answered, but not the carrier's actual need
- **"I thought we were covered"** — policy produced, assumption of adequacy
- **"The form asked for things I couldn't explain"** — documentation gap visible at next renewal question
- **"I don't know what else to add"** — the managing partner who has already done something and doesn't know what's missing

The language is post-effort recognition of inadequacy. The ICP is not in discovery mode. They attempted a response and the response was structurally incomplete.

---

## The Carrier Form Language (What They Received at Renewal)

The C309 ICP describes the carrier renewal form in new terms:

- **"They asked different questions this year"** — evolution from initial attestation
- **"More specific than before"** — the carrier is calibrating its questions based on market learning
- **"They want details about specific matters"** — per-matter AI use evidence
- **"They mentioned vendor contracts"** — carrier now asking about DPA/review status
- **"I didn't realize the form was a specification"** — the carrier form as design document, not compliance checklist

The language reveals that carriers are raising their game faster than the ICP expected. The C309 ICP is discovering that their response was calibrated to last year's form, not this year's.

---

## The Associate Language (What They Now Know)

The C309 ICP has moved from "I didn't know" to "I now know and I don't know what to do":

- **"I know they're using AI tools"** — discovery replaced by ongoing awareness
- **"I can't tell them to stop"** — operational dependency on associate AI use established
- **"They know more about this than I do"** — managing partner acknowledges AI fluency gap with associates
- **"I need a way to oversee something I don't understand"** — supervision framework needed for AI-incompetent managing partners
- **"I signed something that makes me personally responsible"** — personal accountability now active, not theoretical

The language reveals that the C309 ICP has a competence problem alongside an accountability problem. They cannot personally evaluate AI output, but they are personally accountable for decisions made using it.

---

## The Privilege Language (What They're Afraid of Now)

The C309 ICP's privilege language has evolved from vague concern to specific fear:

- **"If I document the AI use, does that create a record that can be used against me?"** — documentation paradox
- **"The client didn't consent to the AI processing their matter"** — consent gap now actionable
- **"If there's a dispute, do I have to show them what the AI did?"** — disclosure fear in dispute context
- **"The vendor could be compelled to produce records"** — third-party vendor records as privilege exposure pathway
- **"I don't know where the AI output is stored or who can see it"** — data residency now a live concern

The privilege language has become operationally specific. The C309 ICP is no longer asking "could this be a problem?" They are asking "what do I do about the problem I now know exists?"

---

## The Documentation Paradox Language (What Stops Them)

The C309 ICP describes a specific paralysis:

- **"If I write it down, it becomes evidence"** — the documentation paradox that stops action
- **"I don't want to create a paper trail that shows what I didn't know"** — retroactive exposure fear
- **"I don't know what to write that doesn't make it worse"** — documentation content uncertainty
- **"The form asks for things that, if I write them down, could be used in litigation"** — carrier form vs. litigation record conflict
- **"I have to document, but documenting feels dangerous"** — unresolved documentation anxiety

The documentation paradox is the primary action blocker for the C309 ICP. The ICP understands intellectually that they need records, but experiences creating records as potentially self-incriminating. This is a distinct psychology from C307 (discovery paralysis) and C308 (sequencing paralysis).

---

## The Fee Agreement Language (What They Haven't Thought About)

The C309 ICP describes client agreements in terms of absence:

- **"I haven't changed the engagement letter in years"** — no AI clause in existing agreements
- **"I don't know what to tell clients about AI"** — no client-facing AI language
- **"If a client asks, I give a vague answer"** — no calibrated client response exists
- **"I'm billing for AI-assisted work — is that a problem?"** — fee agreement AI billing question now live
- **"My clients don't ask, but maybe they should"** — unexpressed client right-to-know

The fee agreement language is new at C309 — a distinct finding that was not surfaced in prior cycles. The commercial relationship with the client now intersects with AI governance in ways the ICP has not yet processed.

---

## The Broker Language (How They Found the Conversation)

The C309 ICP describes their path to service awareness:

- **"My broker mentioned it"** — broker as primary referral channel confirmed
- **"The broker asked questions I didn't expect"** — brokers raising AI governance at renewal conversations
- **"My broker said I should talk to someone about this"** — broker as active referrer, not passive distributor
- **"I didn't know brokers were asking about AI at renewal"** — broker involvement now visible to ICP

The broker language is notably active. The C309 ICP arrived at the service conversation through broker referral, not through content marketing, cold outreach, or self-discovery. The broker is the primary market channel.

---

## The OFL Silence Language (What They're Waiting For)

The C309 ICP describes OFL guidance absence:

- **"I thought the Order would tell us what to do"** — OFL as expected guidance source
- **"They haven't said anything"** — OFL guidance vacuum remains
- **"I'm waiting to see what they say"** — waiting posture as AI governance delay rationale
- **"Without guidance, I don't know what adequate means"** — "adequate" undefined by the bar
- **"The carrier is asking, the Order is silent — I'm caught between them"** — dual authority gap

The OFL silence language reveals a specific expectation that was not met. The ICP expected the Geneva bar to publish guidance that would resolve the "adequate" question. The silence created a waiting posture that the carrier's attestation renewal has now interrupted.

---

## The Geneva-Specific Language Markers (C309)

The C309 ICP uses Geneva-specific professional language that reveals their accumulated context:

- **"Article 321"** — Code Civil professional secrecy, now a live personal liability concern
- **"Ordre"** — OFL as the authority that hasn't spoken
- **"CCP"** — Code of Civil Procedure as litigation disclosure context
- **"nFADP"** — Swiss data protection law, increasingly cited in carrier questions
- **"Attestation carrier"** — the renewal form as the document that revealed what was missing
- **"Broker"** — the intermediary who brought the problem and the solution
- **"Adequate"** — the undefined standard that haunts every decision

---

## What the C309 ICP Does NOT Say

The C309 language reveals what they do not yet use:

- They do not say "evidence architecture"
- They do not say "documentation paradox resolution"
- They do not say "matter-level AI use record system"
- They do not say "supervision protocol for AI-incompetent managing partners"
- They do not say "carrier form as design specification"
- They do not say "disclosure-litigation conflict management"
- They do not say "fee agreement AI clause"
- They do not say "broker referral as primary channel"

The gap between C309 ICP language and service vocabulary is the positioning opportunity.
