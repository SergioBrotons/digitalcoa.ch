## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

### Primary Concerns (in their own language)

**On Professional Secrecy / Attorney-Client Privilege:**
- "Can I put client matters into ChatGPT without breaking professional secrecy?"
- "What happens to my data when I use AI — does it stay in Switzerland?"
- "My carrier expects me to attest that my AI tools are secure. What does 'secure' even mean?"
- "If I use an AI tool and it leaks client information, am I the one liable — or is it the vendor?"
- "The bar guidelines say three options. But internal deployment is too expensive. Outsourcing feels like handing over control. And consent forms — do clients even understand what they're signing?"

**On Bar Guidance:**
- "The SBA published guidelines in June 2024. But they're principles, not a checklist. I still don't know if I'm compliant."
- "There is no AI-specific legislation in Switzerland yet — so what am I even complying with?"
- "The Convention on AI was signed in March 2025, but implementation isn't until end of 2026 at the earliest. We're in a vacuum."

**On Personal Accountability:**
- "I am the one who signs off on technology decisions. If something goes wrong, it's my license on the line."
- "My carrier asked me to complete a PI attestation specifically about AI tools. I didn't know what to answer."
- "A Bundesgericht decision confirmed that external providers can be considered my 'auxiliary' — meaning I remain responsible for what they do with client data."

**On "Adequate" AI Governance:**
- "What does 'adequate' even mean for a two-person firm? I don't have a CTO."
- "Everyone talks about AI governance frameworks but they're built for large organizations."
- "I need something practical. Not a 50-page policy. Something that actually works at boutique scale."

**On Vendor Risk:**
- "The AI vendor says they're GDPR-compliant. But I'm a Swiss lawyer — is that enough?"
- "My carrier wants a DPA with every AI vendor. Most vendors don't have one ready."
- "What if the AI system makes a mistake in a contract? I'm still liable. The machine can't be disciplined."

**On Implementation Reality:**
- "I tried Claude for contract review. It was helpful but I spent more time checking outputs than if I'd done it myself."
- "Anonymizing documents before AI processing is time-consuming and counterproductive."
- "Where do I even start? Do I need local deployment? Is that realistic for a small firm?"

---

## Dominant Emotional Tone

- **Uncertain** — no clear legislation, only guidelines
- **Personally exposed** — they sign off, they carry liability
- **Time-poor** — small firm, no dedicated tech staff
- **Skeptical of vendors** — promises don't match small-firm reality
- **Seeking practical solutions** — not frameworks, but actionable steps

---

## Key Vocabulary They Use

| They Say | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "professional secrecy" | attorney-client privilege |
| "carrier attestation" | PI insurer AI compliance declaration |
| "SBA guidelines" | Swiss Bar Association AI use guidelines (June 2024) |
| "adequate governance" | whatever makes them sleep at night + satisfies their carrier |
| "data stays in Switzerland" | local processing, no US cloud, no GDPR-over-claim |
| "auxiliary" | the vendor/third party who processes data on their behalf |
| "attestation" | formal declaration to their insurance carrier |
| "compliance path" | one of the three SBA options: internal / outsourced / consent |
| "governance framework" | their internal AI policy (however informal) |

---

## What Triggers Action

1. Carrier asks for AI attestation → immediate pressure to document
2. A peer firm experiences an incident → fear of reputational damage
3. Client asks what AI tools are used → must have an answer ready
4. Bar association guidance shifts → they need to recalibrate
5. A vendor cannot provide a DPA → deal-breaker, immediate search for alternatives
