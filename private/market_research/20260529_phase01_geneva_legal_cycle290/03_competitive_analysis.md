## What the Market Has Not Produced

### Net-New Findings for Cycle 290

**Finding 1: No "Swiss Small Law Firm AI Compliance Starter Kit"**
The market has not produced a simple, affordable, implementation-ready package for small Swiss law firms that addresses:
- What AI tools are acceptable under the three SBA compliance paths
- How to document AI use for carrier attestation
- What a minimal but adequate AI governance policy looks like at boutique scale
- How to evaluate AI vendors for professional secrecy compliance

**Finding 2: No Swiss-Contextualized AI Vendor for Law Firms**
Every major AI vendor operates globally. None has produced a Swiss French-language, Swiss-bar-aligned, professional-secrecy-specific product page, DPA, or positioning aimed at Swiss law firms. The trust gap is enormous.

**Finding 3: No Small-Firm Peer Reference Framework**
No one has documented and published anonymized case studies from Swiss small firms (2–5 attorneys) who have implemented AI safely. The market operates on fear, not positive reference. This is a content/positioning gap.

**Finding 4: No "Adequate at Boutique Scale" Standard**
No one has defined what "adequate AI governance" means for a 2–5 person firm. Large firm frameworks are inapplicable. There's an opportunity to define a practical small-firm standard that carriers and the bar could reference.

**Finding 5: No Attorney-Client Privilege AI Evaluation Rubric**
No tool or framework exists specifically for Swiss lawyers to evaluate AI tools against professional secrecy requirements. A simple rubric (data residency, DPA availability, no training on client data, Swiss-specific security certifications) would be immediately useful and shareable.

**Finding 6: No Synthesis Product for FADP + Professional Secrecy + AI**
The intersection of FADP (data protection), Article 321 CP (criminal professional secrecy), and FADP guidance on AI (May 2025) has not been synthesized into practitioner-friendly guidance for Swiss small law firms. This is a pure content/opportunity gap.
