### SECTION 2: Decision 1 — Are You Using AI Tools in Client Work?
**Question:** Does any consultant in your firm use AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Claude, Midjourney, etc.) for: client research, analysis, report drafting, presentation creation, email drafting, or any work product delivered to or used for clients?

**If YES:** Your firm has ACTIVE GPAI obligations under EU AI Act (operative August 2, 2025) AND nFADP obligations (operative since September 2023). Proceed to Decision 2.

**Key compliance reality:** These obligations are not future risks. They are active NOW.

**GPAI obligations that apply to your AI tool use RIGHT NOW:**
- Transparency: clients must be informed when AI is used in preparing deliverables
- Copyright: you must have processes to handle AI output copyright issues
- Incident reporting: serious AI failures must be documented and reported

---

### SECTION 3: Decision 2 — Do You Have EU Market Exposure?
**Question:** Does your consulting firm (a) have EU-based clients, (b) use AI tools with EU data centers or API endpoints, or (c) deliver work product into the EU market?

**If YES:** Your firm faces EU AI Act high-risk obligations by August 2, 2026 (~15 months). Start compliance process now.

**If NO:** Your EU AI Act exposure is limited to GPAI obligations already active. Focus on nFADP compliance for now.

**The IT/Tech Sub-Segment Alert:** IT consultants, software advisors, digital transformation consultants — your AI use is likely CLASSIFIED as high-risk under EU AI Act Annex III if your AI systems interact with: finance, healthcare, education, or critical infrastructure sectors. All prevalent in Geneva. GDPR/nFADP dual compliance is your baseline.

---

### SECTION 4: Decision 3 — Do You Have a DPIA for Your AI Tool Stack?
**Question:** Has your firm completed a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) under nFADP Article 22 for each AI tool that processes personal data (client names, contact details, business information, project data)?

**If NO or UNSURE:** This is your immediate compliance gap. nFADP Art. 22 requires a DPIA BEFORE using AI systems that process personal data.

**DPIA-Lite Framework for Small Consulting Firms:**

For each AI tool, assess:
1. **Purpose limitation:** Is AI used only for the stated purpose?
2. **Data minimization:** Does the AI access only data it needs?
3. **Storage limitation:** Is client data retained or deleted appropriately?
4. **Security:** Are AI tool access controls documented?
5. **Accountability:** Who in your firm is responsible for AI tool compliance?

### SECTION 5: The Dual Compliance Challenge — EU AI Act + nFADP

**Both frameworks apply simultaneously to Geneva consulting firms with EU exposure:**

| Obligation | EU AI Act | nFADP |
|---|---|---|
| Transparency | AI disclosure to clients | Privacy notice updates |
| Data protection | GDPR-equivalent for EU data | Swiss data protection rules |
| DPIA/impact assessment | High-risk conformity assessment | Art. 22 DPIA required |
| Incident reporting | Serious AI incidents to EU authority | Data breach notification |
| Human oversight | Required for high-risk AI | Best practice under nFADP |
| Documentation | Technical documentation required | Processing records required |

**The overlap:** A well-designed AI governance framework satisfies both EU AI Act and nFADP simultaneously. The key is a unified approach, not two separate compliance tracks.
