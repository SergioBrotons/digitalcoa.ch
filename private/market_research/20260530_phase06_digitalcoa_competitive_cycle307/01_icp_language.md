## Purpose

## Section A: English ICP Language

### The Problem-Naming Vocabulary

**When the ICP recognizes the problem, they use these phrases:**

- *"The carrier sent us a form"* — Problem appears as a document arriving, not as a strategic initiative launching. The form is the trigger event. The principal did not seek this; it arrived.

- *"I don't know what I'm signing"* — Attestation language creates personal exposure that the principal did not anticipate. The signature is the moment of personal commitment under Article 321 CC. The principal reads the form and understands they are signing something that affects their professional standing.

- *"My vendor said I'm covered"* — The vendor's DPA language creates a false sense of adequacy. The principal trusted the vendor's assurance. The carrier's form asks for something different from what the vendor provided.

- *"I have a policy, but I don't know if it means anything"* — Documentation exists (NIST framework, internal AI policy, vendor attestation). The principal suspects the documentation doesn't answer the carrier's actual question.

- *"No one ever asked me this before"* — The fourth-generation carrier attestation represents a new category of inquiry. Principals with 10+ years of practice have never seen a form asking about AI authorization chains. The newness is itself a signal.

- *"What does 'human oversight' actually look like?"* — The abstract principle (human oversight) and the operational reality (specific documentation of authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions) are not the same thing. The principal asks for the operational translation.

- *"I think I'm fine, but I'm not sure"* — Risk perception without documentation. The principal believes their practice is reasonable but cannot demonstrate it. The gap between belief and evidence is the problem.

- *"The broker mentioned it at renewal"* — Broker participation in the problem-formation moment. The broker surfaces the problem during a commercial conversation, creating urgency without solution language.

### The Urgency Vocabulary

**When the ICP moves to action, they use these phrases:**

- *"I need this before the renewal"* — Time compression as the primary urgency driver. The renewal conversation is the forcing function. The form has a deadline; the deadline is the renewal date.

- *"Can someone just read this form and tell me what it means?"* — The ICP wants interpretation of a specific document, not a general framework. The form is the artifact. The interpretation is the service request.

- *"The carrier asked for evidence and I don't have any"* — The evidence gap is now experienced as a gap, not just suspected. The carrier asked; the principal has nothing to show.

- *"My colleague had the same problem"* — Peer recognition as validation. The problem is not unique. The colleague's experience confirms the principal's situation is real and shared.

- *"I found your name through [broker/ORDRE/chamber]"* — Referral vocabulary. The named referral is the highest-intent ICP signal. The principal used an institutional channel to find the advisor.

### The Purchase-Language Vocabulary

**When the ICP is evaluating solutions, they use these phrases:**

- *"Is this something I can actually do, or do I need help?"* — Self-assessment of operational capacity. The principal knows they may not be able to execute internally.

- *"How long does this take?"* — Time as the primary constraint. The principal has a running practice. The solution must fit within operational capacity.

- *"Will this actually protect me if the carrier asks?"* — Outcome-focused evaluation. The principal wants protection, not process. The question is: does this work when the carrier asks?

- *"Can you speak to my broker directly?"* — The broker relationship is part of the solution architecture. The principal wants the advisor and broker to coordinate.

- *"Do you work in French?"* — Language as a selection criterion for Geneva SMB principals. The French-language capability is a trust signal, not just a preference.

## Section B: French ICP Language / Langue ICP en Français

### Le Vocabulaire de Reconnaissance du Problème

**Quand l'IPC reconnaît le problème, il utilise ces expressions :**

- *"La caisse m'a envoyé un formulaire"* — Le problème se présente sous forme de document reçu, et non d'initiative stratégique. Le formulaire est l'événement déclencheur. Le titulaire n'a pas cherché cela ; c'est arrivé.

- *"Je ne sais pas ce que je signe"* — Le language de l'attestation crée une exposition personnelle que le titulaire n'avait pas anticipée. La signature est le moment de l'engagement personnel pursuant à l'Article 321 CC. Le titulaire lit le formulaire et comprend qu'il signe quelque chose qui affecte son activité professionnelle.

- *"Mon fournisseur m'a dit que j'étais couvert"* — Le language du DPA du fournisseur crée un faux sentiment d'adéquation. Le titulaire a fait confiance à l'assurance du fournisseur. Le formulaire de la caisse demande quelque chose de différent de ce que le fournisseur a fourni.

- *"J'ai une politique, mais je ne sais pas si elle vaut quelque chose"* — Une documentation existe (cadre NIST, politique IA interne, attestation fournisseur). Le titulaire soupçonne que la documentation ne répond pas à la question réelle de la caisse.

- *"Personne ne m'a jamais posé cette question avant"* — La quatrième génération d'attestations de caisse représente une nouvelle catégorie de demande. Les titulaires avec 10+ ans de pratique n'ont jamais vu de formulaire posant des questions sur les chaînes d'autorisation IA. La nouveauté est elle-même un signal.

- *"Qu'est-ce que 'la supervision humaine' ressemble concrètement ?"* — Le principe abstrait (supervision humaine) et la réalité opérationnelle (documentation spécifique de l'autorisation pour des décisions assistées par IA spécifiques) ne sont pas la même chose. Le titulaire demande la traduction opérationnelle.

- *"Je pense que tout va bien, mais je ne suis pas sûr"* — Perception du risque sans documentation. Le titulaire croit que sa pratique est raisonnable mais ne peut pas le démontrer. L'écart entre la croyance et la preuve est le problème.

- *"Le courtier en a parlé lors du renouvellement"* — Participation du courtier au moment de la formation du problème. Le courtier soulève le problème lors d'une conversation commerciale, créant l'urgence sans language de solution.

### Le Vocabulaire de l'Urgence

**Quand l'IPC passe à l'action, il utilise ces expressions :**

- *"J'ai besoin de cela avant le renouvellement"* — Compression temporelle comme principal facteur d'urgence. La conversation de renouvellement est la fonction contraignante. Le formulaire a une date limite ; la date limite est la date de renouvellement.

- *"Quelqu'un peut-il simplement lire ce formulaire et me dire ce que cela signifie ?"* — La demande de service est l'interprétation d'un document spécifique, et non un cadre général. Le formulaire est l'artefact. L'interprétation est le service demandé.

- *"La caisse a demandé des preuves et je n'en ai pas"* — L'écart de preuves est désormais vécu comme un écart, et non simplement soupçonné. La caisse a demandé ; le titulaire n'a rien à montrer.

- *"Mon collègue avait le même problème"* — La reconnaissance par les pairs comme validation. Le problème n'est pas unique. L'expérience du collègue confirme que la situation du titulaire est réelle et partagée.

- *"J'ai trouvé votre nom via [courtier/ORDRE/chambre]"* — Vocabulaire de recommandation. La recommandation nominative est le signal d'IPC le plus élevé. Le titulaire a utilisé un canal institutionnel pour trouver le conseiller.

### Le Vocabulaire d'Achat

**Quand l'IPC évalue les solutions, il utilise ces expressions :**

- *"Est-ce quelque chose que je peux vraiment faire, ou ai-je besoin d'aide ?"* — Auto-évaluation de la capacité opérationnelle. Le titulaire sait qu'il pourrait ne pas être en mesure d'exécuter en interne.

- *"Combien de temps cela prend-il ?"* — Le temps comme contrainte principale. Le titulaire a une pratique en cours. La solution doit s'adapter à la capacité opérationnelle.

- *"Cela me protégera-t-il réellement si la caisse demande ?"* — Évaluation axée sur les résultats. Le titulaire veut une protection, et non un processus. La question est : cela fonctionne-t-il quand la caisse demande ?

- *"Pouvez-vous parler directement à mon courtier ?"* — La relation avec le courtier fait partie de l'architecture de la solution. Le titulaire veut que le conseiller et le courtier coordonnent.

- *"Travaillez-vous en français ?"* — La langue comme critère de sélection pour les PME genevoises. La capacité en langue française est un signal de confiance, et non simplement une préférence.

## Section C: Competitive Language Patterns

### What Competitors Claim vs. What ICPs Actually Hear

| Competitor Language | ICP Interpretation |
|---|---|
| "AI governance framework" | "Another document I'll never finish reading" |
| "Comprehensive compliance solution" | "This sounds expensive and complex" |
| "End-to-end AI advisory" | "I don't know what this means for my Tuesday" |
| "Swiss market expertise" | "Generic claim — everyone says this" |
| "Boutique-appropriate methodology" | "What does that mean? Is it simplified or just expensive?" |
| "Implementation support" | "Will someone actually do it, or just tell me what to do?" |
| "Practical tools" | "Templates I already have that don't work" |

### The Language That Actually Works

| ICP Trigger Phrase | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| "Read your vendor contract and tell you what your signature commits you to" | Specific, bounded, answers the exact question |
| "Before you sign the carrier form" | Timing specificity — preventive vs. remedial |
| "The form your carrier sent you" | Document-specific, not framework-specific |
| "Evidence of what you actually did" | Contrasts with policy documents that describe intentions |
| "Written for the 4-person firm" | Scale-specific, not enterprise-generic |
| "Talk to your broker directly" | Confirms coordination with existing relationships |
| "In French, if you prefer" | Language confirmation removes a barrier |

### The Invisible Language Gap

**What the market uses:** "AI governance," "AI compliance," "AI risk management," "responsible AI," "AI ethics"

**What the market actually needs:** Evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions, documented in a format the carrier recognizes

**The gap:** "Governance" and "compliance" describe organizational intent. The carrier's form asks for organizational evidence. The market language doesn't distinguish between intent and evidence. The advisor who names this distinction in ICP vocabulary earns the category.

## Section D: The Three-Week Window — ICP Language of Urgency

### How the ICP Describes the Timeline

The Geneva SMB principal receives the carrier attestation form approximately 4–6 weeks before renewal. Their response pattern follows a consistent sequence:

**Week 1 (post-receipt):** Denial and self-assessment
- *"I'll figure this out myself"*
- *"I just need to organize what I have"*
- *"This probably isn't a big deal"*

**Week 2:** Attempted self-resolution fails
- *"I've looked at it — I don't know what they're asking for"*
- *"I have some documents but I don't think they're enough"*
- *"I asked my IT person and they didn't understand the form"*

**Week 3:** External help-seeking with compressed timeline
- *"I need this done before [renewal date in 10 days]"*
- *"Can you meet before the end of the week?"*
- *"The broker said I should talk to someone before I sign"*

**The three-week window is the primary sales opportunity.** The ICP reaches external help-seeking in week 3 with compressed timeline and high motivation. The advisor who is findable during weeks 1–2 (through circuit presence, broker relationships, content visibility) is positioned to receive the week-3 urgency call.

## Net ICP Language Finding

The ICP language vocabulary for Geneva SMB AI accountability is consistent across verticals: the problem arrives as a form, creates personal exposure, and surfaces a gap between policy documentation and carrier evidence requirements. The three-week urgency window is the primary conversion opportunity. The language that converts is document-specific ("read the form," "before you sign," "evidence of what you did"), not framework-specific ("governance," "compliance," "risk management").

The market is speaking evidence language. The competitors are selling framework language. The gap between what the market says it needs and what competitors offer is the primary positioning opportunity.
