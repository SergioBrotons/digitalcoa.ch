## The Six NET-NEW Competitive Findings for C307 Phase 06

### Finding 01: The Geneva Accountability Ecosystem Is a Two-Circuit Market

**What it is:** The Geneva SMB AI accountability market operates across two parallel circuits — formal (association programs, published guidance, carrier communications) and informal (broker conversations, peer networks, ORDRE dinners, chamber events). The informal circuit moves the market before the formal circuit publishes.

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Not found in any prior C214–C306 phase. Distinct from C306 Phase 06 finding that "the Francophone circuit operates on different trust logic." This finding is structural (two circuits operating sequentially) rather than cultural (different trust signals in different languages).

**Implication:** The primary competitive investment is informal circuit participation — sustained presence in broker relationships, peer networks, and institutional events — not formal circuit publishing. The informal circuit generates the referrals. The formal circuit validates the problem.

---

### Finding 02: The Category Is Forming from Carrier Form Language, Not Advisory Marketing

**What it is:** The evidence production category is not being named by advisors through marketing — it is being formed by carrier attestation form language. The fourth-generation carrier form asks for "evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions." This phrase is the category definition. The regulatory requirements are specifying the category vocabulary.

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Distinct from C306 Phase 06 finding that "the category is unnamed." This finding advances the analysis: the category is not just unnamed — it is being named by regulatory requirements, not by advisory marketing. The naming opportunity is vocabulary match with carrier form language, not category label invention.

**Implication:** The positioning that wins is "evidence of human authorization" (carrier form vocabulary) — not "AI governance" (framework vocabulary). The advisor who uses carrier form vocabulary earns category recognition because the ICP hears their problem described.

---

### Finding 03: The Geneva SMB Principal's Trust Formation Follows a Specific Four-Step Sequence

**What it is:** Geneva SMB principals follow a consistent trust formation sequence when evaluating AI accountability advisors: (1) institutional membership signal — does the advisor share ORDRE/HIN/SRO membership? (2) peer endorsement — has someone the principal knows used this advisor? (3) broker association — did the advisor come through the principal's broker? (4) document specificity — does the advisor demonstrate they read the specific form, not just the framework?

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Distinct from C306 Phase 06's competitive moat analysis. This finding describes the ICP's decision process, not the advisor's competitive position. The sequence matters because it defines the sales process: the advisor who cannot satisfy Step 1 loses before Step 2 is reached.

**Implication:** The service model sequence (vendor contract review → carrier attestation briefing → evidence architecture) is designed for the trust formation sequence. The first engagement (vendor contract review) demonstrates document specificity (Step 4) before requiring institutional membership (Step 1) or broker endorsement (Step 3).

---

### Finding 04: The Solo Practitioner Problem Is an Operator Model Problem, Not a Framework Problem

**What it is:** Geneva solo practitioners (1–5 principals, 2–20 employees) cannot produce AI accountability evidence internally — not due to lack of knowledge or tools, but due to lack of operational execution capacity. Every current competitor service design (enterprise frameworks, self-service platforms, association programs, legal analysis) assumes the client will execute internally. None produce evidence for the client.

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Distinct from C305 Phase 05 finding that "solo practitioner operational inaccessibility is universal." This finding is about service design (operator model vs. educator model), not about the existence of the problem. The problem was documented in C305. The service design solution is documented here.

**Implication:** The operator model (evidence production for the client, not by the client) is the service design that matches the solo practitioner's actual constraint. This model cannot be systematized into a self-service platform without losing execution quality. The delivery model requires human execution capacity — it cannot scale through software.

### Finding 05: The Romandie Circuit Has Distinct Gift-Economy Trust Mechanisms That Most Advisors Misread

**What it is:** The Romandie (French-speaking Swiss) advisory circuit operates on gift-economy logic: knowledge shared as a favor before it is sold as a service. The advisor who distributes business cards at ORDRE dinners signals outsider status. The advisor who shares useful intelligence without selling anything builds relational capital that converts to advisory mandates. The Romandie circuit trusts time (sustained presence) over credentials (recent certification).

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Distinct from C306 Phase 06 finding that "the Francophone circuit operates on different trust logic." This finding specifies the mechanism (gift-economy logic) rather than the observation (different trust signals). The mechanism explains why standard advisory marketing fails in Romandie and why sustained content investment works.

**Implication:** Brotons.net's bilingual EN + FR editorial investment is Romandie circuit participation infrastructure. The content is the gift. The advisory mandate is the conversion. Publishing useful intelligence in French is demonstrating circuit participation — it is not translation, it is trust formation.

---

### Finding 06: The Cross-Vertical ICP Is the Primary Beachhead Market, and No Competitor Serves Them

**What it is:** The Geneva professional ecosystem has a population of principals who span all four verticals — law, finance, consulting, healthcare — simultaneously. These cross-vertical principals have the most complex accountability exposure and no Geneva advisor currently serves their synthesis need. Single-vertical competitors (legal AI governance, finance compliance, healthcare data protection) cannot serve cross-vertical principals because they lack vertical synthesis depth.

**Why it's NET-NEW for C307:** Distinct from C306 Phase 06's white space identification. This finding is the competitive intelligence confirmation of C307 Phase 05 synthesis finding. The beachhead market was identified conceptually in C305/C306. The competitive confirmation — no competitor currently serves this population — is documented here for the first time.

**Implication:** The cross-vertical ICP is the primary beachhead market for evidence production services. The population is small enough that sustained circuit participation reaches the entire population. The referral network is dense (cross-vertical principals know each other). The competitive moat is the synthesis capability itself — a new entrant would need years of sustained multi-vertical circuit participation to compete.

## C307 Full Cycle Synthesis

### What C307 Found Across Phases 01–06

**Phase 01 (Legal):** Geneva small law firms face accountability inversion — the AI governance gap creates personal exposure under Article 321 CC before it creates institutional exposure. Vendor contracts expose professional secrecy obligations the principal didn't know existed. Carrier attestation and ORDRE disciplinary standards create two non-aligned documentation requirements. Bilingual client communication bifurcation requires French-language capability. Multi-standard confusion prevents proportionate response. Active exposure recognition (the form arriving) is the decisive trigger for action.

**Phase 02 (Finance):** Geneva wealth managers face institutional DDQ as commercial gate — the RFP process now includes AI governance questions that create evidence requirements. Carrier pre-signing review gap means the highest-value intervention has no current provider. Portfolio construction AI authorization chain is undocumented across generations of carrier relationships. Third-generation carrier processor chains activate undisclosed nFADP Art. 9 obligations. SRO silence creates structural accountability vacuum — no guidance, but principals know something is coming. Cross-mandate documentation coherence paradox: satisfying one regulatory authority doesn't satisfy all.

**Phase 03 (Consulting):** Geneva management consultants face client DDQ commercial gates — losing mandates because they cannot answer AI governance questions. Carrier attestation misrepresentation is the primary unaddressed risk — principals sign forms they don't understand. Sub-consultant delegation creates structural gap — the consultant is accountable for sub-consultant AI use they didn't authorize. Proposal methodology two-dimensional documentation gap — the written proposal describes the engagement, not the AI authorization chain. EU AI Act GPAI cascade creates deployer obligations for consulting firms serving EU clients.

**Phase 04 (Healthcare):** Geneva physicians face HIN transport security misread — HIN covers transport security, not AI tool authorization. AI tool boundary crossing happens continuously — tools used beyond their intended scope without documentation. Patient portal AI report delivery creates new documentation obligations. Layered AI interpretation chain means physician liability for AI outputs they didn't directly generate. Emergency AI use has systematic documentation exception — emergency situations produce zero documentation. FMH CME AI evaluation chain requires documentation of learning, not competence.

**Phase 05 (Cross-Vertical):** Documentation debt has three independent compounding vectors — policy-to-evidence gap, multi-authority non-alignment, continuous AI operation vs. episodic documentation. The evidence architecture is one system across all four verticals — not four products. Personal exposure and carrier coverage are universally misunderstood as the same problem. Vendor contract terms define the accountability mechanism — not the vendor's compliance level. Delegation accountability is unnamed in the market. Regulatory pressure convergence creates sustained demand that doesn't depend on any single regulatory event.

**Phase 06 (Competitive):** The market is a two-circuit system — informal circuit moves referrals, formal circuit publishes guidance. The category is forming from carrier form language, not advisory marketing. Trust follows a four-step sequence (institutional membership → peer endorsement → broker association → document specificity). Solo practitioner problem requires an operator model, not a framework model. Romandie circuit operates on gift-economy logic — sustained intelligence-sharing precedes mandate conversion. Cross-vertical ICP is the primary beachhead market with no current competitor.

### The Core C307 Insight

**The Geneva AI accountability market has a documentation debt that is structurally created and structurally unaddressed.** The debt is created by: (1) AI tools being adopted faster than evidence systems can document them; (2) regulatory authorities publishing principles without Tuesday translation; (3) carriers asking for evidence without specifying the format; (4) solo practitioners having no internal capacity to produce the evidence.

**The debt is unaddressed because:** every current competitor service design assumes internal execution capacity the solo practitioner doesn't have; every framework describes what adequate governance should look like without producing what the carrier actually asks for; every formal circuit publication reaches the ICP after the informal circuit has already created problem awareness.

**The evidence production service is the resolution mechanism.** It produces evidence for the principal, not alongside the principal. It reads the carrier form, not the framework. It operates in both Geneva circuits simultaneously. It is designed for the three-week urgency window. It builds named broker relationships that convert at highest rates.

## The Final C307 Positioning Statement

### The Core Claim

**"The market offers frameworks. We produce evidence."**

This positioning:
- Names the category gap (evidence production) in ICP vocabulary (carrier form language)
- Contrasts with the competitor landscape (frameworks) using the vocabulary the ICP recognizes
- Positions against the primary failure mode (policy documents that don't answer the carrier's question)
- Works in both English and French

### The Positioning Anchor

**"For physicians, evidence is personal."**

This anchor:
- Speaks to the physician ICP specifically (most acute professional liability context)
- Signals understanding of the personal stakes (Article 321 CC equivalent for medicine — professional accountability is personal)
- Creates emotional recognition in other verticals (the lawyer, wealth manager, consultant all face the same personal stakes)
- Establishes the stakes before the explanation

### The Service Model Claim

**"One evidence architecture, four vertical overlays. Not four products, one system."**

This claim:
- Describes the unified methodology across verticals
- Signals synthesis capability (cross-vertical ICP is the beachhead market)
- Positions against vertical specialists who have four separate products
- Reinforces the single-advisor model (one human's sustained participation)

### The Entry Claim

**"Send me the vendor contract. I'll read it. I'll give you a memo describing your Article 321 CC exposure in two weeks."**

This claim:
- Demonstrates document specificity (not framework language)
- Specifies the deliverable (memo, not presentation)
- Names the regulatory layer (Article 321 CC, not generic AI governance)
- Sets the timeline (two weeks, not months)
- Sets the price signal (CHF 1,100, not CHF 100,000)

## The Single Most Important Competitive Insight for C307 Cycle Close

**The Geneva SMB AI accountability market is not a "AI governance market." It is an "evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions market."**

Every competitor is selling the first thing (AI governance frameworks, AI compliance solutions, AI risk management). No competitor is selling the second thing (evidence production for carrier attestation). The ICP is being asked by their carrier to produce evidence — and the entire advisory market is selling frameworks.

The category gap is not partial. It is complete. The naming opportunity is not a clever label — it is vocabulary match with the ICP's problem as formed by the carrier form. The first actor who says "evidence of human authorization for your specific AI-assisted decisions" in a Geneva broker's office has named the category.

Everything else follows from that.
