### Title
**The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework (Swissdamed Edition):**
*Your Practice Has ~13 Weeks. Here Is What You Must Do Before July 1, 2026.*

---

### Section 1 — Swissdamed July 1, 2026: No Grace Period

**Hook:**
Swissmedic has confirmed: there is no transition period for high-risk medical AI. July 1, 2026 is the deadline. If your practice uses AI for diagnostic imaging, symptom triage, or clinical decision support — you are already in the scope of Swissdamed.

---

### Section 2 — What AI Is Your Practice Using?

**Hook:**
Before Swissdamed, you need an AI inventory. Most practices are using more AI than they realize.

**Key points:**
- AI scheduling and appointment management
- AI diagnostic imaging (radiology, pathology)
- AI symptom triage and clinical decision support
- AI EHR and practice management
- Any AI that qualifies as a medical device

---

### Section 3 — Does Your AI Qualify as a Medical Device Under Swissdamed?

**Hook:**
Swissdamed applies to medical devices — including AI. If your AI system meets the definition of a SaMD (Software as a Medical Device), Swissdamed obligations apply directly.

**Key points:**
- Risk classification determines obligation level
- High-risk SaMD: July 1 2026 obligation
- Registration requirement for high-risk devices

---

### Section 4 — The 30/60/90 Day Action Plan

**Hook:**
13 weeks is enough time — if you start now.

**Action plan:**
- **Week 1-2:** AI inventory — what AI is your practice using?
- **Week 3-4:** Risk classification — does any AI qualify as SaMD?
- **Week 5-8:** DPIA under nFADP Art. 5(c)
- **Week 9-12:** Swissdamed registration if required
- **Week 13:** Documentation and annual review

---

### Section 5 — FDPIC Healthcare Enforcement (nFADP)

**Hook:**
Swissdamed is not your only deadline. The FDPIC has designated healthcare as an enforcement priority.

**Key points:**
- nFADP Art. 5(c): Transparency + DPIA for AI processing personal health data
- FDPIC 2024/2025: Healthcare = enforcement priority
- AI diagnostic tools processing patient data = dual obligation (Swissdamed + nFADP)

---

### Section 6 — CHUV Meditron: What Swiss University Hospitals Are Doing

**Hook:**
Lausanne University Hospital (CHUV) tested Meditron, a Swiss medical LLM, with 241 clinicians and 12,500+ evaluations. This is institutional-grade evidence of AI adoption in Swiss healthcare.

**Key points:**
- Swiss university hospitals actively adopting medical AI
- Institutional AI adoption sets the standard
- Small practices must follow or risk gap

---

### Section 7 — Anti-Pattern: What Ungoverned Medical AI Looks Like

**Hook:**
No Swissdamed inventory. No risk classification. No DPIA. No registration. This is the pattern that creates liability.

---
