# Phase 03 — Regulatory & Compliance Context: Geneva Consulting

## Switzerland AI Regulatory Landscape

### Current State: Guidance-Based, Not Law-Based
Switzerland does **not** have a comprehensive AI law analogous to the EU AI Act. Instead:
- Swiss AI regulation is **sector-specific and guidance-driven**
- Federal agencies (BITT, EFPC) issue guidance rather than binding rules for most AI uses
- The Swiss government favors innovation-friendly posture while monitoring EU AI Act developments
- Switzerland is **not** in the EU; EU AI Act does not directly apply, but Swiss firms serving EU clients may face indirect applicability

### Key Regulatory References for AI in Switzerland

| Reference | Origin | Relevance to Small Consultants |
|---|---|---|
| **nFADP** (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) | Swiss | Revised nFADP (in force Sep 2023) — applies to all Swiss firms; GDPR-like but Swiss-specific |
| **EFTA / EU Trade Agreements** | International | Swiss firms with EU operations may face EU AI Act requirements |
| **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** | International | AI Management System standard; certifiable; increasingly expected in B2B contexts |
| **ISO 9001 (general QMS)** | International | Often prerequisite for ISO 42001; already common in consulting firms |
| **NIST AI RMF** | US-origin | Used by some Swiss firms with US operations or partnerships |
| **EU AI Act** | EU | Indirect relevance: Swiss firms with EU clients or subsidiaries face it |

### nFADP — Key Requirements for Small Consultants
- **Legal basis** for processing client data must be established (contract, legitimate interest, consent)
- **Data subject rights** must be handled: access, correction, deletion
- **Data breach notification** required within 72 hours to PFPDT (Swiss data protection authority)
- **AI-specific concerns:** If AI tools process client data, the consultant must have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with the vendor
- **Transfer restrictions:** Client data transferred to US-based AI providers (ChatGPT, Claude) may require specific safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses)

### Client Confidentiality + AI Tools
This is the primary regulatory anxiety for small consulting firms:
1. **What happens to data sent to ChatGPT/Claude?** — not fully clear; no enterprise DPA in most small firm cases
2. **Client consent** — do clients need to be informed/pseudonymized if AI assists with their case?
3. **Professional secrecy** — Swiss lawyers and some advisors have professional secrecy obligations; AI tools may conflict
4. **Vendor risk** — small firms rarely negotiate DPAs with AI vendors

## ISO 42001 — Emerging Standard

### What It Is
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System standard. Provides a framework for organizations that build or use AI systems.

### Relevance to Small Geneva Consulting Firms
- **Certification** is optional but growing in B2B contexts
- **Consultants advising clients on AI** may be expected to have ISO 42001 awareness or alignment
- **ISO 42001 as differentiator** — a small firm with ISO 42001-aligned AI governance may stand out in RFPs
- **Not a compliance burden** — the standard is designed to be proportionate; smaller orgs can implement a lightweight version

### Current Adoption in Switzerland
- Primarily large enterprises and multinationals pursuing certification
- Growing awareness but limited adoption in small consulting boutiques
- **Opportunity for first movers** — small Geneva consulting firms adopting ISO 42001-aligned practices could differentiate

## C115 NET-NEW Regulatory Findings (2026-04-04)

### 1. Swiss SME M&A +16% Volume (2025) Creates Regulatory Due Diligence Demand
Swiss SME M&A activity increased 16% in 2025, driven significantly by succession exits. This M&A wave creates regulatory advisory demand around UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) compliance, AML due diligence, and cross-border transaction structuring. Geneva boutiques advising on M&A must be current on SECO sanctions screening, AMLD alignment, and UBO registry requirements — all areas where small advisory firms can differentiate from generalist competitors.

**Source:** Deloitte CH, Lex Futura, ansarada.com, sigtax.com

### 2. CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) — Switzerland Implementing 2026-2027
Switzerland is implementing CRS 2.0 + CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) starting 2026 with full implementation by 2027. This creates new tax due diligence and reporting obligations for Swiss financial institutions, fintechs, and crypto service providers — many of which are Geneva-based. Geneva consulting firms advising wealth managers, family offices, and crypto service providers will need CARF framework setup, process redesign, and staff training capabilities.

**Source:** Swiss Federal Tax Administration signals, Deloitte CH, KPMG CH

### 3. Intra-Family Succession Decline (60% → 40%) Shifts Regulatory Advisory Mix
The sharp decline in intra-family succession transfers (from 60% to 40% over 15 years) is creating new regulatory advisory demand. External succession transitions involve more external parties, formal legal/regulatory structuring, and cross-border tax considerations than family-internal transfers. Geneva consultants advising family businesses on succession must handle: business valuation regulation, inheritance tax structuring, governance documentation, and external successor onboarding — all with stronger regulatory dimensions.

**Source:** Lombard Odier/HEC Geneva 2025 family business survey, lexfutura.ch, sigtax.com

### 4. nFADP Enforcement Maturing — Data Processing Agreements Becoming Standard Practice
Swiss data protection (nFADP, revised version in force September 2023) enforcement is maturing. For Geneva consulting firms using AI tools, Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with AI vendors are becoming a baseline requirement rather than optional. This creates a specific advisory opportunity: helping small consulting firms audit their current AI tool usage, establish compliant DPAs, and implement data governance frameworks proportionate to their practice size.

**Source:** FDPIC guidance, Swiss data protection practice notes

### 5. Francophone Africa Regulatory Alignment — EU-Native Regulations Crossing into Africa
EU regulatory frameworks (CSRD, DORA, EU AI Act) are creating compliance pressure on African subsidiaries of European companies — many of which are advised by Geneva-based firms with Francophone connections. This creates a regulatory advisory bridge: Geneva consultants with French language skills and EU regulatory expertise can advise Francophone Africa clients on aligning with EU-adjacent standards. The EU's "Brussels Effect" is extending regulatory advisory demand beyond Europe's borders.

**Source:** Lombard Odier Francophone Africa advisory notes, EU CSRD extraterritorial analysis, REYL Group Africa practice

### 6. ISO 42001 AI Management System — Growing Relevance for B2B Consulting Positioning
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 (AI Management System standard) is gaining relevance as a B2B market signal in Switzerland. While adoption remains low among small Geneva boutiques, an increasing number of RFPs and client procurement processes are beginning to reference ISO 42001 alignment as a trust indicator. Geneva consulting firms that adopt ISO 42001-aligned AI governance practices can differentiate on quality assurance and client data protection — particularly valuable when serving financial sector clients, IOs, and NGOs with strict confidentiality requirements.

**Source:** ISO 42001 adoption trends, Swiss consulting procurement patterns
