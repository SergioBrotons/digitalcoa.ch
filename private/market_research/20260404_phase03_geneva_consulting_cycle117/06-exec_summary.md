## What Was Researched

The Geneva small consulting firm vertical (2–15 employees, CHF 500K–5M revenue) for the purpose of determining fit for DigitalCoa.ch advisory services around AI governance and strategy.

---

## Headline Findings

### 1. Market Is Real and Substantial
- Swiss management consulting market: ~USD 3.58B (2025), growing at 4.48% CAGR
- Geneva is Switzerland's #2 consulting hub with 10% rate premium
- Estimated 600+ Geneva consulting firms in the 2–15 employee range
- Market driven by digital acceleration, ESG compliance, AI deployments

### 2. AI Adoption Gap Is Genuine
- Most small Geneva consulting firms are in early/experimental AI stage
- Individual tool adoption (ChatGPT, Copilot) common; firm-level strategy absent
- No clear advisory provider has claimed the "AI governance for small consulting firms" space
- Client-side pressure building: RFPs beginning to ask about AI methodology

### 3. Regulatory Environment Is Manageable, Not Opaque
- Switzerland has no comprehensive AI law (guidance-based, not law-based)
- Primary relevant regulation: nFADP (Swiss data protection, GDPR-like)
- ISO 42001:2023 is emerging as a market signal/differentiator
- Big 4 (KPMG, PwC, EY) dominate AI governance advisory — too expensive and slow for small firms

### 4. Competitive Landscape Has a Gap
- AI tool vendors (ChatGPT, Microsoft, Google) are competitors in the "AI productivity" space
- Big 4 are present in AI governance but not targeting small consulting firms
- No Geneva-based boutique advisory firm has claimed the "AI governance for small consulting" position
- First-mover opportunity exists

### 5. Primary ICP Pain Is Real
- Time poverty: billable hours consumed by non-billable admin and proposal work
- Client expectation gap: clients expect data-driven deliverables
- Competitive anxiety: fear of falling behind larger boutiques with AI resources
- Trust anxiety: uncertainty about client data confidentiality when using AI tools

## C115 Phase 03 Findings (2026-04-04)

### C115 Finding 1: Swiss SME M&A +16% (2025), Succession Exits as Primary Driver
Swiss SME M&A volume grew 16% in 2025, driven significantly by succession exits alongside PE activity. Approximately 100,000 Swiss SMEs face succession decisions; 1 in 3 may not find an internal successor. This creates concrete advisory demand for valuation, due diligence management, and integration planning. Geneva boutiques are well-positioned for smaller deal sizes where Big 4 show limited interest.

### C115 Finding 2: Intra-Family Succession Collapsing — 60% to 40% in 15 Years
Intra-family transfers fell from 60% to 40% over 15 years. Per Lombard Odier/HEC Geneva 2025 survey: 47% of Swiss family businesses have taken zero concrete succession planning steps; only 10% have formal next-generation integration programs; only 11% have completed succession. This structural shift creates sustained multi-year advisory demand for external governance, exit planning, and family constitution work.

### C115 Finding 3: Freelance Platforms Commoditizing Routine Consulting Work
Intensifying competition from freelance platforms (Upwork, Malt, etc.) and independent consultants is threatening boutique economics for routine strategy engagements. Sustainable response: differentiation through vertical specialization, client relationship depth, and proprietary frameworks — not day-rate competition.

### C115 Finding 4: CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) 2026-2027 Advisory Wave
Switzerland implementing CRS 2.0 + CARF from 2026 (full implementation 2027). New tax due diligence and reporting obligations for Swiss financial institutions, fintechs, and crypto service providers — many Geneva-based. Creates specific advisory workload: CARF framework setup, process redesign, reporting systems, and staff training.

### C115 Finding 5: Talent Scarcity Driving Delivery Model Innovation
Swiss consulting firms facing talent scarcity are accessing global senior advisor pools via remote arrangements. Geneva boutiques can build internationally competitive delivery models with lower cost bases while maintaining premium positioning. Hybrid teams + fractional senior advisors = emerging competitive differentiator.

### C115 Finding 6: Francophone Africa — Uncharted Advisory Territory for Geneva Boutiques
Geneva's French-language market has structural advantage for Francophone Africa advisory. EU regulatory alignment pressure (CSRD, DORA) is creating demand from African subsidiaries of European companies. Geneva advisors with FR language + Swiss credibility + EU regulatory expertise are well-positioned for cross-border Francophone Africa advisory — currently underexplored in Geneva consulting analysis.
