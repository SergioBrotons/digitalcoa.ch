### 1. "Lex On" — IPI March 23, 2026 Decision Dilutes Swiss Made Label — MAJOR
**Source:** Le Temps April 1, 2026 — "Controverse sur l'usage de la croix suisse" and "Loi Swissness: l'affaire des chaussures On"  
**Decision maker:** Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IPI), Bern  
**Date:** March 23, 2026 announcement — **CONFIRMED NET-NEW from Cycle 65**

The Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IPI) announced on March 23, 2026 a modification to Swissness rules permitting the use of "Swiss Engineering" and "Swiss Research" labels alongside the Swiss cross — even when production is conducted overseas.

**Previous rule:** Swiss cross use required genuine "Swiss Made" — minimum 60% of production costs in Switzerland and an essential manufacturing step in Switzerland.

**New IPI interpretation (March 23, 2026):** Allows Swiss cross between "Swiss" and "Engineering"/"Research" even when production is abroad.

**Key constraints from IPI:** Swiss cross must be placed exactly between the two terms (e.g., "Swiss" + cross + "Engineering"), and the square side length must not exceed the font size.

**Industry response:** "Nombre d'industriels et d'associations économiques fustigent une dilution du label 'Swiss made', voire une incitation à délocaliser." — Strong opposition from industrialists and economic associations. Debate confirmed as just beginning.

**IP specialists' concern:** The decision is not "cosmetic retouching" — the Swiss cross is "at the summit of the hierarchy of designations" in Swiss IP law. The decision raises fundamental questions about consumer transparency. The IPI's decision was made without open consultation.

**Legal context:** The decision was triggered by the "On" shoes case — Zurich sports equipment maker On won the right to use Swiss cross alongside "Swiss" on shoes produced primarily overseas.
