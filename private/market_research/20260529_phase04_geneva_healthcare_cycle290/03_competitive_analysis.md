# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Healthcare — C290 Phase 04

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

---

## Competitive Landscape

### What Exists

**1. Practice Management Software AI Assistants**
Vendors: Uneo, Am��, and smaller CH-based practice management platforms activating AI features across scheduling, documentation, and billing.
- market.position: Tool providers. Sell to practices on efficiency gains.
- accountability.gap: Contract terms activate AI accountability structures physician has not reviewed. Training documentation is product-oriented, not physician-professionalism-oriented.

**2. FMH Professional Guidance**
- market.position: Standards-setting body. Issues guiding principles for AI use.
- accountability.gap: Principle-level guidance without operational endpoint. FMH circulars reference physician oversight obligation without defining it at small practice scale. No Geneva-calibrated interpretation for 2–10 physician practices.

**3. Swissdamed Registration Framework**
- market.position: Swiss Federal Council's medical device framework.
- accountability.gap: Tier 1/Tier 2 classification criteria written for manufacturers not outpatient physicians. Independent practice physician has no clear registration pathway. Compliance paralysis rather than compliance pathway.

**4. nFADP Compliance Tools**
Vendors: Various DPO-as-a-service offerings, data protection consultancies.
- market.position: General data protection compliance. Legal-centric framing.
- accountability.gap: DPIA templates designed for manufacturing, research, or enterprise deployments. No calibrated product for AI-assisted medical records in independent outpatient practice. Medical record-specific nFADP guidance absent.

**5. Medical AI Gateway (cantonal)**
- market.position: Geneva institutional AI procurement and governance framework for institutional actors.
- accountability.gap: Designed for institutional actors, governance infrastructure unavailable to independent practices. Institutional decisions forming effective standards without methodology transmission to independent sector.

**6. Carrier AI Attestation Products**
Providers: Le+Ré, AXA, Helsana, and others issuing AI-focused questionnaire instruments.
- market.position: Underwriting risk evaluation. Designed to price AI exposure.
- accountability.gap: Form designed by carrier legal team, no pre-signing review service exists in Geneva market, physician signature without independent analysis, no published AI coverage adequacy standard.

**7. Swiss AI Convention Signatory Services**
- market.position: UNESCO-adjacent international framework for AI deployers.
- accountability.gap: Signatory process designed for organizations with governance infrastructure. Independent practice physician faces same signatory requirements as institutional actor with no proportionate methodology for small-scale deployment.

### What's Missing (White Space)

**1. Physician AI Governance Methodology for Small Independent Practice**
The market has:
- general AI governance frameworks (too broad)
- institutional AI governance (inaccessible)
- vendor-provided AI training (product-oriented)
- FMH principle-level guidance (without operational endpoint)
- carrier attestation forms (underwriting-oriented)

The market does not have:
- A proportionate AI governance methodology calibrated to independent 2–10 physician practice scale
- A step-by-step operational definition of FMH oversight obligation at small practice level
- A physician-specific AI competency documentation standard
- A methodology for doing a full AI surface inventory and documentation

**Positioning opportunity:** A briefing that gives the independent Geneva physician what no current product provides — a proportionate, actionable, Geneva-calibrated AI governance methodology for the 2–10 physician outpatient practice.

---

**2. Geneva-Calibrated AI Medical Record Protocol**
The market has:
- general medical record standards (SIKin, SWICA standards, FMH guidelines)
- general documentation guidance
- AI tool vendor training documentation

The market does not have:
- a protocol specifically addressing AI-generated content attribution in Swiss medical records
- a methodology for documenting AI contribution to medical records in a manner FMH-recognized
- guidance on handling patient-initiated AI-generated content from consumer tools entering clinical record
- a protocol for AI-assisted second opinion documentation

**Positioning opportunity:** A briefing that names and addresses the documentation gray zones no current product covers — what to do when AI contributes to a record, when a patient brings AI-generated content, when a second opinion includes AI-generated analysis.

---

**3. Pre-Signing Carrier AI Attestation Review Service**
The market has:
- carrier AI questionnaires
- DPIA templates from data protection consultants
- general insurance coverage review
- FMH professional liability guidance

The market does not have:
- any Geneva-based service offering pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation instruments
- any independent physician AI governance review before carrier signature
- any service that maps carrier AI questionnaire responses to FMH professional accountability requirements before submission
- any product that tests insurance coverage adequacy against specific AI governance decisions at practice level

**Positioning opportunity:** A briefing that serves as the pre-signing review mechanism the market does not have — where the physician understands what they are signing before the carrier form is submitted, and in 90 minutes.

---

**4. Vendor Contract AI Terms Review Service**
The market has:
- general contract review from business lawyers
- IT/Vendor contract review without FMH professional accountability lens
- DPIA vendor assessment frameworks (enterprise-oriented)

The market does not have:
- a service that reviews clinical software vendor AI terms against FMH physician accountability requirements specifically
- any independent Geneva-calibrated review mapping vendor AI terms to nFADP obligations and MedDO classification
- a review that identifies technical inability to opt out of AI features and maps the resulting de facto AI authorization exposure

**Positioning opportunity:** A briefing that specifically addresses what no Geneva current product addresses — what the vendor AI terms actually require the physician to accept, what cannot be opted out of, and what the physician remains professionally responsible for regardless of vendor terms.

---

**5. Cantonal AI Governance Intelligence for Independent Practices**
The market has:
- Medical AI Gateway guidance for institutional actors
- cantonal health authority communications for institutional settings
- FMH general guidance
- Geneva institutional AI governance published outputs

The market does not have:
- any pathway for independent practices to access Geneva institutional AI governance intelligence
- any service translating cantonal institutional decisions into independent practice implications
- any Geneva cantonal practical guidance for small practice AI deployment calibrated to independent physician scale
- any FMH-channeled interpretation of cantonal institutional decisions

**Positioning opportunity:** A briefing that serves as the institutional intelligence translation mechanism the independent physician cannot access otherwise — what institutions are deciding, what that means for independent practice accountability, and how to position before cantonal guidance crystallizes.

---

## Summary: What the Market Does Not Have (Competitive White Space)

| Need | Market Gap |
|------|-----------|
| AI governance methodology at small practice scale | No proportionate operational protocol calibrated to 2-10 physician practice |
| AI medical record documentation standard | No FMH-calibrated, Geneva-appropriate protocol addressing AI attribution, patient AI content, second opinion AI |
| Pre-signing carrier AI attestation review | No Geneva service reviewing carrier forms against physician accountability before signature |
| Vendor AI contract terms review | No service reviewing vendor AI terms against FMH accountability requirements specifically |
| Cantonal intelligence access | No pathway for independent practices to access Geneva institutional AI governance intelligence |

---

## Competitive Positioning Implication

The Geneva healthcare market is tool-rich and governance-poor. Physicians can acquire AI tools easily. They cannot find proportionate governance methodology, operational accountability documentation, or Geneva-calibrated intelligence. The white space is in accountability architecture, not AI adoption guidance.
