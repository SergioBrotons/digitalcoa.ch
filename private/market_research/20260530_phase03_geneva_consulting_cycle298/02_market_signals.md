# Market Signals: Geneva Consulting — AI Accountability
## Small Consulting Boutique, 2–50 Employees | Phase 03 C298

---

## Current Problems and Complaints

### 1. Proposal Speed Creates Legal Ambiguity

Geneva consulting boutiques are winning work on AI-assisted proposal speed — then discovering the speed advantage creates an undocumented methodology gap. The deliverable that arrived faster because of AI also cannot be fully reconstructed in terms of which recommendations came from human judgment versus AI inference. Clients are beginning to ask methodology questions. Boutiques have no prepared answer.

**Signal source:** Client AI governance questionnaires asking for AI methodology documentation in proposal processes. Growing frequency in Q1–Q2 2026 across Geneva institutional client base.

### 2. Carrier Attestation Questions Outpace Market Answers

Insurance carriers have introduced AI-specific questions at renewal that are more sophisticated than the boutique's ability to answer them. The attestation language uses terms like "documented AI governance framework" and "human oversight protocol" without defining what those terms mean at boutique consulting scale. Signing without understanding has become the default behavior.

**Signal source:** Geneva insurance broker network reporting increased AI-specific questions at PI renewals for consulting firms in 2025–2026. Brokers confirming no proportionate SME-calibrated guidance exists.

### 3. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Chain Has No Governance Layer

The three-party consulting chain — boutique partner → sub-consultant → sub-consultant's AI tool — is entirely ungoverned. The primary firm carries accountability for deliverables bearing its name regardless of AI contribution at any tier. No standard subcontract contains AI-specific delegation provisions. No Geneva market service addresses this chain.

**Signal source:** Consulting boutiques reporting informal sub-consultant arrangements with no AI governance provisions. Growing sub-consultant AI usage going undetected.

### 4. nFADP Processor Obligations Reached Consulting Deliverables

The nFADP Article 9 processor obligation has reached consulting deliverables for the first time. Client data processed through AI tools in the course of a consulting engagement now triggers data processing agreement requirements. Most Geneva consulting boutiques do not have DPAs with their AI vendors. The exposure is present-tense, not anticipatory.

**Signal source:** FDPIC enforcement activity pattern showing Article 9 processor violations in professional services contexts. Bar association guidance absent on this specific application.

### 5. Geneva Consulting Peer Circuit Has No Accountability Forum

The Geneva consulting peer network operates differently from Geneva legal and finance peer circuits. Consulting boutiques lack the bar association structure of law firms and the SRO structure of wealth managers. The informal accountability circuit for consulting principals exists through dinner conversations and referral relationships but has no formal governance substrate. AI accountability discussions happen informally with no actionable output.

**Signal source:** Geneva consulting principals reporting peer conversations about AI risk that produce awareness without action or resolution.

### 6. AI Tool Configuration Has Become Unrecoverable Institutional Knowledge

Boutiques that deployed AI tools 18–24 months ago have tool configurations that have drifted through updates, personnel departures, and vendor changes. The institutional knowledge of how AI contributed to past deliverables is not reconstructable. This is not a future risk — deliverables from 2024 are in client files with unrecoverable AI configuration states.

**Signal source:** Boutique consulting firms discovering inability to answer client questions about AI methodology used in completed engagements.

---

## Failure Modes Being Observed

- **Attestation without understanding:** Signing carrier AI attestation forms without comprehension of what was committed
- **Sub-delegation opacity:** Sub-consultant work accepted with no AI governance provisions and no review protocol
- **Client data processor gap:** AI-processed client data with no DPA architecture in place
- **Methodology amnesia:** Past deliverables in client files with unrecoverable AI configuration
- **Competitive paralysis:** boutiques avoiding AI adoption entirely to sidestep accountability questions they cannot answer
- **DIY governance failure:** Principals attempting to build governance frameworks from scratch without templates or methodology, abandoning the effort

---

## Market Timing Signals

- Carrier renewal season creating concentrated attestation pressure in Q4 and Q1
- Institutional clients beginning to require AI governance documentation as proposal prerequisite
- FDPIC enforcement activity pattern suggesting Article 9 professional services focus in 2026
- No Geneva consulting-specific AI governance guidance from any professional body
