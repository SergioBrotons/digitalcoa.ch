# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Consulting — AI Accountability
## Small Consulting Boutique, 2–50 Employees | Phase 03 C298

---

## What Exists in Market

### Large Firm Offerings (Not Fit for Boutique)

- **Big Four AI governance frameworks:** Designed for enterprise-scale deployment, typically CHF 50,000+ engagements, require dedicated compliance teams to implement. Not proportionate to 2–50 person consulting practices. No Geneva Big Four firm offers a boutique-calibrated product.
- **Legal publications on AI governance:** Address law firm specific issues (privilege, bar rules, court procedure). Consulting practice has different professional obligation structure. Content does not transfer.
- **Insurance broker AI questionnaires:** Brokers distribute carrier questions at renewal. They do not help boutiques build the governance infrastructure the questions presuppose. Transactional, not advisory.

### Professional Association Guidance

- **SVA/ASC:** No Geneva consulting-specific AI governance guidance published. General AI guidance exists but does not address boutique consulting practice structure.
- **Swiss Consulting Network / other associations:** No SME-calibrated AI governance framework for consulting boutiques. Generic frameworks circulated without boutique implementation pathway.

### What Is Missing

### 1. No Proportionate Boutique Consulting AI Governance Framework

No provider offers an AI governance framework designed for a 2–50 person Geneva consulting practice. The frameworks that exist are either enterprise-scale or generic. The specific accountability geometry of a consulting boutique — partner accountability, sub-consultant delegation, client data processed through AI, proposal methodology defensibility — has no named solution.

### 2. No Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Protocol

No service addresses the three-party AI delegation chain in consulting engagements. No standard subcontract clause exists for AI delegation in the Geneva market. No methodology exists for managing accountability across the sub-consultant AI chain.

### 3. No Carrier Attestation Preparation Service for Consulting Boutiques

No Geneva service prepares consulting boutiques specifically for carrier AI attestation forms. The insurance product gap (no proportionate AI governance product for small consulting firms) creates a situation where boutiques must attest to undefined adequacy without preparation support.

### 4. No Geneva Consulting AI Peer Accountability Forum

The Geneva consulting community lacks the formal peer structures that exist for law (SVA/ORDRE) and finance (SROs). AI accountability discussions happen in informal circuits with no institutional substrate and no actionable output. No forum exists for consulting principals to discuss AI accountability with peer confidentiality.

### 5. No nFADP Article 9 DPA Solution for Consulting Practices

No Geneva-specific service helps consulting boutiques navigate the Article 9 processor obligation as it applies to consulting deliverables. Generic DPA templates do not address the consulting-specific intersection of client confidentiality, sub-consultant delegation, and AI tool usage.

### 6. No Proposal Methodology Defensibility Product

No service helps consulting boutiques document or defend AI-assisted proposal methodologies. The defensibility question — how to demonstrate the human accountability behind AI-assisted recommendations — is unnamed as a service category.

---

## Competitive Vacuum Summary

| Market Player | Position | Gap for Boutique Consulting |
|---|---|---|
| Big Four | Enterprise AI governance | Not proportionate, not accessible |
| Legal advisors | Attorney-specific AI guidance | Wrong professional framework |
| Insurance brokers | Form distribution | No governance preparation |
| Professional associations | Generic guidance | No boutique-specific implementation |
| Technology vendors | Tool-level | No accountability layer |
| Geneva consulting peer circuit | Informal, no substrate | No formal accountability forum |

**Conclusion:** The small Geneva consulting boutique AI governance market is entirely unserved. The combination of client AI governance questionnaires, carrier attestation, sub-consultant delegation, nFADP Article 9, and proposal methodology defensibility creates a compound accountability problem no existing player addresses. This is not a gap in existing offerings — it is a structural absence.
