## Regulatory Architecture (C315 P03 — Post-Deadline)

The C315 P03 regulatory architecture is the four-regime stack that activates post-deadline for the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner, with the named sub-consultant individual as the integrating mechanism:

1. **EU AI Act Article 26** — deployer obligations extending to named-sub-consultant attribution as named sub-principal
2. **EU AI Act Article 4** — AI literacy obligation extending to "any other person handling the operation and use of AI systems on [the firm's] behalf" (the named sub-consultant individual)
3. **nFADP (New Federal Act on Data Protection) Article 63** — responsible person at the data controller, personal liability extending to sub-consultant data processing
4. **nFADP (New Federal Act on Data Protection) Article 8 / 9** — DPIA / sub-processor disclosure extending to sub-consultant AI vendors
5. **EU AI Act Article 50** — deliverable transparency, activated through the EU client contractual disclosure clause
6. **EU AI Act Article 12** — logging, activated through the sub-consultant's AI vendor sub-processor audit
7. **Carrier professional liability attestation** — sub-consultant-specific clauses at mid-year 2026 refresh
8. **MSA (Member State Authority) Article 99** — procedural letters specifically naming the sub-consultant individual
9. **FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)** — supervisory letters naming the senior partner under nFADP Art.63, with sub-consultant data processing reference

The C314 P03 regulatory architecture was a pre-deadline documentary architecture (Article 3 + Article 26 + Article 4 + nFADP Art.63 + Article 50 + FINMA-adjacent). The C315 P03 regulatory architecture is a post-deadline enforcement architecture — the MSA sub-consultant-named letter pipeline, the sub-consultant-specific carrier attestation clauses, the sub-consultant handover-window enforcement, the Article 50 deliverable transparency enforcement, the sub-consultant AI vendor sub-processor audit, and the three-regime sub-consultant enforcement coordination are now active.

The cross-regime coordination is the central C315 P03 architecture problem: the named sub-consultant individual is the integrating mechanism across MSA Article 99 + EU AI Act Article 26 + nFADP Art.63 + carrier attestation. The named sub-consultant individual is named in the MSA letter, the EU AI Act Article 26 named-sub-principal attribution, the nFADP Art.63 responsible person extension, and the carrier attestation sub-consultant-specific clause. The named sub-consultant individual's AI use on the EU client deliverable triggers all four regimes simultaneously. The named-individual senior partner must produce a single named-sub-consultant evidence architecture that satisfies all four regimes simultaneously.

## EU AI Act Article 26 — Deployer Obligations Extending to Named Sub-Consultant

### Statutory Foundation

EU AI Act Article 26 establishes deployer obligations for natural and legal persons using high-risk AI systems under their authority. Article 26(1) requires deployers to "take appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure they use high-risk AI systems in accordance with the instructions for use accompanying the systems." Article 26(5) requires deployers using high-risk AI systems to "inform natural persons that they are subject to the use of a high-risk AI system" (the Article 50 transparency obligation, applied to deployer).

Article 26(2) requires deployers to "ensure that natural persons assigned to ensure oversight functions in accordance with Article 14 are appropriately qualified, receive adequate training, and have the necessary resources to fulfil those functions."

Article 26(6) requires deployers to "cooperate with the relevant competent authorities in any action those authorities take in connection with the high-risk AI system."

### Sub-Consultant Extension

The sub-consultant — as an external contractor using AI on the EU client deliverable — is operating "under the authority" of the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner (the deployer). The senior partner's engagement letter authorizes the sub-consultant's engagement. The sub-consultant's AI use on the engagement is therefore under the senior partner's authority. The senior partner's Article 26 deployer obligations extend to the sub-consultant's AI use.

The sub-consultant is also a "person handling the operation and use of AI systems on [the firm's] behalf" under Article 4. Article 4 requires deployers to "ensure a sufficient level of AI literacy of their staff and other persons handling the operation and use of AI systems on their behalf." The sub-consultant is "another person handling the operation and use of AI systems on [the firm's] behalf." The senior partner's Article 4 literacy obligation extends to the sub-consultant.

The named sub-consultant individual is therefore subject to:
- Article 26 named-sub-principal attribution (the sub-consultant is named in the MSA letter, named in the carrier attestation, named in the FDPIC file)
- Article 4 AI literacy obligation (the sub-consultant must have an adequate level of AI literacy for the engagement)
- Article 14 human oversight (the senior partner must have documented human oversight of the sub-consultant's AI use)
- Article 12 logging (the sub-consultant's AI use must be logged, with logs available to the MSA on request)

### Post-Deadline Enforcement (July 2026)

The C314 P03 framework treated Article 26 + Article 4 as a pre-deadline documentary requirement. The C315 P03 framework treats Article 26 + Article 4 as a post-deadline enforcement requirement — the MSA has begun issuing Article 99 procedural letters specifically naming the sub-consultant, the carrier has added sub-consultant-specific clauses referencing named sub-consultants, the EU client has activated Article 50 through the contractual disclosure clause, and the sub-consultant's AI vendor has been audited under Article 12.

The senior partner must produce a single named-sub-consultant evidence architecture that satisfies Article 26 + Article 4 + Article 14 + Article 12 + Article 50 simultaneously.

## EU AI Act Article 50 — Deliverable Transparency Enforcement

### Statutory Foundation

EU AI Act Article 50(1) requires providers and deployers of certain AI systems to inform natural persons that they are interacting with an AI system, where this is not obvious from the perspective of a reasonably well-informed natural person exercising due care. For high-risk AI systems, Article 50(3) requires deployers to "inform natural persons that they are subject to the use of a high-risk AI system" before the natural person is subject to the system.

### Consulting-Specific Application

For the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner, the "interaction" is the deliverable. The deliverable does not currently disclose AI use. The deliverable has historically been presented as the senior partner's methodology, the senior partner's judgment, the senior partner's expertise.

After August 2026, undisclosed AI use in the deliverable is a violation. The senior partner must now disclose AI use in the deliverable. The disclosure has two layers:

1. **Firm-level disclosure** (engagement letter AI addendum): the firm discloses that AI tools are used in the engagement, with named AI tool categories and named human review checkpoints.
2. **Deliverable-specific disclosure** (deliverable cover page + methodology documentation): the firm discloses the specific named AI tool used on the specific named deliverable, with the specific named sub-consultant individual who used the tool, with the specific named human review checkpoint, with the specific named deliverable reproducibility statement.

The C314 P03 framework treated Article 50 as a pre-deadline documentary requirement. The C315 P03 framework treats Article 50 as a post-deadline enforcement requirement — the EU client has activated Article 50 through the contractual disclosure clause, and the MSA audit response requires Article 50 compliance demonstration through specific named disclosure.

### EU Client Contractual Disclosure Clause

EU corporate clients have added the following language to engagement letter AI addenda:

> "The Service Provider shall disclose, in the deliverable cover page and in the engagement letter AI addendum, the specific named AI tool used on the specific named deliverable, with the specific named sub-consultant individual who used the tool, with the specific named human review checkpoint, with the specific named deliverable reproducibility statement. Generic disclosure ('we use AI tools') does not satisfy this clause. Firm-level AI policy does not satisfy this clause."

The clause is not satisfied by generic disclosure. The clause is not satisfied by firm-level AI policy. The clause requires the specific 4-element disclosure (named AI tool + named sub-consultant individual + named human review checkpoint + named deliverable reproducibility statement).

The MSA audit response framework now requires the senior partner to demonstrate Article 50 compliance through the contractual disclosure clause. The first Genevoise peer firm has been cited for Article 50 deliverable transparency failure.

## nFADP Article 63 — Personal Liability for Sub-Consultant Data Processing

### Statutory Foundation

nFADP Article 63 establishes criminal penalties for violations of the nFADP. Article 63(1) provides that "whoever wilfully fails to comply with an order issued by the FDPIC or a decision of the appellate authorities, where such order or decision is final and binding, is liable to a fine of up to CHF 250,000." Article 63(2) provides that "where the offence has been committed within a company, the fine is imposed on the natural person who committed the act."

For the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner, nFADP Art.63 personal liability is triggered when:
- The senior partner is the data controller responsible person for the engagement
- The sub-consultant's AI use processes personal data of EU client natural persons (investment committee, C-suite, project sponsor) or Swiss data subjects
- The sub-consultant processes the personal data without the senior partner's documented authorization
- The FDPIC issues a supervisory letter ordering the senior partner to produce the sub-consultant data processing documentation
- The senior partner fails to comply with the FDPIC order

### Sub-Consultant Extension

The sub-consultant is processing personal data "under the authority" of the senior partner (the data controller). The senior partner's nFADP Art.63 personal liability extends to the sub-consultant's data processing activities. The sub-consultant's data processing is unauthorized if the sub-consultant has not signed the data processing agreement, has not been documented in the ROPA, or has not been disclosed in the nFADP data subject information.

The senior partner's nFADP Art.63 exposure is amplified by the sub-consultant's data processing because:
- The sub-consultant may use AI tools that the senior partner has not authorized
- The sub-consultant's AI vendor may have sub-processor chains the senior partner has not disclosed
- The sub-consultant may process personal data in jurisdictions the senior partner has not authorized (e.g., US-hosted sub-consultant AI vendor with CLOUD Act exposure)
- The sub-consultant may transfer personal data outside the EU/Switzerland without the senior partner's documented cross-border transfer authorization

The FDPIC has begun opening nFADP Art.63 files naming the senior partner under sub-consultant data processing. The FDPIC file references the sub-consultant under nFADP Art.63 + EU AI Act Art.26 + carrier attestation — the same three-regime coordination as the MSA letter.

## nFADP Article 8 / 9 — DPIA / Sub-Processor Disclosure Extending to Sub-Consultant AI Vendors

### Statutory Foundation

nFADP Article 8 requires data controllers to conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) where processing is likely to result in a high risk to the personal rights or fundamental rights of the data subject. nFADP Article 9 requires data controllers to designate a representative in Switzerland if they process personal data of Swiss data subjects extensively, regularly, and in a way that involves a high risk.

For the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner, nFADP Art.8 DPIA is triggered when the sub-consultant's AI use processes personal data of EU client natural persons or Swiss data subjects. The DPIA must include:
- Description of the processing operations and purposes
- Assessment of the necessity and proportionality of the processing
- Assessment of the risks to the personal rights and fundamental rights of data subjects
- Measures to address the risks

The DPIA must extend to the sub-consultant's AI use. The DPIA must extend to the sub-consultant's AI vendor's sub-processor chain. The DPIA must extend to the sub-consultant's AI vendor's cross-border data transfer.

### Sub-Consultant AI Vendor Sub-Processor Disclosure

nFADP Art.9 sub-processor disclosure requires the data controller to disclose the sub-processors used in the processing. The sub-consultant is a sub-processor of the senior partner (the data controller). The sub-consultant's AI vendor is a sub-processor of a sub-processor (sub-sub-processor). The senior partner's nFADP Art.9 sub-processor disclosure must extend to the sub-consultant AI vendor.

The sub-consultant AI vendor's sub-processor chain (e.g., ChatGPT Team → OpenAI → Azure cloud infrastructure → sub-processors of OpenAI) is a sub-sub-sub-processor chain. The senior partner's nFADP Art.9 sub-processor disclosure must extend through the entire sub-processor chain.

The senior partner's ROPA must include the sub-consultant, the sub-consultant's AI vendor, and the sub-consultant AI vendor's sub-processor chain.

The senior partner's nFADP Art.8 DPIA must include the sub-consultant's AI use, the sub-consultant AI vendor's sub-processor chain, the cross-border data transfer authorization, and the data subject rights protection.

## MSA Article 99 — Sub-Consultant-Named Procedural Letter

### Statutory Foundation

EU AI Act Article 99 establishes the procedure for the application of the Act by Member State authorities. Article 99(1) requires Member States to designate market surveillance authorities for the AI Act. Article 99(2) requires the market surveillance authorities to apply the Act in accordance with their national procedural frameworks.

For the Geneva boutique consulting senior partner, the MSA's Article 99 procedural letter is the primary enforcement instrument. The letter is addressed to the firm (legal person) but references named individuals (managing partner, named sub-consultant, AI tool vendor contact).

### Sub-Consultant-Specific Article 99 Letter (Post-Deadline)

The C315 P03 framework observes that the MSA's Article 99 procedural letter to Geneva boutique consulting firms has evolved to specifically name the sub-consultant. The MSA's letter:
- Names the sub-consultant individual (the named individual at the sub-consulting firm who used AI on the deliverable)
- Names the specific named AI tool used by the sub-consultant
- Names the specific named deliverable on which the sub-consultant used AI
- Names the specific named engagement (project, mandate, workstream)
- Names the specific named EU client
- Names the specific named timestamp (date of sub-consultant AI use)

The MSA obtained the sub-consultant's name from:
- The EU client's procurement file (sub-processor disclosure submitted by the EU client)
- The senior partner's proposal management vendor logs (if the sub-consultant used the senior partner's vendor)
- The sub-consultant's own AI vendor logs (Article 12 audit)

The letter is forwarded to the Geneva boutique through the EU client contractual notice clause. The senior partner receives a letter with a sub-consultant name they may not be able to trace to a current sub-consultant because the sub-consultant has handed over.

The letter gives the Geneva boutique 30 days to produce:
- Sub-consultant-named AI authorization record
- Sub-consultant-named AI literacy credential (Article 4 satisfaction)
- Sub-consultant supervision protocol (Article 14 satisfaction)
- Named-senior-partner oversight documentation (Article 26 satisfaction for the named sub-consultant as Article 26 named sub-principal)
- Handover-window architecture (current sub-consultant + new sub-consultant, AI authorization continuity)

## Carrier Attestation — Sub-Consultant-Specific Clauses

### Statutory Foundation

Professional liability carriers require named-insured attestation for the consulting firm's professional liability coverage. The 2026 mid-year carrier attestation refresh has introduced three sub-consultant-specific clauses to professional liability attestation language for Geneva consulting senior partners.

### Sub-Consultant AI Authorization Attestation

> "The Named Insured shall maintain a current roster of named sub-consultants using AI on the Named Insured's engagements. The roster shall include, for each named sub-consultant, the named AI tool used, the named deliverable scope, the named engagement, and the named authorization record. The roster shall be updated at the rate of sub-consultant turnover. EU AI Act Article 26 reference. nFADP Art.63 reference."

### Sub-Consultant AI Literacy Attestation

> "The Named Insured shall maintain evidence that each named sub-consultant individual has an adequate level of AI literacy for the engagement. The evidence shall include the named sub-consultant individual's AI literacy credential, the date of credential, and the named sub-consultant individual's engagement AI use profile. EU AI Act Article 4 reference. nFADP Art.63 reference."

### Sub-Consultant Incident Response Attestation

> "The Named Insured shall maintain documented sub-consultant supervision protocol with named escalation chain. The protocol shall include the named-individual senior partner with oversight responsibility, the named-individual escalation chain, and the named-individual reporting cadence to MSA, FDPIC, and carrier. EU AI Act Article 26 incident reporting reference. nFADP Art.63 reporting reference."

### Cross-Reference to MSA Article 99

The three sub-consultant-specific clauses cite EU AI Act Article 26 and nFADP Art.63 by article number. The clauses reference the MSA's Article 99 procedural letter as the upstream enforcement instrument. The clauses reference the FDPIC supervisory letter as the Swiss-side enforcement instrument. The clauses reference the carrier cooperation duty with MSA and FDPIC.

The senior partner's signing posture must integrate:
- The sub-consultant-specific clauses (carrier attestation)
- The sub-consultant-named MSA letter (MSA Article 99)
- The sub-consultant-named FDPIC file (nFADP Art.63)
- The sub-consultant handover-window documentation (Article 26 continuity)

The named sub-consultant is the integrating individual across all four enforcement instruments.

## Three-Regime Coordination Architecture (C315 P03)

The C315 P03 three-regime coordination architecture is the named-sub-consultant individual as the integrating mechanism across:

1. **nFADP Art.63 + Art.8 / Art.9** — Swiss personal liability for the named-senior-partner extending to sub-consultant data processing + DPIA + sub-processor disclosure extending to sub-consultant AI vendors
2. **EU AI Act Art.26 + Art.4 + Art.12 + Art.50** — deployer obligations extending to named sub-consultant + AI literacy obligation extending to named sub-consultant + logging extending to sub-consultant AI vendor + deliverable transparency extending to EU client contractual disclosure clause
3. **Carrier attestation sub-consultant-specific clauses** — sub-consultant AI authorization attestation + sub-consultant AI literacy attestation + sub-consultant incident response attestation
4. **MSA Article 99 procedural letter** — sub-consultant-named letter naming the sub-consultant individual as the integrating individual

The named sub-consultant is the integrating individual across all four enforcement instruments. The named-sub-consultant evidence architecture must satisfy all four instruments simultaneously.

The senior partner must produce a single named-sub-consultant evidence architecture that includes:
- Named-sub-consultant roster (current + past, with dates)
- Named-sub-consultant AI authorization record (signed at engagement start)
- Named-sub-consultant AI literacy credential (dated, refreshable at handover)
- Named-sub-consultant supervision protocol (with named escalation chain)
- Named-sub-consultant incident response protocol (with named reporting cadence)
- Named-sub-consultant handover-window documentation (offboarding + onboarding with three-regime continuity)
- Named-sub-consultant AI vendor inventory (per engagement, per named sub-consultant, per named AI vendor)
- Named-sub-consultant AI vendor sub-processor disclosure (per engagement, per named sub-consultant AI vendor, per named sub-processor chain)
- Named-sub-consultant Article 50 disclosure (per named deliverable, with named AI tool, named human review checkpoint, named reproducibility statement)
- Named-sub-consultant MSA audit response (referencing current named sub-consultant)
- Named-sub-consultant FDPIC file (referencing current and past named sub-consultants)
- Named-sub-consultant carrier attestation addendum (referencing current named sub-consultant, with past sub-consultant reference as handover record)

This is the C315 P03 three-regime sub-consultant enforcement coordination architecture. This is what the first Genevoise peer firm has been cited for failing to produce.
