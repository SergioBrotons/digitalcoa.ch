### Finding 01: Sub-Consultant AI Authorization Roster Audit — First MSA Letter Naming the Sub-Consultant

**The finding:** EU Member State market surveillance authorities have begun issuing Article 99 procedural letters to Geneva boutique consulting firms that specifically name the sub-consultant — the external contractor using AI on the EU client deliverable. The MSA's letter does not name the firm first. It does not name the senior partner first. It names the sub-consultant: the named individual at the sub-consulting firm who used a specific named AI tool on a specific named deliverable, on a specific named engagement, for a specific named EU client. The MSA has obtained the sub-consultant's name from the EU client's procurement file (the EU client submitted the sub-consultant's name in the sub-processor disclosure), from the senior partner's own proposal management vendor (which logs the sub-consultant's tool usage), or from the sub-consultant's own AI vendor logs (which the MSA has audited under Article 12). The letter gives the Geneva boutique 30 days to produce the sub-consultant AI authorization record, the sub-consultant AI literacy credential, the sub-consultant supervision protocol, and the named-individual senior partner's documented oversight of the sub-consultant's AI use. The senior partner cannot produce any of this — the sub-consultant engagement was authorized, the sub-consultant's AI use was not, the sub-consultant handed over three weeks ago, and the new sub-consultant is on the same engagement.

**Mechanism:** Geneva boutique consulting senior partner engages sub-consultant on EU client engagement (regulatory workstream, market entry study, M&A due diligence, ESG advisory) → sub-consultant activates their own AI tool on the client deliverable (drafting, analysis, research, modeling, methodology development) → senior partner unaware of specific tool use → EU client submits sub-consultant name in sub-processor disclosure to MSA under contractual cooperation duty → OR MSA audits sub-consultant's own AI vendor under Article 12 → OR MSA audits senior partner's proposal management vendor under Article 12 → MSA obtains sub-consultant name, named AI tool, named deliverable, named engagement, named EU client → MSA issues Article 99 procedural letter naming the sub-consultant → letter is forwarded to the Geneva boutique through the EU client contractual notice clause → senior partner receives letter with sub-consultant name they cannot trace to current sub-consultant because the sub-consultant has handed over → 30-day response window → senior partner must produce sub-consultant AI authorization record, sub-consultant AI literacy credential, sub-consultant supervision protocol, named-individual senior partner oversight documentation → senior partner cannot produce → first Genevoise peer firm has been named in a sub-consultant-specific MSA letter, peer firm is now in the public reference position, second wave of MSA letters is in the pipeline → first service to publish the sub-consultant AI authorization roster audit response architecture owns the C315 P03 sub-consultant-MSA-letter category.

**Implication:** Sub-consultant AI authorization roster audit response architecture is a discrete, named C315 P03 service. ### Finding 03: Sub-Consultant Handover Window — Monthly Turnover Creates Article 26 Named-Sub-Principal Discontinuity

**The finding:** The Geneva boutique consulting senior partner who engages sub-consultants on a monthly turnover basis now faces a structurally permanent Article 26 named-sub-principal attribution discontinuity. The senior partner's Article 26 deployer obligations attach to named-sub-consultant individuals (Article 26 named sub-principals). The named-sub-consultant individuals turn over monthly. The senior partner's named-sub-consultant roster changes monthly. The carrier attestation refers to named sub-consultants as of a specific date. The MSA audit response refers to named sub-consultants as of a specific date. The named sub-consultant from the carrier attestation is no longer the named sub-consultant on the engagement. The named sub-consultant from the MSA audit response is no longer the named sub-consultant on the engagement. The new named sub-consultant has not signed an AI authorization record, has not completed an AI literacy credential, has not been documented in a supervision protocol. The senior partner is signing carrier attestations and producing MSA audit responses for named sub-consultants who no longer work on the engagement. The discontinuity is structural. The discontinuity is not addressable by annual attestation cadence. The discontinuity requires a handover-window architecture that produces named-sub-consultant continuity documentation at the rate of sub-consultant turnover — not annually, not quarterly, monthly. The senior partner has not built the handover-window architecture. The senior partner has not recognized the discontinuity. The MSA letter, the carrier attestation clause, and the FDPIC Art.63 file have all surfaced the discontinuity simultaneously.

**Mechanism:** Geneva boutique consulting senior partner engages sub-consultant A on EU client engagement → sub-consultant A signs AI authorization record, completes AI literacy credential, is documented in supervision protocol → sub-consultant A hands over after engagement workstream closes (typical 4-12 week cycle) → sub-consultant B is engaged on the next workstream → sub-consultant B has not signed AI authorization record, has not completed AI literacy credential, has not been documented in supervision protocol → senior partner signs carrier attestation referring to sub-consultant A as named sub-consultant (sub-consultant A is no longer on engagement) → MSA audit response refers to sub-consultant A as named sub-consultant (sub-consultant A is no longer on engagement) → FDPIC Art.63 file refers to sub-consultant A as named sub-consultant (sub-consultant A is no longer on engagement) → sub-consultant B's AI use on the engagement is undocumented under Article 26, Article 4, nFADP Art.63, and carrier attestation → first Genevoise peer firm has been cited for sub-consultant handover-window discontinuity, peer firm is now in the public reference position, second wave of discontinuity citations is in the pipeline → first service to publish the sub-consultant handover-window named-sub-principal continuity architecture owns the C315 P03 handover-window category.

**Implication:** Sub-consultant handover-window named-sub-principal continuity architecture is a discrete, named C315 P03 service. It produces a pre-built handover-window documentation system: per-engagement named-sub-consultant roster (current + past, with dates), per-engagement named-sub-consultant AI authorization record (signed by each named sub-consultant individual at engagement start), per-engagement named-sub-consultant AI literacy credential (Article 4 satisfaction, dated, refreshable at handover), per-engagement named-sub-consultant supervision protocol (Article 14 satisfaction, with named escalation chain), per-engagement handover documentation (sub-consultant A offboarding + sub-consultant B onboarding, with AI authorization continuity record, AI literacy continuity record, supervision continuity record), per-engagement MSA audit response (referencing current named sub-consultant only, with past sub-consultant reference as Article 26 named-sub-principal handover record), per-engagement carrier attestation (referencing current named sub-consultant only, with past sub-consultant reference as carrier attestation addendum), per-engagement FDPIC Art.63 file (referencing current and past named sub-consultants as nFADP personal liability extension). The system is refreshed at the rate of sub-consultant turnover — not annual attestation cadence. The first service to publish this architecture owns the C315 P03 handover-window category.

**Ready-to-use language:** "My sub-consultant handed over three weeks ago. The new sub-consultant is using AI on the same EU client deliverable. The carrier attestation refers to the old sub-consultant. The MSA audit response refers to the old sub-consultant. The FDPIC file refers to the old sub-consultant. The new sub-consultant has not signed an AI authorization record, has not completed an AI literacy credential, has not been documented in a supervision protocol.
