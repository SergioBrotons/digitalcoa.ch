# 01 — ICP: Geneva Consulting Buyers

## Target Segment: C-Suite & Senior Management of Geneva Institutions

### Buyer Profile
- **Type:** CFOs, CROs, CEOs, Board Members, Managing Directors
- **Organization:** Banks, asset managers, commodity traders, multinationals, NGOs, Geneva institutions
- **Budget authority:** CHF 50K-500K+ per engagement
- **Decision-making:** Often direct hire or board recommendation; faster than corporate procurement

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Pain Points | Advisory Need |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| CFO | Rate normalization, reporting changes, Basel IV, FX | Treasury/risk/regulatory advisory |
| CRO | Credit risk, operational resilience, AML enforcement | Risk framework advisory |
| CEO | Strategic positioning, regulatory change, talent | Strategic advisory |
| Board/NED | Governance oversight, ESG, regulatory compliance | Board advisory, governance review |
| COO | Operational efficiency, digital transformation | Transformation recovery, process advisory |

### Geneva ICP Sectors
- **Financial institutions:** Banks, asset managers, commodity traders
- **Corporate:** Multinationals with Geneva HQ
- **NGO/IO:** International organizations, foundations
- **Family offices:** UHNW family governance

### Pain Points (C123 Update)
1. **Basel IV implementation pressure** — FINMA enforcing Basel IV RWA floor; Geneva banks restructuring SME lending portfolios
2. **DORA operational resilience compliance** — EU DORA applied by proxy; Geneva financial institutions needing ICT risk framework implementation
3. **Geopolitical de-risking** — US tariff escalation affecting Geneva multinational supply chains; strategic restructuring advisory
4. **Board ESG governance requirements** — FINMA ESG disclosure expectations tightening; boards needing ESG oversight frameworks
5. **Digital transformation program recovery** — Multiple Geneva institutions with failed/budget-overrun digital programs seeking rescue
6. **Talent gap in specialized risk** — CARB/CRS 2.0, DORA, ESG creating demand for specialized interim risk managers

### Buying Triggers
- FINMA examination finding requiring remediation
- Regulatory deadline approaching (DORA, Basel IV, ESG disclosure)
- Board ESG resolution requiring implementation
- Digital transformation program in distress
- Geopolitical event creating urgent advisory need

### Buying Barriers
- Existing advisory relationships (Big 4 dominance)
- Perceived conflict with existing audit relationships
- Board/ownership approval required
- Project scoping and budget approval process

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva corporate; English for international
- Relationship-driven — referral preferred
- Credibility and track record essential
- Discrete — no public marketing of client engagements
