**Status:** FINMA enforcing 72.5% RWA floor from January 2026
**Implementation challenge:** SME credit repricing, portfolio restructuring, capital planning updates
**Impact:** Geneva cantonal and regional banks most affected; advisory demand for restructuring
**Timeline:** Active implementation; first full-year reporting under new rules 2026

### 2. DORA Operational Resilience
**Status:** EU DORA applied to Swiss financial sector by proxy; FINMA guidance expected Q3 2026
**Key requirements:** ICT risk framework, incident reporting, third-party ICT risk management, digital operational resilience testing
**Impact:** All FINMA-supervised institutions (banks, asset managers, payment institutions)
**Timeline:** Q3 2026 FINMA guidance; implementation deadline likely 2026-2027

### 3. FINMA ESG Disclosure Expectations
**Status:** Tightening expectations for supervised institutions; board ESG governance becoming examination focus
**Key requirements:** Board oversight framework, ESG committee structure, disclosure quality
**Impact:** Listed companies, financial institutions, large corporates
**Timeline:** Ongoing; 2026 annual report cycle first full application

### 4. US Tariff Escalation — Geneva Multinational Impact
**Status:** US tariff escalation April 2026 affecting Geneva multinationals with US supply chain exposure
**Key issues:** Supply chain restructuring, pricing strategy, FX hedging, tariff mitigation
**Impact:** Geneva-headquartered industrials, commodity traders, consumer goods companies
**Timeline:** Immediate; ongoing negotiations

### 5. CARB/CRS 2.0 Reporting
**Status:** Common Reporting Standard version 2.0 implementation; first reporting cycle Q3 2026
**Key requirements:** Enhanced due diligence, real-time reporting, data quality
**Impact:** Financial institutions, Geneva banks, asset managers
**Timeline:** Q3 2026 reporting deadline
