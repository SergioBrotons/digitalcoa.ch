# The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist

## Q2 2026 Edition — Geneva Finance Sector

---

### Before You Begin: Three Quick Questions

Answer yes or no to each. If you answer yes to any of these, this checklist is for you.

1. **Do you use any AI tool — including chatbots, writing assistants, or portfolio analytics software — in your client communications, reporting, or investment research?**
2. **Do you serve any clients based in the European Union?**
3. **Do you have any documented policy or written procedure covering how your firm uses AI?**

---

### Part 1: Your AI Footprint

**Quick inventory — 5 questions.**

- [ ] Do you know exactly which AI tools your staff use? (List them: CRM AI, portfolio analytics, chatbot for client comms, document drafting tools, etc.)
- [ ] Do you know where your AI vendor stores its data? (Swiss server? EU? United States?)
- [ ] Do you know which AI vendors have contracts with your firm — and what those contracts say about data ownership and regulatory liability?
- [ ] Do any of your AI tools process client financial data? (Names, assets, investment preferences, portfolio holdings?)
- [ ] Have you documented which AI tools could qualify as "automated decision-making" under Swiss or EU law?

---

### Part 2: Your Regulatory Exposure

**Three frameworks. Most Geneva boutique WMs are exposed under all three without knowing it.**

#### Federal AI Ordinance (Switzerland — in force since January 1, 2025)
- [ ] Has your firm conducted a documented AI risk assessment?
- [ ] Do you have written procedures for AI tool deployment and monitoring?
- [ ] Is there a named individual responsible for AI governance at your firm?
- [ ] Have you documented which AI systems make or materially support decisions affecting clients?

#### EU AI Act (European Union — GPAI obligations in force since August 2, 2025)
- [ ] Does your firm place any AI-powered investment tools on the EU market or serve EU-based clients?
- [ ] Do you use GPAI models (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, etc.) for portfolio analytics, client reporting, or research?
- [ ] If yes to above: have you documented your GPAI provider transparency obligations?
- [ ] Do you have an EU-based authorized representative appointed, as required if placing AI on the EU market?

#### FADP Article 21 (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection — automated decision-making)
- [ ] Do any of your AI tools make decisions that affect clients without human review?
- [ ] Have you documented the data minimization and purpose limitation basis for each AI tool?
- [ ] Can your clients request human review of AI-assisted decisions? Do they know they can?

---

### Part 3: Data Sovereignty Gap

**This is the Geneva-specific advantage — or risk.**

- [ ] Have you documented the data jurisdiction of each AI tool your firm uses?
- [ ] Do your AI vendor contracts specify data sovereignty terms (no US surveillance access, Swiss/EU data residency)?
- [ ] If using BigTech AI (Google, Microsoft, OpenAI): have you assessed the US CLOUD Act exposure for your client data?
- [ ] Do you communicate your data sovereignty approach to clients? Does it appear in your client onboarding documentation?

**Why this matters for Geneva WMs (April 2026):**

Swiss institutional trust was confirmed "excellent" internationally (Federal Council, April 1, 2026). Your clients — particularly Swiss HNW clients and Swiss institutional investors — are operating in a context where data sovereignty and institutional trust are tangible competitive factors. Choosing Swiss-hosted AI infrastructure isn't just a compliance decision. It's a client communication decision.

---

### Part 4: What to Do First

**Ranked by urgency. Three actions. No ambiguity.**

**Priority 1 — Do this before August 2, 2026:**
Document your EU AI Act GPAI exposure. If you serve EU clients and use any external AI model for investment research, portfolio analytics, or client reporting: you have GPAI obligations already in force. The August 2, 2026 deadline is for high-risk AI conformity assessments — but GPAI transparency obligations have been in force since August 2, 2025. Start with a written inventory of every AI tool in use, what data it processes, and which clients it affects.

**Priority 2 — Do this before your next FINMA audit or client ESG review:**
Align with FINMA Guidance 08/2024 on AI governance supervisory expectations. This means: named AI governance responsibility, documented AI tool deployment procedures, and third-party AI provider risk assessment (your BigTech AI vendors are your outsourcing risk).

**Priority 3 — Do this before your next client onboarding cycle:**
Add a data sovereignty statement to your client documentation. Specify where client data is processed and stored, which AI tools are used, and the client's right to human review of AI-assisted decisions. This is a client trust document as much as a compliance document.
