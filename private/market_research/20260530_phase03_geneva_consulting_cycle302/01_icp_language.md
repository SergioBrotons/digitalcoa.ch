# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems

## Phase 03 — C302 | Geneva Consulting (2–50 employees)

---

## 1. Primary Problem Framing

The Geneva consulting boutique ICP frames AI risk through **personal accountability and methodology defensibility**. The managing director or partner is personally named — they cannot hide behind firm structure.

**Characteristic phrases:**

- *"I need to be able to show who asked what question and got what answer — if a client asks me."*
- *"My proposal methodology needs to hold up if the client challenges it. AI-assisted doesn't cut it as an explanation."*
- *"I signed the carrier attestation. What does that actually commit me to?"*
- *"My client sent an AI governance questionnaire. I have two weeks to respond. I don't know what they're actually asking for."*
- *"If I use a sub-consultant who uses AI — am I responsible for their AI use?"*
- *"I can't delegate the accountability. I'm the one who signs."*

---

## 2. Language Vectors — How Problems Are Described

### A. Accountability Gap Language
- *"I'm the one who signs. So I need to know what's happening."*
- *"I can't just say 'our AI does such-and-such.' I need to know what decision it influenced."*
- *"The buck stops with me — but I don't have visibility into the AI layer."*
- *"Personal liability is real. nFADP Article 63 — my name is on that."*

### B. Client Questionnaire Language
- *"My client sent an AI governance questionnaire"*
- *"Corporate client RFP now includes AI governance as a scored section"*
- *"The client's compliance team is asking questions I can't answer"*
- *"Institutional client due diligence — they're getting more detailed every quarter"*
- *"The questionnaire asks for our AI authorization records. What records?"*

### C. Methodology Defensibility Language
- *"My proposal methodology needs to be defensible"*
- *"AI-assisted is not an explanation — it's a label"*
- *"I need to show the reasoning chain, including where AI was used"*
- *"If a client challenges my recommendation, I need to reconstruct the decision process"*
- *"Evidence reconstruction — I didn't know that was a specific requirement"*

### D. Carrier Attestation Language
- *"I signed the carrier form. What did I actually attest to?"*
- *"Carrier form v4 — they want actual records now, not attestations"*
- *"The insurance carrier is asking for 'last three authorization records' — we don't have those"*
- *"Attestation vs. evidence — I didn't realize there was a difference until now"*

### E. Sub-Consultant Delegation Language
- *"If I bring in a sub-consultant who uses AI — am I responsible?"*
- *"AI delegation chain — who owns the documentation at each step?"*
- *"I can't audit every sub-consultant's AI tool stack"*
- *"The client doesn't care that it was a sub-consultant's AI — they hold me accountable"*

### F. nFADP / Regulatory Language
- *"Article 63 — I heard this is personal liability. What does it actually mean for me?"*
- *"The Swiss data protection law — where does AI fit in?"*
- *"nFADP compliance — is this like GDPR? Do I need a DPO?"*
- *"Personal data in AI systems — wealth management clients' data is the most sensitive category"*

---

## 3. Emotional State Markers

The ICP is in **active anxiety**, not passive concern. The triggers are:

1. **Receiving a client AI governance questionnaire** — forces engagement, creates deadline pressure
2. **Signing a carrier attestation** — creates personal commitment without full understanding
3. **Peer enforcement events** — a colleague or peer firm was sanctioned or challenged
4. **Proposal being challenged** — methodology defensibility becomes concrete, not abstract
5. **News of regulatory enforcement** — particularly U.S. events that circulate in Geneva professional circles

**Emotional signature:** *"I know I should do something about this. I don't know what 'something' looks like. And I'm the one who signs."*

---

## 4. Trusted Information Sources

- Geneva professional associations (SBA for legal-adjacent consultants, industry-specific bodies)
- Peer networks and informal circles
- Carrier communications (read carefully, often with confusion)
- U.S. enforcement events that circulate in Geneva professional circles
- ODAGE and similar professional body events

---

## 5. Language That Converts

**What makes them stop and act:**

- *"You're the one who signs. Here's what that actually commits you to."*
- *"The carrier form v4 isn't an attestation — it's an evidence request. There's a difference."*
- *"Client AI governance questionnaires are a new category of forcing function. They're coming."*
- *"Your sub-consultant's AI use is your documentation problem."*
- *"The reconstruction isn't difficult. It's structurally impossible. Here's the difference."*

---

## 6. Language to Avoid

- Abstract compliance language ("you should align with nFADP")
- Generic AI governance frameworks without Swiss-specific grounding
- "AI policy" language — they don't have an AI policy problem, they have an **evidence problem**
- Technical control language — they need accountability language, not technical control language
- Consulting-speak that obscures personal stakes
