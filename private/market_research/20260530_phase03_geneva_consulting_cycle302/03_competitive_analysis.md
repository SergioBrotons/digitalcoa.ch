# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

## Phase 03 — C302 | Geneva Consulting (2–50 employees)

---

## 1. Landscape Overview

The Geneva consulting boutique AI governance market has three broad categories of solution providers. None serve the ICP adequately.

---

## 2. Category 1: Large Enterprise Governance Platforms

**Providers:** ServiceNow, Microsoft Purview, SAP Governance Risk, OneTrust, TrustArc

**What they offer:**
- Enterprise-grade AI governance frameworks
- Policy management, risk assessment, compliance tracking
- Integration with large enterprise data systems
- Audit trails, access controls, data lineage tracking

**What's missing for Geneva consulting ICP:**
- Designed for 500+ employee organizations with dedicated compliance teams
- Pricing calibrated for enterprise, not boutique (€50K–€500K+ annual contracts)
- Implementation requires IT departments, project managers, months of deployment
- Technical architecture assumes enterprise data infrastructure
- No Swiss nFADP-specific configuration
- No Geneva professional standard mapping
- No carrier form v4 evidence documentation module
- No sub-consultant delegation chain documentation

**Why the ICP can't use them:** A 12-person Geneva consulting boutique cannot deploy ServiceNow. The gap between what these platforms offer and what a small consulting firm can operationalize is unbridgeable. These are not solutions for this ICP.

---

## 3. Category 2: Professional Services Firm Frameworks

**Providers:** Big Four frameworks (Deloitte AI Governance, PwC AI Framework, EY AI Risk), large Swiss consulting firms

**What they offer:**
- Frameworks for AI governance and documentation
- Maturity assessments
- Policy templates
- Training programs
- Compliance audits

**What's missing for Geneva consulting ICP:**
- Frameworks designed for large organization compliance, not boutique operations
- Deliverables are documents and assessments, not operational evidence systems
- Engagement model requires ongoing retainer or large project fee
- No client questionnaire response capability
- No carrier form v4 evidence documentation
- No methodology defensibility tool — they produce a framework, not evidence
- "Framework" is the product — the ICP still has to implement it themselves
- No small-firm pricing model (engagements typically start at CHF 30K+)

**Why the ICP can't use them:** The Big Four engagement model is incompatible with boutique firm economics. The ICP gets a framework document, not an evidence system. The documentation debt remains.

---

## 4. Category 3: Generic AI Compliance Tools

**Providers:** Various AI compliance startups, GDPR tools, data protection platforms

**What they offer:**
- GDPR compliance checklists
- AI tool inventorying
- Policy generators
- Training modules
- Data mapping tools

**What's missing for Geneva consulting ICP:**
- Generic GDPR tools miss the Swiss nFADP specificities
- No carrier form v4 evidence documentation
- No client AI governance questionnaire response capability
- No methodology defensibility module
- No sub-consultant delegation chain documentation
- No Geneva professional standard mapping
- No personal liability (Art. 63) specific guidance
- No proposal AI methodology documentation
- English-language dominant, limited FR/DE localization
- No understanding of Geneva professional culture and communication norms

**Why the ICP can't use them:** Generic tools don't address the specific obligation set the ICP faces. They produce the appearance of compliance without the substance of evidence.

---

## 5. Category 4: Nonexistent — The Actual Market Gap

**What the ICP needs and cannot find:**

###5A. Client Questionnaire Response System
- **Missing:** A system that helps the ICP respond to client AI governance questionnaires within deadline
- **Current reality:** The ICP either DIY with a consultant, produces a generic policy document, or ignores the questionnaire
- **What they need:** A structured response builder that maps to common questionnaire frameworks, produces client-ready documentation, and generates evidence artifacts

### 5B. Carrier Form v4 Evidence Documentation
- **Missing:** A tool that captures and organizes the "last three authorization records" in a carrier-ready format
- **Current reality:** The ICP tries to reconstruct retrospectively, fails, and either delays submission or submits incomplete documentation
- **What they need:** An evidence capture system that creates compliant records going forward and retroactively organizes available data

### 5C. Methodology Defensibility Documentation
- **Missing:** A system that documents the AI's role in any specific client deliverable — without making the ICP sound like they outsourced judgment
- **Current reality:** The ICP either over-discloses (damages perceived intellectual authority) or under-discloses (creates liability)
- **What they need:** A documentation format that shows AI-assisted reasoning with human oversight intact, suitable for client scrutiny

### 5D. Sub-Consultant Delegation Chain Documentation
- **Missing:** A framework and documentation system for managing AI governance in the supply chain
- **Current reality:** No standard exists; the ICP provides vague reassurances or ignores the question
- **What they need:** A delegation documentation template that establishes accountability at each step, with evidence requirements

### 5E. nFADP Article 63 Personal Liability Guidance
- **Missing:** Swiss-specific, personally-relevant guidance on what Article 63 liability means for the managing director of a small consulting firm
- **Current reality:** Generic data protection guidance; nFADP articles without operational interpretation; no one translating this to personal exposure
- **What they need:** A clear explanation of personal exposure, what documentation mitigates it, and what the "responsible person" designation actually requires

### 5F. Geneva Professional Standard Evidence Package
- **Missing:** A defined "Geneva standard" for AI governance documentation that the ICP can demonstrate
- **Current reality:** The ICP has no reference point; professional events acknowledge the problem without defining the standard
- **What they need:** A concrete evidence package that maps to Geneva professional expectations, carrier requirements, and client questionnaire standards

## 7. The Positioning Opportunity

**The market offers frameworks. We offer evidence.**

The gap is not in awareness or frameworks — the ICP has attended enough events and read enough guidance to know the problem exists. The gap is in **operational evidence systems** that a small consulting boutique can actually use.

The ICP does not need another framework. They need a system that:
1. Captures evidence as work happens
2. Produces client-ready questionnaire responses
3. Generates carrier form v4 compliant documentation
4. Documents methodology defensibility per engagement
5. Manages the sub-consultant delegation chain
6. Translates nFADP Art. 63 into personal action items

No competitor offers this for the Geneva consulting boutique segment.

## 8. Language to Use vs. Avoid

**Use:**
- evidence system (not framework)
- documentation that actually works (not policy template)
- built for your size (not enterprise-scaled)
- carrier form v4 ready (not generic compliance)
- Geneva standard (not generic international standard)
- personally accountable (not just firm-level)

**Avoid:**
- AI governance framework
- compliance platform
- enterprise solution
- policy management
- risk assessment tool
