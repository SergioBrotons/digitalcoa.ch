### 3. FADP Data Minimization vs. AI Utility Tension — Healthcare Sector Critical (NET-NEW C60 — Sharper Framing)
**Source:** Homburger.ch + Phase 01 C60 synthesis

Federal Council AI strategy objectives include "protection of fundamental rights, including economic freedom" — but for healthcare, the fundamental rights dimension is patient privacy and data protection. The FADP data minimization principle directly conflicts with AI system requirements for comprehensive data. Healthcare AI governance documentation must explicitly address this tension.

**Healthcare implications (NET-NEW C60):** Medical practices using AI for diagnostic support, patient monitoring, or administrative automation must document: (1) the medical necessity of the AI use, (2) the data minimization steps taken, (3) the human oversight mechanism, and (4) the patient's right to human review of AI-assisted decisions. This is not optional — FADP Art. 21 requires it for automated decision-making affecting patients.
