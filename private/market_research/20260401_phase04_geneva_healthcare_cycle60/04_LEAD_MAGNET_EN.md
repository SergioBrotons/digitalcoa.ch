## The Geneva Medical Practice Has 91 Days

Swissdamed — the Swiss Medical Devices and Vigilance System — becomes enforceable on July 1, 2026. If your practice uses medical devices that incorporate AI, or if you use AI tools for clinical decision support, patient monitoring, or administrative tasks, you have approximately 91 days to confirm your compliance posture.

This checklist is for educational purposes. It covers the three regulatory layers that apply to AI use in Geneva medical practices: Swissdamed, the Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP), and the EU AI Act.

---

## Part I: The Three-Layer Regulatory Stack

### Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~91 Days)
The Swiss Medical Devices and Vigilance System requires adverse event reporting for medical devices. If your AI tools constitute or are incorporated in medical devices, Swissdamed reporting obligations apply to your practice.

### FADP Art. 21 — Ongoing
If you use AI to make automated decisions about patients — even preliminary assessments — you must inform patients and give them the right to request human review.

### Federal AI Ordinance — 15+ Months Operative
Human oversight requirements apply to AI-assisted clinical decisions. Document who reviews AI outputs before clinical use.

### EU AI Act — GPAI Obligations Already In Force
Since August 2, 2025, GPAI obligations are in force. If you serve EU patients or use EU-sourced AI tools, the EU AI Act applies. By August 2, 2026, high-risk AI systems (including clinical AI decision support) require conformity assessment.

---

## Part II: The Medical Practice AI Governance Audit

Answer these five questions:

1. **Swissdamed:** Have we documented our adverse event reporting process and designated responsible personnel?
2. **FADP:** Do we have patient consent protocols for AI-assisted clinical decisions?
3. **Federal AI Ordinance:** For each AI tool used in clinical contexts, is there a documented human oversight step?
4. **EU AI Act:** Do we serve EU patients or use AI tools sourced from EU providers?
5. **Data sovereignty:** Are our AI tools hosted on Swiss or EU infrastructure?

*If you answered "no" or "not yet" to any question, your practice has an active compliance gap.*

## Closing

The Geneva medical practice that addresses Swissdamed now and builds AI governance documentation over the next 91 days will be in a defensible compliance position. The practice that waits will face enforcement with incomplete documentation.

The regulatory stack for medical AI is not optional. The question is whether to address it proactively or reactively.
