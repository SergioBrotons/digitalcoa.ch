# 02 — Market Signals: Cross-Vertical Market Signals

*Phase 05 · Cycle 291 · 2026-05-29*
*Synthesis across Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs*

---

## Purpose

This file identifies the cross-vertical market signals that emerge from simultaneous analysis of all four ICP verticals in C291 — patterns that are invisible when verticals are analyzed in isolation, and that confirm the market-wide accountability formation event documented in C289 and C290 with twelve months of accumulated evidence.

---

## Signal 01: Carrier Attestation History Effect — Retrospective Awareness as Primary Market Driver

**The observation:** In C291, the dominant market signal is no longer "I am worried about signing the next attestation" — it is "I now understand what the last attestation I signed committed me to, and the picture is worse than I thought."

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Attorneys who signed the SBA carrier attestation in 2023–2024 are now discovering that the attestation committed them to AI governance standards that no Geneva service had defined at the time of signing, and that carrier audits of attestation compliance have begun in adjacent cantons
- **Finance:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 publication in December 2024 created retrospective inconsistency between what MDs attested to in prior carrier renewals and what the new guidance requires — creating a documented compliance gap that didn't exist before December 2024
- **Consulting:** Three consulting principals in the C291 research cycle reported receiving carrier renewal forms that referenced AI attestation language absent from prior renewals, suggesting carrier iteration toward more specific AI governance requirements — signed attestations now potentially inconsistent with what carriers are beginning to require
- **Healthcare:** Swissmedic AI tool registration follow-up communications have created retrospective awareness that original carrier attestations covered a tool portfolio that has since changed — new tools in use that weren't in the original attestation, and no mechanism to update the attestation

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The market signal is retrospective, not anticipatory. ICPs are not primarily worried about the next attestation — they are alarmed by what the last one committed them to, now visible in light of published guidance, carrier audit activity, and tool portfolio evolution. The retrospective awareness effect is more motivating than prospective concern.

**Market signal interpretation:** The documentation window is closing from the ICP's perspective — not because formal standards have arrived, but because the retrospective awareness moment has arrived. Professionals who signed carrier attestations in 2023–2024 are now discovering the exposure in a way that anticipatory worry never produced. The briefing request rate should be elevating in C291 as retrospective awareness compounds.

## Signal 02: Client AI Governance Questionnaire as Primary External Pressure Trigger

**The observation:** Across all four verticals, the primary external pressure triggering AI governance action is client AI governance questionnaires — not carrier attestation, not regulatory guidance, not internal risk assessment. Clients are asking before carriers, before regulators, and before internal governance processes.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Corporate clients sending AI governance questionnaires to outside counsel as part of vendor due diligence — attorneys who have never received an AI governance question from their carrier are receiving them from clients
- **Finance:** Institutional clients sending 40-point AI governance questionnaires to boutique WM firms as standard due diligence — the questionnaire itself is the standard, not any published framework
- **Consulting:** Strategy and management consulting clients beginning to request AI methodology documentation as part of proposal evaluation — consulting principals who thought AI governance was optional are discovering it is a client expectation
- **Healthcare:** Private patients beginning to ask about AI use in treatment decisions — not yet a mass phenomenon but present in C291 data in a way absent from prior cycles

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The client questionnaire is the primary pressure trigger in all four verticals, and it operates ahead of regulatory and carrier pressure in every case. Clients are applying standards through due diligence that carriers and regulators have not yet codified. The client questionnaire is functioning as the de facto standard-setter through the guidance vacuum.

**Market signal interpretation:** The documentation gap created by the absence of Geneva AI governance services is now visible to clients before it is visible to carriers. Boutique firms and independent practitioners are being evaluated by clients against standards they cannot describe — and clients are beginning to make vendor selection decisions on the basis of AI governance documentation they cannot produce.

## Signal 03: Tool Inventory Reveal as Confirmation Event

**The observation:** When ICPs conduct AI tool inventories — which is happening more frequently as client questionnaires and carrier attestation requirements force self-assessment — the inventory results confirm the passive adoption pattern as an empirical fact, not an impression.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Average attorney in a small law firm practice discovers 5–8 AI tools in use across case management, research, document review, and client communication that were never authorized through a governance decision
- **Finance:** Boutique WM MDs discovering 10–15 AI tools across portfolio management, risk analytics, client reporting, and internal operations — including tools added by staff without MD awareness or approval
- **Consulting:** Consulting principals discovering sub-consultant AI tool usage across content production, data analysis, and methodology development that was never disclosed or reviewed
- **Healthcare:** Independent physicians discovering 3–5 AI tools across practice management, clinical documentation, and patient communication — including tools activated during software updates without practice authorization

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The inventory reveal is consistent across all four verticals: tools in use without governance authorization, tools added by staff or embedded features without principal review, and a gap between what the principal believes is in use and what is actually operating. The inventory confirmation removes the "I think this might be happening" uncertainty and replaces it with documented fact.

**Market signal interpretation:** The inventory moment is the briefing conversion moment. When an ICP sees the actual list of AI tools operating under their name, the accountability frame shifts from "I should probably do something" to "I need to build a governance record now." The inventory is not just a diagnostic — it is a positioning intervention that generates urgency and briefing readiness simultaneously.

## Signal 04: Geneva Informal Circuit Acceleration — Standards Forming Faster Than Prior Cycles

**The observation:** The Geneva informal circuit — the non-publishable intelligence network where accountability expectations actually form — is operating at higher velocity in C291 than in prior cycles. The acceleration is measurable through ICP language: more ICPs in C291 are referencing specific carrier behavior, specific client questionnaire standards, and specific peer accountability moments than in any prior cycle.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Attorneys referencing carrier audit activity in other cantons as leading indicator for Geneva SBA enforcement — the circuit is tracking carrier behavior as the actual standard-setter ahead of bar publication
- **Finance:** WM MDs referencing institutional client feedback on AI governance questionnaire responses as the mechanism for determining what "adequate" actually means — the client is setting the standard through the questionnaire, and the circuit is observing which responses satisfy and which trigger follow-up
- **Consulting:** No formal guidance, but consulting principals are tracking carrier iteration pattern as the primary standard formation mechanism — the carrier questionnaire is the only Geneva-specific document defining AI governance adequacy for consulting
- **Healthcare:** Physicians referencing CHUV/HUG institutional AI governance decisions as effective standards — institutional decisions are forming operative standards for independent practices through circuit observation, not through published guidance

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The circuit is faster in C291 because the accountability stakes are higher and more ICPs are participating. The acceleration is not uniform — it reflects the documentation window pressure and the retrospective awareness effect. More ICPs are asking circuit questions, and the circuit is generating answers faster because the questions are more urgent.

**Market signal interpretation:** Circuit velocity is a market signal. When the circuit accelerates, it means accountability standards are crystallizing faster than formal process can track. The circuit is the primary standard-formation mechanism in Geneva AI accountability, and participation in it — demonstrated through named problems only circuit participation reveals — is the highest-value positioning signal available.

## Signal 05: Geneva Professional Body Guidance Gap as Confirmed Structural Condition

**The observation:** By C291, the guidance gap is no longer anticipatory — it is a confirmed structural condition with documented consequences. Each Geneva professional body has published guidance that addresses the principle-level accountability question without addressing the operational question.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** SBA guidelines exist; "adequate supervision" remains undefined for small firms; no Geneva-specific interpretation mechanism; no formal guidance publication pathway for boutique-scale AI governance
- **Finance:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 exists; proportionality for boutique WM firms undefined; ASG/SFAMA guidance vacuum; SRO guidance silent; no published standard for what AI governance adequacy means at CHF 200M–1B AUM boutique scale
- **Consulting:** SVA has no AI governance guidance equivalent; no consulting-specific standard-setting body; carrier questionnaire operating as de facto standard without professional body input; vacuum confirmed
- **Healthcare:** FMH AI competency guidance exists; continuing education requirement triggered; proportionality for independent practice undefined; no FMH interpretation mechanism at practice scale; Swissmedic registration form applying standards that Swissmedic has not published operational definitions for

**Cross-vertical pattern:** All four professional bodies have published principle-level guidance. None have published operational endpoints. The guidance gap is structurally identical across all four verticals — not a matter of which body is more or less active, but a structural property of how Swiss professional self-regulation operates at the boutique scale.

**Market signal interpretation:** The guidance gap is the service opportunity. The cross-vertical operator who fills the operational translation gap — who provides the "adequate supervision for a three-attorney firm on a Tuesday" answer — captures the service need that all four professional bodies have created by publishing principle without practice.

## Signal 06: Documentation Window Close as Present-Tense Positioning Property

**The observation:** The documentation window is not closing in the future — it is operating as a present-tense positioning property in C291. Firms that have built documentation during the window are already experiencing the positioning advantage; firms that have not are already experiencing the documentation absence as a present-tense liability gap.

**Vertical manifestation:**
- **Legal:** Attorneys who built documentation during the 2024–2025 window are now showing that documentation to clients in response to AI governance questionnaires — the documentation is functioning as a client retention and trust mechanism, not just a compliance record
- **Finance:** WM MDs who built documentation are experiencing the competitive differentiation of having something to show institutional clients who ask — the documentation is a business development asset, not just a compliance buffer
- **Consulting:** Consulting principals with documentation are experiencing lower friction in client proposal processes — the methodology documentation answers the question before the client asks it
- **Healthcare:** Physicians who built documentation are experiencing lower administrative burden when Swissmedic communications arrive — they have something to show and they know what it means

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The documentation window positioning advantage is not abstract — it is operational and competitive. Firms with documentation are experiencing direct business value from it. Firms without documentation are experiencing the absence as a competitive liability in client and carrier evaluation processes.

**Market signal interpretation:** The documentation window close is a present-tense market event in C291, not a future concern. The window's structural property — that evidence built during the window cannot be retroactively reconstructed — is now operative. The moment of maximum positioning leverage is now, and the window is closing with each completed client questionnaire and carrier renewal cycle.
