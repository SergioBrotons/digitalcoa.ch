# 03 — Competitive Analysis: Cross-Vertical Competitive Landscape

*Phase 05 · Cycle 291 · 2026-05-29*
*Synthesis across Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs*

---

## Purpose

This file maps what exists in the Geneva professional AI accountability market across all four verticals in C291, identifies what remains uniformly missing, and isolates the structural market positioning opportunity that emerges from cross-vertical analysis with twelve months of accumulated evidence confirming the competitive gap as permanent, not transitional.

---

## The Cross-Vertical Market Structure: Confirmed in C291

The three-layer competitive structure identified in C289 is confirmed in C291, with the additional finding that Layer 1 providers have not moved to address Layer 2 gaps, and Layer 3 (accidental) providers have expanded their footprint.

**Layer 1 — Institutional-Grade (Overserved):** Present, unchanged, structurally mismatched to boutique scale. No movement in C291 toward proportionate offerings.

**Layer 2 — Boutique-Proportionate (Absent):** Remains uniformly absent across all four verticals. No market participant has entered the gap. The structural absence is confirmed as permanent, not transitional.

**Layer 3 — Accidental (Expanding):** AI vendors, carrier forms, and practice management updates have expanded their footprint in C291 — increasing the accountability problem faster than any governance infrastructure has developed to address it. Layer 3 is the primary growth driver of the accountability gap.

### What Layer 1 Does Not Address

**The proportionality problem:** Every Layer 1 provider operates at a scale that makes proportionate service delivery structurally impossible for the Geneva small-firm MD or independent physician. The economics don't work. A Big 4 consultancy cannot deliver CHF 2,200–2,800 engagements to 3-attorney firms. A general AI governance framework is not calibrated to Swiss carrier attestation requirements, SBA guidelines, or FINMA Guidance 08/2024 specifics.

**The Geneva-specific problem:** Layer 1 providers do not have sustained Geneva ecosystem participation. They have methodology, not circuit intelligence. They can produce AI governance frameworks that are correct at the principle level but operationally useless at the boutique scale because they don't know what "adequate supervision" means for a three-attorney firm on a Tuesday — and that specificity is the entire product.

## Layer 2: What Is Absent (Boutique-Proportionate, Structurally Missing)

### Missing Service 1: Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review (Cross-Vertical Universal)

**The confirmed gap:** No Geneva service in any vertical offers independent pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation forms from the signatory's personal liability perspective. This finding is confirmed in C291 with additional evidence: carrier attestation language has become more specific in C291 renewal cycles, increasing the personal exposure of signatories who sign without review.

**What it would do:**
- Analyze what the professional commits to personally when signing the carrier attestation
- Map commitments to Geneva-sector-specific adequacy standards (SBA for legal, FINMA Guidance 08/2024 for finance, FMH for healthcare, carrier iteration pattern for consulting)
- Identify gaps between current practice and what the attestation requires
- Produce documented basis for signing with confidence, negotiating carrier amendments, or documenting the review decision
- Build evidence architecture during the documentation window

**Why it doesn't exist:** Carrier forms are written by carrier legal teams for carrier risk evaluation. The form is not designed to be understood by the signatory — it is designed to commit the signatory to carrier-defined standards. No Geneva service has positioned itself to represent the signatory's interest against the carrier's document. The service category is structurally absent.

**Why it's more acute in C291:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 has given MDs a reference document that makes prior attestations retrospectively inconsistent. Attorneys are encountering carrier audit activity in adjacent cantons. Healthcare physicians are receiving Swissmedic follow-up communications. The retrospective awareness effect (Signal 01) is driving briefing requests that the pre-signing review service would resolve if it existed.

---

### Missing Service 2: AI Tool Inventory and Authorization Architecture (Cross-Vertical Universal)

**The confirmed gap:** No Geneva service in any vertical offers proportionate AI tool inventory and authorization architecture for small firms and independent practices. The inventory is the diagnostic; the authorization architecture is the output. Both are absent as a service category.

**What it would do:**
- Conduct systematic AI tool inventory across all practice systems and staff-initiated tool usage
- Map each tool to the accountability chain: who authorized it, what data it processes, what vendor terms apply, what carrier attestation coverage it affects
- Build authorization architecture that creates a documented governance decision trail — not a compliance document, but a decision record that shows what was decided, by whom, and why
- Identify tools operating without authorization and produce proportionate remediation options

**Why it doesn't exist:** The inventory service requires Geneva-calibrated methodology that understands carrier attestation implications, nFADP Article 63 personal liability mapping, and professional body guidance at the operational level. No existing provider has built this methodology for the boutique scale. The service is absent, not undersupplied.

**Why it's more acute in C291:** Tool inventories conducted in C291 are confirming 5–15 tools per practice with no authorization documentation. The inventory reveal is the confirmation event that generates briefing readiness. The service that would prevent the inventory reveal from being alarming — the authorization architecture built proactively — is absent.

---

### Missing Service 3: Client AI Governance Questionnaire Response Service (Cross-Vertical Universal)

**The confirmed gap:** No Geneva service in any vertical offers proportionate response support for client AI governance questionnaires. The client questionnaire is the primary external pressure trigger (Signal 02), and no Geneva service exists to help boutique firms and independent practitioners respond to it.

**What it would do:**
- Analyze the specific questionnaire received — map questions to Geneva-sector-specific accountability standards
- Identify gaps between current practice and what the questionnaire requires
- Produce a response that accurately represents the firm's AI governance state without overclaiming or creating false expectations
- Build documentation that addresses the questionnaire and creates residual documentation value beyond the immediate response
- Support the firm's ongoing AI governance development with the questionnaire as the benchmarking mechanism

**Why it doesn't exist:** The service requires Geneva-calibrated methodology for responding to institutional client questionnaires — understanding what the questions are actually asking, what the client expects in response, and what a proportionate Geneva small-firm response looks like. No existing provider has this methodology.

**Why it's more acute in C291:** Client AI governance questionnaires have become standard due diligence in finance, are emerging in legal and consulting, and are beginning in healthcare. The questionnaire is the client's mechanism for setting the AI governance standard, and the firm's response to it determines vendor status. No Geneva service exists to help firms respond accurately and build documentation through the response process.

---

### Missing Service 4: Documentation Window Evidence Architecture (Cross-Vertical Universal)

**The confirmed gap:** No Geneva service in any vertical offers documentation window evidence architecture — the proportionate evidence building that creates positioning advantage during the documentation window close period.

**What it would do:**
- Build evidence architecture during the documentation window that is evaluated against forming standards, not arrived standards
- Create a documentation record that shows the firm's reasoning, decisions, and governance evolution during the window period
- Produce documentation that cannot be retroactively reconstructed after the window closes — the window-period evidence is only accessible during the window
- Position the firm defensibly against standards that are forming, not just standards that have arrived

**Why it doesn't exist:** The documentation window service requires understanding of how Geneva accountability standards form — the informal circuit, the carrier iteration pattern, the institutional client feedback mechanism. Providers who don't participate in the circuit can't build window-period evidence architecture because they don't know what standards are forming.

**Why it's more acute in C291:** The documentation window is a present-tense positioning property in C291, not a future concern. The window's structural property — permanent close with burden-of-proof reversal — is operative now. Firms that build documentation during the window achieve positioning advantage that firms without documentation cannot replicate after the window closes.

## Layer 3: What Is Making the Problem Worse (Accidental Providers)

### AI Vendor Liability Disclaimers (Cross-Vertical Universal)

**The pattern:** AI vendors operating in Geneva professional services markets include liability disclaimers that transfer all accountability to the professional. The vendor terms are not reviewed against professional obligations. The professional signs click-through agreements without understanding the liability transfer.

**Confirmed in C291:** The vendor terms problem is confirmed as universal across all four verticals. Every AI tool in professional use operates under vendor terms that disclaim vendor liability and commit the professional to personal liability for AI outputs. No Geneva service exists to review vendor terms against professional obligations.

**The structural effect:** Vendor liability disclaimers compound the documentation window problem. The professional who signs vendor click-through agreements is creating personal liability exposure that carrier attestations then require them to govern — without any documentation of what they committed to in the vendor agreements. The vendor terms are the hidden layer of the accountability gap.

---

### Carrier Form Evolution (Accelerating Problem)

**The pattern:** Carrier third-generation attestation forms are iterating toward more specific AI governance requirements. The evolution creates retrospective inconsistency between prior attestations and new requirements. The carrier form is becoming a more specific standard-setting instrument without providing any mechanism for the signatory to understand the standard.

**Confirmed in C291:** Carrier attestation language has become more specific in C291 renewal cycles across all four verticals. The iteration pattern is accelerating because carriers are responding to the same liability environment — AI accountability is creating carrier exposure that carriers are managing through more specific attestation requirements. The more specific the attestation, the more exposed the signatory who signed prior versions without understanding what they committed to.

---

### Practice Management System AI Updates (Passive Adoption Driver)

**The pattern:** Practice management system updates are activating AI features by default — without the professional making a governance decision, without patient consent for AI processing, and without documentation of the activation. The professional's practice management system becomes an AI deployment vehicle without the professional's knowledge or consent.

**Confirmed in C291:** Healthcare physicians report AI features activated in practice management systems during updates. Attorneys report AI features in case management systems that arrived during software updates. The passive adoption driver is confirmed in both sectors, with finance and consulting likely affected through portfolio management and project management systems.

The positioning is not about having better content or more methodology — it is about demonstrating sustained Geneva circuit participation through named problems that only circuit participation reveals.
