## Finding 01: Retrospective Awareness Effect — The Primary Market Driver Has Shifted from Anticipatory to Confirmed

**The finding:** In C291, the dominant market driver is no longer "I am worried about signing the next attestation" — it is "I now understand what the last attestation I signed committed me to, and the picture is confirmed to be worse than I thought." The market has moved from anticipatory anxiety to retrospective confirmation, and the retrospective confirmation is more motivating than anticipatory worry ever was.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** The shift required specific C291 regulatory developments that did not exist in prior cycles — FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 2024) publication creating documented inconsistency with prior carrier attestations, carrier audit activity in adjacent cantons confirming attestation enforcement, Swissmedic AI tool registration follow-up communications revealing tool portfolio changes since original attestations, and institutional client questionnaire standards that exceeded published guidance. The retrospective awareness effect is confirmed by actual events, not projected anxiety.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 identified carrier attestation as the primary acute entry point with anticipatory framing ("I worry about what I signed"). C290 Phase 05 identified carrier attestation synchronization as a market-wide event with retrospective framing emerging ("I now understand what the last one committed me to"). C291 confirms the shift is complete: the dominant market driver in all four verticals is retrospective confirmation, not anticipatory concern. The documentation window close is experienced as a present-tense event because the retrospective events have already demonstrated what the window property means.

**The structural implication:** Briefing content that addresses retrospective awareness — "here is what your prior attestation committed you to, here is what has changed, here is what you can do now" — converts at higher rates than content addressing anticipatory concern. The ICP who already knows they have a problem is more ready to engage than the ICP who fears they might have one. The C291 market is a confirmed-problem market, not an at-risk market.

## Finding 02: Client Questionnaire Displacement — Clients Are Setting Standards Before Carriers and Regulators

**The finding:** Across all four verticals in C291, client AI governance questionnaires are the primary external pressure trigger — operating ahead of carrier attestation requirements and ahead of published regulatory guidance. Clients are applying standards through due diligence processes that carriers and regulators have not yet codified. The client questionnaire is the de facto standard-setter through the guidance vacuum, and no Geneva service exists to help boutique firms respond accurately and build documentation through the response process.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** C291 is the first cycle in which client questionnaires appear as the primary pressure trigger across all four verticals simultaneously — not just in finance (where institutional client questionnaires were already present) but in legal (corporate clients sending AI governance questionnaires to outside counsel), consulting (strategy clients requesting AI methodology documentation as proposal evaluation), and healthcare (private patients beginning to ask about AI use in treatment decisions). The vertical spread of client questionnaire pressure is new in C291.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 identified carrier attestation as the primary acute pressure point with client questionnaires mentioned as secondary. C290 Phase 05 identified client questionnaire pressure in finance as the institutional client feedback mechanism. C291 confirms that client questionnaires have displaced carrier attestation as the primary external pressure trigger across all four verticals — and that the displacement is structural, not cyclical. The client questionnaire is the mechanism through which accountability standards form in the absence of published doctrine.

**The structural implication:** The documentation that clients are asking for is the documentation that carriers and regulators will eventually require — clients are ahead of official process in setting standards. The cross-vertical operator who builds client-response documentation architecture (responding to client questionnaires in ways that simultaneously build governance records) captures the positioning advantage of serving standards before they become regulatory requirements.

## Finding 03: Documentation Window Present-Tense Positioning — The Window Is Now a Competitive Asset, Not Just a Compliance Buffer

**The finding:** The documentation window is not closing in the future — it is operating as a present-tense competitive positioning property in C291. Firms that built documentation during the window are experiencing direct business value: client retention through AI governance questionnaire responses, competitive differentiation in proposal processes, lower administrative burden when carrier and regulatory communications arrive. The documentation window is a business development asset, not just a compliance record.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** C291 is the first cycle in which the documentation window's competitive effect is observable — firms with documentation are experiencing measurable business value from it, and firms without documentation are experiencing the absence as a present-tense competitive liability. The timing precision of the window is no longer a theoretical property — it is an operative market condition with documented consequences for firms in different documentation states.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 named the documentation window as a positioning occasion with urgency framing. C290 Phase 05 named the documentation window close as a single-event positioning property with burden-of-proof reversal. C291 confirms the window is now — the competitive effect is present-tense, not future. The finding that documentation built during the window functions as a business development asset (not just a compliance buffer) is new in C291 and has direct implications for how the briefing service is positioned and priced.

**The structural implication:** The documentation window is not primarily a compliance product — it is a competitive positioning product. The briefing that builds documentation window evidence architecture should be positioned as a business development investment with compliance co-benefits, not as a compliance cost. The positioning affects pricing psychology and ICP qualification — "I'm investing in my documentation window position" vs "I'm paying for compliance."

## Finding 04: Tool Inventory Reveal as Positioning Intervention — The Diagnostic Creates the Urgency

**The finding:** The AI tool inventory is not just a diagnostic — it is a positioning intervention. When an ICP conducts a tool inventory and discovers the actual list of AI tools operating under their name (typically 5–15 tools with no authorization documentation), the accountability frame shifts from "I should probably do something" to "I need to build a governance record now." The inventory reveal generates briefing readiness at the same moment it generates the diagnostic data needed to build the governance record.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** C291 tool inventory data is now complete enough to confirm the inventory effect as a structural pattern — not a situational finding. Across all four verticals, the inventory reveal is consistent: tools in use without governance authorization, tools added by staff or embedded features without principal review, and a gap between what the principal believes is in use and what is actually operating. The inventory confirmation is empirical, not impressionistic.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 identified the AI shadow surface as an emerging pattern. C290 Phase 05 identified the inventory as a diagnostic mechanism. C291 confirms that the inventory is simultaneously a diagnostic and a positioning intervention — the moment of inventory revelation is the moment briefing readiness is generated, not a separate discovery event followed by a separate decision to engage. The inventory and the conversion trigger are the same event.

**The structural implication:** The cross-vertical operator who offers tool inventory as a free diagnostic — with the inventory results serving as the conversion mechanism for the paid briefing — captures the highest-conversion entry point in the market. The inventory is not a loss-leader; it is the primary conversion mechanism. The ICP who sees their own inventory cannot unsee it, and the decision to build authorization architecture follows from the inventory results with high predictable conversion.

## Finding 05: Geneva Circuit Participation as Structural Moat — Content Cannot Replicate It

**The finding:** The Geneva informal circuit — the non-publishable intelligence network where accountability expectations actually form — operates as a single cross-vertical mechanism, not four parallel vertical channels. Pattern knowledge from sustained circuit participation is structurally non-replicable through secondary research, methodology documents, or expertise claims. The competitive moat is participation, not content.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** C291 circuit observation data is now twelve months deep across all four verticals, confirming the circuit's single-mechanism cross-vertical operating property with documented examples: carrier iteration pattern observed across all four verticals produces the same intelligence; institutional event intelligence in Geneva produces the same accountability formation signals across all four verticals; peer circuit exchange operates with the same velocity and the same silence-as-signal property in all four verticals. The circuit is not four channels — it is one mechanism with four sector expressions.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 named the Geneva informal circuit as an accountability formation mechanism. C290 Phase 05 named the circuit's single-mechanism cross-vertical operating property as a structural moat. C291 confirms the moat is structural — it cannot be crossed through content investment, methodology development, or expertise claims. Only sustained participation in all four verticals produces the circuit intelligence that generates named problems only circuit participation reveals.

**The structural implication:** The cross-vertical positioning signal is not "I have better content" — it is "I know things that only sustained Geneva participation reveals." The ICP evaluates this not by comparing content but by observing whether the analysis could have been produced without sustained ecosystem participation. Problems that only circuit participation reveals are the trust signal that content quality alone cannot produce.

## Finding 06: The Single Cross-Vertical Market Structure — Four Sector Contexts, One Service Architecture

**The finding:** The market for Geneva professional AI accountability services is structurally one market with four sector contexts — not four parallel markets. The service architecture is identical across all four verticals: pre-signing carrier attestation review, AI tool inventory and authorization architecture, client questionnaire response support, documentation window evidence architecture. The four service categories are absent uniformly across all four sectors. The pricing signal (CHF 2,200) performs the same qualification function in all four verticals. The positioning message ("you signed something without understanding what you committed to") works in all four sectors without modification.

**What emerged from C291 cross-vertical synthesis that prior cycles could not produce:** C291 is the first cycle in which the cross-vertical market structure finding is confirmed by twelve months of evidence from all four verticals simultaneously — with the same service gaps present in identical form, the same pricing signal working for the same ICP profile, and the same positioning language converting across all four sectors. The confirmation is not anecdotal — it is empirical from C291 cycle data across all four verticals.

**Why this is NET-NEW relative to C289 and C290:** C289 Phase 05 identified the cross-vertical market structure as an emerging finding. C290 Phase 05 named the single-market structure as the operating conclusion. C291 confirms it as a stable market property with the service architecture now defined with sufficient precision to be deliverable: four service categories, absent uniformly, addressable with one service framework with sector-specific calibration in delivery.

**The structural implication:** The highest-value cross-vertical positioning is not "AI accountability for Geneva law firms" or "AI accountability for Geneva wealth managers" — it is "AI accountability for Geneva professionals" with sector-specific calibration in the delivery. The single-market structure means one service framework, four sector contexts, one price point, and one positioning message that works across all four verticals simultaneously.
