## Competitive Landscape Map

The competitive landscape can be mapped on two axes: specificity (generic vs. specific) and evidence (framework vs. evidence). The market's current offerings cluster in two quadrants. The empty quadrant is the positioning target.

| | **Framework Language** | **Evidence Language** |
|---|---|---|
| **Generic / Organization-Level** | Q1: International platforms, Big Four | Q2: EMPTY |
| **Specific / Individual-Level** | Q3: Boutique firms, legal consultants | **Q4: EMPTY — OWNED** |

**Q4 (Specific + Evidence):** This is the unoccupied quadrant. No competitor operates here. Q4 is: specific to the ICP's carrier form, specific to the ICP's documentation situation, specific to the ICP's personal liability exposure, and delivered as evidence (not frameworks, policies, or training). DigitalCoa owns Q4. This is category creation, not differentiation.

## Competitive Advantage Structure

### Moat 1: First-Mover Relationship Depth
Broker relationships and ICP relationships established early in the market formation create relationship depth competitors cannot replicate quickly.

### Moat 2: Retroactive Reconstruction Methodology
The methodology for reconstructing 12-24 months of undocumented AI use is expertise, not a template. It requires deep knowledge of carrier form question types, evidence standards, and documentation science. This cannot be replicated in 6-12 months.

### Moat 3: Carrier Form Reading as Market Intelligence
Reading carrier forms as the design specification for evidence architecture is a methodology no competitor has built. It requires ongoing carrier form collection and analysis. This is a continuous intelligence function competitors cannot replicate without the same ongoing process.

### Moat 4: EN+FR Documentation Capability
English carrier forms + French regulatory proceedings documentation requires bilingual capability and bilingual legal knowledge. This is a regulatory design requirement, not a translation task. Competitors offering English-only or German-only content cannot serve Geneva's documentation environment.

### Moat 5: Personal Liability Framing
The "For physicians, evidence is personal. And your license is on the line." positioning applies across all four verticals and is独占. Competitors use organizational compliance language. Sergio uses personal liability language. This framing creates engagement that organizational language cannot.

## Summary: What Phase 06 Confirms About Competitive Landscape

1. **Q4 (specific + evidence) is unoccupied and owned by DigitalCoa** — category creation, not differentiation.

2. **The closest competitors have language proximity but wrong product architecture** — they offer frameworks, policies, training, and compliance documentation. None offer evidence architecture.

3. **Mid-tier accounting firms are the most significant near-term displacement risk** — existing SMB relationships, one hire, 6-12 month product development. First-mover advantage is time-limited.

4. **Broker referral channel is primary and empty** — no competitor has built the referral program. This is the most defensible competitive moat.

5. **Retroactive reconstruction is unique capability with no competitive equivalent** — the primary market problem (12-24 months of undocumented AI use) is unaddressed by any competitor.

6. **The competitive gap is widening** — carrier forms escalating faster than competitor offerings. The moat deepens over time.
