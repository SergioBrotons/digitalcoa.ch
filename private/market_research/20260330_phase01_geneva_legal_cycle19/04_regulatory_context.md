## Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal AI Governance

### Active Now: nFADP Art. 5(c)
**Status:** In force since September 1, 2023
**What it means for law firms:** Any AI tool processing client data must provide transparency on AI use (disclosure to client). Data Protection Impact Assessments required for high-risk AI applications. FADP applies directly to AI-based data processing (FDPIC confirmed, May 8, 2025).
**Penalty architecture:** No direct FDPIC fines under nFADP. Criminal route only: Art. 52 nFADP (wilful disclosure of secret personal data: up to CHF 250,000; negligent: up to CHF 50,000). FDPIC can issue binding decisions, order deletion, suspend processing.
**Compliance gap:** Most Geneva small law firms are NOT compliant with Art. 5(c) AI transparency obligations as of March 2026.

### Swissdamed (July 1, 2026) — ~13 WEEKS
**Status:** No grace period. Swissmedic confirmed hard deadline.
**Legal sector spillover:** Attorneys with healthcare clients will field Swissdamed-related questions. Healthcare AI compliance is a subset of broader AI governance obligations.
**For law firms:** Swissdamed creates urgency cascade — healthcare clients ask attorneys; attorneys need to understand AI governance to advise properly.

### EU AI Act — High-Risk Obligations (August 2, 2026) — ~4.5 MONTHS
**Status:** In force; GPAI transparency obligations active since August 2, 2025; high-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026
**Extraterritorial reach:** Swiss law firms subject if:
- Placing AI systems on the EU market
- Operating AI systems whose output is intended for EU use
- Acting as EU importer/distributor of AI systems
- Providing AI-enabled services to EU clients where output affects EU data subjects
**Compliance gap:** Law firms with EU-facing practice (M&A, contract law, regulatory advisory) likely within scope. Most have not assessed.
**Key obligation:** High-risk AI systems require conformity assessment, technical documentation, human oversight, transparency to users.

### Federal Council AI Regulatory Approach (February 12, 2025) — CONFIRMED
**Status:** Formal strategy announced
**Approach:** "Lean approach" — ratify CoE AI Convention; implement targeted sector-specific adjustments; NO comprehensive horizontal AI law before end-2026
**Formal legislation:** FDJP consultation draft for CoE AI Convention integration expected END-2026
**Implementation plan:** Non-legally binding measures expected END-2026
**Two distinct timelines:** The digitalswitzerland "AI Action Plan" First Draft May 2026 is a separate private-sector process, not a Federal Council milestone.

### FDPIC Enforcement Posture (2024/2025 Annual Report)
**Published:** July 1, 2025
**Key stats:** 1,053 complaints reviewed; 274 consultations; 363 breach notifications
**Enforcement actions 2025-2026:** Inkasso-Team AG (Apr 2025), Cembra Money Bank AG (Jan 2025), Digitec Galaxus (Nov 2025 + Feb 2026), Coop (Oct 2025), X/Twitter (Mar 2025), BLT Baselland Transport (Feb 2026)
**Legal sector:** ABSENT from complaints AND enforcement — law firms not in FDPIC priority focus as of March 2026
**Penalty authority confirmed:** Up to CHF 250,000 for wilful disclosure of secret personal data

### ANBLES / Bar Association Silence
**Status:** No AI guidance published as of March 2026 (~20 months since nFADP)
**Implication:** Small law firms have no professional body signal on AI obligations. ANBLES silence = white space for advisors who move first.

### FDPIC Wearables Guidance (March 2026) — NEW
**Published:** March 2026
**Content:** Privacy risks of wearable devices; continuous data collection; health data sensitivity; legal implications
**Signal:** FDPIC actively mapping AI-adjacent technologies. Expanding scope of AI regulation monitoring.
**Legal sector implication:** Attorney use of AI document review, AI legal research, AI client communication tools could face similar FDPIC scrutiny framework.
