# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal: Ideal Client Profile
## Cycle 19 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Small Law Firms (2–5 attorneys, Geneva)

---

## ICP: The Geneva Small Law Firm

### Demographics
- **Firm size:** 2–5 attorneys + support staff
- **Location:** Geneva canton (primary); expanding to Vaud, Bern, Zurich as secondary
- **Practice areas:** SMB-facing: SME corporate, real estate transactions, family law, employment, privacy/data protection, contract drafting, inheritance/estate planning
- **Revenue context:** CHF 400K–2M annual; price-sensitive; solo partners increasingly squeezed by compliance burden
- **Technology posture:** Active AI adoption underway — Microsoft 365 Copilot, ChatGPT, Clio/cloud-based practice management, Westlaw Edge/Neos (AI-assisted research), AI document review tools
- **Decision-maker:** Managing partner or sole practitioner (owns compliance risk personally)

### Pain Points (Validated by Research)
1. **AI governance confusion:** No idea what obligations apply; assume AI tools = compliance done
2. **Client data exposure:** Using AI on client matters = potential FADP Art. 5(c) violation without disclosure
3. **Timeline disorientation:** EU AI Act feels distant; FDPIC feels abstract; nFADP feels optional
4. **Competitive anxiety:** Larger firms building AI compliance frameworks; small firms falling behind
5. **No dedicated counsel:** ANBLES silent; bar associations quiet; no Geneva-specific AI guidance for small firms
6. **Attorney-client privilege tension:** AI document review potentially breaches confidentiality if data leaves the firm

### Trigger Events
- **FDPIC enforcement on law firms** — not yet (gap confirmed Cycle 19); law firms absent from 1,053 complaints AND enforcement list
- **ANBLES finally publishes AI guidance** — no movement since February 2026
- **Bar association bulletin on AI** — nothing issued yet
- **Competitor firm publishes AI compliance checklist** — no small-firm-focused competitor identified (10th+ confirmation)
- **Geneva legal community AI event** — none observed yet
- **CoE AI Convention ratification** — expected end-2026

### Timing Assessment (as of March 30, 2026)
- **Swissdamed Jul 1, 2026:** ~13 WEEKS — spillover to legal via medical records and healthcare AI use cases
- **Federal Council First Draft May 2026:** ~2 MONTHS — digitalswitzerland multi-stakeholder process (NOT Federal Council formal)
- **Federal Council formal legislation draft:** END-2026 — FDJP consultation draft for CoE AI Convention integration
- **EU AI Act high-risk obligations:** Aug 2, 2026 — ~4.5 MONTHS
- **Advisory window:** ~12–18 months before competitive differentiation closes

### FDPIC Enforcement Gap (Updated Cycle 19)
**Confirmed absence — positive signal for timing:**
- FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (published Jul 1, 2025): 1,053 complaints, 274 consultations, 363 breach notifications
- **Enforcement sectors in 2025-2026:** Debt collection (Inkasso-Team AG), banking (Cembra Money Bank AG), retail/tech (Digitec Galaxus Feb 2026), supermarket (Coop), transport (BLT Baselland Transport AG Feb 2026), social media (X/Twitter Mar 2025)
- **Focus areas named in annual report:** Health, employment, transport, international cooperation
- **Legal sector:** NOT present in complaints, consultations, or enforcement actions — law firms ABSENT from FDPIC enforcement radar
- **Implication:** Law firms have a confirmed timing window before FDPIC enforcement focus shifts to professional services

### Competitive Landscape
**No boutique competitor for small law firm AI governance (10th+ confirmation):**
- Large firm AI practices (Homburger, Lenz & Staehelin, White & Case, CMS, Schellenberg Wittmer): enterprise-only, CHF 15K–50K+ mandates
- VISCHER: data protection expertise; FADP proceedings before FDPIC; NOT small-firm-focused
- FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL: boutique specializing in privacy, cybersecurity, AI governance for small/mid-market companies — CLOSEST to target segment; unique position
- MLL Legal, Walder Wyss, LEXR: recognized for data protection; not boutique for small firms
- ANBLES: silent as of March 2026 (20 months since FADP)
- Geneva Bar / SBA: no AI guidance published as of March 2026

### Addressable Market
- **Geneva: ~350–450 small law firms** (2–5 attorneys; source: Geneva Bar + estimates)
- **Geneva + Vaud: ~550–700 small law firms**
- **Switzerland-wide: ~1,500–2,200 small law firms** in cantons with active AI adoption

### Objections & Responses
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "FDPIC won't come after a small firm" | True — and that's the window. The 1,053 complaints and zero legal sector enforcement = the exact moment to act before FDPIC pattern recognition expands to professional services |
| "We don't use AI that much" | Check Microsoft 365 Copilot, ChatGPT, Clio AI, Westlaw Edge — if any are active, Art. 5(c) nFADP applies NOW |
| "The EU AI Act doesn't apply to us" | If you advise EU clients, place AI in EU market, or your output is intended for EU consumption, it applies |
| "Our bar association will tell us what to do" | ANBLES has been silent 20 months since nFADP; Geneva Bar has issued nothing on AI as of March 2026 |
| "We already have data protection policies" | nFADP Art. 5(c) on AI transparency is specific and new; most existing DPAs don't cover it |
