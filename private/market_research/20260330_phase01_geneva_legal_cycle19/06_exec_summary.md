## Key Findings

### 1. FDPIC Enforcement Gap Confirmed With New Evidence
FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (Jul 1, 2025): 1,053 complaints reviewed, 363 breach notifications, 6 enforcement actions in 2025-2026 across transport, banking, retail, social media. **Legal sector absent from both complaints and enforcement list.** Focus areas named: health, employment, transport, international cooperation. Law firms have not been in the enforcement pattern — positive timing signal, not permanent exemption.

### 2. Two Timelines Clarified
Federal Council formal regulatory approach (Feb 12, 2025): lean approach, ratify CoE AI Convention, formal legislation draft end-2026. **Separate from** digitalswitzerland AI Action Plan (Zero Draft Jan 2026, First Draft May 2026) — private-sector process, not Federal Council milestone. Market confusion about "May 2026" creates advisory differentiation opportunity.

### 3. FDPIC Wearables Guidance (March 2026) — New Regulatory Signal
FDPIC published guidance on wearable devices (March 2026): continuous health data collection, privacy risks, legal implications. Signals FDPIC is actively expanding AI-adjacent regulatory monitoring beyond traditional data protection.

### 4. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL — Only Boutique in Adjacent Space
FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL GmbH identified as only boutique explicitly positioning for small/mid-market companies in privacy and AI governance (30+ years, Daniela Fabian). All other named data protection firms (VISCHER, LEXR, Homburger, Lenz & Staehelin) are enterprise-positioned. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL = closest structural competitor; still focused on privacy broadly, not legal-specific AI governance.

### 5. Competitive White Space Intact (10th+ Confirmation)
No boutique competitor targeting small law firm AI governance in Geneva. ANBLES silent ~20 months. Geneva Bar silent. All large firm AI practices enterprise-only. Window intact.

### 6. Swissdamed Spillover (~13 WEEKS)
Swissdamed Jul 1 2026 ~13 WEEKS — no grace period confirmed by Swissmedic. Healthcare clients cascade AI governance questions to their attorneys. Creates urgency trigger for attorneys who haven't thought about their own AI obligations.
