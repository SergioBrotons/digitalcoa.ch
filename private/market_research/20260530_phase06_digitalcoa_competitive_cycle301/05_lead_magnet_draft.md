---

Not as a theoretical regulatory document. As a specific request for specific records.

"Your last three AI authorization records for this client matter."

The carrier attestation has evolved. It no longer asks for your AI policy. It asks for the record of who decided to use a specific AI tool, in which client context, with what oversight. The evidence standard has replaced the policy standard.

And the documentation infrastructure to answer that question — in most practices — does not exist.

---

The contract terms you agreed to without reading have become the structure that limits what you can do now. "Who made this AI decision? In which client matter? With what authority?"

This is the question carrier attestation now asks. Not "do you have an AI policy?" but "can you show us what actually happened?"

The documentation you have — policies, procedures, training records — is not what the form is asking for. The form wants records. Specific records. Authorization records.

And you don't have them.

### Quand le formulaire carrier arrive et que vous ne pouvez pas produire les dossiers
