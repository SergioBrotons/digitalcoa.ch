### FINMA GUIDANCE 08/2024: STRUCTURAL DRIVER

**Status:** Active since December 2024

**What it requires:**
- Centralized inventory of all AI applications with risk classifications
- Clear accountability and responsibility definitions for AI development, implementation, monitoring, use
- Model testing, control documentation, comprehensive staff training
- Data quality assurance (completeness, correctness, integrity)
- Robust testing and ongoing monitoring of AI systems
- Transparency, explainability, bias mitigation
- Independent verification of AI systems
- Third-party AI due diligence — responsibility cannot be delegated to AI or third parties
- Staff AI literacy training

**Key structural requirement:** Institutions must ensure third-party providers possess necessary skills and experience. Contractual agreements must clearly define responsibilities and liability.

**What this means for DigitalCoa ICPs:**

The FINMA guidance creates upstream pressure. Finance ICPs are receiving questionnaires from their clients asking about AI use in client matters. Consulting ICPs (as sub-consultants) are being asked to demonstrate AI governance they don't control. The attestation chain flows through vendor relationships.

This is not a one-time compliance exercise. It's an ongoing structural requirement that creates sustained market demand for evidence infrastructure.

### EU AI ACT: AUGUST 2, 2026 HIGH-RISK PROVISIONS

**Status:** 2 months from activation

**Extraterritorial reach:**
The EU AI Act applies to Swiss companies if they:
- Develop or provide AI systems used within the EU
- Utilize AI systems that produce outputs in the EU
- Export AI systems into the EU market

**High-risk AI systems include:**
- Critical infrastructure (energy, transport)
- Education and vocational training
- Employment and workforce management
- Access to essential services (banking, insurance)
- Law enforcement
- Migration and border management
- Justice and democratic processes

**Conformity assessment requirements:**
High-risk AI systems require conformity assessments before deployment. Ongoing monitoring, technical documentation, audit trails required.

**What this means for DigitalCoa ICPs:**

Geneva professional practices with EU-facing work (finance, consulting, legal) will face EU AI Act requirements. The August 2 activation date creates a convergence window: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (already active) + EU AI Act high-risk provisions (August 2) creates a dual pressure point for Swiss financial services and consulting.

The market needs a Geneva-calibrated advisory service that addresses both Swiss regulatory requirements AND EU AI Act compliance — without requiring enterprise-scale engagements.

### SBA DAY OF LAWYERS (JUNE 19): ACUTE TRIGGER

**Status:** ~3 weeks from May 30, 2026

**Context:**
The Swiss Bar Association adopted AI guidelines in June 2024. The SBA Day of Lawyers on June 19 represents an annual forcing function where legal professionals must engage with their AI documentation state.

**What happens:**
- Geneva lawyers confront their AI attestation readiness
- Carrier attestation cycle pressure is highest in this period
- Personal liability exposure becomes salient
- Documentation gap becomes undeniable

**What this means for DigitalCoa:**

The June 19 SBA Day creates a conversion window. Legal ICPs who have not prepared attestation-ready evidence packages face the carrier cycle with a visible gap. The peer enforcement effect (seeing colleagues face attestation pressure) converts abstract risk awareness to specific engagement intent.

This is a market trigger — not an abstract regulatory future, but an imminent professional event.

### SWISSDAMED JULY 1, 2026: LIMITED SCOPE

**Status:** 1 month from activation (July 1, 2026)

**Scope:** Medical DEVICES only

**What it covers:** AI-based medical devices in Switzerland

**What it does NOT cover:** General AI attestation for law, finance, consulting practices

**What this means for DigitalCoa:**

Swissdamed is not the regulatory trigger the market initially feared. The limited scope (medical devices only) means legal, finance, and consulting ICPs are not facing Swissdamed attestation — they are facing carrier attestation under FINMA Guidance 08/2024 and potential EU AI Act compliance requirements.

This clarifies the regulatory landscape: the primary forcing function for Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare professionals is carrier attestation, not Swissdamed.

### COUNCIL OF EUROPE AI CONVENTION: MEDIUM-TERM

**Status:** Switzerland signed March 27, 2025

**Implementation timeline:** Draft law expected end of 2026; implementation via sector-specific adjustments post-2026

**What it means:**
Switzerland's AI regulatory approach will adapt existing laws (not create a new AI Act) via sector-specific adjustments. This maintains the sector-specific regulatory model — FINMA for finance, SBA for legal, FMH for healthcare — rather than creating a horizontal AI law.

**What this means for DigitalCoa:**

The medium-term regulatory picture maintains the sector-specific forcing functions that create market demand:
- FINMA guidance for financial services
- SBA professional standards for legal
- Carrier attestation cycles across all verticals

The convergence window is NOW — not post-2026. The structural moat for DigitalCoa is the current regulatory forcing function gap, not future regulatory change.

## CONVERGENCE WINDOW: MAY–AUGUST 2026

### The Window

The period from May 30 to August 2, 2026 represents a convergence of regulatory pressure points:
1. **SBA Day of Lawyers** (June 19) — acute legal engagement trigger

- EU AI Act compliance preparation for Swiss professional practices

## STRUCTURAL MOAT ANALYSIS

### Why DigitalCoa Is Positioned for This Window

**Geneva calibration:**
The service is built for Geneva professional context — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — not generic AI advisory.

**Evidence focus:**
The service addresses evidence reconstruction (carrier form v4 asking for authorization records) not policy documentation.

**Decision attribution:**
The service captures what actually happened — who made which AI decision, in which client matter, with what authority — not theoretical responsibility assignment.

**Boutique scale:**
The service is calibrated for independent practices and small firms, not enterprise engagements. The minimum engagement is accessible.

**Personal attestation alignment:**
The service speaks to individual professional liability exposure, not institutional compliance departments.

### Why the Window Closes

The convergence window is time-limited:
- June 19 SBA Day creates acute pressure in June
- EU AI Act high-risk provisions activate August 2
- After August 2, the market will begin adapting to the new regulatory reality

The first-mover advantage is in the current period — when the documentation gap is visible, the evidence standard has shifted, and the market has not yet adapted.

After the convergence window, either:
1. The market fills (competitors recognize the gap and offer similar services), or
2. ICPs self-select into compliance patterns (creating new habits that are harder to disrupt)

DigitalCoa's positioning advantage is strongest NOW, during the convergence window, not post-August 2026.
