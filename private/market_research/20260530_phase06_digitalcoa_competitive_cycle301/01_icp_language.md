## How ICPs Describe Their AI Accountability Problems in Their Own Words

### LEGAL VERTICAL (C301 Phase 01 synthesis)

ICPs in Geneva legal practice describe the problem as:
- "I can't reconstruct which client matter I used AI for, and which I didn't."
- "The carrier form asks for records — actual records — not a policy document."
- "If something goes wrong in a client matter, I need to show exactly who decided what."
- "I self-certified in June. I had no idea what I was certifying to."
- "My carrier is asking me to attest personally. That's new. That's not a firm question."
- "I signed something that said I'm responsible. I don't have the infrastructure to back that up."

Key linguistic pattern: **"I can't show what happened"** — the inability to reconstruct decision history is the primary expressed anxiety.

### FINANCE VERTICAL (C301 Phase 02 synthesis)

ICPs in Geneva finance describe the problem as:
- "FINMA is asking about AI governance upstream. The questions go to our consultants and vendors — not just to us."
- "We authorized AI use in portfolios. But there's no record of who authorized what, when."
- "The carrier form has four different tracks. We don't know which one applies."
- "Swissdamed uncertainty — we're paying a tax on not knowing what we're liable for."
- "If a model makes a bad call on a client portfolio, I need to be able to say: this person, this day, this authority."
- "Personal attestation liability — I'm on the hook for something I didn't structure."

Key linguistic pattern: **"Who's accountable when the algorithm decides?"** — attribution anxiety at the institutional level.

### CONSULTING VERTICAL (C301 Phase 03 synthesis)

ICPs in Geneva consulting describe the problem as:
- "Our clients send us FINMA-driven questionnaires. We can't answer them because we don't have the documentation."
- "We're a sub-consultant. The prime is responsible for us, but we have no evidence infrastructure."
- "We delegated AI work to a third party. The carrier form is asking about our vendor contracts."
- "We're doing compliance theater. We're filling out forms. We're not making structural decisions."
- "If a client asks me to prove how I used AI in their project — I don't have that record."

Key linguistic pattern: **"We're being asked for evidence we never created."**

### HEALTHCARE VERTICAL (C301 Phase 04 synthesis)

ICPs in Geneva healthcare describe the problem as:
- "My license is on the line if an AI decision I made or approved causes harm."
- "The carrier attestation asks about AI use in diagnostics. I can't show my authorization records."
- "FMH oversight hasn't defined what AI governance looks like. We're in a void."
- "We use AI for triage. If something goes wrong, I need to show who authorized that system."
- "The documentation requirements keep evolving. Every carrier iteration asks for more."

Key linguistic pattern: **"My license, my attestation, my liability — but no one told me what good looks like."**

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language Patterns (C301 Phase 05 synthesis)

The language across verticals converges on six core expressions:

1. **"I can't show what happened"** — evidence reconstruction failure
2. **"Someone else's problem became mine"** — vendor contract cascade
3. **"I signed something I can't back up"** — personal attestation anxiety
4. **"The documentation I have isn't what they're asking for"** — evidence standard shift
5. **"We're doing compliance, not making decisions"** — compliance fatigue
6. **"No one around here knows what good looks like"** — capability gap

## Competitive Market Language (What the Market Says It Offers vs. What ICPs Need)

### What Big 4 and National Firms Say

- "AI governance framework design"
- "EU AI Act compliance readiness"
- "AI risk assessment and classification"
- "Responsible AI strategy and implementation"
- "AI ethics and governance policy development"
- "AI regulatory compliance advisory"

### What ICPs Actually Need (Inferred from C301 Phases 01–05)

- "Someone who can help me reconstruct what happened in a specific client matter"
- "A way to demonstrate who made which AI decision, when, with what authority"
- "Evidence infrastructure — not another policy document"
- "Someone who understands Geneva professional liability exposure"
- "Attestation readiness for June carrier cycles"
- "A partner who can sit across from me and say: this is what good looks like for your practice"

### The Gap

The market speaks in frameworks. ICPs speak in decisions.

The market offers policies. ICPs need records.

The market offers training. ICPs need structural evidence.

The market offers general AI governance. ICPs need Geneva-calibrated, professional-caliber accountability infrastructure.

## EN Version

### How Geneva ICPs Describe Their AI Accountability Problem

**"I can't show what happened."**

This is the sentence that surfaces across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare practices in Geneva. Not as a policy concern — as a personal liability concern.

The carrier attestation has evolved. It no longer asks for your AI policy. It asks for your authorization records — the actual documentation of who decided to use a specific AI tool, in which client context, with what oversight. The evidence standard has replaced the policy standard.

**"I signed something I can't back up."**

Personal attestation means the liability is yours. Not the firm's. Yours. And the documentation to support that attestation doesn't exist in most practices. The click-through acceptance of vendor terms over years has created governance architecture constraints that are now surfacing as personal exposure.

**"The questions my clients are asking — I can't answer them."**

FINMA-driven questionnaires are flowing upstream to consultants and sub-consultants. The attestation chain runs through vendor relationships. If a client asks how you used AI in their project, and you can't produce that record, the compliance gap is visible.

**"No one around here knows what good looks like."**

Capability gap is structural, not temporary. 80% of Swiss organizations show shadow AI activity — employees using unapproved AI tools that bypass governance frameworks. The data infrastructure to support compliance documentation doesn't exist in most practices.

**"I'm doing compliance. I'm not making decisions."**

Compliance fatigue is a self-reinforcing loop. Practices perform compliance without making structural decisions. Strategic classification deferral has become risk accumulation. The compliance is real; the governance is not.

## FR Version

### Comment les ICP de Genève décrivent leur problème de responsabilité IA

**« Je ne peux pas montrer ce qui s'est passé. »**

C'est la phrase qui revient dans les cabinets juridiques, les établissements financiers, les sociétés de conseil et les pratiques de santé à Genève. Non pas comme préoccupation politique — comme préoccupation de responsabilité personnelle.

L'attestation du carrier a évolué. Elle ne demande plus votre politique IA. Elle demande vos dossiers d'autorisation — la documentation réelle de qui a décidé d'utiliser un outil IA spécifique, dans quel contexte client, avec quelle supervision. Le standard de preuve a remplacé le standard de politique.

**« J'ai signé quelque chose que je ne peux pas justifier. »**

L'attestation personnelle signifie que la responsabilité vous incombe. Pas au cabinet. À vous. Et la documentation pour soutenir cette attestation n'existe pas dans la plupart des pratiques. L'acceptation des conditions des fournisseurs par clic au fil des années a créé des contraintes d'architecture de gouvernance qui apparaissent maintenant comme une exposition personnelle.

**« Les questions que mes clients me posent — je ne peux pas y répondre. »**

Les questionnaires dictés par la FINMA remontent vers les conseillers et les sous-conseillers. La chaîne d'attestation traverse les relations avec les fournisseurs. Si un client vous demande comment vous avez utilisé l'IA dans son projet, et que vous ne pouvez pas produire ce dossier, l'écart de conformité est visible.

**« Personne autour de moi ne sait à quoi ressemble le standard correct. »**

Le déficit de compétences est structurel, pas temporaire. 80% des organisations suisses présentent une activité IA fantôme — des employés utilisant des outils IA non approuvés qui contournent les cadres de gouvernance. L'infrastructure de données pour soutenir la documentation de conformité n'existe pas dans la plupart des pratiques.

**« Je fais de la conformité. Je ne fais pas de décisions. »**

La fatigue de conformité est une boucle auto-renforçante. Les pratiques accomplissent de la conformité sans prendre de décisions structurelles. Le report de la classification stratégique est devenu une accumulation de risques. La conformité est réelle ; la gouvernance ne l'est pas.
