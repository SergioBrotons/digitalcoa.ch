### LEGAL VERTICAL MARKET SIGNALS

**Acute Signal: June 19 SBA forced self-assessment**

The Swiss Bar Association (SBA) adopted AI guidelines in June 2024. The June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers represents a forcing function where Geneva lawyers must confront their AI documentation state. The gap between self-certified attestation and actual evidence infrastructure is now structurally visible.

**Carrier evolution signal:**
- Carrier attestation forms have reached v4
- V4 explicitly asks for authorization records ("last three authorization records")
- No longer accepts policy documents as substitute
- Personal attestation language has been strengthened — individual lawyer liability exposure

**Documentation debt signal:**
- Years of click-through acceptance of vendor terms have created governance architecture constraints
- Retroactive documentation is impossible — you cannot reconstruct what you did not record
- The documentation gap is now surfacing as personal liability exposure

**Market failure pattern:**
- Big 4 offer AI governance policy frameworks — not evidence reconstruction
- No Geneva-calibrated service exists for lawyers who need to demonstrate decision attribution
- The market offers generic AI compliance training — not professional-specific attestation readiness

### FINANCE VERTICAL MARKET SIGNALS

**FINMA upstream pressure signal:**

FINMA Guidance 08/2024 requires supervised institutions to ensure AI governance extends to their vendors and consultants. The questionnaires are flowing upstream. Finance ICPs are receiving questions about AI use in client matters — questions they cannot answer because they lack evidence infrastructure.

**Carrier four-track inconsistency signal:**

The four-track carrier attestation structure creates confusion about which track applies to which AI use case. Finance professionals are paying a "Swissdamed uncertainty tax" — the cost of not knowing what they're liable for.

**Portfolio AI authorization gap signal:**

Institutional AI authorization in portfolio management lacks the granular decision attribution that carrier forms are now requesting. The authorization to use AI in portfolios does not equal authorization records for specific AI decisions in specific client matters.

**Personal attestation liability structure signal:**

Finance professionals are discovering that carrier attestation language has shifted from institutional to personal. The professional is now individually liable for AI decisions they authorized but did not structure.

**Market failure pattern:**
- Institutional AI governance frameworks are designed for compliance departments, not for portfolio managers who need decision attribution records
- Big 4 focus on enterprise AI risk — not on the granular decision attribution problem
- No Geneva-based boutique service addresses the portfolio-level AI decision attribution gap

### CONSULTING VERTICAL MARKET SIGNALS

**Sub-consultant delegation gap signal:**

Consulting practices acting as sub-consultants face a structural exposure: the prime consultant is responsible for them, but the carrier attestation chain now runs through vendor relationships. Sub-consultants are being asked to demonstrate AI governance they do not control.

**Client FINMA-driven questionnaire signal:**

Consulting ICPs are receiving questionnaires from their finance clients that flow from FINMA guidance. The questions ask about AI use in client projects — but consulting practices have not built evidence infrastructure for those client engagements.

**Documentation debt structural signal:**

Consulting practices are experiencing a gap between compliance performance and structural governance. They can fill out questionnaires; they cannot produce records of AI use in specific client projects. The compliance is real; the evidence is not.

**Market failure pattern:**
- Large consulting firms offer AI governance frameworks for enterprise clients — not for boutique consulting practices under FINMA scrutiny
- No service addresses the sub-consultant delegation gap specifically
- Generic AI compliance offerings do not speak to the consulting practice's actual exposure

### HEALTHCARE VERTICAL MARKET SIGNALS

**Personal license liability signal:**

Healthcare professionals in Geneva are discovering that carrier attestation language has shifted to personal liability. Their professional license is now implicated in AI decisions they made or approved. The documentation to support that attestation does not exist.

**FMH oversight void signal:**

The FMH (Swiss Medical Association) has not defined what AI governance looks like for Geneva healthcare practices. Professionals are operating in a regulatory void — they know they need governance infrastructure, but no one has told them what the standard is.

**Carrier attestation primary forcing function signal:**

The carrier attestation cycle is the most immediate pressure for healthcare practices. The attestation asks about AI use in diagnostics, triage, and treatment decisions. Professionals cannot produce the authorization records the form requires.

**Market failure pattern:**
- Healthcare AI governance focus is on hospital systems and enterprise — not on the independent practice
- No service addresses the independent healthcare professional's personal license liability exposure
- Generic AI governance does not speak to clinical decision attribution requirements

### CROSS-VERTICAL MARKET SIGNALS

**Evidence standard replacing policy standard signal:**

The carrier form evolution from policy documentation to authorization records represents a market-wide shift. ICPs across all verticals are discovering that their compliance infrastructure is built on policy documents, not evidence records.

**Decision attribution as primary named exposure signal:**

Across verticals, the question being asked is: "Who made which AI decision, in which client matter, with what authority?" This is the primary named exposure in carrier attestation forms. The market has not provided solutions for this.

**Vendor contract trap signal:**

Years of click-through acceptance of AI vendor terms have created governance architecture constraints. The vendor contract trap means ICPs cannot easily exit relationships or restructure governance because contract terms constrain them.

**Compliance fatigue loop signal:**

Market participants are performing compliance without making structural decisions. The compliance is real; the governance is not. Strategic classification deferral has become risk accumulation — not risk reduction.

**Peer enforcement as conversion trigger signal:**

When market participants observe peers receiving carrier assessments, audit findings, or professional liability claims, the conversion from abstract risk awareness to specific engagement intent happens. Peer enforcement events are the primary conversion trigger the market has identified.

**80% shadow AI signal:**

Swiss organizations show widespread shadow AI activity — employees using unapproved AI tools that bypass governance frameworks. This creates documentation gaps that cannot be remediated retroactively. The evidence infrastructure does not exist to reconstruct what happened.

**80% workforce exhaustion signal:**

Despite AI adoption, 80% of Swiss workforce reports fundamental exhaustion. AI has added to workloads without transforming them. The capacity to build evidence infrastructure does not exist within practices.

**8% data infrastructure signal:**

Only 8% of Swiss firms have consistent, cleansed, integrated data structures. The foundational data infrastructure for AI governance compliance does not exist in most practices.

**Market-wide capacity gap:**

The combination of evidence standard shift, decision attribution requirement, and documentation debt means the market needs a service that:
1. Reconstructs what actually happened
2. Creates decision attribution infrastructure for future use
3. Prepares attestation-ready evidence packages
4. Speaks to Geneva professional liability exposure specifically

## EN Version

### Market Signals: What's Broken and What's Moving

**The documentation gap is now structurally visible.**

Carrier form evolution (v4) has moved from policy documentation to authorization records. The questions are specific: "Show us your last three AI authorization records for this client matter." Professionals across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare cannot produce these records. The gap is not theoretical — it is attestation-ready exposure.

**Decision attribution is the named primary exposure.**

Across all four verticals, the carrier attestation forms now ask: "Who made this AI decision? In which client matter? With what authority?" This is not a policy question. It is a records question. The market has not provided solutions for granular decision attribution at the practice level.

**The vendor contract trap is a governance architecture constraint.**

Years of click-through acceptance have created vendor relationships with terms that constrain current governance options. The contract trap means practices cannot simply "implement AI governance" — they must navigate exit planning and contract restructuring as prerequisites.

**Compliance fatigue is a self-reinforcing loop.**

80% of Swiss workforce reports fundamental exhaustion with AI. Practices are performing compliance without making structural decisions. The compliance is real; the governance is not. Strategic classification deferral has become risk accumulation.

**Peer enforcement is the conversion trigger.**

Abstract risk awareness does not convert to engagement intent. Peer events — a colleague receiving a carrier assessment, a practice facing an audit, a professional liability claim — do convert. The market moves on evidence of peer exposure, not on theoretical risk.

**The Geneva-specific need is not being served.**

The market offers:
- Big 4: enterprise AI governance frameworks for compliance departments
- General AI consultancies: strategy and roadmap development
- Training providers: AI literacy programs

The market does not offer:
- Geneva-calibrated accountability infrastructure for professional practices
- Decision attribution reconstruction for specific client matters
- Personal attestation readiness for carrier cycles
- Evidence packages that speak to Geneva professional liability exposure

The gap is first-mover advantage.

## FR Version

### Signaux du marché : ce qui est cassé et ce qui bouge

**L'écart de documentation est maintenant structurellement visible.**

L'évolution des formulaires carriers (v4) est passée de la documentation des politiques aux dossiers d'autorisation. Les questions sont spécifiques : « Montrez-nous vos trois derniers dossiers d'autorisation IA pour ce dossier client. » Les professionnels du juridique, de la finance, du conseil et de la santé ne peuvent pas produire ces dossiers. L'écart n'est pas théorique — c'est une exposition prête pour l'attestation.

**L'attribution des décisions est l'exposition primaire nommée.**

Dans les quatre secteurs verticaux, les formulaires d'attestation des carriers demandent maintenant : « Qui a pris cette décision IA ? Dans quel dossier client ? Avec quelle autorité ? » Ce n'est pas une question de politique. C'est une question de dossiers. Le marché n'a pas fourni de solutions pour l'attribution granulaire des décisions au niveau du cabinet.

**Le piège du contrat fournisseur est une contrainte d'architecture de gouvernance.**

Des années d'acceptation par clic ont créé des relations avec des fournisseurs dont les termes contraignent les options de gouvernance actuelles. Le piège du contrat signifie que les cabinets ne peuvent pas simplement « mettre en place une gouvernance IA » — ils doivent naviguer dans la planification de sortie et la restructuration des contrats comme conditions préalables.

**La fatigue de conformité est une boucle auto-renforçante.**

80% de la main-d'œuvre suisse signale une épuisement fondamental face à l'IA. Les cabinets accomplissent de la conformité sans prendre de décisions structurelles. La conformité est réelle ; la gouvernance ne l'est pas. Le report de la classification stratégique est devenu une accumulation de risques.

**L'application par les pairs est le déclencheur de conversion.**

La conscience abstraite du risque ne se convertit pas en intention d'engagement. Les événements entre pairs — un collègue recevant une évaluation carrier, un cabinet faisant face à un audit, une réclamation de responsabilité professionnelle — convertissent. Le marché avance sur la preuve de l'exposition des pairs, pas sur le risque théorique.

**Le besoin spécifique à Genève n'est pas servi.**

Le marché offre :
- Big 4 : cadres de gouvernance IA d'entreprise pour les départements de conformité
- Cabinets de conseil IA généraux : développement de stratégie et feuille de route
- Formateurs : programmes de culture IA

Le marché n'offre pas :
- Infrastructure de responsabilité calibrée pour Genève pour les pratiques professionnelles
- Reconstruction de l'attribution des décisions pour des dossiers clients spécifiques
- Préparation à l'attestation personnelle pour les cycles carriers
- Paquets de preuves qui parlent à l'exposition de responsabilité professionnelle genevoise

L'écart est un avantage de premier entrant.
