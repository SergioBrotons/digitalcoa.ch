## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Geneva Executive Client Journey — Relationship-Driven Entry
- Primary trigger: peer referral (Geneva's small professional community; trust travels fast)
- Regulatory event (FINMA, new regulation) creates urgency → search for specialist advisor
- Research phase: LinkedIn, Google (specific query), peer referral confirmation, conference encounters
- Trust formation: face-to-face/video > async; Geneva-specific expertise, regulatory knowledge, peer endorsement, published thought leadership (Brotons.net)
- Decision phase: quick once trust established; formal proposal > demo; preference for retainer for ongoing regulatory advisory
- Key insight: client journey is not primarily digital (website → contact). It's relationship-driven: Brotons.net credibility → peer referral → trusted introduction → engagement.

### Finding 02: Geneva Advisory Pricing — Value-Based and Retainer Structures
- Day rate benchmarks: CHF 3,500-6,000 (senior partner), CHF 2,500-3,500 (senior consultant), CHF 2,800-4,500 (boutique)
- Geneva law firms: CHF 600-1,200/hour (partners) for legal advisory
- Geneva strategy boutiques: CHF 15,000-40,000/month retainer for senior partner access
- Value-based pricing accepted for high-stakes regulatory/strategic issues
- Retainer preferred for ongoing regulatory advisory (CHF 8,000-25,000/month for senior partner)
- Project-based: fixed scope and fee for discrete engagements
- Entry-level: regulatory health check at fixed project fee (trust-building, lower commitment)

### Finding 03: Advisory Client Onboarding — Executive Comfort Protocol
- First meeting: genuine discovery, not sales pitch; Geneva executives want to be heard before advised
- Pre-meeting: homework on organization, LinkedIn, regulatory context — Geneva executives expect this
- NDA offered proactively: signals professionalism and establishes confidentiality
- Engagement letter: clear scope, bilateral confidentiality, communication protocols, fee transparency, termination provisions
- Initial discovery: framework-driven but not interrogative; understand real problem behind stated problem
- Deliverables: concise 1-2 page engagement roadmap, not 50-page proposal

### Finding 04: DigitalCoa.ch vs. Brotons.net — Clear Role Separation
- Brotons.net = thought leadership (credibility, interpretation, audience relationship, trust building)
- DigitalCoa.ch = advisory services (client acquisition, conversion, engagement framework)
- Critical boundary: Brotons.net must never feel like a sales funnel; DigitalCoa.ch must never try to be Brotons.net
- Crossover: topic-relevant service mentions in Brotons.net conclusions (per C98) appropriate when genuinely useful
- How they support each other: Brotons.net → credibility → executives seek DigitalCoa.ch when ready to act → experience informs Brotons.net content (anonymised)

### Finding 05: Remote Advisory Delivery — Geneva Executive Expectations
- High-quality video non-negotiable for relationship-building; camera on mandatory
- Hybrid model: initial discovery and milestones in-person; ongoing advisory via video + async
- Geneva executives comfortable with remote relationship maintenance once trust established
- Async communication: fewer but more substantive updates; 24h email response; WhatsApp/signal for urgent
- Written meeting summaries expected (Geneva executives want records)
- Secure document sharing required (Geneva financial clients expect security)
- Time zone: Geneva executives adjust schedules for US/Asia clients; advisory should do the same

### Finding 06: Advisory Client Retention and Expansion
- Ongoing value = anticipation (telling clients what they don't know they need to know), not just reaction
- Proactive regulatory updates: Geneva executives want to know about relevant changes before urgent
- Quarterly regulatory review (30-min video): keeps relationship active, surfaces new issues
- Geneva executive loyalty: rarely switch advisors once trust established; trust and consistency > competitive pricing
- Multi-year relationships common for regulatory advisory
- Expansion pathways: regulatory → board advisory, single family office → multiple entities, due diligence → transaction advisory
- Referral generation: Geneva executives refer when advisor exceeded expectations AND introduction is low-risk; via trusted intermediaries (bankers, lawyers) not direct
- Key retention risk: complacency. Regular evidence of continued relevance essential.
