## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 3)

### EN
**Advisory Client Onboarding — Executive Comfort Protocol**

Geneva executive expectations for advisory engagement onboarding:

First meeting:
- No sales pitch — Geneva executives反感推销; first meeting should be genuine discovery
- Focus: understand the executive's specific situation, constraints, decision-making process
- Geneva executives want to be heard before being advised; active listening is key
- Confidentiality establishment: NDA offered proactively (signals professionalism)
- Outcome: not a proposal but a clear point of view on next steps

Pre-meeting preparation:
- Geneva executives expect advisors to do homework on their organization and situation before first meeting
- LinkedIn profile review, company research, understanding the regulatory context
- Geneva-specific preparation: knowledge of their sector, competitors, recent news

Engagement letter/protocol:
- Clear scope definition (what is and is not covered)
- Confidentiality provisions (explicit, bilateral)
- Communication protocols: who is cc'd, response time expectations, escalation path
- Fee structure transparency: no surprises on billing
- Termination provisions: Geneva executives value easy exit if relationship not working

Initial discovery process:
- Structured but not interrogative: Geneva executives respond toFramework-driven questioning
- Key questions: what is the real problem behind the stated problem? Who else is involved in the decision? What have they tried before? What does success look like?
- Geneva-specific: understanding cantonal/institutional dynamics if relevant

Onboarding deliverables:
- Clear engagement roadmap (not a 50-page proposal; a 1-2 page concise plan)
- Timeline and milestones
- Communication cadence agreement
- First-week deliverable (quick win to build momentum)
