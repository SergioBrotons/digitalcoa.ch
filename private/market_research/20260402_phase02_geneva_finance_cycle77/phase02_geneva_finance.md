## NET-NEW Findings (vs C75/C76)

### 1. Stablecoin FinIA Battle — SBA Warns of "Luxembourg Scenario" [MAJOR NET-NEW]
- **Context:** Federal Council proposed FinIA amendment to regulate stablecoins
- **SBA Position (Martin Hess, Natalie — SBVg consultation response):**
  - Welcomes Federal Council's intent to strengthen Switzerland's stablecoin competitiveness
  - **Major flaw in draft:** Would force Swiss banks to establish **subsidiaries as payment instrument institutions** to issue stablecoins — instead of issuing directly under existing banking licence
  - SBA: "Banks are already comprehensively regulated and meet highest supervisory standards" — direct issuance under banking licence should suffice
  - **EU comparison:** In EU, direct issuance under banking licence IS possible — Swiss requirement would create competitive disadvantage
  - **"Luxembourg scenario" warning:** Just as fund business and bond issuance shifted to Luxembourg in 1980s+ due to better frameworks, same could happen with stablecoins/digital currencies
  - **US GENIUS Act:** Federal US stablecoin law enacted — Switzerland risks falling behind if it adds bureaucratic layers
- **Disintermediation risk:** Real concern — payment instrument institutions collecting customer funds could pull deposits from banks → credit financing implications
- **FinSA classification:** SBA rejects classifying payment-purpose stablecoins under FinSA (investment rules) — creates false expectations and unnecessary bureaucracy; whitepaper transparency obligation is sufficient
- **Stress tests call:** SBA demanding robust regulatory impact assessment before opening stablecoin market widely
- **2026 regulatory milestones:** Decision on Capital Adequacy Ordinance + dispatch on capital backing for foreign participations expected; corporate governance and liquidity legislation consultations Q2-Q3 2026
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — "We do not want a Luxembourg scenario for digital assets"
- **Business implication:** Swiss stablecoin regulation at critical juncture; banks vs FinIA tension unresolved; regulatory outcome material for Swiss financial centre competitiveness

### 2. Banking Stability Package — SBA Criticizes Pace and Proportionality [NET-NEW vs C76]
- **Background:** Credit Suisse crisis → Federal Council 30-measure package
- **Current state (as of Mar 2026):** Ordinance-level consultation held 2025; act-level and liquidity measures still pending parliamentary debate
- **SBA view (Markus Staub, overall project manager):**
  - "Right in the middle of it" — 3 years post-Credit Suisse, still unresolved
  - **Criticism 1:** Federal government spreading consultations over too many stages — no comprehensive macroeconomic impact assessment done
  - **Criticism 2:** Proportionality lacking — same rules applied to systemically important banks AND small regional banks
  - **SBA priorities:** Senior managers regime, liquidity provision, resolvability — genuine stability gains
  - **Skeptical of:** Broad-front tightening without cost-benefit analysis
  - **Liquidity regime:** SBA expressly supports optimizing liquidity provision in crisis; wants SNB to define eligible collateral broadly; smaller banks must not be forced into Extended Liquidity Facility; wants public liquidity backstop enshrined in national law
  - **Global comparison:** EU and US moving toward deregulation — international compatibility at risk if Switzerland keeps tightening
- **2026 milestones:** Federal Council decision on Capital Adequacy Ordinance; corporate governance and liquidity consultations Q2-Q3
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — "Regulatory tightening affects us all" (Mar 25, 2026)
- **Business implication:** Swiss banking regulation still in flux; unresolved regulatory risk for Swiss financial centre positioning

- **2026 performance:** Gold opened ~USD 4,330/oz, broke USD 5,000 decisively, peaked at ~USD 5,600/oz on Jan 28, 2026 — all-time record

- **Trade policy impact:** US tariffs on gold floated and then dropped → immediate price movements show political signals as short-term price drivers
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — "What the volatile price of gold tells us" (Mar 25, 2026)
- **Business implication:** Switzerland's gold trading hub status is both opportunity and geopolitical risk exposure; Tether/gold/stablecoin intersection increasingly relevant

### 6. Nature Transition Finance — CHF 5.3B/Year Needed by 2050 [NET-NEW detail]
- **Study:** SBA commissioned BCG + Quantis — first quantitative assessment of nature financing needs in Switzerland
- **Total annual need by 2050:** CHF 5.3 billion
- **Current spending:** CHF 3.2 billion/year (mostly public — water infrastructure, biodiversity-linked agricultural subsidies)
- **Gap:** CHF 2.1 billion/year incremental — 66% increase required
- **Largest categories (>75% of total):** Water infrastructure (wastewater, river revitalization, nature-based solutions, hydropower rehabilitation) + regenerative agriculture (capital investments + income support for transitioning farmers)
- **Financing split:** 85% publicly financed (public assets, limited commercial viability); 15% private mobilizable
- **Swiss banks' role:** Not materially exposed to nature risks due to adaptation infrastructure + insurance; opportunity for green/sustainability-linked loans and bonds; supply chain financing; advisory services for SMEs
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — "Financing options for Switzerland's nature transition" (Jun 2025, still current)
- **Business implication:** Nature transition = significant new green finance market for Swiss banks; CHF 2.1B/year private capital opportunity
