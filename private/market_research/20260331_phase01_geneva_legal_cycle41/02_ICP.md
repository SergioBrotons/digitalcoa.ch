# Phase 01 Geneva Legal — Cycle 41: ICP Definition

**Cycle 41 — March 31, 2026**

---

## Target ICP: Geneva Small Law Firms

### Primary ICP
**Geneva law firms ≤50 lawyers**
- Solo practitioners to mid-size firms (1-50 lawyers)
- AI-using lawyers without formal AI governance policies
- FADP obligations already applicable (Sep 1 2023 operative)
- Swiss or EU nexus (FADP + potential EU AI Act extraterritorial)

### Decision-Makers
- Managing directors / firm partners
- Practice group leaders
- Compliance officers (small firms: often managing partner兼任)

### Characteristics
- Individual AI use high (71% solo / 67% small firm)
- Formal firm policies low (28%)
- No in-house data protection counsel
- Limited resources for complex compliance frameworks
- High urgency — FADP already operative + May 2026 external pressure event

### Psychographic Profile
- Aware AI is happening but overwhelmed by governance requirements
- Believes AI is necessary (59%) but lacks formal adoption framework
- Concerned about FDPIC enforcement risk (criminal liability CHF 250,000)
- Wants competitive advantage from AI without compliance exposure

### Pain Points
- No clear roadmap for AI governance compliance
- Existing AI tools may violate FADP (Art. 21 human review, Art. 5(c) transparency)
- No affordable Swiss-centric AI governance advisory available
- FADP enforcement absent from legal sector = false sense of security

### Buying Triggers
- May 2026 AI Action Plan First Draft (external pressure event)
- FDPIC enforcement signal in adjacent sectors (retail, banking)
- Competitor firms adopting AI formally
- Peer law firm receiving FDPIC inquiry

---

## Secondary ICP
**Swiss legal boutiques (Geneva/Zurich) without AI governance framework**

### Characteristics
- Specialized practices (M&A, IP, data protection)
- May have EU clients = EU AI Act extraterritorial exposure
- Higher AI adoption (legal tech vendors)
- Enterprise competitors not serving this segment

---

## ICP Size Estimate
- Geneva: ~300-400 law firms (solo to 50 lawyers)
- Switzerland: ~1,500-2,000 law firms in target range
- Addressable Geneva market: ~200-300 firms with AI use + no formal policy
- Addressable Swiss market: ~1,000-1,500 firms
