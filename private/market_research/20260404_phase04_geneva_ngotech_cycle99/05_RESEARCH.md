## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 5)

### EN
**Geneva Tech Talent Pipeline — Brain Drain or Brain Circulation?**

Geneva tech talent dynamics:
- EPFL (Lausanne): top European engineering school; Geneva-based tech companies recruiting heavily; computer science/data science graduates increasingly choosing AI and tech over finance
- UNIGE (Geneva): international relations, law, and emerging tech programs; Geneva IO community drawing humanities/social science talent
- Brain circulation vs. brain drain: Geneva tech talent increasingly working remotely for international companies while living in Geneva — contributing to local economy without formal employment
- Tech talent competition: Geneva-based tech companies competing with Zurich (Crypto Valley), Lausanne, and international tech hubs; salary differential significant
- Cost of living: Geneva's high cost of living pushing tech talent toward remote international employment or relocation

Key trends 2024-2025:
- AI talent premium: AI/data science talent commanding 30-50% salary premiums; Geneva companies struggling to compete with US tech firms
- Remote work enabling retention: Geneva-based tech professionals working remotely for international companies while enjoying Geneva quality of life
- Tech-to-finance pipeline: Geneva financial sector competing with tech startups for same talent pool; banks offering stability premium
- Humanitarian tech talent: unique intersection of tech skills and humanitarian mission attracting specific talent profile to Geneva IOs/NGOs
- University-industry links: EPFL industry partnerships creating pathways for Geneva tech companies to access research talent

Advisory: Geneva tech companies needing talent strategy consulting — competitive intelligence on compensation benchmarks, remote work policies, equity compensation structuring

### FR
**Pipeline de talents tech genevois — fuite ou circulation des cerveaux ?**

Dynamique des talents tech genevois :
- EPFL (Lausanne) : école d'ingénierie européenne de premier plan ; entreprises tech genevoises recrutant massivement
- UNIGE (Genève) : relations internationales, droit et programmes technologiques émergents
- Circulation vs. fuite des cerveaux : talents tech genevois travaillant de plus en plus à distance pour des entreprises internationales tout en vivant à Genève
- Compétition pour les talents : entreprises tech genevoises en compétition avec Zurich (Crypto Valley), Lausanne et hubs internationaux
- Coût de la vie : coût élevé de Genève poussant les talents tech vers l'emploi international à distance ou la relocation

Tendances clés 2024-2025 :
- Prime aux talents IA : talents AI/data science commandant des primes de 30-50%
- Travail à distance permettant la rétention
- Pipeline tech-finance : secteur financier genevois en compétition avec les startups tech
- Talents tech humanitarian : intersection unique des compétences tech et de la mission humanitarian attirant un profil spécifique
- Liens université-industrie : partenariats EPFL-industrie créant des voies d'accès à la recherche pour les entreprises genevoises
