## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Geneva Startup Ecosystem — Scaleup Gap and Regulatory Support
- Strong seed/pre-seed; weaker Series A/B "scaleup gap" — Geneva startups frequently acquired by international buyers rather than scaling to maturity
- EPFL/UNIGE talent pipeline feeding tech; CS/engineering graduates increasingly choosing startups over banking
- Geneva fintech/wealthtech/regtech cluster; FINMA sandbox accessible; Swiss fintech license creating new category
- Regulatory strategy advisory opportunity for Geneva AI/fintech startups navigating FINMA/FDPIC/cross-border requirements
- Geneva cost base challenging for talent retention vs. Zurich/international competitors

### Finding 02: UN/IO Budget Reform — Efficiency Pressure Creating Consulting Demand
- Donor fatigue and geopolitical tensions reducing voluntary contributions; US funding review creating uncertainty
- UN system-wide efficiency mandates: back-office consolidation, shared services, digital transformation
- ICRC budget pressures, WTO dispute resolution backlog, WHO financing reform all active
- Geneva-specific: UNOG as reform implementation hub; private sector engagement growing (complex governance challenges)
- IO consulting: digital transformation, procurement reform, private sector partnership structuring, efficiency/budget advisory — lower margins but stable revenue; relationship-driven

### Finding 03: Geneva Digital Diplomacy — Tech Norms and AI Governance Hub
- ITU (Geneva HQ): global telecom/ICT standard-setting (5G/6G, satellite, internet infrastructure)
- WSIS: Geneva-based internet governance framework process
- UN Secretary-General's AI Advisory Board: Geneva-based AI governance norm-setting
- WTO e-commerce plurilateral negotiations on digital trade rules
- UN cybersecurity GGE negotiations: Geneva-based international cybersecurity norm development
- Internet fragmentation concerns: Geneva as potential reunification/coordination hub
- Advisory: public policy advisory for tech companies engaging Geneva governance processes; AI governance advisory; conference/event economy

### Finding 04: Climate Tech in Geneva — Sustainability Tech Ecosystem
- Carbon accounting software, ESG analytics, circular economy tech, green fintech
- Geneva commodity traders investing in carbon market technology platforms
- Swiss Re and Geneva insurers: climate risk modeling and adaptation tech investments
- UNEP, Climate Champions, Geneva Sustainable Finance: driving demand for sustainability tech
- Swiss Green Bond Framework + regulatory clarity = competitive advantage for Geneva green finance
- Voluntary carbon market integrity concerns driving verification/technology demand
- Advisory: climate tech startup regulatory strategy, impact measurement frameworks, green fintech structuring

### Finding 05: Geneva Tech Talent Pipeline — Brain Circulation Model
- EPFL/UNIGE: CS/engineering graduates; Geneva IO community drawing humanities talent
- Brain circulation vs. brain drain: remote work enabling Geneva tech talent working internationally from Geneva
- AI talent premium: 30-50% premiums; Geneva companies struggling vs. US firms
- Remote work as retention mechanism; tech-to-finance pipeline competing for same talent
- Humanitarian tech talent niche: unique intersection of tech skills and mission at Geneva IOs/NGOs
- University-industry links: EPFL industry partnerships creating research talent access for Geneva companies
- Advisory: talent strategy consulting, compensation benchmarking, remote work policy structuring

### Finding 06: Geneva AI/Data Startups — Niche Positioning
- Distinct from Zurich Crypto Valley; focused on: AI for financial services, AI for humanitarian/IO applications, commodity trading data analytics, AI health tech, sustainability AI
- CERN spin-off AI companies leveraging physics/ML expertise
- Family office and private bank venture arms filling VC gap; international VCs increasingly visiting for AI/health deals
- Valuation gap: Geneva AI startups sometimes undervalued vs. international due to perceived geographic disadvantage
- Key challenge: international perception of Geneva as expensive/conservative; talent competition from US firms
- Opportunity: Geneva AI startups serving Geneva IO/NGO market have unique access advantage
