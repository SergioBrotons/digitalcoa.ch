## What Others Say and What's Missing

---

## 1. The Big4 Landscape

### PwC Switzerland
**What they say**: "AI in the financial industry — aligning with FINMA guidance and the EU AI Act"
**Position**: Advisory on AI governance, FINMA compliance, EU AI Act alignment
**Gap for Geneva SMB**: PwC serves large institutions. Their frameworks assume dedicated AI governance teams. A boutique with 80 employees cannot implement their methodology.

### KPMG Switzerland
**Position**: Risk and compliance focus
**Gap**: Heavy enterprise focus, long engagement timelines, high minimum fees.

### Deloitte Switzerland
**Position**: Research and thought leadership on the Swiss AI-secrecy conflict
**Gap**: More analytical than practical. Reports the problem without offering concrete SMB-priced solutions.

### EY Switzerland
**Position**: Industry research and benchmark reports (81% of wealth management executives view AI as key to scalability)
**Gap**: Research focus, not implementation focus.

**Overall Big4 gap**: All four major firms are structurally oriented toward large institutions. They do not serve the "80-person boutique, managing director accountable, no compliance team, legacy systems" profile.

---

## 2. Cloud Providers — AWS, Azure, Google Cloud

### AWS Switzerland
**What they say**: "AWS is the first hyperscaler to publish a FINMA ISAE 3000 Type II attestation report covering 183 services."
**Position**: Compliance documentation provider
**The offer**: ISAE 3000 Type II attestation reports demonstrating control environments
**The gap for clients**: The attestation proves AWS's controls — but it does not prove the client's AI governance. The client must build their own governance framework and map AWS controls to FINMA expectations.

---

## 3. SROs (Self-Regulatory Organizations)

**What they say**: Nothing. SROs have been silent on AI governance.
**The problem**: SROs have not issued guidance on how FINMA 08/2024 applies to their members. Boutique wealth managers who are SRO members are in a regulatory grey zone.
**The gap**: No SRO-specific AI governance guidance for small wealth managers. No templates, no checklists, no training materials.

## 5. The Geneva Competitive Vacuum

**What's missing for Geneva SMB wealth managers**:

1. **No SMB-priced AI governance solution**: Big4 and enterprise vendors are structurally priced for large institutions. No established player offers "AI governance for 50-200 person wealth managers" at accessible fees.

2. **No Swiss-specific AI governance template**: No standardized framework exists specifically for Swiss SMB wealth managers addressing FINMA 08/2024, nFADP, SRO obligations, and institutional client DDQ requirements in one document.

3. **No "attestation chain" guidance**: No clear, practical explanation of how carrier ISAE 3000 reports translate into client-side FINMA compliance evidence.

4. **No SRO-specific AI guidance**: SROs have been silent. Wealth managers under SRO supervision have no SRO-specific AI governance guidance.

5. **No bilingual (EN/FR) SMB AI governance resource**: Swiss wealth management operates in both official languages. No practical bilingual resource exists for Geneva-based managing directors.

6. **No institutional DDQ preparation service**: When a 40-page AI governance questionnaire arrives from an institutional client, there is no service specifically helping Geneva SMBs prepare responses.

7. **No clear "human-in-the-loop" practical framework**: The FINMA requirement for human oversight lacks practical implementation guidance for boutique advisory practices.
