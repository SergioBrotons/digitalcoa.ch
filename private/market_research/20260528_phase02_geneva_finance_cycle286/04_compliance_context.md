## Switzerland and Geneva-Specific Regulatory Framework

---

## 1. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — The December 2024 AI Governance Document

**What it is**: FINMA published "Guidance 08/2024 on governance and risk management when using artificial intelligence" on December 18, 2024.

**Key requirements**:

### Governance Framework
- Establish a governance framework with clear responsibilities for AI
- Define competencies and responsibilities for development, implementation, supervision, and use of AI
- Integrate AI risk management into existing risk management structures

### AI Inventory
- Maintain a centralized inventory of all AI applications
- Include risk classification for each AI application
- Document mitigation measures for each risk-rated AI application

### Data Quality
- Ensure quality, accuracy, consistency, and completeness of data used in AI systems
- Appropriate access controls and availability protocols
- Address data bias and representativeness issues

### Testing and Monitoring
- Robust testing protocols before deployment
- Continuous monitoring and regular validation of AI models
- Defined performance indicators and ongoing monitoring processes

### Explainability and Documentation
- AI-generated results must be "understandable, explainable, and reproducible"
- Detailed documentation covering: purpose, data selection, model choice, performance measures, assumptions, limitations, testing, fallback solutions

### Independent Review
- Clear distinction between development of AI applications and independent review
- Independent review by qualified personnel who were not involved in development

### Third-Party Providers
- Due diligence on third-party AI providers
- Explicit contractual obligations regarding responsibilities and liability
- Monitoring of third-party AI service providers

**FINMA supervisory activity**: FINMA has been conducting on-site inspections and supervisory exchanges since Q4 2023 to assess AI governance adequacy.

## 2. The SRO Layer

**What is an SRO**: Self-Regulatory Organizations (SROs) are FINMA-recognized bodies that supervise financial intermediaries who are not directly supervised by FINMA. Many Geneva boutique wealth managers are SRO members.

**SROs and AI**: SROs have NOT issued specific AI governance guidance to their members as of May 2026. This creates confusion — FINMA expects AI governance, but the SROs have not communicated what their members must do.

**What SRO members should be doing (even without SRO guidance)**:
- Mapping FINMA 08/2024 requirements to their operations
- Building AI governance frameworks regardless of SRO silence
- Documenting AI tool usage and risk assessments
- Preparing for potential FINMA supervisory reviews

## 3. The nFADP — AI Implications

**Status**: In force since September 1, 2023. Aligns closely with GDPR but includes Swiss-specific additions.

### Art. 63 — Criminal Liability
- Serious data protection violations can result in criminal liability
- **Critical for executives**: Individual managing directors or partners can face personal fines
- This is not a corporate fine — it applies to responsible individuals

### Automated Decision-Making
- Individuals must be informed when they are subject to automated decisions that produce legal effects or significantly affect them
- Right to human review of automated decisions upon request
- Stricter consent requirements for profiling compared to GDPR

### DSFA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)
- Required for high-risk data processing
- AI systems processing sensitive personal data (financial data) likely require DSFA
- Must be completed before processing begins

---

## 4. The Council of Europe AI Convention

**What it is**: Switzerland has signed the Council of Europe AI Convention — an international treaty establishing AI governance principles.

**Status**: Signed; ratification process ongoing. Expected to require legislative changes in Switzerland by end of 2026.

**Implications**: The AI Convention will eventually be integrated into Swiss law. Geneva wealth managers should monitor developments but not act urgently yet.

---

## 5. EU AI Act — Implications for Swiss Wealth Managers with EU Clients

**The practical answer**: Yes, in specific circumstances:
- If Swiss wealth managers process data of EU citizens
- If they provide AI systems to EU-based entities
- If their AI output is used in the EU

**EU AI Act risk categories**:
- **High risk**: AI systems in credit, financial risk assessment, employment decisions
- **Limited risk**: Chatbots, AI-generated content — transparency requirements
- **Minimal risk**: Most other AI applications

**Swiss wealth management context**: Portfolio management AI, client risk profiling, investment recommendation systems may be classified as high-risk under EU AI Act.
