## For DigitalCoa.ch (Executive / Advisory Positioning)

### Angle 1: "The Section 301 Storm: Swiss Exporters Need to Act Now"
- **Hook:** The US has two Section 301 investigations open against Switzerland — industrial overcapacity and forced labor imports. If either succeeds, Swiss exporters face new tariffs. The bilateral trade negotiation is the pressure point.
- **Core argument:** Geneva companies with US market exposure should map their Section 301 exposure, prepare tariff classification reviews, and scenario-plan for both tariff and non-tariff outcomes. The consulting demand is immediate.
- **CTA:** trade compliance readiness assessment / Section 301 exposure audit
- **Tone:** direct, executive-grade, action-oriented

### Angle 2: "ECB Rate Hikes Are Back: What Geneva Companies Must Do Now"
- **Hook:** Eurozone inflation at 2.5% and heading to 3%+ by May. ECB expected to raise rates in April or June. Geneva companies and wealth management clients who positioned for rate cuts now face a reversal.
- **Core argument:** capital markets recalibration, floating rate debt review, EUR/CHF hedging, portfolio rebalancing — all consulting mandates that flow from this macro shift
- **CTA:** interest rate risk review / capital allocation recalibration
- **Tone:** sharp, analytical, CFO/treasurer-focused

### Angle 3: "The Geneva Consulting Firm's Own Sanctions Problem"
- **Hook:** Geneva consulting firms providing legal, accounting, PR, or IT services to Russian clients may need SECO authorization. The professional services ban is broader than most firms realize.
- **Core argument:** consulting firms are both advisors AND potential subjects of Russia sanctions. Their own internal compliance needs to be assessed before they can advise clients credibly.
- **CTA:** professional services sanctions compliance review
- **Tone:** technical, precise, compliance-focused

## For Brotons.net (Thought Leadership / Interpretive)

### Angle 4: "The Rockets and Feathers Economy: Why Swiss Prices Rise Like Rockets and Fall Like Feathers"
- **Hook:** The "rockets and feathers" fuel pricing dynamic reveals something structural about Swiss market economics: oligopoly concentration, asymmetric price adjustment, and the role of the 50% tax component in fuel prices.
- **Core argument:** interpretive essay on the structural economics of Swiss price formation — this is not just about fuel but about how Swiss businesses absorb and pass through cost shocks
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — structural, analytical, illuminating

### Angle 5: "The WTO Is Failing Again: What the Digital Tariff Collapse Means for Swiss Tech"
- **Hook:** The WTO digital tariff moratorium has expired — the third major WTO failure in recent years. Switzerland's digital services exporters now face an uncertain tariff environment with no multilateral framework to rely on.
- **Core argument:** interpretive piece on the multilateral trade system's decline, Switzerland's options, and the strategic implications for Geneva's digital services exporters
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — structural, globally informed, analytical

### Angle 6: "The Geneva Consulting Firm as Client: A Meta-Analysis"
- **Hook:** Geneva consulting firms face a dual challenge: advising clients on sanctions/compliance while ensuring their own Russia-related engagements are compliant. This meta-angle — consultants as clients — is unique to Phase 03.
- **Core argument:** Brotons-style analytical piece on the self-referential nature of the Geneva consulting market: advisory demand creates advisory demand
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — introspective, market-aware, strategically oriented
