## F1: "Rockets and Feathers" — Swiss Fuel Market Oligopoly and the Margin Advisory Opportunity
**Source:** NZZ, TCS data
**Signal:** MEDIUM — operational consulting, Geneva transport/logistics sector

NZZ analysis of Swiss fuel pricing reveals the "rockets and feathers" phenomenon: prices spike rapidly with oil market shocks but fall slowly when oil prices retreat. Key data:
- Diesel: CHF 2.24/l (+4% in 2 days over Easter)
- Petrol (Bleifrei 95): CHF 1.89/l (+15% since Iran war began late February)
- 50% of fuel price = government taxes (Mineralölsteuer, MWST, Importabgaben)
- Swiss market structure: oligopoly — Agrola, Avia, Migrol, Shell, Socar dominate
- "Rockets and feathers": prices adjust upward quickly, downward slowly — oligopoly coordination prevents rapid price cuts even when oil recedes

For Geneva consulting: this creates several advisory angles:
- Fuel cost benchmarking for transport and logistics companies
- Procurement strategy for fuel-intensive businesses
- Operational efficiency analysis for companies with vehicle fleets
- Energy transition planning (EV fleet conversion cost-benefit analysis)
- Hospitality/tourism sector fuel cost exposure analysis (Geneva hotel group shuttle services, restaurant supply logistics)

## F2: US Section 301 Investigations Against Switzerland — Trade Compliance Advisory Demand
**Source:** NZZ (Bundesrat press release)
**Signal:** HIGH — trade compliance consulting, Geneva international firms

The US launched two Section 301 investigations against Switzerland on March 11-12, 2026:
1. **Industrial overcapacity investigation** — targeting alleged Swiss industrial production overcapacity
2. **Forced labor import investigation** — targeting insufficient measures to prevent forced labor imports

Section 301 allows the US President to impose retaliatory tariffs or trade-restricting measures. The Bundesrat has rejected both allegations and is presenting Switzerland's position. Bilateral negotiations on a US-Switzerland trade agreement continue (progress made per the November 14, 2025 joint statement targeting Q1 2026 milestones).

Geneva consulting implications:
- Trade compliance advisory for Swiss exporters to US market
- Tariff classification and mitigation strategy
- Supply chain due diligence for forced labor compliance (overlaps with NUFG corporate liability)
- Export control advisory (overlaps with Phase 01 sanctions findings)
- Market access strategy for US pharma, watches, precision machinery

## F4: WTO Digital Tariff Moratorium Expires — Swiss Digital Services Export Uncertainty
**Source:** NZZ / Reuters
**Signal:** MEDIUM — trade policy consulting, Geneva digital firms

The WTO's moratorium on tariffs for electronic transmissions (digital downloads, streaming) has expired. Key dynamics:
- Brazil blocked extension; US pushed for longer/permanent extension
- WTO Director-General Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala acknowledged the failure but said talks continue
- Developing countries argue the moratorium deprives them of tariff revenue
- New setback for WTO credibility — already under pressure

Switzerland as a major digital services exporter (software, fintech, cloud services, digital consulting) faces potential tariff exposure in major export markets. Geneva digital firms (especially SaaS, cloud, data services) need:
- Market-by-market tariff exposure assessment
- Pricing strategy adjustments for new tariff regimes
- Trade policy engagement (through Swiss trade associations)
- Alternative market development strategies
