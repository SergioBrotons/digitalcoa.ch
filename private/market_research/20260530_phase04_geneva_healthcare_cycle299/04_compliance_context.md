# Compliance Context: Geneva Healthcare — C299 Phase 04

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape

### Primary Regulatory Framework

**FMH Medical Professional Standards**
- Human oversight requirement stated without operational definition
- No Geneva-specific small practice methodology
- Physicians individually responsible for AI decisions made in practice

**nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)**
- In force September 1, 2023
- Personal health data is special category
- Art.63 criminal liability applies to unauthorized processing
- DPA requirements apply to AI tool vendors

**Medical Liability**
- Personal liability of treating physician
- License irreplaceable
- No Swiss federal AI liability law = physician bears 100% liability

**Swissmedic (for AI-enabled medical devices)**
- Regulatory framework for medical devices
- AI-enabled diagnostics may fall under device regulations
- Conformity assessment requirements

---

## The Compliance Gap

The regulations exist. The proportionate implementation for small independent practices does not.

Key gaps:
- FMH human oversight — no operational definition
- nFADP Art.9 DPA — no compliant vendor offering for small practices
- Carrier attestation — no pre-signing review service
- Patient AI content — no documentation category
- Swiss hosting verification — no methodology for independent practices

---

## The Personal Liability Layer

The practicing physician is personally liable for:
- Clinical decisions (with or without AI)
- Data protection compliance
- Carrier attestation signed

No separation between entity-level and personal liability. The license is on the line.
