# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Healthcare — C299 Phase 04

## What Exists, What's Missing

### Current Competitive Landscape

**What Exists:**
- FMH guidelines (principles-based, not operational)
- Generic AI governance frameworks (designed for hospitals/large practices)
- AI tool vendor documentation (vendor-serving, not physician-serving)
- General data protection guidance (not AI-specific)

**What's Missing:**
- Proportionate to small practice scale (2–10 professionals)
- Geneva-specific regulatory context
- Personal liability naming
- FMH human oversight operationalization
- Carrier attestation preparation
- Patient AI content protocol

### The White Space

1. **Pre-signing carrier attestation review** — No service reviews carrier AI attestation forms before signature for small medical practices
2. **FMH oversight operationalization** — Translating "human oversight" into documented practice workflow
3. **Patient AI content protocol** — Documentation category for AI-generated input patient brings to consultation
4. **AI tool evaluation for medical practices** — Structured assessment of AI tools against FMH obligations
5. **Swiss-hosted AI tool verification** — Distinguishing Swiss data residency from Swiss data protection compliance
6. **Medical AI incident protocol** — What to do when AI-assisted clinical decision may have caused harm

---

## The Gap

The gap is not information. The gap is:
- Proportionate implementation for 2–10 person practices
- Personal liability naming (license, personal assets)
- Documentation categories that fit clinical workflow
- Carrier-ready evidence format

No Geneva-specific service addresses these for independent medical practices.
