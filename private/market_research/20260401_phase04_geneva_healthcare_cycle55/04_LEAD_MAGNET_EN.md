## What Swissdamed Actually Requires

Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Database) is the Swiss implementation of EUDAMED-equivalent medical device registration requirements.

From July 1, 2026:
- **Mandatory device registration**: All medical devices must be registered in Swissdamed
- **Vigilance cases**: Immediate registration required from July 1 — no grace period
- **AI-enabled medical devices**: Any AI system used in clinical decision-making is a medical device under Swissmedic interpretation

**This is not a future obligation. It is a July 1, 2026 operative requirement with no transition period.**

## Agentic AI in Clinical Settings — Maximum Liability

Agentic AI systems in clinical settings carry the highest liability exposure in the portfolio. Unlike other sectors, AI errors in healthcare can cause direct patient harm.

The three-tier framework applies — but clinical context elevates every tier:

| System Type | Clinical Application | Liability |
|---|---|---|
| AI Assistant | Patient communication, scheduling, documentation | Practice |
| AI Agent | Clinical documentation, triage support, diagnostic assistance | Practice + clinician |
| Agentic AI | Autonomous clinical decision support | Practice + clinician + Swissmedic |

The legal and professional accountability chain is absolute: the practice and the clinician are always responsible for clinical decisions — regardless of how the AI system is classified.

## The "Envelope vs. Letter" Framework for Healthcare

**Envelope (Safe to Automate — Under Governance):**
- Patient appointment scheduling and reminders
- Billing and insurance claim processing
- Administrative document formatting and filing
- Patient intake form processing
- Prescription refill management (under physician review)

**Letter (Clinical — Highest Risk — Requires Swissdamed + Clinical Governance):**
- AI diagnostic assistance or decision support
- AI triage or patient routing based on clinical data
- AI-enabled medical devices
- Any system that generates or influences clinical recommendations

**The efficiency opportunity:** 8–14 hours per week are recoverable through safe automation of administrative workflows in a typical small medical practice. This is simultaneously efficiency and risk mitigation — it removes the highest-risk AI usage from administrative contexts while recovering physician time.
