## Net-New Findings — Phase 02 C51

### 1. FINMA 08/2024: AI Liability Explicitly Confirmed (NEW)
- Companies deploying AI remain LIABLE for outcomes of automated decisions as if human decisions
- AI must be integrated into existing risk controls and oversight structures
- AI risk analyses documentation required for critical systems before deployment
- **Implication:** This is explicit liability language. Boutique WMs cannot use "the AI made the decision" as a defense.

### 2. FATF Switzerland 5th Country Review 2026-2027 (NEW)
- Switzerland's FATF mutual evaluation is the most significant external pressure on WM firms
- FATF January 2026 horizon scan: AI and deepfakes = emerging ML/TF/PF risks
- Swiss AMLA revision + LETA expected mid-2026 (coincident with FATF review prep)
- **Implication:** WM firms preparing for FATF review will need documented AI governance for AML/KYC

### 3. FATF XAI Requirement: Explainable AI Mandatory (NEW)
- FATF requires AI systems used in AML compliance to provide clear, auditable logic for conclusions
- Multilingual screening + source of funds verification must be explainable
- **Implication:** Black-box AI systems in AML/KYC may not meet FATF requirements. Explainability is now a compliance requirement.

### 4. LETA Mid-2026: Tighter Advisor Obligations (NEW)
- Federal Act on Transparency of Legal Entities (LETA) expected mid-2026
- Federal beneficial ownership register introduced
- Tighter obligations on advisors (likely including WM advisors managing client structures)
- Increased scrutiny on crypto flows
- **Implication:** WM advisors managing structures with beneficial ownership will face new documentation requirements. AI used in this process must be governed.

### 5. Swiss Federal Council: Sector-Specific Approach Confirmed (Corroboration)
