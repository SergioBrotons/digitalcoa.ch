## Three Immediate Regulatory Obligations

### 1. FINMA Circular 08/2024 — AI Governance Now Required + Personal Liability
**Status:** Primary source confirmed December 2024. Obligations already operative.

**NEW in C51 — FINMA explicitly confirms liability:**
> "Companies are liable for outcomes of automated systems as if they were human decisions."

FINMA requires Swiss financial institutions to:
- Maintain an AI tool inventory (all AI systems in use)
- Classify AI risks (operational, compliance, reputational)
- Assign clear responsibility for AI systems
- Train staff on AI use policies
- Document AI-related incidents
- **Document AI risk analyses for critical systems before deployment**
- **Integrate AI into existing risk controls and oversight structures**

The FINMA AI Desk has been established. On-site inspections have been active since Q4 2023.

### 2. CARF/CRS 2.0 — Automatic Exchange of Information
**Status:** Operative January 1, 2026. Systems must be CARF-ready now. Reporting postponed to 2027.

Common Reporting Standard (CRS) Version 2.0 is live with:
- Expanded scope: e-money products, CBDCs, indirect crypto via derivatives
- Tighter due-diligence requirements
- TIN (Tax Identification Number) validation mandatory
- AI systems used in client onboarding, KYC, and reporting must be documented

### 3. FATF 5th Country Review 2026-2027 — Explainable AI Required
**Status:** Active evaluation period. Switzerland under international review.

**NEW in C51 — FATF AI requirements for AML:**
- AI-augmented systems are essential for modern AML compliance
- **Explainable AI (XAI) required**: systems must provide clear, auditable logic for conclusions
- Multilingual screening and source of funds verification must be explainable
- FATF January 2026 horizon scan: AI and deepfakes = emerging ML/TF/PF risks
- **Implication:** Black-box AI in AML/KYC may not meet FATF requirements

**NEW in C51 — Swiss AML reforms mid-2026:**
- AMLA revision expected mid-2026 (coincident with FATF review prep)
- Federal Act on Transparency of Legal Entities (LETA): federal beneficial ownership register
- Tighter obligations on advisors (likely WM advisors managing client structures)
- Increased scrutiny on crypto flows

## The Shadow AI Problem in WM

91% of Swiss financial institutions using AI are using **generative AI**. This means:
- ChatGPT/Claude for client communications
- GenAI for investment research summaries
- Consumer AI tools for market analysis
- Undocumented AI tools processing client financial data

**Shadow AI is primarily consumer GenAI in WM.** This is an FADP high-risk category: financial data is sensitive by definition.

**FDPIC enforcement resources demonstrably increased:** Client financial data is an FADP high-risk category. The enforcement pattern across sectors is established. WM is the next likely enforcement target.
