# 04 — Compliance Context: Geneva/Switzerland Specific Regulatory

**Phase:** C311 Phase 03 | **ICP:** Small Consulting Boutiques, 2–50 employees, Geneva | **Date:** 2026-05-31

---

## Regulatory Framework for Geneva Consulting Boutiques

The Geneva consulting boutique operates in a regulatory environment that creates specific AI governance obligations — some direct, some indirect, some through client relationships, some through carrier requirements. The managing director needs to understand which regulations apply to their specific situation, in what sequence, and with what urgency.

---

## 1. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) — The Primary Direct Obligation

**Status:** In force since September 1, 2023 (approximately 3 years as of mid-2026)

**Article 63 — Personal Liability of the Responsible Person:**
Article 63 establishes that the "responsible person" — typically the managing director or partner — bears personal liability under nFADP. This is not a corporate liability. It is a personal liability that attaches to the individual who holds the responsible person role.

**For the Geneva consulting boutique:**
The managing director processes personal data through AI tools in their consulting work — client information, contact data, project-specific personal data. Every AI tool used in processing personal client data triggers nFADP obligations. The managing director is the responsible person.

**The specific triggering conditions:**
- Processing personal data (client information is personal data under nFADP)
- Using an AI system to process that data (AI tools processing client data = nFADP Article 5(c) processing)
- No documentation of the processing basis, purpose limitation, or data subject rights fulfillment
- No nFADP DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) for the AI systems in use
- No documented human accountability principle for AI-influenced decisions in client work

**The exposure:**
Article 63 liability is personal. The FDPIC can investigate the managing director personally. The carrier's professional liability coverage may not cover nFADP enforcement actions. The client's data subject rights complaints may expose the managing director directly.

**What "adequate" means under nFADP for a consulting boutique:**
"Adequate" documentation for a 2-50 person Geneva consulting firm means:
1. A documented inventory of all AI systems processing personal client data
2. A nFADP DPIA for each AI system (proportionate to the risk)
3. A documented basis for processing (consent, contract, legitimate interest — specific to each AI use case)
4. A human accountability principle — who reviews AI output before it enters the client deliverable
5. Data subject rights procedures — how client data subjects can exercise their rights under nFADP
6. Sub-consultant data processing agreements — who processes client data on behalf of the firm, under what terms

## 2. Client AI Governance Questionnaires — The Procurement Trigger

**Status:** Arriving now, increasing frequency

**What they are:**
Client AI governance questionnaires are vendor due diligence instruments sent by mid-sized corporates, family offices, and institutional clients to their consulting vendors. They ask about the consulting firm's AI governance practices — policies, documentation, methodology, data handling, AI tool inventory.

**Why they matter for the Geneva consulting boutique:**
The client AI questionnaire is a procurement trigger. If the consulting firm cannot respond credibly, the client has grounds to reconsider the engagement. The questionnaire is not a compliance exercise — it is a commercial relationship management tool. The managing director who responds confidently and specifically has an advantage over the one who guesses or delays.

**The regulatory convergence:**
The client's AI governance questionnaire is often calibrated to recognized standards — ISO 27001, NIST AI RMF, or the client's own internal AI governance framework. The consulting firm that has documented its AI governance practices to a recognized standard can respond to any client's questionnaire from a position of evidence, not assertion.

**The Geneva-specific dimension:**
Geneva consulting firms serve clients who are themselves subject to FINMA, nFADP, and EU AI Act obligations. Their questionnaires are designed to ensure their vendors meet the same standards. The consulting firm's AI governance documentation is not just a commercial requirement — it is part of their clients' regulatory compliance chain.

## 3. Carrier Attestation — The Professional Liability Connection

**Status:** Signed at renewal, now under scrutiny as carriers add AI governance questions

**The structural shift (from Phase 01 findings):**
Carrier AI attestation forms have changed question type — from binary yes/no to open-form descriptive. The renewal question now requires infrastructure that does not exist in most Geneva boutiques. The managing director who signed the old form and assumed compliance does not realize they have committed to a documentation system they don't have.

**What the carrier form actually requires:**
The carrier form — for a professional liability (PL) policy, which Geneva consulting firms typically carry — asks about AI governance documentation. The specific questions vary by carrier, but the general structure now requires the managing director to attest to:
- Existence of an AI governance policy
- Inventory of AI systems used in client work
- Documentation of how AI is used in client deliverables
- Human review process for AI-assisted work
- Sub-consultant AI use disclosure and management
- Incident response procedures for AI-related errors

**The personal signature:**
The managing director typically signs the carrier attestation in their own name — not the firm name. This means the commitment is personal. If the carrier asks for evidence of compliance at renewal or in the event of a claim, the managing director is personally accountable for what was attested to.

**The retroactive problem:**
For firms that signed the attestation without having the documentation in place, the retroactive reconstruction question is acute. The documentation that should have been created at the time of signing — or continuously since — does not exist. Retroactive reconstruction is technically possible but legally fragile. The managing director needs both the retroactive solution and the forward documentation practice.

## 4. Sub-Consultant Delegation — The Chain of Responsibility

**The regulatory framework:**
Under nFADP, the consulting firm (as data controller) is responsible for the processing activities of its sub-consultants (as data processors). This means:
- The managing director must have data processing agreements with sub-consultants who process client personal data
- The sub-consultant's AI tool use is within the scope of those agreements
- The managing director is accountable for ensuring the sub-consultant's processing complies with nFADP
- The managing director cannot delegate the responsibility — they can only delegate the task

**The AI delegation problem specifically:**
Traditional sub-consultant agreements cover the work product — the analysis, the report, the deliverable. They do not typically cover the methodology — how the work was produced, what tools were used. When a sub-consultant uses AI tools to produce the work product, the consulting firm is using AI indirectly — but without the same documentation requirements being applied to the sub-consultant.

**What needs to be in the sub-consultant agreement:**
- AI tool inventory disclosure (what AI tools does the sub-consultant use in producing the work)
- Methodology documentation (how was the work produced, what was the AI's role)
- Human review certification (who reviewed the AI output before delivery)
- Data handling obligations (how is client personal data handled in the AI tool's processing)
- Compliance with the same nFADP standards the consulting firm must meet

## 5. Proposal Methodology Defensibility — The Commercial Requirement

**Not a direct regulation — but a commercial obligation with regulatory implications**

**The consulting firm's methodology is:**
- The product being sold (the client pays for the firm's specific way of solving the problem)
- The basis for professional liability (errors in methodology are the basis for PL claims)
- The documentation target (the client can ask how the work was produced, and the firm must be able to answer)

**When AI assists the methodology:**
If AI tools were used in producing the client deliverable — research, analysis, drafting, structure — the methodology documentation must account for:
- What AI was used
- At what stage
- With what human review
- Producing what specific output
- How the AI output was verified before entering the client deliverable

**The defensibility gap:**
The consulting firm that cannot document its AI-assisted methodology has a problem when:
- The client asks how the work was produced
- A PL claim arises involving an AI-assisted deliverable
- The carrier asks how AI was used in the work that gave rise to the claim
- The client's regulator asks how the vendor's AI governance was applied to the client's work

## 6. FINMA AI Guidance (December 2024) — The Indirect Applicability

**Status:** December 2024 baseline guidance; approximately 18 months old as of mid-2026

**Applicability to consulting firms:**
FINMA supervised entities include banks, insurers, securities dealers, and fund management companies. A Geneva consulting boutique is generally not a FINMA supervised entity. However:

- If the consulting firm has a financial services subsidiary
- If the consulting firm is registered as an SRO member (for wealth management or insurance brokerage)
- If the consulting firm's clients include FINMA-supervised entities that flow down AI governance requirements
- If the consulting firm's work touches regulated financial services

...then FINMA AI Guidance creates an indirect standard that the consulting firm may be held to through client contractual requirements.

**The practical implication:**
The consulting firm that can demonstrate alignment with FINMA AI Guidance principles — governance, risk management, transparency, non-discrimination — has a documentation standard that exceeds most client questionnaire requirements and positions well for carrier attestation responses.

## 7. EU AI Act (August 2, 2026) — The Extraterritorial Reach

**Status:** August 2, 2026 operative date for most provisions (~15 months from mid-2026)

**Applicability to Geneva consulting boutiques:**
The EU AI Act applies extraterritorially to:
- Providers placing AI systems on the EU market
- Deployers of AI systems in the EU
- Firms whose AI systems produce outputs used in the EU

For a Geneva consulting boutique:
- If the firm uses AI tools that have EU market nexus (AI tool providers are EU-based or intend EU market)
- If the consulting firm's client work produces outputs used by EU-based clients or in EU markets
- If the firm has EU-based sub-consultants using AI tools

...then EU AI Act obligations may apply, particularly around:
- Transparency (AI-generated content must be identifiable as AI-generated)
- Human oversight (high-risk AI systems require human oversight measures)
- Documentation (high-risk AI system documentation requirements)

**The nFADP convergence:**
Both nFADP and EU AI Act require documentation of AI processing activities. The consulting firm that has built its nFADP documentation framework — AI inventory, DPIA, human accountability principle — is well-positioned to meet EU AI Act requirements if they apply.

## Regulatory Urgency Sequence for Geneva Consulting Boutique

**Now (already in effect):**
- nFADP Article 63 personal liability — already operative
- Client AI governance questionnaires — arriving as procurement triggers
- Carrier attestation — signed and in force
- Sub-consultant data processing agreements — required for any sub-consultant handling client personal data

**Next 6–12 months:**
- Client questionnaire frequency increasing (market awareness growing)
- Carrier AI governance question sophistication increasing (forms being updated)
- EU AI Act August 2, 2026 operative (if EU nexus applies)
- nFADP enforcement trajectory (FDPIC enforcement increasing across sectors)

**Unknown timeline:**
- FINMA AI Guidance updates (gap is structural; no update published since December 2024)
- Geneva bar (OaF) AI guidance for attorneys (consulting firms may have attorney-partners)

## The Compliance Architecture the Consulting Boutique Needs

The Geneva consulting boutique needs a documentation architecture that serves multiple purposes simultaneously:

1. **nFADP compliance** — AI inventory, DPIA, human accountability principle, data subject rights
2. **Client questionnaire response** — documented AI governance practices, methodology documentation, sub-consultant management
3. **Carrier attestation support** — pre-signing review, documentation existence verification, evidence production
4. **Methodology defensibility** — AI-assisted work documented by stage, with human review verification
5. **Sub-consultant chain** — data processing agreements, AI tool disclosure, methodology documentation requirements

One architecture, multiple uses. The managing director does not have the bandwidth to build separate compliance systems for each regulatory instrument.
