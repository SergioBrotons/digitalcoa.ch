## What Exists in the Market

### AI Scribe & Documentation Tools

**Haidy Reporter**
- EU MDR Class I medical device, Swissmedic registered
- ISO 13485 certified
- Listens to consultations, generates clinical notes and referral letters
- Suggests billing codes
- Data stays in Switzerland
- Positioned as most "compliant-ready" for medical practice use

**Intonate**
- Swiss AI scribe founded 2024
- Records doctor-patient consultations, auto-generates SOAP notes
- Supports Swiss German dialects
- Integrates with existing systems
- Positioned as practice-integrated solution

**Evoya AI (Konversa)**
- AI assistant for daily medical practice
- Text assistance, document analysis, knowledge management, voice assistance
- Multilingual (including Swiss German)
- HIPAA-ready infrastructure, BAA available
- Flexible deployment including on-premise
- Data hosted on Swiss servers
- Targets both clinical and administrative tasks

**MPAssist (Scribe AI)**
- Medical AI Suite
- AI chat for medical professionals
- Fully hosted in Switzerland, complete data sovereignty
- Scribe AI converts audio recordings to comprehensive medical reports

### Voice & Administrative AI

**AIAgens**
- Based in Ticino, Switzerland
- AI front-desk platform for Swiss medical practices
- 24/7 AI voice assistant, email automation, appointment scheduling, patient routing
- Medical scribe module
- FADP and GDPR compliant
- Data hosted in Switzerland/EU

**Mira AI**
- AI-powered reception assistant for Swiss medical practices
- Handles structured appointment intake and patient communication
- No medical decision logic — clearly administrative
- Integrates with practice management systems
- Data processed in Switzerland

**Swiss AI Digital Medical Phone Assistant**
- Smart medical bot for phone and chat
- Schedules appointments, prioritizes patients based on AI criteria
- Automates office processes from scheduling to billing

### Medical Information AI

**Ask Dr. Nuts / In a Nutshell**
- Medical chatbot trained with content from Swiss primary care educational program
- Assists Swiss physicians with literature review
- Curated medical information retrieval
- Not a clinical decision tool — clearly informational

### Hospital-Level AI (Geneva-specific)

**HUG AI Hub (Campus Biotech)**
- Innovation hub combining clinicians, researchers, AI experts
- Focus on neurotechnology and AI-informed care
- Develops AI tools for understanding infection risks in transplant recipients
- Medical information chatbots for chronic diseases (e.g., "confIAnce")
- Primarily for institutional/hospital context, not small practice

## What's Missing / Market Gaps

### 1. Small Practice Governance Framework

**Gap:** No solution specifically addresses the governance, compliance, and liability clarity needs of a 2-10 physician practice in Geneva considering AI adoption.

**What exists:** Individual tool vendors (scribes, voice assistants) sell features.
**What's missing:** A structured "AI readiness assessment" and "adoption governance framework" for small independent practices — covering nFADP compliance, MedDO classification, consent frameworks, documentation requirements, and human oversight protocols.

### 2. Independent, Vendor-Agnostic Guidance

**Gap:** Physicians have no trusted, independent source to evaluate AI tools against Swiss regulatory requirements.

**What exists:** Vendor marketing materials, some FMH policy documents.
**What's missing:** An independent comparison framework that evaluates AI tools on: data sovereignty (full chain), FADP compliance path, MedDO classification guidance, consent template adequacy, and documentation for liability defense.

### 3. Practical Liability Clarity Service

**Gap:** No service helps physicians understand exactly how to document human oversight of AI in a legally defensible way.

**What exists:** Abstract FMH guidance on "physician remains responsible."
**What's missing:** Practical templates, documentation frameworks, and decision protocols that a physician can actually implement — covering: how to record AI was used, how to record human override, what the consent form should say, and what the patient information obligation is.

### 4. Consent Form Templates for AI

**Gap:** No widely adopted, legally reviewed consent form templates for AI use in Swiss medical practices.

**What exists:** Generic patient consent forms, some vendor-provided language.
**What's missing:** FADP-compliant, MedDO-aware consent form templates that address: AI-assisted documentation, AI as medical device, data transfers to third-party AI providers, and patient rights under nFADP.

### 5. MedDO/Swissmedic Classification Guidance

**Gap:** Small practices don't know whether their AI tool is a medical device requiring registration.

**What exists:** Swissmedic documentation (technical, not practitioner-friendly), voluntary swissdamed registration.
**What's missing:** Simple decision tree for independent practitioners: "Is your AI tool a medical device? If yes, what class? If yes, what are your registration obligations and timeline?"

### 6. Geneva-Cantonal AI Guidance

**Gap:** No Geneva-specific guidance for independent practices on AI adoption within cantonal medical practice regulations.

**What exists:** Federal frameworks (nFADP, FMH guidelines), some Geneva hospital-level policies.
**What's missing:** Practical, cantonal-level guidance for independent practitioners on AI adoption in Geneva private practice context.

## Key Insight

The market is flooded with **tool-level solutions** (scribes, voice assistants, chatbots) but almost entirely lacks **governance-level solutions** (compliance frameworks, liability clarity, consent templates, MedDO classification guidance).

This gap is the entry point for advisory services targeting Geneva independent practices considering AI adoption.
