# Phase 01 Geneva Legal — ICP (Cycle 5)
## Target Vertical: Small Law Firms (2–5 Attorneys), Geneva

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** 2–5 attorneys (small boutique; not solo, not mid-size)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland; ideally near La Vieille Ville, Grottes, or emerging periphérie
- **Structure:** Traditional Swiss law firm; increasingly: attorneys-in-law, paralegals, legal assistants using AI tools without formal governance
- **Revenue context:** CHF 800K–4M annually; billing hourly or via fixed fees; price-sensitive on technology

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary decision-maker:** Managing attorney / partner
- **Secondary:** Senior associates (increasingly driving AI adoption bottom-up)
- **Decision dynamic:** Small firms — partner decides alone; informal consensus with senior staff
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for regulatory exposure; moderate for operational efficiency tools

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Google Workspace or Microsoft 365; case management software (Aristociel, AdNovum, or Excel); AI use is ad hoc and personal-device-driven
- **AI exposure:** Attorneys using ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude for research, drafting, review; often personal accounts, not firm-sanctioned
- **Technology trust:** High for productivity tools; very low for regulatory guidance from AI
- **IT support:** Often none (attorney manages); occasional external IT consultant

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **nFADP compliance** — most small firms are processors of personal client data; unaware of DPIA requirements for AI-assisted case handling
2. **AI-generated legal research** — attorney still accountable for output; no internal review protocol
3. **Client confidentiality + AI tools** — no formal policy on which AI tools can process client matters
4. **FDJP CoE AI Convention implementation** — expected consultation draft end-2026; small firms have no readiness framework
5. **Transparency obligations** — AI-generated work product disclosure to clients not addressed

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Regulatory deadline or peer activity (not marketing material)
- **Language:** Must be in French (Geneva primary market) or bilingual; English-only is disqualifying for older attorneys
- **Trust marker:** Swiss-specific regulatory reference (FDPIC, FDJP, ANBLES) is essential credibility signal
- **Objection:** "This applies to large firms, not us" — dominant misconception; nFADP Art. 5 applies to any AI-based processing
- **Sales cycle:** 4–12 weeks (Geneva professional trust-building); briefing call → diagnostic → proposal
- **Budget:** CHF 1,500–8,000 for initial compliance engagement; retainer for ongoing monitoring
