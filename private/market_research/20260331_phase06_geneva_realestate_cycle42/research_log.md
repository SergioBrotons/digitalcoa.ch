# ICP — Small Geneva Real Estate Agency AI Governance
## Phase 06 — Geneva Real Estate — Cycle 42

---

## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Target:** Small/mid-market real estate agencies in Geneva, 1–20 agents
- **Alternative ICP:** Independent real estate agents / property managers in Geneva
- **NOT:** Large real estate agencies with compliance departments (RE/MAX, Sotheby's, etc.)
- **Geography:** Canton Geneva primary; broader Swiss French-speaking region secondary

### Psychographics
- **Pain:** Using AI tools for listings, contract drafting, tenant screening, property valuations without formal governance; 75% AI adoption HIGHEST in portfolio means the governance gap is widest here; FADP Art. 5(c) already 2.5+ years operative; retroactive compliance unique entry ("govern the AI you already use")
- **Trigger:** Phase 01 C42 70% projected AI adoption increase; Phase 01 C42 90%+ already using AI; FADP obligations already operative since Sep 2023
- **Urgency:** 75% AI adoption = ~47-point governance gap (75% actual vs. 28% formal); widest gap in the entire portfolio; GAIGI institutional 404 creates no public RE AI guidance; retroactive compliance unique framing
- **Gatekeeper:** Agency principal / owner with operational and legal responsibility

### RE-Specific Pain Points
1. **75% AI adoption HIGHEST in portfolio** — governance gap widest: 75% actual AI use vs. 28% formal adoption = ~47-point gap; most RE agencies are using AI without any formal framework
2. **FADP Art. 5(c) 2.5+ years operative** — already in violation for most RE agencies using AI for tenant screening or property valuations; no grace period available
3. **GAIGI institutional 404** — no public RE-specific AI guidance available from GAIGI (gaigi.ch not SMB-accessible); the regulatory guidance gap is structural
4. **Retroactive compliance unique entry** — "govern the AI you already use" is the only honest and compelling framing for RE agencies: they are already using AI, the question is whether to formalize governance now or after an incident
5. **Tenant screening AI** — AI used for tenant selection likely qualifies as automated decision-making under FADP Art. 21 (right to human review required)
6. **Property valuation AI** — AI-assisted property valuations likely high-risk under FADP DPIA requirements

### Decision-maker Motivations
- Protect agency from FDPIC enforcement (already 2.5+ years of operative FADP obligations)
- Protect agency from professional liability (property transactions = high-value decisions)
- Capture efficiency gains (260h/year applicable to listings, contracts, tenant communications, valuations)
- Stay ahead of Federal Council legislation (end-2026 formal deadline)
- Differentiate from competitors (formal AI governance as trust signal to landlords and tenants)
