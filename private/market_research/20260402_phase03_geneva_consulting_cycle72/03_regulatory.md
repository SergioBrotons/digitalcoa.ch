# 03 — Geneva Consulting: Regulatory & Market Framework

## Regulatory Context for Consulting

### Swiss Financial Sector Consulting — Linked to Finance Phase
- FINMA AI Guidance 08/2024: Drives AI consulting demand in financial services
- Stablecoin legislation: Compliance consulting for banks
- Nature transition: Financing advisory (CHF 5.3B/year opportunity)
- These create direct consulting demand for Geneva-based advisory firms

### Data Protection
- Swiss FADP (in force March 1, 2024): GDPR-aligned
- Consulting firms handling client data: Must demonstrate compliance
- AI consulting: Data processing agreements, DPIAs required

### ESG Reporting
- Swiss Sustainability reporting: Increasingly mandatory for large companies
- EU CSRD: Extraterritorial effect on Swiss companies with EU operations
- SBA ESG Self-Regulation: 86% compliance (January 2026)

## Market Access

### Switzerland-EU Consulting Services
- Bilateral I: Suspended/modified
- Bilateral III package (submitted Parliament March 13, 2026): Legal services implications
- Consulting (non-legal): Generally less restricted than legal services
- EU single market: Not accessible; Switzerland operates independently

### Third-Country Consultants
- UK post-Brexit: Separate arrangements; Berne Agreement covers financial services
- US consultants: Common in Geneva; no special restriction
- Cross-border: Digital delivery increasingly common

## Talent & Professional Standards

### Consulting Professional Standards
- Swiss Association of Consultancy Firms: Not publicly accessible this cycle
- ISO certifications: Common for quality consulting firms
- University degrees: UNIGE, EPFL, ETHZ talent pool for Geneva consulting
