### 1. Trump/Iran War Escalation — Market Crash April 2, 2026 (UPDATE)

**Source:** Le Temps Finance, April 2, 2026 — "Les marchés boursiers replongent, après un énième changement de ton de Donald Trump"

**What happened:**
Trump promised to strike Iran "extremely hard" over the next 2-3 weeks, pushing markets sharply lower after a brief recovery. US markets opened down: Nasdaq -1.67%, S&P 500 -1.22%, Dow Jones -1.27%. European markets followed: Frankfurt -2.31%, Paris -1.39%, Milan -1.86%, London -0.51% (oil-linked stocks limited losses).

**Oil spike — historic WTI/Brent inversion:**
- WTI (US reference): $113.35/barrel (+$13.21) — broke through $110
- Brent (North Sea): $109.55/barrel (+$8.19)
- **WTI now trading above Brent for the first time ever** — a historic inversion signaling US supply disruption

**Rate environment:**
- German 10Y: 3.03% (back above 3% symbolic threshold, up from 2.98% prior; +33bps since pre-war ~2.70%; highest since 2011)
- French 10Y: 3.75% (vs 3.67% prior; +55bps since pre-war 3.20%)
- Italian 10Y: 3.95% (vs 3.82% prior; +13bps in one day)
- USD strengthening: 1.15 USD/CHF direction (CHF as safe haven)
- Markets pricing in more restrictive central bank policy

**Analyst views:**
- BlackRock (world's largest asset manager): favoring "electro-tech" (batteries, power electronics, electric motors for AI/energy/defense/infrastructure)
- PIMCO adverse scenario: 3-month Hormuz closure → gradual reopening → oil avg $100/barrel in 2026 → equity correction
- Fitch adverse scenario: Hormuz closed 3 months → oil $100 avg 2026 → stock markets correct
- Trump Iran "stone age" rhetoric produced opposite effect to stated goal — oil up, yields up, equities down

**Significance:** Geneva hedge funds and private banks heavily exposed to equity/volatile asset classes face continued headwinds. CHF safe haven demand sustained. Lombard credits increasingly attractive vs equity risk.

### 5. BNP Paribas Wealth Management Switzerland — Private Banking Trends

**Source:** Le Temps, April 2, 2026 — "Financer la Suisse de demain: comment le crédit devient un atout stratégique pour la clientèle privée et entrepreneuriale"

**Key quotes from Marc H. Schnurrenberger, Head Swiss Domestic Market, BNP Paribas Wealth Management:**

- **On rates/financing:** "After long period of very accommodative monetary conditions, investors pay more attention to financing costs and risk management. They analyze more finely the balance between liquidity, return and investment horizon."
- **On demand:** "Demand remains sustained for certain assets, notably real estate, especially in major economic centers." But investors increasingly selective with long-term vision.
- **On credit as strategic tool:** "Credit is increasingly integrated into wealth strategy — it allows mobilizing financial assets without selling them, accompanying an acquisition, or structuring a transfer to the next generation. Flexibility is essential when investment opportunities must sometimes be seized quickly."
- **On Swiss attractiveness:** "Switzerland remains a reference financial center thanks to its stability and quality of its ecosystem. ... The Arc Lémanique and Zurich region in particular see sustained demand."
- **On CHF:** "With the constant appreciation of the Swiss franc, holding an asset in this currency also represents a real advantage in the private client asset allocation."
- **BNP Paribas scale:** CHF 517B AUM WM globally; AXA IM absorption summer 2025 → 3rd largest European asset manager (€1,600B AUM). Total balance sheet €2,792B.

**Geneva presence:** Geneva, Zurich, Lugano. Focus on Lombard credits + entrepreneurial holdings financing + high-end residential real estate internationally.
