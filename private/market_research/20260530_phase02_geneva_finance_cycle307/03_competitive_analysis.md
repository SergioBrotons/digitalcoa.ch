## Executive Summary

The Geneva boutique wealth management AI governance market has a confirmed first-mover window. No competitor is specifically serving the 50–200 employee boutique WM segment with a Geneva-calibrated proportionate methodology. Enterprise consultancies operate at wrong scale with wrong language. Generic Swiss compliance firms treat AI governance as a subset of data protection. No one is offering cross-document coherence assessment as a discrete service. ## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

### What Exists

**1. Enterprise AI Governance Consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Accenture, Deloitte)**
- Scale: Wrong. Built for banks, insurers, large asset managers.
- Language: Wrong. Framework-heavy, standard-setting, process-optimization.
- Price: Wrong.6-figure engagements for enterprise-scale AI governance programs.
- Relevance to Geneva boutique WM: Near zero. The ICP cannot engage with these firms — not because of budget alone, but because the language and framework assumptions are entirely incompatible with boutique proportionality constraints.
- Positioning: "AI governance maturity assessment," "AI risk framework implementation," "enterprise AI strategy."

**2. Swiss Data Protection Consultancies (Law Firms, Privacy Boutiques)**
- Scale: Right-ish for some. Smaller privacy boutiques are accessible.
- Language: Data protection framing. nFADP/GDPR primary lens.
- Gap: Treat AI governance as subset of data protection. Address ROPA, DPIA, Art.26 but not the AI governance accountability tracks from FINMA Guidance, institutional client questionnaires, or carrier attestation.
- Missing: The specific AI governance accountability architecture beyond data protection compliance.
- Price: CHF 15,000–80,000 for nFADP compliance programs, but focused on data protection, not AI governance in the accountability sense.

**3. Compliance Software Platforms (OneTrust, TrustArc, SAP GRC)**
- Scale: Wrong. Enterprise platforms.
- Product: Policy management, compliance tracking, risk registers.
- Gap: Software assumes a compliance team and a structured governance architecture. Cannot help the boutique that doesn't have either.
- Price: CHF 50,000–500,000 annual licensing for enterprise compliance suites.

**4. Swiss Regulatory Consultancies (Lenz& Staehelin, Walder Wyss, Pestalozzi)**
- Scale: Right for large clients; wrong for boutique WM.
- Language: Legal analysis, circular interpretation, regulatory risk.
- Gap: Advisory on regulatory meaning; not on proportionate implementation for boutique WM. No AI governance methodology product.
- Price: CHF 500–800/hour partner rates; engagement structures designed for large institutions.

**5. SROs (VQF, FINIA)**
- Product: Membership, AML/KYC oversight, licensing.
- Gap: Zero AI guidance. Entirely absent from the AI governance conversation despite being the primary regulatory interface for boutique WM firms.
- irony: The SROs are requiring AI tools for AML/KYC compliance without issuing any AI governance guidance to their members.

**6. Brotons.net (Thought Leadership)**
- Product: Interpretation, thought leadership, signal on AI governance accountability.
- Positioning: Intellectual authority on Swiss AI governance dynamics.
- Gap: Not a service engagement product. Authority platform, not execution platform.

### What's Missing (The Service Opportunities)

**1. Geneva-Calibrated Proportionate AI Governance Methodology**
- No one offers a boutique-specific AI governance methodology proportionate to 50–200 person Geneva WM firm.
- The methodology must: work for a 12-person investment committee, not require a dedicated compliance team, integrate with existing SRO relationship, satisfy FINMA Guidance proportionate framework standard, survive institutional client questionnaire scrutiny, and not contradict carrier attestation representations.
- This is not a small-enterprise version of enterprise AI governance. It is a different architecture.

**2. Carrier Pre-Signing Review**
- No one offers pre-signing review of carrier AI governance attestation language against actual boutique practice.
- This is not a document review. It is a personal liability exposure mapping exercise.
- The question is not "do we have adequate AI governance controls?" — the question is "what does my signature on this specific language actually commit me to, and does my actual practice satisfy those commitments?"
- Market gap: No Geneva-calibrated professional liability counsel offering carrier pre-signing review as a discrete service.

**3. Cross-Document Coherence Assessment**
- No one offers assessment of whether the boutique's FINMA documentation, institutional client questionnaire response, nFADP ROPA/DPIA, and carrier evidence constitute a coherent cross-referenced documentation portfolio.
- This is the unnamed service category. All four documentation streams are describing the same AI governance reality with no cross-reference methodology.
- Market gap: Cross-document AI governance coherence assessment as discrete service category.

**4. Institutional Client Questionnaire Response Preparation**
- No one offers questionnaire response preparation specifically calibrated to the institutional client's comparative use of responses for mandate selection decisions.
- The ICP needs to understand: what commitments are they making in the response, how will those commitments be used in comparative selection, what language creates minimum adequate commitment vs. over-commitment, how to update prior responses when methodology evolves.
- Market gap: Institutional client questionnaire response advisory — not generic AI governance framework, but specific response language calibration for comparative intelligence context.

**5. Vendor Processor Chain Due Diligence**
- No one offers discrete vendor processor chain due diligence targeting the nFADP Art.9 third-party compliance obligations created by AI vendor click-through terms.
- The service: map AI vendors, surface accepted processor chain terms, identify undisclosed sub-processor arrangements, evaluate data processing location claims, produce processor chain disclosure documentation proportionate to boutique risk profile.
- Market gap: Boutique-scale vendor processor chain due diligence.

**6. Portfolio Construction AI Authorization Chain Documentation Architecture**
- No one offers a sequential authorization chain documentation methodology proportionate to boutique investment committee process.
- The service: design documentation architecture that captures AI-to-human authorization moments at each step of portfolio construction, proportionate to boutique committee structure, with evidence standard calibrated to institutional client and carrier requirements.
- Market gap: Boutique-scale authorization chain documentation architecture.
