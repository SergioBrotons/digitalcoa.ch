## ICP Definition

**Target:** Boutique wealth management firm, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable.
**Core framing:** Institutional AI governance questionnaires as competitive gate; carrier attestation; FINMA proportionate framework; SRO silence; nFADP Art.63 personal liability.

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### Primary Language Register

The ICP speaks in **three interlocking registers**:

**Register 1 — Regulatory Exposure Lingua Franca**
- "Has FINMA said anything specific about AI?"
- "We got a questionnaire from a client — 40 pages — I don't know where to start"
- "Our carrier is asking us to attest to AI governance controls we've never documented"
- "I signed something. I wasn't sure what I was signing."
- "Is there a Swiss standard? Who do we point to?"

**Register 2 — Personal Liability Lingua Franca**
- "My name is on the door. I'm responsible."
- "If something goes wrong with a client portfolio and AI was involved — what does that mean for me personally?"
- "We're a small shop. There's no one else to blame."
- "I can't afford to have this be my problem."
- "Someone has to own this. It's always me."

**Register 3 — Proportionality Lingua Franca**
- "We're not Goldman Sachs. We can't hire a team for this."
- "Whatever framework we put in place has to actually work for a 12-person firm."
- "I don't need to solve this for every bank in Switzerland. I need to solve it for us."
- "The solution can't be more complex than the problem."
- "If it requires a committee, it won't happen."

## What Triggers Concern vs. What Drives Action

### Concern Triggers (Anxiety into Action)
- **Client questionnaire arrival** — concrete, time-bound, real
- **Carrier attestation renewal** — personal signature required, undefined adequacy standard
- **Peer event where AI governance comes up** — social proof of problem exists
- **News about institutional client holding managers to AI governance standards**
- **Someone they know received a regulatory inquiry** — near-miss economy

### Action Barriers (Why Action Stalls)
- "I don't know what I don't know" — information asymmetry
- "We can't afford to get this wrong" — high-stakes decision
- "There's no Geneva-specific guidance" — vacuum creates paralysis
- "Whatever we do has to be proportionate" — enterprise solution is wrong answer
- "Who do we trust for this?" — credibility question before content question

## Key Phrases from ICP

### On Client Questionnaires
- "The client sent us a 40-page AI governance questionnaire"
- "We answered it but I can't tell you what we committed to"
- "Our bigger clients are asking questions we don't have answers to"
- "I don't know if we should be forthright or strategic"

### On Carrier Attestation
- "I signed the attestation. I wasn't sure what it meant."
- "The form asked if we have adequate AI governance controls — adequate for what?"
- "We got a renewal reminder. I almost ignored it. Then I thought about what it meant."
- "My broker signed something. What did he commit us to?"

### On FINMA and SROs
- "Has FINMA actually said anything specific? Not principles — actual requirements."
- "Our SRO has been completely silent on AI."
- "The SRO has nothing to say about AI. We pay them to be quiet."
- "FINMA published something in December. I read it. I still don't know what to do."

### On nFADP Personal Liability
- "Article 63 — that's the criminal one, right? How does that apply to me personally?"
- "I found out we should have a ROPA. We don't have a ROPA."
- "We're processing client financial data with AI tools. What does that require of us?"

### On Vendor Due Diligence
- "Our AI vendor terms — I clicked accept. I don't know what I accepted."
- "We're using three or four AI tools. I couldn't tell you what they do with our client data."
- "Is 'Swiss-hosted' actually meaningful?"

### On Human Oversight
- "AI made a recommendation. I approved it. Do I understand what I approved?"
- "If AI is wrong, how do I prove what I knew and when I knew it?"

## Bilingual Notes (EN + FR)

### EN — Primary Register
Geneva boutique wealth managers are internationally oriented; English is comfortable business language for digital and tools content. Most firms have English-language websites and client communications.

### FR — Geneva Register
French is default for regulatory filings, local networking, and Geneva credibility signals. Geneva French register is distinct from generic French — specific terminology calibration matters.

### Phrases Requiring Careful Calibration

| EN | FR | Note |
|---|---|---|
| AI governance readiness | maturité de gouvernance IA | Proportionate; not over-technical |
| Carrier attestation | attestation de l'assureur | Distinct from audit or certification |
| Personal liability | responsabilité personnelle | MD-specific; Article 63 framing |
| Proportionate framework | cadre proportionné | "Approprié à notre taille" |
| AI tool inventory | inventaire des outils IA | Technical but accessible |
| Vendor due diligence | examen des fournisseurs IA | Action-oriented framing |
| Documentation window | fenêtre de documentation | Tactical positioning term |
| FINMA Guidance | Guidance FINMA | BRAND acronym; not "ruling" |

## ICP Emotional State at Point of Contact

**Pre-contact:** Anxious, concrete, time-bound. Has a triggering event (questionnaire, attestation renewal, peer conversation). Needs permission to act, not education about the problem.

**At contact:** Seeking confirmation that someone understands their specific situation. Needs to hear that the problem is named precisely before they'll engage with the solution.

**Post-contact:** Relief that someone has named what they're experiencing. Decision threshold: "Can this person actually help me navigate this, or are they another consultant giving me a framework?"

---

## What Makes the ICP Engage vs. Exit

### Engage
- Problem named with precision — language mirrors their experience
- Regulatory hooks that create urgency without being alarmist
- Proportionality framing built in from first contact
- Clear that someone understands Geneva-specific dynamics
- Bilingual credibility signal (EN + FR)

### Exit
- Generic AI governance framework language
- Enterprise-scale solution framing
- "AI is the future" or efficiency framing
- Vague regulatory language — "may be required" without specificity
- Any suggestion that the problem is optional or distant
