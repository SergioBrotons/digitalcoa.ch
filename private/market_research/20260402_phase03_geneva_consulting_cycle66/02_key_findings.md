### 1. SwissHoldings Legal Talks March 26, 2026 — Legal Certainty as Business Location Factor
**Source:** swissholdings.ch — SwissHoldings Legal Talks, March 26, 2026  
**Participants:** Prof Dr Reto Föllmi (University of St. Gallen), Prof Dr Andreas Bohrer (President SwissHoldings, Lonza Group), National Councillor Patricia von Falkenstein, Dr Ronald Indergand, Dr Stefan Sulzer (Adecco Group), Felix Küng (Head Legal SwissHoldings)  
**Format:** New SwissHoldings event series on legal/regulatory location issues

Key findings from the panel on "Legal Environment as Location Factor":

- **Prof Dr Andreas Bohrer** (SwissHoldings President, Lonza Group): "Clear, reliable, and practical rules that give companies long-term predictability are crucial." — Positions Swiss sector-specific regulatory approach as deliberate competitive strategy
- **Self-regulation endorsed**: "Companies possess the necessary expertise and practical experience to design effective rules while simultaneously securing the freedom required for innovation and competitiveness" — Swiss holding companies prefer self-regulation over government-imposed detailed frameworks
- **Good regulation requires collaboration**: Federal Council, Parliament, and business community must collaborate — SwissHoldings explicitly positions itself as interlocutor
- **Spring Session 2026 confirmed**: Swiss Parliament adopted 2025 Foreign Trade Policy Report, approved GloBE (Global Anti-Base Erosion) multilateral agreement — Swiss multinationals operating in international tax-transparent framework

**Implication for Geneva Consulting Sector:** Geneva consulting firms advising multinationals on Swiss market entry, regulatory positioning, or AI transformation have a concrete positioning angle: Swiss self-regulation model creates competitive advantage for Swiss-domiciled operations. Consultants who can articulate this framework to international clients have a differentiated advisory story.

### 2. "Reducing Bureaucracy Through Digitalization" — AI Adoption Tension
**Source:** SwissHoldings, March 30, 2026 editorial  
**Participants:** Dirk Lindemann, Dr Christian Ulbrich, Jürg Grossen, Dr Stefan Sulzer (Adecco Group), Dr Gabriel Rumo

Key finding: **AI adoption paradox** identified by Swiss business community:

- **"Digitization only brings real benefits if the underlying processes are simplified first. If complex workflows are merely digitized, the result is digital bureaucracy rather than efficiency."** — Dr Stefan Sulzer, Group GC Adecco Group
- **AI potential for reducing bureaucracy is "viewed differently"** — some see significant opportunities, others warn of new complexity and additional regulation
- **Successful AI use requires** not only technological but also **organizational and cultural prerequisites** — acceptance, trust, employee on-boarding in government and business
- **Coordination challenge**: Digital fragmentation between federal government, cantons, and administrative units prevents full digitalization potential

**Implication for Geneva Consulting Sector:** This is the core consulting pitch: process simplification before AI implementation. Geneva consulting firms have a concrete, evidence-backed narrative for why AI transformation projects fail (digital bureaucracy) and what consulting firms can do to prevent it. This is differentiated from generic "AI strategy" consulting.
