## Executive Regulatory Briefing

### For Swiss Corporate Boards (Large Companies, Geneva HQ)

**URGENT — Action Required Before July 9, 2026:**
1. **LGDE 2.0 engagement**: Federal Council opened consultation April 1 on NEW standalone ESG law (separate from existing LGDE counter-proposal). July 9 deadline. Boards of large Swiss companies must review both legislative tracks. EU Omnibus alignment constraint. 20+ year liability horizon. Recommend external counsel review before July deadline.

2. **OECD Pillar Two compliance update**: Corporate tax guide updated April 2 with revised OECD minimum tax chapters. Federal + cantonal (Geneva) implications. Board reporting requirements may have changed. Finance team should review updated guide and assess material changes to Pillar Two exposure.

**STRATEGIC — Q2-Q3 2026:**
3. **Ukraine S-Status HR compliance**: SEM practice change operative since Nov 1, 2025 for western Ukrainian regions. Companies with Ukrainian employees should audit S-status documentation. SEM processing delays likely — HR planning should account for reduced status grant rates.

4. **CMS digital governance review**: Federal Mobile Security Communication System requalified as key project April 1. Federal administration digital transformation underway. Institutional IT governance best practices increasingly relevant for private sector. Boards with federal exposure should review IT governance frameworks.

### For Financial Institutions (Geneva Banks, Wealth Managers, Fintech)

**HIGH PRIORITY:**
1. **ESG compliance stack multiplication**: Two parallel ESG legislative tracks now in pipeline (LGDE counter-proposal + NEW LGDE 2.0). Financial institutions advising clients on ESG compliance face doubled regulatory complexity. Advisory practice capacity should be assessed.

2. **Swiss fintech AI sector shift**: 35% of Swiss fintech now in AI/data analytics (first time exceeds DLT). Banking infrastructure 39%. SaaS dominant model. This changes the regulatory risk profile for fintech investment and partnership decisions.

**STRATEGIC:**
3. **Labor market AI impact**: Admin/commercial roles -20% YoY; IT -18%; financial/marketing specialists -10%. Financial institutions' own workforce planning affected. Also affects client base: corporate clients' financial sector hiring changing.

4. **Inbound M&A pipeline**: 104 record inbound M&A deals (+65%). Foreign PE bolt-ons +156%. Swiss financial institutions as deal advisors or targets should anticipate increased transaction volume and valuation pressure.

### For Swiss Employers (All Sectors)

**IMMEDIATE:**
1. **LSV+/BDD payment migration**: Direct debit discontinuation Sept 30, 2028. Invoice issuers must migrate to alternative formats. 3+ year runway but planning should start now. ERP and treasury system changes required. External consulting support likely needed.

2. **AI-driven workforce planning**: 400,000 worker shortage + AI displacement of admin/IT roles. Working conditions (94% economist consensus) and real wages (90%) as primary solutions. Employer value proposition restructuring becoming urgent.

**STRATEGIC:**
3. **Continuing education investment**: 78% economist support for continuing education as labor shortage solution. Corporate training budget increase likely necessary. OECD + national policy backing this approach.

4. **Women/older worker participation**: 80% economist support for increasing female labor participation; 78% for older workers. HR policy changes recommended for companies facing talent shortages.
