## Core Promise
The AI decisions your consulting firm makes for its own practice are the same decisions your clients will ask you about. And with Swiss AI fintech at 35% of the market and 400,000 labor shortage driving mandatory AI adoption — the questions are coming faster than most firms are prepared for.

---

## Problem Statement (4 layers)

### Layer 1: Your Own AI Governance Gap
Small consulting firms use AI for proposal drafting, market research, project management, and client reporting — often without a governance framework. The Federal AI Ordinance (Jan 2025, operative 15+ months) and FADP apply to your firm. Your clients are starting to ask. A FINMA or FADP review of your clients may ask about your firm's AI practices as part of vendor due diligence.

### Layer 2: Client Advisory Liability — Two Dimensions
When you advise clients on AI adoption, you're exposed on two dimensions: the AI you recommend, and the AI you use in your own delivery. A client implementation failure traced to your recommendation creates liability. "The vendor said it was ready" is not a defense. The Federal AI Ordinance creates new documentation obligations for consulting recommendations.

### Layer 3: Labor Shortage = AI Adoption Is Mandatory for Many SMB Clients
The KOF study (March 2026) confirms: Switzerland faces a structural labor shortage of 400,000 workers over 10 years. For many Geneva SMBs, AI adoption is no longer a strategic choice — it's a survival necessity. This changes the consulting firm's role: you're not advising clients whether to adopt AI, you're advising them how to govern it responsibly under regulatory constraints while competing in a labor-constrained market.

### Layer 4: GPAI August 2, 2026 + G7/H2 2026 Compounding
GPAI Regulation enters force August 2, 2026 (~4 months). G7 Évian (June 2026) and US Section 301 add complexity for Geneva consulting firms with EU or US-facing SMB clients. The combined effect: consulting firms need GPAI compliance capabilities AND awareness of G7/US trade policy spillover effects on SMB client AI decisions.

## The Four-Quadrant Decision Model

| | Low Risk | High Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **High Value** | Automate now (envelope workflows) | Govern then automate (human review + documentation) |
| **Low Value** | Defer (not worth the friction) | Avoid (high risk, low return) |

**Labor shortage update (C57):** The "High Value" column now includes AI adoption that substitutes for labor shortage — even if the AI risk is moderate. The quadrant shifts: high-value AI that addresses labor constraints moves from "govern then automate" toward "prioritize and accelerate."
