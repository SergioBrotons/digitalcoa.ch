## The Four-Quadrant AI Decision Model

Use this framework both for your own firm and for client engagements.

**High Value + Low Risk → Automate now**
These are the "envelope" workflows: internal research, proposal drafting support, data formatting, admin automation. Safe to adopt, high efficiency return. In a labor shortage context, these automations directly reduce dependence on scarce headcount.

**High Value + High Risk → Govern then automate**
AI-assisted client analysis, strategic recommendations, financial projections, compliance reviews. High value, but requires human review checkpoints, FADP compliance assessment, and documentation before deployment. With the labor shortage as backdrop, the urgency to automate high-value work increases — but the governance requirements don't disappear.

**Low Value + Low Risk → Defer**
Low-return AI uses that don't create meaningful value. Don't prioritize these given the complexity of the current environment.

**Low Value + High Risk → Avoid**
High-risk AI uses with low return — facial recognition for office security, AI hiring tools for small teams, AI-driven performance monitoring. These create governance burden without meaningful return. In a labor-constrained market, the opportunity cost of high-risk/low-value AI is particularly acute.

## The GPAI Readiness Checklist

**1. AI Tool Inventory for Client Engagements**
List every AI tool recommended to clients in the past 18 months. For each: vendor, GPAI classification, technical documentation availability.

**2. Downstream Transparency Documentation**
For each AI system in your recommendations: do you have or can you direct clients to the required GPAI transparency information?

**3. Your Own Firm's GPAI Exposure**
Which AI tools does your firm use that qualify as GPAI systems? Do you have the required documentation?

**4. G7/Trade Policy Client Exposure Assessment**
Do any of your SMB clients operate in trade-exposed sectors where G7 Évian outcomes or US Section 301 developments could affect AI decisions? Have you flagged this in your advisory?

**5. Incident Reporting Protocol**
If a client's AI implementation fails or produces a systemic error, do you have a protocol for how that gets reported under GPAI?

**6. Client Contract AI Clauses**
Do your client contracts address AI recommendation liability and the two-dimensional exposure described above?

**7. Staff GPAI Training**
Do your consultants understand what GPAI means for their client work and their own AI tool use? Do they understand the two-dimensional liability framework?

## Two-Dimensional Liability Self-Assessment

**Dimension 1 — Your Recommendation:**
- Have you documented the basis for every AI tool recommendation in the past 18 months?
- Do you have a vendor assessment process that goes beyond vendor claims?
- Do your client contracts address AI recommendation liability?
- Are you prepared to advise clients on GPAI technical documentation requirements?

**Dimension 2 — Your Delivery:**
- Do you have DPA agreements with every AI vendor processing client data?
- Are AI-assisted deliverables reviewed by a human before client delivery?
- Do clients know when AI is used in their project work?
- Is your own firm's AI governance documented well enough to share with prospective clients as a credibility signal?

**Score:**
- Both dimensions addressed + GPAI readiness: You're ahead of the market. Maintain documentation and monitor G7 implications.
- One dimension addressed: Significant gap. Prioritize the uncovered dimension before August 2026.
- Neither dimension addressed: The convergence of labor shortage, GPAI deadline, and regulatory environment makes this urgent.
