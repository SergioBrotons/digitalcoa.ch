# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 06 DigitalCoa.ch (C118)

## Research Question
How should DigitalCoa.ch optimize its advisory business development, client conversion, and retention?

## C118 Key Findings

### Finding 1: Lead Qualification and Scoring Methodology (NET-NEW C118)
- Most inbound inquiries are not qualified prospects — wrong level, wrong problem, wrong budget
- Qualified prospect signals: C-suite, specific regulatory problem, budget awareness, urgency
- Develop explicit ICP and qualify rigorously before investing time
- **Implication:** Filter early, focus time on qualified prospects; systemize qualification process

### Finding 2: Briefing Call Conversion Optimization (NET-NEW C118)
- First call is where relationship is established or lost — highest-leverage moment
- Geneva executives decide on: credibility, relevance, chemistry
- Key: listen more than talk; diagnose before proposing; don't oversell
- **Implication:** Optimize briefing call structure, develop call guide, practice discovery technique

### Finding 3: Case Study and Testimonial Strategy (NET-NEW C118)
- Geneva executive market trusts peer references over credentials
- Case study format: situation → challenge → approach → outcome → client quote
- Request testimonials at engagement conclusion; always ask permission
- Build case study library systematically; publish with explicit approval
- **Implication:** Case studies are credibility engine; systematize collection and use

### Finding 4: Proposal and Contract Structure (NET-NEW C118)
- Geneva market expects: concise, specific, professional proposals
- Standard structure: situation + recommendation + investment + timeline + next steps
- Retainer preferred over project-based; signals ongoing relationship
- Avoid: excessive detail, legal terms, volume discounts
- **Implication:** Standardize proposal template; always offer retainer as preferred option

### Finding 5: Client Onboarding and Kickoff Process (NET-NEW C118)
- First 30 days set relationship tone; structured onboarding reduces friction
- Kickoff meeting: align on scope, timeline, communication protocols, stakeholders
- Monthly check-ins: progress + emerging issues + value delivered
- **Implication:** Develop systematic onboarding process; consistency builds trust

### Finding 6: Expansion and Upsell Pathways (NET-NEW C118)
- Advisory relationships naturally expand: initial mandate → related issues → ongoing counsel
- Quarterly business review: review value delivered + surface new issues
- Referral generation: satisfied clients refer peers — ask for referrals
- Retainer model: expansion through scope increase, not new project billing
- **Implication:** Systematize quarterly review + referral request cadence; grow through value

## Consolidated Strategic Implications
1. **Business development:** Qualify rigorously → focus on real prospects
2. **Conversion:** Briefing call is everything → optimize, practice, refine
3. **Credibility:** Case studies + testimonials → build library systematically
4. **Revenue model:** Retainer > project-based → predictability + alignment
5. **Growth:** Expansion + referrals > new client acquisition → efficiency + trust
