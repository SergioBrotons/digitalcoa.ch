## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### Primary Frustration Pattern

The ICP does not say "I need an AI governance framework."  
They say: **"I don't know what I'm supposed to have done."**

The accountability gap is the core pain. They are personally named. They cannot delegate their way out of it. And the bar, the carrier, and the client are all asking different questions with no Geneva-specific answer available.

## Language Map: EN

### On Attorney-Client Privilege and AI

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "I use AI for case research — but I never send client data to it" | They believe local usage is safe. They have not considered that prompt context may retain or infer. |
| "The tool is internal — it's not like I'm sending files to a third party" | Privacy-safety conflation. No understanding that cloud-based AI processing may constitute disclosure. |
| "I always review AI output before using it" | They believe human review is the risk mitigation. They do not know what documentation proves this. |
| "I can't prove what my AI did vs. didn't do on a specific matter" | Documentation absence. They feel this acutely when clients ask or carriers audit. |
| "What if a client asks me to explain how I used AI on their case?" | Anticipatory privilege anxiety. They know they cannot currently answer this cleanly. |

### On Carrier PI Attestation

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "The carrier asked what AI tools I use. I answered honestly. But I couldn't explain what that actually means for my practice." | Attestation completed without comprehension. A gap exists between what they signed and what they can evidence. |
| "I signed the form. The renewal went through. But I have no idea if I'm actually covered." | Post-attestation doubt. The form created a record, not a defense. |
| "The carrier wants documentation I don't have" | Silent documentation debt revealed by carrier inquiry. |

### On Bar Guidance Vacuum

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "I asked the FSA — they said to follow best practices. What does that mean for a 3-person firm?" | Abstract guidance creates more uncertainty, not less. |
| "There's no Geneva standard for this. I could be doing everything right or everything wrong." | Standards vacuum creates personal exposure. Absence of definition feels like risk. |
| "I don't know what 'adequate' AI governance means for my size of firm" | Scale-specific guidance gap. Generic frameworks don't map to boutique operations. |
| "I keep waiting for the bar to tell us what to do — but they haven't" | Passive regulatory waiting. Meanwhile exposure accumulates. |

### On Personal Accountability

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "It's on me. If something goes wrong, I'm the one with the problem." | Managing partner personal liability awareness. Not abstract — felt. |
| "I can't hide behind the firm. The carrier will come after me personally." | Personal exposure clarity. The firm structure does not protect the decision-maker. |
| "I didn't deploy AI to create liability — but I also can't ignore it anymore" | Efficiency pressure vs. liability fear. Both real. Neither resolved. |

### On Documentation Debt

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "I didn't realize I was supposed to be documenting this" | Documentation obligation was never made explicit to them. |
| "We don't have anything written down about how we use AI" | Systemic documentation absence across the practice. |
| "If someone asked me to show our AI workflow for a specific case — I couldn't" | Cannot produce evidence. Privilege risk multiplied by documentation absence. |

### On Vendor Contracts

| What they actually say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "We clicked through the vendor agreement — like everyone does" | Contract review is not happening. Terms accepted under operational pressure. |
| "The vendor's terms say they're not responsible if something goes wrong" | Indemnification gap identified but not acted upon. |
| "I don't know if the vendor contract is compatible with Swiss bar rules" | Vendor contract compliance question unanswered, creating silent risk. |

## Language Map: FR

### Sur le secret professionnel et l'IA

| Ce qu'ils disent réellement | Ce qu'ils veulent dire |
|---|---|
| "J'utilise l'IA pour la recherche — mais je n'envoie jamais de données clients" | Ils croient que l'usage local est sécurisé. Ils n'ont pas considéré que le contexte d'une requête peut conserver ou inférer des informations. |
| "L'outil est interne — ce n'est pas comme si j'envoyais des fichiers à un tiers" | Confusion entre confidentialité et sécurité. Ils ne savent pas que le traitement par IA en nuage peut constituer une divulgation. |

## Key Language Findings

1. **"Internal" is the safety word** — ICP believes local/cloud distinction is the privacy boundary. It is not.
2. **"I reviewed it" is the risk mitigation belief** — but it cannot be evidenced without documentation.
3. **"Best practices" is the frustrating non-answer** — from bar, from carriers, from vendors.
4. **"I'm the one with the problem"** — personal accountability is felt, not just known.
5. **"I couldn't show you our AI process"** — documentation debt is systemic, not partial.
6. **"The vendor's terms say they're not responsible"** — indemnification gap is known but not acted upon.
