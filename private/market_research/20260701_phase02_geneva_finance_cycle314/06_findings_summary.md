### Finding 01: Article 3 Deployer Status — Geographic Test by Affected Person, Not by Location

**The finding:** The Geneva boutique WM has been operating on the assumption that the EU AI Act applies to EU-located firms. It does not. Article 3 defines a deployer as "a natural or legal person using an AI system under its authority in a professional capacity." The geographic test is the location of the affected person, not the location of the deployer. A Geneva boutique WM that uses AI portfolio construction on an EU pension fund's mandate is a deployer under Article 3 — by virtue of the EU pension fund being the affected person, not by virtue of the boutique being located in Geneva. The Geneva boutique WM with EU-domiciled institutional clients (pension funds, insurance groups, sovereign wealth funds, Landesbanken) is a deployer for every AI use on those mandates. The exposure is named. The deadline is in late August 2026. The MD does not know this. The carrier attestation does not address it. nFADP Art.63 does not address it. The EU AI Act addresses it — and the MD has not read it.

**Mechanism:** Geneva boutique WM serves EU institutional client → uses AI for portfolio construction / mandate compliance / client reporting on EU-affected mandate → AI use affects EU natural persons (institutional client's investment committee beneficiaries) → boutique meets EU AI Act deployer definition → Article 3 applicability triggered → Article 4, 14, 26, 50 obligations apply → late August 2026 deadline creates enforcement exposure → EU institutional client asks for documented readiness → boutique cannot produce applicability memo.

**Implication:** Article 3 deployer applicability memo is a discrete, named C314 P02 service. It audits whether the Geneva boutique WM is a deployer under Article 3, identifies which mandates create applicability, classifies each AI use by Article 6(3) high-risk analysis (narrow procedural task, improves previously completed human activity, detects decision-making patterns, preparatory task), and produces a Geneva-specific applicability memo the MD can attach to the EU institutional client readiness questionnaire response, the carrier attestation, and the FDPIC filing. The first Geneva boutique WM service to publish the Article 3 deployer applicability memo template owns the C314 P02 EU institutional client retention category. The applicability memo is the answer to the EU institutional client's first question.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You serve EU institutional clients. You use AI on their mandates. Article 3 of the EU AI Act defines deployer by use, not by location. You are the deployer. The deadline is in 30 days. I'm producing your applicability memo — which mandates are in scope, which AI uses are high-risk — before August."

### Finding 04: Sub-Processor Disclosure Asymmetry — The Swiss-Hosted Fallacy Applied to Portfolio Construction

**The finding:** The Geneva boutique WM that selected a "Swiss-hosted" portfolio construction AI vendor on nFADP Art.9 residency grounds in 2024 has a documented misconception. nFADP Art.9 is not satisfied by data residency alone. The Swiss-hosted vendor's sub-processors (logging, telemetry, authentication, monitoring) operate extraterritorially. The vendor's parent-company cloud infrastructure (when non-Swiss) inherits CLOUD Act extraterritorial access. Mandate metadata — query classifications, mandate identifiers, model parameters, AI conversation history with portfolio managers — routes through infrastructure that doesn't honor Swiss borders. The EU AI Act Article 10 (data governance) requires the boutique to demonstrate that input data is relevant and sufficiently representative and free of errors. The carrier attestation asks for "AI training data audit." The EU institutional client's mid-year questionnaire now asks for sub-processor disclosure. The three regimes converge on the boutique's documentation of what the portfolio construction vendor's data flow actually is — not what the vendor's marketing claims. The boutique's prior DDQ response said "Swiss-hosted." The current sub-processor reality contradicts the DDQ response. The contradiction is now visible in the EU institutional client's procurement file.

**Mechanism:** Boutique selected Swiss-hosted portfolio construction vendor in 2024 on Art.9 grounds → prior DDQ response said "Swiss-hosted" → EU institutional client mid-year questionnaire asks for sub-processor disclosure → vendor's terms disclose parent-company U.S. cloud → boutique discloses contradiction in current response → institutional client reads contradiction → boutique's documented misconception is documented → EU AI Act Article 10 data governance review triggered → FINMA Circular 2026/03 §7.4 AI system inventory review triggered → carrier attestation inaccuracy finding → Art.9 review triggered.

### Finding 06: FINMA Circular 2026/03 Section 7.4 — The FINMA-Specific AI Governance Layer No One Has Read

Portfolio construction AI, mandate compliance AI, and client reporting AI used by Geneva WM boutiques typically meet the materiality threshold. The Section 7.4 requirements include: AI system inventory, named deployer accountability, human oversight documentation, and incident response protocol. The EU AI Act readiness questionnaire does not mention FINMA directly. The four regimes do not coordinate. The boutique WM operates in scope of FINMA Circular 2026/03 Section 7.4 and has not begun compliance documentation.
