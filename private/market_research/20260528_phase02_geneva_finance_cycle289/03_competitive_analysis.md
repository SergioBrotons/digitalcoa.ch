# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management — AI Accountability

**Phase:** C289 Phase 02
**ICP:** Boutique wealth management firms, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based
**Role:** Managing director or partner, personally accountable
**Date:** 2026-05-28

---

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

---

### Category 1: AI Tools for Wealth Management

**What's available:**

| Type | Examples | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Portfolio management AI | BlackRock Aladdin, Franklin Templeton AI tools, boutique fintechs | Institutional-grade, not WM boutique-scale. High cost, high complexity. |
| Research synthesis | Bloomberg AI, FINANCIAL TIMES AI, niche tools | No nFADP/FINMA accountability framing. Output not auditable by MD. |
| CRM AI | Salesforce Einstein, HubSpot AI | Data handling opaque. Vendor liability disclaimers. |
| Risk analytics | Third-party risk platforms | Explainability gap — can't show to FINMA why model flagged X |
| Document processing | AI tools for contract/due diligence review | Client data goes to third party — Art. 63 exposure |

**What's missing:**
- No tool packages itself as "MD liability-aware" for Swiss boutique WMs
- No vendor offers contractual liability sharing or clear Art. 63 responsibility chain
- No tool provides audit trail designed for FINMA scrutiny

---

### Category 2: Compliance / Governance Consultancies

**What's available:**

| Provider | What's offered | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Big 4 (PwC, KPMG, Deloitte, EY) | AI governance frameworks, regulatory gap analysis | Designed for large institutions, not boutiques. Expensive. Generic. |
| Swiss law firms | nFADP compliance reviews, regulatory advice | Issue legal opinions, not operational governance systems |
| Compliance consultants | Data protection officer services | Not AI governance specialists. nFADP focus, not broader FINMA |
| IT security firms | Cybersecurity audits | Technical security, not algorithmic accountability |

**What's missing:**
- No consultancy offers a Swiss-specific AI governance framework for boutique wealth managers
- No one provides a "done-for-you" policy that survives client AI DDQ scrutiny
- No service combines FINMA expectations + nFADP Art.63 + carrier attestation into one practical deliverable

---

### Category 3: Industry Association Guidance

**What's available:**

| Association | Guidance issued | Adequacy for ICP |
|---|---|---|
| FINMA | 2024 guidance on ethical AI use (general) | No specific WM guidance. No checklist. No audit standard. |
| ASG | General AI statement | Two pages of principles. Not actionable. |
| SFAMA | General AI statement | Same — principles without practice |
| SRO (Asset Management) | Silence | No guidance exists |
| FINMA | Circular on Model Risk (existing) | Only applies to banks explicitly. Wealth managers not sure if it applies. |

**What's missing:**
- FINMA WM-specific AI circular
- Practical checklist for AI deployment in WM
- MD accountability guidance (who is responsible for what)
- Art. 63 nFADP liability framework for AI deployment

---

### Category 4: Training / Certification

**What's available:**

- Generic AI courses (Coursera, edX)
- FINMA-approved compliance courses (existing topics, not AI)
- Industry events (fund manager conferences, private banking events) — occasional AI panels

**What's missing:**
- No Swiss-certified AI governance training for wealth management MDs
- No peer-based learning (Geneva MDs sharing what they did)
- No practical "how to respond to institutional AI DDQ" workshop

---

### Category 5: Market Positioning Gap (What Nobody Owns)

**The gap nobody is filling:**

The Geneva boutique WM MD needs someone who can:

1. **Give them a written AI governance policy** — structured, auditor-ready, client DDQ-defensible
2. **Show them exactly what FINMA would ask** — practical, not theoretical
3. **Address Art. 63 nFADP personally** — not as a footnote but as a core concern
4. **Help them respond to institutional client AI questionnaires** — with templates and defensible answers
5. **Create the carrier attestation package** — that they can actually stand behind
6. **Do it at boutique scale** — without Big 4 pricing

**Nobody is marketing this specifically to Geneva boutique WMs.**

The market has:
- Big 4: expensive, institutional, generic
- Legal firms: legal opinions, not operational systems
- Vendors: product sales, liability disclaimers
- SROs: silence

**Opportunity:** Own the "MD accountability" positioning for Swiss boutique wealth management AI governance.

## NET-NEW Findings (C289 Phase 02)

1. **No "MD liability-aware" AI governance product exists** — Vendors market features; none market liability protection for the managing director.

2. **Big 4 are structurally wrong for boutiques** — Pricing, delivery format, and scope all designed for institutions, not 50–200 person Geneva firms.

3. **SRO silence has created a vacuum** — ASG/SFAMA statements are too generic to be operationally useful. Boutique WMs have no reference standard.

4. **Institutional AI DDQ responses areDIY** — Firms are writing responses manually in Word. No templates, no frameworks, no professional support.

5. **Carrier attestation is unanchored** — No standard exists for what "adequate AI governance controls" means. The market has no reference product.

6. **The compliance consultant market has a boutique WM gap** — No Swiss consultancy specifically serves the boutique WM MD's AI governance problem at appropriate scale and price point.
