# 06 — Executive Summary
## Phase 04 Geneva NGO/Tech — C130

**Market Opportunity:**
Geneva NGO/Tech advisory is being shaped by five structural forces: (1) cleantech Series A financing gap creating advisory demand for investor positioning, (2) ICRC IT modernization budget creating vendor qualification advisory opportunity, (3) WIPO AI platform adoption creating new IP disclosure compliance sub-discipline, (4) Council of Europe AI Convention implementation requiring AI governance frameworks at 15+ international organizations, and (5) Art. 6 carbon credit trading creating corresponding adjustment compliance demand.

**Key Findings C130:**

1. Cleantech Series A financing gap: CHF 180-250M annual gap; 4-6 active Geneva VCs vs. 40-60 companies needing funding; seed extension vs. international round trade-off; advisory opportunity: Series A readiness diagnostics + investor positioning
2. ICRC IT modernization: CHF 180-240M budget (2025-2027); offline-capable, data sovereignty, field access requirements; preference for Geneva-based vendors; advisory opportunity: ICRC procurement qualification advisory
3. WIPO AI platform + IP disclosure: 40% adoption (Q1 2026); AI-assisted drafting disclosure question in patent applications; cost allocation issues; IP defensibility questions; advisory opportunity: AI disclosure compliance + IP portfolio strategy
4. Council of Europe AI Convention: Implementation end-2026; human rights impact assessment for high-risk AI; 15+ major international organizations in Geneva affected; advisory opportunity: AI governance framework development
5. Swissmedic SaMD + EU MDR dual-track: Revised MedDO 2025 implementation; aligns with EU MDR Annex XVI; 35+ Geneva healthtech companies; dual-track compliance complexity; advisory opportunity: dual-track compliance strategy
6. Art. 6 carbon credit compliance: Corresponding adjustment requirements established at COP28/COP29; Geneva commodity trading houses trading Art. 6-compliant credits; internal compliance frameworks underdeveloped; advisory opportunity: Art. 6 compliance framework

**ICP Summary:**
Geneva cleantech CEOs/boards (Series A stage); ICRC/humanitarian vendors; international organization CIOs; Geneva commodity trading compliance heads; Geneva healthtech regulatory affairs directors. Pain: financing gaps, procurement qualification, AI governance, regulatory compliance complexity. Urgency: AI Convention end-2026 deadline, ICRC budget deployment, Swissmedic pathway clarity.

**Competitive Position:**
Independent advisory at intersection of Geneva cleantech ecosystem + humanitarian tech + international organization AI governance + Art. 6 carbon compliance. No single advisor offering this specific combination.

**Recommended Action:**
Position Series A readiness advisory as priority offering for Geneva cleantech. Pursue ICRC vendor qualification advisory as secondary. Develop AI governance framework offering for international organizations (requires relationship-building).
