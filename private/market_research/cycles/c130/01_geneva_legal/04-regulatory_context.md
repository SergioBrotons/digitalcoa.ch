# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal Advisory

## Key Regulatory Frameworks

### 1. FINMA Supervision Architecture
- **FINMA** — direct supervision of banks, insurers, exchanges; delegates AML/organizational to SROs
- **SROs** (AOOS, SRO-Swiss Banking, Versicherungs-SRO) — conduct delegated FINMA audits
- **VERMAG** — assets under management audit, independent from FINMA
- **Dual-layer burden** — financial intermediaries must comply with both SRO and VERMAG requirements
- **Implication:** VERMAG+SRO advisory is a distinct niche for Geneva asset managers

### 2. Data Protection: nFADP (revised FADP)
- **In force:** March 2024
- **Art. 15** — right of access (mirrors GDPR Art. 15)
- **Art. 17** — right to deletion (mirrors GDPR Art. 17)
- **Cross-border enforcement gap** — unenforceable against foreign controllers without adequacy decision
- **EU adequacy talks** — stalled since 2021; conclusion would create enforcement reciprocity
- **FDPIC role** — mediation only, no penalty authority over foreign controllers
- **Implication:** Swiss entities with EU data flows need cross-border compliance mapping

### 3. Professional Responsibility: OGE/RAV AI Guidance
- **OGE** (Swiss Bar Association) — issued professional responsibility guidance for AI use by lawyers
- **RAV** (Geneva Bar Association) — Geneva-specific implementation
- **Key issue:** Lawyers remain fully liable for AI errors regardless of tool used
- **Advisory implication:** AI deployment governance for law firms and in-house legal departments

### 4. Attorney Trust Accounts: AML Intersection
- **Art. 12 OGE** — dedicated attorney trust accounts required; interest → OGE solidarity fund
- **Art. 138 SCO** — criminal misappropriation liability
- **MROS reporting** — post-2022, attorneys with suspicious client transactions face AML reporting
- **Tension:** Bar confidentiality vs. AML reporting obligation
- **Implication:** Geneva attorney AML compliance advisory

### 5. Swiss SPV / Structured Finance
- **OR Art. 284** — avoidance actions in bankruptcy
- **No statutory bankruptcy-remote vehicle** — unlike Delaware, Cayman
- **True-sale opinions** — standard market practice but opinion-dependent
- **2024 CHF 200M+ ABS case** — tested OR Art. 284; true-sale documentation gaps = primary vulnerability
- **Implication:** Independent SPV opinion work for structured finance transactions

### 6. Geneva Commercial Court (CCIG)
- **Cases:** 400-600/year; 8-12 month resolution
- **Geneva Justiz portal** — mandatory digital filing since 2022
- **Bilingual (FR/DE)** — procedural translation creates cost burden for small-cap parties
- **Implication:** SME commercial dispute advisory; procedural efficiency consulting

### 7. International Arbitration
- **ICC 2026 Rules** — go-live June 1, 2026; expedited procedures + ESG + AI disclosure
- **Swiss Arbitration Centre** — TEF Rules (Trust, Estate, Foundation disputes) since July 2025
- **ICC record caseload 2025** — 894 new cases, 1,869 ongoing; geopolitics + tariffs driving 2026 surge
- **Geneva neutrality** — preferred seat for cross-border disputes
- **Implication:** Geneva arbitration advisory as growing service line

### 8. Switzerland-EU Bilateral Framework
- **Bilateral III negotiations** — Q2 2026 critical phase
- **Market access + data adequacy** — key negotiation items
- **Contingency advisory demand** — companies preparing for various outcomes
- **Implication:** Institutional agreement compliance and contingency planning advisory
