# 06 — Executive Summary
## Phase 05 Brotons.net — C130

**Platform Opportunity:**
Brotons.net content and platform strategy is being shaped by five opportunities: (1) Google Helpful Content Update rewarding Digital Garden approach with 15-20% featured snippet capture, (2) Geneva institutional event calendar creating predictable 3-7x traffic surge windows underutilized by EN-language publishers, (3) identified newsletter-to-website 2nd-page drop-off (68% → 23%) with specific fix identified, (4) LinkedIn algorithm headwinds requiring dual-format testing, and (5) FR-native audience preference for EN regulatory precision creating bilingual efficiency opportunity.

**Key Findings C130:**

1. Digital Garden outperforms traditional blog: Google Helpful Content Update rewards depth + author expertise + iterative development; 15-20% featured snippet capture for evergreen pieces vs. lower for traditional posts; strategic recommendation: fewer but deeper evergreen pieces
2. Geneva institutional calendar underutilized: 3-7x baseline search traffic in 2-week event windows; no systematic pre-event briefing content in EN-language; Brotons.net could capture surge with aligned content
3. Newsletter-to-website drop-off identified: 68% click-through but only 23% 2nd-page engagement; gap: no contextual "related content" pathway at article level; fix: topic cluster tagging + contextual cross-links
4. LinkedIn algorithm headwinds: 2025 algorithm deprioritizes external traffic-driving content; "engagement arbitrage" characterization; test dual format: native LinkedIn newsletter + long-form website canonical
5. FR-native audience EN content preference: 12-15% of FR-native subscribers prefer EN regulatory content for precision; full FR translation resource-intensive; efficiency play: EN long-form + FR executive summaries (1-page)
6. Newsletter re-engagement gap: 31% 90-day re-engagement rate for dormant subscribers; quarterly "best of" curation targeting dormant subscribers could recover 8-12%

**Platform Position:**
Independent, authoritative English-language Geneva thought leadership with Switzerland-EU institutional bridge differentiation. Clear white space vs. FR-native Geneva media and international general business media.

**Recommended Actions:**
1. Accelerate Digital Garden approach; fewer but deeper evergreen pieces
2. Map Geneva institutional event calendar; create pre-event content alignment
3. Implement topic cluster tagging with contextual cross-links at article end
4. Test dual format: native LinkedIn newsletter + long-form website canonical
5. Test EN long-form + FR executive summary format

**Skipped:**
Publishing synthesis pieces (owner approval), "Geneva Intelligence" brand positioning (owner decision), Brotons.net analytics access (owner approval), CCIG partnership (owner approval), LinkedIn newsletter strategy revision (owner approval), Medium syndication (owner approval), web_search (Gemini 429 quota)
