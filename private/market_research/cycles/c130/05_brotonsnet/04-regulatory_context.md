# 04 — Regulatory Context: Brotons.net Content

## Content Regulatory Framework

### 1. Google Search + Helpful Content Update
- **Helpful Content Update (2023+)** — rewards content demonstrating depth, author expertise, iterative development
- **2024-2025 signals** — continues to favor evergreen, well-developed content
- **Featured snippet performance** — 15-20% of tracked search terms for Digital Garden pieces
- **Implication:** Accelerate Digital Garden approach; fewer but deeper evergreen pieces

### 2. LinkedIn Algorithm Policy
- **2025 algorithm change** — deprioritizes content driving external traffic ("engagement arbitrage")
- **Newsletter format impact** — links to website articles penalized relative to native content
- **Strategic response:** Dual format testing — native LinkedIn content + long-form website canonical
- **Implication:** Test LinkedIn native newsletter vs. website article approach

### 3. Swiss Data Protection (FADP/nFADP)
- **Email subscription data** — subject to nFADP requirements
- **Consent requirements** — clear consent for newsletter distribution
- **Data retention** — documented retention policies required
- **Implication:** Ensure FADP compliance for email list management

### 4. EU AI Act (for Content)
- **AI-generated content disclosure** — if using AI in content creation
- **Transparency requirements** — audience should understand content authorship
- **Implication:** Transparent authorship policy; AI tool disclosure if used

### 5. Swiss Media Law
- **Editorial independence** — Swiss media law protections
- **Impressum requirements** — mandatory publisher disclosure
- **Implication:** Maintain editorial independence documentation; impressum compliance
