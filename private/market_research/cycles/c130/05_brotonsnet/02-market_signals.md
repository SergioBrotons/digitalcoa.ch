# 02 — Market Signals: Brotons.net Content Strategy

## Content Market Overview

### Geneva Thought Leadership Landscape
- **Competitors:** Geneva Business News, Le Temps, St. Galler Tagblatt, Swissinfo
- **International:** Financial Times, The Economist, Harvard Business Review
- **Niche:** Geneva Policy Brief, Geneva Observer, International Law Office
- **Gap:** English-language Geneva-specific thought leadership with depth
- **Brotons.net position:** Independent, authoritative, Geneva-specific

### Key Market Signals

#### 1. Google Helpful Content Update + Digital Garden Performance
- 2023+ Helpful Content Update favors depth + author expertise + iterative development
- Brotons.net Digital Garden pieces capturing 15-20% featured snippet positions
- Traditional blog posts underperforming relative to evergreen pieces
- **Implication:** Accelerate Digital Garden approach; fewer but deeper pieces

#### 2. Event-Driven Traffic Patterns
- Geneva institutional events: 3-7x baseline search traffic in 2-week event windows
- ITU council sessions, WTO MC13, WHO EB, ICRC donor meetings, UN Geneva calendar
- Current gap: no systematic pre-event briefing content
- **Implication:** Align content calendar with Geneva institutional event calendar

#### 3. Newsletter-to-Website Drop-off
- 68% newsletter click-through to website
- Only 23% explore a second page (identified drop-off)
- Gap: no contextual "related content" pathway at article level
- **Implication:** Topic cluster tagging + contextual cross-links at article end

#### 4. LinkedIn Algorithm Headwinds
- LinkedIn 2025 algorithm deprioritizing external traffic-driving newsletter content
- "Engagement arbitrage" characterization by LinkedIn content team
- Current format: newsletter links to website articles
- **Implication:** Test "dual format" — native LinkedIn newsletter vs. long-form website canonical

#### 5. FR-Native Audience EN Content Preference
- 12-15% of FR-native subscribers prefer EN content for regulatory/complex topics
- EN regulatory terminology perceived as more precise
- Full FR translation resource-intensive
- **Implication:** EN long-form + French executive summaries (1-page FR summary) efficiency play

#### 6. Newsletter Re-Engagement Gap
- 90-day re-engagement rate for dormant subscribers: 31%
- Passive disengagement significant
- Quarterly "best of" curation emails targeting dormant subscribers
- Comparable publisher A/B tests: 8-12% dormant recovery rate
- **Implication:** Separate content curation workflow for re-activation
