# 03 — Competitive Landscape: Geneva Consulting

## Market Structure

### Tier 1: Global Strategy/Management Consulting
- **McKinsey, BCG, Bain** — strategy; rarely engaged by SMEs
- **Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG** — multi-service; large engagements; conflict perception
- **Strengths:** Brand, resources, global reach
- **Weaknesses:** Not independent, high cost, conflict-heavy, slow for SME segment

### Tier 2: Swiss-National Consulting Firms
- **Advantage** — Swiss focus, institutional relationships
- **treland.group, BDO Consulting** — mid-market specialist
- **Strengths:** Swiss regulatory depth, local relationships
- **Weaknesses:** Limited OT security/board governance niche expertise

### Tier 3: Boutiques and Specialists
- **OT security boutiques** — emerging, small, technical focus
- **Board governance specialists** — executive search + governance consulting
- **Supply chain due diligence specialists** — compliance boutique
- **Strengths:** Deep niche, independent
- **Weaknesses:** Narrow breadth, limited scale

### Tier 4: Independent Advisory (DigitalCoa.ch positioning)
- **Positioning:** Independent regulatory + operational consulting
- **Differentiation:** Board governance readiness + OT security gap + supply chain due diligence + intercantonal tax
- **Gap:** No independent Geneva advisor combining board governance + OT security + supply chain compliance

## Competitive White Space

1. **OT security gap assessment** — IEC 62443 for Geneva precision manufacturing; OT security boutiques are technical but lack regulatory integration
2. **Board governance readiness (40% diversity + Art. 716a)** — executive search firms focus on placement, not governance readiness assessment
3. **Intercantonal tax allocation** — tax firms handle disputes; compliance advisory pre-dispute is underserved
4. **Supply chain due diligence (OR Art. 964j-k)** — compliance firms handling documentation; end-to-end advisory gap
5. **UN agency consulting** — relationship-dependent; requires UNGP expertise
