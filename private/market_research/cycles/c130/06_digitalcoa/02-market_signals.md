# 02 — Market Signals: DigitalCoa.ch Service Strategy

## Advisory Market Overview

### Geneva Boutique Advisory Landscape
- **Positioning:** Independent, conflict-free regulatory and management advisory
- **Competitors:** Big 4 consulting, Swiss national firms, international law firms, boutique specialists
- **Differentiation:** Switzerland-EU institutional bridge, Geneva-specific intelligence, independent voice
- **Pricing:** CHF 2,800-4,500/day; project or retainer model

### Key Market Signals

#### 1. 72-Hour Follow-Up Gap
- 68% of Geneva executives intended to follow up within 72 hours but did not
- Decision paralysis, competing priorities, lack of structured next-step
- Current ad hoc email follow-up insufficient
- **3-touchpoint sequence over 10 business days** could recover 15-20% of lost follow-through
- **Implication:** Post-briefing-call follow-up sequence is high-ROI operational improvement

#### 2. Remote Advisory Quality Standards
- Remote sessions with unclear quality standards generate higher satisfaction variance
- Geneva executives accustomed to in-person relationship-based service
- Specific quality standards that reduce variance by 40%:
  - Session preparation documented with specific client references
  - Session recording available within 24 hours
  - Written follow-up within 48 hours
  - Same-day email response during engagement
  - Senior advisor present (not delegated)
- **Implication:** Communicate quality standards upfront in engagement confirmation

#### 3. Reciprocity Gap and Referral Timing
- 78% of executives who consumed valuable content but did not engage still referred others within 12 months
- Referral incentive: providing value to peers, not expectation of reciprocal business
- Formal referral request at 12-month post-content mark more effective than proximate-to-engagement
- **Geneva Executive Referral Circle** concept: quarterly informal gathering
- **Implication:** 12-month referral timing; referral circle systematizes reciprocity

#### 4. Competence Anxiety Re: AI
- Proliferation of AI tools → executives skeptical of advisors who cannot articulate AI-differentiation
- Prolonged evaluation phase: 3-5 briefing calls before engagement decision
- Competence signals to front-load:
  - Geneva-specific regulatory relationships and information access
  - Demonstrated judgment on ambiguous regulatory questions
  - Accountability positioning (independent, not selling products)
- **Implication:** Front-load competence signals in briefing call confirmation and opening

#### 5. NPS Gratitude Bias
- NPS inflated by 8-15 points due to gratitude bias in boutique advisory
- Personal relationship feeling elevates scores regardless of objective quality
- More reliable metric: **6-month engagement extension rate**
- Extension rate correlates better with actual client value perception
- **Implication:** Use engagement extension rate as primary quality metric

#### 6. Brotons.net Trust-Continuity
- 4+ Brotons.net pieces over 90 days → 34% higher briefing call conversion
- Trust-continuity effect diminishes with direct/transactional transition
- Content-embedded transition optimal:
  - Brotons.net piece addressing specific pain point
  - Embedded reference to DigitalCoa.ch capability
  - Reader self-identifies need → natural booking
- Hard-sell transitions reduce trust-continuity benefit by ~40%
- **Implication:** Optimize content-embedded pathway; avoid hard-sell transitions
