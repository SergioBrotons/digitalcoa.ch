# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Healthcare ICP Talks About AI Problems

## Phase 04 C304 | Geneva Healthcare ICP | Independent Physician / Small Group Practice (2–10 physicians)

---

## Purpose
This document captures how the ICP actually talks about AI problems — in their words, their framing, their sequence of concerns. Not how we talk about them. This language is the raw material for all downstream content.

---

## The Primary Frame: Personal License Accountability

The defining characteristic of Geneva healthcare ICP language is personal stakes language. Every concern expressed by this ICP is ultimately framed in terms of personal professional liability — the physician's license, the physician's name on the record, the physician's signature on the attestation.

**Dominant language pattern:**
- "My license is on the line" — not "the practice is exposed"
- "I signed it" — carrier attestation is a personal signature event
- "I can't explain what the AI did" — records bearing the physician's name with AI content the physician cannot reconstruct
- "If something goes wrong" — adverse outcome framing, always personal

**Secondary language pattern — efficiency necessity:**
- "I can't run the practice without AI" — staffing shortage, administrative load
- "My staff uses it" — delegation is real, personal accountability for staff use is not yet fully named
- "The EHR has AI features now" — AI arrived through system updates, not through governance decision

**Tertiary language pattern — regulatory confusion:**
- "I don't know what Swissdamed requires" — classification determination paralysis
- "FMH says oversight but doesn't say what that means" — operational emptiness of principle-only guidance
- "The carrier wants evidence — I don't know what evidence" — attestation evidence gap

## The Concern Sequence (How They Arrive at the Problem)

### Entry Point: Carrier Attestation Arrives
The sequence always starts with something arriving from outside the practice:
1. Carrier sends AI governance questionnaire at renewal
2. Patient arrives with consumer AI-generated health analysis
3. EHR system update activates new AI features
4. Staff member starts using AI tool without telling the physician
5. cantonal medical association issues guidance requiring documentation

**Key language:** "I got a form from my carrier. It asks about AI governance. I don't know what it's asking for."

### The Personal Signature Moment
The physician realizes the attestation requires their personal signature — not the practice manager, not the administrator. The physician.

**Key language:** "It needs my signature. Not the practice manager. Mine."

### The Evidence Gap Realization
The physician realizes they cannot produce the evidence the attestation represents they have.

**Key language:** "I don't have anything documented about AI. I use it every day but I haven't documented anything."

### The Cascade Discovery
Once the physician begins examining the AI surface of the practice, the cascade begins:
- "My EHR has AI features I didn't activate"
- "My staff are using ChatGPT for patient communications"
- "I don't know what version of the AI tool was active when I made that decision"
- "I signed records with AI content and I can't explain what the AI contributed"

**Key language:** "I didn't know how much AI was already in my practice."

## Specific Language Clusters

### Cluster 1: The Attestation Language Problem
**What they say:**
- "The carrier form asks for evidence of AI governance. What does that mean?"
- "I signed the attestation. I don't know what I committed to."
- "The form wants documentation. What documentation?"
- "I told them we have AI governance. I don't have anything to show for it."

**What they mean:**
The carrier attestation is a documented standard. The physician signed it without understanding what evidence they need to support it. The form is designed for carrier risk evaluation, not physician liability protection. The physician needs pre-signing review and documentation architecture — not after-the-fact scrambling.

---

### Cluster 2: The FMH Oversight Language Problem
**What they say:**
- "FMH says human oversight. It doesn't say what that looks like in practice."
- "What does documented oversight actually mean?"
- "I'm the only physician here. How do I oversee myself?"
- "The guidance doesn't match the reality of running a small practice."

**What they mean:**
FMH guidance is correct in principle and operationally empty at independent practice scale. The requirement for human oversight exists. The translation of that requirement into周二 morning practice operations does not exist. The physician needs an operational protocol, not a principle.

---

### Cluster 3: The nFADP / Patient Confidentiality Language Problem
**What they say:**
- "I use AI to process patient data. Do I need consent for that?"
- "Article 321 CC — professional secrecy — does that apply to AI vendor data flows?"
- "The AI vendor says the data is secure. I don't know what that means."
- "What happens if the AI vendor has a data breach?"

**What they mean:**
Patient data processed through AI tools creates Article 321 CC professional secrecy obligations that most AI vendor contracts do not satisfy. The physician is accountable for professional secrecy violations even when the violation occurs through a third-party AI vendor. The physician needs vendor contract review against Article 321 CC obligations.

---

### Cluster 4: The Swissdamed Language Problem
**What they say:**
- "I heard Swissdamed has a July deadline. Does it apply to my AI tools?"
- "Tier 1 and Tier 2 — those are for device manufacturers, right?"
- "I use AI diagnostic support. Does that count as a medical device?"
- "I don't know whether Swissdamed applies to me."

**What they mean:**
Swissdamed classification determination for AI-enabled diagnostic tools is active and unaddressed. The framework was designed for medical device manufacturers, not independent physician practices. The physician needs a classification determination — not a compliance framework, a definitive answer about whether Swissdamed applies.

---

### Cluster 5: The Efficiency-Liability Paradox Language
**What they say:**
- "I need AI to run the practice. I also can't risk my license."
- "My staff uses AI. I can't supervise every tool they open."
- "If I don't use AI I'll fall behind. If I use AI I don't know what's at risk."
- "There's no way forward that feels safe."

**What they mean:**
The paradox is structural. The efficiency need and the liability risk are not separable at independent practice scale. The service that resolves this is not "AI governance" or "compliance" — it is an efficiency-enabling compliance framework that lets the practice use AI while managing the liability exposure.

---

### Cluster 6: The Solo vs. Multi-Physician Practice Language
**Solo physician language:**
- "I'm the only one here. I can't delegate oversight to anyone."
- "Every decision is mine. Every signature is mine. Every AI tool is mine."
- "The oversight requirement is architecturally impossible for me."

**Multi-physician practice language:**
- "My partners each use different AI tools. I don't know what they're using."
- "I signed the carrier attestation for the whole practice. I can't audit what everyone uses."
- "We never discussed AI governance as a group."

**What both mean:**
The structural problem differs by practice size. Solo practice faces architectural impossibility of self-oversight. Multi-physician practice faces accountability diffusion across independent decision-makers. The service response must be different for each.

---

## Language to Avoid (Our Voice vs. Their Voice)

**Our language (avoid in raw ICP contexts):**
- "AI governance framework"
- "attestation architecture"
- "documentation debt"
- "EU AI Act deployer obligations"
- "Article 63 personal due diligence"

**Their language (use in ICP-facing content):**
- "what you committed to when you signed"
- "what evidence you actually need"
- "what happens if the carrier asks for documentation"
- "what oversight actually looks like in your practice on a Tuesday morning"
- "whether Swissdamed applies to your AI tools"
