# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

## Phase 04 C304 | Geneva Healthcare ICP | Independent Physician / Small Group Practice (2–10 physicians)

---

## Purpose
This document maps what currently exists in the Geneva market for this ICP versus what is structurally missing. The gap is the positioning opportunity.

---

## What Exists

### Category1: General AI Governance Frameworks (International)
**What exists:**
- ISO42001 (AI Management Systems) — international standard, certifiable, designed for organizations with dedicated AI governance staff
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework — US-origin, framework-based, requires internal AI expertise to implement
- EU AI Act requirements — deployer obligations for high-risk AI systems, structurally unsolvable at boutique independent practice scale

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
None of these frameworks are calibrated to:
- Independent physician practice scale (2–10 physicians)
- Geneva/Swiss regulatory context (FMH, Article 321 CC, nFADP, Swissdamed)
- Personal liability model (physician license, not corporate liability)
- The specific documentation requirements of carrier attestation

These are enterprise frameworks. The ICP is a solo or small group practice. The gap between these frameworks and independent practice reality is the service opportunity.

---

### Category 2: Healthcare AI Vendor Compliance Programs
**What exists:**
- AI tool vendors provide "compliance documentation" — terms of service, data processing agreements, security certifications
- EHR vendors provide AI feature activation with standard terms
- Vendor documentation is designed to transfer risk to the practice, not to protect the physician's personal liability position

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
Vendor compliance documentation addresses vendor risk, not physician risk. The vendor's terms of service protect the vendor. The physician's personal liability exposure under FMH, Article 321 CC, and carrier attestation is not addressed by vendor documentation. The physician signs vendor contracts without Article 321 CC review and without understanding the professional secrecy implications.

---

### Category 3: General Data Protection / nFADP Consultants
**What exists:**
- nFADP compliance consultants offering data protection reviews
- General GDPR/nFADP frameworks applied to all business types
- Data protection officers available for larger organizations

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
General nFADP compliance does not address:
- FMH human oversight obligations
- Carrier AI attestation evidence requirements
- Swissdamed classification determination
- Article 321 CC professional secrecy in AI vendor contexts
- Medical record AI attribution requirements

The ICP needs healthcare-specific AI accountability services that address the intersection of nFADP, FMH, carrier attestation, and medical record law — not general data protection.

---

### Category 4: Medical Association Guidance
**What exists:**
- FMH physician ethics principles stating human oversight requirement
- Various cantonal medical association information bulletins on AI
- General AI guidance from international medical bodies (WHO, WMA)

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
FMH guidance is correct in principle and operationally empty. The guidance does not define what documented human oversight looks like at independent practice scale. The guidance does not translate into Tuesday morning practice operations. Physicians receive the guidance, understand it is insufficient, and have no operational alternative.

---

### Category 5: Medical Liability Insurance Brokerage
**What exists:**
- Insurance brokers who place medical liability coverage
- Brokers who distribute carrier AI attestation forms
- Brokers who advise on coverage structure

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
Brokers are paid by carriers to place coverage. The attestation is a carrier risk documentation tool. The broker's interest is in completing the form to bind coverage — not in protecting the physician's personal liability position. Pre-signing review of carrier attestation by a broker creates a conflict of interest. No independent pre-signing review service exists in the Geneva market.

---

### Category 6: Healthcare Technology Consultants
**What exists:**
- HIT consultants who recommend and implement AI tools
- EHR optimization consultants
- Practice management consultants

**Why it doesn't serve this ICP:**
HIT consultants are tool-selection services. They solve "which AI tool should I use." They do not address:
- What evidence the physician needs to support carrier attestation
- FMH human oversight documentation requirements
- Article 321 CC vendor contract review
- Swissdamed classification determination
- Personal liability exposure from AI usage

The HIT consultant's incentive is to implement AI. The physician's liability exposure from AI implementation is not the consultant's concern.

---

## What's Missing (The Positioning Opportunity)

### Missing 01: Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review Service
**What is missing:**
An independent review of carrier AI attestation forms before the physician signs — that analyzes what the attestation commits the physician to personally, what evidence is required to support the representations made, and what documentation gaps exist before signing.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
No one in the Geneva market offers this as a discrete service. Brokers complete forms to bind coverage. IT consultants implement tools. General compliance consultants do not focus on the physician personal liability dimension. The service requires deep knowledge of: carrier attestation architecture, FMH oversight requirements, nFADP obligations, and medical record law — across independent practice scale.

**The opportunity:**
This is the critical intervention point. The attestation creates a documented standard. The physician signs without understanding what they committed to. Pre-signing review is the service that protects the physician's personal liability position before the documentation becomes evidence.

---

### Missing 02: FMH Human Oversight Operational Protocol Service
**What is missing:**
A documented human oversight protocol specific to independent physician practice workflows — that translates FMH's principle-only guidance into Tuesday morning practice operations, satisfies carrier attestation evidence requirements, and works within the structural constraints of small practice dual-role reality.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
Creating this protocol requires:
- Deep FMH guidance knowledge
- Carrier attestation evidence standard knowledge
- Medical record documentation requirements
- Independent practice workflow understanding
- The protocol must be operationally implementable by a solo physician or small group without dedicated compliance staff

No one in the Geneva market has built this. FMH guidance exists without operational translation. Carriers ask for evidence without defining the standard. The service requires someone who understands all three dimensions.

**The opportunity:**
The operational protocol is the deliverable. It is specific to the ICP's practice size, specialty context, and AI tool inventory. It is not a template — it is a working document that establishes what documented oversight looks like in the practice's actual workflow.

---

### Missing 03: Medical Record AI Attribution Protocol
**What is missing:**
A documentation protocol that establishes at time of record creation: what the physician documented independently, what the AI contributed analytically, what the physician reviewed and decided — before the record is signed. This is required infrastructure for medical records containing AI-generated content.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
This service requires:
- Medical record law knowledge
- AI tool behavior knowledge (what different AI tools contribute to records)
- Documentation workflow integration
- Retroactive reconstruction methodology for existing records

The service does not exist because it requires the intersection of legal, medical, and AI technical knowledge that no current market participant possesses.

**The opportunity:**
Medical record AI attribution protocol is required infrastructure, not documentation improvement. The physician's name is on records with AI content. The physician cannot reconstruct AI contribution. The protocol establishes the evidentiary record before the record is signed — not after.

---

### Missing 04: Swissdamed Classification Determination Service
**What is missing:**
A definitive classification determination for the specific AI tools used in an independent practice — that produces a clear answer about whether Swissdamed applies, and what to do if it does. Not a compliance framework. A definitive answer.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
Swissdamed classification was designed for medical device manufacturers. The framework does not describe independent physician practice operations. The determination requires:
- Swissdamed regulatory knowledge
- AI tool classification criteria application
- Medical device vs. clinical decision support distinction
- Independent practice scale calibration

The determination is complex and the regulatory exposure is specific. No Geneva market participant has built a classification determination service calibrated to independent practice.

**The opportunity:**
Swissdamed classification determination is a discrete, bounded service. The output is not a framework — it is a definitive answer: Swissdamed applies / does not apply / partially applies with specific obligations. The physician needs this answer, not a general compliance package.

---

### Missing 05: Multi-Physician Practice Internal AI Accountability Methodology
**What is missing:**
A methodology for small group practices (2–10 physicians) that preserves individual physician autonomy while creating practice-level AI accountability documentation — specifically designed to satisfy carrier attestation requirements where the signing physician certifies to AI governance for the practice.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
The problem requires a methodology that:
- Does not require physicians to report their individual AI use to each other
- Creates practice-level accountability documentation
- Preserves FMH professional independence obligations
- Satisfies carrier attestation evidence requirements
- Works within the independent decision-making authority of each physician

This is a specific governance design challenge that no current market participant has addressed for Geneva small group practices.

**The opportunity:**
The methodology is a discrete service. The output is a practice-level AI accountability structure that the signing physician can honestly represent to the carrier — with evidence supporting the representation.

---

### Missing 06: Efficiency-Enabling Compliance Framework
**What is missing:**
A compliance framework that enables AI adoption for efficiency while managing the liability exposure — not a restrictive governance service that prevents AI use, but a framework that lets the practice use AI without risking the license.

**Why it doesn't exist:**
Current market offerings are binary:
- "Use AI freely" (IT consultants) — creates liability exposure
- "Don't use AI" (restrictive compliance) — creates operational impossibility

No one offers the third option: "Use AI with documented evidence that protects your license." This requires deep knowledge of:
- FMH oversight requirements
- Carrier attestation evidence standards
- nFADP obligations
- Medical record documentation requirements
- The specific liability exposure of each AI tool in use

**The opportunity:**
The efficiency-enabling compliance framework is the core service. It resolves the structural paradox that keeps independent practices immobile. It is not a compliance check — it is an operational enablement service that protects the physician's license while allowing the practice to function.

---

## Competitive Positioning Summary

| Existing Market Offering | What It Actually Does | Why It Fails This ICP |
|---|---|---|
| ISO42001 / NIST frameworks | Enterprise AI governance certification | Not calibrated to independent practice scale or personal liability |
| Vendor compliance docs | Transfer risk to practice | Protects vendor, not physician personal liability |
| General nFADP consultants | Data protection compliance | Does not address FMH, carrier attestation, Swissdamed intersection |
| FMH guidance | Principle-only AI oversight requirement | Operationally empty at independent practice scale |
| Insurance brokers | Complete attestation to bind coverage | Conflict of interest; not independent review |
| HIT consultants | AI tool selection and implementation | Incentive to implement AI; doesn't address liability documentation |
| Medical liability insurance | Coverage provision | Coverage doesn't equal personal liability protection |

**The positioning opportunity is unoccupied.** No one in the Geneva market offers the discrete services this ICP needs: pre-signing attestation review, FMH operational protocol, medical record AI attribution, Swissdamed classification determination, multi-physician accountability methodology, and efficiency-enabling compliance framework.

The market offers frameworks. We produce evidence.
