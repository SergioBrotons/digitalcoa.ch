## Part I: The Four Regulatory Layers

### Layer 1: Federal AI Ordinance
In force since January 1, 2025. Requires human oversight of AI-assisted decisions. Applies to every Swiss business using AI for decisions that affect clients, employees, or counterparties.

### Layer 2: FADP Art. 21
Automated decision-making obligations. Ongoing. Requires disclosure when AI makes decisions that affect individuals — and the right to request human review.

### Layer 3: EU AI Act GPAI — Already In Force
Since August 2, 2025, GPAI obligations are active. If your business places AI on the EU market OR if your AI outputs are used within the EU, the EU AI Act applies to you now. This is not a future problem. Most Geneva businesses in this position do not know it.

**Two-path applicability test:**
1. Are you placing AI tools or systems commercially available to EU users?
2. Is the output of your AI used in decisions or activities within the EU?

### Layer 4: EU AI Act High-Risk — August 2, 2026
By August 2, 2026, high-risk AI systems must undergo conformity assessment. The same date: grandfathering for legacy GPAI models expires. This is approximately four months away.

**Legal (Geneva Law Firms):** SBA AI Guidelines + FADP Art. 21 + EU AI Act for EU-facing practices. 12% have written governance policies (Geneva Bar survey). August 2 deadline is immediate.

**Finance/Wealth Management:** FINMA guidance + CARF/CRS + Federal AI Ordinance + EU AI Act. 91% using BigTech AI; 50% without explicit AI strategy. Four-layer convergence requires integrated response.

**Consulting/SMB:** EU AI Act applicability is the primary question. Most Geneva SMBs don't know if the EU AI Act applies to them. The applicability assessment is the advisory product.

**Healthcare:** Swissdamed July 1 (~91 days) + FADP Art. 21 + Federal AI Ordinance + EU AI Act for EU patient exposure. Four-layer healthcare-specific compliance gap.

**Real Estate:** SVIT guidance + FADP for tenant/buyer data + EU AI Act for EU buyer/investor exposure. Property valuation AI, tenant screening AI, and property management AI all require documented governance.

## Part III: The Universal AI Governance Audit

Answer these seven questions honestly:

1. Do we have a documented AI inventory (specific tools, specific uses, specific users)?
2. Are our AI tools hosted in Switzerland/EU or US BigTech infrastructure?
3. Do our AI vendor contracts address training data, hosting, and EU AI Act compliance?
4. Have we assessed EU AI Act applicability using the two-path test?
5. Do we have human oversight protocols for AI-assisted decisions?
6. Do we have client disclosure language for AI-assisted deliverables?
7. Do we have a written AI governance policy (addressing the sector-specific gap)?

*If you answered "no," "not yet," or "I don't know" to any question, your business has an active AI governance gap.*
