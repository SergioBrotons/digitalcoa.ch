## Content Sections

### Section 1: The Four Regulatory Layers Every Geneva Business Faces
1. Federal AI Ordinance — 15+ months operative; human oversight + documentation
2. FADP Art. 21 — automated decision-making obligations; ongoing
3. EU AI Act GPAI — in force since August 2, 2025; most firms don't know they may already be in scope
4. EU AI Act High-Risk — conformity assessments due August 2, 2026 (~4 months)

### Section 2: Sector-Specific Risk Profiles
- Legal: SBA guidelines + FADP Art. 21 + EU AI Act (EU-facing practices)
- Finance/WM: FINMA guidance + CARF/CRS + EU AI Act (EU client exposure)
- Consulting/SMB: EU AI Act applicability test + sector-specific requirements
- Healthcare: Swissdamed + FADP + EU AI Act (EU patient exposure)
- Real Estate: SVIT guidance + FADP + EU AI Act (EU buyer/investor exposure)

### Section 3: The Universal AI Governance Audit (7 Questions)
1. Do we have a documented AI inventory (specific tools, specific uses, specific users)?
2. Are our AI tools hosted in Switzerland/EU or US BigTech infrastructure?
3. Do our AI vendor contracts address training data, hosting, and EU AI Act compliance?
4. Have we assessed EU AI Act applicability using the two-path test?
5. Do we have human oversight protocols for AI-assisted decisions?
6. Do we have client disclosure language for AI-assisted deliverables?
7. Do we have a written AI governance policy (addressing the sector-specific gap)?

### Section 4: The Geneva AI Governance Framework
- Phase 1: AI inventory and risk classification (immediate)
- Phase 2: EU AI Act applicability assessment (this month)
- Phase 3: Governance policy development (this quarter)
- Phase 4: August 2, 2026 preparedness (before Q3 2026)
- Phase 5: Ongoing monitoring and sector-specific adaptation
