# 02 — Market Signals: DigitalCoa.ch Client Acquisition

## C123 Findings (NET-NEW)

### Finding 01: Geneva Executive Persona — Decision-Making Style
**Signal:** Geneva executives make advisory decisions differently from other markets: (1) trusted peer introduction is primary trust-building mechanism; (2) executive briefings (not proposals) are the entry point; (3) credibility is established through specific Geneva institutional knowledge, not generic consulting credentials; (4) decision time is 2-4 weeks for smaller engagements, 2-3 months for retainers.
**Evidence base:** Geneva executive advisory engagement patterns (C121-122 data), Geneva business culture analysis, peer referral patterns.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — synthesis from engagement data; needs direct validation from clients.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Refine briefing call approach to match Geneva executive decision style; emphasize Geneva-specific credentials; structure engagement to match decision timeline.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered post-briefing-call conversion optimization; C123 covers the executive persona decision-making psychology — upstream from conversion.

### Finding 02: Service Page Conversion — Geneva-Specific Value Proposition
**Signal:** Analysis of DigitalCoa.ch service page performance (C122 indirect data): Geneva executives respond to (1) specific regulatory/strategic topics, not generic "advisory" language; (2) Geneva institutional examples; (3) executive-briefing format descriptions; (4) direct, no-fluff language. Generic consulting language underperforms.
**Evidence base:** DigitalCoa.ch service page engagement data (C122), Geneva executive feedback (C121-122), competitive service page analysis.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — indirect signals; needs direct analytics validation.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Revise service page copy to lead with Geneva-specific regulatory topics (Basel IV, DORA, ESG); use executive-briefing language; add Geneva institutional examples; test direct vs. descriptive format.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered briefing call conversion optimization; C123 covers service page conversion optimization — different touchpoint.

### Finding 03: Thought Leadership as Client Acquisition — Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch Pathway
**Signal:** Brotons.net content is generating warm leads to DigitalCoa.ch (C122 observed). Pattern: Geneva executive reads Brotons.net piece → subscribes → engages with specific topic → reaches out or is contacted. Pathway works but is not systematized.
**Evidence base:** Email inquiry patterns (C122), LinkedIn connection patterns (C122), owner-reported client origin.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — confirmed pathway exists; conversion to engagement not tracked.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Systematize Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch pathway; create content-to-advisory conversion points; track which Brotons.net topics generate DigitalCoa.ch inquiries.
**Note:** Publishing DigitalCoa.ch insights (repurposed Brotons.net content) requires owner approval per IDENTITY.md.
**Angle different from C121:** C121 covered content-to-client conversion (conceptual); C123 covers specific pathway systematization — implementation-ready.

### Finding 04: Lead Qualification Framework — Geneva Executive Criteria
**Signal:** Geneva executive qualification criteria (C123 synthesis): (1) Decision authority — must be C-suite or have direct board access; (2) Geneva institutional context — Geneva HQ or significant Geneva operations; (3) Specific pain point — regulatory, strategic, or transformation; (4) Budget capacity — CHF 50K+ engagement threshold; (5) Trust indicator — referred by trusted peer or credible introduction.
**Evidence base:** Engagement qualification patterns (C121-122), Geneva executive advisory market analysis.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — framework synthesis; needs validation against actual engagements.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Create lead qualification checklist for DigitalCoa.ch inbound inquiries; prioritize leads meeting 4+ criteria; develop disqualified lead nurture path.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered Geneva referral program design; C123 covers lead qualification framework — different acquisition stage.

### Finding 05: Competitive Differentiation — Independent vs. Big 4 Advisory
**Signal:** Geneva executive advisory market differentiation factors: (1) No audit conflict — independent from Big 4 audit relationships; (2) Geneva institutional depth — specific knowledge vs. generic consulting; (3) Executive-level engagement — senior advisor involvement vs. junior team; (4) Outcome orientation — strategic counsel vs. report delivery; (5) Relationship continuity — ongoing advisory vs. project-based.
**Evidence base:** Geneva executive preferences survey (industry data), competitive positioning analysis (C122), owner-reported differentiation factors.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH — competitive positioning widely recognized; specific weight of each factor needs validation.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Refine positioning to emphasize independence (no audit conflict), Geneva institutional depth, and senior engagement model. Create differentiation statement for use in briefing calls and proposals.
**Angle different from C121:** C121 covered executive credibility positioning; C123 covers competitive differentiation framework — more specific competitive context.

### Finding 06: Engagement Model Clarity — Retainer vs. Project vs. Ad Hoc
**Signal:** Geneva executive preference patterns for advisory engagement models: (1) Initial engagement typically project-based (CHF 30-100K) to establish trust; (2) Successful projects lead to retainer (CHF 10-25K/month) for ongoing counsel; (3) Ad hoc briefings for specific events/decisions (CHF 5-20K). Hybrid model (project + retainer) most common for ongoing relationships.
**Evidence base:** Engagement pattern analysis (C121-122), Geneva boutique advisory pricing data.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM — engagement pattern synthesis; pricing data from indirect sources.
**For DigitalCoa.ch:** Structure service offerings to match engagement model progression (project → retainer); price project engagements at CHF 50-150K; position retainer as natural next step after successful project.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered remote delivery quality standards; C123 covers engagement model structure — different commercial design layer.
