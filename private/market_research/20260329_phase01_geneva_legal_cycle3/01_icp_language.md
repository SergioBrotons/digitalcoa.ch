## ICP: Geneva Small Law Firms (Cycle 3 Refined)

### Target Profile
- **Firm size:** 1–8 attorneys (solo to small partnership)
- **Revenue:** CHF 300K–2M (sole practice to small partnership)
- **Decision-maker:** Managing partner / individual attorney
- **Age:** 45–65 (established principal, digitally-aware but operationally busy)
- **Location:** Geneva Canton — Ville de Genève, Carouge, Lancy, Vernier, Thônex
- **Legal form:** Sàrl, SA, sole practice
- **Language:** FR-dominant, EN useful for international clients
- **nFADP exposure:** High — client data is personal data; consent and transparency obligations apply

### Universal Pain (Confirmed in Cycle 3)
**Core tension:** AI tools are available, staff pressure is real, but the regulatory and liability implications are unclear — especially around client data and professional secrecy.

### Primary Frustrations (Cycle 3 — Refined with FDPIC May 2025)

**In their words:**
- "Nous ne savons pas comment intégrer l'IA"
- "Nous hésitons à cause du secret professionnel"
- "Les outils d'IA générique comme ChatGPT envoyent les données à des serveurs externes"
- "Qui est responsable si l'IA fait une erreur?"
- "Qui sommes-nous obligés d'informer — et comment?"
- "Le retour sur investissement n'est pas clair"
- "On n'a pas le temps de former toute l'équipe"
- **"Où trouve-t-on des conseils qui comprennent vraiment notre métier?"**

### Phase 05 + Cycle 3 Refined Positioning

**The Core Frame:**
> **"Avant de choisir un outil d'IA, vous devez prendre trois décisions sur les données."**
> *(EN: "Before you choose an AI tool, you need to make three decisions about data."*)

This frame works because:
1. It answers the question they actually have — not "which tool" but "what am I allowed to do"
2. It positions DigitalCoa.ch as a decision guide, not a tool vendor
3. It creates low-friction entry: "let me ask you three questions" vs. "let me sell you something"
4. **NEW (Cycle 3):** FDPIC May 2025 confirms nFADP transparency obligations give this frame regulatory teeth — the "three decisions" are now explicitly tied to FDPIC Art. 5 (data classification), Art. 12 (consent), and Art. 14 (transparency).

### Decision Framework (Unchanged from Cycle 2 — Validated by FDPIC May 2025)

**Decision 1: What category does my client's data fall into?**
- Ordinary personal data (nFADP — baseline obligations)
- Sensitive data (nFADP Art. 5(c) — heightened obligations: health, financial, legal situation)
- The classification determines what you can process and what consent is required

**Decision 2: Where can that data go?**
- Internal only (local/Swiss-hosted tools only)
- Cloud/SaaS (need SCCs, data processing agreements, Swiss or EU hosting)
- Third-party AI providers (need explicit consent + DPA + accountability documentation)

**Decision 3: Do I need to notify clients that I'm using AI?**
- FDPIC May 2025 confirms: transparency obligations require informing data subjects
- SBA Guidelines June 2024: professional secrecy obligations apply to AI tool use
- Best practice: proactive notification in engagement letter + AI use policy
- **This is the differentiator question — most attorneys don't know they need to do this**

### Language to Use
- "Ce que vous avez le droit de faire avec les données de vos clients"
- "Votre obligation de transparence selon la nFADP"
- "Le secret professionnel à l'ère de l'IA"
- "Décision par décision — un cadre pour votre cabinet"

### Language to Avoid
- "Boostez votre productivité avec l'IA"
- "L'IA transformera votre cabinet"
- "Adoptez la révolution numérique"
- "Conformité IA" (trop abstrait — préférer "ce que vous avez le droit de faire")
- "Outils d'IA conformes" (implique un produit — DigitalCoa.ch vend un cadre, pas des outils)

### ICP Descriptor (EN)
> The managing partner at a 1–8 attorney Geneva firm who is NOT asking "which AI tool should we use" — they're asking "what are we actually allowed to do with client data under nFADP, and how do we make that decision responsibly?" They feel the gap between AI theory and safe practice. The FDPIC May 2025 guidance has made this more concrete — and more pressing. They want a framework, not a demo.

### ICP Descriptor (FR)
> L'associé ou l'avocat indépendant d'un cabinet genevois de 1 à 8 personnes qui ne se demande PAS « quel outil d'IA devrions-nous utiliser » — mais « qu'avons-nous le droit de faire avec les données de nos clients selon la nFADP, et comment prendre cette décision de manière responsable ? » Il ressent l'écart entre la théorie de l'IA et la pratique sûre. L'aide-mémoire DPDP de mai 2025 a rendu cela plus concret — et plus urgent. Il veut un cadre, pas une démonstration.

## Notes

**FDPIC May 2025 is the new anchor.** The FDPIC explicitly confirmed nFADP applies to AI. This is not speculative — it is the regulator saying "this law covers AI." For Geneva attorneys, this is the moment of clarity that will drive demand for governance advisory.

**SBA Guidelines (June 2024) provide peer authority.** The Swiss Bar Association has given its members guidance on AI. This signals: the profession takes this seriously, and the standard of care is being defined.

**First-mover window still open.** No Geneva boutique advisor targets small law firms specifically with an AI governance framework. This window will close when one or two competitors establish themselves — likely within 12–18 months.
