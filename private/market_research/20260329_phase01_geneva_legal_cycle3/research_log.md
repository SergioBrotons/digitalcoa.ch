## What Was Learned (Cycle 3)

1. **FDPIC issued explicit AI+nFADP guidance (May 8, 2025).** This is the most significant new regulatory development. The FDPIC confirmed that nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing — technology-neutral wording covers AI tools. This gives DigitalCoa.ch a precise hook: FDPIC guidance date + article reference.

2. **SBA Guidelines on AI (June 2024) provide the professional framework.** The Swiss Bar Association adopted AI guidelines covering professional secrecy, data protection, and responsible use. This is the peer-authority signal that Geneva attorneys respect.

3. **Federal Council AI strategy (February 2025) + CoE AI Convention (signed March 2025).** Switzerland is choosing a lean regulatory approach — ratifying the Council of Europe AI Convention rather than a standalone Swiss AI Act. Draft implementation bill expected end of 2026. This means: nFADP + professional standards = the operative framework through 2026.

4. **SBA Congress Lucerne 2025 marks a turning point.** The legal community has moved from "curious about AI" to "cautiously implementing AI." This is the moment to be the trusted guide — not the tool vendor.

5. **Geneva has AI governance specialists (large firms).** Gilliéron Avocat, CROCE & Associés, Quinze Cour des Bastions — but these serve mid-to-large clients. No boutique competitor focused on small firms (1–8 attorneys). First-mover window intact.

6. **Competitive discipline confirmed.** Even Cycle 3 web search (1 request) didn't reveal a boutique competitor targeting small Geneva law firms. Window is real.
