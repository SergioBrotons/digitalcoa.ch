# Phase 06: Geneva Real Estate — ICP
# Cycle 16 — 2026-03-30

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

### Primary Segment
Small real estate agencies in Geneva: boutique agencies, property managers (typically 2–10 employees), independent brokers.

### Core Problem
67% of Geneva real estate firms already use AI — the highest adoption rate of any vertical in the DigitalCoa.ch portfolio. Most are using AI without a governance framework. GAIGI is operative ~7–10 months. Swissdamed ~12 weeks creates spillover awareness. Retroactive compliance ("govern the AI you already have") is a lower-friction entry point than anticipatory framing.

### Regulatory Triggers

**GAIGI — Operative ~7–10 Months (Fall 2025 + March 2026)**
Geneva real estate AI governance framework now operative. Category awareness established but SMB gap intact.

**nFADP Art. 5(c) — Active Since 2023**
AI-based automated decisions about individuals require DPIA + human review.

**Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)**
Spillover effect: real estate firms using AI diagnostic tools face Swissdamed obligations.

**EU AI Act GPAI — Active Since August 2, 2025 (~8 months)**
EU nexus firms: transparency obligations already live.

### ICP Statement
Geneva small real estate agency or property manager using AI (the 67% majority) without a governance framework, facing GAIGI obligations ~7–10 months away — and the unique advantage of retroactive compliance ("govern the AI you already have") as a lower-friction entry.

### Addressable Market
~300–500 Geneva small real estate agencies/property managers; 67% already using AI = widest gap in portfolio

### Competitive Absence (7th+ cycle)
No boutique Geneva advisor addresses small RE firm AI governance. GAIGI is institutional/government, not SMB advisory. Structural fact confirmed.

### Key Insight: Retroactive Compliance
Real Estate is unique across all verticals: the entry frame is "govern the AI you already have" — not "prepare for upcoming regulations." Lower friction. Faster value. Earlier engagement.
