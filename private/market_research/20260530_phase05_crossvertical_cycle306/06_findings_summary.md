## Finding 01: Cross-Vertical Evidence Architecture Is a Single Service Model, Not Four Vertical Services

**The finding:** The evidence production problem is structurally identical across all four verticals. The ICP in legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare all face the same fundamental problem: they signed something creating a documented standard, they have no evidence they met it, and the documentation gap is structural (not attitudinal). The service that addresses this problem has the same architecture in every vertical — evidence production infrastructure, not policy documents.

**The structural mechanism:** Carrier form architecture (in every vertical) is designed to collect evidence — representations about what the ICP actually documented. The ICP produces policy documents (what should happen) and believes that satisfies the carrier's evidence request. The carrier wants evidence records (what actually happened). The gap between policy and evidence is not specific to any vertical — it is the universal documentation paradigm failure.

**The service model implication:** One evidence architecture service works across all four verticals. The vertical calibration is in the regulatory framework (Art.321 CC vs. Art.63 vs. Art.52 vs. FMH), not in the evidence production methodology. The ICP in any vertical needs: tool inventory documentation, supervision record production, vendor contract gap analysis, and cross-regulator documentation mapping. The evidence system is the same; the regulatory calibration is vertical-specific.

**The positioning implication:** "Evidence architecture" is the category. It is not "AI governance for legal" or "AI compliance for healthcare." It is evidence architecture — the documentation infrastructure that produces contemporaneous records of what the ICP actually did, not what they intended to do. The category naming is cross-vertical by design.

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified three-vector documentation debt as the universal service frame. C306 Phase 05 extends this by identifying that the evidence architecture itself is the same service model across verticals — not three parallel vertical services, but one cross-vertical evidence production system with vertical-specific calibration.

## Finding 02: The Personal Exposure / Carrier Coverage Separation Is Systematically Misunderstood in All Four Verticals

**The finding:** The ICP in every vertical operates under a mental model where carrier coverage equals personal protection. This mental model does not match the legal structure — carrier coverage and personal liability are separate legal tracks in every vertical, and carrier coverage does not discharge personal liability. The misunderstanding is structural, not informational — the ICP is not missing a fact, they are operating under a false model of how insurance and personal liability interact.

**The structural mechanism:**
- **Legal:** Carrier E&O → firm-level civil claims. Art.321 CC → personal criminal liability. Separate tracks. Broker's E&O may not extend to explanation obligations on carrier forms.
- **Finance:** Carrier pays settlements. Art.63 nFADP → personal administrative liability. Carrier subrogation → personal assets if attestation inaccurate. Separate tracks.
- **Consulting:** Professional liability → firm-level claims. Art.52 nFADP → personal administrative fines (CHF 250,000 for intentional violations). Administrative fines not covered by civil liability policies.
- **Healthcare:** Carrier covers financial consequences. Art.321 CC → personal criminal exposure. Art.63 → personal administrative liability. FMH → professional license implications. Carrier coverage does not protect professional standing or personal criminal exposure.

**The service implication:** The personal evidence package is a discrete service distinct from firm-level compliance documentation. It serves the ICP's individual interest (personal liability protection) rather than the firm's interest (compliance posture). The evidence package must document the ICP's personal oversight — what the individual did, not just what the firm did. This is a different deliverable than firm-level AI governance policy documents.

**The language implication:** The decision trigger is personal exposure language — not firm-level compliance language. "What does this mean for my license?" is the question that produces action. "Does our firm comply with regulations?" is the question that produces consideration. Lead with personal.

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified the three-vector documentation debt and the service model implications. C306 Phase 05 extends this by specifically naming the personal exposure / carrier coverage separation as a universal structural misunderstanding that requires a separate service response — the personal evidence package as a distinct deliverable.

## Finding 03: Vendor Contract Terms Are the Mechanism Through Which Professional Accountability Is Defined — and the ICP Has Not Reviewed Them

**The finding:** In every vertical, the ICP's professional accountability obligations are partially defined by vendor contract terms the ICP accepted without review. The vendor's terms include data processing provisions, sub-processor relationships, model training authorizations, and data retention terms — each of which creates or modifies the ICP's accountability exposure under professional secrecy (Art.321 CC), data protection (nFADP Art.26), and personal liability (Art.63, Art.52) frameworks. The ICP's accountability is not only what the professional code says — it is also what the vendor contracts allow.

**The structural mechanism:**
- **Legal:** Vendor terms may include provisions allowing vendor access to or use of client-related data for model training. Art.321 CC prohibits disclosure. The vendor provisions create exposure the attorney cannot eliminate without losing AI tool functionality.
- **Finance:** Vendor integration chain (multiple AI tools, multiple vendor platforms) creates data processing relationships the ICP has not documented. Art.26 requires documentation of data processing activities. The vendor terms define what each vendor does with client data — and the ICP has not documented this.
- **Consulting:** Sub-consultant AI tools create vendor relationships with terms the managing partner has not reviewed. The managing partner is accountable for client data processing through the entire service chain — including vendor relationships they don't know exist.
- **Healthcare:** Model training provisions allow vendors to use de-identified patient data. Art.321 CC criminal exposure and Art.63 personal exposure are structurally created by these provisions. The physician cannot eliminate model training without losing AI tool functionality — creating a structurally enforced choice between operational necessity and criminal/personal administrative exposure.

**The service implication:** Vendor contract gap analysis is a discrete bounded service — reviewing vendor contracts against professional obligations, identifying where vendor terms create exposure under Art.321 CC, Art.63, Art.52, or FMH standards, and producing documentation of the gap analysis. The output is not just "this is a problem" — it is "here is what the vendor contract obligates you to, here is where it conflicts with your professional obligations, here is what you can do about it."

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified tool sprawl as organic uncontrolled proliferation. C306 Phase 05 extends this by identifying the specific mechanism: vendor terms are the contract mechanism through which professional accountability is defined, and the gap between vendor terms and professional obligations is the actual service opportunity.

## Finding 04: Delegation Accountability Is a Structural Problem, Not a Supervision Problem — and No Existing Service Addresses It

**The finding:** The ICP in every vertical is accountable for AI use by people (staff, associates, sub-consultants) and systems (vendor product updates, AI tool scope drift) they did not directly control. The existing market response ("supervise more thoroughly") is operationally impossible and structurally inadequate — it assumes the ICP can personally review every AI use event, which they cannot. The delegation accountability gap is structural, not personal. No existing service addresses it as a structural problem.

**The structural mechanism:**
- **Legal:** Associates use AI tools not tracked by managing partner. Different practice areas adopt different solutions. The managing partner's signature on AI-assisted work product is present; the AI contribution is not documented. The managing partner is accountable for decisions they did not make and cannot enumerate.
- **Finance:** Institutional knowledge of AI governance documentation is concentrated in one individual (CIO). If that person leaves, the firm's oversight capability leaves with them. The firm's evidence architecture has a single point of failure with no redundancy.
- **Consulting:** Sub-consultants use AI for research and analysis. The managing partner is accountable for deliverables incorporating AI they didn't control. The accountability chain is operationally invisible — the managing partner knows something is happening but cannot enumerate it.
- **Healthcare:** Staff use AI tools under physician supervision. The physician is accountable for decisions made by staff using AI tools the physician did not evaluate. AI tool scope drift (documentation → diagnostic support) happens without physician governance awareness. The patient becomes an uncontrolled AI governance variable (AI-generated reports entering clinical records through patient portal).

**The service implication:** The delegation accountability documentation system makes the invisible accountability chain visible and defensible. It does not require the ICP to personally review every AI use event — it requires the ICP to document the delegation structure (who does what, what the supervision relationship is, what the accountability chain looks like) in a way that demonstrates the ICP's oversight intention and practice. The evidence shows that the ICP had a delegation structure — even if they couldn't personally review every AI use event.

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified solo practitioner operational inaccessibility as a universal market failure. C306 Phase 05 extends this by specifically naming the delegation accountability gap as a distinct structural problem requiring a different service response — not "supervise better" but "document the delegation chain you actually have."

## Finding 05: Multi-Authority Documentation Non-Alignment Is a Systems Design Problem, Not a Document Production Problem

**The finding:** The ICP in every vertical faces documentation demands from multiple authorities simultaneously (carrier + professional association + supervisory authority + clients + courts), each requesting different documentation types. The market's response (separate documentation for each authority) is operationally impractical and creates inconsistent evidence. The multi-authority documentation problem is a systems design problem — it requires an evidence architecture that produces documentation satisfying multiple authority demands simultaneously, not separate document sets for each.

**The structural mechanism:**
- **Legal:** Carrier attestation (self-reported, aggregate) vs. Ordre disciplinary (actual supervision process) vs. litigation discovery (specific matter AI disclosure) vs. FINMA (governance capability) vs. FDPIC (data processing documentation). Five authority types, five documentation types, no unified system.
- **Finance:** FINMA (governance capability evidence) vs. carrier Form v4 (specific practice attestation) vs. institutional client DDQ (oversight records) vs. Art.63 personal due diligence (individual protection) vs. EU AI Act deployer documentation. The documentation architecture that protects against FINMA may not protect against Art.63 personal exposure.
- **Consulting:** Client questionnaire responses (commercial gate) vs. carrier attestation (insurance condition) vs. Art.52 personal documentation (individual protection) vs. EU AI Act GPAI documentation. Four parallel documentation requirements, no unified framework.
- **Healthcare:** FMH human oversight documentation vs. carrier attestation evidence vs. Art.63 personal oversight documentation vs. FADP Art.21 + Art.22 patient disclosure/reversal rights vs. HIN compliance (transport security only). Five non-aligned standards, no mapping.

**The service implication:** Cross-regulator documentation mapping is a discrete service — designing an evidence architecture that serves multiple authorities simultaneously. The design process starts from the evidence requirements of each authority and identifies the evidence artifacts that satisfy multiple authorities at once. The output is a single evidence production system that generates documentation types serving multiple authority demands, rather than separate document sets for each authority.

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified regulatory pressure convergence as a sustained environment. C306 Phase 05 extends this by specifically identifying the systems design problem — the documentation that satisfies one authority may not satisfy another, and the solution is not more documents but a different architecture.

## Finding 06: Continuous AI Operation vs. Periodic Documentation Mismatch Creates Documentation Debt That Accelerates Non-Linearly — and the Gap Is Inherently Unbridgeable at the Current Documentation Paradigm

**The finding:** AI systems in all four verticals operate continuously — automated decisions, data processing, AI tool functionality between human review cycles. The ICP's documentation cadence (annual, quarterly, periodic) does not match the AI systems' operational cadence. The documentation gap is not a resource problem — it is a structural problem inherent in the mismatch between continuous AI operation and periodic human documentation. The gap cannot be closed by producing more documents within the same paradigm. It requires a different documentation architecture.

**The structural mechanism:**
- **Legal:** AI tools process client information in every matter, continuously. Supervision is periodic (matter opening, periodic review, matter closing). The record of AI use in specific matters is retrospective — documenting what the ICP decided at defined checkpoints, not what the AI did between checkpoints.
- **Finance:** Automated rebalancing runs daily. Risk modeling updates continuously. Client data is processed in real time. The oversight documentation is annual or quarterly. There is no record of what the AI actually did between annual reviews — only what it was supposed to do. The AI made hundreds of decisions that are not documented.
- **Consulting:** AI tools operate between proposal and deliverable — during the project, when no documentation event is triggered. The consulting firm's documentation of AI contribution to deliverables is episodic (at defined project milestones), not continuous (capturing AI activity as it occurs).
- **Healthcare:** AI interpretation layers operate on laboratory results and imaging before the physician sees them. The clinical record documents the physician's decision based on AI-interpreted data. The AI's interpretive contribution is not recorded. The documentation gap is invisible — the record shows a physician decision, not an AI-interpretation-then-physician-decision chain.

**The service implication:** The continuous oversight methodology defines what ongoing documentation means operationally for continuously operating AI systems. It is not "document more frequently" — it is "define what the documentation system must capture between human review cycles." The methodology must be specific enough to produce evidence records that demonstrate oversight occurred, without requiring the ICP to review every AI use event.

**Why this is NET-NEW (distinct from C305 Phase 05):** C305 Phase 05 identified three-vector documentation debt (time + tool count + staff use compounding). C306 Phase 05 extends this by specifically identifying the continuous operation vs. periodic documentation mismatch as the structural mechanism of the debt acceleration — and by identifying that the solution is not more documents within the same paradigm but a different documentation architecture.
