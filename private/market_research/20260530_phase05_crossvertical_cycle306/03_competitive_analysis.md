## Cross-Vertical Competitive Landscape — What Exists vs. What's Missing

The competitive landscape across all four verticals reveals a consistent pattern: existing competitors serve vertical-specific compliance needs with enterprise-calibrated solutions that are structurally misaligned with the ICP's actual problem. The white space is not about vertical expertise — it is about evidence production capability applied across verticals.

---

## What Exists Across Verticals

### Category 1: Vertical-Specific Compliance Consultancies

**What they offer:** Regulatory compliance firms that serve one vertical — legal compliance firms, financial regulatory consultancies, healthcare compliance consultants. They understand the specific regulatory framework of their vertical and produce documentation calibrated to vertical-specific requirements.

**Structural limitation:** Their documentation serves vertical compliance (satisfying a specific regulator's requirements) rather than evidence production (documenting what the ICP actually did). Their deliverable is a compliance document, not an evidence record.

**Why this fails the ICP:** The ICP needs evidence that survives cross-regulator scrutiny — documentation that simultaneously satisfies carrier, professional association, supervisory authority, and client demands. Vertical-specific compliance consultants produce documents designed for one regulator at a time.

---

### Category 2: Enterprise AI Governance Frameworks

**What they offer:** ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act compliance frameworks designed for large institutions with legal departments, compliance teams, and documented governance processes. These frameworks are comprehensive and well-structured.

**Structural limitation:** They require implementation resources that the ICP (2-50 person firm, 2-10 physician practice) does not have. They produce policy documents, not evidence records. They are English-language frameworks without Geneva French/English bilingual calibration.

**Why this fails the ICP:** The ICP needs actionable evidence production — not a framework that describes what should exist. The enterprise framework produces a document the ICP puts on a shelf. The ICP needs a service that produces evidence records continuously.

---

### Category 3: Legal Firms

**What they offer:** Legal opinions on AI governance obligations, contract review services, regulatory interpretation authority. They bring legal privilege protection and regulatory interpretation expertise.

**Structural limitation:** Legal firms produce legal interpretations — they describe what should exist. They do not produce evidence records. Their hourly billing model creates cost uncertainty. They are not positioned for continuous evidence production.

**Why this fails the ICP:** The ICP needs someone who produces evidence records as an ongoing service — not someone who tells them what their evidence should look like once. The legal opinion is a document. The ICP needs a documentation system.

---

### Category 4: Technology Vendors

**What they offer:** AI governance documentation embedded in vendor terms of service, compliance packages for enterprise clients, attestation templates aligned with carrier requirements. They have direct access to model documentation and technical specifications.

**Structural limitation:** Vendor documentation serves vendor risk management — it describes the tool, not the ICP's use of the tool. Vendor terms of service do not satisfy carrier or institutional client evidence requirements. Vendor attestation templates create false compliance confidence.

**Why this fails the ICP:** The ICP needs independent assessment — evidence that is not produced by the party whose interests are served by the ICP believing they are compliant. The vendor cannot be the ICP's evidence producer.

---

### Category 5: Insurance Brokers

**What they offer:** Carrier attestation form preparation, coverage continuity guidance, AI risk language for insurance renewal. They have carrier relationships and understand insurance renewal processes.

**Structural limitation:** Broker services are structurally constrained by carrier relationships — their advice must not contradict carrier positions. They cannot provide independent evidence architecture. They cannot map multi-standard compliance simultaneously.

**Why this fails the ICP:** The broker's interest is coverage continuity. The ICP's interest is personal liability protection and evidence production. These are not the same thing. The broker's coverage advice may increase the ICP's personal exposure while maintaining coverage.

## Cross-Vertical White Space: What's Missing Across All Verticals

### Missing Capability 01: Evidence Production as Primary Service

**What the market offers:** Policy documents, framework descriptions, compliance templates, legal opinions.

**What's missing:** A service that produces evidence records as its primary output — contemporaneous documentation of what the ICP actually did, not what they intended to do.

**Why it's missing:** Evidence production is operationally intensive and requires ongoing service delivery. It is easier to sell a policy document than to maintain an evidence production system. The market offers solutions that are document-shaped (policies, frameworks, templates) because documents are discrete deliverables. Evidence production is a continuous service, not a discrete document.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that produces evidence as the primary output — not policy documents, not framework descriptions, evidence records that demonstrate what the ICP actually did.

---

### Missing Capability 02: Cross-Vertical Evidence Architecture

**What the market offers:** Vertical-specific compliance documentation — legal firms produce legal documents, finance consultants produce FINMA-calibrated documents, healthcare consultants produce FMH-calibrated documents.

**What's missing:** A single evidence architecture that serves multiple verticals simultaneously — evidence production methodology that works for a 2-5 attorney firm, a 50-200 employee wealth management firm, a 2-50 person consulting boutique, and a 2-10 physician practice.

**Why it's missing:** Cross-vertical evidence architecture requires understanding what is common across verticals (personal exposure structure, delegation accountability gap, vendor contract alignment failure) and what is vertical-specific (regulatory framework, professional obligations). Existing competitors optimize for one vertical. No one has built the cross-vertical evidence production system.

**The position to claim:** Evidence architecture that is calibrated to the ICP's problem (not to the regulatory framework) — a service that works across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare because it addresses the structural problems that are universal, not the regulatory problems that are vertical-specific.

---

### Missing Capability 03: Personal Liability Evidence Package

**What the market offers:** Firm-level compliance documentation, carrier attestation preparation, professional association guidance.

**What's missing:** A personal evidence package specifically serving the ICP's individual liability protection — documentation that demonstrates the managing partner's or physician's personal oversight of AI systems, distinct from firm-level compliance documents.

**Why it's missing:** Personal liability protection requires understanding the specific personal exposure (Art.321 CC for attorneys, Art.63 for finance and consulting, FMH + Art.321 CC + Art.63 for physicians) and producing evidence that addresses the specific personal risk. Existing services are designed for firm-level compliance, not individual protection. The managing partner and the physician have personal interests that are not served by firm-level documentation.

**The position to claim:** The only service that produces personal evidence protection as a distinct deliverable — serving the ICP's individual liability exposure, not the firm's compliance posture.

---

### Missing Capability 04: Delegation Accountability Documentation System

**What the market offers:** Supervision frameworks that assume the ICP personally reviews every AI use decision, policy templates that describe what adequate supervision should look like.

**What's missing:** A delegation accountability documentation system — a method for making the invisible accountability chain visible and defensible, specifically designed for professional practices where the ICP cannot personally review every AI use event.

**Why it's missing:** The delegation problem is structurally resistant to existing compliance approaches. The existing solution is "supervise more thoroughly" — which is operationally impossible. The market has no solution for the ICP who is accountable for AI use they cannot enumerate, by people they did not directly manage, for records that bear their professional authority.

**The position to claim:** The only service that addresses the delegation accountability gap as a structural problem — documenting the delegation chain so that it becomes visible, enumerable, and defensible, rather than pretending the ICP can personally supervise every AI use event.

---

### Missing Capability 05: Vendor Contract Gap Analysis Against Professional Obligations

**What the market offers:** General data protection reviews, security assessments of vendor contracts, GDPR/FADP compliance documentation.

**What's missing:** A systematic review of vendor contracts against professional obligations (Art.321 CC, professional bar obligations, FMH standards) for small professional practices — not just data protection compliance, but professional accountability implications of vendor data processing relationships.

**Why it's missing:** The vendor contract review requires expertise in both professional obligations (what the ICP is accountable for under their professional framework) and vendor contract terms (what the vendor actually obligates the ICP to). This combination does not exist in any existing service. General data protection consultants don't know Art.321 CC. Legal firms don't know the operational context of independent practice AI tool use.

**The position to claim:** The only service that maps vendor contract terms against professional accountability obligations — identifying where the vendor's data processing creates exposure under Art.321 CC, professional bar standards, and personal liability frameworks.

---

### Missing Capability 06: Multi-Authority Documentation Mapping

**What the market offers:** Carrier-specific attestation preparation, single-regulator compliance documentation, single-authority guidance interpretation.

**What's missing:** A documentation architecture that simultaneously satisfies multiple authority demands — carrier + professional association + FINMA/FDPIC + litigation discovery — from a single evidence production system.

**Why it's missing:** Multi-authority documentation mapping requires understanding what evidence type satisfies which authority's demand and designing an evidence production system that generates those types continuously. This is a systems design problem, not a document production problem. Existing services produce documents for one authority at a time.

**The position to claim:** The only service that produces cross-regulator evidence architecture — documentation that works across carrier, professional association, supervisory authority, and litigation discovery simultaneously, so the ICP builds one evidence system that protects across all relevant authorities.

## The Unoccupied Cross-Vertical Position

**The category:** "Evidence Architecture for Professional Practices"

Not "AI governance consulting" — the market has governance consultants. Not "compliance documentation" — the market has compliance templates. Not "regulatory advisory" — the market has legal firms. The unoccupied position is evidence production — the continuous, contemporaneous documentation of what the ICP actually did with AI systems.

**Why it is unoccupied:** Evidence production is operationally intensive and requires a service model (retainer, ongoing) rather than a project model (document delivery). The market is organized around document-shaped solutions because documents are discrete deliverables with clear ownership. Evidence production is a continuous service that requires the provider to maintain an ongoing relationship with the ICP's documentation system.

**The cross-vertical advantage:** The evidence production methodology is the same across all four verticals — the structural problems are universal (delegation accountability gap, vendor contract alignment failure, continuous operation vs. periodic documentation mismatch). The regulatory framework is vertical-specific, but the evidence architecture is not. This means the same service model works across all four verticals with calibration for vertical-specific regulatory requirements.

**The naming opportunity:** "Evidence architecture" names exactly what the carrier form architecture is asking for — documented evidence of what the ICP actually did, not what they intended to do. The category is unoccupied. The first namer owns it across all four verticals.
