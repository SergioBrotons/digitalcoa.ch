## Cross-Vertical Market Signals — The Universal Problems

The market signals that appear across all four verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) reveal a consistent structural problem: documentation requirements are being imposed on ICPs who were never given a method for producing the required documentation. The gap is not about compliance intent — it is about evidence production infrastructure that does not exist.

---

## Signal 01: The Evidence Architecture Gap Is Universal and Structural

**What it is:** Every vertical is experiencing a divergence between policy documentation and evidence documentation. Carriers, clients, and supervisory authorities have moved from evaluating what firms say they do to evaluating what firms can demonstrate they did. The ICP in every vertical has produced policy documents and believes that is sufficient. The market has moved past policy evaluation.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** Carrier attestation forms are architected to collect evidence — representations about what the firm actually documented. Managing partners with AI governance policies on file are discovering that policies do not satisfy evidence requests.
- **Finance:** Institutional client DDQs are now scoring on evidence records — oversight logs, model validation documents, integration chain evidence. The institutional client has moved from evaluating AI governance frameworks to evaluating AI governance evidence capability.
- **Consulting:** Client AI governance questionnaires and carrier attestation requirements are asking for documented AI governance processes — not policy descriptions. The consulting firm that cannot produce evidence is losing client engagements.
- **Healthcare:** FMH human oversight requirements are being interpreted by carriers and courts as requiring documented evidence of actual oversight — not policy statements about what should happen.

**The market failure:** No existing service produces evidence as its primary output for any vertical. The market offers policies, frameworks, templates, and guidance documents — all of which describe what should exist. None of them produce what is actually being requested: contemporaneous records of what the ICP actually did.

**Why this is universal:** The evidence production problem is not specific to any vertical. It is a structural consequence of carriers and clients having learned that AI governance policies do not reliably predict AI governance capability. The shift to evidence-first evaluation is a market-wide behavior change that affects all four verticals simultaneously.

## Signal 02: The Personal Exposure Misunderstanding Is Universal and Multi-Layered

**What it is:** The ICP in every vertical believes their carrier coverage protects them from personal liability. In every vertical, the carrier coverage and the personal liability exposure are separate legal tracks — and carrier coverage does not discharge personal liability.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal (Art.321 CC):** The managing partner's personal signature on the carrier attestation form creates personal exposure to professional discipline and criminal liability. The carrier's E&O coverage does not protect the managing partner's personal exposure under Art.321 CC.
- **Finance (Art.63 nFADP):** The managing director who signs the carrier attestation is personally exposed under Art.63 for automated decisions that significantly affect clients. Carrier subrogation rights can pursue the managing director's personal assets if the attestation was inaccurate.
- **Consulting (Art.52 nFADP):** The managing partner faces direct personal administrative liability for data processing violations — fines up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations. The firm's professional liability insurance may not cover administrative fines imposed on individuals.
- **Healthcare (FMH + Art.321 CC + Art.63):** The physician's professional license is personally implicated by AI governance decisions. The carrier's coverage does not protect the physician's FMH standing or personal criminal exposure under Art.321 CC and Art.63.

**The market failure:** The misunderstanding is not being corrected by the market until an incident occurs. The ICP believes they are protected because they have coverage — they are not. The personal exposure is active and unaddressed. A service that specifically produces personal evidence protection for the ICP's individual liability exposure is missing from all four verticals.

**Why this is universal:** The structural separation of carrier coverage and personal liability is built into the Swiss regulatory framework. It applies to every professional who signs an attestation in their own name. The misunderstanding persists because no one explains it until after the problem is discovered.

## Signal 03: Vendor Contract Alignment Failure Is Universal Across All Verticals

**What it is:** The ICP accepted vendor terms of service without evaluating whether those terms are compatible with their professional obligations. The vendor's terms serve the vendor's risk management — they do not address the ICP's professional secrecy, data protection, or personal liability obligations. The ICP is contractually committed to obligations they never evaluated.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal (Art.321 CC vendor gap):** Attorney accepted EHR and AI vendor terms without reviewing whether data processing relationships are compatible with Article 321 professional secrecy obligations. No Geneva small firm has systematically reviewed vendor contracts against Art.321 CC.
- **Finance (Art.26 nFADP integration chain gap):** Wealth management firm accepted vendor terms for portfolio analytics, risk modeling, and client management tools without reviewing whether the data processing relationships satisfy Art.26 documentation requirements. The vendor integration chain is undocumented.
- **Consulting (sub-consultant AI delegation + vendor terms):** The consulting firm accepted AI tool vendor terms without understanding that sub-consultant AI use creates additional vendor relationships that are not covered by the firm's own vendor contracts.
- **Healthcare (Art.321 CC + vendor model training provisions):** The physician accepted AI tool terms that include provisions allowing vendor use of de-identified patient data for AI model training — provisions that create Art.321 CC and Art.63 exposure that the physician cannot eliminate without losing AI tool functionality.

**The market failure:** No existing service systematically reviews vendor contracts against professional obligations in any vertical. The service requires: mapping which vendor data processing relationships exist, identifying where vendor terms conflict with professional obligations, and producing documentation of the gap analysis. This is structurally absent from all four vertical markets.

**Why this is universal:** The vendor contract acceptance mechanism (click-through terms of service) is identical across all verticals. The professional obligation framework (Art.321 CC, Art.63, Art.52, FMH) applies to all four verticals. The intersection of vendor terms and professional obligations is a universal problem with a universal structural cause.

## Signal 04: The Delegation Accountability Gap Is Universal and Undocumented

**What it is:** The ICP is accountable for AI use by people and systems they did not directly control — staff, associates, sub-consultants, AI tools operating autonomously, and vendor product development decisions that changed what AI tools do. The accountability chain is operationally invisible. No documentation system captures it.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** Associates use AI tools not tracked by managing partner. Different practice areas adopt different tools. The managing partner's signature on matters incorporating AI-assisted work product is present, but the AI contribution to the work product is not documented.
- **Finance:** CIO manages AI vendor relationships. If the CIO leaves, institutional knowledge of AI governance documentation leaves with them. The firm's oversight capability is concentrated in one individual with no redundancy.
- **Consulting:** Sub-consultants use AI for research and analysis in client engagements. The managing partner is accountable for deliverables that incorporate AI they did not control and cannot enumerate.
- **Healthcare:** Staff use AI tools under physician supervision. The physician is accountable for decisions made by staff using AI tools the physician did not evaluate. The delegation chain is invisible and unmanaged.

**The market failure:** No existing service addresses the delegation accountability chain as a discrete problem. The market offers supervision frameworks that assume the ICP personally reviews every AI use — which is operationally impossible. The service that documents the delegation chain (making the invisible accountability structure visible and defensible) is missing from all four verticals.

**Why this is universal:** The delegation problem is a structural consequence of how AI tools are adopted in professional practices. The adoption happens at multiple levels simultaneously — by the ICP, by staff, by sub-consultants, by vendors through product updates. No one is managing the aggregate accountability. The problem exists in every professional practice regardless of vertical.

## Signal 05: The Multi-Authority Documentation Sequencing Problem Is Universal

**What it is:** The ICP faces documentation demands from multiple authorities simultaneously — carrier, bar association, FINMA, FDPIC, courts, clients. Each authority requests different documentation types. The documentation architecture that satisfies one authority may not satisfy another. No existing service maps which documentation simultaneously satisfies multiple authority demands.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** Carrier attestation evidence (self-reported, aggregate) ≠ Ordre disciplinary documentation (actual supervision process) ≠ litigation discovery (specific matter AI disclosure). Different authorities, different documentation types.
- **Finance:** FINMA guidance (governance capability evidence) ≠ carrier Form v4 (specific practice attestation) ≠ institutional client DDQ (oversight records) ≠ Art.63 personal due diligence (individual protection). Different audiences, different requirements.
- **Consulting:** Client questionnaire responses (commercial gate documentation) ≠ carrier attestation (insurance condition) ≠ Art.52 personal liability documentation (individual protection). The ICP faces three parallel documentation requirements with no unified framework.
- **Healthcare:** FMH human oversight documentation (professional standard) ≠ carrier attestation (insurance condition) ≠ Art.63 personal evidence (criminal standard) ≠ HIN compliance documentation (data protection). Four non-aligned standards, one practice.

**The market failure:** No existing service maps documentation that simultaneously satisfies multiple authority demands. The service that produces cross-regulator evidence architecture is missing from all four verticals. The ICP must either build separate documentation for each authority (operationally impractical) or find a unified evidence architecture.

**Why this is universal:** The multi-regulator environment is a consequence of Switzerland's sector-specific regulatory approach (no horizontal Swiss AI Act) combined with the EU AI Act's extraterritorial reach. The ICP is subject to multiple regulatory frameworks simultaneously, each with different documentation requirements. The problem is structural, not temporary.

---

## Signal 06: The Continuous AI Operation vs. Periodic Documentation Mismatch Is Universal

**What it is:** AI systems in all four verticals operate continuously — automated decisions are made, data is processed, AI tools function between human review cycles. The documentation cadence (annual, quarterly, periodic) does not match the AI systems' operational cadence. The gap between continuous operation and periodic documentation is the documentation debt that accelerates non-linearly.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** AI tools process client information continuously. Matter supervision is periodic. The firm's documentation of AI use in specific matters is retrospective, not contemporaneous.
- **Finance:** Automated rebalancing algorithms run daily. Risk modeling systems update continuously. The oversight documentation is annual or quarterly. The gap between system operation and documentation coverage is unbridgeable at the periodic review cadence.
- **Consulting:** AI tools used in client engagements operate between proposal and deliverable reviews. The consulting firm's documentation of AI contribution to deliverables is episodic, not continuous.
- **Healthcare:** AI interpretation layers operate on laboratory results and imaging before physician review. Clinical documentation records the physician's decision, not the AI interpretation that preceded it. The documentation gap is structural and invisible.

**The market failure:** No existing service addresses the operational mismatch between continuous AI operation and periodic human review. The service that defines what continuous documentation means operationally — and produces a system that captures AI activity between human review cycles — is missing from all four verticals.

**Why this is universal:** The mismatch is inherent in how AI systems operate (continuous, autonomous) and how human professionals document (periodic, event-triggered). The gap is not resolvable by working harder at the same documentation approach. It requires a different documentation architecture — one designed for continuous operation, not periodic review.

## The Universal Service Opportunity

All six market signals share a single structural root: the ICP has operational accountability for AI use but no evidence production infrastructure to document that accountability. The services that address each signal share a common architecture:

**Evidence architecture design** — documenting what the ICP actually did, not what they intended to do.

**Personal exposure documentation** — producing evidence that protects the ICP's individual liability position, distinct from firm-level compliance documents.

**Vendor contract gap analysis** — mapping where vendor terms conflict with professional obligations and documenting the gap.

**Delegation chain documentation** — making the invisible accountability structure visible and defensible.

**Cross-regulator documentation mapping** — producing documentation that satisfies multiple authority demands simultaneously.

**Continuous oversight methodology** — defining what ongoing documentation means operationally for continuously operating AI systems.

*The six universal market signals documented. All six appear across all four verticals. The universal service opportunity: evidence architecture + personal protection + vendor gap analysis + delegation chain documentation + cross-regulator mapping + continuous oversight methodology.*
