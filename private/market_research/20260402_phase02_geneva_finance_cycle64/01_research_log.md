## NET-NEW Finding: Swiss Bankers Association AI Position (C64)

**Source:** Swiss Bankers Association — Artificial Intelligence and Big Data page (swissbanking.ch)
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:15 UTC

### Key SBA Positions on AI (verbatim + paraphrased):

> "AI already indispensable" — Banks are using AI for:
- AI-supported transaction monitoring (anti-money laundering)
- Fraud detection and prevention
- Customer identification
- Process automation
- Efficient querying of in-house knowledge bases

> "Effective, innovation-friendly frameworks essential" — SBA calls for:
- Powerful, secure infrastructure
- High-quality, trusted data
- Sufficiently large talent pool
- Technology-neutral, principles-based laws and regulations
- Broad social acceptance

> "SBA supports Federal Council sector-specific approach" — SBA explicitly endorses:
- Federal Council's decision NOT to follow EU AI Act comprehensive framework
- Targeted, technology-neutral and principles-based approach
- Amendments to existing legislation rather than new AI-specific law
- SBA actively involved in shaping "secure and responsible use of AI in the financial sector"

### Interpretation for DigitalCoa.ch:
- SBA's public position confirms the Federal Council's sector-specific approach has industry buy-in from the Swiss Bankers Association
- **But:** SBA's endorsement of "principles-based approach" means individual Swiss FIs (including boutiques WMs) must do MORE self-governance work — no hard rules forthcoming
- Boutique WMs relying on "principles-based" = high compliance uncertainty
- SBA lists AI use cases (AML, fraud, customer ID, process automation) but does not address GPAI obligations, EU AI Act Annex III risks, or Section 301 forced labour supply chain dimensions
- **Gap:** SBA AI page has no mention of August 2, 2026 EU AI Act GPAI deadline, FADP DPIA obligations, or Section 301 ESG dimensions
