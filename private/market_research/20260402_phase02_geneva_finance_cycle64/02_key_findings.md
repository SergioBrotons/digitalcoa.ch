### Swiss Bankers Association (SBA) — Official AI Position: CONFIRMED

**Source:** swissbanking.ch/en/topics/digitalisation-innovation-and-cyber-security/artificial-intelligence-and-big-data  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:15 UTC

**SBA's AI stance — three key dimensions:**

**1. "AI already indispensable" — Current AI Use Cases in Swiss Banking:**
- AI-supported transaction monitoring (anti-money laundering)
- Fraud detection and prevention
- Customer identification (KYC AI)
- Process automation
- Internal knowledge base querying
- SBA frames these as "cautious approach" — implying maturity and risk awareness

**2. SBA Endorses Federal Council Sector-Specific Approach:**
- SBA explicitly supports NOT following the EU AI Act comprehensive framework
- SBA calls for "technology-neutral, principles-based laws and regulations"
- SBA advocates for amendments to existing legislation rather than new AI-specific law
- SBA is "seeking active involvement" in shaping AI frameworks — implies ongoing advocacy role
- SBA states EU AI Act as "comprehensive framework implemented in stages" — acknowledging it as the alternative model

**3. The Gap SBA Doesn't Address:**
- No mention of EU AI Act Annex III — portfolio AI tools, AVMs, credit scoring AI not discussed
- No mention of August 2, 2026 EU AI Act GPAI deadline
- No mention of FADP DPIA obligations for AI processing client financial data
- No mention of Section 301 forced labour ESG supply chain dimension
- No mention of FINMA Guidance 08/2024 expectations (not referenced anywhere)
- No mention of CARF/CRS convergence

**Implication for DigitalCoa.ch:** SBA's endorsement of "principles-based" approach creates a dangerous gap for boutique WMs. The fewer hard rules, the more self-governance burden — and boutique WMs typically have the least internal resources to execute that self-governance.
