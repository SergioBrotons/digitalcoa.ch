# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 02 Geneva Asset Management

## Research Question
What is the addressable market for strategic advisory targeting Geneva-based asset managers and family offices, particularly around the 2026 regulatory cascade?

## Key Findings (C97 — NET-NEW)

### Finding 1: H1 2026 represents the most significant regulatory volume for Geneva asset managers since FinSA/FinIA (2020)
Six major changes: CRS 2.0/CARF, FINMA Guidance 01/2026, FINMA Circular 2026/1, BFSA, LETA, operational resilience.

**Implication:** Asset managers are overwhelmed. External advisory to interpret, prioritize, and implement is in immediate demand.

### Finding 2: CRS 2.0/CARF creates urgent crypto tax reporting implementation need
Automatic exchange of information now includes crypto-assets. Implementation date: 1 January 2026. Asset managers with any crypto exposure (directly or via funds) need updated due diligence procedures and reporting infrastructure.

**Implication:** Most technically urgent 2026 change; compliance teams seek implementation guidance now.

### Finding 3: Crypto custody governance under FINMA Guidance 01/2026 requires new legal infrastructure
Custodians of crypto-based assets must provide bankruptcy-remote segregation. Foreign custodians require equivalent protection. Asset managers distributing crypto-exposed funds need updated custodian due diligence.

**Implication:** New legal due diligence market; few Geneva advisors are specifically addressing this.

### Finding 4: Nature-related risk (FINMA Circular 2026/1) is the emerging ESG compliance frontier
Not yet receiving same attention as climate risk, but FINMA expects systematic integration of nature-related risks into governance and risk management. Applies directly to ESG-mandated portfolios.

**Implication:** Early-mover advantage for advisors who can provide practical nature risk integration methodology.

### Finding 5: BFSA UK-Switzerland creates cross-border distribution opportunity
Mutual recognition of financial services between Switzerland and UK opens new distribution pathways for Geneva asset managers.

**Implication:** Short-term legal/compliance implementation opportunity; longer-term market access expansion.

### Finding 6: Competitive gap — no Geneva advisory firm is addressing the full 2026 regulatory cascade as a unified package
Big 4 are addressing individually; law firms address legal aspects; no bilingual executive-grade consolidated reference exists.

**Implication:** First-mover opportunity for DigitalCoa to position as the authoritative Geneva AM 2026 regulatory intelligence platform.
