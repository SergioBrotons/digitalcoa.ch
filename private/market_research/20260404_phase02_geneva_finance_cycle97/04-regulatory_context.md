## 2026 Regulatory Developments (NEW — C97 Priority)

### CRS 2.0 + CARF (1 January 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 1
- **What:** Switzerland adopts OECD Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework; CRS 2.0 expands automatic exchange scope
- **Who:** All financial institutions including asset managers with reportable accounts
- **Key change:** Crypto-assets now subject to automatic exchange of information; amended due diligence procedures required
- **Legal implication:** New client tax documentation requirements; crypto fund structures may need reporting infrastructure changes
- **Advisory opportunity:** CRS 2.0 gap assessment, CARF implementation framework, crypto fund tax documentation review

### FINMA Guidance 01/2026 — Crypto Custody (12 January 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 2
- **What:** Supervisory expectations for custody of crypto-based assets
- **Who:** Asset managers offering crypto-exposed funds or discretionary mandates with crypto exposure
- **Key requirement:** Custodians must provide bankruptcy-remote segregation; foreign custodians require equivalent protection
- **Legal implication:** Legal due diligence on crypto custodians; updated fund documentation for crypto-exposed strategies
- **Advisory opportunity:** Crypto custodian legal assessment, fund documentation review, custody agreement analysis

### FINMA Circular 2026/1 — Nature-Related Financial Risks (1 January 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 3
- **What:** Systematic integration of climate and environmental risks into governance and risk management
- **Who:** Primarily banks and insurance companies; portfolio-level implications for AMs with ESG mandates
- **Legal implication:** Nature risk assessment at portfolio level; ESG mandate compliance documentation; regulatory reporting
- **Advisory opportunity:** Nature risk integration framework for ESG-mandated portfolios; regulatory interpretation for AMs

### BFSA — UK-Switzerland Financial Services Agreement (1 January 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 4
- **What:** Mutual recognition of financial services between Switzerland and UK
- **Who:** Swiss asset managers with UK clients or UK distribution ambitions
- **Legal implication:** New cross-border legal frameworks; UK regulatory requirements must be mapped to Swiss requirements
- **Advisory opportunity:** BFSA distribution legal framework; UK compliance mapping for Swiss AMs; cross-border service agreements

### LETA — Beneficial Ownership Register (H2 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 5
- **What:** Federal register of beneficial owners of Swiss legal entities
- **Who:** Asset managers managing corporate vehicles, fund vehicles, or holding structures with corporate beneficial owners
- **Legal implication:** Must identify and report beneficial owners of corporate clients; due diligence procedures updated
- **Advisory opportunity:** LETA compliance gap assessment for AMs, beneficial ownership identification framework

### Operational Resilience — Fully In Force (1 January 2026) — NEW C97 Priority 6
- **What:** All transitional periods for FINMA Circular 23/01 concluded
- **Who:** All FINMA-supervised institutions
- **Legal implication:** Full compliance with technology risk, cyber resilience, and third-party governance requirements
- **Advisory opportunity:** Operational resilience legal framework assessment, third-party vendor contract review, technology risk governance
