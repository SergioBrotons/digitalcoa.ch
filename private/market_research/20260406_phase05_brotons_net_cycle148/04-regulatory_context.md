# 04 — Regulatory Context: Brotons.net (Content Platform)

## Content Platform Regulatory Landscape

### Publishing / Media Regulations
| Framework | Relevance to Brotons.net |
|---|---|
| Swiss press law | Minimal — online publication, no print |
| GDPR (nFADP) | Email list = subscriber data → privacy compliance |
| CAN-SPAM / EU ePrivacy | Email newsletter = direct marketing rules |
| Cookie / tracking regulations | Analytics + ads = cookie consent |
| Platform liability | User comments (if any) — limited liability |

### Content-Specific Considerations
- **No specific "content platform" regulation** in Switzerland
- **AI-generated content disclosure:** Emerging requirement in EU AI Act context
- **Defamation:** Swiss law — no US-style Section 230; publishers have higher responsibility for third-party comments
- **Copyright:** Cross-reference — must ensure original content or proper attribution

### Digital Platform Regulations
| Framework | Relevance |
|---|---|
| EU Digital Services Act (DSA) | If Brotons.net reaches EU audience >45M — unlikely at current scale |
| Swiss Digital Switzerland policy | Generally supportive of digital publishing |

## Compliance-Adjacent Considerations
- **Newsletter GDPR compliance:** Subscriber data must have clear consent, privacy policy
- **Analytics cookies:** If using ads or third-party analytics — cookie consent needed
- **AI content disclosure:** If using AI tools to assist writing — ethical disclosure

## Implications for Brotons.net Operations
1. **Email list management:** GDPR-compliant consent, easy unsubscribe, clear privacy policy
2. **Content originality:** Avoid copyright issues; no AI content flooding
3. **Comment moderation:** If open comments — moderation policy needed
4. **Analytics:** Cookie consent management if using Google Analytics or similar
5. **AI tool disclosure:** If using AI writing tools — transparency about it (trust signal)
