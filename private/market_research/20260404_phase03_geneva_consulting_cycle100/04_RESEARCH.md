## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 4)

### EN
**Geneva Family Business Succession — The "Silver Economy" Advisory Opportunity**

Swiss family business succession crisis — current scale:
- Statistics: ~600,000 SMEs in Switzerland; ~70,000 change hands annually (all types of transfer); Swiss Economic Institute (KOF) estimates 30-40% of Swiss SME owners are >55 years old; succession wave accelerating 2025-2035 as baby boomer generation exits
- Value at stake: ~CHF 100-150 billion in SME succession transactions annually in Switzerland; Geneva canton accounts for ~15-20% of Swiss SME value
- The "70% failure" myth vs. reality: most studies showing 70% of family business sales fail to meet owner objectives; actually, most family business transitions succeed operationally; the failure is in value optimization and emotional/relationship management
- Succession planning gaps: Swiss SME owners typically under-estimate time needed (2-5 years ideal planning horizon); 60% of owners have no formal succession plan; Geneva owners often prioritizing privacy over structured process
- Geneva-specific: concentration of family-owned commodity trading houses, watch industry SMEs, hospitality businesses, professional services firms; many second/third generation owners now facing succession decision
- Options framework: (1) Family succession — gift/sale to family member, often with vendor financing; (2) Management buyout (MBO/MBI) — management or external manager acquires; (3) Trade sale — sale to strategic buyer (private equity, competitor, multinational); (4) Partial exit — private equity minority stake
- Advisory role: succession readiness assessment, business valuation, family governance framework (family charter, family council), buy/sell agreement design, transaction structuring (asset vs. share deal), tax optimization (WZ, direct federal tax, cantonal), post-transaction integration
- Geneva business brokers/intermediaries: Alpharitt, Nellen, KPMG M&A advisory, PwC Deals, strategic boutique firms; advisory mandate often overlaps consulting with transaction execution

### FR
**Succession d'entreprises familiales genevoises — Opportunité consultative « économie grise »**

Crise de succession des entreprises familiales suisses — ampleur actuelle :
- Statistiques : ~600 000 PME en Suisse ; ~70 000 changent de mains annuellement ; Institut économique suisse (KOF) estime 30-40% des propriétaires PME suisses ont >55 ans ; vague de succession s'accélérant 2025-2035
- Valeur en jeu : ~CHF 100-150 milliards en transactions de succession PME annuellement en Suisse ; canton de Genève représente ~15-20% de la valeur PME suisse
- Lacunes de planification successorale : propriétaires PME suisses typiquement sous-estiment le temps nécessaire (horizon idéal 2-5 ans) ; 60% des propriétaires n'ont pas de plan de succession formel ; propriétaires genevois often prioritizing confidentialité
- Spécifique à Genève : concentration de maisons de commerce familiales, PME horlogères, entreprises hôtelières, firms de services professionnels ; многие второго/третьего поколения maintenant confronté à décision de succession
- Cadre des options : (1) Succession familiale — donation/vente à membre famille ; (2) Management buyout (MBO/MBI) ; (3) Vente stratégique ; (4) Exit partiel — stake minoritaire private equity
- Rôle consultatif : évaluation de préparation à la succession, valorisation, cadre de gouvernance familiale, conception buy/sell agreement, structuration transaction, optimisation fiscale, intégration post-transaction
