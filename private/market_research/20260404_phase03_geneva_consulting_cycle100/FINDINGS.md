## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: CFO-as-a-Service — Fractional Finance Leadership for Geneva SMEs
- ~50,000 registered companies in Canton Geneva; most are SMEs; growth-stage companies (Series A/B) lack full-time CFO
- Fractional CFO: CHF 2,000-4,000/day or CHF 8,000-20,000/month retainer for 5-10 days/month
- Significantly below full-time CFO cost (CHF 250,000-400,000/year)
- Target segments: Series A/B startups (investor reporting), family businesses in transition, PE-backed portfolio companies, non-profit/association finance
- Competitors: Big 4 (enterprise rates), boutique finance advisory firms, independent fractional CFOs
- Differentiation: Geneva sector expertise (commodity trading, luxury, pharma, fintech), bilingual EN/FR, international finance experience
- Advisory entry: positioning as fractional CFO provider, content marketing to startup ecosystem, IHEID alumni network leverage

### Finding 02: Post-DORA Cyber Resilience Consulting — Geneva Financial Sector Opportunity
- DORA (EU): in force Jan 2025; applies to Swiss financial firms with EU presence/clients; ICT risk management, incident reporting (4-hour window), TLPT testing every 3 years
- FINMA circular on ICT risk (FINMA Circus 2019/2 revision pending); Swiss institutions with EU cross-border activity must comply with DORA
- Key requirements: ICT governance, risk framework, incident reporting, resilience testing, third-party ICT risk management, information sharing
- Geneva exposure: banks with EU subsidiaries, asset managers marketing to EU investors (AIFMD), insurers with EU cross-border business
- Swiss NCSC reporting obligations; FINMA examination of ICT risk as part of SST
- Advisory: DORA gap assessment, ICT risk framework design, incident response plan development, TLPT coordination, third-party vendor management, board-level training

### Finding 03: Geneva Trade and Customs Consulting — EU Reform Creating Advisory Demand
- EU Customs Package (expected implementation 2025-2028): EU Single Window, ICS3 for security, updated valuation rules — affects Swiss EU-facing companies
- Geneva free trade zone (since 1849): warehouse regime for transit goods, no VAT within zone
- Geneva commodity trading: Trafigura, Vitol, Mercuria, Gunvor — world's 3rd largest hub after London/Houston; customs optimization = significant consulting opportunity
- Swiss FTAs: EU (bilateral), EFTA, 30+ non-EU countries; origin compliance documentation = ongoing administrative burden
- Strategic trade controls: dual-use goods, Swiss military goods regulations; export control compliance for Geneva tech
- Post-Brexit: UK-Swiss trade arrangements for Geneva commodity traders
- Advisory: EU customs reform compliance roadmaps, FTA origin optimization, customs warehouse structuring, strategic trade controls, tariff engineering

### Finding 04: Geneva Family Business Succession Crisis — "Silver Economy" Advisory Opportunity
- ~600,000 SMEs in Switzerland; 30-40% of owners >55 years; succession wave accelerating 2025-2035
- ~CHF 100-150 billion annual SME succession transactions in Switzerland; Geneva ~15-20% of value
- 60% of Swiss SME owners have no formal succession plan; planning horizon should be 2-5 years
- Options: family succession (gift/sale with vendor financing), MBO/MBI, trade sale (PE/strategic), partial exit (PE minority)
- Geneva-specific: family-owned commodity houses, watch industry SMEs, hospitality, professional services — many 2nd/3rd generation now facing succession
- Advisory: succession readiness assessment, business valuation, family governance framework (charter, council), buy/sell agreement, tax optimization, post-transaction integration

### Finding 05: Interim Management — Capacity Augmentation in Geneva
- Interim management: experienced executive placed temporarily (3-18 months) with operational authority; distinct from consulting
- Geneva interim CFO: CHF 2,500-5,000/day (most common assignment); financial restructuring, M&A, regulatory compliance projects
- Interim COO: CHF 2,500-4,500/day; interim HR Director: CHF 2,000-3,500/day
- Why use: leave coverage, sudden departure, transformation mandates, crisis management, M&A integration, startup scale-up
- Geneva interim companies: Top Management Partners, Kuhn & Zwack, Allen & York; specialized by sector
- Boutique consulting firm extension: interim management as premium add-on leveraging existing client relationships
- Differentiation: FR/EN/DE/IT language capability, commodity trading/pharma/finance sector expertise, international backgrounds
- Advisory entry: DigitalCoa.ch as interim CFO/COO provider; content on "when to hire an interim CFO" = highly searchable by Geneva company owners

### Finding 06: Geneva Management Consulting Competitive Landscape — Boutique vs. Big 4
- Big 4: dominant in audit/tax/regulatory compliance; PwC Geneva 500+ professionals; institutional/government consulting heavily concentrated at Big 4
- Boutique firms: strategy (Bovet Strategor), organizational (Mercuri Urval), sector specialists (commodity, pharma, fintech)
- "Best of" model: ex-Big 4 partners at boutique scale offering similar quality lower cost; growing in Geneva
- Pricing: Big 4 CHF 8,000-15,000/day; boutiques CHF 4,000-8,000/day; independent CHF 2,500-5,000/day
- Why clients choose boutiques: faster response, senior attention, sector expertise, confidentiality, language capability, flexible structures
- Why clients stay with Big 4: brand credibility with boards, global network, multi-service, formal procurement processes
- Independent positioning: trusted advisor model — long-term relationship, high trust, specialization; Geneva relationship-driven culture favors personal advisors
- Business model implications: DigitalCoa.ch as boutique advisory with clear sector positioning; thought leadership differentiation; relationship-first approach
