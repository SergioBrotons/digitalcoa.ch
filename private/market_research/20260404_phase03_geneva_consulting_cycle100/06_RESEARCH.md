## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 6)

### EN
**Geneva Management Consulting Competitive Landscape — Boutique vs. Big 4**

Geneva consulting market structure — 2025-2026:
- Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG): dominant in Geneva for audit/tax/regulatory compliance; large consulting practices within audit firms; PwC Geneva has 500+ professionals; government/institutional consulting contracts (UN, World Bank, cantonal government) heavily concentrated at Big 4
- Boutique consulting firms in Geneva: dozens of independent boutiques; strategy consulting (Bovet Strategor, Rev比例为, PLC Consulting), organizational (Mercuri Urval, Hudson), sector specialists (commodity trading advisors, pharma consulting, fintech consulting)
- "Best of" model: boutiques formed by ex-Big 4 partners who left to offer similar quality at lower price point; typically 5-20 person firms; leverage senior experience without Big 4 overhead; growing trend in Geneva
- Pricing differential: Big 4 strategy engagements in Geneva typically CHF 8,000-15,000/day for partner-led work; boutique firms at CHF 4,000-8,000/day for equivalent experience; independent consultants at CHF 2,500-5,000/day
- What drives Geneva clients to boutiques: faster response time, senior attention (no junior staffing), sector expertise, confidentiality (smaller firm = less conflict of interest), language capability (FR-native + EN-fluent), flexible engagement structures
- What keeps Geneva clients with Big 4: brand credibility with boards, global network, multi-service offerings, risk/audit integration, formal procurement processes of large institutions
- Independent consultant positioning: the "trusted advisor" model — long-term relationship, high trust, sector specialization, operational experience; Geneva's relationship-driven business culture favors trusted personal advisors over firms
- Geneva-specific competitive advantages: Geneva location premium for international organizations (UN, WTO, WHO, ICRC); French-language capability essential for cantonal government work; Swiss-German bridge capability valued for cross-border mandates
- Advisory business model implications: DigitalCoa.ch positioning as boutique advisory (vs. Big 4) with clear sector positioning (finance, legal, strategy); content differentiation on thought leadership; relationship-first approach aligned with Geneva business culture

### FR
**Paysage concurrentiel du conseil en management à Genève — Boutique vs. Big 4**

Structure du marché du conseil genevois — 2025-2026 :
- Big 4 (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG) : dominants à Genève pour audit/fiscalité/conformité réglementaire ; grandes pratiques de conseil au sein des cabinets d'audit ; contrats de conseil gouvernemental/institutionnel (ONU, Banque mondiale, gouvernement cantonal) fortement concentrés chez Big 4
- Cabinets de conseil indépendants à Genève : dizaines de boutiques indépendantes ; conseil en stratégie (Bovet Strategor, Rev比例为, PLC Consulting), organisation (Mercuri Urval, Hudson), specialists sectoriels (conseillers en commerce de matières premières, conseil pharma, fintech)
- Modèle « best of » : boutiques formées par anciens associés Big 4 offrant qualité similaire à prix inférieur ; firms de 5-20 personnes typiquement ; croissance de cette tendance à Genève
- Différentiel de prix : missions de stratégie Big 4 à Genève typiquement CHF 8 000-15 000/jour pour travail partner-led ; firms de conseil boutique à CHF 4 000-8 000/jour pour expérience équivalente
- Ce qui pousse les clients genevois vers les boutiques : temps de réponse plus rapide, attention senior (pas de staffing junior), expertise sectorielle, confidentialité, capacité linguistique (FR natif + EN courant)
- Modèle de positionnement du consultant indépendant : « conseiller de confiance » — relation à long terme, haute confiance, spécialisation sectorielle, expérience opérationnelle ; culture d'affaires relationnelle genevoise favorise conseillers personnels indépendants plutôt que firms
