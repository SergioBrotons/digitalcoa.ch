## The Competitive Landscape (C301 Update)

### The Market Has Changed in One Cycle

In C300, the white space was defined by the absence of services at the boutique scale. In C301, the white space is defined by the failure of existing services to address the C301-specific problem: evidence production, not policy documentation.

The competitive landscape in C301 has one dominant failure mode: services that produce documentation for the carrier's previous question, not the carrier's current question.

---

## The Six White Spaces (C301, NET-NEW vs. C300)

---

### White Space 1: Decision Attribution Infrastructure (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service builds decision attribution infrastructure — the documentation architecture that captures who made which AI decision in which client matter, at what time, with what authorization. This is distinct from policy documentation (C300's focus) and from processor chain documentation (C300's focus). This is specifically about the decision chain for AI-assisted professional work.

**What it is:** A decision attribution documentation system: (a) AI tool authorization record by matter, (b) AI-influenced decision log, (c) human review confirmation record, (d) client disclosure record by engagement. The system captures what actually happened — not what was intended to happen.

**Why it couldn't exist before:** Decision attribution infrastructure requires the firm to have already made authorization decisions. In C300, firms hadn't made those decisions. In C301, firms are being asked to demonstrate those decisions — but the decisions were never made. The service builds the infrastructure going forward and addresses the evidence gap for the period when decisions were being made but not captured.

**The structural analogue:** Medical practices needed this — physician oversight documentation that shows what was actually overseen, not just the oversight policy. Legal practices need this — attorney work product documentation that shows how AI was used in a specific matter. Finance practices need this — portfolio decision documentation that shows AI's role in specific decisions.

**Why it's a different service from policy documentation:**
- Policy documentation: "We have a policy that says AI tools must be authorized."
- Decision attribution: "Here is the authorization record for this specific AI tool for this specific matter on this specific date."

The carrier is asking for the second. The market is offering the first.

### White Space 2: Vendor Contract Exit Planning as Governance Architecture Prerequisite (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service treats vendor contract exit planning as a governance architecture prerequisite rather than a legal review exercise. The ICP needs to know what their current vendor contracts actually say about unilateral capability changes, model training data retention, data portability, and termination friction — before they can plan governance improvement.

**What it is:** A vendor contract assessment: (a) mapping of all AI tool vendor contracts against governance improvement scenarios, (b) identification of exit barriers that constrain governance options, (c) negotiation leverage analysis for contract improvement, (d) data portability assessment for governance-relevant data, (e) model training data retention audit for privacy exposure.

**Why it's a prerequisite, not an add-on:** The ICP who wants to improve governance by switching to a more compliant vendor cannot do so until they know whether their current contracts prevent it. The governance improvement plan that doesn't account for vendor contract constraints is incomplete.

**The market failure:** IT vendors and legal reviewers offer contract review as a legal exercise. Neither addresses the governance architecture implications of contract terms. Neither maps contract terms against governance improvement scenarios.

**The Geneva-specific opportunity:** Geneva professional firms have accumulated AI vendor contracts through years of click-through acceptance. The contracts are known quantities in terms of vendor relationship — but unknown in terms of governance implications. A service that maps contract terms against governance scenarios creates immediate client value.

### White Space 3: Evidence Infrastructure for Carrier Form v4 Demands (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service specifically addresses carrier form v4 demands — the current generation of carrier forms that ask for "last three authorization records," "last oversight review evidence," and "portfolio AI decision audit trail." These are evidence demands, not policy documentation demands.

**What it is:** An evidence infrastructure build: (a) authorization record template library for each AI tool category, (b) oversight review record template for medical and financial oversight requirements, (c) decision attribution log structure for consulting methodology provenance, (d) carrier form response preparation that produces evidence, not just policy summaries.

**Why it's distinct from policy documentation:** The market has policy templates. No Geneva market offers evidence infrastructure — the documentation architecture that produces the specific records that carrier form v4 asks for. The distinction is between "here is our policy" and "here are our last three authorization records."

**The market timing:** Carrier forms are in v4 now. The ICP who responds to v4 with policy documentation will be asked for evidence. The ICP who responds with evidence infrastructure gets the renewal.

---

### White Space 4: Peer Enforcement Response Service (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service exists for responding to peer enforcement events — when the ICP hears about a peer being questioned by the bar, fined, or losing a client, they need a specific service that addresses the peer's exact situation, not generic compliance guidance.

**What it is:** A rapid response briefing for peer enforcement events: (a) analysis of what happened to the peer, (b) identification of which specific behaviors and documentation gaps created the exposure, (c) specific actions to reduce similar exposure, (d) timeline for implementation before the next peer event. The service is designed for the 2-3 week window after a peer enforcement event becomes known in the Geneva professional network.

**Why the timing matters:** The peer enforcement signal creates maximum receptivity for 2-3 weeks after the event becomes known. After that, the memory fades and the urgency reduces. The service that arrives in that window with specific naming of the peer's exact situation converts at maximum rate.

**The Geneva-specific constraint:** Geneva professional networks are tight. When an attorney is questioned by the bar, every attorney in the network hears about it within days. When a wealth manager is fined, every wealth manager in the network knows within weeks. The peer signal has a short half-life before it becomes background noise.

**The service structure:** One-time rapid response engagement (CHF 1,800–2,200) designed to address the specific exposure before urgency fades. Followed by ongoing governance retainer if the ICP converts.

### White Space 5: Strategic Classification Decision Support (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service supports the ICP through the strategic classification decision — specifically helping them understand that deferring the decision is not risk reduction, it is risk accumulation, and that the documentation exposure from making the decision is less than the exposure from remaining in classification ambiguity.

**What it is:** A structured classification decision support session: (a) Swissdamed Tier 1/Tier 2 criteria analysis against the firm's actual AI tool use, (b) EU AI Act nexus determination, (c) documentation exposure analysis — what the firm will need to produce once the classification is made, (d) deferral cost analysis — what the exposure is during the deferral period, (e) classification decision record template that creates the documentation the firm needs while protecting their interests.

**Why it's distinct from Swissdamed registration services:** Vendor-driven Swissdamed registration services sell registration compliance. They have commercial interest in the firm being classified broadly (more vendors need registration). The independent classification decision support service is neutral — it helps the ICP make the decision that is proportionate to their actual exposure, not the decision that maximizes vendor registration revenue.

**The deferral cost framework:**
- Swissdamed: Operating AI tools without classification after July 1, 2026 creates regulatory exposure that the classification decision would have resolved.
- EU AI Act: Operating without nexus determination creates exposure for firms with EU client nexus.
- Carrier attestation: Operating without classification creates documentation gaps that carrier form questions will expose.

The deferral doesn't eliminate exposure. It converts regulatory exposure into documentation exposure plus regulatory exposure.

### White Space 6: Cross-Vertical Compliance Coherence Methodology (NET-NEW C301)

**The gap:** In C301, the ICP across all four verticals is managing multiple compliance tracks simultaneously — nFADP, Swissdamed, EU AI Act, carrier attestation, professional standards, client questionnaires — and finding that documentation built for one track is inconsistent with documentation built for another. No Geneva service addresses the coherence problem across multiple compliance tracks for a single firm.

**What it is:** A cross-compliance coherence framework: (a) mapping of all active compliance tracks to a single governance reality, (b) identification of cross-track inconsistencies, (c) documentation synthesis that satisfies multiple tracks simultaneously, (d) carrier form response preparation that accounts for cross-track coherence requirements, (e) ongoing coherence monitoring as the regulatory landscape evolves.

**The finance-specific application:** Finance ICPs are the most advanced case — managing FINMA Circular 08/2024 requirements, institutional client questionnaires, nFADP ROPA/DPIA, and carrier attestation simultaneously. The documentation tracks were built independently by different advisors. No one checks whether they're consistent.

**The consulting-specific application:** Consulting firms face the EU AI Act nexus determination, client methodology provenance requirements, and carrier attestation simultaneously. The methodology documentation for client work and the governance documentation for carrier attestation are structurally different — but need to be coherent.

**The healthcare-specific application:** Healthcare practices face FMH human oversight requirements, nFADP DPA obligations, Swissdamed classification requirements, and carrier attestation simultaneously. The oversight documentation for FMH and the DPA documentation for nFADP serve different purposes — but need to be coherent.

## What Exists vs. What's Needed (C301)

**What exists:**
- Enterprise AI governance platforms (wrong scale)
- Policy documentation templates (answers the old question)
- Carrier-provided guidance (circular, self-interested)
- Vendor compliance anxiety monetization (wrong alignment)
- Bar/SRO/FMH publications (principles without procedure)
- Legal contract review (wrong frame, wrong output)

**What's needed:**
- Decision attribution infrastructure (captures what actually happened)
- Evidence infrastructure (produces carrier form v4 responses)
- Vendor contract exit planning (governance architecture prerequisite)
- Peer enforcement response (converts urgency into engagement)
- Strategic classification decision support (breaks deferral pattern)
- Cross-compliance coherence methodology (manages multiple tracks simultaneously)

**The gap:** Geneva-calibrated, boutique-scale, independent advisory service that builds evidence infrastructure, not policy documentation. This is the specific market position that C301 cross-vertical synthesis identifies as the first-mover opportunity.
