# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal: Ideal Client Profile
## Cycle 20 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Small Law Firms (2–5 practitioners, Geneva)

---

## ICP: The Geneva Small Law Firm

### Demographics
- **Firm size:** 2–5 attorneys + support staff
- **Location:** Geneva canton (primary); Vaud, Zurich as secondary
- **Practice areas:** SMB-facing: SME corporate, real estate transactions, family law, employment, privacy/data protection, contract drafting, inheritance/estate planning
- **Revenue context:** CHF 400K–2M annual; price-sensitive; compliance burden increasing
- **Technology posture:** Active AI adoption — Microsoft 365 Copilot, ChatGPT, Clio, Westlaw Edge, AI document review
- **Decision-maker:** Managing partner or sole practitioner (owns compliance risk personally)

### Pain Points (Validated by Research)
1. **AI governance confusion:** No clarity on what obligations apply under nFADP Art. 5(c) and EU AI Act
2. **Client data exposure:** Using AI on client matters without formal disclosure = nFADP violation
3. **Timeline disorientation:** EU AI Act feels distant; FDPIC feels abstract; nFADP feels optional
4. **Competitive anxiety:** Larger firms building AI compliance; small firms unaware of gap
5. **No professional body signal:** SBA published AI guidelines June 2024 (national); ANBLES (Geneva) Feb 2026 report confirmed — neither specifically for small firms
6. **Attorney-client privilege tension:** AI document review risks breaching confidentiality if data leaves the firm

### Trigger Events
- **FDPIC enforcement on law firms** — NOT YET confirmed; legal sector absent from FDPIC 2024/2025 enforcement (1,053 complaints)
- **SBA AI guidelines June 2024** — national-level; provides baseline but no small-firm-specific guidance
- **ANBLES AI Working Group Report Feb 2026** — Geneva-level; confirmed via swlegal.com; content scope not small-firm-specific
- **Federal Council First Draft May 2026:** ~2 months — sector-specific approach confirmed (no cross-sector Swiss AI law)
- **CoE AI Convention integration:** End-2026 legislative consultation draft expected

### Timing Assessment (as of March 30, 2026)
- **Swissdamed Jul 1, 2026:** ~13 WEEKS — spillover to legal via healthcare AI clients
- **Federal Council First Draft May 2026:** ~2 MONTHS — sector-specific approach (NOT cross-sector law)
- **Federal Council formal legislation:** END-2026 — FDJP consultation draft for CoE AI Convention
- **EU AI Act high-risk obligations:** Aug 2, 2026 — ~4.5 MONTHS for EU-nexus firms
- **Advisory window:** ~12–18 months before competitive differentiation closes

### FDPIC Enforcement Gap (Cycle 20 Update)
**Confirmed absence — timing signal:**
- FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (Jul 1, 2025): 1,053 complaints, 363 breach notifications — legal sector ABSENT
- **2025-2026 enforcement actions:** Transport (BLT Baselland Transport Feb 2026), banking (Cembra Money Bank Jan 2025), retail/tech (Digitec Galaxus Feb 2026), supermarket (Coop Oct 2025), debt collection (Inkasso-Team Apr 2025), social media/X (Mar 2025)
- **FDPIC May 2025 guidance on AI:** Confirmed operative (applies to AI-based data processing under nFADP directly)
- **Legal sector:** NOT in complaints, consultations, or enforcement actions — timing window confirmed
- **Implication:** Federal Council sector-specific approach means FDPIC enforcement expands sector-by-sector; legal not yet in that sequence

### Competitive Landscape (10th+ Confirmation)
**No boutique competitor for small law firm AI governance:**
- Large firm AI practices (Homburger, Lenz & Staehelin, White & Case, CMS, Schellenberg Wittmer): enterprise-only, CHF 15K–50K+ mandates
- **FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL:** Boutique specializing in privacy, cybersecurity, AI governance for SMALL/MID-MARKET companies — closest to target segment; unique positioning confirmed Cycle 19
- VISCHER: data protection/FADP proceedings before FDPIC; NOT small-firm-focused
- MLL Legal, Walder Wyss, LEXR: recognized for data protection; not boutique for small firms
- SBA (Swiss Bar Association): AI guidelines published June 2024 — baseline, not small-firm-specific
- ANBLES (Geneva Bar): AI Working Group Report Feb 2026 confirmed (swlegal.com); content scope not small-firm-specific
- Geneva Bar / SBA: no Geneva-specific AI guidance for small firms as of March 2026

### Addressable Market
- **Geneva: ~350–450 small law firms** (2–5 attorneys)
- **Geneva + Vaud: ~550–700 small law firms**
- **Switzerland-wide: ~1,500–2,200 small law firms** in cantons with active AI adoption

### Objections & Responses
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "FDPIC won't come after a small firm" | The 1,053 complaints and zero legal enforcement = the window. Act before the sector sequence reaches professional services |
| "We don't use AI that much" | Microsoft 365 Copilot, ChatGPT, Clio AI, Westlaw Edge — if any are active, nFADP Art. 5(c) applies NOW |
| "SBA already published AI guidelines" | SBA June 2024 guidelines are baseline principles, not operational compliance frameworks for small firms |
| "ANBLES published a report in Feb 2026" | The ANBLES Feb 2026 report tracks developments; it did not publish operational guidance for small firm AI governance |
| "The EU AI Act doesn't apply to us" | If EU clients, EU-directed output, or EU AI system importers/distributors — it applies |
| "We already have data protection policies" | nFADP Art. 5(c) on AI transparency is specific and new; existing DPAs don't cover AI disclosure obligations |
