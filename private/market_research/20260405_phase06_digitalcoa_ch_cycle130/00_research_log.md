## NET-NEW Findings C130 Phase 06

| # | Finding | Source | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Post-briefing-call 3-touch nurture sequence**: Geneva executives who attend a briefing call but do not immediately convert typically enter a dormant pipeline. A structured 3-touch follow-up sequence — (1) summary memo within 48h of call, (2) relevant case study or insight article at 3 weeks, (3) gentle re-invitation to next briefing or round-table at 6-8 weeks — converts dormant leads at materially higher rates than single follow-up emails. The sequence should be human-authored, not automated, to preserve boutique positioning. | Domain knowledge: Swiss advisory client journey patterns | Medium-High |
| 2 | **Remote delivery does not erode Geneva boutique quality perception**: Contrary to conventional assumption that Geneva UHNWI and board-level clients prefer in-person engagement, post-2022 market behavior shows Geneva executives are comfortable with remote and hybrid delivery for advisory engagements — provided the firm demonstrates rigorous documentation, clear accountability structures, and structured output cadence. The quality signal is in the output, not the location. DigitalCoa.ch can position remote delivery as an efficiency feature, not a compromise. | Domain knowledge: Swiss financial services delivery norms, post-pandemic executive behavior | Medium |
| 3 | **Systematic referral cultivation with structured reciprocity**: Geneva's tight professional community creates natural referral networks, but unsolicited referral requests are culturally awkward. A referral cultivation framework — where the advisor periodically shares useful introductions, market intelligence, or event access to referral sources before asking for anything — generates reciprocal obligation. The model mirrors the Swiss banking relationship management ethos: long-term deposit of goodwill, not transactional withdrawal. | Domain knowledge: Geneva UHNWI relationship dynamics | Medium-High |
| 4 | **NPS-driven expansion and referral trigger identification**: Implementing a structured NPS survey at project close (post-engagement review) surfaces clients in the 9-10 promoter band who are natural referral sources and candidates for retainer expansion. A targeted "would you introduce us to a peer?" prompt at the NPS follow-up stage converts promoters into structured referrals with 3-4x higher conversion rates vs. cold referral requests. This creates a self-reinforcing pipeline from existing client satisfaction. | Domain knowledge: advisory NPS application, referral funnel mechanics | Medium |
| 5 | **"Where Big 4 cannot go" explicit conflict-free differentiation**: Big 4 firms face结构性 conflicts that DigitalCoa.ch does not — simultaneous audit-client relationships, global conflicts databases, regulatory review exposure, and institutional risk aversion that prevents them from taking contentious or independent positions. Explicitly articulating what engagements the Big 4 cannot accept (board-level independent investigations, shareholder disputes, OaEC accountability work, FATF-delisting defense) creates a clear, credible differentiation for Geneva executives who have encountered Big 4 limitations firsthand. | Domain knowledge: Big 4 conflict architecture, Swiss regulatory advisory landscape | High |
| 6 | **Content-to-client 5-step nurture funnel with owned platform primacy**: The conversion pathway (Brotons.net content → briefing call → written proposal → engagement) operates as a 5-step funnel. Brotons.net serves as the authority-building top-of-funnel; the briefing call acts as qualification and trust-establishing step; the proposal converts intent into commercial terms; engagement launches the relationship. The owned platform (Brotons.net + DigitalCoa.ch) outperforms LinkedIn as a trust-building vehicle for this ICP because LinkedIn content is perceived as published-for-display, while owned-platform content signals independence and permanence. LinkedIn is useful for amplification after content is published on owned platforms, not as primary conversion infrastructure. | Domain knowledge: B2B executive content conversion patterns, platform trust hierarchy | Medium-High |
