## Situation Assessment — Geneva Asset Managers / Family Offices

Geneva's wealth management ecosystem faces a convergence of regulatory pressures: Basel IV capital requirements tightening, FINMA AI governance expectations hardening post-survey, AMLD6 alignment creating UBO documentation burden, and Pillar 2 global minimum tax reshaping holding structures. Simultaneously, tokenization infrastructure (DLT Act, SDX) is maturing, creating a new advisory frontier for early movers.

Geneva-based asset managers and family offices are operating in a more complex regulatory environment than 3 years ago. The average Swiss financial institution now has 5 AI applications in production and 9 in development — a fragmented AI landscape that FINMA explicitly flags as a supervisory concern.

### Finding 02: FINMA AI Governance — From Survey to Supervisory Expectation
- FINMA survey (50% AI adoption, 91% GenAI) is now the baseline for supervisory expectations
- Top concerns: data protection, IT security, data management, enterprise risk — all require governance frameworks
- FINMA Circular 2011/7 outsourcing rules apply to AI vendor relationships; AI compliance models must be auditable
- Swiss Banking industry statement: AI can augment but NOT replace human judgment in AML
- **Advisory angle:** AI governance framework design, vendor risk assessment, AML AI model validation, FINMA examination prep

### Finding 03: OECD Pillar 2 / Global Minimum Tax — Geneva UHNWI Holding Structures
- Swiss MTAA (Minimum Tax Act) in force 2024; 15% minimum effective tax rate for groups with CHF 750M+ revenue
- UHNWI family holding structures (Swiss AG, foundation, Liechtenstein Anstalt) require substance review
- Geneva cantonal tax rulings — Pillar 2 limits aggressive ruling practices; existing rulings under scrutiny
- Families below threshold indirectly affected via investment structures and foundations
- **Advisory angle:** Holding structure substance review, cantonal ruling renewal, foundation classification, investment entity analysis

### Finding 04: Russia / Sanctions — Geneva Private Banking Compliance Burden
- SECO maintaining independent Swiss Russia sanctions; EU packages 1–11 adopted with modifications
- FINMA circular: enhanced due diligence for private banking Russia exposure
- Geneva private banks with historical Russia mandates under ongoing wind-down pressure
- Sanctions compliance cost: dedicated resources, legal review, asset freeze monitoring
- **Advisory angle:** Independent sanctions compliance review, enhanced AML framework for Russia-adjacent mandates, UBO identification remediation

### Finding 05: Swiss DLT Act / Tokenization — Institutional Infrastructure Maturing
- DLT Act operational since 2021; SIX Digital Exchange (SDX) institutional-grade tokenized securities platform active
- FINMA DLT trading facility licensing pathway established; first licenses granted
- Federal Council stablecoin guidelines targeting finalization 2026
- Geneva AMs exploring tokenized real-world assets (RWA): private credit, Geneva real estate, art, commodities
- **Advisory angle:** DLT strategy and opportunity assessment, stablecoin compliance when finalized, tokenization governance, RWA tokenization feasibility

### Finding 06: 13th AHV Pension / BVG — Retirement Planning Integration
- 13th AHV pension approved; annual December payment starting 2026 — benefits all AHV recipients including wealthy UHNWI
- BVG reform rejected September 2024; no imminent 2nd pillar structural change
- Pillar 3a retroactive top-up allowed from January 2025 for contribution gaps
- BVG minimum interest rate maintained at 1.25% (2025); 2026 figures unchanged vs. 2025
- Wealth planning integration opportunity for Geneva family offices managing holistic client portfolios
- **Advisory angle:** AHV 13th pension planning, Pillar 3a optimization, holistic retirement income planning integration
