## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Basel IV / FAIRC — RWA Floor Effect on Geneva Counterparties
- 72.5% RWA floor confirmed for Swiss banks using internal models; revised Pillar 2 (SREP) capital surcharges increasing
- Geneva asset managers exposed via counterparty bank capital deterioration and custody concentration risk
- Most Swiss banks completing Basel IV transition 2023–2025; full capital effects now visible
- FINMA SREP process applying higher supervisory expectations for system-relevant institutions
- Geneva IAMs with single-custodian relationships face concentration risk as bank capital buffers compress
- **Advisory angle:** Counterparty resilience assessment, custody diversification strategy, Basel IV capital efficiency analysis

### Finding 02: FINMA AI Governance — From Survey to Supervisory Expectation
- FINMA AI Survey (Nov 2024–Jan 2025): 50% Swiss FIs using AI; 91% GenAI; avg 5 apps in use + 9 in development
- Survey = implicit supervisory baseline; FINMA expects firms to have AI governance frameworks addressing top concerns
- Key concerns: data protection, IT security, data management, enterprise risk — all require documented governance
- FINMA Circular 2011/7 (outsourcing) applies to AI vendor relationships; critical functions at risk if AI vendors fail
- Swiss Banking statement: AI can augment but NOT replace human judgment in AML/KYC — human-in-loop is regulatory requirement
- **Advisory angle:** AI governance framework design, AI vendor risk assessment, AML AI model validation, FINMA examination preparation

### Finding 03: OECD Pillar 2 / Global Minimum Tax — Geneva UHNWI Holding Structures
- Swiss MTAA (Minimum Tax Act) enacted; effective 2024; 15% minimum effective tax for groups with CHF 750M+ global revenue
- Threshold captures significant Geneva-based multinationals, family offices with qualifying investment structures
- UHNWI holding structures (Swiss AG, cantonal foundations, Liechtenstein Anstalt, Luxembourg SCSP) require substance review
- Geneva cantonal tax rulings — Pillar 2 constrains aggressive ruling practices; existing rulings may face scrutiny
- Families below threshold indirectly affected via investment entity classification and foundation structures
- **Advisory angle:** Holding structure substance review, cantonal tax ruling renewal, foundation tax classification, investment entity analysis

### Finding 04: Russia / Sanctions — Geneva Private Banking Ongoing Compliance Burden
- SECO maintains independent Swiss Russia sanctions; EU packages 1–11 adopted with Swiss modifications
- FINMA circular on Russia: enhanced due diligence requirements for private banking mandates with Russia exposure
- Geneva private banks and GFIs continuing to wind down Russia-related mandates and asset freeze compliance
- Sanctions compliance cost: dedicated legal/compliance resources, ongoing UBO identification, transaction monitoring
- UBO remediation for complex Geneva structures with Russia-connected beneficial owners remains acute
- **Advisory angle:** Independent sanctions compliance review, enhanced AML framework for Russia-adjacent mandates, UBO remediation

### Finding 05: Swiss DLT Act / Tokenization — Institutional Infrastructure Maturing
- DLT Act (2021) operational; SIX Digital Exchange (SDX) institutional tokenized securities platform active
- FINMA DLT trading facility licensing pathway established; first licenses granted to Swiss entities
- Federal Council stablecoin guidelines targeting finalization 2026 — will create compliance requirements for stablecoin custody/usage
- Geneva asset managers exploring tokenized real-world assets: Geneva real estate, private credit, art, commodities
- Tokenized share/register: DLT Act enables uncertificated digital securities — reduces administrative overhead for AMs
- **Advisory angle:** DLT/tokenization strategy assessment, stablecoin compliance readiness, RWA tokenization governance, DLT fund structure review

### Finding 06: 13th AHV Pension / BVG — Retirement Planning Integration Opportunity
- 13th AHV pension approved; annual December payment from 2026 — applies to all AHV beneficiaries including UHNWI
- BVG reform rejected September 2024 referendum — no imminent 2nd pillar structural change; current BVG rules maintained
- BVG minimum interest rate: 1.25% (2025); 2026 key figures unchanged vs. 2025
- Pillar 3a: retroactive top-up payments allowed from January 2025 for contribution gaps (BVV 3 amendment)
- 13th AHV creates holistic retirement planning conversation for Geneva family offices managing comprehensive client wealth
- **Advisory angle:** AHV 13th pension planning integration, Pillar 3a optimization, holistic retirement income strategy, client education
