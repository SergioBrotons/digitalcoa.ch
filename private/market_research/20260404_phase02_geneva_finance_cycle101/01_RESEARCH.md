# 01 — ICP: Geneva Asset Managers & Family Offices (C101)

## Source: Domain knowledge + official open sources
## Date: 2026-04-04

## Target Segment: Geneva-Based Asset Managers / Wealth Managers

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** CHF 500M–10B+ AUM (mid-to-large); single family offices typically CHF 100M–1B
- **Revenue:** Management fees + performance fees; revenue correlates directly with AUM
- **Locations:** Geneva primary (Lac Léman corridor: Geneva–Lausanne); some Zug for crypto/alternative
- **Clientele:** UHNWI, family offices, institutional investors, pension funds (for larger AMs)
- **Structure:** Independent asset managers (IAMs), multi-family offices (MFOs), single-family offices (SFOs), bank-affiliated

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CIO / Investment Director | Senior executive, often founder or long-tenured | Portfolio analytics, benchmark attribution, risk reporting |
| CEO / Managing Partner | Business leader | Client reporting, operational scalability, regulatory compliance burden |
| COO / Operations | Non-investment executive | Middle/back office efficiency, data management, vendor oversight |
| Risk Manager | Dedicated role (larger firms) | Regulatory risk reporting, stress testing, ESG risk |
| Client Reporting Lead | Relationship manager + operations | Custom client reporting, PDF generation, client portal management |

### ICP Validation Notes (C101 Update)
- Geneva private banking/investment management remains attractively positioned for advisory
- Regulatory burden (FINMA, SFAMA, AIFMD-equivalent Swiss rules) creating ongoing compliance overhead
- Technology gap persists — smaller IAMs underserved by legacy systems
- Family office segment (SFO/MFO) growing in Geneva; regulatory awareness increasing
- SNB held rates at 0.25% (March 2026); rate environment stable but SARON-based products remain norm

### Buying Triggers (C101)
- AUM growth requiring operational infrastructure upgrade
- FINMA examination findings
- New regulatory requirement (Basel IV capital, DLT Act compliance, ESG disclosure)
- Key personnel departure
- Client demand for better reporting

### Buying Barriers (C101)
- Risk aversion toward cloud/proprietary systems
- Data privacy concerns (client data in external systems)
- Long sales cycles (3–12 months for strategic advisory)
- Decision-makers often owners with strong views

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva IAMs and family offices
- English essential for international investor-facing roles
- Relationship-driven, trust-first approach
- Formal but collegiate tone
