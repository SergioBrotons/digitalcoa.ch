## Regulatory Framework — Key Developments for Geneva Asset Managers / Family Offices

### Priority 1: Basel IV / FAIRC — Capital Requirements Tightening
- **Framework:** FAIRC ordinance (Financial Institutions Amendment), implementing Basel IV in Switzerland
- **RWA Floor:** 72.5% for banks using internal models — increases capital requirements for most Swiss banks
- **Timeline:** Phased implementation 2023–2025; full effects by 2025
- **FINMA role:** Supervises implementation via SREP (Supervisory Review and Evaluation Process)
- **Impact on Geneva AMs:** Indirect via counterparty bank capital; direct for AMs with banking licences
- **Advisory angle:** Capital structure optimization, counterparty risk assessment, RWA efficiency

### Priority 2: FINMA AI Governance — From Survey to Supervisory Expectations
- **Source:** FINMA AI Survey (Nov 2024–Jan 2025): 50% Swiss FIs using AI; 91% use GenAI
- **Key findings:** Avg 5 AI apps in use + 9 in development; fragmented landscape
- **Top concerns:** Data protection, IT security, data management, enterprise risk
- **FINMA expectation:** AI governance frameworks required; models must be auditable and explainable
- **Outsourcing risk:** FINMA Circular 2011/7 applies to AI vendor relationships; critical functions at risk
- **AML:** Swiss Banking statement — AI can augment but NOT replace human judgment
- **Advisory angle:** AI governance framework design, vendor assessment, FINMA examination preparation, AML AI model validation

### Priority 3: AML / AMLD6 Alignment — Ongoing Enforcement
- **Source:** MONEYVAL 2023 evaluation of Switzerland — cited AML compliance gaps
- **AMLD6:** Switzerland partially aligned; 10% UBO threshold creates documentation burden
- **Geneva exposure:** Private banking onboarding, due diligence, enhanced scrutiny on complex structures
- **KYC automation:** 2–3 day onboarding vs. 2–3 weeks previously (RegTech-enabled)
- **AMLD6 effect:** UBO remediation for Geneva structures with Liechtenstein, Luxembourg, Cayman exposure
- **Advisory angle:** AML program design, UBO remediation, enhanced due diligence framework, RegTech vendor selection

### Priority 4: OECD Pillar 2 — Global Minimum Tax Structuring
- **Source:** Swiss MTAA (Minimum Tax Act) — in force 2024
- **Threshold:** CHF 750M+ global revenue for multinational groups
- **Rate:** 15% minimum effective tax rate
- **Family office impact:** UHNWI holding structures may need substance review
- **Geneva:** Cantonal tax rulings — Pillar 2 limits aggressive ruling practices
- **Advisory angle:** Holding structure substance review, cantonal ruling applications, investment entity classification

### Priority 5: DLT Act / Tokenization — Regulatory Infrastructure
- **Framework:** DLT Act (in force 2021); Federal Council stablecoin guidelines (targeting finalization 2026)
- **SDX (SIX Digital Exchange):** Operational tokenized securities platform
- **FINMA:** DLT trading facility licensing pathway established
- **Asset manager angle:** Tokenized real-world assets (private credit, art, real estate); DLT fund structures
- **Advisory angle:** DLT strategy, tokenization opportunity assessment, stablecoin compliance

### Priority 6: Russia / Sanctions — Ongoing Compliance Burden
- **SECO:** Independent Swiss Russia sanctions regime; adopted EU packages 1–11
- **Geneva exposure:** Private banks with historical Russia mandates under ongoing pressure
- **FINMA:** Enhanced due diligence circular on Russia exposure
- **Winding down:** Client mandates, asset freeze compliance, UBO identification
- **Advisory angle:** Sanctions compliance review, enhanced AML review for Russia-adjacent mandates
