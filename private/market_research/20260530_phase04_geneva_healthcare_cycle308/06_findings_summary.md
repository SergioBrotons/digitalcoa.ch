## Key Insight: The Efficiency-Liability Paradox Is the Core Tension

In every other vertical (legal, finance, consulting), AI efficiency gains reduce institutional risk. In healthcare, the physician's personal liability structure inverts this.

The physician's documented personal judgment is the asset. Every AI delegation replaces a documented physician action with an undocumented AI output — reducing the physician's documented evidence trail while their personal liability for the decision remains unchanged.

**The service does not ask the physician to use less AI.** It asks the physician to document their human authorization for each AI-assisted decision — so the efficiency is preserved and the liability exposure is contained.

### Opening Question

### On FMH Human Oversight
"FMH says human oversight is required. What they haven't published is what adequate oversight looks like in a small practice. I can give you a documented protocol — specific steps, recorded at the time, that constitute adequate review."

### On the Efficiency-Liability Paradox
"Every time you sign an AI-assisted letter, your efficiency increases and your documented oversight decreases. I'm not asking you to use less AI. I'm asking you to document your human authorization — so the efficiency gain doesn't come with a liability increase."

### On Swissdamed Operator Obligations
"The vendor's Swissdamed registration covers the tool in the market. Your operator obligations cover how you use it in your practice. Those are different things. Let me show you the five-point operator checklist."

### On Staff AI Use
"You told your staff not to use AI on patient data. Do you have that in writing? Do you have a way to verify they're following it? Because if they don't — and something goes wrong — the decision runs to your license."

### On Carrier Attestation
"The form your carrier sent is a personal certification. When you signed it, you certified that your practice has adequate AI governance. If an AI-related incident occurs, that form is evidence — for or against you. Let me walk through exactly what you certified."

### On Post-Incident Documentation
"There is no standard medical record protocol for AI-assisted decisions in Swiss primary care yet. That window is open for about six months. The physicians who establish their own protocol first are protected. Everyone else is waiting for FMH or Swissmedic to publish one — at which point the gap becomes an audit."
