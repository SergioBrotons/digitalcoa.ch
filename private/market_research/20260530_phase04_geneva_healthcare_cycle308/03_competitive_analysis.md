## What's Missing (The White Space)

### The Geneva Small Practice Healthcare AI Governance Advisory — Unclaimed

**What no competitor provides:**
1. Personal physician accountability positioning (not institutional, not practice-level)
2. FMH human oversight operationalization (practical protocol, not principle)
3. Swissdamed operator obligation clarity for the individual physician
4. Carrier attestation preparation with physician-personal certification framing
5. Staff AI use accountability framework for small practice owners
6. Post-incident documentation protocol specific to Swiss primary care

**Why it remains unclaimed:**
- Tool vendors cannot credibly offer governance advisory (conflict of interest)
- Large consultancies (Deloitte, KPMG, PWC) price for enterprise, not SMB
- Swiss Cyber Institute is closest in positioning but enterprise + cybersecurity framing misses the clinical accountability dimension
- Geneva boutique advisors have not identified healthcare SMB as a distinct vertical with personal liability positioning
- FMH and AMGe publish guidance but do not offer individual advisory services

## The Specific Competitive Gap: Personal License Accountability

**Why the gap persists:**

The market has not connected:
- **AI governance** (enterprise IT/security framing)
- **Small practice** (medical assistant shortage, efficiency pressure, personal liability)
- **Personal physician accountability** (license risk, signature authority, FMH oversight obligation)

Most AI governance advisors pitch institutional risk. The Geneva healthcare ICP faces personal license risk — a fundamentally different value proposition.

**The positioning that works:**
Not "AI governance for your practice." Not "protect your patients' data."
It is: "Document your human oversight so your license is protected when it matters."

## Competitive Response Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Large Consultancy Launches SMB Healthcare AI Advisory
- **Likelihood:** Low-Medium (12–24 month runway)
- **Response:** First-mover content advantage; relationship depth; peer-referral network established
- **Structural moat:** Healthcare regulatory knowledge + Geneva SMB relationships + bilingual capacity

### Scenario 2: AI Tool Vendor Adds Governance Layer
- **Likelihood:** Low-Medium
- **Response:** Conflict of interest framing ("a vendor assessing their own tool"); physician skepticism
- **Structural moat:** Independent positioning; no tool conflicts

### Scenario 3: FMH or AMGe Publishes Practical SMB Protocol
- **Likelihood:** Low (12+ months, if ever)
- **Response:** Practical protocol content; still requires individual implementation support
- **Structural moat:** One-to-one advisory capacity; FMH guidance is general, not case-specific

### Scenario 4: Another Geneva Boutique Enters Healthcare SMB
- **Likelihood:** Medium
- **Response:** Differentiation on personal accountability framing; evidence architecture methodology
- **Structural moat:** Physician-credible expertise; bilingual; Geneva-centric; SMB-specific
