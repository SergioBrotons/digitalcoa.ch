## Competitive Landscape Language Map

### What the Market Says It Needs

**Law firms (Geneva):**
- "We need an AI policy" — firm-level document, wrong specification for carrier questions
- "We have disclosure in our engagement letters" — general clause, not AI-specific architecture
- "Our associates use AI but we don't track it" — authorization chain gap visible but unnamed
- "The carrier form is just a checklist" — comparison trap; form is design specification
- "We're comparing our AI governance to other firms" — peer standard, not carrier standard

**Wealth managers (Geneva):**
- "FINMA says we need human oversight" — principle without operational definition
- "The institutional DDQ is a compliance exercise" — misreads competitive intelligence function
- "Our carrier renewal is routine" — attestation accuracy gap unacknowledged
- "We have documentation for our AI tools" — policy document, not evidence of authorization
- "Our clients ask about AI governance" — client questions activating commercial document gap

**Consulting boutiques (Geneva):**
- "Client questionnaires ask about AI" — commercial gate, not just compliance
- "Our proposal methodology is our IP" — exposure in three directions unnamed
- "We sub-consultants for execution" — delegation chain invisible, carrier attestation signed with no monitoring
- "Our engagement letter predates AI tools" — commercial terms structurally misaligned

**Physicians (Geneva):**
- "FMH says human oversight is required" — stated without protocol
- "Swissmedic operator status unclear" — vendor certification doesn't clarify obligations
- "We use AI but it's not documented" — retroactive gap acknowledged
- "Efficiency and liability move together" — paradox without resolution
- "Staff use AI on patient data" — invisible delegation, personal liability unnamed

## Market Language Evolution: C308 → C310

**C308 language:** "Do I have a problem?" (discovery mode)
**C309 language:** "Why didn't my solution work?" (implementation failure mode)
**C310 language:** "My solution was built to the wrong specification." (structural failure mode)

The language shift marks a market maturation:
- ICPs have tried frameworks, policies, general disclosure clauses
- The carrier form revealed the specification was wrong
- Market now uses "form" language, not "policy" language
- "Matter-level" and "authorization chain" entering ICP vocabulary
- "Retroactive reconstruction" becoming recognized need

## Language Gap: What the Market Cannot Name

**Unnamed in market language:**
1. **Evidence architecture** — the unnamed category; market says "documentation" but means evidence of authorization for specific decisions
2. **Authorization chain** — the invisible liability; ICPs experience it as "my carrier is asking questions about things I didn't know about"
3. **Non-linear documentation debt** — the structural problem; ICPs say "we have years of undocumented AI use"
4. **Matter-level specification** — the form question type shift; ICPs say "we have a firm-level policy but the carrier asks about specific matters"
5. **Commercial document AI clause** — the structural gap; ICPs say "our engagement letter predates AI tools"
6. **Comparison trap** — the false adequacy mechanism; ICPs say "we look similar to other firms our size"

## Positioning Language: What DigitalCoa Says vs. What Market Needs

**DigitalCoa positioning:** "The market offers frameworks. I produce evidence. Your form is the design specification."

**Market need (C310):** Something that:
- Answers the carrier form question, not a general policy question
- Provides retroactive reconstruction, not just forward documentation
- Covers the authorization chain, not just the firm's AI tools
- Works at matter-level, not just firm-level
- Includes an AI clause architecture for commercial documents
- Is proportionate for a small practice, not enterprise-grade

**The gap:** No competitor speaks to this combination. The market offers frameworks (language: "policy", "governance", "oversight", "compliance"). The market does not offer evidence architecture (language: "authorization chain", "matter-level", "retroactive reconstruction", "carrier form specification").

## ICP First-Contact Language

**How ICPs describe their situation:**

*Law firms:* "The carrier sent us a form with my name on it. I don't know what they're asking. I signed it last year. They sent it again. I don't know if I should sign it differently."

*Wealth managers:* "We have an institutional client that's asking about AI governance. I don't know what to put in the response. The DDQ has specific questions. I don't have evidence for most of them."

*Consulting boutiques:* "Our client sent us a questionnaire about AI. It's in our proposal now. I don't know if what we're saying is what they're actually asking. And I don't know if it's true."

*Physicians:* "I use AI for clinical reasoning. I don't know if I'm documenting it correctly. I don't know if what I'm doing is what Swissmedic means by 'human oversight.' I don't have a protocol."
