## Competitive Claims Analysis

### Category 1: AI Governance Frameworks

**What competitors claim:**
- "Comprehensive AI governance framework for your firm"
- "Ready-to-implement AI policies and procedures"
- "Compliant with Swiss and EU AI regulations"
- "Industry-specific AI governance solutions"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Firm-level policy document (PDF, 20-40 pages)
- General principles, not matter-specific questions
- Forward-only documentation, no retroactive reconstruction
- English-only, no EN/FR bilingual design
- One-time deliverable, no ongoing evidence maintenance

**The gap:**
- Carrier form asks matter-level questions → framework provides firm-level answers
- Carrier renewal asks for evidence of authorization → framework provides policy statement
- Retroactive documentation debt → framework has no retrospective component
- Bilingual Geneva requirement → framework is English-only

**C310 signal:** ICPs who implemented frameworks 12-18 months ago now face carrier attestation renewal with documentation that doesn't answer the questions being asked.

### Category 2: AI Compliance Platforms

**What competitors claim:**
- "Automated AI compliance for your business"
- "Real-time AI monitoring and documentation"
- "One-click carrier attestation completion"
- "AI governance software for SMEs"

**What they actually deliver:**
- Self-service questionnaire tool
- Policy template library
- Annual review cycle
- English-only interface
- Generic Swiss regulatory summary

**The gap:**
- Carrier attestation requires specific evidence → platform provides general compliance guidance
- Real-time AI use requires continuous documentation → platform provides periodic checkboxes
- Matter-level documentation → platform provides firm-level dashboard
- Solo practitioner doesn't have time for platform onboarding → no proportionate entry product

**C310 signal:** Self-service platforms fail at the solo practitioner level. The ICP who needs to reconstruct 18 months of undocumented AI use cannot do it through a self-service tool. They need an operator.

**The gap:**

## The Pre-Signing Intervention Gap

**No competitor offers:**
- Pre-signing carrier form review as a defined service
- The carrier form read as market intelligence document
- Matter-level evidence gap analysis before signature

**What the market needs:**
The ICP receives a carrier form with their name on it. They have 2-3 weeks to sign and return it. They don't know:
- What the carrier is actually asking
- What evidence they need to have before signing
- What happens if they sign without adequate documentation
- Whether their signature creates documented personal liability

**The intervention:** Pre-signing carrier form review. Bounded, specific, high-value. The ICP sends the form. Sergio reads it, identifies the specific evidence gaps, provides a memo describing what needs to be documented and in what form.

**This is the highest-value intervention point in the market and zero competitors have built it.**

## The Retroactive Reconstruction Gap

**What competitors claim:** "AI governance framework" / "Compliance checklist" / "Policy template"

**What the market needs:** Evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions made over the past 12-24 months.

**The structural mismatch:**
- Competitors offer forward-only documentation
- The market's primary problem is retrospective
- No competitor has a retroactive reconstruction methodology

**The evidence architecture is designed to handle both:**
- Forward documentation (ongoing)
- Retrospective reconstruction (backward-looking)

**This is the category-defining gap. The market has a problem no competitor solves.**

## Broker Channel: Unaddressed by All Competitors

**What competitors do:** Direct outreach to ICPs (cold email, LinkedIn, website SEO)

**What the market actually is:**
- Geneva professional services operates on broker referral
- Broker (insurance broker, business broker, accountant, other trusted advisor) is the primary trust intermediary
- ICP calls someone they trust first when they receive a carrier form

**No competitor has built:**
- A broker referral program
- A broker briefing format
- A broker co-branding approach
- A broker referral tracking system

**The broker circuit is the competitive moat no one has built.**

## Bilingual EN/FR Gap

**What competitors claim:** "Swiss expertise" / "Geneva-based" / "Local knowledge"

**What they actually deliver:**
- English-only documents
- English-only service delivery
- English-only marketing
- No French regulatory document capability

**What the market needs:**
- Carrier forms in English → responses may need French regulatory context
- French regulatory proceedings → documentation in French
- Client communication in French or English as appropriate
- EN/FR as design requirement, not translation task

**No competitor offers:**
- Standard bilingual EN/FR evidence architecture
- French regulatory document preparation
- Bilingual carrier form response design

**This is a structural service design gap, not a localization task.**
