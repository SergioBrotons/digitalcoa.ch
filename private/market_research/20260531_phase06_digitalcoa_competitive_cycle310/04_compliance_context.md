## The Regulatory Landscape

### 1. Carrier Attestation Forms (Primary Design Specification)
**What they are:** De facto documentation standards created by insurance carriers
**What they require:** Evidence of actual practice, not policy existence
**Who reads them:** No competitor — this is the primary white space
**Key insight:** The carrier form is the design specification. It exceeds any published regulatory standard in operational specificity. Reading it tells you exactly what evidence the carrier wants.

###2. Swiss Professional Bar Associations (ORDRE/SBA)
**What they require:** Professional responsibility compliance, human oversight obligations
**What they publish:** Principles without operational definitions
**Gap:** No Geneva-calibrated, SMB-specific operational protocol exists
**Key insight:** The bar association guidance functions as demand generation for whoever fills the implementation gap.

### 3. FINMA (Financial Market Supervisory Authority)
**What they require:** Adequate AI governance for supervised institutions
**What they publish:** Principles-based guidance without SMB-specific operational definition
**Key insight:** Institutional client DDQ standards exceed FINMA requirements. The competitive gate is set by the DDQ, not by FINMA.

### 4. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection)
**What it requires:** Lawful data processing, data minimization, documented basis for processing
**What it creates:** Personal liability exposure for managing professionals (Art. 52, Art. 63)
**Key insight:** nFADP creates personal criminal exposure that carrier coverage does not fully address. The managing professional's personal liability is separate from corporate liability.

### 5. EU AI Act (August 2026)
**What it requires:** Documentation, transparency, human oversight for AI systems
**What it creates:** Extraterritorial obligations for Swiss firms serving EU clients
**Key insight:** EU AI Act creates documentation obligations that non-EU AI vendors cannot satisfy on behalf of Swiss deployers. The obligation is the deployer's; the vendor cannot provide the required documentation.

### 6. Swiss Federal Council AI Consultation (End 2026)
**What it will produce:** Framework for Swiss AI governance
**What it creates:** Anticipated regulatory convergence
**Key insight:** The regulatory environment is intensifying, not stabilizing. The service model should be ongoing, not one-time.

## The Institutional Pressure Layer

### Institutional Client DDQs
**What they require:** Specific evidence of AI governance, not policy existence
**What they function as:** Competitive intelligence documents and commercial gatekeepers
**Key insight:** No one reads the institutional DDQ as competitive intelligence. The DDQ tells you exactly what the institutional client wants. Reading it is the primary intelligence activity.

### Carrier Form Evolution
**What happened:** Carrier forms evolved from firm-level to matter-level questioning
**What it means:** Policy-level documentation cannot answer matter-level questions
**Key insight:** The carrier form changed the question type. Most documentation was built to answer the old question.

### Broker Intermediary Standards
**What they require:** Named referral language, specific service design, professional presentation
**What they create:** Primary trust intermediary channel for SMB clients
**Key insight:** The broker referral channel is structurally unaddressed by all competitors. First-mover advantage is available and defensible.

### What Competitors Actually Deliver
- Policy documents (not evidence)
- Framework templates (not evidence architecture)
- Generic guidance (not Geneva-calibrated)
- Enterprise-oriented services (not SMB-calibrated)
- English-only content (not bilingual)

### What No Competitor Delivers
- Evidence of actual practice (not policy existence)
- Retroactive reconstruction (not forward-only)
- Authorization chain documentation (not generic framework)
- SMB-calibrated evidence architecture (not enterprise framework)
- Bilingual EN/FR evidence production (not translation)
- Pre-signing carrier form review (not post-signing remediation)

## The Regulatory Convergence Insight

The regulatory environment is not stabilizing — it is intensifying. Multiple regulatory pressures are converging simultaneously:

1. Carrier attestation standards escalating faster than peer norms
2. Institutional DDQ standards exceeding regulatory floors
3. EU AI Act creating extraterritorial obligations
4. Swiss Federal Council AI consultation creating anticipated domestic framework
5. nFADP creating personal liability exposure

The service model must reflect sustained demand, not one-time compliance. The retainer model is the correct response to a sustained intensifying regulatory environment.
