### 1. SBA AI Guidelines (June 2024) — CONFIRMED BASELINE OBLIGATION
**Source:** LexTech Institute / Swiss Bar Association
- SBA Council adopted AI use guidelines for lawyers in June 2024
- These are the formal professional standards for AI use by Swiss attorneys
- **Implication:** Geneva law firms should already have AI governance policies aligned with SBA guidelines — this is an existing obligation, not a future one
- This closes the question: "what AI governance standard should Geneva lawyers follow?" — they should follow SBA guidelines, which are already in effect

### 3. FADP Art. 21 Enforcement Framework — Active + Criminal Penalties
**Source:** FDPIC / multiple legal sources
- FADP applies directly to AI systems (FDPIC confirmed September 2025)
- Art. 21: right to human review of automated decisions; transparency obligations
- Exemptions: contract conclusion where request granted; explicit consent
- Criminal penalties: up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations
- DPIA required for high-risk AI applications
- **Implication:** Law firms using AI for client matter management, document analysis, billing — all potentially in scope for Art. 21

### 4. Federal Council AI Legislation Timeline — Legislative Process to Begin End-2026
**Source:** Federal Council / multiple legal sources
- Federal Council assessment of AI regulatory approaches due end-2024 (now complete)
- Legislative process to begin end-2026
- Ratification of CoE AI Convention planned
- Current gap: FADP applies; no sector-specific AI law yet for legal sector
- **Implication:** August 2, 2026 EU AI Act GPAI deadline is the binding immediate deadline for Geneva law firms with GPAI systems

### 5. Section 301 — Specific Hearing Dates + Legal Sector Exposure
**Source:** HK Law / USTR / admin.ch
- Section 122 10% global tariff: active (150-day limit, until ~late June 2026)
- Section 301 Investigation B (forced labor): hearings April 28, 2026
- Section 301 Investigation A (manufacturing overcapacity): hearings May 5, 2026
- Overcapacity sectors include: aluminum, automobiles, batteries, cement, chemicals, electronics, energy goods, glass, machine tools, machinery, semiconductors, steel, transportation equipment
- **Geneva law firm exposure:** Law firms with manufacturing, trade, or supply chain clients in affected sectors face indirect forced labor documentation demand; Swiss companies with US business face tariff risk

### 6. Swiss-EU Bilateral Package — Now in Parliament
**Source:** admin.ch Federal Council April 1, 2026 press release
- Package adopted by Federal Council April 1, 2026 and submitted to Parliament
- Optional treaty referendum
- No AI-specific provisions in the package
- Multi-framework burden confirmed as structural baseline
