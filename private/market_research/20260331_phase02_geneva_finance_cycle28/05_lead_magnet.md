# The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist

*A 9-section framework for Geneva wealth managers navigating CARF, AMLA, FATF, and AI compliance*

---

## Why This Checklist Matters Now

**CARF is already live.** Since January 1, 2026, the CARF reporting framework has been operative. If your firm uses AI-assisted tools for client communication, portfolio reporting, or compliance workflows, CARF obligations already apply — there's no grace period for AI-assisted reporting that doesn't meet the standard.

**76% of Geneva financial services SMEs already use AI.** (PwC 2024) That's the highest adoption rate of any sector in our research. The governance gap — between AI use and formal policy — is widest here.

**The FATF evaluation is approaching.** Geneva wealth managers face an upcoming FATF mutual evaluation (2026–2027/2028). This is not theoretical — FATF evaluations have resulted in countries being placed on grey lists for far less. Your AML/compliance infrastructure will be examined. AI governance is now part of that infrastructure.

**FDPIC enforcement is building.** The Federal Data Protection Commissioner has opened multiple AI-related investigations in 2025–2026. The pattern is sector by sector. Wealth managers — with their client data, communication records, and AI-assisted workflows — are a natural next target.

---

## Section 1: CARF + AI — What's Already Live

CARF (Common Audit File for Switzerland) became operative January 1, 2026. If your firm uses AI tools to:
- Generate client portfolio reports
- Draft compliance correspondence
- Analyze client communication for AML purposes
- Produce regulatory filings

...then AI-assisted output is subject to CARF obligations. The question is not whether AI can be used — it's whether the AI-assisted output meets CARF standard.

**Check:** Do you have a documented process for AI-assisted CARF-relevant outputs that includes human review and sign-off?

---

## Section 2: Shadow AI Audit — Know What You Already Have

Your advisors are likely using AI tools right now. Some may be using unapproved tools on client matters. This is your highest-immediate-risk item.

**Shadow AI = FDPIC exposure + FINMA compliance exposure + professional liability**
- Advisor uses unapproved AI on client data → firm's obligations potentially breached
- No formal AI policy = no defense: "we didn't know"
- Managing director can face personal criminal liability (up to CHF 250,000) for intentional FADP failures

**Check:** Can you list every AI tool currently used by your team on client matters?

---

## Section 3: AMLA/LETA H2 2026 — 3–6 Month Window

The revised AMLA and LETA implementing ordinance becomes operative H2 2026 (~3–6 months from now). For wealth managers, this means:
- Expanded due diligence obligations
- 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register
- SRO membership obligations for certain activities (real estate transactions, domicile services, establishing non-operational entities)

**Check:** Does your AI governance framework account for AML/KYC as an AI use case with heightened obligations?

---

## Section 4: FATF Evaluation as External Pressure

The FATF mutual evaluation of Switzerland (2026–2027/2028) will examine Switzerland's AML/CFT framework at the national level — and by extension, the compliance infrastructure of regulated entities including wealth managers.

A poor FATF evaluation has real consequences: grey listing, increased scrutiny, reputational damage. Regulators typically respond to negative evaluations by tightening supervision of regulated entities.

**Check:** Is your AI governance framework aligned with the FATF's expectations for technology governance in financial services?

---

## Section 5: EU AI Act — 16 Month Clock

If your firm has any EU nexus — EU clients, EU-domiciled assets, or work product circulating in EU markets — the EU AI Act high-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026.

For wealth managers, this could include:
- AI systems used for credit/risk assessment (if applicable)
- AI systems operating EU-intended outputs
- AI systems as intermediaries in EU financial services

**Check:** Does any practice area, client segment, or matter type create EU nexus? If yes, EU AI Act obligations apply in ~16 months.

---

## Section 6: DPIA — When Is It Required?

A Data Protection Impact Assessment is required under FADP for high-risk AI processing. For wealth managers, high-risk AI use cases likely include:
- AI-assisted client suitability analysis (personal data + financial data)
- AI tools processing client communication for compliance monitoring
- AI systems handling third-party personal data (counterparties, beneficiaries)

**Check:** Have you conducted a DPIA for AI use cases that process client personal data?
