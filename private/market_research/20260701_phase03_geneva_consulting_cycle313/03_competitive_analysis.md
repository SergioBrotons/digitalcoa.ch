## 6 NET-NEW Competitive Findings (Distinct from C214–C313 Phase 02)

### Finding 01: RFP Form-Completion Vendor Market Gap — Auditing the New Competitive Entry

**Mechanism:** The most significant C313 competitive shift in the boutique consulting market is the entry of RFP form-completion vendors — third-party tools offered to boutique consulting firms to "simplify" client RFP response. These vendors process proposal methodology, sub-consultant engagement terms, prior deliverable samples, AI tool inventory through their own AI. Established RFP-completion vendors in this space include Loopio, Responsive, RFPIO, and a growing set of AI-native proposal-assistance tools. The vendor market is operating on click-through terms the boutique has not evaluated. The exposure created by the form-completion vendor is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 carrier form-completion finding and the C313 Phase 02 DDQ form-completion finding, but applied to the client RFP context. No Geneva competitor is auditing which form-completion vendor the boutique is using, what data that vendor processes, whether its terms create nFADP Art.52 exposure, and whether the response it produces is generic (form-completion vendor output) or anchored to the boutique's specific proposal methodology documentation.

The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish an RFP form-completion vendor audit methodology owns the category. The service includes: vendor terms review, data flow analysis, response generic-vs-anchored assessment, alternative direct-RFP-response methodology. The service also addresses the client methodology confidentiality exposure — the form-completion vendor processes client methodology through undisclosed AI infrastructure.

**C313 Phase 01 + Phase 02 bridge:** Phase 01 named the carrier form-completion vector; Phase 02 named the DDQ form-completion vector as the parallel WM manifestation through the institutional client channel; Phase 03 names the RFP form-completion vector as the parallel consulting manifestation through the client RFP channel. Same exposure mechanism, different document, different vendor category, different client relationship.

**Market signal:** Boutique consulting firms using RFP form-completion vendor tools are submitting proposals that are structurally indistinguishable from each other — the client's procurement committee reads past the generic language, and the boutique's specific proposal methodology is invisible. The boutique's mandate is lost because the proposal is generic.

**IMO differentiation angle:** RFP form-completion vendor audit + alternative direct-proposal methodology — only Geneva service auditing which RFP form-completion vendor the boutique uses and providing the direct-response alternative anchored to the boutique's own proposal methodology documentation.

### Finding 02: Geneva Consulting Mid-Engagement Follow-Up Cycle Market Activation — Documentation Cadence Service Gap

**Mechanism:** The mid-engagement client follow-up cycle is now activating for Geneva boutique consulting firms. The cadence is multi-per-engagement — proposal submission at month zero, mid-engagement follow-ups at month three, six, nine, and engagement renewal at month twelve. The boutique's documentation cycle is engagement-letter-centric (annual at most). No Geneva competitor has built a mid-engagement documentation refresh service for the client RFP context. The service gap is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 mid-year carrier check-in documentation refresh finding (legal) and the C313 Phase 02 mid-year institutional questionnaire documentation refresh finding (finance), applied to the consulting engagement context.

The mid-engagement follow-up asks "what changed in last 90 days" — boutique's proposal methodology documentation is 6-9 months old and cannot answer the question. The boutique's engagement renewal is at risk at every mid-engagement follow-up moment. The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish a mid-engagement follow-up documentation refresh methodology owns the C313 mid-engagement category.

**C313 Phase 01 + Phase 02 bridge:** Phase 01 named the mid-year carrier check-in cadence for legal; Phase 02 named the mid-year institutional questionnaire cadence for WM; Phase 03 names the mid-engagement follow-up cadence for consulting. Same mechanism (multi-per-period cadence vs. annual documentation cycle), different document, different forcing function.

**Market signal:** Boutique consulting firms in mid-engagement follow-up moments are most likely to need immediate documentation refresh support and most likely to find that current market offerings require longer engagement timelines than the mid-engagement follow-up cycle allows.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Mid-engagement follow-up documentation refresh — only Geneva service providing quarterly proposal methodology documentation refresh calibrated to the consulting engagement mid-cycle cadence.

### Finding 03: New Sub-Consultant Authorization Handover Service — Consulting-Specific Permanent-Delegation Analog

**Mechanism:** The Geneva boutique consulting market has a structural service gap in sub-consultant authorization chain documentation. When a boutique engages a sub-consultant, the senior partner authorizes the engagement through the engagement letter signature. The senior partner does NOT authorize the sub-consultant's AI tools. The sub-consultant's AI use on the client deliverable is invisible to the senior partner. The sub-consultant's tools are not in the boutique's AI inventory. The sub-consultant's data flows are not in the boutique's nFADP ROPA. The sub-consultant signs nothing acknowledging Art.52 exposure. No Geneva competitor has built a sub-consultant AI authorization documentation service for the boutique consulting context.

The service is the consulting-specific parallel to the C313 Phase 01 summer associate authorization handover documentation (legal, employment-cycle boundary) and the C313 Phase 02 new institutional mandate authorization discontinuity (finance, mandate-cycle boundary), applied to the consulting sub-consultant context. The structural difference: sub-consultant engagement has NO employment-cycle boundary and NO mandate-cycle boundary. The handover window is permanent. The sub-consultant's AI tools may change weekly. The sub-consultant may engage and disengage multiple times during a single client engagement. The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish a sub-consultant AI authorization documentation methodology owns the C313 sub-consultant category.

**C313 Phase 01 + Phase 02 bridge:** Phase 01 named the summer associate authorization discontinuity for legal; Phase 02 named the new institutional mandate authorization discontinuity for WM; Phase 03 names the sub-consultant authorization discontinuity for consulting. Same mechanism (new "arrival" creates authorization gap), different "arrival" type (associate vs. mandate vs. sub-consultant), different handover window (annual vs. mandate-cycle vs. permanent).

**Market signal:** Geneva boutique consulting firms engaging sub-consultants in 2026 are creating documented Art.52 personal exposure gaps that are permanent and never reflected in the boutique's AI inventory. The mid-engagement follow-up 30-90 days later will surface the gap.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Sub-consultant AI authorization documentation — only Geneva service producing authorization chain documentation refreshed at the rate of sub-consultant engagement book change, with documented sub-consultant AI tool disclosure, sub-consultant data processing agreement terms, and sub-consultant supervision architecture for both engagements and disengagements.

No Geneva competitor is auditing proposal management vendor sub-processor chains against nFADP Art.52 requirements and client confidentiality terms.

The service is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 Swiss-hosted vendor sub-processor audit finding (Article 321 legal) and the C313 Phase 02 portfolio construction vendor sub-processor audit finding (Article 9 finance), applied to the proposal management vendor context with its specific Art.52 obligations and client methodology confidentiality complexity. The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish a proposal management vendor sub-processor audit methodology owns the C313 vendor review category for consulting.

Same mechanism (Swiss residency doesn't satisfy data protection / professional secrecy obligation), different obligation framework (legal professional secrecy vs. financial data protection vs. consulting client methodology confidentiality + personal Art.52 exposure).

**Market signal:** Boutique consulting firms with "Swiss-hosted" proposal management vendors have a documented nFADP Art.52 misconception that is now visible to clients through RFP vendor processor questions and to regulators through Art.52 personal exposure documentation.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Proposal management vendor sub-processor audit — only Geneva service mapping proposal management vendor sub-processor chains against nFADP Art.52 requirements and client methodology confidentiality obligations and producing audit memos that satisfy client RFP vendor processor questions.

### Finding 06: Proposal Management Vendor Succession Service Gap — Client Methodology Continuity

**Mechanism:** The Geneva boutique consulting market has a structural service gap in proposal management vendor succession / data portability planning. No Geneva competitor is building proposal management vendor succession methodology that addresses: pre-vendor-selection succession terms in vendor contracts (client methodology export obligations, transition period, controller-change disclosure); mid-vendor-relationship succession monitoring (vendor financial signals, acquisition signals, service-termination signals); post-vendor-exit response (data portability execution, controller-change documentation, successor-controller audit, client notification architecture).

The service is structurally parallel to the C313 Phase 01 vendor succession / data portability finding (legal, Article 321 obligations) and the C313 Phase 02 portfolio construction vendor succession finding (finance, Article 9 + institutional client contractual obligations), applied to the proposal management vendor context with its specific client methodology confidentiality complexity and Art.52 personal exposure. The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish a proposal management vendor succession methodology owns the category.

**C313 Phase 01 + Phase 02 bridge:** Phase 01 named the vendor succession gap for legal (Article 321 obligations); Phase 02 named the portfolio construction vendor succession gap for WM (Article 9 obligations + institutional client contractual obligations); Phase 03 names the proposal management vendor succession gap for consulting (Article 52 obligations + client contractual confidentiality obligations). Same mechanism (vendor exit creates data exposure the boutique is personally accountable for), different obligation framework (legal professional secrecy vs. financial data protection vs. consulting client methodology confidentiality + Art.52 personal exposure).

**Market signal:** Proposal management AI vendors are entering acquisition / consolidation phase in 2026. The boutique consulting senior partner has no succession planning methodology and no contractual terms addressing vendor exit. When vendor exit happens, the client methodology transfers to a successor controller without boutique or client consent.

**IMO differentiation angle:** Proposal management vendor succession / data portability planning — only Geneva service building the pre-vendor-selection succession terms, mid-vendor-relationship monitoring, and post-vendor-exit response architecture for proposal management AI vendor relationships with client methodology exposure and Art.52 personal liability.
