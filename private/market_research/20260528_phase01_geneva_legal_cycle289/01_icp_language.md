# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Small-Law Attorneys Talk About AI Problems

*Phase 01 · Cycle 289 · 2026-05-28*

---

## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

**The entry conversation is about liability, not efficiency.**

> *"I'm an attorney. My name is on the brief. The AI wrote it. Who is responsible?"*

This is the irreducible form of the Geneva small-law AI accountability problem. The attorney speaks in first person singular with personal accountability attached to outputs that are not produced through their own cognitive process. The ICP does not say "we need an AI strategy." They say "I need to know what I'm signing."

---

## English — How the ICP Speaks

### On Carrier Attestation

The ICP encounters carrier AI attestation as an acute, personal event:

> *"My PI carrier sent a questionnaire with an AI section. It asks me to attest personally to our AI governance. I don't know what I'm attesting to. I signed it anyway because I had to."*

Key language markers: **personally**, **attesting**, **didn't know what**, **had to sign**. The personal pronoun dominates. Carrier attestation is experienced as personal exposure, not compliance exercise.

---

### On Attorney-Client Privilege in AI Contexts

> *"My client calls me and asks if we're using AI on their matter. I don't know what to tell them. I don't know what the AI tool does with their data after we're done."*

> *"I read the vendor terms. It's not clear. I can't tell if their data训练 the model. I can't tell if it gets stored in Luxembourg or Dublin."*

> *"What if the AI tool gets breached? What is my obligation to notify the client?"*

Key language markers: **I don't know what to tell them**, **can't tell**, **what if**, **obligation to notify**. The ICP frames privilege concern as vendor data governance uncertainty, not abstract confidentiality principle.

---

### On Bar Guidance Vacuum

> *"I called the bar. They said they don't have guidance on AI. They said it's the attorney's responsibility."*

> *"The SBA published something on AI. It's principles. I don't know what 'adequate supervision' means in practice for my firm."*

> *"Has the Ordre des Avocats said anything official? Nothing I can cite."*

Key language markers: **they don't have guidance**, **it's the attorney's responsibility**, **principles without practice**, **nothing I can cite**. The vacuum is experienced as institutional abandonment, not information gap.

---

### On Associate AI Use and Supervision

> *"My junior associates use AI constantly. I can't evaluate the work they produce because I don't know what the AI does. How do I supervise something I don't understand?"*

> *"They produce good-looking documents. I don't know if they thought it through or just ran it through ChatGPT."*

Key language markers: **I can't evaluate**, **I don't know what the AI does**, **good-looking documents**, **thought it through**. The supervision problem is framed as cognitive authority gap, not process gap.

---

### On Personal Exposure

> *"My name is on the brief. My bar number is on the document. If something goes wrong, it's my license on the line."*

> *"What happens in a bar disciplinary proceeding if AI produced the reasoning and I signed off on it?"*

> *"Is there even a procedure for how bar complaints work when AI is involved?"*

Key language markers: **my name**, **my bar number**, **my license**, **what happens**. The attorney speaks in first person singular throughout. Personal liability is the dominant frame.

---

### On AI Tool Selection

> *"I researched AI tools. I couldn't find anything specifically designed for Swiss law firms. Everything is US-centric."*

> *"My colleague uses one. I don't know if it's properly secure. I don't know what questions to ask."*

> *"There is no Geneva-specific AI tool review. No one has published what a secure AI research tool looks like for a 3-attorney firm."*

Key language markers: **nothing for Swiss**, **don't know if secure**, **no one published**, **what questions to ask**. The selection problem is framed as absence of proportionate Geneva-calibrated guidance.

---

### On Engagement Letter Architecture

> *"Our engagement letters were written in 2022. They don't mention AI. Do we need to tell clients we're using AI? Do we need their consent?"*

> *"What does proper disclosure look like for a small firm? There is no template for this."*

Key language markers: **written in 2022**, **don't mention AI**, **do we need to tell**, **no template**. The ICP recognizes engagement letters are obsolete but doesn't know what to replace them with.

---

### On Client Questionnaire Exposure

> *"A client sent us an AI governance questionnaire. It was 30 pages. I had no idea we were supposed to have answers for this."*

> *"What do we say when a client asks how we use AI on their matters? Do we have a policy? Do we need one?"*

Key language markers: **had no idea**, **were supposed to have answers**, **do we have a policy**. The client questionnaire is experienced as accountability moment arriving before preparation.

## French — Comment le PIC Parle

### Sur l'Attestation Carrier

> *"Ma mutuelle PI m'a envoyé un questionnaire avec une section IA. Il me demande d'attester personnellement de notre gouvernance IA. Je ne sais pas ce que j'atteste. Je l'ai signé quand même parce que je n'avais pas le choix."*

Marqueurs linguistiques : **personnellement**, **attester**, **je ne savais pas ce que**, **j'ai dû signer**. L'attestation carrier est vécue comme une exposition personnelle, pas un exercice de conformité.

---

### Sur le Secret Professionnel en Contexte IA

> *"Mon client m'appelle et me demande si nous utilisons l'IA sur son dossier. Je ne sais pas quoi lui dire. Je ne sais pas ce que l'outil IA fait de ses données après notre intervention."*

> *"J'ai lu les conditions du fournisseur. Ce n'est pas clair. Je ne peux pas savoir si leurs données entraîne le modèle. Je ne peux pas savoir si elles sont stockées à Luxembourg ou Dublin."*

> *"Et si l'outil IA est piraté ? Quelle est mon obligation de notifier le client ?"*

Marqueurs linguistiques : **je ne sais pas quoi lui dire**, **je ne peux pas savoir**, **et si**, **obligation de notifier**.

---

### Sur le Vide Directorial du Barreau

> *"J'ai appelé le barreau. Ils m'ont dit qu'ils n'ont pas de directive sur l'IA. Ils ont dit que c'est la responsabilité de l'avocat."*

> *"La SBA a publié quelque chose sur l'IA. Ce sont des principes. Je ne sais pas ce que 'supervision adéquate' signifie en pratique pour mon cabinet."*

> *"L'Ordre des Avocats a-t-il dit quelque chose d'officiel ? Rien que je puisse citer."*

Marqueurs linguistiques : **ils n'ont pas de directive**, **c'est la responsabilité de l'avocat**, **principes sans pratique**, **rien que je puisse citer**.

---

### Sur l'Utilisation de l'IA par les Collaborateurs

> *"Mes collaborateurs utilisent l'IA en permanence. Je ne peux pas évaluer le travail qu'ils produisent parce que je ne comprends pas ce que l'outil IA fait."*

> *"Ils produisent de bons documents. Je ne sais pas s'ils ont réfléchi ou s'ils ont juste passé le texte au ChatGPT."*

Marqueurs linguistiques : **je ne peux pas évaluer**, **je ne sais pas ce que l'IA fait**, **bons documents**, **réfléchi**.

---

### Sur l'Exposition Personnelle

> *"Mon nom est sur la note de consultation. Mon numéro de barreau est sur le document. Si quelque chose tourne mal, c'est ma license qui est en jeu."*

> *"Que se passe-t-il dans une procédure disciplinaire du barreau si l'IA a produit le raisonnement et que j'ai signé ?"*

> *"Existe-t-il même une procédure pour les plaintes au barreau impliquant l'IA ?"*

Marqueurs linguistiques : **mon nom**, **mon numéro de barreau**, **ma license**, **que se passe-t-il**.

## What This Reveals

The Geneva small-law ICP speaks in **first person singular** when describing AI accountability. The ICP does not describe a firm problem. They describe a **personal liability problem** attached to outputs they cannot fully evaluate or control.

The dominant conversational structures are:
1. **Personal exposure statement** — "My name is on the document"
2. **Authority gap expression** — "I can't evaluate what the AI does"
3. **Institutional abandonment statement** — "The bar told me it's my responsibility"
4. **Tool selection paralysis** — "I don't know what questions to ask"
5. **Retrospective alarm** — "I had no idea I was supposed to have answers for this"

The ICP is not seeking an AI strategy. The ICP is seeking reassurance that **their personal exposure has a proportionate, manageable path forward** that the bar, the carriers, and the courts have not provided.
