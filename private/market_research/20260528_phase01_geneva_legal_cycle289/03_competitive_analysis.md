## What's Missing — The Geneva Market Gaps

### Gap 1: Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review

**What exists:** None. No Geneva service offers independent pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation forms from the signatory's personal liability perspective.

**What the market needs:** A discrete service — not form completion review, not compliance check — but a **personal liability review**: what the MD is committing to personally, what exposure that creates, what proportionate response is required before signing. This service is structurally absent from the Geneva market. The carrier provides the form. No independent reviewer evaluates it from the signatory's position.

---

### Gap 2: Proportionate Small-Firm AI Governance Protocol

**What exists:** AI governance frameworks designed for larger organizations. SBA and Ordre communications provide principles. No Geneva-published methodology describes what a 2–5 attorney firm needs to do operationally to satisfy those principles.

**What the market needs:** A **minimum viable Geneva AI governance protocol** specifically calibrated to what a 3-attorney Geneva firm actually is: one person holding user, compliance officer, and billing attorney roles simultaneously. The protocol is absent. The problem is specific to the Geneva small-firm scale — available frameworks do not address this configuration.

---

### Gap 3: Associate AI Competency Supervision Architecture

**What exists:** No methodology exists for how a supervising partner evaluates AI-assisted associate work product when the partner cannot independently assess the AI tool's output quality.

**What the market needs:** A **small-firm AI supervision protocol** that addresses the structural inversion: the person using the AI most is typically the most junior, and the person legally accountable cannot evaluate what they are signing off on. This documentation architecture is absent and no Geneva market participant has produced one.

---

### Gap 4: Pre-AI Engagement Letter Audit

**What exists:** Engagement letter review services. AI clause templates. No service offers specifically the review of **active matters governed by contracts written before the firm's own AI adoption** with no AI authorization language.

**What the market needs:** A **pre-AI engagement letter AI audit** for active matters: mapping what's disclosed, what isn't, what authorization the client gave implicitly, what disclosure would be appropriate for AI-assisted research and document production. The active-matter gap is a discrete unnamed service category.

---

### Gap 5: Geneva Bar Formal Interpretation Trigger Mechanism

**What exists:** The Ordre des Avocats publishes communications. The SBA publishes principles. No formal mechanism exists for attorneys to trigger a binding formal interpretation on specific AI accountability questions.

**What the market needs:** Structured understanding of **what questions the Geneva bar can or cannot formally resolve, and how the absence of that mechanism shapes the accountability vacuum small firms are operating in**. The formal mechanism is not merely absent — the absence itself is a structural property of how Geneva bar interpretation works, and no market participant has named this structural absence as a discrete service-relevant insight.

---

### Gap 6: Court AI-Specific Procedural Protocol

**What exists:** No Geneva court has published AI-specific procedural directions addressing what happens when opposing counsel requests AI reasoning chains, what discovery procedure applies to AI research metadata, or how Art.21 nFADP provisions interact with attorney privilege in civil procedure.

**What the market needs:** A **Geneva civil procedure AI protocol** — not a prediction of what courts will rule, but a documentation of what attorneys should have in place before questions arrive in court proceedings. This operational protocol is absent. No Geneva service has produced one.

---

### Gap 7: Geneva AI Tool Security Review

**What exists:** US-centric AI tool reviews. No Geneva-specific review evaluating AI research tools against Swiss professional obligations — Art.321 CP, nFADP, attorney-client privilege, attorney work product doctrine.

**What the market needs:** A **Swiss legal AI tool review** — not "is this tool good" but "does this tool meet the minimum requirements for a Geneva attorney using it in client matters." This review is absent. No Geneva publication has produced a proportionate methodology for evaluating AI tools for Swiss legal professional use.

---

### Gap 8: nFADP Art.9 Processor Compliance for Law Firms

**What exists:** General nFADP Art.9 guidance. No Geneva-specific analysis of what third-party processor compliance means for a small law firm using AI tools where the vendor is processing client matter data.

**What the market needs:** A **law firm AI processor compliance mapping** — what client data is flowing to AI vendors, under what authorization, with what processor agreements, and what disclosure is required. The nFADP Art.9 compliance gap is specifically underappreciated at small law firm scale. General GDPR-adjacent understanding does not translate directly to law firm AI processor flows.

---

### Gap 9: Anonymization Adequacy Review

**What exists:** Legal industry guidance suggesting anonymization as an AI governance solution. No Geneva-specific analysis of whether anonymized AI-assisted research satisfies attorney-client privilege and work product doctrine.

**What the market needs:** A **Geneva law firm anonymization adequacy analysis** — testing whether anonymized outputs from AI tools used in client matters constitute legal work product, whether anonymization is legally sufficient safe harbor, or whether it creates false adequacy. This question is live in the market. No Geneva service has answered it.

---

## Summary: What the Competitive Landscape Is Missing

The current Geneva legal AI market provides:

- **Framework-level** awareness that AI governance is an issue
- **Principle-level** guidance without operational methodology
- **Generic** AI tool selection guidance not calibrated to Swiss professional obligations
- **General** nFADP obligations without law-firm-specific processor analysis

The market is missing the **operative layer**: what a specific attorney at a specific firm with a specific carrier attestation needed to sign yesterday actually does on Monday morning.

The services that exist tell Geneva attorneys that AI accountability matters. The services that don't exist tell them what to do about it.
