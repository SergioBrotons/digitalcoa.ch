GENFM replaced LIBOR/CHF LIBOR as the Swiss franc risk-free rate in March 2026. This is a live, active transition creating board-level decision needs: contract repricing, risk model recalibration, IT system changes, hedging strategy decisions. Decision Diagnostic format (2-5 days) directly serves this need. Not covered in prior cycles.

### C115 Finding 2: Swiss Ordinance on Board Diversity (2299a) — Governance Architecture Opportunity
Boards of FINMA-supervised institutions must comply with expanded diversity requirements under the 2299a ordinance. Board composition decisions, expertise matrix gaps, succession planning = specific Governance Architecture engagement territory. Directly links regulatory obligation to advisory service.

### C115 Finding 3: CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework) — July 2026 Deadline
Switzerland implementing CARF (OECD crypto asset reporting framework) with July 2026 effective date. Geneva commodity traders, crypto managers, cross-border wealth structures face urgent compliance decisions: data infrastructure changes, client communication, reporting obligations. Decision Diagnostic well-suited for CARF compliance prioritization decisions at executive/board level. Not covered in prior cycles.

### C115 Finding 4: Multi-jurisdictional Regulatory Calendar Non-Alignment
Geneva executives face simultaneous, non-aligned regulatory deadlines: FINMA (Circular 2026/1, LTPM/AMLA Jul 2026), EU (DORA 2025, AI Act enforcement 2025-26, CRD VI), US (SEC/CFTC), UK (FCA). This creates executive decision overload — prioritization decisions about which compliance to address first, with what resources, on what timeline. Personal Decision Infrastructure directly serves this pain.
