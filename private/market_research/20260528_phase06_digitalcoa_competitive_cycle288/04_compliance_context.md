## Regulatory Context Affecting DigitalCoa.ch's Advisory Practice

### The Geneva Accountability Ecosystem: Structural Properties

Geneva professional accountability operates through a distinct ecosystem with properties that do not exist in any single published regulatory instrument:

1. **Carrier iteration as operative standard-setting:** In the absence of published SVA, FINMA, or ORDRE AI governance guidance for Geneva professional services boutiques, carrier form iteration functions as the de facto operative standard. The carrier's form is written by the carrier's legal team — parties with no Geneva boutique professional services input. The MD signs it, committing to a carrier-defined adequacy standard that is not a published guidance document.

2. **Geneva circuit intelligence as informal mechanism:** Accountability standards form through informal circuit exchange before any published doctrine arrives. The Geneva MD/partner community shares intelligence about carrier expectations, institutional counterparty requirements, and accountability standard formation through mechanisms that are not public. This intelligence is accessible only through sustained participation.

3. **Multi-venue coherence as the operative accountability problem:** The Geneva MD/partner faces accountability across carrier renewal, board reporting, institutional counterparty methodology requests, nFADP obligations, and Geneva circuit informal standards simultaneously. Each venue has different evidentiary requirements, different audiences, and different timing. No single regulatory framework maps what each venue requires or how requirements interact when they contradict.

### Active Regulatory Instruments Affecting the ICP

#### nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) — Active

- **Art. 63:** Personal criminal liability for nFADP violations. Applies to all Geneva professionals processing personal data through AI systems. The MD who cannot document lawful basis for AI-influenced decisions faces personal criminal exposure.
- **Art. 21:** Automated decision explanation obligations. Applies when AI systems influence decisions communicated to clients. The MD must be able to explain what AI contributed to recommendations in client reports — and the explanation must be accessible.
- **Art. 9:** Third-party processor obligations. Applies to AI vendor relationships. The MD as data controller has obligations toward sub-processors that vendor terms may conflict with.
- **Art. 13:** DPIA and explanation obligation. The explanation obligation is present-tense. The response architecture is absent in most Geneva professional services organizations.

#### Carrier AI Attestation — Active, Iterating

- Third-generation carrier AI attestation forms are the closest thing to an operative standard in the Geneva market. They are written by carrier legal teams without Geneva boutique input.
- Fourth-generation forms are arriving with sharper personal liability questions, version-provenance requirements, and multi-venue coherence demands.
- The form is the deadline that makes AI governance real for the MD who has delayed. Pre-signing review is the highest-conversion engagement entry point.

#### Swissdamed — Active, Tiered Deadline

- Swissdamed medical device registration is active. Tier 1 devices face July 1, 2026 deadlines. Tier 2 devices have December 31, 2026.
- The classification pathway is designed for medical device manufacturers, not practicing physicians. Independent practices cannot determine which tier applies without methodology the documentation does not provide.
- The most acute regulatory timing pressure in healthcare right now is Swissdamed classification pathway inaccessibility.

#### Budapest AI Convention — Ratification Active, Window Narrowing

- Switzerland's Parliament ratification process for the AI Convention is active.
- Most Geneva professionals have not heard of it. The advisor who demonstrates awareness and explains its implications signals regulatory timing intelligence that no generic AI governance consultant can match.
- The Convention will create new accountability obligations — particularly in legal services, financial services, and healthcare. The professional who begins building accountability architecture before ratification is building proactive evidence. The professional who waits for implementation guidance will be reacting.
- The window for positioning this as proactive intelligence is open now and begins closing as the ratification process advances and awareness crystallizes.

### The Documentation Window: Timing as Evidence

The documentation window has a specific timing property distinct from a compliance deadline:

- **Before formal standards arrive:** The MD building documentation is evaluated against standards forming through informal circuit mechanisms. The documentation demonstrates proactive governance reasoning.
- **After formal standards arrive:** The MD explaining absent documentation is evaluated against arrived standards. The absence of prior documentation cannot be retroactively reconstructed.

The timing of documentation is evidence of governance reasoning. Building documentation after the window closes cannot retroactively reconstruct the reasoning that would have existed during the window. The window's closing conditions are determined by carrier iteration velocity, institutional client expectation crystallization, and informal circuit standard formation — none of which publish explicit closing dates.

The 90-day window (June through August 2026) is the specific timing frame where rational inertia and summer community absence converge: the professional who knows they need to build something, doesn't know specifically what to build, and building the wrong thing feels more dangerous than building nothing.

### The Evidence Gap Paradox: Compliance Assessment Without Architecture Creates Liability

The evidence gap paradox applies to the ICP who conducted prior AI governance gap assessments without immediately building evidence architecture:

- When a carrier asks "what did you find in your last AI governance review?" a professional who assessed and found gaps now has a documented record of those gaps
- The carrier can ask: What specifically did you find? When did you find it? What did you do about it?
- If the professional found gaps and did not immediately build evidence architecture, the documented assessment becomes evidence of known exposure without remediation

The resolution protocol: preserve the original gap analysis, create a new document: the evidence architecture built in response, include a dated transition note. The pair of documents — gap analysis plus response architecture — demonstrates accountability.

## Compliance Context for DigitalCoa.ch's Own Practice

DigitalCoa.ch faces the same documentation window as every Geneva professional. The practice's own accountability architecture must be treated with the same urgency as the ICP's:

- **Vendor contract review:** Every AI tool and platform used by the practice has vendor terms that create liability on acceptance. The practice must review these terms against its own professional obligations.
- **Carrier form completion:** The practice's own carrier forms are the visible proof of its governance. Any gaps in the practice's own AI governance become conversion issues as the market matures.
- **nFADP obligations:** As a service provider processing client information, the practice has nFADP obligations that must be documented and operational.
- **Documentation window positioning:** The practice's own documentation window is active. What the practice builds during this window is in a fundamentally different legal position than what it builds after.

The practice's own governance completeness is not an internal operational matter — it is an external credibility matter with direct conversion impact.
