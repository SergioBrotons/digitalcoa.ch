## Problem Statement (5 layers)

### Layer 1: FINMA AI Guidance (Updated 2025)
FINMA's 2025 AI guidance specifies human override requirements for AI-driven suitability assessments, robo-advisory disclosure standards, and portfolio rebalancing accountability. WM firms must document which decisions are AI-assisted vs. AI-autonomous and maintain human review trails.

### Layer 2: Federal AI Ordinance + FADP Dual-Track
The dual-track (FADP Art. 21 + Federal AI Ordinance) applies to WM firms. AI tools in client reporting, portfolio analytics, and suitability assessment are subject to risk classification and human review documentation. **As of April 2026, this framework has been operative for over 15 months — most boutiques are still not compliant.**

### Layer 3: AML AI + FADP Operational Tension
AML screening tools require broad, deep client data processing. FADP requires data minimization and purpose limitation. The conflict is real, unresolved in the industry, and creates both regulatory risk and client trust exposure. WM firms need a documented position on how they resolve this tension.

### Layer 4: CARF/CRS 2026 + G7 Évian + US Section 301 Compounding
CARF implementation in 2026 adds reporting obligations for Geneva WMs with crypto holdings. G7 Évian (June 2026) and US Section 301 pressure create additional regulatory pressure vectors for WMs with cross-border US exposure. **H2 2026 is shaping up as the most complex regulatory window for Geneva boutique WMs in a decade.**

### Layer 5: Data Sovereignty as Competitive Dimension
Swiss public mistrust of US tech platforms (Google, TikTok, Meta) is a data sovereignty signal. HNW clients increasingly ask where their financial data is processed. AI vendor selection is now a governance decision, not just a technology decision.

## The Checklist Structure

### Section 1: AI Tool Inventory + Risk Classification
Maps every AI tool in use to Federal AI Ordinance risk categories (with FINMA guidance alignment for WM-specific tools).

### Section 2: FINMA Human Override Documentation
For each AI-assisted decision (suitability, rebalancing, reporting), is there a documented human review trail?

### Section 3: AML AI + FADP Tension Resolution
Has the firm documented its position on the AML AI / FADP data minimization conflict? Is the position defensible under both frameworks?

### Section 4: Robo-Advisory Disclosure Compliance
Are AI-assisted client recommendations properly disclosed per FINMA 2025 guidance?

### Section 5: CARF/CRS + G7 Regulatory Readiness
Can the firm's AI tools used in CARF/CRS reporting demonstrate an auditable decision trail? Has the firm assessed its G7-adjacent exposure?

### Section 6: Data Sovereignty Audit
Where is client data processed? US-based AI tools: has client consent been obtained? Swiss sovereign AI alternatives: has cost-capability tradeoff been documented?

### Section 7: Client Expectation Management
Does the firm have a client-facing AI disclosure policy? Do HNW clients understand when and how AI is used in their service?
