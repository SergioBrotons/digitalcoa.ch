## What's Missing: The Geneva Small Firm AI Governance Gap

### 1. No Small-Firm-Proportionate AI Governance Framework

No existing framework maps to the 2–5 attorney firm context. The NIST/ISO frameworks are enterprise-grade. The EU AI Act is obligations-grade. What small Geneva firms need: **operational, proportionate, attorney-specific, Swiss-Geneva-contextual**.

### 2. No Geneva Bar-Integrated AI Guidance

The Geneva Bar has published nothing actionable. Practitioners need a document that maps Geneva professional responsibility obligations (secret professionnel, Articles 321 CP/Civil Code) to specific AI tool evaluation criteria. This doesn't exist.

### 3. No "Adequate AI Governance" Definition for Carrier Attestation

Small firms need a document they can present to their PI carrier that defines what "adequate AI governance" means in their specific context, in language their carrier can understand and act upon. This has never been produced as a standard deliverable.

### 4. No Attorney-Client Privilege-by-Design AI Tool Certification

No third party has certified any AI tool as attorney-client privilege-compatible under Swiss law. The market has no independent validation mechanism — firms must conduct their own legal analysis of every tool, which is beyond their capacity.

### 5. No Small Firm AI Incident Protocol

When an AI-assisted error occurs in a small firm, there is no documented protocol for:
- Internal incident documentation
- Carrier notification timing
- Bar association self-reporting obligations (if any)
- Client disclosure decisions
This in-actionability creates anxiety disproportionate to actual risk.

### 6. No Bilingual EN/FR AI Governance Starter Kit

The language gap is real: Geneva attorneys function in French but many AI governance resources are English-only. No one has produced a practical, accessible bilingual EN/FR guide that maps existing frameworks to Geneva small firm realities.

## What They Are Buying Today (and Why It's Unsatisfying)

### Current Tool Stack (Observed from Practitioner Interviews)

- **Low cost / free AI tools** (ChatGPT free, Gemini): Used only for internal admin
- **Casetext (paid)**: Not fully trusted for client-facing work; US-centric
- **Clio**: Used for practice management but not AI governance
- **Internal DMS with basic search**: Still primary research tool for many
- **Manual privilege review of all AI outputs**: The current de facto governance

**Why Unsatisfying:**
- None address carrier attestation needs
- None provide Geneva bar-specific compliance framing
- None are designed for the liability profile of a 2–5 attorney firm
- All require individual attorney capacity to evaluate and implement

---

## Key Competitive Insight

**The market has addressed large firm AI governance. It has addressed consumer AI confusion. The boutique professional services firm — specifically the 2–5 attorney Geneva law firm — is entirely unserved.**

The need is real, acute, and growing (pressure from PI carriers, bar guidance vacuum, client expectations). The competitive gap is total. The window to own this category expires as soon as a well-resourced competitor (a big 4 firm, a legal insurer, a bar association) decides to fill it.
