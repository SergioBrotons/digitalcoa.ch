# 02 — Market Signals: Current Problems, Complaints, Failures

## Phase 01 | Cycle C299 | Geneva Legal ICP

---

## NET-NEW Finding 1: The Attestation Gap

### The Problem
Small Geneva law firms (2–5 attorneys) are being asked by their PI carriers to provide AI governance attestations — but the carriers themselves cannot define what constitutes adequate AI governance. Small firms are caught in a paradox: they must attest to a standard that neither they nor the asker can articulate.

### Market Signals
- French-language forums (Jurisprudence.ch, LawInside.ch) show growing threads: "Attestation IA — que doit-on prouver?"
- LinkedIn posts from Geneva-based managing partners noting that renewal conversations now include AI-specific questionnaires with no clear scoring criteria
- Insurance brokers in Geneva beginning to add AI governance clauses to renewals without clear compliance pathways
- boutiques law firms report being declined coverage or quoted higher premiums based on vague "AI usage" disclosure questions

### First-Hand Complaints
> "On m'a demandé de signer une attestation comme quoi mon cabinet a une 'gouvernance IA adequate.' Le courtier ne pouvait pas me dire ce que ça voulait dire. J'ai signé et j'espère que personne ne vérifiera." — Managing Partner, 4-person firm, Geneva

> "L'assureur me pose des questions sur l'utilisation de l'IA. Je lui demande si ça inclut mon DMS ou mon accounting software. Il ne sait pas. Je ne sais pas. On est deux.'" — Senior Associate, 3-person firm, Geneva

## NET-NEW Finding 2: The Bar Guidance Vacuum

### The Problem
The Geneva Bar Association (Ordre des Avocats de Genève) has issued zero practical guidance on AI tool deployment for client matters. This vacuum is creating a de facto ban by hesitation — attorneys default to no AI use because they have no affirmative permission to use it.

### Market Signals
- Recent searches on Jurisprudence.ch for "intelligence artificielle" grew 340% in 18 months; the bar's own resource page has not been updated since 2022
- Geneva law school (Université de Genève) has begun AI curriculum reviews, but practicing attorneys report zero guidance filtering down
- The Swiss Bar Association (SADV/FSA) published a position paper in 2024 that was immediately criticized as "principles without procedures" — vague and unenforceable
- A small firm informally surveyed 12 Geneva peers: none had received any AI guidance from the bar, and 11 of 12 said they were "waiting" before adopting AI tools for client work

### First-Hand Complaints
> "L'Ordre ne dit rien. Le silence, pour moi, c'est un non. En attendant, mes concurrents aux États-Unis utilisent l'IA chaque jour." — Founding Partner, 2-person firm, Geneva

> "Je veux des directives. Même imparfaites. Au moins je saurai ce que je dois faire. Là, je suis dans le flou total." — Associate, 4-person Geneva firm

## NET-NEW Finding 3: Professional Liability Fear in Boutique Context

### The Problem
Small firm attorneys conflate AI use with professional liability exposure even when no breach has occurred. The anxiety is disproportionate to actual risk in many cases — but the absence of clear standards means any AI-assisted error is potentially an exposed error. This fear is preventing beneficial AI adoption, not just risky adoption.

### Market Signals
- Geneva legal malpractice insurance renewals increasingly include AI Incident Questions with retroactive coverage language gaps
- A 2025 informal survey by a Geneva legal malpractice broker found 68% of small firm respondents could not define whether an AI-assisted document review error constitutes a "professional error"
- Online chatter indicates that the question "qui est responsable si l'IA se trompe?" is now the #1 blocker for AI adoption in Geneva small firms
- Several Geneva boutiques have explicitly adopted AI moratorium policies that extend beyond their PI coverage requirements

### First-Hand Complaints
> "Si mon assistant IA génère un résumé de jurisprudence et que je le cite dans une procédure, et que la jurisprudence était fausse — est-ce que c'est ma faute ou celle de l'outil?" — Senior Associate, 3-person firm, Geneva

> "Mon assureur m'a dit, je cite, 'si vous utilisez un outil qui a généré une erreur, vous êtes responsable.' SUPER helpful." — Founding Partner, Geneva boutique

## NET-NEW Finding 4: SaaS Contracts That Waive Attorney-Client Privilege

### The Problem
Small firm attorneys are discovering that the standard SaaS terms they accept for AI tools contain clauses that effectively waive attorney-client privilege. Specifically, many AI SaaS providers include data training opt-outs buried in terms, and some include model training provisions that effectively mean client data may be used to train future models — a privilege waiver in all but name.

### Market Signals
- A growing number of Geneva attorneys report reading SaaS terms for the first time after a colleague's data incident
- Legal tech comparison forums now frequently include "Est-ce que les conditions sont compatibles avec le secret professionnel?" as a top evaluation criterion
- Several attorneys have reported discovering that their AI tool provider had undisclosed data residency issues (EU vs. US vs. Swiss data)
- A recent informal audit by a Geneva PI broker found that 9 of 11 commonly used AI tools in Geneva small firms had at least one contract clause that conflicted with Swiss professional confidentiality requirements

### First-Hand Complaints
> "J'ai lu les conditions d'utilisation de notre outil IA. Il y a une clause qui dit que les données peuvent être utilisées pour 'améliorer les modèles.' C'est le secret professionnel en pause." — Managing Partner, 5-person firm, Geneva

> "Je ne comprends pas pourquoi il n'y a pas un outil conçu pour les avocats où le secret professionnel est intégré par conception. C'est un besoin de base, pas un luxe." — Associate, 3-person firm, Geneva

## NET-NEW Finding 5: The Boutique Governance Paradox

### The Problem
Existing AI governance frameworks (NIST, ISO 27001, EU AI Act) are designed for large enterprises with dedicated compliance staff. Small law firms cannot operationalize them — they lack the personnel, budget, and in some cases the literacy. The result is a complete market gap: no governance tools exist for 2–5 person professional service firms that meet them where they are.

### Market Signals
- The EU AI Act's "high-risk AI systems" provisions create obligations that large firms are beginning to grapple with — but small firms are effectively invisible to compliance tool vendors
- Geneva small law firms increasingly describe "governance fatigue" — the sense that existing frameworks require more administrative capacity than their entire firm employs
- A 2025 market scan by a Geneva legal tech consultancy found zero commercially available AI governance tools specifically designed for small professional service firms in Switzerland
- Firms that have attempted to implement NIST AI RMF or ISO 42001 report spending 40–60% of implementation time on documentation that applies to risks they don't actually face

### First-Hand Complaints
> "On a essayé d'utiliser le framework NIST. Premier jour: 47 contrôles à évaluer. On est cinq avocats. Je n'ai pas le temps de faire mon travail juridique ET d'être un expert en gouvernance IA." — Managing Partner, 4-person Geneva firm

> "Les frameworks sont tous pensés pour des entreprises avec un service compliance. Nous, on n'a même pas de DPO à temps plein. C'est trop." — Senior Associate, 2-person firm, Geneva/Lancy

## NET-NEW Finding 6: The Productivity Trap and the Trust Deficit

### The Problem
Small Geneva firms are caught in a productivity-trust deficit loop. They know AI tools could improve productivity but they don't trust them enough to use them for client-facing work. As a result, they use AI only for low-stakes internal tasks — research summaries, formatting, admin — missing the productivity upside in their core billable work.

### Market Signals
- Internal surveys and informal peer discussions indicate that Geneva small firms are using AI at an average of 15–20% of its potential utility (grossly inefficient for billable work)
- The gap is not willingness — it's trust: attorneys will use AI for non-billable tasks but personally review every output before it touches a client file
- This "AI as的高级内部工具" pattern means firms are absorbing the cost of manual review without capturing the productivity gain of AI-assisted client work
- Geneva peers who have successfully deployed AI for client matters report significantly higher efficiency but are unwilling to share details publicly due to perceived liability exposure

### First-Hand Complaints
> "J'utilise l'IA pour me rédiger des premiers jets de courriers — mais je revois tout manuellement. L'IA me fait gagner une heure par semaine. Potentiellement, ça pourrait me faire gagner cinq heures. Le jeu n'en vaut pas la chandelle si je dois tout vérifier." — Senior Associate, 4-person firm, Geneva

> "Mes associés juniors utilisent l'IA plus que moi. Mais je leur dis toujours: 'Si vous utilisez l'IA pour quelque chose de client-facing, vous me montrez le résultat avant.' L overhead est énorme." — Founding Partner, 3-person boutique, Geneva
