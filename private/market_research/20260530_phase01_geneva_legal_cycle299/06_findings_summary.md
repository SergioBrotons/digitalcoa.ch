## Six NET-NEW Findings — Summary

| # | Finding | Core Insight | Market Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Attestation Gap** | PI carriers are asking small firms to attest to "adequate AI governance" without being able to define what that means | A standard attestation document defining adequate governance for 2–5 attorney firms — in carrier language |
| 2 | **The Bar Guidance Vacuum** | ODAGE has published nothing practical on AI. Geneva attorneys are in self-governance mode by default | Produce what ODAGE has not: practical, proportionate, Geneva-specific AI guidance for small firms |
| 3 | **Professional Liability Fear** | Attorneys conflate AI use with liability exposure even when no breach has occurred — preventing beneficial adoption | Education that separates actual risk from anxiety, paired with a framework to operationalize safe use |
| 4 | **SaaS Contracts That Waive Privilege** | Standard AI SaaS terms contain clauses that effectively waive attorney-client privilege (model training provisions, data access, cross-border disclosure) | Independent tool certification — an attestation that a specific tool meets Geneva attorney-client privilege standards |
| 5 | **The Boutique Governance Paradox** | Enterprise governance frameworks (NIST, ISO 27001, EU AI Act) cannot be operationalized by a 2–5 person firm | Proportionate, boutique-specific governance framework with named responsible attorney, annual review cycle, and carrier-ready outputs |
| 6 | **The Productivity Trap** | High trust deficit means AI is used only for non-billable tasks — firms absorb review overhead without capturing productivity gains in client work | Trusted AI adoption pathway — a structured approach that builds confidence and coverage simultaneously |

### Body Copy Principles

**What works with this ICP:**
- **Direct questions** that mirror their internal monologue ("Comment être sûr que…?", "Qui est responsable si…?")
- **Proportionate framing** — explicitly acknowledge the boutique scale constraint ("Pour un cabinet de 5 avocats…")
- **Personal accountability language** — speak to them as individuals, not as firms ("Votre signature. Votre responsabilité.")
- **Concrete deliverables** — a checklist, a template, a protocol beats any conceptual framework
- **Reassurance without false certainty** — "Vous n'avez pas besoin d'être parfait. Vous avez besoin d'être documenté."

**What does NOT work:**
- Enterprise-scale governance frameworks without scale acknowledgment
- Generic "AI is the future" language that ignores their fear
- Vendor-promise language on confidentiality without evidence
- Compliance theater — solutions that look like bureaucracy
- English-only content — Geneva is French-first

### Objection Handling Language

**"Je n'ai pas le temps de mettre en place une gouvernance IA"**
> "This framework takes a managing partner an afternoon to implement — and it produces exactly what your carrier is asking for."

**"L'ODAGE devrait publier des directives"**
> "They should. They haven't. In the meantime, firms that self-govern with a documented framework are in a stronger position than firms that do nothing."

**"Je ne veux pas risquer mon casier disciplinaire"**
> "The document isn't about being perfect. It's about being able to show your process. That's what 'adequate governance' actually means to a carrier and a bar."

**"Tous les outils IA ont des conditions qui me font peur"**
> "Most do. This is why evaluation before deployment matters. You don't need the perfect tool — you need the right evaluation process."

**"Je ne sais pas ce que mon assureur veut vraiment"**
> "Neither does your insurer — they know they need to ask AI questions, but they haven't defined a standard. That's exactly why having a documented framework is your advantage."

## Emotional State Map

| Trigger | Emotional State | Desired Response |
|---|---|---|
| Carrier renewal with AI questions | Anxious, defensive | Show that documentation is achievable |
| Reading SaaS terms at midnight | Overwhelmed, skeptical | Provide a checklist that makes terms evaluable |
| ODAGE silence | Frustrated, abandoned | Acknowledge frustration, offer the framework they should have |
| Peer competitor adopting AI | Conflicted, curious/jealous | Show safe path to adoption, not "drop everything" |
| AI error in the news | Scared, hypervigilant | Show that risk is manageable, not unavoidable |
| Client asking about AI usage | Exposed, unprepared | Give simple disclosure language |
| Being told "the market is moving fast" | Skeptical, resistant | "Move fast and break things" is not applicable to your risk profile |
