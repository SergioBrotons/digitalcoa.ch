## Executive Summary

Small Geneva consulting boutiques (2–50 employees) are caught in a convergence of three pressures they were not prepared for:

1. **Clients** — primarily financial institutions and large corporates — are sending AI governance due diligence questionnaires to all service providers, expecting documented answers
2. **Insurers** are adding AI-related clauses and exclusions to professional liability renewals without clear guidance on what "compliant AI use" looks like for small firms
3. **nFADP** — now in force — creates personal liability for managing partners (up to CHF 250,000 for intentional breaches) for AI-assisted data processing decisions made without governance

None of the existing market solutions are designed for this specific ICP. This creates a genuine positioning opportunity.

---

## 6 NET-NEW Findings (C293 Phase 03)

### Finding 1: Client AI DDQs Are Now a Sales Blocker
Small Geneva consulting firms are losing or avoiding engagements because they cannot respond to client AI governance questionnaires with adequate documentation. This is being driven by FINMA-regulated clients extending their AI compliance obligations down their vendor chain. The firms that can demonstrate documented AI governance will have a competitive advantage in client selection processes.

### Finding 2: nFADP Personal Liability (CHF 250,000) Is Under-Communicated to This ICP
The nFADP's personal criminal liability provision for managers and executives — unique in European data protection law — has not been clearly communicated to the small consulting community. Most managing partners in Geneva boutiques do not know their personal assets may be at risk for intentional data protection breaches involving AI tools.

### Finding 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Is the Most unmanaged Risk
Small consulting firms routinely delegate to sub-consultants but have no visibility into what AI tools those sub-consultants use. With no contractual AI clauses and no DPAs in place, the primary firm bears liability for AI-assisted outputs it did not produce and cannot audit.

### Finding 4: Insurers Are Creating Compliance Pull Without Offering Solutions
Professional liability carriers are adding AI exclusions to renewals, but no insurance product in the Swiss market is calibrated for "small firm using consumer AI tools with proportionate governance controls." This creates an opportunity for a governance product that also serves as an insurance navigation tool.

### Finding 5: No Player Occupies the "Small Geneva Boutique AI Governance" Position
The market is served by: Big Four (enterprise, expensive), legal advisors (diagnose but don't implement), industry associations (generic playbooks), AI tool vendors (tool-level compliance only), and insurance brokers (adding exclusions reactively). No one has created a proportionate, boutique-specific AI governance solution for this ICP.

### Finding 6: The Combination of All Five Gaps Creates a Natural Product Opportunity
The convergence of client DDQ pressure, insurance renewal risk, nFADP personal liability, sub-consultant delegation gaps, and methodology defensibility concerns points to a single coherent offering: a "Geneva Consulting AI Accountability Program" — a structured, practical engagement for small boutiques that addresses all five gaps simultaneously.

### For Handling Objections

**"We don't use AI in client work" (defensive response):**
> "That's a valid starting position — and it's one worth documenting. Even if the answer today is 'no AI tools in client deliverables,' having that in writing is increasingly useful. Because clients are asking about future plans too, and 'we haven't decided yet' is a different risk profile than 'we've made a deliberate decision not to.'"

**"We're too small for this to matter" (dismissive response):**
> "The nFADP personal liability provision applies to managers at any size — and CHF 250,000 is the same amount whether you're a 5-person firm or a 500-person firm. The question isn't size. It's whether you've made AI decisions deliberately, with documentation, or reactively."

**"We can't afford a big compliance program" (cost objection):**
> "I'm not offering an enterprise compliance program. I'm offering proportionate governance for a boutique practice — something you can actually implement and use. The goal isn't to build a compliance department. It's to be able to answer three questions with confidence: what AI tools you use, how you validate outputs, and who is accountable."

## Key Concepts and Definitions (For Internal Use)

| Concept | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| AI DDQ | AI Due Diligence Questionnaire — a structured set of questions a client sends to vendors to assess their AI governance practices |
| nFADP | New Federal Act on Data Protection — Switzerland's revised data protection law, in force since September 1, 2023 |
| DPA | Data Processing Agreement — a contractual document required under nFADP when third parties handle personal data |
| DPIA | Data Protection Impact Assessment — an analysis required for high-risk data processing activities, including many AI-assisted processing tasks |
| FINMA Guidance 08/2024 | FINMA's supervisory guidance on AI governance for Swiss financial institutions — drives AI DDQ requirements for service providers |
| EU AI Act | EU's Artificial Intelligence Act — in force since August 2024; applies extraterritorially to firms serving EU-based clients |
| ISO 42001 | International standard for AI management systems — enterprise-scale framework that can be selectively adapted for small firms |
| Methodology defensibility | The ability to explain, document, and defend the process by which a consulting recommendation was produced — including AI's role |

## ICP Psychographic Profile

**What they want:** To use AI tools effectively in client work without personal liability exposure, without losing client engagements to AI DDQ failures, and without signing insurance renewals that exclude AI-related coverage.

**What they fear:** A client incident traced back to an AI tool they selected. A personal liability claim under nFADP. An insurance exclusion that voids coverage for the one engagement where something goes wrong.

**What they respond to:** Direct language. Practical tools. Solutions that acknowledge their size. Someone who understands what it's like to be personally accountable for every recommendation.

**What they ignore:** Enterprise compliance frameworks. Generic AI governance playbooks. Vendors who can't explain why their solution fits a 5-person firm.
