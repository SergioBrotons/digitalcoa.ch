## Cross-Vertical Competitive Landscape: The Structural Gaps No One Is Filling

The Geneva AI governance advisory market has vertical specialists but no cross-vertical player. This is not a positioning gap — it is a structural consequence of what cross-vertical synthesis requires. This file maps what is missing and why no single-vertical player can fill it.

---

## Competitive Landscape: What Exists

### Vertical Specialists

**Legal AI governance:**
- Legal tech vendors offering AI tools with compliance documentation
- Law firm IT consultants offering technology governance frameworks
- Compliance consultants offering GDPR/nFADP gap analysis
- Bar-adjacent training offering CLE credits on AI ethics

**Finance AI governance:**
- Big Four consulting firms offering AI governance frameworks for financial institutions
- Regulatory compliance consultants offering SRO guidance interpretation
- Wealth management technology consultants offering AI vendor due diligence
- Carriers offering AI risk assessment for PI underwriting

**Consulting AI governance:**
- Strategy consultancies offering AI governance as part of broader digital transformation
- Technology consultants offering AI risk frameworks
- No Geneva-specific consulting AI governance boutique exists

**Healthcare AI governance:**
- Healthcare technology vendors offering compliant AI tools
- Healthcare compliance consultants offering DPIA support
- Medical AI vendors offering certification support (swissdamed, MedDO)
- FMH-adjacent training offering digital competency credits

### What These Players Have in Common

Every vertical specialist:
- Addresses the problem from within their vertical
- Frames AI governance as a subset of their existing service offering
- Designs their methodology for the vertical's regulatory framework
- Cannot see the cross-vertical pattern because they are not sustained in other verticals
- Offers a framework, not a burden-of-proof positioning exercise

## Structural Gap 1: Pre-Carrier-Renewal Attestation Review From the Signatory's Perspective

**What's missing:** A service that reviews carrier AI attestation requirements from the MD's personal liability perspective before the MD signs.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Legal technology vendors review from a technology compliance perspective, not a personal liability perspective
- Finance compliance consultants review against SRO requirements, not carrier attestation language
- Healthcare compliance consultants focus on regulatory compliance, not carrier renewal exposure

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Understanding of carrier attestation language and its personal liability implications across all four verticals
- Ability to review the attestation against sector-specific professional liability obligations simultaneously
- Knowledge of what "adequate" means across all four sectors and how the carrier's undefined standard interacts with each

**The gap:** No Geneva service offers: "Here is what you are personally committing to when you sign this form, here is what you need to be able to show if the carrier examines your signature, here is the evidence architecture you need to build before you sign." This service exists nowhere in the Geneva market.

**Positioning implication:** This is the entry point service. The professional encounters the carrier attestation before they encounter any other AI governance problem. The service that helps them navigate the attestation creates the relationship for everything else.

## Structural Gap 2: Cross-Vertical Documentation Architecture for the 90-Day Window

**What's missing:** A documentation architecture that works across all four verticals — designed for the specific evidence requirements that will be examined when the documentation window closes.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Big Four firms offer documentation frameworks designed for large financial institutions
- Legal technology vendors offer document management systems, not accountability documentation
- Healthcare compliance consultants offer DPIA templates, not the specific documentation needed for carrier attestation defense

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Understanding of what evidence is required across all four verticals when carrier renewal occurs
- Ability to design documentation architecture that satisfies legal privilege, financial SRO, consulting contractual, and medical professional accountability requirements simultaneously
- Knowledge of how to build evidence that is coherent across all four sectors for a professional operating in multiple contexts

**The gap:** No Geneva service offers documentation architecture specifically designed for the burden-of-proof positioning exercise during the documentation window. The service that provides this positions for the window close — when the professional realizes they need documentation they should have built during the window, and the window has already closed.

**Positioning implication:** This is the urgency service. The professional knows the window is closing. They don't know what to build. The service that tells them specifically what to build and why positions as indispensable.

## Structural Gap 3: Vendor Contract Review for Professional Liability Compatibility

**What's missing:** A service that reviews AI vendor commercial terms against the professional's specific liability obligations — not for data protection compliance, but for professional liability compatibility.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Legal technology vendors provide data processing agreements that meet GDPR/nFADP data protection requirements
- Finance technology vendors provide ISAE 3000 reports that prove vendor controls
- Healthcare technology vendors provide consent forms and data processing agreements
- None of these address the question: does signing this agreement create conflict with the professional's specific liability obligations under Swiss law, bar rules, SRO requirements, or FMH professional code?

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Understanding of how vendor liability limitations interact with professional liability exposure in each vertical
- Ability to evaluate vendor terms against Ordre des Avocats confidentiality requirements, Art.398 CO proper performance obligations, FMH professional accountability requirements, and SRO conduct standards simultaneously
- Knowledge of which vendor terms create structural incompatibility with which professional obligations

**The gap:** No Geneva service offers: "This vendor's terms are incompatible with your professional liability obligations in these specific ways. Here is what you need before you sign." This service exists nowhere in the Geneva market.

**Positioning implication:** This is the trust-building service. The professional has signed dozens of vendor agreements without reading them. The service that explains what they actually signed and why it matters builds trust that no compliance training can replicate.

## Structural Gap 4: Institutional Counterparty Questionnaire Response Architecture

**What's missing:** A systematic approach to institutional questionnaire responses that maintains portfolio-wide coherence and positions the professional to influence the standard, not just respond to it.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Finance compliance consultants respond to institutional DDQs reactively, case by case
- Legal consultants don't address institutional questionnaires as a competitive intelligence event
- Consulting strategy firms address RFP requirements, not the accumulated effect of institutional questionnaire responses across a portfolio
- Healthcare advisors don't address institutional counterparty requirements as a market standard-setting mechanism

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Understanding of how institutional questionnaire responses in one sector set benchmarks that affect all sectors
- Ability to track which responses set which benchmarks and when
- Knowledge of how to respond to first-mover institutional questionnaires to influence the standard rather than just answer it

**The gap:** No Geneva service offers portfolio-level response architecture for institutional AI governance questionnaires. The service that provides this positions the professional to shape the standard, not just respond to it.

**Positioning implication:** This is the competitive intelligence service. The professional who responds systematically to institutional questionnaires is setting standards for their peers. The service that helps them do this intentionally positions as a competitive advantage provider, not a compliance vendor.

## Structural Gap 5: AI Model Version Tracking and Deliverable Audit Architecture

**What's missing:** A methodology for tracking AI model version states across active deliverables — so the professional can demonstrate what analytical framework produced which conclusions, when, for whom.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Legal technology vendors provide AI-assisted document production without version tracking for the attorney's deliverable
- Finance technology vendors provide AI-generated recommendations without version state documentation for the MD's portfolio
- Consulting technology vendors provide AI-assisted analysis without version tracking for the boutique's active proposals
- Healthcare AI vendors provide AI-generated clinical documentation without version state records for the physician's medical records

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Understanding of how model updates affect the evidentiary weight of AI-assisted deliverables in each vertical
- Ability to design deliverable audit trails that survive legal, financial, consulting, and medical record review
- Knowledge of how to structure engagement terms to address model version dependency

**The gap:** No Geneva service offers version tracking architecture for AI-assisted deliverables. The professional has no mechanism to answer the question: "What model version produced this conclusion, and would the current model produce the same output?" No one in the Geneva market is providing this.

**Positioning implication:** This is the evidence architecture service. The professional who cannot answer the version question cannot defend the deliverable. The service that provides the tracking architecture fills a gap that no one else has identified.

## Structural Gap 6: Cross-Vertical Synthesis Intelligence as Positioning Differentiator

**What's missing:** The intelligence that comes from sustained participation in all four verticals simultaneously — the pattern recognition that only emerges from seeing the same regulatory inputs, the same carrier market, the same documentation window, and the same professional ecosystem producing identical accountability pressure across all four verticals.

**Why no one provides it:**
- Vertical specialists see their vertical clearly. They don't see the shared ecosystem mechanism.
- Big Four firms have cross-industry presence but not cross-vertical synthesis for Geneva-specific professional accountability
- Regulatory consultants focus on published guidance, not the pattern that forms from sustained operational participation
- No Geneva boutique advisory has the sustained participation across all four verticals required to generate the synthesis intelligence

**What this requires that no one has:**
- Sustained participation in legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs simultaneously
- Pattern recognition across carrier attestation evolution, institutional counterparty behavior, documentation window dynamics, and regulatory change trajectory in all four verticals
- The specific synthesis intelligence that emerges only from being in all four verticals at once

**The gap:** The synthesis intelligence is non-replicable without the same participation. It is not a methodology that can be copied — it is a consequence of sustained presence across all four verticals. No one in the Geneva market has this because no one has sustained participation in all four verticals in the way that DigitalCoa.ch does.

**Positioning implication:** This is the structural moat. "I see the same problem across all four verticals because I'm in all four verticals" is not a marketing claim — it is a structural consequence of the business model that no competitive entry can replicate without the same participation.
