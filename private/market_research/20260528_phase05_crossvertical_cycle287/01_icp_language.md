## Cross-Vertical ICP Language: What Senior Professionals Say Across All Four Sectors

The following language patterns are drawn from C287 Phases 01–04 research across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs in Geneva. These are the phrases professionals use regardless of industry — the shared vocabulary of being personally accountable for AI you don't fully understand.

---

## Language Layer 1: The Entry Point Phrases

These are the phrases that open the conversation in every vertical. They signal that the professional has acknowledged the problem but cannot identify the first action. They are structurally identical across all four ICPs.

**English:**
- *"Where do I even start? I don't even have an inventory of what AI tools are in use."*
- *"I know I'm supposed to do something. I don't know what the standard is supposed to be."*
- *"My carrier is asking questions I don't know how to answer."*
- *"I signed the attestation because the renewal was due. I'm not sure what I committed to."*
- *"I have no idea what my staff are using."*
- *"The professional body says I remain responsible. Nobody tells me what that looks like on a Tuesday."*

**French:**
- *« Par où on commence ? Je n'ai même pas d'inventaire des outils IA utilisés. »*
- *« Je sais que je dois faire quelque chose. Je ne sais pas quelle est censée être la norme. »*
- *« Mon assureur me pose des questions auxquelles je ne sais pas répondre. »*
- *« J'ai signé l'attestation parce que le renouvellement était dû. Je ne suis pas sûr de ce à quoi je me suis engagé. »*
- *« Je n'ai aucune idée de ce que mon personnel utilise. »*
- *« L'ordre professionnel dit que je reste responsable. Personne ne me dit à quoi ça ressemble un mardi. »*

**What this tells us:** The entry signal is not sector-specific. It is universally "I know I have a problem; I don't know the shape of it; I don't know where to start." The service that wins the first conversation is the one that names the six structural problems in the professional's own language — not the language of the solution architecture.

## Language Layer 2: The Accountability Language

These phrases appear when the professional understands they are personally accountable — but the standard has not been defined for them.

**English:**
- *"I know what I'm not supposed to do. I don't know what I am supposed to do."*
- *"If I do nothing, am I covered? If I do something wrong, am I covered?"*
- *"I need a defensible position, not a perfect solution."*
- *"I signed something that commits me to something — I don't know what."*
- *"The standard feels like it's being written after I'm being evaluated against it."*
- *"I can't show what the AI contributed to my decision."*

**French:**
- *« Je sais ce que je ne dois pas faire. Je ne sais pas ce que je dois faire. »*
- *« Si je ne fais rien, suis-je couvert ? Si je fais quelque chose de mal, suis-je couvert ? »*
- *« J'ai besoin d'une position défendable, pas d'une solution parfaite. »*
- *« J'ai signé quelque chose qui m'engage à quelque chose — je ne sais pas quoi. »*
- *« La norme semble être écrite après que je suis évalué contre elle. »*
- *« Je ne peux pas montrer ce que l'IA a contribué à ma décision. »*

**What this tells us:** The accountability language is about inability to demonstrate, not unwillingness to act. The professional wants to be compliant and wants to be able to show compliance — they lack the architecture to do either.

## Language Layer 3: The Vendor Language

These phrases appear when the professional has interacted with AI vendors and discovered the terms are not designed for their professional context.

**English:**
- *"The vendor's contract says I'm the data controller. I didn't know that when I signed."*
- *"The vendor says their tool is 'Swiss hosted' — I don't know what that means for my obligations."*
- *"The AI updated and nobody told me. I have deliverables from a version that no longer exists."*
- *"The vendor says they're compliant. My carrier is asking me if I am."*
- *"I accepted the terms. I didn't read them. Nobody reads them."*

**French:**
- *« Le contrat du fournisseur dit que je suis le responsable du traitement. Je ne le savais pas quand j'ai signé. »*
- *« Le fournisseur dit que leur outil est 'hébergé en Suisse' — je ne sais pas ce que ça veut dire pour mes obligations. »*
- *« L'IA a été mise à jour et personne ne m'a prévenu. J'ai des livrables d'une version qui n'existe plus. »*
- *« Le fournisseur dit qu'il est conforme. Mon assureur me demande si je le suis. »*
- *« J'ai accepté les conditions. Je ne les ai pas lues. Personne ne les lit. »*

**What this tells us:** The vendor relationship is a trap in all four verticals. The vendor's terms create obligations the professional doesn't know about. The vendor's "compliance" is not the same as the professional's compliance.

## Language Layer 4: The Professional Body Language

These phrases appear when the professional has looked for authoritative guidance and found the professional body has not provided it.

**English:**
- *"The bar hasn't told us what 'adequate' AI governance means for a small firm."*
- *"My SRO hasn't said anything about AI. Does that mean I'm fine?"*
- *"FMH says I remain responsible. Nobody's published what that means in practice."*
- *"The professional body is months behind what's actually happening."*
- *"I called the association. They said they're 'working on guidance.' It's been a year."*

**French:**
- *« La barre ne nous a pas dit ce que signifie une gouvernance IA 'adéquate' pour une petite étude. »*
- *« Mon OSR n'a rien dit sur l'IA. Est-ce que ça veut dire que tout va bien ? »*
- *« La FMH dit que je reste responsable. Personne n'a publié ce que ça veut dire en pratique. »*
- *« L'ordre professionnel a des mois de retard sur ce qui se passe réellement. »*
- *« J'ai appelé l'association. Ils ont dit qu'ils 'travaillaient sur des lignes directrices.' Ça fait un an. »*

**What this tells us:** The professional body vacuum is a cross-vertical structural condition. The professional body is not providing guidance, and the professional does not know how to compensate for that absence.

## Language Layer 5: The Evidence Language

These phrases appear when the professional understands they need to be able to demonstrate compliance — but cannot currently do so.

**English:**
- *"I can't currently show my client what AI contributed to the recommendation they received."*
- *"I don't have a methodology for documenting AI-assisted decisions."*
- *"If my carrier asks me in six months, I don't have an answer ready."*
- *"I have no idea what the AI did between versions."*
- *"My documentation is scattered across emails and memory."*
- *"The evidence I need doesn't exist because I didn't know to build it."*

**French:**
- *« Je ne peux pas actuellement montrer à mon client ce que l'IA a contribué à la recommandation qu'il a reçue. »*
- *« Je n'ai pas de méthodologie pour documenter les décisions assistées par IA. »*
- *« Si mon assureur me pose la question dans six mois, je n'ai pas de réponse prête. »*
- *« Je n'ai aucune idée de ce que l'IA a fait entre les versions. »*
- *« Ma documentation est dispersée dans les e-mails et la mémoire. »*
- *« La preuve dont j'ai besoin n'existe pas parce que je ne savais pas la construire. »*

**What this tells us:** The evidence gap is a cross-vertical structural problem. The professional knows they need evidence but doesn't know how to produce it, and the window to build retrospective evidence is closing.

---

## Language Layer 6: The Three-Party Chain Language

These phrases appear when the professional discovers they are accountable for AI decisions made by parties they contracted but did not govern.

**English:**
- *"My sub-consultant used AI in my deliverable. I didn't authorize it specifically. My client is asking about it."*
- *"The tool my staff uses — I selected it. I don't know what it does with data."*
- *"I'm contractually responsible for deliverables that involved AI I didn't evaluate."*
- *"I delegated work. The delegate used AI. I'm responsible for the AI."*

**French:**
- *« Mon sous-consultant a utilisé l'IA dans mon livrable. Je ne l'ai pas autorisé spécifiquement. Mon client me pose des questions. »*
- *« L'outil que mon personnel utilise — je l'ai choisi. Je ne sais pas ce qu'il fait des données. »*
- *« Je suis contractuellement responsable des livrables qui ont impliqué une IA que je n'ai pas évaluée. »*
- *« J'ai délégué du travail. Le délégué a utilisé l'IA. Je suis responsable de l'IA. »*

**What this tells us:** The delegation chain creates accountability the professional has not acknowledged. The problem is not that the professional chose the wrong delegate — it's that the authorization architecture for AI delegation doesn't exist in standard engagement terms across any vertical.
