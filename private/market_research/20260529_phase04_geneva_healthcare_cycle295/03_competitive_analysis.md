## The White Space: What's Structurally Missing

### White Space 1: Independent Practice AI Accountability Methodology

**What is needed:** A Geneva-calibrated, physician-specific AI accountability methodology that:
- Addresses the specific liability exposure of independent practice (2–10 physicians)
- Is proportionate to the resources and governance capacity of small practice
- Maps to FMH human oversight principle with operational definitions
- Accommodates the single-person governance impossibility by providing alternative documentation structures
- Is specifically calibrated to carrier attestation requirements for independent practice

**Why it doesn't exist:** Existing solutions are either institutional-scale or generic. No service provider has built a Geneva-independent-practice-specific AI accountability methodology that addresses the specific structural constraints of small medical practice.

**Market opportunity:** A discrete service offering: **Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability Briefing** — a 90-minute structured session producing:
- A documented AI surface inventory appropriate for carrier attestation
- A proportionate human oversight documentation architecture
- An AI competency documentation framework meeting FMH implicit requirements
- A patient AI disclosure protocol calibrated to informed consent requirements
- A carrier pre-signing review for independent medical practice

---

### White Space 2: AI Tool Transition Protocol Service

**What is needed:** A methodology for managing AI tool transitions in independent medical practice that:
- Documents the AI state of the medical record at transition point
- Creates proper attribution architecture for historical AI-generated content
- Establishes documentation categories for AI-to-AI influence cascades
- Produces a transition record usable in future carrier challenges or adverse outcome reviews

**Why it doesn't exist:** No Geneva market service addresses AI tool switching in medical practice. Vendor migration support focuses on data migration technicalities, not the clinical record AI attribution architecture.

---

### White Space 3: Multi-Physician Practice Internal AI Governance Coordination

**What is needed:** A governance coordination framework for small group practices (2–10 physicians) that:
- Establishes practice-level AI governance decisions without requiring full-time governance staff
- Creates a documentation system that accounts for each physician's independent AI surface
- Produces a practice-level AI inventory that can satisfy carrier attestation at the practice level
- Addresses the specific accountability diffusion problem in multi-physician independent practice

**Why it doesn't exist:** Small group governance coordination is a structurally different problem from single-physician practice governance and from institutional governance. No Geneva-calibrated methodology exists for this specific scale.

---

### White Space 4: AI-Associated Adverse Outcome Documentation Architecture

**What is needed:** A post-incident documentation architecture specifically designed for AI-associated clinical events in independent practice, addressing:
- AI system state documentation at the time of the clinical event
- Physician's AI-influenced vs. AI-independent decision documentation
- Version/state documentation for AI tools active in the encounter
- The specific documentation categories required for cantonal and carrier reporting

**Why it doesn't exist:** Existing incident review infrastructure was designed for human clinical decision-making. No Geneva medical body or carrier has published AI-associated adverse outcome documentation requirements proportionate to independent practice.

---

### White Space 5: Patient AI Input Documentation Standard

**What is needed:** A clinical documentation protocol for handling patient-generated AI content entering the medical record, including:
- Documentation category for patient-provided AI analyses
- Physician evaluation documentation when independent judgment is exercised against AI input
- The specific documentation architecture for AI-influenced-but-not-AI-determined clinical decisions
- Informed consent framework for AI-influenced clinical encounters

**Why it doesn't exists:** No Geneva medical body has published guidance on the specific clinical documentation requirements for AI-influenced patient encounters in independent practice. Informed consent frameworks have not been updated to account for AI-assisted clinical decision-making.

---

### White Space 6: Carrier Pre-Signing Review for Independent Medical Practice

**What is needed:** A signatory-side pre-signing review service specifically designed for independent medical practitioners facing carrier AI attestation questionnaires, producing:
- A documented analysis of what the attestation covers at practice level
- A documented analysis of what creates personal MD liability vs. firm-level exposure
- A gap analysis between existing practice AI governance and attestation requirements
- A documented recommendation for how to sign (or not sign) with appropriate caveats
- A documentation build plan for addressing identified gaps

**Why it doesn't exist:** Wealth managers are developing this service for financial services MDs. No equivalent service exists for medical practitioners. The medical liability context is different (license vs. portfolio; FMH oversight obligations vs. FINMA obligations), requiring a distinct methodology.

## Competitive Positioning Intelligence

### Key Competitive Observation

The Geneva healthcare AI accountability market for independent practice is at the **same developmental stage that Geneva wealth management AI accountability was 12 months ago** — awareness is forming, carrier pressure is arriving, and no proportionate service exists for the specific ICP.

The window for positioning as the primary Geneva-calibrated independent practice AI accountability resource is open now. It will close as:
- Larger consulting firms begin offering "healthcare AI governance" services designed for institutional scale
- Carrier attestation requirements crystallize without Geneva-independent-practice calibration
- Vendors position their compliance documentation as sufficient for independent practice needs

### Positioning Imperative

DigitalCoa.ch's existing Geneva legal/finance/consulting AI accountability briefing format is directly transferable to the Geneva healthcare independent practice ICP. The same structural accountability logic applies — personal liability intensity, carrier attestation pressure, documentation window, nFADP exposure — with Geneva-specific calibration for the medical professional context.

**The opportunity:** The Geneva independent practice AI accountability briefing is the Phase 04 equivalent of what the Geneva small firm legal AI accountability briefing was for Phase 01 — the first mover in a category that will crystallize within 6–12 months.
