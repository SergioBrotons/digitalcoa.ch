# 01 — ICP Language: Geneva Independent Healthcare Practice

## Phase 04 | Cycle 295 | Geneva Healthcare ICP

---

## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

### Primary Accountability Frame

The Geneva independent physician does not think of AI governance in regulatory terms. They think in terms of **personal professional exposure** — what happens to them when something goes wrong and their name is on the record.

**Dominant internal monologue pattern:**
> *"I am the one who signed it. I am the one who will have to explain it. Not the software vendor, not the institution — me."*

This is the frame through which all AI-related anxiety enters the practice.

## Authentic ICP Vocabulary

### On AI Tool Deployment

| How They Actually Say It | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "I activated it because my assistant showed me how" | No governance decision was made; AI arrived through staff onboarding |
| "It was already in the system when I started" | Prior physician or staff introduced it; current physician inherits responsibility |
| "The software update added it" | Feature accumulation through vendor updates; physician never chose the AI capability |
| "I don't really know what it's doing in the background" | Black-box AI functionality embedded in core practice management software |
| "I just use it for admin" | Underestimation of what constitutes AI-assisted decision in records |

### On Professional Accountability

| How They Actually Say It | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "My license is on the line, not theirs" | Personal liability intensity higher than institutional liability |
| "I can't delegate my medical judgment to a machine" | Human oversight principle stated but operationally undefined |
| "If something goes wrong, it's my signature" | Single-physician accountability for outputs generated by tools they don't control |
| "The carrier questionnaire asked things I couldn't answer" | Carrier attestation arriving before any preparation; personal signature without foundation |
| "I didn't realize I was agreeing to that" | Click-through vendor terms creating obligations incompatible with FMH accountability |

### On Regulatory Obligations

| How They Actually Say It | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "FMH says I need human oversight but doesn't say what that means" | Principle without operational endpoint; structurally impossible to satisfy at small-practice scale |
| "The data protection law applies but it's not written for medical practice" | nFADP operating without Geneva-calibrated medical implementation guidance |
| "I assume the hospital has dealt with this" | HUG/CHUV institutional decisions assumed to set proportional standards; independent practices building in isolation |
| "Nobody from the canton has told me what to do" | Geneva cantonal guidance vacuum for independent practices; no proportionate pathway |

### On Patient Communication

| How They Actually Say It | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "I haven't told patients I'm using AI" | No AI-specific patient disclosure practice established |
| "If patients knew how much was automated they'd be concerned" | Informed consent gap for AI-assisted documentation and decision support |
| "The patient came in having already used AI on their symptoms" | Patient-generated AI content entering medical record; physician accountable for AI output they didn't generate |

### On Financial Exposure

| How They Actually Say It | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "I can't afford to do this wrong" | Small practice has no institutional buffer for liability events |
| "My insurance doesn't really cover this" | Professional liability coverage not designed for AI-adjacent clinical decisions |
| "A CHF 250,000 fine would close me" | Personal liability magnitude understood at visceral level |

## Key Emotional Drivers

1. **Personal ruin恐惧** — CHF 250,000 nFADP fine personal, name on license, career-ending consequence
2. **Carrier questionnaire panic** — Attestation arriving unexpectedly, personal signature on undefined adequacy standard
3. **Legacy AI inheritance** — System acquired before current physician made any governance decision
4. **Inability to explain own records** — AI-generated content under physician's name with no attribution or reasoning preservation
5. **Institutional exclusion** — Decisions forming at HUG/CHUV without transmission to independent sector; measured against standards they didn't participate in forming
6. **Impossibility of compliance** — Multiple frameworks operating simultaneously with no Geneva-calibrated coordination methodology

## Lead Magnet Language That Matches Their Vocabulary

**What activates them:**
- "Before your carrier asks" — external urgency forcing action
- "What you're actually responsible for" — personal liability frame
- "Personally accountable" — not institutional, personal
- " CHF 250,000" — specific, visceral fine magnitude
- "Your license" — career-ending consequence framing
- "No Geneva protocol exists" —告诉他们没有人给他们发过这些具体的指南

**What does NOT activate them:**
- Abstract regulatory framework descriptions
- Efficiency or productivity improvements
- "Best practice" language without personal liability angle
- Generic AI governance frameworks
