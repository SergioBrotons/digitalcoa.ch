## The Seven Deadlines

**1. FADP — NOW**
The revised Federal Act on Data Protection is operative. Human review required for automated decisions affecting individuals. Transparency obligations apply to AI-assisted interactions. Non-compliance exposure starts now.

**2. Federal AI Ordinance — January 1, 2025 (Already Operative — 15 Months)**
Most Geneva businesses are unaware that this has been in force since January 1, 2025. It classifies AI systems by risk level and requires documentation, human oversight for high-risk uses, and transparency disclosures. If you're using AI in customer service, HR, financial decision-making, or client advisory, your AI tools likely need classification under this ordinance. Fifteen months of non-compliance is not a minor gap.

**3. Swiss Fintech AI Consolidation — April 2026**
The IFZ FinTech 2026 study (published April 1, 2026) confirms: AI is now the #1 Swiss fintech category, surpassing distributed ledger technology and process digitalization. AI has become the dominant technology in Switzerland's financial sector — and the governance gap is proportionally significant.

**4. G7 Évian — June 15–17, 2026 (~10 weeks)**
The G7 summit in Évian affects Geneva directly. Swiss cross-border business faces potential sanctions and trade policy implications. Geneva consulting firms with SMB clients in trade-exposed sectors need to assess exposure. International patient data flows may attract scrutiny during the security period. The G7 is the first multi-sectoral Geneva event that cuts across all six advisory areas simultaneously.

**5. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (Healthcare — No Grace Period)**
Medical device database for AI systems used in patient care. No grace period. With new Swissmedic leadership now in place (Executive Director since January 2026), enforcement confidence is higher than it has ever been. If your medical practice uses AI diagnostic, imaging, or treatment planning tools, they need to be registered before July 1 — or you need to stop using them.

**6. EU GPAI Regulation — August 2, 2026 (~4 months)**
The EU General Purpose AI Regulation affects any Geneva business serving EU clients or using GPAI systems in work for European customers. Technical documentation, transparency, and incident reporting obligations apply. Four months after Swissdamed. Two major deadlines in the same calendar year.

**7. Ongoing — Sector-Specific Professional Standards + US Section 301**
Lawyers: SBA Guidelines (June 2024). Finance: FINMA AI guidance (2025). Healthcare: FOM human review requirements. Each sector has an active professional standard concurrent with the general framework. US Section 301 investigations add a trade policy pressure vector for Geneva financial services and consulting firms with US-facing clients.

## The Three Governance Dimensions

Every Geneva business using AI faces three concurrent governance requirements:

**Dimension 1: FADP Legal Obligations**
Human review for automated decisions. Transparency to data subjects. Data minimization and purpose limitation. Documentation of legal basis for processing.

**Dimension 2: Federal AI Ordinance Risk Classification**
Classify every AI tool by risk level. High-risk AI: mandatory human oversight, technical documentation, transparency disclosures. Limited-risk AI: transparency obligations only. Know what you're running.

**Dimension 3: Sector-Specific Professional Standards**
Your industry professional body may have issued AI guidance or standards. These are non-binding but set the standard of care. If a client, regulator, or court asks "what did a reasonable firm in your position do?", the professional standard is part of the answer.

**Legal:** 40% of Geneva Bar members using AI. 12% with a governance policy. 28-percentage-point gap. SBA Guidelines create professional standard on top of FADP and AI Ordinance. Federal AI Ordinance operative 15 months without meaningful compliance.

CARF/CRS 2026 implementation adds reporting obligations.

**Consulting:** GPAI August 2 deadline creates specific obligations for firms serving EU clients. **Real Estate:** FADP Art. 21 applies to automated valuation models and tenant screening. The belief-action gap is the largest: 90% of agents believe AI will transform the industry; fewer than 5% have a governance framework. The "pilot trap" in Geneva real estate is most acute.

**All Sectors:** Swiss structural labor shortage (400,000 workers, KOF 2026) makes AI adoption non-optional across all six sectors. AI is the primary response to workforce constraints. The businesses building compliant AI governance frameworks now will be the ones with the operational capacity to serve clients as the labor market tightens.
