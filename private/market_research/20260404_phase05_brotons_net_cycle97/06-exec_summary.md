# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 05 Brotons.net

## Research Question
What is the optimal content and positioning strategy for Brotons.net given the C97 Phase 01-04 research findings?

## Key Findings (C97 — NET-NEW)

### Finding 1: 2026 regulatory cascade is the dominant content opportunity for Brotons.net
Six major regulatory changes in H1 2026 (more significant than any single period since FinSA/FinIA 2020). No Geneva thought leader is currently publishing comprehensive bilingual (EN+FR) interpretative content on these changes.

**Implication:** Brotons.net should publish the "FINMA 2026 Radar" series immediately — before competitors fill the space.

### Finding 2: First-mover window is NOW — 4-8 weeks before market gets crowded
Borel & Barbey and KPMG Law are already publishing 2026 regulatory updates. They will expand to interpretative content within weeks.

**Implication:** Brotons.net must publish first FINMA 2026 Radar content within the next 4-8 weeks to establish thought leadership position.

### Finding 3: Hub architecture is validated — Brotons.net captures, Substack amplifies, DigitalCoa.ch converts
Brotons.net → Substack → DigitalCoa.ch → Cytria creates a coherent audience journey. Each platform serves a distinct role.

**Implication:** Continue hub architecture; strengthen the content bridge between Brotons.net capture and DigitalCoa.ch conversion.

### Finding 4: Bilingual EN+FR is Brotons.net's most durable competitive differentiation
Most Geneva thought leaders publish in one language only. Brotons.net's bilingual capability reaches both the international professional community (English) and the Geneva French-speaking professional community (French).

**Implication:** Every major piece of content should have both English primary and French executive summary.

### Finding 5: Brotons.net ICP overlaps all 4 market verticals — broad appeal confirmed
The audiences for Brotons.net content (legal, finance, consulting, NGO/tech) overlap with all Phase 01-04 verticals. A single regulatory change (e.g., nFADP enforcement) affects multiple verticals simultaneously.

**Implication:** "FINMA 2026 Radar" series should explicitly address implications across verticals.

### Finding 6: Competitive gap exists in executive-grade bilingual regulatory intelligence
Law firms publish in French; Big 4 publish in English but are expensive/corporate; no independent voice is publishing executive-grade bilingual regulatory intelligence for Geneva.

**Implication:** Brotons.net can own this precise positioning gap.
