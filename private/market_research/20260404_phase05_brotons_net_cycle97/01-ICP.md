# 01 — Brotons.net Positioning: ICP & Platform Strategy

## Brotons.net Platform Role (Validated C97)
Brotons.net is the personal brand hub — the authoritative presence that captures audiences and directs them toward appropriate actions:
- **DigitalCoa.ch** → for executive advisory services
- **Substack** → for ongoing thought leadership content
- **Cytria** → for implementation/operational services

## ICP for Brotons.net Content

### Primary Audience
- **Geneva-based executives** in financial services (banking, asset management, insurance)
- **Managing partners and GCs** at law firms and consulting firms
- **Founders and CEOs** of Geneva-based tech startups and NGOs
- **Senior professionals** aged 35-60 who make strategic decisions

### Audience Pain Points (From C97 Phase 01-04 Research)
1. **Regulatory overwhelm** — 6 major regulatory changes in H1 2026 alone; no consolidated interpretation available
2. **AI governance uncertainty** — what tools to use, how to govern, client data confidentiality
3. **Competitive anxiety** — fear of falling behind on technology and regulatory adaptation
4. **Time scarcity** — need for curated, actionable intelligence rather than raw information

### Content Consumption Patterns
- Short-form: LinkedIn posts, brief interpretations
- Medium-form: Brotons.net articles (1,500-3,000 words)
- Long-form: Substack deep dives and reports
- Recurring: FINMA regulatory briefings

## Platform Architecture (Validated)
| Platform | Role | Content Type |
|---|---|---|
| Brotons.net | Hub — authoritative presence | Interpretative articles, framework posts, SEO content |
| Substack | Content amplifier | Deep dives, reports, recurring briefings |
| LinkedIn | Distribution + engagement | Short-form, commentary, conversation |
| DigitalCoa.ch | Conversion | Service pages, case studies, consultation CTAs |

## Status: VALIDATED C97 — Hub architecture confirmed; 2026 regulatory content as priority
