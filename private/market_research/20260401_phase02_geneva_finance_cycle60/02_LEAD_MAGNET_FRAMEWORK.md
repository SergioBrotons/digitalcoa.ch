## Problem Statement

Geneva boutique wealth managers face a three-layer regulatory convergence that their compliance infrastructure was not designed to handle:

1. **CARF/CRS** — Automatic exchange of financial account information; Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework now adding crypto assets
2. **Federal AI Ordinance** — 15+ months operative
3. **EU AI Act** — GPAI obligations already in force since August 2, 2025; high-risk AI conformity assessments due August 2, 2026

FINMA's own survey (late 2024–early 2025) confirms the problem: 50% of Swiss financial institutions are using AI; 91% are using generative AI chatbots from BigTech providers; only 50% have an explicit AI strategy. The average boutique WM is flying blind while the regulatory horizon approaches at speed.

## Content Sections

### Section 1: The Three-Layer Regulatory Convergence
1. CARF/CRS: Automatic exchange of financial account information (AI used in client reporting and data management)
2. Federal AI Ordinance: Human oversight and technical documentation requirements
3. EU AI Act: GPAI obligations already in force; high-risk AI assessments due August 2, 2026

### Section 2: The WM AI Readiness Audit (6 Questions)
1. Do we have an AI inventory — documented, dated, specific to our WM practice?
2. Are our AI tools hosted on Swiss or EU infrastructure, or US BigTech cloud?
3. Do our AI vendor contracts address data residency, training data use, and EU AI Act compliance?
4. Have we assessed EU AI Act exposure — specifically whether we are "placing AI on the EU market"?
5. Do we have explainable AI (XAI) capabilities for our HNW clients?
6. Does our FINMA reporting and compliance documentation include AI risk factors?

### Section 3: The BigTech Dependency Risk
FINMA's explicit warning: outsourcing to third-party providers = key operational risk.
- 91% of Swiss financial institutions using BigTech AI providers
- Most using external providers, not in-house AI development
- Data sovereignty: client portfolio data on US-hosted AI infrastructure = regulatory exposure

### Section 4: Agentic AI — The Emerging Liability Layer
Autonomous portfolio rebalancing, automated tax-loss harvesting, AI-driven trade execution within defined parameters.
When AI acts (rather than advises), the governance obligation intensifies. Federal AI Ordinance human oversight requirement = direct operational requirement.

### Section 5: August 2, 2026 EU AI Act Preparedness Checklist for WMs
- [ ] AI inventory for all WM-specific applications
- [ ] EU AI Act exposure assessment (placing on EU market / output used in EU)
- [ ] GPAI vendor contract review (authorized representative requirement met?)
- [ ] FADP DPIA for AI-assisted client decision workflows
- [ ] XAI capability assessment for client-facing AI tools
- [ ] FINMA AI governance documentation aligned with FINMA Guidance 08/2024
- [ ] CARF/CRS + AI integration review (AI in compliance documentation)
- [ ] Human oversight protocols for any agentic AI applications
