### 1. EU AI Act GPAI Obligations Already In Force — Finance Sector Amplified Impact (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (via Phase 01 C60 research); applies to finance/WM sector

Geneva wealth managers and asset managers face amplified EU AI Act exposure compared to other sectors:
- **GPAI provider obligations since August 2, 2025:** Any Geneva WM using GPAI models (Claude, GPT-4, Gemini) for portfolio analytics, client reporting, or investment research faces EU AI Act transparency obligations if the output is used within the EU market
- **Asset management AI applications:** AI-driven asset allocation, risk modeling, and client portfolio management tools are likely within scope of high-risk AI classification under EU AI Act Annex III
- **Authorized representative requirement:** Swiss WMs placing AI-powered investment tools on the EU market must appoint an EU-based authorized representative (effective since August 2, 2025)

**Finance/WM implications (NET-NEW C60):** The combination of FINMA AI survey (C59: 50% of Swiss financial institutions using AI, 91% using generative AI chatbots) and EU AI Act GPAI obligations already in force creates an acute compliance gap. Most Geneva boutique WMs are unaware they may already be in breach of EU AI Act obligations.

### 2. Federal Council "No Swiss AI Act" — Finance Sector: Sectoral Regulation by Default (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Homburger.ch (via Phase 01 C60 research); applies to finance/WM sector

Federal Council decision (February 12, 2025): No horizontal Swiss AI Act. Instead, sector-specific regulation. Finance is explicitly named as one of the key sectors that will receive targeted AI rules alongside healthcare and digital platforms.

**Finance/WM implications (NET-NEW C60):** FINMA will likely issue sector-specific AI guidance for financial institutions as part of the Federal Council's sectoral approach. Geneva WMs should anticipate FINMA AI guidance evolving — and should proactively build governance frameworks ahead of new FINMA requirements. The sectoral rules will come; being ready early is a competitive advantage.

### 3. CARF + CRS + EU AI Act — Three-Layer Compliance Convergence for Geneva WMs (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Synthesis from Phase 01 C60 (Federal Council approach, EU AI Act timeline) + Phase 02 C59 carry-forward (CARF/CRS)

Swiss WMs face three converging compliance layers:
1. **CARF/CRS:** Automatic exchange of financial account information; Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) now adding crypto assets to the exchange framework
2. **Swiss Federal AI Ordinance:** Since January 1, 2025 (15+ months)
3. **EU AI Act:** GPAI obligations already in force; high-risk AI conformity assessments August 2, 2026

**Finance/WM implications (NET-NEW C60):** The compliance burden for Geneva boutique WMs is not additive — it is multiplicative. AI tools used for client reporting, portfolio management, and compliance documentation sit at the intersection of all three layers. A single AI-powered workflow may simultaneously trigger CARF data quality obligations, FADP data minimization requirements, and EU AI Act technical documentation requirements. This convergence is the specific problem that boutique WMs face — and the specific problem that generic compliance advisors cannot solve.
