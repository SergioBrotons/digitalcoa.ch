## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings Summary (C60 vs. Prior Cycles)

### Finding 1: EU AI Act GPAI Obligations — Finance Sector Amplified Impact
**What changed:** Geneva wealth managers face amplified EU AI Act exposure vs. other sectors. AI-driven asset allocation, risk modeling, and client portfolio management tools are likely within scope of high-risk AI classification under EU AI Act Annex III. The authorized representative requirement (effective August 2, 2025) applies to Swiss WMs placing AI-powered investment tools on the EU market.

**Why it matters:** Most Geneva boutique WMs are unaware they may already be in breach of EU AI Act obligations. Combined with FINMA's BigTech outsourcing risk warning, the compliance gap is immediate, not future.

**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (via Phase 01 C60 research); applies to finance sector

---

### Finding 2: Federal Council Sector-Specific Approach — FINMA Will Issue Targeted AI Rules
**What changed:** The Federal Council explicitly named finance as one of three key sectors (with healthcare and digital platforms) that will receive targeted AI rules under its sector-specific approach. The "no Swiss AI Act" decision means FINMA, not Parliament, will shape AI governance for financial institutions.

**Why it matters:** Geneva WMs should anticipate FINMA AI guidance evolving and proactively build governance frameworks ahead of new requirements. Being ready before the sectoral rules arrive is a competitive differentiator with clients and with regulators.

**Source:** Homburger.ch (via Phase 01 C60 research)

---

### Finding 3: CARF + CRS + EU AI Act — Three-Layer Convergence
**What changed:** AI tools used for client reporting, portfolio management, and compliance documentation sit at the intersection of three regulatory layers. A single AI-powered workflow may simultaneously trigger CARF data quality obligations, FADP data minimization requirements, and EU AI Act technical documentation requirements.

**Why it matters:** The compliance burden is multiplicative, not additive. Boutique WMs need a single integrated governance response — not three separate compliance exercises. This is the specific problem that generic compliance advisors cannot solve, and the specific problem that boutique WMs need expert help to address.
