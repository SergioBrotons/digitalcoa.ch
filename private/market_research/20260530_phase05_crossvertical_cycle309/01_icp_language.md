## How They Talk Across Verticals

The following language patterns hold across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs in Geneva. These are the phrases they use — not how we describe their situation, but how they articulate it.

---

## Universal Language Patterns

### On Personal Accountability Activating
- "I'm the one who signed. That means it's mine."
- "The form has my name on it. Not the firm's."
- "I can't hide behind the firm anymore — the carrier wants me personally."
- "Every time I sign something now, I read it twice. I didn't used to do that."

### On The Invisible Delegation Problem
- "I didn't know my associate was using that tool on client matters."
- "My medical assistant uses AI for patient notes. I didn't authorize it."
- "The sub-consultant is using AI on deliverables. I found out from the client."
- "My staff figured it out before I did. Now I need to catch up."

### On The "I Didn't Know What I Signed" Revelation
- "I signed the form thinking it was a formality. It wasn't."
- "The language said 'adequate supervision.' I didn't know that was a standard I had to define."
- "I certified to something I couldn't describe. That's what the form asked me to do."
- "My broker said it was routine. Then I read the French version."

### On The Competence-in-AI Problem
- "I can't evaluate the output. I just review the conclusion."
- "I'm responsible for decisions made by a system I don't understand."
- "The form asks if I 'adequately oversee' AI tools. I don't know what that means in practice."
- "I know what the tool does. I don't know what it did."

### On The Vendor-in-the-Circuit Problem
- "The vendor has my client's data. The carrier wants records from me."
- "My AI vendor isn't going to give me their system logs when the carrier asks."
- "The tool provider said they're compliant. I don't know what that means for me."
- "If something goes wrong, the vendor disappears. I'm the one left holding the attestation."

### On Broker Referral as Trust Activation
- "My broker wouldn't refer me to someone who caused a problem."
- "The broker called you first. That told me something."
- "The broker said this protects them as much as it protects me — I needed to hear that."
- "The broker has skin in the game. That's why I trust the referral."

### On Timing as a Market Signal
- "Three weeks before renewal. That's when firms call me."
- "I waited too long. The form is already submitted."
- "I should have done this six months ago. Now I'm behind."
- "The renewal window is when this becomes urgent — not when I should have started."

## Universal ICP Decision Trigger Phrase (C309 Update)

**EN:** "The question isn't whether you're using AI — you know you are. The question is whether the person whose name is on the form can actually produce evidence of what happened. And whether the vendor who holds your client's data will hand over their records when the carrier asks for them. I help you close both gaps — the personal accountability gap and the vendor records gap. Before the audit, not after."

**FR:** "La question n'est pas de savoir si vous utilisez l'IA — vous le savez. La question est de savoir si la personne dont le nom figure sur le formulaire peut réellement produire des preuves de ce qui s'est passé. Et si le fournisseur qui détient les données de votre client vous remettra ses dossiers quand l'assureur les demandera. Je vous aide à combler les deux lacunes — celle de la responsabilité personnelle et celle des dossiers des fournisseurs. Avant l'audit, pas après."

## Key Positioning Language (Universal Across Verticals)

1. **The personal accountability framing:** "The form has your name on it. Not the firm's. That's the first thing I tell every client — and the last thing they forget."

2. **The invisible delegation solution:** "Your team is using AI tools you didn't authorize. That's not a training problem — it's an accountability architecture problem. I build the system that makes delegation visible."

3. **The vendor records gap:** "Your AI vendor isn't going to hand over their system logs when the carrier asks. That's a records gap — and it's yours to close. I help you close it before the audit."

4. **The competence gap framing:** "You can't evaluate the output. You're not supposed to. What you are supposed to do is document that you reviewed it. That's the evidence the carrier wants."

5. **The broker protection framing:** "Your broker referred you because the broker who refers to me is protected. That's not a sales line — it's how the circuit works."

6. **The timing signal:** "Three weeks before renewal. That's when firms call me. Not because that's when the problem starts — but because that's when it becomes impossible to ignore."

- "You can't evaluate the AI output. You're supposed to review it. The evidence is in the review, not the evaluation."
- "Your broker sent you because the broker who refers to me is protected. That's the signal that matters."
- "Three weeks before renewal. That's when firms call me — not because that's when the problem starts, but because that's when it becomes impossible to ignore."
