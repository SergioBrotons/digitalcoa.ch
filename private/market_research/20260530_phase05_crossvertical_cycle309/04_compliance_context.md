# 04 — Compliance Context: Cross-Vertical Synthesis
## Phase 05 C309 | Cross-Vertical Synthesis | All Four Verticals

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Patterns

The following regulatory patterns hold across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare. These are the structural compliance pressures that are activating simultaneously across all four verticals in Geneva.

---

## The Universal Regulatory Pattern

### Pattern 01 — The Evidence Standard Has Shifted from Policy to Contemporaneous Record

**The shift:** The2024 carrier standard asked "do you have a policy?" The2026 carrier standard asks "demonstrate your actual practice." These are structurally different deliverables. A policy document describes what should exist. An evidence record shows what actually happened. The evidence standard shift is structural, not cyclical.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: Carrier attestation now requires evidence of actual supervision practice, not just existence of supervision policy
- Finance: FINMA examination questions now ask for evidence of human oversight in actual investment decisions, not just policy documentation
- Consulting: Client RFP now asks for evidence of methodology defensibility in specific deliverables, not just firm-level AI policy
- Healthcare: Carrier attestation now requires evidence of physician-personal oversight of AI tools used by staff, not just practice-level policy

**The regulatory mechanism:** The carrier form creates the standard. The ICP's signature commits them to it. The carrier audit tests whether the evidence exists. The policy infrastructure built in 2024 cannot satisfy the evidence requirements of 2026.

**Implication:** Documentation debt is not fixable by updating policies. The evidence production system is the correct response to the 2026 standard.

---

### Pattern 02 — Personal Liability Has Activated Through Multiple Vectors

**The activation:** Personal liability is no longer a theoretical risk — it is an active enforcement reality across all four verticals. The vectors of activation are different in each vertical, but the structure is the same: the individual professional is personally exposed in a way that firm-level coverage does not discharge.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: Art.321 CC (Swiss Criminal Code) personal liability for professional decisions; Ordre proceedings name individual attorneys; carrier attestation creates personal certification
- Finance: nFADP Art.63 personal liability for investment decisions; FINMA requires managing director personal attestation; liability allocation for AI-influenced decisions unresolved
- Consulting: nFADP Art.52 personal broker exposure; carrier attestation requires personal broker certification; proposal methodology defensibility is personally attributable
- Healthcare: Physician-personal carrier certification activating; FMH human oversight is physician-personal; Swissmedic operator obligations are physician-personal

**The regulatory mechanism:** Each vertical has a specific personal liability trigger. The common structure is: individual professional signs attestation → individual professional is personally accountable → carrier audit tests individual professional's evidence → firm-level coverage does not protect individual professional from personal liability for attestation misrepresentation.

**Implication:** The service must be designed for the individual professional, not the firm. The evidence system must produce personal evidence of personal accountability, not firm-level compliance documentation.

### Pattern 03 — Multi-Authority Documentation Non-Alignment Is a Structural Problem

**The problem:** The Geneva professional operates under multiple authorities with different language requirements, different documentation standards, and different enforcement mechanisms. Documentation built to satisfy one authority may not satisfy another. The multi-authority environment creates documentation requirements that no single service addresses.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: Carrier form in English + Ordre proceedings in French + Geneva civil procedure (undefined AI disclosure protocol) = three documentation environments, two languages
- Finance: FINMA guidance in French + institutional DDQ in English + EU AI Act (English primary) = three documentation environments, two languages
- Consulting: Client RFP in English + carrier attestation in English + nFADP in French = three documentation environments, two languages
- Healthcare: Carrier form in English + FMH proceedings in French + Swissmedic obligations in French = three documentation environments, two languages

**The regulatory mechanism:** No single authority has published an auditable standard that satisfies all other authorities. The ICP must navigate a documentation environment where the English form and the French proceedings are different documentation requirements — not just different languages.

**Implication:** Bilingual documentation is a regulatory design requirement, not a localization task. The service that builds documentation for both regulatory language environments simultaneously creates a Geneva-specific compliance position that English-only competitors cannot match.

### Pattern 04 — Human Oversight Is Required But Undefined Across All Verticals

**The gap:** FINMA, FMH, Ordre, and carrier attestation requirements all require "adequate human oversight" of AI tools. None of them define what that means operationally for a small Geneva practice. The principles-based guidance is consistent across verticals. The operational definition is absent across verticals.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: "Adequate supervision" of AI tools — no published standard for small law firm; managing partner accountable without reference protocol
- Finance: FINMA requires human oversight — no operational definition for boutique wealth manager; "adequate" is principles-based only
- Consulting: Proposal methodology defensibility requires human oversight — no defined protocol for small consulting boutique
- Healthcare: FMH human oversight stated without operational protocol — no small practice reference standard; physician operates without procedure

**The regulatory mechanism:** The accountability standard is principles-based ("adequate," "appropriate," "sufficient"). The enforcement mechanism tests actual practice against the principles-based standard. The ICP who cannot define "adequate" cannot prove they met it. The gap between principles-based guidance and operational practice is where personal liability lives.

**Implication:** The service that defines "adequate human oversight" operationally for the specific practice — not as a general framework, but as a specific evidence protocol — is the service that closes the gap between principles-based guidance and operational practice.

### Pattern 05 — Delegation Accountability Is Structurally Unresolved

**The unresolved problem:** The accountable professional delegates AI-assisted tasks to staff, associates, sub-consultants, or medical assistants. The delegation creates a chain of accountability the principal cannot fully supervise. The delegation chain is not documented. The principal is personally liable for all of it.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: Associates using AI on firm matters without managing partner authorization; personal subscriptions on firm matters; no delegation chain documentation
- Finance: Portfolio associates implementing AI tools without managing director authorization; AI shadow systems; no delegation chain documentation
- Consulting: Sub-consultants using AI on deliverables without principal's knowledge; client-facing work product affected; no delegation chain documentation
- Healthcare: Medical assistants using AI on patient data without physician authorization; physician personally liable; no delegation chain documentation

**The regulatory mechanism:** The delegation is legal and common. The accountability for delegation outcomes is the principal's. The documentation of the delegation chain is absent. The carrier audit asks for evidence of "supervision" — and the principal has no documentation of what was delegated, to whom, and how it was reviewed.

**Implication:** The delegation accountability documentation is a discrete service component that no competitor offers. The delegation chain documentation (who delegated what, to whom, how it was reviewed, what the output was) is the evidence the carrier actually wants — not a policy statement about delegation principles.

---

### Pattern 06 — Vendor Contract Terms Define the Accountability Mechanism (And Are Silent)

**The structural problem:** The AI vendor's contract defines the accountability mechanism for AI-assisted decisions. Most vendor contracts are silent on: (1) record production obligations when the carrier asks, (2) liability allocation for AI-influenced decisions, (3) data processing obligations under nFADP, (4) operator/processor classification under Swissdamed (healthcare) or FINMA guidance (finance). The vendor contract is the document that defines who is accountable for what — and it is almost universally silent on the accountability questions that matter most.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- Legal: Vendor compelled records privilege pathway — vendor records can be subpoenaed by opposing counsel; attorney-client privilege doesn't protect vendor-held records
- Finance: Vendor AI governance contract gap — liability allocation for AI-influenced investment decisions unresolved; vendor contracts silent on record production
- Consulting: Sub-consultant delegation accountability gap — vendor/sub-consultant contracts don't address AI output documentation obligations
- Healthcare: Swissmedic operator classification — vendor "certification" doesn't clarify physician operator status or obligations; vendor contracts silent on this

**The regulatory mechanism:** The vendor contract is the document that allocates accountability between the ICP and the vendor. If the vendor contract is silent, the ICP bears the full accountability burden for AI-assisted decisions — even when the vendor's system caused the problem. The vendor contract review is the intervention that clarifies the accountability allocation before the audit.

**Implication:** Vendor contract review is a discrete, bounded, high-value service component. The vendor contract determines the accountability mechanism. Most ICPs have not had their vendor contracts reviewed for these specific terms. The vendor contract review is the entry point that reveals the full scope of the accountability gap.

## Cross-Vertical Compliance Synthesis

### The Universal Compliance Problem

The universal compliance problem across all four verticals is the same: the ICP is personally accountable for evidence of AI governance practice that they have not produced and cannot produce using the tools and documentation they currently have. The compliance debt is real. The personal liability is active. The evidence gap is structural.

### The Resolution Architecture

The compliance resolution architecture that works across all four verticals:

1. **Vendor contract review** — identify accountability allocation gaps before the audit
2. **Carrier form pre-signing review** — prevent the next accountability gap from being created
3. **Retroactive documentation reconstruction** — produce evidence of the 12–24 month historical period
4. **Delegation chain documentation** — document who delegated what, to whom, how it was reviewed
5. **Forward evidence capture system** — continuous documentation of AI use going forward
6. **Bilingual documentation design** — documentation that functions in both regulatory language environments

### The Multi-Authority Compliance Framework

The compliance framework that addresses the multi-authority environment:

| Authority | Language | Documentation Requirement | Standard |
|---|---|---|---|
| Carrier Attestation | English | Evidence of practice | Contemporaneous record |
| Professional Order (Ordre/FINMA/FMH) | French | Evidence of professional oversight | Principles-based "adequate" |
| Client/Institutional DDQ | English | Evidence of methodology | Deliverable-specific |
| nFADP | French/English | Evidence of Art. compliance | Specific article requirements |

## Net-New Finding: The Compliance Convergence That Is C309-Specific

**Finding:** The compliance convergence that is new in C309 is the simultaneous activation of personal liability across all four verticals through different vectors — carrier attestation naming individuals, nFADP articles naming individuals, professional orders naming individuals, and vendor records creating individual exposure. In prior cycles, personal liability was a directional risk. In C309, it is an active multi-vector reality.

**Implication:** The service that produces personal evidence of personal accountability — not firm-level compliance documentation — is the service that matches the current compliance environment. The evidence system must be designed for the individual professional who signed the attestation, not the firm that houses them.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your personal liability is active. The carrier asked about you specifically. The Ordre has your name. The nFADP article names you. The evidence system I build is yours personally — not the firm's."
