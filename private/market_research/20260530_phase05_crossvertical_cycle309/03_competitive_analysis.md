## Cross-Vertical Competitive White Space

The following white spaces are confirmed across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare. No competitor has claimed these positions in the Geneva market.

---

## The Six White Spaces

### White Space 01 — Evidence Architecture as Core Product (Not a Feature)

**The white space:** All competitors offer frameworks, policies, templates, or training. No competitor has positioned evidence architecture as their core product — the thing they do, not the thing they also do. Evidence architecture is the unique capability that produces contemporaneous records calibrated to carrier form requirements. This is structurally different from policy documents.

**Why it remains white space:** The market has been offering frameworks for two years. The ICP who tried frameworks and found them insufficient has no alternative. The evidence architecture position is occupied by no one. The ICP who needs evidence — not another framework — has no Geneva service provider.

**Positioning implication:** "The market offers frameworks. I produce evidence." — directly addresses the gap between what the market offers and what the carrier actually requires.

---

### White Space 02 — Retroactive Documentation Reconstruction (Not Forward-Only)

**The white space:** Every competitor addresses forward documentation ("document from today forward"). No competitor offers retroactive reconstruction as a primary service. The majority market segment — the ICP who adopted AI 12–24 months ago and needs evidence of that period — has no dedicated service.

**Why it remains white space:** Retroactive reconstruction is harder than forward documentation. It requires a specific methodology (available evidence architecture, document types, retrospective calibration). Competitors default to forward-only because it's easier and scalable. The ICP who needs retroactive reconstruction has been told "you can't document the past" by every service provider — and has accepted that as truth.

**Positioning implication:** "Most services help you before you adopt AI. I help you after." — directly addresses the majority market segment that no competitor serves.

---

### White Space 03 — Pre-Signing Carrier Form Review (Highest-Value Intervention, Zero Competitors)

**The white space:** No Geneva competitor offers pre-signing carrier form review as a discrete service. The pre-signing intervention is the highest-value moment: it prevents the accountability gap from being created rather than addressing it after the fact. Every competitor offers post-hoc remediation.

**Why it remains white space:** Pre-signing review requires the advisor to understand the accountability language in the form, translate it into an evidence requirement, and produce a gap analysis before the ICP signs. This requires competence in both the vertical regulatory environment and the evidence production methodology. No competitor has built this capability as a discrete service offering.

**Positioning implication:** "Before you sign — send me the form. I'll tell you what your signature commits you to and what evidence you need before you sign it. Same working day." — direct, bounded, highest-value, no competitor offers it.

---

### White Space 04 — Vendor Records Pathway (The Unresolved Accountability Gap)

**The white space:** No competitor addresses the vendor records gap — the structural problem that the AI vendor holds records the ICP is accountable for producing, but the vendor is not obligated to produce them to the carrier. This is the liability allocation problem that vendor contracts are silent on.

**Why it remains white space:** The vendor records gap requires vendor contract analysis, record production pathway design, and evidence architecture that doesn't depend on vendor cooperation. This is a legal-technical problem that most compliance advisors won't touch. The ICP who understands this problem knows no one has solved it for them.

**Positioning implication:** "Your AI vendor won't hand over their system logs when the carrier asks. That's a records gap — and it's yours to close. I help you close it before the audit." — names the problem no competitor names.

---

### White Space 05 — Bilingual EN/FR Documentation as Standard Product (Not Translation)

**The white space:** Every competitor offers English-only documentation. No competitor offers bilingual EN/FR documentation as a standard product feature. Geneva operates in two regulatory languages — English (carrier forms, client DDQs, EU AI Act) and French (Ordre proceedings, FINMA, Swiss domestic regulation). Documentation built in English may not serve proceedings conducted in French.

**Why it remains white space:** Bilingual documentation requires design decisions made during document construction — not translation after English documents are complete. Most competitors are English-only and invisible in the Francophone circuit. The white space is both the documentation design problem and the circuit participation problem.

**Positioning implication:** "Geneva has two regulatory languages. Your documentation has to work in both. Most services build in English and hope the French doesn't matter. I build for both simultaneously." — structural competitive advantage no competitor has claimed.

---

### White Space 06 — Broker-Centric Service Design (Primary Channel, Unaddressed by Competitors)

**The white space:** All competitors design services for the ICP directly. No competitor designs for the broker as the primary audience. The broker is the primary trust intermediary and the primary acquisition channel — and no competitor has built a service description that addresses broker protection explicitly.

**Why it remains white space:** Broker-centric service design requires the service to function as a broker protection mechanism, not just an ICP solution. The broker's exposure reduction must be visible in the service structure, the output, and the engagement terms. This is a structural design decision — not a marketing line. No competitor has made it.

**Positioning implication:** "I work for the broker as much as the client. When the client has evidence of AI governance practice, the broker's exposure drops. That's why the broker refers — and that's why the broker keeps referring." — structural description of how the referral circuit operates, not a sales line.

## Cross-Vertical Competitive Landscape

### The Four Competitor Types and Their Gaps

| Competitor Type | What They Offer | Their Gap | Cross-Vertical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Big Four / Law Firms | Frameworks, policies, training | No evidence production; English-only; large-team model | Legal, Finance, Consulting |
| Legal Tech Vendors | Tool-specific compliance | No retroactive reconstruction; no carrier form specificity; no bilingual | Legal |
| Regulatory Consultants | Guidance documents, gap analyses | No evidence architecture; no operational protocol; no Geneva-specific | Finance, Consulting |
| Healthcare Compliance | Policy templates, CME programs | No physician-personal evidence; no Swissmedic operator clarity; no staff delegation tracking | Healthcare |

### The Structural Moats

1. **Retroactive methodology** — the specific process for reconstructing 12–24 months of undocumented AI use into carrier-ready evidence
2. **Bilingual documentation design** — simultaneous EN/FR document construction, not translation
3. **Broker circuit integration** — the referral relationship architecture that makes the broker a repeat referrer
4. **Pre-signing form review** — the bounded, high-value intervention that prevents the next accountability gap

### What No Competitor Has Built

- Evidence architecture as core product (not a feature)
- Retroactive reconstruction as primary service (not a niche)
- Pre-signing carrier form review as discrete offering
- Vendor records pathway as structured service
- Bilingual EN/FR as standard product feature
- Broker-centric service design

## Net-New Finding: The Competitive Distinction That Is C309-Specific

**Finding:** The competitive distinction that is new in C309 is the convergence of three market activations that make the evidence architecture position more urgent and more defensible than in prior cycles:

1. **Carrier forms now naming individuals personally** — the accountability architecture is no longer abstract; it's the ICP's personal liability
2. **Carriers now asking for vendor-held records** — the vendor records gap is no longer theoretical; it's a live audit question
3. **Staff AI use now visible to carriers** — the invisible delegation gap is no longer invisible; carriers are asking about it

**Implication:** The evidence architecture position is now the only position that matches the current market state. Competitors who offer frameworks, policies, or training are offering solutions to a 2024 problem. The market has moved to a 2026 problem — and no competitor has moved with it.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The market is still offering frameworks. The carrier is already asking for evidence. I produce what the carrier actually wants — not what the market still offers."
