# Phase 01: Geneva Legal — ICP
## Cycle 35 | 2026-03-31

---

## ICP Summary

**Target:** Small law firms in Geneva, 2–20 attorneys (solo to small partnership)  
**Core problem:** 43% of law firms have NO formal AI policy AND no plans to create one (Wolters Kluwer March 2026) — yet 90%+ of lawyers use AI tools individually. This is a 46-point governance gap (69% individual vs 23% formal policies). Law firms are exposed to FDPIC enforcement, professional liability, and client data breaches — with no grace period.

**Window:** Swissdamed July 1 (~13 weeks), Andermatt May 26 (~8 weeks), EU AI Act Aug 2 (~19 weeks). FDPIC enforcement absent from legal sector = timing signal.

---

## ICP Components

### Problem Statement
Small Geneva law firms face a compounding risk stack: individual AI use is near-universal (90%+) but formal firm-level AI governance is rare (23% of firms). The FDPIC is building its enforcement portfolio across transport, retail, and social media. Legal sector has not yet been targeted — but the pattern is clear. Professional secrecy obligations + FADP + Swissdamed make AI governance for small law firms urgent and unique.

### Ideal Client Profile
- 2–20 attorneys, Geneva-based
- Uses AI tools individually (research, drafting, contract review)
- No formal AI policy, no AI governance framework
- Likely using US-based AI tools without understanding data residency obligations
- Aware of AI importance but uncertain about compliance obligations
- Deadline pressure: Swissdamed July 1 (~13 weeks), Andermatt May 26 (~8 weeks)

### Urgency Levers
1. **43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans** (Wolters Kluwer March 2026) — primary urgency
2. **90%+ individual AI use** (Shadow AI = FDPIC + professional liability exposure)
3. **FDPIC enforcement gap** — legal sector absent from 1,053 complaints + 6 enforcement actions
4. **46-point governance gap** (69% individual vs 23% formal policies)
5. **Swissdamed July 1 ~13 WEEKS** — NO GRACE confirmed
6. **Andermatt May 26 ~8 WEEKS** — FDPIC conference
7. **Professional secrecy** — AI tools + client data + US servers = potential breach
8. **DPIA required** — for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence)
9. **Managing director liability** — CHF 250,000 for intentional FADP failures

### Market Size
Geneva bar (OGarage): ~1,200 registered lawyers; small firms (1-5 attorneys) estimated 60-70% of total = ~720–840 addressable small law firm professionals.

## FADP Obligations Summary (Law Firm Context)
1. **Transparency**: disclose purpose + functionality + data sources of AI processing
2. **Right to know**: users must know they interact with AI; training opt-out required
3. **Privacy by Design**: built into AI system planning from start
4. **DPIA**: required for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence = likely high-risk)
5. **Prohibited AI**: mass facial recognition, social scoring
