### Active Obligations (NOW)

**FADP (Sep 1, 2023 — operative)**
- Technology-neutral: directly applies to AI-supported data processing
- FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation
- Enforcement powers strengthened under revised FADP
- FADP Art. 5(c): data processing must not violate personality rights

**FADP Key Requirements for Law Firms:**
1. **Transparency**: disclose purpose + functionality + data sources of AI
2. **AI interaction disclosure**: users must know they interact with AI
3. **Training opt-out**: data used for model training requires consent
4. **Privacy by Design**: built into AI planning from earliest stages
5. **DPIA**: required for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence = likely high-risk)
6. **Prohibited AI**: mass facial recognition, social scoring

**Professional Secrecy + Data Residency:**
- Swiss Code of Obligations / Civil Code: attorney-client privilege obligations
- US CLOUD Act risk for US-based AI tools
- Swiss-hosted solutions required: CASUS (zero data retention confirmed)
- FDPIC expects purpose + functionality + data sources disclosure
