**Primary entry point.**

Before adopting any AI governance framework: what AI tools are your lawyers already using without formal authorization?

**Shadow AI = FDPIC Art. 5(c) violation** (personality rights processing without adequate safeguards)
**Shadow AI = professional liability exposure** (advice given based on AI hallucinations)

### Section 3: FADP Obligations — What the Law Actually Requires

**Technology-neutral + already operative (Sep 1, 2023)**

1. **Transparency**: Must disclose purpose, functionality, data sources of AI processing to affected individuals
2. **AI interaction disclosure**: Clients must know they interact with AI
3. **Training opt-out**: Consent required before using input data for model training
4. **Privacy by Design**: Built into AI planning from start, not afterthought
5. **DPIA**: Required for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence = likely high-risk)
6. **Prohibited AI**: Mass facial recognition, social scoring

**Professional secrecy obligations** add a Swiss-specific layer:
- US-based AI tools may expose client data to CLOUD Act
- Swiss-hosted solutions (e.g., CASUS) required for sensitive matters

### Section 5: DPIA — The Overlooked Obligation

**Most firms don't know they likely need one.**

Data Protection Impact Assessment required when AI-based data processing poses HIGH RISK to personality or fundamental rights.

For law firms, this likely includes:
- AI contract analysis (due diligence, M&A)
- AI document review for litigation
- AI legal research with personal data exposure

**DPIA threshold**: If the AI tool processes personal data AND could cause risk to individuals, a DPIA is required — even if you're only using it internally.

### Section 7: Professional Secrecy + AI Data Residency

**Swiss-specific barrier to generic AI adoption.**

Swiss attorneys have professional secrecy obligations under the Swiss Code of Obligations and Civil Code. Using US-based AI tools (OpenAI, Claude, Gemini) without explicit data residency guarantees could violate:
- Attorney-client privilege
- FADP Art. 5(c) (data processing must not violate personality rights)
- Cantonal bar association rules

**Only use AI tools that:**
1. Host data exclusively in Switzerland or EU
2. Have zero data retention at the LLM provider level
3. Provide written confirmation of no third-party data access
