## Problem Statement (4 layers)

### Layer 1: Swissdamed — ~2 Months: You Are Already Behind
Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Data池) enters force July 1, 2026 with no grace period. With Swissmedic's new Executive Director Vincenza Trivigno now 3 months into her tenure during the implementation window, the enforcement posture is no longer predictable. You are not in a preparation window — you are in a remediation window. What can you complete by July 1? What is your day-1 plan?

### Layer 2: AI Tools Are Part of Your Swissdamed Picture
Swissdamed covers medical device data processed electronically. If your AI tools process medical device data — for scheduling, documentation, clinical decision support, or telemedicine — they are part of your Swissdamed scope. Federal AI Ordinance (in force Jan 1, 2025) adds additional human review requirements for AI-assisted clinical decisions.

### Layer 3: International Patient Data — The G7 Complication
Geneva medical practices serve an internationally mobile patient population. G7 Évian (June 15-17, 2026) security perimeter includes Geneva. US-hosted AI tools processing international patient data face compounded exposure: FADP Art. 13 + Swissdamed + potential G7-adjacent data governance requirements. This is the Geneva-specific dimension that most compliance templates miss.

### Layer 4: Healthcare Labor Shortage — AI as Operational Necessity
The KOF finding of 400,000 worker shortage (March 2026) applies to healthcare: nursing shortages, primary care shortages, medical specialist shortages. AI documentation tools, scheduling automation, and telemedicine AI are not just compliance tools — they are workforce strategy. The practices that adopt AI governance early will have a staffing advantage.
