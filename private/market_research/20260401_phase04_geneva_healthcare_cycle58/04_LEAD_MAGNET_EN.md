## Swissdamed in ~2 Months: The Honest Assessment

Swissdamed (the Swiss Medical Device Data Framework) enters force July 1, 2026. There is no announced grace period.

Swissmedic's new Executive Director, Vincenza Trivigno, has been in place for 3 months during the implementation window. Three months is sufficient time for a new leadership team to establish enforcement posture. The assumption that Swissdamed will have a "soft launch" is no longer reliable.

You are not in a Swissdamed preparation window anymore. You are in a remediation window. The question is no longer "will Swissdamed be enforced?" — the question is "how exposed is your practice on July 1, and what can you close before then?"

## The Four-Layer Problem

### Layer 1: Swissdamed — ~2 Months Remaining

Swissdamed requires medical practices to document and govern electronic medical device data flows. If your AI tools process medical device data — for patient scheduling, documentation, clinical decision support, or telemedicine — they are part of your Swissdamed scope.

The gap between "we're aware of Swissdamed" and "we're Swissdamed-compliant" is significant for most small Geneva practices. The awareness is high (most practitioners have heard of it). The compliance progress is low.

### Layer 2: Your AI Tools Are Part of the Swissdamed Picture

This is the layer most small practices miss. Swissdamed covers medical device data processed electronically. AI tools that process this data — even indirectly — need to be documented in your Swissdamed compliance framework. This includes:
- AI-assisted scheduling systems that pull patient device data
- AI documentation tools that process clinical notes
- AI clinical decision support tools
- AI telemedicine platforms

Federal AI Ordinance (in force January 1, 2025) adds human review requirements for AI-assisted clinical decisions. Both frameworks apply concurrently.

### Layer 3: The Geneva International Patient Dimension

Geneva's private medical practices serve an internationally mobile patient population. G7 Évian (June 15-17, 2026) security arrangements cover Geneva canton — the same canton where your practice operates.

If your AI tools process data for international patients (EU, US, or other nationalities), you face compounded exposure:
- FADP Art. 13 (cross-border data transfers)
- Swissdamed (medical device data governance)
- Potential G7-adjacent data governance requirements during the summit period

This is the Geneva-specific dimension that most generic Swissdamed compliance templates miss entirely.

### Layer 4: Healthcare Labor Shortage Makes AI Operationally Necessary

Switzerland faces a structural healthcare workforce shortage (parallel to the 400,000-worker general shortage identified by KOF in March 2026). AI documentation tools, scheduling automation, and telemedicine AI are not just compliance considerations — they are workforce strategy. The practices that adopt compliant AI governance now will have an operational advantage as the workforce shortage deepens.

## Swissdamed Self-Assessment: Where Does Your Practice Stand?

**Answer these 10 questions:**

1. Have you completed an inventory of all electronic systems that process medical device data in your practice?
2. Have you assessed which of your AI tools process medical device data within Swissdamed scope?
3. Do you have documented human review processes for AI-assisted clinical decisions?
4. Have you classified your AI tools according to the Federal AI Ordinance risk categories?
5. For any AI tools processing data for international patients: have you assessed cross-border data transfer obligations?
6. Does your FADP-compliant data processing documentation include AI tool usage?
7. Have you assessed whether your AI vendors transfer patient data outside Switzerland?
8. Do you have an escalation protocol for AI errors or anomalous outputs in clinical contexts?
9. Has your practice identified a Swissdamed compliance owner (even if part-time)?
10. Do you have a specific plan for what to complete before July 1, 2026 — and what to address in the weeks after?

**Score 8-10:** You are Swissdamed-ready. The framework helps you document and defend your position.

**Score 5-7:** You have a remediation plan to build. The checklist is your starting point.

**Score 0-4:** You are exposed on July 1. This framework is your emergency starting point.

## The Swissdamed + AI Governance Checklist

**Before July 1, 2026 — Priority Actions:**

1. **Medical device data inventory:** List every electronic system that processes patient medical device data
2. **AI tool classification:** Identify which AI tools process medical device data (this is the Swissdamed + AI intersection)
3. **Human review documentation:** Document who reviews AI outputs before they affect patient care
4. **FADP Art. 13 assessment:** For each AI tool, assess cross-border data transfer implications
5. **International patient data mapping:** If you serve non-Swiss patients, map which AI tools process their data
6. **Vendor contracts:** Ensure AI vendor contracts include data processing agreements compliant with Swissdamed
7. **Staff training:** Ensure clinical and administrative staff know which AI tools are in use and the review requirements
8. **Day-1 plan:** Identify what will be in place on July 1 and what gaps will remain

## The International Patient Data Annex (Geneva-Specific)

If your Geneva practice serves international patients, the G7 Évian period (June 15-17, 2026) adds a practical dimension:

**During the G7 period:**
- Review any AI tools processing international patient data
- Consider deferring non-urgent AI-supported patient communications to after June 17
- Document your data governance approach for the G7 period (this is itself a Swissdamed governance action)

**AI vendor assessment for international patient practices:**
- US-hosted AI tools: FADP Art. 13 + Swissdamed compounded exposure
- EU-hosted AI tools: GDPR considerations for EU-resident patients
- Swiss-hosted AI tools: data sovereignty advantage for internationally mobile patients
