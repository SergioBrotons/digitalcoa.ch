# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing
## Phase 02 | Cycle C305 | Geneva Finance / Boutique Wealth Management

---

## What Exists in the Market

### Category1: General AI Governance Frameworks

**What it is:** Generic AI governance frameworks, checklists, and policy templates. Offered by consulting firms, legal firms, and industry associations. Available globally and in Swiss context.

**Limitations for Geneva boutique WM:**
- Generic — not Geneva-calibrated, not Swiss regulatory-specific
- Policy-first — produces a document describing what should exist, not evidence of what exists
- Not sized for 50–200 person firms — designed for larger institutions with compliance functions
- Does not address continuous AI oversight methodology
- Does not distinguish between carrier attestation evidence and Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence
- Does not address institutional DDQ response file production
- Does not address AI vendor integration chain mapping

**Examples:** Big 4 AI governance frameworks, ISACA AI governance materials, ISO42001 (general, not Geneva-calibrated), industry association AI governance guidance.

---

**Limitations for Geneva boutique WM:**
- FINMA-first — does not address nFADP Art. 63 personal liability or cross-framework documentation coherence
- Firm-level governance documentation — does not produce Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence package
- Policy-oriented — produces documentation satisfying FINMA, not evidence demonstrating oversight capability
- Does not address institutional client DDQ response file production
- Does not address continuous AI oversight methodology for24/7 systems
- Does not address AI vendor integration chain mapping
- FINMA documentation ≠ nFADP Art. 63 protection (structural gap, not incremental gap)

**Examples:** Swiss banking law advisory firms, FINMA regulatory advisory practices at Big 4.

## What's Missing (NET-NEW from C304)

### Missing Service 1: Institutional DDQ Response File Production

**What it is:** A structured document responding to institutional client AI governance due diligence questionnaires — organized by question, with specific documentation attached, demonstrating the firm's AI oversight capability.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The institutional DDQ as competitive gate is a new phenomenon. The service category has not been named or defined in the Geneva market. Firms are attempting to respond with general AI governance policies — the wrong document type.

**What it requires:**
- Question-by-question mapping to available documentation
- Identification of documentation gaps per question
- Evidence production for gaps (not policy production)
- Institutional-client-specific formatting and presentation
- Competitive positioning through documentation quality

**Market gap size:** Direct revenue consequence — mandates won or lost based on DDQ response quality. No Geneva service exists specifically for institutional DDQ response file production for boutique WM firms.

---

### Missing Service 2: AI Vendor Integration Chain Mapping

**What it is:** A documented data flow mapping exercise showing which client data touches which vendor infrastructure across the firm's AI tool stack — including integration connections between tools, jurisdiction of vendor servers, and nFADP Art. 26 and Art. 9 exposure identification.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The integration chain problem is newly visible as firms have connected multiple AI tools without mapping data flows. The service category has not been named or defined in the Geneva market.

**What it requires:**
- AI tool stack inventory (all tools, all connections)
- Data flow mapping per tool and per integration
- Vendor server jurisdiction identification
- PII processing scope per tool
- nFADP Art. 26 (data processing agreements) and Art. 9 (sensitive data) exposure identification
- Remediation recommendations per exposure

**Market gap size:** Active exposure in every boutique WM firm with more than three connected AI tools. No Geneva service exists specifically for AI vendor integration chain mapping.

---

### Missing Service 3: Continuous AI Oversight Methodology Development

**What it is:** A Geneva-calibrated methodology defining what continuous AI oversight means for boutique WM firms — specifying oversight frequency, documentation format, supervision log structure, and evidence production requirements for 24/7 AI systems.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The methodology question is unanswered at the Geneva level. Firms are attempting to apply discrete documentation approaches (annual reviews, matter-based records) to continuous AI systems — a structural mismatch that produces no adequate documentation.

**What it requires:**
- Definition of "continuous oversight" in Geneva regulatory context
- Geneva-calibrated oversight frequency recommendations (specific to FINMA and nFADP expectations)
- Documentation format specifications (what a supervision log must contain, how frequently it must be updated)
- Evidence production architecture for continuous AI systems
- Firm-size calibration (specific to 50–200 person boutique WM firms)

**Market gap size:** Every boutique WM firm with automated rebalancing, algorithmic allocation, or continuous AI systems has this problem. No Geneva-calibrated methodology exists.

---

### Missing Service 4: Cross-Framework Documentation Architecture (FINMA + nFADP + Carrier Attestation)

**What it is:** A unified documentation architecture that simultaneously produces evidence satisfying FINMA requirements, nFADP Art. 63 personal due diligence requirements, and carrier Form v4 evidence requirements — one architecture serving three protection objectives.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The market treats FINMA compliance, nFADP compliance, and carrier attestation as separate workstreams. No service exists that produces unified architecture serving all three simultaneously. The structural gap (FINMA documentation ≠ nFADP protection) is not addressed by any existing service.

**What it requires:**
- Understanding of what each framework actually requires (not the surface question, the underlying evidence need)
- Mapping of documentation types that serve multiple frameworks simultaneously
- Evidence production that satisfies all three protection objectives in one production effort
- Architecture that avoids duplicating documentation work while serving all three frameworks

**Market gap size:** Every boutique WM firm subject to both FINMA and nFADP has this problem. The documentation debt created by doing two separate documentation projects (FINMA + nFADP) is significant. Unified architecture eliminates the duplication.

---

### Missing Service 5: Art. 63 Personal Due Diligence Evidence Package (Distinct from Firm-Level Governance Documentation)

**What it is:** A personal due diligence evidence package specifically serving the managing director's individual criminal liability protection under nFADP Art. 63 — distinct from firm-level governance documentation, specifically designed to demonstrate personal oversight activities, controls implemented, and accountability.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The market conflates firm-level governance documentation with personal due diligence evidence. Carrier attestation evidence protects the carrier's subrogation position. Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence protects the managing director's personal assets. These are different document types serving different protection objectives. No Geneva service distinguishes between them or produces both.

**What it requires:**
- Understanding of what Art. 63 actually requires (personal due diligence, personal oversight, personal accountability)
- Evidence types that demonstrate personal oversight (not firm-level governance documentation)
- Documentation of personal decisions, personal controls, personal oversight activities
- Personal liability exposure mapping and mitigation
- Coordination with legal counsel on personal liability exposure

**Market gap size:** Every managing director at a boutique WM firm has personal Art. 63 exposure. The majority believe carrier coverage and personal protection are the same thing. No Geneva service produces the Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence package as a distinct document type.

---

### Missing Service 6: EU AI Act Deployer Due Diligence File for Non-EU Vendor Configurations

**What it is:** A deployer due diligence file demonstrating the due diligence performed on non-EU AI vendors, the conformity gaps identified, and the compensating controls implemented — the specific documentation required to answer the EU AI Act deployer obligation question when vendor EU Declaration of Conformity is structurally unavailable.

**Why it doesn't exist:** The EU AI Act deployer obligation with non-EU vendors is a newly visible structural problem. No Geneva service has defined what "deployer due diligence" looks like when vendor conformity is structurally unavailable. The service category has not been named or defined.

**What it requires:**
- EU AI Act deployer obligation analysis (specific to firm's EU client nexus)
- Non-EU vendor conformity gap assessment
- Deployer due diligence documentation (what was done, what was found, what was implemented)
- Compensating controls documentation
- Ongoing monitoring architecture for vendor conformity changes
- Geneva-calibrated deployer due diligence methodology

**Market gap size:** Every boutique WM firm serving EU clients with non-EU AI vendors has this problem. The problem is active now, not future. No Geneva service addresses it specifically.

## First-Mover Assessment

**What closes the window:** First to publish a specific, operational service definition for each of the six missing service categories. First to produce evidence of client outcomes (mandates retained, carrier renewal completed, FINMA documentation accepted). First to define the Geneva standard for continuous AI oversight methodology.

**Competitive risk:** Generic AI governance frameworks from global consulting firms could be localized to Geneva.
