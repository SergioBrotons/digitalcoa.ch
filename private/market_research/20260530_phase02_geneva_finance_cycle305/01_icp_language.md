# ICP Language: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management — C305 Phase 02
## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems (Distinct from C304)

---

## The ICP Profile

Boutique wealth management firm, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable. Concerned about institutional client AI governance questionnaires, carrier attestation requirements, FINMA expectations, and nFADP Art. 63 personal liability. Multiple AI tools in portfolio construction, analytics, and client management. Serving HNWI, UHNWI, family offices, institutional clients, and EU-based clients.

---

## ICP Language Patterns (NET-NEW from C304)

### On the Institutional Client Questionnaire as Competitive Gate

- "We received the institutional DDQ. It's40 questions on AI governance."
- "We don't have answers for most of them. We might lose the mandate."
- "The RFP scoring weights AI governance. We didn't know that until we read the criteria."
- "The bigger family offices are starting to require this. It's not optional anymore."
- "We can show our investment process. We can't show our AI oversight process."
- "The institutional client isn't asking for a policy. They're asking for records."

### On the AI Vendor Integration Chain Exposure

- "We have five AI tools. They all connect to each other. I didn't map that until last month."
- "The portfolio analytics tool pulls from the CRM. The CRM has client data. The analytics tool processes that data on a US server."
- "We didn't think about the integration chain. We just connected the tools."
- "Which AI tool is actually processing client PII? All of them. What's the data flow? Nobody mapped it."
- "The vendor terms for each tool are separate. The integration between them creates exposure nobody's looking at."

### On FINMA Supervisory Expectations Shifting

- "FINMA sent a letter. Not a questionnaire — a letter asking us to demonstrate our AI oversight."
- "Last year they asked if we had a policy. This year they asked us to show the records."
- "The standard moved. We didn't move with it."
- "Our last FINMA interaction was about whether we answered the AI question. This one is about what we can show."
- "The supervision expectation is different from what we prepared for."

### On Carrier Payment vs. Personal Liability Discovery

- "The carrier said they'd cover us. Then we found out — coverage doesn't protect my personal assets."
- "Article 63 is criminal. The carrier pays the settlement. I face the criminal exposure personally."
- "The lawyer explained it: carrier indemnification and personal criminal liability are separate legal tracks."
- "I can be personally liable under Article 63 even if the carrier pays everything else."
- "Carrier coverage and personal protection — I thought they were the same thing. They're not."

### On Continuous Oversight Methodology Gap

- "We need to oversee AI systems continuously. Nobody told us what continuous oversight documentation looks like."
- "Is it a weekly sign-off? A monthly review? A real-time log? There's no Geneva standard."
- "The systems operate 24/7. Our oversight documentation is annual. There's a structural mismatch."
- "We need a methodology — not just a template. Something that tells us what continuous oversight means for our specific setup."
- "I can document a decision. I don't know how to document continuous oversight."

### On Demonstrating Oversight Capability vs. Having a Policy

- "We have an AI governance policy. The institutional client asked for evidence of oversight capability."
- "A policy describes what should exist. Evidence shows what actually exists."
- "The gap isn't the policy. The gap is that we can't show the oversight actually happened."
- "I can tell you what we do. I can't show you the records that prove it."
- "The institutional client doesn't want to know our philosophy. They want to see our controls in action."

## French ICP Language (NET-NEW from C304)

### Sur le questionnaire institutionnel comme filtre concurrentiel

- "On a reçu le DDQ institutionnel. C'est 40 questions sur la gouvernance IA."
- "On n'a pas de réponses pour la plupart. On risque de perdre le mandat."
- "La pondération du RFP inclut la gouvernance IA. On ne le savait pas."
- "Les grands family offices commencent à l'exiger. Ce n'est plus optionnel."

### Sur l'exposition de la chaîne d'intégration vendor IA

- "On a cinq outils IA. Ils se connectent tous entre eux. Je ne l'ai pas cartographié avant le mois dernier."
- "L'outil d'analytique de portfolio tire du CRM. Le CRM a des données clients. L'outil analytique traite ces données sur un serveur américain."
- "On n'a pas pensé à la chaîne d'intégration. On a juste connecté les outils."
- "Quel outil IA traite réellement les données personnelles? Tous. Quel est le flux de données? Personne ne l'a cartographié."

### Sur le changement des attentes de supervision FINMA

- "FINMA a envoyé une lettre. Pas un questionnaire — une lettre demandant de démontrer notre supervision IA."
- "L'année dernière, ils ont demandé si on avait une politique. Cette année, ils demandent de montrer les records."
- "La norme a changé. On n'a pas changé avec elle."

### Sur la découverte séparation couverture carrier / responsabilité personnelle

- "L'assureur a dit qu'il nous couvrirait. Ensuite on a découvert — la couverture ne protège pas mes actifs personnels."
- "L'Article 63 est criminel. L'assureur paie le règlement. Je fais face à l'exposition criminelle personnellement."
- "La couverture de l'assureur et la protection personnelle — je pensais que c'était la même chose. Ce ne l'est pas."

### Sur le manque de méthodologie de supervision continue

- "On doit superviser les systèmes IA en continu. Personne ne nous a dit à quoi ressemble la documentation de supervision continue."
- "Les systèmes fonctionnent24/7. Notre documentation de supervision est annuelle. Il y a un décalage structurel."
- "On a besoin d'une méthodologie — pas juste un modèle. Quelque chose qui nous dit ce que 'supervision continue' signifie pour notre configuration."

### Sur la démonstration de capacité de supervision vs. avoir une politique

- "On a une politique de gouvernance IA. Le client institutionnel demande une preuve de capacité de supervision."
- "Une politique décrit ce qui devrait exister. Une preuve montre ce qui existe réellement."
- "L'écart n'est pas la politique. L'écart est qu'on ne peut pas montrer que la supervision a réellement eu lieu."

## Emotional State

The Geneva boutique wealth management managing director is not primarily worried about abstract AI risk. They are worried about:

- Losing a mandate they should win because their AI governance documentation doesn't exist
- FINMA's expectations having moved without warning from "do you have a policy" to "show us your records"
- Discovering that carrier coverage and personal criminal liability protection are separate legal tracks
- Not having a methodology for what continuous AI oversight documentation should look like
- Realizing their AI governance policy and their ability to demonstrate oversight capability are different things

The emotional driver is competitive exposure and personal liability discovery — not regulatory compliance in the abstract. They want to protect their mandates, their personal assets, and their firm's competitive position. They want someone who understands the Geneva context specifically and can produce evidence, not just frameworks.

## NET-NEW Language Markers (C305 Phase 02 vs. C304)

1. **"on risque de perdre le mandat"** — institutional mandate loss anxiety now present (vs. C304's "institutional questionnaire" framing)
2. **"pondération du RFP inclut la gouvernance IA"** — RFP scoring mechanism as competitive gate now explicit
3. **"la chaîne d'intégration"** — AI vendor integration chain as named exposure category (vs. C304's individual vendor terms drift)
4. **"quel outil traite réellement les données personnelles"** — tool-specific PII processing question now articulated
5. **"FINMA a envoyé une lettre"** — FINMA letter as distinct from FINMA questionnaire, shifting standard
6. **"la norme a changé"** — supervisory expectation shift now named as active change event
7. **"la couverture ne protège pas mes actifs personnels"** — carrier coverage vs. personal asset protection distinction
8. **"l'Article 63 est criminel"** — Art. 63 criminal nature now explicit in ICP vocabulary
9. **"supervision continue"** — continuous oversight as named methodology gap (vs. C304's "continuous AI authorization")
10. **"capacité de supervision"** — oversight capability vs. policy language
11. **"montrer les records"** — show the records vs. describe the policy
12. **"méthodologie"** — methodology request vs. template request (specific ask for systematic approach)
