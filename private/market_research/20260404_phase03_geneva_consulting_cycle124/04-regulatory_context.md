## Key Regulatory Developments (C124)

### 1. Gender Quota — Compliance Deadline January 2026
- Swiss Code of Obligations Art. 734f: 40% underrepresented gender on boards effective 1 January 2026
- Executive committee 30% quota effective 2028
- Shareholder advisory firms (Ethos, ISS) voting against non-compliant boards
- Geneva listed companies with compliance gap: estimated 30-40% of board seats need to change

### 2. Switzerland-EU Institutional Agreement — Ratification Pending
- Negotiations concluded December 2024; ratification process ongoing
- Swiss Parliament: Spring Session 2026 debate; ratification could complete H2 2026
- Key implications: market access, data flows, free movement, institutional mechanisms
- Geneva SME impact: ~60% of Geneva exports to EU affected

### 3. Swiss Supply Chain Due Diligence — LkSG Alignment Debate
- Parliamentary initiative 21.453 under debate; consultation expected 2026
- German LkSG already affecting Swiss-German subsidiaries (3,000+ DE employees)
- NGO PublicEye campaigns increasing public pressure for Swiss domestic law
- Geneva commodity traders: most affected sector

### 4. SECO Digitalization Support Programs
- Swiss SME digitalization programs: underutilized (estimates: <20% of eligible SMEs apply)
- Digital Ready Program (programme готовность), ERP support subsidies
- SECO advisory budget: CHF 50M/year available; absorption rate ~15%
- Opportunity: advisory firms can help SMEs access available support

### 5. Geopolitical Risk — Sanctions and Export Controls
- Swiss aligned with EU Russia sanctions (8 packages); ongoing updates
- SECO dual-use goods controls: increased scrutiny for China-destined exports
- U.S.-China trade tensions creating spillover for Swiss companies
- Geneva commodity traders: sanctions compliance now operational requirement
