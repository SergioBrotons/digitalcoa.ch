# 01 — ICP: Geneva SMEs, Mid-Market Companies & Family Businesses

## Target Segment: Geneva SMEs, Mid-Market Companies, Family Businesses

### Firm/Client Profile
- **Size:** CHF 10M–500M revenue
- **Employees:** 50-500 employees
- **Type:** Privately held (majority), family-owned (common), some listed (second tier)
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; some Romandie presence; international operations
- **Status:** Geneva economic backbone; CCIG estimates ~15,000 SMEs in Geneva canton

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CEO / Managing Director | Owner or professional manager | Strategic direction, profitability, succession |
| CFO / Finance Director | Professional manager | Cash flow, reporting, financing |
| COO / Operations Director | Process-focused executive | Efficiency, digitalization, supply chain |
| Board Member / Owner | Investor or family member | Governance, risk oversight, strategy |
| HR Director | Senior HR professional | Talent acquisition, compensation, compliance |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Geneva-based mid-market industrial companies (precision instruments, specialty chemicals)
- Commodity trading SMEs (smaller than Vitol/Trafigura)
- Professional services firms (consulting, engineering, architecture)
- Family-owned retail and hospitality businesses (Geneva-specific)
- International organizations suppliers (UNOG, WHO, ITU procurement)
- Luxury goods SMEs (watchmaking components, specialty food)

### Pain Points (C124 Update)
1. **Swiss SME productivity crisis** — digitalization gap widening vs. EU; CCIG reporting skills shortages blocking productivity improvement; Geneva SMEs need operations and digitalization advisory
2. **Switzerland-EU institutional agreement implications** — Geneva businesses unaware of market access/data flow implications; need advisory now before ratification
3. **Supply chain due diligence (LkSG-aligned)** — Swiss Parliament debating law; commodity traders and manufacturers need scope assessment and compliance systems
4. **Board governance reform (gender quota)** — listed companies need board composition advisory; second-tier listed Geneva companies affected
5. **Neutral status geopolitical risk** — Swiss businesses with international operations facing sanctions compliance, dual-use goods, reputational risk
6. **Interim CFO shortage** — full-time CFO hiring unaffordable for mid-market SMEs; interim CFO demand surging

### Buying Triggers
- Institutional agreement ratification confirmed
- Supply chain due diligence law passed
- Gender quota compliance deadline approaching
- Succession planning need (founder approaching retirement)
- Major operational disruption (loss of key customer, supply chain break)

### Buying Barriers
- Price sensitivity: SMEs compare advisory fees against direct cost savings
- Trust gap: unfamiliar advisory firms struggle vs. existing accountant/bank relationships
- Decision speed: family business decisions can move slowly
- Implementation gap: advisory without implementation often seen as worthless

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva domestic SMEs
- English increasingly necessary for international operations
- German useful for cross-border CH-EU matters
- Pragmatic, results-oriented; want clear ROI on advisory spend
- Relationship-based; referrals from trusted networks critical
