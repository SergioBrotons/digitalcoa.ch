## Content Outline

### Section 1: What the Institutional Agreement Means for Geneva Businesses
- Current state: bilateral agreements at risk without framework
- What the agreement changes: institutional mechanisms, state aid rules, market access continuity
- What doesn't change: bilateral agreements remain in force, no EU accession
- Timeline: ratification process and expected dates
- Key sectors affected in Geneva: financial services, commodity trading, professional services

### Section 2: Data Flows — What Businesses Need to Know
- Data adequacy: current Swiss-EU situation and agreement implications
- Cross-border data transfer requirements for Geneva-EU commercial relationships
- GDPR implications for Swiss companies handling EU personal data
- Practical steps: what businesses should do now to prepare

### Section 3: Market Access — Continuity and New Opportunities
- Services trade: what the agreement preserves for Geneva service providers
- Public procurement: improved access to EU markets for Swiss companies
- Financial services: passporting implications for Geneva banks and asset managers
- Action items: businesses should review current EU contracts for continuity clauses

### Section 4: Regulatory Alignment — Managing Compliance
- Level playing field provisions: what Switzerland commits to
- Dynamic alignment: how future EU rules will be adopted
- Sectors most affected: financial services, telecommunications, transport
- Compliance roadmap: what to do in the 6 months before ratification

### Section 5: Geneva SME Decision Checklist
- 10-point checklist: where does my business stand on each dimension
- Immediate actions: what to do in the next 30 days
- Medium-term planning: what to prepare before ratification
- Professional support: when to engage external advisors
