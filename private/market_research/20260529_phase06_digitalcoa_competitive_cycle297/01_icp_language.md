## Cross-Vertical Patterns — What All Four ICPs Say About the Advisory Gap

### Pattern 1: "I signed something. I don't know what I committed to."
The carrier attestation is the crystallizing moment across all verticals. Personal signature, undefined adequacy standard, no pre-signing review. This is the universal acute trigger.

### Pattern 2: "The framework is right but operationally useless."
SAV, FINMA, FMH, nFADP — all described as conceptually correct and operationally undeliverable at boutique scale. The gap between principle and implementation is unnamed and unfilled.

### Pattern 3: "I have something I can't enumerate."
AI surface across legal (staff tool activation), finance (multi-carrier + institutional questionnaire), consulting (multi-tool stack), healthcare (partner AI adoption). The inability to enumerate creates certification against an undefined surface.

### Pattern 4: "When something goes wrong, I improvise."
No Geneva-specific incident response protocol for any vertical. The 48–72 hour evidence window is unnamed. The improvisation under pressure creates secondary exposure.

### Pattern 5: "The Geneva-specific guidance doesn't exist."
SAV AI guidelines operationally undeliverable. Swissdamed classification methodology absent. nFADP Art.9 AI vendor classification undefined. FINMA AI guidance non-existent. Every vertical faces Geneva-specific accountability questions with no Geneva-specific answers.

### Pattern 6: "I need someone who understands Geneva."
The moat is structural — circuit intelligence, carrier form knowledge, institutional pattern accumulation — not replicable from outside Geneva. The ICP wants Geneva-participant advisory, not framework-delivered advisory.

## Language Gaps on Current Site

### Gap 1: No Problem-Naming in ICP Language
The site describes what DigitalCoa.ch does ("decision systems," "governance," "clarity") without naming the specific unnamed accountability gaps that brought the ICP to the conversation. The ICP needs to hear their problem named before they believe the solution is for them.

### Gap 2: No Geneva-Specific Accountability Vocabulary
"Geneva-based" signals location. It does not signal participation in the Geneva accountability ecosystem. The ICP needs to know that the advisory comes from inside the circuit, not just from someone who works in Geneva.

### Gap 3: No Personal Liability Anchor
The ICP signs carrier forms personally. The site does not address personal accountability at any point. The ICP needs to see their personal liability position named before they engage.

### Gap 4: No Vertical-Specific Entry Point
Legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs arrive with different acute triggers. The site has one generic entry point for all. Each vertical needs a specific problem statement before the common framework is offered.
