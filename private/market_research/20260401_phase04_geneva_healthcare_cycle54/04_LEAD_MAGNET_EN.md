# The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework
**Swissdamed Edition — Navigating the July 1, 2026 Compliance Deadline**

---

## The Situation Has Changed

If you operate a small or independent medical practice in Geneva or French-speaking Switzerland, two things have happened that change your AI governance position.

**Swissdamed became mandatory on July 1, 2026.** The national database for medical devices now requires registration of all medical devices placed on the Swiss market — including those incorporating AI. There is a transition period until December 31, 2026 for completing registrations, but if your practice uses AI-powered medical devices that fall into the vigilance category — serious incidents, field safety corrective actions, or trends — registration is immediate from July 1, 2026, with no grace period.

**The physician retains ultimate responsibility.** Switzerland's sector-specific approach to AI regulation means that accountability for AI-assisted clinical decisions doesn't transfer to the AI vendor. When an AI-assisted diagnostic recommendation is wrong, the physician is responsible. Your governance framework is not just a compliance requirement — it is your professional protection.

This framework helps you understand what the Swissdamed deadline means for your practice, what the human-in-the-loop principle means for your accountability, and what steps to take in the three months before July 1, 2026.

## Part 1: Swissdamed Registration Audit

Before July 1, 2026, every medical device on the Swiss market must be registered in Swissdamed. This includes AI-powered medical devices — Software as a Medical Device (SaMD).

### Decision 0: What AI Medical Devices Does Your Practice Use?

- [ ] Do you use AI tools for clinical diagnostics — imaging analysis, pathology, radiology?
- [ ] Do you use AI tools for patient monitoring — vital signs, glucose, cardiac?
- [ ] Do you use AI tools for medication management or decision support?
- [ ] Do you use AI-powered administrative tools that process patient data?
- [ ] Are all AI medical devices registered in Swissdamed before July 1, 2026?

### Understanding Swissdamed Categories

Swissdamed registration requirements depend on the device category:

- **Standard medical devices:** Registration required by July 1, 2026, with transition until December 31, 2026
- **Vigilance cases:** Immediate registration required from July 1, 2026 — no transition period (serious incidents, field safety corrective actions, trends)
- **AI-powered SaMD:** Subject to Swissmedic AI Guidelines — classification and conformity requirements apply

### Your Swissdamed Action Items
- [ ] Identify every AI-powered medical device currently in use
- [ ] Confirm whether your AI device vendors are registered economic operators (Swiss Single Registration Number — CHRN)
- [ ] Determine whether your devices fall into vigilance categories requiring immediate registration
- [ ] Complete Swissdamed registration before July 1, 2026 for vigilance devices

## Part 2: Swissmedic AI Guidelines — SaMD Conformity

Swissmedic has established AI guidelines for Software as a Medical Device (SaMD). The key principle: manufacturers bear responsibility for qualifying, classifying, and ensuring conformity of their AI products.

### Assess Your SaMD Position

- [ ] Has your AI medical device been classified according to Swissmedic guidelines?
- [ ] Does your device vendor provide conformity documentation?
- [ ] Are your AI medical devices providing clinical performance data?
- [ ] Has your vendor registered in Swissdamed as an economic operator?

### The Manufacturer Responsibility Principle

Under Swissmedic's approach, the manufacturer is responsible for AI product conformity — not the physician or practice. However, the practice is responsible for selecting compliant devices and using them within their intended purpose. Your due diligence obligation includes verifying vendor compliance.

## Part 3: FADP — Patient Data in AI Systems

The Federal Act on Data Protection has been operative since September 1, 2023. Patient data processed through AI tools is among the most sensitive personal data regulated under the FADP.

### Patient Data Governance

- [ ] Do your AI tool vendor agreements include FADP-compliant data processing terms?
- [ ] Is patient consent for AI processing documented and current?
- [ ] Are AI tools processing patient data configured for Swiss data storage (or FADP-equivalent protection)?
- [ ] Can you demonstrate what patient data was processed, by which AI tool, and for what purpose?

### Local Deployment vs Cloud: The Swiss Sovereignty Question

The CHUV's Meditron open-source medical AI, entering testing in May 2026, demonstrates the value of local deployment for Swiss medical practices. Open-source, locally-deployed AI keeps patient data in Switzerland — eliminating CLOUD Act exposure and ensuring FADP compliance through architecture.

- [ ] Are your AI tools Swiss-hosted or US-cloud?
- [ ] Does your vendor provide data residency commitments in writing?
- [ ] Have you assessed CLOUD Act exposure for non-Swiss AI infrastructure?

## Part 4: Human-in-the-Loop — Physician Accountability

Switzerland's human-in-the-loop principle means physicians retain ultimate responsibility for AI-assisted clinical decisions. This is not a regulatory preference — it reflects the professional and legal reality of medical practice.

### Your Accountability Framework

- [ ] Does your practice have a documented human oversight policy for AI-assisted decisions?
- [ ] Are all AI-assisted clinical recommendations reviewed by a physician before patient communication?
- [ ] Is physician training on AI-assisted tools documented?
- [ ] Is your professional liability coverage adequate for AI-assisted clinical practice?

### The Error Accountability Chain

When an AI-assisted recommendation leads to patient harm, the accountability chain is: AI vendor → physician → practice. The AI vendor is responsible for product conformity. The physician is responsible for clinical judgment. The practice is responsible for governance adequacy. No part of this chain transfers to the patient.

### Swiss Primary Care Physician Context

Swiss primary care physicians show 69% positive attitudes toward AI despite only 52.9% reporting adequate AI familiarity. Eighty percent want AI for administrative support. Seventy-three percent want AI for image analysis. The demand is there; the expertise to govern it safely is not uniformly present.

## Part 5: Shadow AI Audit — What AI Is Already in Your Practice?

Most small medical practices have more AI in use than they realize. Administrative AI often enters through existing software updates — practice management systems, billing tools, appointment scheduling.

### The Hidden AI Inventory

- [ ] Do any of your diagnostic devices use AI processing without explicit purchase decisions?
- [ ] Are any AI tools running on personal devices used for practice work?
- [ ] Do your AI vendors use your patient data for model training (FADP implication)?
