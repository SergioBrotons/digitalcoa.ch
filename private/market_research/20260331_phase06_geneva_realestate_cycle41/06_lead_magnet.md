# Phase 06 Geneva Real Estate — Lead Magnet: "The Small Real Estate Agency AI Governance Question" — Cycle 41

**Geneva Small Real Estate Agencies — AI Governance Readiness Framework**

---

## C41 Lead Magnet Framing

### The Core Insight
**75% of real estate agencies are using AI — but most have no formal governance.**

75% of Swiss RE firms use AI/ML.
Only ~28% have formal AI policies.
That's a ~47-point governance gap — the widest of all 6 verticals.

And FADP has been operative since September 1, 2023 — 2.5+ years.
GAIGI (the industry body) has published no public AI guidance.

**The opportunity is retroactive compliance: govern the AI you already use.**

---

## Retroactive Compliance: The Unique Entry Point

**Most RE agencies are already using AI — they just haven't governed it yet.**

- AI-assisted property valuations (without formal disclosure)
- AI-generated listing descriptions (without transparency)
- AI-assisted tenant screening (without human review protocols)
- AI-powered market analyses (without formal policy)

Retroactive compliance reframes AI governance from "stop using AI" to "govern what you're already using."

This is uniquely compelling for RE agencies that feel overwhelmed by "AI governance."

---

## The 7-Section Framework

**Section 1: Shadow AI Audit (Decision 0)**
Identify all AI tools used by agents and staff (approved and unapproved).
Property valuations, listings, tenant screening, market analyses.
Risk: FADP Art. 5(c) transparency gap, Art. 21 human review gap.

**Section 2: FADP Art. 5(c) Transparency Compliance**
AI-assisted property valuations require disclosure.
AI-generated listings require disclosure.
Update client communication templates.

**Section 3: Property Valuation AI (Likely High-Risk)**
AI property valuations = likely high-risk under EU AI Act Annex III.
Require DPIA before deployment.
Human review required for final valuations.

**Section 4: Tenant Screening AI (Likely High-Risk)**
AI tenant screening = likely EU AI Act Annex III high-risk.
Human review required for screening decisions.
FADP Art. 21 applies to automated tenant decisions.

**Section 5: Retroactive Compliance ("Govern the AI You Already Have")**
Frame AI governance as formalizing existing AI use.
Not "stop using AI" — "govern what you're already using."
Reduces resistance and compliance friction.

**Section 6: Swiss-Sovereign AI Stack**
US-hosted AI tools create CLOUD Act exposure.
Client data (property data, tenant data) requires Swiss/EU hosting.
FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL for Swiss-sovereign, FADP-aligned AI governance.

**Section 7: Formal AI Policy**
Create agency-level AI governance policy.
Define approved AI tools, use cases, disclosure protocols.
Assign AI governance responsibility.
