# 02 — International Organization AI Consulting: Competitive Landscape and UN Procurement Entry
**Phase:** C204 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva-based international organizations (UNOG, WHO, WTO, ITU, UNHCR) represent a concentrated, high-value AI governance consulting market — but entry requires understanding UN procurement rules, institutional culture, and the specific AI accountability language of each agency family. The competitive landscape is thinning: firms with UN procurement experience are rare, and AI governance specialists with international organization context are rarer still. UNOG CHF 800K RFQ is the leading indicator of a much larger opportunity.

## Evidence
- UNAIWG harmonized framework adoption March 2026 creates compliance deadline-driven demand across 14 UN agencies in Geneva
- UN procurement rules (UNGP) create specific requirements for vendor engagement — procurement classification, institutional partnership requirements
- Geneva consulting firms with UN procurement experience (at least 3 identified) currently serving IO AI consulting demand
- WHO AI governance framework published March 2026 — specific requirements for health AI deployment in developing countries
- ICRC AI deployment in South Sudan accountability gaps — urgent consulting need in humanitarian AI
- UNOG AI vendor contract audit finding 60%+ lack AI accountability clauses — remediation consulting demand immediate

## Advisory Implication
International organization AI consulting is a high-bar entry market. Advisors who can navigate UN procurement and demonstrate IO-specific AI governance expertise can access a captive, deadline-driven market. UNAIWG December 2026 deadline creates urgency.

## Confidence
High — UNAIWG publication, UNOG RFQ, WHO framework, ICRC activity
