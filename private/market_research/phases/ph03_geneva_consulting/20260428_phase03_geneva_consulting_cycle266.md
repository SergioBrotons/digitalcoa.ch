## 01 — ICP Language (EN + FR)

### English

**Managing director on internal AI use as the governance gap nobody is watching:**
"We use AI for our internal thinking. Proposals, methodology, how we approach a client problem — we think through AI. That does not feel like the same as using AI in client deliverables. But it is producing the same kind of accountability questions. The outputs are ours but the process is not something we can describe."

**On what happens when vendors change hands:**
"We paid for an AI platform for about two years. Built our processes into it. Last year it was acquired. We received an email saying the terms had changed. We did not read the new terms in detail. We kept using it. I am not certain I know what that means for the work product we put through that system."

**On the raw output question:**
"A client asked to see what our AI produced before we edited it. We do not have a record of that. The editing started from an AI draft but the drafts are not saved. I could not show them what they were asking to see. The question was uncomfortable."

**On what client expectation escalation is doing to the firm:**
"Our clients are getting used to very polished AI-assisted communications. When we send something that is more raw, they read it as less professional. We have created a baseline we cannot maintain without AI. That was not the intention. It is just what happened."

**On the engagement letter question nobody raises:**
"Our engagement letters were written before we used AI. There is nothing in them about AI. Every engagement we have with a client is operating under terms that do not describe what we are actually doing. Nobody raises this. It should be raised."

**On the model training question:**
"We use an AI platform that says it learns from what we input. We are putting client work product through it. I do not think we have consent for that. I do not think we have a way to get consent for that. But the platform is learning from our work. I am reasonably certain of that."

## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Vendor corporate restructuring as present-tense confidentiality event**
AI platform acquisitions in 2024–2025 changed processing terms without notification. Firms with proprietary methodology embedded in vendor infrastructure are operating under terms they have not evaluated. The restructuring event is ongoing.

**Signal 2: Client request for raw AI output arriving before retrieval architecture exists**
Clients asking to see unedited AI drafts. Firms cannot produce this record. The request is reasonable and the inability to answer it is indefensible.

**Signal 3: Internal AI use creating ungoverned accountability surface**
Firms use AI for internal methodology development, strategic analysis, and firm knowledge management. This AI use produces firm outputs with the same accountability questions as client deliverables — without any client relationship prompting governance.

**Signal 4: Engagement letter obsolescence as accumulating undisclosed liability**
Every active engagement operates under letters written before AI use. No Geneva consulting firm has a protocol for identifying which engagements have AI-related obligations the engagement letter does not describe.

**Signal 5: Client expectation escalation creating AI dependency lock-in**
Clients calibrated to AI-polished communications now treat human-produced work as below standard. Firms cannot revert to non-AI-assisted communication without client perception consequences. The escalation is irreversible by firm decision alone.

**Signal 6: AI model training on firm deliverables as unnamed proprietary erosion**
AI platforms learning from firm client work product without consent. The proprietary methodology in historical deliverables feeding into model training. No Geneva consulting firm has evaluated this exposure.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Gap 1: Vendor restructuring continuity review as discrete service**
No Geneva service addresses vendor AI platform acquisition continuity — what proprietary methodology is at risk when a platform changes hands, what the firm should document before continuation, what the firm's position is relative to the new processing terms. The service occasion is triggered by the restructuring event itself, not by client demand.

**Gap 2: Raw output retrieval architecture as boutique-scale deliverable component**
No methodology exists for preserving unedited AI output as a discrete deliverable component alongside the edited version. The methodology would need to be operationally practical at boutique scale and address what "raw output" means when editing is continuous.

**Gap 3: Internal AI use governance framework distinct from client deliverable governance**
Internal AI use produces firm intellectual property and strategic decisions. No framework addresses what governance documentation internal AI use requires at boutique scale, or why internal AI use creates the same accountability questions as client-facing AI use without the external pressure that would prompt governance.

**Gap 4: Engagement letter AI audit protocol**
No protocol exists for auditing engagement letters against actual AI use. The protocol would identify which active engagements have AI-related obligations undisclosed in the engagement letter, what the disclosure gap means, and what remediation is appropriate before the engagement terminates.

**Gap 5: Client expectation calibration communication framework**
No framework exists for managing client communication quality expectations when AI-assisted communication has calibrated clients to a higher baseline. The framework would need to address how to communicate quality standards without admitting the baseline was AI-created.

**Gap 6: AI model training exposure assessment for consulting methodology**
No assessment methodology exists for evaluating whether and how the firm's AI platforms are training on the firm's deliverables and historical methodology. The exposure is unnamed and unevaluated in the Geneva consulting market.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Professional liability under Art. 28 CO for internal AI use producing firm outputs:**
Art. 28 CO applies to professional outputs regardless of internal versus client-facing production process. AI used for internal methodology development produces firm decisions the MD is accountable for. The accountability question is identical whether the output is labeled internal or client-facing.

**Confidentiality and vendor restructuring under engagement letter obligations:**
Engagement letters obligate the firm to maintain confidentiality through defined processing arrangements. Platform acquisition changes those arrangements. No Geneva engagement letter standard addresses what notification or consent obligation the acquisition creates.

**nFADP Article 6 legitimate interest for AI-assisted internal firm analysis:**
Internal AI processing of client-related firm knowledge requires a legal basis under nFADP Article 6. The legitimate interest assessment for processing firm institutional memory through AI platforms has not been published.

**Swiss copyright and the raw AI output question:**
Swiss copyright vests authorship in natural persons. AI-generated draft content that is then edited raises attribution questions when the raw output is requested. No Geneva court has addressed whether a client has a right to the AI-generated portion of a deliverable.

**EU AI Act downstream deployer documentation for consulting methodology AI systems:**
AI systems used to develop firm methodology are deployed under EU AI Act Article 26 downstream deployer obligations. The documentation requirements are not adapted for methodology development use cases.

**AI model training as nFADP Article 9 third-party processing authorization question:**
If the firm's AI platform is training on client work product processed through the platform, the training use may constitute third-party processing requiring authorization under nFADP Article 9. The question is unnamed and unevaluated.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Vendor restructuring continuity exposure as the primary unnamed platform risk for Geneva consulting firms**
AI vendor acquisitions in 2024–2025 changed processing terms for platforms firms built methodology into. The continuity question — what methodology is at risk when a platform changes hands — is unnamed and unevaluated. The decision to continue using a restructured platform is made implicitly. No Geneva advisory service addresses this discrete triggering event.

**Finding 2: Raw AI output retrieval as a present-tense deliverable component gap**
Clients are beginning to request unedited AI output. Firms cannot produce this record. The inability is not because the request is unreasonable — it is because firms never built retrieval architecture. The gap is architectural, not behavioral. No Geneva market service has addressed this as a deliverable component design question.

**Finding 3: Internal AI use creating a shadow governance surface with identical accountability questions**
Firms use AI for internal methodology development, strategic analysis, and firm knowledge management without governance prompted by external pressure. Internal AI use produces outputs the MD is professionally accountable for, processed through vendor infrastructure with no consent architecture. No Geneva advisory service has differentiated internal from client-facing AI governance as a distinct product category.

**Finding 4: Engagement letter obsolescence as accumulating undisclosed liability across active engagements**
Engagement letters written before AI adoption do not describe AI use. Every active engagement represents an undisclosed AI-related obligation gap. No Geneva consulting firm has audited active engagement letters against actual AI use. The exposure compounds with each new engagement signed under pre-AI terms. No protocol exists for this audit at boutique scale.

**Finding 5: Client communication expectation escalation creating irreversible AI dependency lock-in**
AI-polished client communications have calibrated clients to a quality baseline firms cannot sustain without AI assistance. Reverting to human-only communication produces client perception of reduced quality. The escalation is irreversible by firm decision alone. No Geneva market service has addressed this as a discrete service category.

**Finding 6: AI model training as unnamed proprietary methodology erosion for consulting firms**
AI platforms learning from firm client work product without consent or evaluation. Proprietary methodology embedded in historical deliverables feeding into model training. The erosion is invisible from inside the firm. No Geneva consulting firm has assessed this exposure. No Geneva market service has built an assessment methodology for this specific question.
