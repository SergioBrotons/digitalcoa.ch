# 06 — Geneva M&A AI Due Diligence: Post-SMA Guidance Market Expansion and Mid-Market Deal Flow
**Phase:** C204 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
SMA's AI due diligence guidance is driving market expansion in Geneva M&A AI consulting — not just in large transactions but increasingly in mid-market deals. Geneva M&A advisors report that buyers are now routinely requiring AI-specific due diligence scopes, and sellers are engaging AI governance advisors to prepare sale-ready AI documentation. The mid-market (CHF 20M–200M deal size) is where AI DD demand is growing fastest, as larger firms have established frameworks while mid-market is still building them.

## Evidence
- SMA published AI due diligence guidance concrete requirements mid-2026 — market now responding
- Geneva M&A advisors reporting AI DD becoming standard in mid-market deal scopes as of Q1 2026
- AI governance readiness becoming seller-side preparation requirement — sellers engaging AI consultants to build sale-ready AI documentation
- AI DD scope expansion: AI IP ownership, AI training data liability, AI algorithmic bias exposure, AI regulatory compliance
- Geneva private equity firms developing AI DD checklists for portfolio company acquisitions
- AI target valuation impact: companies with documented AI governance frameworks commanding 10-20% valuation premium in Geneva market

## Advisory Implication
M&A AI due diligence advisory is expanding from specialist niche to mainstream deal support. Mid-market focus is the growth segment. AI governance documentation readiness for sale is a new seller-side service angle.

## Confidence
High — SMA guidance, M&A advisor reports, private equity activity
