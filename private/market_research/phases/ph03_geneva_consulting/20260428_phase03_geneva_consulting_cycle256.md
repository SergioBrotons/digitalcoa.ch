## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Client counterparty AI governance requirements as commercial credential conditions**
Client counterparty AI governance requirements are arriving through commercial relationships, not through regulatory frameworks. The requirements function as commercial credential conditions — the firm must meet them to maintain the commercial relationship, regardless of what Swiss domestic regulation requires.

**Signal 2: Geneva consulting firm AI governance as Geneva-specific cultural problem**
The AI governance problem for Geneva consulting firms has cultural dimensions that Zurich-based frameworks do not address. Geneva advisory culture, relationship dynamics, and professional communication patterns create a specific context that generic AI governance frameworks miss.

**Signal 3: Carrier renewal converting awareness into documentation requirement**
The carrier renewal process is converting general AI governance awareness into a specific documentation requirement. Firms that were aware of AI governance issues in the abstract are now confronting what the carrier actually requires them to represent.

**Signal 4: Senior consultant departure as AI configuration inheritance event**
Senior consultant departures are functioning as AI configuration inheritance events. The departing consultant's AI setup becomes the firm's AI environment without intentional design. The inheritance creates governance exposure that was not anyone's decision.

**Signal 5: Workshop AI output real-time delivery without editorial review as operational compromise**
The operational compromise of delivering workshop AI output without editorial review is known and accepted by most firms. The compromise is rational in the moment; the exposure it creates is not priced into the engagement structure.

**Signal 6: Peer circuit reassurance without structural resolution as self-reinforcing vacuum**
Peer conversations provide reassurance without structural resolution. The vacuum persists because the self-reinforcing pattern — awareness without action, reassurance without resolution — is more comfortable than confronting the underlying problem.

No service exists for reconstructing a Geneva consulting firm's AI history. The reconstruction would need to address what AI was used, how it was configured, what outputs were generated, and how the firm's AI environment evolved over two years of undocumented deployment.

No framework exists for responding to client counterparty AI governance requirements as commercial credential conditions. The framework would need to address how to evaluate the requirements, what to commit to, and how to demonstrate compliance within the commercial relationship.

**Gap 3: Geneva consulting AI governance cultural dimension advisory**
No advisory practice addresses the Geneva-specific cultural dimensions of AI governance for consulting firms. The cultural context — how Geneva advisory relationships work, how professional communication functions, how trust is established — shapes what AI governance looks like in practice.

**Gap 4: Workshop AI output quality architecture for real-time delivery**
No quality architecture exists for real-time workshop AI output delivery. The architecture would need to address how to maintain quality standards when editorial review is structurally absent, without destroying the real-time value that makes workshop AI useful.

**Gap 5: Senior consultant departure AI configuration audit service**
No service exists for auditing the AI configuration left by a departing senior consultant. The audit would need to document what was set up, why it was set up that way, what the risks are, and how to manage the configuration going forward.

No peer accountability mechanism exists with the confidentiality architecture that would make genuine intelligence exchange possible. The structural problem — that open exchange has competitive implications — prevents the informal circuit from functioning as a calibration mechanism.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Art. 398 CO professional liability for AI-assisted consulting deliverables:**
The warranty of proper performance under Art. 398 CO applies to AI-assisted consulting deliverables. When AI-generated content produces suboptimal outcomes, the warranty question is live. The warranty applies to the firm's deliverables regardless of how the content was generated.

**nFADP third-party processor obligations for consulting work product:**
Consulting deliverables incorporating AI-generated content may involve third-party processors. The nFADP Art. 9 processor obligations apply to these configurations. Boutique firms have not systematically evaluated which processors their AI tools use.

**Client counterparty AI requirements as commercial conditions:**
Client counterparty AI governance requirements function as commercial conditions, not regulatory obligations. The conditions are binding through the commercial relationship; non-compliance has commercial consequences, not regulatory ones.

**Federal Council AI Convention end-2026 timeline and consulting sector:**
The Federal Council AI Convention legislative timeline creates retrospective documentation requirements for AI configurations. Consulting firms that used AI without documenting configurations face exposure when the legislation arrives.

**Geneva consulting professional standards and AI:**
Geneva consulting professional standards do not include AI-specific documentation requirements. The gap between general professional standards and AI-specific requirements creates ambiguity about what adequate governance looks like for consulting firms.

**Carrier renewal AI questionnaire as documentation forcing function:**
The carrier renewal questionnaire is functioning as a documentation forcing function. The questions convert awareness of AI governance issues into specific documentation requirements that the firm must answer under penalty of personal liability.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Client counterparty AI governance requirements as commercial credential conditions with no market response framework**
Client counterparty AI requirements binding through commercial relationships without regulatory framing. The commercial credential function means non-compliance has commercial consequences — lost contracts, relationship termination — before regulatory consequences. No market framework exists for evaluating and committing to these requirements within the commercial relationship context.

**Finding 2: Geneva consulting AI governance as culturally specific problem with no Geneva-market advisory addressing it**
The AI governance problem for Geneva consulting firms has cultural dimensions that Zurich-based frameworks do not address. Geneva advisory culture, client relationship patterns, and professional communication norms shape what AI governance looks like in practice. No advisory practice has addressed the Geneva cultural specificity of the problem.

**Finding 3: Carrier renewal documentation requirement converting two-year retrospective AI history into specific representation obligation**
The carrier renewal converts the abstract two-year retrospective AI history into a specific representation obligation. The firm must answer questions about AI governance practices that were not designed to be documented. The representation obligation creates personal liability for managing directors who cannot accurately describe what happened.

**Finding 4: Senior consultant departure AI configuration inheritance as unmanaged firm-state transition with no audit protocol**
When senior consultants depart, their AI configuration becomes the firm's configuration without intentional transition. The unmanaged transition means the firm inherits an AI environment it did not design, with risks it has not assessed. No audit protocol exists for documenting what was inherited and what it means for current governance.

**Finding 5: Workshop AI output real-time delivery constraint as structural quality compromise that rationalizes itself**
The real-time delivery constraint rationalizes the absence of editorial review. The rationalization is functional — firms accept the compromise because the alternative destroys the real-time value. The structural compromise persists because the pricing of engagement structures does not account for the exposure it creates.

**Finding 6: Geneva consulting peer circuit self-reinforcing vacuum preventing structural resolution through social reassurance**
The peer circuit provides social reassurance without structural resolution. The vacuum self-reinforces: awareness without action is more comfortable than confronting the problem, and peer conversation maintains the illusion of progress without requiring it. The structural condition prevents resolution because genuine exchange has competitive implications that make silence rational.
