# C207-05 — Geneva Algorithmic Impact Assessment Standards
**Phase:** C207 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
Emerging demand for algorithmic impact assessment standards in Geneva consulting practice — consulting firms assessing AI systems for client organizations need standardized impact assessment methodologies: bias and discrimination potential, economic displacement effects, environmental impact, societal risk classification. Geneva academic institutions (Université de Genève, IUHEI) developing algorithmic impact assessment curricula that consulting firms are adopting. Algorithmic impact assessment as pre-implementation requirement for high-stakes AI deployments in Geneva.

## Evidence
- Standardized algorithmic impact assessment methodologies under development
- Bias and discrimination potential assessment
- Economic displacement effects assessment
- Environmental impact assessment
- Societal risk classification frameworks
- Université de Genève and IUHEI developing algorithmic impact assessment curricula
- Consulting firms adopting academic algorithmic impact assessment frameworks
- Algorithmic impact assessment as pre-implementation requirement for high-stakes AI deployments

## Advisory Implication
Algorithmic impact assessment as consulting service offering: bias and discrimination testing methodologies, economic displacement assessment frameworks, environmental impact AI assessment tools, and societal risk classification protocols. Consulting firms that develop robust algorithmic impact assessment methodologies will be positioned for pre-implementation AI governance advisory mandates from organizations deploying high-stakes AI systems in Geneva. Université de Genève and IUHEI academic collaboration as methodology development partnership opportunity.

## Confidence
High — academic institution curriculum development, Geneva consulting firm adoption

## FR
### Normes d'Évaluation de l'Impact Algorithmique à Genève
Demande émergente de normes d'évaluation de l'impact algorithmique dans la pratique du conseil genevois — les cabinets de conseil évaluant les systèmes d'IA pour des organisations clientes ont besoin de méthodologies standardisées d'évaluation de l'impact: potentiel de biais et de discrimination, effets de déplacement économique, impact environnemental, classification des risques sociétaux. Les institutions académiques genevoises (Université de Genève, IUHEI) élaborent des programmes d'évaluation de l'impact algorithmique que les cabinets de conseil adoptent. L'évaluation de l'impact algorithmique comme exigence pré-implementation pour les déploiements d'IA à enjeux élevés à Genève.
