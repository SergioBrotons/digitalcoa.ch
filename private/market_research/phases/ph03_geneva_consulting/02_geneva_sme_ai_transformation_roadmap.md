# 02 — Geneva SME AI Transformation Roadmaps: Underserved Mid-Market
**Phase:** C147 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva's SME cluster (~45,000 SMEs, CCIG member base) is underserved by AI transformation consulting. Most AI consulting firms target large enterprises or multinationals. Geneva's mid-market companies — trading houses, manufacturing precision firms, professional services firms, hospitality groups — need structured AI adoption roadmaps but cannot afford McKinsey-tier fees and do not fit boutique strategy firm models.

## Evidence
- CCIG (Chambre de commerce, d'industrie et de services de Genève) SME survey (2025): 72% of Geneva SMEs want AI adoption roadmap but 58% cite cost/complexity as barrier; only 12% have engaged external AI consultant
- Geneva Confederation of Small Business (SGV) data: AI adoption in Geneva SMEs lags Zurich by ~18 months
- Geneva cantonal economic development agency (OCPM) running AI adoption support program — but limited to subsidized workshops, not individual advisory
- Geneva shared services centers (e.g., Centre d'Exploitation Numérique) forming to pool AI infrastructure costs for SME clusters
- Swiss SME digitalization subsidy program (DIMAIN) — cantonal co-funding for AI adoption advisory available but underutilized (awareness gap)

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- Structured AI transformation roadmap for Geneva SME sector
- DIMAIN-subsidized AI advisory delivery
- Geneva SME cluster AI adoption programs (grouped firms)
- Geneva precision manufacturing AI integration advisory
- Geneva professional services AI productivity advisory
- SME AI governance and change management consulting

Pricing model consideration: DIMAIN subsidy makes advisory economically viable for SMEs. Geneva consultants can access DIMAIN co-funding channel.
