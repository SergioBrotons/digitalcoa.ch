## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Client-directed AI liability inversion as structural exposure**
Institutional clients specifying AI tools in engagement terms. The boutique inherits accountability for methodology it did not choose. A distinct inversion of the normal advisor-client accountability relationship.

**Signal 2: Geneva informal information circuit as competitive variable**
AI incident information circulates through personal networks before any public record. Market consequences disproportionate to formal exposure. The informal circuit requires its own management protocol.

**Signal 3: AI error correction economics as missing commercial product**
Error correction after AI failure has no engagement letter provisions, no defined process, no insurance coverage. Carriers treating absence of error-correction protocol as governance gap at renewal.

**Signal 4: AI production provenance architecture gap**
File note systems designed for human work cannot represent AI-assisted production. The mismatch is invisible until a reconstruction challenge arrives.

**Signal 5: Institutional AI evaluation deficit**
Boutique cannot assess its own AI deployment institutionally because evaluation knowledge sits with individuals. No institutional baseline for new tool evaluation or vendor comparison.

**Signal 6: AI pricing-tier risk distribution inversion**
Smallest/fastest engagements carry lowest fees, highest AI intensity, thinnest governance. Highest accountability exposure where governance investment is lowest.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Gap 1: Client-directed AI content accountability framework**
A framework for managing the firm's accountability when clients provide AI-generated content for incorporation into deliverables. The liability inversion is structural and unmanaged.

**Gap 2: Geneva informal network intelligence methodology**
A methodology for systematically gathering and using intelligence from Geneva's informal professional network about AI incidents and governance failures. The network is accessible but not systematically leveraged.

**Gap 3: AI error correction commercial framework**
The commercial framework for correcting AI-assisted errors after delivery. Engagement letter provisions, liability allocation, and insurance coverage for post-delivery errors. A discrete missing product.

**Gap 4: AI production provenance documentation system**
A documentation system that captures what the AI contributed to each deliverable, proportionate to boutique scale. File note systems designed for human production are inadequate.

**Gap 5: Institutional AI evaluation baseline**
A methodology for establishing an institutional baseline for evaluating new AI tools and comparing vendors. The knowledge is individual, not institutional.

**Gap 6: AI pricing-tier risk management**
A framework for managing the risk distribution inversion across pricing tiers. The firm cannot see that the highest-exposure engagements are the lowest-fee ones.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**SVA professional obligations:**
The Swiss Lawyers Association professional competence expectations extend to AI use. The boutique firm that cannot document its AI methodology may be unable to demonstrate the competence the SVA expects.

**nFADP application to consulting work product:**
Client work product processed through AI tools constitutes personal data processing under nFADP. The consultancy's obligations regarding client data flowing through AI systems are not clearly mapped for the boutique context.

**Professional liability insurance:**
Professional indemnity policies do not clearly specify coverage for AI-assisted work. The ambiguity means the firm may not know what is covered until a claim arrives.

**Client AI governance questionnaires:**
Clients include AI governance requirements in engagement letters and RFPs. The firm that cannot respond credibly may lose business. A commercial risk, not just a compliance risk.

**Geneva cantonal obligations:**
Geneva cantonal rules add requirements for client file management and matter supervision. AI tool use that is not systematically documented creates cantonal compliance gaps.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Client-directed AI liability inversion as structural exposure**
When clients push their own AI-generated content into the boutique's workflow, the firm inherits accountability for content it did not choose. The client-driven liability inversion is structural. No framework addresses this.

**Finding 2: Geneva informal information circuit as structural competitive variable**
The informal professional network transmits information about AI incidents before public channels. This is a competitive variable that can be leveraged systematically. The information advantage is real and accessible.

**Finding 3: AI error correction economics as missing commercial product**
The commercial framework for correcting AI-assisted errors after delivery does not exist. Engagement letter provisions for correction costs and liability allocation are missing. This is a discrete product opportunity.

**Finding 4: AI production provenance architecture gap**
File note systems designed for human production cannot capture what the AI contributed versus what the professional contributed. The documentation architecture is structurally mismatched to AI-assisted production.

**Finding 5: Institutional AI evaluation deficit**
The boutique cannot assess its own AI deployment institutionally because the knowledge is embedded in individuals. When those individuals depart, the institutional capacity departs with them. No baseline exists for evaluating new tools or vendors.

**Finding 6: AI pricing-tier risk distribution inversion**
The smallest and fastest engagements carry the lowest fees, the highest AI intensity, and the thinnest governance. The highest accountability exposure is where the governance investment is lowest. The inversion is visible once named, and manageable once visible.
