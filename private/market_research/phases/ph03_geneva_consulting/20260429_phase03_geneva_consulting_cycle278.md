## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Managing directors cannot attribute AI contribution in their own deliverables**
Managing directors reviewing AI-assisted deliverables cannot decompose what the AI contributed versus what the consultant contributed. The inability to attribute is creating a personal accountability problem for signing executives.

**Signal 2: Carrier questionnaires assuming a methodology that does not exist in documented form**
Carrier AI questionnaires assume a methodology that most Geneva consulting boutiques have not documented. The gap between what the questionnaire assumes and what exists is personal liability exposure.

**Signal 3: Client questions about cross-client AI data segregation arriving before any firm has a prepared answer**
Clients asking whether their strategic analysis was processed through the same AI infrastructure as other clients' work. No Geneva consulting firm has a prepared answer. The question is arriving before the answer exists.

**Signal 4: Proposal efficiency creating a billing paradox**
AI-assisted proposals are more efficient to produce. The efficiency creates a billing paradox: should AI-assisted proposals be billed at the same rate? The paradox is unsolved and is creating tension in client conversations.

**Signal 5: Sub-consultant AI delegation creating a three-party chain with no framework**
When consulting firms use sub-consultants who use AI systems, a three-party chain forms. No framework exists for managing accountability in this chain.

**Signal 6: Professional competence visibility paradox**
The more effectively a consulting firm uses AI, the more visible AI use becomes. The paradox is that effective AI use is itself a signal of AI use that clients may evaluate as a negative.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Art. 398 CO warranty of proper performance:**
AI-assisted work product raises the question of what care standard applies and whether it can be met when the MD cannot attribute the AI contribution.

**nFADP Articles 9, 21, and 8:**
Consulting firms processing client data through AI systems may be subject to obligations for sensitive data processing, AI decision documentation, and profiling notification simultaneously.

**Professional secrecy and AI data flows:**
Article 321 CP prohibits disclosure of client information to third parties. When firms use AI systems, client information may be processed by the AI vendor. No Geneva service has published a compatibility analysis.

**Carrier AI attestation as personal liability instrument:**
The carrier AI attestation is a personal liability instrument signed by the MD. The attestation commits to a methodology that is not defined.

**AI Convention obligations through existing client relationships:**
Consulting firms with long-term client relationships may have AI Convention obligations that have already activated.

**EU AI Act downstream obligations:**
EU AI Act obligations may flow downstream to Swiss consulting firms through EU-connected client relationships.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI co-authorship as Article 398 CO warranty of proper performance exposure**
The MD signing an AI-assisted deliverable certifies that the work meets the Article 398 CO standard. When the MD cannot decompose what the AI contributed, the MD cannot confirm the standard has been met.

**Finding 2: Carrier attestation as personal liability instrument with undefined methodology standard**
The carrier attestation commits to a methodology not defined in the policy. No Geneva service has published what "AI methodology" means for a consulting firm.

**Finding 3: Sub-consultant AI delegation three-party chain without accountability framework**
Client to firm to sub-consultant to sub-consultant's AI tool. Three parties, two AI systems, zero accountability architecture.

**Finding 4: Proposal provenance reverse-engineering as emerging competitive exposure**
Stylometric analysis can distinguish AI-generated from human-generated text. The competitive implications are unnamed in Geneva consulting.

**Finding 5: Professional competence visibility paradox as AI adoption deterrent**
Effective AI use is a visible signal of AI use. The most effective AI users are most exposed to client scrutiny.

**Finding 6: Geneva consulting peer accountability vacuum as structural market failure**
No mechanism exists for firms to hold each other accountable for AI governance. The failure means the market cannot self-correct.
