"The proposals are better than they were two years ago. I cannot tell you exactly what contributed to that improvement. The AI contributed something. The team contributed something. I cannot decompose it with confidence. I sign the proposals. I am not sure what I am certifying."

**On the billing conversation:**
"I have been avoiding the billing conversation. If AI makes the work faster, should the client pay less? If the work is better, should we charge more? I have not worked out what the honest answer is. I have been avoiding the question."

**On the carrier attestation:**
"The carrier asked me to confirm our AI methodology. I do not think they have thought through what methodology means for a consulting firm that produces bespoke analysis. I signed it anyway. It felt like signing something I had not read."

**On what happens when a client challenges a proposal:**
"If a client challenges our proposal and we used AI tools to produce it, I do not know how to explain what the AI contributed. I can defend what I wrote. I cannot defend what the AI did."

**On the sub-consultant:**
"We use a sub-consultant for technical work. She uses AI tools. We do not know what she is asking the AI to do with client data. The client does not know either. We are in the middle. We do not have a framework for what that means."

## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Proposal quality improvement without attribution clarity**
Proposals improving without clear attribution between human and AI contribution. The MD signing proposals cannot decompose what contributed to the improvement.

**Signal 2: AI billing paradox remaining unresolved**
Firms avoiding the AI billing conversation with clients. The avoidance compounds exposure silently. No protocol exists for how to conduct this conversation.

**Signal 3: Carrier attestation signing without methodology definition**
Signing carrier attestations without a defined methodology creates personal liability exposure. The attestation commits to something the signatory cannot describe.

**Signal 4: AI contribution opacity creating challenge-response asymmetry**
When a consulting proposal is challenged, the firm can defend what humans wrote but cannot explain what the AI contributed. The asymmetry creates exposure in any adversarial client situation.

**Signal 5: Sub-consultant AI data flow creating three-party accountability void**
The consulting firm in the middle of client and sub-consultant has no framework for managing the AI data flow through the sub-consultant. No party has clear accountability.

**Signal 6: Client AI methodology comparison requests arriving before firms have documentation**
Clients beginning to compare AI methodologies across consulting firms before making formal disclosure requests. The comparison happens before any firm has prepared documentation.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Art. 398 CO warranty of proper performance:**
AI-assisted work product raises the question of what care standard applies when the MD cannot decompose what the AI contributed versus what the consultant contributed.

**nFADP Articles 9, 12, and 21:**
Consulting firms processing client data through AI systems may face simultaneous obligations for automated decision processing, data subject rights, and AI decision documentation.

**Professional liability and sub-consultant AI delegation:**
The consulting firm bears professional liability to the client regardless of sub-consultant AI tool usage. The liability cannot be transferred downstream.

**Carrier AI attestation as personal liability commitment:**
The carrier attestation commits the MD to a methodology that has not been defined. The personal liability exposure is structural and unaddressed.

**AI Convention obligations through existing consulting relationships:**
Consulting firms with long-term client relationships may have AI Convention obligations that have already activated through those relationships.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI co-authorship as Article 398 CO warranty exposure with non-decomposable contribution**
The MD signing an AI-assisted deliverable certifies that the work meets the Article 398 CO standard. When the MD cannot decompose what the AI contributed, the MD cannot confirm the standard has been met. Distinct from prior AI co-authorship findings.

**Finding 2: AI billing conversation avoidance as accumulating exposure with no resolution protocol**
Firms are avoiding the AI billing conversation with clients. The avoidance is not neutral. It creates exposure that compounds silently. The conversation needs a protocol that has not been published. Distinct from prior billing findings.

**Finding 3: Carrier attestation methodology definition gap as consulting-specific market opportunity**
No Geneva service offers methodology definition specifically for consulting firms completing carrier AI attestations. The gap is consulting-specific because consulting firms produce bespoke analysis that does not fit standard methodology templates. Distinct from prior carrier attestation findings.

**Finding 4: Client AI proposal annotation challenge as asymmetric response situation**
When clients return proposals with AI-generated annotations, the consulting firm faces an asymmetric challenge: they can defend what humans wrote but cannot explain what the AI contributed. The asymmetry creates exposure in adversarial situations. Distinct from prior client challenge findings.

**Finding 5: Sub-consultant AI data flow as non-transferable professional liability**
The consulting firm bears professional liability to the client regardless of sub-consultant AI tool usage. The liability cannot be transferred downstream by contract or otherwise. The firm needs a governance framework for sub-consultant AI data flows. Distinct from prior sub-consultant findings.

**Finding 6: Client AI methodology comparison as pre-disclosure exposure before documentation standard exists**
Clients comparing AI methodologies across firms before formal disclosure requests arrive. The comparison happens before any firm has documentation to share. No documentation standard exists for what a firm should be able to say about its AI methodology. Distinct from prior client comparison findings.
