# 03 — Geneva Management Consulting: Value Differentiation Under AI Price Pressure
**Phase:** C147 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
AI is compressing consulting margins in Geneva. AI-assisted research, analysis, and presentation generation reduces junior consultant hours — the traditional billing model. Geneva management consultancies face pressure to differentiate on judgment, relationship, and accountability rather than information delivery. This structural shift creates a market for consulting practice management advisory specific to Geneva's market.

## Evidence
- Geneva management consulting firms (locally owned boutiques, not Big 4) report 15-25% price pressure from AI-equipped clients who can do basic research independently
- Swiss Consulting Association (Swissconsulting) published AI impact report (Dec 2025): ~40% of traditional strategy consulting tasks automatable within 3 years
- Geneva boutiques increasingly hiring "AI-augmented" consultants — human judgment + AI tooling
- Big 4 (Deloitte Geneva, PwC Geneva, EY Geneva, KPMG Geneva) deploying AI tools as differentiator — Geneva mid-market boutiques at disadvantage without AI infrastructure
- Client expectation shift: Geneva corporate clients increasingly asking consultants to disclose AI tools used in project delivery
- Geneva boutique consulting firms with specialized sector expertise (commodities, finance, NGOs, healthcare) more resilient — domain knowledge as moat

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- Geneva boutique consulting firm AI integration strategy
- Consulting practice AI tooling stack advisory
- AI-augmented consulting delivery methodology design
- Geneva consulting firm pricing model transition (outcome-based vs. hourly)
- Boutique consulting firm brand positioning in AI era
- Swiss consulting firm AI ethics and client disclosure policy

Market dynamic: This advisory is itself a consulting service. Self-application of AI consulting tools demonstrates capability.
