# C205 Finding 02 — International Organization AI Vendor Governance

**Phase:** 03 Geneva Consulting | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

The UNAIWG December 2026 deadline is driving international organizations in Geneva to implement AI vendor governance frameworks — covering the full AI procurement lifecycle from vendor AI documentation requirements to ongoing AI system monitoring, with Geneva consulting firms winning significant advisory contracts.

## Key Details

- **AI vendor documentation requirements:** International orgs now requiring AI vendors to submit: AI system documentation, training data provenance, model update history, bias testing results, incident reporting protocols
- **AI procurement lifecycle governance:** Vendor AI assessment built into procurement stages — RFQ AI requirements, vendor AI questionnaire, contractual AI accountability clauses, AI system monitoring during contract
- **AI vendor contractual accountability clauses:** UNOG model contract AI addendum — vendor liability for AI system failures, incident response obligations, AI system replacement/termination rights
- **Geneva consulting firm contracts:** Multiple Geneva consulting firms have won UNAIWG-aligned AI governance advisory contracts with international organizations — CHF 100K-500K engagement range
- **UN procurement barriers:** UN vendor registration requirements, UN sustainability and human rights due diligence requirements applying to AI vendors; compliance complexity creating consulting demand
- **December 2026 deadline impact:** International organizations scrambling to complete AI vendor governance frameworks — peak consulting demand through Q3-Q4 2026
