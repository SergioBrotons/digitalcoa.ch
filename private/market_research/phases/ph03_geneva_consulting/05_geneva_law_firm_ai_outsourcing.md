# 05 — Geneva Luxury Sector AI Governance: Brand Risk Framework as Brand Protection
**Phase:** C204 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva luxury brands are reframing AI governance as brand protection rather than compliance — understanding that AI failures (bias in AI customer profiling, AI-generated content errors, AI supply chain transparency failures) carry brand risk that far exceeds regulatory penalties. Geneva luxury houses are developing AI governance frameworks where brand alignment is the primary design constraint, not regulatory compliance. This is a fundamentally different consulting framing than standard AI governance.

## Evidence
- Geneva luxury brand AI incidents 2025-2026: AI-generated marketing content controversies, AI customer profiling bias claims, AI supply chain transparency failures
- Geneva luxury brand boards treating AI governance as reputational risk committee item — not just legal/compliance
- FHH (Fondation des Hotels de Genève) AI Heritage Protocol — brand heritage protection as core AI governance principle
- Geneva luxury watch sector AI governance: AI in heritage craftsmanship documentation, AI customer service, AI supply chain provenance
- Geneva luxury beauty/lifestyle sector AI: AI content generation brand voice compliance, AI influencer identification
- Geneva luxury brands hiring AI governance roles internally — creating internal consulting demand

## Advisory Implication
Luxury sector AI governance advisory is a premium niche — requires understanding of brand management, heritage preservation, and luxury customer expectations. Framing AI governance as brand protection rather than compliance commands higher value and stronger client commitment.

## Confidence
High — luxury brand activity, FHH protocol, brand incident reports
