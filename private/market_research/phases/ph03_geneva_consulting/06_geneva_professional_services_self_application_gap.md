# C207-06 — Geneva Professional Services AI Self-Application Gap
**Phase:** C207 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
Geneva professional services firms (law, accounting, consulting, engineering) are behind their own clients in AI governance implementation — internal AI tool governance policies incomplete, AI professional liability exposure unaddressed, AI tool disclosure obligations unmet. This creates a credibility gap: professional services firms advising clients on AI governance while their own internal AI governance is deficient. Geneva consulting firms with strong internal AI governance are using this as competitive marketing differentiator.

## Evidence
- Internal AI tool governance policies incomplete across Geneva professional services firms
- AI professional liability exposure unaddressed
- AI tool disclosure obligations unmet
- Credibility gap: firms advising clients on AI governance with deficient internal AI governance
- Geneva consulting firms with strong internal AI governance using this as marketing differentiator
- Internal AI governance remediation as consulting firm business opportunity
- Self-application gap across law, accounting, consulting, engineering sectors

## Advisory Implication
Internal AI governance remediation as consulting firm service offering: helping professional services firms close the self-application gap by implementing internal AI governance frameworks, AI tool disclosure policies, and AI professional liability mitigation measures. The self-application gap is a credibility issue — firms that close it first can market their own AI governance maturity as proof of concept for clients. Internal AI governance assessment as standalone service for professional services firms seeking to validate their own AI governance readiness before marketing AI governance advisory services externally.

## Confidence
High — Geneva professional services firm AI governance adoption patterns

## FR
### Lacune d'Auto-Application IA des Services Professionnels Genevois
Les entreprises de services professionnels genevoises (avocats, comptabilité, conseil, ingénierie) sont en retard par rapport à leurs propres clients dans la mise en œuvre de la gouvernance IA — politiques internes de gouvernance des outils IA incomplètes, exposition à la responsabilité professionnelle IA non traitée, obligations de divulgation des outils IA non satisfaites. Cela crée un déficit de crédibilité: les entreprises de services professionnels conseillant les clients sur la gouvernance IA alors que leur propre gouvernance IA interne est insuffisante. Les cabinets de conseil genevois disposant d'une solide gouvernance IA interne utilisent cela comme différenciateur marketing concurrentiel. La mise en œuvre corrective de la gouvernance IA interne comme offre de services pour les entreprises de services professionnels.
