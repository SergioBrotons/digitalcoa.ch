## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: AI-enhanced deliverable as distinct premium pricing category**
The firm that can credibly deliver AI-enhanced work product has a premium pricing opportunity distinct from AI-assisted traditional work. The market is beginning to distinguish between the two.

**Signal 2: Client-driven AI content entering boutique workflow**
Clients are increasingly providing AI-generated content that enters the boutique's workflow. The firm inherits accountability for content it did not produce and did not choose. The client-driven liability inversion is structural.

**Signal 3: Geneva informal information circuit as competitive variable**
Geneva's tight professional network means that AI incidents and governance failures are known through informal channels before they are public. The personal network information advantage is a structural competitive variable.

**Signal 4: Insurance broker renewal as annual governance audit trigger**
The annual insurance broker renewal has become a predictable, dateable audit event requiring a documented AI position. This is the specific trigger mechanism that makes governance tangible annually.

**Signal 5: AI error correction liability framework gap**
After an AI-assisted error is identified, the commercial framework for correction — economics, engagement letter provisions, liability allocation — does not exist. The error correction is unmanaged commercially.

**Signal 6: AI production provenance documentation architecture gap**
File note systems were designed for human production. They do not capture what the AI contributed versus what the professional contributed. The documentation architecture is structurally mismatched to AI-assisted production.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Gap 1: AI-enhanced deliverable premium pricing methodology**
A methodology for pricing AI-enhanced deliverables as a distinct category — with premium pricing that reflects the distinct value and distinct accountability — does not exist at boutique scale.

**Gap 2: Client-driven AI content accountability framework**
A framework for managing the firm's accountability when clients provide AI-generated content for incorporation into deliverables does not exist. The liability inversion is structural and unmanaged.

**Gap 3: Geneva informal network intelligence gathering**
A methodology for systematically gathering intelligence from Geneva's informal professional network about AI incidents, governance failures, and market developments does not exist. The informal circuit is accessible but not systematically leveraged.

**Gap 4: AI error correction commercial framework**
The commercial framework for post-delivery AI errors — correction economics, engagement letter provisions, liability allocation — is a discrete missing product that boutique firms need.

**Gap 5: AI production provenance documentation system**
A documentation system that captures what the AI contributed to each deliverable — proportionate to boutique scale, operationally practical — does not exist. The file note systems designed for human production are inadequate.

**Gap 6: Insurance renewal AI governance position preparation**
A preparation methodology specifically designed to help the boutique firm present a coherent, documented AI governance position to its insurance broker at renewal does not exist.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**SVA professional obligations:**
The Swiss Lawyers Association competence expectations extend to AI use in professional practice. The boutique firm that cannot document its AI methodology may be unable to demonstrate the competence the SVA expects.

**nFADP application to consulting work product:**
Client work product processed through AI tools constitutes personal data processing under nFADP. The consultancy's obligations regarding client data flowing through AI systems have not been clearly mapped for the boutique context.

**Professional liability insurance:**
Professional indemnity policies do not clearly specify coverage for AI-assisted work. The ambiguity means the firm may not know what is covered until a claim arrives. The coverage gap is structural.

**Geneva cantonal obligations:**
The Geneva cantonal bar rules add requirements specific to client file management and matter supervision. AI tool use that is not systematically documented may create cantonal compliance gaps.

**Client AI governance questionnaires:**
Clients are beginning to include AI governance requirements in engagement letters and RFPs. The firm that cannot respond credibly to these requirements may lose business. This is a commercial risk, not just a compliance risk.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI-enhanced deliverable as distinct premium pricing category**
The firm that can credibly deliver AI-enhanced work product has a premium pricing opportunity. The market is beginning to distinguish between AI-assisted traditional work and genuinely AI-enhanced deliverables. The pricing should distinguish between them.

**Finding 2: Client-driven AI liability inversion as structural exposure**
When clients push their own AI-generated content into the boutique's workflow, the firm inherits accountability for content it did not choose. This is a structural exposure that client-driven AI adoption creates. The firm needs a framework for managing this exposure.

**Finding 3: Geneva informal information circuit as structural competitive variable**
The informal professional network in Geneva transmits information about AI incidents and governance failures before public channels. This is a competitive variable that the firm can leverage systematically. The information advantage is real and accessible.

**Finding 4: Insurance broker renewal as annual governance audit trigger**
The annual insurance broker renewal is a predictable, dateable event that requires a documented AI governance position. This annual trigger is the most actionable forcing function for governance documentation the boutique firm has. It should be used deliberately.

**Finding 5: AI error correction liability framework as missing commercial product**
The commercial framework for correcting AI-assisted errors after delivery does not exist. The engagement letter provisions that would allocate correction costs and liability are missing. This is a discrete product opportunity.

**Finding 6: AI production provenance documentation architecture as structural gap**
File note systems designed for human production cannot capture what the AI contributed. The documentation architecture is structurally mismatched to AI-assisted production. The gap is in the system design, not in the firm's diligence.
