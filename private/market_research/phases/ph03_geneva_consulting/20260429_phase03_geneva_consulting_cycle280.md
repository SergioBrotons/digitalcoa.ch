## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Client-initiated AI methodology comparison across consulting firms**
Geneva institutional clients are beginning to run comparative AI methodology analyses across their consulting firms simultaneously. The comparison surfaces differences in disclosure approach, analytical process assumptions, and AI governance sophistication before any firm has prepared for the comparison. The client already has the analysis before the consulting firm knows the comparison was made.

**Signal 2: Active deliverable version uncertainty as accumulating undisclosed exposure**
Deliverables sent to clients remain in active reference use months after production. AI model updates change the analytical framework that produced those deliverables silently. The engagement letter does not define which model version governs. MDs are not tracking version state of deliverables in active client use. The exposure is invisible and compounding.

**Signal 3: PI carrier coverage ambiguity for AI-assisted errors at point of policy renewal**
Brokers cannot confirm AI-assisted error coverage with precision. MDs are not pressing for the honest answer. The coverage question is being deferred to a post-incident moment when the answer becomes operationally critical. The gap between assumed coverage and confirmed coverage is unaddressed at every renewal.

**Signal 4: Client AI data governance questionnaires arriving without preparation**
Clients are sending AI data governance questionnaires directly to consulting firms. The questions ask about data processing, model configuration, and cross-client segregation. Geneva consulting firms are receiving these questionnaires without having built the response architecture. Answers are being improvised.

**Signal 5: Peer accountability vacuum being filled by carrier questionnaires without Geneva consulting input**
The absence of a Geneva consulting professional body equivalent is creating a structural vacuum. Carrier AI questionnaires are functioning as de facto operative standards for the sector, designed by carriers without Geneva consulting sector input. MDs are signing attestations that define what adequate AI governance means for consulting firms without any Geneva-specific interpretation.

**Signal 6: Proposal footprint accumulation creating client-formed analytical expectations before disclosure**
The cumulative effect of AI-assisted proposals across a client relationship creates a baseline of analytical style and conclusion patterns the client uses to evaluate new proposals. The baseline forms invisibly. When the boutique changes methodology or discloses AI use, the client compares against the accumulated baseline. The disclosure moment arrives after the comparison standard has already been formed.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Art. 398 CO proper performance in AI-assisted consulting:**
The proper performance standard under Art. 398 CO requires that the consultant exercise the care that the profession demands. For AI-assisted consulting, the question of what due care means when the MD cannot decompose the AI contribution from human judgment is unresolved. The Swiss doctrine has not issued guidance on this specific configuration for consulting firms.

**Geneva commercial code obligations and AI deliverable versioning:**
Geneva consulting engagements are governed by commercial code obligations for professional performance. Deliverables produced under one AI model version may be analytically different from deliverables produced under a subsequent version. The engagement letter standard does not define version-locked deliverable obligations. No Geneva authority has addressed this specific intersection.

**Cantonal professional liability exposure for Geneva consulting MDs:**
Geneva managing directors at consulting firms bear personal professional liability for client deliverables. AI tool use creates new attribution ambiguity scenarios where liability cannot be precisely allocated between human judgment and AI contribution. The personal liability exposure exists regardless of whether the engagement letter has been updated to address AI tools.

**Carrier AI attestation as operative standard for Geneva consulting sector:**
Carrier AI attestations function as the operative AI governance standard for Geneva consulting firms in the absence of any Geneva-specific professional body standard. MDs signing carrier attestations are committing to adequacy against a standard designed by insurance carriers, not by Geneva consulting professionals. The standard has no formal appeal mechanism and no Geneva-specific interpretation pathway.

**nFADP obligations for consulting firms processing client strategic data through AI systems:**
Consulting firms processing client strategic information, market analysis, and competitive intelligence through AI tools face nFADP obligations for that processing. The questions clients are beginning to ask about data governance (data flow, model access, cross-client segregation) correspond to nFADP rights the clients hold. Geneva consulting firms have not built the response architecture for these questions.

**Geneva institutional ecosystem accountability formation without regulatory process:**
Geneva institutional clients (banks, NGOs, international organizations) are forming AI governance expectations for their consulting suppliers through informal accountability mechanisms that operate faster than any formal regulatory process. The expectations arrive as client questionnaires and audit requests before any published guidance defines what adequate compliance means. Geneva consulting firms are navigating this without a Geneva-specific institutional accountability mapping.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Cross-firm methodology comparison as discrete competitive intelligence event creating pre-disclosure client positioning**
Geneva institutional clients are now running simultaneous AI methodology comparisons across their consulting firm portfolios. The comparison produces results before any firm knows the comparison was made. Firms that have not prepared a methodology disclosure position are being evaluated against an implicit standard formed by the comparison itself. The competitive intelligence event is not a client audit — it is a comparison operation the client conducts independently. No Geneva service has addressed this as a discrete competitive intelligence response situation requiring a specific preparation architecture distinct from audit response. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 proposal authorship, stylometric detection, and client disclosure findings — those addressed what the firm does with its own proposals, not the client's independent cross-firm comparative analysis operating before any disclosure was prepared.

**Finding 2: Deliverable version drift as active mandate undisclosed analytical dependency creating MD blind spot**
AI model updates silently alter the analytical framework of deliverables in active client use. The client is referencing a conclusion that may have been produced under a model version that no longer exists. The engagement letter contains no version-locked deliverable standard. The MD cannot confirm what the client is relying on without a version tracking protocol that does not exist at boutique scale. The exposure is structurally different from deliverable staleness (which is about conclusions aging) or analytical integrity drift (which is about accumulated influence over time). This finding names the specific mechanism: active deliverables are version-dependent analytical products in continuous analytical drift through vendor-controlled model updates the boutique cannot observe or stop, creating an undisclosed dependency between client decisions and model states the MD cannot verify. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 deliverable versioning findings.

**Finding 3: AI-attribution ambiguity as primary professional liability exposure with no engagement letter architecture addressing it**
When a consulting recommendation causes client harm, the question of whether the AI tool or the human analyst produced the harmful contribution cannot be answered by any existing system. The carrier cannot confirm coverage scope. The engagement letter does not define attribution obligations. The MD signed a carrier attestation warranting AI review methodology while that methodology produces outputs where human and AI contributions are architecturally inseparable. This exposure is primary, not secondary — it exists in every AI-assisted engagement regardless of any other documentation gap. The engagement letter architecture required to address it has not been built anywhere in the Geneva consulting market. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 liability findings which addressed authorization, attribution reconstruction after the fact, or junior-level authority misalignment — this finding names the specific structural attribution impossibility as a primary exposure operating in every active engagement before any failure occurs.

**Finding 4: Geneva consulting peer accountability vacuum as named structural market failure with carrier questionnaire displacement mechanism**
The absence of a Geneva consulting professional body equivalent (SVA covers employment law, not management consulting; no ORDRE for consulting) creates a structural vacuum with a specific competitive consequence: carrier AI questionnaires are functioning as the operative AI governance standard for the sector precisely because no Geneva consulting body exists to produce an alternative. The carrier standard was not designed by or for Geneva consulting firms. MDs are building accountability architecture against a standard they did not participate in forming, with no appeal mechanism, while the firms most capable of contributing to standard formation are the least able to participate in carrier questionnaire design processes. This structural vacuum is distinct from information gaps, guidance absence, or regulatory timing problems — it is a market structure problem with specific competitive consequences that regulatory guidance alone cannot resolve because guidance presupposes a standard-setter that Geneva consulting does not have. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 peer accountability vacuum findings which named the vacuum without isolating the specific carrier questionnaire displacement mechanism as the structural consequence.

**Finding 5: Bespoke analytical work performance evidence architecture as consulting-specific documentation category with no proportionate methodology**
Carrier third-generation AI attestations require evidence that AI-assisted decisions performed as intended over time. The performance evidence model assumes routinized processes with measurable outcome data — a category that does not describe bespoke consulting analysis. Geneva consulting firms producing genuinely novel analytical work cannot construct performance evidence in advance because the work is not repeatable under controlled conditions. The documentation requirement and the work category are structurally incompatible. No Geneva market service has built a consulting-proportionate performance evidence methodology for bespoke analytical work. The gap is not documentation quality — it is documentation category mismatch. Firms producing the highest-quality bespoke analysis are most unable to produce the performance evidence the attestation requires. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 documentation findings addressing quality, coherence, adequacy, or multi-framework interaction — this finding names the specific structural incompatibility between bespoke consulting analysis and the performance evidence documentation category that third-generation carrier attestations require.

**Finding 6: Geneva institutional ecosystem accountability formation operating faster than formal regulatory process as specific MD positioning imperative**
Geneva institutional accountability expectations form through informal circuit mechanisms (carrier iteration patterns, institutional client questionnaire evolution, Geneva event concentration, peer exchange) at a velocity that Bern formal regulatory process cannot match. The accountability questions are being answered informally before any published guidance arrives. MDs who rely on published regulatory guidance are navigating yesterday's accountability questions while today's are already forming through informal mechanisms. The specific positioning imperative is not compliance preparation against arrived standards but sustained institutional participation to observe standards forming before they arrive — a function distinct from both regulatory monitoring and legal research, requiring a different kind of Geneva ecosystem engagement than any current market service provides. Distinct from all prior Phase 03 Geneva institutional ecosystem findings which named participation as valuable without isolating the specific velocity differential between informal ecosystem formation and formal regulatory process as the mechanism creating the MD's primary accountability intelligence challenge.
