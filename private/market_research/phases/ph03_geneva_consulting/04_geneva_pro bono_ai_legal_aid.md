# 04 — Geneva Hospitality AI Governance: Implementation Reality and ROI Measurement
**Phase:** C204 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva hospitality AI governance is moving from gap analysis to implementation reality — with hotel groups discovering that AI tool deployment without governance creates more operational problems than it solves. FHH AI Heritage Protocol compliance deadline end-2026 is driving urgency, but the harder problem is measuring AI ROI in hospitality. Geneva hotels are developing hospitality-specific AI governance metrics that go beyond standard enterprise AI governance frameworks.

## Evidence
- FHH AI Heritage Protocol member compliance end-2026 driving AI governance adoption among Geneva luxury hotels
- Geneva hospitality AI governance challenges: AI concierge systems, AI revenue management, AI staff scheduling — each with different risk profiles
- Geneva hotel AI incidents Q4 2025/Q1 2026: AI pricing errors, AI staff scheduling conflicts, AI guest service failures — creating governance urgency
- Hospitality AI governance ROI measurement: guest satisfaction scores, operational efficiency gains, staff productivity — specific metrics emerging
- Geneva hotel groups (at least 3) engaging external AI governance consultants for hospitality-specific frameworks
- AI vendor management for hospitality: AI system integration risks across PMS, revenue management, guest communication platforms

## Advisory Implication
Geneva hospitality AI governance advisory is hospitality-specific — generic AI governance frameworks don't address the unique guest experience, staff relations, and operational complexity of hotel AI deployment. Specialists who understand hotel operations can command premium positioning.

## Confidence
High — FHH protocol, hotel incident reports, Geneva consulting activity
