# 01 — Geneva as Regulatory Intelligence Hub for Multinational AI Compliance
**Phase:** C147 Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva's concentration of multinational corporate headquarters (~400 Fortune 500 European HQs), international organizations, and trade associations creates a unique opportunity for a "regulatory intelligence" consulting niche: continuous monitoring, interpretation, and strategic advisory on the convergence of Swiss, EU, and international AI regulations. No Geneva boutique currently owns this space with an AI-specific positioning.

## Evidence
- EU AI Act (full application 2026) — multinationals with EU market access need continuous regulatory tracking
- Swiss AI Convention implementation (Federal Council ratification Feb 2025, draft law end-2026) — Swiss-specific obligations emerging faster than companies can track
- Switzerland not in EU — regulatory divergence from EU AI Act creates dual-compliance burden for Swiss HQs
- Geneva multinationals increasingly hiring "AI Regulatory Affairs" roles — internal demand confirmed by recruiting activity
- UNEP, WHO, WTO, ITC all based in Geneva — international organization AI governance creates adjacent advisory market
- Swiss trade associations (economiesuisse, Swissholdings) lobbying on AI regulatory framework — Geneva as association HQ creates coordination demand

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- AI Regulatory Intelligence subscription service (continuous monitoring + interpretation)
- Swiss-EU AI regulatory divergence gap analysis for Swiss multinationals
- AI compliance roadmap for Geneva HQ multinationals
- International organization AI policy advisory
- Trade association AI regulatory coordination services
- Geneva government affairs AI regulatory strategy

This is a recurring revenue model (subscription intelligence) rather than one-shot project consulting.
