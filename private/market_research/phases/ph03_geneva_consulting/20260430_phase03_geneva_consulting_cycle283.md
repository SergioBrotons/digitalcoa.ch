## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Proposal quality improvement without attribution clarity**
Proposals improving in quality without clear attribution between human and AI contribution. The MD signing proposals cannot decompose what contributed to the improvement.

**Signal 2: Carrier attestation questions assuming methodology that does not exist**
Carrier AI attestation questions assuming a methodology that most Geneva consulting boutiques have not documented. The gap between what the questionnaire assumes and what exists is personal liability exposure.

**Signal 3: Client AI challenges to consulting proposals arriving before response frameworks exist**
Clients using AI systems to challenge consulting proposals. The consulting firm cannot evaluate whether the AI found a real problem or generated plausible-sounding objections.

**Signal 4: AI billing paradox remaining unresolved**
The billing paradox for AI-assisted work not being resolved in client conversations. Firms avoiding the conversation creates exposure that compounds silently.

**Signal 5: Sub-consultant AI data flow creating three-party accountability void**
Consulting firm in the middle of client and sub-consultant with no framework for managing AI data flows through the sub-consultant. No party has clear accountability.

**Signal 6: Client AI methodology comparison requests arriving before firms have documentation to share**
Clients beginning to compare AI methodologies across consulting firms before making formal disclosure requests. The comparison happens before any firm has prepared documentation.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**Art. 398 CO warranty of proper performance:**
AI-assisted work product raises the question of what care standard applies when the MD cannot decompose what the AI contributed versus what the consultant contributed.

**nFADP Articles 9, 12, and 21:**
Consulting firms processing client data through AI systems may face simultaneous obligations for automated decision processing, data subject rights, and AI decision documentation.

**Professional liability and sub-consultant AI delegation:**
The consulting firm bears professional liability to the client regardless of sub-consultant AI tool usage. The liability cannot be transferred downstream.

**Carrier AI attestation as personal liability instrument:**
The carrier attestation commits the MD to a methodology that has not been defined. The personal liability exposure is structural and unaddressed.

**Art. 321 CP and AI data flows:**
Professional secrecy obligations apply to client data. When firms use AI systems, client information may be processed by the AI vendor. No Geneva service has published a compatibility analysis.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI co-authorship as Article 398 CO warranty exposure with non-decomposable contribution**
The MD signing an AI-assisted deliverable certifies that the work meets the Article 398 CO standard. When the MD cannot decompose what the AI contributed, the MD cannot confirm the standard has been met. Distinct from prior AI authorship findings.

**Finding 2: Carrier attestation methodology definition gap as consulting-specific exposure**
No Geneva service has published what "AI methodology" means for a consulting firm that produces bespoke analysis. The attestation commits to something the signatory cannot describe. Distinct from prior carrier attestation findings.

**Finding 3: Client AI proposal challenge asymmetry as substantive versus plausible noise evaluation problem**
Client AI systems generating challenges to consulting proposals. The firm cannot evaluate whether the challenge identified a real problem or generated plausible noise. No evaluation framework exists. Distinct from prior client challenge findings.

**Finding 4: AI billing conversation avoidance as accumulating exposure with no resolution protocol**
Firms avoiding the AI billing conversation with clients. The avoidance compounds exposure silently. No protocol exists for how to conduct this conversation. Distinct from prior billing findings.

**Finding 5: Sub-consultant three-party chain with non-transferable liability and no governance framework**
Client to firm to sub-consultant to sub-consultant's AI tool. Three parties, two AI systems, non-transferable professional liability, zero accountability architecture. Distinct from prior sub-consultant findings.

**Finding 6: Client AI methodology comparison as pre-disclosure exposure before documentation standard exists**
Clients comparing AI methodologies across firms before formal disclosure requests arrive. The comparison happens before any firm has documentation to share. No documentation standard exists for what a firm should be able to say about its AI methodology. Distinct from prior client comparison findings.
