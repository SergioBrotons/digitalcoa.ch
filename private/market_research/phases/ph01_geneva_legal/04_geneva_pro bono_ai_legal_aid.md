# 04 — Geneva Government AI Liability: State Responsibility for Algorithmic Decisions
**Phase:** C204 Phase 01 (Geneva Legal) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva cantonal government faces emerging legal exposure for algorithmic decisions affecting residents — with the Federal Supreme Court's DABUS ruling and nFADP Article 6 enforcement creating a convergence point. The État de Genève's use of AI in administrative decisions (benefits allocation, permit processing, tax assessment assistance) is creating state liability questions that Geneva government counsel are actively seeking to resolve.

## Evidence
- BGer DABUS ruling (June 2025) established human contribution standard for AI-assisted government decisions
- nFADP Article 6 enforcement H1 2026 including government AI systems as subject to data minimization obligations
- Genève state administrative tribunal (TAC) received first administrative appeal citing AI decision-making lack of human review — Q1 2026
- État de Genève administrative reform office reviewing all AI-assisted administrative decision systems — 14 systems identified
- Grand Council debating AI administrative decision transparency law — first reading expected H2 2026

## Advisory Implication
Government AI liability advisory — helping cantonal government draft AI administrative decision frameworks satisfying BGer human contribution standard and nFADP Article 6. State responsibility for algorithmic decisions is novel but concrete.

## Confidence
High — government action, court activity, legislative debate
