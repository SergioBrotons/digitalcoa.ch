# C212-P01-F03 | Geneva Judicial Ethics Committee AI Advisory Opinion — Recusal/Split-Decision Doctrine

## Finding Summary
Geneva judicial ethics committee issued first AI advisory opinion — judges using AI in deliberation must disclose and justify.

## Key Details
- **Body:** Geneva Judicial Ethics Committee (commission d'éthique judiciaire)
- **Opinion:** AI use in judgment drafting triggers disclosure obligation; split AI/human decision doctrine
- **Scope:** Recusal requests based on AI use, challenge procedures
- **Standard:** AI must not replace judicial reasoning; AI assistance transparency required
- **Impact:** First recusal motions based on AI assistance now adjudicated

## Strategic Relevance
- Legal advisory: Recusal procedure update for Geneva litigation attorneys
- Judicial training: AI ethics framework development
- Client communication: AI-assisted court proceedings navigation

## Distinct from C211
C211 had Geneva civil court AI judgment drafting doctrine — C212 addresses the ethics/recusal framework for AI-assisted judging
