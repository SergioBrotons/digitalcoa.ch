# C207-04 — Swiss Government AI Liability Discussion Paper — Cantonal Government AI Accountability
**Phase:** C207 Phase 01 (Geneva Legal) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
The Federal Office of Justice (BJ) discussion paper on AI civil liability is expected to generate cantonal input throughout 2026. Genève is preparing its response through the Grand Council's Legal Affairs Committee, focusing on state liability for algorithmic decisions made by cantonal government AI systems. BJ liability discussion paper expected by end-2026. Geneva's position is likely to advocate for clear state liability exemptions for "recommendation-only" AI systems used in public administration.

## Evidence
- BJ discussion paper on AI civil liability expected end-2026
- Genève Grand Council Legal Affairs Committee preparing cantonal input on state liability for algorithmic decisions
- Focus: state liability exemptions for "recommendation-only" AI systems in public administration
- Geneva cantonal AI administrative decision transparency ordinance (Otrante) implementation proceeding independently of federal framework
- Cantonal AI system inventory under active review for compliance with emerging liability standards

## Advisory Implication
Government AI liability advisory is a novel but concrete niche — helping cantonal government draft AI administrative decision frameworks that satisfy the BJ liability discussion paper's emerging standards. The advisory question for private clients: what is the liability exposure when a government AI system that a private firm helped design produces a harmful recommendation? Geneva government contracts counsel need to address AI liability allocation in government AI procurement contracts now, ahead of the BJ discussion paper conclusions.

## Confidence
High — BJ discussion paper timeline, Grand Council Legal Affairs Committee activity

## FR
### Papier de Discussion du Gouvernement Fédéral sur la Responsabilité IA
Le papier de discussion de l'Office fédéral de la justice (BJ) sur la responsabilité civile IA devrait générer des contributions cantonales tout au long de 2026. Genève prépare sa réponse par l'intermédiaire de la Commission des affaires juridiques du Grand Conseil, en se concentrant sur la responsabilité étatique pour les décisions algorithmiques prises par les systèmes d'IA de l'administration cantonale. Le papier de discussion du BJ est attendu pour la fin 2026. La position genevoise plaidera probablement pour des exonérations de responsabilité étatique claires pour les systèmes d'IA « recommandation uniquement » utilisés dans l'administration publique. La mise en œuvre de l'ordonnance cantonale genevoise sur la transparence des décisions administratives liées à l'IA (Otrante) devrait se poursuivre indépendamment du cadre fédéral.
