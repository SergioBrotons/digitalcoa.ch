# Finding 05 — DFJP Federal Legislative Project: AI Transparency Requirements for Civil Procedure by End-2026

**Phase:** Phase 01 Geneva Legal
**Cycle:** C206 | **Date:** 2026-04-06 | **Sources:** Web Search (Pestalozzi Law, Global Legal Insights, BJ.admin.ch, GE justice, Vischer, BaerKarrer, GGI) + Domain Knowledge

## Finding

The Swiss Federal Department of Justice and Police (DFJP) has been tasked by the Federal Council with developing a preliminary draft for new rules concerning AI systems by the end of 2026. This federal legislative project is the most significant upcoming AI transparency requirement affecting civil procedure in Switzerland and will directly impact Geneva courts, law firms, and in-house legal teams.

**Legislative scope (as known):**
- Targeted areas: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, and surveillance in AI contexts
- Expected to introduce mandatory disclosure obligations for AI systems used in civil proceedings
- Preliminary draft due end-2026; timing of consultation and enactment uncertain but likely 2027-2028
- Part of Switzerland's Council of Europe AI Convention ratification pathway (signed March 2025)

**Geneva civil procedure context:**
- Geneva's digital transition of justice is ongoing: federal LPCJ framework for electronic judicial files + March 2026 consultation on OCEP (Ordonnance sur la communication électronique dans les procédures judiciaires et administratives)
- OCEP consultation March 2026 signals infrastructure readiness for mandatory AI disclosure in electronic filings — Geneva courts are building the technical capacity to receive and process AI disclosure
- January 2026 CPC revision (cross-border evidence, videoconferencing without prior authorization) demonstrates Geneva's appetite for procedural modernization

**Key advisory implications:**
- Geneva litigation practitioners need to develop AI disclosure readiness now — even before the law passes, opposing counsel will use anticipated disclosure obligations as pressure points in discovery disputes
- Geneva courts will likely develop informal AI disclosure practices ahead of formal legislation — practitioners should document AI tool use in brief preparation as a precautionary measure
- AI tool vendors serving Geneva law firms will need to provide documentation packages suitable for court disclosure — a market product gap currently
- The consultation process for the DFJP draft (expected 2027) represents a lobbying and comment window Geneva institutional actors should not miss

## Distinct from C205
C205 Finding 5 covered the Geneva civil procedure reform 2026 AI disclosure amendments as "upcoming CPTG revisions." This C206 finding goes further: identifies the specific federal legislative vehicle (DFJP project, end-2026), the OCEP March 2026 consultation as procedural infrastructure, and the Council of Europe AI Convention as the ratification framework — providing a more precise picture of the regulatory pathway and timeline.
