# Finding 05 — C210 Phase 01
**Topic:** Geneva Prosecution Service AI-Mediated Restorative Justice Pilot
**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C210

## Core Finding
The Geneva prosecution service (Ministère public) launched a pilot programme using AI-facilitated victim-offender mediation in select criminal matters — the first systematic deployment of AI mediation tools in Swiss criminal procedure. The pilot runs through end of 2026, with an evaluation by the Geneva Faculty of Law expected in Q1 2027 to determine expansion.

## Key Details
- **Legal basis:** CPP (Code de procédure pénale) Art. 316 restorative justice framework + Geneva prosecution service administrative authority to select mediation modalities
- **Pilot scope:** Property crimes and minor assault cases where both parties consent; AI tool facilitates communication scheduling, pre-mediation preparation, and agreement draft preparation
- **AI role:** Strictly facilitative — AI prepares victim-offender communication summaries, suggests mediation structures, and drafts proposed agreements; human mediators retain final authority
- **Data protection safeguards:** nFADP compliance framework applied; AI-processed victim/offender communications not stored beyond pilot duration; no training on pilot data
- **Procedural fairness concerns:** Geneva defence lawyers raising concerns about AI bias in assessing offender rehabilitation potential; proportionality of AI-generated agreement recommendations; right to human mediation
- **Geneva Faculty of Law evaluation:** Independent assessment of procedural fairness, victim satisfaction, recidivism outcomes, and AI tool reliability — results shape expansion decision

## Practical Implications
- Geneva criminal practice adapting to AI-mediated restorative justice as alternative to standard prosecution
- Defence lawyers need to develop competence in AI-mediated mediation frameworks
- Geneva victims' rights organisations watching pilot outcomes — potential for broader victim-offender AI mediation programmes
- Geneva courts may incorporate AI-mediated agreements as sentencing considerations
- AI tool vendors seeking Geneva prosecution service contract — procurement and liability framework implications
