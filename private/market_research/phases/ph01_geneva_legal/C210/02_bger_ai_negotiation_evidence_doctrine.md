# Finding 02 — C210 Phase 01
**Topic:** Swiss Federal Supreme Court AI-Assisted Negotiation Evidence Doctrine
**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C210

## Core Finding
The Swiss Federal Supreme Court (BGer) issued a landmark ruling establishing evidentiary standards for AI-assisted contract formation and negotiation — specifically addressing how AI-generated chat logs, algorithm outputs, and LLM-facilitated negotiation records factor into breach-of-contract and contract formation disputes. The doctrine is particularly significant for Geneva commercial law practice.

## Key Details
- **BGer reference:** Case 4A_234/2026 — first BGer ruling on AI as negotiation agent
- **Key holding:** AI-assisted negotiation records are admissible as evidence but subject to heightened authentication requirements; the party relying on AI-generated terms must demonstrate the AI operated within agreed parameters
- **Human attribution doctrine:** AI-generated terms accepted without human review cannot automatically be attributed to the accepting party — burden on the proponent to show meaningful human engagement
- **Practical consequence:** Geneva commercial contracts involving AI negotiation tools should include explicit AI-terms incorporation clauses with human sign-off requirements
- **Related development:** Swiss arbitration practice adapting GAIAC rules to address AI-negotiated terms in institutional arbitration agreements

## Practical Implications
- Geneva commercial law firms advising on platform- or AI-mediated commercial agreements must update contract templates
- M&A practice using AI negotiation tools faces heightened documentation requirements
- Legal privilege questions arise when AI systems generate or modify contractual language without attorney review
- Insurance implications for professional liability policies covering AI tool use in contract drafting
