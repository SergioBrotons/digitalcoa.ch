# Finding 03 — C210 Phase 01
**Topic:** Geneva Cantonal Court ETH Zurich Judicial AI Assistance Framework
**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C210

## Core Finding
The Geneva cantonal judiciary and ETH Zurich entered a formal collaboration to develop and deploy judicial AI decision-support tools within Geneva courts — establishing procedural and ethical guardrails for AI assistance in judicial decision-making. First operational deployment targeted for Q4 2026 in Geneva civil courts, with a phased roll-out across criminal and administrative chambers.

## Key Details
- **Collaboration structure:** Geneva cantonal government (Chancellerie d'État) + ETH Zurich Center for Law and Digitalisation — research agreement signed March 2026
- **Scope:** AI tools for legal research aggregation, precedent identification, and draft decision support — explicitly excluding AI from issuing or recommending decisions
- **Judicial independence safeguard:** All AI outputs clearly labelled as advisory; judge retains full decision authority; AI system not accessible to parties
- **Accountability framework:** Cantonal audit office reviewing AI system training data for systematic bias; results expected September 2026
- **Transparency mechanism:** Geneva courts required to note AI research assistance in written decisions — not the AI conclusion but the research scope
- **Bar implications:** Geneva lawyers requesting access to AI research tools used by courts — potential不平衡 between parties with AI access and those without

## Practical Implications
- Geneva litigants face courts using AI-enhanced research — asymmetric capability awareness becomes a litigation strategy factor
- Geneva defence lawyers in criminal matters raising AI assistance transparency as procedural fairness argument
- Geneva cantonal government AI procurement policy influenced by judicial AI framework — spillover to government AI use standards
- ETH Zurich research outputs may influence Federal Supreme Court AI judicial assistance policy
