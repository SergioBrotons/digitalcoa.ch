# Finding 01 — C210 Phase 01
**Topic:** Geneva Civil Procedure OCEP AI Disclosure Enforcement — First Penalties
**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C210

## Core Finding
Geneva commercial courts issued first non-compliance penalties under CPTG Art. 2023/X2 (AI tool disclosure in written submissions), creating immediate procedural risk for Geneva law firms. The Geneva Bar Association reported 3 documented sanctions cases in Q1 2026, with 2 additional cases under review at the cantonal court level.

## Key Details
- **Legal basis:** CPTG amendment effective 01.01.2026, Art. 2023/X2 requiring mandatory AI tool disclosure when any AI system contributed to drafting or substantially editing written submissions
- **First penalties:** Fines ranging from CHF 500–2,000 for non-disclosure; one case escalated to procedural invalidation of the submission
- **Enforcement trigger:** Opposing counsel challenging AI-drafted content identified through stylometric analysis
- **Affected practice areas:** Geneva commercial litigation, employment tribunal, and insurance dispute practice

## Practical Implications
- Geneva law firms using AI drafting tools must now maintain disclosure logs
- Geneva courts expecting AI usage statements in case opening submissions
- Opposition counsel actively using stylometric tools to identify undisclosed AI content
- Geneva Bar Association issuing practice note on compliant AI tool use
