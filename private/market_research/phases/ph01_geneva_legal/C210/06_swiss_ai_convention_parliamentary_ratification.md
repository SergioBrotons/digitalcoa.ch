# Finding 06 — C210 Phase 01
**Topic:** Swiss AI Convention Parliamentary Ratification — Geneva Cantonal Position
**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C210

## Core Finding
The Swiss Federal Assembly began formal parliamentary deliberation on ratifying the Council of Europe AI Convention (AI Convention ETS No. 225), with Geneva cantonal representatives signalling support. The ratification process has significant implications for Geneva-based international organisations, NGOs, and multinational companies subject to the Convention's AI accountability requirements.

## Key Details
- **Convention scope:** Binding international treaty establishing AI accountability obligations for public authorities and private sector; applies to Geneva-based international organisations operating in Switzerland under host state agreements
- **Parliamentary process:** Council of States (Council of thecantons) leading deliberation; National Council following; ratification bill expected to pass with broad cross-party support
- **Geneva cantonal position:** Geneva representation in the Council of States supporting ratification; key arguments include Geneva's role as international organisations hub, need for harmonised AI governance standards, and alignment with Geneva cantonal AI strategy
- **International organisations question:** Whether the AI Convention applies to UNOG, ITU, WHO, WTO, and other Geneva-based international organisations — legal opinion being sought by Federal Department of Foreign Affairs (FDFA)
- **NGO accountability:** Geneva-based NGOs with AI systems processing public data would face new accountability requirements under the Convention
- **Multinational companies:** Geneva-headquartered multinationals with AI systems used in Switzerland subject to transparency and accountability obligations
- **Timeline:** Parliamentary debate expected Q3 2026; ratification potentially effective 2027

## Practical Implications
- Geneva-based international organisations reviewing AI systems against Convention obligations — inventory and gap analysis needed
- Geneva law firms advising multinationals and NGOs on AI Convention compliance — new advisory service line emerging
- Federal Council's position aligning with Geneva cantonal support — ratification momentum building
- Swiss AI governance framework (separate from Convention) still in legislative process — Convention ratification may influence domestic AI law design
