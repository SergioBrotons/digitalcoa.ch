# C207-03 — EU AI Act Service Provider Compliance for Geneva Professional Services
**Phase:** C207 Phase 01 (Geneva Legal) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
Geneva-based law firms, consulting firms, and professional services companies serving EU clients face EU AI Act service provider obligations (Article 3 definitions). When a Geneva-based firm provides AI-enabled services to an EU-based client, the firm may qualify as an EU AI Act "provider" or "deployer" depending on where the AI system is operated and who controls its outputs. Geneva professional services firms are conducting AI system inventories to assess EU exposure.

## Evidence
- EU AI Act Article 3 definitions: "provider" and "deployer" classifications apply extraterritorially to non-EU firms serving EU clients
- Geneva professional services firms conducting AI system inventories to assess EU AI Act exposure
- EU AI Act Article 51 conformity assessment requirements creating demand for Geneva-based conformity assessment advisory
- Cross-border AI service provision from Switzerland requires legal structure analysis per EU AI Act extraterritorial scope
- Geneva law firms and consulting firms with EU client portfolios most exposed

## Advisory Implication
Geneva professional services firms need EU AI Act classification analysis, AI system inventory support, and conformity assessment roadmapping. Cross-border AI service provision from Switzerland creates a structural legal question — advisors who can analyze which Geneva firms qualify as EU AI Act providers versus deployers have a concrete advisory entry point. EU AI Act conformity assessment support is a direct service offering for Geneva-based AI-enabled service providers.

## Confidence
High — EU AI Act extraterritorial scope, Geneva professional services firm activity

## FR
### Conformité au Règlement IA de l'UE pour les Services Professionnels Genevois
Les études genevoises, cabinets de conseil et entreprises de services professionnels servant des clients européens sont confrontés aux obligations de fournisseur ou de déployeur du règlement européen sur l'IA (article 3). Lorsqu'une entreprise genevoise fournit des services activés par l'IA à un client européen, elle peut être qualify comme « fournisseur » ou « déployeur » du règlement européen sur l'IA, selon l'endroit où le système d'IA est exploité et qui contrôle ses résultats. Les entreprises de services professionnels genevoises mènent des inventaires de systèmes d'IA pour évaluer leur exposition européenne. Les exigences d'évaluation de conformité de l'article 51 créent une demande de conseil en évaluation de conformité basée à Genève.
