# C205 Finding 03 — Geneva Law Firm AI Professional Liability

**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

The Geneva Bar Association (OdvG) initiated first-ever professional disciplinary proceedings involving AI tool use by member law firms in Q1 2026 — establishing AI disclosure obligations as active professional conduct requirements.

## Key Details

- **Disciplinary trigger:** AI-generated court submission filed without AI tool disclosure; client privilege implications from AI audit trail access demands
- **OdvG competence standard revision:** Geneva lawyers must now demonstrate AI tool competence before use in client matters — training documentation requirements
- **Mandatory AI disclosure in mandate letters:** As of Q1 2026, Geneva law firms required to disclose AI tool usage scope, limitations, and data handling to clients in writing
- **Privilege waiver risk:** Court orders compelling production of AI tool prompts/inputs in discovery — privilege doctrine being tested by AI audit requirements
- **Professional indemnity insurer response:** Swiss legal liability insurers now requesting AI tool schedules as part of policy renewal — AI-specific exclusions emerging
