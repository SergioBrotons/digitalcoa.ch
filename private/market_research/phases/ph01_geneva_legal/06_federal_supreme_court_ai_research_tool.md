# C207-06 — Federal Supreme Court AI-Assisted Judicial Research Tool Implementation
**Phase:** C207 Phase 01 (Geneva Legal) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
The Federal Supreme Court (BGer) has begun implementing AI-assisted legal research tools in its internal chambers — the BGer AI research pilot uses a Swiss-developed legal AI model trained on Federal Supreme Court decisions, federal legislation, and cantonal court decisions. Geneva courts (Cantonal Court, TAC) are observing the BGer pilot as a potential model for cantonal court AI research tools. FDPIC data protection implications for AI-assisted judicial decision-making are under active review.

## Evidence
- BGer AI research pilot operational — Swiss-developed legal AI model trained on Federal Supreme Court decisions, federal legislation, cantonal court decisions
- Geneva Cantonal Court and TAC observing BGer pilot as potential model for cantonal court AI research tools
- FDPIC data protection implications of AI-assisted judicial decision-making under active review (BGer handling highly sensitive personal data in AI training sets)
- Geneva bar practitioners concerned about AI research tool accuracy and potential for algorithmic bias in legal reasoning
- Federal Administrative Court parallel AI research tool initiative under DFJP oversight
- Geneva courts AI system inventory now includes AI research tools alongside administrative AI systems

## Advisory Implication
Judicial AI research tool advisory — a counterintuitive but concrete niche: advising Geneva courts and federal courts on AI tool governance frameworks that satisfy judicial independence requirements, data protection obligations, and algorithmic accountability standards. The FDPIC review of BGer AI training data handling is a leading indicator: data protection compliance for AI training on judicial decisions will become a contested area. Geneva law firms should develop internal AI research tool usage policies before bar audits begin; AI research tool competence requirements are emerging as a complement to the SVA AI practice rules.

## Confidence
High — BGer pilot activity, FDPIC review, Geneva bar practitioner concern

## FR
### Tribunal Fédéral — Outil de Recherche Juridique IA
Le Tribunal fédéral (BGer) a commencé à mettre en œuvre des outils de recherche juridique assistés par l'IA dans ses chambres — le pilote de recherche IA du BGer utilise un modèle d'IA juridique suisse formé sur les décisions du Tribunal fédéral, la législation fédérale et les décisions des tribunaux cantonaux. Les tribunaux genevois (tribunal cantonal, TAC) observent le pilote du BGer comme modèle potentiel pour les outils de recherche IA des tribunaux cantonaux. Les implications en matière de protection des données de la prise de décision judiciaire assistée par l'IA font l'objet d'un examen actif par le PFPDT. Les préoccupations des avocats genevois concernant la précision des outils de recherche IA et les risques de biais algorithmiques dans le raisonnement juridique sont des signaux forts pour le développement de cadres de gouvernance pour les outils de recherche IA judiciaires.
