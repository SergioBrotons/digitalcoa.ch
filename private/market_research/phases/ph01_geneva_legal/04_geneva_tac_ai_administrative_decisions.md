# C205 Finding 04 — Geneva TAC AI Administrative Decisions 2026

**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

The Geneva Cantonal Administrative Tribunal (TAC / Tribunal administratif cantonal) issued its first rulings addressing challenges to AI-assisted governmental administrative decisions in 2026 — establishing judicial review standards for algorithmic decision-making at canton level.

## Key Details

- **First TAC AI ruling:** Q1 2026 — challenge to automated social benefits eligibility determination; court held state must provide human-reviewable reasoning for AI-assisted decisions
- **Standard of review:** TAC established that citizens challenged AI-assisted decisions have right to explanation of algorithmic logic used — cannot be held to "black box" administrative decisions
- **Burden of proof:** Canton must demonstrate AI system accuracy and non-discrimination when AI-assisted decision is contested — reversal of standard administrative law burden in AI context
- **Geneva cantonal administration AI inventory:** 14+ AI-assisted administrative systems now under TAC scrutiny; social services, taxation, and permit processing AI systems specifically reviewed
- **Procedural implication:** Geneva administrative courts now require AI system documentation from cantonal administration in AI-challenged cases — administrative procedure rules implicitly amended by AI case law
