# Finding 03 — FDPIC nFADP Enforcement Acceleration: 26 Inquiries, Active Investigation, Cookie Guidance Update

**Phase:** Phase 01 Geneva Legal
**Cycle:** C206 | **Date:** 2026-04-06 | **Sources:** Web Search (ICLG, Didomi, Edoeb.admin.ch, Privacydesk, AWSM Tech) + Domain Knowledge

## Finding

The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) has significantly accelerated nFADP enforcement activity through early 2026. As of November 2024, the FDPIC had opened 26 preliminary inquiries and investigations under the new law, with 7 already concluded. By February 2026, an active investigation into a transport company was confirmed. A new cookie and similar technologies guidance was issued in March 2026, updating October 2025 guidance.

**Geneva-specific enforcement context:**
- FDPIC enforcement is nationally applied but Geneva-based organizations face heightened scrutiny due to the density of international organizations, financial institutions, and technology companies subject to cross-border data processing obligations
- The October 2025 cookie guidance and March 2026 update signal the FDPIC's continued focus on digital transparency compliance — a leading indicator of broader enforcement priorities
- The Bürgerforum Schweiz processing ban (October 2025, upheld by Federal Administrative Court) demonstrates the FDPIC's willingness to use its most powerful remedial tool — processing prohibition — rather than just issuing recommendations

**Key enforcement structure facts:**
- FDPIC cannot issue direct administrative fines; willful violations can result in criminal fines up to CHF 250,000, enforced by cantonal prosecution
- FDPIC investigations are trending toward organizations with AI-adjacent data processing (transport, financial services, technology)
- Geneva organizations with AI-based profiling, automated decision-making, or cross-border data flows are at elevated enforcement risk

**Advisory implications:**
- Geneva organizations need nFADP AI system inventory audits before FDPIC inquiry arrives — proactive compliance mapping as defensive strategy
- Cookie consent framework review is urgent given March 2026 guidance update
- AI automated decision-making documentation (Art. 21 nFADP human review obligation) is an enforcement gap Geneva organizations are not yet addressing
- FDPIC investigation response planning is a discrete advisory service — the difference between prepared and unprepared response is material

**Sources:** ICLG Data Protection Switzerland; Didomi blog on nFADP; edoeb.admin.ch latest news; Privacydesk cookie guidance analysis March 2026; AWSM Tech nFADP Guide 2026.

This C206 finding adds: the actual enforcement pipeline scale (26 inquiries, 7 concluded by Nov 2024), the February 2026 active transport company investigation, the March 2026 cookie guidance update, and the Bürgerforum Schweiz processing ban as precedent for FDPIC's most aggressive remedial tool.
