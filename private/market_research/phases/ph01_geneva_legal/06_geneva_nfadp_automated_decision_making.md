# C205 Finding 06 — Geneva nFADP Art. 9 AI Automated Decision-Making Rights

**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

The FDPIC issued operational guidance on nFADP Article 9 automated decision-making rights in 2026, with Geneva organizations becoming the first enforcement priority — establishing AI profiling challenge procedures and AI system inventory obligations.

## Key Details

- **Art. 22 nFADP scope:** Data subjects have right to human review of AI automated decisions with legal or similar significant effects; applies to Geneva organization AI profiling systems
- **FDPIC guidance issued:** Q1 2026 guidance specifies AI system inventory requirement — organizations must document all AI systems making automated decisions affecting individuals
- **Geneva enforcement priority:** FDPIC identified Geneva as priority canton for Art. 9 compliance enforcement Q3 2026 — 20+ Geneva organizations with documented AI profiling systems
- **AI system inventory obligations:** Must include: AI system purpose, data inputs, decision logic category, human oversight mechanism, appeal procedure
- **First FDPIC Art. 9 rulings:** FDPIC issued first rulings on AI profiling challenges — organizations found non-compliant face penalty schedule CHF 10M or 2% global turnover
- **Art. 9 exemption documentation:** Organizations using AI for automated decisions must document why human review is disproportionate or technically impossible — exemption burden on organizations
