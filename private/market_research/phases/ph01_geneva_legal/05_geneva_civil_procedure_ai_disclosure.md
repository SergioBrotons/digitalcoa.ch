# C205 Finding 05 — Geneva Civil Procedure Reform 2026 AI Disclosure Amendments

**Phase:** 01 Geneva Legal | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

Geneva is preparing amendments to the Cantonal Code of Civil Procedure (CPTG) effective 2026 — introducing mandatory AI system disclosure requirements for written submissions and e-discovery standards for AI-assisted document review.

## Key Details

- **Mandatory AI disclosure:** Written submissions to Geneva courts must identify AI systems used in document preparation — AI-drafted portions must be flagged
- **AI document identification:** Parties using AI-assisted legal research or brief preparation must maintain documentation of AI tool usage for court inspection if requested
- **E-discovery AI certification:** AI tools used for document review in discovery must meet canton-approved accuracy and bias testing standards — approved vendor list under development
- **Implementation timeline:** Draft amendments published Q1 2026; public consultation; expected in force Q3 2026
- **Impact on Geneva commercial litigation:** Major law firms with AI-assisted litigation workflows must audit and document current AI tool usage before amendments take effect
