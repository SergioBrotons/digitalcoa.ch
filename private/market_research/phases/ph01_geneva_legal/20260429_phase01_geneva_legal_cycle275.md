## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: AI model deprecation surfacing past research that cannot be reconstructed**
Platform version retirement making past AI research unrecoverable. Research cited in filed briefs cannot be reproduced when challenged. The deprecation event is surfacing a retroactive accountability gap no one anticipated.

**Signal 2: Carrier attestation asking for methodology confirmation the MD cannot define**
Carrier questionnaire asking for methodology confirmation that the managing partner cannot actually provide. The gap between what the attestation asks for and what the firm can confirm is a personal liability exposure.

**Signal 3: Bar continuing to provide no guidance twelve months after initial inquiry**
Bar office confirming no position formed twelve months after specific inquiry. The absence of guidance is not equivalent to permission. Firms are operating without any formal reference point.

**Signal 4: Client direct question about training data exposing vendor terms gap**
Client asking directly whether their information was used to train the AI system. The honest answer requires reading vendor terms. The answer is typically yes without a specific agreement otherwise.

**Signal 5: EU AI Act deployer questionnaires arriving from EU-connected clients**
EU clients sending deployer questionnaires to Swiss attorneys. The questionnaires assume a compliance infrastructure that Swiss law firms do not have and no published standard defines what "EU AI Act compliant legal services" means for a Swiss law firm.

**Signal 6: AI authentication challenge in arbitration proceedings emerging**
Opposing counsel beginning to challenge AI-assisted briefs. No Geneva court has established what the authentication standard is, who bears the burden of proof, or what the procedural consequences are.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**SVA/ORDRE and AI guidance:**
Geneva Bar Association has published no AI-specific guidance as of April 2026. The SBA published AI guidelines in June 2024 that are useful as orientation but provide no operational discharge methodology for small firm obligations.

**nFADP and attorney work product:**
nFADP Articles 13 and 21 apply to AI-assisted decisions affecting data subjects. Attorney work product generated with AI assistance creates a derivation question the nFADP does not resolve for the legal professional context.

**Article 321 CP and vendor terms:**
Article 321 CP requires that client information not be accessible to third parties. Vendor terms of service consistently contain data use provisions for model improvement. No Geneva law firm has obtained legal confirmation that these are compatible.

**FDPIC and AI guidance:**
The Federal Data Protection Information Commissioner has not published guidance specifically addressing attorney use of AI systems. Absence of guidance is not evidence of compliance.

**Geneva civil procedure and AI disclosure:**
No Geneva court has established an AI authentication protocol for disputed filings. The procedural question of how to verify AI-assisted work product authenticity is formally undefined.

**EU AI Act and Swiss law firm deployer status:**
Swiss law firms serving EU-connected clients may qualify as deployers under EU AI Act Annex III. No published standard defines what compliance looks like for a Swiss law firm in this context.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI model deprecation as retroactive accountability gap for filed briefs**
Platform version retirement makes past AI research unrecoverable. Research cited in filed briefs cannot be reproduced when opposing counsel challenges it. No retrieval obligation exists on any Swiss-hosted platform. The gap surfaces at the worst moment: when the brief is on file and the research cannot be reconstructed.

**Finding 2: Carrier attestation personal liability exposure from undefined methodology confirmation**
The carrier asks for methodology confirmation the managing partner cannot define. The attestation commits to a methodology that is not defined in the policy. No Geneva market participant has published what "AI methodology" means in this context. The signatory is personally exposed to an undefined standard.

**Finding 3: Article 321 CP vendor terms compatibility assessment absent from Geneva market**
No Geneva service has produced a vendor terms compatibility assessment for Article 321 CP professional secrecy obligations. The assessment would need to evaluate data use provisions, model improvement rights, and jurisdiction clauses against the statutory obligation. The absence means firms are operating under unresolved conflict.

**Finding 4: EU AI Act Annex III deployer status for Swiss law firms serving EU clients uncharted**
Swiss law firms serving EU-connected clients receive deployer questionnaires with no published standard for what "EU AI Act compliant legal services" means. The compliance pathway is undefined. Firms are improvising responses that may create obligations they cannot satisfy.

**Finding 5: Bar advisory opinion mechanism structurally inaccessible to small firms**
The Geneva bar advisory opinion mechanism is inaccessible to small firms because the questions small firms have are not the questions the mechanism was designed to address. The structural gap means firms operate without guidance from the institution responsible for governing their conduct.

**Finding 6: Geneva civil procedure AI authentication vacuum creating bilateral exposure in arbitration**
No authentication protocol exists for AI-assisted work product in Geneva arbitration. Both sides face symmetric uncertainty: the filing party cannot prove AI authorship and opposing counsel cannot disprove it. The vacuum benefits the party raising the challenge and disadvantages the firm whose brief is challenged.
