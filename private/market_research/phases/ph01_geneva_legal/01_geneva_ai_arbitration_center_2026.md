## Finding

A new neutral AI arbitration center was announced for establishment in Geneva, Switzerland, on February 19, 2026, to address cross-border disputes related to artificial intelligence technology. This represents the first Geneva-based institution specifically designed to adjudicate AI-native disputes, filling a structural gap in Geneva's already dense international arbitration ecosystem.

**Key structural features (as announced):**
- Purpose-built for AI technology disputes — distinct from general commercial arbitration
- Fast, efficient alternative to traditional litigation for AI-related conflicts
- Cross-border orientation consistent with Geneva's international org hub positioning
- Neutral multi-stakeholder governance model implied by "neutral" designation

**Critical gap (as of April 2026):** Specific procedural rules for the center have not yet been published as of early April 2026. This creates a narrow advisory window — Geneva law firms and arbitration practitioners who engage early with rule-drafting consultation processes or publish率先 analysis of expected procedural standards can position as go-to advisors before the market consolidates.

**Advisory implications:**

- Geneva's existing arbitration institutions (ICC, WIPO, LCIA) may experience jurisdictional competition once the new center is operational
