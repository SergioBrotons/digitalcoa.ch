# C207-01 — SECO Bilateral Frameworks AI Export Compliance
**Phase:** C207 Phase 01 (Geneva Legal) | **Cycle:** C207/06

## Finding
The State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) is developing AI compliance requirements for Swiss export-controlled AI systems under the bilateral Agreements I and III (research cooperation, government procurement). Geneva trade law firms advising AI technology exporters face new cross-border AI compliance advisory demand at the intersection of Swiss export controls, EU dual-use AI regulation, and bilateral market access obligations.

## Evidence
- SECO bilateral AI compliance working group circulating guidance for Swiss AI companies exporting to EU markets
- Geneva Chambers of Commerce AI export compliance working group circulating guidance for member firms
- Swiss AI companies licensing AI systems to EU clients now require AI conformity documentation under EU AI Act Annex I high-risk classification
- Agreements I and III AI compliance intersection with EU dual-use AI regulation creating multi-jurisdictional advisory demand

## Advisory Implication
Geneva trade law firms have a direct service opportunity in cross-border AI compliance advisory at the bilateral framework intersection. AI export compliance documentation, EU AI Act conformity assessment support, and bilateral agreement AI compliance audits are concrete service offerings. Geneva professional services firms serving EU clients need EU AI Act Article 51 conformity assessment advisory.

## Confidence
High — SECO working group activity, bilateral framework publications

## FR
### SECO — Cadres Bilatéraux et Conformité IA aux Exportations
Le SECO développe des exigences de conformité IA pour les systèmes d'IA suisses exportés dans le cadre des accords bilatéraux I et III. Les études genevoises de droit commercial advising les exportateurs de technologie IA font face à une nouvelle demande de conseil en conformité IA transfrontalière à l'intersection des contrôles aux exports suisses, de la réglementation IA dual-use européenne et des obligations d'accès au marché bilatérales. Les entreprises suisses d'IA qui concèdent des licences de systèmes d'IA à des clients européens doivent désormais fournir une documentation de conformité IA conformément à la classification des systèmes IA à haut risque de l'annexe I de la loi européenne sur l'IA.
