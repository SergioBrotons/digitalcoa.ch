## 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Executives Describe Their Need for AI Governance Advice

### English

**The maintenance question as a discrete unmet need:**

*"The summary you produced — I keep referring back to it. But three things have changed since we spoke and I don't know whether those changes affect what you wrote. Is there a protocol for that? I don't think we agreed on one."*

*"I have documentation from two years of AI tool decisions that were never designed to fit together. Carrier renewal requests, counterparty questionnaires, board briefings — each one produced a different document. None of them were designed to be coherent with the others. Now I have a drawer full of documents that don't tell a consistent story."*

*"The question I'm most embarrassed to ask: how often should this be updated? Nobody told me. So I just leave it until something forces me to look at it again. That's not a system."*

*"I was asked to describe our AI governance position to a counterparty. I pulled the most recent document. Then I pulled the one from before that. They're different. Not updated — different. I didn't have a way to explain the difference without making it worse."*

*"I know what we decided. I know why we decided it at the time. What I don't know is whether the conditions that led to that decision still hold. Nobody owns that question. I've been watching it become a problem without seeing where to raise it."*

**Triggers:**

- A post-briefing condition change — tool update, staff departure, regulatory communication, counterparty event — that creates genuine uncertainty about whether the written summary still reflects current conditions
- Documentation accumulated across multiple obligation cycles (carrier renewal, counterparty questionnaire, board briefing, client due diligence) that was never designed to form a coherent record, now creating internal inconsistency the executive cannot explain cleanly
- The absence of an update protocol — no defined moment or framework for deciding whether a written position has aged into inadequacy
- A peer conversation in which the executive discovers that others are managing the same documentation problem silently, with no public language for the maintenance question
- A career transition — joining a new board, accepting a new role — that positions the executive as accountable for AI governance decisions they did not make and cannot fully reconstruct

**Expectations:**

- A bounded, specific service that addresses the documentation maintenance question without requiring the executive to commit to ongoing advisory retainer
- Clarity on what triggers a documentation update and what form the update takes
- A coherent record architecture — not one document, but an integrated documentation system that explains its own internal consistency over time
- Discretion about the existence of the advisory engagement itself, beyond discretion about its content
- The ability to refer to the advisory relationship in confidence to a peer without that reference creating downstream exposure

**Distrusts:**

- Any advisory offering that implies open-ended ongoing engagement as the only way to maintain documentation adequacy — executives distrust solutions that create dependency without addressing the underlying maintenance question
- Documentation that was produced to satisfy a specific obligation but cannot be integrated with documentation produced for other obligations
- The suggestion that AI governance documentation is a one-time deliverable — executives who have been through a carrier renewal understand that documentation has a useful life, and they distrust advisors who do not address maintenance
- Any practice that appears to be monetizing the documentation maintenance problem rather than resolving it
- Advisors who describe independence without demonstrating it through specific behavioral exclusions maintained over time

## 02 — Market Signals: Where Geneva Executive Demand Is Emerging

**Signal 1: Documentation coherence as distinct from documentation existence**
The market's anxiety has shifted from "do we have documents" to "do our documents tell a coherent story." Executives who have produced documentation for multiple obligations (carrier renewal, counterparty questionnaire, board briefing) are discovering that their documents, taken together, tell a story they cannot explain. The problem is no longer documentation existence — it is documentation coherence.

**Signal 2: Post-briefing maintenance gap as discrete unmet demand category**
Executives who have received a written summary face a maintenance question nobody addressed at the time of the engagement. The absence of an update protocol creates anxiety: the executive has a document that may or may not reflect current conditions, with no framework for deciding. The maintenance gap is an unmet demand category that has not been named in the market.

**Signal 3: Peer-network silence as information signal gaining market recognition**
More Geneva executives are beginning to interpret peer silence about AI governance as an active information signal — the absence of a conversation is itself a statement about exposure. The recognition of silence as signal is creating demand for advisors who can read the informal circuit and interpret what is not being said.

**Signal 4: Career-transition AI governance debt becoming active trigger**
Senior professionals transitioning between roles — joining a board, accepting a new executive position — are encountering AI governance accountability for decisions they did not make. The transition moment is emerging as a specific advisory occasion distinct from incident-triggered or regulatory-triggered demand.

**Signal 5: Geneva documentation architecture gap as structural market failure**
The absence of an integrated documentation architecture — documentation designed to be coherent across carrier renewal, counterparty questionnaire, and board briefing obligations — is a structural gap. No Geneva advisor currently offers a documentation architecture service. The gap is systemic, not firm-specific.

**Signal 6: Documentation maintenance as a discrete service category unnamed in the market**
Executives are paying for AI governance documentation but not for its maintenance. The maintenance question — what triggers an update, what form the update takes, who owns the update decision — is not addressed by any current advisory offering. Naming the category creates the demand.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis: How Other Advisors Are Positioning AI Governance in Geneva

**Gap 1: Documentation coherence architecture not offered**
No Geneva advisor offers a service specifically designed to produce documentation that is coherent across multiple obligation types. The typical advisory output is a document that satisfies a specific request (carrier response, board briefing, counterparty response). The coherence problem — making documentation from different obligation cycles tell a consistent story — is not addressed.

**Gap 2: Maintenance protocol as discrete service absent from the market**
Maintenance is implied as part of ongoing engagement, not offered as a discrete bounded service. Executives resist ongoing engagement commitments because they distrust the dependency creation. A discrete maintenance service with clear triggers and bounded scope would address a real resistance point.

**Gap 3: Post-briefing update architecture not conceptualized**
No advisor has a named product for the situation: you received a written summary, conditions have changed, what do you do now? The absence is a product design gap. The question is present-tense for executives who have had a briefing and experienced a condition change afterward.

**Gap 4: Career-transition AI governance liability not addressed**
Advisors are not positioning for the senior professional who is joining a board or accepting a new executive role. The accountability attribution question — what are you responsible for when you inherit AI governance decisions you did not make — is not served by current advisory positioning.

**Gap 5: Peer silence intelligence interpretation not offered as service**
No Geneva advisor offers a service that reads the informal circuit on behalf of the executive — interpreting what peers are not saying, what the silence means in terms of exposure, and what patterns are forming below the surface of public communication. The intelligence function is implicit in some advisory practices but not named as a service.

**Gap 6: Documentation coherence verification not offered**
Executives who have documentation from multiple sources have no way to verify whether their documentation tells a coherent story. No Geneva advisor offers a coherence verification service — an assessment that reviews documentation produced across obligation cycles and identifies internal inconsistencies before they become external exposure.

## 04 — Trust Signal Audit: What Credibility, Process Clarity, Discretion, and Independence Signals This ICP Values Most

**Credibility signals that register:**

- Publication presence — not marketing content, but substantive analytical content that demonstrates working knowledge of Geneva-specific AI governance mechanisms
- The specific language of documentation adequacy inversion — the ability to explain the gap between what internal standards deem adequate and what market-formed standards require, without making the executive feel foolish for not having known
- First-conversation Geneva pattern recognition — the advisor's ability to demonstrate familiarity with the informal circuit without performing it, making the executive feel that the conversation is with someone who has been inside these conversations
- Named behavioral exclusions — independence described through what the practice does not do, not through what it claims to be

**Process clarity signals that register:**

- Bounded scope with explicit exit criteria — the executive wants to know when the engagement is complete, not discover that completion is indefinitely deferred
- Documentation coherence as a deliverable — not one document, but an integrated architecture that explains its own internal consistency
- Maintenance trigger specification — what condition change would prompt a review, what the review produces, who decides
- No disguised ongoing engagement — the maintenance question addressed through a discrete service, not as a foot-in-the-door for continuous billing

**Discretion signals that register:**

- Silence about the existence of the engagement, not just its content — the executive wants to be able to reference the advisory relationship to a peer without that reference traveling
- Geneva peer network behavior that does not create downstream exposure — the practice does not use the informal circuit to generate referrals in ways that would compromise the discretion of any individual client
- No published client list, no case studies, no testimonials — the practice's credibility comes from analytical content and process clarity, not from social proof

**Independence signals that register:**

- Behavioral exclusions named explicitly — what this practice will not do, for whom, and why those exclusions matter for the quality of the advice
- No vendor relationships, no platform affiliations, no carrier relationships that could affect advisory independence
- The maintenance question treated as a discrete bounded service, not as a mechanism for creating ongoing dependency — the executive reads the service architecture as a statement about independence

## 06 — Findings Summary: 6 Net-New Insights on DigitalCoa.ch Positioning

**Finding 1: Documentation Coherence Architecture as Discrete Advisory Product**
The documentation coherence problem — documentation produced across multiple obligation cycles that does not tell a coherent story — is a distinct advisory surface not currently served in the Geneva market. No advisor offers a coherence assessment as a discrete product. The gap is structural: documentation is produced to satisfy individual obligations, not designed to form an integrated record. DigitalCoa.ch can name and occupy this position with a discrete, bounded service.

**Finding 2: Post-Briefing Maintenance Protocol as Named Service Category**
The maintenance question — what happens to a written summary after conditions change — is an unnamed category creating real executive anxiety. Executives who have received a written summary lack a protocol for deciding whether that summary has aged into inadequacy. Naming the maintenance category as a discrete service resolves a real resistance point: executives who want bounded engagement without ongoing dependency can now access a specific maintenance service without committing to continuous advisory relationship.

**Finding 3: Documentation Accumulation Debt as Compound Exposure Distinct from Documentation Gap**
Geneva executives are accumulating AI governance documentation across carrier renewal, counterparty questionnaire, board briefing, and client due diligence cycles. This accumulation creates a compound exposure: not the individual document gap, but the coherence gap between documents that were never designed to be read together. The accumulation debt problem is more acute than the documentation gap problem because it compounds over time and is invisible until a specific moment makes it visible.

**Finding 4: Advisory Practice Architecture as Primary Trust Signal Before Analytical Content**
The architecture of the advisory service itself — how the service is designed, what it includes, what it explicitly does not include, how the maintenance question is handled — functions as the primary trust signal for Geneva executives evaluating whether to engage. The service architecture communicates independence before any analytical content is evaluated. Documentation coherence architecture designed as a bounded discrete service performs independence more credibly than any independence claim.

**Finding 5: Peer Silence as Intelligence Mechanism with Advisory Translation Function**
Geneva executives use peer silence as an information signal — the absence of a conversation about AI governance from a specific peer is read as information about that peer's exposure or uncertainty. Reading and interpreting the informal circuit's strategic silence is a distinct advisory capability. The intelligence translation function — making visible what is forming in the informal circuit — adds compounding value to the core documentation coherence service and creates a distinct positioning asset that cannot be replicated by advisors without Geneva embeddedness.

**Finding 6: Career-Transition AI Accountability Inheritance as Underserved Advisory Occasion**
Senior professionals transitioning between roles — joining a new board, accepting an executive position — face AI governance accountability for inherited decisions they did not make. The inheritance problem is distinct from incident-triggered, regulatory-triggered, or counterparty-triggered demand. It is a career-moment occasion with specific characteristics: the professional is not yet invested in the inherited documentation, has a window of opportunity to establish a coherent position before the decisions become entrenched, and faces personal liability for conditions they did not create. The career-transition occasion is the least served of all Geneva AI governance advisory occasions and the most natural entry point for a documentation coherence engagement.
