## 04 — Trust Signal Audit: What Signals Sergio's Practice Should Be Projecting and Why

The Geneva AI governance advisory market has moved. Twelve months ago, the primary trust problem was credibility — does this advisor know what they are talking about. ## 06 — Findings Summary: 6 Net-New Insights Distinct from C214–C270 Phase 06

**Finding 1: The Geneva three-speed accountability dynamic as a discrete structural problem distinct from regulatory timing mismatch**
Geneva principals are experiencing the simultaneous operation of three non-synchronized accountability streams: published regulatory doctrine (FINMA circulars, Federal Council deliberation, nFADP statutory obligations), carrier operative examination practice (third-generation questionnaire evolution, informal examination patterns, case-by-case underwriting decisions), and Geneva institutional ecosystem informal mechanisms (IO accountability requirements, diplomatic partner expectations, Geneva informal circuit outcomes). These three streams use overlapping vocabulary, move at different speeds, and produce different operative standards — and they are not synchronized with each other. **Finding 6: The Geneva three-speed problem as a moat mechanism that compounds through sustained ecosystem participation**
The three-speed accountability dynamic is not merely a demand signal — it is a moat mechanism.
