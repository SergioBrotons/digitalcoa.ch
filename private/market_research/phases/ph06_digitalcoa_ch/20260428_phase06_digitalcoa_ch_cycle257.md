## 02 — Market Signals

**What is working:**

**Carrier renewal calendar as structural demand-generation mechanism:**
The carrier renewal creates a dateable demand moment. Executives plan for the renewal. The planning generates demand for advisory that arrives predictably. The mechanism is structural, not incidental.

**Pre-event positioning as credibility-building timing strategy:**
Positioning before Geneva professional events is more effective than responding afterward. The pre-positioning creates credibility that post-event response cannot replicate. The timing preference is consistent and nameable.

**Brotons.net pre-calibration as prospect qualification mechanism:**
Prospects arriving with Brotons.net reading have already calibrated their expectations. The pre-calibration reduces the education burden in the first conversation. The qualification mechanism operates before contact.

**Written summary portability as document-performance-in-unintended-contexts:**
Written summaries travel to contexts the firm did not design for. The unintended context performance generates the next engagement through document-referral, not advisor-referral. The portability effect compounds over time.

**Geneva peer circuit structural isolation as demand-generation mechanism:**
The structural isolation that prevents open peer exchange generates the demand for external advisory. The silence is not just the absence of exchange; it is the condition that creates the need for the service. The mechanism operates continuously.

**What is not working:**

**Compliance framing for senior executives:**
Compliance framing positions the service as regulatory response. Senior executives who are beyond basic compliance questions do not recognize themselves in the framing. The positioning mismatch means the service is invisible to the executives who most need it.

**Generic methodology positioning without Geneva pattern knowledge:**
Positioning based on methodology claims without demonstrated Geneva pattern knowledge fails to differentiate. The positioning is indistinguishable from large firms making the same claims from outside Geneva.

**Pricing without positioning clarity:**
Pricing without clear positioning clarity creates ambiguity about what the engagement is for. The ambiguity operates against conversion even when the price is appropriate.

**International frameworks as primary hook for Geneva audience:**
International frameworks resonate less with Geneva executives than Geneva-specific problem framing. The Geneva dimension is the primary differentiator; international frameworks are secondary.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Big Four:**
Claim: full-spectrum AI governance advisory from global methodology.
Structural gap: scale mismatch for boutique Geneva firms; independence problem (existing client relationships create conflicts); Geneva pattern knowledge gap (global methodology does not include Geneva-specific formation); deliverable format mismatch (large-firm deliverables do not fit boutique decision-making context).

**Swiss domestic (Zurich/Basel):**
Claim: Swiss domestic regulatory expertise and multi-cantonal knowledge.
Structural gap: multi-regulatory pattern gap for Geneva-specific intersection of international org frameworks and Swiss domestic obligations; independence architecture (domestic firms often have institutional relationships that create conflicts); French-language authority gap for Geneva French-speaking executive segment.

**Geneva boutiques:**
Claim: Geneva embeddedness and client relationship depth.
Structural gap: cross-domain pattern gap (Geneva boutiques tend to be deep in one domain; AI governance advisory requires pattern knowledge across multiple domains simultaneously); scale capacity for comprehensive advisory; independence infrastructure (boutique relationships often create conflicts that prevent genuine independence).

**Legal firms:**
Claim: professional liability framing and bar-adjacent credibility.
Structural gap: multi-domain gap (legal framing addresses legal obligation, not the broader accountability architecture); personal framing mismatch (legal firm positioning is about the firm, not about the executive's personal liability and decision-making problem); Geneva advisory culture gap.

**Tech/cyber:**
Claim: technical AI expertise and implementation capability.
Structural gap: professional accountability framing mismatch (tech/cyber positioning is about AI systems, not about what the executive must demonstrate personally); Geneva community context gap (tech/cyber firms are not embedded in Geneva professional community); written summary register gap (tech/cyber deliverables do not match the register Geneva executives expect in advisory documents).

**Structural gap summary:**
No market participant occupies the intersection of:
- independent AI governance advisory (no conflicts from client relationships, vendor affiliations, or institutional ties)
- Geneva-specific pattern knowledge (not just regulatory knowledge, but the accumulated pattern of how governance questions form and resolve in Geneva)
- written summary product designed for the executive's actual use context
- personal accountability framing (addressing the executive's personal liability and decision-making problem)
- French-language capability for Geneva French-speaking executive segment

The intersection is unfilled. Sergio's practice is positioned in that intersection.

## 04 — Trust Signal Audit

**Signal 1: Geneva institutional embeddedness as first credential**
The first credential is not expertise claims — it is demonstrated embeddedness in the Geneva professional community. The embeddedness is demonstrated through specific knowledge of how things work in Geneva, not through credentials or methodology descriptions. The signal operates before any content is evaluated.

**Signal 2: Behavioral independence through exclusions (falsifiable in real time)**
The exclusion list is the primary trust architecture. The exclusions are specific behaviors the practice will not engage in. The exclusions are falsifiable in real time — any prospect can observe whether Sergio takes engagements that violate the exclusions. The falsifiability is the trust mechanism.

**Signal 3: Written summary reasoning quality as primary evidence**
The quality of the written summary is the primary evidence of the practice's reasoning quality. The summary must demonstrate genuine analytical depth, not just competent summary of existing frameworks. The evidence function of the summary operates before any direct engagement occurs.

**Signal 4: Advisory methodology visibility as precondition for trust**
Prospects need to understand how the advisory works before they can trust it. The methodology visibility is a precondition, not a selling point. The practice must be able to explain its approach clearly without becoming a methodology presentation.

**Signal 5: Accumulated engagement track record as structural moat**
Each engagement builds the accumulated pattern knowledge that makes subsequent engagements more valuable. The track record compounds. New entrants cannot replicate the accumulated moat without going through the same engagement history. The structural moat is time-bound.

**Signal 6: Peer circuit embeddedness as intelligence circuit, not marketing channel**
The practice's embeddedness in the Geneva peer circuit functions as an intelligence circuit, not as a marketing channel. The intelligence function is primary; the marketing function is incidental. Prospects sense the difference between a practice that genuinely participates in intelligence exchange and one that uses the circuit for visibility.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Carrier underwriting cycle as structural Geneva AI governance calendar, not merely triggering event**
The carrier underwriting cycle is not just a triggering event that creates demand. It is a structural calendar that shapes when and how governance questions surface in Geneva. The timing preference for advisory is calibrated to the carrier calendar, not to regulatory publication cycles. The calendar is a service design variable.

**Finding 2: Written summary portability as document-performance-in-unintended-contexts generating next engagement**
The written summary performs in contexts the firm did not design for. The unintended performance generates the next engagement through document-referral — a colleague sees the summary in a context the firm did not create and refers the next prospect. The document-referral mechanism is distinct from advisor-referral. It operates through organizational distribution of the document, not through personal recommendation.

**Finding 3: Evidentiary architecture as core product distinction from documentation completeness**
The core product is not documentation that satisfies completeness checks — it is an evidentiary architecture that will hold when examined by someone who knows what to look for. The distinction is structural: documentation completeness can be achieved without creating an evidentiary architecture that will hold. The product is defined by what it can withstand, not by what it contains.

**Finding 4: Multi-obligation sequencing as primary advisory surface for most sophisticated Geneva executive segment**
The most sophisticated executives are not confused by individual obligations — they are confused by the sequencing of multiple obligations that arrive simultaneously. The sequencing problem is the primary advisory surface. An advisory that addresses the individual obligations misses the actual problem the executive faces.

**Finding 5: Peer silence as structural isolation dynamic generating primary demand for external advisory**
The peer silence is not simply the absence of peer exchange. It is a structural isolation dynamic that generates the primary demand for external advisory. The isolation prevents the informal circuit from resolving the governance questions it surfaces. The demand that results from the isolation is more durable than demand that results from information asymmetry alone.

**Finding 6: Pre-positioning timing preference as primary conversion variable over methodology, credentials, and price**
The preference is for pre-crystallization positioning — engagement before the governance question has fully formed and external parties have begun examining it. The timing preference operates before methodology is evaluated, before credentials are assessed, and before price is discussed. The conversion variable is timing, not substance. The implication is that advisory positioning must reach executives before they are under active examination, not during or after.
