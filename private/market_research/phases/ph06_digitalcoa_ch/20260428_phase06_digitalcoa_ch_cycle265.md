## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Documentation window close as named urgency variable**
The mid-2026 closing date is producing concrete urgency. Executives understand that records not built now will not be reconstructable later. The named date is more motivating than abstract risk framing.

**Signal 2: Triple convergence creating multi-framework sequencing confusion**
FINMA, ISSB, and nFADP Article 21 converging mid-2027 without an integrated methodology. Executives who understand all three are converging are more motivated to act than those who only understand one.

**Signal 3: Carrier questionnaire as primary standard-formation mechanism**
The carrier questionnaire is forming the operative standard faster than published FINMA guidance. Executives who understand this are building response capability for carrier questions, not just FINMA compliance.

**Signal 4: Executive articulation latency as primary conversion barrier**
Executives know something is wrong but cannot articulate it precisely. The gap between recognition and articulation is where conversion fails. The latency is the primary conversion barrier.

**Signal 5: Geneva informal circuit knowledge as structural moat**
Sustained engagement with the Geneva market produces informal circuit knowledge that compounds over time. The moat is not replicable by external competitors who lack sustained market presence.

**Signal 6: Written summary portability generating secondary organic demand**
The written summary travels into contexts the briefing itself does not reach. Portability generates secondary organic demand through client relationships and peer networks.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Documentation window close as named urgency variable converting into engagement**
The mid-2026 closing date produces concrete urgency that abstract AI governance risk framing cannot. Executives understand they have a bounded window to build what they cannot build under examination conditions. The named date is the primary conversion accelerator.

**Finding 2: Triple convergence sequencing architecture as unified advisory product category**
FINMA, ISSB, and nFADP Article 21 converging mid-2027 without an integrated methodology. The sequencing architecture — managing all three frameworks simultaneously — is the advisory product category. No Geneva service has named it as such.

**Finding 3: Carrier questionnaire as de facto operative standard before published FINMA doctrine**
The carrier questionnaire is forming the actual standard faster than published FINMA guidance. The displacement means firms are being measured against a document they cannot read until they receive it. The advisory response is to build for carrier questions, not just FINMA compliance.

**Finding 4: Executive articulation latency as primary conversion barrier with named mechanism**
The gap between recognition and articulation is where conversion fails. The latency mechanism is now named: executives know something is wrong, cannot describe it precisely, and do not engage because they cannot articulate the ask. The mechanism has a name; the solution is specificity of problem description.

**Finding 5: Institutional depth as structural moat with compounding properties**
Sustained engagement with the Geneva market produces informal circuit knowledge that compounds over time. The moat is built through presence, not expertise. External competitors who lack sustained Geneva market presence cannot replicate it with expertise alone.

**Finding 6: Written summary portability as demand generation mechanism with secondary organic reach**
The written summary travels into contexts the briefing itself does not reach — board presentations, succession documents, client relationships, peer conversations. Portability generates secondary organic demand through the summary's circulation. The summary is designed for circulation, not just for the attendee.
