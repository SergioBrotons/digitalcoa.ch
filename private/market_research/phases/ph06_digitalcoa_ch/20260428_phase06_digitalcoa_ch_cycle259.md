## 02 — Market Signals

**What is working:**

**Pre-summit positioning window as distinct advisory timing occasion:**
The concentration of the UN Global Dialogue (July 6–7) and the AI for Good Global Summit (July 7–10) in a 72-hour window creates a specific pre-positioning occasion. Executives are beginning to recognize that positioning before the window closes is structurally different from responding afterward. The window generates its own demand logic, distinct from regulatory deadlines or carrier renewals.

**Geneva three-track disaggregation as primary client service occasion:**
The recognition that Geneva AI governance operates on diplomatic, regulatory, and commercial tracks simultaneously — with different obligations and different consequences — is creating a specific service occasion. Executives who understand the disaggregation seek advisors who can apply it to their specific situation. This is distinct from general AI governance advisory.

**Carrier renewal calendar as structural advisory anchor:**
The carrier renewal creates a predictable, dateable demand moment. Executives plan for the renewal. The planning generates advisory demand that arrives on a predictable schedule. The mechanism is structural, not incidental, and is becoming more acute as third-generation questionnaire substance increases.

**Brotons.net pre-calibration functioning as qualification mechanism:**
Prospects who have read Brotons.net arrive pre-calibrated. The education burden in the first conversation is reduced. The peer circuit has begun functioning as an informal qualification mechanism — executives who circulate Brotons.net content are demonstrating the sophistication that qualifies them for advisory engagement.

**Written summary portability generating document-referral:**
Written summaries travel to contexts the firm did not design for. Colleagues encounter the summary in contexts the firm did not create and refer the next prospect. The document-referral mechanism operates through organizational distribution of the document, not through personal recommendation. The effect compounds over time.

**What is not working:**

**Compliance framing for Geneva senior executives:**
Compliance framing positions the service as regulatory response. Senior Geneva executives who carry personal accountability do not recognize themselves in the framing. The framing signals the wrong level of sophistication and attracts the wrong prospects.

**Generic AI governance positioning without Geneva-specific context:**
Positioning based on methodology claims without demonstrated Geneva pattern knowledge fails to differentiate. The Geneva dimension is the primary differentiator. Without it, the positioning is indistinguishable from large firms making the same claims from outside the market.

**International frameworks as primary positioning hook:**
International frameworks — EU AI Act, ISO 42001, NIST — resonate less with Geneva executives than Geneva-specific problem framing. The Geneva-specific context is what makes the service relevant. International frameworks are relevant only insofar as they intersect with Geneva-specific obligations.

**Pricing without clear positioning justification:**
Pricing without positioning clarity creates ambiguity about what the engagement is for. The ambiguity operates against conversion even when the price is appropriate. The positioning must precede and justify the price.

## 04 — Trust Signal Audit

**Signal 1: Geneva institutional embeddedness as compound credential, not claimed expertise**

Demonstrated embeddedness in the Geneva professional community functions as the primary credential. The signal operates before any content is evaluated. The embeddedness is demonstrated through participation in informal circuits, through accumulated engagement history, through peer circuit intelligence — not through claims. Claims of expertise without demonstrated embeddedness do not carry the same weight. The moat compounds: new entrants cannot replicate embeddedness through investment alone.

**Signal 2: Behavioral exclusions as falsifiable trust architecture**

The exclusion list is the primary trust architecture because the exclusions are falsifiable in real time. Any prospect can observe whether Sergio takes engagements that violate the stated exclusions. The falsifiability is the trust mechanism — the practice is defined by what it will not do, and the prospect can verify whether the definition holds. This is distinct from claimed independence, which cannot be verified before engagement.

**Signal 3: Written summary reasoning quality as primary evidence**

The quality of the written summary is the primary evidence of the practice's reasoning quality. The summary must demonstrate genuine analytical depth: it must show how the specific situation connects to the applicable obligations, what the gaps are, what the priority sequence is, and what the timeline looks like. Competent summary of existing frameworks is insufficient. The summary must demonstrate that the practice has thought through the specific situation at a level the executive cannot replicate internally.

**Signal 4: Advisory methodology visibility as precondition for trust**

Prospects need to understand how the advisory works before they can evaluate whether to engage. The methodology is not a selling point — it is a precondition for trust. The pre-briefing document review request, the 90-minute format, the 48-hour written summary delivery, the fixed scope — these are not delivery logistics. They are trust signals that demonstrate the practice's priorities before the substantive conversation begins.

**Signal 5: Accumulated engagement pattern knowledge as structural moat**

Each engagement builds accumulated pattern knowledge that makes subsequent engagements more valuable. The pattern knowledge is cross-domain (legal, finance, consulting, NGO, international org) and cross-obligation (carrier, counterparty, FINMA, nFADP, board). The accumulation is the structural moat: new entrants cannot replicate it without going through the same engagement history. The practice's value increases not just through individual engagement quality but through the compounding of the pattern knowledge itself.

**Signal 6: Pre-summit positioning credibility as demonstrated judgment signal**

The ability to identify the July 2026 Geneva AI summit concentration as a specific accountability timing occasion — and to position advisory offerings accordingly — demonstrates judgment that is credible precisely because it is pre-crystallization. The signal is: this practice saw the occasion before it arrived and acted accordingly. That credibility transfers to the advisory content itself.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Geneva three-track disaggregation as discrete advisory occasion — not a framing concept, a service design category**

The disaggregation of Geneva AI governance into diplomatic, regulatory, and commercial tracks — identified in Brotons.net Phase 05 as an editorial function — is simultaneously a discrete advisory occasion. Executives who understand the disaggregation seek advisory that applies it to their specific situation: which track applies, in what sequence, with what consequences. The advisory occasion is distinct from general AI governance advisory. It requires accumulated pattern knowledge across all three tracks and the ability to produce written analysis in each register. No Geneva market participant has designed services for this occasion.

**Finding 2: July 2026 Geneva AI summit concentration as named advisory timing occasion distinct from all regulatory and carrier calendars**

The 72-hour concentration of the UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance (July 6–7) and the AI for Good Global Summit (July 7–10) creates a specific pre-positioning occasion. The occasion is distinct from carrier renewal calendars, FINMA publication cycles, and Federal Council deliberation timelines. It is a concentration event: accountability reference points from both summits will circulate through Geneva informal circuits within days, creating post-summit peer pressure that arrives before published doctrine. Executives who position before the window close have a structural advantage over those who respond afterward. The window is a discrete advisory design variable. No Geneva advisory has designed services around this occasion.

**Finding 3: Carrier third-generation questionnaire as primary Geneva AI governance standard-formation mechanism — operating before FINMA published doctrine**

The third-generation carrier questionnaire is functioning as the primary standard-formation mechanism in Geneva AI governance. The five evidence categories it probes are more operationally specific and more immediately consequential than FINMA Guidance 08/2024 or any published FINMA doctrine. The carrier renewal cycle is generating operative standards through commercial underwriting decisions — not through regulatory process. This means Geneva AI governance adequacy is being set by commercial insurance underwriters, not by regulatory authorities. Advisory demand that arrives through the carrier renewal cycle is arriving through this mechanism. Positioning for it requires understanding the carrier's five evidence categories as the actual operative standard, not as a proxy for regulatory adequacy.

**Finding 4: nFADP-FINMA collision as present-tense operational problem at boutique scale — not a theoretical regulatory intersection**

The nFADP-FINMA documentation collision is an active, present-tense operational friction at Geneva boutique scale. Compliance officers are managing the conflict daily: documentation that satisfies FINMA suitability requirements simultaneously violates nFADP data minimization obligations, and documentation that satisfies nFADP Art. 21 explanation requirements creates FINMA disclosure exposure. No Geneva market participant has published a reconciliation methodology for this specific operational intersection. The exposure is not theoretical. It is compounding as more AI-assisted processes run simultaneously under both frameworks. Advisory demand for this specific intersection is underserved and structurally distinct from individual framework coverage.

**Finding 5: GAIGI certification pathway as unmapped competitive variable and advisory occasion — creating two-tier market without market map**

The Geneva AI Governance Institute certification pathway is creating a two-tier market structure for Geneva AI governance advisory that no market participant has mapped. The relationship between GAIGI certification and carrier third-generation questionnaire adequacy, between GAIGI certification and FINMA examination expectations, and between GAIGI certification and client counterparty questionnaire responses is undefined. Geneva international organization clients and EU-connected clients are beginning to reference GAIGI as a credential condition without published clarity on what it establishes. The advisory occasion is: how to sequence GAIGI certification relative to carrier renewal, board accountability obligations, and client counterparty requirements. No Geneva market participant is addressing this sequencing question.

**Finding 6: Documentation window 2023–mid-2026 as bounded evidentiary record hardening into non-reconstructable historical state**

The 2023 through mid-2026 period is a bounded documentation archive that is hardening into a historical state that cannot be retroactively reconstructed. The closure mechanism is not regulatory determination — it is the combination of model version evolution, staff departures, vendor relationship changes, and tool configuration drift that makes retrospective reconstruction increasingly impossible as time passes. The Federal Council June 2026 deliberation will mark the formal boundary: whatever documentation does not exist at that point will not be reconstructable. Advisory demand generated by this closing window is distinct from general retrospective advisory: it is demand for documentation of what was done, in what configuration, with what model version, at what time — and for a determination of what that historical record demonstrates. This is a discrete, named, time-bounded advisory occasion with no Geneva market service addressing it.
