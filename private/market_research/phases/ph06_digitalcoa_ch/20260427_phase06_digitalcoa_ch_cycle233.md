## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Vendor exit creating documentation for systems that no longer exist in documented form**
AI vendors are being acquired, sunset, and consolidated. The documentation for the systems that existed before the exit is gone. The firm is accountable for outputs from systems it can no longer describe. The vendor exit problem is a retrospective exposure that has no forward solution.

**Signal 2: Lateral talent movement absorbing undocumented AI governance histories**
New hires arrive with AI practices formed at prior firms. Those practices were never documented. The hiring firm inherits undocumented AI governance. The integration problem is acute and unaddressed.

**Signal 3: Third-generation carrier underwriting probing early-adoption-period specifically**
Carriers have moved beyond framework documentation to methodology evidence. The third-generation questionnaire probes specifically what the firm did during the 2023 to mid-2024 early adoption period. The early adopters are now being asked to document the undocumented past.

**Signal 4: Board-level AI risk accountability without evaluative literacy**
Boards are accountable for AI risk oversight. They do not have the evaluative literacy to fulfill this oversight. The accountability and the capability are structurally disconnected.

**Signal 5: Annual renewal as structured occasion for bounded advisory**
The annual vendor renewal creates a specific, predictable occasion for bounded advisory. The firm has a defined scope and a defined question. This is the most actionable advisory occasion in the calendar.

**Signal 6: Geneva silence paradox as market information failure**
The firms that most understand AI governance are most silent publicly. The market is forming standards without the input of the firms best positioned to contribute. The information failure is structural.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**What law firms (VISCHER, CMS, Baker McKenzie) are doing:**
Publishing regulatory orientation and framework documentation. Addressing the policy layer. Missing the operational accountability questions that the policy layer does not resolve.

**What Big Four are doing:**
Publishing forward-looking frameworks. Designing end-state governance architectures. Missing the retrospective problems — vendor exits, lateral hires, early-adoption-period documentation — that are the acute exposure right now.

**What boutique consultancies are doing:**
Service-oriented content. Missing the Geneva silence paradox. The firms most capable of contributing to market standard formation are the least visible publicly.

**What platforms are doing:**
Monitoring and compliance tools. Addressing the wrong level. Platforms can track what exists in the documented environment. They cannot reconstruct what existed before documentation began.

**WhatBrotest.net should own:**

**Six specific gaps no one addresses:**

3. Early-adoption-period period boundary documentation — documenting the undocumented past
4. Board AI literacy briefing — specific to Geneva institutional context

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Vendor exit documentation archaeology as distinct retrospective service**
The firm that used an AI vendor that has since been acquired, sunset, or consolidated faces a specific problem: it is accountable for outputs from systems it can no longer describe. The documentation archaeology required to reconstruct this history is a distinct service that no one is offering.

**Finding 2: Lateral integration protocol as specific unmanaged exposure**
New hires with AI practices formed at prior firms carry undocumented AI governance into the hiring firm. The firm inherits this exposure without a protocol for managing it. The lateral integration protocol is specific, nameable, and missing.

**Finding 3: Geneva silence paradox has a specific cultural mechanism**
Geneva's professional culture operates through discrete relationships rather than publication. The firms most capable of contributing to market standard formation are most silent publicly — not from competitive secrecy, but from cultural habit. The mechanism is specific and addressable through a different editorial approach.

**Finding 4: Carrier underwriting has entered third-generation probing early-adoption-period**
The carrier questionnaire has evolved beyond framework documentation to methodology evidence. The third-generation questionnaire probes specifically what the firm did during the 2023 to mid-2024 early adoption period. The early adopters are being asked to document the undocumented past. This is a structural market pressure that will not recede.

**Finding 5: Market self-sorting into tiers through vendor consolidation and lateral hiring**
The market is sorting itself into tiers through three simultaneous mechanisms: vendor consolidation (firms that used acquired/sunset vendors carry documentation debt), lateral hiring (firms that have hired from firms with undocumented AI practices inherit that exposure), and carrier underwriting (carriers are using questionnaire responses to tier the market). The sorting is happening now, without guidance.

**Finding 6: Three-part remediation architecture as the coherent response**
The vendor exit documentation archaeology, the lateral integration protocol, and the early-adoption-period period boundary documentation form a coherent three-part architecture. Each addresses a specific retrospective exposure that the other two do not. Together they constitute a complete retrospective governance assessment methodology.
