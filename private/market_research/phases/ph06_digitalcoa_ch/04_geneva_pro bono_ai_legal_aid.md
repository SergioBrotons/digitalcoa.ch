# 04 — DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Capacity Constraint: Real Scaling Limit and Premium Pricing Logic
**Phase:** C204 Phase 06 (DigitalCoa.ch) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
DigitalCoa.ch's primary scaling constraint is advisory capacity — not demand, not lead quality, not pricing. Geneva executive AI governance advisory is relationship-intensive, requires sustained attention to Geneva institutional developments, and the advisor bandwidth is finite. Premium pricing is not a pricing strategy; it is a demand management mechanism that aligns client acquisition rate with advisory capacity. The constraint is structural: executive advisory quality degrades if volume increases beyond sustainable load.

## Evidence
- Executive AI Governance Audit: 4-6 weeks from initial inquiry to completed audit; briefing-to-mandate conversion adds 6-8 weeks
- Geneva executive clients expect responsive advisor access — advisory capacity directly limits concurrent client load
- Warm lead volume (from Brotons.net pathway) currently exceeds advisory capacity — demand constraint, not pipeline constraint
- Premium pricing (CHF 15K+ for audits, CHF 50K+ for mandates) filters demand to sustainable volume
- Zurich/Basel Swiss German expansion adds geographic diversity but doesn't solve capacity constraint
- AI Regulatory Intelligence subscription as partial leverage: lower touch, recurring revenue that scales better than mandate model

## Advisory Implication
DigitalCoa.ch should embrace capacity constraint as strategic positioning — scarcity of advisory attention is a premium signal, not a business limitation. Invest in AI Regulatory Intelligence subscription as capacity-multiplier. Zurich/Basel and international expansion should be selective, not aggressive. Maintain premium pricing discipline.

## Confidence
High — demand/capacity analysis, pricing evidence
