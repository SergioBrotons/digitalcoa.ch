## 02 — Market Signals: What Is and Is Not Working in Advisory Positioning for AI Governance in Geneva

**What is working:**

**Signal 1: The Geneva multi-venue portability problem as unmet demand**
Geneva principals are arriving at advisory conversations with a specific and articulate problem that no market participant has addressed directly: their documented positions were built for one accountability venue — typically carrier renewal — but are now being encountered in other venues with different evidentiary standards and different audience registers. The board is asking whether the executive personally knows what the AI system is doing. The carrier is asking whether documentation exists. The counterparty is asking what methodology the firm uses. These are not the same question. They require different document architectures. No Geneva market service has addressed the specific problem of multi-venue portability — the requirement that a single documented position holds coherent meaning across carrier examination, board presentation, and counterparty questionnaire contexts simultaneously. This is the problem sophisticated principals are carrying, and it is not being named.

**Signal 2: The retrospective reconstruction problem as present-tense executive liability**
The documentation built in 2023 and 2024 — against standards that have since changed, using tools whose terms have since mutated, by staff who have since departed — is now being encountered in its current form by executives who must defend it. The retrospective reconstruction problem is not a documentation quality issue. It is a present-tense personal liability issue: the executive who signed the attestation, approved the position, presented to the board, or responded to the counterparty questionnaire is personally accountable for a documented position whose original conditions no longer exist. The market has not yet produced a service that addresses the retrospective problem as a present-tense executive liability management occasion, distinct from documentation improvement and compliance check categories.

**Signal 3: The written summary multi-generational travel as emerging executive awareness**
Executives who have had advisory written summaries encountered by successors, board members, and external parties — in contexts the original author did not design for — are developing articulate awareness of the summary as a document with specific generational properties. The summary written during one MD's tenure becomes an asset the successor inherits. It becomes a representation the board reviews. It becomes a document external parties request. Each generation of readers brings different interests and different interpretive frameworks. The executives who have experienced this are describing it in terms that suggest a discrete document design problem: the summary needs to be designed for generational travel, not just for the original executive's use.

**What is not working:**

**Signal 4: Generic AI governance positioning failing to communicate the specific multi-venue problem**
Stating that the practice does "AI governance advisory" or even "Geneva AI governance advisory" is functioning as a noise signal — it does not communicate the specific nature of the problem the practice is designed to address. Principals who carry the multi-venue portability problem are looking for language that demonstrates the practice understands what the problem actually looks like in the room: what the carrier asks that the board does not, what the counterparty requires that the carrier does not, and what the executive must personally attest to that the documentation cannot cover. The generic positioning does not provide this. It communicates that the practice exists in the category, not that it understands the specific shape of the problem.

**Signal 5: The pre-engagement positioning occasion unrecognized and unserved**
The moment before a Geneva principal formally engages an advisor — when they are determining what they are walking into, what the accountability landscape actually looks like across venues, and how their current documented position will hold under the next three accountability occasions — is a discrete advisory occasion that no Geneva market service has named or positioned for. The principal needs pre-engagement positioning intelligence: what does the actual accountability situation look like, what will each venue ask, what are the specific vulnerabilities in the current documented position under each venue's standard. This is not an initial advisory briefing. It is not a framework presentation. It is a pre-engagement intelligence calibration, and it is unserved.

**Signal 6: Geneva institutional ecosystem pattern intelligence treated as context rather than as the core product**
The practice's positioning treats Geneva institutional ecosystem pattern knowledge as background context — as credential evidence that the practice has been in the room. The sophisticated principal who carries the multi-venue problem is looking for something different: they are looking for evidence that the practice knows what the informal circuit revealed in the last three cycles, what the carrier actually penalized versus what the form asked, what the board actually cared about in the last AI governance presentation they reviewed. This intelligence is the core product. When it is positioned as context, the principal cannot verify fit before engaging. When it is positioned as the product — specific, dateable, verifiable pattern references — it performs both the credential and the fit-assessment functions simultaneously.

## 06 — Findings Summary: 6 Net-New Insights Distinct from C214–C271 Phase 06

**Finding 1: The Geneva multi-venue portability problem as a discrete document design problem distinct from three-speed synchronization**
Geneva principals are experiencing a specific accountability challenge that no published framework or advisory positioning has named: documented positions built for one accountability venue are being encountered in other venues with different evidentiary standards, different audience registers, and different questions. Carrier examination asks whether documentation exists and reflects actual practice. Board presentation asks whether the executive personally knows what the system is doing. Counterparty questionnaire asks what methodology the firm uses. These are not the same question. A single documented position must hold coherent meaning across all three simultaneously — and the documents most Geneva principals hold were built for one venue and encountered in the others. This is structurally distinct from the three-speed synchronization finding in C271 Phase 06 (three non-synchronized accountability streams moving at different speeds). The three-speed finding names three streams with different velocities. This finding names the specific document design problem created when a single document must travel across venues whose evidentiary standards and audience registers are incompatible: the document that satisfies the carrier fails the board, and the board-position document fails the counterparty. No Geneva market service has addressed multi-venue portability as a discrete document architecture problem.

**Finding 2: The retrospective reconstruction gap as present-tense personal executive liability distinct from documentation quality failure**
The documentation built in 2023 and 2024 — against standards that have since changed, using AI tools whose terms have since mutated, by staff who have since departed — is now being encountered by Geneva principals who must defend it under current operative standards. The retrospective reconstruction gap is not a documentation quality problem. It is a present-tense personal executive liability: the executive who signed the attestation, approved the board presentation, or responded to the counterparty questionnaire is personally accountable for a documented position whose original conditions no longer exist. The executive cannot reconstruct the original conditions. The executive also cannot uninvent the decisions that were made. This creates a specific present-tense liability that compliance documentation improvement does not address — because the liability exists regardless of whether new documentation is built. This finding is distinct from C270 Phase 06 Finding 4 (written summary aging requiring reassessment pathway architecture) — that finding addressed document aging as a product design variable. This finding names the specific mechanism by which documentation built under changed conditions creates present-tense personal executive liability that is not remedied by new documentation.

**Finding 3: The Geneva institutional ecosystem three-venue feedback loop as a discrete standard-formation mechanism distinct from three-speed disaggregation**
Geneva accountability standards are forming through a specific three-venue feedback loop that is not visible from outside sustained participation: carrier examination patterns in Geneva produce informal intelligence that circulates through the Geneva institutional ecosystem before any publication; that informal intelligence influences what institutional clients and counterparties require from their advisors and partners; those requirements feed back into carrier examination adjustments before regulatory doctrine catches up; and the regulatory doctrine changes produce new carrier examination adjustments. The loop is: carrier examination → Geneva informal circuit intelligence → institutional counterparty requirements → carrier adjustment → regulatory doctrine → carrier examination adjustment. This three-venue feedback loop is distinct from the three-speed disaggregation (C271 Phase 05: three tracks with different speeds and non-synchronization). The three-speed finding disaggregates three parallel streams. This finding names the specific feedback mechanism through which the three venues interact and compound: carrier examination patterns produce intelligence that is not published but that circulates through the Geneva institutional ecosystem and re-enters carrier examination as adjusted standards. No Geneva market participant has mapped the three-venue feedback loop as a discrete intelligence circulation system. Pattern knowledge about how the loop operates at the current moment is the core product of sustained Geneva ecosystem participation.

**Finding 4: The written summary multi-generational travel as a named document design problem distinct from document aging**
The written summary is emerging as a multi-generational liability artifact: it travels across MD tenure, successor inheritance, board review, and external party request — each generation of readers bringing different interests, different interpretive frameworks, and different evidentiary expectations. The document written during one MD's tenure becomes a representation the successor must defend, a record the board reviews, and a document external parties request. The genre requirements are defined by the most demanding unintended audience, not by the original authoring context. This is distinct from document aging (C270 Phase 06 Finding 4: written summary aging requiring reassessment pathway architecture) which addressed temporal aging of a fixed document. This finding addresses the specific structural requirements created by generational audience diversity: what the document must include to protect itself from successor misinterpretation, what it must exclude to avoid creating carrier examination evidentiary exposure, and what register it must maintain for board credibility. The multi-generational design requirement is a present-tense document architecture problem, not a future reassessment occasion.
