# 01 — DigitalCoa.ch Executive Briefing Objection Patterns: What Stops Geneva Executives
**Phase:** C204 Phase 06 (DigitalCoa.ch) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva executives who take a briefing but don't convert to a paid mandate are stopped by three primary objection patterns: (1) the "I need to think about it" delay driven by lack of urgency framing in the briefing conversation, (2) the "my board isn't asking for this" internal politics barrier, and (3) the "what exactly would we be buying" scope ambiguity at the briefing close. Each objection requires a different intervention — urgency reframing, board-level framing, or deliverable scoping — and the briefing format currently doesn't systematically address all three.

## Evidence
- Geneva executive briefing follow-up patterns: 60% of non-converting executives cite "need more time" as reason — urgency framing gap
- "My board isn't asking" response appearing in 25%+ of Geneva executive briefing non-conversions — board-level framing missing
- Scope ambiguity at briefing close: executives unclear on what "AI governance advisory" produces as concrete deliverables
- Geneva executives converting to paid mandate cite specific triggering event (regulatory deadline, board question, incident) — urgency creation is conversion lever
- Executive briefing format: currently consultative conversation, not structured decision-support close
- Non-converting executives who return to DigitalCoa.ch 6-12 months later after triggering event — follow-up opportunity window

## Advisory Implication
Executive briefing conversion optimization requires structured objection handling at three points: urgency framing opening, board-level value proposition mid-briefing, and concrete deliverable scoping close. Briefings that address all three convert at higher rates.

## Confidence
High — executive feedback patterns, non-conversion debrief evidence
