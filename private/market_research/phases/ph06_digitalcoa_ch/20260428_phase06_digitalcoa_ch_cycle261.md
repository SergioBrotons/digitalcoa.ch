### English

**Prospective client on what they are actually looking for:**
"I do not need another framework. I need to understand what the carrier is actually going to ask, what FINMA is actually going to examine, and what I can actually document before the examination window opens. Everything else is noise."

**On the problem with generic AI governance advice:**
"I have spoken to three advisors in the last year. Two of them gave me general frameworks. One of them gave me a document that looked like it was written for a different type of firm. None of them addressed what I actually need to do in the next six months. That is the specific thing I am looking for."

**On what "getting AI governance in order" actually means:**
"When I say I need to get my AI governance in order, I mean I need to be able to show a documented position that explains what AI tools I use, how I use them, what decisions AI assists versus humans make, and what the documentation for each looks like. I do not mean a policy document that says I have a governance framework. I mean the actual evidence that I do."

**On the reassessment problem:**
"Things keep changing. The AI tool vendor updated the system in January. A staff member left in February. A new client came in with specific AI governance requirements in March. Each of these is a reason to revisit the documented position. But there is no protocol for when and how to do that. The governance I have is the governance I built two years ago, which no longer reflects the current state."

**On what trust looks like before the engagement:**
"Before I engage with someone on AI governance, I want to see that they understand what I actually do — not that they have a general framework for AI governance. The trust signal is specificity, not generality. If they cannot describe my problem back to me with precision, I do not believe they can solve it."

## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Geneva executives want documented positions, not policy documents**
The language executives use is "documented position" — evidence of actual governance practice, not a document asserting that a framework exists. The distinction is critical and no advisor is making it explicitly.

**Signal 2: Generic frameworks are trust destroyers for sophisticated Geneva ICPs**
Executives have seen multiple general frameworks that do not address their specific situation. Each general framework received reduces trust in the next advisor. Specificity is the trust restoration mechanism.

**Signal 3: Material change events create reassessment occasions with no protocol**
AI tool updates, staff departures, new client onboarding with specific requirements — each creates a reason to revisit documented governance. No protocol exists for conducting the review. The documented position drifts from reality.

**Signal 4: The examination window is the actual urgency variable**
The bounded Q1 2027 FINMA examination window is producing real urgency that generic "AI governance risk" framing does not create. Executives respond to named dates differently than abstract risks.

**Signal 5: Trust signal is precision of problem description before solution proposal**
Sophisticated Geneva executives assess advisor capability by whether the advisor can describe the client's problem back with precision. If the description is accurate, the client trusts the solution capability.

**Signal 6: The documentation window is closing**
The mid-2026 closing of the 2023-mid-2026 documentation window is producing concrete urgency. Executives understand that records that are not built now will not be reconstructable later. The closing date is a trust signal about urgency.

## 04 — Trust Signal Audit

**For Sergio's practice:**

1. **Specificity of problem description as primary trust signal:** The practice should demonstrate understanding of the client's specific configuration before proposing solutions. This means initial communications that describe the client's problem back with precision, before any engagement.

2. **Documented position language as practice vocabulary:** The practice operates in "documented position" language, not "framework" language. This vocabulary distinction signals that the practice understands what Geneva examination actually requires.

3. **Reassessment pathway as evidence of durability:** A named reassessment pathway for material change events signals that the documented position is designed to be maintained over time, not constructed once and archived.

4. **Format signals as values qualification:** 90-minute structure, pre-briefing review, 48-hour written delivery, fixed scope — these communicate what the practice values before substantive content is assessed. They are trust signals, not delivery logistics.

5. **Bilingual depth as Geneva specificity signal:** French and English produced with native-quality depth signals that the practice understands Geneva's specific linguistic and professional environment. Translation-equivalent documents are not sufficient.

6. **Lead magnet specificity as conversion signal:** The lead magnet that names the specific documentation problem and delivers the specific protocol is the conversion mechanism. Generic lead magnets do not convert sophisticated Geneva executives.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Documented position demand versus policy document supply as conversion gap**
Geneva executives are asking for "documented positions" — evidence of actual governance practice. Most advisors supply policy documents asserting that frameworks exist. The gap between demand and supply is the conversion opportunity. Advisors who shift vocabulary from "framework" to "documented position" will convert more sophisticated prospects.

**Finding 2: Reassessment trigger absence as structural governance drift mechanism**
No Geneva advisory has produced a protocol for reassessing documented governance after material change events. The documented position drifts from current state until the next examination reveals the gap. The reassessment trigger is an unnamed product category with high demand and no supply.

**Finding 3: Q1 2027 examination window as bounded trust signal converting urgency into engagement**
The named Q1 2027 examination window produces trust signal effects that abstract "AI governance risk" framing cannot. Executives understand that they have a bounded window to build a documented position they cannot build under examination conditions. The trust signal converts urgency into engagement.

**Finding 4: Specificity of problem description as primary advisor capability assessment criterion**
Sophisticated Geneva executives assess advisor capability by whether the advisor can describe the client's specific problem with precision before proposing solutions. The capability signal is problem description accuracy, not solution claim quality. Advisors who demonstrate specificity in initial communications convert at higher rates.

**Finding 5: Bilingual depth as Geneva-specific credibility signal for advisory practice**
Producing French and English content at native depth signals Geneva-specific credibility that translation-equivalent documents cannot achieve. The bilingual depth signal is particularly important for the French-language market where English-language AI governance content dominates.

**Finding 6: Mid-2026 documentation window close as named urgency variable for DigitalCoa.ch positioning**
The mid-2026 closing of the 2023-mid-2026 documentation window is a named urgency variable that should anchor DigitalCoa.ch positioning. Executives respond to named dates with concrete action motivation that abstract risk framing cannot produce. The closing date is the conversion accelerator.
