### What Is Working

**Structural accountability framing.** The framing that lands is "accountability architecture problem" — not AI governance as technology compliance, not Swiss regulatory update as news item, but the specific structural condition where documentation built under prior conditions must now hold under changed standards, across multiple venues, simultaneously. This framing describes what the ICP experiences but has not named. The articulation lands because it is precise enough to feel like it was written from inside the problem, not observed from outside.

**Prospect pre-qualification through documented specificity.** The market signal confirmed by prospect behavior: executives who arrive at first contact having already processed content that named their exact accountability situation are not information-gathering. They are confirming a prior assessment — whether this practice operates at the level their situation requires. The specificity of named problems is the qualification signal. A practice that can describe the structural accountability architecture problem in its published content has demonstrated participation in the conditions that produce it.

**Multi-venue documentation coherence as named structural problem.** "Documentation built for one venue does not hold across others" lands because it describes what the ICP has experienced without having named it. Carrier examiner asks for evidence the board presentation does not produce. Institutional counterparty asks for methodology language the prior documentation does not contain. The multi-venue coherence problem is structural, not a documentation quality problem — and no Geneva service has named it as such.

**Documented decline decisions as positioning integrity evidence.** Executives who have been through advisory relationships that over-promised recognize documented refusal decisions as evidence of a genuine standard. A practice that can demonstrate cases where it declined — with specific reasoning — is demonstrating a defined analytical framework, not just a claimed positioning. The documented decision trail is an observable behavioral record from which positioning can be inferred.

**Geneva ecosystem participation observable through reference specificity.** The ICP who has operated in Geneva long enough recognizes whether a reference describes Geneva accountability dynamics from participation or from research. Carrier iteration language, institutional counterparty vocabulary, Geneva informal circuit reference density — these are observable evidence of sustained participation in the ecosystem where accountability standards actually form.

### What Is Not Working

**Generic "AI governance advisory" positioning.** Every international firm, every Big Four affiliate, every Swiss domestic player uses some version of it. The ICP has learned to discount it. Generic positioning produces general inquiries — exploration rather than action on a specific accountability occasion. Low conversion rates, high time-per-inquiry cost, no durable engagement conditions.

**Compliance-advisory conflation.** Positioning AI governance advisory as a compliance function attracts the wrong inquiry profile: firms looking for a vendor to produce documentation satisfying a specific requirement. This framing attracts price competition and short-engagement inquiries. It does not create conditions for the multi-year advisory relationship the ICP's actual accountability exposure requires.

**Per-engagement pricing without relationship architecture.** AI governance accountability is not a one-time documentation project. The firm facing a carrier renewal today will face a modified version of the same question in eighteen months — and the documented position from today will be part of what they defend then. Per-engagement pricing without retainer architecture fails the ICP's actual need and fails to capture the relationship value that compounds over accountability occasions.

**Absence of Geneva AI governance advisory genre conventions.** The market has not established "Geneva AI governance advisory briefing" as a named format with conventions the ICP recognizes. Quality assessment is harder for the prospect evaluating advisory services, and positioning is harder for the practice that could establish those conventions. The first practice to establish the genre conventions captures a trust formation advantage that content quality alone cannot produce.

**Published content volume without demonstrated intelligence architecture.** Content volume without evidence of systematic intelligence tracking — carrier iteration monitoring, Geneva event observation, informal circuit pattern recognition — signals coverage without depth. The ICP who has watched accountability standards form and shift knows the difference between published output and sustained intelligence architecture.

**Finding 01 — Advisory practice as structural bridge in the Geneva accountability information economy, not merely a participant in it.**

The Geneva accountability information economy has a specific structural property: the most operationally relevant intelligence — carrier questionnaire evolution signals, informal circuit standard formation, Geneva event intelligence — circulates through channels that cannot be published. The most exposed principals have the least access to this intelligence because the channels are not publication-eligible, and the principals most exposed have least time for informal circuit participation.

The advisory practice with sustained Geneva ecosystem participation does not just have access to this intelligence. It performs a specific structural function: it translates intelligence from channels that cannot be published into positions that can be acted upon. This translation function is not an editorial strategy. It is a structural position defined by the architecture of the Geneva accountability information economy itself.

The trust formation mechanism: the ICP recognizes that the practice has access to intelligence they cannot obtain from published sources — because the practice named the specific accountability situation they are in before they articulated it themselves. That capacity to name the situation with precision is evidence of participation in channels that cannot be published. The practice is not describing Geneva accountability dynamics from outside observation; it is operating within the economy that produces them.

This finding is distinct from C277 Phase 06 Finding 04 (Geneva accountability information economy structural publication gap) which named the gap mechanism without isolating the practice's specific structural function as a bridge within that economy. This finding identifies the advisory practice as a structural position within the information economy — not a participant who happens to have access, but a mechanism performing a function the economy requires and that cannot be performed through publication.
