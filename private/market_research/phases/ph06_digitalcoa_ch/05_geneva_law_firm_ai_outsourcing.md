## Finding
DigitalCoa.ch's Zurich/Basel Swiss German market entry is in the briefing stage but faces a distinct challenge: Zurich executives discover DigitalCoa.ch through Brotons.net/LinkedIn organically, take a briefing, but the Geneva institutional framing of the briefing doesn't fully resonate with Zurich context. Zurich executives operate in a different institutional frame (FINMA rather than FDPIC, cantonal rather than federal, German-speaking corporate culture rather than French-speaking Geneva). The briefing content needs Swiss German market adaptation to convert Zurich executives to mandates.

- 3 Zurich executive briefings completed Q1 2026 — all discovered via Brotons.net/LinkedIn, none converted to mandate yet
- Zurich executive context: FINMA regulatory frame, Basel Committee influence, Swiss German corporate governance culture
- Geneva framing in DigitalCoa.ch materials resonates with Geneva executives but feels "foreign" to Zurich executives
- Zurich executive decision-making: board-level German Swiss corporate governance style, more formal than Geneva
- DigitalCoa.ch Geneva institutional intelligence less effective in Zurich context — need Zurich institutional context development
- Zurich executives who read Brotons.net and take briefing but don't convert: specific Zurich adaptation needed

## Advisory Implication
Zurich market adaptation requires separate briefing framing: FINMA-centric rather than FDPIC-centric, Basel Committee rather than Geneva courts, German Swiss corporate governance style. Zurich institutional intelligence needs development as distinct capability. The briefing-to-mandate conversion gap in Zurich is a framing problem, not a trust problem.
