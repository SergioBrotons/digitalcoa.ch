They use the same vocabulary but mean different things. I need someone who can disaggregate them and show me which conversation applies to my specific situation."

## 02 — Market Signals: What Is and Is Not Working in Advisory Positioning for AI Governance in Geneva

**Signal 1: Binding/non-binding structural ambiguity is the primary advisory entry point now**
Geneva principals have reached the limit of general AI governance awareness as a demand driver. The question that is actually concentrating their minds is not "should I have a policy" — it is "which parts of what I am building are actually binding and which are not." The legally binding consultation draft and non-binding implementation plan operating in parallel without defined structural relationship is producing a decision paralysis that generic advisory positioning cannot resolve. This is the entry point that is working.

**Signal 2: Federal AI Coordination Office institutional mapping is unmet demand**
The February 1, 2026 establishment of the Federal AI Coordination Office as the contact point within the Federal Chancellery created a named institutional accountability actor. No Geneva advisory or publication has mapped how this actor relates to FINMA supervision, cantonal level obligations, or AI Convention accountability flows. The mapping is a precondition for any actionable advisory — principals cannot determine their obligations without it. This demand is present and unaddressed.

**Signal 3: July 2026 Geneva event concentration is producing a specific accountability preparation window**
The 72-hour concentration of UN Global Dialogue (July 6–7) and AI for Good Global Summit (July 7–10) is functioning as a pre-consultation-draft accountability moment. Geneva institutional players — including Geneva principals serving IO and diplomatic clients — are being asked to demonstrate governance adequacy before formal standards exist. This window is six months before the Federal Council AI regulation bill arrives. The positioning for this window is the specific occasion that is working.

**Signal 4: Geneva three-track conversation disaggregation is a distinct advisory service category**
The Swiss domestic regulatory conversation, the international Geneva diplomatic conversation, and the Geneva IO ecosystem conversation operate with overlapping vocabulary but different operational content and different consequences. No Geneva advisory has claimed disaggregation as a discrete service category. The demand is present: principals serving multiple client types need to know which track applies to which obligation.

**Signal 5: Carrier underwriting as primary standard-formation mechanism is not visible in advisory positioning**
The third-generation carrier questionnaire continues to be more operationally specific than any published regulatory doctrine. Each questionnaire iteration is evidence of operative standard evolution. No Geneva advisory is positioning carrier examination pattern intelligence as a primary service element. This is a structural gap in how the market is communicating what the actual standard is.

**Signal 6: AI Convention public-private accountability boundary is not integrated into advisory positioning**
The AI Convention obligations bind public authorities directly and flow into the private sector through private law mechanisms — client contracts, professional liability standard of care, carrier underwriting. This structural consequence of the Convention is not integrated into advisory positioning. Principals are being told about the regulatory implications of the AI Convention but not about the private law accountability flows that are the actual enforcement mechanisms.

**What is not working:**
- General AI governance frameworks with no Geneva-specific calibration
- Regulatory anticipation positioning without binding/non-binding structural clarity
- Published FINMA doctrine positioning when the operative standard is set through carrier underwriting
- Advisory positioning that treats Geneva as a single conversation rather than a three-track environment
- Documentation quality positioning when the primary problem is architectural coherence
