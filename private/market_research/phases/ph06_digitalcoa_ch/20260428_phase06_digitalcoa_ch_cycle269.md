## 02 — Market Signals

**What is working:**

**Briefing format as trust infrastructure.** The 90-minute briefing format is performing a trust function that content alone cannot. Clients arrive with unresolved questions and leave with a documented position. The format is the product, not the time.

**Written summary as portable accountability artifact.** Clients use the written summary in contexts the practice did not design for — board presentations, carrier discussions, client communications. The portability of the summary is a feature, not a risk. Design for portability.

**Pre-briefing qualification as mutual commitment mechanism.** Requiring pre-briefing qualification changes the nature of the first meeting from information exchange to mutual commitment. Clients who qualify are more likely to act on the output.

**Capacity constraint as quality commitment signal.** Declining engagements where the practice cannot add value signals that the practice is not optimizing for utilization. The constraint is a trust signal, not a business limitation.

**Pattern knowledge from the Geneva ecosystem as compounding differentiator.** Each engagement in the Geneva institutional ecosystem produces knowledge that makes the next engagement more precise. The compounding effect is invisible in the first year and decisive in the third.

**What is not working:**

**Generalist AI governance frameworks.** Frameworks designed for large organizations or generic audiences are not landing with Geneva principals. The problem is not the framework quality — the problem is the distance between the framework architecture and the actual operating context.

**Pricing without scarcity context.** Stating a price without the scarcity context that justifies it positions the briefing as a commodity service. The scarcity context is part of the value proposition, not a negotiation tactic.

**Peer circuit calibration at elevated stakes.** The Geneva informal circuit has become an impression management venue. Principals calibrating their AI governance decisions against peer circuit signals are getting feedback that is optimized for social positioning, not decision quality.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Briefing format as trust infrastructure performing function content cannot perform**
The 90-minute briefing format performs a trust function that written content cannot. The format allows the principal to observe the advisor's reasoning in real time, to challenge it, and to assess whether the advisor's judgment is sound. This function is not replicable by any written artifact. The format is infrastructure, not logistics.

**Finding 2: Written summary portability as undisclosed design variable**
Clients use the written summary in contexts the practice did not design for — board presentations, carrier conversations, client communications. The portability of the summary is a design variable that affects what should go into it. The finding that the summary is being used in unexpected contexts is itself new information about how to design it.

**Finding 3: Pre-briefing qualification as commitment mechanism increasing briefing outcome probability**
Pre-briefing qualification is not a barrier — it is a commitment mechanism. Clients who have invested the qualification time arrive at the briefing with higher intention to act on the output. The qualification process is part of the product design, not a separate administrative step.

**Finding 4: Geneva institutional ecosystem pattern knowledge as compounding moat**
Each engagement in the Geneva institutional ecosystem produces knowledge that makes subsequent engagements more precise. The pattern knowledge compounds. The moat is invisible in the first year and decisive in the third. The compounding property is a structural advantage that is not visible from outside the practice.

**Finding 5: Peer circuit as impression management venue creating durable demand for calibration-immune advisory**
The Geneva informal circuit has shifted toward impression management. Principals are calibrating their AI governance decisions against peer circuit signals that are optimized for social positioning. This creates durable demand for advisory that is immune to peer circuit calibration — advisory that is grounded in the principal's actual decision context, not in what the circuit expects to hear.

**Finding 6: Capacity constraint as quality commitment signal operating at the decision level**
The practice's capacity constraint is a quality commitment signal that operates at the decision level, not the business level. Declining an engagement where the practice cannot add value communicates to the prospective client that the practice is not optimizing for utilization. The constraint signals that the practice has standards for the work it will do.
