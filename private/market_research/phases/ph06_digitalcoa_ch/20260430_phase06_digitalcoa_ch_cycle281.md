## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Geneva legal boutiques:** Legal-regulatory framing that addresses legal questions, not the broader advisory questions executives face. Missing the executive accountability perspective that precedes legal questions.

**Tech/cyber security firms:** Technology security framing that addresses tool security, not decision accountability. Missing the governance and professional liability dimensions that technology security framing cannot address.

**This practice structural advantage:** Geneva-French informal circuit participation producing Geneva-calibrated methodology that cannot be replicated by external analysis. The structural position is the competitive moat, not the content volume or the methodology claims.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: Adequacy definition vacuum as primary demand generation mechanism**
The undefined adequacy standard is generating demand from executives who cannot confirm compliance with a requirement nobody has defined. The vacuum is the primary demand generation mechanism, not the regulatory requirements themselves.

**Finding 2: Documentation window close as structural occasion that determines urgency intensity**
The documentation window close is not a uniform timing event. It is a sequence of occasions when specific accountability is tested. The urgency intensity varies by which venue is testing accountability and when in the window the test occurs.

**Finding 3: Pre-engagement disqualification logic performing trust formation function before content evaluation**
The documented refusal decisions function as trust formation before any content is evaluated. The refusal demonstrates accurate self-assessment. The ICP evaluates fit before evaluating analysis.

**Finding 4: Naming structurally unnamed problems as primary value creation mechanism**
The primary value is not analysis delivery. It is problem naming. Problems that are structurally unnamed cannot be addressed systematically. The naming function creates value before the analysis function.

**Finding 5: Executive accountability occasion density as pricing architecture design variable**
The density of accountability occasions across the calendar determines which executives can engage and when. The pricing architecture should be designed for the executive who faces high-density occasions, not uniform distribution across the calendar.

**Finding 6: Geneva circuit participation as non-replicable trust formation mechanism**
The structural position inside the Geneva informal circuit is the trust formation mechanism that cannot be replicated by content production, expertise claims, or methodology documentation. The position is the moat.
