# 02 — DigitalCoa.ch Trust Signal Architecture: Specific Trust Signals at Each Conversion Stage
**Phase:** C204 Phase 06 (DigitalCoa.ch) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
DigitalCoa.ch trust signals work differently at each conversion stage, and the current architecture doesn't systematically match trust signals to stage. For cold traffic, institutional credibility (Geneva-specific expertise, Brotons.net authority, published methodology) matters most. For warm leads from Brotons.net, peer trust (executives who read and recommend) matters more. For mandate-stage prospects, relationship trust (executive briefing experience, personal credibility of advisor) is decisive. The trust signal architecture needs to address all three stages simultaneously without overwhelming early-stage visitors with late-stage trust content.

## Evidence
- Cold traffic to DigitalCoa.ch: Geneva institutional credibility signals (nFADP, GAIAC, Swiss regulatory references) have highest trust impact
- Brotons.net referral warm leads: peer trust signals (executives who shared, publications citing content) more persuasive than institutional credentials
- Mandate-stage conversion: advisor-executive relationship quality at briefing is decisive — not website trust signals
- DigitalCoa.ch website currently leads with institutional credentials throughout the funnel — mismatch for warm Brotons.net leads
- Geneva executives performing due diligence on DigitalCoa.ch: checking Brotons.net, searching advisor name, reviewing LinkedIn presence
- Trust signal sequencing: institutional credibility → methodology clarity → peer evidence → relationship quality is the effective trust architecture

## Advisory Implication
DigitalCoa.ch website and materials need stage-matched trust signal architecture. Lead with institutional credibility for cold traffic, switch to peer evidence for warm Brotons.net referrals, and invest in briefing quality for mandate-stage conversion. The website should serve all three stages simultaneously.
