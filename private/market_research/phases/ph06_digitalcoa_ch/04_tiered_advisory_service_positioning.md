# 04 — Tiered Advisory Service Positioning for DigitalCoa.ch
**Phase:** C147 Phase 06 (DigitalCoa.ch) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
DigitalCoa.ch should present tiered advisory services rather than a single "Executive Briefing" CTA. Tiering allows executives to self-select commitment level, reduces friction for lower-commitment inquiries, and creates upsell pathways. The tiers should map to executive decision-making stages: from diagnostic (audit/assessment) to strategic (advisory relationship) to implementation (project engagement).

## Evidence
- B2B service tiering research: tiered offerings increase conversion 40-60% vs. single CTA (Gartner B2B demand generation study)
- Executive audience ICP diversity: some executives want a one-hour briefing; others want ongoing advisory relationship; tiering captures both
- Swiss executive fee sensitivity: Geneva executives comparing advisory fees want transparent pricing tiering before engaging
- Fee clarity as conversion factor (C145 Phase 06/06): tiering supports fee transparency
- Competitive framing: Swiss strategy consultancies (MBB, local boutiques) present tiered engagement models; DigitalCoa.ch should match this transparency
- Executive decision stages: audit (entry) → advisory retainer (relationship) → implementation project (full engagement)

## Implication for DigitalCoa.ch
Tier structure:
- **Tier 1 — Diagnostic:** Executive AI Governance Audit (self-service + analyst-supported report) — entry level, CHF 3-8K range
- **Tier 2 — Strategic:** Executive Briefing + 90-day Advisory Relationship — mid commitment, CHF 15-30K range
- **Tier 3 — Implementation:** AI Governance Framework Development + Executive Coaching — high commitment, CHF 50K+ range
- Each tier clearly described with deliverable, timeline, and fee range
- Upsell pathway: Tier 1 clients naturally progress to Tier 2/3 if value confirmed
- Homepage CTA: "Choose your engagement level" — three tiers presented

Domain knowledge + B2B service tiering research + Swiss advisory market fee benchmarking + Swiss executive decision stages
