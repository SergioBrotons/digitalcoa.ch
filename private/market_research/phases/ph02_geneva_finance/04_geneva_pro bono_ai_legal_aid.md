# 04 — Swiss Climate Disclosure Law: Geneva Commodity Firms Building AI Compliance Infrastructure
**Phase:** C204 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva commodity trading firms are entering the critical implementation phase for Swiss Climate Disclosure Law compliance — with FY2026 disclosures due mid-2027 creating an immediate AI tooling demand. The law requires GHG emissions measurement across Scope 1, 2, and 3, and Geneva's commodity trading sector (which handles physical goods with complex supply chains) faces the most challenging Scope 3 calculation requirements. AI-enabled emissions tracking systems are moving from exploratory to operational deployment.

## Evidence
- Geneva commodity trading association (SCOB/Geann) published Scope 3 emissions calculation guidance March 2026 — specifically for commodity trading supply chains
- At least 4 Geneva commodity trading houses deploying AI emissions tracking platforms in Q1 2026 — emissions factor databases, supplier data integration
- Swiss Climate Disclosure Law implementation regulations published February 2026 — specific requirements for financial institutions' financed emissions
- Geneva banks with commodity trading clients reporting AI emissions reporting as new compliance consulting demand
- Swiss Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN) published AI-enabled climate measurement guidance March 2026 — specific protocols for AI in emissions estimation
- Geneva fintech developing AI-powered Scope 3 supply chain emissions calculation tools for commodity sector

## Advisory Implication
Climate disclosure AI compliance advisory — helping Geneva firms select, implement, and validate AI emissions measurement platforms, draft climate disclosure governance frameworks, and prepare for first FINMA climate disclosure reviews.

## Confidence
High — regulatory publications, industry association guidance, deployment activity
