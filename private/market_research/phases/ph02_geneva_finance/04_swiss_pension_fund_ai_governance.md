# 04 — Swiss Pension Fund AI Governance: BVG Occupational Pension Reform
**Phase:** C147 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
The Swiss Occupational Pension Act (BVG, 2nd pillar) reform — in parliamentary debate throughout 2025-2026 — introduces new investment governance requirements that indirectly affect AI deployment in pension fund management. Geneva's pension fund concentration (many Geneva-based institutional investors, family offices, and pension funds of international organizations) creates a specific advisory need around AI governance for institutional asset management.

## Evidence
- BVG reform (BVG 21) enacted with staggered implementation — pension fund trustees now legally required to document investment decision-making processes, creating natural pressure to formalize AI use disclosure
- Swiss Pension Fund Association (ASIP) issued AI guidance (Nov 2025) — recommends human-in-the-loop for all AI-assisted investment decisions in BVG-regulated plans
- Geneva institutional investors (BPK Geneva, pension funds of UN agencies) piloting AI portfolio risk tools — ASIP guidance compliance requires governance framework documentation
- Federal Office of Social Insurance (BSV) monitoring AI use in pension fund administration — first BSV advisory expected Q3 2026
- Geneva's concentration of foundation boards (foundations managing ~CHF 100B+) creates board governance advisory demand for AI oversight

## Advisory Implication
Advisory opportunities:
- Swiss pension fund AI investment governance framework
- BVG reform compliance advisory for Geneva pension fund trustees
- Geneva institutional investor AI oversight board training
- ASIP AI guidance implementation advisory
- Geneva foundation AI governance for board members
- AI vendor due diligence for Geneva pension fund asset managers
