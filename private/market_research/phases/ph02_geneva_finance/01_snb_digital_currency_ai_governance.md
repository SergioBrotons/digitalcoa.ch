# 01 — Swiss National Bank Digital currency/Tokenized Assets AI Governance
**Phase:** C147 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Switzerland's exploration of retail central bank digital currency (CBDC) and the SNB's Project Helvetia findings on tokenized wholesale CBDC have surfaced a new governance challenge: AI systems managing tokenized asset portfolios, settlement algorithms, and programmable money require regulatory frameworks that do not yet exist. Geneva's banks and asset managers (managing ~$2.4T in assets) are the most exposed to this gap.

## Evidence
- SNB Project Helvetia III (2025): tokenized wholesale CBDC settlement via DLT platform — AI-driven settlement optimization emerging as next phase
- FINMA Guidance 01/2026 stablecoin framework (C145 baseline) created licensing pathway but no AI governance overlay
- Swiss Federal Council digital finance strategy (published Jan 2026) explicitly flags AI in tokenized markets as requiring new supervisory tools
- SIX Digital Exchange (SDX) Geneva integration with AI-powered trading — SDX is Geneva-licensed and operates as Swiss national stock exchange infrastructure
- Geneva commodity trading houses (Vitol, Trafigura, Mercuria) exploring AI-optimized settlement on tokenized trade finance platforms

## Advisory Implication
Advisory opportunities:
- Geneva bank AI tokenized-asset governance frameworks
- SNB CBDC compliance advisory for Swiss financial institutions
- Tokenized trade finance AI due diligence
- FINMA AI governance overlay for tokenized asset licensees
- Geneva asset manager AI portfolio system regulatory compliance
- Cross-border tokenized settlement legal framework

# C206 Finding — SNB Wholesale CBDC AI Integration Considerations 2026

**Phase:** 02 Geneva Finance | **Cycle:** C206 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

The Swiss National Bank continues wholesale CBDC exploration with an explicit AI integration layer — addressing programmable money conditions, AI-driven payment vs. settlement distinctions, and technical consultations with Geneva banks in Q2 2026.

## Key Details

- **Wholesale CBDC vs. retail CBDC:** SNB wholesale CBDC (w-CBDC) for financial institution settlement remains the focus; retail CBDC study continues but no decision
- **AI integration layer:** SNB w-CBDC design includes programmable money conditions — AI-driven payment conditionality, AI-executed settlement conditions, AI-optimized liquidity management
- **Payment vs. settlement AI distinction:** AI systems managing payment initiation (front-end) vs. settlement finality (back-end) create distinct regulatory accountability gaps
- **Geneva bank consultation:** SNB technical consultation with Geneva banks Q2 2026 on w-CBDC design choices — Geneva private banks, commodity trading banks (UBS, Credit Suisse, local banks)
- **Programmable money AI execution:** w-CBDC programmable conditions raise questions about AI-executed financial obligations — legal liability for AI-triggered payments, smart contract vs. w-CBDC condition execution
- **Digital CHF timeline:** w-CBDC remains in pilot/research stage; no formal Federal Council decision; 2027-2028 earliest potential operationalization

## Strategic Angle for DigitalCoa.ch

- Swiss FI w-CBDC readiness advisory: helping Geneva banks prepare internal systems for w-CBDC integration
- Programmable money AI governance advisory: structuring AI execution governance for w-CBDC conditional payments
- SNB w-CBDC compliance advisory: advisory on AML/KYC implications of AI-animated w-CBDC transactions
