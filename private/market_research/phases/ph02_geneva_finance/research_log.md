## NET-NEW Findings (this cycle — C214)

1. **Accountability gap as the central concern:** Boutique Geneva wealth managers are not primarily worried about AI efficiency. They are worried about what happens when AI produces a wrong recommendation and someone asks who is responsible. The accountability chain — tool vendor (no financial sector liability framework), platform provider (terms of service disclaim liability), the firm (no AI-specific governance documentation), the advisor (personal professional liability unclear) — is undefined. This accountability gap is the psychological center of the deployment problem.

2. **Informal AI deployment is the actual current state:** Like the small law firm ICP in Phase 01, boutique wealth managers are using AI on client matters informally — personal AI accounts for drafting client reports, analyzing portfolio performance, summarizing compliance documentation — without firm authorization, documentation, or data processing agreements. The risk is unmanaged adoption, not no adoption.

3. **FINMA regulatory silence is creating compliance debt:** FINMA has not published explicit guidance on AI use in Swiss private banking advisory. Existing FINMA Circ. 2018/3 on outsourcing is applied analogically to AI tools, with imperfect fit. The forthcoming FINMA AI model risk classification (Q4 2026) and AI advisory suitability audit program (Q1 2027) are concrete deadlines. Firms deploying AI without documented governance are building compliance debt that will be expensive to service when those deadlines arrive.

4. **The documentation layer is the specific advisory gap:** Boutique wealth managers do not need another bank-scale AI governance framework. They need a practical documentation layer that converts abstract FINMA expectations into decision logs, client disclosure language, and AI tool vendor assessment criteria designed for a 50–200 person firm with a two-person compliance team.

5. **ISSB-aligned disclosure deadlines create a concrete near-term trigger:** Federal Council endorsed ISSB-aligned disclosure standards in March 2026. FY2026 Swiss annual reports due mid-2027 require AI-enabled data collection infrastructure. Geneva wealth managers with fund management mandates face a date-driven compliance requirement, not speculative regulatory risk.

6. **Bilingual Swiss Romandy positioning is a market differentiator:** The Geneva wealth management market has specific regulatory context (FINMA, ASIP, SFAMA) and a French-language preference that is underserved by English-first or German-first AI governance content. Fully bilingual (EN + FR) advisory capacity is a market positioning statement that large international consulting firms cannot easily replicate.
