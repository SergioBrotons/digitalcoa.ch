# 02 — Basel IV Endgame AI Capital Implications for Swiss Banks
**Phase:** C147 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Basel IV implementation (finalized Basel III reforms, effective Swiss implementation 2025-2026) creates a new risk category: AI-model risk in credit risk weighting. Swiss banks using AI for credit scoring and risk assessment must now document model risk management practices that satisfy FINMA's expanded supervisory expectations. Geneva's private banks (the largest category of Swiss bank by number and systemic importance for wealth management) face the sharpest adaptation burden.

## Evidence
- FINMA Circular 2023/1 (revised): minimum requirements for model risk management apply to all Swiss banks using AI-based credit risk models
- Basel Committee on Banking Supervision (BCBS) guidance on AI model validation (Oct 2025) — Swiss implementation via FINMA imprint pending
- Swiss Banking Industry Association (SBA) survey (Dec 2025): 68% of Swiss private banks using or piloting AI credit risk tools; 40% not yet compliant with FINMA model risk documentation requirements
- FINMA supervised banks with AI-based SME lending models required to submit model documentation by Q1 2026 — many missed deadline
- Geneva cantonal bank (Banque Cantonale de Genève, BCGe) announced AI-assisted mortgage scoring system (Jan 2026) — first Geneva retail bank to deploy AI in regulated credit function

## Advisory Implication
Advisory opportunities:
- Basel IV AI model risk management framework implementation
- FINMA-compliant AI credit model validation and documentation
- Geneva private bank AI governance gap assessment
- AI model validation for Swiss banking AI systems
- FINMA examination preparation for AI-model risk
- Cross-border Basel IV AI compliance (Swiss-EU alignment)
