# 03 — Geneva Commodity Trading AI Compliance: Sanctions and AML Frameworks
**Phase:** C147 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva's commodity trading houses (Vitol, Trafigura, Mercuria, Gunvor — all Geneva-headquartered or Geneva-office-present) are deploying AI for trade surveillance, sanctions screening, and counterparty due diligence. The intersection of AI-driven commodity trading decisions and Swiss sanctions enforcement (SECO/Swiss Export Control) is creating a compliance advisory gap. Swiss sanctions law does not yet have AI-specific provisions; FINMA and SECO are applying general compliance standards by analogy.

## Evidence
- SECO (State Secretariat for Economic Affairs) updated dual-use goods export control guidance (Jan 2026) — AI-powered goods classification tools flagged as requiring human oversight documentation
- Geneva commodity trading firms using AI for real-time sanctions screening across counterparties (counterparty AI risk scoring)
- Swiss Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA, GwG) revision (2025) in effect — AI-assisted transaction monitoring triggers enhanced audit requirements
- FINMA circular on AI in AML (2025/2026 guidance pending) — Geneva banks facilitating commodity trade finance under increased scrutiny
- Swiss precious metals and energy trading AI platforms processing hundreds of millions of daily transactions — AI explainability increasingly demanded by Swiss banking correspondents

## Advisory Implication
Advisory opportunities:
- Geneva commodity trading house AI sanctions compliance framework
- AI counterparty due diligence documentation for Geneva trading desks
- Swiss AML AI model validation for commodity trade finance banks
- SECO AI-powered dual-use goods compliance advisory
- Geneva trading house AI model explainability for banking correspondents
- Cross-commodity AI trading risk framework
