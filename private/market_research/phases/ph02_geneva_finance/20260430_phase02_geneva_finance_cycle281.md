## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: AI inventory boundary question unresolved at boutique scale**
FINMA guidance requires AI inventory documentation. The boundary between AI system and basic digital tool is undefined. Boutique firms cannot produce inventories that meet the requirement because they cannot determine what the requirement covers.

**Signal 2: Institutional client questionnaires exceeding firm documentation capacity**
Institutional clients sending AI governance questionnaires that assume documentation infrastructure boutique firms do not have. The questionnaires function as de facto standards before formal standards exist.

**Signal 3: Carrier third-generation questionnaires introducing concepts firms have no policies for**
Carrier renewal questionnaires introducing agentic AI and third-party model dependency questions before firms have policies. Firms respond from necessity, not preparation.

**Signal 4: SIF Q2 2026 anticipatory compliance window creating decision paralysis**
Regulatory window approaching without clear requirements. Firms managing uncertainty by waiting. The paralysis itself is a risk: firms are not positioning for the window.

**Signal 5: AI vendor version updates creating report format changes clients receive before firm reviews**
Model updates changing output format without firm awareness. Clients receiving unevaluated reports during the detection gap. No version tracking protocol exists at boutique scale.

**Signal 6: Boutique MD performing Chief AI Officer function without title, training, or support**
The MD is the only person who can answer AI governance questions at the boutique firm. The function exists without the title, the training, or the structural support that a formal role would bring.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**FINMA Guidance 08/2024 and AI inventory obligations:**
FINMA formalized AI inventory requirements. The boundary between reportable AI systems and non-reportable digital tools is undefined. Boutique firms face compliance obligations they cannot currently discharge.

**SIF Q2 2026 regulatory gap analysis:**
The regulatory gap analysis window is approaching. Firms that have not positioned for the window will face the requirements without preparation. The window is a structural timing event.

**ASIP two-layer accountability:**
ASIP accountability standards apply to the asset management layer and the advisory layer. AI systems introduce complexity into both layers that existing accountability frameworks do not address.

**nFADP Articles 6, 15, and 21:**
AI-inferred client conclusions activate Article 15 accuracy obligations. Client data processed through AI systems activates Article 6 lawful basis questions. Article 21 AI decision documentation obligations accumulate without methodology.

**CoE AI Convention present-tense obligations:**
The AI Convention is in force. Obligations activate through existing client mandates before implementing legislation. The ratification creates obligations firms have not yet recognized.

**FIDLEG Article 26 proportionate methodology absence:**
No proportionate methodology exists for FIDLEG Article 26 compliance at boutique scale. The absence means boutique firms cannot confirm they are compliant.
