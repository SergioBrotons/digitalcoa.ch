## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: Approval continuity gap across AI model updates**
Approvals given for AI configurations do not survive vendor updates. The continuity gap creates undefined personal liability for the person who approved the prior version. No methodology exists for managing this gap.

**Signal 2: Client-requested AI rationale as relay accuracy problem**
When clients ask for the AI rationale behind a recommendation, the relay accuracy matters. Approximate answers presented as professional explanations create material liability exposure. The problem is specific to boutique scale where the same person generates and explains recommendations.

**Signal 3: FINMA audit readiness for AI as preparation-is-exposure event**
Preparing for FINMA AI audit with no published standard to prepare against. The preparation effort itself becomes an exposure event because it reveals what the firm does not have.

**Signal 4: Cross-regulatory documentation collision at boutique scale**
FINMA documentation requirements and nFADP data minimization obligations are in direct collision at boutique scale. No resolution framework exists for firms navigating both simultaneously.

**Signal 5: Custodian AI integration accountability boundary confusion**
AI integration with custodian systems creates accountability boundaries that are unclear. The firm does not know where its accountability ends and the custodian's begins.

**Signal 6: Documentation adequacy inversion**
Internal documentation standards appear adequate until compared against market-formed standards. The inversion is visible once named and manageable once visible.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**FINMA Circular 2016/1 and AI model risk:**
FINMA Circular 2016/1 establishes documentation expectations for investment decisions. AI-assisted decisions fall under these expectations. The documentation requirements for AI-assisted decisions are more complex than for manual decisions because the AI's reasoning must also be documented.

**FINMA Circular 2017/1 and AI suitability:**
FINMA Circular 2017/1 requires suitability documentation for investment advice. AI-generated recommendations must be documented as suitable. The documentation requirement for AI-generated recommendations is operationally different from documentation for human-generated recommendations.

**ASIP pension fund AI governance Q3 2026:**
ASIP guidance on AI governance for pension funds due Q3 2026. The guidance will create specific obligations for firms managing pension fund assets with AI tools. The timing creates a preparation window.

**SFAMA/AMAS AI supervisory reading problem:**
SFAMA/AMAS standards create obligations through supervisory reading, not published text. The obligation exists because a regulator interpreted something, not because a rule was written. This is a distinct category from published regulatory gaps.

**ISSB AI sustainability claims:**
ISSB endorsement March 2026, FY2026 reports due mid-2027. AI-generated sustainability claims must meet ISSB documentation standards. The intersection of ISSB requirements and FINMA audit timing creates a compound exposure window.

**nFADP Articles 6, 8, 12, and 21:**
Data minimization (Art. 6), purpose limitation (Art. 8), data subject rights (Art. 12), and AI-assisted decision explanation (Art. 21) all apply to AI systems processing client financial data. The obligations are simultaneous and partially conflicting.
