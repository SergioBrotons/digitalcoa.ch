# 06 — Geneva Family Office AI Governance: Investment AI Due Diligence and Regulatory Pressure
**Phase:** C204 Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva family offices are facing dual pressure on AI governance: investment AI due diligence requirements and emerging regulatory expectations. Family offices managing Geneva-based private wealth are increasingly using AI for portfolio management, market intelligence, and deal sourcing — creating both governance gaps and regulatory exposure. FINMA's increased scrutiny of AI in wealth management is reaching the family office segment, and Geneva family offices have limited internal AI governance capacity.

## Evidence
- FINMA wealth management guidance updated Q1 2026 — AI usage disclosure requirements extending to smaller private wealth managers
- Geneva family office association (SAFK — Swiss Association of Family Offices) published AI governance best practices March 2026
- At least 6 Geneva single family offices deploying AI investment platforms Q4 2025/Q1 2026 — deal sourcing, portfolio monitoring, risk analysis
- Multi-family offices in Geneva reporting client demand for AI governance frameworks as fiduciary risk mitigation
- Swiss private bankers reporting family office clients asking about AI investment platform governance documentation
- Geneva legal community developing family office AI governance advisory service offerings in response to demand

## Advisory Implication
Family office AI governance advisory is a differentiated offering in the Geneva wealth management market — AI investment platform governance documentation, board-level AI oversight frameworks, and regulatory compliance for family office AI use.

## Confidence
Medium — industry association activity, wealth management advisor reports
