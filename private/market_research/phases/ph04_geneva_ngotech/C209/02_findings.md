# Finding 02 | C209 | Geneva NGO/Tech

## Topic
EPFL AI technology transfer Geneva commercialisation — spin-off governance and IP licensing AI frameworks

## EN
EPFL's technology transfer office (TTO) has developed a structured AI IP licensing and spin-off governance framework specifically addressing the unique challenges of transferring AI research out of academia into commercial application — going beyond the CERN IP governance noted in C208 finding 3 by focusing on the full lifecycle of AI spin-offs rather than research data IP. The framework covers four stages: AI model ownership clarification at the research stage (distinguishing between training data ownership, model architecture contributions, and fine-tuning contributions), IP protection strategy for AI inventions (patent vs. trade secret vs. open source decision trees), licensing terms for AI model access by spin-offs (model weight licensing, training data access agreements, compute infrastructure access terms), and governance standards for AI spin-offs receiving EPFL institutional investment (AI ethics oversight committees, acceptable AI use restrictions, data governance requirements mirroring EPFL's research standards). Geneva-based venture capital funds investing in EPFL AI spin-offs are increasingly requiring compliance with this framework as a condition of investment — creating a quasi-regulatory standard for AI spin-off governance that is diffusing beyond EPFL to other Swiss academic institutions. The framework addresses a genuine gap: early-stage AI spin-offs often lack the governance infrastructure to manage the complex IP and ethical questions that arise from commercialising academic AI research.

## FR
Le bureau de transfert de technologie de l'EPFL (TTO) a développé un cadre structuré de licence de PI IA et de gouvernance des spin-offs spécifiquement adressé aux défis uniques du transfert de la recherche IA académique vers l'application commerciale — allant au-delà de la gouvernance de PI CERN notée au résultat C208 n°3 en se concentrant sur le cycle de vie complet des spin-offs IA plutôt que sur les données de recherche. Le cadre couvre quatre étapes: clarification de la propriété du modèle IA au stade de la recherche, stratégie de protection de PI pour les inventions IA, conditions de licence pour l'accès au modèle IA par les spin-offs, et normes de gouvernance pour les spin-offs IA recevant un investissement institutionnel EPFL. Les fonds de capital-risque genevois investissant dans les spin-offs IA de l'EPFL exigent de plus en plus la conformité à ce cadre comme condition d'investissement — créant une norme quasi-réglementaire pour la gouvernance des spin-offs IA qui se diffuse au-delà de l'EPFL à d'autres institutions académiques suisses.
