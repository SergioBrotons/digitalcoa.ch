# Finding 01 | C209 | Geneva NGO/Tech

## Topic
Geneva healthtech AI medical device Swissmedic approval pathway — structured navigation service for AI SaMD

## EN
Swissmedic has published dedicated guidance on the approval pathway for AI-based Software as a Medical Device (AI SaMD) — creating a distinct regulatory navigation market for Geneva's healthtech sector. The guidance distinguishes between AI SaMD classified as Class A (low risk, notification only), Class B (moderate risk, conformity assessment), Class C (high risk, intensive conformity assessment with clinical data requirements), and Class D (highest risk, pre-market approval with mandatory clinical investigation). Geneva healthtech firms developing AI diagnostic, prognostic, or therapeutic support tools must navigate this classification, prepare Swissmedic Technical Documentation, and engage a Swissmedic-recognised Notified Body for Class B and above. The guidance introduces a specific AI SaMD pre-submission meeting process — Swissmedic will meet with developers before formal submission to provide informal classification confirmation and documentation guidance. Geneva consulting firms are beginning to offer AI SaMD Regulatory Navigation services: helping healthtech clients classify their AI products, prepare Swissmedic submissions, and manage the pre-submission meeting process. This is distinct from the ISO 13485 quality management system certification noted in earlier cycles, focusing specifically on the Swissmedic regulatory pathway and AI-specific documentation requirements.

## FR
Swissmedic a publié une orientation dédiée sur la voie d'approbation pour les logiciels AI en tant que dispositifs médicaux (AI SaMD) — créant un marché distinct de navigation réglementaire pour le secteur healthtech genevois. L'orientation distingue entre les AI SaMD classés comme Classe A (faible risque, notification uniquement), Classe B (risque modéré, évaluation de conformité), Classe C (risque élevé, évaluation de conformité intensive avec exigences de données cliniques), et Classe D (risque le plus élevé, approbation pré-commercialisation avec investigation clinique obligatoire). Les entreprises healthtech genevoises développant des outils de diagnostic, pronostic ou thérapeutique assistés par IA doivent naviguer cette classification, préparer la Documentation Technique Swissmedic, et s'engager avec un Organisme Notifié reconnu par Swissmedic. L'orientation introduit un processus spécifique de réunion de pré-soumission Swissmedic. Des cabinets de conseil genevois commencent à offrir des services de Navigation Réglementaire AI SaMD.
