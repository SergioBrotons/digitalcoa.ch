# Finding 03 | C209 | Geneva NGO/Tech

## Topic
WMO AI climate modeling Geneva standards — first AI weather model certification framework

## EN
The World Meteorological Organization (WMO), headquartered in Geneva, has published the first international certification framework for AI weather and climate modeling systems — a landmark development for the AI in environmental science sector. The framework establishes minimum performance standards for AI meteorological models used in official weather services, aviation, maritime, and climate research: model accuracy benchmarks against established numerical weather prediction (NWP) baselines, uncertainty quantification requirements (AI models must provide calibrated probability distributions, not point estimates), physical consistency constraints (AI model outputs must satisfy conservation laws and physical parameter limits), and reproducibility standards (model版本 control, training data provenance documentation, and inference environment specifications). The framework introduces a WMO AI Weather Model Certification (WMO-AIWMC) designation that national meteorological services can apply for, with Swiss Federal Office of Meteorology and Climatology (MeteoSwiss) participating in the inaugural certification cohort. Geneva-based climate AI startups and research institutions are positioning to obtain WMO-AIWMC certification as a quality differentiator in the growing market for AI climate services — particularly for emerging market national meteorological services that are adopting AI weather tools without the traditional NWP infrastructure. The certification framework creates a new compliance market in Geneva's climate technology sector.

## FR
