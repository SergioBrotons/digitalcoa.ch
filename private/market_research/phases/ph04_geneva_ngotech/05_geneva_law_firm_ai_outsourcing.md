# 05 — EPFL AI Spin-Off Fund Deployment: Sectors and Governance Requirements
**Phase:** C204 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
The EPFL Geneva Innovation Park CHF 15M cantonal AI spin-off fund is deploying — with sector allocation becoming clear in Q1 2026. The fund is targeting AI applications in healthtech, climate tech, and fintech, with explicit AI governance requirements attached to funding. EPFL is using fund deployment as leverage to establish AI governance standards in Geneva AI startups, creating a model for AI innovation policy that other cantons are watching.

## Evidence
- EPFL Geneva Innovation Park Plan-les-Ouates CHF 15M AI spin-off fund: deployment began Q4 2025, sector priorities emerging Q1 2026
- Fund sector allocation: 40% healthtech AI, 30% climate tech AI, 20% fintech AI, 10% general deep tech AI
- AI governance requirement attached to fund: all recipients must adopt ISO/IEC 42001 AI management system or equivalent
- EPFL AI spin-off due diligence incorporating AI ethics assessment — board-level review of AI risk management
- Canton of Geneva using EPFL fund as AI governance policy lever — requiring AI impact assessment for funded companies
- Geneva AI startup ecosystem responding: AI governance as funding prerequisite creating demand for AI governance advisory

## Advisory Implication
AI governance as funding prerequisite creates captive demand — Geneva AI startups seeking EPFL funding need AI governance frameworks. This is a structured entry point for advisory relationships with early-stage AI companies.

## Confidence
High — EPFL fund structure, Canton of Geneva policy, AI startup ecosystem response
