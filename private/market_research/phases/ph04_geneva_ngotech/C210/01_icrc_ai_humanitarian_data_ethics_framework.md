## Finding

The International Committee of the Red Cross (ICRC) has developed an AI Humanitarian Data Ethics Framework for Geneva-based humanitarian organisations deploying AI systems in conflict and disaster settings. The framework addresses three core principles: (1) do-no-harm AI data minimisation — minimising personal data collection in conflict zones, (2) AI accuracy accountability — ICRC liability for AI-driven decisions affecting displaced persons and detainees, and (3) AI access equity — ensuring AI systems do not create barriers to humanitarian assistance for vulnerable populations. Geneva humanitarian NGOs are beginning to align their AI governance with ICRC standards, creating a distinct AI ethics advisory market. The framework is distinct from general AI ethics guidelines by virtue of its conflict-zone applicability and international humanitarian law integration.
