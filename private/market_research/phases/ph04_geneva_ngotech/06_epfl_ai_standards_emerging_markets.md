# C206 Finding 06D — EPFL AI Standards Contribution for Emerging Markets — Geneva Technology Transfer

**Phase:** 04 Geneva NGO/Tech | **Cycle:** C206 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

EPFL (École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne) is contributing Swiss AI governance standards to emerging market deployment contexts — developing AI governance frameworks adapted for African and Asian deployment through Geneva technology transfer channels, with UNAIWG AI collaboration positioning Swiss AI governance as an exportable framework.

## Key Details

- **EPFL AI governance for emerging markets:** EPFL research groups developing AI governance frameworks specifically adapted for emerging market deployment contexts — lower-resource settings, different regulatory traditions, capacity constraints
- **Geneva technology transfer AI strategy:** Université de Genève and EPFL technology transfer offices developing AI governance frameworks for spin-off companies deploying AI systems internationally
- **Swiss AI governance as exportable framework:** Swiss AI governance standards (ISO 42001, Swiss AI Act principles, nFADP data protection) positioned as adaptable framework for developing country AI regulation
- **UNAIWG collaboration:** EPFL collaborating with UNAIWG on AI governance capacity building for 14 UN agencies — EPFL AI governance curriculum for UN staff development
- **SDC/Swiss development cooperation AI programs:** Swiss Agency for Development and Cooperation funding EPFL AI governance programs in partner countries — Swiss AI governance framework as development cooperation tool
- **Geneva conference role:** Geneva International Legal Week and Geneva Technology Summit increasingly featuring AI governance frameworks for emerging markets — Geneva as the platform for Swiss AI governance export
