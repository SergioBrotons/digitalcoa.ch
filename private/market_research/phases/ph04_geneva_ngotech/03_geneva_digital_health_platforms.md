# 03 — Geneva Digital Health Platforms: Swissmedic Pathway and AI Integration
**Phase:** C147 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva's healthtech cluster (hospitals: HUG; medtech: Medtronic Geneva office, Alcon; biotech: clear cell labs, Sophia Genetics based in Geneva region) is generating AI-powered digital health platforms that require Swissmedic regulatory navigation. The Swissmedic SaNV (Swiss Agency for Therapeutic Products) approval pathway for AI-enabled medical devices is a distinct regulatory advisory niche — combining regulatory strategy, clinical evidence design, and reimbursement pathway navigation.

## Evidence
- Swissmedicguidance on AI-enabled medical devices (2025 revision) — software as medical device (SaMD) classification criteria updated
- Geneva University Hospital (HUG) AI health applications — AI diagnostic imaging, AI clinical decision support, AI patient monitoring — all requiring Swissmedic classification
- Sophia Genetics (Geneva-area) — genomic AI platform operating globally; Swissmedic Class B device registration pathway experience
- Swiss digital health reimbursement (DRG/KVG reform) — AI-enabled digital health tools need KVG reimbursement pathway, not just Swissmedic approval
- Federal Office of Public Health (BAG) digital health strategy 2024-2028 — AI explicitly mentioned as priority
- Geneva healthtech startups increasingly pursuing EU CE Mark alongside Swissmedic approval simultaneously

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- Swissmedic Class I/IIa/IIb AI medical device registration strategy
- Swissmedic + CE Mark dual-registration advisory
- Geneva healthtech startup Swissmedic pathway planning
- AI clinical evidence design for Swissmedic submission
- KVG reimbursement pathway for AI digital health tools
- HUG AI partnership commercialization framework for Geneva startups
