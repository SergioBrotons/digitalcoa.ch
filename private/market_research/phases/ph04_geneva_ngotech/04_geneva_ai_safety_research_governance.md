# 04 — Geneva AI Safety Research and Governance Tools Ecosystem
**Phase:** C147 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva is becoming a hub for AI safety research and governance tool development — distinct from the dominant US/UK AI safety ecosystem. The Geneva-based Center for AI Safety (CAIS) equivalent activity, ICRC AI policy work, UNIDIR (UN Institute for Disarmament Research, Geneva) AI weapons governance research, and EPFL's machine learning security research create a unique cluster. This ecosystem generates demand for governance tools, advisory, and academic-commercial translation.

## Evidence
- UNIDIR (Geneva) AI weapons governance research program (launched 2024, expanding 2026) — Geneva as AI disarmament research hub
- ICRC autonomous weapons policy (AI decision-support limits in armed conflict) — Geneva as humanitarian AI safety hub
- EPFL Computer Science and Communications Systems (CSC) AI security research — Lausanne/Geneva axis
- Geneva Science and Diplomacy Anticipator (GESDA) — Geneva-based, working on AI science diplomacy and governance tools
- Geneva AI governance startup ecosystem: several early-stage Geneva-based companies developing AI auditing, AI interpretability, and AI governance tools
- Swiss National Centre for Cybersecurity (NCSC, Geneva) — AI security incident response and AI vulnerability disclosure

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- AI safety governance framework for Geneva research institutions
- AI weapons governance advisory for Geneva diplomatic community
- AI governance tool commercialization advisory for Geneva startups
- EPFL/UNIGE AI research governance compliance advisory
- Geneva AI safety standards alignment (international standards: ISO/IEC 42001 AI MS, NIST AI RMF)
- Cross-border AI safety diplomacy advisory for Geneva missions/permanent representations
