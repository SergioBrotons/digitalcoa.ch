## 01 — ICP Language (EN + FR)

### English

**Executive director on the AI tool that changed how they work:**
"We deployed an AI tool for beneficiary data management. The tool worked well for eight months. Then the vendor updated the model. The output changed. We did not notice for six weeks. During that six weeks, we made decisions based on outputs that were not what we had evaluated. When we discovered the change, we had to go back and reconstruct what had happened. We could not fully do it."

**On what the board approved versus what is actually happening:**
"The board approved our AI governance approach eighteen months ago. Since then, we have added three new AI tools. None of them went through board approval. We did not tell the board because we did not think the additions were significant. Now we have a situation where what the board approved and what is actually happening are two different things."

**On the field staff and the AI tools they are using:**
"Field staff are using AI tools in ways we did not anticipate. Some of them are using consumer AI tools on their phones for work purposes. The data flows through their personal accounts. We do not have a way to see what is happening. We found out about a problem three months after it occurred."

**On what the donor questionnaire asked versus what we actually do:**
"The donor sent a questionnaire asking about our AI governance. The questions assumed a level of formality we do not have. We answered as best we could. I am not sure our answers were accurate. I am not sure they were inaccurate. I do not know what the donor will do with the information."

**On the nFADP data subject request that arrived:**
"A beneficiary made a data subject request under nFADP. The request asked us to explain how an AI system had made a decision about their case. We could not produce the explanation. The AI system that made the decision was no longer in the same configuration. We did not have documentation of what the system knew at the time."

**On the program close and what was left behind:**
"When the program closed, we had AI-generated analytical outputs that were still in use by the partner organization. The outputs had been produced by a model version that no longer exists. We could not update them. We could not tell the partner organization what had changed."

## 02 — Market Signals

**Signal 1: AI model version update creating silent decision gap with no detection mechanism**
AI model updates creating periods where decisions are made on unevaluated output. The gap is silent: the firm does not know it is happening. No detection mechanism exists at NGO scale.

**Signal 2: Board-approved AI governance diverging from actual AI practice without notification protocol**
Board approved AI governance that has diverged from actual AI practice. The divergence is not visible because there is no notification protocol for adding new AI tools between board reviews.

**Signal 3: Field staff consumer AI usage creating invisible data flows**
Field staff using consumer AI tools on personal devices for work purposes. Data flows through personal accounts outside organizational visibility. The usage is invisible until a problem surfaces.

**Signal 4: Donor AI questionnaire assumptions exceeding NGO documentation capacity**
Donor questionnaires assuming documentation that NGOs do not have. The assumption gap creates a response problem: accurate responses reveal gaps, favorable responses require claiming capacities that do not exist.

**Signal 5: nFADP data subject requests for AI decisions that cannot be explained**
nFADP requests for AI decision explanations arriving for decisions made by AI systems that have since been updated or replaced. The explanation cannot be produced because the system's state at decision time is not documented.

**Signal 6: Program-close AI output orphaning as structural obligation persistence**
AI-generated outputs persisting after program close without update capability or notification protocol. Partner organizations using outputs that have changed without awareness.

## 03 — Competitive Analysis

**Gap 1: AI version tracking protocol for NGO program delivery**
No protocol exists for tracking AI model versions across active programs and detecting when output has changed due to vendor updates. The protocol would need to be implementable at NGO scale with limited administrative capacity.

**Gap 2: Board-approved AI governance staleness detection mechanism**
No mechanism exists for detecting when board-approved AI governance frameworks have become stale relative to actual AI practice. The mechanism would need to trigger notification when new AI tools are added.

**Gap 3: Field staff consumer AI usage governance framework**
No framework exists for governing field staff consumer AI usage for work purposes. The framework would need to address data flows through personal accounts and limited IT capacity for monitoring.

**Gap 4: Donor AI questionnaire response support**
No service exists for helping NGOs respond to donor AI governance questionnaires in ways that are accurate to actual practice and adequate for donor requirements.

**Gap 5: nFADP AI decision explanation protocol for NGOs**
No protocol exists for producing AI decision explanations under nFADP when the AI system has been updated or replaced. The protocol would need to address version documentation requirements.

**Gap 6: Program-close AI output transfer and update protocol**
No protocol exists for managing AI-generated outputs at program close. The protocol would need to address version documentation, partner notification, and update capability.

## 04 — Compliance Context

**ZEWO certification and AI governance:**
ZEWO standards do not yet include specific AI governance requirements. NGOs seeking certification face AI governance questions they cannot yet answer from published standards.

**Donor AI conditions as de facto standards:**
Institutional donors imposing AI governance conditions that function as operational standards. Non-harmonized conditions arriving simultaneously require incompatible evidentiary formats.

**nFADP Articles 13, 15, and 21:**
AI-inferred beneficiary profiles activating Article 6 lawful basis questions. Automated decision explanations required under Article 13 for decisions made by AI systems that have changed. Article 21 AI decision documentation obligations accumulating without methodology.

**FADP registration for AI-augmented data processing:**
NGOs processing beneficiary data through AI systems may have FDPIC registration obligations that have not been assessed. The registration obligation exists regardless of awareness.

**Geneva humanitarian AI standards ecosystem:**
UNAIWG, ICRC, and OCHA frameworks developed in Geneva operating independently of Swiss regulatory review. Organizations following Geneva standards may be in unknown Swiss law compliance positions.

**AI vendor contract indemnification asymmetry:**
NGO vendor contracts indemnifying the vendor for NGO use with no reciprocal vendor obligation to indemnify the NGO. Downstream liability accepted without vendor counter-obligation.

## 06 — Findings Summary

**Finding 1: AI model version update propagation creating silent decision gap with no detection mechanism as named structural failure**
Vendor model updates creating periods where decisions are made on unevaluated output. The gap is silent and structural. No detection mechanism exists at NGO scale. The failure is named and discrete from prior version update findings.

**Finding 2: Board-approved AI governance divergence without notification protocol as invisible architecture decay**
Board-approved governance that has diverged from actual AI practice without any notification mechanism. The divergence is invisible until a board review or external audit triggers examination. This is distinct from staleness detection — this is the absence of any notification system for divergence.

**Finding 3: Field staff consumer AI usage through personal accounts as structurally ungovernable data flow**
Field staff using consumer AI tools on personal devices for work purposes creating data flows through personal accounts outside organizational visibility. The data flow is structurally ungovernable with existing NGO capacity and governance frameworks.

**Finding 4: Donor AI questionnaire design assumptions creating response paradox for under-documented NGOs**
Donor questionnaires designed against organizational AI governance assumptions that exceed NGO documentation capacity. The response paradox: accurate responses reveal gaps, favorable responses require claiming capacities that do not exist. Named as discrete problem distinct from prior questionnaire findings.

**Finding 5: nFADP data subject request for AI decision explanation where system state is not documented as present-tense compliance gap**
nFADP requests for AI decision explanations arriving for decisions made by AI systems no longer in the same configuration. The explanation cannot be produced because the system's state at decision time was not documented. Present-tense compliance gap named as distinct from prior nFADP findings.

**Finding 6: Program-close AI output orphaning with no update capability or partner notification protocol as structural obligation persistence**
AI-generated outputs persisting after program close with no update capability and no partner notification protocol. Partner organizations using outputs that have changed without awareness. The obligation persists after the funding ends and after the organizational relationship ends.
