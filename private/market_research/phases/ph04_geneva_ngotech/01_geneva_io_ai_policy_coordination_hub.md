# 01 — Geneva as International Organization AI Policy Coordination Hub
**Phase:** C147 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Geneva hosts the densest concentration of international organizations globally (~35 UN agencies, WTO, WHO, WTO, ILO, ITU, WIPO, UNHCR, ICRC, IFRC, Red Cross — all with AI policy mandates). These organizations are independently developing AI governance frameworks, creating redundancy, fragmentation, and coordination costs. Geneva is emerging as the de facto "AI policy coordination hub" for international organizations — a role no single entity currently owns.

## Evidence
- UN Secretary-General's AI Advisory Body (2024) — seated in New York, but Geneva UN agencies (UNEP, ITU, WHO, UNHCR) implementing
- ITU AI for Good Summit (Geneva, annual) — Geneva as AI for Development coordination platform
- WTO AI in trade working group — Geneva-based, developing AI trade governance
- WHO Digital Health Department (Geneva) — normative AI guidance for global health systems
- ICRC AI in humanitarian contexts policy (2025) — AI weapons/decision-support limits
- UN Geneva AI governance coordination attempts (Cross-Agency Working Group on AI, 2025) — early stage, understaffed
- Geneva International Disarmament Academy — AI weapons governance training

Advisory offerings:

- Cross-IO AI policy harmonization advisory

- Geneva IO AI procurement policy advisory
