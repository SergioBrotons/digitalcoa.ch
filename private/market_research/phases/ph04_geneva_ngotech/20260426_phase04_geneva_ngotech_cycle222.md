## 02 — Market Signals

1. **Donor AI contract conditions creating audit exposure** — NGOs signed AI-related donor conditions without the governance to satisfy them. Audit risk is real and present-tense.

2. **FADP beneficiary rights operational impossibility** — AI processing of beneficiary data creates obligations the NGO lacks capacity to fulfill at scale.

3. **Vendor term monitoring failure** — No NGO has a process for detecting when AI vendor terms change. Changes happen without organizational awareness.

4. **Board AI literacy deficit as governance failure** — Board members cannot evaluate what they are being told about AI. The governance conversation is managed, not informed.

5. **AI-generated proposal authenticity unresolved** — NGOs submitting AI-generated proposals without clarity on donor expectations or disclosure norms.

6. **Geneva AI knowledge production disconnected from NGO scale** — Frameworks designed for large organizations are not implementable at NGO scale without dedicated compliance staff.

## 06 — Findings Summary

### 6 Net-New Findings (Distinct from C214–C221 Phase 04)

1. **Donor AI contract conditions as audit exposure with no governance to satisfy** — The NGO signed conditions it cannot currently satisfy. The audit risk is present-tense, not hypothetical.

2. **FADP beneficiary rights operational impossibility is structural, not resource-based** — NGOs serve the exact population with enhanced data rights under FADP. The gap is architectural, not a matter of more resources.

3. **Vendor term monitoring failure at NGO scale creates silent exposure accumulation** — Without any monitoring process, vendor term changes create legal basis invalidation that accumulates without organizational awareness.

4. **Board AI literacy deficit is a governance failure, not a training problem** — Board members cannot evaluate what they are told about AI because the underlying governance question is unsolved, not because they lack information.

5. **AI-generated proposal authenticity is an unaddressed donor relationship risk** — The NGO's authenticity in donor relationships is being defined by AI tool vendors without organizational input or awareness.

6. **Framework proportionality gap is the structural market failure keeping NGOs from governance** — Geneva AI frameworks are designed for organizations with compliance infrastructure. The gap between framework ambition and NGO implementation capacity is the core problem no one is solving.
