# 02 — AI in Multilateral Humanitarian Logistics: Geneva ICRC/UNHCR Innovation
**Phase:** C147 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
The ICRC (International Committee of the Red Cross, Geneva) and UNHCR (Geneva) are deploying AI in humanitarian supply chain and logistics optimization — but at significantly higher risk than private sector deployments. AI failures in humanitarian contexts have life-or-death consequences. The humanitarian AI innovation gap (documented as 3-5 years behind private sector in C145/C146) is now producing specific AI governance needs around accountability, explainability, and failure modes in conflict zones.

## Evidence
- ICRC Innovation Hub (Geneva) piloting AI for conflict-affected population needs assessment and resource allocation
- UNHCR AI for refugee camp logistics optimization — AI-powered supply chain routing for humanitarian aid
- UNHR IED (Innovation, Data and Emerging Technology) team — AI deployment in humanitarian response
- Geneva Hackathon for Humanitarian AI (Oct 2025, hosted by ICRC + UNHCR + ITU) — 120+ teams, focus on AI accountability in humanitarian settings
- ICRC AI Policy (2025): requires human-in-the-loop for all AI decisions affecting civilian populations; no fully autonomous weapons AI support
- Donor pressure on humanitarian AI accountability — SIDA, EU DG ECHO requiring AI governance documentation for funded AI projects
