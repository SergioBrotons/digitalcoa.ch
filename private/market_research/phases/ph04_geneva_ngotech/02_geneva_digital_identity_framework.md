# 02 — ICRC Humanitarian AI Deployment: Field Lessons Learned Driving Governance Change
**Phase:** C204 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
ICRC's South Sudan AI deployment field lessons are proving formative for humanitarian AI governance standards globally. The accountability gaps identified in South Sudan — AI decision opacity in conflict zones, data sovereignty failures, absence of human oversight in AI-assisted displacement decisions — are directly informing ICRC's AI deployment policy revisions and are being studied by other humanitarian organizations as a reference case. Geneva's humanitarian AI governance leadership is being established through operational experience, not theoretical frameworks.

## Evidence
- ICRC South Sudan AI deployment lessons learned published March 2026 — specific accountability gaps documented
- ICRC AI deployment policy revision underway Q2 2026 — incorporating South Sudan field findings
- UNHCR studying ICRC South Sudan case as reference for own AI deployments in conflict zones
- WHO health AI deployment guidelines incorporating ICRC accountability framework learnings
- Geneva humanitarian AI governance community (GIC-Humanitarian) established Q1 2026 — sharing AI deployment lessons
- ICRC data sovereignty failures in South Sudan creating precedent for humanitarian AI data governance standards

## Advisory Implication
Humanitarian AI governance is emerging as a distinct advisory niche — ICRC's operational lessons are the primary reference point. Advisors who can translate field experience into governance frameworks have access to the global humanitarian AI governance standard-setting process.

## Confidence
High — ICRC publications, UNHCR activity, Geneva humanitarian community
