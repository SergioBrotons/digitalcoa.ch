# C205 Finding 01 — CERN AI Data Processing Ethics Framework 2026

**Phase:** 04 Geneva NGO/Tech | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

CERN published its first AI data processing ethics framework in 2026 — affecting Geneva-based AI research collaborations that use CERN's large hadron collider and computing data, with implications for AI training data provenance requirements and Geneva university AI partnerships.

## Key Details

- **Framework scope:** AI systems processing CERN experimental data and computing resources; AI training data provenance documentation required; AI model transparency requirements for CERN AI publications
- **Geneva university AI partnerships:** Geneva-based AI research collaborations with CERN (Université de Genève, EPFL) must comply with CERN AI ethics framework — AI data sharing agreements must include AI ethics provisions
- **AI training data provenance:** CERN framework requires documented provenance chain for AI training datasets — applies to AI models trained on CERN data; Geneva AI researchers must implement data documentation protocols
- **Technology transfer implications:** CERN AI framework affects commercial AI applications derived from CERN AI research — Geneva-based AI companies using CERN technology transfer must address AI ethics compliance
- **AI model transparency:** CERN AI publications must document AI methodology including training data, model architecture, and validation approach — increases reproducibility standards for Geneva AI research community
- **International recognition:** CERN AI ethics framework positioned as reference standard for large-scale scientific AI — Geneva as global scientific AI ethics reference point
