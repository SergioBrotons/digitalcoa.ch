# 06 — Geneva Academic AI Spin-Off Commercialization: EPFL/UNIGE Tech Transfer
**Phase:** C147 Phase 04 (Geneva NGO/Tech) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
EPFL (Lausanne, Geneva/Lemanic region) and UNIGE are generating AI spin-off companies at an accelerating rate. The tech transfer pathway from academic AI research to commercial deployment in Geneva is creating demand for: IP strategy, AI governance compliance, regulatory navigation, and corporate governance advisory for early-stage AI companies. This is distinct from CERN tech transfer (deep physics/engineering focus) and addresses the AI-specific commercialization gap.

## Evidence
- EPFL AI spin-offs: >15 AI-focused companies spun off in past 3 years; sector focus: health AI, fintech AI, energy AI, security AI
- UNIGE tech transfer office (PAF, Plateau de l'Innovation) — Geneva-based AI company support infrastructure
- Geneva-based AI startups in FONGIT, Hephaistos incubator, and Cltech accelerators — AI governance advisory need as companies scale
- Swiss Startup Label requirements and investor due diligence — AI governance increasingly a due diligence item for Series A+ funding
- Swiss Innovation Agency (Innosuisse) AI business development coaching — Geneva AI startups accessing Innosuisse support
- Geneva corporate venture arms (SwissRe, Nestlé Venture, Pictet) investing in Geneva AI startups — corporate governance requirements

## Advisory Implication
Advisory offerings:
- Geneva AI spin-off IP strategy and AI governance framework
- Innosuisse AI startup commercialization advisory
- Swiss Startup Label AI governance readiness assessment
- Series A+ AI startup due diligence support for Geneva VCs
- Geneva AI corporate venture governance advisory
- AI spin-off regulatory compliance roadmap (Swiss and EU markets)
