# C205 Finding 02 — Brotons.net Evergreen Content Refresh Strategy

**Phase:** 05 Brotons.net | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

20+ Brotons.net articles published in 2023-2024 contain outdated Geneva regulatory references and require factual updates to maintain authority — an evergreen content refresh program could recover suppressed search rankings and sustain reader trust.

## Key Details

- **Refresh necessity:** Articles on Geneva regulatory topics (nFADP, Swiss AI Act early drafts, FINMA guidance) from 2023-2024 now contain outdated references — regulatory evolution has made some articles factually stale
- **Evergreen shelf life:** Brotons.net's 12+ month readership data shows strong sustained interest in regulatory explainers; refresh updates maintain search ranking momentum rather than rebuilding from zero
- **Refresh scope:** Each article requires: regulatory update review, factual accuracy check against current regulatory text, updated CTA if service pathway has changed, date stamp update
- **Refresh priority framework:** Articles identified by: (1) regulatory topic stability — topics where regulation has materially changed, (2) traffic sustainability — articles still receiving search traffic, (3) authority risk — articles potentially damaging trust if facts are wrong
- **Estimated refresh workload:** 20+ articles × 2-3 hours each = 40-60 hours total refresh program; phased approach by priority
- **Refresh timing:** Regulatory refresh articles most valuable when regulatory developments are fresh — Q2 2026 ideal window before summer publishing slowdown

## Strategic Angle

- Evergreen refresh is higher-ROI than new content creation for existing high-traffic articles
- Refresh program maintains Brotons.net regulatory authority while new gap-cluster articles build new authority
- Factual accuracy directly protects trust signal architecture
