## 02 — Market Signals

What's happening in the Swiss AI governance information space. What's resonating. What's missing.

### Active Market Dynamics

**nFADP enforcement post-18-month reality.** The Federal Data Protection Information Commissioner (FDPIC) has now completed roughly 18 months of nFADP operational enforcement since the law's effective date. Firms that addressed compliance structurally in 2024 are discovering a second-order problem: the documentation they built for the first wave of requirements is inadequate for the substantive accountability questions now arriving. Not a policy gap — a methodology gap. The question moved from "do you have a policy" to "can you demonstrate what actually happened and why you made the decisions you made."

**Court activity outpacing published doctrine.** Geneva civil courts are handling AI-related procedural questions — privilege claims involving AI research queries, evidence authenticity for AI-generated documents, professional conduct questions arising from AI-assisted filings — without published resolutions. Practitioners in active matters are navigating judicial expectations that don't yet appear in any formal guidance. The informal circuit is carrying information about what specific courts are requiring, but this intelligence is fragmented and circulates only within personal networks.

**Federal Council post-deliberation framing gap.** The Federal Council's June 2026 AI policy deliberation will produce a directional statement. That statement will not resolve the accountability questions Geneva professional services principals face. The gap between regulatory direction and operational adequacy is the specific tension that Brotons.net readers are navigating — and no Geneva publication is addressing it with precision.

**Client AI audit requests becoming technically specific.** Counterparty and client AI governance questionnaires have progressed from generic framework questions (do you have an AI policy) to specific technical queries (which model version, what training data, what human review process, what is the reviewer's qualification). This second-generation questionnaire is arriving in Geneva now and finding firms structurally unprepared — not because they lack a policy, but because they cannot answer the technical questions being asked.

**Broker/dealer informal standard formation.** The insurance and banking intermediary community in Geneva is forming informal adequacy standards through market practice — which firms can obtain coverage, which attestation formats carriers accept, which questionnaire responses trigger additional scrutiny. This standard formation is operating below published doctrine and is more immediately consequential for practitioners than anything in regulatory guidance.

### Resonating Themes
- Personal liability discharge methodology — the question behind the question
- Documentation adequacy inversion — internal standard adequate, market-formed standard different
- Reassessment trigger architecture — the governance event nobody has named as an event

### What's Missing from the Market
- No Geneva publication translating court-level AI procedural intelligence into actionable practitioner guidance
- No analysis of what the Federal Council's post-deliberation statement will and will not resolve
- No bilingual substantive analysis serving the French-language professional community specifically
- No editorial voice addressing the accountability preparation problem with the precision practitioners need

This shift has implications for editorial tone — the audience responds to personal exposure framing more acutely than organizational compliance framing.
