**Title: The Geneva Accountability Architecture Has Five Levels. Most Publications Are Mapping Three.**

**Subtitle:** What the Federal AI Coordination Office decision actually determines — and why the answer shapes what every Geneva operator owes in the next eighteen months.

**Angle:** The Geneva accountability architecture is a five-level vertical structure, not a horizontal framework. Each level operates with different standards, different timing, different consequences. Most AI governance analysis maps three levels: regulatory authority, organizational policy, tool configuration. It misses two critical levels — the informal standard-setting circuit and the client counterparty expectation formation. The analysis that maps three levels produces incomplete governance recommendations.

The five levels:

1. **Federal AI Coordination Office** — the formal standard-setting level. The decision about what function it performs determines the operative standard for everything below it. This is the structural question that shapes all other levels.

2. **Cantonal supervisory community** — FINMA, SIC, and equivalent bodies forming implicit expectations through behavioral patterns. Not formal doctrine. Observable through what they accept, what they challenge, what they ignore.

3. **Geneva institutional ecosystem** — the informal standard-setting circuit. Decisions made in this level determine what adequate governance means before formal doctrine arrives. Not accessible to external writers. The intelligence remains in unpublished form.

4. **Carrier underwriting standards** — the insurance market's crystallization of accountability expectations. Fourth-generation evolution from process documentation to outcome attribution. The carrier standard is the most operationally concrete expression of accountability expectations.

5. **Client counterparty channels** — the expectation formation level. What clients begin to expect before they can articulate it. Emerging accountability standards that will become conventional before formal doctrine catches up.

**The documentation window close as burden-of-proof reversal:**
Before July 2026: building documentation evaluated against future standards. After: explaining why missing documentation was not built. The reversal is the structural event. Operators who understand the five-level architecture can position their documentation against the correct standard. Operators who map only three levels will build documentation against the wrong standard.

**Reader outcome:** The reader understands which five levels matter, which level is currently forming, which documentation window is closing, and what the burden-of-proof reversal means for their specific accountability exposure.
