# 02 — Brotons.net Evergreen vs. News-Driven Content Strategy
**Phase:** C147 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Brotons.net should optimize for evergreen content over news-driven content. The Geneva AI governance space rewards depth and framework thinking over breaking news. News-driven content has a short shelf life and requires high publishing frequency to stay relevant. Evergreen content (frameworks, interpretations, analysis of enduring questions) compounds in value over time, generates consistent search traffic, and supports return visitor strategy.

## Evidence
- Google SEO research: evergreen content generates 8x more traffic than news content after 6 months
- Brotons.net audience (executives, lawyers, consultants) search for solutions to problems, not news — problem-solving queries are evergreen
- Geneva AI governance is an emerging field with many foundational questions still unresolved — these are evergreen topics (e.g., "how should a Geneva law firm govern AI use?", "what does the Swiss AI Convention mean for multinationals?")
- Content refresh cycles: evergreen content can be refreshed annually; news content requires weekly attention
- Internal linking value: evergreen content allows systematic internal linking architecture over time; news content becomes stale quickly
- SEO indexing: Google ranks sites with consistent, high-quality evergreen content higher for commercial queries

## Implication for Brotons.net
Content framework:
- 70% evergreen (frameworks, guides, interpretations, analysis of regulatory implications)
- 20% semi-evergreen (Geneva institutional developments with lasting relevance)
- 10% timely commentary (breaking Geneva AI governance news requiring rapid response)

Each piece of evergreen content should have a "canonical" version that is updated periodically, with news-driven content linking back to canonical evergreen pieces.
