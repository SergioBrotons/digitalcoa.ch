## 02 — Market Signals

### What's happening in the Swiss AI governance information space

**AI tool vendor deprecation as a documentation continuity event arriving now.** AI reporting tools, portfolio analytics platforms, and proposal generation systems are being deprecated by vendors — vendor exits, product discontinuations, model sunsets. For Geneva boutiques that adopted AI tools between 2023 and 2026 without vendor continuity planning, the deprecation creates a specific documentation event: the client reports, engagement records, and communications generated by that tool are in active client files, may be referenced in ongoing advisory relationships, and may be subject to client request or regulatory examination. The tool that generated the record no longer exists in its operating form. No Geneva boutique has a protocol for what vendor deprecation of an AI tool requires in terms of documentation continuity management, client file integrity, or the ability to respond to questions about reports generated by a system that no longer operates.

**Client AI sophistication inversion creating a new class of unprepared questions.** Clients who have hired dedicated AI leadership — heads of AI, AI governance officers — are beginning to ask the questions that Geneva consulting firms and professional services firms are not prepared to answer in client-facing contexts: data residency and cross-border data flows, model version history and update protocols, training data separation and client confidentiality at the infrastructure level, vendor financial stability and tool continuity commitments, what happens to client information when an engagement closes. These questions are arriving from the most sophisticated clients first, then diffusing downward through the market. The inversion is structural: the client's AI maturity has outpaced the firm's AI transparency preparation. The firm is improvising responses in real time in client-facing contexts where uncertainty is professionally costly.

**Proposal version archaeology creating retrospective accountability exposure.** AI proposal tools log full version history — every iteration, every direction tested, every framing considered and abandoned. For a boutique consulting firm, this version record now makes the recommendation development process transparent in a way it never was before the AI tool. A client can reconstruct which recommendation directions were considered and dropped, and ask why the firm moved from framing A to framing B. The boutique must answer for a decision process that may not have been documented at the time — not because it was negligent, but because the version transparency arrived with the AI tool, not before it. The accountability surface has expanded without the documentation architecture expanding to match it.

**PI renewal attestation creating unquantified personal liability through unchecked boxes.** The carrier AI attestation on PI renewal forms is structurally miscalibrated for boutique scale: managing directors are personally signing confirmations of AI review process adequacy with no reference methodology, no Geneva professional community consensus on what "adequate" means at boutique scale, and no carrier-level validation of what the attestation actually requires. The box is checked. The personal signature is real. The exposure is unquantified. The gap between the attestation's legal weight and the signer's understanding of what they are confirming is the specific personal liability problem that Geneva principals face — and that no Geneva publication has named precisely enough to be useful.

**Sub-consultant AI infrastructure creating an undocumented three-party confidentiality chain.** Specialist sub-consultants engaged on boutique engagements are operating their own AI tools under different vendor terms, different data residency arrangements, and different retention policies than the boutique's own infrastructure. Client information enters the sub-consultant's AI system under a vendor relationship the boutique has not evaluated and cannot govern — yet the boutique has contractual confidentiality obligations to the client that now extend implicitly to a third-party AI infrastructure. The chain is structurally ungoverned and undocumentable at boutique scale without specific protocol.
