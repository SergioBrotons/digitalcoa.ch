## 05 — Editorial Content Draft

### English

**The Geneva Courts AI Decision Record: What We Know and What We Don't**

There is no published record of how Geneva courts are treating AI in contested matters. Attorneys are operating on anecdotal evidence. The anecdotal record is not reliable. This is a structural information failure that the Geneva legal profession has not addressed.

**What we know:**

Geneva courts are seeing AI-assisted filings. Attorneys are using AI for legal research, document drafting, and case preparation. Some attorneys are using AI in ways that create procedural questions. The courts have not published guidance on any of these questions.

The first contested motion on AI-related procedural questions in each Geneva court is creating the local standard by response — not by published opinion. The attorneys involved do not know they are creating precedent. The precedent is forming silently.

**What we do not know:**

We do not know how specific Geneva courts are treating the question of AI contribution to procedural filings. We do not know how courts are evaluating the adequacy of AI-assisted legal research. We do not know what disclosure obligations courts are imposing on attorneys who use AI in contested matters.

We do not know whether any Geneva court has addressed the question of AI-generated evidence or AI-assisted witness preparation. We do not know whether the courts are treating AI as a tool that augments attorney work product or as a separate source that requires independent verification.

**What Brotons.net is doing:**

Brotons.net is beginning systematic tracking of Geneva courts AI decisions. This is the first publication in the Geneva market to treat the court record as a beat rather than an absence.

The tracking will be published as a running record, updated as decisions emerge. Where decisions create precedent, the precedent will be analyzed. Where decisions leave questions open, that too will be noted.

The Geneva courts AI decision record is not being tracked anywhere else. Brotons.net will track it.
