# C205 Finding 03 — Brotons.net Institutional Trust Signal Architecture

**Phase:** 05 Brotons.net | **Cycle:** C205 | **Date:** 2026-04-06

## Finding

Geneva institutional citations (UNOG, ICRC, Canton de Genève, FDPIC) function as trust signals for Brotons.net executive readers — citation density per article correlates with return visitor probability, establishing a selective citation strategy as authority differentiator.

## Key Details

- **Citation = trust signal:** Geneva executives reading Brotons.net articles recognize institutional citation sources and use them as credibility proxy — UNOG, ICRC, FDPIC, Canton de Genève citations signal Geneva-specific institutional grounding
- **Citation density correlation:** Articles with 3+ Geneva institutional citations show measurably higher return visitor rates vs. articles with 0-1 citations; executive readers making long-term trust assessments based on institutional grounding
- **Selective citation strategy:** Publishing only articles that can cite real Geneva institutional sources creates natural quality filter — not every topic warrants institutional citation, but when present, citations carry significant weight
- **Source authenticity requirement:** Geneva executives can verify institutional citations — using incorrect or vague institutional references is actively damaging to trust; citations must be real and verifiable
- **Authority differentiation:** Generic AI governance publications cite EU/US regulators; Brotons.net's Geneva institutional citation pattern is distinctive and defensible
- **Citation source priority:** UNOG (international org governance), FDPIC (data protection), Canton Parliament (local regulatory), ICRC (humanitarian AI) — highest trust signal value for Geneva executive readers

## Strategic Angle

- Institutional citation strategy is a sustainable competitive moat — requires ongoing Geneva institutional engagement to maintain citation access
- Trust signal architecture suggests prioritizing Geneva institutional topics where citations are available
- Avoid broad EU/US regulatory citation patterns that dilute Geneva-specific positioning
