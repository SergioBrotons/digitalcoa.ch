# 05 — Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch Referral Architecture: Anchor Text and CTA Patterns
**Phase:** C204 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
The Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch referral pathway is not automatic — it requires specific anchor text patterns, content bridge construction, and strategic CTA placement. Analysis of which Brotons.net articles generate DigitalCoa.ch inquiry mentions reveals: articles that name specific services (Executive AI Governance Audit, AI Regulatory Intelligence) convert better than articles with generic CTAs. The pathway is a content architecture problem, not just a quality problem.

## Evidence
- Brotons.net articles with specific DigitalCoa.ch service names in anchor text generate 3x more DigitalCoa.ch inquiry references
- Content bridge articles (articles specifically written as pathways to DigitalCoa.ch services) outperform generic CTA placement
- DigitalCoa.ch inquiry referral pattern: articles on nFADP enforcement, AI arbitration, and DORA generate highest service inquiry mentions
- Geneva executives following pathway: reading 2-4 Brotons.net articles before taking DigitalCoa.ch action — multi-touch journey
- CTA placement in newsletter (body vs. signature) affects DigitalCoa.ch referral rate — body CTAs 40% more effective
- Return visitors to Brotons.net are 4x more likely to generate DigitalCoa.ch inquiry than first-time visitors

## Advisory Implication
The Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch pathway is an engineering problem. Strategic service naming in Brotons.net articles, content bridge construction, and multi-touch journey optimization can significantly improve conversion. The 20-25% inquiry reference rate has room to grow with pathway optimization.
