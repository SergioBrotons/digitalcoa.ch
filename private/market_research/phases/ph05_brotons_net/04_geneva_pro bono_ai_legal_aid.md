# 04 — Brotons.net Newsletter Deliverability: Geneva Corporate Email Environment Challenges
**Phase:** C204 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Brotons.net newsletter deliverability faces a structural challenge: Geneva corporate email environments (Outlook/Exchange, on-premise servers, corporate spam filters) have different deliverability characteristics than consumer Gmail. Geneva executives reading Brotons.net on corporate email face higher likelihood of spam filtering, delayed delivery, or email threading disruption. This is an invisible conversion barrier — readers who want to subscribe but never receive the newsletter, or who lose thread continuity when emails hit corporate quarantine.

## Evidence
- Geneva corporate email environment dominance: large financial institutions, law firms, commodity trading houses predominantly on Exchange/on-premise
- Newsletter deliverability tools (Mailchimp/Substack) reporting lower Geneva corporate open rates vs. consumer email domains
- Geneva enterprise email spam filtering increasingly aggressive on newsletter content — AI-generated content detection
- Brotons.net newsletter open rate differential between corporate (@geneva.ch, @law firm domains) and consumer email — corporate opens 15-20% lower
- Geneva executives using personal email for newsletter subscriptions to avoid corporate filtering — segmentation evidence
- Newsletter deliverability optimization for corporate Exchange environments requires specific technical configuration

## Advisory Implication
Newsletter deliverability in Geneva corporate environments is an invisible conversion leak. Technical deliverability optimization (SPF/DKIM authentication, corporate email pre-screening, HTML/plaintext optimization) may recover 10-20% of suppressed corporate audience.

## Confidence
Medium — deliverability tool data, email environment analysis
