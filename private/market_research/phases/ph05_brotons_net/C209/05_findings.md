# Finding 05 | C209 | Brotons.net

## Topic
Brotons.net institutional citation strategy — building inbound links from Swiss institutional sources

## EN
Brotons.net is developing an active institutional citation strategy to build inbound links from Swiss institutional sources — distinct from the passive approach of waiting for institutional sources to naturally reference Brotons.net content. The strategy focuses on three mechanisms: first, primary source citation — where Brotons.net references Swiss institutional sources (Federal Council reports, FINMA guidance, FDPIC decisions) in its content, it actively notifies the issuing institution (via appropriate contact channels) that its work has been cited and contextualised by Brotons.net, creating awareness without directly requesting links; second, institutional resource linking — where Brotons.net creates or updates AI governance resource pages that aggregate Swiss institutional AI documents, it invites relevant institutions to review and validate the accuracy of Brotons.net's descriptions, creating natural relationship-building touchpoints; third, institutional newsletter cross-promotion — where Brotons.net covers Geneva cantonal or federal AI governance developments in depth, it offers to feature the relevant institutional communications (with editorial independence preserved) in exchange for consideration of reciprocal link acknowledgment. Early indicators suggest that Swiss institutional actors are receptive to citation notification — several Geneva cantonal bodies have begun following Brotons.net on LinkedIn and have shared Brotons.net AI governance analysis in internal institutional communications, driving both referral traffic and domain authority improvements.

## FR
Brotons.net développe une stratégie active de citation institutionnelle pour construire des liens entrants depuis les sources institutionnelles suisses — distincte de l'approche passive d'attendre que les sources institutionnelles citent naturellement le contenu Brotons.net. La stratégie se concentre sur trois mécanismes: premièrement, citation de source primaire; deuxièmement, liaison de ressources institutionnelles; troisièmement, cross-promotion de newsletter institutionnelle. Les premiers indicateurs suggèrent que les acteurs institutionnels suisses sont réceptifs à la notification de citation — plusieurs organismes cantonaux genevois ont commencé à suivre Brotons.net sur LinkedIn et ont partagé l'analyse de gouvernance IA Brotons.net dans leurs communications institutionnelles internes, entraînant à la fois du trafic de référence et des améliorations de l'autorité de domaine.
