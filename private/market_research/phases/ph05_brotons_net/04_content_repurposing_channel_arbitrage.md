# 04 — Brotons.net Content Repurposing and Channel Arbitrage Strategy
**Phase:** C147 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Each Brotons.net long-form content piece should be systematically repurposed across multiple formats and channels to maximize return on editorial investment. The Geneva AI governance content is well-suited for multi-format distribution: the audience consumes content differently on LinkedIn (short-form), via email newsletter (curated digest), and on Brotons.net (deep analysis). Channel arbitrage — publishing content first on Brotons.net then adapting for distribution channels — creates compounding reach.

## Evidence
- Content marketing research: 3x the reach for content repurposed across 3+ formats vs. single-format publishing
- LinkedIn organic reach for B2B professional content: short-form (3-5 sentences) + link outperforms long-form posts for Geneva executives
- Geneva professional LinkedIn culture: highly active, algorithmically favors original content with institutional framing
- YouTube/audio opportunity: Geneva professionals commute (Gare de Genève, CFF routes) — podcast/audio version of Brotons.net analysis suits commute consumption
- Geneva newsletter culture: Swiss professionals check email morning/evening — newsletter distribution timing matters for open rates
- Repurposing sequence: long-form article → LinkedIn post series → newsletter excerpt → podcast script → quote/insight pull

## Implication for Brotons.net
Content repurposing workflow:
- Every long-form Brotons.net article: minimum 3 repurposed outputs (LinkedIn post, newsletter section, quote/insight graphic)
- LinkedIn-first distribution of Geneva AI governance commentary — builds professional credibility and drives newsletter sign-ups
- Podcast/audio: "Brotons.net Commentary" short (8-12 min) format covering one Geneva AI governance development
- Internal linking between repurposed content maintains SEO value
