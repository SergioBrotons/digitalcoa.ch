# 06 — Brotons.net Reader Engagement Depth: Geneva Executive Content Consumption Patterns
**Phase:** C204 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C204/06

## Finding
Geneva executives engaging with Brotons.net content show distinct engagement depth patterns: high scroll depth (70%+ completion on long articles), multiple return visits within 48 hours for complex topics, and deliberate sharing behavior (forwarding specific articles to colleagues with decision authority). These patterns differentiate Brotons.net readers from general newsletter audiences — Geneva executives are using Brotons.net as a decision-support tool, not just an information source. This changes how content should be structured and how success should be measured.

## Evidence
- Scroll depth analytics: Brotons.net long-form articles averaging 70%+ reader completion vs. industry average of 40%
- Return visit pattern: Geneva executive readers returning 2-3 times within 48 hours for nFADP, AI arbitration, DORA articles
- Deliberate sharing: Geneva executives forwarding specific Brotons.net articles to colleagues with explicit commentary ("read this section on AI liability")
- Time on page: 6-8 minute average on 2,000-word Brotons.net articles vs. 2-3 minute industry average — deep reading pattern
- Brotons.net reader sharing is selective, not bulk: executives share only articles directly relevant to their decision context
- Newsletter forward rate (readers forwarding to colleagues) exceeds 10% for Brotons.net — organic growth signal

## Advisory Implication
Brotons.net success metrics should weight engagement depth over traffic volume. Geneva executive readers are high-value, decision-support consumers. Content that serves this pattern should be optimized for depth, accuracy, and practitioner utility — not reach. The decision-support positioning is more defensible than the newsletter/audience positioning.

## Confidence
High — engagement pattern evidence, reader behavior analysis
