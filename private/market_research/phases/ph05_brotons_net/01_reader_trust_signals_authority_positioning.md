# 01 — Brotons.net Reader Trust Signals and Authority Positioning
**Phase:** C147 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Brotons.net operates in a category (Geneva AI governance, strategic commentary) where reader trust is the primary conversion mechanism. Trust signals — author credibility, source transparency, methodology disclosure, and consistent quality — determine whether readers return, subscribe to the newsletter, and eventually become DigitalCoa.ch inquiries. Most Geneva-focused publications fail on trust signals even when content quality is high.

## Evidence
- Geneva professional audience (lawyers, executives, consultants, NGO staff) highly sensitive to source credibility — institutional affiliations, prior publications, track record
- Academic studies on B2B thought leadership trust: cited sources and methodology transparency are top-2 trust factors for executive readers
- Brotons.net positioning (Geneva AI governance lens) has no direct competitor with strong authority signals — gap exists
- LinkedIn research on B2B content trust: brand familiarity + author credibility explain 67% of B2B reader trust decisions
- Geneva publications (Le Temps, Genève Innovation) lack AI governance-specific depth — Brotons.net has content advantage but may underinvest in trust signal amplification

## Implication for Brotons.net
Priority trust signal investments:
- Author byline with credentials, institutional affiliation, prior relevant publications
- Source citation protocol (inline links to primary sources, not just general references)
- Methodology note on how content is produced (frequency, sources, editorial review)
- Publication date prominence — content freshness signals for Geneva AI governance time-sensitive topics
- Institutional endorsement display (if Geneva institutions reference or link to Brotons.net)

Publishing discipline (identified in C145/C146) must be paired with trust signal discipline.
