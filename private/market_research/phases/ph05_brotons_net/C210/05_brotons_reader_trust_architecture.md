## Finding

Brotons.net should systematically build reader trust through a credibility architecture: (1) author credentials displayed on every article — role, institutional affiliation, AI governance expertise, (2) source transparency — every factual claim linked to primary sources (FINMA circulars, court rulings, legislative texts), (3) update tracking — articles clearly dated and flagged when Swiss regulatory context has changed, (4) methodology disclosure — editorial standards and how Brotons.net handles conflicts of interest. This architecture addresses the growing practitioner skepticism toward AI content online and positions Brotons.net as a trusted reference in an environment where AI-generated noise is increasing. The approach differs from institutional citation strategy (C209) by focusing on on-site credibility signals rather than off-site citation building.
