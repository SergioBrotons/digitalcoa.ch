## 02 — Market Signals

What's happening in the Swiss AI governance information space. What's resonating. What's missing.

### Active Market Dynamics

**The documentation adequacy inversion: guidance exists, operative standard unformed.** Geneva professional services firms are discovering that regulatory guidance on AI governance exists in sufficient quantity to create false confidence. The problem is not the absence of guidance — it is that guidance describes what firms should assess, not what adequate assessment looks like operationally. A firm with a documented AI governance policy and a firm that can demonstrate substantive review adequacy are operating in different territories. The gap between those territories is not visible until examination. This is the documentation adequacy inversion: believing you are addressing a defined question when the definition is still forming.

**FDPIC as pattern-enforcing institution, not rule-setting institution.** The market treats FDPIC as a regulatory guidance body and structures its information consumption accordingly — reading published guidance, waiting for clarification, monitoring for updates. This reading method misses the institution's actual function. FDPIC operationalizes enforcement through pattern — behavioral triggers, remediation formats accepted, settlement conditions reached. These operational patterns do not follow from published doctrine; they precede it. A firm reading FDPIC as a guidance body will systematically misread what FDPIC enforcement looks like in practice, because practice formation and doctrine publication are different events happening at different times with different evidentiary status.

**Geneva civil procedure AI authentication standard forming through unpublished procedural events.** When a Geneva civil court questions the authenticity of an AI-generated document submitted in proceedings, when a party challenges the procedural status of an AI-assisted filing, when a judge asks for the model version that produced a submitted analysis — these are not published procedural standards. They are procedural events that create precedent for the practitioners involved and circulate through the informal circuit. The authentication problem for AI-generated content in Geneva civil proceedings has no published resolution. What exists is a series of unpublished procedural moments where practitioners discovered the question was live.

**Federal Council June 2026 deliberation: institutional responsibility ambiguity as the structural outcome.** The Federal Council's forthcoming deliberation on Swiss AI governance policy is not primarily a question about AI regulation. It is a question about institutional responsibility: which Swiss institution oversees what, how international AI governance frameworks interface with Swiss institutional architecture, how responsibility allocates across FDFA, EFV, BK, and the supervisory authorities. The deliberation's most important consequence will not be what it resolves — it will be what institutional responsibility questions it leaves open. For Geneva professional services firms, the allocation of supervisory responsibility determines which institution's expectations they are preparing to meet.

**Geneva AI governance documentation archaeology period boundary closing.** AI tools deployed in 2023 and 2024 are now past the point where their configuration states can be reconstructed from active records. Model versions have updated, tool configurations have drifted, staff who configured systems have departed, vendor platforms have changed. The 2023-2026 period is now a bounded documentation archaeology problem: firms cannot reconstruct what they deployed and how it functioned, but they are increasingly accountable for it. The market has not named this as a distinct problem with a distinct remediation architecture.

### Resonating Themes
- The FDPIC as pattern-enforcing institution — not guidance consumption but examination preparation
- The period boundary closing on 2023-2024 AI configuration reconstruction
- The institutional architecture question underlying the Federal Council deliberation
- The documentation adequacy inversion where guidance exists but operative standard is unformed

### What's Missing from the Market
- No Geneva publication is reading FDPIC as a pattern-enforcing institution and providing the operational intelligence methodology
- No bilingual analysis of the 2023-2026 documentation archaeology problem as a bounded, dateable, actionable category
- No analysis of the Federal Council deliberation as institutional architecture question (not regulatory question)
- No mapping of the EU AI Act service provider classification pressure arriving via Swiss firms' EU client relationships independently of any Swiss domestic regulatory determination
