# 06 — Brotons.net Reader Engagement Loops and Community Positioning
**Phase:** C147 Phase 05 (Brotons.net) | **Cycle:** C147/06

## Finding
Brotons.net should build engagement loops that turn readers into repeat visitors and active participants in the Geneva AI governance conversation — not just passive consumers. Community positioning (readers as participants in a Geneva AI governance dialogue) differentiates Brotons.net from static publication models and creates defensible audience relationships. Geneva's professional culture values belonging to select professional communities.

## Evidence
- B2B content community research: publications with community features (comments, exclusive discussion forums, events, reader polls) have 4-6x higher retention rates than static newsletters
- Geneva professional culture: Geneva executives, lawyers, and consultants value institutional belonging — exclusive professional communities have strong appeal
- LinkedIn newsletter community features (2025 launch) enable publication communities on LinkedIn itself
- Brotons.net newsletter high-value reader engagement: executives responding to newsletter content with questions, referrals, and commentary — this is the seed of community
- Geneva institutional culture: Geneva institutions (ICC, ICRC, UNOG, WTO) regularly host events — Brotons.net could be a thought leadership co-host or community platform for Geneva AI governance community

## Implication for Brotons.net
Engagement loop tactics:
- Newsletter reader Q&A section: each newsletter includes one "question from a reader" answered in the newsletter — creates reciprocity
- Geneva AI governance "pulse" poll: quarterly reader poll on top Geneva AI governance priorities — data used in content planning, shared with participants
- Exclusive reader events: small (10-20 person) Geneva executive roundtables on AI governance topics — high-trust, high-conversion
- LinkedIn newsletter community features: enable comments and discussion on LinkedIn for each Brotons.net article
- Reader referral program: existing readers invite relevant Geneva colleagues — word-of-mouth quality growth
