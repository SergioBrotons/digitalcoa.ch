# Ideal Customer Profile — Phase 01 Geneva Legal — Cycle 53

## Primary ICP: Small Law Firm Decision-Maker

### Firmographics
- **Size:** 1-20 lawyers / 1-50 employees
- **Location:** Geneva, Vaud, Zurich (Tier 1: Geneva)
- **Type:** Independent law firm, not BigLaw or boutique enterprise
- **Revenue context:** SMB economics, not enterprise billing
- **Practice areas:** Corporate, employment, data protection, M&A (not litigation-heavy)

### Psychographic Triggers (C53 Updated)
1. **Using AI informally** — 70% of legal professionals (C52); 90%+ at individual level
2. **No formal AI policy** — 43% of firms have no policy + no plans (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026)
3. **12% planning soon** (C53 NEW) — actively considering, not yet committed
4. **Swiss-specific caution** — Deloitte CH confirms Swiss firms more cautious; CLOUD Act + professional confidentiality concerns
5. **Client trust concern** — AI governance as competitive differentiator emerging
6. **Geneva exposure** — serving international/HNW clients with AI expectations

### Technology Profile
- Using ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini for research, drafting, summarization, correspondence (primary tasks confirmed C50)
- Awareness of Swiss-hosted alternatives (FABIAN/CASUS) emerging but not yet formalized
- 38% save 1-5h/week — efficiency case clear but governance absent
- 42% using legal-specific AI tools (doubled, C52)

### Decision-Making Profile
- Solo/small firm managing partner or practice head
- NOT technology decision-maker by training — legal background primarily
- Aware of AI importance but lacks framework for formal governance
- Receptive to efficiency framing before regulatory framing
- Geneva Bar Association member — self-regulatory guidelines (June 2024) not mandatory = awareness gap

### Pain Hierarchy (C53 Updated)
1. **Client data confidentiality + FADP exposure** — using non-Swiss AI with client data
2. **No framework for AI decisions** — 43% no policy; 12% planning but without governance structure
3. **CLOUD Act uncertainty** — US-based AI tools + Swiss client confidentiality
4. **Enforcement timing risk** — "expected to comply" without enforcement yet
5. **Efficiency vs. risk tradeoff** — 190h/year saved but 36pt governance gap
6. **C53 NEW: Training data rights** — FADP Art. 26 means client data used for AI training requires disclosure

### Urgency Signals
- 12% planning AI adoption "soon" (C53 NEW) — pre-adoption intercept window
- Three timelines running: FADP NOW + May 2026 + end-2026
- 90% forecast 2027 (law firms routinely incorporating AI)
- Geneva LegalTech hotspot active (awareness events)

### Qualification Questions
1. Do you know what AI tools your lawyers are currently using for client work?
2. Does your firm have a written AI policy or usage guidelines?
3. Are you aware of your FADP obligations as a data controller when using AI?
4. Do you know if client data processed through AI tools is used for model training?
5. Are your AI tools Swiss-hosted or EU-hosted for data residency compliance?
