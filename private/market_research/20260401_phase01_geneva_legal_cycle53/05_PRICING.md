# Pricing Framework — Phase 01 Geneva Legal — Cycle 53

## SMB Law Firm Pricing Context
- 1-20 lawyers; annual fees typically CHF 5,000-50,000 for compliance services
- Geneva SMB economics: manageablescope, not enterprise budgets
- Efficiency ROI already quantified: 190h/year = substantial value if governance enables fuller AI adoption

## Proposed Entry Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1: Shadow AI Audit (Decision 0) — Entry
- **Purpose:** Inventory + risk identification; opens relationship
- **Deliverable:** Written AI inventory report + FADP exposure summary
- **Target:** Managing partner / practice head
- **Timeline:** 2-3 weeks
- **Indicative range:** CHF 1,500-3,500 (one-time)
- **Rationale:** Low-risk commitment; Decision 0 as required first step; efficiency angle

### Tier 2: FADP Compliance Gap Assessment
- **Purpose:** Full FADP stack assessment (Art. 5(c)/16/21/26 + DPIA)
- **Deliverable:** Written gap analysis + prioritized remediation roadmap
- **Timeline:** 4-6 weeks
- **Indicative range:** CHF 3,500-7,500 (one-time)
- **Rationale:** FADP-specific; dual Art. 21 + Art. 26 compliance (C53 NEW framing); justifies premium over generic compliance

### Tier 3: AI Governance Framework (Annual Retainer)
- **Purpose:** Ongoing governance: policy development, staff training, annual review, FADP monitoring
- **Deliverable:** Policy documentation, training materials, quarterly reviews, regulatory monitoring
- **Timeline:** Annual retainer
- **Indicative range:** CHF 2,000-4,000/year (retainer)
- **Rationale:** Three timelines running (C53 refined); ongoing monitoring value; May 2026 + end-2026 touchpoints

### Geneva-Specific Pricing Angles (C53)
- Swiss-specific caution driver: premium for Swiss-sovereign expertise justified
- 12% near-term pipeline: early engagement discounts to capture pre-adoption cohort
- Client trust positioning: justify governance investment as competitive differentiator, not just compliance
