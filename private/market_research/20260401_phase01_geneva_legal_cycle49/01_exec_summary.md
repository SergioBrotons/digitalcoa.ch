## Net-New Findings (Cycle 49)

### ⭐ CONFIRMED: FDPIC Enhanced Enforcement Powers — Law Firms Explicitly In Scope
**Source:** FDPIC official guidance (edoeb.admin.ch); CMS Law AI Regulation Scanner; Shearman/AO Shearman; JDSupra; White & Case AI Watch

- **FADP directly applicable to AI:** FDPIC has explicitly confirmed the revised FADP (in force Sep 1, 2023) is technology-neutral and applies to all forms of AI-supported data processing — no new AI-specific law required
- **Law firms explicitly in scope:** FDPIC guidance applies cross-sector; law firms handling client personal data are directly subject to FADP obligations
- **Enhanced enforcement powers confirmed:** FDPIC can now issue binding orders to modify, suspend, or discontinue data processing; impose significant fines and sanctions
- **FADP enforcement pattern:** FDPIC issued enforcement decisions against X/Twitter (March 2025), Coop (January 2026), and a joint statement with 60+ DPAs (February 2026) — enforcement timing risk confirmed across sectors
- **Law firm enforcement status:** No confirmed FDPIC enforcement decisions against law firms specifically — enforcement absent but powers confirmed = timing risk (stronger urgency framing than "no enforcement")
- **Key obligations for law firms:**
  - Transparency: disclose purpose, functionality, data sources of AI processing
  - Right to know: clients have right to know if they're communicating with AI
  - Privacy by Design and Default: data protection from earliest planning stages
  - DPIA: required for high-risk AI processing
  - Prohibited: comprehensive real-time facial recognition, "social scoring"
  - Art. 21: right to human review of automated decisions producing legal effects
