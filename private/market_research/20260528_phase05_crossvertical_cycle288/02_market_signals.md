# Cross-Vertical Market Signals — Phase 05 C288
# What's failing simultaneously across all four ICPs

## Market Signal 1: The Carrier Attestation Acceleration

**Signal:** All four verticals experiencing third-generation carrier questionnaires with evidence requirements exceeding published standards.

**Legal:** PI carriers now asking for specific AI tool configurations, model version tracking, and reassessment protocols — documentation no Geneva law firm has systematically built.

**Finance:** D&O and professional liability carriers probing AI governance adequacy with questions referencing standards that don't appear in any published Swiss regulatory guidance.

**Consulting:** Professional liability carriers requesting methodology documentation for AI-assisted deliverables — firms cannot produce coherent methodology records.

**Healthcare:** Malpractice carriers adding AI-specific questions to renewal applications — practitioners answering without understanding what answers commit them to.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** Carrier iteration velocity is now the de facto standard-setting mechanism. Each renewal cycle produces sharper questions. Professionals who renew without building documented positions are accumulating exposure between cycles.

## Market Signal 2: Client Counterparty Convergence

**Signal:** Same institutional counterparty types sending structurally identical AI governance questionnaires across all four verticals simultaneously.

**Legal:** Corporate clients conducting AI due diligence before matter acceptance — same questionnaire sent to all Panel firms.

**Finance:** Institutional investors requesting AI governance documentation from asset managers — same framework applied across the sector.

**Consulting:** Corporate clients requiring AI methodology disclosure as part of RFP evaluation — same questions to all competing boutiques.

**Healthcare:** Hospital systems and insurance providers requesting AI documentation from independent practitioners — same credentialing requirements.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** Counterparty convergence means the first organizations to respond intentionally to AI governance questionnaires will shape the standard for their peer group. Uncoordinated responses are setting the benchmark. Responding strategically — not just filling out the form — creates competitive advantage and standard-setting influence.

## Market Signal 3: Documentation Archaeology Problem

**Signal:** All four verticals discovering that AI governance decisions from 2023–2024 are now permanently unreconstructable.

**Legal:** Client matters from 2022–2024 with AI-assisted drafting but no configuration record — original decision-maker departed, tool configuration changed, memory faded.

**Finance:** Investment recommendations made under AI models that have since been updated — recommendation provenance cannot be reconstructed.

**Consulting:** Proposals and deliverables from 2023–2024 with AI contribution but no methodology documentation — boutique cannot explain what AI did or how.

**Healthcare:** Treatment plans and diagnostic records from AI-assisted consultations where the AI tool configuration at the time is unknown and unrecoverable.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** The documentation archaeology problem is structural, not fixable by better intentions. The original documentation wasn't built because the obligation hadn't been named. The window for building contemporaneous records has closed for the past period. The only intervention available is building evidence architecture for current and future AI decisions — with the understanding that those decisions will face the same problem in 2–3 years if not addressed now.

## Market Signal 4: Vendor Contract Signature Without Evaluation

**Signal:** Across all four verticals, professionals signing AI vendor terms without reading, understanding, or evaluating against professional obligations.

**Legal:** Legal software AI features activated with terms that override attorney professional duties — no law firm has evaluated these terms against bar obligations.

**Finance:** Financial data and analytics platforms adding AI features under terms that disclaim liability for investment advice — no compliance officer has reviewed these against FINMA obligations.

**Consulting:** Proposal and document tools updating terms to cover AI-generated content — no managing director has evaluated the professional liability implications.

**Healthcare:** Clinical decision support systems with terms that disclaim liability for treatment decisions — no practitioner has evaluated against medical professional obligations.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** Vendor contract signature is the most common and least-examined professional liability event occurring daily across all four verticals. The signature accepts indemnification limitations, data processing authorizations, and liability disclaimers that contradict professional obligations — and it happens automatically through software updates with no individual decision moment. This is the single highest-frequency structural exposure that no vertical has named or addressed.

## Market Signal 5: Reassessment Protocol Absence

**Signal:** No Geneva professional services firm or practice in any vertical has a trigger-based reassessment protocol for AI governance decisions already made.

**Legal:** Law firms with AI tools deployed 18–36 months ago that have never reassessed whether those tools still constitute adequate governance under current conditions.

**Finance:** Compliance officers who approved AI tools at deployment and have no protocol for reassessing when conditions change — approval remains static while tools and environments evolve.

**Consulting:** Boutiques with AI-assisted deliverables in active client use with no reassessment trigger when client circumstances, regulatory environment, or tool configuration changes.

**Healthcare:** Practitioners using AI diagnostic tools approved once at purchase with no mechanism for reassessing when clinical evidence, regulatory approval status, or tool performance changes.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** The absence of reassessment protocol is the mechanism by which documentation adequacy decays. Without trigger-based reassessment, every AI governance decision made in 2023–2025 will be evaluated against standards that have evolved since the decision was made — and the evaluation will show a gap that didn't exist when the decision was made. Building trigger-based reassessment protocol into current AI governance architecture is the only way to prevent the same problem in 2027–2028.

## Market Signal 6: The Personal Liability Acknowledgment Gap

**Signal:** Across all four verticals, senior professionals are beginning to acknowledge personal accountability for AI governance decisions — but have no framework for understanding what that accountability requires.

**Legal:** Managing partners signing carrier attestations without understanding what they're personally warranting under Swiss cantonal law.

**Finance:** Compliance officers approving AI systems while knowing their approval is personally liable under FINMA supervisory standards.

**Consulting:** Managing directors committing to AI methodology in proposals while knowing they're personally accountable for the methodology's accuracy.

**Healthcare:** Practitioners authorizing AI-assisted clinical decisions while knowing they're personally liable for the outcome under medical professional standards.

**Cross-vertical interpretation:** Personal liability acknowledgment is spreading through the market as a cultural shift, not a compliance transition. Professionals are moving from "I didn't know I was accountable" to "I'm beginning to understand I'm accountable" — but the shift is not yet accompanied by operational frameworks for what accountable looks like in practice. This creates a specific market opportunity: providing the operational framework before the professional tries to build one alone and discovers it's more complex than anticipated.
