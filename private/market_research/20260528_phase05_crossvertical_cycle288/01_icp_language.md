# Cross-Vertical ICP Language — Phase 05 C288
# Patterns across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs

## Shared Vocabulary: What Professionals Say Across All Four Verticals

### Universal Phrases (Confirmed Across All Four ICPs)

1. **"I don't know what I'm supposed to have built"**
   - Legal: attorney can't identify which AI governance obligations apply to their specific tools
   - Finance: compliance officer doesn't know what evidence would satisfy carrier attestation
   - Consulting: managing director unsure what "adequate AI governance" means for their boutique
   - Healthcare: practitioner doesn't know which AI documentation requirements apply to their practice
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** The phrase is identical across verticals; same confusion, same stakes, same absence of resolution source

2. **"By the time I find out what I'm supposed to have done, it will be too late"**
   - Legal: discovering carrier attestation requirements during renewal, not before
   - Finance: learning AI governance criteria when FINMA examination questions arrive
   - Consulting: client AI questionnaire arriving before internal methodology documented
   - Healthcare: audit revealing documentation gaps after decisions already made
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** anticipatory governance impossible when the standard doesn't exist in advance

3. **"I'm signing something I haven't read because everyone signs it"**
   - Vendor contracts: AI tool terms accepted without legal review (all four verticals)
   - Carrier attestations: renewal forms signed based on peer behavior, not documented evaluation
   - Client questionnaires: responses prepared by copying what others submit
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** normative signature behavior replacing individual accountability assessment

4. **"The person who made that decision left"**
   - Legal: predecessor's AI tool choices now the current attorney's accountability
   - Finance: prior compliance officer's AI approval now unsupported by any record
   - Consulting: founding partner's AI configuration embedded in firm methodology with no documentation
   - Healthcare: prior practice owner's AI setup creates ongoing liability for current practitioner
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** professional inheritance problem structural to all four verticals — no succession protocol exists anywhere

5. **"I can't explain to a client how I made that recommendation"**
   - Legal: AI-drafted argument in court filing with unexplainable reasoning chain
   - Finance: AI-generated investment recommendation under Version N that no longer exists
   - Consulting: AI-assisted analysis client's board will question without reconstructable methodology
   - Healthcare: AI treatment recommendation with no patient-facing explanation protocol
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** explainability failure is not a communication problem — it's an accountability infrastructure problem

6. **"My carrier is asking questions I don't have answers to"**
   - Legal: PI carrier AI questionnaire with no Geneva bar guidance
   - Finance: D&O carrier probing AI governance adequacy with no published standard
   - Consulting: professional liability carrier requesting AI methodology documentation
   - Healthcare: malpractice carrier asking about AI-assisted diagnosis documentation
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** carrier iteration is the closest thing to an active standard, and it's operating faster than regulatory guidance

7. **"I'm accountable for something I didn't choose to do"**
   - Legal: AI feature activated in existing software without attorney decision
   - Finance: AI recommendation from system the compliance officer didn't select
   - Consulting: client's AI methodology inherited into deliverables without boutique control
   - Healthcare: AI diagnostic tool installed by vendor, not chosen by practitioner
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** accountable-for-without-authorizing is universal across all four verticals, structurally identical

8. **"The standard keeps moving but I can't see where it is"**
   - Legal: Geneva court expectations shifting with no published doctrine
   - Finance: FINMA examination questions referencing standards that don't appear in published guidance
   - Consulting: client DD questionnaires referencing frameworks the boutique hasn't implemented
   - Healthcare: regulatory requirements arriving through audit findings before publication
   - **Cross-vertical signal:** invisible standard is more dangerous than absent standard because professionals don't know to look

### Vertical-Specific Vocabulary Additions

**Legal-specific:**
- "citation verification" (AI-generated citations contaminating court filings)
- "privilege waiver through metadata" (research query logs revealing work product)
- "electronic seal scope" (what certifying attorney actually warrants)
- "client AI content ingestion" (adopting client-provided AI output as firm work product)

**Finance-specific:**
- "model version continuity" (approval under Version N applies to version that no longer exists)
- "custodian AI chain" (SIX/SwissRepoNet/SIC undocumented accountability cascade)
- "ISSB/FINMA collision" (same AI-generated sustainability claims subject to two regulatory streams)

**Consulting-specific:**
- "proposal provenance" (AI-generated proposals showing AI density to sophisticated clients)
- "configuration inheritance" (departing employee's embedded knowledge leaving with them)
- "sub-consultant AI output" (absorbing third-party AI work product as principal with no delegation framework)

**Healthcare-specific:**
- "treatment recommendation provenance" (AI-generated clinical recommendation with no explanation protocol)
- "patient consent architecture" (FADP Art. 13 explanation in accessible form for vulnerable populations)
- "clinical AI procurement" (device approved at purchase, ungoverned at deployment)

### French-Language Cross-Vertical Vocabulary

- "reddition de comptes" (accountability) — used with weight and personal implication across all four verticals
- "obligation de moyens" vs "obligation de résultat" — French legal framework applied to AI governance discussions
- "diligence raisonnable" — reasonable due diligence invoked for AI tool selection across verticals
- "fonds propres" / "fonds propres supplémentaires" — capital adequacy framing applied to AI governance investment
- "clause de responsabilité" — liability clause in vendor contracts, engagement letters, carrier attestations
- "protocolle de réévaluation" — reassessment protocol as missing governance mechanism (all verticals)
- "architecture de traçabilité" — traceability architecture as the unnamed thing all four verticals lack

## Cross-Vertical Pattern: Language Convergence at Accountability Moments

Across all four verticals, professionals converge on accountability language during specific trigger moments:
- Carrier renewal season → language intensifies
- Client AI questionnaire receipt → language becomes urgent
- Regulatory examination announcement → language becomes precise
- Peer incident disclosure → language becomes technical

This convergence suggests a shared linguistic infrastructure that responds to accountability pressure — professionals have access to the same vocabulary pool and activate it when stakes are clear. The opportunity is to provide that vocabulary before the trigger event forces activation.
