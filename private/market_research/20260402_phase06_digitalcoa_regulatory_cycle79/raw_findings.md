# Phase 06 DigitalCoa Regulatory — Raw Findings | C79
*Regulatory findings from C79 Phase 01 (Legal) + Phase 02 (Finance). Synthesized.*

## Swiss AI Regulatory Landscape (C79)

### FADP / DSFA (Already Applicable)
- FADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing (FDPIC May 2025 communication confirmed)
- Semi-automated decisions may trigger extended information obligations under FADP (Pestalozzi analysis)
- For Geneva financial sector: this applies now, not waiting for AI Convention bill

### Copyright for AI Training (Unsettled)
- Copyright questions for AI training: legally unsettled
- Legislative amendments needed — timeline unknown
- For clients: document AI training data provenance; don't assume fair use

### Product Liability Act (Already Applicable, Updates Anticipated)
- Federal Product Liability Act applies to AI (Pestalozzi)
- Revisions anticipated to align with EU Product Liability Directive
- AI systems as products: deploying party bears liability risk

### FINMA AI Guidance (June 2026 — 8 WEEKS AWAY)
- FINMA actively addressing AI implications in financial markets
- FINMA AI guidance deadline: June 2026
- Key questions: Will it follow EU AI Act risk classification? What transparency obligations?
- For clients: inventory AI systems before June; determine risk classification

### AI Convention Bill (End-2026 Consultation Draft)
- FDJP leading drafting
- Scope: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, supervision
- Public sector first; private sector implementation at Swiss discretion
- End-2026: consultation draft published

### OFCOM Non-Binding Measures Plan (End-2026)
- OFCOM coordinating legally non-binding measures
- Industry solutions and voluntary commitment declarations
- Plan to be completed by end-2026
- BFFA/SECO/FDFA involved

### Liability Debate: Art. 97 vs Art. 101 CO
- Art. 97(1) CO: fault-based (traditional)
- Art. 101(1) CO: deploying party fully responsible for auxiliary persons
- DETEC questioning: should AI contractual liability follow Art. 101 standard?
- If shifted: deploying party bears full liability for AI errors
- For Geneva financial clients: review AI vendor contracts now

### Federal Administration AI Guidelines (Already In Effect)
- General AI guidelines + factsheets on generative AI for Confederation staff
- Humans remain responsible for any AI-generated content
- 8-measure implementation plan (Federal Chancellery)
- AI Competence Network forums operational
- AI contact point at Federal Chancellery since Feb 1, 2026

### Swiss Middle Ground Confirmed
- No overarching horizontal AI regulation (EU AI Act approach rejected)
- Sector-specific where possible; cross-sector only for fundamental rights
- International interoperability via Council of Europe Convention
- Mix of horizontal legislative adjustments + sectoral solutions

## Geneva-Specific Regulatory Angle
- OFCOM tripartite platform: 300 members + 80 federal representatives
- Plateforme Tripartite coordinates Swiss positions on international AI processes
- Geneva hosting role in international AI governance confirmed
- OFCOM: non-binding measures development ongoing
