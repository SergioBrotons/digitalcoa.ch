# ICP — Small Geneva Medical Practice AI Governance
## Phase 04 — Geneva Healthcare — Cycle 42

---

## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Target:** Small medical practices in Geneva (1–5 physicians), independent doctors, dental practices, physiotherapy practices
- **Alternative ICP:** Small clinics (6–20 staff), aesthetic medicine practices
- **NOT:** Hospitals (institutional governance), large medical groups with dedicated compliance staff
- **Geography:** Canton Geneva primary; broader Swiss French-speaking region secondary

### Psychographics
- **Pain:** Using AI tools for clinical notes, scheduling, patient communication, diagnostic support without formal governance; 57% hallucination risk in AI-assisted diagnosis; Swissdamed July 1 registration deadline ~13 weeks away with NO grace period
- **Trigger:** Swissdamed July 1 2026 (#1 most urgent deadline in portfolio, NO grace period); Phase 01 C42 70% projected AI adoption increase signals normalization
- **Urgency:** Life safety context makes governance gap most dangerous in healthcare; Swissdamed-first sequencing confirmed #1 across all verticals; 57% AI error/hallucination rate = patient safety issue
- **Gatekeeper:** Practicing physician / practice manager with clinical and business responsibility

### Healthcare-Specific Pain Points
1. **Swissdamed July 1, 2026 ~13 WEEKS NO GRACE** — #1 most urgent deadline in entire portfolio; Swissdamed registration mandatory for medical device dealers; AI tools used in patient care likely qualify as medical devices under Swissmedic oversight
2. **57% hallucination risk** — AI diagnostic/treatment suggestions may be factually incorrect; human review is not just FADP compliance — it is patient safety
3. **Swissdamed/EUDAMED dual registration burden** — Swissdamed and EUDAMED are NOT interconnected; practices with EU nexus must register on both systems
4. **CHUV Meditron May 2026** (~1 month) — institutional AI adoption signals mainstream medical AI availability
5. **FADP directly applicable to medical AI** — FDPIC May 2025; patient data processed by AI = Art. 21 right to human review + Art. 5(c) transparency
6. **55% data protection violations** — healthcare is the sector with highest data protection violation rates; AI tools compound this risk

### Decision-maker Motivations
- Avoid Swissdamed registration penalty (July 1, 2026, NO grace period)
- Protect patient safety (57% hallucination risk + life safety context)
- Protect practice from FDPIC enforcement (healthcare is FDPIC enforcement priority)
- Capture efficiency gains (260h/year applicable to clinical admin: notes, scheduling, patient communication)
- Stay ahead of institutional AI adoption (CHUV Meditron May 2026 signals mainstream availability)
