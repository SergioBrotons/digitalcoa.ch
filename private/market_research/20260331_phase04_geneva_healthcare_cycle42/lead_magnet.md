# Lead Magnet: "The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework" (Swissdamed Edition)
## Phase 04 — Geneva Healthcare — Cycle 42

---

## Positioning
For Geneva small medical practices and independent doctors who are using AI tools in clinical workflows — for notes, scheduling, patient communication, or diagnostic support — but have no formal governance framework. Swissdamed July 1, 2026 is ~13 weeks away with NO grace period. The question is not whether to govern AI — it is whether to govern it before or after a regulatory finding or a patient safety incident.

**Primary urgency lever (Cycle 42):** Swissdamed July 1, 2026 ~13 WEEKS NO GRACE — #1 most urgent deadline in the entire portfolio. AI tools used in patient care likely qualify as medical devices under Swissmedic oversight. Registration deadline is immovable. No exceptions. The 57% AI hallucination rate makes governance not just a compliance issue — it is a patient safety issue.

**Secondary urgency lever:** FADP directly applicable to medical AI (FDPIC May 2025 confirmation). Patient data processed by AI = Art. 21 right to human review for automated decisions affecting treatment. This is already operative — not future risk.

## ICP Entry Points

### Decision 0: Swissdamed Registration Assessment (Decision 0 — #1 Priority)
Determine whether any AI tool used in the practice qualifies as a medical device under Swissmedic/MedDO oversight. If the practice uses AI for: diagnostic support, treatment recommendations, patient monitoring, clinical decision support — Swissdamed registration may be required. The July 1, 2026 deadline has NO grace period.

**Why this works:** Swissdamed deadline is immovable. Most small medical practices do not know whether their AI tools require registration. The Shadow AI audit + Swissdamed assessment identifies gaps before the deadline.

### Decision 1: AI Clinical Use Risk Triage
For each AI tool used in clinical workflows: classify by risk level (low/medium/high). AI tools that suggest diagnoses or treatment plans = HIGH RISK (57% hallucination rate). Human review is required for all high-risk AI outputs before patient action.

**Why this works:** 57% AI hallucination rate means AI diagnostic suggestions fail more often than not without human review. This is a patient safety issue, not just a compliance issue. Risk triage creates immediate awareness without requiring immediate process overhaul.

### Decision 2: FADP Clinical AI Compliance Mapping
Map which AI tools process patient personal data. For each: (1) identify legal basis under FADP Art. 13 et seq., (2) assess whether Art. 21 right to human review applies (automated decisions producing legal effects), (3) document data source disclosure under Art. 16.

**Why this works:** FADP is directly applicable to medical AI (FDPIC May 2025). Most small practices do not know their AI tools create FADP obligations. FADP compliance mapping surfaces the gap without prescribing solutions.

### Decision 3: Swissdamed/EUDAMED Dual Registration Check
If the practice has EU nexus (EU patients, EU suppliers, EU distribution): determine whether EUDAMED registration is also required. Swissdamed and EUDAMED are NOT interconnected — separate registrations required.

**Why this works:** Dual registration burden is a specific known gap. Practices with EU nexus often do not realize they need both registrations. The dual registration check is a specific action item with a specific deadline.

### Decision 4: CHUV Meditron Institutional Readiness
Assess readiness for mainstream medical AI adoption. CHUV Meditron May 2026 (~1 month) signals that institutional-grade medical AI is becoming available to small practices. Governance framework now creates readiness for the next wave of medical AI tools.

**Why this works:** The governance framework is not just about current AI tools — it creates institutional readiness for the next wave. Small practices that govern AI now will be better positioned to adopt CHUV-grade tools when they become available.

### Decision 5: Patient Data AI Governance Protocol
Document the practice's policy for using AI with patient data: (1) which AI tools can access patient data, (2) what data they can access, (3) human review requirement before AI outputs affect patient care, (4) data residency requirement (Swiss or EU servers only).

**Why this works:** 55% data protection violations = healthcare is the highest-risk sector. The patient data AI governance protocol is the operational implementation of decisions 1-4. It is the document the practice can show to Swissmedic, FDPIC, or patients if challenged.
