## Cycle 24 | 2026-03-30

- **Focus**: Privacy law + AI governance
- **Target**: Mid-market; not clearly SMB-accessible

#### Legal AI Tool Vendors (NOT Competitors — Tools ≠ Advisory)
- CASUS (getcasus.com) — Swiss legal AI platform; workflow tools ≠ governance advisory
- **Assessment**: Technology providers; advisory gap intact

### Competitive Absence — Quantitative Confirmation
- **10th+ consecutive cycle**: no boutique competitor for small Geneva law firm AI governance advisory
- Structural white space confirmed: no bar-endorsed AI governance framework for small firms
- FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL is sole identified boutique; limited accessibility

### Structural White Space Definition
- **Position**: Integrated nFADP + EU AI Act + professional responsibility framework for 2–5 attorney firms
- **Gap**: No bar-endorsed equivalent; no mass-market accessible advisor; no peer-endorsed framework
- **Entry**: Retroactive compliance path ("govern the AI you already have") — lower friction than anticipatory compliance

---

## Positioning Recommendation

### Unique Entry Point
1. **Retroactive compliance framing**: "You already use AI tools. Do you know what data is being processed? What your obligations are under nFADP? Under the EU AI Act if you serve EU clients?"
2. **Integrated framework**: nFADP Art. 5(c) + EU AI Act + SBA professional responsibility (no competitor offers this integration)
3. **Bar-adjacent credibility**: FDPIC enforcement signals + Swiss legal professional standards

### Competitive Moat
- Bar endorsement gap (ANBLES silent; no bar AI governance framework)
- FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL positioning is mid-market, not SMB mass-market
- First-mover advantage in SMB mass-market AI governance advisory for Geneva/Vaud legal firms

### Target Client Profile
- Geneva/Vaud small law firms (2–5 attorneys)
- Managing partner or sole practitioner with AI tool adoption pressure
- Urgency: FDPIC enforcement signals, client AI questions, Federal Council May 2026 First Draft
