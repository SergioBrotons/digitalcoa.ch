## The Decision Framework

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit — Do You Know What AI Your Firm Is Using?

Before the governance framework: the Shadow AI problem.

Your employees — associates, assistants, paralegals — are likely using AI tools you haven't approved. General-purpose AI (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.) used without documentation creates:

- **FDPIC exposure**: client personal data processed by unsanctioned third-party AI systems
- **Professional liability**: AI output you can't verify or explain
- **Compliance blindspot**: no record of what AI is being used for which client matters

**Key question**: When did you last audit what AI tools your team is actually using?

This is the entry point. A Shadow AI audit creates immediate urgency and positions the governance framework as the solution.

---

### Decision 1: What AI Tools Are You Using — and What Data Is Being Processed?

Before anything else: inventory.

AI tools in a small law firm typically include:
- Legal research platforms (Clarus, CASUS, law firm proprietary tools)
- Document drafting and review assistants
- Client communication AI ( chatbots, automated responses)
- Calendar, scheduling, and administrative AI

**Key question**: Are these tools processing **personal data** of clients, counterparties, or third parties?

If yes: nFADP Article 5(c) obligations are already active. Data subjects have the right to:
- Know they are subject to automated processing
- Request human review of automated decisions
- Refuse to be subject to automated decisions with legal effect

**This is not hypothetical.** It is active law.

---

### Decision 2: Do You Have EU AI Act Obligations?

The EU AI Act is not just an EU problem. Geneva law firms serving EU clients, placing AI tools on the EU market, or acting as AI importers/distributors may already be subject.

**Active now (GPAI — General Purpose AI, since August 2, 2025)**:
- Transparency: must disclose AI-generated content
- Prohibition: no AI systems that manipulate through emotional inference, exploit vulnerabilities, or conduct social scoring
- Deep fake disclosure: required

**August 2, 2026 (high-risk obligations)**:
- If your AI tools are listed in EU AI Act Annex III (credit, employment, essential services, etc.)
- Conformity assessment required before EU market placement

**Key question**: Do any of your AI tools serve EU-based clients or process EU personal data in connection with EU-market services?

---

### Decision 3: Human Accountability for AI-Assisted Work Product

The Swiss Federal Administrative Court ruled in **June 2025**: AI cannot be named as inventor or author. Human contributors must be named.

**Practical implications**:
- AI-assisted legal work: human attorney must be identifiable as responsible
- AI tool output: cannot be represented as human-generated if it is not
- Professional liability: AI tool errors do not excuse professional negligence

**SBA AI Guidelines (June 2024)**: Competence, confidentiality, non-discrimination, human oversight — all require that AI tools be supervised by licensed attorneys who remain accountable.

---

### Decision 4: What Is Your nFADP Documentation Obligation?

Under nFADP Article 5(c), if you make automated decisions with legal effect on individuals, you must:
- Inform the data subject that automated processing will occur
- Provide meaningful information about the logic used
- Implement measures to allow data subjects to request human review
- Be able to demonstrate compliance if FDPIC requests it

**For small firms**: Documentation of AI tool usage, data processing scope, and human review procedures is the baseline.
