# ICP Language: Geneva Healthcare — C303 Phase 04

## How Independent Practices Talk About AI Problems

### The Geneva Healthcare ICP

Independent physician practices, 2–10 professionals, Geneva-based. Senior physician personally accountable for every clinical and administrative decision. License is irreplaceable. The emotional driver is personal, irreplaceable license liability — not AI efficiency. The carrier attestation is the acute forcing function.

---

## ICP Language Patterns

### The License Frame
- "My medical license is irreplaceable — I can't get it back if I lose it"
- "If something goes wrong and I used AI, am I still covered?"
- "The carrier asked about AI and I didn't know what to say"
- "I'm responsible for everything that happens in this practice"

### The Carrier Attestation Problem
- "I signed something for the carrier. I don't actually know what I committed to"
- "They asked if I have adequate AI governance. I have no idea what adequate means"
- "The form keeps getting longer. Each version asks for more specific things"
- "I signed because I didn't want to have to explain why I didn't"

### The FMH Oversight Problem
- "FMH says human oversight — but what does that actually mean?"
- "I reviewed the AI output and made a decision. Is that oversight?"
- "If the AI is better than me at this, is it still oversight if I just agree with it?"
- "The requirement says human oversight. It doesn't say what that looks like in a 3-person practice"

### The Patient AI Content Problem
- "A patient came in with an AI analysis last week. I had to pretend I understood what it was"
- "Patients are bringing in AI-generated health information. How do I document that I evaluated it?"
- "If I disagree with the AI, what do I write in the record?"

### The Swiss Hosting Confusion
- "The tool says Swiss hosting — that should be fine, right?"
- "Swiss data protection law — does that mean my patient data is protected?"
- "I don't actually know where the data goes when I use this tool"

### The Vendor Trust Problem
- "I signed up for this tool and started using it. I don't actually know what it does with the data"
- "The terms of service were 40 pages. I didn't read them"
- "By continuing to use the service, you agree to..."

### The Efficiency Paradox
- "We need AI to handle the administrative burden — we're short-staffed"
- "But if something goes wrong because of AI, my license is on the line"
- "I can't afford to not use it. I also can't afford to use it wrong"

## French ICP Language

### Cadre de responsabilité professionnelle
- "Ma licence médicale est irréplaceable — je ne peux pas la récupérer si je la perds"
- "Si quelque chose tourne mal et que j'ai utilisé l'IA, suis-je encore couvert ?"
- "L'assureur a demandé si j'ai une gouvernance IA adéquate. Je n'ai aucune idée de ce que adéquate signifie"

### Le problème de l'attestation d'assureur
- "J'ai signé quelque chose pour l'assureur. Je ne sais pas vraiment à quoi je me suis engagé"
- "Le formulaire ne cesse de s'allonger. Chaque version demande des choses plus spécifiques"
- "J'ai signé parce que je ne voulais pas avoir à expliquer pourquoi je ne l'avais pas fait"

### Le problème de surveillance FMH
- "La FMH dit surveillance humaine — mais qu'est-ce que cela signifie exactement ?"
- "J'ai examiné le résultat de l'IA et pris une décision. Est-ce une surveillance ?"
- "La requirement dit surveillance humaine. Rien ne dit à quoi cela ressemble dans un cabinet de 3 personnes"

### Le contenu IA du patient
- "Un patient est venu avec une analyse IA la semaine dernière. J'ai dû faire semblant de comprendre"
- "Les patients apportent des informations de santé générées par l'IA. Comment documenter que je l'ai évaluée ?"

### La confusion sur l'hébergement suisse
- "L'outil dit hébergement suisse — c'est bien, n'est-ce pas ?"
- "La loi suisse sur la protection des données — est-ce que cela signifie que mes données patients sont protégées ?"

### Le paradoxe de l'efficacité
- "Nous avons besoin de l'IA pour gérer la charge administrative — nous sommes sous-staffés"
- "Mais si quelque chose tourne mal à cause de l'IA, ma licence est en jeu"
- "Je ne peux pas me permettre de ne pas l'utiliser. Je ne peux pas non plus me permettre de l'utiliser mal"

## Key Vocabulary

| English | French | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| "license is on the line" | "licence en jeu" | Personal professional stakes irreplaceable |
| "carrier asked" | "l'assurance a demandé" | External accountability trigger |
| "adequate governance" | "gouvernance adéquate" | Undefined standard, personal signature required |
| "human oversight" | "surveillance humaine" | FMH requirement without operational definition |
| "Swiss hosting" | "hébergement suisse" | Data residency proxy for compliance confidence |
| "efficiency AI" | "IA d'efficacité" | Non-negotiable due to staffing shortage |
| "I signed something" | "j'ai signé quelque chose" | Retrospective exposure recognition |

## The Emotional State

The Geneva healthcare ICP is not worried about AI adoption in abstract. They are worried about their license. The carrier attestation activated personal exposure they didn't understand at signing. The FMH requirement is stated without a compliance pathway. The efficiency pressure (staffing shortage) forces AI adoption while the liability risk advises against it. The gap between "I need this to survive" and "this could cost me my license" is the defining tension.
