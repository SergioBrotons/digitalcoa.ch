# Compliance Context: Geneva Healthcare — C303 Phase 04

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape

### Primary Regulatory Framework

**FMH Medical Professional Standards**
- Human oversight requirement stated without operational definition
- No Geneva-specific small practice methodology
- Physicians individually responsible for AI decisions made in practice

**nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)**
- In force September 1, 2023
- Personal health data is special category under Art.5(c)
- DPA requirements apply to AI tool vendors
- Art.63 criminal liability for unauthorized processing

**Swissmedic / Swissdamed**
- Swissdamed July 1, 2026 deadline applies to medical devices
- Does NOT apply to general AI systems or AI attestation
- AI-enabled medical devices may trigger classification obligations
- Classification criteria written for manufacturers, not independent practices

**Medical Liability**
- Personal liability of treating physician
- License irreplaceable
- No Swiss federal AI liability law = physician bears 100% liability

---

## The Compliance Gap

The regulations exist. The proportionate implementation for small independent practices does not.

Key gaps:
- FMH human oversight — no operational definition for small practice
- nFADP Art.9 DPA — no compliant vendor offering for small practices
- Swissdamed classification — criteria not calibrated for independent practice AI tools
- Carrier attestation — no pre-signing review service
- Patient AI content — no documentation category

---

## The Personal Liability Layer

The practicing physician is personally liable for:
- Clinical decisions (with or without AI)
- Data protection compliance
- Carrier attestation signed

No separation between entity-level and personal liability. The license is on the line.

---

## Critical Timing

**Swissdamed July 1, 2026:**
- Applies to medical devices — NOT general AI tools
- AI-enabled diagnostic tools used by independent practices may qualify
- Classification determination required before deadline
- Most independent practices have not conducted this determination

**FMH Human Oversight:**
- Stated requirement, no operational definition
- Documented protocol required but undefined
- Carrier attestation asking for evidence of oversight

**Carrier Attestation:**
- Personal signature required
- "Adequate AI governance" undefined
- Evidence documentation now required (carrier form v4)
