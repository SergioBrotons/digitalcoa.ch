# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Healthcare — C303 Phase 04

## What Exists, What's Missing

### Current Competitive Landscape

**What Exists:**
- FMH guidelines (principles-based, not operational)
- Generic AI governance frameworks (designed for hospitals/large practices)
- AI tool vendor documentation (vendor-serving, not physician-serving)
- General data protection guidance (not AI-specific)
- EMR platform AI features (hospital-centric, not independent practice-calibrated)

**What's Missing (for Independent Practices 2–10 professionals):**
- Proportionate to small practice scale
- Geneva-specific regulatory context
- Personal license liability naming
- FMH human oversight operationalization
- Carrier attestation preparation for independent practices
- Patient AI content documentation protocol
- Swissdamed classification determination for AI-enabled tools
- nFADP Art.9 compliant vendor stack for small practices

---

## The White Space

1. **Pre-signing carrier attestation review** — No service reviews carrier AI attestation forms before signature for small medical practices. The personal signature activates liability without the physician knowing what's required.

2. **FMH oversight operationalization** — Translating "human oversight" into a documented workflow specific to small practice operations. Not principle-level — specific to what a 3-physician practice actually does at 8:30 AM Wednesday.

3. **Patient AI content protocol** — Documentation category for AI-generated input patient brings to consultation. Includes: documenting that AI input was evaluated, physician's independent judgment recorded, final clinical decision.

4. **Swissdamed classification determination** — Determining whether AI tools used in the practice qualify as medical devices under Swissdamed. The July 1 deadline applies to medical devices, not general AI — but AI-enabled diagnostic tools may trigger classification.

5. **Efficiency AI + compliance AI convergence service** — For healthcare, efficiency AI is non-negotiable (staffing shortage). The service that enables AI adoption for efficiency while managing liability is distinct from the compliance-first service.

6. **nFADP Art.9 processor chain evaluation** — Vendor sub-processors, data residency, model training data retention. Evaluating whether current AI tool stack complies with nFADP DPA requirements.

---

## The Gap

The gap is not information. The gap is:
- Proportionate implementation for 2–10 person practices
- Personal license liability naming
- Documentation categories that fit clinical workflow
- Carrier-ready evidence format
- Swissdamed classification determination methodology
- FMH oversight documented protocol

No Geneva-specific service addresses these for independent medical practices at 2–10 physician scale.
