# Market Signals: Geneva Healthcare — C303 Phase 04

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

### Primary Market Signals

**1. Carrier Attestation for Medical Practices**
Medical liability insurers are beginning to ask about AI tool usage at renewal. The forms have evolved from generic to version-specific and audit-trail-demanding. The physician signs without understanding what "adequate AI governance" requires, or whether they have it. The attestation creates a record, not a defense.

**2. The Efficiency-Liability Paradox**
Medical practices are short-staffed and need AI for administrative efficiency. But using AI creates liability exposure under FMH oversight requirements, nFADP obligations, and carrier attestation representations. The practice is caught between operational necessity and liability risk. Neither option is safe.

**3. FMH Human Oversight Undefined**
FMH states human oversight requirement without operational definition. Small practices cannot determine what documented oversight looks like. The requirement exists; the compliance pathway does not.

**4. Patient AI Content Arriving Without Protocol**
Patients arriving with consumer AI-generated health analyses. The physician must evaluate and document AI input they did not request. No documentation category exists for this scenario. The highest-accountability moment — where the physician deliberately overrides or rejects patient-provided AI — is the least-documented.

**5. Swiss Hosting Confused with Compliance**
Swiss data residency confused with Swiss data protection compliance. Many practices using AI tools with US-based API processing regardless of Swiss storage label. "Swiss hosting" creates false confidence.

**6. Vendor Terms Accepted Without Review**
AI tool terms of service accepted without review. "By continuing to use" is a legal acceptance. No practice has a vendor term monitoring process. The accumulation of years of unevaluated acceptance creates undisclosed obligations.

---

## Failure Modes

**Attestation failure:** Practice signs carrier attestation without documentation to support it. If a claim occurs, the attestation becomes evidence of inadequate governance.

**Documentation failure:** AI-assisted decision documented only as the final clinical decision. The AI's contribution is invisible in the record.

**Vendor failure:** Practice uses AI tool that retains training data or uses inputs for model improvement. Patient data potentially exposed without practice knowledge.

**Oversight failure:** Physician accepts AI recommendation without documented evaluation. If the recommendation was wrong, no evidence exists that human oversight occurred.

**Classification failure:** Practice uses AI tool that qualifies as medical device under Swissdamed but never conducted classification determination. Operating illegally without knowing it.

---

## The Structural Problem

The healthcare ICP has a specific structural problem: they need AI for efficiency (staffing shortage is real), but the liability risk is personal and irreplaceable (license). The carrier attestation is the forcing function that makes the gap visible — but it doesn't tell them what to build.

The documentation debt accumulated silently. Tools were adopted without decision events. The gap is now visible and the physician cannot retroactively create the records that should have been created at adoption.
