## Seven Cross-Vertical Market Patterns

### Pattern 1: The Accountability Infrastructure Void (Cross-Vertical)

**The signal:** Across all four verticals, the Geneva boutique market is structurally unable to produce the documentation evidence required by carrier attestation, institutional questionnaires, and professional standards. This is not a firm-specific failure — it is a structural market design gap. No Geneva boutique advisory has positioned to fill it.

**The mechanism:** Every available framework (FINMA 08/2024, SAV June 2024 guidance, FMH principle-level AI guidance) is designed for institutional scale. The 2–50 person firm faces identical accountability triggers with none of the institutional infrastructure. The market has a gap between where accountability pressure is applied and where governance capacity exists.

**The opportunity:** A proportionate, boutique-calibrated accountability infrastructure service does not exist in the Geneva market. The first mover to claim this position and substantively deliver it owns the market for the duration of the first-mover window.

---

**The signal:** The carrier / association attestation wave is arriving at different velocities across verticals — but all four are moving toward the same destination within 18–24 months.

| Vertical | Current Wave Phase | Primary Attestation Trigger |

| Finance | Active, third-generation | Carrier AI questionnaire; institutional client DDQ |

**The implication for DigitalCoa.ch:** Legal and finance verticals are in-scope NOW. Consulting and healthcare will follow. The market entry sequencing (legal + finance first, consulting + healthcare follow) aligns with the attestation wave differential.

---

### Pattern 3: The Documentation Window Is Present-Tense and Irreversible

**The signal:** The most strategically significant cross-vertical market signal: the documentation window is not a future deadline metaphor. It is a present-tense positioning event. Firms that have been building documentation since 2024–2025 have evidence of contemporaneous decision-making. Firms that defer are accumulating decisions made without documentation — and retroactive documentation is reconstructable and legible as such.

**The irreversibility mechanics:** Regulators, carriers, and institutional clients are developing pattern recognition for documentation quality. Contemporaneous documentation (built while decisions were being made) has different evidentiary weight than reconstructed documentation (built after questions arrive). The differential compounds over time.

**The urgency framing:** "The documentation window is not a deadline you're approaching — it closed on decisions you made in 2024 and 2025. What you do now determines what you can defend in 2026 and beyond."

### Pattern 4: Peer Disclosure Unlocks Action — The Geneva Circuit Is the Primary Channel

**The signal:** Across all four verticals, the activation event is peer disclosure — a colleague sharing a concrete attestation or questionnaire experience. The Geneva informal professional circuit (association events, peer dinners, cantonal professional networks) operates as the trust transmission mechanism for this market.

**The implication:** Cold outreach has lower conversion in this market than peer-referred discovery conversations. DigitalCoa.ch's sustained participation in Geneva professional circuit events is not a marketing tactic — it is the primary distribution infrastructure. Content investment compounds through circuit participation, not despite it.

**The pattern by vertical:**
- Legal: Colleague mentioned their SAV documentation at a BAR event
- Finance: MD mentioned the carrier AI questionnaire at a family office conference
- Consulting: Partner mentioned client AI DDQ response failure at a peer dinner
- Healthcare: Physician mentioned signing the carrier attestation at an FMH event

### Pattern 5: Shadow AI Is Ubiquitous and Ungoverned Across All Verticals

**The signal:** The accountable professional in a Geneva boutique does not control the AI decisions made by their team. Associates (legal), portfolio managers (finance), sub-consultants (consulting), staff (healthcare) are using AI tools outside the accountable professional's governance visibility — creating a distributed accountability gap that external attestation and questionnaire instruments are beginning to probe.

| Vertical | Shadow AI Form |
|---|---|
| Legal | Associates using consumer ChatGPT on client matter data without authorization |
| Finance | Principals deploying agentic AI without documented authorization architecture |
| Consulting | Sub-consultants running AI on client deliverables with no disclosure |
| Healthcare | Staff using AI administrative tools without policy; physicians unaware |

**The common result across all four:** Zero visibility at the accountable professional level, no compliant DPA, full personal accountability regardless of decision control.

---

### Pattern 6: The Insurance Market Is Pricing AI Governance Now

**The signal:** Insurance brokers across all four verticals are incorporating AI governance into renewal pricing. Firms without documented AI governance are experiencing:
- Higher premium quotes
- Declined coverage (rare, but appearing)
- Excess load for "unmanaged AI risk"

The insurance market is pricing AI governance risk before any standardized SMB framework exists — creating a present-tense competitive differentiation between documented and undocumented firms.

**The implication:** Insurance underwriting documentation is an underappreciated discrete service entry point. Not "AI governance consulting" as a broad category — specifically "insurer-ready AI governance documentation" as a discrete deliverable a broker can evaluate. The entry framing is: "Are you ready for what your next renewal is going to ask?"

---

### Pattern 7: First-Mover Window Remains Open

**The signal:** No Geneva boutique advisory has claimed the SMB AI accountability infrastructure position across any vertical in any cycle through C295. The competitive landscape remains:
- Big 4: Out of range structurally (price, firm size, enterprise focus)
- nFADP generalist consultants: Generalist, not practice-scale calibrated, not AI-specific
- AI tool vendors: Tool-sellers with conflict of interest; cannot advise on accountability
- Healthcare IT / WM platforms: Infrastructure only, not advisory
- Professional associations: Guidance only, no methodology, no hands-on delivery

**Window assessment:** Open. Estimated 12–18 months before competitive entry signals emerge more clearly.
