## ICP Definition

### Primary Audience Segments

**Segment 1: Geneva-Based Executive Decision-Makers**
- **Role:** C-suite, Managing Director, CFO, CRO, Board Chairman at Geneva-headquartered companies
- **Profile:** 40-60 years old; French-speaking background with English fluency; values intellectual depth and practical relevance
- **Reading behavior:** Newsletter-first; scan Brotons.net for in-depth analysis; limited time; prefer concise, actionable insights
- **Decision drivers:** Strategic relevance to Geneva business context; credibility of author/source; practical applicability
- **Pain points:** Information overload; generic business content that doesn't reflect Geneva context; lack of time for lengthy reads

**Segment 2: Geneva Professional Services Practitioners**
- **Role:** Partners, Directors, Senior Managers at Geneva law firms, advisory firms, banks, asset managers
- **Profile:** 35-55 years old; legally/financially trained; value precision and regulatory accuracy
- **Reading behavior:** Search + newsletter + social (LinkedIn); use content for client context and professional development
- **Decision drivers:** Regulatory accuracy; Geneva-specific context; intellectual rigor; peer relevance
- **Pain points:** Generic Swiss content that lacks Geneva depth; no Geneva-specific intellectual platform; no bilingual (EN+FR) resource

**Segment 3: Geneva-Based Academics and Policy Professionals**
- **Role:** Professors, researchers, policy advisors at IHEID, Graduate Institute, UNOG, think tanks
- **Profile:** 30-65 years old; intellectually curious; value rigorous analysis and original thinking
- **Reading behavior:** Search + newsletter; share with networks; use for teaching and research context
- **Decision drivers:** Intellectual quality; original analysis; Geneva diplomatic/multilateral context
- **Pain points:** Too practitioner-focused; insufficient intellectual depth; lack of Geneva policy analysis

## Audience Needs (Brotons.net Value Proposition)

### What Geneva Executives Need from Brotons.net
1. **Geneva-specific strategic intelligence** — Not generic business commentary; Geneva-specific analysis with practical relevance
2. **Regulatory clarity** — FINMA, SECO, Swiss regulatory developments explained with implementation relevance
3. **Cross-border perspective** — EU-CH, international, geopolitical context for Geneva decision-making
4. **Trusted signal source** — A platform they can rely on for consistent quality and Geneva authenticity

### What Geneva Practitioners Need from Brotons.net
1. **Accuracy and credibility** — Regulatory information they can trust; properly attributed; Geneva-specific
2. **Professional development value** — Content that adds to their professional knowledge base
3. **Client context** — Understanding of Geneva business dynamics to serve clients better
4. **EN+FR bilingual access** — Content in both languages for Geneva professional context

### What Geneva Academics/Policy Professionals Need from Brotons.net
1. **Real-world relevance** — Practitioner perspectives on Geneva dynamics; grounding academic analysis
2. **Geneva institutional insight** — Understanding of how Geneva institutions actually function
3. **Quality analysis** — Original, rigorous thinking; not recycled conventional wisdom

## ICP Decision Journey

### Awareness
- Discover Brotons.net via: LinkedIn share by peer, Substack newsletter discovery, search for Geneva-specific content, colleague referral
- First impression: "Finally, Geneva-specific business content that isn't generic"

### Consideration
- Read 1-3 articles/pieces; evaluate quality, Geneva authenticity, intellectual depth
- Compare: "Is this better than HNW, FT, or generic Swiss business content?"
- Key questions: Is this actually Geneva? Is the analysis credible? Will this be useful for my work?

### Conversion
- Subscribe to newsletter (free) — low-commitment first step
- Return visits: 2-4 pieces per month
- Engagement signals: Newsletter opens, LinkedIn follows, website return visits

### Advocacy
- Share with Geneva peers (LinkedIn, email, word of mouth)
- Reference in professional contexts
- Become a content topic suggestion or contributor

## ICP Conversion Pathway to DigitalCoa.ch

**Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch Conversion Mechanism:**
1. Brotons.net piece on regulatory topic (e.g., FINMA stablecoin guidance) demonstrates regulatory depth
2. Reader subscribes; receives 3-4 pieces; trusts Brotons.net quality
3. Reader faces specific regulatory challenge (e.g., evaluating stablecoin authorization)
4. Reader discovers DigitalCoa.ch through Brotons.net article reference or search
5. Reader requests briefing call; discovery conversation; advisory engagement

**Key requirement:** Brotons.net content must credibly demonstrate the regulatory expertise that DigitalCoa.ch would deliver — not just attract clicks, but attract the right clients.

## ICP Content Preferences

**Format preferences:**
- 800-1,500 words for main articles (executive reading time budget)
- Executive summary format with key takeaways first
- EN primary, FR for Geneva-specific pieces (or bilingual versions)
- Charts/visualizations for data-heavy pieces
- Original analysis, not summary of news

**Topic priorities:**
1. Geneva regulatory developments (FINMA, SNB, SECO, Swiss regulatory)
2. Geneva business strategy (M&A, geopolitics, international business)
3. Geneva institutional analysis (IOs, diplomacy, multilateral context)
4. Executive decision-making frameworks (for Geneva context)
5. Swiss/European business environment

**Avoid:**
- Generic business content not specific to Geneva
- News summary (readers can get that elsewhere)
- Content that could be written by any business publication
- AI-generated-sounding generic content
