---

### Content Format Standards

**Article (Standard):**
- Length: 800-1,500 words
- Structure: Executive summary first; key takeaways; analysis; implications; next steps
- Tone: Calm, sharp, restrained, credible
- Language: EN primary; FR for Geneva-specific topics (or bilingual version)
- Attribution: Source all data; cite institutions; disclose limitations

**Executive Brief:**
- Length: 300-500 words
- Structure: One key insight; context; implications; recommended action
- Use: Rapid response to Geneva regulatory developments

**Data Visualization:**
- Use: For regulatory data, trend analysis, comparison frameworks
- Standard: Clear labels; source attribution; Brotons.net branding

**Podcast/Audio (Future):**
- Use: Deep-dive interviews; longer-form discussion
- Format: 20-30 minutes; EN primary

---

### Content Production Cadence

**Minimum viable cadence:** 2 pieces per month (one major article + one executive brief)

**Target cadence:** 4 pieces per month
- Week 1: Major analytical piece (1,000-1,500 words)
- Week 2: Executive brief (300-500 words) + data visualization
- Week 3: Major analytical piece (1,000-1,500 words)
- Week 4: Executive brief (300-500 words) + content refresh/update

**Calendar-aligned content:**
- FINMA annual report release (Q1): Series of 4 pieces
- SNB rate decisions (quarterly): Rapid analysis + implications brief
- Geneva institutional events: Context + analysis pieces

### Content Quality Standards

**Must-haves:**
- Geneva-specific relevance (not generic business content)
- Original analysis (not news summary)
- Proper attribution and source citation
- Acknowledged limitations
- Practical executive implications
- EN+FR where Geneva context requires

**Must-nots:**
- AI-generated-sounding content
- Generic Swiss content without Geneva specificity
- Unfounded speculation
- Claims not supported by evidence
- Content that could appear on any business publication
- Hype language or AI clichés

## EN+FR Bilingual Strategy

**Framework: 70/30 EN/FR ratio**

**English content:**
- Global business context; international topics
- FINMA/SNB regulatory analysis (international audience)
- Geneva institutional analysis (EN-speaking IO professionals)

**French content:**
- Geneva-specific business strategy
- Swiss regulatory analysis (Geneva practitioner audience)
- CCIG/Geneva Chamber content partnerships

**Bilingual content:**
- Major analytical pieces: EN + FR versions
- Executive briefs: EN primary; FR translation
- Data visualizations: EN primary with FR labels where relevant

**Geneva bilingual expectation:**
- Geneva executives expect content in their working language
- Brotons.net EN+FR capability is a genuine competitive differentiator
- French quality must be native-level; not machine translation

## Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch Pathway

**Content bridge strategy:**
1. Brotons.net article demonstrates regulatory expertise (e.g., FINMA stablecoin guidance)
2. Article references DigitalCoa.ch for implementation advisory (when appropriate)
3. Reader clicks through to DigitalCoa.ch
4. Reader requests briefing call
5. Discovery + advisory engagement

**Bridge content types:**
- Regulatory analysis: "What it means for Geneva institutions" → DigitalCoa.ch implementation support
- Strategic frameworks: "How to apply this" → DigitalCoa.ch advisory engagement
- Market intelligence: "What this signals" → DigitalCoa.ch strategic advisory

**Pathway transparency:**
- Bridges must be genuine, not commercial manipulation
- DigitalCoa.ch services must match the expertise demonstrated in Brotons.net content
- Reader trust is paramount; pathway credibility depends on content quality
