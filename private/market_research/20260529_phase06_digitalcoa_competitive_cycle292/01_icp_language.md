## How ICPs Talk About the Problem (Market Language)

The Geneva senior professional — managing director, partner, practice leader — who is the DigitalCoa.ch ICP describes their accountability problem in specific language that reveals their cognitive state and what kind of response they are primed to receive. This language is observed across all four verticals.

### The Confirmed-Problem Vocabulary

The primary ICP state in C292 is not anticipatory anxiety. It is retrospective alarm. The ICP who has arrived at C292 Phase 06 has already experienced the confirmation moment: they signed something, they received an institutional questionnaire, they found tools in their inventory they didn't know were there. They now know their position is worse than they thought and can name this in their own language.

**How they name the problem:**

*"I've already signed something I wasn't fully sure what it meant."* — This is the retrospective confirmation sentence. The ICP is not asking "what might I have committed to?" They are stating a known fact. The response this language is primed for is naming what they committed to, in language that fits their specific context.

*"I got a questionnaire from a client I couldn't answer properly."* — Client AI governance questionnaire as a discrete accountability occasion. The ICP received something from a client (institutional client or private client of the law firm) and discovered they were not in a position to respond properly. The response is about what a proper response architecture looks like.

*"I found 12 AI tools in my firm I didn't know were deployed."* — Inventory reveal as the crystallizing moment. The ICP who says this has the experience and is naming it post-facto. They are not worried about it in the abstract — they are managing the confirmed consequence.

*"My carrier sent me an attestation form. I signed it. I didn't know what I was committing to."* — Carrier attestation as retrospective confirmation, not anticipatory preparation. The ICP wants to understand what they signed, not how to prepare for next year.

*"Someone asked me how I authorize what the AI does continuously. I had no documented answer."* — Agentic AI continuous authorization as the named unnamed question. This language emerges after the ICP tries to answer fourth-generation carrier questions.

### The Refusal/Decline Response Pattern

ICPs who are not yet ready to engage describe the problem in ways that reveal their non-decision:

*"I'm aware there are AI governance questions but I haven't had the moment yet."* — Anticipatory, not yet retrospective. The briefing that will work for this ICP is different from the one for the confirmed-problem ICP.

*"I have a general sense this is something I should be thinking about."* — Aspirational awareness without experiential trigger. Not converted yet — needs another triggering event or the confirmation engagement.

*"My firm is too small to be on anyone's radar for this."* — False security still active for a subset of the ICP population. Not yet with the problem. Response requires different framing.

*"I don't think we use that much AI."* — Active denial state. Most exposed segment — using more AI than acknowledged, with no documentation. The briefing for this ICP requires a different entry point (inventory reveal vs. naming confirmed exposure).

### Language Markers of the Four ICP Verticals

**Legal ICP (small firm, 2–5 attorneys):** Speaks in terms of specific professional obligations — Art.321 CP, bar obligations, client confidentiality. Attorney-client privilege language is primary. When asked about AI, they name it in terms of what they can and cannot do with client data. The accountability question feels like a bar compliance question before it feels like a regulatory question.

**Finance ICP (boutique wealth management, 50–200 employees):** Speaks in terms of institutional client accountability and carrier expectations. Investment discretion language is primary. The accountability question feels like a fiduciary question before it feels like a data protection question. Third-generation carrier attestation language is already in their active vocabulary because the institutional client DDQ has already arrived.

**Consulting ICP (boutique, 2–50 employees):** Speaks in terms of client methodology expectations and proposal defensibility. Client delivery language is primary. The accountability question feels like a client relationship question before it feels like a legal question. Client AI governance questionnaire language has entered their vocabulary through client pressure, not internal governance decisions.

**Healthcare ICP (independent physician, 2–10 physicians):** Speaks in terms of FMH standards and carrier attestation. Medical standard of care language is primary. The accountability question feels like a medical liability question before it feels like a data protection question. The carrier/insurance AI attestation language is newer for this ICP population and less sophisticated than the other three verticals.

### Language That Signals Ready-to-Engage State

Beyond the confirmed-problem vocabulary, certain phrases signal the ICP is in the engagement-ready state:

*"Is there someone in Geneva who actually knows what they're doing on this?"* — The search for specificity. This question names the market gap directly. The ICP has looked and found generic AI governance advisors and generic consultancy responses.

*"I need someone who actually understands the Geneva piece."* — The Geneva-specificity demand. The ICP recognizes that general methodology documentation doesn't solve a Geneva-specific accountability problem.

*"Not a consultant who read the white papers. Someone who's actually in the rooms."* — The circuit participation demand. The ICP is already evaluating whether the advisor has been present in the formation process, not just whether they have read the published doctrine.

*"We'll probably need to talk about this for more than an hour."* — The engagement depth signal. The ICP recognizes that a 90-minute briefing is a beginning, not a complete resolution.

*"Can you look at what I already signed before we talk about what comes next?"* — The pre-signed documentation review signal. This language names the acute service need: reviewing what the ICP already committed to before designing what comes next.

### Language That Reveals Conversion Obstacles

*"My partner thinks this is IT, not management."* — Internal alignment obstacle. The accountability problem is also an organizational governance problem. The briefing needs to be framed in terms that open organizational alignment, not just individual accountability.

*"We've been meaning to deal with this."* — Chronic deferral without acute trigger yet. The ICP is not in the confirmed-problem state — still in anticipatory deferral. Different briefing required.

*"I don't want to be the person who flags this in my firm."* — Naming anxiety. The ICP who does the naming becomes responsible. There is a specific social cost to being the person who says "we have a problem" in a small professional firm. The briefing needs to name this and provide the ICP with political cover.

## How the Market Names the Accountability Problem (vs. How Non-Specialist Advisors Name It)

### Market Language (What the ICP Uses)
- Attestation form / formulaire d'attestation
- Client questionnaire / questionnaire client sur l'IA
- Carrier expectation / attente de l'assureur
- Professional liability / responsabilité professionnelle
- Documentation adequacy / adéquation de la documentation
- Geneva-specific / spécifique à Genève
- Someone who's actually in the rooms / quelqu'un qui est vraiment dans les cercles

### Generic Consultant Language (What the ICP Rejects)
- AI strategy
- Digital transformation
- Innovation enablement
- Compliance framework
- Risk management solution
- AI best practices
- Technology adoption

The ICP who has arrived at the accountability problem through experience — not reading about it in a white paper — hears generic consultant language and knows immediately that the speaker does not share their problem. The specificity of the language being used is itself a trust signal.
