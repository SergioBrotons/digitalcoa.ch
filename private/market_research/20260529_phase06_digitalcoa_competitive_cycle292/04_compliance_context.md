## Regulatory Context for DigitalCoa.ch's Advisory Practice

DigitalCoa.ch operates at the intersection of professional accountability regulation and AI governance practice. The regulatory environment directly shapes the practice's service architecture, the ICP's accountability exposure, and the competitive landscape. This file identifies the specific regulatory dimensions that affect the practice's own positioning and service design.

### Regulatory Dimension 01: nFADP Enforcement Posture as Client Conversion Driver

**The regulatory fact:** FDPIC confirmed in May 2025 that existing nFADP applies directly to AI without regulatory gap. Enforcement is live, not future-risk. For all four vertical ICP populations, AI tool deployment is now subject to present-tense enforcement risk under existing data protection law.

**Why it matters for the practice:** The enforcement-live posture converts the ICP who was previously deferring AI governance decisions. The Geneva attorney who has been waiting for clearer guidance, the wealth manager who assumed nFADP was a 2027 issue, the consulting principal who thought AI governance was a client-side concern — the enforcement-live confirmation activates these ICPs toward action. The FDPIC posture shift is the most significant single regulatory development for client conversion in the past twelve months. The briefing that names this as a present-tense development — not a future regulatory change — and maps its specific implications for the ICP's own professional context converts at higher rates than briefings that treat it as policy evolution.

**The compliance framing:** "The FDPIC confirmed in May 2025 that Geneva professionals are subject to enforcement risk under existing data protection law. This is not a future concern. It is a present-tense condition that has been in place since the moment you put AI tools in front of your client work."

### Regulatory Dimension 02: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — Institutional Questionnaire Standard-Setting

**The regulatory fact:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 2024) created a documented inconsistency problem for firms that signed earlier AI governance attestations. It also established the evidence standard foundation for institutional client AI governance questionnaires — documents filed at 3 to 6-month intervals asking about AI-assisted decisions, human authorization moments, and portfolio construction methodology.

**Why it matters for the practice:** The institutional client questionnaire (DDQ) is the primary acute entry point for Finance ICPs. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 is the document that established what carriers and institutional clients are evaluating against when they ask about AI governance. The practice's service architecture must be calibrated against this standard — not against the general nFADP obligations or the carrier attestation form language. The Geneva-specific methodology for the institutional questionnaire response is the practice's core service, and FINMA 08/2024 is its regulatory foundation.

**The compliance framing:** "FINMA Guidance 08/2024 established the evidence standard for AI governance in the Geneva wealth management context. The institutional questionnaires you're receiving are probing whether your firm has architecture that can produce evidence meeting this standard — not whether you have policies that describe your intent."

### Regulatory Dimension 03: Agentic AI Exceeding Existing Regulatory Scope

**The regulatory fact:** Existing regulatory guidance — FINMA Guidance 08/2024, carrier attestation forms, institutional questionnaires — was written for generative AI applications: discrete AI-assisted decisions, tool-level authorization documentation, model version disclosure. The accelerating deployment of agentic AI (systems that execute continuously and autonomously) falls outside the coverage of instruments written for discrete AI events. No Swiss regulatory instrument currently addresses continuous autonomous AI execution.

**Why it matters for the practice:** The practice's continuous authorization methodology is positioned in a regulatory gap. This is not a vulnerability — it is a strategic advantage. The practice that produces the proportionate methodology for continuous authorization at boutique scale is not competing against existing regulatory standards (which don't exist for this problem). The practice is defining the standard before regulators name it. The regulatory framing positions the methodology as ahead-of-regulatory, not behind it.

**The compliance framing:** "The regulatory guidance you have was written for a different kind of AI — the kind where you decide to use it for a discrete decision and document that decision. The AI arriving in your firm now is different. It executes continuously and autonomously, and the documentation standard required to defend continuous autonomous AI is different from what existing instruments ask for. That methodology doesn't exist yet. That is what we're building."

### Regulatory Dimension 04: EU AI Act Art.4 Extraterritorial AI Literacy Obligation

**The regulatory fact:** EU AI Act Art.4 AI literacy obligation has been live since February 2025. For Geneva firms with EU client relationships, data flows, or service provision to EU-incorporated entities, this obligation applies extraterritorially. No Swiss authority enforces it. No Geneva firm is implementing it because Swiss authorities have not instructed them to.

**Why it matters for the practice:** The EU AI Act Art.4 situation creates a specific regulatory asymmetry in the Geneva professional context: EU-facing Geneva firms have an undisclosed live compliance obligation under EU law that their Swiss regulatory environment does not acknowledge. The practice that names this as a discrete service category — evaluating EU AI Act Art.4 literacy compliance for Geneva firms with EU nexus — captures a market segment no competitor has identified. The regulatory framing positions this as: "You're already subject to it. Your Swiss advisors haven't told you. That is a problem."

**The compliance framing:** "The EU AI Act AI literacy obligation has been live since February 2025 for any Swiss firm processing EU client data or serving EU-incorporated entities. This obligation has no Swiss equivalent, no Swiss authority has instructed you to implement it, and no Geneva firm is implementing it. You are subject to it anyway — and you are in violation of it without knowing."

### Regulatory Dimension 05: Criminal vs. Administrative Documentation Architecture Requirements

**The regulatory fact:** nFADP Art.63 personal criminal liability operates on a different evidentiary standard (beyond reasonable doubt, personal intent or negligence) and a different enforcement logic (criminal prosecution) than administrative fine frameworks. Documentation built to satisfy carrier attestation requirements and nFADP administrative compliance is not designed for criminal defense evidentiary standards.

**Why it matters for the practice:** Every Geneva MD, partner, and practice leader is personally exposed under Art.63. The documentation architecture required to defend a criminal charge against AI governance decisions is different from the documentation architecture that satisfies carrier attestation. This is currently unnamed as a discrete service category in the Geneva market. The practice that names the distinction and builds the dual-standard documentation architecture addresses a need that no competitor has identified — and every professional in Geneva is exposed to a criminal liability risk they believe is covered by their existing documentation.

**The compliance framing:** "The documentation you have was built to satisfy carrier attestation requirements and nFADP administrative obligations. If you are charged under Art.63 — personal criminal liability for data protection violations — your carrier attestation documentation is not your defense. Criminal defense has a different evidentiary standard, a different architecture requirement, and a different relationship to your AI governance decisions than the documentation you have built so far. The documentation you need for both simultaneously is a different document — and the market has no service that builds it."

### Regulatory Dimension 06: The Swiss AI Coordination Deliberation as Price-Setting Variable

**The regulatory fact:** The Federal Council's ongoing deliberation on AI coordination authority — whether Bern establishes a standard-setting body or merely a contact point — will determine what the accountability standard is for Geneva MDs evaluating their AI governance adequacy for the next decade. The deliberation is currently forming through informal circuit channels. The outcome is not yet published.

**Why it matters for the practice:** The practice's Bern participation intelligence is the competitive asset. The deliberation is producing decisions that will shape the accountability standards the ICP is evaluated against — and the intelligence on where the deliberation is heading is only accessible through sustained Bern participation. The regulatory framing positions the practice's methodology as being formed in response to the same deliberation process that is forming the standards: "I am in the rooms when these decisions are made. That is what positions the methodology differently from anything you can get from reading what has been published."

**The compliance framing:** "The question of what 'adequate' AI governance means for the next decade is being decided right now — not in published doctrine, but in the Federal Council's deliberation process on AI coordination authority. The outcome is not yet public. The methodology I build for you is calibrated against what is forming in that process, not against what has already arrived in published form."

## The Regulatory Framing Rule

For each regulatory dimension, the positioning rule is: name the regulatory development as a conversion event, not as background information. The ICP who has experienced the retrospective confirmation moment does not need regulatory education. They need to hear that the regulatory change explains what they already know — that their situation is worse than they thought and the path forward requires a different architectural response than they are currently building.

The regulatory framing that converts:
- Names the regulatory development
- Connects it directly to the ICP's confirmed past exposure
- Positions the existing documentation as inadequate before the new methodology is introduced
- Frames the new methodology as ahead of the standard, not behind it

The regulatory framing that does not convert:
- Provides regulatory education as background context
- Treats the regulatory development as information the ICP should know before they decide whether to engage
- Explains what the regulatory development means in general terms

The regulatory briefing for the confirmed-problem ICP must begin with the regulatory event as confirmation, not explanation. *"FINMA Guidance 08/2024 changed what your attestation actually committed you to"* — not *"There is a FINMA guidance document you should know about."*
