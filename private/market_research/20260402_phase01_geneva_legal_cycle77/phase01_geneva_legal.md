### 3. TEF Rules (Supplemental Swiss Rules for Trust, Estate and Foundation Disputes) [NET-NEW detail]
- **In force:** July 1, 2025
- **Problem addressed:** Private wealth disputes involving multiple generations, complex cross-border assets, privacy needs — courts ill-suited
- **Key innovation:** "Entitled persons" concept (not just parties) — includes heirs, beneficiaries, unborn persons, those lacking legal capacity
- **Article 2:** Detailed notification mechanisms for entitled persons
- **Article 3:** Swiss Arbitration Centre Court can appoint arbitrators where entitled persons lack legal representation
- **Drafters:** David Brownbill KC (XXIV Old Buildings), Werner Jahnel (LALIVE), Stephanie Pfisterer (Homburger) among others
- **Source:** Swiss Arbitration Summit 2026 Innovation Conference
- **Business implication:** Geneva arbitration expanding into private wealth/estate space — opportunity for Geneva private client advisors

### 7. German Arbitration Reform 2026 [CROSS-BORDER RELEVANCE]
- **Jan 27, 2026:** German Federal Ministry of Justice published revised draft bill to modernize German arbitration
- **Key changes:** Digital proceedings, electronic awards, video hearings, publication of awards (with consent), multi-party arbitrator appointments
- **Sections 55 and 1031 ZPO** modified — nationality-based capacity rules replaced with lex fori/habitual residence; written form broadened beyond "writing"
- **Context:** Germany's Commercial Courts (English-language proceedings, virtual hearings, specialized senates) now competitive with arbitration
- **Source:** advant-beiten.com
- **Business implication:** Germany modernizing arbitration framework — EU/international arbitration landscape shifting; Switzerland must maintain competitiveness
