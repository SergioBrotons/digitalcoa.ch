# 01 — ICP: Geneva NGOs, IO, and Technology Ecosystem

## Target Segment: International Organizations, NGOs, Geneva Tech, Humanitarian Tech

### Organization Profile
- **Type:** UN agencies, international NGOs, Geneva-based tech startups, IO procurement arms, humanitarian tech organizations
- **Size:** 10-2,000+ employees
- **Budget:** CHF 1M-500M+
- **Geneva presence:** UNOG main hub, 30+ UN agencies, 400+ NGOs with Geneva offices

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Pain Points | Advisory Need |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| Executive Director / CEO | Strategy, funding, partnerships | Strategic advisory |

### Geneva ICP Examples
- **UN agencies:** UNHCR, WHO, UNCTAD, ITU, WMO, ILO, ICRC (Geneva hub)
- **NGOs:** MSF, ICRC, CICR, Geneva International, numerous human rights NGOs
- **Tech ecosystem:** cleantech, fintech, humanitarian tech startups
- **IO procurement:** UNOG, ITU, WHO procurement arms

### Pain Points (C123 Update)
1. **Deeptech commercialization gap** — Geneva EPFL/UniGe spin-offs struggling to bridge research-to-product; Series A gap wider post rate normalization
2. **UN/IO AI governance frameworks** — ITU, UNOG, WHO developing AI use policies; need advisory on responsible AI implementation
3. **Humanitarian logistics digitalization** — ICRC, UNHCR digital supply chain modernization; private sector tech partnership advisory
4. **Geneva commercial arbitration surge** — ICC/SCAI cases up; Geneva positioning as arbitration hub vs. Paris, London
5. **Sustainable finance talent pipeline** — ESG data, green bonds, impact measurement talent scarce in Geneva talent pool
6. **Digital identity for displaced populations** — UNHCR/WHO biometric/identity initiatives; humanitarian tech contracting opportunities

### Buying Triggers
- UN/IO AI governance framework mandates
- ICRC/humanitarian organization digital transformation funding approved
- Geneva arbitration hub marketing creating demand
- EPFL/UniGe spin-off funding round
- ESG mandate requiring talent assessment

### Buying Barriers
- UN/IO procurement bureaucracy (long vendor approval cycles)
- NGO funding constraints (project-based, grant-funded)
- Tech startup budget constraints
- Multi-stakeholder decision-making in IO

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva NGO/IO; English for international
- Mission-driven — alignment with impact values matters
- Relationship-driven; peer referrals preferred
- Formal processes in IO; faster in startups
