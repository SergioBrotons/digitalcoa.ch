### 5. FINMA Institutional Framework — Operational Description Available
**Source:** finma.ch/en/finma/finma-an-overview/ (live fetch)
- FINMA: independent supervisory authority since Jan 1, 2009
- Institutional, functional, and financial independence from political authorities
- Mandate: protect creditors, investors, policyholders; ensure financial market function
- Supervisory tasks: authorisation, supervision, enforcement (Art. 6 FINMA-G)
- Regulated entities: banks, insurance companies, financial institutions, collective investment schemes, asset managers, fund management companies
- Eight divisions; delegates supervisory work to audit firms and appointed agents
- Supervisory objectives: protect financial market clients, ensure functioning markets, strengthen confidence, ensure integrity, competitiveness, sustainability
- **Significance:** FINMA regulatory framework is clear and accessible. Eight-division structure confirms complexity of Swiss financial regulation. Delegation model (audit firms, agents) is a relevant governance consideration for supervised entities.
