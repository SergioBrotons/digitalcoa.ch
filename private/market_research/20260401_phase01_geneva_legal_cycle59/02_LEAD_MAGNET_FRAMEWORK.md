## Problem Statement (4 layers — C59 Update)

### Layer 1: Legal obligation gap (FADP + AI Ordinance + Section 301 — ACTIVE)
Geneva law firms face three concurrent frameworks: FADP Art. 21 (human review of automated decisions), the Federal AI Ordinance (in force Jan 1, 2025 — now 15+ months operative), and **US Section 301 with a written comment deadline of April 15, 2026** — 14 days away. Using AI in cross-border trade compliance, export controls, or US-adjacent advisory without a documented governance framework means you're exposed on a live regulatory deadline.

### Layer 2: Professional standard gap (SBA)
The Swiss Bar Association Guidelines (June 2024) established a standard of care for AI use. Non-binding, but it sets the benchmark. If a client complains or a court reviews your AI-assisted work, the question won't be "did you use AI?" — it will be "did you have a governance framework?"

### Layer 3: Liability exposure gap (malpractice)
Swiss legal malpractice insurers have flagged AI-assisted work. "The AI recommended it" is not a defense. Your firm is liable for every AI-assisted decision — including AI-powered tariff classification and export control screening under an evolving US regime.

### Layer 4: Multi-event regulatory convergence (C59 Update — most acute yet)
Four concurrent pressure points:
- **Section 301:** April 15 written comments deadline — 14 days
- **US tariff regime instability:** Supreme Court February 2026 ruling + new 10% duty for 150 days + ongoing negotiations
- **G7 Évian:** June 15-17, 2026 — ~75 days
- **GPAI:** August 2, 2026 — ~4 months
- **CoE AI Convention:** Swiss consultation draft expected end of 2026 (~9 months)

## The Bridge: Envelope vs. Letter

**Automating the envelope (safe):** Routing, scheduling, reminders, time tracking, billing preparation, document formatting, conflict check support. These don't touch privileged content. Safe to automate NOW.

**Automating the letter (needs governance):** AI interpreting case facts, drafting legal positions, generating advice, analyzing contracts, advising on Section 301 compliance, AI-powered tariff classification. These require a governance framework before automation.

The five workflows in the "envelope" category represent 8–14 hours/week recoverable — without creating new legal exposure.
