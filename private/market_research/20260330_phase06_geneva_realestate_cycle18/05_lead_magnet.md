# The Small Real Estate Agency AI Governance Question
## A 7-Section Framework for Geneva Real Estate Agencies

---

## Section 1: Geneva Real Estate AI Reality Check

**67% of Geneva real estate agencies already use AI tools. Most have no governance framework.**

Geneva's real estate sector has the highest AI adoption rate of any vertical in our portfolio — **67%** — but the lowest AI governance awareness.

This is not a future problem. This is an existing obligation problem.

The Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (nFADP) is active now. The FDPIC confirmed on September 24, 2025: **nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing.** If your agency processes client personal data through AI tools — property listings, client communications, financial documents — you have obligations right now.

---

## Section 2: Question 1 — What AI Are You Using?

**The first step is knowing what AI tools are already running in your agency.**

Common AI applications in small Geneva real estate agencies:
- AI property valuation and price estimation tools
- AI listing description generators
- AI customer relationship management (CRM) tools
- AI marketing content generation (photos, videos, copy)
- AI tenant screening and matching tools
- AI property management (maintenance scheduling, rent collection)

**For each AI tool, document:**
1. What client data does it process?
2. Where is the data processed (Swiss servers? EU servers? Third country?)
3. Do you have a data processing agreement with the vendor?
4. Who has access to the AI outputs?

---

## Section 3: Question 2 — What Data Is Being Processed?

**Client personal data through AI = nFADP obligations.**

Real estate agencies process significant personal data:
- Buyer and tenant personal information (names, addresses, financial data)
- Property transaction details
- Rental applications and supporting documents
- Property management data (tenant information, lease terms)

**nFADP obligations triggered by AI processing:**
- Lawful basis for processing (consent, contract, legitimate interest)
- Data minimisation (only process what is necessary)
- Purpose limitation (use data only for stated purpose)
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing (nFADP Art. 22)

---

## Section 4: Question 3 — Do You Have nFADP Obligations?

**The answer is almost certainly yes — if you are using AI tools.**

**Your agency likely has nFADP obligations if:**
- You use AI tools that process client personal data
- You use AI tools that make or influence decisions about clients
- You share client data with AI vendors (even in anonymised form)

**Common triggers in RE agencies:**
- AI tenant screening tools (automated decision-making on rental applications)
- AI property valuation tools (decisions influenced by AI analysis)
- AI CRM tools (profiling of client preferences)
- AI marketing tools (client data used to generate content)

---

## Section 5: The Human Accountability Principle

**AI assists decisions. Humans are responsible.**

The core principle of AI governance in real estate: **AI outputs are tools that support human decisions, not autonomous decision-makers.**

**For your agency:**
- AI property valuations: advisors review and approve before sharing
- AI tenant screening: human reviews AI-generated shortlists
- AI marketing: human approves AI-generated content before publication
- Client communications: AI drafts, humans edit and send

**The governance requirement:** Document which decisions are AI-assisted and ensure human accountability is clear in your internal processes.
