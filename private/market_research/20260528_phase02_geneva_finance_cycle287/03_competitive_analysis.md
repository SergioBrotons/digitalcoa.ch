## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

### Existing Market Offerings

**1. Generic AI Governance Frameworks**
- ISO 42001 (AI Management System) — enterprise-scale, designed for organizations with dedicated compliance functions, not applicable to 2-person MD + PM boutique operations
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework — US-origin, not mapped to Swiss regulatory environment, requires adaptation for Swiss market that doesn't exist
- EU AI Act compliance guides — designed for EU jurisdiction, not directly applicable to Swiss regulatory environment even where content is relevant

**What they miss:** Generic frameworks require adaptation to Swiss regulatory architecture, FINMA expectations, nFADP obligations, carrier attestation requirements, and Geneva-specific institutional dynamics. No Geneva-market service offers this adaptation.

**2. Big Four AI Governance Consulting**
- Limited applicability to boutique scale (50-200 employees, 1-3 managing directors)
- Priced for institutional clients, not proportionate to boutique budget
- No Geneva-specific regulatory calibration — Big Four frameworks are global products adapted locally
- Carried AI governance knowledge, not Geneva accountability ecosystem pattern knowledge

**What they miss:** Big Four don't operate at boutique wealth management scale and don't have Geneva-specific pattern intelligence. Their frameworks are designed for institutions, not applicable without substantial adaptation.

**3. Swiss Banking Association Guidelines**
- SBA AI Guidelines published June 2024 — principle-level guidance, not operational methodology
- Designed for institutions with compliance infrastructure
- At boutique scale, principles without methodology produce compliance theater — the guideline is acknowledged but cannot be operationalized

**What they miss:** The gap between principle and operational methodology is where boutique wealth managers operate without guidance. SBA guidelines name the problem without solving it at boutique scale.

**4. Carrier-Provided Resources**
- Carrier AI risk questionnaires (third-generation) — the instrument creating exposure, not a solution to it
- Carrier ISAE 3000 reports on vendor platforms — prove vendor controls, not professional governance
- Carrier guidance on completing attestation forms — explains what to fill in, not what you're committing to personally

**What they miss:** Carriers have no interest in disclosing the personal liability implications of what MDs are signing. Carrier resources are designed to satisfy carrier underwriting requirements, not to protect MD personal interests.

**5. Legal firm AI governance offerings (Geneva market)**
- Some Geneva law firms offering AI governance advice as adjacent to data protection practice
- nFADP compliance focus, not wealth management specific
- Not designed for the intersection of carrier attestation, institutional client questionnaires, and personal MD liability

**What they miss:** Legal counsel addressing AI governance from data protection perspective misses the wealth management-specific accountability architecture (carrier attestation, institutional counterparty requirements, portfolio construction AI accountability).

**6. Technology Vendors' AI Governance Documentation**
- AI vendors (e.g., Franklin, Orion, portfolio management systems) providing documentation about their AI tools
- Vendor documentation addresses what the tool does, not how the boutique uses it
- Cannot be used to demonstrate boutique professional governance to institutional clients or carriers

**What they miss:** Tool documentation is not usage documentation. Institutional clients asking for methodology evidence need to understand how the boutique uses AI, not what the tool is capable of.

### What's Actually Missing from the Market

**1. Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review Service**
Missing: No Geneva market service offering MD-focused review of what carrier attestation commits the signatory to personally.

Current state: MD signs carrier attestation (written by carrier legal team, no independent counsel from signatory perspective) committing to undefined adequacy standard with personal liability implications.

Why it's missing: Carriers have no incentive to disclose what MDs are committing to. No Geneva professional services provider has built a service specifically addressing MD personal exposure in carrier attestation.

The service would need to: map what the attestation commits the MD to, identify where personal liability attaches, evaluate whether existing practice satisfies the commitments, and produce documentation architecture that can be built before the signing deadline.

**2. Institutional Client AI Questionnaire Response Architecture**
Missing: No Geneva service offering portfolio-wide institutional questionnaire response infrastructure for boutique wealth managers.

Current state: Boutique receiving AI governance questionnaires from multiple institutional clients, responding to each as isolated event, creating potential inconsistency across responses and no systematic tracking.

Why it's missing: Requires cross-institutional-counterparty intelligence (what are the converging requirements, what creates cross-response commitments, how to maintain portfolio-wide coherence) that only sustained institutional participation produces.

The service would need to: build response architecture that satisfies multiple institutional requirements simultaneously, produce coherent evidence package across client relationships, and manage the disclosure/non-disclosure decision for each question category.

**3. nFADP Art.15/21 AI-Inferred Content Response Protocol**
Missing: No Geneva service offering nFADP Art.15/21 response protocol specifically for AI-inferred content in wealth management client reports.

Current state: Boutique sending client reports containing AI-inferred content (performance attribution, risk tolerance inferences, financial intention projections) without nFADP analysis of whether profiling notifications are required, whether Art.21 automated decision explanations are owed, and what the firm's response architecture should be.

Why it's missing: Requires understanding of both nFADP automated decision/profiling obligations and wealth management-specific AI inference patterns — a combination that doesn't exist in any single Geneva service.

The service would need to: identify AI-inferred content in client reports, evaluate nFADP notification/explanation obligations, build response protocol for data subject requests on AI-inferred content.

**4. Geneva Wealth Management AI Governance Documentation Architecture**
Missing: No Geneva-market proportional methodology for boutique-scale AI governance documentation that satisfies carrier third-gen requirements, institutional client questionnaires, and nFADP obligations simultaneously.

Current state: Boutique building documentation reactively against individual requirements without coherence mapping, producing documentation that doesn't hold together as a system.

Why it's missing: Requires sustained participation in carrier questionnaire evolution, institutional client requirement formation, and regulatory doctrine development — pattern knowledge that only sustained participation produces.

The service would need to: build documentation system coherent across all three requirement tracks, design documents that serve multiple audiences without inconsistent signals.

**5. AI Model Update Governance Protocol for Boutique Wealth Managers**
Missing: No Geneva service offering AI model update governance protocol specifically designed for boutique wealth management operations.

Current state: Boutique experiencing model updates as silent operational changes with no detection mechanism, no reassessment trigger, no documentation protocol for what changed.

Why it's missing: Boutique wealth managers don't have IT infrastructure to track model version changes across the multiple AI tools used in portfolio management. No service has been built for this specific operational gap.

The service would need to: establish model version tracking architecture proportionate to boutique operations, define reassessment triggers when model updates occur, build documentation protocol for what changed and what decisions were affected.

**6. Geneva SRO AI Guidance Interpretation Service**
Missing: No Geneva market service offering SRO-specific AI guidance interpretation for wealth management boutiques.

Current state: SRO has not issued AI guidance for wealth managers. Boutique interpreting SRO silence in conflicting ways with no Geneva-specific interpretation framework.

Why it's missing: SRO guidance interpretation requires sustained participation in the Geneva accountability ecosystem to understand what the SRO's silence means relative to what obligations are actually forming.

The service would need to: map what Geneva-specific AI obligations are forming through SRO participation patterns, provide interpretation of where SRO guidance will land when it arrives, build proportionate documentation for boutique scale.

**7. Pre-Carrier-Renewal AI Governance Review**
Missing: No discrete service category specifically addressing the 90-day pre-carrier-renewal window as a distinct accountability occasion for Geneva wealth managers.

Current state: Boutique treats carrier renewal as administrative compliance event, not as specific accountability occasion requiring particular documentation architecture built in a defined window.

Why it's missing: The 90-day pre-renewal window is when MD can build evidence architecture for decisions already made. After renewal, the window is closed. No service has been built specifically for this timing property.

The service would need to: map existing practice against carrier third-gen requirements, identify evidence gaps for decisions made during undocumented window, build proportionate documentation architecture before renewal deadline.
