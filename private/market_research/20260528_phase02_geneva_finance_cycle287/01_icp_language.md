## How Geneva Boutique Wealth Managers Talk About AI Problems

### Primary Registration Language (French)

- *"Notre firme utilise l'IA pour l'analyse de portefeuille"* — used to mean compliance checkbox, now increasingly a liability admission trigger
- *"Nous avons un processus de validation humaine"* — the go-to response to institutional AI governance questionnaires; increasingly insufficient as clients probe the specific mechanism, not the existence of a process
- *"Notre carrier nous a demandé de signer une attestation"* — the acute pain point phrasing; personal signature anxiety is dominant in French-speaking wealth managers
- *"Le client nous a envoyé un questionnaire de 40 pages sur l'IA"* — the crisis trigger phrase; the institutional DDQ as moment of recognition

### Primary Registration Language (English)

- *"We've been using AI tools for portfolio analysis for a couple of years now"* — disarming opener that typically signals exposure the speaker doesn't recognize
- *"Our compliance team reviewed the carrier attestation"* — rarely true at boutique scale where compliance team is the MD
- *"I signed it because the carrier required it"* — personal signature without personal understanding of what was committed
- *"The institutional client asked about our AI governance framework"* — triggers awareness of documentation gap

### Forum and LinkedIn Language Patterns

**Carrying Attestation Language:**
- "carrier attestation" — used generically, almost never with specific generation referenced (third-generation is what creates the exposure, but firms don't know to specify)
- "ISAE 3000" — appearing more frequently as carriers reference their own reports, boutique trying to understand what the carrier's own certification means for their obligations
- "AWS 183 services" — specific technical detail appearing in third-gen carrier questionnaires that surfaced unexpectedly for many boutiques

**SRO Silence Language:**
- "I asked my SRO about AI and they said they'd get back to me" — the institutional silence as experienced by boutique MDs
- "FINMA hasn't issued guidance on this specifically" — used as false comfort; absence of guidance is not comfort when personal liability is on the line
- "We don't have a specific framework for this" — confession language that appears when boutique is responding to institutional questionnaire

**Institutional Client Questionnaire Language:**
- "due diligence questionnaire on AI" — formal framing, typically 20-40 pages from institutional clients with sophisticated internal AI governance
- "explain how AI was used in our portfolio construction" — the specific question that surfaces the attribution problem
- "what is your AI governance policy" — generic on its surface, answers reveal the gap between policy and operational evidence
- "provide evidence of human oversight" — the evidentiary standard that exposes documentation absence

**nFADP Personal Liability Language:**
- "Article 63" — appearing in French Geneva wealth management discourse as MDs begin to understand personal criminal liability exposure
- "responsabilité personnelle du director" — French formulation of the personal accountability concern
- "traitement automatisé des décisions" — Art.21 nFADP language appearing in compliance discussions at firms previously focused only on Art.6
- "profilage client" — profiling language surfacing as MDs realize client report content is AI-inferred, not just data-processed

**Entry Point Language (when they realize they need help):**
- "my carrier renewal is in 90 days and they're asking questions I've never seen before" — specific timing language tied to carrier questionnaire evolution
- "we received a questionnaire from a client and I don't know how to answer page 12" — specific moment of exposure recognition
- "I signed something and I'm not sure what it means" — the post-signing realization as distinct from pre-signing preparation
- "our AI tool updated and I don't know what changed" — the model update trigger as discovery moment

## What They're Actually Concerned About (In Their Own Words)

1. **Signature anxiety**: "What exactly did I sign when I signed the carrier attestation?"
2. **Client questionnaire paralysis**: "How do I respond to a 40-page institutional AI governance questionnaire when I don't have the documentation they're asking for?"
3. **Silent model updates**: "The AI tool updated and I don't know what changed in our portfolio recommendations"
4. **SRO vacuum**: "My SRO hasn't said anything about AI obligations — is that because there's nothing to know, or because they haven't figured it out yet?"
5. **FINMA expectations opacity**: "FINMA hasn't issued specific AI guidance for wealth managers — does that mean we're fine, or does it mean we're exposed and they just haven't said so?"
6. **Personal liability crystallization**: "Article 63 nFADP — does that actually apply to me as a wealth manager?"
7. **Institutional counterparty requirements**: "Our biggest client sent us an AI governance questionnaire — they're asking questions about portfolio construction methodology, not just policy"
8. **Documentation retroactivity**: "I need to document decisions made over the past two years — can I do that now, or is it too late?"

## Language Warning Signs

**Phrases indicating higher exposure than ICP realizes:**
- "We use AI for portfolio analysis" (without evidence architecture)
- "Our PM reviews all AI-assisted recommendations" (without documented review)
- "We're compliant with Swiss regulations" (without specific regulatory mapping)
- "We have an AI governance policy" (without operational evidence)

**Phrases indicating accurate exposure recognition (better qualified prospects):**
- "I need to understand what I committed to when I signed the carrier attestation"
- "The institutional client is asking for methodology evidence, not just policy"
- "Our nFADP profiling notification was never issued — I need to understand the exposure"
- "The 90-day window before carrier renewal is when I can actually do something"
