## The FINMA Reality Check

FINMA published Guidance 08/2024 in December 2024. It established:

- A requirement for **clear governance structures** around AI use
- An **AI inventory** — every tool, classified by risk
- **Third-party vendor due diligence** — because most GenAI tools are vendor-hosted
- **Staff training** — personnel using AI must understand the risks
- **Ongoing monitoring** — AI performance tracked continuously
- An **FINMA AI Desk** — a dedicated oversight function
- **On-site inspections** — FINMA is already in the field examining AI applications

The guidance is technology-neutral and principles-based. But it's also already operative. And FINMA has been examining AI in asset management since Q4 2023.

## The Shadow AI Problem in Wealth Management

GenAI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and similar) are:

- Easy to adopt without formal procurement
- Often used by relationship managers and research analysts directly
- Processing client data: portfolio information, client communications, investment theses
- Subject to vendor terms that may allow data retention and model training
- Outside traditional IT governance processes

This is the 91%. And it's the 67-point gap's source.

## The 8-Section FINMA-Aligned Governance Checklist

**Section 1: AI Inventory**
- [ ] All GenAI tools used by staff identified (tool name, user role, data types)
- [ ] Risk classification assigned (low/medium/high/critical)
- [ ] AI inventory documented and current

**Section 2: Governance Structure**
- [ ] AI responsibility assigned at management level
- [ ] Decision rights for AI adoption documented
- [ ] Escalation path for AI incidents defined

**Section 3: Vendor Due Diligence**
- [ ] DPAs in place with all AI vendors
- [ ] Data residency confirmed (US = CLOUD Act exposure)
- [ ] Retention and deletion terms reviewed
- [ ] Sub-processor list obtained and assessed

**Section 4: Risk Management**
- [ ] Model risk assessed (bias, explainability, robustness)
- [ ] Operational risk assessed (IT, cyber, vendor)
- [ ] Legal and reputational risks documented
- [ ] Mitigation controls in place

**Section 5: FINMA Compliance**
- [ ] Art. 21 FADP: human review available for automated client decisions
- [ ] Art. 5(c) FADP: lawful basis documented for all AI processing
- [ ] Art. 16 FADP + DPIA: completed for high-risk AI uses
- [ ] Privacy by Design: evaluated at AI procurement stage

**Section 6: Client Transparency**
- [ ] AI use disclosed where material to client outcomes
- [ ] AI-generated content reviewed before delivery
- [ ] Client consent documented where required

**Section 7: Staff Training**
- [ ] AI training completed for all personnel using AI tools
- [ ] Acceptable use policy communicated and acknowledged
- [ ] Incident reporting process communicated

**Section 8: Ongoing Monitoring**
- [ ] AI performance metrics defined and tracked
- [ ] Periodic governance review scheduled (at least annually)
- [ ] New AI tool evaluation process documented
- [ ] CARF/CRS 2.0 compliance maintained for reporting obligations
