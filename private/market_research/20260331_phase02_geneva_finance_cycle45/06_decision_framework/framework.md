# Phase 02 — Geneva Finance/WM — Cycle 45
## Decision Framework: Boutique Wealth Manager AI Governance

### 6-Decision Framework Aligned to FINMA Circ. 08/2024

---

## Decision 0: AI Inventory (Shadow AI Audit)
**Entry point — low friction, high value**

- Map all GenAI tools currently in use (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.)
- Document: tool name, user role, data types processed, frequency
- Classify by risk: low (internal research) / medium (client-adjacent) / high (client data) / critical (investment decisions)
- Identify: which tools process client portfolio data, communications, or research

**Deliverable:** AI Inventory Register with risk classification

---

## Decision 1: Governance Structure
**FINMA Circ. 08/2024 — Governance requirement**

- AI responsibility assigned: who at management level owns AI risk?
- Decision rights: who approves new AI tools?
- Escalation: how are AI failures or incidents reported and resolved?
- Board reporting: is AI governance a standing agenda item?

**Deliverable:** AI Governance Charter — responsibility assignment document

---

## Decision 2: Vendor Due Diligence
**FINMA Circ. 08/2024 — Third-party risk + FINMA AI Desk focus**

- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) in place with all AI vendors?
- Data residency: US-based tools = CLOUD Act exposure for Swiss client data
- Retention: what happens to prompts and outputs after the session?
- Deletion: can client data be permanently deleted on request?
- Sub-processors: who else in the vendor's infrastructure has access?
- FINMA expectation: institutions using AI in critical processes should contact FINMA in advance

**Deliverable:** Vendor Risk Register + DPA status tracker

---

## Decision 3: FINMA Circ. 08/2024 Compliance Assessment
**10 requirements from FINMA guidance**

1. Governance structure — clear assignment of AI responsibility ✓/✗
2. AI inventory — all tools documented with risk classification ✓/✗
3. Risk management — model risk + operational risk assessed ✓/✗
4. Robustness — AI outputs validated before use in decisions ✓/✗
5. Transparency — AI use disclosed where material ✓/✗
6. Non-discrimination — no unjustified bias in AI-assisted decisions ✓/✗
7. Staff training — personnel trained on AI risks ✓/✗
8. Third-party risk — vendor due diligence completed ✓/✗
9. Incident response — AI failures detected, reported, remediated ✓/✗
10. Ongoing monitoring — AI performance continuously tracked ✓/✗

**Deliverable:** FINMA Compliance Gap Assessment — requirement by requirement

---

## Decision 4: FADP Compliance (Art. 21 / Art. 5(c) / DPIA)
**AI tools processing client data**

- Art. 21 FADP: Do any AI tools make automated decisions with legal or significant effects on clients? Is human review available and documented?
- Art. 5(c) FADP: Is there a lawful basis for processing client data through each AI tool?
- Art. 16 FADP + DPIA: For high-risk AI uses — investment recommendations, risk assessment, client scoring — is a DPIA required and completed?
- Privacy by Design: Was data protection evaluated before adopting each AI tool?
- Privacy by Default: Are tools configured to minimum necessary data processing?

**Deliverable:** FADP AI Compliance Assessment

---

## Decision 5: CARF / CRS 2.0 Integration
**Regulatory compliance layer**

- CRS 2.0: Is AI used in any client onboarding, due diligence, or reporting workflows? Are CARF-ready systems in place even though reporting is postponed to 2027?
- AI in AML/KYC: Does AI-assisted client screening meet FINMA expectations?
- AI in investment research: Are AI-generated analyses documented and attributable?
- FATF evaluation: Is AI governance documented for the mutual evaluation?

**Deliverable:** CARF/CRS 2.0 AI Integration Review
