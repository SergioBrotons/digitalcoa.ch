# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 06 DigitalCoa.ch

## Research Question
What is DigitalCoa.ch's positioning, target client, competitive differentiator, and highest-value growth strategy?

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: DigitalCoa.ch occupies a specific, defensible niche
- Position: Executive advisory on AI strategy and governance for Swiss financial institutions
- Core differentiator: FINMA regulatory fluency + AI governance specialization + implementation linkage (via Cytria)
- This is NOT a generalist strategy consultancy — it's a specialized niche advisory
- **Validated by C96 research:** FINMA AI survey (50% AI adoption, 25% more coming) creates immediate demand for exactly this kind of advisory

### Finding 2: The target client is well-defined but needs traffic validation
- Primary ICP: C-suite at Swiss banks, asset managers, insurance companies
- Trigger: New AI adoption, FINMA examination, regulatory change
- Barrier: "We already have a Big 4 relationship" — must address directly
- **Gap:** No clear view of current website traffic volume or lead conversion rate

### Finding 3: The three-brand ecosystem (DigitalCoa.ch + Cytria + Substack) creates a strategic advantage
- DigitalCoa.ch: Executive advisory
- Cytria: AI implementation (can receive referrals from advisory for execution)
- Substack: Thought leadership + audience building
- Brotons.net: Hub that connects all three
- **This ecosystem creates a complete client journey from awareness to implementation**

### Finding 4: Lead generation path is clear but implicit
- Current path: LinkedIn/Substack → DigitalCoa.ch → Briefing call → Engagement
- The "Executive AI Briefing" is the implicit lead magnet — needs formalization
- Regulatory Intelligence Briefing could be a recurring touchpoint
- **Gap:** No visible content on DigitalCoa.ch that functions as a lead magnet

### Finding 5: Pricing and services need clarity for conversion
- Services: AI Governance, Executive Decision Architecture (mentioned on brotons.net)
- Pricing: Not visible on DigitalCoa.ch
- **Unclear:** What is the entry-level offering? What is the typical engagement scope?
- **Recommendation:** Create a clear service ladder: Briefing → Assessment → Engagement

## Strategic Implications for DigitalCoa.ch

### Priority Actions
1. **Formalize the "Executive AI Briefing"** — create a formal offering page, make it the primary CTA
2. **Create a service ladder** — Briefing (free/low-cost) → Readiness Assessment (paid) → Advisory Engagement (retained)
3. **Build the regulatory intelligence offering** — quarterly briefing as recurring touchpoint
4. **Leverage the ecosystem** — Cytria as implementation fallback; Substack as audience builder
5. **Publish AI governance content on DigitalCoa.ch** — this IS the lead magnet for the target ICP

- "Not a generalist consultancy. A specialist in AI governance, regulatory risk, and executive decision architecture."
- "We help leaders make AI decisions with clarity and regulatory confidence."

## C115 Phase 06 Findings (2026-04-04)

### C115 Finding 1: GENFM board decision trigger (March 2026)
GENFM replaced LIBOR/CHF LIBOR in March 2026. Geneva financial institutions face active repricing, IT, and risk decisions. Decision Diagnostic (2-5 days) well-suited for board-level GENFM decision assessment.

### C115 Finding 2: Bilingual positioning as structural market gap
Most Geneva boutiques = FR-only; most international firms = EN-only. DigitalCoa.ch bilingual EN+FR fills a structural gap for executives operating across cantonal and international contexts. Strategic, not cosmetic.

### C115 Finding 3: Swiss Board Diversity Ordinance (2299a) governance decisions
FINMA-supervised institution boards must comply with expanded diversity requirements. Board composition, expertise gaps, succession = Governance Architecture territory. Direct link: regulatory obligation → advisory service need.

### C115 Finding 4: CARF July 2026 deadline
Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework implementation in Switzerland. Geneva crypto managers, commodity traders, cross-border structures face urgent compliance decisions. Decision Diagnostic serves CARF compliance prioritization decisions at executive/board level.

### C115 Finding 5: Geneva peer referral networks as primary trust infrastructure
Brotons.net creates awareness; Geneva peer networks (CCIG, alumni, diplomatic circles) generate trusted introductions. Advisory credibility flows through personal referral. DigitalCoa.ch must invest in network position, not just content position.

### C115 Finding 6: Multi-jurisdictional regulatory calendar coordination
FINMA + EU + US + UK regulatory deadlines non-aligned and simultaneous. Executive decision prioritization overload is the pain. Personal Decision Infrastructure directly addresses executive capacity to manage multi-jurisdictional regulatory decision calendars.
