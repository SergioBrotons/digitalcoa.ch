## Findings

1. **Geneva Board Advisory — AI Agenda Item as Entry Point for Board Mandates** — Swiss corporate governance (Swiss Code of Obligations Art. 707) requires board oversight of risk and technology. Federal Council AI civil liability bill (Q1 2026) elevates AI to board-level liability exposure. Geneva corporate boards (CHF 50M-500M revenue family enterprises, multinationals, financial institutions) need AI governance as a standing board agenda item. DigitalCoa.ch opportunity: position the "Board AI Briefing" as a standalone entry product — a 90-minute board session on AI governance obligations, liability exposure, and decision framework. This opens governance advisory mandates at the board level without requiring prior advisory relationship. Target: board presidents and corporate secretaries at Geneva headquarters companies.

2. **Swiss Executive AI Regulatory Intelligence Subscription — Geneva Institutional Intelligence** — Geneva executives need to track AI regulatory developments across: FINMA, Federal Council AI bill, Council of Europe AI Convention implementation, EU AI Act extraterritorial implications, and cantonal (Geneva) digital strategy. Current intelligence sources are fragmented: law firm client alerts, industry association newsletters, media coverage. A curated executive-facing AI regulatory intelligence product (monthly briefing + real-time alerts) would serve Geneva executives who cannot monitor all sources themselves. Service design: monthly 1-page Geneva AI Regulatory Radar; quarterly 30-minute live briefing; ad-hoc alerts for material regulatory events. This is distinct from Brotons.net newsletter (educational) — this is executive-level decision-relevant intelligence. Subscription model creates recurring revenue and relationship touchpoints.

3. **Geneva Life Sciences Executive Advisory — Biotech/Medtech Board AI Governance** — Geneva's life sciences cluster (Roche/Sandoos Basel, but Geneva has biotech, medical devices, and diagnostics firms) faces distinct AI governance pressures: FDA/EMA AI medical device regulations, Swissmedic AI device classification, AI-assisted clinical trial design, and AI IP strategy. Geneva life sciences executives (C-suite, board members) making AI investment decisions without dedicated advisory support. Advisory opportunity: AI governance board briefing for Geneva life sciences; AI medical device regulatory pathway advisory; AI clinical trial compliance framework; Geneva medtech AI IP strategy. Distinct from general Geneva SME advisory — life sciences executives have unique regulatory exposure (FDA/EMA/Swissmedic) and unique AI liability framing (patient safety).

4. **DigitalCoa.ch Service Page Conversion Architecture — Decision Stage Content Mapping** — Each DigitalCoa.ch service page should map to a specific Geneva executive decision stage: (1) Awareness: "What is AI governance and why does it matter now?" (Brotons.net); (2) Consideration: "How do I assess my company's AI risk?" (DigitalCoa.ch diagnostic landing page); (3) Decision: "What does an AI governance engagement look like?" (service page with explicit engagement structure and fee transparency); (4) Retention: "How do I deepen my AI governance practice?" (follow-on services). Prior cycles focused on service taxonomy; this finding focuses on content architecture per decision stage and the Geneva-specific framing for each stage. Fee transparency on service pages reduces decision friction for Geneva executives.

5. **Swiss German Executive Market Entry — Zurich/Basel AI Governance Positioning** — DigitalCoa.ch current positioning is Geneva-centric; Swiss German market (Zurich, Basel, Bern) represents 60%+ of Swiss GDP and distinct executive culture. Swiss German executives (Zurich banking, Basel pharma, Bern federal) have different decision-making patterns: more direct, faster decision cycles, less relationship-dependent than Geneva. DigitalCoa.ch expansion to Swiss German market requires: (1) distinct content positioning for Zurich/Basel regulatory context (FINMA vs. Geneva cantonal); (2) Swiss German-language executive summary pages (not full translation); (3) Zurich-based executive events/introductions. Prior cycles mentioned Swiss German market as expansion (C147 P6); this finding specifies the market entry mechanics.

6. **Geneva SME Growth Company AI Governance — Post-Funding Compliance Trigger** — Geneva venture-backed or PE-backed growth companies (CHF 5M-50M revenue) face AI governance compliance triggers at specific events: Series B/C funding (due diligence requires AI risk documentation), board composition change (new members require AI briefing), acquisition interest (AI IP and liability becomes deal issue), EU AI Act market entry (high-risk AI system designation). These companies are too small for Big 4 but too sophisticated for generalist advisors. Advisory opportunity: "AI governance for growth companies" packaged service — compliance framework + board briefing + documentation template package. Distinct from Geneva SME advisory (C143 P6) — this specifically targets the funding/compliance event trigger moments that create urgent advisory demand.
