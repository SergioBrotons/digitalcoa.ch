# 01 — ICP: Brotons.net Audience & Platform Strategy

## Target Segment: Geneva-Based Thinkers, Executives, and Institutional Professionals

### Reader Profile
- **Type:** Geneva-based executives, policy professionals, academics, institutional investors, IO/NGO professionals, legal and finance professionals
- **Reading behavior:** Thought leadership focused; values depth over breadth; bilingual EN/FR preferred; return readership high
- **Platform preference:** Owned website + newsletter (Substack); some LinkedIn

### Reader Decision-Making Unit
| Segment | Profile | Content Need |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| Geneva executives | C-suite, MD, partner level | Strategic framing, Geneva market intelligence |
| Policy professionals | IO/NGO, Geneva diplomatic | Institutional analysis, regulatory interpretation |
| Academics/researchers | IHEID, UniGe, think tanks | Analytical depth, original framing |
| Institutional investors | Geneva banks, family offices | Market intelligence, risk signals |
| Legal/finance professionals | Geneva law firms, advisors | Regulatory analysis, practical implications |

### Known Reader Segments
- LinkedIn connections: Geneva C-suite, MD level, policy professionals
- Substack subscribers: Mix of EN/FR readers, institutional and individual
- Web visitors: Organic search from Geneva, direct traffic, LinkedIn referral

### Pain Points (C123 Update)
1. **Content fatigue — too much mediocre content** — Geneva professionals overwhelmed by generic business content; high-quality, Geneva-specific thought leadership稀缺
2. **Newsletter format staleness** — Standard newsletter format losing engagement; readers seeking format innovation (audio, visual, interactive)
3. **EN+FR bilingual routing inefficiency** — Current routing based on browser/language detection inconsistent; readers wanting seamless bilingual experience
4. **Substack vs owned website routing** — Substack newsletter driving traffic to website but conversion path unclear; duplicate content risk
5. **Distribution channel overload** — LinkedIn algorithm changes, Substack algorithmic suppression; owned audience building increasingly important
6. **Thought leadership quality verification** — Readers skeptical of content without genuine expertise backing; credibility-based selection increasing

### Buying/Engagement Triggers
- Geneva institutional event (WEF, UN events, regulatory announcements)
- Regulatory change affecting Geneva (SNB, FINMA, EU developments)
- Geopolitical events with Geneva implications
- Return visitor seeking specific topic
- Referral from trusted peer

### Buying/Engagement Barriers
- Substack subscription fatigue
- Too many newsletters competing for attention
- Content discovery depending on platform algorithms
- Time-constrained professionals

### Language / Cultural Fit
- Bilingual EN/FR — both equally credible
- Geneva institutional voice — elevated but accessible
- Independent, non-corporate tone
- No marketing language — intellectual credibility first
