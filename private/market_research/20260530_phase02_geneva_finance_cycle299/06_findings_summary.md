### Finding C299-P2-F1: Portfolio Construction AI Authorization Chain as Discrete Sequential Integrity Requirement
**What it is:** Portfolio construction AI operates continuously and directly affects client financial outcomes — categorically different from advisory AI used for reporting. The sequential chaining from AI output to human authorization moment with own evidence standard is a discrete documentation requirement no Geneva market methodology addresses. Carrier third-generation attestation specifically probes "AI-influenced portfolio decisions" and requires documented human authorization.

**Why it matters:** Boutiques are being asked to document a sequential integrity requirement they never built a record for. The continuous nature of portfolio construction AI makes it architecturally incompatible with documentation frameworks designed for discrete decisions. No proportionate methodology for continuous authorization documentation exists at boutique WM scale — because this category has not been named.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The AI that builds your portfolios is different from the AI that generates reports. The authorization chain from AI recommendation to human approval in portfolio construction — we never documented that. Now the carrier is asking for evidence we don't have."

**Distinct from C214–C298:** Named discrete chaining architecture gap, not general evidence naming or third-gen carrier evidence.

### Finding C299-P2-F3: Carrier Attestation as Personal Liability Instrument Without Pre-Signing Review Market for WM
**What it is:** Carrier attestation forms require the managing director's personal signature on undefined "adequate AI governance" standards designed by underwriting logic, not professional liability architecture. No Geneva market service offers pre-signing personal liability review for WM managing directors. Medical practitioners have developed this service; WM managing directors are structurally equivalent without an equivalent. The signature creates personal exposure under nFADP Art. 63 that carrier D&O insurance does not explicitly discharge.

**Why it matters:** The documentation that would protect against Art. 63 criminal liability is architecturally different from documentation that satisfies carrier underwriting evidence requirements. Carrier attestation documentation may satisfy the carrier while leaving the managing director's criminal defense exposure undocumented. The market gap is structural — identical exposure profile to medical practitioner market, but no service exists for WM.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The carrier's form requires your personal signature on 'adequate AI governance.' What does 'adequate' mean? The carrier can't tell you. I can. That's what the 90-minute briefing is for."

**Distinct from C214–C298:** Named specific pre-signing review market absence for WM MDs as structural equivalent to medical practitioner market, not general carrier attestation pressure.

### Finding C299-P2-F5: Four-Track Regulatory Cluster Coherence Paradox as Discrete Service Category
**What it is:** Four independent documentation systems (FINMA Circular 08/2024, institutional client questionnaire responses, nFADP ROPA/DPIA/Art.26, carrier attestation evidence) describe the same AI governance reality with different formatting, evidence standards, update cycles, named responsible persons, and disclosure scopes. No Geneva methodology for cross-referencing these four documentation streams exists. Documentation may be internally inconsistent despite individually satisfying each track. Cross-track coherence management is a discrete service category unnamed in the market.

**Why it matters:** Firms may be individually adequate across each track while being systematically inconsistent across all of them. The documentation you built for the carrier may contradict what you attested to FINMA. The institutional questionnaire response may describe your AI governance in terms that conflict with your nFADP ROPA. You may be creating evidence that works against you in a challenge because the tracks were built independently.

**Ready-to-use language:** "FINMA, institutional clients, nFADP, and carrier attestation — four different accountability tracks, four different documentation systems, one AI governance reality. You built them independently. Now they're being reviewed together. The question is whether they're consistent."

**Distinct from C214–C298:** Named specific four-system coherence paradox and cross-reference architecture gap as discrete service category, not general parallel accountability.
