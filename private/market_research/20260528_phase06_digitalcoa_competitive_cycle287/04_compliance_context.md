## Primary Regulatory Contexts

### Context 01: nFADP and the Active Documentation Window

**The regulatory framework:** The Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP, revised) entered into force with a transition period. The consultation draft process that follows initial publication is the active documentation window.

**What it means for the ICP:**
- Art. 63 nFADP: Administrative and criminal liability provisions apply to inadequate AI governance, including personal liability for managing directors
- Art. 21: Data protection impact assessment obligations for high-risk AI processing are active
- Art. 9: Anonymization standards for AI training data must meet a specific threshold — which most vendor claims of "anonymization" do not meet
- The documentation window before the consultation draft arrives is the active burden-of-proof period

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- The nFADP framework is the primary regulatory substrate for all four verticals
- Art. 63 personal liability exposure is the specific accountability trigger that most ICPs have not yet engaged with
- The documentation window framing (burden-of-proof exercise, not compliance deadline) is the primary urgency message
- The Art.9 anonymization false adequacy is a specific trap that affects legal and healthcare ICPs most acutely

**Positioning opportunity:** The ICP who understands the documentation window as a burden-of-proof exercise has a fundamentally different engagement posture than the ICP who understands it as a compliance deadline. The briefing should ensure the ICP leaves with this distinction clear.

### Context 02: AI Convention (Budapest) Ratification — Active in Switzerland

**The regulatory framework:** Switzerland signed the Council of Europe Budapest Convention on AI in September 2024. The Swiss Parliament ratification process is active in 2026.

**What it means for the ICP:**
- The AI Convention will create obligations for AI systems in high-stakes domains — including legal services, financial services, and healthcare
- The professional who begins compliance architecture before ratification is ahead of the market
- The professional who waits for implementation guidance will be behind

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- This is a Geneva-specific regulatory timing signal that most Geneva professionals have not yet heard of
- Demonstrating awareness of the AI Convention ratification process signals regulatory timing intelligence that is differentiated from generic AI governance consultants
- The briefing can position the ICP's current documentation window as preparation for AI Convention obligations — connecting the immediate urgency to the longer regulatory horizon

**Positioning opportunity:** The AI Convention creates a longer regulatory narrative that contextualizes the immediate documentation window urgency. The professional who understands both is the professional who is most likely to engage now rather than defer.

### Context 03: Professional Liability Carrier Market Evolution

**The regulatory framework:** Professional liability insurance is governed by FINMA for financial institutions and by private carrier terms for legal, consulting, and healthcare professionals. The carrier market is iterating independently of regulatory requirements.

**What it means for the ICP:**
- The third-generation carrier attestation form now requires personal MD/partner/physician signature on undefined adequacy standards
- The carrier is not providing a definition of "adequate" — they are asking the professional to attest to adequacy they cannot demonstrate
- Each renewal cycle introduces new AI-related questions without guidance on what satisfies them
- The carrier's silence on adequacy creates a de facto standard through what carriers accept at renewal

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- The carrier renewal is the highest-conversion entry signal across all four verticals
- The pre-renewal review is the specific service format that addresses the accountability moment most precisely
- The carrier form iteration tracker produces the intelligence that feeds the pre-renewal outreach sequence
- The carrier's de facto standard through acceptance is the market's evidence of what "adequate" actually means in practice

**Positioning opportunity:** "What your carrier accepts at renewal is your de facto adequacy standard. Your carrier's next form may have new questions. Here's what we're tracking."

### Context 04: SRO and FINMA 08/2024 — The Supervisory Vacuum

**The regulatory framework:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 on AI use in financial services has been published. SRO self-regulatory bodies have not published equivalent guidance.

**What it means for the ICP (finance vertical):**
- FINMA 08/2024 provides a framework reference but no specific adequacy standard
- SROs are silent — creating a guidance vacuum that the ICP must navigate without supervisory direction
- The ICO (independent conduct organization) structure means different SROs may interpret adequacy differently
- Boutique wealth managers are particularly exposed: they have institutional client expectations, carrier attestation requirements, and no SRO guidance

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- The SRO vacuum is a specific structural condition that DigitalCoa.ch's Geneva participation can address
- The ICP who asks "what does my SRO expect" is asking a question no SRO is currently answering
- The briefing can address this vacuum directly: "Your SRO hasn't published guidance. Here's how professionals in your position are navigating the silence."

**Positioning opportunity:** The SRO vacuum creates a positioning moment: the advisor who acknowledges the vacuum and provides a practical navigation approach is more credible than the advisor who pretends the guidance exists.

### Context 05: FMH and Medical Professional Accountability — The Operationalization Gap

**The regulatory framework:** The FMH (Swiss Medical Association) has issued guidance that physicians remain personally responsible for AI-assisted decisions. The MedDO (Medical Device Ordinance) classifies certain AI tools as medical devices.

**What it means for the ICP (healthcare vertical):**
- FMH says "responsible" without operationalizing what responsible means in practice
- MedDO classification creates a specific compliance obligation for AI tools that qualify as medical devices — but the classification is not obvious and many physicians are using AI tools that may require it without knowing
- Patient consent forms have not been updated for AI-assisted clinical decisions
- Swissdamed registration deadline (July 2026) creates a specific timeline pressure for medical device AI tools

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- The healthcare vertical is the most clearly underserved in Geneva
- The independent physician ICP (2–10 physicians) with Swissdamed deadline pressure is a specific white space
- The FMH operationalization gap is a direct positioning opportunity: "The FMH says you're responsible. Here's what that means for your specific practice."

**Positioning opportunity:** The healthcare vertical is the highest-urgency entry point in the near term (Swissdamed July 2026) and the most underserved market in the long term.

### Context 06: Geneva Professional Codes — The Unwritten Standard

**The regulatory framework:** Geneva attorneys operate under the Swiss Bar Act (PARS), wealth managers under FINMA oversight and SRO codes, consulting professionals under various industry association codes, and physicians under FMH and cantonal regulations.

**What it means for the ICP:**
- Each professional code says "you remain responsible" for AI-assisted professional decisions
- No professional code has published a specific adequacy standard
- The unwritten standard is emerging through carrier acceptance, client questionnaire responses, and peer behavior — not through regulatory publication

**What it means for DigitalCoa.ch:**
- The unwritten standard is what the practice tracks through carrier form iteration, institutional questionnaire benchmarking, and peer network observation
- The synthesis intelligence is, in part, intelligence on what the unwritten standard is becoming
- This is why the cross-vertical participation is structurally necessary: the unwritten standard is forming differently in each vertical, and only sustained cross-vertical presence can track how the same mechanisms produce different standards

**Positioning opportunity:** "The standard isn't published anywhere. It's forming in your carrier's renewal questions, your client's questionnaire, and what your peers are doing. I track all four verticals simultaneously, which is how I can tell you where the standard is heading."
