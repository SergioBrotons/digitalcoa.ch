## What Resonates vs. What Doesn't

### What Resonates

**Naming the specific mechanism (not the category):**
✅ *"Your carrier's ISAE 3000 report proves your vendor's controls. It doesn't prove yours."*
❌ *"AI governance is important for your business."*

✅ *"The documentation window is a burden-of-proof exercise. After it closes, you can't recreate the evidence."*
❌ *"You need to document your AI use."*

**Matching the entry signal language:**
✅ *"The entry signal is identical across all four verticals: 'Par où on commence? Je n'ai même pas d'inventaire.'"*
❌ *"We offer comprehensive AI governance advisory services."*

**Demonstrating Geneva-specific participation:**
✅ *"I see the same problem across attorneys, wealth managers, and consultants — because I'm in all three communities."*
❌ *"We have expertise in AI governance best practices."*

**Naming the tension the ICP feels (C287-evolved):**
✅ *"You've probably already made some AI governance mistakes. The question is whether you know what they are — and whether you can show what you did about them."*
❌ *"You need a governance framework."*

**The incremental accumulation acknowledgment (C287-specific):**
✅ *"Most of the AI you're responsible for already arrived — through software updates, staff activation, vendor feature rollouts. You didn't make a governance decision about it. You're still responsible for it."*
❌ *"You should evaluate your AI tools."*

**The vendor contract acknowledgment (C287-specific):**
✅ *"You've already signed vendor terms that may create conflicts with your professional obligations. You didn't know to evaluate them. Nobody does. That's part of what we map."*
❌ *"Make sure your vendor contracts are compliant."*

### What Doesn't Resonate

- Generics: "AI governance framework," "compliance roadmap," "AI strategy"
- Enterprise framing: "enterprise AI governance," "board-level AI oversight"
- Training framing: "AI training course," "workshop on AI tools"
- Vendor framing: "AI tool evaluation," "vendor selection"
- Certification framing: "get certified for AI," "ISO 42001 preparation"
- Abstract urgency: "the regulatory landscape is changing" (C287: ICPs now know this; they need specificity on what changed and what to do)

## Cross-Vertical Patterns That Apply to DigitalCoa.ch

### The Ecosystem Self-Recognition Signal (C287-Evolved)

The most significant C287-evolution in ICP language: ICPs are now arriving with the ecosystem framing already partially formed. They have been talking to peers across verticals and noticed the same pattern. They have not yet found anyone who names it.

**Positioning implication:** The synthesis moat framing no longer needs to be introduced from scratch. The ICP has already observed the pattern in their own peer network. DigitalCoa.ch's task is to confirm and name what the ICP has already noticed — not to teach a new concept.

**Language that performs confirmation:**
- *"You've probably already noticed this with your peers — the same problem appearing across all four verticals. You haven't found anyone who sees the connection yet."*

### The Incremental Accumulation Reckoning (C287-Specific)

C287 Phase 01–04 findings confirm that the incremental accumulation problem has now reached the accountability reckoning stage: ICPs are beginning to realize they may already be responsible for AI they did not consciously adopt. This is a new entry language layer that was not present in prior cycles.

**Positioning implication:** The entry language now has a second layer beneath "where do I start" — "what am I already responsible for that I didn't know about." The briefing must address both.

**Language that opens both layers:**
- *"The first question isn't what AI you should adopt. It's what AI you're already responsible for — and whether you can show what you did about it. Most professionals haven't asked that question yet. The ones who did are the ones engaging right now."*
