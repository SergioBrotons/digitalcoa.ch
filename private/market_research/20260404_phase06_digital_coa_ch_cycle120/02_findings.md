# 02 — NET-NEW Findings
**Phase:** Phase 06 DigitalCoa.ch | **Cycle:** C120 | **Date:** 2026-04-04

## Research Question
What are the highest-value, differentiated advisory business opportunities for DigitalCoa.ch in C120, distinct from C119 and C118 angles?

---

## Finding 1: Executive Audience Persona Refinement — Beyond Job Title

### Signal
Generic ICP definitions ("GCs at mid-size companies") lack the specificity needed for effective positioning and conversion. Refined personas with context, pain points, and decision-making patterns are needed.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Develop 2-3 specific executive personas with context, pain points, decision patterns
- **Differentiation:** Not client journey mapping (C119) — this is persona design at a deeper level
- **Revenue model:** Better targeting, more effective content, higher conversion
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Geneva executives in finance, legal, compliance functions

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Persona 1: GC navigating regulatory change (DORA, CSRD, AI liability) — needs trusted external counsel
- Persona 2: CFO managing FX/risk amid SNB normalization — needs specialized advisory
- Persona 3: Family office/executive managing cross-border complexity — needs integrated advisory
- Target: refine messaging and content to match specific personas

---

## Finding 2: Service Description Clarity — Eliminating Ambiguity

### Signal
Executive buyers need to quickly understand what DigitalCoa.ch does and whether it fits their needs. Ambiguous service descriptions create friction and lose prospects.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Rewrite service description with clear scope, deliverable examples, and ideal engagement types
- **Differentiation:** Not pricing architecture (C119) — this is value proposition and scope clarity
- **Revenue model:** Faster client decision, reduced sales friction, better-qualified inquiries
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Executive buyers are time-constrained; clarity compounds

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Each service should have: what it is, what it delivers, who it's for, typical duration
- Avoid: vague advisory language, undefined scope, open-ended descriptions
- Target: executive should understand offering in 30 seconds

---

## Finding 3: Client Onboarding Experience — Friction Reduction

### Signal
The transition from "interested prospect" to "engaged client" has friction points that lose qualified clients. Optimizing the onboarding experience reduces this friction.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Map onboarding journey; identify friction points; redesign for executive convenience
- **Differentiation:** Not onboarding process (C118) — this is experience design, not checklist
- **Revenue model:** Higher conversion from inquiry to engagement, faster time-to-value
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Executives expect seamless, high-touch onboarding

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Friction reducers: pre-call briefing materials, clear agenda, concise discovery process
- Time investment: 30-minute discovery call maximum; clear next steps within 24 hours
- Target: minimize executive time investment while maximizing value perception

---

## Finding 4: Thought Leadership as Client Acquisition Engine

### Signal
Thought leadership content (Brotons.net) should directly support DigitalCoa.ch client acquisition, not just build general brand awareness. Each piece should have a clear pathway to client engagement.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Connect Brotons.net content to DigitalCoa.ch conversion pathways; make implicit pathways explicit
- **Differentiation:** Not executive credibility positioning (C119) — this is acquisition-focused, not credibility-focused
- **Revenue model:** Content-driven lead generation, warm inquiry conversion, referral activation
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Geneva executives respond to relevant, specific thought leadership

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Each major Brotons.net piece should have clear DigitalCoa.ch CTA for relevant readers
- Engagement pathways: briefing call request, white paper download, referral introduction
- Target: measurable conversion from Brotons.net reading to DigitalCoa.ch inquiry

---

## Finding 5: Competitive Differentiation — Geneva Advisory Positioning

### Signal
DigitalCoa.ch competes with Geneva law firms, Big 4 advisory, and independent consultants. Clear differentiation is needed to stand out in a crowded market.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Define specific positioning vs. competitors; articulate unique combination of expertise and approach
- **Differentiation:** Not covered in prior DigitalCoa.ch cycles — specific competitive framing
- **Revenue model:** Win the right clients, avoid price competition, command positioning premium
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Geneva's competitive density makes differentiation critical

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Differentiation dimensions: international experience + Geneva depth, legal + finance + strategy, independent + credible
- Avoid: "full-service advisory," "trusted partner," generic positioning
- Target: executives can articulate why DigitalCoa.ch vs. alternatives within 60 seconds

---

## Finding 6: Engagement Model Clarity — Retainer/Project/Advisory Board

### Signal
Executives prefer clarity on engagement structure before committing. Making different engagement models explicit helps qualify prospects and set expectations.

### Strategic Opportunity
- **Action:** Define and publish engagement model options; make the right fit obvious
- **Differentiation:** Not pricing architecture (C119) — this is model structure, not pricing levels
- **Revenue model:** Faster sales cycle, better-qualified engagements, higher client satisfaction
- **DigitalCoa.ch specificity:** Executives value flexibility and clarity on terms

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Engagement types: advisory retainer, discrete project, advisory board seat, hybrid
- Each type: scope, commitment, pricing basis, ideal fit
- Target: client knows what they're getting before they engage

---

## NET-NEW Assessment
**Total: 6 findings — all distinct from C119 (client journey mapping, pricing architecture, remote delivery quality, referral program, content-to-client conversion, executive credibility positioning) and C118 (lead qualification, briefing call conversion, case study strategy, proposal structure, onboarding process, expansion)**
