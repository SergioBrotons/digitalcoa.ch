# Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting | Cycle 75 | Findings

## NET-NEW Findings (C75 Phase 03)

### Finding 1: Swiss Bureaucracy Cost — CHF 30 Billion/Year in Avoidable Costs (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** economiesuisse.ch — article, March 25, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.economiesuisse.ch/en/articles/reducing-bureaucracy-federal-government-and-cantons-are-called-upon
- **Finding:** BSS Volkswirtschaftliche Beratung + ifo Institute analysis: CHF 30 billion/year in bureaucratic costs could be avoided through efficient and digital government processes. Geneva alone: CHF 2.8 billion/year in lost economic output from bureaucracy. Confederation and cantons called upon for concrete reduction measures. Federal Council and administration more proactive in issuing new regulations. Administrative burden on companies continuously increasing.
- **Legal relevance:** Regulatory burden consulting, process optimization services, digital government consulting, cost reduction advisory. Geneva as business location competitiveness.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW

### Finding 2: Economiesuisse "Leaders in Exchange" — Business-Education Leadership Program (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** economiesuisse.ch — article, March 30, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.economiesuisse.ch/en/articles/leading-stronger-together-leaders-exchange-training-program
- **Finding:** "Leaders in Exchange" program — 8-month program pairing experienced business leaders with school principals in tandem pairs. Participants discuss specific leadership issues, reflect on their roles, observe each other's organisations. A joint offering of Association of School Principals Zurich VSLZH, Young Enterprise Switzerland YES, and economiesuisse. Prof. Dr. Rudolf Minsch (Chief Economist) and Nadine Wüthrich listed as contacts. Signals business-education leadership development as priorities for Swiss competitiveness.
- **Legal relevance:** Executive education, leadership development consulting, Swiss human capital strategy.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW

### Finding 3: Bilaterals III — Business Support for EU Package with Lean Implementation Demand (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** economiesuisse.ch — press release, March 13, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.economiesuisse.ch/en/press-release/bilaterals-iii-strengthening-competitiveness-uncertain-times
- **Finding:** Economiesuisse explicitly supports Bilaterals III package ("isolation is not an option for the economy"). Key elements: Horizon Europe association, electricity agreement, stable market access. Demands "lean and business-friendly implementation" in Swiss legislation, avoiding additional bureaucratic burdens. Federal Council adopted dispatch March 20, 2026. Parliamentary process now active. Bilaterals III seen as critical for Swiss export economy and innovation capacity.
- **Legal relevance:** EU regulatory alignment consulting, export compliance, Horizon Europe research funding advisory, electricity sector consulting.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW

### Finding 4: Swiss Winter Electricity Crisis — Zero Progress Despite Renewables (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** economiesuisse.ch — Lukas Federer article, March 29, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.economiesuisse.ch/en/articles/winter-electricity-ten-years-stepping-place-are-enough
- **Finding:** Despite 40% increase in installed production capacity over 15 years, Swiss winter electricity production has not increased in the last 10 years. Gap between installed capacity and actual production widening. Demand expected to increase ~33% by 2050. Critical phase December-February shows virtually no improvement from renewable expansion. Lukas Federer (Head of Energy, economiesuisse) calls for serious supply security approach.
- **Legal relevance:** Energy infrastructure consulting, energy policy advisory, electricity security compliance. Geneva-based energy consulting and trading firms.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW
