## F2: Swiss Health Digitization — Interoperability Crisis Driving IT Consulting Demand
**Source:** Le Temps editorial, April 2, 2026  
**Signal:** MEDIUM-HIGH — health IT consulting

Federal Council adopting centralized approach to electronic health records — not making everyone happy (cantonal resistance). Key structural problem: proprietary vendor lock-in. Care providers (clinics, hospitals, cabinets médicaux) all use different software systems that don't communicate. Third-party vendors have no incentive to enable data exchange.

Bright spot: HUG (Geneva University Hospitals) + Hôpital du Valais co-developing their own clinical software — specifically to escape vendor lock-in. Vaud hospital system considering Epic Systems (US). Le Temps editorial calls for national strategic vision to break the deadlock.

Consulting angles: health IT strategy, vendor selection, interoperability architecture, change management, data governance frameworks. Significant opportunity for Geneva-based health IT consultancies.
