## Purpose
Document how ICPs (Ideal Client Profiles) describe their AI accountability problems in their own words — specific to the Geneva professional services market. This is the language DigitalCoa uses to reach them. Not the language of frameworks or compliance — the language of evidence, liability, and survival.

---

## ICP Language Map — Geneva Professional Services

### Vertical 1: Geneva Legal (Managing Partners, Senior Associates)

**The problem they describe:**
"I have documentation debt I didn't know I was accumulating. Every week my associates use AI tools and nothing gets recorded. My carrier attestation form is asking for records I don't have. I signed the attestation — my name is on it. That's personal."

**How they describe the urgency:**
"I know I have a problem. I've known for probably 18 months. I just didn't know what to call it or what to do about it. Now my carrier is asking specific questions and I have no records."

**What they think they need:**
"A policy document. A framework. Something I can show my carrier that says I have a system."

**What they actually need:**
"Evidence. I need to be able to show records — actual records — of every AI authorization decision my firm has made. I don't have those records. I need someone to build the system that produces them."

**The gap in their language:**
They say "policy." They need "evidence." They say "framework." They need "authorization records." They say "AI governance." They need "proof I made the right decision."

**Language to use:**
- "Authorization records"
- "Decision evidence"
- "Documentation that protects your name"
- "Carrier-ready records"
- "The documentation your attestation form actually requires"

### Vertical 2: Geneva Finance (Wealth Management, Asset Management, Family Offices)

**The problem they describe:**
"I have AI tools running 24/7. Authorizations happen constantly. I have no record of any of them. My carrier wants to see my AI governance evidence. I can't produce it — I don't have it. I have no idea what my systems are doing."

**How they describe the urgency:**
"My institutional clients are sending me AI governance questionnaires. They want to know how I use AI, what decisions are made by AI, what oversight I have. I can answer 8 questions confidently. The other 32 — I either guessed or left blank. Neither is acceptable."

**What they think they need:**
"AI strategy. A document that says we have an AI governance framework."

**What they actually need:**
"Continuous authorization architecture. A system that records every AI decision, who authorized it, what the parameters were, and what the output was. Evidence of supervision, not just policy statements."

**The gap in their language:**
They say "framework." They mean "continuous evidence system." They say "oversight." They mean "24/7 supervision records." They say "AI governance." They mean "proof my carrier and my clients can inspect."

**Language to use:**
- "Continuous authorization records"
- "AI decision attribution"
- "Supervision evidence"
- "Carrier-ready documentation"
- "Your institutional clients' questionnaire — answered confidently"

### Vertical 3: Geneva Consulting (Management Consulting, Strategy, Professional Services)

**The problem they describe:**
"My clients are asking about my AI methodology. I don't have one documented. I use AI tools in my client work. I have no evidence of how I used them, what prompts I gave, what outputs I relied on. If a client asks, I have nothing."

**How they describe the urgency:**
"If a client challenges a recommendation I made with AI assistance — I can't show my work. I can't prove what I did. That's a professional liability problem. I also have professional secrecy obligations. My AI vendor contracts — I signed them without review. I don't know what data flows to them."

**What they think they need:**
"Methodology documentation. Something that says I have a process."

**What they actually need:**
"Evidence of methodology. Not a document that describes your process — actual records of your process in action. Prompt logs, output records, client-specific decision documentation. The evidence that proves your methodology exists."

**The gap in their language:**
They say "methodology." They need "evidence of methodology in use." They say "process." They need "process records." They say "professional liability." They mean "inability to defend their work when challenged."

**Language to use:**
- "Methodology evidence"
- "Professional liability protection"
- "Client-specific records"
- "Vendor data flow transparency"
- "Your name on the attestation — protected"

### Vertical 4: Geneva Healthcare (Independent Medical Practices, Physicians, Specialists)

They say "regulation." They mean "personal liability." They say "compliance." They mean "license protection." They say "AI governance." They mean "evidence that keeps them practicing."

**Language to use:**
- "For physicians, evidence is personal"
- "License protection, not framework compliance"
- "FMH supervision records"
- "Carrier form evidence"
- "Your name on the form — defended"

## Cross-Vertical ICP Language Patterns

### Pattern 1: "I know I have a problem but I don't know what to call it"
**What they say:**
"I know I have documentation debt. I've known for months. I just don't know what to do about it or who to ask."
"I know I'm supposed to have something but I don't know what the carrier actually wants to see."
"I know I signed something but I don't know what I agreed to."

**What this reveals:**
They have the problem. They know it. They don't have the language to ask for help, and they don't know what good looks like. The market is offering frameworks. They need evidence.

**Language to use:** "Documentation debt" — name it. "Evidence system" — describe it. "Carrier-ready records" — show it.

---

### Pattern 2: "My name is on the form — that's personal"
**What they say:**
"I'm the managing director. The attestation form has my name on it."
"I'm the FMH-registered physician. I'm personally responsible."
"I'm the partner. If there's a breach, it's my liability."
"I can't treat this as abstract compliance. It's my name. It's my license. It's my career."

**What this reveals:**
Personal liability is the emotional trigger. Business compliance language doesn't convert. "Your name. Your liability." speaks directly to the person who holds the attestation obligation.

**Language to use:** "Your name. Your liability. Your evidence system." — explicitly. "The documentation that protects your name." — always.

---

### Pattern 3: "I use AI constantly but I have no records"
**What they say:**
"My team uses AI every day. I have no idea what they're doing."
"I have 24/7 AI systems running. No records."
"Every client matter involves AI tools. I have no documentation."
"I signed vendor contracts. I don't know what data they have."

**What this reveals:**
The adoption happened. The evidence production did not. This is not a future problem — it's a present accumulated debt. Every day without a system adds to the debt.

**Language to use:** "Stop the compounding." "Debt freeze." "Forward-looking evidence capture." — concrete action, not retrospective.

---

### Pattern 4: "I need something I can actually use"
**What they say:**
"I don't have a compliance department."
"I can't hire a full-time person to run software."
"I need something that works within my practice."
"I need someone who understands Geneva, understands my vertical, and can build this with me."

**What this reveals:**
Enterprise solutions don't work. Software platforms don't work. The ICP needs human advisory that produces operational evidence. The solution must fit within the firm's actual capacity.

**Language to use:** "Built for Geneva professional firms." "Human-delivered evidence systems." "Works within your practice — not a software project."

---

## Language to Never Use

- "Framework" (ICP doesn't say this; competitor language)
- "Compliance" (abstract, not personal)
- "AI governance" alone (too generic, no urgency)
- "Risk management" (business frame, not personal)
- "Policy document" (what they think they need; not what they actually need)

## Language to Always Use

- "Evidence" — the word ICPs use
- "Authorization records" — what carrier forms test for
- "Decision attribution" — what supervision requires
- "Your name, your liability" — the personal frame
- "Carrier-ready documentation" — the specific standard they need to meet
- "Stop the compounding" — the urgency action
- "For physicians, evidence is personal" — healthcare-specific anchor
