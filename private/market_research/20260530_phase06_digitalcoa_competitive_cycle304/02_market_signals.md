## Market Signal Map — C304 Phase 06

### Signal 1: Carrier Attestation Forms Are Upgrading in Real-Time — The Window Is Closing

**The signal:** Geneva carriers are actively revising AI attestation forms. Form v4 is circulating in legal. Carrier forms in finance and healthcare are following. Each version requires more specific evidence documentation.

**What ICPs report:**
"Last year the carrier form asked one question about AI. This year it asks twelve. Each one requires specific records I don't have."
"My carrier sent form v4. It's asking for AI authorization logs. I've never produced an AI authorization log in my life."
"The form requires evidence of supervision. I have no supervision records. I signed the attestation anyway."

**Why it matters:** The market is not in a stable compliance state — it's in active escalation. Each carrier form update creates a new documentation requirement that ICPs cannot meet. The evidence gap is widening, not narrowing.

**Timing pressure:** Carrier form v4 is the immediate forcing function for legal. Finance and healthcare forms are following within months. The window to position as the solution before ICPs feel acute pressure is now.

**The opportunity:** ICPs who receive form v4 and can't produce evidence become high-intent leads. The question is whether they find DigitalCoa before they try to patch the problem with a framework vendor.

### Signal 2: Institutional Client Questionnaires Are Becoming Client Retention Tools

**The signal:** Institutional clients (family offices, asset managers, corporate legal departments) are sending AI governance questionnaires as part of their own compliance reviews. ICPs who cannot answer confidently face client relationship risk — not just regulatory risk.

**What ICPs report:**
"I got a 40-question AI governance questionnaire from a family office client. They want to know how I use AI, what oversight I have, what data flows to third parties. I can answer 8 questions. The rest — I either guessed or left blank."
"If I answer their questionnaire and they see I have nothing — that's worse than not answering. They'll know I have no system."
"This isn't just regulatory. If I can't answer their questions, they move their business."

**Why it matters:** The institutional client questionnaire has become a competitive evaluation mechanism. ICPs who fail lose clients. The urgency is business-critical, not just compliance-related.

**The opportunity:** Evidence system solves both regulatory and commercial survival. "Your client is asking the same questions your carrier is asking. Be ready for both."

### Signal 3: Vendor Contract Trap Creates a Locked Client Population

**The signal:** A significant portion of Geneva professional firms are locked into AI vendor relationships they cannot unwind. Historical data exposure, contract terms, and data portability limitations create a situation where switching vendors doesn't resolve the problem — it just changes it.

**What ICPs report:**
"I want to leave my AI vendor. I went to review what data they have. They said I need to submit a formal request and it will take 6–8 weeks and require legal review."
"I signed the contract three years ago. I had no idea what I was agreeing to. Now I can't prove what they've done with my data."
"I can't unwind the historical exposure. I can only build going forward. But no one offers that."

**Why it matters:** Trapped firms are a distinct market segment. They cannot improve their position through vendor switching. Only a forward-looking evidence system makes governance manageable within their constraint. No competitor has named this service.

**The opportunity:** "Vendor contract trap resolution" is an unclaimed service category. Firms who understand they're trapped will recognize themselves immediately — and seek the named solution.

**Why it matters:** The Geneva SMB segment is structurally excluded from every enterprise solution. The market has no offer for the 10-person law firm, the 8-person wealth management practice, the 6-person consulting boutique, the independent physician with 2 staff.

**The opportunity:** "Built for Geneva professional firms — not enterprises." The human-delivered evidence system that works within the firm's actual capacity is the only solution designed for this segment.

### Signal 5: nFADP Art. 63 Personal Liability Is Named But Not Operationalized

**The signal:** ICPs know nFADP Art. 63 creates personal liability. They know their name is on the attestation. They know it's personal. But they have no operational pathway to produce the evidence that protects them.

**What ICPs report:**
"I know Art. 63 means personal liability. I know my name is on the form. I just don't know what to do about it."
"I've read about personal liability. I understand the risk. But I don't know what evidence I need to produce or how to produce it."
"I need evidence that protects me personally — not just evidence that satisfies the regulation."

**Why it matters:** The market has awareness of personal liability risk without operational solutions. ICPs who understand the risk are looking for a way to operationalize protection — and finding no pathway.

**The opportunity:** "Your name. Your liability. Your evidence system." directly addresses the gap between awareness and action. The evidence system that produces personal protection is the solution.

### Signal 7: Documentation Debt Compounding Is Creating Psychological Burden

**The signal:** ICPs who have accumulated documentation debt describe a psychological burden — the problem is always present, always growing, and they're not sure how to address it without making it worse.

**What ICPs report:**

### Signal 8: "Swissdamed July 1" Is Creating Healthcare Compliance Panic

**The signal:** Swissdamed medical device classification deadline is creating specific panic in the Geneva independent practice community. Physicians don't know if they're subject, don't know their classification, and don't have documentation to make the determination.

**What ICPs report:**

"I need a classification determination before July 1. I have no documentation system to make it."

**Why it matters:** Swissdamed creates an acute June window in healthcare — the classification determination must happen before July 1. This is the highest-urgency vertical-specific trigger in the current market.

**The opportunity:** Swissdamed classification determination as a discrete entry service — "Do you know your Swissdamed classification? Let's determine it and document it before July 1." The entry service that opens the ongoing evidence system relationship.
