# ICP Language: Geneva Small Law Firm — Phase 01 C298

## Who You're Reading

Small Geneva law firm, 2–5 attorneys. Managing partner or senior associate. The person whose name is on the engagement letter and who signs the carrier attestation. They are not thinking about AI strategy. They are thinking about the Monday morning when a client matter requires a decision they made with AI assistance, and they cannot fully reconstruct what the AI told them versus what they decided independently.

## Language They Use

**On carrier attestation:**
- "I signed the form. I didn't understand what I was attesting to."
- "The carrier wants to know if we have 'adequate AI governance.' Nobody told me what that means."
- "My PI carrier is asking about AI. I've never had to answer a question like this before."

**On attorney-client privilege and AI:**
- "We're using an AI tool for research. Is that a privileged channel?"
- "If the client's matter is in the system, and the AI vendor can see it—that's a problem, isn't it?"
- "We switched to a Swiss-hosted tool. That should cover it."

**On the bar guidance vacuum:**
- "The SAV guide was a start. It didn't tell me what to actually do on Monday morning."
- "I called the Ordre. They said they are working on something. That's not guidance."
- "There is no Geneva-specific guidance. Everything is either too general or too enterprise."

**On personal exposure:**
- "I am the one who signs everything. Whatever AI governance means, I'm the one who's accountable."
- "If something goes wrong with an AI-assisted matter, it's my name on the record."
- "My associates are using AI tools I didn't approve and I don't fully understand."

**On the documentation problem:**
- "We have no idea what AI we used on which matters over the past two years."
- "The attestation asks me to certify continuity. We can't reconstruct that from before."
- "The documentation window is already closed. We didn't know there was a window."

**On vendor and tool questions:**
- "Our contract with the AI vendor has terms we never read. They probably have our client data."
- "The tool updated itself. Is the output we relied on still valid?"
- "We stopped using one AI tool. Does that mean anything for open client matters?"

**On what should happen:**
- "I need to know what 'adequate' means before my next renewal."
- "Something should exist for a firm our size. The frameworks are all for larger organizations."
- "I'd pay for someone to walk me through this before I sign the next carrier form."

## Distinct Vocabulary Markers

- **"Attestation"** not "questionnaire" — they have received the carrier form and they're treating it as an attestation event
- **"Adequate"** as the undefined standard — carrier uses it, nobody defines it locally
- **"Swiss-hosted"** as assumed protection — a label firms rely on without examining the actual data flow
- **"The window"** as retrospective moment — firms beginning to recognize documentation decisions have a time dimension
- **"My name on the record"** as the personal exposure frame — identity-level accountability, not firm-level
- **"Ordre"** as the locally trusted but absent authority — firms look to Geneva bar specifically, not just national SAV

## What They Are Not Saying (Yet)

They are not yet using:
- "processor chain" (they don't know nFADP Art.9 applies to their vendor stack)
- "authorization chain" (they haven't thought about AI-to-decision documentation)
- "extraterritorial AI literacy" (they don't know EU AI Act Art.4 may apply to them)

They are functioning on instinct, vendor marketing, and the fear that something should be in place but they don't know what.
