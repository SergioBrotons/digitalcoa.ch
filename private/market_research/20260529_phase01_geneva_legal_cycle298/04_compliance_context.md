# Compliance Context: Geneva / Switzerland — Phase 01 C298

## Primary Regulatory Framework

### Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) — Applicable Now
The nFADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data. No implementing legislation required. FDPIC has confirmed enforcement posture shifted from guidance-first to enforcement-live as of May 2025. Key articles for small law firms:

- **Art.5** — Lawful basis for processing; AI-assisted client matter processing requires documented lawful basis
- **Art.6** — Processing of sensitive personal data; client matters frequently involve sensitive categories
- **Art.9** — Third-party processor obligations; AI vendor contracts create Art.9 processor relationships — most small firms have unevaluated click-through acceptance activating these obligations
- **Art.20** — Data subject access rights; AI-assisted decisions affecting client matters may trigger Art.20 response obligations
- **Art.21** —Right to explanation for automated decisions; applies to AI-assisted decisions in client matters
- **Art.22** — Reversal right; applies where AI substantially informs client matter decisions
- **Art.63** — Criminal liability; personal criminal liability for managing partner or attorney using AI in client matters where conditions for criminal liability are met. Different evidentiary standard, different enforcement logic, from administrative fine

### Professional Secrecy — Art.321 CP
Attorney-client privilege is protected under Swiss Criminal Code Art.321. The protection applies to confidential communication. The question of whether AI research channels constitute confidential communication channels is not resolved by server location alone. "Swiss-hosted" labels address data residency, not the legal qualification of a communication channel. Public AI platforms may not constitute privileged communication channels regardless of where data is stored. Small firms using consumer AI tools for client matter research without assessing this question are operating with unresolved professional secrecy exposure.

### Code of Civil Procedure — Art.167a
January 2025 CPC amendment creates accountability split between external counsel (Art.321 CP refusal/withdrawal rights) and in-house counsel (Art.167a CPC). AI tool governance does not currently map to this split. Small firms with hybrid functions — where attorneys may function in both external and in-house counsel roles — face inconsistent accountability standards simultaneously. No Geneva guidance document addresses how AI governance differs between these two professional roles.

### SAV/SBA National AI Guidance — June 2024
Swiss Bar Association published AI guidance in June 2024. The guidance tells small firms what to do (apply existing rules) without operative implementation path for 2-5 attorney scale. Firms find it too general for practical decisions. The national guidance does not address Geneva-specific cantonal bar dynamics or Ordre des Avocats de Genève positioning.

### Ordre des Avocats de Genève — Guidance Vacuum
The Geneva cantonal bar has published no Geneva-specific AI guidance. Firms explicitly express frustration that a local source they trust has not published guidance calibrated to their scale. The national SAV framework is too general for firm-level decisions. The absence of Ordre-level guidance is experienced as a discrete pain point, not just an extension of the national vacuum.

### EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Application
EU AI Act Article 4 AI literacy obligation live since February 2, 2025. Applies to providers and deployers of AI systems operating within EU market. Swiss law firms with EU client relationships may be in scope as deployers. Switzerland's Federal Council announced sector-specific approach in February 2025 — ratifying the Council of Europe Convention on AI, with sector-specific legislative changes in key areas. Legislative consultation draft expected end of 2026. Geneva firms with EU nexus operating under dual-compliance obligation: EU AI Act Art.4 with no Swiss domestic equivalent. Extraterritorial AI literacy obligation is live; no Genevacalibrated methodology for professional services firms has addressed it.

### Council of Europe AI Convention — Ratification Path
Switzerland intends to ratify the CoE Convention on AI. Creates deployer obligations for firms with EU nexus before implementing legislation arrives. Private law implications flowing through existing client mandate contracts and carrier underwriting terms. Active engagement for Geneva firms already; not a future risk — present-tense operative exposure already triggered by existing client contracts and carrier forms.

## Geneva-Specific Regulatory Dynamics

### Cantonal Institution Intelligence Architecture
Effective standards forming through Geneva institutional ecosystem — HUG/CHUV governance rooms, carrier underwriting intelligence, bar circuit exchanges. Independent small law firms structurally excluded from this formation process while simultaneously being measured against standards formed without their participation. Formation mechanism creates measurement against arrived standards the firms did not contribute to forming. This structural condition means firms outside the informal circuit cannot access the intelligence through published channels.

### Formation Velocity
Multiple simultaneous formation channels (carrier iteration, client contract clauses, EU developments, FDPIC posture, institutional circuit exchanges) creating standards faster than published guidance reflects. Firms outside informal circuit building documentation against arrived standards while operative standards may be different from those arrived. Current cycle formation velocity wider and more consequential than prior cycles.

### Documentation Window Time Dimension
Window is present-tense positioning event — decisions documented before close evaluated against existing standards. Decisions not documented before close, when later challenged, require retroactive reconstruction of never-documented reasoning. New documentation cannot reproduce window-period analytical context. Window close has irreversible positioning quality — firms in active documentation window have structurally different options than firms already outside it. The window is not a compliance deadline; it is a burden-of-proof positioning event.

## What's Missing in Regulatory Guidance for Small Firms

The regulatory framework provides:
- nFADP — applies directly, creates obligations, enforcement posture confirmed as live
- Art.321 CP — professional secrecy protection, unresolved AI channel qualification question
- SAV guidance — general principles, too general for operative decisions
- CoE Convention path — future obligations already flowing through current contracts
- EU AI Act Art.4 — live extraterritorial literacy obligation for firms with EU nexus

The regulatory framework does NOT provide:
- Operative definition of "adequate AI governance" at 2-5 attorney scale
- Geneva-calibrated methodology for AI documentation proportionate to small firm operations
- Cantonal bar implementation guidance with Monday-morning decision support
- Pre-signing carrier attestation review service
- Shadow AI enumeration protocol for boutique scale
- Documentation architecture for the specific decisions small firms face
- Processing register (nFADP Art.22 register) specification appropriate for law firm operations
