# 02 — NET-NEW Findings
**Phase:** Phase 06 DigitalCoa.ch | **Cycle:** C121 | **Date:** 2026-04-04

## Research Question
What are the highest-value, differentiated DigitalCoa.ch advisory business optimization opportunities for C121, distinct from C120 (executive persona, service description clarity, onboarding friction reduction, thought leadership acquisition, competitive differentiation, engagement model clarity)?

---

## Finding 1: Client Journey Refinement — Post-Briefing-Call Experience

### Signal
The briefing call is the critical conversion moment for DigitalCoa.ch. Clients who convert do so at or immediately after the briefing call. However, the post-briefing-call experience (follow-up, proposal delivery, expectation setting) has not been systematized. Gap between good briefing call and decisive client experience exists.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Systematize the 48-hour post-briefing-call sequence: personalized summary, proposal or terms of engagement, clear next-step timeline, and expectation calibration document
- **Distinct angle:** Not onboarding friction reduction (C120) — C120 focused on pre-engagement friction; C121 focuses on the post-briefing-call conversion moment
- **Revenue model:** Higher briefing-call-to-engagement conversion rate, reduced sales cycle, improved client expectation calibration
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva executives expect seamless, concise, professional follow-up; any friction at this stage signals broader advisory quality concerns

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Immediate action: Design 48-hour post-briefing-call sequence
- Key element: Personalized engagement summary specific to what was discussed in the briefing call
- CTA: "What's next" clarity — explicit timeline and next step

---

## Finding 2: Proposal Quality and Structure — Geneva Executive Expectations

### Signal
Geneva executives receiving advisory proposals expect concise, decision-relevant documents — not lengthy scoping documents. The most effective professional services proposals in the Geneva market are: 2-3 pages maximum, problem statement, proposed approach, team, and fees/range, in that order.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Review and restructure proposal format to match Geneva executive expectations: shorter, more direct, decision-focused rather than scope-detailed
- **Distinct angle:** Not service description clarity (C120) — C120 was about website/inbound description; C121 is specifically the outbound proposal document
- **Revenue model:** Faster client decision cycles, higher proposal acceptance rate, stronger professional impression
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's executive culture values precision and efficiency; overly detailed or defensive proposals signal lack of confidence

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Format recommendation: 2-3 page executive proposal, with detailed scope as appendix
- Content order: Problem → Proposed approach → Team → Fees/range
- Tone: Confident, concise, no hedging language

## Finding 3: Briefing Call Conversion Optimization — Pre-Call Qualification and Setup

### Signal
Not all briefing calls convert. Analysis of professional services briefing call conversion shows that pre-call qualification and briefing call structure are the two most controllable variables. Calls with clear pre-call agendas and structured opening minutes convert at significantly higher rates.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Implement a brief pre-call confirmation email that sets agenda, confirms fit, and prepares the executive for a productive 30-minute call; structure the call itself with a clear 3-segment format: discovery, fit assessment, and mutual next steps
- **Distinct angle:** Not onboarding friction reduction (C120) — this is specifically the briefing call conversion mechanics, not the broader onboarding
- **Revenue model:** Higher briefing call conversion rate, reduced time on unqualified calls, better client quality fit
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva executives are time-constrained; a well-structured call signals respect for their time

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Pre-call: 24-hour confirmation with agenda and fit criteria
- Call structure: Discovery (10 min) → Fit assessment (10 min) → Mutual next steps (10 min)
- Post-call: Immediate 48-hour follow-up (per Finding 1)

## Finding 4: Remote Delivery Quality Assurance — Geneva Executive Standards

### Signal
DigitalCoa.ch delivers advisory services remotely (video, secure document sharing). Geneva executives have high standards for remote interaction quality: stable video, punctuality, pre-shared agendas, concise post-meeting summaries. Delivery quality variance directly affects client perception and retention.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Develop a remote delivery quality standard (pre-call tech check, backup video link, screen-share discipline, summary SLA) and communicate it to clients proactively as a quality signal
- **Distinct angle:** Not engagement model clarity (C120) — C120 was about the overall engagement model; C121 is specifically the remote delivery execution quality
- **Revenue model:** Client retention improvement, client satisfaction scores, referral quality
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's financial sector has normalized high-quality remote advisory delivery; clients compare DigitalCoa.ch to their best-in-class service experiences

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Quality standard checklist: pre-call tech check, backup link ready, summary within 24 hours, punctuality buffer
- Proactive communication: Share delivery quality commitment in onboarding materials
- Competitive signal: Position remote delivery quality as a differentiator vs. larger firms with inconsistent delivery

## Finding 5: Referral Program Design — Geneva Advisory Network Dynamics

### Signal
The Geneva advisory market runs on trusted referrals. Executives refer peers to advisors they trust personally, not to firms with the most impressive website. The referral mechanism in Geneva is: a brief personal introduction (by email or phone), not a digital referral link or formal program.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Design a "trusted introduction" referral program that makes it easy for satisfied clients to introduce peers via brief personal email/phone, not a formal referral form; provide clients with a simple "introduction framework" that makes their referral feel natural
- **Distinct angle:** Not engagement model clarity (C120) — C120 was about the overall model; C121 is specifically the referral activation mechanism
- **Revenue model:** Higher referral volume from existing clients, improved client quality fit (referred clients convert at higher rates), lower client acquisition cost
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's relationship-driven culture means a personal referral carries significantly more weight than any digital marketing

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Referral mechanism: Simple 2-sentence introduction template clients can use
- When to ask: After a successful engagement delivery, not during sales process
- Gratitude: Immediate personal acknowledgment of referral (not generic thank-you)
- Follow-through: Keep referring client informed of outcome

## Finding 6: Content-to-Client Conversion — Systematic Lead Nurture for High-Intent Readers

### Signal
Broader site content (not just dedicated insight articles) attracts readers who are higher-intent than newsletter subscribers but lower-intent than briefing call requesters. This middle tier needs systematic lead nurture: a content-driven email sequence that builds toward a briefing call invitation.

### Market Opportunity
- **DigitalCoa.ch application:** Develop a 3-email nurture sequence triggered by specific content downloads or high-engagement site behavior (e.g., multiple page views on service pages, time-on-site above threshold). Sequence: Value reinforcement → Specific challenge framing → Briefing call invitation
- **Distinct angle:** Not thought leadership acquisition (C120) — C120 was about acquiring thought leadership credibility; C121 is specifically converting existing content engagement into client relationships
- **Revenue model:** Passive pipeline generation from evergreen content, reduced reliance on cold outreach, improved content ROI measurement
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva executives are cautious about direct sales outreach; a content-nurture approach that respects their information consumption is more effective

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch
- Trigger: 3+ service page visits OR specific content download (e.g., Geneva regulatory report)
- Email 1 (day 3): Value reinforcement — "Based on your interest in [topic], here is our perspective"
- Email 2 (day 10): Challenge framing — "Common questions executives ask about [topic]"
- Email 3 (day 21): Briefing call invitation — soft ask, not pressure
- Unsubscribe: Easy opt-out at each email
