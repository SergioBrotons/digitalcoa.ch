## Three Immediate Regulatory Obligations

### 1. FINMA Circular 08/2024 — AI Governance Now Required
**Status:** Primary source confirmed December 2024. Obligations already operative.

FINMA requires Swiss financial institutions to:
- Maintain an AI tool inventory (all AI systems in use)
- Classify AI risks (operational, compliance, reputational)
- Assign clear responsibility for AI systems
- Train staff on AI use policies
- Document AI-related incidents

The FINMA AI Desk has been established. On-site inspections have been active since Q4 2023.

### 2. CARF/CRS 2.0 — Automatic Exchange of Information
**Status:** Operative January 1, 2026. Systems must be CARF-ready now. Reporting postponed to 2027.

Common Reporting Standard (CRS) Version 2.0 is live with:
- Expanded scope: e-money products, CBDCs, indirect crypto via derivatives
- Tighter due-diligence requirements
- TIN (Tax Identification Number) validation mandatory
- AI systems used in client onboarding, KYC, and reporting must be documented

### 3. FATF Mutual Evaluation — Switzerland 2026-2027-2028
**Status:** Active evaluation period. FATF 2026-2027/2028.

External pressure forcing WM firms to:
- Strengthen AI-enhanced AML/KYC controls
- Document AI decision rationale for compliance audits
- Ensure AI governance covers client risk scoring and transaction monitoring

## The Shadow AI Problem in WM

91% of Swiss financial institutions using AI are using **generative AI**. This means:
- ChatGPT/Claude for client communications
- GenAI for investment research summaries
- Consumer AI tools for market analysis
- Undocumented AI tools processing client financial data

**Shadow AI is primarily consumer GenAI in WM.** This is an FADP high-risk category: financial data is sensitive by definition.

## The Six-Decision Shadow AI Audit for WM

| Decision | Question | WM-Specific Risk |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Do you know every AI tool your team uses for client work? | Client financial data exposure |
| 1 | Are AI-generated client communications reviewed before sending? | Mis-selling / suitability risk |
| 2 | Is client financial data processed by AI tools documented in your inventory? | FINMA Circ. 08/2024 violation |
| 3 | Do you have a KYC/AML AI governance framework? | FATF evaluation exposure |
| 4 | Are AI tools used for investment recommendations? | FINMA licensing / suitability |
| 5 | Is your AI vendor due diligence documented? | Outsourcing risk (BIS Volcker-type) |
| 6 | Do you have an AI incident response protocol? | Mandatory breach notification |

## FADP Cross-Phase Integration (Legal Vertical → WM)

From Phase 01 C50:

**69% individual AI use vs. 34% formal firm policies = 35-point gap in law firms.**

In WM firms, this gap is even wider: 92% belief vs. 17% deployment = ~75 points.

**The FADP applies to WM AI use:**
- FADP Art. 5(c): AI processing of client financial data must have legal basis
- FADP Art. 21: Automated decisions affecting client portfolios require human review
- FADP Art. 16: Client financial data cannot be used to train AI models without explicit consent
- DPIA required for AI systems processing high-risk financial data

**FDPIC enforcement in WM:** Timing signal. FDPIC enforcement has been absent in legal sector but operational in others (X/Twitter March 2025, Coop January 2026). WM is likely the next enforcement priority given FATF evaluation context.
