# 01 — ICP — Geneva Legal (Cycle 21)

## ICP: Small Geneva Law Firms (2–5 Attorneys)

**Created:** 2026-03-30 | **Cycle:** 21 | **Phase:** 01 | **Vertical:** Geneva Legal

---

## Ideal Client Profile

### Demographics
- **Firm size:** 2–5 attorneys (small boutique)
- **Location:** Geneva, Vaud (dual market)
- **Practice areas:** Corporate/M&A, contracts, family law, employment, estate planning
- **Revenue context:** Billing hourly or fixed-fee; AI adoption = cost efficiency play

### Pain Layer
- **AI tool adoption accelerating** — using ChatGPT, Clio, docReview, contract analysis AI without formal governance policy
- **FDPIC building enforcement portfolio** — 1,053 complaints 2024/2025; 6 enforcement actions 2025-2026; law firms NOT yet targeted but pattern is clear
- **Regulatory timeline ambiguity** — Federal Council "lean approach" (Feb 12, 2025); no cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026; FDJP consultation draft end-2026; sector-specific only
- **ANBLES silent since Feb 2026 report** — no small-firm-specific guidance from bar
- **Swissdamed spillover** — Jul 1, 2026 ~13 WEEKS away (healthcare deadline) creating broader Swiss AI governance awareness
- **FADP directly applicable to AI** — FDPIC confirmed Sep 2023 nFADP Art. 5(c) applies to AI-based data processing

### Decision-Maker Profile
- **Managing partner or sole proprietor** — controls budget and practice decisions
- **Time-poor, billable-hour constrained** — wants efficiency gains, fears non-compliance liability
- **No dedicated IT or compliance staff** — governance falls to attorney personally
- **Aware AI exists, unsure what obligations apply**

### Buying Psychology
- **Liability fear > efficiency desire initially** — once they understand FDPIC enforcement is building, urgency activates
- **Wants "done for me"** — checklist or framework they can implement without external consultants
- **Peer influence** — Geneva legal community is tight; early adopters validated by peer success
- **First-mover window** — no boutique competitor serving this segment; window ~12-18 months before market entry

### Window of Opportunity
- **Active now:** FADP (nFADP) Art. 5(c) — transparency + DPIA obligations already in force
- **~2 months:** Federal Council First Draft (May 2026) — external pressure event
- **~13 weeks:** Swissdamed Jul 1, 2026 — healthcare deadline creating spillover awareness
- **~13 weeks:** FDPIC wearables guidance Mar 2026 — FDPIC scope expanding
- **~9-15 months:** FDJP consultation draft (end-2026) — formal legislative signal
- **No boutique competitor confirmed** — 10th+ consecutive cycle of competitive absence
