**The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question:**
*Nine Decisions Geneva Attorneys Must Make Before Using AI*

---

### Section 1 — The FDPIC Is Watching (Enforcement Gap = Your Window)

**Hook:**
The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) handled 1,053 data protection complaints in 2024/2025. Six enforcement actions followed in 2025-2026. Not one targeted a Geneva law firm. This is not reassurance — it is a warning.

**Key points:**
- FDPIC enforcement: Transport companies, retailers, social media platforms
- Pattern: FDPIC builds portfolio sector by sector
- Law firms ABSENT from enforcement — but scope is expanding (FDPIC wearables guidance March 2026)
- Your window: The attorneys who act before enforcement arrive will define the standard

---

### Section 2 — Which AI Tools Are You Using Today? (Inventory)

**Hook:**
Before you can govern AI, you need to know which AI you are actually using.

**Key points:**
- Email/calendar AI assistants
- Document review and contract analysis tools
- Legal research AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Casus, etc.)
- Client intake and matter management AI
- Billing and time-tracking AI
- Any AI processing client names, case details, financial information

**Action item:** Complete an AI inventory for your practice

---

### Section 3 — What Data Is Being Processed? (nFADP Art. 5(c))

**Hook:**
The Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP), in force since September 1, 2023, applies directly to AI-based data processing. You do not need a new AI law to have AI obligations.

**Key points:**
- nFADP Art. 5(c): Transparency obligation for AI processing
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing
- Client names, case details, financial information = personal data
- FDPIC confirmed: AI-based data processing = existing obligations apply

---

### Section 4 — Your SBA Obligations (June 2024 Guidelines)

**Hook:**
The Swiss Bar Association published AI guidelines in June 2024. They are not optional.

**Key points:**
- Confidentiality: No client-sensitive data in AI tools without adequate protection
- Competence: You must critically review AI outputs for accuracy and bias
- Non-discrimination: AI training data biases exist; you are responsible
- Human oversight: AI assists, does not replace professional judgment
- Transparency: You must disclose AI usage to clients

---

### Section 5 — The Swissdamed Spillover Effect (~13 Weeks)

**Hook:**
Switzerland's most concrete AI regulation deadline is not about law firms — but it will affect how your clients see your obligations.

**Key points:**
- Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Database): July 1, 2026 — ~13 WEEKS AWAY
- No grace period for high-risk medical AI
- Your healthcare clients are asking questions now
- Legal clients across all sectors will follow

---

### Section 6 — The Federal Council Timeline (Two Tracks)

**Hook:**
Switzerland is not passing a cross-sector AI law. It is doing something more nuanced — and more confusing for practitioners.

**Key points:**
- Track 1 (Formal): Federal Council strategy Feb 12, 2025; FDJP consultation draft end-2026; CoE AI Convention
- Track 2 (Multi-stakeholder): Federal Council First Draft May 2026 (~2 months away)
- Sector-specific approach: Healthcare, finance, transport get tailored measures
- Your obligation: Act within current nFADP framework, not waiting for new law

---

### Section 7 — Three Decisions Before You Use Any AI Tool

**Hook:**
Every AI tool adoption by a Geneva attorney involves three implicit decisions. Most attorneys never make them consciously.

**Key points:**
1. **Confidentiality decision:** Are you allowed to put this client data into this AI tool? (SBA + nFADP)
2. **Competence decision:** Do you understand what this AI tool does and what it might get wrong? (SBA)
3. **Disclosure decision:** Are you obligated to tell the client you used AI? (SBA transparency duty)

---

### Section 8 — The Anti-Pattern (What Incompetent AI Governance Looks Like)

**Hook:**
There is a wrong way to adopt AI in legal practice. It looks like this.

**Key points:**
- No AI inventory — tools adopted ad hoc
- Client data entered into free AI tools with no DPA
- AI outputs accepted without professional review
- No disclosure to clients
- No documentation of AI decision-making process

---

### Section 9 — The Framework (Nine Decisions for Your Practice)

**Hook:**
Here is the nine-section framework every Geneva small law firm needs before using AI.

**Framework:**
1. [ ] AI Inventory: What AI tools are you using?
2. [ ] Data Classification: What client data is in each tool?
3. [ ] nFADP Assessment: Do you have Art. 5(c) obligations?
4. [ ] SBA Compliance: Do your AI tools meet SBA guidelines?
5. [ ] DPA Review: Does your AI vendor have an adequate Data Processing Agreement?
6. [ ] Bias Check: Have you reviewed AI outputs for training data bias?
7. [ ] Client Disclosure: Have you disclosed AI usage where required?
8. [ ] Documentation: Have you documented your AI governance decisions?
9. [ ] Annual Review: Do you review AI tools annually for compliance?
