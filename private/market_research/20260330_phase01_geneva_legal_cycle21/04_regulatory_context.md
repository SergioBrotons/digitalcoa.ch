## Active Obligations NOW (nFADP)

### FADP / nFADP — Already in Force
- **Effective:** September 1, 2023
- **FDPIC confirmed:** nFADP Art. 5(c) directly applicable to AI-based data processing
- **Obligations:**
  - Transparency about AI processing (Art. 5(c)(1))
  - Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for high-risk processing (Art. 5(c)(2))
  - Right to object to automated decisions (Art. 5(c)(3))
  - Rectification and deletion rights apply to AI outputs (Art. 5(c)(4))

### FDPIC Enforcement Architecture
- **Criminal route only** — FDPIC cannot issue direct fines under nFADP
- **Enforcement route:** Criminal complaint → Federal Office of Police (Fedpol) → Public Prosecutor
- **Pattern:** FDPIC building enforcement portfolio via guidance + referrals
- **6 enforcement actions 2025-2026:** Transport (x2), banking, retail (x2), social media
- **Law firms NOT yet targeted:** Timing signal (window open)

### FDPIC Wearables Guidance — March 2026
- FDPIC scope expanding beyond initial enforcement targets
- Law firms using AI for client intake, matter management, document review face scrutiny

## Swiss Domestic Layer

### SBA Guidelines — June 2024 (National Baseline)
- **Source:** Swiss Bar Association confirmed Jun 2024
- **Scope:** All Swiss lawyers
- **Key duties:**
  - Confidentiality: No sensitive client data in AI tools without adequate protection
  - Competence/diligence: Critical analysis of AI outputs for accuracy and bias
  - Non-discrimination: Awareness of bias in AI training data
  - Human oversight: AI as "legal assistant not replacement"
  - Transparency with clients regarding AI usage

### ANBLES AI Working Group Report — February 2026
- **Source:** swlegal.com PDF confirmed
- **Status:** Swiss legal community actively tracking; report silent on small firm specifics
