### 2. TEF Rules Innovation Conference — Full Detail (NET-NEW beyond C80)
- **Christopher Boog** (Schellenberg Wittmer) — President of Arbitration Court, Swiss Arbitration Centre
- TEF Rules conceived to address trust/estate/foundation disputes: multi-generational, complex cross-border assets, privacy needs, emotional dynamics
- Drafters panel: **David Brownbill KC** (XXIV Old Buildings), **Werner Jahnel** (LALIVE), **Stephanie Pfisterer** (Homburger)
- Key innovation: unilateral arbitration clauses in trust/estate/foundation instruments (Article 178(4) PILA / Article 358(2) CPC confirm validity)
- Article 2 TEF Rules: "entitled persons" concept (not party-centric; heirs, beneficiaries, unborn, incapacitated)
- Article 3: Swiss Arbitration Centre Court can appoint arbitrators where entitled persons not legally represented
- Disclosure obligation on claiming party
- Comparative: Swiss Arbitration Centre Supplemental Rules for Corporate Law Disputes (2023) — similar subjective scope concerns

### 3. International Commercial Courts vs Arbitration (NET-NEW article)
- Fresh Perspectives article, Swiss Arbitration, 25/03/2026
- **Mirella Janett** (Walder Wyss)
- Key debate: International commercial courts emerging as specialized forums — complementary vs competitive to arbitration
- **Franziska Studer** (Nivalion AG): commercial courts enhance access to justice, create attractive litigation funding investment opportunities
- **Clemens Heusch** (Nokia, Head Global Litigation): big players in "waiting mode" until more practical experience; early movers needed
- **Rodrigo Carè** (Galadari Advocates): DIFC Courts becoming go-to for interim measures supporting arbitration:
  - **Carmon v Cuenda (2024)**: DIFC Court of Appeal confirmed power to issue freezing orders before foreign arbitral award is final — only connection was a Dubai bank account
  - **Trafigura v Gupta (2025)**: confirmed under 2025 DIFC Court Law — no physical asset link to DIFC required
  - DIFC Courts = powerful synergy with arbitration for asset recovery/preservation
- Consensus: arbitration remains dominant; commercial courts = welcome addition to toolbox

### 4. ASA Below 40 Event — "Beyond the Record: Should Arbitrators Seek Their Own Facts?" (NET-NEW)
- Held during Swiss Arbitration Summit 2026, 26/03/2026
- **Angelina Petti** (von Segesser Law Offices) keynote
- **Sebastian Lukic** (Schönherr) — adversarial system, limits arbitrators' right to seek facts on initiative
- **Kartik Singh** (Baker McKenzie) — Singapore/UK common law perspective, similarly favors adversarial
- **Nadja Harraschain** (rothorn legal) — German civil law, German courts confirm arbitrators may/should take inquisitorial role under Article 1042(4) German CCP (mirrored in DIS Rules 2018 Article 28)
- **Vincent Reynaud** (LALIVE) moderator
- Key principles: arbitral record = product of party consent/autonomy; due process risk if tribunal relies on facts outside record; enforceability jeopardy
- Corruption red flags: ICC 2024 Report on Red Flags; upcoming ICC umbrella report on corruption
- Thin record: tribunal may draw attention to missing material, but must be proportionate
- Tools: requesting clarification from parties > independent online research; transparency paramount
- Counsel advice: never assume prior knowledge; "hope for the best, prepare for worst"; absent respondent cases may trigger more tribunal inquiries
