## Opening Problem Statement

**GPAI (Government Procurement of AI) — operative since August 2, 2025.**

For consulting firms with EU public sector contracts or EU-nexus operations, the EU's GPAI (General Purpose AI) regulation has been active since August 2, 2025. That is ~8 months of active legal obligation.

The GPAI requires:
- Transparency obligations for general-purpose AI models
- Documentation of AI training data sources
- Energy and resource consumption disclosure
- Compliance with EU copyright law for training data

For consulting firms using AI tools in deliverables, this means: if you are using AI to produce work for EU clients, you are in GPAI scope.

**NEW in C51:** The FDPIC has significantly increased enforcement resources (2024/2025 Annual Report). The FADP has explicit prohibitions on AI applications undermining informational self-determination. Consulting firms using AI for client profiling, deliverable analysis, or performance scoring may be in scope.

## The Consulting Firm AI Governance Framework

### The 9-Section Framework

1. **AI Inventory**: What AI tools does your firm use, and for what purposes?
2. **Data Classification**: Which client data is processed by AI tools?
3. **Vendor Due Diligence**: Are your AI tool vendors' data practices documented?
4. **Output Review Protocol**: Who reviews AI-generated deliverables before client submission?
5. **FADP Compliance**: Art. 5(c) legal basis, Art. 21 human review, DPIA if needed
6. **GPAI Obligations** (if EU-nexus): Copyright, transparency, documentation
7. **Employee Policy**: Clear guidelines for AI use in client work
8. **Incident Response**: What happens when AI produces an error?
9. **Training**: Are your consultants trained on AI governance?

## Why Consulting Firms Are Different

- **Deliverables are the product**: Unlike law firms where AI assists research, consulting firms deliver AI-assisted reports, strategies, analyses
- **Client confidentiality is paramount**: Competitive intelligence, M&A data, financial projections — all processed by AI tools
- **GPAI is specifically relevant**: Consulting firms producing reports with AI assistance for EU public sector = direct GPAI scope
- **73% abandoned AI projects**: Skills gap is the primary failure mode — governance framework must address capability, not just compliance
- **40% AI skills gap**: Training investment required alongside governance
- **FADP hard prohibitions**: AI applications that profile client behavior or undermine informational self-determination are prohibited — consulting firms using AI for client scoring need to audit against this
