## Three Immediate Regulatory Obligations

### 1. FINMA Circular 08/2024 — AI Governance Now Required
**Status:** Primary source confirmed December 2024. Obligations already operative.

FINMA requires Swiss financial institutions to:
- Maintain an AI tool inventory (all AI systems in use)
- Classify AI risks (operational, compliance, reputational)
- Assign clear responsibility for AI systems
- Train staff on AI use policies
- Document AI-related incidents

The FINMA AI Desk has been established. On-site inspections have been active since Q4 2023.

### 2. CARF/CRS 2.0 — Automatic Exchange of Information
**Status:** Operative January 1, 2026. Systems must be CARF-ready now. Reporting postponed to 2027.

Common Reporting Standard (CRS) Version 2.0 is live with:
- Expanded scope: e-money products, CBDCs, indirect crypto via derivatives
- Tighter due-diligence requirements
- TIN (Tax Identification Number) validation mandatory
- AI systems used in client onboarding, KYC, and reporting must be documented

### 3. FATF Mutual Evaluation — Switzerland 2026-2027-2028
**Status:** Active evaluation period. FATF 2026-2027/2028.

External pressure forcing WM firms to:
- Strengthen AI-enhanced AML/KYC controls
- Document AI decision rationale for compliance audits
- Ensure AI governance covers client risk scoring and transaction monitoring

## The Shadow AI Problem in WM

**NEW from Phase 01 C51:** 91% of Swiss financial institutions using AI are using **generative AI**. Shadow AI is primarily consumer GenAI in WM.

**NEW from Phase 01 C51:** The FDPIC has explicitly confirmed that FADP applies to AI-based data processing. AI tools processing client financial data must:
- Disclose purpose, functionality, and data sources (Transparency)
- Implement Privacy by Design and Default
- Complete DPIA for high-risk processing
- Notify promptly of data security breaches

**NEW FADP prohibition signal:** The FADP prohibits AI applications designed to undermine privacy and informational self-determination. WM firms using AI for client profiling, behavior scoring, or surveillance-like functions may be approaching prohibited territory.
