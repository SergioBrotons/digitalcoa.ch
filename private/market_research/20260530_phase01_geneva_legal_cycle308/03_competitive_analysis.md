# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal — C308 Phase 01

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing — C308

## Confirmed Competitive Landscape (C308 Update)

### Large Firm Advisors — Structural Non-Competitors (Confirmed from C307)
- **CMS Switzerland, Schellenberg Wittmer, White & Case, Niederer Kraft Frey** — enterprise pricing, large firm scale, structural mismatch for 2–5 attorney Geneva firms
- **Status unchanged:** Not addressable competitors for the ICP

### OFL (Ordre des Avocats de Genève) — Structural Non-Provider (Confirmed from C307)
- No AI governance guidance published for Geneva attorneys (confirmed across all cycles)
- OFL silence continues to create the structural guidance vacuum — now entering its third year
- **C308 update:** No new OFL guidance observed as of May 2026
- Market implication: The vacuum is not closing — it is widening

### ANBLES (Association neuchâteloise) — Awareness Reference Only
- **Status:** February 2026 report remains the only sectoral AI guidance from Swiss bar associations
- **C308 update:** No Geneva-specific OFL follow-up to ANBLES report
- Market implication: Managing partners who have seen ANBLES report are more informed but no better equipped — awareness without tools

### Insurance Brokers — Structural Access Point, Not Compliance Service
- **Role:** Present carrier attestation forms, facilitate renewal conversations, relationship-based trust
- **C308 update:** Brokers increasingly aware that the attestation they present creates personal liability — but still do not explain this to managing partners
- **Gap unchanged:** Do not provide AI governance framework, do not explain carrier form implications, E&O coverage unclear for form explanation role
- Market implication: Broker relationship remains the highest-value referral pathway for the ICP

### Self-Service Platforms — International, Non-Geneva-Specific
- **Available:** International AI governance templates (ISACA, NIST, ISO frameworks), AI policy generators, vendor due diligence checklists
- **C308 update:** Some platforms now include "Swiss law" modules — but these are generic Swiss law, not Geneva canton-specific professional obligations
- **Gap unchanged:** Not Geneva-specific, not attorney-specific, not Article 321 CC calibrated, not bilingual, not managing-partner-personal-liability-framed
- Market implication: Self-service attempt returns the ICP to market for human-guided Geneva-specific advisory

### DigitalCoa / Sergio Brotons — Addressable Position (Confirmed from C307)
- **Position:** Boutique Geneva-specific AI governance advisory for small firms
- **Competitive differentiation:** Geneva-canton specific, attorney-client privilege focused, Article 321 CC expertise, bilingual EN+FR, carrier-attestation-to-evidence architecture, managing partner personal liability lens, matter-level documentation design
- **C308 unique fit:** Matter-level documentation systems, associate shadow AI reviews, Swissdamed scope clarification, Geneva civil procedure AI disclosure protocol, renewal window positioning
- **Competitive status:** No Geneva-specific boutique competitor confirmed — structural white space maintained

## What's Missing (White Spaces) — C308

### Missing 01: Matter-Level AI Use Documentation System for Small Law Firms

**What's missing:** A documentation system that maps AI use to specific client matters — producing per-matter evidence of AI tool authorization, AI use description, supervision performed, and human review completed. Distinct from firm-level AI policy documentation. Required by carriers at renewal. Required by Geneva civil procedure in litigation. Required by OFL in disciplinary proceedings.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** Firm-level policy templates don't produce matter-level records. General compliance platforms are designed for firm-level documentation. Matter-level documentation requires understanding both AI tool operation and Geneva professional obligations at the matter level. No Geneva service offers matter-specific AI use documentation.

**Entry condition:** The service produces a matter-level AI use log architecture — a lightweight system that attorneys can use to record, for each client matter, what AI was used, how, and with what supervision. The output is a documented evidence architecture per matter type, not a single firm-wide policy.

---

### Missing 02: Associate Personal AI Subscription Audit for Firm Client Matters

**What's missing:** A discrete audit service that identifies which personal AI subscriptions associates are using on firm client matters, maps those vendor relationships to the firm's professional obligations, and produces recommendations for which subscriptions to authorize, which to restrict, and which vendor contracts to put in place. Distinct from general vendor due diligence — the associate personal subscription angle is specific to the small firm governance problem.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** General IT audits review firm-owned systems. General vendor due diligence reviews firm-signed contracts. The associate personal subscription problem is a discrete governance gap that neither IT audits nor vendor due diligence addresses — because the contracts are personal, not firm-level.

**Entry condition:** The service requires conducting associate interviews, reviewing personal AI tool usage on firm matters, mapping vendor contracts to professional obligations, and producing a tiered recommendation (authorize/restrict/replace). No Geneva service offers this specific combination.

---

### Missing 03: Geneva Civil Procedure AI Disclosure Protocol Advisory

**What's missing:** A specific advisory on what Geneva small firms should do to prepare for potential AI disclosure obligations in civil litigation — given that no cantonal court guidance exists. The advisory maps what is currently known about CPC disclosure obligations, what reasonable AI disclosure preparation looks like given that vacuum, and how to build matter-level records that are defensible even without specific court guidance.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** Geneva civil procedure AI disclosure is a genuine vacuum — no court has issued guidance, no bar opinion exists, no legal academic commentary addresses this specifically for small Geneva firms. The service requires taking a position on the undisclosed question — which requires both litigation experience and Geneva court knowledge.

**Entry condition:** The service requires expertise in Geneva civil procedure (CPC), understanding of AI tool operation, and the ability to design a pre-litigation documentation preparation approach for firms that have no specific court guidance to follow. No Geneva service offers this.

---

### Missing 04: Swissdamed Scope Clarification for Legal Services (vs. Medical Device Scope)

**What's missing:** A specific clarification service that explains what Swissdamed does and does not cover for legal services firms — distinguishing the September 2026 medical device implementation from general AI accountability obligations for attorneys. Required because clients are asking about Swissdamed, brokers are reinforcing a waiting posture, and firms are misallocating attention to a regulation that does not apply to them.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** Swissdamed is primarily a medical/health sector concern. General AI governance advisors do not track Swissdamed specifically. Geneva legal sector advisors do not have Swissdamed expertise. The intersection — Swissdamed scope analysis for legal services — is unfilled.

**Entry condition:** The service requires Swissdamed regulatory expertise and Geneva legal professional context. The deliverable is a brief memo: what Swissdamed covers (medical devices), what it does not cover (legal AI), and what legal AI governance obligations exist regardless of Swissdamed. No Geneva service offers this intersection.

---

### Missing 05: Renewal Window Positioning and Documentation Preparation

**What's missing:** A specific renewal preparation service for managing partners who have already signed an initial carrier attestation and now face their first renewal with an active attestation on file. The renewal conversation with the carrier is different from the initial attestation: the carrier has a baseline, can compare years, and will ask more specific questions. The renewal preparation service produces documentation that is calibrated to the carrier's specific question patterns at renewal.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** General AI governance advisory does not track carrier-specific renewal question patterns. The renewal window is a specific market moment with specific ICP psychology (more informed, more peer-anxious, more action-ready) that requires a different advisory approach than initial contact.

**Entry condition:** The service requires understanding carrier attestation renewal patterns across multiple carriers and policy years — proprietary knowledge about what carriers actually ask at renewal, calibrated documentation, and the timing to prepare before the renewal window opens. No Geneva service offers renewal-specific positioning.

---

### Missing 06: Matter-Type Risk Stratification for Small Firm AI Governance

**What's missing:** An AI governance documentation system that differentiates by matter type — recognizing that litigation, corporate, employment, family, and real estate matters have different AI risk profiles, different client data sensitivities, and potentially different disclosure obligations. The output is a matter-type risk matrix that maps which AI uses require what level of documentation, authorization, and supervision — within a single small firm.

**Why it hasn't been filled:** General AI governance frameworks treat firms as homogeneous units. Matter-type differentiation requires understanding both specific practice area risks and specific AI tool applications within those practices. No Geneva service offers matter-type stratification for small firm AI governance.

**Entry condition:** The service requires Geneva legal practice area knowledge (litigation, corporate, employment, family, real estate), AI tool application knowledge for each practice area, and documentation design skill. No Geneva service offers this combination for 2–5 attorney firms.

## Competitive Response Scenarios (C308 Update)

### Scenario A: OFL Publishes AI Guidance Before Next Cycle
- **Probability:** Low in near term (12–18 months)
- **Impact:** Would provide official anchor but not eliminate service need — guidance is baseline, not implementation, and won't address matter-level documentation
- **Response:** Position as implementation partner for OFL guidance — firms still need matter-specific documentation and associate subscription audit

### Scenario B: Carrier Publishes Detailed AI Attestation Requirements
- **Probability:** Medium — carriers are increasingly specific about evidence requirements at renewal
- **Impact:** Would accelerate ICP urgency and make documentation requirements clearer
- **Response:** Position as the service that produces exactly what carriers are asking for — matter-level evidence, not firm-level policy

### Scenario C: International Self-Service Platform Launches Geneva-Specific Legal Module
- **Probability:** Medium — platforms expanding EU/Switzerland coverage
- **Impact:** Would provide cheaper alternative for self-directed firms
- **Response:** Emphasize matter-level documentation, associate subscription audit, and Swissdamed clarification — none of which generic platforms address

### Scenario D: Broker Launches AI Renewal Preparation Service
- **Probability:** Low-Medium — brokers could expand their carrier form role
- **Impact:** Would change the referral conversation but not eliminate the implementation need — brokers facilitate, they don't implement
- **Response:** Position as the implementation partner for broker-referred clients

## C308 Competitive Summary

**Structural white space maintained:** No Geneva-specific boutique AI governance competitor for small firms — consistent with all prior cycles including C307.

**Six new white spaces identified for C308:**
1. Matter-level AI use documentation system (not firm-level policy)
2. Associate personal AI subscription audit for firm client matters
3. Geneva civil procedure AI disclosure protocol advisory
4. Swissdamed scope clarification for legal services
5. Renewal window positioning and documentation preparation
6. Matter-type risk stratification for small firm AI governance

**C308 positioning opportunity:** The market is not just "AI governance" — it is "AI governance calibrated to the specific renewal question, the specific matter type, and the specific associate tool use." The first service that addresses matter-level documentation, associate shadow AI, and renewal-specific positioning captures the next layer of the ICP's actual problem.
