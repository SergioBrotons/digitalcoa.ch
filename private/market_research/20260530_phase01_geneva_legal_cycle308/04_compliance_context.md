# Compliance Context: Geneva Legal — C308 Phase 01

## Geneva/Switzerland Specific Regulatory — C308

---

## Regulatory Landscape Overview (C308)

### Primary Regulatory Framework (Confirmed from C307 + New C308 Developments)

**1. Article 321 CC — Professional Secrecy (Swiss Criminal Code)**
- Article 321 CC criminalizes disclosure of confidential information obtained in professional capacity
- Applies to attorneys, physicians, clergy, and designated professionals
- AI vendor access to client information processed through AI tools: potential Article 321 CC breach
- **C308 update:** No Geneva-specific OFL guidance on Article 321 CC and AI vendor access published — the privilege exposure via vendor remains unaddressed at the bar level
- Personal criminal exposure: confirmed, active, unmitigated for small firms without vendor contract reviews

**2. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) — Active Obligation**
- In force since 2014, revised nFADP in force September 2023
- Applies to any processing of personal data of Swiss residents
- AI tool use in client matters = processing personal data under nFADP
- Data Processing Agreements required with AI vendors processing client personal data
- **C308 update:** FDPIC enforcement portfolio building continues — law firms not yet targeted but gap is timing signal, not exemption. Associates using personal AI subscriptions create a specific nFADP exposure: the associate's personal account may not have a compliant DPA with the vendor at all.

**3. EU AI Act — Nexus-Based Exposure**
- EU AI Act entered into force August 2, 2026 (published in EU Official Journal)
- Applies to AI systems placed in service or used in the EU, or whose outputs are used in the EU
- Geneva firms with EU client matters or EU-intended AI outputs: EU AI Act nexus exposure
- General-purpose AI (GPAI) obligations: Article 51-53 cascade to users of GPAI systems
- **C308 update:** ~14 months from August 2026 enforcement start — public awareness spike imminent (August 2026). Geneva firms with EU client matters have a narrow window to establish documentation before enforcement attention increases.

**4. Swissdamed — Medical Device Registry, NOT General Legal AI**
- Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Registry): Federal Council ordinance in force, implementation ongoing
- **September 2026:** Medical device scope implementation
- **Critical C308 clarification:** Swissdamed covers medical devices under the Therapeutic Products Act (TPA) — it does NOT cover general AI applications for legal services
- **Market confusion risk:** Geneva attorneys and their clients are conflating Swissdamed's medical device scope with general AI accountability obligations for legal services
- Swissdamed creates: (1) false waiting posture for firms expecting Swissdamed to clarify their AI governance obligations, (2) client communication pressure as clients ask about Swissdamed compliance
- **The nFADP and Article 321 CC remain the applicable obligations for Geneva attorneys regardless of Swissdamed scope**

**5. Ordre des Avocats de Genève (OFL) — No AI Guidance Published**
- OFL has not published AI governance guidance for Geneva attorneys
- No FAQ, no template, no training requirement, no advisory opinion on AI use
- **C308 status:** No new OFL guidance as of May 2026 — guidance vacuum continues into third year
- OFL disciplinary framework applies to AI use but no specific AI-related disciplinary standard defined
- The guidance vacuum creates: ICP cannot point to an official standard, cannot say "OFL says X," cannot defend their AI governance choices by reference to official guidance

**6. Carrier Attestation Forms — Evolving Specificity**
- Carrier attestation forms now evolving: from general "do you have an AI policy?" to specific "show us matter-level AI use records"
- **C308 update:** Renewal attestation conversations reporting more granular carrier questions
- Personal signature: personal liability exposure unchanged — the managing partner's signature remains personally committing
- Evidence requirements: carriers asking for documentation of actual AI use across client matters, not policy statements
- Renewal window: distinct trigger from initial attestation — carrier has a baseline from prior year, can compare, can ask more specific questions

**7. Geneva Cantonal Court — No AI Disclosure Protocol**
- Federal Code of Civil Procedure (CPC): disclosure obligations for documents and evidence material to the dispute
- **C308 status:** No Geneva cantonal court has issued guidance on AI use disclosure in civil proceedings
- No established procedure for: (a) preserving AI use records, (b) disclosing AI involvement in specific matters, (c) responding to opponent's discovery requests about AI tools used
- **The absence of guidance is not permission — it is uncertainty**
- Pre-litigation preparation: managing partners who build matter-level AI use documentation before litigation have defensible evidence; managing partners who do not build records have nothing to disclose and the absence may be treated as inadequate governance

## C308 Regulatory Developments and Updates

### Swissdamed Scope Clarification (C308 Critical Update)
- Swissdamed implementation: September 2026 for medical device scope (TPA-covered products)
- Swissdamed does NOT cover: general AI tools used by legal services firms
- Misconception being corrected: Geneva attorneys who believe Swissdamed will clarify their AI governance obligations are waiting for a regulation that does not apply to them
- Correct framework: Geneva attorneys are governed by nFADP (data protection), Article 321 CC (professional secrecy), carrier attestation requirements (insurance), and OFL professional standards — none of which Swissdamed replaces or clarifies

### Carrier Renewal Pattern Evolution (C308 Update)
- Carrier attestation at renewal: more specific questions emerging
- Pattern shift: "Do you have an AI policy?" → "Show us your matter-level AI use records"
- Managing partners who produced firm-level policy at initial attestation: now being asked for year-over-year comparison and specific matter evidence
- Renewal creates: documented evidence baseline that carriers will use to assess adequacy over time

### EU AI Act August 2026 Awareness Spike (C308 Timing)
- ~14 months from August 2026 enforcement start
- Geneva firms with EU client matters: deadline approaching for establishing GPAI documentation
- Client pressure: EU-facing clients increasingly asking about AI governance as EU AI Act awareness grows
- No Geneva-specific EU AI Act guidance for small law firms — guidance vacuum continues

### FDPIC Enforcement Portfolio Building (C308 Update)
- FDPIC AI enforcement portfolio building: continued sector-by-sector approach
- Law firms: not yet targeted — gap remains timing signal, not exemption
- Associates using personal AI subscriptions: additional FDPIC exposure angle (DPA adequacy for associates' personal accounts)
- Geneva law firm window: ~12–18 months before FDPIC attention turns to legal sector as a distinct category

## Multi-Authority Documentation Requirements (C308 Update)

### Authority 1: Insurance Carrier — Renewal Evidence
- **What they want:** Matter-level AI use evidence at renewal — not firm-level policy statements
- **What they ask for:** Documentation of what AI was used in specific client matters, with supervision evidence
- **C308 update:** Renewal questions more granular than initial attestation
- **Documentation type:** Matter-level AI use logs, not firm-level governance policies

### Authority 2: Ordre des Avocats de Genève (OFL) — Disciplinary Documentation
- **What they want:** Evidence of professional supervision and competence maintenance for AI-assisted work
- **What they ask for:** Documentation of actual supervision process — no OFL AI-specific standard defined
- **Form:** Disciplinary proceedings if client complaint or bar investigation initiated
- **Documentation type:** Supervision framework documentation, matter-level records

### Authority 3: FDPIC — nFADP Compliance
- **What they want:** DPA compliance for AI vendors processing client personal data
- **What they ask for:** Data Processing Agreement compliance, data subject rights implementation
- **C308 update:** Associates' personal AI subscriptions may lack compliant DPAs — creating additional nFADP exposure
- **Documentation type:** DPA compliance documentation for each AI vendor (firm-level AND associate personal subscriptions)

### Authority 4: Cantonal Court — Litigation Discovery
- **What they want:** Disclosure of AI use in specific client matters in litigation
- **What they ask for:** Matter-specific AI use disclosure, potentially including vendor identities and data processed
- **C308 status:** No Geneva cantonal court AI disclosure protocol exists — the question is genuinely unanswered
- **Documentation type:** Matter-level AI use documentation — defensible even without specific court guidance

### Authority 5: EU AI Act Nexus — GPAI Documentation
- **What they want:** Documentation of GPAI system use in EU-intended matters
- **What they ask for:** GPAI provider identification, output logging, human oversight documentation
- **Applies to:** Geneva firms with EU client matters or EU-intended AI outputs
- **Documentation type:** GPAI-specific matter-level records

## Geneva-Specific Professional Context (C308 Update)

### Geneva Bar (OFL) — Continued Guidance Vacuum
- OFL has not published AI governance guidance — confirmed across all cycles C214–C307
- No FAQ, no template, no training requirement, no advisory opinion on AI use
- C308 status: No change — guidance vacuum is now entering its third year
- Managing partners navigating this vacuum without an official anchor remain exposed to the maximum: no standard to point to, no OFL guidance to follow, personal liability unchanged

### Associate Shadow AI — New Structural Problem
- Associates using personal AI subscriptions on firm client matters: discrete structural problem for small firms
- Vendor contract: between associate personally and vendor — firm has no DPA
- Managing partner has no visibility: does not know which personal tools are processing client data
- Privilege exposure: associate personal account may be processing privileged client communications without firm-level authorization
- The problem is structural: standard associate employment practices do not cover this governance gap

### Matter-Type Risk Differentiation — Operational Reality
- Geneva small firms handle: litigation, corporate/M&A, employment, family, real estate, and other matters
- AI risk profile differs by matter type:
  - **Litigation:** court disclosure obligations, opponent discovery requests about AI use, evidence standards
  - **Corporate/M&A:** deal confidentiality, privileged deal communications, due diligence AI use
  - **Employment:** employee personal data, HR-sensitive information, works council considerations
  - **Family:** highest sensitivity personal data, custody and financial information
  - **Real Estate:** property data, financial information, tenant/buyer personal data
- No Geneva service differentiates AI governance requirements by matter type — firms apply one uniform policy to all

### Personal Liability — Unchanged and Absolute
- Geneva attorneys: personal liability exposure for professional conduct unchanged
- Carrier attestation signature: personal signature, not firm signature
- Article 321 CC: personal criminal exposure for privilege breach
- OFL disciplinary: personal professional license exposure
- Associate shadow AI: does not eliminate managing partner's personal liability for firm client matters
- The managing partner's personal liability is the operative exposure regardless of what associates do

## C308 Compliance Context Summary

**Active regulatory obligations (unchanged from C307):**
1. Article 321 CC — professional secrecy, AI vendor access creates potential breach exposure
2. nFADP — active, AI tool use in client matters requires DPA compliance
3. EU AI Act — nexus-based, August 2026 enforcement start, EU client matters create exposure
4. OFL — no AI guidance published, guidance vacuum creates uncertainty
5. Carrier attestation — personal signature, undefined "adequate" standard, renewal questions more specific

**C308 new regulatory dimensions:**
6. Swissdamed — medical device scope, does NOT cover legal AI, creates false waiting posture
7. Associate personal subscriptions — additional nFADP exposure, no firm DPA exists
8. Geneva civil procedure — no AI disclosure protocol exists for litigation
9. Carrier renewal evolution — matter-level evidence requests replacing firm-level policy questions

**Key compliance gap (C308):** The ICP now faces a compound problem: firm-level documentation they built for initial attestation may not satisfy carrier renewal questions; associate personal subscriptions created unmanaged vendor relationships; Swissdamed is distracting from obligations that actually apply; and no civil procedure guidance exists for AI disclosure in litigation. The service must address all of these simultaneously — not one at a time.
