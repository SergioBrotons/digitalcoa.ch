## The Geneva Small Law Firm AI Accountability Briefing

### What Your Carrier Is Actually Asking For at Renewal — And What You Don't Have Yet

---

I work with managing partners at Geneva boutique firms. 2 to 5 attorneys. Geneva-based. Most of them signed their carrier attestation form without reading it carefully. I understand — I've seen these forms. They arrive with renewal paperwork, they look routine, and everyone signs.

Then the questions start.

This year, the questions are different. The carrier isn't just asking whether you have an AI policy. They're asking you to show them, matter by matter, what AI you used, how you authorized it, and how you supervised it.

If you're like most of the managing partners I work with, you don't have that.

You have a firm-level document. Maybe a policy statement. Maybe something your associate put together when the form arrived. What you don't have is a record for each client file — which AI tools were used, on what, with what supervision, and how you can demonstrate that a human authorized the output.

That's the gap. And at renewal, it matters.

I review the AI tools being used in your firm — including the ones your associates brought in on personal subscriptions that you may not know about. I then produce a documentation architecture: what records to keep, at the matter level, so that when your carrier asks, you can show them. So that when a client asks, you can answer. So that if a matter goes to court, you have something defensible.

## The Associate Personal Subscription Problem

This comes up in almost every firm I review.

One of your associates — your most technically capable one, usually — is using their personal ChatGPT or Claude account on your client matters. They didn't think to ask. The vendor contract is between them personally and the vendor. There's no Data Processing Agreement with your firm. Your client's file data has been flowing through a personal account that your firm has no visibility into and no contract governing.

You didn't know. Now you do.

The question is what you do about it. I can help you answer that — and build the vendor authorization framework that should have been in place from the beginning.

---

## The Swissdamed Confusion

Your clients are asking whether you're compliant with Swissdamed. Your broker may have mentioned it. You've probably been waiting to see what Swissdamed requires before doing anything else.

Here's the problem: Swissdamed covers medical devices under the TPA. It doesn't cover the AI tools your law firm uses.

The obligations that actually apply to you — Article 321 CC, nFADP, your carrier attestation — are unchanged by Swissdamed. And they're still your responsibility.

The firms that are waiting for Swissdamed to clarify their position are delaying action on obligations that already exist.

---

## The Geneva Civil Procedure Problem

If one of your matters goes to litigation, the Geneva courts will have questions about AI use. What AI tools were used? On what? How was the output reviewed?

There is no cantonal guidance on how to answer those questions. No Geneva court has issued a protocol. Your carrier knows this. Your clients may not yet, but they will.

The managing partners who are moving now — building matter-level AI use records before a dispute arises — are the ones who will have something defensible to show. The ones who wait are building their evidence after the question is already asked in a courtroom.

### Ce que votre assureur vous demande réellement lors du renouvellement — et ce qui vous manque encore

Ils arrivent avec les documents de renouvellement, ils ont l'air routiniers, et tout le monde signe.

Puis les questions commencent.

Cette année, les questions sont différentes. L'assureur ne vous demande pas simplement si vous avez une politique AI. Il vous demande de lui montrer, dossier par dossier, quel outil AI vous avez utilisé, comment vous l'avez autorisé, et comment vous l'avez supervisé.

Si vous ressemblez à la plupart des associés gérants avec qui je travaille, vous n'avez pas cela.

Vous avez un document au niveau du cabinet. Peut-être une déclaration de politique. Peut-être quelque chose que votre avocat adjoint a préparé quand le formulaire est arrivé. Ce que vous n'avez pas, c'est un dossier pour chaque fichier client — quels outils AI ont été utilisés, pour quoi, avec quelle supervision, et comment vous pouvez démontrer qu'un être humain a autorisé le résultat.

C'est l'écart. Et lors du renouvellement, ça compte.

Je passe en revue les outils AI utilisés dans votre cabinet — y compris ceux que vos avocats adjoints ont apportés via des abonnements personnels dont vous ne savez peut-être pas l'existence. ## Le Problème des Abonnements Personnels des Avocats Adjoints

Cela revient dans presque chaque cabinet que je examine.

L'un de vos avocats adjoints — le plus compétent techniquement, généralement — utilise son compte ChatGPT ou Claude personnel sur vos dossiers clients. Il n'a pas pensé à demander. ## La Confusion Swissdamed

Vos clients vous demandent si vous êtes conforme à Swissdamed. Votre courtier l'a peut-être mentionné. Vous avez probablement attendu de voir ce que Swissdamed exige avant de faire quoi que ce soit d'autre.

Les obligations qui vous concernent réellement — l'article 321 CC, la nFADP, votre attestation auprès de l'assureur — ne sont pas modifiées par Swissdamed. Et elles restent votre responsabilité.

## Le Problème de Procédure Civile Genevoise

Les associés gérants qui agissent maintenant — en construisant des dossiers d'utilisation de l'AI au niveau du dossier avant qu'un litige n'apparaisse — sont ceux qui auront quelque chose de défendable à montrer. Ceux qui attendent construisent leurs preuves après que la question a déjà été posée dans une salle d'audience.
