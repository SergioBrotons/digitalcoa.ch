## Problem Statement (3 layers)

### Layer 1: The GPAI Deadline Is Real and Approaching
The EU's GPAI Regulation becomes applicable August 2, 2026. If your consulting firm advises any SMB clients operating in or connected to EU markets, GPAI compliance is on their horizon. More importantly: if your own consulting practice uses AI systems that could be classified as systemic risk AI (high-value decisions affecting multiple clients), you face GPAI obligations yourself.

The gap: most Geneva SMB-focused consulting firms have not assessed their own GPAI exposure, let alone their clients'. This is both a risk and an opportunity.

### Layer 2: 400,000 Reasons Your SMB Clients Need AI (and One Regulation to Manage)
Switzerland's structural labor shortage (KOF: 400,000 workers over 10 years) is the business-case multiplier for SMB AI adoption. Your clients are not asking "should we use AI?" — they are asking "how do we adopt AI without creating new problems?" The consulting firm that answers both questions — with a practical decision framework — wins the SMB advisory relationship.

### Layer 3: G7 Évian as the Q3 2026 SMB Uncertainty Multiplier
G7 Évian (June 15-17, 2026) is confirmed. Swiss SMB clients in trade-exposed sectors (manufacturing, logistics, cross-border professional services) face potential regulatory uncertainty in H2 2026. Consulting firms that can translate G7 outcomes into SMB-relevant guidance become indispensable advisors — not just consultants.

## The Four-Quadrant AI Decision Framework (from C57)

Applied to SMB client AI decisions:

**High Value + Low Risk → Automate immediately**
Examples: internal scheduling, time tracking, document formatting, basic research support. No governance required beyond standard tool evaluation.

**High Value + High Risk → Govern then automate**
Examples: AI-assisted client deliverable generation, AI-analyzed strategic recommendations, AI-supported financial modeling. Requires documented human review, FADP compliance, and GPAI assessment before deployment.

**Low Value + Low Risk → Defer**
Examples: AI-assisted internal memos, AI-generated meeting notes. These can wait — resources should go to quadrant 2.

**Low Value + High Risk → Avoid**
Examples: AI making hiring decisions, AI generating legally binding terms, AI replacing professional judgment in regulated advice. These create more problems than they solve for most SMBs.
