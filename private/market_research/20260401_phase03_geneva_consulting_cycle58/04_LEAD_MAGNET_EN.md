## The Three-Layer Problem

### Layer 1: The GPAI Deadline Is Real — and Your Firm Is Exposed Before Your Clients

The EU's GPAI (General Purpose AI) Regulation becomes enforceable August 2, 2026. It creates obligations for deployers of GPAI systems — including consulting firms that use AI tools to support client engagements.

If your consulting firm uses AI systems that could affect multiple clients' high-stakes decisions (e.g., AI-analyzed strategic recommendations, AI-supported financial modeling, AI-generated compliance guidance), you may face GPAI systemic risk obligations.

The consulting firms that have assessed their own GPAI exposure — and can demonstrate a governance framework — will be the credible advisors. The firms that haven't done this work will be advising clients on a regulation they haven't applied to themselves.

### Layer 2: 400,000 Reasons Your SMB Clients Need AI

Switzerland faces a structural labor shortage of approximately 400,000 workers over the next 10 years (KOF, March 2026). For your SMB clients, this is not an abstract economic statistic — it is a daily operational reality. Hiring is harder. Retention is harder. Finding specialized talent for project work is harder.

AI adoption is not a "nice to have" for most Swiss SMBs — it is becoming a structural necessity. Your clients know this. They are asking: "How do we adopt AI without creating new compliance problems, new liability exposure, and new operational risks?"

The consulting firm that answers that question — with a practical decision framework — wins the advisory relationship. The firm that answers only the technical implementation question — without addressing governance and compliance — is only doing half the job.

### Layer 3: G7 Évian Creates Q3 2026 Regulatory Uncertainty Your SMB Clients Can't Navigate Alone

The G7 summit in Évian (June 15-17, 2026) is now formally confirmed with Swiss Confederation budget commitment. For SMB clients in trade-exposed sectors — manufacturing, logistics, cross-border professional services — G7 outcomes in June will produce regulatory implications in Q3 2026.

Swiss SMBs cannot monitor and interpret G7 regulatory implications while running their businesses. Your consulting firm can. The firm that positions itself as the "G7-readiness interpreter" for Swiss SMBs in H2 2026 earns a trust premium that generic consulting engagements cannot create.

## The Four-Quadrant AI Decision Framework

Not all AI decisions are equivalent. Here is the practical framework for SMB client engagements:

**Quadrant 1 — High Value, Low Risk: Automate now**
Tools and workflows that create significant efficiency without meaningful new exposure.
- Internal scheduling and calendar management
- Document formatting and template population
- Basic research synthesis
- Time tracking and billing support

**Quadrant 2 — High Value, High Risk: Govern then automate**
AI uses that create meaningful value but carry compliance and liability risk.
- AI-assisted strategic recommendations to clients
- AI-supported financial analysis and modeling
- AI-analyzed compliance assessments
- AI-generated deliverable drafting (human-reviewed before delivery)

**Quadrant 3 — Low Value, Low Risk: Defer**
AI uses that are interesting but not worth prioritizing.
- AI-generated internal meeting summaries
- AI-assisted email drafting
- AI-based low-stakes administrative tasks

**Quadrant 4 — Low Value, High Risk: Avoid**
AI uses that create more problems than they solve for most SMBs.
- AI making hiring or firing decisions
- AI generating legally binding terms without human review
- AI replacing professional judgment in regulated activities

The four-quadrant framework is both a client advisory tool and a consulting firm self-assessment instrument. Start with your own firm's AI use — map it, classify it, govern it.

## Self-Assessment: Is Your Consulting Firm GPAI-Ready?

**Answer these 10 questions about your firm's current AI governance:**

1. Have you assessed which AI tools your firm uses that could be classified as GPAI systems?
2. Do you have documented human review processes for AI outputs that affect client decisions?
3. Have you classified your AI tools according to the Federal AI Ordinance risk categories?
4. Is your AI use covered by your existing professional liability insurance?
5. Do you have a documented escalation protocol for AI errors or anomalous outputs?
6. For any AI tools processing EU-resident client data: have you assessed GPAI obligations?
7. Have you informed clients when AI tools were used to support your recommendations?
8. Do you have a vendor assessment process for new AI tools before adoption?
9. Has your team been trained on when AI use requires disclosure to clients?
10. Do you have a timeline to complete your firm's GPAI readiness assessment before August 2, 2026?

**Score 8-10:** You are GPAI-ready. You can advise clients credibly.

**Score 5-7:** You have internal work to do. Close your own gap before scaling client offerings.

**Score 0-4:** You are exposed. This framework is your starting point.
