### Finding 05: US CLOUD Act Exposure Through US-Hosted AI Scribe Vendor — Post-Deadline MSA Article 12 Audit Reveals Named-Physician Activity to EU Authorities

**The finding:** EU Member State market surveillance authorities have begun Article 12 logging audits on US-hosted AI scribe vendors serving Geneva médecin installé practices. The MSA sends the Article 12 audit request to the US-hosted AI scribe vendor (Microsoft Nuance DAX, Abridge, Suki — together ~70% of Geneva market). The US-hosted vendor's logging infrastructure produces engagement-level logs revealing the named-physician activity (named-physician individual using the AI scribe on the named-encounter), the named-AI-scribe usage pattern (per named-encounter, per named-EU-patient, per named-timestamp), the named-substitute médecin remplaçant activity (the named individual who used the AI scribe during the named coverage period), the named-EU-patient identifier (because the AI scribe processes the consultation audio which contains the named-EU-patient voice), the named-data-residency (US cloud), the named-vendor cooperation duty exposure (US CLOUD Act 2018, 50 USC §2713 — US federal subpoena authority over US-cloud-stored data regardless of data subject location), and the named-physician GDPR data controller responsibility (because the US-hosted vendor is processing EU-cross-border patient data under named-physician data controller authority). The US-hosted vendor discloses the named-physician to the MSA under vendor cooperation duty (the EU MSA exercises Article 12 authority; the US-hosted vendor cooperates because EU market access is conditional on cooperation; the cooperation is permitted under US CLOUD Act because US authorities have not restricted the disclosure).
