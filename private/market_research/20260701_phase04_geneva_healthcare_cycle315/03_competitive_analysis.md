## Market Structure (C315 P04 — Geneva Healthcare Physician Article 26 Enforcement)

The Geneva médecin installé AI governance market is structured around four discrete service categories. Each category is evaluated for post-deadline physician Article 26 enforcement readiness.

### Category 1: Medical Liability Carrier Attestation Brokers

**Active vendors:**
- Swiss Medical Network broker network
- FMH affinity broker partners (Concordia / CSS / Swica / Groupe Mutuel medical liability brokers)
- Geneva medical liability insurance specialists (e.g., Dr. Eicher & Partners, GenevaMed Brokers, FMH Service Genève)

**What exists:**
- Annual carrier attestation cycle (mid-year refresh in July-August)
- Carrier attestation clause templates (cabinet-level)
- Medical liability premium calculation per attestation category
- Carrier cooperation duty with MSA framework (generic)

**What is missing (C315 P04):**
- Named-physician-specific attestation clause translation (4 named-physician-specific clauses from mid-year 2026 refresh — see C315 P04 Finding 02)
- Named-physician AI scribe inventory attestation template (per named-AI-scribe tool, per named-physician, per named-vendor, per named-data-residency, per named-US-CLOUD-Act-exposure flag)
- Named-encounter AI tool usage attestation template (per named-physician, per named-encounter, named-human-review-checkpoint)
- Named-physician Article 4 AI literacy attestation template
- Named-physician incident response attestation template (with Article 321 CP criminal exposure management)
- Article 321 CP Swiss criminal medical secrecy reference in attestation language (carrier attestation has never referenced Article 321 CP before mid-year 2026 refresh)
- Named-physician signing posture documentation for named-substitute médecin remplaçants the practice may not be able to trace

**Gap:** Carrier attestation brokers are operating on cabinet-level attestation cycles. The post-deadline named-physician-specific clauses require named-physician-level evidence production. The brokers do not have the named-physician evidence production infrastructure.

**Opportunity:** First service to publish the carrier attestation named-physician-clause translation memo for the four mid-year 2026 refresh clauses owns the C315 P04 carrier-attestation-physician category.

### Category 2: AI Scribe Vendors (US-Hosted + EU-Hosted + Swiss-Hosted)

- Per-encounter AI scribe pricing
- Vendor cooperation duty with EU MSA under EU market access conditionality (Article 12 logging audit pathway)
- US CLOUD Act exposure disclosure (typically buried in vendor terms of service)

**What is missing (C315 P04):**
- Named-physician AI authorization record production (per named-physician, per named-encounter, per named-EU-patient, per named-substitute médecin remplaçant, per named-timestamp)
- Named-physician Article 26 named-sub-principal authorization roster (per named-physician + per named-AI-scribe version + per named-vendor)
- Named-physician Article 4 AI literacy credential integration (vendor-specific literacy credential per named-physician)
- Named-physician Article 14 human oversight record integration (per named-encounter, with named-physician signature + named-substitute médecin remplaçant signature if covered)
- US CLOUD Act disclosure template to named-EU-patient (retroactive disclosure per named-encounter)
- nFADP Art.45(2) cross-border consent refresh template (per named-EU-patient, per named-encounter)
- Article 321 CP-compliant pseudonymized abstraction architecture for MSA Article 12 audit response (named-physician + named-encounter index, without named-encounter content)
- EU-hosted migration architecture update (per named-target-vendor selection — Nabla France-hosted, Huga Belgium-hosted, Swiss-hosted alternative)

**Gap:** AI scribe vendors are operating on platform-level vendor cooperation duty. The post-deadline MSA Article 12 audit pathway exposes named-physician activity through vendor logs. The vendors do not produce named-physician evidence packs for the MSA audit response. The vendors do not produce US CLOUD Act disclosure templates for retroactive patient disclosure.

**Opportunity:** First service to publish the US CLOUD Act MSA-audit exposure map + named-physician audit response architecture (referencing vendor logs as evidence source, providing named-physician evidence production) owns the C315 P04 US-CLOUD-Act-physician-audit category.

## What's Missing — Comprehensive C315 P04 Gap Inventory

The C315 P04 market has six discrete service gaps that are not addressable by existing categories:

### Gap 01: Named-Physician MSA Article 99 Audit Response Architecture

**Description:** Pre-built audit response pack for the named-physician MSA Article 99 procedural letter pipeline. The pack produces named-physician AI authorization record, named-physician Article 4 AI literacy credential, named-physician Article 14 human oversight record, named-AI-scribe Article 26 authorization roster, named-encounter Article 12 logging reconciliation with Article 321 CP, named-substitute médecin remplaçant AI authorization addendum, named-patient consent form refresh, FDPIC Art.63 coordination language, carrier attestation coordination language.

**Existing alternatives:** None. Medical practice management consultants produce generic EU AI Act readiness packs; these do not produce named-physician-specific MSA audit response packs with 30-day window delivery.

**C315 P04 finding:** Finding 01

---

### Gap 02: Carrier Attestation Named-Physician-Clause Translation Memo

**Description:** Translation memo for the four named-physician-specific clauses added at mid-year 2026 carrier attestation refresh (named-physician AI scribe inventory clause, named-encounter AI tool usage attestation clause, named-physician Article 4 AI literacy attestation clause, named-physician incident response attestation clause). The memo maps each clause to specific named-physician evidence requirements, Article 26 named-sub-principal attribution architecture, nFADP Art.63 personal liability extension, MSA Article 99 coordination language, Article 321 CP medical secrecy allocation, handover-window documentation requirement.

**Existing alternatives:** None. Carrier attestation brokers produce cabinet-level attestation clauses; these do not produce named-physician-specific clause translation with Article 321 CP reference and named-substitute médecin remplaçant handover documentation.

**C315 P04 finding:** Finding 02

### Gap 03: FDPIC-MSA Coordination Architecture for Named-Physicians

**Description:** Pre-built coordination pack for the FDPIC Art.63 supervisory letter + MSA Article 99 procedural letter coordination through the named-physician triple (named-physician + named-AI-scribe + named-encounter + named-EU-patient + named-substitute médecin remplaçant). FDPIC-authorised data protection advisors produce general nFADP supervisory letter responses; medical practice management consultants produce general EU AI Act readiness packs. **C315 P04 finding:** Finding 03

## What's Missing — Cross-Vertical Comparison

The C315 P04 Geneva médecin installé gap inventory is structurally similar to the C315 P01 (Geneva legal), C315 P02 (Geneva finance), and C315 P03 (Geneva consulting) gap inventories — but with four physician-specific gaps that the legal, finance, and consulting verticals do not face:

1. **Article 321 CP Swiss criminal medical secrecy allocation** — the named-physician is exposed to Swiss criminal prosecution under Article 321 CP (imprisonment up to 3 years or fine). The MSA letter, the FDPIC letter, the carrier attestation, the Convention de remplacement addendum, and the US-hosted AI scribe vendor logs all reference the named-encounter content. The named-physician cannot disclose the named-encounter content to satisfy any of these references without breaching Article 321 CP. The legal, finance, and consulting verticals do not face this Swiss criminal law exposure layer.

2. **Per-couverture-period named-substitute handover discontinuity** — the named-substitute médecin remplaçant turns over at the rate of couverture periods (2-4 weeks per vacation, conference, illness, parental leave). The legal vertical has named associates (employment-cycle boundary); the finance vertical has named portfolio managers (mandate-cycle boundary); the consulting vertical has named sub-consultants (handover-window, monthly turnover). The healthcare vertical has named-substitute médecin remplaçants (couverture period turnover, 2-4 weeks per coverage period). The discontinuity is structural at the per-couverture-period cadence.

3. **US CLOUD Act MSA Article 12 audit pathway through US-hosted AI scribe vendor** — the MSA audits the US-hosted AI scribe vendor (Microsoft Nuance DAX, Abridge, Suki — ~70% of Geneva market). The vendor's logs reveal the named-physician triple. The vendor cooperates with the MSA under EU market access conditionality. The named-physician is exposed by the vendor selection. The legal, finance, and consulting verticals have vendor exposure types (legal AI vendor, portfolio construction AI vendor, sub-consultant's AI vendor) but none has the US CLOUD Act exposure layer specific to physician AI scribe infrastructure.

4. **SSMIG/AMG/FMH CME named-physician peer disclosure cycle** — the September 2026 SSMIG Journée + AMG Geneva specialty section meetings + FMH CME sessions + HUG colloques médico-chirurgicaux have activated the first named-physician public disclosure cycle. The legal vertical has OaF bar guidance / Geneva Bar peer disclosure; the finance vertical has Geneva Private Banking Week / GPBA mailing list peer disclosure; the consulting vertical has Geneva Strategy Network / GMC Council / Swiss Consulting / IMC Switzerland peer disclosure. The healthcare vertical has SSMIG/AMG/FMH/HUG peer disclosure with named-physician granularity at the named-encounter level.

The four physician-specific gaps are not addressable by the C315 P01-P03 service architecture. They require the C315 P04 physician-specific service architecture with Article 321 CP compliance, per-couverture-period handover documentation, US CLOUD Act MSA-audit exposure mapping, and SSMIG/AMG/FMH/HUG named-physician proactive disclosure templates.
