# Phase 01 Regulatory Context — Geneva Legal / Small Law Firms
## Cycle 9 (2026-03-30)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

### 1. EU AI Act — GPAI (General Purpose AI)
- **Status:** Operative since August 2, 2025 (~7.5 months already live as of late March 2026)
- **Nature:** Transparency, copyright handling, incident documentation for GPAI model providers and deployers
- **Law firm relevance:** If firm uses ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, or any GPAI system for legal work — GPAI deployer obligations are active
- **Key GPAI obligations:**
  - Transparency: disclose AI use to clients
  - Copyright: ensure training data compliance for AI outputs in legal deliverables
  - Incident documentation: log AI failures

### 2. EU AI Act — High-Risk
- **Date:** August 2, 2026 (~15 months)
- **Nature:** Conformity assessment for high-risk AI systems
- **Law firm relevance:** Law firms using AI in client-facing decision-making (risk assessment, case evaluation) may face high-risk classification if EU nexus applies

### 3. FDJP CoE AI Convention Consultation
- **Timeline:** End-2026 (~9 months)
- **Nature:** Federal Council CoE AI Convention consultation draft; focus areas: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- **Law firm relevance:** Will crystallize Swiss AI governance standards; triggers market-wide awareness spike

### 4. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection)
- **Status:** Operative since September 1, 2023 (~2.5 years)
- **Nature:** AI processing of client data = Art. 5(c); FDPIC enforcement trajectory confirmed
- **Law firm relevance:** Client data is highly sensitive; AI processing triggers DPIA requirements

### 5. FDPIC Enforcement (Carry-Forward)
- **Status:** 4 confirmed AI investigations 2025-2026; law firms NOT yet targeted
- **Pattern:** Transport, retail, social media — professional services not yet in scope
- **Timing signal:** Law firms are a logical next target given FDPIC enforcement portfolio building

### 6. AMLA (Cross-Phase — Legal Advisors)
- **Timeline:** H2 2026 (~3-6 months)
- **Legal relevance:** Lawyers providing advisory services (investment advice, estate planning) may face AMLA scope expansion; must join FINMA-recognized SRO
