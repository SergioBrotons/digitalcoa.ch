### AI-Specific Regulation
- **Switzerland:** No hard AI law. Guidance-based via algorithms and AI report (2023)
- **ISO 42001:** AI management system standard — emerging as de facto certification
- **EU AI Act implications:** Swiss firms with EU operations may need to comply voluntarily
- **FINMA AI guidance:** Banks must disclose AI model risk — affects legal documentation needs

## Status: VALIDATED — Updated C114

### C114 Regulatory Context Updates (2026-04-04)

#### NEW: FINMA Circular 2026/1 — Nature-related Financial Risks (Effective Jan 1, 2026)
- **What:** New FINMA circular mandating systematic integration of nature-related financial risks into governance and risk management for banks and insurers
- **Stages:** Entering force in stages from January 1, 2026
- **Scope:** Quantitative integration requirements beyond qualitative disclosures; covers biodiversity, ecosystem services, physical and transition risks
- **Geneva impact:** All Geneva financial institutions (banks, asset managers, insurers) subject to FINMA supervision need compliance programs
- **Legal advisory demand:** ESG disclosure compliance, transition plan design, greenwashing defense, governance restructuring, ESG-related litigation defense
- **Strategic note:** Nature-related risk legal advisory is a genuinely new service line not yet widely offered by Geneva law firms — positioning opportunity

#### NEW: Climate and Innovation Act + Revised CO2 Act (Effective Jan 1, 2025)
- **Climate and Innovation Act:** Legally binding Swiss framework for 2050 climate targets; requires financial industry to align financial flows with climate goals
- **Revised CO2 Act:** Mandates climate-related financial risk assessments for FINMA-supervised entities; SNB assesses financial system stability implications
- **Ordinance on Climate Disclosures:** Already in force (Jan 1, 2024); first reports due 2025 for 2024 FY; further amendments expected Jan 1, 2027 including net-zero transition plans
- **Implication:** Sustained ESG regulatory workload for Geneva financial institutions and their legal advisors through 2026-2027

#### NEW: FINMA Supervisory Convergence with EU/ECB
- FINMA's supervisory practices increasingly converging with EU and ECB regulators
- Expectation: institutions move beyond qualitative disclosures to quantitative integration of climate and nature-related risks
- Swiss intent to adapt framework in alignment with finalized European rules to maintain competitiveness
- **Advisory angle:** Cross-border regulatory alignment advisory for Geneva firms serving EU-connected Swiss institutions

#### NEW: ESG-related Litigation Risk Rising
- Increased regulatory expectations and public awareness leading to rise in ESG-related litigation and greenwashing claims globally
- Even Swiss companies not directly subject to mandatory ESG rules facing voluntary framework adoption pressure (TCFD, GRI, CDP)
- **Advisory angle:** Greenwashing defense, ESG-related litigation advisory, corporate governance defense

### C115 Regulatory Context Updates (2026-04-04)

#### NEW: FINMASA Amendments September 2025 — Cross-border Enforcement Architecture
- **What:** Swiss Federal Council adopted proposed amendments to Financial Market Supervision Act (FINMASA) September 2025
- **Key changes:** Strengthens international cooperation provisions; modifies administrative assistance procedure for market abuse (potentially limiting client right to be heard and appeal in cross-border cases); may authorize foreign supervisors to conduct audits of Swiss outsourcing arrangements of foreign financial institutions
- **Geneva impact:** Geneva financial institutions with cross-border operations and their legal counsel face new enforcement exposure and procedural constraints
- **Advisory demand:** Cross-border enforcement defense, international administrative assistance procedure compliance, foreign audit preparation

#### NEW: FinIA Revision October 2025 — New FINMA License Categories
- **What:** Federal Council public consultation October 2025 on Financial Institutions Act (FinIA) amendments
- **Proposed new categories:** "Payment instrument institutions" and "crypto-institutions" under direct FINMA supervision (shift from SRO oversight)
- **Timing:** Expected effective late 2026 or early 2027
- **Geneva impact:** Geneva fintech and payments sector must obtain new licenses; legal advisory demand for licensing applications, compliance programs, ongoing obligations
- **Advisory demand:** Fintech licensing, crypto-institution regulatory authorization, payment institution compliance framework

#### NEW: LETA — Beneficial Ownership Register (H2 2026)
- **What:** Federal Act on Transparency of Legal Entities and Identification of Beneficial Owners (LETA) entering force H2 2026
- **Scope:** Centralized federal register of beneficial owners; all Swiss legal entities must disclose ultimate beneficial owners; aligns Switzerland with FATF/G20 international recommendations
- **Geneva impact:** All Geneva companies, foundations, trusts with Swiss trustees must register UBOs; privacy implications for high-profile individuals
- **Advisory demand:** UBO registration compliance, corporate transparency advisory, privacy litigation risk assessment for non-compliant entities

#### NEW: CRS 2.0 and CARF — Crypto in Automatic Exchange (January 1, 2026)
- **What:** Switzerland implementing CRS 2.0 and Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework from January 1, 2026
- **Scope:** Financial institutions must apply new tax due diligence and reporting obligations for crypto-assets; automatic exchange of information expanded to crypto holdings
- **Geneva impact:** Geneva private banks, asset managers, crypto institutions must adapt AML/KYC processes to CARF requirements
- **Advisory demand:** CARF compliance framework, crypto tax reporting legal obligations, cross-border information exchange compliance

#### NEW: Swiss-EU Trade Agreement 2025 + Bern Financial Services Agreement (UK)
- **What:** New Swiss-EU trade agreement consolidated trade relations 2025; Bern Financial Services Agreement (BFSA) with UK entering force January 1, 2026
- **BFSA:** Mutual recognition regime facilitating cross-border financial services between Switzerland and UK
- **Geneva impact:** Increased M&A valuations for Swiss targets 2026+; cross-border financial services advisory demand for UK-Switzerland corridors
- **Advisory demand:** Cross-border financial services regulatory advisory, UK-Switzerland transaction support

#### NEW: TEF Rules July 2025 — Trust/Estate/Foundation Dispute Resolution
- **What:** Swiss Arbitration Centre Supplemental Swiss Rules for Trust, Estate and Foundation Disputes (TEF Rules) in force July 2025
- **Scope:** Specialized arbitration rules for private wealth disputes including trust validity, trustee liability, estate distribution, foundation governance
- **Geneva impact:** Geneva as seat for international trust/estate disputes strengthening institutionally; Swiss Arbitration Summit 2026 Innovation Conference dedicated to TEF Rules
- **Advisory demand:** International estate planning with Geneva arbitration clause, trust litigation strategy, cross-border succession advisory with TEF arbitration option

### C113 Regulatory Context Updates (2026-04-04)
- **Swiss Federal Council AI Outline (Feb 2026):** Lean regulatory approach — no standalone AI statute; targeted amendments to FADP, ISA, CO, CC, UCA, CopA expected by end-2026
- **Council of Europe AI Convention (signed March 2025):** Ratification planned; consultation draft for Swiss legislative amendments expected end-2026; transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight obligations
- **FDPIC AI Guidance (May 2025):** FADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing; Geneva law firms as data controllers must ensure AI tool compliance with FADP
- **Product Liability Act Revision:** PrLA revisions aligning with EU Product Liability Directive; covers AI-enabled products; both supplier and user liability
- **ISA Cybersecurity Reporting:** Information Security Act creates incident reporting duties for AI deployments in critical sectors; financial sector and law firms advising clients on ISA compliance
