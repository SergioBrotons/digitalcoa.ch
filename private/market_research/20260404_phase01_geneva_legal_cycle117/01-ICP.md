# 01 — ICP: Geneva Large/ELM Legal Firms

## Target Segment: Large Law & Enterprise Legal Management (ELM)

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** 50+ attorneys, typically 100-500+
- **Revenue:** CHF 50M–500M+ annual
- **Locations:** Geneva primary, Zurich secondary, possible Swiss romande presence
- **Practice Areas:** Corporate/M&A, banking & finance, litigation, IP, tax (full-service or specialized)
- **Status:** Often Swiss member of international networks (Allen & Overy, Latham, Baker McKenzie affiliates)

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| General Counsel / Managing Partner | C-suite or senior equity partner | Portfolio visibility, cost containment, outside counsel management |
| COO / Finance Director | Non-lawyer executive | Invoice validation, matter budgeting, e-billing compliance |
| IT / Knowledge Management | Senior IT manager or CIO | Document management, IP protection, AI tool integration |
| Practice Group Leaders | Senior partners | Matter profitability, staffing efficiency, client reporting |

### Known Geneva Examples
- Lenz & Staehelin (Geneva HQ, 300+ lawyers)
- Pestalozzi Attorneys at Law (Geneva, international network)
- Vischer AG (Geneva/Zurich, 80+ lawyers)
- Baker McKenzie (Geneva affiliate)
- Walder Wyss (Geneva office, 100+ lawyers)
- Nokia, Nestlé, Roche in-house teams (potential ELM buyers)

### Pain Points (C117 Updated)
1. **Outside counsel cost opacity** — invoice auditing is manual, error-prone
2. **Matter overflow** — spikes in M&A or litigation create staffing gaps
3. **Regulatory change management** — Basel IV, BVG, AML, CRS 2.0/CARF creating sustained legal workload
4. **AI governance liability** — lawyers fully liable for AI errors; need AI governance frameworks
5. **Geneva partner succession crisis** — Baby Boomer retirement wave 2025-2026; client relationship retention risk
6. **In-house legal AI reducing external spend** — routine work compressed; firms need clear value differentiation
7. **Swiss sanctions compliance** — SECO enforcement intensifying; law firms as trusted compliance intermediaries
8. **Collective redress mechanism** — new representative actions framework (Jan 2026); courts preparing; advisory need emerging

### Switzerland as Trust/Estate/Foundation Arbitration Seat
- TEF Rules live July 2025 (Swiss Arbitration Centre)
- ICC record caseload 2025: 894 new cases, US$299B pending
- Geneva's neutrality + legal tradition + SFT efficiency = preferred seat
- Geopolitical instability fueling cross-border dispute demand

### ICP Signal Indicators
- Firms hiring legal operations staff
- Announcing AI tool pilots (CaseFox, LawVu, Legal Suite, Acuity)
- Facing Basel IV, BVG reform, CRS 2.0 compliance deadlines
- Partner succession planning announcements
- SECO/sanctions advisory demand signals
- Geneva government administrative reform = cantonal enforcement capacity increase
