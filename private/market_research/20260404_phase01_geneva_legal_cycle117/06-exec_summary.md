# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 01 Geneva Legal (C117)

## Research Question
What is the addressable market for enterprise legal management (ELM) advisory targeting large Geneva law firms and in-house legal departments at financial institutions?

## C117 Key Findings

### Finding 1: Swiss Collective Redress Mechanism Creates New Advisory Territory (NET-NEW C117)
- Swiss Parliament passed Civil Procedure Code amendments introducing representative actions (consumer protection + environmental claims)
- Effective January 2026; Geneva courts preparing procedural framework; first cases expected 2026
- Advisory need: standing, notice procedure, defense strategy for representative claims
- Geneva law firms must advise corporate clients on new class-action-adjacent exposure
- **Implication:** Geneva commercial litigation practices need advisory support on representative action defense

### Finding 2: Geneva Government Legal Services Restructuring — Cantonal Enforcement Expanding (NET-NEW C117)
- Canton of Geneva undergoing administrative reform of legal services function (2025-2026)
- Consolidation of in-house cantonal legal counsel across departments
- Signals increased cantonal enforcement capacity expected 2026
- Geneva administrative law practitioners benefit from advisory demand around cantonal administrative procedures
- **Implication:** New institutional client segment for Geneva administrative law advisory

### Finding 3: EU-Swiss Institutional Framework — Ongoing Legal Uncertainty (NET-NEW C117)
- InstA negotiations resumed mid-2025; unresolved market access questions creating legal uncertainty
- Geneva firms operating in EU face contingency planning demand
- Law firms advising on no-deal scenario and risk mitigation
- **Implication:** Cross-border EU-Swiss legal advisory remains active need; contingency planning service line

### Finding 4: Geneva Private Wealth Asset Disputes — Art, Wine, Yacht (NET-NEW C117)
- UHNW clients in Geneva increasingly involved in cross-border asset disputes (art seizure, wine cellar ownership, yacht arrest)
- Geneva courts handling international art law cases; advisory opportunity in art law and asset recovery
- Relatively underserved niche in Geneva legal market
- **Implication:** Specialist private wealth litigation advisory opportunity in Geneva

### Finding 5: Swiss Sanctions Enforcement — Lawyers as Compliance Gatekeepers (NET-NEW C117)
- SECO sanctions enforcement intensifying; Geneva law firms advising commodity traders, family offices, private banks
- Russia-related sanctions navigation + compliance advisory = new client category
- Mid-size firms and family offices needing sanctions training = demand signal
- Geneva lawyers as trusted intermediaries in SECO compliance chain
- **Implication:** Sanctions compliance advisory as growing service line for Geneva law firms

### Finding 6: Digital Evidence and E-Discovery Advisory Gap in Switzerland (NET-NEW C117)
- Swiss Civil Procedure Code digital evidence rules evolving; Geneva courts dealing with ESI
- No comprehensive e-discovery framework in Switzerland = risk management + advisory opportunity
- Law firms advising on digital evidence preservation, legal hold, data forensics
- **Implication:** E-discovery and digital evidence advisory as emerging service line
