## ⭐ NET-NEW C63 Findings (vs. C62)

### 1. FDPIC September 24, 2025 Update — Cross-Sectoral Consulting Implications (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** edoeb.admin.ch/en/ai-and-data-protection (September 24, 2025)
- **Consulting-specific:** FDPIC sharpened AI face/image/voice manipulation obligations — consulting firms building AI-powered client tools (presentations, reports, content) must clearly indicate AI-generated content
- Criminal law dimension: AI-generated content that manipulates faces/images/voices "may be completely unlawful in a specific case" — new risk surface for consulting deliverables
- DPIA required for high-risk AI-supported processing — consulting firms processing client data through AI tools trigger this obligation

### 2. Swiss-EU Bilateral Package — ABSENT from April 1 Federal Council (Structural Confirmation)
- **Source:** Federal Council April 1, 2026 (6 agenda items — bilateral package not present)
- **Confirmed:** Multi-framework burden is structural baseline — consulting firms face FADP + EU AI Act GPAI + sector-specific obligations simultaneously
- **Consulting implication:** Consulting firms advising clients on multi-jurisdiction AI compliance face the same complexity they must help clients navigate

### 3. Section 301 Forced Labour — NEW SMB Consulting Product Opportunity
- **Source:** Federal Council April 1, 2026 (admin.ch) — Section 301 Investigation B forced labour
- **New product surface:** Supply chain ESG forced labour documentation — Swiss SMBs with international supply chains need compliance documentation
- **Consulting opportunity:** SMB supply chain forced labour compliance gap = consulting advisory product for Geneva/Swiss SMBs

### 4. Federal Council "No Swiss AI Act" — Sector-Specific Approach Creates Consulting Differentiation
- **Source:** Homburger.ch (confirmed in C60-C62 research)
- **Consulting opportunity:** Federal Council sector-specific approach = consulting frameworks calibrated to specific sectors become more valuable
- **Geneva consulting angle:** International organizations, NGOs, diplomatic community = unique client base with cross-border AI governance needs

### 5. Swiss SMB AI Adoption Gap — Labor Shortage Context
- **Source:** Prior cycles (KOF data ~400,000 labor shortage)
- **Consulting angle:** Swiss labor shortage = primary driver for SMB AI adoption; consulting firms positioned to advise on AI-driven productivity
- **GPAI self-assessment gap:** Consulting firms themselves need GPAI readiness before advising clients — creates two-track offering
