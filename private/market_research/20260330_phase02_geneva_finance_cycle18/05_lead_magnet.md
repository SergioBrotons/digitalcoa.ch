## Section 4: CARF — Live Since January 1, 2026

**The regulation that boutique Geneva WMs may not have addressed yet.**

CARF (Common Reporting Standard with AEOI) is not new for 2026 — the new version (CRS 2.0/CARF) became operative on January 1, 2026. As of March 30, 2026, this obligation has been live for 3 months.

**Your obligations under CARF/CRS 2.0:**
- Due diligence procedures for new accounts
- Reporting of financial account information
- Documentation of account holder identity and tax status
- Retention of documentation

**AI intersection:** AI tools that process financial account data for reporting purposes must be included in your CARF compliance documentation.

## Section 5: AMLA/LETA H2 2026 — What Changes for Wealth Managers

**The 30-day discrepancy reporting obligation is the immediate priority.**

**AMLA revision key changes:**
- 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register (mandatory)
- Advisor scope expansion: lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs must join FINMA-recognized SRO
- Enhanced customer due diligence for high-risk clients
- Beneficial ownership transparency

**LETA (Transparency Act):**
- Transparency Register for beneficial ownership
- 30-day discrepancy reporting obligation
- Linked to AMLA revision timeline

**AI intersection:** AI transaction monitoring systems must align with AMLA/LETA reporting obligations. Discrepancy detection = AI system validation requirement.
