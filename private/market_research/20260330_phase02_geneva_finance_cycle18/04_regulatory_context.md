## Regulatory Framework for Geneva Boutique Wealth Managers

### Active Regulations (Now)

#### 1. CARF/CRS 2.0 — LIVE SINCE JAN 1, 2026
- **Status:** Operative since Jan 1, 2026 (~3 months live)
- **Application:** All FINMA-licensed institutions and SRO members in Switzerland
- **Obligation:** Common Reporting Standard new version — automatic exchange of financial account information
- **Urgency:** Already in effect — compliance gap if not addressed
- **Confidence:** High

#### 2. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — Current Baseline
- **Status:** Current (no 2026 update published)
- **Application:** FINMA-supervised institutions
- **AI relevance:** AI systems in wealth management subject to existing FINMA guidance on technology risk
- **Gap:** No specific FINMA AI circular for wealth managers published 2025–2026
- **Confidence:** High

#### 3. AMLA (Anti-Money Laundering Act) Revision — H2 2026
- **Status:** Consultation machinery active (concluded Jan 30, 2026)
- **Timeline:** H2 2026 implementation (~3–6 months)
- **Key obligation:** 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register
- **Advisor scope expansion:** Lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs must join FINMA-recognized SRO
- **Confidence:** High

#### 4. LETA (Transparency Act) — H2 2026
- **Status:** Linked to AMLA revision; H2 2026 implementation
- **Key obligation:** Transparency Register for beneficial ownership
- **30-day discrepancy reporting:** Confirmed
- **Confidence:** High

#### 5. FATF Mutual Evaluation 2026–2027
- **Status:** Ongoing preparation
- **Application:** Switzerland subject to FATF mutual evaluation
- **Impact:** International AML/CFT compliance standard review; Geneva WMs with international clients face heightened scrutiny
- **Confidence:** High

### Pending Regulations

#### 6. EU AI Act — High-Risk Obligations Aug 2, 2026
- **Status:** Operative Aug 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)
- **Application:** Geneva WMs with EU market nexus
- **Obligation:** High-risk AI system compliance if EU AI Act applies
- **Confidence:** High
