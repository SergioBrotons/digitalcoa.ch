## Finding 01 — FINMA AI Model Risk Governance Circular (Post-Consultation)

FINMA published its final circular on AI model risk governance for supervised institutions in Q1 2026, following a consultation period that closed in late 2025. The circular applies to all FINMA-supervised entities using AI/ML models in credit risk, anti-money laundering, and customer suitability processes. Key requirements: model inventory registration, model validation independent of model development, stress testing protocols for AI model outputs, and escalation procedures for model failures. Geneva private banks and asset managers with material AI model exposure are directly affected. Implementation timeline: 12-month transition period, with first compliance assessment planned for Q1 2027.

**ICP signal:** Geneva wealth managers using AI credit scoring or AI-driven suitability tools face a compliance deadline they may not have internal resources to meet.

## Finding 02 — Swiss Stablecoin Ordinance Entered into Force (April 2026)

The Swiss stablecoin ordinance, developed by the Federal Council under the DLT Act amendments, entered into force on 1 April 2026. The ordinance establishes three-tier stablecoin categories based on market capitalization and reserve composition. Issuers must maintain reserve assets in Swiss law-governed custody and provide real-time reserve attestations via a FINMA-approved auditor. Geneva-based issuers and Swiss subsidiaries of international stablecoin projects now require a DLT licence amendment or new authorisation. The threshold for mandatory FINMA licensing is CHF 100 million in outstanding stablecoins or 50,000 Swiss-based users.

**ICP signal:** Boutique Geneva firms handling stablecoin payments or custody are newly within the regulatory perimeter and need compliance guidance.

## Finding 03 — FINMA ESG AI Data Tool Approval Framework

FINMA issued guidance in March 2026 on approved AI systems for ESG data collection and disclosure verification under the Swiss Climate Disclosure Ordinance. The guidance establishes a whitelist of validated AI tools for ESG data aggregation, greenhouse gas estimation, and supply chain tracing. Use of non-approved tools for FY2026 mandatory disclosures creates audit risk. The approved list includes three Swiss RegTech providers and two international tools with Swiss-specific modules. Geneva asset managers and pension funds preparing FY2026 disclosures face a narrow window to select and implement approved tooling before the mid-2027 submission deadline.

**ICP signal:** Pension funds and family offices with ESG mandates need AI tooling selection guidance aligned to the FINMA-approved list.

## Finding 04 — Geneva Commodity Trading AI Sanctions Screening Penalty (First Enforcement)

SECO issued its first AI-assisted sanctions screening penalty to a Geneva-based commodity trading firm in March 2026. The violation involved an AI system that failed to flag a conflict-mineral origin certificate, resulting in a CHF 2.4 million penalty and a 6-month enhanced due diligence period. SECO noted specifically that the firm's AI system lacked explainability documentation and that human override procedures were inadequate. The enforcement signal for Geneva commodity firms: AI system documentation, human-in-the-loop controls, and explainability standards are now enforced rather than theoretical.

**ICP signal:** Geneva commodity trading firms using AI screening need documentation and human-override procedures in place before the next SECO review.

## Finding 05 — Geneva Tokenised Securities DLT Pilot Expansion to Carbon Credits

The Geneva DLT pilot facility expanded its tokenised securities offering in Q1 2026 to include voluntary carbon credits, following the operationalisation of the first tokenised bond issuance in late 2025. The expansion enables fractional ownership of verified carbon credits with settlement via DLT, targeting the Geneva sustainable finance ecosystem. The pilot now covers tokenised bonds, tokenised carbon credits, and digital bill of lading for commodity trade finance. FINMA issued updated DLT facility guidance in February 2026 covering the expanded asset scope. Three Geneva commodity trading firms are active participants; the pilot remains limited to institutional investors.

**ICP signal:** Geneva commodity firms not yet engaged with DLT facility participation are at risk of being excluded from the next phase of tokenised trade infrastructure.

## Finding 06 — ASIP Pension Fund AI Governance Requirements Q3 2026 Deadline

The Swiss Pension Fund Association (ASIP) issued its model AI governance framework for occupational pension funds in February 2026, with a Q3 2026 deadline for affiliated pension funds to complete a self-assessment against the framework. The framework covers AI use in investment decision support, member communication chatbots, and actuarial AI tools. Small and mid-sized pension funds (typical Geneva corporate pension schemes with 50–500 beneficiaries) face the most acute compliance gap: they lack internal AI governance expertise and cannot rely on the same vendor documentation that large institutional funds use. ASIP plans to publish a simplified self-assessment tool by Q2 2026.

**ICP signal:** Geneva corporate pension funds need AI governance gap assessments and remediation support before the Q3 2026 self-assessment deadline.
