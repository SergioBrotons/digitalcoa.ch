## Pain Clusters (C126 Fresh Angles)

### Pain 1: WIPO AI-Powered IP Platform Integration
International organizations and Geneva-based IP-intensive companies face decisions about integrating WIPO's emerging AI-powered IP platform tools (WIPO Translate, WIPO Sequence, AI-assisted patent search). Understanding platform capabilities, limitations, and strategic implications requires advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- How do WIPO's AI tools affect our IP strategy and prosecution workflow?
- Should we integrate WIPO AI platforms into our internal IP management systems?
- What are the implications of AI-assisted patent examination for our IP portfolio quality?
- How do WIPO AI tools compare to commercial alternatives for our use case?

### Pain 2: ICRC Operational AI Deployment in Conflict Zones
ICRC is deploying AI systems in operational contexts (image analysis for damage assessment, NLP for document processing, predictive analytics for conflict prediction). Deploying AI in high-stakes humanitarian contexts creates unique ethical, operational, and technical challenges requiring specialist advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- What AI governance framework is appropriate for AI deployed in conflict zone operations?
- How do we ensure AI system reliability and safety when lives depend on outputs?
- What are the FADP/data protection implications of AI processing conflict zone data?
- How do we structure human oversight of AI-assisted humanitarian decisions?

### Pain 3: UN/IO AI Procurement Standards and Compliance
Geneva-based international organizations are developing AI procurement standards and requirements for vendors. Tech companies targeting UN/IO procurement contracts need to understand these standards to position their products effectively.

**Trigger questions:**
- What AI-specific requirements do UN/IO procurement processes impose on vendors?
- How do we structure our product development to meet UN/IO AI procurement standards?
- What certifications or assessments do we need to become a qualified UN/IO AI vendor?
- How do we position our AI product's ethical framework for UN/IO procurement evaluators?

### Pain 4: Geneva Humanitarian Procurement Pathways
Geneva-based tech companies developing humanitarian technology face complex procurement pathways to reach ICRC, UNHCR, and major NGOs. Understanding procurement frameworks, vendor qualification processes, and partnership structures requires specialist advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- What is the ICRC/UNHCR procurement process and how do we qualify as a vendor?
- Are there framework agreements or standing offers we can join?
- How do we structure our offering for humanitarian procurement requirements?
- What partnerships or consortium structures make sense for humanitarian tech companies?

### Pain 5: Geneva Tech Startup Niche — Humanitarian and Climate Tech
Geneva's unique position as the humanitarian capital creates a niche for tech startups focused on humanitarian and climate technology. Positioning, funding, and growth strategies for Geneva-based humanitarian tech startups require understanding of the Geneva ecosystem.

**Trigger questions:**
- How do we position our Geneva humanitarian tech startup for international impact?
- What funding sources are available for Geneva-based humanitarian tech?
- How do we navigate the Geneva international organization ecosystem for partnerships?
- What is the optimal growth path for a Geneva-based humanitarian tech company?

### Pain 6: Geneva Digital Health AI — Swissmedic Regulatory Pathway
Geneva-based digital health AI companies developing clinical decision support or diagnostic AI face Swissmedic regulatory pathways. Understanding Swissmedic requirements, clinical validation standards, and market access strategy requires specialist regulatory advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- What Swissmedic requirements apply to our digital health AI product?
- How do we structure clinical validation studies for Swissmedic submission?
- What is the pathway to CE marking (EU MDR) alongside Swissmedic approval?
- How do we position our digital health AI for Swiss healthcare market access?
