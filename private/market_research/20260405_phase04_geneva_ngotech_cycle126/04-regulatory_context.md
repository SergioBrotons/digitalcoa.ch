### 1. WIPO AI Policy Framework — AI Inventorship and IP Rights

**Authority:** WIPO (World Intellectual Property Organization) + IGE (Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property)
**Governing Law:** PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) + Swiss Patents Act + WIPO AI Policy Guidance
**Status as of 2026:** WIPO published AI inventorship guidance (2024-2025); AI-generated works copyright question unresolved; WIPO serving as global standard-setting body for AI IP policy

**Key Issues:**
- AI inventorship: WIPO guidance requires human inventor for patent protection; pure AI-generated inventions face protection gaps
- AI-assisted inventions: Human-AI collaborative inventions eligible for patent protection with proper disclosure
- AI-generated content: Copyright protection unclear; Swiss Copyright Act being interpreted for AI-generated works
- WIPO AI tool usage: Implications for confidentiality of patent disclosures processed through WIPO AI tools

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva IP-intensive companies (pharma, biotech, tech) using AI in R&D need advisory on AI inventorship disclosure requirements, AI-assisted invention documentation, and IP strategy for AI-generated outputs.

**EN:** "WIPO's AI inventorship guidance creates patent protection complexity for Geneva AI-assisted inventions — requiring clear documentation strategies and IP advisory for AI-augmented R&D."
**FR:** "Les orientations de l'OMPI sur l'invention IA créent une complexité de protection des brevets pour les inventions assistées par IA genevoises — nécessitant des stratégies de documentation claires et un conseil en PI pour la R&D augmentée par IA."

### 2. ICRC AI Ethics Framework — Operational AI in Conflict Zones

**Authority:** ICRC (International Committee of the Red Cross)
**Governing Law:** ICRC Internal AI Ethics Framework + International Humanitarian Law (IHL) principles
**Status as of 2026:** ICRC developing internal AI ethics framework for operational AI deployment; framework being studied by WHO, UNHCR, and other international organizations as model

**Key Principles:**
- Humanity: AI must serve humanitarian purposes; must not harm or endanger affected populations
- Necessity: AI deployment justified only when it meaningfully improves humanitarian response
- Proportionality: AI risks weighed against operational benefits
- Precaution: High-stakes contexts require enhanced caution; AI deployment requires continuous monitoring
- Accountability: Clear human responsibility for AI-assisted decisions
- Transparency: AI systems must be explainable to affected populations and humanitarian workers

**Geneva Relevance:** Advisory opportunity: Help ICRC and similar organizations operationalize AI ethics principles, develop AI governance structures, design human oversight mechanisms, and create AI incident response procedures.

**EN:** "ICRC's emerging AI ethics framework for operational humanitarian AI creates a model for international organizations — advisory on framework operationalization and governance implementation represents a unique engagement opportunity."
**FR:** "Le cadre d'éthique IA émergent du CICR pour l'IA opérationnelle humanitarian crée un modèle pour les organisations internationales — le conseil sur l'opérationnalisation du cadre et la mise en œuvre de la gouvernance représente une opportunité d'engagement unique."

### 3. UN/IO AI Procurement Standards — Emerging Framework

**Authority:** UN Secretariat + individual agencies (WHO, ITU, UNHCR, WFP)
**Governing Law:** UN Financial Regulations and Rules + individual agency procurement policies
**Status as of 2026:** No unified UN system AI procurement standard; individual agencies developing own requirements; WHO AI health technology assessment framework most advanced; ICRC AI procurement guidelines under development

**Key Requirements:**
- AI system documentation: Technical documentation, training data description, bias testing results
- Data protection: AI systems must comply with applicable data protection law (including FADP for Swiss-based IOs)
- Ethical AI certification: Self-certification or third-party audit of AI ethical alignment
- Performance validation: Evidence of AI system performance in relevant contexts
- Vendor responsibility: Clear vendor accountability for AI system performance and incidents

**Geneva Relevance:** Tech companies targeting UN/IO procurement need to understand and align with emerging AI requirements; advisory opportunity in standards alignment and documentation preparation.

**EN:** "Emerging UN/IO AI procurement standards create documentation and compliance requirements for tech vendors — advisory on standards alignment and qualification preparation represents a practical engagement opportunity."
**FR:** "Les normes d'approvisionnement en IA des OI/ONU émergentes créent des exigences de documentation et de conformité pour les fournisseurs tech — le conseil sur l'alignement des normes et la préparation de la qualification représente une opportunité d'engagement pratique."

### 4. Swissmedic SaMD Framework — Digital Health AI Regulation

**Authority:** Swissmedic (Swiss Agency for Therapeutic Products)
**Governing Law:** Therapeutic Products Act (TPA / HMG) + Swissmedic Software as a Medical Device guidance
**Status as of 2026:** Swissmedic published SaMD classification guidance aligned with IMDRF (International Medical Device Regulators Forum) framework; EU MDR alignment ongoing; Digital Health AI companies requiring Swiss market access must navigate SaMD classification

**Key Requirements:**
- SaMD classification: Risk-based classification (Class A, B, C, D) based on significance of information and healthcare situation
- Clinical evaluation: Evidence of clinical performance required for higher-risk SaMD
- Quality management: ISO 13485 quality management system required for manufacturer
- Post-market surveillance: Ongoing performance monitoring and incident reporting
- CE marking: Required for EU market access; Swissmedic accepts certain CE-marked products

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva digital health AI companies developing SaMD products need Swissmedic regulatory strategy, classification assessment, clinical validation planning, and regulatory submission support.

**EN:** "Swissmedic's SaMD framework creates a defined but complex pathway for Geneva digital health AI companies — regulatory strategy and submission advisory represents a high-value specialist engagement."
**FR:** "Le cadre SaMD de Swissmedic crée une voie définie mais complexe pour les entreprises d'IA numérique de santé genevoises — le conseil en stratégie réglementaire et en soumission représente un engagement spécialisé de haute valeur."

### 5. FADP — AI Processing in Humanitarian/IO Contexts

**Authority:** FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)
**Governing Law:** Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nLPD)
**Status as of 2026:** FADP in force; FDPIC published guidance on AI and data protection; AI systems processing personal data subject to FADP obligations

**Key Requirements:**
- Lawful basis: AI processing of personal data requires legal basis (consent, legitimate interest, contract)
- Transparency: Data subjects informed when AI is used to process their data
- Data quality: AI outputs must be based on accurate, complete, current data
- Automated decision-making: Profiling and automated decisions subject to transparency and explanation rights
- Cross-border transfers: ICRC/UNHCR data transfers subject to FADP adequacy and transfer mechanism requirements

**Geneva Relevance:** ICRC and UN agencies with Swiss presence processing personal data via AI systems face FADP compliance obligations; Geneva tech companies developing AI systems for humanitarian use must embed FADP compliance in design.

**EN:** "FADP AI provisions apply to AI systems processing personal data by Geneva IOs and humanitarian organizations — creating AI governance requirements that intersect with ICRC AI ethics frameworks."
**FR:** "Les dispositions IA de la LPD s'appliquent aux systèmes d'IA traitant des données personnelles par les OI et organisations humanitaires genevoises — créant des exigences de gouvernance IA qui intersectent avec les cadres d'éthique IA du CICR."

### 6. International Humanitarian Law — AI Weapons and Autonomous Systems

**Authority:** UN (through various bodies) + Geneva Convention framework
**Governing Law:** Geneva Conventions + Additional Protocols + CCW (Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons)
**Status as of 2026:** International discussions ongoing on autonomous weapons systems; no binding international agreement yet; ICRC advocating for human control over weapons

**Key Issues:**
- Human control: IHL requires meaningful human control over weapons and force decisions
- Autonomous AI weapons: Technically capable but legally and ethically problematic under IHL (principle of distinction, proportionality)
- ICRC position: Human control over weapon use essential; autonomous weapons systems should be prohibited
- Geneva diplomatic process: CCW discussions ongoing in Geneva; Switzerland active participant

**Geneva Relevance:** This is a longer-term strategic context issue for Geneva tech companies considering defense/hybrid technology applications. Not an immediate advisory opportunity, but awareness important for positioning.

**EN:** "International humanitarian law discussions on autonomous weapons systems are ongoing in Geneva — this longer-term context shapes the ethical framework within which Geneva AI companies should position their products."
**FR:** "Les discussions du droit humanitaire international sur les systèmes d'armes autonomes se poursuivent à Genève — ce contexte à plus long terme façonne le cadre éthique dans lequel les entreprises d'IA genevoises devraient positionner leurs produits."
