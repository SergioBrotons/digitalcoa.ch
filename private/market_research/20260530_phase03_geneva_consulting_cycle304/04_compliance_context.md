# Compliance Context: Geneva Consulting — C304 Phase 03

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape

### Primary Regulatory Framework

**nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)**
- In force September 1, 2023
- Art.63 criminal liability for unauthorized processing
- Personal liability for managing directors — corporate structure does not protect
- DPA requirements for AI tool vendors

**Swiss Code of Obligations (OR)**
- Art.2: Contract ambiguity interpreted against drafting party
- Engagement letters silent on AI create ambiguity risk for consulting firms
- Contract law foundations at risk in AI context

**FINMA Guidance 08/2024**
- Financial sector AI governance expectations
- Institutional clients sending AI questionnaires to consulting firms serving them
- Downstream pressure on consulting firms from FINMA-regulated clients

**Carrier Attestation**
- Carrier forms evolving to ask for specific methodology documentation
- Personal signature required
- "Adequate AI governance" undefined for consulting methodology

---

## The Compliance Gap

The regulations exist. The consulting-specific implementation does not.

Key gaps:
- Engagement letter AI clauses — no Geneva-specific template
- Methodology documentation for AI use — no proportionate standard
- Sub-consultant delegation framework — no accountability standard
- Client questionnaire response methodology — no structured approach
- Personal liability under Art.63 — no specific protection framework for managing directors

---

## The Personal Liability Layer

The managing director of a consulting firm is personally liable for:
- nFADP Art.63 violations (criminal, personal)
- Client contract representations (including AI-related)
- Sub-consultant delegation (accountable for their work)
- Carrier attestation signed (personal representation)

The corporate structure protects the firm's assets, not the managing director's personal assets or professional license.
