# Market Signals: Geneva Consulting — C304 Phase 03

## Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures

### Primary Market Signals

**1. Client AI Governance Questionnaires as Primary Forcing Function**
Clients are sending AI governance questionnaires to consulting firms as part of RFP processes and ongoing compliance. The consulting firm that cannot respond loses the client or loses the new mandate. The firm that responds incompletely creates documented exposure. Neither option is safe.

**2. Proposal Methodology AI Documentation Gap**
Consulting proposals use "AI-assisted analysis" as a descriptive phrase without defining what it means. No documentation exists for: what AI tools were used, what data was input, what methodology was applied, what the AI output was, how the output was evaluated. The proposal is silent on the methodology's AI component.

**3. Engagement Letter AI Silence**
Engagement letters (lettres de mission) have no AI clauses, no data processing framework, no liability allocation for AI-assisted work. The contract that governs the entire engagement is silent on the most significant operational risk the firm faces. Under Swiss law (OR Art. 2), ambiguity in the contract is interpreted against the drafting party — the consulting firm bears the ambiguity risk.

**4. Sub-Consultant Delegation Chain Undocumented**
The chain from managing director → sub-consultant → AI tool is open and undocumented. The managing director is accountable for the sub-consultant's work but has no visibility into what AI tools are being used, how, and with what data. The delegation chain creates specific exposure that cannot be attributed to any individual decision.

**5. nFADP Art.63 Personal Liability Active**
nFADP Art.63 creates personal criminal liability for unauthorized processing of data. The managing director's personal assets and professional license are at risk, not just the firm's corporate liability. The corporate structure does not protect the individual.

**6. Carrier Attestation Without Methodology**
The carrier attestation for consulting firms has evolved to ask for specific governance documentation. The managing director signed without having methodology documentation to support the attestation. The next carrier renewal will ask for more specific evidence.

## Failure Modes

**Client challenge failure:** Client questions AI use in a deliverable. Firm cannot document what AI tools were used, what data was processed, what methodology was applied. Client relationship damaged, exposure documented.

**RFP failure:** Client sends AI governance questionnaire as part of RFP. Firm cannot respond adequately. Not selected for the mandate. Market signal sent to peers.

**nFADP Art.63 failure:** Data processing during a consulting engagement violates nFADP. Personal liability for managing director. Corporate structure provides no protection.

**Sub-consultant failure:** Sub-consultant uses AI in client work without managing director's knowledge. Client harmed. Managing director liable for sub-consultant's AI use. No documentation of the delegation or its limits.
