# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting — C304 Phase 03

## How Small Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems

### The Geneva Consulting ICP

Small consulting firm, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable. AI tools deployed in client engagements. Client AI governance questionnaires arriving. Proposal methodology must be defensible. Sub-consultant delegation creates additional exposure. Personal liability under nFADP Art.63.

The core problem: methodology is the product. If AI use undermines methodology defensibility, the consulting firm's value proposition collapses.

---

## ICP Language Patterns

### The Client Questionnaire Trigger
- "A client sent us an AI governance questionnaire. We didn't know how to answer."
- "We've never documented how we use AI in our proposals."
- "The client's compliance team is asking questions we can't answer."
- "If we can't answer, we lose the client. If we answer wrong, we're exposed."

### The Methodology Defensibility Problem
- "Our methodology is what clients pay for. If AI is part of it, how do we document it?"
- "If a client challenges our work and AI was involved, can we defend it?"
- "The proposal says 'AI-assisted analysis.' What does that actually mean?"
- "We don't have a consistent way to describe AI use in our deliverables."

### The Sub-Consultant Delegation Gap
- "We hire sub-consultants who use AI tools. We're responsible for their work."
- "We have no visibility into what AI tools they're using."
- "The chain of accountability is completely unclear."
- "If a sub-consultant's AI use causes a problem, we're exposed."

### The Carrier Attestation Problem
- "The carrier asked about AI governance at renewal. I signed something."
- "I didn't have a methodology to point to. The form was very specific."
- "I don't know what I signed or what evidence I need."
- "The next version will be worse."

### The Engagement Letter Silence
- "Our engagement letters don't mention AI at all."
- "We have no AI clauses in our contracts."
- "If a client asks how we used AI in their project, what do we say?"
- "The contract is silent on the most significant operational risk we face."

### The Personal Liability Awareness
- "nFADP Art.63 is personal liability. Not corporate. Personal."
- "If something goes wrong, it's my license, my assets, my personal exposure."
- "The consulting firm structure doesn't protect me."
- "I signed something personally. I don't think my corporate structure helps."

## French ICP Language

### Le déclencheur du questionnaire client
- "Un client nous a envoyé un questionnaire de gouvernance IA. Nous ne savions pas comment répondre."
- "Nous n'avons jamais documenté comment nous utilisons l'IA dans nos propositions."
- "L'équipe de conformité du client pose des questions auxquelles nous ne pouvons pas répondre."

### Le problème de défensibilité de la méthodologie
- "Notre méthodologie est ce que les clients paient. Si l'IA fait partie, comment documenter ?"
- "Si un client conteste notre travail et que l'IA était impliquée, pouvons-nous le défendre ?"

### L'écart de délégation au sous-consultant
- "Nous embauchons des sous-consultants qui utilisent des outils IA. Nous sommes responsables de leur travail."
- "Nous n'avons aucune visibilité sur les outils IA qu'ils utilisent."

### Le problème de l'attestation d'assureur
- "L'assureur a demandé la gouvernance IA lors du renouvellement. J'ai signé quelque chose."
- "Je n'avais pas de méthodologie à montrer. Le formulaire était très spécifique."

### Le silence de la lettre de mission
- "Nos lettres de mission ne mentionnent pas l'IA du tout."
- "Nous n'avons pas de clauses IA dans nos contrats."

### La conscience de la responsabilité personnelle
- "L'art. 63 LPD est une responsabilité personnelle. Pas corporative. Personnelle."
- "Si quelque chose tourne mal, c'est ma licence, mes actifs, mon exposition personnelle."

## The Emotional State

The Geneva consulting ICP is not abstractly worried about AI. They are worried about:
- Losing a client because they can't answer an AI governance questionnaire
- Their methodology being challenged and found unsupported
- A sub-consultant using AI in a way that exposes them
- Personal liability under nFADP Art.63 for something they didn't even know was a risk

The emotional driver is professional survival — not regulatory compliance. They want to keep clients, protect methodology, avoid personal liability, and not lose their reputation.

The service that names their specific situation in their own language gets the call.
