## Primary Problem Framing
**The question isn't whether your law firm is using AI. It's whether you can defend your AI use to a client, a regulator, or a court.**

Swiss law firms are using AI at nearly universal rates (70%). Most individual lawyers have adopted AI tools. But formal firm-level governance? The gap between individual AI use (70%) and formal firm AI policies (34%) is 36 percentage points — and that gap is your liability exposure.

The Federal Act on Data Protection has been operative since September 1, 2023. There is no grace period. The FDPIC has intensified enforcement across three sectors since early 2025 (X/Twitter, Coop, a Swiss transport company). Law firms have not yet been targeted — but they are explicitly in scope.

And the three timelines are converging:
- **Now**: FADP obligations already operative
- **May 2026**: digitalswitzerland AI Action Plan First Draft (external pressure)
- **End 2026**: Federal Council formal legislation (CoE AI Convention implementation)

## FADP Compliance Stack
- **Art. 5(c)**: Lawfulness of data processing — AI processing must have legal basis
- **Art. 7**: Privacy by Design and Default — built into AI system from start
- **Art. 21**: Right to human review of automated decisions producing legal effects
- **Art. 22**: DPIA mandatory for high-risk AI (personality/fundamental rights impact)
- **Art. 26**: Transparency — must disclose AI use; right to know if data used for training
- **Art. 27**: Data breach notification (AI security incidents)

## Shadow AI Audit — Decision 0 (Primary Entry Point)
Six questions every small law firm needs to answer:
1. Which AI tools are your lawyers using today? (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, CASUS, others?)
2. Are AI outputs going into client deliverables — memos, contracts, opinions, research?
3. Is client confidential data being entered into AI tools?
4. Are AI tools being used for due diligence, contract analysis, or litigation research?
5. Do your engagement letters address AI use?
6. Do your malpractice or cyber liability policies cover AI errors?
