## Regulatory Framework (Geneva Consulting)

### Primary: GPAI (EU AI Act — Geneva Nexus)
- **GPAI (General Purpose AI)** operative since **August 2, 2025** (~8.5 months already)
- Transparency obligations: AI system documentation, technical documentation, EU AI Act compliance
- **GPAI obligations are NOT limited to EU-based firms** — any firm with EU clients or EU-nexus operations
- Geneva consulting firms with EU-based clients likely in scope
- GPAI high-risk: AI systems used in employment decisions, creditworthiness, essential services

### Secondary: nFADP Framework
- **FADP Art. 5(c)** active since Sep 1, 2023 — transparency obligations for AI
- FADP applies to AI-supported data processing without new legislation
- FDPIC expects **purpose + functionality + data sources** disclosure
- Criminal liability: CHF 250,000 for intentional FADP failures

### Tertiary: Federal Council AI Action Plan
- **Federal Council "lean approach"** confirmed (no cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026)
- **Zero Draft** AI Action Plan confirmed January 2026
- **First Draft May 2026 ~8 WEEKS** — next external pressure event
- **FADP consultation draft end-2026** — CoE AI Convention implementation

### Cross-Phase Regulatory Context
- **Swissdamed July 1 ~13 WEEKS** — healthcare spillover
- **EU AI Act Aug 2, 2026** (~19 WEEKS) — high-risk AI provisions
- **Geneva AI Action Summit 2027** — Geneva hub positioning

## Dual Compliance Challenge (Consulting)

Consulting firms with EU nexus face dual compliance:
1. **GPAI obligations** (EU AI Act — transparency, documentation)
2. **nFADP obligations** (Swiss law — transparency, Privacy by Design, DPIA)

This dual compliance challenge is a complexity barrier for small firms AND a differentiation opportunity for advisory.
