## The Practice's Own Compliance Obligations

### Own AI Governance Documentation — The Credibility Infrastructure

**The specific obligation:** The practice advises clients on AI accountability while the ICP is evaluating whether to trust that advice. Any gap between the practice's stated expertise and its own documented accountability architecture creates a credibility asymmetry that collapses the advisory relationship.

**Why this is a compliance issue for the practice itself:** The practice's own AI tool usage (any GenAI assistant used for briefing preparation, client communication, or research), its own DPA status under nFADP, and its own human authorization documentation for AI-assisted advisory conclusions must be documented — not because the practice is subject to carrier attestation, but because prospective clients will evaluate the practice's own governance before engaging.

**The specific documentation the practice needs:**
- AI tool inventory: what tools, what data enters them, what processing occurs
- DPA status: is the practice a controller or processor, which tools are under DPA, what is the lawful basis
- Human authorization records: evidence that a human reviewed and authorized AI-assisted advisory conclusions before delivery
- Security and access controls: AI tool access controls, retention policies, access logs

**The regulatory driver:** FDPIC May 2025 enforcement posture shifted from guidance-first to enforcement-live. The existing nFADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data. The practice should not assume it is in a different compliance category than its clients.

**Why it matters to the practice's competitive position:** The ICP who asks "how do you govern your own AI?" before engaging is not an edge case. The practice's own governance completeness is the primary credibility instrument. A competitor who points to the practice's own undocumented AI surface as evidence of incomplete expertise wins the competitive comparison.

### nFADP Applicability to the Practice's Advisory Content

**The specific obligation:** The practice generates advisory content containing client-specific analytical conclusions. If those conclusions constitute personal data under nFADP (e.g., conclusions about a client's specific compliance exposure), the practice must assess whether its advisory delivery involves data processing subject to nFADP.

**Why it is a compliance issue for the practice itself:** nFADP applies to processing of personal data by controllers established in Switzerland or targeting persons in Switzerland. Advisory conclusions about named clients are processing of personal data. The practice should have a ROPA (Record of Processing Activities) entry for its advisory delivery service, even if the data subjects are B2B clients.

**The specific documentation the practice needs:**
- ROPA entry identifying advisory content generation as a processing activity
- Lawful basis assessment for any AI-assisted content generation involving client data
- Data processing agreements with any AI tool vendors used in advisory delivery

---

### Professional Liability and D&O Coverage for the Practice's Own Advisory Work

**The specific obligation:** The practice's work is personal liability advisory — the practice advises professionals on their personal exposure. The practice itself should assess whether its D&O and professional liability policies explicitly cover AI governance advisory services, or whether there is a coverage gap between "general advisory" and "AI governance advisory specifically."

**Why it is a compliance issue for the practice itself:** The practice advises clients to audit their own insurance coverage for AI governance exposure. The practice should apply the same discipline to its own coverage. If the practice is sued for negligent AI governance advice, the policy should confirm coverage before the claim arrives.

**The specific documentation the practice needs:**
- Written confirmation from the practice's PI/D&O insurer on whether AI governance advisory falls within policy coverage
- Confirmation that AI-related claims are covered under current policy language
- Assessment of any coverage gap between general advisory coverage and specific AI accountability governance advisory

## Cross-Vertical Compliance Themes Affecting the Practice

### Theme 01 — Dual-Track Documentation Architecture Requirement

**The finding:** Every professional in every vertical simultaneously faces two documentation obligations: administrative compliance evidence (carrier attestation, institutional questionnaire, nFADP ROPA) and criminal defense architecture (Art.63 nFADP criminal liability, professional standards personal liability, physician personal liability through FMH). These two documentation tracks require different architectures, different evidence standards, and different timing.

**Why it matters for the practice's service design:** The practice cannot serve both tracks with one documentation system. The service should name the dual-track requirement explicitly: clients face two documentation obligations with different architectural requirements, and the practice's methodology addresses both simultaneously.

**Naming for positioning use:** "Your carrier's form asks you to prove adequate governance. Your personal liability exposure asks you to prove something different — in a different venue, under a different standard. Most documentation only addresses the first."

---

### Theme 02 — Documentation Window Irreversibility as Regulatory Timing Mechanism

**The finding:** The documentation window is a present-tense irreversible positioning mechanism, not a future compliance deadline. Decisions made without contemporaneous documentation cannot be retroactively documented with credible evidence of contemporaneous reasoning. The window's closure creates irreversible competitive disadvantage for firms that deferred.

**Why it matters for the practice's service design:** The urgency is not "you need to comply with future regulation." The urgency is "decisions you've already made without documentation are accumulating, and the window for fixing them is still open for present decisions but not past ones." The service framing should be: "The documentation window is still open for your next decision. It is not open for decisions that have already happened."

---

### Theme 03 — Carrier Iteration Velocity Raising Evidence Standards

**The finding:** Carrier AI governance instruments are in their third or fourth generation. Each iteration probes more precisely at the same question: evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions, documented at the moment of decision. The evidence standard is rising each quarter. A firm measured against today's carrier form will be measured against a higher standard in six months.

**Why it matters for the practice's service design:** The practice's methodology must be forward-compatible with carrier iteration evolution. The documentation architecture produced should be designed with the trajectory of carrier question evolution in mind — not just today's form. The service should position itself as calibrated to the evolving standard, not just the current snapshot.

---

### Theme 04 — Formation Participation Generating Pre-Publication Intelligence

**The finding:** The standards that will govern Geneva accountability are forming before they are published. The practice's participation in Bern deliberation, FINMA/SRO consultation processes, and Geneva institutional ecosystem generates intelligence about what standards will require — before they arrive in published form.

**Why it matters for the practice's service design:** The practice's pre-publication intelligence is the primary competitive advantage in client conversations. The practice can advise clients on what the standard will require before the standard is published — which means clients who engage the practice are positioned with formed standards, not arrived standards.

**Naming for positioning use:** "I don't just read the FINMA guidance. I was in the room when the proportionality question was discussed. That's why I know what 'proportionate' actually means for a 60-person Geneva wealth manager."
