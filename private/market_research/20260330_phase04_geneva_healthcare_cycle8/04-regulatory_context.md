# Phase 04 Regulatory Context — Geneva Healthcare / Small Medical Practices
## Cycle 8 (2026-03-30)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

### 1. Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Registry)
- **Date:** July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks) for high-risk SaMD; December 31, 2026 for medium-risk SaMD
- **Nature:** All medical devices including SaMD (Software as a Medical Device) must be registered with Swissmedic via Swissdamed
- **CRITICAL — NO GRACE PERIOD for high-risk SaMD (Cycle 7 carry-forward):** Swissmedic confirmed: SaMD with serious incident reporting obligations have NO transitional period. Registration was required from day one.
- **High-risk SaMD tools requiring immediate July 1 action:**
  - AI diagnostic imaging systems
  - AI symptom triage tools
- **Medium-risk SaMD (grace period to Dec 31, 2026):** AI scheduling/communications, AI EMR documentation/scribes
- **Boutique practice relevance:** Small practices using any AI tool that qualifies as a medical device must register

### 2. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection)
- **Status:** Operative since September 1, 2023 (~2.5 years)
- **Nature:** Patient data = sensitive personal data; AI processing = Art. 5(c); DPIA required under Art. 22
- **Boutique practice relevance:** Any AI tool processing patient records requires nFADP compliance

### 3. FDPIC Enforcement
- **Timeline:** Healthcare = priority enforcement sector (2024/2025 Annual Report confirmed)
- **Nature:** FDPIC is building enforcement portfolio; healthcare is named priority
- **Boutique practice relevance:** Enforcement cases expected 2026-2027; practices should not assume small size provides protection

### 4. AI for Good Global Summit (Cross-Phase)
- **Date:** July 7–10, 2026 (~13 weeks), Palexpo Geneva
- **Preceded by:** Inaugural UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance, July 6–7, Palexpo Geneva
- **Boutique practice relevance:** Geneva is global AI governance capital for a full week; natural conversation anchor post-Swissdamed deadline
