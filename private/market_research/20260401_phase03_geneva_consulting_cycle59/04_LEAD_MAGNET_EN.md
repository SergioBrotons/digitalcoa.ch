## What 15 Hours Per Week Per Consultant Actually Means

Early GenAI pilots in consulting and financial services show that automation of administrative, research, and reporting tasks can free up 12–18 hours per consultant per week. For a small firm of five consultants, that's 60–90 hours of recovered capacity weekly — the equivalent of 1.5 to 2 additional billable consultants.

What does that buy?
- More client facetime per engagement
- Faster turnaround on deliverables
- Higher-quality analysis (AI-assisted research + human judgment)
- More competitive pricing without margin compression

The productivity case is straightforward. The question is how to capture it without creating new exposure.

## The Three Questions Before You Adopt Any AI Tool

**1. What client data will this touch?**
Any tool that processes, stores, or analyzes client work product creates a data governance obligation. If the tool is US-hosted, you're adding FADP exposure and FINMA outsourcing risk on top of whatever value it provides.

**2. Can I explain the output to a client or regulator?**
The Federal AI Ordinance requires human oversight of AI-assisted decisions. If you can't explain how an AI tool reached its output — a market analysis, a risk assessment, a strategic recommendation — you can't document your human oversight requirement.

**3. What's my liability if the AI is wrong?**
Consulting firms are liable for their advice. If you use AI to generate analysis, you own the analysis — AI errors are your errors. A governance framework doesn't eliminate this liability; it documents how you managed it.

## The Two-Track GPAI Readiness Model

GPAI (General-Purpose AI under the EU AI Act) has an August 2, 2026 implementation deadline. This creates a dual opportunity for small consulting firms:

**Track A — Internal Readiness (Your Firm)**
Your firm needs to be GPAI-ready by August 2, 2026. This means:
- Documenting which AI tools you use in client work
- Establishing a human oversight process for AI-assisted analysis
- Assessing BigTech AI provider risk (outsourcing risk applies to consulting firms advising financial services clients)
- Building an AI use policy that your consultants can follow

**Track B — Client Advisory Product (Your Market)**
Your clients will need GPAI guidance in 2026. Small consulting firms that build the advisory product now will have a differentiated offering when demand peaks. The product: GPAI readiness assessment + AI governance framework for SMBs.

The two tracks reinforce each other. Your internal readiness demonstrates credibility for the client advisory product. Your client advisory experience deepens your internal expertise.
