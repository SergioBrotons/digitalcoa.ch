# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Small-Firm Lawyers Talk About AI (EN)

## Phase 01 | Cycle C294 | ICP: Geneva small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, managing partner or senior associate personally accountable

---

## How They Describe the Problem

**The primary frame: personal accountability for a decision they didn't fully understand.**

These are not people who avoided AI out of conservatism. Many tried it — or had associates try it — and now face a gap between what they've done and what they can defend to a client, a judge, or the Swiss Bar Association (SAV).

Their language reflects this:

---

### On Professional Secrecy / Confidentiality

> *"I can't put client facts into a system I don't control. That's Article 13 BGFA — it's not optional."*

> *"The question isn't whether AI is useful. The question is whether the tool respects professional secrecy. And most tools don't."*

> *"My client data leaves my firm's network and goes somewhere I can't audit. That's not acceptable."*

They use **"professional secrecy"** (secret professionnel / Anwaltsgeheimnis), not "attorney-client privilege" — the latter is more Anglo-American framing. Geneva counsel think in Swiss legal terms: BGFA Article 13, StGB Article 321, and the SBA's own guidelines.

---

### On What "Safe AI" Means

> *"Enterprise-grade, zero data retention, hosted in Switzerland — that's the minimum. Anything else and I'm exposed."*

> *"I need a DPA that actually means something. Not a vendor's terms of service that says 'we may use your data' in the same paragraph where they say they don't."*

> *"If the tool trains its model on my contract drafts, that's not a DPA problem — that's a professional liability problem."*

---

### On Bar Guidance

> *"The SAV published guidelines in June 2024. That's something. But it doesn't tell me which tools are approved, which are compliant, or what 'adequate' looks like for a four-person firm."*

> *"There's no approved list. No certification. The SAV says 'do your due diligence' — but due diligence on an AI vendor is not something a small firm can actually do."*

> *"The guidance is a framework, not a green list. And I'm the one who's accountable if I get it wrong."*

---

### On Personal Exposure

> *"If I use AI to draft a contract and there's a problem, it's my liability — not the software vendor's."*

> *"I can't say 'the AI made the error.' The law says I'm responsible for the mandate. AI doesn't change that."*

> *"The EU AI Act is coming. The Council of Europe AI Convention — Switzerland signed it in March 2025. What does that mean for my practice? Nobody's told me."*

---

### On the Shadow AI Problem

> *"My associates are already using ChatGPT. They don't ask me. They're just trying to get work done faster."*

> *"Microsoft 365 Copilot — IT rolled it out and said it was fine. But at the first data protection audit, we found conversations were being retained in the tenant. Without a proper DPA. That's an Art. 9 revFADP issue."*

> *"I can't ban it. Banning it just means I don't know about it."*

---

### On What They Actually Need

> *"I need to know: can I use this tool for this type of matter, with this level of client data, and sleep at night?"*

> *"I need something that helps me write a proper AI policy for the firm — one that actually reflects the SAV guidelines and the revFADP."*

> *"I need an attestation that I can show a client, or a judge, or the SAV if something goes wrong."*

---

## Key Terminology Patterns

| What they say | What they mean |
|---|---|
| "Professional secrecy" (secret professionnel) | Attorney-client privilege in Swiss legal terms |
| "Adequate governance" | The standard the SAV expects but doesn't define |
| "DPA that actually means something" | Art. 9 revFADP compliant agreement with real protections |
| "Carrier attestation" | Written confirmation a tool meets professional secrecy requirements |
| "The tool respects my confidentiality" | No data retention, no model training, no third-party access |
| "Exposure" | Personal liability — professional, disciplinary, or both |
| "The SAV guidelines" | The SBA's June 2024 "Guidance on dealing with AI" |
| "Shadow AI" | Unsanctioned AI use by staff that management doesn't know about |

---

## Language to Avoid (Our Voice vs. Their Voice)

**Don't say:** "AI-powered legal solutions."  
**They say:** "A tool I can trust with client data."

**Don't say:** "Leverage AI to streamline your practice."  
**They say:** "Something that saves time without creating liability."

**Don't say:** "Next-generation technology."  
**They say:** "Something that actually respects professional secrecy."

**Don't say:** "Comprehensive AI governance framework."  
**They say:** "An AI policy I can defend."

---

## Insight for Lead Magnet

They do not respond to AI capability demonstrations. They respond to language that acknowledges the specific fear: **"I am personally on the hook for every decision about which tools I allow in my practice. The question is not 'is this tool powerful?' — it's 'can I justify this tool to the SAV if challenged?'"**

The entry point is accountability, not efficiency.
