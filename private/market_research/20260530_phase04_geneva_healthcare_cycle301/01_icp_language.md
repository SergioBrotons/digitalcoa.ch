# ICP Language: Geneva Healthcare — Phase 04 C301

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

The Geneva independent practitioner does not say "I need an AI strategy." They say "I need to know what's going to happen to my license." The physician is the accountability unit. Not the practice. Not the system. The physician personally.

---

## Primary Phrasing Patterns

### On Carrier Attestation
- "My carrier is asking me to sign something about AI. What does that mean?"
- "They want confirmation that a human is overseeing the AI. But what does 'oversight' actually mean?"
- "I don't know what I'm attesting to. That's the problem."
- "If I sign it and something goes wrong — am I liable?"

### On FMH Human Oversight
- "FMH says human oversight is required. Fine. But what counts as oversight?"
- "There's no operational definition. Just a principle."
- "Does reading the output count? Does counter-signing? Does actually second-guessing the AI?"
- "I need to know what the minimum standard is, or I'm exposed."

### On Patient Confidentiality with AI
- "I'm using a US-based AI tool. Is that a problem under nFADP?"
- "My patients' data is going somewhere. I need to know where."
- "Do I need a BAA with every AI vendor? What even is a BAA?"
- "The tool vendor says they're compliant. But what does that mean for me specifically?"
- "If there's a breach and patient data leaks — that's on me, not them."

### On Personal Liability
- "My medical license is irreplaceable. AI can't take that risk from me."
- "If the AI gives bad advice and I follow it, I'm still the one in front of the patient."
- "I can't delegate my judgment to a machine."
- "The liability gap is real. There's no case law. No guide. Just me."
- "Documentation debt — I have to prove I used it correctly after the fact."

### On Documentation Debt
- "AI generates notes fast. But I still have to justify every decision."
- "If I'm audited, I need to show I didn't just accept what the AI said."
- "Retroactive documentation is impossible. You can't reconstruct clinical reasoning after the fact."
- "The more AI I use, the more documentation I potentially create — for myself."

### On Swissdamed Confusion
- "Swissdamed is for medical devices. Does my AI documentation tool count?"
- "July 1st deadline — is this something I need to worry about or not?"
- "I can't tell if my AI tool is a medical device or just software."
- "The uncertainty itself is a cost. I need clarity, not another deadline."

---

## Secondary Concerns

- **AI accuracy and reliability**: "The AI might be confident and wrong. That's worse than a human error."
- **Integration with existing workflows**: "I don't have time to redesign how I work. The AI has to fit."
- **Specialist skill shortage**: "I can't hire an AI specialist. I'm the specialist."
- **Cost and reimbursement**: "Insurance doesn't cover AI tools. So it's a cost I absorb."
- **EU AI Act spillover**: "Some of my patients are EU-based. Does that change my exposure?"

## Language Markers to Use

| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| "AI adoption" | "deploying AI in your practice" |
| "AI compliance" | "protecting your license while using AI" |
| "AI governance" | "demonstrating human oversight" |
| "AI policy" | "what you're signing when your carrier asks" |
| "AI risk management" | "the liability question no one has answered" |

---

## Voice and Tone Notes

- Calm. Restrained. Not alarmist.
- Acknowledge the real stakes without amplifying anxiety.
- Precision matters. "Human oversight" is not a definition.
- The physician is personally on the line — validate that, don't exploit it.
- Frame solutions around reducing personal exposure, not around "getting AI right."
