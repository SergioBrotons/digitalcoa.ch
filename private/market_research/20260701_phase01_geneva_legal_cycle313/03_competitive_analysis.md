## Twelve White Spaces (Six C312 + Six C313)

### C312 White Spaces (still unoccupied)

**1. Pre-Signing Carrier Form Review** *(still unoccupied)*
No competitor intercepts before the managing partner signs. Pre-signing review prevents the gap from being created. Post-signing remediation is incomplete.

**2. Geneva Localization of AI Governance** *(still unoccupied)*
Generic frameworks are not Geneva-calibrated. The carrier form is the de facto standard. No competitor reads it as such.

**3. Junior Associate AI Surface Documentation** *(now expanded to include new-hire handover)*
Personal device ChatGPT generating firm exposure with zero managing partner visibility. C313 adds: the handover window creates a temporal authorization gap that compounds annually.

**4. Retroactive Reconstruction Methodology** *(still unoccupied)*
12–24 months of undocumented AI use. No competitor has the methodology.

**5. Broker Referral Program** *(still unoccupied)*
No competitor has built a broker referral program for AI governance services.

**6. "Adequate" Definition for Small Geneva Firms** *(still unoccupied)*
First to define becomes the authority.

### C313 White Spaces (NET-NEW)

**7. Form-Completion Vector Exposure Review**
Carriers and broker platforms are introducing AI-assisted form completion. The ICP is using AI to answer the AI attestation. The form-completion tool processes the very categories of information the form is asking about. No competitor has built a service that audits which form-completion tools are used, what data they process, what Article 321 exposure they create, what vendor terms govern their use. This is the next iteration of the vendor exposure review — specifically focused on the form-completion vector.

**8. Authorization Handover Documentation Service**
New associate cycles create authorization gaps within days. The managing partner signs annual attestations but doesn't update authorization documentation at associate arrival cadence (which is faster than annual). No competitor has built a service that captures authorization state at the cadence of personnel change, not the cadence of attestation renewal.

**9. Swiss-Hosted Vendor Sub-Processor Audit**
The Swiss-residency claim is the most common documented misconception in the Geneva boutique market at C313. The firm that selected a "Swiss-hosted" vendor on residency grounds has Article 321 exposure through sub-processors and metadata. No competitor has built a service specifically targeting the residency assumption — auditing what sub-processors a Swiss-hosted vendor uses, what parent-company cloud infrastructure they inherit, what extraterritorial access they enable, and whether Article 321 is actually satisfied.

**10. Mid-Year Carrier Check-In Documentation Refresh**
Mid-year check-ins are now standard. Documentation cycles are annual. The gap widens with every mid-year intervention. No competitor has built a service that produces within-cycle documentation — refreshed quarterly, updated at personnel change, designed for the mid-year check-in moment rather than the annual renewal moment.

**11. Peer-Attestation Gap Analysis with Documented Benchmark**
Bar-conference season produced public peer disclosures. The comparison trap moved from inference to direct comparison. No competitor has built a service that uses these public disclosures as a benchmark — mapping what specific peer firms declared against what the client firm declared, with a documented gap analysis the client can use to either update their position or document why their position differs.

**12. AI Vendor Succession / Data Portability Planning**
The AI vendor may go bankrupt, get acquired, terminate service. The firm's client data transfers with the asset sale. Article 321 doesn't terminate. No competitor has built a service that addresses vendor succession specifically — what data export obligations the vendor has, what portability terms exist, what the firm should require in vendor contracts for succession scenarios, and what to do when succession happens without warning.

## The Competitive Compression (Why This Matters Now)

In C311 and C312, the white-space count was six. By C313, with six new white spaces added during the pause, the total is twelve. The competitor's addressable white space just doubled in 31 days.

**Every competitor that built a C312 framework is now a C313 framework.** They are selling C312 solutions to C313 problems. The C313 ICP needs the C313 service.

The market is also fragmenting differently. In C311-C312, the competitor set was:
- Boutique AI governance firms (language proximity, wrong delivery model)
- Mid-tier accounting firms (existing relationships, one hire away)
- Legal tech vendors (vendor-interest-conflict, not a threat)

In C313, a new competitor entry vector has emerged:
- **Form-completion tool vendors** — they are now embedded in the carrier attestation workflow. They have carrier relationships the boutique AI governance firms don't. They are positioning as "the carrier form completion partner." They are the next major competitive threat because they have the delivery infrastructure — every carrier renewal flows through their tool.

This is an emergence: the AI governance market is being unbundled. The form-completion vendors are unbundling the attestation delivery from the attestation evidence. The form-completion vendors own the delivery layer. The boutique AI governance firms will need to own the evidence layer (which is what Sergio builds). The unbundling is structural and will accelerate.

## The C313 Competitive Response

The competitor that was selling "AI governance frameworks" in C312 is now selling "AI governance frameworks + form-completion integration." That integration is the wrong response to the C313 problem. The form-completion tool is the exposure vector. Integrating into it amplifies the exposure.

The C313 competitor response is to **not integrate with the form-completion tools**. The C313 service independently audits the form-completion tool, documents what data it processes, and provides the alternative: answering the form directly, with the firm's own evidence architecture, without the form-completion vendor exposure layer.

This is a clean competitive position: "The form-completion tool processes your matter descriptions through their vendor. We show you what that creates. And we produce the evidence that lets you answer the form without using their tool."
