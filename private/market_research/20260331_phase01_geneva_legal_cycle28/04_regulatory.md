# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal — Regulatory Context
## Cycle 28 — March 31, 2026 — 01:31 UTC

## Active Regulations

### 1. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nFADP)
**Status:** Operative since September 1, 2023
**AI relevance:** Technology-neutral — directly applicable to AI-supported data processing (confirmed by FDPIC multiple statements in 2025–2026)

**Key FDPIC Statements:**
- FDPIC May 8, 2025: FADP explicitly applies to AI-based data processing
- FDPIC Jan 28, 2026 (Data Protection Day): Reiterated direct applicability to AI-supported processing; generative AI concerns highlighted
- FDPIC Feb 23, 2026: Joint statement with 60 DPAs on AI-generated images
- FDPIC Feb 26, 2026: Transport company bodycam investigation initiated

**Key obligations:**
- Transparency: Users must know if interacting with AI; disclose purpose/functionality/data sources
- Privacy by Design and Default: Data protection from initial AI development stages
- DPIA required for high-risk AI processing (contract analysis, due diligence = likely high-risk)
- Prohibited: Mass real-time facial recognition, social scoring

**Enforcement:**
- FDPIC enforcement portfolio building: 1,053 complaints 2024/2025; legal sector absent = timing signal
- Enforcement actions 2025–2026: transport x3, banking, retail x2, social media
- Criminal liability: Managing director up to CHF 250,000 for intentional FADP failures

### 2. EU AI Act (Extraterritorial)
**Status:** August 2, 2026 — high-risk obligations activate
**Application:** Swiss firms with EU market nexus (placing AI on EU market, operating EU-intended output, acting as EU importer/distributor)
**Timeline:** ~16 months from March 2026

### 3. Council of Europe AI Convention
**Status:** Signed March 2025; consultation draft for Swiss implementation expected end-2026
**Focus areas:** Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight

### 4. Federal Council AI Action Plan
**Status:** Zero Draft January 2026; First Draft May 2026; second consultation underway
**Approach:** Sector-specific; lean approach confirmed (no cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026)
**FDJP consultation draft:** End-2026

### 5. GPAI Transparency Obligations
**Status:** Active since August 2, 2025 (~8 months already operative)
**Application:** Firms subject to EU AI Act GPAI obligations (EU nexus required)

## Key Compliance Points for Small Geneva Law Firms
1. FADP already applies — no new law required for obligation to exist
2. DPIA required if using AI for high-risk processing (contract analysis, due diligence)
3. Shadow AI = FDPIC exposure (employees using unapproved tools = firm liability)
