## Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit
*Before any strategy — know what you already have*

Your attorneys are likely using AI tools right now. Some may be using unapproved tools on firm devices or client data. This is your highest-immediate-risk item.

**Shadow AI = FDPIC exposure + professional liability exposure**
- Employee uses unapproved AI on client confidential data = firm's FADP obligations breached
- No formal AI policy = no defense: "we didn't know"
- Managing director can face personal criminal liability (up to CHF 250,000) for intentional failures

**Decision 0 Action:** Conduct a confidential shadow AI inventory. No blame. No punishment. Map what AI tools are already in use before any regulatory contact.

---

## Decision 1: What Data Are You Processing with AI?

AI tools process data. Under FADP, this triggers obligations regardless of the tool's origin.

**High-risk AI processing (requires DPIA):**
- Contract analysis using AI → client confidential data processed
- Due diligence using AI → third-party personal data processed
- Document review using AI → potentially sensitive matters processed

**Standard AI processing:**
- Drafting using AI (non-confidential)
- Translation using AI (with appropriate data controls)
- Legal research using AI (public sources)

**Decision 1 Action:** Map every AI tool currently in use against the data it processes. Identify which use cases touch client confidential information.

---

## Decision 2: Do You Have EU Nexus?

The EU AI Act activates August 2, 2026 (~16 months). If your firm has any EU clients, places AI-driven work product into EU circulation, or acts as EU market intermediary, you face extraterritorial EU AI Act obligations.

**EU AI Act obligations activate for:**
- Firms placing AI systems on the EU market
- Firms operating AI-driven outputs intended for EU use
- Firms acting as EU importers/distributors of AI systems
- Firms subject to GPAI transparency obligations (active since August 2, 2025)

**Decision 2 Action:** Determine whether any practice area, client relationship, or matter type creates EU nexus. If yes, EU AI Act high-risk obligations apply August 2, 2026.

## Decision 3: Dual Compliance — FADP + EU AI Act

For Geneva firms with EU exposure, compliance is not a single regulation — it is a dual obligation:

| Obligation | Source | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Data protection (including AI) | FADP (nFADP) | Active since Sep 1, 2023 |
| AI transparency | GPAI obligations | Active since Aug 2, 2025 |
| AI high-risk obligations | EU AI Act | Activates Aug 2, 2026 |
| AI transparency (CH) | Federal Council action plan | First Draft May 2026 |

**Decision 3 Action:** Map your AI use cases against both frameworks. The FADP is active now. The EU AI Act is 16 months away. Neither requires waiting.
