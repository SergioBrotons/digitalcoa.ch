### Finding 01 — Pre-Briefing Qualification Call as Discrete Trust Formation Mechanism Operating Before Engagement

**The finding:** The 20-minute qualification call is not a commercial screening step. It is a trust formation occasion where the practice demonstrates whether it can precisely describe the prospect's situation before proposing engagement terms. The executive who experiences this precision — the practice describing their accountability geometry in terms that demonstrate prior familiarity with exactly this configuration — forms trust before any substantive analysis has been delivered. The mechanism operates through recognition, not evaluation.

**Mechanism:** Simultaneously, the call functions as a service. The executive who has a genuine accountability situation benefits from the calibration even if they do not engage, because the calibration demonstrates whether the questions they are forming are the right questions. This dual function produces referrals from non-converting qualified executives — people who did not engage but who benefited from the call and refer others who share their situation.

**Distinct from C214-C299:** All prior Phase 06 pre-qualification findings addressed the qualification call as a conversion step or a fit-assessment. None isolated the specific dual calibration-and-service function that produces qualified referrals from non-converting prospects, operating through recognition before evaluation.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The call is not a commercial step. It's a calibration — whether I can describe your situation precisely enough that you know I have seen it before."

### Finding 02 — Written Summary Survivability as Primary Deliverable Property Distinct from Conversation Summary

**The finding:** The written summary is not a conversation summary. It is a document designed to survive into future accountability contexts — board review, institutional counterparty examination, carrier renewal questionnaire response — that the original engagement did not explicitly design for. The executive's primary need is a defensible document in six months when the accountability question arrives that the conversation did not anticipate.

**Mechanism:** This survivability property is not an output format choice. It is an architectural design constraint that determines what the written summary must be: the question is not what did we discuss, but what will this document need to survive when the board asks about it, when the carrier examiner requests methodology evidence, when the successor reviews it. The written summary designed as a survivable document has different architectural properties than a summary designed as a conversation record — different evidence density, different register calibration, different forward-looking positioning.

**Distinct from C214-C299:** All prior Phase 06 written summary findings addressed output quality, deliverable format, or documentation standards. None isolated the architectural design constraint of survivability into future contexts with different evidentiary requirements — the deliverable property that determines all other properties.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The test is whether this document holds coherent meaning in six months when the board asks questions you cannot answer from memory. Most advisors produce conversation summaries. I produce documents designed to survive into contexts the conversation did not design for."

### Finding 05 — Return Engagement Density at Accountability Occasion Moments as Behavioral Evidence of Framework Durability

**The finding:** The executives who return across multiple accountability occasions — carrier renewal, board meeting, institutional counterparty inquiry, Federal Council consultation window — are demonstrating that the documented positions held under conditions that changed between engagements. Return density at accountability occasion moments is different from general client satisfaction or repeat engagement.

**Mechanism:** It specifically demonstrates that the analytical framework has properties that survive the conditions that invalidate simpler frameworks. The executive who returned after the carrier renewal arrived with modifications did not return because the initial engagement was satisfactory. They returned because the documented position from the initial engagement held under conditions that were different from the conditions under which it was produced. This is behavioral evidence of framework durability under changed conditions — the strongest trust signal because it cannot be fabricated and because the test conditions were the executive's own accountability exposure, not an evaluation the practice designed.

**Distinct from C214-C299:** All prior Phase 06 retention and referral findings addressed return density as a business metric or client satisfaction indicator. None isolated the specific accountability occasion return pattern as behavioral evidence of framework durability under changed conditions — the pattern that demonstrates the analytical framework's survival properties, not just its initial quality.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The executives who return across carrier renewals and board meetings are not returning because the first engagement was satisfactory. They're returning because what we documented held under conditions that changed."

### Finding 06 — Geneva Circuit Referral as Second-Order Evaluation Operating Through Referrer's Own Accountability Exposure as Test Condition

**The finding:** The referral from someone inside the Geneva circuit is not a first-order recommendation. It is a second-order evaluation: the referrer has tested the practice under specific accountability conditions and is evaluating it against their own exposure. The executive receiving the referral is receiving an evaluation that already happened — someone whose professional reputation was at stake assessed whether the analysis held under conditions that mattered to them.

**Mechanism:** The test condition is the referrer's own accountability exposure, not a scenario the practice constructed. This creates a structurally different referral dynamic: the referrer's credibility is at stake in the evaluation, which means the evaluation is more reliable than a referral where the referrer has no accountability exposure to the question. The second-order evaluation mechanism means that the practice's referrals carry weight proportional to the referrer's own accountability exposure — referrals from executives who used the practice on matters where their personal liability was engaged are categorically different signals from referrals from executives who had a satisfactory experience.

**Distinct from C214-C299:** All prior Phase 06 referral findings named the circuit referral as a trust signal without isolating the specific second-order evaluation mechanism where the referrer's own accountability exposure is the test condition determining the referral's weight — the mechanism that makes the referral structurally different from a standard client satisfaction referral.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The referral you received came from someone whose professional reputation was at stake in the same question. They tested the analysis against their own accountability exposure. That's a different kind of referral than a satisfied client reference."
