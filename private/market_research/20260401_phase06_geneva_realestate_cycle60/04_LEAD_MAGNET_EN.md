## The Geneva Real Estate AI Governance Problem

Geneva's real estate agencies are, by a significant margin, the least prepared of all six Geneva sectors for the AI governance challenge. Survey data shows 25% using AI tools — AVMs, tenant screening platforms, CRM automation, property matching tools — but approximately 5% with documented governance policies. This is the widest belief-action gap in the Geneva market.

The Federal AI Ordinance has been operative for fifteen months. EU AI Act obligations for General-Purpose AI have been in force since August 2, 2025. August 2, 2026 is approximately four months away. And there is no SVIT-specific guidance on AI governance for Geneva real estate agencies.

This checklist is for educational purposes. Answer these five questions honestly.

## Part I: The Five Real Estate AI Governance Questions

1. **AVMs:** Do you use Automated Valuation Models in your property listings? Is there a documented human review of every AVM output before client delivery? (Almost no Geneva agency can answer yes to this question.)

2. **Tenant Screening AI:** Do you use tenant screening AI or creditworthiness assessment tools? Have you assessed whether these fall within EU AI Act Annex III high-risk AI classification?

3. **Client Consent:** Do you have documented client consent protocols for AI-assisted property decisions — specifically, disclosure that an AVM was used and that the client has the right to request human review?

4. **Data Sovereignty:** Are your AVM tools, CRM automation, and property matching platforms hosted on Swiss or EU infrastructure — or on US BigTech infrastructure?

5. **EU AI Act Exposure:** Have you assessed whether the EU AI Act applies to your agency — specifically if you serve EU buyers, EU investors, or use EU-sourced AI platforms?

## Part II: The AVM PILOT TRAP

The Automated Valuation Model is the most systemic compliance gap in Geneva real estate.

Here is why: AVMs generate automated valuations. Those valuations are used in decisions that affect data subjects — buyers, sellers, tenants. Under FADP Art. 21, data subjects have the right to be informed when automated decisions are made about them and to request human review. Under the Federal AI Ordinance, high-risk AI decisions require documented human oversight.

Every Geneva agency using AVMs without documented human review is doubly exposed — under two frameworks simultaneously. And the EU AI Act likely classifies AVMs as high-risk AI (Annex III: creditworthiness/financial standing assessment).

This is not a hypothetical risk. It is the standard practice of virtually every Geneva real estate agency. The exposure is systemic.
