## Finding 01 — FINMA AI Credit Risk Model Governance Circular: Implementation Guidance

FINMA published implementation guidance in April 2026 accompanying its Q1 2026 AI model risk governance circular, specifically addressing credit risk model requirements for Geneva private banks and asset managers. The guidance clarifies three contentious areas: the independence standard for AI model validation teams (acceptable reporting structures), the documentation requirements for AI models used in credit decisions affecting client outcomes, and the stress testing frequency for AI credit risk models in volatile market conditions. The guidance also establishes a phased implementation timeline: large institutions (balance sheet above CHF 5 billion) must comply within 9 months; smaller institutions within 15 months. Geneva wealth managers with material AI-driven credit exposure are directly in the first cohort.

**ICP signal:** Geneva private banks and asset managers need AI credit risk model gap assessments against the FINMA circular and implementation guidance — a defined advisory engagement with a regulatory deadline.

## Finding 02 — SECO AI Diamond and Conflict Mineral Trade Finance Compliance

SECO issued specific guidance in March 2026 on AI systems used in diamond and conflict mineral trade finance compliance, addressing a gap in the existing AML/CFT framework that did not previously address AI-specific obligations. The guidance requires Geneva commodity trading firms dealing in diamonds, tin, tantalum, tungsten, or gold from conflict-affected regions to implement AI systems with explainability documentation, human override procedures, and origin chain of custody verification. The guidance follows a SECO audit of Geneva commodity trading firms in Q4 2025 that found inconsistent AI tool documentation across the sector. Non-compliance with the AI-specific requirements now risks SECO enforcement action separate from standard AML failures.

**ICP signal:** Geneva commodity trading firms handling minerals or diamonds need AI trade finance compliance audits against the SECO guidance — a niche compliance market with a newly created regulatory obligation.

## Finding 03 — SNB Wholesale CBDC AI Integration Technical Consultation Findings

The Swiss National Bank (SNB) published the findings of its technical consultation on AI integration in wholesale Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) systems in April 2026. The consultation identified three key findings: AI-driven liquidity management in wholesale CBDC systems introduces systemic risk concerns not present in traditional systems; AI model governance for wholesale CBDC payment orchestration requires standards beyond existing payment system oversight; and privacy-preserving AI techniques (federated learning, differential privacy) are necessary for wholesale CBDC transaction monitoring. The SNB has not committed to a wholesale CBDC issuance timeline but has indicated it will require AI governance standards as a precondition for any future wholesale CBDC pilot with Swiss financial institutions.

**ICP signal:** Geneva financial institutions preparing for potential wholesale CBDC integration need AI governance frameworks aligned to SNB's emerging technical standards — a forward-looking advisory niche.

## Finding 04 — Geneva Tokenized Securities AI Lending Platform Operational

A Geneva-based financial infrastructure firm launched the first operational tokenized securities AI lending platform in April 2026, enabling institutional investors to use tokenized securities (bonds, equities, and funds) as collateral for AI-mediated lending transactions. The platform uses AI for credit assessment of borrowing counterparties, collateral valuation, and risk-based margin calculation. FINMA reviewed the platform under the DLT Act framework and issued a formal no-action letter clarifying that the AI lending mechanism did not require an additional banking licence beyond the firm's existing DLT facility authorisation. The platform marks the first operational AI-mediated securities lending product in Geneva's tokenized securities ecosystem and is being watched by other DLT facility operators as a potential model.

**ICP signal:** Geneva institutions with DLT facilities or tokenized securities operations need AI governance assessments for AI lending and credit products — this is an emerging regulatory frontier.

## Finding 05 — FINMA/MRK Algorithmic Trading Compliance Guidance

FINMA and the Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority's Market Supervision division (MRK) jointly issued guidance in April 2026 on algorithmic trading compliance for Geneva-based firms, addressing AI-driven order generation, execution algorithms, and market-making systems. The guidance requires algorithmic trading firms to implement AI system documentation covering: algorithm design rationale, backtesting methodology and results, stress testing under extreme market conditions, and kill-switch procedures. The guidance also requires that AI execution algorithms used for market-making maintain minimum bid-ask spread parameters subject to automatic human override. Three Geneva commodity trading and trading advisory firms were named in the guidance as reference examples of compliant AI trading system design.

**ICP signal:** Geneva algorithmic trading firms and commodity trading firms with AI execution systems need algorithmic trading compliance audits against the FINMA/MRK guidance — a defined but underserved advisory market.

## Finding 06 — FINMA/FOEN Sustainable Finance AI Disclosure XBRL Standard

FINMA and the Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN) jointly published a technical standard in April 2026 for AI-enabled sustainability disclosure collection and verification, aligned with the Swiss Climate Disclosure Ordinance. The standard specifies a machine-readable XBRL taxonomy for AI-processed ESG data submissions, enabling FINMA to process AI-verified sustainability disclosures programmatically. Key requirement: firms using AI tools to compile ESG data must maintain AI decision audit trails showing how the AI system derived ESG metrics from raw data, and must submit AI verification methodology documentation alongside XBRL filings. The standard is mandatory for large Swiss publicly listed companies and institutional investors with FY2026 disclosures due mid-2027.

**ICP signal:** Geneva asset managers, pension funds, and listed companies with FY2026 ESG disclosure obligations need AI ESG disclosure governance assessments against the XBRL standard — a compliance deadline-driven market.
