## F1: Swiss Tech Fragility — EPF Budget Cuts + Capital Gap
**Source:** Le Temps editorial, March 26, 2026  
**Signal:** HIGH — innovation ecosystem risk

Swiss tech is simultaneously world-class and fragile. Switzerland ranks #1 in IMD 2025 digital competitiveness, top 10 in patent filings (EPO), EPFL/ETHZ in global top 50. Yet Le Temps editorial warns of structural fragility:

1. **EPF budget cuts:** Confederation imposing significant financial pressure on EPFL/ETHZ — described as "quasi-suicidal strategy" given their centrality to Swiss innovation
2. **Capital gap:** Scale-ups (CHF 1-5M to CHF 50M+ funding stage) regularly cannot find Swiss capital and turn to US investors
3. **Acquisition pattern:** Amazon, Meta regularly acquire Swiss tech "fleurons" — founders/investors benefit, but substance goes to US

NGO angle: this is a policy advocacy opportunity for Geneva think tanks and innovation policy NGOs.

## F2: Amazon Acquiring Swiss AI/Robotics — RIVR Delivery Robots
**Source:** Le Temps, March 26, 2026  
**Signal:** MEDIUM-HIGH — tech acquisition pattern

Amazon acquired RIVR (Zurich-based delivery robot company). This follows the recurring pattern of US tech giants absorbing Swiss AI/robotics companies before they reach full independent scale. Implications:
- Geneva region has delivery robotics ecosystem at risk of pre-mature acquisition
- Policy question: should Swiss tech champions receive state support to remain independent?
- Brain drain: engineering teams may stay in Switzerland post-acquisition but strategic decisions move to US
- NGO advocacy angle: technology sovereignty, digital autonomy

## F3: The Swiss "Logitech Problem" — Companies Acquired Before Becoming Global Leaders
**Source:** Le Temps, April 1, 2026  
**Signal:** MEDIUM — innovation policy

Project Switzerland (Deep Tech Nation foundation — Swisscom + UBS) explicitly aims to produce "the next Logitech." The Logitech problem: Switzerland produces world-class technology companies that tend to get acquired before reaching their full potential as independent global leaders. Le Temps editorial: "Soyons conscients des succès...mais aussi de leur fragilité."

Geneva NGO angle: this is a policy debate. Should Switzerland do more to retain tech champions? What is the role of public-private partnerships like Project Switzerland?
