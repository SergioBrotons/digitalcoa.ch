# Independent Physician Practice — Cycle 294

## Compliance Context: Framework Summary

---

### Primary Frameworks Governing Geneva Independent Practice AI Use

**1. FMH Professional Standards (Art.ords et Directives)**
- FMH human oversight principle: stated requirement for physician to maintain oversight of AI-assisted decisions, no operational endpoint defined, no documentation requirement, no competency standard
- Professional liability framework: physician accountable for decisions made with AI assistance, accountability attribution to signing physician
- Scope: national, de facto operative for all FMH members in independent practice

**2. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, as amended 2024/2025)**
- Art.5: lawful basis for processing (consent, legitimate interest, contractual necessity, legal obligation, vital interest, public interest)
- Art.6: processing of sensitive personal data requires explicit lawful basis beyond general categories
- Art.8: profiling notification obligation — AI-assisted portfolio construction = profiling, subjects have right to notification and human review
- Art.9: processing of health data requires explicit consent + specific purpose limitation
- Art.20: right of access to automated decision information
- Art.21: right to explanation for automated decisions, human review right
- Art.22: right to reversal of automated decisions — applies where AI substantially informs decisions reflected in patient record
- Art.63: criminal liability provisions including personal liability for managing directors
- Scope: national, directly applicable, enforcement active

**3. Art.321 CP (Swiss Criminal Code)**
- Professional secrecy obligations extend to patient data physician controls
- Vendor data extraction processing creates external data flow under vendor's claimed lawful basis potentially in conflict with physician's Art.321 CP obligations
- Scope: national, enforced, criminal penalty

**4. Carrier AI Attestation Requirements**
- Underwriting-designed questionnaire committing physician to undefined adequacy standard
- Personal liability instruments without pre-signing review pathway
- Arriving in healthcare 6–12 months after legal/finance wave
- Scope: Geneva market, multi-carrier

**5. Swissdamed (Swiss Federal Institute for Professional Licensing)**
- Medical device classification framework (Tier 1/Tier 2) written for manufacturers, structurally inapplicable to outpatient independent practices
- Creates compliance paralysis rather than resolution
- Scope: national, for medical device characterization

**6. Geneva Institutional Governance (HUG/CHUV)**
- De facto standards forming through governance rooms without methodology transmission to independent practice
- Institutional decisions accessible to carriers/patients/legal proceedings without independent practice participation
- Scope: Geneva institutional, indirect operative effect on independent practice standard

**7. Clinical Documentation Standards**
- Medical record authorship obligations: records authoritative under physician name
- AI contribution to records not documented at time of writing
- No Geneva-specific standard for documenting AI-influenced clinical decisions
- Art.22 reversal protocol interacts with record integrity creating structurally incompatible obligations

---

### Key Compliance Paradoxes Specific to Independent Practice

**Structural compliance impossibility:**
Same individual holds: user (AI tool operator) + compliance officer (responsible for governance adherence) + billing (responsible for billing accuracy) + treating physician (responsible for clinical decisions) simultaneously — no role separation possible, no independent review structure exists at practice scale, oversight principle structurally impossible to fulfill as stated.

**Three-consent incompatibility:**
Treatment consent + nFADP data processing consent + AI-specific consent disclosure cannot be satisfied by single consent form — architectural incompatibility requires own integration design at independent practice scale with no template.

**AI vendor / professional secrecy conflict:**
Vendor click-through acceptance activates data extraction provisions under vendor's claimed lawful basis — physician's Art.321 CP obligations run directly against vendor's claimed processing authorization — no proportionate review mechanism exists at independent practice scale.

---

### Regulatory Timeline Factors

**Present-tense obligations:**
- nFADP Art.22 reversal right: active now, not delayed
- Art.321 CP professional secrecy: active now, vendor data extraction in progress
- Carrier attestation arriving: healthcare 6–12 months behind legal/finance
- Documentation window: present-tense positioning event, not future deadline
- Audit-readiness: exposure exists without awareness

**Key timing insight:**
Independent practices are building documentation after operative standards have already formed through informal Geneva channels — retroactive reconstruction is the condition most Geneva independent practices currently operate under.
