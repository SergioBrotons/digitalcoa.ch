# 03_Competitive_Analysis.md — Phase 04 C294: Geneva Healthcare ICP
# Independent Physician Practice — Cycle 294

## What Exists vs. What's Missing

---

### What EXISTS in the Market

**Existing solutions serving this ICP:**

1. **FMH AI Guidance (2019 framework, updated)** — human oversight principle stated, no operational endpoint defined, principle-level only, no documentation requirement, no review frequency, no competency standard. Useful as legitimacy anchor (we're following FMH) but provides zero operational structure for independent practice. Operating as compliance permission slip giving appearance of oversight without substance.

2. **Carrier AI Attestation Forms** — arriving in healthcare market, underwriting-designed questionnaires committing physician to undefined adequacy standard. Designed for carrier risk evaluation, not physician professional liability protection. Pre-signing review service absent from Geneva market. Operating as liability instrument without discharge pathway.

3. **Vendor AI Tools** — deployed across independent practice stack through click-through acceptance, accumulation problem (multi-tool surface built through incremental updates), terms accepted without FMH-compatibility review, data extraction clauses embedded in click-through with no separate consent pathway.

4. **Swissdamed** — medical device classification framework with criteria written for manufacturers, structurally inapplicable to outpatient independent practices, creating compliance paralysis rather than compliance resolution.

5. **nFADP** — applies to healthcare AI as data controller/processor obligations, but enforcement framework and physician-specific guidance absent, Geneva market operates under unnamed compliance obligations with no proportionate methodology.

6. **General AI governance frameworks** — national/international frameworks (EU AI Act, ISO AI standards) too broad for independent practice use, no Geneva-calibrated, physician-specific AI governance methodology exists at practice scale.

7. **Geneva hospital AI governance** — HUG/CHUV operating under institutional governance infrastructure with resources independent practices lack, forming effective standards through governance rooms without methodology transmission to independent sector.

---

### What is MISSING from the Market

**White space — completely absent:**

1. **Geneva independent practice AI governance documentation system** — no product exists that creates proportionate AI governance documentation architecture at independent practice scale (2–10 physicians). No audit trail system, no review scheduling, no documentation template calibrated to physician accountability, no FMH-compatible standards framing, no carrier-attestation-ready evidence structure. Market completely empty.

2. **Pre-signing carrier AI attestation review service for medical practitioners** — entirely absent, no Geneva service offers signatory-side review of carrier AI attestation before signature, no undefined adequacy standard definition available in market, physician signs carrier forms committing to standards they cannot define.

3. **Three-consent architecture integration template** — no Geneva-calibrated template exists for integrating treatment consent + nFADP data processing consent + AI-specific consent disclosure as coherent single instrument. Practices attempting compliance have no reference document, incomplete documentation creates evidence trail used against them.

4. **AI-influenced clinical decision documentation protocol** — no Geneva protocol for documenting AI-influenced clinical decisions at point of clinical choice, specifically deferral documentation, referral documentation, AI-influenced-but-not-AI-determined clinical judgments. Medical-legal documentation standard absent from market entirely.

5. **Professional secrecy / vendor data extraction conflict resolution service** — no Geneva service offers evaluation of vendor click-through terms against physician professional secrecy obligations, no mechanism for physician to maintain AI functionality while controlling data flows, no proportionate vendor-term review service for independent practices.

6. **FMH-compatible AI training documentation standard** — no standard exists for what AI competency documentation independent practice physician must maintain, no training documentation system, physician maintains effectively zero AI training documentation with no awareness of what documentation they should maintain.

7. **Geneva institutional intelligence translation service** — no mechanism translates HUG/CHUV governance decisions into independent practice actionable guidance, no channel for methodology transmission, independent practices build in isolation from institutional intelligence.

8. **AI triage/deferral accountability documentation framework** — specific to AI triage referral deferral accountability mismatch, no Geneva market product addresses the documented AI suggestion + physician deferral scenario, no medical-legal documentation standard for AI-influenced clinical deferral.

9. **nFADP Art.22 medical record reversal protocol** — no protocol exists for executing Art.22 automated decision reversal requests in clinical context, no proportionate clinical notation methodology satisfying Art.22 without altering medical record integrity, physician faces two incompatible obligations (record integrity + reversal execution) with no guidance.

10. **AI medical record authorship documentation system** — no system exists for documenting AI contribution to medical records at time of writing, retroactive documentation impossible when incident occurs, no Geneva-calibrated methodology for maintaining record authorship documentation.

---

### Competitive Positioning Implication

**Why this matters for positioning:**
DigitalCoa.ch existing content addresses Geneva legal and finance ICP accountability language. No Geneva-calibrated, physician-specific AI governance content addressing independent practice accountability problems at the persona level this ICP operates at. No competitor has claimed this specific white space territory with physician-specific first-person accountability framing.

**Key gap:**
All existing AI governance content is either:
- Too general (national/international frameworks)
- Too hospital/institutional (HUG/CHUV resources)
- Too manufacturer-oriented (Swissdamed)
- Designed for other ICPs (legal, finance, consulting)

Independent physician practice accountability gap is completely unaddressed at physician-persona voice level in Geneva market.
