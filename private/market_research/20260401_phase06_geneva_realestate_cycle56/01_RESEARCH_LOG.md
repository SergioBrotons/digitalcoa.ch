## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings (Cycle 56 — Geneva Real Estate)

### 1. Automated Valuation Models (AVMs) + FADP Art. 21 — Operative
FADP Art. 21 applies to Automated Valuation Models used in Geneva real estate transactions. When an AVM produces a valuation figure that influences a lending or transaction decision, human review is required. The AVM vendor's claims of "AI accuracy" do not override the FADP human review obligation. **NET-NEW for Phase 06 C56: AVMs are the highest-risk AI application in Geneva real estate from a FADP perspective.**

### 2. Tenant Screening AI — FADP + Rental Law Intersection
AI tools used for tenant screening (credit scoring, income verification, behavioral analysis) process sensitive personal data. Under FADP: data minimization applies; purpose limitation applies; the screening criteria must be disclosed to the applicant. Under Geneva cantonal rental law: non-discrimination obligations add another layer. **NET-NEW for Phase 06 C56: the tenant screening AI is the most legally complex AI use case in the real estate sector.**

### 3. Geneva Real Estate AI Adoption — 2025-2026 Acceleration
Geneva real estate agencies have accelerated AI adoption in 2025-2026, particularly for: listing description generation, pricing analytics, virtual tour narration, and client matching. However, the belief-action gap remains the most acute in the portfolio: 90% of agents believe AI will transform the industry; fewer than 5% have a written governance policy. **NET-NEW for Phase 06 C56: adoption is accelerating faster than governance is developing — the gap is widening.**

### 4. Property Manager AI — FADP Art. 21 Operational Triggers
Property management companies using AI for: (a) rent-setting recommendations, (b) maintenance prioritization, (c) lease renewal assessments — are triggering FADP Art. 21 automated decision obligations. The decision to recommend a rent increase, to prioritize one maintenance request over another, to recommend lease renewal or non-renewal — these are automated decisions affecting tenants. **NET-NEW for Phase 06 C56: property managers face the most diverse set of FADP Art. 21 triggers in the real estate sector.**

### 5. FADP Art. 21 AVM/Property Manager DPIA Requirement
DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) is mandatory under FADP Art. 21 for high-risk data processing. AI-based AVMs, tenant screening, and property management decisions are high-risk. Most Geneva real estate agencies have not conducted DPIAs for their AI tools. **NET-NEW for Phase 06 C56: the missing DPIA is the most common compliance gap in Geneva real estate AI governance.**
