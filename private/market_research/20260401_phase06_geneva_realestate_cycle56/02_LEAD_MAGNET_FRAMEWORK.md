# Phase 06 C56 — Lead Magnet Framework: "The Small Real Estate Agency AI Governance Question"

## Positioning
**For:** Small Geneva real estate agencies (1–20 agents) using AI for listings, pricing, tenant screening, or property management
**Against:** Real estate tech vendor AI pitches; generic property tech content
**Unique hook:** AVMs + FADP Art. 21 operative + tenant screening legal complexity + widest belief-action gap in the portfolio (90% / <5%)

---

## Core Promise
The Geneva real estate sector has the most acute AI governance gap in the market. The AI tools you're already using — AVMs, tenant screening, pricing analytics — are creating legal exposure that most agencies haven't identified.

---

## Problem Statement (4 layers)

### Layer 1: AVMs + FADP Art. 21 (Highest-Risk AI in Real Estate)
Automated Valuation Models produce figures that influence lending and transaction decisions. Under FADP Art. 21, every automated valuation requires human review before being used in a consequential decision. The AVM vendor's "AI accuracy" claim doesn't eliminate your human review obligation.

### Layer 2: Tenant Screening AI — Most Legally Complex AI Use Case
AI tenant screening tools: (a) process sensitive personal data (credit, income, behavioral signals), (b) must comply with FADP data minimization and purpose limitation, (c) must disclose screening criteria to applicants, (d) must comply with Geneva cantonal non-discrimination obligations. Most agencies using these tools haven't addressed all four.

### Layer 3: Property Management AI — Diverse FADP Triggers
Rent-setting recommendations, maintenance prioritization, lease renewal assessments — each is an automated decision triggering FADP Art. 21 human review. Property managers have the most diverse set of FADP triggers in the real estate sector.

### Layer 4: The Widening Gap
AI adoption accelerating; governance not keeping pace. 90% of agents believe AI will transform the industry. <5% have a written governance policy. The gap is widening.
