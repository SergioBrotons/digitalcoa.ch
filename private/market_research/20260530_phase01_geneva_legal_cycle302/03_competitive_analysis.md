# Competitive Analysis — Geneva Legal (Phase 01, C302)

## ICP Profile
Small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based. Managing partner or senior associate personally accountable for AI decisions. Concerned about attorney-client privilege in AI systems, carrier PI attestation, bar guidance gaps, and what "adequate" AI governance means at boutique scale.

---

## What Exists in the Market

### Large Consulting Firm Offerings

**McKinsey / BCG / Deloitte AI Governance Frameworks**

- What they offer: Comprehensive AI governance frameworks, risk assessment methodology, policy documentation, board-level reporting
- Target: Enterprise legal departments, multinational corporations, BigLaw
- Gap for Geneva ICP: Over-engineered for 2–5 person firms. The frameworks are designed for organizations with dedicated compliance teams. A 12-factor governance matrix doesn't help when you have two attorneys and no compliance staff.

**Key weakness:** Large consulting outputs are designed to be audited by large organizations. They produce documentation that looks impressive but cannot be operated by a small firm without dedicated support.

---

### Legal Tech Platforms

**CASUS (Swiss legal AI)**

- What it offers: Swiss-hosted legal AI platform, data protection compliant, professional secrecy compatible
- Target: Swiss law firms, particularly small to mid-sized
- Gap for Geneva ICP: CASUS addresses the tool problem (Swiss-hosted, no data retention) but does NOT address the documentation debt problem. A new compliant tool doesn't retroactively reconstruct the evidence trail for past AI decisions.

**Key weakness:** CASUS solves the forward-looking tool selection problem. It does not solve the retrospective documentation debt problem that carriers are now demanding evidence for.

---

### Bar Association Guidance

**SBA (Swiss Bar Association) Guidelines — June 2024**

- What it offers: Non-binding recommendations on responsible AI use, confidentiality obligations, supervision requirements
- Target: All Swiss attorneys
- Gap for Geneva ICP: Guidelines are principles-based, not documentation-based. They say "supervise AI output" but don't define what documentation constitutes adequate supervision evidence.

**Key weakness:** The SBA guidelines create obligation without providing documentation methodology. The ICP is required to demonstrate "adequate supervision" but no one has defined what adequate supervision documentation looks like for a small firm.

---

### Professional Indemnity Carriers

**Zurich, AXA, AIG, Swiss reinsurance carriers**

- What they offer: PI insurance policies, increasingly with AI attestation requirements on renewal forms
- Target: Law firms, consultants, professional services
- Gap for Geneva ICP: Carriers ask for evidence (authorization records, AI decision logs, vendor attestation) but do not provide methodology for producing that evidence. The carrier is the forcing function but not the solution provider.

**Key weakness:** Carriers have created an evidence standard without an accessible evidence production method. The ICP is being measured against documentation requirements that no one has written a how-to guide for.

---

### Generic IT/Security Consultants

**Geneva-based IT consultancies, cybersecurity firms**

- What they offer: Data protection compliance, GDPR/revFADP compliance, IT security audits
- Target: SMEs across all sectors
- Gap for Geneva ICP: Generic IT consultants understand data protection but not attorney-client privilege in AI contexts. They can audit your data flows but cannot advise on carrier attestation evidence requirements or bar guidance compliance.

**Key weakness:** The legal professional obligation dimension (Article 321 CP, LLCA, SBA guidelines) requires someone who understands both legal professional obligations AND AI documentation systems. This intersection is specialist, not generalist.

---

### Nonexistent Market Solutions

**What is NOT available for the Geneva ICP:**

1. **No "small firm AI attestation evidence system"** — no product or service specifically designed to help 2–5 person law firms produce carrier-form-compliant evidence documentation
2. **No "Geneva-standard AI governance" definition** — no one has published what "Geneva-standard" documentation looks like for small firm AI use
3. **No retrospective documentation reconstruction service** — no one offers a methodology for concurrent evidence production that doesn't require stopping billable work
4. **No "Geneva legal AI governance" certification or attestation product** — no independent attestation product that the carrier would accept as evidence equivalent
5. **No junior-senior fluency bridging tool** — nothing designed to help the accountable senior attorney supervise AI output they cannot independently verify

---

## Competitive Positioning Analysis

### Current Market State

The market offers:
- Frameworks (large consulting)
- Tool compliance (legal AI platforms)
- Principles (bar guidance)
- Evidence demands (carriers)

The market does NOT offer:
- Evidence production methodology for small firms
- Geneva-standard documentation definition
- Concurrent documentation reconstruction
- Independent attestation products acceptable to carriers

---

### Positioning Vacancy

> **"The market offers frameworks. We offer evidence."**

C301 identified the positioning vacancy. C302 confirms it is still open.

The Geneva ICP needs evidence reconstruction — not another framework. The evidence must be:
- Producible by a 2–5 person firm without dedicated compliance staff
- Concurrent with operations (not retrospective)
- Geneva-standard (meeting carrier and bar expectations)
- Personal liability-reducing (protecting the signing attorney)

No competitor is offering this. The SaaS legal AI platforms solve tool selection. The consulting firms offer frameworks. The bar offers principles. The carriers demand evidence. The gap is clear.

---

### What Would Convert the ICP

The ICP would convert if offered:

1. **A documentation system that runs concurrent with operations** — not a historical audit that stops work
2. **Evidence reconstruction that covers past decisions without requiring time travel** — a methodology for building backward-looking evidence trails
3. **A Geneva-standard definition they can point to** — something that answers "what does adequate look like for a small firm"
4. **Carrier-compatible attestation evidence** — documentation the carrier would accept as meeting form v4 requirements
5. **Personal liability reduction** — something that demonstrably reduces the signing attorney's personal exposure

---

### Competitive Barriers to Entry

1. **Trust barrier** — the ICP needs to trust that the evidence they produce will actually satisfy carrier requirements; this requires either carrier endorsement or documented precedent
2. **Technical complexity** — building evidence systems that produce carrier-compatible documentation requires specialist knowledge of both AI systems and carrier attestation formats
3. **Conflict with existing vendor relationships** — some legal tech vendors may resist evidence documentation services that expose gaps in their compliance posture

---

### C301 vs C302 Competitive View

**C301 finding:** Positioning vacuum exists. No competitor offers evidence reconstruction.

**C302 finding:** The vacuum persists. The market has not moved to fill it. ODAGE March 21 event confirms demand signal is strong. The June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers (~2 weeks away) is the next acute engagement trigger.

---

## What's Missing (Net-New Finding)

### The Geneva Standard Definition Gap

No one has defined what "Geneva-standard AI governance documentation" means for a 2–5 person law firm. This is:

1. A market gap (no one offers this definition)
2. A trust opportunity (being the first to define Geneva-standard positions you as the authority)
3. A conversion mechanism (once you define the standard, you become the vendor that meets it)

### The Concurrent Evidence Production Gap

The ICP cannot stop working to audit. But they also cannot face carrier renewal without evidence. The market has no solution for concurrent evidence production — building the documentation trail while continuing operations.

### The Independent Attestation Product Gap

The ICP needs something they can show the carrier as evidence of compliance. The market has no independent attestation product specifically designed for small firm AI governance — one that carriers would recognize as equivalent to a formal audit.
