# ICP Language — Geneva Legal (Phase 01, C302)

## ICP Profile
Small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based. Managing partner or senior associate personally accountable for AI decisions. Concerned about attorney-client privilege in AI systems, carrier PI attestation, bar guidance gaps, and what "adequate" AI governance means at boutique scale.

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### The Privilege Framing

The ICP does NOT say "I'm worried about AI governance."

The ICP says:

- *"If I put client information into ChatGPT, who owns it after?"*
- *"My carrier is asking me to sign a form about AI. What exactly am I attesting to?"*
- *"If something goes wrong with an AI-assisted filing, who is liable — me or the software vendor?"*
- *"I don't know what my associates are putting into which tools."*
- *"The bar guidelines say I have to supervise AI output. How do I supervise something I don't understand?"*

**Core concern:** Not abstract AI risk — personal exposure when signing carrier forms or facing a disciplinary complaint.

---

### The Attestation Anxiety

- *"The carrier form now asks about AI authorization records. What records? I don't have those."*
- *"Signing the attestation feels like signing a blank check."*
- *"If I attest and something happens, I'm personally exposed."*
- *"My predecessor signed something — I don't know what the firm actually has."*
- *"I need to know what 'adequate' AI governance means for a two-person firm."*

**The shift:** Carrier forms have moved from policy attestation (do you have a policy?) to evidence attestation (show me your last three authorization records). This is new and creating acute anxiety.

---

### The Junior-Senior Fluency Inversion

- *"My junior associate uses AI tools daily. I don't know what they're using or how."*
- *"I'm the one who signs the forms. She's the one actually using it."*
- *"She tells me 'it's fine, I checked it' but I can't verify what 'it' is."*
- *"I need to supervise but I don't have the technical background to supervise effectively."*

**The inversion:** The person using AI daily is not the person accountable. The accountable person often lacks fluency with the tools in use.

---

### The Documentation Debt Framing

- *"I didn't build these systems. They were here when I joined."*
- *"We started using AI tools organically. There was no documentation at the time."*
- *"Going back to reconstruct decisions from 18 months ago is structurally impossible."*
- *"I know we have a debt. I don't know how to pay it down without stopping all work."*

**The structural problem:** Documentation debt accumulated organically and is now being retroactively demanded by carriers and bar guidance. The ICP cannot reconstruct the past. They can only build forward-looking evidence systems.

---

### The Geneva Label as Signal

- *"We're a Geneva firm. That means something to clients and counterparties."*
- *"If I'm attesting to AI governance, I need to be able to show Geneva-standard documentation."*
- *"Clients ask about our AI tools now. I need a coherent answer."*
- *"The ODAGE event on March 21 — everyone I know is going. It's the only structured guidance available."*

**The signal value:** "Geneva" functions as a quality threshold marker. ICPs understand they need to demonstrate Geneva-standard governance but don't know what that documentation looks like.

---

### The Vendor Contract Trap Language

- *"We signed the vendor agreement. It seemed standard."*
- *"Now I realize the vendor can retain our data for training. That wasn't highlighted."*
- *"We've been using that tool for two years. I can't unwind the past."*
- *"If we switch vendors, what happens to our historical data?"*
- *"The contract trap is real — I can't get equivalent coverage from another vendor because they all have different terms."*

**The trap:** Vendor terms were accepted before the privilege implications were understood. Now switching doesn't erase the historical data exposure, and new contracts don't resolve it either.

---

## Language to Avoid (Not How They Talk)

- ❌ "AI governance framework"
- ❌ "algorithmic accountability"
- ❌ "AI risk management framework"
- ❌ "responsible AI deployment"
- ❌ "AI compliance posture"

These are consultant language. The ICP does not use them.

---

## Language to Use (How They Actually Talk)

- ✅ "carrier attestation"
- ✅ "what I'm signing"
- ✅ "authorization records"
- ✅ "who approved this AI decision"
- ✅ "what my associate used"
- ✅ "documentation debt"
- ✅ "Geneva-standard governance"
- ✅ "privilege waiver risk"
- ✅ "personal exposure"
- ✅ "supervision without fluency"

---

## Key ICP Frustration Points

1. **The form asks for evidence they don't have** — carrier form v4 now requires "last three authorization records" as evidence standard, not policy attestation

2. **The accountable person lacks technical fluency** — senior partner signs, junior associate uses daily

3. **Retroactive documentation is structurally impossible** — systems were built without audit trails; cannot reconstruct

4. **Vendor contracts cannot be unwound** — terms accepted before privilege implications understood; switching doesn't resolve historical data

5. **No clear "Geneva standard" definition** — ODAGE event March 21 shows demand for guidance; supply is thin

6. **Peer enforcement is the conversion trigger** — not regulatory awareness; documented disciplinary cases (AI legal citations) convert abstract concern into action
