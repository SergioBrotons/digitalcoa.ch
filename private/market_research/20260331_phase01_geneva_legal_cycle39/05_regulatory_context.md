## Active Regulatory Framework (Operative Now)

### FADP (Swiss Data Protection Act — nFADP)
- **Effective:** September 1, 2023 ✅ (2.5+ years operative)
- **AI Relevance:** Art. 5(c) Privacy by Design/Default; Art. 21 Right to human review for automated decisions producing legal effects; DPIA for high-risk AI processing
- **Enforcement:** FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)
- **Penalty Architecture:** Criminal route only (no direct fines under nFADP); CHF 250,000 for intentional failures
- **FDPIC Enforcement Gap:** Legal sector absent from 1,053 complaints + 6 enforcement actions (transport, banking, retail, social media)

### EU AI Act (For EU-Nexus Firms)
- **Effective:** August 2, 2025 (GPAI); August 2, 2026 (high-risk) ✅ GPAI operative
- **AI Relevance:** Extraterritorial for Swiss firms with EU nexus (clients, operations, products/services)
- **Classification:** Annex I (prohibited), Annex III (high-risk), Annex II (standard-risk)
- **Obligations:** Transparency, human oversight, technical documentation, conformity assessment for high-risk

### Council of Europe AI Convention
- **Signed:** March 2025 ✅
- **Incorporation:** Swiss legislation expected end-2026
- **Nature:** Human rights, democracy, rule of law framework
- **Relevance:** Baseline for future Swiss AI law; adds accountability + oversight obligations

### GPAI (AI Act — General Purpose AI)
- **Operative:** August 2, 2025 ✅ (~8 months active)
- **Relevance:** GPAI model providers' obligations (transparency, technical documentation)
- **End-User Impact:**间接 — law firms using GPAI tools must provide API对接 information

## FADP Compliance Stack (For Geneva Law Firms)

### Layer 1: Art. 5(c) Privacy by Design & Default
- AI tool selection: FADP-compliant tools only
- Default settings: highest privacy
- Data minimization: only necessary data
- **Status:** Operative since Sep 1, 2023

### Layer 2: Art. 21 Right to Human Review
- Automated decisions producing legal effects must allow human review
- AI-assisted contract analysis, due diligence, legal opinions = likely covered
- **Status:** Operative since Sep 1, 2023
- **Note:** No implementing regulation yet; principle active

### Layer 3: DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)
- Required for high-risk AI processing (Art. 22 nFADP)
- Contract review, due diligence AI = likely high-risk
- **Status:** Operative since Sep 1, 2023

### Layer 4: Transparency Obligations
- Disclose AI use to clients/counterparties when relevant
- FADP Art. 6 lawful basis + Art. 8 transparency
- **Status:** Operative since Sep 1, 2023

### Layer 5: Data Processor Agreements
- AI tool vendors = data processors
- Written DPA required under FADP
- **Status:** Operative since Sep 1, 2023

- **Source:** FADP Art. 21 + FDPIC guidance
