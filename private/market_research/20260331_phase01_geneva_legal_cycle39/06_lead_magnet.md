## Framework Structure (9 Sections)

### Section 1: Shadow AI Audit — Decision 0
*"How much AI is already running through your firm without formal governance?"*

- Inventory of all AI tools used by attorneys and staff
- FADP Art. 21 human review exposure
- Risk classification: which uses produce "legal effects"
- Exit criterion: complete AI inventory with FADP classification

**Why Decision 0:** Shadow AI is the entry point — most small law firms already have AI in use. Audit before building policy.

---

### Section 2: The 28% Problem — Quantified Governance Gap
*"28% of firms ≤50 lawyers have formally adopted GenAI. ~90%+ are using it individually. Here is what the 62-point gap means for you."*

- 28% formal GenAI adoption (Thomson Reuters 2025)
- ~90%+ individual AI use (Wolters Kluwer 2026)
- 43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026)
- 35-46 point governance gap quantified
- Competitive advantage of early governance

**Why This Works:** Quantified gap creates urgency without blame. Most firms are in the same position.

---

### Section 3: FADP Art. 21 — Your Most Urgent Already-Operative Obligation
*"Art. 21 FADP has been operative since September 1, 2023. Your AI-assisted legal opinions may already require human review."*

- Art. 21 FADP: right to human review for automated decisions producing legal effects
- Contract analysis, due diligence, legal opinions = likely covered
- Operational requirements: how to provide human review in practice
- Documentation: what clients can request

**Why Art. 21 First:** Already operative (2.5 years). No grace period. No notice required.

---

### Section 4: Privacy by Design AI Procurement
*"Before adopting any new AI tool: FADP Art. 5(c) requires Privacy by Design. Here is the procurement checklist."*

- FADP Art. 5(c): Privacy by Design + Default obligations
- AI vendor due diligence: Swiss-hosted? EU AI Act compliant? FADP processor agreement?
- CASUS: Swiss-sovereign zero data retention alternative
- Tool classification: high-risk vs. standard-risk under EU AI Act
- Data processor agreement template requirements

**Why Procurement:** Most firms will add AI tools in the next 12 months. Better to govern the acquisition than retrofit later.

---

### Section 5: DPIA for High-Risk AI Processing
*"Contract review and due diligence AI likely qualifies as high-risk processing under FADP Art. 22. Here is the Geneva law firm-specific DPIA framework."*

- FADP Art. 22: DPIA required for high-risk AI
- Legal workflow AI = likely high-risk (contract review, due diligence, legal research)
- Geneva law firm DPIA: 8-step framework adapted from FDPIC guidelines
- When DPIA is required vs. when privacy by design is sufficient

**Why DPIA Now:** Approaching regulatory deadlines make this obligation increasingly visible. Early completion = competitive differentiator.

---

- Governance as efficiency protection, not bureaucratic overhead
