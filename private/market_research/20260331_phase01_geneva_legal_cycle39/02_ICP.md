# ICP Definition — Phase 01 Cycle 39
## Geneva Small Law Firm AI Governance

---

## Primary ICP (Legal — Unchanged from Cycle 38)

### Firmographics
- **Size:** 1-50 lawyers (solo practitioners to boutique)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland (canton Geneva)
- **Sector:** Legal services (law firms, not in-house counsel)
- **AI Status:** Actively using AI tools (individual level) without formal firm-wide governance policy
- **Revenue Relevance:** AI tools used for: contract review, legal research, due diligence, client communications, billing/practice management

### Psychographics / Decision Drivers
- **Primary Fear:** FDPIC enforcement + professional liability exposure from unmanaged AI use (Shadow AI)
- **Secondary Fear:** Client expectations evolving toward AI-augmented services without governance framework
- **Timing Sensitivity:** High — approaching May 2026 Federal Council First Draft and EU AI Act August 2, 2026 deadlines
- **Tech Sophistication:** Moderate to high (individuals using AI tools daily; firms not capturing this under formal governance)
- **Compliance Sophistication:** Low to moderate (FADP awareness; limited understanding of AI-specific obligations)

### Buying Triggers
1. Direct FDPIC enforcement signal in legal sector (currently absent — creates false sense of safety)
2. Peer law firms adopting formal AI policies (network effect)
3. Client RFPs or due diligence questionnaires mentioning AI governance
4. Media coverage of AI errors/hallucinations in legal contexts
5. Swissdamed July 1, 2026 spillover awareness (other sectors creating urgency)
6. Geneva AI Action Summit 2027 (longer-term positioning incentive)

### Barriers to Adoption
- Perceived cost vs. uncertain ROI
- Lack of SMB-accessible advisory (enterprise-only competitive landscape)
- "No enforcement in legal sector" — false confidence
- Unclear FADP obligations for AI-specific use cases
- Resistance to formalizing what individuals already do informally

### Addressable Market Signal
- 200-350 Geneva law firms (≤50 lawyers, AI-using, no formal policy) — estimate
- 43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans (Wolters Kluwer Mar 2026) across all firm sizes
- 28% formal GenAI adoption (≤50 lawyers, Thomson Reuters 2025) vs. ~90%+ actual individual use = governance gap quantifiable
- 10th+ competitive absence confirmed (FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL only SMB-accessible boutique)

- **Key Difference:** More likely to have some governance; higher EU AI Act exposure (Annex III high-risk)

- **SBA AI Guidelines Jun 2024:** Non-binding baseline for professional conduct
