# Phase 05 — Brotons.net Audit | Cycle 94
**Content Angles**

## For DigitalCoa.ch

### Angle 1: "The Hub Model — When a Landing Page Is the Right Architecture"
- **Hook:** Brotons.net is intentionally a single landing page routing to three properties. This is a deliberate lead-routing strategy, not a content gap.
- **Core argument:** for a personal brand with distinct service lines (advisory, implementation, writing), hub-and-spoke can be the right model
- **CTA:** confirm whether DigitalCoa.ch should absorb more of the Brotons.net routing function
- **Tone:** calm, analytical

### Angle 2: "Brotons.net Strategic Question: Hub or Content Platform?"
- **Hook:** No blog, no articles on-domain. Is this a deliberate choice or an opportunity being left on the table?
- **Core argument:** SEO footprint is zero; Substack content is discoverable but on third-party domain; on-domain content would improve authority signals
- **CTA:** owner strategic decision needed
- **Tone:** constructive, not critical

## For Brotons.net

### Angle 3: "Clarity Under Constraint — The Framework as Content"
- **Hook:** The writing lives on Substack. What if the frameworks themselves were hosted on Brotons.net as permanent reference content?
- **Core argument:** Substack is transient; on-domain content is permanent and discoverable; the frameworks are the intellectual property worth owning on-domain
- **CTA:** decision on whether to migrate or duplicate key frameworks to Brotons.net
- **Tone:** Brotons voice — thoughtful, forward-looking
