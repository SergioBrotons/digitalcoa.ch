## Core Insight: Phase 02 (C312 Finance)

The boutique WM managing director is managing dual accountability tracks — institutional client DDQ and carrier attestation — with no Geneva-calibrated methodology for either. The same structural problems identified in Phase 01 (carrier form architecture, unauthorized delegation, vendor compliance assumption, "adequate" undefined) are now being felt through the institutional client channel. The institutional DDQ is a competitive intelligence document. No one is reading it as such. The authorization chain was never documented. Model drift monitoring is now a live obligation. Cross-mandate coherence is becoming externally visible. The Phase 01 finding ("my solution was built to the wrong specification") extends here: the boutique built firm-level policy documentation; the institutional client is asking about mandate-specific authorization chain evidence.

**Phase 01 (C312 legal) → Phase 02 (C312 finance) synthesis:** The carrier form and the institutional DDQ are parallel forcing functions. Both create personal accountability. Both require pre-document reading. Both produce evidence the MD is personally accountable for. The evidence architecture is the same; the intelligence is different.

## 6 NET-NEW Findings (Phase 02 — Distinct from Phase 01)

### Finding 01: Institutional DDQ as portfolio-specific competitive gate activating cross-mandate exposure visibility

**The problem:** Managing multiple institutional mandates creates documentation consistency obligations across mandates that boutique WM firms did not design their AI governance for. Each institutional client asked slightly different questions; the boutique answered independently; cross-mandate inconsistencies are now visible.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You have four institutional mandates. They all use AI differently. The client wants to know about your AI governance — but which one?"

**IMO intervention:** Cross-mandate coherence assessment — evaluating AI governance documentation consistency across multiple institutional mandates.

---

### Finding 02: Portfolio construction AI authorization chain documentation gap creates compound evidence problem

**The problem:** The authorization chain — what AI recommended, what the PM reviewed with specific known information, what the investment committee approved, when — is not documented. When institutional clients or carriers ask for evidence of human authorization, the boutique cannot produce it.

**Ready-to-use language:** "We have an investment committee. AI generates ideas. We discuss them. We approve or modify. But I can't document what the AI actually recommended at each step, and what I specifically knew when I approved."

**IMO intervention:** Portfolio construction authorization chain documentation architecture — proportionate to boutique investment committee, evidence that survives institutional client inquiry.

---

### Finding 03: Model drift monitoring obligation now activated through institutional DDQ re-review practice

**The problem:** Institutional clients that received 2024 DDQ responses are now conducting re-reviews asking specifically for model drift monitoring evidence. The 12-24 months of undocumented AI operation includes undocumented model monitoring. This is now a visible evidence gap.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Our big client asked us to update our AI governance questionnaire response. One of the new questions is about model monitoring. We don't have a documented monitoring system."

**IMO intervention:** Model drift monitoring documentation architecture — building a proportionate monitoring documentation system that satisfies institutional re-review requirements.

---

### Finding 04: Carrier attestation renewal including third-generation vendor processor chain questions creating undisclosed nFADP Art.9 exposure

**The problem:** Third-generation carrier attestation now asks about vendor sub-processors, data termination protocols, and model training data use. Vendor click-through terms created undisclosed processing the boutique accepted without evaluation. Carrier interrogation is activating Art.9 obligations the boutique didn't know existed.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The carrier renewal form asked about our AI vendors' sub-processors. I didn't know we were supposed to know that."

**IMO intervention:** Vendor portfolio construction contract evaluation — discrete service surfacing undisclosed model training data use and processor chain terms.

---

### Finding 05: Multi-mandate boutique WM firms creating cross-institutional-client coherence exposure now visible through Geneva peer network

**The problem:** Geneva's tight professional network means institutional clients are comparing DDQ responses across boutiques. Inconsistent responses reveal that boutiques responded to each DDQ independently without cross-document coherence methodology.

**Ready-to-use language:** "We have three institutional clients. They all asked slightly different questions. We answered each one separately. I don't know if the responses are consistent."

**IMO intervention:** Institutional DDQ pre-reading as competitive intelligence — extracting what standard the client is applying before responding, enabling calibrated responses.

---

### Finding 06: Institutional client AI governance DDQ reading as competitive intelligence practice unnamed in boutique WM market

**The problem:** No Geneva boutique WM firm is using the institutional DDQ as competitive intelligence systematically. The firms that read the DDQ before responding gain competitive positioning advantages — knowing what the client actually wants, calibrating responses to the client's specific concern set, understanding where the client's standard is moving. Firms that answer as compliance exercise miss this.

**Ready-to-use language:** "I started reading the DDQ as a document about what the client actually wants to know — not just a checklist of questions. That changed how I think about responding."

**IMO intervention:** Institutional DDQ pre-reading as competitive intelligence service — highest-value intervention point, parallel to Phase 01 carrier form pre-signing review.
