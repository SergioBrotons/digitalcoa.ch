## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### Primary Language Register — Boutique WM-Specific

**Register 1 — Institutional Accountability Lingua Franca**
- "Our institutional client sent us a 40-page AI governance questionnaire. Forty pages."
- "They want to know how we use AI in portfolio construction. I can describe what we do. I can't document it."
- "Our client's investment committee is asking questions about AI methodology. They're comparing us to other managers."
- "We answered the questionnaire last year. Now they want an updated response. I don't know what's changed."
- "The DDQ has become a competitive intelligence document. I didn't think of it that way until recently."

**Register 2 — Personal Liability Lingua Franca**
- "My name is on the door. The moment AI was involved in a client portfolio decision, my name is on that decision."
- "If the model drift goes the wrong way and AI was involved — what did I know, when did I know it, and can I prove I reviewed it?"
- "I'm the signatory. I can't subdivide my accountability."
- "The carrier form asks me to attest to controls I don't have a system to verify. I signed it anyway."
- "Article 63 — that's for data breaches. But what if AI-assisted portfolio construction causes client harm? Is that different?"

**Register 3 — Proportionality Lingua Franca**
- "We're 65 people. We don't have a chief compliance officer. I do everything."
- "Whatever we build has to work for our investment committee — two partners, one analyst, me."
- "The enterprise framework is designed for a 2,000-person institution. We're 65."
- "I don't need to solve AI governance for Credit Suisse. I need to solve it for our 12 client mandates."
- "Proportionate doesn't mean inadequate. It means designed for our specific situation."

## What Triggers Concern vs. What Drives Action

### Concern Triggers (Anxiety into Action)
- **Institutional client DDQ arrival** — concrete, time-bound, competitive consequence attached
- **Carrier attestation renewal** — personal signature required; MD must attest personally
- **Peer Geneva WM firm DDQ outcome** — hearing that another boutique lost a mandate due to DDQ response
- **Model performance incident with AI involvement** — near-miss making the authorization chain concrete
- **Institutional client re-review request** — 2024 response is being re-examined in 2026

### Action Barriers (Why Action Stalls)
- "I don't know what I committed to in last year's DDQ response" — documentation amnesia
- "The DDQ and the carrier form are asking different questions" — no cross-document coherence methodology
- "Whatever I build has to work for our specific investment committee structure" — proportionality concern
- "Our vendor handles AI governance" — vendor compliance assumption creating hidden exposure (per Phase 01 finding)
- "The standard keeps moving" — documentation standard escalation faster than documentation cycle

## Bilingual Notes (EN + FR)

### EN — Primary Register
Geneva boutique WM firms operate in English for institutional client communications, client portals, and investment reporting. French is default for regulatory filings, SRO relationships, and Geneva-specific networking. The DDQ arrives in English. The carrier form is in English. The FINMA guidance is available in all four languages. Bilingual navigation is a daily operational reality, not a localization task.

### FR — Geneva Register
French-language regulatory communications carry distinct weight in Geneva. SRO interactions, local professional networking, and Geneva Chamber of Commerce context operate in French. The FR register for AI governance must be precise without being technical — "gouvernance IA proportionnée" rather than technical AI terminology.

### Key Bilingual Terminology

| EN | FR | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Institutional DDQ | Questionnaire d'évaluation IA du client | "DDQ" widely used in EN; FR needs full term |
| AI governance readiness | Maturité de gouvernance IA | Proportionate, not over-technical |
| Model drift monitoring | Surveillance des dérivations du modèle | Technical but accessible in context |
| Authorization chain | Chaîne d'autorisation | Sequential human review documentation |
| Carrier attestation | Attestation de l'assureur | Insurance underwriting instrument |
| Portfolio construction AI | IA dans la construction de portefeuille | Core WM use case |
| Personal liability | Responsabilité personnelle | MD-specific; Art.63 framing |
| Proportionate framework | Cadre proportionné | "Adapté à la taille du cabinet" |
| Documentation window | Fenêtre de documentation | Tactical positioning term |
