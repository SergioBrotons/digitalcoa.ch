## Key Regulatory Developments (C124)

### 1. CBAM Definitive Regime (EU) — Active January 2026
- EU CBAM transitional phase ended 1 January 2026; definitive regime operational
- Importers of steel, aluminum, cement, fertilizers, electricity, hydrogen required to declare embedded emissions
- Swiss parallel SVUT under parliamentary debate; not yet enacted
- Geneva impact: ~40 commodity trading houses with CBAM-relevant imports

### 2. Swiss Digital Notarial Framework — NGNotV Revision
- Civil Code Art. 55ff ZGB amended 2024 enabling digital notarial acts
- NGNotV revision in consultation; expected completion H2 2026
- Geneva pilot programs: remote notarization already in trial use
- Geneva Chamber of Notaries preparing members; training ongoing

### 3. BGFA Art. 28 FOJ Consultation — Q2 2026 Closing
- FOJ consultation on third-country lawyer admission opened January 2026
- Key question: whether to extend EU/EFTA-equivalent treatment to UK post-Brexit
- Geneva has highest concentration of third-country qualified lawyers in Switzerland
- IBA Geneva 2026 conference (October) addressing cross-jurisdictional practice

### 4. Switzerland-EU Institutional Agreement — Data Transfer Provisions
- Negotiations concluded December 2024; pending ratification
- Data transfer provisions include litigation discovery carve-outs
- GDPR adequacy for Switzerland remains valid but with gaps
- FADP revision (2023) partially aligned with GDPR but differences persist

### 5. Commercial Court of Geneva Digital Mandate — Active January 2026
- Digital case management system (JUSTICE) launched October 2025
- Electronic filing mandatory for all new commercial matters > CHF 50,000
- Migration of existing cases ongoing through H1 2026

### 6. CHF/USD Volatility — Market Trigger
- No specific regulation; however, force majeure and re-opener clauses interpreted against Swiss contract law (OR Art. 19/20)
- ICC and SCAI arbitration rules govern commodity disputes
- SNB policy implications: SNB rate decisions affecting CHF/USD
