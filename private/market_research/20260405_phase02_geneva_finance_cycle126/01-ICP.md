## Pain Clusters (C126 Fresh Angles)

### Pain 1: Stablecoin and Digital Currency Integration
Geneva commodity traders and financial institutions face decisions on whether to integrate CHF-stablecoins (e.g., planned CHC stablecoin) into treasury operations, FX settlement, and trade finance workflows. Unclear regulatory pathway under FINMA's evolving DLT Act guidance creates implementation uncertainty.

**Trigger questions:**
- Should we hold CHF-stablecoins for intra-day liquidity management?
- What are FINMA's reserve and custody requirements for stablecoin holdings?
- How do we account for stablecoin positions under FINMA/PCAOB standards?

### Pain 2: Repo Market Collateral Optimization
Geneva asset managers face pressure to optimize collateral efficiency as repo market infrastructure evolves (SIX x-clear/Euroclear interoperability, SFAMA repo standardization). Rising collateral demand from clearing mandates creates operational burden.

**Trigger questions:**
- How do we structure our collateral inventory for repo financing efficiency?
- What is the impact of SOFR/CHF hybrid yield curves on repo pricing?
- Are there operational advantages to moving from bilateral to centrally cleared repo?

### Pain 3: Post-Credit Suisse Resolution Oversight Burden
FINMA's evolved supervisory expectations post-2025 CS resolution create compliance burden for Geneva financial institutions (G-SIBs, systemically relevant institutions). Higher loss-absorbing capacity requirements, resolvability assessments, and contingency planning demand specialized advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- What are the updated TBTF 2.0 capital and LAC requirements under FINMA's revised expectations?
- How do we document resolvability for FINMA's supervisory assessment?
- What are the operational implications of FINMA's new early intervention framework?

### Pain 4: EUR/CHF Volatility and FX Hedging
EUR/CHF implied volatility at elevated levels (2024-2026 period of uncertainty around ECB/SNB divergence) creates hedging costs and risks for Geneva commodity traders with EUR-denominated costs and CHF reporting. Options-based hedging programs require specialist advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- What hedging instruments are appropriate given our EUR exposure and cost structure?
- How do we optimize our FX hedging program given current vol environment?
- Should we consider cross-currency swaps or FX options for longer-dated exposures?

### Pain 5: Swiss Pension Fund Infrastructure Allocation
BVG reform creates second-pillar investment capacity for infrastructure debt. Geneva-based pension funds and collective foundations face decisions on infrastructure debt as a new allocation category, requiring advisor support on structuring, documentation, and monitoring.

**Trigger questions:**
- What is the appropriate allocation to infrastructure debt under BVG reform parameters?
- How do we structure documentation and monitoring for infrastructure debt mandates?
- Which Geneva-based or Geneva-adjacent infrastructure projects are suitable for our risk profile?

### Pain 6: Wholesale CBDC Cross-Border Settlement
BIS Project Agora's Geneva-Singapore corridor creates FX settlement opportunities for Geneva trading houses. Early adopters gain operational efficiency in correspondent banking; late adopters risk being excluded from evolving settlement infrastructure.

**Trigger questions:**
- What is the operational readiness required to participate in wholesale CBDC settlement?
- How does wholesale CBDC affect our correspondent banking relationships?
- What are the accounting and regulatory reporting implications of CBDC holdings?
