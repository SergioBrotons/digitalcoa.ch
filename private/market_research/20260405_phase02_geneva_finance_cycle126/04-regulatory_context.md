## Regulatory Context: Key Frameworks for Geneva Finance Advisory

---

### 1. FINMA DLT Act — Stablecoin Authorization Framework

**Authority:** FINMA (Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority)
**Governing Law:** Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FinfraG) + DLT-specific guidance
**Status as of 2026:** FINMA has published stablecoin authorization guidance (2024-2025), with specific reserve custody, audit, and investor protection requirements for CHF-backed stablecoins authorized in Switzerland.

**Key Requirements:**
- Reserve assets: High-quality, liquid assets (cash, short-term government securities) at minimum 100% of issued stablecoin value
- Custody: Segregated custody at Swiss-regulated bank or securities firm
- Audit: Annual third-party audit of reserve assets by FINMA-recognized auditor
- Investor protection: Clear redemption rights and procedures
- AML: Full AML compliance per FINMA's AML Ordinance

**Geneva Relevance:** Commodity trading firms and Geneva financial institutions exploring stablecoin treasury integration need advisory on how to structure stablecoin pilot programs compliant with FINMA's framework.

**EN:** "FINMA's DLT Act stablecoin framework establishes clear authorization requirements for CHF-stablecoins — Geneva institutions need advisory on structuring compliant pilot programs before full-scale treasury integration."
**FR:** "Le cadre stablecoin de la FINMA basé sur la loi DLT établit des exigences claires d'autorisation pour les stablecoins en CHF — les institutions genevoises ont besoin d'un conseil pour structurer des programmes pilotes conformes avant une intégration complète dans la trésorerie."

### 2. FINMA TBTF 2.0 / Resolution Planning Framework

**Authority:** FINMA + Federal Council
**Governing Law:** Swiss Too-Big-To-Fail Act (TBTF) + FINMA Resolution Ordinance
**Status as of 2026:** Updated TBTF 2.0 framework published, with higher loss-absorbing capacity requirements and enhanced resolvability assessment methodology following the 2025 Credit Suisse resolution.

**Key Requirements:**
- Minimum Required Eligible Liabilities (MREL): Higher thresholds for G-SIBs and D-SIBs operating in Switzerland
- Internal Loss-Absorbing Capacity (ILAC): Intragroup loss-absorbing capacity must be documented and tested
- Recovery and Resolution Plans (RRP): Annual update and FINMA review
- Resolvability assessments: Qualitative and quantitative assessment of resolution feasibility
- Early intervention triggers: Defined thresholds triggering mandatory remediation

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva-based systemically relevant financial institutions (6-8 G-SIBs/D-SIBs with Geneva presence, ~15-20 additional institutions subject to enhanced expectations) need support in preparing compliant resolvability assessments and recovery planning documentation.

**EN:** "FINMA's TBTF 2.0 framework raises the bar for resolvability documentation and ILAC structuring — Geneva's systemically relevant institutions face specific documentation and assessment requirements that create specialist advisory demand."
**FR:** "Le cadre TBTF 2.0 de la FINMA relève le niveau d'exigence pour la documentation de résolvabilité et la structuration ILAC — les institutions systémiques genevoises font face à des exigences spécifiques de documentation et d'évaluation qui créent une demande de conseil spécialisé."

### 3. BVG Reform — Second Pillar Investment Parameters

**Authority:** Federal Council + Occupational Benefits Commission (OBC)
**Governing Law:** Occupational Pension, Provident and Insolvency Insurance Act (BVG) + BVG-OO (Ordinance)
**Status as of 2026:** BVG reform implementation ongoing; new investment parameter guidelines published by OBC; Geneva collective foundations (institutions de prévoyance) adjusting investment policies accordingly.

**Key Changes Relevant to Geneva Finance Advisory:**
- Infrastructure debt: Expanded allowable allocation categories for second-pillar institutions
- Alternative investments: Enhanced risk disclosure requirements for non-traditional allocations
- ESG integration: Mandatory ESG risk consideration in investment policy statements (IPS)
- Duration matching: Updated liability-matching requirements for pension fund asset-liability management

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva-based pension funds and collective foundations structuring new infrastructure debt mandates need advisory on IPS language, manager due diligence, structuring documentation, and ongoing monitoring frameworks.

**EN:** "BVG reform implementation creates new investment parameter guidelines that expand Geneva pension fund allocation capacity to infrastructure debt — requiring updated investment policy statements, due diligence frameworks, and monitoring procedures."
**FR:** "La mise en œuvre de la réforme LPP crée de nouveaux paramètres d'investissement qui élargissent la capacité d'allocation des caisses de pension genevoises à la dette infrastructure — nécessitant des déclarations de politique de placement mises à jour, des cadres de due diligence et des procédures de suivi."

### 4. SIX x-clear / Euroclear Repo Clearing — SFAMA Standardization

**Authority:** SIX x-clear + Euroclear + SFAMA (Swiss Funds and Asset Management Association)
**Governing Law:** FinfraG (Financial Market Infrastructure Act) + ISDA/GMRA documentation standards
**Status as of 2026:** SFAMA repo standardization initiative in implementation phase; SIX x-clear and Euroclear interoperability expanded; migration from bilateral to centrally cleared repo ongoing for Geneva asset managers.

**Key Requirements:**
- Central counterparty clearing (CCP) for standardized repo contracts
- Margin requirements under clearing house rules (initial margin + variation margin)
- GMRA/ISDA documentation updates for centrally cleared repo
- Collateral eligibility criteria under CCP rules (SOFR/CHF hybrid yield curve)

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva asset managers with repo-financed securities lending programs need advisory on clearing migration strategy, collateral optimization, margin model implications, and legal documentation updates.

**EN:** "SFAMA repo standardization and SIX/Euroclear interoperability expansion are driving Geneva asset managers toward centrally cleared repo — requiring legal documentation updates, collateral optimization advisory, and margin model reassessment."
**FR:** "La normalisation des pensions SFAMA et l'expansion de l'interopérabilité SIX/Euroclear poussent les gérants d'actifs genevois vers des pensions livrées centralisées — nécessitant des mises à jour de documentation juridique, un conseil en optimisation des garanties et une réévaluation des modèles de marge."

### 5. BIS Project Agora — Wholesale CBDC Framework

**Authority:** BIS (Bank for International Settlements) + SNB + MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore)
**Governing Law:** No specific Swiss legislation yet; operating under SNB experimental framework
**Status as of 2026:** Project Agora wholesale CBDC corridor between Switzerland and Singapore is operational (Q1 2026 milestone); designated Swiss and Singapore financial institutions participating in live FX settlement transactions.

**Key Framework Elements:**
- FX settlement (SGD/CHF, USD/CHF cross-currency settlement)
- Trade finance settlement (pilot phase)
- Correspondent banking replacement (direct CBDC settlement between participating institutions)
- No retail e-franc; wholesale only (institutional participants)

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva commodity trading houses and financial institutions evaluating or already participating in the Agora corridor need operational readiness advisory, treasury system integration guidance, and strategic assessment of CBDC corridor expansion.

**EN:** "BIS Project Agora's live Geneva-Singapore wholesale CBDC corridor establishes a new FX settlement paradigm — participating Geneva institutions need operational readiness assessment and treasury integration advisory as the corridor expands."
**FR:** "Le corridor CBDC de gros opérationnel BIS Project Agora entre Genève et Singapour établit un nouveau paradigme de règlement FX — les institutions genevoises participantes ont besoin d'une évaluation de préparation opérationnelle et d'un conseil en intégration de trésorerie alors que le corridor s'élargit."
