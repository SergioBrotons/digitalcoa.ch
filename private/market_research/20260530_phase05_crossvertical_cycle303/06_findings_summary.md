# Findings Summary: Cross-Vertical — C303 Phase 05

## 6 NET-NEW Cross-Vertical Findings: Distinct from C214–C302

### Finding 01 — The Universal Documentation Debt Structure Is Now Named

**The finding:** The documentation debt is not sector-specific. Across all four verticals — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — the structure is identical: tools adopted without decision events → no records created → debt accumulates silently → forcing functions arrive (carrier renewal, client questionnaire, June 19 event) → ICP cannot produce evidence → personal liability exposed. The structure is universal and structural.

**Mechanism:** Tools adopted via vendor push updates, feature rollouts, or unsolicited service term acceptances. No go/no-go decision occurred. No documentation created. The gap accumulated invisibly. Now carrier form evolution makes the gap visible and evidence-required.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same structure. Legal (carrier PI attestation), finance (carrier WM attestation), consulting (carrier attestation + client questionnaire), healthcare (carrier attestation + FMH oversight). All have the same documentation debt.

**Implication:** The service that addresses this is not vertical-specific. It is cross-vertical. The universal entry point is the attestation debt audit.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You have a documentation debt. You didn't create it consciously — it accumulated through tools you adopted without documenting the decision. Now the carrier is asking for evidence. I can show you what you have and what you need."

### Finding 02 — Carrier Form v4 Marked the Transition from Policy Standard to Evidence Standard

**The finding:** Carrier forms have shifted from asking "do you have a policy?" to asking "provide your last three authorization records." This is a fundamental change in what carriers are actually requiring. The ICP who built a policy in response to v1/v2 forms now finds that v4 requires something structurally different — contemporaneous records of actual decisions, not a policy document.

**Mechanism:** Carrier form evolution: v1 (policy acknowledgment) → v2 (policy + general AI governance) → v3 (specific controls) → v4 (evidence of authorization records). Each iteration narrows what counts. The v4 ICP cannot answer with a policy they built in v1 — they need records from the moment of adoption.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** All four verticals are subject to the same carrier form evolution. The timing varies (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare each at different stages of the evolution), but the destination is identical.

**Implication:** The service is not "build a policy." The service is "build an evidence system." The policy was the wrong target from the beginning.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your carrier isn't asking for a policy anymore. They're asking for records. That's a different problem."

### Finding 03 — The Vendor Contract Trap Is a Governance Architecture Constraint, Not a Compliance Issue

**The finding:** The vendor contract trap — data portability limitations, model training data retention, termination provisions — is named as a governance architecture constraint, not as a compliance violation. The ICP who wants to improve governance by switching vendors cannot do so because the commercial terms prevent it. This is not a legal problem to be solved; it is an architectural constraint to be designed around.

**Mechanism:** Vendor contracts written to protect vendor interests (model training data, termination friction, data portability limits). Years of click-through acceptance accumulated obligations. The exit barrier is structural — switching vendors doesn't undo past exposure and doesn't guarantee future compliance.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical uses AI tools. Every vertical has vendor contracts with similar terms. The exit barrier is universal.

**Implication:** The service is not "help me switch vendors." The service is "help me build evidence for what I have." Vendor exit planning is a prerequisite to governance improvement, not the governance improvement itself.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You can't switch your way out of this. The tools you signed up for have trapped your data and your governance options. What you can do is build forward from where you are."

### Finding 04 — Peer Enforcement Is the Conversion Trigger, Not Regulatory Awareness

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, peer enforcement events — a Geneva attorney questioned by bar, a wealth manager fined for sub-processor violations, a consulting firm losing a client over methodology provenance, a physician facing carrier scrutiny — convert faster and more durably than regulatory awareness or abstract liability arguments. The mechanism is personal identification: "that could be me."

**Mechanism:** Peer event occurs → ICP recognizes themselves in the scenario → personal fear activates → action taken to avoid being the person without documentation in a peer context. The ICP doesn't need to understand the regulation. They need to see themselves in the story.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The conversion trigger is identical across all four verticals. The specific peer events differ (bar disciplinary, carrier fine, client loss, license threat), but the psychological mechanism is the same.

**Implication:** Outreach that names peer enforcement events converts better than outreach that leads with regulatory language. The entry point is the story, not the framework.

**Ready-to-use language:** "There's a Geneva attorney who just had to explain to the bar why he couldn't produce the AI governance records the carrier asked for. I helped him rebuild. You probably don't want to wait until it's you."

### Finding 05 — The Documentation Paradox Blocks Action Even When Willingness Exists

**The finding:** The ICP across all four verticals is not unwilling — they are blocked. They understand they need documentation, have the intent to build it, but cannot produce the first document because no proportionate template exists for their firm size and practice type. The blank-page paralysis is structural, not attitudinal.

**Mechanism:** Existing frameworks (ISO 27001, NIST, etc.) require documentation infrastructure that a 2–50 person practice cannot produce. The ICP is caught between ignoring the framework (feels risky) and building the full framework (feels impossible). The gap between intent and action is the documentation paradox.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same blank-page paralysis. The legal ICP, finance ICP, consulting ICP, and healthcare ICP all face the same documentation architecture problem at their scale.

**Implication:** A proportionate documentation template set — calibrated to 2–50 person professional firms, specific to each vertical — is the missing tool. The template is not a policy template. It is a decision record architecture.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The problem isn't willingness. The problem is you don't know what the document should contain. I can show you — specific to your firm size and practice type."

### Finding 06 — Strategic Classification Deferral Accumulates Risk, Doesn't Reduce It

**The finding:** The ICP who defers Swissdamed classification determination (or any regulatory classification decision) believing they are reducing exposure is accumulating risk. Deferral doesn't create a grace period — it creates a period of operating without classification, which has its own regulatory consequences. The classification decision itself requires documenting the tools that trigger the classification — which means the documentation debt grows while classification is deferred.

**Mechanism:** Classification deferral → tools never documented → documentation debt grows → classification eventually required → cannot produce documentation of tools because records don't exist → operating without classification AND documentation debt exposed simultaneously.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has classification decisions (Swissdamed for healthcare, EU AI Act high-risk for consulting serving banking clients, FINMA expectations for WM). The deferral pattern is universal.

**Implication:** The service is not "determine your classification." The service is "document your tools so the classification decision can be made." The classification determination is downstream of the documentation architecture.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Deferring the classification decision doesn't reduce your exposure — it adds regulatory exposure to your documentation exposure. Let me show you what's actually required."
