## Current Market Problems (C300 Phase 03)

### Signal 1: Client-Contracted AI Governance Questionnaires Now Standard in Geneva Consulting Engagements

**What's happening:** Geneva consulting boutiques are increasingly receiving engagement contracts that include AI governance disclosure requirements. These are not regulatory requirements — they are contractual requirements imposed by clients, typically corporate clients, institutional buyers, and increasingly, public sector entities. The questionnaire asks about the consulting firm's AI methodology, tool inventory, data handling practices, and sub-consultant AI management.

**Why this is new in C300:** In C299, the market was discussing AI governance as an emerging concern. In C300, it has become a contractual fixture. The client sends the engagement contract with the AI clause embedded — the consulting firm has no negotiating position, because refusing the clause means not getting the engagement.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"Le client nous envoie le contrat avec la clause IA.波澜 On ne peut pas négocier — soit on accepte, soit le contrat va ailleurs.波澜 On signe, mais on ne sait pas ce qu'on a accepté."*

**What this creates:**
- Contractual AI governance obligations the firm has no framework to fulfill
- Silent accumulation of undisclosed client data processing through AI tools
- No methodology for documenting what AI was used in the engagement deliverable
- No internal AI tool inventory that matches what the contract requires the firm to represent

**Signal strength:** HIGH — this is the primary forcing function for the consulting ICP in C300

### Signal 2: Proposal Methodology Defensibly Crisis — AI-Assisted Work Cannot Be Reconstructed

**What's happening:** Geneva consulting boutiques are using AI tools in proposal development, research synthesis, data analysis, and report production — but with no methodology for documenting AI's role in the work. When clients ask questions about deliverables (which is increasing), the firm cannot reconstruct what AI did, at what stage, and with what data.

**Why this is critical:** The consulting firm's value is their methodology and intellectual capital. If they cannot demonstrate the integrity of their work product, they are exposed in client disputes and in carrier claims. The methodology is the defensible core of the consulting opinion — and AI use without documentation undermines that methodology.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"Le client demande: 'comment est-ce que vous êtes arrivés à cette recommandation?'波澜 Je ne peux pas dire: 'on a utilisé ChatGPT pour ça.'波澜 Et je ne peux pas non plus prétendre que tout vient de notre cerveau."*

**What this creates:**
- Undefendable methodology when client challenges the deliverable
- Carrier exposure when the professional liability claim requires demonstrating the work was properly done
- No documentation trail for any AI-assisted work in the engagement
- Increasing client sophistication — clients are getting better at asking these questions

**Signal strength:** HIGH — methodology defensibility is existential for consulting boutiques

### Signal 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Chain — Nobody Knows What's Being Used

**What's happening:** Geneva consulting boutiques routinely sub-contract portions of engagements to other boutiques, independent consultants, and specialist firms. In C300, these sub-consultants are using AI tools — and the delegating firm has no visibility into what tools, what data, what methodology, or what liability chain has been created.

**Why this creates specific exposure:** The managing director signed the engagement contract with the AI clause. They delegated work to a sub-consultant. The sub-consultant used AI tools. The client suffers a bad outcome. The client challenges the engagement contract's AI clause. The delegating firm cannot produce documentation of the sub-consultant's AI use — because they never required it.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"On mandate un spécialiste pour une partie du travail.波澜 Il utilise les outils qu'il veut.波澜 Si ça cause un problème chez le client, c'est notre problème?波澜 Je ne sais pas quelle est la chaîne."*

**What this creates:**
- Unmonitored AI tool use by sub-consultants across the engagement
- No contractual requirement for sub-consultants to disclose AI tool use
- Liability chain the managing director cannot close because it was never structured
- No documentation of what AI-assisted work was delivered by sub-consultants

**Signal strength:** MEDIUM-HIGH — most small Geneva boutiques delegate work regularly, and the AI delegation gap is architectural

### Signal 4: Professional Liability Carrier Attestation — Signing Without Knowing What's Committed

**What's happening:** Professional liability (RC professionnelle / errors & omissions) carriers serving Geneva consulting firms are now including AI attestations in renewal documents. The attestation asks the managing director to confirm adequate AI governance — without defining what adequate means, without specifying what AI tools are in use, and without requiring any supporting documentation.

**Why this creates the specific consulting gap:** The legal ICP (Phase 01) and finance ICP (Phase 02) both face carrier attestation — but the consulting ICP's attestation is specifically about client delivery AI, not internal operations. The attestation asks: "Do you have adequate AI governance for client engagements?" The firm does not know what "adequate" means and has no documentation to demonstrate it.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"L'assureur RC me demande de signer l'avenant IA.波澜 Les questions portent sur les engagements clients, sur les outils utilisés avec les données clients.波澜 C'est exactement là où on utilise l'IA — mais on n'a rien de documenté.波澜 Je signe, et ensuite je croise les doigts."*

**What this creates:**
- Personal signature on undefined adequacy standard
- No documentation that can be produced if carrier requests evidence
- Managing director personal exposure — their signature personally commits them
- No Geneva-specific consulting framework for what the attestation should cover

**Signal strength:** HIGH — this is the same attestation debt pattern as Phase 01 and Phase 02, but with consulting-specific delivery exposure

### Signal 5: Engagement Letters Silent on AI — No Contractual Protection Framework

**What's happening:** Geneva consulting engagement letters (lettres de mission) do not contain AI-related clauses. They do not specify: (a) what AI tools may be used in the engagement, (b) how client data is processed through AI systems, (c) what happens to client data when the engagement ends, (d) how AI-assisted work product should be interpreted, (e) who bears risk when AI-assisted analysis produces an incorrect recommendation.

**Why this creates exposure:** When something goes wrong — a client disputes a recommendation, a data breach occurs through an AI tool, a sub-consultant's AI use causes a problem — the engagement letter provides no contractual framework for allocating risk. The silence means the firm has no contractual protection and no liability limitation.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"Nos lettres de mission ne mentionnent pas l'IA.波澜 Quand on a un problème, est-ce que la lettre nous protège?波澜 Je n'ai aucune idée.波澜 On n'a jamais eu à y penser avant."*

**What this creates:**
- No contractual framework for AI tool use in client engagements
- No liability limitation for AI-assisted work product
- No data processing clause covering AI system access to client data
- No disclosure language for clients about AI's role in the deliverable
- No sub-consultant AI clause in the engagement letter

**Signal strength:** HIGH — engagement letter AI gap is structural across the market

### Signal 6: nFADP Art. 63 Personal Liability — The Managing Director Realizes They're Exposed

**What's happening:** Geneva consulting managing directors are learning — often from carrier attestation forms or from peer conversations — that nFADP Art. 63 creates personal criminal liability for data protection failures. They are beginning to understand that their use of AI tools in client engagements may constitute data processing under nFADP, and that they have never evaluated this exposure.

**Why this is distinct for consulting ICP:** Unlike legal (internal data) or finance (portfolio data), the consulting ICP processes client operational data through AI tools — project data, strategic information, personnel data, operational metrics. This is exactly the kind of personal data nFADP protects — and the managing director has been processing it through AI tools without knowing it.

**Specific complaint pattern:**
> *"Je traite des données clients avec des outils IA — stratégique, opérationnel, RH parfois.波澜 Le nFADP est en vigueur.波动 Si violation, c'est moi qui suis responsable comme directeur.波动 Je ne sais même pas quels outils utilisent mes consultants."*

**What this creates:**
- Personal criminal liability awareness (Art. 63)
- No inventory of which AI tools are processing which client data
- No DPA (data processing agreement) framework for AI tools used in engagements
- No documented lawful basis for client data processing via AI systems
- No data retention or termination protocol for client data in AI systems

**Signal strength:** MEDIUM-HIGH — awareness is growing, but the consulting ICP is still behind legal and finance on this

## What Failures Look Like (C300)

### Failure Mode 1: The Client Challenge
A client challenges a consulting deliverable — they believe the recommendation was produced by AI without proper methodology. The firm cannot reconstruct the work. The engagement letter is silent on AI. The client terminates the engagement and raises professional liability concerns. The carrier asks for methodology documentation. There is none.

### Failure Mode 2: The Carrier Audit
A professional liability carrier, following third-generation questionnaire protocols, requests documentation of AI governance for client engagements. The managing director cannot produce documentation of: (a) which AI tools were used, (b) what client data was processed, (c) what methodology was applied to AI-assisted work, (d) what the sub-consultant's AI use looked like. The carrier denies coverage on the basis that inadequate governance was demonstrated.

### Failure Mode 3: The Data Breach Through AI Tool
A client's confidential data — processed through a consulting firm's AI tool — is exposed through the AI vendor. The client demands the consulting firm explain what happened. The engagement letter has no AI data clause. The consulting firm has no DPA with the AI vendor. The managing director personally certified the carrier attestation. Personal exposure under Art. 63 is now active.

### Failure Mode 4: The Sub-Consultant AI Problem
A sub-consultant using AI tools in a delegated engagement produces a flawed analysis that the client relies on for a major decision. The client challenges the consulting firm. The consulting firm cannot trace the AI tool's role in the sub-consultant's work. The engagement letter has no AI delegation clause. The liability chain is not closed.
