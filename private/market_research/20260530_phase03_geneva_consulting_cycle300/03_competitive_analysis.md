# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

## Phase 03 | Cycle C300 | Geneva Consulting ICP

---

## Current Market Landscape

### Existing Providers Serving Geneva Consulting Firms on AI

| Provider Type | What They Offer | Gap for Geneva Consulting Boutique |
|---|---|---|
| **Big 4 / Strategy consultancies** | AI governance frameworks, compliance documentation, methodology documentation | Over-priced for 2–50 person boutiques; frameworks built for enterprise, not SMB consulting firms; not Geneva-calibrated |
| **Legal advisors** | nFADP compliance, data processing agreements, contract AI clauses | Focus on legal-specific data, not consulting-specific methodology and client delivery |
| **IT/Technology consultants** | AI tool implementation, vendor selection, tool integration | Focus on tool deployment, not client engagement methodology or proposal defensibility |
| **Insurance brokers** | Carrier attestation completion, insurance product matching | Help fill out the form, don't review the underlying governance or advise on methodology |
| **Compliance consultants** | Generic GDPR/nFADP compliance programs | Not calibrated for consulting firm's specific exposure: client engagements, methodology provenance, sub-consultant delegation |
| **Digital transformation advisors** | AI adoption roadmaps, digital strategy | Not addressing the liability and defensibility problem; framing AI as opportunity, not risk |

### What's Missing for Geneva Consulting Boutiques

---

## Gap 1: Client Engagement AI Governance Framework

**What should exist:** A governance framework specifically designed for Geneva consulting boutiques managing AI tool use in client-facing engagements — covering proposal methodology, deliverable production, client data processing, and AI's role in the consulting opinion.

**What actually exists:** Generic AI governance frameworks written for enterprises or technology companies. No Geneva-specific consulting framework that addresses the specific problem: how do you defend an AI-assisted consulting methodology to a client?

**Market gap:** The consulting firm needs a methodology documentation system that: (a) records AI's role in each engagement deliverable, (b) can be produced if the client challenges the work, (c) maps to whatever the client asked in the engagement contract's AI clause, (d) creates a defensible audit trail from prompt to recommendation.

**Why no one is addressing it:** The problem is specific enough that generic compliance consultants don't recognize it, and the market is small enough that Big 4 don't bother.

---

## Gap 2: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework

**What should exist:** A delegation framework that Geneva consulting boutiques can use when contracting sub-consultants — specifying what AI tools may be used, what documentation is required, what liability the delegating firm retains, and what the sub-consultant must represent about their AI governance.

**What actually exists:** Standard sub-consultant contracts with general liability provisions. No AI-specific delegation clause. No requirement that sub-consultants disclose their AI tool inventory or methodology.

**Market gap:** The consulting firm cannot close the liability chain because they never structured it. The sub-consultant is using AI tools with no obligation to disclose it, and the engagement contract has no AI delegation clause. When something goes wrong, the managing director cannot produce the documentation that would show where responsibility sits.

**Why no one is addressing it:** This is a consulting-specific structural problem that general legal advisors don't see as a discrete service category.

---

## Gap 3: Engagement Letter AI Clause Template for Consulting Engagements

**What should exist:** An engagement letter AI clause template calibrated for Geneva consulting boutiques — covering AI tool use disclosure, client data processing through AI systems, AI-assisted work product interpretation, sub-consultant AI delegation, and liability allocation for AI-assisted analysis.

**What actually exists:** No market-standard AI clause for consulting engagements. Some law firms are developing their own; no consulting-specific template exists.

**Market gap:** The engagement letter is silent on AI. The managing director needs AI-specific contract language that: (a) limits liability for AI-assisted work product, (b) discloses AI's role in the deliverable, (c) specifies data processing obligations for AI tools accessing client data, (d) allocates risk for AI-related errors. This is a 30-minute template solution that would protect the firm for every engagement.

**Why no one is addressing it:** Legal tech vendors focus on contract review, not engagement letter AI clause drafting for consulting firms.

---

## Gap 4: Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review for Consulting Firms

**What should exist:** A pre-signing review service for professional liability carrier attestation forms — specifically for managing directors at consulting boutiques who are signing personal attestations about AI governance in client engagements without knowing what they are committing to.

**What actually exists:** Insurance brokers who help complete the form. No independent review that assesses: (a) whether the attestation's definition of adequate AI governance is achievable, (b) whether the firm's actual AI use matches what the attestation requires, (c) what personal liability the managing director is accepting by signing.

**Market gap:** The medical practitioner analogue from Phase 02 (finance) shows this service exists for doctors. No equivalent service exists for consulting managing directors — who face the same personal signature exposure, the same undefined adequacy standard, and the same lack of pre-signing review.

**Why no one is addressing it:** The structural equivalence to the medical practitioner market has not been named. Once named, the service becomes obvious.

---

## Gap 5: Proposal AI Disclosure Methodology

**What should exist:** A methodology framework for Geneva consulting boutiques to document AI's role in proposals — what AI tools were used in the analysis, what data was processed, how the recommendation was derived, what human oversight was applied.

**What actually exists:** No methodology. Consulting firms either disclose AI use (and don't know how to frame it) or don't disclose it (and hope it doesn't come up).

**Market gap:** When a client asks how the proposal was produced — which is the question clients are starting to ask — the consulting firm needs a defensible answer that: (a) shows intellectual integrity, (b) demonstrates proper methodology, (c) documents AI's role without undermining the consulting opinion's credibility.

**Why no one is addressing it:** This is a consulting methodology problem, not a legal or compliance problem. No Geneva-calibrated consulting methodology advisor is addressing it.

---

## Gap 6: Geneva Consulting-Specific Swissdamed/EU AI Act Classification

**What should exist:** A classification assessment specifically for Geneva consulting boutiques — determining whether Swissdamed Tier 1/Tier 2 applies to their AI use in client engagements, and whether the EU AI Act creates nexus obligations for their EU-client-facing work.

**What actually exists:** Swissdamed criteria written for manufacturers and medical device companies. EU AI Act nexus determination guidance written for system operators, not consulting service providers. No Geneva consulting-specific classification tool.

**Market gap:** A 2–50 person consulting boutique using AI in client engagements cannot determine: (a) whether Swissdamed applies to their client delivery AI tools, (b) whether they are a high-risk AI system user under EU AI Act if they serve EU clients, (c) what proportionate documentation is required for their classification. The classification determines what compliance requires — and the consulting firm cannot determine their classification.

**Why no one is addressing it:** The Geneva consulting market is small enough that no advisory practice has built this as a specific service — even though the need is real and immediate.

| Generic compliance consultants | nFADP/GDPR programs | Not calibrated for consulting client delivery exposure |
| Digital transformation advisors | AI adoption | Framing AI as opportunity, not liability |

**DigitalCoa.ch opportunity:** Position as the Geneva-calibrated consulting AI governance specialist — addressing the specific liability chain, methodology defensibility, sub-consultant delegation, and carrier pre-signing review that no other provider is addressing.

**Service differentiation:** Not a framework vendor, not a compliance template provider — a consulting methodology advisor who understands that the consulting firm's value is their methodology, and that AI use without documentation undermines that methodology.
