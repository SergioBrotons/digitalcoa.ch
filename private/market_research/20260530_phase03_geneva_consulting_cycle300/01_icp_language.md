# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems

## Phase 03 | Cycle C300 | Geneva Consulting ICP

---

## ICP Definition (Phase 03)

**Firm type:** Small consulting boutique, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based
**Primary decision-maker:** Managing director or partner, personally accountable
**Core concerns:**
- Client AI governance questionnaires embedded in engagement contracts
- Carrier attestation for professional liability coverage
- Proposal and deliverable methodology defensibility
- Sub-consultant AI tool delegation and chain liability
- Personal exposure under nFADP Art. 63

**Distinction from Phase 01 (Legal) and Phase 02 (Finance):**
- Legal ICP: AI governance for internal law firm operations and client disclosure
- Finance ICP: AI governance for portfolio management and institutional clients
- **Consulting ICP: AI governance for client-facing engagement delivery and methodology defensibility**

---

## How the ICP Describes Their AI Problems

### 1. The Client Questionnaire Bind

The ICP does not say "I need AI governance documentation." They say:

> *"Nos clients commencent à inclure des clauses sur l'IA dans nos contrats d'engagement.波澜 Des questions sur notre méthodologie, sur les outils qu'on utilise, sur la façon dont on gère les données.波澜 Je ne sais pas ce qu'on est censé répondre."*

Language they use:
- "clause IA" / "questionnaire IA" — client-initiated AI disclosure requirement
- "contrat d'engagement" — engagement contract as the trigger point
- "méthodologie" — methodology as the defended work product
- "ce qu'on est censé répondre" — what they're supposed to say

Key observation: The trigger is contractual, not regulatory. The client is imposing the requirement through the engagement contract, not a regulator. This changes the framing from compliance to contract performance.

---

### 2. The Proposal Methodology Defensibility Problem

The ICP does not say "I need to document my AI use." They say:

> *"Quand je soumets une proposition, j'indique ma méthodologie.波澜 Si j'utilise l'IA dans la méthodologie, comment est-ce que je défends ça?波澜 Qu'est-ce qui se passe si le client demande: 'comment exactly est-ce que vous avez produit cette analyse?'"*

Language they use:
- "défendre ma méthodologie" — defend methodology in proposal
- "produit cette analyse" — how the deliverable was produced
- "utilisation de l'IA dans le rapport" — AI use in the report itself
- "traçabilité" — traceability of AI-assisted work

Key observation: The ICP is concerned about being unable to reconstruct the provenance of their work product. If a client questions a deliverable, they need to explain what role AI played — and whether that compromises the integrity of the consulting opinion.

---

### 3. The Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Gap

The ICP does not say "I need to manage third-party AI risk." They say:

> *"Je travaille avec des sous-traitants.波澜 Certains utilisent l'IA dans leur travail.波澜 Si l'IA du sous-consultant cause un problème chez le client final, est-ce que c'est mon problème?波澜 Je ne sais pas où finit ma responsabilité."*

Language they use:
- "sous-consultant" / "sous-traitant" — sub-consultant or subcontractor
- "chaîne de responsabilité" — chain of responsibility
- "où finit ma responsabilité" — where their liability ends
- "mandat complet" — full mandate accountability

Key observation: The ICP operates in a network of delegation. They sub-contract portions of engagements, often to other boutiques or independent specialists. AI tool use by sub-consultants creates an unmonitored liability chain that no one has mapped.

---

### 4. The Carrier Attestation Gap (Consulting-Specific)

The ICP does not say "I signed an AI attestation." They say:

> *"L'assureur RC professionnelle me demande de signer un avenant IA.波澜 Les questions sont vagues.波澜 Je ne sais pas si je dois dire que j'utilise l'IA ou non.波澜 Si je dis non et que j'utilise effectivement l'IA, qu'est-ce que ça change?波澜 Si je dis oui, qu'est-ce que j'ai engagé?"*

Language they use:
- "RC professionnelle" — professional liability / professional indemnity
- "avenant IA" — AI rider/endorsement to insurance policy
- "engagé" — what they've committed to by signing
- "questions vagues" — vague questions in the attestation form

Key observation: The consulting ICP's carrier attestation is specifically about client delivery AI, not internal operations. The attestation asks about AI in client engagements — which is precisely where the ICP's exposure lives.

---

### 5. The Engagement Letter AI Silence Problem

The ICP does not say "My contracts don't cover AI." They say:

> *"Nos lettres de mission ne mentionnent pas l'IA.波澜 Rien sur la façon dont on utilise les outils, sur ce qu'on fait des données du client, sur les sous-traitants.波澜 Si un problème survient, est-ce que nos lettres de mission nous protègent?波澜 Je n'ai pas de réponse."*

Language they use:
- "lettres de mission" — engagement letters
- "ne mentionnent pas l'IA" — don't mention AI
- "nous protègent" — protect us
- "clause de responsabilité" — liability clause

Key observation: The engagement letter is silent on AI — which means the ICP has no contractual framing for how AI tools are used in client work, what data is processed, and who bears risk when AI-assisted work produces a bad outcome.

---

### 6. The Personal Liability Under nFADP (Consulting-Specific)

The ICP does not say "I'm concerned about nFADP Art. 63." They say:

> *"Le nFADP — je sais que c'est en vigueur.波澜 Est-ce que je suis responsable personnellement si je traite des données clients avec des outils IA sans le savoir?波澜 Je suis le directeur.波动 Est-ce que c'est moi qui suis responsable?"*

Language they use:
- "responsable personnellement" — personally liable
- "directeur" — director
- "outils IA" — AI tools
- "sans le savoir" — without knowing

Key observation: The consulting ICP is aware of nFADP but has not made the connection between their specific client data processing (through AI tools in engagements) and their personal liability as managing director.

## The ICP's Language Pattern Summary

| Problem | How they describe it | What they don't say |
|---|---|---|
| Client questionnaire | "Nos clients commencent à inclure des clauses" | "I need AI governance documentation" |
| Methodology defensibility | "Comment je défends ma méthodologie?" | "I need to document AI use in proposals" |
| Sub-consultant delegation | "Où finit ma responsabilité?" | "I need sub-consultant AI governance" |
| Carrier attestation | "Qu'est-ce que j'ai engagé?" | "I need to understand the attestation" |
| Engagement letter gap | "Est-ce que nos lettres nous protègent?" | "My contracts lack AI clauses" |
| Personal nFADP liability | "Est-ce que c'est moi qui suis responsable?" | "Art. 63 creates personal exposure" |

**Core pattern:** The ICP describes problems as **accountability gaps** — where does my responsibility end, what have I committed to, do my documents protect me? They frame AI not as a technology problem but as a **contractual and personal liability problem**.

## Emotional State Summary

The Geneva consulting ICP is:
- **Contractually exposed** — clients imposing AI requirements through engagement contracts, no internal framework to respond
- **Methodologically uncertain** — unsure how to defend AI-assisted work in proposals and deliverables
- **Delegation-blind** — sub-consultant AI use creating unmonitored liability chain they can't see
- **Personally anxious** — managing director personally accountable, no clear protection framework
- **Documentarily silent** — engagement letters silent on AI, no contractual framing for AI tool use
- **Time-poor** — 2–50 person boutique, managing director doing everything, no capacity for AI governance projects

**Their primary fear:** A client dispute over a deliverable where AI was used, and they cannot reconstruct what AI did, cannot defend the methodology, and the engagement letter is silent on AI liability.
