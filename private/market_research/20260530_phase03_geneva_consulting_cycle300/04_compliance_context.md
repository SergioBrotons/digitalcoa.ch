## Regulatory Framework for Geneva Consulting Boutiques

### 1. nFADP — Federal Act on Data Protection (Operational since September 1, 2023)

**What's relevant for consulting firms:**

The nFADP applies to Geneva consulting boutiques that process personal data through AI tools in client engagements. The relevant obligations:

- **Art. 6 — Lawful basis:** Client data processed through AI tools requires a documented lawful basis. In consulting engagements, the basis is typically contractual performance or legitimate interest — but this must be documented, not assumed.
- **Art. 16 — Transparency:** Clients must be informed when AI tools access their data. The engagement letter must disclose AI's role in processing client data.
- **Art. 20 — Data processing agreements:** Any AI vendor that processes client data on behalf of the consulting firm requires a DPA. Most AI tool terms of service do not constitute adequate DPAs under nFADP.
- **Art. 26 — Data subject rights:** Clients have rights over their data processed through AI systems — including access, correction, and deletion rights. The consulting firm must be able to respond to these rights requests for data held in AI vendor systems.
- **Art. 63 — Personal criminal liability:** The managing director can face personal criminal liability for data protection violations. This is not a corporate exposure — it is personal and specific.

**Consulting-specific exposure:**
Client data in consulting engagements includes strategic plans, personnel information, financial data, organizational structures, M&A target information — all clearly personal data under nFADP. Processing this data through AI tools without a documented lawful basis, without client disclosure, and without vendor DPAs creates active Art. 63 exposure for the managing director.

**What's missing:** No Geneva consulting-specific guidance on how nFADP applies to AI-assisted consulting engagements — the specific problem of client data processing through AI tools in engagement delivery.

### 2. Swissdamed — Medical Devices Database (Tier 1 registration opening July 1, 2026)

**What's relevant for consulting firms:**

Swissdamed is primarily designed for medical device manufacturers and users. However, the question of whether Swissdamed applies to consulting firms using AI tools in client engagements is genuinely unclear — and the classification criteria are written for medical device contexts, not consulting service contexts.

**The classification problem:**
- Swissdamed has Tier 1 (registration) and Tier 2 (assessment) classifications
- The criteria reference device classification rules that don't translate to consulting service delivery
- A Geneva consulting boutique using AI in client engagements cannot determine: (a) whether Swissdamed applies to their AI tool use, (b) whether they are a user of a Tier 1 or Tier 2 AI system, (c) what registration or assessment obligations apply
- The classification question is blocking proportionate compliance action

**Why this matters for consulting firms:**
Swissdamed creates registration and assessment obligations for AI system users in Switzerland. If a consulting firm is using AI tools that fall within Swissdamed's scope, they have registration obligations opening July 1, 2026. The firm cannot determine whether they're in scope — and getting it wrong in either direction creates exposure.

**What's missing:** No Geneva-calibrated Swissdamed classification assessment for consulting boutiques. The classification guidance does not address service-delivery AI use — only medical device AI use.

### 3. EU AI Act — European Union Artificial Intelligence Act

**What's relevant for Geneva consulting firms:**

The EU AI Act creates obligations for operators of high-risk AI systems. Geneva consulting boutiques that serve EU-based clients may be subject to EU AI Act obligations — particularly if their AI use in client engagements falls within the high-risk AI system definition.

**The nexus problem:**
- EU AI Act applies to operators of high-risk AI systems in the EU
- A Geneva-based consulting firm serving EU clients is likely an operator if they use AI in those engagements
- The question of whether a consulting firm's AI use in client engagements constitutes high-risk AI system operation is not clearly answered
- The EU AI Act's high-risk criteria focus on systems making consequential decisions — consulting recommendations are consequential, but the classification isn't explicit

**Consulting-specific uncertainty:**
If a consulting firm uses AI to produce client recommendations that affect business decisions (strategic, financial, operational), the question of whether this constitutes high-risk AI system operation under EU AI Act is genuinely unclear. The risk is that the firm is operating in a grey zone — and the EU AI Act's implementation is still developing.

**What's missing:** No Geneva consulting-specific EU AI Act nexus assessment. The firm needs to know: (a) whether EU AI Act applies to their Geneva-based firm serving EU clients, (b) whether their client delivery AI constitutes high-risk AI system operation, (c) what proportionate compliance looks like.

### 4. Professional Liability Insurance (RC Professionnelle) — AI Attestation Requirements

**What's relevant for Geneva consulting firms:**

Professional liability carriers serving Geneva consulting boutiques are now including AI attestations in renewal documents. These attestations ask the managing director to confirm adequate AI governance for client engagements — without defining what adequate means.

**The attestation gap:**
- The attestation asks about AI use in client engagements, data processing through AI tools, sub-consultant AI delegation
- The managing director signs personally — creating personal exposure under the attestation
- No documentation is required at signing — but the carrier can request documentation if a claim arises
- The attestation form is designed by the carrier, not by any regulatory standard
- The managing director signs without knowing what "adequate AI governance" actually requires them to have

**What the attestation typically asks:**
1. Does the firm use AI tools in client engagements?
2. Are those tools documented and governed?
3. Is client data processed through AI systems in accordance with applicable data protection law?
4. Does the firm have a policy for AI tool use in client work?
5. Are sub-consultants required to disclose AI tool use?

**Consulting-specific exposure:** The attestation covers exactly the gaps identified in this research: client engagement AI, methodology documentation, sub-consultant delegation, client data processing. The managing director signing this attestation is personally committing to AI governance they don't have.

### 5. Geneva Commercial Code — Professional Services Liability

**What's relevant:**

Geneva consulting boutiques operating as SARL, Sàrl, or SA are subject to Swiss Commercial Code provisions on professional liability. The managing director's personal liability exposure is not limited to nFADP Art. 63 — professional liability claims can expose personal assets if negligence is demonstrated.

**AI-specific professional liability:**
If a consulting recommendation produced with AI assistance leads to client damage, the professional liability claim must demonstrate: (a) what methodology was applied, (b) what role AI played in the recommendation, (c) whether proper professional standards were followed. Without methodology documentation, the firm cannot defend the AI-assisted work.

**What's missing:** No Geneva-specific professional liability guidance addressing AI-assisted consulting work — the specific documentation requirements, the standard of care for AI-assisted methodology, the liability allocation when sub-consultant AI causes damage.

---

### 6. FINMA Supervision — Not Directly Applicable

**Note:** FINMA supervises financial market participants — banks, insurers, asset managers. Geneva consulting boutiques are generally not FINMA-supervised unless they hold a specific license (e.g., as an asset manager). This means FINMA Circular 08/2024 and other financial sector AI guidance do not directly apply to consulting firms.

**However:** Consulting firms that serve financial sector clients may receive AI governance questionnaires from those clients that reference FINMA expectations — creating an indirect FINMA influence on consulting firm AI governance expectations.
