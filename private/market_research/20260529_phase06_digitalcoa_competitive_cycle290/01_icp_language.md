## Positioning Language — What Lands and Why

**What the Geneva MD/partner/practice leader recognizes as "this is for me":**

1. **"Accountability across multiple venues simultaneously"** — This phrase names the specific problem nobody else names. The Geneva ICP is managing carrier renewal, board reporting, institutional counterparty methodology requests, and informal Geneva circuit standards formation simultaneously. Each venue has a different evidentiary standard, a different audience, and a different timing. No single framework maps what each venue requires or how the requirements interact when they contradict each other. This is the specific problem that lands because it describes what they experience but have not been able to name.

2. **"Geneva circuit intelligence"** — The ICP who has been watching accountability standards form through informal channels recognizes the difference between published doctrine and sustained circuit participation. The reference to "who is in the room when standards form" signals participation in mechanisms that are not accessible through public channels. This is a recognition signal, not an explanation signal. The ICP who needs it knows immediately.

3. **"Documentation window"** — The phrase performs a specific function: it converts an abstract regulatory timing concept into a personal urgency frame. The documentation window is not a compliance deadline. It is the period before crystallized standards arrive during which positions can be built proactively versus defensively. The ICP who has been watching the carrier iteration pattern recognizes the timing property immediately.

4. **"Personal attestation"** — Carrier third-generation attestation requires a personal signature. The ICP has been signing documents committing to "adequate AI governance controls" without ever having analyzed what "adequate" means under Geneva conditions for their specific situation. The phrase "personal attestation" converts an abstract compliance concept into a specific personal liability question.

5. **"Written summary designed to survive"** — The ICP who has been through a carrier renewal or board meeting where documentation was requested understands immediately the difference between a conversation summary and a document that holds coherent meaning across accountability occasions six months later. The phrase "survive into future contexts" describes the specific property they need and have not been able to find in any other advisory output.
