### Finding 1: Pre-Engagement Geneva Circuit Verification as ICP Authentication Mechanism Operating Independently of All Other Trust Signals

**The finding:**
The ICP evaluating the practice before engagement has one structurally available verification mechanism: whether the practice can name Geneva-specific accountability dynamics that are not accessible through public channels. This verification mechanism is the only one available because most operationally relevant Geneva accountability intelligence — carrier iteration patterns, informal circuit standard formation, institutional event intelligence — is not publication-eligible. The practice cannot demonstrate circuit participation through published content because the intelligence is defined by its non-publication. The ICP must evaluate circuit access through named references and the specific character of how problems are described, not through any documentable proof.

**The specific mechanism:**
The ICP evaluates whether the practice names problems — which carriers are moving fastest, what the Geneva informal circuit is forming versus what Bern has published, who is in the room when standards form — with a specificity that could only come from sustained participation. The recognition mechanism operates before any substantive engagement: the ICP who has been watching accountability standards form through Geneva informal channels recognizes the difference between someone who has read about Geneva and someone who has been in it. The specificity of the named problems is the observable proof of participation. This is structurally different from all other trust signals — content quality, format precision, documented decisions — which can be fabricated. The circuit verification mechanism cannot be fabricated because the intelligence it tests is defined by non-publication.

### Finding 2: Documentation Window Close as Crystallizing Event with Asymmetric Burden-of-Proof Producing Permanent Accountability Geometry Change

**The finding:**
The documentation window is not a compliance deadline — it is a crystallizing event that permanently changes the accountability geometry for every Geneva MD. Before the window closes, positions can be built prospectively with evidence reflecting the reasoning that produced them. After the window closes, positions can only be explained defensively with new documentation that cannot retroactively reconstruct the original reasoning conditions. The asymmetry is permanent: the MD who built during the window has evidence of reasoning process; the MD who explains after closure has documentation that cannot demonstrate the reasoning that produced it — only the conclusion. This asymmetry compounds over time as the window becomes increasingly distant — each year of new documentation building on post-window defensive positions widens the gap between what the MD can demonstrate and what the pre-window MD could demonstrate.

**The specific mechanism:**
The window close is a crystallizing event, not a compliance deadline. The structural property is that positions built before the window close are evaluated against forming standards — standards that have not yet arrived when the positions were built. Positions explained after the window close are evaluated against arrived standards — standards that have crystallized through the window period. The MD who built during the window was building against standards they could observe forming but had not yet arrived. The MD who explains after closure is explaining against standards that have crystallized and are no longer forming. The difference is not just timing — it is the fundamental relationship between the documented position and the standards against which it is evaluated. The window close is a permanent structural change in the accountability geometry, not a deadline that passes.

**Distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings:**
This finding is distinct from C284 Finding 2 (written summary survivability into future accountability contexts) which addressed document design for multi-venue travel, not the specific mechanism of the documentation window as crystallizing event producing permanent asymmetry between pre-window and post-window accountability geometry. This finding is distinct from C286 Phase 06 (documentation window burden-of-proof reversal as central urgency frame) which named the urgency frame without isolating the specific crystallizing event property and the permanent asymmetry it creates between pre-window and post-window positions. This finding is distinct from all prior Phase 05 documentation window findings which addressed the timing property without isolating the specific crystallizing mechanism and the permanent accountability geometry change it produces.

### Finding 3: Advisory Relationship Architecture as Compound Asset Producing Two Structurally Different Outputs Simultaneously

**The finding:**
The advisory engagement produces two outputs simultaneously through the same engagement activity — client-confidential intelligence and public methodology evidence. The client engagement generates specific accountability intelligence about the ICP's situation that is confidential to the client relationship. The same engagement produces documented positions on how accountability questions resolve that become public methodology evidence accessible to all future prospects. The two outputs do not compromise each other: the confidential client intelligence improves the practice's pattern recognition and analytical framework, which improves the public methodology evidence; the public methodology evidence does not reveal any client-confidential information because it is derived from the pattern across engagements, not from any individual engagement's specifics. The mechanism is a compound asset architecture: each engagement adds to the public methodology evidence without compromising client confidentiality, and the accumulated public methodology evidence is the practice's primary trust formation instrument for future prospects.

**The specific mechanism:**
The compound asset mechanism operates through the cross-referencing property of the methodology evidence. Each documented position on how an accountability question resolves cross-references prior documented positions, creating a dense cross-referenced public record that demonstrates the analytical framework through accumulated positions rather than through methodology documents. A prospect evaluating the practice can observe the cross-referenced pattern across multiple documented conclusions and infer the analytical framework that produced them — which is more reliable than any methodology claim because it is derived from observed decisions under specific conditions, not from asserted capabilities. The mechanism compounds because each additional engagement adds documented positions that cross-reference the accumulated record, increasing the density and therefore the credibility of the inferred analytical framework. The client confidentiality and public methodology compound asset are not in tension — they are produced by the same engagement architecture through different derivation pathways.

**Distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings:**
This finding is distinct from C284 Finding 5 (return engagement density at accountability occasion moments as behavioral evidence of framework durability) which addressed the retention signal of returning clients, not the compound asset mechanism that produces public methodology evidence from every engagement including first-time engagements. This finding is distinct from C277 Finding 2 (documented advisory methodology as public evidence asset distinct from client work product confidentiality) which named the distinction without isolating the specific compound asset mechanism — that client confidentiality and public methodology evidence are produced simultaneously through the same engagement architecture and compound each other. This finding is distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings addressing trust signals through content quality, format precision, pricing, or capacity constraint — this finding addresses the specific compound asset mechanism through which the advisory relationship architecture produces two outputs that compound each other.

### Finding 4: Geneva Accountability Ecosystem Cross-Generational Pressure Transfer Creating Succession Accountability Burden Without Discharge Mechanism

**The finding:**
Geneva accountability obligations formed during one generation of MDs are transferring to successor MDs through documented position inheritance without any designed discharge mechanism. The successor MD inherits documented positions from decisions made by predecessors in accountability contexts that have since changed — carrier standards that have iterated, regulatory frameworks that have crystallized, Geneva circuit standards that have formed since the original positions were documented. The successor is accountable for positions they did not make, under standards that may have changed since the positions were documented, with no mechanism for evaluating whether the inherited position is adequate under current standards or what a proportionate adjustment would look like. The cross-generational transfer is structural to the Geneva accountability ecosystem: the documentation window close means positions from the window period are now being examined by successors who did not build them, and the burden-of-proof asymmetry means the successor cannot retroactively reconstruct the reasoning that produced the inherited positions.

**The specific mechanism:**
The cross-generational transfer creates a specific accountability burden that no current advisory framework addresses: the successor must simultaneously manage current accountability obligations and inherited accountability positions from predecessors. The successor's own decisions are being made under current conditions — carrier third-generation attestation, Federal Council deliberation crystallizing the coordination function, nFADP Art.13 explanation obligations operative — while managing inherited positions from decisions made under different conditions. The two accountability tracks are not separable: the inherited positions affect the successor's current decision space, and the successor's current decisions will be inherited by their own successors. The cross-generational transfer is not a one-time succession event — it is a continuous structural condition of the Geneva accountability ecosystem where each generation of MDs inherits positions from the prior generation while creating positions that the next generation will inherit.

**Distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings:**
This finding is distinct from C280 Phase 06 Finding 1 (exit liability architecture as discrete advisory category) which addressed the departing MD's personal accountability for AI governance decisions made at the entity they no longer control — this finding addresses the successor's inherited accountability burden, not the departing MD's exit liability. This finding is distinct from C273 Phase 06 Finding 4 (written summary multi-generational travel as document design problem) which addressed document design for successor + board + external party travel simultaneously — this finding addresses the specific cross-generational accountability transfer mechanism and the structural absence of a discharge mechanism for inherited positions. This finding is distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings addressing the current MD's accountability — this finding addresses the specific structural condition created when accountability obligations transfer across MD generations without designed discharge mechanism.

### Finding 5: Capacity Constraint Visibility as Demonstrated Accurate Self-Assessment Signal Performing Positioning Function Through Decision-to-Refuse Documentation

**The finding:**
The maximum-three-engagements-per-month constraint is a demonstrated accurate self-assessment signal — not because it creates scarcity, but because it is maintained through documented decisions to refuse. Each refusal decision is a documented instance of the practice evaluating a prospect against its analytical framework and declining because the prospect does not fit. The documented refusals — with specific reasoning about why the prospect was not appropriate — are observable before any substantive engagement. The executive evaluating the practice can observe the decision trail and infer the analytical framework from the pattern of what was declined and why. The mechanism is not scarcity psychology — it is decision-to-refuse documentation that demonstrates a defined analytical framework before any substantive engagement has occurred. The constraint is visible through the documented decision trail, not through a claim about capacity limits.

**The specific mechanism:**
The positioning function of the capacity constraint operates through the documented decision trail, not through the constraint itself. The constraint is only a trust signal if it is visible — and it is visible through the documented decisions to refuse that demonstrate the practice declined opportunities that did not fit its analytical framework. The executive evaluating the practice can observe: here is a documented decision to decline this type of prospect for this specific reason. The next documented decision to decline a similar prospect demonstrates consistency. The pattern across documented decisions demonstrates an analytical framework that has been applied consistently. The mechanism is fundamentally different from scarcity marketing, which uses artificial constraint to create urgency. This mechanism uses documented decision-to-refuse to demonstrate analytical framework — the constraint is real, not manufactured, and the decisions demonstrate the framework that produces the constraint.

### Finding 6: Geneva Authenticity Signals as ICP Self-Selection Mechanism Operating Through Circuit Belonging Recognition Before Analytical Evaluation

**The finding:**
The ICP evaluates the practice through a pre-analytical authenticity signal: does this practice belong in the Geneva accountability circuit? The evaluation operates through circuit belonging recognition — the ICP recognizes whether the practice's reference character, named problems, and specific calibration are consistent with what the ICP knows about the Geneva accountability ecosystem from their own participation. This is not analytical evaluation — it is not assessing whether the practice's analysis is correct. It is assessing whether the practice is inside the circuit that produces the intelligence the ICP needs. The authenticity signal operates before analytical evaluation because the ICP cannot evaluate the analysis without engaging — but they can evaluate whether the practice appears to belong to the circuit that produces the accountability intelligence they need. The signal is binary at the pre-engagement stage: the practice either appears to be in the Geneva circuit or it does not. If it does, the ICP moves to the qualification call. If it does not, no amount of analytical quality will convert.

**The specific mechanism:**
The circuit belonging recognition operates through the specific character of how Geneva accountability problems are named. The ICP who has been watching accountability standards form through Geneva informal channels has developed an intuition for what belongs in the circuit and what does not. The practice's reference to "Geneva circuit intelligence" or "what the informal circuit is forming versus what Bern has published" — these are signals that register as belonging to the circuit the ICP participates in. The practice's specific references to carrier iteration patterns, Geneva event formation, and institutional actor behavior — these are signals that register as circuit participation, not circuit observation. The authenticity mechanism is pre-analytical: it filters before the ICP evaluates whether the practice can solve their problem, because the ICP first evaluates whether the practice has access to the circuit intelligence that would allow them to solve it.

**Distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings:**
This finding is distinct from C284 Finding 1 (pre-briefing qualification call as trust formation occasion) which addressed the qualification call as the trust formation mechanism after the ICP has already recognized the practice as a candidate — this finding addresses the pre-engagement authenticity evaluation that determines whether the ICP considers the practice a candidate at all. This finding is distinct from C284 Finding 3 (carrier iteration intelligence as observable intelligence function) which named the intelligence function without isolating the specific circuit belonging recognition mechanism through which the ICP evaluates whether the intelligence is real before evaluating whether it is accurate. This finding is distinct from all prior Phase 06 findings addressing content quality, format precision, documented decisions, or pricing as trust signals — this finding addresses the specific pre-analytical circuit belonging recognition that determines whether the ICP engages with the practice at all, operating before any evaluation of analytical quality.
