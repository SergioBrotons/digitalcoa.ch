## English — Lead Magnet

### Executive AI Governance Briefing: The Geneva Accountability Architecture Problem

Every Geneva executive I work with arrives with the same underlying problem. They are managing accountability across multiple venues simultaneously — carrier renewal, board reporting, institutional client methodology requests, nFADP obligations, and the informal standards forming through the Geneva circuit. Each venue has a different evidentiary standard, a different audience, and a different timing. No single framework maps what each venue requires or how the requirements interact when they contradict each other.

That is the problem this briefing addresses.

---

**Who this is for:**
Senior executives and managing directors at Geneva-based organizations who are managing AI governance accountability across multiple venues and cannot find Geneva-specific guidance that addresses their actual situation. The briefing is not for organizations that need general AI governance training. It is for executives who need to understand what their specific accountability exposure is, across the five venues that are operating on their firm simultaneously.

---

**What the briefing covers:**

**1. The multi-venue coherence problem.**
The five accountability venues — carrier renewal, board reporting, institutional counterparty methodology requests, nFADP obligations, and Geneva circuit standard formation — operate with different standards, different timing, and different evidentiary requirements. The documentation you build for one venue may not satisfy another. The briefing maps the specific incoherences that Geneva boutique organizations face and what a proportionate coherence architecture looks like.

**2. The documentation window.**
What you need to have built before the window closes, in what format, and what the difference is between documentation built during the window and documentation built after. The timing distinction is the operative point — new documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct the reasoning that would have been built during the window period.

**3. The Federal Council consultation response.**
What the deliberation will require before it arrives, and what you can position in the anticipatory window. The consultation response architecture is different from post-publication compliance building — the window before crystallized standards arrive is categorically different from the period after.

**4. The carrier third-generation shift.**
What the evolution from process documentation to liability absorption capacity means for your next renewal. What you are personally attesting to when you sign the carrier attestation, and what the attestation commits you to personally that the document language may not make clear.

**5. The nFADP Art.13 explanation obligation.**
The explanation obligation is present-tense. The response architecture is absent in most Geneva firms. What you need to build now and what happens when the first request arrives before the architecture is in place.

**6. The Geneva circuit standard formation.**
What is forming through the informal circuit before any published doctrine arrives, and how to position for it. Which carriers are moving fastest and what the iteration logic means for your next renewal. Who is in the room when standards form and what that means for the intelligence you need.

## Français — Lead Magnet

Chaque cadre genevois avec qui je travaille arrive avec le même problème sous-jacent. Ils gèrent la reddition de comptes à travers plusieurs lieux simultanément — renouvellement du porteur, rapports au conseil, demandes de méthodologie des clients institutionnels, obligations nFADP, et les normes informelles qui se forment à travers le circuit genevois. Chaque lieu a une norme probatoire différente, un public différent, et un timing différent. Aucun cadre unique ne cartographie ce que chaque lieu requiert ou comment les exigences interagissent quand elles se contredisent.

C'est le problème que ce briefing aborde.

---

**Pour qui c'est :**
Cadres supérieurs et directeurs généraux d'organisations basées à Genève qui gèrent la reddition de comptes de gouvernance de l'IA à travers plusieurs lieux et ne peuvent pas trouver de guidance genevoise spécifique qui adresse leur situation. Le briefing n'est pas pour les organisations qui ont besoin d'une formation générale en gouvernance de l'IA. Il est pour les cadres qui ont besoin de comprendre quelle est leur exposition spécifique à la reddition de comptes, à travers les cinq lieux qui opèrent simultanément sur leur cabinet.

---

**Ce que couvre le briefing :**

**1. Le problème de cohérence multi-lieux.**
Les cinq lieux de reddition de comptes — renouvellement du porteur, rapports au conseil, demandes de méthodologie des clients institutionnels, obligations nFADP, et formation des normes du circuit genevois — opèrent avec des normes différentes, des timings différents, et des exigences probatoires différentes. **5. L'obligation d'explication nFADP Art.13.**
L'obligation d'explication est actuelle. L'architecture de réponse est absente dans la plupart des cabinets genevois. Ce que vous devez construire maintenant et ce qui se passe quand la première demande arrive avant que l'architecture soit en place.

Ce qui se forme à travers le circuit informel avant l'arrivée de toute doctrine publiée, et comment se positionner pour cela. Quels porteurs bougent le plus vite et ce que la logique d'itération signifie pour votre prochain renouvellement. Qui est dans la pièce quand les normes se forment et ce que cela signifie pour l'intelligence dont vous avez besoin.
