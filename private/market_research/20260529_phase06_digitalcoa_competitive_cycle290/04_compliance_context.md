### Carrier Underwriting Evolution as Primary Regulatory Signal

**What is happening:**
Carrier third-generation AI governance attestation is not a compliance documentation exercise — it is an underwriting evolution. The carriers are shifting from evaluating whether a firm has AI governance policies to evaluating whether the MD personally can demonstrate adequate AI governance controls under personal attestation. The questionnaire is not asking for policy documents — it is asking for evidence of actual governance behavior, review patterns, and decision documentation.

**The specific shift:**
First generation: Does the firm have an AI governance policy?
Second generation: Can the firm demonstrate its AI governance implementation?
Third generation: Can the MD personally attest to adequate AI governance controls, and what evidence exists that the attestation reflects actual governance behavior?

**Why this shift matters for the practice:**
The shift from policy documentation to personal liability absorption capacity is the specific structural change that creates the market for DigitalCoa.ch's advisory service. The MD who signs a third-generation attestation is personally exposed to liability for governance behavior they may not have documented. The practice's pre-renewal review is positioned specifically to address this shift — it is not a compliance documentation exercise, it is a personal liability mapping exercise that produces documented evidence of governance behavior before the carrier asks for it.

**The carrier iteration signal:**
The practice tracks carrier iteration patterns as a discrete intelligence function. The carriers are not moving in unison — some are moving faster than others, and the differential creates competitive intelligence about which standards are likely to become operative across all carriers. The Geneva circuit intelligence about carrier iteration is the primary observable proof of the practice's sustained circuit participation.

### nFADP Obligations as Present-Tense Operating Condition

**What is happening:**
The nFADP implementation date of September 1, 2023 has passed. The obligations are not future events — they are present-tense operating conditions. Art.13 explanation obligation is operative now. Art.9 processor obligations are operative now. The documentation window for building compliant architecture is not a future compliance deadline — it is a present-tense structural condition with a permanent close date.

**The specific obligations the practice addresses:**

**Art.13 Explanation Obligation:**
The explanation obligation is present-tense. Data subjects have the right to request explanation of AI-influenced decisions. The response architecture is absent in most Geneva professional services firms. The practice builds pre-architecture before the first request arrives — which is the only way to build a response architecture that survives the request.

**Art.9 Third-Party Processing:**
The processor obligations are operative for any firm using non-Swiss AI vendor infrastructure. The authorization scope must be evaluated against vendor terms. The vendor terms may have mutated since the original authorization was given. The practice audits the processor chain as a discrete service category.

**Art.21 Automated Decision:**
The automated decision obligations are operative. Firms using AI for decisions that affect data subjects must be able to demonstrate the logic and evidence for those decisions. The practice addresses this as a multi-venue coherence problem — the documentation required for Art.21 compliance must hold coherent meaning across carrier, board, and counterparty venues simultaneously.
