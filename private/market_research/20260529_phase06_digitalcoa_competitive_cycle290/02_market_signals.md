## Current Market Signals — DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Practice

### Signal Category 1: Carrier Market Evolution

**What is happening:**
Carrier third-generation AI governance attestation is not a documentation exercise. It is an underwriting evolution. Carriers are shifting from process documentation (first generation) to liability absorption capacity (third generation). The questionnaire is not asking whether a firm has AI governance policies. It is asking whether the MD personally can demonstrate adequate AI governance controls under personal attestation. The shift is structural and the market has not adjusted its response architecture accordingly.

**Why it matters for positioning:**
The carrier evolution is the primary acute pressure point for Geneva MDs. It creates a concrete near-term problem (next renewal) with personal liability consequences. The MD who receives a third-generation questionnaire and has not analyzed their personal exposure is in a structurally weaker position than the MD who has. The practice's documented carrier iteration intelligence is observable before engagement — the specific character of how the evolution is described reveals whether it comes from sustained tracking or from current research.

**Positioning implication:**
Carrier third-generation attestation as entry point. The qualification call can demonstrate carrier iteration intelligence immediately by describing the specific shift from process documentation to liability absorption capacity and what it means for what the MD is personally attesting to. This is the highest-conversion entry signal currently operating in the Geneva market.

### Signal Category 3: Documentation Window Permanence

**What is happening:**
The documentation window is not a compliance deadline in the conventional sense. It is the period before crystallized standards arrive during which positions can be built proactively versus defensively. The structural property of the window is that new documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct the reasoning that would have been built during the window period. This is a burden-of-proof inversion, not a documentation quantity problem.

**Why it matters for positioning:**
The MD who understands the documentation window property understands why pre-engagement positioning is categorically different from post-draft review. The engagement is not producing a document. It is producing a defensible position that was built during the window period when it could still be built proactively. The written summary is not a summary of the conversation — it is evidence of a reasoning framework built under conditions that no longer exist.

**Positioning implication:**
Documentation window as urgency frame. The practice frames the timing property of the window as the central urgency of the engagement — not what the document says but when it was built and why that timing matters. This framing converts an abstract regulatory concept into a personal positioning imperative for the MD.

## What Is Working in Advisory Positioning

**Named structural accountability framing over general AI governance compliance framing.** The executives who convert are those who encounter the framing "accountability architecture problem" — specifically the problem of documentation built under prior conditions needing to hold under changed standards across multiple venues simultaneously. General AI governance positioning does not convert this segment.

**Pre-briefing qualification architecture performing dual function.** The 20-minute qualification call functions as both prospect calibration and prospect service. The executive who has a genuine accountability situation benefits from the calibration even if they do not engage. The call demonstrates whether the practice can precisely describe the situation before proposing engagement terms. This dual function produces referrals from non-converting qualified executives.

**Documented decision trail as behavioral positioning record.** Documented decisions to decline — with specific reasoning — demonstrate a defined analytical framework before any substantive engagement. The executive evaluating the practice can observe what was declined and why. This behavioral record is more credible than any positioning claim.

**Written summary as survivable document distinct from conversation summary.** The executives who most need the briefing understand that they will need a defensible document in six months when accountability questions arrive that the conversation did not design for. The written summary positioned as a survivable document into future accountability contexts is the product property that differentiates the offering.

**Capacity constraint visibility as accurate self-assessment signal.** The maximum-three-engagements-per-month constraint, when visibly maintained, is a trust signal because it demonstrates accurate self-assessment about what the practice can deliver at the calibration level it claims. This is not scarcity marketing — it is a genuine operational constraint reflecting the sustained personal participation that Geneva calibration requires.

## What Is Not Working

**Generic "AI governance advisory" positioning.** Every international firm and Swiss domestic player uses some version of it. The ICP discounts it. Generic positioning produces general inquiries — exploration rather than action on a specific accountability occasion. Low conversion, high time-per-inquiry cost, no durable engagement conditions.

**Compliance-advisory conflation attracting wrong inquiry profile.** Positioning AI governance advisory as a compliance function attracts firms looking for documentation production. This framing attracts price competition and short-engagement inquiries. It does not create conditions for the multi-year advisory relationship the ICP's actual accountability exposure requires.

**Per-engagement pricing without relationship architecture.** The ICP facing a carrier renewal today will face a modified version of the same question in eighteen months — and the documented position from today will be part of what they defend then. Per-engagement pricing without retainer or subscription architecture fails the ICP's actual need and fails to capture relationship value that compounds over accountability occasions.

**Published content volume without demonstrated intelligence architecture.** Content volume without systematic intelligence tracking — carrier iteration monitoring, Geneva event observation, informal circuit exchange — signals coverage without depth. The ICP who has watched accountability standards form and shift recognizes the difference between published output and sustained intelligence architecture.

**Absence of Geneva AI governance advisory genre conventions.** The market has not established "Geneva AI governance advisory briefing" as a named format with conventions the ICP recognizes. Quality assessment is harder for prospects evaluating advisory services. The first practice to establish the genre conventions captures a trust formation advantage that content quality alone cannot produce.

## Pricing Signal Evidence — 90-Minute Briefing Model

**What the market supports:**
CHF 2,200–2,800 per engagement for Geneva boutique MD/partner-level executives. The pricing performs a dual function: it communicates accurate self-assessment (this is what the service is worth) and it performs an ICP qualification function (the executive who baulks at the price was not the ICP; the executive who accepts it has already self-qualified through the price signal).

**Why CHF 2,200–2,800 works:**
The price is above commodity advisory range, which filters for executives who have genuine accountability exposure rather than exploratory interest. The price is below what the exposure justifies for a Geneva MD whose personal liability is engaged — the cost of an inadequate position at carrier renewal or board meeting is multiples of the briefing investment. The price is positioned as accurate self-assessment, not premium positioning.

**Format signals supporting the 90-minute model:**
The 90-minute briefing is long enough to form genuine analytical engagement and short enough to respect senior executive time discipline. The pre-briefing qualification call establishes whether the engagement is appropriate before scheduling. The written summary delivery within 48 hours creates a second touchpoint and demonstrates follow-through discipline. Maximum three engagements per month creates visible capacity constraint.

**What is not working in pricing:**
Hourly billing structures the engagement as time-for-money, which is architecturally incompatible with the accountability positioning the practice is claiming. Fixed-fee per engagement without relationship architecture fails the ICP who will face multiple accountability occasions over time. Discounting signals inaccurate self-assessment and undermines the qualification function the price performs.
