# 06 — Executive Summary
## Phase 01 Geneva Legal — C130

**Market Opportunity:**
Geneva legal advisory market is being reshaped by four structural forces: (1) Switzerland-EU institutional uncertainty creating advisory demand, (2) AI governance and professional responsibility emerging as distinct practice area, (3) personal liability accountability for boards and GCs intensifying post-CS, and (4) commodity trading sanctions enforcement generating time-sensitive mandates.

**Key Findings C130:**

1. Switzerland-EU Bilateral III: Critical Q2 2026 negotiation phase; financial services market access + data adequacy at stake; contingency advisory demand rising
2. Swiss AI Act + OGE/RAV professional standards: Enforcement architecture under design; lawyers using GenAI face new professional responsibility obligations
3. FINMA personal liability enforcement: AktG Art. 716a personal accountability precedent; board/GC personal exposure increasing
4. ICC 2026 Rules (June 1 go-live): Expedited procedures + ESG + AI disclosure requirements; arbitrage opportunity for independent advisors
5. Swiss litigation funding: Omni Bridgeway + Swiss Legal Finance active; disclosure obligations creating new advisory niche
6. Geneva arbitration surge: Tariff-driven commodity disputes + sanctions force majeure clauses; ICC + SCAI case volume elevated

**ICP Summary:**
Managing Partners, GCs, and Compliance Heads at Geneva financial institutions + mid-large law firms. Pain: regulatory change volume + AI governance + personal liability. Urgency: FINMA examination, enforcement, bilateral negotiations.

**Competitive Position:**
Independent, conflict-free regulatory counsel with AI governance specialization + Switzerland-EU institutional bridge advisory.

**Recommended Action:**
Develop Regulatory Radar quarterly briefing as primary lead magnet. Pursue AI governance readiness assessment as secondary differentiator.

**Skipped:**
