# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

**Phase:** C287 Phase 01
**ICP:** Small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva
**Cycle:** 2026-05-28

---

## Competitive Landscape Overview

### What Exists in the Market

#### 1. General-Purpose AI Tools (BigTech)
- **ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini** — widely available, low cost, no Swiss-specific compliance
- Used by most attorneys informally
- No attorney-client privilege architecture
- Data residency: US/EU, not Switzerland
- Contract terms: uniform, non-negotiable, not nFADP-compliant
- **Gap:** These tools serve the AI user, not the law firm. No professional liability alignment.

#### 2. LegalTech AI Tools (International)
- **Casetext, Harvey, EvenLegaland, Spellbook** — legal-specific AI tools
- Some have enterprise tiers with DPA capabilities
- Primarily US/UK market focus
- Swiss bar recognition: limited
- nFADP compliance: generally absent or unclear
- **Gap:** International tools not built for Swiss regulatory context (nFADP, SBA guidelines, attorney privilege under Swiss law)

#### 3. Swiss LegalTech - Document Management
- **Lextree, LawFluent, Advokate** — Swiss-focused legal practice tools
- Primarily document management, billing, case management
- AI features: nascent, mostly draft assistance
- **Gap:** No Swiss AI governance framework offering; tools are practice management, not AI governance solutions

#### 4. IT/Security Consultants
- General cybersecurity firms offering "AI security audits"
- Not specialized in legal profession
- Deliverables: technical security assessments, not professional responsibility alignment
- **Gap:** Law firm gets a technical report but no clear path to carrier attestation or bar compliance

#### 5. Management Consulting / Big Law Alums
- Strategy consultants offering "AI governance frameworks"
- Cost: disproportionate for small firms (CHF 15k–80k for a framework document)
- Outputs: generic frameworks, not operationally implementable at boutique scale
- **Gap:** Too expensive, too abstract, wrong audience

#### 6. Bar Association Resources (SBA/FSA)
- Published AI guidelines (June 2024)
- Generic, principle-based
- No implementation checklist
- No small-firm-specific guidance
- OAG Geneva: organizes AI events, no operational tools
- **Gap:** Principles without practice — the gap between "you must protect confidentiality" and "here's how a 3-attorney firm does that" is unaddressed

---

## What's Missing (Market Gaps)

### Gap 1: A Defensible Small-Firm AI Governance Framework

**What small firms need:**
- A documented AI use policy
- A data processing agreement template for AI vendors
- An internal AI tool inventory (what's used, by whom, on what data)
- Staff training / acknowledgment framework
- Carrier attestation response template
- A clear "what adequate looks like for our size" definition

**What exists:** Nothing at accessible cost and scale for 2–5 attorney Geneva firms.

**Why it matters:** Carrier attestation questions are now unavoidable. Without a framework, firms either leave attestation blank or answer falsely. Both are bad.

### Gap 2: nFADP-Compliant AI Vendor Due Diligence

**What small firms need:**
- A vendor assessment checklist specifically for AI tools (data residency, sub-processor chain, DPA compatibility with Swiss professional secrecy)
- Pre-screened AI tools that meet Swiss law firm standards
- A process for reviewing and approving new AI tools

**What exists:** Generic vendor security questionnaires, not AI-tool-specific, not attorney-focused.

### Gap 3: Attorney-Client Privilege Protection Framework

**What small firms need:**
- Clear guidance on when AI use risks privilege waiver
- Safe harbor practices for AI-assisted work product
- Documentation that privilege is preserved
- A defensible position if challenged by bar or carrier

**What exists:** Abstract warnings. No operational framework. US Heppner ruling has been discussed in Swiss bar but no Swiss-specific safe harbor guidance published.

### Gap 4: Real-Time Staff AI Use Visibility

**What small firms need:**
- A way to know what AI tools staff are using
- An approval/denial process for new AI tools
- Audit trail for AI use in client matters
- Without hiring IT staff or implementing enterprise software

**What exists:** Nothing purpose-built for small law firms.

### Gap 5: Swiss-Specific AI Training for Attorneys

**What small firms need:**
- Not "AI 101" — specific training on professional responsibility, privilege, nFADP, carrier attestation
- In French and German, Geneva-relevant
- Delivered by people who understand law firm economics
- Not a 3-hour seminar — modular, on-demand, specific

**What exists:** OAG events are general. Private training is expensive. No digital on-demand product.

## Competitive Positioning

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness for Small Geneva Firm |
|---|---|---|
| BigTech AI (ChatGPT, etc.) | Free or low cost, easy access | No Swiss compliance, no privilege protection, non-negotiable contracts |
| LegalTech AI (Casetext, etc.) | Legal-specific features | Not Swiss-built, not nFADP-aware, expensive for small firms |
| Swiss LegalTech (Lextree, etc.) | Swiss-local, trusted | No AI governance layer, practice management focus |
| IT Security Consultants | Technical expertise | Not law-firm specialist, deliverables too technical |
| Management Consultants | Strategic frameworks | Way too expensive, wrong scale, abstract |
| Bar Association Resources | Authoritative | No operational implementation, no small-firm focus |
| **DigitalCoa.ch Position** | **Fills all gaps above — Swiss-native, attorney-accountability-focused, small-firm-scaled** | **Must be credible, must demonstrate Swiss legal market expertise, must price for boutique** |

---

## The Real Opportunity

The market is not served by any player that:
1. Speaks to small Geneva law firms in their own language (FR + EN)
2. Understands both the legal professional obligations AND the operational reality of a 2–5 person firm
3. Provides a framework that satisfies carrier attestation AND bar guidelines AND nFADP
4. Is position-able as "the practical AI governance solution for Swiss boutique firms"
5. Is affordable at small-firm budget level (not enterprise pricing)

No one is doing this yet. The gap is real, growing, and the urgency is increasing as carrier attestation becomes mandatory.
