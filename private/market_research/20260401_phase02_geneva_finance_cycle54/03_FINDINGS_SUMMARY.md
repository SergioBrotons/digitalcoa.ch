## ⭐ Key Findings — Phase 02 C54

### 1. Agentic AI Shift (2026) — Governance Gap Widens Structurally
Moving beyond LLM prompts to autonomous AI agents executing complex workflows. This fundamentally changes the governance risk profile — autonomous decisions require governance frameworks proportionate to decision consequence. WM firms deploying or planning agentic AI without governance = highest risk.

### 2. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 Primary Source — Obligations Already Operative
FINMA published Guidance 08/2024 on December 18, 2024. Key requirements: AI inventory, risk classification, assigned responsibilities, data quality controls, testing/monitoring, explainability, independent verification. On-site inspections active since Q4 2023. No ignorance defense for any Swiss WM firm.

### 3. Belief-Action Gap Confirmed Portfolio Maximum (~75pt)
92% of Swiss managers see AI as important; only 17% in deployment phase. 40% still in ideation/pilots. All face same FINMA expectations regardless of deployment stage.

### 4. CARF/CRS 2.0 Live Since January 1, 2026
CRS 2.0 operative with expanded scope. CARF design constraints active. AML/CFT AI monitoring obligations already running. No grace period.

### 5. SMB Competitive White Space Confirmed
No boutique-focused WM AI governance advisory competitor identified. Large consulting firms (PwC, Oliver Wyman) and legal specialists dominate. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL enterprise-only confirmed.

### 6. EU AI Act Annex III Extraterritorial Impact
Swiss WM firms with EU operations or EU-client-facing AI face stricter high-risk AI system requirements. Annex III categories directly relevant: creditworthiness, insurance risk, AML compliance.

### 7. Major Banks Scaling Fast — Boutique Pressure Intensifying
Pictet, Lombard Odier, Linvo making substantial AI investments. Competitive pressure on boutiques increasing as clients expect AI-enabled service.

### 8. Hybrid Model = Boutique Opportunity
67% of firms integrating digital + traditional face-to-face. Boutique advisory model (human judgment + digital tools) well-positioned if paired with strong governance.
