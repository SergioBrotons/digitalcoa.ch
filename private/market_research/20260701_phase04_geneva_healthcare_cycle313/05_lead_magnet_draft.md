## Service Module Architecture (12 Modules — 1 Architecture)

The Geneva Physician's AI Accountability Briefing delivers one evidence architecture with twelve service modules. The six C312 modules are retained from prior cycles. The six C313 Phase 04 modules are the new ones.

### C312 Modules (Retained)

1. **Vendor Contract Review** — bounded, specific, CHF 1,100. Reviews the AI vendor's Data Processing Agreement, sub-processor chain, and data residency attestation. Use this module before signing any new AI vendor contract or before any vendor contract renewal.

2. **Carrier Attestation Preparation** — encounter-level, CHF 2,200. Reviews the medical liability carrier attestation form, identifies the encounter-level evidence required, produces the attestation response anchored to physician-specific encounter evidence. Use this module before signing the annual attestation or before submitting the mid-year check-in response.

3. **Patient Consent Form Architecture** — face-level, CHF 1,650. Reviews the patient consent form, identifies AI tool disclosure gaps, produces a consent form revision with explicit AI tool disclosure (AI scribe, AI clinical decision support, AI drug-interaction checkers, sub-specialty AI delegation). Use this module before any new AI tool adoption or at any patient consent form update moment.

4. **Médecin Remplaçant Convention AI Clause** — CHF 1,100. Reviews the Convention de remplacement or coverage agreement, identifies AI governance gaps, produces the AI clause covering: (a) replacing physician AI tool use authorization, (b) encounter documentation distinguishing remplaçant vs. personal encounters, (c) patient consent form coverage during remplaçant period, (d) carrier attestation disclosure during remplaçant period.

5. **AI Tool Inventory Documentation** — CHF 1,650. Documents the physician's AI tool inventory with: (a) per-tool data residency attestation, (b) per-tool sub-processor chain, (c) per-tool Swissmedic medical device status (CH-REP, UDI), (d) per-tool patient consent form coverage, (e) per-tool carrier attestation disclosure status.

6. **Post-Incident Documentation Protocol** — CHF 2,200. Designs the post-incident documentation protocol for AI-assisted clinical decision errors. Includes: (a) incident identification and reporting, (b) carrier disclosure requirements, (c) Swissmedic operator incident reporting (where applicable), (d) patient disclosure obligations, (e) documentation archive requirements.

### C313 Phase 04 Modules (New)

7. **Medical Liability AI Attestation Form-Completion Vendor Audit** — CHF 1,650. Audits the attestation-completion workflow the physician uses. Identifies the attestation-completion tools (AI assistant, attestation-completion vendor, copy-paste from EMR export) that process patient encounter transcripts through vendors the carrier has not separately evaluated. Produces a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to physician-specific encounter evidence. Triggered by annual attestation cycle, mid-year carrier check-in, attestation-completion tool change, AI tool inventory change.

8. **Patient Consent Form Freshness Audit (Mid-Year Carrier Compliance)** — CHF 1,650. Audits the patient consent form against the carrier mid-year check-in requirements (consent form date, AI tool inventory disclosed, freshness against current AI tool adoption). Produces a consent form freshness audit memo and a consent form revision plan. Triggered by mid-year carrier check-in form receipt, new AI tool adoption, patient consent form update necessity.

9. **Médecin Remplaçant + Sub-Specialty Referral AI Authorization Documentation** — CHF 2,200. Documents the AI governance handover for médecin remplaçant coverage periods and sub-specialty referrals with AI delegation. Includes: Convention de remplacement AI clause, replacing physician AI tool use authorization, encounter documentation distinguishing remplaçant vs. personal encounters, sub-specialty AI delegation documentation (radiology, pathology), sub-specialty AI vendor data residency documentation. Triggered by new Convention de remplacement signing, coverage period activation, sub-specialty referral with AI use.

10. **EMR-AI Extension Vendor Data Residency Audit (HIN Corridor Scope)** — CHF 2,200. Audits the data residency and sub-processor chain for each AI extension operating on patient data within (or alongside) the Swiss-certified EMR. Maps the HIN corridor scope limitation, assesses Art. 321 CP medical secrecy exposure and nFADP Art.63 exposure for each AI extension, produces an EMR-AI extension vendor residency audit memo per tool. Triggered by new AI extension adoption, existing AI extension vendor change, EMR vendor change, sub-processor chain change, carrier or auditor EMR-AI extension question.

11. **Named-Physician AI Governance Peer Benchmark Gap Analysis (Geneva Medical Conferences)** — CHF 1,650. Monitors the named-physician AI governance case studies published at Geneva medical conferences (AMG specialty sections, FMH Continuing Medical Education, SSMIG Journée, HUG Grand Rounds). Maps the specific Geneva physician's current AI governance documentation (attestation, consent form, Convention de remplacement, EMR-AI extension documentation) against the named-physician benchmarks. Produces a peer benchmark gap analysis with remediation priorities. Triggered by annual Geneva medical conference cycle, new named-physician case study publication, attestation cycle preparation, consent form update timing.

12. **AI Scribe / Clinical Decision Support Vendor Succession / Patient Medical Records Portability Planning** — CHF 2,200. Plans for AI scribe / clinical decision support / drug-interaction AI vendor succession events (bankruptcy, acquisition, service termination, regulatory pivot). Assesses patient medical records exposure under Art. 321 CP medical secrecy and nFADP Art.63 on vendor exit, prepares patient consent form successor-controller language, reviews vendor contract succession clauses, plans patient notification architecture for vendor exit events. Triggered by AI vendor financial signal, vendor acquisition announcement, vendor service termination announcement, vendor contract renewal.

## Lead Magnet Promotion Channels

### Primary Channel: Médecin-Conseil Broker Referral
The médecin-conseil broker is the primary acquisition channel for Geneva physicians, analogous to the broker / specialty broker channels in C313 Phase 01 (legal), C313 Phase 02 (finance), and C313 Phase 03 (consulting). Medical liability brokers operating through Generali médecin-conseil, Bâloise médecin-conseil, Mobilière médecin-conseil, AXA Winterthur médecin-conseil, Allianz Suisse médecin-conseil, Zurich médecin-conseil, plus specialty brokers are the physician-facing delivery channel. The first actor to build a médecin-conseil broker referral program for Geneva physician AI governance services owns the primary channel.

### Secondary Channels: Geneva Medical Conference Speaking + AMG Geneva Section Workshop Leadership
AMG Geneva specialty section meetings (médecine interne, cardiologie, psychiatrie, pédiatrie, gynécologie, etc.), FMH Continuing Medical Education sessions, SSMIG Journée case study presentations, HUG Grand Rounds case study presentations. The C313 lead magnet position is to deliver one named-physician case study (with consent) or one AMG section workshop presentation per Geneva medical conference cycle. The case studies become the named-physician benchmarks.

### Tertiary Channel: Direct Geneva Physician Practice Outreach
Targeted outreach to Geneva independent physician practices through AMG membership directory (department-by-department), FMH specialty title directory, Geneva cantonal physician registry (Service du médecin cantonal). Direct practice outreach is the most expensive channel but the highest conversion for the C313 six-net-new findings (the physician needs to have the encounter-level attestation surprise, the patient consent freshness gap, the médecin remplaçant coverage gap, the EMR-AI extension residency gap, the named-physician peer benchmark gap, or the AI scribe vendor succession gap before the lead magnet produces engagement).
