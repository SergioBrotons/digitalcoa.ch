# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Consulting — C294 Phase 03

## What Exists, What's Missing, and the White Space

### Current Competitive Landscape

**Layer 1 — Management Consultancies (McKinsey, BCG, Accenture, etc.)**
Big firms have AI governance methodologies and frameworks. They are designed for enterprise clients with compliance departments, procurement processes, and large engagement budgets. A 5-person Geneva consulting boutique cannot access or afford these firms. Their methodologies are not portable to boutique scale.

**Layer 2 — Swiss Industry Associations (Swissholdings, Economiesuisse, industry chambers)**
Publish AI guidance for members. The guidance is principles-based, generic, and designed for larger organizations. Small consulting boutiques do not see themselves in this guidance. It tells them what to do without telling them how to do it at their scale.

**Layer 3 — AI Tool Vendors**
AI tool vendors (Microsoft, Google, Salesforce, specialized AI platforms) provide governance information about their own tools. This is vendor-side governance — designed to sell the tool, not to serve the consultant's accountability. It does not address how the consultant defends AI-assisted recommendations to clients.

**Layer 4 — Insurance Brokers**
Beginning to ask AI governance questions at proposal and renewal. They are risk assessors, not advisors. They cannot tell the consultant what to build — only what the current underwriting criteria are. This creates pressure without solution.

**Layer 5 — Geneva Legal Advisors**
Some Geneva law firms offer AI governance advice as part of data protection or technology law practices. This is legal analysis, not governance architecture. It tells the consultant what the law says, not how to run their practice.

---

### The White Space

**What Is Missing:**
A boutique-scale AI governance methodology for Geneva consulting firms — proportionate to the firm's size, adapted to the consulting engagement model, addressing the specific accountability chain from client engagement through sub-consultant delegation, with documentation architecture that satisfies client DDQs and insurance underwriting.

**Specific White Space Areas:**

1. **Client AI DDQ Response Architecture**
No service exists that helps Geneva consulting boutiques respond to client AI governance questionnaires at the engagement stage. The boutique needs a methodology to document AI-assisted work in a way that satisfies client due diligence without exposing the firm's proprietary methods.

2. **Proposal AI Methodology Defensibility Framework**
No boutique-scale framework exists for documenting AI tool evaluation, recommendation rationale, limitations considered, and human judgment applied. This is distinct from tool vendor assessment — it is about the consultant's decision process, not the tool's technical specifications.

3. **Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Protocol**
No Geneva-specific protocol for managing, documenting, and supervising sub-consultant AI use within client engagements. The existing frameworks (ISO 42001, vendor compliance guides) are not designed for project-based sub-delegation relationships.

4. **nFADP Personal Liability Naming + Protection**
No service specifically names nFADP personal liability exposure for Geneva consulting professionals and architects proportionate protection. This is distinct from entity-level data protection compliance — it addresses the individual's exposure, not the firm's.

5. **Insurance AI Governance Documentation**
No service helps boutiques produce the specific documentation insurance brokers are now asking for. The broker asks questions; no advisor helps the boutique answer them with documentation architecture.

---

### The Structural Moat

The Geneva consulting AI governance position is not claimed by any current provider. The first advisor to:
- Name the specific accountability problems Geneva consulting boutiques face
- Produce proportionate documentation methodology at boutique scale
- Address the sub-consultant delegation gap with a specific protocol
- Name nFADP personal liability exposure for this specific ICP

...will own the position because:
- The engagement creates the documentation that becomes the audit trail
- The relationship is sticky — accountability advisor is not easily changed
- The methodology becomes the internal standard for the firm
- Client DDQ responses become a competitive differentiator

---

### Competitive Response to Expect

When a credible independent position is established:
- Vendors will push "our tool has all the governance you need"
- Insurance brokers will claim their questionnaire process is sufficient
- Big consulting firms will say "we already do this for large clients"
- Legal advisors will say "we cover AI governance in our data protection practice"

Response to all: the Geneva boutique consulting firm faces specific accountability problems — client DDQ blocking engagements, proposal methodology defensibility, sub-consultant delegation, personal liability — that none of these providers have specifically addressed at boutique scale.
