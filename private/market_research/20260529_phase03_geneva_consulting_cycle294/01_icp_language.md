# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting — C294 Phase 03

## How Small Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems

### The Geneva Consulting ICP Profile

Small consulting firm, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable for every engagement and recommendation. Operates without internal legal, compliance, or IT infrastructure. Uses AI tools for client work, business development, and internal operations — often selected for productivity reasons without governance evaluation.

---

## ICP Language Patterns

### The Client AI DDQ Trigger
When a client sends an AI governance questionnaire:
- "A client sent us a questionnaire about AI and I didn't know how to answer most of it"
- "We've had to decline two engagements this quarter because we couldn't respond to the AI DDQ"
- "The client's form asked about our AI governance framework — we don't have one"
- "I told the client we had adequate AI governance but I couldn't back it up with anything"

### The Proposal Defensibility Problem
When recommending AI tools or methodologies to clients:
- "I recommended a specific AI tool to a client and then wondered if I could actually defend that recommendation"
- "If something goes wrong with the AI-assisted analysis, am I liable for the tool's failure?"
- "I don't actually know how the tool works — I just know it produces good outputs"
- "The client is paying for my judgment, not the tool's output"

### The Sub-Consultant Delegation Gap
When sub-delegating work:
- "I sub-contract part of my engagements to independent consultants — I have no idea what tools they use"
- "My sub-consultants send me AI-generated outputs and I present them to my client as if I produced them"
- "If the sub-consultant's AI makes an error, I'm accountable to my client — but I didn't make the decision"
- "I trust my sub-consultants but I can't verify what they're doing with AI"

### The Personal Liability Naming
When learning about nFADP:
- "I thought data protection liability was a firm-level thing — I didn't know it was personal"
- "The fine is CHF 250,000 — that's my personal house"
- "If I sign a contract with an AI clause, am I personally guaranteeing something?"
- "My general liability covers AI mistakes — or does it?"

### The Insurance Discovery
When discussing coverage:
- "The insurance broker asked about our AI tools at renewal — I had nothing to show them"
- "We were quoted significantly higher once the insurer found out we used AI in our work"
- "One insurer declined us entirely because of AI usage with no explanation"
- "I didn't realize insurance was already pricing AI risk"

### The Productivity Paradox
Why AI was adopted initially:
- "My clients expect faster turnaround — AI is the only way to deliver"
- "My competitors are using AI and I can't compete on price"
- "I started using AI to handle more clients without hiring"
- "The tools were free or cheap — the productivity gain was immediate"

## French ICP Language

### Déclencheur questionnaire client IA
- "Un client nous a envoyé un questionnaire sur l'IA et je ne savais pas comment répondre"
- "Nous avons dû décliner deux mandats ce trimestre car nous ne pouvions pas répondre au DDQ IA"
- "Le formulaire du client demandait notre cadre de gouvernance IA — nous n'en avons pas"

### Problème de défensibilité des propositions
- "J'ai recommandé un outil IA spécifique à un client et je me suis demandé si je pouvais défendre cette recommandation"
- "Si quelque chose se passe mal avec l'analyse assistée par IA, suis-je responsable de la défaillance de l'outil ?"
- "Je ne sais pas vraiment comment l'outil fonctionne — je sais juste qu'il produit de bons résultats"

### Lacune de délégation aux sous-conseillers
- "Je sous-traite une partie de mes mandats à des consultants indépendants — je n'ai aucune idée des outils qu'ils utilisent"
- "Mes sous-conseillers m'envoient des résultats générés par IA que je présente à mon client comme si je les avais produits"
- "Si l'IA du sous-conseiller commet une erreur, je suis responsable envers mon client"

### Signal assurance
- "Le courtier m'a demandé nos outils IA lors du renouvellement — je n'avais rien à lui montrer"
- "On nous a proposé une prime significativamente plus élevée une fois que l'assureur a découvert notre utilisation de l'IA"

## Key Vocabulary

| English Phrase | French Equivalent | What They Mean |
|---|---|---|
| "client AI DDQ" | "questionnaire client IA" | Client sending AI governance questionnaire as engagement prerequisite |
| "to defend the recommendation" | "défendre la recommandation" | Ability to justify AI tool/methodology choice if challenged |
| "sub-consultant" | "sous-conseiller" | Independent contractor doing part of engagement work |
| "personal liability" | "responsabilité personnelle" | Individual, not firm-level, exposure to fines/prosecution |
| "adequate AI governance" | "gouvernance IA adéquate" | undefined term they know they need but can't name |
| "the carrier asked" | "l'assureur a demandé" | Insurance renewal or proposal stage question about AI |
| "the client asked" | "le client a demandé" | Client questionnaire or DDQ about firm's AI usage |

## Entry Point Language

The phrase that opens the conversation:
- "I think we have an AI problem" / "Je crois que nous avons un problème d'IA"
- "Something came up at our insurance renewal" / "Quelque chose est apparu lors de notre renouvellement d'assurance"
- "A client sent us a questionnaire about AI" / "Un client nous a envoyé un questionnaire sur l'IA"
- "I recommended an AI tool and now I'm wondering..." / "J'ai recommandé un outil IA et maintenant je me demande..."

The conversation starts with a triggering event, not a general concern.
