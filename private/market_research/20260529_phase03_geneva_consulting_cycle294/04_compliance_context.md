# Compliance Context: Geneva Consulting — C294 Phase 03

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape for Small Consulting Boutiques

### Primary Regulatory Framework

**nFADP (Revised Federal Act on Data Protection)**
Applies to: All consulting firms processing Swiss personal data in client engagements.

Key provisions:
- Art.8: Profiling — AI-assisted analysis of client data may constitute profiling with notification obligations
- Art.9: Lawfulness of processing — AI tool selection requires lawful basis analysis for each processing activity
- Art.63: Criminal liability — personal liability for unauthorized personal data processing; CHF 250,000 ceiling applies to individuals, not just entities
- Art.20/21: Right to explanation for automated decisions — client may have right to understand how AI assisted in consulting recommendation

DPA requirements:
- Data processing agreements required with AI tool vendors
- Standard commercial DPAs often do not cover AI-specific processing (model training, inference, activity logging)
- Vendor terms of service accepted by individual consultant may create personal obligations

**Council of Europe AI Convention (March 2025)**
Applies to: Consulting firms deploying AI in client work.

Key obligations:
- Deployer due diligence for AI systems used in professional services
- Human oversight obligations — defined by the deployer, not the vendor
- Documentation obligations — the deployer must document AI use in client engagements
- Implementation in Switzerland in progress — obligations are live during deliberation period

**Insurance Market AI Governance Requirements**
Not regulation, but creating effective market pressure:
- AI usage questions at proposal and renewal
- Documentation requirements implied by questions asked
- Premium differentiation based on governance architecture
- Declination risk for firms with no documented AI governance

### The Geneva Consulting Specific Regulatory Profile

**What Applies to This ICP:**
- nFADP for all client data processing
- AI Convention for AI deployment in client engagements
- Insurance underwriting criteria for AI risk
- Client contractual AI governance requirements (through DDQs and engagement terms)

**What Does Not Apply (Yet):**
- FINMA oversight — unless the consulting firm also holds financial licenses
- FMH standards — medical consulting, not general consulting
- SRO frameworks — only for regulated financial intermediaries

**The Regulatory Timing Problem:**
The consulting boutique faces a regulatory landscape where obligations are:
- Live (nFADP)
- Emerging (AI Convention during implementation period)
- Market-driven (insurance requirements, client DDQs)
- Undefined in boutique-scale terms (no specific methodology exists)

The firm cannot wait for clearer guidance — the obligations are already present.

---

### Documentation Requirements Specific to Consulting

**Client Engagement Documentation:**
- AI tools used in client engagement
- Data inputs and processing activities
- Human review and authorization of AI-assisted recommendations
- Sub-consultant AI use within the engagement

**Proposal Documentation:**
- AI tool/methodology recommendation rationale
- Limitations and model considerations
- Human judgment applied to AI outputs
- Client acknowledgment of AI-assisted recommendations

**Sub-Consultant Delegation Documentation:**
- Which sub-consultants use AI tools
- What AI tools are used in delegated work
- How AI-assisted outputs are reviewed and incorporated
- Liability allocation for AI-assisted sub-delegated work

**Insurance Documentation:**
- AI tools in use across the firm
- Governance framework for AI deployment
- AI-related incident history
- Human oversight mechanisms

---

### The Gap

No Geneva-specific guidance translates these regulatory requirements into:
- Proportionate documentation for a 5-person consulting boutique
- A methodology that fits the consulting engagement model
- A sub-consultant delegation protocol
- A personal liability protection framework for the individual consultant

The regulatory framework exists. The boutique-scale implementation methodology does not.
