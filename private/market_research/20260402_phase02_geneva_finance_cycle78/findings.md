### 1. SBA Stablecoin FinIA Battle — "We Do Not Want a Luxembourg Scenario" (March 26, 2026) [NET-NEW]
- **Source**: Swiss Bankers Association (SBA/swissbanking.ch) interview with Martin Hess (Head of Digital Finance) + Natalie (SBVg lead on FinIA consultation)
- **Core conflict**: Federal Council's FinIA amendment draft would force Swiss banks to establish subsidiaries as payment instrument institutions to issue stablecoins
- **SBA position**: Banks are already comprehensively regulated — should be able to issue stablecoins directly under existing banking license; EU allows this
- **"Luxembourg scenario"** = forced subsidiary requirement creating bureaucracy without safety benefit
- **US GENIUS Act**: New federal US stablecoin legislation — "game changer" creating urgent competitive pressure
- **SBA demands**: Stress tests + regulatory impact assessment on financial stability before opening stablecoin gates widely
- **Disintermediation risk**: Real — if Swiss banks can't issue directly, money flows to new actors; if CH doesn't adapt, decisions made abroad
- **FinSA angle**: Stablecoins in the draft serve payment purposes, not financial instruments — FinSA application questioned

### 4. Nature Transition Financing — Detailed Breakdown [NET-NEW Detail]
- **Source**: SBA-commissioned Boston Consulting Group (BCG) + Quantis study
- **CHF 5.3B/year needed by 2050** (confirmed consistent with C75/C77)
- **Current spend**: CHF 3.2B/year (public financing for water infrastructure + biodiversity-linked agricultural subsidies)
- **Gap**: CHF 2.1B/year additional incremental investment needed (~66% increase)
- **85% publicly financed** (high proportion of publicly owned assets + limited commercial viability)
- **Largest financing areas (>75% of total)**:
  - Water infrastructure: wastewater management, river revitalisation, nature-based solutions (NbS), hydropower rehabilitation
  - Regenerative agriculture: capital investments + income support for transitioning farmers
- **Swiss banks' role**: Not materially exposed to nature risks due to adaptation infrastructure + insurance coverage; but can support via green/sustainability-linked loans, sustainable supply chain financing, advisory for SMEs
