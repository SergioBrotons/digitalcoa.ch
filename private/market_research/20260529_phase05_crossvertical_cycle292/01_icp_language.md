# 01 — ICP Language: Cross-Vertical ICP Language — The Geneva Register

*Phase 05 · Cycle 292 · 2026-05-29*
*Synthesis across Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs*

---

## Purpose

This file identifies the language patterns that function identically across all four ICP verticals in C292 — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — and explains how C292's cross-vertical synthesis produces language structures that did not appear in any prior Phase 05 synthesis. The C292 vertical findings (Phases 01–04) introduced new mechanisms not present in C291 — agentic AI continuous authorization, institutional circuit intelligence exclusion, client questionnaire documentation paradox, non-traditional standard-setting displacement, EU AI Convention unnamed obligations, and criminal documentation architecture distinction. These produce language patterns only C292 synthesis can generate.

---

## The Core Finding

The C292 cross-vertical language patterns reflect a market that has moved beyond uncertainty into confirmed operative exposure. In C291, the language was about "I may have a problem." In C292, the language is about "I have confirmed the problem and I still don't know what to do."

The four Geneva professional ICPs — small-law attorney, boutique WM managing director, consulting MD, independent physician — use the same structural language in C292, now with C292-specific regulatory developments (Art. 167a CPC January 2025, FDPIC May 2025 enforcement-live posture, FINMA Guidance 08/2024 inconsistency with existing attestations, Swissmedic AI tool registration follow-ups, AI Convention March 2025 signatory) creating confirmed past exposure that is more motivating than anticipated future risk.

## The C292 Universal Linguistic Structures

### Structure 1: "I Signed Something. Now I Know What It Committed Me To. It's Worse Than I Thought." — Retrospective Confirmation Language

**Legal (C292 pattern):**
> "The CPC amendment changed the in-house counsel privilege rules in January 2025. I looked at my carrier attestation from before that and realized I'm attesting to a framework that doesn't account for the structural split. I signed something that was already out of date when I signed it."

**Finance (C292 pattern):**
> "FINMA's Guidance 08/2024 came out in December. I compared it to my carrier attestation from 2023. They're inconsistent. My attestation commits me to standards that are different from what the regulator published. I'm out of compliance with something I signed two years ago."

**Consulting (C292 pattern):**
> "The client changed their MSA. Added an AI governance section. My lawyer reviewed it and said it was standard. But the AI governance section has specific obligations — tool disclosure, methodology documentation, human oversight verification. I signed something committing to standards I didn't know I was accepting. The client is already asking about compliance."

**Healthcare (C292 pattern):**
> "Swissmedic sent a follow-up on the AI tool registration. The questions are different from what I answered last year. My original attestation doesn't cover the tools I'm actually using now. The form asked about tools that were current six months ago. The market has moved. My attestation hasn't."

**The C292 linguistic mechanism:** The ICP has retrospective confirmation of past exposure. The C292 developments — Art. 167a CPC, FDPIC enforcement-live, FINMA Guidance 08/2024, AI Convention signatory, Swissmedic follow-ups — are not projections. They are events. The ICP can point to the specific document or development that confirmed their exposure. This is categorically different from anticipatory anxiety. The ICP is not worried something bad might happen — they know something bad has already happened and are now managing the confirmed consequence.

**Why it works across verticals:** The carrier form is the same instrument in every sector — written by underwriting teams, presented as a signature requirement for policy renewal, experienced as personal liability, and now revealed as inconsistent with C292 regulatory developments. No Geneva professional body in any sector offers pre-signing review. The cost of that absence is now measurable and confirmed.

### Structure 2: "AI Is Acting Continuously. I Don't Have a Single Decision Point to Show." — Agentic Authorization Language

**Legal (C292 pattern):**
> "The AI case management system is making decisions continuously throughout the day. It's not one tool I turned on. It's a continuous process. When someone asks me to show what human authorization happened, I can show I turned on the system. I can't show what happened every time it made a decision between then and now."

**Finance (C292 pattern):**
> "The portfolio construction AI is rebalancing continuously. It's not a discrete event I can point to. It's running all the time. My carrier form asks what specific AI-assisted decisions I reviewed. The system doesn't create those decision points. I don't have the evidence."

**Consulting (C292 pattern):**
> "The AI analysis tool runs continuously on client deliverables. I review the final output. I don't know what the model did at 2am when it was processing the client data. The carrier wants evidence of human review. I can show the final review. I can't show the continuous monitoring."

**Healthcare (C292 pattern):**
> "The AI diagnostic support runs continuously. It's not one decision I made. It's running in the background all the time. When Swissmedic asks what human oversight happened, I can show I turned it on. I can't show what it does every time it processes a patient."

**The C292 linguistic mechanism:** The C292-specific finding is agentic AI — systems that execute autonomously without discrete human authorization moments. The accountability question is not "did we use AI?" but "did we authorize what AI did autonomously, continuously, and can we demonstrate that authorization?" This language structure appears for the first time in C292 Phase 02 (finance) and is confirmed across all four verticals in C292 synthesis. The language is structurally different from prior "AI arrived before I governed it" patterns because it describes continuous autonomous action, not accumulated tools.

**Why it works across verticals:** Agentic AI is arriving in all four verticals before any Geneva-calibrated governance methodology exists for it. The specific evidence gap — documented human authorization moments — is required by third-generation carrier forms but is structurally impossible for continuous AI systems. The ICP names this impossibility directly: they cannot show what the system does between authorization and output.

### Structure 3: "Someone Else Is Setting the Standard. I Don't Know Who." — Non-Traditional Standard-Setting Language

**Legal (C292 pattern):**
> "My carrier sent me an AI governance checklist. My software vendor sent a compliance guide. The SBA has guidelines but they're general. I don't know which standard applies to me and whether the standard was designed to protect me or to protect someone else."

**Finance (C292 pattern):**
> "The carrier's AI governance questionnaire is more specific than anything published by FINMA or my SRO. The carrier's questionnaire is essentially defining what adequate AI governance means for boutique WMs. The carrier wrote the form for their own risk evaluation. They're the ones setting the operative standard."

**Consulting (C292 pattern):**
> "My vendor sent a compliance guide. My carrier sent a checklist. An industry body published a self-assessment framework. I'm following standards designed by parties whose primary interest isn't my accountability protection. Adopting someone else's definition of adequate governance means accepting their accountability framework."

**Healthcare (C292 pattern):**
> "The FMH principle says human oversight is required. The carrier asks specific questions about AI governance in the attestation. Swissmedic wants documentation in the tool registration. No Geneva authority has published what 'adequate' means for an independent practice. I'm being measured against a standard that nobody has defined."

**The C292 linguistic mechanism:** Non-traditional standard-setting — vendors, carriers, and industry bodies publishing frameworks designed for their own accountability interests, not for Geneva boutique accountability. The ICP experiences this as operative standard-setting by parties with different interests, without the ICP evaluating whether those standards serve their specific exposure profile. This is new in C292 Phase 03 consulting findings and confirmed across all four verticals in synthesis.

**Why it works across verticals:** The guidance vacuum is being filled by non-traditional actors — and the ICP knows this is happening but has no alternative. The standards are being set by parties who bear different risks than the boutique practitioner. The ICP is measured against standards they did not evaluate and cannot change.

### Structure 4: "I Am Outside the Room Where the Standard Is Being Set." — Institutional Intelligence Exclusion Language

**Legal (C292 pattern):**
> "HUG and CHUV have AI governance committees. They're making decisions about AI standards. Those decisions form the operative standard. I'm a two-attorney firm in Geneva. I don't have access to those rooms. I'm building my governance against standards that are forming somewhere I'm not."

**Finance (C292 pattern):**
> "The institutional circuit in Geneva — that's where the accountability expectations are actually forming. Carrier iterations, informal exchanges, institutional decisions. By the time anything gets published, the decision has already been made. The question is whether you were in the room."

**Consulting (C292 pattern):**
> "The accountability formation velocity in Geneva is faster than published guidance reflects. The carrier's latest questionnaire iteration, the clause the client's lawyer inserted into their MSA, the question that came up at a Geneva institutional exchange — those are where the standards are forming. Not in the published guidance."

**Healthcare (C292 pattern):**
> "CHUV and HUG have governance committees, legal resources, compliance infrastructure. When these institutions make AI governance decisions, those decisions shape the operative standard. I build governance in isolation. The standard is being set by institutional participants who have resources I don't have."

**The C292 linguistic mechanism:** Structural exclusion from the rooms where standards form. The ICP names this as a structural disadvantage, not an information gap. The institutional intelligence is not unavailable through published channels — it is structurally unavailable because the formation process is not public. This is new in C292 Phase 04 (healthcare) and confirmed across all four verticals in synthesis.

**Why it works across verticals:** The formation mechanism is the same in all four sectors: institutional decisions at well-resourced entities form operative standards that apply to independent practitioners and boutique firms without their participation. The circuit intelligence is the structural moat — it cannot be replicated through secondary research.

The C292 four structures transfer across verticals with minimal or no modification:

- Finance: portfolio construction AI continuous rebalancing without authorization moments
- Consulting: AI analysis tool continuous processing of deliverables without review architecture
- Healthcare: AI diagnostic support continuous processing without oversight documentation

**Structure: "Someone else is setting the standard. I don't know who."**
- Legal: carrier checklist, vendor compliance guide, SBA guidelines — different standards, different interests
- Finance: carrier questionnaire defines adequate governance for boutique WM — underwriting design
- Consulting: vendor guide, carrier checklist, industry self-assessment — external frameworks for external accountability
- Healthcare: carrier attestation, Swissmedic registration, FMH principle — no Geneva-calibrated standard exists

**Structure: "I am outside the room where the standard is being set."**
- Legal: HUG/CHUV governance decisions forming operative standard without independent practitioner participation
- Finance: institutional circuit forming accountability expectations through non-public channels
- Consulting: Geneva accountability formation velocity outpacing published guidance
- Healthcare: institutional intelligence structural exclusion from standard-formation process

## Recognition Language: What Creates Briefing Readiness in C292

The C292 recognition trigger:
> "You signed the carrier attestation. Your client sent an AI governance questionnaire. The FINMA guidance came out in December. Swissmedic followed up on your AI tool registration. In every case, you're being asked to show documentation you don't have, built to standards nobody has defined, against commitments you made without reviewing what they required."

This sentence names the confirmed C292 condition — retrospective confirmation of past exposure — and creates the recognition condition that leads to briefing request. The ICP who hears this recognizes their own situation and cannot unrecognize it.
