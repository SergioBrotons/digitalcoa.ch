### HIGH PRIORITY REGULATORY (Q2 2026)

#### R1: NUFG/LGDE — Corporate Governance Regulation
**Source:** Phase 01 + 02
**Status:** Consultation open until July 9, 2026

**What it is:** Federal Council indirect counter-proposal to Konzernverantwortungsinitiative. Bundesgesetz für nachhaltige Unternehmensführung.

**Scope:**
- Due diligence: ~30 Swiss companies (5,000+ employees + CHF 1.5B+ revenue)
- Reporting: ~100 Swiss companies (1,000+ employees + CHF 450M+ revenue)

**Key provisions:**
- Sorgfaltsprüfungspflicht (due diligence obligation) — extends beyond current EU requirements in 15+ areas
- State supervisory authority: RAB (Federal Audit Oversight Authority)
- Civil liability: limited to subsidiaries; Verschuldungshaftung (negligence-based)
- Penalties: up to 3% of global turnover
- 5-year prescription period from knowledge; 20-year substantive period
- Swiss Finish: 15+ points where Swiss rules exceed EU requirements

**Timeline:**
- Consultation: Now — July 9, 2026
- Expected: National Council + Council of States debate late 2026
- Referendum risk: Likely if passed as drafted

**DigitalCoa angle:** Board liability advisory — NUFG compliance as new executive responsibility

#### R2: Trump Pharma Tariffs — US Trade Regulation
**Source:** Phase 02
**Status:** Announcement made; implementation 120-180 days

**What it is:** Trump administration executive order on pharmaceutical tariffs.

**Key provisions:**
- Up to 100% tariffs on some pharmaceutical imports to US
- Switzerland rate: 15% (reduced vs other countries)
- EU, Japan, South Korea: 15%
- UK: 10%
- Exemptions: Roche (via Genentech) + Novartis — through US manufacturing agreements
- Companies committing to US factory construction: 20% tariff during construction period
- "Most favored nation" pricing + US factory commitment = full exemption

**Timeline:**
- Implementation: 120 days (smaller companies) to 180 days (larger companies)
- Estimated effective: Q3-Q4 2026

**DigitalCoa angle:** Swiss pharma board nearshoring decision — regulatory/tariff as capital allocation driver

#### R3: Swissness IP Regulations — IPI Practice Change
**Source:** Phase 01
**Status:** Announced March 23, 2026; effective now

**What it is:** IPI (Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property) changed practice on Swiss cross usage.

**Key provisions:**
- Swiss cross CAN now be inserted between "Swiss" and "Engineering" or "Research"
- Example: "Swiss Engineering AG" can use Swiss cross
- IPI insists: cross must be exactly between two terms; square side length ≤ font size
- IPI position: Swiss cross at TOP of appellation hierarchy (not just white cross on red square)
- IP specialists disagree with this broad interpretation

**Timeline:** Announced March 23, 2026; already in effect; no legislative process

**DigitalCoa angle:** Swiss brand governance — IPI practice change creating brand risk for Swiss companies
