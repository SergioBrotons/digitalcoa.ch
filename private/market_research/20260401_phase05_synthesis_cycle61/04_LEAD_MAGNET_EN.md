### How Many of These Apply to Your Firm?

Geneva firms face six overlapping AI governance obligations simultaneously. Most don't know how many apply to them — or which ones they're already in breach of.

**The six obligations:**

1. **Federal AI Ordinance (Switzerland)** — in force since January 1, 2025 (16+ months)
2. **EU AI Act GPAI obligations** — in force since August 2, 2025
3. **EU AI Act High-Risk AI conformity** — August 2, 2026 deadline
4. **FADP Article 21** — ongoing obligation for automated decision-making
5. **Swissdamed** — July 1, 2026 enforcement (~91 days)
6. **Section 301 / Forced Labour** — supply chain ESG compliance (April 15 written comments deadline)

### The Three 2026 Deadlines

**Ranked by urgency. All three are in 2026. None should be treated as optional.**

**Deadline 1 — July 1, 2026 (~91 days)**
**Swissdamed enforcement**

Applies to: Any firm processing health-related personal data of Swiss patients or operating in Swiss healthcare. This is the most imminent enforcement date.

What it means: FADP gap assessment must be complete. Privacy notices must be updated. Data breach procedures must be in place. External service provider agreements must be reviewed.

What happens if you miss it: Swiss FDPIC enforcement has authority to impose sanctions. Remediation after enforcement is more costly than remediation before.

**Deadline 2 — June 15–17, 2026 (~75 days)**
**G7 Évian**

Applies to: All Geneva firms serving international clients or operating in sectors affected by G7 foreign policy outcomes. The G7 communiqué will shape cross-border investment restrictions, foreign buyer rules, and potentially AI governance standards.

What it means: International HNW clients may delay decisions pending G7 outcomes. Foreign buyer restrictions in Geneva real estate, cross-border investment mandates, and AI governance frameworks may be affected. Geneva firms should have a G7 impact assessment ready for their specific client segments.

What happens if you ignore it: Transaction uncertainty becomes transaction loss. The firm with a prepared G7 impact briefing for clients is the firm that retains client trust through the G7 period.

**Deadline 3 — August 2, 2026 (~4 months)**
**EU AI Act double deadline**

Applies to: Any Geneva firm using high-risk AI (Annex III: credit decisions, employment decisions, healthcare decisions, financial algorithms) or HR AI systems. The legacy GPAI grandfathering expires on this date. High-risk AI conformity assessments must be complete.

What it means: If your firm uses AI for portfolio management (WM), AVM (real estate), HR decisions (any firm), clinical decisions (healthcare), or credit decisions — and you serve EU markets or EU clients — your AI systems must have conformity assessments documented before August 2, 2026.

What happens if you miss it: EU enforcement of the AI Act is activating. Non-compliance with high-risk AI requirements can result in fines up to 3% of global annual turnover or EUR 15M, whichever is higher.

### The Compound Cost Insight

**Why treating frameworks separately is inefficient.**

A single AI workflow in a Geneva wealth management firm simultaneously triggers:
- Federal AI Ordinance (AI risk assessment + documentation)
- EU AI Act GPAI (transparency obligations for external AI model use)
- FADP Art. 21 (automated decision-making affecting clients)
- CARF/CRS (data quality for client financial information)

A single AI workflow in a Geneva medical practice simultaneously triggers:
- Swissdamed (health data processing procedures)
- FADP Art. 21 (automated clinical decision-making)
- Federal AI Ordinance (AI risk assessment)
- EU AI Act Annex III (if serving EU patients with AI diagnostic tools)

**The firm that builds one integrated AI governance framework covering these simultaneously is more efficient — and more defensible — than the firm that builds six separate compliance documents.**

The Swiss-EU bilateral package — referenced in prior cycles as a potential compliance harmonization pathway — is confirmed absent from the April 1, 2026 Federal Council agenda. Multi-framework compound compliance cost is the baseline condition, not a temporary anomaly. Plan accordingly.

### The Integrated Governance Response

**One framework, multiple obligations.**

The integrated AI governance framework that covers your portfolio obligation should include:

1. **AI Tool Inventory** — every AI tool used, what data it processes, which clients it affects, which vendors, where data is stored

2. **Regulatory Applicability Map** — for each AI tool, which obligations apply (Federal AI Ordinance / EU AI Act GPAI / FADP Art. 21 / Swissdamed / EU AI Act Annex III / Section 301)

3. **Obligation Gap Assessment** — for each applicable obligation, current state vs. required state with specific remediation actions

4. **Named Governance Responsibility** — one individual accountable for AI governance across all frameworks

5. **August 2, 2026 Readiness File** — conformity documentation for any AI tool that may qualify as Annex III high-risk AI, prepared for enforcement activation
