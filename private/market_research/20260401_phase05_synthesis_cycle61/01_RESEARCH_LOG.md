### 1. Compound Compliance Cost Framework — Multi-Layer Burden Across All Six Sectors (NET-NEW C61)
**Source:** Synthesis from Phases 01–04 C61 findings

All six Geneva sectors face compounding multi-layer compliance costs simultaneously:
- Federal AI Ordinance (16+ months operative)
- EU AI Act GPAI obligations (in force since August 2, 2025)
- FADP Art. 21 (ongoing)
- Swissdamed (July 1, 2026 — ~91 days for healthcare; impacts legal, consulting, WM sectors with healthcare client segments)
- Section 301 forced labour (April 15, 2026 written comments — ongoing exposure for finance, consulting, real estate with supply chain mandates)
- CARF/CRS implementation (ongoing for finance/WM sector)

**Implication (NET-NEW C61):** The compound compliance cost is not additive — it is multiplicative. A single AI workflow in a Geneva wealth management firm can simultaneously trigger obligations under Federal AI Ordinance, EU AI Act GPAI, FADP Art. 21, and CARF data quality requirements. No single framework addresses this convergence. The firms that build an integrated AI governance framework — covering all obligations simultaneously — will be more efficient than those treating each framework separately.

### 4. August 2, 2026 Double Deadline — Confirmed and Cross-Sectoral (NET-NEW C61 Update)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (via Phase 01 C60 carry-forward); Federal Council (April 1, 2026 synthesis)

August 2, 2026 confirmed as double deadline:
1. **EU AI Act high-risk AI conformity assessments** — legacy GPAI grandfathering expires; firms must have conformity assessments completed for Annex III high-risk AI systems
2. **HR AI conformity assessments** — all high-risk AI systems used in employment context (affecting WM firms with HR AI, consulting firms with HR AI tools, healthcare practices with staffing AI) must be compliant

**Cross-sector impact:**
- Legal: Law firms using AI for case assessment or document review — likely Annex III
- Finance/WM: Portfolio AI, AVMs, risk modeling — likely Annex III
- Consulting: HR AI tools for client talent assessment — HR AI conformity required
- Healthcare: AI diagnostic tools, clinical decision support — likely Annex III
- Real Estate: AVMs, tenant screening AI — likely Annex III + HR AI for internal staffing

**Implication (NET-NEW C61):** August 2, 2026 is not just a Geneva compliance date — it is a European compliance date. Any Geneva firm using high-risk AI with EU market exposure faces this deadline simultaneously with European firms. The competitive disadvantage of being non-compliant is now European in scope, not just Swiss.
