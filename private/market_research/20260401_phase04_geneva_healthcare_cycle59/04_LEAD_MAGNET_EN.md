## What Swissdamed Actually Requires

Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Adverse Event System) is Switzerland's sector-specific system for reporting adverse events involving medical devices. If your practice uses AI-assisted diagnostic tools, patient monitoring systems, imaging AI, or any AI system that touches patient data and could affect clinical decisions, you have Swissdamed reporting obligations.

The July 1, 2026 deadline means you need:
- A documented process for identifying AI-assisted medical device incidents
- A documented process for reporting to Swissmedic via Swissdamed
- Staff trained on the new reporting requirements
- AI governance documentation that connects to your Swissdamed obligations

Most small practices don't have any of this yet.

## The Three-Layer Compliance Problem

Your practice faces three concurrent data protection and AI governance frameworks:

**Layer 1 — FADP (Swiss Data Protection Law)**
Patient data must be processed lawfully, for specified purposes, with data minimization, and with appropriate security. AI tools that analyze patient data for diagnostic support or administrative purposes must comply.

**Layer 2 — Swissdamed (July 1, 2026)**
Sector-specific adverse event reporting. AI-assisted medical devices in your practice that malfunction or contribute to adverse events must be reported.

**Layer 3 — Federal AI Ordinance**
Human oversight of AI-assisted clinical decisions is required. You must be able to demonstrate that a human reviewed and took responsibility for any AI-generated recommendation affecting patient care.

---

## The Acute Tension: Data Minimization vs. AI Utility

FADP requires data minimization — collect only what you need. AI systems perform better with comprehensive data. This is not a theoretical conflict. It's an operational governance question every small practice using AI must answer.

The answer is not to refuse AI. The answer is to document your rationale: which AI tools, what patient data they process, why that data is necessary for the intended purpose, and how you satisfy the minimization requirement within your specific clinical context.

## The Small Practice AI Inventory

What AI tools does a 1-10 practitioner medical practice typically use? Check what applies to yours:

- AI-assisted diagnostic imaging or analysis tools
- Patient scheduling or administrative automation
- AI-powered clinical decision support (symptom checkers, drug interaction checkers)
- Remote patient monitoring with AI analysis
- Practice management software with AI features
- AI-assisted note-taking during consultations

Any of these that process patient data creates FADP obligations. Any that could contribute to adverse events creates Swissdamed obligations. Any that affect clinical decisions creates Federal AI Ordinance human oversight obligations.

## Your 90-Day Swissdamed + AI Governance Sprint

**Week 1–2: AI Tool Inventory**
Identify every AI tool in your practice that touches patient data. Document: tool name, vendor, what data it processes, where it's hosted (Swiss vs. US vs. other), who has access.

**Week 3–4: FADP Gap Assessment**
Map your current patient data handling against FADP requirements. Where is the gap between current practice and lawful processing?

**Week 5–6: Swissdamed Process Documentation**
Document your adverse event identification and reporting process. Who is responsible? What triggers a report? How do you connect AI-assisted incidents to Swissdamed?

**Week 7–8: Federal AI Ordinance Human Oversight Documentation**
For each AI tool that affects clinical decisions, document: who reviews the AI output, what the review process looks like, how decisions are documented.

**Week 9–10: Staff Training**
Ensure everyone who uses AI tools in your practice understands the governance framework and their responsibilities.

**Week 11–12: Gap Closure and Documentation**
Finalize and test your Swissdamed reporting process. Ensure your AI governance documentation is complete and accessible.
