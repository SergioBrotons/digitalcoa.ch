# Phase 04 C59 — Lead Magnet Framework: "The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework (Swissdamed Edition)"

## Positioning
**For:** Small Geneva medical practices (1–10 practitioners) using or considering AI tools — with a specific Swissdamed July 1, 2026 remediation trigger
**Against:** Generic healthtech vendor pitches; compliance checklists built for hospitals
**Unique hook:** Swissdamed is ~90 days away. This is your remediation window — not your preparation window. This framework gives small practices a structured way to close the gap before July 1.

---

## Core Promise
Your AI tools and your Swissdamed obligations are creating a three-layer compliance exposure. This framework helps you close the gap in 90 days.

---

## Problem Statement (3 layers)

### Layer 1: Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — ~90 Days, Remediation Window
Swissmedic leadership is approximately 3 months into implementation. Enforcement posture is less predictable than during the preparation phase. Small practices without a remediation plan are now in the highest-risk period: they think they have more time than they do.

### Layer 2: Three-Layer Data Protection Framework
Small medical practices face three concurrent frameworks:
- **FADP Art. 13:** Patient data protection, purpose limitation, data minimization
- **Swissdamed:** Adverse event and pharmacovigilance reporting (sector-specific, July 1, 2026)
- **Federal AI Ordinance:** Human oversight of AI-assisted clinical decisions

The most acute tension: FADP's data minimization principle vs. AI's need for comprehensive patient data.

### Layer 3: International Patient Data (G7)
Geneva practices serving international patients (G7 nationals) face compounded exposure: FADP + Swissdamed + G7 national data protection regulations simultaneously.

---

## Lead Magnet Structure

1. **The Swissdamed 90-Day Sprint:** What you need to have documented by July 1, 2026 — and how to prioritize if you're starting now.

2. **The Three-Layer Compliance Assessment:** FADP + Swissdamed + Federal AI Ordinance — score yourself across all three.

3. **Data Minimization vs. AI Utility — The Resolution:** How to document your data governance approach when AI requires more data than FADP's minimization principle prefers.

4. **The Small Practice AI Inventory:** What AI tools does a 1-5 practitioner medical office typically use, and which ones touch patient data?

5. **CTA:** Download the Swissdamed + AI Governance Checklist for Small Medical Practices → lead capture. Book a 30-minute Swissdamed remediation review → consultation.
