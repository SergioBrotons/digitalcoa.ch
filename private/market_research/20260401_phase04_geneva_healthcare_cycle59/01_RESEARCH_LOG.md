### 2. Three-Layer Data Protection Framework for Medical Practices
**Source:** The Compliance Digest synthesis, Swiss data protection principles

Medical practices face three concurrent data protection frameworks:
- **FADP (Swiss Data Protection Law):** Lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, confidentiality, accountability
- **Swissdamed:** Sector-specific pharmacovigilance and medical device adverse event reporting system (July 1, 2026)
- **Federal AI Ordinance:** AI-specific obligations when AI is used in patient-facing or clinical decision support

Additionally: **HRA (Human Research Act)** for practices involved in clinical research.

**Healthcare implications (NET-NEW C59):** The most operationally acute tension is **data minimization vs. AI utility**: FADP's data minimization principle requires collecting only necessary data, but AI systems require comprehensive data to function effectively. Medical practices using AI for diagnostic support, patient monitoring, or administrative automation must navigate this tension explicitly in their governance documentation.
