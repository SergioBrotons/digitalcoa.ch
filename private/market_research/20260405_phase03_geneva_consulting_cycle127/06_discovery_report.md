## EN — Discovery Report: Geneva Consulting Market (C127)

### Research Question
What are the highest-value, under-addressed Geneva consulting market opportunities for advisory positioning in Q2 2026?

### Six NET-NEW Findings

**1. CSRD Phase 2 Implementation Rush — Swiss EU-Listed Company Advisory**
CSRD Phase 2 reports are due 2026 (covering FY2025) for large EU-listed Swiss companies and EU-parented Swiss subsidiaries. Many underestimated Phase 1 and now face Phase 2 deadlines with gaps in data infrastructure, double materiality assessments, and auditor readiness. Geneva sustainability consultants report acute demand for catch-up programs, data quality remediation, and auditor-ready disclosure packages. Advisory opportunity: CSRD Phase 2 catch-up, data remediation, disclosure packages. *Confidence: High.*

**2. DORA ICT Third-Party Risk Management — TPRM Consulting**
DORA applies to Swiss financial institutions with EU operations or counterparties. The critical and under-addressed dimension: ICT third-party risk management (TPRM). Institutions must map critical ICT subcontractors, assess concentration risk, and build exit plans. Advisory opportunity: TPRM framework design, critical supplier mapping, DORA gap assessment, ICT outage tabletop exercises. *Confidence: High.*

**3. Geneva SMB Restructuring Wave — Operational Excellence Advisory**
Geneva SMBs (50-250 employees) face a structural restructuring wave driven by post-COVID organizational debt, CHF strength margin compression, regulatory pile-up (CSRD, DORA, LETA, Basel IV), and talent shortages. These firms cannot afford Big 4 fees but need strategic and operational support. Advisory opportunity: operational diagnostic + implementation, organization redesign, regulatory burden rationalization. *Confidence: High.*

**4. Switzerland-EU Institutional Agreement — SME Market Access Advisory**
The Switzerland-EU institutional agreement enters implementation in 2026, creating new market access opportunities and compliance obligations for Swiss SMEs: financial services passport uncertainty, professional qualification mutual recognition, public procurement access, customs procedures. Geneva SMEs need practical guidance on applicability and positioning. Advisory opportunity: EU market entry readiness assessment, compliance gap analysis. *Confidence: High.*

**5. Interim Risk and Compliance — Specialized Talent as Structural Gap**
Geneva financial institutions and corporates face simultaneous demand for CARB, CRS, DORA, sanctions, and LETA expertise — creating a structural gap in specialized risk and compliance talent. This is not a temporary surge but an ongoing structural condition. Advisory opportunity: interim risk talent placement + embedded advisory, risk function sprint setup. *Confidence: High.*

**6. AI Governance Multi-Framework Fragmentation Advisory**
Geneva organizations face AI governance fragmentation: FADP (Swiss), EU AI Act (for EU-market-facing entities), DORA AI provisions, and ISO/IEC AI standards — each with different risk classifications and requirements. Organizations need a unified AI governance framework mapping to multiple regulatory requirements. Advisory opportunity: multi-standard AI governance framework, AI risk inventory and classification, AI audit readiness. *Confidence: High.*
