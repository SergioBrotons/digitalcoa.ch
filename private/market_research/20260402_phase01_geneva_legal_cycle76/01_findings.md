## NET-NEW FINDINGS

### 1. BGE 9C_606/2025 (Feb 24, 2026) — Debt Push-Down Interest Non-Deductibility [MAJOR]
**Case:** Direct Federal Tax and Cantonal and Municipal Taxes 2010–2013 (Geneva)
**Significance:** Landmark ruling — first Federal Supreme Court analysis of debt push-down deductibility in LBO context.

- Leveraged buyout followed by immediate downstream merger (BidCo into TargetCo)
- Acquisition tranche (~76% of loan, ~CHF 7.2m) financed purchase price paid to former shareholders
- Renovation tranche (~24%, ~CHF 2.3m) financed renovation works at TargetCo
- After merger, TargetCo claimed deduction for all interest expenses
- Tax authorities denied acquisition tranche only; accepted renovation tranche
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Acquisition tranche interest NOT deductible — lacks "commercial justification" under Art. 58(1)(b) FDTA
- **Key reasoning:** Acquisition debt does not serve TargetCo's income-generating activity (residential property rental); no objective causal link between acquisition financing and TargetCo's business purpose
- **Precedent-setting:** Court relied on commercial justification doctrine rather than traditional tax avoidance doctrine — establishes new analytical framework
- **Renovation tranche:** Deductible (directly connected to TargetCo's rental activity)
- **Principle of periodicity:** Deductibility assessed independently post-merger at TargetCo level — cannot be analyzed globally with pre-merger BidCo position

### 2. BGE 9C_371/2025 (Mar 5, 2026) — Ne Bis in Idem in Swiss Tax Evasion Fines [Geneva]
**Case:** Direct Federal Tax and State and Municipal Taxes 2013–2015 (Geneva)
- Tax evasion based on monetary benefits from controlled companies + undeclared bank deposits + equity interests
- Tax authorities fined BOTH the company (for tax evasion) AND the individual (for incitement and aiding and abetting)
- Appellant claimed violation of ne bis in idem principle (double jeopardy)
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** No ne bis in idem violation — company fine and individual fine do not concern "the same subject matter" under ECHR case law
- Fine amounts upheld — appellant unable to demonstrate arbitrariness
- **Relevance:** Strengthens Swiss tax enforcement toolkit for corporate tax evasion with individual involvement

### 3. BGE 9C_297/298/2025 (Feb 23, 2026) — Agricultural Property Reclassification [Geneva]
**Case:** Direct Federal Tax and Cantonal and Municipal Taxes 2016 (Geneva)
- Sale of parcel belonging to deceased A. and his brother
- Central question: whether parcel remained "agricultural property" under Art. 18(4) DBG at time of sale
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Agricultural use ceased in September 2015 — PRIOR to ownership transfer (December 22, 2015 and February 1, 2016)
- Parcel therefore NOT qualifying agricultural property → full capital gains tax treatment applies
- Appeal by heirs dismissed
- **Relevance:** Strict temporal test for agricultural property qualification; ownership transfer date vs. actual use cessation date decisive

### 4. BGE 9C_274/2024 (Feb 27, 2026) — Managed Care VAT Exemption Denied
**Case:** Value-Added Tax 2017–2019
- Issue: whether services under managed care contracts qualify as VAT-exempt medical treatments under Art. 21(2)(3) VATA
- Appellant argued services were integral part of medical treatment → exempt
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Services are independent flat-rate coordination services — no direct link to individual medical treatments → taxable
- Court noted that Art. 21(2)(no. 3bis) VATA subsequently introduced specifically to address this gap — demonstrates legislative intent that prior period services were NOT exempt
- **Relevance:** Important for managed care organizations, health insurers, and medical coordination service providers in Switzerland

### 5. BGE 9C_620/2025 (Feb 27, 2026) — Real Estate Gains Tax Reinvestment Calculation Error
**Case:** Real Estate Gains Tax 2020 (Bern)
- Issue: calculation of partial tax deferral due to reinvestment
- Lower court wrongly deviated from absolute method in its calculation, even while acknowledging the error
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Appeal by tax administration upheld — calculation error corrected
- **Relevance:** Technical but practical for real estate transaction tax planning in Bern

### 6. BGE 9C_314/2025 (Mar 3, 2026) — Heirs Liable for Undetected Water Leak Charges
**Case:** Water Fees 2017–2021 (Leuzigen/BE)
- High water consumption from undetected leak over several months
- Heirs of former tenant contested liability for water/sewerage charges
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Heirs liable for charges even where leak was undetected and unknown — no exemption for good-faith tenants/heirs
- **Relevance:** Consumer protection implication; property liability exposure for undetected infrastructure failures

### 7. BGE 9C_578/2025 (Feb 26, 2026) — Pension Buyback Three-Year Waiting Period Strictly Applied
**Case:** Federal Direct Tax and State and Municipal Taxes 2021 (Basel-Landschaft)
- Taxpayer made pension buyout after receiving hardship fund support payment
- In same year, received two lump-sum payments from pension fund
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Three-year waiting period is an objective time limit — ANY lump-sum payments within period preclude deductibility regardless of causal link to purchase
- Exception only: temporary bridging pension under independent pension law instrument
- Taxpayer could not rely on legal department information because essential facts were not fully disclosed
- **Relevance:** Pension optimization strategies in Switzerland face strict temporal constraints

### 8. BGE 9C_380/2025 (Feb 25, 2026) — Property Tax Valuation: Single Shareholder Company
**Case:** State and Municipal Taxes 2019 (Zurich)
- Sole shareholder since 2018; dispute over stock corporation valuation for property tax
- Tax authority initially applied practical method (double income / single asset weighting)
- After objection, corrected to single weighting of income value (due to company personal nature)
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Tax authority's corrected method not arbitrary — company not more person-specific than lawyer/architect practices; profit fluctuations adequately considered
- **Relevance:** Property tax valuation of closely-held companies in Switzerland

### 9. BGE 9C_9/2026 (Mar 2, 2026) — VAT Substantive Gap Filling from Commercial Balance Sheet
**Case:** Value-Added Tax 2017–2020
- FTA audit revealed CHF 102,858 additional assessment
- Discrepancies between VAT returns and commercial balance sheet (both formal and substantive)
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** FTA correctly filled substantive gaps by reference to commercial balance sheet; taxpayer must accept commercial balance sheet figures even if bookkeeping errors occurred
- **Relevance:** Swiss VAT compliance — bookkeeping accuracy critical; FTA has strong gap-filling powers

### 10. BGE 9C_387/2025 (Mar 5, 2026) — Restaurant Equipment Loans as Independent VAT-Liable Services
**Case:** Value-Added Tax 2016–2020
- Company loaned outdoor serving stations, refrigerators, outdoor lighting, menu display cases to restaurants
- In exchange for corresponding delivery rights (exclusive supply arrangements)
- **Federal Supreme Court ruling:** Economic link established — loans are independent, VAT-liable counter-performance for delivery rights
- Not merely ancillary to purchase/delivery obligations
- **Relevance:** Commercial arrangements with mutual benefits require careful VAT analysis
