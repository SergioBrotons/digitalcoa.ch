# Phase 02 — Geneva Finance: Regulatory Framework
## Cycle 69 | 2026-04-02 | Geneva Finance AI Governance

## Applicable Regulations

### 1. FINMA Regulatory Framework
- FINMA: principles-based regulation, no specific AI ordinance
- FINMA 08/21: automated client suitability checks (suitability)
- FINMA 23/1: operational risk — increasingly relevant for AI/ML
- FINMA supervision approach: technology-neutral with risk-based focus
- FINMA AI survey (2025): 50% Swiss FIs using AI, 91% generative AI chatbots
- BigTech dependency risk explicitly flagged by FINMA
- AI guidance: no specific FINMA AI circular as of research date

### 2. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP)
- FDPIC: FADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data
- DPIA (Art. 22): mandatory for high-risk AI systems in finance
- Client data AI applications = high risk by default
- WM portfolio AI = potentially FADP Art. 21 (profiling) + Art. 22 (DPIA)

### 3. EU AI Act — Financial Services
- Annex III likely captures: credit scoring AI, insurance underwriting AI, investment AI
- Extraterritorial: Swiss FIs serving EU clients face Annex III obligations
- August 2, 2025: GPAI obligations already in force
- August 2, 2026: HR AI conformity + legacy GPAI grandfathering expiry
- FINMA to issue sector-specific rules (Federal Council sector-specific approach)

### 4. U.S. Section 301 — Finance Angle
- Forced labour: supply chain ESG documentation requirements
- Swiss FIs with ESG mandates: Section 301 adds documentation burden
- Financial implications: tariff uncertainty affects WM client portfolios

### 5. Swiss Bankers Association (SBA)
- SBA digital finance: "digital innovations are a prerequisite for Switzerland's competitiveness"
- SBA AI position: endorses Federal Council sector-specific approach vs EU AI Act comprehensive framework
- Swiss Banking Outlook: SNB 0.00% all 2026, AuM rising, commission business up
- Richard Hess (SBA Digital Finance): Top 100 Digital Shapers 2026

### 6. SNB Monetary Policy
