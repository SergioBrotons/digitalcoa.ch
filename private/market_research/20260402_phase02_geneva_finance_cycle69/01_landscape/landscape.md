# Phase 02 — Geneva Finance: Market Landscape
## Cycle 69 | 2026-04-02 | Geneva Finance AI Governance

## Geneva Financial Center Overview
- Private banking: CHF ~2.4T AuM (Swiss private banking)
- Geneva as %: approximately 25-30% Swiss financial center
- Asset management, wealth management, family offices
- Multi-family offices increasingly important
- Regulatory hub: FINMA, SNB, Swiss Bankers Association (SBA)
- International mix: UHNWIs, family offices, institutional

## AI Governance Relevance in Finance
- AML/KYC AI: automated transaction monitoring, fraud detection
- Portfolio management AI: algorithmic trading, robo-advisory
- Client communication AI: chatbots, document processing
- Credit scoring AI: alternative data credit assessment
- AI governance requirements: FADP DPIA + FINMA circulars + EU AI Act Annex III likely
- Swiss banking AI: "already indispensable" (SBA primary source)

## Regulatory Framework
- FINMA: principles-based regulation, no specific AI ordinance yet
- SNB: monetary policy, systemic risk monitoring
- SBA: digital finance, AI position endorsed
- EU AI Act Annex III: high-risk AI (credit, insurance, investment) likely captures Swiss FIs serving EU clients
- August 2, 2026: EU AI Act double deadline (HR AI conformity + GPAI grandfathering expiry)
- FADP: Art. 22 DPIA required for AI systems processing personal data

## Key Demand Signals
- SBA "AI already indispensable" = regulatory acknowledgment
- Section 301 forced labour angle = supply chain ESG for FIs
- Swiss institutional trust "excellent" = data sovereignty competitive differentiator
- FINMA survey: 50% Swiss FIs using AI, 91% generative AI chatbots
- BigTech dependency risk for Swiss WM AI (FINMA survey)

## Competitive Landscape
- Big 4: global AI governance frameworks for financial services
- Swiss banks: developing in-house AI governance
- Boutique WM: gap in AI governance — too small for Big 4, too busy for in-house
- Geneva wealth managers: exposed to FADP + FINMA + EU AI Act simultaneously
