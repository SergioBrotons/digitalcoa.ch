## NET-NEW Findings (Distinct from C214–C293)

### Finding 1: nFADP Art.8 Profiling Notification Gap as Present-Tense Unnamed Obligation

**Finding:** AI-assisted portfolio construction constitutes profiling under nFADP Art.8. Data subjects (clients) have right to notification and human review. Geneva boutique wealth management firms are not issuing profiling notifications as a category. Obligation is present-tense regardless of firm awareness. Inferred characteristics from transaction data processing without profiling notification = active nFADP exposure.

**Distinct from prior findings:** Prior Phase 02 findings addressed Art.6 lawful basis, Art.20/21 explanation rights, Art.63 criminal liability. This finding isolates Art.8 profiling notification as a discrete unnamed obligation category with its own activation pathway distinct from other nFADP articles. Not addressed in any prior Phase 02 cycle.

**Ready-to-use language:** "AI-assisted portfolio construction is profiling under Swiss data protection law. Your clients have a right to know when AI analyzes their financial behavior to make recommendations. If you're not issuing profiling notifications, you're operating outside the law — regardless of what your AI governance documentation says."

**Evidence base:** nFADP Art.8 explicitly grants data subjects right to notification when profiling produces legal effects or significantly affects them. AI-generated risk tolerance inferences, spending pattern analysis, life stage projections used in portfolio recommendations clearly meet this threshold.

### Finding 2: CoE AI Convention Deployer Obligations Creating Present-Tense Undisclosed Private Law Exposure

**Finding:** CoE AI Convention creates deployer obligations for Geneva boutique WM operating now through existing mandate contracts and carrier underwriting terms — before any implementing legislation. AI governance clauses may already exist in client mandates as accepting obligations. Carrier insurance terms may incorporate Convention compliance as coverage condition. Boutique unaware they may already be contractually committed to AI governance standards through contracts predating AI adoption.

**Distinct from prior findings:** Prior findings named CoE AI Convention future obligations and implementation timeline. This finding isolates present-tense private law obligations flowing through existing contracts — not future implementing legislation. Different mechanism, different urgency, different exposure category.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The Council of Europe AI Convention creates obligations now — not when Switzerland implements it. Your existing client mandates may already contain AI governance commitments you didn't know you were accepting. Carrier insurance terms may treat Convention compliance as a coverage condition. This isn't a future regulatory risk. It's a present-tense contract exposure."

**Evidence base:** Convention text creates obligations for "deployers" of AI systems — defined to include organizations that use AI in professional contexts. No implementing legislation required for private law obligations to flow through contracts.

### Finding 3: Carrier Attestation Personal Liability Architecture Incompatibility With Criminal Defense Architecture

**Finding:** Carrier attestation documentation and nFADP Art.63 criminal defense documentation require different architectural approaches. Carrier attestation evidence standard (specific human authorization moments for specific decisions) designed for underwriting risk evaluation — not for criminal evidentiary standard. Documentation built to satisfy carrier attestation may be architecturally inadequate for criminal defense if Art.63 liability triggers. Existing AI governance programs and pre-signing review built for administrative exposure inadequate for criminal defense.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292 Phase 02 named documentation architecture difference between criminal defense and administrative compliance without isolating specific architectural incompatibility mechanism. This finding specifies what the architecture difference actually is — carrier evidence architecture designed for underwriting risk evaluation vs. criminal defense architecture requiring different evidence categories, standards, and preservation approaches.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your carrier attestation commits you to an evidence standard designed by underwriters — not to a criminal defense standard. If an AI-related incident triggers personal liability under Swiss data protection law, the documentation you built for your carrier may not protect you. These are two different evidentiary architectures. You're probably building one and assuming it serves both."

**Evidence base:** nFADP Art.63 criminal liability has different evidentiary threshold, different enforcement mechanism, different defense requirements than administrative fine. Carrier attestation designed for underwriting risk evaluation by definition serves carrier interests, not signatory criminal defense.

### Finding 4: Institutional Client Cross-Firm Simultaneous Comparison Effect as Market Standard Formation Mechanism

**Finding:** Institutional clients running simultaneous multi-boutique AI governance comparisons across manager roster. Each boutique responds independently without knowing comparison is happening. Aggregate of independent responses creates de facto market standard treating documentation quality as governance quality. Sophisticated institutional clients using questionnaire responses as competitive intelligence about manager quality. Boutique not knowing comparison is happening cannot strategically calibrate response — responds as if compliance exercise, loses competitive positioning.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292 Phase 02 first named competitive intelligence documentation paradox. C293 Phase 02 confirmed cross-firm comparison effect as structural market distortion. This finding isolates specific mechanism: simultaneous multi-firm comparison creating artificial market consensus rewarding documentation sophistication over actual architecture. Prior findings named effect without isolating simultaneous comparison as specific market-level distortion mechanism.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your institutional client didn't send you an AI governance questionnaire for compliance purposes. They sent it as part of a simultaneous comparison across their manager roster. They're ranking firms by documentation quality — not by actual governance architecture. How you respond affects your standing in that comparison. You don't know what the other firms said. Neither do they. But your client is building a ranking."

**Evidence base:** Institutional clients managing multiple external managers have operational incentive to compare governance quality across roster. AI governance questionnaire provides standardized comparison metric. Documentation sophistication proxies for governance quality in absence of deeper evaluation methodology.

### Finding 5: Agentic AI Authorization Documentation Gap as Structurally Unresolvable At Boutique Scale

**Finding:** Boutique wealth management using AI for continuous portfolio management (agentic AI — executing trades, rebalancing, generating communications without per-transaction human authorization) has no documentation architecture to satisfy carrier third-generation evidence requirements. Authorization architecture designed for discrete decisions (human reviewed X, then AI executed Y) incompatible with continuous AI operation. No Geneva market methodology exists for proportionate continuous authorization documentation. Boutiques with agentic AI deployment face documentation gap that cannot be resolved by improving documentation quality — gap is architectural, not operational.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C292 Phase 02 named agentic AI creating continuous authorization exposure different from generative AI documentation. This finding specifies why the gap is structurally unresolvable at boutique scale: authorization architecture designed for discrete events cannot be adapted for continuous processes by definition. Prior findings addressed the exposure; this finding addresses why proportionate solution doesn't exist and cannot be created by improving existing documentation approach.

**Ready-to-use language:** "If you're using AI that operates continuously — making portfolio decisions, executing trades, rebalancing without you reviewing each action — your authorization documentation probably doesn't exist in any form. The carrier is now asking for evidence of specific human authorization moments. Continuous AI operation doesn't produce those moments. This isn't a documentation gap you can close by building better documents. It's an architectural incompatibility. And no Geneva service is offering a methodology for resolving it."

**Evidence base:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 written for discrete AI use cases. Carrier third-generation evidence requirements probe discrete human authorization moments. Agentic AI by design operates without per-event authorization. No middle-ground documentation architecture exists in market.

### Finding 6: Documentation Window Burden-of-Proof Reversal Producing Irreversible Positioning That Cannot Be Retrofitted

**Finding:** Documentation window close creates irreversible burden-of-proof inversion. Firms building documentation during window (now) build evidence of what human authorization preceded AI-assisted decisions. Firms building after window closes attempt retroactive documentation of decisions made during window — but retroactive documentation cannot reconstruct window-period reasoning, cannot produce evidence of what actually happened, produces documents that reveal themselves as retroactive when challenged. Window is not a deadline metaphor — it is a present-tense irreversible positioning event. Boutiques deferring AI governance documentation investment are accumulating exposure that cannot be discharged through subsequent documentation effort.

**Distinct from prior findings:** C280–C293 Phase 02 consistently named documentation window as key concept and burden-of-proof inversion. This finding isolates specific mechanism of irreversibility: retroactive documentation produces documents that demonstrate themselves as retroactive when examined, revealing reconstruction rather than original decision trail. Prior findings named the inversion; this finding specifies why subsequent effort cannot cure the positioning effect.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your documentation window isn't a deadline you can extend. It's a positioning event that's happening now. If you're waiting to build documentation until a carrier asks specific questions, you're building retroactively — and retroactive documentation reveals itself as retroactive when examined. The carrier will know you built it after the fact. The evidence standard you're building against isn't the one that existed when the decisions were made. It's the one that exists now. You can't retroactively document what human authorization preceded decisions that already happened."

**Evidence base:** Burden-of-proof reversal is established in regulatory practice. Carrier third-generation evidence requirements explicitly probe whether documentation reflects original decision process or subsequent reconstruction. Retroactive documentation quality distinguishable from contemporaneous documentation under cross-examination.
