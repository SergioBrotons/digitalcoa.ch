# ICP — Small Geneva Law Firm AI Governance
## Phase 01 — Geneva Legal — Cycle 42

---

## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Target:** Small/mid-market law firms in Geneva, 1–20 lawyers
- **Alternative ICP:** Solo practitioners in Geneva with 0–2 lawyers
- **NOT:** Enterprise law firms (Walder Wyss, Lenz & Staehelin, Homburger, VISCHER — enterprise-only confirmed)
- **Geography:** Canton Geneva primarily; broader Swiss French-speaking region secondary

### Psychographics
- **Pain:** Using AI tools without formal governance (Shadow AI); belief AI is useful but unclear on compliance obligations; no time or budget for complex compliance frameworks
- **Trigger:** Approaching deadline (Swissdamed Jul 1 ~10wks; digitalswitzerland May 2026 ~4wks; EU AI Act Aug 2 ~19wks) or peer adoption visible
- **Urgency lever:** 70% projected increase in AI adoption 2026; 90%+ already using AI tools; enforcement gap (no law firm FDPIC enforcement confirmed)
- **Gatekeeper:** Managing partner / sole practitioner with budget authority

### Legal ICP Pain Points (Cycle 42 additions)
1. **Data residency requirement** — must use Swiss or EU servers (professional confidentiality + FADP Art. 21)
2. **FADP directly applicable to AI** — no new law needed; obligations already operative (FDPIC May 2025)
3. **Governance gap widening** — 70% increase projected but formal policies remain absent (43% no formal policy + no plans)
4. **Swiss-specific tool requirement** — generic AI tools may not meet Swiss legal system requirements (Co/Cc/DSG/FADP compatibility)
5. **Small firm agility paradox** — faster adoption than enterprise but minimal governance infrastructure

### Decision-maker Motivations
- Protect firm from FDPIC enforcement (managing director liability, CHF 250,000 criminal)
- Avoid professional liability exposure (SBA professional rules)
- Capture efficiency gains already demonstrated (65% save 1-5h/week; 260h/year)
- Prepare for incoming regulatory pressure (May 2026 external milestone, end-2026 formal deadline)
