# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management — AI Accountability

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

---

### What Exists

**1. FINMA Guidance 08/2024**
- Published December 18, 2024
- Principles-based, risk-based, technology-neutral
- Expects AI inventory, governance framework, data quality, model risk management, human oversight
- Applies to all supervised institutions, including boutique wealth managers
- Does NOT provide sector-specific guidance for boutiques — the principles are clear but "good enough" for a 50–200 person firm is undefined

**2. EU AI Act Alignment Pressure**
- Financial institutions with EU exposure are being pushed toward EU AI Act alignment
- EU AI Act high-risk AI provisions (credit scoring, insurance risk) create obligations even for non-EU firms with EU clients
- FINMA itself references EU AI Act as a benchmark for preparedness
- Market exists: EU AI Act compliance consulting (Big 4, specialized boutiques)

**3. Generic AI Governance Frameworks**
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework — comprehensive but designed for large organizations
- ISO/IEC 42001 (AI Management System standard) — certification available, but boutique firms don't have the internal capacity to implement
- COSO AI governance guidance — useful but not sector-specific
- These frameworks exist but are not accessible to boutique wealth managers without substantial external support

**4. Data Protection / nFADP Consulting**
- Data protection officers (DPOs) and nFADP compliance consultants exist
- nFADP gap assessments available
- BUT: nFADP consultants typically do not address AI-specific model risk or AI governance inventory questions

**5. Technology Vendor Offerings**
- AI-powered portfolio analytics tools (increasingly common)
- AI-enhanced CRM systems
- Document processing and data extraction AI
- All marketed to wealth managers with minimal governance documentation
- Vendors not providing compliance frameworks — that's outside their scope

**6. SRO Resources (Limited)**
- SROs in Switzerland have issued general technology guidance
- FINMA-authorized SROs have begun some AI awareness content
- But no sector-specific AI governance guidance for wealth management has been published by any Swiss SRO as of early 2025

---

### What's Missing for Geneva Boutiques

**Gap 1: Sector-Specific Interpretive Guidance**
No document exists that takes FINMA's principles and translates them into: "here is what a Geneva boutique wealth manager with 50–200 employees and CHF 2–10B AUM actually needs to have in place." The big banks have teams to make this translation themselves. Boutiques don't.

**Gap 2: Boutique-Scaled AI Inventory Tooling**
AI inventory tools exist in the enterprise space (governance, risk, and compliance platforms). These are designed for large institutions with dedicated GRC teams and six-figure budgets. No equivalent tool is marketed or adapted for boutique wealth management — something that a managing director without a compliance team can actually use.

**Gap 3: Institutional Questionnaire Response Templates**
No consultant or template library exists specifically for helping boutique wealth managers respond to institutional AI governance questionnaires. The questionnaires are being sent; no one has published a response framework for this specific ICP.

**Gap 4: Personal Liability Clarity for nFADP Art. 63**
The nFADP revised law with Article 63 personal liability provisions has not been clearly interpreted for managing directors of wealth management firms in the context of AI systems. Legal memos exist but aren't framed for this specific audience.

**Gap 5: Carrier Attestation Preparation Support**
Insurance brokers are facilitating carrier attestations but not helping boutiques build the underlying framework. The attestation process is treated as a paperwork exercise, not a governance development opportunity.

**Gap 6: SRO-Style Guidance for Boutiques Who Can't Wait**
The gap between "waiting for SRO guidance" and "building your own framework" has no middle option — no lightweight, credible, Geneva-specific guidance product that a managing director can use to make defensible decisions now.

---

### Competitive Landscape Observation

**Advisory firms and consultants serving this space:**
- Big 4 audit firms: conducting FINMA-aligned AI governance reviews for larger banks; boutiques are not their target client
- Specialized Swiss fintech consulting firms: emerging but not yet positioned as "boutique wealth management AI governance"
- Legal firms: providing nFADP gap analysis; not AI governance in the FINMA sense
- Technology vendors: selling tools, not frameworks

**No clear market leader** positioned specifically as "the partner for Geneva boutique wealth management AI accountability."
