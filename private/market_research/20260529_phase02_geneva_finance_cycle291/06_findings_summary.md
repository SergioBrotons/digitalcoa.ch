## 6 NET-NEW Findings (Distinct from All Prior Cycles C214–C290)

### Finding 01: Institutional AI Governance Questionnaire as Primary Wake-Up Call (NEW Mechanism)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles identified institutional AI governance questionnaires as a trigger event. C291 isolates the specific mechanism: the questionnaire arrives as a due diligence process, not a compliance exercise, activating the firm's exposure in a context where guessing is legally distinct from non-compliance.

**The finding:** The institutional client's investment committee is using the AI governance questionnaire as part of their own governance obligations. The firm's response becomes part of a legal record. If the firm cannot answer basic threshold questions, the failure is not just about AI governance — it's about general governance quality. The institutional client relationship itself becomes exposed through the questionnaire process.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named institutional questionnaires as a trigger. This finding isolates the specific legal character of the due diligence process — guessing vs. non-compliance — and the relationship exposure that makes the questionnaire more than an administrative event.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Your client's 40-page questionnaire isn't a compliance form. It's part of their governance record. They're building a file on you. The question isn't whether to answer — it's what your answer says about your firm."*

---

### Finding 02: Carrier Attestation as MD Personal Liability Instrument (Reinforced and Sharpened)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles identified carrier attestation as an accountability exposure. C291 sharpens the specific mechanism: the personal signature requirement activates personal liability consequences that entity-level coverage does not address.

**The finding:** Carrier AI attestations require the managing director's personal signature. The attestation commits the signatory to "adequate" AI governance against a standard designed by underwriting, not by the regulated community. The personal signature creates personal exposure that professional liability insurance does not automatically cover. The MD who signs without understanding what they committed to is exposed personally in a way the firm's entity coverage was not designed to address.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the undefined adequacy standard. This finding names the specific personal liability mechanism activated by the personal signature requirement and the coverage gap between entity-level PI and personal MD exposure.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The form uses your name. The signature is yours. What you committed to when you signed is something the form doesn't define and your carrier hasn't explained. Your PI policy covers the firm's negligence. It doesn't cover your personal attestation of adequacy."*

---

### Finding 03: AI Inventory as Foundational Governance Act (Not Documentation Exercise)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles named the AI inventory as a documentation requirement. C291 identifies the specific structural role: the inventory is not the output of governance — it is the foundational governance act that makes all other governance tractable.

**The finding:** Without an AI inventory, the firm cannot answer the institutional client's questionnaire, discharge the carrier attestation, respond to FINMA supervisory expectations, or manage the nFADP Art.9 processor chain. The inventory is the governance act, not a record of it. Building it forces systematic awareness of every AI application in use, every vendor with data access, every model update that changes the firm's risk position. The act of building it is the governance.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the inventory as a documentation gap. This finding identifies the inventory as the primary governance act — the thing that, once done, makes everything else structurally possible.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The AI inventory isn't a form you fill in. It's the act of building it that does the work. You can't govern what you haven't named."*

### Finding 04: SRO Guidance Vacuum as Structural, Not Temporary (NEW Framing)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles identified SRO silence as a guidance gap. C291 reframes it structurally: the vacuum is not a temporary gap awaiting resolution — it is the consistent behavior of SROs managing their own institutional exposure by publishing principle without procedure.

**The finding:** SROs face less exposure from issuing vague principle-level guidance than from issuing specific operational guidance that later proves inadequate. The vacuum is not evidence that guidance is coming. It is evidence that the SRO has determined not to provide it. The firm that waits for SRO guidance is waiting for a structural condition to resolve, which it will not.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named SRO silence as a gap. This finding reframes it as the consistent, structural behavior of institutions managing their own exposure — not a gap awaiting a solution, but a permanent condition requiring a different response strategy.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The SRO isn't working on guidance in secret. The silence is the guidance. They're telling you: figure it out yourself, at your own scale, and document what you decided and why."*

### Finding 05: nFADP Art.63 Personal Liability as the Unnamed MD Exposure

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles named nFADP Art.63 as a structural exposure. C291 isolates the specific unnamed nature: the MD personal liability exposure under Art.63 is not named in any carrier questionnaire, any SRO guidance, or any institutional client questionnaire — it exists independently of all of them.

**The finding:** The personal criminal liability exposure under nFADP Art.63 flows from unlawful processing of personal data. The MD who deployed AI tools processing client financial data without a documented lawfulness assessment is personally exposed. This exposure is not addressed by the firm's PI coverage, not addressed by the carrier attestation, not addressed by the institutional client questionnaire. It exists independently. It is unnamed in the firm's documentation. It is the MD's personal exposure.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named Art.63 as a structural exposure. This finding isolates the specific unnamed character — it exists independently of all the other named exposures and is not addressed by any of the documentation the firm is currently building.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"There's an Art.63 exposure that no one has named in any of the documents you're currently building. It's your personal criminal exposure for deploying AI tools that processed client data without a documented lawfulness assessment. Your carrier attestation doesn't cover it. Your institutional client questionnaire doesn't address it. It's yours alone."*

### Finding 06: Documentation Window Burden-of-Proof Reversal Accelerating (Reinforced)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles named the documentation window as a timing consideration. C291 confirms it is accelerating: the Federal Council's AI consultation draft is expected before end of 2026, and the window is closing faster than the market is building documentation.

**The finding:** The asymmetry between firms building documentation and firms waiting is widening. The firms building now are being evaluated against forming standards and documented as having made deliberate governance decisions during the window. The firms waiting are accumulating evidence of delay with no external benchmark to justify it. After the draft arrives, the gap between the two groups becomes a permanent record.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the window as a timing factor. This finding confirms the acceleration and the widening asymmetry — the window is not just closing, the gap between acting and waiting is becoming structurally apparent.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The firms that built documentation during the window have evidence of deliberateness. The firms that waited have evidence of delay. When the draft arrives, both groups are visible. Only one group has evidence of having figured it out before someone told them what the answer was supposed to be."*
