# ICP Language: Geneva Legal — C307 Phase 01

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems — C307

---

### The Geneva Legal ICP (C307)

Boutique firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based. Managing partner personally accountable. Signed carrier attestation without understanding what it committed them to. No documentation of AI use in any matter. Junior associates more fluent with AI than managing partner. Bar guidance vacuum. Attorney-client privilege exposure now active. Personal liability on the line.

---

## ICP Language Patterns — C307

### The Accountability Inversion Problem
- "I'm the one who's responsible. But I can't actually evaluate what the associates are doing with AI."
- "They know more about the tools than I do. That's the inversion — the person who's accountable knows the least."
- "I sign off on everything. But I don't know what I'm signing off on."
- "The buck stops with me. But the knowledge doesn't."
- "How do I supervise something I don't understand?"

### The Privilege Exposure Problem
- "The client tells me something in confidence. Then an AI tool processes it. Is that still privileged?"
- "I didn't think about the vendor having access to what I sent. They just had access."
- "The contract said the vendor could use the data to improve the model. I didn't read that part."
- "If a vendor gets access to a privileged communication — is that a waiver? Who knows?"
- "I think I may have waived privilege without knowing it."

### The Multi-Standard Confusion Problem
- "In French, clients assume I'm following the Geneva standard. What is the Geneva standard?"
- "In English, clients assume I have a policy. Do I need one? Which one?"
- "The Ordre hasn't said anything. So is there a standard or not?"
- "Different clients expect different things. Different languages, different assumptions."
- "I'm operating in two regulatory contexts and I don't know what either one actually requires."

### The Carrier-Ordre Documentation Confusion
- "The carrier wants evidence of AI governance. The Ordre might want something different."
- "If I show the carrier what I have — does that create a record the Ordre can use against me?"
- "Can a document I created for the carrier be used in a disciplinary proceeding?"
- "I don't know whether what I'm building for one authority protects me or exposes me with another."
- "The documentation I need for the carrier and the documentation I need for the Ordre might not be the same thing."

### The Knowledge Dependency Problem
- "I depend on my associates to use the AI tools. I depend on them to tell me if something went wrong."
- "But they're not the ones with the personal liability. I am."
- "How do I know what they're actually doing? How do I document supervising something I can't evaluate?"
- "The delegation happens. The accountability doesn't transfer. That's the problem."
- "I can't stop the delegation. I can't evaluate the delegation. But I'm the one who's responsible."

### The Active Exposure Recognition Problem
- "The problem isn't theoretical anymore. I know I've been using AI."
- "I know I signed something. I know I didn't read it."
- "The question now isn't 'should I be worried?' The question is 'what do I do now?'"
- "I need to know what I actually did. Not what I should have done. What I actually did."
- "And I need to know what to show if someone asks."

## French ICP Language — C307

### Le problème de l'inversion de responsabilité
- "Je suis celui qui est responsable. Mais je ne peux pas évaluer ce que les associés font avec l'IA."
- "Ils en savent plus que moi sur les outils. C'est l'inversion."
- "La responsabilité s'arrête chez moi. Mais pas la connaissance."

### Le problème de l'exposition du secret professionnel
- "Le client me dit quelque chose en confidence. Ensuite, un outil d'IA le traite. Est-ce encore privileged ?"
- "Je n'ai pas pensé que le fournisseur avait accès à ce que j'ai envoyé."
- "Le contrat disait que le fournisseur pouvait utiliser les données pour améliorer le modèle. Je n'ai pas lu cette partie."

### Le problème de la confusion multi-normes
- "En français, les clients supposent que je suis la norme genevoise. Quelle est la norme genevoise ?"
- "En anglais, les clients supposent que j'ai une politique. Ai-je besoin d'une politique ? Laquelle ?"
- "L'Ordre n'a rien dit. Alors y a-t-il une norme ou pas ?"

### Le problème de la confusion carrier-Ordre
- "L'assureur veut des preuves de gouvernance IA. L'Ordre pourrait vouloir quelque chose de différent."
- "Si je montre à l'assureur ce que j'ai — est-ce que cela crée un dossier que l'Ordre peut utiliser contre moi ?"

### Le problème de la dépendance au savoir
- "Je dépends de mes associés pour utiliser les outils d'IA. Je dépends d'eux pour me dire si quelque chose s'est mal passé."
- "Mais ils ne sont pas ceux qui ont la responsabilité personnelle. Moi, je le suis."

## The Emotional State — C307

The C307 ICP has moved past the initial discovery phase. They now:
- Know they signed something with personal implications
- Know their associates are using AI tools they can't evaluate
- Know vendor contracts were accepted without review
- Know attorney-client privilege may have been compromised
- Know the Ordre hasn't provided guidance — and that the vacuum is not the same as permission
- Know they need evidence of what they actually did, not what they should have done

The service need has shifted: from "should I be worried?" to "what do I do now, in what order, and who do I call first?"

The language they use is: accountability inversion, privilege exposure, multi-standard confusion, carrier-Ordre documentation sequencing, knowledge dependency, and active exposure recognition.

The call comes when they realize they need to act — not when they learn about the problem.
