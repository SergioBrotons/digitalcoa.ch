## NET-NEW Findings (C75 Phase 04)

### Finding 1: Brotons.net — C75 Content Angles Derived from Phase 01-03 Findings (NET-NEW)

**7 NET-NEW Brotons Content Angles from C75 Geneva Research:**

**Angle 1: "The Swiss-EU Package: Geneva's Role in the Most Significant Swiss Legislation in Years"**
- Source: C75 Phase 01 Finding 1 (Swiss-EU Stabilisation Package)
- Angle: 94 EU acts, 3 federal laws, institutional reforms. Brotons lens: what this means for decision-making frameworks in Swiss institutions. How Geneva-based organizations must adapt their institutional logic.
- Rationale: Swiss-EU package is a major institutional shift. Brotons thought leadership opportunity — interpretive analysis of what institutional adaptation looks like in practice.

**Angle 2: "After HRC61: What Geneva's Human Rights Stage Tells Us About International Governance"**
- Source: C75 Phase 01 Finding 2 (HRC61 ended Mar 31, 2026)
- Angle: HRC is the world's most important human rights body, based in Geneva. Brotons lens: what does the end of HRC61 signal about multilateral governance capacity? International institutions as decision-making systems under strain.
- Rationale: Brotons' decision-philosophy voice is well-suited to analyzing multilateral governance.

**Angle 3: "Switzerland's Winter Electricity Crisis: A Failure of Institutional Coordination, Not Technology"**
- Source: C75 Phase 03 Finding 4 (Winter Electricity Crisis)
- Angle: Despite 40% more installed capacity, zero winter electricity progress in 10 years. Brotons lens: this is a decision-making failure, not a technology gap. Institutional coordination under political constraints. Why good data doesn't produce good decisions.
- Rationale: Perfect Brotons angle — systems thinking applied to infrastructure failure.

**Angle 4: "The Stablecoin Battle: Trust, Regulation, and the Disintermediation of Money"**
- Source: C75 Phase 02 Finding 1 + 5 (Stablecoin Regulation + Stress Tests)
- Angle: SBA vs Federal Council on stablecoin licensing. Brotons lens: what does this tell us about how institutions construct trust in financial systems? The stress test demand is a decision-making framework. Disintermediation risk.
- Rationale: Finance + decision philosophy = strong Brotons fit.

**Angle 5: "The CHF 30 Billion Bureaucracy Cost: Why Good Institutions Produce Bad Outcomes"**
- Source: C75 Phase 03 Finding 1 (Bureaucracy Cost)
- Angle: CHF 30B/year avoidable through digital processes. Geneva CHF 2.8B alone. Brotons lens: this is an institutional decision-making problem — why do well-intentioned processes produce bad outcomes? The accumulation of regulation as constraint system.
- Rationale: Constraint philosophy is core Brotons territory.

**Angle 6: "International Geneva's Institutional Bet: CHF 70M/year on Multilateral Governance"**
- Source: C75 Phase 01 Finding 4 (UN Partnership Renewal)
- Angle: Federal Council committed CHF 70M/year to UNDP, UNFPA, UNICEF, UN Women. Boosting International Geneva. Brotons lens: what does institutional investment in multilateral governance say about Switzerland's decision-making elite? Is this a rational bet?
- Rationale: Geneva-specific + institutional analysis = Brotons sweet spot.

**Angle 7: "Switzerland's Nature Transition: The Financing Gap Is a Governance Problem"**
- Source: C75 Phase 02 Finding 3 (Nature Transition CHF 5.3B/year)
- Angle: BCG/SBA study — CHF 5.3B/year needed, private gap of CHF 2.1B/year. Brotons lens: why does a rich country like Switzerland have a nature financing gap? The governance of public-private coordination. Is this a decision-making failure?
- Rationale: Systems + governance + finance intersection.
