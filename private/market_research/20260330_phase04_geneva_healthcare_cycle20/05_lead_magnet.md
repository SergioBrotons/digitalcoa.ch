## 8-Section Framework (Swissdamed Edition)

### Section 1: Swissdamed — What It Means for Your Practice

Swissmedic's ordinance on medical device registration and reporting. For AI-enabled medical devices:
- Registration requirements for SaMD (Software as a Medical Device)
- Serious incident reporting obligations
- Post-market surveillance requirements

**If you use AI diagnostic imaging or AI symptom triage:** These are likely in scope.

### Section 2: The ~13-Week Timeline

From March 30 to July 1, 2026: approximately 13 weeks.

### Section 3: AI Tools in Scope

**High-risk SaMD categories:**
- AI diagnostic imaging (AI-assisted X-ray, MRI, CT interpretation)
- AI symptom triage (AI-driven initial patient assessment)
- AI clinical decision support (AI-assisted diagnosis recommendations)

**Lower-risk AI (not Swissdamed-primary):**
- Practice management AI (scheduling, billing)
- AI documentation (transcription, note generation)
- AI patient communication

### Section 4: What Swissmedic Requires

1. Device registration (if not already registered)
2. Serious incident reporting procedure
3. Post-market surveillance documentation
4. AI system documentation for in-scope tools

### Section 5: nFADP Parallel Obligations

AI tools processing patient data also trigger nFADP Art. 5(c) transparency obligations. Patient must be informed when AI is used in their care.

### Section 6: FDPIC Healthcare Priority

FDPIC's 2024/2025 annual report named healthcare as a priority focus area. AI governance for patient data is increasingly under FDPIC radar.

### Section 7: 30/60/90 Day Action Plan

- **30 days:** Inventory AI tools in use. Identify which are SaMD. Determine registration status.
- **60 days:** Implement serious incident reporting procedure. Update patient consent for AI use.
- **90 days (by May 1):** Complete Swissdamed compliance documentation. Ensure all in-scope AI tools are registered.

### Section 8: Why Act Now — 13 Weeks

1. No grace period confirmed by Swissmedic
2. First Geneva medical practice to face Swissdamed enforcement will set market precedent
3. FDPIC healthcare priority + Swissdamed = dual enforcement risk
4. AI for Good (Jul 7-10) is a natural post-deadline conversation anchor
