## 6 NET-NEW Findings (Distinct from All Prior Phase 06 Cycles C214–C290)

### Finding 01: Retrospective Awareness Effect as Primary Market Driver Shift

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior Phase 06 cycles identified the market as anticipatory — professionals planning for future attestation requirements. C291 Phase 06 isolates a structural shift: the primary market driver has moved from anticipatory anxiety to retrospective alarm. ICPs are not primarily worried about the next attestation. They are alarmed by what the last attestation committed them to, now visible in light of FINMA Guidance 08/2024, carrier audit activity, and completed AI inventories revealing 5–15 tools deployed without authorization.

**The finding:** The documentation window was positioned as a forward-looking competitive asset. It is now a present-tense liability variable. The firms that built during the window have documented evidence of deliberateness. The firms that didn't are experiencing retrospective exposure: they can see what they committed to, they can see they didn't build anything, and they now know the attestation was evaluated against the same forming standards they were evaluated against. The gap is now visible, not future.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the documentation window as a timing factor. This finding confirms the window has passed into the retrospective zone — the competitive differentiation between acting and waiting firms is now a present-tense market condition, not a future risk.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The window isn't closing. It closed. The question now is: what did you build during it, and what does your attestation say about you in light of what everyone else built?"*

### Finding 05: Geneva Circuit Participation as Primary Trust Credential (Displacing Methodology)

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior Phase 06 cycles named methodology as the primary credential. C291 Phase 06 confirms a structural shift: methodology documentation has become table stakes (every credible advisor can produce methodology documentation). The primary trust credential has shifted to circuit participation — the observable fact of having been present in the rooms where standards formed, having participated in the decisions that shaped the accountability environment, having been present when the Geneva circuit reached its conclusions.

**The finding:** The ICP evaluates trust through a specific sequence: first, circuit belonging recognition — is this person in the same rooms I am, or in rooms I can't access? Second, methodology coherence — does the methodology explain what I experienced in those rooms? Third, proportionality — does the solution fit my scale, or is this designed for someone else? The circuit participation question is binary and evaluative before any substantive evaluation begins. If the answer is no, the ICP does not proceed to methodology evaluation.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named methodology as the primary credential alongside circuit participation. This finding isolates the specific hierarchy: circuit participation is now the primary gate, and methodology coherence is evaluated only after the circuit participation threshold is crossed.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The question isn't whether I have a methodology. Every credible advisor has a methodology. The question is: was I in the rooms where the standards that methodology is responding to were actually formed, or am I responding to published standards after the fact?"*

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named future-oriented triggers (upcoming renewal, anticipated regulation). This finding confirms the retrospective shift is structural and permanent — the ICP who has already experienced the retrospective moment is in a different conversion state than the ICP who is anticipating future risk.
