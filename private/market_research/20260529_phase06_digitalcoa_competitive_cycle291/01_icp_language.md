## How Geneva MDs, Partners, and Practice Leaders Talk About DigitalCoa.ch

### English

**On what prompted the search:**
"I had signed the carrier form. I could not tell you exactly what I had committed to, which is exactly the problem. The question was not whether I needed help — I could see the questions arriving before they landed. The question was whether there was anyone who could speak about it with the precision the situation required."

**On what the first qualification call revealed:**
"It was the first conversation in which someone named what I was experiencing before attempting to solve it. Most conversations I had tried before were solution-focused before the problem had been named. This one described the accountability geometry first. By the end of the call I knew whether the practice could see my situation, and I knew before they proposed anything."

**On what the written summary would be used for:**
"Not for my own records. I needed a document that would be defensible in six months when the board asks questions I cannot reconstruct from memory. The conversation was useful. The summary is what I will actually use. Those are different outputs."

**On Geneva-specific calibration:**
"What distinguished this from regulatory update briefings I had received from larger firms was that it described how the standards were forming, not just what they were. The carrier's iteration logic, the informal circuit's effect on what 'adequate' means in practice, the specific timing of when this becomes unavoidable versus when it is still optional — that is intelligence from inside the formation process, not observation of the output."

**On why the cross-vertical framing mattered:**
"My situation is not purely a legal question or a finance question. The carrier form touches all of it simultaneously. A practice that can see across the verticals simultaneously is the only one that could name the specific problem I have, not a version of it mapped from a single sector."

## Language Register Analysis

### What the ICP is NOT looking for
- General AI governance education
- Framework comparison or selection guidance
- Compliance checklist or documentation template
- Multi-session advisory program without acute trigger
- International firm with Swiss presence but not Swiss depth

### What the ICP IS looking for
- Someone who can name the specific accountability geometry before proposing solutions
- Evidence of sustained circuit participation across Geneva accountability ecosystem
- Documentation that survives into future accountability contexts, not just records the conversation
- Pricing proportionate to the acute urgency, not scaled to enterprise engagement
- Proof that the advisor has navigated the same personal liability terrain they are describing

### Registration Markers (How ICPs Signal Readiness)
- "I signed something and I need to understand what I committed to"
- "The board is asking questions I cannot answer from memory"
- "My institutional clients are beginning to ask methodology questions"
- "I have talked to two people and I am now looking for a third"
- "I need a document I can defend in six months, not a summary of what we discussed"

### Key Linguistic Markers for Positioning
- Accountability geometry (not AI governance framework)
- What you signed vs. what you built (not compliance gap)
- Before it arrives (not regulatory deadline)
- The circuit (not the market)
- Survive into (not document for)
