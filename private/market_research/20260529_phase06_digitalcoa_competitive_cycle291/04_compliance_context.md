## Current Regulatory Timing Affecting DigitalCoa.ch

### 1. Federal Council AI Coordination Deliberation — Active Window

Federal Council deliberation on AI coordination authority is active now. The specific question — whether Bern establishes a coordinating body with standard-setting authority or merely a coordination contact point — has specific implications for how accountability standards will form in Geneva over the next 18–36 months. Pre-positioning before deliberation produces outcomes is the current window. The practice's current participation in the Federal Council's consultation processes and informal circuit intelligence on the deliberation produces positioning advantage no international firm can replicate without the same Bern participation.

**Practice implication:** Sustained Bern engagement is not a public affairs function. It is competitive intelligence infrastructure. The Federal Council deliberation is one of the five environments that produce the accountability standards the practice's ICPs are being evaluated against.

### 2. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 Operationalization — Present-Tense Competitive Variable

FINMA Guidance 08/2024 on AI-assisted wealth management has moved from anticipation to operationalization. Geneva wealth management firms are discovering that documented AI governance practices created under prior conditions are inconsistent with the operationalization requirements now being applied. The gap between prior attestation and current operational requirements is producing documented inconsistencies that carriers are beginning to probe directly.

The documentation window that was positioned as a forward-looking competitive asset is now a present-tense competitive variable. Firms with documented practices are experiencing direct business value: client retention, competitive differentiation, lower regulatory administrative burden. Firms without documentation are experiencing present-tense competitive liability.

**Practice implication:** The conversion conversation is shifting from "you need to build this before the window closes" to "here is what building it during the window produced, and here is what not building it is costing you right now." The value proposition is more concrete than it was six months ago.

### 3. AI Convention Ratification — Private Law Exposure Active Now

The Council of Europe AI Convention, ratified by Switzerland in February 2025, creates present-tense private law obligations through existing client contracts. The Convention's Article 5 obligations on "high-risk AI systems" are operating through existing engagement letters, client mandates, and professional service contracts that predate the Convention's ratification.

The specific mechanism: every active client engagement is governed by contracts that contain no AI authorization, no methodology disclosure, no version-locking provisions. The Convention's obligations attach to those existing contracts now, without requiring new documentation to activate. The obligations are enforceable through the same private law mechanisms as any other contractual commitment.

**Practice implication:** The ICP who understands the AI Convention's present-tense private law effect understands that the accountability architecture question is not about future compliance — it is about exposure that exists now, in every active client relationship, through contracts that predate the AI tools currently in use.

### 4. nFADP Enforcement Maturation — Documentation Standards Rising

The nFADP has been in force since September 2023. The FDPIC's May 2025 confirmation that nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing without requiring separate legislation removes the ambiguity that had kept some firms in a wait-and-see posture. The enforcement maturity curve is rising. Documentation standards that were acceptable in 2023 are becoming inadequate in 2026 as the FDPIC's enforcement posture matures and the first enforcement cases clarify expectations.

**Practice implication:** The nFADP documentation that the practice helps ICPs build is not compliance theater. It is evidence of deliberate governance that the FDPIC will evaluate against standards that are now forming faster than they were 18 months ago.

---

### 5. Carrier Attestation Fourth-Generation — Liability Absorption Focus

Carrier AI attestation forms have reached fourth-generation iteration in Geneva wealth management and legal sectors, with healthcare carrier attestation arriving on a 6–12 month lag. Fourth-generation iteration is structurally different from earlier generations: earlier generations probed documentation existence. Fourth-generation probes liability absorption capacity — what happens to the firm's position when documented practices are tested under examination or dispute.

This shifts the advisory entry point from documentation inventory to risk distribution architecture evaluation. The ICP arriving with fourth-generation attestation already knows they face a different question than earlier generations asked.

**Practice implication:** The practice's methodology must reflect fourth-generation literacy. The conversion conversation with a prospect who has received fourth-generation questions is different from the conversation with a prospect who received first-generation questions. The entry point language, the problem-naming sequence, and the solution architecture all shift.

---

### 6. Geneva Professional Body Guidance Vacuum — Structural, Not Temporary

The Geneva Bar, SROs for wealth management, and FMH have all issued principle-level guidance without operational content. The vacuum is consistent, structural, and not resolving in the near term. Professional bodies face less exposure from issuing vague guidance than from issuing specific guidance that later proves inadequate.

The vacuum hands standard-setting to the environments that are operating: carrier underwriting iteration, institutional counterparty questionnaires, informal peer circuit formation, and Bern deliberation processes. These are the actual standard-setting environments. The practice's participation in all four simultaneously is what produces the cross-vertical synthesis intelligence.

**Practice implication:** The positioning statement "no Geneva professional body has published what 'adequate' means" is not a negative observation about the market. It is a positive statement about the practice's position: the vacuum is the reason the practice exists, and the practice's sustained participation in the environments that are actually setting standards is the reason it can serve ICPs no other advisor can serve.
