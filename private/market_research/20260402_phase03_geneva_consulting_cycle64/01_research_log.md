## NET-NEW Finding 1: KOF Labor Shortage Study — March 25, 2026

**Source:** kmu.admin.ch (SECO SME Portal) — "Increasing labor shortage in Switzerland" (25.03.2026)  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:17 UTC

**Key data from 103 economists surveyed by KOF:**

| Finding | Stat |
|---------|------|
| Expected labor shortage over 10 years | ~400,000 workers |
| Demographic change as main factor | 87% of economists |
| Skills mismatch as factor | 63% |
| Insufficient foreign labor access | 43% |
| Immigration as moderate/significant solution | 73% |
| Monetary policy contribution (rejected) | Only 26% think it contributes |

**Top company-level measures (economist consensus):**
- Improving working conditions: 94%
- Increasing real wages: 90%
- Public action to increase female labor participation: 80%
- Promoting continuing education: 78%

**Implications for Geneva consulting sector:**
1. AI adoption is a structural solution to demographic-driven labor shortage — consulting firms positioned to help clients
2. Skills mismatch (63%) = consulting firms offering AI upskilling have a market
3. Continuing education promotion (78%) = AI training products validated by economist consensus
4. 400,000-worker gap = significant consulting engagement opportunity in AI-enabled productivity

## NET-NEW Finding 2: IFZ FinTech 2026 Study — April 1, 2026

**Source:** kmu.admin.ch (SECO SME Portal) — "Fintech: growth slows" (01.04.2026)  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:17 UTC

**Key data:**

| Finding | 2025 | 2024 | Trend |
|---------|------|------|-------|
| Total Swiss fintech companies (incl. Liechtenstein) | 529 | 508 (+4%) | Growth slowing |
| AI/Data Analytics/Big Data segment | 35% | 28% | **▲ Fastest growing** |
| Distributed Ledger Technology | 34% | — | Stable |
| Process Digitalization/Automation/Robotics | 31% | — | Stable |
| Banking Infrastructure segment | 39% | 36% | ▲ Growing |
| Wealth Management segment | 37% | 39% | ▼ Declining |

**Key insight:** AI/Data Analytics has become the **largest single fintech segment** in Switzerland — 35% of all fintech companies. This is the first time AI has overtaken DLT. The rise of AI explains both new company creation and repositioning of established firms.

**Wealth management decline interpretation:** Likely reflects AI applications being reclassified into "AI/Data Analytics" rather than pure wealth management — the underlying AI adoption in WM is growing.

**Geneva positioning:** Geneva and Zurich remain among the top 5 global fintech hubs (behind Singapore). Venture capital activity has slowed globally.

**Implications for Geneva consulting sector:**
1. AI is the #1 fintech growth category — consulting firms need credible AI practices to serve fintech clients
2. SaaS is the dominant monetization model — consulting around SaaS AI tool selection and implementation is a service line
3. Banking infrastructure growing (39%) — AI consulting for banking infrastructure modernization is a live market
4. Wealth management declining as % = WM firms need AI differentiation — consulting opportunity to help WM firms compete
