## ⭐ NET-NEW C64

### NET-NEW 1: KOF Labor Shortage Study — March 25, 2026

**Source:** SECO SME Portal (kmu.admin.ch) — KOF survey of 103 economists  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:17 UTC

**Most significant finding for consulting sector:**

> "73% of economists believe immigration will moderately or significantly reduce this structural shortage"

This is a critical market signal: Swiss companies — and by extension their consulting advisors — face a structural 400,000-worker labor gap. Immigration is the primary economic policy response. AI adoption is the private sector operational response.

**Consulting sector implications:**

1. **AI as labor shortage solution:** Consulting firms advising Swiss SMBs on AI adoption are addressing the #1 structural economic challenge
2. **Skills mismatch (63%):** AI upskilling and training is a validated consulting product (economist consensus)
3. **Continuing education (78%):** AI training services validated at policy level
4. **Working conditions + wages (94%, 90%):** Non-AI responses to labor shortage — but AI can amplify these investments

### NET-NEW 2: IFZ FinTech 2026 Study — April 1, 2026

**Source:** SECO SME Portal (kmu.admin.ch) — Lucerne University of Applied Sciences and Arts  
**Fetched:** 2026-04-01 22:17 UTC

**AI is now the #1 Swiss fintech segment:**

- 35% of Swiss fintech companies active in AI/Data Analytics/Big Data (up from 28% in 2024)
- AI has overtaken DLT (34%) as the largest segment
- Process automation (31%) is third
- Geneva + Zurich: top 5 global fintech hubs
- Banking infrastructure (39%) and wealth management (37%) are the two largest vertical segments

**Consulting sector implications:**

1. **AI practice credibility:** Consulting firms need demonstrable AI capabilities to serve the growing AI-focused fintech segment
2. **WM consulting opportunity:** WM declining as % (from 39% to 37%) despite AI growth — WM firms need AI consulting to compete
3. **Banking infrastructure:** 39% of fintech activity — AI consulting for core banking modernization is a live service line
4. **SaaS monetization:** AI SaaS tools dominate — consulting for SaaS AI tool selection, implementation, and governance is a natural offering

## Geneva Consulting Sector AI Governance — Four-Layer Exposure (C64 Update)

1. **FADP (Swiss)** — Consulting deliverables using AI: client data AI processing needs DPIA if high-risk; FDPIC September 2025 update applies
2. **EU AI Act GPAI** — Consulting firms using GPAI tools for client work must assess conformity; August 2, 2026 legacy deadline
3. **Section 301 (Forced Labour)** — NEW SMB consulting product: supply chain ESG forced labour documentation; consulting firms advising clients on ESG mandates need Section 301 compliance framework
4. **Federal Council Sector-Specific** — No Swiss AI Act; SBA sector-specific approach; consulting firms must self-assess GPAI obligations

**Unique consulting dimension:** Consulting firms using AI for internal knowledge management, proposal generation, research synthesis, and client deliverable automation face GPAI + FADP obligations — but also have an opportunity to build AI governance practices for clients
