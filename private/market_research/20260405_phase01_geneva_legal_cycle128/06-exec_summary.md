### Situation

Geneva's legal market is being reshaped by four converging forces: (1) the EU Data Act creating extraterritorial B2B data sharing obligations for Swiss companies with EU market access; (2) Swiss climate litigation becoming procedurally real — December 2025 Holcim Zug ruling admitted the first private Swiss climate claim including worldwide subsidiaries, with direct board liability under Art. 964 OR; (3) Switzerland definitively rejecting class action reform — only injunctive relief available under Art. 89 SCCP; and (4) procedural law amendments effective January 2026 creating new commercial dispute and contract review requirements. These are not regulatory predictions — they are live legal developments requiring client advisory.

---

### Key Findings

**Finding 1 — EU Data Act B2B Compliance: September 2026 Deadline for Swiss Companies**
The EU Data Act's "Access by Design" requirements for connected products become effective September 12, 2026. Swiss manufacturers and service providers with EU market access face mandatory B2B data sharing obligations covering both personal and non-personal data. This is a distinct compliance obligation separate from FADP and GDPR — with its own enforcement logic and its own deadline. Geneva law firms have not yet positioned this as a dedicated service line. Advisory opportunity: EU Data Act compliance advisory for Swiss precision manufacturing, medtech, industrial equipment companies.

**Finding 2 — Swiss Climate Litigation Precedent Set: Holcim Zug Ruling + KIG/KIV**
The Cantonal Court of Zug declared admissible (December 17, 2025) the first Swiss private climate lawsuit against Holcim — including worldwide subsidiary activities. Combined with the Climate and Innovation Act (KIG/KIV, effective January 1, 2025) and Art. 964 OR (effective July 20, 2025) imposing personal fines up to CHF 100,000 on board members for ESG failures, Swiss directors face concrete personal liability for climate harm. Geneva listed companies and financial institutions are in the highest-exposure category. Advisory opportunity: board ESG liability defense, climate litigation response, KIG/KIV transition plan compliance.

**Finding 3 — Swiss Verbandsklage Reform Rejected: Class Actions for Damages Not Available**
Switzerland's Council of States and National Council Legal Committee have definitively rejected comprehensive class action bills. Only Art. 89 SCCP injunctive relief is available — associations may seek removal, injunctions, or declaratory judgments, but NOT damages for a group. International clients assuming Swiss defendants face US/EU-style class action exposure are operating under a misconception. Advisory opportunity: class action defense for Swiss companies; cross-border litigation strategy; Art. 89 association action navigation.

**Finding 4 — Swiss Procedural Law Amendments Effective January 2026**
Debt collection/bankruptcy reform, defect notification rules for goods/services contracts, and new criminal stalking provisions all took effect January 1, 2026. These changes require updates to commercial contract review practices and Geneva court procedural strategy. Advisory opportunity: updated commercial contract templates; defect notification compliance; criminal law dimension of business disputes.

**Finding 5 — CYIO Cyber Resilience: NIS2-Equivalent Obligations Without Swiss Legislation**
Switzerland's Critical Infrastructure Ordinance (CYIO) creates domestic cyber resilience obligations for financial sector critical infrastructure, but without corresponding NIS2 legislation. Geneva financial institutions face confusion about: what CYIO requires, how it relates to FINMA supervisory expectations, and whether NIS2-equivalent contractual requirements from EU counterparties apply to them. Advisory opportunity: CYIO gap analysis; NIS2 contractual requirement advisory; regulatory clarity on applicable framework.

**Finding 6 — Legal Privilege in Swiss Internal Investigations: Multi-Jurisdictional Exposure**
Swiss law does not recognize attorney-client privilege for internal investigations the way US/UK law does. Geneva financial institutions facing simultaneous FINMA + US DOJ/FCA investigations are particularly exposed to privilege waiver risk. No Geneva law firm has positioned internal investigation privilege structuring as a distinct service offering. Advisory opportunity: investigation design for privilege protection; cross-border privilege management; FINMA investigation response.
