# 01-ICP.md — Geneva Legal: Ideal Client Profile
## CYCLE 128 — Phase 01 Geneva Legal

## ICP: Large/ELM Legal Firms in Geneva

### Firmographics
- **Target firm size:** 20-200+ lawyers; international partnerships with 2+ global offices
- **Key firms:** Lenz & Staehelin, Vischer, Walder Wyss, Kellerhals Carrard, Python, BianchiSchwartz, Meyerlustenberger + internationals with Geneva presence (Clifford Chance, White & Case, Latham)
- **Geneva concentration:** Geneva-headquartered or Geneva-dominant offices
- **Practice areas served:** Banking & finance, dispute resolution, corporate/M&A, private wealth, regulatory

### Decision-Makers
- **Primary:** Managing Partner, Head of Banking & Finance, Head of Dispute Resolution
- **Secondary:** General Counsel / Head of Legal Operations (for large firms with in-house legal ops)
- **Influencers:** Practice group leaders, senior associates managing matters

### Pain Points (C128 Fresh Angles)
1. **EU Data Act B2B compliance (Sept 2026):** Manufacturers/servicers with EU exposure need new contract frameworks, product data sharing policies, and dispute resolution procedures — not covered by existing FADP/GDPR counsel relationships
2. **Climate litigation advisory:** Holcim Zug ruling (Dec 2025) + KIG/KIV effective Jan 2025 + Art. 964 OR board liability creates new client need for climate litigation defense and board ESG liability protection
3. **Verbandsklage misconception correction:** Clients incorrectly assume Swiss class action mechanism exists; need clarification on Art. 89 SCCP injunctive relief only — creates advisory opportunity for both defendants and potential plaintiffs
4. **CYIO/NIS2-adjacent cyber resilience:** No standalone Swiss NIS2 but CYIO obligations for financial sector critical infrastructure — clients confused about what applies and how to coordinate with FINMA expectations
5. **Internal investigation privilege:** Multi-jurisdictional investigations (FINMA + US DOJ/FCA simultaneously) create complex privilege management challenges Geneva financial institutions haven't adequately addressed

### Buying Behavior
- **Vendor selection:** Relationship-driven, reputation in specific practice area, partner-level engagement
- **Evaluation criteria:** Track record in specific regulatory area, partner accessibility, pricing (AFAs/AFAs blended), understanding of Geneva financial market dynamics
- **Sales cycle:** 3-12 months for new relationship; existing relationships expand through matter quality

### Trigger Events
- FINMA investigation or enforcement action
- New regulatory requirement (EU Data Act, KIG/KIV, Art. 964 OR)
- Climate litigation filing
- Internal investigation triggered by board or regulator
- Cross-border dispute involving Swiss-seated arbitration or Geneva court

### Budget/Mandate Size
- Regulatory advisory mandates: CHF 50-300K per matter
- Transactional/litigation: CHF 200K-2M+ per matter
- Retainer relationships: CHF 100-500K/year for ongoing regulatory counsel

### Competitive Positioning Notes
- Differentiators: Geneva-specific financial market knowledge + regulatory intelligence + international arbitration capability
- Weakness to avoid: Generic Big 4 or US firm positioning; price-only competition
- Geneva moat: Neutral seat, multilingual capability (EN/FR/DE), deep financial institution relationships
