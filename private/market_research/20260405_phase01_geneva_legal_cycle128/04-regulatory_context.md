## CYCLE 128 — Phase 01 Geneva Legal

## Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal Market (C128 Fresh Angles)

### Regulatory Landscape Overview

Geneva legal advisory demand is driven by the intersection of:
- Swiss domestic regulation (FINMA, FDFA, SECO, Federal Council)
- EU regulatory extraterritoriality (EU AI Act, EU Data Act, DORA, NIS2 expectations)
- International frameworks (Council of Europe AI Convention, FATF, ICC arbitration rules)
- Domestic Swiss legislation in force/amendment

---

### C128 Key Regulatory Developments

#### 1. EU Data Act — B2B Data Sharing (Applicable: September 12, 2025; Key Deadline: September 12, 2026)
**Jurisdiction:** EU extraterritorial — applies to Swiss companies serving EU market
**Admin authority:** EU Commission (enforcement); Swiss companies comply voluntarily or through EU market access terms
**What it requires (for September 2026):**
- New connected products designed to allow users to access data generated by use
- Fair, non-discriminatory B2B data sharing contract terms
- Right for users to share data with third parties
- Applies to both personal and non-personal data from connected products/services
**Swiss legal overlap:** nFADP (personal data), Swiss contract law (OR), product liability (PrLA)
**Geneva relevance:** Precision manufacturing, medtech, industrial equipment, IoT companies with EU sales
**Citation:** PwC CH EU Data Act guide; Europa.eu Data Act fact pages

---

#### 2. Swiss Climate and Innovation Act (KIG) and Ordinance (KIV)
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland domestic
**Effective:** January 1, 2025
**What it requires:**
- Legally binding Swiss climate targets by sector
- Companies seeking financial support must develop transition plans
- Financial industry mandate to align financial flows with climate-compatible objectives
**Enforcement:** Various cantonal and federal authorities depending on sector
**Geneva relevance:** All Geneva companies with financial support needs; Geneva financial institutions (banks, asset managers)
**Key risk:** Board liability under Art. 964 OR (see below)

---

#### 3. Article 964 OR — Direct Board Liability for ESG Failures
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland domestic
**Effective:** July 20, 2025
**What it requires:** Board members directly accountable for:
- Approving sustainability report
- Due diligence on child labor and conflict minerals
**Penalties:** Up to CHF 100,000 for false statements; up to CHF 50,000 for negligence
**Geneva relevance:** Listed companies with Geneva HQ; companies subject to climate disclosure requirements
**Source:** engageability.ch; Swiss Code of Obligations Art. 964

---

#### 4. Holcim Zug Ruling — Climate Litigation Admissibility (December 17, 2025)
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland (Canton of Zug)
**What it established:** First Swiss civil court admits private climate lawsuit against a Swiss corporation including worldwide subsidiary activities; creates director liability for failure to anticipate climate harm
**Precedential value:** Cantonal level — not binding nationally but highly persuasive
**Geneva relevance:** Geneva-listed companies; Geneva financial institutions as defendants or lenders
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin; Loyens Loeff; IFLR

---

#### 5. Verbandsklage — No Damages Class Action in Switzerland
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland domestic
**Current status:** Both Council of States and National Council Legal Committee rejected comprehensive class action bills; no damages-based class action mechanism exists
**Available mechanism:** Art. 89 SCCP — associations of national/regional importance may bring injunctive relief, removal, or declaratory actions only — NOT damages
**Alternative:** Party joinder under Art. 70-71 SCCP for individual damages claims
**Geneva relevance:** International clients pursuing claims against Swiss defendants; Swiss companies as defendants
**Source:** Swiss Holdings; Chambers Practice Guide Collective Redress 2025

---

#### 6. Swiss Procedural Law Amendments — January 1, 2026
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland domestic (federal)
**What changed:**
- Debt collection and bankruptcy law amendments
- Defect notification rules for purchase/sale of goods and services
- Criminal law stalking provisions (new offense)
**Geneva relevance:** Commercial disputes; Geneva courts' procedural handling; criminal dimension of business disputes
**Source:** Charles Russell Speechlys; domain knowledge

---

#### 7. CYIO — Critical Infrastructure Cyber Resilience
**Jurisdiction:** Switzerland domestic
**Admin authority:** Federal Office for Civil Protection (FOCP)
**What it covers:** Systemically important financial institutions (SIFEM), other critical sectors
**Overlap with NIS2:** Swiss companies with EU counterparties may face NIS2-equivalent contractual requirements; no formal NIS2 adoption in Switzerland
**Geneva relevance:** Geneva banks, asset managers, insurance companies designated as critical infrastructure
**Source:** Domain knowledge; FOCP publications

---

#### 8. Council of Europe AI Convention — Swiss Implementation Timeline
**Jurisdiction:** International (Council of Europe); Switzerland signatory (signed March 2025)
**Federal Council implementation:** Expected end of 2026
**What it covers:** Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight for AI systems
**Primary focus:** Public sector AI; private sector also affected through procurement and service provision
**Geneva relevance:** Geneva AI companies, Geneva financial institutions deploying AI, Geneva institutions with public sector AI procurement
**Status:** Consultation draft expected H2 2026; Geneva firms advising international clients = growing opportunity
