### C128 Fresh Competitive Angles

**Finding A: EU Data Act — Advisory Gap in Geneva**
Geneva's dominant law firms have not positioned EU Data Act compliance as a distinct service line. The EU Data Act (effective September 2026 B2B product requirements) creates a new compliance domain that doesn't fit neatly into existing practice groups (IP, data protection, commercial contracts). Firms with strong commercial contracts + IP + data protection practices could develop a cross-practice EU Data Act offering. Big 4 legal arms are beginning to address this through their EU offices. Independent Geneva boutiques have an opening to own this niche before the majors consolidate.

**Finding B: Climate Litigation Defense — Limited Swiss Specialist Supply**
The Holcim Zug ruling (December 2025) creates a new category of advisory work: climate litigation defense for Swiss companies and boards. Few Geneva firms have dedicated climate litigation practices. Lenz & Staehelin has published on this (sourcing citation). The advisory demand is likely to outpace specialist supply in the near term — creating an opportunity for firms to position early or for independent advisors to offer this as a distinct service.

**Finding C: Verbandsklage Confusion = Advisory Opportunity**
Swiss law does not permit class actions for damages. International clients — especially US plaintiffs' firms — often assume Swiss defendants are exposed to class action litigation. Geneva firms with international arbitration/dispute resolution practices are well-positioned to advise both defendants (class action defense) and international plaintiffs (Art. 89 association action navigation). This is a positioning play rather than a crowded market.

**Finding D: Internal Investigation Privilege — Untapped Advisory Niche**
No Geneva firm has prominently marketed internal investigation privilege structuring as a distinct service offering. The market is implicit — financial institutions conduct investigations and lawyers advise — but no firm has clearly articulated the privilege risk and structured offering. This is a gap that aligns with Brotons.net positioning: regulatory intelligence + advisory. A Geneva-specific advisory note on "How to Conduct Internal Investigations Without Waiving Privilege Under Swiss Law" could be distinctive content.

**Finding E: CYIO/NIS2-Adjacent Cyber Resilience — Regulatory Complexity Creates Demand**
The confusion around CYIO vs. DORA vs. NIS2-equivalent obligations for Swiss financial institutions creates advisory demand that existing practices (banking regulatory, cybersecurity law) haven't clearly addressed. FINMA-licensed institutions need to understand their CYIO obligations and how they relate to contractual NIS2 expectations from EU counterparties. This is a regulatory intelligence + advisory intersection — aligns with Brotons.net/DigitalCoa.ch positioning.
