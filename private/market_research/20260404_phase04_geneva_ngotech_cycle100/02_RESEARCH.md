## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 2)

### EN
**Geneva Blockchain Ecosystem — Trade Finance and Humanitarian Applications**

Geneva blockchain landscape — 2025-2026:
- Geneva vs. Zug distinction: Zug hosts major crypto exchanges and token issuers (Ethereum Foundation historically, exchanges); Geneva's blockchain identity is different — focus on enterprise blockchain applications in trade, finance, and international organizations
- Geneva blockchain organizations: WEF Blockchain Council (Geneva-based secretariat), Humanitarian Blockchain Foundation (Geneva), Hyperledger Foundation (global, strong Geneva community), Geneva Digital Trade Chain (trade finance blockchain initiative)
- Key applications in Geneva context: (1) Trade finance: blockchain bill of lading, letter of credit digitization via Geneva Trade Chain / Marco Polo (TradeIX); major Geneva commodity traders (Trafigura, Vitol) piloting blockchain trade finance; (2) Humanitarian aid: ICRC exploring blockchain for humanitarian cash transfers (区块链for refugee assistance); WFP already using blockchain for refugee assistance in Jordan (SCMoh camp); (3) Carbon markets: blockchain-based carbon credit registries being developed by Geneva-based environmental organizations
- Regulatory: FINMA guidance on blockchain/DLT (DLT-FG framework — see Phase 02 finding); Swiss blockchain regulation more permissive than EU MiCA (though MiCA now in force)
- Geneva blockchain startups: several Geneva-based blockchain ventures; Crypto Basel (blockchain hub association); DiploFoundation (Geneva-based NGO working on blockchain governance)
- Challenges: enterprise blockchain adoption slower than expected; interoperability between blockchain platforms remains unsolved; energy consumption concerns for proof-of-work
- WEF role: World Economic Forum (Geneva) publishes extensively on blockchain governance; WEF Global Technology Governance Report; multistakeholder blockchain governance frameworks
- Advisory implications: blockchain strategy for trade finance, DLT-FG licensing advisory, blockchain governance frameworks for NGOs, carbon market tokenization advisory

### FR
**Écosystème blockchain genevois — Finance du commerce et applications humanitaires**

Paysage blockchain genevois — 2025-2026 :
- Distinction Genève vs. Zoug : Zoug héberge les principaux échanges crypto et émetteurs de tokens ; identité blockchain de Genève est différente — focus sur applications blockchain d'entreprise dans le commerce, la finance et les organisations internationales
- Organisations blockchain genevoises : WEF Blockchain Council (secrétariat basé à Genève), Humanitarian Blockchain Foundation (Genève), Hyperledger Foundation (communauté forte à Genève), Geneva Digital Trade Chain (initiative blockchain finance du commerce)
- Applications clés : (1) Finance du commerce : connaissement blockchain, numérisation des lettres de crédit via Geneva Trade Chain / Marco Polo ; principaux traders de matières premières genevois (Trafigura, Vitol) piloting blockchain trade finance ; (2) Aide humanitaire : CICR explorant blockchain pour transferts de trésorerie humanitaires ; PAM utilisant déjà blockchain pour assistance aux réfugiés en Jordanie ; (3) Marchés du carbone : registres de crédits carbone basés sur blockchain en développement par organisations environnementales basées à Genève
- Défis : adoption blockchain d'entreprise plus lente que prévu ; interopérabilité entre plateformes blockchain non résolue ; préoccupations de consommation d'énergie pour proof-of-work
- Rôle du WEF : Forum économique mondial (Genève) publie extensivement sur la gouvernance blockchain ; cadres de gouvernance blockchain multipartites
- Implications consultatives : stratégie blockchain pour finance du commerce, conseil en licence DLT-FG, cadres de gouvernance blockchain pour ONG, conseil en tokenisation de marchés carbone
