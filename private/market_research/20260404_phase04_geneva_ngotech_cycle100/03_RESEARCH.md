## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 3)

### EN
**Geneva Impact Investing — ESG Metrics Infrastructure and Advisory Opportunities**

Geneva impact investing ecosystem — 2025-2026:
- Geneva-based ESG standards organizations: UN Environment Programme Finance Initiative (UNEP FI) headquartered in Geneva; Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI, Paris-based but strong Geneva institutional investor base); Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB — now part of Value Reporting Foundation, merged into IFRS Foundation as ISSB)
- ISSB/IFRS Sustainability Standards: IFRS S1/S2 issued 2023 by ISSB (global headquarters in Toronto/Connecticut); IFRS S2 = climate disclosures aligned with TCFD; European adoption underway; Swiss adoption uncertain (FINMA monitoring)
- TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures): now formally recommended by all G20 members; Geneva-based multinationals increasingly required to produce TCFD-aligned disclosures; institutional investors demanding TCFD reports for investment decisions
- UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs): Geneva-originated framework; UN Working Group on Business and Human Rights meets annually in Geneva; supply chain human rights due diligence increasingly required under Swiss mHRDD law
- Geneva's role in impact data: Geneva Impact Investing Hub (Graduate Institute); Geneva Sustainable Finance Alliance; Geneva Association of Sustainability Professionals; WEF ESG metrics working groups
- Impact measurement methodologies: IRIS+ (catalog of impact metrics, maintained by Global Impact Investing Network — GIIN); HIPSO (Harmonized Indicators for Private Sector Operations, hosted by IFC, Geneva); Geneva-based GIIN members contributing to impact data standardization
- Impact investing market size: global impact investing market ~USD 1.57 trillion (2024 GIIN survey); Swiss institutional investors increasingly allocating to impact strategies (visible at Geneva private banks)
- Green bond impact reporting: Swiss Green Bond framework requires impact reporting (see Phase 02 finding); ISSB/IFRS S2 climate metrics alignment for green bond frameworks
- Advisory opportunities: TCFD/ISSB-aligned disclosure frameworks, impact measurement system design, ESG data infrastructure, green bond impact reporting, UNGP human rights due diligence reporting

### FR
**Finance d'impact Genève — Infrastructure des métriques ESG et opportunités consultatives**

Écosystème de finance d'impact genevois — 2025-2026 :
- Organisations genevoises de normes ESG : UN Environment Programme Finance Initiative (UNEP FI) headquartered à Genève ; Principles for Responsible Investment (PRI, base parisienne mais forte base d'investisseurs institutionnels genevois)
- ISSB/IFRS Sustainability Standards : IFRS S1/S2 émis 2023 ; adoption européenne en cours ; adoption suisse incertaine (surveillance FINMA)
- TCFD : formellement recommandé par tous les membres du G20 ; multinationales genevoises de plus en plus required à produire des disclosures TCFD-aligned
- Principes directeurs de l'ONU sur les droits de l'homme et les entreprises (UNGPs) : cadre originating de Genève ; groupe de travail de l'ONU se réunit annuellement à Genève
- Rôle de Genève dans les données d'impact : Geneva Impact Investing Hub (Graduate Institute) ; Geneva Sustainable Finance Alliance ; WEF ESG metrics working groups
- Méthodologies de mesure d'impact : IRIS+ (catalogue de métriques d'impact, maintenu par GIIN) ; HIPSO (IFC, Genève) ; GIIN members based à Genève contribuant à la normalisation
- Opportunités consultatives : cadres de disclosure TCFD/ISSB-aligned, conception de systèmes de mesure d'impact, infrastructure de données ESG, reporting d'impact pour obligations vertes
