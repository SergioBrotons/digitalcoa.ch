## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 6)

### EN
**Tech Talent Migration to Geneva — Filling the Skills Gap**

Geneva tech talent migration — 2025-2026:
- Russian tech talent: post-2022 invasion of Ukraine, significant outflow of Russian tech professionals from Russia; some relocated to Geneva/Switzerland; Swiss L/B work permits available for qualified professionals; Russian-speaking tech community in Geneva growing
- Ukrainian tech professionals: ~7,000 Ukrainian refugees in Geneva canton as of 2024; significant portion are tech professionals (IT, software development, engineering); Swiss政府 (SEM) humanitarian permits enable work authorization; Geneva tech companies actively recruiting Ukrainian talent
- Middle Eastern talent: GCC nationals (UAE, Saudi, Qatar) sending students to Swiss universities (EPFL, Université de Genève, ETH); post-graduation talent retention in Geneva; family wealth management and finance sector jobs for GCC nationals with Swiss education
- Talent gap in Geneva: chronic shortage of software engineers, data scientists, cybersecurity professionals; Swiss tech salary differential vs. US making recruitment difficult; migrant talent filling critical gaps
- Geneva tech employers hiring migrants: major tech employers (Google Geneva, IBM Geneva, Siemens Geneva, Novartis Tech) hire internationally; Geneva's financial sector (banks, commodity trading houses) hire Russian/Arabic-speaking tech professionals for compliance and trading systems
- Startup talent: Geneva startups benefit from migrant talent; FONGIT startups actively recruiting internationally; startup visa (Swiss境外 start-up visa program) available for non-EU nationals
- Immigration framework: Swiss work permit categories (L short-term, B first permit, G cross-border); EU/EFTA nationals have free movement; non-EU nationals face quota system (annual L-permit quotas per canton); Geneva's tech quota competitive
- UN/intergovernmental organizations: large Russian/Arabic-speaking professional community in UNOG, WHO, WTO; tech professionals in UN IT departments; former UN staff transitioning to private sector
- Advisory opportunities: work permit applications for non-EU tech professionals, corporate immigration compliance for tech employers, talent assessment for Russian/Ukrainian credentials, cultural integration programs, HR advisory for companies hiring migrant tech talent
