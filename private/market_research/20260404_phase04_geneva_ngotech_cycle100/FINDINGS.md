## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Geneva as AI Governance Capital — Institutional Infrastructure
- ITU AI for Good Summit (Geneva): largest AI governance multistakeholder event; ITU leading on AI safety standards and inclusive AI for SDGs; ITU HQ in Geneva
- UN AI Advisory Body (2023): 39 members global; co-chaired by US/China/EU/Africa; UN GA adopted AI governance resolution Sep 2024; Geneva-based ITU, OHCHR, WHO designated implementation partners
- WHO AI governance: Geneva-based WHO issued health AI ethics and governance guidelines (2021, updated 2024); health AI regulatory framework development
- WTO engagement: Geneva-based WTO engaging on AI and trade (e-commerce negotiations, Data Free Flow with Trust)
- Geneva diplomatic infrastructure: 180+ embassies, 40+ international organizations, 450+ NGOs with UN status — uniquely positioned for multistakeholder AI governance
- Advisory: AI governance compliance for Geneva companies engaging UN/international processes, public-private collaboration frameworks, Geneva as platform for AI norm-setting engagement

### Finding 02: Geneva Blockchain Ecosystem — Trade Finance and Humanitarian Applications
- Geneva's blockchain identity distinct from Zug: enterprise blockchain for trade finance, humanitarian aid, carbon markets — not crypto trading
- Geneva Digital Trade Chain: blockchain bill of lading, letter of credit digitization; commodity traders (Trafigura, Vitol) piloting
- ICRC/WFP: blockchain for humanitarian cash transfers, refugee assistance (WFP Jordan SCMoh camp already operational)
- WEF Blockchain Council: secretariat in Geneva; multistakeholder blockchain governance frameworks
- Swiss DLT-FG framework: more permissive than EU MiCA for enterprise blockchain applications
- Carbon market tokenization: Geneva-based environmental organizations developing blockchain carbon credit registries
- Advisory: blockchain strategy for trade finance, DLT-FG licensing, blockchain governance for NGOs, carbon market tokenization

### Finding 03: Impact Investing and ESG Metrics Standardization — Geneva's Infrastructure Role
- UNEP FI headquartered in Geneva; Geneva institutional investors driving PRI adoption
- TCFD: now recommended by all G20 members; Geneva multinationals increasingly required to produce TCFD-aligned disclosures
- IFRS S1/S2 (ISSB): global sustainability standards; European adoption underway; Swiss FINMA monitoring
- UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs): Geneva-originated framework; supply chain human rights due diligence required under Swiss mHRDD law
- Impact measurement: IRIS+ (GIIN), HIPSO (IFC Geneva) — impact metrics standardization with Geneva contributors
- Swiss impact investing: ~USD 1.57 trillion global market; Geneva private banks increasingly allocating to impact strategies
- Advisory: TCFD/ISSB-aligned disclosure frameworks, impact measurement system design, ESG data infrastructure, green bond impact reporting

### Finding 04: UN/IO Reform and Efficiency Mandates — Consulting Demand from Geneva Institutional Ecosystem
- Our Common Agenda (UN Secretary-General): 75+ commitments including data strategy, UN Security Council reform, prevention agenda
- US (22% of UN budget) and European contributors pushing value-for-money; real-terms budget cuts despite expanding mandates
- Geneva-based agencies affected: WHO, WTO, ILO, ITU, UNHCR, WMO — all facing efficiency mandates
- UN Digital Strategy (2022): all agencies required to digitize; cloud migration (Google Cloud); Geneva agencies as implementation sites
- Geneva permanent missions (180+) and INGOs (450+): budget pressures creating demand for strategic planning and governance consulting
- UN procurement open to Swiss companies; Geneva-based consulting firms have natural relationship advantage
- Advisory: UN engagement strategy, UN procurement bid support, organizational efficiency assessments, digital transformation roadmaps, INGO board governance

### Finding 05: Geneva Deep Tech / Climate Tech Venture Landscape
- Key investors: Verve (climate tech), Alpine Space Ventures (space tech), Alpha Associates (life sciences), Sictic (ICT)
- FONGIT: Geneva's main tech incubator, 60+ resident startups; Biopôle (life sciences, near Geneva)
- Notable startups: Climework (direct air capture), Astrocast (satellite IoT), ClearSpace (orbital debris), Sophia Genetics (AI genomics, Geneva-founded)
- Swiss deep tech investment: ~CHF 2-3 billion annually (2024); Geneva/Zurich corridor 70%+
- Federal support: Innosuisse, EPFL spinouts; Geneva university ecosystem (Université de Genève, HEPIA)
- Climate tech investment increasing; European Defence Fund creating new dual-use tech investment near Geneva
- Advisory: deep tech due diligence, climate tech impact measurement, IP strategy for startups, corporate venture engagement, HR advisory for scaling startups

### Finding 06: Tech Talent Migration to Geneva — Filling the Skills Gap
- Russian tech professionals: post-2022 outflow; some relocated to Geneva; Swiss L/B permits available; Russian-speaking tech community growing
- Ukrainian refugees: ~7,000 in Geneva canton; significant tech professionals; Swiss government humanitarian permits enable work authorization; Geneva companies actively recruiting
- Middle Eastern talent: GCC nationals studying at EPFL/Université de Genève; post-graduation retention in finance sector
- Chronic Geneva shortage: software engineers, data scientists, cybersecurity professionals; migrant talent filling critical gaps
- UN/intergovernmental: Russian/Arabic-speaking professionals in UNOG, WHO, WTO IT departments; transitioning to private sector
- Swiss immigration framework: EU/EFTA free movement; non-EU quota system; tech quota competitive; startup visa available
- Advisory: work permit applications for non-EU tech professionals, corporate immigration compliance, credential assessment, cultural integration programs
