### 2. ASA below 40 Event — "Should Arbitrators Seek Their Own Facts?" (NET-NEW)
**Source:** https://www.swissarbitration.org/asa-below-40-friends-event-report-2026/
**Date:** 26/03/2026
**Summary:** Angelina Petti (von Segesser Law Offices) delivered keynote on tribunal fact-finding initiative. Panel: Sebastian Lukic (Schellenberg, adversarial/common law perspective), Kartik Singh (Baker McKenzie, Singapore/UK), Nadja Harraschain (rothorn legal, German civil law), moderated by Vincent Reynaud (LALIVE). Key findings: (1) Civil law arbitrators may/should take inquisitorial role under Article 1042(4) German ZPO / DIS Rules Article 28; (2) ICC 2024 Report on Red Flags of Corruption provides structured tools; ICC upcoming umbrella report on corruption; (3) In cases of sham arbitrations with both parties withdrawing once tribunal probes irregularities, little can be done but tribunal avoids facilitating impropriety; (4) In absent-respondent cases, tribunals may transfer burden of proof without explicit notice; (5) Online research by tribunals is more intrusive than requesting clarification from parties.

### 3. International Commercial Courts — Fresh Perspectives (NET-NEW)
**Source:** https://www.swissarbitration.org/fresh-perspectives-on-whats-shaping-arbitration/
**Date:** 25/03/2026
**Author:** Mirella Janett, Walder Wyss
**Summary:** Expert perspectives on international commercial courts vs arbitration. Key findings: (1) DIFC Courts are rapidly becoming a go-to forum for interim measures that support arbitration — in Carmon v Cuenda (2024), DIFC Court of Appeal confirmed it can issue freezing orders before a foreign arbitral award is final, with only a Dubai bank account as connection; (2) Trafigura v Gupta (2025) reaffirmed these powers under the 2025 DIFC Court Law — no physical asset link to DIFC required; (3) Nivalion AG (litigation funder): international commercial courts enhance access to justice and create attractive investment opportunities; (4) Nokia: big players in "waiting mode" until more practical experience — early movers needed; (5) Overall consensus: arbitration will continue to dominate cross-border disputes; international commercial courts are a welcome complementary addition.

### 5. TEF Rules — "Entitled Persons" Concept Deep-Dive (NEW detail)
**Source:** https://www.swissarbitration.org/swiss-arbitration-summit-2026/ + Swiss Arbitration news
**Date:** 26/03/2026 (recap at Summit)
**Summary:** The Innovation Conference at the Summit 2026 dedicated to TEF Rules. Key concept: "entitled persons" (replacing classic bilateral consent). Article 2 TEF Rules: detailed mechanisms for identifying, notifying, and representing entitled persons (heirs, beneficiaries, future/unborn beneficiaries, individuals lacking legal capacity). Article 3: Arbitration Court can appoint arbitrators where not all entitled persons are legally represented. Disclosure obligation rests with claiming party. Drafters: David Brownbill KC (XXIV Old Buildings), Werner Jahnel (LALIVE), Stephanie Pfisterer (Homburger); moderated by Benjamin Gottlieb (Schellenberg Wittmer). Christopher Boog led opening remarks.
