### Signal 2 (NEW): 53% Small Firm Generative AI Adoption — Nearly Doubled in One Year
**Source:** Wolters Kluwer 2026, arenema.com, llamalab.ai
**Type:** Market adoption data
**Confidence:** High (multiple sources)
**Relevance:** Geneva legal

Generative AI adoption among small firms and solo practitioners **nearly doubled from 27% (2023) to 53% (2024)**. This is the fastest-adoption growth segment in the legal market. Combined with 61% of European small law firms already using AI tools (Wolters Kluwer 2026), the adoption gap is closing fast — but governance gap remains wide.

**Implication:** The window for positioning as an AI governance advisor for small firms is narrowing. By 2027, a majority of small firms will be using AI. Without governance frameworks, they face regulatory exposure.

### Signal 6 (NEW): Swiss Law Firm AI Unique Barriers — Data Sovereignty + Swiss Law Compatibility
**Source:** getcasus.com, swlegal.com (ANBLES Feb 2026)
**Type:** Market need signal
**Confidence:** High
**Relevance:** Geneva legal

Swiss law firms face unique barriers that US/UK AI platforms cannot address:
1. **Data residency** — client confidentiality under nFADP requires Swiss or EU-hosted AI
2. **Swiss law compatibility** — AI trained on US/UK law has limited relevance for Swiss Code of Obligations, Civil Code, DSG
3. **CASUS** (Swiss legal AI platform) positioned as data-sovereign alternative — confirming market demand

**Implication:** Generic AI governance frameworks from US/UK vendors do not address Swiss-specific needs. This creates white space for Swiss-focused AI governance advisory.
