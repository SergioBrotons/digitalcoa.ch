## Executive Summary

### Phase 01 Geneva Legal — Cycle 30 Key Findings

**Market Context:**
Geneva's small law firms (2–5 attorneys) face a widening gap between AI adoption and governance readiness. 53% of small firms are now using generative AI — nearly doubled from 27% in 2023. 52% of Swiss organizations use AI agents — above the 46% global average. Yet formal AI governance policies remain rare, and the FDPIC is actively building enforcement cases across other sectors. Law firms are not yet targeted. That window will not stay open.

**Regulatory Reality:**
The revised Federal Data Protection Act (nFADP) applies directly to AI-supported data processing — confirmed by the FDPIC in 2025-2026 guidance (edoeb.admin.ch). No new Swiss AI law is needed; the obligations are already live. The FDPIC expects purpose, functionality, and data sources disclosure for any AI deployment. DPIA obligations for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence) are operative. Privacy by Design triggers at AI procurement, not deployment. The EU AI Act adds extraterritorial obligations for Swiss firms with EU clients or market presence — high-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026.

**Competitive Landscape:**
No boutique firm has established itself as the go-to AI governance advisor for small Geneva law firms after 10+ consecutive cycles of confirmation. Large firms (Homburger, Lenz & Staehelin, Walder Wyss, VISCHER) confirmed enterprise-only via Chambers 2026. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL is the closest structural competitor but not confirmed SMB-geneva-legal-exclusive. The white space is structural and real.

**Timing:**
- NOW: Shadow AI risk + DPIA obligations are present tense, not future
- January 20, 2026: AI Action Plan second consultation began (digitalswitzerland)
- May 2026: Federal Council First Draft expected — press coverage will spike client awareness
- August 2, 2026: EU AI Act high-risk obligations activate
- End-2026: Federal Council formal consultation draft (CoE AI Convention implementation)
- FDPIC enforcement: Building cases across sectors; law firms not yet targeted = window open

**Personal Liability:**
Managing directors face up to CHF 250,000 in criminal liability for intentional nFADP failures (Art. 52). Shadow AI — employees using unapproved tools — is the most immediate exposure and the most actionable entry point for advisory.

**ROI Case:**
Firms with formal AI strategy are nearly 4x more likely to experience significant benefits. AI governance is not a compliance cost center — it is a competitive advantage. Technology spending growth in law firms reached 9.7% in 2025. Budget exists.

**Switzerland-Specific Needs:**
Generic US/UK governance frameworks do not address Swiss-specific requirements: nFADP + FDPIC enforcement patterns + Swiss Code of Obligations/Civil Code compatibility + data residency requirements + French/German/Italian trilingual obligations. CASUS (Swiss legal AI) confirms data sovereignty is a market requirement.
