## Lead Magnet Structure

### Section 1: The Widening Gap
**Purpose:** Establish the problem — AI adoption is accelerating, governance is not

Key statistics:
- 53% of small firms/solo practitioners using gen AI in 2024 (nearly doubled from 27% in 2023) — fastest adoption growth
- 61% of European small law firms already using AI tools (Wolters Kluwer 2026)
- 52% of Swiss organizations using AI agents (above 46% global average)
- Yet significant % using AI without formal governance policies
- **9.7% law firm technology spending growth 2025** — investment acceleration

The gap framing:
"Geneva's small law firms are adopting AI faster than they are governing it. The adoption curve has shifted dramatically — 53% of small firms now using gen AI, nearly doubled in one year. But formal AI governance policies remain rare. This gap creates both risk and opportunity."

---

### Section 2: What Your AI Is Actually Doing
**Purpose:** Shadow AI audit — Decision 0 before regulatory compliance

Key questions:
- Q1: What AI tools are your attorneys using today? (Explicit + implicit)
- Q2: What client data is being processed? (Confidential, sensitive, third-party)
- Q3: Where is that data going? (US cloud, Swiss server, EU, unknown)
- Q4: Do your attorneys know which AI they're using? (Shadow AI = FDPIC + professional liability)

Shadow AI framing:
"Shadow AI — employees using unapproved AI tools — is the most immediate risk. Your attorneys may be using AI tools you don't know about, on client data you haven't authorized. Under the revised nFADP, this exposes the firm to FDPIC enforcement and professional liability."

Urgency lever: Shadow AI is a present tense problem, not a future regulatory risk.

---

### Section 3: What FDPIC Actually Expects
**Purpose:** Regulatory specificity — FADP applies to AI NOW

Key FDPIC positions (edoeb.admin.ch, ongoing 2026):
1. **Purpose disclosure** — what AI is being used for
2. **Functionality disclosure** — what the AI system actually does
3. **Data sources disclosure** — what data is being processed and where it comes from

nFADP operative obligations:
- Art. 5(c): Lawfulness, proportionality, data minimization — AI must comply
- Art. 26-27: DPIA required for high-risk AI processing — contract analysis, due diligence, HR AI = likely high-risk
- Art. 7-8: Privacy by Design and Privacy by Default — triggered at AI procurement, not deployment

FDPIC enforcement pattern:
- 1,053 complaints processed 2024/2025
- 6 confirmed AI enforcement cases 2025-2026 (transport x2, banking, retail x2, social media)
- Law firms ABSENT from enforcement — but enforcement portfolio is building

Timing signal framing:
"The FDPIC has opened AI enforcement cases against transport companies, retailers, and social media platforms. So far, no law firms — but the pattern is clear. The question is not whether the FDPIC will target law firms. The question is whether your firm is prepared before that happens."

---

### Section 4: The DPIA Trigger — Is Your AI High-Risk?
**Purpose:** Immediate operative obligation identification

AI applications in small law firms likely HIGH-RISK (DPIA required):
1. **Contract analysis AI** — processing large volumes of client confidential data, automated decision logic
2. **Due diligence AI** — M&A, corporate transactions, sensitive deal data
3. **AI-assisted legal research** — external data sources, client matter data in third-party systems
4. **AI HR tools** — employee performance, screening, monitoring tools

DPIA requirement framing:
"If you're using AI for contract analysis or due diligence, you are likely processing high-risk data under nFADP Art. 26-27. A DPIA is not optional. It is an operative legal obligation. The question is not whether you need one — it's whether you have one."

### Section 5: The August 2 Deadline — EU AI Act
**Purpose:** International framework compliance for Swiss firms with EU nexus

Key dates:
- **August 2, 2025:** GPAI transparency obligations operative (8+ months already active)
- **August 2, 2026:** EU AI Act high-risk obligations activate

Swiss firms with EU nexus (clients, office, AI output):
- Subject to EU AI Act high-risk obligations
- Applies to AI placed on EU market, operating EU-intended output, or acting as importer/distributor

Scope question framing:
"If any of your AI systems affect EU clients, process data for EU-based matters, or generate output intended for the EU market — you may already be subject to EU AI Act obligations. The August 2, 2026 deadline is real and approaching."
