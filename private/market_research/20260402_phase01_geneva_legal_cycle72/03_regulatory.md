# 03 — Geneva Legal: Regulatory & Compliance Landscape

## Financial Services Regulation

### FINMA Oversight
- Swiss financial market regulation consolidated under FINMA
- Supervises: Banks, insurers, asset managers, OTFs, financial infrastructure
- Geneva concentration: Private banks, wealth managers, family offices
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 18, 2024): Concrete AI governance requirements for supervised institutions; AI Desk established; Federal Council rules expected end 2026
- Enforcement trend: Increasing focus on operational resilience, cyber risk, AI use cases

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- Switzerland: FINMA supervisory organization; MROS (Meldestelle) as financial intelligence unit
- 2024 AML ordinance revisions: Enhanced due diligence for high-risk clients
- Geneva-specific: UNOG, embassies, IOC, ICRC — elevated PEP exposure
- Cross-border dimension: EU AMLD6 implementation in progress

### Investment Screening
- Investment Screening Act: In force; applies to foreign direct investment in sensitive sectors
- Sectors: Energy, water, food, health, transport, telecommunications, media, security, data, space
- Notification: Required above threshold; sanctions for non-compliance
- Relevance to legal advisors: Transaction structuring, deal-level screening compliance

### ESG / Sustainable Finance
- SBA ESG Self-Regulation: Effective January 1, 2026; 86% bank compliance (C71 finding)
- Increasingly niche: As mandatory EU taxonomy expands, voluntary Swiss SBA standard may differentiate
- Federal Council: Sustainable finance strategy; SNB responsible investment approach
- FINMA: ESG disclosure requirements for regulated entities

## Data Protection

### Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / DPA)
- Revised FADP: In force since March 1, 2024 (aligned with EU GDPR)
- Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC): Oversight
- FDPIC website: DNS failure — blocked this cycle
- Key obligations: Data processing registers, DPIAs, data breach notification, cross-border transfer restrictions
- Geneva-specific: Health data, financial data, UN/IOC employee data = elevated sensitivity

### Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) Analogue
- Switzerland: No direct DORA equivalent; FINMA circulars on operational risk apply
- Cross-border firms: EU DORA applies to EU presence
- Trend: FINMA increasing attention to ICT risk, third-party dependencies

## Tax Compliance

### Automatic Exchange of Information (AEOI)
- Switzerland: 110+ partner states; CRS compliant
- FATCA: US-Swiss intergovernmental agreement in force
- Geneva relevance: Private banking due diligence, self-certification requirements

### Withholding Tax
- Refund mechanism: EU savings agreement; bilateral treaties
- Issue: Withholding tax on interest income; EU dispute (amended bilateral package)

### OECD BEPS / Pillar Two
- Switzerland: Implementing OECD Pillar Two (15% minimum corporate tax)
- cantonal adjustments: Geneva maintaining competitiveness
- Effective: January 1, 2025 (Swiss domestic implementation)

## Legal Services Market Access

### Switzerland-EU Legal Services
- Bilateral I: Suspended/modified; services annex affected
- Bilateral III package: Submitted Parliament March 13, 2026 — implications for legal services market access TBD
- No EU single market passport for Swiss legal services providers
- Swiss lawyers: Admission to EU bars requires individual EU state examination

### Third-Country Lawyers
- Swiss Bar admission: Cantonal bar exam (Geneva: AVS required)
- EU/EFTA nationals: Reciprocity provisions
- UK post-Brexit: Separate recognition arrangements; Berne Financial Services Agreement (CH-UK, in force January 1, 2026) covers financial services not legal services

## Class Actions / Collective Redress

- Switzerland: No US-style class action regime
- Collective action: FinTAB (financial market supervision); Act on Collective Capital Investments; investor protection associations
- Recent trend: Increased use of Verbände actions (consumer protection associations)
- Arbitration preference: Many financial disputes canalized to arbitration rather than court

## Regulatory Risk Factors (2026)

1. FINMA AI governance rules finalization (end 2026) — implementation timelines for Geneva firms
2. Bilateral III legal services provisions — market access outcomes
3. DORA / digital resilience — EU convergence pressure on Swiss firms
4. Pillar Two corporate tax — structural implications for firm structuring
5. CS-UB merger arbitrations — outcomes may set precedent for regulatory liability
6. Space law — emerging regulatory framework (April 2026 ASA Geneva event)
