# 02 — Geneva Legal: Key Institutions & Decision-Making Bodies

## Courts

### Swiss Federal Supreme Court (Bundesgericht / Tribunal Fédéral)
- **Seat:** Lausanne (Federal constitutional court)
- **Geneva relevance:** Final appellate review for Geneva-seated arbitrations under Chapter 12 PILA; grounds extremely narrow (public policy, due process, independence)
- **2026 note:** Review of UBS-CS merger arbitrations potentially reaches this level

### Geneva Courts
- **Tribunal de première instance** — first instance civil and commercial
- **Cour de justice** — appellate level for Geneva canton
- **Chambre arbitrale** — dedicated arbitration chamber within Geneva court system

## Arbitration Institutions

### Swiss Arbitration Centre (formerly SCAI)
- **Founded:** 2004 as SCAI by six Swiss Chambers of Commerce
- **2021:** Renamed Swiss Arbitration Centre; strengthened cooperation with ASA
- **Rules:** Swiss Rules of International Arbitration (based on UNCITRAL Rules)
- **Scope:** Administer arbitrations under Swiss Rules; SCCA handles sports arbitration separately
- **Governance:** Board of Swiss Chambers' Arbitration Institution
- **Legacy:** Agreements referencing "SCAI" or "Chambers of Commerce arbitration" still valid and administered

### ICC International Court of Arbitration
- **Headquarters:** Paris (11e)
- **Geneva practitioners:** Active ICC arbitrators based in Geneva; ICC DRS participated in Swiss Arbitration Summit 2026
- **2026:** Revised Rules of Arbitration enter force June 1, 2026
- **2025 volume:** 881 cases filed; $299B in pending disputes
- **Key figures:** Claudia Salomon, President

### WIPO Arbitration and Mediation Center
- **Seat:** Geneva (WIPO HQ)
- **Specialization:** Intellectual property disputes, domain name disputes (.ch, .li, international), technology agreements
- **Relevance:** Geneva-based multinationals frequently use WIPO for IP matters
- **UDRP:** Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy administered by WIPO

### UNCITRAL
- **Role:** UN Commission on International Trade Law — sets model law standards
- **2026 activity:** Commission session June 22 – July 3, 2026 (NY); finalizing electronic arbitral award recognition; Working Group III on ISDS reform
- **AI Colloquium:** February 16–17, 2026, NY — AI in dispute resolution
- **Switzerland status:** Not UNCITRAL member (unlike EU states); Swiss law not based on UNCITRAL Model Law but compatible

## Regulatory Bodies

### FINMA
- **Role:** Financial market regulator — Swiss financial services firms, banks, insurers, OTFs
- **Geneva relevance:** Banks, wealth managers, asset managers, family offices
- **2024/2026 AI guidance:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (AI governance framework, December 2024)
- **Website access:** finma.ch — some pages restructured/404 in current cycle

### Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IGE/IPI)
- **Seat:** Bern (central), Geneva satellite office
- **Role:** Patent, trademark registration; IP policy
- **WIPO connection:** Close working relationship via Geneva location

### State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO)
- **Role:** Bilateral agreements, trade policy, investment screening
- **ACCTS consultation:** 404 — inaccessible this cycle
- **Bilateral EU package:** Submitted Parliament March 13, 2026

### Federal Office of Justice (BJ)
- **Role:** International legal cooperation, arbitration law, private international law
- **UN Treaty role:** Depositary functions for Switzerland
