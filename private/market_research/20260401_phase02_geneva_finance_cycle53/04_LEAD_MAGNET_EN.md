**2. Operational risk.** 78% of Swiss wealth managers are exploring agentic AI — autonomous systems that execute workflows without human review. ## Your AI Governance Readiness Checklist

Work through these six decisions to map your current state:

**Decision 0 — Shadow AI Audit**
Before building a governance framework, you need to know where AI already exists in your practice. Answer honestly:
- Which GenAI tools are your relationship managers using today? (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Bloomberg AI, others?)
- Are any AI outputs reaching clients — in reports, summaries, portfolio commentaries, or communications?
- Is client data being entered into AI tools, even incidentally?
- Are AI tools being used for portfolio construction, risk assessment, or compliance monitoring?
- Who reviews AI-generated content before it goes to clients?
- Do your IT and vendor contracts address AI third-party risk?

**Decision 1 — AI Inventory**
Do you have a written inventory of every AI tool in use across your firm? FINMA Guidance 08/2024 requires this. Most boutique WMs don't have one.

**Decision 2 — Risk Classification**
Have you classified your AI tools by risk level? High-risk: AI in client-facing communications, portfolio recommendations, risk assessment. Lower-risk: internal admin, research, formatting.

**Decision 3 — Accountability Assignment**
Is there a named individual accountable for each AI tool's governance? Not IT. Not compliance in general. A specific person with specific responsibility.

**Decision 4 — Data Quality and Privacy**
Client data in AI tools creates FADP exposure. Are you processing data lawfully (FADP Art. 5(c))? Are you disclosing AI use to clients (FADP Art. 26)? Are you maintaining the right to human review of automated decisions (FADP Art. 21)?

**Decision 5 — Staff Awareness and Training**
Do your relationship managers understand the risks of GenAI? Do they know what they can and cannot share with AI tools? Most WM teams don't have clear guidance. That's the gap.

## What Most Boutique WMs Are Getting Wrong

**Waiting for regulatory clarity.** The regulations aren't unclear. CARF/CRS 2.0 is operative. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 is published. The SIF Report will add nuance, but the obligations already exist.

**Treating AI governance as an IT project.** It's not. It's a business governance issue. The accountability starts with the managing partner, not the IT vendor.

**Assuming vendor compliance is sufficient.** Your AI vendors have their own compliance posture. Your supervisory obligations to FINMA are yours.

**Underestimating the client trust dimension.** AI governance is increasingly a differentiator in the UHNW/HNW segment. Clients are asking. Firms that can demonstrate a governance framework have a competitive advantage in client retention and acquisition.
