### SECTION 2: Decision 1 — Are You Using AI Tools in Client Work?

**If YES:** Your firm has ACTIVE GPAI obligations (operative August 2, 2025) AND nFADP obligations. These are not future risks — they are live compliance gaps.

**GPAI obligations active RIGHT NOW:**
- [ ] Transparency: disclose AI use to clients (in proposals, reports, communications)
- [ ] Copyright: ensure AI outputs used in deliverables comply with training data copyright
- [ ] Incident documentation: log AI failures or harmful outputs

**If NO:** You have a grace period — but AI adoption is inevitable in consulting. Use this window to build a governance framework before deployment.

---

### SECTION 4: Decision 3 — Do You Have a DPIA for Your AI Tool Stack?

**If NO:** nFADP Art. 22 requires a DPIA BEFORE using AI systems that process personal data. The compliance gap is immediate — not theoretical.

A Data Protection Impact Assessment for your AI stack covers:
- What personal data each AI system processes
- Whether the processing is necessary and proportionate
- What risks the processing poses to data subjects
- What measures you've implemented to mitigate those risks

---

### SECTION 5: Dual Compliance Challenge — EU AI Act + nFADP

A well-designed AI governance framework satisfies both EU AI Act and nFADP simultaneously. The same governance structure that addresses GPAI transparency obligations also documents your nFADP compliance.

**Key overlap:** Both frameworks require:
- Transparency about AI use (EU AI Act GPAI + nFADP Art. 6 transparency rights)
- Human oversight of AI-influenced decisions (EU AI Act Annex III + nFADP Art. 5 accountability)
- Incident/failure documentation (EU AI Act + nFADP Art. 24 security obligations)
