# Phase 03 ICP — Geneva Consulting / Small Firms
## Cycle 8 (2026-03-30)

## Addressable Profile
Boutique consulting firms in Geneva, 2–20 consultants; management consulting, IT consulting, strategy, HR consulting, or marketing consulting; CHF 500K–5M revenue; Geneva (Praille-Acacias, La Praille, or emerging periphérie). Managing Partner or CEO owns technology decisions.

## Addressable Problem
AI tools (ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot) embedded in client workflows — but no structured AI governance framework. Geneva consulting firms face dual compliance (EU AI Act GPAI + nFADP) with no boutique-tier advisory offering to close the gap. GPAI obligations have been live since August 2, 2025 (~7.5 months).

## Regulatory Urgency Stack (Cycle 8 — Carry-Forward + Synthesis)
1. **EU AI Act GPAI** — operative since August 2, 2025 (~7.5 months already live); transparency, copyright, incident documentation obligations active
2. **nFADP** — operative since September 1, 2023; AI processing of client business data = Art. 5(c) triggered; DPIA required
3. **EU AI Act high-risk** — August 2, 2026 (~15 months; IT/tech sub-segment with EU market nexus)
4. **FDJP consultation** — end-2026 (~9 months; will crystallize AI governance standards; trigger market-wide awareness spike)
5. **Swiss Federal Council CoE AI Convention** — consultation draft end-2026 same wave

## Timing Signal
- GPAI ~7.5 months already live (August 2, 2025 operative) — most Geneva consulting firms likely non-compliant
- FDJP consultation end-2026 ~9 months — advisory window narrowing; post-consultation market awareness spike will trigger competitive entry
- Global AI governance market $2.54B→$8.23B (Gartner Feb 2026) — demand signal confirmed
- No boutique consulting AI governance competitor in **8 consecutive cycles**

## Urgency Drivers
- GPAI obligations active: transparency (disclose AI use to clients), copyright handling, incident documentation
- nFADP DPIA required before deploying AI processing personal client data
- IT/Tech sub-segment: EU AI Act Annex III high-risk AI systems (finance, healthcare, education, critical infrastructure) — Geneva prevalent
- FDJP consultation end-2026 will trigger market-wide awareness; first-mover advantage closes post-consultation

## First-Mover Rationale
Window: approximately 9–15 months (FDJP consultation end-2026 = market awareness trigger; EU AI Act high-risk August 2026 = second pressure wave). No boutique advisory competitor in Geneva consulting AI governance (8 confirmations). Global market $2.54B→$8.23B confirms demand trajectory.
