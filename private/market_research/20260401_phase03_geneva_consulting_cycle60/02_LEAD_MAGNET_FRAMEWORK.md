## Problem Statement

The productivity case for generative AI in consulting is proven: Deloitte and PWC research confirms significant time savings through automated documentation, research assistance, and administrative tasks. The labor shortage in Switzerland (400,000 open positions per KOF) makes AI adoption a competitive necessity, not a choice.

But the consulting firms that moved fastest on AI adoption — without building governance frameworks — now face a compliance problem that the productivity narrative didn't prepare them for:

- EU AI Act GPAI obligations have been in force since August 2, 2025
- Federal AI Ordinance has been operative since January 1, 2025 (15+ months)
- CoE AI Convention implementation is underway; consultation draft end of 2026
- August 2, 2026: EU AI Act high-risk AI conformity assessments due

Most small consulting firms know the productivity story. Almost none know the compliance story. That gap is your advisory opportunity.

1. Do we use any AI tools (chatbots, document tools, research tools)?

3. Do we have vendor contracts addressing training data and EU AI Act compliance?
4. Have we assessed whether EU AI Act applies to our firm?

**Track 2 — Client Advisory Offering**
1. GPAI readiness assessment for SMB clients
2. EU AI Act applicability test (two-path: placing on EU market / output used in EU)
3. Sector-specific governance framework development
4. Implementation roadmap to August 2, 2026 deadline
