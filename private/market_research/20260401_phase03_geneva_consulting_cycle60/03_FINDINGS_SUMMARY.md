## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings Summary (C60 vs. Prior Cycles)

### Finding 1: EU AI Act GPAI Obligations Already In Force — SMB Advisory Opportunity
**What changed:** Consulting firms can immediately offer EU AI Act exposure assessments to SMBs. Most SMBs do not know whether the EU AI Act applies to them. A structured two-path applicability test (placing on EU market / output used in EU) is an immediate consulting product requiring no prior AI governance knowledge from the client.

**Why it matters:** The window for "SMB education" on EU AI Act is open right now. By August 2026, SMBs will be in crisis mode. Consulting firms that move now own this advisory space.

**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (via Phase 01 C60 research)

---

### Finding 2: Federal Council Sector-Specific Approach — Framework Differentiation Opportunity
**What changed:** Federal Council explicitly pursuing sector-specific AI governance solutions through existing laws, self-declaration agreements, and industry-specific frameworks. FDJP leading CoE AI Convention implementation. Consulting firms can develop sector-specific AI governance frameworks for specific industries — not generic AI implementation.

**Why it matters:** The firm that develops a financial-services-specific AI governance framework, or a healthcare-specific one, is differentiated from competitors selling generic AI adoption consulting. The self-declaration pathway creates an industry association consulting opportunity.

**Source:** Homburger.ch (via Phase 01 C60 research)

---

### Finding 3: Two-Track GPAI Readiness — Internal First, Then Client
**What changed:** The two-track product is now sharpened: the consulting firm itself must have GPAI governance in place (August 2025 obligations already in force) before credibly advising clients. The living case study — "we did this for ourselves first" — is the competitive differentiator against generalist IT implementation firms.

**Why it matters:** Consulting firms that delayed their own AI governance are now in the awkward position of advising SMBs on something they haven't solved themselves. The firms that act now close that gap and gain a credible advisory position.
