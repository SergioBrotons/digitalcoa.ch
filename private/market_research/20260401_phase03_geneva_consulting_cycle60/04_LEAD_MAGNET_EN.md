## Part I: The Regulatory Reality for Geneva Consulting Firms

### EU AI Act GPAI Obligations — Already In Force Since August 2, 2025

The EU AI Act's obligations for General-Purpose AI (GPAI) have been in force since August 2, 2025. If your consulting firm serves any clients in the EU — or if the output of your AI-assisted work is used in EU business decisions — the EU AI Act applies to you now. This is not an August 2026 problem. It is an active exposure.

The two-path applicability test:
1. **Are you placing AI systems on the EU market?** — Includes offers commercially available to EU users (EUR pricing, EU-directed platforms, EU client relationships)
2. **Is the output of your AI used within the EU?** — Even if your AI operates entirely outside the EU, if the results are used in EU decisions, the EU AI Act applies

### Federal AI Ordinance — 15+ Months Operative

Since January 1, 2025, the Swiss Federal AI Ordinance has required human oversight of AI-assisted decisions. If your consultants use AI to produce deliverables that go to clients — even draft deliverables reviewed by a human — you need documented human oversight protocols.

### August 2, 2026 — Double Deadline

By August 2, 2026: High-risk AI conformity assessments are due under the EU AI Act. Simultaneously, grandfathering for legacy GPAI models expires. Your August 2, 2026 readiness checklist (see Part III) should be well underway by now.

## Part II: The Two-Track GPAI Readiness Approach

The consulting firm that credibly advises SMB clients on AI governance must first govern its own AI use. This is not just good practice — it is a competitive differentiator. The firm that can say "we did this for ourselves first, here is what we learned" has a case study that generic IT implementation firms cannot match.

### Track 1: Internal Governance (Your Firm First)

1. **AI Inventory:** Document every AI tool used in your consulting delivery — chatbots, document tools, research assistants, administrative tools
2. **Vendor Contracts:** Do your contracts address: data residency, training data prohibition, EU AI Act compliance representations?
3. **EU AI Act Exposure:** Have you assessed whether the EU AI Act applies to your firm? (Use the two-path test above)
4. **Human Oversight Protocols:** For each AI use case, is there a documented human review step before client delivery?
5. **Client Disclosure:** Do your engagement letters include AI disclosure language?

### Track 2: Client Advisory Offering

Your advisory product for SMB clients should be structured as a phased engagement:

**Phase 1 — AI Readiness Assessment**
- GPAI applicability test (two-path)
- AI inventory and risk classification
- Gap analysis against Federal AI Ordinance and EU AI Act requirements

**Phase 2 — Governance Framework Development**
- AI governance policy for the client firm
- Sector-specific compliance roadmap (finance, healthcare, legal, real estate)
- Implementation roadmap to August 2, 2026 deadline

**Phase 3 — Ongoing Advisory**
- Quarterly AI governance review
- New AI tool evaluation protocol
- Regulatory monitoring (CoE AI Convention implementation, FINMA guidance evolution)

## Part IV: The Sector-Specific Opportunity

The Federal Council's decision to pursue sector-specific AI governance — rather than a single Swiss AI Act — means that different industries will face different requirements. Consulting firms that develop sector-specific AI governance frameworks can offer differentiated advisory products:

- **Financial services:** FINMA guidance + EU AI Act + Federal AI Ordinance
- **Healthcare:** Swissmedic/BAG guidance + FADP + CoE AI Convention
- **Legal:** SBA guidelines + FADP Art. 21 + EU AI Act (for EU-facing practices)
- **Real estate:** SVIT guidance + Federal AI Ordinance + FADP

The firm that builds a sector-specific framework — validated through its own implementation experience — can offer a differentiated consulting product in a market where most competitors are selling generic AI adoption advice.
