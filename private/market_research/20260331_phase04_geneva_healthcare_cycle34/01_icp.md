# Phase 04: Geneva Healthcare / Small Medical Practices
## ICP — Ideal Client Profile

**Cycle 34 | Date: 2026-03-31**

---

## ICP Definition

### Primary ICP
- **Profile:** Small medical practice, independent physician, dental practice
- **Geography:** Geneva and surrounding canton (1–22 Geneva practitioners estimated)
- **Practice size:** 1–10 practitioners (single-doctor to small group practice)
- **Firm type:** Physicians, dentists, specialists (dermatology, radiology, cardiology sub-segments most exposed to AI diagnostic tools)
- **Technology posture:** Active AI use (individual staff level) without formal practice-level AI governance framework
- **典型 AI exposures:** AI diagnostic imaging, AI symptom triage tools, AI scheduling, AI patient communication

### AI Adoption State (Phase 04)
- Staff using AI tools at individual level → Shadow AI exposure
- No formal practice AI policy → governance gap
- No Swissdamed registration → immediate compliance gap (Jul 1, 2026)
- No EU AI Act readiness → extraterritorial risk (AI diagnostic imaging, AI triage)

### Decision-Maker
- **Primary:** Independent physician / practice owner (personal liability awareness)
- **Secondary:** Practice manager (operational compliance)
- **Tertiary:** Cantonal medical society / FMH affiliation

## ICP Pain Points (Ranked by Urgency)

### 🔴 URGENT (~13 WEEKS)
1. **Swissdamed hard deadline Jul 1, 2026** — NO GRACE PERIOD (confirmed Swissmedic primary source)
   - Medical device registration obligation
   - No transitional period for serious incident device obligations
   - ~13 WEEKS as of 2026-03-31
   - **This is the #1 most urgent deadline in the entire portfolio**

2. **Shadow AI in clinical practice** — staff using AI diagnostic tools without practice-level policy
   - AI diagnostic imaging outputs
   - AI symptom triage
   - Patient data → AI tool processing without DPA/processor agreements
   - FADP exposure

### 🟡 PRESSURE (~1-2 months)
3. **AI diagnostic imaging = high-risk SaMD under EU AI Act**
   - Extraterritorial EU AI Act obligations
   - AI medical devices classified high-risk
   - Swiss firms with EU patients = EU nexus

4. **CHUV Meditron May 2026 (~1 month away)**
   - 241 clinicians, 12,500+ evaluations completed
   - Swiss clinical AI validation signals regulatory awareness
   - AI governance becoming clinical standard

5. **43% NO formal AI policy + NO plans** (Wolters Kluwer March 2026 — Phase 01 Cycle 34 cross-phase finding)
   - Medical practices mirror this statistic
   - Practice staff using AI individually = Shadow AI clinical exposure
   - Phase 01 finding directly applicable to healthcare vertical

### 🟢 STRATEGIC (3–6 months)
6. **Swissdamed/EUDAMED dual registration burden**
   - Not interconnected
   - Separate registration requirements
   - Administrative overhead for small practices

7. **AI medical device post-market surveillance**
   - Serious incident reporting obligation
   - Swissmedic oversight

8. **AI errors/hallucinations as top threat** (57% — Wolters Kluwer March 2026)
   - Clinical decision support AI = patient safety risk
   - Liability exposure for AI-assisted diagnosis

9. **Data protection violations** (55% — Wolters Kluwer March 2026)
   - Patient health data (special category under FADP Art. 5(c))
   - AI tool → processor agreements required
   - DPIA for high-risk AI processing
