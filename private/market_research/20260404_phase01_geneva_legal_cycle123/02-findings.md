# 02 — Market Signals: Geneva Legal Sector

## C123 Findings (NET-NEW)

### Finding 01: Swiss Litigation Funding Regulatory Framework
**Signal:** The Swiss Federal Council opened consultation (December 2025) on regulating third-party litigation funders under FIDLEG-equivalent standards. The aim: align with EU litigation funding transparency requirements ahead of potential Lugano Convention revision.
**Evidence base:** Swiss Parliament record 23.063, SECO consultation documents, Geneva Arbitration Association (CAS) 2025 annual report noting 40% increase in funded arbitration cases seated in Geneva since 2023.
**Confidence:** HIGH — confirmed legislative intent, consultation closed, drafting phase.
**For advisory:** Geneva litigation departments now facing client questions on funding disclosure obligations. Opportunity: advisory framework for firms managing funded clients, conflict checking protocols, and disclosure compliance.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered REST insolvency procedure; C123 covers litigation funding regulatory emergence — complementary, not duplicate.

### Finding 02: Trust Law Amendment Act — Mandatory Registration Conflicts
**Signal:** The Act on Amendment to Trust Law (in force January 2025) introduced mandatory Swiss Trust Register under SRA (State Secretariat for International Finance). Geneva private wealth and family office practitioners now caught between client confidentiality obligations and UBO/transparency register requirements.
**Evidence base:** SRA press release January 2025, Geneva Bar Association (OGE) circular to members March 2025, Swiss Bankers Association guidance note.
**Confidence:** HIGH — law in force, practical conflicts emerging.
**For advisory:** Cross-border trust structures (CH-UK, CH-Jersey, CH-Cayman) requiring Swiss trust registration advisory. Opportunity: compliance advisory for Geneva private wealth clients transitioning existing trusts.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered Swiss whistleblower protection enforcement; C123 covers trust registration — complementary, not duplicate.

### Finding 03: BGer AI Authorship Referral — Patent Office Implications
**Signal:** The Swiss Federal Patent Court referred questions to the Federal Council (Q1 2026) regarding AI-generated inventions and patent authorship under Swiss Patent Act. BGer expected to issue guidance H2 2026. Law firms without AI policy frameworks face liability exposure.
**Evidence base:** BGer case reference 4A_234/2025 (preliminary reference), Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (Ipi.ch) consultation on AI inventorship, Geneva IP practice group leads interview synthesis.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH — confirmed referral, timing uncertain but H2 2026 plausible.
**For advisory:** IP practice groups in Geneva firms need AI inventorship policies before BGer ruling. Opportunity: policy drafting, client advisory memos, internal training.
**Angle different from C121:** C121 covered ICC 2025 rules Geneva; C123 covers patent/AI authorship — distinct.

### Finding 04: Sanctions-GDPR Collision — SECO/FDPIC Jurisdiction Gap
**Signal:** SECO's mandatory publication of sanctioned individuals under the Embargo Act conflicts with EU-aligned GDPR Art. 17 erasure requests when sanctioned persons (post-delisting or falsely listed) seek data deletion. Geneva banks and financial intermediaries receiving conflicting instructions from SECO vs. FDPIC.
**Evidence base:** FDPIC annual report 2025 (published March 2026), SECO sanctions list updates, European Data Protection Board Art. 17 guidance alignment question, Geneva Data Protection Association conference March 2026.
**Confidence:** HIGH — documented regulatory conflict, multiple institutions affected.
**For advisory:** Financial institutions, law firms, and trust companies needing framework for handling sanctioned-person erasure requests. Opportunity: advisory protocol, client training, regulatory representation.
**Angle different from C122:** C122 covered Swiss-EU data transfer compliance; C123 covers sanctions-GDPR collision — new angle.

### Finding 05: Geneva Civil Procedure — Permanent Remote Proceedings Protocol
**Signal:** Geneva's Cour de justice (Cantonal Supreme Court) issued permanent protocol (March 2026) allowing video testimony in civil cases without party consent. This creates enforceability complications in cross-border proceedings and Lugano Convention applicability questions.
**Evidence base:** Cour de justice Geneva press release March 2026, Geneva Bar Association (OGE) guidance, Federal Supreme Court e-justice roadmap 2026-2028.
**Confidence:** HIGH — protocol issued, effective immediately.
**For advisory:** International commercial litigants with Geneva-seated proceedings need guidance on remote testimony admissibility abroad. Opportunity: procedural advisory for international commercial arbitration vs. domestic litigation.
**Angle different from C121:** C121 covered Federal Tribunal e-justice reform; C123 covers specific Geneva remote proceedings protocol — related but new angle.

### Finding 06: UK-CH Commercial Disputes — Post-Lugano Uncertainty
**Signal:** Switzerland and UK concluded bilateral consultation on trade disputes (February 2026) without resolving Lugano Convention re-accession. UK is not party to Lugano; Geneva commercial courts now applying outdated bilateral treaty framework. ICC Geneva reporting surge in UK-CH commercial dispute referrals.
**Evidence base:** ICC Geneva arbitration statistics 2025 (published Q1 2026), Swiss-UK bilateral consultation summary, SECO cross-border commerce guidance update, Geneva Chamber of Commerce (CCIG) member alert March 2026.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH — bilateral consultation concluded without resolution, dispute surge confirmed.
**For advisory:** UK companies with Swiss subsidiaries, Swiss companies with UK counterparties, and Geneva law firms handling UK-CH contracts need interim advisory framework. Opportunity: contract clause drafting, dispute resolution clause advisory, litigation risk assessment.
**Angle different from C120:** C120 covered Lugano Convention UK re-accession; C123 covers post-consultation uncertainty and ICC surge — current state update.
