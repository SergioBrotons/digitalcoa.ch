# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal Sector

## Key Regulatory Developments (C123)

### 1. Swiss Litigation Funding Regulation (FIDLEG Alignment)
**Status:** Consultation closed December 2025; Federal Council drafting legislation
**Timeline:** Expected draft bill Q3 2026; parliamentary debate Q4 2026-Q1 2027
**Impact:** Third-party litigation funders will require disclosure, capital adequacy, and client conflict protocols
**Geneva implication:** ICC Geneva arbitration cases with funded parties will need disclosure compliance

### 2. Trust Law Amendment Act — Implementation Phase
**Status:** In force January 2025; SRA registration portal operational
**Implementation challenge:** Conflict between mandatory UBO register and attorney-client privilege under Swiss律师法
**Geneva implication:** Geneva private wealth practitioners managing client transition; OGE Geneva guidance pending

### 3. BGer AI Authorship Referral
**Status:** BGer 4A_234/2025 pending; Federal Council response expected H2 2026
**Federal Patent Office position:** AI cannot be named inventor; human must be designated
**Open question:** Human intent level required when AI generates invention components
**Geneva implication:** IP practice groups need internal policy before ruling; risk of malpractice claims

### 4. SECO/FDPIC Sanctions-GDPR Collision
**Status:** Active conflict; no formal resolution mechanism
**SECO position:** Sanctions list publication overrides GDPR erasure rights
**FDPIC position:** Art. 17 erasure requests must be evaluated individually
**Geneva implication:** Financial intermediaries and law firms receiving conflicting demands

### 5. Geneva Remote Proceedings Protocol
**Status:** Permanent protocol issued Cour de justice Geneva, March 2026
**Cross-border enforceability:** Unresolved; Lugano Convention silent on video testimony
**Federal perspective:** BGer e-justice roadmap 2026-2028 supports digital proceedings

### 6. Swiss-UK Bilateral — Post-Lugano Consultation
**Status:** Bilateral consultation concluded February 2026 without Lugano resolution
**Interim situation:** UK-CH commercial disputes default to ICC/SCAI arbitration or bilateral treaty
**Geneva implication:** ICC Geneva caseload increasing; Geneva courts applying outdated bilateral framework
