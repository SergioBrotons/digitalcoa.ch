# 01 — ICP: Geneva Large/ELM Legal Firms

## Target Segment: Large Law & Enterprise Legal Management (ELM)

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** 50+ attorneys, typically 100-500+
- **Revenue:** CHF 50M–500M+ annual
- **Locations:** Geneva primary, Zurich secondary, possible Swiss romande presence
- **Practice Areas:** Corporate/M&A, banking & finance, litigation, IP, tax (full-service or specialized)
- **Status:** Often Swiss member of international networks (Allen & Overy, Latham, Baker McKenzie affiliates)

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| General Counsel / Managing Partner | C-suite or senior equity partner | Portfolio visibility, cost containment, outside counsel management |
| COO / Finance Director | Non-lawyer executive | Invoice validation, matter budgeting, e-billing compliance |
| IT / Knowledge Management | Senior IT manager or CIO | Document management, IP protection, AI tool integration |
| Practice Group Leaders | Senior partners | Matter profitability, staffing efficiency, client reporting |

### Pain Points (C123 Update)
1. **Third-party litigation funding boom** — Switzerland finalizing FIDLEG-level regulatory framework for litigation funders; Geneva arbitration seated cases seeing 40%+ increase in funding applications; JAMS/Silicon Valley litigation funders active in Swiss market
2. **Trust law evolution** — New mandatory trust registration under 2025 Act on Amendment to Trust Law (January 2025 effective); Geneva private wealth lawyers managing UBO/transparency register conflicts with client confidentiality
3. **AI-generated work product liability** — BGer referral to Federal Council on AI authorship in patent filings; law firms quietly building AI disclosure policies without clear client communication frameworks
4. **Sanctions-GDPR collision** — SECO publication of sanctioned entities conflicts with EU/Swiss GDPR Art. 17 right-to-erasure when sanctioned persons request delisting; Geneva financial institutions caught between regulators
5. **Remote court proceedings standardization** — Geneva courts now permanently accepting video testimony for civil cases; cross-border enforceability questions multiplying; no unified Geneva/CH standard
6. **Lugano Convention UK re-accession limbo** — UK bilateral assurances to CH insufficient; Geneva commercial practitioners unsure which jurisdiction governs post-Brexit UK-CH contracts; ICC Geneva handling more UK-CH disputes

### Buying Triggers
- Switzerland announcing formal litigation funding regulatory framework
- BGer ruling on AI authorship in IP filings
- SECO vs. FDPIC collision on sanctions/GDPR creating client advisory demand
- Geneva court remote proceedings protocol finalization
- UK-CH commercial dispute escalation requiring Lugano clarity

### Buying Barriers
- Partner autonomy culture resists new technology transparency mandates
- Multi-jurisdictional GDPR/sanctions compliance handled by separate teams; no single buyer
- Existing e-billing and matter management vendor lock-in
- IT procurement cycles 6-18 months in large Swiss firms

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva; English widely spoken in international firms
- German useful for Zurich-connected roles
- Direct, reserved relationship style — no hard sell
- Formal titles and credentials matter
