# Competitive Analysis — Geneva Consulting Boutique AI Accountability
## C312 Phase 03 — Cycle 312
## ICP: Small consulting boutiques, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based

---

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

### What Exists

**Generic AI governance frameworks** — Large consulting firms (Big Four, international strategy firms) publish AI governance frameworks. These are firm-level, designed for large organizations, and address none of the specific accountability vectors for a 2–50 person Geneva boutique.

**Compliance software platforms** — Various AI governance and compliance platforms exist. All are designed for enterprise-scale deployment, require implementation cycles, and are priced for organizations with dedicated compliance functions. Not designed for managing directors who need answers in days, not months.

**Training and workshops** — Various providers offer AI governance training. Training addresses knowledge gaps, not evidence gaps. The consulting boutique MD does not need to learn about AI governance — they need to produce evidence of AI governance methodology for specific client and carrier documents.

**Policy document templates** — AI policy templates are available from various sources. A policy document does not satisfy carrier attestation questions about specific methodology, sub-consultant delegation, or proposal defensibility.

### What's Missing (The White Space)

**1. Client AI questionnaire pre-review service**
No competitor offers a service where a Geneva consulting expert reviews the client's AI governance questionnaire before the MD responds. The client questionnaire is competitive intelligence — it reveals what the client's procurement process actually requires. Pre-review allows the MD to respond accurately and strategically, not defensively.

**2. Sub-consultant AI delegation framework**
No competitor offers a delegation framework specifically for consulting boutiques that addresses the specific gap: MD authorized the engagement, not the AI tools. A delegation framework that documents authorization, monitoring, and accountability for sub-consultant AI use — designed for 2–50 person firms with no compliance department.

**3. Proposal methodology defensibility documentation**
No competitor offers a service that helps the consulting boutique document AI-assisted methodology for specific proposals in a way that is defensible to clients, carriers, and courts. This is distinct from policy documentation — it is evidence of specific methodology for specific deliverables.

**4. Retroactive methodology reconstruction for consulting engagements**
No competitor offers a methodology reconstruction service for consulting firms that have already produced AI-assisted deliverables without documentation. The retroactive reconstruction problem is distinct from forward documentation — it requires different tools, different evidence standards, and different outputs.

**5. Carrier form pre-signing review for consulting firms**
The pre-signing carrier form review is the highest-value intervention point. No competitor has built this as a discrete service for consulting boutiques. The carrier form is a design specification — it tells you exactly what evidence the carrier needs. Pre-signing review allows the MD to produce the evidence before signing, not after.

**6. Engagement letter AI clause architecture**
No competitor offers an engagement letter AI clause framework specifically designed for consulting boutiques — covering scope of AI use, disclosure obligations, client rights, and sub-consultant AI provisions. The engagement letter is a commercial document that predates AI and now needs an AI architecture.

## The Naming Opportunity

The market has not named the specific service the consulting boutique needs. The opportunity is to name it before competitors do:

- **Evidence architecture** — the discipline of producing, organizing, and maintaining evidence of AI governance methodology calibrated to carrier forms and client questionnaires
- **Methodology defensibility documentation** — the specific evidence of how AI was used in specific proposals and deliverables
- **Sub-consultant delegation framework** — the documentation system for AI use by third parties on client engagements
- **Carrier form pre-signing review** — the highest-value, lowest-competition intervention point

No competitor has used these terms in the Geneva market. Owning the naming is owning the category.

---

## Key Competitive Insight

The competitive landscape serves consulting firms as if they have compliance departments, implementation timelines, and enterprise-scale documentation needs. The Geneva consulting boutique needs something else: evidence, calibrated to specific documents, produced by the managing director in days, not months.

The market gap is not knowledge. It is evidence.
