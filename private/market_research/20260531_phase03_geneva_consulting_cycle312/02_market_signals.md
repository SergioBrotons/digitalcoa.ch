# Market Signals — Geneva Consulting Boutique AI Problems
## C312 Phase 03 — Cycle 312
## ICP: Small consulting boutiques, 2–50 employees, Geneva-based

---

## Active Problems, Complaints, and Failures

### 1. Client AI Governance Questionnaires Becoming Commercial Gates

The primary commercial trigger for Geneva consulting boutiques is not regulatory — it is procurement. Large corporate clients and institutional buyers are now including AI governance questions in their RFP and vendor qualification processes.

Signal pattern: Managing directors reporting that AI questionnaire responses are being scored, not just collected. Failure to respond adequately to AI governance questions means losing the mandate to a competitor who can respond.

Manifestations:
- "The RFP had a 12-point AI governance section. We had no idea what to write."
- "The client's procurement team scored us on AI maturity. We didn't know we were being scored."
- "The DDQ asked how we supervise AI use by sub-consultants. I had to admit I don't."
- "The client asked for our AI governance documentation. We don't have any."
- "We lost a mandate we were qualified for because we couldn't answer the AI questions."

### 2. Carrier Attestation Creating Personal Exposure

Consulting boutique carrier forms have escalated in specificity. The managing director is named personally. The questions now cover sub-consultant AI delegation, proposal methodology, and AI tool inventory — areas where documentation is universally absent.

Signal pattern: MDs reporting that signing the carrier form feels like signing something they cannot defend. Fear of misrepresentation is primary, not ignorance.

Manifestations:
- "The form asks about AI methodology for client deliverables. I can't describe ours."
- "I don't know what AI tools our sub-consultants use. The form asks me to certify compliance for them."
- "What does 'adequate' mean for AI supervision of a consulting engagement?"
- "If something goes wrong on a client project and I signed an AI attestation, what does that mean for my personal exposure?"
- "The renewal is in three weeks. I don't have the documentation to answer the questions honestly."

### 3. Proposal Methodology Indefensibility as Active Business Problem

The consulting proposal is the primary commercial product. AI-assisted proposals are now common. The methodology that produced the proposal cannot be described, traced, or defended — creating simultaneous commercial and legal exposure.

Signal pattern: MDs who used AI to build proposals now facing client questions they cannot answer. The problem is not that they used AI — it is that they cannot explain what they did.

Manifestations:
- "The client asked us to walk through our methodology. I couldn't do it for the AI portions."
- "We used AI to analyze their data and produce recommendations. They asked how we validated the AI output. We couldn't answer."
- "A competitor used our AI methodology question against us in a pitch — they could describe theirs."
- "If I can't describe the methodology, should I have said we use AI in the proposal at all?"
- "The proposal commits us to a methodology. If the methodology is indefensible, the proposal is a liability."

### 4. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Invisible Accountability Gap

Geneva consulting boutiques regularly engage sub-consultants for specialized expertise. These sub-consultants are using AI tools on client projects. The MD signed the carrier form certifying compliance — without any monitoring system for sub-consultant AI use.

Signal pattern: MDs who did not know sub-consultants were using AI until clients mentioned it. No delegation framework exists. No documentation pathway exists.

Manifestations:
- "A client mentioned they could see AI-generated content in a deliverable. The sub-consultant hadn't told me."
- "I authorized the sub-consultant's engagement. I didn't authorize their AI tools."
- "If the sub-consultant uses AI and something goes wrong, is it my problem or theirs?"
- "The sub-consultant has their own carrier insurance. Does that cover AI use on my client's project?"
- "I have no contract clause that covers AI use by third parties on my client's work."

### 5. Engagement Letter Pre-AI Architecture Creating Client Relationship Friction

Engagement letters for consulting mandates predate AI as a standard delivery tool. Clients are now asking about AI use. The MD has no AI clause framework, no standard response, and no calibrated disclosure approach.

Signal pattern: MDs improvising AI disclosure responses in real-time client conversations, creating inconsistency and potential misrepresentation.

Manifestations:
- "A client asked if we use AI on their project. I said yes. They asked how. I wasn't prepared."
- "I told one client we don't use AI on their work. Then we did. Now I have a misrepresentation issue."
- "If the client has an AI policy, do I need to comply with it as their consultant?"
- "The engagement letter has no AI clause. The client asked for our AI governance approach. We have none."
- "A client asked us to sign their AI governance vendor requirements. I didn't know what I was signing."

### 6. Retroactive Documentation Debt for Completed Engagements

Geneva consulting boutiques have 12–24 months of AI-assisted deliverables completed without methodology documentation. Clients are now asking questions. Carriers are asking questions. The documentation does not exist and cannot be reconstructed from memory.

Signal pattern: MDs realizing that past proposals, analyses, and deliverables were AI-assisted — and there is no documentation trail.

Manifestations:
- "Eighteen months of client work was partially AI-produced. I can't reconstruct what was AI and what wasn't."
- "The client is asking for evidence of human review. I know it happened, but I didn't document it."
- "A carrier audit question asks about AI methodology for a project completed last year. I can't answer it."
- "If a client disputes an AI-assisted recommendation, what evidence do I have that a human reviewed it?"
- "The associate who produced the work left the firm. Their AI use is undocumented."

---

## Key Market Signal: Acquisition Mode + Risk Management Simultaneously

The Geneva consulting boutique ICP is not choosing between winning new mandates and managing existing AI exposure. They are facing both simultaneously. The client AI questionnaire is the forcing function for new mandates. The carrier attestation is the forcing function for existing exposure. The retroactive documentation debt is the accumulated weight of every AI-assisted engagement without methodology documentation.

The market is not asking "do I have a problem?" The market is asking "how do I win the next mandate AND contain the exposure from the last twenty?"
