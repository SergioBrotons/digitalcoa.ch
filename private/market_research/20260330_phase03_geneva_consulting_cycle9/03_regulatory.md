## Regulatory Framework: Geneva Consulting AI Governance

### Active Regulations (Now)

#### 1. EU AI Act — GPAI (Operative August 2, 2025)
**Status:** ACTIVE SINCE AUGUST 2, 2025 (~7.5 months)

**What is GPAI:**
General-Purpose AI (GPAI) covers AI systems like large language models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot) and other foundation models used across multiple domains.

**Current Obligations (ACTIVE NOW):**
- **Transparency to users:** Disclose to users when they are interacting with an AI system
- **Disclose AI-generated content:** Identify AI-generated content in outputs to users
- **Technical documentation:** GPAI providers must maintain technical documentation

**High-Risk Obligations (ACTIVATING AUGUST 2, 2026 — 12 months away):**
- Technical documentation (detailed)
- Data governance measures
- Logging and monitoring
- Human oversight measures
- Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity measures
- Conformity assessments (for Annex III high-risk AI systems)

**Who is subject:**
Swiss consulting firms with EU market nexus:
- EU-based clients
- AI outputs used in EU
- AI outputs affecting EU persons

**Violations:**
Up to EUR 35 million or 7% of global annual turnover (whichever is higher) for high-risk violations.

**Source:** EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689); CMS Law, White & Case, Lenz & Staehelin confirm extraterritorial reach for Swiss firms with EU nexus.

#### 2. nFADP Art. 5(c) — ACTIVE NOW
**Status:** In force since operative date (various phases through 2023-2024)

**Obligations:**
- AI-based data processing must be transparent (purpose, functionality, data sources)
- Users have right to know if they are interacting with AI
- Purpose limitation: data processed only for stated purposes
- Data minimisation: only necessary data processed

**FDPIC Enforcement:**
4 confirmed AI investigations 2025-2026:
- Transport company (February 2026) — not consulting
- Joint statement AI-generated images (February 2026) — cross-DPA
- X/Twitter AI training (March 2025)
- Coop AIassan (October 2025)

**No FDPIC enforcement targeting consulting firms yet — timing signal for advisory window.**

### Emerging Regulations

#### 3. Federal Council Zero Draft AI Action Plan — January 2026
**Status:** Zero Draft presented January 2026; public consultation ongoing

**Key elements:**
- "AI Action Plan for Switzerland" co-created with business, science, civil society
- First Draft + progress on preliminary actions: May 2026
- Federal Council explicitly pursuing sector-specific, technology-neutral approach
- NO cross-sector AI law (unlike EU AI Act)
- Adapts existing legal frameworks (FADP, ISA, Code of Obligations, Product Safety Act)
- End-2026: implementation plan for non-binding measures (industry solutions, voluntary commitments)

**Implication for Geneva consulting firms:**
No domestic Swiss AI law relief before end-2026 at earliest. GPAI extraterritorial + nFADP = binding framework until Federal Council legislation passes.

**Source:** Pestalozzi Law, Lenz & Staehelin, bk.admin.ch, swissinfo.ch, digitalswitzerland.com

---

#### 4. Council of Europe AI Convention — Implementation in Progress
**Status:** Switzerland signed March 2025; ratification + implementation in progress

**Timeline:**
- Ratification process ongoing
- Consultation draft legislation: end-2026
- Focus areas: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight

**Scope:**
- Convention applies directly to public sector
- Switzerland to decide how to extend to private entities via national regulation
- Risk-based methodology, fundamental rights protection

**Implication:**
End-2026 is the next concrete Swiss domestic regulatory signal after Federal Council Zero Draft. This is the next forcing function for Geneva consulting firms after August 2, 2026 GPAI high-risk activation.

---

#### 5. Federal Council Sector-Specific Approach
**Status:** Confirmed and active

**What it means:**
- NO dedicated cross-sector AI law
- Existing laws (FADP, ISA, Product Safety Act, Code of Obligations, Civil Code) apply to AI
- Sector-specific adjustments where needed
- Federal Council prefers non-binding measures (industry solutions, voluntary commitments)

**Implication for small consulting firms:**
Existing obligations under FADP, ISA, and Code of Obligations apply NOW. No new Swiss law will arrive before end-2026 at earliest to simplify or reduce these obligations. GPAI remains the primary binding external framework for firms with EU nexus.
