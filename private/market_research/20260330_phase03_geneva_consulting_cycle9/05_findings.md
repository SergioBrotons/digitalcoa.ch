## Key Findings: Phase 03 Geneva Consulting (Small Firms) — Cycle 9

### Finding 1: GPAI Operative 7.5 Months — Small Firms Largely Unaware
EU AI Act GPAI has been active since August 2, 2025 (~7.5 months). Transparency obligations already apply to Geneva consulting firms with EU nexus. High-risk GPAI obligations activate August 2, 2026. Most small Geneva consulting firms are unaware they are already subject to GPAI. This is the primary market opportunity in this vertical.

**Confidence:** High — multiple sources confirm GPAI operative date and extraterritorial reach for Swiss firms with EU nexus (CMS Law, White & Case, Lenz & Staehelin, Pestalozzi Law).

---

### Finding 2: Federal Council "Lean Approach" Confirmed — No Domestic Relief Before End-2026
Federal Council explicitly pursuing sector-specific, technology-neutral approach. NO cross-sector AI law. Adapts existing frameworks (FADP, ISA, Code of Obligations). CoE AI Convention consultation draft expected end-2026. Federal Council non-binding measures (industry solutions, voluntary commitments) expected end-2026. Federal Council AI Action Plan Zero Draft presented January 2026; First Draft May 2026.

**Implication:** GPAI extraterritorial is the primary binding framework for Geneva consulting firms through end-2026 at minimum. No domestic Swiss law will provide relief.

**Confidence:** High — Federal Council confirmed this approach via multiple legal sources.

---

### Finding 3: 9th Consecutive Competitive Absence Confirmed
No boutique AI governance competitor specifically serving small Geneva consulting firms (1–20 employees) confirmed. Swiss Cyber Institute, Govern AI, Swiss Infosec AG, AI Legal & Strategy AG, Modulos AG, GAIGI — all enterprise/institutional focused. Govern AI (govern-ai.ch) is the closest governance-focused indirect competitor but is Zurich-based enterprise advisory.

**Confidence:** High — consistent across 9 research cycles.

---

### Finding 4: 40% Geneva SMEs Lack AI Skills — Structural Awareness Gap
PwC 2024 Geneva research: 40% of SMEs lack in-house AI skills. This creates a structural gap between AI adoption (high) and governance awareness (low) among small consulting firms. The gap is widened by Federal Council's sector-specific approach — no domestic SMB AI guidance is coming before end-2026 to close it.

**Confidence:** High — PwC Geneva research.

---

### Finding 5: Federal Council AI Action Plan Creates May 2026 External Pressure Event
Federal Council AI Action Plan Zero Draft presented January 2026; public consultation ongoing. First Draft + progress on preliminary actions expected May 2026. This creates an external pressure event for Swiss firms in Q2 2026, approximately 2 months away.

**Implication:** May 2026 First Draft is a natural forcing function for client outreach. Geneva consulting firms will become more receptive to AI governance advisory after May 2026.

**Confidence:** Medium — multiple sources confirm January 2026 Zero Draft and May 2026 timeline; primary doc not fetched due to Gemini quota.

---

### Finding 6: Geneva Consulting ICP — EU Nexus + AI Tool Use + No Governance Framework
Small Geneva consulting firm (1–20 employees), using AI tools in client work (research, drafting, analysis, forecasting), EU market nexus (EU clients or EU-directed outputs), no formal AI governance framework, not aware of GPAI obligations active since August 2025.

**Confidence:** High — consistent ICP across multiple cycles, now sharpened with GPAI timing.

---

### Finding 7: Advisory Window ~9 Months to August 2, 2026 High-Risk GPAI Activation
GPAI high-risk obligations (conformity assessment, technical documentation, human oversight, data governance) activate August 2, 2026. This is the primary near-term forcing function for Geneva consulting firms with high-risk AI use. For standard-risk GPAI users, transparency obligations are already active.

**Confidence:** High — EU AI Act timeline confirmed.
