## Why This Framework Exists

You are already using AI in your consulting practice.

The question is not whether you use AI. The question is whether you understand what you are now obligated to disclose, document, and justify — and whether you are exposed to liability for the outputs AI produces on your behalf.

The EU AI Act has been operative since August 2, 2025. Its General-Purpose AI (GPAI) provisions already require disclosure when you use AI systems in client work. High-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026. Switzerland's Federal Council is not passing a domestic AI law to relieve this pressure — it is pursuing a sector-specific approach that adapts existing frameworks (nFADP, ISA, Code of Obligations).

Small Geneva consulting firms are in the exposure gap: high AI adoption, low regulatory awareness, no dedicated domestic guidance, and a August 2, 2026 deadline approaching.

This framework gives you a practical, decision-oriented path through that gap.

### Section 2: Decision 1 — Do You Have EU AI Act GPAI Exposure?

**GPAI applies to your firm if:**
- You provide consulting services to clients in the EU, OR
- Your AI system outputs are used by individuals or entities in the EU, OR
- Your AI outputs affect persons in the EU in any way

**If YES:** GPAI obligations are active NOW (August 2, 2025 operative date). Your immediate obligation: disclose to users (clients) that you are using an AI system and identify the AI system by name/version. Document this disclosure.

**If NO:** You are subject to nFADP Art. 5(c) obligations (active NOW) but not GPAI. Proceed to Section 3.

**GPAI transparency checklist:**
- [ ] Identify all AI systems used in client-facing work
- [ ] Draft AI disclosure statement for client communications
- [ ] Document which AI systems process EU nexus client data
- [ ] Flag any AI outputs that constitute "high-risk" AI systems under Annex III

---

### Section 3: Decision 2 — Are You Processing Personal Data via AI?

**nFADP Art. 5(c) applies if:**
- You use AI to process personal data (client data, third-party data, employee data)
- AI systems analyze, profile, predict, or generate outputs based on personal data

**If YES:** You must ensure:
- Transparency: clients must know an AI system is processing their data
- Purpose limitation: data used only for stated purposes
- Data minimisation: only necessary data is processed
- Users have right to know if they are interacting with AI (Art. 23 FADP)

**If NO:** You have lighter nFADP exposure. Proceed to Section 4.

---

### Section 4: Decision 3 — What Is Your AI Risk Classification?

**Classify your AI systems by risk level:**

**HIGH RISK (GPAI Annex III domains — August 2, 2026 obligations):**
- AI used in employment decisions (hiring, performance, termination)
- AI used in credit or financial advisory to clients
- AI used in education or vocational training of clients
- AI used in healthcare advisory or medical decision support
- AI used in legal advisory that constitutes legal services

**If any of the above apply:** High-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026. Begin technical documentation, data governance, human oversight, and conformity assessment preparation now.

**STANDARD RISK (all other consulting AI use):**
- Research AI, drafting AI, analysis AI, forecasting AI, communication AI
- Subject to GPAI transparency obligations (ACTIVE NOW)
- Not subject to high-risk conformity assessment

**NO RISK (minimal AI use):**
- AI used only internally, no client data, no EU nexus
- Minimal regulatory exposure but should document AI use
