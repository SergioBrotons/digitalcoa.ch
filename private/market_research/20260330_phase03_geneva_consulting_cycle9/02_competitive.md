## Competitive Assessment

### Confirmed Absence
**No boutique AI governance competitor specifically serving small Geneva consulting firms (1–20 employees) confirmed across 9 consecutive research cycles.**

### Structural Reasons for Absence
1. Small consulting firms perceived as too small for dedicated AI governance advisory
2. Perceived lack of regulatory urgency (until recently — GPAI now active)
3. No domestic cross-sector AI law creates ambiguity about what "Swiss AI governance" means for SMB
4. GPAI extraterritorial nature not yet widely understood by small firms
5. Federal Council "lean approach" reinforces perception that Swiss SMBs have time

### What This Means for DigitalCoa.ch
- **First-mover window intact** in Geneva consulting SMB AI governance advisory
- Geneva-based + consulting vertical specialization = unique positioning
- Govern AI is closest indirect competitor in governance space but enterprise-focused
- GAIGI is Geneva-based but institutional/government-focused
- Small consulting firms (1–20 employees) represent a genuinely unserved niche

### Competitive Differentiation Needed
DigitalCoa.ch positioning for Geneva consulting SMB:
- Geneva-rooted presence (vs. Zurich competitors)
- Small firm-first pricing/approach (vs. Big 4/enterprise boutiques)
- Understanding of GPAI obligations already active for small firms (vs. competitors focused on large enterprise compliance)
- Consulting-specific framing (vs. generic AI governance frameworks)
