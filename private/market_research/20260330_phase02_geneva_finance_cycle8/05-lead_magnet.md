### Section 3: The AMLA Advisor Obligation — Are You In Scope?

**New in 2026:** The revised AMLA extends obligations to advisors — including wealth managers providing investment advice, estate planning, or family office services.

Triggering activities that bring advisors into AMLA scope:
- Assisting with real estate transactions
- Establishing or restructuring non-operational legal entities
- Providing domicile or registered office services

If you are an advisor under the revised AMLA definition, you must:
- [ ] Join a FINMA-recognized Self-Regulatory Organization (SRO)
- [ ] Implement enhanced KYC procedures for beneficial owners
- [ ] Report discrepancies between your KYC records and the new Transparency Register within 30 days
- [ ] Verify identities, nature and extent of control, maintain evidence
- [ ] Report suspicious activities to MROS (Money Laundering Reporting Office Switzerland)

**Action:** Confirm whether your current SRO membership covers the expanded 2026 advisor obligations. If you have no SRO membership, this is your immediate priority. The FATF mutual evaluation 2026-2027 means SROs will be scrutinized for compliance coverage.

### Section 5: The FINMA AI Guidance Baseline (Guidance 08/2024 — December 2024)

FINMA's December 2024 guidance establishes four pillars for AI governance in supervised entities:

1. **Governance:** Board-level accountability for AI systems; documented AI inventory
2. **Risk Management:** nFADP DPIA for all AI processing personal data; ongoing monitoring
3. **Transparency:** Client disclosure of AI-assisted decisions; explainability where feasible
4. **Non-Discrimination:** Testing for algorithmic bias; fairness audit procedures

**Reality check:** If your firm has not addressed these four pillars in any structured way, you are behind FINMA's baseline expectation. FINMA Circular 2026/1 (nature-related financial risks, January 2026) signals FINMA is expanding its risk governance expectations — AI governance is part of this broader trend.
