## State of Phase 02 Geneva Finance — Cycle 8

### Core ICP
Boutique Geneva wealth managers (2–20 employees, CHF 100M–2B AUM) face a 3-layer compliance stack in 2026: Swiss domestic (nFADP + FINMA AI Guidance 08/2024 + AMLA/LETA H2 2026 + CARF/CRS 2.0 live), Swiss international (CRS 2.0 + FATF mutual evaluation 2026-2027), and EU extraterritorial (EU AI Act August 2, 2026 if EU nexus). FATF mutual evaluation adds external pressure dimension not previously highlighted.

### Key Cycle 8 Finding
**FATF Mutual Evaluation 2026-2027 + AMLA/LETA H2 2026:** The most significant new finding in Cycle 8 is the explicit connection between Switzerland's AMLA overhaul and the FATF mutual evaluation scheduled for 2026-2027. Geneva WM firms now face a dual pressure: domestic regulatory implementation AND external international assessment of compliance readiness. The FATF evaluation adds urgency and external accountability to what might otherwise be seen as an abstract regulatory deadline. Wealth managers with advisory component (investment advice, estate planning, family office services) are explicitly in expanded AMLA scope and must join a FINMA-recognized SRO.
