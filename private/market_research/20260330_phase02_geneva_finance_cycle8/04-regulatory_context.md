# Phase 02 Regulatory Context — Geneva Finance / Boutique WM
## Cycle 8 (2026-03-30)

## Primary Regulatory Framework

### 1. CARF / CRS 2.0
- **Status:** Operative since January 1, 2026 (3 months live as of late March 2026)
- **Nature:** Automatic exchange of financial account information; OECD standard; CRS 2.0 = new data fields (balances, UUIDs), pre-population, expanding DAC2/CARF automated exchanges
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Immediate obligation; no transitional period; 2025 data reporting due 2026

### 2. AMLA Revision (Switzerland)
- **Status:** Parliament adopted September 2025; consultation on implementing ordinances (LETO, AMLO) concluded January 30, 2026
- **Implementation:** Second half of 2026 (H2 2026 = July-December 2026; ~3-6 months from late March 2026)
- **Referendum period:** 3 months; entry into force after expiry
- **NEW — FATF Mutual Evaluation 2026-2027:** Switzerland's AMLA overhaul directly driven by FATF's upcoming mutual evaluation; FATF mutual evaluation scheduled for 2026-2027 creates external enforcement pressure alongside domestic regulatory timeline
- **Key obligations for WM:**
  - Extended scope for "advisors" (lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs) — must join FINMA-recognized SRO
  - Wealth managers with advisory component (investment advice, estate planning, family office services) may face dual SRO pressure
  - Advisors must: perform due diligence, identify beneficial owners, document purpose of corporate structures, report suspicious activities to MROS
  - Stronger beneficial ownership verification rules
  - 30-day reporting of discrepancies between KYC records and Transparency Register
  - Crypto/VASP AML obligations further entrenched
  - Specific triggering activities: real estate transactions, establishing/restructuring non-operational legal entities, domicile/registered office services
- **Enforcement:** FINMA supervisory model via SRO oversight; penalties up to CHF 500,000 for intentional violations
- **Transitional periods:** "Measured in months, not years" — no specifics given

### 3. LETA (Transparency Register)
- **Status:** H2 2026 same implementation wave as AMLA
- **Nature:** Central non-public beneficial ownership register; Federal Office of Justice administration
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Must identify, verify, file UBO information; 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register; aligns with FATF standards ahead of mutual evaluation

### 4. Swissdamed (Healthcare Data)
- **Date:** July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Cross-sector data governance precedent; if WM firm processes any health-related data (family office, estate planning context), nFADP obligations apply

### 5. EU AI Act
- **Date:** August 2, 2026 (~15 months)
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Extraterritorial — if WM firm places AI on EU market, operates EU-intended output, or acts as EU importer/distributor; GPAI obligations already operative August 2, 2025

### 6. FINMA AI Guidance 08/2024
- **Date:** December 2024 (FINMA Guidance 08/2024; non-legal, sector-specific measures)
- **Nature:** Governance, risk management, transparency, non-discrimination for supervised entities' AI applications; four pillars: governance (board accountability, AI inventory), risk management (nFADP DPIA, ongoing monitoring), transparency (client disclosure, explainability), non-discrimination (bias testing, fairness audit)
- **Status:** ~15 months old; no updated guidance published; gap is structural
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Baseline expectation; first Swiss-specific AI governance framework for supervised entities

### 7. FINMA Circular 2026/1
- **Date:** January 1, 2026
- **Nature:** Nature-related financial risks; integrates climate and environmental risks into governance and risk management processes of banks and insurance companies
- **Boutique WM relevance:** Indirect — signals FINMA's expanding risk governance expectations; Geneva WM firms with environmental/ESG positioning may face increased scrutiny
