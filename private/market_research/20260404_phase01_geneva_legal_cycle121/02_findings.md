## Finding 1: Swiss Federal Tribunal E-Justice Reform — Electronic Filing and Digital Procedure Expansion

### Signal
The Swiss Federal Tribunal (BGer) and Geneva's cantonal courts have accelerated electronic filing (e-justice) rollout under the 2024-2027 judicial digitization roadmap. By end 2026, mandatory electronic filing for commercial cases is expected across Geneva's courts.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Law firms and corporate legal departments need to restructure filing workflows, data security protocols, and e-signature compliance for court submissions
- **Distinct angle:** Not procedural general (C99/C100) — this is specifically the electronic transformation of litigation practice
- **Revenue model:** Workflow compliance consulting, e-filing system implementation support, digital evidence management
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's concentration of international commercial litigation makes it an early adoption hub

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "E-Justice 2026: How Geneva's Electronic Filing Requirement Affects Your Litigation Strategy"
- Target ICP: Corporate litigants, international law firms with Geneva offices, in-house counsel

## Finding 2: Geneva Arbitration Reform — ICC 2025 Procedural Amendments and Emergency Arbitrator Expansion

### Signal
The ICC revised its Arbitration Rules effective 2025, introducing enhanced emergency arbitrator provisions, expedited procedure for smaller disputes, and clearer digital hearing protocols. Geneva-based institutions (CAG, WIPO Arbitration Center) have updated their own rules in response.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Companies with ICC or Geneva-seated arbitration clauses need to understand revised procedural implications, particularly emergency arbitrator availability and time limits
- **Distinct angle:** Not arbitration seat comparison (C118) or Lugano enforcement (C120) — this is specific ICC/CAG rule changes and their strategic impact
- **Revenue model:** Clause drafting and review, procedural strategy counseling, representation in emergency arbitrator proceedings
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva is the primary ICC arbitration seat for French-speaking disputes; WIPO Arbitration Center handles IP disputes globally

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "ICC 2025 Rules: What Changed for Geneva-Seated Arbitrations"
- Target ICP: International trade counsel, IP litigators, commodity traders, construction firms with arbitration clauses

## Finding 3: Swiss Sanctions Enforcement — Third-Country Evasion Vectors and Commodity Trade Finance Exposure

### Signal
SECO has intensified scrutiny on third-country sanctions evasion involving Swiss financial intermediaries and commodity trading houses. New enforcement guidance issued Q1 2026 addresses ship-to-ship transfers, phantom shipment routing, and dual-use goods through third countries.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Commodity trading firms, shipping companies, and Swiss banks need enhanced due diligence frameworks for third-country transactions with Russia/believed-Russia nexus
- **Distinct angle:** Not SECO frozen asset restructuring (C120) — this is proactive compliance for ongoing commercial activity using third-country routes
- **Revenue model:** Compliance due diligence audits, sanctions policy drafting, transaction screening reviews
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's commodity trading houses (Trafigura, Vitol, Mercuria group companies) and private banking sector are primary exposure points

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "SECO Tightens Third-Country Sanctions Scrutiny: Implications for Swiss Commodity Finance"
- Target ICP: Commodity trading GCs, private banking compliance officers, Swiss export finance teams

## Finding 4: SchKG Debt Enforcement Reform — Modernization of Swiss Debt Collection and Bankruptcy

### Signal
Switzerland's Federal Council has advanced legislative amendments to the Debt Enforcement and Bankruptcy Act (SchKG), with reforms targeting the debt collection certificate process for SMEs, digital notification requirements, and extended moratorium procedures for companies in financial distress.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** SMEs and their creditors need guidance on reformed debt collection procedures, accelerated bankruptcy timelines, and new moratorium rights
- **Distinct angle:** Not previously covered in any Geneva legal cycle — debt enforcement reform is distinct from civil procedure general
- **Revenue model:** Debtor-side restructuring counseling, creditor enforcement strategy, contract risk allocation for B2B transactions
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's concentration of SMEs and trading intermediaries creates high-volume debt enforcement demand

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "SchKG Reform 2026: What Swiss SMEs Must Know About New Debt Enforcement Rules"
- Target ICP: SME owners, finance directors, B2B creditors, restructuring advisors

## Finding 5: Switzerland AEOI Expansion — CRS Reforms and New Automatic Exchange Partner States

### Signal
Switzerland continues expanding its Automatic Exchange of Information (AEOI) network, with new partner states added in 2025-2026 and CRS due diligence standards strengthened. The Federal Council also introduced domestic AEOI pilot for certain domestic accounts.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Private banking clients, family offices, and multinationals with Swiss structures need updated AEOI compliance frameworks as the network expands
- **Distinct angle:** Not FATF AML (C118/C119) — AEOI is specifically the tax information exchange regime, distinct from anti-money laundering
- **Revenue model:** AEOI compliance reviews, trust/foundation disclosure analysis, voluntary disclosure program counseling
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva private banking sector is the primary affected industry; UHNWI clients with complex structures need specific guidance

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "AEOI Network Expands: Compliance Implications for Swiss Private Wealth Structures"
- Target ICP: Private banking relationship managers, family office principals, international tax counsel

## Finding 6: Post-COVID Commercial Lease Disputes — Geneva Litigation Wave and Force Majeure Clause Interpretation

### Signal
Geneva courts are processing a sustained wave of commercial lease disputes arising from COVID-19 rent non-payment and rent reduction claims. The Federal Supreme Court's 2024 ruling on commercial rent COVID compensation has created a new body of case law now being applied at Geneva cantonal level.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Commercial landlords, tenants, and property managers need litigation strategy and settlement frameworks for COVID-era lease disputes
- **Distinct angle:** Not previously covered in Geneva legal cycles — this is a distinct dispute category driven by a specific historical event with ongoing case law development
- **Revenue model:** Dispute resolution strategy, lease renegotiation facilitation, litigation representation
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's dense commercial rental market (Rue du Rhône, La Praille, Les Acacias) has high concentration of affected leases

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Geneva Commercial Lease Disputes: COVID-Era Claims and the Emerging Case Law Framework"
- Target ICP: Commercial landlords, retail tenants, property managers, real estate GCs
