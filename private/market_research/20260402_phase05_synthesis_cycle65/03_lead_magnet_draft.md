## EN

# The Geneva AI Governance Portfolio
### Seven Regulatory Layers. Six Sectors. One Framework.

**April 2, 2026 — Geneva, Switzerland**

Geneva's professional firms — law firms, wealth managers, consulting practices, medical offices, real estate agencies — are navigating a regulatory environment that has no unified framework, no single authority, and no clear roadmap.

**Seven distinct regulatory layers** apply simultaneously:
1. US Section 301 — ~12.5 days to written comments deadline
2. EU AI Act GPAI — already in force since August 2025
3. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP) — DPIA required for AI systems
4. Federal AI Ordinance — 16+ months; enforcement gap widest in real estate
5. ACCTS — new consultation opened; July 8 deadline
6. Swiss-EU Bilateral Stabilisation — data sovereignty advantage confirmed
7. CoE AI Convention — implementation ongoing

**Geneva firms that develop a coherent AI governance framework now will:**
- Differentiate on trust and compliance credibility
- Reduce client multi-framework navigation burden
- Position for G7 regulatory intelligence proximity advantage
- Capture AI governance advisory as a service product

**The Geneva AI Governance Portfolio briefing** covers all seven layers across all six sectors, with Geneva-specific guidance, actionable timelines, and a cross-sectoral compliance checklist.

**2 avril 2026 — Genève, Suisse**

3. LPDo-2 — AIPD requise pour les systèmes d'IA
4. Ordonnance IA fédérale — 16+ mois; lacune la pluslarge dans l'immobilier
5. ACCTS — nouvelle consultation; délai 8 juillet
6. Stabilisation bilatérale Suisse-UE — avantage souveraineté des données confirmé
7. Convention IA du CdE — mise en œuvre en cours
