## Cycle 17 | Updated: 2026-03-30

## Active Regulatory Framework

### nFADP (Swiss Data Protection Act — Revised)
- **Status:** In force
- **Art. 5(c):** Active — AI-based data processing requires explicit consent or other lawful basis
- **Art. 23:** Penalty provisions — criminal prosecution route (no direct FDPIC fines for AI violations)
- **DPIA:** Required for high-risk AI processing of personal data

### EU AI Act (Extraterritorial)
- **GPAI (General Purpose AI):** Operative since Aug 2, 2025 — transparency obligations ACTIVE NOW
- **High-risk obligations:** Activate Aug 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)
- **Swiss nexus triggers:** Placing AI on EU market, operating EU-intended output, acting as EU importer/distributor
- **Implication:** Geneva law firms with EU client nexus face dual nFADP + EU AI Act compliance

### Federal Council AI Strategy
- **Lean approach:** Explicitly confirmed Feb 12, 2025
- **Two timelines:** (1) Formal legislative — end-2026; (2) Multi-stakeholder First Draft — May 2026
- **No cross-sector AI law before formal process:** Sector-specific only
- **Next pressure event:** Federal Council First Draft May 2026 (~2 months)

### FDPIC Enforcement
- **Current portfolio:** AI investigations in transport, retail, social media sectors
- **Law firms:** NOT yet targeted — timing signal for first-mover advantage
- **Penalty architecture:** Criminal prosecution route (Art. 23 nFADP + Swiss Criminal Code); no direct fines

### ANBLES / Cantonal Bars
- **ANBLES:** No new AI guidance published as of March 2026
- **Geneva bar:** No specific AI guidance available (OFL silent)
- **Structural absence:** Creates advisory white space for DigitalCoa.ch
