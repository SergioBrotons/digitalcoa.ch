# 06 — Executive Summary: DigitalCoa.ch (Executive Advisory Platform)

## Market Opportunity

DigitalCoa.ch occupies a specific, defensible position: executive-grade trusted advisor for Geneva's regulatory complexity. The executive audience (C-suite, GC, board members, family office principals) needs more than legal advice — they need strategic framing of regulatory risk at board level. DigitalCoa.ch can provide this: synthesis, judgment, and trusted counsel.

The opportunity: executives with Geneva operations (financial institutions, commodity trading, IO/NGOs, tech startups) face regulatory convergence (AI governance, DORA-parity, CRS 2.0, FinIA, nature-related risk) simultaneously. They need a trusted advisor who can synthesize across these converging obligations — not a lawyer for each silo, not a Big Four team, but a trusted Geneva advisor who understands the whole picture.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Executive Briefing is the Primary Conversion Mechanism
Busy executives prefer 30-60 minute briefings over formal proposals, RFPs, or long sales processes. The executive briefing — name-to-name, executive-to-executive — is the primary conversion mechanism for executive advisory services. DigitalCoa.ch's primary CTA should be "Request an Executive Briefing" — low friction, trust-forming, naturally selective.

### Finding 2: AI Governance is the Signature Executive Advisory Vertical
Board-level AI governance (AI liability, compliance frameworks, strategic positioning) is the 2026 executive advisory opportunity. No Geneva boutique is positioned as executive-grade AI governance advisor for C-suites and boards. DigitalCoa.ch can own this by: (1) publishing executive-level AI governance thought leadership (via Brotons.net); (2) offering executive briefings specifically on AI governance; (3) developing board-level AI governance frameworks as an advisory service.

### Finding 3: Brotons.net ↔ DigitalCoa.ch Pathway is the Core Lead Generation Strategy
Brotons.net builds trust and authority with Geneva professional audience; DigitalCoa.ch converts trust into advisory conversations. The pathway: (1) Brotons.net article on Geneva regulatory convergence → (2) reader subscribes to newsletter → (3) newsletter reader with commercial intent sees DigitalCoa.ch CTA → (4) requests executive briefing → (5) advisory relationship forms.

### Finding 4: Family Enterprise Succession Advisory is a Natural Geneva Vertical
Geneva's UHNW family office concentration + succession wave 2026-2030 + regulatory exposure = natural advisory vertical for DigitalCoa.ch. Family offices need: governance advisory, succession structure, regulatory exposure assessment, trusted executor relationships. This is a relationship-driven service — and DigitalCoa.ch can be the trusted Geneva advisor for this segment.

### Finding 5: Executive Lead Quality Trumps Volume
One qualified C-suite lead (CFO of Geneva asset manager, GC of Geneva bank, board member of family enterprise) is worth more than 100 generic marketing leads. DigitalCoa.ch should optimize for lead quality, not lead volume: clear ICP targeting, selective CTA, trust-building before the first call.

## Strategic Implications for DigitalCoa.ch

1. **Lead with executive briefing CTA** — "Request an Executive Briefing" as primary conversion mechanism on all pages
2. **Position AI governance as signature vertical** — board-level AI governance advisory is the 2026 opportunity
3. **Build Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch pathway** — thought leadership is top-of-funnel; advisory is conversion
4. **Target executive ICP selectively** — C-suite, GC, board members, family office principals in Geneva
5. **Optimize for lead quality** — trust signals, credentials, clear positioning before first call
6. **Maintain executive-grade brand** — no commercial clutter, no generic CTAs, no AI clichés

## Risks and Open Questions

- Executive advisory services require personal credibility and track record — brand alone insufficient
- Big Four dominate executive advisory spend — differentiation requires clear, defensible positioning
- Lead quality vs. volume trade-off — owner must commit to selectivity
- Primary research (lead data, conversion rates) requires owner approval
