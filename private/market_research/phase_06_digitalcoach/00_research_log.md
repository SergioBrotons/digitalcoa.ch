## NET-NEW Findings This Session (C145 Phase 06)

### Finding 01: Executive Briefing is Primary Conversion Mechanism — "Request an Executive Briefing"
Busy C-suite executives, GCs, and board members prefer 30-60 minute direct conversations over formal proposals, RFPs, or long sales cycles. Executive briefing (name-to-name, executive-to-executive, no pitch, no slides) is the primary conversion mechanism for executive advisory services. DigitalCoa.ch CTA should be "Request an Executive Briefing" — lowest friction, highest trust, naturally selective for qualified leads. No lengthy intake forms; name + company + brief context only.

*Different from Phase 05 (Brotons.net):* Phase 05 covered newsletter as audience development mechanism (Brotons.net primary CTA). Phase 06 covers executive briefing as conversion mechanism (DigitalCoa.ch primary CTA). Different platforms, different funnels.

### Finding 02: AI Governance is the Signature Executive Advisory Vertical for DigitalCoa.ch
Board-level AI governance (AI civil liability Art. 41 OR, FINMA AI guidance, compliance defense, board-level AI risk oversight) is the 2026 executive advisory opportunity. No Geneva boutique positioned as executive-grade AI governance advisor for C-suites and boards. DigitalCoa.ch can own this: (1) executive briefings on AI governance; (2) board-level AI governance frameworks; (3) AI governance advisory engagements for Geneva financial institutions, family offices, and tech companies. This is distinct from Brotons.net thought leadership (Phase 05 Finding 1) — DigitalCoa.ch converts thought leadership audience into advisory engagements.

*Different from Phase 05 Finding 1:* Phase 05 Finding 1 was about Brotons.net as THE Geneva AI governance thought leadership platform. This Phase 06 finding is about DigitalCoa.ch converting that thought leadership positioning into executive advisory engagements.

### Finding 03: Brotons.net ↔ DigitalCoa.ch Pathway is Core Lead Generation — Warm Transfer, Not Cold Call
Brotons.net builds trust and authority with Geneva professional audience (thought leadership, regulatory interpretation, AI governance analysis). DigitalCoa.ch converts trust into advisory conversations (executive briefings, advisory engagements, lead generation). The pathway: (1) Brotons.net article on regulatory convergence → (2) reader subscribes to newsletter → (3) newsletter CTA to DigitalCoa.ch executive briefing → (4) executive requests briefing → (5) advisory relationship forms. This is warm transfer, not cold call. The pathway requires: clear cross-links, appropriate CTAs, content that signals when to move from Brotons.net to DigitalCoa.ch.

*Different from Phase 05 Finding 6:* Phase 05 Finding 6 identified the cross-pollination strategy as a concept. Phase 06 sharpens to specific pathway mechanics: content → newsletter → CTA → briefing → engagement.

### Finding 04: Family Enterprise Succession Advisory is Natural Geneva Vertical for DigitalCoa.ch
Geneva's UHNW family office concentration (400+ SFOs/MFOs), succession wave 2026-2030, regulatory exposure (FinIA, CRS 2.0, nature-related risk), and governance needs (foundation restructuring, Art. 914/916 ZGB) create a natural advisory vertical. Family offices and family enterprises need: governance advisory, succession structure, regulatory exposure assessment, trusted advisor relationships. This is relationship-driven — and DigitalCoa.ch can be the trusted Geneva advisor for this segment if positioned correctly.

*Different from Phase 02 (Geneva Finance) Finding 4:* Phase 02 covered nature-related risk disclosure as a service line for Geneva financial institutions. Phase 06 specifically addresses family office succession advisory as a distinct vertical: UHNW governance, foundation restructuring, succession execution, and trusted advisor relationship.

### Finding 05: Executive Lead Quality Trumps Volume — Selective ICP Targeting Required
One qualified C-suite lead (CFO of Geneva asset manager, GC of Geneva bank, board member of family enterprise) is worth more than 100 generic marketing leads. DigitalCoa.ch should optimize for lead quality, not lead volume: clear ICP targeting (who is the right client), selective CTA (not a generic "contact us"), trust signals before first call (credentials, thought leadership, positioning clarity). Owner must commit to selectivity — serving the right clients, not all clients.

*Different from Phase 05 (Brotons.net):* Phase 05 Finding 2 covered newsletter as primary audience development mechanism (volume play for thought leadership). Phase 06 covers executive advisory lead quality (selectivity play for conversion). Different strategies for different platforms.

### Finding 06: Executive Advisory Fee Clarity is a Conversion Factor — Transparency Reduces Friction
Executives hesitate before requesting briefings when fee structure is unclear. Transparency on fee structure (even indicative: "executive briefings are complimentary" or "advisory engagements typically range from X to Y") reduces friction and increases conversion. DigitalCoa.ch should clarify fee philosophy on the website — not necessarily exact rates, but enough to reduce uncertainty.

*Different from Phase 05:* This finding is specific to DigitalCoa.ch's conversion optimization — not covered in Brotons.net research.
