# 01 — ICP: DigitalCoa.ch Audience (Executive Advisory Platform)

## Platform Profile: DigitalCoa.ch
- **Role:** Executive clarity, strategic framing, advisory positioning, leadership relevance, lead acquisition
- **Primary Business Outcome:** Increase qualified executive traffic, generate briefing calls and audit interest, improve lead quality, strengthen executive credibility, convert trust into warm advisory conversations
- **Feel:** Executive-grade, structured, calm, commercially credible, useful for decision-makers
- **Positioning:** Distinct from Brotons.net (thought leadership, interpretive, higher tolerance for experimental essays) — DigitalCoa.ch is higher tolerance for service framing, lower tolerance for ambiguity, verbosity, and intellectual drift

## Target Audience Segments
| Segment | Profile | Decision Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| C-suite executives | CEO, CFO, Managing Director of Geneva/Nyons companies | Regulatory change affecting business model; strategic decision requiring expert counsel |
| GCs / Legal Directors | In-house counsel at Geneva financial institutions, corporates | External counsel for specific matters; AI governance; regulatory defense |
| Board members | Board directors at Swiss listed companies, family enterprises | Governance best practice; regulatory compliance oversight |
| Family office principals | UHNW family office executives | Succession planning; governance; regulatory exposure |
| Institutional investors | Asset managers, pension funds, sovereign wealth funds | Regulatory due diligence on Swiss investments; governance advisory |

## Pain Points (Executive Hypotheses)
1. **Regulatory complexity at board level** — C-suite/board need to understand regulatory risk without deep technical detail
2. **AI governance uncertainty** — Executives unsure about liability, compliance, strategic implications of AI adoption
3. **Lead time for expert counsel** — When a regulatory or strategic issue arises, executives need rapid access to trusted advisors
4. **Geneva-specific intelligence gap** — International executives with Geneva operations lack local trusted advisor
5. **Succession and governance** — Family enterprises and family offices facing succession wave need structured advisory
6. **Crisis preparedness** — Executives need relationships with crisis advisors before crises occur

### Buying Triggers (Executive Decision Triggers)
- Regulatory examination findings (FINMA, SECO)
- Board-level AI governance mandate
- Strategic transaction (M&A, capital raise, IPO)
- Leadership transition (new CEO, new GC)
- Regulatory change materially affecting business (DORA, AI Act, FinIA revision)
- Crisis event (enforcement action, dispute, incident)

### Buying Barriers (Executive Hesitation)
- "We already have advisors" — existing relationships hard to displace
- "We can handle this internally" — overconfidence in in-house capability
- Uncertainty about fee structures — executives prefer clarity before engagement
- "Who is DigitalCoa.ch exactly?" — brand clarity needed for executive trust
- Lengthy procurement processes — large enterprises have long vendor qualification cycles

## Language / Tone
- English primary (international executive audience)
- French useful for Romandie executives
- Tone: executive-grade, structured, calm, credible — no hype, no AI clichés
- Service framing acceptable (unlike Brotons.net)
- CTA must be clear and low-friction

- Testimonials/trust signals
