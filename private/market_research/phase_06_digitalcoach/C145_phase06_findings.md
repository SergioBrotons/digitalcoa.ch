## Findings

1. **Executive Briefing is Primary Conversion Mechanism — "Request an Executive Briefing"** — C-suite/GC/board members prefer 30-60 minute direct conversations over proposals/RFPs. Executive briefing (name-to-name, no pitch) is primary conversion mechanism. CTA: "Request an Executive Briefing" — name + company + brief context only.

2. **AI Governance is Signature Executive Advisory Vertical for DigitalCoa.ch** — Board-level AI governance (Art. 41 OR, FINMA AI guidance, compliance defense) is 2026 opportunity. No Geneva boutique positioned as executive-grade AI governance advisor for boards. DigitalCoa.ch can own: executive briefings, board-level AI governance frameworks, AI governance advisory.

3. **Brotons.net ↔ DigitalCoa.ch Pathway is Core Lead Generation — Warm Transfer** — Pathway: Brotons.net article → newsletter subscription → DigitalCoa.ch executive briefing CTA → briefing request → advisory relationship. Warm transfer, not cold call. Requires clear cross-links, appropriate CTAs, content signaling when to move from thought leadership to advisory.

4. **Family Enterprise Succession Advisory is Natural Geneva Vertical** — Geneva UHNW family office concentration (400+ SFOs/MFOs) + succession wave 2026-2030 + regulatory exposure (FinIA, CRS 2.0) + governance needs (Art. 914/916 ZGB foundation restructuring). Advisory: governance, succession structure, regulatory exposure assessment, trusted advisor relationship.

5. **Executive Lead Quality Trumps Volume — Selective ICP Targeting Required** — One qualified C-suite lead worth more than 100 generic leads. Optimize for quality: clear ICP targeting, selective CTA, trust signals before first call. Owner must commit to selectivity — serving right clients, not all clients.

6. **Executive Advisory Fee Clarity is Conversion Factor — Transparency Reduces Friction** — Executives hesitate when fee structure unclear. Transparency (even indicative) reduces friction and increases conversion. Clarify fee philosophy on website — not exact rates, but enough to reduce uncertainty.
