## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Target:** Boutique wealth management firms in Geneva, 1–20 employees, UHNW/HNW client base
- **Alternative ICP:** Independent asset managers (IARAs) / family offices, Geneva
- **NOT:** Global private banks (UBS, Credit Suisse, Pictet — enterprise infrastructure), institutional asset managers

### Psychographics
- **Pain:** Using AI tools for research, reporting, portfolio analysis without formal governance; FADP Art. 21 right to human review obligations unclear; CARF/CRS 2.0 already live but AI integration not formalized
- **Trigger:** FATF mutual evaluation approaching (2026-2027/2028); FINMA 08/2024 obligations already operative; Phase 01 C42 70% projected AI adoption increase signals market normalization

- **Gatekeeper:** Managing partner / CIO with AUM responsibility

### WM-Specific Pain Points
1. **CARF/CRS 2.0 already operative** — systems must be CARF-ready since Jan 1, 2026; AI integration into reporting = FADP Art. 21 exposure
2. **FINMA 08/2024 obligations already operative** — AI tool inventory + risk classification + responsibility assignment + staff training mandatory
3. **FATF evaluation 2026-2027/2028** — external pressure forcing AI governance formalization
4. **78% exploring agentic AI** — next wave of WM AI adoption creates new governance challenges (autonomous decision support)
5. **95% scaled AI vs 28% formal = 67-point gap** — widest governance gap in entire portfolio
6. **FADP directly applicable to WM AI** — obligations already operative, not future risk

### Decision-maker Motivations
- Avoid FATF mutual evaluation findings (reputational, licensing risk)
- Protect client data under FADP Art. 21 (right to human review for automated decisions)
- Formally document AI use for CARF/CRS 2.0 compliance (systems must be auditable)
- Capture efficiency gains (260h/year applicable to research/reporting/client communications)
- Stay ahead of regulatory tightening (Phase 01 C42: 70% projected increase signals market acceleration)
