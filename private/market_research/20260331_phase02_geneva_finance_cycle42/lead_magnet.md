# Lead Magnet: "The Boutique Wealth Manager's AI Governance Readiness Checklist"
## Phase 02 — Geneva Finance — Cycle 42

---

## Positioning
For Geneva boutique wealth managers and independent asset managers who have scaled AI use across research, reporting, and client communications — but lack a formal governance framework that satisfies CARF/CRS 2.0, FINMA 08/2024, and FADP obligations already in force.

**Primary urgency lever (Cycle 42):** 95% scaled AI use vs. 28% formal adoption = 67-point governance gap. The widest governance gap in the entire portfolio. FATF evaluation approaching (2026-2027/2028) creates external forcing function that makes AI governance formalization unavoidable — boutique WMs should get ahead of it before the evaluation, not during it.

**Secondary urgency lever:** CARF/CRS 2.0 already operative since Jan 1, 2026. Systems must be auditable. AI tools integrated into reporting = FADP Art. 21 exposure. No ignorance defense available.

---

## ICP Entry Points

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit (FINMA + FATF Entry)
Identify every AI tool currently in use by the WM firm: research tools, portfolio analytics, client communication platforms, document processing. Classify by data sensitivity (client PII, financial data, investment recommendations).

**Why this works:** FINMA 08/2024 requires AI tool inventory as the first mandatory obligation. FATF evaluation will assess whether AI use is formally governed. The Shadow AI audit surfaces the gap between what exists and what is documented — creating urgency without prescribing solutions.

### Decision 1: CARF/CRS 2.0 AI Integration Review
Map which AI tools touch CRS/CARF-reportable data flows. Document: (1) what data AI tools access, (2) where data is processed, (3) whether the AI vendor has data residency guarantees. Systems must be CARF-ready — this is a live obligation, not a future risk.

**Why this works:** CARF/CRS 2.0 has been operative since Jan 1, 2026. The next reporting cycle is approaching. Firms that have integrated AI into their reporting without formal documentation are currently exposed.

### Decision 2: FINMA AI Risk Classification
Per FINMA Circ. 08/2024: classify AI tools by risk level (low/medium/high). Assign internal responsibility for each tool. Document staff training completed.

**Why this works:** FINMA 08/2024 obligations are already operative. Risk classification + responsibility assignment + staff training = the three concrete actions FINMA expects. Most boutique WMs have not formally completed this.

### Decision 3: FADP Art. 21 Human Review Protocol
For every AI tool that generates output used in client-facing decisions (portfolio recommendations, risk assessments, reporting): document whether the output is reviewed by a human before the client sees it. Art. 21 FADP requires the right to human review for automated decisions producing legal effects — financial recommendations likely qualify.

**Why this works:** FADP is directly applicable to AI (FDPIC May 2025 confirmation). Most boutique WMs do not know Art. 21 applies to AI-assisted portfolio analysis. Identifying this obligation creates urgency without requiring immediate process overhaul.

### Decision 4: Agentic AI Risk Assessment
78% of wealth/asset managers are exploring agentic AI (autonomous AI agents that execute decisions without human-in-the-loop). If the WM firm is using or considering agentic AI: assess whether current governance covers autonomous decision-making or only assisted decision-making.

**Why this works:** Agentic AI is the next WM adoption wave. Firms that establish governance frameworks now will be ahead of the next regulatory tightening. The Shadow AI audit can include an agentic AI assessment as a forward-looking component.

### Decision 5: FATF Compliance Documentation
Document the firm's AI governance decisions in a format ready for FATF mutual evaluation review. This includes: AI tool inventory, risk classifications, responsibility assignments, staff training records, FADP Art. 21 review protocols.

**Why this works:** FATF evaluations of Switzerland are ongoing (2026-2027/2028). Boutique WMs that can demonstrate a structured AI governance approach will fare better than those that cannot. Documentation is evidence of good faith.

## Compliance Stack (3-Layer, All Already Operative)
1. **FINMA Layer:** Circ. 08/2024 — AI tool inventory + risk classification + responsibility assignment + staff training

3. **FADP Layer:** Art. 21 right to human review + Art. 5(c) transparency + Art. 16 data source disclosure + DPIA for high-risk processing
