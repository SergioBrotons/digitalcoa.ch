## DigitalCoa.ch — Competitive Position

### Direct Competitors (Decision Advisory / Executive Coaching)
| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | DigitalCoa.ch Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Independent executive coaches | Personal network, flexibility | Limited frameworks, no governance depth | Governance-first + framework depth |
| McKinsey/BCG coaching arms | Brand, resources | Expensive, institutional feel, conflict | Independent, human-led, Geneva-focus |
| INSEAD/IMD executive education | Brand, methodology | Academic, not hands-on | Decision infrastructure, not education |
| Psychology-based coaches | Personal trust, warmth | Limited organizational governance | Organizational + personal decision infrastructure |

### Adjacent Competitors (Governance Advisory)
| Competitor | Strengths | Weaknesses | DigitalCoa.ch Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Big 4 governance practices | Scale, brand | Expensive, audit conflicts, impersonal | Independent, human-led, no conflicts |
| Swiss corporate governance boutique | Swiss-specific | Limited AI governance | AI governance positioning stronger |
| Legal firms (governance) | Legal rigor | Expensive, not practical | Practical decision infrastructure |

### Differentiation Strategy
**DigitalCoa.ch moat:**
1. "Governance-first" positioning — unique vs. coaching firms that start with psychology
2. "Human-led" — directly addresses AI governance fear; resonates with FINMA human-in-loop requirement
3. "Decision infrastructure" — systemic, not just advisory opinions; clients get repeatable systems
4. Geneva-based, independent — no auditor conflicts, no Big 4 overhead
5. Framework-based — repeatable, scalable, defensible methodology
