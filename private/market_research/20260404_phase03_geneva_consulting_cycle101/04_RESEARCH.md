# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Consulting (C101)

## Source: Domain knowledge + publicly available regulations
## Date: 2026-04-04

## Regulatory Framework — Key Developments for Geneva Consulting

### Priority 1: nFADP / Swiss Data Protection — Enforcement Active
- Switzerland's revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP, nDSG) — fully in force since September 2023
- Ordinance (nDPO) operational; FDPIC enforcement powers active
- Key requirements: lawful basis, data minimisation, retention limits, subject access rights, breach notification
- AI-specific risk: training AI models on personal data requires lawful basis; Swiss residents' data rights must be respected
- Geneva consulting firms using AI tools: risk of non-compliance if personal/client data processed without proper framework
- **Advisory angle:** nFADP compliance audit for consulting firms, AI data processing framework, data retention policy, breach response plan

### Priority 2: ISO 42001 — AI Management System Standard
- ISO 42001 (AI management systems): published 2023; certification available from 2024
- Switzerland'sState Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO): promoting AI standards adoption
- Certification demonstrates AI governance maturity; useful for firms advising on AI implementation
- Competitive differentiation for Geneva consulting firms: ISO 42001 certification signals AI governance capability
- **Advisory angle:** ISO 42001 gap assessment, certification roadmap, AI policy development, AI governance framework

### Priority 3: DORA / ICT Risk — Geneva Financial Sector Impact
- EU DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act): in force January 2025; applies to Swiss firms with EU financial clients
- FINMA ICT risk circular: ongoing supervisory focus; ICT risk as part of SST
- Key requirements: ICT governance framework, incident reporting (4-hour window), TLPT testing every 3 years, third-party ICT risk management
- Geneva exposure: banks, asset managers, insurers with EU cross-border business
- **Advisory angle:** DORA gap assessment, ICT risk framework, incident response plan, TLPT coordination

### Priority 4: CSRD / ESG Reporting — Large Company Deadline Passed
- EU CSRD: large companies >250 employees and >EUR 40M revenue — reports due 2025 (first filings)
- Swiss companies with EU subsidiaries or EU-listed securities in scope
- ~3,000 Swiss companies affected; ~300 Geneva-based large enterprises
- ESG data framework required: most companies lack internal infrastructure
- **Advisory angle:** CSRD gap analysis, ESG data framework design, double materiality assessment, EU taxonomy alignment

### Priority 5: Swiss AI Regulation — Guidance-Based Framework
- Switzerland: no hard AI law; Federal Council AI guidelines + sector-specific guidance
- Swiss-position on EU AI Act: not adopting directly; monitoring for equivalence
- SECO AI guidelines: trustworthy AI principles; data quality, transparency, human oversight
- ISO 42001 + SECO guidelines = current Swiss AI governance framework
- SECO monitoring: any future hard AI law likely to follow EU AI Act equivalence approach
- **Advisory angle:** AI governance framework based on ISO 42001 + SECO guidelines; AI risk assessment
