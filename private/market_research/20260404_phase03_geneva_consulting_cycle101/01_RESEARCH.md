# 01 — ICP: Geneva Consulting Firms (C101)

## Source: Domain knowledge + OSEO/SEDEX registry
## Date: 2026-04-04

## Target Segment: Geneva-Based Consulting Firms

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** ~800 registered consulting firms in Canton Geneva; ~75% are 1–5 person operations (micro-firms)
- **Revenue:** CHF 100K–5M for boutiques; above CHF 5M for established mid-size firms
- **Locations:** Geneva city, La Côte corridor; Geneva's international positioning attracts multi-national consulting presence
- **Clientele:** SMEs, family businesses, startups, international organizations, commodity trading firms, financial institutions
- **Structure:** Sole practitioners, partnerships, flat-hierarchy boutiques, franchise networks (KPMG/EY subsidiaries)

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| Managing Partner / Founder | Owner-operator; drives decisions | New client acquisition, pricing pressure, talent retention |
| Engagement Manager | Delivery lead | Scope management, profitability, quality control |
| Senior Consultant | Senior delivery | Workload, client relationship management, upskilling |
| Business Development | Growth focus | Proposal writing, positioning, pipeline |

### Pain Points (C101 Update)
1. **AI adoption gap** — Geneva consulting firms (especially micro-boutiques) not leveraging AI tools; competitive risk
2. **Regulatory complexity** — clients facing multiple overlapping regulations (AI, ESG, data protection, EU compliance)
3. **Talent shortage** — difficulty recruiting experienced consultants in Geneva (cost of living, salary expectations)
4. **Pricing pressure** — clients expecting more value; commoditization of execution work
5. **Business development** — senior time consumed by BD; limited systematic approach
6. **Digital delivery** — remote/hybrid client delivery requires new tools and processes

### Buying Triggers (C101)
- Founder fatigue / burnout
- Regulatory deadline (CSRD, DORA, nFADP enforcement)
- Client demand for AI-enabled services
- New market entry (EU, UK post-Brexit)
- Succession planning
- Competitive threat from AI-enabled competitors

### Buying Barriers (C101)
- Price sensitivity in SME segment
- Preference for known networks and referrals
- Uncertainty about AI value vs. risk
- Decision speed — long sales cycles for strategy work
