## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: AI Readiness Consulting — Geneva SMEs Creating New Advisory Category
- Switzerland ranks 3rd globally in AI adoption readiness (IMD 2025); but Geneva SMEs (1–50 employees) significantly lag large enterprises
- AI tool market: Swiss companies spending estimated CHF 2–4B annually on AI tools/services; fragmented consulting supply
- Geneva-specific: AI adoption cycles beginning at international organizations, NGOs, commodity firms, Geneva-based SMEs
- New advisory categories emerging: AI readiness assessment, AI tool selection, AI implementation, change management
- nFADP/AI risk: consulting firms using AI tools face data protection compliance exposure if client personal data involved
- ISO 42001 (AI management systems): published 2023; certification pathway available; SECO promoting AI standards adoption
- **Advisory angle:** AI readiness assessment for Geneva SMEs, AI tool selection consulting, nFADP AI compliance framework, ISO 42001 certification roadmap

### Finding 02: CSRD / ESG Advisory — Geneva Large Companies Facing Reporting Deadlines
- EU CSRD: large companies >250 employees, >EUR 40M revenue — first reports due 2025; ~3,000 Swiss companies affected, ~300 Geneva-based large enterprises
- Swiss companies with EU subsidiaries or EU-listed securities in scope regardless of Swiss domicile
- EU taxonomy alignment: Swiss sustainable finance framework partially aligned but not equivalent; gap analysis needed for Swiss companies with EU exposure
- ESG data framework: most affected companies lack internal infrastructure to collect required non-financial data
- Anti-boycott risk: ESG strategies excluding certain sectors may trigger scrutiny under Swiss competition law
- Federal Council sustainability reporting guidance: Swiss domestic requirements evolving alongside EU CSRD
- **Advisory angle:** CSRD gap analysis, ESG data framework design, double materiality assessment, EU taxonomy alignment review

### Finding 03: Supply Chain Resilience / Geopolitical Risk — Geneva Commodity Hub Advisory
- Geneva = world's 3rd largest commodity trading hub (after London/Houston); Trafigura, Vitol, Mercuria, Gunvor, Louis Dreyfus all Geneva-based
- Geopolitical fragmentation: Russia/Ukraine conflict, US-China trade tensions, tariff uncertainty — supply chain review now mainstream
- Switzerland-Russia sanctions: Geneva commodity traders affected; ongoing compliance advisory demand
- Swiss manufacturing (watchmaking, precision engineering, medtech): complex multi-country supply chains under cost/resilience pressure
- Nearshoring/reshoring trend: post-pandemic acceleration; consulting opportunity for supply chain redesign
- Freight cost volatility: container rate fluctuation driving logistics optimization consulting demand
- **Advisory angle:** Supply chain resilience assessment, nearshoring analysis, geopolitical risk framework, commodity trade compliance

### Finding 04: Public Funding Advisory — Untapped Niche for Geneva SMEs
- Innosuisse: Swiss innovation agency; average CHF 350K+ per innovation project funded; many SMEs unaware or unable to navigate application
- Geneva cantonal programs: OCE (Geneva Economic Development Office) SME support packages; often underutilized
- Switzerland 2025–2028: significant federal funding available for digitalization, sustainability, R&D; application complexity creates barriers
- Grant writing/advisory: virtually no dedicated boutique firm offering this service specifically to Geneva SMEs
- Differentiation: very low competitive density; first-mover advantage significant in this niche
- Innosuisse regional contact points in Geneva: potential referral partnership
- **Advisory angle:** Public funding identification and strategy, Innosuisse application support, cantonal program navigation, overall funding strategy

### Finding 05: nFADP / ISO 42001 — AI Compliance for Geneva Consulting Firms
- Switzerland's nFADP (revised Federal Act on Data Protection): fully in force since September 2023; FDPIC enforcement powers active
- AI training data risk: using personal data to train AI models requires lawful basis under nFADP; consulting firms exposed
- ISO 42001 (AI management systems): published 2023; certification available from 2024; SECO promoting AI standards adoption
- Consulting firms using AI tools without proper data processing agreements and governance frameworks: regulatory exposure
- ISO 42001 certification: competitive differentiation for consulting firms positioning as AI governance advisors
- **Advisory angle:** nFADP AI compliance audit, ISO 42001 gap assessment, AI governance framework, data processing agreement design

### Finding 06: Pricing Strategy / Profitability — Geneva Boutique Survival Advisory
- Geneva consulting market: pricing pressure from AI-capable competitors and remote consultancies driving rates below sustainable levels
- Micro-boutique sustainability: 1–3 person firms priced below CHF 1,500/day face margin pressure; Geneva cost base makes this untenable
- Value-based pricing shift: boutique firms learning to price on outcome delivered, not time spent
- Geneva rate benchmarks: senior consultant CHF 2,500–5,000/day; partner CHF 4,000–8,000/day; significant variation by sector/specialization
- Survival challenge: many Geneva micro-boutiques facing make-or-break pricing decisions in 2026–2027
- **Advisory angle:** Pricing strategy review, profitability diagnostic, value-based pricing transformation, boutique business model optimization
