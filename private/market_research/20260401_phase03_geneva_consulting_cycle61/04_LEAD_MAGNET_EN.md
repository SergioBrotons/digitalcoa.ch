### The Consulting Firm's Paradox

You are advising clients on AI transformation and governance. But what about your own AI governance?

EU AI Act GPAI obligations have been in force since August 2, 2025. If your consulting firm uses external AI tools — Claude, GPT-4, Gemini, or others — to produce client deliverables, you may already be within scope of EU AI Act obligations for providers placing AI outputs on the EU market.

Most Geneva consulting firms don't know this. The ones that do — and have documented their position — have both a defensible compliance stance and a compelling case study for client advisory.

---

### Step 1: Does the EU AI Act Apply to Your Firm?

**The two-path test.**

Answer yes if either applies:

**Path A — Placing on EU market:**
Does your firm provide services to clients in the EU, using AI tools in the delivery of those services?

**Path B — Output used in EU:**
Does your firm's work product — proposals, research, analysis, reports, models — end up being used in the EU market by your clients or their affiliates?

If yes to either: your firm is potentially within scope of EU AI Act GPAI obligations. The obligations that have been in force since August 2, 2025 include transparency requirements for GPAI model providers. The obligations coming August 2, 2026 include high-risk AI conformity assessments for systems that could affect fundamental rights or safety.

### Step 5: The Two-Track GPAI Readiness Path

**Track 1 — Internal (for your firm):**

The consulting firm that governs its own AI use before advising clients has:
- A documented regulatory position (not just a policy document)
- A living case study to show clients
- A defensible stance if EU AI Act enforcement activates

Priority actions for Track 1:
1. Conduct GPAI applicability assessment (this framework, Step 1)
2. Document AI tool inventory for client work (this framework, Step 2)
3. Add AI transparency clauses to client contracts and engagement letters
4. Appoint a named individual responsible for AI governance at your firm

**Track 2 — Client Advisory (for your clients):**

The consulting firm that offers GPAI readiness assessment as a structured advisory product can address:
- "Does the EU AI Act apply to my business?" (applicability assessment)
- "What do I need to do before August 2, 2026?" (gap analysis and roadmap)
- "How do I document this for my board or investors?" (governance framework)

Priority actions for Track 2:
1. Develop a structured GPAI applicability questionnaire for SMB clients
2. Build a EU AI Act gap analysis template for Geneva SMBs
3. Position forced labour supply chain advisory as a new product line (see Step 4)
