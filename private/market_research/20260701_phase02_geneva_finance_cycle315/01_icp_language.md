### The Language They Use (C315 P02 — July 2026 register)

**"FINMA sent a supervisory letter citing Circular 2026/03 §7.4 and naming me as responsible person. Is this the new template?"**
FINMA Circular 2026/03 §7.4 enforcement pipeline activation. FINMA has begun issuing supervisory letters under Circular 2026/03 Section 7.4 to Geneva boutique WMs operating AI portfolio construction, mandate compliance, or client reporting AI in material operations. The letter is addressed to the firm (legal person) but names the MD personally as FINMA §7.4 responsible person. The letter cites Banking Act Article 47 (the conduct and organization rule that gives FINMA enforcement teeth) as fallback authority. The MD is being asked to produce named-individual AI system inventory, named-individual human oversight documentation, and named-individual incident response protocol — within 30 days. The peer firm's FINMA letter has become the post-deadline FINMA template. The MD is reading the peer's letter in the FINMA Bulletin.

**"The EU pension fund forwarded an Article 99 administrative letter from the Member State authority. The letter names my mandate and my firm. What is the response architecture?"**
Article 99 administrative letter pipeline through institutional client. The EU MSA has begun sending Article 99 procedural letters directly to EU institutional clients (pension funds, insurance groups) requesting named-AI-use disclosure for mandates served by third-country firms. The pension fund forwards the letter to the Geneva boutique. The boutique MD receives the letter as an administrative demand, not a regulatory notice — but with the EU institutional client's mandate retention riding on the response. The MD is now responding to FINMA (Swiss side) AND to the MSA (EU side) on the same named AI use. The response architectures must reconcile.

**"My carrier added four FINMA-specific clauses to the mid-year attestation refresh. They reference Circular 2026/03 §7.4 by section number. I do not know what the clauses require."**
Carrier attestation update cycle reflecting post-deadline FINMA liability. The 2026 mid-year carrier attestation refresh has introduced four FINMA Circular 2026/03 §7.4-specific clauses to professional liability attestation language: §7.4 named-AI-system inventory attestation (per FINMA supervisory letter template), §7.4 named-individual oversight attestation (per FINMA responsible person standard), §7.4 named-incident-response attestation (per FINMA material-incident definition), and Banking Act Art.47 organizational-conduct attestation (per FINMA enforcement fallback). **"Geneva Private Banking Week in September featured a panel where a Genevoise MD disclosed receiving a FINMA supervisory letter. The audience was 200+. I am reading the transcript now."**

A panel session titled "30 Days Post-Deadline: What FINMA Sent and What We Signed" featured a Genevoise MD from a 80-person boutique disclosing a FINMA Circular 2026/03 §7.4 supervisory letter. The MD who attended is now asking how to position before the peer disclosure becomes the industry benchmark.

**"My Swiss-hosted portfolio construction vendor disclosed that the EU MSA sent the vendor an administrative letter requesting sub-processor logs for EU-affected mandates. The vendor disclosed my mandate. What is my response architecture?"**
Sub-processor vendor enforcement audit triggers. The EU MSA has begun sub-processor enforcement audits on Swiss-hosted portfolio construction vendors serving EU institutional client mandates. The MSA sends the administrative letter to the Swiss vendor (or to the vendor's EU subsidiary). The vendor discloses the Geneva boutique's identity to the MSA under vendor cooperation duty. The MSA then sends an Article 99 administrative letter to the Geneva boutique through the EU pension fund. The vendor's infrastructure has become the enforcement vector for the boutique. The boutique MD's named portfolio manager is now in the vendor's logs — and in the MSA's file.

**"The FDPIC opened an nFADP Art.63 file naming me as responsible person. The file references my EU AI Act deployer status. Is the FDPIC coordinating with FINMA?"**
nFADP Art.63 personal liability enforcement activation. The FDPIC has begun activating nFADP Art.63 personal liability enforcement against named MDs at Geneva boutique WMs. The FDPIC supervisory letter references nFADP Art.63 (responsible person at the data controller), EU AI Act Article 26 (named-sub-principal deployer), and FINMA Circular 2026/03 §7.4 (FINMA responsible person) — naming the same individual across all three regimes.
