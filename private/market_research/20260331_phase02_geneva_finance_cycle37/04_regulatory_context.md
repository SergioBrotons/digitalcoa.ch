## Active Regulatory Framework

### 1. CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework)

**Status: LEGAL FRAMEWORK Jan 1, 2026 — REPORTING POSTPONED to 2027**

**⭐ CRITICAL CORRECTION (Phase 02 Cycle 37):** CARF postponed — not applying in 2026. OECD interpretative guidance ongoing; scope uncertainties for certain financial instruments.

- Swiss legislation (AEOIA + AEOI Ordinance) still enters force Jan 1, 2026
- Design constraints for Swiss entities re: digital value and transparent structures ACTIVE
- CARF implementation: 2027 at the earliest
- First exchange of information: 2027 (postponed from original 2026)
- Non-compliance penalties: up to CHF 250,000
- **Preparation window still exists — design constraints active NOW**

---

### 2. CRS 2.0 (Common Reporting Standard)

**Status: OPERATIVE — Since Jan 1, 2026**

CRS 2.0 confirmed live as planned — unlike CARF. Expanded scope:
- Specified electronic money products
- Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)
- Indirect crypto assets through derivatives and investment vehicles
- Tighter due-diligence and anti-avoidance rules
- Continuous monitoring for tax residency changes + crypto-linked instruments

---

### 3. FATF Mutual Evaluation

**Status: Ongoing — 2026-2027/2028**

FATF evaluation creates external pressure for WM firms to demonstrate AML/AI governance readiness.

---

### 4. FINMA Circular 08/2024

**Status: OPERATIVE — Dec 2024**

Primary source confirmed (finma.ch). Key requirements:
- AI tool inventory mandatory
- Risk classification for each AI tool
- Clear responsibility assignment for AI development/implementation
- Staff training on AI governance
- Data quality, model explainability, human oversight, continuous monitoring
- AI risks: model bias, lack of robustness, operational vulnerabilities, third-party dependencies, legal/reputational damage

---
