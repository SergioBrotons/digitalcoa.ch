# Findings Summary: Geneva Consulting Boutique — AI Accountability
## Phase 03 | C310 | 2026-05-31

---

## Key Insights

### The Commercial Forcing Function

The Geneva consulting boutique is experiencing AI accountability pressure primarily through **commercial channels** (client questionnaires, broker inquiries, proposal competition) before regulatory channels create urgency. This is a significant structural finding: the market is self-organizing around commercial consequence ahead of regulatory compliance.

The client AI governance questionnaire is now the primary forcing function for consulting boutiques — as the institutional DDQ is for wealth managers and the carrier attestation renewal is for law firms. The client's procurement function is setting a standard the Swiss regulatory system hasn't yet established.

**Implication:** Services addressing commercial vulnerability (client questionnaire response, proposal methodology documentation, sub-consultant delegation protocols) will activate ICPs before regulatory compliance services.

---

### The Proposal-as-Liability-Document Problem

The consulting proposal has become simultaneously the boutique's primary sales document and a new category of liability document. The AI-assisted proposal commits the firm in three directions:

1. **Capability commitment:** Client expects AI-assisted delivery matching the proposal's methodology description
2. **Methodology exposure:** If the proposal loses, the prospect has a documented AI methodology they can implement without payment
3. **Standard-setting:** The AI description becomes the client's audit standard for the engagement

This is a NET-NEW finding specific to Phase 03 (consulting context). The legal and finance verticals don't face the same proposal-as-liability exposure because their deliverables are more regulated and less proposal-dependent.

---

### The Sub-Consultant Delegation Chain Is Invisible

The prime consultant's delegation chain to sub-consultants has no documentation infrastructure. The prime consultant signs carrier attestation forms covering "AI use by third parties on client matters" while having no visibility into sub-consultant AI tool stacks.

This is the consulting equivalent of the law firm associate AI problem — but with external parties, no employment relationship, and no firm-controlled documentation system. The accountability chain runs through the sub-consultant without any evidence trail.

**The carrier form question structure confirms this is the carrier's next escalation point.** After firm-level policy (Phase 01) and matter-level authorization (Phase 02), the third escalation is third-party delegation — and consulting boutiques are the vertical most exposed to third-party delegation without monitoring.

---

### The Bilingual Documentation Problem Is a Design Requirement

The Geneva consulting boutique operates in two documentation environments simultaneously: English (carrier forms, client questionnaires, EU-based client contracts, proposal documents) and French (nFADP compliance, professional association guidance, Swiss client communications).

This is not a translation problem. It is a design problem. The documentation system must be capable of producing evidence that satisfies English-language carrier form questions AND French-language nFADP obligations — as one integrated documentation architecture, not as translated versions of separate documents.

No competitor offers this as a designed product.

---

### Carrier Form Escalation Pattern Confirmed in Consulting

The consulting boutique is 12–18 months behind the law firm on carrier attestation escalation. The pattern is identical: form sophistication increasing faster than firm documentation, individual professional naming, questions shifting from firm-level to matter-level, and documentation requirements becoming more specific.

**The pre-signing carrier form review is the highest-value intervention** — and the one no competitor has built for consulting boutiques. The window is now open; the carrier form questions are specific enough to create liability, and the documentation to support answers doesn't exist.

---

### Personal nFADP Article 52 Activating Through Commercial Channels

nFADP Article 52 personal liability for "responsible persons" is activating through broker conversations and client inquiries — not regulatory enforcement. The broker is the informal accountability translator, converting regulatory obligation into commercial consequence language the ICP understands.

This pattern is confirmed: regulatory awareness comes through the commercial network, not through direct regulatory communication. The Geneva informal circuit (broker, peer, network) is the primary accountability translation mechanism.

## 6 NET-NEW Findings for Phase 03 (C310)

**Finding 1: Client AI governance questionnaire as primary commercial gate**
The client procurement questionnaire is now the primary forcing function for Geneva consulting boutiques. This is distinct from the legal vertical (carrier renewal trigger) and the finance vertical (institutional DDQ trigger). The consulting boutique's commercial vulnerability (losing the mandate) activates before regulatory compliance urgency. The client questionnaire is a competitive gate, not a compliance exercise.

**Finding 2: Proposal methodology liability in three directions simultaneously**
The AI-assisted proposal creates simultaneous exposure in three directions: capability commitment (client expects delivery matching proposal), methodology theft (lost proposal becomes competitor's free consulting framework), and standard-setting (proposal becomes audit standard for the engagement). No competitor addresses the proposal-as-liability-document problem. The solution requires pre-submission proposal review, methodology traceability documentation, and AI use disclosure calibrated to evidential capacity.

**Finding 3: Sub-consultant delegation chain is structurally unmonitored**
The prime consultant has no documentation infrastructure for sub-consultant AI tool use on client matters. Carrier attestation covers third-party AI use; the prime consultant has no system to generate evidence of oversight. No standard contract language exists. No monitoring service exists. This is a specific, solvable problem requiring: sub-consultant AI disclosure requirement in contracts, a simple reporting mechanism, and documentation protocol that satisfies carrier form questions.

**Finding 4: Engagement letter commercial terms predate AI — creating structural misalignment**
The standard Geneva consulting engagement letter predates AI. Commercial terms don't address: AI tool use on engagements, AI-generated content in deliverables, client data processing by AI vendors, sub-consultant AI delegation liability, client audit rights for AI methodology, IP ownership of AI-generated content, or liability allocation for AI-assisted decisions. Sophisticated clients (multinationals, EU-based) are beginning to push AI clauses into engagement letters. The boutique has no framework for negotiating these — or inserting their own.

**Finding 5: Carrier form as design specification applies to client questionnaires as well**
The carrier form is the design specification for AI accountability documentation (confirmed from Phase 01 and 02). This principle extends to client AI governance questionnaires: the questionnaire is the design specification for documentation required to win the mandate. The questionnaire is a competitive intelligence document. No one is reading it as such. The boutique that reads the client questionnaire as a specification document — and builds documentation to answer it — wins the mandate and avoids the 18-month follow-up audit.

**Finding 6: Proportionate service architecture gap is preventing market activation**
The Geneva consulting boutique ICP can articulate the AI accountability problem. The ICP cannot find a proportionate solution. Big Four frameworks are disproportionate (months, six figures). AI training is disproportionate (wrong trigger, wrong context). Legal review is document-specific, not system-specific. The ICP identifies the problem, seeks help, finds no proportionate solution, and defers action. The documentation debt compounds. The solution architecture: specific (carrier form, client questionnaire, engagement letter), proportionate (90-minute briefing + document production, not 6-month program), and operational (documented methodology, not framework language).

## ICP Trigger Sequence (Phase 03 Specific)

1. Client AI governance questionnaire received (trigger: procurement, not regulatory)
2. Three days to respond — answered ad hoc, signed by managing director
3. AI tool use in proposal preparation mentioned or implied in response
4. Mandate won or lost — both create liability (delivery expectation OR methodology exposure)
5. Carrier renewal form received with new AI-specific questions
6. Managing director realizes: the answers are on file, the documentation doesn't exist
7. Sub-consultant uses AI on client matter — carrier form covers this, no monitoring system
8. Broker mentions nFADP Article 52 exposure — personal, not firm-level
9. Next client questionnaire arrives — cycle repeats

**Intervention points:**
- Pre-submission client questionnaire review (highest-value pre-mandate intervention)
- Pre-signing carrier form review (highest-value accountability intervention)
- Pre-proposal AI methodology documentation (prevents proposal-as-liability exposure)
- Sub-consultant AI disclosure protocol (closes the delegation gap)
