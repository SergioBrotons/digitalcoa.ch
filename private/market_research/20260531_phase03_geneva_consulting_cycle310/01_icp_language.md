# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting Boutique — AI Accountability
## Phase 03 | C310 | 2026-05-31

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### Primary Problem Framing

The Geneva consulting boutique ICP (2–50 employees, managing director personally accountable) frames AI problems in terms of **commercial vulnerability**, not regulatory compliance:

**The core framing:**
- "I signed something. I don't know what it means."
- "My client sent me a questionnaire. I don't know how to answer it."
- "I used AI to prepare the proposal. What did I commit to?"
- "My sub-consultant used AI. I'm responsible for what they did."
- "The client is asking about our methodology. I don't have a methodology."

**Commercial context language:**
- "We won the last mandate without disclosing AI. The next one requires it."
- "I can't compete on AI and also be liable for it."
- "Our proposal described AI capabilities we don't actually have documented."
- "The client asked for evidence. I had nothing to show them."

**Carrier/attestation language:**
- "The carrier form has my name on it. I signed it without reading the changes."
- "They changed the form. Added new questions. I didn't notice."
- "I answered based on what the form said last year. This year it asks differently."

**Personal liability language:**
- "If something goes wrong on a client engagement, it's my name on the door."
- "I can't delegate accountability. That's what being a partner means."
- "The nFADP exposure is personal. My firm's liability protection doesn't cover it."

**Delegation language:**
- "I send work to sub-consultants. I don't know what tools they use."
- "The engagement letter doesn't say anything about AI. Should it?"
- "My sub-consultant is using AI on my client's data. Am I monitoring this?"

**Proposal/methodology language:**
- "Our pitch describes our approach. If we lose and they use our methodology, I have no recourse."
- "The client wants to know our AI methodology. I made it sound better than it is."
- "If I disclose our AI tools in the proposal, I'm creating a standard I have to meet."

### Geneva-Specific Markers
- French regulatory context acknowledged but not integrated into commercial English communications
- Carrier form language in English, nFADP obligations in French — two documentation environments
- Small network: broker trust operates at personal level, not institutional
- Bilingual documentation is expected by clients but rarely delivered correctly

### What Triggers Action
1. Client procurement questionnaire received (three-strike escalation pattern)
2. Sub-consultant mentions AI use on active matter
3. Carrier renewal form received with changed questions
4. Proposal written using AI, client asks for methodology documentation
5. Broker mentions another firm disclosed AI in proposal and won

### What Prevents Action
1. "I don't know what a defensible methodology looks like."
2. "Every time I try to document it, I realize how exposed I am."
3. "My retainer structure predates AI. The commercial terms don't address this."
4. "I can't afford to lose the client by asking the wrong questions."
5. "There's no standard. How do I know what 'adequate' means?"

## Language Not to Use (Market Rejects)
- "AI governance framework" — too abstract, not actionable
- "best practices" — undefined, unverifiable
- "adequate controls" — meaningless without carrier form reference
- "AI policy" — firms have these; they don't solve the problem
- "compliance checklist" — wrong frame entirely
- "risk assessment" — academic, not commercial

## What They Need to Hear
- The form your client sent you is the specification
- Your sub-consultant's AI use is your accountability gap
- Your proposal methodology is your liability disclosure
- The retainer predates AI — your commercial terms don't cover this
- "Defensible" means "documented to the level your carrier will ask for"
