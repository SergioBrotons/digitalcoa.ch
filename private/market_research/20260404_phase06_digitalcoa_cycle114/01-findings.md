### Finding 1 — FINMA 2026 Regulatory "Four-Alarm Fire": Executive Decision Overload Creating Advisory Urgency
Four simultaneous regulatory pressures converging on Geneva FS executives in 2026: (1) FINMA Circular 2026/1 on Nature-related Financial Risks (Jan 1, 2026), (2) FINMA Circular 23/01 operational resilience transitional periods concluding (Jan 1, 2026), (3) Transparency of Legal Entities Act + AMLA revision entering force (Jul 1, 2026), (4) CARF implementation beginning (2026). Geneva FS executives facing four concurrent compliance decision cycles. This creates acute decision infrastructure demand: executives need frameworks to prioritize, sequence, and resource-regulate these simultaneous pressures. DigitalCoa.ch "Decision Diagnostic" and "Governance Architecture" services directly serve this pain.

**Advisory angle:** FINMA 2026 "four-alarm fire" as marketing hook for Geneva FS decision advisory; urgency framing for Decision Diagnostic service.

### Finding 6 — "Decision Confidence" vs. "Decision Analysis": Positioning on Human Dimensions
C113 finding noted "executive decision confidence as positioning differentiator." Going further: distinction between decision analysis (providing information) and decision confidence (moving forward with clarity) is the core positioning. Geneva executives are not short of information — they are short of frameworks to act decisively under uncertainty and regulatory pressure. DigitalCoa.ch's "human-led" positioning vs. AI tools (which provide analysis but not confidence) is the real differentiation. This positions DigitalCoa.ch as the anti-AI-anxiety service: helping leaders use AI tools without becoming dependent on them for decisions.

**Advisory angle:** Reposition DigitalCoa.ch from "decision analysis" to "decision confidence under AI pressure" — explicitly human-led, anti-paralysis framing; resonates with Geneva executives experiencing AI-induced decision uncertainty.
