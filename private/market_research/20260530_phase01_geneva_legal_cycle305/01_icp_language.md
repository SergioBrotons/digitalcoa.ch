# ICP Language: Geneva Legal — C305 Phase 01

## How Small Law Firms Talk About AI Problems

### The Geneva Legal ICP

Small law firm, 2–5 attorneys, Geneva-based. Managing partner personally accountable for AI decisions. Carrier asking AI governance questions at renewal. Bar guidance vacuum. Attorney-client privilege exposure. Personal liability active.

---

## ICP Language Patterns

### The Carrier Attestation Problem
- "I signed the form. Like everyone does."
- "It asked questions about how I use AI. I answered honestly."
- "Six months later I realized — I had no idea what I attested to."
- "The form created a standard I couldn't demonstrate I met."
- "The carrier didn't ask me to prove it at the time. But the form assumed proof existed."

### The Professional Secrecy Problem
- "The tool processes everything on the vendor's servers. Not mine."
- "I didn't think about professional secrecy when I started using AI."
- "The vendor's terms of service — I clicked through them. Like everyone does."
- "Article 321 CC — I didn't know I was supposed to review the vendor contract against my professional secrecy obligations."
- "No one told me. The vendor certainly didn't."

### The Documentation Gap
- "The conversation is full of frameworks. 'Follow best practices.' 'Use AI responsibly.'"
- "What no one talks about is the evidence."
- "Not the policy gap. The evidence gap."
- "When the carrier asks whether you supervise AI tools adequately — they don't want your policy. They want what you actually documented."

### The Retention Problem
- "We use AI in almost every case now."
- "There's no record of what we used. Which tools. Which prompts. What we reviewed."
- "I don't know how long I'm supposed to keep those records."
- "One year? Seven years? The duration of the matter plus some? There's no Geneva guidance."
- "Every case adds to the retention debt. And I don't know when the clock stops."

### The Client Question Problem
- "A client asked during intake: 'Will you be using AI on my case? How will my information be protected?'"
- "I had a vague clause in my engagement letter. Something about 'technology-assisted tools.' I wasn't sure what it meant."
- "I didn't have a clear answer for the client."
- "They didn't ask me to explain my AI governance. They just asked a direct question. I didn't have a direct answer."

### The Junior Associate Problem
- "The associates use AI more than I do. They know the tools better."
- "I don't know what they're entering into those tools. Which client data is going where."
- "And I'm responsible for it. Personally."
- "The form asks about my supervision of AI usage. That includes everyone in my practice."
- "I can't supervise what I don't understand. But the form doesn't care about that distinction."

### The Adequacy Problem
- "I went looking for a Geneva standard. A definition of 'adequate' AI governance for a small firm."
- "There isn't one."
- "No one has defined what adequate means for a two-to-five attorney Geneva firm."
- "The FSA confirms the obligation exists. The carrier attestation acknowledges it."
- "That's not a gap in the market. That's a first-mover opportunity."

## French ICP Language

### Le problème de l'attestation d'assureur
- "J'ai signé le formulaire. Comme tout le monde."
- "Six mois plus tard, j'ai réalisé — je n'avais aucune idée de ce à quoi j'avais attesté."
- "Le formulaire a créé une norme que je ne pouvais pas démontrer respecter."

### Le problème du secret professionnel
- "L'outil traite tout sur les serveurs du fournisseur. Pas les miens."
- "Article 321 CP — je ne savais pas que je devais examiner le contrat du fournisseur contre mes obligations de secret professionnel."
- "Personne ne m'a dit. Le fournisseur certainement pas."

### Le problème de documentation
- "La conversation est pleine de cadres. 'Suivez les meilleures pratiques.'"
- "Ce que personne ne parle, c'est de la preuve."
- "Pas l'écart de politique. L'écart de preuve."

### Le problème de rétention
- "Nous utilisons l'IA dans presque chaque affaire maintenant."
- "Il n'y a pas de trace de ce que nous avons utilisé."
- "Je ne sais pas combien de temps je suis censé conserver ces enregistrements."

### Le problème des associés juniors
- "Les associés utilisent l'IA plus que moi. Et je suis responsable."
- "Je ne peux pas superviser ce que je ne comprends pas."

### Le problème de l'adéquation
- "Je suis allé chercher une norme genevoise. Une définition de 'adéquat' pour un petit cabinet."
- "Il n'en existe pas."
- "Ce n'est pas un écart sur le marché. C'est une opportunité de premier entrant."

## The Emotional State

The Geneva legal ICP is not abstractly worried about AI. They are worried about:
- Having signed something they don't understand
- Not being able to produce evidence if the carrier asks
- Professional secrecy exposure they didn't know existed
- Not knowing what "adequate" means for their firm size
- Being personally responsible for things they don't understand

The emotional driver is personal vulnerability — not regulatory compliance. They want to protect their license, their clients, and their practice. They want someone who understands the Geneva context specifically.

The service that names their specific situation in their own language gets the call.
