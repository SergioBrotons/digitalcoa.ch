# Compliance Context: Geneva Legal — C305 Phase 01

## Geneva/Switzerland Regulatory Landscape

### Primary Regulatory Framework

**Article 321 of the Swiss Criminal Code (Professional Secrecy)**
- Prohibits disclosure of information acquired in professional practice
- Applies to all communications, documents, and data in professional context
- AI vendor contracts may create disclosure triggers — data processed on vendor servers potentially accessible to third parties
- Vendor terms of service accepted without review may create Article 321 exposure
- No Geneva-specific AI vendor contract review methodology exists

**Carrier Attestation Architecture**
- Carrier form v4 asks for "last three authorization records" — not policies
- Form creates documented standard at moment of signing
- Evidence required at renewal or audit, not at signing
- "Adequate" undefined — standard is whatever the firm represented it to be

**FSA (Bar Association) Guidance**
- FSA has acknowledged AI governance obligation exists
- No operational definition of "adequate" for small firms
- No Geneva-specific, size-specific guidance available

**Swiss Code of Professional Conduct**
- Client confidentiality obligations apply to AI use
- Engagement letters should address AI governance
- Supervision obligations extend to AI usage by all firm personnel

---

## The Compliance Gap

The regulations exist. The operational implementation for small Geneva firms does not.

Key gaps:
- Article 321 vendor contract review — no Geneva-specific methodology
- Carrier attestation evidence production — no documentation system
- Retention protocol — no Geneva guidance on how long to keep AI use records
- Client intake AI response — no specific framework
- "Adequate" definition — no Geneva standard for small firms
