### Finding 01 | SNB FX Intervention Asymmetry — Signal-Based Positioning Advisory
**Sector:** Swiss National Bank / FX Policy / Geneva Banks + Corporates
**Angle:** The SNB has operated with a de facto EUR/CHF floor intervention posture for much of the 2011-2024 period. The current policy configuration — negative interest rate tiering (still partially in effect), balance sheet normalization, and FX intervention readiness — creates an asymmetric risk for Geneva market participants. The SNB's intervention is discretionary rather than mechanical: it signals readiness but commits to no specific level. Geneva private banks, commodity houses, and Swiss exporters face opposite FX exposures ( importers vs. exporters) but both lack systematic frameworks for reading SNB intervention signals. Advisory opportunity: SNB intervention signal monitoring framework, asymmetric FX risk positioning for Geneva corporates, SNB policy reaction function analysis for treasury teams.

**Differentiation from prior cycles:** C124-C127 focused on SNB rate normalization and balance sheet normalization (post-CS unwind). C128 focuses on the SNB's FX intervention asymmetry as a discrete, signal-based advisory niche — a distinct risk management and positioning angle not previously covered.

**Confidence:** High (domain knowledge — SNB policy communications, Swiss FX market structure)

### Finding 03 | BVG Reform — Infrastructure Debt Allocation Advisory Window

### Finding 05 | OECD Pillar Two Minimum Tax — Wealth Structuring and Trust Implications for Geneva UHNW

**Angle:** OECD Pillar Two (Global Minimum Tax, 15%) took effect in Switzerland January 2025 for large multinational groups. For Geneva's ultra-high-net-worth families — many of whom operate structures straddling multiple jurisdictions — the minimum tax creates new considerations for trust structures, family investment holding companies, and philanthropic vehicles. Advisory opportunity: Pillar Two impact analysis on existing trust/holding structures, Geneva family office restructuring for minimum tax optimization, philanthropic foundation governance review under Pillar Two, and cross-border wealth structuring for Geneva UHNW clients with multi-jurisdictional footprints.

### Finding 06 | SARON Compounding — LIBOR Legacy Contract Remediation Advisory
**Sector:** Financial Infrastructure / SARON Transition / Geneva Banking Operations
**Angle:** The transition from LIBOR to SARON (Swiss Reference Rate) for existing contracts is operationally complex. A specific technical issue: SARON is a compounded overnight rate, not a term rate, which means legacy contracts referencing SARON without proper compounding mechanics will misprice interest. Geneva banks and corporates with pre-transition loan agreements, facility letters, and interbank contracts that reference SARON in an ambiguous way face financial mis-pricing and potential disputes. FINMA and SIX have both flagged remediation timelines. Advisory opportunity: SARON contract remediation audit, compounded SARON calculation framework implementation, LIBOR-to-SARON fallback clause standardization, and interbank market transaction renegotiation support for Geneva counterparties.

**Differentiation from prior cycles:** C126/C127 Phase 02 covered repo clearing infrastructure, collateral transformation, and SARON generally as a reference rate context. C128 isolates the compounded SARON vs. LIBOR legacy contract remediation as a discrete technical advisory deliverable — a narrower, more implementation-focused angle on the same underlying rate transition.

**Confidence:** High (domain knowledge — FINMA, SIX, Swiss National Bank communications)
