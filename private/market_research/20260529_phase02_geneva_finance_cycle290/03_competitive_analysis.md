# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Finance — Boutique Wealth Management

**Phase:** C290 Phase 02
**Date:** 2026-05-29
**ICP:** Boutique wealth management firms, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based.
**Focus:** AI tool deployment for client portfolios. What exists in market vs. what's missing.

---

## What Exists in the Market

### Large Private Banks (UBS, Credit Suisse, Julius Baer)
**What's available:**
- Internal AI governance frameworks (proprietary, not shared)
- Dedicated AI risk and compliance teams
- Vendor relationships with enterprise AI providers
- Formal explainability and documentation processes
- Regulatory relationships with FINMA

**Gap from ICP perspective:**
- Solutions are not designed for boutique scale
- Too expensive, too complex, too enterprise-focused
- Nothing relevant trickles down to 50-200 person firms

**Language they use:** "The big banks have entire AI governance teams. We have one compliance officer who's also doing compliance for everything else."

---

### Regulatory Consultants (Big 4, Specialist Boutique Firms)
**What's available:**
- Generic AI governance frameworks (ISO/IEC 42001 derivatives)
- GDPR/FADP compliance reviews that mention AI
- FINMA guidance interpretation consulting
- General AI risk assessments

**What's missing:**
- Swiss-specific AI governance guidance for wealth management
- Solutions specifically designed for boutique firms (50-200 employees)
- Solutions that address the managing director/partner's personal liability exposure specifically
- Integration with Swiss SRO frameworks (since SROs have been silent)
- Solutions that handle both FINMA and nFADP in one framework
- Practical, operational AI governance that doesn't require a team of 10

**Language they use:** "We talked to three consultants about AI governance. They all gave us generic frameworks that assume we have more resources than we do."

---

### AI Tool Vendors (Portfolio Management Systems, Risk Platforms)
**What's available:**
- AI-powered portfolio analytics
- AI-driven risk reporting
- AI-assisted client communication tools
- Automated compliance monitoring

**What's missing:**
- Vendor accountability for their AI systems' regulatory compliance
- Documentation that satisfies FINMA requirements
- Clear liability allocation when AI-assisted decisions cause harm
- Support for the boutique firm's AI governance documentation burden

**Language they use:** "Our AI vendor says their system is 'compliant.' When I ask what that means for my FINMA obligations, they can't tell me."

---

### Legal Firms (Swiss Financial Services Law Specialists)
**What's available:**
- nFADP compliance reviews
- General regulatory compliance advice
- FINMA guidance interpretation

**What's missing:**
- AI governance as a defined service offering
- Practical AI governance frameworks for boutique wealth managers
- Integration of AI governance with existing compliance obligations
- Clear guidance on managing personal liability exposure from AI

**Language they use:** "Our lawyer knows about nFADP and FINMA. They're not sure what to tell us about AI specifically."

---

### SROs (Asset Management Switzerland, others)
**What's available:**
- Silence

**What's missing:**
- Actionable AI governance guidance for members
- Swiss-specific interpretation of FINMA 08/2024
- Practical AI governance standards for boutique wealth managers
- Integration of AI governance into existing SRO frameworks

**Language they use:** "Our SRO told us they are 'monitoring the situation.' That's not guidance. That's a non-answer."

---

## What's Missing — The Opportunity

### Gap 1: Boutique AI Governance Framework
**What doesn't exist:**
A practical, Swiss-specific AI governance framework designed for boutique wealth management firms (50-200 employees) that:
- Addresses FINMA Guidance 08/2024 requirements in operational terms
- Integrates with nFADP obligations (especially Art. 63)
- Can be implemented by a compliance officer without a team of AI specialists
- Produces documentation that satisfies institutional client questionnaires
- Generates evidence for carrier attestation without misrepresenting AI usage

**Why it matters:**
Boutique firms are currently trying to adapt enterprise frameworks (too complex) or generic frameworks (not Swiss-specific) to their context. Neither works. The gap is a dedicated boutique AI governance solution.

---

### Gap 2: Institutional AI Questionnaire Response Service
**What doesn't exist:**
A service that helps boutique wealth managers respond to institutional client AI governance questionnaires — either by:
- Preparing the firm to answer independently (through a governance framework)
- Directly helping draft responses that are accurate and defensible

**Why it matters:**
Institutional clients are sending these questionnaires now. Boutique firms are losing clients or damaging credibility by not answering. There's no specialized service helping them respond.

---

### Gap 3: Carrier Attestation Support
**What doesn't exist:**
A service that:
- Inventory the firm's actual AI usage (including shadow AI)
- Translate AI usage into answers that satisfy carrier questionnaires
- Document AI governance controls that close coverage gaps
- Prepare the firm to answer carrier AI questions accurately

**Why it matters:**
Carriers are asking AI questions on renewal forms. Boutique firms are guessing or avoiding. No one is helping them navigate this systematically.

---

### Gap 4: Managing Director Personal Liability Protection Framework
**What doesn't exist:**
A framework specifically designed to protect the managing director or partner from personal liability arising from:
- AI tool deployment for client portfolios
- nFADP Art. 63 exposure amplified by AI
- FINMA accountability expectations for AI explainability

**Why it matters:**
The managing director is the accountable party. Existing solutions focus on the firm, not the person. The personal liability angle is underexploited and highly relevant to the ICP.

---

### Gap 5: Swiss SRO AI Governance Integration
**What doesn't exist:**
A framework that bridges the SRO silence — helping boutique firms implement AI governance in alignment with SRO expectations even without explicit SRO guidance, anticipating future SRO requirements.

**Why it matters:**
Firms waiting for SRO guidance are accumulating compliance debt. A framework that positions firms ahead of SRO requirements (rather than behind them) would be highly valuable.

---

### Gap 6: Shadow AI Audit and Governance Service
**What doesn't exist:**
A discrete service to:
- Inventory all AI tool usage within the firm (authorized and unauthorized)
- Document what data AI tools have access to
- Establish proper authorization, oversight, and documentation protocols
- Produce an AI inventory that satisfies regulators, carriers, and clients

**Why it matters:**
Shadow AI is the most immediate and uncontrollable risk. No service currently exists to help boutique firms audit and govern their actual AI usage in a discrete, bounded engagement.

---

## Competitive Landscape Summary

| Provider Type | What's Available | Critical Gaps for ICP |
|---|---|---|
| Large private banks | Internal proprietary frameworks | Not accessible to boutiques, not relevant |
| Big 4 / regulatory consultants | Generic AI governance frameworks | Not Swiss-specific, not designed for boutique scale |
| AI tool vendors | AI-powered portfolio tools | No regulatory documentation, no liability clarity |
| Legal firms | nFADP and general FINMA advice | No specific AI governance offering |
| SROs | Silence | No guidance, creating vacuum |

**Conclusion:** The market has not developed solutions specifically for Geneva boutique wealth managers navigating AI governance, carrier attestation, institutional questionnaires, FINMA 08/2024, SRO silence, and nFADP Art. 63. This is an open positioning space for a focused advisory offering.
