# ICP Language: Geneva Finance — Boutique Wealth Management

**Phase:** C290 Phase 02
**Date:** 2026-05-29
**ICP:** Boutique wealth management firms, 50–200 employees, Geneva-based. Managing director or partner personally accountable.
**Focus:** Struggling with AI tool deployment for client portfolios and data.

---

## How They Talk About AI Problems

### The Accountability Frame
They don't say "we need better AI governance." They say:

- **"Who's responsible when the algorithm recommends a portfolio that turns bad?"**
- **"I can't explain to a client why their allocation changed — the system just did it."**
- **"If something goes wrong with AI, it's my license on the line, not the vendor's."**
- **"We got an AI governance questionnaire from a large institutional client. No one on our team can answer half the questions."**

The personal liability framing is dominant. They're not abstractly concerned about AI — they're concretely afraid of being the named accountable party when something fails.

### The Documentation Problem
They describe explainability as a compliance burden, not a technical preference:

- **"FINMA wants to see that we understand what our AI tools are doing. But we don't have that visibility."**
- **"Every time a client asks about AI, we have to wing it. That's not sustainable."**
- **"We know what the model outputs. We don't know why."**
- **"The documentation the AI vendor provides is marketing material, not regulatory evidence."**

### The Carrier Attestation Problem
- **"Our insurer sent us a questionnaire about AI usage. We don't even know what AI we're using."**
- **"Carrier attestation feels like a trap. Sign the wrong thing and you're uninsured for an AI-related claim."**
- **"We've started saying 'no AI' in our attestations just to avoid the exposure. But that's not true either."**

### The nFADP Art. 63 Problem
- **"Article 63 of the new data protection law makes the managing partner personally liable for data breaches involving client information. Add AI into that mix and it's a liability nightmare."**
- **"We process client financial data with AI tools. What happens if that data gets mishandled? I'm personally exposed."**
- **"nFADP compliance is already complex. AI amplifies every data protection risk."**

### The SRO Silence Problem
- **"We've asked our SRO about AI governance expectations. The answer was essentially 'we'll let you know.'"**
- **"The SRO hasn't published anything actionable on AI. We're operating in a vacuum."**
- **"Everyone keeps saying 'wait for guidance.' But clients and carriers aren't waiting."**

### The Shadow AI Problem
- **"I know people are using ChatGPT for client notes. I haven't authorized it, but I also can't prove it's not happening."**
- **"Every advisor has their own set of AI tools. I have no visibility."**
- **"Shadow AI is the real risk. At least the tools we formally approved, we can document."**

### The Competitive Pressure Problem
- **"Larger private banks have entire AI governance teams. We have one compliance officer who's also doing everything else."**
- **"Our clients are asking about AI. Our competitors are claiming they have it. We need to catch up without creating more risk."**
- **"We can't afford to fall behind on AI. But we also can't afford to get it wrong."**

## Language Markers to Use

| What they're actually saying | How to mirror it |
|---|---|
| "I can't explain what the AI is doing" | explainability gap |
| "I'm the one on the hook" | personal accountability |
| "We got a questionnaire from a client" | institutional AI governance questionnaire |
| "Our insurer wants documentation" | carrier attestation |
| "No guidance from our SRO" | regulatory vacuum / SRO silence |
| "Article 63 exposure" | nFADP Art. 63 personal liability |
| "People using unapproved AI" | shadow AI |
| "We don't know what AI we're running" | AI inventory gap |

---

## Emotional State

**Primary emotion:** Paralyzed by personal liability exposure while peers appear to be moving forward.

**Secondary emotion:** Frustrated by SRO silence and lack of actionable Swiss-specific guidance.

**Tertiary emotion:** Trapped between client expectations (AI capability) and regulatory/compliance risk (AI governance).

**Behavioral signal:** They're not asking "should we adopt AI?" They're asking "how do we adopt AI safely without personally bearing catastrophic risk?"
