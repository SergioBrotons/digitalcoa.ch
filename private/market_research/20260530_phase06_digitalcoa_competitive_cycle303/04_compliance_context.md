### Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 — Medical Devices Only

**The clarification:** Swissdamed July 1, 2026 deadline applies specifically to medical DEVICE manufacturers under IVDR (In Vitro Diagnostic Regulation). It does NOT apply to general AI attestation for medical practices.

**The confusion:** Small medical practices have been told they are subject to Swissdamed. Some have waited for Swissdamed compliance guidance that doesn't apply to them. Others who correctly identify nFADP + FMH as applicable still lack a compliance pathway.

**What it means for ICPs:**
- Medical practices need to know whether Swissdamed applies to them (it likely doesn't for general AI use)
- nFADP + FMH guidance applies to medical practices regardless of Swissdamed status
- The confusion itself creates decision paralysis — practices don't act because they don't know which framework applies

**What competitors say:** "Prepare for Swissdamed." / "Swissdamed compliance by July 2026."

**What competitors miss:** Generic Swissdamed messaging creates confusion for medical practices that aren't actually subject to the July 1 deadline for AI use (only devices). Competitors who don't clarify this are adding to the confusion, not resolving it.

**DigitalCoa position:** "We clarify which framework applies to your practice — Swissdamed, nFADP, or FMH — and build the compliance pathway for the framework that actually governs you."

### nFADP — Active Now, Enforcement Beginning

**The timeline:** nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) is in force. The 2023 transition period has ended. Enforcement is beginning — FDPIC has issued guidance, professional bodies are integrating obligations, carriers are updating attestation forms.

**What it means for ICPs:**
- Art. 63 personal liability for managing directors and responsible individuals is active
- FDPIC guidance (May 8, 2025) acknowledges nFADP applies to AI — no compliance pathway
- Carrier form v4 evidence requirements are testing Art. 63 compliance in practice

**The enforcement mechanism:** nFADP enforcement runs through:
- Carrier attestation (evidence requirements in malpractice and professional liability forms)
- Professional body disciplinary processes (SBA for lawyers, FMH for physicians, FINMA-supervised entities)
- Institutional client questionnaires (client-driven enforcement of AI governance standards)
- Civil liability (data subjects can assert claims under Art. 63)

**What competitors say:** "nFADP compliance." / "Data protection for AI systems." / "Art. 63 personal liability awareness."

**What competitors miss:** Generic nFADP positioning doesn't connect the regulatory text to the operational evidence requirement. ICP knows they have obligations; they don't know what evidence their carrier is testing for.

**DigitalCoa position:** "nFADP Art. 63 is active. Your carrier is testing whether you have evidence — not whether you have a policy. We build the evidence system."

**What's happening:** Every regulatory body, carrier, and institutional client is moving toward the same operational requirement: evidence of AI authorization decisions.

**The convergence:**
- nFADP Art. 63 → evidence of responsible person designation and decision attribution

**Why it matters:** The convergence creates a single operational requirement (evidence) that satisfies all frameworks simultaneously. A firm with a working evidence system has compliance evidence for every framework — not fragmented compliance for each framework separately.

### The Documentation Paradox — Why the Window Is Narrow

**The paradox:** ICP knows they have a documentation problem. ICP knows they need to act. ICP cannot act because they don't know what "good" looks like for their specific practice.

**The failure mode:** Awareness → paralysis → accumulation → crisis → forced compliance (expensive, incomplete, stressful) → repeat.

**The window:** The compliance horizon is 14 months wide for EU AI Act. Carrier form v4 evidence requirements are active now. Institutional questionnaire requirements are being sent now. The window is not a single deadline — it's a compounding risk that gets more expensive to address the longer ICP waits.

**Why the window is narrow for the unprepared:** Documentation debt compounds. Every AI decision without evidence capture adds to the debt. The longer ICP waits, the more debt accumulates, the more expensive the solution, the more painful the audit.

**Why the window is wide for the prepared:** A firm that begins now (May 2026) has 14 months to build the evidence system before EU AI Act compliance becomes urgent. The firm that begins in Q3 2026 has 6 months. The firm that begins in Q1 2027 has 3 months. The firms that begin now create a systematic advantage.

### Carrier Form v4 — The Specific Evidence Standard

**What it requires:** "Last three authorization records" per AI system in use.

**What this means operationally:**
- Each AI system in use requires a documented authorization record
- The record documents: who authorized, when, with what scope, what decision it supported
- "Last three" means the three most recent authorization decisions for each system
- The records must be available at attestation time — not reconstructable on demand, available

**Why it's the forcing function:** Carrier form v4 is specific, operational, and testable. The carrier auditor can verify whether the records exist. The ICP knows exactly what they need to produce — and knows they don't have it.

**The documentation debt:** Most Geneva professional firms adopted AI systems 2–3 years ago without documentation protocols. The systems were never built to capture authorization records. The documentation debt is structural, not a workflow problem.

**What competitors offer:** Policy documents, framework alignment assessments, general AI governance recommendations.

**What DigitalCoa provides:** The evidence capture system that creates authorization records prospectively — so the firm has the last three records when the carrier asks for them.
