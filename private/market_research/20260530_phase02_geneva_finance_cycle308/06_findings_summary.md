## C308 Phase 02 NET-NEW Findings (6 Total)

### Finding 1: Institutional DDQ as Competitive Gatekeeper
Institutional clients (family offices, pension funds) are using AI governance DDQs as a **pre-investment screening tool**. Failure to respond adequately means lost mandate. This is a commercial gate, not a compliance exercise. The ICP has no prepared answer and no documentation.

### Finding 2: FINMA Supervisory Expectation Gap (No Written Standard, Active Examinations)
FINMA has not published specific AI governance guidance, but field examinations are already including AI questions. The ICP cannot prepare against a known standard — they can only react to what examiners actually ask. The supervisory preference appears to be evidence of a process, not proof of a perfect process.

### Finding 3: nFADP Article 63 Personal Liability — "Partner Personally Responsible" Framing Activating
Article 63 creates **direct personal liability** for managing directors and partners. Wealth management firms process high volumes of client PII as core business activity, creating a specific Article 63 exposure profile. The "me personally" framing is activating in partner-level conversations. Documentation is personal liability defense, not just compliance.

### Finding 4: Carrier Attestation Misrepresentation Risk During Renewal (Distinct from Initial Attestation)
The renewal attestation is riskier than the initial attestation. The ICP who signed without full knowledge is now more informed. More knowledge at renewal means higher misrepresentation risk if the attestation is inaccurate. The ICP who is more informed is paradoxically more exposed at renewal.

### Finding 5: Portfolio Construction AI Tool Authorization Chain (Multi-Layer, Multi-Vendor)
Portfolio construction AI tools (optimization, risk modeling, allocation algorithms) are widely used but undocumented. The human authorization chain for investment decisions made with AI assistance is not documented. The ICP cannot produce evidence for institutional DDQ questions about investment methodology or carrier questions about investment process.

### Finding 6: Boutique Firm Documentation Coherence Problem (50–200 Employees, One Partner Accountable)
The accountability model (one managing director, full personal liability) is structurally misaligned with the operational reality (distributed AI use across multiple employees and mandates, no systematic documentation). The boutique firm cannot maintain four separate documentation streams with one managing director and no dedicated compliance team.

## Key Insights

### Insight 1: Four Simultaneous Regulatory Pressures, One Documentation System
The Geneva boutique wealth management ICP faces four simultaneous pressures:
1. FINMA supervisory examination (no written standard, active questions)
2. Institutional DDQ commercial gate (no documentation, lost mandates)
3. Carrier attestation renewal (misrepresentation risk, informed ICP)
4. nFADP Article 63 personal liability (personal exposure, direct liability)

These four pressures require **one coherent documentation system**, not four separate documentation exercises.

### Insight 2: The Documentation Coherence Problem Is Structural, Not Operational
The boutique wealth management structural reality (50–200 employees, one managing director or partner personally accountable) cannot support four separate documentation streams. The solution is not better compliance — it is an evidence architecture designed for boutique scale.

### Insight 3: The Evidence Architecture Is the Product
The market has frameworks, tools, and analysis. It needs evidence. The category is not "AI governance" — it is "evidence of human authorization for specific AI-assisted decisions at boutique wealth management scale."

### Insight 4: Personal Liability Framing Activates the ICP More Than Firm-Level Liability
The "me personally" framing of Article 63 creates urgency that firm-level liability does not. The managing director or partner who learns they are personally exposed acts differently than the firm that learns it has a compliance gap.

### Insight 5: Renewal Window Is the Decisive Trigger
The carrier renewal window creates acute urgency. The ICP knows the carrier will ask again. The renewal-specific documentation needs (misrepresentation risk, informed ICP, gaps between attested and actual) are distinct from initial attestation needs.
