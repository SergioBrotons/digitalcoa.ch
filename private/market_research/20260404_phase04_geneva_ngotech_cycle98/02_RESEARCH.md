## Finding

**Geneva Deep Tech Startup Funding Rebounds — CERN Spin-offs Lead Quantum and Biotech Clusters**

Geneva's deep tech startup ecosystem — anchored by CERN technology transfer and EPFL (Lausanne) proximity — is showing signs of funding recovery after the 2022-2024 venture capital contraction. Quantum computing, advanced biotech, and climate technology are the strongest sectors.

Key dynamics:

**1. CERN知识产权 transfer pipeline:** CERN's Knowledge Transfer programme has accelerated spin-off formation, particularly in quantum sensing, medical imaging, and advanced computing. Geneva-based CERN spin-offs are attracting Series A interest from European deep tech investors.

**2. Quantum technology cluster forming:** A Geneva-based quantum technology cluster is emerging — firms working on quantum sensing (for pharma quality control), quantum encryption (for financial services), and quantum computing applications. Geneva's finance sector is a natural anchor customer.

**3. Innosuisse funding expansion:** Switzerland's Innosuisse (Innovation Agency) has expanded its startup coaching and funding instruments. Geneva-based deep tech startups are accessing Innosuisse innovation cheques to bridge pre-seed funding gaps.

**4. Geneva venture capital landscape:** Geneva-based VCs (Geneva Venture Partners, Alpine Ventures) are seeing renewed LP appetite after 2024 performance improvements. Fundraising conditions for new Geneva VC funds are improving relative to 2023 lows.

**5. Biotech cluster around Geneva-Lausanne axis:** The Geneva-Lausanne biotech cluster (home to Merck Serono, Lonza, and multiple biotech startups) is generating cross-sector innovation. AI-driven drug discovery startups from this cluster are attracting international pharma interest.
