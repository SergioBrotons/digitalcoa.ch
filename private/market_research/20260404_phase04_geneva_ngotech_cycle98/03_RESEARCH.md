# Finding 03 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 04: Geneva NGO/Tech
**Source:** UN OCHA, Geneva International, Swiss Development Finance (SDC)  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Humanitarian Funding Model Under Strain — Geneva NGOs Pivot to Outcome-Based Contracting**

The traditional humanitarian funding model — large institutional donors (SDC, FCDO, USAID, ECHO) providing multi-year project grants to Geneva-based NGOs — is under structural strain. Donor governments are demanding greater value-for-money accountability, outcome measurement, and shift toward unrestricted funding.

Key dynamics:

**1. Shift from project to programme funding:** Major donors are consolidating project-specific grants into longer-term programme funding arrangements. Geneva NGOs must demonstrate systematic outcomes across multiple interventions — requiring more sophisticated M&E (monitoring, evaluation, accountability) systems.

**2. Humanitarian-tech funding emergence:** Humanitarian technology startups in Geneva — focused on refugee logistics (iDDEN), disaster response coordination, and conflict zone communications — are attracting a new category of impact-first investor and donor funding.

**3. Private sector engagement in humanitarian response:** Geneva IFRC (International Federation of Red Cross) and major Geneva NGOs are increasing private sector engagement (not just government donors). Corporate humanitarian partnerships, cause marketing, and employee engagement programmes are growing revenue streams.

**4. Climate humanitarian nexus:** The overlap between climate adaptation funding and humanitarian response is creating new funding instruments. Geneva-based NGOs with climate-humanitarian programming are well-positioned for these emerging funds.

**5. NGO financial sustainability pressures:** Administrative cost allocation debates (how much overhead is legitimate) continue to strain Geneva NGO finances. The pressure to demonstrate impact while maintaining low overhead ratios creates strategic and operational tensions.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** The evolution of humanitarian funding is a story about global accountability and changing power dynamics in development. A thoughtful analysis of what the shift to outcome-based contracting means for humanitarian effectiveness would attract broad interest.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** NGOs as institutional clients need advisory services on funding strategy, private sector partnerships, and impact measurement frameworks. This is a direct advisory services angle for DigitalCoa.ch's NGO client base.
