# Market Signals — Phase 04 C296 Geneva Healthcare
## Current Problems, Complaints, Failures

*Documented market signals from Geneva healthcare ICP engagement and regulatory developments.*

## Primary Market Problem: Carrier AI Attestation Wave Arriving in Healthcare

Carriers are extending AI governance attestation instruments into the healthcare sector — previously concentrated in finance and legal verticals, now arriving at independent medical practices with questionnaires calibrated to underwriting risk assessment, not physician professional liability protection.

**Observed problem:** Physicians receiving attestation forms with 2-4 week response windows, no Geneva-calibrated reference standard available to evaluate what "adequate AI governance controls" means at independent practice scale, personal signature creating personal liability commitment without pre-signing review pathway.

**Failure mode:** Urgency-driven documentation creation using vendor-downloaded templates and internet searches — documentation assembled does not accurately represent actual AI practice, creates commercial representations to carrier that cannot be supported with evidence, signed by physician who has not evaluated accuracy.

---

## Clinical Documentation Gap: AI Tool Switching Mid-Record Liability

When a physician switches AI tools mid-patient-record, the medical record becomes a continuous stream of AI-generated content from multiple systems with no transition documentation. The physician cannot identify which AI generated which clinical content. Records are authoritative under the physician's name with no AI attribution architecture. This creates a cascade where each AI-to-AI influence compounds under the physician's accountability.

**Observed problem:** No documentation standard exists in Geneva for mid-record AI tool transitions. When carrier or FMH requests documentation of AI governance, the physician cannot reconstruct which AI system contributed which content.

**Failure mode:** Accumulated AI tool switching creates retrospective accountability exposure the physician cannot discharge — name on record, AI contribution invisible, every prior decision now potentially involving AI systems that cannot be enumerated.

## Patient-Override Documentation Void

When a patient arrives with consumer AI-generated health analyses and the physician exercises independent clinical judgment making a different decision than AI recommended, the record documents patient AI input and physician decision but not the specific deliberation moment where the AI input was evaluated and rejected.

**Observed problem:** This is a discrete documentation category — AI-influenced-but-not-AI-determined clinical decision — with no Geneva protocol, no naming, no defined requirement. Despite being a moment of high clinical accountability, no documentation standard exists.

**Failure mode:** Physician who exercised sound clinical judgment may be unable to demonstrate the evaluation process in retrospective review because the moment of AI recommendation evaluation is not documented.

---

## Multi-Physician Practice Internal Accountability Diffusion

In small group practices (3-6 physicians), each physician independently adopts different AI tools, activates different EHR AI features, implements different AI-assisted documentation practices without practice-level AI governance coordination.

**Observed problem:** When the practice receives carrier attestation, the signing physician certifies to AI surface they cannot fully enumerate. No Geneva methodology exists for addressing internal accountability diffusion at small-group practice scale.

**Failure mode:** Carrier attestation signed by one physician for a multi-physician practice creates documentation of AI governance the signing physician cannot independently verify — internal accountability diffusion becomes external liability.

---

## Post-Incident AI Documentation Architecture Absence

When an adverse clinical outcome occurs in an independent practice, existing incident documentation infrastructure designed for human clinical decision-making has no category for: AI system state at time of event, AI tool version at time of event, AI recommendation made, physician's AI-influenced vs. AI-independent decisions.

**Observed problem:** Physician's name on record, AI's contribution invisible to review process. No Geneva medical body or carrier has published AI-associated adverse outcome documentation requirements proportionate to independent practice scale.

**Failure mode:** Post-incident review processes unable to evaluate AI's contribution to adverse outcomes because no architecture exists to document AI system state at the time of the event.

---

## AI Competency Documentation Wrong-Standard Paradox

Independent practices building AI competency documentation (vendor training certificates, staff attendance records, online course completions) to satisfy implied FMH AI competency requirements that FMH has never operationally defined.

**Observed problem:** Documentation inadequate to evidentiary purpose while creating false confidence of compliance. Practice has evidence of compliance that would not survive carrier or FMH substantive challenge.

**Failure mode:** False adequacy of wrong-standard compliance — attempting compliance creates compliance-looking evidence that does not actually comply. Paradox: trying to do the right thing creates a worse position than doing nothing.

---

## Carrier Attestation Stampede as Self-Incriminating Documentation Pattern

Independent practices experiencing stampede response to carrier AI attestation — receiving questionnaires, attempting to document AI governance retroactively under time pressure, using vendor templates and internet searches — creates documentation that does not accurately represent actual AI practice, creates commercial representations to carrier that cannot be supported with evidence.

**Observed problem:** Signed by physicians who have not evaluated whether documentation is accurate. Attempt to comply creates documentation that will be used against signing physician if challenge occurs.

**Failure mode:** Urgency-driven documentation creation produces self-incriminating evidence — documentation assembled under pressure that becomes the documented standard of care for the practice, even though it doesn't reflect actual practice.

---

## nFADP Art.22 Reversal Right vs. Medical Record Integrity Structural Paradox

Art.22 applies where AI substantially informs patient record decisions — patient can request reversal, physician must execute — but medical records cannot be casually reversed without clinical process and record integrity obligations.

**Observed problem:** Two incompatible obligations simultaneously active with no Geneva protocol for proportionate clinical notation satisfying Art.22 without altering medical record architecture.

**Failure mode:** Physician attempting to comply with Art.22 reversal obligation may compromise medical record integrity; physician preserving record integrity may be non-compliant with Art.22.

---

## Vendor Data Extraction Clause as Active Professional Secrecy Exposure

Click-through vendor terms include data extraction clauses for model training and benchmarking. Accepted for core functionality, activates processing under vendor's claimed lawful basis. Physician's Art.321 CP professional secrecy obligations extended to data physician controls, vendor data flow creates external processing the physician cannot stop without losing core functionality.

**Observed problem:** Physician accepting terms not evaluated for obligations incompatible with FMH accountability. No independent medical practice has reviewed vendor data extraction provisions against their professional secrecy obligations.

**Failure mode:** Active professional secrecy exposure through vendor terms accepted without evaluation — processing physician controls creating obligations the physician is accountable for but cannot stop.
