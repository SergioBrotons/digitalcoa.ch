# Compliance Context — Phase 04 C296 Geneva Healthcare
## Geneva/Switzerland Specific Regulatory Environment

*Documented regulatory framework for Geneva independent medical practice AI accountability.*

---

## 1. FMH Framework — Human Oversight Without Operational Definition

**Status:** Operative standard, no operational endpoint defined.

FMH has stated human oversight as the principle governing AI use in medical practice. This is the de facto operative standard for Geneva independent physicians.

**The gap:** FMH has defined the principle without defining what Wednesday looks like in a two-physician independent practice. No documentation requirement, no review frequency, no competency standard, no operational endpoint.

**For the independent physician:** The principle is mandatory but undefined. Physician accountable for oversight but no defined measure of what oversight requires. Creates compliance theater where documentation doesn't map to actual practice.

---

## 2. nFADP — Applicable Now, Enforcement Live Since May 2025

**Status:** In force, enforcement posture confirmed active since May 2025 FDPIC posture shift.

The Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection is directly applicable to AI systems processing personal data. No regulatory gap between nFADP and AI — enforcement possible without new legislation.

**Key articles for independent medical practice:**

**Art.5 — Lawful basis for AI data processing:**
- AI processing of patient data requires documented lawful basis
- Most AI tools activated in clinical context require purpose limitation analysis
- Physician responsible for documenting lawful basis, not vendor

**Art.6 — Proportionality principle:**
- AI data processing must be proportionate to stated purpose
- Each AI feature activated requires proportionate evaluation
- No Geneva methodology for proportionate evaluation at practice scale

**Art.8 — Profiling notification:**
- AI-assisted clinical decision-making may constitute profiling
- Patients have right to notification and human review
- Right to notification of automated decision-making applied to AI-assisted medical decisions
- No Geneva independent practice has issued profiling notifications as category

**Art.9 — DPA requirement:**
- Data Processing Agreements required with AI tool vendors
- Third-party processor chains create obligations the physician bears
- Click-through acceptance may create Art.9 obligations without evaluation

**Art.21 — Automated decision obligations:**
- Right to meaningful information about automated decision-making
- Right to human review of AI-assisted decisions
- Physician must be able to explain AI contribution to specific decisions

**Art.22 — Reversal right:**
- Patient can request reversal of AI-informed decisions
- Physician must execute reversal while maintaining record integrity
- Structural paradox: Art.22 and medical record integrity push in opposite directions
- No Geneva protocol for satisfying both simultaneously

**Art.63 — Criminal liability:**
- Criminal penalties for violations involving personal data processing
- Personal liability for managing physician who approves AI systems creating violations
- Different evidentiary standard than administrative compliance documentation

**Art.75 / Art.76 — Supervisory authority enforcement powers:**
- FDPIC has enforcement authority, confirmed enforcement posture May 2025
- Enforcement currently active, not prospective 2027 risk

## 3. Swissdamed — Classification Pathway Inaccessible at Independent Practice Scale

**Status:** Framework exists, criteria written for manufacturers not physicians.

Swissdamed is Switzerland's database for medical devices and the authorization pathway for clinical AI tools. Classification tiers have operational consequences.

**The gap:** Classification criteria are designed for manufacturers applying for market authorization. Independent outpatient physicians evaluating whether their existing AI tools require Swissdamed classification find the framework structurally inapplicable to their situation.

**Tier 1 / Tier 2 distinctions:** Have operational meaning for manufacturers but don't translate to what independent physicians are responsible for in daily practice governance.

**Compliance paralysis:** Independent practices examining Swissdamed guidance find no pathway for their scale and use case — not because they lack compliance infrastructure but because the framework wasn't designed for their situation.

---

## 4. Professional Secrecy — Art.321 CP Extension to AI-Processed Data

**Status:** Active obligation, vendor terms creating unevaluated exposure.

Swiss Criminal Code Art.321 establishes physician professional secrecy obligations. AI tools processing patient data extend professional secrecy to those data flows.

**The structural problem:** Click-through vendor terms include data extraction clauses for model training and benchmarking. Accepting those terms activates processing under vendor's claimed lawful basis, extending physician's Art.321 CP obligations to data the physician cannot control. Physician accountable for vendor data flows they cannot stop without losing core functionality.

**No Geneva protocol exists** for evaluating vendor terms against Art.321 CP professional secrecy obligations at independent practice scale.

---

## 5. Carrier AI Attestation Instruments — Underwriting Design, Not Medical Liability Design

**Status:** Arriving in healthcare sector, creating personal liability without discharge pathway.

Carriers are extending AI governance attestation to independent medical practices. Instruments designed by carrier underwriting teams for risk evaluation, not by medical liability professionals for physician protection.

**What attestation commits physician to:**
- Personal signature on undefined adequacy standard
- "Adequate AI governance controls" — no Geneva-calibrated definition
- Documentation requirements not defined by FMH or Geneva carriers
- Personal liability exposure triggered by signature, not by governance failure

**The structural gap:** No pre-signing review service exists in Geneva market for medical practitioners evaluating carrier attestation instruments. Physician signs commitment without understanding what is required or what evidence supports compliance.

---

## 6. Medical Liability — Parallel Obligation Track

**Status:** Independent of regulatory framework, creates compound exposure.

Swiss medical liability doctrine operates parallel to nFADP and FMH guidance — different obligations, different evidence standards, different enforcement logic.

**AI contribution to adverse outcomes:** When adverse outcome occurs, existing medical liability framework has no category for AI system state, version, or recommendation. Physician's name on record, AI contribution invisible to liability review.

**No Geneva protocol** for documenting AI contribution in post-incident review contexts proportionate to independent practice.

---

## 7. Geneva Institutional Intelligence Gap

**Status:** Standards forming without independent practice participation.

HUG (Hôpitaux Universitaires de Genève) and CHUV (Centre Hospitalier Universitaire Vaudois) decisions are forming effective standards through governance rooms independent physicians do not access. These decisions are accessible to carriers, patients, and legal proceedings without transmission to independent practices building in isolation.

**The structural exclusion:** Independent practices are measured against standards formed without their participation. Institutional decisions made in rooms where independent physicians have no voice create operative benchmarks applied to practices that had no opportunity to participate in their formation.

**Formation mechanism vs. information gap:** This is not simply an information gap — the formation mechanism itself excludes independent practice participation. Even with perfect information about decisions, independent physicians cannot participate in the formation process.

---

## 8. Documentation Timing — Window Properties

**Status:** Present-tense positioning events, not future compliance deadlines.

Documentation window has specific timing properties:
- Before window closes: firms building documentation evaluated against forming standards
- After window closes: firms explaining absence evaluated against arrived standards
- New documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct window-period reasoning
- Documentation window close is irreversible positioning event

**Implication:** Independent practices not building documentation now are building evidence of absence, not adequacy. Carrier attestation evidence requirements designed to evaluate window-period decisions, not post-window responses.

---

## Summary: Regulatory Environment Properties

1. **FMH principle without operational endpoint** — mandatory but undefined
2. **nFADP enforcement live since May 2025** — present-tense exposure not 2027 risk
3. **Swissdamed classification pathway exists but inaccessible** at independent practice scale
4. **Professional secrecy obligations extended** through vendor terms without evaluation
5. **Carrier attestation operating as personal liability instrument** without pre-signing review
6. **Medical liability framework has no AI documentation category** — creates compound exposure
7. **Institutional standards forming without independent practice participation** — structural exclusion
8. **Documentation window has irreversible positioning properties** — timing matters
