# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Small Consulting Boutiques
## Phase 03 — C292 | DigitalCoa.ch Market Research

---

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

---

### Existing Market Landscape

**1. AI Governance Generalists**
Large consulting firms (Big Four, major strategy boutiques) offer AI governance advisory. Positioned for enterprise clients. Not calibrated for 2–50 employee Geneva consulting firms. Not focused on personal liability of the managing director. Not Geneva-specific. Not offering proportionate boutique-scale methodology.

**2. Legal Technology Providers**
Legal tech vendors selling AI tools to law firms. Their "governance" content addresses tool use within their platform. Not independent. Not serving the boutique consulting firm's accountability interest. Their governance framing serves their commercial interest.

**3. Data Protection Consultants**
nFADP compliance consultants offering general data protection advisory. Address AI through a data protection lens. Not focused on professional liability, carrier attestation, proposal defensibility, or sub-consultant delegation. Different accountability frame from what Geneva consulting firms need.

**4. Technology Vendors**
AI tool vendors publishing "AI governance guides" for their users. These are designed to reduce vendor liability, not to protect the professional liability of the consulting firm. The frameworks they recommend serve their commercial interests. No independent Geneva consulting-specific AI governance framework exists from any vendor source.

**5. Industry Associations**
Swiss industry associations (economiesuisse, SwissHoldings) publish AI governance position papers. Written for large enterprises. Not operational guidance for small consulting firms. Not Geneva-specific. Not calibrated to the personal liability of the managing director.

---

### What's Missing (White Space)

**1. Geneva Consulting AI Governance — Boutique Scale**
No market participant offers Geneva consulting-specific AI governance advisory calibrated to 2–50 employee firms. The specific accountability intersection of personal liability, carrier attestation, client AI questionnaires, sub-consultant delegation, and nFADP personal exposure — addressed as a unified problem for a specific ICP — does not exist in the market.

**2. Pre-Vendor-Terms Audit Service**
No market participant offers a discrete service for evaluating AI vendor terms against a consulting firm's client confidentiality obligations, engagement letter provisions, and nFADP processor obligations. Firms are accepting vendor terms as click-through conditions without evaluating what they authorized. No proportionate boutique-scale methodology for vendor term audit exists.

**3. Client Contract AI Clause Review**
No market participant offers a discrete service for identifying, evaluating, and negotiating AI governance clauses in client MSAs and SOWs before acceptance. The specific problem — AI governance obligations embedded in commercial contracts, evaluated as contract items not AI governance items — is not addressed as a discrete service category anywhere in the Geneva market.

**4. AI Convention Deployer Mapping for Boutique Scale**
No market participant has published a Geneva consulting-specific mapping of AI Convention deployer obligations for boutiques with EU client nexus. The specific dual-compliance challenge — nFADP + AI Convention — operating simultaneously on the same AI-assisted delivery practice is unnamed in the market. No proportionate methodology exists for boutique-scale AI Convention compliance.

**5. Deliverable Provenance Architecture for Boutique Mandate Books**
No market participant offers a discrete service for managing deliverable version provenance across active client mandates as AI model versions update and retire. The specific problem — active deliverables produced under model versions that no longer exist, the firm unable to reproduce or verify what was delivered — is not addressed as a discrete service category anywhere in the Geneva market.

**6. Geneva Consulting-Specific AI Governance Standard Evaluation**
No market participant offers a service for evaluating whether externally-imposed AI governance standards (vendor frameworks, carrier checklists, industry association self-assessments) are appropriate for a specific boutique's practice, client mix, and risk profile. The structural problem — adopting someone else's standard as operative benchmark without boutique-calibrated evaluation — is unnamed in the market.

---

### Competitive Positioning Implications

**The Geneva consulting accountability vacuum is real and structural.** Every previous competitive confirmation (C287–C291 Phase 03) confirmed the same finding: no Geneva market participant is serving this ICP with proportionate, boutique-scale, Geneva-specific, personal-liability-focused AI governance advisory.

The six white spaces above represent specific unnamed service categories — each a discrete deliverable, each a logical engagement entry point, each with a clear client problem as the trigger.

**The non-traditional player displacement risk is the new competitive threat.** The most acute competitive risk is not another advisory firm entering the space. It is vendors, carriers, and industry bodies defining "adequate AI governance" for Geneva consulting firms through their own frameworks — and those frameworks becoming operative benchmarks without boutique evaluation. The firm that helps Geneva consulting firms evaluate and respond to externally-imposed standards — on their own terms — occupies a structurally different and more defensible position than firms simply publishing their own frameworks.
