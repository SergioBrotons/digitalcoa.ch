## The Documentation Gap No One Talks About

The conversation about AI in professional practice is full of frameworks, guidance, and principles. "Follow best practices." "Use AI responsibly." "Maintain professional standards."

What no one talks about is **the evidence**.

When the carrier asks whether you supervise AI tools adequately — they don't want to see your policy. They want to see what you actually documented about how you supervised those tools, for which matters, and what you found when you reviewed the output.

That's the gap. Not the policy gap. The evidence gap.

## What I Learned About Vendor Contracts

I didn't think about vendor contracts when I started using AI tools. The tool was "internal." It was just me, using it to assist with my work.

Then I realized: the tool processes everything on the vendor's servers. Not mine. The vendor can access it. Their sub-processors can access it. And the vendor's terms of service? I clicked through them. Like everyone does.

I went back and tried to read them. The data retention clause. The audit rights. The jurisdiction clause. I had accepted all of it — without knowing whether any of it conflicts with what my professional obligations require of me.

**I didn't know I was supposed to have reviewed the vendor contract against my professional secrecy, confidentiality, and data protection obligations. No one told me. The vendor certainly didn't.**
