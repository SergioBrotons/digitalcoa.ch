## How They Talk Across Verticals

The following language patterns hold across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs in Geneva. These are the phrases they use — not how we describe their situation, but how they articulate it.

---

## Universal Language Patterns

### On Accountability Ownership
- "I'm responsible for something I can't actually control."
- "I signed it. That means it's mine."
- "The tool made the decision — I just signed off on it."
- "I don't know what my team is actually using."
- "I'm the one with the license. That means I'm the one who's accountable."
- "The vendor says their system is secure. But I'm the one who has to explain it if something goes wrong."

### On Documentation and Evidence
- "We have policies. I don't know if they count as evidence."
- "The carrier asked for documentation. We produced documentation. I don't know if it's the right documentation."
- "If I'm audited, what do I show them?"
- "I have records of what we decided. I don't have records of what the AI did."
- "We built a documentation system. Then we realized it was built for the wrong thing."
- "The record shows a human decision. It doesn't show what the AI contributed."

### On Vendor and Delegation Gaps
- "We signed the vendor's contract. I didn't read it."
- "The vendor says the data is processed securely. I have no way to verify that."
- "My sub-consultant uses AI. I'm accountable for their work."
- "The tool updated. I didn't know. It started doing something new."
- "The AI interpretation came before I did. I don't know how to document that."
- "My patient got an AI-generated report. It has my name on it."

### On Multi-Authority Confusion
- "The carrier wants one thing. The Ordre wants something different. I don't know what satisfies both."
- "FINMA says adequate. Nobody defines what adequate means."
- "The Swiss regulation says one thing. The EU AI Act says another. We do EU work."
- "We have documents for the client. We have documents for the carrier. They don't talk to each other."
- "I got a questionnaire from our biggest client. We didn't know how to answer it."
- "We responded to the RFP. The AI governance section — we had to leave it blank."

### On the Decision Trigger
- "I signed the carrier form. Then I thought about what I actually certified."
- "My client sent me an AI governance questionnaire. I realized we didn't have answers."
- "A colleague got a claim. I started looking at our carrier attestation."
- "I found out what the tool was actually doing with client data."
- "The OFL hasn't said anything. I assumed that meant we were fine. I'm not sure that's right."
- "I know we have to do something. I don't know what to do first."

### On the Action Phase
- "We're not in discovery anymore. We need to move."
- "What do I actually need to have in place — not conceptually, but on paper?"
- "I have six things hitting me at once. I need to know the sequence."
- "I need something that works for a five-person firm, not a five-hundred-person firm."
- "Give me the sequence. Start with what matters most."
- "I need to be able to show something. Not just know something."

## Universal ICP Decision Trigger Phrase

**EN:** "You've already figured out that this is real. You signed something. You know the tools are in use. You know the gap is there. Now the question is what to do about it — and in what order."

**FR:** "Vous avez déjà compris que c'est réel. Vous avez signé quelque chose. Vous savez que les outils sont utilisés. Vous savez que l'écart existe. Maintenant, la question est de savoir quoi faire — et dans quel ordre."

## Key Positioning Language (Universal Across Verticals)

1. **The evidence gap:** "You have documents. You don't have evidence. The carrier wants evidence."

2. **The personal exposure:** "Your signature is personal. The liability is personal. Your firm's policies protect the firm — they don't protect you."

3. **The delegation trap:** "You can't stop delegating. You can't review every output. But you can document the structure — and that's what protects you."

4. **The sequencing problem:** "You know you need to act. You don't know what to do first. The sequence matters more than the comprehensiveness."

5. **The vendor contract gap:** "The vendor's contract defines what you're accountable for. You signed it without reading it. That signature is part of your professional accountability structure."

6. **The proportionate solution:** "Everything on the market is built for larger firms. You're five people. The solution has to fit how you actually operate."

## Language That Closes (Cross-Vertical)

The following phrases have been tested across verticals and produce the highest engagement response:

- "The carrier's form is not a compliance checklist. It's a legal document with your signature on it."
- "What the vendor contract actually obligates you to — most professionals have never read it."
- "Send me the form before you sign. I'll tell you what your signature commits you to."
- "The documentation that satisfies your carrier may create exposure in a disciplinary proceeding. I can design both so they work together."
- "Before you answer that client questionnaire — you need to know what you're actually certifying."
- "The Geneva accountability ecosystem has two circuits. I work in both."
