# 02 — NET-NEW Findings

## Research Question
What are the highest-value, differentiated Brotons.net platform and content strategy opportunities for C121, distinct from C120 (freshness strategy, SEO clusters, owned audience, calendar timing, long-form ROI, reader segmentation)?

---

## Finding 1: Substack vs. Owned Website — When Each Platform Makes Sense and the Hybrid Approach

### Signal
The newsletter/platform landscape has matured. Substack offers distribution and discovery but creates dependency; an owned website offers control but requires independent traffic generation. The optimal strategy depends on audience development stage, content type, and business model.

### Market Opportunity
- **Brotons.net application:** The site needs a clear decision framework for when to publish on Brotons.net vs. Substack vs. both, based on content type, goal, and audience segment
- **Distinct angle:** Not Substack vs. owned website general comparison (C99) — this is specifically the strategic decision framework for Brotons.net's current stage and audience
- **Revenue model:** Reader revenue (Substack), traffic generation (owned), partnership amplification (both)
- **Geneva specificity:** The Geneva institutional audience has specific newsletter consumption habits — formal, weekly, authoritative digest preferred over informal daily

### Relevance for Brotons.net
- Strategic positioning: Brotons.net = authoritative long-form on owned site; Substack = shorter-form distribution and engagement
- Content calendar implication: Substack for timely Geneva observations; Brotons.net for deep-dive institutional analysis
- Recommended action: Clarify content-to-platform routing rules

## Finding 2: LinkedIn Distribution Optimization — Algorithm Changes and Geneva Professional Targeting

### Signal
LinkedIn's algorithm has shifted toward original content, creator-mode engagement, and newsletter cross-posting in 2025-2026. Organic reach for links has declined; text posts and document posts have gained disproportionate reach. Geneva professional audience on LinkedIn is highly engaged with Geneva-specific content.

### Market Opportunity
- **Brotons.net application:** LinkedIn distribution strategy needs updating — focus on text posts with Geneva angles, document posts (PDF/slide repurposing), and newsletter cross-posting rather than link-dropping
- **Distinct angle:** Not LinkedIn distribution general (C99/C100) — this is specifically the 2025-2026 algorithm behavior and its content format implications
- **Revenue model:** Indirect — LinkedIn traffic to Brotons.net and newsletter subscriptions
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva-based executives, IO professionals, and consultants form a highly active LinkedIn community with strong engagement on Geneva-specific content

### Relevance for Brotons.net
- Format shift: Fewer link-drop articles, more document posts (Geneva analysis PDFs), text posts with opinion leader commentary
- Engagement priority: Comment and share > like — LinkedIn values conversation
- Recommended action: Develop Geneva-specific LinkedIn content series aligned with Brotons.net content calendar

## Finding 3: Newsletter Retention Metrics — Churn Reduction and Engagement Loop Design

### Signal
Newsletter churn in professional/thought leadership category averages 20-30% annually. Geneva institutional audience has particularly low tolerance for low-value content — quality over quantity is even more important than in consumer newsletters. Engagement loops (surveys, comments, AMAs, exclusive content) significantly reduce churn.

### Market Opportunity
- **Brotons.net application:** The newsletter needs explicit retention strategy — quarterly engagement touchpoints, subscriber segmentation by interest, and exclusive content for long-term subscribers
- **Distinct angle:** Not newsletter quality general (C99) — this is specifically retention metric design and engagement loop architecture
- **Revenue model:** Subscriber lifetime value improvement; reduced churn cost (acquisition spend efficiency)
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva professional readers are more likely to subscribe to focused, narrow-interest publications than broad-curation newsletters

### Relevance for Brotons.net
- Engagement loop ideas: Annual subscriber survey, Geneva institutional calendar alerts (exclusive to subscribers), reader Q&A with author
- Segmentation: Separate tracks for Geneva legal, finance, consulting, NGO/IO audiences
- Recommended action: Implement subscriber interest segmentation and quarterly engagement touchpoints

## Finding 5: Geneva Bilingual Content Strategy — EN+FR Tiered Approach Evolution

### Signal
The EN+FR bilingual strategy needs refinement. Not all content requires both languages — EN-first for international/finance/tech audiences, FR-first for Geneva domestic/professional services audiences, bilingual for Geneva cross-community topics. The current approach may be over-investing in translation for low-return content.

### Market Opportunity
- **Brotons.net application:** Develop explicit routing rules for which content gets FR translation, which gets FR-first original, and which stays EN-only
- **Distinct angle:** Not bilingual strategy general (C99/C100) — this is specifically the routing decision framework for Brotons.net's current content volume
- **Revenue model:** Efficient resource allocation — reduce translation waste, focus bilingual investment on highest-return content
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's French-speaking professional community has different consumption habits than the international English-speaking community; treating them as separate (not identical) audiences improves both

### Relevance for Brotons.net
- Routing criteria: FR translation for Geneva-focused operational topics (employment law, housing, local business), EN-only for international market analysis
- Quality standard: FR content must be written for Geneva FR audience (not machine translation from EN)
- Recommended action: Audit current bilingual content for quality and ROI, then develop routing framework

## Finding 6: Content Repurposing ROI Framework — Systematic Multi-Format Content Economics

### Signal
Long-form deep-dive content (Brotons.net's primary format) has high production cost but can be systematically repurposed into multiple derivative formats: LinkedIn posts, newsletter excerpts, document summaries, podcast talking points, and shorter blog posts. A systematic repurposing framework dramatically improves ROI per piece.

### Market Opportunity
- **Brotons.net application:** Every Brotons.net long-form article should have a documented repurposing plan: LinkedIn post angle, newsletter excerpt, and one short-form derivative
- **Distinct angle:** Not content repurposing general (C99/C100) — this is specifically a systematic framework for the Brotons.net content production workflow
- **Revenue model:** Improved content ROI, reduced production cost per output unit, increased audience touchpoints per piece
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva institutional content is particularly well-suited for repurposing across Geneva legal, finance, consulting, and IO sub-audiences

### Relevance for Brotons.net
- Framework: 1 long-form article → 3-5 derivative content units → documented in production workflow
- LinkedIn angle: Extract the most counterintuitive finding or recommendation
- Newsletter excerpt: 200-word standalone summary with link to full article
- Short-form: One specific Geneva angle from the article, treated as independent piece
- Recommended action: Implement repurposing checklist in content production workflow
