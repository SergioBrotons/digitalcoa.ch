## Swiss Wealth Management Market — Context

### Market Size & Scope
- Switzerland remains the world's largest cross-border wealth management center (~$2.4T in AUM)
- Geneva specifically: ~CHF 400–500B in private banking assets
- Boutique and small private banks (≤CHF 20B AUM) represent a significant share of the ecosystem
- Consolidation trend: larger banks absorbing smaller players; independent boutiques face margin pressure
- **AI adoption (FINMA survey, Nov 2024–Jan 2025):** ~50% of ~400 Swiss financial institutions surveyed are already using or developing AI applications; **91% of those adopters use generative AI** (e.g., chatbots)

### Key Trends (2025–2026)

**1. FINMA AI Governance Guidance — Critical Development**
- **FINMA Guidance 08/2024** published December 18, 2024: "Governance and risk management when using artificial intelligence"
- Four primary risk areas: (1) governance and responsibility, (2) robustness and reliability, (3) explainability, (4) discrimination and bias
- Technology-neutral, principle-based approach — existing FINMA requirements for sound organization apply to AI
- Since Q4 2023: FINMA conducting on-site inspections and supervisory dialogues with AI-adopting institutions
- **2025 outlook:** FINMA will refine expectations via further guidance or circulars; institutions using AI in critical processes should contact FINMA in advance
- **Implication for boutiques:** Even small FINMA-licensed firms are now in scope; no specific SMB exemption

**2. nFADP Compliance Reality (effective September 1, 2023)**
- Personal data of natural persons only (not legal entities — change from prior law)
- Strengthened data subject rights: access, rectification, deletion, portability
- Privacy by Design and Privacy by Default requirements
- Data breach notification: must notify FDPIC; banks must coordinate with FINMA
- Sensitive data (genetic, biometric) requires explicit consent
- Dual compliance: Swiss firms with EU clients must meet both nFADP AND GDPR
- Penalties: up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations; individuals can be personally sanctioned
- **Implication for boutiques:** Even small firms processing client financial data need ROPA, DPIA processes, documented consent

**3. Digital Disruption at the Boutique Level**
- Legacy core banking systems (Avaloq, Finnova) are enterprise-only; smaller firms use SaaS/PaaS alternatives
- Emergence of wealth management SaaS: Domalys, QBIS, private-label portfolio management tools
- AI-assisted client reporting and document drafting tools gaining traction among mid-tier wealth managers
- Cybersecurity: FINMA and Swiss Fed Council issued new guidance (2025); cyber risk is a board-level concern
- Cross-border data transfer restrictions affecting Swiss firms using cloud/AI SaaS with non-Swiss providers

**4. Client Expectation Shift**
- UHNW/HNW clients expect digital portals, real-time reporting, encrypted communication
- Next-gen inheritors (younger) demand tech-native interfaces and ESG integration
- Discretion remains paramount — privacy and data sovereignty non-negotiable
- AI-generated communications must be disclosed under FINMA guidance

**5. Talent & Competition**
- Boutique firms struggle to attract tech-savvy talent; compete with Big4 on compensation
- Growing demand for "wealth tech" roles even in boutiques
- Succession challenge: many boutiques founded 1980s–1990s face founder-exit planning; digital assets are part of valuation

## Geneva-Specific Signals

### Geneva Finance Ecosystem
- Strong presence of family offices and multi-family offices (Geneva: 60+ family offices estimated)
- Private banking clusters: Rue du Rhône, Place du Bourg-de-Four, La Ville, Champod
- CCIG (Chambre du commerce, de l'industrie et des services de Genève) active; year-round networking
- Swiss Banking School, Université de Genève: continuing education in wealth management
- Key events: Geneva Private Banking Forum, AHV (Swiss Banking Association) events

### Boutique Digital Adoption (Observational)
- Typical: website + LinkedIn company page + occasional newsletter
- Less common at micro-boutique level: client portals, AI tools, advanced CRM, automated reporting
- Significant gap between stated digital strategy and actual deployment at 2–10 person firms
- Hiring for "digital transformation" or "technology coordinator" roles is a clear adoption signal

## Competitive Tech Landscape (Wealth Management SaaS/AI)

### Portfolio Management & Reporting
- **Domalys** — Cloud-based portfolio management for Swiss wealth managers
- **QBIS** — Portfolio management system; Swiss-focused
- **Avaloq** (Enterprise only) — Core banking for larger private banks
- **Finnova** (Enterprise only) — Core banking for Swiss and Liechtenstein banks

### Client Relationship Management
- **Salesforce Financial Services Cloud** — Enterprise CRM for wealth management
- **Dynamix** — Swiss-focused wealth management CRM
- **HubSpot** (SMB) —通用 CRM; often used by smaller boutiques without dedicated WM software

### AI Tools (Emerging in Swiss WM)
- **External AI SaaS tools** — Majority of Swiss AI adopters (91%) use external/generative AI; vendor due diligence is a compliance requirement
- **AI document analysis** — Contract review, legal document processing (subject to confidentiality obligations)
- **Automated client reporting** — Performance reporting automation (data governance requirements apply)
- **Robo-advisory** — Subject to specific FINMA licensing requirements; less common at boutique level
