## Swiss Wealth Management Market — Context

### Market Size & Scope
- Switzerland remains the world's largest cross-border wealth management center (~$2.4T in assets under management)
- Geneva specifically: ~CHF 400–500B in private banking assets
- Boutique and small private banks (≤CHF 20B AUM) represent a significant share of the ecosystem
- Consolidation trend: larger banks absorbing smaller players; independent boutiques face margin pressure

### Key Trends (2025–2026)

**1. Digital Disruption at the Boutique Level**
- Legacy core banking systems (Avaloq, Finnova) are expensive to maintain; smaller firms cannot afford full deployments
- Emergence of SaaS/PaaS alternatives for portfolio management (Domalys, QBIS, private-label solutions)
- AI-assisted client reporting and document drafting tools gaining traction among mid-tier wealth managers
- Cybersecurity concerns rising: SEC, FINMA, and Swiss Fed Council all issued new guidance (2025)

**2. Regulatory Intensification**
- FINMA's revised circular on operational risk and governance (2025)
- AEOI (Automatic Exchange of Information) expansion — more jurisdictions, more reporting
- Swiss Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) revisions: increased KYC documentation for beneficial owners
- CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive) extending to financial sector — ESG data governance requirements emerging
- Cross-border data transfer regulations affecting how Swiss firms handle client data with cloud providers

**3. Client Expectation Shift**
- UHNW/HNW clients increasingly expect digital portals, real-time reporting, encrypted communication
- Younger inheritors (next-gen) demand tech-native interfaces and ESG integration
- Discretion remains paramount — privacy and data sovereignty are non-negotiable

**4. Talent & Competition**
- Boutique firms struggle to attract tech-savvy talent; compete with Big4 and large banks on compensation
- Growing demand for "wealth tech" roles (AI/data analysts, digital transformation leads) even in boutiques
- Succession challenge: many boutiques founded in 1980s–1990s face founder-exit planning

## Geneva-Specific Signals

### Geneva Finance Ecosystem
- Strong presence of family offices and multi-family offices (Geneva: 60+ family offices)
- Private banking clusters around Rue du Rhône,Place du Bourg-de-Four, La Ville
- Chamber of Commerce (CCIG) active in promoting Geneva finance; networking events year-round
- Swiss Banking School, Université de Genève offer continuing education in wealth management

### Notable Boutique Players (2–20 advisors)
- Private banks with <CHF 10B AUM:bardividend,gonet & cie, lauranne-boutique equivalents
- Multi-family offices:蜂巢,geneva family office network
- Sector associations: Swiss Banking Association (SBA),Asset Management Association Switzerland (AMAS)

### Digital Adoption Observations
- Geneva boutiques typically: website, LinkedIn, occasional newsletter
- Less common: client portals, AI tools, advanced CRM, automated reporting
- Gap between stated digital strategy and actual deployment is significant in this segment
