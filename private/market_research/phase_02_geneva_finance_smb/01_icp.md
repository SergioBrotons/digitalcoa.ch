# Phase 02 — ICP: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management

## Vertical: Finance / SMB — Boutique Wealth Management Firms

## Target Profile

### Firm Characteristics
- **Headquarters:** Geneva (Rue du Rhône, La Ville, Champod, acacias)
- **Size:** 2–20 employees; typically 2–5 advisors
- **AUM range:** CHF 100M–2B (small boutique)
- **Structure:** SARL, Sàrl, or small SA; often family-owned or founder-led
- **Clients served:** UHNW and HNW individuals, families, Swiss resident executives
- **Regulatory:** FINMA-licensed or affiliated; subject to Swiss AML/KYC obligations

### Decision-Maker Profile — Primary: Managing Director / Founder
- 45–65 years old
- Legal or economics background; may hold CFA or legal certification
- Operates as sole final decision-maker or with 1–2 partners
- Digitally aware but not necessarily tech-native
- Time-poor; values efficiency and credibility
- Primary concern: client retention, regulatory compliance, AUM growth

### Decision-Maker Profile — Secondary: Operations / Compliance Lead
- 35–55 years old
- Handles day-to-day compliance, reporting, client onboarding
- More likely to engage with tools, process documentation
- Pain points: KYC refresh cycles, regulatory reporting, data handling
- Influences recommendations upward to Managing Director

## Key Signals

### Triggers for Outreach
- Published article or comment on wealth management trends, family office topics
- Announcement of new client segment (e.g., crypto-adjacent, ESG)
- Hiring activity indicating growth (wealth advisors, client services)
- Participation in Swiss finance events (Geneva Private Banking Forum, AHV)
- New website or branding refresh indicating strategic repositioning

### Buying Indicators
- Explicit mention of "digital transformation," "client portal," or "process automation"
- Job postings for "digital transformation," "technology coordinator," or CRM/advisory software
- Content on alternative assets, ESG investing, or cross-border estate planning
- References to "modernization" or "efficiency" on firm website

## Needs & Pain Points

### Top 3 Pain Points
1. **Client data governance** — Managing structured + unstructured client data across multiple systems (CRM, portfolio management, document management); GDPR/FinSa compliance for client data
2. **Regulatory reporting burden** — Increasing AML/KYC documentation requirements; FATCA/AEOI reporting; keeping pace with FINMA guidance
3. **Client communication at scale** — Personalized outreach for AUM reporting, market updates, estate planning touchpoints; maintaining personal relationship digitally

### Underlying Tension
Boutique firms compete on personal relationships and discretion. They fear that digital tools (AI, automation) could compromise the human-centered brand they sell. Adoption barriers are cultural as much as technical.

## Qualification Questions (for conversation)
1. How do you currently manage client document lifecycles?
2. What does your KYC refresh process look like annually?
3. How are you communicating portfolio performance to clients currently?
4. What does "digital" mean to your firm today?
5. How are you preparing for increased regulatory scrutiny in Swiss private banking?
