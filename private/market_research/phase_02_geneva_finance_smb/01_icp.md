## Target Profile

### Firm Characteristics
- **Headquarters:** Geneva (Rue du Rhône, La Ville, Champod, Acacias districts)
- **Size:** 2–20 employees; typically 2–5 advisors; some larger at 10–20
- **AUM range:** CHF 50M–2B (small boutique); some mid-tier at CHF 2–15B
- **Structure:** SARL, Sàrl, or small SA; often family-owned or founder-led
- **Clients served:** UHNW and HNW individuals, families, Swiss resident executives, some international
- **Regulatory:** FINMA-licensed or affiliated via SRO (VQF, FINIA); subject to Swiss AML/KYC obligations

### Primary Decision-Maker: Managing Director / Founder
- 45–65 years old
- Legal or economics background; may hold CFA, CIIA, or legal certification
- Sole final decision-maker or with 1–2 partners
- Digitally aware but not necessarily tech-native
- Time-poor; values efficiency and credibility
- Primary concern: client retention, regulatory compliance, AUM growth, succession planning

### Secondary: Operations / Compliance Lead
- 35–55 years old
- Handles day-to-day compliance, reporting, client onboarding
- More likely to engage with tools and process documentation
- Pain points: KYC refresh cycles, regulatory reporting, data handling
- Influences recommendations upward to Managing Director

## Needs & Pain Points (Validated)

### Top 3 Pain Points
1. **Client data governance** — Managing structured + unstructured client data across multiple systems (CRM, portfolio management, document management); nFADP compliance for sensitive financial and health data
2. **Regulatory reporting burden** — Increasing AML/KYC documentation; AEOI/FATCA reporting cycles; keeping pace with FINMA Guidance 08/2024 on AI governance; SRO compliance
3. **Client communication at scale** — Personalized outreach for AUM reporting, market updates, estate planning touchpoints; maintaining personal relationship while scaling

### Underlying Tension
Boutique firms compete on personal relationships and discretion. They fear that digital tools (AI, automation) could compromise the human-centered brand they sell. Adoption barriers are cultural as much as technical. Any AI offering must preserve the human relationship narrative.

### Emerging Pain Points (post-2024)
- **AI governance uncertainty** — FINMA Guidance 08/2024 creates new compliance expectation; boutiques don't know what a "proportionate AI governance framework" looks like
- **nFADP + GDPR dual compliance** — Managing clients who are EU citizens requires both frameworks; documentation burden doubled
- **Succession planning** — Many boutiques founded 1980s–1990s face founder-exit; digital assets and client data transitions are complex

## Qualification Questions (for conversation)
1. How do you currently manage client document lifecycles and data across your systems?
2. What does your KYC refresh process look like annually — where does time go?
3. How are you communicating portfolio performance to clients currently?
4. What does "digital" mean to your firm today — internal tools, client-facing, or both?
5. Have you seen FINMA's guidance on AI risk management (Guidance 08/2024)? What does compliance look like at your scale?
6. Are you preparing for any succession or ownership transition in the next 3–5 years?
