## The Five Pre-Pause Answer Patterns — What Did Not Change During the Pause

The lead magnet structure makes the competitive gap visible by enumerating the five pre-pause answer patterns that did not change during the pause, against the post-pause question type that did change during the pause. The structural mismatch is the C313 competitive gap DigitalCoa addresses.

### Pattern 1: The Vendor's Pre-Pause Term of Service (Did Not Change)

**Pre-pause:** "Tool documentation. Vendor certification. Marketing claim about Swiss-hosted data residency. Sub-processor chain undisclosed. Vendor succession terms silent on professional accountability continuity."

**Post-pause question:** "Which AI tools are you using on each matter / mandate / engagement / encounter between January 1 and June 30, 2026? For each tool, what is the per-tool sub-processor chain (authentication / telemetry / model inference / backup / parent-company cloud)? What are the vendor succession terms if the vendor files for bankruptcy, is acquired, or terminates Swiss service? Does the succession terms address the professional accountability continuity gap (Article 321 CC / Article 9 / Article 52 / Article 321 CP + Article 63)?"

**The mismatch:** The vendor's pre-pause term of service provides tool-level documentation, vendor certification, and a marketing claim. The post-pause question asks for per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure, vendor succession planning with professional accountability continuity, and per-tool authorization documentation. The vendor's pre-pause term of service does not address any of these. The vendor's term is now structurally off-disclosure.

**The C313 service:** Per-tool sub-processor chain audit against the professional accountability framework, with vendor succession planning terms added to vendor contracts before the next succession event.

### Pattern 2: The Framework Consultant's Pre-Pause Policy Document (Did Not Change)

**Pre-pause:** "AI governance framework. Responsible AI principles. Risk management framework. Policy document. Tool-level disclosure format. Annual review cycle. Pre-pause forward-looking documentation."

**Post-pause question:** "For each matter / mandate / engagement / encounter between January 1 and June 30, 2026, what was the specific encounter-level evidence of AI use? What was the per-encounter consent form? What was the per-encounter supervising professional authorization? What was the per-encounter evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions? Show me the documentation dated within the prior six months for the mid-year check-in. Show me the documentation that addresses the named-peer benchmark from the June 2026 Geneva conference circuit."

**The mismatch:** The framework consultant's pre-pause policy document addresses the pre-pause question (tool-level disclosure, framework compliance). The post-pause question requires encounter-level evidence with authorization chain documentation. The framework consultant's policy document is now structurally off-specification. The consultant's annual deliverable cannot refresh at the multi-per-period cadence the post-pause question requires.

**The C313 service:** Post-pause specification conversion — converting the pre-pause framework to post-pause encounter-level evidence architecture, with multi-per-period documentation cadence matching the post-pause audit cadence.

### Pattern 3: The Broker's Pre-Pause Annual Renewal (Did Not Change)

**Pre-pause:** "Annual carrier attestation. Annual renewal. Tool-level disclosure form. Pre-pause deliverable structure. Bilingual EN/FR renewal support."

**Post-pause question:** "Mid-year carrier check-in arriving three weeks after I returned from the pause. Asks for encounter-level evidence per matter between January 1 and June 30, 2026, with per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure, per-tool authorization documentation, and named-peer benchmark readiness. The broker's annual renewal deliverable does not address any of this. The broker's renewal process does not include a mid-year check-in service."

**The mismatch:** The broker's pre-pause annual renewal process addresses the pre-pause question (annual carrier attestation). The post-pause mid-year carrier check-in is structurally new. The broker's annual deliverable cannot be reused for the mid-year check-in without redesign. The broker's bilingual EN/FR support extends to the annual renewal — not the mid-year cycle.

**The C313 service:** Mid-year check-in support — bilingual EN/FR — calibrated to the post-pause question type, with the broker as the channel for both the annual renewal and the mid-year check-in support.

### Pattern 4: The Trainer's Pre-Pause Workshop Slides (Did Not Change)

**Pre-pause:** "AI governance principles. Awareness slides. Pre-pause regulatory landscape. Pre-pause case law / regulatory enforcement summary. Knowledge transfer as deliverable. Annual workshop cycle. Annual continuing education credits (CLE, CME)."

**Post-pause question:** "I attended the workshop last year. I understand the principles. I still cannot answer the post-pause question. The question requires evidence records the workshop did not prepare me to produce. The workshop's next session is in Q4 2026 — the next attestation cycle is in August 2026."

**The mismatch:** The trainer's pre-pause workshop delivers awareness. The post-pause question requires evidence records. The trainer's workshop pre-pause is structurally off-evidence. The annual workshop cycle cannot refresh at the post-pause multi-per-period cadence.

**The C313 service:** Named-peer benchmark monitoring — mapping the C313 ICP's documentation against the named-peer benchmark published at Geneva professional conferences in June 2026, with named-peer case study adaptation (consent-adapted template for the ICP's vertical and practice structure).

### Pattern 5: The Lawyer's Pre-Pause Legal Opinion (Did Not Change)

**Pre-pause:** "Legal opinion on AI governance obligations. Regulatory interpretation. Hourly-billed legal advice. Privilege protection. Descriptive deliverable."

**Post-pause question:** "I have the legal opinion. The legal opinion describes what the law says. The post-pause question requires evidence records. The legal opinion is descriptive, not evidentiary. The legal opinion's deliverable structure (legal opinion document) does not address the post-pause attestation question. The legal opinion was not produced to identify the AI tool used to generate it."

**The mismatch:** The lawyer's pre-pause legal opinion is descriptive of the regulatory framework. The post-pause question requires evidence records of what the ICP actually did with AI. The legal opinion's deliverable structure (description) is now structurally off-evidence-record. The legal opinion's evidentiary foundation is the AI the post-pause question asks about.

**The C313 service:** Post-pause attestation loop closure audit — auditing the AI tool used to prepare the post-pause attestation response, evaluating whether the response becomes generic (because every professional using the same workflow produces similar language), producing a remediation plan for an attestation response anchored to vertical-specific evidence.
