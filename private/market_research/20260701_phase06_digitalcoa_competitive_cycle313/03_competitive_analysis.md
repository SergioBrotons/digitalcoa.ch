## C313 Post-Pause Competitive Landscape

The 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) shifted the accountability channels in every Geneva vertical simultaneously — and the competitive landscape added a new structural dimension that no competitor has yet addressed. The pre-pause competitive map (eight categories of competitors — framework vendors, compliance consultancies, technology vendors, brokers, training programs, legal firms, enterprise platforms, conference circuit publications) is intact. The post-pause competitive map adds: the post-pause universal evidence architecture category, where the cross-vertical evidence architecture methodology crosses every vertical with vertical-specific calibration, in the Q4 (post-pause universal) quadrant of the competitive map.

This file documents the full C313 competitive landscape, the white space that emerged during the pause, and the response scenarios for each competitor category.

## What Exists Across Verticals — Pre-Pause Categories (All Still Operating)

### Category 1: AI Governance Framework Vendors (International / Francophone)

**Examples (representative):** Large international AI governance consultancies offering AI governance frameworks, Responsible AI principles, AI risk management frameworks, ethics guidelines, AI policy development.

**What they offer:** Generic policy documents, process descriptions, training materials, organization-level documentation, English-only content, pre-pause forward-looking documentation only, one-size-fits-all templates.

**Structural limitation (C312):** Their documentation architecture was designed for the pre-pause question type. The C312 question was tool-level (do you use AI?). Their deliverables (policy documents, framework templates) do not address encounter-level evidence.

**Structural limitation (C313 post-pause):** Every pre-pause framework deliverable is now structurally off-specification for the C313 post-pause question. The framework is a policy document. The post-pause question requires encounter-level evidence. The framework vendor cannot retrofit the policy document to produce encounter-level evidence. The retrofit is a category change.

**C313 ICP experience:** The Geneva SMB ICP who purchased a framework vendor's deliverable in 2025 is discovering the deliverable does not address the 2026 post-pause question type. The framework vendor's customer success team is responding with "the framework still applies." The ICP is discovering the framework does not apply.

**Response scenario (Q4 2026 / Q1 2027):** The framework vendor rebrands as "post-pause AI governance." Adds an encounter-level evidence module. The module is a bolted-on consultancy service (engagement-by-engagement, expensive, multi-week delivery). The category is repositioned but the delivery model is consulting (not evidence production). DigitalCoa's Q4 (post-pause universal) position remains unoccupied.

**C313 white space this category creates:** Framework vendors operate at Q2 (generic + framework) of the post-pause map. The Q4 (post-pause universal) category remains empty. The framework vendors' retrofit creates Q2.5 (consulting-services-attached-to-framework) — a transitional category. The Q4 (post-pause universal) category belongs to whoever publishes the cross-vertical evidence architecture first.

## The C313 Post-Pause White Space — What's Missing Across All Verticals

### Missing Capability 01: Post-Pause Specification Conversion Service

**What the pre-pause market offers:** Forward-looking documentation (framework / policy / compliance package) calibrated to pre-pause question type (tool-level).

**What's missing post-pause:** A service that maps the ICP's pre-pause documentation against the post-pause question type (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level), identifies the structural mismatches between pre-pause deliverable and post-pause requirement, and produces a post-pause-calibrated documentation architecture that answers the post-pause question.

**Why it's missing:** The post-pause question type is structurally new. Every pre-pause competitor built for the pre-pause question type. The retrofit from pre-pause to post-pause is a category change — not a feature add. No competitor has the documentation architecture to deliver the retrofit.

**The position to claim:** The only service in the Geneva market that maps pre-pause documentation against the post-pause question type, identifies structural mismatches, and produces post-pause-calibrated documentation architecture.

### Missing Capability 02: Multi-Per-Period Documentation Refresh Service

**What the pre-pause market offers:** Annual documentation review and update.

**What's missing post-pause:** Documentation refresh calibrated to post-pause multi-per-period cadence (annual + mid-year + quarterly + monthly depending on vertical and channel). Triggered at each cadence, delivered at each freshness standard required (6 months for mid-year check-in, 3 months for institutional follow-up, 3 months for engagement follow-up).

The Geneva ICPs who returned from the pause discovered the new question type simultaneously.

The Geneva competitors who were noticing the pause discovered the new question type simultaneously.

**The C313 first-mover window is now closing at a measurable pace:**

- **Q3 2026 (July–September):** The first post-pause attestation cycles complete across all four verticals. The Geneva ICPs who fail the C313 question type will publish their failures publicly (carrier disputes, DDQ rejections, RFP non-renewals, medical liability coverage gaps). Legal firms will offer "attestation loop closure" services. Technology vendors will add "sub-processor chain audit" features. Insurance brokers will add "named-peer benchmark monitoring" to their service offerings.
