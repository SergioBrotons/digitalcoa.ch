## The Post-Pause Competitive Landscape — Claims vs. Delivery

The 31-day pause between C312 (May 31) and C313 (July 1) was not a market pause. It was a market shift masked as a pause. While Geneva professionals and Geneva competitors were not actively attending to AI accountability, every accountability channel in every Geneva vertical restructured its question from tool-level to encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence with multi-per-period cadence, authorization chain split, sub-processor chain disclosure, named-peer benchmark direct reading, and vendor succession continuity. The C313 competitive landscape reveals the gap between what competitors claim to deliver (still pre-pause language) and what the post-pause accountability channels actually demand (post-pause question type).

This file documents the post-pause market signals — the structural gaps between competitor offerings and the post-pause C313 requirements. Each gap is a competitive failure mode documented across the Geneva SMB market in C313.

## Signal 01: The Pre-Pause Competitive Set Is Now Off-Specification — Every Competitor Built for the Wrong Question

**What it is:** Every competitor in the Geneva AI accountability space — framework vendors, compliance consultancies, technology vendors, brokers, training programs, legal firms, enterprise platforms — built their product for the pre-pause question type: tool-level disclosure, policy documentation, framework compliance, annual cycle, single authorization event, vendor-hosted marketing claim, vendor succession hypothetical, named-peer inference. The C313 question type — encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence with multi-per-period cadence, authorization chain split, sub-processor chain disclosure, named-peer benchmark direct reading, vendor succession continuity — is structurally new.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** The carrier's C312 attestation asked "Do you use AI tools? List them." The carrier's C313 attestation asks "For each client matter closed between January 1 and June 30, 2026, identify any matter where AI tools were used. For each such matter, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the date and duration of use, (c) the client consent form covering the AI use, (d) the supervising attorney who authorized the AI use, (e) the matter-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." Every framework vendor's C312 deliverable (AI governance policy document) does not address matter-level evidence. The annual cycle deliverable does not match the multi-per-period cadence. The legal opinion does not address the attestation loop closure.
- **Finance:** The institutional client's C312 DDQ asked "Do you use AI tools for portfolio construction? List them." The institutional client's C313 DDQ now asks "For each institutional mandate active between [date range], identify any portfolio decision where AI tools were used. For each such decision, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the mandate agreement authority for AI use, (c) the supervising portfolio manager who authorized the AI use, (d) the mandate-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." Every compliance consultancy's C312 deliverable (annual compliance package) does not address mandate-level evidence. The annual cycle deliverable does not match the mid-year questionnaire cadence. The portfolio construction AI vendor documentation does not address the per-tool sub-processor chain.
- **Consulting:** The commercial client's C312 RFP questionnaire asked "Do you use AI tools for research? List them." The commercial client's C313 RFP follow-up now asks "For each engagement deliverable produced between [date range], identify any deliverable where AI tools were used. For each each such deliverable, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the engagement letter authority for AI use, (c) the supervising consultant who authorized the AI use, (d) the engagement-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." Every training program's C312 deliverable (AI governance training materials) does not address engagement-level evidence. The pre-engagement vendor documentation does not address mid-engagement follow-up readiness. The proposal management vendor documentation does not address vendor succession.
- **Healthcare:** The medical liability carrier's C312 attestation asked "Do you use AI tools (scribe, clinical decision support, drug-interaction, image analysis)? List them." The medical liability carrier's C313 attestation now asks "For each patient encounter between January 1 and June 30, 2026, identify any encounter where AI tools were used. For each such encounter, provide: (a) the AI tool(s) used, (b) the patient consent form covering the AI use, (c) the supervising physician who authorized the AI use, (d) the encounter-level evidence of human authorization for AI-assisted decisions." Every framework vendor's C312 deliverable (Responsible AI principles document) does not address encounter-level evidence. The annual consent form does not match the mid-year check-in freshness standard. The EMR-AI extension vendor documentation does not address the HIN corridor residency fallacy.

**The market failure:** Every competitor's product is now off-specification. The pre-pause product category is no longer the post-pause product category. The transformation is not incremental. It is structural. The competitor who built "AI governance framework" pre-pause cannot answer "which decisions did the AI make, with what consent, with what supervision, with what authorization chain, on which specific encounter / matter / mandate / engagement between January 1 and June 30, 2026?" post-pause. The product was designed for the wrong question. The redesign is not a feature add. It is a category change.

**Why this is universal across C313:** The pre-pause question type was the same across verticals (tool-level disclosure). The post-pause question type is the same across verticals (encounter-level / matter-level / mandate-level / engagement-level evidence). Every competitor's pre-pause product is therefore off-specification for every vertical. The transformation is universal — every competitor's product is off-specification across every vertical.

## Signal 03: The Pre-Pause Authorization Chain Documentation Is Now Off-Evidence — Single Event Documentation Does Not Match Post-Pause Split Documentation

**What it is:** The pre-pause authorization chain documentation (employment authorization for legal staff / mandate authorization for finance mandates / engagement authorization for consulting sub-consultants / coverage authorization for médecin remplaçant) was a single event. The post-pause question requires evidence of authorization as two separate events — principal relationship authorization AND AI use authorization as separate documentation events. The pre-pause documentation system was designed for one event. The post-pause question requires two.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** Pre-pause authorization chain documentation: summer associate hires sign an employment contract. The employment contract is the authorization. Post-pause authorization chain documentation: the summer associate signs an employment contract AND signs an AI use authorization as a separate event (the AI use authorization specifies which AI tools are authorized for which matter types, with what consent form, with what supervision record). Pre-pause framework vendor documentation produced an "AI governance framework" that referenced AI use as a policy. Post-pause carrier attestation requires AI use authorization as a documented event per summer associate per matter. The pre-pause framework deliverable is not the post-pause authorization evidence.
- **Finance:** Pre-pause authorization chain documentation: mandate agreement authorized new institutional mandate. The mandate agreement is the authorization. Post-pause: the mandate agreement authorizes the mandate AND the AI use authorization is a separate documentation event (specifying which AI tools are authorized for which mandate decisions, with what mandate agreement authority, with what portfolio construction oversight log). Pre-pause compliance consultancy produced a mandate governance document. Post-pause institutional DDQ requires AI authorization as a documented event per mandate per decision. The pre-pause mandate governance document is not the post-pause authorization evidence.
- **Consulting:** Pre-pause authorization chain documentation: sub-consultant SOW authorized the sub-consultant engagement. The SOW is the authorization. Post-pause: the SOW authorizes the engagement AND the AI use authorization is a separate documentation event (specifying which AI tools the sub-consultant may use, with what engagement letter authority, with what proposal methodology authorization). Pre-pause training program produced "responsible AI use" guidance. Post-pause client RFP follow-up requires sub-consultant AI authorization as a documented event per engagement. The pre-pause training material is not the post-pause authorization evidence.
- **Healthcare:** Pre-pause authorization chain documentation: Convention de remplacement authorized médecin remplaçant coverage. The Convention is the authorization. Post-pause: the Convention authorizes the coverage AND the AI use authorization is a separate documentation event (specifying which AI tools the remplaçant may use, with what patient consent authority, with what supervision log). Pre-pause framework vendor produced "responsible AI for physicians" guidance. Post-pause medical liability carrier attestation requires remplaçant AI authorization as a documented event per coverage period. The pre-pause physician guidance is not the post-pause authorization evidence.

**The market failure:** Every pre-pause authorization chain documentation system was designed for the single-event authorization pattern. The post-pause question requires evidence of authorization chain split (principal relationship authorization AND AI use authorization as separate documentation events). The pre-pause system cannot be retrofitted — it was designed for the wrong event structure. The retrofit is not a feature add. It is a documentation architecture change.

**Why this is universal across C313:** The pre-pause single-event authorization pattern is identical across verticals (employment / mandate / engagement / coverage). The post-pause split documentation requirement is identical across verticals (principal authorization + AI use authorization as separate events). The retrofit challenge is universal — every pre-pause authorization documentation system is structurally off-evidence for the post-pause split documentation requirement in every vertical.

**The service implication:** Post-pause authorization chain split documentation is the discrete, named C313 cross-vertical competitive service. It produces AI use authorization as a separate event from principal relationship authorization, with template documentation for each vertical's principal relationship type (employment contract / mandate agreement / sub-consultant SOW / Convention de remplacement), handover documentation triggers at each principal relationship event (hire date / mandate start / sub-consultant engagement / coverage period start), AI tool inventory updates at each handover (different principal = different AI tools), and authorization chain documentation refresh at each audit cadence. Universal across verticals.

## Signal 04: The Pre-Pause Swiss-Hosted Vendor Marketing Claim Is Now Off-Disclosure — Single-Layer Marketing Does Not Match Post-Pause Sub-Processor Chain Disclosure

**What it is:** Pre-pause vendor marketing addressed the most visible layer of the data processing chain (the vendor's own server, often Swiss-hosted, often certified). The post-pause accountability channel now requires per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure — not the vendor's own server location, but the per-tool sub-processor chain (authentication, telemetry, model inference, backup, parent-company cloud, parent-company sub-processors). The pre-pause marketing claim is structurally one-layer; the post-pause disclosure requirement is structurally multi-layer.

**Cross-vertical confirmation:**
- **Legal:** Pre-pause law firm vendor marketing: "Swiss-hosted, HIN-protected, nFADP-compliant." Post-pause carrier attestation requires: "For each AI tool used in client matters, provide: (a) the vendor's own server location, (b) the authentication sub-processor location, (c) the telemetry sub-processor location, (d) the model inference sub-processor location, (e) the backup sub-processor location, (f) the parent-company cloud location." The pre-pause marketing claim provides one layer (vendor's own server). The post-pause disclosure requires six layers. Every Swiss-hosted vendor's marketing is off-disclosure from the moment the carrier requires per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure.
- **Finance:** Pre-pause wealth management vendor marketing: "Swiss-hosted, FINMA-aligned, nFADP-compliant." Post-pause institutional DDQ requires: "For each AI tool used in mandate decisions, provide the vendor's own server location + the authentication / telemetry / model inference / backup / parent-company cloud sub-processor locations." The pre-pause marketing claim is off-disclosure.
- **Consulting:** Pre-pause consulting vendor marketing: "Swiss-hosted, GDPR-aligned, nFADP-compliant." Post-pause client RFP follow-up requires per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure. The pre-pause marketing claim is off-disclosure.
- **Healthcare:** Pre-pause physician EMR vendor marketing: "Swiss-certified, HIN-protected." Post-pause medical liability carrier attestation requires per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure for the AI extensions (scribe, clinical decision support, drug-interaction, image analysis). The EMR certification covers the EMR software. It does not cover the AI extensions. The AI extensions operate in US-based cloud. The pre-pause EMR certification is off-disclosure.

**The market failure:** Every vendor's marketing claim is structurally one-layer. Every post-pause accountability channel's disclosure requirement is structurally multi-layer. The mismatch is not a feature add. It is a marketing claim obsolescence event. The vendor cannot satisfy the post-pause disclosure with the pre-pause marketing language. The vendor must either redesign the marketing (which is a category change) or commission a per-tool sub-processor chain audit (which most vendors cannot afford to do for every Geneva SMB customer).

**Why this is universal across C313:** Every vertical's accountability channel now requires per-tool sub-processor chain disclosure (the same six-layer structure across verticals). Every vendor in every vertical markets at the one-layer level. The mismatch is universal across verticals.

**The service implication:** Per-tool sub-processor chain audit against the professional accountability framework is the discrete, named C313 cross-vertical competitive service. It maps each vendor's per-tool sub-processor chain against the professional accountability framework (Article 321 CC / Article 9 nFADP / Article 52 nFADP / Article 321 CP medical secrecy + Article 63), identifies which sub-processors create extraterritorial exposure, assesses whether each sub-processor is compatible with the professional accountability framework, and produces a per-tool sub-processor audit memo. Universal across verticals with vertical-specific calibration.
