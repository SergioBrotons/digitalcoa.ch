# The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question
## A 9-Section Framework for Geneva Practices (2-5 Attorneys)

---

## Section 1: The Geneva AI Governance Gap

Geneva's small law firms face a regulatory convergence they haven't named.

Four deadlines. Four months. No framework.

- Swissdamed: July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks away)
- EU AI Act: August 2, 2026 (~4-5 months away)
- AMLA/LETA: H2 2026 (~3-6 months away)
- FDJP consultation: End-2026 (~9 months away)

Meanwhile, the Federal Council confirmed a "lean approach" — no cross-sector binding Swiss AI law before end-2026.

And the FDPIC has opened 4 AI investigations in the past 18 months. Transport companies. Retailers. Social media platforms. **So far, no Geneva law firms.**

That's not absence of risk. That's a window.

---

## Section 2: Why Geneva Small Firms Are Different

Large law firms have compliance departments. IT teams. Budget.

Small practices (2-5 attorneys) have:
- Existing nFADP obligations via data processing
- AI tools adopted ad hoc (contract analysis, due diligence, legal research)
- Professional secrecy obligations that AI can violate
- No internal framework for AI accountability

The gap between adoption and governance is widest in small firms.

## Section 3: The Enforcement Gap — Positive Signal

The FDPIC has been building its AI enforcement portfolio:
- Transport company investigation (February 2026)
- Joint DPA statement on AI-generated images (February 2026)
- X/Twitter AI training investigation (March 2025)
- Coop AI profiling investigation (October 2025)

**Law firms are not on that list. Yet.**

This is the same pattern that preceded enforcement in other sectors: awareness-building, guidance publication, then enforcement. The Swiss Bar Association published AI Guidelines in June 2024. The FDPIC has a 4-investigation portfolio. The gap between guidance and enforcement in legal is approximately 12-18 months.

## Section 4: The Four-Deadline Cluster

### Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)
Health data processing obligations. No grace period. For firms handling any health-related client data — this is immediate.

### EU AI Act — August 2, 2026 (~4-5 months)
If your firm has EU clients, places AI systems on the EU market, or operates AI with EU-intended output — GPAI transparency obligations are already active. High-risk AI obligations activate August 2, 2026.

### AMLA/LETA — H2 2026 (~3-6 months)
Anti-money laundering obligations for client-facing work. If your corporate or wealth management clients face AML compliance — their obligations become your conversation.

### FDJP Consultation — End-2026 (~9 months)
Draft bill on CoE AI Convention implementation. Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight. First binding Swiss AI framework for professional services.

## Section 5: The 3-Decision Framework

Every Geneva small law firm faces the same three decisions now:

**Decision 1: Do you use AI for client-adjacent work?**
If yes: nFADP Art. 5(c) transparency obligations are already active.

**Decision 2: Do you have EU-nexus clients or operations?**
If yes: EU AI Act GPAI transparency obligations are active now; high-risk obligations activate August 2, 2026.

**Decision 3: Do you handle health data?**
If yes: Swissdamed July 1, 2026 is ~12 weeks away.

The order matters. Swissdamed is first. Then EU AI Act. Then AMLA/LETA. Then FDJP.

## Section 6: Why Act Now

**The FDPIC enforcement gap is closing.**

Four investigations in 18 months. A 4-investigation portfolio doesn't stay at four indefinitely. The Swiss Bar Association has published guidance. The Federal Council has confirmed its lean approach — which means guidance, not mandates, until end-2026 at earliest.

That window — approximately 12-18 months — is the first-mover window.

Advisors who move now:
- Build authority before the market floods
- Serve clients before compliance becomes mandatory
- Define the framework before regulators do

---

## Section 7: The AI Accountability Gap

AI doesn't eliminate professional liability. It shifts it.

- AI hallucinations in legal research → professional negligence exposure
- AI-generated contract clauses → oversight obligation
- AI processing of confidential client data → data protection liability
- AI decisions affecting litigation strategy → accountability remains with the attorney

The Swiss Bar Association Guidelines (June 2024) are clear: lawyers remain accountable for AI-assisted work. The question is whether your framework reflects that accountability.
