## Why No One Has Claimed This Position

### Structural Reasons

1. **The segment is too small for large firms** — a3-person practice at CHF 300K–1.2M revenue cannot sustain Big 4 engagement
2. **The regulatory knowledge is too deep for generalists** — nFADP Art. 5(c) + Swissmedic + FMH professional standards requires healthcare-specific expertise
3. **The relationship requirement is too high for vendors** — physicians need trusted peer-adjacent advisor, not a vendor selling a product
4. **The language is too specific for general AI governance frameworks** — "FMH human oversight" is not in any generic AI governance framework
5. **The geographic proximity requirement is real** — Geneva physician community is relationship-driven; remote advisors from Zurich are not accessible

### Timing Reasons

1. **Carrier AI attestation is new** — the carrier form as forcing function has emerged in the past 12–18 months
2. **Swissdamed July 2026 deadline is new** — the device registration deadline has created a concrete compliance checkpoint
3. **The market is not yet aware of the advisory category** — physicians don't know "AI governance advisory for small practice" is a service they should seek
