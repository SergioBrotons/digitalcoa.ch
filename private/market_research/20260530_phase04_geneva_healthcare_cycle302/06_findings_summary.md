## Headline Findings

### NET-NEW Findings (C302 Phase 04)

---

**Finding 1: Carrier Attestation as Primary Acute Forcing Function**

The malpractice carrier AI attestation form is the primary conversion trigger for independent physician practices — not regulatory awareness, not peer incident. The carrier has shifted from passive insurer to active compliance monitor. The physician receives a concrete document with a policy renewal deadline, cannot answer it, and has no broker or advisor who can help. Fear of coverage implications converts abstract concern to action faster than any FDPIC guidance document.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your carrier sent you an AI attestation form. You don't know what it requires. I do."

---

**Finding 2: FMH Human Oversight Stated Without Operational Definition**

FMH has stated that human oversight of AI is required. FMH has not defined what that means in a small practice context. No FMH section has published practical guidance for what "documented human oversight" looks like for a 2–5 physician practice. The requirement is acknowledged; the compliance pathway is absent. This is the core anxiety point — the physician cannot comply because no one has defined compliance.

**Ready-to-use language:** "FMH says human oversight is required. FMH hasn't told you what that means in practice. I will."

---

**Finding 3: Physician License as Irreplaceable Personal Asset — Highest Stake of Any Vertical**

The FMH license is framed by ICPs as irreplaceable — unlike attorney bar admission or WM career capital, a medical license cannot be re-earned after revocation. Disciplinary action is personal, not organizational. nFADP Art. 63 personal liability applies to the responsible individual physician. This creates the highest personal accountability stakes of any vertical.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your FMH license is not something you can afford to risk. The good news: governance framework reduces that risk practically."

---

**Finding 4: nFADP + Swissmedic Dual-Layer Paralysis**

Small Geneva practices face two simultaneous regulatory layers (nFADP Art. 5(c) for data + Swissmedic for device classification) with no clear guidance on which applies when. The question is not "what does each framework require" — it is "which framework applies to my situation." Swissdamed July 1, 2026 makes this paralysis concrete and urgent. No existing advisory service addresses the dual-layer question in practical small-practice terms.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Most small practices don't know whether their AI tool is a medical device, a data processor, or both. Here's how to find out — and what it means for you."

---

**Finding 5: Anticipatory Breach Fear — Peer Scare as Primary Trigger**

Geneva physicians express fear of being "the next headline" in a healthcare data breach, but this fear is anticipatory — no breach has occurred in their practice. The trigger is peer incidents (colleague's "breach scare") and media coverage, not regulatory guidance. A peer breach scare converts faster than any regulatory document. The physician is willing to invest in prevention before an incident, if the investment is framed as affordable and practical.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The gap between how your AI tools actually work and what you think they do is where your exposure lives. Let me close that gap before an incident."

---

**Finding 6: Medical Assistant Shortage Makes Efficiency AI Non-Negotiable**

Geneva's medical assistant shortage (highest wages in Switzerland, structural demographic crisis) has reached a threshold where efficiency AI adoption is no longer a choice — it is a practice survival issue. The clause du besoin (July 2025) makes existing practice licenses more economically valuable, reinforcing the incentive to invest in efficiency. Compliance and efficiency are no longer separate conversations — they converge in the small practice context.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're short-staffed, overwhelmed with admin, and you can't afford to lose anyone. AI efficiency tools can help — but only if they're governed properly."
