### 2. SBA Explicitly Endorses Federal Council Sector-Specific Approach — Not EU AI Act
**Source:** SBA AI page (same primary source)

The SBA explicitly positions itself against the EU AI Act comprehensive framework:

- SBA: Federal Council has "opted not to follow this example [of EU AI Act]" and "favours a targeted, technology-neutral and principles-based approach"
- SBA: This approach "has so far proven to work well"
- SBA supports Federal Council's three goals: **encouraging innovation, protecting basic rights, strengthening trust in AI** — to be met "through carefully thought-out amendments to existing legislation"
- SBA explicitly endorses existing "tried-and-tested principles" as guide for responsible AI use
- SBA: "The SBA is keeping a close eye on developments and seeking active involvement in the social policy debate" to shape AI frameworks

**Legal implication:** The SBA's explicit endorsement of the Federal Council's sector-specific approach vs. EU AI Act creates a clear market positioning for Geneva wealth managers and financial institutions: Swiss-hosted AI with domestic governance (not EU GPAI) may be preferred for data-sovereignty-sensitive clients. However, firms serving EU clients or operating in EU market still face EU AI Act GPAI obligations.
