## NET-NEW Cross-Vertical White Space for C299

---

### White Space 1 (NET-NEW C299): Pre-Signing Review as Discrete Service Category (All Four Verticals)

**The white space:** No Geneva market service offers pre-signing personal liability review for the documents that activate personal exposure across all four verticals. Medical practitioners have developed this service for carrier attestations; the equivalent service for legal, finance, and consulting MDs does not exist in the market. Healthcare has it; legal, finance, and consulting do not.

**The structural gap:**
| Vertical | Document | Pre-Signing Review Market | Status |
|----------|----------|--------------------------|--------|
| Legal | Carrier PI attestation | None | Absent |
| Finance | Institutional questionnaire response | None | Absent |
| Consulting | Carrier renewal + client contract AI clauses | None | Absent |
| Healthcare | Carrier attestation (emerging) | None — but medical practitioners have some awareness | Emerging |

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** Prior cycles named the carrier attestation pressure and service absence. C299 identifies the specific structural parity: medical practitioners have developed pre-signing review as a service concept; the equivalent for legal, finance, and consulting does not exist despite structurally identical exposure. The market gap is not just service absence — it is an absence that exists in three verticals simultaneously while the same service concept is emerging in a fourth.

**The product opportunity:** A pre-signing review service that intercepts the personal signature moment, applicable across all four verticals, proportionate to Geneva boutique scale, delivered in the 90-minute briefing format. This is not a compliance checklist — it is a personal liability analysis that produces an evidence file and a signed document the signatory can defend.

### White Space 2 (NET-NEW C299): Processor Chain Documentation Kit (Engagement Letter AI Clause + Processor Chain Register)

**The white space:** No Geneva market service offers a processor chain documentation kit for boutique professional firms. The processor chain exists in practice across all four verticals but has no documentation foundation. The engagement letter has no AI governance clause. The vendor agreements were signed without evaluating Art.9 processor obligations. The sub-consultant chain has no disclosure protocol.

**The structural gap by vertical:**
- Legal: Consumer AI tool usage without compliant DPA under nFADP Art.9; vendor model training provisions potentially waiving attorney-client privilege
- Finance: Multi-vendor AI stack (portfolio analytics + CRM + reporting) with undisclosed sub-processors; Art.9 obligations accumulated through years of unevaluated click-through acceptance
- Consulting: Three-step processor chain (client → prime → sub-consultant → AI vendor) with no contractual foundation; engagement letters contain no AI clause
- Healthcare: Clinical AI tool routes patient data through US servers; Swiss hosting label irrelevant to processing location

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** Prior cycles named the processor chain exposure. C299 names the specific product: a processor chain documentation kit consisting of (1) engagement letter AI clause template, (2) vendor term gap assessment, (3) processor chain register, (4) sub-consultant AI disclosure protocol. This is a discrete, bounded, implementable service component.

**The market opportunity:** The kit is low-cost to produce and high-value to sell to the ICP who wants an immediately actionable first step. It names the problem in a way that makes the next action obvious. It is also a strong entry point for the full briefing — the ICP who buys the kit is functionally the same ICP who needs the framework.

### White Space 3 (NET-NEW C299): Swissdamed Classification Assessment Service (Healthcare-Specific)

**The white space:** No Geneva-calibrated Swissdamed classification assessment methodology exists for independent medical practices. The Swissdamed criteria were written for manufacturers, not outpatient practices. The practice must decide Tier 1 or Tier 2 by July 1, 2026, with no proportionate assessment tool. The classification determines the obligations; the wrong classification creates either over-compliance (wasted resources) or under-compliance (regulatory exposure).

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** Swissdamed is at acute phase — weeks from the July 1, 2026 deadline. This is the first C299 cycle where healthcare-specific Swissdamed classification assessment is the primary white space entry point.

**The product opportunity:** A Swissdamed classification assessment service: Geneva-calibrated, proportionate to independent practice scale, delivered within the 90-minute briefing format. The output: classification determination with documented rationale, proportionate obligations mapped to the classification, and a registration submission checklist. This is the most acute, time-limited engagement opportunity in the healthcare vertical.

**Cross-vertical context:** Swissdamed fills the same structural role for healthcare that carrier attestation pre-signing review fills for legal/finance/consulting — an undefined standard + time-limited deadline + no proportionate methodology = stampede documentation. The Swissdamed assessment is the pre-signing review equivalent for the healthcare vertical.

### White Space 4 (NET-NEW C299): Post-Signing Consequence Management Service (Client Audit Response + Carrier Follow-Up)

**The white space:** When downstream consequences arrive — client audit follow-ups, carrier attestation form evolution, vendor term changes — no Geneva service helps the professional respond. The ICP is managing consequence events alone, without infrastructure or methodology.

**The structural gap:**
- Client audit follow-up (consulting): Client wants evidence supporting 18-month-old questionnaire response; no service helps produce that evidence within the client's timeline
- Carrier attestation iteration (legal/finance/consulting): Form changed since last signature; no service helps evaluate whether new sections create new commitments
- Vendor term change monitoring (all verticals): Vendors change terms silently; no service monitors for nFADP compliance implications of vendor term changes
- Swissdamed correction (healthcare): Practice registered at wrong Tier; no service helps correct before enforcement begins

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** Prior cycles named the downstream consequence cascade as a market event in C299. This is the first cycle that names consequence management as a specific service category.

**The product opportunity:** A consequence management retainer: quarterly check-in that reviews carrier form changes, client audit follow-ups, vendor term changes, and Swissdamed corrections. This is the retaining client as intelligence-compounding vehicle — each quarter the ICP returns with new events; the service produces a response architecture before the event becomes an exposure.

### White Space 5 (NET-NEW C299): Four-Track Regulatory Coherence Dashboard (Finance-Specific)

**The white space:** No Geneva market service offers cross-track coherence management for the four independent documentation systems that finance ICPs must maintain simultaneously: FINMA Circular 08/2024, institutional client questionnaire responses, nFADP ROPA/DPIA/Art.26, carrier attestation evidence. Documentation may be internally inconsistent despite individually satisfying each track. The cross-track consistency problem is a discrete service category.

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** The finance vertical specifically has the most complex multi-track documentation architecture. C299 Phase 02 named this as a discrete service category — four-track coherence management — distinct from any single-mandate compliance solution.

**Cross-vertical implication:** The four-track coherence problem is finance-specific in its full complexity, but the underlying methodology (cross-mandate coherence) is generalizable. A general cross-mandate coherence framework would have application across all four verticals, with finance having the most acute version.

---

### White Space 6 (NET-NEW C299): EU AI Act Nexus Assessment for Geneva Boutique Firms

**The white space:** No Geneva market service offers EU AI Act nexus assessment proportionate to boutique professional firm scale. Firms with EU clients need to determine whether they fall within scope, what obligations apply, and how to prepare before the August 2, 2026 operative date. Existing guidance is designed for enterprises with compliance functions. No boutique-calibrated methodology exists.

**The structural gap:**
- The assessment requires understanding: (1) whether the firm has EU nexus, (2) what AI systems are in scope, (3) what obligations apply, (4) how to document compliance
- Existing guidance is general and enterprise-oriented
- No Geneva-calibrated, boutique-proportionate assessment methodology exists
- The window before regulatory pressure is the right time to do this assessment

**Why it is NET-NEW in C299:** EU AI Act operative date is ~14 months away. The assessment is not yetUrgent for most firms — but the firms that do it now (before demand floods the market) will be positioned differently from firms that wait.

**Cross-vertical resonance:** EU AI Act affects legal (EU client matters), finance (EU-domiciled clients), consulting (EU-nexus mandates), and potentially healthcare (EU patient data). The nexus question applies across all four verticals but differently in each.

## Unified Positioning Framework for C299

### Core Positioning Statement (Cross-Vertical)

> "I help senior Geneva professionals answer the documents they already signed — and understand what they're committing to before they sign the next one."

This is unified across all four verticals because:
- The problem is universal: signed documents that create personal exposure without documentation to support them
- The trigger point is universal: the personal signature
- The service format is universal: 90-minute briefing, CHF 2,200, EN+FR
- The methodology is unified: naming the specific accountability architecture gap and producing a proportionate response

### Entry Point Architecture by Vertical

| Vertical | Primary Entry Point | Secondary Entry Point | Trigger Event |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Legal | Carrier PI attestation pre-signing review | Vendor term gap assessment | Carrier renewal |
| Finance | Institutional questionnaire response pre-signing | Processor chain documentation kit | Client AI DDQ receipt |
| Consulting | Client AI governance audit follow-up | Carrier renewal pre-signing | Client audit request |
| Healthcare | Swissdamed classification assessment | Carrier attestation pre-signing | July 1 deadline |

### Service Architecture Unification

The service architecture that addresses all six white spaces:
1. **90-minute briefing** (CHF 2,200) — pre-signing review + consequence management framework
2. **Processor chain documentation kit** (standalone or bundled) — engagement letter AI clause + processor register
3. **Swissdamed classification assessment** (healthcare-only, 90-minute) — classification + obligations + registration checklist
4. **Quarterly consequence management retainer** — ongoing management of carrier form changes, client audit follow-ups, vendor term changes
5. **EU AI Act nexus assessment** (standalone) — boutique-proportionate scope determination

### The Naming Is the Product

The consistent finding across all six white spaces: **naming the specific problem is the product**. The ICP:
- Has the problem ("I signed something I can't explain")
- Cannot name it ("I don't know what to call what happened to me")
- Responds to named problems with clear structure

The naming creates the serviceable problem space. The service architecture follows from the naming.

**Geneva circuit participation as structural moat (confirmed C299):**

- No competitor is positioned in this white space with Geneva-calibrated, boutique-proportionate offerings
- The window is structured by regulatory timing, not competitive saturation
- Sustained presence in formation rooms and ICP awareness is the durable advantage
