## Purpose

This file identifies cross-vertical language patterns that are NET-NEW to C299 — drawing from C299 Phase 01–04 vertical research. These patterns span all four verticals and describe how the ICP articulates the same structural accountability problem through different sector-specific framings.

**Prior cross-vertical language findings (C286–C298):**

- C289: Four convergences (carrier attestation + institutional client questionnaire + FINMA anticipation + nFADP Art.63); waiting-for-guidance producing dual exclusion; documentation window as burden-of-proof reversal

## NET-NEW Cross-Vertical Language Structures from C299 Phases 01–04

---

### Structure 1 (NET-NEW C299): "I Signed on Autopilot"

This is a behavioral pattern that emerged distinctively in C299 across all four verticals. The ICP no longer describes ignorance — they describe deliberate non-reading as normalization. The carrier form, vendor contract, or renewal attestation is signed like an email acknowledgment. The signature does not represent governance evaluation.

**Legal (C299 Phase 01 — SaaS vendor terms):**
> "The SaaS contract came in a hyperlink. I clicked accept. I didn't read it. My attorney-client privilege may have been waived by a contract I signed on my phone between hearings."

**Finance (C299 Phase 02 — institutional questionnaire):**
> "I signed the institutional questionnaire response on my phone, between two client calls. The compliance team had cleared the approach. I'm not sure I had time to read it before signing."

**Consulting (C299 Phase 03 — carrier renewal):**
> "The carrier renewal comes every year. It's different this year — new sections appeared. 'Human oversight protocol.' 'AI governance framework.' I filled in what I could and signed. On my phone, on the train."

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04 — Swissdamed registration):**
> "The Swissdamed registration deadline is July 1. I haven't had time to figure out what Tier I'm in. I filled in the form based on what I could infer. I signed it."

**The linguistic mechanism:** The ICP describes a behavioral sequence — high frequency, low attention, signature as ritual — that creates legal commitment. The signature event is described as administrative friction resolution, not governance evaluation. The key indicator is the ICP does not express surprise at their own behavior; it is normalized.

**Cross-vertical resonance:** This is the most acute behavioral finding from C299. The ICP has accepted that they sign important documents without evaluation — because the documentation volume has made thorough review structurally impossible. The attestation fatigue is a structural adaptive response to documentation overload, not a choice.

### Structure 2 (NET-NEW C299): "The Downstream Consequences Are Arriving"

In prior cycles (C295–C297), the dominant ICP language described an awareness problem — the ICP did not understand what the documents meant or why they mattered. In C299, the language has shifted. The ICP understands the consequences; they are living in them.

**Legal (C299 Phase 01):**
> "I signed the carrier attestation two years ago. I had no idea what 'adequate AI governance' meant. The carrier sent a follow-up this year asking what we've implemented since. We have no evidence to show."

**Finance (C299 Phase 02 — cross-firm institutional comparison):**
> "I left half the institutional questionnaire blank last year. This year they sent a follow-up asking specific questions about our AI vendor stack. I started getting nervous. We can't enumerate our AI vendors."

**Consulting (C299 Phase 03 — client audit request):**
> "A client sent an AI governance audit follow-up — they want evidence supporting our questionnaire response from 18 months ago. We answered from knowledge and memory. We don't have supporting documentation."

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04):**
> "I discovered that the AI tool I subscribed to two years ago had a model training clause in the terms I accepted. My patient records were used to train the model. I didn't know. I can't prove the consent was adequate."

**The linguistic mechanism:** The ICP describes a sequence — signing, silence, arrival of downstream event — where the consequences of the signature have become concrete. The downstream event is a client follow-up, carrier iteration, vendor term change, or Swissdamed deadline. The key emotional marker is not surprise at the problem, but the lived experience of its concrete arrival.

**Cross-vertical resonance:** This is confirmed as the unified C299 ICP state. The market has crossed from "I don't understand" to "I signed and I'm living with it." The ICP across all four verticals is now describing the same temporal arc.

### Structure 3 (REINFORCED C299): "The Accountable Person Still Can't Evaluate the AI"

This structure was named as NET-NEW in C298. In C299 it is reinforced across all four verticals with sector-specific richness that sharpens the mechanism.

**Legal (C299 Phase 01 — associate AI proficiency):**
> "My most AI-proficient junior associates are the ones using AI most actively. I supervise their work — but I can't independently evaluate whether the AI output they incorporated is accurate. My name is on every brief."

**Finance (C299 Phase 02 — portfolio construction AI):**
> "The AI builds the portfolio continuously. I authorize trades. But the authorization happens against recommendations the AI generated — and I can't trace back which decisions were AI-influenced versus human-initiated."

**Consulting (C299 Phase 03 — sub-consultant AI):**
> "The sub-consultant sent back a clearly AI-generated section. I can't tell you what the AI contributed versus what the human contributed. My name is on the deliverable."

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04 — patient-generated AI):**
> "A patient came in with ChatGPT-generated analysis. I overrode the AI recommendation and made a different clinical decision. I documented my decision. I did not document the specific moment where I evaluated and rejected the AI recommendation."

**Why reinforced in C299:** The C299 instances are more specific than C298 about the mechanisms. The accountability inversion is not just implied — it is described through concrete, named scenarios that the ICP has actually encountered.

### Structure 4 (REINFORCED C299): "The Carrier Form Keeps Changing"

This structure emerged in C298 as "Attestation fatigue." In C299, the mechanism is better specified: the carrier forms iterate each year, adding new terms and sections that create new commitments without triggering a deliberate re-evaluation by the signatory.

**Legal (C299 Phase 01 — iterative carrier form):**
> "The form I signed last year is different this year. New terms appeared. 'AI literacy documentation.' 'Vendor AI governance attestation.' I don't know if those sections applied to me, or if I was supposed to have documentation for them."

**Finance (C299 Phase 02 — third-generation questionnaire):**
> "The third-generation carrier questionnaire now asks about sub-processors, data termination protocols, acquisition scenarios. These weren't in last year's form. I'm now being asked to attest to things I never evaluated."

**Consulting (C299 Phase 03 — carrier renewal):**
> "I think I signed something about AI at last renewal. The language was different this year — new terms appeared. I signed the same way I signed last year: without a systematic review."

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04 — Swissdamed iteration):**
> "Swissdamed registration forms keep changing between versions. The criteria I thought we fell under in Q1 are different in the current version. I don't know which version the carrier is expecting."

**The linguistic mechanism:** The ICP describes form evolution as externally driven and structurally unmanageable — the carrier controls what the form asks; the ICP signs when it arrives. Each iteration is a new commitment event; none trigger a systematic re-evaluation.

### Structure 5 (NET-NEW C299): "The Sub-Consultant Processor Chain Is My Liability"

This structural exposure was implied in C289 and C295–C298. In C299 Phase 02 (finance) and Phase 03 (consulting) specifically, it is now the named primary blind spot — the processor chain exists in practice with no documentation architecture.

**Finance (C299 Phase 02 — vendor processor chain):**
> "I accepted the data processing terms for our portfolio analytics AI, our CRM AI, and our reporting AI. All three have sub-processors I've never evaluated. Under nFADP Art. 9, I may be responsible for all of those sub-processor flows."

**Consulting (C299 Phase 03 — sub-consultant chain):**
> "The sub-consultant uses their own AI tools. I don't know which ones. I can't audit what AI they used to produce the work I'm delivering to my client. My engagement letter has no AI governance clause."

**Legal (C299 Phase 01 — implied in vendor data extraction):**
> "The standard SaaS contract terms include model training for the vendor's AI. If the vendor uses a third-party AI provider, I'm in their processor chain — and their processor chain is invisible to me."

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04 — AI tool processor chain):**
> "The clinical AI tool has a US-based API. Patient data routes through US servers. I selected the tool because it was Swiss-hosted. I didn't evaluate where the API called home."

**Cross-vertical resonance:** The processor chain problem is structurally identical across all four verticals. In each case, the professional is the data controller for flows they cannot enumerate, because the chain was created through unevaluated vendor agreement acceptance.

### Structure 6 (NET-NEW C299): "Swissdamed as the New Carrier Attestation — Same Structural Problem"

Swissdamed is producing the same attestation pattern that legal, finance, and consulting experienced with carrier forms — but for medical practices at independent practice scale. The medical practice has no framework for answering Swissdamed questions; Swissdamed questions were not designed for independent practice scale; the July 1, 2026 deadline creates time pressure that produces stampede documentation.

**Healthcare (C299 Phase 04):**
> "Swissdamed asks me to classify our AI tools by risk tier. The criteria were written for manufacturers, not outpatient practices. I don't know if I'm Tier 1 or Tier 2. The classification determines my obligations. I have to decide by July 1. I have no methodology."

> "I filled in the Swissdamed registration form without understanding it. I guessed the classification tier. The form was long and I was in between patients."

**The linguistic mechanism:** Swissdamed creates urgency through deadline; the medical practice responds with stampede documentation. The form is not designed for independent practice scale; the practice cannot find proportionate guidance; the July 1 deadline arrives regardless.

**Cross-vertical resonance:** This is "The Attestation Gap" pattern applied to healthcare. The structure is: undefined standard + carrier deadline + no Geneva-calibrated methodology = self-incriminating stampede documentation.

## The Universal Cross-Vertical Problem Statement (C299 Version)

> "I signed documents I did not read carefully. The meaning of what I signed has drifted through iterative form changes I did nothing to cause. Downstream consequences are arriving — client audits, carrier follow-ups, vendor term changes, regulatory deadlines — and I have no documentation architecture to respond from. The accountable person cannot evaluate the AI that evaluation depends on. The processor chain is invisible. The standard is undefined. The deadline is fixed. The signature is mine."

This is identical across a Geneva attorney, wealth manager, consulting principal, and physician in May 2026. The sector context changes the details. The accountability architecture problem is structurally one thing.

## Cross-Vertical Pattern Change from C298 to C299

| Dimension | C298 State | C299 State |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Primary problem | Awareness gap — ICP doesn't understand | Consequence gap — ICP understands and is living with it |
| Dominant emotional register | Uncertainty, searching | Fatigue, inhabited consequence |
| Documentation posture | Retroactive scramble to build | Behavioral normalization of non-review |
| Carrier form pattern | Novel attestation pressure | Iterative form creep with new sections each year |
| Sub-consultant processor chain | Implied exposure | Named primary blind spot with specific processor chain language |
| Substantive trigger | First attestation arrival | Downstream event arrival (audits, renewals, deadline) |
| Regulatory cluster | Approaching but not yet acute | Swissdamed July 1, 2026 — weeks away |

The C299 ICP is not more aware than the C298 ICP. The C299 ICP has lived longer in the problem. The language reflects inhabiting the consequence, not discovering the problem.
