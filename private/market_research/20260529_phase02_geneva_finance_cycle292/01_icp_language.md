# 01 — ICP Language: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management

## Phase 02: Geneva Finance — Cycle 292

---

## ICP Profile
- **Firm type:** Boutique wealth management, Geneva
- **Size:** 50–200 employees
- **Role:** Managing Director or Partner, personally accountable
- **Core problem:** AI tool deployment for client portfolios and data
- **Key pressures:** Institutional AI governance questionnaires, carrier attestation, FINMA expectations, SRO silence, nFADP Art.63 personal liability

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### The Language of Personal Accountability

The Geneva boutique WM MD does not say "we need an AI strategy." The MD says:

> *"If something goes wrong with an AI-assisted recommendation and the client suffers, who is responsible? Me. My name is on the door. The carrier will ask me to sign something. And I don't know what I'm signing."*

The personal liability frame activates before the operational efficiency frame. Every conversation about AI in a Geneva boutique WM firm is ultimately a conversation about the MD's personal exposure.

---

### Primary Language Patterns

#### Pattern 1: "The client sent us a questionnaire"
Institutional clients send AI governance questionnaires — 20, 30, 40 pages. The MD receives it and says:

> *"I got this from [institutional client] legal. Forty pages. I don't know what most of it means. I forwarded it to my team and they don't know either. We need to respond in three weeks."*

**What this reveals:** The institutional questionnaire is the first concrete touchpoint with AI governance accountability. The MD has no pre-built response framework. The questionnaire creates urgency before any strategic AI decision was made.

**Language markers:** "got this," "forty pages," "don't know what it means," "forwarded to my team," "three weeks."

---

#### Pattern 2: "My carrier is asking me to sign something about AI"
The carrier attestation question arrives at renewal. The MD says:

> *"My broker sent over the renewal. There's a new section on AI. It says I'm attesting to adequate AI governance controls. I don't know what that means. I don't know what I have. And I have to sign it personally."*

**What this reveals:** Carrier attestation is the acute personal liability moment. The MD's signature creates personal exposure. There is no pre-signing review service available. The MD is being asked to commit to an undefined standard.

**Language markers:** "new section on AI," "adequate AI governance controls," "sign it personally," "my broker sent it over."

---

#### Pattern 3: "FINMA is going to say something eventually"
The MD has general awareness that FINMA is thinking about AI. But:

> *"I know FINMA is working on something. I don't know what it says. I don't know when it arrives. My SRO hasn't said anything. I don't know if I'm already behind on something I should have done."*

**What this reveals:** Anticipatory anxiety without actionable intelligence. The MD knows regulatory expectations are coming but has no Geneva-specific guidance about what they are. SRO silence compounds the uncertainty.

**Language markers:** "working on something," "don't know what it says," "when it arrives," "my SRO hasn't said anything."

---

#### Pattern 4: "My team started using AI tools"
Shadow AI is already present in the firm. The MD discovers it reactively:

> *"I found out my team is using [tool name]. I didn't know. I don't know what data they've put in. I don't know if client data went somewhere. And now the carrier wants me to sign that I have adequate governance."*

**What this reveals:** AI adoption happened before governance decisions. The MD is accountable for AI usage they did not authorize, through tools they did not evaluate, creating exposure they are only now discovering.

**Language markers:** "found out," "didn't know," "don't know what data," "client data went somewhere."

---

#### Pattern 5: "I've been using AI in my recommendations"
The MD personally uses AI in client work:

> *"I've been using [tool] to help think through portfolio construction. I mention it when I remember. But I don't have a protocol. And when I look back at two years of reports, I can't tell you which parts were AI-assisted and which weren't."*

**What this reveals:** Accumulated accountability gap across the mandate book. The MD cannot reconstruct the AI usage history of active client relationships. This creates exposure at carrier renewal, institutional questionnaire response, and nFADP Art.13/21 moments.

**Language markers:** "been using," "when I remember," "don't have a protocol," "can't tell you," "two years of reports."

---

#### Pattern 6: "The SRO says nothing applies to us yet"
The SRO (self-regulatory organization) guidance vacuum is a known structural condition:

> *"I called the SRO. They said AI isn't in their guidance yet. So I'm in a situation where I know something is coming, nothing has arrived, and I'm using AI tools that my clients are asking about."*

**What this reveals:** SRO silence is experienced as guidance vacuum, not as permission. The MD understands the vacuum is temporary. The operative question is what to do during the vacuum period.

**Language markers:** "called the SRO," "isn't in their guidance yet," "nothing has arrived," "clients are asking about it."

---

#### Pattern 7: "If the FDPIC comes after me"
The FDPIC (Federal Data Protection Inspector) enforcement posture shift is creating new anxiety:

> *"I read that the data protection authority is starting to enforce the nFADP now. For AI. I process client financial data through AI tools. Am I a data processor? What are my obligations? I don't have a lawyer who can tell me."*

**What this reveals:** nFADP obligations are understood to be live and enforceable, but the specific obligations for AI-assisted wealth management are undefined. The MD understands exposure exists but cannot identify the specific compliance pathway.

**Language markers:** "starting to enforce now," "for AI," "am I a data processor," "don't have a lawyer who can tell me."

## What the ICP Does Not Say (But Means)

| ICP Does Not Say | What They Mean |
|---|---|
| "We need an AI governance framework" | "I'm personally exposed and I can't name the edges of my exposure" |
| "Our AI usage is low-risk" | "We use AI but we haven't documented it, and if someone asked, I couldn't explain it" |
| "We comply with FINMA guidance" | "FINMA hasn't said anything specific to us, so we're waiting" |
| "We have vendor contracts" | "We clicked through vendor terms and we don't know what we agreed to" |
| "Our AI is accurate" | "I don't know what AI said in client reports because I don't track AI version states" |

## Lead Magnet Language Alignment

The lead magnet must speak in the MD's voice and use the MD's language:

**What to say:** "Your client sent you a 40-page AI governance questionnaire. Now what?"

**What NOT to say:** "Let's discuss your AI governance framework and optimal tool selection strategy."

The MD who receives a 40-page institutional questionnaire is not thinking about frameworks. They are thinking about their signature on a carrier form, their name on the door, and a client who is asking questions they cannot answer.
