## Phase 02: Geneva Finance — Cycle 292

## Market Structure

The Geneva boutique wealth management sector (50–200 employees) occupies a specific position in the Swiss financial ecosystem: large enough to face institutional client AI governance expectations and FINMA/SRO oversight, small enough to lack the resources of the large private banks.

The AI governance advisory market for this ICP does not have a proportionate solution. Large bank solutions are not accessible. Small firm solutions are not calibrated to the specific exposure profile. No Geneva-specific boutique WM AI governance advisory product exists at the right scale and price point.

### What Exists vs. What's Missing

#### What Exists

1. **General AI governance frameworks** — ISO 42001, EU AI Act, generic AI governance principles. Not Switzerland-specific. Not calibrated to WM MD personal liability.

2. **Carrier-provided guidance** — Carrier attestation forms. The carrier provides the question, not the answer. Forms written for underwriting risk evaluation, not professional liability protection.

3. **SRO guidance (absence of)** — SROs have not issued WM-specific AI guidance. Their silence is structural, not temporary.

4. **FINMA Guidance 08/2024** — Principles-based. Operative. Does not provide proportionate methodology for boutique WM implementation.

5. **nFADP general obligations** — Art.63 criminal liability, Art.21 automated decision rights, Art.8 profiling notifications. Named in regulation. Not operationalized for boutique WM.

6. **Institutional client questionnaires** — Institutional clients provide the questions. The boutique WM must answer without Geneva-specific response methodology.

#### What's Missing

1. **Geneva-specific boutique WM AI governance methodology** — No Geneva service has built a proportionate AI governance methodology specifically for 50–200 employee Geneva boutiques with personal MD liability accountability.

2. **Carrier attestation pre-signing review service** — No Geneva service offers MD-side review of what the carrier form commits the signatory to personally. The MD signs without independent analysis.

3. **Institutional AI questionnaire response framework** — No Geneva service offers questionnaire response architecture calibrated to institutional client competitive dynamics and Swiss regulatory obligations simultaneously.

4. **nFADP Art.21 operational protocol** — No Geneva boutique has an Art.21 response protocol for AI-assisted portfolio decisions. The obligation exists; the discharge mechanism does not.

5. **Agentic AI governance for boutique WM** — No Geneva service addresses agentic AI (continuous execution) governance at boutique scale. Frameworks are written for larger institutions.

6. **Documentation window positioning methodology** — No service frames the documentation window as a present-tense burden-of-proof positioning exercise, not a future compliance deadline.

7. **MD personal liability exposure mapping** — No service maps the specific intersection of carrier attestation, FINMA expectations, nFADP Art.63, and institutional client accountability on the individual MD's personal exposure.

## Competitive Positioning Opportunity for DigitalCoa

### Where the Gap Is

The Geneva boutique WM MD faces a compound accountability problem that no single Geneva service addresses:

- Institutional clients asking AI governance questions
- Carrier requiring personal signature on undefined adequacy standard
- FINMA guidance anticipated without specific WM calibration
- SRO silence as structural condition
- nFADP obligations present-tense and unnamed
- Documentation window closing with every day of undocumented AI usage

**No competitor serves this exact problem intersection at proportionate boutique scale.**

### Competitive Moat

1. **Geneva-specific calibration** — More accurate inside Geneva than any non-Geneva competitor. Non-replicable by firms without sustained Geneva ecosystem participation.

2. **Boutique scale proportionality** — Large bank solutions are not accessible. Small firm solutions are not calibrated. DigitalCoa serves exactly the gap between.

3. **Boutique MD personal liability framing** — The market talks about "AI governance frameworks." The MD thinks about "what am I personally exposed for, what does my signature commit me to, what happens when the client asks." Positioning must use the MD's language.

4. **Institutional circuit intelligence** — Institutional clients are setting AI governance expectations through their questionnaires. Sustained participation in Geneva institutional ecosystem provides intelligence about how expectations are forming, before published guidance arrives.

5. **Bilingual EN+FR** — Geneva boutique WMs operate in both languages. Institutional clients span both languages. Competitors primarily serve German-speaking Swiss financial sector.
