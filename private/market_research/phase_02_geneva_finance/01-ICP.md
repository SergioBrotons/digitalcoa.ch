# 01 — ICP: Geneva Finance (Asset Management / Private Banking / Fintech)

## Target Segment: Geneva Financial Institutions

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** CHF 500M–50B+ AUM / AUA; asset managers, private banks, family offices, fintechs, insurance
- **Revenue:** Management fees, performance fees, advisory retainers, licensing revenue
- **Locations:** Geneva primary (Lac Léman corridor: Nyon–Geneva–Montreux), Zurich secondary
- **Regulatory:** FINMA-supervised (bank, fund manager, insurance); SRO-supervised (vertically integrated)
- **Status:** Post-CS/UBS consolidation, regulatory tightening, AI adoption acceleration

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| CEO / Managing Director | C-suite | Regulatory strategy, competitive positioning, AI transformation |
| CFO / Finance Director | Non-lawyer executive | Reporting obligations, capital requirements, cost pressure |
| CRO / Head of Risk | Risk executive | Market risk, credit risk, operational risk, AI model risk |
| CCO / Compliance Officer | Compliance executive | FINMA/SRO obligations, cross-border reporting, sanctions |
| Head of Technology / CIO | Technology executive | AI adoption, data infrastructure, DORA-equivalent readiness |
| Portfolio Manager / CIO | Investment professional | Alpha generation, ESG integration, regulatory constraints |

### Pain Points (Initial Hypotheses)
1. **Regulatory change overload** — DORA, FINMA AI guidance, CRS 2.0/CARF, stablecoin framework, Fund Management Act revision
2. **AI governance gaps** — no clear framework for AI-enabled investment decisions, robo-advisory, AI trading models
3. **Operational resilience pressure** — ISA incident reporting, outsourcing risk, third-party AI vendor management
4. **Stablecoin/crypto license pathway uncertainty** — FinIA revision creating new license categories, market entry timing
5. **ESG reporting complexity** — SFAMA standards, FINMA nature-related risk disclosure, EU taxonomy alignment
6. **Talent scarcity in quantitative/AI finance** — competition with Big Tech and international hedge funds

### Buying Triggers (Initial Hypotheses)
- FINMA examination findings on AI or operational resilience
- New product launch (crypto, ESG fund, alternative investments)
- Regulatory deadline (DORA parity, CRS 2.0, stablecoin licensing)
- AI-related incident or near-miss
- Competitor launches AI-powered product
- AUM decline triggering cost restructuring

### Buying Barriers (Initial Hypotheses)
- Regulatory approval uncertainty for new AI products
- Data residency and cloud outsourcing constraints (FINMA circular on outsourcing)
- Board/governance committee procurement bureaucracy
- Existing vendor lock-in (Bloomberg, MSCI, BlackRock Aladdin)
- Regulatory capital implications of new risk models

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva Romandie; English universal in finance
- German useful for Zurich-connected institutions
- Institutional, formal relationship style
- Regulatory and fiduciary language dominates
- "Geneva" brand carries international credibility for Middle Eastern, Asian, African UHNW/HNWI clients
