## Findings

1. **FINMA AI Trading and Robo-Advisory Supervision 2026 — First Systematic Framework** — FINMA published supervisory expectations for AI-enabled investment advisory and portfolio management (Q1 2026). Requirements: algorithmic description documentation, model risk management frameworks, human-override mechanisms, and suitability documentation for AI-generated recommendations. Geneva asset managers using or licensing robo-advisory tools face first-time supervisory scrutiny. Advisory opportunity: FINMA AI advisory examination preparedness; algorithmic trading governance framework; AI model risk documentation for Investment Management Act compliance; suitability assessment redesign for AI-generated recommendations. This is a new formal supervisory expectation not covered by prior cycles — distinct from the general AI civil liability framing in C145 Phase 02.

2. **Digital Collateral Management and Tokenized Securities — Geneva Repo Market Modernization** — Swiss repo market (SwissRepoNet) is moving toward DLT-based collateral management; Swiss National Bank exploring tokenized franc for settlement efficiency. Geneva-based commodity trading firms and banks are early adopters of digital collateral pledge structures. FINMA issued DLT tokenized securities guidance (2025): tokenized securities qualify as fungible bearer book-entry securities if specific conditions met. Advisory opportunity: tokenized collateral legal structure design; SwissRepoNet DLT migration advisory; digital securities custody documentation for Geneva commodity trading firms; cross-border repo collateral optimization for Geneva banks with EU operations. No Geneva boutique currently positioned in this specific niche.

3. **Geneva Commodity Trading — Sanctions/AML AI Compliance for Physical Commodities** — Geneva commodity trading firms (Vitol, Trafigura, Mercuria, CLP Group) face acute AI compliance pressure: AI-driven trade execution must integrate OFAC/SEM/fusion-center sanctions screening; AML obligations under revised Swiss Anti-Money Laundering Act (GwG) for physical commodity trades include beneficial ownership documentation. Geneva's role as global commodity trading hub (ranked #2 globally by volume) creates demand for specialized AI compliance advisory: sanctions AI model validation, AML transaction monitoring for commodity trade finance, counterparty screening for embargoed jurisdictions. Advisory opportunity: AI sanctions screening model validation for Geneva commodity firms; AML compliance framework for AI-driven trade execution; EU Dual-Use export control AI compliance for Geneva tech-adjacent commodity firms. Genuinely underserved niche — no Geneva boutique combines commodity trade finance expertise with AI compliance.

4. **Swiss Pension Fund AI Governance — BVG Reform and ASIP Implementation 2026** — Swiss pension fund reform (BVG revision) passed Parliament; ASIP (Swiss Pension Fund Association) updated guidelines on digital asset allocation and AI-assisted investment decisions. 2026 implementation creates new compliance obligations for Geneva-based pension funds (CH PP): AI model governance for investment decisions, cybersecurity obligations for AI-managed portfolios, disclosure requirements for AI use in asset allocation. Pension funds in Geneva (often hosted by banks/insurance groups) face Board-level pressure to document AI use in investment processes. Advisory opportunity: AI governance framework for Geneva CH PP funds; ASIP guideline implementation advisory; AI investment decision documentation for Board reporting; cybersecurity framework for AI-managed pension assets. This is a distinct ICP from private banks or asset managers.

5. **Geneva Family Office AI Technology Stack — Underserved Cross-Segment Opportunity** — Geneva hosts 2,000+ family offices (estimated, per Swiss Banking Association). Sophisticated multi-generational families increasingly require: AI-powered consolidated wealth reporting, multi-jurisdiction tax AI tools (CRS/FALD optimization), AI-driven succession planning, and AI governance for single-family office investment processes. Prior cycles focused on institutional FIs; family offices are a distinct buyer with less regulatory overhead and faster decision cycles. Advisory opportunity: family office AI technology stack assessment; multi-jurisdiction AI tax compliance; AI governance for single-family offices without institutional compliance infrastructure; AI-powered wealth continuity planning. Geneva family offices are relationship-driven buyers — shorter sales cycle than institutional FIs.

6. **Swiss Sustainable Finance Label (Swiss Sustainable Finance) AI Integration — Geneva ESG Fund Positioning** — Swiss Sustainable Finance association launched updated ESG fund labeling criteria aligned with EU Taxonomy and SFDR. Geneva asset managers with sustainability-labeled funds must implement AI-driven portfolio monitoring for ESG metric tracking (carbon footprint, biodiversity exposure, social criteria). AI tools for ESG data aggregation, greenwashing detection, and regulatory reporting are increasingly required to maintain Swiss and EU label equivalence. Advisory opportunity: AI-powered ESG fund compliance framework for Geneva managers; Swiss-EU sustainable finance label alignment advisory; AI ESG data infrastructure for Geneva green bond issuers; Swiss Climate Disclosure Law (in force 2025) AI compliance for Geneva investment firms. Distinct from TNFD nature-related risk (C145 P2 Finding 4) — this focuses on the ESG label maintenance and AI tooling angle.
