## Cycle 19 | Date: 2026-03-30 | Vertical: Boutique Wealth Management

## ICP: The Geneva Boutique Wealth Manager

### Demographics
- **Firm type:** Independent boutique wealth manager / family office (Geneva-based; also Vaud, Zurich as secondary)
- **AUM range:** CHF 200M–2B (typically single-family office or small multi-family office)
- **Staff:** 3–12 employees (portfolio managers, relationship managers, operations, compliance)
- **Client type:** UHNWI / HNW families; entrepreneurs, inheritors, corporate executives
- **Technology posture:** Active AI adoption in portfolio analytics, risk modeling, client reporting, compliance monitoring
- **Decision-maker:** Managing Partner / CIO (often the founder or senior partner; owns compliance risk)
- **Regulatory posture:** FINMA-regulated or SRO-member; subject to FinSA, FinSO, AMLA, CARF

### Pain Points (Validated by Research)
1. **Triple regulatory pressure:** CARF live (Jan 1 2026), AMLA/LETA H2 2026 (~3-6mo), FATF evaluation 2026-2027 — overlapping obligations simultaneously
2. **AI governance gap:** Using AI for portfolio management, client reporting, risk modeling without clear framework
3. **SRO membership obligations:** AMLA scope expansion includes lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs — triggering SRO membership discussions
4. **EU AI Act overlap:** Wealth managers with EU clients face extraterritorial EU AI Act obligations
5. **FATF mutual evaluation creating urgency:** Switzerland's 2026-2027 FATF evaluation elevates AMLA compliance from technical to strategic
6. **CARF/CRS 2.0 already live:** Automated exchange of financial account information — retroactive compliance and new AI applications in scope

### Trigger Events
- **FATF mutual evaluation (2026-2027):** Creates external pressure for Swiss AMLA compliance — elevating WM compliance posture
- **AMLA/LETA implementing ordinance publication:** Confirms exact scope and timeline for H2 2026
- **FINMA AI guidance circular:** Any FINMA movement on AI guidance for wealth managers creates urgency
- **SIF "Switzerland as a fintech nation" follow-up:** SIF confirmed March 27 2026 engagement — follow-up publications likely
- **CARF enforcement case:** First enforcement action using CARF data would validate the live obligation

### Timing Assessment (as of March 30, 2026)
- **CARF live:** OPERATIVE since Jan 1 2026 (~3 months already)
- **AMLA/LETA H2 2026:** ~3-6 MONTHS — implementing ordinance expected
- **FATF mutual evaluation:** 2026-2027 — external pressure elevating AMLA
- **EU AI Act high-risk:** Aug 2 2026 ~4.5 MONTHS (if EU nexus)
- **Swissdamed spillover:** ~13 WEEKS (healthcare clients' AI governance cascade)
- **SIF sustainable finance:** "Major opportunity" confirmed Mar 27 2026 — ESG AI applications in scope

### Regulatory Context Summary
**3-Layer Compliance Stack:**
1. **Swiss domestic:** FinSA/FinSO conduct rules, FINMA Circ. 2026/1 (nature risks), SRO obligations
2. **Swiss international:** CARF/CRS 2.0 (live Jan 1 2026), AMLA/LETA H2 2026, FATF mutual evaluation
3. **EU extraterritorial:** EU AI Act (if EU client nexus), GDPR + AI Act overlap

### Objections & Responses
| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "We already have a compliance team" | CARF has been live since Jan 1. AI applications in portfolio management, reporting, and risk modeling are new obligations most existing compliance frameworks don't cover |
| "FATF evaluation is Switzerland's problem, not ours" | FATF evaluation triggers SRO/FINMA scrutiny at the firm level. Wealth managers are a primary evaluation target |
| "We're too small for EU AI Act" | If you have EU clients, EU-directed output, or your AI systems process EU data subjects' information, extraterritorial reach applies |
| "Our technology vendor handles AI compliance" | Technology vendors handle system compliance. You retain client-facing accountability for advice, reporting, and AI-assisted decisions |
