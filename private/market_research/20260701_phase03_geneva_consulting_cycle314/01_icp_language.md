# ICP Language: Geneva Consulting — C314 Phase 03

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems — Bilingual EN + FR

---

## Overview

The Geneva boutique consulting senior partner (2–10 professionals), personally accountable under nFADP Art.63 for AI use on client deliverables, has crossed the C313 threshold ("I thought the proposal was confidential. It isn't anymore.") and now speaks in C314 language. The C314 language is shaped by three convergent forces: the EU AI Act ~30-day deadline (late August 2026), the EU corporate client RFP channel that now explicitly references the EU AI Act, and the nFADP Art.63 personal liability that names the senior partner by role. The senior partner has stopped talking about "client methodology" or "engagement letters." The senior partner is now talking about the EU AI Act question that arrived in the last client RFP.

The C314 ICP language is short, time-stamped, and references a named sub-consultant or a named client deliverable. It is not "we need an AI governance framework." It is "the EU client's RFP now asks who is responsible for the AI use on this engagement, by name, and I am."

The C314 P03 language is structurally distinct from C314 P01 (legal — "T-minus 30 days. I don't know if I'm in scope.") and C314 P02 (finance — "My institutional client just asked for EU AI Act readiness. The deadline is in 30 days. I am the answer they need, and I don't have it."). The consulting senior partner's language has three additional characteristics that legal and finance do not share:

1. **Sub-consultant visibility.** The consulting senior partner routinely names the sub-consultant in the language — "my sub-consultant on the regulatory workstream used AI on the deliverable" — because the sub-consultant is the most structurally fragile link in the consulting AI chain and is now being asked about by EU clients.
2. **Methodology-as-deliverable specificity.** The consulting senior partner references specific deliverables ("the market sizing model," "the regulatory mapping memo," "the implementation roadmap") because the deliverable is the unit of personal accountability under the EU AI Act, not the firm or the engagement.
3. **Documented client conversations.** The consulting senior partner references documented client conversations ("the kickoff call," "the steering committee meeting," "the methodology review session") because these conversations now create nFADP Art.63 personal exposure when AI is used to summarize, analyze, or document them.

## ENGLISH

### Headline Sentence (C314 Phase 03 — Consulting)

**"My EU client just asked who is responsible for the AI use on this engagement, by name. The deadline is in 30 days. The answer is me. The named individual. Across four regimes. And I cannot produce the documentation that proves it."**

This sentence captures the C314 P03 transition: the EU client RFP inquiry has named the senior partner personally (not the firm), the EU AI Act deployer obligations apply by use on the engagement (not by firm location), the named individual requirement applies across four regimes (nFADP Art.63, EU AI Act Article 26, carrier attestation, contractual confidentiality), and the documentation gap is now visible in a specific RFP response window.

### Tone Markers

- **Time pressure dominates.** Phrases like "30 days," "end of August," "next RFP cycle," "engagement renewal," appear in nearly every statement.
- **Personal accountability is named.** The senior partner uses "I," not "we." The senior partner does not delegate the language to operations or compliance. The senior partner owns the question.
- **The sub-consultant is named or implicitly present.** The senior partner references "my sub-consultant on the regulatory workstream," "my analytics sub-consultant," "my implementation sub-consultant." The sub-consultant is no longer invisible — it is the most fragile link in the AI chain.
- **Cross-regime convergence is recognized.** The senior partner references nFADP Art.63, EU AI Act Article 26, carrier attestation, and client contractual confidentiality in the same breath. The regimes are not separate conversations.
- **The deliverable is the unit of accountability.** The senior partner does not reference "the firm" or "the practice" — they reference "the deliverable," "the methodology," "the kickoff call summary." The personal liability attaches to the named deliverable, not to the firm.

### Vocabulary Drift — What the C314 P03 ICP Stops Saying

- "Sub-consultant engagement" — replaced by "sub-consultant AI authorization"
- "Methodology" — replaced by "AI-assisted methodology documentation"
- "Engagement letter" — replaced by "engagement letter with AI clause architecture and EU AI Act disclosure"
- "Client confidentiality" — replaced by "client methodology confidentiality through documented AI chain"
- "We are AI-enabled" — replaced by "I am the named deployer for the AI used on this engagement"
- "Sub-consultant management" — replaced by "sub-consultant AI authorization chain with documented handover"
- "Annual review" — replaced by "per-engagement documentation refreshed at engagement change"
- "Knowledge management" — replaced by "AI training data audit including sub-consultant AI processing"

### Marqueurs de Ton (FR)

- **La pression temporelle domine.** « 30 jours », « fin août », « prochain cycle RFP », « renouvellement de mission ».
- **La responsabilité personnelle est nommée.** L'associé principal utilise « je », pas « nous ».
- **Le sous-consultant est nommé ou implicitement présent.** « Mon sous-consultant sur le flux de travail réglementaire », « mon sous-consultant analytique », « mon sous-consultant mise en œuvre ». Le sous-consultant n'est plus invisible.
- **La convergence inter-régimes est reconnue.** Article 63 nFADP, article 26 du règlement IA, attestation d'assureur, confidentialité contractuelle client — mentionnés dans la même respiration.
- **Le livrable est l'unité de responsabilité.** L'associé principal ne fait pas référence à « le cabinet » ou « la pratique » — il fait référence à « le livrable », « la méthodologie », « le résumé de l'appel de démarrage ». La responsabilité personnelle s'attache au livrable nommé.

## Service Naming Implication

The C314 P03 service is named to match the language. Not "AI governance for Geneva consulting." Not "EU AI Act compliance package." The C314 P03 service names what the senior partner actually needs:

**"The Geneva Boutique Consulting EU AI Act 30-Day Deployer Readiness Pack"**

The pack contains:
1. **Article 3 deployer applicability memo** — which client engagements are in scope, by named deliverable
2. **Sub-consultant AI authorization chain documentation system** — refreshed at engagement change, signed by both senior partner and sub-consultant
3. **Methodology-as-deliverable AI use disclosure language** — Article 50 transparency for engagement letters, annual statements, deliverable cover pages
4. **Documented client conversation protocol** — AI transcription + AI summarization consent language, nFADP Art.63 personal liability framing
5. **nFADP Art.63 personal liability map** — senior-partner-named, sub-consultant-named, deliverable-named coverage across four regimes
6. **FINMA-adjacent AI governance pack** — for when the consulting firm's fintech client is asked by FINMA about sub-advisor AI governance under Circular 2026/03 §7.4

The naming owns the C314 P03 category. The first Geneva boutique consulting service to publish this exact six-module 30-day deployer readiness pack owns the EU AI Act deployer accountability category for the EU-client-serving Geneva boutique consulting market.
