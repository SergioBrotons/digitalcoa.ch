### Finding 1 of 6: Vendor-Coordinated Swissdamed Notice Campaign as Commercial Strategy Disguised as Compliance Notification

**The finding:** As the July 1, 2026 Swissdamed registration deadline approaches, AI tool vendors are sending coordinated communications to Geneva independent practices suggesting their AI tools may require registration. The communications are designed to position the vendor as the solution to the compliance question they are simultaneously creating anxiety about. The vendor determines classification (conflict of interest), offers to help with registration (commercial dependency creation), and does not provide neutral, proportionate assessment for independent practice use context.

**The mechanism:** Vendor creates compliance anxiety → vendors positions as resolution → independent practice, with no other Geneva-calibrated authority to consult, accepts vendor determination as final. The commercial interest in broad classification interpretation is not disclosed. The physician's interest in proportionate compliance is not aligned with the vendor's commercial interest.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific vendor-coordinated notice campaign as a market behavior, not the Swissdamed deadline as a regulatory obligation. Prior cycles identified the Swissdamed regulatory timeline as a pressure factor. C300 identifies the specific commercial infrastructure — vendor-driven anxiety monetization — as the active market mechanism in the 5-week window before the deadline.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Votre vendor vous a envoyé un avis — mais le vendor a un intérêt commercial à ce que vous vous enregistriez. La vraie question est : est-ce que votre outil, tel que vous l'utilisez dans votre cabinet, répond réellement aux critères de classification à votre échelle ? Personne.ne va vous donner une réponse neutre — sauf si vous savez quoi demander."*

### Finding 2 of 6: Carrier Attestation Format Evolution as Documentation Infrastructure Gap Revealer

**The finding:** Carrier AI attestation forms have evolved from generic "do you use AI" questions to version-specific, audit-trail-demanding questions. The evolution is significant: prior form versions allowed the physician to respond generically with an acceptable degree of imprecision. Current versions ask for evidence of infrastructure the physician does not have, that the physician can now identify. The gap between attestation standard and practice capability is now visible to the physician in a way it was not in prior cycles.

**The mechanism:** Carrier format evolution forces the physician to confront the documentation infrastructure gap directly — not through abstract understanding of what documentation "should" exist, but through specific questions they cannot answer with current documentation. The current carrier form is an inventory of what the practice does not have.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific attestation format evolution as a market forcing function — not the attestation as a general pressure concept, but the evolved format as the specific mechanism that makes the documentation gap visible and acute. Distinct from C214–C296 carrier attestation pressure which named the general pressure without identifying the format evolution as a discrete accelerant.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Le formulaire d'attestation a changé. Les questions d'il y a six mois permettaient une marge. Les questions d'aujourd'hui exigent des preuves précises — version de l'outil, infrastructure de suivi, documentation de mise à jour. Ce n'est pas un hasard. L'assureur voit quelque chose que les cabinets qui déposent des sinistres liés à l'IA commencent à révéler. La question 'êtes-vous prêt ?' est visible maintenant dans des termes très spécifiques."*

### Finding 3 of 6: Vendor Contract Exit Barrier Mechanism as Governance Improvement Blocker

**The finding:** When a Geneva independent practice attempts to improve AI governance by switching to a better-proportione AI tool, it discovers that vendor contracts contain data portability limitations, model training data retention provisions, and termination barriers that make exit structurally complex or commercially punitive. The physician who wants to govern better cannot make the governance change because of accumulated vendor dependency created in a click-wrap agreement when AI governance was not on their radar.

**The mechanism:** The physician accepted standard vendor terms during initial AI tool deployment (when governance awareness was low). Those terms created switching costs: accumulated patient data in vendor system, AI-assisted workflow dependency, contract terms binding future vendor relationships. When the physician later attempts to evaluate alternative tools or implement governance improvement through vendor change, the exit barriers prevent the resolution. The governance improvement action is structurally blocked by commercial terms from a prior decision state.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific vendor exit barrier as a discrete mechanism blocking governance improvement — not shadow AI as an ungoverned stack (C214–C296), but the specific inability to change vendors as a governance resolution path. Distinct from C287–C295 shadow AI finding which named presence of multiple ungoverned tools without identifying the vendor exit barrier mechanism that prevents resolution through choice.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Si vous décidiez aujourd'hui de changer d'outil IA pour des raisons de conformité, que découvrez-vous ? Votre contrat vendor contient des limitations de portabilité des données. Les données patients accumulées ne sont pas facilement transférables. Les conditions de formation du modèle могут être retenues. Le coût de changement que vous n'avez pas negotiate est maintenant empêchant l'amélioration de gouvernance dont vous avez besoin. Ce n'est pas un problème technologique — c'est un problème contractuel."*

### Finding 4 of 6: nFADP Art.9 Processor Chain Discovery as Present-Tense Accountability Activation

**The finding:** As physicians begin seriously evaluating their AI governance state, they are discovering the vendor sub-processor chain created through click-through vendor agreements. Under nFADP Art.9, the physician as data controller has obligations regarding third-country processor transfers. Most practices have never evaluated their vendor's sub-processor list. The discovery occurs at the moment of governance evaluation — when the physician is most exposed because they now know the chain exists and cannot produce the documentation to evaluate it.

**The mechanism:** The physician accepts vendor terms, which include data processing provisions and sub-processor authorizations. The physician never sees the sub-processor list. The physician begins taking AI governance seriously (often triggered by carrier attestation or Swissdamed notice). During governance review, the physician discovers the processor chain is an undocumented accountability. The discovery produces immediate anxiety — the physician is now accountable for something they cannot enumerate.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific timing mechanism — processor chain discovery occurring at the moment of governance evaluation, not at adoption — as a discrete activation trigger. Distinct from C298/C299 nFADP Art.9 naming which addressed the obligation generally without isolating the specific discovery timing mechanism that produces acute accountability anxiety in the May 2026 market.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Êtes-vous au courant que vous êtes responsable de la chaîne de sous-traitance de votre vendor ? Chaque entity qui traite les données de vos patients via votre outil d'IA — et qui se trouve hors de Suisse — crée une obligation nFADP Art.9 dont vous êtes responsable. La plupart des vendors ne publient pas cette liste automatiquement. La plupart des physicians ne savent pas qu'ils doivent la demander. La question n'est pas de savoir si votre vendor est conforme — c'est de savoir si vous avez evaluate ce que votre vendor vous a divulgué."*

### Finding 5 of 6: Retroactive Documentation Impossibility as Naming of Architectural Governance Failure

**The finding:** When a Geneva independent practice attempts to produce retroactive documentation of AI governance decisions, it confronts a documentation paradox: for tools activated through software updates, unsolicited EHR integrations, or vendor-driven feature activations 12-24 months ago, no deliberative decision event occurred. Documentation requires a decision. Where no decision occurred, there is nothing to document. The attempt to build retroactive documentation produces either fabricated decision events (fraudulent compliance) or honest empty pages (revealing architectural absence). The physician understands for the first time that the governance gap is not procedural — it is architectural, and architecture cannot be retrofitted.

**The mechanism:** The physician approaches documentation as if it can be constructed after the fact: "I will document what we decided about AI tool X." This approach fails because the underlying assumption is wrong — there was no decision to document. The AI tool features were activated by default, by update, by vendor push, without the physician's deliberative involvement. The governance gap is structural: it was created in the architecture of how AI tools entered the practice, not in the procedures that followed.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific retroactive documentation impossibility as a discrete crisis — distinct from all prior Phase 04 documentation gap findings which named the void without isolating the specific mechanism that explains why retroactive documentation cannot close the gap. Distinct from C297 documentation gap naming which treated the gap as procedural without naming the architectural origin.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Quand vous essayez de documenter rétrospectivement votre gouvernance d'IA, vous rencontrez un problème : pour les outils activés il y a 18 mois via des mises à jour que vous n'avez pas demandées, il n'y a pas de décision à documenter. Les décisions de gouvernance qui n'ont jamais été prises ne peuvent pas être documentées après coup. Ce n'est pas un problème de procédure — c'est un problème structurel. построить documentation rétrospectivement ne résout pas le problème de gouvernance. La solution est architecturale et prospective."*

### Finding 6 of 6: FMH Human Oversight Operational Translation Absence as Named Practice-Size Problem

**The finding:** FMH human oversight requirement for AI tools in medical practice exists without operational definition at independent practice scale. The requirement is real in FMH professional standards. The operational meaning is absent from any published Geneva guidance. "Human oversight" interpreted by a 3-5 physician independent practice where one physician holds user/admin/complained roles by default is not the FMH standard being described in the publications — but the publications offer no alternative for this practice size. The physician cannot determine what documented human oversight looks like for their specific operational context.

**The mechanism:** FMH publications establish intent-based standard (physician intends to oversee AI tool use). The carrier attestation evidence standard is output-based (physician can demonstrate oversight occurred for each AI-influenced decision). The gap between intent and output is not addressed by FMH. The physician trying to fulfill the FMH requirement using carrier attestation as evidence guide discovers that FMH's principle and the documentation requirement use different frameworks. Neither the FMH principle nor the carrier evidence standard provides a proportionate methodology for a 3-physician practice.

**Why this is distinct from C214–C299:** This finding names the specific practice-size dependency of the human oversight operational gap — "What does human oversight look like at 8:30 AM Wednesday for a solo physician?" is a different question from "What does human oversight mean in principle?" Prior Phase 04 findings named FMH principle-without-operational-endpoint without isolating the specific scaling problem that makes the translation gap acute precisely at 2-10 physician independent practice scale.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Le FMH dit surveillance humaine. Ça ne dit pas ce que ça ressemble le mercredi à 8h30 pour un cabinet de trois médecins. Le FMH publishing dit 'médecin garde la responsabilité.' Ce n'est pas une définition operationnelle. 'Gardez la responsabilité' ne vous dit pas ce que vous devez documenter, comment vous le faites, et ce que l'assureur attend comme preuve. Je peux vous montrer ce que surveillance humaine documentée ressemble pour votre taille de cabinet."*

## Business Positioning

The Geneva market for independent practice AI accountability services has specific characteristics as of May 2026:
- Swissdamed 5-week deadline creates acute, time-bound pressure
- Carrier format evolved to require documentation infrastructure most practices don't have
- Vendor notice campaign active in market
- No Geneva-calibrated service exists for independent practice Swissdamed classification determination
- No pre-signing carrier review for medical practice attestation (unlike finance sector)
- No FMH operational standard for small practice oversight
- No Art.22/record integrity protocol
- No vendor contract exit barrier assessment service

The C300 findings confirm: the market has progressed from awareness gap to active exposure management. The positioning that works now is not awareness-raising — it is consequence-resolution. Naming specific failures, providing specific methodology, positioning the briefing as the resolution pathway for physicians who are already in the acute exposure phase.
