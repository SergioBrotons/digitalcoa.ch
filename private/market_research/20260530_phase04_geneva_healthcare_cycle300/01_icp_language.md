# 01 — ICP Language: Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability
## Phase 04 C300 | Geneva Healthcare | Independent Physician / Small Group Practice (2–10 physicians)

---

## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

### Primary Language Register

The Geneva independent physician approaches AI accountability through the lens of their license. The conversation is never abstract for these ICPs — every AI governance question is absorbed as a threat to the professional existence they have spent fifteen years building. They do not frame their AI problem in compliance language. They frame it in terms of what is at stake for their ability to practice.

Core language they use:
- *"Mon cabinet est à mon nom"* — The practice is in their name. The liability follows the name.
- *"Si je perds ma reconnaissance FMH, je perds tout"* — If FMH recognition is lost, everything is lost.
- *"Je ne peux pas me permettre un cas"* — They cannot afford a case. Not a liability case — a licensing case.
- *"Mon patient me fait confiance, pas à l'outil"* — The patient trusts them, not the tool. This is both ethical conviction and legal fact.
- *"Je ne sais pas听不懂 ce que levendor me dit sur la conformité"* — Vendor compliance language is听不懂 (unintelligible) to them.
- *"Le FMH devrait nous donner une réponse, pas des principes"* — FMH offers principles where they need answers.

### Emotional Topology

**Layer 1 — Existential license threat (highest intensity):**
The physician understands that unlike a fine, a licensing action cannot be paid away. The accountabilities accumulated over a career are at stake. This creates urgency that financial exposure cannot replicate.

**Layer 2 — Carrier attestation arrival (acute trigger):**
The carrier document arrives with a deadline. The physician has not prepared. They do not have an AI governance framework. They do not know what they are signing. They sign anyway because the alternative is coverage interruption.

**Layer 3 — Patient confidentiality extension:**
Art.321 CP obligations feel intuitively understood — the physician knows what professional secrecy means. What they do not understand is how AI tool data processing extends those obligations to vendor-accessible information they cannot control.

**Layer 4 — Swissdamed deadline proximity (July 1, 2026):**
The deadline is now approximately 5 weeks away. This creates a concrete pressure point that abstract governance debates cannot. The physician has received vendor communications suggesting their AI tools may require registration. They do not know what to do with this information.

**Layer 5 — Peer isolation:**
No one in their immediate professional circuit is publicly navigating this. The conversations that happen are private, not open. Each practice is building its response independently, in parallel, without shared methodology.

### French-Language Geneva Register Markers

- *"À mon échelle"* — At my scale. This is the phrase that names the central problem. What works at hospital scale does not work à mon échelle.
- *"Un cabinet comme le mien"* — A practice like mine. The physician is aware the governance problem is sized for their specific practice type.
- *"Mon nom est sur le dossier"* — My name is on the record. This is the irreducible accountability fact.
- *"Je ne peux pas me permettre de ne pas savoir"* — I cannot afford not to know. License exposure is not insurable for most independent physicians.
- *"Les questions de l'assureur me font peur"* — The carrier's questions frighten me. Carrier attestation triggers fear, not action — because the physician cannot see a path to adequate response.
- *"Ce que je ne sais pas me fait plus peur que ce que je sais"* — What they do not know frightens them more than what they know. The shadow AI surface, unknown to the signing physician, is more threatening than the known AI surface.

### The Framing They Respond To

**They respond to:** License-centric framing. Not "you may be fined" but "your ability to practice is what is at risk." This resonates because it names what they actually cannot absorb.

**They do not respond to:** Abstract compliance frameworks. "ISO 27001 equivalent" or "NIST alignment" produces response failure — these frameworks are designed for organizations with infrastructure the independent practice does not have.

**They respond to:** Named, present-tense problems described in their own operational terms. "The carrier asks you to certify your AI governance is adequate. You don't know what adequate means at your scale. You sign anyway because coverage continuity requires it." This they recognize.

**They do not respond to:** Future-risk framing. "In 2027 this may become an issue" does not move them. Present-tense exposure (Swissdamed in 5 weeks, carrier attestation arriving now) moves them.

### What Triggers Movement vs. What Produces Paralysis

**Produces movement:**
- Carrier attestation with a deadline — acute, concrete, cannot be deferred
- Swissdamed registration question arriving from vendor — specific, finite, has a date
- A colleague who had a case — peer experience translates where general information does not
- Patient presenting with AI-generated health analysis — direct, in-record, creates immediate documentation anxiety

**Produces paralysis:**
- FMH principles without operational endpoint — generates no actionable path
- General AI governance frameworks — enterprise tools create intimidation
- "Proportionate" without scale reference — term is meaningless until the practice size is specified
- Multi-year regulatory timeline — sounds distant enough to defer

### The Specific Silence They Keep

There is a problem the Geneva independent physician cannot name publicly, even to a colleague they trust: their EHR and practice management tools are making decisions about their own accountability in ways they did not architect, cannot enumerate, and cannot undo without losing core functionality.

French verbal marker for this unspeakable problem: *"Je ne suis pas maître de—"* (I am not master of—)

Completion varies: the AI system they activated without evaluation, the documentation architecture built by default, the vendor data flows they accepted in click-through terms, the model updates that changed tool behavior without notice.

This is the ICP's most acute unformulated anxiety. The accountability they cannot delegate. The control they cannot recover. The vendor relationship they cannot exit without losing the practice management system.

## ICP Language Distinction from Prior Cycles

The Phase 04 C300 ICP language is distinct from prior phase cycles (C214–C299) in that it isolates the specific linguistic markers of the clinician who has received vendor communications about Swissdamed registration and carrier attestation simultaneously, and who is now navigating the intersection between a July 1, 2026 medical device registration deadline and an undefined AI governance adequacy standard — without a Geneva-calibrated methodology for either determination.

Prior cycles named carrier pressure, documentation void, and FMH principle-without-operational-endpoint. C300 names the specific cluster of simultaneous obligations converging over the same 5-week window: Swissdamed medical device classification + carrier attestation adequacy + FMH undefined oversight standard — and maps these to the specific linguistic register of the Geneva independent practice physician.
