# Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting: Competitive Landscape
## Cycle 70 | 2026-04-02 | Geneva Consulting AI Governance

## Competitive Tier Analysis

### Tier 1: Global Consulting Majors
**Players:** McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Accenture, Deloitte
**Strengths:** Global reach, established AI governance practices, EU AI Act capacity
**Weaknesses:** Expensive; generic frameworks; no Swiss-specific positioning

### Tier 2: Swiss Specialist Consultancies
**Players:** Innosys, Keyabest, Swiss specialist firms
**Strengths:** Swiss regulatory depth; FADP/FINMA knowledge; EN/FR capacity
**Weaknesses:** Limited AI governance specific capacity; small scale

### Tier 3: Geneva Consulting Boutiques
**Strengths:** Geneva positioning; international client base; bilingual EN/FR
**Weaknesses:** No AI governance practice; no GPAI specific knowledge; most exposed

### Tier 4: Independent Consultants
**Strengths:** Specialist depth; flexible
**Weaknesses:** No full-service; no GPAI conformity assessment capacity

## Competitive Gap: Consulting AI Governance Advisory
- Consulting firms advise on AI governance but haven't fixed their own GPAI frameworks
- No Geneva consulting boutique offering GPAI self-assessment + client advisory
- SwissHoldings self-regulation preference: consulting opportunity for self-regulation framework advisory
- August 2, 2026 deadline: consulting firms need own GPAI conformity before credibly advising

## Positioning Recommendations
- GPAI conformity for consulting firms: first-mover advisory opportunity
- Swiss self-regulation framework: advisory product for Swiss multinationals
- Digital bureaucracy simplification: pre-AI governance process optimization
- Bilingual EN/FR consulting AI governance: limits competition
