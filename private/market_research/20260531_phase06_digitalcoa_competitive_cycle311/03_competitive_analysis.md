## Competitive Landscape Overview

The Geneva SMB AI accountability market has five distinct competitor categories. Each operates in a different positioning space. None address the specific question Sergio answers: evidence architecture for the professional whose name is on the carrier form.

---

## The Five Competitor Categories

### Category 1: Global Professional Services (Big Four)

**Players:** PwC Switzerland, EY Switzerland, KPMG Switzerland, Deloitte Switzerland

**Positioning:** Enterprise AI governance, regulatory compliance, organizational risk management

**Target client:** Large corporations, financial institutions, multinationals with compliance teams

**Service model:** Large engagement teams, multi-month projects, organizational policy documents

**Pricing:** CHF 50,000–500,000+ for enterprise engagements

**Strengths:**
- Brand recognition and institutional credibility
- Regulatory relationships and regulatory intelligence
- Global methodology and benchmarking
- Large teams with sector specialists

**Weaknesses for Geneva SMB:**
- Engagement size inappropriate for small firms (10-50 professionals)
- Pricing inaccessible for boutique operations
- Organizational focus doesn't address individual carrier questions
- Methodology designed for large compliance teams, not practice-running professionals
- No retroactive reconstruction offering
- No EN+FR documentation architecture

**The gap they leave:** The individual professional whose name is on the carrier form. The small firm that needs evidence, not a framework.

---

### Category 2: Swiss Law Firm AI Initiatives

**Players:** Major Geneva law firms (Lenz & Staehelin, Pestalozzi, Walder Wyss, Lalive) — AI task forces and working groups

**Positioning:** Internal AI use guidelines, associate AI training, client matter disclosure protocols

**Target client:** Internal only — no external service offering

**Service model:** Internal organizational development, knowledge management

**Pricing:** Internal cost center, not revenue-generating

**Strengths:**
- Peer credibility within legal community
- Understanding of carrier form dynamics from inside
- Regulatory context from practice experience

**Weaknesses:**
- Internal focus — no external client service
- Firm-level policy doesn't address individual exposure
- No service offering for other verticals
- No retroactive documentation
- No EN+FR evidence architecture for external use

**The gap they leave:** Other verticals (finance, consulting, healthcare). External clients. Individual accountability evidence.

---

### Category 3: nFADP / Data Protection Compliance Consultancies

**Players:** Swiss data protection consultancies, privacy law firms, GDPR/nFADP specialist firms

**Positioning:** nFADP compliance for AI systems, Article 21 transparency documentation, DPIAs for AI

**Target client:** Data protection officers, organizations processing personal data with AI systems

**Service model:** Compliance documentation projects, privacy-by-design implementation

**Pricing:** CHF 10,000–80,000 for AI-specific nFADP projects

**Strengths:**
- Deep nFADP expertise
- Article 21 experience
- Privacy documentation methodology

**Weaknesses for Sergio's market:**
- Focus on data protection, not professional liability
- Article 63 not their primary concern
- Carrier attestation not their focus
- No evidence architecture for carrier forms
- No retroactive documentation
- No EN+FR carrier-specific documentation

**The gap they leave:** Professional liability documentation. Carrier attestation evidence. Personal Article 63 exposure.

---

### Category 4: LegalTech Vendors

**Players:** International AI legal platforms, Swiss legal tech startups, document automation vendors with AI features

**Positioning:** AI-powered legal research, contract analysis, document review, compliance checking

**Target client:** Law firms, corporate legal departments, in-house counsel

**Service model:** Software subscriptions, platform licensing, tool functionality

**Pricing:** CHF 500–5,000/month for platforms; one-time implementation fees

**Strengths:**

**Weaknesses:**
- Vendor holds records client is accountable for
- No evidence architecture for carrier forms
- No retroactive documentation
- No individual accountability documentation

**The gap they leave:** The records-production obligation. The vendor-compelled records problem. The client's personal exposure.

---

**Players:** Geneva Business School executive education, Swiss professional associations, online AI governance courses, consulting firms offering workshops

**Positioning:** AI governance knowledge, responsible AI use, understanding AI risks

**Target client:** Professionals seeking AI literacy, organizations training staff

**Service model:** Workshops, seminars, online courses, executive education programs

**Pricing:** CHF 500–5,000 per professional for multi-day programs

## The Six Competitive Findings (Net-New C311 P06)

### Finding 1: No Competitor Offers Evidence Architecture
Every competitor offers frameworks, policies, training, tools, or compliance documentation. None offer what the carrier actually asks for: evidence of what happened.

**The specific gap:** Carrier form asks "can you produce evidence of human oversight?" Every competitor answers "here's a framework for overseeing AI." No competitor answers "here's the evidence you need to produce."

**Sergio position:** Evidence architecture is the only offering that answers the carrier's actual question.

---

### Finding 2: No Competitor Offers Retroactive Reconstruction
Every competitor offers forward-looking services. The market has 12-24 months of undocumented AI use. No competitor offers to reconstruct evidence for what already happened.

**The specific gap:** "Going forward" is not an answer to "I already signed." The professional needs evidence for the period they already certified.

**Sergio position:** Retroactive reconstruction is a distinct, never-offered service that addresses an urgent, universal problem.

---

### Finding 3: No Competitor Addresses the Individual Carrier Attestation
Big Four and law firm task forces address organizational governance. nFADP consultancies address privacy compliance. Training providers address general knowledge. None address the managing partner's individual carrier attestation exposure.

**The specific gap:** The carrier form names an individual. The individual's personal exposure is not addressed by any competitor's organizational or general offering.

**Sergio position:** Carrier form pre-review is the highest-value, lowest-competition intervention point.

---

### Finding 4: No Competitor Closes the Vendor Records Gap
LegalTech vendors create vendor records gaps. Their contracts don't require record production. No competitor offers a vendor records pathway for their clients.

**The specific gap:** Client is accountable for vendor-held records. Vendor has no obligation to produce them. The gap is structural.

**Sergio position:** Vendor records pathway establishment is a service no competitor offers — and the gap is confirmed by every carrier audit.

---

### Finding 5: No Competitor Offers EN+FR Evidence Architecture
Every competitor offers English-language frameworks. Geneva ICPs operate across both languages. No competitor offers evidence architecture designed for English carrier forms and French regulatory proceedings.

**The specific gap:** English carrier form + French regulatory proceeding = documentation mismatch. No competitor addresses both simultaneously.

**Sergio position:** EN+FR evidence architecture is a Geneva-specific market requirement no competitor has built.

---

### Finding 6: No Competitor Differentiates on Personal Liability
Every competitor focuses on organizational compliance, privacy compliance, or knowledge transfer. None focus on the professional's personal liability — the name on the form, the license on the line.

**The specific gap:** "For physicians, evidence is personal. And your license is on the line." No competitor uses this framing. No competitor builds around personal liability.

**Sergio position:** Personal accountability framing is the highest-differentiation competitive position in the Geneva SMB market.

---

## The Competitive Synthesis

None of them offer retroactive reconstruction. None of them close the vendor records gap. None of them offer EN+FR evidence architecture.**

**Sergio answers all five questions simultaneously. That's the competitive position. That's why the market has no equivalent.**
