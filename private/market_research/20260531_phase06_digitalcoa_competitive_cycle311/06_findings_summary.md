## Phase 06 Competitive Findings (Net-New, 6 Total)

### Finding 1: No Competitor Offers Evidence Architecture
Every competitor — Big Four, law firm AI task forces, nFADP consultancies, LegalTech vendors, training providers — offers frameworks, policies, training, or compliance documentation. None offer what the carrier actually asks for: evidence of what happened.

**The gap:** Carrier form asks "can you produce evidence of human oversight?" Every competitor answers "here's a framework for overseeing AI." No competitor answers "here's the evidence you need to produce."

**Market implication:** Evidence architecture is the only offering that answers the carrier's actual question. This is the primary competitive white space.

---

### Finding 2: No Competitor Offers Retroactive Reconstruction
Every competitor offers forward-looking services. The market has 12-24 months of undocumented AI use. No competitor offers to reconstruct evidence for what already happened.

**The gap:** "Going forward" is not an answer to "I already signed." The professional needs evidence for the period they already certified.

**Market implication:** Retroactive reconstruction is a distinct, never-offered service that addresses an urgent, universal problem. It is the highest-immediate-value intervention point.

---

### Finding 3: No Competitor Addresses the Individual Carrier Attestation
Big Four and law firm task forces address organizational governance. nFADP consultancies address privacy compliance. Training providers address general knowledge. None address the managing partner's individual carrier attestation exposure.

**The gap:** The carrier form names an individual. The individual's personal exposure is not addressed by any competitor's organizational or general offering.

**Market implication:** Carrier form pre-review is the highest-value, lowest-competition intervention point. The individual question is unanswered in the market.

---

### Finding 4: No Competitor Closes the Vendor Records Gap
LegalTech vendors create vendor records gaps. Their contracts don't require record production. No competitor offers a vendor records pathway for their clients.

**The gap:** Client is accountable for vendor-held records. Vendor has no obligation to produce them. The gap is structural and universal.

**Market implication:** Vendor records pathway establishment is a service no competitor offers — and the gap is confirmed by every carrier audit. The problem is growing as AI tool adoption increases.

---

### Finding 5: No Competitor Offers EN+FR Evidence Architecture
Every competitor offers English-language frameworks. Geneva ICPs operate across both languages. No competitor offers evidence architecture designed for English carrier forms and French regulatory proceedings.

**The gap:** English carrier form + French regulatory proceeding = documentation mismatch. No competitor addresses both simultaneously.

**Market implication:** EN+FR evidence architecture is a Geneva-specific market requirement no competitor has built. It's not a translation task — it's a design requirement for the documentation itself.

---

### Finding 6: No Competitor Differentiates on Personal Liability
Every competitor focuses on organizational compliance, privacy compliance, or knowledge transfer. None focus on the professional's personal liability — the name on the form, the license on the line.

**The gap:** "For physicians, evidence is personal. And your license is on the line." No competitor uses this framing. No competitor builds around personal liability as the primary positioning anchor.

**Market implication:** Personal accountability framing is the highest-differentiation competitive position in the Geneva SMB market. It applies across all four verticals — legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — as the carrier form names individuals in each.
