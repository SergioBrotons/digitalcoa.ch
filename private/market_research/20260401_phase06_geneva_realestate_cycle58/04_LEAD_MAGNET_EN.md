## The Triple Compounding Pressure

### Pressure 1: The Federal AI Ordinance Compliance Gap (16+ Months Wide)

The Federal AI Ordinance has been in force for over 16 months. If your agency uses AVMs, tenant screening AI, property management AI, or any tool that supports decisions affecting clients, you are likely subject to it — whether you know it or not.

The most acute exposure is the AVM (Automated Valuation Model). AVMs used to support property pricing decisions are likely classified as high-risk AI under the Federal AI Ordinance. High-risk AI requires documented human review before the output affects a client decision.

If you have an AVM on your website that generates property valuations, and a client makes a decision based on that valuation without a human reviewing it first: you may be non-compliant with the Federal AI Ordinance. And you may not have known it.

### Pressure 2: Market Uncertainty From Two Directions

Geneva's luxury property market has a significant international buyer base — HNWI, diaspora buyers, diplomatic community, and G7-affiliated professionals. Two pressures are converging on this market simultaneously:

**Foreign property purchase restrictions:** Swiss policy discussions continue on restricting foreign property purchases. If enacted, these restrictions would directly reduce your addressable market.

**G7 Évian (June 15-17, 2026):** The G7 summit in Évian is now ~75 days away. Security arrangements in Geneva may affect property viewings and transaction logistics. For the international buyer market, G7 creates near-term transaction uncertainty.

Agencies serving international buyers face a compounded market pressure — exactly when they need operational efficiency from AI the most.

### Pressure 3: AI Democratization Without Governance

AI tools for real estate are now accessible to small Geneva agencies. Listing description generation, virtual tour AI, tenant screening automation, market analysis — these tools were previously only available to large agencies with dedicated technology budgets.

This democratization is a double-edged sword: agencies can now operate with AI efficiency at small-agency scale — but governance-free AI adoption creates regulatory exposure precisely when the market is most uncertain.

## The PILOT TRAP: AVMs and the Double-Exposure Problem

The most specific and acute compliance risk for Geneva real estate agencies is the PILOT TRAP:

**P**roperty **I**isting **A**utomated **L**valuation **T**ool **R**isk: **A**ssessment **P**olicy **R**equired

AVMs on property listings create exposure on two frameworks simultaneously:
- **FADP Art. 21:** An automated valuation that affects a client's property decision is likely an automated decision under FADP — human review required.
- **Federal AI Ordinance:** An AVM supporting pricing decisions is likely high-risk AI under the ordinance — documented human oversight required.

If your agency has an AVM generating valuations on your listings without documented human review: you are likely non-compliant on both frameworks simultaneously.

## The Self-Assessment: Is Your Agency PILOT TRAP Compliant?

**Answer these 10 questions:**

1. Have you classified your AI tools according to the Federal AI Ordinance risk categories?
2. For every AVM on your listings: is there documented human review before the valuation is visible to clients?
3. Do you have documented human review processes for tenant screening AI outputs before making rental decisions?
4. Have you assessed whether your property listing description generation AI transfers data outside Switzerland?
5. Do you have a documented escalation process for AI valuation errors?
6. Have you classified your client matching and lead scoring AI according to Federal AI Ordinance risk levels?
7. Does your FADP-compliant data processing documentation include all AI tool usage?
8. For AI tools processing EU-resident client data: have you assessed GPAI implications?
9. Have you informed clients when AI tools were used in their property transaction?
10. Do you have a specific plan to complete your AI governance documentation before the G7 window (June 15, 2026)?
