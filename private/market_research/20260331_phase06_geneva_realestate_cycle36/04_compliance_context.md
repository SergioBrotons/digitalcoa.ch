## Compliance Complexity (RE-Specific)

### Shadow AI in Real Estate
RE agents using unapproved AI for:
- Listing descriptions (ChatGPT, Claude) — client property data shared with US AI
- Property valuation AI — automated valuation models processing personal data
- Tenant screening AI — creditworthiness assessment = high-risk under EU AI Act
- Market analysis AI — aggregate data processing + disclosure

### Retroactive Compliance Challenge
Unlike other verticals preparing for future deadlines, RE firms have:
- Been using AI tools for months/years without governance
- Already shared client property data with US-based AI tools
- Already made valuation/screening decisions with AI input
→ All of this retroactive exposure is already in FADP violation

### DPIA for Property AI
Data Protection Impact Assessment required for:
- AI property valuation tools (high-risk automated decision-making)
- AI tenant screening systems (creditworthiness = EU AI Act Annex III)
- AI market analysis tools (personal data processing)

## Compliance Entry Points

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Real Estate Audit
Primary entry point: Map every AI tool used in property transactions, listings, tenant screening, and valuations. This surfaces retroactive FADP exposure across all RE AI use cases.

### Retroactive Compliance Assessment
Secondary entry point: Assess existing AI deployments against FADP Art. 5(c) disclosure requirements — what was shared, with whom, for what purpose, without disclosure.

### DPIA for High-Risk RE AI
Tertiary entry point: Data Protection Impact Assessment for property valuation AI and tenant screening AI.
