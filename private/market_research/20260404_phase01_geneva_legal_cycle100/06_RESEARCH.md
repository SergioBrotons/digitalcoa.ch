## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 6)

### EN
**Geneva as International Commercial Court — Competitive Repositioning**

Geneva's dispute resolution ecosystem — global competitive context:
- English Commercial Court (London): remains world's leading commercial court for contracts governed by English law; post-Brexit no longer automatically accessible to EU defendants; visa/access barriers for some MENA/African parties; Geneva-based law firms with English law capability actively positioning to capture EU-party disputes
- DIFC Courts (Dubai): growing caseload; DIFC Court of Appeal now final appellate forum for DIFC; English law (substantive) applies in DIFC; Geneva companies with UAE/MENA exposure face dual-jurisdiction risk; DIFC-LCIA arbitration also available
- Singapore International Commercial Court (SICC): English language proceedings; increasingly preferred for Asia-Pacific intra-regional disputes; ICC/LCIA also seated in Singapore; limited utility for European-facing Geneva disputes
- Geneva's distinctive advantages: neutral seat (not EU, not UK, not US — significant for Russia/sanctioned-party disputes, Iran, China); Swiss arbitration record caseload growth (+25% in 2025); Swiss-seated awards enforceable under New York Convention in 170+ states; strong legal infrastructure
- Geneva's challenge: no dedicated international commercial court (unlike DIFC, SICC); parties use Tribunal de première instance (French procedure); English-speaking chambers available but less established than English Commercial Court; potential reform discussion on creating Geneva International Commercial Court (GICC)
- English law as governing law: majority of Geneva-seated commercial contracts use English law as governing law (parties prefer English law substance + Geneva seat); Geneva lawyers advising on English law contracts = significant practice area; law firms with magic circle/london-trained lawyers at premium
- Geneva arbitration institutions: Swiss Arbitration Centre (Geneva) — pre-eminent; ICC International Court of Justice (Paris — but ICC arbitrations frequently seated in Geneva); LCIA (London); AAA/ICDR (New York)
- Geneva lawyers providing: (1) English law contract advice (Swiss-qualified lawyers with English law training); (2) Geneva-seated arbitration advocacy; (3) interim measures (Swiss courts support arbitral proceedings under PILA); (4) enforcement of foreign judgments in Switzerland (bilateral treaties + reciprocity)

### FR
**Genève comme tribunal commercial international — Repositionnement concurrentiel**

Écosystème de règlement des différends de Genève — contexte concurrentiel mondial :
- English Commercial Court (Londres) : demeure le tribunal commercial mondial leader pour contrats soumis au droit anglais ; post-Brexit n'est plus automatiquement accessible aux défendeurs UE ; Genève se positionne pour les différends impliquant des parties UE
- DIFC Courts (Dubaï) : charge de travail croissante ; droit anglais (substantif) applicable au DIFC ; entreprises genevoises avec exposition UAE/MENA face à un risque de double juridiction
- Singapore International Commercial Court (SICC) : procédures en anglais ; de plus en plus préféré pour différends intra-asiatiques ; utilité limitée pour différends européens
- Avantages distinctifs de Genève : siège neutre (ni UE, ni UK, ni US — significatif pour différends impliquant la Russie/sujets sous sanctions, Iran, Chine) ; record d'arbitrage suisse (+25% en 2025) ; sentences suisses exécutables sous Convention de New York dans 170+ États
- Défis de Genève : pas de tribunal commercial international dédié (contrairement au DIFC, SICC) ; tribunaux genevois.apply French procedure ; chambres anglophones moins établies ; discussion potentielle sur la création d'un Geneva International Commercial Court (GICC)
- Droit anglais comme loi applicable : majorité des contrats commerciaux avec siège Genève utilisent le droit anglais comme loi applicable ; avocats genevois conseillant sur contrats de droit anglais = pratique significative
- Institutions d'arbitrage genevoises : Swiss Arbitration Centre (Genève) — prééminent ; Cour internationale d'arbitrage de la CCI (Paris — arbitrages CCI fréquemment siège Genève)
