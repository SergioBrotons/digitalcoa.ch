## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Swiss DLT Act Amendments Expanding Digital Asset Market — Tokenization Advisory
- DLT-FG amendments in progress: expanding tokenized securities scope; lighter regime for lower-threshold tokenized assets
- FINMA RWA tokenization guidance: stablecoin issuance requirements, e-money token classification, asset-referenced token (ART) prudential treatment
- SIX Digital Exchange operational for institutional-grade tokenized securities settlement
- Geneva private banks (UBS, Pictet, Lombard Odier) actively offering tokenized bonds/fund units to HNWI/UHNWI clients
- Cross-border challenge: MiCA (EU) vs. DLT-FG (CH) compliance for Swiss managers serving EU clients
- Advisory entry points: digital asset custody structuring, tokenization regulatory compliance, MiCA/DLT-FG gap analysis, stablecoin issuance advisory

### Finding 02: Cross-Border Remote Work + Platform Work Directive — Geneva Employer Compliance
- No bilateral CH-UE dedicated remote work treaty; Bilateral Agreements free movement governs but creates dual social security/tax nexus for EU employees working remotely from EU
- CH-UK: Mind the Gap arrangement covers some mobility; UK Statutory Residence Test creates tax nexus for Swiss-resident employees working from UK
- Geneva frontalier rules: cross-border remote work beyond 25% of working time may affect L-permit status (OCPM)
- EU Platform Work Directive (Dec 2024, transposition ongoing): reclassification presumption for platform workers; algorithmic transparency; applies to Swiss platforms serving EU users
- Occupational health and safety OR Art. 328 obligations extend to home workstations regardless of employee location
- Advisory: cross-border remote work policy design, L-permit compliance, platform worker classification assessments, remote work agreements

### Finding 03: Swiss Collective Redress Maturing — Class Action Defense + EU RAD Exposure
- FinSA Art. 86-101: opt-in collective proceedings for financial mis-selling; FINMA or recognized consumer org certification; Geneva courts competent nationally
- Swiss Cartel Act Art. 12a: follow-on damages after WEKO decisions; private enforcement growing post-2022 revision
- Climate claims: BGer 2023 rejected standing for climate claims under current law; cantonal legislative initiatives in Geneva/Zurich underway
- EU RAD (transposed 2023): Swiss traders serving EU consumers face representative actions; defensive compliance demand
- Geneva as preferred forum: internationally recognized judgments, potentially English-language proceedings
- Advisory: defendant-side class action defense strategy, securities litigation preparedness, RAD compliance for Swiss-EU consumer-facing businesses

### Finding 04: Swiss IP Box Regime + AI Inventorship — Geneva Pharma/Biotech Advisory
- Swiss IP box: up to 90% cantonal/city tax relief on qualifying IP income; Geneva implementation competitive but requires nexus approach compliance
- Federal NID regime available for non-qualifying IP income; wealth tax implications for IP assets on balance sheet in Geneva
- EPO Geneva sub-office: ~40% of European patent filings processed through Geneva; growing unitary patent demand from Swiss companies
- AI inventorship: Switzerland follows EPO Ballweg (2020) — AI cannot be inventor; human must be identifiable; no pending reform
- Geneva pharma/biotech cluster (Novartis, Roche, 200+ biotechs): patent portfolio management, freedom-to-operate opinions, licensing agreements = significant advisory market
- Patent thickets in biotech: collaborative patent pools emerging as litigation alternative
- Advisory: IP box optimization, patent portfolio strategy, freedom-to-operate, biotech licensing structures, AI inventorship compliance

### Finding 05: Swiss Succession Law + Digital Asset Inheritance — UHNWI Estate Planning
- Digital assets (crypto/NFT) as succession assets: Swiss Civil Code Art. 470a (2022) framework exists but practical enforcement complex; smart contract death triggers represent new frontier
- Geneva notary process: probate inventories for estates >CHF 10,000; ancillary administration for cross-border assets; hardware wallet/multi-sig access challenges for heirs
- Hague Succession Convention (CH party): applicable law = habitual residence; Geneva notaires navigate EU successors' claims vs. Swiss tax obligations
- UHNWI instruments: family foundations, Anglo-Saxon discretionary trusts recognized under PILA Art. 2(2)(d), annuity agreements, life insurance with Geneva insurers
- Swiss Arbitration Centre Supplemental Rules (in force July 2025): trust/estate/foundation dispute procedures including emergency arbitrator; confidentiality advantage for UHNWI
- Crypto succession advisory emerging: pre-death key management protocols, institutional custodian succession services, digital asset executor services

### Finding 06: Geneva as International Commercial Court — Competitive Repositioning vs. London/Dubai/Singapore
- Geneva's neutral seat advantage: significant for Russia/sanctioned-party, Iran, China disputes; Swiss-seated awards enforceable in 170+ states under New York Convention
- Swiss Arbitration Centre: record +25% caseload 2025; Geneva as premier non-EU, non-UK seat for international disputes
- DIFC (Dubai) and SICC (Singapore): growing but limited European utility; English Commercial Court London still dominant for English law contracts
- Challenge: no dedicated Geneva International Commercial Court (GICC) — parties use Tribunal de première instance (French procedure); potential reform discussion
- English law as governing law: majority of Geneva-seated commercial contracts governed by English law; Swiss-qualified lawyers with English law training at premium
- Advisory: English law contract advice by Swiss-qualified lawyers, Geneva-seated arbitration advocacy, interim measures via Swiss courts supporting arbitration, foreign judgment enforcement in Switzerland
