**Section 3: The Professional Secrecy Problem — Your AI Tools May Already Violate Client Confidentiality**

- Every Geneva firm using ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Google AI, or other US-hosted tools faces potential CLOUD Act exposure
- Data residency requirement = concrete, immediate discovery question for outreach

**Section 4: The EU AI Act — August 2, 2026 — Your Geneva Firm May Already Be Subject**

- High-risk AI obligations: risk management, data governance, technical documentation, human oversight, accuracy
- Penalty: up to €35M or 7% global turnover
- ~4.5 months to assess — firms with EU nexus need to act now
