# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal: Regulatory Context
## Cycle 14 | 2026-03-30

## Regulatory Context — Phase 01 Cycle 14

### Active Obligations — NOW

**1. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nFADP)**
- Operative since September 1, 2023
- Technology-neutral: directly applicable to AI-based data processing
- Key obligations:
  - Transparency: purpose, functionality, data sources of AI systems
  - Privacy by Design and Default (Art. 7 FADP)
  - DPIA required for high-risk AI contexts (Art. 22 FADP)
  - Right to transparency for automated decisions with legal/significant effects
  - Right to request human review of automated decisions

**2. Professional Secrecy — Geneva Law Firms (CRITICAL CONSTRAINT)**
- Art. 13 Lawyer's Act: strict professional secrecy obligation
- Art. 321 Swiss Criminal Code: criminal liability for confidentiality breach
- AI tool implication: providers cannot have permanent access to client content
- Data residency: Swiss-hosted AI tools required to avoid US CLOUD Act risk
- Enforcement: violation = criminal liability under Art. 321 SCC

**3. FDPIC Enforcement Trajectory**
- FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (Jul 2025): >1,000 cases, >100 interventions, >360 breach reports
- Powers: can order AI services adjusted, interrupted, or discontinued
- Feb 2026: joined 60+ DPAs in joint statement on AI-generated images
- Enforcement gap for law firms: confirmed (timing signal still active)

### Imminent Deadlines

**4. EU AI Act — August 2, 2026 (4.5 months)**
- Extraterritorial: Geneva firms with EU nexus must comply
- High-risk AI system obligations: risk management, data governance, technical documentation, human oversight, accuracy
- Applies to: Geneva firms placing AI on EU market, operating EU-intended output, acting as EU importer/distributor
- Penalty: up to €35M or 7% global turnover

**5. Federal Council AI Action Plan — May 2026 (~2 months)**
- Federal Council First Draft expected May 2026
- "Lean approach" confirmed: sector-specific, no cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026
- Next external pressure event for Geneva advisory market

**6. FDJP Consultation Draft — End 2026 (~9 months)**
- CoE AI Convention implementation: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- Soft-law/non-binding instruments preferred per Federal Council
- ~9-12 months before any binding obligation materializes

### Cross-Vertical Deadlines

**7. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)**
- Healthcare AI/SaMD deadline with NO grace period
- Spillover: law firms advising healthcare clients must understand Swissdamed implications
- Professional liability: AI diagnostic tools used by healthcare clients = client advisory obligation
