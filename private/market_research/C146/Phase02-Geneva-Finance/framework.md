### Vector 02
- **Topic:** FINMA Supervisory Notice 05/2025 — "Resilience by Design" from January 2026
- **Finding:** FINMA Guidance 05/2025 (effective January 1, 2026) reinforces operational resilience requirements with enhanced specificity. All Swiss financial institutions, regardless of size, must demonstrably define critical functions, disruption tolerances, and testing procedures by January 1, 2026. Combined with FINMA Circular 2023/1 (in force since January 1, 2024), the transitional periods end January 1, 2026 for full compliance. FINMA's resilience-by-design approach requires identification/protection against threats, response protocols, restoration capability, and incident learning. ICT, critical data, and cyber risk are central. DORA extraterritorial reach for Swiss FIs with EU operations adds complexity — FINMA principle-based approach vs. DORA rule-based approach requires gap analysis for Geneva institutions.
- **Source:** finma.ch; swissgrc.com; deloitte.com; pwc.ch; ey.com; swiss-risk.org; bonnard-lawson.com; enterprise-it-security.com
- **Signal Strength:** HIGH — imminent compliance deadline (Jan 1, 2026), concrete supervisory notice
- **Advisory Opportunity:** Geneva financial institutions DORA gap analysis vs. FINMA 05/2025 compliance; ICT third-party risk management for Geneva FIs with EU exposure; operational resilience testing frameworks; Geneva bank critical function mapping; cyber resilience advisory for Geneva commodity trading banks
- **Status:** NET-NEW C146 ✅ (builds on C145 Phase 02 DORA-parity finding with specific Guidance 05/2025 requirements)

### Vector 03
- **Topic:** Swiss REMIT 2026 — Wholesale Energy Market Transparency Framework for Geneva Commodity Traders
- **Finding:** Federal Act on Supervision and Transparency in the Wholesale Energy Market (Swiss REMIT) entering into force 2026, aligned with EU REMIT and REMIT II (Regulation EU 2024/1106). Applies to Swiss and non-Swiss entities trading electricity and gas in Swiss wholesale market. Mandatory reporting: transactions, trading orders, fundamental data (generation/storage capacity), inside information disclosure. Registration with Swiss Federal Electricity Commission (ElCom) required. Non-Swiss entities must appoint Swiss legal representative. Penalties: up to 15% of Swiss annual turnover for legal entities, trading bans, up to 5 years imprisonment. Algorithmic trading compliance requirements. Geneva commodity traders (oil, gas, metals) directly affected. FinfraG (FMIA) revisions also pending parliamentary consideration 2025.
- **Source:** remitcloud.de; lenzstaehelin.com; elcom.admin.ch; pestalozzilaw.com; table.media; dtcc.com
- **Signal Strength:** HIGH — concrete legislation, specific compliance obligations, imminent enforcement
- **Advisory Opportunity:** Geneva commodity trading firms Swiss REMIT compliance programs; ElCom registration advisory; algorithmic trading compliance for Geneva energy traders; insider trading/market manipulation policies for Geneva wholesale energy traders; Swiss REMIT vs. EU REMIT II dual compliance for Geneva multinationals
- **Status:** NET-NEW C146 ✅

### Vector 04
- **Topic:** Bern Financial Services Agreement (BFSA) UK-Switzerland — January 1, 2026 mutual recognition
- **Finding:** Bern Financial Services Agreement (BFSA) between Switzerland and United Kingdom enters into force January 1, 2026, establishing mutual recognition for cross-border financial services. Geneva financial institutions with UK market access or UK client bases face new compliance pathway. The agreement reduces duplicative regulation for Swiss firms passporting into UK and vice versa. First cross-border financial services mutual recognition regime between Switzerland and UK since Brexit. Significant for Geneva private banks, asset managers, and insurance companies with UK-domiciled clients. Timing: coincides with January 1, 2026 compliance deadlines for CRS 2.0/CARF and FINMA resilience requirements — creating a dense regulatory stack for Geneva institutions.

- **Advisory Opportunity:** Geneva FIs UK market access BFSA compliance; UK-Switzerland cross-border financial services regulatory mapping; Swiss asset manager UK FCA equivalence advisory; Geneva private bank UK client documentation under BFSA; dual compliance (BFSA + FINMA) advisory for Geneva institutions

### Vector 05
- **Topic:** Swiss Senior Managers Regime — TBTF reform September 2025 individual accountability
- **Finding:** Federal Department of Finance announced comprehensive TBTF reform package June 6, 2025, directly shaped by Credit Suisse collapse. Central element: Senior Managers Regime (SMR) assigning documented responsibilities to board members, executive committee members, heads of control functions, and major business division heads. Responsibilities must be documented and submitted to FINMA. Draft Banking Act revisions with SMR provisions expected first half 2026. Objectives: enable FINMA to hold individuals accountable for regulatory breaches, improve governance and risk culture. Swiss Bankers Association supports SMR but warns against excessive "Swiss finish" that could harm competitiveness. UBS directly affected as sole remaining G-SIB in Switzerland. Enhanced FINMA enforcement tools including increased fine powers proposed.
- **Source:** efd.admin.ch; swlegal.com; caplaw.ch; spglobal.com; ey.com; swissbanking.ch; globalrelay.com
- **Signal Strength:** HIGH — concrete legislative proposal, imminent parliamentary process, Credit Suisse aftermath
- **Advisory Opportunity:** Geneva systemically important banks SMR documentation and governance; senior executive accountability frameworks for Geneva financial institutions; FINMA enforcement defense advisory for Geneva executives; SMR internal audit programs for Geneva banks; TBTF compliance for Geneva branches of Swiss G-SIBs
- **Status:** NET-NEW C146 ✅

### Vector 06

Geneva DNFBP sector (particularly commodity traders, family offices, lawyers, and trust companies) faces expanded due diligence obligations. Finerio timing: mid-2026 implementation aligns with FATF evaluation pressure on Switzerland.
