## The Documentation Problem Nobody Names

There's a gap between what you have and what you need. It's specific. It's operational. And it's not abstract.

You have an AI governance policy. It describes what should exist.

You don't have authorization records. Those show what actually exists — when each AI system was approved, who approved it, what data it accesses, and how human oversight was documented.

Your carrier attestation requires your personal signature. It asks about "adequate AI governance." You signed it. You didn't have anyone review what you were certifying before you signed.

Here's what the carrier now requires: evidence. Not attestation. Evidence. Form v4 asks for the last three authorization records per AI tool — not a policy document, records showing actual approvals with actual names, actual dates, and actual data access documentation.

You don't have those records.

I want to be precise about what this means: the gap is not that you made a bad decision. The gap is that you made decisions — about which AI tools to deploy, how to authorize them, what data to give them access to — and those decisions were never documented in the format the carrier now requires.

---

## The Personal Liability Dimension You Already Know Is Real

nFADP Article 63 creates personal criminal liability for responsible persons. You are the responsible person. Your personal assets are at risk if an AI-related data breach occurs and you cannot demonstrate oversight.

The carrier's insurance product does not discharge this exposure. The insurance product responds to claims. Art. 63 personal liability is criminal — and the evidence of due diligence that would protect you personally is specific documentation, not a policy document.

I am not a lawyer. I am not providing legal advice. What I am telling you is what I've observed working with managing directors in your position: the documentation that would protect you personally under Art. 63 is specific, producible, and not being produced by anyone in the Geneva market at boutique WM scale.

## What I'm Offering: Evidence Production, Not Framework Documents

The market offers frameworks. I produce evidence.

The distinction matters. A framework is a document that describes what should exist. Evidence is documentation that shows what exists, when it was authorized, and with what records.

What I produce for boutique wealth management firms:

**Authorization Records** — Documentation that satisfies carrier Form v4 requirements: which AI tools are deployed, when each was authorized, who approved it, what data it accesses, and the human oversight documentation for each system.

**Decision Attribution Files** — For portfolio construction AI and AI-influenced investment recommendations: documentation showing the authorization chain from AI output to human approval, with specific records that satisfy FinSA suitability requirements.

**Vendor Processor Chain Documentation** — The sub-processor chain disclosure that satisfies nFADP Art. 9 obligations and carrier attestation requirements simultaneously. You need to know which vendor sub-processors have access to client data. This documents that.

**Personal Liability Evidence Package** — For the managing director who needs to demonstrate Art. 63 due diligence: documentation showing oversight, controls, and accountability for AI systems processing client data.

**Institutional Questionnaire Response File** — The document that answers every section of the institutional client AI governance DDQ — organized by question, with specific documentation attached. Not a framework. A response file with evidence attached.

## Le Problème de Documentation Que Personne Ne Nomme

Il y a un écart entre ce que vous avez et ce dont vous avez besoin. C'est spécifique. C'est opérationnel. Et ce n'est pas abstrait.

Vous avez une politique de gouvernance IA. Elle décrit ce qui devrait exister.

Vous n'avez pas de records d'autorisation. Ceux-ci montrent ce qui existe réellement — quand chaque système IA a été approuvé, qui l'a approuvé, à quelles données il accède, et comment la surveillance humaine a été documentée.

Votre attestation de l'assureur exige votre signature personnelle. Elle demande une « gouvernance IA adéquate ». Vous l'avez signée. Vous n'avez fait revue par personne de ce que vous certifiiez avant de signer.

Voici ce que l'assureur exige maintenant: des preuves. Pas une attestation. Des preuves. Le formulaire v4 demande les trois derniers records d'autorisation par outil IA — pas un document de politique, des records montrant des approbations réelles avec des noms réels, des dates réelles et une documentation réelle sur l'accès aux données.

Vous n'avez pas ces records.

Je veux être précis sur ce que cela signifie: l'écart n'est pas que vous avez pris une mauvaise décision. L'écart est que vous avez pris des décisions — sur quels outils IA déployer, comment les autoriser, à quelles données leur donner accès — et ces décisions n'ont jamais été documentées dans le format que l'assureur exige maintenant.

Je ne suis pas avocat. Je ne fournis pas de conseil juridique. Ce que je produis pour les cabinets de gestion de fortune boutique:
