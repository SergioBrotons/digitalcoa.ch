# 04 — Compliance Context: Geneva / Switzerland Specific Regulatory
## Phase 04 C297 | Geneva Healthcare | Independent Physician / Small Group Practice (2–10 physicians)

---

## Regulatory Landscape for Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability

### Primary Regulatory Frameworks

**1. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nFADP)**
- Active since 1 September 2023 (revised version)
- Applies to AI tools processing personal health data
- Art.5: Lawful basis required — most AI tools activated through click-through acceptance without evaluating which lawful basis applies
- Art.6: Processing of sensitive personal data (health data) requires explicit consent or specific statutory basis
- Art.8: Profiling notification — AI-assisted documentation tools may infer patient data elements triggering Art.8 obligations
- Art.9: Third-party processor compliance — vendor sub-processors create Art.9 processor chain the independent practice bears without evaluation
- Art.21: Automated decision obligations — AI-assisted diagnostic support may trigger automated decision provisions with right to explanation
- Art.22: Reversal right — patient can request reversal of AI-influenced decisions, but medical records cannot be casually reversed without clinical process integrity consequences
- Art.63: Criminal liability — personal criminal exposure for non-compliance, maximum CHF 250,000 + imprisonment for willful violations
- FDPIC enforcement posture shift (May 2025): Enforcement is present-tense, not 2027 prospective. FADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data.

**2. Medical Devices Ordinance (MedDO)**
- Governs AI software qualifying as medical device
- From 1 July 2026: mandatory Swissdamed registration for all medical devices placed on Swiss market
- Swissdamed based on EU EUDAMED system
- Tier 1 / Tier 2 classification thresholds — criteria written for manufacturers, operationally unclear for independent practice
- Classification question: Does the practice's AI tool qualify as a medical device? Does it require registration? What is the practice's obligation as "operator" vs. manufacturer?
- No Geneva-calibrated guidance for independent practice Swissdamed compliance pathway

**3. Therapeutic Products Act (TPA)**
- Governs therapeutic products and medical devices
- AI-powered medical devices must comply with EU MDR/IVDR requirements through Swiss-TPA framework
- Implications for diagnostic AI tools used in Geneva independent practice

**4. FMH Professional Medical Standards**
- FMH principle: physician retains ultimate responsibility for all clinical decisions aided by AI
- Principle provides no operational endpoint: no documentation requirement, no review frequency, no competency standard
- Human oversight concept undefined operatively for independent practice
- One person holds user/compliance/billing roles simultaneously — principle cannot be operationally fulfilled at small-practice scale
- No FMH operational implementation guidance for 2-10 physician practice proportionate to scale

**5. Article 321 CP (Swiss Criminal Code)**
- Professional secrecy obligation applies to patient health data
- AI tool data processing extends Art.321 CP obligations to data the physician controls in vendor systems
- Click-through vendor terms accepting data usage for model training creates external processing the physician cannot stop without losing core functionality
- Structural professional secrecy exposure from AI vendor data flows

**6. Council of Europe AI Convention**
- Swiss Federal Council decided February 2025 to enable ratification
- Draft proposal for incorporation into Swiss law anticipated end of 2026
- Creates deployer obligations for AI systems operating in Geneva practice context
- Obligations exist before implementing legislation — present-tense private law exposure
- Interaction with existing nFADP creates dual-compliance track

**7. Carrier Medical Liability Attestation**
- Swiss medical liability insurance carriers sending AI attestation questionnaires
- Attestation designed by underwriting logic — commits MD to undefined "adequate AI governance" standard
- Signing physician creates personal liability on carrier-defined terms without Geneva-calibrated pre-signing review
- Attestation coverage vs. personal liability scope — no mapping service exists
- Medical practice attestation 6-12 months behind finance/legal sector in market development

### Geneva-Specific Institutional Dynamics

**HUG/CHUV institutional circuit:** Effective operational standards forming through institutional governance rooms. Independent practice decisions made in isolation from these formation mechanisms. Standards accessible to carriers and legal proceedings without transmission to independent sector.

**Ordre des Médecins de Genève:** Canton-level medical professional authority. No Geneva-specific AI guidance published for independent practice. National FMH guidance too general for operative decisions.

**Canton de Genève Service de la santé publique:** No canton-specific AI guidance for independent practices. Practices operate under Federal framework without Geneva-calibrated local operational guidance.

**Service du监护人:** No Geneva-specific AI governance guidance for outpatient practice. Federal frameworks apply without local interpretation.

## Regulatory Intersections Creating Compound Exposure

### Critical Intersection Points

**1. FADP + MedDO + Art.321 CP**
AI tool qualifies under MedDO (medical device registration required), processes patient data under FADP, and extends physician's Art.321 CP professional secrecy obligations to vendor-accessible data. Three frameworks simultaneously active with different authorities and enforcement mechanisms.

**2. Carrier Attestation + FMH + FADP**
Carrier attestation asks for AI governance documentation. FMH defines human oversight principle without operational endpoint. FADP requires lawful basis documentation for AI-processed data. Three different accountability tracks describing the same AI governance reality with non-aligned evidence standards.

**3. Art.22 Reversal Right + Medical Record Integrity**
Patient requests reversal of AI-influenced clinical decision under Art.22. Medical records require clinical process integrity — cannot be casually reversed. Two incompatible simultaneous obligations. No Geneva protocol for managing this conflict.

**4. Swissdamed Registration + Vendor Contract Terms**
Swissdamed registration deadline 1 July 2026 requires classification of AI tools as medical devices. Vendor contract terms may include data processing provisions that create Art.321 CP exposure independent of classification question. Classification answer does not resolve contract law exposure.

**5. Carrier Attestation + Art.63 Criminal Liability**
Carrier attestation evidence standard (designed for underwriting) architecturally inadequate for Art.63 criminal defense. Documentation satisfying carrier may not survive criminal challenge under different evidentiary standard.

---

## Enforcement Reality

**FDPIC (May 2025 posture shift):** Present-tense enforcement active — FADP applies directly to AI systems processing personal data. No new legislation required. Most independent practices operating under assumption that enforcement is future risk — now have present-tense exposure.

**Carrier attestation:** Designed for underwriting evaluation, not legal defense. MD signature creates personal liability on carrier-defined terms without knowing what those terms require.

**FMH human oversight:** Reference to physician accountability without defining operational standard. Creates "principle present, endpoint unenforceable" dynamic.

**MedDO Swissdamed:** Mandatory registration from 1 July 2026. Enforcement mechanism unclear for independent practice non-registration. Deadline approaching with classification methodology absent.

---

## What Is Not Regulated (The Named Gaps)

- No operational definition of human oversight at small-practice scale
- No proportionate AI governance documentation standard for independent practice
- No multi-physician practice AI coordination requirement
- No clinical documentation standard for AI-influenced decisions at independent practice scale
- No post-incident AI documentation protocol proportionate to independent practice
- No Geneva-specific guidance from Ordre des Médecins de Genève on AI accountability
- No carrier pre-signing review service calibrated to medical professional liability
