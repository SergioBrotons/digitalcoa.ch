## Finding 01 — Executive Briefing Objection Patterns: Budget Cycle and Authority Alignment

DigitalCoa.ch briefing inquiry data from Q1 2026 confirms two primary conversion blockers that prevent briefing-to-mandate progression. The budget cycle blocker: Geneva executives typically initiate AI governance research in Q3 and Q4, but discretionary advisory budgets are often locked by Q3 planning cycles — creating a timing mismatch where genuine interest cannot be converted to engagement until the following fiscal year. The internal authority blocker: the person who submits the briefing inquiry (typically a compliance manager or legal counsel) is rarely the person who controls the budget or can authorise the engagement, and the path from briefing to internal buy-in is unclear. The conversion strategy implication: brief the compliance manager but design the briefing output to be board-presentable, and offer a follow-up stakeholder alignment session as a natural next step.

**Conversion insight:** Briefing design that produces a board-ready summary — not just an internal working document — removes the internal authority blocker by giving the compliance manager a tool to sell the engagement upward.

## Finding 04 — DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Capacity Constraint: Waitlist Logic

The capacity constraint (solo senior practitioner) has emerged as a natural qualification mechanism: Geneva executives who receive a waitlist response or are told availability is limited report higher perceived value of the engagement than those who receive immediate availability. This is consistent with Geneva boutique advisory market dynamics where scarcity signals premium quality. The operational implication: managing inquiry intake so that not every inquiry converts to a briefing — intentionally creating a qualified wait — is consistent with premium positioning and actually improves conversion quality. The constraint should be explicitly communicated: "I work with a small number of organisations per quarter to ensure genuine senior attention."

**Capacity insight:** A waitlist or availability-management approach is not a conversion problem — it is a trust signal and a qualification mechanism that improves lead quality and supports premium pricing.
