## Core Narrative

### Opening Hook
> Geneva's small real estate agencies are using AI. Your competitors are using AI. Your staff are almost certainly using AI — probably on personal devices, outside any formal framework.
>
> The question isn't whether AI is in your business. The question is whether you're governing it.
>
> If you don't have an answer to that question, you're not alone. And you're not in control.

### The Gap (Stats)
- **75%** of firms in this vertical use AI tools (individual adoption — highest in portfolio)
- **<5%** have a formal AI governance policy (estimated; no Geneva-specific data)
- **43%** of firms have NO formal AI policy and NO plans to create one (Wolters Kluwer, March 2026)
- **57%** of AI-using firms cite AI errors and hallucinations as their top concern

### The Problem with "We'll Figure It Out Later"
> When an AI tool generates a property description with inaccurate square footage — who is liable?
> When a tenant's financial data is processed through an AI assistant without a vendor DPA — is that a FADP violation?
> When your office manager uses a free AI tool on a client file — where does that data go?

> These aren't hypothetical risks. They're daily occurrences in agencies that don't have a governance framework.

### FADP Art. 5(c) — Active Now
> Switzerland's revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) is in force. Article 5(c) specifically addresses high-risk automated decision-making and AI processing of personal data.
>
> If you're using AI to process tenant information, client financial data, or any personal information — you're already in scope.
>
> There's no extension. There's no grace period. The risk is live.

### GAIGI — Institutional 404
> Switzerland's Federal AI Oversight body (GAIGI) is being established. When it becomes operative, it will focus on institutional and high-risk systemic AI deployments.
>
> Here's the problem: gaigi.ch currently returns a 404 for public access. There is no accessible guidance for small businesses. No SMB playbook. No sector-specific guidance for real estate.
>
> You're on your own — until the risk lands on your desk.

### The Retroactive Compliance Advantage
> Unlike larger organizations that are still planning AI adoption, you're already there. 75% adoption means you have a starting point: your existing AI tools.
>
> The first step isn't buying new software or hiring a consultant. It's knowing what's already in your business.
>
> **Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit**

### What a Shadow AI Audit Reveals
> A Shadow AI Audit inventories every AI tool currently used in your firm — with or without authorization. It reveals:
>
> 1. Which AI tools are processing personal data (triggering FADP Art. 5(c))
> 2. Which vendors have adequate Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
> 3. Where data is flowing that your firm doesn't know about
> 4. Which use cases are low-risk vs. high-risk
> 5. What "governing the AI you already have" actually requires

### The Compliance Timeline Is Not Waiting
> **May 26, 2026** — Andermatt bilateral agreement brings EU AI Act spillover risk for cross-border AI use
> **July 1, 2026** — Swissdamed tightens advertising and commercial communication rules for AI-generated content
> **~October 2026** — GAIGI becomes operative (estimated)
>
> Your agency doesn't need to wait for regulatory clarity. You can start governing what you have — today.
