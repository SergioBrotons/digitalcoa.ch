## Core Promise
Your AI tools are creating legal exposure you don't know how to manage — and your clients are starting to ask questions.

---

## Problem Statement (3 layers)

### Layer 1: Legal obligation gap (FADP + AI Ordinance)
Geneva law firms face two concurrent frameworks: FADP Art. 21 (human review of automated decisions) and the Federal Council AI Ordinance (in force Jan 1, 2025). Using AI in case assessment, billing automation, or contract review without a documented governance framework means you're already non-compliant — and you probably don't know it.

### Layer 2: Professional standard gap (SBA)
The Swiss Bar Association Guidelines (June 2024) established a standard of care for AI use. Non-binding, but it sets the benchmark. If a client complains, or a court reviews your AI-assisted work, the question won't be "did you use AI?" — it will be "did you have a governance framework?"

### Layer 3: Liability exposure gap (malpractice)
Legal malpractice insurers are now flagging AI-assisted work in liability assessments. "The AI recommended it" is not a defense. Your firm is liable for every AI-assisted decision — whether you reviewed it adequately or not.

---

## The Bridge: Envelope vs. Letter

**Automating the envelope (safe):** Routing, scheduling, reminders, time tracking, billing preparation, document formatting, conflict check support. These don't touch privileged content. These are safe to automate NOW.

**Automating the letter (needs governance):** AI interpreting case facts, drafting legal positions, generating advice, analyzing contracts for substantive terms. These require a governance framework before automation.

The five workflows in the "envelope" category represent 8–14 hours/week recoverable — without creating new legal exposure.
