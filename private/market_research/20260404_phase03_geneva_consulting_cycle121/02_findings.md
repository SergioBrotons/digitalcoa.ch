## Finding 1: CSRD Double Materiality Assessment — Beyond Compliance Checkbox

### Signal
Swiss companies above EU CSRD thresholds (and voluntary adopters) are moving from single materiality (financial) to double materiality (financial + impact) assessment. Geneva consulting demand emerging for rigorous double materiality methodology — not just compliance checkboxes.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Large Swiss companies with EU operations, Swiss parent companies of EU subsidiaries, and ambitious mid-sized companies using CSRD for competitive positioning
- **Distinct angle:** Not post-CSRD operational integration (C120) — this is specifically the double materiality assessment methodology itself, a prerequisite for strategy-aligned sustainability reporting
- **Revenue model:** Double materiality assessment projects, stakeholder engagement facilitation, methodology training, board workshop delivery
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's multinational HQs and listed companies are primary targets

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Double Materiality: The CSRD Approach Geneva Companies Are Getting Wrong"
- Target ICP: Sustainability executives, CFOs, board members, sustainability consultants

## Finding 2: Geopolitical Risk Advisory — Switzerland's Sanctions Position and Neutrality Debate

### Signal
The Ukraine conflict has intensified scrutiny of Swiss neutrality doctrine. Parliamentary debates on re-export of Swiss-origin military goods and Switzerland's participation in EU sanctions regimes are creating uncertainty for Geneva-based multinationals and commodity trading houses with complex geopolitical exposure.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Corporate boards and GCs need geopolitical risk frameworks to navigate Swiss-EU political tensions, sanctions extension risks, and potential neutrality doctrine changes
- **Distinct angle:** Not regulatory investigation crisis comms (C120) — this is proactive geopolitical risk assessment and scenario planning, not reactive crisis management
- **Revenue model:** Geopolitical risk assessment reports, board advisory sessions, scenario planning workshops, country risk reviews
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's concentration of commodity traders, multinational HQs, and IO/international organization presence creates unique geopolitical exposure

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Switzerland's Neutrality Doctrine Under Pressure: What Geneva Boards Must Monitor"
- Target ICP: Corporate board members, GCs, risk management executives, compliance officers at multinationals

## Finding 3: Interim CFO and Senior Finance Management — Talent Gap Advisory

### Signal
Geneva-based companies face increasing difficulty sourcing permanent senior finance talent (CFO, VP Finance, Finance Director) due to salary compression, talent emigration to cantonal public service, and demand from multinational HQs. Interim finance management is becoming the primary solution.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Companies in CFO transitions, PE-backed portfolio companies, fast-growing SMEs, and companies undergoing financial transformation need vetted interim finance talent
- **Distinct angle:** Not operational cost restructuring (C99/C120) — this is specifically the talent market gap and interim management solution, a distinct consulting offering
- **Revenue model:** Interim placement (retained search), interim CFO as service, finance function assessment, CFO onboarding support
- **Geneva specificity:** PE/venture portfolio companies in Geneva and Vaud (Lausanne tech corridor) are particularly affected

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Why Geneva Companies Are Turning to Interim CFOs — And When You Should Too"
- Target ICP: SME owners, PE/VC portfolio company boards, CFOs in transition, HR/finance directors

## Finding 4: Swiss FDI Screening — Inbound Investment Review Advisory

### Signal
Switzerland's inbound FDI screening regime (in effect since 2021, expanded scope effective 2024) covers acquisitions in sensitive sectors (energy, transport, health, finance, critical infrastructure) by non-Swiss acquirers. Enforcement has intensified in 2025-2026, with SECO issuing more review decisions.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Non-Swiss acquirers (EU, US, UK, Asia) pursuing Swiss target acquisitions need pre-deal FDI screening assessment and deal timeline management; Swiss sellers need buyer qualification
- **Distinct angle:** Not non-EU Swiss market entry (C120) — this is specifically the regulatory review process for M&A transactions, a distinct deal-cycle advisory niche
- **Revenue model:** FDI pre-assessment reports, deal timeline management, regulatory submission support, deal risk opinions
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's biotech, commodities, and financial services sectors are primary FDI-sensitive industries

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Swiss FDI Screening: What Foreign Acquirers Get Wrong About the Geneva Deal Process"
- Target ICP: M&A deal teams, corporate development executives, M&A lawyers, private equity deal professionals

## Finding 5: Operational Cost Restructuring — CHF Structural Strength and Margin Pressure

### Signal
The Swiss franc has maintained structural strength against the EUR despite ECB tightening, creating persistent margin pressure for Geneva's export-oriented service economy (hospitality, professional services, retail, luxury). Cost restructuring advisory demand is rising.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Geneva professional services firms, hospitality groups, and export-facing SMEs need rigorous cost structure reviews to maintain margins in a structurally strong CHF environment
- **Distinct angle:** Not operational cost reduction (C99/C100) — this is specifically the structural CHF strength factor and its strategic implications, not generic cost cutting
- **Revenue model:** Cost structure diagnostic, shared services design, procurement optimization, pricing strategy review, location analysis for back-office functions
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's high-cost, export-dependent economy is particularly exposed; French-border firms face additional EUR/CHF dynamics

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Structural CHF Strength: A Cost Restructuring Imperative for Geneva Service Companies"
- Target ICP: Professional services CFOs, hospitality group finance directors, Geneva SME owners, luxury retail operators

## Finding 6: Digital Transformation Recovery — Failed ERP and Tech Program Advisory

### Signal
Multiple Geneva companies (and nationally) have experienced high-profile digital transformation failures — overshooting budgets, missing deadlines, or abandoning systems post-implementation. Post-mortem and recovery consulting demand is growing, with a specific opportunity around ERP consolidation after failed SAP/Oracle implementations.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Companies with failed or stalled digital transformation programs need independent assessment, rescue planning, and lessons-learned analysis before committing to another program
- **Distinct angle:** Not AI adoption change management (C120) — this is specifically transformation failure recovery and remediation, a distinct consulting niche
- **Revenue model:** Independent program assessment, rescue/redesign planning, implementation management office (IMO) support, vendor negotiation support
- **Geneva specificity:** Large Geneva employers (banks, insurers, pharma) have had documented transformation failures; mid-sized companies increasingly affected

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Digital Transformation Failures: Why Geneva Companies Keep Getting It Wrong"
- Target ICP: CIOs, CFOs who inherited failed programs, board members overseeing technology investments, procurement officers managing ERP contracts
