## Competitive Landscape — Overview

The Geneva AI governance market has a structural gap: no boutique advisory firm serves small professional firms (1–20 employees) with operational evidence systems. The market offers frameworks; DigitalCoa offers evidence.

|---|---|---|---|

## What Each Competitor Says They Offer

### Big 4 (KPMG, PwC, Deloitte, EY)

**What they say:** "AI Governance Framework," "Regulatory Compliance Advisory," "Risk Management," "Digital Transformation"

**Actual offering:** Enterprise-grade compliance frameworks, policy documentation, large-engagement risk assessments. Minimum engagement: CHF 50K+. Typical engagement: CHF 150K–500K+.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "They quoted me more than my annual revenue"
- "They have no experience with firms my size"
- "They gave me a framework document, not a working system"
- "They don't understand Geneva professional practice"
- "I never heard back after the first call"

**What they miss:** The small firm segment is economically incompatible with their model. They cannot provide boutique, affordable, operational evidence systems because their economics require large engagements.

### Swiss Cyber Institute

**What they say:** "AI Security Governance," "Cyber Resilience," "Professional Certifications"

**Actual offering:** Security-focused training and certifications. Content covers data breach prevention, system security, cybersecurity controls.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "The certification was useful for security, not for governance"
- "I still can't answer my carrier's attestation form"
- "Security and governance are different things — I needed governance"
- "They don't understand nFADP Art. 63 personal liability"

**What they miss:** Security is a subset of governance. The carrier attestation form, institutional client questionnaire, and decision attribution requirements are governance problems, not security problems. Security consultants cannot provide governance advisory.

### Generic GDPR Consultants

**What they say:** "GDPR/nFADP Compliance," "Data Protection Officer," "Privacy Framework," "Policy Documentation"

**Actual offering:** Data protection frameworks, privacy notices, consent mechanisms, data subject rights processes, DPIA documentation.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "They gave me a privacy notice template"
- "GDPR framework doesn't answer my carrier's questions"
- "They don't understand AI decision attribution"
- "The framework is for data protection, not for AI governance"
- "I needed to know who authorized which AI decision — they couldn't help"

**What they miss:** AI governance is distinct from data protection. Decision attribution, AI authorization records, carrier attestation evidence standards, and institutional client AI governance questionnaires are not data protection problems. GDPR consultants are structurally limited to data protection scope.

### Local Geneva Consultants

**What they say:** "AI Governance for Geneva Professional Firms," "nFADP Advisory," "Digital Compliance"

**Actual offering:** Awareness events, workshops, general guidance documents, introductory seminars.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "I attended their seminar — it was good awareness but I still don't know what to do"
- "They told me what to worry about, not how to build a system"
- "The workshop was helpful but there's no follow-through"
- "They don't have any Geneva firm references in my vertical"

**What they miss:** Awareness without system is insufficient. ICP has attended awareness events and still cannot produce carrier attestation records. The gap between awareness and operational compliance is not addressed by local consultants.

### Legal Tech Vendors (Contract Analysis AI, Document AI)

**What they say:** "AI-Powered Legal Research," "Contract Analysis," "Document Automation"

**Actual offering:** AI tools for legal work — contract analysis, document review, legal research, due diligence automation.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "They sell tools, not governance systems"
- "The vendor is part of the problem — I need independent advisory"
- "AI vendors have conflicts of interest on governance"
- "My AI vendor can't advise me on governance — they ARE the governance question"

**What they miss:** Vendors are part of the problem (vendor contract trap). Independent advisory, free from vendor conflicts, is what ICP needs. Legal tech vendors cannot fill the advisory role because their interests are aligned with AI adoption, not governance accountability.

### Compliance SaaS Platforms

**What they say:** "Automated Compliance," "Compliance Management System," "AI Governance Platform"

**Actual offering:** SaaS platforms for enterprise compliance management — policy management, audit tracking, compliance workflow automation.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "The platform is designed for enterprises, not small firms"
- "I need a person who understands my practice, not a software dashboard"
- "The pricing is enterprise-scale, not SMB-scale"
- "I tried a compliance platform — it requires too much internal resource to maintain"

**What they miss:** Small Geneva professional firms need human advisory, not software platforms. The operational burden of maintaining a compliance platform is incompatible with small firm resources.

### Professional Bodies (SBA, FMH, ODAGE)

**What they say:** "AI Governance Awareness," "Professional Standards," "Member Education"

**Actual offering:** Awareness events, guidance documents that acknowledge the problem without defining compliance.

**Why ICP finds them inadequate:**
- "The SBA event was helpful but gave no compliance pathway"
- "FMH says oversight is required but doesn't define what that means"
- "ODAGE recognizes the risk but doesn't supply a solution"
- "My professional body acknowledges the problem but doesn't solve it"

**What they miss:** Professional bodies are structurally limited to awareness and guidance. They cannot and will not provide operational compliance systems — that would create liability. The gap between professional body acknowledgment and operational compliance is the advisory opportunity.

## What Competitors Miss — The White Space

### White Space 1: No One Defines "Geneva-Standard AI Governance Documentation"

**The gap:** "Geneva standard" is referenced by ICPs as the quality threshold they need to meet. No one has defined what it means. No competitor, professional body, or regulatory body has published a practical definition.

**Why it matters:** The first entity to define Geneva-standard AI governance documentation becomes the reference authority. Every ICP who references "Geneva standard" will eventually reference the first entity that defined it.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** Define the standard through client work and thought leadership. Create the reference document that ICPs and professional bodies reference as "Geneva standard."

### White Space 2: No Boutique Advisory for 1–20 Employee Geneva Professional Firms

**The gap:** Big 4 are structurally excluded from small firm market by economics. Local consultants offer awareness without system. Generic GDPR consultants are not AI-governance-specific. No competitor is positioned for Geneva small professional firms.

**Why it matters:** The addressable market (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare — all small firm segments) has no dedicated AI governance advisor. First-mover has the opportunity to establish vertical-specific positioning before competitors respond.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** Own the Geneva small professional firm segment across all four verticals. Build reference clients in each vertical. Create the market position before Big 4 notice the segment.

### White Space 3: No Evidence Capture System — Only Documentation Retrospective

**The gap:** Every competitor offers documentation (frameworks, policies, templates). No competitor offers an evidence capture system — operational tooling that creates records before they're needed, not retrospective reconstruction.

**Why it matters:** Retroactive documentation is structurally impossible (carrier form v4 confirms this). ICP needs records that were created at the time of the AI decision — not documentation assembled after the fact.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** Build and provide evidence capture methodology — the operational system that creates authorization records at the time of AI decision, not retrospective documentation.

### White Space 4: No Vertical-Specific Reference Experience

**The gap:** ICPs need a Geneva firm in their vertical that has solved this problem. No competitor has documented reference clients in any Geneva vertical. References are absent from the market.

**Why it matters:** "Has this been done for another Geneva [legal/finance/consulting/healthcare] firm?" is the primary ICP question. No competitor can answer yes with a verifiable reference.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** Build the first set of reference clients in each vertical. Document the methodology. Create the reference experience that no competitor can match.

### White Space 5: No Resolution of Vendor Contract Trap

**The gap:** Every competitor ignores the vendor contract trap. They offer frameworks for switching vendors or improving new vendor contracts — but no one addresses the core problem: historical data exposure that cannot be unwound.

**Why it matters:** A significant portion of ICPs are stuck in vendor contracts they cannot exit. The trap prevents them from improving governance through vendor switching. No one offers a pathway for trapped firms.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** Address the vendor contract trap directly. Provide the forward-looking evidence framework that makes governance manageable even when switching is not available. This is a differentiator no competitor offers.

### White Space 6: No "Evidence System" Positioning

**The gap:** Every competitor positions around frameworks, policies, compliance, risk, security. No one positions around evidence. "Evidence" is the word that ICPs use to describe what they need — but no competitor uses it.

**Why it matters:** Positioning as "evidence" rather than "compliance" or "governance" directly addresses the ICP's actual need. The market says "frameworks" — DigitalCoa says "evidence." The distinction is the differentiator.

**DigitalCoa opportunity:** "The market offers frameworks. We offer evidence." is not just a tagline — it names the white space no competitor has claimed.
