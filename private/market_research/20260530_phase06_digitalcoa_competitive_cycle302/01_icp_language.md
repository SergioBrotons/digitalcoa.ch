## How ICPs Describe the Problem They're Trying to Solve

### Language Pattern 1: "I need something that actually works"

**ICP frames:** "I've attended three AI governance seminars. They all told me what to do. None of them told me how to prove I did it."
**What this reveals:** ICP has absorbed awareness (seminars, ODAGE events, carrier attestation requirements) without receiving a workable compliance system. Frameworks are abundant; evidence systems are absent.

**ICP frames:** "My carrier sent me a form with twelve questions about AI authorization records. I don't have those records. I don't know how to get them."
**What this reveals:** The carrier attestation form (carrier form v4 evidence standard) is the forcing function. ICP can name the exact document they cannot produce. The gap is operational, not conceptual.

**ICP frames:** "I asked my current IT vendor what my AI governance documentation should look like. They gave me a generic GDPR checklist. That's not what I need."
**What this reveals:** Generic IT vendors and compliance consultants cannot provide Geneva-standard documentation because they haven't defined it. ICP has already tried the obvious path and found it inadequate.

### Language Pattern 2: "I need someone who understands my world"

**ICP frames:** "I'm a managing director of a wealth management firm. I can't afford to have my name on a regulatory breach. But I also can't run my business without AI tools. I need someone who gets both."
**What this reveals:** Dual-urgency — business necessity + personal liability. Not academic interest. The responsible person designation under nFADP Art. 63 makes this personal.

**ICP frames:** "I'm a senior partner. I know AI is being used in my practice. I know I need to supervise it. But I don't understand what the systems actually do. How can I supervise something I don't understand?"
**What this reveals:** Junior-senior fluency inversion. The accountable person lacks the technical fluency to fulfill supervision obligations. The problem is structural, not a training gap.

**ICP frames:** "My institution sent me a 40-question AI governance questionnaire. Two-week deadline. I had to admit I couldn't answer 30 of them. That was humiliating and alarming."
**What this reveals:** Institutional client questionnaires (Phase 02 finding) have created a specific conversion trigger: the ICP was exposed in front of a client. Shame + fear drives action.

### Language Pattern 3: "I need to be able to show this to someone"

**ICP frames:** "If a regulator asks me what AI decisions were made in my practice last year and by whom, I cannot answer that question. I need to be able to answer that question."
**What this reveals:** Decision attribution gap is named explicitly. The ICP knows the regulatory expectation and knows they cannot meet it.

**ICP frames:** "I signed an AI vendor contract three years ago. I had no idea what I was agreeing to. Now I can't leave because all my data is in their system. And I can't prove what they've done with it."
**What this reveals:** Vendor contract trap acknowledged. ICP understands switching doesn't resolve historical exposure. Only forward-looking evidence system available.

### Language Pattern 4: "I need Geneva-standard documentation — but what is that?"

**ICP frames:** "Everyone says 'Geneva standard.' No one tells me what that actually means for my practice."
**What this reveals:** The Geneva label is aspirational but undefined. ICP wants to claim Geneva-standard compliance but has no reference document for what that is. This is the first-mover opportunity.

**ICP frames:** "When I show my AI governance documentation to my professional association, I want them to say 'yes, this is what we expect.' I need that validation."
**What this reveals:** Peer enforcement (bar association, carrier, institutional client) is the actual validation mechanism, not regulatory certification. ICP seeks peer recognition, not regulatory approval.

## What ICPs Say They Need — in Their Own Words

**"I need a system that creates the records before I need them, not a consultant who helps me reconstruct them after the fact."**
→ Evidence system, not documentation retrospective

**"I need someone who has done this for another Geneva [legal/finance/consulting/healthcare] firm and can show me what good looks like."**
→ Reference experience in same vertical

**"I need to understand what my team actually does with AI, not what a textbook says they should do."**
→ Practical, operational, not theoretical

**"I need one document that answers all three questions: what AI did we use, who authorized it, and can we prove it?"**
→ Integrated evidence system, not fragmented compliance tasks

**"I need to know that if a carrier auditor looks at my documentation, they'll say it's sufficient. I need that confidence."**
→ Carrier attestation readiness as proxy for compliance

**"I need to be able to hand this to my professional association and have them say 'yes, this meets our standard.'"**
→ Peer validation as the actual enforcement mechanism

## ICP Fragrance — Bilingual

**EN:** Geneva-based managing directors and partners in legal firms (1–20 employees), wealth management boutiques (50–200 employees), management consulting practices (1–15 employees), and small medical practices (1–10 employees) who face nFADP Art. 63 personal liability for AI governance failures, receive institutional client AI governance questionnaires they cannot answer, and must demonstrate evidence of AI authorization decisions to carriers. They are experienced professionals with budget authority, real personal liability exposure, and structural documentation debt they cannot resolve with existing tools. They need evidence — not frameworks. They need someone who has defined Geneva-standard AI governance documentation, not someone who offers generic compliance advice.

**FR:** Directeurs généraux et associés basés à Genève dans des études juridiques (1 à 20 collaborateurs), des maisons de gestion de patrimoine (50 à 200 collaborateurs), des cabinets de conseil en gestion (1 à 15 collaborateurs) et des cabinets médicaux (1 à 10 collaborateurs), soumis à la responsabilité personnelle nFADP Art. 63 pour les failures de gouvernance de l'IA, recevant des questionnaires de gouvernance de l'IA de leurs clients institutionnels qu'ils ne peuvent pas répondre, et devant démontrer des preuves d'autorisation des décisions liées à l'IA aux assureurs. Ce sont des professionnels expérimentés avec un pouvoir budgétaire, une exposition réelle à la responsabilité personnelle, et une dette de documentation structurelle qu'ils ne peuvent pas résoudre avec les outils existants. Ils ont besoin de preuves — pas de cadres. Ils ont besoin de quelqu'un qui a défini la documentation de gouvernance de l'IA au standard genevois, pas d'un conseiller en conformité générique.
