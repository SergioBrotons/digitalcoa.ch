# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Legal — Small Law Firms (2–5 Attorneys)

**Cycle:** C304 Phase 01  
**Date:** 2026-05-30

---

## What Exists in the Market vs. What's Missing

### Market Landscape

#### 1. Bar Association Guidance

**What exists:**
- FSA (Geneva Bar Association) AI guidance updates (ongoing through 2025–2026)
- Swiss Bar Association (SBA) position papers on AI in legal practice
- EU AI Act cross-border considerations for Swiss firms handling international matters

**Gap:** Guidance is abstract, principles-based, and scale-agnostic. "Follow best practices" is the consistent output. No Geneva-specific, size-specific, actionable protocol exists.

**What this means for positioning:** We define "best practices for a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm" before anyone else does.

---

#### 2. Carrier AI Attestation Support

**What exists:**
- Insurance brokers offering to help complete carrier AI attestation forms
- General AI risk assessment tools offered by brokers as add-on services

**Gap:** The form is treated as a compliance checkbox. The evidence production the form implies is not addressed. Brokers complete the form; they do not produce the documentation the form assumes exists.

**What this means for positioning:** Carrier attestation support without evidence production is incomplete. We produce the evidence the form requires.

---

#### 3. General AI Governance Frameworks

**What exists:**
- ISO 42001 AI management system framework
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework
- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) compliance tools
- Various AI governance checklists and policy template libraries

**Gap:** These frameworks are designed for larger organizations with compliance departments. None is mapped to Swiss professional secrecy obligations, Geneva bar standards, or the specific scale of a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm. The frameworks are jurisdiction-agnostic and size-agnostic.

**What this means for positioning:** Generic framework ≠ Geneva standard. The market opportunity is the translation layer — taking rigorous principles and making them operational at boutique scale.

---

#### 4. Legal Technology Vendors

**What exists:**
- AI legal research platforms (Clio, Harvey, etc.)
- Document automation and contract analysis tools
- Cloud-based practice management systems

**Gap:** Vendors offer tools, not governance. Vendors have inherent conflicts of interest: their business model requires client data processing. Vendor security claims do not address professional secrecy compliance. Vendor terms of service may conflict with article 321 CC obligations.

**What this means for positioning:** We are not a vendor. We are the firm's advocate — reviewing vendor arrangements against the firm's obligations, not selling the vendor's solution.

---

#### 5. Professional Liability Insurance Brokers

**What exists:**
- Coverage gap analysis for AI-related claims
- Risk assessment questionnaires
- Carrier negotiation support

**Gap:** Insurance brokers identify coverage gaps. They do not remediate them. Coverage assessment without remediation leaves the firm with a documented gap and no solution.

**What this means for positioning:** We close the gap. Not just identify it.

---

#### 6. Consulting and Advisory Firms

**What exists:**
- Big Four AI governance advisory services (Deloitte, PwC, etc.)
- Legal process outsourcing firms offering AI compliance services
- General cybersecurity firms offering AI risk assessments

**Gap:** These firms are designed for enterprise engagements. Their minimum engagement scope exceeds the entire annual budget of a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm. No firm with genuine Geneva expertise and boutique-scale delivery exists.

**What this means for positioning:** We are the Geneva boutique solution. No conflicts, no enterprise overhead, no scale mismatch.

---

## What's Missing (The Actual Gaps)

### Gap 1: Geneva-Specific Evidence Production System

**What is missing:** No solution produces carrier-ready evidence documentation for a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm. This is the single largest gap.

**What this means:** The ICP can complete the carrier attestation. They cannot produce the evidence the attestation implies should exist. This is the core problem no one is solving.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We produce the evidence. Not the framework — the evidence."

---

### Gap 2: Vendor Contract Review Against Professional Secrecy

**What is missing:** No one offers systematic review of AI vendor contracts against article 321 CC professional secrecy obligations.

**What this means:** Every Geneva firm using AI tools has accepted vendor terms that may conflict with their professional duties. No one has told them. No one has negotiated or documented the conflict.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We reviewed what you signed. Against what you're required to maintain. The gap is our work."

---

### Gap 3: Retention Period Documentation Protocol

**What is missing:** No Geneva-specific guidance on AI documentation retention periods for small law firms. No protocol for maintaining AI use records in a manner consistent with professional secrecy obligations.

**What this means:** The ICP doesn't know how long to keep AI use records. No one has defined this for them. The retention dimension compounds the documentation debt.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We define the retention standard. Before the audit defines it for you."

---

### Gap 4: Staff AI Supervision Protocol for Boutique Firms

**What is missing:** No solution addresses the managing partner's obligation to supervise junior associates' AI usage in a 2–5 attorney firm.

**What this means:** The supervisory accountability extends to staff AI use. But the firm's size makes formal supervision structures impractical. No scaled protocol exists.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We build the supervision protocol that fits a 3-attorney firm. Not a 300-person one."

---

### Gap 5: Client-Facing AI Disclosure Protocol

**What is missing:** No Geneva-specific protocol for how small law firms should disclose AI usage to clients in engagement letters and during matters.

**What this means:** Firms are improvising AI clauses in engagement letters without knowing if they are adequate, complete, or consistent with bar guidance.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We give you the client-facing protocol. Before a client asks and you don't have an answer."

---

### Gap 6: Geneva Standard Definition — First-Mover Advantage

**What is missing:** No one has defined what "adequate AI governance documentation" means for a 2–5 attorney Geneva firm.

**What this means:** The Geneva standard is undefined. The firm that defines it — with enough specificity that carriers, bar, and clients accept it — captures the market.

**Positioning opportunity:** "We defined the Geneva standard. Before anyone else did."

## Key Competitive Insight

**The market offers tools, frameworks, and guidance. No one offers evidence.**

The C303 positioning anchor — "The market offers frameworks. We produce evidence." — remains valid and unchallenged.

C304 advances this: the evidence gap is now compounded by:
- Unknown retention periods
- Vendor contract conflicts with professional secrecy
- Client-facing disclosure obligations
- Staff supervision requirements

The evidence gap is not just about documenting AI use. It is about producing documentation that meets Geneva-specific professional secrecy standards, satisfies carrier audit expectations, and responds to client disclosure obligations — simultaneously.
