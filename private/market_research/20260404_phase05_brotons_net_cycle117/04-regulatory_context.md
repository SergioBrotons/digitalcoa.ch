# 04 — Regulatory Context: Brotons.net Content Governance

## Content Governance Framework

### What Brotons.net Must Navigate
Brotons.net publishes analysis and interpretation of regulatory/policy topics. This creates certain content governance requirements:

### FINMA / Financial Market Regulation
- Publishing regulatory analysis is permissible as editorial/thought leadership
- Must not imply regulatory advice or guarantee regulatory accuracy
- Must not present speculative interpretations as established law
- Safe harbor language needed for regulatory-focused pieces

### Swiss Competition Law
- No disparagement of identifiable firms or named competitors
- Factual claims must be defensible
- Opinion/analysis protected; unsubstantiated factual claims risky

### Data Protection (FADP)
- If publishing content about individuals (case studies, profiles), FADP applies
- Anonymization required for any client/company references
- No publishing of personal data without consent

### Professional Responsibility (SAV/FSA)
- If positioning as "legal analysis," must note it is not legal advice
- Cannot create client-attorney relationship through content
- Appropriate disclaimers for regulatory/legal content
