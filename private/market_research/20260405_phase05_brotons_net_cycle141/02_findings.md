## Finding 01: Geneva Conference Intelligence Repurposing — Post-Event Analysis 48-72hr

Geneva hosts a dense institutional calendar — WTO, WHO, UN Climate, OECD, SECO, ITU, WIPO, and dozens of professional associations — that generates material for high-value post-event intelligence content. The 48-72 hour post-event analysis window is the critical differentiator: institutional readers who attended events seek synthesis and interpretation; those who missed events seek structured briefing. Brotons.net's conference intelligence repurposing framework addresses both audiences through a two-format strategy: (1) a rapid-scan 500-word post-event brief within 48 hours of major Geneva events, structured with key decisions, implications for Swiss/Romandie executives, and strategic planning implications; (2) a deep-dive 2,000-word analysis within 7-10 days incorporating additional institutional sources, follow-up interviews, and cross-event synthesis. The operational model requires pre-event preparation (speaker list analysis, background briefing drafting) to enable rapid post-event publication. The institutional event calendar drives 3-5x traffic surges when aligned with high-interest Geneva events, making this content format both a reader acquisition and retention tool.

## Finding 02: AI Search Engine Optimization — ChatGPT/Perplexity Citation Optimization

The emergence of AI-powered search (ChatGPT Search, Perplexity) as an institutional reader discovery channel creates new citation optimization requirements beyond traditional SEO. AI search engines index and cite sources differently from Google: they prioritize authoritative bylines, structured data, primary source citations, and institutional affiliation signals. Brotons.net's AI search optimization framework focuses on five elements: (1) author institutional bylines with explicit professional affiliations and credentials; (2) FAQPage schema markup on regulatory Q&A content to capture People Also Ask and AI search citations; (3) primary source citation networks through explicit reference to official documents (admin.ch, FINMA, SNB, SECO, FDPIC, WTO, WIPO) within body text; (4) recency signals including explicit publication dates, update timestamps, and content freshness notices on technical regulatory content; (5) canonical deep-dive pages that represent comprehensive treatment of topics rather than summary overviews. The citation optimization strategy targets the growing segment of Geneva executives who use AI search to identify authoritative sources on Swiss regulatory and market intelligence topics.

## Finding 03: Geneva Executive Reading Behavior — Scan vs. Deep-Read Dual-Mode

Geneva executives exhibit two distinct reading modes: rapid scan (90 seconds or less, focused on headlines and key conclusions) and deep-read (15-30 minutes for comprehensive analysis). The content strategy must address both modes simultaneously — a single article cannot serve both needs efficiently. The dual-mode design principle creates two distinct content tiers: (1) scan-optimized content with FR-primary executive summary headers, bulleted key conclusions at the top of every article, and concise 3-5 line executive framing before detailed analysis; (2) deep-read content with FR-primary comprehensive analysis, detailed regulatory interpretation, and explicit recommendation frameworks. The design principle applies equally to EN-primary and FR-primary articles, with language selection based on the target audience's reading preference (FR for Swiss Romandie executives, EN for international Geneva executives). The dual-mode architecture increases content production cost but significantly improves both reader acquisition (scan mode for discovery) and reader retention (deep-read mode for trust building).

## Finding 04: Swiss Romandie Newsletter Syndication — French Institutional Publication Partnership

Swiss Romandie newsletter syndication through French institutional publications offers a zero-cost distribution channel for Brotons.net content targeting the French-speaking Swiss executive audience. Publication partnership candidates include: (1) IHEID (Graduate Institute) alumni publications and working paper series, which reach Geneva international affairs professionals; (2) CCIG (Chambre de commerce,'industrie et des services de Genève) member briefings, which reach Geneva corporate executives; (3) Tribune de Genève and Le Temps editorial content syndication, which reach Geneva business readers; (4) RSR (Radio Suisse Romande) digital content partnerships, which reach broader Romandie audiences including institutional investors and government officials. The syndication model requires content adaptation: editorial-grade writing standards, French institutional register, and explicit attribution frameworks. The partner publication receives exclusive or first-publication rights for the Romandie edition while Brotons.net retains EN publication rights. The syndication channel accelerates subscriber acquisition in Romandie without paid acquisition costs, targeting the institutional professional audience most relevant to Geneva advisory service discovery.

## Finding 05: Institutional Citation Network — Zero-Cost Subscriber Acquisition via IHEID/SFAMA/CCIG

The institutional citation network — academic and professional bodies that cite authoritative sources in their publications, conference proceedings, and member communications — represents a zero-cost subscriber acquisition channel for Brotons.net. The model works through three mechanisms: (1) IHEID faculty citing Brotons.net research in academic publications or course reading lists, creating student and researcher subscriber flow; (2) SFAMA (Swiss Funds and Asset Management Association) referencing Brotons.net analysis in industry guidance or conference materials, reaching Swiss asset management professionals; (3) CCIG member briefings incorporating Brotons.net regulatory analysis, reaching Geneva corporate members. The institutional citation network requires proactive engagement: submitting research for IHEID working paper series, presenting at SFAMA events, and contributing to CCIG regulatory commentary. The acquisition cost is zero marginal cost per citation, but requires initial investment in establishing credibility with institutional gatekeepers. The model aligns with the SOUL.md principle of authority building through institutional validation rather than volume marketing.

## Finding 06: Corporate Team Subscription Tier — CHF 500-1,500/Year for 10-Seat Institutional Access

A corporate team subscription tier at CHF 500-1,500 per year enables institutional access for 10 users within a single organization, targeting Geneva SMEs, family offices, and mid-market companies with multiple decision-makers consuming Brotons.net content. The tiered access model generates recurring revenue from a previously underserved segment — organizations where multiple executives benefit from Brotons.net content but individual subscriptions are impractical. The pricing structure reflects value segmentation: (1) Base tier CHF 500/year for up to 5 users, includes weekly summary + access to scan-mode content; (2) Standard tier CHF 1,000/year for up to 10 users, includes full content library + deep-dive reports + quarterly strategy briefing; (3) Premium tier CHF 1,500/year for up to 10 users, includes Standard tier plus 2 seats for monthly 30-minute advisory calls or video briefings. The corporate tier converts the existing newsletter subscriber base (individual readers) into institutional accounts while maintaining the individual subscription tier for personal subscribers. The model requires account management infrastructure, but the revenue per corporate account significantly exceeds individual subscriptions.
