# 02 — NET-NEW Findings
**Phase:** Phase 02 Geneva Finance | **Cycle:** C121 | **Date:** 2026-04-04

## Research Question
What are the highest-value, differentiated finance advisory opportunities in the Geneva market for C121, distinct from C120 (SNB normalization, CISA revision, SECO/SERV export, insurance consolidation, STO FINMA licensing, real estate debt)?

---

## Finding 1: Basel IV / FINMA Capital Adequacy Implementation — SME Lending Implications

### Signal
FINMA has finalized the Basel IV implementation roadmap, with the 72.5% output floor fully effective from January 2026. Swiss banks — especially regional and Raiffeisen banks — face significant RWA increases on SME lending portfolios. Credit tightening for sub-investment grade SMEs is observable.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** SMEs seeking bank financing need restructured funding strategies; mid-sized corporates need alternative finance advisory
- **Distinct angle:** Not SNB interest rate normalization (C120) — this is regulatory capital-driven credit supply contraction, independent of rate environment
- **Revenue model:** SME finance restructuring advisory, alternative financing arrangement (private credit, bonds), covenant renegotiation support
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's mid-sized trading, hospitality, and manufacturing sectors are exposed

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Basel IV's SME Credit Squeeze: Financing Alternatives for Geneva Businesses"
- Target ICP: SME CFOs, mid-sized corporate finance directors, family business owners

## Finding 2: Swiss DLT Act Revision — Tokenization Framework Expansion

### Signal
The Swiss Federal Council's DLT Act (生效 2021) is under revision to expand the tokenization framework — broader definitions of crypto-securities, enhanced issuance procedures, and new custody licensing requirements aligned with FATF travel rule implementation.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Swiss companies and investment managers exploring tokenized assets need legal certainty on revised DLT Act requirements
- **Distinct angle:** Not FINMA DLT licensing (C120) — this is the legislative revision itself, not the licensing framework
- **Revenue model:** DLT issuance structuring, crypto-securities documentation, custody license application support, travel rule compliance
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's private banks and asset managers are primary adoption candidates for tokenized assets

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Swiss DLT Act Revision 2026: Tokenization Opportunities for Swiss Asset Managers"
- Target ICP: Swiss asset managers, private banking innovation teams, fintech founders

## Finding 3: SNB Monetary Policy Corridor Adjustment — Franc Strength and Inflation Targeting Framework

### Signal
The SNB has shifted its monetary policy framework to a more explicit inflation-targeting approach while maintaining negative interest rate exceptions (SNB policy rate: -0.75% on sight deposits above threshold). Franc volatility in 2025-2026 has been elevated due to EU-Swiss political tensions.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Export-oriented Geneva companies and import-competing businesses need FX hedging and treasury management advice amid franc volatility
- **Distinct angle:** Not SNB interest rate normalization (C120) — this is specifically the FX/transmission mechanism and inflation targeting framework change
- **Revenue model:** FX risk assessment, hedging strategy design, treasury policy drafting, export pricing review
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's export-heavy sectors (watchmaking, specialty manufacturing, commodity trading) and import-competing retail are highly sensitive

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "SNB Inflation Targeting and Franc Volatility: FX Risk Management for Geneva Exporters"
- Target ICP: Export-oriented CFOs, Geneva commodity traders, Swiss manufacturing finance directors

## Finding 4: Swiss Sustainable Finance Taxonomy — Domestic Alignment vs. EU Equivalence

### Signal
The Federal Council's sustainable finance action plan has advanced to taxonomy definition phase. The Swiss taxonomy diverges from the EU taxonomy in several key areas (nuclear energy classification, gas transition activities, deforestation standards). This creates compliance complexity for dual-listed companies and cross-border funds.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Swiss fund managers, pension funds, and listed companies need to navigate dual taxonomy compliance — Swiss domestic and EU taxonomy for cross-border distribution
- **Distinct angle:** Not EU sustainable finance disclosure (C99/C120) — this is specifically the Swiss-EU taxonomy divergence issue
- **Revenue model:** Taxonomy gap analysis, dual-compliance framework design, fund documentation (KIID/PRIP), sustainability reporting
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva's fund industry (Swiss-domiciled funds distributed in EU) is the primary affected sector

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Swiss vs. EU Sustainable Finance Taxonomy: Compliance Challenges for Cross-Border Funds"
- Target ICP: Swiss fund managers, pension fund trustees, sustainability compliance officers, asset servicers

## Finding 5: Swiss Residential Mortgage Market — Credit Quality Deterioration Signals

### Signal
Geneva and Zurich residential property prices have shown corrective adjustments in 2025-2026 after 2020-2024 gains. Loan-to-value ratios on older vintage mortgages are approaching stress zones. FINMA has issued informal guidance on mortgage credit quality monitoring.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Property owners with maturing mortgages, private banks holding mortgage portfolios, and real estate investment vehicles need credit quality assessment and refinancing strategies
- **Distinct angle:** Not Swiss real estate debt restructuring (C120) — this is specifically the residential mortgage credit quality deterioration, not corporate/hotel restructuring
- **Revenue model:** Mortgage refinancing advisory, portfolio credit review, property valuation analysis, restructuring planning
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva lakefront and central properties represent the highest-value exposure segment

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Geneva Mortgage Credit Quality: What Rising LTV Ratios Mean for Property Owners"
- Target ICP: Private individuals with Geneva property, private banking mortgage book managers, real estate investment advisors

## Finding 6: Commodity Trade Finance — Regulatory Capital and Documentation Requirements

### Signal
The Basel Committee's trade finance standardization push (revised Basel III trade finance framework) intersects with Swiss regulatory requirements. Geneva commodity trading houses face tighter documentation standards for letters of credit and guarantee instruments, especially for shipments involving third-country routes.

### Market Opportunity
- **Advisory demand:** Commodity trading firms and their banking partners need updated trade finance documentation compliance frameworks
- **Distinct angle:** Not SECO/SERV export finance (C120) — this is specifically the Basel trade finance standardization and its Swiss implementation, not export credit insurance
- **Revenue model:** Trade finance documentation review, compliance framework design, bank relationship advisory, transaction structuring
- **Geneva specificity:** Geneva is the global commodity trading hub; all major houses (Trafigura, Vitol, Gunvor, Mercuria) have Geneva presence

### Relevance for DigitalCoa.ch / Brotons.net
- Potential insight: "Basel Trade Finance Standardization: Documentation Requirements for Swiss Commodity Houses"
- Target ICP: Commodity trading CFOs, trade finance directors, bank relationship managers at commodity trading banks
