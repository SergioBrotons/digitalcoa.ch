## Core Problem Language Patterns

### On AI Tool Deployment Decision

> "I don't know what I'm accountable for when I sign off on using AI in a client engagement."
> — Geneva managing director, mid-sized boutique

> "The question isn't whether to use AI anymore. The question is what happens when something goes wrong and I have to explain what the tool did."
> — (fr) "La question n'est plus vraiment si on utilise l'IA. C'est ce qu'on dit au client quand quelque chose ne va pas."

> "I've been running AI-assisted projects for two years. I can't tell you what documentation I'm supposed to have. Nobody can."
> — Managing partner, 8-person consulting boutique

### On Carrier Attestation Personal Exposure

> "The insurance form asks me to sign personally that our AI review process is adequate. Adequate for what? Against what standard? I signed it because everyone signs them, but I had no idea what I was committing to."
> — (fr) "Le formulaire d'assurance me demande de signer personnellement que notre processus de révision IA est adéquat. Adéquat pour quoi ? Selon quelle norme ? Je l'ai signé parce que tout le monde le signe, mais je ne savais pas vraiment à quoi je m'engageais."

> "The attestation is designed by the carrier's lawyers to protect the carrier. When I sign it, I'm committing to something I can't even define."
> — Managing director, boutique consulting firm

### On Billing Model Incompatibility

> "AI makes us faster. Faster means less billable time. Less billable time means either higher rates or lower margins. We haven't solved either."
> — Senior partner, 12-person firm

> (fr) "L'IA nous rend plus rapides, mais notre modèle est basé sur le temps. Si je livre en deux heures ce que je facturais comme huit heures, comment je justifie le même prix ?"

> "The efficiency gain from AI goes somewhere. In our current model, it goes straight to margin compression. That doesn't feel like a sustainable position."
> — Geneva consulting MD

### On Client AI Governance Expectations

> "Clients are starting to ask AI questions in every proposal discussion. Not because they understand AI governance—they don't—but because their own boards are asking them. They're carrying the pressure down to us."
> — (fr) "Les clients commencent à poser des questions sur l'IA dans chaque discussion de proposition. Pas parce qu'ils comprennent la gouvernance IA—Ils ne la comprennent pas—mais parce que leurs propres conseils d'administration leur posent ces questions."

> "We've had three clients in the last six months ask for some kind of AI methodology documentation. We don't have it. We had to have a conversation about what we could actually show them."
> — Boutique managing partner

### On Engagement Letter Obsolescence

> "Our engagement letters were written before we started using AI tools. They say nothing about AI. The contracts governing our AI-assisted work are the old contracts."
> — (fr) "Nos lettres de mission ont été rédigées avant que nous commencions à utiliser des outils IA. Elles ne disent rien sur l'IA. Les contrats qui régissent notre travail assisté par IA sont les anciens contrats."

> "At this point, if a client asked us to explain how AI was used in their project, we'd have to reconstruct it from memory. That's not a comfortable position."
> — Small Geneva consulting firm MD

### On Proposal Defensibility

> "When a client asks how we arrived at a recommendation in an AI-assisted proposal, what do I actually say? 'The AI helped'? That's not a defensible methodology."
> — (fr) "Quand un client nous demande comment nous sommes arrivés à une recommandation dans une proposition assistée par IA, qu'est-ce que je dis exactement ? 'L'IA a aidé' ? Ce n'est pas une méthodologie défendable."

> "We've won work based on proposals that probably contained AI-generated analytical frameworks. If the client ever compared our proposal style against another firm's, they'd probably notice they sound similar. That should worry us."
> — Senior consultant, boutique firm

### On Sub-Consultant Delegation Chain

> "When we use a sub-consultant who also uses AI tools, what exactly are we accountable for? Their AI use? Our oversight of it? There's no framework that tells us."
> — (fr) "Quand nous utilisons un sous-consultant qui utilise également des outils IA, pour quoi sommes-nous exactement responsables ? Leur utilisation de l'IA ? Notre supervision de celle-ci ? Il n'existe pas de cadre qui nous le dise."

### On Peer Accountability Vacuum

> "There's no equivalent to what lawyers have with their bar. We're just peers talking to each other, and nobody wants to be the one who admits they don't have this sorted."
> — (fr) "Il n'existe pas d'équivalent à ce que les avocats ont avec leur barreau. Nous sommes juste des pairs qui se parlent, et personne ne veut être celui qui admet qu'il n'a pas tout résolu."

> "I've asked around. Every firm I know is in the same position. That makes me feel slightly better and significantly worse."
> — Geneva MD

### On Analytical Framework Continuity

> "We built up a specific way of working with the AI tool—specific prompts, specific configurations. When the model updated, some of that stopped working the same way. We didn't even notice until we looked at an old deliverable."
> — (fr) "Nous avons développé une façon spécifique de travailler avec l'outil IA—des invites spécifiques, des configurations spécifiques. Quand le modèle a été mis à jour, une partie de cela a cessé de fonctionner de la même manière. Nous ne l'avons même pas remarqué avant de regarder un ancien livrable."

## Entry Point Signals

When does the Geneva consulting MD realize they have a problem?

1. **First carrier renewal with AI attestation questions** — Attestation form arrives with AI-specific questions; MD realizes personal signature required for something undefined
2. **Sophisticated client asks AI methodology question** — Client asks something specific; MD cannot produce documentation
3. **Proposal comparison anxiety** — MD notices AI-assisted proposals from different boutiques sounding structurally similar; wonders if clients notice too
4. **Model update mid-engagement** — Active client work affected by tool update without notice or recovery path
5. **Sub-consultant AI delegation question** — Sub-consultant proposes using their own AI tools; MD has no framework for what to allow or require
6. **Departing staff takes AI institutional knowledge** — Staff who built AI workflows leaves; institutional knowledge in prompts and configurations departs with them
