## Primary Regulatory Framework

### Council of Europe AI Convention

**Status:** Signed by Switzerland March 27, 2025. Ratification in process. Consultation draft bill expected end 2026.

**What it means for Geneva consulting MDs:**
- Convention obligations flow through existing client contracts before implementation legislation arrives
- Art.5 obligations on "high-risk AI systems" apply to professional services when AI systems support decisions with legal or similarly significant effects
- Private law dimension: obligations enforceable through existing mandate contracts and professional liability claims
- No published Geneva-specific guidance on what Convention compliance means for consulting boutiques
- MD personal liability exposure activates through client contract terms, not through published guidance

**Key operational reality:**
> The Convention creates obligations now, through your existing client contracts, before the consultation draft becomes law. Your engagement letters — which say nothing about AI — may already be binding you to AI governance standards the Convention requires.

### Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP)

**Status:** In force. FDPIC confirmed May 2025: nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing.

**What it means for Geneva consulting MDs:**
- **Art.6: Lawfulness** — Processing must have legal basis. AI processing of client data in vendor platforms requires authorization assessment most boutiques haven't done.
- **Art.8: Profiling notification** — AI-assisted analysis of client situations may constitute profiling, triggering notification rights boutique doesn't know about.
- **Art.15/21: Automated decision explanation** — Client has right to explanation of AI-assisted conclusions. Boutique may not have discharge protocol.
- **Art.9: Third-party processors** — When sub-consultant uses AI tools processing client data, Art.9 authorization chain becomes active exposure.
- **Art.22: Register of processing activities** — Boutique should maintain AI processing register. Most don't know what it should contain.

**Key operational reality:**
> Your AI tool vendor is a third-party data processor under nFADP. Your engagement letters probably don't authorize the specific processing your vendor is doing. This has been true for 2-3 years.

### FINMA Guidance 08/2024

**Status:** In force for supervised institutions. Influence extending into professional services through carrier underwriting standards.

**What it means for Geneva consulting MDs:**
- FINMA expects supervised institutions to maintain AI inventory, governance frameworks, accountability documentation
- Carriers are incorporating FINMA-influenced expectations into AI attestation questions for non-FINMA-supervised clients
- Boutique consulting firms with financial services clients face compounding obligations
- Carrier iteration pattern tracking reveals FINMA expectations migrating into consulting sector standards

### Federal AI Coordination Office / Contact Point

**Status:** Contact point function transferred to Federal Chancellery February 1, 2026. Function crystallization ongoing.

**What it means for Geneva consulting MDs:**
- No published definition of what the Contact Point actually does beyond coordination
- Geneva consulting has no equivalent body publishing guidance
- Market still waiting for Federal Council consultation draft (expected end 2026) before Geneva-specific standards clarity

## Professional Liability and Insurance Context

### Carrier AI Questionnaire Evolution

**Current state:** Professional liability carriers iteratively adding AI-specific questions to renewal forms. Three generations visible:

- **First generation:** General AI use disclosure (existence question)
- **Second generation:** AI policy documentation existence (policy question)
- **Third generation:** Specific AI review decisions, evidence of what MD reviewed, performance evidence of methodology in operation (evidence question)

**The specific problem for Geneva consulting:**
- Third-generation questions require methodology performance evidence boutiques cannot produce with current documentation
- Carrier standards forming without Geneva consulting input
- MD signature commits to adequacy against carrier-defined standard carrier has not defined for consulting sector
- No published interpretation of what "adequate" means at 2-15 person consulting firm scale

### Personal Liability Under revFADP

**Key development:** Individuals, not just companies, face criminal fines up to CHF 250,000 for intentional or grossly negligent nFADP breaches under revised act.

**What this means for consulting MDs:**
- Personal liability exposure for AI governance failures, not just entity liability
- AI-attributable errors in client deliverables may trigger personal exposure
- Carrier attestation personal signature becomes personal liability commitment, not entity commitment
- No Geneva market service offering personal exposure mapping against existing coverage

### Art.398 CO Proper Performance and MD Liability

**The structural problem:** Swiss Code of Obligations Art.398 defines agent's proper performance obligations. AI tool use creates ambiguity about what "proper" performance requires when:

- AI produces defensible outputs through pattern synthesis not principled derivation
- MD cannot independently evaluate AI-assisted analytical conclusions
- Vendor controls tool behavior, not MD
- Engagement letter defines no AI methodology standard

MD may be personally liable for AI-assisted deliverable deficiencies while structurally unable to evaluate what the AI produced.

## Geneva Professional Ecosystem Context

### SVA / Professional Body Vacuum

**Problem:** No Geneva consulting equivalent to:
- Ordre des Avocats (Geneva bar for lawyers)
- SFAMA/SRO (financial industry self-regulatory organizations)
- SUISA / professional associations with AI guidance authority

**Consequence:** 
- No Geneva consulting-specific AI governance guidance exists
- Carrier questionnaires function as de facto operative standard
- No feedback loop to standards development
- MD operating without professional body accountability framework

### Peer Accountability Circuit

**What exists:** Informal peer exchanges (Geneva business dinners, industry groups, LinkedIn discussions) function as sole calibration mechanism.

**What's missing:**
- No confidentiality protection equivalent to bar privilege
- No formal peer review framework
- No mechanism for sharing accountability intelligence without commercial exposure
- Circuit provides social reassurance without substantive calibration at elevated stakes

### nFADP Register Requirements for Small Firms

**The specific gap:** nFADP Art.22 requires processing activity register. No Geneva authority published guidance on what consulting boutique's AI processing register should contain. FDPIC guidance letter era has ended; operational audit era has begun. No small-firm register template exists from any Geneva authority.

## Timing and Documentation Window

### Mid-2026 Documentation Window

**The dynamic:** Documentation window between now and end-2026 (when consultation draft arrives) creates burden-of-proof positioning exercise, not just documentation improvement.

**Before window closes:**
- Firm building documentation evaluated against forming standards
- Evidence standard is what Geneva ecosystem determines it to be, not what eventually gets published
- Proportionate architecture can be built without reactive compliance effort

**After window closes:**
- Firm explaining why documentation doesn't exist
- Standards arrived; absence now culpable
- New documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct window-period evidence

**The acute positioning imperative:**
Geneva consulting MDs who build proportionate documentation architecture before the consultation draft arrives will be evaluated against standards they're helping to form. MDs who wait will be evaluated against standards others formed without their input.

## Geneva Institutional Ecosystem

### Carrier Questionnaire as De Facto Standard-Setter

**Mechanism:** Carrier iterations produce observable standard-formation:
- Each renewal questionnaire reveals what carriers determined adequate since last iteration
- Pattern tracking across iterations shows where standards are moving
- Geneva consulting MD operating without visibility into this standard-formation process

**The accountability formation loop:**
Carrier examination outcomes → Geneva peer circuit intelligence → Institutional counterparty requirement formation → Carrier questionnaire adjustment → repeat

This loop generates operative standards before any published doctrine. Most exposed MDs (least time for circuit participation) have least access to intelligence about standards forming.

### Geneva Institutional Density and AI Convention Implementation

**The specific Geneva property:** Geneva institutional ecosystem (international organizations, diplomatic community, multinational corporate presence) produces AI accountability expectations that arrive in consulting client relationships before Bern formal process publishes guidance.

**Implication for consulting MDs:**
- Clients with international parent companies may be carrying AI governance expectations from jurisdictions with stricter requirements
- Downstream deployer obligations from EU AI Act flow into Geneva consulting engagements
- Geneva consulting accountability standards form through institutional density, not through published regulatory doctrine
