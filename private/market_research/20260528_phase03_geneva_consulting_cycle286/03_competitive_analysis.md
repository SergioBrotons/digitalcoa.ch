## What Exists

### Large Firm Services (Not Serving This ICP)

**KPMG Switzerland**
- ISO 42001 certification readiness
- EU AI Act compliance frameworks
- AI governance frameworks for large enterprises
- Market position: multinational corporations, financial institutions
- Gap: Not designed for 2-15 person boutiques; methodology doesn't scale down; pricing inaccessible

**PwC Switzerland**
- AI governance, responsible AI consulting
- EU AI Act compliance services
- Market position: large corporates, listed companies
- Gap: Same as KPMG — enterprise scale, enterprise pricing, enterprise documentation requirements

**Swiss Cyber Institute**
- AI Governance 360 program
- Certified AI Governance Professional (AIGP) certification
- Market position: broad professional audience, individual credentialing
- Gap: Training/credentialing focus, not boutique-scale advisory; generalist, not Geneva-specific

**Lenz & Staehelin / CMS / Pestalozzi**
- Legal guidance on AI regulation
- Market position: large law firm clients, corporate transactions
- Gap: Law firm focus not consulting boutique; advice on legal framework, not accountability architecture

**No equivalent service exists for:**
1. Carrier AI attestation pre-signing review (personal liability instrument review)
2. Engagement letter AI audit for active client relationships
3. Proposal defensibility reconstruction methodology
4. Sub-consultant AI delegation framework

**Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review**

**Boutique-Scale AI Governance Documentation Methodology**
- ISO 42001 too enterprise; no Geneva-service adapted to 2-15 person firm
- Generic AI governance frameworks designed for organizations with separate compliance functions
- Small consulting firm MD is user, compliance officer, billing attorney, and client manager simultaneously
- No methodology proportionate to boutique scale

**Engagement Letter AI Architecture Service**
- No service offering engagement letter AI audit for active client relationships
- No framework for mid-relationship AI methodology disclosure
- No tool for determining what disclosure is contractually appropriate vs. exposure-creating

**Geneva Consulting-Specific AI Accountability Positioning**
- No Geneva-based boutique advisor offering AI governance services specifically for consulting firms
- The Big 4 and Swiss Cyber Institute serve different ICPs
- No firm positioned as "the Geneva consulting firm's AI accountability advisor"

## What's Missing (Market Gaps)

### Gap 1: Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review Service

**What it is:** Independent review of carrier AI attestation form from signatory's personal liability perspective before signing.

**What it would require:**
- Analysis of what MD personally commits to by signing
- Mapping against Swiss professional liability doctrine (Art.398 CO)
- Assessment of coverage implications
- Identification of gaps between attestation commitments and operational reality
- Proportionate remediation architecture for boutique scale

**Current state:** No Geneva market participant offers this. MDs are unreviewed signatories on personal liability instruments.

### Gap 2: Engagement Letter AI Architecture

**What it is:** Methodology for determining what AI authorization, methodology disclosure, version-locking, and attribution language engagement letters should contain for consulting engagements.

**What it would require:**
- Geneva-specific legal framework analysis (CO, nFADP, AI Convention obligations)
- Boutique-scale proportionate framework (not enterprise policy document)
- Active client relationship review protocol
- Disclosure vs. exposure analysis for different disclosure approaches

**Current state:** No service exists. Engagement letters remain AI-silent for active relationships.

### Gap 3: Proposal Defensibility Reconstruction

**What it is:** Retrospective methodology for demonstrating how AI-assisted proposal was produced, what analytical framework was used, what MD reviewed and approved.

**What it would require:**
- Prompt-to-delivery chain documentation architecture
- Client-facing methodology disclosure framework
- Attribution language for consulting deliverables
- Documentation for what firm genuinely holds independently of AI influence

**Current state:** No service. Boutiques cannot answer client methodology questions about proposals already delivered.

### Gap 4: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework

**What it is:** Framework defining what AI delegation to sub-consultants requires in consulting context — authorization protocols, nFADP processor obligations, accountability chain documentation.

**What it would require:**
- Geneva consulting-specific sub-delegation framework
- nFADP Art.9 third-party processing mapping
- Prime boutique accountability architecture
- Framework proportionate to boutique contractual practice

**Current state:** No framework. Sub-consultant AI delegation is ungoverned.

### Gap 5: Multi-Obligation Documentation Interaction Mapping

**What it is:** Methodology for managing simultaneous documentation obligations under carrier questionnaires, client AI governance requests, engagement letter standards, AI Convention, and nFADP.

**What it would require:**
- Document relationship mapping across obligation systems
- Coherence architecture (system-level property distinct from individual document quality)
- Boutique active portfolio management framework
- Compounding effects analysis across client engagements

**Current state:** No service. Boutiques manage each obligation independently, producing documents that individually appear adequate but collectively present incoherent picture.

### Gap 6: Geneva Consulting AI Accountability Briefing (Lead Magnet)

**What it is:** 90-minute briefing for Geneva consulting MDs on the six accountability architecture problems that exist before any framework names them.

**Current state:** No Geneva market participant offers this specific format, price point, or content focus. The market is the gap.

## Competitive Positioning Assessment

### Where Positioning Space Exists

**Geneva-specific boutique consulting AI accountability advisory:**
- Not KPMG (enterprise focus)
- Not PwC (enterprise focus)  
- Not Swiss Cyber Institute (credentialing focus)
- Not law firms (legal doctrine focus)
- Not generic AI governance consultants (not Geneva-specific, not consulting-boutique-scale)

**The specific positioning opportunity:**
- Geneva consulting MD as the client
- AI governance accountability architecture problem as the frame
- Boutique-scale proportionate methodology
- Personal liability exposure as the acute pain point (carrier attestation)
- Geneva-specific regulatory context (CoE AI Convention, nFADP, carrier questionnaire evolution)

### What Would Differentiate

1. **Naming the specific problem precisely** — Not "AI governance" but "the carrier attestation you signed last week exposes you personally, and nobody in Geneva reviewed it before you signed"
2. **Geneva-specific accountability formation intelligence** — Sustained participation in Geneva ecosystem producing pattern knowledge competitors cannot replicate without same participation
3. **Boutique-scale methodology** — Enterprise frameworks don't work at 2-15 person scale; methodology must be designed for the scale that actually needs it
4. **Multi-obligation interaction mapping** — Single-document solution fails; what competitors offer is individual documents, not document system coherence
