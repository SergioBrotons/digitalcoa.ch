## 6 NET-NEW Findings (Distinct from All Prior Cycles)

### Finding 01: Carrier Attestation Personal Signature as Primary Acute Pain Point

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles identified carrier attestation as a structural exposure. C286 isolates the personal signature mechanism as the acute pain point that creates urgency distinct from the structural exposure.

**The finding:** Third-generation carrier AI questionnaires now require the managing director's personal signature committing to "adequate" AI review methodology. "Adequate" is undefined. The form is written by the carrier's lawyers to protect the carrier. The MD's personal signature creates personal liability exposure the entity signature never created.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named carrier attestation as a documentation gap. This finding names the personal signature as a specific mechanism that activates urgency and personal exposure — distinct from documentation quality or methodology adequacy questions.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The form uses your name. The signature is yours. What you committed to when you signed is something the form doesn't define and your carrier hasn't explained."*

### Finding 02: Engagement Letter Architecture Collapse for All Active Relationships

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles identified engagement letter obsolescence as a structural observation. C286 maps the specific active-relationship exposure and names the "AI authorization gap" as a discrete unnamed service category.

**The finding:** Every active client engagement is governed by contracts written before the firm's AI adoption. Those contracts contain no AI authorization, no methodology disclosure language, no version-locking, no client disclosure obligations. Actual delivery practice has superseded the governing documents silently and continuously for 2-3 years. The exposure exists in every active relationship simultaneously.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings mentioned engagement letter obsolescence as part of the accountability gap. This finding isolates the active-relationship architecture collapse as the specific operational problem requiring discrete service attention, and names it as a service category ("engagement letter AI architecture audit") distinct from general documentation improvement.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Your engagement letters govern work that is no longer the work those letters describe. Every active client relationship is governed by contracts written before your firm started using AI. That's not a documentation gap. That's an active exposure in every engagement you currently have."*

### Finding 03: Proposal Stylometric Convergence as Structural Accountability Problem

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles mentioned AI-assisted proposal quality and proposal defensibility. C286 identifies the specific mechanism — stylometric convergence across boutiques using similar tools — as a structural problem creating exposure before any individual firm's proposal quality is evaluated.

**The finding:** Multiple Geneva consulting boutiques using similar AI tools over similar timeframes produce analytically parallel proposals. When clients run comparative analysis — which they are beginning to do — structurally similar proposals from different boutiques raise questions about independent judgment that no individual firm's documentation can answer. The problem exists regardless of individual proposal quality.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings addressed proposal defensibility from the individual firm's documentation gap perspective. This finding isolates the cross-firm stylometric convergence mechanism as a structural property that makes the problem unsolvable through individual firm documentation alone.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"You've looked at proposals from different boutiques. They sound similar. That's not a coincidence. It's a structural property of how AI synthesis works across parallel tool use. And it's a problem before your client ever asks a question about it."*

### Finding 04: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Chain Without Any Framework

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles named sub-consultant delegation as an accountability gap. C286 maps the specific nFADP Art.9 third-party processing chain flowing to the prime boutique, identifies the engagement letter silence on delegation, and names the "sub-consultant AI delegation framework" as a discrete unnamed service category.

**The finding:** Prime boutique contracts sub-consultant using AI tools. Client holds prime boutique accountable. Prime boutique cannot describe what AI the sub-consultant used or how. nFADP Art.9 processor authorization chain may activate for client data processed through sub-consultant AI. No Geneva professional body has published guidance. No engagement letter standard addresses it. No service exists to help prime boutique build this framework.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the sub-consultant delegation as an accountability gap. This finding maps the specific legal mechanism (Art.9), names the specific decision points requiring documentation architecture, and identifies the service category as a discrete market opportunity.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"When your sub-consultant uses AI tools you haven't evaluated, your client holds you accountable for the output. You hold the accountability. They hold the tools. Nobody has built the framework for what that relationship is supposed to look like."*

### Finding 05: Vendor Terms Structural Incompatibility With MD Professional Liability

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles mentioned AI vendor lock-in and authorization evolution gaps. C286 identifies the specific structural incompatibility between vendor terms and MD personal professional liability architecture as a discrete undisclosed exposure mechanism.

**The finding:** AI platform vendor terms are written for vendor commercial interests. They cannot simultaneously satisfy vendor terms, Art.398 CO proper performance obligations, and MD personal liability. Standard platform terms create conditions where all three cannot be achieved together. The MD signs the terms. The team uses the platform. The incompatibility is never mapped.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings addressed vendor authorization gaps and lock-in. This finding isolates the specific legal incompatibility between vendor contractual terms and MD professional liability doctrine, names it as structurally unresolvable through vendor negotiation, and positions proportionate documentation architecture as the only available response.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"Your vendor's terms were written by lawyers protecting a technology company's interests. They weren't written to be compatible with your personal professional liability exposure under Swiss law. They don't need to be. You signed them anyway."*

### Finding 06: Documentation Window Burden-of-Proof Reversal as Positioning Imperative

**NET-NEW vs. prior cycles:** Prior cycles named the documentation window as a timing consideration. C286 isolates the specific burden-of-proof reversal mechanism and the retroactive impossibility property as the positioning imperative that creates urgency distinct from compliance deadline thinking.

**The finding:** Before the consultation draft arrives, firms building documentation are evaluated against forming standards. Their documentation is evidence of what they determined adequate, when, and why. After the draft arrives, firms without documentation are explaining absence. New documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct window-period reasoning. The decisions made during the window — only creatable during the window — are the evidence that matters.

**Why it's distinct:** Prior findings named the documentation window as a timing factor. This finding isolates the specific burden-of-proof reversal mechanism, the retroactive impossibility property, and the positioning imperative that makes pre-draft documentation a strategic decision rather than a compliance exercise.

**Ready-to-use language:** *"The window is not a deadline. It's a burden-of-proof exercise. What you document during it is evidence of what you thought and why. After it closes, new documentation can't recreate that evidence. The decisions you make now — they're the only ones that count."*
