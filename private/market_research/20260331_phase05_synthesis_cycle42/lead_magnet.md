## Universal Lead Magnet Framework
Each of the 6 lead magnets follows the same 6-decision framework structure, adapted to vertical-specific context:

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit (Universal Entry — Zero Cost)
Identify every AI tool currently in use. No external involvement. No cost. One internal meeting. Surfaces the gap without prescribing a solution.

### Decision 1: FADP Compliance Mapping (Universal — Obligations Already Operative)
FADP applies to AI-supported data processing without new legislation (FDPIC May 2025 confirmed). Map which AI tools process personal data and whether the firm has compliant legal basis.

### Decision 2: Data Residency Check (Universal — Swiss/EU Servers Required)
US CLOUD Act creates exposure for any firm using US-based AI tools. Swiss/EU data residency eliminates this exposure.

### Decision 3: DPIA for High-Risk AI Processing (Universal — Vertical-Specific Thresholds)
Data Protection Impact Assessment required when AI processing is likely to result in high risk to rights and freedoms. Thresholds vary by vertical (legal: contract analysis; WM: portfolio recommendations; healthcare: diagnostic support; RE: property valuations).

### Decision 4: Privacy by Design AI Procurement Policy (Universal — Prevents Future Drift)
Any new AI tool adopted must go through a procurement assessment: (1) data residency, (2) FADP compliance basis, (3) transparency, (4) human review availability.

### Decision 5: Documentation and Staff Briefing (Universal — FDPIC Expects This)
Document governance decisions. Brief staff. Assign ongoing responsibility. FDPIC expects purpose + functionality + data sources disclosure for AI deployments.
