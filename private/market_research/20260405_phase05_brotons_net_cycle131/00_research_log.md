**NET-NEW Findings This Session (C131 Phase 05)**

1. **Geneva executive podcast/audio content strategy** — Brotons.net is primarily text-based. Geneva executives consume content during commutes and travel. A bilingual EN+FR executive audio briefing (10-15 minutes, key regulatory/commercial developments of the week, delivered via LinkedIn audio and podcast platforms) would capture executive attention during commute time when reading is not possible. Key insight: audio content performs differently from written content — it requires conversational delivery, summary-first structure, and Geneva-specific framing. The opportunity is not repurposing written articles but creating audio-native content designed for executive listening. Differentiation: Brotons.net's analytical depth adapted to audio format.

2. **Geneva institutional partnership content syndication** — IHEID (Geneva Graduate Institute), CCIG (Chambre de commerce, d'industrie et des services de Genève), and SFAMA (Swiss Fund and Asset Management Association) publish calendars, reports, and event notices. Brotons.net could establish content syndication relationships where institutional partners republish or link to Brotons.net analysis in exchange for access to Brotons.net's content for their member communications. The benefit for institutional partners: quality analysis that enhances their member communications. The benefit for Brotons.net: distribution amplification and institutional credibility signals. French-language summary alongside English analysis creates natural fit with CCIG and IHEID FR-dominant communications.

3. **Brotons.net topic authority clusters — Geneva compliance database positioning** — Search intent for "Geneva regulatory analysis" and "Swiss compliance briefing" is underserved by generic legal databases. Brotons.net can build topic authority clusters around Geneva-specific compliance themes (Geneva arbitration, Swiss sanctions compliance, Geneva financial regulation, EU-CH bilateral implications). Each cluster: one pillar page + 4-6 supporting articles + one lead magnet. Target: featured snippet capture and "people also ask" positioning for Geneva-specific regulatory questions. This positions Brotons.net as the authoritative Geneva compliance knowledge base, not just a blog.

4. **Newsletter deliverability — SPF/DKIM/DMARC optimization for Geneva institutional domains** — Geneva IO professionals and Geneva executive readers are increasingly using email filtering and corporate email with strict SPF/DKIM/DMARC requirements. Substack email deliverability to corporate Geneva domains (icrc.org, unog.ch, WHO.int, WTO.org, bluewin.ch, geneva.edu) is inconsistent due to DMARC policies. Technical deliverability optimization (proper SPF alignment, BIMI for brand trust signals, inbox placement testing) can measurably improve open rates for Geneva executive segments. This is a technical but high-ROI improvement for the newsletter-to-briefing-call conversion funnel.

5. **Brotons.net Geneva knowledge hub positioning — distinct from standalone blogs and legal databases** — Brotons.net occupies a specific niche: Geneva institutional intelligence interpreted for decision-makers. The competitive set is not other independent blogs (which are too generalist) and not legal databases (which are too technical). The positioning is "Geneva decision-maker's intelligence layer" — between the raw institutional source and the formal legal analysis. This positioning requires explicit articulation on the site (about page, value proposition page) and in content (Geneva-specific framing, decision-maker relevance statement in every article). The outcome sought: Geneva executives should think "Brotons.net for Geneva intelligence" not "another legal blog."

6. **Brotons.net reader conversion path optimization — from reader to lead** — The current newsletter-to-briefing-call conversion path has multiple friction points: no explicit call to action on articles, newsletter CTAs are generic, no segment-specific landing pages for different reader personas (CFO, CEO, diplomat, NGO director). The improvement: (1) topic-specific landing pages for each content cluster with persona-matched CTAs, (2) "Geneva Briefing" as a premium newsletter tier (weekly audio briefing + written analysis + monthly live Q&A), (3) conversion tracking by content topic to identify which themes drive the highest briefing call intent. This is a content marketing funnel optimization exercise distinct from pure content creation.
