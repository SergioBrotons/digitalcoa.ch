## The Seven Deadlines

**1. FADP — NOW**
The revised Federal Act on Data Protection is operative. Human review required for automated decisions affecting individuals. Transparency obligations apply to AI-assisted interactions. Non-compliance exposure starts now.

**2. Federal AI Ordinance — January 1, 2025 (Already Operative)**
Most Geneva businesses are unaware that this has been in force since January 1, 2025. It classifies AI systems by risk level and requires documentation, human oversight for high-risk uses, and transparency disclosures. If you're using AI in customer service, HR, financial decision-making, or client advisory, your AI tools likely need classification under this ordinance.

**3. DigitalSwitzerland AI Guidance — May 2026**
Industry guidance on AI governance best practices, expected to clarify risk classification and create a Swiss-specific framework that supplements the Federal AI Ordinance. Likely to become the de facto standard for SMB compliance.

**4. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (Healthcare)**
Medical device database for AI systems used in patient care. No grace period. If your medical practice uses AI diagnostic, imaging, or treatment planning tools, they need to be registered before July 1 — or you need to stop using them.

**5. EU GPAI Regulation — August 2, 2026**
The EU General Purpose AI Regulation affects any Geneva business serving EU clients or using GPAI systems in work for European customers. Technical documentation, transparency, and incident reporting obligations apply. Four months after Swissdamed. Two major deadlines in the same calendar year.

**6. Federal Council AI Act — End 2026**
Switzerland's federal AI legislation, expected to harmonize and clarify the current ordinance-level framework. The final form will matter for all six sectors.

**7. Ongoing — Sector-Specific Professional Standards**
Lawyers: SBA Guidelines (June 2024). Finance: FINMA AI guidance (2025). Healthcare: FOM human review requirements. Each sector has an active professional standard that runs concurrent with the general regulatory framework.

## The Three Governance Dimensions

Every Geneva business using AI faces three concurrent governance requirements:

**Dimension 1: FADP Legal Obligations**
Human review for automated decisions. Transparency to data subjects. Data minimization and purpose limitation. Documentation of legal basis for processing.

**Dimension 2: Federal AI Ordinance Risk Classification**
Classify every AI tool by risk level. High-risk AI: mandatory human oversight, technical documentation, transparency disclosures. Limited-risk AI: transparency obligations only. Know what you're running.

**Dimension 3: Sector-Specific Professional Standards**
Your industry professional body may have issued AI guidance or standards. These are non-binding but set the standard of care. If a client, regulator, or court asks "what did a reasonable firm in your position do?", the professional standard is part of the answer.

## The Six-Sector Governance Gap

**Legal:** 40% of Geneva Bar members using AI. 12% with a governance policy. 28-percentage-point gap. SBA Guidelines create professional standard on top of FADP and AI Ordinance.

**Finance:** FINMA's 2025 AI guidance adds specific requirements for suitability assessments, robo-advisory, and portfolio rebalancing. AML AI creates a specific FADP tension: AML tools require maximum data; FADP requires data minimization. Unresolved industry-wide.

**Consulting:** GPAI August 2 deadline creates specific obligations for firms serving EU clients. Two-dimensional liability: consulting firms are exposed on both their own AI use and their AI recommendations to clients.

**Healthcare:** Swissdamed July 1 is the sharpest single deadline in the portfolio. 35% of Geneva practices using AI; fewer than 15% have assessed Swissdamed registration requirements. FOM human review standard: AI as second opinion, not autonomous decision-maker.

**Real Estate:** FADP Art. 21 applies to automated valuation models and tenant screening. The belief-action gap is the largest in the portfolio: 90% of agents believe AI will transform the industry; fewer than 5% have a governance framework. Most acute "pilot trap" in Geneva.

**All Sectors:** The Federal AI Ordinance has been operative since January 1, 2025. Most businesses don't know. Most AI tools in use haven't been classified. The compliance clock started 15 months ago.
