# 03 — Competitive Landscape
## Geneva Consulting — C131

## EU-CH Bilateral Agreement Advisory

**Key Players:** Lenz Staehelin (Geneva), Kellerhals Carrard (Bern/Geneva), Wenger & Vieli (Zurich/Geneva), Bär & Kahler (Geneva), Swiss Business Federation economiesuisse, Geneva Chamber of Commerce CCIG

**Differentiation:**
- Primarily law firms providing legal scenario analysis and compliance mapping
- Independent non-law-firm advisory for SME strategic planning is underserved
- Big 4 have EU-CH practices but typically at large multinational level

**Opportunity:** SME-level EU-CH dual-track strategic advisory. Independent, non-legal, focused on operational contingency planning for Geneva SMEs. Positioning as "strategic advisor" rather than legal counsel.

## Board Diversity Quota Advisory

**Key Players:** Hewer Advisory (Geneva women on boards specialist), AWPE (Geneva board placement), University of Geneva Executive Education (board training), Heidrick & Struggles (Zurich/Geneva board search), Spencer Stuart Switzerland

**Differentiation:**
- Board search firms focus on placement; less on compliance program design
- Big 4 governance advisory is conflicted for audit clients
- Independent Art. 716a compliance documentation advisory is thin

**Opportunity:** Independent board diversity compliance advisory for Geneva listed companies — gap analysis, nomination committee support, documentation. Positioning without audit conflict.

## Supply Chain Due Diligence SME Advisory

**Key Players:** Swiss Export (Geneva-based trade compliance), BDO sustainability advisory, Carpathian (supply chain compliance), various legal boutiques, Am revoc (Geneva compliance training)

**Differentiation:**
- Most supply chain due diligence providers target large enterprises
- SME-priced, simplified due diligence documentation frameworks are underserved
- Geneva Chamber of Commerce (CCIG) offers some member services but not dedicated consultancy

**Opportunity:** SME-priced supply chain due diligence gap assessment and documentation templates for Geneva SMEs below the 500-employee threshold but facing buyer demands.

## Interim Management Coordination

**Key Players:** Switzerland Interim Management Association (SIMA), Devises (Geneva-based interim management), Badenoch & Clark (Geneva), Hubl (Geneva headhunting), Brainnet (Zurich/Geneva)

**Differentiation:**
- Generalist interim management firms cover broad needs; specialized compliance niches underserved
- Big 4 interim practices exist but audit conflict limits access for audit clients
- No Geneva-focused specialized compliance interim management coordinator

**Opportunity:** Specialized interim management coordination for CARB/CRS/DORA/ESG compliance niches. Advisory + matching service rather than pure placement.

## Cantonal Innovation Incentive Advisory

**Key Players:** OCE Geneva economic development agency, SECO/inno-swiss, Geneva startups/coworking spaces, Geneva finance consortium, Rwoods (Geneva innovation consulting), various cantonal innovation agencies

**Differentiation:**
- OCE/SECO are government agencies — advisory is administrative, not strategic
- Private innovation consultancies focus on large corporates or VC-backed startups
- SME innovation incentive advisory (strategic alignment + application) is underserved

**Opportunity:** SME innovation incentive advisory — helping Geneva SMBs identify relevant programs, align innovation projects strategically, and optimize application. Not just form-filling.

## Departing IO Professional Wealth Advisory

**Key Players:** Geneva private banks (Lombard Odier, Pictet, Bordier, Baumann), Geneva wealth managers, Julius Baer IO specialist desk, CIG (Geneva independent wealth advisory)

**Differentiation:**
- Private banks target IO professionals as private banking clients (AUM-based)
- No independent advisory focused on JMDI pension optimization and departure transition planning
- UN/IO HR offices are passive — no structured departure advisory offered

**Opportunity:** Independent wealth advisory for departing IO professionals — JMDI optimization, tax residency planning, Geneva private sector career transition. Not competing with private banks for AUM; competing on expertise.
