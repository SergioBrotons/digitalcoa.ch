# Market Signals — Phase 03 Geneva Consulting — Cycle 290
# Small Consulting Boutique, 2–50 Employees — Geneva

## Current Problems, Complaints, Failures

---

### Problem 1: Client Questionnaire Response Becoming Implied Warranty

**Signal:** Geneva consulting boutiques receiving client AI governance questionnaires are treating response preparation as a documentation exercise. The boutique drafts "we maintain human oversight of all AI-assisted deliverables" and submits. The response document becomes a corporate record. The client then evaluates each subsequent deliverable against the standards the boutique self-certified. The boutique has no mechanism tracking whether its actual delivery practice matches its stated methodology. The questionnaire response is an implied warranty against itself.

**Failure mode:** The boutique cannot reconstruct whether human oversight was performed on deliverables produced before the questionnaire response. Post-questionnaire, every deliverable inconsistency with documented methodology creates a liability chain the boutique cannot manage.

**Market consequence:** The boutique's own AI governance questionnaire response is generating accountability exposure the boutique did not have before responding.

---

### Problem 2: Carrier Form Signed, Coverage Confirmation Unobtainable

**Signal:** Small Geneva consulting boutiques signing carrier third-generation AI attestation forms. Carrier forms require personal MD signature committing to AI governance controls. The existing PI policy predates the carrier form and contains no explicit AI coverage language. Neither carrier nor broker can confirm in writing that AI-assisted professional error is covered. MD is personally exposed on a document the firm's insurance may not defend.

**Failure mode:** Two structurally different questions—attestation adequacy and insurance coverage—are answered as one. When the boutique asks for clarification, the carrier does not provide written AI professional liability coverage gap analysis. Broker cannot provide Geneva-specific mapping. The MD signs a personal exposure document with no coverage confirmation pathway.

**Market consequence:** Carrier attestation creates personal liability without corresponding insurance coverage confirmation. The MD who signed has exposure they cannot transfer to insurance without obtaining a map that does not exist in the Geneva market.

---

### Problem 3: AI Deliverable Timestamp Documents Presentation, Not Analytical Validity

**Signal:** AI model continuous updates are silently propagating into active client deliverables. The deliverable has a timestamp showing when it was presented. The AI model generating the analytical framework has updated multiple times since the mandate began. The MD cannot confirm what model version state the analysis reflects. Timestamp certifies presentation date, not analytical framework validity under mandate conditions.

**Failure mode:** Sophisticated clients or carriers asking "what model version produced this analysis under what conditions" cannot be answered. Art.398 CO proper performance requires analysis reflecting conditions as of mandate date. The boutique presenting deliverables cannot confirm this property. Evidence gap exists even when no error has occurred.

**Market consequence:** Deliverable version state is structurally undocumented across the Geneva boutique mandate book. A dispute or audit requiring version state documentation cannot be satisfied retroactively.

---

### Problem 4: Peer Circuit AI Governance Silence is Interpreted as Embarrassment Signal

**Signal:** Geneva consulting boutique MDs attempting to benchmark AI governance methodology through peer dialogue receive no concrete responses. When asked "how are you handling client AI governance questionnaires?" in professional settings, the response is either general or absent. The MD interprets this as: no peer has a methodology they are willing to stand behind publicly. The silence is treated as an embarrassment signal, not a market failure.

**Failure mode:** Successful peer dialogue on AI governance accountability requires a peer to publicly expose their methodology. The first peer to do so is visible wrong—which prevents peer exchange. The market-wide absence of peer methodology dialogue prevents benchmarking and creates isolation-as-default for all boutiques.

**Market consequence:** Each boutique independently discovers that peer consultation yields no concrete AI governance methodology responses. This individual experience across multiple boutiques confirms an industry-wide gap. The isolation is not individual failure but structural condition produced by the market's inability to distribute accountability methodology.

---

### Problem 5: Engagement Letter AI Disclosure Decision Creates Structural Paradox

**Signal:** Boutique MD deciding whether to include AI methodology language in engagement letters faces a structural paradox. Including AI language creates nFADP Article 9 processing obligations toward the client. The engagement letter documents the processing. The legal basis for that processing must be established. If not established, the engagement letter is evidence of unbased processing. Excluding AI language preserves privilege over methodology but creates a client who can claim non-disclosure.

**Failure mode:** No Geneva market service maps this trade-off at boutique scale. The MD evaluates whether to disclose in isolation. The engagement letter is written by legal templates that do not contain Geneva-calibrated AI disclosure decision architecture. Neither the disclosure option nor the non-disclosure option is risk-free.

**Market consequence:** The engagement letter AI disclosure decision varies across boutiques without methodology. Some over-disclose, creating nFADP exposure. Some under-disclose, creating client disclosure risk. The market has not produced a proportionate decision framework for this specific architectural question.

---

### Problem 6: Geneva Accountability Formation Velocity Differential Creating Operative Exposure Before Publication

**Signal:** Geneva boutique MDs discovering that operative accountability expectations have already formed when they inquire about what published guidance requires. The Federal Council AI deliberation, carrier questionnaire iterations, and institutional client accountability requirements create effective standards through informal channels before any Bern-published doctrine exists. A boutique asking "what standard must we meet?" receives a published answer describing something already superseded by what's operative in Geneva.

**Failure mode:** Waiting for published doctrine before building documentation is waiting for a position that has already expired. The boutique building documentation against published doctrine discovers the operative standard is different. The documentation window close means the boutique that built the right documentation before the standard crystallized has a defensible position; the boutique that waited has only explanations for why it did not.

**Market consequence:** The accountability formation velocity differential—informal Geneva mechanisms forming standards faster than Bern formal process—creates a structural timing asymmetry. Boutique MDs who participate in informal Geneva circuits have intelligence unavailable to those who rely on published doctrine. No Geneva consulting-specific intelligence function addresses this differential at boutique scale.

---

## Cross-Signal Pattern: Accountability Without Infrastructure

The six signals above share a structural driver: accountability expectations are forming faster than the infrastructure to meet them. Boutique MDs are personally accountable for AI governance frameworks they did not design, cannot audit, and cannot confirm against published standards. The personal accountability arrives before the methodology that discharges it.

The structural pattern:
- Client questionnaires create implied warranties without delivery practice alignment
- Carrier attestation creates personal signature exposure without coverage confirmation
- AI delivery timestamps document presentation not analytical validity
- Peer silence prevents methodology distribution creating market-wide isolation
- Engagement letter disclosure decision lacks Geneva-specific decision framework
- Accountability formation velocity outpaces formal doctrine publication

Each of these is a discrete named service category that does not yet exist in the Geneva market at boutique scale.
