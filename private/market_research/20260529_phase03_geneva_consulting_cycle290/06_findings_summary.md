# Findings Summary — Phase 03 Geneva Consulting — Cycle 290
# Small Consulting Boutique, 2–50 Employees — Geneva

## Six NET-NEW Findings for C290 Phase 03

---

### Finding 1: Client Counter-Questionnaire Accountability Chain

**Full finding:** A boutique that responds to a client AI governance questionnaire creates an implied warranty against its own response. Every subsequent AI-assisted deliverable is measured against the standards the boutique self-certified in the questionnaire document. The boutique has no mechanism for tracking whether actual delivery practice matches its stated methodology. This is not a disclosure risk — it is a delivery practice alignment problem. The boutique's own response document becomes the benchmark against which its own performance is measured.

**ICP language:** "We answered their AI questionnaire. Now every time we deliver something, they might be measuring it against what we wrote."

**Service implication:** Pre-questionnaire response alignment methodology. Not a template — a methodology for evaluating what the boutique can actually commit to before it commits. The delivery practice must be aligned to the questionnaire response before submission, not after.

---

### Finding 2: Carrier PI Insurance Coverage Completeness Problem

**Full finding:** The MD signs a carrier AI attestation committing to AI governance controls. The PI policy predates the attestation form and has no explicit AI coverage language. Two structurally separate questions — attestation adequacy and insurance coverage — are answered as one by the carrier and broker. No Geneva carrier has published AI professional liability coverage position. No Geneva broker has issued Geneva-calibrated AI professional liability gap analysis. The personal signature is on a document whose coverage the firm cannot confirm.

**ICP language:** "I signed the carrier form. But I've never actually seen a policy that says AI is covered. Nobody can tell me in writing."

**Service implication:** Pre-incident personal exposure mapping against existing PI policy coverage. Named, documented exercise identifying what the attestation commits personally, what the existing policy covers, and what gap remains uninsured. Proportionate to boutique scale. Not a legal opinion — a structured pre-incident analysis.

---

### Finding 3: AI Deliverable Timestamp Integrity

**Full finding:** AI model continuous updates propagate silently into active client deliverables. The deliverable timestamp documents presentation date, not analytical framework validity under mandate conditions. Art.398 CO requires analysis reflecting mandate-period conditions. The boutique cannot confirm this property without version tracking it does not have. Sophisticated clients or carriers asking "what model version produced this analysis under what conditions?" cannot be answered. This is a present-tense structural evidentiary gap for every AI-assisted deliverable in active client use.

**ICP language:** "The recommendation has a date on it. But nobody can tell you what the model was thinking on that date versus what it's thinking now."

**Service implication:** Deliverable version state documentation architecture proportionate to boutique mandate book. Not an enterprise IT solution — a named methodology for tracking AI model version state across active client engagements.

---

### Finding 4: Peer Circuit Silence as Accountability Embarrassment Signal

**Full finding:** Peer silence on concrete AI governance methodology across the Geneva consulting circuit is structurally interpreted as embarrassment, not information gap. No peer has a methodology they are willing to discuss publicly. The first boutique to publicly discuss AI governance methodology is visible wrong. This structural condition prevents peer methodology exchange, creating market-wide isolation-as-default. The boutique that initiates structured AI governance dialogue ahead of peers captures asymmetric competitive advantage — demonstrating methodology most competitors cannot match because they have none.

**ICP language:** "I've asked three peers. Nobody really has an answer. Which means nobody is comfortable talking about it."

**Service implication:** Peer circuit AI governance methodology dialogue — as positioning move. The boutique that documents and discusses its AI governance methodology in peer contexts before others do so captures the first-mover advantage in a market where everyone is isolated by the same condition.

---

### Finding 5: Engagement Letter AI Disclosure Obligation Paradox

**Full finding:** Engagement letter AI methodology disclosure creates nFADP Article 9 processor processing obligations toward the client. The engagement letter documents the processing. If the legal basis is not established, the engagement letter is evidence of unbased processing. Non-disclosure preserves privilege but creates client disclosure risk. The disclosure decision determines which accountability regime governs the boutique-client AI relationship. No Geneva protocol maps this trade-off at boutique scale.

**ICP language:** "If I write in the engagement letter that we use AI, I think that creates some kind of data processing obligation I'm not sure I've satisfied. But if I don't write it, the client might claim they didn't know."

**Service implication:** Engagement letter AI disclosure decision framework. Named mapping of what disclosure language creates what obligations, what non-disclosure creates what exposure, and what proportionate architecture exists at boutique scale.

---

### Finding 6: Geneva Accountability Formation Velocity Displacement

**Full finding:** Operative accountability standards form through Geneva informal channels before Bern formal doctrine arrives. Carrier questionnaire iterations, institutional client requirements, and Geneva institutional exchanges create effective standards through sustained informal participation. The boutique relying on published doctrine discovers it is describing superseded operative conditions. The documentation window before standard crystallizes is structurally different from documentation after. What is built before the standard crystallizes determines evidentiary position. What is built after can only describe what arrived. No Geneva consulting-specific intelligence function addresses the velocity differential at boutique scale.

**ICP language:** "By the time anything gets published, the decision has already been made somewhere. The question is whether you were in the room."

**Service implication:** Geneva accountability formation velocity intelligence. Tracking which accountability signals are crystallizing through sustained informal participation — not monitoring published doctrine but monitoring the formation process itself.
