### F1: Switzerland Iran Sanctions — Major Expansion Dec 2025 (web sources, April 2026)
- Swiss Federal Council decision 12 December 2025: significantly expanded Switzerland's Iran sanctions
- Restored regime to pre-2016 levels following EU "snapback" of JCPOA (triggered by E3 — France, Germany, UK — on 28 August 2025 after failed UN negotiations to extend JCPOA framework)
- UN sanctions reinstated as mandatory for Switzerland on 20 October 2025 (listings adopted)
- Key new measures:
  - Dual-use goods restrictions (Annex 1 items — prohibitions; Annex 2 items — prior authorization required)
  - Unmanned aerial vehicles/rockets technology (Annex 3 — prohibitions)
  - Financial sector: prohibition on opening accounts with Iranian banks, correspondent banking relationships, branches in Iran
  - Transfers >CHF 10,000 must be reported to SECO; >CHF 50,000 require prior SECO authorization
  - Oil/petroleum products from Iran: prohibition (with narrow exceptions for contracts predating 12 Dec 2025, completed by 13 March 2026)
  - Raw materials: new restrictions to prevent EU sanctions circumvention
- Swiss Humanitarian Trade Arrangement (SHTA) remains operational, overseen by SECO
- Enforcement: penalties up to CHF 540,000 fine or 5 years imprisonment (intentional); CHF 100,000 (negligent)
- Geneva law firms active in this space: Schellenberg Wittmer (established sanctions group, regularly liaises with authorities), Dentons, Baker McKenzie (2 partners in Geneva, 6 in Zurich)
- Baker McKenzie: "most leading Swiss and international multinationals" as clients across all sectors, including investigations
- Dentons: supported European Commission's Due Diligence Helpdesk for Iranian SMEs
- Legal demand: compliance reviews, internal investigations, cross-border transaction advisory, governmental investigations and enforcement actions, litigation and arbitration
- Circumvention is a primary enforcement focus — Swiss companies operating internationally need robust due diligence

### F4: SECO Enforcement Infrastructure
- SECO maintains searchable sanctions list and online tool
- Newsletter service for updates (SECO + FINMA)
- Systematic Compilation of Federal Legislation publishes sanctions lists
- Switzerland has implemented all relevant UN sanctions

### F5: EU–Switzerland Sanctions Divergence Risk

- Circumvention prevention is stated goal of December 2025 expansion
- Companies with EU + Swiss exposure need coordinated compliance advice
- Legal angle: advisory on dual-regime compliance, licensing differences between CH and EU
