# Phase 01 C47 — Decision Framework: Shadow AI Audit

## 6-Decision Framework for Small Law Firms

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Inventory
**"What AI tools are your lawyers already using — with or without firm knowledge?"**
- Individual GenAI use: 71% (likely higher in GenAI-savvy Geneva)
- 43% of firms have NO formal AI policy
- Most common: Claude/GPT for legal research, contract drafting, client communications
- Data residency risk: most US-based AI tools = CLOUD Act exposure
- Entry point: anonymous staff survey + technical audit of network logs (if available)

### Decision 1: FADP Applicability Assessment
**"Which of your AI use cases process personal data under FADP?"**
- FADP applies to all AI processing personal data (operative since Sep 1, 2023)
- Key obligations: Art. 5(c) lawfulness + Art. 21 human review + Art. 16 DPIA
- No new law needed — obligations already live

### Decision 2: Tool Governance
**"Which tools meet Swiss data residency requirements?"**
- FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL: Swiss-hosted, FADP-compliant, SMB-accessible
- CASUS: Swiss legal AI, zero data retention, CH/EU hosting
- Swiss-Noxtua: Swiss data centers, FADP-compliant
- DeepLaw (deepcloud.swiss): Swiss law research, ISO 27001:2022, FADP/GDPR compliant
- US tools (ChatGPT, Claude API): CLOUD Act risk for confidential client data

### Decision 3: Client Communication Policy
**"Are your clients told when AI assists with their matter?"**
- FADP transparency obligation: right to know if AI is used in processing
- Art. 21: right to human review of automated decisions
- Proactive disclosure = trust building + risk mitigation

### Decision 4: DPIA Requirements
**"Does your AI use require a formal DPIA?"**
- DPIA required for high-risk AI (contract analysis, due diligence, legal opinions)
- Low-risk: AI-assisted drafting without personal data processing
- Threshold: if it affects client rights or produces legal effects = DPIA warranted

### Decision 5: EU AI Act Exposure
**"Do you serve EU clients with AI-assisted work?"**
- EU AI Act: extraterritorial for AI systems placed on EU market
- High-risk AI systems: compliance required by August 2, 2026
- Contract review, due diligence, legal advice = likely high-risk under Annex III
- If EU nexus: dual compliance (FADP + EU AI Act)

### Decision 6: Formal Policy Documentation
**"When your bar association, clients, or FDPIC asks — can you show your AI governance?"**
- Minimum: acceptable use policy + DPIA records + vendor assessment
- Best practice: AI governance committee + annual review + staff training log
- Not optional if FADP applies (it does)

---
Decision 0 is primary entry point. Most small firms discover they are already at Decision 3+ without realizing it.
