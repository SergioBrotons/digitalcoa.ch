## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 4)

### EN
**Swiss Lump-Sum Tax Regime — Under Continued International Scrutiny**

The Swiss lump-sum taxation regime (Article 14a DBG / SRITA) allows non-Swiss resident individuals to pay a fixed annual tax rather than on worldwide income, provided they meet conditions:
- CHF 400,000+ annual tax payment threshold
- Not engaged in Swiss gainful employment
- Geneva canton offering favorable lump-sum frameworks

International context:
- EU has long criticized the regime as preferential treatment; Swiss- EU institutional agreement negotiations affected by this issue
- OECD BEPS framework: pressure to align with substance-over-form requirements
- Switzerland has made adjustments over the years to address concerns while maintaining competitiveness

2024-2025 status: Regime still operative but under continued review. Geneva actively promoting the regime's legitimacy through substance requirements. Clients increasingly sophisticated — proper real estate holdings, family presence, cantonal substance documentation required to withstand challenges.

Client advisory considerations:
- Proper documentation of Swiss living costs and global income for lump-sum calculation
- Estate planning integration (Geneva succession law + lump-sum exit planning)
- Interaction with tax treaties (ordinary taxation vs. lump-sum election)
- Cross-border family office structuring for UHNW clients

Advisory opportunity: Geneva private banks and wealth managers offering lump-sum regime migration advisory for clients relocating from high-tax jurisdictions — France, Germany, Italy specifically targeted

### FR
**Régime d'imposition forfaitaire suisse — sous surveillance internationale continue**

Le régime suisse de taxation forfaitaire (art. 14a AHV/IFF) permet aux résidents non-suisses de payer un impôt fixe annuel :
- Seuil CHF 400 000+ de paiement fiscal annuel
- Non engagé dans une activité lucrative en Suisse
- Canton de Genève offrant des cadres forfaitaires favorables

Contexte international :
- L'UE critique depuis longtemps le régime comme traitement préférentiel
- Cadre BEPS OCDE : pression pour aligner sur les exigences de substance
- La Suisse a apporté des ajustements pour répondre aux préoccupations tout en maintenant la compétitivité

Opportunités de conseil : Migration vers le régime forfaitaire pour clients provenant de juridictions à fiscalité élevée (France, Allemagne, Italie)
