## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 3)

### EN
**Swiss ESG Labeling — Anti-Israel Screening Creating Regulatory and Litigation Risk**

The Swiss ESG fund labeling environment (Swiss Fund and Asset Management Association labels, UN PRI framework) has been complicated by:
- Pro-Palestinian activism within Swiss asset management firms pressing for anti-Israel exclusion screens
- Multiple Geneva-based managers implementing or considering negative screening based on Israeli involvement
- Associated risks: discrimination claims, reputational damage, potential breach of fiduciary duty if investment decisions driven by political rather than financial factors
- FINMA and relevant self-regulatory bodies reviewing fiduciary duty boundaries in ESG screening

Key legal exposure:
- Swiss Anti-Discrimination Law (Art. 3 Para 2 OR / employment law) — discrimination against Israeli companies or individuals in investment decisions creates potential liability
- UN Resolution 1701 / international sanctions compliance — humanitarian law considerations
- Institutional investor fiduciary duty: mixing political screens with financial analysis may breach duty to maximize returns
- Anti-boycott regulations: Swiss companies cannot refuse to do business with countries based on political boycotts not sanctioned by Swiss government

Advisory opportunities:
- Geneva ESG fund governance reviews (legally defensible screening criteria)
- Fiduciary duty compliance audits for asset managers
- Board-level training on ESG screening legal boundaries

### FR
**Labels ESG suisses — controverse sur le boycott anti-israélien**

L'environnement des labels ESG suisses compliqué par :
- Activisme pro-palestinien au sein des gestionnaires d'actifs genevois — pression pour des exclusions anti-Israël
- Gestionnaires genevois mettant en œuvre ou envisageant des filtres négatifs basés sur l'implication en Israël
- Risques associés : réclamations pour discrimination, atteinte à la réputation, violation potentielle du devoir fiduciaire
- FINMA et autorités de régulation examinant les limites du devoir fiduciaire dans le filtrage ESG

Exposition juridique clé :
- Loi suisse anti-discrimination
- Résolution 1701 NU / conformité aux sanctions internationales
- Devoir fiduciaire des investisseurs institutionnels : mélange de critères politiques et financiers peut constituer une violation
- Réglementation anti-boycott : entreprises suisses ne peuvent refuser de faire affaires avec des pays selon des boycotts non approuvés par le gouvernement suisse
