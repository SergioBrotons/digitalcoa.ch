## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Basel IV / FAIR Implementation — Capital Requirements Recalibration
- Switzerland implementing Basel IV through FAIR framework revision; 72.5% RWA floor confirmed
- Large Swiss banks (G-SIBs): higher CET1 requirements, systemic risk buffers
- FINMA circular revision in progress; implementation likely 2026-2027
- Geneva banks: capital planning reassessment required; RWA optimization strategies in demand; operational burden from enhanced reporting

### Finding 02: BVG Reform — Largest Pension Restructuring in Decades
- BVG/OPP2 reform in active legislative process; conversion rates under pressure
- Means-tested supplements replacing current point system for low-income retirees
- Higher coordination deduction → lower insured salary for lower-wage Geneva workers
- Near-retiree transition measures (born 1966-1971) controversial
- AHV/BVG coordination with AVS 21 (2024) creating multi-layer change for Geneva HR departments
- Advisory: employer transition planning, pension fund governance, asset reallocation strategy

### Finding 03: Swiss ESG Labeling — Anti-Israel Screening Creating Legal Exposure
- Pro-Palestinian activism pressing Geneva managers toward anti-Israel exclusion screens
- Key legal risks: discrimination claims (Art. 3 Para 2 OR), fiduciary duty breach, anti-boycott law violations
- FINMA reviewing fiduciary duty boundaries in ESG screening decisions
- Legally defensible ESG screening criteria = new advisory niche
- Anti-boycott regulations: Swiss companies cannot refuse business with countries based on unsanctioned boycotts

### Finding 04: Swiss Lump-Sum Tax — International Pressure + UHNW Advisory Opportunity
- Article 14a DBG/SRITA still operative: CHF 400k+ annual tax payment threshold
- EU/BEPS pressure ongoing; Switzerland maintaining through substance requirements
- Geneva promoting regime legitimacy via documentation standards
- Advisory opportunity: lump-sum regime migration advisory for France/Germany/Italy HNWIs relocating
- Estate planning integration and cross-border family office structuring in demand

### Finding 05: Swiss Sanctions Enforcement — Russia Frozen Assets and Compliance Demand
- SECO administering sanctions lists; significant Russian private wealth frozen in Switzerland
- Geneva private banks: ongoing compliance burden; legal uncertainty on custody claims and rehypothecation
- Sanctions evasion risk: non-Russian beneficial owners of Russian-linked structures creating screening complexity
- License applications to SECO for humanitarian exemptions growing
- Frozen asset disposal/restructuring frameworks not yet developed — future political resolution question
- Commodity traders and private banks need sanctions compliance programs

### Finding 06: SNB Wholesale CBDC — Project Helvetia, No Retail E-Franc Decision
- Project Helvetia (SNB + BIS + SIX): wholesale CBDC pilot for tokenized asset settlement — Phase 3 complete
- Focus: wholesale/interbank digital CHF only — retail e-franc not approved, disintermediation concerns dominant
- SIX Digital Exchange (SDX) integration with wholesale digital CHF: real-time T+0 potential
- Geneva as settlement hub: potential strengthening with wholesale CBDC infrastructure
- Geneva asset managers with tokenized products: direct settlement rail access
- Cross-border wholesale CBDC: Project mBridge (China/Thailand/UAE/HK) — Switzerland not yet participant
