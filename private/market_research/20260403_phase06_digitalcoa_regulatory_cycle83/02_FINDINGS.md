## NET-NEW Findings (vs C82 Phase 06 DigitalCoa Regulatory)

### 1. EDÖB AI and Data Protection Guidance — September 24, 2025 (NET-NEW detail)
**Source:** https://www.edoeb.admin.ch/de/ki-und-datenschutz
**Date:** 24 September 2025 (original guidance November 9, 2023; reinforced May 8, 2025 and September 24, 2025)
**Summary:**
- Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (DSG) is technology-neutral and directly applicable to AI-based data processing — no new AI-specific law needed
- AI system manufacturers, providers, and users must ensure high levels of informational self-determination and privacy from the design stage
- **Transparency obligations**: Users of AI chatbots must be informed they're talking to a machine; must disclose if data is used for model training; right to object to automated individual decisions
- **Fundamental rights protection**: High-risk AI requires a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DSFA/privacy impact assessment)
- **Prohibited applications**: AI-based data processing that undermines privacy rights, including real-time facial recognition in public spaces, comprehensive social surveillance and scoring — consistent with authoritarian-state practices
- **X (Twitter) precedent**: Users can object to use of public posts for AI training (Grok case concluded March 20, 2025)

## Swiss Regulatory Positions Summary (for DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Use)

### Data Protection / AI
- **Position**: DSG directly applicable to AI (no separate AI law); EDÖB is enforcing
- **Key obligations**: Transparency (chatbot disclosure), Impact Assessment (high-risk), Right to object (automated decisions), prohibition of surveillance/social scoring
- **Implication**: Organizations using AI must disclose AI use, conduct impact assessments, allow opt-out

### Financial Sector AI
- **Position**: FINMA no dedicated AI circular found; FinIA/FinSA general framework applies
- **Key obligation**: Supervised institutions expected to be "proactive and forward-looking" per FINMA guidance framework
- **Implication**: Financial sector AI governance largely self-directed under existing supervisory principles

### Fintech / Digital Finance
- **Position**: SIF frames Switzerland as "Fintech-Nation" — education, openness, innovation + reliable institutions
- **Key message**: Switzerland's competitive advantage is regulatory reliability, not regulatory sprint
- **Implication**: Swiss AI/fintech companies face a stable rather than rapidly-changing regulatory environment

## Bilingual Summary / résumé bilingue

### EN
Switzerland's EDÖB has issued its most comprehensive AI and data protection guidance to date (September 24, 2025), making the revised DSG directly applicable to AI systems without need for separate legislation. Key obligations: chatbot transparency disclosure, data protection impact assessments for high-risk AI, and prohibition of surveillance/social scoring applications. FINMA has issued no dedicated AI circular, leaving Swiss financial institutions to navigate AI governance under general supervisory principles — creating both flexibility and uncertainty. SIF continues to position Switzerland as "Fintech-Nation" built on stability and openness rather than rapid regulatory change.

### FR
La Suisse a publié ses lignes directrices les plus complètes sur l'IA et la protection des données (24 septembre 2025), rendant la LDS directement applicable aux systèmes d'IA. Obligations clés : transparence des chatbots, évaluations d'impact pour l'IA à haut risque, interdiction des applications de surveillance. La FINMA n'a publié aucune circulaire dédiée à l'IA, laissant les institutions financières suisses naviguer sous les principes généraux de surveillance. Le SIF positionne la Suisse comme « Fintech-Nation » fondée sur la stabilité et l'ouverture.
