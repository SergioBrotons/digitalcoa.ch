## Domain Knowledge Synthesis

### Private Damages in Switzerland
- **Cartel Act Art. 12a (2022 revision):** Switzerland modernized private enforcement of competition law. Follow-on damages actions now clearly admissible after WEKO (Swiss Competition Commission) infringement decisions. Prior to 2022, follow-on actions faced procedural uncertainty.
- **Injunction rights:** Any party that has suffered loss can bring damages action before cantonal commercial courts (Geneva Commercial Court is a preferred forum for complex cartel damages cases).
- **Quantification:** Full liability for actual loss + interest; contributory negligence can reduce recovery. In theory, passing-on defense is available but practically limited.
- **Limitation period:** 5-year limitation from date when plaintiff knew or reasonably could have known of damage, perpetrator identity, and anti-competitive nature. Absolute 10-year ceiling from infringement end.
- **WEKO leniency program:** Companies self-reporting cartel participation receive immunity or reduced fines — leniency markers. Follow-on civil litigation exposure remains but leniency applicants gain priority.
- **Cartel insurance:** A growing but niche market — Geneva insurers offering D&O and business interruption coverage for cartel-related investigation costs and damages.

### Geneva as Damages Forum
- Geneva Commercial Court (Tribunal de première instance, 1ère chambre civile) has developed expertise in complex competition damages — English-language proceedings available, internationally recognized judges.
- Cross-border dimension: EU cartel victims increasingly suing Swiss defendants in Geneva where Swiss subsidiary was involved in EU-wide cartel (e.g., automotive parts, financial benchmarks, freight forwarding).
