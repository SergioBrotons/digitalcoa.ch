## Web Research + Domain Knowledge Synthesis

### Regulatory Framework (C101 Fresh: CSRD convergence, CIA 2025)
- **Climate Reporting Ordinance (effective Jan 2024):** Large public interest companies (≥500 employees) must base climate disclosures on TCFD recommendations, including transition plan aligned with Switzerland's 2050 net-zero target.
- **Climate and Innovation Act (CIA, effective Jan 2025):** Switzerland's national net-zero framework by 2050 — creates mandatory intermediate targets, strengthens basis for climate-related disclosures.
- **CSRD convergence (proposed, consultation closed March 2025):** Proposed amendment to Art. 964a et seq. Swiss Code of Obligations would align Swiss non-financial reporting more closely with EU CSRD — broader scope (smaller companies), mandatory external audit of sustainability disclosures, European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS) reference.
- **SIX DCG (Directive on Corporate Governance, rev. 2023):** "Comply or explain" principle for governance disclosures; most recently revised Jan 2023.
- **SIX Directive on Management Transactions (amended Feb 2024):** Mandatory disclosure of transactions in company securities by board members and executive management.

### Shareholder Activism (C101 Fresh: activist campaign surge)
- **Activist campaigns:** Nearly 20 publicly known activist campaigns launched at Swiss companies in 2024, including large-cap targets — significant increase from prior years.
- **2023 company law reform effect:** Lowered thresholds for shareholders to bring forward agenda items and proposals — emboldening activist investors.
- **Ethos Foundation and institutional investor engagement:** Geneva-based responsible investment foundation actively uses voting powers to challenge executive pay, board elections, and sustainability disclosure quality.
- **ISS/Glass Lewis influence:** Swiss listed companies heavily influenced by proxy advisory firms — governance recommendations carry significant voting outcome risk.
