## Domain Knowledge Synthesis

### Swiss M&A Transaction Landscape
- Switzerland (particularly Geneva/Zurich) is a significant M&A hub for mid-market and large-cap cross-border transactions — pharmaceuticals, commodities, financial services, luxury goods sectors dominant.
- Post-2020 trend: increased use of locked-box pricing mechanisms over traditional closing accounts mechanism — reduces post-closing disputes but creates different risk allocation.
- Material Adverse Change (MAC) clauses: Swiss law and practice less developed than Delaware law; Geneva courts and arbitral tribunals apply Swiss contract law principles with increasing reference to international standards.

### Post-Closing Dispute Categories
1. **Earn-out disputes:** Common in Swiss biotech/pharma acquisitions. Valuation methodology disagreements, milestone achievement disputes (regulatory approval delays), accounting interpretation. Geneva courts and arbitral tribunals frequently seat these.
2. **Fundamental representations breach:** Swiss law (CO Art. 211) — buyer must notify seller of defects promptly; specific warranty claim procedures apply. International transactions typically use SPA arbitration (SICC, DIS, Swiss Arbitration Centre).
3. **MAC/MAE claims:** Rarely succeed under Swiss law — courts apply high threshold for material adverse change; COVID-19 era MAC claims generally failed in Swiss-seated arbitrations.
4. **Warranty & indemnity (W&I) insurance:** Growing market in Swiss M&A — Geneva brokers and London-market W&I insurers active; claims frequency increasing as policy wordings are tested.
5. **Representations & Warranties insurance claims:** Post-closing claims against sellers with W&I coverage — disputed claims handling becoming a specialized area.
6. **Antitrust clearance conditions:** Swiss merger control (Cartel Act Art. 10) — SECO clearance conditions increasingly subject to dispute where parties disagree on remedy scope.

### Geneva Arbitration Advantages
- Swiss Arbitration Centre (in force July 2025: trust/estate/foundation supplemental rules)
- English language proceedings available
- Arbitral awards enforceable in 170+ New York Convention states
- Interim measures available through Swiss courts (Art. 183 lex arbitri)
