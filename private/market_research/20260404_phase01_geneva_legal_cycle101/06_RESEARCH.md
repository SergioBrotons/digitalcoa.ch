## Domain Knowledge Synthesis

### Pay Equity and Transparency
- **Gender Pay Equity (Art. 416 OR):** Swiss employers with ≥100 employees must conduct internal pay equity analysis every 4 years (first deadline: June 2024 for many companies). Results must be communicated to employees. Government analysis of aggregated results published; individual company results are confidential.
- **Federal Act on Gender Equality (GEA) reform (2024):** Strengthened equal pay provisions; burden of proof shifted to employer in equal pay cases; increased penalties for discrimination.
- **EU Pay Transparency Directive (2023):** Not directly applicable to Switzerland but Swiss companies with EU subsidiaries or EU-facing operations face EU directive compliance — creating parallel Swiss/EU compliance burden for Geneva multinationals.

### Human Rights Due Diligence — mHRDD
- **Swiss Responsible Business Initiative (RBI) / counter-proposal (in force 2025):** Swiss companies with ≥1,000 employees globally must conduct human rights and environmental due diligence, report on it, and establish complaints mechanism. Applies to Swiss parent companies and foreign companies with Swiss presence.
- **Geneva impact:** Geneva-based multinationals (commodity traders, commodity finance, financial institutions with global supply chains) face significant mHRDD compliance burden.
- **Cross-border dimension:** Swiss mHRDD overlaps with EU CSDDD (Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive) — Swiss companies with EU operations face dual compliance requirements.
- **Supply chain mapping:** Due diligence extends to suppliers in high-risk sectors (raw materials, agriculture, manufacturing) — commodity traders and trading houses particularly exposed.

### Platform Work — EU Directive + Swiss Response
- **EU Platform Work Directive (Dec 2024, transposition ongoing):** Algorithmic management transparency, worker reclassification presumption — applies to Swiss platforms serving EU market.
- **Swiss platform economy:** Multiple Geneva-based and Swiss platforms (jobs, delivery, transport) affected; reclassification risk for gig economy workers.
- **Status of workers reform:** Ongoing political debate in Switzerland on worker vs. self-employed status — no legislation passed yet but pressure increasing.

### Geneva Employment Law Specifics
- Geneva cantonal employment law adds layer for Geneva-based employers — cantonal scale allowances, frontalier-specific rules (L-permit implications of remote work), Geneva collective bargaining agreements (CCT) in sectors like construction, hospitality, security.
- OR Art. 328 occupational health and safety — extends to home workstations (remote work pandemic legacy).
