# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal: Market Landscape
## Cycle 69 | 2026-04-02 | Geneva Legal AI Governance

## Regulatory Actors Present in Geneva
- FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner) — AI/FADP enforcement lead
- FDJP (Federal Department of Justice and Police) — CoE AI Convention implementation lead
- SECO (State Secretariat for Economic Affairs) — Section 301 U.S. trade policy response
- UVEK (Federal Department of Environment, Transport, Energy and Communications) — Section 301 primary source
- Federal Council — overall Swiss government position

## Geneva-Specific Market Characteristics
- International Geneva: cross-border legal work, diplomatic community, NGOs
- Law firms: mix of global majors (Lenz & Staehelin, Homburger, Niederer Kraft Frey), Swiss majors, Boutiques
- Regulatory density: FADP + EU AI Act extraterritorial + Federal AI Ordinance + CoE AI Convention
- Client base: multinationals, financial institutions, international organizations, family offices

## Market Size Indicators
- Swiss legal market: ~CHF 8B annually
- Geneva as %: approximately 30-35% of Swiss legal market
- AI governance as %: nascent, <2% of legal spend currently
- AI governance addressable (5-year): CHF 80-120M Swiss-wide

## Key Demand Signals
- FADP DPIA requirements for AI systems
- EU AI Act GPAI obligations (in force since August 2, 2025)
- Section 301 written comments deadline April 15, 2026
- August 2, 2026 double deadline (HR AI conformity + legacy GPAI grandfathering expiry)
- CoE AI Convention consultation end-2026

## Competitive Landscape (Legal AI Governance)
- Big 4 legal divisions: established but generic frameworks
- Swiss law firms: slowly developing AI governance practices
- Boutiques: gap — too small for Big 4, too busy for in-house development
- Independent consultants: fragmented, no Geneva-specific positioning
