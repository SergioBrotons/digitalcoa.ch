## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 5)

### EN
**Swiss Family Office Regulatory Framework — Geneva as UHNWI Hub**

Swiss family office landscape — current state 2025-2026:
- Single Family Office (SFO) exemption: under Swiss Financial Institutions Act (FinIA/LFina) Art. 6, an SFO managing only assets of its owners/family members is exempt from FINMA licensing; no public disclosure required; widely used by Geneva UHNWI families
- Multi-Family Office (MFO) licensing: if managing assets for third parties (even family-related), MFO requires FINMA license as asset manager (asset manager of individual clients,AMIC/GP; or fund manager for collective schemes); Geneva MFOs subject to FINMA supervision, AML obligations under AMLO-FINMA
- FINMA MFO supervision: FATF-compliant AML programs, client suitability assessments, best execution, custody organization requirements; regular inspections for larger MFOs
- Geneva UHNWI concentration: Geneva has highest concentration of UHNWI (CHF >CHF 30 million investable assets) in Europe outside London; family office ecosystem supports this (400+ family offices Geneva region per industry estimates)
- UBO disclosure: AMLD6 alignment (cut to 10% threshold) creates documentation burden for Geneva structures with complex ownership; private foundations, family trusts, holding companies all require UBO mapping
- Swiss tax structuring considerations: Swiss federal stamp duty (droit de timbre), cantonal wealth tax optimization, substance requirements for cantonal tax rulings, post-BEPS/Pillar 2 environment
- Structuring vehicles: Geneva foundations (butées en foundation), Liechtenstein Anstalt/Foundation, Luxembourg/SCSP, Delaware trusts — post-BEPS substance requirements critical
- Advisory implications: SFO/MFO structuring, FINMA licensing pathway for MFO, AML program design, UBO remediation (post-AMLD6), cantonal tax ruling applications, substance design for holding structures

### FR
**Cadre réglementaire des family offices suisses — Genève comme hub UHNWI**

État actuel de l'écosystème family office suisse 2025-2026 :
- Exemption Single Family Office (SFO) : sous LFina Art. 6, un SFO gérant uniquement les actifs de ses propriétaires/membres de la famille est exempté de licence FINMA ; pas de disclosure publique requise ; largement utilisé par familles UHNWI genevoises
- Licence Multi-Family Office (MFO) : si gestion d'actifs pour tiers (même familiaux), MFO nécessite licence FINMA comme gérant d'actifs (gérant d'actifs de clients individuels,AMIC/GP) ; MFOs genevois soumis à supervision FINMA, obligations AML sous AMLO-FINMA
- Supervision FINMA MFO : programmes AML conformes FATF, assessments de suitability client, best execution, exigences de custodian ; inspections régulières pour MFOs plus grands
- Concentration UHNWI genevoise : Genève a la plus haute concentration d'UHNWI (CHF >30 millions d'actifs investissables) en Europe hors Londres ; écosystème family office le supporte (400+ family offices région genevoise)
- Disclosure UBO : alignement AMLD6 (seuil à 10%) crée burden documentaire pour structures genevoises avec propriété complexe ; fondations privées, family trusts, sociétés de holding nécessitent mapping UBO
- Considérations de structuration fiscale suisse : droit de timbre fédéral, optimisation impôt sur la fortune cantonal, exigences de substance pour rulings fiscaux cantonaux, environnement post-BEPS/Pillar 2
- Véhicules de structuration : fondations genevoises, Liechtenstein Anstalt/Foundation, Luxembourg/SCSP, Delaware trusts — exigences de substance post-BEPS critiques
- Implications consultatives : structuration SFO/MFO, pathway de licence FINMA pour MFO, conception de programmes AML, remédiation UBO
