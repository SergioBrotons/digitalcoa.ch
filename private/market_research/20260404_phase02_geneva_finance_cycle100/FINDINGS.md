## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Swiss Sustainable Finance Taxonomy — EU Alignment Gap Driving Advisory Demand
- Swiss Green Bond framework active (Federal Council 2021); Swiss Federal Finance Ministry guidelines; federal green bond issuance program
- Swiss taxonomy largely aligned with EU but not equivalent; EU Taxonomy applies to Swiss institutional investors with EU portfolio companies; SFDR disclosure requirements apply to Swiss UCITS/AIFMs marketing to EU investors
- FINMA greenwashing enforcement active 2024-2025; investment funds using "sustainable" without meeting EU/Swiss taxonomy at legal risk
- Geneva private banks (UBS, Pictet, Lombard Odier, Bordier) face EU regulator scrutiny for sustainability claims
- Geneva as impact finance hub (Blue Orchard, responsAbility, Swiss Re GI) — advisory demand for impact measurement, SDG alignment
- Advisory: taxonomy gap analysis (Swiss vs. EU), green bond framework design, SFDR PAI disclosure compliance, ESG fund restructure, greenwashing risk reviews

### Finding 02: Swiss Pension Fund Crypto Allocation — Regulatory Frontier, No Authorization Yet
- BVG current rules require "bankable assets"; digital assets not on FINMA eligible list
- OFAS discussion paper on BVG reform digital asset allocation (2024-2025); parliamentary debate ongoing; no final authorization
- Some cantonal pension funds (BVK, PKZH, PK SBB) conducted internal studies; institutional-grade custody solutions (SEBA Bank) developed
- Geneva GFIs (gestionnaires de fortunes indépendants) advising BVG-linked client foundations face crypto allocation questions
- FINMA sandbox: pension funds exploring structured products (not direct crypto) to stay within current rules
- Advisory: regulatory monitoring, trustee education, crypto-structured product selection, BVG compliance for pension-adjacent portfolios

### Finding 03: RegTech/AI in Swiss Private Banking Compliance — Adoption Accelerating
- Geneva private banks invested heavily in automated KYC platforms; onboarding 2-3 days vs. 2-3 weeks previously
- MONEYVAL 2023 evaluation cited AML compliance gaps; driving urgency for RegTech adoption
- AI-assisted transaction monitoring reduces false positives (industry average: 95% of alerts false positives)
- FINMA Circular 2011/7 (outsourcing) applies to RegTech vendor relationships; AI compliance models must be auditable/explainable
- Swiss RegTech companies: Scanbo (KYC), AdNovum (identity verification), surveil (transaction monitoring)
- FINMA expects governance frameworks for AI compliance tools; no specific AI regulation yet but supervisory scrutiny increasing
- Advisory: RegTech vendor selection and integration, AI compliance governance framework design, AML model validation, FINMA examination preparation

### Finding 04: Geneva Real Estate Finance — Mortgage Refinancing Risk and Commercial Stress
- Swiss mortgage market ~CHF 1.4 trillion; Geneva ~20% of Swiss residential volume; median Geneva apartment ~CHF 12,000-15,000/m²
- SARON-based reference rates rose from negative; 10-year fixed mortgages now 2.5-3.5%; refinancing risk acute for 2019-2021 fixed-rate holders
- FINMA stress test at 5%; amortization requirements (15 years to age 65); LTV limits (20% equity, 15% new build)
- Buy-to-let: Geneva rental yield 2.5-3.5% gross; challenged by rising interest costs
- Geneva office vacancy elevated post-COVID; prime office rents softening; commercial mortgage restructuring demand rising
- BCGE (Banque cantonale de Genève) key mortgage provider; cantonal AIMP affordable housing programs
- Advisory: mortgage refinancing strategy, interest rate hedging (caps, swaps), portfolio stress testing, covenant renegotiation, sale-and-leaseback

### Finding 05: Geneva Family Office Regulatory Framework — SFO/MFO Structuring
- SFO exemption: FinIA Art. 6 — managing only owner/family assets exempts from FINMA license; widely used by Geneva UHNWI families
- MFO licensing: managing third-party assets requires FINMA license (AMIC/GP); subject to AML obligations under AMLO-FINMA
- Geneva UHNWI concentration highest in Europe outside London; 400+ family offices in Geneva region
- AMLD6 alignment (10% UBO threshold) creates documentation burden for complex Geneva structures
- Post-BEPS/Pillar 2 substance requirements critical for holding structures (Liechtenstein Anstalt, Luxembourg SCSP, Delaware trusts)
- Advisory: SFO/MFO structuring, FINMA licensing pathway, AML program design, UBO remediation, cantonal tax ruling applications, substance design

### Finding 06: Geneva Reinsurance Hub — Parametric Insurance and ILS Market Developments
- Geneva: Swiss Re, Munich Re (Geneva branch), Everest Re; 2nd largest reinsurance market globally after Bermuda
- European nat cat losses 2022-2024 driving reinsurance pricing; rates up 15-30% for European nat cat-exposed treaties
- Parametric insurance: Swiss Re and Munich Re developing parametric triggers (wind speed, earthquake magnitude, rainfall thresholds) for climate risk
- Swiss institutional investors (pension funds, cantonal banks) significant ILS investors; cat bonds via Cayman/Ireland SPVs
- Geneva asset managers (Pictet AM, Swiss Re Asset Management) developing ILS fund products for HNWI/UHNWI
- FINMA supervises Swiss reinsurers under ISA; Swiss Solvency Test (SST) capital adequacy requirements tightened post-2022
- Advisory: parametric insurance product design, ILS investment advisory, reinsurance program optimization, corporate insurance placement
