### Finding 02: Geneva Humanitarian AI Adoption Lag — 3-5 Years Behind Private Sector
ICRC, UNHCR, WHO, and major NGOs (MSF, Save the Children) exploring AI for humanitarian response (natural language translation, damage assessment, refugee flow analysis, medical triage) but adoption is 3-5 years behind private sector. Barriers: data protection in conflict zones, humanitarian principles (neutrality, impartiality), lack of internal AI expertise, donor restrictions on technology spending. Advisory opportunity: humanitarian AI strategy frameworks aligned with humanitarian principles; AI tool selection for humanitarian contexts; AI governance for ICRC/UN agencies with unique mandate constraints. This is a genuinely new advisory vertical — not covered by Big Four or Geneva boutiques.

*Different from Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting):* Humanitarian AI is a distinct vertical from general consulting AI strategy. Geneva's IO/NGO ecosystem makes this a natural Phase 04 finding.

Advisory opportunity: Swissmedic regulatory strategy for Geneva healthtech; EU MDR parallel pathway planning; reimbursement strategy (KT錶, basic insurance); regulatory timeline and budget planning. *Different from Phase 03 (Geneva Consulting) Finding 04:* Phase 03 covered healthcare regulatory strategy for Geneva consulting firms. Phase 04 specifically addresses healthtech startups as clients — a distinct buyer segment with distinct needs (Swissmedic approval, not consulting firm strategy).
