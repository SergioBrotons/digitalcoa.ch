# 06 — Executive Summary: Geneva NGO/Tech

## Market Opportunity

Geneva hosts a unique ecosystem: 40+ international organizations (UNOG, UNHCR, WHO, WTO, ITU, ILO), 170+ diplomatic missions, major NGOs (ICRC, MSF, Save the Children, WWF), and a growing tech startup cluster (CERN spin-offs, FONGIT, healthtech). Each segment faces distinct challenges — IOs need digital transformation and AI strategy, NGOs need operational efficiency and impact measurement, and tech startups need regulatory navigation. Yet no Geneva advisor serves this ecosystem with a unified positioning.

This cross-segment positioning opportunity — the advisor who understands Geneva's IO/NGO/tech ecosystem and can serve multiple segments within it — is defensible because: (1) each segment has distinct needs requiring specialist knowledge; (2) the ecosystem's complexity creates barriers to generalist competitors; (3) Brotons.net/DigitalCoa.ch can use thought leadership to own this space.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: IO Procurement is the #1 Barrier to Geneva Boutique Access
UN/IO procurement rules are complex enough to block small Geneva boutiques from accessing a market worth hundreds of millions annually in consulting services. No Geneva boutique has positioned as the specialist advisor helping other boutiques navigate IO procurement. This is a uniquely Geneva niche opportunity.

### Finding 2: Humanitarian AI Adoption is 3-5 Years Behind Private Sector
ICRC, UNHCR, WHO, and major NGOs are exploring AI for humanitarian response, data analysis, and operational efficiency — but lag private sector AI adoption by 3-5 years. The humanitarian AI strategy advisory market is genuinely underserved and growing.

### Finding 3: Geneva HealthTech Regulatory Navigation is Genuinely Underserved
Geneva healthtech startups (medical devices, digital health, diagnostics) face complex Swissmedic approval pathways and EU MDR alignment requirements. Geneva regulatory advisors with healthtech expertise are limited. This is a greenfield advisory opportunity.

### Finding 4: CERN/EPFL Tech Transfer is a Natural Geneva Vertical
CERN spin-offs and EPFL transfer companies create a specific advisory need: IP commercialization, spin-off structuring, international market entry. Geneva's proximity to CERN and EPFL gives it natural advantage over Zurich or international competitors.

### Finding 5: Geneva Tech Startup AI Governance is Emerging Demand
Geneva tech startups deploying AI (fintech, healthtech, sustainability) face AI governance requirements but lack budget for Big Four. A boutique advisor offering startup-appropriate AI governance frameworks is underserved.
