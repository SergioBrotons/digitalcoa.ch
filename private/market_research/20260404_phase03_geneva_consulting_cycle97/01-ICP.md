# 01 — ICP: Geneva Small Consulting Firms

## Target Segment: Small Consulting Firms (2-15 employees)

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** 2-15 employees; typically managing partners + consultants
- **Revenue:** CHF 500K–5M annual
- **Specializations:** Strategy, digital transformation, HR, finance, operations, sustainability
- **Locations:** Geneva city center; hybrid/remote common
- **Client type:** Mid-market companies, startups, SMEs, some institutional

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| Managing Partner / Founder | 40-60 years, experienced | Strategic direction, client acquisition, pricing |
| Senior Consultant | 30-45 years, expert | Time management, deliverable quality, tool efficiency |
| Junior Consultant | 25-35 years, emerging | Learning curve, process adherence |

### Geneva Examples
- Boutique strategy firms (10-30 consultants)
- Digital transformation consultancies
- Sustainability/ESG advisory boutiques
- HR and organizational development firms
- Financial advisory boutiques (wealth, restructuring)

### Pain Points (Updated C97)
1. **Time poverty** — non-billable admin and proposal work consumes 30-40% of available hours
2. **Client data confidentiality** — anxiety about using AI tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) with confidential client information
3. **Competitive pressure** — fear of falling behind larger boutiques with dedicated AI resources
4. **Client expectations** — clients increasingly expect data-driven, AI-augmented deliverables
5. **AI adoption decisions** — which tools, how to deploy, how to govern
6. **NEW — Regulatory exposure** — AMLA revision may require SRO affiliation for firms advising on financial transactions
7. **NEW — nFADP enforcement** — client data handling in consulting engagements under greater scrutiny
8. **NEW — LETA compliance** — corporate consulting engagements may require beneficial ownership identification

### Buying Triggers (Updated C97)
- New client RFP requiring AI methodology description
- nFADP/privacy complaint or near-miss
- AMLA revision creating compliance uncertainty
- Managing partner意识到 AI gap vs. competitors
- New ISO 42001 certification consideration
