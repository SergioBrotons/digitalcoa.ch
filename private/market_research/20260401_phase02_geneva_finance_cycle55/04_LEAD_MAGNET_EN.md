## FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — What It Actually Requires

FINMA issued Guidance 08/2024 on December 18, 2024, setting clear expectations for AI governance in Swiss financial institutions. This guidance is already operative — it does not await further legislation.

Key obligations for wealth managers:

- **AI Risk Classification**: All AI systems must be classified by risk level. High-risk AI (credit decisions, client suitability, portfolio allocation) requires enhanced governance.
- **Human Oversight**: Automated decisions affecting clients require meaningful human review. "Meaningful" means the human can actually intervene — not just theoretically.
- **Model Risk Management**: AI models used in investment decisions must be documented, tested, and regularly validated.
- **Data Governance**: AI systems processing client data must comply with FINMA data governance requirements — including data residency for Swiss client data.
- **Incident Reporting**: AI-related incidents must be reported to FINMA under existing incident reporting frameworks.

**These obligations are already running. There is no transition period.**

## The Agentic AI Liability Layer — New in 2026

2026 marks the structural shift from AI as a tool to AI as an agent. Agentic AI systems can autonomously plan, execute, and adapt to achieve goals — without requiring human input at each step.

For wealth managers, this creates three distinct liability tiers:

| System Type | Autonomy Level | WM Application | Liability |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI Assistant | Low | Portfolio reporting, document drafting | Firm |
| AI Agent | Moderate | Rebalancing triggers, compliance monitoring, client communication | Firm |
| Agentic AI | High | Autonomous portfolio execution, algorithmic trading | Firm |

The legal position is unambiguous:

> "It wasn't me, it was my AI" is not a defense that courts or regulators will accept.

Under current Swiss law, AI systems lack legal capacity. The firm is always liable for decisions made or executed by AI systems — regardless of the degree of autonomy.

**The governance gap is not just about compliance. It's about whether you have structural exposure to a liability event you cannot control.**

## CARF/CRS 2.0 — AI Creates New Reporting Obligations

The Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) and Common Reporting Standard (CRS) Version 2.0 both became operative on January 1, 2026.

For wealth managers using AI in any part of the data collection, processing, or reporting chain, this creates a new compliance surface:

- **AI-assisted data processing for CRS/CARF**: If AI tools are processing client financial data for CRS reporting purposes, they must meet the same data quality and documentation standards as manual processes.
- **Automated reporting pipelines**: AI-driven reporting must be auditable. Regulators must be able to trace how a figure was generated — even if the figure was generated by an AI system.
- **FATF alignment**: The Financial Action Task Force is actively monitoring AI use in financial services. CRS/CARF enforcement is one vector; AML/KYC is another.

**CARF/CRS 2.0 operative January 1, 2026 = compliance urgency right now.**

## The Swiss-Sovereign AI Question

Wealth management client data is among the most sensitive in existence. Client net worth, asset allocation, family circumstances, inheritance intentions — this data cannot leave Switzerland without creating regulatory, fiduciary, and reputational risk.

US hyperscaler AI platforms (ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini) process data on servers outside Switzerland. For wealth management use cases involving client data, this creates:

- **FADP exposure**: Personal data processed outside Switzerland without adequate safeguards may violate FADP data residency expectations
- **FINMA data governance concerns**: FINMA-supervised institutions have heightened obligations around client data security
- **Fiduciary risk**: Using non-Swiss AI platforms for client portfolio data may breach fiduciary duty of care

Swiss-sovereign AI tools (Swiss-Noxtua, FABIAN, CASUS) offer an alternative: models trained on Swiss legal and financial data, operating from Swiss data centers, with zero data retention commitments.

**For wealth management, Swiss-sovereign AI is not a preference. It's a fiduciary requirement.**

## The 30-Point Readiness Checklist

**FINMA Guidance 08/2024 Alignment:**

1. Have you classified all AI systems by risk level?
2. Do you have documentation for every AI system in use?
3. Is there meaningful human review for all automated client decisions?
4. Are AI model validation reports being produced and archived?
5. Do you have a data processing agreement with every AI vendor?
6. Is client data residency maintained for all AI processing?
7. Is your AI incident response protocol documented?
8. Do you have a named AI governance owner at the partner level?

**CARF/CRS 2.0 AI Compliance:**

9. Have you mapped AI use in your CRS/CARF reporting pipeline?
10. Are AI-assisted reporting outputs auditable to regulator standards?
11. Do you have documentation for AI data transformations in reporting?
12. Is FATF AML/KYC AI use mapped and controlled?

**Agentic AI Liability:**

13. Are any AI systems operating at the agentic (autonomous execution) level?
14. Do you have predefined boundaries and kill switches for AI agents?
15. Is your professional liability insurance adequate for AI-assisted decisions?
16. Have you updated client agreements to reflect AI-assisted service delivery?

**Swiss-Sovereign AI:**

17. Are AI tools processing client financial data operating from Swiss data centers?
18. Do you have zero data retention commitments from AI vendors?
19. Has your data protection officer reviewed AI vendor DPAs?
20. Is AI use in portfolio management documented for fiduciary compliance?

**Strategic Positioning:**

21. Do you have an AI adoption roadmap with governance milestones?
22. Is your competitive positioning on AI governance documented?
23. Do you have a client communication strategy on AI use?
24. Are your staff trained on AI governance and professional conduct?
25. Do you have a framework for evaluating AI tool additions?
26. Is your AI governance framework reviewed annually?
27. Do you monitor FDPIC enforcement patterns for relevance to WM?
28. Are AI conflicts of interest identified and managed?
29. Is your AI governance framework consistent with FINMA expectations?
30. Do you have a clear escalation path for AI-related incidents?

**Score 0–10:** Critical gaps. Immediate action required.
**Score 11–20:** Significant gaps. Priority remediation.
**Score 21–25:** Moderate gaps. Governance development needed.
**Score 26–30:** Leading practice. Annual review and refinement.
