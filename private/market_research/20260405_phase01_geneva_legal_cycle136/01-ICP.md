# 01-ICP.md — Geneva Legal Large/ELM Firms

## Geneva Legal — Large Law / Executive Legal Management

### Refined ICP Definition
Swiss private law attorneys (Art. 12 BGFA) and executive legal officers (GCL/GC) at:
- Geneva-headquartered financial institutions (private banks, commodity traders, insurance)
- International law firms with Geneva offices (Lenz & Staehelin, Vischer, Walder Wyss, CMS, Baker McKenzie)
- Family offices and UHNW legal trusts (Art. 11e BGFA)
- International organisations with Geneva legal departments (UNOG, ITU, WIPO, WHO, ICRC)

### Legal Decision-Maker Profile
- **Role:** General Counsel, Head of Legal, Managing Partner, Senior Associate (compensation/structuring)
- **Concern:** Regulatory liability, jurisdictional ambiguity, client data governance, litigation exposure
- **Trigger events:** FINMA enforcement, regulatory change, cross-border dispute, M&A, sanctions escalation
- **Buying behaviour:** Trusted-peer introduction over vendor outreach; evidence over marketing
- **Budget authority:** CHF 500K–5M/year legal spend; project-based for advisory, retainer for trusted advisors
- **Language:** EN-primary for international matters; FR for domestic/regulatory; DE for Federal-level

### What This ICP Does NOT Want
- Generic legal tech vendor pitches
- AI-generated content without legal authority citation
- Surface-level regulatory summaries that miss Swiss-specific nuance
- Conflict-of-interest advisors (Big 4 legal arms, bank-affiliated counsel)

### Strategic Implication
The ICP is reachable primarily through trusted institutional networks (Geneva Bar Association, IHEID alumni, CCIG peer groups). Direct outbound has low conversion. The pathway is Brotons.net content → trusted introduction → advisory engagement.
