## NET-NEW Findings (C75 Phase 02)

### Finding 1: Swiss Stablecoin Regulation — SBA Warns Against "Luxembourg Scenario" (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — SBA newsroom article
- **URL:** https://www.swissbanking.ch/en/media-politics/news/we-do-not-want-a-luxembourg-scenario-for-digital-assets
- **Finding:** The Swiss Bankers Association (SBA/SBVg) is actively consulting on the Federal Council's proposed stablecoin rules. Key dispute: the proposal would require Swiss banks to establish subsidiaries as payment instrument institutions to issue stablecoins. SBA argues this creates unnecessary bureaucracy — banks already comprehensively regulated, should be able to issue stablecoins directly under their existing banking licence. SBA calls for "stress tests" and regulatory impact assessment before expanding stablecoin permissions. The catalyst: US "GENIUS Act" enacted federal stablecoin legislation, creating urgency for Switzerland. German e-money institution AllUnity announced a CHF stablecoin launch, aware of Swiss regulatory work.
- **Legal relevance:** FinIA amendment, stablecoin licensing requirements, bank competitive positioning vs EU, disintermediation risk, client protection under FinSA.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW (C74 noted FINMA institutional but stablecoin was not reported)

### Finding 3: Nature Transition Financing — CHF 5.3 Billion/Year Gap Identified by SBA/BCG (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — SBA newsroom
- **URL:** https://www.swissbanking.ch/en/media-politics/news/financing-options-for-switzerlands-nature-transition
- **Finding:** Swiss Bankers Association commissioned BCG+Quantis study: Switzerland needs CHF 5.3 billion/year in nature financing by 2050 (up from CHF 3.2 billion currently). 85% publicly financed. Incremental private gap: CHF 2.1 billion/year. Largest areas: water infrastructure (hydropower rehabilitation, river revitalisation) and regenerative agriculture. Swiss banks not materially exposed to nature risks due to adaptation infrastructure and insurance coverage. Banks positioned to offer green/sustainability-linked loans, sustainable supply chain financing. Nature = emerging ESG product line.
- **Legal relevance:** Sustainable finance regulation, nature-related disclosures, green bond standards, ESG advisory compliance.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW (nature transition is distinct from climate/carbon — new angle)

### Finding 4: Swiss Stablecoin Ecosystem — AllUnity CHF Stablecoin Announced, Competitive Pressure (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch (referenced in stablecoin article)
- **Finding:** German regulated e-money institution AllUnity announced the launch of a Swiss-franc stablecoin. This is specifically positioned to take advantage of Swiss regulatory work. Signals that foreign stablecoin issuers see Swiss regulatory framework as close to enabling and are moving to capture CHF stablecoin market before Swiss banks themselves can.
- **Legal relevance:** First-mover advantage for non-Swiss issuers, competitive threat to Swiss banking stablecoin business.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW

### Finding 5: SBA Stablecoin Stress Tests Call — Financial Stability Risk Assessment Demanded (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** swissbanking.ch — SBA consultation response (same article as Finding 1)
- **Finding:** SBA calls for robust regulatory impact assessment with "stress tests" before expanding stablecoin permissions. Specific concerns: genuine value stability of stablecoins, safeguarding financial market stability, mitigating disintermediation risk (money flowing from bank balance sheets to payment instrument institutions). If large volumes of customer funds collect in non-bank stablecoin issuers, credit financing and crisis resilience of banking system could be affected. For CHF stablecoins specifically, disintermediation risk exists regardless of country of issuance.
- **Legal relevance:** FINMA regulatory impact assessment methodology, stablecoin stress testing frameworks, systemic risk monitoring.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW
