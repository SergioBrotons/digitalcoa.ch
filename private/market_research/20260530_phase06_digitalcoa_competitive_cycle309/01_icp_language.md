## 1. Market Language Overview

The Geneva SMB market's language around AI accountability has evolved from Phase 01 (legal) through Phase 05 (cross-vertical) into a distinct vocabulary. The competitive landscape uses its own language — language that reveals positioning, gaps, and opportunities for Sergio.

### The Language Gap

**What competitors say:** "AI governance," "compliance framework," "risk assessment," "policy documentation," "AI ethics," "digital transformation"

**What ICPs actually need:** "carrier form," "attestation," "personal liability," "reconstruction," "documentation debt," "supervision evidence"

**The gap:** Competitors speak in solutions; ICPs speak in problems. The market's language is solutions-oriented because competitors built that language. The ICPs' language is problem-oriented because they live with the problems daily.

## 2. Competitor Language Patterns

### Pattern A: Framework Language

**Typical phrasing:**
- "We help you establish an AI governance framework"
- "Comprehensive AI risk assessment"
- "AI policy development and implementation"
- "Governance, risk, and compliance (GRC) for AI"

**What this reveals:** These competitors are selling documents, not evidence systems. "Framework" and "policy" are document terms. ICPs don't need documents — they need evidence packages that survive carrier scrutiny.

### Pattern B: Compliance Language

**Typical phrasing:**
- "nFADP compliance for AI systems"
- "EU AI Act readiness assessment"
- "Regulatory compliance gap analysis"
- "Data protection and AI governance alignment"

**What this reveals:** Compliance language positions AI accountability as a legal problem requiring legal solutions. This is wrong for SMBs — their problem is operational, not legal. The compliance framing leads with regulatory risk, not broker trigger. ICPs respond to broker trigger more immediately than regulatory risk.

### Pattern C: Technology Language

**Typical phrasing:**
- "AI tool integration strategy"
- "Copilot and Gemini enterprise deployment"
- "AI tool audit and monitoring"
- "Automated AI governance tooling"

**What this reveals:** Technology language is designed for IT buyers, not professional principals. Physicians, lawyers, and consultants don't think of themselves as IT buyers. Technology language creates distance from the actual ICP problem.

### Pattern D: Consulting Methodology Language

**Typical phrasing:**
- "Proprietary methodology for AI accountability"
- "Structured approach to evidence production"
- "Phased implementation roadmap"
- "Best practice alignment"

**What this reveals:** Methodology language signals process but not outcome. ICPs want to know what they get, not how it's produced. "Evidence architecture designed to carrier form specifications" is more effective than "our proprietary phased methodology."

### Pattern E: Insurance-Adjacent Language

**Typical phrasing:**
- "Professional liability AI exposure"
- "Carrier attestation compliance"
- "Insurance renewal AI documentation"
- "D&O protection through AI governance"

**What this reveals:** This language is closest to the actual ICP trigger, but it remains generic. "Insurance documentation" is not the same as "carrier form-specific evidence package." Competitors who use insurance-adjacent language are closer to the market need but haven't operationalized it.

## 3. ICP Language Patterns (from Phases 01–05)

### The Form Language

**What ICPs say:**
- "I got a form from my broker — it asks about AI"
- "The carrier is asking me to attest to something"
- "I don't know what they're asking for"
- "I signed it but I don't know if what I said was right"
- "My broker said I need to have something in place"

**What this reveals:** ICPs arrive through the broker channel. They arrive because a form arrived. The language is form-first, not risk-first. Competitors who lead with "AI governance risk" miss this trigger moment entirely.

### The Evidence Language

**What ICPs say:**
- "Do I have enough documentation?"
- "Can I reconstruct what happened?"
- "If they audit me, what do I show them?"
- "I don't have anything written down"
- "We use AI but I can't prove how"

**What this reveals:** ICPs know they have a documentation problem. They don't know it has a name ("evidence architecture") or a solution. The market hasn't given them the language to describe what they already know is missing.

### The Personal Liability Language

**What ICPs say:**
- "Is this personal or is this the firm?"
- "Am I personally on the hook?"
- "My name is on the form — what does that mean?"
- "If something goes wrong, is it me?"
- "Am I exposing myself personally?"

**What this reveals:** The personal liability dimension is the deepest ICP concern. Competitors who frame AI accountability as corporate compliance are answering the wrong question. "For physicians, evidence is personal" — this is not marketing language, it is the actual ICP concern, named precisely.

### The Reconstruction Language

**What ICPs say:**
- "It's been happening for 18 months — can I go back?"
- "I didn't document anything at the time"
- "Is it too late to fix it?"
- "How do I reconstruct what I don't have?"
- "We made decisions with AI before we had any system"

**What this reveals:** Retroactive reconstruction is the primary ICP need and the most under-named capability in the market. No competitor offers retroactive reconstruction as a service category. ICPs don't know this option exists.

## 5. Six NET-NEW ICP Language Findings for Phase 06

### Finding 1: "Attestation" Is the Trigger Word

The market has standardized on "attestation" as the term for carrier AI documentation requirements. ICPs received "attestation forms." Brokers send "attestation requirements." This word is now the primary trigger phrase — it connects the broker channel to the documentation need. No competitor uses "attestation" in their marketing language, but it is the actual trigger word for the ICP.

**Implication:** Use "attestation" explicitly. "Carrier attestation compliance" or "attestation-ready evidence package" connects directly to the ICP's trigger moment.

### Finding 2: "Documentation debt" Is ICP Language, Not Market Language

ICPs use "debt" metaphor naturally: "I have 18 months of documentation debt," "can I work off the debt?" "is it accumulating?" The market doesn't use this language. Competitors use "gap analysis" or "compliance backlog." Neither is as accurate or as emotionally resonant as "documentation debt."

**Implication:** "Documentation debt" is the ICP's own language. Using it in marketing signals that Sergio understands the ICP's situation at the level of lived experience, not abstract consulting frameworks.

### Finding 3: "Reconstruction" Is Unknown to ICPs as a Capability

ICPs do not know that retroactive reconstruction is a service category. They assume their documentation debt is permanent and irreversible. The word "reconstruction" opens a door they didn't know existed.

**Implication:** "Retroactive reconstruction" is not just a feature — it is a market education message. Explaining that documentation debt can be reconstructed is itself valuable to ICPs and is not offered by any competitor.

### Finding 4: "Show me what you have" Is the ICP Test

ICPs have a specific mental model for evaluating AI accountability services: they will ask you to show them evidence from a past decision and evaluate whether you can produce it. This is the "show me what you have" test. Competitors who offer frameworks fail this test because frameworks don't produce past evidence. Sergio passes it because evidence architecture is designed to produce and reconstruct evidence.

**Implication:** The lead magnet should explicitly invite the "show me what you have" test. "If your broker asked you right now to produce evidence — could you do it?" is more effective than abstract positioning.

### Finding 5: "Bilingual" Is a Regulatory Requirement, Not a Feature

ICPs in Geneva know they need French AND English documentation. They don't think of this as a feature — they think of it as a requirement. Competitors who position bilingual as a premium feature are misreading the market. For Geneva SMBs, bilingual is the baseline expectation, not a differentiated service.

**Implication:** Do not position bilingual as a differentiator. Position it as the default delivery mode. "We deliver in French and English because that is how Geneva works."

### Finding 6: "Personal" Is the Most Emotionally Loaded Word

The word "personal" in "personal liability," "personal exposure," "personal attestation," "my name is on the form" — this is the highest-resonance language in the ICP vocabulary. Competitors systematically avoid "personal" because it sounds alarming. But the ICP is already alarmed. The word "personal" validates their concern and signals that Sergio understands what is at stake.

**Implication:** Use "personal" deliberately and early. "For physicians, evidence is personal" is not just the positioning anchor — it is the most accurate description of what the ICP is experiencing.
