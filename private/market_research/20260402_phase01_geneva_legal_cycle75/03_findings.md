### Finding 1: Swiss-EU Stabilisation Package — Major Legal Framework Adopted (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** news.admin.ch — Federal Council press release, March 2026
- **URL:** https://www.news.admin.ch/en/newnsb/HWvQ3sxWQ8hU-xZ4yDnSf
- **Finding:** Federal Council adopted the "Stabilisation and further development of Swiss-EU relations" package on March 20, 2026. Agreements signed March 2, 2026. ### Finding 3: Crans-Montana Fire Disaster (Jan 1, 2026) — Federal Victim Support Framework (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** news.admin.ch — BJ report, Federal Council Feb 25, 2026
- **URL:** https://www.news.admin.ch/de/newnsb/ER9i2eK0k86m
- **Finding:** Federal Council approved comprehensive victim support framework for Jan 1, 2026 Crans-Montana fire disaster: CHF 50,000 solidarity payment per victim (draft law sent to Parliament for emergency Spring session passage), up to CHF 20M federal contribution to out-of-court settlement (roundtable process), CHF 8.5M to cantons for victim assistance. Federal Office of Justice analysis identified systemic gaps: existing victim support system (Opferhilfe, social insurance, liability insurance) designed for individual cases, not mass casualty events. Insurance assets andhaftungssummen insufficient for large-scale events.
- **Legal relevance:** Victim law, mass tort litigation, out-of-court settlement practice in Switzerland. Geneva-adjacent (Valais). Systemic gap in Swiss law = potential regulatory/policy advocacy angle.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW (C74 noted OAG Geneva bomb case; Crans-Montana is separate event)

### Finding 5: Federal Council — Nuclear Weapons Treaty Position Confirmed (NET-NEW)
- **Source:** news.admin.ch — Nuclear Weapons Ban Initiative press release
- **URL:** https://www.news.admin.ch/en/newnsb/pGAnACKbvc8jyUpe-I9xY
- **Finding:** Federal Council reaffirmed rejection of Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (TPNW), recommending popular vote rejection without counter-proposal. Initiative submitted Dec 2025 with 105,674 valid signatures. Key legal reasoning: accession would restrict Swiss foreign policy flexibility, damage security cooperation with key NATO partners, and constitutionally embed a treaty no nuclear-weapon state has joined. Federal Council prefers NPT framework.
- **Legal relevance:** Public international law, disarmament law, popular initiative process, constitutional law. Geneva-based international law academics and practitioners.
- **Cycle:** C75 NET-NEW (C73 noted Nuclear Weapons Ban rejection but not the 2025-2026 popular initiative details)
