# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Healthcare AI Accountability
## Phase 04 C292 — Geneva Independent Practice

---

## What Exists vs. What's Missing

### Existing Market Offerings

**1. FMH Guidance Infrastructure**
- FMH has stated human oversight principle for AI in clinical practice
- No operational endpoint defined for independent practice scale
- No documentation requirement, no review frequency, no competency standard
- De facto operative standard through absence of alternatives

**2. Swissdamed Classification System**
- Medical device classification for AI clinical decision support tools
- Tier 1/Tier 2 classification criteria written for manufacturers, not outpatient physicians
- Compliance pathway inaccessible at independent practice scale
- Creates paralysis rather than resolution

**3. Carrier AI Attestation Instruments**
- Medical malpractice carriers deploying AI attestation questionnaires
- Designed by underwriting teams, not medical liability specialists
- Commit MD to "adequate AI governance controls" without defining adequate
- No pre-signing review service in Geneva market for medical practitioners

**4. Vendor AI Training Programs**
- Feature tutorials, platform walkthroughs
- Do not map to FMH oversight requirements
- Document platform familiarity not clinical AI competency

**5. General nFADP Compliance Resources**
- Data protection officer services designed for larger organizations
- DPIA templates written for manufacturers and tech companies
- Not calibrated to healthcare practice workflow or clinical documentation

### What's Missing (White Space)

**1. Geneva-Calibrated Medical AI Governance Methodology**
**Gap:** No service exists that maps FMH human oversight principle to operational decisions at 2–10 physician practice scale.
**What would fill it:** A methodology that tells the independent physician what Tuesday looks like — what specific documentation to maintain, what review frequency is proportionate, what competency evidence to keep.
**Distinct from:** General AI governance frameworks (designed for other contexts), FMH principle statements (no operational endpoint), carrier questionnaires (underwriting instruments not accountability tools).

**2. Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review Service**
**Gap:** No Geneva market service offers signatory-side review of carrier AI attestation instruments for medical practitioners.
**What would fill it:** A review that evaluates what the MD commits to personally, whether existing coverage responds, where exposure remains uninsured after signing.
**Distinct from:** Insurance broker services (address coverage product selection, not personal liability exposure), legal review (not calibrated to carrier attestation specific risk architecture).

**3. Patient-AI-Content Medical Record Protocol**
**Gap:** No Geneva protocol exists for documenting patient-initiated AI content entering medical records.
**What would fill it:** Documentation architecture for patient-brought AI analysis: how to note it in the record, what attribution looks like, what liability follows, how to manage the patient's expectation about what the physician is endorsing.
**Distinct from:** General medical record documentation standards (don't address AI content attribution), informed consent frameworks (don't address AI-specific disclosure for patient-generated content).

**4. AI-Assisted Second Opinion Documentation Protocol**
**Gap:** No Geneva protocol for documenting AI-assisted second opinion consultation between independent physicians.
**What would fill it:** Documentation architecture for the accountability chain: original physician → consulting colleague → colleague's AI tool → output in original record. What each party's documentation obligation is, what the original physician must verify, what disclosure is required.
**Distinct from:** General second opinion consultation standards (don't address AI attribution), medical record documentation standards (don't address multi-party AI system accountability).

**5. Medical Record AI Authorship Architecture**
**Gap:** No documentation standard for distinguishing physician-authored content from AI-generated content in medical records.
**What would fill it:** A record architecture that allows the physician to document what AI contributed, what the physician's independent judgment was, and what the AI's reasoning process was — at the time of record creation.
**Distinct from:** EHR documentation templates (don't have AI authorship fields), general AI governance documentation (don't address clinical record evidentiary requirements).

**6. Vendor AI Terms Medical Practice Evaluation Service**
**Gap:** No Geneva service offers proportionate evaluation of vendor AI terms against FMH physician accountability obligations.
**What would fill it:** A review that maps vendor contract terms against physician accountability requirements, identifies specific provisions creating exposure, recommends proportionate responses.
**Distinct from:** General legal contract review (not calibrated to FMH accountability architecture), IT vendor evaluation (not calibrated to professional liability implications).

**7. Three-Architecture Consent Integration Service**
**Gap:** No Geneva service addresses the structural gap between treatment consent, nFADP data processing consent, and AI-specific consent disclosure.
**What would fill it:** Consent architecture that handles all three as separate systems with coherent integration — what each consent must contain, when each applies, how they interact.
**Distinct from:** General consent form templates (treat all consent as single category), nFADP consent frameworks (don't address treatment consent integration).

**8. AI Competency Documentation Standard**
**Gap:** No Geneva-calibrated AI competency documentation standard for independent physicians.
**What would fill it:** Documentation requirement defining what the physician must maintain about their own AI training, limitations awareness, clinical oversight competency.
**Distinct from:** Vendor training certificates (don't map to FMH requirements), general competency frameworks (not designed for physician accountability context).

### Competitive Position Assessment

**DigitalCoa.ch positioning opportunity:** The Geneva healthcare AI accountability market has no service provider addressing the independent practice scale with proportionate methodology. The legal and finance verticals already have documented market gaps. The healthcare vertical is structurally identical with additional complexity (clinical documentation, patient record liability, FMH professional obligations).

**The competitive moat:** Geneva French-language healthcare AI governance content in the accountability register — written for the independent physician in the liability language they use — does not exist anywhere in the market. The bilingual content creates trust through linguistic and cultural precision that generic AI governance platforms cannot replicate.

**Service design implication:** A 90-minute briefing format calibrated to the Geneva independent practice, in the liability language the ICP uses, in French and English, is the primary entry product. Carrier pre-renewal review as the immediate conversion trigger. Medical record AI authorship documentation as the highest-need ongoing service.
