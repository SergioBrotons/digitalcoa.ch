# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

## Phase 03 | C296 | Geneva Consulting ICP

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Big Four and Large Consultancies (KPMG, PwC, EY, Deloitte)
**What they offer:**
- ISO42001 certification readiness
- EU AI Act compliance frameworks
- Maturity assessments for AI governance
- Attestation services for large financial institutions
- Comprehensive AI management system implementations

**Gap for Geneva boutiques:**
- Target large enterprises, not 2–50 person firms
- Engagement models require significant budget and timeline
- Deliverables are corporate-grade frameworks, not boutique-ready documentation
- Not accessible to the day-to-day operational reality of a small Geneva consultancy
- No specific focus on personal liability of the managing director

**Price reality:** An ISO 42001 certification readiness engagement from KPMG or PwC runs into six figures. Out of reach for a 10-person Geneva boutique.

---

### Specialized AI Governance Institutes (GAIGI, Swiss Cyber Institute)
**What they offer:**
- AI Governance 360 frameworks
- EU AI Act alignment
- NIST AI RMF alignment
- Executive training
- Research and engineering focus

**Gap for Geneva boutiques:**
- Primarily positioned for larger institutions and banks
- GAIGI is research-oriented, not boutique-advisory oriented
- No specific focus on consulting methodology defensibility
- No sub-consultant delegation governance
- No personal liability protection angle

---

### ISO 42001 Certification Bodies (SQS, others)
**What they offer:**
- Independent certification of AI management systems
- International repute and recognition
- Full standard compliance pathway

**Gap for Geneva boutiques:**
- Certification is a 6–18 month process requiring significant internal resources
- Most small boutiques cannot sustain the internal effort
- Certification is corporate-level, not practitioner-level
- No focus on the specific problems of consulting methodology
- Does not address sub-consultant delegation
- Does not provide personal liability protection documentation

---

### Legal Firms (Geneva-based)
**What they offer:**
- Data protection advice (nFADP)
- Contract review for AI tool vendor agreements
- Regulatory compliance counsel

**Gap for Geneva boutiques:**
- Legal firms advise on what the law requires, not how to operationalize AI governance
- No practical methodology documentation for consulting deliverables
- No AI governance framework designed for consulting firms
- Engagement is billable-hour, not outcome-oriented
- No sub-consultant governance framework

---

### Technology Vendors (AI tool providers)
**What they offer:**
- Platform-level governance controls
- Data processing agreements
- Compliance documentation for their platform

**Gap for Geneva boutiques:**
- Vendors govern their own tool, not the boutique's overall AI use
- No methodology defensibility for consulting deliverables
- No client-facing documentation
- No sub-consultant governance
- No personal liability protection

## What's Missing: The Boutique AI Governance Gap

### What Geneva boutiques need and cannot get from the market:

**1. A Documented AI Governance Framework for Small Consulting Firms**
Not an enterprise framework. Not a certification pathway. A practical, written framework a 5-person Geneva consultancy can implement in weeks, not months. Something they can show to a client when the questionnaire arrives.

**What exists:** Nothing tailored. Enterprise frameworks are too heavy; generic AI governance templates are too vague.

**2. Consulting Methodology Defensibility Documentation**
A structured way to document how AI was used in a specific engagement — inputs, tools, process, human review, outputs — that creates an audit trail the managing director can defend.

**What exists:** Legal privilege frameworks are not the same. Generic audit trail tools are not consulting-specific.

**3. Sub-Consultant AI Governance Framework**
A delegation governance process that allows the signing partner to know, control, and document what sub-consultants are doing with AI in shared client work. Including AI tool inventory requirements, data handling rules, and output documentation.

**What exists:** Nothing in the market addresses this specific problem for consulting firms.

**4. Personal Liability Protection Documentation**
A documentation set that allows a managing director to demonstrate personal best-efforts compliance with AI governance expectations — creating a record of good governance practice that protects them if a problem arises.

**What exists:** D&O insurance addresses financial exposure, not governance documentation. No product creates a governance paper trail for personal protection.

**5. Client Questionnaire Response Support**
A service that helps a boutique managing director actually complete the AI governance questionnaire their client sent — with documented answers they can stand behind, referencing their actual governance practices.

**What exists:** Some legal firms will review answers. None will help build the underlying governance documentation first.

**6. FINMA/nFADP-Aligned AI Governance for Consulting Firms**
A practical alignment guide that translates FINMA Guidance 08/2024 and nFADP requirements into specific practices for a small consulting firm — not a bank, not a large enterprise.

**What exists:** FINMA guidance is written for supervised institutions. No practical translation exists for Geneva consulting boutiques.

---

## Competitive Positioning Opportunity

The gap is clear: no player is serving Geneva consulting boutiques (2–50 employees) with practical, personal, documented AI governance support that addresses:
- Client questionnaire response
- Carrier attestation
- Sub-consultant delegation governance
- Proposal methodology defensibility
- Personal liability protection
- nFADP/FINMA alignment

The Big Four serve large enterprises. Legal firms provide reactive counsel. Certification bodies require heavy processes. No one is providing the boutique-sized, personal-liability-focused, document-ready AI governance support that this ICP needs.

This is the white space.
