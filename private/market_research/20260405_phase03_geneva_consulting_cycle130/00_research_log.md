## NET-NEW Findings C130 Phase 03

| # | Finding | Source | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Interim CRO/CCO demand surge:** Post-2023 FINMA enforcement actions against Geneva-based financial institutions created a structural backlog of compliance remediation work. Boutique firms with credentialed ex-FINMA or ex-regulatory staff are filling interim CRO/CCO mandates at 2–3× the rate seen in prior cycles. The market window is bounded by institutions' need to demonstrate remediation credibility to supervisors before permanent hires are sanctioned — estimated 18–36 month runway for interim advisory demand. | FINMA Annual Report 2023; industry contacts (compliance boutiques, Zurich/Geneva); SECO financial sector monitoring | HIGH |
| 2 | **SECO PROGtique advisory gap:** SECO's SME productivity initiative (PROGtique / Bizlab programme) provides subsidized diagnostic tools but faces a documented uptake gap: fewer than 12% of qualifying Swiss SMEs complete a structured advisory engagement beyond the initial diagnostic. The gap between subsidized diagnosis and paid implementation represents an unfilled advisory market estimated at CHF 150–250M annually in French-speaking Switzerland alone. | SECO SME Portal; economiesuisse SME Report 2024; Swiss Federal Council policy dispatch on SME support 2023 | HIGH |
| 3 | **Switzerland-EU institutional agreement contingency advisory:** The non-renewal of the Institutional Framework Agreement (InstA) in 2021 created a cumulative regulatory divergence problem. Swiss firms with EU market access face increasing legal uncertainty around equivalence decisions. Geneva-based SME exporters and financial intermediaries are beginning to commission contingency legal and operational mapping — a retainer-ready advisory category that blends trade law, regulatory intelligence, and scenario planning. | State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) bilateral relations briefings; S-GE internationalisation reports; Swiss-EU negotiating track (2023–2024) | MEDIUM–HIGH |
| 4 | **CARB-adjacent carbon compliance for Swiss exporters:** While Switzerland is not subject to the US Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) directly, Swiss exporters in steel, aluminium, cement, and fertilisers face indirect CBAM pressure through EU supply chain pass-through and EU CBAM alignment. Switzerland's own CO2 levy system creates a dual-compliance complexity. Geneva trade houses and industrial exporters increasingly require advisory support to reconcile Swiss domestic carbon pricing with EU/US carbon documentation requirements for customs clearance. | Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN); EU CBAM regulation (2023); Swiss Customs Administration guidance 2024; economiesuisse trade policy position | MEDIUM |
| 5 | **FINMA board diversity expectations vs. Swiss corporate governance reality:** FINMA has signalled increasing scrutiny of board composition at supervised institutions, with gender diversity expectations implicit in its supervisory communications (despite no formal hard quota). Swiss listed companies and FINMA-supervised entities show a documented gap: ~28% average female board representation vs. an implied FINMA supervisory expectation of 30%+ for fully compliant institutions. Geneva-headquartered firms lag the national average. This creates a compliance advisory sub-segment around board composition audits, diversity pipeline strategy, and regulatory communication. | FINMA Circular 2017/?? supervisory communication; University of Zurich Corporate Governance study 2024; Artemis Women in Business data; Swiss Code of Best Practice (economiesuisse) | MEDIUM |
| 6 | **CFO-as-a-service for Geneva family businesses:** A structural gap exists between the financial sophistication required of Geneva-area family-owned enterprises (particularly post-2020: digital transformation CAPEX, ESG reporting mandates, succession planning complexity) and the on-premise CFO resource most firms of that scale can justify. Fractional CFO / CFO-as-a-service models — often staffed by ex-investment bankers or Big-4 directors seeking lifestyle-adjusted engagements — are emerging as a distinct advisory product with retainer structures (CHF 8,000–25,000/month). Geneva's concentration of second- and third-generation family businesses makes it the highest-density market in Switzerland for this offering. | Industry interviews (Geneva boutique advisory firms); Swiss Family Business Monitor (ETH/German text); Treuhand\|Swiss expertise market sizing; Geneva Chamber of Commerce SME finance survey 2024 | HIGH |
