### EN
**DigitalCoa.ch Client Onboarding — Discovery Framework and First 30 Days**

Best practice advisory onboarding — Geneva market:
- Discovery philosophy: the first engagement is not about selling — it's about understanding; Geneva clients (especially family businesses and institutional executives) are relationship-first; they hire advisors they trust, not firms with the best pitch deck
- Pre-onboarding (before first meeting):
  1. Research the client: company, key executives, recent news, regulatory context, their competitive position
  2. Identify the decision-maker: who has authority to hire; typically CFO, CEO, or board chair for advisory engagements
  3. Prepare 3 targeted questions: specific to their situation, not generic; demonstrates genuine preparation
- First meeting (60-90 minutes):
  1. Listen first (80% of time): understand their situation, challenges, priorities
  2. Ask diagnostic questions: "What's the most pressing regulatory challenge right now?" "If you could fix one thing about your compliance process, what would it be?" "What keeps you up at night related to this?"
  3. Avoid: pitching services, over-talking, using jargon, being unprepared
  4. Outcome: not a proposal — it's a shared understanding of whether there's a fit
- Discovery framework (for Geneva regulatory advisory):
  - **Context layer**: current regulatory environment, upcoming deadlines, recent regulatory developments
  - **Stakeholder layer**: who owns this issue internally; who approves decisions; who will be day-to-day contact
  - **Urgency layer**: why now; what's the consequence of inaction; what's the timeline
  - **Success layer**: what does a successful outcome look like; how will they measure success
  - **Constraint layer**: budget range, internal resource availability, political dynamics
- Post-meeting:
  1. Send personalized follow-up within 24 hours: summarize what was discussed, confirm understanding, outline potential engagement approach
  2. If fit: send detailed proposal within 3-5 business days
  3. If no fit: say so honestly; offer alternative or referral; this builds trust for future referrals
- First 30-day roadmap (for engaged client):
  - Week 1: kickoff meeting, establish working rhythm, request necessary documents, confirm scope and timeline
  - Week 2-3: diagnostic phase — interviews, document review, regulatory landscape mapping
  - Week 4: interim findings presentation — share initial observations, validate direction, adjust scope if needed
- Client onboarding red flags: client expects immediate deliverables without diagnostic; client is difficult to reach/review; scope keeps expanding without budget adjustment; internal sponsor loses authority

### FR
**Onboarding client DigitalCoa.ch — Cadre de découverte et feuille de route des 30 premiers jours**

Meilleures pratiques onboarding conseil — contexte genevois :
- Philosophie de découverte : le premier engagement n'est pas de vendre — c'est de comprendre ; clients genevois (surtout entreprises familiales et cadres institutionnels) sont relation-first
- Pré-onboarding (avant première réunion) :
  1. Rechercher le client : entreprise, cadres clés, nouvelles récentes, contexte réglementaire
  2. Identifier le décideur : qui a l'autorité d'embaucher
  3. Préparer 3 questions ciblées : spécifiques à leur situation
- Première réunion (60-90 minutes) :
  1. Écouter d'abord (80% du temps)
  2. Poser des questions diagnostiques
  3. Éviter : pitcher services, trop parler, utiliser jargon
  4. Résultat : pas une proposition — c'est une compréhension partagée de s'il y a un fit
- Feuille de route des 30 premiers jours :
  - Semaine 1 : réunion de lancement, établir rythme de travail, demander documents nécessaires
  - Semaine 2-3 : phase diagnostique — interviews, review de documents, cartographie du paysage réglementaire
  - Semaine 4 : présentation des conclusions provisoires
