## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: DigitalCoa.ch Competitive Positioning — Geneva Boutique Advisory Landscape
- Big 4 in Geneva: PwC (500+), Deloitte (300+), EY, KPMG — dominant in audit/tax; advisory practices subscale relative to audit
- Geneva boutique market: Bovet Strategor, Rev比例, PLC Consulting — strategy boutiques; sector specialists in commodity trading, pharma, fintech
- "Best of" model: ex-Big 4 partners at boutique scale; 5-20 person firms; growing trend
- DigitalCoa.ch positioning options: (1) Sector specialist in Geneva regulatory/compliance; (2) Geographic specialist for Geneva institutional market; (3) Format differentiator — remote-first, senior-only; (4) Intelligence layer — Brotons.net thought leadership as client acquisition channel
- Positioning principle: be specific and own a category — not "strategy consulting" but "regulatory strategy for Geneva asset managers"
- Rate positioning: CHF 3,000-3,500/day = premium boutique; CHF 4,000-4,500/day = elite boutique equivalent to Big 4 partner
- Client selection: decline misaligned work to build reputation

### Finding 02: DigitalCoa.ch Pricing Strategy — Optimizing Revenue Model
- Day-rate model: CHF 2,800-4,500/day for boutique Geneva; clear and simple; incentivizes efficiency
- Project-based (fixed fee): predictable for client; requires precise scoping; increasingly preferred by CFOs
- Monthly retainer: CHF 8,000-20,000/month for 5-10 days/month; predictability for both parties
- Value-based pricing: highest margin; harder to implement; based on value delivered (e.g., "saves CHF 200,000 in potential FINMA fines")
- Hybrid models: retainer + success fee; retainer + variable fee tied to outcomes
- Recommended approach: free/low-cost discovery (2h) → fixed-price proposal → project-based or day-rate engagement → retainer for key clients
- Pricing communication: never apologize for rates; frame as quality signal; senior-only model = no junior burn

### Finding 03: DigitalCoa.ch Client Onboarding — Discovery Framework and First 30 Days
- Discovery philosophy: first engagement is not about selling — it's about understanding; Geneva clients are relationship-first
- Pre-onboarding: research client, identify decision-maker, prepare 3 targeted questions
- First meeting (60-90 min): 80% listening; ask diagnostic questions; avoid pitching/jargon
- Discovery framework: Context → Stakeholder → Urgency → Success criteria → Constraints
- Post-meeting: personalized follow-up within 24h; detailed proposal within 3-5 days if fit; honest no if no fit
- First 30-day roadmap: Week 1 kickoff → Week 2-3 diagnostic → Week 4 interim findings presentation
- Red flags: client expects immediate deliverables without diagnostic; scope creep without budget adjustment

### Finding 04: DigitalCoa.ch Service Line Expansion — Adjacent Revenue Opportunities
- Expansion criteria: leverages existing expertise, serves existing clients, attractive margins, no major new credentialing
- High-priority adjacents: (1) Fractional CFO (CHF 8,000-20,000/month retainer) — natural extension for CFO-level clients; (2) Regulatory project management — managing actual compliance projects; (3) Board advisory (CHF 5,000-15,000/board mandate); (4) Training/workshops (CHF 5,000-8,000/day) — repeatable and scalable; (5) RegTech vendor selection
- Sequencing: Add Fractional CFO now → Training/Workshops next quarter → Board Advisory when first mandate secured
- Revenue impact: adding fractional CFO and training can increase annual revenue per senior advisor 30-50% without proportional cost increase
- Services to avoid: audit/accounting (requires credentialing), legal services (requires bar), IT implementation (too far from core)

### Finding 05: DigitalCoa.ch Lead Qualification and Pipeline Management
- Lead sources: Brotons.net email engagement (highest conversion), referrals (30-50% close rate), LinkedIn inbound, web contact form, conference/events
- Lead scoring: Cold (1-5) → Warm (6-15) → Qualified (16-30) → Sales-ready (31+)
- CRM: HubSpot free tier sufficient; pipeline stages: Lead → Qualified → Discovery Call → Proposal → Negotiation → Closed
- Conversion process: auto-responder within 2h → personalized outreach within 48h → follow-up at 7 days → discovery call within 5 days → proposal within 3-5 days
- Benchmarks: lead-to-proposal 20-30%; proposal-to-close 50-60%; sales cycle 4-8 weeks
- Geneva consideration: clients move slowly; maintain contact via value-add content during long cycles
- Nurture: monthly value-add email for 6 months before re-engaging with sales intent

### Finding 06: DigitalCoa.ch Client Retention and Expansion Revenue — Referral Engine
- Quarterly review cadence: 60-min video call; agenda 48h in advance; written summary within 5 days
- Quarterly review purpose: strategic check-in (not sales call); uncovers expansion opportunities; surfaces at-risk signals
- Expansion opportunities: follow-on engagements at quarterly review, adjacent services introduction, scope expansion
- Referral timing: at engagement close, at quarterly review, after delivering great work
- Referral infrastructure: referral "introduction" email template ready; acknowledge referrals (small gift or dinner)
- Geneva referral ecosystem: law firm partners (Lenz Cailler, Borel & Barbey, Vischer, Walder Wyss), Brotons.net audience, CCIG/SFAMA/IHEID events, private bankers (Pictet, Lombard Odier)
- At-risk signals: infrequent communication, no response to meeting requests, budget cuts, champion role change
