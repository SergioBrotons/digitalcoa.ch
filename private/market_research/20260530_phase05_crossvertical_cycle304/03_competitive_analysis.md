## The Competitive Landscape Map

### Layer 1: Generic AI Governance Frameworks

**Players:** ISO, NIST, EU AI Act, various consultancies
**Offer:** Abstract principles, general governance structures, self-assessment tools
**Gap:** No Geneva-specific, SMB-professional, practically implementable protocol

**White space for DigitalCoa.ch:** The Geneva professional practice is not a large enterprise. The frameworks designed for enterprise governance do not translate to a 5-person Geneva law firm or a solo medical practice. The white space is SMB-specific AI governance for Geneva professional practices — a market with no current specialist.

---

### Layer 2: Legal-Specific AI Guidance

**Players:** Swiss Bar Association (SBA), Geneva Bar Association, various law firms
**Offer:** General guidance on AI use by lawyers; ethics opinions
**Gap:** No Geneva-specific protocol for AI vendor oversight, client data management, or evidence production for small law firms

**White space for DigitalCoa.ch:** The Geneva bar guidance is principles-based. It does not tell a 3-attorney Geneva firm how to operationalize the principles. The specific question — "what does Article 321 CC compliance look like for an AI-assisted brief review?" — has no Geneva-specific answer.

---

### Layer 3: Finance-Specific AI Guidance

**Players:** FINMA, asset management industry associations, fintech consultants
**Offer:** FINMA guidance on AI use in financial services; general AI risk frameworks
**Gap:** No boutique wealth management-specific protocol; no Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence production service

**White space for DigitalCoa.ch:** FINMA guidance addresses regulated entities but does not produce evidence. The specific problem — that FINMA compliance ≠ Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence — is not addressed by any current service provider. The boutique WM firm needs a service that produces both.

---

### Layer 4: Healthcare-Specific AI Guidance

**Players:** FMH, Swiss Medical Association, hospital IT departments
**Offer:** General FMH guidance on AI use; hospital-grade AI governance frameworks
**Gap:** No independent solo/small practice AI governance solution; no Swissdamed classification service for independent physicians

**White space for DigitalCoa.ch:** Hospital AI governance frameworks do not translate to a solo Geneva physician. The solo practice dual-role problem — being the AI supervisor and the AI user simultaneously — has no current solution. The efficiency-liability paradox (using AI helps the practice but creates liability) has no current resolution service.

---

### Layer 5: Cross-Vertical AI Consultants

**Players:** Big Four, international compliance consultancies
**Offer:** Enterprise-grade AI governance, AI risk frameworks, regulatory compliance programs
**Gap:** Not Geneva-specific; not SMB-priced; not personal-professional-liability-focused

**White space for DigitalCoa.ch:** The Geneva SMB professional principal needs a trusted boutique advisor — not an enterprise consultant. The relationship is personal: the principal's license, reputation, and personal liability are at stake. A boutique Geneva advisor who understands the specific vertical, the specific Swiss regulatory context, and the specific personal exposure is not currently available in the market.

## The White Space Synthesis

### The Universal White Space

**Across all four verticals, there is one unoccupied market position:**

> **"Geneva SMB professional practice AI governance — evidence production, not framework adherence — delivered by a trusted boutique Geneva advisor with personal professional liability expertise."**

No current competitor occupies this position because:
1. Generic framework vendors are not Geneva-specific
2. Geneva-specific advisors (lawyers, accountants) have not developed AI governance expertise
3. The problem crosses verticals (no single vertical specialist sees the cross-vertical pattern)
4. The service requires both technical AI understanding and specific Swiss professional regulation expertise

---

### The Cross-Vertical Unifying Problem

**Every professional practice in Geneva faces the same structural problem:**

```
Tools adopted → No evidence produced → Debt accumulates silently
→ Regulatory forcing function arrives → Principal cannot produce evidence
→ Personal liability exposed
```

This structure is **identical** across:
- A 3-attorney Geneva law firm using AI for brief review
- A boutique wealth management firm using AI for portfolio analysis
- A small Geneva consulting firm using AI for client deliverables
- A solo Geneva physician using AI for administrative tasks

**The evidence gap is universal. The structural problem is universal. The solution must be universal in architecture, vertical in application.**

- System design for real-time evidence production

**Ongoing AI Governance Advisory**
- Quarterly review of vendor contracts
- Annual evidence audit
- Regulatory change monitoring (Swissdamed, EU AI Act, FINMA guidance)
- Carrier attestation preparation

## Key Insight

**The white space is not a single service — it is a complete advisory category that does not currently exist in the Geneva market.** Generic frameworks, international consultancies, professional associations, and template vendors all fail to address the specific intersection that Geneva SMB professional practices require: Swiss professional liability expertise + AI governance technical understanding + evidence production system design. This intersection is DigitalCoa.ch's addressable market position.
