## The Universal Compliance Structure

### The Documentation Debt Cycle

Across all four verticals, the same structural compliance failure is operating:

```
Phase 1: AI Tool Adoption
├── Driven by: efficiency necessity, competitive pressure, staff demand
├── Trigger: staff departure creates coverage gap; peer adoption creates competitive fear
├── Documentation created: NONE
└── Vendor contract signed: YES (without review)

Phase 2: Silent Accumulation
├── Duration: 12–36 months typically
├── Documentation debt: compounds with each AI-assisted decision
├── No forcing function: regulatory environment not yet active
├── Awareness level: LOW — principal believes vendor handles compliance
└── Risk: increases with each deployment

Phase 3: Forcing Function Arrival
├── Form: carrier attestation, client question, audit, incident, peer problem
├── Effect: forces principal to confront documentation absence
├── Response options: CREATE EVIDENCE NOW (structurally impossible)
│   or ATTEST WITHOUT EVIDENCE (worse than not attesting)
└── Timeline pressure: acute at forcing function moment

Phase 4: Exposure Crystallization
├── Evidence gap is now: documented, visible, timestamped
├── Regulatory contact: may now include the documentation gap
├── Personal liability: exposed without defense
└── Solution required: evidence production (not documentation)
```

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Pressures

### Pressure 1: Professional Secrecy / Professional Obligations

**EN Obligation:** Article 321 Swiss Criminal Code — professional secrecy
**Applies to:** Physicians, lawyers, notaries (absolute)
**Effect on AI:** Unreviewed AI vendor contracts are a prima facie violation risk
**Cross-vertical extension:** Finance and consulting professionals have equivalent contractual confidentiality obligations

**The Compliance Gap:** Professionals do not know that sending client data to an AI vendor — without a reviewed DPA that includes professional secrecy protections — is a potential violation. The vendor contract review is not a "nice to have." It is a professional obligation trigger.

---

### Pressure 2: Carrier/Insurer Attestation Architecture

**Applies to:** Professional liability insurers (legal, medical, consulting)
**Mechanism:** Annual or biennial attestation forms asking about AI tool use
**The Problem:** The attestation creates a documented standard that, if attestations are inaccurate, makes the situation worse than not attesting

**Cross-vertical pattern (C304 Phase 01 + Phase 04):**
- Legal: carrier attestation architecture creates documented standard without evidence production
- Healthcare: same carrier attestation mechanism, same documentation paradox
- The attestation form is designed to assess carrier risk — it creates documented evidence of professional risk

**The Compliance Gap:** Completing the attestation requires evidence that does not exist. Not completing it creates coverage questions. The attestation form is an acute forcing function with no resolution pathway in the current market.

---

### Pressure 3: Swissdamed Classification Obligation

**Applies to:** Medical device manufacturers and distributors (July 1, 2026 — medical devices only)
**Status for independent physicians:** NOT medical device manufacturers — Swissdamed does NOT require independent solo physicians to classify AI systems as medical devices under the current regulatory framework
**What IS required:** Independent physician practices still have nFADP obligations, FMH professional guidance obligations, and carrier attestation requirements

**The Misunderstanding Risk:** Some independent physicians believe Swissdamed requires them to classify their AI tools as medical devices. This is incorrect for the current framework scope. However, the confusion is creating decision paralysis — physicians are waiting for Swissdamed guidance that does not apply to them.

**The Compliance Gap:** Independent physicians need a competent assessment of what Swissdamed means for their specific practice — not a general framework explanation, but a specific determination of their obligations. This is a discrete, bounded service: Swissdamed Classification Determination Review.

---

### Pressure 4: EU AI Act — August 2026

**Applies to:** Swiss companies that place AI systems in the EU market or use AI systems that process EU data subjects
**Geneva professional practice relevance:** Indirect but growing
- Finance: EU AI Act deployer obligations if serving EU HNW clients
- Consulting: EU AI Act if delivering services to EU-based clients
- Legal: EU AI Act if handling EU-origin client data
- Healthcare: EU AI Act if processing EU patient data

**The Structural Problem:** EU AI Act compliance requires Declaration of Conformity from the AI system provider. Non-EU vendors (US-based AI companies, for example) cannot provide EU Declaration of Conformity. This creates a structurally unsolvable compliance problem for Geneva SMB firms using non-EU AI tools.

**The Compliance Gap:** The EU AI Act deployer obligation is structurally impossible to satisfy at boutique WM scale if using non-EU AI vendors. Geneva firms need a service that assesses their specific EU AI Act exposure and produces a compliance architecture that is achievable — not a framework that tells them what the law requires in abstract terms.

---

### Pressure 5: FINMA + Art. 63 Personal Due Diligence

**Applies to:** FINMA-licensed wealth management firms
**FINMA compliance:** Does NOT automatically produce Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence
**Art. 63 Personal Due Diligence:** Different protection objective — requires different documentation

**The Conflict (C304 Phase 02):** FINMA-compliant documentation does not satisfy Art. 63 personal due diligence requirements. The same attestation cannot serve both masters. Geneva boutique WM firms are potentially exposed on both dimensions without realizing the documentation they produced for FINMA does not protect them personally under Art. 63.

**The Compliance Gap:** FINMA-licensed firms need a parallel documentation architecture that produces Art. 63 personal due diligence evidence alongside FINMA compliance documentation. This is a specific service that no current provider offers.

---

### Pressure 6: Documentation Retention Periods

**Applies to:** All four verticals — but entirely undefined
**Status:** No Geneva-defined, AI-specific documentation retention period exists
**What professionals assume:** "We keep client records for 10 years" (from pre-AI era)
**What is actually required:** Retention periods for AI-assisted decisions, AI prompts, AI outputs, and supervision records are entirely undefined

**The Compliance Gap:** Without defined retention periods, professionals cannot create evidence architecture. They do not know what to keep, for how long, or in what form. The retention debt compounds silently. This is the most invisible but most structurally important compliance gap.
