## Cross-Vertical Market Signals

### Signal 1: The Post-Adoption Reckoning

**Observed across:** All four verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare)

**Signal:** Professionals adopted AI tools rapidly (2023–2025) driven by efficiency necessity and competitive pressure. Now the regulatory environment is catching up, and the same professionals face a common problem: they cannot produce evidence of what the AI did, what it was told, what it generated, and what decisions were made with its assistance.

**Market implication:** The post-adoption phase is now. The gap between AI adoption and evidence production is a market that did not exist 18 months ago and is now acutely active. Every professional who adopted AI in the past 24 months has documentation debt.

**Quantification signal:** Documentation debt compounds. Every month without evidence architecture, the exposure grows. The market for evidence production services is therefore not a one-time fix — it is an ongoing service architecture need.

---

### Signal 2: The Carrier/Insurer Activation

**Observed across:** Legal and healthcare (legal professional liability; medical malpractice)

**Signal:** Liability carriers are now sending AI-specific attestation forms. Geneva legal firms and medical practices are receiving these forms and encountering two problems simultaneously:
1. They do not know what the attestation actually requires
2. They do not have the evidence the attestation asks them to confirm exists

**Market implication:** Carrier attestation is becoming a forcing function. When a principal receives an attestation form, they move from观望 (wait-and-see) to action. The attestation creates a specific, deadline-adjacent, consequence-laden moment of clarity. This is the highest-intent market signal in the Geneva professional market.

**The paradox signal:** Completing the attestation without evidence makes the situation worse. Not completing it creates immediate coverage questions. The principal is trapped between two forms of exposure.

---

### Signal 3: The Geneva Standard Vacuum

**Observed across:** All four verticals — Geneva-specific regulatory and professional context

**Signal:** Geneva SMB professional practices have no Geneva-specific AI governance protocol. The frameworks available are either:
- International/generic (ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF) — too abstract for a 5-person Geneva law firm
- EU-origin (EU AI Act) — not Swiss law, creates confusion about jurisdictional applicability
- US-origin (various) — irrelevant in Swiss regulatory context

**Market implication:** The Geneva standard first-mover opportunity remains active. A Geneva-specific, SMB-professional-appropriate, professionally-verified AI governance protocol has no current competitor in the market. The Ville de Genève concentration of SMB professional practices creates a definable geographic market for this specific offering.

**Timeline signal:** The EU AI Act August 2026 enforcement creates a public discussion moment. Geneva professionals will become aware that AI governance is not optional in Europe — and will look for Swiss-appropriate solutions.

---

### Signal 4: The Vendor Contract Revelation

**Observed across:** All four verticals

**Signal:** Professional firms signed AI vendor DPAs (Data Processing Agreements) without reviewing the terms. The revelation happening now — across all verticals — is that these contracts:
- Allow vendor access to client/professional data
- Do not include professional secrecy protections (Art. 321 CC for physicians and lawyers; equivalent professional obligations for finance and consulting)
- Contain change clauses allowing terms modification without renewed consent
- Rarely include evidence production or audit rights

**Market implication:** Vendor contract review is an immediate, discrete, high-value service. It addresses a felt problem (the professional has the contract, has never read it, suspects something is wrong) with a clear deliverable (a review memo + red-line recommendations). It is an ideal entry-point service.

---

### Signal 5: The Staff Supervision Gap

**Observed across:** All four verticals — particularly acute in legal and healthcare

**Signal:** Junior staff are using AI tools without documented supervision protocols. The principal is personally liable for staff conduct under professional regulations, but:
- No supervision protocol exists
- No documentation of staff AI use is being created
- The principal does not know what tools staff are using
- The principal has not reviewed what the AI tools are doing with client data

**Market implication:** Staff supervision protocol development is a discrete, bounded service with clear deliverables. It addresses a specific liability (personal exposure for staff conduct) with a concrete product (written protocol + staff acknowledgment form + supervision log template). It is a follow-on service from vendor contract review.

---

### Signal 6: The Documentation Retrofit Impossibility

**Observed across:** All four verticals — but structurally impossible only now being understood

**Signal:** Professionals are realizing that retroactive documentation of AI decisions and processes is not possible. The evidence must be produced at the time of the decision or process — it cannot be reconstructed afterward. This is creating a category shift in how professionals think about documentation:

- **Before:** "We'll document if something goes wrong"
- **After:** "If we didn't document it at the time, we can't document it now — and there's no point documenting it now"

**Market implication:** The market is moving from "we'll deal with documentation later" to "we need evidence architecture now." This is a service category that did not exist 18 months ago. Evidence architecture design is a distinct service from documentation templates — it is the system that makes real-time evidence production possible.

## Cross-Vertical Market Size Signal

**Geneva SMB Professional Concentration:**
- Geneva Canton: ~3,200 registered legal practices (avocats, notaires)
- Geneva Canton: ~420 registered medical practices (médecins indépendants)
- Geneva Canton: ~280 wealth management / family office firms
- Geneva Canton: ~180 small consulting firms (strategy, management, digital)
- **Total addressable Geneva SMB professional market: ~4,080 practices**
- **At 1–15 employees per practice, principals at ~4,080 practices have direct AI governance exposure**

**Market timing signal:** June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers (Geneva) creates a Geneva legal-specific activation moment. Swissdamed July 1, 2026 creates a healthcare-specific activation moment. EU AI Act August 2026 creates a broader market activation. The window between now and August 2026 is the first-mover service deployment window.

---

## Key Insight

**The market is not asking for AI governance frameworks. The market is asking for evidence production systems that work for small professional practices.** The forcing functions (carrier attestations, Swissdamed, EU AI Act) are creating urgency without creating solutions. The gap between urgency and solution is the service opportunity — and it spans all four verticals simultaneously because the underlying problem (post-adoption evidence debt) is universal.
