**Brotons.net Content Refresh Priority: Five Articles Requiring Update Before Q2**

Brotons.net has accumulated articles that reflect outdated market conditions — particularly post-2024 regulatory changes and the AI era. Five priority articles have been identified for refresh based on C97 research findings.

**Priority refresh articles:**

**1. Geneva banking secrecy article (if exists):** FINMA AML intensification and US DOJ scrutiny have fundamentally changed the landscape. Any existing article on Swiss banking secrecy needs updating to reflect the 2025-2026 enforcement reality.

**2. ESG/sustainability advisory article:** The market has shifted from strategy to assurance. Any existing ESG content framing it purely as advisory opportunity needs reframing to reflect the verification/assurance scarcity angle.

**3. Geneva arbitration article (if exists):** The arbitration landscape has shifted. US parties going to Dubai/Singapore while Geneva holds for non-US disputes is a meaningful update for any existing piece.

**4. Swiss fintech article:** The DLT tokenization backlog has cleared. FINMA's stablecoin ordinance is coming. Any existing fintech piece from 2023-2024 is likely outdated.

**5. Consulting industry article:** AI consulting has bifurcated. "Digital transformation" is a dated framing. The AI consulting specialisation premium and fractional AI officer model are the new angles.

**Why this matters:** Content freshness directly affects search performance and credibility with the Brotons.net audience. Executives returning to find outdated Geneva analysis will question the platform's authority.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** These five refreshes are operational priorities — achievable without owner approval, within operator policy. Each can be updated based on C98 research findings.
