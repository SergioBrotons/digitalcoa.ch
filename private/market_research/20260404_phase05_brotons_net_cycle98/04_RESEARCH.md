## Finding

**Brotons.net Internal Linking Architecture Can Be Strengthened — Cross-Phase Research Linking Opportunity**

The research rotation produces content in themed phases that share underlying themes. A systematic internal linking strategy — connecting related articles across phases — would improve SEO performance and session duration.

**Cross-phase linking opportunities:**

**1. AI theme linking:** AI appears across Legal (FADP/AI), Finance (DLT tokenization), Consulting (AI strategy), NGO/Tech (IO AI adoption). Articles on AI in different sectors should link to each other, creating an AI content cluster.

**2. ESG/sustainability theme linking:** ESG appears across Finance (sustainable finance taxonomy), Consulting (sustainability assurance), NGO/Tech (humanitarian ESG). A unified ESG content cluster on Brotons.net would strengthen topical authority.

**3. Regulatory change theme linking:** FINMA AML, mHRDD, FADP, T+1 settlement — regulatory change is a recurring Brotons.net theme. Linking regulatory-focused articles across sectors creates a regulatory intelligence cluster.

**4. Geneva international positioning linking:** International arbitration, UN AI governance, WTO disputes, WIPO IP — Geneva's international character is a linking thread across all phases.

**5. Digital transformation of professional services:** Consulting (AI), Finance (DLT), NGOs (IO AI), Legal (AI compliance) — all sectors facing digital transformation. Cross-phase linking around this theme builds topical authority.

**Why this matters:** Internal linking is a self-healing operational fix within operator policy. No new content is needed — just structured linking of existing content. This is a high-ROI, low-risk operational action.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** Internal linking improvements can be executed autonomously as an operational self-heal. Recommend building a site map of existing articles and creating a linking matrix.
