# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Consulting

## Key Regulatory Framework

### Swiss Professional Services Regulation
- **OR Art. 394ff** — Mandate contract rules for consulting engagements
- **SRO oversight** — Certain consulting activities require SRO membership for AML obligations
- **Art. 904 OR** — Consultant liability limits and exclusion clauses
- **OGE professional rules** — Geneva Bar Association ethical rules for legal consultancy

### Consulting Sector-Specific Regulation
| Area | Regulation | Impact on Geneva Consultants |
|---|---|---|
| AML (if handling client funds) | GwG, revised 2024-2025 | AML compliance obligations for consultants receiving client funds |
| Data protection | nFADP (2023-2024) | Client data processing, AI training data consent (Art. 9) |
| AI-generated advice | Art. 41 OR (proposed) | AI-assisted consulting liability; compliance defense |
| IO procurement | UN/IO procurement rules | Compliance requirements for UN/IO advisory contracts |
| Financial services | FinSA | If consulting involves financial instrument advice, FinSA licensing may apply |

### Geneva-Specific Regulatory Considerations
- **CCIG (Chambre de commerce, d'industrie et des services de Genève)** — Business registration, certification for Geneva-based consultancies
- **Geneva Cantonal professional license** — Certain regulated professions require cantonal authorization
- **Geneva labor law (LTr)** — Consultant vs. employee classification (Art. 14 LTr) — misclassification risk
- **Swiss withholding tax on consulting fees** — Withholding tax obligations for foreign consultants providing services to Swiss entities
