# 01 — ICP: Geneva Consulting (Management Consulting / Professional Services)

## Target Segment: Geneva Consulting & Professional Services Firms

### Firm Profile
- **Size:** 20-500+ consultants; boutique to mid-market management consulting, strategy consulting, sector specialists
- **Revenue:** CHF 5M–200M+ annual; project-based billing, retainers
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; possible Lausanne, Zurich, Nyon presence
- **Sector Focus:** Financial services consulting, public sector, healthcare, energy transition, international organizations
- **Status:** Post-pandemic project normalization; AI adoption creating both efficiency pressure and new service opportunities

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| Managing Partner / CEO | Senior executive | Pipeline visibility, talent retention, AI strategy |
| Finance Director / CFO | Non-consulting executive | Project profitability, resource utilization, billing accuracy |
| Practice Lead / Partner | Revenue owner | Client relationship, proposal win rate, intellectual property |
| Senior Consultant / Manager | Delivery lead | Project execution efficiency, research quality, AI-assisted analysis |
| Business Development | Commercial executive | Market intelligence, proposal development, thought leadership |

### Known Geneva Examples
- **Strategic finance consulting:** Advesto, Nord Sum, Proash, Ferrandi Conseil (Paris/Geneva)
- **Public sector consulting:** ARTicon, Econcept, Interface (Geneva)
- **Financial services consulting:** Portima, Inopus, KNPV Consulting (Geneva/Zurich)
- **Digital transformation consulting:** Blue-infinity (Geneva), Key Consulting Group
- **Sector specialists:** E棱 (energy), Quantik (strategy), Actar (actuarial consulting)
- **International organizations:** UN/IO procurement consulting (Geneva-specific)

### Pain Points (Initial Hypotheses)
1. **Proposal win rate pressure** — more competition, tighter client budgets, shorter decision cycles
2. **Talent scarcity and retention** — Geneva competes with NGOs, FIs, international organizations for qualified consultants
3. **AI-assisted research efficiency** — consultants unsure how to use AI tools without quality loss
4. **Knowledge management** — institutional knowledge trapped in individual consultants, not systematized
5. **International competition** — Big Four and international boutiques competing for Geneva consulting engagements
6. **International organization (IO) procurement complexity** — UN/IO procurement rules create barriers for small Geneva boutiques

### Buying Triggers (Initial Hypotheses)
- New strategy initiative or market entry
- Regulatory change requiring specialist knowledge
- Digital transformation project
- Board-level AI strategy mandate
- Cost reduction / efficiency improvement mandate
- IO procurement bid opportunity

### Buying Barriers (Initial Hypotheses)
- Strong existing relationships with incumbent advisors
- Short-term project mindset vs. long-term advisory partnerships
- Consultant credibility / track record requirements
- Procurement bureaucracy (especially for IO contracts)
- AI tool adoption concerns (confidentiality, quality)

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva Romandie consulting; English universal in international/finance consulting
- German useful for multi-Swiss market engagements
- Thought leadership is a key business development currency
- Geneva IO ecosystem creates unique consulting niche (UN/IO advisory, humanitarian, trade association)
