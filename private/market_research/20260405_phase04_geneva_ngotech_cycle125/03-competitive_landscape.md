#### Competitive Dynamics — Humanitarian Tech
- ICRC and UNHCR procurement: competitive, international bids
- Geneva-based firms have geographic advantage and understanding of Geneva institutional context
- Major technology vendors (Microsoft, Palantir, Salesforce) often preferred vendors
- Geneva boutique firms compete on specialization and institutional relationships
- Advisory opportunity: procurement navigation and contracting methodology for smaller firms

### Competitive Gaps

**Gap 01: Humanitarian Tech Contracting Advisory**
ICRC and UNHCR procurement is complex. Smaller Geneva tech firms lack procurement expertise. Advisory: how to navigate UNHCR/ICRC procurement, bid preparation, contracting methodology. No clear Geneva advisor owns this niche.

**Gap 02: Cleantech Fundraising Strategy Advisory**
Most fundraising advisors focus on general tech. Cleantech investor landscape is specialized. Advisory: investor target strategy, narrative development, milestone planning for Geneva cleantech startups.

**Gap 03: AI Governance Advisory for IO Institutions**
Geneva AI governance bodies (ITU, UN AI Advisory Board) creating demand for advisory support. No clear Geneva advisory firm owns IO AI governance advisory.

**Gap 04: ESG Talent Strategy for Financial Institutions**
2,400+ ESG talent gap in Switzerland by 2027. Advisory opportunity: upskilling programs, university partnerships, international recruitment strategies for Geneva financial institutions.
