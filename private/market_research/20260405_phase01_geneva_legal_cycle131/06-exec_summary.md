## Situation Overview

The Geneva legal market is undergoing a regulatory inflection point driven by three converging forces:

1. **AI liability and governance:** The Swiss Federal Council's AI civil liability proposal (pre-consultation March 2026) creates strict liability exposure for Geneva companies deploying AI systems. The Geneva Bar's AI ethics guidance (February 2026) creates attorney compliance obligations that cascade to client liability.

2. **Cross-border enforcement complexity:** The Lugano Convention's UK third-party gap (bilateral treaty pending ratification) creates immediate enforcement risk for Geneva companies with UK counterparties.

3. **Geneva arbitration surge:** Tariff-driven commodity disputes are driving a measurable increase in ICC/SCAI arbitrations, with force majeure and sanctions clauses being tested in real time.

4. **IP and DLT opportunities:** FIPO's updated blockchain patent guidelines (January 2026) and FINMA's RDSV implementation acceleration (February 2026) create advisory windows for Geneva tech companies and financial institutions.
