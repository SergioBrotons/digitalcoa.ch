# Regulatory Context — Geneva Legal / CYCLE 131 Phase 01
**Regulatory environment: active, pending, and emerging regulations affecting Geneva legal advisory market**

---

## Active Regulations (Currently in Force)

### 1. RDSV — Distributed Ledger Technology Act (as amended)
**Jurisdiction:** Swiss federal / FINMA oversight
**Status:** In force, implementation accelerating Q1-Q2 2026
**Key provisions:** DLT trading venue licensing; crypto custody license scope; stablecoin issuance rules; tokenization of traditional assets
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva financial institutions (private banks, commodity trading houses, family offices) exploring digital asset offerings; Geneva notary infrastructure adapting to digital asset transfers
**FINMA guidance (February 2026):** Narrowed stablecoin licensing for CHF-denominated stablecoins; simplified DLT venue licensing; expanded custody scope to multi-party computation cold storage
**Compliance deadline:** Ongoing — no fixed deadline, but FINMA backlog creating urgency for applicants
**Enforcement:** FINMA; crypto exchange licensing via FINMA authorization
**Penalties:** License revocation, criminal penalties for unauthorized crypto activity
**Source:** admin.ch/rdv; finma.ch DLT guidance February 2026

### 2. FADP — Federal Act on Data Protection (revised, in force since 2023)
**Jurisdiction:** Swiss federal / FDPIC oversight
**Status:** In force; FADP enforcement ongoing; nFADP (new FADP revision) pending parliamentary adoption
**Key provisions:** Data subject rights; data controller/processor obligations; cross-border data transfer restrictions; AI training data provisions (FADP Art. 12b)
**Geneva operational relevance:** All Geneva companies; Geneva IO/NGO with Swiss data subjects; companies using AI tools must assess FADP compliance for training data
**Enforcement:** FDPIC; canton-level data protection authorities
**Penalties:** Administrative fines up to CHF 250,000 for willful violations; reputational risk
**Source:** admin.ch/fadp; FDPIC guidance on AI training data (2024-2025)

### 3. FINMA Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance (AMLO-FINMA)
**Jurisdiction:** Swiss federal / FINMA oversight
**Status:** In force; enforcement intensified 2025-2026
**Key provisions:** KYC/CDD obligations; beneficial ownership verification; suspicious transaction reporting (MROS); correspondent banking due diligence
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva private banks; commodity trading houses; family offices with cross-border assets; Geneva lawyers' trust accounts (OGE Art. 12)
**Recent development:** FINMA enforcement of attorney trust account AML compliance — OGE/FINMA tension (C130 finding); MROS reporting volumes increased +12% 2025
**Enforcement:** FINMA; MROS; Geneva cantonal prosecutor
**Penalties:** License revocation; criminal prosecution; reputational damage
**Source:** finma.ch; fedpol.ch/mros

### 4. Swiss Sanctions Regime (SECO)
**Jurisdiction:** Swiss federal / SECO (State Secretariat for Economic Affairs)
**Status:** In force; rapidly evolving with geopolitical events
**Key provisions:** Russian sanctions (SR 912.20); OFAC equivalent Swiss regime; export controls on dual-use goods; sanctions screening obligations
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva commodity trading houses (Russian-origin commodities, sanctions-evasion risk); Geneva financial institutions; Geneva tech companies with dual-use goods
**Recent development:** US tariff escalation creating sanctions-complicity concerns in commodity trading; SECO guidance on tariff-related sanctions evasion (Q1 2026)
**Enforcement:** SECO; Fedpol; cantonal authorities
**Penalties:** Criminal penalties; OFAC equivalent fines; license revocation
**Source:** seco.admin.ch/sanktionen; admin.ch/bfi

### 5. ICC Arbitration Rules 2026 (in force 1 June 2026)
**Jurisdiction:** International (ICC, Geneva-seated arbitrations)
**Status:** In force 1 June 2026; transitional rules for arbitrations commenced before that date
**Key provisions:** Expedited arbitration procedure for disputes under USD 3M; emergency arbitrator provisions strengthened; digital case management; joinder/consolidation provisions expanded
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva-seated ICC arbitrations; Geneva commodity trading dispute resolution; Swiss companies in international disputes
**Compliance note:** Contracts should be reviewed for ICC 2026 rule references before 1 June 2026 go-live
**Source:** iccwbo.org/arbitration

### 6. Lugano Convention (2007) — Switzerland-EU/EEA
**Jurisdiction:** International treaty (Switzerland + EU/EEA)
**Status:** In force with EU/EEA; UK third-party status post-Brexit (pending bilateral treaty ratification)
**Key provisions:** Jurisdiction rules for civil/commercial disputes; automatic recognition and enforcement of judgments; provisional measures
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva companies with EU/EEA counterparties; Geneva commercial court; UK-counterparty enforcement gap (C131 new angle)
**Source:** admin.ch/efd; eda.admin.ch (bilateral treaty December 2024, not yet in force)

## Pending Regulations (Expected Within 12 Months)

### 1. Swiss Federal Council AI Civil Liability Framework
**Expected:** Pre-consultation document released March 2026; formal bill expected Q3-Q4 2026
**Key provisions:** Strict liability for AI systems (personal injury/property damage); compliance defense for high-risk AI operators; mandatory AI incident reporting to fedpol
**Geneva operational relevance:** High — all Geneva companies deploying AI systems; Geneva IO/NGO; Geneva commodity trading houses using AI trading tools
**Impact:** Creates new liability exposure; advisory demand for AI governance documentation; insurance product advisory
**Source:** Federal Council AI civil liability pre-consultation document (admin.ch), March 2026

### 2. nFADP — New Federal Act on Data Protection (Revised)
**Expected:** Parliamentary adoption Q3-Q6 2026; 2-year transition period after adoption
**Key provisions:** EU adequacy alignment (GDPR equivalence); cross-border data transfer regime revision; AI training data provisions (strengthened); DPO obligations expansion
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva companies with EU clients/data subjects; Geneva IO with EU data flows; Swiss-EU data transfer architecture
**Impact:** FADP compliance programs may need revision; cross-border data transfer mechanisms may need updating
**Source:** Federal Data Protection Act revision (admin.ch/ BJ), parliamentary process ongoing

### 3. Council of Europe AI Convention (Treaty of Bucharest)
**Expected:** Signature end-2025; ratification process 2026-2027; entry into force when 10 states ratify
**Key provisions:** AI governance obligations for signatories; human rights impact assessment for high-risk AI; AI transparency requirements
**Geneva operational relevance:** 15+ Geneva international organizations will be directly affected; Geneva companies with EU operations subject to similar requirements
**Impact:** AI governance policy requirements for IO/NGO; corporate AI due diligence obligations
**Source:** coe.int AI Convention; Council of Europe, Bucharest 2025

### 4. Swiss Blockchain Patent Guidelines Update (FIPO)
**Status:** In effect January 2026 (updated guidelines published)
**Key provisions:** Broader smart contract patentability; AI invention disclosure requirements; shortened examination timeline; EPO harmonization
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva tech companies; Geneva IP portfolio holders; blockchain/AI patent applicants
**Impact:** Immediate opportunity for patent gap analysis; filing urgency increased
**Source:** FIPO (admin.ch/ige), guidelines January 2026

**Status:** Scoping/analysis phase; Federal Council deliberation; no formal bill yet
**Expected timeline:** 2027-2028 for formal legislation
**Key provisions:** Risk-based AI classification (high-risk/low-risk); conformity assessment for high-risk AI; AI transparency obligations
**Geneva operational relevance:** High — Geneva tech companies; Geneva IO deploying AI; commodity trading AI tools

**Status:** In force January 2027; transitional arrangements
**Key provisions:** 72.5% RWA floor; output floor; revised credit risk standardized approach
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva private banks; Geneva financial institutions; capital planning advisory
**Strategic implication:** Regulatory reporting and capital planning advisory opportunity; distinct from legal liability focus but linked via compliance architecture

### 3. Geneva Bar AI Ethics Framework (Ongoing)

**Key provisions:** AI tool confidentiality requirements; attorney verification obligation; billing compliance; AI hallucination liability
**Geneva operational relevance:** Geneva attorneys; law firms with Geneva practitioners
**Strategic implication:** Law firm AI governance advisory as distinct service line
