# ICP Language: Cross-Vertical Synthesis
## Phase 05 — Cycle C286 | Cross-Vertical Patterns Across Legal, Finance, Consulting, Healthcare

---

## Cross-Vertical Language: What All Four ICPs Are Saying

The following language patterns appear across Geneva legal (Phase 01), Geneva finance (Phase 02), Geneva consulting (Phase 03), and Geneva healthcare (Phase 04) — not in similar form, but in identical structural position. These are the phrases that surface when senior professionals in different industries describe the same structural experience: personal accountability for AI governance they did not design and cannot operationally discharge.

---

## The Universal Articulation: "I Know What I'm Not Supposed to Do"

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "I know what I'm not supposed to do. I don't know what I am supposed to do."
> *"Je sais ce que je ne dois pas faire. Je ne sais pas ce que je dois faire."*

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "I was told the nFADP applies to AI, but nobody explained concretely what that changes for us."
> *"On m'a dit que le nFADP s'applique à l'IA, mais personne ne m'a expliqué concrètement ce que ça change pour nous."*

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "I don't know what I'm accountable for when I sign off on using AI in a client engagement."
> "I know I need to do something. I don't know what the standard is supposed to be."

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "Je reste responsable — mais concrètement, qu'est-ce que ça veut dire?"
> "Qu'est-ce qui est obligatoire en 2025 pour l'IA dans mon cabinet?"

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The awareness/competence gap is identical across all four ICPs. The professional knows the problem exists. They cannot find the solution architecture. The gap is not knowledge acquisition — it is operational discharge mechanism.

---

## The Universal Signature Problem

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "What am I actually certifying when I sign a pleading the AI helped draft?"
> "I signed off on the AI tool. I have no idea what it's actually doing with the queries."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "I'm asked to sign carrier attestations, but I don't even know what that means in practice."
> *"On me demande de signer des attestations carrier, mais je ne sais même pas ce que ça veut dire concrètement."*
> "I signed the AI attestation section because the renewal was due. Everyone signs them."

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "The insurance form asks me to sign personally that our AI review process is adequate. Adequate for what? Against what standard? I signed it because everyone signs them."
> *"Le formulaire d'assurance me demande de signer personnellement que notre processus de révision IA est adéquat. Adéquat pour quoi ? Selon quelle norme ?"*

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "The FMH says I remain responsible — but gives no specific framework for what that means operationally."
> "I signed the consent form update. I'm not sure what I was confirming."

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The personal signature as accountability mechanism has activated simultaneously across all four verticals. In each case, the professional is signing something personally — attestations, forms, carrier documents — without knowing what they are committing to. The signature creates personal liability. The standard being attested to is never defined in the document.

---

## The Universal "Where Do I Start?" Entry Signal

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "What does adequate AI governance look like for a two-person firm?"
> "If the FDPIC audits my firm, I don't know what I'd show them."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "Par où on commence ? Je n'ai même pas d'inventaire de nos outils IA."
> "Where do we start? I don't even have an inventory of our AI tools."

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "I've been running AI-assisted projects for two years. I can't tell you what documentation I'm supposed to have. Nobody can."
> "Par où commencer? Je n'ai même pas d'inventaire."

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "Par où commencer? Je ne veux pas faire d'erreur."
> "J'ai entendu parler de Konversa, Haidy, Intonate — qu'est-ce qui est sérieux?"

**Cross-vertical pattern:** "Par où on commence?" / "Where do we even start?" is the universal entry signal across all four verticals. The professional has acknowledged the problem but cannot identify the first action. No Geneva authority has published a first step. The professional is paralyzed by scope, not by complexity.

## The Universal Vendor Terms Problem

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "The tool provider's terms say the data isn't used for training. But what does that actually mean for my matters?"
> "I have no idea what happens to my AI-assisted work if the tool provider changes their terms."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "Our cloud provider informed us they have a new attestation. I'm being asked to sign a document — but I don't know what we're certifying."
> "AWS has 183 services in scope. Are all of them relevant to us?"

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "The vendor's terms were written by lawyers protecting a technology company's commercial interests. They weren't written to be compatible with your professional liability structure."
> "I signed the terms. My team uses the platform. I haven't mapped where the incompatibility is."

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "'Hosted in Switzerland' is necessary but not sufficient. Many vendors use Swiss-hosted infrastructure but route data through US-based LLM providers."
> "Vendors market 'HIPAA ready' or 'FADP compliant' — but what does that mean for a Swiss medical practice?"

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The vendor terms structural incompatibility with professional accountability architecture is universal. In each vertical, the professional has signed platform terms that serve the vendor's commercial model, not the professional's liability structure. No vertical has mapped the specific incompatibility points.

## The Universal Professional Body Vacuum

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "The SVA hasn't said anything specific. The Geneva bar hasn't either. So I'm waiting."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "My SRO is supposed to supervise me. But they haven't said a word about AI governance."
> "I called my SRO and asked about FINMA 08/2024. They said they hadn't looked into it yet."

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "There's no equivalent to what lawyers have with their bar. We're just peers talking to each other."
> *"Il n'existe pas d'équivalent à ce que les avocats ont avec leur barreau."*

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "FMH says you remain responsible. But what does that actually mean operationally? Nobody's published the specific framework."

**Cross-vertical pattern:** All four professional bodies — Geneva bar, SROs, consulting associations, FMH — have issued principle-level guidance (remain responsible, ensure compliance) without operational content. No body has defined what adequate AI governance means for its members. The vacuum is simultaneous, not sequential.

## The Universal nFADP Exposure Discovery

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "The nFADP clock started when I first used a cloud AI tool. I didn't notice."
> "I didn't know I needed a legal basis. The tool provider said the data wasn't used for training. I assumed that was the end of the question."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "I learned that the nFADP has criminal liability for executives. Is that real? Could I personally face fines?"
> "Art. 63 nFADP — I keep seeing this reference. What does it actually mean for me as managing director?"

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "Your AI tool vendor is a third-party data processor under nFADP. Your engagement letters probably don't authorize the specific processing your vendor is doing. This has been true for 2-3 years."

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "The nFADP came into force September 1, 2023. Most small practices don't know what it requires for AI-assisted documentation."
> "Est-ce que je dois faire une analyse d'impact pour mes outils IA? DSFA — c'est quoi exactement?"

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Art. 63 nFADP personal criminal liability is being discovered simultaneously across all four verticals. The obligation activated in 2023 when nFADP came into force. Most professionals in all four verticals are still in a pre-awareness state about obligations that have been running for years.

## The Universal Client/Patient Expectation Gap

**Legal (Phase 01):**
> "Clients are starting to ask what AI tools I use. I don't know what to tell them."
> "My client is a bank. They have more AI governance policy than I do."

**Finance (Phase 02):**
> "A US client sent us a 40-page questionnaire on AI. We didn't know what to answer."
> "I need to prove to a family office that we control our AI systems — but we don't even have an inventory."

**Consulting (Phase 03):**
> "Clients are starting to ask AI questions in every proposal discussion. Not because they understand AI governance — but because their own boards are asking them."
> "We've had three clients in the last six months ask for some kind of AI methodology documentation. We don't have it."

**Healthcare (Phase 04):**
> "A patient asks about the AI tool being used: 'Est-ce que mes données sont protégées?'"
> "The practice manager flags that current software is updating with AI features they haven't evaluated."

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Sophisticated counterparties (clients, patients, institutional investors) are asking AI governance questions before the professional has developed answers. The expectation gap is not future anxiety — it is present-tense active exposure in every active relationship.

## French-Language Cross-Vertical Register

The French register is consistent across all four verticals:

- *"Je sais ce que je ne dois pas faire. Je ne sais pas ce que je dois faire."* — universal
- *"Par où on commence ?"* — universal entry signal
- *"Je ne savais pas ce à quoi je m'engageais."* — universal signature discovery
- *"Le compteur nFADP a commencé quand j'ai utilisé l'outil. Je ne m'en suis pas aperçu."* — universal clock discovery
- *"Mon bareau / mon SRO / mon association professionnelle n'a rien dit sur l'IA."* — universal institutional vacuum
- *"Les conditions du fournisseur ne sont pas conçues pour ma responsabilité professionnelle."* — universal vendor terms mismatch
- *"Mon client / patient m'a posé une question à laquelle je n'avais pas de réponse."* — universal counterpart expectation gap

## Key Insight

The same nine language patterns appear across all four ICPs in identical structural positions. This is not parallel development — it is the same structural condition producing the same articulable experience across industries that share Geneva as a professional context.

The common denominator: senior professionals in Geneva — managing partners, managing directors, senior physicians — are personally accountable for AI governance frameworks that no Geneva authority has defined, using tools whose terms were not designed for their liability architecture, measured by standards that are being defined after the fact through carrier questionnaires and client questionnaires, while the documentation window closes.
