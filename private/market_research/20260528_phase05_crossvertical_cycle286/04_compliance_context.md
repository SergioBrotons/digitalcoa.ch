# Compliance Context: Cross-Vertical Synthesis
## Phase 05 — Cycle C286 | Regulatory Patterns Across Legal, Finance, Consulting, and Healthcare

---

## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Framework

The following six regulatory mechanisms affect all four Geneva verticals identically. They are not parallel obligations that happen to look similar — they are the same mechanisms operating in the same regulatory ecosystem, producing the same accountability exposure across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare. The cross-vertical pattern is only visible from synthesis. No single-vertical analysis captures it.

---

## Mechanism 1: nFADP — In Force Since September 2023, Still Not Operationalized

**Status:** In force. FDPIC confirmed May 2025: nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing without requiring separate legislation.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** Client matter data processed through AI research tools triggers Art.6 lawfulness requirement. Most firms have not identified their legal basis for AI processing of client communications. Art.9 third-party processor obligations apply when law firms use AI vendors processing client data.

**Finance:** Client portfolio data, financial information, and personal circumstances processed through AI wealth management tools. nFADP Art.8 profiling notification rights may be triggered when AI systems draw inferences about client financial situations. Art.15/21 automated decision explanation obligations apply to AI-assisted investment recommendations.

**Consulting:** Client business data, project information, and organizational analysis processed through AI tools. Art.22 register of processing activities should exist. Most consulting boutiques have no AI processing register and cannot describe what they are processing through which vendor.

**Healthcare:** Patient data processed through AI diagnosis support, practice management, or administrative tools. nFADP applies to patient health data with heightened sensitivity rules. Art.6 lawfulness basis requires explicit authorization for health data AI processing.

**The common structural property:** nFADP has been in force for over two years. None of the four verticals have built the processing registers, lawfulness assessments, or automated decision explanation protocols it requires. The obligation exists now. The compliance infrastructure does not exist in any of the four verticals.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** nFADP applies to all four verticals identically. The FDPIC has provided no sector-specific guidance for any of them. The documentation window between now and the Federal Council's AI consultation draft (expected end 2026) is the only period when proportionate nFADP compliance architecture can be built with evidence of anticipatory compliance intent.

## Mechanism 2: CoE AI Convention — Ratified by Switzerland February 2025, Private Law Obligations Active

**Status:** Ratified by Switzerland February 27, 2025. Consultation draft implementation bill expected end 2026.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** Convention Art.5 obligations on "high-risk AI systems" apply when AI systems support legal decisions with significant effects on clients. Engagement letters — which predate AI adoption in every active matter — may already be binding attorneys to AI governance standards the Convention requires.

**Finance:** AI-assisted investment recommendations, wealth management decisions, and financial planning outputs trigger Convention obligations on high-risk AI systems. Client contracts containing no AI methodology language may be failing Convention requirements without the firm's awareness.

**Consulting:** AI-assisted analytical conclusions, strategic recommendations, and organizational assessments in client deliverables may constitute AI systems producing legally significant effects. The Convention's private law dimension activates through existing mandate contracts, which are uniformly AI-silent.

**Healthcare:** AI diagnosis support systems are explicitly enumerated in Convention Annex III as high-risk. Physician's personal liability for AI-assisted clinical decisions activates through professional conduct obligations and patient injury claims, both of which the Convention shapes through its accountability requirements.

**The common structural property:** The AI Convention creates present-tense obligations through existing client contracts and professional conduct frameworks — before implementation legislation arrives. The obligations are enforceable now. The professional bodies in all four verticals have provided no guidance on what the Convention requires. The professional is exposed without knowing what they are exposed to.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** Convention obligations flow through existing contracts. Every active client engagement in every vertical is governed by contracts that predate the Convention's ratification. The gap between what those contracts require and what AI-assisted delivery practice actually involves is active exposure in every vertical simultaneously.

## Mechanism 3: Carrier Attestation Evolution — Three Generations in 24 Months

**Status:** Professional liability carriers iteratively updating AI-specific questionnaire sections. Three generations observable.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** PI insurer questionnaires asking attorneys to confirm AI review methodology, document what AI tools are used, and demonstrate human oversight of AI-assisted work. Third-generation questions now require performance evidence.

**Finance:** Wealth manager carriers requesting ISAE 3000 Type II attestation documentation from AI service providers, then asking managing directors to confirm what governance review the managing director performed on the provider's attestation. The carrier's attestation and the MD's governance are not the same document.

**Consulting:** Third-generation carrier questionnaires requiring MD personal signature on attestation that AI review methodology is "adequate." Adequacy standard undefined. Personal signature creates personal liability. No Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of what the MD commits to.

**Healthcare:** Medical practice liability carriers including AI-specific questions in renewal forms. FMH guidance states physicians remain responsible. Carrier questionnaires are beginning to ask physicians to document what AI systems are used, what training staff have received, and what oversight protocols exist.

**The common structural property:** Each renewal cycle reveals a new generation of carrier expectations. The standard is moving through carrier iteration, not through published regulatory doctrine. Professionals in all four verticals are being evaluated against standards they cannot see forming, because the standard is forming inside carrier underwriting processes not published for public review.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** Carrier questionnaire iteration pattern tracking is a specific intelligence function no Geneva market participant is offering as a service. The pattern reveals where the standard is moving before any published guidance. The professional who tracks the pattern can anticipate the next generation of questions. The professional who doesn't track it discovers the new standard only when it appears in the renewal form.

## Mechanism 4: Professional Body Vacuum — Simultaneous and Structural

**Status:** Geneva Bar, SROs, consulting associations, and FMH have all issued principle-level guidance without operational content.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** The Geneva Bar states attorneys remain responsible for AI-assisted work. No operational framework for what "responsible" means for a 2-5 attorney firm with no compliance function.

**Finance:** SROs (self-regulatory organizations) supervise member firms. Zero AI governance guidance published for any SRO in Geneva. Members are uncertain whether SRO silence means they are adequately supervised or inadequately unsupervised.

**Consulting:** No equivalent to the legal bar's guidance. The Swiss Association (SVA) has not published consulting-specific AI governance guidance. The peer circuit provides social reassurance without substantive calibration.

**Healthcare:** FMH states physicians remain professionally accountable for AI-assisted clinical decisions. No FMH-published decision tree, checklist, or minimum viable protocol for what "accountable" means in an independent practice context.

**The common structural property:** All four professional bodies are managing their own institutional exposure by stating the accountability principle without defining the operational content. The professional is in the same position after reading the guidance as before. The vacuum is not an oversight — it is the consistent behavior of professional bodies that face less exposure from saying something vague than from saying something specific that later proves inadequate.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** The professional body vacuum is structurally permanent in the near term. The Federal Council's Federal AI Coordination Office has been given a contact-point function, not a standard-setting mandate. No Geneva professional body has the institutional incentive to publish specific AI governance standards for boutique-scale members. The vacuum will not be filled by professional body guidance in time to matter.

## Mechanism 5: FINMA 08/2024 Influence Migrating Into Non-FINMA Verticals

**Status:** FINMA Guidance 08/2024 published for supervised institutions. Influence extending into professional services, consulting, and healthcare through carrier underwriting standards.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** FINMA's AI inventory, governance framework, and human oversight expectations are beginning to appear in law firm PI carrier questionnaires through cross-pollination — carriers use similar questions across sectors.

**Finance:** For supervised institutions: the primary AI governance regulatory reference. For non-supervised boutiques: the standard that carriers are importing into their own questionnaires as proxy for "adequate AI governance."

**Consulting:** Consulting firm MDs with financial services clients face FINMA-influenced client AI governance expectations in addition to carrier attestation requirements. The compounding effect creates documentation requirements from multiple sources simultaneously.

**Healthcare:** Physicians with institutional client relationships (hospital affiliations, insurance contracts) may face FINMA-influenced AI governance expectations in those relationships, separate from FMH obligations.

**The common structural property:** FINMA's AI guidance has溢出效应 (overflow effect) into non-FINMA supervised sectors through carrier questionnaires and client counterparty expectations. The supervised sector's regulatory standard is becoming the de facto standard for the unsupervised sector through market mechanisms, not through regulatory extension.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** Geneva professionals who assume FINMA guidance doesn't apply to them because they are not FINMA-supervised are operating under a false assumption. The guidance's influence is migrating into their carrier questionnaires and client relationships regardless of their supervised status.

## Mechanism 6: Documentation Window Close — Burden-of-Proof Reversal

**Status:** Federal Council AI consultation draft expected end 2026. The window between now and then is a burden-of-proof exercise, not a compliance deadline.

**Cross-vertical evidence:**

**Legal:** "I know I should document. But what? The bar hasn't said. The FDPIC hasn't said. I'll wait." Waiting means the documentation built after the consultation draft arrives cannot recreate the evidence of what the attorney determined adequate during the window, and why.

**Finance:** "FINMA 08/2024 is clear that AI governance needs documentation. But there's no template for a boutique wealth manager. I'm building something from scratch." Building from scratch during the window is evidence of deliberateness. Waiting until the standard arrives and then building to specification is evidence of reactive compliance.

**Consulting:** "The documentation window is not a compliance deadline. It's a positioning exercise. What you document during it is evidence of what you thought and why." The evidence is only creatable during the window. After it closes, new documentation cannot retroactively recreate the reasoning.

**Healthcare:** "Qu'est-ce qui est obligatoire? Nobody's published the checklist. I don't know what to build." Building something proportionate during the window creates evidence of anticipatory compliance intent. Waiting until the checklist arrives creates evidence of reactive compliance behavior.

**The common structural property:** Before the consultation draft arrives, documentation built is evaluated against forming standards — the professional's judgment is part of the standard-forming process. After the draft arrives, documentation is evaluated against arrived standards — the professional is explaining their compliance with standards others set without their input. The evidence generated during the window is irreplaceable. It cannot be created retroactively.

**The specific cross-vertical insight:** The documentation window is the single most valuable positioning opportunity available to Geneva professionals in all four verticals simultaneously. The cost of building proportionate documentation during the window is lower than the cost of explaining its absence after the window closes. No other regulatory timing event in the near term offers this asymmetry.
