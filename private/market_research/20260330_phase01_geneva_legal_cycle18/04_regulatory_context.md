## Regulatory Framework for Geneva Small Law Firms

### Active Regulations (Now)

#### 1. nFADP (Swiss Federal Data Protection Act) — Art. 5(c) ACTIVE
- **Status:** In force
- **Application:** Applies directly to AI-supported data processing (FDPIC homepage Sep 24 2025 confirmation)
- **Penalty architecture:** Criminal prosecution route only — no direct fines under nFADP for data protection violations; criminal liability for unlawful data processing
- **Obligation:** Law firms processing client personal data through AI tools must ensure lawful basis, data minimisation, purpose limitation
- **DPIA:** Required for high-risk AI processing under nFADP Art. 22
- **Confidence:** High

#### 2. EU AI Act — GPAI Obligations ACTIVE SINCE AUG 2 2025
- **Status:** Operative since Aug 2 2025 (~8.5 months)
- **Application:** GPAI system providers and distributors — applies to Geneva law firms that:
  - Place AI systems on the EU market
  - Operate AI systems with EU-intended output
  - Act as EU importer/distributor of AI systems
- **Obligation:** Transparency obligations for GPAI systems; high-risk AI system obligations for systems affecting EU data subjects
- **Confidence:** High

#### 3. Federal Council Two Timelines (Confirmed Cycle 16)
- **Timeline 1 — Federal Council formal process:** End-2026 sector-specific approach (NO cross-sector Swiss AI law before formal process)
- **Timeline 2 — Multi-stakeholder First Draft:** May 2026 (~2 months away) — Federal Council AI Action Plan First Draft
- **Confidence:** High

#### 4. FDPIC Enforcement Trajectory
- **Status:** Building AI enforcement portfolio
- **Confirmed cases:** Transport company (Feb 2026), joint statement (Feb 2026), X/Twitter (Mar 2025), Coop (Oct 2025)
- **Law firms:** NOT yet targeted — positive timing signal
- **Penalty architecture:** Criminal prosecution only (no direct fines under nFADP)
- **Confidence:** High

### Pending Regulations

- **Focus areas:** Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- **Confidence:** Medium (government source)

#### 6. ANBLES/OFL Geneva — No New Guidance (February 2026)
