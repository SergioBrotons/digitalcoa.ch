## Section 3: What Data Is Being Processed?

**Client data through AI = nFADP obligations.**

Personal data processed through AI tools triggers:
- Lawful basis requirement (nFADP Art. 6)
- Data minimisation (Art. 5(1)(c))
- Purpose limitation (Art. 5(1)(b))
- DPIA requirement for high-risk processing (Art. 22)

Client case files, contracts, personal identification information, financial data — all subject to nFADP when processed through AI systems.

**The DPIA Question:** Have you conducted a Data Protection Impact Assessment for your AI tools?

## Section 4: The EU AI Act — Are You Affected?

**Geneva attorneys with EU clients or operations face active obligations.**

The EU AI Act's GPAI obligations have been operative since **August 2, 2025** (~8.5 months).

**You may be affected if you:**
- Use AI systems placed on the EU market
- Operate AI systems intended for EU output
- Act as importer/distributor of AI systems in the EU
- Serve EU-based clients with AI-generated work product

**Obligations if affected:**
- Transparency for AI-generated content (disclose when content is AI-generated)
- Technical documentation for GPAI systems
- Copyright compliance for training data
- Systemic risk monitoring

**The Geneva Reality:** The Federal Council's "lean approach" means Switzerland will not rush a cross-sector AI law. But Geneva attorneys serving EU clients face EU obligations now.

## Section 9: The nFADP DPIA Framework for AI Tools

**What a Geneva law firm DPIA actually looks like:**

**Step 1: Describe the Processing**
- AI tool name and purpose
- Types of personal data processed
- Data subjects (clients, counterparties, third parties)
- Data flows (input, processing, output, storage)

**Step 2: Assess Necessity and Proportionality**
- Is the AI tool necessary for the service?
- Could a less invasive alternative achieve the same result?
- Is data minimisation respected?

**Step 3: Identify and Assess Risks**
- Risk: Unlawful processing of client confidential data
- Risk: AI-generated errors in legal analysis
- Risk: Third-party data breach through AI vendor
- Risk: Non-compliance with EU AI Act if EU nexus

**Step 4: Measures to Address Risks**
- Technical: encryption, access controls, vendor DPAs
- Organisational: AI usage policy, training, incident response
- Monitoring: regular DPIA review, vendor compliance monitoring

**Step 5: Consultation and Documentation**
- DPO consultation (if appointed)
- Documentation retained for FDPIC review
- Annual DPIA review cycle
