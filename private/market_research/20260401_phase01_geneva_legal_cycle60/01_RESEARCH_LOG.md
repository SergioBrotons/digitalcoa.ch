## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings (Cycle 60 — Fresh Sources)

### 1. Federal Council Rejects "Swiss AI Act" — February 12, 2025 (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Homburger.ch (primary Swiss law firm research)

The Federal Council decided on **February 12, 2025** that Switzerland will NOT implement broad, horizontal AI regulation — there will be no "Swiss AI Act." Instead, the approach is three-pronged:
1. **Strengthen Switzerland as a location for innovation**
2. **Ensure protection of fundamental rights, including economic freedom**
3. **Ensure compatibility with main trading partners, especially the EU** — explicitly to avoid any "Swiss finish" (additional regulatory burden vs. EU)

This is the clearest official statement of intent on Swiss AI regulation direction. It confirms that the regulatory approach will remain sector-specific, building on existing laws.

**Legal sector implications (NET-NEW C60):** Geneva law firms advising clients on AI cannot point to a single Swiss AI statute. Instead, they must navigate a layered stack: FADP + Federal AI Ordinance + SBA professional obligations + CoE AI Convention adaptation + EU AI Act extraterritorial reach. This complexity is the product — the more regulatory layers, the more need for expert legal guidance.

### 2. EU AI Act GPAI Obligations Already In Force Since August 2, 2025 (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (major Geneva law firm)

Contrary to what some Swiss firms may believe, **GPAI obligations are NOT waiting for August 2026** — the following have been in force since **August 2, 2025:**
- GPAI provider obligations (technical documentation, transparency on copyrighted training data, design to prevent illegal content generation)
- GPAI EU market presence requirement (foreign providers must appoint EU-based authorized representative)
- Governance framework (EU AI Office, AI Board operational)
- Penalties up to EUR 35M or 7% of worldwide annual turnover (in force since August 2025)

**What arrives August 2, 2026 (~4 months away):** High-risk AI conformity assessments, technical documentation, data governance, human oversight, cybersecurity requirements.

**Legal sector implications (NET-NEW C60):** Geneva law firms using GPAI models (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) for client work or internal operations have been subject to EU AI Act obligations since August 2025. If they have EU-based clients or use EU-facing AI tools, they may already be in breach of EU AI Act requirements. This is an urgent advisory opportunity — law firms may not know they are already subject to these obligations.

### 3. EU AI Act Extraterritorial Application to Swiss Law Firms — Precisely Defined (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin

The EU AI Act applies to Swiss companies in two scenarios:
- **Placing AI systems/GPAI models on the EU market** — includes offers on websites, platforms, or services directed at the EU (signaled by EUR pricing, local delivery options, targeted advertising, or commercial availability to EU users)
- **Using AI output within the EU** — even if the AI system operates entirely outside the EU (e.g., hosted on Swiss servers), if the output is used in EU business decisions, the EU AI Act applies

**Legal sector implications (NET-NEW C60):** A Geneva law firm that uses AI to draft contracts, conduct due diligence, or produce legal opinions for a client who then uses those outputs within the EU may trigger EU AI Act obligations. The "placing on the market" concept is broader than many Swiss lawyers realize.

### 4. CoE AI Convention Implementation — Lead Department and Industry-Specific Solutions (NET-NEW C60)
**Source:** Homburger.ch

Implementation details confirmed:
- **Federal Department of Justice and Police (FDJP)** is leading the drafting
- **Public consultation draft: End of 2026** (~9 months away)
- Non-binding measures include: **self-declaration agreements** and **industry-specific solutions**
- Expected proposal components: stricter transparency requirements, mandatory AI-generated content labeling, political advertising rules on social media, data protection revisions to prevent AI-driven surveillance, potential DPIA extension
- **Key sectors targeted:** finance, healthcare, digital platforms

**Legal sector implications (NET-NEW C60):** The industry-specific solutions pathway is significant — it means sector-specific AI governance frameworks will emerge, likely first in finance and healthcare. Legal advisors in those sectors should track FDJP developments closely. The 9-month window to consultation draft means Geneva law firms have limited time to provide input to the consultation process.

### 5. August 2, 2026 High-Risk AI Deadline — 4 Months Away (NET-NEW C60 Detail)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin

The August 2, 2026 date now carries additional weight: it is not only the EU AI Act high-risk AI deadline but also the date when **grandfathering rules for legacy GPAI models expire** and the full expanded high-risk AI regulatory scope takes effect. The final provisions of the EU AI Act enter August 2, 2027.

**Legal sector implications:** The combined August 2026/2027 timeline creates a rolling compliance window. Geneva law firms advising EU clients on AI deployment must track both the domestic Swiss regulatory layer and the EU AI Act compliance timeline simultaneously.
