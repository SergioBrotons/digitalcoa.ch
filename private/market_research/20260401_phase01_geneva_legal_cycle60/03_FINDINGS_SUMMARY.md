## ⭐ NET-NEW Findings Summary (C60 vs. Prior Cycles)

### Finding 1: Federal Council Rejects "Swiss AI Act" — February 12, 2025
**What changed:** The Federal Council made the most explicit statement yet: no broad horizontal AI regulation is coming. The approach is sector-specific, building on existing laws, with the explicit goal of avoiding "Swiss finish" (additional burden beyond EU standards).

**Why it matters:** Swiss law firms cannot wait for a single "Swiss AI Act" to understand their obligations. The regulatory stack is now definitively multi-source: FADP + Federal AI Ordinance + SBA + EU AI Act (extraterritorial) + CoE AI Convention. This complexity is the product.

**Source:** Homburger.ch (major Swiss law firm, primary source quality)

---

### Finding 2: EU AI Act GPAI Obligations Already In Force Since August 2, 2025
**What changed:** Contrary to common assumption, GPAI obligations are NOT waiting for August 2026. Since August 2, 2025: transparency on copyrighted training data, GPAI model EU market presence requirements (EU authorized representative mandatory for non-EU providers), and penalties up to EUR 35M have been in force.

**Why it matters:** A Geneva law firm using any major AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.) for client-facing work may already be within scope of EU AI Act obligations if any aspect of their practice touches the EU market. This is an immediate compliance gap, not a future one.

**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (major Geneva law firm, primary source quality)

---

### Finding 3: EU AI Act Extraterritorial Application Precisely Defined
**What changed:** The "placing on the market" concept is broader than most Swiss lawyers realize. It includes any commercial availability to EU users signaled by EUR pricing, EU-directed advertising, or EU-facing services. The "output used in EU" pathway means even server-side Swiss AI processing for EU client decisions triggers EU AI Act.

**Why it matters:** Geneva law firms with any international practice (especially US-Switzerland-EU triangle) need an EU AI Act exposure assessment now, not as a future planning exercise.

**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin

---

### Finding 4: CoE AI Convention Implementation — Lead Department and Industry-Specific Solutions
**What changed:** Federal Department of Justice and Police (FDJP) leading the implementation. Public consultation draft end of 2026 (~9 months). Non-binding measures include self-declaration agreements and industry-specific solutions — meaning sector bodies (legal, finance, healthcare) may get their own AI governance frameworks.

**Why it matters:** The Geneva legal profession (via SBA or Geneva Bar) could develop its own AI governance self-declaration framework before the FDJP consultation. This is a positioning opportunity for proactive legal associations — and a signal that law firms should engage with their professional bodies now.

**Source:** Homburger.ch

---

### Finding 5: August 2, 2026 Is a Double Deadline
**What changed:** August 2, 2026 is not only the high-risk AI conformity assessment deadline — it is also when grandfathering for legacy GPAI models expires and the full expanded EU AI Act scope takes effect. August 2, 2027 brings the final provisions.

**Why it matters:** The compliance horizon extends to 2027. Geneva law firms need a multi-year AI governance roadmap, not a single-action plan.

**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin

---

### Finding 6: Federal Council's Three Strategic Pillars Define Swiss AI Posture
**What changed:** The Federal Council's three stated objectives — innovation location, fundamental rights protection, EU compatibility — explicitly frame Switzerland as a jurisdiction that will not gold-plate AI regulation. This is a competitive positioning statement as much as a regulatory one.

**Why it matters:** The implication for law firms: Swiss clients (especially those with EU exposure) face a dual compliance burden — Swiss law flexibility PLUS EU AI Act extraterritorial requirements. The lawyer who can navigate both is worth more than the lawyer who can explain either alone.

**Source:** Homburger.ch
