## Problem Statement

Geneva small and mid-size law firms face a widening gap between AI adoption and AI governance. Survey data shows 40% already using AI tools, yet only 12% have written governance policies. As of April 2026, this gap is now a liability problem — not just an operational one.

The regulatory stack has become too complex to navigate informally:
- **FADP Art. 21** — Automated decision-making obligations
- **Federal AI Ordinance** — In force since January 1, 2025 (15+ months)
- **SBA AI Guidelines** — June 2024 dual professional obligation
- **EU AI Act** — GPAI obligations in force since August 2, 2025; high-risk AI conformity assessments due August 2, 2026 (~4 months)
- **CoE AI Convention** — Swiss implementation consultation draft end of 2026
- **Section 301** — US trade policy AI implications; written comments due April 15, 2026 (14 days)

A law firm that does not have a documented AI governance framework faces:
1. FADP non-compliance liability (up to CHF 250,000; applies to responsible individuals)
2. Professional liability exposure for AI-assisted work
3. EU AI Act extraterritorial exposure if any EU market involvement
4. Malpractice insurer scrutiny (already flagged by Swiss insurers)

## Content Sections

### Section 1: The Five Regulatory Deadlines Affecting Geneva Law Firms (April 2026)
1. **FADP Art. 21** — In force; automated decision obligations
2. **Federal AI Ordinance** — 15+ months operative
3. **EU AI Act GPAI** — Already in force since August 2, 2025
4. **EU AI Act High-Risk** — Conformity assessments due August 2, 2026 (~4 months)
5. **Section 301** — Written comments deadline April 15, 2026 (14 days)

### Section 2: The Geneva Law Firm AI Governance Audit (5 Questions)
Self-assessment checklist:
- Q1: Have we documented which AI tools are used, by whom, and for what client tasks?
- Q2: Do we have written client consent protocols for AI-assisted work?
- Q3: Do our AI vendor contracts include data processing agreements with Swiss/EU hosting guarantees?
- Q4: Have we conducted a FADP DPIA for our AI-assisted workflows?
- Q5: Do we have an EU AI Act exposure assessment (even if we think we don't)?

### Section 3: The "Envelope vs. Letter" Framework for Safe AI Adoption
Five safe workflows:
1. Internal research and knowledge management (envelope)
2. Draft review and redlining (envelope, human-in-loop)
3. Contract template population with human review (letter, requiring disclosure)
4. Due diligence checklists with human sign-off (letter, full disclosure)
5. Client communication draft with review (envelope)

### Section 4: The August 2, 2026 EU AI Act Preparedness Checklist
What law firms need to do in the next 4 months:
- [ ] Inventory all AI tools used in practice
- [ ] Classify AI use cases by risk level
- [ ] Prepare technical documentation for high-risk AI uses
- [ ] Appoint EU authorized representative if placing anything on EU market
- [ ] Establish human oversight protocols for AI-assisted decisions
- [ ] Update client engagement letters for AI disclosure
- [ ] Review AI vendor contracts for EU AI Act compliance clauses

### Section 5: What Geneva Law Firms Should Do This Month
Priority actions ranked by urgency:
1. **This week (before April 15):** Review Section 301 exposure in trade advisory practice
2. **This month:** Complete FADP DPIA for AI-assisted workflows
3. **This quarter:** Establish written AI governance policy (address the 12% gap)
4. **Before August 2:** Complete EU AI Act exposure and preparedness assessment
