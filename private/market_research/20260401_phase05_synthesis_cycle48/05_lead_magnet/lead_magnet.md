## Opening Hook

Two-thirds of Swiss SMEs are already using AI tools.

Only one-third have internal rules governing how those tools are used.

This is not a technology story. It is not a skills story. It is a governance story.

Every Geneva business — legal, financial, consulting, medical, real estate — is facing the same moment: AI tools are already deployed, governance frameworks are not, and the enforcement moment is approaching across every sector simultaneously.

The question is not whether your industry will face AI governance scrutiny. The question is whether your business will have the framework in place when it arrives.

The AXA KMU-Arbeitsmarktstudie 2025 confirms what the market data across all sectors suggests: the governance gap is universal, not sector-specific.

- **Consulting**: 73% of AI projects abandoned — not because the technology failed, but because governance was not in place to move from pilot to production.

Across all six sectors, the pattern is the same: AI adoption is near-universal or accelerating. Governance frameworks are the exception, not the rule. The regulatory timelines are converging.

## The Convergence: Six Timelines Running Simultaneously

**Timeline 1 — FADP: Already Operative (All Sectors)**
The revised Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP), in force since September 1, 2023, applies to every Swiss business processing personal data through AI tools. This is every business. The FDPIC confirmed FADP applies to AI in May 2025 guidance. The obligations are not future obligations — they are already operative. Criminal penalties up to CHF 250,000 for individuals.

**Timeline 2 — Sector-Specific Deadlines (Varying Urgency)**

| Sector | Deadline | Urgency |

**Timeline 3 — Market Professionalization (12–18 Months)**
The Swiss AI governance market is currently fragmented and undersupplied with advisory services. In 12–18 months — after the first wave of enforcement actions and regulatory clarity — the market will professionalize. The first-mover advantage in advisory credibility is available now, before that professionalization.

## The Three-Layer Liability Stack (All Sectors)

Every business using AI carries three layers of personal exposure:

**Layer 1 — FADP Compliance**
Already operative across all sectors. Art. 5(c), Art. 16, Art. 21, DPIA, Privacy by Design — all already in force. Criminal penalties up to CHF 250,000.

**Layer 2 — Professional Liability**
Sector-specific: lawyers (FINMA-licensed advisors for wealth managers, physicians in healthcare, property professionals in real estate). The licensed professional bears personal responsibility for work product — AI-assisted or not.

**Layer 3 — Sector-Specific Regulatory Exposure**
- Legal: Art. 21 FADP right to human review for automated legal decisions
- Wealth Management: FINMA personal license accountability for AI-assisted recommendations
- Consulting: GPAI product liability for EU-nexus firms
- Healthcare: Swissdamed compliance + clinical malpractice exposure
- Real Estate: EU AI Act Annex III (property valuation AI likely high-risk)

## The Envelope Test: Universal Framework for Every Business

Before any formal policy work, every business can apply the Envelope Test:

**Safe (low risk)**: Scheduling, formatting, routing, document template population, billing preparation, data aggregation for reporting

**Not safe without DPA and DPIA (high risk)**: Any AI processing that reads, interprets, analyzes, summarizes, or generates content based on personal data, client information, financial data, patient data, or proprietary business information

The Envelope Test is the same across all six sectors. The governance framework is sector-specific; the starting filter is universal.

## A Cross-Sector Minimum Viable Framework

1. **AI Tool Inventory with Envelope Test**: Document every AI tool in use across the business; apply the envelope/letter filter
2. **Data Processing Agreement Register**: DPAs with all vendors processing personal data; confirm Swiss or EU residency
3. **Sector-Specific Regulatory Assessment**: Identify which sector timelines apply to your business (Swissdamed, CARF/CRS, GPAI, EU AI Act)
4. **Human Accountability Chain**: Named individuals responsible for each AI tool's outputs — sector-licensed where applicable
5. **Incident Response Procedure**: What happens when AI produces an error, a data breach, or a client complaint about an AI decision?
