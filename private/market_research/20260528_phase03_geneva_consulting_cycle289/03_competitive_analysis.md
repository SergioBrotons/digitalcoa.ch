# 03 Competitive Analysis — Phase 03 C289

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing

---

## What Exists

### General AI Governance Consultancies

**Global AI governance frameworks** — ISO 42001, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act compliance frameworks. Designed for large organizations with dedicated compliance infrastructure. Do not address Geneva consulting specific accountability structures (carrier attestation, client questionnaires, sub-consultant delegation chains). Not calibrated to small boutique scale or Swiss professional liability doctrine.

**Data protection consultancies** — Focus on nFADP compliance as general data protection obligation. Address data processing, consent, DSARs. Do not address consulting-specific accountability questions (proposal methodology defensibility, client questionnaire portfolio management, sub-consultant delegation). Not connected to carrier attestation or professional liability.

**Legal technology consultants** — Address AI tool selection and implementation. Focus on vendor evaluation, contract review, data residency. Do not address ongoing accountability obligations once tools are deployed, or the specific carrier/client questionnaire accountability moment.

### Swiss Market

**SRO frameworks** —Exist for financial sector (FINMA-supervised institutions). Provide safe harbor documentation for AI governance in finance. Do not exist for consulting sector.

**Bar association guidance (ORDRE)** — Geneva Bar provides ethical guidance for attorneys. No Geneva-specific AI governance guidance for legal practices, and the bar itself has signaled limitations in providing AI-specific guidance to members.

**SVA (Swiss Association of Actuaries)** — Professional body for consulting actuaries. Does not cover general management consulting. No AI governance framework for consulting sector members.

**Carrier-provided AI governance questionnaires** — Function as the de facto operative standard for Geneva consulting AI governance. Designed for underwriting risk evaluation, not for professional liability protection. No Geneva consulting input into their formation.

## What's Missing

### Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review

**Missing:** No Geneva market service offering independent review of carrier third-generation attestation from the signatory's personal liability perspective.

**What it would do:** Analyze what the MD commits to personally when signing. Map commitments to Geneva consulting-specific adequacy standards. Identify gaps between current firm practice and what the attestation requires. Provide documented basis for either signing with confidence or negotiating amendments before signature.

**Why it doesn't exist:** Carrier attestation is designed as a one-directional instrument. No Geneva service has positioned itself as representing the signatory's interest against the carrier's document. The market gap is structural.

**Why it matters now:** Third-generation attestations are creating personal liability exposure for MDs who signed without understanding what they were committing to. The exposure is already present. The service gap is already acute.

---

### Client Questionnaire Portfolio Management

**Missing:** No Geneva market service helping boutique consulting firms manage accumulated client AI governance questionnaire commitments across their active client portfolio.

**What it would do:** Track what the firm has committed to across all client questionnaire responses. Identify inconsistencies between commitments made to different clients. Build portfolio-level accountability coherence. Prepare the firm for cross-client comparison or audit scenarios.

**Why it doesn't exist:** Client questionnaires are experienced as one-off events, not as portfolio-forming commitments. The market has not framed questionnaire responses as a firm-level accountability asset requiring systematic management.

**Why it matters now:** Client AI governance questionnaires are the primary accountability formation mechanism in the Geneva consulting market. Each response creates commitments that accumulate into a firm-wide accountability record. Without portfolio management, the firm cannot reconstruct what it has committed to.

---

### Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Authorization Architecture

**Missing:** No Geneva market service designing engagement letter architecture for AI-delegated consulting work that addresses nFADP Art.9 processor flows, authorization protocols, and accountability chain mapping.

**What it would do:** Audit existing sub-consultant relationships for AI delegation exposure. Design engagement letter amendments that define authorization requirements, AI tool constraints, and processor chain documentation obligations. Map nFADP Art.9 flows across the three-party chain (client → prime boutique → sub-consultant → AI tool).

**Why it doesn't exist:** Sub-consultant AI delegation is treated as a commercial contracting question, not an AI governance question. nFADP Art.9 processor flows are not commonly understood in the consulting sector as applying to professional services delivery chains.

**Why it matters now:** Prime boutiques are contractually and professionally accountable for sub-consultant deliverables. When a client raises an AI governance question about sub-consultant work, the MD cannot answer because the delegation chain was never authorized or documented.

---

### Proposal Defensibility Architecture

**Missing:** No Geneva market service helping boutique consulting firms build AI-assisted proposal defensibility — documentation that demonstrates methodology construction when clients probe.

**What it would do:** Build internal protocol for AI-assisted proposal production that creates documentation trail from prompt through final deliverable. Define what defensibility documentation consists of for Geneva consulting proposals. Train MDs and senior consultants on documentation requirements for AI-assisted work.

**Why it doesn't exist:** Proposal defensibility is not experienced as an accountability category in the Geneva consulting market. Proposals are evaluated on quality and strategic fit, not on methodological provenance. The market has not connected AI-assisted proposal production to the specific accountability exposure created when clients probe methodology.

**Why it matters now:** Sophisticated clients are beginning to ask about AI methodology in proposals. When the question arrives, the firm discovers it cannot answer. The exposure exists in every AI-assisted proposal currently in client possession.

---

### AI Model Version Tracking Architecture

**Missing:** No Geneva market service implementing AI model version tracking for boutique consulting firms with ongoing client deliverables.

**What it would do:** Implement version tracking architecture that records which AI model version was used for each client deliverable. Establish reassessment trigger protocols for when model updates may affect active deliverables. Build version state documentation for carrier attestation and client questionnaire responses.

**Why it doesn't exist:** Version tracking is not on the radar of Geneva consulting firms as an accountability obligation. The concept of silent model update propagation creating active deliverable exposure is not in market discourse.

**Why it matters now:** AI tools used in client work are continuously updated. The updates are silent. Active client deliverables may be operating on analytical conditions that changed since production. Without tracking architecture, the boutique cannot confirm the basis for recommendations currently in client use.

---

### Documentation Window Positioning Service

**Missing:** No Geneva market service framing documentation as burden-of-proof positioning — specifically for boutique consulting firms that must act before standards crystallize.

**What it would do:** Identify which documentation obligations are in active window periods. Build proportionate documentation architecture evaluated against forming standards, not arrived standards. Position the firm to demonstrate appropriate governance during window, not reactive compliance after close.

**Why it doesn't exist:** Documentation is framed as compliance obligation in the market. The positioning function of documentation during open windows is not in market discourse. The distinction between compliance deadline and burden-of-proof positioning exercise is not made.

**Why it matters now:** Some documentation windows have already closed. Firms that built documentation during open windows are in a different evidentiary position than firms that are now explaining the absence of documentation for decisions made during those periods.

## French — Analyse Competitive

**Ce qui existe:** Cadres généraux de gouvernance IA (ISO, NIST, EU AI Act) non calibrés pour le contexte genevois. Cabinets de protection des données sans specialization conseil. Консультанты juridique-tech sans perspective responsabilité professionnelle.

**Ce qui manque — Pré-revue de signature d'attestation courtier:** Aucun service genevois n'offre une révision indépendante de l'attestation tierce génération du point de vue de la responsabilité personnelle du signataire. Le document est conçu comme instrument à sens unique. Le marché n'a pas positionné de service représentant l'intérêt du signataire contre le document du courtier.

**Ce qui manque — Gestion de portefeuille de questionnaires clients:** Le marché n'a pas encadré les réponses aux questionnaires comme un actif de responsabilité de cabinet nécessitant une gestion systématique. Chaque réponse est vécue comme un événement unique. L'effet de portefeuille n'est pas reconnu.

**Ce qui manque — Architecture d'autorisation de délégation IA sous-conseillers:** Les flux Art.9 nFADP ne sont pas compris dans le secteur du conseil comme s'appliquant aux chaînes de prestation de services professionnels. Le вопрос de la délégation IA sous-conseillers n'est pas traité comme question de gouvernance.

**Ce qui manque — Architecture de défendabilité des propositions:** La défendabilité des propositions n'est pas experienced comme catégorie de responsabilité. Les propositions sont évaluées sur la qualité et l'adéquation stratégique, pas sur la provenance méthodologique. Le lien entre production IA et exposition spécifique n'est pas fait.

**Ce qui manque — Architecture de suivi de version de modèle IA:** Le concept de propagation silenciese des mises à jour de modèle créant une exposition sur les livrables actifs n'est pas dans le discours du marché genevois. Le tracking de version n'est pas sur le radar des cabinets genevois.

**Ce qui manque — Service de positionnement de fenêtre de documentation:** La documentation est encadrée comme obligation de conformité. La fonction de positionnement de la documentation pendant les fenêtres ouvertes n'est pas dans le discours. La distinction entre date limite de conformité et exercice de positionnement de charge de preuve n'est pas faite.
