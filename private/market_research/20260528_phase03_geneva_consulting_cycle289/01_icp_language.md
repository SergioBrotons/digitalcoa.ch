# 01 ICP Language — Phase 03 C289

## ICP Definition
Small Geneva consulting boutique, 2–50 employees. Managing director or partner personally accountable. Primary concerns: client AI governance questionnaires, carrier attestation, proposal methodology defensibility, sub-consultant AI delegation, and personal liability under nFADP.

---

## How the ICP Talks About AI Problems

### On Client AI Governance Questionnaires

> "We've had three clients in the last six months send us AI governance questionnaires. Different formats, different questions. We answered them. But I have no idea what we actually committed to."

- Client questionnaires arrive on client's timeline, not firm's
- Responses create accumulated commitments firm cannot reconstruct as a system
- No framework for understanding what answering reveals about the firm to the client
- Managing director signs responses personally but has no protocol for what constitutes adequate answers
- The questionnaire is experienced as a test the firm didn't prepare for, with consequences the firm doesn't fully understand

### On Carrier Attestation

> "The carrier attestation is written by the carrier's lawyers. I sign it personally. What 'adequate' means is defined by the carrier. I'm committing to a standard I can't actually read."

- Carrier attestation experienced as a signature event, not a governance architecture
- "Adequate" is undefined in any Geneva consulting context
- MD cannot get independent review before signing personal liability instrument
- The attestation is the operative standard even though it wasn't designed for Geneva consulting
- Post-signature realization: the standard is now in place and the firm doesn't know if it meets it

### On Proposal Methodology Defensibility

> "We submitted a proposal that was substantially AI-assisted. The client asked us to present our methodology. I couldn't explain exactly how the recommendation was constructed."

- Proposal submission creates a methodological commitment the firm may not be able to defend
- AI-assisted proposals may be indistinguishable in format from non-AI proposals — client cannot see methodology through document
- When clients probe methodology, the firm discovers its own defensibility gap
- No internal protocol for what AI-assisted proposals require in terms of documentation before submission
- Deliverables in active client use may have analytical frameworks the MD cannot reconstruct

### On Sub-Consultant AI Delegation

> "We use sub-consultants on larger engagements. Some of them are using AI tools. We're accountable to the client for everything that goes out under our name. But we have no visibility into what AI the sub-consultant is using or what their outputs look like."

- Prime boutique responsible for sub-consultant deliverables — contractually and in client's perception
- Sub-consultant AI use creates a three-party chain: client → prime boutique → sub-consultant → AI tool
- No authorization architecture governing what sub-consultant AI can be used for in work product
- nFADP Art.9 processor flows apply to this chain but have not been mapped in engagement letter architecture
- When something goes wrong, the prime MD is accountable; the AI that caused it was not selected by the MD

### On Personal Liability

> "I'm the one who signs everything. Carrier attestation, client contracts, proposals. If AI is in the mix and something goes wrong, I'm the one they come to. I don't have a framework for what I'm actually liable for."

- Personal signature as the moment of personal accountability transfer
- AI integration amplifies this: AI tools operating below MD's awareness in deliverables the MD signed
- No Geneva consulting equivalent to bar association or SRO providing liability framework clarification
- MD experiencing accountability without institutional infrastructure
- nFADP Art.63 personal liability creates criminal exposure in addition to professional liability

---

## Key Language Patterns

**"I signed it and then realized what it meant"** — carrier attestation, client contracts, proposals
**"We don't have a framework for that"** — AI governance questions, sub-consultant delegation, client questionnaires
**"I can't reconstruct how we got here"** — proposal methodology, analytical framework continuity
**"I'm personally on the line"** — attestation, delegation, personal liability
**"The client asked about our methodology"** — proposal defensibility as acute moment
**"We didn't know we were supposed to"** — documentation obligations, AI governance decisions
**"We're trying to figure it out alone"** — no Geneva peer accountability equivalent

## French — Comment Ils en Parlent

> « On a reçu trois questionnaires de gouvernance IA de clients différents en six mois. On a répondu. Mais je n'ai aucune idée de ce qu'on s'est engagé à faire vraiment. »

- Les questionnaires arrivent selon le calendrier du client, pas celui du cabinet
- Les réponses créent des engagements accumulés que le cabinet ne peut pas reconstruire comme système
- Aucun cadre pour comprendre ce que répondre révèle sur le cabinet au client
- Le directeur signe les réponses personnellement mais n'a pas de protocole pour savoir ce qui constitue une réponse adéquate
- Le questionnaire est vécu comme un examen pour lequel le cabinet n'a pas préparé, avec des conséquences que le cabinet ne comprend pas pleinement

> « L'attestation du courtier est rédigée par les avocats du courtier. Je la signe personnellement. Ce que 'adéquat' signifie n'est défini nulle part dans le contexte genevois. Je m'engage envers une norme que je ne peux pas réellement lire. »

> « On utilise des sous-conseillers sur des mandats plus importants. Certains utilisent des outils IA. Nous sommes responsables devant le client de tout ce qui porte notre nom. Mais on n'a aucune visibilité sur les outils IA que le sous-conseiller utilise. »

> « Je suis celui qui signe tout. L'attestation du courtier, les contrats clients, les propositions. Si l'IA est impliquée et que quelque chose tourne mal, c'est moi qu'on vient voir. Je n'ai pas de cadre pour ce que je suis réellement responsable. »

## Patterns de Langage Clés

**« J'ai signé, puis j'ai compris ce que ça signifiait »** — attestation courtier, contrats clients, propositions
**« On n'a pas de cadre pour ça »** — gouvernance IA, délégation sous-conseillers, questionnaires clients
**« Je ne peux pas reconstruire comment on en est arrivé là »** — méthodologie proposition, continuité du cadre analytique
**« Je suis personnellement engagé »** — attestation, délégation, responsabilité personnelle
**« Le client a demandé notre méthodologie »** — défendabilité de proposition comme moment aigu
**« On essaie de comprendre tout seuls »** — pas d'équivalent genevois de responsabilité entre pairs
