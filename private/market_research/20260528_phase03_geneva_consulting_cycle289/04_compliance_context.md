# 04 Compliance Context — Phase 03 C289

## Geneva/Switzerland Specific Regulatory Landscape

## nFADP (LPD/BLPD) — Federal Act on Data Protection

### Art. 6 — Lawfulness of Processing
Processing must have a lawful basis. For Geneva consulting firms using AI tools, the lawful basis question is not theoretical — it's active. Client data entering AI vendor infrastructure requires authorization basis. The authorization basis may have changed through vendor terms mutations that went unnoticed.

**Geneva-specific note:** The FDPIC has not published guidance on what constitutes adequate authorization basis for professional services firms using AI tools in client work. The question is unanswered at the Swiss level. Geneva consulting firms are making authorization decisions without an authoritative reference.

### Art. 8 — Profiling Notification
AI-assisted profiling of clients (recommendation systems, risk categorization, behavioral analysis) constitutes profiling under nFADP Art.8. Data subjects have the right to notification and human review of profiling decisions.

**Geneva-specific note:** Geneva consulting firms have not issued profiling notifications as a category. The obligation has not been integrated into client communication standards or engagement letter architecture. The gap is present-tense and unnamed.

### Art. 9 — Third-Party Processors
When prime boutique uses sub-consultant who uses AI tools, the AI vendor becomes a third-party processor in the data flow chain. Art.9 requires authorization basis for processing, processor agreement, and documentation of flows.

**Geneva-specific note:** Engagement letter standards in Geneva have not been updated to address AI vendor processor status. The three-party chain (client → prime → sub-consultant → AI tool) creates processor flows that no current engagement letter architecture maps. Prime boutique bears responsibility for sub-consultant AI tool compliance without having evaluated the vendor's terms or infrastructure.

### Art. 13 — Right to Explanation
For automated decisions with significant effect, data subjects have the right to request explanation of the logic involved. AI-assisted consulting recommendations may constitute automated decisions.

**Geneva-specific note:** No Geneva consulting firm has a protocol for responding to Art.13 requests for AI-assisted recommendation explanation. The question of what constitutes an automated decision with significant effect in consulting context has not been tested. The obligation exists without a discharge mechanism.

### Art. 21 — Profiling Affecting Rights
Automated decisions that significantly affect data subjects require human review opportunity. AI-influenced client recommendations with material consequences trigger this obligation.

**Geneva-specific note:** In litigation contexts, opposing counsel is beginning to use Art.21 as a procedural instrument to probe AI methodology. No Geneva court has ruled on the procedural status of Art.21 requests in civil proceedings. The doctrine is forming through case-by-case decisions. Consulting firms may face Art.21 requests from clients who want to understand the basis for recommendations that affected their decisions.

### Art. 63 — Personal Liability
Intentional violations of data protection obligations can result in personal criminal liability of up to CHF 50,000. The MD who deploys AI tools in client work without adequate governance architecture may be personally exposed.

**Geneva-specific note:** Art.63 is not a theoretical risk. The provision creates direct personal criminal exposure for MDs who make AI deployment decisions without proportionate governance documentation. The exposure is already present in firms that have deployed AI tools without the architecture this article requires.

## Professional Liability Framework — Art. 398 CO

Swiss Code of Obligations Art. 398 governs mandate contracts. The mandate holder must perform with the care of a proper contractor (Sorgfalt eines sorgfältigen Fachmanns).

**Geneva-specific note:** AI-assisted consulting deliverables must satisfy Art.398 proper performance standard. When AI tools produce outputs, the MD signing those outputs is certifying compliance with Art.398 — even when the MD cannot independently verify the AI-generated content meets that standard. The standard creates personal accountability for deliverables the MD did not produce through their own analytical process.

**The incompatibility:** Vendor terms of AI tools are designed for commercial delivery, not for Art.398 compliance. Satisfying vendor terms and satisfying Art.398 proper performance may be structurally incompatible under standard platform terms.

## Carrier Attestation — Insurance Market Standard

Carrier third-generation questionnaires function as the operative standard for Geneva consulting AI governance. The documents are designed by carrier underwriting teams to evaluate risk, not by Geneva consulting professionals to define professional accountability.

**Geneva-specific note:** The standard operates without Geneva consulting input. Carrier iteration patterns reveal how the standard is evolving — but the evolution is driven by underwriting logic, not professional practice. MDs signing carrier attestation are committing to a standard being formed without their participation or review.

**Structural displacement:** The carrier questionnaire has displaced any Geneva consulting-specific AI governance framework that might have formed through professional body participation. The vacuum is filled by underwriting instruments, not professional standards.

## Geneva Consulting Accountability Vacuum

Geneva consulting has no equivalent to:
- **SRO** (Self-Regulatory Organization) for financial sector — providing defined safe harbor standards
- **ORDRE des Avocats Genevois** — providing bar ethics guidance including AI guidance limits
- **SVA** (Swiss Association of Actuaries) — providing professional standards for consulting actuaries

**The consequence:** MDs are individually navigating accountability questions that should have institutional infrastructure. The vacuum means:
- No Geneva-specific interpretation of what carrier questionnaire adequacy means
- No engagement letter standard updated for AI delegation
- No methodology for pre-signing carrier attestation review
- No framework for sub-consultant AI authorization
- No peer accountability circuit for consulting-specific AI governance questions

**The amplification mechanism:** The vacuum doesn't just leave MDs without guidance — it actively amplifies their exposure. Each MD making decisions without collective input creates individual exposure that compounds across the sector. The absence of collective response means each firm's exposure is isolated and unmanaged.

## AI Convention (Council of Europe) — Ratification Path

Switzerland is on a path toward AI Convention ratification. Ratification creates present-tense obligations flowing through existing client contracts and carrier underwriting terms.

**Geneva-specific note:** The AI Convention deployer obligations are already operative through:
- Existing client mandates with AI components
- Carrier underwriting terms referencing AI governance standards
- Institutional client questionnaire expectations

The obligations exist before implementing legislation arrives. Geneva consulting firms may already be in breach of AI Convention deployer obligations through routine client engagement delivery — with no awareness that the obligations are operative.

## Documentation Window Timing

Several documentation windows relevant to Geneva consulting are in active or closing phases:

**Client questionnaire accumulation window:** Active now — each questionnaire response is a positioning event that shapes the firm's accountability record going forward. No retroactive correction for responses already made.

**Carrier attestation evolution window:** Ongoing — carrier third-gen to fourth-gen evolution creates new commitments. Each evolution is an active window for documentation positioning.

**AI Convention operationalization window:** Opening — ratification creates new accountability architecture requirements. The window for pre-implementation positioning has begun.

**nFADP enforcement window:** Active — Art.63 personal liability is already operative. The window for building documentation architecture that demonstrates governance intent is not closed — it's always active, but the burden increases as exposure accumulates without proportionate documentation.

## French — Contexte de Conformité

**nFADP — Art.6 lawful processing:** La base légale pour le traitement des données clients via des outils IA n'est pas определена. Le FPODP n'a pas publié de directives sur ce qui constitue une base légale adéquate pour les cabinets de conseil genevois utilisant des outils IA dans les mandats clients.

**nFADP — Art.8 profilage:** Les cabinets genevois n'ont pas émis de notifications de profilage comme catégorie. L'obligation existe sans mécanisme de publication ou d'architecture de réponse.

**nFADP — Art.9 tiers processeurs:** La chaîne à trois niveaux (client → cabinet principal → sous-conseiller → outil IA) crée des flux de processeurs tiers que aucune architecture de lettre de mission actuelle ne cartographie. Le cabinet principal porte la responsabilité de la conformité des outils IA du sous-conseiller sans avoir évalué les conditions ou l'infrastructure du fournisseur.

**nFADP — Art.13 droit à l'explication:** Aucun cabinet genevois n'a de protocole pour répondre aux demandes Art.13 d'explication de recommandations assistées par IA. L'obligation existe sans mécanisme de décharge.

**nFADP — Art.63 responsabilité personnelle:** Les violations intentionnelles des obligations de protection des données entraînent une responsabilité criminelle personnelle jusqu'à CHF 50 000. Le directeur qui déploie des outils IA sans architecture de gouvernance adéquate peut être personnellement exposé.

**Art.398 CO — Obligation de diligence:** Les livrables de conseil assistés par IA doivent satisfaire la norme de diligence professionnelle. Les conditions des fournisseurs d'IA sont conçues pour la livraison commerciale, pas pour la conformité Art.398. Répondre aux conditions du fournisseur et satisfaire Art.398 peut être structurellement incompatible sous les conditions standard de la plateforme.

**Vide de responsabilité du conseil genevois:** Aucun équivalent SRO, barreau, ou association professionnelle pour le secteur du conseil genevois. Les directeurs naviguent individuellement des questions de responsabilité qui devraient avoir une infrastructure institutionnelle.

**Convention IA — Obligations opérationnelles:** La ratification crée des obligations présentes circulant à travers les mandats clients existants et les conditions d'assurance. Les obligations existent avant l'arrivée de la législation d'implémentation. Les cabinets genevois peuvent déjà être en violation des obligations de déployeur de la Convention IA sans en être conscients.
