### Finding 01: Client Questionnaire Portfolio Accumulation Effect as Primary Accountability Formation Mechanism

**The finding:** Client AI governance questionnaires are the primary accountability formation mechanism for Geneva consulting boutiques — operating as a portfolio-level commitment system that no firm currently manages as such.

**What's new:** Prior research identified client questionnaires as individual accountability events. This finding isolates the portfolio-level accumulation effect: each questionnaire response creates commitments that accumulate across the entire client book, and the firm cannot reconstruct its portfolio-wide commitment position. The third questionnaire may reveal contradictions the firm doesn't know it created with the first.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding identifies the specific mechanism by which client questionnaires function as accountability formation — not through individual questionnaire adequacy but through accumulated commitment inconsistency across the client portfolio. The portfolio effect creates firm-wide exposure invisible from any single questionnaire response.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Each client questionnaire you answer creates accumulated commitments you can't see as a system. The third response may contradict the first — and you won't know until a client asks."

### Finding 02: Carrier Attestation as Personal Liability Instrument Without Pre-Signing Review Pathway

**The finding:** Carrier third-generation attestation operates as a personal liability instrument for Geneva consulting MDs — signed routinely, without pre-signing review from the signatory's perspective, committing to an undefined adequacy standard designed by carrier underwriting teams without Geneva consulting input.

**What's new:** Prior research identified carrier attestation as a general accountability instrument. This finding isolates the specific structural absence: no Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of what the MD commits to personally, and no Geneva consulting-specific interpretation of the adequacy standard exists. The document is written by the carrier's legal team; no independent review exists before personal signature.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding names the specific service gap as a discrete market category — pre-signing carrier attestation review from the signatory's perspective — and identifies why the gap is structural rather than temporary: the document is designed as a one-directional instrument with no mechanism for signatory-side review by design.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're signing a personal liability document written by the carrier's legal team, committing to 'adequate controls' defined by underwriting logic, with no Geneva-specific interpretation of what adequate means — and no service has positioned itself to review it from your side before you sign."

### Finding 03: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Three-Party Chain as nFADP Art.9 Structural Exposure

**The finding:** Sub-consultant AI delegation creates a three-party chain (client → prime boutique → sub-consultant → AI tool) with nFADP Art.9 third-party processor flows that no current engagement letter architecture maps — leaving prime boutique accountable for AI tool compliance it never evaluated.

**What's new:** Prior research named sub-consultant delegation as a general accountability gap. This finding isolates the specific nFADP Art.9 mechanism: the AI tool vendor becomes a third-party processor in the data flow chain, with Art.9 authorization, processor agreement, and documentation obligations flowing to the prime boutique as the controller. The engagement letter was written before this configuration existed; it doesn't address AI delegation processor obligations.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding identifies the specific legal mechanism creating exposure — not delegation as a general practice problem but nFADP Art.9 processor chain compliance failure as a discrete structural condition. The prime MD is accountable for sub-consultant AI tool compliance without having evaluated the vendor's terms or data processing architecture.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're contractually and professionally accountable for AI tool compliance in deliverables from sub-consultants you selected. The nFADP processor flows apply to that chain. Your engagement letter doesn't map them. You're carrying compliance responsibility for infrastructure you've never evaluated."

### Finding 04: AI Model Silent Update Propagation as Undetectable Active Deliverable Contamination

**The finding:** Continuous silent AI model updates create undetectable version state drift in active client deliverables — the analytical framework that shaped a proposal may be operating on a different model version today, with no version tracking architecture, no reassessment trigger protocol, and no carrier questionnaire asking about it.

**What's new:** Prior research identified deliverable staleness and analytical drift. This finding isolates the specific silent update propagation mechanism: model updates are continuous and silent; active deliverables may be based on model states that no longer exist; no mechanism exists for the boutique to detect this contamination. The MD cannot confirm the basis for recommendations currently in client use.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding names the specific exposure mechanism — not that deliverables age or staleness accumulates, but that silent model updates create version state contamination undetectable without architecture the market has not built. The exposure is present-tense for every active client deliverable produced with AI tools.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The AI tool you used for that proposal is being updated continuously — without your knowledge. The model version that produced your recommendation may not be the model version running today. You have no version record. You can't confirm what analytical conditions your active deliverables are based on."

### Finding 05: Documentation Window Close as Burden-of-Proof Inversion Requiring Pre-Architecture, Not Retroactive Reconstruction

**The finding:** Documentation windows operate as burden-of-proof positioning exercises, not compliance deadlines — and the close of certain windows has already created an evidentiary inversion that retroactive reconstruction cannot address.

**What's new:** Prior research named documentation windows as timing issues. This finding isolates the specific inversion mechanism: before window close, building documentation is evaluated against forming standards (flexible, interpretive); after window close, explaining absence is evaluated against arrived standards (fixed, demanding). New documentation cannot retroactively reproduce the reasoning that existed at the time decisions were made. The window positioned the firm; the close locks the position.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding distinguishes the documentation window (positioning exercise) from the compliance deadline (absolute date with penalty consequences) — and identifies why retroactive reconstruction fails: it reproduces current reasoning, not the reasoning that existed during the window period. The gap is not addressable through subsequent documentation improvement.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The documentation window has closed for some of these obligations. You are now in a position of explaining why documentation doesn't exist for decisions made during a period when no documentation architecture was built. Retroactive reconstruction cannot fill that gap — it can only show what you think now, not what you knew then."

---

### Finding 06: Geneva Consulting Accountability Vacuum as Structural Amplification Mechanism Displacing Professional Self-Determination

**The finding:** The absence of any Geneva consulting peer accountability equivalent (no SRO, no bar equivalent, no professional body covering management consulting AI governance) creates a structural vacuum that amplifies MD exposure by displacing professional self-determination with carrier underwriting standards.

**What's new:** Prior research named the accountability vacuum as an information or guidance gap. This finding isolates the specific amplification mechanism: carrier questionnaires function as de facto operative standard for the sector — designed without Geneva consulting input, forming without professional participation, applied to firms that had no voice in their formation. The vacuum doesn't leave MDs unsupported — it hands the standard-setting function to carriers whose instruments were designed for underwriting risk evaluation, not professional liability protection.

**Why it's structurally new:** This finding identifies why the vacuum is structurally permanent (no legal privilege equivalent to bar, no SRO safe harbor equivalent, no mechanism to trigger formal standard-formation response regardless of question validity) and specifically names the displacement mechanism: professional self-determination is displaced by carrier underwriting logic because no alternative institutional structure exists to perform that function.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Geneva finance has SROs. Geneva law has the Ordre. Geneva consulting has nothing. The carrier questionnaire has become the de facto operative standard for the sector — designed by underwriting teams, not by consulting professionals. The vacuum doesn't just leave you without guidance. It hands the standard to people who were solving a different problem."
