# 02 Market Signals — Phase 03 C289

## Current Problems, Complaints, Failures

---

## Signal 1: Client Questionnaire Portfolio Accumulation Effect

**What we're observing:** Small Geneva consulting boutiques are receiving client AI governance questionnaires from multiple clients simultaneously — without any portfolio-level view of what the firm has committed to across relationships.

**The failure:** Each questionnaire answered in isolation. The third questionnaire reveals commitments that contradict the first. The MD cannot explain the inconsistency because no one was tracking accumulated commitments at the firm level.

**Why it's structural:** Client AI governance questionnaires are the primary accountability formation mechanism in the Geneva consulting market right now. Each client sets their own format, timing, and expectations. The firm responds without understanding that each response becomes part of a firm-wide accountability record the client can reference.

**The acute moment:** When two clients compare notes — or when one client asks about commitments made to another — the MD discovers the inconsistency with no ability to reconstruct or explain it.

---

## Signal 2: Carrier Attestation Post-Signature Realization

**What we're observing:** MDs signing carrier third-generation attestation as a routine event, then discovering post-signature that the document creates personal liability exposure against a standard they cannot determine they've met.

**The failure:** The attestation was experienced as a formality. It was not experienced as a personal liability instrument. No Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of what the signatory commits to personally.

**Why it's structural:** Carrier questionnaires function as the de facto operative standard for Geneva consulting AI governance, designed by carrier underwriting teams, not by Geneva consulting professionals. The MD signs a document committing to "adequate AI governance controls" with no Geneva-specific interpretation of what adequate means.

**The acute moment:** When a carrier investigation is triggered — not when it's resolved. The MD signed a document they cannot reconstruct having reviewed adequately.

---

## Signal 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Three-Party Chain Without Authorization Architecture

**What we're observing:** Prime boutique contracts with sub-consultant for specialized work. Sub-consultant uses AI tools in delivery. Client holds prime boutique accountable for all outputs under the engagement. nFADP Art.9 processor flows apply to the three-party chain — but no engagement letter architecture addresses this.

**The failure:** The prime boutique's engagement letter was written before AI delegation became common. It doesn't define what AI tools the sub-consultant can use, what authorization the prime boutique must obtain before AI tools are deployed in client work, or how nFADP processor obligations flow through the chain.

**Why it's structural:** Sub-consultant AI delegation is not a temporary situation — it's a permanent feature of how larger consulting engagements are delivered in Geneva. The accountability architecture for it has not been built into engagement letter standards.

**The acute moment:** When client raises an AI governance question about work product that originated with a sub-consultant. The prime MD cannot explain the AI methodology because the sub-consultant's AI use was never authorized or documented.

---

## Signal 4: AI Model Silent Update Propagation Into Active Client Deliverables

**What we're observing:** AI tools used in proposal development and client deliverables are updated by vendors silently — without notification to the boutique. The analytical framework that shaped a proposal submitted three months ago may be operating on a different model version today.

**The failure:** The boutique cannot confirm that deliverables in active client use were produced under the same analytical conditions the MD reviewed. No version tracking architecture exists. No reassessment trigger protocol exists.

**Why it's structural:** AI model updates are continuous and silent. Vendor infrastructure does not notify users when the underlying model changes. Active client deliverables may be based on model states that no longer exist — with no mechanism for the boutique to detect this.

**The acute moment:** When a client asks about a recommendation the firm made, and the analytical framework has drifted since the proposal was submitted. The MD cannot explain the current basis for a recommendation because the model that produced it has been silently updated.

---

## Signal 5: Documentation Window Close as Burden-of-Proof Inversion

**What we're observing:** Geneva consulting boutiques discovering that documentation obligations they didn't know about have already activated. The documentation window — the period when building documentation was evaluated against forming standards — has already closed for some obligations.

**The failure:** The firm is now in a position where it must explain the absence of documentation for decisions made during a window that has closed. Retroactive reconstruction cannot reproduce the reasoning that existed at the time of the decision.

**Why it's structural:** The documentation window is not a compliance deadline — it's a burden-of-proof positioning exercise. Before the window closes, firms building documentation are evaluated against forming standards. After the window closes, firms without documentation are evaluated against arrived standards. New documentation cannot retroactively reconstruct the evidence that the window required.

**The acute moment:** When a client questionnaire, carrier inquiry, or regulatory request arrives and asks for evidence of decision-making from a period when no documentation architecture existed.

---

## Signal 6: Geneva Consulting Accountability Vacuum as Structural Amplification Mechanism

**What we're observing:** Geneva consulting boutiques operating without any peer accountability equivalent — no SRO safe harbor, no bar association with AI governance guidance, no professional body providing consulting-specific AI standards.

**The failure:** MDs are individually navigating accountability questions that their peers in finance and law have institutional infrastructure to address collectively. The Geneva consulting accountability vacuum means each MD is making decisions that affect the entire sector's accountability posture — without any mechanism for those decisions to be informed by collective experience.

**Why it's structural:** Carrier questionnaires function as the de facto operative standard for Geneva consulting — designed without Geneva consulting input, applied to firms that had no voice in their formation. The vacuum is not an information gap — it's a structural absence that creates compound exposure for every boutique operating within it.

**The acute moment:** When a carrier introduces a new questionnaire requirement and the MD discovers the standard was formed without consulting sector participation — and there's no mechanism to provide input or seek clarification.

## French — Signaux de Marché

**Signal 1 — Accumulation de portefeuille de questionnaires clients:** Les cabinets genevois reçoivent des questionnaires de gouvernance IA de plusieurs clients simultanément sans aucune vue de portefeuille sur les engagements accumulés. Chaque réponse fournie en isolation crée un dossier de responsabilité que le cabinet ne peut pas reconstruire.

**Signal 2 — Réalisation post-signature de l'attestation courtier:** Les directeurs signent l'attestation tierce génération comme un événement routinier, découvrant ensuite que le document crée une exposition à responsabilité personnelle contre une norme qu'ils ne peuvent pas déterminer avoir atteinte.

**Signal 3 — Chaîne de délégation IA à trois niveaux sans architecture d'autorisation:** Le cabinet principal est responsable devant le client pour les livrables du sous-conseiller utilisant des outils IA. Les flux Art.9 nFADP s'appliquent à la chaîne mais ne sont pas cartographiés dans les standards de lettres de mission.

**Signal 4 — Propagation silenciese des mises à jour de modèle IA dans les livrables actifs:** Les outils IA sont mis à jour silencieusement par les fournisseurs sans notification. Les livrables en cours d'utilisation peuvent être basés sur des états de modèle qui n'existent plus, sans mécanisme de détection.

**Signal 5 — Fermeture de la fenêtre de documentation comme inversion de la charge de preuve:** La fenêtre de documentation n'est pas une date limite de conformité — c'est un exercice de positionnement de charge de preuve. La reconstruction rétroactive ne peut pas reproduire le raisonnement qui existait au moment de la décision.

**Signal 6 — Vide de responsabilité du conseil genevois comme mécanisme d'amplification structurelle:** Les questionnaires des courtiers fonctionnent comme norme operative de facto pour le conseil genevois, conçus sans la participation du secteur genevois, appliqués à des cabinets qui n'avaient aucune voix dans leur formation.
