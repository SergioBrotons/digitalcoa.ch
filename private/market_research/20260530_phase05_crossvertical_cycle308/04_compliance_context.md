## Cross-Vertical Regulatory Architecture

The regulatory patterns that govern AI governance differ by vertical but share structural characteristics that create identical documentation demands across all four. This file maps the universal structural patterns and the vertical-specific calibrations.

---

## Universal Structural Pattern 01: Retroactive Documentation Debt Compounds Across Three Independent Vectors

**The pattern:** In every vertical, documentation debt accumulates along three independent vectors that compound simultaneously. Addressing one vector (typically time, via "document going forward") does not reduce the other two. The compounding structure is the same across all four verticals — only the regulatory context differs.

| Vector | Legal | Finance | Consulting | Healthcare |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Time | Months of undocumented AI use on matters | Months of undocumented AI decisions in portfolios | Months of undocumented AI contributions in engagements | Months of undocumented AI interpretation in clinical decisions |
| Tool count | Each AI tool processes different matter types without documentation | Each AI system added to investment process adds documentation requirement | Each AI tool used by sub-consultants adds documentation requirement | Each AI tool in practice workflow adds documentation requirement |
| Staff use | Each associate using AI creates independent documentation obligation | Each staff member using AI creates documentation obligation for CIO | Each sub-consultant using AI is invisible to managing partner | Each staff member using AI adds to physician's accountability |

**The structural implication:** The documentation solution must address all three vectors simultaneously. A linear forward-only solution compounds the debt along the two vectors not being addressed. The service that freezes all three vectors and builds a forward evidence capture system that captures all three simultaneously is the correct response to the problem structure.

## Universal Structural Pattern 02: Broker Incentive Architecture Drives Market Access

**The pattern:** In every vertical, the broker (insurance broker, independent financial advisor, PI insurer, carrier broker) is the primary trust intermediary and the primary acquisition channel. The broker's referral decision is driven by their own exposure to carrier claims from clients they refer. A service that demonstrably reduces broker claim exposure generates broker referrals. A service that doesn't explicitly address broker protection gets fewer referrals.

| Vertical | Primary Broker Type | Broker Protection Mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Insurance broker | Reduces carrier claim exposure when attorney has evidence of AI governance practice |
| Finance | Independent financial advisor | Reduces E&O exposure when client has documented AI governance |
| Consulting | PI insurer / insurance broker | Reduces PI claim exposure when managing partner has evidence of AI governance across sub-consultant chain |
| Healthcare | Medical malpractice carrier broker | Reduces malpractice claim exposure when physician has documented human oversight of AI |

**The structural implication:** The service design must consider the broker as the primary client — not just the ICP. The broker's trust in the service is what generates referrals. The service that protects the broker (and makes that protection visible) captures the referral channel. The broker referral architecture is universal across all four verticals.

## Universal Structural Pattern 03: The Carrier Form Is an Accountability Architecture, Not a Compliance Checklist

**The pattern:** In every vertical, the carrier attestation form creates a documented standard that the ICP attested to meeting. The signature is personal. The standard created by the form language may not be documented by existing practice. The gap between what the ICP certified to and what they can demonstrate is the accountability exposure.

| Vertical | Form Language Creates | ICP Exposure |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | "Adequate supervision of AI tools" = documented supervision standard | Art.321 CC personal criminal exposure if standard not met |
| Finance | "Adequate AI governance framework" = documented evidence standard | Art.63 nFADP personal administrative exposure if standard not met |
| Consulting | "Management of AI use across service chain" = documented oversight standard | Art.52 nFADP personal administrative exposure if standard not met |
| Healthcare | "Human oversight of AI-assisted decisions" = documented oversight standard | Art.321 CC personal criminal + HRA personal administrative exposure if standard not met |

**The structural implication:** Pre-signing review of the carrier form is the highest-value intervention point. The ICP who understands what their signature commits them to before signing can build the evidence architecture before the accountability gap is created. Post-signing remediation is more expensive and less complete. The pre-signing intervention is the service that prevents the problem rather than addressing it after the fact.

## Universal Structural Pattern 04: Vendor Contract Terms Partially Define Professional Accountability

**The pattern:** In every vertical, the AI vendor's contract terms create or modify professional accountability obligations. The ICP accepted vendor terms without reviewing them against their professional obligations. The accountability exposure is embedded in contract language — not in the ICP's intentions. The vendor contract review is not a general due diligence exercise — it is a professional obligation review.

| Vertical | Vendor Contract Creates | Professional Exposure |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Vendor DPA grants vendor access to submitted client data for model training | Art.321 CC: vendor access to privileged communications may constitute disclosure |
| Finance | Vendor Terms of Service include sub-processor provisions for client financial data | Art.26 nFADP: sub-processor chain creates undocumented data processing relationships |
| Consulting | Sub-consultant AI vendor terms not reviewed; main firm accountable for entire chain | Art.52 nFADP: managing partner responsible for processing throughout service delivery chain |
| Healthcare | Vendor model training provisions allow de-identified patient data use for AI improvement | Art.321 CC: model training may breach professional secrecy obligations |

**The structural implication:** The vendor contract is the accountability mechanism that the ICP signed without reading. The specific question — where do these vendor terms conflict with my professional obligations? — is a bounded, specific service with a clear deliverable. The vendor contract review against professional obligation framework is a discrete engagement that protects the ICP across all future carrier audits.

## Universal Structural Pattern 05: The Evidence Standard Has Shifted from Policy to Contemporaneous Record

**The pattern:** In every vertical, carriers and regulators have shifted from requesting policy documents (existence of a framework) to requesting evidence of actual practice (contemporaneous records of what happened). The documentation paradigm in use — policy documents, periodic summaries — was designed for the 2024 standard. The 2026 standard requires contemporaneous event records.

| Vertical | 2024 Standard (Policy) | 2026 Standard (Evidence) |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | "Our firm has an AI supervision policy" | "Here is the record of how this specific matter's AI use was supervised" |
| Finance | "Our firm has an AI governance framework" | "Here is the contemporaneous record of what our AI systems did between annual reviews" |
| Consulting | "Our firm monitors sub-consultant AI use" | "Here is the record of what AI each sub-consultant used on this specific engagement" |
| Healthcare | "Our practice has human oversight of AI" | "Here is the record of the AI interpretation layer before the physician's decision" |

**The structural implication:** The policy infrastructure built in 2024 cannot satisfy the evidence requirements of 2026. Updating policies does not close this gap. The documentation paradigm must shift from policy documents (what should exist) to evidence records (what actually happened). This requires a different documentation system, not additional policy documents.

## Universal Structural Pattern 06: Geneva Bilingual Regulatory Environment Requires Dual-Language Documentation Design

**The pattern:** In every vertical, the Geneva ICP operates in two regulatory language environments: English (carrier forms, client DDQs, EU AI Act) and French (Ordre proceedings, FINMA, Swiss domestic regulation). Documentation built in one language may not serve proceedings conducted in the other. The documentation system must be designed to function in both regulatory language environments simultaneously.

| Vertical | English Language Context | French Language Context |
|---|---|---|
| Legal | Carrier attestation form | Ordre disciplinary proceedings; FDPIC investigations |
| Finance | Institutional client DDQ; FINMA Guidance | FINMA examination proceedings; Swiss regulatory framework |
| Consulting | Client RFP; EU AI Act GPAI documentation | nFADP proceedings; Swiss domestic regulatory framework |
| Healthcare | Carrier attestation form | FMH proceedings; HRA administrative proceedings |

**The structural implication:** Bilingual documentation is not translation — it is a design requirement. Documentation that satisfies English-language carrier demands must also function in French-language regulatory proceedings. The service that builds documentation for both language environments simultaneously creates a structural advantage that English-only competitors cannot match.

## Vertical-Specific Regulatory Calibration

### Legal (Geneva Small Law Firms)
- **Primary:** Art.321 CC (professional secrecy — criminal personal liability), Art.63 nFADP (personal administrative)
- **Secondary:** OFL guidance (undefined), EU AI Act (EU matters), carrier attestation
- **Documentation trigger:** Carrier attestation signature, Ordre inquiry, client complaint, FDPIC investigation
- **Key calibration:** Retroactive documentation gap is largest in legal (18–24 months of undocumented matters common); carrier form pre-signing review is distinctive high-value intervention

### Finance (Geneva Boutique Wealth Management)
- **Primary:** Art.63 nFADP (personal administrative), FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (proportionate framework)
- **Secondary:** SRO standards (undefined on AI), institutional client DDQ (competitive gate), carrier attestation
- **Documentation trigger:** Institutional DDQ response, carrier attestation renewal, FINMA inquiry
- **Key calibration:** Institutional DDQ as competitive gatekeeper; retroactive portfolio documentation for 12–18 months is the primary documentation challenge

### Consulting (Geneva Boutique Advisory)
- **Primary:** Art.52 nFADP (personal administrative: up to CHF 250,000 for intentional violations), EU AI Act GPAI cascade
- **Secondary:** Client RFP AI criteria (commercial gate), carrier attestation, sub-consultant delegation chain
- **Documentation trigger:** Client RFP with AI governance scoring, carrier attestation signature, EU AI Act GPAI compliance deadline
- **Key calibration:** Sub-consultant chain documentation is the distinctive structural need; retroactive documentation across multiple sub-consultants is the primary challenge

### Healthcare (Geneva Physicians)
- **Primary:** Art.321 CC (professional secrecy — criminal), HRA personal administrative, FMH professional standards
- **Secondary:** nFADP Art.63 (personal), carrier attestation, HIN compliance (transport only)
- **Documentation trigger:** FMH credentialing inquiry, carrier attestation, patient complaint, HRA audit
- **Key calibration:** Human oversight documentation (AI interpretation layer before clinical decision) is the distinctive clinical governance need; retroactive documentation of 8–12 months of AI-assisted reports is the primary challenge

## Cross-Vertical Documentation Architecture

### The Evidence Architecture (Same Across All Four Verticals)

The evidence architecture has four components, identical in structure across all four verticals:

1. **Tool Inventory Documentation** — what AI tools process client/patient data, under what authorization, with what vendor terms
2. **Supervision Record Production** — how AI-assisted decisions are reviewed, by whom, with what documentation
3. **Vendor Contract Gap Analysis** — where vendor terms create exposure under the applicable professional obligation framework
4. **Cross-Authority Documentation Mapping** — how evidence records simultaneously serve multiple authority demands

### The Retroactive + Forward Capture Design

The evidence architecture must address both the retroactive gap and the forward capture system simultaneously:

- **Retroactive component:** Document what happened during the period before the evidence system was built. This is bounded (specific months, specific tools, specific staff use) and produces evidence that satisfies carrier requirements for the historical period.
- **Forward component:** Build a continuous evidence capture system that documents what happens from today forward. This is ongoing and produces contemporaneous records.

### The Three-Vector Freeze Design

The evidence architecture freezes all three compounding vectors simultaneously:

- **Time vector:** Documentation captures events in real time, eliminating retroactive accumulation
- **Tool count vector:** Each new tool is documented at adoption, with full retroactive coverage for that tool's use period
- **Staff use vector:** Each staff member's AI use is documented at onboarding and tracked continuously

### The Bilingual Documentation Design

The evidence architecture produces documentation that functions in both regulatory language environments:

- **English output:** Carrier attestation support, client DDQ responses, EU AI Act documentation
- **French output:** Ordre proceedings support, FINMA examination support, Swiss domestic regulatory compliance
- **Design principle:** Documentation is built in both languages simultaneously — not translated after the fact
