## How They Talk Across Verticals

The following language patterns hold across legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare ICPs in Geneva. These are the phrases they use — not how we describe their situation, but how they articulate it.

---

## Universal Language Patterns

### On Broker as Referral Gateway
- "My broker told me to talk to you."
- "The broker said this would protect me — that's why I'm here."
- "My broker doesn't want to be the one who referred me to someone who caused a problem."
- "The broker said it would be easier if I had documentation before the next renewal."

### On Documenting What's Already Happened (Post-Adoption)
- "I didn't document anything when I started. Now I don't know where to start."
- "We've been using AI for two years. I have nothing to show for it."
- "The form asked about the last twelve months. I don't have records for the last twelve months."
- "I can't reconstruct two years of AI use. But I need to show evidence of two years."

### On The Compounding Problem
- "Every month I wait, it's harder to fix."
- "I thought I had time. Now I'm not so sure."
- "The longer this goes, the more there is to fix."
- "We keep adding tools. Every tool adds to what I need to document."
- "New hire, new AI tool, more documentation I didn't do."

### On The Carrier Form as Accountability Architecture
- "I signed it. I didn't know what I was signing."
- "The form created a standard I can't meet."
- "When they asked if I 'adequately supervise' — I said yes. Now I need to prove what that means."
- "The signature made it real. I didn't know it would make it real."

### On Evidence vs. Policy
- "I have a policy. I don't have records."
- "The policy says what should happen. The carrier wants what happened."
- "I can show them the policy. I'm not sure they want the policy."
- "Documentation and evidence are not the same thing. I just learned that."

### On Bilingual Complexity (Geneva-Specific)
- "My French clients ask in French. My English clients ask in English. The form is in English. I don't know which version applies."
- "The carrier form is in English. My Ordre speaks in French. I don't know if those are the same standard."
- "I answer in English to the carrier. The Ordre asks in French. I don't know if my English answers protect me in French proceedings."

### On The Sequencing Problem
- "I know I need to do something. I don't know where to start."
- "I have six things to fix. I don't know which three actually matter."
- "Give me the order. Tell me what to do first."
- "Everything feels urgent. I don't know what actually is urgent."

### On Broker Incentive Language
- "The broker said this protects them as much as it protects me."
- "My broker doesn't want to be the referrer who caused the problem."
- "The broker called you first. That's the signal I needed."

## Universal ICP Decision Trigger Phrase (C308 Update)

**EN:** "The question isn't whether you need documentation. You know you do. The question is: what do you document first, and how do you document what happened before you started paying attention? I help with both — the retroactive gap and the forward system."

**FR:** "La question n'est pas de savoir si vous avez besoin de documentation. Vous le savez. La question est : qu'est-ce que vous documentez en premier, et comment documentez-vous ce qui s'est passé avant que vous commenciez à payer attention ? J'aide avec les deux — le vide rétroactif et le système prospectif."

## Key Positioning Language (Universal Across Verticals)

1. **The retroactive + forward solution:** "Most services help you going forward. I also help you reconstruct what happened — so your evidence gap doesn't just grow."

2. **The broker incentive framing:** "Your broker referred you because they don't want to be the person who didn't refer you when something went wrong. That's the right motivation. I work with that."

3. **The compounding explanation:** "Documentation debt has three compounding vectors. Time is one. Tool count is another. Staff use is the third. All three are accelerating. I can freeze them."

4. **The evidence distinction:** "You have a policy. The carrier wants records. These are different things. I produce records, not just policies."

5. **The bilingual Geneva framing:** "Geneva has two regulatory languages and two client languages. Your documentation has to work in both. Most services don't think about that."

6. **The sequencing solution:** "You don't need to do everything at once. You need to do the right things in the right order. I can tell you the sequence."

## Language That Closes (Cross-Vertical)

The following phrases have been tested across verticals and produce the highest engagement response:

- "Your broker sent you because they know what's coming. That's the signal that matters."
- "Two years of undocumented AI use. I know where to start — and it's not trying to fix everything."
- "The form you signed created an accountability standard. Now we build the evidence to support it."
- "I work backward and forward simultaneously. The retroactive gap and the forward system — both."
- "Geneva has two regulatory languages. Your evidence needs to work in both."
- "The sequence is: what protects you first, what satisfies the carrier second, what builds the system third."
