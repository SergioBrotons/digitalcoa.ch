## Cross-Vertical Problem Mapping

The following problems appear across all four verticals (legal, finance, consulting, healthcare) in structurally identical forms. Each problem has the same mechanism regardless of the vertical — only the regulatory framework varies.

---

## Problem 01: The Retroactive Documentation Gap Is the Majority Market Problem

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Attorney used AI for 18 months, has no contemporaneous records, now needs to show evidence for carrier renewal
- Finance: CIO implemented AI tools 14 months ago, documentation system built at month 12, gap for months 1–12 unresolved
- Consulting: Managing partner has five sub-consultants using AI tools for over a year, no records of any of it
- Healthcare: Physician has been using AI-assisted patient reports for 8 months, records show the report, not the AI interpretation layer

**The common mechanism:** Pre-adoption and audit-response services exist. Post-adoption documentation gap (the largest market segment by ICP count) has no dedicated service. The ICP who adopted AI tools 6–24 months ago and now faces a carrier audit doesn't know where to start because retroactive reconstruction feels impossible.

**The market signal:** "I can't reconstruct two years of AI use. But I need to show evidence of two years." This is not a rare situation — it is the majority of the market. The ICP who adopted AI tools in 2023–2025 without documentation infrastructure is now facing carrier attestation requests and has no path forward that doesn't feel overwhelming.

---

## Problem 02: Broker Incentive Architecture Drives Referral Behavior, Not ICP Direct Search

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Attorney's insurance broker is the primary referral source for AI governance services; broker incentive is to reduce their own exposure to a carrier claim from the attorney
- Finance: Independent financial advisor's broker is the primary referral source; broker wants to refer to someone credible who reduces their E&O exposure
- Consulting: Consulting firm's PI insurer and broker are the primary referral gateway; broker referral to an unknown provider is a reputational risk for the broker
- Healthcare: Medical malpractice carrier's broker is the primary referral source; broker wants evidence of physician's AI governance to reduce carrier claim exposure

**The common mechanism:** The broker (insurance broker, independent financial advisor, PI insurer) is the primary trust intermediary in the Geneva professional circuit — not a search engine, not a digital ad, not a content piece. The broker's incentive to refer is highest when the referral reduces the broker's own exposure to a claim. Services that position the broker as protected by the referral (not just the client) capture the broker referral channel more effectively.

**The market signal:** "My broker told me to talk to you." This phrase is the most common first sentence in ICP initial contacts. The market does not respond to content marketing alone — it responds to broker-referred trust signals.

## Problem 03: The Three-Compounding-Vectors Problem Is Not Widely Understood

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Associate AI use added → managing partner accountable for additional undocumented vector; new matter types add complexity; time compounds all three
- Finance: New AI tools added to investment process → documentation debt increments for all prior months of undocumented use; staff headcount increases documentation obligations
- Consulting: Sub-consultant count increases → each sub-consultant's AI use is invisible and adds to documentation debt; client engagements accumulate without documentation
- Healthcare: New staff member added → their AI use adds to documentation debt; new AI tool added → increments all prior undocumented months; time compounds without documentation

**The common mechanism:** Documentation debt compounds along three independent vectors: time (accelerates with every month of undocumented use), tool count (each new tool added increments documentation requirements retroactively), and staff use (each person using AI tools adds an independent documentation obligation). Most advisors describe documentation debt as a linear problem ("just document going forward"). It is not linear — it is non-linear and accelerating.

**The market signal:** Most services address one vector ("just document going forward"). The ICP who understands all three vectors knows that linear solutions don't work. The service that names the compounding structure and explains why it requires a different solution architecture gains credibility with sophisticated ICPs who have already tried simple solutions and found them insufficient.

## Problem 04: Carrier Forms Are Accountability Architecture, Not Compliance Checklists

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Carrier attestation form creates a documented standard of "adequate supervision" that managing partner cannot meet with existing documentation
- Finance: Carrier attestation creates Art.63 personal liability exposure; CIO signed without understanding the personal administrative exposure embedded in the language
- Consulting: Carrier attestation language commits managing partner to supervision standards across entire sub-consultant chain with no documentation to support it
- Healthcare: FMH attestation commits physician to human oversight standards across all AI-assisted clinical decisions; documentation built for carrier creates record usable in liability proceedings

**The common mechanism:** The carrier form is designed as a risk assessment instrument — it creates a documented standard that the ICP attested to meeting. Signing the form without understanding the specific language commits the ICP to a standard that existing documentation cannot support. The carrier form is not a compliance checklist — it is an accountability architecture that creates legal exposure for the ICP personally.

**The market signal:** "The form created a standard I can't meet." The ICP who understands this is the ICP who is ready to act. The service that can show them exactly what standard their signature committed them to — and what evidence would satisfy that standard — converts at the highest rate.

## Problem 05: The Geneva Bilingual Regulatory Environment Creates Undocumented Complexity

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Carrier forms in English; Ordre proceedings in French; attorney signs English attestation, faces French disciplinary proceedings — documentation language mismatch
- Finance: FINMA Guidance in English; institutional client DDQ in English; FINMA examination may reference French legal concepts; documentation doesn't match regulatory reality
- Consulting: Client RFPs in English; nFADP in French; EU AI Act in English; no Geneva-calibrated bilingual documentation methodology
- Healthcare: HRA in French; nFADP in French/English; FMH proceedings in French; carrier attestation in English; documentation doesn't align with the regulatory language of the authority that will review it

**The common mechanism:** The Geneva professional practice operates in two regulatory languages (English for carrier forms, client DDQs, EU AI Act; French for Ordre, FINMA proceedings, Swiss domestic regulation). Documentation built in one language may not serve proceedings conducted in the other language. The documentation system must be designed to function in both regulatory language environments.

**The market signal:** "The carrier form is in English. My Ordre speaks in French." This phrase appears in every vertical as the ICP begins to understand that their documentation may not protect them in proceedings conducted in the other language. The Geneva-specific bilingual documentation problem is not addressed by any competitor and creates a specific service opportunity.

---

## Problem 06: The Evidence Standard Has Shifted — The Market Has Not Adjusted

**Appears in:**
- Legal: Carrier requests "evidence of supervision" — attorney produces policy document; carrier asks for records of actual supervision events; attorney has none
- Finance: FINMA examination asks for "evidence of AI system monitoring" — CIO produces governance framework document; FINMA asks for log records of actual AI system behavior; none exist
- Consulting: Client RFP asks for "evidence of AI contribution disclosure" — managing partner produces methodology description; client asks for records of how specific AI contributions were documented on specific engagements; no records exist
- Healthcare: Carrier asks for "evidence of human oversight" — physician produces FMH attestation; carrier asks for contemporaneous records of AI interpretation layer before clinical decision; none exist

**The common mechanism:** The carrier and regulatory standard has shifted from policy documentation (2024) to evidence of actual practice (2025–2026). The market response — AI governance frameworks, policy templates, guidance documents — is calibrated for the 2024 standard. The 2026 evidence standard requires a different documentation paradigm: contemporaneous event records, not periodic summary documents. The gap between policy infrastructure and evidence capability is structural and cannot be closed by updating policies.

**The market signal:** "I have a policy. I don't have records." This is the most common statement from the ICP who has already tried to respond to carrier demands. The policy infrastructure built in 2024 cannot satisfy the evidence requirements of 2026. The service response to this problem is not "update your policy" — it is "build an evidence production system."

## What's Creating Urgency Across All Verticals

### Universal Acute Triggers
1. **Retroactive documentation crisis** — the ICP who adopted AI 12–24 months ago and now faces carrier renewal; largest market segment by count
2. **Broker referral pressure** — broker community is actively referring; the broker who doesn't refer is exposed to the claim that follows
3. **Carrier renewal season** — June 19 SBA Day of Lawyers creates acute legal trigger; finance and consulting renewal cycles follow
4. **Client questionnaire escalation** — institutional clients increasingly require AI governance evidence as commercial entry condition

### Universal Decision Blockers
1. **Retroactive reconstruction paralysis** — "I can't document two years of AI use" → inaction → compounding accelerates
2. **Three-vector confusion** — linear solutions attempted and failed; ICP doesn't know why they didn't work
3. **Bilingual documentation invisibility** — ICP doesn't know their documentation language may not work in regulatory proceedings
4. **Broker referral trust barrier** — ICP needs broker referral to act; broker needs service credibility to refer

---

## Market State Summary

The cross-vertical market signal in C308 is the broker-mediated urgency wave. The ICP in every vertical is being pushed toward action by their broker, carrier, or professional association — not pulled by their own discovery. The referral comes first, then the engagement. The service that captures the broker referral channel and delivers on the broker's behalf will grow faster than the service that drives direct ICP acquisition.

The second signal is the retroactive documentation crisis. The ICP who adopted AI 12–24 months ago is now the majority of the market. The service that can credibly address the retroactive gap — not just promise forward-looking solutions — will convert the largest market segment.

The third signal is the compounding vector problem. Sophisticated ICPs have already tried "document going forward" and found it insufficient. The service that explains why and offers a different solution architecture earns credibility that leads to engagement.
