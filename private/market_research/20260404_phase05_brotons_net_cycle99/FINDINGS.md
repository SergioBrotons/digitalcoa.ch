## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Content Distribution Channels — LinkedIn + Substack + Search
- LinkedIn dominant for Geneva professional discovery; organic thought leadership performs well for peer-level content
- Substack: growing Geneva professional newsletter medium; recommendations algorithm helps discovery; EN+FR multilingual support
- Direct search: long-tail regulatory keywords (lower volume, high intent) for reference content
- Geneva institutional networks: UNOG/WTO/WHO/ICRC staff following sector-specific content; professional association distribution
- Conference spillover: Geneva's dense conference calendar creating content demand around events
- Recommended priority: LinkedIn (professional network) + Substack (direct relationship) + Search (reference discovery)

### Finding 02: Thought Leadership Performance Measurement
- Quality metrics: return visitor rate, time on page, LinkedIn shares with substantive commentary, inbound professional contacts, competitive citations
- Proxy metrics without analytics: LinkedIn engagement rate, Substack open rates, inbound contact volume, professional mentions
- What NOT to measure: total page views (vanity), social likes (low signal), bounce rate (readers may leave satisfied)
- Key insight: thought leadership success measured over 12-24 months; consistency of quality and cadence matters more than any single piece
- Geneva-specific: small professional community — word-of-mouth and professional network referrals measurable through inbound contact tracking

### Finding 03: Substack + Owned Website Hybrid Strategy
- Substack as primary distribution (built-in discovery, subscriber relationship, lower overhead)
- Owned website as repository/SEO foundation (long-term brand asset, evergreen reference content)
- Content flow: Substack summaries link to full owned website articles
- Cross-pollination: Substack audience → website exploration → broader content discovery
- Aligns with "Brotons.net = hub; Substack = content platform" confirmed in C98
- Substack algorithm recommendation network valuable for audience development

### Finding 04: Bilingual Content Strategy (EN+FR)
- Not full 1:1 translation — too resource intensive
- Tier 1 (always translate): Geneva-specific regulatory content, local market insights, Geneva institutions coverage
- Tier 2 (selectively translate): international topics with Geneva angle, synthesis pieces
- Tier 3 (English only): broad international context, technical deep-dives for international audience
- Swiss French voice differs from Parisian — more direct, international vocabulary
- French audience reached through CCIG networks, professional associations, French-language LinkedIn, Swiss Romandie newsletters
- Substack bilingual newsletter: EN primary, FR summary/highlights for key pieces

### Finding 05: Content Partnership Opportunities
- Geneva think tanks: Graduate Institute (IHEID), CCIG, Geneva International Forum, ICTSD
- Professional associations: Geneva Bar, SFAMA, economiesuisse Geneva, AGEF
- Geneva media: Le Temps (digital), AGEFI, Genève.home
- IO knowledge platforms: UNOG Library, WTO Research Portal, WHO Digital Health Observatory, ICRC Humanitarian Knowledge Hub
- Partnership types: cross-promotion, co-authorship, event-linked content, data partnerships, newsletter exchanges
- Benefits: audience development, authority association, distribution amplification, SEO inbound links
- Risk: must not compromise editorial independence

### Finding 06: Editorial Calendar Strategy — Cadence Discipline
- Quality over quantity: 2-4 substantive pieces per month outperforms 8 shallow pieces
- Weekly: Substack newsletter (short-form, 5-8 min read)
- Monthly: long-form article on Brotons.net (comprehensive, 15-20 min read)
- Quarterly: synthesis piece pulling multi-phase research
- Event-linked: rapid-response pieces for Geneva institutional decisions
- 6-week forward planning with Geneva institutional calendar alignment
- Reserve 20% capacity for event-driven opportunities
- Geneva calendar: WEF/Davos Jan, Geneva Summit Feb, WSIS events Apr-Jun, UNGA Sep, WTO MC Dec, FINMA/SNBC rate decisions year-round
- Biggest failure mode: cadence collapse. Sustainable pace beats ambitious cadences that cause burnout.
