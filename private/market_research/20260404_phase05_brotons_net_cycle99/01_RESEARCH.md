## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 1)

### EN
**Content Distribution Channels for Geneva Professional Thought Leadership**

Discovery patterns for Geneva professional audience:
- LinkedIn dominant: Geneva professionals (finance, legal, consulting, IO/NGO) heavily LinkedIn-engaged; organic thought leadership performs well for peer-level content
- Substack: growing as Geneva professional newsletter medium; Geneva audiences subscribing to cross-border business/policy newsletters; Substack recommendations algorithm helps discovery
- Direct search: Geneva practitioners searching specific regulatory/industry queries (lower volume, higher intent than general business)
- Geneva institutional networks: UNOG, WTO, WHO, ICRC staff following institutional and sector-specific content; professional association distribution (SVS, Geneva Bar, swissholdings)
- Conference/event spillover: Geneva's dense conference calendar (Geneva Summit, WTO MC, WSIS, etc.) creating content demand around events

Content distribution strategy considerations:
- LinkedIn pulse/opinion articles: high visibility for Geneva professional network; easy bilingual adaptation (EN/FR)
- Substack: subscription model creates direct audience relationship; recommendations algorithm can surface to new readers; cross-promotion with Geneva policy newsletters valuable
- Professional association newsletters: lower reach but very targeted; Geneva-specific publications (CCIG, Geneva Chamber of Commerce)
- Search: long-tail regulatory keywords (lower volume, high intent) drive discovery for reference content

Key insight: Geneva professional audience values intellectual depth and Geneva-specificity over general business commentary. Content distribution should prioritize LinkedIn (professional network) + Substack (direct relationship) + Search (reference discovery).

### FR
**Canaux de distribution de contenu pour la pensée directive professionnelle genevoise**

Modèles de découverte pour l'audience professionnelle genevoise :
- LinkedIn dominant : professionnels genevois (finance, juridique, conseil, IO/ONG) fortement engagés sur LinkedIn
- Substack : croissance comme médium de newsletter professionnelle genevoise ; recommandations algorithmiques aidant la découverte
- Recherche directe : praticiens genevois cherchant des requêtes réglementaires/spécifiques
- Réseaux institutionnels genevois : personnel NU/OMC/OMS/CICR suivant du contenu institutionnel et sectoriel
- Conference/event spillover : calendrier dense de conférences genevois créant une demande de contenu autour des événements

Stratégie de distribution de contenu :
- Articles d'opinion LinkedIn : haute visibilité pour le réseau professionnel genevois
- Substack : modèle d'abonnement créant une relation directe avec l'audience
- Newsletters d'associations professionnelles : portée plus faible mais très ciblée
- Recherche : mots-clés réglementaires long-tail (volume plus faible, intention plus élevée)
