## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 3)

### EN
**Substack vs. Owned Website for Geneva Thought Leadership**

Platform comparison for Brotons.net content strategy:

**Substack:**
- Pros: built-in distribution (Substack recommendations), email delivery, subscriber relationship, lower technical overhead, EN+FR multilingual support, easy discovery through Substack reader network
- Cons: brand on Substack domain (substack.com), limited design control, algorithm dependency, competitor adjacency (mixes with all other Substack content)
- Best for: regular newsletter cadence, building direct subscriber relationship, testing content angles quickly
- Geneva fit: Substack strong for Geneva policy/business audience; many Geneva professionals already Substack subscribers

**Owned Website (Brotons.net):**
- Pros: full brand control, SEO ownership, content longevity, no platform dependency, integration with other Brotons.net content, clearer brand identity
- Cons: requires distribution effort (no algorithmic boost), technical maintenance, slower audience building
- Best for: foundational thought leadership content, SEO-targeted reference articles, brand authority building
- Geneva fit: Geneva professionals searching specific topics will find owned website content; better for evergreen reference content

**Recommended hybrid approach:**
- Substack as primary distribution channel for new content (builds subscriber relationship, gets discovery)
- Owned website as repository and SEO foundation (long-term brand asset)
- Content flows: Substack summaries link to full owned website articles
- Cross-pollination: Substack audience → website exploration → broader content discovery

Geneva-specific: Substack's EU/policy audience overlaps well with Brotons.net Geneva focus. The two-platform approach aligns with "hub vs. content platform" clarification (confirmed in C98: Brotons.net = hub; Substack = content distribution)

### FR
**Substack vs. site propriétés pour la pensée directive genevoise**

Comparaison de plateforme pour la stratégie de contenu Brotons.net :

**Substack :**
- Avantages : distribution intégrée (recommandations Substack), livraison par email, relation abonnée, frais généraux techniques plus faibles
- Inconvénients : marque sur domaine Substack, contrôle de conception limité, dépendance algorithmique
- Idéal pour : cadence régulière de newsletter, construction de relation abonnée directe
- Adaptation Genève : Substack fort pour audience politique/affaires genevoise

**Site propriétés (Brotons.net) :**
- Avantages : contrôle complet de marque, propriété SEO, longévité du contenu, pas de dépendance à une plateforme
- Inconvénients : effort de distribution requis, maintenance technique, construction d'audience plus lente
- Idéal pour : contenu de pensée directive fondamental, articles de référence ciblés SEO
- Adaptation Genève : professionnels genevois cherchant des sujets spécifiques trouveront le contenu du site propriétés

**Approche hybride recommandée :**
- Substack comme canal de distribution principal pour nouveau contenu
- Site propriétés comme dépôt et fondement SEO
- Flux de contenu : résumés Substack liant vers articles complets sur site propriétés
- Pollinisation croisée : audience Substack → exploration website → découverte de contenu plus large
