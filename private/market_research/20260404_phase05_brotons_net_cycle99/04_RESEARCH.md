## Findings (Cycle 99 — Slot 4)

### EN
**Bilingual Content Strategy (EN+FR) for Geneva**

Geneva's linguistic reality: multilingual professional community. French is the local language; English is the international business language. Geneva professionals typically bilingual (FR/EN) with many also speaking German/Italian.

French-language audience characteristics:
- Geneva French-speaking professionals (lawyers, bankers, consultants, IO staff) consume content in French for local topics, in English for international topics
- Swiss Romandie professional media: Le Temps (now digital-only), Bilan, PME magazine, Agefi — limited Geneva-specific business coverage
- Geneva Chamber of Commerce (CCIG) communications in French; professional associations FR-dominant
- French-language Geneva content has less competition than English-language Geneva content

Content strategy recommendation:
- Not full 1:1 translation — too resource-intensive
- Tier 1 (always translate): Geneva-specific regulatory content, local market insights, Geneva institutions coverage
- Tier 2 (selectively translate): international topics with Geneva angle, synthesis pieces, audience development content
- Tier 3 (English only): broad international context, technical deep-dives for international audience
- Substack bilingual newsletter: EN primary, FR summary/highlights for key pieces

French voice considerations:
- Swiss French professional tone differs from Parisian French — more direct, international vocabulary
- Geneva-specific references (CCIG, REP, OCPM, cantonal institutions) require local knowledge
- Avoid "French translation of English content" approach — should feel native to each language audience

Audience development: French-language Geneva audience reached through CCIG networks, professional associations, French-language LinkedIn, and Swiss Romandie newsletter ecosystems

### FR
**Stratégie de contenu bilingue (EN+FR) pour Genève**

Réalité linguistique de Genève : communauté professionnelle multilingue. Le français est la langue locale ; l'anglais est la langue des affaires internationale. Professionnels genevois typiquement bilingues (FR/EN).

Caractéristiques de l'audience francophone :
- Professionnels genevois francophones (avocats, banquiers, consultants, personnel IO) consommant du contenu en français pour les sujets locaux, en anglais pour les sujets internationaux
- Médias professionnels romands : Le Temps (digital), Bilan, PME magazine, Agefi — couverture affaires genevoise limitée
- Communications de la CCIG en français ; associations professionnelles FR dominantes
- Contenu genevois francophone a moins de concurrence que le contenu anglophone

Recommandation de stratégie de contenu :
- Pas de traduction 1:1 — trop intensif en ressources
- Tier 1 (toujours traduire) : contenu réglementaire genevois spécifique, aperçus du marché local
- Tier 2 (traduire sélectivement) : sujets internationaux avec angle genevois, pièces de synthèse
- Tier 3 (anglais seulement) : contexte international large, approfondissements techniques
- Newsletter bilingue Substack : EN primaire, résumés/highlights FR pour pièces clés
