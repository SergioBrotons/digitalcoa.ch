### Finding 1: Switzerland-UK MRA Art. 26 Review — Q3 2026 Financial Services Deadline
The Mutual Recognition Agreement (MRA) Article 26 review, scheduled for Q3 2026, creates a contingent advisory demand for Geneva-based financial services firms with UK operations. Three scenarios are on the table: (a) full equivalence extension; (b) limited equivalence with sector carve-outs; or (c) expiry of UK equivalence for Swiss firms, requiring FCA authorization. Geneva-based asset managers, family offices, and insurance intermediaries with UK distribution face immediate strategic planning needs. Advisory work includes: scenario modeling, contingency planning, and regulatory pathway analysis for each outcome. Geneva trade associations (SFAMA, VSMA) are actively preparing position papers.

### Finding 2: CSRD Phase 2 Large Entity Deadline — Double Materiality Assessment Advisory Surge
The CSRD Phase 2 large-entity deadline (turnover >EUR 50M, >250 employees) is creating a June 2026 advisory surge in Switzerland. Geneva-based multinationals with EU subsidiaries are the primary target, but Swiss domestic CSRD-equivalent reporting is being driven by cantonal requirements in Geneva and Zurich. Double materiality assessment (DMA) methodology—assessing both financial impact and impact on the environment—is the primary advisory gap. Geneva consulting firms and sustainability advisors face a 6-month window to capture DMA advisory mandates before the regulatory deadline forces reactive (and higher-margin) compliance work.

### Finding 3: DORA ICT Third-Party Risk Management — Financial Sector US Hyperscaler Dependency Advisory
The Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), in force for Swiss financial institutions with EU operations or counterparties, creates immediate ICT third-party risk management (TPRM) advisory demand. Geneva-based banks, asset managers, and insurance firms with AWS/Azure/GCP dependencies face TPRM gap assessments required under DORA Art. 5-6. The critical advisory question: how to achieve DORA compliance when US hyperscalers refuse to sign EU-standard ICT contracts. Geneva advisory firms are developing workaround frameworks, including: (a) contractual fallback clauses; (b) DORA-compliant exit planning; and (c) EU-based alternative cloud migration assessments. This is a high-margin, time-sensitive advisory category.

### Finding 4: Geneva Professional Services AI Workflow Re-Engineering Advisory
Geneva-based law firms, fiduciary companies, and consulting practices are increasingly seeking advisory on AI workflow re-engineering, particularly for: (a) contract review and due diligence automation; (b) regulatory change monitoring and client alerting; (c) financial modeling and forecasting automation; and (d) client onboarding and KYC automation. The advisory opportunity spans process design, tool selection (with specific attention to EU AI Act compliance), and change management for professional services staff. Geneva boutique advisory firms with legal and compliance expertise are uniquely positioned vs. generic AI consultants who lack sector-specific knowledge.

### Finding 5: Swiss Precision Manufacturing FDI Nearshoring — SECO Advisory Demand
US tariff-driven reshoring is generating advisory demand for Swiss precision manufacturing firms considering nearshoring to Switzerland or diversification of production outside of Asia. SECO's FDI screening regime, which became fully operational in 2024, requires notification for acquisitions of Swiss firms in sensitive sectors (technology, healthcare, infrastructure). Geneva-based industrial groups and their advisors face: (a) FDI screening application support; (b) nearshoring feasibility analysis for French/German/Swiss locations; and (c) supply chain resilience advisory for US tariff exposure. This advisory category is growing with each escalation in US trade policy.

### Finding 6: Geneva Fractional CFO/Finance Director Specialization — CSRD/DORA/ESG Premium
The fractional CFO market in Geneva continues to show premium widening for specialists in CSRD, DORA, and ESG compliance. Day rates for CSRD-specialist fractional CFOs have reached CHF 2,500-3,500/day (vs. CHF 1,800-2,500 for generalist fractional CFOs). The premium is driven by: (a) scarcity of dual finance/sustainability expertise; (b) time-sensitive regulatory deadlines; and (c) board-level accountability creating demand for experienced senior professionals rather than junior sustainability staff. Geneva boutique advisory firms and independent CFOs operating fractional models are capturing this premium, particularly for SME/mid-market clients who cannot afford Big 4 retainers.
