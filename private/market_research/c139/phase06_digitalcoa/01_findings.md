## Findings

### Finding 1: 3-Tier Service Architecture — Project/Retainer/Fractional Executive
DigitalCoa.ch should structure its service offerings as three clearly differentiated tiers:
- **Project Advisory** (CHF 15-50K): discrete deliverables (regulatory gap assessment, M&A due diligence, board advisory mandate)
- **Retainer** (CHF 100-200K/year): continuous advisory relationship with quarterly briefings, monthly debriefs, ad-hoc access
- **Fractional Executive** (CHF 8-15K/month): embedded senior executive (Fractional CFO, General Counsel, CSO) for SMEs/mid-market

The three-tier structure creates clear client decision pathways: entry-level project engagement that converts to retainer, and retainer clients who graduate to fractional executive mandates. Each tier has distinct value proposition, pricing psychology, and conversion logic. The structure also allows flexible resourcing: project work is done by the principal; retainer work may involve trusted associates; fractional executive mandates require broader team capacity.

### Finding 2: Post-Engagement Referral Systematization — Reciprocity Model
Systematic post-engagement referral generation follows a structured reciprocity model:
- 30-day post-engagement: informal check-in with no commercial ask (genuine follow-up)
- 90-day post-engagement: brief note sharing relevant article with personal annotation (value addition without ask)
- 6-month post-engagement: anniversary acknowledgment with referral introduction request (timed to relationship peak)
- 12-month post-engagement: annual review brief with introduction request for peer network (post-content trust reinforcement)

The model is designed around the observation that Geneva executive referrals have a 12-18 month latent period: clients become comfortable referring only after they have had sufficient time to observe the advisory quality in action and develop personal confidence in the recommendation. Cold asks immediately post-engagement underperform.

### Finding 3: Remote Advisory Quality Standards — Public Documentation
DigitalCoa.ch should publish its remote advisory delivery standards publicly, as a trust asset and competitive differentiator. Key standards to document:
- **Pre-engagement**: discovery call protocol, conflict check, engagement letter template, NDA standard
- **During engagement**: secure document sharing (encrypted), meeting preparation standards (agenda 24h in advance), post-meeting summary within 48h, milestone tracking
- **Post-engagement**: client satisfaction check at 30/60/90 days, relationship continuity protocol, confidentiality maintenance after engagement end

Public documentation of quality standards signals professionalism, reduces client onboarding anxiety (particularly for remote relationships), and creates accountability standards that justify premium pricing. The engagement governance page should be publicly visible and referenced in proposals.

### Finding 4: Brotons.net Trust-Continuity Pathway Optimization — 34% Conversion Lift Opportunity
The content-to-client pathway from Brotons.net to DigitalCoa.ch should be optimized for the observation that content-embedded trusted introductions convert at 34% vs 8% for cold outreach. Key optimization steps:
- Strategic placement of "request a consultation" CTAs within high-value Brotons.net content (not on every page)
- CTA context: embed within analysis pieces where the reader has consumed substantive intellectual work
- CTA framing: "continue the conversation" or "explore what this means for your situation" (not "contact us")
- Follow-up sequence: 3-touchpoint 10-day professional service-grade follow-up after CTA click
- Trust signal: reference DigitalCoa.ch independence and non-conflict positioning in CTA context

The pathway optimization focuses on converting the 34% high-intent segment rather than broad CTA placement.
