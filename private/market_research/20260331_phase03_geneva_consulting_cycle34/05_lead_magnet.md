## Core Frame

### The Urgency Proposition
> The EU AI Act GPAI has been operative since August 2, 2025. Transparency obligations are already active. If your firm has EU-nexus clients and has been using AI without disclosing it — you have already been in violation for approximately 7+ months.

### The Skills Gap + Failure Rate Proposition
> 73% of consulting firms that attempted AI adoption abandoned the project. The primary reason: skills gap. 43% of firms have no formal AI policy and no plans to create one. The problem is not willingness — it is knowing where to start and how to govern it at firm level.

### The Shadow AI Proposition
> Your consultants are almost certainly using AI tools today — without firm knowledge, without disclosure, and potentially without proper data handling. That is Shadow AI. Before you build an AI policy, you need to know what you already have running.

---

## Framework: 5 Decisions for Small Consulting Firms

### Decision 0: Shadow AI Audit (NEW in Cycle 34 — Pre-Decision)
**"Before you decide anything, know what you already have."**

The 43% no-policy finding means most small consulting firms have NO IDEA:
- Which consultants are using which AI tools
- What client data is being processed through AI
- Whether AI outputs are being disclosed (or not)
- What the current exposure actually is

**This is Decision 0 — not "should we adopt AI" but "what AI are we already running?"**

- 5-minute internal survey: which AI tools, for what purposes, on what data
- Output: Shadow AI inventory — the baseline for every subsequent decision
- Urgency: Immediately relevant under GPAI transparency obligations (already operative)

---

### Decision 1: Do You Have EU-Nexus? (GPAI Threshold)
**"If yes, GPAI is already your problem."**

- EU-based clients ✓
- EU-based projects ✓
- EU-facing operations ✓
- Geneva international organizations ✓

**If any yes:** GPAI transparency obligations are active NOW. No grace period.

---

### Decision 2: nFADP Art. 5(c) — AI Disclosure Baseline
**"Swiss law already requires you to disclose AI use to clients."**

- Applies to any AI involvement in client work (proposals, analysis, reports, recommendations)
- No new law needed — nFADP is technology-neutral
- FDPIC expects disclosure (purpose + functionality + data sources)

---

### Decision 3: GPAI Transparency — EU-Nexus Clients
**"If EU clients are involved, you must disclose AI-generated or AI-assisted content."**

- GPAI Article 50: AI-generated text, images, data must be disclosed
- Technical documentation required for GPAI
- Aug 2, 2026: high-risk provisions activate (adds conformity assessment)

---

### Decision 4: Build a Firm-Level AI Governance Policy
**"The 43% with no plans are accumulating risk. The firms that act now get first-mover advantage."**

A small firm AI governance policy should cover:
1. Approved AI tools (Shadow AI inventory → approved list)
2. Client disclosure standards (what requires disclosure, how)
3. Data handling rules (no client confidential data in external AI tools without DPA)
4. Output review requirements (human oversight of AI-generated content)
5. Documented consent framework
