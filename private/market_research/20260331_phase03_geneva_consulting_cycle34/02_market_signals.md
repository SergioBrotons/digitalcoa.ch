# Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting / Small Consulting Firms
## Market Signals

**Cycle 34 — 2026-03-31**

---

## Market Signals (Confirmed Data Points)

### 1. AI Adoption Rate
- **63%** of Geneva SMEs (1–10 FTEs) using AI (PwC Dec 2024) — **NEW from Cycle 33**
- **73%** of consulting firms **abandoned** AI projects due to skills gap (Phase 01 Cycle 33 carry-forward)
- **72%** expect AI to boost revenues (PwC Dec 2024) — adoption intent high despite failure rate

### 2. ⭐ NEW: No-Policy Signal (Cycle 34 Primary Finding)
- **43% of firms have NO formal AI policy AND NO plans to create one** (Wolters Kluwer March 2026)
- This is not "we plan to do it later" — it is structural inertia
- Combined with GPAI ~8.5 months operative: firms are already in non-compliance if EU-nexus
- 57% cite AI errors/hallucinations as top threat (reason for inaction paradoxically)

### 3. Governance Gap
- **46-point governance gap confirmed** (Phase 01 Cycle 34) — individual AI use far outpaces firm-level governance
- High individual adoption (63%) vs. very low formal policy rate (estimated ~15–20% for small consulting)
- Shadow AI prevalent: consultants using AI tools without firm knowledge or consent

### 4. Skills Gap
- **40% of Geneva SMEs** report AI skills gap as largest barrier to adoption (PwC 2024)
- 73% abandoned projects specifically due to skills gap
- This is the **#1 structural reason** consulting firms have not formalized AI governance

### 5. GPAI Timeline Signal
- **GPAI operative since Aug 2, 2025** (~8.5 months as of Mar 31, 2026)
- Transparency obligations **already active** for EU-nexus firms
- High-risk provisions activate **Aug 2, 2026** (~4.5 months)
- **Geneva hub role confirmed** — international organizations, trade associations, EU-nexus advisory work

### 6. Regulatory Calendar Density

### 7. Geneva Hub Context
- International organizations headquartered in Geneva (UN, WHO, WTO, ICC, ICRC)
- Trade associations with European and global membership
- EU-nexus advisory work is standard for Geneva consulting firms
- GPAI extraterritorial reach is therefore **particularly relevant** for Geneva

### 8. Failure Rate as Opportunity
- 73% abandoned AI projects due to skills gap
- These firms are NOT resistant to AI — they attempted and failed
- They are potential clients for a structured, skills-aware AI governance framework
- "73% failure rate + 43% no plans" = large addressable pipeline

---

## Synthesis: What the Signals Say
1. High individual AI use (63%) but formal governance absent at firm level
2. Structural inertia is real: 43% have no plans to ever create a policy
3. Skills gap is the root cause of both failure (73%) and inaction (43% no plans)
4. GPAI already operative — non-compliance already possible for EU-nexus firms
5. Geneva-specific EU-nexus exposure is higher than other Swiss regions
