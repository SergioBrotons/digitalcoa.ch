## Dual Compliance Architecture: nFADP + EU AI Act (GPAI)

### Layer 1: Swiss — nFADP (Operative Now)
- **Status:** Active since Sep 1, 2023
- **Key obligation for consulting firms:** Art. 5(c) — transparency about AI involvement in client work
- **Enforcement:** FDPIC (no direct fines for firms; criminal prosecution route for intentional failures)
- **Application:** Any consulting firm using AI in client-facing work (proposals, analysis, reports, recommendations)
- **Geneva exposure:** High (international client base, data residency requirements)

### Layer 2: EU — GPAI / EU AI Act (Operative Since Aug 2, 2025)
- **Status:** **~8.5 months ACTIVE** — transparency obligations already in force
- **Key obligation:** Transparency about AI use (GPAI Article 50 — users must disclose AI-generated content)
- **EU-nexus threshold:** Any firm with EU-based clients, EU projects, or EU-facing operations
- **Geneva exposure:** **High** — Geneva is EU-nexus by definition for many consulting firms (international orgs, trade associations, cross-border advisory)
- **High-risk provisions:** Activate Aug 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)
- **Penalty:** Up to €35M or 7% global turnover for non-compliance

### Critical Timing: Already In Violation
Firms with EU-nexus clients who have been using AI tools without disclosing AI involvement have been in **active GPAI violation since Aug 2, 2025** (~7+ months).

## Unique Aspect: Shadow AI Exposure for Consultants

Consulting firms face a **specific Shadow AI risk** that legal and finance firms do not:
- Consultants use AI for **client-adjacent work** (market research, competitive analysis, report drafting)
- Client confidentiality may be compromised by unsanctioned AI tool use
- Client expectations of "consultant judgment" vs. "AI-assisted output" create professional liability exposure
- This is distinct from internal use — it is **client-facing exposure**
