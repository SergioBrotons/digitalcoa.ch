# Finding 05 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 01: Geneva Legal
**Source:** Swiss legislative framework, ESG regulatory trend analysis  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Swiss Mandatory Human Rights Due Diligence (mHRDD) Drives Surge in Corporate Governance Legal Work**

Switzerland's revised obligation framework — building on the 2021 Constitutional amendment and subsequent implementation legislation — is moving from voluntary ESG to mandatory human rights due diligence for large Swiss companies. Geneva-based corporations are directly in scope.

Key dimensions of the compliance wave:

**1. mHRDD applies to companies meeting two thresholds:** (a) CHF 40+ million turnover and (b) 500+ employees globally. Many Geneva-headquartered firms meet both — creating immediate legal obligation, not aspiration.

**2. Supply chain due diligence is the primary obligation:** Companies must map their supply chains, identify human rights and environmental risks, take remediation measures, and report publicly. Legal counsel is needed for: contract clause drafting, supplier audit protocols, remediation frameworks, and board-level reporting.

**3. Civil liability exposure:** Non-compliance creates civil liability risk — shareholders and NGOs are increasingly willing to sue. Geneva firms are seeing early case development around director liability for inadequate due diligence processes.

**4. Intersection with EU CSDDD:** The EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) has extraterritorial reach for Swiss companies operating in the EU. Geneva companies doing significant EU business face dual-standard compliance requirements — a premium advisory area.

**5. Greenwashing liability:** The Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) and the Swiss Takeover Board have both flagged greenwashing as an enforcement priority. Advertising and disclosure claims tied to human rights or ESG performance now carry litigation risk.
