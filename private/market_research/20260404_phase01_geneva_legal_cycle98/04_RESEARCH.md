# Finding 04 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 01: Geneva Legal
**Source:** Swiss legal framework, Geneva arbitration ecosystem, industry intelligence  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Swiss Arbitration Seats Lose Appeal Among Some US Companies as Neutrality Concerns Rise — Geneva Remains Resilient for EU-Asia Disputes**

Geneva has long been the gold standard for international arbitration in Europe — trusted by parties from the US, Asia, Middle East, and Europe. However, 2025-2026 developments have created a bifurcation:

**1. US parties increasingly prefer Dubai (DIFC) or Singapore as neutral seats** over traditional European centers, citing perceived EU regulatory alignment and concerns about Swiss banking transparency obligations interfering with arbitral proceedings. This is a real trend but limited to USlitigants with strong bilateral considerations.

**2. Geneva remains preeminent for EU-Asia-Middle East disputes:** The ICC Court of Arbitration (Paris) and the Geneva Chamber of Arbitration (CAG) both handle large caseloads of disputes where neither party is US-influenced. Geneva-seated arbitration remains the preferred neutral ground for Chinese, Indian, Middle Eastern, and European commercial parties.

**3. Russian/CIS disputes displaced from Western seats:** Russian parties and parties with Russian counterparty exposure have been effectively displaced from London, Paris, and Zurich as preferred seats. Some are redirecting to Geneva — creating unexpected caseload in a market segment that is ethically complex to serve.

**4. Geneva-seated investor-state arbitration (ISA) growing:** Switzerland has ratified multiple bilateral investment treaties (BITs) that enable investor-state claims against foreign governments. Geneva-based law firms have active ISA practices serving Swiss outward investors claiming against host-state regulatory actions.

**5. Emergency arbitrator procedures gaining use:** The CAG's emergency arbitrator mechanism is increasingly used in commercial disputes where interim relief is needed before a full tribunal is constituted — a fast-growing niche that generates premium fees.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** A nuanced analysis of how geopolitical shifts are reordering the arbitration landscape — who is going where and why — would be distinctive and widely read among Geneva's arbitration community.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** Executive clients involved in cross-border commercial disputes need clear, practical guidance on whether to arbitrate in Geneva vs. other seats, and why Geneva remains compelling for non-US parties.
