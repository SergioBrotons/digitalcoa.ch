## Finding

**Geneva's IP Dispute Caseload at WIPO Grows as AI-Generated Content Raises Novel Ownership Questions**

The World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO), headquartered in Geneva, administers the global IP filing system and handles domain name disputes, patent coordination, and IP licensing disputes. WIPO's recent guidance on AI-generated works is creating new advisory territory for Geneva IP practitioners.

Key developments:

**1. AI inventorship still unresolved under Swiss law:** The Swiss Federal Institute of Intellectual Property (IPI) has not issued definitive guidance on AI-assisted inventions. This creates a grey zone that companies filing patents in Switzerland must navigate — and for which they need specialized advisory support.

**2. WIPO Arbitration and Mediation Center activity:** WIPO's center handles disputes arising from .swiss domain names, Madrid System trademark disputes, and international patent licensing conflicts. Geneva-based law firms with WIPO credentials have a structural advantage in this niche.

**3. Trade secret protection urgency:** As AI tools proliferate in Geneva's pharmaceutical, watchmaking, and financial sectors, trade secret litigation is rising. The 2023 Swiss TPA revisions improved trade secret protection — but enforcement remains technically complex.

**4. Cross-border IP enforcement:** Geneva's international character means IP disputes routinely involve parallel proceedings in multiple jurisdictions. Law firms with international arbitration capability (Geneva-seated) are preferred by tech and pharma clients.

**5. Swiss compulsory licensing precedent:** Switzerland's willingness to invoke compulsory licensing (recently relevant during COVID-19 vaccine supply disputes) creates ongoing legal precedent that pharma and biotech clients track closely.
