# Finding 06 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 01: Geneva Legal
**Source:** Swiss banking law, FINMA regulatory framework, Geneva private banking intelligence  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**FINMA Intensifies Focus on Beneficial Ownership Verification as Swiss Private Banking Faces Renewed US DOJ Scrutiny**

Geneva's private banking sector — long the backbone of the city's legal economy — faces a new regulatory pressure cycle. FINMA has updated its anti-money laundering (AML) supervisory guidance with sharper beneficial ownership verification requirements, coinciding with renewed US Department of Justice focus on Swiss banking secrecy in cross-border tax cases.

Key dimensions:

**1. Beneficial ownership thresholds tightened:** FINMA now requires private banks to identify and verify beneficial owners at the level of 10% (down from 25%) ownership stakes, and to continuously monitor for shell company structures across multiple jurisdictions simultaneously.

**2. US DOJ "revoked" foreign bank compliance programs:** The US DOJ has signalled increased willingness to revoke Non-Prosecution Agreements (NPAs) and Deferred Prosecution Agreements (DPAs) with Swiss banks found to have inadequate AML programs — raising the stakes for banks with legacy US compliance exposure.

**3. Cross-border client onboarding complexity:** Geneva banks onboarding new clients from high-risk jurisdictions need enhanced due diligence (EDD) that goes beyond standard KYC — creating significant legal advisory and documentation work.

**4. Whistleblower protections strengthened:** Switzerland's revised whistleblower law (revSwbIG) creates stronger protections and reporting channels for bank employees flagging AML concerns. Banks need updated internal compliance frameworks — legal advisory work.

**5. CRS and FATCA reporting under strain:** The Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and US FATCA compliance requirements are being applied with increased rigor by both Swiss and US authorities. Reporting errors create penalty exposure and retroactive client relationship review obligations.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** Geneva banking secrecy has been a political lightning rod for decades. A clear-eyed, non-sensational analysis of how AML compliance actually works — and what it means for legitimate private banking clients — would be valuable and distinctive.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** UHNW clients and family offices operating through Geneva structures need to understand their compliance obligations. Advisory content that helps them prepare for enhanced due diligence requests from banks is high-value and conversion-oriented.
