# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva NGO/Tech (C101)

## Source: Domain knowledge + publicly available regulations
## Date: 2026-04-04

## Regulatory Framework — Key Developments for Geneva NGO/Tech

### Priority 1: UN Digital Strategy / AI Governance Integration
- UN Secretary-General's Digital Cooperation roadmap (2020): all UN agencies now implementing digital transformation
- UN Digital Strategy (2022): cloud-first policy (Google Cloud selected); Geneva agencies as implementation sites
- UN AI Advisory Body (2023): 39 members; UN GA AI governance resolution adopted September 2024; ITU/WHO/UNEP designated implementation partners
- Geneva IO exposure: WHO (health AI ethics), ITU (AI standards), WTO (AI and trade), UNEP (AI and environment)
- **Advisory angle:** AI governance compliance for Geneva companies engaging UN/international processes, public-private collaboration frameworks

### Priority 2: Swiss nFADP / Data Protection for NGOs
- Switzerland's revised nFADP (nDSG): fully in force since September 2023; FDPIC enforcement active
- NGOs: particularly exposed — beneficiary data is personal data; consent, retention, security requirements apply
- ICRC: data protection framework for humanitarian data; Geneva NGOs can adopt ICRC framework as best practice
- Cloud services: UN cloud migration (Google Cloud); Swiss NGOs using US cloud providers face additional scrutiny
- **Advisory angle:** nFADP compliance for NGOs, beneficiary data protection framework, cloud compliance, ICRC framework adoption

### Priority 3: Swiss Strategic Goods / Dual-Use Technology Controls
- Strategic Goods Ordinance (SR 946.202.1): controls export of dual-use goods and technologies
- Federal Council updates: ongoing alignment with EU dual-use regulations
- Geneva companies: precision engineering, satellite technology, advanced materials — all affected
- AI for surveillance/demographics: emerging concern; potential future controls on AI applications in strategic goods category
- **Advisory angle:** Export control compliance, strategic goods due diligence, technology transfer review

### Priority 4: Swiss Climate Disclosure Requirements
- Switzerland: voluntary TCFD adoption; Federal Council recommending TCFD/ISSB-aligned disclosure
- Large Swiss companies (est. ~300 in Geneva): increasingly expected to produce climate-related financial disclosures
- UNEP FI: Geneva-based; PRI/TCFD alignment driving ESG disclosure expectations for Geneva financial institutions
- Swiss green bond framework: Federal Council 2021; federal green bond issuance program — impact reporting requirements
- **Advisory angle:** TCFD-aligned climate disclosure framework, ISSB compliance, green bond impact reporting

### Priority 5: Swiss NCSC / Cybersecurity Obligations
- Switzerland's NCSC (National Cyber Security Centre): reporting obligation for cyber incidents affecting critical infrastructure
- NGOs: not all critical infrastructure, but beneficiary data systems increasingly scrutinized
- UN agencies: targeted by sophisticated APT groups; WHO, UN agencies high-profile targets 2020–2024
- SwissCERT: incident response coordination; NCSC guidance for organizations
- **Advisory angle:** Cybersecurity gap assessment for NGOs, incident response planning, cyber insurance, nFADP breach notification
