## Section 2: What's Already Live and Enforceable

You've been under CARF obligations for approximately 3 months.

**What CARF means for your AI:**

You're approximately 3–6 months from implementation.

**AML obligations that will hit soon:**
- 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register

- Enhanced due diligence for AI-assisted client onboarding

## Section 3: The 3-Layer Compliance Stack for Geneva Boutique WM

**Layer 1 — Swiss Domestic**
- nFADP Art. 5(c): AI-based automated decisions about individuals require DPIA + human review
- Active since 2023 — you've had years to comply

**Layer 2 — Swiss International**
- CARF/CRS 2.0: Live since Jan 1 2026 (3 months already operative)
- AMLA: H2 2026 (~3–6 months away)
- LETA: H2 2026 (~3–6 months away)
- FATF evaluation: 2026–2027 (international pressure driver)

**Layer 3 — EU Extraterritorial**
- EU AI Act GPAI: Active since Aug 2 2025 (~8.5 months live)
- High-risk obligations: Aug 2 2026 (~4 months away)
- Applies if you have EU clients, EU-intended outputs, or operate as EU importer/distributor

**The Compliance Gap:**
No FINMA guidance specifically addresses AI governance for boutique WM firms.
FINMA Guidance 08/2024 remains the baseline — no 2026 specific circular.
You're operating in a guidance vacuum with 3 layers of active and approaching regulation.
