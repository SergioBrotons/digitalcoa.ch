## SATW — Geneva 2027 AI Summit (NEW IN C79)
- Source: satw.ch
- Switzerland pursuing three goals: (1) contribute to international AI governance, (2) strengthen Geneva as multidisciplinary cooperation centre, (3) present Switzerland as innovative AI location
- Switzerland as neutral mediator between USA, EU, China — reputation for fair and inclusive processes is strategic advantage
- Swiss approach: mix of horizontal legislative adjustments + sectoral solutions wherever possible
- International interoperability via Council of Europe Convention
- Planned consultation draft end-2026: public sector first; targeted private-sector measures (transparency); industry solutions and codes of conduct supplement legal framework
- Democracy needs update: existing legislative procedures too slow for technological pace
- OFCOM (Federal Office of Communications) coordinating development of legally non-binding measures together with private sector
- Mr Schneider (SATW): "Using 19th-century tools to solve 21st-century problems"
- Contact for Platforme Tripartite: ir@bakom.admin.ch

## Legal500 — Switzerland AI Guide (March 2026) (NEW IN C79)
- Source: legal500.com
- FADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing (FDPIC May 2025 confirmed)
- Copyright questions for AI training unsettled; legislative amendments needed
- Federal Product Liability Act applies to AI (revisions anticipated to align with EU Product Liability Directive)
- FINMA actively addressing AI implications in financial markets
- Three essential pieces of advice for AI clients:
  (1) Identify and classify AI systems; determine roles and applicable laws; risk assessment
  (2) Embed compliance from design phase; invest in training and governance policies
  (3) Establish cross-functional AI governance structure; hybrid model recommended
- Three governance models: centralized, decentralized, hybrid (recommended)
- AI system inventory recommended for all organisations

## Pestalozzi — AI Legislation Analysis (NEW IN C79)
- Source: pestalozzilaw.com
- Swiss approach confirmed: sector-specific where possible; cross-sector only for fundamental rights
- No overarching horizontal AI regulation (EU AI Act approach rejected)
- AI Convention signed March 2025; Federal Council ratified Feb 2025
- FDJP leading bill drafting (end-2026 consultation draft)
- Scope: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, supervision
- Convention directly applicable to public sector; private sector implementation at Swiss discretion
- Key open liability question: Art. 97(1) CO (fault-based) vs Art. 101(1) CO (auxiliary persons — deploying party fully responsible)
- DETEC questions whether AI contractual liability needs alignment
- FADP transparency obligations may extend to semi-automated decisions
