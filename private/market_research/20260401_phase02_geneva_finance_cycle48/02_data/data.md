**Source:** CASUS citing AXA KMU-Arbeitsmarktstudie 2025 (published March 25 2026 via CASUS blog)

### Meikai Efficiency Data Applied to WM
**Source:** Meikai.ch law firm automation article
**Original finding:** 8–14 hours/week lost to administrative inefficiencies in 4–8 lawyer firm. 10–15% time-to-bill gap in manual billing practices. CHF 34,440/year recovered from intake automation alone.
**WM relevance:** WM workflows with quantifiable administrative loss:
- Client onboarding and KYC documentation
- Portfolio reporting preparation
- Compliance documentation and update tracking
- Billing and invoicing
- Meeting scheduling and follow-up
**Quantitative extrapolation:** If WM advisors spend similar proportions of time on admin tasks, a WM advisory practice could expect comparable or greater losses given the higher compliance burden (CARF/CRS + FINMA + FATF).
**Corroboration value:** MEDIUM — law firm case study; WM workflows differ but admin burden is structurally similar.

### CASUS March 25 2026 Applied to WM
**Source:** CASUS blog (published March 25 2026 — 6 days ago)
**Original finding:** FDPIC updated guidance May 8 2025; Switzerland three regulatory goals (innovation + fundamental rights + public trust); end-2026 consultation proposals for AI legislation.
**WM relevance:** FADP obligations are already operative for WM firms. The "innovation" goal is relevant for WM firms deploying AI in investment processes. The "public trust" goal is particularly relevant for WM — client trust in financial advice is paramount.
**Corroboration value:** HIGH — CASUS is Swiss legal AI vendor; the FDPIC guidance is primary.
