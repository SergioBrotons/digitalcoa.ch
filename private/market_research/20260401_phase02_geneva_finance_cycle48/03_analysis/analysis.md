### Finding 3: Agentic AI Is Already in WM Pipelines — The Next Wave Is Already Here
**Phase 02 C47 source:** 78% of WM firms exploring agentic AI
**Phase 01 C48 source:** CASUS on FADP obligations
**WM significance:** Agentic AI = autonomous AI agents that take actions (rebalance portfolios, send client communications, execute trades) without human-in-the-loop. This is qualitatively different from the current AI use (research, drafting, analysis) and creates substantially higher regulatory exposure:
- FINMA suitability requirements: can an AI agent ensure a recommendation is suitable without human review?
- FATF AML flags: can an AI agent detect and report suspicious transactions autonomously?
- CARF/CRS: can AI agents handle CRS reporting without human oversight?
**The Envelope Test applied to WM:** Agentic AI that reads portfolio data and takes action = clearly in the "letter" category, not "envelope." This requires DPAs, DPIAs, and formal policies.
**Confidence:** MEDIUM-HIGH — 78% exploration is Phase 02 C47 confirmed finding.

### Finding 5: Three-Layer Liability Stack Applied to WM Advisors
**Phase 01 C48 source:** Chambers civil liability analysis
**WM significance:** The 3-layer liability stack applies to WM advisors:
1. FADP compliance obligation (already operative; transparency, Privacy by Design, DPIA)
2. Professional liability (FINMA-licensed advisors bear personal responsibility for investment recommendations — AI-assisted or not)
3. Product liability (if AI investment tool causes financial harm)
**New urgency lever for WM ICPs:** Personal FINMA license exposure is more immediate than FADP criminal liability. A WM advisor whose AI-assisted recommendation causes client harm cannot claim the AI made the decision.
**Confidence:** HIGH — FINMA professional responsibility is well-established.
