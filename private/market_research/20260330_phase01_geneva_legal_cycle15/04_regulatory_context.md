# Phase 01: Geneva Legal — Regulatory Context
# Cycle 15 — 2026-03-30

## Regulatory Context

### Active Now (Operative)
1. **nFADP Art. 5(c)** — ACTIVE since 2023 for AI-based automated decision-making
   → Any law firm using AI to make decisions about individuals (hiring, billing, client selection) triggers Art. 5(c) obligations
   → FDPIC confirmed May 8 2025: nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing

2. **EU AI Act GPAI** — ACTIVE since Aug 2 2025 (~8.5 months live)
   → Transparency obligations for GPAI systems now operative
   → Firms with EU nexus (clients, operations, outputs) face immediate compliance obligations

3. **Federal Council Digital Switzerland Strategy 2026**
   → Confirmed Jan 2026: "lean approach" — no cross-sector Swiss AI law before end-2026
   → First Draft AI Action Plan: May 2026 (~2 months away)

### Approaching Deadlines
4. **Swissdamed** — Jul 1 2026 (~12 weeks; no grace period)
   → High-risk SaMD: AI diagnostic imaging, AI symptom triage
   → Spillover into professional services awareness
   → Law firms using AI diagnostic tools: immediate obligation

5. **Federal Council First Draft** — May 2026 (~2 months)
   → External pressure event for Geneva professional services
   → May trigger awareness spike + demand for guidance

6. **EU AI Act high-risk obligations** — Aug 2 2026 (~4 months away)
   → High-risk AI systems: additional obligations activate
   → EU nexus firms already facing GPAI obligations now face high-risk layer

### Future Timeline
7. **FDJP consultation draft** — end-2026 (~9 months)
   → CoE AI Convention implementation
   → Focus: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight

8. **CoE AI Convention** — end-2026 implementation
   → Council of Europe binding instrument
   → Federal Council participating in consultation

9. **ABA AI guidance** — Mar 2026 (global norm signal)
   → US legal market governance standards → Swiss market signal
