# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal — ICP

## ICP: Small Geneva Law Firm (2–5 Attorneys)

### Core Profile
**Who:** Small law firms in Geneva, typically 2–5 attorneys, operating independently or within small partnerships.

**What they manage:** Client data, case files, communications, contracts, regulatory filings — all increasingly processed, drafted, or managed through AI tools (document review, legal research, contract analysis, client communication).

**Their AI reality:** Using AI tools daily. Not thinking about governance. Not aware of nFADP obligations triggered by AI-based data processing. Not aware of EU AI Act obligations if they serve EU clients or process EU-origin data.

### Key ICP Characteristics

**AI adoption reality:**
- High. Legal research, contract review, document drafting increasingly AI-assisted.
- Low. Governance awareness. nFADP obligations understood as applying to "other businesses."

**Data sensitivity:**
- Extremely high. Client privileged information, case files, personal data, financial data, health data (for certain practice areas).
- Any AI tool processing this data triggers nFADP Art. 5(c) obligations.

**Regulatory awareness gap:**
- EU AI Act: Most small Geneva firms with EU client nexus are unaware of high-risk classification for legal services AI.
- nFADP: Most small Geneva firms are unaware that FDPIC explicitly confirmed (May 8, 2025) nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing.
- FDPIC enforcement: Most small Geneva firms are unaware FDPIC has opened 4 AI investigations 2025-2026 — and that law firms are not yet among them (but pattern is clear).

**EU exposure:**
- High for firms with EU clients, EU-intended AI outputs, or EU data processing.
- Even without EU exposure: nFADP (Swiss domestic) obligations are active now.

**FDPIC enforcement gap — POSITIVE TIMING SIGNAL:**
- FDPIC has confirmed 4 AI investigations 2025-2026 across transport, retail, and social media.
- Law firms NOT yet targeted — but the enforcement portfolio is building.
- This gap represents the first-mover advisory window.

### Compliance Triggers to Communicate
1. **nFADP Art. 5(c):** AI-based data processing = automatic high-risk trigger. If the firm processes client data through any AI tool, they have obligations.
2. **FDPIC May 8, 2025:** Explicit confirmation — nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing. No ambiguity left.
3. **EU AI Act August 2, 2026:** If the firm serves EU clients, uses AI on EU market, or processes EU-origin data — high-risk obligations activate.
4. **FDJP Consultation Draft:** End-2026 — will add Swiss domestic layer on top of EU AI Act.
5. **FDPIC 4 Investigations:** Pattern recognition — law firms are next. Not if, when.
