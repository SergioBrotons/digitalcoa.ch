## Competitive Absence Analysis

### Why No Competitor Has Entered

1. **Perceived market size:** Small Geneva law firms (2–5 attorneys) seen as too small for traditional advisory economics.
2. **Complexity barrier:** nFADP + EU AI Act dual compliance is technically demanding; generalist consultants avoid.
3. **Geneva-specific knowledge:** French-language Geneva market requires local presence and cultural understanding.
4. **Regulatory tracking burden:** Keeping pace with FDJP consultation, FDPIC enforcement signals, and EU AI Act implementation is resource-intensive.
5. **No proven playbook:** No competitive benchmark for how to position and package AI governance for small legal practices.

### Why This Window Is Still Open

1. **FDPIC enforcement gap is real and documented:** No law firm has been targeted yet. The window is still open.
2. **Regulatory knowledge is concentrated:** FDJP, FDPIC, EU AI Act, CoE AI Convention — keeping current across all four requires specialization.
3. **Geneva-specific context matters:** French-language communication, cantonal law understanding, Geneva professional culture — generalist consultants lack this.
4. **First-mover advantage is real:** The firm that establishes category ownership for "Geneva small law firm AI governance" will have durable positioning.
