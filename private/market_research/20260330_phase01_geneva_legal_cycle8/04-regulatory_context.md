## Layer 1: Swiss Domestic — nFADP (Active Now)

### nFADP Article 5(c) — AI-Based Data Processing
**Status: ACTIVE — No grace period**

Any processing of personal data through AI-based tools automatically triggers nFADP high-risk obligations (Art. 5(c)).

**FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation:**
> "The revised Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) applies directly to the use of artificial intelligence (AI) systems that process personal data."

**Implication for small Geneva law firms:**
- Client data processed through AI legal research tools = nFADP Art. 5(c) trigger
- Contract data processed through AI = nFADP Art. 5(c) trigger
- Any AI tool touching client information = obligations under nFADP

**Key obligations triggered:**
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) required before deploying high-risk AI systems
- Data protection registry (Art. 12) must be updated
- Data breach notification obligations (Art. 24)
- Legal basis documentation for all AI-based data processing

## Layer 3: EU Extraterritorial — EU AI Act (August 2, 2026)

### EU AI Act — High-Risk AI Systems in Legal Services
**Status: OPERATIVE August 2, 2026 (~16 months)**

**Who is affected in Geneva:**
- Law firms with EU-based clients
- Law firms processing EU data or AI outputs intended for EU market
- Law firms acting as AI importers/distributors into EU

**High-risk classification:**
Legal services AI is classified as high-risk under EU AI Act Annex III:
- AI systems used in law enforcement
- AI systems used in judicial administration
- AI systems providing legal advice where stakes are high

**Obligations (high-risk):**
- Risk management system (Art. 9)
- Data governance (Art. 10)
- Technical documentation (Art. 11)
- Transparency and user information (Art. 13)
- Human oversight (Art. 14)
- Accuracy and robustness (Art. 15)
- Logging and record-keeping (Art. 12)
- CE marking and market placement (Arts. 19–21)
