## F1: Russia Oil Revenue Surge — USD 17.6B/Month, Ural Crude at USD 93.40
**Source:** NZZ Pro / CREA / Carnegie Endowment / Bloomberg
**Signal:** HIGH — paradox for Geneva sanctions compliance, commodity trading opportunity

Russia's oil and gas revenues have surged since the Iran war began. CREA estimates Russia earned ~USD 12B in the first weeks of March, extrapolating to ~USD 17.6B/month — a 25% increase over February. Sergei Wakulenko (Carnegie): state revenues from oil/gas up 7-8B USD, doubling vs. prior month. Ural crude price: from USD 40 (end Feb) to USD 93.40 (end March) — highest in 4 years.

Why: Hormuz blockade → Asia scrambles for alternative supply → Russia fills the gap. Even US allied countries (Philippines, South Korea) buying Russian oil/naphtha. India paying premium above Brent for Russian crude.

Ukraine drone attacks on Russian ports (Primorsk, Ust-Luga) causing temporary disruptions — exports down 2/3 in late March — but overall revenue trajectory is up. Bloomberg estimate: if prices hold, Russia earns +USD 40B through year-end.

Paradox for Geneva: Switzerland's Russia sanctions (19th package, LNG ban) are designed to reduce Russia's energy revenue — but the Iran war is generating the opposite effect at scale. Geneva commodity traders face a complex advisory question: how to comply with Russia sanctions while navigating a market where Russia's position is strengthening.

## F2: Swiss Corporate Liability Law — Bundesrat Counter-Proposal Creates "Swiss Finish"
**Source:** NZZ / Economiesuisse
**Signal:** HIGH — Swiss legal/consulting demand, compliance burden

Bundesrat Beat Jans presented the government's counter-proposal to the second Konzernverantwortungsinitiative (corporate liability initiative) on April 2, 2026. Key elements:

- New law: Bundesgesetz für nachhaltige Unternehmensführung (NUFG)
- Berichterstattungspflicht: sustainability reporting for companies with 1000+ employees and CHF 450M+ global net revenue (~100 companies affected)
- Sorgfaltspflicht: risk-based due diligence on human rights and environmental standards for companies with 5000+ employees and CHF 1.5B+ global revenue (~30 companies)
- Civil liability: parent company liable for subsidiary violations (Verschuldenshaftung — negligence-based, not strict liability)
- Penalties: up to 3% of global turnover, forced dissolution of subsidiaries, profit confiscation, debarment from public contracts
- State supervision: expansion of RAB mandate
- Vernehmlassung (consultation) now open

Economiesuisse calls it "high risk" — in 15+ material areas, the Swiss version is stricter than the EU CSDDD. Key concerns:
- 5-year limitation period + 20-year substantive claim period (vs. EU standard)
- Extraterritorial application: foreign plaintiffs can invoke Swiss law for foreign damages
- Evidentiary disclosure requirements exceeding EU standards

Geneva financial sector implication: The ~30 large companies affected (likely including commodity traders, banks, insurers) will need significant legal/compliance advisory. Geneva law firms and consulting firms are well-positioned to capture this demand. This is a multi-year legislative process but the compliance planning cycle starts now.
