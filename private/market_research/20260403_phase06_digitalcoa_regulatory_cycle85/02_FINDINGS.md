### Theme 1: Swissness Rule Change — IPI March 23, 2026 (IP/Branding Regulation)

**Source:** Ph01 Geneva Legal

**What changed:**
- IPI reinterpreted Swissness trademark rules to allow Swiss cross + "Swiss Engineering/Research" for foreign-manufactured products
- Graphic rules: Swiss cross placed exactly between "Swiss" and "Engineering/Research"; square side ≤ font size
- Prior rule: ≥60% domestic production cost + essential Swiss manufacturing step

**Regulatory implications:**
- IPI's accommodation is an administrative reinterpretation — not legislation. Vulnerable to legal challenge.
- The Federal Supreme Court (BGE) could be asked to rule on whether IPI exceeded its interpretive authority
- Geneva luxury, pharma, watch, precision engineering sectors most affected
- Trade association response: likely formal challenge to IPI accommodation

**Timeline:** Active now; legal/political challenges expected  
**DigitalCoa relevance:** Executive briefing for Geneva boards on Swissness rule uncertainty and brand risk

### Theme 2: Konzernverantwortungsinitiative — LGDE Consultation Until July 9, 2026

**Source:** Ph03 Geneva Consulting

**Regulatory framework:**
- Federal Council recommends rejecting the popular initiative (strict global liability for Swiss multinationals)
- Proposes LGDE (Loi sur la gestion durable des entreprises) — Special Law on Sustainable Business Management
- Consultation open until July 9, 2026

**Key obligations under LGDE (as proposed):**
- Large companies only: risk analysis of human rights and environmental impacts
- Mandatory measures and audited sustainability reporting
- Supervisory authority to verify compliance
- Two variants on parent company liability — closer to current law but with clarification

**Regulatory timeline:**
- Consultation closes: July 9, 2026
- Federal Council response: post-consultation drafting
- Popular vote: likely 2026 or 2027

**DigitalCoa relevance:** Executive advisory opportunity for Geneva multinational boards — choosing consultation submission strategy and compliance posture

### Theme 3: WTO Digital Tariff Moratorium Expired — March 31, 2026 (Trade/Digital Regulation)

**Source:** Ph02 Geneva Finance + Ph01 Geneva Legal

**What changed:**
- 28-year WTO consensus on no customs duties for digital transmissions (e-books, software, streaming, downloads) expired
- Countries may now impose tariffs on digital goods and services
- First time since 1998 digital trade has no multilateral tariff protection

**Regulatory implications:**
- WTO members free to introduce digital tariffs (domestic implementation required)
- Switzerland (WTO member): Swiss Digital tariff policy not yet announced in response
- Geneva digital services companies: streaming, SaaS, software — potential new cost layer
- CH intermediary platforms (Swiss financial centers): potential tariff pass-through

**Timeline:** Active — countries beginning to implement  
**DigitalCoa relevance:** Geneva executives with digital revenue streams need tariff exposure assessment

### Theme 4: HUG DPI+ vs CHUV Epic — Institutional AI Governance / Data Sovereignty

**Source:** Ph01 Geneva Legal + Ph03 Geneva Consulting

**Regulatory dimension:**
- HUG (Geneva) + Valais Hospital: co-developing DPI+ clinical software — Swiss, independent
- CHUV (Vaud): choosing Epic Systems (US-based)
- Vaud parliament examining Epic decision for data sovereignty concerns

**Regulatory context:**
- No Swiss federal law mandating domestic vs. foreign health IT
- Hospital systems making their own governance choices
- Federal level: possible future health data sovereignty requirements as AI in healthcare grows

**Emerging regulatory themes:**
- Swiss health AI governance framework: developing but not yet codified
- FDA (US) vs. Swissmedic regulatory divergence for AI medical devices
- Cross-border health data: GDPR interface with Swiss health data law

**DigitalCoa relevance:** Institutional AI governance advisory emerging as a regulatory frontier

---

### Theme 5: Swiss Financial Center — US Enforcement (FinCen/MBaer) + Iran Sanctions

**Source:** Ph02 Geneva Finance

**Regulatory developments:**
- MBaer Merchant Bank (Geneva): FinCen exclusion from US financial system for facilitating Iranian Revolutionary Guard, Russian oligarch, Venezuelan oil smuggler money laundering
- Swiss financial center: renewed US enforcement scrutiny
- US-Iran conflict (since Feb 28, 2026): Swiss businesses with any Iran nexus face elevated sanctions risk

**Regulatory context:**
- Switzerland observes UN/EU sanctions on Iran independently (Swiss ordinance-based system, not EU law)
- Swiss banks and trading firms: heightened due diligence on Iran-adjacent counterparties
- FINMA: supervisory focus on sanctions compliance at Swiss financial institutions

**Timeline:** Active enforcement  
**DigitalCoa relevance:** Executive advisory for Geneva boards on Iran sanctions exposure and US extraterritorial enforcement risk
