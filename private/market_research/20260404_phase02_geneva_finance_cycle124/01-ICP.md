# 01 — ICP: Geneva Private Banking, UHNW Succession & Family Offices

## Target Segment: Geneva Private Banks, UHNW Families, Family Offices

### Firm/Client Profile
- **Type:** Private banks (Geneva HQ or major Geneva office), single family offices, multi-family offices, UHNW individuals
- **AUM:** CHF 500M–20B+ (private banks); CHF 50M–500M+ (single family offices)
- **Locations:** Geneva primary; some with Luxembourg, Singapore, Dubai offices
- **Regulatory:** FINMA supervised (banks); Geneva cantonal regulation (family offices)
- **Status:** Geneva is Europe's largest private banking center; ~CHF 1.5T+ in private bank assets

### Decision-Making Unit
| Role | Name/Profile | Pain Points |
|---|---|---|
| UHNW Principal | 60+ age, multi-generational wealth | Succession planning, family governance, asset protection |
| Family Office CEO/CIO | Professional manager, family member or external | Investment strategy, fee pressure, reporting |
| Private Bank Relationship Manager | Senior RM, Geneva-based | Client retention, succession expertise gap, product gaps |
| CFO / Controller (Family Office) | Finance professional | Reporting, FX hedging, operational efficiency |
| Next Generation (30-50) | Heirs, inheritors | Education, governance participation, digital comfort |

### Pain Points (C124 Update)
1. **UHNW succession planning wave** — Geneva's UHNW demographic (60+) entering succession window; complex asset structures (direct equities, real estate, art, private equity, offshore) require specialist advisory; most private banks offer standard wealth planning, not specialist succession
2. **CHF/EUR parity compression** — CHF approaching EUR parity affecting Swiss real estate values; family office direct real estate portfolios showing stress; mortgage-backed structures revaluing
3. **Basel IV capital planning deadline** — January 2027 implementation; private banks with RWA optimization opportunities need capital advisory before final rules lock in
4. **Basel IV / FAIR RWA optimization** — mid-tier Geneva private banks still need advisory on RWA optimization strategies; regulatory capital advisory opportunity
5. **U.S. tariff escalation impact on Swiss exports** — Geneva commodity traders and exporters seeking SERV export finance; trade finance advisory demand increasing
6. **VVG insurance reform** — Geneva insurance companies preparing for digital distribution requirements; family office clients with corporate insurance programs need review

### Buying Triggers
- UHNW principal reaching 65 without succession plan in place
- Family office considering multi-family office transition
- Basel IV RWA gap analysis reveals capital optimization opportunity
- U.S. tariff changes affecting client's primary export markets
- Family dispute or death triggering immediate succession need

### Buying Barriers
- Long-standing private bank relationships create retention friction
- UHNW clients highly protective of privacy; reluctant to engage external advisors
- Succession planning perceived as expensive and complex
- Multi-generational family governance tensions slow decisions
- Regulatory approval requirements for external advisory on FINMA matters

### Language / Cultural Fit
- French primary for Geneva domestic UHNW; English dominant for international families
- German useful for Swiss-German UHNW families with Geneva ties
- Ultra-discrete, relationship-based; trust must be established over time
- Direct communication but sensitive topics handled carefully
