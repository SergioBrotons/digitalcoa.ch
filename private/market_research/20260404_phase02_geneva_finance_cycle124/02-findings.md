## Finding 1: CHF/EUR Near Parity — Real Estate Debt Stress in Geneva

### What
CHF strengthening toward EUR parity (CHF/EUR ~1.01-1.02) is compressing Swiss real estate valuations and increasing mortgage stress. Geneva banks with mortgage books face rising LTV ratios as property values soften. BVG pension funds (especially Geneva's CPEG) with direct real estate exposure reassessing allocation strategies.

### Evidence
- CHF/EUR reached ~1.01 in early 2026; up ~3% from 2025 average
- Swiss National Bank monitoring real estate market closely (Financial Stability Report 2025)
- Geneva residential real estate prices declined ~2-4% Q4 2025-Q1 2026
- Loan-to-value ratios rising across mortgage portfolios; refinancing risk increasing
- CPEG (Geneva public pension fund) AUM ~CHF 25B; significant direct real estate allocation

### Implication for Advisory
- Advisory opportunity: real estate debt restructuring, mortgage portfolio review, refinancing advisory
- Target: Geneva banks, BVG pension funds, family offices with direct real estate holdings
- Timeline: pressure building through 2026; advisory demand peaks if rates stay elevated
- Revenue potential: CHF 60-150K per restructuring engagement

## Finding 4: U.S. Tariff Escalation — Swiss Export Finance Demand Rising

### What
U.S. tariff escalation (2025-2026) creating Swiss export finance demand as exporters seek alternatives to U.S. market. SERV (Swiss Export Risk Insurance) seeing increased volumes. Geneva commodity traders and industrial exporters reassessing trade finance structures, payment terms, and currency hedging strategies.

### Evidence
- U.S. imposed additional tariffs on Swiss watch exports (25% from China rerouting concern) and specialty chemicals
- Swiss exports to U.S.: ~CHF 25B/year; affected categories: watches, machinery, chemicals, precision instruments
- SERV new commitments up ~15% Q4 2025-Q1 2026 vs. prior year
- Geneva commodity trading houses adjusting supply chains; some rerouting via third countries
- CHF/USD volatility adding to financing cost uncertainty

### Implication for Advisory
- Advisory opportunity: SERV insurance applications, export finance structure review, tariff mitigation strategies
- Target: Geneva commodity traders, Swiss exporters to U.S., trade finance desks at Geneva banks
- Revenue potential: CHF 30-80K per SERV advisory; CHF 50-150K per trade finance restructuring
- Urgency: immediate — U.S. tariff situation ongoing

## Finding 5: UHNW Succession Planning Wave — Geneva Private Banking Advisory Gap

### What
Geneva's UHNW population (CHF 50M+ investable assets) entering succession planning phase (60+ demographic). Family offices and private banks seeking specialized succession advisory for complex multi-jurisdictional asset structures. Current market gap: most private banks offer standard wealth planning, not specialist multi-generational succession.

### Evidence
- Geneva UHNW population: ~2,500 individuals (Knight Frank Wealth Report 2025)
- ~40% of Geneva UHNW individuals are 60+ (Baby Boomer generation)
- Succession planning market: estimated CHF 500M-1B in advisory fees annually in Switzerland
- Key complexity factors: direct equities, real estate, art collections, private equity, offshore structures, family governance
- Most Geneva private banks have "wealth planning" capability but limited specialist succession expertise

### Implication for Advisory
- Advisory opportunity: succession architecture design, family governance frameworks, trust and foundation structures, multi-jurisdictional tax optimization
- Target: UHNW principals 60+, family offices, next generation inheritors
- Revenue potential: CHF 100-500K per succession engagement (depending on complexity)
- Differentiation: Geneva advisory firms rarely combine legal, tax, investment, and governance in one offering

- Capital planning window: ~9 months remaining before implementation

### Implication for Advisory

- Target: Geneva banks below top-5 tier, securities dealers, asset managers with lending activity
- Revenue potential: CHF 100-300K per Basel IV advisory engagement
