# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists versus What's Missing
## Phase 03 C299 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees

---

## What Exists in the Market

### What Exists: Generic AI Governance Policy Templates
**Products:** ISO 42001 AI Management System templates, NIST AI Risk Management Framework guides, EU AI Act compliance checklists published by major consulting firms (Deloitte, PWC, EY)

**The gap these leave open:** Templates designed for organizations with compliance departments. No template addresses the specific situation of a managing director who signed three carrier attestations, has three sub-consultants, uses three AI tools with client data, and has no documentation architecture. Templates create the appearance of governance without creating the documented evidence chain that protects the MD personally.

---

### What Exists: Sub-Consultant Engagement Standard Clauses
**Products:** Swiss Professional Association engagement letter templates ( fiduciaries, trust law firms), NDA templates, general service agreement provisions

**The gap these leave open:** No AI governance clause exists in any standard Geneva boutique engagement letter template. No language addresses sub-consultant AI tool use disclosure, processor chain documentation requirements, or nFADP Art.9 proportionate obligations for the prime boutique. The sub-consultant delegation gap is structurally unaddressed.

---

### What Exists: Carrier Attestation Completion Services
**Products:** Insurance broker completion assistance — helping the MD fill in carrier forms accurately and completely

**The gap these leave open:** Broker assistance focuses on form completion accuracy, not on what the MD's signature actually commits them to personally. No service performs a pre-signing legal exposure review against Geneva professional liability doctrine. The carrier form is treated as administrative, not as a personal liability document. The MD who gets help completing the form gets no help understanding what they are certifying.

---

### What Exists: Client AI Questionnaire Response Help
**Products:** Compliance consultants who help firms respond to client AI governance questionnaires on a per-engagement basis

**The gap these leave open:** One-time response assistance does not create cross-client consistency infrastructure. Each response is handled separately. No tracking system exists to detect contradictions across client responses over time. The MD who gets help with one questionnaire gets no system for managing the next three.

---

### What Exists: D&O Insurance and Professional Liability Coverage
**Products:** Swiss corporate D&O policies, professional liability policies with personal director coverage

**The gap these leave open:** D&O covers financial exposure after a claim is adjudicated. It does not create documentation architecture that would prevent the claim or demonstrate governance diligence in a pre-claim scenario. No insurance product addresses the documentation continuity problem — the MD needs a governance evidence file, not just a financial coverage policy.

---

### What Exists: nFADP Compliance Templates
**Products:** Data Processing Register templates (Art.12 nFADP), DPIA boilerplate forms, data retention schedule generators

**The gap these leave open:** Templates assume a compliance function. Template completion does not map nFADP obligations to consulting delivery practice — not general data protection but specifically AI-assisted client work. No template addresses the processor chain for AI tools used within client engagements.

---

## What Is Specifically Missing for Geneva Consulting ICP

### Missing 1: Post-Attestation Build Service — Carrier Renewal Gap
A service that arrives at the carrier renewal moment: reviews what was previously certified, identifies the governance gap between prior attestation and current attestation requirements, and builds the documentation architecture needed to answer "what has changed in your AI governance practice" with evidence. The MD facing a renewal attestation has higher motivation than the MD facing an abstract future form. No service exists to serve that specific moment.

---

### Missing 2: Client Questionnaire Audit Response Service
A service that responds to client AI governance audit requests — specifically: audits the boutique's existing questionnaire responses, identifies evidence gaps, builds the documentation package that supports what was claimed, and creates a response architecture that prevents self-contradiction across clients. The client audit request is a new event pattern in the market. No service exists specifically for this scenario.

---

### Missing 3: Sub-Consultant AI Processor Chain Documentation Kit
A service that provides the engagement letter AI clause language specifically for Geneva boutique consulting — language that establishes sub-consultant AI disclosure obligations, maps the three-party processor chain (prime → sub-consultant → AI vendor), creates documentation requirements proportionate to boutique scale. The language must be Geneva-calibrated, not imported from enterprise frameworks.

---

### Missing 4: AI Vendor Terms Change Management Service
A service that monitors AI vendor terms changes and evaluates them against the boutique's existing Data Processing Agreements, nFADP compliance posture, and processor chain documentation. When a vendor changes terms, the service identifies what changed, what risk was created, what action is required, and builds the documentation trail showing the MD evaluated and responded to the change. No service exists for this — it is entirely unaddressed in the Geneva boutique market.

---

### Missing 5: EU AI Act Nexus Assessment for Geneva Boutique Consulting Firms
A service that assesses whether and how the EU AI Act applies to a Geneva consulting boutique with EU-domiciled clients — specifically: whether any existing engagement letter AI governance clauses have already been triggered, what obligations already apply, what documentation is required before August 2026, and what a proportionate compliance build looks like at boutique scale. This is distinct from general EU AI Act guidance — it is specific to the boutique consulting firm's existing client relationships and engagement letter language.

---

### Missing 6: Methodology Documentation as Annual Consulting Deliverable
A service that produces the documented analytical authorization chain as an annual deliverable for the boutique's client portfolio — a year-end review of AI-assisted recommendations delivered across all mandates, demonstrating the reasoning chain from client question to analytical framework to recommendation, with version control, human review documentation, and defensibility evidence. This is not a compliance audit. It is a consulting practice documentation deliverable that serves the MD's personal governance interest.

---

## White Space Summary

**The white space has evolved.** In earlier cycles, the gap was "no one offers AI governance at boutique scale." In Cycle 299, the gap is more specific: no one offers **post-document governance services** — the services required after the forms were signed and the exposure accumulated.

The market has:
- Form templates for documents that have already been created
- No services for managing the downstream consequences of those documents

The MD who signed three carrier attestations in 2024 needs:
- A carrier renewal response service (not form completion — personal liability analysis)
- A client audit response service (not questionnaire response — evidence package build)
- A sub-consultant processor chain service (not contract template — active delegation architecture)
- An AI vendor terms monitoring service (not legal review — change management infrastructure)
- An EU AI Act nexus assessment (not general guidance — boutique-specific obligation mapping)
- A methodology documentation service (not deliverable — governance evidence build)

**The competitive moat:** The boutique that first names and delivers these services as specific, discrete offerings — not as part of a general AI governance framework — defines the category. Geneva-calibrated, bilingual, MD-personally-facing, proportionate to 2–50 person scale. Replicable by large firms at enterprise scale. Not replicable at boutique scale with Geneva specificity.

---

## Positioning Implication

The MD in Cycle 299 is not buying "AI governance." They are buying "I signed things and now I need to know what I committed to and how to build the infrastructure I should have built when I signed."

The positioning question is not "do you need AI governance?" The positioning question is: "What did you sign, and do you have the documentation to match it?"

This is a more compelling, more urgent, more personal question than any generic AI governance positioning can achieve.
