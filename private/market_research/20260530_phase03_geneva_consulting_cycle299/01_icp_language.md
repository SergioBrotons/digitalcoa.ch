# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Small Consulting Boutiques Talk About AI Problems
## Phase 03 C299 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees | EN

---

## How They Describe the Problem

*"The carrier form has my name on it. I signed it on my phone. I was on a train."*

*"Every client questionnaire now has an AI section. I answer it from memory. I have no idea if my answers contradict each other."*

*"My sub-consultant sent me the analysis. I didn't ask what ran it. I presented it to the client. I'm still responsible."*

*"I clicked 'Accept' on three AI tools this month. Nobody in the firm reviewed the terms. I was the one who clicked."*

*"When the client's technical team asks about our methodology, I realize I have a deliverable but no paper trail."*

*"My carrier renewal came with a new AI attestation. Same questions as last year. I have new answers. I don't know if they want the old ones or the new ones."*

## Key Language Patterns

### On Client AI Governance Questionnaires — Multiplication Effect
- *"Three clients, three questionnaires, same quarter. Nobody warned me this would be a thing"*
- *"I answered the first one from gut instinct. By the third, I'd figured out what I was doing. But now the first two are inconsistent"*
- *"My client's legal team sent a 60-page AI governance questionnaire. My own lawyer said he didn't know what half of it meant"*
- *"The questionnaire asks if I have a 'documented AI methodology.' I have methodology. I have no idea if it's documented"*
- *"I sent the questionnaire to our IT person. We don't have an IT person. We have a freelance guy who handles our server"*

### On Carrier Attestation — Fatigue and Acceleration
- *"The renewal came with a new attestation form. My broker said 'just do what you did last year.' I don't know what I did last year"*
- *"I signed the first one carefully. The fifth one I signed on autopilot. I think that's worse"*
- *"Three carriers. Three different definitions of 'adequate AI governance.' I signed them all. I still don't know what I certified"*
- *"The form asks me to confirm our AI governance meets an adequacy standard. The standard is not defined anywhere in the document"*
- *"I asked my broker what happens if I don't sign. He said 'they won't renew.' I signed"*

### On Sub-Consultant Delegation — Invisible AI Use
- *"I hired a sub-consultant for the strategy module. He used an AI tool. I don't know which one. I presented the output to my client"*
- *"When I ask what tools they use, they say 'just AI.' That's not an answer I can do anything with"*
- *"The sub-consultant's PI covers their work. My client relationship covers mine. The AI-generated part is nobody's explicitly"*
- *"I didn't know I was supposed to ask. I didn't know there was a right answer"*
- *"The sub-consultant sent the analysis at 11pm on a Sunday. I know it wasn't human-produced. I can't prove it either"*

### On AI Vendor Terms — Accepted Without Review
- *"I accepted the terms of service. I click accept more than I read. I know that's true. I'm not proud of it"*
- *"The AI tool processes client data. The terms say the vendor isn't responsible for what I do with the output. I didn't read that part"*
- *"I don't know where the data goes. The vendor's terms mention 'sub-processors.' I don't know who those are"*
- *"The free version and the paid version have different data terms. We upgraded last month. Nobody checked the data terms when we upgraded"*
- *"I clicked 'Accept' on behalf of the firm. The firm is me. That doesn't feel like it solved anything"*

### On Proposal Methodology Defensibility — The Gap Widens
- *"I can tell you what we recommended. I cannot tell you what the AI analyzed to get there"*
- *"The client asked for our 'AI-assisted analytical methodology.' I sent them our proposal template. That's not the same thing"*
- *"My thinking is in the recommendation. The AI's thinking is in a process I can't show"*
- *"I used to document my reasoning in the proposal. Now the proposal has AI output in it. I don't know where my reasoning went"*
- *"The client wants to understand how we arrived at this. I can show them the answer. I can't show them the chain"*

### On Personal Liability — The Accumulation Effect
- *"The engagement letter has my name. The carrier attestation has my signature. The vendor terms have my click. It's all me"*
- *"D&O covers financial judgments. It doesn't tell me how to avoid creating the situation that produces the judgment"*
- *"I'm not building a practice to hand it over when the liability lands. I need to know what I'm actually signing"*
- *"Every time I open an AI tool now, I think about what's in the documentation file that I'm not creating"*
- *"I realized last week I've been running client data through AI tools for two years and I have no idea what the documentation looks like"*

## What They Don't Say (Yet)

They do not say "third-party processor chain."
They do not say "nFADP Art.9 Data Processing Agreement."
They do not say "methodology documentation architecture."
They do not say "cross-client response consistency tracking."
They do not say "attestation review as a discrete governance act."

They say:
- *"I clicked accept"*
- *"I don't know what I agreed to"*
- *"I signed it anyway"*
- *"I don't know what ran it"*
- *"I can't show the chain"*
- *"It's all me"*

The language is personal, fragmented, and accumulating — not systematic. The anxiety is not about one document. It is about the accumulated exposure from multiple unsystematic decisions made under time pressure.

---

## New Language Pattern in Cycle 299: "Attestation Fatigue" and "Click-Through Liability"

A new linguistic pattern has emerged in Cycle 299 that was not present in previous cycles: the **normalization of unexamined acceptance**.

In earlier cycles, the MD described a specific anxiety about a specific document. In Cycle 299, the pattern has shifted to **acceptance without recollection** — "I clicked accept," "I signed it on my phone," "I was on a train." The MD is now processing AI tool adoption, carrier forms, and client questionnaires as administrative friction to be resolved, not as governance decisions to be evaluated.

This normalization is itself a risk signal. The MD who signs carrier attestation on autopilot is creating the same legal exposure as the MD who reads every word carefully — but without the documented review that would demonstrate governance diligence.

**The language of fragmentation** is new. The MD is no longer describing one problem. They are describing a system of overlapping exposures where each document is signed in isolation and none are connected to a coherent governance framework.

**The language of personal accumulation** is also new. "It's all me" — not "I signed one thing" or "my name is on the engagement." The MD has internalized that all these separate decisions accumulate in a single personal liability profile.

This is the language of someone who has crossed from "I'm dealing with a new problem" into "I'm living inside an unresolved situation."
