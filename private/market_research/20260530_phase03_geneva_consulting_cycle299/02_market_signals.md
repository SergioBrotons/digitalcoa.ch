# 02 — Market Signals: Current Problems, Complaints, and Failures
## Phase 03 C299 | Geneva Consulting ICP | 2–50 Employees

---

## Primary Market Signals

### Signal 1: "Second Attestation" — Carrier Renewal Exposes No Governance Build
**What's happening:** Managing directors at Geneva consulting boutiques are receiving their first carrier AI attestation renewal — forms they signed 12–18 months ago are now re-presented with updated language and additional questions. The MD is being asked to re-attest to their AI governance adequacy. The problem: nothing was built. The first attestation was signed in the same conditions as before — under time pressure, without a governance framework, on undefined adequacy standards. The renewal exposes that no systematic response occurred.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"Last year's form asked if I had adequate AI governance controls. I signed it. This year's form asks the same question and adds six new ones. I still don't have adequate controls. I just have a signature from last year."*

**Failure mode:** The first attestation created a documentation timestamp. The renewal creates a comparison point. The carrier is effectively asking: "What has changed in your AI governance practice since you last signed?" The MD has no answer — because nothing systematic was built. The renewal attestation becomes evidence of non-progress.

---

### Signal 2: Client Questionnaire Audit — Post-Response Enforcement Arrives
**What's happening:** Geneva consulting boutiques that responded to client AI governance questionnaires in 2024–2025 are now receiving follow-up requests from the same clients — requests for documentation evidence supporting the claims made in the questionnaire response. The client's own AI governance team is auditing the firm's responses. The MD who answered from memory, without documentation, now faces a documentation request they cannot satisfy.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"The client came back three months later and asked for evidence of what I said in the questionnaire. I sent them our AI policy document — which I wrote the week before. They asked which version was in effect at the time of the response. I couldn't answer that question."*

**Failure mode:** Client questionnaire responses are being treated as representations by clients' AI governance audit teams. The boutique that responded without documentation architecture now faces evidence requests they cannot fulfill. The gap between attestation response and evidence package is now an active exposure point.

---

### Signal 3: Sub-Consultant AI Error — The MD Responds, The Sub-Consultant Disappears
**What's happening:** A sub-consultant using AI in a boutique consulting engagement produced an error in the deliverable — incorrect data in a market analysis, wrong assumption in a financial projection. The client identified the error. The prime boutique MD is responding to the client's complaint. The sub-consultant who produced the error is unreachable or disputes the error was AI-generated. The prime boutique carries the client relationship. The processor chain is invisible.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"The sub-consultant's analysis had a fundamental error. The client found it. I had to explain it to the client. The sub-consultant said the AI tool gave them that output. They won't respond to the client's technical team directly. I'm the one in front of the client."*

**Failure mode:** AI-generated errors in sub-consultant deliverables create client relationship exposure for the prime boutique. No engagement letter AI clause exists. No processor chain documentation exists. The sub-consultant's AI tool vendor terms are unknown. The MD bears the response burden without any delegation infrastructure.

---

### Signal 4: Methodology Challenge — The Proposal Cannot Defend Its Own Chain
**What's happening:** A boutique consulting firm delivered an AI-assisted proposal. The client's internal technical team reviewed it and asked for the analytical methodology — specifically, how the recommendation was derived from the underlying data. The MD cannot produce the reasoning chain. The AI-assisted analysis was produced, but the analytical steps between data input and recommendation output were never documented. The gap is not about the conclusion — it's about the process.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"The client's team asked specifically: what data did the AI analyze, what version of the model was used, what was the human review step before delivery? I couldn't answer any of those questions. I had the recommendation. I didn't have the chain."*

**Failure mode:** AI-assisted proposals that skip the methodology documentation step are functionally undefendable when challenged. The inability to produce the chain is not a technical gap — it's a governance gap. The client is asking questions that reveal the firm cannot explain how its AI-assisted recommendations were produced.

---

### Signal 5: AI Vendor Terms Change — The Boutique Has No Contractual Position
**What's happening:** An AI vendor that the boutique has been using for 12–18 months changes its terms of service — new data processing provisions, new sub-processor disclosures, new limitation of liability language. The boutique has been using the tool with client data. The terms changed. The boutique did not notice. The first time the MD becomes aware is when they receive a notification of the change along with an acceptance button.

**Complaint verbatim:** *"The vendor sent an email saying 'we've updated our terms.' The email said 'by continuing to use the service you accept the new terms.' I had been using the tool for 18 months. Client data had been processed through it. I didn't know what changed. I didn't know what I was accepting."*

**Failure mode:** AI vendor terms changes create documentation gaps that are invisible until a problem occurs. The boutique has no change management process for vendor terms. The acceptance mechanism ("by continuing to use the service") creates a legal acceptance with no affirmative review. The MD who accepted terms on autopilot is now potentially bound to new data processing provisions they have not evaluated.

---

### Signal 6: EU AI Act Proximity — "August 2026 Is Not Abstract Anymore"
**What's happening:** Geneva consulting boutiques with EU-based clients are now treating the EU AI Act as a present-tense concern, not a future one. The August 2, 2026 operative date is approximately 14 months away. For boutiques that serve EU-domiciled clients or have EU market nexus, the proximity creates specific anxiety: what does compliance actually require at boutique consulting scale, and do any existing client contracts contain AI governance clauses that have already been triggered?

**Complaint verbatim:** *"I have three EU clients. The EU AI Act is in 14 months. I don't know if I'm already in scope through those client relationships. I don't know what my existing engagement letters say about AI governance. I don't know where to start."*

**Failure mode:** EU AI Act proximity anxiety without a Geneva-calibrated methodology for boutique firms creates paralysis, not action. The MD knows the deadline is approaching. They have no proportionate framework for understanding what obligations, if any, already apply through existing client relationships, and what they need to build before August 2026.

---

## Observable Market Failure Pattern

The failure pattern in Cycle 299 is distinct from prior cycles: it is not about the **arrival** of new obligations, but about the **sequelae** of obligations that arrived and were handled unsystematically.

The boutique firms that responded to their first client AI governance questionnaires in 2024–2025 are now facing the downstream consequences — client audits, renewal attestations, sub-consultant errors, AI vendor changes. The gap is no longer about ignorance. It is about the absence of a continuous governance architecture that would have addressed these downstream events.

The market failure is: **no one built the infrastructure after signing the first forms.**

This creates a specific and new opportunity: the MD who now faces a carrier renewal, a client audit request, and an EU AI Act deadline simultaneously is more motivated to invest in systematic governance than the MD who faced these as abstract future concerns. The pain is concrete. The motivation to act is higher.
