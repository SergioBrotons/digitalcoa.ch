# Phase 03 — Geneva Consulting / Small Firms: Regulatory Context
## Cycle 14 | 2026-03-30

## Regulatory Context — Phase 03 Cycle 14

### Active Obligations — NOW

**1. Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nFADP)**
- Operative since September 1, 2023
- Technology-neutral: directly applicable to AI-based data processing
- DPIA required for high-risk AI contexts (Art. 22 FADP)
- FADP may offer exemptions for SMEs <250 employees (domestic Swiss market)
- GDPR has NO size exemptions for EU nexus

**2. EU AI Act GPAI — Operative Since August 2, 2025**
- GPAI transparency obligations: technical documentation, downstream provider info, training data summary, copyright compliance policy
- EC enforcement powers: August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)

**3. EU AI Act High-Risk — August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)**
- Applies to: Swiss consulting firms placing AI on EU market, EU users, EU-output users
- Requirements: risk management, data governance, technical documentation, human oversight, accuracy
- Penalties: up to €35M or 7% global annual turnover
- High-risk categories: employment/HR AI, essential services AI, education AI, goods/services AI

### Imminent Deadlines

**4. Federal Council First Draft AI Action Plan — May 2026 (~2 months)**
- First Draft + update on preliminary actions for broader AI Action Plan
- "Lean approach" confirmed: no cross-sector Swiss AI Act before end-2026
- Next external pressure event for Geneva consulting market

**5. Full Swiss AI Legislation — Not Before 2028 (NEW — Refinement)**
- CoE AI Convention consultation draft end-2026
- Full implementation of Swiss AI legislation: not before 2028
- Window is LONGER than previously estimated for Swiss domestic market

**6. MRA Update Risk — 2027**
- Mutual Recognition Agreement with EU may need update for dual certification
- AI products with AI components may require dual certification from 2027
