### Section Framework (9 Sections)

**Section 1: The Geneva Small Consulting Firm AI Reality**
- 34% Swiss SMEs using AI (↑ from 22% within one year — rapid acceleration)

**Section 2: The Compliance Gap — Why Your AI Tool Use Creates Obligations**
- FADP operative Sep 1, 2023: nFADP Art. 5(c) active for AI-based data processing
- DPIA required for high-risk AI contexts (Art. 22 FADP)
- FADP may offer exemptions for SMEs <250 employees (domestic Swiss market)
- GDPR has NO size exemptions: if you serve EU clients, you must comply regardless of size
- EU AI Act GPAI operative since Aug 2, 2025: technical documentation + copyright compliance obligations

**Section 3: The EU AI Act — August 2, 2026 — Your Clients May Already Require It**
- EU AI Act applies extraterritorially: if you place AI on EU market, serve EU users, or EU-output is used in EU
- High-risk AI obligations: risk management, data governance, technical documentation, human oversight
- GPAI obligations: Aug 2, 2025 (operative) + Aug 2, 2026 (EC enforcement)
- Penalties: up to €35M or 7% global turnover
- ~4.5 months to assess — firms with EU nexus need to act now

**Section 4: The Swiss Domestic Advantage — FADP vs GDPR**
- Pure-Swiss firms (no EU nexus): FADP applies; SME exemptions possible
- EU-nexus firms: GDPR applies; NO size exemptions
- Practical implication: Swiss domestic positioning = lighter compliance burden
- But: FADP DPIA still required for high-risk AI contexts
- Entry angle: "Do you know whether your AI tools create FADP obligations?"

**Section 5: The Regulatory Calendar — Five Deadlines, Four Months**
- Federal Council First Draft May 2026 (~2 months — next external pressure event)
- EU AI Act high-risk Aug 2 (~4.5 months — GPAI enforcement)
- Swissdamed Jul 1 (~12 weeks — spillover to life sciences/healthcare consulting clients)
- CoE AI Convention FDJP draft end-2026 (~9 months)
- MRA update risk from 2027 (dual certification for AI products)

**Section 6: The Geneva Competitive Landscape**

- NONE focused on AI governance compliance, FADP obligations, or DPIA processes

**Section 7: The Internal AI Governance Framework — What You Should Have**
- AI inventory: list of all AI tools used in client work and internal operations
- AI use classification: who uses what, for what purpose, with what client data
- DPIA process: when and how to conduct data protection impact assessments
- Client data handling: AI tool selection criteria based on data residency and professional secrecy
- Copyright compliance: AI training data = your outputs may have IP implications

**Section 8: The Professional Liability Angle**

- Geneva consulting firms: client data + AI = FADP obligations + professional liability risk
- Documentation: your AI use must be documentable for client assurance and regulatory review
