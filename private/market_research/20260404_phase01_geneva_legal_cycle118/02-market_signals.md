## Active Market Signals (Geneva Legal, April 2026)

### Signal 1: ComCo Digital Markets Enforcement — New Advisory Demand
- Swiss Competition Commission (ComCo) increasingly active on digital platform markets
- Federal Administrative Court rulings on platform liability expanding
- Geneva-based tech platforms and digital marketplace operators facing novel exposure
- **Signal:** Advisory demand for digital competition law — platform compliance, data portability mandates, interoperability requirements
- **Source:** ComCo decisions 2025-2026; Federal Administrative Court rulings; media coverage

### Signal 2: DORA Implementation — Swiss Directive Adoption Underway
- Federal Council submitted DORA implementation bill to Parliament (early 2026)
- Swiss financial sector (banks, insurance, asset managers, FMIs) facing Jan 2025 EU deadline already passed; Swiss implementation will reference EU standards
- Operational resilience frameworks, ICT risk management, incident reporting
- **Signal:** Gap analysis + implementation advisory for Swiss financial institutions that transposed EU DORA requirements
- **Source:** Federal Council press releases, SIF guidance, FINMA circular drafts

### Signal 3: FADP Cross-Border Transfer Guidance — Updated FDPIC Position

- Adequacy decisions, standard contractual clauses, binding corporate rules
- New challenge: AI model training data cross-border transfers creating FADP exposure
- **Signal:** Geneva organizations need cross-border transfer compliance audits; AI training data a new exposure vector
- **Source:** FDPIC publications 2025-2026, FADP implementation guidance

### Signal 4: Geneva-Seated International Arbitration Spillover

- **Signal:** Advisory for Geneva companies involved in international arbitration — evidence management, enforcement strategy, award challenges
- **Source:** ICC arbitration statistics, CAS caseload reports, WIPO dispute data

### Signal 5: Switzerland FATF Evaluation — Mutual Recognition Stakes
- FATF mutual evaluation of Switzerland completed March 2026; report published
- Key findings: AML/CFT framework strength, beneficial ownership adequacy, sanctions enforcement capacity
- FATF technical compliance ratings will affect Swiss financial centre global standing
- **Signal:** Beneficial ownership compliance advisory; AML framework upgrade demand for Swiss financial institutions
- **Source:** FATF mutual evaluation reports, FINMA guidance updates post-FATF

### Signal 6: Financial Market Infrastructure Legal Risk — DORA for FMIs

- DORA implementation for financial market infrastructures (FMIs) particularly complex
- Systemic risk implications; FINMA heightened scrutiny on FMIs
