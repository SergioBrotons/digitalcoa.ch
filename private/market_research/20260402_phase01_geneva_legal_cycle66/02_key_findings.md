### 1. Swiss-EU Bilateral Package FORMALLY ADOPTED by Federal Council — MAJOR
**Source:** news.admin.ch/en/newnsb/HWvQ3sxWQ8hU-xZ4yDnSf (Federal Council press release)  
**Date:** April 1, 2026 — CONFIRMED from primary source

The Federal Council formally adopted the "Stabilisation and further development of Swiss–EU relations" package at its April 1, 2026 meeting. Key points:

- **Agreements signed March 2, 2026** are now formally adopted by the Federal Council
- **Sent to Parliament** for approval (Federal Assembly)
- **94 EU legislative acts** are relevant for Switzerland under this package
- Three new federal laws being created: electricity, food safety, health
- **Horizon Europe participation confirmed** — strengthens Switzerland as innovation location
- Federal Council seeks "regular high-level dialogue with the EU at ministerial level"
- "Institutionalised parliamentary cooperation" with EU
- Safeguard clauses confirmed: free movement of persons, land transport, electricity, food safety
- Package creates "reliable framework conditions for companies" and "closely aligns free movement of persons with labour market needs"

**Legal sector implication:** The bilateral package's passage removes a long-standing uncertainty. Law firms advising international clients on Swiss market entry, data flows, or EU-Swiss commercial relationships now have clearer framework conditions. However, it also means Geneva law firms may face MORE client activity on cross-border matters as the package reduces uncertainty — creating advisory opportunity.

### 3. ACCTS — Entry Into Force Imminent (Early 2026)
**Source:** TESS Forum, SECO.admin.ch, WTO sources

- ACCTS (Agreement on Climate Change, Trade and Sustainability) signed November 15, 2024 by Switzerland, Costa Rica, Iceland, New Zealand
- **Entry into force for Switzerland expected: beginning of 2026** — confirmed
- **New Zealand and Iceland have already ratified** (as of March 2026)
- **94 EU legislative acts** in bilateral package may overlap/conflict with ACCTS obligations
- ACCTS covers: environmental goods (tariff elimination on 300+ goods), environmental services, fossil fuel subsidies, voluntary eco-labelling
- **Consultation opened April 1, 2026** (Federal Council dispatch to Parliament) — deadline July 8, 2026

**Legal sector implication:** ACCTS creates a new compliance and advisory market for law firms at the intersection of trade law, environmental regulations, and commercial agreements. Geneva firms with international trade practices have a concrete new service area opening in 2026.
