# Phase 01 — Geneva Legal — Cycle 45
## Decision Framework: Shadow AI Audit + FADP Compliance

### 6-Decision Framework for Small Law Firm AI Governance

---

## Decision 0: AI Inventory (Shadow AI Audit)
**Entry point — low friction, high value**

- Map all AI tools currently used by lawyers and staff in the firm
- Document: tool name, user role, data types processed, frequency
- Identify: which tools process client data vs. internal firm data
- Risk flag: tools used for contract analysis, legal research, drafting, correspondence

**Deliverable:** AI Inventory Register — baseline exposure map

---

## Decision 1: Lawful Basis (Art. 5 FADP)
**Assessment of each identified AI tool**

- Is there a valid legal basis under Art. 5 FADP for processing client data?
- Consent obtained? Contract performance? Legal obligation?
- Purpose limitation: is client data used only for the stated purpose?
- Data minimization: does the tool collect only what it needs?

**Deliverable:** AI Lawful Basis Assessment per tool

---

## Decision 2: Vendor Contract Review
**Contract and vendor risk assessment**

- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) in place with vendor?
- Data residency: where is data stored? (US = CLOUD Act exposure)
- Retention: what happens to data after the session ends?
- Deletion: can client data be permanently deleted on request?
- Sub-processors: who else has access to the data?

**Deliverable:** Vendor Contract Risk Register

---

## Decision 3: FADP Compliance Assessment
**Art. 21 + Art. 5(c) + DPIA obligations**

- Art. 21 FADP: Do any AI tools make automated decisions with legal effects on clients? If yes, is human review available and documented?
- Art. 5(c) FADP: Is processing lawful, fair, and transparent?
- Art. 16 FADP + DPIA: For high-risk AI uses (contract analysis, due diligence, risk assessment): is a DPIA required and completed?
- Privacy by Design: Was data protection evaluated at procurement stage?
- Privacy by Default: Are tools configured to minimum necessary data processing?

**Deliverable:** FADP Compliance Gap Assessment

---

## Decision 4: Confidentiality and Privilege Review
**Cross-border and privilege exposure**

- Does tool's data residency create exposure to foreign law (esp. US CLOUD Act)?
- Are client confidences maintained when data crosses borders?
- AI-generated work product: who owns it? Who is liable for errors?
- Professional secrecy obligations: which jurisdiction's obligations apply?

**Deliverable:** Confidentiality Risk Assessment

---

## Decision 5: Retention, Supervision, and Documentation
**Work product and ongoing governance**

- Is AI-generated work product reviewed and supervised before client delivery?
- Is AI use documented in client files where material?
- Is there an AI acceptable use policy for the firm?
- Is there a process for staff to report new AI tools?
- Is there a schedule for periodic AI governance review?

**Deliverable:** AI Governance Policy + Implementation Roadmap

---

## Implementation Sequence

1. **Week 1-2:** Decision 0 (AI Inventory) — Shadow AI Audit
2. **Week 3-4:** Decision 1-2 (Lawful Basis + Vendor Contracts)
3. **Week 5-6:** Decision 3 (FADP Compliance Assessment)
4. **Week 7-8:** Decision 4 (Confidentiality Review)
5. **Week 9-10:** Decision 5 (Policy + Documentation)
6. **Ongoing:** Annual review cycle + new tool evaluation process
