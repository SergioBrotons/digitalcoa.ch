## What "FADP Directly Applicable" Actually Means

The FDPIC confirmed on May 8, 2025: Swiss data protection law applies to AI. No new legislation required. Obligations are already operative.

For your firm, this means:

- **Art. 21 FADP** — Clients have the right to human review of automated decisions affecting them
- **Art. 5(c) FADP** — AI tool usage must have a lawful basis; vendor contracts matter
- **Art. 16 FADP + DPIA** — High-risk AI uses (contract analysis, due diligence, predictive filing) require formal impact assessments
- **Privacy by Design** — AI procurement must evaluate data protection from the first evaluation stage
- **Privacy by Default** — AI tools must be configured to minimize data exposure by default

The FDPIC has stated that law firms are **expected to comply**. Enforcement in the legal sector is not yet visible — but "not yet" is not the same as "never."

## The Shadow AI Problem

71% individual AI use means most law firm employees are already using AI tools the firm hasn't evaluated, approved, or secured.

Shadow AI creates three simultaneous exposures:

1. **FADP exposure** — Unvetted AI tools processing client data may violate purpose limitation, data minimization, and cross-border transfer rules
2. **Professional liability** — AI-generated work product without proper supervision creates negligence risk
3. **Confidentiality risk** — US-based AI tools may be subject to the US CLOUD Act, creating client privilege exposure

---

## The 6-Decision Shadow AI Audit Framework

Before adopting AI governance policies, map what's already in use:

**Decision 0: AI Inventory** — Which AI tools are being used, by whom, for what?
**Decision 1: Lawful Basis** — Is there a valid legal basis for processing client data through this tool?
**Decision 2: Vendor Contract** — Who is the vendor, where is data stored, what are the retention and deletion terms?
**Decision 3: FADP Compliance** — Does the tool meet Art. 21 (automated decisions), Art. 5(c) (lawfulness), and Art. 16 (DPIA)?
**Decision 4: Confidentiality** — Does the tool's data residency create privilege exposure under Swiss or foreign law?
**Decision 5: Retention and Supervision** — How is AI-generated work product documented, reviewed, and retained?
