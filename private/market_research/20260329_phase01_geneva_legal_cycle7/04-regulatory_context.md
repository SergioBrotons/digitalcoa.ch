## Active Regulatory Framework

### nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection)
- **Status:** Operative since September 1, 2023
- **AI applicability:** FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation — nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing
- **Key obligations for Geneva law firms:**
  - Art. 5(c): Lawful basis required for AI processing of personal data
  - Art. 22: DPIA required for high-risk AI data processing
  - Art. 8–13: Data subject rights (access, correction, deletion, portability)
  - Art. 6: Proportionality principle for AI processing
- **Enforcement:** FDPIC is actively building AI enforcement portfolio (2025-2026: transport, retail, social media sectors; law firms NOT yet targeted)
- **FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report:** Legal sector is FDPIC priority enforcement area — confirmed

### EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)
- **Status:** GPAI provisions operative August 2, 2025; high-risk provisions operative August 2, 2026
- **Swiss relevance:** Extraterritorial — applies to any entity placing AI on EU market, operating EU-intended outputs, or acting as EU importer/distributor
- **Geneva legal relevance:** Firms with EU-based clients, EU-intended AI outputs, or EU data center/API usage face dual nFADP + EU AI Act obligations
- **High-risk AI systems:** Annex III categories — creditworthiness, employment/HR, essential services, law enforcement — some relevant to legal practice management
- **August 2, 2026 deadline:** ~15 months — specific, concrete, enforceable
- **GPAI operative since August 2, 2025:** Already active obligation for GPAI providers/users

### Council of Europe AI Convention
- **Status:** Switzerland signed; FDJP preparing consultation draft for national implementation
- **FDJP timeline:** Consultation draft expected end-2026 (~9 months)
- **FDJP focus areas:** Transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- **Impact:** Will trigger market-wide awareness spike when published; likely catalyst for competitive landscape activation

### FDPIC Enforcement Posture (Cycle 7 Update)
**FDPIC AI enforcement portfolio (2025-2026):**
- X/Twitter AI training case (March 2025) — preliminary investigation concluded
- Coop Group AI surveillance cameras (October 2025) — closed without formal investigation (compliant)

**Law firm enforcement:** NOT YET. Gap is positive evidence, not absence. FDPIC is building portfolio sector-by-sector. Geneva law firms should not interpret the current gap as immunity.
