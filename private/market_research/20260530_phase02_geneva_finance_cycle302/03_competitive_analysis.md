## NET-NEW Competitive Dimensions (C302 Phase 02)

### What Changed vs. Prior Cycles

**1. Institutional Client RFP as Competitive Pressure**
Prior cycles focused on regulatory pressure as the primary conversion trigger. C302 Phase 02 adds a new dimension: institutional mandates now include AI governance as a scored RFP criterion. This creates market pressure independent of regulatory enforcement.

**2. Carrier Attestation as Second Enforcement Vector**
In prior cycles, carrier attestation was noted as a secondary concern. C302 Phase 02 confirms the shift to evidence standard (form v4 "last three authorization records") is now active in the finance vertical. Insurance carriers are functioning as de facto enforcement agents.

**3. The50–200 Employee Gap Is the Addressable Segment**
Prior cycles used2–20 employees as the ICP. C302 Phase 02 shifts to 50–200 employees — the firm size that:
- Faces institutional client due diligence
- Has sufficient AUM to be material for institutional mandates
- Has compliance needs beyond what small boutiques face
- Cannot afford big4 engagement models

**4. Personal Liability as Emotional Conversion Trigger**
nFADP Art. 63 personal liability for the managing director/partner is the emotional trigger that converts abstract concern into action. This is distinct from institutional compliance pressure — it is personal, not corporate.
