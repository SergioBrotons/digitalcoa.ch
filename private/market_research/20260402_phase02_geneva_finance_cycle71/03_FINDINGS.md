## Fresh Research Today — NET-NEW Findings

### NET-NEW Finding C71-01: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — Concrete AI Governance Framework Published
- **Published**: December 18, 2024 (FINMA-Aufsichtsmitteilung 08/2024)
- **Scope**: All FINMA-supervised institutions using AI
- **Key risk categories addressed**: operational risk (model robustness, explainability, bias), data risk (security, quality, availability), IT/cyber risk, third-party dependency, legal/reputational risk
- **FINMA observation**: Most institutions "still in early stages" of AI governance
- **AI Desk**: FINMA operates an AI Desk to monitor AI systems and review cross-sector AI regulations
- **Broader pipeline**: Federal Council instructed to prepare consultation draft for AI rules by end of 2026; SIF analysis expected Q4 2025/Q1 2026
- **EU AI Act**: Swiss firms with EU operations should track August 2026 high-risk system deadlines
- **Source**: finma.ch, pwc.ch, six-group.com, unit8.com

### NET-NEW Finding C71-02: Berne Financial Services Agreement (Switzerland-UK) — In Force January 1, 2026
- **Signed**: December 2023, Berne; entered force January 1, 2026
- **Scope**: Wealth management, securities services, insurance, asset management, financial market infrastructures
- **Approach**: Outcomes-based mutual recognition — each side recognizes equivalent regulatory outcomes
- **FINMA-FCA-BoE MoU**: Signed September 2025, formalizing supervisory cooperation
- **Beneficiaries**: Swiss wealth managers gaining simplified UK market access; UK firms gaining wholesale access to Switzerland
- **Significance**: First time Switzerland has a bilateral financial services agreement with a major jurisdiction post-Brexit — template for future deals
- **Source**: pwc.ch, finma.ch, ey.com, gov.uk, lenzstaehelin.com

### NET-NEW Finding C71-03: Swiss SBA Updated ESG Self-Regulation — In Force January 1, 2026
- **Updated guidelines**: Effective January 1, 2026
- **Coverage**: Binding self-regulation for ESG integration, portfolio management, investment advice, greenwashing prevention
- **86% compliance**: 86% of banks already implemented initial self-regulation for portfolio management
- **FINMA mandatory sustainability rules**: Rolling out for 2025-2026 for private banks (climate data, governance, ESG product integration)
- **Swiss Climate Scores**: Additional optional indicators effective January 2025
- **Sustainable Finance Act**: Anticipated late 2026 — would standardize cantonal incentives and ESG rating framework nationally
- **EY Banking Barometer 2026 signal**: 86% of banks expect stable/declining customer demand — sustainable finance increasingly niche/institutional
- **Source**: swissbanking.ch, ey.com, swissfinancecouncil.org

### NET-NEW Finding C71-04: Project Helvetia III (Wholesale CBDC) — Extended to June 2026 / Mid-2027
- **Launched**: December 2023
- **Partners**: SNB + SIX Digital Exchange (SDX); commercial banks including UBS, Commerzbank
- **Scope**: Tokenized securities settlement using wholesale CHF CBDC; primary and secondary market transactions; DvP settlements
- **Extension**: Extended to June 2026 (some sources say mid-2027)
- **SNB position**: Extension ≠ permanent rollout commitment; decision depends on transaction volume and institutional adoption
- **Relevance for Geneva finance ICP**: CBDC infrastructure shaping next-gen settlement; wealth managers need to understand tokenization implications
- **Source**: sdx.com, snb.ch, crypto.news, bloomberglaw.com, coingeek.com
