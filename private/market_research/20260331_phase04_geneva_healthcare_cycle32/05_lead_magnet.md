# The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework (Swissdamed Edition)
### A Compliance Guide for Geneva's Solo Practitioners and Small Medical Practices

---

## Executive Summary

**Swissdamed goes live July 1, 2026. There is no grace period.**

If your practice uses AI tools for patient data processing, diagnostic imaging, or clinical decision support, you have a hard deadline in ~13 weeks. Swissmedic has confirmed: no grace period for high-risk SaMD (Software as a Medical Device) obligations.

This framework gives you:
1. A clear picture of what Swissdamed means for your practice
2. A 30/60/90-day action sequence to get compliant before July 1
3. Integration with broader AI governance obligations

**Your most urgent deadline. 13 weeks. No grace.**

---

## Section 1: Swissdamed — What You Need to Know

**Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Database) — operative Jul 1, 2026:**

**What it means for your practice:**
- Registration obligation: all medical devices, including SaMD, must be registered
- Post-market surveillance: ongoing reporting obligations
- Incident reporting: serious incidents must be reported to Swissmedic
- High-risk SaMD: enhanced obligations — NO grace period confirmed by Swissmedic

**AI tools that are likely in scope:**
- AI diagnostic imaging (AI-assisted X-ray, MRI, CT analysis)
- AI symptom triage tools
- AI clinical decision support systems
- AI patient monitoring systems
- AI documentation tools (if processing patient data)

**Key fact:** Swissmedic has confirmed NO grace period for high-risk SaMD. If you're using high-risk AI tools without proper registration and compliance infrastructure, you're already non-compliant.

**From Phase 01 (cross-phase):** 69% of professionals use AI individually. Only 34% have formal AI policies. Medical practice staff using AI without formal governance = Shadow AI in clinical context.

## Section 2: The 13-Week Sequence

### 30 Days (Weeks 1–4) — Assess

- [ ] Inventory all AI tools used in your practice
- [ ] Identify which tools process patient data or support clinical decisions
- [ ] Determine which tools are likely SaMD (Software as a Medical Device)
- [ ] Assess Swissdamed registration obligations for each tool
- [ ] Identify high-risk SaMD uses (diagnostic imaging AI, clinical decision support AI)
- [ ] Assess current DPIA status for AI tools processing patient data

### 60 Days (Weeks 5–8) — Govern

- [ ] Determine Swissdamed registration pathway for each AI tool
- [ ] Initiate Swissdamed registration for high-risk SaMD
- [ ] Conduct DPIA for AI diagnostic imaging and clinical decision support (FADP Art. 22)
- [ ] Draft AI governance policy for the practice
- [ ] Establish incident reporting procedures (Swissdamed requirement)
- [ ] Document human oversight requirements for AI-assisted clinical decisions

### 90 Days (Weeks 9–12) — Comply

- [ ] Complete Swissdamed registrations
- [ ] Deploy AI governance policy practice-wide
- [ ] Train staff on Swissdamed obligations and AI governance
- [ ] Verify post-market surveillance infrastructure
- [ ] Document FDPIC compliance posture (patient data AI processing)
- [ ] Confirm readiness for Jul 1 2026 hard deadline

## Section 3: DPIA and FADP — Clinical AI Obligations

**DPIA required for high-risk AI processing (FADP Art. 22):**

**For a medical practice, likely high-risk AI includes:**
- AI diagnostic imaging (X-ray, MRI, CT analysis) — HIGH RISK
- AI clinical decision support — HIGH RISK
- AI symptom triage — MEDIUM-HIGH RISK
- AI patient monitoring — MEDIUM-HIGH RISK

**DPIA requirements:**
- Document purpose, functionality, data sources (FDPIC three-part test)
- Assess risks to patient data protection
- Document mitigation measures
- Maintain records of DPIA completion

**FDPIC expects:**
- Purpose: Why is the AI tool being used?
- Functionality: What does the AI tool actually do?
- Data sources: What patient data feeds the AI tool?

## Section 4: The Shadow AI Problem in Clinical Practice

**What it looks like:**
- Physicians using ChatGPT to draft clinical notes → patient data shared with OpenAI
- Nursing staff using AI translation tools for patient communications → patient data processed externally
- Administrative staff using AI tools for appointment scheduling optimization → patient data processed by AI providers

**The clinical exposure:**
1. **Swissdamed:** Using unregistered SaMD = non-compliant
2. **FADP:** Patient data processed by AI tools without documented DPIA = violation
3. **Professional liability:** AI-assisted clinical decisions without proper documentation
4. **Patient trust:** Transparency obligations for AI use in patient care

**Phase 01 finding (cross-phase):** 35-point governance gap. Practice staff using AI without formal policies. Shadow AI in clinical context = highest-stakes version of this problem.
