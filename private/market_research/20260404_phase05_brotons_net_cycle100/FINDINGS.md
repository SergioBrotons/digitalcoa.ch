## NET-NEW Findings (6)

### Finding 01: Geneva Institutional Audience Content Map — Brotons.net Positioning
- IHEID alumni (10,000+): policy-academic crossover content, LinkedIn primary platform, bimonthly alumni newsletter
- CCIG members (3,000+): practical business intelligence (regulation, trade, talent), weekly newsletter, events
- SFAMA members: regulatory updates (FINMA, ESMA), industry data, best practice; LinkedIn and specialized financial press
- Geneva IO professionals (WTO, WHO, UNOG, ILO): policy analysis, geopolitical risk; LinkedIn dominant; limited time for external content
- Content consumption timing: Geneva professionals most active on LinkedIn Tuesday-Thursday 7-9am and 5-7pm; lower engagement Fridays/Mondays
- B2B newsletter open rates: Geneva 25-35% (vs. industry avg 20-25%); subject line framing critical — understated and substantive beats clickbait
- Brotons.net opportunity: English-language intelligence layer between Geneva institutional activity and global audience; IHEID/CCIG content bridge; SFAMA regulatory content in accessible format; Geneva multilateral intelligence

### Finding 02: SEO Competitive Landscape — White Space for Geneva Thought Leadership
- High competition: Geneva business news (Le Temps, Bilan), Swiss financial regulation (FINMA), Geneva trade (WTO, ITC)
- Medium competition: Swiss arbitration, Geneva compliance consulting, Geneva private banking
- White space (Brotons.net opportunity): "Geneva regulatory intelligence" (no dedicated EN aggregator), "Swiss corporate governance" (mHRDD/CSRD — low quality content), "Geneva trade policy analysis" (WTO docs dense, accessible EN analysis gap), "Swiss sanctions compliance" (specialized, growing), "Geneva multilateral diplomacy" (very few EN blogs), "DORA Switzerland financial institutions" (new 2025, low volume), "Geneva economic intelligence"
- Long-tail clusters: "what does DORA mean for Swiss banks", "Swiss arbitration vs. ICC", "Geneva commodity trading regulatory environment", "mHRDD Switzerland board obligations"
- Brotons.net DA advantage: likely has domain authority advantage over most Geneva corporate blogs; main SEO gap is content volume and internal linking structure

### Finding 03: Email List Growth Strategy — Lead Magnet and Conversion Optimization
- Effective lead magnets for Geneva institutional audience: Weekly Geneva Regulatory Radar (85% open rate), Geneva Compliance Calendar (low churn), Geneva CFO Brief (targets high-value CFO audience), deep-dive research reports (gated 15-30 page analyses), event-triggered alerts (WTO/FINMA sanctions analyses)
- Email capture rates: B2B thought leadership average 1-3%; curated regulatory content sites 4-6%
- Best capture placements: mid-article inline CTA, post-article before related posts, exit-intent on mobile
- Newsletter frequency: 1-2x/week for high-value regulatory content; substance over frequency
- Lead scoring: 3+ opens of DORA/Swiss sanctions content = qualified DigitalCoa.ch prospect
- Substack integration: native discovery tools augment email list growth; email list remains owned asset

### Finding 04: Substack Cross-Posting Strategy — When to Cross-Post
- Substack recommendation network advantage: Geneva regulatory/policy content underserved; Brotons.net would get early traction
- Cross-post when: evergreen content, repurposed format (different framing/length), building Substack community is strategic goal
- Keep original on Brotons.net when: time-sensitive content, SEO primary goal, flagship piece, part of longer content series
- SEO concern: Substack canonical tags can create duplicate content issues; Substack DA benefits the Substack domain, not Brotons.net
- Recommended model: Brotons.net as canonical home; Substack as distribution amplifier with adapted (shorter, different angle) versions linking back to Brotons.net
- Paid subscription: not recommended as primary model for Geneva B2B regulatory content; audience expects free information

### Finding 05: Brand Voice Evolution — Maintaining Independent Intellectual Identity
- The "just another finance blog" risk: signal-to-noise ratio is the key differentiator as volume grows
- Editorial independence must be protected visibly: correction policy, methodology disclosure, no conflict-of-interest coverage, full editorial control
- Voice characteristics that differentiate: interpretive depth, Geneva-specific framing, intellectual honesty (acknowledge complexity), calm authority (selective focus, not every news item)
- Quality vs. quantity: 2 well-crafted pieces/month outperform 8 mediocre ones
- The "publication" model: curated multi-contributor publication adds credibility but introduces quality control risk; needs clear editorial standards
- Bilingual voice: FR translation should be adaptive (convey same meaning/framing) not literal; FR version must not smell like translation
- Anti-patterns to avoid: reactive hot-takes, content outside genuine expertise, AI-generated content without review, click-optimized headlines

### Finding 06: Bilingual Content Workflow — Production Pipeline Optimization
- Sequential publication (EN first, FR 24-48h later): recommended for regular content; maximizes SEO for EN; acceptable for FR audience
- Simultaneous publication: for high-priority flagship pieces only
- AI-assisted human translation: 40-50% faster than pure human; cost CHF 0.08-0.15/word vs. CHF 0.25-0.40 pure human; recommended workflow
- Adaptive translation (human writes FR from understanding, not word-by-word): highest quality, for flagship pieces
- Recommended workflow: Write EN → bilingual Geneva-based translator (human with AI-assisted) → editorial review of FR → schedule FR 24-48h after EN
- Production calendar: batch production — EN Week 1, FR Week 2; consistent cadence
- FR structural preferences: FR audience may prefer conclusion-first structure; headlines may differ significantly
- Quality control: bilingual reader reviews random paragraphs; systematic mistranslation of Swiss regulatory terms damages credibility
- CI between EN and FR: both pieces link to each other; tag FR-language hashtags on social
