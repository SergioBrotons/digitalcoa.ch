## Findings (Cycle 100 — Slot 4)

### EN
**Substack Cross-Posting vs. Original Content — Strategic Framework**

Substack strategy for Brotons.net — 2025-2026:
- Substack's recommendation network: Substack actively recommends writers to new readers based on topic alignment; Geneva regulatory/policy content is underserved on Substack; Brotons.net content would likely get early recommendation traction
- Substack's paid subscription model: most Geneva B2B newsletters remain free (competition for paid subscriptions in regulatory/policy space is tough); Substack takes 10% of paid subscription revenue; free newsletter still benefits from discovery
- Cross-posting vs. original strategy framework:
  - **Cross-post on Substack when:** (1) Content is evergreen or time-insensitive (will drive ongoing discovery); (2) Repurposed format (e.g., a 3,000-word Brotons.net article becomes a 1,500-word Substack essay with different framing); (3) Building Substack community is strategic goal
  - **Keep original on Brotons.net only when:** (1) Content is time-sensitive (news analysis, regulatory round-up) — Substack would be redundant; (2) SEO value is primary goal — Substack canonical tags sometimes create issues; (3) Content is part of a longer content series designed to keep readers on Brotons.net; (4) The piece is the "flagship" piece designed to demonstrate depth/authority
- SEO implications: Google treats Substack content well (AMP-friendly, fast loading); but Substack domain DA (Domain Authority) benefits the Substack domain, not Brotons.net; cross-posting without canonical tags = potential duplicate content issue
- Recommended approach: Substack as distribution amplifier, not content repository; Brotons.net as the canonical home; Substack posts should be adapted (shorter, different angle) rather than exact copies
- Geneva audience Substack behavior: Substack adoption in Geneva professional community growing but not universal; LinkedIn remains primary discovery; Substack works best for building loyal recurring reader base
- Hybrid model: Brotons.net publishes comprehensive, SEO-optimized long-form pieces; Substack publishes shorter, more personal essays and commentary on same themes with link back to Brotons.net for full piece; both channels serve different reader needs
- Monetization consideration: Substack paid subscription not recommended as primary revenue model for Geneva B2B regulatory content; the audience expects free information; if monetization needed, consider premium tier for deep-dive research reports (not newsletter)

### FR
**Republication Substack vs. Contenu Original — Cadre Stratégique**

Stratégie Substack pour Brotons.net — 2025-2026 :
- Réseau de recommandation Substack : Substack recommande activement writers à nouveaux lecteurs selon alignement de sujet ; contenu réglementaire/politique genevois sous-représenté sur Substack ; contenu Brotons.net能够得到 recommandation précoce
- Modèle d'abonnement payant Substack : la plupart des newsletters B2B genevoises restent gratuites ; Substack prend 10% du revenue d'abonnement payé
- Cadre de stratégie republication :
  - **Republicier sur Substack quand :** Contenu evergreen ou insensible au temps ; format repurposed (Brotons.net article → essai Substack plus court avec framing différent)
  - **Garder original sur Brotons.net seulement quand :** Contenu urgent (analyse d'actualités) ; SEO valeur est but principal ; pièce est flagship piece ; série de contenu conçue pour garder lecteurs sur Brotons.net
