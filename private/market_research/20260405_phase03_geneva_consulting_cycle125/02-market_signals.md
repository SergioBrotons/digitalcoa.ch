# 02 — Market Signals: Geneva Consulting

## Geneva Consulting Market Signals (C125)

### Signal 01: CSRD Double Materiality Assessment — Advisory Demand
**What:** Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) implementation in Switzerland (via alignment). Swiss companies required to conduct double materiality assessments: evaluating both (a) how sustainability factors affect the company's financial performance, AND (b) how the company's operations affect people and environment. Large enterprises already in scope; SME supply chain pressure beginning.
**Why it matters:** Swiss multinationals and large enterprises need specialized support for double materiality assessments. SMEs in supply chains of CSRD-obligated companies will need help understanding their role. Geneva consulting firms with sustainability practices capturing this demand.
**Source:** EU CSRD, Swiss alignment legislation
**Confidence:** High — CSRD in force, Swiss alignment progressing
**Angle:** C124 covered DORA ICT and FINMA ESG board governance; C125 focuses on CSRD double materiality specifically and SME cascade effect

### Signal 02: Swiss Geopolitical Risk Advisory — Growing Demand
**What:** Switzerland's traditional neutral status under pressure from US/EU/China tensions, sanctions regimes, and trade tensions. Geneva commodity traders, multinationals, and financial institutions seeking structured geopolitical risk frameworks, scenario planning, and strategic advisory.
**Why it matters:** Geneva is global commodity trading hub and home to multinationals with complex geopolitical exposure. Geopolitical risk has moved from niche to mainstream advisory demand. Consulting firms with international relations and risk practices well-positioned.
**Source:** SECO, Swiss diplomatic sources, Geneva trading industry
**Confidence:** High — market demand confirmed
**Angle:** C124 covered US tariff escalation; C125 focuses on broader Swiss neutrality pressure and structured geopolitical risk framework advisory

### Signal 03: Interim CFO Talent Gap — SME & Mid-Market Surge
**What:** Swiss SME and mid-market companies struggling to recruit experienced permanent CFOs. Market characterized by: candidate scarcity, high salary expectations, long search timelines. Demand for interim CFOs (CHF 2,500-5,000/day) accelerating for CFO transitions, project-based work, crisis situations, and maternity/paternity leave coverage.
**Why it matters:** Geneva region has significant SME and mid-market company population. CFO gaps create material risk for companies managing regulatory reporting, investor relations, and operational finance. Interim CFO providers and finance function outsourcing firms capturing demand.
**Source:** Swiss recruitment industry, CFO networks, finance function outsourcing providers
**Confidence:** High — confirmed market trend
**Angle:** C124 covered interim CFO SME surge generally; C125 focuses on the talent gap mechanics and specific Geneva market dynamics

### Signal 04: FDI Screening Advisory — SECO Pipeline Increasing
**What:** Swiss SECO (State Secretariat for Economic Affairs) FDI screening regime for foreign investment in sensitive Swiss sectors (energy, water, food, health, finance, technology). Advisory demand from non-Swiss acquirers navigating SECO review process and Swiss target companies managing foreign investment situations.
**Why it matters:** Swiss M&A transactions increasingly subject to FDI screening review. Both acquirers and targets need advisory support: SECO process navigation, risk assessment, mitigation structuring, and transaction timeline management.
**Source:** SECO, Swiss M&A market
**Confidence:** High — FDI screening regime active, pipeline growing
**Angle:** C124 covered Basel IV SME credit; C125 focuses specifically on FDI screening regime and SECO advisory opportunity

Operational efficiency advisory remains relevant.
**Source:** Swiss economic data, Geneva company reports
**Confidence:** High — ongoing structural challenge
**Angle:** C124 covered CHF strength broadly; C125 focuses on specific cost restructuring advisory for Geneva companies with failed transformation programs

### Signal 06: Digital Transformation Failure Recovery — Advisory Wave
**What:** Multiple Geneva companies with digital transformation programs that failed, stalled, or underdelivered post-2022-2025. **Why it matters:** Failed transformations are expensive (often CHF 1-10M+); companies need independent advisors who can assess what went wrong, stabilize the program, and chart a recovery path. This is a different skill set from initial transformation advisory.
**Source:** Geneva consulting market, IT industry
**Confidence:** Medium-High — confirmed pattern, specific companies not tracked
**Angle:** C124 covered digital transformation rescue (3-4 Geneva firms); C125 focuses on the broader pattern and recovery methodology
