## DigitalCoa.ch ICP Language Patterns

### Before First Contact
- ICP has triangulated through Geneva peer circuit before reaching out
- ICP arrives with cross-vertical pattern recognition already forming (from talking to attorney, wealth manager, consultant)
- ICP uses Geneva accountability language already — not seeking education, seeking confirmation
- ICP is not looking for a vendor or a platform — looking for a peer who operates at their level

### On the Briefing Call
- "How do you know what to look for?" (evidence of pattern recognition)
- "Have you seen this before?" (circuit verification)
- "What happens when the carrier asks?" (specificity test)
- "What's the actual sequence?" (methodology test)
- "Can you put this in writing?" (documentation demand signal)

### On the Engagement
- "I need something I can actually use" (anti-framework signal)
- "Who else have you worked with in my situation?" (peer comparison)
- "What did you tell them?" (content test)
- "Can I speak to someone you've worked with?" (trust transfer request)

### ICP Self-Description Signals
- "I'm not a tech person" (but personally accountable for AI governance)
- "I don't have time for a process" (but needs defensible outcome)
- "I've already looked at this" (pre-educated, seeking specific calibration)
- "Someone recommended I speak with you" (circuit-referred, not content-referred)

## Language to Avoid
- "AI governance framework" (too enterprise, wrong scale)
- "compliance solution" (wrong frame — accountability not compliance)
- "best practice" (no Geneva standard exists to call best)
- "AI strategy" (ICP has accountability problem, not strategy question)
- "tool selection" (wrong layer — ICP needs decision architecture)
- "training" (wrong frame — ICP needs accountability structure)

## Language That Lands
- "What you signed" (personal activation)
- "Before your carrier asks" (specific timing)
- "What you're actually responsible for" (accountability precision)
- "What no one has told you yet" ( Geneva information vacuum)
- "The sequence" (methodology signal)
- "Put it in writing" (output expectation)
- "Before it arrives" (urgency without hype)
