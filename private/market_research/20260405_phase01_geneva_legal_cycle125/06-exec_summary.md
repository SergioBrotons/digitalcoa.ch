## NET-NEW Findings: 6 Key Discoveries (C125 Phase 01)

### Finding 01: ICC 2025 Rules in Force — Geneva Commodity Arbitration Advisory Gap
ICC's updated arbitration rules (2025) are now in force for new Geneva-seated arbitrations. Key changes — compressed 30-day response windows, enhanced document production, expanded emergency arbitrator procedures — directly affect Geneva's commodity trading dispute practice. Most Geneva law firms have updated internal templates but client advisory on operational impact (contract clause updates, trading house briefings) is underserved. Opportunity for specialized advisory content and briefings.

### Finding 02: CCIG Commercial Court E-Filing Mandate — Live Advisory Demand
CCIG's mandatory electronic filing for Geneva Commercial Court (commercial disputes >CHF 50,000) went live January 2026. Transition support, protocol training, and workflow documentation advisory is fragmented. Opportunity: standardized CCIG e-filing advisory package targeting Geneva law firms and in-house legal teams; unique Geneva-specific positioning.

### Finding 03: FDPIC FADP AI Training Data Guidance — Law Firm and Corporate Advisory
The FDPIC issued interpretive guidance on AI training on Swiss personal data under FADP (distinct from GDPR). Key: consent or legitimate interest required; no research opt-out equivalent. Every Geneva law firm and corporate legal department is navigating this. Advisory frameworks, policy templates, and contract clauses are underserved. Position as thought leader before market gets crowded.

### Finding 04: VVG Reform Digital Distribution — First-Wave Disputes Emerging
VVG (Insurance Contract Act) reform's digital distribution provisions are creating transitional disputes as insurers implement new compliance requirements. Geneva-based insurers and insured corporate clients are litigating coverage questions under the revised framework. Advisory opportunity for firms with insurance practices: compliance frameworks and dispute resolution.

### Finding 05: SchKG Debt Enforcement Reform — Advisory Preparation Window
Swiss debt enforcement law (SchKG) modernization proposals advancing in FOJ: digital asset attachment procedures, updated default judgment timelines, cross-border coordination improvements. Reform creates advisory demand for Geneva creditors (trading houses, banks) and debtors to update contract clauses and enforcement strategies before legislation finalizes.

### Finding 06: AEOI-CRS Partner Jurisdiction Expansion 2026 — Compliance Advisory
Switzerland expanded its AEOI-CRS (Automatic Exchange of Information) partner jurisdiction list for 2026. Swiss financial institutions, commodity trading house treasury operations, and their legal counsel must update withholding, due diligence, and reporting frameworks. Most advisory work in this space goes to Big 4 tax counsel. Opportunity: legal advisory angle on AEOI documentation, contractual protections, and FTA dispute resolution.
