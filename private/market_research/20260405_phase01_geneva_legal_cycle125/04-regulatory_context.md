# 04 — Regulatory Context: Geneva Legal

## Key Regulatory Developments — Geneva Legal Practice (C125)

### Regulatory Item 01: ICC Arbitration Rules 2025
**Authority:** International Chamber of Commerce (ICC), Paris
**Status:** In force for new arbitrations filed after March 2025
**Key Changes:**
- Compressed timelines: 30-day acknowledgment, faster Terms of Reference formation
- Enhanced document production: ICDR-style broad document requests with tribunal oversight
- Emergency arbitrator: expanded scope and faster appointments
- Digital case management: mandatory use of ICC's digital case platform
- Multi-party provisions: clarified joinder and consolidation procedures
**Geneva Impact:** Geneva is ICC's #2 seat globally. Changes directly affect all SCAI and ICC Geneva-seated arbitrations. Commodity trading disputes (ICC contract disputes, trade finance, insurance) most affected.
**Advisory Implications:** Contract clause updates, client briefings, internal law firm protocol changes
**Source:** ICC Paris, ICC Switzerland

### Regulatory Item 02: CCIG Commercial Court E-Filing Protocol
**Authority:** Geneva Chamber of Commerce (CCIG), Geneva Courts Administration
**Status:** Mandatory electronic filing live January 2026 for commercial disputes >CHF 50,000
**Key Requirements:**
- Structured digital submissions via CCIG portal
- Digital signatures mandatory
- Encrypted communications for confidential matters
- Case management dashboard for tracking
**Geneva Impact:** All Geneva law firms with commercial litigation practices affected; in-house legal teams at Geneva trading houses and banks must adapt internal matter management
**Advisory Implications:** Workflow transition support, protocol training, contract clause updates referencing new procedure
**Source:** CCIG Geneva

---

### Regulatory Item 03: FADP AI Training Data — FDPIC Interpretive Guidance
**Authority:** Federal Data Protection Information Commissioner (FDPIC)
**Status:** Guidance issued; enforcement posture under FADP (not GDPR)
**Key Points:**
- AI training on Swiss personal data constitutes "processing" under FADP
- Consent or legitimate interest basis required
- No equivalent to GDPR's "soft opt-out" for research
- Corporate clients need internal AI training data policies
- Law firms themselves face questions about AI tool use on client matters
**Geneva Impact:** Every Geneva law firm and corporate legal department is navigating this; FDPIC is actively monitoring
**Advisory Implications:** FADP AI training data compliance frameworks, contract clauses, internal firm policies
**Source:** FDPIC, Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (FADP)

### Regulatory Item 04: VVG Insurance Contract Act Reform — Digital Distribution
**Authority:** Swiss Federal Office of Justice (FOJ)
**Status:** Reform ongoing; digital distribution provisions creating transitional disputes
**Key Points:**
- New digital distribution compliance requirements for insurers
- Transitional provisions create interpretation disputes
- Coverage questions arising under new framework vs. old contracts
- Geneva courts handling first-wave litigation
**Geneva Impact:** Geneva-based insurers (Swiss Re, Zurich, specialty insurers), insurance brokers, and insured corporate clients
**Advisory Implications:** Coverage dispute resolution, compliance advisory for insurers, corporate insured advisory
**Source:** FOJ, VSV (Swiss Insurance Association), Geneva courts

## Geneva-Specific Regulatory Dynamics

**ICC/SCAI Geneva Arbitration Ecosystem:**
Geneva hosts ICC's second-largest caseload globally. 2025 rules changes affect every new commodity trading, trade finance, insurance, and banking arbitration filed in Geneva. SCAI (Swiss Chambers' Arbitration Institution) is the primary administering institution for mid-size Geneva arbitrations — coordination between ICC and SCAI procedures is common.

**CCIG Commercial Court:**
Unique to Geneva — the CCIG Commercial Court handles commercial disputes under a specialized procedural framework. The e-filing mandate is a significant modernization. Geneva firms have had since January 2026 to adapt; some are further along than others.

**FOJ Regulatory Pipeline:**
FOJ is managing multiple Geneva-relevant reforms simultaneously (VVG, SchKG, BGFA foreign lawyer admission). Law firms with FOJ relationships and regulatory advisory practices are well-positioned.

**FDPIC Enforcement Posture:**
FDPIC is signaling active monitoring of AI training data practices. Unlike EU GDPR enforcement (which has larger fines), FADP enforcement is more guidance-first but growing. Geneva law firms and corporate clients are seeking clarity.
