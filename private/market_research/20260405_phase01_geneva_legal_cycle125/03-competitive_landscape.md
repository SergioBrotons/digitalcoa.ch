### Competitive Gaps & Opportunities

#### Geneva Legal Market — Identified Gaps (C125)

**Gap 01: ICC 2025 Rules Advisory Capacity**
Most Geneva firms have updated their arbitration templates but client advisory on the operational impact (30-day回应 windows, document production changes, emergency arbitrator procedures) is underserved. Opportunity: specialized briefings, contract clause updates, and in-house training for trading house legal teams.

**Gap 02: FADP AI Training Data Compliance Advisory**
FDPIC guidance is new. Most Geneva law firms are advising clients reactively rather than proactively. Opportunity: develop standardized FADP AI training data compliance frameworks for corporate clients; position as thought leader before market gets crowded.

**Gap 03: AEOI-CRS Expansion Compliance**
Swiss financial institutions and commodity trading houses need practical AEOI compliance updates for new partner jurisdictions. Most advisory work in this space goes to Big 4 tax counsel. Opportunity: legal advisory angle on AEOI documentation, contractual protections, and dispute resolution with FTA.

**Gap 04: VVG Digital Distribution Transitional Advisory**
VVG reform digital distribution provisions are creating first-wave disputes. Law firms with insurance practices can capture this advisory work — currently fragmented, no clear market leader in Geneva for VVG digital distribution advisory.

**Gap 05: CCIG Commercial Court E-Filing Protocol Advisory**
Transition support for CCIG Commercial Court e-filing is largely ad hoc. Opportunity: develop standardized CCIG e-filing protocol advisory package for law firms and in-house teams; could be offered as training or documentation service.

**Gap 06: SchKG Reform Preparation Advisory**
SchKG modernization is approaching legislative phase. Forward-looking advisory on contract clause updates, enforcement strategy changes, and cross-border coordination improvements is currently underserved. Opportunity: position now before reform finalizes.
