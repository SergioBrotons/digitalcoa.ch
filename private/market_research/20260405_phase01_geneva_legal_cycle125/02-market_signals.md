## Geneva Legal Market Signals (C125)

### Signal 01: CCIG Commercial Court E-Filing Mandate — Live January 2026
**What:** Geneva Chamber of Commerce (CCIG) Commercial Court implemented mandatory electronic filing for commercial disputes >CHF 50,000 as of January 2026. New case management portal requires structured submissions, digital signatures, and encrypted communications.
**Why it matters:** Geneva law firms and in-house legal departments at trading houses and banks must update internal workflows, template contracts (with updated dispute resolution clauses referencing e-filing protocol), and client reporting systems. Advisory demand for transition support is live.
**Source:** CCIG, Geneva courts administration
**Confidence:** High — CCIG confirmed live implementation
**Angle:** Different from C124 (which covered CCIG digital mandate broadly); C125 drills into the Commercial Court e-filing protocol specifics and workflow adaptation demand

### Signal 02: ICC 2025 Rules in Force — Commodity Disputes Impact
**What:** ICC arbitration rules updated 2025, in force for new filings from March 2025. Key changes: compressed timelines (30-day acknowledgment, faster Terms of Reference), enhanced document production (ICDR-style), emergency arbitrator procedures expanded, and digital case management.
**Why it matters:** Geneva is the global #1 seat for commodity trading arbitration. ICC/SCAI caseload up ~20% (C124). The 2025 rule changes disproportionately affect Geneva trading house disputes where speed, document-heavy evidence, and multi-party complexity are endemic. Law firms must update client advice, contract clauses, and internal case handling.
**Source:** ICC Paris, ICC Switzerland, SCAI
**Confidence:** High — ICC 2025 rules confirmed in force
**Angle:** C124 covered ICC/SCAI surge generally; C125 focuses specifically on ICC 2025 rule changes and their practical impact on Geneva commodity arbitration workflow

### Signal 03: FDPIC FADP AI Training Data Guidance
**What:** The Federal Data Protection Information Commissioner (FDPIC) issued interpretive guidance on whether Swiss personal data can be used to train AI systems. Key question: does Swiss FADP (unlike EU GDPR) restrict AI training as a form of "processing"? Guidance suggests consent or legitimate interest frameworks apply.
**Why it matters:** Every Geneva law firm and corporate legal department using AI tools (drafting, research, contract review) faces this question. Outside counsel advising corporate clients on AI tool adoption and FADP compliance obligations. Also relevant for law firms themselves using AI on client matters.
**Source:** FDPIC, Swiss Federal Data Protection Act (FADP)
**Confidence:** High — FDPIC guidance documented
**Angle:** Different from C124 (which covered FADP AI training data via FOJ guidance angle); C125 focuses on FDPIC-specific interpretive guidance and law firm/client advisory implications

### Signal 04: VSV Insurance Dispute Surge — VVG Reform Digital Distribution
**What:** The Swiss Insurance Association (VSV) reports increasing litigation volume in insurance coverage disputes. The ongoing VVG (Insurance Contract Act) reform includes new digital distribution provisions — transitional disputes arising as insurers implement new distribution compliance requirements. Key issue: retroactivity and coverage interpretation under revised framework.
**Why it matters:** Geneva is a major insurance hub (Swiss Re, Zurich, AXA, and specialty insurers). Coverage disputes under VVG are litigated in Geneva courts. Law firms with insurance practices see advisory and litigation demand from insurers and insured parties navigating the transitional framework.
**Source:** VSV, Swiss Federal Office of Justice, Geneva courts
**Confidence:** Medium-High — VVG reform ongoing, dispute trend observed
**Angle:** Different from C124 (which covered CBAM/SVUT customs disputes); C125 focuses on insurance contract disputes arising from VVG reform digital distribution provisions

### Signal 06: AEOI-CRS Partner Jurisdiction Expansion 2026

**Why it matters:** Geneva's financial and commodity trading ecosystem has significant cross-border financial exposure. Every Swiss financial institution and trading house with international treasury operations must update AEOI compliance frameworks. Law firms providing advice on AEOI implementation, dispute resolution with tax authorities, and contract documentation for counterparties in new jurisdictions.
**Source:** State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF), FTA (Federal Tax Administration), OECD CRS
**Confidence:** High — AEOI expansion confirmed for 2026
