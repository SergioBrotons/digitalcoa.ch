## Regulatory Architecture

### Primary: Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~13 WEEKS NO GRACE)
**#1 most urgent deadline in portfolio — no grace period confirmed**

**Swissdamed Scope:**
- Medical devices placed on Swiss market require registration
- AI-enabled medical devices = Swissdamed scope
- Registration must be completed before market placement
- **NO grace period** — automatic non-compliance after July 1

**Swissdamed/EUDAMED:**
- Swissdamed and EUDAMED are NOT interconnected
- Swiss practices with EU device sales must register on BOTH systems
- Dual registration burden = additional compliance complexity

**Swissmedic Primary Source:**
- Swissmedic (Swiss Agency for Therapeutic Products) manages Swissdamed
- Swissmedic confirmed: no grace period after July 1, 2026

---

### Secondary: FADP — Operative since September 1, 2023
**2.5+ years already live**

**FDPIC confirmed (May 2025)**: FADP directly applicable to AI-supported data processing

**Art. 21 FADP: Human Review**
- Automated decisions producing legal effects must allow human review
- AI-assisted clinical decisions, diagnoses, treatment recommendations = Art. 21 applicability
- **Life safety context**: human review is not just legal requirement but medical necessity

**Art. 5(c) FADP: Transparency**
- Must disclose AI involvement in patient communications
- AI-generated clinical reports, notes, recommendations = disclosure required

**Art. 16 FADP: Data Integrity**
- AI systems processing patient data must maintain integrity
- Patient confidentiality extends to AI tools

**DPIA**: Required for high-risk clinical AI (diagnostic imaging, treatment planning)

---

### Tertiary: EU AI Act (Extraterritorial)
**GPAI operative since August 2, 2025**

**Annex III High-Risk AI:**
- AI medical devices used in clinical context = likely Annex III high-risk
- Swiss practices with EU nexus = EU AI Act extraterritorial applicability
- Conformity assessment, registration, human oversight required
