### Section Framework (9 Sections)

**Section 1: The Geneva Boutique Wealth Manager AI Reality Check**
- 50% Swiss financial institutions already using AI; 40% still in early phase (ideation/pilot)
- 90%+ using GenAI; shift to Agentic AI (autonomous execution) = new risk category
- Boutique WMs: personalized service + agility + trust = must maintain AI governance without losing identity
- Hybrid advisory models: AI handles data/compliance; human manages relationships

**Section 2: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — What You Must Have in Place Now**
- AI inventory: complete list of all AI tools used, classified by risk
- Risk classification: mandatory for every AI application
- Risk management: data quality, model quality, bias, explainability, IT/cyber, third-party
- Testing and monitoring: continuous; independent reviews where appropriate
- Outsourcing controls: contractual clauses, responsibility allocation for third-party AI
- Employee expertise: staff using AI must have sufficient training
- ~15 months since Guidance issued — FINMA will expect evidence of compliance

**Section 3: CARF/CRS 2.0 — Operative Now (Even If Exchange Is Delayed)**
- CARF: legal framework live January 1, 2026; cross-border exchange delayed to January 2027
- CRS 2.0: operative January 1, 2026 — no delay; digital assets, e-money, CBDCs, tokenized assets now in scope
- Geneva WM must have CARF/CRS 2.0 infrastructure operative NOW
- Enhanced self-certifications, TIN verification, continuous monitoring required
- Crypto-asset reporting: stablecoins, cryptocurrencies, NFTs — broad definition

**Section 4: AMLA/LETA H2 2026 — Your Advisory Activities May Have Changed Your Obligations**
- LETA Transparency Register: identify and register ultimate beneficial owners
- 30-day discrepancy reporting: KYC vs. Transparency Register (new obligation)
- Advisor scope expansion: real estate transactions, non-operational entity establishment, domicile services = SRO obligations triggered
- Geneva wealth managers: if you advise on these activities, you likely need FINMA SRO membership under revised AMLA
- FATF evaluation 2026-2027: international pressure elevates domestic AMLA to compliance event

**Section 5: The 3-Layer Compliance Stack**
- Layer 1 — Swiss domestic: FINMA Guidance 08/2024 + AMLO-FINMA + LETA + SRO obligations
- Layer 2 — Swiss international: FATF evaluation + AMLA revision + CARF/CRS 2.0
- Layer 3 — EU extraterritorial: EU AI Act Aug 2 2026 (credit scoring, insurance risk assessment = high-risk)
- All three layers can apply simultaneously to a Geneva boutique WM

**Section 6: The AI Risk Register — What to Monitor**
- Data quality and model accuracy: AI outputs can be biased or inaccurate
- Explainability: can you explain AI-driven decisions to clients and regulators?
- Third-party dependencies: outsourcing AI = outsourcing control
- IT/cyber: AI systems are attack surfaces
- Legal/reputational: professional liability for AI-assisted advice
- Agentic AI: autonomous execution = new risk category requiring additional controls

**Section 7: The Regulatory Calendar**
