# Phase 02 — Geneva Finance / Boutique WM: Regulatory Context
## Cycle 14 | 2026-03-30

## Regulatory Context — Phase 02 Cycle 14

### Active Obligations — NOW

**1. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 18, 2024) — AI Governance**
- AI inventory: all AI tools must be classified and inventoried
- Risk classification: mandatory for all AI applications
- Risk management: data quality, model quality, bias, explainability, IT/cyber, third-party
- Testing and monitoring: mandatory; independent reviews where appropriate
- Outsourcing controls: additional tests, controls, contractual clauses for third-party AI
- Employee expertise requirement
- Integration with existing governance structures

**2. CARF/CRS 2.0 — Operative January 1, 2026**
- CARF: legal framework live; cross-border exchange delayed to January 2027
- CRS 2.0: operative January 1, 2026 (no delay) — digital assets, e-money, CBDCs, tokenized assets
- Geneva WMs must have CARF/CRS 2.0 infrastructure operative NOW
- Enhanced self-certifications, TIN verification, continuous monitoring required

**3. AMLA (Current)**
- Revised AMLA adopted September 2025; enters force H2 2026
- LETA Transparency Register: beneficial owners registered
- 30-day discrepancy reporting: KYC vs. Transparency Register
- Advisor scope expansion: real estate transactions, non-operational entities, domicile services → SRO obligations

### Imminent Deadlines

**4. AMLA/LETA — H2 2026 (~3-6 months)**
- Federal Council implementing ordinances concluded January 30, 2026 (machinery active)
- Advisor scope expansion triggers SRO membership for Geneva wealth managers
- FATF evaluation pressure: Switzerland's next FATF evaluation = international compliance event

**5. EU AI Act — August 2, 2026 (~4.5 months)**
- High-risk AI obligations: credit scoring, insurance risk assessment (relevant for WM with lending/insurance products)
- FINMA Guidance 08/2024 has outlook section suggesting institutions consider EU AI Act standards
- Extraterritorial: Geneva WMs with EU clients or EU-nexus AI systems

**6. Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks)**
- Spillover: Geneva WMs advising healthcare clients must understand Swissdamed implications
- Professional liability: AI diagnostic tools used by healthcare WM clients
