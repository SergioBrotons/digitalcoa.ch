# Competitive Analysis: Geneva Healthcare AI Accountability
## Phase 04 — Cycle C287

---

## What Exists in the Market

### AI Tool Vendors (Competitive Landscape)

The Swiss healthcare AI vendor market is active and growing. Key players:

- **Konversa** — Swiss-native AI documentation tool, strong FMH association presence, Swiss-hosted infrastructure
- **Haidy** — AI practice assistant with German-language optimization, broad Swiss market penetration
- **Intonate** — AI scribe and clinical documentation tool, positioning as medical-device-adjacent
- **Medida** — Practice management AI with scheduling and documentation features
- **MPAssist** — Multi-language Swiss AI assistant for medical practices
- **AIAgens / Mira AI** — Emerging players with varying Swiss market focus
- **EMR-embedded AI** — Epic, CGM, and other EMR providers adding AI features directly into existing software without separate adoption decisions

**What vendors offer:** Tool functionality, Swiss data hosting claims, FMH-adjacent positioning, medical device registration where applicable.

**What vendors do not offer:** Governance frameworks, professional liability architecture, nFADP compliance assessment, carrier attestation support, Swissmedic classification evaluation, consent form design, human oversight documentation methodology.

### Professional Services (Gap Analysis)

**What exists:**
- General legal advisory on nFADP (law firms, but not medical practice specialization)
- IT security firms offering general AI security assessments
- Generic data protection officer services
- FMH continuing education on digital topics (abstract, not operational)

**What does NOT exist (addressable gap):**
- Medical practice-specific AI governance advisory
- Carrier pre-signing review for physician PI insurance with AI attestation
- MedDO classification evaluation service
- Swissmedic swissdamed registration support for small practices
- AI vendor contract review for physician professional liability compatibility
- Human oversight documentation protocol design for independent practices
- Geneva cantonal AI governance advisory for medical practices

### The Structural Gap

The market is organized around two distinct models:

1. **Tool vendors** — who have commercial incentive to minimize friction and maximize adoption, and therefore de-emphasize governance complexity
2. **Generic advisors** — who have no medical practice specialization and no understanding of the specific liability architecture physicians operate under

The physician is caught between:
- Vendors who want them to adopt quickly and don't explain the liability details
- Generic advisors who don't understand the medical practice context

There is no advisor in the Geneva market who speaks both languages: who understands the clinical workflow and the professional liability architecture simultaneously.

## What's Missing (The Addressable Gap)

### Gap 1: Pre-Adoption Evaluation Service

No service exists for the physician who is evaluating an AI tool before signing. What is needed:
- Vendor contract review for physician professional liability compatibility
- Data chain mapping (Swiss hosting vs. full data path through US LLMs)
- MedDO classification evaluation for the specific tool
- nFADP compliance gap assessment for the specific use case
- FMH professional obligation alignment check

**The market opportunity:** A pre-adoption evaluation brief — not a recommendation of which tool, but an assessment of what the physician is accepting when they sign.

### Gap 2: Existing Practice AI Audit

No service addresses the physician who has already adopted AI (incrementally, through software updates) and now needs to understand their accumulated exposure. The audit question is not "should you be using AI?" — it's "what have you already accepted, what obligations has that created, and what documentation do you need to be able to produce?"

**The market opportunity:** A retrospective exposure assessment for practices already using AI tools without governance architecture.

### Gap 3: Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review

PI insurance carriers are now adding AI-specific attestation questions to renewal forms. No Geneva service offers pre-signing review specifically for physicians — what the attestation commits them to, what they can and cannot represent, and what gap analysis should be completed before signing.

**The market opportunity:** A structured pre-signing review for medical practice PI renewal with AI attestation components.

### Gap 4: Human Oversight Documentation Protocol

FMH guidance requires physicians to maintain human oversight of AI-assisted decisions. No practical protocol exists in the Geneva market for what this documentation should contain, what format it should take, and how it should be maintained in daily clinical practice.

**The market opportunity:** A documentation protocol framework specifically designed for independent medical practice — proportionate to the scale of the practice, workable in actual clinical workflow, and defensible if ever reviewed.

### Gap 5: MedDO Classification Navigation

Swissmedic swissdamed registration involves technical documentation requirements that are designed for device manufacturers, not independent physicians. No advisory service exists to help physicians navigate the classification question and, where registration is required, prepare the appropriate documentation or identify when professional advisory support is needed.

**The market opportunity:** Classification guidance plus registration support for physicians whose AI tools may qualify as medical devices.

## Entry Points by Competitive Position

**Physician sees vendor marketing** → "This looks credible, but I need someone to tell me what I'm actually signing up for"
**Physician gets carrier renewal with AI questions** → "I need to understand what I'm attesting to before I sign this"
**Physician hears about swissdamed deadline** → "How do I know if it applies to me and what do I do?"
**Physician has patient ask about data** → "I need to be able to answer this — or know that I should refer"

Each entry point maps to a specific service category in the addressable gap.
