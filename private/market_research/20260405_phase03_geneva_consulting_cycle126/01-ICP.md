## Pain Clusters (C126 Fresh Angles)

### Pain 1: Post-Credit Suisse M&A Advisory and Corporate Restructuring
Geneva companies face a changed M&A landscape following the 2025 Credit Suisse resolution and UBS integration. Increased scrutiny on Swiss corporate transactions, new TLAC/MREL requirements affecting deal structuring, and uncertainty about UBS's post-integration acquisition appetite create advisory demand.

**Trigger questions:**
- How do revised TLAC requirements affect deal financing structures?
- Should we pursue M&A now or wait for market clarity post-UBS integration?
- How do we structure cross-border transactions given FINMA/SECCO scrutiny?
- What are the implications of Swiss banking concentration for our sector?

### Pain 2: AI-Driven Regulatory Compliance Consulting
Swiss companies implementing AI systems face a fragmented and evolving regulatory landscape: FADP AI provisions, DORA AI operational resilience requirements, EU AI Act implications for Swiss companies serving EU clients, and FINMA AI governance expectations for regulated entities.

**Trigger questions:**
- How does the FADP apply to our AI systems, and what AI-specific obligations do we have?
- What are the DORA AI requirements applicable to our Swiss operations?
- How do we structure an AI governance framework that satisfies both Swiss and EU requirements?
- What AI risk assessment methodology should we implement?

### Pain 3: CARB Tax Advisory for Geneva Commodity Traders
The Swiss Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CARB / SVUT) implementation creates new compliance and cost implications for Geneva-based commodity traders importing carbon-intensive goods. Understanding CARB scope, exemptions, and cost pass-through strategies requires specialist advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- Which of our import streams are subject to CARB?
- How do we structure CARB cost pass-through in commodity contracts?
- What documentation and reporting do we need for CARB compliance?
- Are there strategic advantages for early CARB compliance adopters?

### Pain 4: Interim Treasury Advisory for Finance Function Gaps
Swiss CFOs facing sudden departures, maternity/paternity leave, or restructuring need immediate treasury and finance function support. The Geneva market has a shortage of experienced interim CFOs and finance directors, creating a specialist advisory opportunity.

**Trigger questions:**
- Who can lead our finance function during a transition period?
- How do we maintain financial controls and stakeholder confidence during a CFO transition?
- What is the realistic timeline and cost for finding a permanent CFO replacement?
- How do we ensure continuity of regulatory compliance during transition?

### Pain 5: Swiss SME EU-Switzerland Institutional Agreement Adaptation
Swiss SMEs serving EU markets face strategic decisions about how to position themselves as the EU-Switzerland institutional agreement progresses. Market access implications, regulatory alignment decisions, and competitive positioning vis-à-vis EU competitors require advisory.

**Trigger questions:**
- How does the EU-CH institutional agreement affect our market access to EU clients?
- Should we establish an EU subsidiary, and if so, where?
- How do we position our services/ products competitively as EU-CH alignment evolves?
- What regulatory investments should we make now vs. wait-and-see?

### Pain 6: Geneva Family Office Governance Advisory
Geneva-based family offices managing CHF 50M–2B+ in assets face governance challenges as wealth transfers from first to second generation. Family charter development, investment governance, risk management frameworks, and succession planning create specialist advisory demand.

**Trigger questions:**
- How do we structure governance that maintains family cohesion while protecting assets?
- What investment policy and oversight framework is appropriate for our family office?
- How do we plan succession of the family office leadership?
- What risk management framework should a Geneva family office implement?
