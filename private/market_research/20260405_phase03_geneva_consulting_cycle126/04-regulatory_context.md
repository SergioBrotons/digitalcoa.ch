### 1. FADP AI Provisions — Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

**Authority:** FDPIC (Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner)
**Governing Law:** Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP / nLPD), Art. 20-23
**Status as of 2026:** FDPIC published AI-specific guidance (2024-2025); FADP revision fully in force; FDPIC enforcement capacity increased

**Key Requirements:**
- Transparency: Data subjects must be informed when AI-based automated decision-making is used
- Right to explanation: Data subjects have right to request explanation of automated decisions
- Profiling: AI-driven profiling requires explicit consent or legitimate interest justification
- Data quality: AI system outputs must be based on accurate, complete, and current data

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva companies using AI for customer scoring, hiring, credit decisions, fraud detection, or algorithmic trading face FADP compliance obligations requiring AI governance frameworks and documentation.

**EN:** "FADP's AI provisions (Art. 20-23) create specific obligations for Geneva companies using AI in automated decision-making — requiring AI governance frameworks, transparency documentation, and data quality procedures."
**FR:** "Les dispositions IA de la LPD (art. 20-23) créent des obligations spécifiques pour les entreprises genevoises utilisant l'IA dans la prise de décision automatisée — nécessitant des cadres de gouvernance IA, une documentation de transparence et des procédures de qualité des données."

### 2. DORA — Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU, with Swiss Implications)

**Authority:** EU (applies to EU financial entities; Swiss entities with EU clients affected)
**Governing Law:** EU Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) — entered into force January 2025
**Status as of 2026:** DORA fully applicable; Swiss companies serving EU financial clients face DORA requirements as contractual obligations or through EU client supply chain requirements

**Key Requirements:**
- ICT risk management framework
- ICT-related incident reporting
- Digital operational resilience testing (vulnerability assessments, penetration testing)
- Third-party ICT risk management (including AI service providers)
- AI-specific: DORA references AI operational resilience in ICT risk management

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva financial institutions (banks, asset managers, insurance) with EU client exposure face DORA compliance as a market access requirement. Geneva non-financial companies providing ICT services to EU financial institutions also face DORA third-party requirements.

**EN:** "DORA creates digital operational resilience requirements for Geneva financial institutions with EU market access — requiring ICT risk frameworks, incident reporting procedures, and third-party AI provider management."
**FR:** "DORA crée des exigences de résilience opérationnelle numérique pour les institutions financières genevoises ayant accès au marché européen — nécessitant des cadres de risque TIC, des procédures de signalement d'incidents et une gestion des fournisseurs d'IA tiers."

### 3. EU AI Act — Swiss Market Access Implications

**Authority:** EU
**Governing Law:** EU Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act) — entered into force 2024; phased implementation through 2027
**Status as of 2026:** EU AI Act in force; high-risk AI system obligations beginning to apply; Swiss companies with EU market access face EU AI Act classification requirements

**Key Requirements (High-Risk AI Systems):**
- Conformity assessment before market placement
- Risk management system throughout lifecycle
- Data governance and data quality requirements
- Technical documentation and logging
- Transparency and provision of information to users
- Human oversight measures
- Accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity requirements

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva companies developing or deploying AI systems used in EU (AI products, AI-enabled services) need to assess EU AI Act classification and compliance requirements. Geneva commodity trading firms using AI in trading algorithms face potential high-risk classification.

**EN:** "The EU AI Act creates market access compliance requirements for Geneva companies with AI products or services in the EU — requiring classification assessment, conformity evaluation, and technical documentation."
**FR:** "La loi européenne sur l'IA crée des exigences de conformité d'accès au marché pour les entreprises genevoises avec des produits ou services d'IA dans l'UE — nécessitant une évaluation de classification, une évaluation de conformité et une documentation technique."

### 4. CARB / SVUT — Swiss Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism

**Authority:** Federal Office for the Environment (FOEN) + Swiss Customs
**Governing Law:** CO2 Act revision / SVUT ordinance
**Status as of 2026:** CARB/SVUT implementation in initial phase; covered import categories defined; certificate surrender obligations in force for steel, aluminum, cement, fertilizers, electricity, hydrogen

**Key Requirements:**
- Importers of covered goods must surrender CARB certificates matching embedded carbon content
- CARB certificate price: ~CHF 120/tonne CO2 (aligned with Swiss domestic CO2 levy)
- Third-country exporters must provide carbon content documentation to Swiss importers
- Exemptions for imports from countries with equivalent carbon pricing (EU ETS equivalent threshold)
- Annual reporting to FOEN

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva commodity trading firms importing steel, aluminum, fertilizers, cement, and other covered goods face CARB compliance obligations with direct cost and documentation implications.

**EN:** "CARB/SVUT implementation creates compliance obligations and cost impacts for Geneva commodity traders importing carbon-intensive goods — requiring documentation systems, cost pass-through strategies, and competitive positioning assessment."
**FR:** "La mise en œuvre du SVUT crée des obligations de conformité et des impacts de coûts pour les maisons de trading genevoises important des biens à forte intensité de carbone — nécessitant des systèmes de documentation, des stratégies de передача затрат et une évaluation du positionnement concurrentiel."

### 5. FINMA TBTF 2.0 — Resolution Planning Requirements (Reference for M&A Advisory)

**Authority:** FINMA
**Governing Law:** Swiss Too-Big-To-Fail Act + FINMA Resolution Ordinance
**Status as of 2026:** Updated TBTF 2.0 framework in force; resolvability assessment methodology updated post-CS resolution

**Key Requirements (for reference in M&A advisory):**
- MREL/TLAC: Minimum eligible liabilities thresholds for systemically relevant institutions
- Resolution pre-clearance: FINMA review of transactions that could affect resolvability
- ILAC documentation: Internal loss-absorbing capacity documentation for G-SIBs/D-SIBs

**Geneva Relevance:** M&A transactions involving Geneva financial institutions or companies with systemic relevance require consideration of TBTF 2.0 implications in deal structuring and regulatory pre-clearance.

**EN:** "FINMA's updated TBTF 2.0 framework affects M&A deal structuring for Geneva systemically relevant institutions — requiring consideration of resolution pre-clearance and TLAC implications in transaction design."
**FR:** "Le cadre TBTF 2.0 mis à jour de la FINMA affecte la structuration des fusions-acquisitions pour les institutions systémiques genevoises — nécessitant la prise en compte de la pré-autorisation de résolution et des implications TLAC dans la conception des transactions."

### 6. Swiss Corporate Governance — Board Responsibilities

**Authority:** Swiss Code of Obligations (OR) + Swiss Takeover Board
**Governing Law:** Swiss Code of Obligations Art. 663-698 (corporate governance disclosure); SWX Corporate Governance Directive
**Status as of 2026:** Increased board responsibility for regulatory compliance oversight (CSRD board accountability, DORA oversight, ESG governance); proxy advisor scrutiny increasing

**Key Requirements:**
- Board duty of care and duty of loyalty
- Oversight obligation for regulatory compliance (including AI, DORA, ESG)
- ESG governance: Board-level ESG oversight expectations increasing
- CSRD: Board accountability for sustainability reporting (for large Swiss companies with EU nexus)

**Geneva Relevance:** Geneva corporate boards face increased governance obligations for regulatory compliance oversight — requiring board advisory support, compliance framework design, and board training.

**EN:** "Swiss board governance obligations are expanding to include explicit oversight responsibilities for AI governance, DORA, ESG, and regulatory compliance — creating board advisory and training demand for Geneva corporate boards."
**FR:** "Les obligations de gouvernance du conseil d'administration suisse s'élargissent pour inclure des responsabilités de surveillance explicites pour la gouvernance IA, DORA, ESG et la conformité réglementaire — créant une demande de conseil et de formation au conseil d'administration pour les conseils d'administration genevois."
