# 01 — ICP Language: How Geneva Independent Physicians Talk About AI Problems

## Phase 04 C289 | Geneva Healthcare ICP | 2026-05-28

---

## How the ICP Speaks About AI Problems

### The Adoption Language Problem

Geneva independent physicians do not describe their situation as "choosing an AI vendor." They describe something else entirely:

**The arrival framing:**
> "I didn't decide to use AI. It arrived. My practice management system updated. The template auto-completed. My staff started using it. I didn't sign anything."

**The accountability dawning:**
> "I realized if something goes wrong in a record I signed, it's my name on it. I don't know what the AI did. I don't know what it's doing now."

**The evidence gap recognition:**
> "When I look at what I signed, I can't reconstruct the AI's contribution. I can only say what I intended. But when an incident happens, they don't ask what I intended — they ask what I documented."

**The regulatory strangeness:**
> "Swissdamed says I might need to register something, but the categories seem written for device manufacturers, not for what I do. I still don't know if I'm Tier 1 or Tier 2. I still don't know who to ask."

**The institutional gap:**
> "I see CHUV and HUG making careful AI decisions, but I don't know how they do it. FMH puts out principles. I don't know what those principles mean for my practice on a Tuesday morning."

**The carrier moment:**
> "My insurance renewed. There's a form asking about AI governance. I have no idea what to answer. I don't know who does this, and I don't know what happens when I sign it."

**The patient conversation absence:**
> "My patients don't ask about AI. But I'm starting to wonder if they should. And I'm starting to wonder if I've adequately explained anything."

## French Language Register

**L'arrivée non-demandée:**
> "Je n'ai pas décidé d'utiliser l'IA. Elle est arrivée. Le système de gestion du cabinet a été mis à jour. Le modèle s'est rempli tout seul. Le personnel a commencé à l'utiliser. Je n'ai rien signé."

**La prise de conscience de la responsabilité:**
> "J'ai réalisé que si quelque chose se passe mal dans un dossier que j'ai signé, c'est mon nom dessus. Je ne sais pas ce que l'IA a fait. Je ne sais pas ce qu'elle fait maintenant."

**L'absence de protocole:**
> "Swissdamed dit que je devrais peut-être enregistrer quelque chose, mais les catégories semblent conçues pour les fabricants d'appareils, pas pour ce que je fais. Je ne sais toujours pas si je suis Tier 1 ou Tier 2."

**Le moment carrier:**
> "Mon assurance s'est renouvelée. Il y a un formulaire sur la gouvernance de l'IA. Je n'ai aucune idée de quoi répondre. Je ne sais pas qui fait cela, et je ne sais pas ce qui se passe quand je le signe."

---

## Key Language Characteristics

1. **Passive adoption framing** — AI arrived, did not choose
2. **Retrospective accountability realization** — names on records trigger question
3. **Regulatory strangeness** — frameworks that seem designed for other actors
4. **Institutional intelligence gap** — can see institutional caution, cannot access methodology
5. **Carrier form as acute moment** — renewal form functions as governance occasion
6. **Absence of peer language** — no Geneva forum where this is discussed in physician register

---

## What Is NOT Said (Silence Zones)

Physicians do not use language like:
- "AI governance architecture"
- "accountability framework"
- "processor/controller obligations"
- "documented decision chain"
- "attestation adequacy"

They use language like:
- "what do I sign"
- "who do I ask"
- "what does this mean for my practice"
- "what happens if"
- "I don't know who does this"
