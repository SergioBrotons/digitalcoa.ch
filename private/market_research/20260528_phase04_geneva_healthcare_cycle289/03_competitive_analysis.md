# 03 — Competitive Analysis: What Exists vs. What's Missing

## Phase 04 C289 | Geneva Healthcare ICP | 2026-05-28

---

## What Exists in the Market

### 1. Regulatory Doctrine (Does Not Serve Small-Practice Operations)

**What exists:**
- FMH physician ethics principles on AI (general, principle-level)
- Swissdamed registration framework (manufacturer-oriented categories)
- nFADP data protection obligations (framework without medical practice translation)
- MedDO medical device obligations (classification thresholds for devices, not practice operations)
- Swiss Federal Office of Public Health AI guidance (institutional scale orientation)

**What it does:**
Provides general obligations at a level of abstraction that produces appearance of compliance without operational substance for 2–10 physician independent practices.

**What it does not do:**
Translate regulatory doctrine into specific documentation requirements, operational workflows, or evidence standards that an independent physician can implement on a Tuesday morning.

---

### 2. Practice Management Software Vendors

**What exists:**
- AI-assisted documentation features embedded in primary care practice management systems
- Auto-complete templates, AI-generated clinical note suggestions, diagnostic coding assistance
- Vendor terms of service with AI data processing provisions
- "Swiss-hosted" or "GDPR-compliant" data storage claims

**What it does:**
Makes AI adoption invisible to the physician — features arrive through updates, operate without governance decision, and produce records with undocumented AI contribution.

**What it does not do:**
Provide physician accountability documentation, AI governance frameworks for independent practices, or nFADP DPIA methodology calibrated to small medical practices. Vendors optimize for feature adoption and data processing infrastructure, not physician professional liability protection.

---

### 3. Professional Liability Carriers

**What exists:**
- Third and fourth-generation AI attestation forms requiring physician personal signature
- Attestation questions about AI governance frameworks, oversight mechanisms, documented AI usage
- Renewal questionnaires that function as de facto operative standards

**What it does:**
Creates documentation requirements without defining what satisfies them. Produces carrier-specific risk evaluation instruments that operate as the physician's primary AI governance framework in practice.

**What it does not do:**
Provide pre-signing review services, Geneva-specific adequacy standards, or methodology for what constitutes adequate AI governance for a small independent practice. Carriers use attestation to evaluate risk, not to protect physician professional standing.

---

### 4. Institutional Hospital AI Governance

**What exists:**
- CHUV, HUG, and cantonal hospital AI deployment governance frameworks
- Multi-disciplinary AI oversight committees
- Internal AI tool evaluation protocols
- Institutional-level AI governance documentation standards

**What it does:**
Makes careful, proportionate institutional AI deployment decisions through adequately resourced governance processes.

**What it does not do:**
Transmit methodology, documentation standards, or governance intelligence to independent practices. Institutional governance operates inside institutional boundaries, not accessible to the Geneva independent practice community. Independent practices observe institutional caution without access to institutional methodology.

---

### 5. Medical-Focused IT and Cybersecurity Consultants

**What exists:**
- General cybersecurity services for medical practices
- Data protection compliance programs (GDPR/nFADP)
- IT infrastructure reviews
- Vendor selection assistance

**What it does:**
Addresses general data protection and IT security, not AI-specific medical accountability. Services are calibrated to the practice as a data controller in general, not as a clinical environment where AI generates authoritative medical records.

**What it does not do:**
Address physician professional liability for AI-assisted clinical decisions, FMH oversight documentation requirements, or the specific intersection of patient confidentiality and AI analytical contribution in medical records. General IT consultants cannot substitute for Geneva medical AI accountability expertise.

---

### 6. General Legal Advisors

**What exists:**
- Swiss law firms offering regulatory compliance advice
-Data protection counsel
- Healthcare regulatory specialists (hospital scale)

**What it does:**
Provides doctrine-level legal analysis on applicable frameworks.

**What it does not do:**
Serves small independent practice scale or medical AI-specific accountability questions. General legal counsel does not have FMH physician ethics context, carrier attestation pre-signing review methodology, or Swissdamed classification analysis calibrated to independent practice operations.

---

## What's Missing in the Market

### Missing Service 01: Independent Practice AI Governance Methodology

**What should exist:**
A proportionate governance methodology specifically designed for 2–10 physician independent practices that translates FMH principles into specific documentation requirements, operational workflows, and evidence standards.

**Gap:**
No Geneva service exists that provides physician-specific AI governance methodology for independent practices. The FMH-principle-to-operations translation is absent.

---

### Missing Service 02: Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review

**What should exist:**
A service that reviews carrier AI attestation forms before signature, analyzes what the physician commits to personally, identifies coverage gaps, and produces documentation that protects physician professional liability position.

**Gap:**
No Geneva market service offers pre-signing review of carrier AI attestation forms. Physicians sign personal liability instruments without independent analysis. The service gap is structural — carriers design forms for risk evaluation, and no independent review function exists in the market.

---

### Missing Service 03: Medical Record AI Attribution Protocol

**What should exist:**
A documentation protocol for AI-assisted medical records that establishes what the physician documented, what the AI contributed analytically, and what the physician reviewed and decided — creating an auditable record of AI analytical contribution that survives physician attestation.

**Gap:**
No Geneva service provides medical record AI attribution methodology for independent practices. Records are authoritative without documented AI contribution. When incidents occur, physicians cannot reconstruct AI analytical contribution retroactively.

---

### Missing Service 04: Swissdamed Classification Navigation

**What should exist:**
Classification navigation that translates Swissdamed criteria into independent practice obligations, determines applicable tier, identifies registration requirements, and produces documentation satisfying Swissdamed requirements at independent practice scale.

**Gap:**
No Geneva service provides Swissdamed classification analysis for independent outpatient practices. Criteria are manufacturer-oriented, and no independent calibration exists to translate them for physician operations. Practices remain in compliance paralysis.

---

### Missing Service 05: Patient AI Consent Architecture

**What should exist:**
Consent form architecture and patient communication methodology that addresses AI-assisted documentation in clinical contexts — what physicians must disclose, what language satisfies disclosure obligations, and how AI-assisted documentation operates within the consent framework.

**Gap:**
No Geneva service provides patient AI consent architecture for independent practices. Existing consent forms predate AI-assisted documentation and are structurally inadequate for the AI disclosure question. No methodology exists for what AI disclosure means in an outpatient clinical context.

---

### Missing Service 06: Independent Practice AI Governance Intelligence Feed

**What should exist:**
An intelligence function that tracks institutional AI governance decisions forming across Geneva healthcare institutions and translates methodology and standards into proportionate guidance for independent practices.

**Gap:**
No Geneva channel exists transmitting institutional AI governance intelligence to independent practices. Institutional decisions form effective standards without independent practice access. Practices build governance positions in isolation, unable to observe the standards forming around them.

## Competitive Positioning Implication

**The market is tool-saturated and governance-starved at independent practice scale.**

Vendors provide AI features without accountability infrastructure. Carriers create documentation requirements without adequacy standards. Institutions build governance without transmitting methodology. General IT and legal services address general compliance without medical AI-specific context. Independent practices are structurally unsupported for the specific accountability problems they face.

The gap is not knowledge availability in abstract — it is Geneva-calibrated, physician-proportionate, operationally implementable methodology at independent practice scale.

This is the exact structural gap that a Geneva-focused AI accountability briefing service is positioned to fill.
