## Competitive Tier Analysis

### Tier 1: Global Private Banks (Geneva Presence)
**Players:** UBS, Credit Suisse successor, Lombard Odier, Pictet, UBP
**Strengths:** Scale, global reach, established AI governance
**Weaknesses:** Expensive; not boutique-focused; generic frameworks

### Tier 2: Geneva WM Boutiques (CHF 500M–5B AuM)
**Strengths:** Geneva positioning; international client base; data sovereignty angle
**Weaknesses:** No AI governance practice; most exposed to FADP + FINMA + EU AI Act + Section 301 simultaneously
**Gap Opportunity:** CHF 150-300/hour AI governance advisory for Geneva WM boutiques

### Tier 3: Big 4 Advisory
**Players:** Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC
**Strengths:** Multi-jurisdictional; EU AI Act capacity from EU offices
**Weaknesses:** Expensive (CHF 350-600/hour); generic frameworks; conflicts of interest

### Tier 4: Independent Regulatory Consultants
**Strengths:** Specialist depth; flexible engagement
**Weaknesses:** No full-service capacity; no Geneva brand positioning
