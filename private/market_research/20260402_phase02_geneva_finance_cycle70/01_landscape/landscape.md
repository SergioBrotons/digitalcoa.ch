## Swiss Financial Centre Overview
- Total assets under management: CHF 9,300 billion
- Employment: ~160,000 full-time equivalents
- Service exports share: 16% of Swiss service exports
- Swiss banks operate globally but remain rooted in home market
- Sound banks, reliable legal system, stable currency = safe haven
- Multilingual, globally-minded workforce
- Source: SwissBanking.ch financial centre overview (primary source, C70 confirmed)

## Geneva Wealth Management Market
- Geneva: second-largest Swiss financial centre after Zurich
- Geneva WM boutiques: CHF 500M–5B AuM range most exposed to AI governance
- International client base: diplomatic, family offices, multinational executives
- Bilingual EN/FR standard in Geneva WM
- Data sovereignty as competitive differentiator: "excellent" Swiss institutional trust

## AI Adoption in Swiss Finance
- FINMA survey (C67 confirmed): 50% Swiss FIs using AI, 91% generative AI chatbots
- SBA: digital innovations "prerequisite for Switzerland's competitiveness" (confirmed C70)
- BigTech dependency risk flagged by FINMA
- SBA endorses Federal Council sector-specific approach vs EU AI Act comprehensive framework
- Swiss institutional trust "excellent" = data sovereignty competitive differentiator for U.S./international clients
- Source: FINMA survey; SwissBanking.ch

## Regulatory Density (Geneva Finance)
- FINMA: principles-based regulation (institutional independence confirmed)
- FADP: DPIA required for AI systems processing personal data
- EU AI Act: GPAI obligations in force since August 2, 2025; Annex III high-risk likely captures WM portfolio AI
- Federal AI Ordinance: 16+ months operative; sector-specific approach
- Section 301: forced labour supply chain ESG documentation burden
- August 2, 2026: EU AI Act double deadline

## Market Size Indicators
- Swiss legal market for AI governance: CHF 80-120M addressable (5-year)
- Geneva WM market: CHF 500M–5B AuM boutiques most exposed
- Geneva WM AI governance: underserved, no boutique advisor with FADP + EU AI Act GPAI + FINMA + Section 301

## Competitive Landscape
- Big 4: established but generic; expensive (CHF 350-600/hour)
- Swiss private banks (UBP, Lombard Odier, Pictet): developing AI governance internally
- Geneva WM boutiques: most exposed, least served — gap opportunity
- Independent consultants: fragmented, no Geneva WM positioning
