# 03 — Competitive Analysis: Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability

## Phase 04 — Cycle 293

## What Exists in the Geneva Healthcare AI Governance Market vs. What Is Missing

---

## Current Market Presence

### What Does Exist

**1. FMH Position Statements**
FMH has issued statements acknowledging AI in medical practice and affirming human oversight principle. These statements provide high-level accountability framing but no operational translation for independent practice. They serve as a reference point for the principle, not a implementation methodology.

**Availability:** Public, widely circulated, French and German versions available.

**Limitations:** Principle-only. No documentation requirement specification, no competency standard, no review frequency definition, no operational endpoint. Functions as a directional statement more than a compliance framework.

**2. General nFADP / Data Protection Resources**
Swiss data protection authority (FDPIC) guidance on AI and data protection exists at a general level. Legal and compliance market offers data protection advisory services. Some general AI governance tools and templates exist at the European level.

**Availability:** Public resources, general advisors in Geneva market, FDPIC published positions available.

**Limitations:** nFADP applies to AI processing but no Geneva-calibrated physician-specific integration methodology exists. Generic data protection frameworks do not map to independent practice small-scale multi-framework accountability management.

**3. EMR Vendor Documentation and Guidance**
EMR vendors provide product documentation, terms of service, AI feature descriptions, and support portals. Vendors provide terms of service agreements and in-product disclosures.

**Availability:** Direct from vendors, available during software setup, click-through acceptance workflow.

**Limitations:** Vendor documentation serves vendor commercial interests, not physician professional accountability requirements. Terms are designed to enable vendor data processing and limit vendor liability, not to help physician map accountability obligations. No vendor provides proportionate independent-practice AI governance guidance calibrated to FMH physician accountability.

**4. Insurance Carrier Attestation Forms**
Carriers have developed AI governance attestation forms for medical practice renewals. These documents exist and are actively circulated.

**Availability:** Distributed by carriers at renewal, required for policy continuation.

**Limitations:** Forms are designed for underwriting risk evaluation, not physician professional liability protection. No pre-signing review service exists in Geneva market. Forms commit physician to an undefined standard without providing the standard definition or review pathway. Carrier forms create the accountability exposure while providing no tool to address it.

**5. General Swiss Healthcare AI Discussion Platforms**
Webinars, conferences, and publications addressing AI in Swiss healthcare broadly. Swiss MedTech industry associations, hospital networks, and academic medical centers host events discussing AI governance in healthcare settings.

**Availability:** Available through professional networks, conferences, association memberships.

**Limitations:** Content targets institutional settings (hospitals, clinic groups) not independent practice scale. Most Geneva independent practices do not have staff dedicated to tracking these developments. The institutional context makes guidance inapplicable as-is to small practice.

## What Is Structurally Missing

### Market Gaps Specific to Geneva Independent Practice AI Accountability

**Gap 1: Pre-Signing Carrier Attestation Review Service for Medical Practitioners**
No Geneva market participant offers signatory-side review of carrier AI attestation forms for physicians. The wealth management market has begun developing pre-signing review for carrier attestation. No equivalent exists for medical practitioners.

**Why it matters:** Carrier forms are designed by underwriting teams, not physician liability teams. The physician signing commits to an undefined adequacy standard without any independent Geneva-calibrated analysis of whether their current AI governance position satisfies that standard. No review = no discharge pathway = personal liability exposure from signed attestation that remains unexamined until an incident triggers evaluation.

**Evidence:** Carrier AI attestation wave is actively circulating. No Geneva medical practice service advertises pre-attestation review. No physician knows what "adequate AI governance controls" means for their specific practice before signing.

---

**Gap 2: Independent-Practice-Disproportionate Documentation Protocol**
No Geneva-specific documentation protocol exists for independent practices managing AI-assisted documentation across 2–10 physician groups. Documentation standards implied by FMH oversight principle are written for institutional-level compliance, not small practice operations. What minimal viable documentation looks like for a 2-physician practice is nowhere specified.

**Why it matters:** FMH oversight principle is unenforceable at independent practice scale without operational definition. Present-tense AI-assisted medical record for a 24-month period is already built with zero documented AI contribution. When an incident occurs, the physician's accountability position requires evidence that does not exist and cannot be retroactively built.

**Evidence:** No FMH publication, no canton guidance, no professional association publication addresses what minimal documentation compliance looks like for 2–10 physician independent practice. Guidance applies at institutional scale or not at all.

---

**Gap 3: Geneva-Context AI Governance Training with Certification Mapping**
No Geneva-calibrated AI training path maps to FMH physician competency expectations. Vendors offer product training. General AI courses exist. No AI training for physicians addresses the intersection of: FMH oversight principle, nFADP data processing obligations, patient confidentiality requirements, AI documentation accountability, and medical record authorship.

**Why it matters:** If a physician is accountable for AI-assisted decisions and FMH requires human oversight, what training demonstrates adequate AI competency for medical practice? No standard exists. No certification path. No documentation standard for what AI training a physician must maintain.

**Evidence:** No Geneva medical association or canton offers specific AI competency certification for physicians. No continuing education credit structure specifically addresses AI governance for independent practice. Physician's AI competency is demonstrated through whatever training vendor supplied or general course completion — neither mapped to FMH accountability requirements.

---

**Gap 4: Patient-Generated AI Content Medical Record Protocol**
No documentation standard, no workflow protocol, no liability mapping for when patient-generated AI content is incorporated into physician's medical record under physician's professional authority in Geneva.

**Why it matters:** Three-party chain (patient → consumer AI → physician record → physician liability) operates entirely ungoverned. Patient arrives with AI analysis. Physician incorporates into record. Professional accountability attaches to content the physician did not generate, did not evaluate with their own methods, and may not have the training to critically assess.

**Evidence:** No Swiss guidance, no canton protocol, no EMR workflow modification addresses this category. The category exists in practice but has no accompanying governance architecture.

---

**Gap 5: AI-Assisted Second Opinion Documentation Protocol Between Independent Practices**
No documentation standard governing the accountability distribution when an independent physician's colleague uses AI to produce a second opinion that enters the original physician's patient record. No protocol defining: what the consulting physician must document about AI use, what the original physician must document about receiving AI-assisted input, what the record must reflect about AI involvement, what consent is required.

**Why it matters:** Peer consultation is routine medical practice. AI-assisted peer consultation creates a three-party accountability chain. Original physician becomes professionally responsible for AI-generated content from a system they did not deploy, configure, or review. No Geneva protocol defines the documentation obligations for each party.

**Evidence:** No FMH guidance, no canton guidance, no professional association protocol, no EMR workflow addresses AI-assisted second opinion in physician-to-physician consultation context.

---

**Gap 6: Three Consent Architecture Integration Service**
No integration protocol, no proportionate methodology, no documentation template exists for independent practice to manage three simultaneously active consent structures: treatment consent (FMH/HRA), nFADP data processing consent, and AI-specific consent disclosure. The three have structurally incompatible requirements.

**Why it matters:** A single consent form cannot satisfy all three simultaneously. Attempting to collapse them produces either false adequacy (when one form claims to cover all three) or compliance theater (when three forms are provided without integration). No proportionate architecture exists for independent practice to manage all three as coherent system.

**Evidence:** Physicians report attempting to address triple consent through their existing single treatment consent form. No Canton de Genève guidance, no FMH protocol, no practice management resource addresses how the three consent structures interact for independent practice.

---

**Gap 7: Geneva Institutional Intelligence Access Path for Independent Practices**
No pathway exists for independent practices to access institutional intelligence about AI governance standards forming at HUG/CHUV/Geneva institutional ecosystem level. Standards form through governance rooms where independent practices have no participation mechanism. The intelligence is operationally relevant — carriers reference institutional standards in underwriting, legal proceedings reference institutional practices in liability determinations.

**Why it matters:** Independent practices are building AI governance decisions without operational intelligence available to institutional counterparts. The gap between what institutions know about operative standards and what independent practices build in isolation is structural, not informational.

**Evidence:** HUG and CHUV participate in formal and informal standard-formation channels. Independent physicians do not. No Geneva service is offered to translate institutional level intelligence to independent practice level, or to represent independent practice perspective in ongoing standard formation.

---

**Gap 8: Vendor AI Terms Audit Against FMH Accountability Requirements**
No service exists for independent practices to audit their existing vendor AI terms against their FMH professional accountability requirements. What would an FMH-accountable vendor AI terms evaluation look like? What specific contractual provisions are incompatible with physician accountability? No Geneva market participant has developed proportionate analysis methodology for independent practice.

**Why it matters:** Technical opt-out impossibility means many physicians are operating under vendor terms they accepted through click-through without evaluation. The physician may not be aware of data usage for model training provisions, API processing location, or third-party data sharing that creates obligations incompatible with their FMH accountability. No audit = no mapping of where vendor terms and physician accountability are structurally incompatible.

**Evidence:** No Geneva market service offers vendor AI terms analysis for independent medical practice. No canton or FMH resource identifies the specific contractual provisions that require independent practice attention.

## Key Finding for Positioning

The Geneva independent practice healthcare AI accountability market is: **tool-saturated and governance-starved.** Physicians have more AI tools entering their practice than any governance framework can manage. The market has no proportionate, Geneva-specific, independent-practice-calibrated service addressing the intersection of FMH accountability, carrier attestation, nFADP obligations, and AI-assisted documentation.

**The service opportunity:** A briefing or documentation review service written in the physician's language, addressing personal liability rather than regulatory compliance, proportionate to independent practice scale (2–10 physicians), and calibrated to Geneva-specific regulatory context — including FMH, canton guidance, carrier attestation requirements, and nFADP — that no current market participant provides.
