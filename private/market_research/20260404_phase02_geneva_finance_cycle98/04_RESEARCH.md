# Finding 04 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 02: Geneva Finance
**Source:** Swiss fund industry associations (SFAMA, Swiss Funds & Asset Management), FINMA  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Swiss Fund Industry Consolidation Accelerates — Regulatory Cost Burden Favours Larger Geneva Managers**

The Swiss fund industry is experiencing a structural consolidation wave driven by the rising cost of regulatory compliance under FINMA's updated rules. Smaller and mid-sized Geneva-based fund managers are being acquired by larger international groups or are merging to achieve scale economics.

Key dimensions:

**1. FINMA licensing costs drive consolidation:** The cost of maintaining a FINMA fund management license — including compliance, audit, AML, and ESG disclosure requirements — has risen significantly. Sub-scale managers cannot achieve ROI on license infrastructure.

**2. Cross-border fund distribution (CBCD/DACIF):** Switzerland's ability to distribute funds into the EU under the revised CBCD (Collective Bermudian/DACIF framework equivalent) remains conditional on EU recognition. Uncertainty around recognition is influencing M&A strategy for Geneva managers with EU distribution.

**3. Delegation model scrutiny:** FINMA's updated guidance on portfolio management delegation (outsourcing to external asset managers) has made the legal documentation of delegation chains more complex. Managers with opaque delegation structures face remediation costs.

**4. ETF and passive fund pressure on active managers:** Geneva-based active fund managers face persistent AUM pressure from US and Irish-domiciled ETFs. This revenue pressure accelerates M&A as firms seek product shelf expansion through acquisition.

**5. Tokenized fund structures:** Swiss regulatory framework for tokenized investment funds (under DLT Act provisions) is becoming clearer. Larger Geneva managers with the capital to invest in tokenization infrastructure are gaining competitive advantage over smaller players.

**Signal for Brotons.net:** Fund industry consolidation is a structural story with long-term implications for Geneva's financial ecosystem. A clear-eyed analysis of why this is happening and who benefits would be widely read.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** Investor clients assessing Geneva fund managers need to understand which firms are structurally sound and which face regulatory cost pressure. Advisory angle: due diligence on fund managers.
