# Finding 02 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 02: Geneva Finance
**Source:** FINMA, SIX Group, Swiss fintech ecosystem analysis  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Geneva Fintech Licensing Backlog Clears as FINMA Streamlines DLT Tokenization Approvals**

FINMA has made measurable progress on processing backlogged fintech license applications, particularly for distributed ledger technology (DLT) and tokenization platforms. Geneva's fintech ecosystem — centred around the Canton of Geneva's support for digital assets — is seeing renewed momentum.

Key developments:

**1. DLT platform authorizations:** FINMA has approved a growing list of DLT trading facilities and tokenization platforms under the existing DLT Act framework. Geneva-based applicants have been among the recipients, including platforms tokenizing real estate, art, and private equity stakes.

**2. Tokenization of real-world assets (RWA):** Geneva private banks and asset managers are exploring RWA tokenization as a distribution mechanism for alternative investments. Legal frameworks for tokenized bonds, funds, and art are becoming more defined.

**3. Stablecoin regulation incoming:** Switzerland's proposed stablecoin ordinance (building on existing fintech sandbox provisions) will create a new licensing category. Geneva financial institutions positioning for stablecoin issuance need early legal advisory to shape compliance frameworks.

**4. Banking-as-a-Service (BaaS) regulatory clarity:** FINMA has issued updated guidance on BaaS arrangements, clarifying when API-connected financial service providers need a banking license vs. a fintech license. This has resolved months of regulatory uncertainty for Geneva BaaS startups.

**5. Crypto custody for institutional clients:** Geneva's private banks are expanding institutional crypto custody services following clearer regulatory guidance. The compliance requirements for cold storage, insurance, and segregation are generating specialized legal work.
