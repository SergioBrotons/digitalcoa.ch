## What Changed This Cycle

### New Finding: FATF Mutual Evaluation 2026-2027 — Confirmed External Driver
Switzerland's AMLA overhaul is directly driven by the FATF mutual evaluation scheduled for 2026-2027. This adds an international compliance scrutiny layer to the domestic AMLA/LETA timeline. Geneva WM firms face external FATF assessors examining SRO coverage, beneficial ownership verification, and AMLA compliance documentation — alongside the domestic H2 2026 implementation. This elevates AMLA from a domestic regulatory update to an international compliance event.

### New Finding: No 2026 FINMA AI Circular Released
Primary source confirmation (Lenz & Staehelin, FINMA official): FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 18, 2024) remains the current baseline for AI governance in Swiss financial institutions. No specific "2026 FINMA AI guidance circular" has been released. FINMA Circular 2026/1 (nature-related financial risks, January 2026) signals expanding risk governance expectations — AI governance is part of this trend but no new standalone FINMA AI guidance.

### New Finding: SIF AI Report Expected Late 2025 / Early 2026
The State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF) is expected to publish a comprehensive report analyzing financial market regulations for AI gaps and obstacles — late 2025 or early 2026. This will inform future Swiss AI regulatory direction and may identify specific gaps in WM sector AI governance.

### Competitive Landscape Update
Three new entrants identified in adjacent spaces:
- **AI Risk (ai-risk.co):** Boutique advisory specializing in Agentic AI strategy for regulated industries including financial services
- **MTF Solutions Switzerland:** AI advisory including company-specific AI guidelines, policies, legal/ethical/data protection risk assessment
- **TA Advisory (Geneva):** Legal/regulatory compliance and corporate governance for private clients and family offices

None are dedicated boutique WM AI governance advisory competitors — the specific positioning gap remains intact.

### Geneva Boutique WM AI Adoption Confirmed
Named Geneva boutique WM firms (GFO Geneva Family Office, MJ&Cie, Geneva Trust) confirmed using AI internally for risk management, operational efficiency, personalized client experiences, and compliance monitoring — but not offering external AI governance advisory services. The gap is both competitive AND informational: Geneva WM firms are AI users, not AI governance advisors.
