# Phase 02 Regulatory Context — Geneva Finance / Boutique WM
## Cycle 9 (2026-03-30)

---

## Layer 1: Swiss Domestic Regulation

### nFADP (Swiss Data Protection Act)
- **Status:** Operative; FDPIC enforcement active
- **Key date:** FDPIC May 8, 2025 confirmed — AI-based data processing falls directly under nFADP Article 5(c)
- **Implication:** Every AI application processing personal client data triggers nFADP obligations; DPIA required
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### FINMA Guidance 08/2024 (December 18, 2024)
- **Status:** Current baseline (~15 months old as of March 2026)
- **Scope:** Four pillars — Governance, Risk Management, Transparency, Non-Discrimination
- **No 2026-specific FINMA AI circular released** (Lenz & Staehelin, FINMA official source confirmed)
- **FINMA Circular 2026/1:** Nature-related financial risks (January 2026) signals expanding FINMA risk governance expectations — AI governance part of this trend
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### AMLA Revision (Anti-Money Laundering Act)
- **Status:** H2 2026 (~3-6 months) — implementation machinery active since January 30, 2026
- **Key changes:**
  - Extended to advisors (wealth managers providing investment advice, estate planning, family office services)
  - SRO membership required for in-scope advisors
  - Beneficial ownership verification
  - 30-day discrepancy reporting to LETA Transparency Register
  - MROS suspicious activity reporting
- **Triggering activities:** Real estate transactions; establishing/restructuring non-operational entities; domicile/registered office services
- **FATF mutual evaluation 2026-2027:** Drives Switzerland's AMLA overhaul urgency
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### LETA (Limited Liability Companies and Mandatory Disclosure)
- **Status:** H2 2026 (~3-6 months, parallel with AMLA)
- **Key obligations:** Central transparency register; UBO (Ultimate Beneficial Owner) filing; 30-day discrepancy reporting
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### CARF/CRS 2.0
- **Status:** ALREADY LIVE since January 1, 2026
- **Key changes:** New data fields (account balances, UUIDs); pre-population; DAC2/CARF automated exchanges expanding
- **Implication:** 3 months already operative; 2025 data due in 2026 reporting cycle
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### Swissdamed
- **Status:** July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks away)
- **Scope:** Cross-sector data governance precedent; SaMD serious incident obligations
- **Implication:** Data governance norm setter for Geneva WM; spillover from healthcare
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Layer 2: Swiss International Regulation

### FATF Mutual Evaluation 2026-2027
- **Status:** Scheduled; external compliance assessment
- **Impact:** Switzerland's AMLA overhaul directly driven by FATF standards compliance; Geneva WM firms face external scrutiny alongside domestic regulatory implementation
- **FINMA role:** Recognizing and overseeing SROs that supervise in-scope advisors
- **Implication:** FATF evaluators will assess SRO coverage of Geneva WM advisors; documentation urgency increased
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### OECD CRS 2.0
- **Status:** Already live (January 1, 2026)
- **Confidence:** HIGH

## Layer 3: EU Extraterritorial (If EU Market Nexus)

### EU AI Act (August 2, 2026)
- **Status:** High-risk obligations operative August 2, 2026; GPAI already live since August 2, 2025
- **Scope:** Extraterritorial if Geneva WM firm has EU market nexus (places AI on EU market, operates EU-intended output, or acts as EU importer/distributor)
- **GPAI already live:** ~7.5 months of operative GPAI obligations already passed
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### GDPR (Legacy Obligations)
- **Status:** Ongoing for any EU data subjects
- **Confidence:** HIGH

### DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act)
- **Status:** January 2025 operative
- **Scope:** Financial sector digital operational resilience; applicable to WM firms with EU nexus
- **Confidence:** HIGH
