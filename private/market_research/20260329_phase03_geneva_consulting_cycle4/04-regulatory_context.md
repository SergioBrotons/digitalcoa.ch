## nFADP — The Universal Entry Point

### FDPIC May 8, 2025 — The Explicit Confirmation
The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner explicitly confirmed on **May 8, 2025** that the nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing. This is the definitive regulatory signal that nFADP enforcement covers AI tool usage.

**What this means for small Geneva consulting firms:**
- Using ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any AI tool to process client data = data processing under nFADP
- A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with the AI vendor is required
- The legal basis for processing must be documented
- Breach notification obligations apply

### Key nFADP Requirements for Small Consultants

| Requirement | What It Means for the ICP |
|---|---|
| Legal basis for processing | Must have contract, legitimate interest, or consent before using AI on client data |
| Data subject rights | Must handle client requests (access, correction, deletion) |
| **Breach notification: "as soon as possible"** | nFADP requires notification "as soon as possible" — not strictly 72 hours like GDPR. Still mandatory and enforceable. |
| **DPA with AI vendors** | Using ChatGPT/Claude without a DPA = potential nFADP violation |
| Transfer restrictions | US-based AI providers may require Standard Contractual Clauses |
| Privacy by Design / Privacy by Default | Must integrate data protection from the earliest stages of AI tool selection |

## EU AI Act GPAI — Now Operative (Cycle 4 Addition)

### Status: August 2, 2025 — GPAI Rules Active
The EU AI Act's General Purpose AI (GPAI) rules **became operative on August 2, 2025**. This is not a future date — it is now active law.

**Implications for Geneva consulting firms with EU clients:**

| GPAI Obligation | Practical Meaning |
|---|---|
| Technical documentation | Must document AI systems used in client delivery |
| Transparency | Must inform clients when AI is used in producing deliverables |
| Training data copyright | Must be aware of copyright status of AI model training data |
| Systemic risk models | Enhanced obligations for most powerful models (not directly relevant to small firms) |
| Non-compliance penalties | €7.5M–35M or 1%–7% of global annual turnover |

**The GPAI operative date is a conversation trigger:** "Are your EU clients asking about your AI methodology? They will be — and GPAI is now operative."

## Federal Council 2026 AI Action Plan (Cycle 4 Addition)

### What It Says
- **Sector-specific approach confirmed**: No horizontal AI act. Targeted amendments to existing laws where necessary.
- **FDJP consultation draft**: End of 2026 — scope: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight
- **Non-binding measures**: Industry solutions and voluntary commitment declarations; DETEC-led implementation plan by end of 2026
- **Federal Administration AI sub-strategy 2026–2030**: Skills, trust, efficiency — signals what "good AI governance" looks like
- **Political framing**: "Innovation-friendly framework without excessive regulatory burden" — Geneva business community will respond positively

### Implications for the Advisory Window
The FDJP consultation draft doesn't arrive until end of 2026. The period **2026 through early 2027** is the advisory opportunity window — before Swiss law hardens. The current regulatory environment is:
- Soft enough to allow compliance positioning without rigid rules
- Structured enough that nFADP enforcement is real and active
- Clear enough that EU AI Act obligations for EU-facing firms are defined

**After the FDJP consultation and parliamentary ratification of the CoE AI Convention, the landscape will be more defined and potentially more restrictive. The window is now.**

## CoE AI Convention — Switzerland Signed March 2025

**Framework Convention on Artificial Intelligence, Human Rights, Democracy and the Rule of Law**
- Signed by Switzerland in March 2025
- Ratification process: Swiss legal amendments expected by **end of 2026**
- Creates framework for 2027+ — not immediately operative but signals direction
- FDJP consultation draft will define implementation scope

**Implication:** The CoE AI Convention reinforces that AI governance is a rights/human rights issue, not just a technical one. For consulting firms, this means client expectations around AI governance will increase.
