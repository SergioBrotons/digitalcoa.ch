# Phase 04 Geneva Healthcare — ICP (Cycle 5)
## Target Vertical: Small Medical Practices (2–10 Physicians), Geneva/Vaud

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** 2–10 physicians; micro-practice to small group
- **Location:** Geneva city + Vaud (Lausanne anchor via CHUV); increasingly telemedicine/virtual care
- **Structure:** Independent practices; group practices; dental; specialist (dermatology, cardiology, psychiatry); FMH-affiliated
- **Revenue model:** TARMED (physician tariff); cantonal health insurance reimbursement; private pay

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary decision-maker:** Practice owner / Chief Physician
- **Secondary:** Practice manager (if exists; often physician manages admin)
- **Decision dynamic:** Physician-driven; informal; fast for small practices
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for clinical risk; moderate for technology adoption; compliance is regulatory, not competitive

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Praxis software (Twin, Aesculap, or legacy); medmatically/B家; imaging systems; increasingly: AI-assisted diagnostics, scheduling AI, patient communication AI
- **AI exposure:** High and growing — diagnostic AI, scheduling, patient communication, billing automation, documentation (EMR AI scribe)
- **Technology trust:** High for clinical tools (CE/FDA certified); low for administrative AI governance awareness
- **IT support:** External IT vendor or none; practice manager or physician manages

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **Swissdamed (Switzerland Medical Device Database)** — operative July 1, 2026 (~3 months); SaMD registration required
2. **nFADP** — operative since September 2023; patient data is sensitive personal data; AI processing = Art. 5(c) + DPIA Art. 22
3. **FDPIC enforcement trajectory** — healthcare + legal are FDPIC priority sectors (FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report, July 2025)
4. **EU AI Act** — extraterritorial; if AI SaMD has EU market nexus
5. **CHUV Meditron** — Swiss medical LLM testing May 2026; Vaud institutional AI context; medical AI norm-setting
6. **AI for Good Global Summit 2026** — July 7–10, Geneva — premier health AI conference

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Swissdamed July 1, 2026 (most concrete regulatory deadline in portfolio); peer activity at AI for Good
- **Language:** French (Geneva primary) or bilingual EN/FR; German acceptable for Vaud; English for international medicine
- **Trust marker:** FMH reference, Swissmedic, FDPIC; CHUV institutional anchor strengthens Vaud credibility
- **Objection:** "Our Praxis software vendor handles compliance" — usually false; AI SaMD governance is the practice's responsibility
- **Sales cycle:** 4–12 weeks; physician trust is personal and peer-referred; FMH network is key
- **Budget:** CHF 1,500–6,000 for initial AI governance framework; retainer for ongoing

### Swissdamed: The #1 Concrete Deadline
- **Operative:** July 1, 2026 (~3 months from now)
- **Scope:** All medical devices including Software as a Medical Device (SaMD) must be registered
- **Small practice implication:** AI diagnostic tools, AI scheduling, patient AI communication tools may qualify as SaMD
- **Action required:** Inventory all AI tools used in clinical workflow; determine if Swissdamed registration required
- **Enforcement:** Swissmedic enforcement; FDPIC parallel nFADP enforcement for AI data processing
