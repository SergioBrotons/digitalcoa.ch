# The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework
**A practical governance guide for Swiss consulting firms facing the August 2026 compliance window**

---

## The Situation Has Changed

If you are running a small or mid-sized consulting firm in Geneva or French-speaking Switzerland, the AI governance question is no longer theoretical. It became concrete when:

**The GPAI obligations became real in August 2025.** The General Purpose AI Code of Practice published on July 10, 2025, created compliance obligations that are already running. If your firm develops, trains, or provides access to AI models — or uses AI in ways that affect client outcomes — these obligations already apply.

**The August 2, 2026 deadline is four months away.** High-risk AI system compliance requirements activate in August 2026. This is not a distant future event. It is a hard compliance deadline in four months.

**Your clients are already there.** Sixty-three percent of the smallest firms in Geneva (1-10 employees) are already using AI. Your consulting clients have adopted AI faster than most governance frameworks have been updated. The gap between what your clients use and what you formally govern is your exposure.

**Geneva is becoming the global AI governance capital.** The 2027 World AI Summit will be held in Geneva. The ITU AI for Good Global Summit is annual in Geneva. Your city's position in the global AI governance conversation creates an opportunity for firms that understand this space.

This framework is not about adopting more AI. It is about governing the AI you already use and preparing for the August 2, 2026 obligations that will define the compliance baseline for Swiss consulting firms.

---

## Part 1: Shadow AI Audit — Decision 0

Before you can govern AI, you need an honest inventory of what exists. For most small consulting firms, this is the most revealing exercise in the framework.

### What AI Are Your Consultants Actually Using?

- [ ] Have you documented every AI tool currently used by your consultants for research, analysis, drafting, or client deliverables?
- [ ] Do you know which of these tools process or store client data?
- [ ] Are AI-augmented deliverables reviewed by humans before client delivery?
- [ ] Do you have quality controls for AI-assisted consulting work?

### The Decision 0 Principle

Govern the AI you already use before adopting more. This is Decision 0 — it comes before any decision to expand AI usage. Most firms that have failed with AI governance jumped to adoption without completing this step.

---

## Part 2: GPAI Obligations — Are You Already Subject?

The Global Partnership on AI (GPAI) obligations became operative on August 2, 2025. These apply to providers of general purpose AI models.

### Assess Your Position

- [ ] Does your firm develop, train, fine-tune, or provide access to AI models?
- [ ] Do you offer AI-powered tools or platforms to clients?
- [ ] Do you integrate third-party AI models into your consulting deliverables?

If the answer to any of these is yes, GPAI obligations already apply to your firm.

### GPAI Documentation Requirements
- [ ] Do you maintain technical documentation for your AI models?
- [ ] Do you have transparency documentation for AI system users?
- [ ] Have you assessed whether your models pose systemic risk?

### The GPAI Code of Practice (July 10, 2025)
The GPAI Code of Practice provides the operational framework. Key requirements: documentation, transparency about copyrighted training data, safety and security measures. The Code is voluntary but establishes the compliance baseline.

## Part 3: August 2, 2026 — The High-Risk Deadline

The high-risk AI obligations under the EU AI Act activate on August 2, 2026 (~4 months). This is the most time-sensitive obligation in this framework.

### Are Any of Your AI Systems High-Risk?

AI systems are high-risk if they:
- Make or influence decisions with legal or significant financial consequences
- Are used in employment, credit, education, or essential services
- Process biometric data or make assessments about individuals

### High-Risk Compliance Requirements (August 2, 2026)
- [ ] Have you conducted a conformity assessment for high-risk AI systems?
- [ ] Do you have risk management systems in place?
- [ ] Are your AI systems traceable and documented?
- [ ] Do you have human oversight measures for high-risk AI decisions?
- [ ] Is your accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity adequate?

### The EU AI Act Extraterritorial Reach
If your consulting firm serves clients in the EU or operates across borders, the EU AI Act applies regardless of Swiss domicile. This is not optional for firms with EU operations.

---

## Part 4: FADP — Data Protection for Consulting Deliverables

The Federal Act on Data Protection has been operative since September 1, 2023. For consulting firms, client data processed through AI tools is the primary FADP exposure.

### Client Data Governance
- [ ] Do your client agreements cover AI-assisted processing of their data?
- [ ] Are clients informed when their data is processed through AI tools?
- [ ] Can you demonstrate what data was processed, by which tool, and for what purpose?

### The Accountability Gap
Consulting firms are trusted with sensitive client information. AI tools that process this information without proper governance create FADP exposure that your professional liability insurance may not cover.

---

## Part 5: Quality and Accountability in AI-Augmented Consulting

When a consulting recommendation contains an error, the accountability is clear. When an AI-augmented recommendation contains an error, the accountability is less clear — until you have governance in place.

### Documenting AI Augmentation
- [ ] Do your deliverables distinguish AI-assisted analysis from human analysis?
- [ ] Do you have a quality review process for AI-generated content?
- [ ] Is your professional liability coverage adequate for AI-augmented services?

### The Efficiency-Growth Paradox
Sixty-six percent of organizations report gains from AI. But efficiency gains from AI do not automatically translate into governance maturity. The firms that will have problems are those that scaled AI use without scaling governance in parallel.

Geneva is becoming the global AI governance capital. The 2027 World AI Summit. The ITU AI for Good Global Summit. The UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance. These are not just events — they are signals about where Geneva's competitive advantage lies.

### Leverage the Geneva Ecosystem
- [ ] Can your firm position within Geneva's AI governance ecosystem?

- [ ] Can you demonstrate Geneva AI governance competence to clients?

### The Competitive Frame
The smallest Geneva firms (1-10 employees) show 63% AI adoption. They are your clients. They are adopting AI faster than governance frameworks are being updated. You can be the firm that helps them close that gap — or you can be a firm that closes its own gap and then helps them.
