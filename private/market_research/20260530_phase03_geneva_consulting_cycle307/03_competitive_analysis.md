# Phase 03 Competitive Analysis — Geneva Consulting Boutique
## C307 Phase 03 (2026-05-30)

## What Exists in Market vs. What's Missing (C307 NET-NEW Layer)

### Competitive Landscape Overview

The Geneva consulting boutique AI governance advisory market is nascent. The primary competitive dynamics are:

1. **Large advisory firms** (Big4, major strategy consultancies) — targeting larger clients, priced for enterprise
2. **Legal tech platforms** — offering AI governance tools for law firms, not general consulting
3. **Insurance brokers** — flagging AI governance as a coverage issue, not providing advisory services
4. **Generic AI policy template providers** — one-size-fits-all templates that don't address boutique risk profiles
5. **Regulatory consultants** — focused on large-institution compliance, not small boutique operations

### What Exists

#### Large Advisory Firms (Big 4, Major Strategy Consultancies)
- **Positioning**: Comprehensive AI governance frameworks, multi-jurisdiction compliance, enterprise-grade documentation
- **Strength**: Brand credibility, comprehensive coverage, regulatory relationships
- **Weakness for Boutique ICP**: Price point (CHF 15,000–50,000+ for initial engagement), institutional orientation, not proportionate to boutique scale, not Geneva-specialist
- **Gap**: No established offering specifically for2–50 person Geneva consulting boutiques at proportionate price

#### Legal Tech Platforms (Clio, Iron Mountain, etc.)
- **Positioning**: AI governance tooling for law firms, practice management, document management
- **Strength**: Practice management integration, attorney-client privilege considerations built in
- **Weakness for Boutique ICP**: Oriented toward law firms, not general consulting; different risk profile; different client base
- **Gap**: Not applicable to general consulting boutiques (non-legal)

#### Insurance Brokers (Large Brokers)
- **Positioning**: AI governance as insurance risk factor, carrier attestation guidance
- **Strength**: Access to carrier attestation forms, understanding of coverage gaps
- **Weakness for Boutique ICP**: Not advisory in the methodology sense; flag problems but don't resolve them; broker liability concerns limit how much guidance they provide
- **Gap**: No structured advisory service to help boutiques actually complete carrier attestations accurately

#### Generic AI Policy Template Providers
- **Positioning**: Off-the-shelf AI governance policies, ISO42001 alignment, AI usage policies
- **Strength**: Low cost, fast delivery, comprehensive coverage
- **Weakness for Boutique ICP**: Templates designed for larger organizations; not proportionate; don't address sub-consultant delegation, carrier attestation accuracy, client RFP gates, or personal liability
- **Gap**: No template designed for small Geneva consulting boutique risk profile

#### Regulatory Consultants (Compliance Boutiques)
- **Positioning**: Regulatory compliance advisory, nFADP/GDPR compliance, EU AI Act preparation
- **Strength**: Regulatory expertise, documentation frameworks
- **Weakness for Boutique ICP**: Not consulting-sector-specific; not Geneva-focused; not proportionate to small boutique operations; sales cycle too long for boutique urgency
- **Gap**: No regulatory consultant offering specifically designed for small Geneva consulting boutique immediate needs (client questionnaire response, carrier attestation accuracy, proposal methodology defensibility)

### What's Missing (C307 NET-NEW White Spaces)

#### White Space 1: Client RFP AI Response Service
**What it is**: A structured service to help Geneva consulting boutiques respond to client AI governance questionnaires embedded in RFPs. Not a generic policy — a client-specific response document that documents the boutique's actual practice against the client's specific criteria.

**Why it's missing**: Client RFP AI gates are new. Most advisory providers are not offering client-specific AI questionnaire response services. The boutiques that can respond to these questionnaires quickly and professionally will win mandates that others lose.

**Service design**:3–5 day sprint to review client's RFP criteria, document boutique's actual AI practice, produce response document, review for accuracy and defensibility. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction**: This is a pre-pitch intervention, not a post-problem remediation. The value is commercial — winning the mandate — not just compliant.

#### White Space 2: Carrier Attestation Accuracy Review
**What it is**: A structured review of what a managing partner actually certified when signing the carrier attestation, compared against actual practice. Produces a gap analysis, a corrected attestation narrative, and a documentation improvement plan.

**Why it's missing**: Insurance brokers flag the risk but don't provide the review service. Legal consultants charge too much for the proportionate scope. The boutique is left with a signed attestation and no understanding of the gap between what was signed and what exists.

**Service design**: 2–3 day review engagement. Document current AI practice. Compare against carrier attestation language. Identify misrepresentation risk. Produce corrected documentation and attestation narrative. CHF 2,200–3,500.

**Competitive distinction**: The attestation accuracy review is a distinct service from general AI governance advisory. It is specifically about the document the managing partner signed and the personal exposure it creates.

#### White Space 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework
**What it is**: A lightweight documentation framework specifically designed for Geneva consulting boutiques with1–3 sub-consultants. Addresses the accountability chain from managing partner to sub-consultant AI use. Produces a delegation register, a use log, and a client disclosure protocol.

**Why it's missing**: Enterprise AI governance platforms are not designed for sub-consultant delegation scenarios. Legal tech platforms are for law firms. The boutique needs a proportionate framework that fits their operational reality.

**Service design**: 1-day workshop + documentation production. Map sub-consultant relationships. Document AI tool use per engagement. Create delegation register template. Produce client disclosure protocol. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction**: No competitor is offering a sub-consultant AI delegation framework specifically designed for small Geneva consulting boutiques. This is a structurally distinct risk that larger advisory firms don't address.

#### White Space 4: Proposal Methodology Defensibility Package
**What it is**: A structured documentation package that makes proposal methodology defensible under professional liability standards. Addresses both disclosure requirements and traceability requirements. Produces a methodology documentation template, a disclosure checklist, and a defensibility review process.

**Why it's missing**: Generic AI governance frameworks don't address proposal methodology specifically. The professional liability exposure from AI-assisted recommendations is not addressed by templates designed for AI tool deployment — it's addressed by methodology documentation practices.

**Service design**: 2-day engagement. Review current proposal methodology. Document AI-assisted components. Produce methodology documentation template. Create defensibility review checklist. Bilingual EN/FR.

**Competitive distinction**: No competitor is offering a proposal methodology defensibility service specifically for consulting boutiques. The service is distinct from general AI governance advisory because it is specifically about the proposal document and the professional liability exposure it creates.

#### White Space 5: Personal nFADP Article 52 Exposure Assessment
**What it is**: A structured assessment of managing partner personal exposure under nFADP Article 52. Produces a personal liability exposure report, a documentation adequacy assessment, and a personal asset protection strategy. Distinct from corporate compliance assessment.

**Why it's missing**: Most nFADP advisory services are oriented toward corporate compliance officers, not managing partners with personal liability exposure. The personal asset dimension is distinct and requires separate advisory attention.

**Service design**: 1-day assessment engagement. Review current AI practice. Assess personal liability exposure under nFADP Article 52. Review insurance coverage for personal fines. Produce personal exposure report and documentation strategy. CHF 2,200–3,500.

**Competitive distinction**: No competitor is offering a personal nFADP Article 52 exposure assessment specifically for managing partners of small consulting boutiques. This is distinct from corporate compliance advisory because the liability is personal, not institutional.

#### White Space 6: Proportionate AI Governance Retainer for Geneva Consulting Boutiques
**What it is**: An ongoing advisory retainer designed specifically for small Geneva consulting boutiques. Provides proportionate, practical AI governance support on an as-needed basis. Bilingual EN/FR. Geneva-based. Operationally concrete, not abstractly strategic.

**Why it's missing**: The retainer model for AI governance advisory doesn't exist at the small boutique tier. Large advisory firms offer retainers for large clients. Legal tech platforms offer subscriptions for law firms. But a proportionate, Geneva-based, bilingual retainer for small consulting boutiques — covering client questionnaire response, carrier attestation accuracy, sub-consultant delegation, and methodology defensibility — is not available.

**Service design**: Monthly retainer,4–6 hours/month. Priority access. Bilingual EN/FR. Geneva-based. Covers all Phase 03 service components on an ongoing basis. CHF 1,500–2,500/month.

**Competitive distinction**: No competitor is offering a proportionate monthly retainer for small Geneva consulting boutiques. The retainer model creates durable revenue and positions DigitalCoa.ch as the ongoing AI governance partner for this ICP.

## Structural Moats

1. **Geneva-specific market knowledge**: Understanding of Geneva consulting boutique operations, client base, peer networks, and commercial dynamics
2. **Bilingual EN/FR capability**: Client communications in both languages without translation overhead
3. **Proportionate service design**: Services designed for 2–50 person firms, not scaled-down enterprise frameworks
4. **Personal liability focus**: Managing partner personal exposure addressed directly, not buried in corporate compliance frameworks
5. **Sequenced entry logic**: The service sequence (client questionnaire → carrier attestation → methodology defensibility → sub-consultant delegation → personal liability) is itself a competitive asset — it shows the ICP that the advisor understands their operational reality

## Competitive Vulnerability
The primary vulnerability is large advisory firms entering the boutique tier with lower-priced standardized offerings. The mitigation is the Geneva-specific market knowledge, the bilingual capability, and the proportionate service design — none of which large firms can deliver at small boutique scale.
