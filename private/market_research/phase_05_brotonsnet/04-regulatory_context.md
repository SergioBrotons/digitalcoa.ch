# 04 — Regulatory Context: Brotons.net (Content/Publishing Platform)

## Key Regulatory Framework for Publishing

### Swiss Media/Legal Considerations
| Area | Regulation | Impact on Brotons.net |
|---|---|---|
| Defamation law | Swiss Civil Code Art. 28ff; Criminal Code Art. 173-174 | Content must avoid defamatory claims; fact-based analysis protected |
| Copyright | Swiss Copyright Act (URG) | Proper attribution; no reproduction of proprietary content |
| Data protection | nFADP | Newsletter subscriber data; analytics compliance |
| Advertising/marketing | Federal Act against Unfair Competition (UWG) | Clear distinction between content and commercial promotion |
| AI-generated content | Emerging regulatory discussion | Disclosures may be required for AI-assisted content |

### Platform-Specific Considerations
- **Newsletter regulations:** Anti-spam rules for commercial email in Switzerland (UWG + Federal Act on Telecommunications)
- **Cookie/analytics:** Swiss cookie consent requirements for website analytics
- **Accessibility:** No specific Swiss accessibility mandate for private websites (but EU EAA may apply for EU-facing sites)
- **Hosting/data:** GDPR applies if serving EU audiences; nFADP applies for Swiss readers

## Publishing Discipline (Per SOUL.md)
- Publish only when content is: on-brand, coherent, useful, structurally sound, metadata-complete, internally linked
- Do not publish when: strategically ambiguous, facts weak, voice off-brand, CTA confused, page duplicates existing material
- Quality outranks quantity
