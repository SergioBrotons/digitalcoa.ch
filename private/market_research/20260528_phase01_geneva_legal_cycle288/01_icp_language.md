## Key Language Observations

1. **"Personally accountable"** — This is the core emotional state. They are not worried about the firm. They are worried about themselves, personally, as the responsible attorney.

2. **"Defensible"** — Not "optimal" or "best practice." They want to know what they can defend to the bar, to the carrier, to the client.

3. **"I don't know what I don't know"** — There is significant anxiety about gaps in knowledge, not just gaps in tools.

4. **"Small firm"** — The self-identification as a small firm is load-bearing. Enterprise frameworks don't resonate. They want solutions that acknowledge their scale.

5. **"Responsible late adopters"** — They want to use AI, but on their own terms — carefully, defensibly, at a pace they can control.

6. **Carrier-driven urgency** — The carrier attestation question is a new and concrete trigger. This is more immediate than philosophical concerns about the AI Convention.

7. **SVA/Geneva Bar gap** — They recognize that national SBA guidelines exist but feel a gap in Geneva-specific guidance. The cantonal dimension matters.
