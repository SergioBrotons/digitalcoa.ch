## Landscape Overview

The market for AI governance solutions targeting small Swiss law firms is nascent and fragmented. Existing players fall into several categories: general legal tech vendors who have added AI modules, general data protection consultants, international legal AI platforms, Swiss legal associations providing guidance, and managed services providers. None are specifically serving the 2–5 attorney Geneva boutique with an integrated, practitioner-facing AI governance framework.

---

## Category 1: General Legal Tech Platforms (International)

**Examples:** Clio, Ironclad, Filevine, LawDroid, Kira Systems

**What they offer:**
- Contract lifecycle management with AI-assisted drafting and review
- Document automation and management
- AI-powered legal research tools

**Gap for Geneva small firms:**
- These platforms are designed for larger firms or US-centric workflows
- Pricing is calibrated to larger organizations
- No specific Swiss professional obligations module (nFADP Art. 9, BGFA Art. 13)
- No carrier attestation support
- No French/Geneva market localization
- Vendors are not positioned as governance advisors — they are productivity tools

**What they're missing:** The compliance and governance layer. They help attorneys do more with AI, but they don't help them understand what they're doing, why it's defensible, or how to demonstrate that to a carrier or bar.

---

## Category 2: Swiss Legal Associations (SBA / SVA)

**What they offer:**
- SBA Guidelines on AI Use (June 2024) — principles for responsible AI use
- SVA events and continuing education on AI topics
- University of Geneva courses on AI and privacy (2 ECTS micro-credential)

**Gap for Geneva small firms:**
- Guidelines are principles-based, not operationally implementable
- No small-firm-specific governance toolkit
- No template documentation (DPAs, AI registers, internal policies)
- Educational courses are time-intensive (14 hours + 40 hours assignment for micro-credential)
- The SVA has not published Geneva-specific operational guidance

**What they're missing:** Implementation layer. The SBA and SVA provide the framework of what should happen but not the operational tools for a 3-attorney practice to actually implement it.

**Gap for Geneva small firms:**

## What's Missing in the Market — The Gap Analysis

### Gap 1: No Small-Firm-Specific AI Governance Framework

There is no available framework specifically designed for a 2–5 attorney Geneva law firm to implement AI governance in a practical, operationally feasible way. What exists:
- 50-page policy templates from international compliance vendors
- Generic data protection documentation
- SBA principles with no implementation pathway

What is needed:
- A compact, practical framework that acknowledges the resource constraints of a small practice
- Something a managing partner can implement without a compliance team
- A framework that maps to carrier attestation requirements

### Gap 2: No Swiss Law Firm AI Vendor Due Diligence Tool

No tool or template exists that is calibrated to evaluate AI vendors specifically for Swiss law firm use. What attorneys need:
- A due diligence questionnaire specifically for AI vendors covering training data, hosting, sub-processing, DPA requirements
- A decision framework for evaluating Swiss-hosted vs. EU-hosted vs. US-hosted AI tools
- A training data clause review guide for AI vendor contracts

### Gap 3: No AI Register / Tool Inventory Solution for Law Firms

No one has offered small Swiss law firms a practical AI register — a documented inventory of AI tools in use, what they're used for, what data they access, and what safeguards are in place. This is the missing document for:
- Carrier attestation
- Bar compliance demonstration
- Internal governance

### Gap 4: No Carrier Attestation Preparation Service

Insurance brokers and carriers are asking AI attestation questions but no one is providing preparation services to small law firms. The need:
- Attestation readiness review
- Gap identification against carrier requirements
- Documentation package to support attestation
- Ongoing maintenance of attestation materials

### Gap 5: No Geneva-Cantonal AI Governance Guidance

The SBA provides national-level guidance. No Geneva-specific (SVA) operational guidance exists for small firms. The cantonal dimension matters because:
- Geneva bar obligations may have cantonal specificities
- Geneva attorneys identify as Geneva practitioners, not just Swiss practitioners
- A Geneva-specific resource would have higher credibility and resonance

### Gap 6: No French-Language AI Governance Resources for Swiss Law Firms

The market has almost no French-language resources for Swiss attorneys on AI governance. Most AI governance content is in English or German. Geneva is French-speaking. A French-language resource from a credible Swiss source would have significant differentiation value.

## Competitive Positioning Observations

1. **The market is offering principles without implementation.** The SBA guidelines are the clearest example: valuable but not actionable at small firm scale.

2. **No one is occupying the "small firm AI governance" position.** This is an open positioning space. The international platforms are too large, the general consultants are too generic, the legal associations are too high-level.

3. **The carrier attestation gap is a concrete entry point.** No competitor is specifically addressing the carrier attestation question as a lead-in to broader AI governance. This could be a specific wedge.

4. **French-language gap is significant.** A credible French-language AI governance resource for Geneva attorneys would be differentiated even if the content exists in English elsewhere.

5. **The "responsible late adopter" identity is underserved.** Most AI content in the legal market targets either early adopters (enthusiastic about AI) or AI refusers (skeptical). The middle identity — disciplined, defensible, scale-aware adoption — is not being served by any competitor.

---

## Net New Finding (C288 Phase 01)

**The Geneva small law firm AI governance market has a specific structural gap:** no provider is offering a practitioner-grade, operationally feasible, Swiss-calibrated AI governance framework designed for the 2–5 attorney boutique. All existing players either provide principles without implementation (SBA), generic compliance without law firm context (privacy consultants), or productivity tools without governance (legal tech vendors). The market position to occupy is: **the practical AI governance partner for small Swiss law firms, bridging the gap between SBA principles and daily practice.**
