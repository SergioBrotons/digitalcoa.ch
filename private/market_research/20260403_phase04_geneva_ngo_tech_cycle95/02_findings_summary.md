## F1: Anthropic Claude Code Leak — AI Security Implications for Swiss Companies
**Source:** NZZ, April 3, 2026
**Signal:** MEDIUM-HIGH — AI security, Geneva tech sector, RegTech

Anthropic accidentally released the source code of its Claude Code AI agent (which writes computer programs automatically). The leak revealed the "harness" — the orchestration layer that manages file access, software calls, and model routing for AI agents. Key implications:

- Claude Code projected to generate USD 2.5B revenue in 2026 (~15% of Anthropic's total)
- The leak spread rapidly; a version rewritten via OpenAI Codex in a different programming language went viral on GitHub (50,000 approvals in <2 hours, 100,000+ forks)
- Anthropic filed copyright lawsuits; some workarounds found
- The leak revealed that AI agents are collecting significant user interaction data (undurchsichtiges Sammeln von Nutzerdaten)

For Geneva tech/NGO sector:
- Swiss companies using AI coding agents (Claude Code, Copilot, etc.) face new data security questions — what data are these agents collecting, where does it go, is it sanctioned-asset-adjacent?
- The leak revealed that AI agent "harnesses" often involve systematic data collection — compliance teams need to assess AI tool usage under Swiss data protection (nDSG) and potentially under sanctions screening
- Geneva-based RegTech/compliance tech firms have an opportunity: AI tool compliance auditing — assessing whether company-approved AI tools are creating data exposure
- Geneva NGOs working on digital rights should note: the "undurchsichtige Datensammlung" (opaque data collection) by AI agents is a privacy/security concern

## F2: US Section 301 Industrial Overcapacity Investigation — Swiss Precision Manufacturing at Risk
**Source:** NZZ (Bundesrat press release), Phase 02 findings
**Signal:** HIGH — Swiss precision manufacturing, Geneva tech sector

The US Section 301 investigation targets Swiss industrial overcapacity. This has direct implications for Geneva's precision manufacturing sector:
- Swiss watchmaking (Geneva and Jura)
- Swiss medical devices
- Swiss precision engineering components
- Swiss industrial equipment

If the US determines Swiss industrial capacity constitutes "unfair" competition due to state support or structural advantages, tariffs could follow. Geneva manufacturing associations (APRES-GE, etc.) would be central to any industry response.

For Geneva tech/NGO angle:
- Geneva think tanks and industry associations need to engage with the Section 301 process proactively
- Geneva trade policy NGOs could play a role in framing Switzerland's position
- The overcapacity narrative connects to the BASF China bet: European companies going to China while US investigates them for overcapacity → the global industrial policy paradox

## F3: BASF EUR 8.7B China Bet — Swiss Chemical Sector Positioning
**Source:** NZZ, April 3, 2026
**Signal:** MEDIUM — Geneva industrial consulting, Swiss chemical sector

BASF opened its Zhanjiang plant in southern China — EUR 8.7B investment, the largest single site investment in BASF history. Key facts:
- Reduced from original EUR 10B plan due to market conditions
- 18 facilities, 32 production lines, 1M tonnes/year ethylene capacity
- China = 50% of global chemical market
- BASF China revenue: EUR 8.2B (2025) down from EUR 8.6B (2024)
- China auto sales -23.1% in Jan-Feb 2026 after subsidy cuts
- China's overall investment fell -3.8% in 2025 (first decline in modern history)
- China real estate crisis continues to dampen demand

Swiss connection:
- Clariant (Swiss competitor) also suffering from China weakness
- Geneva-based industrial consultancies advising on China strategy: this is a cautionary data point
- Swiss precision manufacturing companies with China exposure face similar market pressure
- The BASF bet is also a bet on 3-4 year recovery — a view Geneva companies with China patience may share

## F5: Swiss Precision Manufacturing — Dual Exposure: US Tariffs + EU Carbon Border Adjustment
**Source:** NZZ, Phase 02 findings, Phase 03 findings
**Signal:** MEDIUM-HIGH — Geneva precision manufacturing, industrial policy

Swiss precision manufacturers face a dual squeeze:
1. **US:** Section 301 industrial overcapacity investigation → potential tariffs
2. **EU:** Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) → carbon cost on imports, including from Switzerland (which is linked to EU ETS but not fully aligned)

Geneva precision manufacturers (watchmaking, medtech, specialty components) face compliance costs on both fronts simultaneously. The intersection of US trade policy and EU climate policy creates a specific burden for export-oriented Swiss manufacturers.

NGO angle: Geneva-based industry associations (GAMETH, VISOR, industry groups) and chambers of commerce are the natural collective response vehicles. Geneva think tanks could provide policy research support.
