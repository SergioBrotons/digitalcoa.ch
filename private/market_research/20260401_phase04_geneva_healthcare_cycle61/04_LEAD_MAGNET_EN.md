# The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework

## Swissdamed Edition — Q2 2026

---

### Two Deadlines. Ninety-One Days. One Practice.

Swissdamed enforcement is July 1, 2026 — ~91 days from now.

EU AI Act Annex III conformity assessments for high-risk healthcare AI are August 2, 2026 — ~4 months from now.

Most Geneva small medical practices have not completed either.

This framework helps you assess your dual compliance exposure and prioritize actions — starting with the most urgent deadline.

---

### Part 1: Does Swissdamed Apply to Your Practice?

**Three questions. Answer yes to any and Swissdamed applies.**

1. Does your practice process health-related personal data of patients? (Clinical records, diagnosis, treatment history, billing data for medical services)
2. Does your practice use any automated data processing systems — including scheduling, billing, clinical note systems, or AI-assisted tools — that process patient data?
3. Does your practice work with external service providers (labs, imaging centers, insurance companies, IT vendors) who process patient data on your behalf?

If yes to any of the above: Swissdamed applies to your practice. The July 1, 2026 enforcement date is not a preparation deadline — it is an enforcement date.

---

### Part 2: Swissdamed Compliance Gap Assessment

**Are you ready for July 1, 2026?**

- [ ] Have you conducted a documented FADP gap assessment for your practice's data processing activities?
- [ ] Do you have updated privacy notices for patients (covering all data processing purposes, AI tools, and external data processors)?
- [ ] Have you assessed which AI tools your practice uses for clinical or administrative purposes and documented their data processing characteristics?
- [ ] Do you have a data breach notification procedure in place (required under both FADP and Swissdamed)?
- [ ] Have you assessed your external service providers (labs, imaging, IT) for their Swissdamed compliance?
- [ ] Do you have documented procedures for patient rights requests (access, correction, deletion)?
- [ ] Have you conducted a data minimization review — eliminating data that is no longer necessary for its original purpose?

**If you answered no to two or more of the above:** You are not ready for Swissdamed enforcement. The next 91 days require active remediation, not continued preparation assessment.

### Part 4: EU AI Act Annex III Exposure

**Does your AI diagnostic or clinical support tool face EU AI Act requirements?**

The EU AI Act Annex III classifies AI systems used in healthcare as high-risk, including:
- AI systems intended to diagnose or screen for diseases
- AI systems used in clinical decision support
- AI systems used for patient monitoring in high-acuity settings

**Does this apply to your practice if you serve EU patients?**

Answer yes if:
- You serve patients resident in EU countries
- Your AI tool was developed or is provided by an EU-based vendor
- The output of your AI tool is used in EU clinical contexts

If yes to any of the above: your AI tool may qualify as high-risk AI under EU AI Act Annex III. The August 2, 2026 conformity assessment deadline applies.

**Actions:**
- [ ] Identify which of your AI clinical tools could qualify as Annex III high-risk AI
- [ ] Assess whether your EU-based AI vendor has begun their conformity assessment process
- [ ] Document your AI tool usage and EU patient exposure for your next compliance review

---

### Part 5: International Patients and Data Sovereignty

**Geneva practices serving HNW and UHNW international patients face additional considerations.**

- [ ] Do your international patients (EU, US, Middle East, Asia) have expectations about where their health data is stored and processed?
- [ ] Do your AI tools store patient data on servers outside Switzerland?
- [ ] Do you have consent frameworks that address cross-border data transfer for international patients?
- [ ] Does your practice have data sovereignty documentation that you can share with international patients upon request?

Swiss institutional trust is confirmed "excellent" internationally (Federal Council, April 1, 2026). For international HNW patients selecting a Geneva practice, Swiss data residency and sovereignty are tangible trust signals.

### Part 6: What to Do — 30 / 60 / 90 Days

**Next 30 days (before May 1, 2026):**
1. Complete your Swissdamed gap assessment (Part 2 above). Identify your two most critical gaps.
2. Complete your AI tool inventory (Part 3 above). Document every AI tool and its data processing characteristics.
3. Notify your team of the July 1 enforcement date and your top remediation priorities.

**Next 60 days (before June 1, 2026):**
1. Remediate your two most critical Swissdamed gaps (likely: patient privacy notices + AI tool documentation).
2. Assess your EU AI Act Annex III exposure for any AI diagnostic or clinical support tools (Part 4 above).
3. Review external service provider agreements for Swissdamed compliance.

**Next 90 days (before July 1, 2026):**
1. Complete Swissdamed remediation. Ensure all patient privacy notices are updated and accessible.
2. Document your Swissdamed compliance file — FADP gap assessment, AI tool inventory, data breach procedure, patient rights procedure.
3. If serving EU patients with AI diagnostic tools: assess EU AI Act conformity timeline with your vendor.
