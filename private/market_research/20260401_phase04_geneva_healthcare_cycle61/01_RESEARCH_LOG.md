### 3. EU AI Act Healthcare Sector — Extraterritorial Implications Sharper (NET-NEW C61 Update)
**Source:** Lenz & Staehelin (via Phase 01 C60 carry-forward); Federal Council April 1, 2026 synthesis

EU AI Act Annex III high-risk AI includes AI systems used in medical devices and clinical decision support. Geneva medical practices serving EU patients or using AI tools with EU-developed components face compounded extraterritorial exposure: FADP Art. 21 + Swissdamed + Federal AI Ordinance + EU AI Act Annex III simultaneously.

**Implication (NET-NEW C61 — sharper framing):** The EU AI Act healthcare extraterritorial issue is not abstract. Any Geneva practice using AI diagnostic tools, AI-powered imaging analysis, AI clinical decision support, or AI patient monitoring — particularly if serving EU residents or using EU-sourced AI tools — is potentially within scope of EU AI Act Annex III high-risk AI. The August 2, 2026 conformity assessment deadline applies to these systems. Geneva practices need to assess: which of their AI tools could be classified as Annex III high-risk AI?
