# 02 — Market Signals
## Geneva Finance — C131

## Geneva Insurance and Reinsurance Market

**Reinsurance tied assets authorization wave (Jan 1 2026):**
FINMA requiring new authorization applications for reinsurers' shares of provisions for tied assets from January 1, 2026. This affects Geneva life insurers with reinsurance arrangements, reinsurance intermediaries, and captive reinsurance structures. The small insurers regime (updated January 31, 2025) creates a bifurcated landscape: larger insurers face full SST reporting; qualifying small insurers have reduced requirements but must maintain risk transparency.

**IFRS 17 decision-useful capital data:**
Swiss dual reporters (primarily insurance groups with international presence) are now required to produce IFRS 17-compliant measurements. The internal capital allocation implications are significant: finance functions can now generate portfolio-level capital cost data that was not available under IFRS 4. Geneva CFO offices need support translating actuarial outputs into strategic resource allocation decisions.

## Swiss Financial Infrastructure

**SIC5 instant payment migration:**
SIX Group is implementing the next generation of Swiss payment infrastructure (SIC5), introducing real-time 24/7 settlement capability and ISO 20022 messaging standards. Geneva financial institutions with treasury functions and payment operations need to update core banking systems, correspondent banking interfaces, and cash management frameworks. The EUR/CHF and USD/CHF cross-border payment flows require reconciliation with EU PSD2-equivalent instant payment standards.

**Private credit / direct lending market:**
CHF-denominated direct lending is emerging as a institutional allocation category in Switzerland. Geneva pension funds, insurance groups, and family offices are allocating to direct lending funds seeking yield pickup over Swiss government bonds. Fund formation is occurring under both FinMIA (regulated) and AIFM (non-Swiss fund distribution) frameworks. The market is nascent relative to EU/US private credit but growing as Swiss institutional investors diversify from traditional fixed income.

## Climate Risk and Disclosure

**TCFD/ISSB Swiss adoption 2026:**
Federal Council adopting ISSB-based climate disclosure standards for Swiss listed companies and large foundations. FINMA expected to incorporate climate risk disclosure expectations into its supervisory framework for regulated institutions (banks, asset managers, insurance). Geneva institutional investors face dual pressures: their own disclosure obligations and portfolio-level Scope 3 financed emissions reporting for investee companies subject to TCFD requirements. The timeline for mandatory reporting is financial year 2026, creating an advisory window through 2026.

## Operational Risk and ICT

**FINMA ICT risk circular — 2025/2026 examination priority:**
FINMA's updated ICT risk expectations focus on: third-party risk management (cloud outsourcing, critical suppliers), operational resilience testing, and cyber incident reporting. Geneva financial institutions with international supplier networks and complex technology stacks are particularly exposed. The FINMA Guidance 01/2026 on crypto custody reinforces ICT risk dimensions for institutions with digital asset exposure.

**Key statistics:**
- FINMA identified ICT risk as standing supervisory priority 2025-2026
- Geneva financial centre: ~380 FINMA-supervised institutions (banks, asset managers, insurers)
- Swiss insurance sector: ~90 supervised insurers; ~25 reinsurers
- SIX Group processing ~150 million payments annually (pre-SIC5)
