# 04 — Regulatory Context
## Geneva Finance — C131

## FINMA Reinsurance Tied Assets Authorization

**Legal basis:** Articles 70 ff. Insurance Supervision Ordinance (ISO), revised 2023
**Key date:** January 1, 2026 — new FINMA authorization applications required for reinsurers' shares of provisions for tied assets (new contracts and changes to existing relationships)
**Applicability:** Geneva life insurers, reinsurance intermediaries, captive reinsurance structures (particularly in Geneva commodity trading groups)
**Supervisor:** FINMA
**Status:** Active compliance window open now — advisory mandates being issued

## IFRS 17 Insurance Contracts (Swiss Adoption)

**Legal basis:** Swiss law adoption of IFRS 17 for dual reporters; Swiss Professional Association of Insurance (SVV) guidance
**Key requirements:** Current fulfillment value measurement, portfolio-level aggregation, separate measurement of acquisition cash flows
**Internal capital allocation:** IFRS 17 produces risk-adjusted return data at portfolio level — decision-useful for capital allocation but requires internal reporting framework development
**Intersection with Swiss SST:** FINMA requires Swiss Solvency Test (SST) calculation alongside IFRS 17 — dual reporting creates complexity for Geneva insurance groups
**Status:** IFRS 17 in force for Swiss dual reporters; internal capital allocation frameworks still maturing

## SIC5 and ISO 20022 Migration

**Legal basis:** National Bank of Switzerland (SNB) payment system regulations; FINMA payment services oversight
**Key requirements:** Real-time 24/7 settlement capability, ISO 20022 messaging standard adoption, interoperability with EU instant payments
**Timeline:** SIC5 implementation in progress (2025-2026); full migration expected 2026
**Applicability:** All Swiss financial institutions with payment operations, treasury functions, and correspondent banking relationships
**Cross-border dimension:** EU PSD2-equivalent instant payment standards need reconciliation with Swiss SIC5; EUR/CHF and USD/CHF corridors particularly affected
**Status:** Active migration phase — advisory mandates available for treasury updates and system migration planning

## Swiss TCFD/ISSB Climate Disclosure Adoption

**Legal basis:** Federal Council decision adopting ISSB-based climate disclosure standards (2025-2026); Swiss Code of Obligations amendments for listed company disclosure
**Key requirements:** TCFD-aligned climate risk disclosure (governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets); Scope 1, 2, and material Scope 3 emissions; portfolio climate risk for financial institutions
**Timeline:** Federal Council adoption expected 2026; mandatory reporting likely financial year 2026 or 2027
**Applicability:** Swiss listed companies ( SIX-listed), large foundations, Geneva pension funds (as institutional investors with disclosure obligations), FINMA-supervised institutions (FINMA climate risk expectations)
**FINMA dimension:** FINMA expected to incorporate TCFD-aligned climate risk disclosure expectations into supervisory framework for banks, asset managers, and insurers
**Status:** Legislative process 2025-2026; advisory window now open for preparatory work

## FINMA ICT Risk Circular

**Legal basis:** FINMA circulars on operational risk and ICT risk; DORA soft adoption for EU-facing Swiss institutions (effective January 2025)
**Key requirements:** Third-party risk management (especially cloud outsourcing and critical technology suppliers), operational resilience testing, cyber incident reporting, business continuity planning
**FINMA examination priority:** ICT risk is standing priority for FINMA examinations 2025-2026
**FINMA Guidance 01/2026:** Crypto custody ICT risk dimensions (operational resilience for custody platforms)
**Applicability:** All FINMA-supervised institutions in Geneva (~380 entities)
**Status:** Active examination priority — gap assessments and framework upgrades underway

## Swiss Private Credit / Direct Lending Regulatory Perimeter

**Legal basis:** Financial Market Infrastructure Act (FinMIA); Alternative Investment Fund Managers Act (AIFM) for non-Swiss fund distribution; Swiss Collective Investment Schemes Act (CISA)
**Key classification questions:** When does direct lending fund require FinMIA authorization? What is the threshold for AIFM registration? When is a fund unregulated (family office structure)?
**Current status:** Regulatory perimeter being tested; CHF-denominated direct lending funds forming under various structures
**Geneva dimension:** Geneva institutional investors allocating to direct lending need regulatory classification advisory; fund managers need FinMIA/AIFM navigation
