## NET-NEW Findings This Session (C131 Phase 02)

1. **FINMA reinsurance tied assets authorization wave — Jan 1 2026 hard deadline** — FINMA authorization of reinsurers' shares of provisions for tied assets requires new applications from January 1, 2026, for new reinsurance contracts or changes to existing relationships. Geneva life insurers and reinsurance intermediaries with existing reinsurance arrangements face a compliance window to submit updated authorization applications to FINMA. The small insurers regime (updated January 31, 2025) offers reduced SST reporting burden for qualifying firms but requires risk position transparency maintenance. This creates immediate advisory demand for Geneva insurance groups, reinsurance intermediaries, and captive reinsurance structures in commodity trading houses.

2. **Swiss instant payment infrastructure — SIC5 migration and ISO 20022 creating treasury advisory demand** — The Swiss payment infrastructure is undergoing migration to real-time instant payments (SIC5) with ISO 20022 messaging standards. Geneva banks, commodity trading houses with treasury functions, and corporate treasuries need to update payment systems, correspondent banking relationships, and cash management frameworks. SIX Group is implementing the infrastructure changes. Geneva institutions with cross-border payment flows (particularly EUR/CHF and USD/CHF) need to reconcile Swiss instant payment capability with EU instant payment standards under PSD2-equivalent frameworks. Advisory demand spans system migration planning, treasury policy updates, and liquidity management implications of 24/7 real-time settlement.

3. **Swiss private credit / direct lending fund formation — CHF-denominated market maturing** — A domestic Swiss private credit and direct lending market is emerging as an alternative to traditional bank credit. CHF-denominated direct lending funds are forming under Swiss regulatory frameworks (FinMIA or AIFM), attracting Geneva institutional investors (pension funds, insurance groups, family offices) seeking yield premium over Swiss government bonds. The market size is still small relative to EU/US private credit but growing. Advisory opportunities include: fund formation structuring, investor allocation strategy for Geneva institutions, credit risk assessment frameworks for direct lending portfolios, and regulatory classification (FinMIA vs. AIFM vs. unregulated) decisions. Geneva's commodity trading houses are also active borrowers in this market.

4. **Swiss TCFD/ISSB climate disclosure adoption — Federal Council 2026 timeline creating listed company advisory wave** — Switzerland's Federal Council is adopting ISSB-based climate disclosure standards (aligned with EU CSRD approach but Swiss-native), with implementation expected for listed companies and large foundations from financial year 2026. FINMA is expected to incorporate climate risk disclosure expectations into its supervisory framework for regulated financial institutions. Geneva institutional investors managing assets for institutions subject to these disclosures need TCFD-aligned portfolio climate risk disclosure frameworks. This creates advisory demand in: climate risk quantification methodology, Scope 3 financed emissions inventory, TCFD-aligned reporting framework design, and investor portfolio climate stress testing. Geneva asset managers and pension funds with listed equity or corporate bond portfolios are directly in scope.

5. **IFRS 17 insurance contracts — decision-useful capital allocation data creating finance function advisory demand** — IFRS 17 (Swiss adoption for dual reporters) requires insurance companies to measure and report insurance contract liabilities using current fulfillment values rather than historical cost. For Geneva-based insurance groups and reinsurance intermediaries, IFRS 17 produces more decision-useful internal capital allocation data at the portfolio level. Finance functions need to develop new internal reporting frameworks linking IFRS 17 actuarial outputs to capital allocation decisions, performance measurement, and board risk reporting. The intersection of IFRS 17 with Swiss SST (Swiss Solvency Test) capital calculations requires specialized advisory expertise. Geneva CFO and CRO offices need support in translating IFRS 17 outputs into strategic capital decisions.

6. **FINMA ICT risk circular 2025/2026 — operational resilience third-party risk examination priority** — FINMA updated its ICT risk circular expectations in 2025-2026, emphasizing third-party risk management (particularly cloud outsourcing and critical technology suppliers), operational resilience testing, and cyber incident reporting. Geneva financial institutions (banks, asset managers, insurance groups) are subject to FINMA examination priorities that include ICT risk as a standing priority. The FINMA Guidance 01/2026 on crypto custody also has ICT risk dimensions (operational resilience for custody platforms). Advisory demand includes: ICT risk framework gap assessment vs. FINMA expectations, third-party risk register development for Geneva institutions with international supplier networks, cyber incident response plan development, and operational resilience testing (including cross-border ICT failure scenarios relevant to Geneva's international financial infrastructure).
