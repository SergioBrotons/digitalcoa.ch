# 06 — Executive Summary
## Phase 02 Geneva Finance — C131

**Market Opportunity:**
Geneva financial institutions are managing a portfolio of regulatory deadlines spanning insurance (reinsurance tied assets Jan 1 2026, IFRS 17 capital allocation), payments (SIC5 instant payment migration), climate disclosure (TCFD/ISSB adoption 2026), and operational risk (FINMA ICT circular examination priority). These deadlines are largely independent of the Basel IV/FAIR work covered in C130, creating a complementary advisory surface area for Geneva CFO, CRO, and compliance offices. Geneva's concentration of life insurers, reinsurance intermediaries, commodity trading treasuries, and institutional investors means multiple client types are affected simultaneously.

**Key Findings C131:**

1. **FINMA reinsurance tied assets authorization wave (Jan 1 2026)** — New FINMA authorization applications required for reinsurers' shares of provisions for tied assets from January 1 2026. Geneva life insurers, reinsurance intermediaries, and captive reinsurance structures face a hard compliance deadline. Independent advisory without Big 4 audit conflict is available for this narrow, time-sensitive niche.

2. **SIC5 instant payment migration + ISO 20022 treasury advisory** — Swiss payment infrastructure migration to real-time 24/7 settlement with ISO 20022 messaging is creating treasury management advisory demand across Geneva banks, commodity trading treasuries, and corporate treasury functions. Cross-border EUR/CHF and USD/CHF reconciliation with EU PSD2-equivalent standards is a specific complexity.

3. **Swiss CHF-denominated private credit / direct lending market maturing** — Institutional investors (Geneva pension funds, insurance groups, family offices) are allocating to CHF direct lending funds as a yield diversification tool. Regulatory classification (FinMIA vs. AIFM vs. unregulated) creates advisory demand for both fund managers and institutional allocators.

4. **Swiss TCFD/ISSB climate disclosure — Federal Council adoption 2026** — Switzerland adopting ISSB-based climate disclosure standards for listed companies and large foundations. FINMA expected to incorporate climate risk expectations into its supervisory framework. Geneva institutional investors face dual pressures: their own disclosure obligations and Scope 3 financed emissions reporting for investee companies.

5. **IFRS 17 internal capital allocation — decision-useful data creating finance function advisory demand** — IFRS 17 produces portfolio-level capital cost data previously unavailable under IFRS 4. Geneva insurance finance functions need new internal reporting frameworks linking IFRS 17 outputs to strategic capital allocation and board risk reporting. Dual IFRS 17 + Swiss SST reporting creates specific complexity.

6. **FINMA ICT risk circular — operational resilience examination priority 2025/2026** — FINMA standing examination priority emphasizes third-party risk (cloud outsourcing), operational resilience testing, and cyber incident reporting. Geneva FIs with international supplier networks are particularly exposed. FINMA Guidance 01/2026 on crypto custody reinforces ICT dimensions for institutions with digital asset exposure.
