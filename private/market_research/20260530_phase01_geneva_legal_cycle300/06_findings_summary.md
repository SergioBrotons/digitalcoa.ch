## Six NET-NEW Findings — Summary

| # | Finding | Core Insight | Market Opportunity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Attestation Debt** | Firms signed PI carrier AI attestations without having any underlying governance documentation. The gap is documented, not hypothetical — it is the real exposure. | A document that bridges what was signed to what must actually exist — bridging the attestation gap with a real governance foundation |
| 2 | **Carrier Assessment Incompetence** | The gap is bidirectional: carriers ask AI questions but cannot evaluate the answers. Neither party has a shared standard. The attestation is theater. | Position as the neutral standard-setter: produce what the carrier should be asking and what the firm should be able to show |
| 3 | **The June 19 Forcing Function** | The SBA Day of Lawyers on June 19 is acting as a community-level deadline. Firms are mobilizing. | 5 | **Shadow AI — The Ungoverned Firm Stack** | Small firms have 4–8 unevaluated AI tools used across different attorneys with no central inventory. The governance gap is architectural, not just procedural. | Create an AI tool inventory document that captures the full estate in one session |
| 6 | **The Documentation Paradox — Blank-Page Paralysis** | Firms that accept the need to document are blocked not by unwillingness but by blank-page paralysis. They want to act but don't know what a governance document should look like. | Deliver a ready-to-fill template (not a framework of principles) that removes the blank-page barrier entirely |

### Body Copy Principles

**What works with the C300 ICP:**
- **Acknowledgment of the specific bind** — "I know I signed something I can't prove" resonates more than "you should have a governance framework"
- **Bidirectional incompetence framing** — carriers don't know either; this is a systemic gap, not a personal failure
- **June 19 as a forcing function** — time-bound, community-level, creates peer accountability
- **Blank-page removal** — offer the template, not the framework; the starting point is the blocker
- **Shadow AI inventory** — the "how many tools do you have?" question is disarming and creates immediate engagement
- **Documentation as liability reduction** — not "you need to comply" but "here's what protects you when the carrier asks"

**What does NOT work with the C300 ICP:**
- Passive waiting framing ("lorsque vous serez prêt")
- Enterprise governance language ("NIST controls")
- Vendor-promise language on security ("our tool is 100% secure")
- Principles-without-procedure restatements (exactly what the bar hasn't delivered)
- English-only content for Geneva-first audience
