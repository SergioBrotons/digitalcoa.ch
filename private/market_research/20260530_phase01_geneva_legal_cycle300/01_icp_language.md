## Cultural-Linguistic Notes

- **French-first in Geneva**, but technical terms (DPA, nFADP, CLOUD Act, AI RMF) frequently code-switched to English — a signal of fluency, not confusion
- **Impatient readiness** replacing willing-waiting: the June 19 SBA event signals that the waiting period is over
- **Consequence-aware**: language now reflects lived experience of attestation pressure, not hypothetical risk awareness
- **Specificity hunger**: generic frameworks no longer satisfy — they want named tools, named standards, named deadlines
- **Proportionality as identity marker**: the boutique scale constraint is now stated as a design requirement, not an excuse
- **Contractual determinism**: increasing focus on what "signed contracts say" rather than what "providers promise" — a maturation of skepticism

## Net-New Language Markers (C300 Distinct from C299)

1. **"Le 19 juin"** — SBA Day on AI is now in the conversation as a reference point
2. **"CLOUD Act"** — specific threat now named explicitly, not just implied
3. **"Je refuse"** — stronger rejection language for non-compliant tools (new agency, not just hesitation)
4. **"zero retention"** — this phrase is appearing as a red line in evaluations
5. **"temps presse"** — temporal urgency language replacing "on verra" / "j'attends"
6. **"convention de traitement" (DPA)** — specific contractual ask replacing vague "je veux des garanties"
