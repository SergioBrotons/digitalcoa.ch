# Findings Summary: Cross-Vertical — C300 Phase 05

## 6 NET-NEW Cross-Vertical Findings

---

### Finding 01 — The Attestation Debt is the Universal Entry Point

**The finding:** Across all four verticals, the attestation debt — signed carrier AI attestations without underlying governance documentation — is the universal first-contact finding. It is not sector-specific. It is not knowledge-specific. It is universal across law, finance, consulting, and healthcare. The ICP signed something they cannot prove, and the gap is now visible to them through carrier form evolution.

**Mechanism:** Carrier forms have evolved from generic to version-specific and audit-trail-demanding. The ICP can now see exactly what infrastructure they don't have — not because they researched it, but because the form tells them.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same attestation debt. The legal vertical (PI attestation), finance vertical (carrier attestation), consulting vertical (carrier attestation), and healthcare vertical (carrier attestation) all have the same structure.

**Implication:** The attestation debt audit is the universal entry point service. It names the gap the ICP already knows exists, in language they already use.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You signed something for your carrier. Can you show me what you signed and the documentation that backs it up? Most firms can't."

### Finding 02 — Retroactive Documentation is Structurally Impossible

**The finding:** For AI tools activated via unsolicited updates 12–24 months ago, no decision event occurred. There is nothing to document retroactively — because the adoption event never happened as a discrete documented decision. The governance gap is architectural, not procedural. This finding spans all four verticals: legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare all have tools that were updated into AI capability without the firm's awareness or decision.

**Mechanism:** Vendor push updates, feature rollouts, and service term acceptances that occurred automatically. The firm never made a go/no-go decision. There is no document to produce retroactively.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same structural gap. The tool updated. The firm didn't decide. The documentation framework requires a decision that never occurred.

**Implication:** The service is not retroactive documentation. It is a forward governance architecture that creates the decision events going forward. The past cannot be documented — but the future can be.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You can't document a decision that never happened. What you can do is build the architecture so every future update goes through a documented decision process."

---

### Finding 03 — Vendor Exit Barriers Prevent Governance Resolution

**The finding:** Data portability limitations, model training data retention, and termination provisions in vendor contracts create structural exit barriers. The ICP who wants to improve governance by switching to a more compliant vendor cannot do so — because the vendor contract prevents it. The governance improvement path is blocked by commercial terms, not by technical impossibility.

**Mechanism:** Vendor contracts written to protect vendor interests, not ICP interests. Model training data retention means switching vendors means losing the benefit of prior interactions. Termination provisions create friction. The exit barrier is a feature, not a bug.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical uses the same AI tools. Every vertical has the same vendor contract terms. The exit barrier is universal.

**Implication:** A vendor contract review and exit planning service is a discrete offering. The ICP needs to know what they're locked into before they can plan their governance improvement.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Before you can improve your AI governance, you need to know what your current vendor contract actually says about data portability, model training, and termination. Most firms don't know what they signed."

### Finding 04 — The Four-Vertical Documentation Paradox

**The finding:** The ICP across all four verticals wants to act but doesn't know what the document should contain. This is not a knowledge gap — it is a blank-page paralysis. The ICP understands they need documentation, has the intent to build it, but cannot produce the first document because no proportionate template exists for their firm size and practice type.

**Mechanism:** Existing frameworks (ISO 27001, NIST, etc.) are designed for enterprises. They require documentation infrastructure that a 2–10 person practice cannot produce. The ICP is caught between ignoring the framework (feels risky) and building the full framework (feels impossible).

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same blank-page paralysis. The legal ICP, finance ICP, consulting ICP, and healthcare ICP all face the same documentation architecture problem at their scale.

**Implication:** A proportionate documentation template set — calibrated to 2–50 person professional firms, specific to each vertical — is the missing tool. The template is not a policy template. It is a documented decision architecture.

**Ready-to-use language:** "The problem isn't willingness. The problem is you don't know what the document should contain. I can show you — specific to your firm size and practice type."

### Finding 05 — The Timing Convergence Creates Stacked Urgency

**The finding:** Three distinct timing pressures are converging in 2026 — June 19 SBA event (legal), Swissdamed July 1 (medical devices), and end-2026 Swiss Federal Council AI consultation draft — creating stacked urgency that makes the ICP more receptive to engagement than any single deadline would alone. The ICP is not responding to one deadline — they are feeling multiple deadlines simultaneously.

**Mechanism:** The June 19 SBA event creates peer-pressure urgency (other firms are mobilizing). Swissdamed July 1 creates anxiety (even if not directly applicable). The end-2026 consultation creates long-term uncertainty. Together, they create a receptivity window that did not exist when each deadline was evaluated individually.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** The timing convergence affects all verticals, but with different acute triggers. Legal has June 19. Healthcare has Swissdamed July 1. Finance has institutional questionnaire season. Consulting has client contract cycles. The stacked urgency is cross-vertical even if the specific triggers differ.

**Implication:** The timing convergence is the engagement trigger. Any outreach that arrives before the deadlines create maximum receptivity.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Three deadlines are converging. You're feeling the pressure from all of them simultaneously. I can help you address them in the right order."

---

### Finding 06 — The Universal Trust Deficit Blocks Adoption and Productivity

**The finding:** The trust deficit — too much risk to use AI in high-value client work, too much overhead to manually compensate — is the universal structural problem across all four verticals. AI is used only in low-stakes administrative tasks where errors have low consequence. The productivity gain that motivated AI adoption in the first place is not being captured. The ICP is absorbing manual overhead without capturing the productivity benefit.

**Mechanism:** The ICP cannot trust AI output sufficiently to accept liability for errors in client-facing work. AI is limited to lower-stakes tasks. The high-value applications — where AI would provide the most productivity gain — are blocked by liability risk. The trust deficit is structural, not attitudinal.

**Cross-vertical pattern:** Every vertical has the same trust deficit structure. The legal ICP, finance ICP, consulting ICP, and healthcare ICP all face the same paradox: they want to use AI for high-value work, but cannot accept the liability risk.

**Implication:** A governance framework that enables appropriate trust — not over-trusted, not under-trusted — is the tool that unlocks productivity without unacceptable liability exposure.

**Ready-to-use language:** "You're using AI for the wrong tasks because you don't trust it enough for the right ones. A governance framework changes the trust calculus."
