## The White Space (Cross-Vertical)

### White Space 1: Pre-Signing Attestation Review (Cross-Vertical)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service exists for pre-signing review of carrier attestation forms for small professional firms. Medical practitioners developed this service for their attestation exposure. Legal, finance, and consulting managing directors face the identical exposure — personal signature on undefined adequacy standard — without the service existing for them.

**What it is:** A 30-minute structured review of the carrier attestation form before signature, assessing: (a) what the managing director is personally attesting to, (b) what documentation exists at the time of signing, (c) what gap exists between attestation and reality, (d) what the personal liability exposure is under nFADP Art.63 if the attestation cannot be defended.

**The structural analogue:** Medical practitioners needed this service — and developed it. The market solved it for doctors. The identical structural problem exists for attorneys, wealth managers, and consulting principals.

**The cross-vertical opportunity:** The same pre-signing review methodology applies across all four verticals with vertical-specific content: legal focuses on PI carrier attestation and privilege implications; finance focuses on carrier attestation and institutional client questionnaire pre-signing; consulting focuses on carrier attestation and client contract AI clause pre-signing; healthcare focuses on Swissdamed classification and carrier attestation.

---

### White Space 2: Processor Chain Documentation Kit (Cross-Vertical)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service produces a proportionate processor chain documentation kit for small professional firms. The processor chain is the primary named exposure across all four verticals. The documentation required (processor register, DPA review, sub-processor authorization chain, cross-border transfer records) is low-cost to produce and high-value across all verticals.

**What it is:** A standardized processor chain documentation kit: (a) vendor AI tool inventory, (b) processor register with sub-processor disclosure, (c) nFADP Art.9 DPA gap assessment, (d) cross-border transfer documentation, (e) ongoing monitoring methodology. Built once, applies across all four verticals with vertical-specific vendor examples.

**The market opportunity:** The processor chain kit is the standalone product that opens the door to the full briefing. It produces immediate client value, creates documentation the client can show carriers and clients, and establishes the advisor's methodology before the full governance engagement begins.

---

### White Space 3: Cross-Mandate Coherence Methodology (Finance Primary, All Verticals)

**The gap:** No Geneva methodology exists for managing coherence across FINMA Circular 08/2024, institutional client questionnaire responses, nFADP ROPA/DPIA, and carrier attestation — four independent documentation streams describing the same AI governance reality with different evidentiary standards. Finance ICPs specifically are managing all four tracks simultaneously. Cross-track inconsistency means a firm can be individually adequate across each track while being systematically inconsistent across all of them.

**What it is:** A cross-mandate coherence framework: (a) mapping of all four documentation tracks to a single governance reality, (b) inconsistency identification, (c) resolution hierarchy, (d) documentation synthesis that satisfies multiple tracks simultaneously. No Geneva service offers this. Each track was built independently; no one checks whether they're consistent.

**The finance-specific urgency:** Finance ICPs are in the most advanced documentation state — all four tracks are live simultaneously. The incoherence exposure is present-tense, not theoretical.

### White Space 4: Swissdamed Classification Assessment (Healthcare Primary, Consulting Secondary)

**The gap:** Swissdamed Tier 1/Tier 2 classification criteria were written for manufacturers. Independent practices cannot determine their tier with confidence. No Geneva-calibrated, independent-practice-proportionate Swissdamed classification assessment service exists. The July 1, 2026 deadline creates time pressure that prevents proportionate self-assessment. Vendor-coordinated notice campaigns are positioning vendors as the classification authority — with commercial interest in broad interpretation.

**What it is:** A 90-minute Swissdamed classification briefing: (a) classification criteria analysis against the practice's actual AI tool use, (b) documented rationale for classification decision, (c) registration documentation preparation, (d) ongoing obligations by tier. The service is time-limited by the July 1 deadline. First-mover positioning expires after the deadline.

**The consulting application:** Consulting boutiques face the same classification paralysis for EU AI Act nexus determination. The methodology is transferable; the classification criteria differ.

---

### White Space 5: Consequence Management Retainer (Cross-Vertical)

**The gap:** No Geneva market service exists for managing downstream consequences of already-signed attestations, already-submitted questionnaires, and already-committed client contract clauses. The ICP is already in the cascade. They need ongoing management, not one-time assessment.

**What it is:** A monthly retainer for consequence management: (a) carrier follow-up response management, (b) client audit response support, (c) regulatory deadline navigation, (d) ongoing documentation maintenance, (e) renewal preparation. The pre-signing review opens the relationship; the consequence management retainer keeps it.

**Why it's distinct from compliance-as-a-service:** Consequence management is not "maintain your compliance." It's "manage the consequences of what you've already committed to." This is a different service with different value proposition and different urgency.

---

### White Space 6: Methodology Provenance System for Consulting (Consulting Only, but Cross-Vertical Analogue Exists)

**The gap:** Consulting boutiques cannot document AI's role in their proposals and deliverables. The methodology is their product — when they cannot demonstrate its integrity, they cannot defend their recommendations. No methodology provenance system exists for boutique consulting firms.

**What it is:** A lightweight methodology documentation system for consulting firms: (a) AI tool use log by engagement, (b) client disclosure language for AI-assisted work, (c) sub-consultant AI representation requirements, (d) engagement letter AI clause template. The system documents methodology provenance without undermining intellectual authority.

**Cross-vertical analogue:** Legal has client confidentiality documentation; finance has portfolio construction authorization records; healthcare has FMH human oversight documentation. The principle is the same — document the decision architecture, not just the output.
