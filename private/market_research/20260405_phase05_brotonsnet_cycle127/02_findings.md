### Finding 02 | Newsletter-to-Engagement Pipeline — Beyond Open Rate Metrics
**Sector:** Audience Development / Brotons.net / Newsletter Strategy
**Angle:** The standard newsletter metrics (open rate, CTR) are lagging indicators. The real engagement pipeline for Brotons.net should track: (1) click-to-read depth (time on linked article, not just newsletter); (2) return visit rate from newsletter → brotons.net; (3) secondary action rate (newsletter → Substack follow vs. direct website visit); (4) referral rate (forwarded newsletter → new subscriber). Implementing these metrics requires: UTM parameter discipline, analytics event tracking on read depth, and a secondary conversion action (newsletter → website registration or Substack follow). Content opportunity: a "How to read Brotons.net" guide that routes readers to deeper engagement.

**Differentiation from prior cycles:** C125/C126 focused on retention loops and conversion funnels; C127 focuses specifically on engagement pipeline metrics beyond opens — a measurement and optimization angle.

**Confidence:** High (domain knowledge)

### Finding 04 | Brotons.net Owned Platform Depth — Why Archive Beats Newsletter
**Sector:** Content Architecture / Brotons.net / Owned Platform Strategy
**Angle:** Brotons.net's structural advantage over Substack: permanent, searchable, linkable archive organized by topic cluster. **Differentiation from prior cycles:** C126 mentioned owned-platform-first strategy; C127 provides the specific operational inversion — article first, newsletter second — and explains why this architecture serves the Geneva institutional audience better.
