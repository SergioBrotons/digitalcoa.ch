## EN — Discovery Report: Brotons.net Content & Platform Strategy (C127)

### Research Question
How can Brotons.net's content strategy and platform architecture be differentiated and strengthened in Q2 2026?

### Six NET-NEW Findings

**1. IO/NGO Institutional Intelligence Sourcing — WIPO/ICRC/ITU Report Synthesis**
Brotons.net's authority rests on Geneva institutional intelligence. The next differentiation lever: systematic sourcing from IO/NGO public documents — WIPO AI IP reports, ICRC humanitarian outlooks, ITU AI for Good outputs — as primary content inputs. This positions Brotons.net as a Geneva intelligence aggregator with institutional credibility, not just a commentator. Approach: assign each IO/NGO a content pillar, publish synthesis before/after major events. *Confidence: High.*

**2. Engagement Pipeline Metrics — Beyond Open Rate**
Standard newsletter metrics (open rate, CTR) are lagging indicators. Real engagement pipeline: (1) click-to-read depth (time on linked article); (2) return visit rate from newsletter → brotons.net; (3) secondary action rate (newsletter → website registration or Substack follow); (4) referral rate (forwarded newsletter → new subscriber). Implementation: UTM discipline, analytics event tracking on read depth, secondary conversion action. *Confidence: High.*

**3. LinkedIn Newsletter as Discovery Layer — Geneva Executive Routing**
LinkedIn's 2026 algorithm favors Newsletter content for professional reach. For Brotons.net: LinkedIn Newsletter = discovery layer for Geneva executives not yet newsletter subscribers. Strategy: publish Geneva regulatory intelligence summaries on LinkedIn Newsletter, route to brotons.net for full archive and depth. ICP match: Geneva executives LinkedIn-active but not yet subscribers. *Confidence: High.*

**4. Article-First Architecture — Permanent Archive, Alerting Newsletter**
Brotons.net's structural advantage: permanent, searchable, linkable archive organized by topic cluster. Content strategy inversion: write the article first (canonical version with full citations), then publish newsletter as the alerting layer. This makes brotons.net the reference library, not just a newsletter delivery vehicle. *Confidence: High.*

**5. EN+FR Tiered Bilingual Strategy — Not 50/50**
Bilingual strategy should be tiered, not equal split: (1) English-primary: Geneva regulatory analysis, international arbitration — global audience; (2) French-primary: Geneva local institutional intelligence, CCIG, Swiss legal/fiscal — Geneva French-speaking professionals; (3) Truly bilingual content: Geneva developments with equal EN/FR relevance. Avoids 50/50 dilution in both languages. *Confidence: High.*

**6. Pre-Event Institutional Calendar — Anticipatory Intelligence**
Geneva institutional calendar (ITU Council 2026, WIPO Assemblies, UNOG Governing Council, ICC events, FINMA consultation deadlines, SNB policy dates) provides a predictable editorial timing system. Brotons.net should publish BEFORE events/deadlines as anticipatory intelligence, not after as commentary. Key positioning distinction: pre-event briefing platform for Geneva decision-makers. *Confidence: High.*
