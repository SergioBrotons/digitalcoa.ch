# Framework 01: ICP — Geneva Small Law Firm AI Governance
## Phase 01 Geneva Legal — Cycle 31

---

## ICP Definition

### Firmographics
- **Location**: Geneva, Switzerland (primary); also cantonal/multi-cantonale
- **Firm size**: 2–5 attorneys (small law firms, boutiques, solo practitioners)
- **Specialization**: General practice, business law, employment, real estate, family law, litigation
- **Revenue range**: Boutique — typically CHF 500K–3M annual revenue
- **IT maturity**: Variable — most have basic practice management software, limited IT staff
- **Regulatory exposure**: FADP/nFADP, bar association rules, professional liability

### Decision-Maker
- **Primary**: Managing partner or sole practitioner
- **Secondary**: Office manager (if any)
- **Characteristics**: Time-constrained, practical, skeptical of vendor hype, risk-averse on client matters

### Psychographics
- **Attitude to AI**: Pragmatic adopters (individual use without formal policy); watching but not yet committed
- **Risk tolerance**: Low for client confidentiality and professional liability; moderate for operational efficiency
- **Decision style**: Conservative; want peer validation and regulatory clarity before committing
- **Trust signals**: Swiss credentials, bar association recognition, peer recommendations, regulatory alignment

### AI Adoption Profile
- **69%** of individual legal professionals using gen AI (Shadow AI prevalent)
- **34%** of firms have formal AI policies (35-point governance gap)
- **53%** small firm gen AI adoption 2024 (doubled from 27% in 2023)
- **12%** planning adoption soon (near-term pipeline)
- **Primary uses**: Drafting, legal research, document review, contract analysis, due diligence
- **Key barriers**: Data residency (client confidentiality), Swiss legal framework training, German/French/Italian language requirements

### Trigger Events
1. FDPIC enforcement reaching law firms (not yet, but pattern building)
2. Federal Council AI Action Plan May 2026 First Draft
3. EU AI Act August 2 2026 extraterritorial (for EU-nexus firms)
4. Competitor/adversary adopting AI governance
5. Bar association guidance or requirement
6. Swissdamed healthcare spillover to professional services

### Addressable Market — Geneva
- ~300–500 small law firms/boutiques in Geneva canton
- Addressable (using AI, no formal policy): ~200–350 firms
- Segment growing: 53% adoption trend + 12% planning

### Competitive Positioning Entry Points
1. FABIAN PRIVACY LEGAL — only boutique competitor in structural white space
2. Shadow AI audit — unique, low-friction entry
3. FDPIC compliance gap — universal obligation, no enforcement yet
4. 35-point governance gap quantified — urgency without alarm
