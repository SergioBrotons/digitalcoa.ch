# Phase 02 ICP — Geneva Finance / Boutique Wealth Management
## Cycle 7 (2026-03-29)

## Addressable Profile
Boutique wealth management firms and multi-family offices in Geneva, 2–20 employees, managing CHF 100M–2B AUM, serving HNW/UHNW clients internationally. CIO or Managing Partner owns compliance decisions. Typical regulatory touchpoints: FINMA, SRO, nFADP, CARF/CRS, EU AI Act (if EU nexus), AMLA.

## Addressable Problem
AI adoption accelerating without corresponding governance framework. Geneva WM firms using AI for portfolio management, client communication, risk analysis — but no dedicated AI governance advisory tier for boutique scale. Compliance function is under-resourced for the 2026 regulatory cluster.

## Regulatory Urgency Stack (Cycle 7 — Updated)
1. **CARF/CRS 2.0** — operative since January 1, 2026 (already live obligation)
2. **AMLA revision** — second half of 2026 (H2 2026; consultation concluded Jan 30, 2026; referendum period ongoing; ~3-6 months from late March 2026)
3. **LETA (Transparency Register)** — H2 2026 same implementation wave; beneficial ownership central register; 30-day discrepancy reporting
4. **Swissdamed** — July 1, 2026 (~12 weeks; cross-sector, not WM-specific but spillover to WM data governance)
5. **EU AI Act** — August 2, 2026 (~15 months; extraterritorial if WM firm has EU market nexus)
6. **FINMA AI Guidance** — December 2024 baseline (non-legal, sector-specific measures; first Swiss AI governance framework)

## Timing Signal
- AMLA/LETA H2 2026 = ~3-6 months out (refined from Cycle 6 "2-3 months IMMINENT"; timeline slightly relaxed — not imminent but approaching)
- Consultation on implementing ordinances concluded January 30, 2026 → implementation machinery now active
- Advisor scope expansion under AMLA: lawyers, notaries, trustees, CSPs required to join FINMA-recognized SRO — wealth managers with advisory component may face dual pressure
- FINMA AI Guidance (December 2024) now ~15 months old — no updated guidance published — gap is structural
- No boutique WM firm has filled the AI governance advisory gap in 7 consecutive competitive confirmations

## Urgency Drivers
- CARF/CRS 2.0 already live (no excuse for non-compliance)
- AMLA/LETA H2 2026 countdown active (consultation concluded; referendum period running)
- EU AI Act August 2, 2026 (if EU market nexus) — 15 months
- 30-day discrepancy reporting to Transparency Register (once LETA enters force) — new specific obligation
- Agentic AI trend (2026): autonomous compliance monitoring emerging — early adopters gain advantage

## First-Mover Rationale
Window: approximately 9–15 months (H2 2026 regulatory cluster peak; EU AI Act August 2026; FINMA guidance gap ongoing). No boutique advisory competitor in Geneva WM AI governance (7 confirmations). Technology vendors (Cube Finance, Swiss Compliance AI, Modulos AI) are enablers, not advisors.
