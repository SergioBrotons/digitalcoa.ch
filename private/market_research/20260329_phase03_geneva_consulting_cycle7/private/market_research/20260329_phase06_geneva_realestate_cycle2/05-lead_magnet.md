### Section 3: Question 2 — What Data Is Being Processed?
**Purpose:** Identify high-risk processing activities

**Data risk assessment for RE agencies:**

| Data Type | Risk Level | nFADP Article |
|---|---|---|
| Tenant names, addresses | Standard | Art. 6 |
| Tenant financial data (income, credit) | HIGH | Art. 5(c) |
| Biometric data (facial recognition) | HIGHEST | Art. 5(c) explicit |
| Health-related tenant data | HIGHEST | Art. 5(c) |
| Occupant behavior (smart building) | HIGH | Art. 5(c) possible |
| Property characteristics | Low | Not personal data |
| Employee data | Standard | Art. 6 |

**Key insight:** AI tenant screening processes financial data = Art. 5(c) HIGH RISK. Smart building systems processing occupant behavior = potentially Art. 5(c). Facial recognition = Art. 5(c) explicit.

### Section 5: The Human Accountability Principle
**Purpose:** Address the specific fear trigger for RE professionals

**Critical legal point:** AI valuations do NOT replace your expert accountability.

Under Swiss law and professional standards:
- Certified real estate experts remain legally responsible for valuations
- AI is an aid to the expert, not a substitute
- If an AI-valuation is wrong, the certified expert is liable — not the AI vendor
- This applies to all AI tools used in RE practice

**The nFADP implication:** As data controller, the RE agency bears responsibility for AI system outputs affecting data subjects (tenants, buyers, occupants).

### Section 6: The Compliance Timeline — Why Now
**Purpose:** Create urgency without false pressure

| Deadline | What It Means for You |
|---|---|
| **NOW** | nFADP is in force — obligations are current |
| **Jul 1, 2026** | Swissdamed — health-adjacent RE data (disability, medical notes) |
| **Aug 2, 2026** | EU AI Act high-risk operative — RE agencies with EU market nexus |
| **End-2026** | CoE AI Convention implementation — transparency, oversight, accountability obligations |

**Advisory window:** NOW through end-2026. After regulatory convergence, compliance becomes harder and more expensive.

### Section 7: Your Retroactive Compliance Path
**Purpose:** Make action feel achievable

**Three-step approach for small RE agencies:**

**Step 1: AI Inventory (1 hour)**
- List every AI tool used in your agency
- Identify data types processed by each
- Identify which tools serve as data processors (vendors) vs. controllers (your agency)

**Step 2: DPIA Priority Review (1 day)**
- Focus on HIGH and HIGHEST risk processing (tenant screening, smart building, facial recognition)
- Document what you have, what you don't have
- Identify the 3 most critical gaps

**Step 3: Compliance Documentation (1 week)**
- Execute data processing agreements with AI vendors
- Update tenant information notices to disclose AI use
- Document managing director accountability
- Prepare breach notification procedure

**DIY or advisor-supported:** Steps 1–2 can be done internally with the right framework. Step 3 benefits from professional support for legal precision.
