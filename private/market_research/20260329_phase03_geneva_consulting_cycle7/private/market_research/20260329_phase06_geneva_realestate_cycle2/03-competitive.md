### AI Tool Vendors (Not Advisory Competitors — Market Reference)

AI tool vendors serve RE agencies as technology suppliers, not governance advisors:

| Vendor Type | Examples | Role | Advisory Conflict? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Property valuation AI | Automated valuation models | Tool vendor | Yes — vendor interest is to sell tools, not assess compliance |
| CRM / marketing AI | chatbots, automation | Tool vendor | Minimal |
| Smart building / IoT | Access control, sensors | Tool vendor | Yes — vendor interest is to install systems |
| Tenant screening AI | Credit scoring, background checks | Tool vendor | Yes — processes sensitive data as processor |

**Key competitive insight:** AI tool VENDORS are not advisory competitors — but they create the advisory opportunity. RE agencies using AI tools typically receive vendor-provided "compliance" information that serves the vendor's interest (selling the tool), not the agency's legal interest (actual nFADP compliance).

## Competitive Threat Assessment

### Threat 1: AI Tool Vendors Adding Governance Services
AI valuation and tenant screening vendors could add "compliance consulting" to their offering. Currently no evidence of this; vendors benefit from selling tools, not from advising on compliance limitations. **Risk: Low near-term.**

### Threat 2: Swiss Infosec / IT Security Firms Expanding
Swiss Infosec AG and similar firms could expand from cybersecurity into AI governance. Currently no RE SMB focus. **Risk: Medium-term.**

### Threat 3: GAIGI Developing SMB Services
Geneva AI Governance Institute (operative since Fall 2025) could develop SMB-focused services. Unlikely given institutional positioning. Would also be non-competitive with boutique SMB advisory. **Risk: Low near-term.**

### Threat 4: New Boutique Entrant
A Geneva-based boutique could emerge targeting the same SMB RE AI governance opportunity. No evidence of this currently. The window is open now. **Risk: Real — window closes 18–24 months.**
