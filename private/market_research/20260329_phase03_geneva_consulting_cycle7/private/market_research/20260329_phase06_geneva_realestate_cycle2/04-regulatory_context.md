## Primary Regulatory Framework — nFADP

**Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP / SR 235.1)**
- **In force:** September 1, 2023
- **FDPIC enforcement confirmed:** 2024/2025 Annual Report (July 2025); healthcare priority; financial and RE sectors secondary but not exempt
- **nFADP applies to AI-based data processing:** FDPIC May 8, 2025 explicit confirmation

### nFADP Provisions Most Relevant to RE

| Article | Subject | RE Application |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) | Processing of sensitive personal data (financial, biometric, health) | Tenant credit screening, facial recognition access, disability-related tenant data |
| Art. 6 | Lawfulness of processing | All AI processing must have legal basis |
| Art. 8 | Transparency obligations | Inform tenants/buyers about AI use |
| Art. 9 | Data subject rights | Access, rectification, deletion requests |
| Art. 12 | Proportionality of data processing | Collect only necessary data |
| Art. 22 | DPIA requirement | Mandatory for high-risk AI processing |
| Art. 24 | Accountability | Managing director personally accountable |
| Art. 26 | Data breach notification | "As soon as possible" — cloud AI breaches included |
| Art. 28 | Data processing agreements | Required with all AI tool vendors |

### RE-Specific nFADP Triggers

**HIGH sensitivity:**
- AI tenant screening (financial data, Art. 5(c))
- Facial recognition access control (biometric data, Art. 5(c))
- Smart building IoT systems (occupant behavior data)
- Health-related tenant data (disability accommodations, medical notes — potentially Art. 5(c))

**STANDARD sensitivity:**
- Marketing automation (contact data)
- Chatbot/CRM (contact data)
- Property valuation AI (property data, not personal)

## EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Reach to Swiss RE

**EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689):**
- **High-risk AI systems:** August 2, 2026 (in force)
- **GPAI systems:** August 2, 2025 (operative — from Phase 03 Cycle 4)

### Swiss RE Agencies Subject to EU AI Act If:

1. **Placing AI systems on the EU market** — RE agencies offering AI-based property valuations, tenant screening, or other AI services to EU clients
2. **Operating EU-intended AI output** — AI systems whose outputs are intended for use in the EU
3. **Acting as EU importer/distributor** — RE agencies importing EU-based AI tools

**Dual compliance requirement (Phase 05 Cycle 4 synthesis):**
- nFADP (Swiss domestic)
- EU AI Act (if EU market nexus)
- Geneva RE agencies with any EU-facing operations face dual compliance burden

**Geneva RE EU nexus commonalities:**
- International tenants from EU countries
- Property transactions involving EU nationals
- AI valuation tools trained on EU market data
- CRM/marketing tools with EU data centers

## Geneva-Specific Regulatory Context

### OFL — Office Cantonal du Logement

**Role:** Cantonal housing and real estate regulatory authority
**Relevance:** All Geneva RE agencies must hold OFL cantonal license; OFL may increasingly reference AI governance in licensing reviews
**Opportunity:** OFL compliance infrastructure creates natural touchpoint for AI governance advisory

### Geneva Tenant Protection Laws

**Strict cantonal tenant protection framework:**
- Geneva tenant laws are among the strictest in Switzerland
- AI tenant screening tools must comply with both nFADP AND Geneva tenant protection laws
- Dual regulatory layer creates complexity for small agencies
- Advisory opportunity: Navigate both frameworks simultaneously

### Smart Building / IoT Regulatory Context

**Smart building systems in Geneva rental properties:**
- IoT sensors, smart thermostats, access control systems increasingly standard in new builds
- Data processed: occupant behavior, access patterns, energy usage
- nFADP Art. 5(c) applies if data can be linked to individuals
- DPIA likely required before deployment in residential buildings
- Property managers bear data controller responsibility for building-wide systems

## Key Regulatory Takeaways for Lead Magnet

1. **nFADP Art. 5(c) + Art. 22 DPIA:** AI tenant screening, smart building, facial recognition = HIGH risk processing
2. **Managing director personal liability (Art. 24):** Strong fear trigger for owner-managed agencies
3. **AI vendor DPAs:** Most RE agencies have not executed formal agreements with AI tool vendors
4. **Dual compliance (nFADP + EU AI Act):** Geneva RE agencies with EU nexus already face both frameworks
5. **CoE AI Convention end-2026:** Advisory window before implementation creates compliance rush
6. **GAIGI institutional legitimacy:** Category is real; the question is who serves SMB market
