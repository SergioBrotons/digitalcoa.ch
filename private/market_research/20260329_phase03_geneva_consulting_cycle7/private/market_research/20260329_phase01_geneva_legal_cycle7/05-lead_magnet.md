### SECTION 2: Decision 1 — Are You Using AI? (The Practical Test)
**Question:** Has any attorney, paralegal, or assistant in your firm used an AI tool (ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Claude, or other) for any work task — including research, drafting, review, or client communication?

**If NO:** Your firm has no current AI governance obligations. But the window to prepare proactively is closing — FDJP consultation draft expected end-2026.

**If YES:** Your firm has active nFADP obligations under Art. 5(c) and likely Art. 22 (DPIA requirement). Proceed to Decision 2.

**Compliance action:** Document all AI tools currently used by firm personnel. Personal device use counts.

---

### SECTION 3: Decision 2 — Are You Processing Client Data with AI?
**Question:** Does any AI tool you use have access to or process: (a) client names, matter details, or case information, or (b) any other personal data related to clients, counterparties, or third parties?

**If NO (AI use is data-free):** Your nFADP exposure is limited. Audit your prompts — the line between data-free and data-adjacent is thin.

**If YES:** Your firm is a data controller under nFADP Art. 5(c). You must:
- Establish lawful basis for AI processing (Art. 6 nFADP)
- Conduct a DPIA before continuing (Art. 22 nFADP)
- Implement data subject rights processes (Art. 8–13 nFADP)

**Cycle 7 signal:** FDPIC enforcement gap — law firms are NOT yet targeted, but FDPIC is building its AI enforcement portfolio across other sectors. The transport company, X/Twitter, and joint statement cases demonstrate active enforcement posture. Geneva law firms should not interpret the current gap as immunity.

### SECTION 6: The Three-Part Geneva Small Firm AI Governance Framework

**Part A — AI Inventory**
Create and maintain a written inventory of all AI tools used by firm personnel. Include: tool name, purpose, data access scope, provider jurisdiction.

**Part B — DPIA Lite**
For each AI tool that processes personal data: complete a lightweight DPIA (Art. 22 nFADP). Assess: (1) purpose limitation, (2) data minimization, (3) accuracy, (4) storage limitation, (5) security, (6) accountability.

**Part C — Human Accountability Principle**
For legal work: every AI-assisted output must be reviewed and approved by a licensed attorney before use. Document this review process. This is your legal professional responsibility anchor under OFL professional rules.
