# Phase 03 — Regulatory & Compliance Context: Geneva Consulting

## Switzerland AI Regulatory Landscape

### Current State: Guidance-Based, Not Law-Based
Switzerland does **not** have a comprehensive AI law analogous to the EU AI Act. Instead:
- Swiss AI regulation is **sector-specific and guidance-driven**
- Federal agencies (BITT, EFPC) issue guidance rather than binding rules for most AI uses
- The Swiss government favors innovation-friendly posture while monitoring EU AI Act developments
- Switzerland is **not** in the EU; EU AI Act does not directly apply, but Swiss firms serving EU clients may face indirect applicability

### Key Regulatory References for AI in Switzerland

| Reference | Origin | Relevance to Small Consultants |
|---|---|---|
| **nFADP** (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) | Swiss | Revised nFADP (in force Sep 2023) — applies to all Swiss firms; GDPR-like but Swiss-specific |
| **EFTA / EU Trade Agreements** | International | Swiss firms with EU operations may face EU AI Act requirements |
| **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** | International | AI Management System standard; certifiable; increasingly expected in B2B contexts |
| **ISO 9001 (general QMS)** | International | Often prerequisite for ISO 42001; already common in consulting firms |
| **NIST AI RMF** | US-origin | Used by some Swiss firms with US operations or partnerships |
| **EU AI Act** | EU | Indirect relevance: Swiss firms with EU clients or subsidiaries face it |

### nFADP — Key Requirements for Small Consultants
- **Legal basis** for processing client data must be established (contract, legitimate interest, consent)
- **Data subject rights** must be handled: access, correction, deletion
- **Data breach notification** required within 72 hours to PFPDT (Swiss data protection authority)
- **AI-specific concerns:** If AI tools process client data, the consultant must have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with the vendor
- **Transfer restrictions:** Client data transferred to US-based AI providers (ChatGPT, Claude) may require specific safeguards (Standard Contractual Clauses)

### Client Confidentiality + AI Tools
This is the primary regulatory anxiety for small consulting firms:
1. **What happens to data sent to ChatGPT/Claude?** — not fully clear; no enterprise DPA in most small firm cases
2. **Client consent** — do clients need to be informed/pseudonymized if AI assists with their case?
3. **Professional secrecy** — Swiss lawyers and some advisors have professional secrecy obligations; AI tools may conflict
4. **Vendor risk** — small firms rarely negotiate DPAs with AI vendors

## ISO 42001 — Emerging Standard

### What It Is
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System standard. Provides a framework for organizations that build or use AI systems.

### Relevance to Small Geneva Consulting Firms
- **Certification** is optional but growing in B2B contexts
- **Consultants advising clients on AI** may be expected to have ISO 42001 awareness or alignment
- **ISO 42001 as differentiator** — a small firm with ISO 42001-aligned AI governance may stand out in RFPs
- **Not a compliance burden** — the standard is designed to be proportionate; smaller orgs can implement a lightweight version

### Current Adoption in Switzerland
- Primarily large enterprises and multinationals pursuing certification
- Growing awareness but limited adoption in small consulting boutiques
- **Opportunity for first movers** — small Geneva consulting firms adopting ISO 42001-aligned practices could differentiate
