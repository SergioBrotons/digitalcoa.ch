# Phase 02 Geneva Finance/SMB — ICP (Cycle 5)
## Target Vertical: Boutique Wealth Management, Geneva

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** CHF 50M–500M AUM; 2–15 relationship managers; 1–3 compliance/operations staff
- **Location:** Geneva (Rue du Rhône, La Ville, or emerging Perle du Lac / Champ-Baron area)
- **Structure:** Independent boutique WM (not tied to a large banking group); family offices (CHF 30M–200M AUM); increasingly: external asset managers (EAM) affiliated with cantonal banks
- **Revenue model:** Management fee (AUM basis points) + performance fees; advisory mandates

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary decision-maker:** CEO / Managing Partner / CIO
- **Secondary:** Chief Compliance Officer (if exists) or senior RM
- **Decision dynamic:** Owner-partner driven; small team; fast informal decisions possible
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for regulatory exposure; moderate for technology adoption

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Bloomberg Terminal, MSCI, RiskMetrics; core banking platform (Finantix, InvestGlass, Additiv, or legacy); CRM (Salesforce, custom)
- **AI exposure:** Emerging — AI used for portfolio construction suggestions, risk reporting, client communication drafting; CRM intelligence; often personal device AI use not firm-sanctioned
- **Technology trust:** High for data aggregation; moderate for AI advisory output
- **IT support:** Small internal team or external IT vendor (not dedicated compliance tech)

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **FINMA Circ. 2025/02** — FinSA/FinSO conduct rules operative January 1, 2025; client suitability, documentation
2. **CARF + CRS 2.0** — OECD Common Reporting Standard 2.0 operative January 1, 2026; automatic exchange of financial account information
3. **Revised AMLA** — September 2025 revision; implementation mid-2026; KYC/AML enhanced obligations
4. **LETA** — September 2025 revision; implementation H2 2026; legal assistance in tax matters
5. **FINMA Circ. 2026/01** — Nature-related financial risks; ESG integration into risk management
6. **AI governance** — nFADP applies; FINMA AI guidance emerging; no boutique WM-specific framework exists
7. **EU AI Act extraterritorial** — August 2, 2026 high-risk operative; WM AI systems with EU nexus exposed

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Regulatory deadline or peer activity (Geneva WM community is close-knit; peer adoption is strong signal)
- **Language:** French or bilingual EN/FR; English-only acceptable for international family offices
- **Trust marker:** FINMA reference, Swiss banking regulation credibility; Geneva WM community references
- **Objection:** "We have compliance covered" — typically means traditional AML/KYC, not AI governance
- **Sales cycle:** 6–16 weeks; Geneva WM relationships are trust-based; introduction via referral is key
- **Budget:** CHF 3,000–15,000 for initial AI governance compliance framework; retainer for ongoing monitoring
