## Active Regulatory Framework

### 1. FINMA Circular 2025/02 (FinSA/FinSO Conduct Rules)
- **Operative:** January 1, 2025
- **Scope:** Financial services conduct rules; client suitability; information duties; cost disclosure
- **Geneva WM relevance:** Every boutique WM firm must apply; AI tools used in advisory must be documented within FinSA framework
- **AI governance link:** AI-assisted advisory output must meet FinSA documentation requirements; no exemption for AI

### 2. CARF + CRS 2.0 (Common Reporting Standard)
- **Operative:** January 1, 2026
- **Content:** OECD Common Reporting Standard 2.0; enhanced financial account information exchange
- **Geneva WM relevance:** All client accounts subject to CARF; AI tools processing CARF-reportable data need governance
- **AI governance link:** AI systems processing account data for CARF purposes = data processing obligations under nFADP

### 3. Revised Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA)
- **Revision adopted:** September 2025
- **Implementation:** Mid-2026
- **Content:** Enhanced KYC obligations; beneficial ownership verification; ongoing transaction monitoring
- **Geneva WM relevance:** Boutique WMs subject to stricter AML/KYC protocols; AI tools used in client onboarding or transaction monitoring must comply
- **AI governance link:** AI-based client screening or transaction monitoring = AMLA + nFADP dual obligations

### 4. LETA (Federal Act on International Mutual Legal Assistance)
- **Revision adopted:** September 2025
- **Implementation:** H2 2026
- **Content:** Legal assistance in criminal and civil tax matters; cross-border information exchange
- **Geneva WM relevance:** WMs with international client base (typical Geneva WM profile) subject to LETA procedures
- **AI governance link:** AI systems processing LETA-relevant client data need governance framework

### 5. FINMA Circular 2026/01 (Nature-Related Financial Risks)
- **Operative:** January 1, 2026
- **Content:** Nature-related risks (biodiversity, climate) integration into risk management and disclosure
- **Geneva WM relevance:** All FINMA-supervised institutions including boutique WMs
- **AI governance link:** AI tools used in ESG reporting or nature-risk analysis need governance; AI-generated ESG data subject to nFADP

### 6. Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP)
- **Effective:** September 1, 2023
- **Geneva WM relevance:** WM firms process substantial personal financial data; AI-assisted processing = nFADP Art. 5(c) triggered
- **FDPIC enforcement:** Active (FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report, July 2025)
- **DPIA requirement:** AI systems processing personal financial data require DPIA under Art. 22 nFADP
- **FDPIC May 8, 2025:** Explicit — nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing

### 7. EU AI Act — Extraterritorial Application
- **GPAI operative:** August 2, 2025
- **High-risk obligations:** August 2, 2026
- **Geneva WM relevance:** WMs using AI systems with EU market nexus (EU-based data centers, EU client accounts, EU-listed products) face dual nFADP + EU AI Act obligations
- **Dual compliance reality:** nFADP + FINMA rules + EU AI Act = 3-layer compliance burden for internationally-exposed Geneva WMs
