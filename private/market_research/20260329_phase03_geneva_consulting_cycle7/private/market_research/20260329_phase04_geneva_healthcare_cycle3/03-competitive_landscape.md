## Competitive Gap Analysis

### What the Market Needs That No One Is Providing

**"AI governance advisory for small Geneva medical practice (1–8 physicians, 2–15 staff)"**

| Need | Current Provider | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| nFADP Art. 5(c) compliance for health data processing | Legal firms (expensive) / self | No boutique advisory with practical small-practice framework |
| Swissmedic device classification for AI tools | Not addressed | No one explains this to small practices in practical terms |
| AI tool vendor due diligence (DPIA, DPA) | Not addressed | No advisory service helps small practices audit their AI vendors |
| Staff AI training + governance policy | Not addressed | IT vendors handle IT, not governance |
| Breach response planning | Legal firms (expensive) | No affordable, practical advisory for small practices |
| swissdamed registration guidance | Swissmedic (self-service) | No advisory helping small practices determine if their tools require registration |
| Liability framework (physician accountability for AI) | Not addressed | No practical guidance for practicing physicians |

### Why Large Firms Won't Capture This Segment
- **Pricing:** Small practice (CHF 300K–1.2M revenue) cannot afford Big 4 rates
- **Relationship:** Physicians need trusted peer-adjacent advisor, not anonymous consultancy
- **Language:** Geneva bilingual (FR + EN) — large firms often EN-only or FR-only
- **Scope:** Small practice needs practical clarity, not comprehensive compliance program
- **Accessibility:** 1-hour consultation at CHF 300–500 is accessible; 20-hour project at CHF 30,000 is not

### Why IT/Tech Vendors Won't Capture This Segment
- **Incentive mismatch:** Vendors want to sell tools; governance advisory is orthogonal to their revenue model
- **Expertise gap:** SwissGPT Health (AlpineAI) is a product; doesn't provide nFADP compliance framework
- **Swissmedic knowledge:** Most IT vendors unfamiliar with medical device classification requirements
