## FDPIC May 2025 — Implications for Wealth Management

From Phase 01 Cycle 3 findings (FDPIC clarification published May 8, 2025):

**Key finding:** The FDPIC's May 2025 clarification explicitly confirms that the nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing. For wealth management firms:

1. **Client financial data = special category under nFADP** — UHNW/HNW client portfolios, investment histories, asset allocations, risk profiles
2. **AI-based processing of client data = nFADP obligations trigger** — ROPA, DPIA, Art. 26 automated decision-making disclosure
3. **Consent or legitimate interest (Art. 5(c))** — WM firms using AI tools must establish lawful basis for processing; special attention to health data (some WM clients have private banking health-related exposures)
4. **Vendor due diligence** — If using external AI SaaS (91% of Swiss FIs), data processing agreements required under nFADP Art. 9
5. **Breach notification** — 72-hour notification to FDPIC + affected individuals required

**Why this matters for boutique WM outreach:**
- FDPIC May 2025 removes ambiguity about whether nFADP applies to AI tool use in finance
- WM firms are now on notice that AI-based client data processing requires nFADP compliance infrastructure
- This creates a clear, credible entry point for outreach: "Has your firm assessed its AI tool compliance posture?"

## FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — Key Points for Boutique WM

From Phase 02 Cycle 2 (FINMA Guidance 08/2024 — published Dec 18, 2024):

### What FINMA Expects
1. **Proportionate AI governance frameworks** — Scaled to firm size and risk profile
2. **AI risk inventory** — Know what AI tools are in use and why
3. **Vendor due diligence** — For external AI SaaS (91% of adopters); contractual and technical controls
4. **Human oversight** — AI-assisted decisions must be reviewable by humans
5. **Documentation** — AI governance policies, risk assessments, monitoring protocols
6. **ML/Tech Bureau requirements** — For firms using AI in customer-facing advisory

### What Boutiques Typically Lack
- Dedicated compliance officer
- Formal AI risk inventory
- Data processing agreements with AI vendors
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) for AI tools
- Incident response protocols for AI-related data breaches

**This gap = the engagement opportunity**

## nFADP Obligations — WM-Specific Application

From Phase 02 Cycle 2 + FDPIC May 2025:

### WM-Relevant nFADP Articles

| Article | Obligation | WM Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5 | Lawful basis (consent or legitimate interest) | Client financial data processing via AI |
| Art. 6–8 | Data subject rights | Client access, correction, deletion requests |
| Art. 9 | Data processing agreements | External AI SaaS vendors |
| Art. 14–15 | ROPA (Record of Processing Activities) | AI tool inventory required |
| Art. 22 | DPIA (for high-risk processing) | AI-based credit/scoring, automated advisory |
| Art. 26 | Automated decision-making disclosure | AI-assisted investment recommendations |
| Art. 29 | Breach notification (72 hours) | AI vendor breach or internal AI failure |

### WM-Specific Considerations
- **Client confidentiality:** Swiss banking secrecy (Bankgeheimnis) + nFADP; AI tools must not breach confidentiality
- **Health data:** Some private banking clients have health-related exposures (life insurance, pension planning); health data is special category under nFADP
- **International clients:** EU citizen clients = GDPR + nFADP dual compliance
- **SRO rules:** VQF, FINIA anti-money laundering obligations + AI tool use = intersection risk
