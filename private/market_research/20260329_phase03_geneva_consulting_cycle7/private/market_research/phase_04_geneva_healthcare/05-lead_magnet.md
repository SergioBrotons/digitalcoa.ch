## Lead Magnet Concept

### Proposed Title

**"The Small Medical Practice AI Decision Framework"**
*(French: "Le Cadre de Décision IA pour les Petits Cabinets Médicaux")*

---

## Rationale

### Why This Lead Magnet Works for This ICP

1. **Addresses the core anxiety directly:** Practice owners know they need to do something about AI but don't know where to start or what questions to ask
2. **Healthcare-specific:** Existing AI governance frameworks (Big 4, generalist) are not targeted at small medical practices
3. **Bilingual positioning:** Geneva medical community is naturally bilingual; FR primary makes sense for local relevance, EN for broader reach
4. **Low-friction entry point:** The framework is practical, not theoretical — physicians will see immediate utility
5. **Trust-building:** Positioning as a governance guide (not a tool vendor) establishes advisory credibility
6. **CTA pathway:** Leads naturally toward discovery call or "AI readiness check" — low-commitment first step

---

## Content Architecture

### Structure (Target: 8–12 pages / PDF, EN + FR versions)

#### Section 1: The Current Situation (Context)
- Where is Geneva healthcare AI right now?
- Why small practices are simultaneously most exposed and most under-resourced
- Key statistic: staffing shortage + administrative burden = time crisis

#### Section 2: The 3-Axis Decision Framework
A practical decision tool for small practice AI adoption:

**Axis 1 — Data Sensitivity Check**
- Does the AI process patient health data?
- If yes → nFADP compliance gate (DPA required, data residency check)
- If no → Lower regulatory burden

**Axis 2 — Clinical Impact Check**
- Does the AI influence diagnosis, treatment, or care decisions?
- If yes → Swissmedic certification check + liability framing
- If no → Operational AI (scheduling, billing, communication) — lower risk

**Axis 3 — Organizational Readiness Check**
- Staff training, change management, workflow integration
- AI tool is only as effective as the team using it

#### Section 3: The nFADP Compliance Checklist for AI Tools
- 7-point checklist: data residency, DPA, training consent, breach notification, DPIA, data minimization, patient rights
- Practical for a non-technical physician to review with their IT vendor

#### Section 4: AI Tool Evaluation Questions
- 10 questions to ask any AI vendor before signing a contract
- Includes: Swissmedic certification, data hosting, training data policy, SLA, exit rights

#### Section 5: The Geneva Practice AI Roadmap
- Phase 1 (0–3 months): Quick wins — scheduling automation, appointment reminders
- Phase 2 (3–6 months): Mid-tier — AI-assisted documentation, patient communication
- Phase 3 (6–12 months): Advanced — diagnostic AI (if applicable), EPR integration

#### Section 6: Resources & References
- FMH digital health guidelines
- nFADP text (SR 235.1)
- ISO 42001 overview
- FDPIC guidance on AI and data protection

## Positioning and Tone

### Brand Voice
- **Calm authority:** Not alarmist; not dismissive of AI
- **Practical wisdom:** Physicians respond to clear, actionable guidance — not hype
- **Swiss-precision:** Structured, clear, no fluff; mirrors how physicians think
- **Geneva-specific:** References local context (Geneva Canton, FMH, HIN)

### Emotional Arc
1. **Recognition:** "This is exactly my situation" — admin overload, staff shortage
2. **Reassurance:** "AI can help, but it needs to be done right" — governance first
3. **Empowerment:** "Here's a practical framework I can use this week" — action-oriented
4. **Invitation:** "Let's discuss your specific situation" — soft CTA to discovery call

## Gate and Nurture Sequence

### Gate Mechanism
- **Landing Page:** Dedicated DigitalCoa.ch page targeting "Geneva medical practice AI governance"
- **Capture:** Name + Email + Practice Specialty (optional)
- **Delivery:** Instant PDF download (email delivered via automation)

### Nurture Sequence (Post-Download)
1. **Day 1–2:** Follow-up email — "Which axis was hardest for your practice?" (engagement question)
2. **Day 5–7:** Email — "Common AI mistakes small medical practices make" (content value)
3. **Day 10–14:** Email — "How to evaluate an AI vendor in 10 questions" (repurposed from guide)
4. **Day 18–21:** Email — Discovery call CTA: "15-minute AI readiness check for your practice" (low-friction conversion)

## Bilingual Strategy

### Approach
- **Primary version:** English (EN) — broader reach, professional positioning
- **Local version:** French (FR) — Geneva primary market, cultural relevance
- **Distribution:** FR version distributed via Geneva-specific channels; EN version via broader Swiss + LinkedIn
- **Format:** Single PDF with EN and FR side-by-side (2-column) or sequential sections

### FR Adaptation Notes
- Title: "Le Cadre de Décision IA pour les Petits Cabinets Médicaux"
- Geneva Canton references throughout (DSG, FMH, HIN, Swissmedic — same acronyms in FR)
- nFADP → "LPD" (Loi fédérale sur la protection des données)
- Regulatory references maintained (SR numbers universal)
- Tone slightly warmer in FR version to match French-speaking business culture
