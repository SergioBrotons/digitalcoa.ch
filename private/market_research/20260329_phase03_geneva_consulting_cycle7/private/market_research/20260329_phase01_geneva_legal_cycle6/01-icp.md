# Phase 01 Geneva Legal — ICP (Cycle 6)
## Target Vertical: Small Law Firms (2–5 Attorneys), Geneva

## ICP Definition

### Firmographic
- **Size:** 2–5 attorneys (small boutique; not solo, not mid-size)
- **Location:** Geneva, Switzerland; La Vieille Ville, Grottes, emerging periphérie
- **Structure:** Traditional Swiss law firm; attorneys, paralegals, legal assistants using AI tools without formal governance
- **Revenue context:** CHF 800K–4M annually; billing hourly or fixed fees; price-sensitive on technology

### Role & Decision-Making
- **Primary:** Managing attorney / partner
- **Secondary:** Senior associates (bottom-up AI adoption driver)
- **Decision dynamic:** Partner decides alone; informal consensus with senior staff
- **Risk tolerance:** Low for regulatory exposure; moderate for efficiency tools

### Technology Posture
- **Current state:** Google Workspace or Microsoft 365; case management (Aristociel, AdNovum, or Excel); AI use ad hoc, personal-device-driven
- **AI exposure:** ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude for research, drafting, review; personal accounts, not firm-sanctioned
- **IT support:** Usually none (attorney self-manages); occasional external IT consultant

### Regulatory Stress Points
1. **nFADP compliance** — processors of personal client data; unaware of DPIA requirements for AI-assisted case handling
2. **AI-generated legal research** — attorney still accountable for output; no internal review protocol
3. **Client confidentiality + AI tools** — no formal policy on which AI tools process client matters
4. **FDJP CoE AI Convention implementation** — consultation draft expected end-2026; no readiness framework exists in the market
5. **Transparency obligations** — AI-generated work product disclosure to clients unaddressed
6. **Geneva-specific gap** — OFL (Ordre des avocats genevois) has not published AI-specific professional guidance; no official compliance anchor exists for Geneva small firms

### Buying Psychology
- **Trigger:** Regulatory deadline or peer activity (not marketing material)
- **Language:** French primary; bilingual (EN/FR) preferred
- **Trust marker:** Swiss-specific regulatory reference (FDPIC, FDJP, ANBLES, OFL) essential
- **Objection:** "This applies to large firms, not us" — dominant misconception; nFADP Art. 5 applies to any AI-based processing
- **Sales cycle:** 4–12 weeks; briefing call → diagnostic → proposal
- **Budget:** CHF 1,500–8,000 initial engagement; retainer for ongoing monitoring

### Competitive Landscape (Cycle 6 — no fresh scan, quota exhausted)
- **Confirmed no boutique competitor:** 6th consecutive cycle (Cycles 1–6)
- **Large firm competitors (non-competitive for small firm ICP):**
  - CMS Switzerland, Pestalozzi, White & Case, Schellenberg Wittmer, Lenz & Staehelin, Baker McKenzie
- **Technology providers (non-competitive — tools, not advisory):**
