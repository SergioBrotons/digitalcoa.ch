## Lead Magnet Positioning

### Core Value Proposition
> **EN:** Geneva medical practices face a structural crisis: staff shortages, rising administrative burden, and AI tools arriving faster than governance frameworks. This guide gives practice owners a clear, practical framework to evaluate AI adoption decisions — covering patient data protection (nFADP), liability, and operational efficiency — so they can act confidently.

Ce guide donne aux propriétaires de cabinets un cadre clair et pratique pour évaluer les décisions d'adoption de l'IA — couvrant la protection des données patients (nFADP), la responsabilité et l'efficacité opérationnelle — afin qu'ils puissent agir en toute confiance.

### Primary Hook
nFADP Art. 5(c) — Health data is the most protected data category under Swiss law. AI tools processing patient data require explicit governance. Most small practices don't know what they don't know.

### Secondary Hook
The "clause du besoin" (July 2025) caps new practitioners in Geneva. Your existing staff are more precious than ever. AI efficiency isn't a luxury — it's succession planning.

### Section 2: The nFADP Framework for Medical AI (2 pages)
- **Primary entry point** — why health data is different under Swiss law
- Art. 5(c): What "sensitive" data classification means in practice
- The 7-point nFADP checklist for AI adoption:
  1. Do we have a documented legal basis for AI processing patient data?
  2. Have we signed a written DPA with every AI vendor?
  3. Is patient data used for training? Is there separate consent?
  4. Can patients exercise their rights (access, correction, deletion) via our AI system?
  5. Where is patient data hosted? (Swiss server preferred; EU acceptable; US requires assessment)
  6. Have we conducted a DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment)?
  7. Do we have a 72-hour breach notification procedure in place?
- **Physician-specific angle:** FDPIC enforcement in healthcare is the sharpest enforcement risk

### Section 3: Swissmedic and AI Diagnostic Tools (1 page)
- When does an AI tool require Swissmedic certification?
- Key questions to ask AI vendors about device certification
- ISO 13485 as vendor evaluation framework
- **Physician-specific liability:** Who is liable when AI-assisted diagnosis goes wrong?

### Section 4: The 3-Axis Decision Framework (2–3 pages)
**Axis 1: Patient Data Protection (nFADP Compliance)**
- Red / Amber / Green assessment for each AI tool under consideration
- Privacy by Design checklist

**Axis 2: Clinical Liability (Swissmedic + Professional Standards)**
- Is the tool certified? Is it within our scope of practice?
- FMH digital health guidelines alignment

**Axis 3: Operational Efficiency (Staff + Practice Impact)**
- Medical assistant workload reduction potential
- Implementation disruption risk
- ROI framing for practice owner

**Framework output:** A simple 3×3 matrix that helps physicians assess any AI tool against these three axes before committing
