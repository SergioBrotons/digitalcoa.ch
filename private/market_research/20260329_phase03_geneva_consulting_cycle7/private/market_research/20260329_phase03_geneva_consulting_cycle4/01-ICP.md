### Core Pain (Cycle 4 — Sharper with GPAI Operative + Advisory Window)

**The consulting firm's AI paradox — now with dual regulatory pressure:**
- Swiss SME AI adoption hit 34% in 2025 (up from 22% in 2024). Their competitors are adopting.
- 57% of Swiss SMEs reported efficiency improvements from AI in 2025.
- **EU AI Act GPAI became operative August 2, 2025** — Geneva consulting firms with EU clients face active compliance obligations.
- Dual regulation: EU AI Act (GPAI operative) + Swiss nFADP (FDPIC enforcement active).
- FDPIC confirmed on May 8, 2025 that nFADP applies directly to AI-supported data processing.
- Using ChatGPT, Claude, or any AI tool without a Data Processing Agreement = potential nFADP violation.
- The advisory window is NOW: FDJP consultation draft for CoE AI Convention expected end of 2026. After that, Swiss law hardens.
- **Swiss Federal Council 2026 action plan: sector-specific approach (no horizontal AI act) — meaning nFADP + sector guidance remains the framework through 2026+.**

**Key phrase:** *"I want to use AI without it becoming a liability for my clients — or for me — and now I also have EU obligations I didn't have last year."*

**Key data point (Cycle 4):** *"The EU AI Act's GPAI rules became operative August 2, 2025. If you have EU clients, you're already subject to them. Swiss nFADP applies regardless. You face dual regulation — and most small consulting firms don't know it."*
