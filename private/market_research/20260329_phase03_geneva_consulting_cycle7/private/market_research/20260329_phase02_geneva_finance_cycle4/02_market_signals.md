## Market Signals

### NEW in Cycle 4 (2026-03-29)

#### 1. FINMA Circular 2025/02 — Rules of Conduct under FinSA/FinSO
- **Effective:** January 1, 2025 (transition to June 30, 2025 for certain provisions)
- **Content:** Standardizes information provision and client support in financial services; addresses duty to provide information, suitability/appropriateness tests, conflict of interest management, third-party compensation
- **Relevance:** Directly affects Geneva wealth managers and external asset managers; FINMA identified insufficient risk disclosure (especially CFDs) during supervisory reviews
- **Source:** Pestalozzi Law, Grant Thornton, Vischer, FINMA

#### 2. SIF AI Analysis — Expected Q4 2025 / Q1 2026
- **What:** State Secretariat for International Finance (SIF) comprehensive analysis of all financial market regulations to identify AI-related gaps or obstacles
- **Timeline:** Report expected Q4 2025 or Q1 2026
- **Relevance:** Will map regulatory landscape for Swiss financial institutions including wealth managers; output may trigger additional FINMA guidance
- **Source:** SIF.admin.ch

#### 3. Revised Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) — Adopted September 2025
- **Expected force:** Mid-2026
- **Key change:** Broadens scope to include "advisors" (lawyers, notaries) involved in financial transactions — due diligence obligations now apply
- **Relevance:** Wealth managers and family offices working with legal advisors face new compliance obligations; cross-referral relationships require documentation
- **Source:** Lenz Staehelin, Sigtax

#### 4. Federal Act on Transparency of Legal Entities (LETA) — Adopted September 2025
- **Expected force:** Second half 2026
- **Content:** Central non-public Transparency Register for beneficial owners; CHF 500,000 fines for intentional breaches; reporting discrepancies required from financial intermediaries
- **Relevance:** Wealth managers must verify and update beneficial owner data; client onboarding and due diligence processes require update
- **Source:** Lenz Staehelin, Sigtax, Mondaq

#### 5. Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF) + CRS 2.0
- **Effective:** January 1, 2026
- **Content:** Crypto-assets integrated into automatic exchange of information; new tax due diligence and reporting obligations
- **Relevance:** Wealth managers with crypto-exposed clients (even indirect via structured products) must implement new reporting workflows
- **Source:** Various

#### 6. FINMA Circular 2026/01 — Nature-Related Financial Risks
- **Effective:** January 1, 2026 (staged implementation)
- **Content:** Supervisory expectations for banks and foreign bank branches on climate- and nature-related risk management
- **Relevance:** Less direct for small boutiques; material for family offices with ESG mandates and sustainable investment strategies
- **Source:** Pestalozzi Law

#### 7. AI Tool Ecosystem Maturing — Named Platforms Active in Switzerland
New AI-powered platforms confirmed active in Swiss wealth management:
- **InvestGlass AI** (Swiss-hosted, Geneva-aligned) — AI co-pilot for private bankers
- **Additiv (addWealth)** — modular API-first wealth platform
- **Altoo** — Swiss wealth data aggregation
- **Neurons Lab (ARKEN)** — RM automation and compliance traceability
- **Nevis** — AI meeting summaries, client email generation
- **Linvo** — Swiss multi-family office with high AI compliance focus

**Implication:** 91% external AI SaaS adoption (from FINMA 08/2024) confirmed by growing Swiss-specific vendor ecosystem. These tools are processing client financial data — triggering nFADP Art. 5(c) and Art. 26 ADMD obligations.

#### 8. FINMA Supervisory Focus 2025-2026
- AML/KYC compliance enhancement
- CFD risk disclosure (identified as insufficient in supervisory reviews)
- AI governance (Guidance 08/2024 operative)
- Nature-related risks (Circular 2026/01)
