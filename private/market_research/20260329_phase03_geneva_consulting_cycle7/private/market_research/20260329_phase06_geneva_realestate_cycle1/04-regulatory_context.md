## Primary Regulatory Framework: nFADP

### FDPIC May 8, 2025: The Universal Anchor

The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC) confirmed explicitly on May 8, 2025:
**The Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP / LRPD) applies directly to AI-based data processing.**

For Geneva RE agencies, this means: any AI tool processing tenant data, buyer/seller data, property data, or smart building data must comply with nFADP. The RE agency is the data controller; the AI tool vendor is typically a data processor.

### nFADP Articles Relevant to RE Agencies Using AI

| Article | Content | RE Application |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) | Sensitive personal data categories | Tenant financial data, biometric data (facial recognition), health data (if health-related property) |
| Art. 6 | General data protection principles | Transparency, purpose limitation, proportionality |
| Art. 8 | Lawfulness of processing | Legal basis for AI processing activities |
| Art. 12 | Information obligations | Informing tenants/buyers about AI processing |
| Art. 14 | Right to access | Tenant/buyer rights to access AI-processed data |
| Art. 26 | Data security obligations | Technical and organizational measures for AI systems |
| Art. 27 | Data breach notification | Obligation to notify FDPIC and affected persons of breaches |
| Art. 29 | Data protection impact assessments (DPIA) | Required for high-risk AI processing (tenant screening, smart building) |

## Geneva RE-Specific Regulatory Context

### Cantonal Licensing Requirements

Geneva real estate agencies must hold cantonal licensing to operate. This creates:
- A defined registry of legitimate RE agencies (OFCOM / cantonal register)
- Professional standards that may incorporate AI ethics expectations
- An industry association touchpoint (section romande of Swiss real estate associations)

### Smart Building and IoT Regulations

Buildings with automated systems (smart heating, access control, security) generate occupant data that may constitute personal data under nFADP:
- Usage patterns can identify individuals
- Access logs are personal data
- Facial recognition in buildings is explicit Art. 5(c) sensitive data

**DPIA requirement likely triggered** for RE agencies managing buildings with smart systems.

### Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Considerations

RE transactions are subject to AML requirements in Switzerland. AI tools used for:
- Client due diligence
- Transaction monitoring
- Beneficial ownership verification

...may intersect with AML obligations. RE agencies should coordinate nFADP compliance with AML compliance.

### Who Is Affected

- Have subsidiaries or branches in EU countries

## The Human Accountability Principle — Critical for RE

### What It Means
Swiss law (and now the CoE AI Convention) is clear: **AI-assisted decisions in professional services do not replace human expert accountability.**

For real estate:
- **AI property valuations:** The certified expert remains legally responsible for the valuation, not the AI tool
- **AI tenant screening:** The agency remains responsible for the screening decision, not the AI vendor
- **AI marketing:** The agency is responsible for the content, not the AI tool

### Why This Matters for Advisory
This is the key trust-building point for Geneva RE agencies:
1. AI tools are aids, not replacements
2. The expert's accountability remains — so they need to understand what the AI is doing
3. This creates legitimate demand for AI governance advisory (not just AI adoption advice)

## DPIA Requirements for Geneva RE Agencies

### When Is a DPIA Required?
Under nFADP Art. 29, a Data Protection Impact Assessment is required when processing is "likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects."

**High-risk AI processing in RE context:**
- Automated tenant/credit screening at scale
- Smart building systems with occupant monitoring
- Facial recognition access control
- Systematic processing of sensitive personal data

### DPIA Content for RE AI Systems
A DPIA for RE AI use should address:
1. **Purpose:** Why is the AI system processing personal data?
2. **Data minimization:** Is only necessary data being processed?
3. **Legal basis:** What is the lawful basis for processing?
4. **Human oversight:** Can a human review AI decisions?
5. **Accuracy:** How accurate is the AI system? What are the error rates?
6. **Breach risk:** What happens if this data is compromised?
7. **Vendor assessment:** What is the vendor's data processing agreement?

## Compliance Checklist for Geneva RE Agencies Using AI

### Immediate nFADP Obligations
- [ ] Inventory all AI systems processing personal data
- [ ] Assess which systems process sensitive data (Art. 5(c))
- [ ] Determine which systems require DPIAs
- [ ] Ensure data processing agreements with AI vendors
- [ ] Establish breach notification procedures
- [ ] Train staff on nFADP obligations for AI use

### Data Subject Rights (Tenants, Buyers, Sellers)
- [ ] Right to access AI-processed data
- [ ] Right to rectification
- [ ] Right to deletion (where applicable)
- [ ] Right to object to automated decisions
- [ ] Right to human review of automated decisions

### Enhanced Obligations (If Sensitive Processing)
- [ ] DPIA completed and documented
- [ ] Explicit consent obtained where required
- [ ] Data protection advisor appointed (if required)
- [ ] FDPIC notification for high-risk processing
