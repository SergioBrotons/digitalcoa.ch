## Executive Summary

Phase 06 completes the five-vertical research portfolio for DigitalCoa.ch. Geneva real estate is the fifth and final vertical — and the most surprising one: 67% of Geneva RE SMEs have already adopted AI, the highest rate of any vertical. The challenge is not adoption; it is governance. Most agencies have limited knowledge of their nFADP obligations despite widespread AI use.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: 67% AI Adoption — The Highest of All 5 Verticals
Geneva real estate leads all verticals in AI adoption. Unlike legal, finance, consulting, and healthcare — where AI adoption is 9–34% and advisory focuses on adoption decisions — real estate is already there. The advisory entry point is different: "You've already adopted AI. Are you compliant?"

### Finding 2: The Regulatory Awareness Gap Is the Primary Opportunity
67% AI adoption with limited regulatory awareness creates a specific advisory opportunity. RE agencies believe their AI vendor handles compliance. They don't realize they remain the data controller with full nFADP obligations — and personal liability for their managing director.

### Finding 3: AI Valuations Don't Replace Human Expert Accountability
Swiss law is explicit: certified real estate experts remain legally responsible for AI-assisted valuations. This is the trust-building anchor for the Phase 06 lead magnet. RE principals need governance advice to understand and fulfill their accountability, not just to adopt AI.

### Finding 4: DPIA Requirements Likely Apply to Most RE AI Use
AI tenant screening, smart building systems, and facial recognition access control all likely require Data Protection Impact Assessments under nFADP Art. 29. Most small RE agencies have never heard of a DPIA.

### Finding 5: No Boutique RE AI Governance Competitor Confirmed
Consistent with the pattern across all four previous verticals, no boutique advisory firm has established itself for Geneva small RE agencies. The first-mover window is open.

## The Core Strategic Insight — Phase 06 Addition

**Real estate is the proof of concept for the AI governance advisory market.** 67% adoption demonstrates that Geneva SMBs are willing to use AI. The governance gap demonstrates they don't know what that means for their legal obligations. This pattern — high adoption, low awareness — likely exists to some degree in all five verticals. Real estate makes it visible.

The nFADP compliance question is not "should you adopt AI?" — it's "you've adopted AI, do you know what that obligates you to?" This is a more credible and less salesy entry point than the adoption question. It meets the Geneva SMB where they are, not where we wish they were.
