### Psychographic Profile

**Pain Points:**
- **Tenant/owner data management:** Processing personal data for rentals, sales, and property management — GDPR-like obligations under nFADP
- **AI tool adoption uncertainty:** 67% of Geneva RE SMEs already using AI (Q1 2024); decision-maker uncertainty about compliance obligations
- **Tenant credit checks:** Automated credit scoring involves sensitive financial data processing — nFADP Art. 5(c) may apply
- **Smart building data:** Usage data from smart building systems may constitute personal data processing
- **Security systems:** Facial recognition or access control systems = sensitive data under nFADP
- **Administrative efficiency pressure:** Staffing costs, time spent on non-revenue activities
- **Regulatory awareness gap:** Many Geneva RE companies have limited knowledge of AI regulations despite high adoption rate

**Fears:**
- **nFADP penalty risk:** Managing directors held personally liable for nFADP violations
- **Client data breach:** Tenant/buyer/seller personal data compromised through AI systems
- **Automated decision errors:** AI valuation errors leading to client loss or liability
- **Reputational damage from AI incidents:** "I didn't understand what I was signing up for"

**Motivations:**
- **Protect the business:** Avoid penalties and liability
- **Efficiency without risk:** Adopt AI for efficiency without triggering nFADP violations
- **Client trust:** Demonstrate to clients that their data is protected
- **Professional credibility:** Show cantonal licensing board / professional association that AI use is compliant

### Decision-Making Journey

1. **Trigger:** AI tool vendor pitch, peer adoption, or regulatory signal (FDPIC guidance, cantonal update)
2. **Evaluation:** "Do we have nFADP obligations? What does compliance look like?"
3. **Decision:** "We need a trusted advisor who understands real estate + AI governance"
4. **Engagement:** Lead magnet → readiness check → limited engagement

### Buying Signals

- Agency is evaluating or has adopted AI valuation tools
- Agency uses automated tenant screening or credit checks
- Agency uses smart building / IoT systems with data processing
- Cantonal or national RE association issues AI guidance
- Peer RE agency faces nFADP complaint or penalty
- Geneva AI Governance Institute (GAIGI) issues sector guidance

## ICP Fragrance (Bilingual)

> **EN:** Geneva-based small real estate agencies and property management firms (1–10 employees, residential focus, CHF 300K–2M revenue) led by experienced principals aged 40–65, operating under nFADP obligations for tenant, buyer, and property data, navigating AI adoption (67% of Geneva RE SMEs already using AI) with structural uncertainty about nFADP compliance for AI-powered valuation tools, tenant screening systems, and smart building technologies. They are pragmatists with budget authority, real efficiency pressures, and no trusted boutique advisor who understands both AI governance and their specific real estate context. Managing directors face personal liability for nFADP violations.

> **FR:** Petites agences immobilières genevoises et cabinets de gérance (1 à 10 collaborateurs, focus résidentiel, chiffre d'affaires de CHF 300K à 2M), dirigés par des responsables expérimentés âgés de 40 à 65 ans, soumis aux obligations nFADP pour les données locataires, acheteurs et biens immobiliers, confrontées à l'adoption de l'IA (67% des PME immobilières genevoises utilisent déjà l'IA) avec une incertitude structurelle concernant la conformité nFADP pour les outils d'évaluation immobilière assistés par l'IA, les systèmes de sélection des locataires et les technologies de bâtiment intelligent. Ce sont des pragmatiques avec un pouvoir budgétaire, de réelles pressures d'efficacité, et aucun conseiller boutique de confiance qui comprend à la fois la gouvernance de l'IA et leur contexte immobilier spécifique. Les directeurs généraux engagent leur responsabilité personnelle en cas de violations nFADP.

## Why Geneva Real Estate Is Different From Other Verticals

| Dimension | Geneva RE vs. Other Verticals |
|---|---|
| **AI adoption rate** | 67% — HIGHEST of all 5 verticals (vs. 9–34% elsewhere) |
| **Regulatory awareness** | LOWEST — high adoption but limited knowledge of obligations |
| **Accountability clarity** | HIGH — AI valuations explicitly do NOT replace human expert accountability |
| **Data types** | Tenant data, financial data, property data, smart building data |
| **Entry point** | "You're already using AI — do you know your nFADP obligations?" |
| **Conversion path** | Efficiency already proven → governance and compliance needed |

**Key insight:** Unlike other verticals where AI adoption is low and governance is the primary concern, Geneva RE already has high AI adoption. The advisory entry point is different: not "should you adopt AI?" but "you've adopted AI — are you compliant?"
