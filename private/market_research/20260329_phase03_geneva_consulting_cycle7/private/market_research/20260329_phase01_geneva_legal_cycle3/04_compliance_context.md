## Compliance Context: Geneva Small Law Firms + AI Governance

### Overview
Geneva attorneys operating AI tools face a dual compliance track: (1) data protection obligations under nFADP, and (2) professional conduct obligations under the Swiss Bar Association (SBA) Guidelines and cantonal bar rules. Both tracks are now clarified by recent guidance.

---

## Regulatory Framework (Cycle 3 — Updated)

### 1. Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) — The Data Protection Floor

**Status:** In force since September 1, 2023

**What it means for attorneys using AI:**
- Any AI tool processing client personal data = data processing under nFADP
- Law firm = data controller (for client data) + may be data processor (for AI vendor)
- FDPIC confirmed (May 8, 2025): nFADP's technology-neutral wording covers AI-based data processing

**Key nFADP articles for attorneys:**

| Article | Obligation | Practical Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Art. 5(c) | Sensitive data (legal situation = sensitive) | Explicit consent required for AI processing of case data |
| Art. 6 | Lawful basis | Need to identify basis for processing (typically: consent or legitimate interest) |
| Art. 12 | Transparency | Must inform clients when AI is used — this is Decision 3 in the lead magnet |
| Art. 14 | Right to information | Clients can ask what data is processed and how |
| Art. 15 | Right to correction | Clients can request correction of AI-processed data |
| Art. 22 | DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) | Required if AI processing is "likely to result in high risk" — attorneys should do one |
| Art. 27 | Data processing agreements | Required with AI tool vendors (DPAs/SCCs) |

**Attorney-specific note:** Client legal situation data = Art. 5(c) sensitive personal data. This is the highest protection category. AI processing of case-related information requires explicit, informed consent.

### 2. Swiss Bar Association (SBA) AI Guidelines — The Professional Standard

**Status:** Adopted June 2024

**What it means for Geneva attorneys:**
- The SBA has issued official guidance on AI use by lawyers
- Professional secrecy (Art. 13 LLCA) applies to AI tool use — attorney must ensure tool preserves confidentiality
- Attorney remains responsible for AI-assisted work product — automation does not transfer liability
- Internal AI policy recommended for law firms

**Key SBA Guidelines implications:**
1. **Professional secrecy:** AI tools used must maintain client confidentiality
   - Cloud/SaaS tools: require data processing agreements + confidentiality verification
   - External servers: attorney must ensure Swiss or equivalent data protection standards
2. **Accountability:** Attorney is responsible for work product regardless of AI involvement
3. **Informed consent:** Client should be informed of AI tool use (aligns with nFADP Art. 12)
4. **Documentation:** Recommended to maintain records of AI tool use and data flows

**Note:** Full SBA Guidelines document needs direct review (sba.ch). Key points above from training knowledge.

### 3. Federal Council AI Strategy + Council of Europe AI Convention

**Status:** Strategy announced February 2025; Convention signed March 2025

**What it means for attorneys:**
- Switzerland is ratifying the CoE AI Convention rather than adopting a standalone Swiss AI Act
- Draft implementation bill expected end of 2026
- Through 2026: nFADP + SBA Guidelines = operative framework
- Post-2026: new obligations will emerge (transparency, oversight, accountability for AI systems)

**Timeline implication:** The current window (2025–2026) is the time to establish AI governance practices. Firms that wait for the 2026 law will be behind those that act now.

### 4. Cantonal Health Law (Not primary for legal, noted for cross-vertical)

**Context:** For healthcare-adjacent legal work (medical malpractice, health data in litigation), additional cantonal health law obligations may apply.

## ISO 42001 AI Governance — The Differentiator

**ISO 42001:** The international standard for AI management systems, published 2023. Certifiable. Becoming relevant for professional services procurement.

**Why it matters for Geneva attorneys:**
- Provides a structured framework for AI governance — exactly what small firms need
- ISO 42001 certification signals to clients and counterparties: "we take AI governance seriously"
- Differentiates DigitalCoa.ch from firms that only do nFADP compliance
- **Phase 05 confirmed:** ISO 42001 + nFADP = the stack that positions DigitalCoa.ch as the premium advisor

**For small law firms:** ISO 42001 certification may be 12–18 months away as a realistic goal. The path is: nFADP compliance → AI governance framework → ISO 42001 readiness → certification.

## Geneva Attorney AI Governance Checklist (from Cycle 3 Regulatory Analysis)

Based on FDPIC May 2025 + SBA Guidelines June 2024, a Geneva attorney using AI should:

- [ ] **Classify the data** — Is the client data sensitive (Art. 5(c))? Does AI processing require explicit consent?
- [ ] **Verify the tool** — Does the AI vendor have a data processing agreement? Are servers Swiss/EU? Can vendor access our data?
- [ ] **Inform the client** — Does the engagement letter disclose AI tool use? Is consent obtained where required?
- [ ] **Document the process** — Is there a record of which AI tools are used, for what, with what data?
- [ ] **Maintain oversight** — Is the attorney reviewing AI-assisted work product before client delivery?
- [ ] **Consider a DPIA** — For high-risk AI processing, a Data Protection Impact Assessment is required
- [ ] **Review the internal AI policy** — Does the firm have a written AI use policy?

## Notes

**nFADP legal basis:** Confirmed as primary operative law for AI governance through 2026. FDPIC guidance provides enforcement signal. SBA Guidelines provide professional enforcement signal.

**SBA enforcement:** The Geneva Bar Association can discipline attorneys for professional misconduct. AI use that breaches client confidentiality or professional secrecy could trigger bar discipline in addition to nFADP liability.

**Two-track compliance:** Geneva attorneys face both data protection liability (FDPIC) and professional conduct liability (SBA/Geneva Bar) for AI governance failures. This dual exposure is unique to the legal vertical.
