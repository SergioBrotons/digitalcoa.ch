## Competitive Landscape: Geneva Legal AI Governance (Cycle 3)

### Summary Finding (Confirmed)
**No boutique competitor** targets Geneva small law firms (1–8 attorneys) specifically for AI governance advisory. The first-mover window remains open. The competitive landscape consists of three layers: large international firms (out of range), vertical-specific tool vendors (not advisors), and professional associations (guidance but not hands-on).

---

## Competitive Layers

### Layer 1: Large International Law Firms — Out of Range
**Examples:** Lenz & Staehelin, Pestalozzi, Walder Wyss, Baker McKenzie (Geneva)
**Position:** AI governance for large corporates and financial institutions
**Why out of range for small firms:**
- Engagement minimum: CHF 100K+
- Target: multinationals, financial institutions, public companies
- Not interested in CHF 8K–15K AI governance assessments for small firms
- No specific offering for 1–8 attorney practices

**Relevance to DigitalCoa.ch:** Benchmarks pricing and positioning. Confirms that CHF 8K–15K for SMB AI governance is in a gap between free SBA guidance and CHF 100K+ law firm advisory.

### Layer 2: Geneva AI Governance Specialists — Different Segment
**New in Cycle 3:** Geneva has law firms and lawyers positioning in AI governance — but targeting mid-to-large clients, not small firms.

**Gilliéron Avocat (Geneva)**
- Specialty: IP, IT Law, Data Protection, AI governance
- Target: mid-size to large clients
- Website: gillieronavocat.ch
- Note: This firm serves the upper market, not the 1–8 attorney segment

**CROCE & Associés SA (Geneva)**
- Focus: digital solutions for legal work
- Incorporates AI for internal firm use
- Not positioning as external AI governance advisor to other firms

**Quinze Cour des Bastions (Geneva)**
- Lawyers Nicolas Capt, Léonore Bozovic — recognized tech law experts
- University of Geneva involvement
- Advisory focus: mid-size institutions, not small practices

**Akin Gump (Geneva office)**
- International firm advising on regulatory issues including new technologies
- Engagement scale: large multinationals and financial institutions

**Assessment:** These Geneva specialists exist but do not target small law firms. They serve mid-to-large clients who need regulatory counsel, transaction support, and institutional advice. Small firms (1–8 attorneys) are not their market.

### Layer 3: Professional Associations — Guidance Without Hands-On
**Swiss Bar Association (SBA / SAV)**
- Issued AI Guidelines (June 2024)
- Provides ethical framework and professional standards
- Does NOT provide: individual firm assessments, hands-on implementation, decision frameworks
- **This is the permission structure** — SBA acknowledging AI creates demand for governance

**Geneva Bar Association (Ordre des Avocats de Genève)**
- Local implementation of SBA guidelines
- Educational events, not hands-on advisory
- **DigitalCoa.ch positioning:** The firm that helps attorneys actually implement SBA guidelines

### Layer 4: AI Tool Vendors — Not Advisors
**Examples:** Harvey AI, Lexis+AI, Clio (legal practice management)
- Sell AI tools
- Not advisors on governance or compliance
- Have data processing agreements — but don't help firms understand their obligations
- **DigitalCoa.ch positioning:** Help firms decide which tools to use and how to govern them — before signing with vendors

### Layer 5: Business Consultants / Digital Advisors — No Legal Specialization
**Examples:** Digital advisory firms targeting SMBs generally
- Generic AI consulting
- No legal sector specialization
- No understanding of professional secrecy (Art. 13 LLCA) or bar obligations
- **DigitalCoa.ch positioning:** The only Geneva advisor combining AI governance + legal sector specialization
