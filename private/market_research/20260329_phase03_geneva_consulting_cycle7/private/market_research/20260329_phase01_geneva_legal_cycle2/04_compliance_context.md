## Regulatory Framework (Cycle 2 — Phase 05 Synthesis Integration)

### Primary Regulatory Stack

**1. nFADP (Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection) — AS OF 2026**
- In force since 1 September 2023
- **Phase 05 finding:** Universal entry point across ALL verticals. For legal, this is layered with professional secrecy obligations.
- Key provisions: Art. 6 (lawfulness), Art. 8 (consent), Art. 5(c) (data subject rights)
- **Legal-specific complexity:** When AI processes client case data, is consent required? Does the attorney-client privilege affect notification obligations?

**2. Article 13 LLCA (Professional Secrecy)**
- Swiss Lawyers Act (Loi sur la profession d'avocat)
- Absolute professional secrecy obligation for attorneys
- **Key question for AI:** Does using a Swiss-hosted AI tool constitute "disclosure" under Art. 13? (Answer: It depends on the tool's data flows — which is exactly why a decision framework is needed)

**3. Article 4 CPC (Code of Civil Procedure)**
- Related confidentiality obligations in litigation context
- Amplifies data sensitivity for certain matter types

**4. SAV/FSA Guidelines on AI Use (June 2024)**
- Swiss Lawyers Association guidelines
- First formal guidance addressing AI use by attorneys
- **Gap:** Guidelines are aspirational but don't give small firms a practical decision process
- **Phase 05 connection:** These guidelines are the trigger that makes attorneys feel they need to "do something" — but they don't tell them what

**5. CCBE Guide on AI (October 2025)**
- Cross-border dimension from European counterpart organizations
- Relevant for Geneva attorneys handling international matters

**6. ISO 42001 AI MS (Phase 05 Synthesis — New Addition for Cycle 2)**
- The AI Management System standard
- **Phase 05 finding:** ISO 42001 + nFADP stack = core differentiation
- For legal: ISO 42001 certification would demonstrate systematic AI governance commitment
- **Practical value:** A small law firm can demonstrate "we have a systematic approach to AI decisions" via documented governance process — without full certification cost

## The Compliance Paradox (Cycle 2 Insight)

**Phase 05 identified a universal paradox:** Small firms want to be compliant but don't know what compliance means for AI. The formal frameworks (DPIA, AI committee, ISO 42001) are designed for enterprises, not 2–5 attorney shops.

For Geneva legal specifically, the paradox is acute:
- They feel the obligation to have AI governance (SAV/FSA guidelines)
- They don't have the structure to implement enterprise-grade governance
- They fear making a mistake that triggers disciplinary consequences
- The result: paralysis or denial

**Sergio's entry point:** "You don't need a compliance department. You need to know the three decisions that determine whether your AI use is safe for client data."

## Notes
Compliance context is rich but access is the challenge. The regulatory landscape creates the problem (they feel they need to act) but doesn't provide the solution (a practical decision process for small firms).

Phase 05 synthesis confirmed: nFADP is the hook, ISO 42001 governance framing is the differentiator. For legal specifically, professional secrecy (Art. 13 LLCA) is the additional unique hook that other verticals don't have.
