## Current Problems (Visible in Market)

### Geneva Bar Association / Professional Body Language
- OAG (Ordre des Avocats Genève) runs generative AI training modules
- Module: "L'intelligence artificielle générative dans le quotidien de l'avocat"
- Event: "Intelligence artificielle et contrats : Quels enjeux pratiques?"
- Forty-Four Avocats (Geneva firm) published November 2024 alert on AI dangers: fictitious case law generated by AI
- SAV/FSA published "Guidelines for attorneys regarding AI use" (June 2024)

### How Attorneys Describe AI Struggles
- Hesitation facing generic AI tools (ChatGPT etc.)
- Fear of transmitting confidential data to external servers
- Concern about data being used to train models
- Skepticism about ROI

### Visible Failures or Pain Points
- AI "hallucinations": fictitious case law
- Data leaks via unsecured tools
- "Shadow IT": attorneys using unapproved tools
- AI errors undetected before submission to client

### Trigger Moments (Phase 05 Cross-Vertical Validation)
These triggers are universal across all 4 verticals. The Geneva legal-specific additions:
1. Un competitor or colleague begins using AI (feeling of being behind)
2. A client asks "why aren't you using AI?"
3. A data incident or AI hallucination story in the press
4. OAG training opens eyes to how much they don't know
5. Fee pressure / efficiency demand
6. **Legal-specific:** SAV/FSA guideline update triggers re-examination of firm policy
7. **Legal-specific:** A client asks about data rights under nFADP (becoming more common per Phase 05 pattern)

### Specific AI Tools Being Evaluated
**Swiss-hosted options (preferred):** Legartis, Swiss-Noxtua, Whisperit.ai, Omnilex, LARI
**International:** Spellbook, CoCounsel, Lexis+ AI, Westlaw Precision, Clio, Harvey AI

### Geneva-Specific Context
- 2–5 attorneys max = very limited IT resources
- Strong tradition of confidentiality = high barrier to cloud AI
- SAV/FSA guidelines (June 2024), CCBE guide (October 2025)
- Article 13 LLCA (professional secrecy), Art. 4 CPC
- 68% of Swiss law firms have banned ChatGPT for professional secrecy reasons
- **Phase 05 addition:** nFADP introduces formal data rights language that attorneys don't yet have scripted responses for

## Phase 05 Cross-Vertical Validation
Phase 05 synthesis confirmed that Geneva legal is part of a ~1,800–2,630 firm addressable market across all 4 verticals. The Geneva legal sub-segment (small law firms 2–5 attorneys) represents the highest trust-threshold vertical — professional secrecy is not just a compliance requirement but a core professional identity marker.

This means:
- The barrier to entry is higher (they won't adopt AI casually)
- But the loyalty once trust is established is also higher
- The decision framework approach is uniquely suited to this vertical
- No competitor is targeting this specific sub-segment with governance framing
