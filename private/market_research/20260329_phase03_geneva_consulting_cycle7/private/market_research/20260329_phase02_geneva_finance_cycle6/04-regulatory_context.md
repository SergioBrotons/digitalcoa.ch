## Active Regulatory Framework

### 1. CARF/CRS 2.0 — Operative Since January 1, 2026 (NOW LIVE)
- **Status:** Operative January 1, 2026 — 3 months live as of Cycle 6
- **Scope:** Automatic exchange of financial account information; CRS 2.0 enhances AI-related data processing coverage
- **Geneva WM implication:** Any AI tool processing CARF-reportable client financial data has live governance obligations
- **AI governance gap:** AI hallucination risk = CARF accuracy risk. AI systems generating or analyzing CARF data must have documented accuracy controls and audit trails
- **Cycle 6 sharpening:** Obligation is no longer upcoming — WMs have been operating under CARF/CRS 2.0 for 3 months with no dedicated AI governance framework

### 2. Revised AMLA — ~2-3 Months Away (MOST URGENT)
- **Status:** September 2025 revision; implementation mid-2026 (~April-June 2026)
- **Cycle 6 sharpening:** "Mid-2026" is now approximately 8-12 weeks away. This is imminent.
- **Scope:** Enhanced KYC/AML obligations; AI in client onboarding, transaction monitoring, beneficial ownership verification
- **Geneva WM implication:** AI used in KYC/AML = AMLA + nFADP dual obligations
- **Key intersection:** AI-based KYC verification, transaction screening, and client risk scoring all face AMLA + nFADP simultaneously
- **Cycle 6 added:** AMLA is the single most urgent near-term regulatory catalyst for Geneva boutique WMs

### 3. LETA Revision — ~3-6 Months Away
- **Status:** September 2025 revision; H2 2026 operative (~July-December 2026)
- **Scope:** Cross-border legal assistance in tax matters; international client base exposure
- **Geneva WM implication:** AI processing of client data potentially subject to LETA procedures = legal hold, data retention, cross-border transfer documentation obligations
- **Cycle 6 sharpening:** H2 2026 window now concrete — approximately 12-26 weeks away

### 4. FINMA Circular 2025/02 — Operative Since January 1, 2025
- **Status:** Operative January 1, 2025 (15 months)
- **Scope:** FinSA/FinSO conduct rules; client suitability, documentation, disclosure
- **Geneva WM implication:** AI-based advisory or portfolio suggestions must comply with FinSA suitability rules; nFADP obligations layered on top

### 5. FINMA Circular 2026/01 — Operative Since January 1, 2026
- **Status:** Operative January 1, 2026 (3 months)
- **Scope:** Nature-related financial risks; ESG integration into risk management
- **Geneva WM implication:** AI in ESG reporting or nature-risk analysis = nFADP Art. 5(c) obligations; DPIA required

### 6. EU AI Act — High-Risk Operative August 2, 2026 (~15 Months)
- **Status:** GPAI operative August 2, 2025; high-risk operative August 2, 2026
- **Geneva WM implication:** WMs with EU-based clients, EU data center API endpoints, or EU-intended AI outputs face EU AI Act high-risk obligations
- **Dual compliance:** nFADP + EU AI Act = 3-layer challenge (Swiss domestic + Swiss international + EU extraterritorial)
- **Geneva-specific exposure:** Most Geneva boutique WMs serve UHNWI clients with EU connections. EU nexus probability is higher than domestic WM context would suggest.

### 7. nFADP — Operative Since September 1, 2023
- **Status:** Operative 2.5 years
- **Geneva WM implication:** AI processing of personal financial data = Art. 5(c) triggered; DPIA required for AI-based processing
- **FDPIC enforcement:** FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (July 2025) confirms active enforcement trajectory
