## Switzerland AI Regulatory Landscape

### Current State: Guidance-Based, Not Law-Based
- Switzerland does **not** have a comprehensive AI law analogous to the EU AI Act
- AI regulation is **sector-specific and guidance-driven**
- Federal agencies (BITT) issue guidance rather than binding rules
- Switzerland is **not** in the EU; EU AI Act does not directly apply
- Swiss firms serving EU clients may face indirect applicability

### Key Regulatory References for Small Geneva Consultants

| Reference | Origin | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **nFADP** | Swiss | Revised nFADP in force Sep 2023 — applies to ALL Swiss firms |
| **ISO/IEC 42001:2023** | International | AI Management System standard; growing B2B expectation |
| **ISO 9001** | International | Often prerequisite for ISO 42001 |
| **EU AI Act** | EU | Indirect: Geneva firms with EU clients face it |
| **FINMA guidance** | Swiss | Relevant only for finance-adjacent consulting |

## nFADP — The Universal Entry Point (Phase 05 Synthesis Confirmed)

### Why nFADP is the Hook for This Vertical
Every small consulting firm in Geneva is now using (or has used) ChatGPT, Claude, or similar AI tools — almost certainly without:
1. A signed Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with the vendor
2. A documented legal basis for sending client data to the vendor
3. A 72-hour breach notification procedure

This is a real, current exposure. **nFADP is enforcement-active.** The PFPDT (Swiss data protection authority) has enforcement capacity.

### Key nFADP Requirements for Small Consultants

| Requirement | What It Means for the ICP |
|---|---|
| Legal basis for processing | Must have contract, legitimate interest, or consent before using AI on client data |
| Data subject rights | Must handle client requests (access, correction, deletion) |
| 72-hour breach notification | If AI vendor breaches, consultant has 72h to notify PFPDT |
| DPA with vendors | Using ChatGPT/Claude without a DPA = potential nFADP violation |
| Transfer restrictions | US-based AI providers require Standard Contractual Clauses |

### The AI + nFADP Problem in Practice
1. Consultant sends client data to ChatGPT to draft a proposal → likely no DPA → potential violation
2. Consultant uses AI to summarise a client interview → client data processed outside Switzerland → exposure
3. Consultant doesn't know any of this is an issue → nFADP enforcement is unexpected and disruptive

**This is the entry point: the first conversation DigitalCoa.ch has with a Geneva consulting firm leader should be about nFADP + AI tools.**

## ISO 42001 — The Differentiator (Phase 05 Synthesis Confirmed)

### What It Is
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System standard. Provides a structured framework for organisations that build or use AI systems.

### Why It Matters for Small Consultants
- **B2B signal:** A small firm that can say "our AI use is ISO 42001-aligned" stands out in RFPs
- **Client trust:** ISO 42001 alignment demonstrates seriousness about AI governance
- **Competitive differentiation:** No small Geneva consulting firm currently has this
- **Proportionate:** The standard is designed to be scalable; smaller orgs can implement lightweight versions

### Current Adoption in Switzerland
- Primarily large enterprises and multinationals
- Growing awareness but near-zero adoption in small consulting boutiques
- **First-mover opportunity:** Small Geneva consulting firms adopting ISO 42001-aligned practices can lead their segment
