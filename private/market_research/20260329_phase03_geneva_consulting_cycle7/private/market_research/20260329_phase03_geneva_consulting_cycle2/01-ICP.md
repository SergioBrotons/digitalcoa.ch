## Vertical Definition
Consulting firms in Geneva operating with 2–15 employees, generating CHF 500K–5M annual revenue. Predominantly sole proprietors and boutique practices offering specialized advisory services to mid-market and large clients. Sectors: strategy consulting, IT/technology consulting, organizational/HR consulting, sustainability/ESG consulting, financial consulting (non-regulated).

- **Average Deal Velocity:** 4–12 weeks

### Core Pain (Cycle 2 — Sharpened)

**The consulting firm's AI paradox:**
- They know AI is here. Their clients are asking about it. Their junior staff expect it.
- But using AI tools risks the one thing that makes them credible: **client confidentiality**
- nFADP is now in force. The 72-hour breach notification clock is real.
- Every AI vendor they try adds a new data risk they don't understand.
- Doing nothing isn't safe either — competitors with AI-augmented delivery are winning pitches.

**Key phrase:** *"I want to use AI without it becoming a liability for my clients — or for me."*

### Psychographic / Pain Indicators

- **Time poverty:** Billable hours consumed by admin, reporting, proposal writing
- **Client expectation gap:** Clients increasingly expect data-driven deliverables and real-time reporting
- **Talent pressure:** Hard to hire; must do more with fewer people
- **Technology confusion:** Overwhelmed by AI options; fear of wrong choice
- **Data governance anxiety** *(NEW in Cycle 2 — nFADP urgency):* Client data in AI tools = potential breach liability. 72-hour notification rule. DPA obligations they didn't know they had.
- **Competitive anxiety:** Larger boutiques building AI delivery; small feels left behind

### Technology Posture

- **Current State:** Office 365 / Google Workspace, basic CRM, LinkedIn
- **AI Adoption:** Individual experimentation (ChatGPT, Claude) — not firm strategy
- **Data Maturity:** Low-to-moderate; client data often in spreadsheets or entry CRM
- **nFADP Awareness:** Low — most don't know what a DPA with an AI vendor is
- **ISO 42001 Awareness:** Near zero — but resonates when explained

### Buying Triggers

1. Client explicitly requesting AI-enhanced deliverables or methodology
2. Competitor adopting AI-driven delivery model
3. Proposal win rate decline
4. Key senior consultant departure (knowledge loss)
5. nFADP enforcement concern (knock-on advisory need)
6. RFP mentioning AI governance or data protection requirements

### Buying Blocks

- **Trust:** "Will AI compromise my client confidentiality?"
- **ROI Uncertainty:** "What's the actual payback for my firm?"
- **Integration Complexity:** "My team won't adopt new tools"
- **Regulatory risk:** "Am I now liable under nFADP for what ChatGPT does with my client's data?"
- **Cost visibility:** Unclear total cost of AI-augmented service delivery

## Addressable Segments

### Segment A: Strategy & Management Consultants (~35%)
- Pain: Proposal and deliverable production heavily manual

- Pain: Keeping pace with rapid tech change; delivering to non-technical clients
- AI Relevance: Very High — already AI-adjacent; risk of commoditisation

### Segment C: HR & Organisational Consultants (~20%)
- Pain: High-touch model limits scale; remote/hybrid challenges
- AI Relevance: Medium-High — candidate sourcing, assessment, report writing

### Segment D: Sustainability & ESG Consultants (~15%)
- Pain: Rapid regulatory evolution; data collection from clients
- AI Relevance: High — data aggregation, reporting, scenario modelling

### Segment E: Miscellaneous Specialists (~5%)
- Legal consulting (non-law firm), tax advisory, interim management
- AI Relevance: Moderate

## ICP Summary Statement

> **EN:** Small Geneva consulting firms (2–15 employees, CHF 500K–5M revenue) led by experienced managing partners aged 40–60. They face a specific paradox: AI is essential for competitive delivery, but using it risks the client confidentiality that is their core asset. With nFADP now in force, the stakes are no longer theoretical — there is a 72-hour breach notification clock, DPA obligations with AI vendors, and real enforcement risk. They need a guide they can trust.

> **FR:** Petits cabinets de conseil genevois (2 à 15 employés, CHF 500K–5M de chiffre d'affaires), dirigés par des associés-gérants expérimentés de 40 à 60 ans. Ils font face à un paradoxe spécifique : l'IA est essentielle pour rester compétitif, mais l'utiliser risque de compromettre la confidentialité client qui est leur actif central. Avec la nFADP en vigueur, les enjeux ne sont plus théoriques — obligation de notification de violation en 72 heures, DPA avec les fournisseurs d'IA, risque réel de sanction. Ils ont besoin d'un guide fiable.
