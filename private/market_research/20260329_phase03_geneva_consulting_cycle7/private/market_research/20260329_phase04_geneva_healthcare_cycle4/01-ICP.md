## ICP Summary

> **EN:** Geneva and Vaud medical practices (1–8 practitioners, 2–15 staff) led by experienced physicians aged 45–65, facing a structural staffing crisis as nearly 40% of Geneva doctors plan retirement within 5 years (AMGe 2023), struggling with administrative overload, medical assistant shortages (Geneva highest wages in CH), patient data security, and AI adoption decisions that affect patient privacy, clinical liability, and practice efficiency. FDPIC's 2024/2025 Annual Report (Jul 2025) confirms nFADP enforcement has begun — healthcare data breaches are a priority area. swissdamed mandatory registration July 1, 2026 adds device-level compliance urgency. Only 42% of Swiss medical groups have formal AI governance policies (MGMA Jan 2026). No clear trusted advisor exists for small medical practice AI governance in Geneva or Vaud.

> **FR:** Cabinets médicaux de Genève et de Vaud (1 à 8 praticiens, 2 à 15 collaborateurs), dirigés par des médecins expérimentés âgés de 45 à 65 ans, confrontés à une crise structurelle du personnel alors que près de 40% des médecins genevois prévoient de prendre leur retraite dans les 5 ans (AMGe 2023), et qui luttent contre la surcharge administrative, la pénurie d'assistantes médicales (salaires genevois les plus élevés de Suisse), la sécurité des données patients et les décisions d'adoption de l'IA. Le rapport annuel 2024/2025 du PFPDT (juillet 2025) confirme que l'application de la nFADP a commencé — les violations de données de santé sont une priorité. L'enregistrement obligatoire swissdamed dès le 1er juillet 2026 ajoute une urgence de conformité. Seulement 42% des groupes médicaux suisses disposent de politiques formelles de gouvernance de l'IA (MGMA, jan. 2026). Aucun conseiller de confiance n'existe pour la gouvernance de l'IA des petits cabinets médicaux à Genève ou Vaud.

4. **Peer behavior** — another physician's experience or incident

## Pain Points

### Top 5 Pain Points (Ranked)

1. **Administrative overload** — physicians spend 40–50% of time on admin; AI promises relief but implementation is risky
2. **Medical assistant shortage** — Geneva has highest medical assistant wages in Switzerland; retention crisis is structural (AMGe confirmed)
3. **Patient data security** — nFADP Art. 5(c) sensitive data; FDPIC enforcement priority; reputational risk from breach
4. **AI tool evaluation** — no trusted framework to assess which AI tools are compliant, safe, effective; only 42% have formal governance (MGMA)
5. **Regulatory uncertainty** — Federal Council Feb 2025 confirmed no comprehensive AI law; sector-specific rules evolve; physicians unsure what applies

### Secondary Pain Points
- **Professional liability** — who is liable when AI-assisted decision is wrong?
- **Swissmedic device classification** — is the AI tool I use a "medical device" requiring registration?
- **Staff training** — how to bring team along on AI adoption without risk
- **Swiss AI sovereignty** — CHUV Meditron signals Swiss institutional AI; small practices need to understand implications
- **Data quality** — AI systems need good data; small practices have fragmented records
- **Legacy IT infrastructure** — outdated practice management systems don't integrate with modern AI
