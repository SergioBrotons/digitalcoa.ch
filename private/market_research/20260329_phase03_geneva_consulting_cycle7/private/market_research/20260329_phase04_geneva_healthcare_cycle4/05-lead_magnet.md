## Framework Structure

### Section 1: Why AI Governance Matters Now (Geneva + Vaud Context)
**EN:**
The gap between "using AI" and "governing AI" is where risk lives. In Geneva and Vaud, nearly 40% of physicians plan retirement within 5 years, medical assistant wages are the highest in Switzerland, and the Federal Council has confirmed: there will be no comprehensive Swiss AI law. Instead, existing rules — the nFADP, Swissmedic, and cantonal health law — are your operative standard.

The FDPIC's 2024/2025 Annual Report (July 2025) confirms that nFADP enforcement has begun — healthcare data breaches are a priority area. The FDPIC has concluded initial proceedings under the new law. Only 42% of Swiss medical groups have formal AI governance policies (MGMA, January 2026). The swissdamed mandatory registration deadline is July 1, 2026.

Meanwhile, institutional AI adoption is accelerating: CHUV (Lausanne) is testing Meditron, a Swiss medical LLM, in its emergency room from May 2026. This signals where Swiss healthcare AI is heading — and small practices will face the same governance questions, without the institutional support.

**Key Points:**

### Section 2: The 3 Axes of Medical Practice AI Governance

**Axis 1: nFADP Compliance — Patient Data Protection**
Health data is the most sensitive category under Swiss law (Art. 5(c)). This axis covers:
- Identifying AI tools that process patient data
- Data Processing Agreement (DPA) requirements
- Privacy notice updates
- Consent framework for AI-assisted processing
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) — when required
- Breach notification: "as soon as possible" vs. GDPR 72h
- FDPIC enforcement reality: healthcare data breaches are a priority (Annual Report Jul 2025)

**Axis 2: Swissmedic Compliance — Medical Device Rules**
If your AI tools qualify as medical devices (Software as Medical Device / SaMD), Swissmedic rules apply. This axis covers:
- How to determine if your AI tool is a medical device
- swissdamed registration — what it means for you as an operator
- The July 1, 2026 compliance deadline (~15 months)
- EU AI Act medical device extension (August 2027) — indirect relevance
- CHUV Meditron context: institutional AI as medical device classification is live in Vaud
- Professional liability: who is responsible for AI-assisted decisions

**Axis 3: Operational Efficiency — The Business Case**
AI governance isn't just about compliance — it's about sustainable practice management. This axis covers:
- Medical assistant shortage: efficiency AI as succession planning
- Clause du besoin July 2025: protecting your practice asset
- CHUV Meditron: what Swiss institutional AI adoption means for small practices
- AI tool evaluation framework: what to ask vendors
- Staff training: bringing your team along on AI adoption
- Geneva Health Forum 2026 + AI for Good Summit July 7-10, 2026: what's coming in healthcare AI

### Section 3: 7-Point nFADP Compliance Checklist

**For Small Geneva/Vaud Medical Practices**

1. **AI Tool Inventory:** List every AI tool that processes patient data (scheduling, dictation, billing, communication, image analysis)
2. **DPA Execution:** Confirm each AI vendor has signed a Data Processing Agreement meeting nFADP Art. 32 requirements
3. **Privacy Notice Update:** Confirm your patient privacy notice discloses AI tool usage (Art. 26 disclosure obligations)
4. **Consent Review:** Assess whether AI-assisted processing requires explicit patient consent (Art. 5(c) + Art. 6 legal basis)
5. **Swissmedic Classification:** Determine whether your AI tools qualify as medical devices (SaMD assessment)
6. **swissdamed Status:** Verify your AI tool vendors have registered in swissdamed (mandatory July 1, 2026)
7. **Staff Training:** Ensure all staff using AI tools understand basic governance requirements

### Section 4: The AI Readiness Self-Assessment

**Quick Diagnostic (5 Questions)**

1. Do you have a written list of all AI tools used in your practice? (Yes / No / Not sure)
2. Have you signed Data Processing Agreements with all AI tool vendors? (Yes / No / Some / Not sure)
3. Have you updated your patient privacy notice to disclose AI tool usage? (Yes / No / Not sure)
4. Do you know whether your AI tools qualify as medical devices under Swissmedic rules? (Yes / No / Not sure)
5. Are your staff trained on AI governance and data protection obligations? (Yes / Some training / No)

**Scoring:**
- 5 "Yes" — Strong position; schedule a review
- 3–4 "Yes" — Moderate gaps; schedule a discovery call
- 1–2 "Yes" — Significant gaps; prioritize within 90 days
- 0–1 "Yes" — Critical gaps; schedule immediately
