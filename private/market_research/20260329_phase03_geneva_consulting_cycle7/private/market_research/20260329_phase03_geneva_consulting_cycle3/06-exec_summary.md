## ICP Summary — Geneva Consulting

**Target:** Small Geneva consulting firms (2–15 employees, CHF 500K–5M revenue), managed by experienced partners aged 40–60.

**Core paradox:** 34% of Swiss SMEs now use AI and 57% report efficiency gains. But using AI tools without a DPA = potential nFADP violation. The FDPIC confirmed this explicitly on May 8, 2025. For a consulting firm, client confidentiality is the core asset — and it's the thing most at risk from unmanaged AI tool use.

**The IT/tech consultant sub-segment** (~25% of ICP) is the highest-risk/highest-conversion segment within Phase 03. They use AI most intensively; their data (code, architecture, technical docs) is high-sensitivity; they understand technology risk but not regulatory risk.

**Key phrases:**
- EN: *"34% of Swiss SMEs use AI. 57% report efficiency gains. But the FDPIC confirmed on May 8, 2025: using AI tools without proper data agreements is a compliance problem under Swiss law."*
- FR: *"34 % des PME suisses utilisent l'IA. 57 % constatent des gains d'efficacité. Mais le FDPIC a confirmé le 8 mai 2025 : utiliser des outils d'IA sans contrats de données appropriés est un problème de conformité en vertu du droit suisse."*
