## EN Version: The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question

### A 3-Decision Framework for Geneva Attorneys

---

**The question every Geneva attorney needs to answer before using AI:**

*What client data is your AI tool processing, where is it going, and what do you have to tell your clients?*

---

### Background

On May 8, 2025, Switzerland's Federal Data Protection Commissioner (FDPIC) confirmed what many attorneys suspected: the Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) applies directly to AI-based data processing.

On June 2024, the Swiss Bar Association (SBA) issued AI Guidelines requiring attorneys to maintain professional secrecy, ensure human oversight, and establish clear policies for AI tool use.

Together, these two documents set the standard of care for Geneva attorneys in 2025–2026.

The good news: compliance is a process, not a revolution. Most small Geneva law firms can get to a defensible AI governance position with three clear decisions.

---

### Decision 1: What data are you processing?

Before you deploy any AI tool, map the data flows.

- What client data will the AI tool access or process?
- Is it personal data under nFADP (anything that identifies a person)?
- Does the AI tool store, train on, or share this data?
- Where are the servers — Switzerland, EU, or elsewhere?

**What you need:** A data flow map for each AI tool used in your practice.

**The nFADP anchor:** Art. 5(c) — processing must have a specified purpose. Art. 8 — adequate data security. If you don't know where data goes, you can't confirm security.

---

### Decision 2: Where is the data going?

AI vendors vary widely on data residency and sub-processing.

- Does the AI vendor have a Privacy Policy that covers your use case?
- Have you signed a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with the vendor?
- Is client data used to train the AI model?
- Can the vendor demonstrate compliance with nFADP?

**What you need:** A vendor assessment for each AI tool. At minimum: Privacy Policy + DPA.

**The nFADP anchor:** Art. 9 — data processing by third parties requires a written contract. Art. 16 — cross-border transfers require adequate protection.

**The SBA anchor:** The SBA specifically recommends legal-specific AI tools over public consumer AI models. If you're using ChatGPT without a DPA, you have work to do.

---

### Decision 3: What do your clients need to know?

Client consent and transparency obligations under nFADP and SBA Guidelines.

- Have you disclosed your AI tool usage in your engagement letter?
- Does your Privacy Policy cover AI-assisted legal services?
- Are there matters where you should obtain explicit client consent before using AI?
- Is your AI use policy documented and available to clients on request?

**What you need:** A revised engagement letter template with AI disclosure language. A firm AI use policy.

**The FDPIC anchor:** May 2025 guidance requires transparency about AI processing — purpose, functionality, data sources. The SBA anchor: informed consent may be required before using AI with client data.

---

### The Compliance Checklist

**AI Governance Readiness — Geneva Small Law Firm**

Before deploying any AI tool:

- [ ] Identify all AI tools used in the firm (internal staff + external contractors)
- [ ] Map what client data each tool accesses or processes
- [ ] Confirm each vendor has a nFADP-compliant Privacy Policy
- [ ] Sign a Data Processing Agreement with each vendor
- [ ] Conduct a DPIA for any high-risk AI processing (Art. 22 nFADP)
- [ ] Review SBA AI Guidelines (June 2024) obligations
- [ ] Assess whether client consent is needed before AI use on their matter
- [ ] Draft/revise engagement letter to include AI disclosure
- [ ] Establish a written firm AI use policy
- [ ] Assign responsibility for AI governance oversight

---

### The Timeline

**Now (2025–2026):** nFADP + SBA Guidelines + FDPIC guidance = the operative standard.

**End 2026 (expected):** FDJP consultation draft for CoE AI Convention implementation. New obligations will emerge as Switzerland integrates the Convention into national law.

**The window:** Firms that establish defensible AI governance practices now will be ahead of the 2026 implementation wave. Firms that wait for the law will be playing catch-up.

## FR Version: La Question de la Gouvernance de l'IA pour les Petits Cabinets Juridiques

### Les 3 décisions pour les avocats genevois

**Contexte réglementaire:** PFPDT (8 mai 2025) + Directives du Barreau suisse (juin 2024)

**Décision 1 : Quelles données traite votre outil d'IA ?**
Cartographier les flux de données. Identifier les données personnelles des clients accessibles à l'outil.

**Décision 2 : Où vont les données ?**
Évaluer la conformité du fournisseur. Politique de confidentialité + contrat de traitement des données (CTD).

**Décision 3 : Que devez-vous communiquer à vos clients ?**
Obligations de transparence. Consentement éclairé. Clause IA dans la lettre de mission.
