## Compliance Context: Geneva Legal AI Governance

### Operative Regulatory Framework (2025–2026)

**1. Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP) — in force since September 1, 2023**
Technology-neutral. Applies to any processing of personal data, including AI-based processing. Key obligations for law firms:
- Lawful basis for processing (typically legitimate interest or consent)
- Privacy policy (AI tool usage must be disclosed)
- Data subject rights (access, correction, deletion)
- Data security (Art. 8 nFADP) — AI tool vendors must be vetted
- DPIA (Data Protection Impact Assessment) for high-risk AI processing (Art. 22 nFADP)
- Breach notification "as soon as possible" (not 72 hours like GDPR — Swiss specificity)

**2. FDPIC Guidance (May 8, 2025) — AI + nFADP**
The Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner issued explicit guidance confirming:
- nFADP applies directly to AI-based data processing
- Transparency required: purpose, functionality, and data sources of AI processing must be disclosed
- AI applications designed to undermine privacy or informational self-determination are prohibited
- Data controllers (law firms) remain responsible for AI tool compliance

**3. Swiss Bar Association AI Guidelines (June 2024)**
Non-binding but authoritative. Key provisions for attorneys:
- Professional secrecy: client data processed by AI tools must remain confidential
- Self-learning AI systems pose particular risk of inadvertent disclosure
- Human oversight required: attorneys remain professionally liable for AI-assisted work
- Informed consent may be required before using third-party AI programs with client data
- Secure, legal-specific AI tools recommended over public consumer AI models
- Law firms must establish clear AI use policies and provide staff training

**4. Council of Europe AI Convention (signed March 27, 2025)**
Switzerland signed. Ratification and implementation through Swiss law expected after 2026. Legislative consultation draft from FDJP expected end-2026.

**5. Federal Council AI Strategy (February 2025)**
Lean regulatory approach: sector-specific + cross-sector fundamental rights. No standalone Swiss AI Act planned.

## Compliance Obligations: Small Geneva Law Firm AI Checklist

**Before adopting any AI tool:**

- [ ] Identify what personal/client data the AI tool will process
- [ ] Confirm the AI vendor has a compliant Privacy Policy (Art. 19 nFADP)
- [ ] Sign a Data Processing Agreement with the AI vendor (Art. 9 nFADP)
- [ ] Conduct a DPIA if the AI processing is high-risk (Art. 22 nFADP)
- [ ] Assess whether professional secrecy obligations (SBA Guidelines) are triggered
- [ ] Determine if client informed consent is required before using AI with their data
- [ ] Establish internal AI use policy for the firm

**Ongoing obligations:**

- [ ] Monitor AI tool data flows and vendor compliance
- [ ] Report data breaches "as soon as possible" (nFADP Art. 24)
- [ ] Maintain human oversight of all AI-assisted legal work
- [ ] Document AI tool usage in client matter files

## Geneva-Specific Considerations

**Geneva Bar Association:** Follows SBA national guidelines. Geneva attorneys are bound by SBA professional conduct standards, including AI use guidelines.

**Cross-border data:** If AI tool processes data on US/EU servers, cross-border transfer obligations under nFADP Art. 16 may apply.

**Professional privilege:** Different from professional secrecy. Attorney-client communications enjoy protection, but this does not extend to all firm data. AI tool usage can affect privilege claims.
