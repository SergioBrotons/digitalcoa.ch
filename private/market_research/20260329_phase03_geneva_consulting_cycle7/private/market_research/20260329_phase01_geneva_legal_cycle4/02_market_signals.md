## Market Signals: Geneva Legal AI Governance

### Regulatory Horizon (Updated Cycle 4)

**Consultation draft for CoE AI Convention implementation — expected end-2026**
The Federal Department of Justice and Police (FDJP) is preparing the legislative proposal for CoE AI Convention integration. This will be submitted for consultation by end-2026. The focus areas: transparency, data protection, non-discrimination, oversight in AI contexts. This is the key regulatory event for 2026.

**Operative framework through 2026:** nFADP + SBA Guidelines + FDPIC guidance = unchanged from Cycle 3.

**Sector-specific approach confirmed:** Switzerland continues to regulate AI primarily through existing laws (data protection, financial supervision, professional standards) rather than a standalone AI Act. For attorneys: SBA Guidelines + nFADP is the operative standard.

**ANBLES AI Working Group Report (February 2026):** The Swiss Legal Professionals Association (ANBLES) published an AI Working Group Report in February 2026. This document represents the Swiss legal community's active engagement with AI governance — beyond mere curiosity. Geneva attorneys who attend SBA events or follow ANBLES publications are already aware AI governance is coming.

## Geneva Legal Market Signals

**Geneva Bar:** No separate AI guidelines from the Geneva Bar specifically; Geneva attorneys follow SBA national guidelines. Geneva Bar Association activities (continuing education, events) increasingly cover AI topics.

**SBA Congress Lucerne 2025:** Legal community shifted from curiosity to cautious implementation. The ABA also published AI guidance in March 2026 (US market; illustrative of global norm acceleration).

**Swiss legal tech ecosystem:** Clio (legal practice management), LegalTech GPT, Kira Systems (contract review), Relativity (e-discovery) are present in Geneva. Small firms using these tools without understanding data flows = nFADP exposure.

**Client expectations:** Corporate clients increasingly asking law firms about AI usage, data security, and confidentiality protocols in RFPs and engagement letters.

## Market Awareness Gap

**The gap:** Most small Geneva law firms (1–8 attorneys) are aware AI exists but have not systematically assessed:
1. What client data their AI tools process
2. Whether their AI vendors are DPA-compliant
3. Whether they need a DPIA before deploying AI
4. What their SBA professional secrecy obligations mean in practice for AI tools

**The opportunity:** DigitalCoa.ch fills the gap between free SBA guidelines and expensive large-firm advice. The 3-decision framework (Data Classification → Data Location → Client Notification) maps directly to nFADP + SBA requirements.
