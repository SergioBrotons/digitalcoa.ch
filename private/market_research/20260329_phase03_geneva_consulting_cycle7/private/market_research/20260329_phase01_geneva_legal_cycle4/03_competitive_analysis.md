## What Competitors Are Doing

**Large international firms (CMS, Pestalozzi, etc.):**
- Publishing AI regulation guides and client alerts
- Hosting webinars on EU AI Act implications
- Advising multinationals on cross-border AI compliance
- NOT targeting small Geneva law firms

**SBA / ANBLES:**
- Issuing non-binding guidance and working group reports
- Educating through congresses and publications
- NOT providing hands-on AI governance advisory to individual firms

**LegalTech vendors (Clio, Kira, etc.):**
- Selling AI tools to law firms
- NOT providing governance frameworks or compliance advisory
- Terms of service typically place compliance responsibility on the firm

**DigitalCoa.ch differentiator:**
- Boutique, Geneva-focused, small firm-native
- Speaks both data protection (nFADP/FDPIC) and professional standards (SBA)
- Provides hands-on AI governance assessment and roadmap
- Price point calibrated for small firm economics (CHF 8K–15K assessment)

## Competitive Risks to Monitor

1. **Large firm pivot:** CMS or Pestalozzi launches a small-firm-specific AI governance product at CHF 5K–10K. (Low probability near-term; would require significant market education investment.)

2. **LegalTech vendor adds compliance layer:** Clio or similar adds built-in AI governance compliance tools. (Low probability — not their core competency.)

3. **New boutique entrant:** A former Big Law attorney launches targeted AI governance advisory for Geneva small firms. (Medium probability; worth monitoring through 2026.)

4. **SBA provides free advisory:** The bar association creates a practical implementation guide that answers most small firm questions. (Low probability — SBA is a professional body, not a service delivery organization.)

**Current risk level: LOW** — no immediate competitive threat. Window likely 18–24 months before competitive landscape shifts materially.
