### SECTION 1: Geneva Real Estate AI Reality Check
**Hook:** Real estate has the highest AI adoption rate of any vertical in our portfolio — 67% of Swiss RE professionals are using AI tools. But Geneva small agencies have almost no AI governance framework. The question is not whether AI is being used. The question is whether the AI use is compliant — and most of it is not.

- 67% AI adoption — highest of any portfolio vertical (Phase 06 Cycle 1)
- GAIGI operative since Fall 2025 (~1-5 months) — institutional framework now live
- nFADP operative since September 1, 2023 — AI processing of client personal data = obligations triggered
- EU AI Act August 2, 2026 (~15 months) — agencies with EU market nexus face dual obligations
- Retroactive compliance is the unique RE challenge — most agencies have already been using AI for months/years without governance

**AI processing of personal data = nFADP Art. 5(c) triggered:**
- Client names, contact details
- Property data (which may contain personal information)
- Transaction history
- Financial information related to clients
- Tenant/buyer/seller personal data

---

### SECTION 4: Do You Have nFADP Obligations?
**If you've used AI with any client data:** Yes. Retroactively. nFADP has been operative since September 1, 2023.

**The retroactive compliance path:** You cannot undo past non-compliance. But you can:
1. Document the AI tools you've been using
2. Assess what client data they've processed
3. Implement DPIA-lite for each tool going forward
4. Establish a compliant AI governance framework now

---

### SECTION 5: GAIGI + EU AI Act Context
**GAIGI operative Fall 2025 (~1-5 months):** The Geneva real estate industry body framework is live. Small agencies should be aligning with GAIGI standards now.

**EU AI Act August 2, 2026 (~15 months):** RE agencies with EU market nexus (international listings, EU-based clients) face EU AI Act obligations.

---

### The 4-Step Retroactive Compliance Path

**Step 1 — Inventory (now):** Document every AI tool used, what data it accessed, for how long.

**Step 2 — Assess (within 30 days):** Which AI tools processed personal data without DPIA? Which clients were affected?

**Step 3 — Remediate (within 60 days):** Complete DPIA-lite for each AI tool. Implement data subject rights processes.

**Step 4 — Govern (ongoing):** Establish ongoing AI governance framework aligned with GAIGI. Document for future FDPIC inquiries.
