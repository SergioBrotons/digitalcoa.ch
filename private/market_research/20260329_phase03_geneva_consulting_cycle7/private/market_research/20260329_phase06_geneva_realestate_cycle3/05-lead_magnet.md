### SECTION 2: Q1 — What AI Are You Using? (The Honest Inventory)
**Question:** Which AI tools does your agency use for: listing descriptions, photo enhancement, pricing analytics, chatbot/lead response, tenant screening, contract drafting, marketing?

**Most common Geneva RE AI tool stack:**
- Listing description generation (ChatGPT, specialized RE AI tools)

**For each tool: Do you know if it processes personal data?** (Tenant names, contact info, financial info, property details = personal data under nFADP)

### SECTION 3: Q2 — What Data Is Being Processed?
**Question:** When your AI tools process listing data, tenant applications, buyer profiles, or transaction records — is that data being sent to cloud servers? Used for training? Shared with third parties?

**Key RE data risks:**
- Tenant application data (names, income, employment, references) = sensitive personal data
- Buyer financial profiles = personal data
- Property details with owner information = personal data linked to identifiable persons
- Communication records with AI tools = may include personal data

**AI vendor reality:** Most AI tools process your data on cloud servers (often US-based). Your nFADP obligations as data controller apply regardless of where the AI vendor processes data.

---

### SECTION 4: Q3 — Do You Have nFADP Obligations?
**Question:** Has your agency completed a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) under nFADP Article 22 for your AI tools?

**If NO:** Your agency is likely in breach of nFADP, which has been operative since September 1, 2023.

**The nFADP baseline for RE agencies:**
- Tenant and buyer data is personal data under nFADP Art. 3
- AI processing of this data = Art. 5(c) trigger
- DPIA required before using AI systems that process personal data (Art. 22)
- Data subject rights (Art. 8–13) must be implemented

**FDPIC enforcement trajectory:** FDPIC 2024/2025 Annual Report (July 2025) confirms FDPIC monitoring all sectors. RE is not currently a priority sector, but enforcement can catch up to adoption.

---

### SECTION 5: The Human Accountability Principle
**For real estate work — human accountability cannot be delegated to AI.**

Key principle: Every AI-assisted output in a real estate transaction must be reviewed and approved by a licensed agent before use.

- AI-generated listing descriptions: agent reviews before publication
- AI-generated pricing estimates: agent validates before client presentation
- AI tenant screening recommendations: agent reviews before making decisions
- AI chatbot responses to tenant/buyer inquiries: agent oversight of AI communication

**Why this matters:** RE agents are professionally liable for their advice and decisions. AI-generated content that leads to a bad outcome is the agent's liability — not the AI vendor's. Document your human review process.
