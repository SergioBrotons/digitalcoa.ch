# Phase 02 Regulatory Context — Geneva Finance / Institutional & Private Banking
## Cycle 122 (2026-04-04)

## Key Regulatory Frameworks Relevant to Geneva Finance

This document outlines the critical regulatory considerations impacting Geneva's financial institutions, as identified for Cycle 122. The focus is on frameworks directly influencing liquidity, counterparty risk, compliance, institutional markets, sustainable finance distribution, and operational capacity.

### 1. Swiss National Bank (SNB) Monetary Policy & Liquidity Management

*   **Framework:** SNB policy rate, quantitative easing/tightening measures, balance sheet operations.
*   **Status:** Active balance sheet normalization phase (ongoing 2025-2026).
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Reduced CHF liquidity in the interbank market, increased short-term funding costs for banks, pressure on deposit pricing, potential for money market stress. Institutions must adapt liquidity management and funding strategies.

### 2. Basel III Framework & Counterparty Credit Risk (SA-CCR)

*   **Framework:** Basel Committee on Banking Supervision standards, implemented by FINMA. Standardised Approach for Counterparty Credit Risk (SA-CCR).
*   **Status:** Fully implemented, with ongoing monitoring.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Procyclical margin calls from CCPs (e.g., SIX x-clear) under volatile market conditions directly increase counterparty credit exposures, thereby driving up capital requirements under SA-CCR for repo and securities financing desks. This requires sophisticated collateral management and risk hedging.

### 3. Anti-Money Laundering Act (AMLA) & Anti-Money Laundering Ordinance (AMLO)

*   **Framework:** Swiss Federal Act on Combating Money Laundering and Terrorist Financing (AMLA), revised.
*   **Status:** Parliament adopted September 2025. Consultation on implementing ordinances (LETO, AMLO) concluded January 30, 2026. Entry into force expected **H2 2026** (~3-6 months from April 2026) after potential referendum.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Significantly enhanced due diligence obligations, particularly for beneficial ownership verification and source of wealth. **New 30-day discrepancy reporting obligation** to the Transparency Register. Expanded scope for "advisors" (including wealth managers, trustees, CSPs) requiring FINMA-recognized SRO membership. FINMA enforcement patterns are active.

### 4. Transparency Register and Due Diligence Ordinance (LETA)

*   **Framework:** Federal Act on the establishment of a register of beneficial owners of legal entities and trusts.
*   **Status:** Implementing ordinances consolidated with AMLA; expected to enter into force in **H2 2026**.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Mandates the identification and reporting of beneficial owners to a central register. Institutions must have robust processes to ascertain and verify beneficial ownership for their clients and report any discrepancies within 30 days of discovery.

### 5. FINMA Supervisory & Enforcement Powers

*   **Framework:** Swiss Financial Market Supervisory Authority (FINMA) mandates.
*   **Status:** Active and evolving. Notably, FINMA is focusing enforcement on specific areas: 
    *   **AML/Sanctions:** Failures in enhanced due diligence for UHNW individuals, sanctioned persons, and complex ownership structures.
    *   **AI Governance:** While guidance issued Dec 2024, FINMA expects supervised entities to demonstrate risk management and governance for AI applications.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Direct enforcement risk, reputational damage, operational costs for remediation, and potential for capital surcharges if AML/compliance programs are deemed inadequate.

### 6. Swiss Pension Fund Legislation (BVG)

*   **Framework:** Federal Law on Occupational Old Age and Survivors' Insurance (BVG), including ordinances (e.g., BVV 2).
*   **Status:** Ongoing structural changes and regulatory pressures.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Pension fund consolidation is driven by regulatory compliance costs, investment complexity, and actuarial challenges. This creates opportunities for asset managers to acquire mandates from consolidated entities, requiring understanding of fiduciary duties and specific investment regulations for pension assets.

### 7. Sustainable Finance Regulations & Taxonomy

*   **Frameworks:** 
    *   **Swiss Sustainable Finance Taxonomy:** Voluntary, science-based (Federal Council action plan, 2021). 
    *   **EU Taxonomy Regulation & SFDR:** Legally binding for EU entities, extraterritorial impact for Swiss firms with EU market nexus.
    *   **MiFID II Sustainability Preferences:** German implementation requires specific suitability checks for sustainable products.
*   **Status:** EU Taxonomy operative, SFDR Article 6/8/9 requirements in effect, German implementation active.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** Divergence between Swiss and EU/German classifications creates product labeling confusion and distribution friction for asset managers selling into the DACH region. Firms must navigate differing disclosure requirements, and potentially reclassify or dual-label products.

### 8. Swissdamed (Healthcare Data Protection)

*   **Framework:** Federal Act on Data Protection in the Health Sector.
*   **Status:** Enters into force **July 1, 2026** (~12 weeks away).
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** While healthcare-specific, it reinforces the trend towards stringent data protection in Switzerland. Any Geneva financial institution processing health-related client data (e.g., in estate planning, family office contexts) must ensure compliance.

### 9. Trade Finance Regulations & Best Practices

*   **Frameworks:** UCP 600 (Uniform Customs and Practice for Documentary Credits), ISBP (International Standard Banking Practice), relevant Basel standards for trade finance risk.
*   **Status:** Evolving best practices and regulatory expectations.
*   **Implications for Geneva Finance:** While not a new regulation, the shortage of experienced personnel means that adherence to, and understanding of, these complex international standards is critical. Banks and trading houses need expertise to manage risk effectively in the current environment.

## Cycle 122 Regulatory Highlights

*   **H2 2026 Compliance Cluster:** AMLA/LETA and Swissdamed converge, requiring coordinated preparation.
*   **SNB Normalization:** A fundamental shift in the CHF monetary environment impacting all banking operations.
*   **CCP Margin & Capital Link:** The direct interplay between market volatility, CCP margin calls, and Basel III SA-CCR capital requirements is a critical risk factor.
*   **DACH Taxonomy Gap:** A persistent issue for cross-border distribution, demanding specialized advisory.
*   **FINMA Enforcement Focus:** A clear signal for immediate AML compliance reviews in private banking.
