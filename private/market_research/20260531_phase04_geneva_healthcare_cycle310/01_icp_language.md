## How Physicians Talk About AI Problems (C310)

### Primary ICP Language Pattern

**The Core Frame (C310 confirmed):** "I deployed AI tools in my practice — and now I'm realizing I don't know what I was actually responsible for."

This is distinct from prior cycles. In C308-C309, the frame was "I signed a form." In C310, the frame has shifted to "I implemented something and I don't have the documentation trail." The retroactive exposure is now the primary ICP anxiety.

### The Six Simultaneous Pressures (C310 Frame)

C310 identifies six pressures that are distinct from prior cycle findings:

1. **Retroactive AI deployment exposure**
   - Physician language: "I started using AI diagnostic support tools in 2023. I didn't document anything at the time. Now carriers are asking specific questions — and I don't have a single record from when I actually started."
   - This is distinct from C308-C309: the problem is no longer "I'm adopting AI going forward" — it's "I adopted AI 12–24 months ago and the documentation doesn't exist."
   - The July 1, 2026 swissdamed registration deadline adds specific urgency to this retroactive gap.

2. **Clinical reasoning accountability gap**
   - Physician language: "The AI suggested a diagnosis. I agreed with it. I documented my agreement. But what do I actually document about how much weight I gave the AI's input? How do I prove my clinical reasoning was actually mine?"
   - This is distinct from administrative AI use (scheduling, billing). Clinical reasoning AI creates a different accountability structure — Swissmedic inquiries about diagnostic decisions will ask about the AI's role in the reasoning chain.

3. **nFADP data minimization conflict**
   - Physician language: "My AI diagnostic tool needs full patient history to work. But nFADP says I should only process data that's necessary. I don't know how to resolve this — the tool requires everything, the law says minimize."
   - This is distinct from the carrier attestation frame. nFADP Article 6 data minimization is now an active physician concern for AI-enabled diagnostic workflows.

4. **FMH human oversight silence**
   - Physician language: "I asked FMH directly what 'human oversight' means in my practice. They pointed me to the principle. They didn't give me a protocol. So I'm implementing something with no reference standard — and if something goes wrong, I'm the one who'll be judged."
   - This is distinct from C308-C309: the problem has moved from "no standard published" to "I've tried to get clarity and was referred back to the principle."

5. **Real-time patient encounter documentation gap**
   - Physician language: "During the patient encounter, I'm supposed to be thinking clinically — not documenting AI involvement. But if I don't capture something in the moment, it doesn't exist. The documentation gap happens in real time, during the consultation."
   - This is the operational documentation gap — not the administrative record, but the clinical reasoning capture in real time.

6. **AI tool tiering without a framework**
   - Physician language: "I use AI for scheduling, for note documentation, for diagnostic support, for patient communication. They're all different tools. Are they all creating the same liability? Or are some riskier than others? I have no way to tier them."
   - Physicians are adopting AI tools across different risk categories without any framework for differentiating their documentation obligations.

### The Physician-to-Physician Channel (C310)

Physicians learn about AI accountability failures through peer networks:

- **How physicians hear about problems:** "At the last AMGe event, three colleagues mentioned carriers had sent them new forms. One said Swissmedic sent an inquiry about a diagnostic AI tool. Word travels fast."
- **How physicians share solutions:** Medical society conversations, AMGe network, WhatsApp groups of Geneva physicians — not broker channels, not consulting firms.
- **Trust formation:** "I trust what my colleague tells me from their actual experience. I don't trust a vendor's brochure or a consultant's presentation."
- **Decision trigger:** "When a colleague I respect tells me she had to respond to a Swissmedic inquiry about her AI tool, I go home and check my own situation."

### The Peer Question Sequence (C310)

1. "When did you start using AI diagnostic tools in your practice? Do you have documentation from that period?"
2. "What did you actually document about your clinical reasoning when AI was involved in the diagnosis?"
3. "Does your AI tool require more patient data than you would normally collect? Did you consider the data minimization question?"
4. "Have you asked FMH what human oversight means in practice? What did they tell you?"
5. "What would you show Swissmedic if they asked about a specific patient case where your AI tool was involved?"
6. "Do you tier your AI tools by risk? Or do you treat them all the same?"

### The Evidence Language (C310)

Physicians in C310 do not respond to "AI governance framework." They respond to:

- "Documentation of what you actually did — not what you intended to do"
- "Evidence of your clinical reasoning when AI was involved"
- "A record from when you started using the AI, not from when you got the carrier form"
- "Evidence that your judgment was in the loop — not just your signature on a policy"
- "A format that Swissmedic would recognize if they asked about a specific case"
- "Documentation that doesn't require you to stop practicing to produce"

### The Personal License Frame (C310 confirmed)

**Core positioning anchor (C310 confirmed):** "For physicians, evidence is personal."

This is confirmed as the highest-stakes vertical anchor. A wealth manager's license can be suspended. A physician's license — the patient safety consequences are immediate, and the license is irreplaceable.

### What Physicians Are NOT Saying (C310)

Physicians are not saying:
- "I need an AI governance framework" — this is consultant language
- "I want to understand the regulatory landscape" — abstract, not personal
- "Can you help me with my AI strategy?" — not the immediate concern
- "I need a comprehensive compliance program" — not the frame

Physicians ARE saying (C310 confirmed):
- "I started using AI 18 months ago. I have no documentation from that period. What do I do now?"
- "How do I document my clinical reasoning when AI suggested the diagnosis — do I just say I agreed?"
- "My AI tool needs everything. nFADP says minimize. I don't know how to handle this."
- "I asked FMH what human oversight means and they gave me back the principle — no protocol."
- "During the patient encounter, I'm thinking clinically, not documenting AI involvement — but if I don't capture it in the moment, it doesn't exist."
- "I use four different AI tools. Are they all creating the same exposure?"

Geneva physicians operate in both French and English. The AMGe network is French-dominant. International physicians (WHO, UN, HUG) operate in English.

- "L'outil d'IA diagnostic nécessite l'historique complet du patient. Mais la nFADP dit minimiser les données. Je ne sais pas comment résoudre ce conflit."
- "Le PFPDT m'a confirmé que la supervision humaine est requise. Mais il ne m'a pas dit ce que je dois documenter concrètement."

**English physician language sample:**

- "The AI tool requires full patient history to function. But nFADP says minimize data. I don't know how to reconcile this."
- "I asked FMH what human oversight means in practice. They referred me back to the principle. No protocol."

### Decision Trigger Sequence (C310)

1. **Retroactive gap activation:** Carrier asks specific question about AI use in 2023-2024 — physician has no documentation from that period.
2. **swissdamed July 1, 2026 deadline:** Real deadline now visible on calendar — registration obligations still unclear.
3. **Peer Swissmedic inquiry:** Colleague received Swissmedic inquiry about diagnostic AI tool — peer network shares details.
4. **Clinical incident with AI involvement:** Patient outcome raised questions about AI's role in diagnosis — documentation gap becomes acute.
5. **Carrier attestation renewal:** New form with physician-personal certification language arrives.
6. **Medical society discussion:** AMGe event where peers compare notes on what they're documenting.

### The Urgency That Works (C310)

"The form your carrier sent you asks about AI use starting in 2023. You have no documentation from that period. That's the gap."

"Swissmedic doesn't ask if you have a framework. They ask: what did you document about your diagnostic reasoning when AI was involved?"

"For physicians, evidence is personal. And your license is on the line."

"Your AI tool needs full patient data. nFADP says minimize. That's a conflict you need to resolve before a patient incident — not after."
