### What Is Missing from the Market

**Boutique-scale AI governance advisory** — no firm offers:
- AI governance advisory specifically designed for 50–200 employee Geneva WM firms
- Managing director personal liability focus (Art. 63 nFADP)
- Institutional client questionnaire preparation support
- Carrier attestation response support
- FINMA AI guidance alignment without enterprise overhead
- SRO compliance integration at boutique scale

**Why the gap persists:**
- Large advisory firms (Big 4, Strategy&, Oliver Wyman) have boutique WM market below their minimum engagement scale
- Swiss law firms focus on legal compliance, not AI governance operational frameworks
- Technology vendors sell tools, not governance advisory
- Boutique consulting firms exist but have not focused specifically on Geneva WM + AI governance intersection

## NET-NEW Finding C298-P2-F3
**The "too small for Big 4, too specific for boutique consulting" gap:** Geneva WM firms (50–200 employees) fall between two market failures: too small for Big 4 engagement minimums, and too specific in their AI governance needs for general boutique consulting firms. This structural gap is why 7+ cycles have confirmed no competitor. The advisory offer must explicitly state: "Designed for the boutique WM firm that is too small for Big 4 and too specific for generalist advisors." This language directly addresses the ICP's experience of being caught between available options.
