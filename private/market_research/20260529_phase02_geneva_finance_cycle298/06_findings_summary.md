# Phase 02 Findings Summary — Geneva Finance / Boutique WM
## Cycle 298 (2026-05-29)

## NET-NEW Findings (Distinct from C214–C297)

### Finding C298-P2-F1: Institutional Client AI Governance Questionnaires as the Primary Forcing Function
**What it is:** The specific trigger for action is not abstract regulatory fear — it is receiving a specific 40-point questionnaire from an institutional client that the managing director cannot answer. This creates simultaneous client retention fear and personal embarrassment.

**Why it matters:** The advisory positioning should lead with "when your institutional client sends you that questionnaire" rather than "FINMA guidance." The former is a specific, immediate business threat. The latter is abstract regulatory anxiety. The ICP responds to specific.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your institutional client has sent you a 40-point AI governance questionnaire. Can you answer it?"

---

### Finding C298-P2-F2: Carrier Attestation Is a Distinct Forcing Function from Client Questionnaires
**What it is:** Client questionnaires create retention fear (revenue threat). Carrier attestations create coverage and cost fear (operational/cost threat). These are two separate forcing functions that hit at different times.

**Why it matters:** A managing director may act on a client questionnaire before acting on a carrier attestation, even though both are urgent. The advisory should address both — client questionnaire first (revenue), carrier attestation second (cost). The entry point should acknowledge both in sequence.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Two questionnaires are arriving at Geneva boutique wealth managers: one from your institutional clients (client retention), one from your carrier (coverage and premium). Most managing directors respond to the first and forget the second. Both matter."

---

### Finding C298-P2-F3: The "Too Small for Big 4, Too Specific for Boutique Consulting" Structural Gap
**What it is:** Geneva WM firms (50–200 employees) fall between two market failures: too small for Big 4 engagement minimums, and too specific in AI governance needs for general boutique consulting firms. This structural gap explains why no competitor has emerged across 7+ cycles.

**Why it matters:** The advisory offer must explicitly name this gap: "Designed for the boutique WM firm that is too small for Big 4 and too specific for generalist advisors." This language directly validates the ICP's experience of being caught between available options.

**Ready-to-use language:** "If Big 4 firms have returned your inquiry with a minimum engagement size you can't justify, and generalist consultants don't understand Swiss financial regulatory specificity — that's the gap I built DigitalCoa.ch to fill."

---

### Finding C298-P2-F4: SRO Silence Is a Distinct Pain Point from FINMA Silence
**What it is:** The ICP distinguishes between FINMA silence (which they somewhat expected — FINMA rarely publishes detailed guidance) and SRO silence (which they did not expect — SROs are supposed to provide practical operational guidance to their members). SRO silence creates a specific pain point: even their own industry body has abandoned them on AI governance.

**Why it matters:** The advisory offer's SRO alignment credentials are more valuable because of this distinction. Positioning as "we translate SRO expectations into practical boutique WM governance frameworks" directly addresses this pain point. The ICP wants someone who understands what the SRO should be saying even if it isn't saying it.

**Ready-to-use language:** "Your SRO has said nothing about AI governance. I understand why that feels like abandonment — they are supposed to provide practical guidance. I translate what SROs should be saying into frameworks that work for boutique-scale WM firms."

---

### Finding C298-P2-F5: Three-Layer Pressure Structure for Lead Magnet Framing
**What it is:** The ICP experiences three distinct pressure layers:
1. Institutional client questionnaire — immediate client retention threat
2. Carrier attestation — operational/cost/coverage threat
3. Personal liability under nFADP Art. 63 — emotional core that makes action non-optional

**Why it matters:** The lead magnet should structure content in this order: client questionnaire first, carrier attestation second, personal liability as the emotional framing that makes inaction feel dangerous. This matches the actual experience sequence of the ICP.

**Ready-to-use language:** "I help managing directors answer the institutional client AI governance questionnaire — and build the framework that covers carrier attestation and FINMA baseline simultaneously. Because when Art. 63 applies, it's your personal liability, not just the firm's."
