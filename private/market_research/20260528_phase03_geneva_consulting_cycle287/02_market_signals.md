# Market Signals — Geneva Consulting (C287 Phase 03)

## Current Problems / Complaints / Failures

### 1. Carrier Third-Gen Attestation — Personal Signature Without Defined Standard
MDs receive carrier attestation requiring personal signature. The form commits to "adequacy" against carrier-designed criteria with no Geneva consulting input into what adequacy means. No SVA equivalent exists to publish Geneva-specific interpretation. MD signs, creating personal liability, with no independent review pathway. The instrument functions as de facto operative standard for the sector precisely because no alternative standard-setter exists.

**What's failing:** MDs cannot obtain pre-signing review of what they are committing to personally. No Geneva service offers carrier attestation pre-signing review from the signatory's perspective.

### 2. Client AI Governance Questionnaires — Portfolio-Wide Exposure Recognition
Sophisticated clients — institutional counterparties, international organizations, diplomatic partners — are sending AI governance questionnaires as part of due diligence. The questionnaire operates as de facto standard-setting mechanism through first-responder effect: whatever the first questionnaire asks becomes the benchmark against which all subsequent responses are evaluated. Geneva consulting firms receiving these questionnaires face portfolio-wide obligation asymmetry. Responses prepared reactively under client pressure create representations that may not reflect firm practice.

**What's failing:** No Geneva consulting firm has portfolio-level AI governance questionnaire response infrastructure. Each questionnaire is handled reactively, creating accumulated inconsistencies across active client relationships.

### 3. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Chain — Structural Three-Party Exposure
Prime boutique contracts with client, delegates to sub-consultant who uses AI in deliverables. nFADP Art.9 third-party processing authorization absent. Accountability chain unmapped. Engagement letter standard does not address this configuration. Vendor terms sub-consultant operates under were not evaluated by prime boutique. MD is contractually accountable to client for deliverables produced by AI tools MD has not authorized or reviewed.

**What's failing:** No Geneva consulting firm has a documented sub-consultant AI delegation framework. The chain exists operationally with no documentation architecture for it.

### 4. AI Model Update Silent Propagation — Active Deliverable Analytical Drift
AI model updates occur silently. Active client deliverables may be based on analytical framework from model versions that no longer exist. No reassessment trigger protocol. No version-locked deliverable standard in engagement letters. No carrier questionnaire asks about it explicitly. MD cannot confirm what model version produced conclusions client still references.

**What's failing:** Geneva consulting firms have no version tracking architecture for AI-assisted deliverables. Active deliverables may be operating under analytical conditions that changed without firm awareness or client notification.

### 5. Proposal Defensibility Chain — Primary Documentation Gap
Every AI-assisted proposal in current client possession represents a documentation gap. Client holds proposals the firm cannot currently defend against methodology scrutiny. Carrier third-gen questionnaire asks specifically about three AI decisions in past 12 months — most Geneva consulting boutiques cannot identify which proposals would be in scope of such a question, let alone what review preceded each.

**What's failing:** No Geneva consulting firm has a proposal defensibility architecture that addresses what the firm would need to produce if a client or carrier asked how each active proposal's conclusions were reached and reviewed.

### 6. Analytical Framework Ownership — Intellectual Property and Accountability Exposure
AI tool accumulates prompt libraries, workflow configurations, client-specific model adaptations — institutional capital sitting in vendor infrastructure boutique does not own. Vendor uses accumulated capital to improve product for all users. Boutique bears production costs, retains liability, receives none of value contributed. Additionally: when vendor updates model, accumulated framework may change, eroding the boutique's ability to explain why a past recommendation was made in specific terms.

**What's failing:** Geneva consulting firms do not have AI institutional capital ownership architecture. The exposure is invisible until vendor model changes expose accumulated dependency.

## Signal Summary — What the Market Is Telling Us

The Geneva consulting sector is experiencing a structural accountability formation event. The carrier third-generation questionnaire is the most operationally specific standard-setting mechanism currently operating in the market — not because carriers intended to set sector standards, but because no Geneva consulting body exists to provide an alternative. MDs are signing personal attestations to adequacy against a standard that has not been defined for their sector. The documentation window is closing (mid-2026) with a burden-of-proof reversal: firms that did not build documentation architecture during the window will be explaining absence against arrived standards, not building compliance against forming standards.

The six problems above are structurally independent — each requires its own solution architecture. No Geneva market participant has built a coherent response to all six simultaneously. The market opportunity is not a single service but a coherent advisory framework that addresses all six in their structural interaction across an active client portfolio.
