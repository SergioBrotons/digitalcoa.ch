# ICP Language — Geneva Consulting (C287 Phase 03)

## ICP Profile
Small Geneva consulting boutique, 2–50 employees. Managing director or partner personally accountable for AI decisions in client engagements. No SVA equivalent, no ORDRE, no SFAMA. Carrier questionnaire is de facto operative standard for sector.

## Primary Entry Point Signals
- Client sends AI governance questionnaire requiring firm-level response
- Carrier sends third-generation attestation requiring personal MD signature
- Sub-consultant uses AI in deliverables without documented authorization chain
- Client asks how recommendations were produced (methodology question)
- AI model update changes active deliverable conclusions

## How Geneva Consulting MDs Describe AI Problems

### English
- "I'm signing something that says our AI governance is adequate — but I have no idea what adequate means for a consulting firm"
- "My client sent me a 40-page AI questionnaire. I don't know what half of it means for our work"
- "We use AI in proposals, in analysis, in research. When did I become responsible for all of it?"
- "I delegated to a sub-consultant. They used AI. The client is asking me how it worked. What do I say?"
- "The carrier wants to know what three AI decisions we made recently and how we reviewed them. We don't have that record."
- "My client is asking questions about our AI methodology that I can't answer because the engagement letter doesn't cover it"
- "I can't tell which parts of our proposals are us and which are AI — and my client is starting to notice"
- "Every time the AI model updates, I have to figure out whether anything we sent before is now based on different logic"

## Language Patterns That Signal ICP Awareness
- "Adequacy standard" — carrier attestation language, MD doesn't know what it means
- "AI delegation chain" — sub-consultant exposure, three-party accountability
- "Methodology defensibility" — client governance questionnaire trigger
- "Version state" — AI model update concern for active deliverables
- "Personal signature" — carrier attestation creates personal liability
- "Documentation window" — urgency framing for anticipatory action
- "Burden of proof" — how firm positions itself before vs after documentation close
- "De facto standard" — carrier questionnaire operating without Geneva consulting input

- "Please describe your firm's AI governance framework for client engagements"
- "What AI tools does your firm use in analysis and proposal development?"
- "How do you document the role of AI in your deliverables?"
- "Who is accountable for AI-assisted recommendations?"
- "What is your firm's policy on AI use by sub-consultants?"
- "How do you handle AI model updates affecting active client deliverables?"
- "Please provide evidence of human review of AI-assisted work"

## Carrier Attestation Language (Third-Generation)
- "The undersigned personally attests to the adequacy of the firm's AI governance"
- "Describe three AI-assisted decisions made in the past 12 months and the review process for each"
- "What methodology does the firm use to ensure AI outputs meet professional standards?"
- "How does the MD maintain accountability for AI-assisted deliverables?"

## Internal Vocabulary Deficit
MDs describe governance failures in terms of symptoms not categories:
- "We don't have a process" (not: we lack AI decision documentation architecture)
- "I don't know what the carrier wants" (not: operative adequacy standard undefined)
- "The AI changed something and we didn't catch it" (not: model version state drift in active deliverables)
- "We didn't authorize the sub-consultant's AI use" (not: third-party processor authorization absent)

## Entry Point Hierarchy (Geneva Consulting)
1. Carrier third-gen attestation requires MD personal signature (acute — forces action)
2. Client AI governance questionnaire requires firm-level response (acute — external pressure)
3. Sub-consultant AI delegation question from client (moderate — relationship trigger)
4. AI model update changes deliverable conclusions (moderate — operational)
5. Peer circuit awareness of Geneva accountability formation (low — no acute trigger)

## Key Finding: No Geneva-Specific Standard-Setter for Consulting
Geneva law has Ordre des Avocats. Geneva finance has SFAMA/AMAS. Geneva consulting has nothing equivalent. The carrier questionnaire is the only operative standard for the sector — designed without Geneva consulting input, signed by MDs who have not reviewed it against any Geneva-specific benchmark. The vacuum is structural, not informational.
