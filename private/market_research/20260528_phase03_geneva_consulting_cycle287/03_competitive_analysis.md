# Competitive Analysis — Geneva Consulting AI Accountability (C287 Phase 03)

## Current Market Landscape

### What Exists in the Geneva Market

**Large Consultancies (Big Four + Major Strategy Firms)**
- Position AI governance as enterprise risk management
- Deliver through large teams at enterprise-scale fees
- Framework-heavy, methodology documentation oriented
- Do not address small boutique scale (2–50 employees)
- Do not address personal MD liability dimension at boutique level
- Do not address carrier third-generation attestation specifically
- Cost structure incompatible with small boutique engagement budget

**Insurance Brokers / P&C Specialist Firms**
- Offer carrier questionnaire preparation as compliance documentation service
- Help firms complete the form to carrier satisfaction
- Do not address the personal liability dimension of MD signature
- Do not address pre-signing review from signatory's perspective
- Do not have Geneva consulting-specific adequacy framework
- Treat the attestation as a form to be completed, not a personal liability instrument to be analyzed

**Legal Advisory (Geneva Bar + Specialized Data Protection Counsel)**
- Address nFADP compliance architecture for law firms
- Do not address consulting-specific AI governance (proposals, deliverables, sub-consultant delegation)
- Do not have carrier attestation review methodology
- Do not address Geneva consulting accountability vacuum
- Bar guidance vacuum for consulting sector broader than bar jurisdiction

**Swiss Business Consultants / SVA**
- SVA (Swiss Association of Administrative Management) covers public sector finance, not consulting
- No Geneva consulting body equivalent to Ordre des Avocats or SFAMA/AMAS
- No published Geneva-specific AI governance standard for consulting sector
- No peer accountability framework for consulting MDs
- Carrier questionnaire operates as de facto sector standard precisely because no alternative exists

### What's Missing

**1. Geneva Consulting AI Accountability — Carrier Attestation Pre-Signing Review**
No Geneva service offers pre-signing review of carrier attestation from the MD's personal liability perspective. The instrument commits MD personally to adequacy representation against carrier-designed standard without Geneva consulting input. No service maps what MD is committing to, what coverage gap exists, what the undefined adequacy standard means at boutique scale.

**2. Proposal Defensibility Architecture**
No service addresses the documentation gap across the active proposal portfolio. The carrier third-gen question (three AI decisions in past 12 months and review process for each) requires evidence architecture most Geneva consulting boutiques do not have and cannot reconstruct reactively.

**3. Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Framework**
No service offers a three-party delegation framework that maps nFADP Art.9 authorization, accountability chain, engagement letter standard, and vendor terms evaluation simultaneously. The prime boutique is exposed without a documented response.

**4. AI Model Version State Tracking for Active Deliverables**
No Geneva service offers boutique-scale version tracking methodology for AI-assisted deliverables. No protocol for reassessment triggers when vendor model updates occur. No engagement letter architecture addressing version-locked deliverable standards.

**5. Geneva Consulting-Specific Adequacy Standard**
No Geneva body has published interpretation of what carrier questionnaire adequacy means at boutique consulting scale (2–50 employees). The vacuum is structural: no ORDRE, no SVA equivalent, no SFAMA analog for consulting. MDs sign to adequacy against a standard that does not exist in Geneva-specific form.

**6. Multi-Obligation Documentation Coherence**
No service maps interactions between carrier attestation obligations, client AI governance questionnaire responses, engagement letter standards, AI Convention obligations, and nFADP requirements simultaneously across a boutique's active client portfolio.
