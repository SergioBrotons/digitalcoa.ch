# Findings Summary — Geneva Consulting AI Accountability (C287 Phase 03)

## 6 NET-NEW Findings — Distinct from C214–C286 Phase 03

### Finding 1: Carrier Third-Gen Attestation Creates Personal Liability Against Operative Standard That Does Not Exist in Geneva-Specific Form

Carrier third-generation AI questionnaires require MD personal signature attesting to adequacy of the firm's AI governance. The standard (carrier-designed) has not been defined for the Geneva consulting sector by any Geneva body. No SVA equivalent exists to publish Geneva-specific interpretation. No service offers pre-signing review from the signatory's perspective. MD signs, creating personal liability, with no independent analysis of what is committed. The attestation operates as de facto operative standard for the sector precisely because no alternative standard-setter exists. This is distinct from all prior carrier attestation findings (C214–C286) which named the instrument, the absence of review services, and PI coverage ambiguity without isolating the specific mechanism: operative standard formation without Geneva consulting input into its definition, creating personal liability for commitments MD has not evaluated against any Geneva-specific benchmark.

### Finding 2: Client AI Governance Questionnaire Portfolio Effect Creates Accumulated Inconsistency Exposure Below Firm-Level Awareness

Institutional counterparties send AI governance questionnaires as due diligence. The first questionnaire sets the benchmark — whatever it asks becomes the evaluation standard for all firms the client is assessing simultaneously. Geneva consulting firms respond reactively, case by case, without portfolio-level response architecture. This creates accumulated inconsistencies across active client relationships that surface as commercial representations when a sophisticated client compares responses or when carrier renewal asks for portfolio-level AI governance evidence. The exposure is invisible during normal operations because no mechanism exists to track what representations were made in which conversations across the client portfolio. This is distinct from all prior Phase 03 proposal defensibility findings which addressed individual proposal documentation quality, not the specific portfolio-wide accumulated inconsistency mechanism created by reactive questionnaire responses.

### Finding 3: Sub-Consultant AI Delegation Three-Party Chain Operates Without Any Documented Authorization Architecture

Prime boutique contracts with client, delegates to sub-consultant who uses AI in deliverables. nFADP Art.9 third-party processing authorization is absent. Accountability chain is unmapped. Engagement letter standard does not address this configuration. Vendor terms the sub-consultant operates under were not evaluated by prime boutique. MD is contractually accountable to client for deliverables produced by AI tools MD has not authorized or reviewed. When client asks how AI worked in the sub-consultant's contribution, the prime boutique has no documented response. The three-party configuration exists operationally with no documentation architecture for it. This is distinct from all prior Phase 03 sub-consultant delegation findings which named the problem without isolating the specific absent authorization mechanism and the structural incompatibility between prime boutique liability and sub-consultant AI vendor terms.

### Finding 4: AI Model Update Silent Propagation Creates Active Deliverable Version State Drift Undetectable Without Architecture

AI model updates occur silently with no requirement for advance notice or version-locked deliverable standards in engagement letters. Active client deliverables may be based on analytical framework from model versions that no longer exist. No Geneva consulting boutique has version tracking architecture for AI-assisted deliverables. No engagement letter provision requires notification or reassessment when model version changes. MD cannot confirm what model version produced conclusions client still references. The version state drift is structurally undetectable without architecture specifically designed to detect it — normal review processes do not flag it because the deliverables themselves have not changed, only the model that produced them. This is distinct from all prior Phase 03 deliverable staleness findings which addressed temporal aging without isolating the specific silent update propagation mechanism creating version-state-dependent analytical drift in active deliverables.

### Finding 5: Documentation Window Close Inverts Burden of Proof Creating 90-Day Burden-of-Proof Positioning Exercise

The mid-2026 documentation window close creates a specific urgency mechanism that most Geneva consulting firms are treating as a compliance deadline rather than a burden-of-proof positioning exercise. Before the window closes: firm builds documentation evaluated against forming standards (what carriers, institutional clients, and regulators are beginning to require). After the window closes: firm explains why documentation that does not exist was not built — evaluated against arrived standards, not forming standards. New documentation cannot retrospectively reconstruct the reasoning that should have existed during the window period. The 90-day exercise is not about building better documentation — it is about positioning the firm's burden-of-proof posture relative to standards that will arrive. Documentation built after the window closes cannot substitute for documentation that should have been built during it. This is distinct from all prior Phase 03 documentation window findings which treated the close as a deadline rather than a burden-of-proof inversion mechanism.

### Finding 6: Geneva Consulting Accountability Vacuum Creates Structural Amplification Mechanism Named in No Prior Cycle

Geneva consulting has no professional body equivalent to Ordre des Avocats (law), SFAMA/AMAS (finance), or SVA (public sector finance). The carrier third-generation questionnaire is the only operative standard for the sector — designed without Geneva consulting input, signed by MDs who have not evaluated it against any Geneva-specific benchmark. The vacuum is not merely informational — it structurally amplifies exposure because: (1) carrier questionnaire functions as de facto standard precisely because no alternative exists; (2) no feedback path exists to trigger correction when the standard is misapplied to boutique scale; (3) MDs are committing personal liability to adequacy against carrier-designed criteria without any Geneva-calibrated interpretation of what adequacy means; (4) the vacuum prevents collective response development because no institutional mechanism exists to convene Geneva consulting on AI accountability questions. This structural amplification mechanism — named as discrete finding for the first time in C287 Phase 03 — is distinct from all prior Phase 03 peer accountability vacuum findings which identified the vacuum without isolating the specific amplification mechanism created by the vacuum's structural permanence.

## Key Insight — C287 Phase 03

The Geneva consulting sector is experiencing a structural accountability formation event that is different in kind from what other professional sectors face — not because the AI risks are different, but because the accountability vacuum is categorically more severe. Law firms have the Ordre des Avocats. Asset managers have SFAMA/AMAS. Consulting boutiques have nothing equivalent. The carrier questionnaire — designed by insurance actuaries with no consulting sector input — is operating as the operative standard for the sector. MDs are signing personal attestations to adequacy against a standard no Geneva body has defined. The documentation window is closing with the sector having developed no collective interpretation of what it is being asked to demonstrate.

The six structural problems above are not separate issues — they interact across an active client portfolio in ways that make each one more acute than it appears in isolation. The carrier attestation you signed without review creates personal liability that affects how you must respond to client AI governance questionnaires. The sub-consultant delegation chain connects to your nFADP obligations as data controller. The AI model updates are changing the analytical framework of proposals you cannot currently defend. The documentation window close determines what evidence you can produce when a client or carrier asks a question.

This is not a compliance checklist. This is a structural accountability situation that requires a coherent response — one that accounts for the interaction between all six problems across your specific client portfolio.

The Geneva consulting firms that build documentation architecture during the window — with coherent positioning across all six problems — will be evaluated against standards they helped shape through their anticipatory compliance. The firms that wait will be evaluated against standards they had no part in forming.
