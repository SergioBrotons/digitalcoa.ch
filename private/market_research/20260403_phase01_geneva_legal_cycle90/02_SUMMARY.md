### Top Findings (Executive Level)

1. **Nuclear Weapons Ban Initiative — Federal Council Recommends Rejection (April 3, 2026)**
   Federal Chancellery confirmed: initiative filed Dec 2025 with 105,674 valid signatures. Would mandate Swiss accession to UN Treaty on Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons (TPNW). Federal Council recommends rejection — no accession to TPNW; no counter-proposal. Core rationale: none of nuclear-weapon states joined; would impair security cooperation with key partners (NATO). Maintain NPT framework instead. Sent to Parliament.

2. **Switzerland-EU Stabilisation Package — Adopted, Dispatch to Parliament (April 3, 2026)**
   Federal Council adopted the full 'Stabilisation and further development of Swiss-EU relations' package (agreements signed March 2, 2026). Dispatch sent to Parliament. 94 EU legislative acts relevant for Switzerland. Creates 3 new federal laws. Includes: electricity market alignment, food safety, health cooperation, state aid rules, participation in EU programmes (Horizon Europe). Safeguard clauses secured in: free movement, land transport, electricity, food safety. Geneva as a hub: EU Delegation to Switzerland is based in Bern; International Geneva implications for institutional cooperation.

3. **UN Partnerships Renewed — CHF 70M/year 2026-2028 (March 20, 2026)**
   Federal Council decided to continue cooperation with UNDP, UNFPA, UNICEF, UN Women at CHF 70M/year. Supports Switzerland's International Cooperation Strategy 2025-2028. Directly boosts standing of International Geneva.

4. **Rape Victims Accident Insurance — LAA Reform (April 1, 2026)**
   Federal Council presented modification of Accident Insurance Act (LAA) to close legal gap: sexual assault injuries systematically recognized as accidents even with impaired discernment or resistance, including chemical submission. ~40 victims/year refused coverage previously. Geneva judicial system implications; criminal and insurance law intersection.

5. **Quai 9 Drug Consumption Room — Geneva City/Canton Agree Montbrillant Relocation (April 2, 2026)**
   Years of negotiation concluded: Geneva city and canton agreed to relocate Quai 9 from current location. Relocation to Montbrillant. Partnership with Première Ligne association. Public health, criminal justice, and cantonal/city governance intersection. "Win-win" described by authorities.

6. **Geneva Corela Clinic Case — Police Tribunal Hearing (April 1, 2026)**
   Geneva Corela clinic case (previously covered in C88) returned to Police Tribunal. Article notes case "loses air" (se dégonfle). Continued Geneva healthcare and criminal law proceedings.

7. **Swissness IP — On Shoes Case Continues; IP Specialists Concerned (April 1, 2026)**
   Le Temps covered ongoing Swissness law implications from On shoes case. IP law specialists expressed concern about implications for Swiss trademark and origin labeling law. Geneva-based IP practices implication.

8. **Geneva Masons — Labor Law Local Exception Under Threat (April 1, 2026)**
   Geneva masons fighting to preserve local labor exception. Estimated income reduction of CHF 400-500/month if exception removed. Geneva-specific collective bargaining. Geneva labor law and social partner dynamics.
