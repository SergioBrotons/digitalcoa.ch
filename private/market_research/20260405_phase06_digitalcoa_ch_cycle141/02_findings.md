## Finding 01: Geneva Cross-Border M&A Decision Support — CHF 50-500M Mid-Market

Geneva mid-market cross-border M&A transaction advisory (CHF 50-500 million deal value) represents a high-value, underserved segment where boutique advisory firms compete effectively against Big 4 and investment banks. The cross-border dimension introduces Swiss regulatory approvals (ComCo merger control, FINMA change-of-control authorizations for financial sector transactions), foreign regulatory approvals (EU, UK, US antitrust where applicable), and multi-jurisdictional structuring requirements. The mid-market segment is sufficiently complex to require specialized expertise but below the threshold where bulge-bracket investment banks prioritize coverage. DigitalCoa.ch's positioning targets the deal decision moment — the 6-12 month period when a Geneva corporate executive or family office principal must make a go/no-go decision on a transaction and needs independent, unconflicted advice to support that decision. The service includes regulatory risk assessment, deal structuring alternatives, fairness opinion-equivalent analysis, and integration planning advisory. The engagement model ranges from single-decision fixed-fee projects (CHF 25,000-75,000) to multi-transaction retainers (CHF 100,000-200,000 annually) for family offices and mid-market corporates with active M&A programs.

## Finding 02: Post-CSRD Board Sustainability Decision Architecture

The CSRD Phase 2 implementation creates a structural demand for board-level sustainability decision architecture — frameworks that help boards discharge their oversight responsibility for sustainability risks and opportunities without generating compliance box-ticking. The double materiality assessment, mandated for CSRD Phase 2 large entities by June 2026, produces a materiality matrix that boards must approve, monitor, and integrate into strategic decision-making. DigitalCoa.ch's board sustainability decision architecture service addresses the gap between CSRD compliance (producing the required reports) and CSRD value (using sustainability analysis to improve strategic decisions). The service includes board education sessions on sustainability governance, materiality matrix interpretation guidance, KPI integration into board reporting, and sustainability integration into existing board committee structures. The target client profile includes Geneva-based mid-market companies entering their first CSRD compliance cycle, boards with newly appointed sustainability committee chairs, and family company boards where sustainability integration represents a governance modernization priority.

## Finding 03: Geneva UHNW Relocation Decision Infrastructure — Cantonal Migration Advisory

The 2026 cantonal tax environment — with Geneva implementing a 0.75% annual wealth tax on UHNW individuals and other cantons competing aggressively with preferential tax regimes — is driving a relocation decision advisory wave among Geneva UHNW individuals and families. The cantonal migration advisory service addresses the full decision infrastructure: cantonal tax comparison analysis, residence permit and settlement analysis, cantonal wealth tax implications, federal income tax optimization, social security contribution exposure, inheritance and gift tax planning across cantons, and lifestyle/governance factors that affect quality of life decisions. The advisory model requires multi-disciplinary collaboration: Swiss tax counsel, cantonal tax advisors in candidate cantons, immigration specialists, family governance advisors, and independent wealth advisors. DigitalCoa.ch's positioning as a conflict-free, independent advisor — not a product seller or asset manager — addresses the trust gap that UHNW individuals face when evaluating cantonal migration. The engagement typically runs 8-12 weeks for a comprehensive relocation analysis, with follow-on retainer relationships for ongoing cantonal tax monitoring.

The departing IO professional profile — senior, internationally experienced, governance-expert, institutionally credible — is attractive to Geneva foundations that need board members with governance pedigree, and to family offices establishing impact investment mandates. DigitalCoa.ch's IO/NGO executive advisory service addresses two segments: (1) transition advisory for departing IO officials navigating private sector engagement, board positioning, and entrepreneurial option evaluation; (2) foundation board advisory for Geneva foundations seeking to recruit IO-experienced board members, including board composition analysis, governance charter revision, and candidate identification support. The service creates a long-term relationship pathway: IO transition advisory relationships mature into ongoing advisory relationships when departing officials establish family enterprises or join Geneva corporate boards.

## Finding 05: Conflict-Free Independent Positioning — vs. Big 4/Bank/Law Firm Conflicts

The competitive moat of conflict-free independent positioning is materializing as a decisive client acquisition factor for Geneva advisory mandates. The Big 4 (Deloitte, EY, KPMG, PwC), major banks (UBS, CS successor, Julius Baer), and international law firms (Lenz & Staehelin, Pestalozzi, Walder Wyss) all have structural conflicts on multi-client mandates: they serve multiple parties in transactions, maintain bank relationships that create competing interests, and have institutional incentives that can compromise advisory independence. DigitalCoa.ch's conflict-free positioning creates a documented candor framework — explicit conflict disclosure protocols, engagement letter language establishing independence obligations, and anonymized case examples demonstrating how independent advice differs from conflicted advice. The positioning addresses the growing client sophistication about advisory conflicts: Geneva executives who have experienced Big 4 or bank advisory conflicts are actively seeking independent alternatives. The documentation of conflict-free methodology — rather than mere assertion of independence — creates the trust asset that converts prospects into clients.

## Finding 06: Geneva CFO Treasury Decision Support — SNB Rate Environment

The SNB's March 2026 rate cut to 0.25% and the resulting EUR/CHF parity dynamics create acute treasury decision support demand for Geneva CFOs managing multi-currency balance sheets. The treasury advisory service addresses: (1) SNB policy scenario analysis and EUR/CHF rate forecasting for treasury planning; (2) hedging strategy design for CHF/EUR/USD exposure using vanilla and structured instruments; (3) cash management optimization across CHF, EUR, USD, and GBP accounts; (4) working capital finance structuring given SNB rate environment changes; (5) intercompany loan and treasury function centralization for Geneva groups with international subsidiaries. The SNB rate environment creates specific advisory opportunities: the NIR removal means previously protected deposits face repricing risk; the EUR/CHF parity concern means unhedged EUR receivables face CHF translation risk at any rate spike. DigitalCoa.ch's CFO treasury advisory engages at the 2-day diagnostic level (CHF 15,000-25,000) for initial mandate, with a 90-day retainer option (CHF 8,000-12,000/month) for ongoing treasury monitoring and decision support. The service attracts CFO clients who may later engage broader advisory relationships on M&A, sustainability governance, or strategic planning mandates.
