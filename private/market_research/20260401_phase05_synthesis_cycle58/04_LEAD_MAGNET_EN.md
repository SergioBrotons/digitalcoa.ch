## The Seven-Regulatory Portfolio — Where Geneva Organizations Stand

### Obligation 1: FADP Art. 21 & Art. 26 — NOW
Human review for automated decisions (Art. 21) and transparency obligations (Art. 26) are already operative. Any Geneva organization using AI in ways that produce legal effects or significantly affect individuals is already subject to these obligations.

### Obligation 2: Federal AI Ordinance — January 1, 2025 (16+ months operative)
The Federal Council's AI Ordinance has been in force for over 16 months. It is the most underenforced obligation in the portfolio — largely because most Geneva organizations don't know they are subject to it. AI tools in legal research, contract review, financial analysis, medical decision support, and other professional services are likely high-risk AI under the ordinance.

### Obligation 3: Section 301 — Active (April 1, 2026 formal engagement)
The Federal Council formally engaged with US Section 301 investigations on April 1, 2026. Organizations in Geneva with cross-border US-adjacent business face compounded compliance exposure.

### Obligation 4: G7 Évian — June 15-17, 2026 (~75 days away)
The G7 summit in Évian is the Geneva proximity event of 2026. All six sectors face indirect regulatory implications: trade policy outcomes, sanctions enforcement posture, cross-border data flow agreements, and international client advisory needs.

### Obligation 5: Swissdamed — July 1, 2026 (~91 days away)
Swissdamed (Swiss Medical Device Data池) enters force with no grace period. Healthcare organizations processing medical device data electronically must be compliant on day one. Swissmedic's new leadership (in place since January 2026) signals enforcement intent.

### Obligation 6: EU GPAI Regulation — August 2, 2026 (~123 days away)
The EU's General Purpose AI Regulation creates obligations for Geneva organizations deploying GPAI systems — particularly in legal advisory, financial services, and consulting. Most affected organizations have not begun GPAI readiness assessments.

### Obligation 7: Federal Council AI Act — End 2026 (~274 days away)
Switzerland's Federal Council AI Act is pending. It will create the next layer of federal AI governance obligations — extending beyond the current ordinance framework.

## The Three-Dimensional Urgency Model

**Dimension 1 — Immediate deadlines (Q2-Q3 2026):**
G7 Évian (75 days) → Swissdamed (91 days) → GPAI (123 days). Organizations should complete their immediate compliance assessments before June 15.

**Dimension 2 — Active obligations (already operative):**
FADP Art. 21/26 and Federal AI Ordinance are already in effect. Organizations are exposed whether they know it or not. Documentation of current state is the immediate priority.

**Dimension 3 — Emerging pressure (Q4 2026 and beyond):**
Federal Council AI Act (274 days), Section 301 ongoing effects, Swiss fintech consolidation phase. Strategy for the next 12 months should account for this dimension.
