# Phase 03 C56 — Lead Magnet Framework: "The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework"

## Positioning
**For:** Small Geneva consulting firms (5–30 consultants) advising SMB clients on AI adoption, or using AI in their own practice
**Against:** Generic AI implementation advice; tech vendor frameworks
**Unique hook:** GPAI deadline urgency + two-dimensional liability framing + four-quadrant decision model — all grounded in Geneva market reality

---

## Core Promise
The AI decisions your consulting firm makes for its own practice are the same decisions your clients will ask you about. Do them right here first.

---

## Problem Statement (3 layers)

### Layer 1: Your Own AI Governance Gap
Small consulting firms use AI for proposal drafting, market research, project management, and client reporting — often without a governance framework. The Federal AI Ordinance (Jan 2025) and FADP apply to your firm. Your clients are starting to ask.

### Layer 2: Client Advisory Liability
When you advise clients on AI adoption, you're exposed on two dimensions: the AI you recommend, and the AI you use in your own delivery. A client implementation failure traced to your recommendation creates liability. "The vendor said it was ready" is not a defense.

### Layer 3: GPAI August 2, 2026 Deadline
If your firm serves EU clients or uses GPAI systems in client work, the EU GPAI Regulation applies from August 2, 2026. Technical documentation, transparency, and incident reporting obligations will be required. Most small consulting firms are not prepared.

---

## The Four-Quadrant Decision Model

| | Low Risk | High Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **High Value** | Automate now (envelope workflows) | Govern then automate (human review + documentation) |
| **Low Value** | Defer (not worth the friction) | Avoid (high risk, low return) |

This framework applies both to your own firm and to client advisory engagements.
