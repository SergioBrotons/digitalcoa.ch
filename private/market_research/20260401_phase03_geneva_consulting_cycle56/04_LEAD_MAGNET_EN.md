# The Small Consulting Firm AI Decision Framework
**How Geneva's SMB consulting firms navigate AI adoption, governance, and the August 2, 2026 GPAI deadline**

---

You're advising clients on AI adoption. You're also using AI in your own practice. These two things are more connected than most small consulting firms realize — and the regulatory deadlines are converging.

August 2, 2026 is four months away. That's when the EU GPAI Regulation takes effect. If you serve European clients, or if your consulting recommendations involve GPAI systems, you're affected.

This framework gives you the decision tools and governance structure to act before the deadline — and to use that preparation as a competitive differentiator.

---

## The Three-Layer Problem

### Layer 1: Your Own AI Governance Is Your Credibility

Small consulting firms use AI for proposal drafting, market analysis, project planning, and client reporting. Most do this without a documented governance framework.

The Federal AI Ordinance (January 1, 2025) and FADP now apply to your firm directly. When a client asks — and they will start asking — "how do you use AI in your work for us?", you need an answer that sounds as sophisticated as your advice.

The firms that have a governance framework ready will use it as a credibility signal. The firms that don't will be explaining gaps instead of demonstrating expertise.

### Layer 2: Your Client Advisory Liability Is Two-Dimensional

When you advise a client on AI adoption, you're exposed in two directions:

**First dimension — your recommendation:** If the AI system you recommend leads to a client implementation failure — data breach, regulatory violation, operational breakdown — your consulting firm may bear liability. "The vendor told us it was compliant" is not a professional defense.

**Second dimension — your delivery:** If you use AI tools in delivering your consulting recommendations (analysis, reports, presentations), those tools are subject to FADP and the Federal AI Ordinance. Client data processed through your AI tools must be handled appropriately.

Both dimensions require documentation. Neither can be hand-waved.

### Layer 3: GPAI Regulation — August 2, 2026

The EU General Purpose AI Regulation takes effect August 2, 2026. For Geneva consulting firms serving EU clients, this creates specific obligations:

- **Technical documentation:** If your consulting recommendations involve GPAI systems, you must be able to provide or direct clients to technical documentation of how those systems work
- **Transparency to downstream deployers:** Your clients (the deployers) must receive clear information about AI system capabilities and limitations — you need to know what that means for your recommendations
- **Incident reporting:** Systemic AI failures must be reported. If you're advising on the AI, you're in the reporting chain.

Most small consulting firms are not prepared. The GPAI readiness gap is an opportunity for the firms that move first.

## The Four-Quadrant AI Decision Model

Use this framework both for your own firm and for client engagements.

**High Value + Low Risk → Automate now**
These are the "envelope" workflows: internal research, proposal drafting support, data formatting, admin automation. Safe to adopt, high efficiency return.

**High Value + High Risk → Govern then automate**
AI-assisted client analysis, strategic recommendations, financial projections, compliance reviews. High value, but requires human review checkpoints, documentation, and FADP compliance assessment before deployment.

**Low Value + Low Risk → Defer**
Low-return AI uses that don't create meaningful value. Don't prioritize these.

**Low Value + High Risk → Avoid**
High-risk AI uses with low return — facial recognition for office security, AI hiring tools for small teams, AI-driven performance monitoring. These create governance burden without meaningful return.

## The GPAI Readiness Checklist

**1. AI Tool Inventory for Client Engagements**
List every AI tool recommended to clients in the past 18 months. For each: vendor, GPAI classification, technical documentation availability.

**2. Downstream Transparency Documentation**
For each AI system in your recommendations: do you have or can you direct clients to the required GPAI transparency information?

**3. Your Own Firm's GPAI Exposure**
Which AI tools does your firm use that qualify as GPAI systems? Do you have the required documentation?

**4. Incident Reporting Protocol**
If a client's AI implementation fails or produces a systemic error, do you have a protocol for how that gets reported under GPAI?

**5. Client Contract AI Clauses**
Do your client contracts address AI recommendation liability and the two-dimensional exposure described above?

**6. Staff AI Training on GPAI**
Do your consultants understand what GPAI means for their client work and their own AI tool use?

## Two-Dimensional Liability Self-Assessment

**Dimension 1 — Your Recommendation:**
- Have you documented the basis for every AI tool recommendation in the past 18 months?
- Do you have a vendor assessment process that goes beyond vendor claims?
- Do your client contracts address AI recommendation liability?

**Dimension 2 — Your Delivery:**
- Do you have DPA agreements with every AI vendor processing client data?
- Are AI-assisted deliverables reviewed by a human before client delivery?
- Do clients know when AI is used in their project work?

**Score:**
- Both dimensions addressed: You're ahead of most. Maintain documentation.
- One dimension addressed: Significant gap. Prioritize the uncovered dimension.
- Neither dimension addressed: Both clients and regulators will find the gap eventually.
