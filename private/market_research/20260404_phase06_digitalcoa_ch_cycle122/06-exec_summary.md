# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 06 DigitalCoa.ch — Advisory Conversion, Fee Design, and Delivery Standards

## Research Question

How should DigitalCoa.ch structure its post-briefing-call conversion process, fee model offerings, remote advisory delivery standards, referral network development, and client onboarding to maximize qualified lead conversion and long-term client retention in the Geneva market?

## Overview

Phase 06 addresses the operational and relationship architecture that sits between a successful briefing call and a long-term advisory client relationship. Where Phase 05 focused on market positioning and content-driven lead generation, Phase 06 focuses on the conversion and retention mechanics that determine whether advisory interest converts to signed engagements and ongoing relationships.

The Geneva market presents a specific set of dynamics: a relationship-first professional culture where trust must be established before commitment is requested; a fee-sensitive mid-market where retainer predictability competes with project-based outcome clarity; a video-native executive population with calibrated quality expectations for remote delivery; and a professional referral network that operates on genuine relationship investment rather than transactional exchange. These dynamics are not unique to Geneva, but their specific configuration — and the expectations they create — is distinctly Geneva.

Six net-new findings emerged from this cycle. Three address conversion mechanics: the correct response to the post-briefing "send me more information" objection (addressing three distinct executive profiles), the proposal format that actually drives budget approval with Geneva GCs (one-page standalone summary, problem-by-problem scope, fee range not single number), and the segmentation logic for retainer vs. project fee models by ICP (mid-size companies prefer retainers; startups and entrepreneurs prefer projects; the fee model itself communicates positioning signals). Two findings address delivery and relationship quality: remote advisory delivery standards that meet Geneva executive video and document security expectations, and a structured onboarding architecture that reduces the first-30-day trust risk that causes early-stage relationship failure. One finding addresses referral network development: how to build and maintain a Geneva advisory referral network through lawyers, private bankers, and IO professionals without being transactional.

---

## Key Findings

- **Finding 1 (C122-FRESH-01):** The "send me more information" post-briefing-call objection has three distinct profiles — internal-alignment stall, authority uncertainty, and trust-building delay — and responding correctly requires identifying which profile is active before choosing the response format. Sending a generic capabilities deck is the most common and most damaging mistake.

- **Finding 2 (C122-FRESH-02):** Geneva GC budget approval requires proposals structured for internal justification: a one-page standalone executive summary written in the client's business language, a problem-by-problem scope (not deliverable-by-deliverable), a fee range (not a single number), and explicit decision-support language the GC can use verbatim in their internal approval request.

- **Finding 3 (C122-FRESH-03):** Fee model segmentation by ICP is a positioning decision, not just a pricing decision. Geneva mid-size companies (50-250 employees, family-controlled or institutional) prefer retainers (CHF 3,000-8,000/month) for budget predictability and ongoing access. Geneva entrepreneurs and startups prefer project-based engagements (CHF 5,000-15,000) for defined scope and outcome clarity. Presenting the wrong fee model to the wrong segment damages trust before an engagement begins.

- **Finding 4 (C122-FRESH-04):** Remote advisory delivery to Geneva executives requires explicit quality commitments: 1080p minimum video, external microphone, pre-shared agendas 24 hours before, written meeting summaries within 24 hours, and secure document sharing for sensitive materials. Geneva financial sector clients assess these standards against their own internal video meeting practices — inadequacy signals professional under-investment.

- **Finding 5 (C122-FRESH-05):** Geneva's advisory referral network runs through three professional communities — corporate lawyers, private banking relationship managers, and IO/NGO procurement professionals — and referral network development requires genuine relationship investment over time, value-first contact (useful information before referral asks), and face-to-face Geneva event presence. The referral ask only comes after trust is established.

- **Finding 6 (C122-FRESH-06):** Post-engagement onboarding determines long-term retention more than the quality of the initial advisory work. Geneva-specific onboarding requires: a 60-90 minute structured kickoff (not a 30-minute call), a written engagement charter covering communication cadence and escalation protocols, and a 30-day explicit expectation check-in before any issue becomes a cancellation risk.

## Strategic Implications

- **Conversion rate improvement:** The correct response to the "send me more information" objection — properly segmented by executive profile — should measurably improve post-briefing-call conversion rates. Each response format (decision brief, executive summary for distribution, case example with no ask) is calibrated to a specific deferral mechanism, reducing the default to generic information packaging.

- **Proposal quality as competitive differentiator:** Geneva advisory competitors (Big 4, boutiques, solo practitioners) all produce proposals, but most produce proposals that serve the advisor's structure rather than the client's internal approval process. A proposal formatted specifically for Geneva GC budget approval — with standalone executive summary, fee range, and decision-support language — should stand out in competitive evaluation contexts.

- **Fee model clarity reduces sales friction:** ICP-segmented fee model presentation eliminates the awkward fee negotiation that occurs when an advisor proposes a retainer to a startup founder or a project engagement to a family business CFO who expected ongoing access. Presenting the fee model that matches the client's preference before they ask reduces decision friction.

- **Proactive delivery standard communication as trust signal:** Most Geneva advisory providers do not proactively communicate their video quality, document security, and responsiveness standards. DigitalCoa.ch committing to explicit standards — and meeting them consistently — creates a trust signal that competitors who leave standards implicit cannot match.

- **Systematic referral network investment over time:** The Geneva professional community is small enough that reputation travels. Advisors who invest in genuine relationships with Geneva lawyers, bankers, and IO professionals — without immediately asking for referrals — build a referral channel that generates warm introductions over years, not quarters.

- **Onboarding systematization reduces early-stage churn:** The first 30 days of an advisory engagement are the highest-risk period for relationship failure. Structured onboarding with a written engagement charter and explicit 30-day check-in addresses the most common failure modes before they materialize.

## Recommended Next Actions

1. **Develop three standardized post-briefing-call response templates** — one for each "send me more information" profile (internal-alignment, authority uncertainty, trust-building delay). Each template should be a one-page document, not a deck. Test with next five post-briefing conversions and measure response quality.

2. **Redesign the advisory proposal template** for Geneva GC budget approval context: add standalone one-page executive summary (separated from full proposal), restructure scope from deliverable-by-deliverable to problem-by-problem, present fee as a range with two engagement options, add explicit decision-support paragraph the GC can use internally.

3. **Publish a public advisory delivery quality commitment** on DigitalCoa.ch: 1080p video minimum, external microphone standard, 24-hour post-meeting summary, secure document sharing for sensitive client materials. This commitment, once public, creates accountability and differentiates on professional standards.

4. **Initiate one relationship per month** with a Geneva referral community professional (lawyer, private banker, or IO procurement contact): value-first contact with useful information relevant to their client base, no referral ask attached for first 6 months minimum.

5. **Create a standard engagement charter template** for use at advisory kickoff: communication cadence options, response time commitments, escalation protocol, confidentiality scope, success metrics. Use in every new engagement from the next signature forward.

6. **Run a 30-day onboarding audit** on the last three signed advisory clients: Did each receive a kickoff meeting of at least 60 minutes? Was an engagement charter sent within the first week? Was a 30-day expectation check-in conducted? Capture learnings and close gaps.
