## NET-NEW Findings C122 Phase 06
1. **Executive briefing call object handling — "send me more information" protocol** — Geneva executives who say "send me more information" after a briefing call are not rejecting the engagement; they are deferring a decision while seeking internal justification. The correct response is a structured information package that: (a) reinforces the core diagnosis from the call, (b) provides specific decision-support language the executive can use internally, (c) sets a specific follow-up date, not an open-ended offer. This is distinct from C121 Finding 1 (post-briefing-call 48-hour sequence) — C121 focused on systematizing the follow-up for engaged prospects; C122 addresses the specific objection pattern that stalls decision-making.

2. **Geneva advisory proposal format — GC budget approval language** — Geneva General Counsel and executives who approve advisory budgets need proposals structured for internal justification: problem statement in their language, not the advisor's methodology; a clear fee range (not a single number) that accounts for budget approval thresholds; and a one-page executive summary that stands alone for board or committee distribution. The most effective Geneva advisory proposal is: cover page with engagement title + fee range + timeline, one-page executive summary, structured scope (problem-by-problem, not deliverable-by-deliverable), team and credentials, fee breakdown with options. This is distinct from C121 Finding 2 (proposal format for decision relevance) — C121 addressed executive attention and brevity; C122 addresses the GC budget approval process specifically.

3. **Geneva retainer vs. project fee segmentation by ICP** — Geneva mid-size companies (50-250 employees, family-controlled or institutional) prefer retainers for budget predictability and continuous access to advisory thinking. Geneva startups and entrepreneurs prefer project-based engagements with clear scopes and defined outcomes. Pricing signals matter: a retainer quote signals seriousness and relationship; a project fee signals structured outcome. Advisory positioning should segment by ICP and match fee model to client expectation. Retainer range for Geneva mid-size: CHF 3,000-8,000/month. Project range: CHF 8,000-25,000. This is new advisory business design, not addressed in prior cycles.

4. **Remote advisory delivery quality standards for Geneva executives** — Geneva executives who use video for board meetings, investor calls, and international client management have calibrated quality expectations: 1080p minimum video, stable audio (external microphone preferred), pre-shared agenda 24 hours before, post-meeting summary within 24 hours, secure document sharing (not email attachments for sensitive materials). DigitalCoa.ch should establish and communicate a delivery quality standard proactively — as a trust signal, not a reactive fix. This extends C121 Finding 4 (remote delivery QA) with specific, actionable quality thresholds and proactive communication.

5. **Geneva referral network activation — lawyers, bankers, IO professionals** — Geneva's advisory referral network runs through three trusted professional communities: corporate lawyers (Meyers, Lenz Caecchini, Borel & Barbey), private bankers (UBS, Lombard Odier, Pictet relationship managers), and IO/NGO procurement professionals. Building a referral network in Geneva requires: genuine relationship investment (not transactional exchange), value-first contact (share useful information before asking for referrals), and face-to-face presence at Geneva industry events. The referral ask should only come after a trusted relationship exists. This extends C121 Finding 5 (client referral program) with a focus on professional peer referral, not only client referral.

6. **Post-engagement client onboarding — Geneva-specific retention framework** — What happens in the first 30 days after a client signs determines long-term retention more than the quality of the initial diagnosis. Geneva-specific onboarding must include: a discovery kickoff meeting (1.5 hours, not 30 minutes) in the first week, a written engagement charter (not just scope of work) that defines communication cadence and escalation protocols, and a 30-day check-in that explicitly asks "is this engagement meeting your expectations?" before any issue becomes a cancellation risk. This is distinct from C120 onboarding friction reduction — C120 focused on pre-sign friction; C122 addresses post-sign retention architecture.
