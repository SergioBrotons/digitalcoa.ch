# 05 — Lead Magnet Concept: The Geneva Executive Advisory Qualification Guide

## Lead Magnet Title

**"The Geneva Executive Advisory Qualification Guide: What High-Performing Leaders Do Before Engaging an Outside Advisor"**

### Subtitle

*A practical checklist for Geneva-based executives evaluating strategic advisory relationships — based on how the best advisory clients engage, what they ask before signing, and what separates productive advisory relationships from expensive mistakes.*

---

## Format Recommendation

**Format:** Structured PDF guide (12-15 pages) with companion checklist
**Delivery:** Direct download from DigitalCoa.ch landing page; gated by short form (name, company, role, email)
**Style:** Executive-grade, not academic. Written for decision-makers who read quickly and act decisively.
**Language:** English primary; key checklist elements in French sidebar

### Why This Format

Geneva executives respond to materials that respect their time and signal that the author understands their context. A short, well-structured guide that functions as an immediately useful tool creates stronger conversion than a lengthy research report. The checklist format gives the prospect a reason to exchange their contact information for something genuinely useful — not just another thought leadership piece they will skim and archive.

The checklist structure also serves as a qualification mechanism: executives who download it and find it relevant are more likely to be genuine prospects. Those who find it irrelevant self-select out before entering a nurture sequence.

---

## Three Key Takeaways

### Takeaway 1: The Four Questions Every Executive Should Answer Before Engaging an Advisor

The guide's core content presents four diagnostic questions that Geneva executives should be able to answer before engaging external advisory:

1. **What specific decision are we trying to make?** (Not "improve our strategy" — a specific decision with a timeline and alternatives)
2. **What internal capacity do we already have, and what gap does external advisory fill?** (Avoid paying for advice you could generate internally)
3. **What does success look like in 90 days?** (Measurable outcomes, not activity metrics)
4. **Who is the budget decision-maker, and what language do they need to approve this?** (Aligning advisory investment with internal approval process)

These four questions are the same diagnostic framework used in DigitalCoa.ch's own discovery process. Sharing them upfront is both genuinely useful and a natural demonstration of advisory discipline.

### Takeaway 2: The Geneva Advisory Due Diligence Checklist

A practical checklist that executives use to evaluate advisory relationships before committing. Items include:

- Request the advisor's specific Geneva or Swiss market references (not just sector references)
- Ask how the advisor handles conflicts of interest between clients in the same sector
- Request professional liability insurance documentation for engagements above CHF 10,000
- Confirm the senior advisor who sells the engagement is the same person who delivers it
- Define communication cadence and response time expectations before signing
- Establish how the engagement will be evaluated at 30, 60, and 90 days

This checklist signals that DigitalCoa.ch welcomes the same scrutiny it recommends to prospects — a trust-building mechanism, not a barrier.

### Takeaway 3: The Retainer vs. Project Decision Framework

A practical framework helping executives determine which engagement model fits their situation:

- **Choose a retainer when:** You need ongoing access to advisory thinking, have recurring strategic decisions, or are in a transition period requiring sustained support
- **Choose a project engagement when:** The challenge is discrete and bounded, you have a specific deliverable need, or you are testing a relationship before committing to ongoing work
- **Warning signs that suggest you need a different model:** Vague scope with project pricing (overruns likely), retainer with no defined access schedule (relationship goes dormant)

The framework is non-promotional — it helps executives choose the right engagement model regardless of which they select, building trust that DigitalCoa.ch is advisor-first rather than revenue-first.

## Target ICP

**Primary ICP:** Geneva-based C-suite executives (CEO, MD, General Counsel) at mid-size companies (50-250 employees) who are evaluating or have decided to engage external advisory support. Decision-makers in the consideration phase who have not yet committed.

**Secondary ICP:** Founders and CEOs of Geneva-based startups and growth-stage companies (Series A and B) who need strategic advisory but are uncertain how to engage external advisors efficiently.

**Tertiary ICP:** Operations and Strategy Directors at Geneva-based family businesses who are working on succession, digital transformation, or operational challenges and need external advisory support to navigate internal dynamics.

### Why These ICPs

These segments represent DigitalCoa.ch's highest-conversion audience: executives who are actively considering advisory support (not early-stage awareness), have budget authority or direct board access (not purely influencing), and are based in Geneva (the core geographic focus). They download useful tools, not generic reports.

## Conversion Path Rationale

### Entry Point

A Geneva executive discovers the guide through organic search (queries like "how to choose an advisor Geneva," "advisory engagement checklist," or "Geneva executive consultant"), through a LinkedIn post or share, or through a referral from a professional network contact.

### Capture Mechanism

The short form (name, company, role, email) is positioned as a qualification step, not a marketing gate. The framing is: "This guide is most useful for senior executives making advisory decisions. To ensure you receive the version most relevant to your situation, please share a few details." This reduces perceived spam risk and increases completion rates.

### Nurture Sequence

**Day 0 (immediate):** Send the guide with a brief personal note from the DigitalCoa.ch advisor. No sales language — just context on why this guide was written and an invitation to reply with questions or to schedule a 20-minute call.

**Day 7:** Send a single relevant insight — one of the four diagnostic questions explored in more depth. No ask.

**Day 14:** Send a case example (anonymized) that demonstrates how a Geneva executive used the guide's framework to structure an advisory engagement.

**Day 21:** Brief calendar check-in — "Is this guide useful? Are you currently evaluating advisory support?" Low pressure, genuine inquiry.

**Day 30:** If no response to Day 21, send one final relevant insight and close the sequence gracefully. If engagement is warm, offer a 20-minute exploratory call.

### Why This Sequence

This sequence is structured to mirror what DigitalCoa.ch recommends to its own clients: build trust before asking for commitment, provide value before requesting a decision, and close gracefully rather than persisting past the point of diminishing returns.

Executives who convert through this sequence arrive at a briefing call already oriented toward the advisory relationship framework — making the call more productive and faster-moving.

## Differentiation from Standard Thought Leadership

Most advisory lead magnets are thinly disguised sales materials: a list of "10 things to look for in an advisor" that happens to describe exactly what the author offers. The Geneva Executive Advisory Qualification Guide works differently:

- It qualifies the prospect before they engage (protecting both parties from poor-fit engagements)
- It demonstrates the advisor's discipline and framework before any commitment
- It creates reciprocity — the prospect received something genuinely useful before any ask
- It functions as a trust-building mechanism for Geneva's relationship-first professional culture
