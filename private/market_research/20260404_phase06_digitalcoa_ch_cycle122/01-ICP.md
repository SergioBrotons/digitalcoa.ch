# 01 — ICP: DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Clients

## Core ICP: Geneva-Based Executive Decision-Makers Seeking Advisory Clarity

DigitalCoa.ch serves Geneva-based executives who need strategic clarity on technology, operations, or organizational challenges and who prefer working with a single senior advisor rather than a larger firm.

---

## Primary ICP Segment: Geneva Mid-Size Companies

### Profile
- **Company size:** 50-250 employees
- **Ownership:** Family-controlled, founder-led, or institutional minority stake
- **Sector:** Professional services, trading, commodity, fintech, luxury goods, healthcare services
- **Location:** Canton of Geneva; some in Nyon, Versoix, or Geneva lake corridor
- **Revenue estimate:** CHF 10M-100M annually
- **Decision-maker role:** CEO, Managing Director, or General Counsel
- **Age/demographics:** 40-60 years old, predominantly male in traditional sectors, increasing female representation in professional services

### Pain Points
1. **Technology decision fatigue** — too many vendors, too many frameworks, no internal anchor for evaluation
2. **Regulatory complexity navigation** — Swiss + EU regulatory layers (nFADP, EU AI Act, FINMA) without dedicated compliance team
3. **Board and investor pressure** — needing to show strategic coherence without disrupting operations
4. **Succession or transition planning** — generational transitions, ownership changes, or M&A scenarios
5. **Digital transformation without disruption** — incremental change that doesn't break what works

### Decision-Making Characteristics
- ** Buying process:** CEO or MD initiates; GC or CFO involved for budget approval above CHF 15,000
- **Timeline:** 4-12 weeks from initial contact to signed engagement
- **Trust signals that matter:** Referrals from trusted peers, demonstrated Geneva market knowledge, concise communication
- **Budget range:** CHF 3,000-8,000/month retainer or CHF 8,000-25,000 project fee
- **Preferred engagement model:** Retainer (predictability, ongoing access); project-based for discrete challenges

### Communication Preferences
- Email for first contact and proposal delivery
- Video call (Zoom/Meet) for briefing calls
- Brief, substantive documents (not lengthy decks)
- French useful but English sufficient for most Geneva executives

---

## Secondary ICP Segment: Geneva Entrepreneurs and Startup Founders

### Profile
- **Company stage:** Seed to Series A, or founder-owned SMEs in early growth
- **Sector:** SaaS, fintech, healthtech, climate tech, professional services startups
- **Location:** Geneva, Lausanne corridor, Plan-les-Ouates
- **Decision-maker role:** Founder, CEO, sometimes CTO for technical decisions
- **Age/demographics:** 30-45 years old, internationally oriented, English-primary in tech sector

### Pain Points
1. **Investor narrative clarity** — needing to articulate strategy clearly for fundraising
2. **Regulatory navigation** — FINMA licensing, nFADP compliance, EU AI Act readiness
3. **Scaling operations** — from startup chaos to structured operations without losing speed
4. **Advisory access** — wanting senior thinking without full-time cost
5. **Team building** — hiring, structuring, retaining key roles

### Decision-Making Characteristics
- **Buying process:** Founder initiates directly; fast decision if fit is clear
- **Timeline:** 1-4 weeks from initial contact to signed engagement
- **Trust signals that matter:** Proof of concept with similar companies, specific sector knowledge, speed and responsiveness
- **Budget range:** CHF 5,000-15,000 project engagement (retainer less common at this stage)
- **Preferred engagement model:** Project-based with defined scope; short sharp engagements preferred

### Communication Preferences
- English primary for most tech-sector founders
- Slack or WhatsApp for rapid communication
- Not lengthy documents — prefer bullet points and concise frameworks
- Responsive to concise, direct communication

---

## Tertiary ICP Segment: International Organizations and NGOs with Geneva Presence

### Profile
- **Organization type:** UN agencies, international NGOs, diplomatic missions, Geneva-based foundations
- **Size:** Small offices (5-20 staff Geneva) to medium representations (50-200 staff)
- **Decision-maker role:** Country/Regional Director, Operations Director, Head of Programs
- **Language:** French essential for Swiss-based organizations; English for international bodies

### Pain Points
1. **Digital transformation with limited budgets** — doing more with constrained resources
2. **Data governance** — beneficiary data, nFADP compliance, donor data requirements
3. **Technology selection** — avoiding vendor lock-in, managing legacy systems
4. **Remote work infrastructure** — hybrid setups, security, collaboration tools
5. **AI adoption** — staff using AI tools without organizational policy or governance

### Decision-Making Characteristics
- **Buying process:** Formal procurement process for larger engagements; direct hire for smaller advisory
- **Timeline:** 8-20 weeks due to internal approval processes
- **Trust signals that matter:** Understanding of IO/NGO context, multilingual capability, UN/IO network references
- **Budget range:** CHF 5,000-20,000 project; retainers less common due to grant-based funding cycles
- **Preferred engagement model:** Project-based; clear deliverables tied to funding periods

## Signals That Indicate Strong ICP Fit

**High-fit indicators:**
- Referred by a trusted Geneva peer (warm introduction)
- Specific, defined challenge articulated clearly in first contact
- Decision-maker has budget authority or direct board access
- Timeline of 1-6 months (not "exploring indefinitely")
- Geneva-based organization or significant Geneva operations

**Lower-fit indicators:**
- Vague "digital transformation" request without specific problem definition
- Request for lengthy proposal before a briefing call
- Budget below CHF 3,000 or significantly above CHF 15,000/month
- Non-Geneva based with no Geneva operations
- Request for work product before any engagement relationship
