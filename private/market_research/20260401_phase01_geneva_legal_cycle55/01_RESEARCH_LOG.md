### 3. Agentic AI Three-Tier Autonomy Framework — Legal Liability Mapping
**AI assistants (low autonomy) → AI agents (moderate autonomy) → Agentic AI (high autonomy, full decision execution).** Legal liability = always attributed to the firm, not the AI. "It wasn't me, it was my AI" is not a valid defense in court. **NET-NEW for Phase 01 C55 — agentic AI governance gap now structurally mapped with liability framing.**

Source: Haerting.ch, CMS Law agentic AI + EU AI Act analysis

### 4. The "Envelope vs. Letter" Distinction — Safe AI Automation Framework
**Automating the envelope is safe. Automating the letter is not.** Routing, scheduling, reminders, document formatting, billing preparation do not touch privileged content. The risk increases only when AI interprets document content for substantive purposes, drafts advice, or processes data through platforms lacking DPAs. **NET-NEW framing for Phase 01 — operationalizes FADP/privilege tension into actionable guidance.**

Source: Meikai.ch AI Automation for Swiss Law Firms

### 5. Five Safe-to-Automate Workflows for Swiss Law Firms
1. New matter intake (conflict check → ID docs → mandate letter → matter open)
2. Time recording reminders and narrative support
3. Billing preparation (draft bill generation from time entries)
4. Invoice chasing sequences (30/60/90 day automation)
5. Executed document tracking (signature follow-up triggers)

**These five workflows represent the 8-14 hours/week recoverable capacity.**

Source: Meikai.ch

### 6. FADP + SBA Guidelines Dual Obligation Framework
Small law firms face: (1) FADP operative obligations (Art. 21 human review, Art. 26 transparency, DPIA for high-risk) + (2) SBA AI Guidelines standard of care (non-binding but establishes professional duty). The combination creates a dual-track compliance obligation for any law firm using AI. **NET-NEW for Phase 01 C55.**
