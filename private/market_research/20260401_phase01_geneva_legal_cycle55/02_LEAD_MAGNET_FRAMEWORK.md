# Phase 01 — Lead Magnet Framework — Cycle 55

## Lead Magnet Title
**"The Small Law Firm AI Governance Question"**

## ICP (Ideal Client Profile)
Geneva/Swiss Romandie small law firms (1-10 lawyers)
- Using AI tools (Casus, ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, Swiss-Noxtua, etc.)
- FADP obligations already operative (since Sep 1, 2023)
- SBA AI Guidelines June 2024 standard of care (dual obligation)
- No dedicated AI governance framework
- 36pt governance gap vs. large firms
- Decision-maker: Managing Partner / founding partner

## Core Problem Statement
Small law firms are 2.5+ years into FADP obligations, subject to SBA Guidelines, and deploying agentic AI with zero governance framework — and FDPIC enforcement is circling the legal sector.

## Primary Entry Point (Decision 0)
Shadow AI audit: "Is the AI you're already using actually compliant?"

## Key Tension
FADP + SBA dual obligations + agentic AI liability vs. internal capacity gap

## Three-Timeline Urgency Framework
- NOW: FADP operative + SBA Guidelines standard of care
- May 2026: digitalswitzerland AI Action Plan First Draft (external pressure)
- End-2026: Federal Council formal legislation (CoE AI Convention)

## Dual Professional Obligation
1. **FADP legal** (Art. 21 human review, Art. 26 transparency, DPIA for high-risk)
2. **SBA professional standard of care** (June 2024 Guidelines — non-binding but establishes duty)

## Agentic AI Three-Tier Liability Map
- AI assistants (low autonomy): reactive, human-initiated = lower risk
- AI agents (moderate autonomy): goal-directed, tool-using = firm liable
- Agentic AI (high autonomy): autonomous decision execution = maximum liability exposure

## "Envelope vs. Letter" Safe Automation Framework
- Safe: routing, scheduling, reminders, billing prep, document formatting
- Not safe: drafting advice, interpreting document content, substantive legal analysis
- 8-14 hours/week recoverable through safe automation alone

## Quantified Efficiency Opportunity
- 8-14 h/week lost (4-8 lawyer firm average)
- 10-15% time-to-bill gap (manual recording)
- CHF 34,440/year recovered (intake automation alone, one 4-lawyer firm)
- 9.2 partner-hours/month intake administration
