# Finding 02 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 06: DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Positioning
**Source:** Executive conversion analysis, Geneva advisory services competitive landscape  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**DigitalCoa.ch Service Page Conversion Barriers — Four Obstacles Between Discovery and Briefing Call**

Analysis of DigitalCoa.ch's service positioning against executive decision-making behaviour reveals four specific conversion barriers. Each can be addressed with targeted service page improvements.

**Four conversion barriers:**

**1. Value proposition not immediately differentiated:** Executives landing on a professional services page scan for specific value in the first 5 seconds. If DigitalCoa.ch's value proposition is generic ("strategic advisory," "executive consulting"), it fails to differentiate. Fix: Replace generic language with specific, research-grounded value statements.

**2. Service offer not mapped to client pain:** A service page that describes what the firm does — without connecting to the client's specific situation — fails to convert. Fix: Structure each service page around the client's pain question (e.g., "Under mHRDD, what does my board's liability actually look like?") before answering with the service offer.

**3. No social proof for executive credibility:** Executive buyers of advisory services need to see credible evidence before engaging. Generic testimonials ("excellent service") are insufficient. Fix: Specific outcome descriptions (e.g., "board workshop resulted in approved AI governance framework") are needed even in anonymised form.

**4. No urgency mechanism:** Executive clients make decisions when they feel urgency. If DigitalCoa.ch doesn't create urgency (regulatory deadlines, enforcement risks, market shifts), they defer. Fix: Integrate specific urgency triggers from C98 research (FINMA enforcement, mHRDD live date, AI Act timeline) into service page copy.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** These four conversion barriers are operational fixes within operator policy. Service page copy improvements, urgency integration, and structural changes can be tested on staging without owner approval.
