# Finding 04 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 06: DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Positioning
**Source:** DigitalCoa.ch site architecture analysis, Brotons.net to DigitalCoa.ch pathway analysis  
**Date:** 2026-04-03

## Finding

**Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch Conversion Pathway — Three Specific Internal Linking Improvements**

The pathway from Brotons.net (upstream thought leadership) to DigitalCoa.ch (downstream advisory) is the core funnel mechanism. Three specific improvements can increase conversion through this pathway.

**Three internal pathway improvements:**

**1. Contextual "advisory" mentions in Brotons.net articles:** Brotons.net articles should contain contextual references to DigitalCoa.ch advisory services — not promotional, but framing the thought leadership as connected to practical application. Example: "Organizations navigating this challenge may benefit from a structured advisory engagement — DigitalCoa.ch works with executives on precisely these questions." Placed at the natural decision point in each article.

**2. Service page mirroring content cluster themes:** DigitalCoa.ch service pages should mirror the themes of Brotons.net content clusters. When Brotons.net publishes on "AI governance in Geneva," DigitalCoa.ch should have a service page on "Executive AI Governance Advisory" that appears in the same search context.

**3. CTAs at article conclusion vs. generic service page CTAs:** The current CTA approach (generic "contact us" or service page CTAs) is low-converting. Replacing with article-conclusion CTAs that are specific to the article's topic ("For a briefing on what this means for your board, contact DigitalCoa.ch") increases relevance and conversion probability.

**Why this matters:** The Brotons.net → DigitalCoa.ch pathway is the primary conversion funnel. Systematic improvements to this pathway compound over time. Each research finding on Brotons.net should generate a corresponding pathway action.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** Internal pathway improvements are within operator policy — they involve site content and linking, not structural architecture changes. This is a self-heal improvement that can be executed on staging.
