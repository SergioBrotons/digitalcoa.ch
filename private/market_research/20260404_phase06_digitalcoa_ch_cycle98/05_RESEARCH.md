# Finding 05 — CYCLE 98 | Phase 06: DigitalCoa.ch Advisory Positioning
**Source:** DigitalCoa.ch trust-building content analysis, Geneva executive client journey  
**Date:** 2026-03

## Finding

**DigitalCoa.ch Trust-Building Content — Four Specific Pieces That Precede Briefing Call Decisions**

Executive clients of advisory services buy based on trust before they buy based on expertise. DigitalCoa.ch needs trust-building content that precedes the service conversation — making the first call feel like a natural next step.

**Four trust-building content pieces:**

**1. Case study (even anonymised):** "How a Geneva family office prepared for FINMA AML examination — a case narrative." Even with client confidentiality, a narrative approach that describes the situation, the process, and the outcome builds trust. The specificity matters more than the named client.

**2. Methodology explainer:** "How DigitalCoa.ch approaches an executive advisory engagement — a clear description of process, timeline, and client involvement." Executives want to understand how the work actually happens before committing.

**3. Principal background and expertise credibility:** "The background and decision-making experience of the advisory principal(s)." Executive clients are buying the person, not just the firm. Transparent, specific credential presentation (not a CV) builds trust.

**4. Industry-specific thought leadership:** "The Geneva specific perspective — why depth of Geneva market knowledge matters for advisory work." This differentiates DigitalCoa.ch from generic consulting firms. The Geneva-specific positioning is itself a trust signal.

**Why these matter:** Trust is built through specificity, transparency, and demonstrated understanding. Generic "about us" and "services" pages don't create trust. The four content pieces above address specific trust barriers that Geneva executives experience when evaluating advisory relationships.

**Signal for DigitalCoa.ch:** Trust-building content is within operator policy for content creation. These pieces can be developed on staging and prepared for owner review before production publishing.
