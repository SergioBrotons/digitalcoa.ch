# Messaging Framework — Geneva Real Estate AI Governance
## Phase 06 Cycle 44

## Core Message
**"75% of Geneva real estate agencies are using AI tools. GAIGI has no public guidance for you. FADP has been operative for 2.5 years. Do you know which AI tools you're using for tenant screening, property valuations, and contracts — and whether you're compliant?"**

## Key Themes

### Theme 1: 75% Adoption — Widest Governance Gap
**Highest Adoption, Least Governance**
> "Real estate agencies have the highest AI adoption rate in Swiss professional services — 75%. Yet formal governance is near zero. That 47-point gap is where regulatory exposure lives."

### Theme 2: GAIGI 404 — The Guidance Vacuum
**No One Is Writing Guidance for You**
> "GAIGI, Switzerland's asset management industry association, has no public AI governance guidance for real estate. There is quite literally no official guidance written for your sector. That's not a gap — it's an opportunity."

### Theme 3: FADP 2.5+ Years Operative
**No Grace Period, No Excuse**
> "FADP Article 5(c) has been operative since September 1, 2023. FDPIC confirmed in May 2025: existing obligations already cover AI-supported data processing. 2.5 years of accumulated exposure with no enforcement action doesn't mean compliance — it means the clock is running."

### Theme 4: Retroactive Compliance
**Start With What You Already Have**
> "Before choosing new tools or drafting policies: audit the AI you already use. Tenant screening, property valuations, contract analysis — these are likely already subject to FADP Art. 21. Retroactive compliance is the fastest entry point."

### Theme 5: Two-Timeline Urgency
**May 2026 + End-2026**
> "digitalswitzerland publishes its AI Action Plan First Draft in May 2026. Federal Council legislation follows end-2026. Your AI tools are already regulated. The window for first-mover advantage is closing."

## Tone
- Calm, precise, industry-aware
- No hype, no scare tactics
- Guidance-vacuum framing over compliance-frightening
