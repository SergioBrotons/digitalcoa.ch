# 02 — Market Signals: Phase 02 Geneva Finance (C118)

## Market Signals (Geneva Finance, April 2026)

### Signal 1: Swiss Sustainable Finance Taxonomy — Implementation Underway
- Federal Council adopted sustainable finance action plan; Swiss taxonomy aligned with EU taxonomy where possible
- SFAMA issuing guidance on sustainable finance disclosures for Swiss funds
- Swiss banks incorporating climate risk into credit risk frameworks per FINMA guidance
- **Signal:** Taxonomy compliance advisory — gap analysis between EU and Swiss approaches; transition finance structuring
- **Source:** Federal Council press releases, SFAMA publications, FINMA climate risk guidance

### Signal 2: DLT Act Tokenization — Swiss Fund Market Developments
- DLT Act (in force 2021; amended 2024-2025) enabling tokenized fund units
- SIX Digital Exchange processing DLT-based securities; first tokenized bond issuances
- Swiss asset managers exploring tokenized fund structures for alternative assets
- **Signal:** Tokenization advisory — structuring, legal framework, DLT Act compliance, investor onboarding
- **Source:** SIX DD, FINMA DLT guidance, Federal Council legislative files

### Signal 3: SNB Interest Rate Corridor — Impact on Geneva Private Banking Margin
- SNB maintaining negative interest rate exemption threshold (negative rates ended but corridor structure persists)
- CHF weakness/strength dynamics affecting Geneva private banking foreign asset yields
- Rate environment supporting CHF mortgage market; pressure on net interest margin
- **Signal:** Treasury risk advisory — FX hedging, interest rate risk management, portfolio duration
- **Source:** SNB monetary policy statements, SNB financial stability reports

### Signal 4: Swiss Banking GenAI Adoption — FINMA AI Guidance
- FINMA published guidance on AI use in Swiss financial institutions (credit scoring, chatbots, AML)
- Geneva banks piloting GenAI for client reporting, compliance monitoring, portfolio analytics
- Accountability/explainability requirements under FINMA AI guidance creating implementation challenges
- **Signal:** AI governance advisory for Geneva banks — FINMA compliance, model risk management, explainability
- **Source:** FINMA AI guidance publications, Swiss Bankers Association reports

### Signal 5: Swiss Real Estate Financing — Rate Environment Effects
- Swiss mortgage market showing rate sensitivity; Refi rate increases passed to end borrowers
- Geneva residential property valuations under pressure; loan-to-value covenant breaches emerging
- Cantonal bank (Banque Cantonale de Genève) and Raiffeisen managing exposure
- **Signal:** Real estate financing advisory — covenant restructuring, refinancing, workout advisory
- **Source:** SNB mortgage market reports, Geneva cantonal bank filings, Swiss Real Estate Association data

### Signal 6: FINMA Enforcement Priorities 2025-2026
- FINMA increased enforcement activity in: AML (Russian sanctions evasion), greenwashing, operational resilience
- Notable cases: Swiss bank AML fine (2025), asset manager greenwashing settlement (2025)
- FINMA risk-based supervision approach; increased scrutiny of model risk and AI
- **Signal:** Compliance framework review — AML, greenwashing defense, operational resilience
- **Source:** FINMA enforcement actions 2025-2026, FINMA annual reports, media coverage

## Synthesis
Geneva finance advisory demand driven by:
1. Sustainable finance taxonomy (regulatory change)
2. DLT/tokenization (market structure change)
3. Rate environment (macro)
4. AI adoption (technology change)
5. Real estate credit quality (credit cycle)
6. FINMA enforcement (compliance pressure)
