# 03 — Competitive Landscape: Phase 02 Geneva Finance (C118)

## Competitive Set: Geneva Finance Advisory

### Tier 1 — Geneva Private Banks (Internal Advisory)
**UBS Geneva, Credit Suisse successor entity, Pictet, Lombard Odier, Rothschild & Co Geneva**

- Strengths: balance sheet, relationships, internal research
- Weakness: conflict of interest on products, less objective on regulatory change
- **Threat:** Banks selling regulatory change as justification for product sales

### Tier 2 — Advisory Boutiques
**Accurisk, Othmer & Partner, Schneider Partners, VermögensPartner**

- Strengths: independent, regulatory focus, objectivity
- Weakness: small scale, limited geographic reach, no multi-disciplinary
- **Opportunity:** Independent regulatory advisory — objectivity differentiates from bank internal advisory

### Tier 3 — Big 4 Advisory
**Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG (Geneva offices)**

- Strengths: multi-disciplinary, global network, existing client relationships
- Weakness: generic frameworks, high price, conflicts in audit/consulting separation
- **Opportunity:** Niche Geneva regulatory advisory at lower price point than Big 4

### Tier 4 — Academic / Think Tank
**Graduate Institute, ETH Zurich (Finance), Swiss Finance Institute**

- Thought leadership influence; indirect competitive factor
- **Opportunity:** Research partnerships, content co-creation, credibility

## Underserved Niches (Differentiation Opportunities)
1. **DLT/tokenization advisory for Geneva asset managers** — nascent, few specialists
2. **Sustainable finance taxonomy gap analysis** — EU vs Swiss divergence = high complexity
3. **GenAI governance for Geneva banks** — FINMA AI guidance = new demand
4. **Real estate workout advisory** — credit quality deterioration = emerging need
5. **FINMA enforcement defense** — specialist niche, limited competition
6. **Treasury risk advisory for family offices** — underserved, growing

## Positioning
**"Independent Geneva finance regulatory intelligence"** — not a law firm, not a Big 4, not a bank
- Independence = credibility with GC and board
- Geneva depth = specific, actionable, not generic
- Regulatory focus = current relevance
