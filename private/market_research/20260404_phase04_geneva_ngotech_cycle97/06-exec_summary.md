# 06 — Executive Summary: Phase 04 Geneva NGO & Tech

## Research Question
What is the addressable market for advisory services targeting Geneva NGOs and tech startups around cyber governance, data protection, and regulatory compliance?

## Key Findings (C97 — NET-NEW)

### Finding 1: NCSC mandatory cyber reporting creates urgent advisory need
Since April 2025, critical infrastructure operators (including humanitarian organizations and tech startups) must report cyber incidents to NCSC. Most affected organizations don't have formal incident response plans.

**Implication:** Cyber incident response plan development is immediate advisory opportunity; few Geneva advisors are targeting NGOs and startups specifically.

### Finding 2: Two-segment approach is validated but requires distinct content strategies
NGOs: governance/transparency focus; data protection for beneficiaries; operational security
Tech startups: regulatory navigation; nFADP compliance; cyber + data governance

**Implication:** Shared framework is possible (cyber governance + data protection) but go-to-market must be segmented.

### Finding 3: FONGIT is the key distribution channel for Geneva tech startups
FONGIT supports 100+ startups; provides coaching, legal support, administrative services. Potential referral partnership could give DigitalCoa direct access to startup decision-makers.

**Implication:** FONGIT partnership is highest-value channel opportunity for tech startup segment.

### Finding 4: 2026 regulatory cascade creates fragmented but real advisory demand for startups
CRS 2.0/CARF (crypto tax), LETA (beneficial ownership), nFADP enforcement, NCSC reporting, AMLA revision — Geneva startups are navigating 5+ regulatory changes simultaneously.

**Implication:** Consolidated "Geneva Startup Regulatory Readiness Assessment" creates first-mover advantage.

### Finding 5: nFADP enforcement intensifying creates data governance need for both segments
Swiss DPA enforcement activity increasing in 2026. Tech startups with user data and NGOs with beneficiary data both face exposure from AI tool usage and data handling.

**Implication:** Data governance framework advisory is cross-segment opportunity.

### Finding 6: Environmental NGOs face new nature-related reporting expectations
FINMA Circular 2026/1 signals increased institutional expectation for nature risk assessment. Environmental NGOs face donor/institutional pressure to demonstrate nature risk governance.

**Implication:** Nature risk governance framework for environmental NGOs is a differentiated niche.
