# 04 — Messaging Framework: Geneva Boutique Wealth Management

## Phase 02: Geneva Finance — Cycle 293

---

## Core Messaging Architecture

The DigitalCoa.ch message to the Geneva boutique WM MD is not "AI governance is important." The MD already knows that. The message is: "You are personally named on documents that create obligations you cannot currently evidence. Here is what that actually means, and here is the proportionate path to having evidence."

The messaging architecture is built around four escalating accountability frames, each calibrated to a specific MD decision point:

---

## Message 1: The Attestation You Already Signed

**Trigger:** MD has signed or is about to sign a carrier attestation with an AI governance clause.

**Message:** "Your name is on an attestation that says you have adequate AI governance controls. If a client suffers a loss and the carrier investigates, the first question will be: did the Managing Director have evidence of adequate AI governance controls when they signed? If the answer is no — and right now, for most Geneva boutiques, the answer is no — the attestation has transferred personal liability from the carrier to you. You didn't buy insurance. You bought a document that documents your exposure."

**What this does:** Activates the personal liability frame. The MD has already taken an action (signing the attestation). The message recontextualizes that action from "compliance done" to "liability documented." This is the hook.

---

## Message 2: The Questionnaire You Didn't Know Was a Comparison

**Trigger:** MD has received or is preparing to respond to an institutional AI governance questionnaire.

**Message:** "Your institutional client sent that questionnaire to your competitors at the same time. They're running a simultaneous comparison across their manager roster. Your response doesn't just go to the client — it goes into a comparison matrix. A response without underlying architecture is worse than no response, because the comparison reveals the gap between what you said and what you have. The boutique that says 'we have comprehensive AI governance' and has no evidence will be ranked below the boutique that says 'here is our architecture' and has documented it. Know what the comparison is measuring before you write the response."

**What this does:** Refames the institutional questionnaire from a compliance obligation to a competitive intelligence event. The MD now understands there is a strategic dimension they hadn't considered.

---

## Message 3: The Explanation You Don't Know How to Give

**Trigger:** MD has deployed AI-assisted portfolio tools and has not addressed the nFADP Art.20 right to explanation.

**Message:** "Your clients have the right under Swiss data protection law to request an explanation of any AI-assisted decision that significantly affects them. In wealth management, your portfolio recommendation is a decision that significantly affects the client. If a client asks you to explain how AI assisted in constructing their portfolio, you face a structural conflict: giving a genuine explanation means disclosing your proprietary methodology, which is your competitive advantage. Giving a vague explanation doesn't satisfy the legal requirement. You've been hoping no one asks. Some clients will start asking. Here's how to make the decision deliberately before you're forced to make it reactively."

**What this does:** Surfaces a live exposure the MD hasn't yet felt acutely. Positions DigitalCoa.ch as the advisor who helps the MD make the proprietary methodology decision before a client or regulator forces it.

---

## Message 4: The Architecture Before the Documentation

**Trigger:** MD is considering building an AI governance documentation response.

**Message:** "Most boutiques start with documentation. They produce an AI governance policy, fill in the attestation template, write the institutional response. What they end up with is a document that represents obligations they can't evidence. When FINMA or a carrier asks to see the architecture behind the policy, they have nothing. The document made the exposure worse, not better. Here's the order: build the architecture first. Then the documentation describes what you actually have. The documentation is only as good as the thing it describes."

**What this does:** Establishes DigitalCoa.ch's methodological principle. Positions the firm as the advisor that builds the architecture, not the one that produces the document. Differentiates from template providers and big law firms.

## Secondary Messaging Themes

### The Geneva Calibration Message
"Geneva boutique wealth management has specific regulatory obligations, a specific competitive context, and a specific institutional client relationship dynamic that no generic AI governance framework addresses. FINMA Guidance 08/2024 was written for supervised financial institutions broadly. We translate it into proportionate methodology for your specific firm size, your specific tool stack, and your specific accountability exposure."

### The Proportionality Message
"You don't need enterprise-scale AI governance. You need architecture that is proportionate to what you actually have — and evidence of that architecture that is specific enough to satisfy FINMA, your carrier, and your institutional clients. Most boutiques don't need more documentation. They need better evidence of what they already have."

### The MD-First Message
"This is not an IT project. This is not a compliance project. This is a personal accountability decision for the person whose name is on the door. We work with the MD and the partner group first. The governance architecture gets built around the accountability structure, not the other way around."

## Language Principles

**Use the MD's language:**
- Say "attestation" not "carrier AI governance clause"
- Say "questionnaire" not "institutional AI due diligence framework"
- Say "your name is on it" not "the firm bears responsibility"

**Be direct about personal liability:**
- The MD understands this is about them personally
- Do not soften it with institutional framing
- The message works because it names the personal stake directly

**Avoid:**
- "AI strategy" — the MD doesn't have an AI strategy problem
- "Comprehensive solution" — the MD has a specific accountability problem
- "Best practice" — best practice doesn't exist for Geneva boutiques
- "Cutting-edge" — the MD wants to reduce exposure, not demonstrate innovation
- "AI governance framework" as a standalone term — use "architecture" or "what you actually have"
