## Framework Section

**Subhead:** The 3-Decision Framework for Geneva Law Firms

**Decision 1: Are you using AI?**
If any attorney, paralegal, or assistant in your firm uses any AI tool for any work task — including research, drafting, or review — your firm has active nFADP governance obligations. Document every tool currently in use.

**Decision 2: Are you processing client data with AI?**
If any AI tool has access to client names, matter details, or case information, your firm is a data controller under nFADP Art. 5(c). You must establish lawful basis, conduct a DPIA (Art. 22), and implement data subject rights processes.

**Decision 3: Do you have EU AI Act exposure?**
If your firm has EU-based clients, uses AI tools with EU-based data centers, or operates AI systems with outputs intended for the EU market, you face dual nFADP + EU AI Act obligations. High-risk deadline: August 2, 2026.
