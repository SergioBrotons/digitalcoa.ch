## Opening

67% of Geneva real estate agencies are using AI tools. That is the highest adoption rate of any professional vertical in the city.

The governance frameworks are not keeping pace.

Most agencies have no formal AI policy. No inventory of what tools are in use. No documented accountability structure for AI-assisted outputs. No plan for what happens when an AI tool produces an error in a property listing, a client communication, or a market analysis.

This is the retroactive compliance problem. It is not a future tense issue. It is the current state of most of the market.

---

## Section 1: Where AI Lives in a Geneva Real Estate Agency

The AI use cases in a typical Geneva real estate agency cluster around four areas:

**Marketing and listing:** AI tools drafting property descriptions, generating marketing copy, optimizing listing text for different platforms.

**Client communication:** AI-assisted drafting of client emails, follow-up sequences, meeting preparation summaries.

**Market research:** AI tools processing comparable property data, market trend analysis, neighbourhood profiling.

**Administrative automation:** Scheduling, calendar management, CRM updates, document organization.

Each of these involves data. Some involves personal data — client names, financial information, property details. The more sensitive the data, the higher the governance obligation.

---

## Section 2: GAIGI and the Regulatory Context

GAIGI — the Geneva Real Estate Agent Professional Regulation — is being implemented. The professional practice standards for Geneva real estate agents are in active development. The introduction of formal AI governance standards into this process is a reasonable expectation.

Simultaneously: nFADP has been operative since September 2023. Any AI tool processing personal client data — which includes property details, financial information, and identity data — triggers nFADP Art. 5(c) data controller obligations. For AI tools used in property valuation or credit assessment, Art. 22 DPIA obligations may already be active.

For agencies with EU market activity, the EU AI Act adds a further layer. Property valuation AI may qualify as high-risk AI systems, with full obligations activating August 2, 2026.

---

## Section 3: The Retroactive Compliance Path

The framing for real estate agencies is different from most professional services verticals. You are not being asked to decide whether to adopt AI. You have already adopted it.

What you are being asked to do is govern what you have adopted.

The retroactive compliance path has four steps:

**1. Inventory your AI tools** — all of them, including personal devices and unofficial use (Shadow AI)

**2. Classify by data sensitivity** — client-facing tools with personal data are highest risk; market research tools are medium; pure administrative tools are lower

**3. Establish accountability** — a named person responsible for AI outputs in client-facing contexts; documented review process

**4. Set a review cycle** — AI tools change frequently; annual review is the minimum

## Section 4: Why Now Is Different

The urgency is not hypothetical. GAIGI is being implemented. FDPIC enforcement in adjacent sectors is documented. The EU AI Act deadline is concrete.

The agencies that do this work now — before the professional standards are locked in, before the first enforcement actions in real estate, before the August 2026 EU AI Act deadline — will be the ones that have a defensible position.
