## Opening

The EU AI Act's GPAI transparency obligations became operative on August 2, 2025. That is 8 months ago.

If your Geneva consulting firm serves EU-based clients, uses AI tools with EU-based data infrastructure, or produces outputs intended for the EU market, those obligations have been applying to your GPAI providers — and indirectly, to your firm — since that date.

You may not have heard about it from your clients yet. That will change.

---

## Section 1: What GPAI Obligations Actually Mean for Consulting Firms

GPAI — General Purpose AI — covers foundation models and general-purpose AI systems. Most commercial AI tools used in consulting fall into this category.

The operative GPAI obligations since August 2, 2025 require GPAI providers to:
- Maintain technical documentation including training data summaries and model specifications
- Publish summaries of training data used, including copyright compliance
- Implement policies for acceptable use
- Maintain incident response processes

For consulting firms, the practical implication is twofold:

First, you must disclose when content you deliver is AI-generated — in proposals, reports, presentations, or client communications.

Second, you must have an acceptable use policy — a document governing how your team uses AI tools in client work.

Neither of these is complex. Neither is optional for EU-nexus firms.

## Section 2: Why May 2026 Is a Market Trigger

The Federal Council's AI Action Plan reaches its First Draft milestone at the Digital Gipfel Schweiz Andermatt on May 26, 2026. This is a government-level event that will receive coverage from Swiss business media and professional associations.

When it does, the Geneva consulting community will have a collective conversation about AI governance. The firms that already have a framework — documented policies, acceptable use guidelines, client disclosure processes — will be the reference points that other firms point to.

The firms that don't have a framework will be the ones asking what to do after the moment has passed.

---

## Section 3: The Dual Compliance Challenge

For Geneva consulting firms, the compliance stack is not single-layered:

**nFADP (Swiss domestic):** Any AI tool processing personal client project data triggers Art. 5(c) data controller obligations. DPIA may be required under Art. 22 for high-risk processing. Operative since September 2023.

**EU AI Act GPAI (if applicable):** Transparency obligations active since August 2, 2025. High-risk AI obligations activate August 2, 2026 for firms with EU nexus.

**Client expectations (market-driven):** Institutional clients are increasingly including AI governance requirements in supplier frameworks. Having a documented policy is becoming a contract requirement.

---

## Section 4: The Practical Path

The practical path is not to build a compliance department. It is to document three things:

1. Which AI tools your firm uses, for what, and on what data
2. Your firm's policy for acceptable AI use in client work
3. Your process for disclosing AI-generated content to clients

From these three, the specific obligations — GPAI, nFADP, or contractual — become navigable.
