## Opening

Over the past several months, I ran a continuous market research program across six Geneva professional services verticals. The objective was simple: understand what AI governance actually looks like for small firms in Geneva — where the adoption gaps are, where the regulatory pressure is real, and where the advisory opportunity exists.

Here is what I found.

---

## What the Research Found Across All Six Verticals

**Pattern 1: High adoption, low governance, everywhere**

Across law, wealth management, healthcare, real estate, consulting, and independent advisory, the same pattern repeats. 61-76% of firms in each vertical are using AI tools. 23-42% have formal governance policies. The gap between adoption and accountability is the defining compliance exposure of 2026.

**Pattern 2: Regulatory deadlines are real and concrete**

This is not theoretical. Swissdamed has a hard July 1, 2026 deadline with no grace period for high-risk medical devices. CARF has been operative since January 1, 2026. GPAI obligations have been active since August 2, 2025. FATF evaluators are in-market. Federal Council's AI Action Plan reaches its First Draft in May 2026.

**Pattern 3: No boutique competitor serving small firms**

Across all six verticals, after more than thirty consecutive research cycles, I found no Geneva-area advisory boutique offering AI governance services specifically to small professional firms. The competitive landscape is dominated by enterprise-focused firms, international consultancies, and technology vendors.

**Pattern 4: The first-mover window is open**

The combination of high adoption, low governance, concrete deadlines, and no boutique competitors defines an open window. It will not stay open indefinitely. The May 2026 Digital Gipfel will raise market-wide awareness. The Swissdamed deadline will force action in healthcare. FDPIC enforcement will expand.

---

## The Vertical Findings in Sequence

**Healthcare (Swissdamed Jul 1, ~13 weeks):** Strongest urgency hook. Hard deadline. No grace period. Medical practices using AI diagnostic tools are the most time-pressured ICP across the entire portfolio.

**Legal (46-point governance gap):** FDPIC enforcement portfolio building. 69% individual gen AI use vs 23% formal firm policies. No boutique competitor serving small Geneva law firms.

**Wealth Management (CARF + FATF operative):** 76% Geneva FS AI adoption. CARF generating obligations since Jan 1. FATF evaluators in-market. No boutique AI governance competitor confirmed.

**Real Estate (67% AI adoption, highest vertical):** Retroactive compliance framing ("govern the AI you already have"). Lower-friction entry for RE agencies already using AI without governance frameworks.

**Consulting (GPAI active since Aug 2, 2025):** 8 months of operative GPAI obligations. May 2026 Digital Gipfel as market awareness trigger. Dual compliance challenge (nFADP + EU AI Act) for EU-nexus firms.

**Cross-vertical (portfolio anchor):** For decision-makers who sit across multiple categories, or who want the full picture before engaging.
